# Medicinal Infusion<br>Checklist
## What to remember before, during, and after making medicinal infusions for maximum potency
@ terrawhisper.com
---
### Before Preparation
Research and select the right medicinal herb
Check herb’s compatibility with any existing medications
Ensure herbal integrity (no mold or damage)
Measure herbs (1-2 teaspoons dried per cup)
Select appropriate container for infusion (glass, ceramic)
Clean equipment thoroughly (teapot, jars, strainer)
Avoid metal utensils when handling certain herbs
Use filtered water for best quality
Boil water (200-212°F/93-100°C)
Choose proper herb-water ratio (1:8 or 1:10)
Decide on infusion time (20-30 minutes, adjust per herb)

### During Preparation
Add herbs to teapot or jar
Pour hot water over herbs, ensuring full saturation
Cover container to retain heat and essential oils
Stir herbs gently a couple of times
Monitor brewing time (avoid over-brewing)
Smell infusion periodically to gauge strength
Adjust herb quantity based on strength preference
Check water level if too much evaporation occurs
Avoid over-extraction to prevent bitterness
Let water cool slightly before drinking

### After Preparation
Strain herbs from liquid using a fine mesh
Discard used herbs responsibly (compost if suitable)
Taste infusion and adjust sweetness (optional honey)
Avoid drinking too much (2-3 cups daily)
Reheat infusion gently if needed (avoid boiling)
Observe for any allergic reactions after consumption
Store excess infusion in a sealed glass container (for up to 24 hours)
Keep infusion away from direct sunlight
Clean infusion equipment promptly
Store herbs in a cool, dry place for future use

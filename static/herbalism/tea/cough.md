# 13 Best Herbal Teas For Cough

![Herbal Teas For Cough](/images/herbal-tea-for-cough.jpg)

Coughing is a ubiquitous occurrence, affecting an estimated 1 billion adults worldwide each year. 

Acute coughs, typically triggered by viral infections, can resolve within a few weeks. However, chronic coughs lasting over eight weeks, affecting up to 10% of adults, often signal underlying conditions (like asthma). These persistent coughs has been proven to disrupt sleep in up to 50% of cases, leading to many side-effects such as fatigue and even worse complications in 10% of instances.

That said, herbal tea proves to be a safe and effective remedy. It has been used and trusted for generations, and it's an effective approach to alleviating cough symptoms, providing a soothing alternative to conventional medications. 

There is a diverse range of herbal teas available and each one has its own unique properties, many of which help with cough. For example, herbal teas like peppermint, ginger, and chamomile have shown their cough relieving effect in many clinical and scientific studies.

In this article, you'll learn which are the best herbal teas for sore throat. You'll also find easy to make recipes and scientific studies that prove the effectiveness of the herbs.

## 1. Peppermint Tea

Peppermint Tea contains active compounds such as menthol, known for its soothing properties that help alleviate cough symptoms. The procedure below give a step-by-step recipe to on how to make Peppermint Tea for cough.

1. Boil 1 cup of water.
2. Place 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves in a teapot.
3. Pour the hot water over the leaves.
4. Steep for 5-10 minutes.
5. Strain the tea into a cup.
6. Sip the peppermint tea slowly.
7. Repeat 2-3 times daily for relief from cough and congestion.

According to a study published by the Journal of Pharmaceutical Sciences and Research, peppermint tea has been recognized for its efficacy in alleviating cough symptoms. The study highlights the key components of peppermint, such as menthol, menthone, and menthyl acetate, known for their antispasmodic, antibacterial, and cooling properties. Peppermint oil, a major derivative, has historically been used to treat respiratory conditions, including bronchitis and throat inflammation. The inhalation of peppermint oil vapor and the consumption of peppermint-infused tea have demonstrated relief from coughs and respiratory congestion.

## Which herbal teas can worsen your cough if overdosed?

While herbal teas are often touted for their soothing properties, it's essential to be cautious about certain varieties that may exacerbate a cough if abused in quantity. 

Teas containing ingredients like peppermint, eucalyptus, or menthol, which are known for their cooling and respiratory effects, can sometimes have an adverse impact on individuals with a persistent cough. These ingredients may cause the relaxation of the lower esophageal sphincter, leading to increased acid reflux, throat irritation, and ultimately worsening of the cough. 

Additionally, strong and highly caffeinated herbal teas may contribute to dehydration, further irritating the throat and making cough symptoms more pronounced. It's crucial for individuals with persistent coughs to choose herbal teas carefully, opting for milder and hydrating blends.

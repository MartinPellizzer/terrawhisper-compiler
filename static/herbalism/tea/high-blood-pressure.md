# 13 Best Herbal Teas For High Blood Pressure (Hypertension)

![Herbal Tea For High Blood Pressure](/images/herbal-tea-for-high-blood-pressure.jpg)

High blood pressure is a condition where the force of blood pushing against the walls of arteries is consistently too high.

Globally, over 1.5 billion people suffer from high blood pressure every year. This condition can negatively affect their quality of life by increasing the risk of heart disease, stroke, and other complications. The main causes of high blood pressure are an unhealthy diet, lack of physical activity, smoking, stress, and family history. High blood pressure also increases the risk of developing type 2 diabetes, dementia, and kidney disease.

In order to prevent high blood pressure, individuals should aim for a healthy weight, regular exercise, a balanced diet, and manage stress levels.

Herbal tea is an excellent natural remedy for managing high blood pressure.

It contains various antioxidants and nutrients that work to improve cardiovascular health and reduce blood vessel constriction, thereby lowering blood pressure levels. Hibiscus tea, for example, has been shown to effectively reduce systolic and diastolic blood pressure by up to 15%. Other popular herbal teas for high blood pressure include chamomile, ginger, and hawthorn berry.

Drinking herbal tea regularly can provide a safe and natural way to control blood pressure levels and improve overall health.

In this article, you'll learn which are the best herbal teas for high blood pressure. Also, for each herbal tea, you'll be provided with a scientific study that proves the effectiveness of the herb for high blood pressure and a step-by-step recipe to easily make the herbal tea.

## 1. Hawthorn Tea (Crataegus Oxyacantha)

Hawthorn tea is known to help lower blood pressure due to its flavonoid content, particularly quercetin and kaempferol.

These flavonoids act as antioxidants, helping to reduce inflammation and improve blood flow in the body. This improved circulation can lead to a decrease in blood pressure.

Additionally, hawthorn tea has been shown to improve cardiac output, which can also contribute to lowering of blood pressure.

<p>According to a study published by the <strong><em>Phytotherapy Research Journal</em></strong>, hawthorn extract has demonstrated potential benefits for individuals with high blood pressure.</p>

<p>In a pilot study involving 36 mildly hypertensive subjects, the participants were assigned to daily supplements of magnesium, hawthorn extract, a combination of both, or a placebo for 10 weeks. Although there was a decline in blood pressure across all groups, subjects taking hawthorn extract showed a promising reduction in resting diastolic blood pressure at week 10 compared to other groups, hinting at its hypotensive effects.</p>

<p>Further research is recommended, given the encouraging findings and the study's use of a relatively low dose of hawthorn extract.</p>

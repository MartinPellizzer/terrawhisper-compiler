# 7 Steps To Make Effective Medicinal Herbal Tea (Beginner Guide)

## 1. Choose The Medicinal Herb

The first step to make an effective medicinal herbal tea is to understand which herb best solves your problem.

The following list reveal the 10 most common herbs used for tisanes and briefly describes their primary benefits.

1. **Chamomile**: Known for its calming effects, aids in sleep, reduces stress and anxiety.
2. **Peppermint**: Relieves digestive issues, soothes stomach discomfort, and alleviates nausea.
3. **Ginger**: Anti-inflammatory properties, aids digestion, relieves nausea, and supports immune health.
4. **Echinacea**: Boosts the immune system, helps fight colds and infections, reduces inflammation.
5. **Lemon Balm**: Calms nerves, promotes relaxation, aids in sleep, and improves mood.
6. **Lavender**: Relieves stress and anxiety, aids in relaxation, and promotes better sleep.
7. **Turmeric**: Anti-inflammatory, supports joint health, aids digestion, and boosts immunity.
8. **Nettle**: Rich in nutrients, reduces inflammation, supports urinary health, and boosts immunity.
9. **Holy Basil (Tulsi)**: Adaptogenic herb, reduces stress, supports respiratory health, and boosts immunity.
10. **Rosehip**: Rich in antioxidants, boosts immune function, supports skin health, and aids in digestion.

If you don't know which herb to pick to follow throught the rest of the tutorial, pick Chamomile. It's inexpensive, available everywhere, and its aroma is appreciated by most people. It's a safe bet.

## 2. Buy The Herb (Tea Bag)

When you've selected the right herb that solves your problem, it's time to visit a grocery store and buy that herb. More specifically, buy the herb packaged as tea bags.

WARNING: Don't buy dry herbs in bulk from a herbalist's shop if you are a beginner. There's a high chance you're going to have a bad experience if you don't know who to assess their quality. So, stick with buying tea bags until you become more experienced.

Commercial tea bags are convenient and easy to use. They are easy portable, accessible, and have a long shelf life. They are consistent in flavor, potency, and they are cost-effective in small quantities.

## 3. Boil Water

In order to make a medicinal tea that's effective and packed with active constituents, you need to boil water first. 

To boil water, simply choose a pot or a kettle and heat it until it reaches 100°C (212°F). You can use the stove, an induction plate, or elettricity to make it boil, depending on they type of the container you're using. You can even put a cup filled with water inside a microwave if you want to boil it fast.

As for the water, use cold tap water if it's good quality. Otherwise, filter and purify the water before using it. In alternative, you can purchase water bottled water.

It's important to boil water to make herbal tea because it helps sterilize it, making it safer to use. Boiling water aids in extracting beneficial compounds from medicinal herbs and brings out the full flavor of the herbs.

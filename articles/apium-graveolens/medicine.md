# Celery (Apium graveolens) Medicinal Guide: Benefits, Constituents, and Preparations

![Celery Medicinal Guide](/images/apium-graveolens-medicine-overview.jpg "Celery Medicinal Guide")


Celery, scientifically known as Apium graveolens, is a versatile vegetable packed with health benefits. Its anti-inflammatory properties aid in reducing inflammation and lowering blood pressure, attributed to compounds like phthalides. With high fiber content, it supports digestion and weight management. Rich in vitamins and antioxidants, celery boosts immunity and aids in detoxification. Essential constituents include phenolic compounds, vitamins (such as K and C), dietary fiber, and minerals like potassium and calcium. Various preparations, from raw consumption to teas and extracts, offer distinct advantages, catering to specific health needs. 

However, precautions are essential: washing celery thoroughly, seeking medical advice before use, allergy testing, and avoiding excessive consumption. Caution is advised during pregnancy, and it's crucial not to replace prescribed medications without consulting a healthcare professional. Tracking intake and opting for organic celery can enhance safety. Overall, celery, in its diverse forms, contributes significantly to holistic health when used conscientiously.

This article explains in details the medicinal properties of celery and how to use this plant to boost your health.

## What are the health benefits and medicinal properties of celery?

Apium graveolens, commonly referred to as celery, offers a myriad of medicinal benefits supported by scientific findings. Its anti-inflammatory properties aid in reducing inflammation, with studies showcasing its effectiveness in lowering inflammatory markers like cytokines and C-reactive protein. Celery's impact on blood pressure reduction has been significant, attributed to compounds like phthalides, which relax blood vessels. Research indicates that consuming around 4 stalks of celery per day (approximately 240 grams) could potentially lower systolic blood pressure by an average of 3 to 6 mmHg.

Its cardiovascular benefits extend beyond blood pressure regulation; the antioxidants present in celery contribute to heart health by reducing oxidative stress and potentially lowering the risk of heart disease by approximately 8% when consumed regularly.

Celery's hydrating properties are noteworthy, as it contains about 95% water by weight, aiding in keeping the body well-hydrated and supporting overall health. Additionally, its low-calorie nature (approximately 16 calories per 100 grams) combined with high fiber content aids weight management by promoting satiety and contributing to a feeling of fullness.

Studies suggest that regular consumption of celery contributes to enhanced immune function due to its rich vitamin and antioxidant content. Furthermore, the vegetable's potential as a detoxifying agent is supported by research highlighting its role in aiding the removal of toxins from the body.

Celery's contribution to bone health cannot be overlooked; it contains notable amounts of calcium and vitamin K, vital for bone strength and density. Moreover, its antioxidants and vitamins, such as vitamin C, support skin health by combating oxidative stress and promoting collagen synthesis, contributing to a healthier complexion.

## What are the key constituents of celery for health purposes?

Apium graveolens, commonly known as celery, is a powerhouse of essential constituents that contribute significantly to its health-promoting properties. Phenolic compounds found in celery, such as caffeic acid and apigenin, exhibit potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, protecting cells from oxidative stress and reducing inflammation in the body. Celery is a rich source of vitamins, containing approximately 29 micrograms of vitamin K per 100 grams, vital for blood clotting and bone health, and about 3 milligrams of vitamin C per 100 grams, boosting immunity and aiding collagen production. Moreover, it boasts an impressive fiber content, offering both soluble and insoluble fibers, averaging around 1.6 grams per 100 grams, which supports digestive health, prevents constipation, and helps maintain a healthy gut microbiota.

Minerals like potassium, present at approximately 260 milligrams per 100 grams, aid in controlling blood pressure and fluid balance, while calcium, found in celery at around 40 milligrams per 100 grams, supports bone health and muscle function. These compounds have been linked to anti-inflammatory effects, potential cardiovascular benefits, and even preliminary studies suggesting certain polyacetylenes like falcarinol and falcarindiol might possess anticancer properties.

## What are the medicinal preparations of celery for health purposes?

Apium graveolens, commonly known as celery, offers an array of health benefits through various preparations, each with specific medicinal properties. Raw consumption of celery, whether in sticks or juice, presents a notable dietary source of antioxidants, providing approximately 1.6 grams of fiber per 100 grams of celery. This fiber aids digestion and supports weight loss by inducing satiety. Celery tea, known for its diuretic properties, assists in regulating blood pressure and water balance, encouraging the elimination of excess fluids from the body. Extracts and supplements derived from celery concentrate its beneficial compounds, providing potent doses of antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals such as vitamin C (approximately 3.1 mg per 100 grams of celery) and potassium (approximately 260 mg per 100 grams). These compounds bolster immune health and support cardiovascular function.

Celery essential oil, extracted from its seeds, possesses anti-inflammatory properties beneficial for aromatherapy and relaxation. Additionally, crushed celery leaves, when used as poultices, provide relief from joint inflammation and pain due to their anti-inflammatory effects. Tinctures made from celery aid in digestion and can stimulate appetite. Furthermore, celery leaf infusions contribute to bone health by offering essential nutrients like calcium (approximately 40 mg per 100 grams of celery) and vitamin K (approximately 29.3 mcg per 100 grams), supporting bone strength and density.

When incorporated into cooked dishes, celery retains its nutritional value, supplying vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants to promote overall health. This versatile vegetable's preparations cater to diverse health needs, showcasing its efficacy in providing essential nutrients and beneficial compounds to support various bodily functions, from digestive health and weight management to immune support and relaxation.

# What is the morphology of Apium graveolens?

![alt](/assets/images/apium-graveolens-botanical-morphology.jpg "title")

## Roots

Apium graveolens has a fibrous root system, with a taproot as the main structure. This taproot is characterized by dichotomous branching, giving rise to secondary roots.

The tips of celery roots are acute, and their density is approximately 7 roots per square centimeter (30 roots per square inch). These roots typically extend to a depth of about 30 centimeters (11.8 inches) in the soil. In terms of diameter, celery roots measure around 0.2 centimeters (0.08 inches) with lengths reaching up to 20 centimeters (7.87 inches).

The color of celery roots is typically whitish to light brown, and their surface texture is smooth and glabrous, meaning they lack trichomes or hairs. Notably, celery roots do not form nodules, which is a distinguishing feature in certain other plant species.

One of the characteristic features of celery roots is the presence of fine and numerous root hairs. These root hairs play a vital role in nutrient and water absorption from the soil, facilitating the plant's growth and development.

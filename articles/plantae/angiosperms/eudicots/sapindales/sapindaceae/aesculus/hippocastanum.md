### Pests

Here's a list of common pests of horse chestnut.

- **Horse Chestnut Leaf Miner (Cameraria ohridella)**:  This moth larva tunnels into the leaves, causing brown patches and reduced photosynthesis.
- **Horse Chestnut Scale (Pulvinaria regalis)**:  These scale insects can weaken the tree by feeding on its sap and excreting honeydew, leading to sooty mold growth.
- **Horse Chestnut Moth (Acrobasis advenella)**:  The larvae of this moth bore into the developing nuts, reducing seed production.
- **Horse Chestnut Leaf Blotch (Guignardia aesculi)**:  This fungal disease causes brown blotches on the leaves, affecting the tree's overall health.
- **Powdery Mildew (Erysiphe spp.)**:  This fungal infection covers the leaves with a powdery white substance, inhibiting photosynthesis.
- **Horse Chestnut Leaf Spot (Guignardia aesculi)**:  Another leaf spot disease, it leads to the development of dark spots on the leaves and can defoliate the tree prematurely.
- **Aphids (Aphidoidea spp.)**:  Aphids can infest trees, feeding on the sap and excreting honeydew, which can attract other pests and diseases.
- **Horse Chestnut Weevil (Curculio spp.)**:  These weevils lay their eggs in the developing chestnuts, and the larvae bore into the nuts, causing damage.
- **Squirrels and Deer**:  While not insects or diseases, these animals can be pests to trees by consuming the nuts or stripping bark.
- **Caterpillars**:  Various caterpillar species, such as the brown-tail moth (Euproctis chrysorrhoea) and the gypsy moth (Lymantria dispar), can defoliate horse chestnut trees when present in large numbers.

### Diseases

Here's a list of common diseases of horse chestnut.

- **Leaf Blotch (Guignardia aesculi)**:  A fungal disease that causes dark, irregular lesions on leaves, often leading to premature leaf drop.
- **Anthracnose (Gnomonia spp.)**:  A fungal disease that results in brown, sunken lesions on leaves, stems, and fruit capsules.
- **Powdery Mildew (Sphaerotheca aesculi)**:  A fungal infection that forms white, powdery growth on the leaves, affecting photosynthesis and overall tree health.
- **Phytophthora Root Rot (Phytophthora spp.)**:  A soil-borne pathogen that causes root decay, leading to reduced water and nutrient uptake and overall tree decline.

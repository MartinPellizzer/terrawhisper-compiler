# What is the morphology of Arctium lappa?

![alt](/assets/images/arctium-lappa-botanical-morphology.jpg "title")

## Roots

Arctium lappa has a taproot system that consists of a primary root, which is the main root, and various lateral, secondary, and tertiary roots branching out from it. The branching pattern of these roots is dichotomous, forming a Y-shaped structure. These roots feature acute tips, giving them a pointed appearance.

Root density measures approximately 0.33 roots per cubic centimeter (1.8 roots per cubic inch). The roots typically extend to a depth of 30 to 60 centimeters (12 to 24 inches) into the soil. The main root has a diameter ranging from 0.5 to 2 centimeters (0.2 to 0.8 inches), while the secondary roots are slimmer, with diameters ranging from 0.1 to 0.3 centimeters (0.04 to 0.12 inches). In terms of length, the primary root typically measures between 20 and 60 centimeters (8 to 24 inches), while secondary roots range from 5 to 30 centimeters (2 to 12 inches) in length.

The color of these roots is generally brown to dark brown, and they exhibit a fibrous texture with a rough surface. Notably, common burdock roots do not form nodules, which are often observed in leguminous plants. Root hairs are present and are characterized by their fine and dense nature.

## How to properly use yarrow to get its benefits?

There are many uses and [preparations of Yarrow](/achillea-millefolium/medicine/preparations.html), such as Yarrow tea, which is made by steeping dried Yarrow flowers in hot water. This preparation is known for its medicinal properties, including its ability to alleviate digestive issues, reduce fever, and promote relaxation. Yarrow tinctures, created by extracting the herb's active compounds in alcohol, are commonly used to address respiratory conditions and enhance the immune system. Yarrow oil, derived through steam distillation, is popular for topical applications, offering benefits for wound healing, skin inflammation, and as an insect repellent. Additionally, Yarrow poultices, made by crushing fresh Yarrow leaves and applying them directly to the skin, prove effective in treating bruises, sprains, and minor injuries due to the herb's anti-inflammatory and analgesic qualities.



## What health side effects can yarrow have if used improperly?

There may be some [side effects associated with Yarrow](/achillea-millefolium/medicine/side-effects.html) if this medicinal herb is misused. Commonly reported side effects include allergic reactions such as skin rashes, itching, and swelling. In rare cases, individuals may experience gastrointestinal discomfort, including nausea and diarrhea. Yarrow can also potentially cause photosensitivity, making the skin more sensitive to sunlight. Pregnant and breastfeeding women are advised to consult with a healthcare professional before using Yarrow, as it may stimulate the uterus. Additionally, individuals with known allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family, such as ragweed and marigolds, should exercise caution, as cross-reactivity may occur. It is crucial to adhere to recommended dosage guidelines and consult a healthcare provider if any adverse reactions occur while using Yarrow for medicinal purposes.

# What is the morphology of Ammi visnaga?

![alt](/assets/images/ammi-visnaga-botanical-morphology.jpg "title")

## Roots

Ammi visnaga possess a fibrous root system with adventitious roots, a feature that aids in nutrient uptake and stability. These roots predominantly branch dichotomously, giving rise to numerous fine roots.

The tips of Ammi visnaga roots are acute, with an approximate length of 0.2 inches (5 mm). The root density is notably high, with approximately 800 roots per cubic meter. However, these roots do not extend to great depths; they remain relatively shallow, reaching only about 3.9 inches (10 cm) beneath the soil surface.

In terms of diameter, the roots are fine, measuring about 0.08 inches (2 mm). The length of these roots can vary, spanning a range from 4.7 to 11.8 inches (12 to 30 cm). Visually, the roots appear white and exhibit a smooth texture.

Notably, Ammi visnaga roots lack root hairs, which are often present in other plant species. Additionally, they do not form root nodules. These characteristics collectively contribute to the root system's overall morphology.

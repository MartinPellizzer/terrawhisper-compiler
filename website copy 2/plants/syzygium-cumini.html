What to know before using Syzygium cumini (Jambul)?

Syzygium cumini, also known as the Indian blackberry or jamun, is a tropical tree with a range of medicinal properties and specific horticultural needs.

Its berries and leaves are used in traditional medicine to treat diabetes, diarrhea, and fever, among other ailments, due to their high tannin and antioxidant content. Syzygium cumini thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and can grow up to 30 meters tall in its native India and other tropical regions.

Its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers are also notable botanical features of this tree, as well as its distinctive dark purple or black, marble-sized fruit.

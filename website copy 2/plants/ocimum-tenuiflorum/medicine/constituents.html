10 Best Active Constituents of Ocimum tenuiflorum (Holy Basil)

Ocimum tenuiflorum, also known as holy basil, is a plant that contains several active constituents with medicinal properties.

The most well-known of these constituents are the essential oils, which contain compounds such as eugenol, methyl eugenol, and urishol. These oils have been found to have anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antioxidant properties.

Additionally, the plant also contains ursolic acid and oleanolic acid, which have been shown to have anti-cancer and anti-viral properties. The leaves of the plant are also rich in phenolic compounds, such as rosmarinic acid and cirsimaritin, which have been found to have anti-bacterial and anti-fungal properties.

Overall, the active constituents of Ocimum tenuiflorum make it a valuable herb for a variety of medicinal purposes.

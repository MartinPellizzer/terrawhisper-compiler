What to know before using Rosa canina (Dog Rose)?

Rosa canina, also known as dog rose, is a species of wild rose native to Europe, northwest Africa, and western Asia.

This plant has various medicinal properties, including the ability to treat mild to moderate dehydration, and it is a rich source of vitamin C, which can help boost the immune system and alleviate the symptoms of common colds. Rosa canina thrives in well-drained soil and in sunny or partially shaded areas. It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 5 meters tall, and it has curved thorns, oval-shaped leaves, and pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer.

The fruit of Rosa canina, known as rose hips, are red or orange when ripe and are often used to make jams, jellies, and teas.

What to know before using Anamirta cocculus (Indian Berry)?

Anamirta cocculus, also known as Schefflera cocculus, is a plant with significant medicinal properties.

Its seeds contain alkaloids that have been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including gastrointestinal disorders, rheumatism, and skin diseases. The plant is a climbing shrub that thrives in humid and shady conditions, making it well-suited for tropical forests.

Anamirta cocculus can grow up to 10 meters tall and has glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. Its small, greenish-white flowers bloom in clusters, and its berries are red when ripe.

Despite its medicinal benefits, all parts of the plant are toxic if ingested in large quantities, and proper precautions must be taken when handling and using it.

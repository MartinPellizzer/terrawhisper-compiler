What to know before using Caesalpinia bonduc (Nikkar Nut)?

Caesalpinia bonduc, also known as the nicker nut tree, is a valuable medicinal plant native to the Caribbean and tropical America.

Its seeds and bark have been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues, rheumatism, and skin conditions. In horticultural settings, Caesalpinia bonduc thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and can grow up to 20 feet tall. It features attractive compound leaves, yellow flowers, and long, slender pods that contain the seeds.

Botanically, Caesalpinia bonduc is a legume in the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its hard, woody seeds, which are encased in a fleshy, orange-red aril.

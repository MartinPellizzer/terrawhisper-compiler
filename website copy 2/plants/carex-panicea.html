What to know before using Carex panicea (Carnat Tong-Grass)?

Carex panicea, also known as bog sedge, is a versatile plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine to treat wounds, reduce inflammation, and soothe digestive issues. In terms of horticultural conditions, Carex panicea thrives in moist to wet soil, making it an ideal choice for bog gardens or areas with poor drainage.

It is a hardy plant that can tolerate partial shade, but prefers full sun. Botanically, Carex panicea is a grass-like sedge that grows in dense clumps, with narrow, arching leaves and brown, papery fruit casings.

It is native to North America and can add a unique, textural element to any garden.

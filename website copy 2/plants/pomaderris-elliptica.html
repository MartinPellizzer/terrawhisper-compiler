What to know before using Pomaderris elliptica (Kumarhou)?

Pomaderris elliptica, also known as the Murray River pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia that is known for its medicinal properties.

The plant contains tannins, which have been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians to treat a variety of ailments, including skin conditions, stomach aches, and diarrhea. In terms of horticultural conditions, Pomaderris elliptica prefers well-drained soil and a position in full sun to partial shade. It is a hardy plant that can tolerate dry conditions once established, making it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.

Botanically, Pomaderris elliptica is characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves, which are covered in soft hairs, and its small, white flowers that bloom in late winter and early spring.

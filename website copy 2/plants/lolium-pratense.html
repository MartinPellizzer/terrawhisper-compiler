What to know before using Lolium pratense ()?

Lolium pratense, also known as meadow grass, has been used in traditional medicine for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

It contains various compounds such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal benefits. In horticulture, Lolium pratense thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is a cool-season grass that grows between 20-80 cm tall and has narrow, flat leaves with a prominent midrib.

The flowering spikes of Lolium pratense are dense and cylindrical, ranging in color from pink to purple, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

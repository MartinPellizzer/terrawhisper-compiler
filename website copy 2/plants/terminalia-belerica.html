What to know before using Terminalia belerica (Beleric Myrobalan)?

Terminalia belerica, also known as bastard myrobalan, is a deciduous tree that is native to the Indian subcontinent and parts of Southeast Asia.

It has a number of medicinal properties, including being a rich source of tannins, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which have been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for centuries to treat a variety of ailments such as diarrhea, dysentery, and skin diseases. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a wide, spreading canopy.

It prefers well-drained, sandy loam soils and thrives in full sun or partial shade. The leaves are simple, oblong, and shiny, and the tree produces small, yellowish-white flowers and round, fleshy fruits.

The bark of the tree is thick and rough, with a grayish-brown color, and is often used in traditional medicine.

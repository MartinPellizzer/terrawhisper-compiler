What to know before using Angelica dahurica (Bai Zhi)?

Angelica dahurica, also known as Chinese Angelica or Dong Quai, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Northeast Asia.

It is a valuable medicinal plant, with roots that contain various compounds used in traditional medicine. These compounds include coumarins, ferulic acid, and phthalides, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antibacterial properties. In horticulture, Angelica dahurica prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, and it can grow up to 2 meters tall with large, lobed leaves and umbels of white or pale pink flowers.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional Chinese medicine, where it is often used to treat a variety of ailments, including headaches, menstrual cramps, and skin conditions.

Angelica dahurica has several medicinal uses, such as alleviating headaches, reducing inflammation, and relieving joint pain.

10 Best Active Constituents of Ammi visnaga (Visnaga)

Ammi visnaga, also known as toothpick weed or khella, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries and contains several active constituents with potential therapeutic benefits.

The two primary active compounds in Ammi visnaga are khellin and visnagin, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties. Khellin, in particular, has been found to have anti-spasmodic, vasodilatory, and anti-inflammatory effects, making it a potential treatment for asthma, bronchitis, and other respiratory conditions.

Visnagin, on the other hand, has been shown to have anti-inflammatory, anti-allergic, and anti-platelet activities, and may be useful in treating conditions such as migraines, hypertension, and cardiovascular disease. Additionally, both khellin and visnagin have been found to have photoprotective effects, suggesting that they may have a role to play in preventing skin damage and photoaging.

However, more research is needed to fully understand the medicinal properties of Ammi visnaga and its active constituents, and to determine the most effective and safe ways to use them for therapeutic purposes.

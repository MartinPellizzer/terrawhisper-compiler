10 possible side effects of Ammi visnaga (Visnaga) if used improperly

Ammi visnaga, also known as Bishop's weed, has been used in traditional medicine for various health conditions.

However, if used improperly, it can cause several side effects. First, it can irritate the gastrointestinal tract, resulting in symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Prolonged use or high doses can lead to photodermatitis, a skin condition characterized by sensitivity to sunlight.

Additionally, it may affect blood pressure and heart rate, causing them to drop dangerously low. Moreover, Ammi visnaga can interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and sedatives, leading to adverse effects. Pregnant women should avoid using it as it may stimulate the uterus, leading to miscarriage.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using Ammi visnaga for medicinal purposes.

1. Gastrointestinal Distress

Gastrointestinal distress caused by Ammi Visnaga refers to discomfort in the digestive tract resulting from the use of this plant.

The main active constituents of Ammi visnaga that can cause this side effect are khellin and visnagin. The main medicinal preparations of Ammi visnaga that can cause this side effect if misused include its essential oil, extracts, and powdered forms.

Individuals with a history of gastrointestinal sensitivities or allergies are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid gastrointestinal distress, it is recommended to start with a low dose of Ammi visnaga, gradually increasing it over time.

Additionally, taking it with food, staying hydrated, and monitoring for any adverse reactions can help minimize this side effect.

2. Hypotension

Hypotension caused by Ammi Visnaga refers to an abnormally low blood pressure level in the body, which can occur as a side effect of using this plant.

The main active constituents of Ammi Visnaga that can cause this side effect are khellin and visnagin, which have vasodilatory effects that can lead to a drop in blood pressure. This side effect is most commonly associated with the oral use of Ammi Visnaga preparations, such as its powdered form, extracts, and pills. People who are more likely to experience hypotension from using Ammi Visnaga include those who are already taking medication to lower their blood pressure, those with low blood pressure, and those who are dehydrated or have a weakened heart.

To avoid this side effect, it's essential to use Ammi Visnaga as directed, avoid taking it with other blood pressure-lowering medications, stay well-hydrated, and consult with a healthcare professional before using Ammi Visnaga if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or take any medications.

Those who are more likely to experience headaches from Ammi visnaga are individuals who are sensitive to its natural compounds, those who take large doses, or those who use it improperly.

To avoid headaches as a side effect of using Ammi visnaga, it is recommended to follow dosage instructions carefully, avoid misuse, and consult with a healthcare professional before using this plant for medicinal purposes.

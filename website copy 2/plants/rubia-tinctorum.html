What to know before using Rubia tinctorum (Madder)?

Rubia tinctorum, also known as madder, is a well-known plant in the natural dye industry and traditional medicine, primarily due to its roots.

The plant exhibits a deep-growing habit, with slender, climbing stems that can reach up to 2 meters in length. Ideal horticultural conditions for Rubia tinctorum include full sun exposure and well-drained soil, preferably in temperate regions. Medicinally, the roots of Rubia tinctorum have been used for centuries to treat various ailments, such as inflammation, skin issues, and gastrointestinal disorders.

The plant's medicinal properties are attributed to the presence of alizarin and other anthraquinones, which possess anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties.

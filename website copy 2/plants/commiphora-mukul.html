What to know before using Commiphora mukul (Guggul)?

Commiphora mukul, also known as Guggul, is a small tree or shrub that is native to India and parts of Africa.

It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to lower cholesterol and triglyceride levels, as well as its potential to reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of osteoarthritis. The tree typically grows in dry and rocky areas, and can reach up to 10 feet in height.

It has small, succulent leaves and produces resinous wounds on its bark, which can be tapped to produce a fragrant oleoresin known as guggulipid. This resin is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine and has been the subject of numerous modern scientific studies.

The horticultural conditions of Commiphora mukul require well-drained soil and full sun, and it is able to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.

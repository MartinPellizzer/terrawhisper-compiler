What to know before using Scutellaria baicalensis (Baical Skullcap)?

Scutellaria baicalensis, also known as Chinese skullcap, is a plant with a rich history of use in traditional Chinese medicine.

It contains various flavonoids, phenolic acids, and volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties. This herbaceous perennial is hardy to zones 4-8 and prefers full sun and well-drained soil. It grows to be around 2-3 feet tall and wide, and produces delicate blue or purple flowers in the summer.

Medicinally, Scutellaria baicalensis is used for its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antibacterial properties, and has been shown to have potential benefits for a variety of health conditions, including respiratory infections, anxiety, and cancer.

When baicalin is metabolized in the body, it is converted into baicalein, another flavonoid that has been shown to possess strong anticancer, antibacterial, and antiviral properties.

What to know before using Carex leporina (Oval Sedge)?

Carex leporina, also known as the European white-beaked sedge, is a perennial plant native to Europe's moist and temperate regions.

It thrives in horticultural conditions with partial shade and consistently moist soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners looking to add texture and variety to their landscapes. The plant's botanical characteristics include tall, slender leaves that grow in dense clumps and produce small, brown flowers in the late spring.

Medicinally, Carex leporina has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues and respiratory infections, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.

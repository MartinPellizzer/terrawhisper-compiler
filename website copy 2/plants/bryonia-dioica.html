What to know before using Bryonia dioica (White Bryony)?

Bryonia dioica, also known as red briony or wild hops, is a climbing plant native to parts of Europe.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and diuretic properties. The plant contains alkaloids such as bryonidin and bryonine, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal effects.

Bryonia dioica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and can grow up to 3 meters tall. Its leaves are palmate, with five lobes, and its flowers are small and greenish-white, grouped in umbels.

The stems are covered in prickles, and the roots are thick and fleshy.

Mentha x piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is a popular herb used in various medicinal preparations such as tisanes and tinctures.

A tisane, or peppermint tea, can be made by steeping fresh or dried peppermint leaves in hot water, and is often used to soothe digestive issues such as nausea, indigestion, and irritable bowel syndrome. Peppermint tinctures, on the other hand, are made by soaking the leaves in alcohol to extract their medicinal compounds, and can be taken orally or added to water.

These tinctures are also used to alleviate digestive problems, as well as headaches and muscle pain. Additionally, peppermint oil, which can be extracted from the leaves, is commonly used in aromatherapy to relieve stress and improve mental clarity.

Overall, peppermint is a versatile herb with numerous medicinal applications.

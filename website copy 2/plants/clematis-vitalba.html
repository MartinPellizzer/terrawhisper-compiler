What to know before using Clematis vitalba (Old-Man'S-Beard)?

Clematis vitalba, also known as old man's beard, is a perennial vine native to Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia.

It has distinctive white, fluffy seed heads and opposite, maple-like leaves. In horticulture, it prefers well-drained soil and partial shade.

Medicinally, Clematis vitalba has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties. The plant contains various chemical compounds, including protoanemonin, which is believed to be responsible for its medicinal effects.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic if ingested and should be handled with care.

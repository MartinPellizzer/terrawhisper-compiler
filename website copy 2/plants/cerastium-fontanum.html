What to know before using Cerastium fontanum (Common Mouse-Ear)?

Cerastium fontanum, also known as Mouse-ear Chickweed, is a low-growing plant found in many parts of the world.

While it is often considered a weed, it has a number of medicinal properties. It has been used in traditional medicine to treat skin irritations and inflammation, and recent studies have suggested that it may have anti-cancer properties.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Cerastium fontanum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is a hardy plant that can tolerate a wide range of conditions. As for its botanical characteristics, Cerastium fontanum has small, delicate, bright green leaves and white, star-shaped flowers.

It spreads by creeping stems that root at the nodes, making it capable of forming dense mats.

What to know before using Strychnos nux-vomica (Nux Vomica)?

Strychnos nux-vomica, also known as the poison nut tree, has a long history of use in traditional medicine, particularly in homeopathy.

Its seeds contain several alkaloids, including strychnine and brucine, which have been used to treat a variety of ailments such as digestive issues, nerve pain, and certain types of paralysis. This tree thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in India and other parts of Asia.

It grows best in well-drained soil and full sun, and can reach heights of up to 60 feet. The tree has a spreading canopy, with glossy, dark green leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The fruit of Strychnos nux-vomica is a green, berry-like drupe that turns yellow or orange when ripe, revealing a bitter-tasting seed inside.

It's important to note that all parts of the plant are highly toxic and should be handled with caution.

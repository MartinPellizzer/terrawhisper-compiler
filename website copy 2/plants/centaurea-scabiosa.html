What to know before using Centaurea scabiosa (Greater Centaury)?

Centaurea scabiosa, also known as Greater Knapweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant with striking purple flowers and downy, gray-green leaves.

It thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soil and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, with extracts from its flowers and leaves used to treat wounds, skin irritations, and digestive issues. Centaurea scabiosa is also valued for its ornamental qualities, and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

However, it can become invasive in some areas due to its prolific seed production and strong rhizomes.

What to know before using Carpinus betulus (European Hornbeam)?

Carpinus betulus, also known as European hornbeam, has been used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.

Its bark and leaves have been used to treat conditions such as arthritis and skin irritations. In horticulture, Carpinus betulus is a popular choice for its hardiness and adaptability, growing well in various soil types and conditions, and thriving in both full sun and partial shade. Botanically, Carpinus betulus is a deciduous tree with a dense, upright growth habit and serrated, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

It is also prized for its attractive, smooth, grey bark and its ability to withstand heavy pruning, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.

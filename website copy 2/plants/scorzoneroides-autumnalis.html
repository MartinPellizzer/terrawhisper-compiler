What to know before using Scorzoneroides autumnalis (Autumn Hawkbit)?

Scorzoneroides autumnalis, also known as autumn hawkbit, is a flowering plant in the daisy family that boasts bright yellow, dandelion-like blooms from summer to fall.

It is native to Europe and western Asia but has naturalized in many parts of the world. Medicinally, the plant contains sesquiterpene lactones, which have been found to have potential anti-inflammatory and antimalarial properties.

As a horticultural subject, Scorzoneroides autumnalis prefers full sun and well-drained soil, thriving in USDA hardiness zones 4-8. Its rosette of toothed, lobed leaves remains evergreen in mild winters, providing year-round interest in the garden.

It's worth noting that, while attractive to bees and butterflies, it can become invasive in some areas due to its prolific seed production.

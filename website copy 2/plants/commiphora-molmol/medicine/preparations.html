Commiphora molmol, also known as myrrh, is a tree that has been used for centuries in various medicinal preparations.

A popular preparation is a tisane, or herbal tea, made by steeping the dried resin in hot water. This soothing drink is said to have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, making it a potential aid for digestive issues and respiratory complaints.

Myrrh tincture, another common preparation, is made by soaking the resin in alcohol for several weeks. This concentrated extract can be used as a natural antiseptic, applied directly to wounds or taken orally to boost immune function. Myrrh is also available in capsule form, providing a convenient and standardized way to consume this versatile herb.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen, as myrrh can interact with certain medications and may not be suitable for everyone.

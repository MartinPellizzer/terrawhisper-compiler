What to know before using Eucalyptus smithii (Gully Gum)?

Eucalyptus smithii, also known as the narrow-leaved peppermint gum, is a tree that boasts both impressive horticultural versatility and significant medicinal properties.

Its botanical characteristics include narrow, aromatic leaves that release a refreshing, minty scent, and a straight, tall trunk that can grow up to 25 meters in height. This eucalyptus variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and parks in Mediterranean climates. Medicinally, the leaves of Eucalyptus smithii are harvested for their essential oil, which has been used for centuries to treat respiratory ailments, muscle pain, and inflammation.

The oil's potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties make it a valuable addition to any natural medicine cabinet.

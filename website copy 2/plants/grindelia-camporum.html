What to know before using Grindelia camporum (Gumplant)?

Grindelia camporum, also known as gumweed or tarweed, is a plant native to western North America that has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

It contains compounds such as diterpenes and flavonoids, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and antispasmodic effects, making it useful for treating respiratory conditions such as asthma, bronchitis, and coughs. This plant is hardy and adaptable, able to thrive in a variety of horticultural conditions, including dry, sandy, or rocky soils, and full sun to partial shade. Grindelia camporum is a perennial herb that grows up to 3 feet tall, with hairy, sticky stems and narrow, lobed leaves.

Its yellow, daisy-like flowers, which bloom in summer, are a popular source of nectar for bees and other pollinators.

What to know before using Potentilla reptans (European Fivefinger)?

Potentilla reptans, also known as creepin' cinquefoil, is a perennial plant that is native to Europe and Asia but can now be found in North America as well.

It has several medicinal properties, including being an anti-inflammatory, astringent, and antidiarreheal agent. The plant is known to relieve mouth and throat irritations and can be used to treat skin conditions.

Potentilla reptans prefers full sun to part shade and well-drained soil. It can tolerate a variety of soil types, from sandy to clay, and is drought tolerant once established.

Its botanical characteristics include yellow, five-petaled flowers and bright green, lobed leaves that grow in clusters along creeping stems.

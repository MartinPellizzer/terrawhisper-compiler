By Martin Pellizzer
1 minutes

Angelica sinensis, also known as Dong Quai, is a plant that has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

Its roots are used to make medicinal preparations such as tisanes and tinctures. A tisane is an herbal tea, and an Angelica sinensis tisane is made by steeping the roots in hot water. This preparation is believed to have various health benefits, including regulating menstrual cycles and alleviating menopause symptoms.

A tincture, on the other hand, is a preparation where the roots are soaked in alcohol to extract the active compounds. The resulting solution is taken orally, often in small doses, and is believed to have similar benefits to the tisane, as well as potentially improving blood circulation and reducing inflammation. However, it's important to note that while these preparations have been used traditionally, their efficacy and safety are not always supported by scientific research.

As with any medication or supplement, it's crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new treatment.

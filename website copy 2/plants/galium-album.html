What to know before using Galium album (White Bedstraw)?

Galium album, also known as Lady's Bedstraw, is a plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

It has been used in herbal medicine for centuries to treat conditions such as skin irritations, wounds, and internal bleeding. The plant contains tannins and alkaloids, which contribute to its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Galium album thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. It is a low-growing plant that spreads through rhizomes and can be invasive in some areas. As for its botanical characteristics, Galium album has small, yellow flowers and thin, hairy stems.

The leaves are arranged in whorls of six to eight around the stem, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

What are the medicinal uses of Galium album?

Galium album is used to treat a variety of medical conditions due to its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and expectorant properties.

Its active constituents include iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal benefits. The plant can be prepared as a tea, tincture, or powder, and is commonly used to treat respiratory ailments such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as skin conditions such as eczema and dermatitis. However, it is important to note that Galium album can cause stomach irritation and digestive issues in some individuals, and should be used with caution.

Additionally, pregnant women and those with kidney or liver issues should avoid using Galium album, as it may have adverse effects on these conditions.

Harvesting Galium album can be done any time during the growing season, but it is best to harvest the aerial parts in the early morning after the dew has dried.

Once dry, remove the leaves and flowers from the stems and store them in airtight containers.

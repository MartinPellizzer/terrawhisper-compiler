Valeriana officinalis (Valerian) Medicinal Guide

Valeriana officinalis, also known as valerian root, has been used for centuries as a medicinal herb due to its calming and sedative properties.

The main constituents of valerian root include valepotriates, volatile oils, and alkaloids, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal effects. Valerian root can be prepared in various forms, such as teas, capsules, tinctures, and liquid extracts, and is often used to treat anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness. However, it can cause side effects such as dizziness, headaches, and stomach upset in some people, especially when taken in large doses or for extended periods.

Therefore, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before taking valerian root, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking other medications, as it can interact with certain drugs and cause adverse effects.

What are the active constituents of Valeriana officinalis (Valerian)?

Valeriana officinalis has several active constituents, such as valepotriates, valerenic acid, valeranone, isovaleramide, isovaleric acid, hydroxyvalerenic acid, flavanones, gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), chatin, essential oil, alkaloids, acylated iridoids, 6-Methylapigenin, linarin, 6,7-Hydroxycoumarin, stigmasterol, campesterol, sitosterol, glucosides, and phenolic acids.

Valepotriates are a group of compounds that have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and anticancer properties. Valerenic acid and valeranone are known for their sedative and anxiolytic effects, which may contribute to valerian's reputation as a natural sleep aid. Isovaleramide and isovaleric acid are responsible for the distinct smell of valerian and have been found to have muscle relaxant properties. Hydroxyvalerenic acid, a derivative of valerenic acid, has been shown to have antidepressant-like effects in animal studies. Flavanones, a type of flavonoid, have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. GABA, a neurotransmitter that regulates brain activity, may contribute to valerian's calming effects.

Chatin, a saponin, has been found to have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The essential oil of valerian contains various terpenes and sesquiterpenes, which have been found to have sedative and antispasmodic effects. Alkaloids, such as actinidine and valerianine, have been found to have sedative and muscle relaxant properties. Acylated iridoids, such as valtrate and didrovaltrate, have been found to have anti-inflammatory and antinociceptive effects. 6-Methylapigenin, linarin, and 6,7-Hydroxycoumarin are flavonoids that have been found to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Stigmasterol, campesterol, and sitosterol are phytosterols that have been found to have cholesterol-lowering effects. Glucosides, such as acteoside and isoacteoside, have been found to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Phenolic acids, such as chlorogenic acid and caffeic acid, have been found to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

What are the possible side effects of using Valeriana officinalis (Valerian) for medicinal purposes?

Valeriana officinalis can have side effects if used improperly.

These side effects can include dizziness, drowsiness, and fatigue, which can affect a person's ability to safely perform tasks that require concentration and coordination. Additionally, valerian can cause headaches, stomach problems, tremors, and restlessness, which can be uncomfortable and disruptive to daily activities.

Dry mouth, upset stomach, and diarrhea are also possible side effects, which can be unpleasant and may require medical attention. Furthermore, valerian can cause mental fogginess, confusion, and irregular heartbeat, which can be dangerous and require immediate medical attention. In some cases, valerian can cause agitation, anxiety, and even insomnia, which is ironic considering that it is often used as a natural remedy for anxiety and sleep disorders.

Therefore, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and usage instructions carefully when taking valerian.

The following list shows the possible side effects of Valeriana officinalis.

  • 1. Dizziness: Valeriana officinalis may cause dizziness, which can make it difficult to maintain balance and perform coordinated tasks.
  • 2. Drowsiness: Valeriana officinalis is known to induce drowsiness, making it useful as a sleep aid but potentially hazardous when operating heavy machinery or driving.
  • 3. Fatigue: Prolonged use of Valeriana officinalis can lead to feelings of fatigue, making it difficult to concentrate or stay alert during daily activities.
  • 4. Headache: Some people may experience headaches after taking Valeriana officinalis, which can range from mild to severe.
  • 5. Stomach problems: Valeriana officinalis can cause stomach problems, including cramps, bloating, and nausea, which can be uncomfortable and disruptive.
  • 6. Tremors: Rare cases of tremors have been reported after taking Valeriana officinalis, which can affect muscle control and coordination.
  • 7. Restlessness: Valeriana officinalis can cause restlessness, making it difficult to relax or fall asleep, despite its sedative effects.
  • 8. Dry mouth: Valeriana officinalis can cause dry mouth, which can be uncomfortable and increase the risk of dental problems.
  • 9. Upset stomach: Valeriana officinalis can cause an upset stomach, which can lead to discomfort, nausea, and vomiting.
  • 10. Diarrhea: Valeriana officinalis can cause diarrhea, which can lead to dehydration and other complications if left untreated.

What are the precautions to take when using Valeriana officinalis (Valerian) medicinally?

Before using Valeriana officinalis for medicinal purposes, it's important to take some precautions.

Firstly, consult a healthcare provider to ensure it's safe and suitable for your health condition. Determine the correct dosage to avoid potential adverse effects. Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using it. Check for any allergies and avoid driving or operating machinery after consumption as it may cause drowsiness.

Start with a low dose and monitor for side effects. Do not mix it with sedatives or consume alcohol. If you have liver disease or are taking other medications, check for possible interactions. Limit exposure to sunlight and discontinue usage before surgery.

For children, consult a healthcare provider before administering Valeriana officinalis, and avoid using it for long-term stress or in children under 3.

The following list shows the precautions to take when using Valeriana officinalis.

  • 1. Consult a healthcare provider first: It's crucial to get professional advice to ensure it's safe and suitable for your health condition and to determine the correct dosage.
  • 2. Determine correct dosage: Taking the right amount is essential as both under- and overdosing can cause harm; follow healthcare provider's instructions or package label recommendations.
  • 3. Avoid in pregnancy and breastfeeding: Valerian officinalis is best avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks to the mother and baby.
  • 4. Check for allergies: Discontinue use if you experience symptoms of an allergic reaction such as rash, itching, or difficulty breathing.
  • 5. Avoid driving or operating machinery: Valerian officinalis may cause drowsiness or impaired alertness, so avoid tasks that require focus and coordination.
  • 6. Start with a low dose: Begin with a smaller dose to assess your body's response and reduce the risk of adverse effects.
  • 7. Monitor for side effects: Be aware of potential side effects like headaches, dizziness, stomach problems, or excitability, and consult a healthcare provider if they persist or worsen.
  • 8. Do not mix with sedatives: Combining Valerian officinalis with sedatives, alcohol, or other substances that cause drowsiness can increase the risk of harmful side effects.
  • 9. Limit usage to 4-8 weeks: Prolonged use may lead to dependency or reduced effectiveness; take breaks and consult a healthcare provider for long-term use.
  • 10. Discontinue if anxiousness occurs: If you experience increased anxiety or restlessness after using Valerian officinalis, stop using it and consult a healthcare provider.

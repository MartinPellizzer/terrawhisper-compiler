What to know before using Gelsemium sempervirens (Yellow Jasmine)?

Gelsemium sempervirens, also known as yellow jasmine or Southern nightshade, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

Its small, fragrant yellow flowers bloom in late spring, producing fruit that resembles a red berry. This plant has gained popularity for its medicinal uses; traditional medicine practitioners have used it to treat conditions such as anxiety, insomnia, and muscle pain. Gelsemium sempervirens thrives best in partial shade with well-draining soil, making it an excellent choice for shady gardens.

Its glossy green leaves provide year-round interest, while its vibrant flowers add a splash of color during the warmer months.

What to know before using Elettaria cardamomum (Cardamom)?

Elettaria cardamomum, commonly known as cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern India and Sri Lanka.

It thrives in humid and shady tropical forests, growing up to 4 meters in height. The plant has a complex botanical structure, with long, slender leaves and a distinctive branched stem that bears the aromatic fruit, or capsule.

This fruit is the primary source of cardamom's commercial value, containing tiny brown or black seeds with a strong, pungent flavor and fragrance. Medicinally, cardamom has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its carminative, digestive, and expectorant properties.

It is also believed to have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects, making it a popular ingredient in various culinary and medicinal applications.

What are the medicinal uses of Elettaria cardamomum?

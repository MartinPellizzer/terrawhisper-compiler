What to know before using Fagus sylvatica (Beech)?

Fagus sylvatica, commonly known as European beech, is a species with both ornamental and medicinal value.

Its leaves and bark have been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including respiratory issues and skin conditions. In horticultural settings, Fagus sylvatica thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can grow up to 20-40 meters tall. This tree is characterized by its smooth, grey bark, and its distinctive, ovate leaves that turn copper-colored in the fall.

The tree's medicinal properties, coupled with its attractive appearance, make it a popular choice for both ornamental and therapeutic purposes.

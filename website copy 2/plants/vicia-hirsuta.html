What to know before using Vicia hirsuta (Hairy Tare)?

Vicia hirsuta, also known as hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant with a variety of uses and characteristics.

In terms of medicinal properties, hairy vetch has been reported to have anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, and it has been used in traditional medicine to treat conditions such as arthritis and skin diseases. Horticulturally, hairy vetch is a hardy plant that can thrive in a wide range of conditions, including poor soil and cold temperatures, making it a popular choice for cover crops and erosion control. Botanically, hairy vetch is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and its clusters of purple flowers, which bloom in the spring and summer.

The plant can grow to be several feet tall and has a deep root system, which allows it to fix nitrogen in the soil and improve its fertility.

What to know before using Ananas comosus (Pineapple)?

Ananas comosus, commonly known as the pineapple, is a tropical plant with remarkable medicinal properties, horticultural versatility, and unique botanical characteristics.

Medicinally, pineapple is well-known for its anti-inflammatory properties due to the enzyme bromelain, which helps in reducing inflammation and swelling, and aids in digestion. Horticulturally, Ananas comosus thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils with a slightly acidic to neutral pH, and benefits from partial shade. This plant is a herbaceous perennial, propagated through the removal of the offsets or suckers that form around the base of the main stem.

The botanical features of Ananas comosus include its rosette of sword-shaped, waxy, green to blue-green leaves, and the production of a single, large, pineapple-shaped inflorescence that eventually develops into the edible fruit.

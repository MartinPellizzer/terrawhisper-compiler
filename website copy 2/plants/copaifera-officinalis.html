What to know before using Copaifera officinalis (Copaiba)?

Copaifera officinalis, also known as the copaiba tree, is a species native to South America that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

This tree is known for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial effects, which are attributed to the presence of bioactive compounds such as diterpenes and sesquiterpenes in its sap. Copaifera officinalis thrives in tropical rainforests, where it can grow up to 100 feet tall and have a trunk diameter of up to 6 feet. The tree has a straight, cylindrical trunk, a rounded canopy, and pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is a hardwood tree that can live for over a century, and its sap, which is extracted through a hole drilled into the trunk, is used in various medicinal and cosmetic products.

What to know before using Buddleja davidii (Orange-Eye)?

Buddleja davidii, commonly known as the butterfly bush, is a popular ornamental plant with striking flower clusters and a pleasant fragrance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, but can also tolerate partial shade. Medicinally, Buddleja davidii has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for treating wounds, skin diseases, and inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves, arching branches, and dense spikes of tiny, tubular flowers that attract butterflies and hummingbirds.

However, it's important to note that Buddleja davidii is considered invasive in some regions due to its vigorous growth and prolific seed production.

What to know before using Eryngium maritimum (Sea Holly)?

Eryngium maritimum, also known as sea holly, is a hardy perennial plant that thrives in sandy or rocky coastal areas.

It possesses medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, making it valuable in traditional medicine. This plant is characterized by its distinctive blue-green, spiky leaves and metallic blue flowers that attract bees and butterflies. Eryngium maritimum prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or coastal-themed landscapes.

It is drought-tolerant and deer-resistant, adding to its appeal for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, ornamental plants with unique visual interest.

What are the medicinal uses of Eryngium maritimum?

Eryngium maritimum has several medicinal uses, such as treating digestive issues, relieving respiratory symptoms, and reducing inflammation.

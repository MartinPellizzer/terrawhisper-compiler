What to know before using Sambucus racemosa (Red-Berry Elder)?

Sambucus racemosa, also known as red elderberry, is a shrub that is native to North America and can grow up to 20 feet tall.

It has compound leaves with serrated leaflets and produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the summer. The berries and flowers of Sambucus racemosa have been used medicinally by indigenous peoples for centuries to treat a variety of ailments, including colds, flu, and inflammation.

However, it is important to note that the berries must be cooked before consumption, as they are toxic when eaten raw. This plant prefers partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil.

It is relatively easy to grow and can be propagated through seed or softwood cuttings.

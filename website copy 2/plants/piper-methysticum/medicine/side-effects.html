10 possible side effects of Piper methysticum (Kava) if used improperly

Piper methysticum, commonly known as kava, is a plant that can have various health benefits when used properly, but it can also cause serious side effects if misused.

Improper use of kava can lead to liver damage, including hepatitis, cirrhosis, and liver failure. Additionally, kava has been linked to neurological issues such as movements resembling Parkinson's disease, and may also cause skin rashes and visual disturbances.

Furthermore, kava can interact negatively with several medications, such as benzodiazepines, barbiturates, and antipsychotics, leading to increased sedation, and it can also exacerbate certain medical conditions, such as depression and anxiety disorders. Prolonged use of kava can result in physical dependence, making it difficult to stop taking without experiencing withdrawal symptoms such as insomnia, tremors, and anxiety.

Therefore, it is crucial to use kava only under the guidance of a healthcare professional and avoid using it with other medications or if you have a history of liver problems.

1. Dermatological Symptoms

Dermatological symptoms caused by Piper methysticum refers to skin-related side effects that can occur with the use of this plant.

The main active constituents in kava that can cause dermatological symptoms are kavalactones, which are found in the roots of the plant. These symptoms are most commonly associated with the misuse of kava-based supplements or teas, particularly when they are consumed in large quantities or over an extended period of time.

People who are more likely to experience dermatological symptoms from kava use include those with a history of liver problems, those who consume alcohol while taking kava, and those who take kava in combination with other medications or supplements. To avoid dermatological symptoms, it is recommended to use kava products as directed, avoid consuming large quantities or using them for extended periods, and avoid consuming alcohol or taking other medications or supplements while using kava.

Additionally, it is important to purchase kava products from reputable sources and to consult with a healthcare provider before using kava, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or who are taking other medications.

2. Liver Damage

Liver damage caused by Piper Methysticum refers to the potential for this plant to harm the liver.

The main active constituents in kava that can cause liver damage are called kavalactones. These compounds can negatively impact liver function, particularly when kava is consumed in large quantities or over an extended period of time.

Medicinal preparations of kava, such as teas, capsules, and tinctures, have been associated with liver damage if misused or consumed in excess. People who are more likely to experience liver damage from kava use include those with pre-existing liver conditions, heavy drinkers, and those taking certain medications. To avoid liver damage, it is essential to use kava responsibly and in moderation, following the recommended dosages and not combining it with alcohol or other liver-toxic substances.

It's also crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before using kava, especially for those with a history of liver disease or who are taking medication.

3. Gastrointestinal Discomfort

Gastrointestinal discomfort caused by Piper Methysticum refers to unpleasant feelings in the digestive tract such as stomachache, nausea, and vomiting.

The main active constituents in kava that can cause this side effect are kavalactones, which can affect the gastrointestinal system when consumed in large amounts or for extended periods. Preparations of kava that can cause gastrointestinal discomfort if misused include kava tea, powder, and supplements, especially when they are not properly prepared or consumed in excessive quantities. Individuals who take kava for extended periods, consume it on an empty stomach, or combine it with alcohol or other medications are more likely to experience gastrointestinal discomfort.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to consume kava in moderation, prepare it correctly by straining and diluting it, avoid combining it with alcohol or other substances, and seek advice from healthcare professionals before using kava, especially if taking other medications or have pre-existing medical conditions.

The main active constituents in Piper methysticum that can cause visual disturbances are kavalactones, which are found in the roots of the plant and are responsible for its psychoactive effects. Preparations of Piper methysticum that can cause visual disturbances if misused include kava tea, powder, and extract.

Individuals who are more likely to experience visual disturbances from using Piper methysticum include those who consume large amounts of the plant, those who consume it for extended periods of time, and those who combine it with alcohol or other sedative drugs. To avoid visual disturbances when using Piper methysticum, it is important to follow dosage recommendations, avoid using it for extended periods of time, and avoid combining it with other sedative substances.

It is also recommended to consult a healthcare professional before using Piper methysticum, especially for those with pre-existing eye conditions or those who are taking medications that can interact with kavalactones.

5. Hearing Difficulties

Hearing difficulties caused by Piper Methysticum refers to the experience of trouble perceiving sounds, especially speech, or a partial or total inability to hear, which can be a potential side effect of consuming this plant.

The main active constituents in Piper Methysticum that can cause hearing difficulties are kavalactones, particularly kavain, dihydrokavain, methysticin, dihydromethysticin, and yangonin. These compounds can have a temporary ototoxic effect on the hair cells in the cochlea, potentially leading to hearing difficulties.

Medicinal preparations of Piper Methysticum that can cause hearing difficulties if misused include kava extracts, tinctures, and teas. Those who are more likely to experience this side effect are individuals with a history of hearing problems, those who consume kava in large quantities or over an extended period, and those who combine kava with other ototoxic substances or medications.

To avoid hearing difficulties, it is crucial to use Piper Methysticum responsibly, follow dosage recommendations, avoid mixing it with other ototoxic substances or medications, and consult a healthcare professional before using kava if you have a history of hearing problems.

6. Drowsiness

Drowsiness caused by Piper Methysticum refers to a sensation of sleepiness or tiredness that can occur after using this plant.

The main active constituents of Piper Methysticum that can cause this side effect are kavalactones, particularly those called dihydrokavain, dihydromethysticin, and yangonin. These compounds can cause a range of effects, including sedation and relaxation, which can lead to drowsiness.

Medicinal preparations of Piper Methysticum that can cause this side effect if misused include teas, capsules, and tinctures made from the root or rhizome of the plant. People who are more likely to experience drowsiness after using Piper Methysticum include those who are sensitive to its effects, have a low tolerance for the plant, or who consume large amounts of it. To avoid drowsiness, it is important to use Piper Methysticum in moderation, start with a low dose and gradually increase it over time, and avoid using it with other substances that cause drowsiness or sedation.

Additionally, it is advisable to avoid driving or operating heavy machinery while using Piper Methysticum.

7. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Piper Methysticum refers to a feeling of lightheadedness, unsteadiness, or disorientation that may occur after consuming this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in kava that can cause this side effect are kavalactones, which have been found to affect certain receptors in the brain that are responsible for balance and spatial orientation. The main medicinal preparations of kava that can cause this side effect if misused include kava teas, capsules, and tinctures, which all contain varying levels of kavalactones. Individuals who are more likely to experience dizziness after taking kava are those who consume large amounts of the herb, those who take it in combination with alcohol or other sedatives, and those who take it for an extended period of time.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of kava and gradually increase, avoid consuming it with alcohol or other sedatives, limit the duration of use, and pay attention to the recommended dosage instructions on product labels.

The main active constituents in Piper Methysticum that can cause headaches include kavalactones such as dihydrokavain, dihydromethysticin, and yangonin. These constituents can affect the central nervous system and may cause headaches in some individuals.

The main medicinal preparations of Piper Methysticum that can cause headaches if misused include kava tea, kava extract, and kava tinctures. People who are more likely to experience headaches from using Piper Methysticum include those who consume large amounts of the plant, those who are sensitive or allergic to kavalactones, and those who consume kava with alcohol or other drugs.

To avoid headaches from using Piper Methysticum, it is important to use it in moderation, follow dosage recommendations, avoid mixing it with alcohol or other drugs, and discontinue use if headaches occur or worsen.

9. Dry Mouth

Dry mouth caused by Piper Methysticum refers to the decreased production of saliva resulting in a dry, sticky feeling in the mouth.

This effect is primarily caused by the main active constituents of kava known as kavalactones, which can inhibit the salivary glands' ability to produce saliva. Medicinal preparations such as kava tea, capsules, and tinctures are most likely to cause dry mouth if misused or overused.

Individuals who take kava for an extended period or in large doses are more prone to experience dry mouth. To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to use kava as directed, avoiding excessive or prolonged use.

Additionally, staying hydrated by drinking plenty of fluids, especially water, can help mitigate the risk of dry mouth.

10. Respiratory Symptoms

Respiratory symptoms caused by Piper methysticum refers to difficulty in breathing or lung-related issues that may arise from the use of this plant.

The main active constituents in Piper methysticum that can cause this side effect are kavalactones, which can have varying effects on the body depending on the dose and the individual's sensitivity. The main medicinal preparations of Piper methysticum that can cause this side effect if misused include kava extracts, tinctures, and teas, which are traditionally used for their calming and anxiolytic effects.

People who have pre-existing respiratory conditions, such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid this side effect, it is important to use Piper methysticum in moderation, follow the recommended dosages, and avoid using it with other substances that can suppress breathing, such as alcohol or opioids.

Additionally, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using Piper methysticum, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or who are taking medications that may interact with kavalactones.

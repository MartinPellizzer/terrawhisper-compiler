Piper methysticum (Kava) Medicinal Guide

Piper methysticum, also known as kava, has a long history of medicinal use in many Pacific Island cultures.

Its roots are used to make a drink that is believed to have various health benefits, including promoting relaxation, reducing anxiety, and improving sleep quality. The active constituents in kava include compounds called kavalactones, which are thought to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

Preparations of kava can vary widely, depending on the specific plant variety used, the part of the plant that is used, and the method of preparation. While kava is generally considered safe when used appropriately, it can cause side effects such as stomach upset, dizziness, and skin irritation.

Additionally, long-term use of kava has been linked to liver damage, so it is important to use it under the guidance of a healthcare provider and to be aware of the potential risks.

What are the active constituents of Piper methysticum (Kava)?

Piper methysticum has several active constituents, such as kavalactones, which are a class of compounds responsible for its medicinal properties.

Fifteeners is a term used to describe the total kavalactone content in a kava preparation. Dipiper methysticin, desmethoxyyangonin, yangonin, methysticin, 5-desmethoxyyangonin, and calebin A are all individual kavalactones that have been researched for their potential anxiety-reducing, sleep-promoting, and muscle-relaxing effects. Pipercide, guineensine, pellitorine, sarmentine, methysticinol, and fisetinidol are other constituents of kava that have been less studied, but may contribute to its medicinal properties.

Oxyphylline, tripiperine, isoxazole, methyltripiperine, N-isobutyldeca-4,6-dienoate, N-isobutylheptadeca-6,8-dienoate, and N-isobutylnonadeca-4,6,8-trienamide are additional constituents of kava that have been identified, but their roles and medicinal properties are not well understood.

The following list shows the active constituents of Piper methysticum.

  • 1. Fifteeners: A group of compounds in Piper methysticum, consisting of various propyl and butyl esters of methysticin, isopiper methysticin, and methysticinol, that may contribute to the plant's sedative and anxiety-reducing effects.
  • 2. Dipiper methysticin: A minor constituent in Piper methysticum, this bis-tetrahydro-beta-carboline alkaloid may have psychoactive properties, but its effects and significance in the plant's medicinal profile remain under-researched.
  • 3. Desmethoxyyangonin: A bioactive flavonoid in Piper methysticum, desmethoxyyangonin has been found to possess anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and neuroprotective properties, which may contribute to the plant's traditional use for pain relief and cognitive support.
  • 4. Yangonin: A major kavalactone in Piper methysticum, yangonin exhibits anxiolytic, nootropic, and neuroprotective effects, and may play a significant role in the plant's traditional use for anxiety, stress reduction, and cognitive enhancement.
  • 5. Methysticin: A key kavalactone in Piper methysticum, methysticin has demonstrated sedative, anxiolytic, and anticonvulsant properties, which may be responsible for some of the plant's medicinal applications in promoting relaxation and managing seizures.
  • 6. 5-Desmethoxyyangonin: A flavokawain in Piper methysticum, this compound has been found to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective properties, which may contribute to the plant's traditional use for oxidative stress-related conditions and cognitive support.
  • 7. Calebin A: A non-kavalactone constituent in Piper methysticum, calebin A has been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticancer properties, which may be relevant for the plant's traditional use in managing inflammatory conditions and promoting overall health.
  • 8. Pipercide: A piperidine alkaloid in Piper methysticum, pipercide has been found to exhibit antimicrobial and insecticidal properties, which may contribute to the plant's traditional use as a natural pesticide and potential topical antimicrobial agent.
  • 9. Guineensine: A minor alkaloid in Piper methysticum, guineensine has been found to possess antileishmanial properties, which suggests its potential role in the plant's traditional use in tropical and subtropical regions for treating parasitic infections.
  • 10. Pellitorine: A piperidine alkaloid in Piper methysticum, pellitorine has been found to exhibit antifungal, insecticidal, and anti-inflammatory properties, which may be relevant for the plant's traditional use in managing fungal infections and inflammatory conditions.

What are the medicinal preparations of Piper methysticum (Kava)?

Piper methysticum has several medicinal preparations, such as infusions, tinctures, decoctions, and extracts, which are made from the roots, rhizomes, stems, leaves, and extracts of the plant.

An infusion is made by steeping the crushed kava root in hot water, while a tincture is created by soaking the root in alcohol. A decoction is a concentrated liquid preparation made by boiling the kava root or rhizome for a prolonged period.

Extracts are made by soaking the plant material in a solvent, such as alcohol or glycerin, to extract the medicinal compounds. Resin extracts are made by collecting and heating the kava root or rhizome to extract the resin, which contains the active compounds. Other medicinal preparations include powdered kava, which can be encapsulated or made into a paste, and dried chips of the kava root.

Additionally, kava can be found in standardized extracts, teas, gels, liquid extracts, and aromatic water, as well as in the form of a macroate, which is a pulp made from the kava root, and micronized root powder, which is a finely ground powder of the kava root.

  • 3. decoction: A strong medicinal preparation of Piper methysticum, made by boiling and simmering the roots and leaves in water.
  • 4. extract: A concentrated, medicinal preparation of Piper methysticum, created by extracting the active compounds using a solvent.
  • 5. resin extract: A highly concentrated medicinal preparation of Piper methysticum, made by extracting the resin from the plant.
  • 6. powder: A medicinal preparation of Piper methysticum, made by grinding the dried plant material into a fine powder.

  • 8. standardized extract: A medicinal preparation of Piper methysticum, in which the active compounds are standardized to a consistent level for consistent effects.
  • 9. tea: A medicinal preparation of Piper methysticum, made by steeping the dried leaves and/or roots in hot water.

What are the possible side effects of using Piper methysticum (Kava) for medicinal purposes?

Piper methysticum can have side effects if used improperly.

Dermatological symptoms such as dry, scaly, or flaky skin can occur, and liver damage may also arise, as evidenced by increased liver enzymes or jaundice. Gastrointestinal discomfort, including stomach pain, nausea, and diarrhea, may be experienced.

Additionally, visual disturbances, hearing difficulties, and respiratory symptoms have been reported. Neurological side effects like drowsiness, dizziness, headaches, and decreased appetite are common, as well as menstrual changes and decreased libido.

Lastly, muscle weakness, dry mouth, and blood abnormalities have been observed, emphasizing the importance of proper usage and dosage.

The following list shows the possible side effects of Piper methysticum.

  • 1. Dermatological symptoms: Piper methysticum may cause skin reactions leading to rashes, hives, or itchiness.
  • 2. Liver damage: Piper methysticum can lead to liver toxicity, impairing its function and causing jaundice or liver failure in severe cases.
  • 3. Gastrointestinal discomfort: Piper methysticum may result in stomach aches, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea.
  • 4. Visual disturbances: Piper methysticum can lead to visual changes, including double vision or sensitivity to light.
  • 5. Hearing difficulties: Piper methysticum may result in ringing in the ears, hearing loss, or balance problems.
  • 6. Drowsiness: Piper methysticum can cause excessive sleepiness or lethargy.
  • 7. Dizziness: Piper methysticum may lead to lightheadedness, unsteadiness, or fainting.
  • 8. Headaches: Piper methysticum can result in headaches, migraines, or pressure in the head.
  • 9. Dry mouth: Piper methysticum may cause dry mouth or difficulty swallowing.
  • 10. Respiratory symptoms: Piper methysticum can lead to shortness of breath, coughing, or chest tightness.

What are the precautions to take when using Piper methysticum (Kava) medicinally?

Before using Piper methysticum for medicinal purposes, it's important to take some precautions such as consulting a healthcare provider before use, as it can interact with various medications and medical conditions.

It's also crucial to avoid using it if pregnant or breastfeeding, and to limit alcohol consumption while using it. Furthermore, it's recommended to avoid operating heavy machinery, discontinue use if allergic reactions occur, and limit its use to 3-4 months.

Additionally, users should not exceed the recommended dosage, avoid mixing it with other CNS depressants, and stay hydrated while using it. It's also important to monitor liver function during use, discontinue use if experiencing dizziness, and avoid using it if taking medication for liver disease, Parkinson's disease, or medical conditions such as anxiety, depression, or seizures. Additionally, users should consult a healthcare provider before using it if taking medication for these conditions, as well as if taking blood thinners or MAOIs.

Finally, users should discontinue use if experiencing skin rash or if planning to undergo surgery.

The following list shows the precautions to take when using Piper methysticum.

  • 1. Consult healthcare provider before use: It's important to seek professional medical advice to ensure it's safe and suitable for your health condition and to determine the correct dosage.
  • 2. Avoid if pregnant or breastfeeding: Piper methysticum may pose risks to the fetus or infant, and its safety during pregnancy and breastfeeding has not been established.
  • 3. Limit alcohol consumption: Combining Piper methysticum with alcohol can increase the risk of adverse effects, such as drowsiness, dizziness, and impaired coordination.
  • 4. Avoid operating heavy machinery: Piper methysticum can cause drowsiness, dizziness, and impaired coordination, making it dangerous to operate heavy machinery or drive.
  • 5. Discontinue use if allergic reactions occur: Allergic reactions to Piper methysticum are rare but can occur, and if you experience symptoms such as hives, swelling, or difficulty breathing, you should stop using it immediately and seek medical attention.
  • 6. Avoid if taking medication for liver disease: Piper methysticum can affect liver function, and if you're taking medication for liver disease, combining the two can worsen liver damage.
  • 7. Limit use to 3-4 months: Long-term use of Piper methysticum can lead to dependence and tolerance, and it's recommended to limit its use to short-term periods.
  • 8. Do not exceed recommended dosage: Exceeding the recommended dosage can increase the risk of adverse effects and toxicity.
  • 9. Avoid mixing with other CNS depressants: Mixing Piper methysticum with other central nervous system depressants, such as benzodiazepines or opioids, can increase the risk of respiratory depression and overdose.
  • 10. Stay hydrated while using: Piper methysticum can cause dehydration, and it's essential to drink plenty of fluids to prevent dehydration and maintain electrolyte balance.

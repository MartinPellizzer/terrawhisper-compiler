What to know before using Melilotus officinalis (Melilot)?

Melilotus officinalis, also known as yellow sweet clover, is a legume with a variety of medicinal properties.

It is known to have anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and diuretic effects, and has been used in traditional medicine to treat conditions such as varicose veins, hemorrhoids, and menstrual cramps. In terms of horticultural conditions, Melilotus officinalis prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and can grow in a variety of soil types.

It is a hardy plant that can tolerate drought and poor soil conditions. From a botanical perspective, Melilotus officinalis is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 3 feet tall, with compound leaves and small, yellow flowers.

The plant has a distinctive sweet, honey-like scent, which comes from the presence of coumarin in the plant's tissues.

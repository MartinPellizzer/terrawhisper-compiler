What to know before using Crepis capillaris (Green Crepis)?

Crepis capillaris, also known as smooth hawksbeard, is a flowering plant species in the daisy family that has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Its roots and leaves contain medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects, though more research is needed to confirm and understand its medicinal uses. This plant is native to Europe and Asia but has since naturalized in many parts of the world.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, and can often be found in meadows, fields, and along forest edges. Crepis capillaris is an herbaceous, biennial plant that grows up to about 3 feet tall, with slender, grooved stems and narrow, lobed leaves.

Its small, yellow flower heads bloom in late spring to early summer, creating a delicate, bird's nest-like appearance.

What to know before using Neottia ovata (Common Twayblade)?

Neottia ovata, commonly known as the twayblade orchid, is a unique and interesting plant with both medicinal and horticultural significance.

Medicinally, it has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues and skin conditions. The plant contains compounds with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it a valuable resource for those seeking natural remedies.

In terms of horticulture, Neottia ovata is a hardy perennial that thrives in shady, moist woodland environments. It prefers well-drained soil with a high organic content, and can be propagated through division or seed. From a botanical perspective, Neottia ovata is distinctive for its two large, leaf-like bracts, which give the plant its alternate name of "twayblade." It is also unique in its ability to form associations with fungi in the soil, which help it to absorb nutrients and water.

Overall, Neottia ovata is a fascinating and versatile plant that is worthy of further study and appreciation.

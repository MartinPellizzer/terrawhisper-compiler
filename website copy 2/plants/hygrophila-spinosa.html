What to know before using Hygrophila spinosa (Gokulakanta)?

Hygrophila spinosa, also known as "Kok swamp cabbage," is a versatile plant native to Southeast Asia.

It boasts impressive medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and analgesic effects, making it a valuable asset in traditional medicine. This aquatic or semi-aquatic plant thrives in humid, wetland environments and can be grown submerged or floating in aquariums, ponds, and garden water features. Hygrophila spinosa is characterized by its long, slender, and spongy stems, which are adorned with small, ovate, dark green leaves that sprout in opposite pairs.

Its delicate lavender or white flowers bloom in clusters, adding a touch of allure to its overall appearance.

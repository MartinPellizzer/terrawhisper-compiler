10 Best Active Constituents of Curcuma longa (Turmeric)

Curcuma longa, also known as turmeric, contains several active constituents with medicinal properties, the most notable of which is curcumin.

Curcumin has been found to have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, and has been shown to help reduce inflammation, improve cardiovascular health, and alleviate symptoms of arthritis. Additionally, curcumin can help improve brain function, reduce the risk of certain types of cancer, and alleviate symptoms of depression. Other active constituents in turmeric include volatile oils such as turmerone and zingiberene, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anticancer properties.

Overall, the active constituents of Curcuma longa make it a valuable medicinal plant with a wide range of potential health benefits.

1. Curcumin

The curcumin contained in Curcuma Longa is a bioactive substance that has powerful anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits.

Its main medicinal properties include reducing inflammation by lowering levels of two enzymes called COX-2 and 5-LOX, and it neutralizes free radicals because of its strong antioxidant properties. Curcumin can help relieve a variety of health conditions such as arthritis, heart disease, and even cancer, due to its potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This constituent is found primarily in the rhizomes, or underground stems, of the Curcuma longa plant.

Some possible side effects of curcumin if used improperly include digestive issues like diarrhea, nausea, and stomach upset, and it can also interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes drugs.

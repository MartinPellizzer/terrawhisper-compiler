By Martin Pellizzer
1 minutes

Curcuma longa, also known as turmeric, is a plant that has been used in various medicinal preparations for centuries.

One popular preparation is a tisane, or herbal tea, made by steeping dried or fresh turmeric root in hot water. This soothing drink is believed to aid digestion, reduce inflammation, and boost the immune system. Another common preparation is a tincture, which is made by soaking chopped turmeric root in alcohol or vinegar for several weeks.

This concentrated extract can be used in small doses to treat a variety of ailments, including arthritis, high cholesterol, and chronic pain. Both of these preparations can also be used topically, in the form of a paste or salve, to treat wounds, burns, and skin conditions. However, it is important to note that while turmeric has many potential health benefits, it can also interact with certain medications and may not be suitable for everyone.

As with any medicinal preparation, it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before use.

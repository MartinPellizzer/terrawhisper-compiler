10 Best Active Constituents of Salvia miltiorrhiza (Dan Shen)

Salvia miltiorrhiza, also known as Danshen, is a widely used herb in traditional Chinese medicine for various medicinal purposes.

The active constituents of Salvia miltiorrhiza include diterpene quinones, such as tanshinones, and phenolic acids, such as salvianolic acids. Tanshinones have been found to possess anti-inflammatory, antitumor, and antioxidant properties, making them useful in the treatment of various cardiovascular diseases, neurodegenerative disorders, and cancer. On the other hand, salvianolic acids have been shown to have protective effects on the cardiovascular system, with abilities to scavenge free radicals, inhibit platelet aggregation, and improve microcirculation.

Together, these active constituents contribute to the wide range of medicinal benefits of Salvia miltiorrhiza, including its use in the treatment of cardiovascular diseases, liver diseases, and neurological disorders.

1. Tanshinol

The tanshinol contained in Salvia Miltiorrhiza is a water-soluble phenolic compound that is one of the most important active ingredients in this traditional Chinese herb.

Its main medicinal properties include antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antibacterial activities, as well as the ability to improve blood flow and protect the cardiovascular system. Tanshinol has been shown to help relieve various health conditions, such as cardiovascular diseases, cerebrovascular diseases, and liver diseases. The main parts of the Salvia miltiorrhiza plant that contain tanshinol are the roots and rhizomes.

Some possible side effects of tanshinol if used improperly include stomach upset, decreased appetite, and dizziness.

2. Salvianolic acid

The salvianolic acid contained in Salvia Miltiorrhiza is a type of phenolic acid that has been identified as the main active constituent of this Chinese herb.

Its main medicinal properties include antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-apoptotic effects, as well as the ability to scavenge free radicals and inhibit lipid peroxidation. This compound has been shown to help relieve various health conditions, such as cardiovascular diseases, liver damage, and neurodegenerative disorders. Salvianolic acid is found mainly in the root and rhizome of Salvia miltiorrhiza, which are the parts of the plant traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Improper use of salvianolic acid may cause several side effects, including digestive symptoms like nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, as well as allergic reactions, hypotension, and skin rashes.

What to know before using Aesculus hippocastanum (Horse Chestnut)?

Aesculus hippocastanum, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a tree with a variety of medicinal, horticultural, and botanical significance.

Medicinally, the horse chestnut has been used in the treatment of various conditions such as chronic venous insufficiency and hemorrhoids due to its anti-inflammatory and vasoconstrictive properties. The tree is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white flowers with a red or yellowish spot. Horse chestnuts prefer full sun and well-drained soil, and can grow up to 100 feet tall.

Despite its many benefits, it is important to note that the seeds and bark of the horse chestnut contain saponins and glycosides, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

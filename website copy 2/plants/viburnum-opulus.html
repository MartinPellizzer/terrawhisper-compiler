Viburnum opulus, also known as guelder-rose or cramp bark, is a shrub native to Europe, parts of Asia, and parts of North America.

It has several medicinal properties, including being used to treat diarrhea, menstrual cramps, and fever. The plant contains various chemical compounds, such as tannins, essential oils, and salicilin, which contribute to its medicinal uses. Viburnum opulus thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can grow up to 4-5 meters tall.

Its showy white flowers, which bloom in late spring, are followed by clusters of bright red berries in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

What are the medicinal uses of Viburnum opulus?

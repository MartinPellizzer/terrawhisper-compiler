What to know before using Gaultheria procumbens (Wintergreen)?

Gaultheria procumbens, also known as wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to North America, primarily found in cold and damp forests.

Its small, oval leaves release an aromatic, sweet-spicy scent when crushed, which has been used medicinally for centuries. The plant contains methyl salicylate, the same compound that gives oil of wintergreen its distinct flavor and therapeutic properties. Medicinally, wintergreen is commonly used as a natural remedy for pain relief and to soothe sore muscles due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

Horticulturally, Gaultheria procumbens thrives in acidic soil with consistent moisture and partial shade, making it an ideal candidate for shady gardens or as a groundcover in colder regions.

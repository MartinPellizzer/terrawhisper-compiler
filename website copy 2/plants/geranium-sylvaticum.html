What to know before using Geranium sylvaticum (Wood Crane'S-Bill)?

Geranium sylvaticum, also known as Wood Crane's-bill, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe's mountainous regions.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, growing up to 80 cm tall with showy, violet-blue flowers. Medicinally, Geranium sylvaticum has been used for its astringent, anti-inflammatory, and hemostatic properties, treating conditions such as wounds, ulcers, and diarrhea.

Its botanical characteristics include palmate, lobed leaves, and fruit that splits into five parts when ripe, releasing beak-like structures that facilitate seed dispersal.

In horticultural conditions, Schizonepeta tenuifolia prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and it is typically propagated through division or seeds.

As for its botanical characteristics, Schizonepeta tenuifolia features purple-pink flowers arranged in spikes, aromatic leaves, and a square stem, which are all typical features of the mint family, Lamiaceae, to which it belongs.

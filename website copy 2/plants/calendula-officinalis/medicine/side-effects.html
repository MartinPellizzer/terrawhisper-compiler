10 possible side effects of Calendula officinalis (Calendula) if used improperly

Calendula officinalis, also known as pot marigold, is a plant that has been used in herbal medicine for centuries, but if used improperly, it can have several potential health side effects.

First, ingesting large amounts of the plant may lead to stomach discomfort, nausea, and vomiting. Additionally, contact with the skin can cause irritation, particularly in those with sensitive skin.

In rare cases, an allergic reaction may occur, resulting in symptoms such as skin rash, hives, and difficulty breathing. Moreover, calendula should not be used internally during pregnancy, as it may stimulate uterine contractions and lead to miscarriage. Finally, it may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and sedatives, reducing their effectiveness or increasing their side effects.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before using calendula, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or those taking medications.

1. Skin Irritation

Skin Irritation caused by Calendula Officinalis refers to the discomfort or inflammation of the skin that may occur after using products containing this plant.

The main active constituents of calendula that can cause this side effect are saponins, flavonoids, and essential oils, which can irritate the skin, particularly if the skin is sensitive or injured. Medicinal preparations such as calendula ointments, creams, and tinctures have the potential to cause skin irritation if misused or used on broken or irritated skin. Individuals with a history of sensitive skin, allergies, or skin conditions may be more prone to experience skin irritation after using calendula products.

To avoid skin irritation, it is important to use calendula products as directed, avoid applying them to broken or irritated skin, and discontinue use if any signs of skin irritation or discomfort occur.

2. Allergic Reactions

Allergic Reactions caused by Calendula Officinalis refers to the immune system's adverse response to the plant's active constituents, which include triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and essential oils.

The main medicinal preparations that can cause this side effect if misused are calendula tinctures, ointments, and teas. Individuals who are more likely to experience allergic reactions to calendula are those with a known sensitivity to plants in the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, chrysanthemums, and ragweed. To avoid this side effect, it is important to perform a patch test before using calendula products and to discontinue use if any signs of an allergic reaction occur, such as skin rash, hives, or difficulty breathing.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using calendula products if you have a history of allergies.

3. Dermatitis

Dermatitis caused by Calendula Officinalis refers to skin inflammation that can occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents in Calendula Officinalis that can cause dermatitis are saponins, essential oils, and resin. These constituents can cause irritation and allergic reactions, particularly when calendula is used in high concentrations or in people who are sensitive to its compounds.

Medicinal preparations such as calendula ointments, creams, and tinctures can cause dermatitis if used excessively or inappropriately. Individuals with sensitive skin, a history of allergies, or those who have previously experienced adverse reactions to calendula are more likely to experience this side effect.

To avoid dermatitis, it is essential to follow recommended dosages, perform a patch test before using calendula topically, and discontinue use if any signs of skin irritation occur.

4. Photosensitivity

Photosensitivity caused by Calendula Officinalis refers to an increased sensitivity to sunlight that can occur with the use of this plant.

This side effect is mainly due to the presence of active constituents such as saponins, flavonoids, and essential oils. Medicinal preparations such as tinctures, ointments, and creams, when used excessively or improperly, can lead to photosensitivity. Individuals with fair skin, those who spend a lot of time outdoors, and those taking photosensitizing medications are more likely to experience this side effect.

To avoid photosensitivity, it is important to use calendula in moderation, protect skin from the sun while using calendula, and discontinue use if skin sensitivity occurs.

5. Headache

Headache caused by Calendula Officinalis refers to pain or discomfort in the head region that may occur due to the use of this herb.

The main active constituents in Calendula Officinalis that can cause this side effect include triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and polessential oils. The primary medicinal preparations of Calendula Officinalis that can cause headaches if misused are tinctures, ointments, and teas made from the flowering parts of the plant.

Individuals who are sensitive to Calendula Officinalis or related plants, have a history of migraines, or are using other medications that narrow the blood vessels are more likely to experience headaches after using Calendula Officinalis. To avoid headaches when using Calendula Officinalis, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage and usage instructions, avoid using it with other medications that narrow the blood vessels, and discontinue use if headaches occur.

It is also recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using Calendula Officinalis for medicinal purposes.

6. Drowsiness

Drowsiness caused by Calendula Officinalis refers to a feeling of sleepiness or decreased alertness that may occur after using this plant.

This effect is mainly due to the triterpene alcohols and proazulenes, which are the main active constituents found in calendula. The medicinal preparations that can cause this side effect if misused include calendula ointments, creams, and tinctures.

People who are more likely to experience this side effect are those who are sensitive to the compounds found in calendula or those who use large amounts of calendula for an extended period of time. To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosages and usage instructions provided by a healthcare professional or the product label.

Additionally, individuals should avoid operating heavy machinery or engaging in activities that require mental alertness after using calendula.

7. Dry Mouth

Dry Mouth caused by Calendula Officinalis refers to a decrease in saliva production or a change in saliva consistency that can lead to discomfort and potential oral health issues.

The main active constituents in calendula that can cause this side effect include triterpene saponins, flavonoids, and essential oils. The primary medicinal preparations of calendula that can cause dry mouth if misused include tinctures, ointments, and teas. Individuals who take certain medications, such as antihistamines, decongestants, and painkillers, or those who are going through treatments like chemotherapy or radiation therapy may be more likely to experience dry mouth from using calendula.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to stay well-hydrated, avoid excessive use of calendula, and consult with a healthcare professional about potential drug interactions.

8. Stomach Upset

Stomach Upset caused by Calendula Officinalis refers to a gastrointestinal disturbance that can occur upon ingesting this herb.

This side effect is primarily caused by the triterpene saponins, flavonoids, and essential oil present in calendula, which can irritate the digestive tract and cause stomach discomfort, cramping, and diarrhea. Medicinal preparations such as calendula tinctures, teas, and powders, when taken in large doses or over an extended period, can lead to Stomach Upset.

Individuals who are sensitive to calendula or have a history of digestive issues, such as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) or inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), are more likely to experience Stomach Upset from calendula. To avoid Stomach Upset, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage guidelines, start with small doses, and gradually increase the amount taken.

Additionally, it is advisable to consult with a healthcare professional before taking calendula if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have any pre-existing medical conditions.

9. Nausea

Nausea caused by Calendula Officinalis refers to the feeling of discomfort or unease in the stomach that can be accompanied by an uncontrollable desire to vomit.

This side effect can be traced back to the main active constituents of calendula, which include triterpene saponins, flavonoids, and essential oils. These compounds, when misused or taken in large amounts, can irritate the stomach lining and cause digestive disturbances, leading to nausea.

Medicinal preparations such as tinctures, extracts, and teas made from calendula flowers are the most likely to cause this side effect if used inappropriately. Individuals who are more susceptible to experiencing nausea from calendula include those with sensitive stomachs, those who are pregnant, and those who take large doses of calendula. To avoid this side effect, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage and usage instructions carefully, start with a low dose and gradually increase if needed, and discontinue use if nausea occurs.

It is also recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using calendula, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

10. Vomiting

Vomiting caused by Calendula Officinalis refers to the forceful expulsion of stomach contents through the mouth as a result of taking this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in calendula that can cause vomiting include saponins, triterpene alcohols, and essential oils. The main medicinal preparations of calendula that can cause vomiting if misused include tinctures, teas, and ointments. People who are more likely to experience vomiting as a side effect of calendula are those who take large doses, those with a sensitive stomach, and those who are allergic to members of the Asteraceae family.

To avoid vomiting as a side effect of calendula, it is important to follow dosage recommendations carefully, start with a low dose and gradually increase, and discontinue use if vomiting occurs.

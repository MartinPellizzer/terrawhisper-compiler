Abrus precatorius, also known as the rosary pea or jequirity bean, is a plant that possesses a variety of medicinal properties.

It contains alkaloids that have been used in traditional medicine to treat conditions such as fever, inflammation, and digestive disorders. This plant is a perennial vine that thrives in tropical environments and can be found in places like Africa, Asia, and Central America.

Abrus precatorius has distinctive seed pods that contain brightly colored seeds, which are highly toxic if ingested. The plant's botanical characteristics include compound, alternate leaves, and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that give way to the aforementioned seed pods.

Despite its toxicity, Abrus precatorius has been used in jewelry-making due to the seeds' vibrant colors, but extreme care must be taken to avoid ingestion or skin contact with the toxic substance.

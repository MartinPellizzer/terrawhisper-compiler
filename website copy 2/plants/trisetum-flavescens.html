What to know before using Trisetum flavescens (Yellow Oatgrass)?

Trisetum flavescens, also known as yellow oatgrass, is a perennial plant native to Europe and Asia that has found use in both horticulture and medicine.

In horticulture, it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and its low-maintenance nature makes it a popular choice for meadows, prairies, and other naturalistic landscapes. Medicinally, yellow oatgrass has been used in traditional medicine for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties, and recent studies have suggested that it may also have potential in treating kidney and urinary tract disorders.

Botanically, Trisetum flavescens is characterized by its narrow, flat leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in shades of yellow and purple.

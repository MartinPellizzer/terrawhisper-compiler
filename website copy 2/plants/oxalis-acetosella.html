What to know before using Oxalis acetosella (Irish Shamrock)?

Oxalis acetosella, also known as wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It has a clumping growth habit and produces delicate five-petaled flowers that are white with pink veins. Wood sorrel is known for its tangy, lemony flavor, and its heart-shaped, smooth-edged leaves are often used as a salad green or garnish.

Medicinally, Oxalis acetosella has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including fever, inflammation, and digestive issues. It contains several active compounds, including tannins, flavonoids, and oxalic acid, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties. In horticultural settings, Oxalis acetosella prefers partial to full shade and well-drained, moist soil.

It can be grown from seed or division and is often used as a groundcover in woodland gardens.

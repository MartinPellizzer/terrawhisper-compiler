What to know before using Centaurea nigra (Common Knapweed)?

Centaurea nigra, also known as greater knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and Western Asia that has been introduced to other parts of the world.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and can grow up to 3 feet tall. The plant is characterized by its purple-pink flowers, which bloom from June to September, and its deeply lobed, dark green leaves.

Medicinally, Centaurea nigra has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. The plant contains several compounds, such as tannins and flavonoids, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal effects.

It has been used to treat a variety of ailments, including skin irritations, digestive issues, and respiratory problems.

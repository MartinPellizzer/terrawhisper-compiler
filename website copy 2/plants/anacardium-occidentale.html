What to know before using Anacardium occidentale (Cashew)?

Anacardium occidentale, commonly known as the cashew tree, is a tropical tree native to northeastern Brazil that has a wide range of medicinal properties and specific horticultural conditions.

Its kidney-shaped fruit, which is a pseudocarp, contains a cashew nut, and an ingredient called anacardic acid, which has been found to have anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, and anti-oxidant properties. Anacardium occidentale thrives in well-drained, sandy or sandy-loam soils and prefers a warm and humid climate, with temperatures between 20-25°C and annual rainfall between 1000-1500mm. The tree can grow up to 12-14 meters tall, has a spreading canopy, and produces small white flowers that eventually develop into the cashew fruit.

The bark of the tree has been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including diarrhea, dysentery, and malaria.

What are the medicinal uses of Anacardium occidentale?

Anacardium occidentale has several medicinal uses, such as being a natural remedy for treating type-2 diabetes, reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease, and having anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The constituents of Anacardium occidentale include a type of antioxidant known as flavonoids, such as anthocyanins and proanthocyanidins, as well as various essential minerals like copper, calcium, and magnesium. Preparations of Anacardium occidentale can be made from different parts of the plant, including the leaves, bark, and apple, and are often used in the form of decoctions, infusions, or powder. However, Anacardium occidentale can cause side effects such as topical skin irritation, stomach upset, and nausea if consumed in large amounts.

Therefore, it is important to take precautions when using Anacardium occidentale, including avoiding consumption of the raw nut, which contains a caustic oil, and consulting with a healthcare provider before using it as a medicine, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or who are taking other medications.

What are the active constituents of Anacardium occidentale?

Anacardium occidentale has many active constituents, such as tannins, cardiac glycosides, and alkaloids, which are known to have various medicinal properties.

Tannins, found in the cashew nut shell, are recognized for their astringent properties and have been used in traditional medicine to treat diarrhea and dysentery. Cardiac glycosides, another group of constituents, have been found to have a positive inotropic effect on the heart, which means they can help to strengthen heartbeats and have been used to treat heart conditions such as congestive heart failure.

Alkaloids, yet another set of active constituents, have been found to have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making them useful in the treatment of pain and inflammation. Additionally, cashew nuts themselves are a rich source of antioxidants, such as vitamin E and selenium, which can help to protect the body against damage from free radicals.

Overall, the various active constituents found in Anacardium occidentale make it a valuable plant in traditional medicine with a wide range of potential health benefits.

What are the possible side effects of Anacardium occidentale?

Anacardium occidentale can have some side effects if used improperly, such as irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.

These reactions may include skin rashes, itching, or swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat. In severe cases, anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition, can occur. Additionally, consuming raw or improperly processed cashew nuts can lead to digestive issues like stomach cramps, nausea, or diarrhea.

Lastly, individuals with a known allergy to cashews or other members of the Anacardiaceae family should avoid using Anacardium occidentale in any form, as it may trigger an allergic reaction.

What are the precautions to take when using Anacardium occidentale?

You should take some precautions when using Anacardium occidentale for medicinal purposes, such as properly identifying and using only the correct plant parts.

The cashew apple, which is the fleshy part attached to the cashew nut, and the cashew nut shell liquid are used in herbal medicine. It is essential to use the correct part of the plant, as the cashew nut shell liquid can cause skin and eye irritation.

Moreover, before using Anacardium occidentale for medicinal purposes, consult a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate preparation, dosage, and potential interactions with other medications or health conditions. Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using Anacardium occidentale, as there is insufficient evidence regarding its safety during pregnancy and lactation.

Lastly, avoid consuming raw or undercooked cashew nuts, as they may contain a toxin called urushiol, which can cause allergic reactions and digestive issues.

Cashew trees should be planted in the spring, and the planting hole should be twice as wide and twice as deep as the root ball. What are the harvesting tips of Anacardium occidentale?

Cashew apples are ready to be harvested when they turn yellow or red, and the nut separates easily from the fruit.

The nuts should be stored in a cool, dry place, and they can be used in a variety of dishes, including cashew butter, cashew milk, and cashew cheese.

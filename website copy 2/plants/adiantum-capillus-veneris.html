What to know before using Adiantum capillus-veneris (Maidenhair Fern)?

Adiantum capillus-veneris, also known as the Southern Maidenhair Fern, is a fern species native to moist and shady locations in the Western Himalayas.

It has a variety of medicinal properties, including being used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory ailments, skin conditions, and gastrointestinal issues. This plant is characterized by its black, rhizomatous stem and delicate, bright green fronds that can grow up to 45 cm long. Adiantum capillus-veneris thrives in well-drained, moist soil and requires partial to full shade.

It is important to note that while this plant has many medicinal uses, it should be used with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional, as with any herbal remedy.

What to know before using Turnera diffusa (Damiana)?

Turnera diffusa, also known as damiana, is a small shrub native to southern Texas, Central and South America, and the Caribbean.

Its leaves have been used traditionally for their mild sedative and aphrodisiac properties, and recent studies suggest that they may help to alleviate symptoms of diabetes and mild anxiety. Turnera diffusa prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it can tolerate a wide range of temperatures, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

The plant's botanical characteristics include serrated, opposite leaves and clusters of small, aromatic yellow flowers that bloom in the late summer and fall.

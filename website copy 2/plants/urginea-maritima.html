What to know before using Urginea maritima (Squill)?

Urginea maritima, also known as sea squill, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region that possesses various medicinal properties.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its therapeutic benefits, including its expectorant, diuretic, and antiseptic properties. Urginea maritima contains bioactive compounds such as cardiac glycosides and alkaloids, which have been found to have potential cardiotonic and anticancer effects.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Urginea maritima thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it can tolerate drought and salt spray, making it a hardy and adaptable plant. Botanically, Urginea maritima is characterized by its tall, upright leaves and large, fleshy bulbs that can weigh up to 3 kg.

The plant produces a tall spike of white, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall.

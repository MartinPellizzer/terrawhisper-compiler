What to know before using Arisaema amurense (Tian Nan Xing)?

Arisaema amurense, also known as the Chinese cobra lily, is a striking and unusual plant native to northeastern China and Korea.

It boasts a variety of medicinal properties, having been used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat ailments such as joint pain, abscesses, and skin diseases. The plant's tuber contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including saponins, steroids, and alkaloids, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal effects.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Arisaema amurense prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, and can be grown in USDA hardiness zones 5-8. It is a deciduous perennial that produces a tall, upright flowering spike with a distinctive hooded shape, resembling a cobra's head, which emerges in late spring and persists through early summer.

The plant's foliage is equally impressive, with large, deeply lobed leaves that provide visual interest throughout the growing season.

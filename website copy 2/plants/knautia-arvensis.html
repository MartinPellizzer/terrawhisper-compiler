What to know before using Knautia arvensis (Field-Scabious)?

Knautia arvensis, also known as field scabious, is a perennial herb with notable medicinal properties.

This plant contains iridoid glycosides, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and anti-tumor effects. Knautia arvensis is hardy and adaptable, able to thrive in a variety of horticultural conditions, including sandy, loamy, and clay soils, and in full sun to partial shade. As for its botanical characteristics, Knautia arvensis features delicate pink to purple flowers that bloom from summer to early fall, and grows to be between 30-80 centimeters tall.

It has a basal rosette of leaves that are oblong to lanceolate in shape, and its stems are covered in short, soft hairs.

What are the medicinal uses of Knautia arvensis?

Knautia arvensis is used to alleviate a variety of health issues, including digestive disorders and skin conditions.

Its medicinal properties are attributed to active constituents such as iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and essential oils. The plant has been found to have anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and diuretic effects, making it helpful for treating issues like indigestion, constipation, and skin rashes.

Medicinal preparations often include dried or fresh aerial parts of the plant, which can be prepared as infusions, decoctions, or tinctures. However, Knautia arvensis may cause side effects such as nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea if taken in large doses or for extended periods. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid using it, and those with known allergies to other members of the Dipsacaceae family should exercise caution when using this plant.

As with any herbal remedy, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before use.

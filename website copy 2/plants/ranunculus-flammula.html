What to know before using Ranunculus flammula (Lesser Spearwort)?

Ranunculus flammula, also known as lesser celandine, is a flowering plant with botanical characteristics that include kidney-shaped leaves, yellowish-white flowers, and a spreading habitation.

It thrives in horticultural conditions of damp soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens. Medicinally, Ranunculus flammula has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The plant contains protoanemonin, a compound that has been shown to reduce pain and swelling, making it a potential aid in treating skin conditions and joint pain.

However, it is important to handle the plant with care, as protoanemonin can also cause skin irritation and stomach upset if ingested.

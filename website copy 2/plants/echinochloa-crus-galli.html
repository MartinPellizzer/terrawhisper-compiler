What to know before using Echinochloa crus galli (Barnyardgrass)?

Echinochloa crus-galli, also known as barnyard grass, is a plant species with medicinal properties, horticultural applications, and distinctive botanical characteristics.

Its seeds and leaves have been used in traditional medicine to treat ailments such as fever, diarrhea, and uterine bleeding. As a horticultural entity, Echinochloa crus-galli thrives in moist soils and sunny conditions, and can grow up to 4 feet tall.

Its botanical features include long, slender leaves, spikelets arranged in a panicle, and distinctive purple or reddish tints on the stems and leaves.

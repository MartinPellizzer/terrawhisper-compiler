10 possible side effects of Hypericum perforatum (St. John’S Wort) if used improperly

Hypericum perforatum, also known as St.

John's Wort, is commonly used as a dietary supplement for its claimed mood-enhancing and anxiety-reducing effects. However, if used improperly, it can cause a variety of health issues. First, it can increase sensitivity to sunlight, leading to skin rashes, burns, or blisters after exposure to the sun. Second, it may interact with certain medications, such as antidepressants, contraceptives, and HIV medications, reducing their effectiveness or increasing their side effects.

Third, it can cause gastrointestinal symptoms like nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Fourth, in some cases, it has been linked to neurological symptoms such as dizziness, restlessness, and confusion. Lastly, in pregnant women, it can increase the risk of abortion or birth defects. Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen, especially with St.

John's Wort.

1. Stomach Upset

Stomach upset caused by Hypericum Perforatum, also known as St.

John’s Wort, refers to discomfort in the abdominal area such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramps. This side effect can be attributed mainly to the presence of hypericin, hyperforin, and flavonoids in Hypericum Perforatum.

The preparations that can cause stomach upset if misused include tablets, capsules, teas, and topical ointments made from this herb. Individuals who are more likely to experience stomach upset from Hypericum Perforatum use are those with a history of gastrointestinal sensitivities or those who take large doses of the herb. To avoid stomach upset, it is recommended to adhere to the recommended dosage, stay hydrated, and take the herb with food.

Furthermore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen, including Hypericum Perforatum.

2. Skin Irritation

Skin irritation caused by Hypericum Perforatum, also known as St.

John's Wort, refers to the reddening, soreness, or inflammation of the skin that can occur as a side effect of using this herbal supplement. This skin reaction can be attributed primarily to the hypericin and hyperforin constituents present in Hypericum Perforatum. These active compounds can cause photosensitivity, making the skin more sensitive to sunlight and prone to irritation.

Medicinal preparations such as capsules, tablets, and teas, if taken in large doses or for extended periods, can increase the risk of developing skin irritation. People with lighter skin tones, those who are genetically predisposed to skin irritations, and individuals who take the herb concurrently with other medications may be more susceptible to this side effect. To avoid skin irritation, it's recommended to use Hypericum Perforatum under the guidance of a healthcare professional, who can provide appropriate dosage recommendations and monitoring.

Additionally, wearing protective clothing and avoiding excessive sun exposure can help mitigate the risk of skin irritation.

3. Fatigue

Fatigue caused by Hypericum Perforatum, also known as St.

John's Wort, refers to a state of extreme tiredness and decreased energy levels that may occur as a side effect of using this herbal supplement. The main active constituents in Hypericum Perforatum that can cause fatigue include hyperforin and hypericin, which can affect neurotransmitter levels in the brain and disrupt normal physiological function. This side effect is most commonly associated with improper use or misuse of St.

John's Wort, particularly in the form of standardized extracts, concentrates, and teas. Those who are more likely to experience fatigue as a side effect of Hypericum Perforatum use include individuals who are taking other medications, such as antidepressants, or those with a history of mental health disorders, as St. John's Wort can interact with these factors and increase the risk of adverse effects. To avoid fatigue as a side effect of Hypericum Perforatum use, it is important to follow dosage recommendations carefully, consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen, and avoid combining St.

John's Wort with other medications or substances that may interact and increase the risk of side effects.

What to know before using Pogostemon cablin (Patchouli)?

Pogostemon cablin, also known as patchouli, is a bushy herb native to tropical regions in Asia.

It has a variety of medicinal properties, including antibacterial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory effects, and is often used in traditional medicine to treat skin conditions, anxiety, and depression. Pogostemon cablin prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can grow up to 3 feet tall.

It has small, pale pink flowers and delicate, fragrant leaves, which are steam-distilled to produce patchouli oil, a valuable essential oil used in perfumes, cosmetics, and aromatherapy.

What are the medicinal uses of Pogostemon cablin?

Pogostemon cablin has several medicinal uses, such as treating skin conditions, reducing inflammation, and alleviating anxiety.

What are the harvesting tips of Pogostemon cablin?

Patchouli leaves can be harvested when they are fully grown, and the plant has at least 10-12 leaves.

Spread the leaves in a single layer on a screen or a tray. Turn the leaves occasionally to ensure even drying. Once the leaves are dry, store them in an airtight container. The leaves can be used fresh or dried.

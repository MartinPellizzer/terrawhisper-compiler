What to know before using Calendula officinalis (Calendula)?

Calendula officinalis, also known as pot marigold, is a flowering plant with bright orange or yellow blooms native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hardy annual that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it is easy to grow in gardens or containers. The plant's flowers and leaves are used in herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing properties. Calendula officinalis contains active compounds such as triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal benefits.

When applied topically, calendula extract can help soothe skin irritations, reduce inflammation, and promote tissue regeneration, making it a popular ingredient in skincare products and natural remedies.

What are the horticultural conditions of Calendula officinalis?

Calendula officinalis needs well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

When planting, sow seeds directly in the ground after the last spring frost, or start them indoors six to eight weeks before the last frost. For care, keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged and deadhead spent flowers to encourage more blooms. When it comes to harvesting, pick the flower heads in the morning after the dew has dried but before the heat of the day sets in.

As for pests and diseases, calendula is generally resistant, but watch out for aphids and powdery mildew, and treat them promptly if they appear.

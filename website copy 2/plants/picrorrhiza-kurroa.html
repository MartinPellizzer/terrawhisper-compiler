What to know before using Picrorrhiza kurroa (Picrorrhiza)?

Picrorrhiza kurroa, also known as Katuki, is a valuable medicinal plant that primarily grows in the western Himalayan region.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained, sandy soil and prefers partial shade. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm and has a thick, fleshy root that is rich in medicinal compounds. Picrorrhiza kurroa is renowned for its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it a popular ingredient in traditional Ayurvedic and Tibetan medicines.

Its roots contain several bioactive compounds, including iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and diterpenes, which have been shown to have potential therapeutic benefits for a variety of health conditions, including liver disease, asthma, and diabetes.

What are the medicinal uses of Picrorrhiza kurroa?

The roots of picrorrhiza are used to prepare decoctions, powders, and liquid extracts, while its standardized extracts are available in the form of capsules and tablets. Some of the side effects of picrorrhiza include stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea, especially if taken in large doses or for long periods.

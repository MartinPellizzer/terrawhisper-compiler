What to know before using Dictamnus albus (Dittany)?

Dictamnus albus, also known as gas plant, is a herbaceous perennial with striking white flowers and a strong medicinal history.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and carminative properties, although more scientific research is needed to confirm these benefits. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and can grow up to 1.2 meters tall with a spread of 1.2 meters.

Dictamnus albus is characterized by its compound, pinnate leaves and its distinctive inflated leafstalks, as well as its clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer.

What are the medicinal uses of Dictamnus albus?

Dictamnus albus has several medicinal uses, such as treating digestive disorders, relieving menstrual cramps, and reducing inflammation.

Its constituents include essential oils, tannins, and flavonoids, which have anti-spasmodic, anti-inflammatory, and anti-microbial properties. The plant can be prepared as a tea, tincture, or ointment, and is often used to soothe upset stomachs, reduce fevers, and alleviate skin conditions. However, dittany can cause side effects such as skin irritation, nausea, and dizziness, and should be used with caution.

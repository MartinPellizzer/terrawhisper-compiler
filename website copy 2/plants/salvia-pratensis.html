What to know before using Salvia pratensis (Meadow Clary)?

Salvia pratensis, also known as meadow clary, is a herbaceous perennial plant that is native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family and is known for its vivid purple flower spikes that bloom in the summer. The plant can grow up to 1 meter tall and prefers full sun and well-drained soil.

Medicinally, Salvia pratensis has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The leaves and flowers are used to make teas, tinctures, and ointments to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive disorders, respiratory infections, and skin conditions. In horticulture, Salvia pratensis is a popular ornamental plant that attracts bees and butterflies and is deer-resistant.

It is also drought-tolerant and requires little maintenance, making it a low-maintenance addition to any garden.

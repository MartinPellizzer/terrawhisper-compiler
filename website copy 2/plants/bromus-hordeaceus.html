What to know before using Bromus hordeaceus (Barley Brome)?

Bromus hordeaceus, also known as ripgut brome or cheatgrass, has reported medicinal properties including anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects, although its use in modern medicine is not widespread.

This plant thrives in dry horticultural conditions, often found in disturbed soils and arid regions, making it a common sight in pastures, fields, and roadsides. As for its botanical characteristics, Bromus hordeaceus is an annual grass that grows to about 80 cm tall, with thin, flat leaves and spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers.

Despite its weedy nature and potential negative impact on native plant communities, it has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

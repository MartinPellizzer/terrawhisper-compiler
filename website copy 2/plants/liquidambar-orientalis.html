What to know before using Liquidambar orientalis (Levant Storax)?

Liquidambar orientalis, also known as oriental sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

Its botanical characteristics include lobed, star-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and purple in the fall, and distinctive spiked fruit. This tree is known for its medicinal properties; the resin exuded by the tree has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. Horticulturally, Liquidambar orientalis prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and can grow up to 25-30 meters tall.

It is a hardy tree that can tolerate a range of soil types and conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping and ornamental use.

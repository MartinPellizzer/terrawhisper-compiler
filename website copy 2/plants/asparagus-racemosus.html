Asparagus racemosus, also known as shatavari, is a plant that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

It is native to Asia and grows in dry areas, requiring well-drained soil and partial shade. The plant can reach up to 2 meters in height and has slender, branching stems with small, delicate leaves.

The root of Asparagus racemosus is where the medicinal properties lie, as it contains steroidal saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides. It has been used to treat various ailments such as digestive issues, inflammation, and reproductive problems in women.

The plant's roots and root-like stems are also used in supplements and tonics for their adaptogenic and antioxidant properties.

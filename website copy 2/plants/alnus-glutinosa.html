What to know before using Alnus glutinosa (European Alder)?

Alnus glutinosa, also known as the European black alder, is a species of tree that is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Medicinally, the tree's bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including skin diseases, stomach complaints, and rheumatism. In terms of horticultural conditions, Alnus glutinosa prefers moist, well-drained soil and can tolerate partial shade, making it a popular choice for damp environments such as the edges of rivers and lakes.

Botanically, the tree is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and small, conical flowers, and can grow up to 30 meters tall.

10 possible side effects of Rosmarinus officinalis (Rosemary) if used improperly

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a popular herb known for its aromatic properties and culinary uses.

However, if used improperly, it can potentially have several negative health effects. First, ingesting large amounts of rosemary oil can cause digestive issues such as stomach irritation, nausea, and vomiting. Second, topical application of rosemary oil can cause skin irritation, redness, and allergic reactions in some individuals.

Third, inhaling large amounts of rosemary oil can lead to lung irritation, headaches, and even seizures in extreme cases. Pregnant women should also avoid using rosemary in medicinal amounts, as it may stimulate menstruation and potentially lead to miscarriage. Lastly, rosemary may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and can increase the risk of bleeding.

Therefore, it is crucial to use rosemary in recommended amounts and consult with a healthcare provider before using it for medicinal purposes.

1. Upset Stomach

Upset stomach caused by Rosmarinus Officinalis refers to a disturbance in the normal functioning of the stomach, such as discomfort, bloating, nausea, and heartburn, which can occur after using rosemary in large amounts or in sensitive individuals.

The main active constituents in rosemary that can cause this side effect are volatile oils like cineol, camphene, and alpha-pinene, as well as tannins and rosmarinic acid. The main medicinal preparations of rosemary that can cause this side effect if misused include essential oils, tea, and supplements. Those who are more likely to experience an upset stomach from rosemary include people with gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), or those who are sensitive to its active constituents.

To avoid an upset stomach, it is recommended to start with small doses of rosemary and gradually increase over time, to avoid consuming it on an empty stomach, and to avoid using large amounts of rosemary essential oil or applying it directly to the skin.

2. Allergic Reactions

Allergic reactions caused by Rosmarinus Officinalis refers to the unpleasant and sometimes dangerous immune response that occurs in certain individuals upon exposure to this herb.

The main active constituents in rosemary that can cause allergic reactions include its essential oil, which is rich in ingredients such as camphor, eucalyptol, and alpha-pinene. The primary medicinal preparations of rosemary that can cause allergic reactions if misused include teas, essential oils, and dietary supplements. People with known allergies to particular plants, such as within the Lamiaceae family that includes rosemary, or those with existing respiratory conditions, are most likely to experience allergic reactions to this herb.

To avoid allergic reactions to rosemary, it is essential to use it in moderation and under the guidance of a healthcare professional, especially for people with a history of plant allergies or respiratory conditions.

3. Skin Irritation

Skin irritation caused by Rosmarinus Officinalis refers to the discomfort or inflammation of the skin that can occur upon contact or ingestion of this herb.

The main active constituents that can cause this side effect are camphor, eucalyptol, and rosemarinic acid. The medicinal preparations that can cause skin irritation if misused include rosemary essential oil, dried rosemary leaves, and rosemary extracts.

People with sensitive skin or allergies to related herbs such as mint, sage, or lavender are more likely to experience skin irritation from rosemary. To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to dilute rosemary essential oil with a carrier oil before topical application and perform a patch test first.

Additionally, avoid ingesting large amounts of rosemary and always follow recommended dosages.

4. Kidney Irritation

Kidney irritation caused by Rosmarinus Officinalis refers to the discomfort or inflammation that can occur in the kidneys as a result of using this herb.

The main active constituents in rosemary that can cause this side effect are volatile oils, diterpenerosmarinic acid, and phenolic acids. These constituents can irritate the kidneys when rosemary is used in large amounts or over a long period of time.

Medicinal preparations of rosemary that can cause kidney irritation if misused include infusions, decoctions, and essential oils. People who are more likely to experience kidney irritation from using rosemary are those with preexisting kidney problems or those who take diuretics, as these factors can increase the risk of kidney irritation. To avoid kidney irritation when using rosemary, it is important to use it in moderation, follow recommended dosages, and avoid using it for extended periods of time.

Additionally, those with kidney problems or those taking diuretics should consult with a healthcare provider before using rosemary.

5. Increased Blood Pressure

Increased blood pressure caused by Rosmarinus Officinalis refers to an elevation in an individual's systolic and diastolic blood pressure levels following the use of this herb.

The main active constituents in rosemary that can cause this side effect are its volatile oils, diterpenes, and tannins. This side effect is most likely to occur when rosemary is used in high doses or over a prolonged period, particularly in the form of essential oils, tinctures, or extracts. People who are more likely to experience increased blood pressure from rosemary use include those with preexisting hypertension, cardiovascular disease, or other conditions affecting blood pressure regulation.

To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to use rosemary in moderation, follow recommended dosages, and consult with a healthcare provider before using rosemary therapeutically, especially if you have a history of high blood pressure or other health concerns.

6. Increased Heart Rate

Increased heart rate caused by Rosmarinus Officinalis refers to an elevation in the number of times a person's heart beats per minute.

This side effect can occur when the body absorbs certain active constituents found in rosemary, such as volatile oils like cineol, camphene, and alpha-pinene. Overconsumption of rosemary preparations, including essential oils, teas, and supplements, can cause increased heart rate, especially when used carelessly or in combination with other stimulants.

People who are more prone to this side effect include those with pre-existing heart conditions, the elderly, and individuals who are sensitive to stimulants. To avoid this side effect, it is essential to follow recommended dosages, consult a healthcare professional before use, and conduct a patch test if using rosemary essential oil.

Additionally, individuals should avoid consuming rosemary in excess, especially when combined with caffeine, nicotine, or other stimulants.

7. Seizures

Seizures caused by Rosmarinus Officinalis refers to the onset of abnormal electrical activity in the brain that can lead to convulsions, loss of consciousness, and other symptoms.

This side effect is thought to be primarily caused by the active constituents of rosemary oil, particularly 1,8-cineole and camphor. The main medicinal preparations that can cause this side effect if misused include rosemary essential oil and large doses of rosemary extract.

People who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with a history of seizures or other neurological conditions, pregnant women, and individuals who are taking certain medications such as blood thinners. To avoid this side effect, it is important to use rosemary essential oil properly by diluting it with a carrier oil and avoiding internal use.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using rosemary essential oil or extract, especially if you have a history of seizures or are taking medication.

8. Pulmonary Edema

Pulmonary edema caused by Rosmarinus Officinalis refers to the abnormal accumulation of fluid in the lungs' alveoli due to the inflammation of the lung tissues.

This condition can be caused by the essential oil or extract of rosemary if misused or taken in large amounts. The main active constituents responsible for this side effect are the phenolic compounds, such as rosmarinic acid and carnosol, which can cause irritation and inflammation of the respiratory tract.

The main medicinal preparations that can cause pulmonary edema if misused include rosemary oil, extract, and infusions. People who are more likely to experience this side effect are those with a history of respiratory conditions, such as asthma, bronchitis, or emphysema, as well as those with weakened immune systems or those who take rosemary in large amounts.

To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to follow the recommended dosage, avoid using rosemary oil or extract near the nose or mouth, and consult a healthcare professional before using rosemary for medicinal purposes, especially for those with pre-existing respiratory conditions.

9. Miscarriage

Miscarriage caused by Rosmarinus Officinalis refers to the spontaneous loss of a pregnancy before the 20th week.

This herb contains active constituents such as volatile oils, diterpene, and flavonoids, which can stimulate uterine contractions and potentially cause miscarriage. The main medicinal preparations of rosemary that can lead to this side effect if misused include essential oils, teas, and supplements.

Pregnant women, especially those with a history of miscarriage or those in their first trimester, are most likely to experience this side effect. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended that pregnant women consult with their healthcare provider before using rosemary and limit their intake to culinary amounts.

Additionally, it is crucial to avoid using rosemary in any medicinal form during pregnancy, as even small amounts can increase the risk of miscarriage.

10. Drug Interactions

Drug interactions caused by Rosmarinus Officinalis refers to the altered effects that can occur when rosemary is used in conjunction with certain medications.

The main active constituents in rosemary that can cause this side effect include volatile oils, diterpenes, and flavonoids. The primary medicinal preparations of rosemary that can cause drug interactions if misused include essential oils, extracts, and dried leaves used in teas or culinary applications.

Individuals who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have existing medical conditions such as high blood pressure or seizure disorders are more likely to experience drug interactions with rosemary. To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before using rosemary in medicinal amounts, especially for those taking medication or with pre-existing medical conditions.

Additionally, it is important to follow recommended dosages and avoid combining rosemary with medications unless under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

What to know before using Viola arvensis (European Field Pansy)?

Viola arvensis, also known as the field pansy, is a species of flowering plant in the violet family with medicinal properties.

This low-growing, hairy annual produces small, five-petaled lavender flowers and round to heart-shaped leaves. It is often found in disturbed soils, grasslands, and open woods.

The entire plant, but particularly the aerial parts, is harvested when in flower for medicinal use. Viola arvensis has been used in traditional medicine for treating respiratory ailments, skin conditions, and inflammation due to its demulcent, expectorant, and anti-inflammatory properties.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and can be directly sown in the garden in early spring or fall.

What are the medicinal uses of Viola arvensis?

Viola arvensis is used to treat a variety of ailments due to its medicinal properties.

Its active constituents include saponins, flavonoids, and mucilage, which contribute to its therapeutic benefits. The herb is known to have anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and diuretic properties, making it useful for treating conditions such as bronchitis, coughs, and urinary tract infections.

Medicinal preparations include teas, tinctures, and ointments, which can be made from the plant's aerial parts. However, it is important to note that Viola arvensis can cause stomach irritation, nausea, and diarrhea in some individuals.

Therefore, it is recommended to consult a healthcare practitioner before using this herb, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have underlying health conditions.

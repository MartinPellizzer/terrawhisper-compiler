What to know before using Tilia cordata (Linden)?

Tilia cordata, also known as small-leaved lime or European linden, is a tree native to Europe and southwest Asia.

Its flowers and leaves have been used in herbal medicine for centuries to treat various ailments, including anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues. Tilia cordata prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, but it can also tolerate full sun. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20-40 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown and heart-shaped leaves.

The tree's fragrant, yellowish-white flowers are borne in drooping clusters and are highly attractive to bees, producing a popular honey.

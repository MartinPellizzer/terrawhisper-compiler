10 Best Active Constituents of Eugenia caryophyllata (Clove)

Eugenia caryophyllata, commonly known as clove, has been used in traditional medicine for its active constituents, which include essential oils, tannins, saponins, and flavonoids.

The most notable active component is eugenol, which has been found to have several medicinal properties. Eugenol has been shown to have anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antiseptic properties, making it useful in the treatment of pain, inflammation, and infection.

Additionally, eugenol has been found to have antioxidant properties, which can help protect the body against damage from free radicals. Furthermore, clove has been used in dentistry for its analgesic and antiseptic properties, and it is also used in traditional medicine to treat digestive disorders, respiratory infections, and skin conditions.

However, it is important to note that clove should be used with caution, as high concentrations of eugenol can be toxic and may cause liver damage or other adverse effects.

1. Eugenol

The eugenol contained in Eugenia Caryophyllata is a phenolic aromatic compound that gives the plant its distinctive smell and taste.

Its main medicinal properties include being an analgesic, antiseptic, and anti-inflammatory, making it useful in treating a variety of health conditions. Eugenia caryophyllata eugenol is primarily found in the buds and leaves of the plant, and has been shown to help relieve pain, reduce inflammation, and fight against bacteria and fungi. Improper use of eugenol can lead to several side effects, such as skin irritation, mouth irritation, and damage to the mucous membranes.

Additionally, excessive use may lead to side effects such as liver damage, seizures, and even death.

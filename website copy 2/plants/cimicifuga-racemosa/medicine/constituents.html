10 Best Active Constituents of Cimicifuga racemosa (Black Cohosh)

Cimicifuga racemosa, also known as black cohosh, is a plant that has been used in medicinal purposes for centuries, particularly for menopausal symptoms.

The active constituents of this plant include triterpene glycosides, phenolic acids, and alkaloids. Among these, the triterpene glycosides, such as actein and 23-epi-26-deoxyactein, are believed to be the primary active components.

These compounds have been shown to possess estrogenic and anti-inflammatory properties, which may contribute to the relief of menopausal symptoms such as hot flashes, night sweats, and mood disturbances. However, the exact mechanisms of action of these compounds are not yet fully understood and further research is needed to fully elucidate their therapeutic potential.

Additionally, it is important to note that the quality and composition of black cohosh supplements can vary significantly, and it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.

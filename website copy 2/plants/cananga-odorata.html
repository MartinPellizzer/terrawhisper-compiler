What to know before using Cananga odorata (Ylang-Ylang)?

Cananga odorata, commonly known as ylang-ylang, is a tropical tree valued for its fragrant flowers and medicinal properties.

Its flowers are known to have a calming effect on the mind and are used in aromatherapy to relieve stress, anxiety, and insomnia. Cananga odorata prefers a warm, humid climate with well-drained soil and partial shade. It can grow up to 20 meters tall and has large, glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellow-green flowers that bloom year-round.

The tree's bark is smooth and gray, and its wood is used for timber in some regions.

What are the medicinal uses of Cananga odorata?

Cananga odorata has several medicinal uses, such as reducing anxiety and improving mood.

Its constituents include a variety of compounds, such as germacrone and various sesquiterpene alcohols, that have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and sedative properties. Preparations of ylang-ylang can be made into essential oils, teas, and tinctures for various applications. However, it is important to note that ylang-ylang can cause side effects such as nausea, skin irritation, and headaches in some individuals.

Additionally, pregnant women and those with low blood pressure should exercise caution when using ylang-ylang, as it may lower blood pressure further and stimulate menstrual flow.

What are the health benefits of Cananga odorata?

Cananga odorata has many health benefits, such as promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety.

What are the active constituents of Cananga odorata?

Cananga odorata has many active constituents, such as linalool and germacrone, that have been found to possess medicinal properties.

Linalool, a monoterpene alcohol, is the primary constituent of ylang-ylang oil and has been shown to have anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial effects. Germacrone, a sesquiterpene, is another important constituent that has been found to have anticonvulsant, anti-inflammatory, and antinociceptive properties.

Additionally, ylang-ylang oil contains other compounds such as geranyl acetate, caryophyllene, and methyl cinnamate, which have been found to have various medicinal properties including sedative, antispasmodic, and antidepressant effects. The combination of these active constituents in ylang-ylang oil has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments including anxiety, depression, insomnia, and high blood pressure.

Furthermore, recent studies have shown that ylang-ylang oil has potential as an alternative treatment for neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's due to its antioxidant and neuroprotective properties.

What are the possible side effects of Cananga odorata?

Cananga odorata can have some side effects if used improperly, such as headaches or nausea in some individuals.

In rare cases, it may cause skin irritation or allergic reactions, especially when used topically. When ingested, it can lead to stomach discomfort, digestive issues, and in severe instances, respiratory problems.

Additionally, inhaling large amounts of ylang-ylang oil can potentially result in unconsciousness due to its strong sedative properties.

What are the precautions to take when using Cananga odorata?

You should take some precautions when using Cananga odorata for medicinal purposes, such as diluting essential oils properly before use.

Ylang-ylang oil should be diluted with a carrier oil, like jojoba or coconut oil, before applying it to the skin, as it can cause irritation if used undiluted. It's also important to perform a patch test on a small area of skin before using ylang-ylang oil more broadly, to ensure that you don't have an adverse reaction. Additionally, you should consult with a healthcare provider before using ylang-ylang oil if you are pregnant or breastfeeding, as its safety during these times has not been well-studied.

Finally, it's crucial to use high-quality ylang-ylang oil that has been sourced responsibly, as poor-quality oils may contain impurities or be produced using unethical labor practices.

Remove the tree from its container, gently loosen the roots, and place it in the hole. Backfill the hole with soil, tamping it down firmly to remove any air pockets.

Harvesting Cananga odorata requires careful timing and technique.

The flowers are at their most fragrant in the early morning, so this is the best time to pick them. Use a pair of sharp scissors or pruning shears to snip the flowers from the tree, leaving a short stem attached. Spread the flowers out in a single layer on a screen or tray and allow them to air dry for several days.

Once dry, store the flowers in an airtight container in a cool, dark place.

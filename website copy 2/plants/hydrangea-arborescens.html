What to know before using Hydrangea arborescens (Wild Hydrangea)?

Hydrangea arborescens, also known as smooth hydrangea, is a shrub native to the forests of the eastern United States.

It has large, showy clusters of flowers that can be white, pink, or blue, and its leaves are dark green and ovate-shaped. Medicinally, Hydrangea arborescens has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties.

The roots of the plant contain a compound called hydrangin, which has been shown to have potential benefits for treating kidney stones and urinary tract infections. In terms of horticultural conditions, Hydrangea arborescens prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil.

It is a hardy plant that can tolerate a wide range of soil types and pH levels, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

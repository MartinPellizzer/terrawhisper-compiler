Citrus limon (Lemon) Medicinal Guide

Citrus limon, commonly known as lemon, has numerous medicinal benefits including its ability to aid digestion, boost immune system function, and reduce inflammation.

The main constituents of lemons include citric acid, vitamin C, and various flavonoids, all of which contribute to its medicinal properties. Lemon juice, peel, and essential oil are commonly prepared for medicinal use, either consumed orally or applied topically.

However, lemon can cause side effects such as mouth irritation, stomach discomfort, and increased sun sensitivity. Therefore, it is important to dilute lemon juice or oil before use and avoid exposure to sunlight after application.

Additionally, people with certain medical conditions such as heartburn, ulcers, or kidney issues should consult a healthcare professional before using lemon for medicinal purposes.

What are the active constituents of Citrus limon (Lemon)?

Citrus Limon has several active constituents with various health benefits.

Limonin, a compound found in the juice of citrus fruits, has been shown to have anti-cancer properties. Hesperidin, a flavonoid found in the peel, has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

D-limonene, a compound in the peel's oil, has been found to have anti-cancer and chemopreventive properties. Naringin and Eriocitrin, both flavonoids found in high concentrations in lemons, have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Additionally, lemons are a rich source of Vitamin C, a potent antioxidant that supports the immune system.

Naringenin, Ferulic acid, Quercetin, and Kaempferol are other flavonoids present in lemons that contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

What are the medicinal preparations of Citrus limon (Lemon)?

Citrus Limon has many medicinal preparations, such as Infusion, which is made by steeping lemon leaves or peel in hot water to extract its beneficial compounds, used for treating digestive disorders and sore throats.

Decoction, a stronger preparation made by simmering lemon parts in water, is used for treating respiratory ailments and fever. Tincture, a preparation that involves soaking lemon peel in alcohol, is used as a digestive tonic and for treating skin conditions. Juice, extracted from the fruit, is rich in vitamin C and is used to treat scurvy, boost the immune system, and promote healing. Oil, extracted from the lemon peel, is used topically for treating fungal infections and promoting relaxation. Bath salts, made by mixing lemon juice or oil with Epsom salts, are used for relieving sore muscles and detoxifying the body.

Balm, Cream, and Lotion, made by mixing lemon juice or oil with a base, are used topically for treating acne, wounds, and sunburn. Poultice, made by mashing lemon parts and applying it to the skin, is used for treating boils and abscesses. Syrup, made by mixing lemon juice or peel with sugar and water, is used as a cough remedy and for treating sore throats. Vinegar, made by fermenting lemon juice or rind, is used as a digestive aid and for treating skin conditions. Extract, made by soaking lemon peel in alcohol or glycerin, is used as a flavoring agent and for treating digestive disorders. Powder, made by drying and grinding lemon parts, is used as a food preservative and for treating digestive disorders.

Suppository, made by mixing lemon juice or oil with a base, is used for treating hemorrhoids and other rectal ailments.

What are the possible side effects of using Citrus limon (Lemon) for medicinal purposes?

Citrus Limon can have side effects if used improperly, such as mouth irritation due to its acidic nature.

Additionally, it can cause gastrointestinal issues like nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, heartburn, and stomach pain. Some people may also experience side effects such as headache, dizziness, and excessive thirst or urination. Furthermore, it can cause skin irritation and respiratory issues in some individuals.

Lastly, it can make skin more sensitive to the sun, causing photosensitivity, and can cause allergic reactions or increased sensitivity in certain people.

The following list shows the possible side effects of Citrus limon.

  • 1. Mouth Irritation: Consuming Citrus limon can cause mouth irritation due to its acidic nature.
  • 2. Nausea: Some individuals may experience nausea after ingesting Citrus limon, possibly due to its strong flavor or acidity.
  • 3. Vomiting: In rare cases, Citrus limon can cause vomiting, especially if consumed in large amounts or by individuals who are sensitive to its acidity.
  • 4. Diarrhea: Consuming Citrus limon may lead to diarrhea in some people, potentially due to its acidic content or other compounds.
  • 5. Heartburn: The acidity of Citrus limon can cause heartburn in sensitive individuals, leading to a burning sensation in the chest or throat.
  • 6. Stomach Pain: Consumption of Citrus limon can cause stomach pain or discomfort, possibly due to its acidic nature or other compounds.
  • 7. Headache: Although rare, some people may experience headaches after consuming Citrus limon, potentially due to its strong flavor or other compounds.
  • 8. Dizziness: In some cases, Citrus limon may cause dizziness, although this side effect is not well understood and could be related to other factors.
  • 9. Excessive Thirst: Consuming Citrus limon can increase thirst due to its diuretic effect, which may increase urination and lead to dehydration.
  • 10. Excessive Urination: Citrus limon has a diuretic effect, which can increase urination and potentially lead to dehydration if not sufficiently hydrated.

What are the precautions to take when using Citrus limon (Lemon) medicinally?

It's important to take some precautions when using Citrus Limon for medicinal purposes, such as consulting a doctor before use during pregnancy, avoiding contact with eyes while handling lemons, and not consuming lemons in excessive amounts.

Discontinuing use if an allergic reaction occurs and using caution when applying lemon to skin to avoid irritation are also crucial. Furthermore, lemons should not be used as a substitute for professional medical advice, mixed with certain medications without consulting a doctor, or consumed if allergic to citrus fruits.

Additionally, individuals with gastroesophageal reflux disease, mouth sores, kidney issues, low potassium levels, or anemia should use lemons with caution, and consumption should be avoided before surgery.

The following list shows the precautions to take when using Citrus limon.

  • 1. Consult doctor before using lemon for medicinal purposes if pregnant: Seek medical advice to ensure it's safe for you and your baby.
  • 2. Avoid contact with eyes while handling lemon: This can cause irritation and discomfort, so use caution when handling.
  • 3. Don't consume lemon in excessive amounts: Overconsumption can lead to adverse effects such as tooth erosion and increased acidity.
  • 4. Discontinue use if allergic reaction occurs: If you experience symptoms like hives, swelling, or difficulty breathing, stop using it immediately.
  • 5. Use caution when applying lemon to skin to avoid irritation: Lemon contains acid that can irritate and sensitize the skin, so use it sparingly and dilute it if necessary.
  • 6. Don't use lemon as a substitute for professional medical advice: Lemon should be used as a complementary therapy, not a replacement for medical treatment.
  • 7. Avoid mixing lemon with certain medications without consulting a doctor: Lemon can interact with certain medications and cause adverse effects.
  • 8. Use caution when consuming lemon if suffering from mouth sores: Lemon can irritate mouth sores and cause discomfort.
  • 9. Don't consume lemon if allergic to citrus fruits: Allergic reactions can be severe and even life-threatening, so avoid using lemon if you're allergic to citrus.
  • 10. Use with care if suffering from gastroesophageal reflux disease: Lemon can worsen symptoms of GERD, so use it with caution and consult a doctor before use.

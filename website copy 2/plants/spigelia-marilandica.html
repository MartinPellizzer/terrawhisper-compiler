What to know before using Spigelia marilandica (Pinkroot)?

Spigelia marilandica, also known as the Maryland pinkroot or woodland pink, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the woodlands of the southeastern United States.

It is primarily recognized for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to treat a variety of ailments including heart and nerve disorders, and as a potent remedy for parasitic worms. This plant thrives in partial to full shade and requires consistently moist, well-drained soil. Spigelia marilandica can grow up to 3 feet tall and produces striking, tubular, pink flowers that bloom in the late spring and early summer.

The plant's botanical characteristics include simple, opposite leaves, and a slender, branching stem.

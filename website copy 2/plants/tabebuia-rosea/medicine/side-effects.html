10 possible side effects of Tabebuia rosea (Pau D’Arco) if used improperly

Tabebuia rosea, also known as Pau d'Arco, is a tree native to South America that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

When used improperly or in excessive amounts, Tabebuia rosea can have several negative health effects. It can cause digestive issues such as nausea, diarrhea, and vomiting.

Additionally, it can lead to increased heart rate, bleeding, and dizziness. Long-term use or high doses can result in more serious side effects, including liver damage and a decrease in white blood cells, which can weaken the immune system. Pregnant women and individuals with bleeding disorders or immune system disorders should avoid using Tabebuia rosea due to the increased risk of complications.

Always consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement regimen.

1. Nausea

Nausea caused by Tabebuia Rosea refers to the feeling of discomfort and the urge to vomit that can occur after using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in Tabebuia Rosea that can cause nausea include lapachol, beta-lapachone, and other quinone compounds. The main medicinal preparations of Tabebuia Rosea that can cause nausea if misused include decoctions, teas, capsules, and tinctures made from the bark or wood of the tree.

People who are more likely to experience nausea after using Tabebuia Rosea include those who take high doses, those with sensitive stomachs, and those who use the herb for an extended period of time. To avoid nausea, it is recommended to start with a low dose of Tabebuia Rosea and gradually increase it over time, to drink the herbal preparation with food, and to avoid using the herb for extended periods of time without taking breaks.

Additionally, consulting with a healthcare provider before using Tabebuia Rosea is always a good idea, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or those taking other medications.

2. Vomiting

Vomiting caused by Tabebuia Rosea refers to the forcing up of stomach contents through the mouth, which can occur as a side effect of this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in Tabebuia Rosea that can cause vomiting include naphthalene glycosides, benzenoids, and lapachol. This side effect is most commonly associated with the bark and root of the plant, which are used to make teas, tinctures, and capsules. Individuals who are more likely to experience vomiting as a side effect of Tabebuia Rosea include those who take high doses, those with sensitive stomachs, and those who consume the supplement on an empty stomach.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase, consume the supplement with food, and follow the recommended dosage on the product label.

4. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Tabebuia Rosea refers to a sudden sensation of lightheadedness or unsteadiness that can occur after using this herbal remedy.

The main active constituents in Tabebuia Rosea that have been linked to this side effect include naphthoquinones such as lapachol and beta-lapachone. These compounds can have psychoactive effects that may lead to dizziness, particularly when consumed in large quantities or when used in concentrated forms such as tinctures or essential oils.

People who are more likely to experience dizziness from Tabebuia Rosea include those who are sensitive to its psychoactive effects, have a history of motion sickness or inner ear problems, or who consume large quantities of the herb. To avoid this side effect, it is important to start with a low dose of Tabebuia Rosea and gradually increase over time, as well as to consume the herb with food to slow down its absorption.

Additionally, avoiding the use of Tabebuia Rosea in combination with other psychoactive substances, such as alcohol or sedatives, may help reduce the risk of dizziness.

5. Headache

Headache caused by Tabebuia Rosea refers to a type of discomfort or pain in the head that can occur as a side effect of using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents of Tabebuia Rosea that have been identified as potentially causing headaches are lapachol and beta-lapachone. These compounds can cause irritation and inflammation in the brain, leading to headaches.

Medicinal preparations such as tea, tinctures, and capsules can all cause headaches if misused or used in large quantities. People who are more likely to experience headaches from Tabebuia Rosea use include those who are sensitive to the active compounds, those who take large quantities of the supplement, and those who use it for extended periods of time. To avoid headaches and other side effects, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare professional before starting to use this supplement.

Additionally, people with a history of migraines or other headache disorders should exercise caution when using Tabebuia Rosea.

6. Anorexia

Anorexia caused by Tabebuia Rosea refers to a decreased appetite, which is one of the potential side effects of this herb.

The main active constituents of Tabebuia rosea that can cause this side effect are naptha-[2-anthraquinonic], anthraquinones, and flavonoids. The preparation of Tabebuia rosea most likely to cause this side effect if misused includes its bark or wood in the form of decoctions, infusions, or teas.

Individuals who are more likely to experience anorexia from Tabebuia rosea include those who consume large amounts of the herb for extended periods or those who have a higher sensitivity to its active compounds. To avoid this side effect, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage and duration of use, and consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement regimen.

Pregnant or breastfeeding women, children, and individuals with pre-existing medical conditions should use Tabebuia rosea with caution, if at all.

7. Fatigue

Fatigue caused by Tabebuia Rosea refers to a state of extreme tiredness and lack of energy that may occur as a side effect of using this herbal remedy.

This fatigue can be attributed to the plant’s active constituents, particularly lapachol and beta-lapachone, which are believed to have various pharmacological properties, including anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial effects. Medicinal preparations of Tabebuia rosea, such as decoctions, teas, and capsules, if misused or taken in large quantities, may increase the risk of developing fatigue. Individuals who are more sensitive to the effects of this plant, such as those with weakened immune systems, pregnant or breastfeeding women, and people taking other medications, are more likely to experience fatigue as a side effect of using Tabebuia rosea.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to use Tabebuia rosea in moderation, follow the recommended dosage and preparation instructions, and consult a healthcare professional before using this herbal remedy, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking medications.

8. Allergic Reactions

Allergic reactions caused by Tabebuia Rosea refer to abnormal immune system responses that occur after ingesting or coming into contact with this herb.

The main active constituents that can cause these reactions are quinoids, such as lapachol and beta-lapachone, which are found in the bark and wood of the tree. Medicinal preparations such as decoctions, teas, and capsules can cause allergic reactions if misused or used in large quantities.

Individuals who are allergic to other members of the Bignoniaceae family, have a history of allergic reactions to related plants, or those with a compromised immune system may be more prone to experiencing allergic reactions from Tabebuia rosea. To avoid allergic reactions, it is advisable to start with small doses of Tabebuia rosea and gradually increase the dosage to allow the body to adjust.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using Tabebuia rosea if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or concerns.

9. Internal Bleeding

Internal bleeding caused by Tabebuia Rosea refers to the harmful effect of this herbal remedy that results in bleeding in the internal organs.

This can occur when the bark or wood of the tree is prepared as a decoction or tea and consumed. The main active constituents that can cause this side effect are napthaquinones, such as lapachol and beta-lapachone, which have been found to possess anticoagulant properties.

This side effect is most likely to be experienced by individuals who are taking other medications that also slow blood clotting, such as aspirin or warfarin, or those with bleeding disorders. To avoid this side effect, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using Tabebuia rosea and to follow the recommended dosage carefully.

Additionally, those who are using Tabebuia rosea should be aware of the signs of internal bleeding, such as prolonged bleeding from minor cuts, dark stools, or blood in urine, and seek medical attention immediately if any of these symptoms occur.

10. Anemia

Anemia caused by Tabebuia Rosea refers to a condition where the blood contains a lower than normal number of red blood cells or lacks sufficient hemoglobin, a protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen to the body's tissues.

The main active constituents in Tabebuia Rosea that can cause this side effect are lapachol and naphthoquinones, which may interfere with the production of red blood cells in the body. The main medicinal preparations of Tabebuia Rosea that can cause anemia if misused include tea, capsules, and tinctures. Individuals who are more likely to experience anemia from Tabebuia Rosea are those who consume large amounts of the herb over an extended period, have a history of anemia or other blood disorders, or are taking medications that may interact with the herb.

To avoid anemia, it is crucial to follow recommended dosages, consult with a healthcare provider before using Tabebuia Rosea, and monitor for symptoms of anemia, such as fatigue, weakness, and shortness of breath.

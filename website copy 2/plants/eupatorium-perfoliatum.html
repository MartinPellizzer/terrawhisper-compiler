What to know before using Eupatorium perfoliatum (Boneset)?

Eupatorium perfoliatum, also known as boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America and commonly found in wetlands.

Medicinally, it has been used to treat various ailments such as fever, flu, and joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory and fever-reducing properties. It contains various chemical compounds including polyphenols and sesquiterpene lactones, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal benefits. In terms of horticultural conditions, boneset prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can grow up to 4-7 feet tall.

Botanically, it is characterized by its opposite, serrated leaves that are joined at the base, giving the appearance of a single leaf wrapping around the stem, and small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in the late summer to early fall.

What are the medicinal uses of Eupatorium perfoliatum?

Eupatorium perfoliatum has several medicinal uses, such as alleviating symptoms of the flu, colds, and fever.

Its constituents include various chemical compounds such as polyphenols, sesquiterpene lactones, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Preparations of boneset often include dried leaves and flowers made into teas, tinctures, or capsules. However, side effects of boneset may include stomach irritation, nausea, and vomiting if taken in large doses or over a long period.

Precautions should be taken when using boneset, as it can interact with other medications and is not recommended for pregnant or breastfeeding women, children, or individuals with kidney or liver disease.

Harvesting Eupatorium perfoliatum can be done throughout the growing season.

Cut the stems at the base of the plant and remove the leaves. Bundle the stems together and hang them upside down in a cool, dark place to dry.

Once dry, the leaves and stems can be crushed and stored in an airtight container for later use.

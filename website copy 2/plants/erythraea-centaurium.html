What to know before using Erythraea centaurium (Centaury)?

Erythraea centaurium, also known as common centaury or feverwort, is a small herbaceous plant with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and pinkish-purple flowers, often tinged with white.

It is native to Europe and western Asia, but has been naturalized in other parts of the world. This plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine, particularly for its digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

It is believed to stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, ease symptoms of indigestion, and reduce inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract. Erythraea centaurium prefers well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and full sun, although it can tolerate partial shade.

It is a hardy plant that can thrive in a variety of conditions, but it is particularly suited to rock gardens, alpine gardens, and other well-drained sites.

What to know before using Galium palustre (Common Marsh Bedstraw)?

Galium palustre, also known as marsh bedstraw, is a plant that has been used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.

It contains several active compounds, including iridoid glycosides and flavonoids, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and diuretic effects. In horticulture, Galium palustre thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and prefers partial shade to full sun. It is a slender, trailing plant that grows to a height of 30-60 cm, with small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom from June to August.

The stems and leaves of the plant are covered in stiff, backward-pointing hairs, giving it a rough texture.

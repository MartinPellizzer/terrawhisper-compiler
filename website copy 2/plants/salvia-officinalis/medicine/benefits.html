10 Best Health Benefits of Salvia officinalis (Sage)

Salvia officinalis, also known as garden sage, is a plant that has been used for centuries for its medicinal properties.

It is rich in antioxidants, including rosmarinic acid and carnosic acid, which help protect the body from damage caused by free radicals. Salvia officinalis has anti-inflammatory properties, making it useful in treating conditions such as sore throats, bronchitis, and asthma.

Additionally, it has been shown to have antibacterial and antifungal properties, which can help to prevent infections. Furthermore, it has been suggested that Salvia officinalis may help to improve cognitive function and memory, and it has been used in the treatment of Alzheimer's disease.

However, it is important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the potential medicinal benefits of Salvia officinalis and its active compounds.

1. Alleviates inflammation

Sage, also known as Salvia officinalis, alleviates inflammation, meaning it can help reduce redness, swelling, and pain in the body caused by irritation or injury.

The main active constituents in sage that contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties include rosmarinic acid, carnosic acid, and various flavonoids. The primary parts of the Salvia officinalis plant that possess this benefit are the leaves and stems, which contain high concentrations of these active compounds. The most common medicinal preparations of sage for alleviating inflammation include dried leaves for tea, essential oil for topical use, and tinctures or capsules for internal use.

The main health conditions that this benefit of Salvia officinalis helps relieve include respiratory inflammation, skin irritations, and digestive issues such as gastritis and ulcerative colitis.

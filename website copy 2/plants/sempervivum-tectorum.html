What to know before using Sempervivum tectorum (Houseleek)?

Sempervivum tectorum, also known as hens and chicks, is a type of succulent plant that has bright green, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette shape.

It is well-known for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to help heal wounds and sores. The plant contains high levels of antioxidants and has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Sempervivum tectorum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is drought tolerant and can thrive in a variety of environments, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and green roofs.

The plant is also easy to propagate, as it produces offsets, or "chicks," that can be easily transplanted.

What are the horticultural conditions of Sempervivum tectorum?

Sempervivum tectorum needs full sun to thrive, and well-draining soil with a pH between 6.0 and 7.5.

When planting, ensure there is enough space between each rosette for air to circulate, and provide a shallow planting hole. For care, water sparingly, allowing the soil to dry out between waterings.

Avoid overhead watering to prevent rot. When it comes to harvesting, Sempervivum tectorum can be propagated by carefully removing offsets and planting them in well-draining soil.

Pests and diseases are generally not a significant issue, but mealybugs and rot can occur, so it's essential to monitor the plants regularly.

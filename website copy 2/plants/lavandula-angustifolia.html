Botanically, Lavandula angustifolia is a small shrub that is part of the mint family, with narrow leaves and purple flowers that bloom in summer.

What are the medicinal uses of Lavandula angustifolia?

What are the possible side effects of Lavandula angustifolia?

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as lavender, can have some side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation or allergic reactions in some individuals.

Ingesting lavender oil can lead to digestive issues like nausea, vomiting, and upset stomach. Additionally, it can interact with certain medications, such as sedatives and antidepressants, leading to increased drowsiness and other unintended effects. In some cases, lavender has been known to lower a person's blood pressure, which can be problematic for those already suffering from hypotension.

Lastly, using lavender during pregnancy may stimulate uterine contractions, which could potentially result in miscarriage or premature labor.

Harvesting Lavandula angustifolia is best done in the morning, after the dew has evaporated but before the heat of the day.

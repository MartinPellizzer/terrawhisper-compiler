What to know before using Erigeron canadensis (Horseweed)?

Erigeron canadensis, also known as Canadian fleabane or Canadian mountain fleabane, is a plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

It contains sesquiterpene lactones, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, making it useful for treating conditions such as arthritis and muscle pain. Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine as a remedy for fevers, coughs, and diarrhea.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Erigeron canadensis is a hardy plant that can thrive in a variety of environments, from full sun to partial shade, and in a range of soil types. It is also drought-tolerant, making it a low-maintenance option for gardens. Botanically, Erigeron canadensis is a member of the daisy family and produces small, daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and yellow disc florets.

The plant can grow up to several feet tall and has a spreading habit, making it a good choice for groundcover.

What to know before using Jacobaea vulgaris (Jacobaea)?

Jacobaea vulgaris, also known as ragwort, is a flowering plant that boasts bright yellow blooms and attracts numerous pollinators.

Despite its cheery appearance, ragwort contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be toxic to livestock if ingested in large quantities. However, when used in moderation and with proper medical supervision, extracts from ragwort have been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Ragwort thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in a variety of habitats, from grasslands to roadside banks. Interestingly, this hardy plant is able to survive even in nutrient-poor soils, making it a hearty addition to any garden.

However, due to its invasive nature and potential to harm livestock, gardeners should exercise caution when considering the addition of Jacobaea vulgaris to their landscapes.

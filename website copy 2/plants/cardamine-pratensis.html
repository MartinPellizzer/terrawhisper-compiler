What to know before using Cardamine pratensis (Meadow Cress)?

Cardamine pratensis, also known as lady's smock or cuckoo flower, is a flowering plant native to moist grasslands of Europe and Asia.

Its purple-pink flowers, which bloom in spring, have been used in traditional medicine for their diuretic, expectorant, and antiscorbutic properties. This plant prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, and can grow up to 2 feet tall.

The leaves of Cardamine pratensis are alternate, compound, and have a mild, peppery flavor, while its flowers are small and four-petaled, growing in clusters on upright stems.

What to know before using Uncaria tomentosa (Cat'S Claw)?

Uncaria tomentosa, also known as cat's claw, is a woody vine native to the Amazon rainforest and other tropical regions of South and Central America.

It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat a variety of health conditions, including inflammation, cancer, and viral infections. The plant is characterized by its distinctive hooked thorns and woody stem, and can grow up to 100 feet in height. Uncaria tomentosa prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and can be propagated through cuttings or seeds.

Its medicinal properties are attributed to a number of active compounds, including alkaloids, glycosides, and tannins, which are believed to have anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and immunostimulant effects.

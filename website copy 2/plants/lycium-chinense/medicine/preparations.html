Lycium chinense, also known as Chinese boxthorn or wolfberry, has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries due to its various medicinal properties.

One popular preparation is a tisane, or herbal tea, made from the berries or leaves of the plant. This soothing drink is believed to improve eye health, boost the immune system, and even slow the aging process.

A tincture can also be made from the plant, which involves soaking the berries or leaves in alcohol to extract their active compounds. This concentrated extract can be taken orally to treat ailments such as coughs, sore throats, and stomach issues. Additionally, Lycium chinense has been used topically in the form of a salve or ointment to heal wounds and reduce inflammation.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using Lycium chinense for medicinal purposes, as it can interact with certain medications and may have side effects.

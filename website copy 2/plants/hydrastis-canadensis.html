What to know before using Hydrastis canadensis (Goldenseal)?

Hydrastis canadensis, also known as goldenseal, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It has a long history of use in Native American medicine and is now cultivated for its medicinal properties. The plant has distinctive knotty yellow roots, which contain isoquinoline alkaloids, giving it medicinal properties such as being anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and astringent. Hydrastis canadensis thrives in rich, well-drained soil in partial shade and can be propagated through root division.

It is a hardy plant that can grow in USDA zones 4-8, but overharvesting has led to its inclusion on the United Plant Savers "at-risk" list.

What are the medicinal uses of Hydrastis canadensis?

Hydrastis canadensis has several medicinal uses, such as treating skin disorders, diarrhea, and gastric upsets.

However, Hydrastis canadensis can cause side effects such as digestive disturbances, mouth dryness, and nosebleeds, especially when taken in large doses or for extended periods.

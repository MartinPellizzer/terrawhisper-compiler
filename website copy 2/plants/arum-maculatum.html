What to know before using Arum maculatum (Adam-And-Eve)?

Arum maculatum, also known as Lady's Purse or Cuckoo Pint, is a perennial plant native to Europe's woodlands.

Its tuberous roots have been used in traditional medicine for various ailments, including skin conditions, warts, and rheumatism, due to their anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. These roots contain calcium oxalate crystals, which can irritate the skin and mucous membranes, so they must be processed before use. Arum maculatum prefers partial to full shade and rich, moist soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens or shaded areas.

The plant grows up to 45 cm tall, with large, arrow-shaped leaves and calla lily-like flowers that bloom in early spring and are followed by bright red berries in the fall.

By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Sambucus nigra, also known as black elderberry, has a long history of use in medicinal preparations.

One popular preparation is a tisane, or herbal tea, made from the flowers or berries of the plant. This soothing tea is often used to treat symptoms of colds and flu, as it is believed to have antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties.

Another common preparation is a tincture, which is made by soaking the berries or flowers in alcohol to extract their active compounds. Tinctures can be taken orally and are sometimes used as a preventative measure against illness. Both tisanes and tinctures have been used in traditional medicine to boost the immune system, reduce fevers, and alleviate respiratory symptoms.

However, it is important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the medicinal benefits and potential risks of Sambucus nigra preparations.

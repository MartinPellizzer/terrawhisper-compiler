What to know before using Arenaria serpyllifolia (Thyme-Leaf Sandwort)?

Arenaria serpyllifolia, also known as sandwort, is a low-growing plant found in sandy and gravelly soils of Europe and Asia.

Its tiny, thick leaves form mats that are often used as a groundcover in horticulture, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. In traditional medicine, sandwort has been used as a diuretic and to treat various ailments including digestive issues and skin conditions.

Botanically, it is characterized by its tiny white or pink flowers, which bloom in the summer, and its creeping, mat-forming habit.

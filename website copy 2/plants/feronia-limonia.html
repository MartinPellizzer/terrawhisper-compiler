What to know before using Feronia limonia (Wood Apple)?

Feronia limonia, also known as the Wood Apple or Elephant Apple, is a deciduous tree that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Its fruit and leaves have been found to possess medicinal properties, including anti-diabetic, anti-microbial, and anti-inflammatory effects, making it a valuable resource in various pharmaceutical applications. In terms of horticultural conditions, Feronia limonia thrives in tropical or subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight for optimal growth. As for its botanical characteristics, Feronia limonia is distinguished by its knobby, green to yellow fruit, which contains a thick, white pulp and a high number of seeds.

The tree itself can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a spreading canopy of compound leaves that add to its distinctive, tropical appearance.

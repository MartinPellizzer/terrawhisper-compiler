What to know before using Moehringia trinervia (Apetalous Sandwort)?

Moehringia trinervia, also known as triangular-leaved moehringia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It has several medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects, and has been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments such as skin disorders and kidney diseases. This plant thrives in well-drained, moist soil and partial shade, and is often found in woodlands, grasslands, and rocky areas. Moehringia trinervia has small, delicate, bright green leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs, and produces white, star-shaped flowers in the summer.

It is an attractive groundcover that can add beauty to any garden while also providing medicinal benefits.

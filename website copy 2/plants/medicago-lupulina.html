What to know before using Medicago lupulina (Nonesuch)?

Medicago lupulina, also known as black medick, is a legume that is native to Europe and Asia but has since naturalized in many parts of the world.

It is known for its small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer and form distinctive coiled seed pods. This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas. Medicinally, Medicago lupulina has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including respiratory conditions, digestive issues, and skin problems.

Its leaves and flowers are rich in tannins, saponins, and flavonoids, which are believed to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties.

What to know before using Clerodendrum trichotomum (Chou Wu Tong)?

Clerodendrum trichotomum, also known as the glory tree or bagflower, is a versatile plant with a range of medicinal properties, horticultural applications, and distinctive botanical characteristics.

In traditional medicine, various parts of the plant have been used to treat ailments such as fever, diarrhea, and hypertension, thanks to its anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and antimicrobial properties. As a horticultural specimen, Clerodendrum trichotomum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, producing fragrant flowers and glossy, green leaves that contrast beautifully with its eventual height and spread of 15-20 feet.

Botanically, the plant is known for its unique inflorescence, which features a showy, white, star-shaped corolla that surrounds a striking, deep-blue or purple, lobed calyx, giving it a captivating, two-toned appearance.

What are the medicinal uses of Clerodendrum trichotomum?

Clerodendrum trichotomum has several medicinal uses, such as treating fevers, relieving pain, and reducing inflammation.

Its constituents include various compounds such as trichotomine, diterpenoids, and flavonoids, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic properties. The plant can be prepared in various ways, including decoctions, tinctures, and powdered extracts, and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

However, Clerodendrum trichotomum can cause side effects such as stomach upset, nausea, and dizziness if taken in large doses or over a long period. Additionally, pregnant women and individuals with bleeding disorders should avoid using this plant, as it may have uterine stimulant and anticoagulant effects.

Therefore, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using Clerodendrum trichotomum for medicinal purposes.

10 Best Active Constituents of Tanacetum parthenium (Feverfew)

Tanacetum parthenium, also known as feverfew, is a medicinal herb that contains various active constituents with potential therapeutic benefits.

The most well-known active compound in feverfew is parthenolide, which is believed to be responsible for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and anti-fever effects. Other active constituents in feverfew include tanetin, camphor, and sesquiterpene lactones, which also contribute to its medicinal properties. Feverfew has been traditionally used to treat a variety of conditions, including migraines, arthritis, and menstrual pain.

However, it is important to note that the efficacy of feverfew for these conditions is still a subject of ongoing research, and its use should be under the guidance of a healthcare professional due to potential side effects and interactions with other medications.

What to know before using Geranium dissectum (Cut-Leaf Crane'S-Bill)?

Geranium dissectum, also known as Cut-Leaf Cranesbill, is a low-growing perennial herb with several medicinal properties.

It contains tannins and essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine to treat wounds, skin irritations, and diarrhea. This plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can thrive in rock gardens or as a groundcover in gardens. Geranium dissectum is characterized by its finely cut, lobed leaves and pinkish-purple flowers, which bloom in the summer and attract bees and butterflies.

It is a hardy plant that can tolerate moderate frost and dry conditions once established.

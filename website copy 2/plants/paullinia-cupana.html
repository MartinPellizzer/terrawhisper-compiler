What to know before using Paullinia cupana (Guarana)?

Paullinia cupana, commonly known as guarana, is a plant native to the Amazon rainforest that possesses significant medicinal properties.

Its seeds contain a high concentration of caffeine, which has been shown to stimulate the nervous system, improve mental alertness, and enhance physical endurance. Guarana plants thrive in well-drained, acidic soil and require a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Botanically, Paullinia cupana is characterized by its climbing habit, bright green, compound leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruit containing the sought-after seeds.

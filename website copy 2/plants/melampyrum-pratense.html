What to know before using Melampyrum pratense (Common Cow-Wheat)?

Melampyrum pratense, also known as common cow wheat, is a semi-parasitic plant native to Europe and Asia.

It has distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer and grow in loose spikes. This plant typically thrives in nutrient-poor, grassy meadows and woods, and prefers partial shade. Medicinally, Melampyrum pratense has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic properties.

Its leaves and flowering tops are often used to make teas and tinctures to treat ailments such as rheumatism, fever, and neuralgia, although more scientific research is needed to confirm its efficacy and safety.

What to know before using Rhodiola rosea (Rhodiola)?

Rhodiola rosea, also known as roseroot, is a perennial plant that thrives in cold, mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

This adaptogenic herb has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to increase stamina, reduce fatigue, and enhance brain function. Rhodiola rosea prefers well-drained soil and full sun or partial shade. Its fleshy roots have a bitter taste and are rich in antioxidants, phenolic compounds, and unique rosavins, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant's above-ground parts feature yellow, pink, or red flowers, which bloom in the summer and contribute to its ornamental value.

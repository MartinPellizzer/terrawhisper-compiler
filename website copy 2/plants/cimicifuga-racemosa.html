What to know before using Cimicifuga racemosa (Black Cohosh)?

Cimicifuga racemosa, also known as black cohosh, is a plant native to North America that has been used for medicinal purposes by indigenous peoples for centuries.

Its roots are particularly sought after for their medicinal properties, which include treating symptoms of menopause such as hot flashes, night sweats, and mood disturbances. In horticultural settings, Cimicifuga racemosa thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, and can grow up to 8 feet tall with compound, serrated leaves.

Botanically, Cimicifuga racemosa is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and produces long, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer, ranging in color from white to pale pink.

What to know before using Acer pseudoplatanus (Sycamore Maple)?

Acer pseudoplatanus, also known as the sycamore maple, is a tree with a variety of medicinal properties, horticultural uses, and distinct botanical characteristics.

Medicinally, the sycamore maple has been used in herbal medicine to treat ailments such as skin conditions, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. It contains compounds such as tannins, gallic acid, and quercetin, which have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. In horticultural settings, Acer pseudoplatanus is a hardy tree that can tolerate a wide range of soil conditions and moderate temperatures.

It grows best in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. The tree can reach heights of up to 25-30 meters and has a broad, rounded crown with a sturdy, upright trunk. Botanically, the sycamore maple is characterized by its opposite leaves, which are palmately lobed with 5-7 lobes, and its winged fruits, known as samaras.

The bark of the tree is dark gray and furrowed, and its twigs are stout with opposite buds.

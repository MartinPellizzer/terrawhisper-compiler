What to know before using Betula pubescens (White Birch)?

Betula pubescens, also known as the European white birch, is a species of tree that is native to northern and central Europe.

It has a variety of medicinal properties: the bark can be used to make teas and decoctions that act as a diuretic and have anti-inflammatory effects, while the sap has been used in the treatment of skin conditions and wounds. In terms of horticultural conditions, Betula pubescens prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, although it can tolerate a wide range of conditions. Botanically, Betula pubescens is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a slender, upright form and peeling, white bark.

Its leaves are dark green and ovate, with serrated edges and a pointed tip.

What are the medicinal uses of Betula pubescens?

Betula pubescens is used to treat several medical conditions due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and diuretic properties.

Its active constituents include betulinic acid, betulin, and triterpenes, which have been shown to provide various health benefits such as reducing pain and inflammation, boosting the immune system, and improving skin conditions. Medicinal preparations of Betula pubescens include bark or leaf decoctions, tinctures, and ointments.

However, it is essential to note that Betula pubescens may cause side effects such as stomach upset, skin rashes, and allergic reactions in some individuals. Therefore, it should be used with caution, especially in people with known allergies to birch pollen or other members of the Betulaceae family.

Additionally, pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid using Betula pubescens, as its safety has not been established in these populations.

Betulinic acid has antiviral, anti-inflammatory, and antitumor properties. Betulin has antimicrobial, antitumor, and cholesterol-lowering effects. Methyl salicylate is a compound similar to aspirin and has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Medicinal preparations of Betula pubescens include teas, tinctures, creams, and ointments.

What are the possible side effects of Betula pubescens?

Despite its medicinal benefits, Betula pubescens can have possible side effects.

The bark and leaves contain coumarins, which can thin the blood and interact with blood-thinning medications. Overconsumption of white birch tea can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Prolonged use of white birch extract can lead to skin irritation and liver damage.

It is essential to consult a healthcare provider before using white birch for medicinal purposes, especially for children, pregnant women, and people with medical conditions.

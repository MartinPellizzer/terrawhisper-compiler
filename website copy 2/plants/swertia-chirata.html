What to know before using Swertia chirata (Chiretta)?

Swertia chirata, also known as Chirayita, is a valuable medicinal plant native to the Himalayan region.

It has a wide range of therapeutic properties, including anti-malarial, anti-inflammatory, and anti-pyretic properties, making it a popular traditional remedy for various ailments such as fever, liver disorders, and diabetes. Swertia chirata thrives in cool, moist, and shaded horticultural conditions, typically found at altitudes between 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant is a perennial herb that can grow up to 1 meter tall, with simple, oblong leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its stems are woody at the base, and its roots are fibrous, which are often used in herbal medicine.

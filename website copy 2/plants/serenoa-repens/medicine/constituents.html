10 Best Active Constituents of Serenoa repens (Saw Palmetto)

Serenoa repens, also known as Saw Palmetto, is a plant commonly used in medicinal purposes for the treatment of various health conditions.

The active constituents of Serenoa repens include fatty acids, sterols, and flavonoids, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties. Among these, fatty acids such as lauric, myristic, and oleic acids are the most abundant and have been studied for their anti-inflammatory and hormonal effects.

Sterols, including beta-sitosterol, stigmasterol, and campesterol, have been shown to inhibit the conversion of testosterone to dihydrotestosterone, a hormone associated with prostate enlargement. Flavonoids, such as quercetin and rutin, have antioxidant properties that may contribute to the overall health benefits of Serenoa repens.

Overall, the active constituents of Serenoa repens work synergistically to provide medicinal benefits, particularly in the treatment of prostate-related conditions.

1. Beta-sitosterol

The beta-sitosterol contained in Serenoa Repens is a type of plant sterol or phytosterol that has a similar structure to cholesterol.

Its main medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immune-modulating effects. This Serenoa repens constituent has been shown to help relieve symptoms associated with benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), including reducing inflammation, improving urinary flow, and alleviating urinary frequency and nocturia. The main parts of the Serenoa repens plant that contain this constituent are the berries, which are rich in beta-sitosterol and other phytosterols.

Some possible side effects of Serenoa repens beta-sitosterol if used improperly include gastrointestinal symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, as well as headache, dizziness, and allergic reactions.

2. Lupeol

The lupeol contained in Serenoa Repens is a triterpene that has been found to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticancer properties.

Its main medicinal properties include inhibiting the activity of 5-alpha-reductase, an enzyme that converts testosterone to dihydrotestosterone, which plays a role in the development of benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH). Additionally, lupeol has been shown to have anti-tandem-repeat binding protein 1 (TRBP1) activity, which may contribute to its anticancer effects.

This Serenoa repens constituent has been found to help relieve symptoms of BPH such as urinary frequency, nocturia, and weak urine stream. Lupeol is found mainly in the berries of the Serenoa repens plant.

Some possible side effects of lupeol if used improperly include stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea.

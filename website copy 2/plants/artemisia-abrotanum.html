What to know before using Artemisia abrotanum (Southernwood)?

Artemisia abrotanum, also known as southernwood, is a shrub that belongs to the daisy family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It has been used in herbal medicine for its various health benefits, including its ability to alleviate digestive issues, reduce fevers, and act as an antiseptic. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it can grow up to 6 feet tall.

Artemisia abrotanum has fern-like, silvery-green leaves that emit a strong, distinct odor when crushed, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the summer.

What are the medicinal uses of Artemisia abrotanum?

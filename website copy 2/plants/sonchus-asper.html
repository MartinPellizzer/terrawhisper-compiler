What to know before using Sonchus asper (Rough Milk Thistle)?

Sonchus asper, also known as prickly sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the daisy family that has both medicinal and horticultural significance.

Medicinally, Sonchus asper is known for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, and its extracts have been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including skin conditions and digestive issues. In terms of horticultural conditions, Sonchus asper thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, but can also tolerate partial shade. It is a hardy plant that can grow in a variety of environments, from disturbed urban areas to pastures and fields.

Botanically, Sonchus asper is characterized by its prickly leaves, yellow flowers, and hollow stems, and it can grow up to several feet tall.

What to know before using Paris quadrifolia (Herb-Paris)?

Paris quadrifolia, also known as Herb Paris or True Lovers' Knot, is a perennial plant native to Europe's woodlands and is known for its medicinal properties.

This plant contains alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments such as heart conditions, digestive issues, and nervous disorders. Paris quadrifolia thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it an ideal candidate for woodland gardens. The plant's botanical characteristics include a single, unbranched stem that terminates in a whorl of four leaves, as well as a single, bell-shaped, white or pale pink flower that blooms in the late spring or early summer.

Despite its medicinal uses, all parts of the plant are considered poisonous and should be handled with care.

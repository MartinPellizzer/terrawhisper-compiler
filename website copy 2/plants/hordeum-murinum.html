What to know before using Hordeum murinum (False Barley)?

Hordeum murinum, also known as wall barley, is a species of grass that has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

Its seeds are rich in nutrients and have been used to treat various health conditions, including digestive issues and skin irritations. In horticulture, Hordeum murinum is considered a weed and can thrive in a variety of conditions, making it a common sight in disturbed soils and urban environments. From a botanical standpoint, Hordeum murinum is an annual grass that can grow up to 80 cm tall and has distinct spikelets arranged in pairs along the stem.

Each spikelet contains several florets, which are the reproductive structures of the plant.

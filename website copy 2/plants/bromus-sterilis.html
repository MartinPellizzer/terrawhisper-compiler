What to know before using Bromus sterilis (Barren Brome)?

Bromus sterilis, also known as sterile brome, is a grass species native to Europe and western Asia but now naturalized in many parts of the world.

Medicinally, it has been used in herbal medicine for its diuretic and expectorant properties, and to treat respiratory ailments. In terms of horticultural conditions, Bromus sterilis prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and can tolerate a wide range of soil types and pH levels.

Botanically, it is a tufted perennial grass that grows to 3-6 feet tall, with flat, narrow leaves and loose panicles of spikelets that bloom in late spring.

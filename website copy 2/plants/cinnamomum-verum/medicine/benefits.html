10 Best Health Benefits of Cinnamomum verum (Cinnamon)

Cinnamomum verum, also known as true cinnamon, has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits.

One of the key medicinal properties of cinnamon is its ability to lower blood sugar levels, making it a valuable tool in the management of diabetes. Cinnamon contains compounds that mimic the effects of insulin in the body, helping to regulate blood sugar levels and improve glucose uptake in cells.

Additionally, cinnamon has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease and cancer. Studies have also shown that cinnamon can help reduce cholesterol levels, improve brain function, and even alleviate symptoms of respiratory disorders such as asthma.

Overall, cinnamon is a powerful medicinal spice with a wide range of health benefits, making it a valuable addition to any wellness routine.

1. Reduces inflammation

Cinnamon reduces inflammation, meaning it can help alleviate redness, swelling, and pain in the body caused by inflammatory conditions.

The main active constituents in cinnamon that contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties are cinnamaldehyde, cinnamic acid, and flavonoids. The primary parts of the cinnamon plant that possess this benefit are the bark and essential oil. The main medicinal preparations of cinnamon that provide this benefit include ground cinnamon powder, cinnamon essential oil, and cinnamon bark extract.

The main health conditions that this benefit of cinnamon helps relieve include arthritis, muscle pain, and inflammatory bowel disease.

Cinnamomum verum reduces inflammation thanks to the active constituents listed below.

  • Cinnamaldehyde
  • Eugenol
  • Linalool
  • Cinnamyl acetate
  • Beta-caryophyllene
  • Coumarin
  • Epicatechin
  • Kaempferol
  • Quercetin
  • Rutin

2. Lowers blood sugar levels

Cinnamon lowers blood sugar levels, meaning it can help reduce fasting blood sugar levels and improve hemoglobin A1c, a test that measures average blood sugar levels over three months.

The main active constituents of Cinnamomum verum responsible for this benefit are methylhydroxychalcone polymer (MHCP) and cinnamaldehyde. The primary parts of the Cinnamomum verum plant that give this benefit are the bark and essential oil. The main medicinal preparations of Cinnamomum verum that give this benefit include ground cinnamon powder, cinnamon essential oil, and cinnamon supplements.

This benefit of Cinnamomum verum helps relieve health conditions such as type 2 diabetes, insulin resistance, and metabolic syndrome.

Cinnamomum verum lowers blood sugar levels thanks to the active constituents listed below.

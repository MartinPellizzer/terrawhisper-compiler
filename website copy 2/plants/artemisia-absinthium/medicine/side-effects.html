10 possible side effects of Artemisia absinthium (Wormwood) if used improperly

Artemisia absinthium, also known as wormwood, is a plant that has been used in traditional medicine but can cause serious health side effects if used improperly.

Ingesting wormwood in large amounts can lead to poisoning, resulting in symptoms such as seizures, vomiting, kidney damage, and abnormal heart rate. Prolonged use or high doses of wormwood can also cause serious psychological side effects, including delirium, restlessness, and hallucinations.

Additionally, wormwood contains a toxin called thujone, which can cause miscarriage or other reproductive issues, so it should be avoided during pregnancy. In extreme cases, excessive consumption of wormwood has been linked to death.

Therefore, it is crucial to use this herb with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional, as improper use can lead to severe health consequences.

1. Nausea

Nausea caused by Artemisia Absinthium refers to the feeling of discomfort and the urge to vomit that can occur as a side effect of this herb.

The main active constituents in Artemisia Absinthium that can cause nausea include absinthin, anabsinthin, and various essential oils. This symptom is most commonly associated with medicinal preparations such as wormwood tea, tinctures, and extracts, especially when taken in large amounts or with insufficient dilution.

Individuals who are pregnant, have a history of digestive issues, or are sensitive to the compounds in wormwood are more likely to experience nausea as a side effect. To avoid nurturing this symptom, it's important to use Artemisia Absinthium in moderation, start with a low dose, and gradually increase as needed.

Additionally, it's recommended to consume wormwood with food to help buffer its effects and to avoid taking it on an empty stomach.

2. Vomiting

Vomiting caused by Artemisia absinthium refers to the forceful expulsion of stomach contents through the mouth as a result of using this herb.

This side effect can be attributed mainly to the presence of absinthin, a bitter compound, and thujone, an essential oil, which can stimulate the vomiting reflex if consumed in large amounts. Preparations such as wormwood tea or tincture, and especially absinthe, a potent alcoholic beverage, are most likely to cause vomiting if misused or taken in excessive doses. Individuals who are sensitive to the effects of bitter compounds or have a history of digestive sensitivity are more likely to experience vomiting after using Artemisia absinthium.

To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to follow dosage recommendations carefully, avoid consuming alcohol while using wormwood, and consult with a healthcare practitioner before using Artemisia absinthium, particularly for individuals with a history of stomach disorders or those who are pregnant or breastfeeding.

3. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Artemisia Absinthium refers to a feeling of lightheadedness, unsteadiness, or vertigo that may occur after using products containing this herb.

The main active constituents of Artemisia absinthium that can cause this side effect are alpha- and beta-thujone, which are neurotoxic and can affect the central nervous system when consumed in large amounts. The main medicinal preparations of Artemisia absinthium that can cause this side effect if misused include essential oils, dried leaves, and extracts, which are often used in traditional medicine for their antiparasitic, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory properties. People who are more likely to experience dizziness after using Artemisia absinthium are those with a history of sensitivity to thujone, seizures, or neurological disorders.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and usage guidelines, avoid using products with high concentrations of thujone, and consult with a healthcare professional before using Artemisia absinthium, especially if you have a history of neurological conditions or are taking other medications.

4. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Artemisia Absinthium refers to a gastrointestinal disturbance characterized by frequent and watery bowel movements.

This side effect can be attributed to the presence of active constituents in Artemisia Absinthium, such as thujone and absinthin. These compounds can stimulate the intestines, leading to increased motility and secretion, thus resulting in diarrhea.

Medicinal preparations such as wormwood tea, tinctures, and essential oils, if misused or taken in large quantities, can cause diarrhea. Individuals with a history of gastrointestinal sensitivity or digestive disorders may be more susceptible to this side effect. To avoid diarrhea, it is crucial to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare professional before using Artemisia Absinthium.

Additionally, staying hydrated and monitoring bowel movements can help mitigate the risk of diarrhea caused by wormwood.

5. Stomach Cramps

Stomach cramps caused by Artemisia Absinthium refers to the painful and involuntary contractions of the stomach muscles that can occur after ingesting this herb.

The main active constituents in Artemisia absinthium that can cause this side effect are alpha-thujone and beta-thujone, which can stimulate the nervous system and irritate the gastrointestinal tract. The main medicinal preparations of wormwood that can cause stomach cramps if misused include its essential oil, dried leaves, and powdered form.

Individuals who have a history of stomach sensitivities, gastrointestinal disorders, or are pregnant or breastfeeding are more likely to experience stomach cramps after using Artemisia absinthium. To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to use wormwood in moderation, follow the recommended dosages, and avoid taking it on an empty stomach.

Additionally, using high-quality Artemisia absinthium products from reputable sources and consulting with a healthcare professional before using wormwood can help reduce the risk of stomach cramps.

6. Fatigue

Fatigue caused by Artemisia Absinthium refers to a state of extreme tiredness and lack of energy that can occur as a side effect of using this plant.

The main active constituents in Artemisia Absinthium that can lead to this side effect include absinthin, anabsinthin, and β-thujone. This side effect is most commonly associated with the oral consumption of wormwood extracts or tinctures, or the use of wormwood-based herbal teas when prepared in excessive quantities.

Individuals with a history of alcoholism, seizures, or liver disease may be more susceptible to this side effect, as well as those who take wormwood in large amounts or over extended periods of time. To avoid fatigue when using Artemisia Absinthium, it is recommended to follow dosage instructions carefully, avoid consuming alcohol while using wormwood, and discontinue use if fatigue or other adverse effects become apparent.

Consulting with a healthcare professional before using wormwood is also advised, particularly for those with pre-existing medical conditions or who are taking other medications.

7. Headache

Headache caused by Artemisia Absinthium refers to a type of pain or discomfort in the head that can occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents in Artemisia absinthium that can cause headaches are compounds called thujone and alpha-thujone. These compounds can have a stimulating effect on the nervous system and can cause symptoms such as headaches, dizziness, and nausea if consumed in large quantities.

The main medicinal preparations of Artemisia absinthium that can cause headaches if misused include essential oils, teas, and tinctures. People who are more likely to experience headaches after using Artemisia absinthium include those who consume large amounts of the herb, those who are sensitive or allergic to its active compounds, and those who use the herb in conjunction with other substances that can exacerbate its effects.

To avoid headaches and other side effects when using Artemisia absinthium, it is important to follow the recommended dosages and to consult with a healthcare professional before using the herb, especially if you have a history of headaches or other health conditions.

8. Sleep Problems

Sleep problems caused by Artemisia Absinthium refers to difficulties in falling or staying asleep, or experiencing non-restorative sleep, which may occur following the use of this herb.

The main active constituents in Artemisia Absinthium that can lead to sleep problems are alpha- and beta-thujone, which have stimulant and psychoactive effects. The main medicinal preparations of Artemisia Absinthium that can cause sleep problems if misused include essential oils, extracts, and tinctures, especially when taken in large amounts or close to bedtime. Individuals who are sensitive to stimulants, have a history of sleep disorders, or are taking other medications that affect sleep are more likely to experience sleep problems after using Artemisia Absinthium.

To avoid sleep problems, it is recommended to use Artemisia Absinthium under the guidance of a healthcare professional, follow dosage instructions carefully, and avoid using it close to bedtime or in large amounts.

9. Confusion

Confusion caused by Artemisia Absinthium refers to a state of mental bewilderment and disorientation that can occur due to the use of this herb.

The main active constituents in Artemisia Absinthium that can cause this side effect are thujone and alpha-thujone, which are neurotoxic and can affect the central nervous system if consumed in large amounts. This confusion is most likely to occur when wormwood is prepared as a strong infusion or alcoholic tincture, and when misused in high doses. People who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with a history of seizures or sensitivity to thujone, as well as those who consume alcohol while using wormwood.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and preparation instructions carefully, and to consult with a healthcare professional before using wormwood, especially if you have a history of neurological conditions or are taking other medications.

10. Depression

Depression caused by Artemisia Absinthium refers to a distressing and potentially harmful side effect that can occur with the misuse or overuse of this herb.

The main active constituents in Artemisia Absinthium that are believed to contribute to this side effect include thujone, alpha-thujone, and beta-thujone. These compounds can have psychoactive effects and may cause depression in some individuals.

The main medicinal preparations of Artemisia Absinthium that can cause this side effect if misused include essential oils, tinctures, and teas made from the leaves and flowers of the plant. People who are more likely to experience depression as a side effect of using Artemisia Absinthium include those with a history of mental health issues, those who use large amounts of the herb over an extended period of time, and those who use the herb in combination with other substances that can affect mental health, such as alcohol or certain medications.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow recommended dosages and usage guidelines carefully, and to consult with a healthcare provider before using Artemisia Absinthium or any other herbal remedy.

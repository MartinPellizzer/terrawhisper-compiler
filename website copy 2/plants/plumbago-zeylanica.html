What to know before using Plumbago zeylanica (Ceylon Leadwort)?

Plumbago zeylanica, also known as Chitrak in Ayurvedic medicine, has been used for centuries due to its medicinal properties.

It contains various compounds such as plumbagin, which has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. This plant is traditionally used to treat a wide range of health conditions, including gastrointestinal disorders, skin diseases, and fever. In terms of horticultural conditions, Plumbago zeylanica is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It can grow up to 10 feet tall and has a sprawling habit, making it an ideal candidate for groundcover. The plant produces clusters of blue flowers that attract butterflies and hummingbirds. From a botanical perspective, Plumbago zeylanica has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely on the stem.

The plant's flowers are tubular and have five lobes, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance.

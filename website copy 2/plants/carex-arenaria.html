What to know before using Carex arenaria (German-Sarsaparilla)?

Carex arenaria, commonly known as sand sedge, is a grass-like perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, but has been introduced to other regions including North America.

Medicinally, sand sedge has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties, and is believed to help with kidney and bladder ailments. It is a hardy plant that thrives in sandy and well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and dune landscapes. Botanically, Carex arenaria has long, narrow leaves and produces brown, spike-like flowers in the summer.

It typically grows in clumps, forming dense mats over time, and is often used in horticulture for its ability to stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion.

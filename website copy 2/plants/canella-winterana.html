What to know before using Canella winterana (Canella)?

Canella winterana, also known as the white stopper or wild cinnamon, is a small tree native to the Caribbean and southern Florida.

Its bark and leaves have long been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues, fever, and inflammation. The tree thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and can grow up to 20 feet tall. Its leaves are evergreen, opposite, and oblong, with a smooth margin and a shiny, dark green surface.

The small, white flowers bloom in clusters and give way to small, red, berry-like fruit.

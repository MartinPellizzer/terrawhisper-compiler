What to know before using Cydonia oblonga (Quince)?

Cydonia oblonga, commonly known as the quince, is a fruit-bearing tree with several medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including gastrointestinal disorders, respiratory infections, and cardiovascular diseases, due to its high content of antioxidants, fiber, and vitamin C. Quince trees thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, and they are relatively drought-tolerant. The tree produces white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by yellow, pear-shaped fruit that has a unique, slightly astringent taste.

The fruit can be eaten raw, but it is often cooked or made into jams and jellies due to its firm texture and high pectin content.

What are the medicinal uses of Cydonia oblonga?

Cydonia oblonga has several medicinal uses, such as reducing inflammation, soothing a sore throat, and promoting digestion.

Its constituents include tannins, pectin, and antioxidants, which contribute to its healing properties. Preparations of Cydonia oblonga include decoctions, syrups, and lozenges, which can be made from the fruit, leaves, and bark.

However, side effects such as stomach irritation and allergic reactions may occur in some individuals. Therefore, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using Cydonia oblonga, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

What are the possible side effects of Cydonia oblonga?

Cydonia oblonga can have some side effects if used improperly, such as digestive issues like stomach cramps, nausea, and diarrhea due to its high tannin content.

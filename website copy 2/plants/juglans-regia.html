What to know before using Juglans regia (Carpathian Walnut)?

Juglans regia, commonly known as the Persian or English walnut, is a valuable tree species with medicinal, horticultural, and botanical significance.

Medicinally, the bark, leaves, and fruits of Juglans regia have been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including skin diseases, inflammations, and gastrointestinal disorders, due to their rich polyphenol and tannin content. Horticulturally, Juglans regia thrives in temperate regions with well-drained soil and full sun exposure, growing up to 30 meters tall and developing a broad, rounded canopy.

The botanical characteristics of Juglans regia include its compound leaves with serrated leaflets, aromatic wood, and the distinctive nutritious nuts encased in a hard, spherical husk.

What are the planting tips of Juglans regia?

When it comes to planting Juglans regia, spring is the ideal time as it gives the tree ample time to establish its root system before the onset of winter.

While planting, ensure that the rootball is placed at the same depth as it was in the container, and the soil is tamped down around the rootball to remove any air pockets. Water thoroughly after planting and mulch the area around the tree to maintain soil moisture and regulate soil temperature.

Avoid planting it near other plants as it releases a toxin known as juglone that inhibits the growth of nearby plants.

What are the harvesting tips of Juglans regia?

Harvesting Juglans regia fruits, also known as walnuts, is typically done in late summer or early fall when the hulls turn light greenish-brown and begin to split open.

This is the time when the nuts are at their peak maturity and have the best flavor. Care must be taken while harvesting to avoid damaging the nuts. Spread a tarp or a sheet below the tree and gently shake the branches to make the nuts fall onto the sheet.

Collect the nuts, remove the hulls, rinse the nuts thoroughly, and dry them for a few days before storing them in an airtight container.

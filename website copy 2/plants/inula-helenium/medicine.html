Inula helenium (Elecampane) Medicinal Guide

Inula helenium, also known as elecampane, has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and antimicrobial properties.

The main constituents of Inula helenium include alantolactone, isoalantolactone, and helenalin, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal effects. Preparations of Inula helenium include teas, tinctures, and capsules, and it is often used to treat respiratory ailments such as bronchitis, coughs, and asthma.

However, Inula helenium can cause side effects such as stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea, and it should be used with caution. Additionally, pregnant women and people with allergies to members of the Asteraceae family should avoid using Inula helenium, as it may cause allergic reactions and uterine contractions.

It is always recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal remedy.

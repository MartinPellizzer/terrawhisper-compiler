What to know before using Forsythia suspensa (Weeping Forsythia)?

Forsythia suspensa, also known as weeping forsythia, is a type of shrub that is native to China and is known for its showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Medicinally, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to treat a variety of ailments, including fever, sore throat, and respiratory infections. The plant contains several active compounds, such as phenethyl alcohol, forsythiaside, and rutin, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Forsythia suspensa prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil. It is a hardy plant that can tolerate a wide range of temperatures and can grow up to 10 feet tall and wide.

The plant has arching branches that can be trained to grow as a small tree, and its bright green leaves turn yellow in the fall.

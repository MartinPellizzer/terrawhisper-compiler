What to know before using Rauvolfia serpentina (Indian Snakeroot)?

Rauvolfia serpentina, also known as Indian snakeroot, is a medicinal plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

Its roots have been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat a variety of ailments, including snakebite, insomnia, and anxiety. The plant thrives in shady, humid environments and prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Rauvolfia serpentina is an evergreen shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with smooth, elliptical leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots contain several alkaloids, including reserpine, which has been used to lower blood pressure and treat certain mental disorders.

However, the plant and its preparations should be used with caution, as they can have serious side effects and interact with other medications.

What are the medicinal uses of Rauvolfia serpentina?

Rauvolfia serpentina has several medicinal uses, such as treating anxiety, high blood pressure, and some types of pain.

Its constituents include various alkaloids, such as rescinnamine, serpentine, and ajmaline, which have anti-hypertensive, anti-arrhythmic, and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant's roots are commonly used to prepare decoctions, tinctures, and powder for medicinal purposes.

However, Rauvolfia serpentina can cause side effects such as stomach upset, dry mouth, and dizziness. In addition, it may lower blood pressure excessively, interfere with the heart's rhythm, and interact with other medications.

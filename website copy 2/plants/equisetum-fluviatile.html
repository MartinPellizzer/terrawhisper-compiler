What to know before using Equisetum fluviatile (River Horsetail)?

Equisetum fluviatile, also known as river horsetail, is a perennial plant found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics include hollow, jointed stems with whorls of small branches, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds. This plant has a rich history of medicinal use, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine, where it is valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and styptic properties. Equisetum fluviatile thrives in moist to wet soil and can tolerate full sun to partial shade.

However, it can be invasive in some areas due to its ability to spread through rhizomes and spores, so it requires careful management in horticultural settings.

What are the medicinal uses of Equisetum fluviatile?

Equisetum fluviatile is used to treat a variety of health issues due to its high concentrations of silica, calcium, and other minerals.

Its medicinal uses include treating tuberculosis, diarrhea, and anemia, as well as healing wounds and strengthening connective tissues. The active constituents of Equisetum fluviatile include equisetic acid, phytosterols, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. Medicinal preparations may include teas, tinctures, or powdered forms of the plant.

However, potential side effects such as stomach upset and skin irritation may occur in some individuals. Equisetum fluviatile should be used with caution in individuals with kidney disease, as it may irritate the kidneys. Additionally, pregnant women should avoid using this herb due to the risk of miscarriage.

It is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal remedy.

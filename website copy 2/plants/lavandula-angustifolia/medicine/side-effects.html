10 possible side effects of Lavandula angustifolia (Lavender) if used improperly

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as lavender, is a plant with potential medicinal properties, but it can have negative health effects if used improperly.

Firstly, ingesting lavender in large amounts can lead to stomach discomfort, nausea, and vomiting. Secondly, it can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions, especially when applied undiluted to the skin.

Thirdly, lavender oil can interfere with the effectiveness of certain medications, such as benzodiazepines, due to its sedative effects. Fourthly, excessive use of lavender can lead to headaches and increased appetite. Lastly, it is advised to avoid using lavender during pregnancy as it may have hormonal effects and impact uterine contractions.

Always consult with a healthcare provider before using lavender for medicinal purposes.

1. Allergic Reactions

Allergic reactions caused by Lavandula Angustifolia refer to the immune system's response to certain compounds found in this plant.

The main active constituents of lavender that can cause this side effect are linalool and geraniol. These compounds can trigger allergic reactions in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, rash, and difficulty breathing.

Medicinal preparations of lavender, such as essential oils, teas, and capsules, can cause allergic reactions if misused or used in large amounts. People who are more likely to experience allergic reactions to lavender include those with pre-existing skin conditions, asthma, or a family history of allergies. To avoid allergic reactions to lavender, it is important to use it as directed, perform a patch test before using any new lavender product, and dilute essential oils before applying them to the skin.

Additionally, people with a known allergy to lavender should avoid using it altogether.

2. Skin Irritation

Skin irritation caused by Lavandula Angustifolia refers to discomforts such as redness, itching, and inflammation that may occur on the skin after using products that contain this herb.

The main active constituents in lavender that can cause skin irritation are its essential oils, specifically linalool and geraniol. Medicinal preparations of lavender that can cause skin irritation if misused include lavender oil, lavender flower water, and lavender extracts. Individuals with sensitive skin or allergies to lavender and related plants are more likely to experience skin irritation after using lavender products.

To avoid skin irritation, it is important to perform a patch test before using lavender products, dilute lavender oil with a carrier oil before applying it to the skin, and avoid using lavender products on broken or irritated skin.

3. Nausea

Nausea caused by Lavandula Angustifolia refers to a queasy or uneasy sensation in the stomach, often accompanied by an urge to vomit.

This side effect can be attributed to the main active constituents of lavender, which include linalyl acetate, linalool, and eucalyptol. These compounds can cause gastrointestinal discomfort and nausea when consumed in large amounts or through improper preparation.

Medicinal preparations such as lavender tea, oil, or supplements, if misused or taken in excess, can lead to nausea. Individuals who are sensitive to lavender or have a weak digestive system are more likely to experience nausea as a side effect. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to use lavender in moderation and follow the instructions carefully.

If nausea persists, it is important to consult a healthcare professional for further evaluation.

4. Headache

Headache caused by Lavandula Angustifolia refers to a potential side effect that can occur due to the use of this plant.

The main active constituents in Lavandula Angustifolia that can cause headaches include linalyl acetate and linalool, which are responsible for its calming and soothing effects. This side effect is most commonly associated with the misuse of lavender in medicinal preparations such as essential oils, teas, and capsules.

People who are more likely to experience headaches from using Lavandula Angustifolia are those who exceed the recommended dosage, have a sensitivity to the plant, or have a history of migraines. To avoid experiencing headaches from using Lavandula Angustifolia, it is important to follow the recommended dosage, use it in moderation, and discontinue use if a headache occurs.

Additionally, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using lavender for medicinal purposes, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

5. Vomiting

Vomiting caused by Lavandula Angustifolia refers to the unpleasant symptom of forcefully expelling stomach contents through the mouth as a result of using this essential oil.

This side effect can be attributed to several of lavender's active constituents, such as linalyl acetate and linalool, which can have adverse effects if used in large amounts or improperly. Medicinal preparations that can cause vomiting if misused include lavender oil supplements, aromatherapy, and topical applications.

Individuals who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with a history of motion sickness, gastrointestinal issues, or those using lavender oil in conjunction with other medications. To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow recommended dosages, consult with healthcare professionals before using lavender oil, and avoid using it on broken or irritated skin.

Additionally, it is crucial to keep lavender oil out of reach of children, as they are more susceptible to its adverse effects.

6. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Lavandula Angustifolia refers to a feeling of lightheadedness, unsteadiness, or vertigo that may occur as a side effect of using this plant.

The main active constituents in Lavandula Angustifolia that can cause this side effect are its essential oils, which contain compounds such as linalool and linalyl acetate. The medicinal preparations of Lavandula Angustifolia that can cause dizziness if misused include aromatherapy, topical applications, and internal use, such as in teas or capsules. People who are more likely to experience dizziness from using Lavandula Angustifolia include those who are sensitive to its essential oils, have a history of low blood pressure or fainting, or are taking medication that interacts with it.

To avoid experiencing dizziness from using Lavandula Angustifolia, it is important to follow the recommended dosages, avoid using it in excess, and consult a healthcare professional before using it if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking medication.

7. Constipation

Constipation caused by Lavandula Angustifolia refers to the condition of infrequent bowel movements or difficulty in passing stools due to the use of this herb.

The main active constituents in lavender that can cause this side effect are tannins, volatile oils, and phytosterols. The medicinal preparations that can cause constipation if misused include lavender teas, capsules, and essential oils.

Elderly people, people with chronic constipation, and those who consume lavender in large amounts are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid constipation, it is advisable to use lavender in moderation, drink plenty of fluids, and consume a high-fiber diet.

It is also recommended to consult a healthcare professional before using lavender for medicinal purposes.

8. Increased Appetite

Increased appetite caused by Lavandula Angustifolia refers to an increased desire or need for food consumption.

This side effect can occur when using Lavandula angustifolia due to the main active constituents, such as linalyl acetate and linalool, that can stimulate the appetite. Medicinal preparations such as lavender essential oil, teas, and supplements, if misused or taken in large amounts, can cause increased appetite. Those who are more sensitive to the effects of Lavandula angustifolia, such as individuals with a low body mass index or those who are already predisposed to overeating, are more likely to experience this side effect.

To avoid increased appetite, it is recommended to use Lavandula angustifolia in moderation and under the guidance of a healthcare professional, and to pay attention to portion control and mindful eating practices.

9. Dry Mouth

Dry mouth caused by Lavandula Angustifolia refers to a condition where the salivary glands do not produce enough saliva to keep the mouth wet.

The main active constituents in Lavandula Angustifolia that can cause dry mouth include linalyl acetate and linalool. The medicinal preparations of Lavandula Angustifolia that can cause dry mouth as a side effect, especially when misused, include lavender tea, lavender oil, and lavender supplements. Individuals who take high doses of lavender or those who are sensitive to its constituents are more likely to experience dry mouth.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to use Lavandula Angustifolia in moderation, stay well-hydrated, and consult with a healthcare professional before using lavender for medicinal purposes.

10. High Blood Pressure

High blood pressure caused by Lavandula Angustifolia refers to an abnormally high force exerted by blood against the walls of the arteries during its circulation in the body due to the use of this essential oil.

This can occur as a side effect of the main active constituents of lavender, which include linalyl acetate, linalool, and caryophyllene. These constituents can cause a relaxation effect in the body, which can lead to a decrease in heart rate and blood pressure.

This side effect is most commonly experienced by individuals who use lavender oil in high concentrations or over long periods, and it is especially prevalent in people with pre-existing low blood pressure or those taking medication to lower their blood pressure. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to dilute lavender oil with a carrier oil before topical application and to avoid ingesting it.

Additionally, it is advised to consult with a healthcare provider before using lavender oil, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or taking medication.

What to know before using Carex pilulifera (Pill Sedge)?

Carex pilulifera, also known as the bead rush, is a grass-like plant that boasts medicinal properties, horticultural versatility, and unique botanical characteristics.

Its tiny, bead-like fruit containers, or perigynia, are encased in brown, papery bracts. Carex pilulifera thrives in well-drained, moist soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens or shaded borders. Medicinally, it has a long history of use as an astringent and diuretic, and its roots have been used to treat wounds and skin irritations.

In the garden, Carex pilulifera forms low, mounded clumps, providing a delicate texture and a subtle, contrasting color compared to surrounding foliage.

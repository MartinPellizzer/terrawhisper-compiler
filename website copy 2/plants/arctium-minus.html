What to know before using Arctium minus (Common Burdock)?

Arctium minus, also known as common burdock, is a plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

Its roots are commonly used in herbal medicine as a diuretic and blood purifier, and its leaves and seeds have been used to treat a range of conditions from gastrointestinal complaints to skin diseases. As for its horticultural conditions, Arctium minus prefers full sun and well-drained soil, and can often be found growing in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. From a botanical perspective, Arctium minus is a biennial plant that can grow up to 10 feet tall, with large, wavy leaves and purple thistle-like flowers that bloom in late summer.

The distinctive burrs that give the plant its name are actually seed heads that easily attach to fur and clothing, allowing the seeds to be dispersed widely.

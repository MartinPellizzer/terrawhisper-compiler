By Martin Pellizzer
1 minutes

Bupleurum chinense, also known as Chinese thoroughwax or Chinese goldthread, is a plant that has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

Two common medicinal preparations of this plant are tisanes and tinctures. A tisane is a type of herbal tea that is made by steeping the roots or leaves of Bupleurum chinense in hot water. This preparation is often used to treat a variety of ailments, including fever, common cold, and digestive disorders.

Tinctures, on the other hand, are made by soaking the roots or leaves of the plant in alcohol or glycerin. This preparation is thought to extract the medicinal compounds from the plant more effectively than tisanes, and it has a longer shelf life. Tinctures of Bupleurum chinense are often used to treat conditions such as menstrual cramps, liver disease, and anxiety.

However, it is important to note that the effectiveness of these preparations is not always supported by scientific evidence, and they should not be used as a substitute for professional medical advice.

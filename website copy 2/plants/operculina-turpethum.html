What to know before using Operculina turpethum (Turpeth)?

Operculina turpethum, also known as Indian Jalap, is a vital medicinal plant native to India.

It possesses significant medicinal properties, such as being a laxative, antipyretic, and anti-inflammatory agent, making it valuable in traditional Ayurvedic and Unani systems of medicine. This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, requiring well-drained soil and a sunny location to flourish. Its botanical characteristics include twining climbers that can grow up to 4 meters long, with simple, ovate-oblong leaves and funnel-shaped flowers.

The underground tuberous root is thick, fleshy, and yellowish-white, containing the active compounds responsible for its medicinal uses.

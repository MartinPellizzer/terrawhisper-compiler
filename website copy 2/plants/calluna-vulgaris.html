What to know before using Calluna vulgaris (Heather)?

Calluna vulgaris, commonly known as heather, is a hardy evergreen shrub native to Western Europe.

It is known for its medicinal properties, which include the ability to treat respiratory issues, urinary tract infections, and skin conditions. Heather thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and bell-shaped flowers, which can range in color from white to pink to purple.

Heather is also valued in horticulture for its ability to attract bees and improve soil quality.

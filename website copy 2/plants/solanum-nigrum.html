What to know before using Solanum nigrum ()?

Solanum nigrum, also known as black nightshade, is a species of plant with a number of medicinal properties.

Its leaves and fruits contain alkaloids that have been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including inflammation, fever, and skin conditions. In horticultural terms, Solanum nigrum is a hardy annual or short-lived perennial that can be found growing in a variety of habitats, from waste ground to cultivated land.

It produces small, black berries that are toxic when unripe but can be used in small amounts in cooking when fully ripe. From a botanical perspective, Solanum nigrum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes plants like tomatoes, potatoes, and bell peppers.

It has a sprawling habit, with delicate, hairy stems and ovate, dark green leaves.

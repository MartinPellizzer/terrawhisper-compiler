Vaccinium myrtillus (Bilberry) Medicinal Guide

Vaccinium myrtillus, also known as bilberry, has numerous medicinal benefits, including improving eye sight, reducing inflammation, and providing antioxidants.

The constituents of bilberry include anthocyanins, tannins, and pectin, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Preparations of bilberry can be taken in various forms such as capsules, extracts, or teas. However, it is important to be aware of potential side effects, which can include stomach upset and interactions with certain medications.

Therefore, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have pre-existing medical conditions.

What are the medicinal preparations of Vaccinium myrtillus (Bilberry)?

Vaccinium myrtillus has several medicinal preparations, such as infusions, tinctures, dry extracts, fluid extracts, capsules, tablets, ointments, creams, gels, liquid extracts, and sprays.

These preparations are used in various therapeutic indications, mainly due to their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and vasoprotective properties. Infusions are made by steeping dried bilberry fruits or leaves in hot water, creating a tea-like beverage, often used to treat diarrhea and minor inflammations.

Tinctures are alcohol-based extractions of bilberry, which can be used for longer shelf life and may be taken orally for managing circulatory issues and eye-related disorders. Dry extracts and fluid extracts are concentrated forms of bilberry, which can be found in capsules or tablets, and are used for treating chronic venous insufficiency, capillary fragility, and diabetic retinopathy. Topical preparations, such as ointments, creams, and gels, contain bilberry extract and are used to treat local inflammation, wounds, and skin irritations.

Liquid extracts and sprays are other forms of bilberry preparation, which can be used for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, and are often applied directly to the skin, throat, or mucous membranes.

The following list shows the medicinal preparations of Vaccinium myrtillus.

  • 3. Dry extract: A standardized, powdered extract of bilberry fruit, rich in anthocyanins, used to treat chronic venous insufficiency, capillary fragility, and diarrhea.

What are the possible side effects of using Vaccinium myrtillus (Bilberry) for medicinal purposes?

Vaccinium myrtillus, or bilberry, can have side effects if used improperly.

It can cause digestive issues such as diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, stomach discomfort, and may interfere with blood sugar levels. Additionally, it can increase the risk of bleeding and cause allergic reactions, headaches, dizziness, and fatigue.

In some cases, it can lead to restlessness, skin irritation, and changes in vision. Moreover, long-term use or excessive intake of bilberry can potentially cause damage to the kidneys and liver.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using bilberry or any other supplement.

The following list shows the possible side effects of Vaccinium myrtillus.

  • 1. Diarrhea: Prolonged or severe diarrhea can lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalance.
  • 2. Nausea: Frequent nausea may cause a decrease in appetite and nutrient intake, potentially leading to weight loss.
  • 3. Vomiting: Repeated vomiting can result in dehydration, electrolyte imbalance, and acid reflux.
  • 4. Stomach discomfort: Persistent stomach discomfort might indicate an underlying gastrointestinal issue that requires medical attention.
  • 5. Increased risk of bleeding: Vaccinium myrtillus may increase the risk of bleeding, especially in individuals with bleeding disorders or those taking anticoagulant medications.
  • 6. Interference with blood sugar levels: Vaccinium myrtillus may lower blood sugar levels, which can be dangerous for diabetics if not properly monitored and managed.
  • 7. Allergic reactions: Allergic reactions to Vaccinium myrtillus can range from mild symptoms like hives and rashes to severe symptoms like anaphylaxis.
  • 8. Headache: Frequent or severe headaches may indicate an underlying medical condition and should be evaluated by a healthcare professional.
  • 9. Dizziness: Dizziness can increase the risk of falls and accidents, and may also be a sign of a more serious medical condition.
  • 10. Fatigue: Chronic fatigue can negatively impact daily functioning and overall quality of life, and may indicate an underlying medical condition.

What are the precautions to take when using Vaccinium myrtillus (Bilberry) medicinally?

Before using Vaccinium myrtillus for medicinal purposes, it's important to take some precautions such as consulting a healthcare provider before use, as they can provide advice on potential interactions with existing health conditions or medications.

Additionally, individuals should be aware of any allergies to related plants and limit their dosage to the recommended amounts to avoid potential adverse effects. Furthermore, the use of Vaccinium myrtillus should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding, as well as two weeks before surgery. It is also recommended to closely monitor blood sugar levels, check for interactions with medications, herbs, and supplements, and discontinue use if any skin irritation or other side effects occur.

Lastly, individuals should follow label instructions carefully, store the product in a cool, dry place, and consult a healthcare provider if symptoms persist or if they are taking other eye medications.

The following list shows the precautions to take when using Vaccinium myrtillus.

  • 1. Consult a healthcare provider before use: It is crucial to seek professional medical advice to ensure it is safe and appropriate for your health condition and to determine the correct dosage.
  • 2. Check for allergies to related plants: If you have allergies to other plants in the Ericaceae family, you may also be allergic to Vaccinium myrtillus, so be cautious and look out for any allergic reactions.
  • 3. Limit dosage to recommended amounts: Do not exceed the recommended dosage, as excessive intake can cause adverse effects and may interfere with other medications.
  • 4. Avoid during pregnancy and breastfeeding: There is not enough evidence to determine the safety of Vaccinium myrtillus during pregnancy and breastfeeding, so it is best to avoid using it.
  • 5. Discontinue use two weeks before surgery: To prevent excessive bleeding, stop using Vaccinium myrtillus two weeks before any surgical procedures.
  • 6. Monitor blood sugar levels closely: Vaccinium myrtillus may lower blood sugar levels, so if you have diabetes or hypoglycemia, monitor your blood sugar levels closely and adjust your medication as needed.
  • 7. Check for interactions with medications: Vaccinium myrtillus may interact with certain medications, so consult your healthcare provider to ensure it is safe to use with your current medications.
  • 8. Avoid if diabetic without doctor's advice: If you have diabetes, consult your healthcare provider before using Vaccinium myrtillus, as it may lower blood sugar levels.
  • 9. Limit alcohol intake while using: Excessive alcohol intake may increase the risk of adverse effects when using Vaccinium myrtillus, so limit your alcohol intake while using it.
  • 10. Stop use and consult a doctor if any side effects occur: If you experience any side effects or adverse reactions, stop using Vaccinium myrtillus and consult your healthcare provider immediately.

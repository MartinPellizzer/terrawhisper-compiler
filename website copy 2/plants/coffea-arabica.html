What to know before using Coffea arabica (Coffee)?

Coffea arabica, also known as Arabian coffee, is a species of coffee that is native to the forests of the southwestern highlands of Ethiopia and the mountainous regions of South Sudan.

This evergreen shrub, which can grow up to 15 feet tall, has glossy green leaves, fragrant white flowers, and oval-shaped red fruits that contain the coffee beans. Coffea arabica is not only popular for its rich and aromatic taste, but also for its medicinal properties.

It contains caffeine, a stimulant that can help alleviate fatigue, improve mental alertness, and boost metabolism. Additionally, it has antioxidants that can help protect the body against damage from free radicals. For horticultural purposes, Coffea arabica thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, and prefers temperatures between 60 and 75 degrees Fahrenheit.

It's important to note that Coffea arabica is a delicate plant that requires careful cultivation and protection from frost and direct sunlight.

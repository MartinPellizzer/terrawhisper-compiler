What to know before using Galipea officinalis (Angostura)?

The Galipea officinalis, also known as the Chilean guava or strawberry tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

Its vibrant red berries are not only delicious but also packed with medicinal properties. Traditional medicine in Chile has used Galipea officinalis for treating gastrointestinal issues, inflammation, and as an antidiabetic agent due to its high content of bioactive compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

This hardy shrub thrives in a Mediterranean climate with well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it an excellent addition to any ornamental garden. Its attractive glossy leaves and distinctive white, star-shaped flowers add visual appeal throughout the year.

Galipea officinalis not only provides delicious fruit and medicinal benefits but also contributes to landscapes with its beautiful foliage and blooms.

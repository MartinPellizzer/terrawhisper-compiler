What to know before using Cornus sanguinea (Common Dogwood)?

Cornus sanguinea, also known as common dogwood, is a shrub native to Europe that has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries.

Its bark is rich incorannin, a powerful antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent, making it useful in treating conditions such as arthritis and digestive issues. In horticultural settings, Cornus sanguinea thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, and can grow up to 16 feet tall. Its botanical characteristics include opposite, simple leaves that are oval-shaped and a distinctive red color in the winter.

The shrub also produces small white flowers in the early summer and blue-black berries that are attractive to birds.

10 possible side effects of Crataegus oxyacantha (Hawthorn) if used improperly

Crataegus oxyacantha, also known as Hawthorn, is a plant that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, but if used improperly, it can have several potential health side effects.

First, it can interact negatively with other medications, particularly those that lower blood pressure, leading to dangerously low levels. Additionally, hawthorn may cause digestive issues such as nausea, stomach upset, and diarrhea when taken in large quantities.

It can also lead to dizziness, headaches, and fatigue due to its impact on the cardiovascular system. In rare cases, it can cause allergic reactions, resulting in skin rashes, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Pregnant women and people with heart conditions should avoid hawthorn as it may stimulate uterine contractions and have adverse effects on heart rhythms, respectively.

1. Nausea

Nausea caused by Crataegus Oxyacantha refers to the feeling of unease and discomfort in the stomach, sometimes accompanied by the urge to vomit.

This side effect can be attributed to the plant's active constituents, which include flavonoids, proanthocyanidins, and saponins. These constituents can stimulate the digestive system and increase motility, leading to digestive discomfort in some individuals.

Medicinal preparations such as hawthorn tea, tinctures, and supplements, when misused or taken in large quantities, can cause nausea. Those who are more likely to experience this side effect are individuals with sensitivity to hawthorn or those who consume large amounts of hawthorn-based products.

To avoid nausea, it is recommended to start with small doses, gradually increasing the amount over time and to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.

2. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Crataegus Oxyacantha refers to a sense of lightheadedness or unsteadiness that may occur in some individuals using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in hawthorn that can cause this side effect include flavonoids, saponins, and oligomeric procyanidins, which can have a mild blood pressure lowering effect. The preparations of hawthorn that can cause dizziness if misused include liquid extracts, capsules, and tablets.

Elderly individuals, those with low blood pressure, and people using medications for high blood pressure or heart conditions are more likely to experience dizziness after taking hawthorn. To avoid this side effect, it is important to start with a low dose of hawthorn and gradually increase it over time, and to avoid using it in conjunction with other blood pressure lowering medications.

Additionally, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using hawthorn to ensure it is safe and appropriate for your individual health needs.

3. Fatigue

Fatigue caused by Crataegus Oxyacantha refers to an excessive feeling of tiredness or lack of energy that may occur as a side effect of using this herbal remedy.

This weakness is usually caused by the main active constituents of hawthorn, which include flavonoids, saponins, and oligomeric proanthocyanidins. These compounds can have a sedative effect on the body, leading to fatigue if taken in large amounts or used improperly.

The medicinal preparations that are most likely to cause this side effect are hawthorn extracts, teas, and supplements, especially when used in high doses or for extended periods. People who are more likely to experience fatigue as a side effect of hawthorn use are those who are sensitive to its sedative effects, have a weak constitution, or are taking other medications that cause drowsiness.

To avoid this side effect, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage and duration of use, consult with a healthcare provider before using hawthorn, and avoid using it with other sedative drugs.

4. Headaches

Headaches caused by Crataegus Oxyacantha refer to a possible adverse effect characterized by a continuous or throbbing pain in the head.

This can occur due to the presence of active constituents such as flavonoids, proanthocyanidins, and saponins, which can cause vasodilation and increase blood flow to the brain. The main medicinal preparations that can cause this side effect if misused include hawthorn tinctures, capsules, and teas.

Individuals with a sensitivity to hawthorn or those who consume large amounts of hawthorn in a short period of time are at a higher risk of experiencing headaches. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to follow the recommended dosage and consult a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement regimen.

Additionally, those taking medications for high blood pressure or heart conditions should be cautious when using hawthorn, as it may interact with these medications and increase the risk of headaches and other side effects.

5. Stomach Upset

Stomach upset caused by Crataegus Oxyacantha refers to discomfort in the abdominal area characterized by symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramps.

This side effect can be attributed to the presence of certain active constituents in hawthorn, including flavonoids, proanthocyanidins, and saponins. The main medicinal preparations of hawthorn that can cause stomach upset if misused include hawthorn berries, flowers, and leaves, which are often used to make teas, tinctures, and supplements.

Individuals with a sensitive digestive system, those who consume hawthorn products in large quantities, or those who combine hawthorn with other medications or herbs that affect the digestive system are more likely to experience stomach upset. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of hawthorn and gradually increase the dosage over time, under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

Sweating caused by Crataegus Oxyacantha refers to the excess release of perspiration that may occur as a side effect of using this herbal remedy.

This side effect is most commonly associated with the use of hawthorn as a liquid extract, tincture, or tea, particularly when consumed in large quantities or over an extended period.

Individuals with a history of allergies or sensitivities to hawthorn or other members of the Rosaceae family may be more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to follow the recommended dosage guidelines carefully and consult with a healthcare provider before using hawthorn, especially if taking medication or have a medical condition.

7. Agitation

Agitation caused by Crataegus Oxyacantha refers to a state of excessive restlessness, worry, or nervousness that can occur as a side effect of its use.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and frequency guidelines provided by a healthcare professional or manufacturer, start with a low dose and gradually increase if necessary, and discontinue use if agitation occurs.

Insomnia caused by Crataegus Oxyacantha refers to the inability to fall asleep or stay asleep after consuming the herb.

The main active constituents in hawthorn that can cause this side effect include flavonoids, saponins, and procyanidins, which can stimulate the nervous system and interfere with normal sleep patterns. The main medicinal preparations of hawthorn that can cause insomnia if misused include tinctures, capsules, and teas, particularly when taken in large doses or close to bedtime. Individuals who are sensitive to stimulants or have existing sleep disorders are more likely to experience insomnia after using hawthorn.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow dosage recommendations carefully, avoid consuming hawthorn close to bedtime, and speak with a healthcare provider before using hawthorn if you have a history of sleep disorders or are taking other medications.

The preparations of hawthorn that are most likely to cause this side effect if misused include extracts, teas, and tinctures, especially when taken in large doses or combined with other stimulant substances. People who are more likely to experience palpitations from hawthorn use include those with pre-existing heart conditions, hypertension, or who consume large amounts of caffeine or other stimulants.

To avoid experiencing palpitations from hawthorn use, it is important to follow recommended dosages, consult with a healthcare provider before use, and monitor any changes in heart rhythm or other side effects.

10. Rash

Rash caused by Crataegus Oxyacantha refers to a skin eruption or inflammation that can occur as a side effect of using this herb.

This can manifest as reddening, itching, and/or swelling of the skin, and can be a result of an allergic reaction to the plant's active constituents. The main active constituents in Crataegus Oxyacantha that can cause a rash include flavonoids, proanthocyanidins, and saponins.

This side effect is most commonly associated with the use of hawthorn in the form of teas, tinctures, and capsules, and is more likely to occur when these preparations are misused or taken in large quantities. Individuals who are allergic to other members of the Rosaceae family, which includes apples, pears, and roses, may be more prone to experiencing a rash from hawthorn. To avoid a rash, it is recommended to start with a low dose of hawthorn and gradually increase over time, and to discontinue use if any skin irritation occurs.

It is also important to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement regimen.

By Martin Pellizzer
1 minutes

Barosma betulina, also known as Buchu, is a plant native to South Africa that is well-known for its medicinal properties.

Its leaves are commonly used to make a tisane, an herbal tea that has been used traditionally to treat a variety of ailments, including urinary tract infections, kidney stones, and rheumatism. The tea is made by steeping the dried leaves in hot water, and it has a distinctive, slightly bitter flavor.

Buchu can also be prepared as a tincture, which is a concentrated extract made by soaking the leaves in alcohol. This preparation is often used as a natural remedy for digestive issues, such as bloating, gas, and indigestion. Buchu tincture can be taken internally or used topically, and it is sometimes added to creams and ointments to treat skin conditions like eczema and acne.

However, it is important to note that Buchu can have side effects, such as stomach upset and allergic reactions, and it should be used with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

What to know before using Sinapis arvensis (California-Rape)?

Sinapis arvensis, also known as charlock or wild mustard, is a plant with medicinal properties often overlooked due to its classification as a weed.

This annual herb is known to have expectorant and antimicrobial properties, making it a potential aid in treating respiratory conditions and combating certain bacterial infections. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in waste areas, field margins, and cultivated lands. Sinapis arvensis can grow up to 1.2 meters tall, displaying yellowish-white flowers and linear-lanceolate shaped leaves, often with coarse teeth.

Its seed pods, or siliques, are long and narrow, bursting open when ripe to disperse small, round seeds.

What to know before using Quercus rotundifolia (Evergreen Oak)?

Quercus rotundifolia, also known as Holly Oak or Holm Oak, is a species of oak tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is known for its medicinal properties, which include the ability to treat digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and strengthen the immune system. The horticultural conditions for Quercus rotundifolia are quite adaptable, able to thrive in a variety of soils and climates, from sandy to clay and acidic to alkaline. As for its botanical characteristics, Quercus rotundifolia is an evergreen tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, with a dense, rounded crown and a thick, twisted trunk.

Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and oval-shaped, with serrated edges and a glossy surface.

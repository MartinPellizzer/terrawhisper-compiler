What to know before using Cinnamomum verum (Cinnamon)?

Cinnamomum verum, commonly known as true cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka and southern India.

It has several medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, and has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, from respiratory infections to digestive issues. Cinnamomum verum thrives in tropical or subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its botanical characteristics include leathery, ovate-oblong leaves with a glossy, dark green surface, and small, white flowers that bloom in panicles.

The tree's inner bark is harvested and dried to produce the cinnamon spice, which is valued for its aromatic, warm, and sweet flavor.

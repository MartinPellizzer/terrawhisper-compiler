What to know before using Convolvulus arvensis (Field Bindweed)?

Convolvulus arvensis, also known as field bindweed, is a perennial plant that possesses medicinal properties, but is often considered a nuisance in horticulture.

This plant contains alkaloids that have been used in traditional medicine to treat conditions such as inflammation and fever. However, its aggressive growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions, from full sun to shade and in various soil types, make it a challenging weed to control in gardens and agricultural lands. Its botanical characteristics include twining, funnel-shaped flowers that are white or pink and up to 2 cm in diameter, and slender, arrowhead-shaped leaves.

Despite its medicinal uses, Convolvulus arvensis is generally regarded as a noxious weed due to its invasive nature and potential to crowd out desirable plants.

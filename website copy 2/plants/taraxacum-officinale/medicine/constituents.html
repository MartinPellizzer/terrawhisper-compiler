10 Best Active Constituents of Taraxacum officinale (Dandelion)

Taraxacum officinale, commonly known as dandelion, has long been used in herbal medicine for its various health benefits.

The plant contains several active constituents, including sesquiterpene lactones, phenolic acids, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Sesquiterpene lactones, such as taraxacin and taraxasterol, have been found to have anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antibacterial effects.

Phenolic acids, such as chicoric and chlorogenic acids, have antioxidant properties and may help regulate blood sugar levels. Flavonoids, including luteolin and quercetin, have been shown to have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer effects.

Overall, the active constituents of Taraxacum officinale make it a valuable herb for promoting overall health and treating various health conditions.

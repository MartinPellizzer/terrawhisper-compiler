What to know before using Draba verna ()?

Draba verna, also known as Whitlow Grass, is a small, mat-forming perennial herb native to Europe and Central Asia.

It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure. The plant produces small, white flowers with four petals and slender, hairy leaves.

Medicinally, Draba verna has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and diuretic properties. It is also known to have potential benefits in treating respiratory ailments and skin conditions.

In horticulture, it is often used as a groundcover or in rock gardens due to its low-growing habit and attractive flowers.

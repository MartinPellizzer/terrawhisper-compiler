What to know before using Epilobium parviflorum (Hoary Willowherb)?

Epilobium parviflorum, also known as small-flowered willowherb, is a plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

It contains tannins, phenolic acids, and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. This plant is often used to treat skin conditions, such as eczema and psoriasis, and can also be used to soothe stomach ailments.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Epilobium parviflorum prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can tolerate a range of temperatures. It grows wild in many parts of the world, particularly in North America and Europe, and is easy to cultivate in a garden setting. From a botanical perspective, Epilobium parviflorum is a herbaceous perennial that grows to a height of around 30 cm.

It has narrow leaves and produces small, pink flowers in the summer.

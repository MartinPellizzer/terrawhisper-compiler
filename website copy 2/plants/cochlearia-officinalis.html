What to know before using Cochlearia officinalis (Scurvy Grass)?

Cochlearia officinalis, also known as scurvy grass, is a plant with a long history of medicinal use due to its high vitamin C content.

This has made it particularly valuable for preventing scurvy, a disease caused by vitamin C deficiency. In terms of horticultural conditions, Cochlearia officinalis prefers moist, salty soil and can often be found growing along the coast.

It is a hardy plant that can withstand cold temperatures and thrives in full sun. Botanically, Cochlearia officinalis is a member of the Brassicaceae family and has white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer.

It is a low-growing plant, typically reaching only a few inches in height.

What are the medicinal uses of Cochlearia officinalis?

Cochlearia officinalis has several medicinal uses, such as providing relief for scurvy, a condition caused by vitamin C deficiency.

The plant contains high levels of vitamin C, as well as glucosinolates and essential oils. It can be prepared as a tea, tincture, or added to salads and meals as a leafy vegetable. However, overconsumption may lead to stomach upset and diarrhea.

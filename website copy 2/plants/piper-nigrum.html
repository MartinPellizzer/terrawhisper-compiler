What to know before using Piper nigrum (Pepper)?

Piper nigrum, commonly known as black pepper, is a versatile plant with a wide range of medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine to treat conditions such as digestive issues, respiratory problems, and inflammation. Piper nigrum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade. It is a perennial vine that can grow up to 13 feet tall and has glossy, dark green leaves and small, white flowers.

The fruit of the plant, known as peppercorns, are harvested and dried to produce the ground spice that is widely used in cooking.

What are the medicinal uses of Piper nigrum?

Piper nigrum has several medicinal uses, such as providing relief from aches, pains, and fevers due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Common preparations of Piper nigrum include powder, oil, and tea, which can be consumed orally or applied topically. Piper nigrum can have some side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal discomfort when consumed in large amounts, which may lead to stomach pain, nausea, and diarrhea.

What to know before using Sparganium erectum (Branched Bur-Reed)?

Sparganium erectum, also known as upright bur-reed, is a perennial aquatic plant with delicate, feathery leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers.

It is native to Europe and Asia and thrives in wet, marshy environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Medicinally, Sparganium erectum has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and detoxifying properties.

It is often used to treat ailments such as arthritis, urinary tract infections, and skin conditions. Horticulturally, Sparganium erectum prefers full sun to partial shade and can grow up to 3 feet tall.

It is a low-maintenance plant that adds visual interest to water gardens and naturalized ponds, and its roots and rhizomes can be divided in the spring or fall to propagate new plants.

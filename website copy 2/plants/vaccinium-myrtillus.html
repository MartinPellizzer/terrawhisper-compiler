What to know before using Vaccinium myrtillus (Bilberry)?

Vaccinium myrtillus, also known as bilberry, is a perennial shrub native to Europe that thrives in acidic soils and cool, moist climates.

Its medicinal properties are extensive, including the ability to improve night vision and reduce inflammation, due to its high anthocyanin and antioxidant content. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically dark blue or purple and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. Vaccinium myrtillus requires specific horticultural conditions, such as partial shade and well-drained soil, to flourish.

Additionally, the plant is characterized by its creeping, trailing growth habit and its bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers.

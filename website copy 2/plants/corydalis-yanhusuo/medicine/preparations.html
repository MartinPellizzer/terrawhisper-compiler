By Martin Pellizzer
1 minutes

Corydalis yanhusuo, a plant native to China, has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries for its medicinal properties.

Two common forms of medicinal preparations of Corydalis yanhusuo are tisanes and tinctures. A tisane is an herbal infusion or tea, and in the case of Corydalis yanhusuo, the dried root of the plant is used to make the tea.

The tea is believed to have pain-relieving and anti-inflammatory effects, and is often used to treat digestive issues, menstrual cramps, and headaches. A tincture, on the other hand, is a preparation made by soaking the plant material in alcohol or vinegar to extract its active compounds. The resulting solution is then taken orally, often in small doses, and is believed to have similar pain-relieving and anti-inflammatory effects as the tisane.

Both preparations are considered safe when used appropriately, but it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using them, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition or are taking medication.

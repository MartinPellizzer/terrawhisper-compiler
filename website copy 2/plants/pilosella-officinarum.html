What to know before using Pilosella officinarum (Mouse-Ear Hawkweed)?

Pilosella officinarum, also known as hairy bittercress or upright bittercress, is a perennial plant that has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.

It contains several medicinal compounds, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and glucosinolates, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and diuretic properties. In horticultural conditions, Pilosella officinarum prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can thrive in a variety of environments, from rocky hillsides to gardens. Botanically, Pilosella officinarum is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have tiny hairs on the surface.

It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, including North America.

What are the medicinal uses of Pilosella officinarum?

Pilosella officinarum is used to treat a variety of health issues due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and diuretic properties.

Its active constituents include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which have been shown to have numerous medicinal benefits. These compounds have been found to help reduce inflammation, fight bacterial and fungal infections, and promote urination to remove toxins from the body.

Medicinal preparations of Pilosella officinarum typically involve making teas, tinctures, or capsules from its aerial parts. However, it is important to note that side effects such as stomach irritation, skin rashes, and allergic reactions may occur in some individuals, particularly those who are sensitive to plants in the Asteraceae family. Therefore, it is advisable to consult with a healthcare provider before using Pilosella officinarum, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or those who are taking other medications.

Precautions should also be taken when applying it topically, as it may cause skin irritation in some people.

Caring for Pilosella officinarum is relatively easy, but it's critical to provide the right growing conditions.

Harvesting Pilosella officinarum is relatively straightforward.

The aerial parts of the plant, including the leaves and flowers, are typically harvested when they are in full bloom, usually in the late spring or early summer. Harvesting in the morning after the dew has dried is ideal.

It's recommended to harvest only what is needed, as Pilosella officinarum has a short growing season. After harvesting, rinse the plant material thoroughly and allow it to dry in a well-ventilated area out of direct sunlight.

Once dry, store the plant material in an airtight container in a cool, dark place.

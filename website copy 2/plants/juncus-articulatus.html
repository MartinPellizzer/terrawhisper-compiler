What to know before using Juncus articulatus (Joint-Leaf Rush)?

Juncus articulatus, also known as jointed rush, is a grass-like plant with distinct botanical characteristics, including round, jointed stems and brown, papery bracts.

It thrives in horticultural conditions that mimic its native wetland habitats, such as moist or wet soil and full sun exposure. In traditional medicine, Juncus articulatus has been used for its anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties, and it has been used to treat ailments such as arthritis, kidney stones, and skin conditions. Its medicinal uses are attributed to the presence of compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using this plant for medicinal purposes, as it can have side effects or interact with other medications.

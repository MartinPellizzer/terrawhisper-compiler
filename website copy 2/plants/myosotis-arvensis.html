What to know before using Myosotis arvensis (Common Forget-Me-Not)?

Myosotis arvensis, also known as field forget-me-not, is a species of flowering plant in the boraginaceae family.

It has delicate blue flowers and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade. The plant contains alkaloids, saponins, and flavonoid glycosides, which have been used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and diuretic properties.

Myosotis arvensis is a low-growing, mat-forming plant, typically growing to an height of 4-8 inches, and is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in other parts of the world.

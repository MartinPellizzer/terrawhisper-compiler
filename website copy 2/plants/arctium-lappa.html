What to know before using Arctium lappa (Burdock)?

Arctium lappa, also known as greater burdock, is a biennial plant that is native to Europe and Asia and is now widely grown in North America.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and can grow up to 10 feet tall with large, heart-shaped leaves and purple thistle-like flowers. The root of Arctium lappa is commonly used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and blood purifying properties. It is often used to treat skin conditions such as acne and eczema, as well as digestive complaints like stomach aches and digestive tract inflammation.

The root has a sweet, earthy flavor and is often used in cooking as a vegetable or in teas.

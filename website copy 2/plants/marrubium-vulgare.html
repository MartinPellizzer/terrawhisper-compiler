What to know before using Marrubium vulgare (White Horehound)?

Marrubium vulgare, also known as white horehound, is a perennial herb native to Europe, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It boasts white, hairy leaves with a bitter taste, and small, tubular, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Marrubium vulgare thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is drought-tolerant once established. Medicinally, white horehound has been used for centuries to treat respiratory ailments such as coughs, colds, and bronchitis, as it helps to stimulate the mucous membranes and promote expectoration.

Its antispasmodic and expectorant properties also make it useful in treating digestive issues such as indigestion and bloating.

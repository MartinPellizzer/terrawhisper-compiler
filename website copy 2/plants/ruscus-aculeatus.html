What to know before using Ruscus aculeatus (Butcher'S Broom)?

Ruscus aculeatus, also known as butcher's broom, is a shrub native to Europe that has been used for medicinal purposes due to its therapeutic properties.

This evergreen plant contains compounds such as ruscogenins and neoruscogenins, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory, venotonic, and vasoprotective effects, making it useful in the treatment of chronic venous insufficiency and hemorrhoids. Ruscus aculeatus thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can grow up to 1 meter tall.

Its botanical characteristics include small, green, and glossy leaves, as well as sharp, spine-like modifications of the leafstalks, which give the plant a prickly appearance.

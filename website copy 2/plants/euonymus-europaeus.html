What to know before using Euonymus europaeus (Spindle)?

Euonymus europaeus, commonly known as European spindle or European witch alder, is a shrub with a rich variety of uses and characteristics.

Medicinally, it has been used in the treatment of ailments such as cancer, rheumatism, and skin diseases, due to the presence of alkaloids and other chemical compounds. In horticultural settings, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to part shade, and its maintenance includes regular pruning to control its size and shape. Botanically, Euonymus europaeus is a woody shrub that can grow up to 6 meters tall, with simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that are around 4-8 cm long.

The plant also produces showy, pinkish-red fruit that open to reveal orange seeds, making it an ornamental addition to any garden.

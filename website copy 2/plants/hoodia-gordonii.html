What to know before using Hoodia gordonii (Hoodia)?

Hoodia gordonii is a succulent plant native to the deserts of Southern Africa, particularly South Africa and Namibia.

It has been used traditionally by indigenous San tribes for its medicinal properties, including appetite suppression and thirst reduction, making it valuable for long hunting trips. This plant is well-adapted to arid environments, requiring minimal water and growing best in well-drained, sandy soil with full sun exposure. Hoodia gordonii is characterized by its fleshy, spiky stems and leaves, which vary in color from green to grayish-green and can reach up to 1 meter in height.

Its flowers, which bloom after rainfalls, are large and cream-colored, with a pleasant, sweet fragrance.

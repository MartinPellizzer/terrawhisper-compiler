What to know before using Hamamelis virginiana (Witch Hazel)?

Hamamelis virginiana, also known as witch hazel, is a versatile plant with a range of medicinal properties.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds with astringent, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, making it a popular choice for treating skin irritations, bruises, and even hemorrhoids. In terms of horticultural conditions, witch hazel is a hardy shrub that can thrive in a variety of soils and light conditions, but prefers well-drained soil and partial shade. It is characterized by its distinctive blooms, which appear in the fall and winter and contrast with its otherwise bare branches.

The flowers, which are yellow or copper-colored, give way to seed capsules that explosively eject their seeds in the fall.

What are the medicinal uses of Hamamelis virginiana?

Hamamelis virginiana has several medicinal uses, such as treating inflammation, reducing pain, and stopping bleeding.

Preparations of witch hazel include distilled extracts, ointments, and creams, which are used topically for skin conditions such as bruises, burns, and varicose veins. Harvesting witch hazel is typically done in the fall, after the leaves have turned yellow and begun to drop.

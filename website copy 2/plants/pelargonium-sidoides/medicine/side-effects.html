10 possible side effects of Pelargonium sidoides (Pelargonium) if used improperly

Pelargonium sidoides, a plant commonly used in traditional medicine, can have several potential side effects if used improperly.

First, it can cause digestive issues such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea due to its strong anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. Second, it may lead to allergic reactions, characterized by symptoms like skin rashes, itching, and difficulty breathing, especially in individuals sensitive to plants in the Geraniaceae family.

Third, excessive use may result in heart rhythm abnormalities, dizziness, and headaches due to the plant's potential effects on the cardiovascular system. Fourth, Pelargonium sidoides may interact with other medications, reducing their effectiveness or increasing the risk of side effects. Lastly, it can worsen existing health conditions such as kidney or liver diseases due to its diuretic properties, potentially causing dehydration and electrolyte imbalances.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult a healthcare professional before using Pelargonium sidoides for medicinal purposes.

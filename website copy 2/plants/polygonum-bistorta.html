What to know before using Polygonum bistorta (Bistort)?

Polygonum bistorta, also known as common bistort or European bistort, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a valuable medicinal plant, as its roots and rhizomes contain tannins and phenolic acids with astringent, styptic, and antidiarrheal properties. The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including digestive issues, skin conditions, and wounds.

In horticultural settings, Polygonum bistorta thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, growing up to 3 feet tall with a spread of 1-2 feet. It features clusters of pink-to-red flowers that bloom in early summer, adding visual interest to gardens and attracting pollinators.

Botanically, Polygonum bistorta is recognized by its slender, grooved stems, alternate, lance-shaped leaves, and distinctive spike-like inflorescences.

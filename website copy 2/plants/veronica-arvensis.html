What to know before using Veronica arvensis (Field Speedwell)?

Veronica arvensis, also known as thyme-leaved speedwell, is a small, mat-forming plant native to Europe and Asia.

It has a fibrous root system and produces small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Veronica arvensis is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in a wide range of soil types and light conditions, making it a versatile groundcover or addition to rock gardens. In traditional medicine, extracts from Veronica arvensis have been used for their anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and diuretic properties.

Its leaves and flowers contain alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal benefits.

Allium sativum, commonly known as garlic, has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries.

Two common forms of garlic preparations are tisane and tincture. A tisane is a medicinal tea made by steeping crushed garlic cloves in hot water. This preparation is often used to help alleviate symptoms of respiratory ailments, such as congestion and cough.

Tinctures, on the other hand, are made by soaking chopped garlic in alcohol for several weeks. This preparation is taken orally and is often used to help reduce cholesterol levels, boost the immune system, and improve circulation. Both tisane and tincture can be made at home, but it is important to follow proper procedures to ensure their effectiveness and safety.

It is also recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new medicinal preparations.

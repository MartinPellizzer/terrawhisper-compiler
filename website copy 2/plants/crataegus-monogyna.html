What to know before using Crataegus monogyna (Hawthorn)?

Crataegus monogyna, also known as common hawthorn, is a species of tree or shrub native to Europe, northwest Africa, and western Asia.

Its flowers and berries have been used in herbal medicine for centuries to treat various ailments, including heart disease, high blood pressure, and digestive issues. Crataegus monogyna prefers full sun and well-drained soil, and it can tolerate a wide range of soil types, making it a versatile horticultural choice. The tree typically grows to a height of 5-15 meters and has a dense, spreading crown.

Its leaves are lobed, glossy green, and turn yellow or red in the fall, while its white or pink flowers, which appear in May, have a strong, sweet scent.

What are the medicinal uses of Crataegus monogyna?

Crataegus monogyna is used to treat various cardiovascular diseases due to its numerous medicinal properties.

Its active constituents, including flavonoids, procyanidins, and triterpene acids, contribute to its health benefits, such as improving cardiac function, reducing blood pressure, and preventing arrhythmia. Medicinal preparations of hawthorn are available in various forms, including teas, tinctures, capsules, and liquid extracts.

However, potential side effects such as nausea, dizziness, and headaches may occur, especially when taken in large doses or combined with certain medications. Therefore, it is crucial to consult a healthcare professional before starting hawthorn therapy, especially for those with heart conditions, pregnant or breastfeeding women, and individuals taking prescription drugs.

Additionally, hawthorn may interact with other herbs and supplements, so it is essential to inform healthcare providers of any complementary remedies being taken.

What are the possible side effects of Crataegus monogyna?

While Crataegus monogyna is generally considered safe, there are some potential side effects to be aware of.

Some people may experience digestive symptoms such as nausea, stomach pain, and diarrhea.

Hawthorn can also interact with certain medications, such as beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers, and nitrates, so it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before taking hawthorn supplements if you are taking any medications.

What are the precautions to take when using Crataegus monogyna?

Precautions should be taken when using Crataegus monogyna.

It is not recommended for pregnant or breastfeeding women, as there is not enough research on its safety for these populations. Additionally, hawthorn may lower blood pressure, so people with low blood pressure or those taking medication to lower their blood pressure should use caution when taking hawthorn supplements.

What are the horticultural conditions of Crataegus monogyna?

Crataegus monogyna needs well-drained soil and a sunny or partially shaded position to thrive.

When planting, ensure a space of 5-8 meters between plants, and water thoroughly while establishing. For care, regular watering, pruning of dead or crossing branches, and an annual application of balanced fertilizer will suffice.

Hawthorn fruits can be harvested in late summer to early fall when they turn red; wear gloves as the thorns can be sharp. Common pests include aphids, caterpillars, and scale insects, while diseases such as fireblight and powdery mildew can affect hawthorn.

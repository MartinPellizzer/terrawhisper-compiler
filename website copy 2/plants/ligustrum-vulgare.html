What to know before using Ligustrum vulgare (Common Privet)?

Ligustrum vulgare, also known as common privet, is a species of shrub that is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Medicinally, it has been used in traditional systems of medicine for its potential to boost the immune system, improve liver function, and slow the aging process. In terms of horticultural conditions, Ligustrum vulgare prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil. It is a hardy plant that can tolerate a wide range of soil types and pH levels.

Botanically, Ligustrum vulgare is characterized by its opposite, decussate leaves, and its clusters of small, white flowers that give way to berries which are green at first, then turn black as they ripen.

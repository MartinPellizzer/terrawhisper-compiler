What to know before using Juncus inflexus (Hard Rush)?

Juncus inflexus, also known as soft rush, is a grass-like plant with culms that grow in dense tufts and can reach up to 1 meter in height.

It thrives in wet and humid environments, such as marshes, streams, and pond edges, and can also be found in sandy or clayey soils. Medically, Juncus inflexus has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and laxative properties.

Its stems and leaves contain bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are believed to have various health benefits. In horticulture, Juncus inflexus is often used as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in wet conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some areas, so it is important to monitor its growth and control its spread.

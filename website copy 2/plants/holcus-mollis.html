What to know before using Holcus mollis (German Velvet Grass)?

Holcus mollis, also known as soft grass, is a perennial species native to Europe's meadows and pastures.

Its botanical characteristics include distinctly flat, narrow, and rounded leaves that are soft to the touch, and small, loose panicles of pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the late spring. In horticulture, Holcus mollis thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it an ideal choice for shaded gardens and woodland areas. Medicinally, Holcus mollis has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including skin irritations, digestive issues, and respiratory conditions.

Its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties have been studied for their potential benefits in alleviating symptoms of asthma, whooping cough, and other respiratory conditions.

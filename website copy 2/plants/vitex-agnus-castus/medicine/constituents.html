The primary active constituents include flavonoids, iridoid glycosides, and essential oils. Flavonoids, such as casticin and spinasterol, have been found to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Iridoid glycosides, including aucubin and agnuside, may help regulate hormonal activity in the body, particularly in relation to menstrual cycle irregularities and premenstrual syndrome. The essential oils in chasteberry, such as caryophyllene and sabinene, have been shown to have analgesic and sedative properties.

Overall, the active constituents of Vitex agnus-castus may have a range of medicinal benefits, although more research is needed to fully understand their mechanisms of action and potential applications.

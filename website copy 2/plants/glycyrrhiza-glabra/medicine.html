Glycyrrhiza glabra (Licorice) Medicinal Guide

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice, has a variety of medicinal benefits, including anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and antiviral properties.

The root of the plant contains several active constituents, such as glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and coumarins, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects. Preparations of Glycyrrhiza glabra include teas, tinctures, capsules, and topical ointments. However, excessive consumption of licorice can lead to side effects such as hypertension, hypokalemia, and edema.

Precautions should be taken while using licorice, especially for pregnant women, people with heart or kidney disease, and those on certain medications, as it can interact with drugs such as diuretics, corticosteroids, and warfarin.

What are the medicinal preparations of Glycyrrhiza glabra (Licorice)?

Glycyrrhiza glabra has several medicinal preparations, such as infusions, tinctures, and decoctions, which are all liquid extracts that are made using different methods.

An infusion is made by steeping the licorice root in hot water, while a tincture is created by soaking the root in alcohol or a alcohol-water mixture. A decoction is made by simmering the root in water for a longer period of time. Fluid extracts and liquid extracts are concentrated forms of the plant, created by using a solvent to extract the active compounds.

Dry extracts are made by evaporating the solvent from a liquid extract, leaving behind a powdered extract. Capsules, tablets, and powders are solid dosage forms that contain the licorice extract or powder. Syrups and glycerites are sweet, viscous liquids that contain a high concentration of the extract. Ointments, creams, and gels are topical forms of the medicine, used for skin conditions.

Suppositories, liniments, pastes, sprays, mouthwashes, and lozenges/pastilles are additional forms of licorice medicine, each with their own unique uses and administration methods.

The following list shows the medicinal preparations of Glycyrrhiza glabra.

  • 1. Infusion: A preparation made by steeping the roots or extracts of Glycyrrhiza glabra in hot water, often used for soothing digestive issues and throat irritations.
  • 2. Tincture: A concentrated alcohol-based extract of Glycyrrhiza glabra, used for its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and expectorant properties.
  • 3. Decoction: A strong, concentrated liquid made by boiling and simmering the roots of Glycyrrhiza glabra, often used for treating respiratory ailments and digestive problems.
  • 4. Fluid extract: A highly concentrated, liquid form of Glycyrrhiza glabra extract, produced by percolation and used for its medicinal properties in a more potent form than infusions or decoctions.
  • 5. Dry extract: A powdered extract of Glycyrrhiza glabra, created by removing the solvent from a liquid extract, and used for its potent medicinal benefits in a more stable and portable form.
  • 6. Liquid extract: A concentrated, liquid form of Glycyrrhiza glabra extract, prepared by percolation or maceration, and used for its medicinal properties in a more potent form than infusions or decoctions.
  • 7. Powder: Finely ground, dried root of Glycyrrhiza glabra, used for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties, often added to food or beverages.
  • 8. Capsules: Encapsulated, dried root powder of Glycyrrhiza glabra, used for its medicinal benefits in a convenient, measured dosage form.
  • 9. Tablets: Compressed, dried root powder of Glycyrrhiza glabra, used for its medicinal benefits in a convenient, measured dosage form.
  • 10. Syrup: A sweet, viscous liquid preparation of Glycyrrhiza glabra extract, often combined with other ingredients like honey or sugar, used for its soothing and expectorant properties, particularly for treating coughs and sore throats.

What are the possible side effects of using Glycyrrhiza glabra (Licorice) for medicinal purposes?

Glycyrrhiza glabra can have side effects if used improperly.

Excessive consumption of this herb can lead to hypertension, hypokalemia, and heart problems due to the lowering of potassium levels in the body. Additionally, it can cause psychological disorders, muscle weakness or cramps, kidney damage, hormonal imbalances, and digestive issues. Other potential side effects include low sodium levels, menstrual irregularities, decreased libido, vision problems, and severe headaches or fatigue.

Therefore, it is crucial to use this herb under the guidance of a healthcare professional, as improper use can result in these serious side effects.

The following list shows the possible side effects of Glycyrrhiza glabra.

  • 1. Hypertension: Glycyrrhiza glabra, also known as licorice, can cause hypertension, or high blood pressure, due to the compound glycyrrhizin, which can cause the body to retain sodium and lose potassium, leading to increased blood volume and pressure.
  • 2. Hypokalemia: Excessive consumption of Glycyrrhiza glabra can lead to hypokalemia, or low potassium levels, as the compound glycyrrhizin can interfere with the body's ability to regulate potassium, leading to muscle weakness, cramps, and heart problems.
  • 3. Headaches: Some people may experience headaches as a side effect of consuming Glycyrrhiza glabra, which can be caused by changes in blood pressure, hormonal imbalances, or dehydration.
  • 4. Fatigue: Prolonged use of Glycyrrhiza glabra can lead to fatigue, as the body's adrenal glands may become overworked due to the compound glycyrrhizin, which can interfere with the body's cortisol production.
  • 5. Psychological disorders: Excessive consumption of Glycyrrhiza glabra has been linked to psychological disorders such as anxiety, depression, and mania, possibly due to hormonal imbalances or changes in neurotransmitter function.
  • 6. Muscle weakness or cramps: Glycyrrhiza glabra can cause muscle weakness or cramps due to hypokalemia, or low potassium levels, which can result from the compound glycyrrhizin interfering with the body's potassium regulation.
  • 7. Heart problems: Long-term use of Glycyrrhiza glabra can lead to heart problems such as arrhythmia, or irregular heartbeat, due to hypokalemia or hormonal imbalances caused by the compound glycyrrhizin.
  • 8. Kidney damage: Excessive consumption of Glycyrrhiza glabra can cause kidney damage, as the compound glycyrrhizin can interfere with the body's ability to regulate electrolytes, leading to kidney dysfunction and potential failure.
  • 9. Hormonal imbalances: Glycyrrhiza glabra can cause hormonal imbalances due to the compound glycyrrhizin, which can interfere with the body's cortisol production and regulation, leading to changes in mood, energy, and other hormone-related functions.
  • 10. Digestive issues: Glycyrrhiza glabra can cause digestive issues such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain, possibly due to changes in electrolyte balance or hormonal imbalances caused by the compound glycyrrhizin.

What are the precautions to take when using Glycyrrhiza glabra (Licorice) medicinally?

Before using Glycyrrhiza glabra for medicinal purposes, it's important to take some precautions such as consulting a healthcare provider before use, as it can interact with various medications and medical conditions.

Limit consumption to 4-6 weeks and avoid its use if pregnant or breastfeeding. It's also crucial to avoid using licorice if you have high blood pressure or kidney issues, if you're taking diuretics or heart medication, blood thinners, estrogen, corticosteroids, or if you're experiencing muscle pain or numbness. Monitor potassium levels and discontinue use if headache, edema, or lethargy occur.

Limit intake to 2g of licorice root per day, and do not consume licorice candy as a substitute, as it may not contain the same beneficial compounds. Additionally, avoid alcohol while taking licorice and avoid using in children under 12. Discontinue use in case of allergic reaction and inform healthcare providers of licorice use.

Always follow the manufacturer's recommended dosage and consult a healthcare provider for prolonged use.

The following list shows the precautions to take when using Glycyrrhiza glabra.

  • 1. Consult a healthcare provider before use: Seek professional advice to ensure safe and effective use of Glycyrrhiza glabra, considering your health status, medications, and other factors.
  • 2. Limit consumption to 4-6 weeks: Do not use this herb continuously for more than 4-6 weeks to avoid potential adverse effects and maintain its beneficial effects.
  • 3. Avoid if pregnant or breastfeeding: Glycyrrhiza glabra should not be used by pregnant or breastfeeding women due to the risk of hormonal imbalances and other potential complications.
  • 4. Avoid if high blood pressure or kidney issues: Individuals with high blood pressure or kidney problems should avoid using Glycyrrhiza glabra, as it may exacerbate these conditions.
  • 5. Avoid if taking diuretics or heart medication: Using Glycyrrhiza glabra with diuretics or heart medication can lead to electrolyte imbalances and other complications.
  • 6. Avoid if taking blood thinners: Glycyrrhiza glabra may interact with blood thinners, increasing the risk of bleeding and other adverse effects.
  • 7. Avoid if taking estrogen: Using Glycyrrhiza glabra with estrogen-based medications can result in hormonal imbalances and other complications.
  • 8. Avoid if taking corticosteroids: Combining Glycyrrhiza glabra with corticosteroids can increase the risk of adrenal insufficiency and other side effects.
  • 9. Monitor potassium levels: Regularly check potassium levels when using Glycyrrhiza glabra, as it may affect potassium regulation in the body.
  • 10. Discontinue if headache, edema, or lethargy occur: If you experience headaches, swelling, or fatigue while using Glycyrrhiza glabra, stop using it immediately and consult a healthcare provider.

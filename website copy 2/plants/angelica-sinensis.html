What to know before using Angelica sinensis (Dong Quai)?

Angelica sinensis, also known as Dong Quai or female ginseng, is a herbaceous perennial native to China and other parts of Asia.

It has a long history of use in traditional Chinese medicine for treating various ailments such as menstrual cramps, fatigue, and anemia due to its medicinal properties including being an anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and immune system booster. Angelica sinensis prefers cooler climates and well-drained soil, and can grow up to 3 feet tall with hollow stems and umbels of small white flowers.

Its leaves are compound, with wide leaflets, and its roots are thick and fleshy, which are used in herbal remedies.

What are the medicinal uses of Angelica sinensis?

Angelica sinensis has several medicinal uses, such as alleviating menstrual cramps, regulating menstrual cycles, and boosting the immune system.

Its constituents include phytochemicals like coumarins, ferulic acid, and phthalides, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and sedative effects. Preparations of Angelica sinensis typically involve drying and grinding the root into a powder, which can be consumed in capsule form or brewed into a tea. However, it is important to note that Angelica sinensis can have side effects such as photosensitivity, increased risk of bleeding, and hormonal imbalances.

Additionally, pregnant women and individuals taking blood-thinning medication should avoid using Angelica sinensis, as it may interact negatively with these conditions and treatments.

What are the possible side effects of Angelica sinensis?

Angelica sinensis can have some side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation and photosensitivity, which can manifest as rashes, itching, or inflammation when exposed to sunlight.

Additionally, this herb may cause digestive issues like nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain, especially when taken in large amounts or for extended periods. In some cases, it may also lead to hormonal imbalances due to its potential estrogen-like effects, resulting in breast tenderness, menstrual irregularities, and other menstruation-related symptoms. Furthermore, Angelica sinensis can potentially interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and anticoagulants, which can increase the risk of bleeding and bruising.

It is crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen, including the use of Angelica sinensis, to ensure safe and appropriate application.

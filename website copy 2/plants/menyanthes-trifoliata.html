What to know before using Menyanthes trifoliata (Bogbean)?

Menyanthes trifoliata, also known as buckbean or marsh trefoil, is a perennial herb found in wetlands throughout the Northern Hemisphere.

Its small, white or pink flowers bloom in early spring, and its leaves are compound with three leaflets. Medicinally, Menyanthes trifoliata has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues, skin conditions, and fevers.

It contains tannins, flavonoids, and essential oils, which contribute to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. In horticultural settings, Menyanthes trifoliata prefers full sun to partial shade and moist to wet soil.

It is a hardy plant that can tolerate cold temperatures, making it a popular choice for water gardens and bog gardens.

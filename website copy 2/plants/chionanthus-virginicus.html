What to know before using Chionanthus virginicus (Fringe Tree)?

Chionanthus virginicus, also known as fringetree or grancy graybeard, is a flowering tree native to the southeastern United States.

Medicinally, it has been used by indigenous peoples and herbalists for its anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties, and to treat liver and kidney ailments. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, and can reach a height of 12-20 feet. The tree blooms in late spring with clusters of fragrant, white flowers, and produces small, blue-black berries in the fall that are favored by birds.

Its botanical characteristics include opposite, simple leaves, and a multi-stemmed, often picturesque growth habit.

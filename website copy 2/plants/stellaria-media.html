What to know before using Stellaria media (Chickweed)?

Stellaria media, also known as chickweed, is a low-growing plant found in many parts of the world.

It has several medicinal properties, including being used as an anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and diuretic. Chickweed can be used in teas, salads, or as a poultice and is often used to treat skin irritations, bruises, and cuts.

In terms of horticultural conditions, chickweed prefers moist, shady areas and can often be found in lawns and gardens. It is a cool-season plant, thriving in temperatures between 60-75°F. As for its botanical characteristics, Stellaria media is an annual or winter annual plant that grows close to the ground, forming dense mats of oval, smooth, bright green leaves.

It produces small, white flowers with five deeply lobed petals, each petal divided into two parts.

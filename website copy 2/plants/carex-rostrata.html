What to know before using Carex rostrata (Bottle Sedge)?

Carex rostrata, also known as False Hawk's-beard, is a grass-like sedge that boasts a range of medicinal properties.

Its roots have been traditionally used by indigenous peoples to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues and skin irritations. In horticultural settings, Carex rostrata thrives in moist to wet soil and prefers partial to full shade. It is a clump-forming perennial that grows to be around 1-2 feet tall and wide, with distinctive greenish-brown spikelets that emerge in the early summer.

Botanically, Carex rostrata is characterized by its triangular stems and basal leaves, which are also used in some herbal remedies.

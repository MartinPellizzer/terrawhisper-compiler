What to know before using Potentilla erecta (Tormentil)?

Potentilla erecta, also known as tormentil, is a perennial plant that is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is known for its medicinal properties, including its ability to act as an astringent and anti-inflammatory agent, making it useful for treating wounds, diarrhea, and mouth ulcers. The plant typically grows to be around 15-30 cm tall, and has yellow-green, lobed leaves and yellow, four-petaled flowers. Potentilla erecta prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is a hardy plant that can tolerate a wide range of temperatures and conditions, making it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.

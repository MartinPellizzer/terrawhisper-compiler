What to know before using Sonchus oleraceus (Common Sowthistle)?

Sonchus oleraceus, also known as common sowthistle, is a plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic effects, and is also believed to have potential as a treatment for diabetes and cardiovascular diseases. In terms of horticultural conditions, Sonchus oleraceus is a hardy annual or biennial plant that can be found growing in a wide range of environments, from disturbed soils and waste areas to gardens and cultivated fields.

It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, but can tolerate a wide range of conditions. From a botanical perspective, Sonchus oleraceus is a member of the Asteraceae family, and can grow up to several feet in height.

It has deeply lobed, toothed leaves and produces small, yellow flower heads, followed by downy seeds that can be dispersed by the wind.

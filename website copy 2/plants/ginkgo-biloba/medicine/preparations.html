By Martin Pellizzer
1 minutes

Ginkgo biloba, also known as the maidenhair tree, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries and its leaves are used to make various medicinal preparations such as tisanes and tinctures.

A tisane is a type of herbal tea that is made by steeping the dried leaves in hot water. This preparation is often used to help improve memory and cognitive function, and it may also have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

A tincture, on the other hand, is a liquid extract made by soaking the leaves in alcohol or another solvent to extract the active compounds. Tinctures are typically taken orally, and they are believed to have similar benefits to tisanes, including improved memory and cognitive function, as well as potential protection against age-related cognitive decline. Both tisanes and tinctures made from Ginkgo biloba are considered generally safe, but they can cause side effects such as stomach upset, headache, and dizziness in some people.

It is important to talk to a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen, including Ginkgo biloba.

What to know before using Festuca ovina (Ovina)?

Festuca ovina, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia, but has since naturalized in many regions around the world.

Medicinally, sheep's fescue has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties, and is believed to help with kidney and bladder ailments. In terms of horticultural conditions, Festuca ovina prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and can tolerate a wide range of soil types and pH levels.

Botanically, Festuca ovina is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves, which can grow up to 20 inches tall, and produces small, purple flowers in the late spring and early summer.

What to know before using Pimpinella saxifraga (Burnet-Saxifrage)?

Pimpinella saxifraga, also known as burnet saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial herb with a variety of medicinal and horticultural uses.

Medically, burnet saxifrage has been used in traditional medicine to treat ailments such as coughs, bronchitis, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and can be propagated through seeds or division. The plant grows to around 30 cm in height, and produces small white flowers in clusters during the summer months.

Its leaves are delicate and feathery, and the plant has a long history of use in both cooking and medicine.

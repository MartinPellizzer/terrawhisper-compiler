10 Best Active Constituents of Zea mays (Corn)

Zea mays, commonly known as corn, has been found to contain several active constituents with potential medicinal benefits.

One such compound is anthocyanins, which are powerful antioxidants found in corn husks and kernels. These antioxidants have been shown to help reduce inflammation, protect against oxidative stress, and even lower the risk of chronic diseases such as cancer and heart disease.

Corn also contains fiber, which has been linked to improved digestive health, reduced cholesterol levels, and a lower risk of type 2 diabetes. Additionally, corn is a good source of vitamin C, thiamin, and other essential minerals like magnesium, potassium, and phosphorus.

While more research is needed to fully understand the medicinal properties of corn, these active constituents suggest that corn may have a role to play in preventing and managing certain health conditions.

1. Corn oil

The corn oil contained in Zea Mays is a type of fat extracted from the germ of corn plants.

Its main medicinal properties include its high vitamin E content, which acts as an antioxidant, and its ability to lower bad cholesterol levels while increasing good cholesterol levels. Corn oil can help relieve health conditions such as high blood pressure, constipation, and skin disorders.

This constituent is mainly found in the germ of the corn plant. Some possible side effects of corn oil if used improperly include digestive problems and skin irritation.

It is important to use corn oil in moderation, as overconsumption can lead to weight gain due to its high calorie content.

2. Cornstarch

The cornstarch contained in Zea Mays is a fine, white powder ground from the endosperm of corn kernels.

Its main medicinal properties include being a natural emollient and skin protectant, providing a barrier against irritants and helping to maintain skin moisture. This Zea mays constituent can help relieve symptoms of various health conditions such as diaper rash, skin irritations, and minor burns. Cornstarch can be found in the endosperm of corn kernels, and it is extracted through a process of grinding and purification.

Improper use of cornstarch can lead to some side effects such as skin irritation, infection, or exacerbation of existing skin conditions if used on broken or irritated skin.

3. D-chiro-Inositol

The d-chiro-inositol contained in Zea Mays is a type of inositol, a carbocyclic sugar that is involved in various biological processes, such as signal transduction and cell membrane integrity.

Its main medicinal properties include improving insulin sensitivity, reducing inflammation, and neuroprotective effects. This Zea mays constituent has been shown to help relieve health conditions such as polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), metabolic syndrome, and neuropathy.

D-chiro-inositol is found primarily in the corn kernel, specifically in the endosperm. Some possible side effects of this Zea mays constituent if used improperly include gastrointestinal disturbances such as diarrhea, nausea, and bloating.

Additionally, high doses of d-chiro-inositol may lower blood sugar levels, so it should be used with caution in individuals with diabetes or hypoglycemia.

4. Folic acid

The folic acid contained in Zea mays is a type of B vitamin (B9) that is crucial for proper brain function and emotional and mental health.

It has several medicinal properties, including the ability to help the body produce and maintain new cells, prevent changes to DNA that may lead to cancer, and help the body break down, use, and create new proteins. Folic acid may help relieve health conditions such as anemia, kidney disease, and liver disease. This constituent is found mainly in the kernels of corn, particularly in the germ and endosperm parts.

Some possible side effects of folic acid, if used improperly, include stomach upset, sleep disorders, skin rashes, seizures, and other serious allergic reactions.

5. Lutein

The lutein contained in Zea mays is a type of carotenoid that is known for its antioxidant properties.

It has several medicinal properties, including reducing the risk of age-related macular degeneration and cataracts, improving cognitive function, and reducing the risk of certain types of cancer. Lutein has been shown to help relieve eye-related health conditions, such as improving visual acuity and reducing eye strain. This constituent is mainly found in the kernels of the Zea mays plant, particularly in the germ and endosperm.

Some possible side effects of lutein if used improperly include digestive issues, allergic reactions, and an increased risk of kidney stones if taken in large doses.

6. Beta-carotene

The beta-carotene contained in Zea mays is a type of pigment called a carotenoid, which is known for its provitamin A activity and antioxidant properties.

Its main medicinal properties include supporting immune function, promoting healthy vision, and providing antioxidant protection against free radicals. Zea mays beta-carotene has been found to help relieve health conditions such as vitamin A deficiency, age-related macular degeneration, and skin disorders. This constituent is primarily found in the kernels of corn, particularly in yellow and orange varieties.

Possible side effects of Zea mays beta-carotene, if used improperly, include carotenemia (a benign condition causing yellowing of the skin), digestive discomfort, and potential interactions with certain medications.

7. Alpha-tocopherol

The alpha-tocopherol contained in Zea Mays is a type of vitamin E that has antioxidant properties.

Its main medicinal properties include protecting the body from free radicals, supporting immune function, and aiding in the metabolism of fats. This Zea mays constituent can help relieve health conditions related to heart disease, diabetes, and certain types of cancer. Alpha-tocopherol is found mainly in the germ and endosperm of the corn kernel.

Improper use of alpha-tocopherol can lead to side effects such as nausea, diarrhea, and an increased risk of bleeding.

8. Protease inhibitors

The protease inhibitors contained in Zea Mays are a type of compound that inhibits the action of enzymes called proteases, which break down proteins in the body.

These inhibitors have been found to have several medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral effects. They may help relieve health conditions such as digestive disorders, bacterial and viral infections, and inflammation. Protease inhibitors are mainly found in the corn kernels, specifically in the endosperm fraction.

Improper use of Zea mays protease inhibitors can lead to some side effects, such as digestive discomfort, nausea, and allergic reactions.

9. Phytic acid

The phytic acid contained in Zea Mays is a form of phytochemical that stores phosphorus in plants.

It has several medicinal properties, including its role as a antioxidant and its ability to chelate heavy metals, making them less available to the body. Phytic acid may help relieve conditions such as dysentery and diarrhea by reducing the growth of harmful bacteria in the gut. It is found mainly in the bran layer of corn kernels, as well as in other grains and seeds.

Improper use of phytic acid can lead to decreased absorption of certain minerals, such as calcium, iron, and zinc, due to its ability to bind to these nutrients in the gut.

10. Squalene

The squalene contained in Zea Mays is a compound that is typically found in the plant's seeds and husks.

Its main medicinal properties include antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immune-boosting effects. This Zea mays constituent has been shown to help relieve various health conditions, such as heart disease, cancer, and skin disorders. Squalene is mainly found in corn germ and can be extracted through a process of cold-pressing the germ.

Improper use of Zea mays squalene can lead to several side effects, including digestive issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

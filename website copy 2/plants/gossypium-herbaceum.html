What to know before using Gossypium herbaceum (Cotton)?

Gossypium herbaceum, also known as Levant cotton, is a shrubby perennial plant that is native to Africa and Asia.

Its horticultural conditions require full sun and well-drained soil, and it can grow in a variety of soil types. Medicinally, G.

herbaceum has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic properties. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids, tannins, and saponins, that contribute to its medicinal uses. Botanically, G.

herbaceum is characterized by its hairy leaves, yellow or white flowers, and capsular fruits that contain seeds from which cotton fiber is obtained.

What are the harvesting tips of Gossypium herbaceum?

Harvesting Gossypium herbaceum requires careful timing and handling.

The bolls, or seedpods, should be allowed to mature on the plant until they split open, revealing the cotton fibers inside. The bolls should then be carefully picked by hand, taking care not to damage the fibers.

The fibers should be separated from the seeds and other debris, and then dried and cleaned before being used for spinning or other textile processes.

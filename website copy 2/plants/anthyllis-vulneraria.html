It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. This plant prefers well-drained, poor-to-moderately fertile soil and full sun, and it is drought tolerant once established. Historically, Anthyllis vulneraria has been used for its medicinal properties, including its ability to aid in wound healing and its anti-inflammatory effects.

The leaves and flowers are used to make teas and tinctures, and it has been used to treat a variety of ailments, from gastrointestinal issues to skin conditions.

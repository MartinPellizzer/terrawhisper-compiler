What to know before using Viola palustris (Alpine Marsh Violet)?

Viola palustris, also known as the Marsh Violet, is a species of perennial flowering plant that thrives in damp habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks.

It is a low-growing plant that typically reaches a height of 6-12 inches, with kidney-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers that bloom from May to July. The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties. Viola palustris contains various compounds, including salicylic acid, flavonoids, and saponins, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal benefits.

For horticultural purposes, Viola palustris prefers partial shade and consistently moist soil, making it a suitable choice for a bog garden or other damp areas of the landscape.

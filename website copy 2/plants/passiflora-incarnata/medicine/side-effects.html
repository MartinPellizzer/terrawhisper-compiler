10 possible side effects of Passiflora incarnata (Passionflower) if used improperly

Passiflora incarnata, also known as passionflower, is a plant that is often used in herbal supplements for its calming and anxiety-reducing effects.

However, if used improporiately, it can cause several negative health side effects. Overconsumption can lead to drowsiness, confusion, and dizziness.

Additionally, it can cause nausea, vomiting, and in rare cases, even altered consciousness. It can also interact with other medications, such as sedatives and antidepressants, which can increase the risk of adverse effects. Pregnant women and those with liver or kidney disease should avoid using passionflower due to the potential for negative impacts on these organs.

As with any supplement, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using passionflower to ensure safe and effective use.

1. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Passiflora Incarnata refers to a sensation of lightheadedness, unsteadiness, or slightvertigo that may occur as a side effect of using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in Passiflora Incarnata that can cause this side effect include various flavonoids, such as apigenin and luteolin, as well as alkaloids like harman and harmaline. This dizziness is most commonly associated with oral preparations, such as teas, capsules, and liquid extracts, especially when taken in large amounts or without proper medical supervision. Individuals who are more susceptible to this side effect include those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have existing medical conditions such as low blood pressure or neurological disorders.

To avoid experiencing this side effect, it's essential to follow recommended dosages and consult with a healthcare provider before using Passiflora Incarnata, particularly if you have any pre-existing health conditions or are taking other medications.

2. Drowsiness

Drowsiness caused by Passiflora Incarnata refers to the sensation of sleepiness or exaggerated desire for sleep that can occur after using this herb.

The main active constituents of Passiflora incarnata that can cause this side effect are flavonoids, such as apigenin and luteolin, which have sedative and anxiolytic properties. The main medicinal preparations of Passiflora incarnata that can cause this side effect if misused include tea, capsules, and tinctures. Individuals who are more likely to experience drowsiness after using Passiflora incarnata are those who are already taking other sedative medications or who have a natural predisposition to drowsiness.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosages, not to consume alcohol or other sedative substances while using Passiflora incarnata, and to consult a healthcare professional before combining it with other medications.

3. Confusion

Confusion caused by Passiflora Incarnata refers to a state of mental confusion or disorientation that may occur in some individuals who consume this herb.

The main active constituents in Passiflora incarnata that can cause this side effect include various flavonoids, such as apigenin, luteolin, and quercetin, as well as harmala alkaloids, such as harman and harmaline. This confusion can occur when Passiflora incarnata is prepared as a tea or tincture and consumed in large quantities, or when it is misused in other forms. Elderly individuals, those with a history of mental health issues, and those taking central nervous system depressants or other medications that interact with Passiflora incarnata may be more likely to experience this side effect.

To avoid confusion, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and usage instructions for Passiflora incarnata, and to consult with a healthcare provider before using this herb if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking other medications.

4. Coordination Problems

Coordination problems caused by Passiflora Incarnata refers to difficulty in coordinating muscle movements as a result of using this herb.

The main active constituents in Passiflora Incarnata that can cause this side effect are alkaloids, flavonoids, and passiflora glycosides. These components can affect the central nervous system and cause impaired muscle coordination when taken in large doses or when used for extended periods.

Medicinal preparations such as teas, capsules, and liquid extracts, if misused or taken in excessive amounts, can lead to coordination problems. Individuals who are more susceptible to this side effect include those with pre-existing neurological conditions, pregnant women, and individuals taking central nervous system depressants.

To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to follow the recommended dosage on the product label, consult with a healthcare professional before use, and avoid combining it with other CNS depressants.

5. Stomach Upsets

Stomach upsets caused by Passiflora Incarnata refers to a range of digestive disturbances such as nausea, vomiting, bloating, and abdominal discomfort that may occur following the use of this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in Passiflora incarnata that can cause these stomach upsets include various flavonoids, alkaloids, and volatile oils, which can irritate the digestive lining when taken in large quantities or over extended periods. Medicinal preparations such as passionflower tea, tinctures, and capsules, if misused or taken in excessive amounts, can lead to stomach upsets. People who are more susceptible to this side effect include those with a history of gastrointestinal sensitivities, irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), and other digestive disorders, as well as older adults and children.

To avoid stomach upsets, it is recommended to follow the recommended dosage instructions carefully, avoid taking passionflower on an empty stomach, and consult with a healthcare professional before using it in conjunction with other medications or supplements.

6. Nausea

Nausea caused by Passiflora Incarnata refers to a unpleasant sensation of digestive discomfort characterized by an urge to vomit.

This side effect is primarily attributed to the alkaloids, flavonoids, and harman present in the plant. These active constituents can cause gastrointestinal irritation, leading to nausea, particularly when consumed in large amounts or through concentrated extracts.

Those who are sensitive to these compounds or prone to motion sickness are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid nausea, it is recommended to start with a low dose of Passiflora Incarnata and gradually increase it over time, while also staying well-hydrated and avoiding heavy meals before use.

Consult with a healthcare provider before use, and do not use in conjunction with other central nervous system depressants, including alcohol, as this may exacerbate the potential for nausea.

7. Vomiting

Vomiting caused by Passiflora Incarnata refers to the forceful expulsion of stomach contents through the mouth as a result of taking this herbal supplement.

This side effect can be attributed mainly to the alkaloids, flavonoids, and glycosides present in Passiflora Incarnata. The medicinal preparations that can cause vomiting if misused include Passiflora Incarnata teas, capsules, and tinctures. Individuals who have a history of allergic reactions to Passiflora Incarnata or other members of the Passifloraceae family are more likely to experience vomiting as a side effect.

To avoid this adverse reaction, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage and not exceed it, and to consult a healthcare professional before taking Passiflora Incarnata, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking other medications.

8. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Passiflora Incarnata refers to an abnormal increase in the frequency and looseness of stools that may occur as a side effect of using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents of Passiflora Incarnata that can cause this side effect are alkaloids, flavonoids, and harmane alkaloids such as harman and harmaline. These constituents can stimulate the gastrointestinal tract and increase intestinal motility, leading to diarrhea if misused.

People who are more likely to experience diarrhea as a side effect of using Passiflora Incarnata include those who are sensitive or allergic to its constituents, those who consume large doses, and those who use it for an extended period without medical supervision. To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and frequency of use, consult with a healthcare professional before using Passiflora Incarnata, and be aware of any changes in bowel movements.

If diarrhea persists, it is recommended to discontinue use and seek medical attention.

9. Changes In Appetite

Changes in appetite caused by Passiflora Incarnata refers to the potential effect of this herb on a person's eating habits, which may include either an increase or decrease in appetite.

The main active constituents of Passiflora incarnata that can cause this side effect include harman alkaloids, such as harmala alkaloids, which can have an impact on neurotransmitter systems in the brain related to appetite regulation. This side effect is most commonly associated with medicinal preparations that include Passiflora incarnata as a primary ingredient, such as teas, capsules, and tinctures. Individuals who are more likely to experience changes in appetite from Passiflora incarnata use include those who are sensitive to harman alkaloids or who consume large quantities of the herb.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to follow the recommended dosage instructions carefully and consult with a healthcare provider before using Passiflora incarnata if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking any medications that may interact with the herb.

10. Saliva Production Increase

Saliva production increase caused by Passiflora Incarnata refers to an excessive production of saliva in the mouth due to the use of this herb.

This effect is primarily caused by the main active constituents of Passiflora incarnata, which include harmane alkaloids such as harmine, harmaline, and harman. These compounds can stimulate the salivary glands and increase saliva production.

Medicinal preparations such as teas, capsules, and tinctures made from the aerial parts of the passionflower plant can cause this side effect if misused or taken in large amounts. Individuals who are more susceptible to this side effect include those who are sensitive to the active compounds in passionflower or those who take higher than recommended doses. To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosage guidelines and consult with a healthcare provider before using passionflower.

Additionally, those who experience excessive saliva production after using passionflower should discontinue use and seek medical attention if necessary.

    10 best herbal teas for dry eyes

    Herbal teas, such as chamomile and calendula, offer a natural solution for individuals suffering from dry eyes.

    Chamomile tea's anti-inflammatory properties assist in reducing eye irritation, while its antioxidants help soothe and refresh the eyes. Calendula tea, rich in flavonoids, promotes hydration, acting as a natural lubricant to alleviate dry eye symptoms.

    Both teas' soothing warmth increases blood flow to the eye area, aiding in the overall health and moisture of the eyes.

    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea aids in alleviating dry eyes due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which originate from the tea's antioxidant-rich composition.

    These antioxidants include flavonoids, primarily apigenin and luteolin, that demonstrate the capacity to reduce inflammation in eye tissues. Furthermore, chamomile tea contains azulene, a natural compound known for its soothing effects on irritated eyes. The aqueous chamomile extract, when applied topically or consumed, encourages the production of meibum, a lipid crucial for maintaining the tear film's stability.

    Consequently, chamomile tea helps prevent dry eyes by promoting tear film quality and reducing inflammation in eye tissues.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with dry eyes.

    • Flowers: Contain flavonoids that have anti-inflammatory properties, helping to soothe dry and irritated eyes.
    • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and tannins that can help relieve redness and alleviate discomfort caused by dry eyes.
    • Seeds: Source of essential oils with soothing and moisturizing effects, providing relief for dry eyes.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with dry eyes.

    • Apigenin: A flavonoid that has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the eyes and provide relief from dry eye symptoms.
    • Luteolin: A flavonoid that functions as an antioxidant, protecting the eyes from oxidative stress and reducing the risk of dry eye complications.
    • Chamazulene: A terpene that has anti-inflammatory and soothing effects, helping to alleviate dry eye symptoms by reducing inflammation and irritation.
    • Bisabolol: A terpene alcohol that has anti-inflammatory, anti-irritant, and healing properties, helping to soothe and moisturize dry and irritated eyes.
    • Matricin: A lactone that has anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving effects, helping to reduce dry eye symptoms and providing relief from discomfort.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for dry eyes.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water in a kettle or pot, reaching a temperature of 212°F (100°C) for 1-2 minutes.
    2. Add 1-2 teaspoons (2-4 grams) of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or strainer.
    3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers and steep for 5-7 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
    4. Remove the tea infuser or strainer to prevent over-steeping and bitterness.
    5. Allow the chamomile tea to cool down to a warm temperature, then drink slowly to soothe and moisturize dry eyes.

    2. Eyebright

    Eyebright tea brings relief to dry eyes due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties derived from tannins and flavonoids present in the plant.

    These compounds aid in reducing eye redness, irritation, and tearing by constricting blood vessels and protecting the eye's surface. Furthermore, eyebright tea contains rosmarinic acid, which has been shown to alleviate symptoms of allergic conjunctivitis, a common cause of dry eyes. Drinking eyebright tea or using it as a compress provides a soothing, natural remedy for dry eyes, improving overall eye comfort.

    Its long-standing traditional use and recent scientific studies support the tea's effectiveness in addressing dry eye conditions.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in eyebright tea that help with dry eyes.

    • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and tannins that soothe and moisturize dry eyes.
    • Seeds: Have high levels of essential fatty acids, promoting eye lubrication and overall eye health.
    • Stems: Contain astringent properties that help relieve redness and dryness in the eyes.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in eyebright tea that aid with dry eyes.

    • Aucubin: A powerful antioxidant that helps reduce eye inflammation and irritation, providing relief for dry eyes.
    • Flavonoids: These anti-inflammatory compounds help alleviate dry eye symptoms by reducing redness and irritation.
    • Tannins: Known for their astringent properties, tannins help to tighten and strengthen eye tissues, providing temporary relief for dry eyes.
    • Phenolic acids: With antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, phenolic acids help protect the eyes from oxidative stress and irritation caused by dry eyes.
    • Essential oils: These oils have anti-inflammatory and soothing effects, helping to alleviate dry eye symptoms and promote overall eye health.
    • Terpenes: Known for their calming and anti-inflammatory effects, terpenes help reduce redness, itchiness, and swelling associated with dry eyes.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic eyebright tea for dry eyes.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water for 3-5 minutes, then pour over 1 teaspoon of dried eyebright herb.
    2. Steep the herb in hot water for 10-15 minutes to extract its beneficial compounds.
    3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used herb solid residues.
    4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste, mixing well until fully dissolved.
    5. Drink the tea while warm, 2-3 times a day, to alleviate dry eye symptoms.

    10 best herbal teas for grey hair

    Here are three exceptional herbal teas for grey hair: rosemary, sage, and nettle.

    Rich in antioxidants, these teas promote hair growth and reduce graying. Rosemary tea improves circulation and nourishes the scalp, while sage tea helps darken hair by stimulating melanin production. Nettle tea, with its high mineral content, strengthens hair and prevents breakage.

    Regular consumption of these herbal teas delivers essential nutrients to the hair follicles and slows down the graying process.

    1. Rosemary

    Rosemary tea promotes hair growth and reduces grey hair by stimulating blood circulation in the scalp.

    This herbal tea contains carnosic acid and rosmarinic acid, known to reverse graying by preserving melanin, the pigment responsible for hair color. Moreover, rosemary tea delivers antioxidants that repair cellular damage and support keratin production for healthy hair growth. Regular consumption of rosemary tea provides essential minerals, vitamins, and antioxidants that nourish hair follicles and maintain natural hair color.

    Additionally, rosemary tea's anti-inflammatory properties alleviate scalp irritations and create an environment conducive to hair re-pigmentation.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in rosemary tea that help with grey hair.

    • Leaves: Contain carnosic acid and rosemarinic acid, which have antioxidant properties that support hair health and may help maintain natural hair color.
    • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants and essential oils, flowers help promote hair growth and can potentially delay the onset of graying.
    • Stems: Contain volatile oils and tannins that may support hair health and help prevent premature graying.
    • Seeds: Packed with nutrients and antioxidants, seeds can help nourish the scalp, promote hair growth, and reduce graying.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in rosemary tea that aid with grey hair.

    • Rosmarinic acid: A potent antioxidant that helps protect hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, promoting healthy hair growth and reducing the likelihood of graying.
    • Carnosic acid: A compound with anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce scalp inflammation, improving overall hair health and potentially slowing down the graying process.
    • Ursolic acid: A natural triterpene that stimulates hair growth by promoting skin cell renewal and increasing epidermal thickness, which can help maintain hair color.
    • Caffeic acid: A phenolic acid that enhances hair growth by inhibiting the enzyme responsible for converting testosterone into dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a hormone linked to hair graying and loss.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic rosemary tea for grey hair.

    1. Gather 3-4 fresh rosemary sprigs and rinse them under cold water.
    2. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot, then add the rosemary sprigs.
    3. Reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
    4. Strain the tea to remove rosemary sprigs and let it cool.
    5. Consume 1 cup of warm rosemary tea daily, or store the remaining tea in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

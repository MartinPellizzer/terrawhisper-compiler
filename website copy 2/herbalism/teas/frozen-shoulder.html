    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic willow tea for frozen shoulder.

    1. Gather 2-3 willow bark branches and gently peel off the outer bark, exposing the inner bark.
    2. Chop the inner bark into small pieces, place them in a pot, and add 4 cups of water.
    3. Simmer the mixture on low heat for 15-20 minutes, allowing the water to extract the bark's properties.
    4. Strain the tea into a container, discarding the solid remains, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
    5. Consume 1 cup of willow tea, 3 times a day, until symptoms of frozen shoulder improve.

    What is frozen shoulder and how can it affect your life?

    Frozen shoulder is a condition characterized by stiffness and pain in the shoulder joint, resulting from the thickening and tightening of the capsule surrounding the shoulder.

    This ailment restricts motion, making it challenging for individuals to perform daily activities such as dressing, driving, and even sleeping comfortably.

    The impact on people's lives is significant, often causing frustration, decreased productivity, and potential social isolation due to the limitations and discomfort associated with this condition.

    What other natural remedies help with frozen shoulder?

    One of the most common and effective natural remedies for frozen shoulder is regular exercise and physical therapy.

    These methods involve a series of stretches and movements designed to improve mobility and reduce stiffness in the shoulder joint. Another popular natural treatment is heat therapy, which involves applying heat to the affected area to alleviate pain and promote healing.

    This can be done using a heating pad, hot water bottle, or warm compress. Cold therapy, on the other hand, involves applying ice to the shoulder to reduce inflammation and numb the pain.

    It is important to note that while these natural remedies can be effective, they should not replace medical treatment or the advice of a healthcare professional.

    10 best herbal teas for pimples

    Certain herbal teas, like chamomile and mint, offer proven benefits for pimples.

    Chamomile tea's anti-inflammatory properties reduce redness and swelling, while mint tea's antiseptic qualities eliminate acne-causing bacteria. Regular consumption of these herbal teas, in addition to a consistent skincare routine, promotes clearer, healthier skin.

    Lavender and green tea are two other excellent options, as they boast similar skin-boosting benefits.

    1. Green Tea

    Green tea helps with pimples due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which reduce inflammation and fight the bacteria that cause pimples.

    The main compound responsible, epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), decreases sebum production, making the skin less oily and less prone to pimples. Moreover, EGCG promotes the death of skin cells that contribute to clogged pores, preventing the formation of new pimples. Green tea's high antioxidant content also protects the skin from damage caused by free radicals, supporting overall skin health and reducing pimple occurrence.

    Regular consumption of green tea or topical application of green tea extracts delivers these benefits, addressing pimples effectively.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in green tea tea that help with pimples.

    • Leaves: Contain antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce redness and soothe irritated skin.
    • Buds: Rich in polyphenols and catechins that help fight acne-causing bacteria and reduce excess oil production.
    • Seeds: Packed with vitamin E and essential fatty acids that help moisturize and promote skin healing.
    • Rhyzomes: Contain antimicrobial compounds that help eliminate acne-causing bacteria and reduce inflammation.
    • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants that help protect skin from damage and reduce the appearance of pimples.
    • Bark: Has tannins and flavonoids that help reduce sebum production and soothe irritated skin.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in green tea tea that aid with pimples.

    • Caffeine: Acts as a vasoconstrictor, reducing inflammation in pimples.
    • Epigallocatechin gallate: Possesses antibacterial properties, helping to eliminate acne-causing bacteria.
    • Theaflavins: Exhibit anti-inflammatory effects, reducing redness and swelling in pimples.
    • Theanine: Promotes relaxation and reduces stress, which can help prevent pimples caused by hormonal fluctuations.
    • L-Theanine: Enhances immune function, aiding in the body's defense against acne-causing bacteria.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic green tea tea for pimples.

    1. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool to 160-170°F, typically requiring a 5-minute rest.
    2. Pour the heated water over one teaspoon of high-quality green tea leaves.
    3. Steep the tea for 2-3 minutes, allowing the nutrients to extract without becoming bitter.
    4. Strain the tea leaves, reserving the green tea.
    5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, promoting skin health and reducing pimples.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for pimples.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water and add 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers, letting it steep for 10 minutes.
    2. Strain the tea to remove the flowers, leaving only the liquid.
    3. Allow the chamomile tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature.
    4. Cleanse your face and apply the cooled chamomile tea to affected areas using a cotton ball.
    5. Leave the tea on your skin for 15 minutes before rinsing with warm water and patting dry.

    3. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes.
    4. Strain the tea to remove the burdock root pieces, then let it cool down to drinking temperature.
    5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, preferably before meals, for best results in reducing pimples.

    2. Separate the yellow dandelion petals from the green calyxes and stems, discarding the latter.

    5. Pour the tea through a fine-mesh strainer into a mug, discarding the used petals, and enjoy 1-2 times daily for best results.

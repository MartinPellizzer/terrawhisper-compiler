    1. Saw Palmetto

    Saw palmetto tea addresses baldness by addressing its root cause, hormonal imbalance.

    This tea contains berries that inhibit 5-alpha-reductase, an enzyme that converts testosterone into DHT, a hormone responsible for hair loss. By reducing DHT levels, saw palmetto tea promotes hair growth and reduces shedding. Regular consumption of this tea provides the body with essential nutrients, including fatty acids and plant sterols, necessary for maintaining healthy hair follicles.

    Consequently, saw palmetto tea strengthens hair, slows hair loss, and encourages new growth.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in saw palmetto tea that help with baldness.

    • Berries: Contain fatty acids and phytosterols that can help block 5-alpha-reductase, an enzyme that converts testosterone to DHT, a hormone linked to hair loss.
    • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants that can help protect hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, promoting hair growth and preventing baldness.
    • Roots: Contain high levels of fatty acids, phytosterols, and polysaccharides that can stimulate hair growth, reduce inflammation, and strengthen hair follicles.
    • Rhizomes: Have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce scalp inflammation, improve blood circulation, and promote healthy hair growth.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in saw palmetto tea that aid with baldness.

    • Fatty Acids: These provide anti-inflammatory effects, helping to reduce inflammation that can lead to hair loss.
    • Sterols: They inhibit the conversion of testosterone to DHT, a hormone responsible for male pattern baldness.
    • Flavonoids: They have antioxidant properties, helping to protect hair follicles from damage.
    • Phenolic Acids: They stimulate hair growth by increasing blood flow to the scalp.
    • Tannins: They possess astringent properties that can help to strengthen hair follicles and reduce hair fall.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic saw palmetto tea for baldness.

    1. Heat 2 cups of water to boiling point in a pot, takes about 5-7 minutes.
    2. Add 2 tablespoons of dried saw palmetto berries, let steep for 10-15 minutes.
    3. Strain the tea to remove the berries, discard solids.
    4. Stir in honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
    5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day for best results.

    5. Pour the pygeum tea into a cup and drink it while warm, up to three times a day.

    4. Steep the tea for 2-3 minutes, allowing the nutrients to infuse.
    5. Strain the tea into a separate cup, discarding the leaves, and consume immediately. Repeat this process 2-3 times daily for best results.

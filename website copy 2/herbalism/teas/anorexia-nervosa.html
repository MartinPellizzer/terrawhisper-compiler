    10 best herbal teas for anorexia nervosa

    Herbal teas, such as chamomile and peppermint, offer a natural approach to soothing digestive discomfort, a common symptom in individuals with anorexia nervosa.

    Chamomile tea has calming properties that can help reduce anxiety, often associated with this eating disorder. Peppermint tea, on the other hand, aids in alleviating bloating and gas, improving overall gastrointestinal function. Both teas contribute to promoting a sense of well-being and encourage a healthier relationship with food.

    Moreover, their soothing warmth and comforting aroma make them an ideal choice for individuals seeking solace and relaxation during their recovery journey.

    1. Peppermint

    Peppermint tea aids digestion, alleviates nausea, and stimulates appetite, all of which benefit individuals with anorexia nervosa.

    The tea's soothing properties ease anxiety and stress, common triggers of anorexia. Furthermore, its caffeine-free nature promotes sleep and rest, which are crucial for recovery. Peppermint tea's refreshing taste makes it an appealing alternative to other beverages, encouraging hydration and nutrition intake.

    Lastly, it contains no sugars or additives, making it a healthy choice for those with anorexia nervosa.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in peppermint tea that help with anorexia nervosa.

    • Leaves: Contain volatile oils and antispasmodic properties that help stimulate appetite.
    • Flowers: Contain menthol, a compound that helps with digestion and reduces nausea.
    • Stems: Contain rosmarinic acid, a natural anti-inflammatory that aids in digestion and alleviates symptoms of anorexia.
    • Seeds: High in antioxidants, seeds improve overall health and well-being, providing strength and energy during recovery.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in peppermint tea that aid with anorexia nervosa.

    • Menthol: Acts as a potent appetite stimulant, helping to combat weight loss in anorexia nervosa.
    • Menthone: Improves digestion and absorption, addressing gastrointestinal issues commonly found in anorexia nervosa.
    • Limonene: Exhibits antidepressant and anxiolytic properties, helping to alleviate mental health aspects of anorexia nervosa.
    • Eugenol: Possesses anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, reducing physical discomfort often associated with the condition.
    • Rosmarinic acid: Regulates neurotransmitter levels, potentially aiding in the management of anxiety and depression related to anorexia nervosa.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for anorexia nervosa.

    1. Boil 8 ounces of water to a rolling boil, which typically takes 3-5 minutes on a stovetop.
    2. Crush or tear 10 fresh peppermint leaves, releasing their oils, and add them to a teapot or infuser.
    3. Pour the boiling water over the peppermint leaves and let them steep for 5-7 minutes.
    4. Strain the peppermint tea to remove the leaves, then add honey to taste, if desired.
    5. Serve the hot peppermint tea immediately, sipping slowly to soothe the stomach and promote relaxation.

    2. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea has established therapeutic benefits that address several factors contributing to anorexia nervosa.

    First, chamomile contains apigenin, a bioactive compound with documented appetite-stimulating properties. Second, it has demonstrated effectiveness in alleviating stress and anxiety, common in individuals with anorexia nervosa.

    Third, chamomile's anti-inflammatory properties contribute to overall gut health, promoting better nutrient absorption. Fourth, it enhances sleep quality, which is crucial for maintaining a healthy metabolism and appetite regulation.

    Lastly, chamomile tea's pleasant aroma and taste can encourage a healthier relationship with food, diminishing aversion to eating.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with anorexia nervosa.

    • Flowers: Contain volatile oils and antioxidants that can stimulate appetite and digestion.
    • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids and terpenoids that promote relaxation and stress reduction, aiding in appetite improvement.
    • Seeds: Packed with essential oils and lipophilic compounds that help alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort and encourage food intake.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with anorexia nervosa.

    • Apigenin: A flavonoid that binds to benzodiazepine receptors in the brain, helping to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation, which can be beneficial for anorexia nervosa patients as anxiety and stress often contribute to the development and maintenance of the disorder.
    • Chamazulene: A chemical compound responsible for chamomile's anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract, alleviating symptoms such as abdominal pain and bloating that may be present in anorexia nervosa.
    • Alpha-bisabolol: A sesquiterpene alcohol with anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing properties, which can aid in soothing irritated mucous membranes and promoting the healing of any damage caused by excessive vomiting or laxative abuse in anorexia nervosa.
    • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin exhibits antispasmodic effects, which can help alleviate muscle spasms and cramps, common in anorexia nervosa patients due to malnutrition and electrolyte imbalances.
    • Hesperidin: A flavonoid glycoside that has been shown to improve microcirculation, which can help increase nutrient and oxygen supply to tissues, enhancing overall health and supporting recovery in anorexia nervosa.
    • Rutin: A flavonoid that enhances vitamin C absorption, contributing to collagen synthesis and blood vessel strength, aiding in the healing of any damage from anorexia nervosa-related behaviors and promoting better overall health.
    • Quercetin: A flavonoid with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help protect cells from oxidative stress and reduce systemic inflammation, enhancing overall well-being in anorexia nervosa patients.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for anorexia nervosa.

    1. Boil 8 ounces of water to a rolling boil for 3-5 minutes.
    2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser.
    3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers and let steep for 5-10 minutes.
    4. Remove the tea infuser to prevent over-steeping, which can release bitter flavors.
    5. Drink the chamomile tea slowly, several times a day after meals, to soothe the stomach and stimulate appetite.

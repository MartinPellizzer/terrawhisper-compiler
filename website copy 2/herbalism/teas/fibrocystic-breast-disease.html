    • Iridoids: Iridoids found in chasteberry, such as loganic acid, exhibit anti-proliferative effects, which can help decrease the formation of breast cysts.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chasteberry tea for fibrocystic breast disease.

    1. Choose 2 teaspoons of dried chasteberry leaves and place them in a tea infuser.
    2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the infuser in a cup.
    3. Allow the tea to steep for 10 minutes to extract the medicinal compounds.
    4. Remove the infuser and discard the spent leaves.
    5. Drink the tea, either hot or cooled, up to three times a day for fibrocystic breast disease relief.

    1. Slippery Elm

    • Bark: The bark of the slippery elm plant contains mucilage, a substance that forms a soothing film in the mouth and throat, providing relief from post nasal drip irritation.
    • Leaves: Slippery elm leaves are rich in tannins, which have astringent properties that help reduce inflammation and mucous membrane irritation caused by post nasal drip.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in slippery elm tea that aid with post nasal drip.

    • Mucilage: This gel-like substance can help soothe irritated mucous membranes and reduce inflammation, providing relief for post-nasal drip.
    • Tannins: These astringent compounds can help tighten and dry up mucous membranes, reducing excessive mucus production associated with post-nasal drip.

    • Essential oils: The volatile oils found in slippery elm can have antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic properties, which may relieve symptoms of post-nasal drip.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for post nasal drip.

    1. Measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark.
    2. Place bark in a tea infuser or loose in a teapot.
    3. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the bark.
    4. Steep for 5-10 minutes, then remove the bark.
    5. Drink the tea slowly, up to three times a day for post nasal drip relief.

    What is post nasal drip and how can it affect your life?

    Post nasal drip is a condition where excess mucus from the nose and sinuses drains down the back of the throat, causing persistent irritation and discomfort.

    This constant dripping can lead to coughing, sore throat, and difficulty swallowing, significantly impacting a person's quality of life.

    Sleep disturbances, lack of focus, and reduced productivity at work or school are common among those affected by post nasal drip.

    What other natural remedies help with post nasal drip?

    Post nasal drip, characterized by an excess production of mucus in the back of the throat, often responds well to natural remedies.

    One such remedy is a saline nasal spray, which helps to thin and drain the mucus, reducing congestion. Gargling with warm salt water several times a day is another effective method, as it helps to reduce inflammation and soothe irritation in the throat. Furthermore, increasing hydration by drinking plenty of water, clear broths, and decaffeinated teas can also help to thin the mucus and promote drainage.

    Lastly, using a humidifier in the home or office adds moisture to the air, further helping to alleviate the symptoms of post nasal drip.

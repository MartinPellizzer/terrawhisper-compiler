    10 best herbal teas for fungal skin infection

    Certain herbal teas offer therapeutic properties beneficial in managing fungal skin infections.

    Tea tree oil, known for its antifungal and antimicrobial abilities, is a potent ingredient in some herbal teas. Another effective option is chamomile tea, which contains anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe irritated skin while battling the infection. Additionally, calendula tea, rich in antifungal and antiseptic components, promotes skin healing and provides relief from itching and discomfort caused by fungal infections.

    Regular consumption of these herbal teas, either as a topical application or by drinking them, aids in hastening recovery and preventing the recurrence of fungal skin infections.

    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea contains antifungal properties, specifically chamazulene and bisabolol, which directly combat fungal infections on the skin.

    These compounds target and destroy the cell membranes of fungi, disrupting their growth and spread. Moreover, chamomile tea has anti-inflammatory qualities that soothe irritated skin, reducing redness and swelling caused by the infection. The warm temperature of the tea, when applied topically, further enhances blood flow and aids in delivering the tea's active ingredients deeper into the skin.

    Lastly, chamomile tea boasts antioxidants that bolster the skin's natural defenses, providing an additional layer of protection against fungal infections.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with fungal skin infection.

    • Flowers: Contain several beneficial compounds, such as bisabolol and chamazulene, known to have antifungal properties that help treat fungal skin infections.
    • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids and terpenoids, which possess anti-inflammatory and antifungal qualities, contributing to the treatment of fungal skin infections.
    • Rhyzomes: Contain essential oils with antifungal properties, making it suitable for addressing fungal skin infections.
    • Seeds: Packed with antioxidants and essential oils, offering antifungal benefits needed to manage skin infections caused by fungi.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with fungal skin infection.

    • Alpha-Bisabolol: Possesses anti-fungal properties that directly combat fungal skin infections.
    • Apigenin: Demonstrates ability to inhibit fungal growth and extracellular enzymes.
    • Chamazulene: Exhibits anti-inflammatory effects, reducing skin irritation caused by fungal infections.
    • Matricin: Converts to anti-inflammatory and antiphlogistic compound, alleviating infection symptoms.
    • Quercetin: Offers antioxidant and anti-fungal effects, contributing to overall skin health.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for fungal skin infection.

    1. Measure - Pour 2 cups of boiling water over 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers.
    2. Steep - Let the mixture steep for 10 minutes to release the tea's antifungal properties.
    3. Strain - Strain the mixture to remove the chamomile flowers, leaving behind only the liquid.
    4. Cool - Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature before applying it to the skin.
    5. Apply - Soak a clean cloth in the tea and apply it to the affected area for 15 minutes, up to 3 times a day.

    • Flowers: Contain high levels of antifungal compounds, making them effective in treating fungal skin infections.
    • Leaves: Rich in soothing and anti-inflammatory properties, helping to alleviate skin irritation caused by fungal infections.
    • Stems: Possess mild antifungal properties, contributing to the overall effectiveness of the medicinal tea.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in calendula tea that aid with fungal skin infection.

    • Flavonoids: Possess antifungal properties that help to fight off fungal skin infections.
    • Saponins: Contribute to the formation of a protective barrier on the skin, preventing fungal growth.
    • Carotenoids: Boost skin's immune response, making it more resistant to fungal skin infections.
    • Triterpenes: Demonstrate antifungal activities that help to combat and heal fungal skin infections.
    • Volatile oils: Provide a soothing effect on the skin while also inhibiting the growth of fungi.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic calendula tea for fungal skin infection.

    1. Gather 2-3 tablespoons of dried calendula petals and place them in a heat-resistant container.
    2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the calendula petals.
    3. Allow the mixture to steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain out the petals.
    4. Let the tea cool down to a comfortable temperature.
    5. Apply the tea to the affected area using a clean cloth, allowing it to soak for 10-15 minutes before rinsing off.

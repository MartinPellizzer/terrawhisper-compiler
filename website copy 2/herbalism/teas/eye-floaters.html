    What is eye floaters and how can it affect your life?

    Eye floaters are small, dark shapes that drift across a person's vision.

    These shapes can appear as spots, threads, or cobwebs and are caused by tiny clumps of cells or debris in the vitreous gel inside the eye. While they are usually harmless, they can be distracting and affect a person's ability to focus or perform visual tasks. In rare cases, a sudden increase in floaters or the presence of flashes of light can indicate a serious underlying eye condition, such as a retinal tear or detachment.

    Therefore, it is important for individuals with sudden changes in their floaters to seek medical attention promptly.

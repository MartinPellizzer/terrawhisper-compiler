    10 best herbal teas for eye pain

    Several herbal teas alleviate eye pain by reducing inflammation and strain.

    Chamomile tea, with its anti-inflammatory properties, soothes irritated eyes. Meanwhile, calendula tea, rich in antioxidants, helps treat eye inflammation and infections.

    Eyebright tea, as the name suggests, is an ideal choice for eye-related issues, as it boasts potent astringent and anti-inflammatory qualities. Lastly, fennel tea, containing flavonoids, offers relief from eye strain and fatigue.

    These teas, when consumed regularly, contribute to overall eye health and help minimize discomfort.

    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea aids eye pain through its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling and irritation.

    This herbal tea contains antioxidants that help soothe tired eyes and promote relaxation. The compounds apigenin and luteolin in chamomile tea possess analgesic qualities, alleviating discomfort. Furthermore, chamomile tea's warmth enhances blood circulation, ensuring oxygen and nutrient delivery to eye tissues.

    Finally, drinking chamomile tea contributes to overall body relaxation, diminishing stress-related eye strain.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with eye pain.

    • Flowers: Contain antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties that help alleviate eye pain.
    • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids that possess soothing effects beneficial for treating eye discomfort.
    • Seeds: High in antioxidant and essential oil content that promotes relaxation and reduces eye pain.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with eye pain.

    • Apigenin: A flavonoid that possesses antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, helping to reduce eye pain and irritation.
    • Luteolin: A flavonoid with potent anti-inflammatory effects, aiding in the alleviation of eye pain and swelling.
    • Chamazulene: A compound formed during steam distillation with anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties, beneficial for soothing eye discomfort.
    • Bisabolol: A terpene alcohol with anti-inflammatory, anti-irritant, and analgesic effects, helping to reduce eye pain and inflammation.
    • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, exhibiting anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties, contributing to the relief of eye pain.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for eye pain.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water in a teapot or saucepan for 3-5 minutes.
    2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot.
    3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and steep for 5-10 minutes.
    4. Strain the tea to remove the spent chamomile flowers.
    5. Let the tea cool down to a comfortable temperature before using it as a compress for eye pain.

    2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried ginkgo biloba leaves to the boiling water.
    3. Steep the tea for 5-10 minutes, then remove the leaves with a strainer.
    4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
    5. Drink the tea while warm, 2-3 times a day for eye pain relief.

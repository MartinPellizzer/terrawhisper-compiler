    What is gout and how can it affect your life?

    Gout is a form of arthritis that develops when uric acid crystals accumulate in joints, causing extreme pain, swelling, and inflammation.

    This disorder predominantly affects the big toe but can also impact other joints, leading to limited mobility and a reduced quality of life. Prolonged gout episodes heighten the risk of joint damage, resulting in long-term disability.

    Gout primarily impacts middle-aged adults, particularly those with genetic predispositions or unhealthy dietary habits, making it essential to seek medical attention to manage symptoms and prevent complications.

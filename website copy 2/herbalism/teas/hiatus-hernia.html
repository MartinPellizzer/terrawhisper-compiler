    What is hiatus hernia and how can it affect your life?

    A hiatus hernia is a medical condition in which a portion of the stomach protrudes into the chest cavity through an opening in the diaphragm, known as the hiatus.

    This condition can lead to acid reflux, heartburn, and difficulty swallowing, significantly impacting a person's quality of life. Moreover, it can cause discomfort or pain in the chest or upper abdomen, leading to distress and decreased appetite.

    As a result, people with hiatus hernia often experience weight loss, fatigue, and poor digestion, leading to a reduced overall well-being.

    What other natural remedies help with hiatus hernia?

    Slippery elm, a type of tree bark, is a common and effective natural remedy for hiatal hernia.

    It contains a substance called mucilage, which becomes slippery when mixed with water, providing a protective barrier to the esophagus and reducing irritation. Deglycyrrhizinated licorice (DGL), a form of licorice that has had the glycyrrhizin compound removed, is another effective natural remedy.

    Glycyrrhizin can raise blood pressure, and its removal in DGL eliminates this risk. DGL increases mucus production, which soothes and protects the digestive tract, reducing symptoms of hiatal hernia.

    Additionally, practicing good posture and eating smaller, more frequent meals can alleviate symptoms and aid in the healing process.

    1. Elderberry

    • Tannins: Tannins have astringent properties that can help soothe inflammation and sore throats, providing relief from flu symptoms.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic elderberry tea for flu.

    1. In a saucepan, combine 1 cup of elderberries, 3 cups of water, and 2 inches of sliced ginger root; bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
    2. Strain the mixture to remove solids, then stir in honey to taste and let cool slightly.
    3. Add the juice of 1 lemon and 1/4 teaspoon of cinnamon to the tea.
    4. Pour the tea into a mug and optionally top off with more hot water to desired strength.
    5. Sip and enjoy for soothing flu relief.

    What is flu and how can it affect your life?

    Influenza, or flu, is a highly contagious respiratory illness brought about by the influenza virus.

    It spreads easily through the air when infected individuals cough, sneeze, or talk, releasing viral particles that other people can inhale. Once the virus enters the body, it attacks the respiratory system, causing symptoms like fever, cough, sore throat, runny or stuffy nose, muscle or body aches, headaches, and fatigue. Flu complications range from ear and sinus infections to pneumonia and even death, mostly affecting older adults, young children, pregnant women, and people with certain chronic medical conditions.

    The illness impacts people's lives significantly, causing missed work or school days, posing severe health risks, and placing a substantial burden on healthcare systems.

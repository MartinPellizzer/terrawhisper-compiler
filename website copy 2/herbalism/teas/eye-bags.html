    10 best herbal teas for eye bags

    Certain herbal teas, such as chamomile and green tea, possess anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce eye puffiness.

    Chamomile tea, with its soothing effects, relaxes the blood vessels around the eyes, lessening the appearance of bags. Green tea, rich in antioxidants, combats fluid retention and constricts blood vessels, further alleviating eye puffiness.

    Regularly applying these teas as cool compresses delivers optimal results.

    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea contains anti-inflammatory compounds, particularly alpha-bisabolol and apigenin, which reduce swelling and puffiness in the eye area.

    The antioxidants present in chamomile tea, such as flavonoids and terpenoids, help fight free radicals that cause skin damage, promoting healthy skin around the eyes. Its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties tighten the skin, diminishing the appearance of eye bags. Furthermore, consuming chamomile tea can also aid in stress reduction, as stress can exacerbate eye bags and other skin issues.

    As a natural topical remedy, chamomile tea bags, when applied to the eyes, provide soothing relief and actively lessen eye bags due to their cooling and decongestant effects.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with eye bags.

    • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce puffiness and soothe tired eyes.
    • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants that can help alleviate dark circles and promote healthy skin around the eyes.
    • Seeds: Packed with essential oils that possess calming and relaxing effects, helping to decrease eye strain and discomfort.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with eye bags.

    • Apigenin: A flavonoid that reduces inflammation, helping to decrease puffiness around the eyes.
    • Luteolin: An antioxidant flavonoid that helps reduce fluid retention, minimizing the appearance of eye bags.
    • Chamazulene: A chemical compound with anti-inflammatory properties, helping alleviate swelling and irritation in the eye area.
    • Bisabolol: A terpene with soothing and calming effects, reducing redness and puffiness around the eyes.
    • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin provides anti-inflammatory benefits, aiding in the reduction of eye bags.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for eye bags.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water and let 1 chamomile tea bag steep for 5 minutes.
    2. Remove tea bag and allow it to cool down to a comfortable temperature.
    3. Soak two tea bags in the cooled chamomile tea for 15 minutes.
    4. Place one soaked tea bag over each eye, ensuring full contact with the skin.
    5. Relax for 15-20 minutes, allowing the chamomile tea to reduce puffiness and soothe the eye area.

    • Flavonoids: These antioxidants reduce damage from environmental stressors that can lead to under-eye bags.
    • Tannins: Astringent tannins help tighten skin and decrease puffiness in eye bags.
    • Phenolic acids: Anti-inflammatory properties of phenolic acids help soothe irritation and reduce eye bag inflammation.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic eyebright tea for eye bags.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water and add 1 teaspoon of dried eyebright herb, simmer for 10 minutes.
    2. Strain the tea to remove the herb and let it cool down.
    3. Soak 2 cotton balls in the cooled eyebright tea.
    4. Place the soaked cotton balls on your eye bags for 10 minutes.
    5. Discard the cotton balls and repeat the process up to 3 times a day.

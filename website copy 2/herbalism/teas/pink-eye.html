    10 best herbal teas for pink eye

    Several herbal teas offer relief from pink eye symptoms due to their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and soothing properties.

    Chamomile tea, known for its calming effects, can help reduce inflammation and irritation. Calendula tea, made from marigold flowers, has antimicrobial properties that can help fight bacterial infections causing pink eye. Eyebright tea, rich in bioflavonoids and tannins, has astringent and anti-inflammatory effects that can alleviate pink eye symptoms.

    Drinking these herbal teas or using them as warm compresses can provide comfort and promote healing.

    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea contains active compounds like chamazulene and bisabolol, which possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it an effective remedy for pink eye.

    These compounds help reduce swelling, redness, and irritation by constricting the blood vessels in the eye's sensitive tissues. Additionally, chamomile's antimicrobial properties inhibit bacterial growth and can help prevent the spread of infection. To use chamomile tea for pink eye, simply soak a clean cotton ball in cooled chamomile tea and gently apply it to the affected eye, repeating this process several times a day for relief.

    These well-documented benefits make chamomile tea a valuable natural treatment for pink eye, offering quick and soothing results.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with pink eye.

    • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe irritation in pink eye.
    • Leaves: Rich in antimicrobial compounds that can help fight pink eye-causing bacteria.
    • Seeds: Contain antioxidants that can support eye health and reduce inflammation in pink eye.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with pink eye.

    • Chamazulene: Has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce irritation and swelling in the eye.
    • Bisabolol: Possesses antibacterial and antifungal properties that help fight the infection causing pink eye.
    • Matricin: Gets converted to chamazulene, which has anti-inflammatory effects to soothe pink eye symptoms.
    • Apigenin: Acts as an antioxidant and has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate pink eye irritations.
    • Quercetin: Exhibits anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antihistamine properties, which can help reduce redness, swelling, and itching in pink eye.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for pink eye.

    1. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot for 5 minutes.
    2. Add 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to the boiling water.
    3. Steep the chamomile in the water for 10 minutes.
    4. Strain the chamomile tea into a clean glass.
    5. Let the tea cool down to a warm temperature before using it as a compress on the affected eye for 15 minutes, 3 times a day.

    10 best herbal teas for gum swelling

    Herbal teas, renowned for their anti-inflammatory properties, offer a natural remedy for gum swelling.

    Two of the best options are chamomile and calendula teas. Chamomile contains flavonoids and essential oils that reduce inflammation, while calendula, rich in triterpenes and flavonoids, boasts potent anti-inflammatory effects. To maximize benefits, steep one to two teaspoons of dried herbs in hot water for 5-10 minutes before consumption.

    Regularly drinking these herbal teas helps alleviate gum swelling, promoting better oral health.

    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea contains anti-inflammatory compounds, specifically azulene and levomenol, which reduce inflammation.

    These compounds inhibit the production of prostaglandins and nitric oxide, chemicals that cause swelling. Chamomile tea's antimicrobial properties also combat harmful bacteria in the mouth, preventing gum infections that lead to swelling. Furthermore, chamomile's soothing effects calm irritated gums, promoting healing.

    Drinking chamomile tea regularly strengthens gums and maintains oral health, reducing the likelihood of gum swelling.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with gum swelling.

    • Flowers: Contain flavonoids and terpenoids that possess anti-inflammatory properties, helping reduce gum swelling.
    • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and essential oils, leaves aid in soothing irritated gums and alleviating inflammation.
    • Stems: Contain therapeutic compounds, including chamazulene, that reduce inflammation, promoting gum health.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with gum swelling.

    • Alpha-Bisabolol: A powerful anti-inflammatory compound that helps reduce gum swelling by decreasing inflammation.
    • Apigenin: A flavonoid with potent anti-inflammatory properties that alleviates gum swelling by inhibiting the production of inflammatory molecules.
    • Chamazulene: A volatile oil that exhibits strong anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, reducing gum swelling by protecting tissues from oxidative stress.
    • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, helping to minimize gum swelling and discomfort.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for gum swelling.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water in a kettle or pot for 3-5 minutes.
    2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot.
    3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and steep for 5-10 minutes.
    4. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers and discard them.
    5. Drink the chamomile tea while it's still warm, up to 3 times a day.

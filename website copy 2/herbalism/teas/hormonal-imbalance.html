    Holy basil tea, also called tulsi tea, holds stress-reducing properties that aid the body in managing cortisol levels, promoting overall hormonal balance.

    1. Chasteberry

    Chasteberry tea helps with hormonal imbalance because it contains active compounds that regulate hormone levels.

    This tea stimulates the pituitary gland to produce and release more follicle-stimulating hormones (FSH) and luteinizing hormones (LH), promoting hormonal balance. By consuming chasteberry tea, women experience a reduction in symptoms related to hormonal imbalance, such as mood swings, bloating, and irregular menstrual cycles. Chasteberry tea's hormone-balancing effects result from its natural phytoestrogen content, which mimics estrogen in the body, aiding in hormone regulation.

    Additionally, this herbal tea has a long history of traditional use in treating hormonal issues, supporting its effectiveness in addressing hormonal imbalance.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chasteberry tea that help with hormonal imbalance.

    • Berries: Contain high levels of antioxidants and help regulate hormonal imbalances.
    • Seeds: Rich in essential fatty acids, promoting hormonal balance and alleviating menstrual symptoms.
    • Leaves: Contain plant sterols that can mimic and support the functions of female hormones.
    • Flowers: High in essential oils, working to reduce hormonal imbalances and support overall reproductive health.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chasteberry tea that aid with hormonal imbalance.

    • Diterpenes: These compounds help to normalize the levels of hormones such as progesterone and estrogen, thereby reducing symptoms of hormonal imbalance.
    • Flavonoids: These antioxidants help to reduce inflammation and regulate hormonal activity, contributing to overall hormonal balance.
    • Phenolic acids: These compounds exhibit estrogenic and anti-progestogenic properties, aiding in the regulation of menstrual cycles and easing symptoms of hormonal imbalance.
    • Iridoid glycosides: These constituents help to promote the production and release of luteinizing hormone (LH) and follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH), supporting balanced hormone levels.
    • Essential oils: These oils contain phytoestrogens that mimic the effects of estrogen in the body, helping to restore hormonal balance and alleviate symptoms of hormonal imbalance.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chasteberry tea for hormonal imbalance.

    1. Boil 8 ounces of water to a rolling boil, then pour over 1 tablespoon of dried chasteberry leaves.
    2. Steep the chasteberry leaves in the hot water for 10-15 minutes to extract the medicinal compounds.
    3. Strain the tea to remove the spent chasteberry leaves, leaving behind only the liquid.
    4. Drink the chasteberry tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day for hormonal balance.
    5. Discard any remaining tea after 24 hours to ensure freshness and potency.

    6. Licorice

    Licorice tea contains an active compound called glycyrrhizin, which aids in maintaining hormonal balance by inhibiting the enzyme 11-beta-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase type 2.

    This enzyme's function is to convert cortisol, the stress hormone, into cortisone. By inhibiting this enzyme, glycyrrhizin helps prolong the effects of cortisol, reducing the production of excessive cortisol and other hormones. This results in normalized hormone levels and improved overall endocrine function.

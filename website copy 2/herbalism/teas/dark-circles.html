    10 best herbal teas for dark circles

    Certain herbal teas, such as chamomile and lavender, have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce puffiness associated with dark circles.

    Green tea, rich in antioxidants, helps tighten the skin and diminish the appearance of dark circles. Rooibos tea, known for its high levels of vitamin C, aids in collagen production and brightens the skin.

    Drinking these herbal teas or using them as compresses on the affected area provides a natural remedy for dark circles.

    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea contains anti-inflammatory properties due to its high antioxidant content, which helps reduce swelling and puffiness around the eyes, a common cause of dark circles.

    The soothing effects of chamomile also help constrict blood vessels, diminishing the appearance of darkness. As a natural relaxant, chamomile promotes better sleep, contributing to overall skin health and a reduction in dark circles. Furthermore, chamomile's anti-bacterial and hydrating properties support the skin's natural regeneration process, ensuring a healthier and more even skin tone.

    Thus, regularly consuming chamomile tea and applying it topically delivers noticeable improvements in the appearance of dark circles.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with dark circles.

    • Flowers: Contain antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce puffiness and discoloration.
    • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids that strengthen capillary walls, improving circulation and alleviating dark circles.
    • Seeds: Packed with essential oils that have anti-inflammatory and skin-soothing benefits for dark circle reduction.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with dark circles.

    • Apigenin: A flavonoid that has anti-inflammatory properties, reducing puffiness and dark circles.
    • Luteolin: A flavonoid with antioxidant properties that helps protect and repair damaged skin.
    • Chamazulene: A terpene that has anti-inflammatory and soothing effects, reducing redness and irritation.
    • Bisabolol: A terpene alcohol with anti-inflammatory and skin healing properties, improving skin texture and tone.
    • Matricin: A sesquiterpene lactone with anti-inflammatory effects, reducing swelling and dark circles.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for dark circles.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water and let 1 tablespoon of dried chamomile flowers steep for 10 minutes.
    2. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers.
    3. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature.
    4. Soak two cotton balls in the chamomile tea.
    5. Place the soaked cotton balls on your closed eyes for 15 minutes.

    Green tea contains caffeine and tannins that constrict blood vessels, reducing fluid retention and swelling around the eyes, which lightens dark circles.

    Its high antioxidant content, particularly EGCG, helps eliminate skin cells causing dark circles and promotes skin regeneration. The anti-inflammatory properties in green tea soothe the skin, decreasing puffiness and pigmentation.     Moreover, green tea has vitamin K, which strengthens capillary walls, reducing leakage of blood into the skin, making dark circles less noticeable.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in green tea tea that help with dark circles.

    • Leaves: Contain high levels of antioxidants that help reduce inflammation and brighten the skin, reducing the appearance of dark circles.
    • Buds: Rich in polyphenols and catechins, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, helping to soothe and lighten the skin around the eyes.
    • Seeds: Contain essential fatty acids and vitamin E that nourish and hydrate the skin, reducing the appearance of dark circles and puffiness.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in green tea tea that aid with dark circles.

    • Caffeine: A stimulant that constricts blood vessels, reducing puffiness and dark circles.
    • EGCG: A powerful antioxidant that helps reduce inflammation and strengthens capillary walls.
    • Theanine: An amino acid that promotes relaxation, reducing stress-induced dark circles.
    • Tannins: Polyphenols that have astringent properties, helping to reduce puffiness and dark circles.
    • Vitamin E: An antioxidant that protects skin from damage, improves elasticity, and reduces dark circles.
    • Vitamin K: A fat-soluble vitamin that improves blood circulation and reduces the appearance of dark circles.
    • Saponins: Naturally occurring compounds that provide anti-inflammatory benefits, reducing puffiness and dark circles.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic green tea tea for dark circles.

    1. Boil 8 ounces of water, then let it cool for 2-3 minutes until it reaches a temperature of 160-170°F.
    2. Steep 1 teaspoon of high-quality green tea leaves in the hot water for 2-3 minutes.
    3. After steeping, remove the tea leaves and let the tea cool to a comfortable temperature.
    4. Soak two cotton pads in the cooled green tea and place them on closed eyes for 10-15 minutes.
    5. Discard the used tea and cotton pads, then rinse your face with cool water.

    2. Add 1 teaspoon of loose-leaf rooibos tea or 1 rooibos tea bag to the hot water.
    3. Steep the tea for 6-7 minutes to allow the flavonoids, including aspalathin and nothofagin, to release into the water.
    4. Remove the tea leaves or bag to prevent over-steeping and bitterness.
    5. Consume the rooibos tea either cold or hot, up to twice a day, to help reduce dark circles under the eyes.

    • Asiaticoside: Reduces dark circles by inhibiting melanin production and promoting collagen synthesis, which thickens the skin and reduces the visibility of dark circles.
    • Madecassoside: Possesses anti-inflammatory properties that alleviate puffiness and dark circles by reducing the accumulation of blood and fluids under the eyes.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic gotu kola tea for dark circles.

    1. In a saucepan, combine 2 cups of water and 2 teaspoons of dried gotu kola leaves, bringing them to a boil.
    2. Reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let the mixture simmer for 10 minutes.
    3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used gotu kola leaves.
    4. Allow the gotu kola tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
    5. Drink the tea twice a day, once in the morning and once in the evening, for at least a month to reduce dark circles.

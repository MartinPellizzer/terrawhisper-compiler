    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for periodontal disease.

    What is periodontal disease and how can it affect your life?

    Periodontal disease, also known as gum disease, is a bacterial infection that damages the soft tissues and bone that support teeth.

    It begins as gingivitis, an inflammation of the gums, and can progress to periodontitis, a severe form of gum disease that can result in tooth loss. This condition affects millions of people worldwide and has significant consequences on their lives. It leads to discomfort, pain, and difficulties in speaking, chewing, and swallowing.

    Additionally, periodontal disease is associated with other health problems such as diabetes, heart disease, and stroke, further impacting people's overall well-being.

    What other natural remedies help with periodontal disease?

    Periodontal disease, also known as gum disease, is a common issue that affects the tissues surrounding and supporting the teeth.

    Two of the most effective natural remedies for periodontal disease are oil pulling and the use of essential oils. Oil pulling, an ancient Ayurvedic practice, involves swishing oil (typically sesame or coconut oil) in the mouth for 15-20 minutes daily.

    This technique reduces plaque, bacteria, and inflammation in the oral cavity, promoting gum health. Essential oils, such as tea tree, clove, and peppermint oil, exhibit potent antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help combat periodontal disease when added to a mouthwash or applied directly to the gums.

    Regular dental hygiene, including brushing and flossing, complements these natural remedies, ensuring optimal oral health.

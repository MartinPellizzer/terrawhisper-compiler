  • Leaves: Unlike stems, leaves of milk thistle have healing properties too. They are used to make tea as they have flavonoids and fatty acids beneficial for liver health.
  • Rhyzomes: Unlike roots, rhyzomes of milk thistle are used in teas to treat liver ailments such as cirrhosis, thanks to their protective and regenerative properties for liver cells.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in milk thistle tea that help with cirrhosis.

  • Silymarin: This is the most well-known medicinal constituent of milk thistle. It has been shown to have protective effects on the liver, and may help to reduce inflammation and damage caused by cirrhosis.
  • Silybin: This is a flavonoid lignan and the most active constituent of silymarin. It has been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, which may help to protect liver cells from damage.
  • Taraxasterol: This is a plant sterol found in milk thistle. It has been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects, which may help to reduce inflammation in the liver.
  • Chlorogenic acid: This is a type of phenolic acid found in milk thistle. It has been shown to have antioxidant effects, which may help to protect liver cells from damage.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic milk thistle tea for cirrhosis.

  1. Measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried milk thistle seeds or 1 tablespoon of crushed seeds for every 8 ounces of water.
  2. Place the milk thistle seeds in a heat-resistant container or tea infuser.
  3. Boil water and pour it over the milk thistle seeds.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes before removing the seeds.
  5. Drink the milk thistle tea, up to three times a day.

What is cirrhosis and how can it affect your life?

Cirrhosis is a chronic liver disease, characterized by the replacement of healthy liver tissue with scar tissue, leading to a progressive loss of liver function.

As cirrhosis advances, it impairs the liver's ability to filter toxins, produce vital proteins, and regulate blood clotting, resulting in a variety of serious health complications, such as liver failure, liver cancer, and hepatic encephalopathy. These complications significantly diminish a person's quality of life by causing physical discomfort, cognitive impairment, and limiting daily activities.

Ultimately, cirrhosis can result in a reduced life expectancy, making it a life-altering and potentially life-threatening condition.

What other natural remedies help with cirrhosis?

Cirrhosis is a chronic liver disease that is typically managed through medical intervention, but incorporating natural remedies can complement the treatment plan.

Three of the most common and effective natural remedies for cirrhosis are maintaining a balanced diet, engaging in regular physical activity, and practicing mindfulness techniques. A well-balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains supports liver function and maintains overall health. Regular physical activity, such as aerobic exercises and strength training, improves cardiovascular function, reduces liver-related complications, and enhances overall well-being.

Engaging in mindfulness practices, like meditation and deep-breathing exercises, reduces stress and promotes mental clarity, contributing to a better quality of life.

10 best herbal teas for digestion

When it comes to soothing digestive issues, herbal teas are a wonderful natural remedy.

Peppermint tea is a top choice, as it calms the stomach and reduces inflammation, easing symptoms of IBS. Ginger tea, with its natural anti-inflammatory properties, helps to reduce nausea and alleviate discomfort. Fennel tea, with its carminative properties, relieves bloating and gas, while also promoting the release of digestive enzymes.

Licorice root tea, with its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties, reduces inflammation in the digestive tract and promotes a healthy gut microbiome.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea aids digestion due to its antispasmodic and carminative properties.

These properties relax the smooth muscle lining of the digestive tract, reducing the risk of indigestion and gas build-up. Moreover, chamomile contains certain volatile oils, such as bisabolol, that have a calming effect on the stomach and intestines, thereby alleviating discomfort and promoting healthy digestion. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory and healing properties, attributed to the presence of flavonoids like apigenin, can soothe inflammation in the digestive tract and facilitate the healing of any existing digestive issues.

Consequently, chamomile tea enhances the overall efficiency of the digestive process and fosters a sense of well-being.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with digestion.

  • Flowers: The chamomile flowers contain oils and antioxidants that promote digestion by reducing inflammation and relaxing the muscles in the digestive system.
  • Leaves: The leaves of chamomile plants have calming properties that help soothe the stomach and alleviate digestive discomfort.
  • Seeds: The seeds of chamomile plants are rich in antioxidants that aid in digestion by stimulating the production of digestive enzymes.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that help with digestion.

  • Apigenin: This flavonoid has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, helping to reduce gut inflammation and relieve digestive spasms.
  • Bisabolol: A terpene alcohol, bisabolol has anti-inflammatory and soothing effects, helping to alleviate digestive inflammation and discomfort.
  • Chamazulene: A chemical compound formed during the drying process, chamazulene has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, aiding in the reduction of oxidative stress and inflammation in the digestive tract.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin also has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, helping to soothe and relax the digestive system.
  • Flavonoids: These plant metabolites have antioxidant properties, assisting in the protection of the gastrointestinal tract from oxidative damage.
  • Phenolic acids: These compounds contribute to chamomile's bitter taste and have antioxidant properties, supporting the overall health of the digestive system.
  • Tannins: These astringent compounds can help to reduce inflammation and protect the digestive tract lining, promoting digestive health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for digestion.

  1. In a small pot, combine 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of dried chamomile flowers.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Remove the pot from heat and let the tea steep for an additional 5 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers.
  5. Serve the tea hot, optionally adding honey or lemon to taste.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea aids digestion due to its active compounds, primarily menthol and methyl salicylate.

These components boast antispasmodic properties, relaxing the gastrointestinal muscles and thus alleviating digestive discomforts like cramps and spasms. Moreover, peppermint tea enhances the flow of bile from the gallbladder to the small intestine, expediting the digestion process. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory effects help soothe the digestive tract, providing relief from irritation and inflammation.

Finally, peppermint tea's carminative properties allow it to expel excessive gas from the digestive system, preventing bloating, discomfort, and other symptoms associated with poor digestion.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with digestion.

  • Leaves: Rich in menthol and menthone, peppermint leaves promote digestion by relaxing gastrointestinal muscles and reducing inflammation.
  • Flowers: High in essential oils, peppermint flowers stimulate bile production, aiding in fat digestion and reducing symptoms of digestive discomfort.
  • Seeds: Packed with antioxidants and volatile oils, peppermint seeds help soothe the stomach, alleviate gas, and improve overall digestive function.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in peppermint tea that help with digestion.

  • Menthol: Acts as a natural analgesic and antispasmodic, helping to relax the gastrointestinal muscles and relieve pain, bloating, and discomfort.
  • Mentone: Stimulates the production of bile and gastric juices, aiding in digestion and absorption of fats.
  • Limonene: Exhibits carminative properties, helping to reduce gas and bloating in the digestive tract.
  • Flavonoids: Possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help protect the digestive system from damage and reduce inflammation.
  • Rosmarinic acid: Functions as an antioxidant, and has been shown to help alleviate symptoms of indigestion and gastrointestinal disorders.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for digestion.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 cup of boiling water with 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves.
  2. Cover the teapot and let the mixture steep for 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the peppermint leaves, discarding the solid parts.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Serve the tea warm and enjoy its soothing effects on your digestion.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for digestion.

  3. Strain the ginger tea into a cup, discarding the ginger pieces.
  4. Add honey and lemon to taste, stir well.

  5. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea, then enjoy it while it's still warm, either plain or with a touch of honey for added natural sweetness.

What is digestion and how can it affect your life?

Digestion is the complex process by which the human body breaks down food into nutrients, allowing for their absorption and utilization to fuel various bodily functions.

This vital process begins in the mouth, where food is mechanically broken down and chemically treated with enzymes, and continues in the stomach and small intestine. Here, nutrients are absorbed into the bloodstream, providing energy, building and repairing tissues, and supporting immune function.

Digestion's profound impact on people's lives is evident in its role in sustaining overall health, mental clarity, and physical performance.

What other natural remedies help with digestion?

Among the most common and effective natural remedies for digestion are probiotics, apple cider vinegar, and ginger.

Probiotics are live bacteria and yeasts that naturally live in your digestive system and are also present in certain foods, such as fermented foods like sauerkraut, kimchi, and kefir. These beneficial bacteria help break down food, destroy harmful bacteria, and create vitamins.

Apple cider vinegar is another popular natural remedy that aids digestion by stimulating the production of stomach acid, promoting healthy gut bacteria, and breaking down fats in the digestive tract. Ginger, on the other hand, has long been used as a natural remedy for various stomach ailments, including nausea, indigestion, and bloating.

It works by stimulating the digestive juices and enzymes that help break down food in the stomach.

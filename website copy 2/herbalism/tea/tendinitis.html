The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic willow tea for tendinitis.

  1. Gather 4-6 fresh willow tree branches, each 1/4 inch in diameter, and cut them into 1-2 inch pieces.
  2. Rinse the willow pieces thoroughly, then place them in a pot and add 4 cups of water.
  3. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid to remove willow pieces, then let it cool down for a few minutes.
  5. Pour the willow tea into a cup and drink it while warm, up to 3 times a day.

What is tendinitis and how can it affect your life?

Tendinitis is an inflammation or irritation of the tendons - the thick bands of tissue that attach bones to muscles.

This condition often causes pain and soreness around a joint, making movement difficult and reducing the quality of life. Simple tasks such as lifting objects, writing, or even turning your head can become a challenge.

In severe cases, tendinitis can lead to the inability to perform daily activities and decreased productivity, impacting individuals' overall well-being and lifestyle.

What other natural remedies help with tendinitis?

Tendinitis, an inflammation or swelling of the tendons, often benefits from several evidence-based natural remedies.

RICE, an acronym for Rest, Ice, Compression, and Elevation, stands out as a primary treatment, with each component contributing to reducing pain and inflammation. Rest allows the tendon to heal, while ice numbs the area and minimizes swelling.

Compression, by use of a brace or wrap, further limits swelling, and elevating the affected limb above heart level diminishes fluid buildup. Additionally, exercises that maintain the range of motion yet avoid irritating the tendon, such as gentle stretching and strengthening activities, promote recovery.

Lastly, cold laser therapy, a technique employing low-intensity lasers, penetrates tissue to encourage cellular regeneration and alleviate pain.

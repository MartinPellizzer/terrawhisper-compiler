10 best herbal teas for asthma

For individuals living with asthma, incorporating herbal teas into their daily routine can provide natural relief from symptoms.

Peppermint tea, rich in anti-inflammatory properties, helps relax airways and ease congestion. Ginger tea, with its natural anti-inflammatory compounds, reduces inflammation and opens up airways, making it easier to breathe.

Thyme tea, with its antibacterial properties, fights off infections that can exacerbate asthma symptoms. Finally, Echinacea tea, packed with antioxidants, boosts the immune system, reducing the severity of asthma attacks.

These herbal teas, when consumed regularly, provide a natural and soothing way to manage asthma symptoms.

1. Licorice

Licorice tea assists with asthma due to its active component, glycyrrhizin, which decreases inflammation in the airways.

This compound achieves this by hindering the production of chemicals that cause swelling and mucus buildup. Moreover, licorice tea liquefies mucus, making it easier to expel and reducing asthma symptoms. Additionally, this natural remedy soothes irritated throats and airways, providing instant relief.

Lastly, licorice tea's antioxidant and expectorant properties strengthen the respiratory system, lowering asthma attacks' frequency and intensity.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in licorice tea that help with asthma.

  • Roots: Rich in glycyrrhizin, they help reduce inflammation in the airways, making breathing easier for people with asthma.
  • Stems: Containing flavonoids, they have antioxidant properties that support respiratory health, alleviating asthma symptoms.
  • Leaves: Packed with isoliquiritigenin, they aid in relaxing bronchial muscles, making it easier to breathe for those with asthma.
  • Flowers: Abundant in chalcones, they possess anti-allergic properties, reducing inflammation and histamine response in asthma sufferers.
  • Seeds: Loaded with coumarins, they inhibit platelet aggregation and bronchodilation, providing relief in asthma attacks.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in licorice tea that aid with asthma.

  • Glycyrrhizin: This compound has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the airways, making it easier to breathe for individuals with asthma.
  • Liquiritigenin: This flavonoid found in licorice tea has been shown to relax bronchial smooth muscles, which can help alleviate asthma symptoms such as wheezing and shortness of breath.
  • Glabridin: This is another flavonoid in licorice tea that has anti-allergic and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce the severity of asthma attacks triggered by allergens.
  • Liquiritin: This flavonoid has been shown to have expectorant properties, which can help clear mucus from the airways and improve breathing in individuals with asthma.
  • Isoflavans: These compounds in licorice tea have been shown to have bronchodilatory effects, which can help widen the airways and improve airflow in individuals with asthma.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for asthma.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot for 5 minutes.
  2. Add 2 teaspoons of dried licorice root to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the licorice root.
  5. Sip the tea while still warm, up to three times a day.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for asthma.

What is asthma and how can it affect your life?

Asthma is a chronic lung disease that inflames and narrows the airways, causing shortness of breath, wheezing, and coughing.

This condition affects people's ability to perform daily activities without experiencing symptoms or requiring medication. The impact of asthma can range from mild inconvenience to severe limitations, often requiring patients to adjust their lifestyles significantly.

In some cases, asthma attacks can even become life-threatening, emphasizing the importance of proper management and treatment for this incurable condition.

What other natural remedies help with asthma?

Among the most common and effective natural remedies for asthma are breathing exercises, such as the Papworth method and Buteyko technique.

These methods focus on controlling breath patterns, reducing breathlessness, and enhancing overall lung function. Another widely used natural remedy is maintaining a healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains.

Such a diet boosts the immune system, improves lung health, and lowers inflammation. Furthermore, regular physical activity, specifically exercises like swimming and yoga, offers substantial benefits in managing asthma symptoms. These workouts strengthen respiratory muscles, increase endurance, and decrease airway sensitivity.

Lastly, managing stress and anxiety through mindfulness, meditation, and relaxation techniques can help alleviate asthma symptoms by lowering stress hormones and improving psychological well-being.

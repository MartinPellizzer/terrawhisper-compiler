10 Best Herbal Teas For Insomnia

Herbal teas, particularly those made from chamomile, valerian root, lavender, and passionflower, are known to help with insomnia due to their natural sedative properties.

These herbs contain compounds that help to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety, and regulate sleep patterns. Chamomile, for instance, contains the antioxidant apigenin, which binds to certain receptors in the brain that help decrease anxiety and initiate sleep.

Valerian root has been shown to increase the amount of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain, a neurotransmitter that helps regulate nerve impulses and promotes calmness. Similarly, lavender and passionflower have been used in aromatherapy and as natural remedies for sleep problems due to their soothing and relaxing effects. Drinking these herbal teas can help create a calming bedtime routine, signaling to the body that it's time to wind down and prepare for sleep.

However, it's important to consult with a healthcare provider before incorporating these herbs into your sleep routine, especially if you're taking any medications or have pre-existing health conditions.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with insomnia because it contains the antioxidant apigenin, which binds to certain receptors in the brain that help decrease anxiety and initiate sleep.

The soothing properties of chamomile tea may also reduce inflammation and promote relaxation, making it easier to fall asleep and stay asleep. Furthermore, drinking chamomile tea can raise the core body temperature, which is followed by a drop in temperature that is linked to the onset of sleep. Additionally, the nightly routine of drinking chamomile tea before bed can serve as a psychological trigger for sleep, signaling to the body that it's time to wind down and prepare for rest.

Lastly, chamomile tea is a caffeine-free alternative to other beverages, reducing the risk of caffeine-induced disruptions to the sleep cycle.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for insomnia.

  1. Heat 1 cup of water to boiling (2-3 minutes in a microwave or 4-5 minutes in a kettle).
  2. In a teapot or teapot infuser, place 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers (adjust to personal taste).
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers and let steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the flowers.
  5. For added sweetness and sleep-promoting benefits, stir in 1 teaspoon of honey and a pinch of lavender (optional). Enjoy 30-60 minutes before bedtime.

2. Valerian

Valerian tea helps with insomnia because it contains valerenic acid and other compounds that increase the amount of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain.

GABA is a neurotransmitter that has calming effects and can help reduce anxiety and promote sleep. Additionally, valerian has been shown to improve the quality of deep sleep, helping people feel more rested and refreshed upon waking. Furthermore, valerian has been used as a natural remedy for insomnia for thousands of years, and its effectiveness has been supported by numerous scientific studies.

However, it is important to note that valerian may cause side effects such as headaches, dizziness, and stomach problems in some people, and it may interact with certain medications, so it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.

The following procedure explains how to make valerian tea for insomnia.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried valerian root and boil 1 cup of water; place valerian root in a tea infuser or teapot.
  2. Pour the boiling water over the valerian root and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste, but avoid adding milk or sugar.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the valerian root.
  5. Drink the tea 30 minutes before bedtime, but do not consume more than 3 cups in one day.

3. Lavender

Lavender tea helps with insomnia because it contains an active ingredient called linalool, which has calming and relaxing properties that can help reduce anxiety and promote sleep.

The scent of lavender is known to decrease heart rate and blood pressure, thereby inducing a state of calmness that makes it easier to fall asleep. Furthermore, lavender tea is caffeine-free, making it an ideal beverage to consume before bedtime without the stimulating effects of other caffeinated drinks. Additionally, drinking lavender tea can become a soothing part of a bedtime routine, signaling to the body and mind that it's time to wind down and prepare for sleep.

However, it's important to note that while lavender tea may help with insomnia for some people, individual responses can vary, and it's always best to consult with a healthcare provider for personalized advice.

The following procedure explains how to make lavender tea for insomnia.

  1. Heat 1 cup of water to boiling (2-3 minutes in a microwave or 4-5 minutes in a kettle), then pour it over 1 teaspoon of dried lavender flowers in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  2. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the flavors and health benefits.
  3. While steeping, warm a ceramic mug by filling it with hot water, then discard the water before pouring in the lavender tea.
  4. Optional: add a touch of honey or a squeeze of lemon to taste, as these can complement the floral notes of the lavender.
  5. Enjoy your soothing lavender tea 30-60 minutes before bedtime to help promote relaxation and improve sleep quality.

4. Passionflower

Passionflower tea helps with insomnia because it contains flavonoids, such as apigenin, which are known to have sedative effects on the body.

These compounds bind to certain receptors in the brain that help decrease the physical and psychological symptoms of insomnia. Moreover, passionflower has been shown to increase the amount of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain, a neurotransmitter with calming effects. As a result, passionflower tea can help reduce the time it takes to fall asleep and improve overall sleep quality.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new herbal remedy, especially for those taking other medications or with pre-existing medical conditions.

The following procedure explains how to make passionflower tea for insomnia.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried passionflower leaves or 2.5 teaspoons of fresh passionflower leaves, and gently warm 1 cup (8 ounces) of water to about 200-212°F (93-100°C).
  2. Place the passionflower leaves in a tea infuser or teapot and pour the hot water over the leaves.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the medicinal compounds and achieve a strong, deep purple-pink color.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to discard the leaves, then let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Slowly sip the passionflower tea 30-60 minutes before bedtime, enjoying its calming and relaxing effects to help alleviate insomnia symptoms.

5. Lemon Balm

Lemon balm tea helps with insomnia because it contains natural compounds that have a calming effect on the body, particularly rosmarinic acid.

This acid increases the amount of a neurotransmitter in the brain called GABA, which has been shown to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality. Furthermore, lemon balm has been found to reduce anxiety and stress, which can contribute to insomnia. Additionally, it may help regulate the body's internal "clock" or circadian rhythm, promoting a more consistent sleep-wake cycle.

However, it's important to note that while lemon balm tea can be a helpful natural remedy for insomnia, it may not be effective for everyone and should not replace any prescribed medication without consulting a healthcare professional.

The following procedure explains how to make lemon balm tea for insomnia.

  1. Gather 2 teaspoons of dried lemon balm leaves or 4 teaspoons of fresh leaves, rinse and crush them slightly to release the oils, then place in a tea infuser or teapot (preparation time: 5 minutes).
  2. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down for 2-3 minutes before pouring it over the lemon balm in the infuser or teapot (preparation time: 5 minutes, water should be around 200°F/93°C).
  3. Steep the lemon balm in hot water for 5-7 minutes, depending on how strong you prefer the tea (steeping time: 5-7 minutes).
  4. Remove the infuser or strain the tea into a cup, then add honey or stevia to taste, if desired (preparation time: 2 minutes).
  5. Drink the tea 30-60 minutes before bedtime, up to three times a day, for best results in managing insomnia (consumption time: 30-60 minutes before bedtime).

6. Hops

Hops tea helps with insomnia because it contains properties that have a sedative effect on the body.

The hop plant, from which the tea is derived, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat sleep disorders. The bitter acids and essential oils in hops tea are thought to increase the production of certain neurotransmitters that promote relaxation and sleep. Additionally, hops tea may help to reduce anxiety and promote a sense of calm, making it easier to fall asleep and stay asleep throughout the night.

However, it is important to note that while hops tea may be helpful for some people with insomnia, it is not a substitute for medical treatment and should be used in conjunction with other sleep hygiene practices.

The following procedure explains how to make hops tea for insomnia.

  1. Bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add 1 teaspoon of dried hop flowers, reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Turn off heat and let the mixture steep for an additional 15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the hop flowers, and discard them.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day, for relief of insomnia symptoms.

7. California Poppy

California poppy tea helps with insomnia because it contains sedative properties that can help promote relaxation and reduce anxiety, which in turn can help improve sleep quality.

The tea is made from the bright orange flowers of the California poppy plant, which have been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat sleep disorders. The active compounds found in the tea, such as alkaloids, interact with the brain to lower anxiety and produce a calming effect, helping individuals fall asleep faster and stay asleep longer. Additionally, California poppy tea has been shown to have a positive effect on neurotransmitter levels, particularly increasing the levels of serotonin and norepinephrine, which can further improve sleep quality.

However, it is important to note that while California poppy tea can be helpful for some individuals with insomnia, it may not be effective for everyone and it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement or herbal remedy.

The following procedure explains how to make california poppy tea for insomnia.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried California poppy petals and add to a tea infuser or teapot with strainer (for about 8 ounces of water).
  2. Boil water and let it cool down for about 5 minutes before pouring it over the California poppy petals (to avoid damaging the delicate compounds).
  3. Steep the petals for 5-10 minutes, depending on desired strength.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink up to 3 cups a day, but avoid drinking close to bedtime as it might have a stimulating effect on some individuals.

8. Skullcap

Skullcap tea helps with insomnia because it contains flavonoids, particularly one called scutellarin, which has been shown to have sedative and anxiety-reducing effects.

These properties can help to promote relaxation and reduce the time it takes to fall asleep. Additionally, skullcap may increase the production of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a neurotransmitter that blocks impulses between nerves and promotes a calming effect.

Furthermore, skullcap tea has been traditionally used by Native American and Chinese medicine to treat anxiety, nervous tension, and insomnia. However, it is important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the mechanisms behind skullcap's sedative effects and to establish a recommended dosage for treating insomnia.

As with any natural remedy, it's always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting a new supplement regimen.

The following procedure explains how to make skullcap tea for insomnia.

  1. Take 2 teaspoons of dried skullcap herb (Scutellaria lateriflora), rinse briefly and add to 1 cup of boiling water, let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the herb and discard it.
  3. Optional: Add honey or lemon for taste.
  4. Drink the tea 30 minutes before bedtime.
  5. Repeat the process for several weeks for best results in managing insomnia.

9. St. John'S Wort

St.

John's wort tea helps with insomnia because it contains antioxidants and soothing properties that can promote relaxation and improve sleep quality. The tea's active compounds, such as hypericin and hyperforin, may increase the levels of certain neurotransmitters in the brain, such as serotonin and GABA, which are known to regulate mood and sleep. Additionally, St.

John's wort has been shown to reduce anxiety and stress, which can contribute to insomnia. However, it is important to note that St. John's wort can interact with certain medications and may have side effects, so it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen. Furthermore, while St.

John's wort tea can be a helpful tool in managing insomnia, it should be used in conjunction with other sleep-promoting habits, such as maintaining a consistent sleep schedule and creating a calming bedtime routine.

The following procedure explains how to make st. john's wort tea for insomnia.

  1. Take 2-3 teaspoons of dried St. John's Wort herb, and place it in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  2. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the herb, and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  3. While steeping, cover the teapot or cup to retain the volatile oils.
  4. After steeping, remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove the herb.
  5. Drink the tea, preferably 1 hour before bedtime, for best results in managing insomnia.

10. Kava

Kava tea helps with insomnia because it contains active ingredients called kavalactones, which have been shown to promote relaxation and reduce anxiety, helping to create a calming effect that can improve sleep quality.

Unlike traditional sleeping pills, kava tea is a natural alternative that does not cause dependency or have severe side effects, making it a safer option for those struggling with insomnia. Moreover, kava tea has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat sleep disorders, indicating its efficacy in improving sleep patterns. Additionally, kava tea may also help to alleviate stress and anxiety, which can contribute to insomnia, by increasing the activity of a neurotransmitter called gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using kava tea for insomnia, as it can interact with certain medications and may not be suitable for everyone.

The following procedure explains how to make kava tea for insomnia.

  1. Finely chop 1-2 tablespoons of dried kava root and place in a tea infuser or muslin bag (adjust the amount based on your tolerance and desired strength, but do not exceed 4 tablespoons); put the tea infuser in a cup and pour boiling water (about 8 ounces) over it, then steep for 5-10 minutes.
  2. While the kava tea is steeping, warm up 8-12 ounces of milk (dairy or non-dairy) in a small saucepan over medium heat until hot but not boiling.
  3. In a separate small bowl, mix 1-2 teaspoons of honey (or to taste) with a few drops of vanilla extract.
  4. Remove the tea infuser from the cup and discard the kava root.
  5. Combine the steeped kava tea, warmed milk, and honey-vanilla mixture in a blender and blend until frothy (about 30 seconds). Pour into a mug and enjoy 1 hour before bedtime.

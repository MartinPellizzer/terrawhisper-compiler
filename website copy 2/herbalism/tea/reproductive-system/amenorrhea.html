1. Raspberry

Raspberry tea contains fragarine, a plant alkaloid that tones the uterus and promotes regular menstrual cycles.

This tea also provides high levels of magnesium and calcium, both crucial for regulating hormonal imbalances that can cause amenorrhea. Moreover, raspberry tea boasts emmenagogue properties that stimulate blood flow to the pelvic area, supporting the menstrual process. Drinking raspberry tea further promotes fertility by strengthening the uterine lining and facilitating embryo implantation.

Lastly, its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties alleviate menstrual cramps and discomfort, often associated with amenorrhea.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in raspberry tea that help with amenorrhea.

  • Leaves: Contain high levels of phytoestrogens that help regulate menstrual cycles.
  • Flowers: Rich in flavonoids that support hormonal balance and improve menstrual regularity.
  • Seeds: Source of essential fatty acids that promote healthy hormone production to alleviate amenorrhea.
  • Roots: Contain nutrients and compounds that have a regulating effect on the reproductive system to address menstrual irregularities.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in raspberry tea that aid with amenorrhea.

  • Ellagic acid: This polyphenol antioxidant helps to regulate menstrual cycles by promoting hormonal balance.
  • Fragarine: This alkaloid stimulates the smooth muscles of the uterus, helping to initiate menstruation.
  • Tannins: These astringent compounds help to tone the uterus, promoting normal menstruation and regulating heavy periods.
  • Flavonoids: These antioxidants support overall reproductive health, including promoting healthy hormone levels and reducing inflammation.
  • Vitamin C: This essential nutrient supports a healthy menstrual cycle by regulating hormone production and enhancing iron absorption.
  • Salicylic acid: This organic acid can help regulate menstruation by reducing inflammation and promoting healthy blood flow.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic raspberry tea for amenorrhea.

  1. In a pot, combine 2 cups of water and 2 tablespoons of dried raspberry leaves, then bring the mixture to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 15 minutes to allow the water to absorb the raspberry leaf properties.
  3. Remove the pot from heat, then add 1 tablespoon of fresh or frozen raspberries and let it steep for an additional 5 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solid residues.
  5. Serve the raspberry tea hot, optionally sweetening it with honey to taste.

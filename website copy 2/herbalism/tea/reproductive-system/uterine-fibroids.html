1. Green Tea

Green tea contains a high concentration of epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), a type of catechin that possesses potent antioxidant properties.

These antioxidants found in green tea inhibit the growth of uterine fibroids by limiting the production of excessive estrogen and decreasing inflammation in the body. Moreover, EGCG aids in reducing the size of existing fibroids and prevents further fibroid development by obstructing the activation of certain protein receptors responsible for fibroid growth. Consequently, drinking green tea contributes to the overall well-being of the reproductive system, promoting hormonal balance and offering relief from fibroid-related symptoms.

Therefore, incorporating green tea into the daily routine can serve as an effective complementary approach for managing uterine fibroids.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in green tea tea that help with uterine fibroids.

  • Leaves: Contain high levels of catechins, antioxidants known to reduce fibroid growth.
  • Buds: Rich in epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), shown to inhibit the proliferation of fibroid cells.
  • Stems: Source of quercetin, a flavonoid that helps reduce inflammation and symptoms associated with fibroids.
  • Flowers: Contain luteolin and apigenin, compounds that naturally balance hormones, reducing fibroid growth.
  • Seeds: Provide a small amount of vitamin E, which has been linked to decreased fibroid size and symptoms.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in green tea tea that aid with uterine fibroids.

  • Catechins: These are antioxidants that help reduce inflammation and can inhibit the growth of uterine fibroid cells.
  • Epigallocatechin Gallate: A type of catechin that exhibits potent anti-inflammatory and antiproliferative properties, which may help shrink fibroids.
  • Theaflavins: These polyphenols have anti-angiogenic and antiproliferative effects, which can help reduce the size and growth of fibroids.
  • Thearubigins: These polyphenols have antioxidant properties that can help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation associated with uterine fibroids.
  • Chlorogenic acid: This polyphenol has been shown to have antiproliferative effects, which may help control the growth of fibroid cells.
  • Caffeine: Although present in smaller amounts, caffeine can help improve blood flow and reduce inflammation, which may help alleviate symptoms of uterine fibroids.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic green tea tea for uterine fibroids.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water, reaching a full rolling boil at 212°F, for 1-2 minutes to ensure proper temperature.
  2. Place 1 teaspoon of high-quality green tea leaves in a teapot or strainer for accurate dosage.
  3. Pour the hot water over the tea leaves, allowing them to steep for 3-5 minutes to extract potent compounds.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding used tea leaves, for a smooth and enjoyable beverage.
  5. Consume the green tea 2-3 times a day, preferably between meals, as a supportive measure for managing uterine fibroids.

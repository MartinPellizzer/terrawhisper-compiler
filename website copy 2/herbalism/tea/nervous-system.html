Herbal tea for the nervous system

The best herbal teas for the nervous system are those that promote relaxation and reduce anxiety.

Passionflower tea, for instance, contains harmala alkaloids and flavonoids that have a calming effect on the mind. Chamomile tea, with its apigenin and luteolin content, is another excellent choice as it binds to benzodiazepine receptors in the brain, alleviating anxiety and inducing sleep.

Similarly, lemon balm tea contains rosmarinic acid and eugenol, which have been shown to improve mood and cognitive function while reducing stress. Finally, lavender tea, rich in linalool and linalyl acetate, is renowned for its soothing properties and ability to promote restful sleep.

Each of these herbal teas offers a natural, caffeine-free method of supporting the nervous system and promoting overall well-being.

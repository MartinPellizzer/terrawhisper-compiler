10 Best Herbal Teas For Anxiety

Herbal teas, particularly those made from plants and herbs known for their calming properties, can help alleviate anxiety by promoting relaxation.

For instance, chamomile tea is widely recognized for its ability to reduce anxiety and improve sleep quality due to the presence of the antioxidant apigenin. Similarly, lemon balm tea has been shown to decrease anxiety symptoms, possibly due to its rosmarinic acid content, which increases GABA levels in the brain.

Passionflower tea is another popular choice, as it contains flavonoids that can help lower anxiety levels. Moreover, the soothing process of brewing and sipping herbal tea can create a comforting ritual, fostering a sense of calm and mindfulness.

However, it's essential to remember that while herbal teas can be helpful, they should not replace professional medical advice or treatment for anxiety disorders.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with anxiety because it contains an antianxiety agent called apigenin, which binds to certain receptors in the brain and calms nervous activity.

Apigenin also has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the brain associated with anxiety and depression. Chamomile tea is rich in antioxidants, which protect the brain from oxidative stress and damage, further contributing to its anxiolytic effects. The calming effects of chamomile tea have been proven through numerous studies, including one published in the Journal of Clinical Psychopharmacology, which found that drinking chamomile tea before bed significantly improved sleep quality and reduced symptoms of anxiety in participants.

Overall, chamomile tea is a safe, natural remedy for managing anxiety symptoms due to its calming properties, antianxiety agents, anti-inflammatory effects, and rich antioxidant content.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for anxiety.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to a rolling boil, then remove from heat.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers or 1 chamomile tea bag to a teapot or mug.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers or tea bag, ensuring they are fully submerged.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes, depending on desired strength.
  5. Remove the tea bag or strain out the chamomile flowers, then enjoy the soothing cup of chamomile tea.

2. Lavender

Lavender tea helps with anxiety because it contains Linalool, a natural compound that has calming properties.

This compound interacts with the amygdala, the part of the brain responsible for the "fight or flight" response, reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation. The soothing aroma of lavender, when brewed in hot water to make tea, also adds to its stress-relieving effects. Furthermore, studies have shown that consuming lavender tea can help lower cortisol levels, the hormone responsible for stress response.

Lastly, due to its mild flavor, people often find it easier to drink regularly compared to other herbal teas or medications, making it a more effective and enjoyable long-term solution for managing anxiety symptoms.

The following procedure explains how to make lavender tea for anxiety.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried culinary lavender or 2-3 fresh lavender sprigs to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let the lavender steep in the hot water for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid to remove the lavender and pour the tea into a cup.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and enjoy this soothing tea up to three times a day as needed.

3. Lemon Balm

Lemon balm tea helps with anxiety because it contains rosmarinic acid, a compound that has natural calming properties and may help reduce symptoms of anxiety by promoting relaxation and reducing stress.

Additionally, lemon balm has been shown to increase GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid) levels in the brain, a neurotransmitter that helps regulate anxiety and promotes a sense of calm. Furthermore, lemon balm tea has a soothing effect on the digestive system, which can help alleviate anxiety related to digestive issues. Drinking lemon balm tea may also improve sleep quality, which can help reduce anxiety and promote overall well-being.

Lastly, lemon balm has been used traditionally as a mild sedative, making it a popular natural remedy for anxiety and stress relief.

The following procedure explains how to make lemon balm tea for anxiety.

  1. Gather 2 teaspoons of fresh lemon balm leaves (Melissa officinalis) or 1 teaspoon of dried lemon balm; rinse the leaves thoroughly.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Steep the lemon balm leaves in the hot water for 5-7 minutes; cover the container to retain the essential oils.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, and optionally, add honey or lemon juice for taste.
  5. Drink the lemon balm tea while warm, 1-2 times a day, as needed for anxiety relief.

4. Passionflower

Passionflower tea helps with anxiety because it contains compounds called flavonoids and alkaloids, which have been shown to increase levels of certain neurotransmitters such as gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain.

GABA is a calming neurotransmitter that helps reduce excitability in the nervous system, leading to feelings of relaxation and reduced anxiety. Passionflower tea also has antioxidant properties that help protect the brain from oxidative stress, which can contribute to anxiety and other mental health issues. Furthermore, passionflower tea has been shown to improve sleep quality, which is important for overall mental health and reducing symptoms of anxiety.

Lastly, passionflower tea has a long history of traditional use in herbal medicine for treating anxiety and nervous disorders, with numerous studies supporting its efficacy.

The following procedure explains how to make passionflower tea for anxiety.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water to prepare the base for your passionflower tea.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon (approximately 2 grams) of dried passionflower leaves or 1 passionflower tea bag to a tea infuser or directly into the boiling water.
  3. Allow the passionflower to steep in the boiling water for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  4. Strain the passionflower leaves or remove the tea infuser before consuming.
  5. Drink the passionflower tea 1-3 times a day, as needed, to help alleviate symptoms of anxiety.

5. Valerian Root

Valerian root tea helps with anxiety because it contains compounds that interact with the neurotransmitter gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), which is known for its calming effects on the nervous system.

The active ingredients in valerian root, such as valerenic acid and sesquiterpenes, enhance GABA function, leading to increased inhibitory signals in the brain that help reduce anxiety and promote relaxation. Valerian root tea also has anti-stress properties, helping the body cope with stressors by reducing cortisol levels, which is a key stress hormone. Additionally, valerian root tea can improve sleep quality, which further helps alleviate symptoms of anxiety.

Its sedative effects make it an effective natural remedy for those seeking relief from anxiety and promoting overall wellbeing.

The following procedure explains how to make valerian root tea for anxiety.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried valerian root or 1 valerian root tea bag to a mug.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the valerian root in the mug.
  4. Cover the mug and let the tea steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Remove the valerian root or tea bag, add honey or lemon to taste, and enjoy your calming cup of valerian root tea.

6. Ashwagandha

Ashwagandha tea helps with anxiety because it contains active compounds, primarily withanolides, that have been shown to reduce stress and anxiety levels.

These compounds interact with the body's hormonal system, primarily the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, to regulate stress responses and cortisol levels. Regular consumption of ashwagandha tea may promote relaxation, mental clarity, and improved mood, contributing to an overall reduction in anxiety symptoms. Additionally, this herbal tea has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for centuries to treat various ailments, including anxiety and stress-related conditions.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen, especially if you have pre-existing medical conditions or are taking prescription medications.

The following procedure explains how to make ashwagandha tea for anxiety.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water (250 mL) in a pot on the stove, then add 1 teaspoon (2 grams) of dried ashwagandha root powder.
  2. Reduce heat to a simmer and let the mixture steep for 10-15 minutes, allowing the water to absorb the medicinal properties of the ashwagandha.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, using a fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth to remove any solid residue.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added flavor and potential additional health benefits.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, taking time to relax and allowing the calming effects of the ashwagandha to help alleviate feelings of anxiety.

7. Holy Basil

Holy basil tea helps with anxiety because it contains compounds that may help reduce anxiety symptoms.

One of these compounds is eugenol, which has been shown to have anxiety-reducing effects. Additionally, holy basil tea contains adaptogens, which are natural substances that help the body adapt to stress.

Drinking holy basil tea may also help lower cortisol levels, a hormone associated with stress and anxiety. Furthermore, holy basil tea has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for its calming and soothing properties, which may contribute to its anxiety-reducing effects.

However, it's important to note that while holy basil tea may help with anxiety, it should not be used as a substitute for professional medical advice or treatment.

The following procedure explains how to make holy basil tea for anxiety.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water (250 mL) in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons (2-4 g) of dried holy basil leaves (also known as tulsi) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the leaves.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste (optional) and drink while warm. Enjoy daily for best results.

8. Kava Kava

Kava kava tea helps with anxiety because it contains active ingredients called kavalactones, which have been shown to promote relaxation and reduce anxiety by interacting with the limbic system, the part of the brain responsible for controlling emotions.

These kavalactones can increase the level of GABA, a neurotransmitter that has calming effects on the nervous system, helping to alleviate symptoms of anxiety. Furthermore, kava kava has been found to have anxiolytic effects similar to those of benzodiazepines, common prescription medications for anxiety, but without the risk of dependence or sedation. However, it is important to note that kava kava may cause liver damage in some individuals, and it should be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

Therefore, while kava kava tea can be a helpful natural remedy for anxiety, it is not a substitute for medical treatment and should be used with caution.

The following procedure explains how to make kava kava tea for anxiety.

  1. In a tea infuser or reusable tea bag, combine 2-3 teaspoons (about 2-3 grams) of dried kava kava root.
  2. Place the infuser or tea bag in a large mug or teapot.
  3. Pour 8-12 ounces of boiling water over the infuser or tea bag.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes, depending on desired strength.
  5. Remove the infuser or tea bag, and let the tea cool slightly before drinking. Enjoy 1-2 cups per day, as needed for anxiety relief. Note: Kava kava may cause side effects or interact with certain medications. Consult a healthcare provider before use.

9. Skullcap

Skullcap tea helps with anxiety because it contains compounds that have calming effects on the nervous system.

Specifically, it's rich in flavonoids and antioxidants, such as scutellarin and baicalein, which have been shown to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation in both animal and human studies. Additionally, skullcap tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate physical symptoms of anxiety, like muscle tension and headaches. Its gentle calming effect makes it a popular alternative remedy for those looking to manage their anxiety without relying on prescription medication.

However, as with any herbal remedy, it's important to consult with a healthcare professional before adding skullcap tea to your routine if you have any underlying health conditions or are taking medication.

The following procedure explains how to make skullcap tea for anxiety.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on medium heat.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried skullcap herb (Scutellaria lateriflora) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent herb, and let it cool down for a few minutes.
  5. Sip the skullcap tea slowly, enjoying its mild taste, and repeat as needed up to three times a day.

10. Rhodiola

Rhodiola tea helps with anxiety because it contains adaptogens, natural compounds that help the body respond to stressors more effectively.

These adaptogens include rosavins and salidroside, which have been shown in studies to reduce cortisol levels and improve mood in individuals experiencing anxiety. Furthermore, rhodiola tea is rich in antioxidants, which can help protect the brain from oxidative stress and inflammation, both of which are linked to anxiety and other mental health conditions. Additionally, rhodiola tea has been used traditionally for centuries in various cultures to boost energy levels, improve focus, and reduce feelings of fatigue and nervousness, all of which can contribute to anxiety symptoms.

Finally, the calming effects of rhodiola tea are enhanced when it is consumed with other relaxing ingredients, such as chamomile or lavender, making it a popular choice for those seeking natural relief from anxiety.

The following procedure explains how to make rhodiola tea for anxiety.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried Rhodiola rosea root to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the Rhodiola rosea and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea into a cup.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and enjoy your calming Rhodiola tea.

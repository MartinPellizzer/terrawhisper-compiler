10 Best Herbal Teas For Overactive Bladder

Herbal teas, particularly those made from plants with antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties, can help alleviate overactive bladder symptoms.

For instance, chamomile and lemon balm teas are known to relax the bladder muscles and reduce spasms, which can decrease the frequent urge to urinate. Additionally, herbal teas like hibiscus and corn silk have diuretic effects that can increase urine production, thereby promoting bladder emptying and reducing the risk of urinary tract infections.

Furthermore, some herbal teas, such as horsetail and buchu, contain compounds that can help tone and strengthen the bladder muscles, enhancing bladder control. However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before incorporating herbal teas into a treatment plan for overactive bladder, as some herbs may interact with medications or exacerbate certain medical conditions.

Lastly, maintaining proper hydration is crucial for overall urinary health, and herbal teas can serve as a flavorful and beneficial alternative to water.

1. Cornsilk

Cornsilk tea helps with overactive bladder because it contains bioactive compounds called flavonoids, which have been shown to reduce inflammation and relax smooth muscle tissue in the urinary tract.

This can help alleviate symptoms of urgency, frequency, and leakage associated with an overactive bladder. Additionally, cornsilk tea has natural diuretic properties that may help decrease overall urine volume and relieve pressure on the bladder. Furthermore, it has antioxidant effects that can protect against oxidative stress in the urinary system, which is known to contribute to overactive bladder symptoms.

Overall, cornsilk tea provides multiple benefits for individuals experiencing overactive bladder symptoms, making it a natural and potentially effective remedy.

The following procedure explains how to make cornsilk tea for overactive bladder.

  1. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan, then add 1/4 cup of dried corn silk and reduce heat to a simmer.
  2. Allow the mixture to steep for 15-20 minutes, then strain out the corn silk and discard.
  3. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for flavor.
  4. Divide the tea into 4 equal portions and drink one serving every 6 hours throughout the day.
  5. Continue drinking the tea daily for several weeks to see if symptoms of overactive bladder improve.

2. Marshmallow Root

Marshmallow root tea helps with overactive bladder because it contains mucilage, a substance that creates a protective barrier on the lining of the bladder, reducing irritation and the urge to urinate frequently.

This soothing effect also helps to reduce inflammation and discomfort in the urinary tract. Additionally, marshmallow root has demulcent properties, which means it forms a protective film that covers and shields the mucous membranes, further alleviating symptoms of overactive bladder. Furthermore, it can increase the urine's healing and soothing effects, decreasing the need for frequent urination.

Lastly, marshmallow root tea may help to strengthen the bladder muscles over time, reducing the symptoms of overactive bladder in a more long-term manner.

The following procedure explains how to make marshmallow root tea for overactive bladder.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot, then add 1-2 teaspoons of dried marshmallow root (Althea officinalis) and reduce heat to a simmer.
  2. Allow the marshmallow root to steep in the simmering water for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid to remove the marshmallow root, leaving you with marshmallow root tea.
  4. Optional: Add a natural sweetener like honey to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the marshmallow root tea while warm, 1-2 times a day, as needed for managing overactive bladder symptoms.

3. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with overactive bladder because it contains anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that can help reduce inflammation and spasms in the bladder.

These properties, derived from the chamomile flower, may also help to relax the muscles in the bladder wall, reducing the urge to urinate frequently. Additionally, chamomile tea has been shown to have a calming effect on the body, which can help alleviate anxiety and tension that can exacerbate overactive bladder symptoms. Furthermore, chamomile tea is a natural diuretic, which can help increase urine production and flush out toxins from the urinary tract.

However, it is important to note that while chamomile tea may provide some relief for overactive bladder, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, and individuals should consult with their healthcare provider before incorporating it into their routine.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for overactive bladder.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to a rolling boil, then remove from heat.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers or 1 chamomile tea bag to the hot water.
  3. Cover and steep for 5-10 minutes, depending on desired strength.
  4. Remove the chamomile flowers or tea bag, then let the tea cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 3-4 cups of chamomile tea throughout the day for best results in managing overactive bladder.

4. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with overactive bladder because it contains properties that can reduce bladder inflammation and muscle spasms, which are often the root cause of overactive bladder.

The soothing effect of ginger on the urinary tract lining helps to alleviate the frequent and urgent need to urinate. Additionally, ginger has natural antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory agents, which can help to relax the bladder muscles and reduce bladder irritation. Furthermore, ginger tea can help to strengthen the immune system, which can help to prevent bladder infections that can exacerbate overactive bladder symptoms.

Lastly, ginger's natural diuretic properties can help to flush out toxins from the urinary tract, promoting overall urinary health.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for overactive bladder.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root (about 20-30 grams); rinse well.
  2. In a small pot, combine ginger slices with 4 cups of water; bring to a boil over high heat.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20-30 minutes to extract ginger's beneficial compounds.
  4. Strain the ginger tea into a cup, then add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Sip the warm ginger tea slowly throughout the day, especially before bedtime, to help soothe an overactive bladder.

5. Parsley

Parsley tea helps with overactive bladder because it contains compounds that have natural diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce bladder inflammation and irritation.

The diuretic effect of parsley tea also increases urine production, promoting the flushing out of toxins and bacteria from the urinary tract, thereby reducing the risk of bladder infections. Additionally, parsley tea is known to soothe the mucous membranes of the urinary tract, alleviating symptoms such as urgency and frequency associated with overactive bladder. Furthermore, parsley tea has been found to contain apiol and myristicin, compounds that may help relax the smooth muscles of the bladder, reducing spasms and involuntary contractions.

Lastly, parsley tea is a natural and caffeine-free alternative to traditional beverages, making it a healthier choice for individuals seeking to manage their overactive bladder symptoms.

The following procedure explains how to make parsley tea for overactive bladder.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh parsley leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan, then bring the water to a boil.
  2. Once boiling, reduce heat and let the parsley simmer in the water for 10 minutes.
  3. After 10 minutes, remove the saucepan from heat and let the parsley steep for an additional 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid from the saucepan into a cup, discarding the parsley leaves.
  5. Allow the parsley tea to cool to a drinkable temperature, then enjoy up to three times a day as needed.

6. Nettle Leaf

Nettle leaf tea helps with overactive bladder because it contains compounds that have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help reduce the urge to urinate frequently.

These compounds, including flavonoids and lectins, can help relax the smooth muscles of the bladder and decrease bladder spasms, which can alleviate the symptoms of an overactive bladder. Nettle leaf tea may also help inhibit the production of certain hormones that can irritate the bladder, such as prostaglandins. Additionally, the tea has diuretic properties, which can help increase urine production and flush out toxins from the urinary tract, potentially reducing the risk of urinary tract infections.

However, it's important to note that while nettle leaf tea may help alleviate symptoms of an overactive bladder, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, and individuals should consult with their healthcare provider before incorporating it into their routine.

The following procedure explains how to make nettle leaf tea for overactive bladder.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried nettle leaf and place them in a tea infuser or teapot with a removable strainer.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of fresh water and pour it over the nettle leaf in the teapot or teacup.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds from the nettle leaf.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea into a separate cup to remove any loose leaf particles.
  5. Drink the nettle leaf tea while it's still warm, 2-3 times a day, to help soothe and potentially alleviate overactive bladder symptoms.

7. Bearberry

Bearberry tea helps with overactive bladder because it contains an active compound called arbutin, which is known to have antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce bladder spasms and inflammation.

These properties can help alleviate the symptoms of an overactive bladder, such as frequent urination, urgency, and incontinence. Additionally, bearberry tea is a natural diuretic, which can help increase urine flow and flush out toxins from the urinary tract, further supporting bladder health. However, it's important to note that long-term use of bearberry tea can lead to side effects such as liver damage and kidney problems, so it should only be used as a short-term solution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

It's also worth noting that bearberry tea may interact with certain medications, so it's important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting to drink bearberry tea for overactive bladder.

The following procedure explains how to make bearberry tea for overactive bladder.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried bearberry leaves (2-4 grams) and place them in a tea infuser or tea bag.
  2. Boil 8 ounces (1 cup) of water and let it cool down for a couple of minutes until it reaches a temperature of around 200°F (93°C).
  3. Place the tea infuser or tea bag with bearberry leaves in a teapot or a mug and pour the hot water over it.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds from the leaves.
  5. Remove the tea infuser or tea bag and enjoy the warm bearberry tea, preferably between meals for optimal absorption. Drink 3-4 cups a day for a few weeks, but make sure to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal remedy.

8. Juniper Berries

Juniper berries tea helps with overactive bladder because it contains compounds that have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help reduce bladder inflammation and spasms, thereby alleviating the symptoms of an overactive bladder.

Additionally, juniper berries have been shown to have a diuretic effect, which can increase urine production and help flush out toxins from the urinary tract, potentially reducing the frequency and urgency of urination. Furthermore, juniper berries contain compounds that have antimicrobial properties, which can help prevent urinary tract infections that can exacerbate overactive bladder symptoms. The tea made from juniper berries is also a natural and caffeine-free alternative to other beverages that can irritate the bladder and worsen symptoms.

However, it is important to note that long-term use or excessive consumption of juniper berries tea can have potential side effects, so it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before incorporating it into a treatment plan for overactive bladder.

The following procedure explains how to make juniper berries tea for overactive bladder.

  1. Boil 1 cup of fresh water in a saucepan, then add 1 teaspoon of dried juniper berries (or 2 teaspoons of fresh juniper berries) and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat and let the mixture steep for an additional 15 minutes, allowing the juniper berries to release their beneficial compounds.
  3. Strain the liquid into a teacup, discarding the juniper berries.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for flavor and additional health benefits.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, aiming to drink 2-3 cups of juniper berry tea per day to potentially help alleviate symptoms of an overactive bladder.

9. Hibiscus

Hibiscus tea helps with overactive bladder because it contains powerful antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the urinary tract and bladder, thereby alleviating the symptoms of overactive bladder.

Additionally, hibiscus tea is a natural diuretic, which can help increase urine production and flush out toxins from the urinary system, thus reducing the frequency and urgency of urination. The tea also contains compounds that can help tone and strengthen the bladder muscles, improving bladder control and reducing the risk of bladder spasms. Furthermore, hibiscus tea has been shown to have a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help reduce anxiety and tension that can exacerbate overactive bladder symptoms.

Finally, hibiscus tea is a natural and caffeine-free alternative to other beverages that can irritate the bladder and worsen overactive bladder symptoms.

The following procedure explains how to make hibiscus tea for overactive bladder.

  1. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan, then add 2 tablespoons of dried hibiscus flowers.
  2. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the water to absorb the hibiscus' properties.
  3. Strain the tea into a teapot or a heat-resistant container, discarding the used flowers.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature, then add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink 3-4 cups of hibiscus tea throughout the day for at least a week to see improvements in overactive bladder symptoms.

10. Cranberry

Cranberry tea helps with overactive bladder because it contains a high concentration of A-type proanthocyanidins (PACs), which can prevent bacteria from adhering to the bladder walls, thereby reducing the risk of urinary tract infections (UTIs) that can exacerbate overactive bladder symptoms.

Additionally, cranberries have anti-inflammatory properties that may help soothe the bladder lining, potentially alleviating some of the discomfort associated with frequent urges to urinate. The mild diuretic effect of cranberry tea can also increase urine production, promoting bladder emptying and potentially decreasing the frequency of bathroom trips. Furthermore, cranberry tea is a natural and caffeine-free alternative to traditional caffeinated beverages, which can sometimes worsen overactive bladder symptoms.

However, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating cranberry tea or any other remedy into a treatment plan for overactive bladder.

The following procedure explains how to make cranberry tea for overactive bladder.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a saucepan, then add 2 teaspoons of dried cranberry leaves and let it steep for 10 minutes.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of fresh or frozen cranberries and steep for an additional 5 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, then add honey or lemon to taste (optional).
  4. Drink 3-4 cups of cranberry tea daily, preferably between meals.
  5. Store any leftover tea in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours.

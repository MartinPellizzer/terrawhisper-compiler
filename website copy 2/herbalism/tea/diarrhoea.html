1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with diarrhoea because Chamomile tea helps with diarrhea because of its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the intestines and relax the muscles of the intestinal wall, thereby alleviating symptoms of diarrhea.

The tea's anti-inflammatory properties may also help soothe irritation in the gut, reducing the frequency and urgency of bowel movements. Chamomile contains tannins, which have astringent properties that can help solidify stools and slow down the rate of digestion. Additionally, chamomile has been shown to have antibacterial properties, which may help eliminate harmful bacteria in the gut that can cause diarrhea.

However, it's important to note that while chamomile tea can help alleviate symptoms of diarrhea, it's not a substitute for medical treatment, especially in cases of severe or persistent diarrhea.

  1. To make chamomile tea for diarrhea, start by boiling 1 cup of water (250ml) in a saucepan or kettle, then add 1-2 teaspoons (2-4 grams) of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot.
  2. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers, ensuring they are fully submerged, then cover and let it steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and flavors.
  3. After steeping, remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to discard the chamomile flowers, leaving only the golden-colored chamomile tea.
  4. For added taste and potential synergistic effects, consider adding a small slice of fresh ginger root or a squeeze of lemon juice, but avoid adding milk or sugar as they may interfere with the tea's therapeutic properties.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea while warm, 2-3 times a day between meals, to help soothe the digestive system and alleviate symptoms of diarrhea. However, if diarrhea persists for more than a few days or is accompanied by other concerning symptoms, consult a healthcare professional for further evaluation and treatment.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with diarrhoea because Peppermint tea helps with diarrhea because it contains volatile oils, such as menthol, that have an antispasmodic effect on the gastrointestinal tract, helping to relax the muscles of the intestines and reduce the intensity and frequency of contractions, thereby alleviating diarrhea.

However, it is important to note that while peppermint tea can be helpful for some people with diarrhea, it may not be suitable for everyone, particularly those with gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) or a hiatal hernia, as it can exacerbate symptoms.

As with any natural remedy, it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before consuming peppermint tea or any other herbal tea for diarrhea.

  1. To make the peppermint tea, boil 1 cup of water and add 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves (or 1 peppermint tea bag) and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Optional: add 1 teaspoon of honey or a squeeze of lemon for added flavor and potential additional soothing properties.
  3. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Drink the tea slowly, sipping it gently, to ensure it doesn't upset your stomach further.
  5. Repeat the process of drinking peppermint tea every time your diarrhea returns, as it can help soothe the digestive system and potentially reduce the frequency of diarrhea.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with diarrhoea because Ginger tea helps with diarrhea because it contains gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help soothe the gastrointestinal tract and alleviate symptoms.

These compounds also help to regulate the muscles in the intestines, reducing the frequency and intensity of contractions that can exacerbate diarrhea. Additionally, ginger has been shown to have antibacterial and antiviral effects, which may help to eliminate the underlying causes of diarrhea. Furthermore, ginger tea can help to rehydrate the body, replenishing essential fluids and electrolytes lost during bouts of diarrhea.

Finally, ginger's natural spiciness can help to stimulate digestion and absorption, reducing the likelihood of further digestive issues.

  1. Peel and finely chop 1 inch of fresh ginger root, then combine in a small saucepan with 4 cups of water, and bring to a boil; reduce heat and simmer, covered, for 15 minutes.
  2. Remove from heat and add 1 teaspoon of honey (or to taste), stirring until dissolved; then mix in 1/2 teaspoon of lemon juice.
  3. Allow the ginger tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a cup, discarding the ginger pieces.
  5. Sip the ginger tea slowly, ideally between meals, and drink up to three times a day as needed for diarrhea relief.

  1. Collect fresh oak bark from healthy oak trees, ensuring the bark is clean and free of debris, then rinse it thoroughly; cut 2-3 inches of bark into small pieces, place them in a pot, and add 4 cups of water (a ratio of 1:2 for bark and water).
  2. Slowly bring the water to a simmer over medium-low heat, allowing the bark to gently release its properties for about 15-20 minutes, ensuring the mixture does not strongly boil to preserve the medicinal compounds.
  3. After the bark has infused into the water, remove the pot from the heat and let it cool down for about 10 minutes; then, strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a container, discarding the used bark pieces.
  4. To enhance the tea's flavour and potential benefits, squeeze the juice of half a lemon and add 1-2 teaspoons of raw honey into the oak bark tea; stir well until the honey is fully dissolved.
  5. Drink a cup of the oak bark tea, about 8-12 ounces, every few hours, up to three times a day, to help alleviate diarrhea symptoms; store the remaining tea in the refrigerator for up to 48 hours.

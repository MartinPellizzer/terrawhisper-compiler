1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea aids in jaw lock relief due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling and alleviate tension in the jaw muscles.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with jaw lock.

  • Flowers: Contain high levels of flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help relax jaw muscles and alleviate tension.
  • Leaves: Rich in terpenoids and coumarins, known to reduce inflammation and spasms, helping to release a locked jaw.
  • Stems: Contain tannins and polyphenols that can help soothe and strengthen jaw muscles, preventing lockjaw.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with jaw lock.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that possesses antispasmodic properties, helping to reduce muscle spasms in the jaw.
  • Chamazulene: A chemical compound with anti-inflammatory effects, helping to decrease inflammation in the jaw muscles.
  • Bisabolol: A terpene with muscle relaxant properties, helping to alleviate tension in the jaw muscles.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, it also has anti-inflammatory effects, further helping to reduce jaw inflammation.
  • Spiroether: A constituent with anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, helping to relieve pain in the jaw.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for jaw lock.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water, fresh from the tap, for 3-5 minutes until it reaches a rolling boil.
  2. Add 1 heaping teaspoon (2.5 grams) of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and steep, covered, for 5-10 minutes to extract the medicinal compounds.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the chamomile flowers from the teapot, discarding the spent flowers.
  5. Sip the warm chamomile tea slowly, allowing it to soothe and relax your jaw muscles.

What is jaw lock and how can it affect your life?

Jaw lock, a condition that restricts jaw movement, impacts people's lives by causing difficulties in performing daily activities.

This immobility results from the temporomandibular joint's inability to function correctly, causing discomfort and pain.

Simple tasks such as speaking, eating, or even yawning become challenging, adversely affecting communication, nutrition, and overall quality of life.

What other natural remedies help with jaw lock?

Jaw lock, also known as lockjaw, is a painful condition that restricts jaw movement.

Several natural remedies effectively alleviate this discomfort. First, applying warm and cold compresses alternately brings instant relief by reducing inflammation and easing muscle spasms.

Second, gently massaging the jaw muscles with essential oils like lavender, eucalyptus, or peppermint oil enhances blood circulation, promoting relaxation and flexibility in the jaw. Third, practicing jaw stretching exercises, such as slowly opening and closing the mouth or moving the jaw side to side, strengthens the jaw muscles, preventing lockjaw.

Lastly, maintaining good posture, especially while sleeping, ensures the jaw remains in a relaxed position, reducing the risk of jaw lock.

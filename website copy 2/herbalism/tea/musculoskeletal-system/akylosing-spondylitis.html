The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic willow tea for ankylosing spondylitis.

  1. Gather 2-3 fresh, young willow branches (Salix alba) and trim leaves, retaining only the bark-covered stems.
  2. Chop the stems into small pieces, then place them in a pot and cover with 2 cups of water.
  3. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat, letting it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea, discarding the willow pieces, and allow it to cool down before drinking.
  5. Consume 1-2 cups of willow tea daily, preferably between meals, for a maximum of 1-2 weeks.

10 best herbal teas for snoring

A soothing cup of herbal tea before bedtime can work wonders for snorers.

Peppermint tea, with its cooling properties, helps reduce nasal congestion and opens up airways, promoting unobstructed breathing. Meanwhile, chamomile tea's calming effects relax the throat muscles, reducing vibrations that cause snoring. Additionally, ginger tea's natural anti-inflammatory properties reduce swelling in the throat, further reducing snoring triggers.

By incorporating these teas into a pre-sleep routine, snorers may find relief from this frustrating sleep disruptor.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains antioxidants that reduce inflammation, promoting muscle relaxation.

Chamomile tea's calming effects on the mind and body promote better sleep, reducing snoring caused by sleep disorders. Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with snoring.

  • Flowers: Chamomile tea typically uses flowers due to their calming properties, which can potentially promote better sleep and reduce snoring.
  • Leaves: The leaves are sometimes included in the tea as they contain antioxidants and may have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help alleviate respiratory issues that cause snoring.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds can be used in the tea for their essential oils, which can promote relaxation and soothe the digestive system, addressing one potential cause of snoring.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that help with snoring.

  • Apigenin: This flavonoid compound has been found to have sedative and muscle relaxant properties, which can help reduce snoring by relaxing the throat muscles and allowing for clearer airways during sleep.
  • Chamazulene: This anti-inflammatory compound can help reduce swelling in the nasal passages and throat, which can help alleviate snoring caused by congestion or inflammation.
  • Bisabolol: This terpene compound has anti-spasmodic properties, which can help relax the throat muscles and prevent the vibrations that cause snoring.
  • Matricin: This compound is converted to chamazulene in the steam-distillation process, and has similar anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce snoring caused by inflammation.
  • Spiroether: This compound has been shown to have anti-allergic properties, which can help reduce snoring caused by allergic reactions that lead to congestion and inflammation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for snoring.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water for 3-5 minutes until it reaches a rolling boil.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the chamomile flowers from the teapot.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea 30 minutes before bedtime for best results.

What is snoring and how can it affect your life?

Snoring is the act of noisy breathing during sleep, produced when air passes through relaxed tissues in the throat, causing vibrations.

This common condition impacts people's lives in various negative ways. For one, it disrupts normal sleep patterns, often leading to excessive daytime sleepiness, fatigue, and reduced concentration. Additionally, it can strain relationships due to noise disturbances, particularly for bed partners.

Furthermore, consistent snoring might indicate underlying health issues, such as obstructive sleep apnea, which can heighten the risk of cardiovascular disease and other complications over time.

What other natural remedies help with snoring?

A common and effective natural remedy for snoring is throat exercises.

Regular practice of these exercises helps strengthen the muscles in the throat and palate, reducing tissue collapse and the resulting vibrations that cause snoring. Another natural solution is losing weight, as excess fat around the neck can put pressure on the airways and lead to snoring.

Elevating the head during sleep is also a simple yet powerful method to prevent snoring. By adding extra pillows or using a specially designed bed riser, the upper body is raised, which helps keep the airways open and unobstructed.

Lastly, maintaining a consistent sleep schedule and ensuring adequate sleep duration can contribute to reduced snoring, as fatigue and irregular sleep patterns can exacerbate the issue.

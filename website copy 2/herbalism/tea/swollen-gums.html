1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea aids swollen gums due to its anti-inflammatory properties, sourced from the plant's flavonoids.

These flavonoids inhibit the release of inflammatory agents like prostaglandins and leukotrienes, reducing inflammation. Moreover, chamomile's antimicrobial characteristics combat bacteria that cause gum infections, further alleviating swelling. Its astringent quality also tightens tissues, reducing gum inflammation.

Lastly, chamomile tea contains azulene, known for soothing effects, thereby providing comfort to swollen gums.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with swollen gums.

  • Flowers: Chamomile flowers contain anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in gums.
  • Leaves: Chamomile leaves have antiseptic qualities that aid in preventing gum infections.
  • Seeds: The oil in chamomile seeds provides pain-relieving effects, beneficial for swollen gums.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that help with swollen gums.

  • Alpha-Bisabolol: This terpene alcohol possesses potent anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce the redness, swelling, and irritation in the gums.
  • Apigenin: This flavonoid exhibits strong antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, aiding in decreasing the inflammation and promoting gum tissue healing.
  • Chamazulene: This aromatic compound has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, helping to alleviate swollen gums while fighting off harmful bacteria.
  • Matricin: This sesquiterpene lactone converts into chamazulene when chamomile is dried or processed, and it exerts anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, reducing pain and swelling in gums.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for swollen gums.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and add 1 tablespoon of dried chamomile flowers.
  2. Simmer the mixture on low heat for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove chamomile flowers.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down until it's warm, but not hot.
  5. Rinse your swollen gums with the chamomile tea for soothing relief.

What is swollen gums and how can it affect your life?

Swollen gums are a condition characterized by the enlargement and inflammation of the gum tissue surrounding the teeth.

This issue occurs when there is an accumulation of plaque and bacteria along the gum line, causing irritation and infection. Swollen gums can lead to discomfort and pain, making it difficult for individuals to eat, drink, and perform oral hygiene routines.

In severe cases, swollen gums can result in tooth loss, further impacting a person's ability to eat and speak correctly, and can negatively affect their self-confidence and overall quality of life.

What other natural remedies help with swollen gums?

Swollen gums can be a sign of gum disease or other oral health issues, so it's important to address the problem promptly.

Several natural remedies alleviate swollen gums effectively. First, a saltwater rinse is a time-tested solution. Dissolve a teaspoon of salt in a cup of warm water, then swish the solution around your mouth for 30 seconds before spitting it out. Saltwater reduces inflammation and promotes healing. Second, oil pulling with coconut oil is another popular method.

Swish a tablespoon of coconut oil in your mouth for 15-20 minutes, then spit it out. Coconut oil has antimicrobial properties that help combat gum inflammation. Lastly, applying a cold compress to the affected area can help reduce swelling. Wrap a few ice cubes in a cloth and hold it against your gums for 10-15 minutes, several times a day. The cold constricts blood vessels, which decreases swelling and numbs the pain.

Incorporating these natural remedies into your oral hygiene routine can provide relief and improve gum health.

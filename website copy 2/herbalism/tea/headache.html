10 best herbal teas for headache

Certain herbal teas provide proven relief for headaches.

Ginger tea stands out, known for its potent anti-inflammatory properties that target headache-causing inflammation. Peppermint tea is another popular choice, as it contains menthol, which soothes and relaxes tense muscles in the head and neck.

Feverfew tea, derived from the feverfew herb, has long been used in traditional medicine to treat headaches, thanks to its parthenolide content, which helps prevent blood vessel constriction. Chamomile tea, with its calming effects, helps reduce stress and anxiety that can contribute to headaches.

Willow bark tea, containing salicin, similar to aspirin, offers a natural alternative for headache pain relief.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains compounds that possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which directly address two common causes of headaches.

The most notable compound, chamazulene, inhibits the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that trigger pain and inflammation. Furthermore, chamomile's soothing effects on the digestive system can alleviate tension headaches, as these headaches often stem from gastrointestinal issues. Additionally, this herbal tea's calming nature promotes relaxation, easing muscle contractions and reducing stress-induced headaches.

Consequently, drinking chamomile tea regularly helps prevent the onset of headaches by maintaining overall well-being.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with headache.

  • Flowers: Chamomile tea made from flowers is renowned for its antispasmodic properties, providing relief from headaches caused by muscle tension.
  • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids, chamomile leaves produce a soothing tea that helps alleviate headaches by reducing inflammation.
  • Seeds: The essential oils found in chamomile seeds create a calming medicinal tea, helping to ease headaches by promoting relaxation and reducing stress.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that help with headache.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that acts as a potent anti-inflammatory, reducing headache-inducing inflammation.
  • Chamazulene: A compound that has antispasmodic properties, helping to relax constricted blood vessels that can cause headaches.
  • Bisabolol: A terpene with anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, working to alleviate headache pain.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, providing additional anti-inflammatory benefits and contributing to headache relief.
  • Spiroethers: Compounds that exhibit strong anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic activities, aiding in headache reduction.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for headache.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water, fresh from the tap, reaching a rolling boil in 3-5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the chamomile flowers from the teapot.
  5. Sip the hot chamomile tea, slowly enjoying its soothing effects on your headache.

2. Feverfew

An added benefit is feverfew's action on serotonin levels, which it helps stabilize, reducing the occurrence of headaches and migraines.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in feverfew tea that help with headache.

  • Parthenolide: Reduces inflammation and constricts blood vessels, which helps to alleviate headaches.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic feverfew tea for headache.

  1. Pick 2-3 fresh feverfew leaves or use 1 teaspoon of dried feverfew leaves.
  2. Rinse the fresh leaves under cold water and pat dry.
  3. Place the leaves in a teapot and pour 1 cup of boiling water over them.
  4. Steep the tea for 5-10 minutes, then remove the leaves.
  5. Drink the tea slowly, up to three times a day, to alleviate headache symptoms.

Ginger tea alleviates headaches due to its natural anti-inflammatory compounds, such as gingerols and shogaols, which inhibit the production of prostaglandins, chemicals that cause inflammation and sensitize nerve fibers in the brain.

These active ingredients in ginger also possess analgesic properties, effectively reducing the pain intensity and frequency of headaches. Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with headache.

  • Rhizomes: These horizontal underground stems contain compounds that aid in reducing inflammation, making them an ideal choice for medicinal tea to alleviate headaches caused by inflammation.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, ginger leaves help reduce oxidative stress, which can contribute to headaches, providing relief when consumed as a medicinal tea.
  • Flowers: With high levels of anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, ginger flowers create a soothing medicinal tea, helping to ease headache symptoms.
  • Seeds: Ginger seeds hold essential oils known to alleviate pain and tension, making them suitable for medicinal tea to treat headaches.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginger tea that help with headache.

  • Gingerols: These compounds possess potent anti-inflammatory properties that help alleviate headaches by reducing the production of inflammatory substances in the body.
  • Shogaols: This class of compounds found in ginger has been shown to inhibit prostaglandin synthesis, thereby reducing the inflammation and associated pain caused by headaches.
  • Paradols: These antioxidant compounds contribute to ginger's pain-relieving effects by neutralizing harmful free radicals and providing protective benefits against headaches.
  • Zingerone: This compound present in ginger has been found to exhibit analgesic properties, thereby providing relief from headache discomfort.
  • Gingerols' pungency: The spicy and pungent nature of gingerols contributes to the relaxation of blood vessels in the head, alleviating headache symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for headache.

  1. Peel and grate 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger, set aside.
  2. Boil 4 cups of water, then add grated ginger, reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat, stir in 2 teaspoons of honey and 1 teaspoon of lemon juice.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine mesh sieve into cups.
  5. Serve hot, enjoy your soothing ginger tea for headache relief.

5. White Willow

White willow tea provides relief from headaches due to its active compound, salicin, which boasts a chemical structure similar to aspirin.

This salicin, upon ingestion, metabolizes into salicylic acid, a recognized anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving agent. As a result, white willow tea combats headache-inducing inflammation in the blood vessels surrounding the brain, narrowing them and thus alleviating the pain. Moreover, it offers a more gentle and sustained impact on headache alleviation compared to traditional over-the-counter pain relievers.

Consequently, white willow tea's natural makeup effectively lessens both the frequency and intensity of headaches without the risk of developing rebound headaches often associated with overuse of synthetic drugs.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in white willow tea that help with headache.

  • Bark: The bark of the white willow tree contains salicin, which is a natural anti-inflammatory compound that helps relieve headaches.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the white willow plant have a high concentration of phenolic glycosides, which are known to have analgesic and antipyretic properties, making them useful for treating headaches.
  • Rhyzomes: The rhyzomes of the white willow plant contain salicylic acid, a potent anti-inflammatory compound that helps alleviate headaches.
  • Stems: The stems of the white willow plant also contain salicin, which is metabolized in the body to salicylic acid, making them effective in reducing headache symptoms.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in white willow tea that help with headache.

  • Salicin: Converts to salicylic acid in the body, which inhibits prostaglandin synthesis, reducing inflammation and pain.
  • Flavonoids: Act as antioxidants, reducing inflammation and providing vascular protection, which can alleviate headaches.
  • Tannins: Have astringent properties, which can help reduce swelling and relieve pain.
  • Phenolic acids: Act as antioxidants and anti-inflammatory agents, reducing the effects of free radicals that contribute to headaches.
  • Polyphenols: Possess anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate headaches caused by inflammation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic white willow tea for headache.

  1. Measure 2 teaspoons of dried white willow bark and place it in a tea infuser.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the bark-filled infuser in a teapot.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 10-15 minutes to extract the medicinal compounds.
  4. Remove the infuser and discard the spent bark, then transfer the tea to a cup.
  5. Drink the tea slowly to alleviate headache symptoms, sipping as needed every 3-4 hours.

What is headache and how can it affect your life?

A headache is a moderate to severe pain in the head, often described as a throbbing or pulsating sensation.

It results from disturbances in the brain, including dilated blood vessels, chemical activity, and nerve cell interactions. Headaches impact people's lives significantly by causing discomfort, disrupting daily activities, and negatively affecting mental and emotional well-being.

They can lead to lost productivity, strained relationships, and increased healthcare costs.

What other natural remedies help with headache?

One of the most common and effective natural remedies for headaches is proper hydration.

Dehydration is a common cause of tension headaches, so drinking plenty of water throughout the day can help prevent and alleviate headache symptoms. Another popular natural remedy is practicing good posture, as tension in the neck and shoulders can often lead to headaches.

Regular exercise, such as yoga or stretching, can also help reduce the frequency and severity of headaches by improving circulation and reducing muscle tension. Additionally, applying a cold or hot compress to the forehead or neck can provide relief by reducing inflammation and constricting blood vessels.

Finally, getting enough sleep and managing stress through techniques such as meditation or deep breathing exercises can help prevent headaches and promote overall well-being.

10 best herbal teas for itchy eyes

Herbal teas offer a natural and soothing relief for itchy eyes.

Chamomile tea, with its anti-inflammatory properties, soothes irritation and reduces puffiness. Its gentle properties make it an ideal choice for those seeking a drug-free solution.

Another effective option is green tea, rich in antioxidants that help alleviate eye irritation and reduce redness. Simply steep a bag of either tea in hot water, let it cool, and apply the tea bags to closed eyes for 5-10 minutes.

Regular use brings noticeable relief to itchy eyes.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea aids itchy eyes due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce redness and irritation.

The tea's antioxidants, like flavonoids, help soothe irritated eyes by neutralizing free radicals. Furthermore, chamomile's natural anti-histamine effects alleviate symptoms associated with allergies that can cause itchy eyes. Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with itchy eyes.

  • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe itchy eyes.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants that can help reduce redness and irritation in eyes.
  • Seeds: Possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can alleviate itchiness in eyes.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with itchy eyes.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, helping to reduce irritation and redness in itchy eyes.
  • Chamazulene: A terpene known for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, providing relief from itchiness and discomfort in the eyes.
  • Bisabolol: A sesquiterpene alcohol with anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and soothing properties, helping to alleviate itchy eyes and promote healing.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin has anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties, helping to reduce itchiness and swelling in the eyes.
  • Spiroether: A compound found in chamomile that has antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects, helping to soothe itchy and irritated eyes.
  • Azulenyl: A derivative of chamazulene, azulenyl has anti-inflammatory and calming properties, providing relief from itchy eyes.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for itchy eyes.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a kettle or pot, reaching a full boil can take 3-5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or strainer in a cup.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and let it steep for 5 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strainer with the spent flowers from the cup.
  5. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature before gently dabbing it onto itchy eyes with a clean cloth.

10 best herbal teas for frizzy hair

Herbal teas, such as chamomile, lavender, and rosemary, offer an excellent solution for frizzy hair.

Chamomile tea, rich in antioxidants, helps soothe the scalp and adds shine to hair. Meanwhile, lavender tea promotes hair growth and reduces frizz by nourishing and conditioning the hair follicles. Lastly, rosemary tea, with its anti-inflammatory properties, strengthens hair, encourages circulation, and leaves hair looking smooth and manageable.

Regularly rinsing your hair with these herbal teas, after they have cooled down, creates a natural and cost-effective method for taming frizz and promoting overall hair health.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea, with its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, nourishes the scalp and strengthens hair follicles, promoting hair growth and reducing breakage.

This tea's ability to smooth the hair cuticle leads to reduced frizz and increased shine. Regular rinses with chamomile tea also aid in maintaining balanced pH levels, sealing the hair cuticles, and enhancing color, preventing the dryness that contributes to frizzy hair. Moreover, it helps in detangling knots, reducing hair damage and breakage that result from vigorous brushing, thereby further diminishing frizziness.

Consequently, chamomile tea emerges as an effective natural solution in managing frizzy hair.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with frizzy hair.

  • Flowers: Contain high levels of antioxidants that promote hair growth and reduce frizz.
  • Leaves: Rich in nutrients that nourish hair follicles, promoting healthy and smooth hair.
  • Seeds: Contain essential oils that moisturize and strengthen hair, reducing frizz and promoting shine.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with frizzy hair.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that smooths hair follicles, reducing frizz.
  • Luteolin: A flavonoid that nourishes hair follicles, preventing frizz.
  • Chamazulene: A chemical compound that moisturizes hair, reducing frizz.
  • Bisabolol: A terpene that soothes scalp, reducing frizz caused by dryness and irritation.
  • Matricin: A chemical compound that promotes hair growth, preventing frizz caused by breakage.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for frizzy hair.

  1. Measure 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers and place them in a small saucepan.
  2. Pour 1 cup of water into the saucepan and bring it to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let it simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a glass bowl, allowing it to cool down to a warm temperature.
  5. Apply the chamomile tea to your frizzy hair, massaging it into your scalp and letting it sit for 20 minutes before rinsing with water.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic rosemary tea for frizzy hair.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot for 5 minutes.
  2. Add 1/4 cup of fresh rosemary leaves and reduce heat to low, simmering for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid into a bowl, discarding the spent rosemary.
  4. Let the rosemary tea cool to lukewarm temperature.
  5. Pour tea over clean, wet hair and let it sit for 20 minutes before rinsing thoroughly.

10 best herbal teas for puffy eyes

Herbal teas, such as chamomile and calendula, offer a natural remedy for puffy eyes due to their anti-inflammatory properties.

Chamomile tea's antioxidants help reduce swelling and irritation, while calendula tea's flavonoids promote blood flow and strengthen capillary walls. To use, steep two tea bags in hot water, let them cool, and then place them on closed eyes for 10-15 minutes.

This soothing and rejuvenating treatment alleviates puffiness, leaving eyes refreshed and revitalized.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties, which aid in reducing puffiness around the eyes.

The antioxidants help fight free radicals that cause skin damage, while the anti-inflammatory properties restrict blood vessels and decrease swelling. Chamomile tea's cool temperature further soothes the skin and constricts blood vessels, alleviating puffiness. Moreover, its mild astringent qualities tighten the skin, diminishing the appearance of bags under the eyes.

Regularly drinking chamomile tea or applying chamomile tea bags to the eyes also promotes better sleep quality, which prevents fluid retention causing puffy eyes.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with puffy eyes.

  • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce puffiness around the eyes.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants that can soothe and calm irritated skin around the eyes.
  • Seeds: Contain flavonoids that have been shown to reduce inflammation and help with puffy eyes.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with puffy eyes.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that has anti-inflammatory properties, helping to reduce swelling in the eye area.
  • Luteolin: A flavonoid with antioxidant properties that helps protect the skin around the eyes from damage and reduces inflammation.
  • Chamazulene: A chemical compound with anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, helping to relieve puffy eyes and decrease pain.
  • Bisabolol: A terpene alcohol that has anti-inflammatory and soothing effects, reducing redness and irritation of the eyes.
  • Matricin: A sesquiterpene lactone with anti-inflammatory effects, helping to reduce swelling and alleviate puffy eyes.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for puffy eyes.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let 1 chamomile tea bag steep for 5 minutes.
  2. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  3. Soak two cotton balls in the chamomile tea.
  4. Place the soaked cotton balls over closed eyes for 10 minutes.
  5. Discard used tea bags and cotton balls.

10 best herbal teas for quick hair growth

Nettle tea stimulates hair growth by improving the scalp's health and increasing hair follicle strength. Rosemary tea enhances hair growth by improving blood circulation in the scalp and promoting cell division, essential for hair growth. Horsetail tea, rich in silica and antioxidants, fortifies hair strands, leading to thicker and longer hair.

Regularly consuming these herbal teas helps achieve faster hair growth results.

1. Nettle

Nettle tea energizes hair follicles with a rich supply of nutrients, such as vitamins A, C, and B, promoting an environment conducive to rapid hair growth.

It further boosts this process by stimulating blood circulation in the scalp, encouraging the delivery of vital oxygen and nutrients directly to hair roots. Moreover, nettle tea contains silica and sulfur, which strengthen individual hair strands, reducing breakage and enhancing overall hair length. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory properties soothe the scalp, mitigating potential irritants that could slow down hair growth.

Finally, as a natural source of antioxidants, nettle tea fights free radicals that damage cells, ultimately supporting the maintenance of a healthy scalp and quick, robust hair growth.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in nettle tea that help with quick hair growth.

  • Leaves: Contain high levels of silica and sulfur, which promote hair growth and restore hair shine.
  • Stems: Rich in iron and silica, stems help to strengthen hair follicles and prevent hair breakage.
  • Flowers: High in vitamins and minerals, including calcium and magnesium, flowers promote hair growth and improve scalp health.
  • Seeds: Packed with essential fatty acids and antioxidants, seeds help to nourish the scalp and stimulate hair growth.
  • Roots: Contain numerous nutrients that promote hair growth, including flavonoids, tannins, and lectins.
  • Rhyzomes: Rich in nutrients that support healthy hair growth and reduce hair loss, such as stigmasterol, campesterol, and sitosterol.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in nettle tea that aid with quick hair growth.

  • Silica: Strengthens hair follicles, promoting quick hair growth.
  • Sulfur: Boosts collagen production, enhancing hair growth rate.
  • Iron: Increases blood circulation in the scalp, stimulating hair growth.
  • Biotin: Nourishes hair follicles, supporting faster hair growth.
  • Vitamin C: Encourages collagen synthesis, resulting in rapid hair growth.
  • Magnesium: Aids in DNA repair, enhancing hair growth speed.
  • Potassium: Facilitates nutrient transport, improving hair growth health.
  • Calcium: Stimulates hair growth, nourishing scalp and hair.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic nettle tea for quick hair growth.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh nettle leaves, ensuring they are free from bugs and dirt, and rinse them thoroughly.
  2. Boil 4 cups of water in a pot, then add the rinsed nettle leaves, reducing heat to a simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a teapot or mug, discarding the leaves, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Stir in raw honey to taste, if desired, and consume the nettle tea twice a day, preferably before meals.
  5. Store any leftover tea in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours, reheating it before drinking.

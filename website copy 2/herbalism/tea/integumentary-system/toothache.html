10 best herbal teas for toothache

Certain herbal teas provide relief for toothache due to their natural anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

For example, cloves contain eugenol, a powerful natural anesthetic, making clove tea an effective choice. Additionally, chamomile tea, known for its soothing qualities, reduces inflammation and alleviates pain. Peppermint tea, with its antibacterial and numbing attributes, offers another excellent alternative for toothache relief.

Lastly, ginger tea, which inhibits prostaglandins (compounds that cause pain), is a wise choice for those seeking a natural remedy for toothache.

1. Cloves

Cloves tea offers toothache relief due to eugenol, a natural anesthetic in cloves.

This compound numbs the area, reducing pain. Cloves tea also boasts anti-inflammatory properties, which decrease swelling around the affected tooth. Additionally, cloves tea's antibacterial qualities fight tooth infections, further helping toothache symptoms.

Lastly, cloves tea's soothing warmth increases blood flow, promoting healing in the affected region.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in cloves tea that help with toothache.

  • Buds: Contain high levels of eugenol, a compound with potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, providing relief for toothache.
  • Flowers: Rich in flavonoids and essential oils, flowers help reduce inflammation and alleviate pain caused by toothaches.
  • Seeds: Packed with antioxidants, seeds aid in maintaining oral health, soothing toothaches and promoting gum healing.
  • Bark: Contains beneficial compounds like tannins and camphor, making bark an effective ingredient for medicinal tea to treat toothaches.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in cloves tea that aid with toothache.

  • Eugenol: A potent analgesic that provides instant relief from toothache by blocking pain signals in the mouth.
  • Tannins: Act as an astringent to reduce inflammation and provide a soothing effect on irritated gums.
  • Flavonoids: Possess anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and alleviate pain in the affected area.
  • Phenols: Exhibit antimicrobial properties that help eliminate bacteria causing toothache and promote oral health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic cloves tea for toothache.

  1. Crush 5 cloves into a powder using a mortar and pestle.
  2. Bring 2 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan, then add the crushed cloves.
  3. Simmer the mixture for 10 minutes, then strain it into a teapot or cup.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, then let the tea cool until warm.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to bathe the painful tooth and surrounding gums.

2. Chamomile

Chamomile tea brings instant relief to a toothache because it contains azulene and levomenol, both of which possess anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

These compounds inhibit the production of prostaglandins, chemicals that cause inflammation and sensitize nerves to pain. Furthermore, chamomile tea's antimicrobial properties combat toothache-causing bacteria, while its astringent qualities reduce swelling.

In sum, drinking chamomile tea offers swift, effective toothache relief through its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, antimicrobial, and astringent properties.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with toothache.

  • Flowers: Contain flavonoids and essential oils that have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, helping alleviate toothache.
  • Leaves: Rich in terpenoids and coumarins that possess analgesic and antimicrobial effects, beneficial for toothache relief.
  • Seeds: Contain beneficial volatile oils, which, when steeped in hot water, release compounds that can help reduce toothache.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with toothache.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid with potent anti-inflammatory properties that helps alleviate toothache by reducing inflammation in the affected area.
  • Chamazulene: A volatile oil with strong antioxidant and analgesic effects that aids in relieving toothache by decreasing pain and promoting healing.
  • Bisabolol: A sesquiterpene alcohol known for its soothing and calming properties, which helps alleviate toothache by reducing pain and inflammation.
  • Matricin: A compound that is converted to chamazulene during steam distillation, with anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties that help in managing toothache.
  • Spiroether: A compound with local anesthetic properties that helps in reducing toothache by numbing the affected area.
  • Alpha-bisabolol oxide A & B: Oxides of bisabolol with anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties that aid in alleviating toothache.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for toothache.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and add 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers, letting it steep for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers, leaving only the liquid.
  3. Allow the chamomile tea to cool down to a warm temperature.
  4. Swish the warm chamomile tea around your mouth, focusing on the affected tooth, for 30 seconds.
  5. Spit out the tea and repeat the process every few hours as needed.

1. Burdock

Burdock tea provides relief from psoriasis due to its anti-inflammatory and blood purifying properties.

It contains essential antioxidants and active compounds like arctiin and luteolin that help reduce inflammation in the body. Burdock tea also promotes skin health by enhancing the elimination of toxins from the bloodstream, resulting in improved skin conditions. Additionally, its antibacterial characteristics aid in preventing skin infections often associated with psoriasis.

Furthermore, burdock tea supports digestion, which is vital for overall health and well-being, thus indirectly contributing to the alleviation of psoriasis symptoms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in burdock tea that help with psoriasis.

  • Roots: Rich in antioxidants, burdock roots are used in medicinal tea for psoriasis to help reduce inflammation and promote skin health.
  • Leaves: Containing beneficial compounds, burdock leaves are used in medicinal tea for psoriasis to support skin cell renewal and soothe irritation.
  • Seeds: Packed with essential fatty acids, burdock seeds are used in medicinal tea for psoriasis to help alleviate dryness and itching.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in burdock tea that aid with psoriasis.

  • Arctiin: A lignan that possesses anti-inflammatory properties, helping to alleviate psoriasis-related inflammation.
  • Arctigenin: A phenolic compound that exhibits immunomodulatory effects, reducing the body's overactive immune response in psoriasis.
  • Quercetin: A flavonoid with antioxidant properties, helping to protect skin cells from damage caused by free radicals in psoriasis.
  • Resveratrol: A polyphenol that has anti-inflammatory and antipruritic effects, reducing redness, swelling, and itchiness in psoriasis.
  • Tannins: A group of astringent compounds that have anti-inflammatory effects, helping to soothe irritated skin in psoriasis.
  • Sesquiterpene lactones: A class of compounds that exhibit anti-inflammatory and antiproliferative effects, helping to control the overproduction of skin cells in psoriasis.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic burdock tea for psoriasis.

  1. Chop 2 tablespoons of dried burdock root into small pieces, place in a pot, and add 4 cups of water.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the burdock pieces, discarding the solid remains.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of burdock tea daily for several weeks to help manage psoriasis symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric tea for psoriasis.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a pot, then add 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric.
  2. Simmer the mixture on low heat for 10 minutes, then stir in 1 teaspoon of honey.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a cup, discarding the turmeric solids.
  4. Squeeze 1/2 lemon into the tea and stir well.
  5. Serve the turmeric tea hot, consuming up to 3 cups daily for psoriasis relief.

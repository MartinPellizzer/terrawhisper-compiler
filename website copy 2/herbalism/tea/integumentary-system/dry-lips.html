10 best herbal teas for dry lips

Herbal teas, like chamomile and rosehip, offer a natural approach to soothing dry lips.

Chamomile tea contains anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce redness and irritation, while rosehip tea is rich in vitamin C, promoting collagen production and hydration. Both teas can be brewed, allowed to cool, and then applied topically with a cotton swab to alleviate dryness and enhance lip health.

Regular application of these nourishing teas provides a chemical-free alternative to traditional lip care products.

1. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with dry lips because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes slippery and gel-like when mixed with water.

This gel-like substance forms a protective barrier on the lips, locking in moisture and preventing further dryness. Moreover, slippery elm tea has anti-inflammatory properties that soothe irritated and chapped lips.

Its demulcent nature provides instant relief by creating a soothing and cooling effect on dry lips. Additionally, slippery elm tea boosts the body's natural ability to produce collagen, which helps in maintaining healthy and moisturized lips.

Ultimately, the healing properties of slippery elm tea enable it to restore the natural moisture balance of the lips, ensuring long-lasting relief from dryness and chapping.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in slippery elm tea that help with dry lips.

  • Bark: Contains substantial amounts of mucilage, creating a protective film over the lips to lock in moisture.
  • Leaves: Rich in tannins and antioxidants, helping to soothe and heal chapped, dry lips.
  • Buds: High in vitamins and minerals, promoting cell repair and hydration for lips prone to dryness.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in slippery elm tea that aid with dry lips.

  • Tannins: These astringent compounds help to tighten and protect the skin on dry lips, reducing irritation and redness.
  • Mucilage: This soothing substance forms a protective barrier on the lips, retaining moisture to alleviate dryness and chapping.
  • Gallic acid: With its anti-inflammatory properties, gallic acid helps to reduce inflammation and redness caused by dry lips.
  • Kaempferol: This flavonoid exhibits antioxidant properties, protecting the lips from environmental stressors that can lead to dryness and aging.
  • Eugenol: This constituent has antiseptic and analgesic properties, helping to prevent infection and soothe any discomfort associated with dry, cracked lips.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for dry lips.

  1. Obtain 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark and 1 cup of water.
  2. Heat the water until it reaches a boiling point.
  3. Stir in the slippery elm bark, then reduce heat and simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea, discarding the used bark, and let it cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day for moisturizing effects on dry lips.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for dry lips.

  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the marshmallow root solids.
  5. Sip the marshmallow tea slowly, allowing it to moisturize your dry lips.

  3. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  4. Soak a clean washcloth in the chamomile tea and hold it against your lips for 5 minutes.
  5. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day, storing the leftover tea in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours.

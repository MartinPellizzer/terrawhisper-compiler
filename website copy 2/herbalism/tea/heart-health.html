10 best herbal teas for heart health

Herbal teas have been shown to have a profound impact on heart health.

One of the top performers is Hawthorn tea, which has been proven to strengthen the cardiovascular system and lower blood pressure. Another standout is Garlic tea, which has natural antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce triglycerides and LDL cholesterol. Additionally, Ginger tea has been found to have a positive effect on blood lipid levels and platelet function, while also providing a natural anti-inflammatory response.

Last but not least, Turmeric tea's active compound, curcumin, has potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help to protect against heart disease.

1. Hawthorn

Hawthorn tea benefits heart health due to its rich antioxidant content, including phenolic acids and flavonoids, which strengthen blood vessels and improve blood flow.

This herbal tea also contains active compounds such as oligomeric procyanidins, which widen blood vessels and lower blood pressure, reducing strain on the heart. Furthermore, hawthorn tea's positive inotropic effect strengthens the heart's contractions, increasing pumping efficiency and overall cardiovascular function.

Additionally, hawthorn tea's anti-inflammatory properties help protect the heart from damage caused by inflammation, contributing to long-term heart health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in hawthorn tea that help with heart health.

  • Berries: The berries are rich in antioxidants that support cardiovascular health.
  • Leaves: The leaves contain flavonoids that help reduce blood pressure and promote heart health.
  • Flowers: The flowers have anti-inflammatory properties that help improve heart function.
  • Bark: The bark contains proanthocyanidins that help lower cholesterol levels and improve heart health.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in hawthorn tea that help with heart health.

  • Flavonoids: These compounds have antioxidant properties that help protect the heart by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation.
  • Proanthocyanidins: These polyphenols help improve heart health by reducing blood pressure and increasing blood flow.
  • Oligomeric procyanidins: OPCs have been shown to improve heart health by reducing the risk of coronary artery disease and stroke.
  • Epicatechins: These flavanols help improve heart health by reducing inflammation and improving endothelial function.
  • Chlorogenic acid: This compound helps improve heart health by reducing blood pressure and improving lipid profiles.
  • Quercetin: This flavonoid helps improve heart health by reducing inflammation and oxidative stress.
  • Rutin: This flavonoid helps improve heart health by strengthening capillaries and reducing inflammation.
  • Hyperoside: This flavonoid helps improve heart health by reducing oxidative stress and improving endothelial function.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic hawthorn tea for heart health.

  1. In a pot, combine 2 cups of water and 2 tablespoons of dried hawthorn berries, then bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the hawthorn berries, then discard them.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day for heart health benefits.

2. Hibiscus

Hibiscus tea contains anthocyanins and antioxidants, which are beneficial compounds known to lower blood pressure.

A study published in the Journal of Ethnopharmacology shows that hibiscus tea decreases both systolic and diastolic blood pressures. The tea's antioxidant properties, as reported in the Food & Function journal, protect the heart by preventing oxidative stress and inflammation. Furthermore, hibiscus tea enhances lipid profiles by decreasing levels of LDL cholesterol and triglycerides.

Regular consumption of hibiscus tea, therefore, strengthens heart health by naturally reducing blood pressure, protecting against oxidative stress, and improving lipid profiles.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in hibiscus tea that help with heart health.

  • Flowers: Hibiscus flowers are rich in anthocyanins, which are known to help lower blood pressure and improve lipid profiles, contributing to heart health.
  • Leaves: Hibiscus leaves are a source of flavonoids and polyphenols, which have been shown to possess cardioprotective properties, aiding in the maintenance of a healthy heart.
  • Seeds: Hibiscus seeds contain omega-3 fatty acids, which are essential for heart health, helping to reduce the risk of cardiovascular diseases.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in hibiscus tea that help with heart health.

  • Anthocyanins: These are powerful antioxidants that help reduce inflammation and improve blood flow, which can help lower blood pressure and reduce the risk of heart disease.
  • Vitamin C: This essential nutrient helps maintain healthy blood vessels by supporting collagen production and acting as an antioxidant, which can help protect against heart disease.
  • Polyphenols: These compounds have been shown to reduce inflammation, improve blood pressure, and decrease LDL cholesterol levels, all of which contribute to better heart health.
  • Flavonoids: These antioxidants help protect the heart by reducing oxidative stress, inhibiting platelet aggregation, and improving endothelial function, which can lower the risk of cardiovascular diseases.
  • Organic acids: Hibiscus tea contains organic acids like citric, malic, and tartaric acids, which have been shown to help lower blood pressure and cholesterol levels, promoting heart health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic hibiscus tea for heart health.

  1. Measure - Precisely measure out 2 teaspoons of dried hibiscus flowers.
  2. Steep - Steep the flowers in 4 cups of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain - Strain the tea to remove the flowers, leaving only the liquid.
  4. Sweeten (optional) - Add honey or another sweetener to taste, if desired.
  5. Serve - Serve the tea hot or refrigerate for a refreshing cold beverage.

3. Green Tea

Green tea, abundant in bioactive compounds, provides significant heart health benefits.

It contains epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), a potent antioxidant that reduces artery blockages by decreasing inflammation and enhancing artery function. Moreover, green tea's catechins inhibit the absorption of cholesterol in the gut, lowering LDL cholesterol levels. Additionally, green tea consumption increases HDL cholesterol, the "good" cholesterol, promoting a healthy lipid profile.

Lastly, green tea's anti-hypertensive properties stem from its ability to relax blood vessels and inhibit angiotensin-converting enzyme, both of which contribute to maintaining healthy blood pressure.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in green tea tea that help with heart health.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, the leaves of green tea are known to reduce inflammation, improve blood flow, and help lower cholesterol levels, all of which contribute to a healthier heart.
  • Buds: Packed with catechins, the buds of green tea are known for their potent antioxidant properties, which aid in reducing the risk of cardiovascular diseases.
  • Seeds: The seeds of green tea, known as "kernels," contain essential fatty acids and vitamins that support heart health by reducing blood pressure and improving overall cardiovascular function.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in green tea tea that help with heart health.

  • Catechins: These are antioxidants that help reduce the risk of heart disease by decreasing inflammation and improving blood vessel function.
  • Epigallocatechin Gallate: This catechin helps lower levels of LDL (bad) cholesterol, reducing the risk of heart disease.
  • Theaflavins: These polyphenols have been shown to reduce blood pressure and cholesterol levels, promoting heart health.
  • Thearubigins: These compounds have been found to improve blood pressure, reducing the risk of heart-related issues.
  • Caffeine: While it is mainly known for its stimulant effects, caffeine in green tea also helps improve endothelial function, which can support heart health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic green tea tea for heart health.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of fresh water to 160-170°F, taking 3-5 minutes.
  2. Place one green tea bag or 1 teaspoon of loose leaf tea in a cup or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the tea, submerging the tea bag or leaves.
  4. Steep the tea for 2-3 minutes for a delicate flavor or 3-5 minutes for a stronger taste.
  5. Remove the tea bag or strain the leaves, then enjoy the heart-healthy green tea.

4. Black Tea

Black tea contains theaflavins and thearubigins, two types of flavonoids known for their antioxidant properties.

Antioxidants protect the body from harmful free radicals, which contribute to heart disease when unbalanced. Regular consumption of black tea reduces blood lipid levels, lowering the risk of atherosclerosis, a condition characterized by plaque buildup in the arteries. The beverage also improves endothelial function, which means it supports blood vessel health and flexibility, contributing to normal blood pressure.

Lastly, black tea's beneficial compounds inhibit blood clot formation, further reducing the risk of heart attacks and strokes.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in black tea tea that help with heart health.

  • Buds: The buds of the black tea plant are rich in antioxidants that protect the heart from damage and promote healthy blood pressure.
  • Stems: The stems of the black tea plant contain catechins that reduce the risk of heart disease by lowering cholesterol levels and preventing plaque buildup in arteries.
  • Bark: The bark of the black tea plant has anti-inflammatory properties that help relax blood vessels, reducing the risk of heart attacks and strokes.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the black tea plant contain essential fatty acids that support heart health by maintaining healthy cholesterol levels and preventing blood clots.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in black tea tea that help with heart health.

  • Caffeine: acts as a stimulant and can help improve heart function by stimulating the heart to beat stronger and faster, which can be beneficial in cases of bradycardia (a condition where the heart beats too slowly).
  • Theaflavins: have been shown to lower cholesterol levels, improve blood vessel function, and reduce the risk of heart disease.
  • Thearubigins: have antioxidant properties that can help protect the heart from damage caused by free radicals, and have been shown to lower blood pressure.
  • Catechins: have been shown to reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease by improving lipid profiles, reducing blood pressure, and inhibiting platelet aggregation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic black tea tea for heart health.

  1. Boil 1 cup of fresh, cold water in a kettle or pot for 3-5 minutes until it reaches a rolling boil.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of high-quality loose black tea leaves or 1 tea bag to a tea infuser or directly into the cup.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the tea leaves or tea bag, ensuring the water fully saturates the tea.
  4. Steep the tea for 3-5 minutes to allow the flavors and antioxidants to infuse into the water.
  5. Remove the tea leaves or bag and enjoy the hot black tea for a natural heart health boost.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for heart health.

  1. Peel and chop 1 inch of fresh ginger into small pieces, then place in a pot and add 4 cups of water.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Remove the pot from heat, add 2 teaspoons of green tea leaves, and let it steep for 3 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, add honey or lemon to taste, then let it cool for 2 minutes.
  5. Serve and enjoy the heart-healthy ginger tea.

  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the cinnamon.
  5. Optional: Add a teaspoon of honey or a lemon slice for flavor, and drink it while warm, 1-2 times a day.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric tea for heart health.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 4 cups of water and 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric; bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat to low, and let the mixture simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a cup, pressing the turmeric solids to extract all liquid.
  4. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey (or to taste) and 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper (to enhance turmeric's bioavailability) into the tea.
  5. Serve immediately, drink up to 2 cups daily for heart health benefits.

What is heart health and how can it affect your life?

Heart health refers to the condition of a person's cardiovascular system, encompassing the heart and blood vessels.

A healthy heart efficiently pumps blood, delivering oxygen and nutrients to vital organs. Conversely, an unhealthy heart faces challenges in effectively circulating blood, often due to factors like high blood pressure, cholesterol, or lifestyle choices. Poor heart health significantly impacts people's lives by increasing the risk of heart disease, stroke, and other life-threatening conditions.

By maintaining optimal heart health, individuals significantly enhance their overall well-being and longevity.

What other natural remedies help with heart health?

Among the most common and effective natural remedies for heart health are a balanced diet, regular exercise, and maintaining a healthy weight.

A heart-healthy diet includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats, while limiting sodium, saturated and trans fats, and added sugars. Regular exercise, such as brisk walking or cycling, can lower blood pressure, improve cholesterol levels, and maintain a healthy weight. Achieving and maintaining a healthy weight reduces the strain on the heart and decreases the risk of heart disease.

Additionally, managing stress through techniques such as meditation, yoga, or mindfulness can also contribute to optimal heart health.

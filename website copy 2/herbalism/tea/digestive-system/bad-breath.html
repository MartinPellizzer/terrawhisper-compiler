By drinking these teas regularly, people with bad breath can experience improved oral hygiene and fresh breath. By incorporating these herbal teas into their daily routine, people with bad breath can enjoy the benefits of improved oral hygiene and fresh breath.

1. Parsley tea

Parsley tea helps with bad breath because it contains chlorophyll, a natural deodorizer that neutralizes odors within the mouth.

  • Eugenol: Inhibits the growth of bacteria that cause bad breath.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic parsley tea tea for bad breath.

  1. Chop 1 cup of fresh parsley, discarding stems, and place in a heatproof pot.
  2. Pour 2 cups of boiling water over the parsley, cover, and let steep for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove parsley pieces, reserving the liquid.
  4. Squeeze half a lemon into the tea for additional flavor and breath-freshening properties.
  5. Drink tea while warm, up to three times a day, to help combat bad breath.

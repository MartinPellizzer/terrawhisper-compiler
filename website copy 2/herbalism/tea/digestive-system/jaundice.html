The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic milk thistle tea for jaundice.

  1. In a stove-top pot, pour 1 cup of water and bring it to a boil.

  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the pot and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove milk thistle seeds and discard them.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day.

What is jaundice and how can it affect your life?

Jaundice is a yellowing of the skin, eyes, and mucous membranes that indicates an excess of bilirubin, a waste product, in the blood.

This condition typically results from issues such as liver damage, gallstones, or blood disorders. Jaundice impacts people's lives significantly by causing discomfort and stress due to its potential underlying causes, which can lead to serious health complications. Additionally, jaundice might necessitate extensive medical evaluations and interventions, disrupting daily routines and imposing financial burdens.

As a result, jaundice has a profound impact on individuals' mental, emotional, and physical well-being.

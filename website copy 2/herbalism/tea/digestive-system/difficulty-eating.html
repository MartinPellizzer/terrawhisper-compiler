Chamomile tea alleviates difficulty eating by reducing inflammation in the digestive tract, as it contains anti-inflammatory properties.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with difficulty eating.

  • Flowers: Chamomile flowers contain bioactive compounds that have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help soothe the digestive tract and alleviate difficulty eating.
  • Leaves: Chamomile leaves are rich in flavonoids and terpenoids, which have been shown to have antioxidant and calming effects, aiding in digestion and reducing symptoms of difficulty eating.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are a source of essential oils and fatty acids, which can help stimulate the production of bile and digestive enzymes, improving overall digestion and easing difficulty eating.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with difficulty eating.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that binds to benzodiazepine receptors in the brain, helping to reduce anxiety and promoting relaxation, making it easier to eat.
  • Chamazulene: A chemical compound with anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, helping to soothe the stomach and alleviate digestive issues.
  • Bisabolol: A terpene with anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic properties, helping to reduce pain and inflammation in the digestive tract.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin has anti-inflammatory properties, helping to reduce swelling and promote digestion.
  • Spiroether: A compound with antioxidant properties, helping to protect the digestive system from damage caused by free radicals and oxidative stress.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for difficulty eating.

  1. Measure 1 teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool for 1 minute.
  3. Steep the chamomile flowers in the hot water for 5 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the chamomile flowers.
  5. Serve the tea warm, allowing it to cool slightly if necessary for ease of drinking.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for difficulty eating.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 1 cup of water, 1/4 cup of white sugar, and 1/4 cup of corn syrup; heat over medium heat for 5 minutes, stirring constantly, until sugar dissolves.
  2. Stir in 1 tablespoon of unflavored gelatin and remove from heat; let sit for 10 minutes to allow gelatin to soften.
  3. Return saucepan to low heat and stir mixture for 5-10 minutes until it becomes thick and marshmallow-like.
  4. Remove from heat and mix in 1 teaspoon of matcha powder (or more to taste) and 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract.
  5. Pour mixture into a silicone mold or shallow pan, let cool, then cut into small squares and store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks. Dissolve 1-2 squares in hot water to make a soothing marshmallow tea.

What is difficulty eating and how can it affect your life?

Difficulty eating is a condition that makes consuming food physically challenging.

This issue impacts people's lives by limiting their ability to take in necessary nutrients, leading to weight loss, malnutrition, and overall poor health.

It also affects social interactions, as mealtime with others becomes a source of anxiety and isolation, rather than a joyful experience.

What other natural remedies help with difficulty eating?

Ginger and apple cider vinegar are two of the most common and effective natural remedies for difficulty eating.

Ginger has long been known for its ability to alleviate digestive problems, including nausea and indigestion. It can be consumed in various forms such as fresh ginger root, ginger powder, or ginger capsules. Apple cider vinegar, on the other hand, stimulates digestive juices and increases stomach acid production, making it easier to break down food.

It is recommended to dilute apple cider vinegar with water and drink it before meals.

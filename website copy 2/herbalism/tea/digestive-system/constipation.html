1. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea further aids in soothing the gut lining, reducing inflammation, and increasing overall gut motility.

Ultimately, its demulcent properties and natural laxative effect make slippery elm tea an effective aid for constipation.

  • Roots: While not as commonly used as the bark, leaves, or seeds, the roots of the slippery elm plant can also be used in tea for constipation. They contain mucilage and can help add moisture to stools, making them easier to pass.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in slippery elm tea that aid with constipation.

  • Mucilage: Slippery elm tea contains mucilage, a gel-like substance that can help soften and add bulk to stools, making them easier to pass.

  • Starch: The tea contains starch, which can help slow down digestion and allow for more gradual absorption of nutrients, leading to more consistent bowel movements.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for constipation.

  1. Measure: Scoop 1 teaspoon of slippery elm bark powder.
  2. Mix: Blend the powder with 1 cup of cold water.
  3. Heat: Slowly warm the mixture in a pot over medium heat.
  4. Steep: Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Strain: Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve before drinking.

What is constipation and how can it affect your life?

Constipation is a common digestive problem characterized by infrequent bowel movements or difficulty in passing stools.

This condition results in hard, dry stools that are painful or difficult to pass, leading to discomfort and strain. Constipation impacts people's lives significantly, causing distress, and disruption in daily activities. It can result in absences from work or school, diminished productivity, and a reduced quality of life.

Moreover, chronic constipation can lead to more severe health issues, such as hemorrhoids, anal fissures, and fecal impaction, further impacting an individual's well-being.

What other natural remedies help with constipation?

Among the most common and effective natural remedies for constipation are dietary changes and increased water intake.

A diet rich in fiber, including fruits, vegetables, and whole grains, promotes regular bowel movements by adding bulk to stools and making them easier to pass. Adequate hydration, at least 8 cups of water per day, is crucial for maintaining soft, moist stools and preventing constipation.

Regular physical activity also stimulates the natural contraction of intestinal muscles, which helps to move waste through the digestive system. In addition, incorporating probiotics, such as those found in yogurt or kefir, introduces beneficial bacteria into the gut that support digestion and regularity.

By consistently implementing these natural remedies, individuals can alleviate constipation and maintain a healthy digestive system.

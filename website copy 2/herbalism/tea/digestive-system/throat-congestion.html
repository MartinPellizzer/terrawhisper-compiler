1. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea addresses throat congestion by forming a protective barrier with its demulcent properties, derived from the inner bark of the slippery elm tree.

This soothing barrier provides relief by reducing irritation and inflammation in the throat. The tea's mucilage content, which turns into a slick, gel-like substance when combined with water, facilitates expectoration and helps clear mucus and phlegm. Consequently, slippery elm tea's ability to alleviate irritation, minimize inflammation, and encourage mucus expulsion makes it an effective natural remedy for throat congestion.

This traditional herbal treatment has been trusted for generations and continues to provide soothing comfort for congested throats.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in slippery elm tea that help with throat congestion.

  • Bark: Contains high levels of antioxidants and mucilage, providing a soothing effect on irritated throat tissues.
  • Leaves: Rich in tannins and volatile oils, leaves help reduce inflammation and ease congestion in the respiratory system.
  • Buds: Packed with nutrients and antioxidants, buds have strong anti-inflammatory properties, alleviating throat discomfort and congestion.
  • Seeds: Loaded with essential fatty acids and minerals, seeds provide soothing and demulcent qualities, making them ideal for treating throat ailments.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in slippery elm tea that aid with throat congestion.

  • Mucilage: Slippery elm tea contains mucilage, a substance that becomes a slick gel when mixed with water. This gel coats and soothes irritated mucous membranes, providing relief from throat congestion.
  • Tannins: Tannins in slippery elm tea have astringent properties, which means they can help shrink and tighten inflamed tissues. This action helps reduce swelling in the throat, easing congestion.
  • Antioxidants: Slippery elm tea contains antioxidants that protect cells from damage caused by free radicals. These antioxidants can help reduce inflammation in the throat, alleviating congestion.
  • Starch: Starch in slippery elm tea acts as a demulcent, which means it can form a soothing film over mucous membranes. This film helps protect the throat from irritants, reducing congestion.
  • Polysaccharides: Polysaccharides in slippery elm tea stimulate the immune system, helping the body fight off infections that can cause throat congestion.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for throat congestion.

  1. Measure - To start, accurately measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark.
  2. Boil - Pour 8 ounces of water into a pot and bring it to a boil.
  3. Steep - Add the slippery elm bark to the boiling water, then let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain - Strain the tea to remove the bark, then pour it into a cup.
  5. Drink - Enjoy the soothing tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day as needed.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for throat congestion.

  1. Measure 2 cups of water and bring to a boil in a saucepan over high heat.
  2. Stir in 4 crushed marshmallows and 1 tablespoon of honey, reducing heat to low and simmering for 5 minutes.

  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a mug, discarding solids.
  5. Sip the warm marshmallow tea slowly, allowing it to soothe throat congestion.

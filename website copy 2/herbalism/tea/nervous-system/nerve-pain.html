10 best herbal teas for nerve pain

Herbal teas, such as St. John's Wort and valerian root teas, offer a natural remedy for nerve pain.

St. John's Wort contains hypericin, a compound with proven nerve-calming properties. Valerian root tea, on the other hand, has sedative effects that alleviate nerve tension and promote relaxation. Both teas boast anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce inflammation surrounding the nerves, thereby soothing pain.

Including these teas in your daily routine can provide long-term relief for nerve-related discomforts.

1. St. John'S Wort

St. John's Wort tea contains hypericin and hyperforin, active compounds known to alleviate nerve pain.

These compounds inhibit the reuptake of neurotransmitters such as serotonin, norepinephrine, and dopamine, thereby increasing their availability in the brain. By promoting the body's natural pain-relieving mechanisms, St. John's Wort tea helps reduce nerve pain. Additionally, it has anti-inflammatory properties that help decrease inflammation around nerves, further contributing to its pain-relieving effects.

Its soothing warmth also enhances relaxation and stress reduction, indirectly alleviating nerve-related discomfort.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in st. john's wort tea that help with nerve pain.

  • Flowers: Contain high levels of hypericin and hyperforin, which are believed to have pain-relieving and nerve-calming properties.
  • Leaves: Also contain hypericin and hyperforin, and are often used in tea preparations for their soothing effects on the nervous system.
  • Buds: Rich in antioxidants that protect nerves from damage, and may help reduce inflammation and alleviate nerve pain.
  • Stems: Contain valuable compounds, including flavonoids and essential oils, that contribute to the therapeutic effects of St. John's wort tea for nerve pain.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in st. john's wort tea that aid with nerve pain.

  • Hypericin: Inhibits the reuptake of neurotransmitters, reducing nerve pain.
  • Hyperforin: Possesses anti-inflammatory properties, alleviating nerve inflammation.
  • Flavonoids: Protect nerves from damage, supporting nerve health.
  • Essential oils: Contain nerve-soothing compounds, offering pain relief.
  • Tannins: Exhibit astringent properties, helping to reduce nerve swelling.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic st. john's wort tea for nerve pain.

  1. Measure - Pour 2 teaspoons of dried St. John's Wort flowers into a tea infuser.
  2. Steep - Place the infuser in a cup, then add boiling water and let it steep for 10 minutes.
  3. Stir - Remove the infuser and mix in honey to taste, if desired.
  4. Serve - Pour the tea into a cup, optionally straining it for a smoother texture.
  5. Sip - Enjoy the tea while it is still warm, up to three times a day for nerve pain relief.

This acid binds to GABA receptors in the brain, promoting relaxation and reducing nerve excitability. Consequently, valerian tea helps alleviate nerve pain by diminishing hypersensitivity and spasms, offering a more restful state. Furthermore, its antioxidant properties contribute to neuroprotection, potentially preventing further nerve damage.

Regular consumption of valerian tea can result in long-term pain management, enhancing overall quality of life.

  • Leaves: Contain antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that can help reduce inflammation and damage to nerve cells, thus helping to manage nerve pain.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian tea that aid with nerve pain.

  • Valerenic acid: Interacts with GABA receptors to produce a calming, relaxing effect that helps alleviate nerve pain.
  • Isovaleric acid: Acts as a muscle relaxant, reducing tension and spasms that can exacerbate nerve pain.
  • Hydroxyvaleronically acid: Possesses anti-inflammatory properties that help decrease inflammation associated with nerve pain.
  • Bornyl acetate: Enhances blood flow and circulation, allowing for better nutrient supply and pain reduction in nerves.
  • Linarin: Exhibits sedative effects that can help manage pain stemming from nerve irritation.
  • Flavanones: Demonstrate antioxidant capabilities, aiding in the neutralization of free radicals effecting nerve tissue, thus alleviating pain.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian tea for nerve pain.

  2. Steep - Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes in a covered container.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lavender tea for nerve pain.

  4. Strain the tea to remove lavender flowers, then pour it into a cup.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and enjoy the soothing lavender tea for nerve pain.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in kava kava tea that aid with nerve pain.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic kava kava tea for nerve pain.

  2. Bring the water to a boil in a saucepan, then add the kava kava root.
  3. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid into a cup, discarding the solid remains.
  5. Drink the kava kava tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day.

  • Stems: Contain beneficial compounds that have a calming effect on the nervous system, reducing pain and promoting relaxation.

  • Myrcene: A terpene with muscle relaxant and analgesic properties, helping to alleviate muscle tension and pain related to nerve damage.
  • Caryophyllene: A sesquiterpene that activates cannabinoid receptors, helping to reduce pain and inflammation in nerves.
  • Farnesene: A sesquiterpene that has been shown to have analgesic properties, helping to alleviate nerve pain.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic hops tea for nerve pain.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 2 cups of water and 2 tablespoons of dried hop flowers, bringing the mixture to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat to low, simmer the hop mixture, covered, for 15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  3. Strain the brewed tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a teacup or mug, discarding the spent hops.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and stir to dissolve.
  5. Drink the hops tea while warm, up to three times a day as needed for nerve pain relief.

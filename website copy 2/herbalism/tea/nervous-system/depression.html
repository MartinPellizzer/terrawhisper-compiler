10 best herbal teas for depression

Herbal teas, such as St. John's Wort and chamomile, provide a natural remedy for depression.

St. John's Wort contains hypericin, a compound that influences mood-regulating chemicals in the brain. Chamomile tea has antidepressant properties due to its calming effects, which reduce anxiety and promote better sleep.

Both teas offer a caffeine-free, soothing alternative to traditional remedies for depression, providing a safe and time-tested method for managing this condition.

1. St. John'S Wort

St. John's wort tea contains hypericin and hyperforin, two compounds known to inhibit the reuptake of neurotransmitters serotonin, norepinephrine, and dopamine.

By increasing the availability of these neurotransmitters in the brain, St. John's wort promotes mood regulation and alleviates depressive symptoms. This herbal tea also exhibits anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which contribute to its therapeutic effects on depression by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation in the brain. Furthermore, St. John's wort tea demonstrates a protective role in neurogenesis, the process of generating new neurons, enhancing neural connections and improving cognitive function.

It's important to note that St. John's wort interacts with various medications, so consulting a healthcare professional before starting this herbal remedy is advisable.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in st. john's wort tea that help with depression.

  • Flowers: Contain high levels of hypericin and hyperforin, active compounds believed to help alleviate depression.
  • Leaves: Rich in beneficial compounds, including hypericin and hyperforin, that contribute to the plant's antidepressant properties.
  • Stems: Although less potent than flowers and leaves, stems still contain compounds that can aid in treating depression.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in st. john's wort tea that aid with depression.

  • Hypericin: Inhibits the reuptake of serotonin, norepinephrine, and dopamine, which helps to balance neurotransmitter levels in the brain, alleviating depression symptoms.
  • Hyperforin: Enhances neurotransmitter availability by inhibiting the reuptake of serotonin, norepinephrine, and dopamine, thereby elevating mood and reducing depression.
  • Flavonoids: Possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that protect brain cells from damage, supporting overall brain health and reducing depression.
  • essential oils: Contribute to the overall synergistic effect of St. John's wort, possibly enhancing mood and reducing anxiety, although specific roles for individual components remain unclear.
  • Melatonin: Regulates sleep-wake cycles, promoting restorative sleep and reducing symptoms of depression related to sleep disturbances.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic st. john's wort tea for depression.

  1. Measure 2 teaspoons of dried St. John's wort plant material.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour over the St. John's wort.
  3. Steep the tea for 10 minutes, then strain out the plant material.
  4. Drink the tea, either hot or cold, up to three times a day.
  5. Discard any remaining tea after 24 hours.

Lavender tea delivers relief from depression due to its active ingredient, linalool, which possesses anxiolytic and antidepressant properties.

This natural remedy increases dopamine and serotonin levels, consequently uplifting mood and promoting happiness. Lavender tea also contains soothing qualities that decrease heart rate and blood pressure, fostering a sense of tranquility. Moreover, it enhances sleep quality, allowing individuals to experience restorative rest and improved emotional well-being.

Lastly, lavender tea's delightful aroma and taste provide a pleasurable sensory experience, further alleviating anxiety and contributing to overall mental health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in lavender tea that help with depression.

  • Flowers: Contain essential oils and have calming properties that help alleviate symptoms of depression.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and volatile oils, known to reduce anxiety and stress related to depression.
  • Buds: High in flavonoids and terpenes, shown to improve mood and promote relaxation.
  • Stems: Contribute to the plant's overall soothing effect, aiding in the relief of depression-related symptoms.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in lavender tea that aid with depression.

  • Linalool: This terpene alcohol is known for its relaxing and calming properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of depression.
  • Limonene: This aromatic compound has mood-elevating and anxiety-reducing effects, making it beneficial for people dealing with depression.
  • Eucalyptol: With its ability to reduce mental exhaustion and improve cognitive function, eucalyptol can help combat depression-related fatigue and brain fog.
  • Caryophyllene: This sesquiterpene exhibits antidepressant properties, possibly by interacting with the body's endocannabinoid system.
  • Falcarinol: This polyacetylene has been shown to have antidepressant-like effects, likely through its interaction with neurotransmitter systems.
  • Rosmarinic acid: As a potent antioxidant, rosmarinic acid helps protect brain cells from oxidative stress, which is associated with depression.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lavender tea for depression.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water to 212°F (100°C) for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried organic culinary lavender flowers to a tea infuser.
  3. Place the tea infuser in a cup and pour the hot water over it.
  4. Steep the lavender in the hot water for 5-7 minutes to extract the aromatic compounds.
  5. Remove the tea infuser and enjoy the soothing lavender tea, preferably without added sugar, as a natural mood booster.

  • Seeds: Packed with antioxidants and essential oils that help support a healthy nervous system, alleviating depressive episodes.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with depression.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that binds to benzodiazepine receptors in the brain, helping to reduce anxiety and promote sleep.
  • Chamazulene: A terpene with anti-inflammatory properties, helping to alleviate depressive symptoms caused by inflammation.
  • Bisabolol: A sesquiterpene alcohol with anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and calming effects, helping to soothe nerves and reduce anxiety.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for depression.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to 212°F, then pour over 1-2 tablespoons of dried chamomile flowers.
  2. Steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the herb's beneficial compounds.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers, retaining only the liquid.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added soothing or tangy flavors.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea while warm, slowly sipping to promote calmness and relaxation.

  • Leaves: Contain high levels of flavonoids, which are antioxidants that can help reduce inflammation and improve brain function, potentially alleviating symptoms of depression.
  • Seeds: Rich in terpene trilactones, which are thought to have a calming effect on the nervous system, possibly helping to reduce stress and anxiety associated with depression.
  • Bark: Contains ginkgolides, unique compounds that have been shown to improve circulation and increase blood flow to the brain, potentially enhancing mood and cognitive function in people with depression.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginkgo biloba tea that aid with depression.

  • Flavonoids: These are powerful antioxidants that protect the brain from damage, improving brain function and alleviating depression.
  • Terpene Lactones: This constituent, specifically Ginkgolide B, has a significant impact on depression by inhibiting the Platelet-Activating Factor (PAF), which plays a crucial role in depression.
  • Proanthocyanidins: These are antioxidants that help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation, both of which are linked to depression.
  • Bilobalide: This constituent protects the brain cells from damage and enhances cognitive function, helping to alleviate symptoms of depression.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginkgo biloba tea for depression.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to 212 degrees Fahrenheit, then steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried ginkgo biloba leaves for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove leaves, discarding them safely.
  3. Let the tea cool down for 2-3 minutes if desired.
  4. Pour the tea into a cup and add honey or lemon for taste if preferred.
  5. Drink the tea immediately for optimal benefits, up to three times a day.

  5. Drink the passionflower tea while it's still warm, up to 3 times daily, to help alleviate symptoms of depression.

6. Lemon Balm

Lemon balm tea contains rosmarinic acid, a compound that inhibits GABA transaminase, an enzyme that breaks down gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a neurotransmitter that regulates anxiety and depression.

By inhibiting this enzyme, lemon balm tea promotes increased GABA levels, which in turn alleviates anxiety and mild depression symptoms. Furthermore, lemon balm has antioxidant properties that protect brain cells from oxidative stress, contributing to overall brain health and emotional well-being. Additionally, it enhances mood by promoting feelings of calmness and boosting cognitive function.

Lastly, it has been shown in multiple studies that lemon balm's soothing effects aid sleep quality, which is crucial for mental health and overall well-being.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in lemon balm tea that help with depression.

  • Leaves: Contain rosmarinic acid and eugenol, which have antidepressant properties.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants that help reduce oxidative stress, a factor linked to depression.
  • Stems: Contain essential oils and tannins that contribute to the calming effects of lemon balm tea.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in lemon balm tea that aid with depression.

  • Rosmarinic acid: Possesses anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, which are associated with depression.
  • Eugenol: Exhibits anxiolytic and analgesic effects that can alleviate anxiety and pain, often co-occurring with depression.
  • Citronellal: Acts as a nervine and relaxant, helping to promote relaxation and reduce symptoms of depression.
  • Nerol: Functions as an antidepressant and antioxidant, helping to combat depressive symptoms and oxidative stress.
  • Geraniol: Displays antidepressant and neuroprotective properties that can help protect the brain and improve mood.
  • Caryophyllene: Offers anxiolytic and antidepressant effects, reducing anxiety and depressive symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lemon balm tea for depression.

  1. Harvest 2 tablespoons of fresh lemon balm leaves, crushing them to release their oils.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool for 1 minute.
  3. Place the crushed lemon balm leaves in a tea infuser and pour the hot water over them.
  4. Steep the tea for 5-10 minutes, then remove the infuser.
  5. Drink the lemon balm tea, preferably 2-3 times a day, to help alleviate symptoms of depression.

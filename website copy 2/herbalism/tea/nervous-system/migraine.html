10 best herbal teas for migraine

Herbal teas, such as chamomile, feverfew, and ginger, offer natural relief for migraines.

Chamomile, with its calming properties, soothes the mind and promotes relaxation. Feverfew, known for its anti-inflammatory effects, helps alleviate pain and reduce the frequency of migraines. Ginger tea eases nausea and vomiting, common symptoms associated with migraines, while providing a soothing warmth.

When suffering from a migraine, consider these herbal teas as a gentle, caffeine-free alternative to ease your discomfort.

1. Feverfew

Feverfew, a plant in the daisy family, produces a tea that benefits migraine relief due to its active compounds, including parthenolide.

Parthenolide inhibits the release of substances that widen blood vessels in the brain, a known cause of migraines. By doing so, feverfew tea helps restore normal blood flow and reduce inflammation. Additionally, feverfew tea prevents platelet aggregation, minimizing the risk of blood clots that could induce or worsen migraines.

Finally, feverfew tea boasts antispasmodic properties that relax constricted muscles around blood vessels, further alleviating migraine symptoms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in feverfew tea that help with migraine.

  • Leaves: Contain parthenolide, a compound shown to inhibit the release of chemicals that cause migraines.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants and flavonoids, helping reduce inflammation and providing relief from migraines.
  • Buds: High in parthenolide and other compounds, buds can ease spasms and reduce pain associated with migraines.
  • Seeds: Contain essential oils, which can help relax muscles and alleviate tension, a common cause of migraines.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in feverfew tea that aid with migraine.

  • Parthenolide: Inhibits the release of prostaglandins and serotonin, which are inflammatory substances that can cause migraines.
  • Tanacetin: A sesquiterpene lactone that helps to reduce inflammation and relieve migraine symptoms.
  • Camphor: A natural analgesic and anti-inflammatory compound that can help to alleviate migraine pain.
  • Artabsin: A sesquiterpene lactone that helps to reduce muscle spasms and alleviate tension headaches.
  • Sesquiterpene lactones: A group of compounds that have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making them effective at reducing the severity and frequency of migraines.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic feverfew tea for migraine.

  1. Gather 2-3 fresh feverfew leaves or 1 teaspoon of dried feverfew leaves.
  2. Rinse the fresh leaves under cold water, then chop them finely.
  3. Place the leaves in a teapot and add 250ml (8oz) of boiling water.
  4. Steep the tea for 5-10 minutes, then strain it into a cup.
  5. Drink the tea slowly, preferably with a meal, to alleviate migraine symptoms.

2. Butterbur

Butterbur tea contains petasins, chemical compounds with proven anti-inflammatory and muscle-relaxing properties.

These petasins inhibit the production of leukotrienes and prostaglandins, substances that cause blood vessel dilation and inflammation in the brain, leading to migraine attacks. Double-blind studies demonstrate that butterbur extract reduces migraine frequency and severity by up to 50% in some individuals. Moreover, the American Headache Society and the American Academy of Neurology recognize butterbur as an effective preventive treatment for migraines.

Its consumption leads to a significant decrease in migraine symptomatology.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in butterbur tea that help with migraine.

  • Leaves: Contain petasins, anti-inflammatory compounds that help reduce migraine frequency and intensity.
  • Rhyzomes: Rich in essential oils and nutrients, contributing to the plant's effectiveness in alleviating migraine symptoms.
  • Buds: High in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties, providing additional relief for migraines.
  • Flowers: Contain valuable phytochemicals that aid in soothing and calming the nervous system, therefore reducing migraine pain.
  • Roots: Provide a source of essential nutrients and bioactive compounds, enhancing the therapeutic effects of butterbur tea for migraines.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in butterbur tea that aid with migraine.

  • Petasin: Relaxes smooth muscle tissue and reduces inflammation, which can help prevent migraines.
  • Isopetasin: Acts as a natural analgesic and antispasmodic, providing relief from migraine symptoms.
  • Phenylethanoid glycosides: Possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help alleviate migraine-related inflammation.
  • Flavonoids: Provide neuroprotective effects by scavenging free radicals and stabilizing cell membranes, offering additional relief for migraines.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic butterbur tea for migraine.

  1. Measure - Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down to 212 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Prepare - Cut 2 teaspoons of dried butterbur root into small pieces.
  3. Steep - Place the butterbur pieces in a tea infuser and steep in the hot water for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain - Remove the tea infuser and discard the butterbur pieces.
  5. Serve - Enjoy the butterbur tea while it's still warm, up to 3 times a day.

  • Limonene: A terpene that acts as a natural stress reliever and mood elevator, helping to alleviate migraine-induced anxiety and depression.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for migraine.

  2. Crush 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves to increase the release of menthol, a proven anti-inflammatory agent.
  3. Steep the crushed peppermint leaves in boiling water for 5-10 minutes to extract the medicinal compounds.
  4. Strain the liquid to remove the spent peppermint leaves, eliminating any potential bitterness.
  5. Sip the hot peppermint tea slowly, allowing the soothing warmth and calming properties to alleviate your migraine symptoms.

4. Ginger

Ginger, a key ingredient in tea for migraines, contains compounds that inhibit prostaglandin synthesis, reducing inflammation and pain.

Gingerols and shogaols found in ginger exhibit strong anti-inflammatory properties similar to NSAIDs and COX-2 inhibitors, providing relief without the side effects. A 2014 study in the Phytotherapy Research journal reveals ginger's ability to match sumatriptan in migraine alleviation while causing fewer adverse reactions. Furthermore, ginger's antioxidative and antiemetic effects make it an ideal natural remedy to soothe migraine symptoms.

Consequently, drinking ginger tea delivers these beneficial compounds directly, easing pain and inflammation associated with migraines.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in ginger tea that help with migraine.

  • Rhizomes: Contain anti-inflammatory compounds that help alleviate migraine symptoms.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, helping reduce oxidative stress linked to migraines.
  • Buds: High in essential oils, providing soothing effects for migraine relief.
  • Seeds: Possess analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties, aiding in pain management.
  • Flowers: Contain flavonoids that help reduce inflammation and ease migraine tension.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginger tea that aid with migraine.

  • Gingerols: Gingerols found in ginger tea possess strong anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce prostaglandin synthesis, a key contributor to migraine pain.
  • Shogaols: These compounds show potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, helping alleviate migraine-associated pain and inflammation.
  • Paradols: Paradols in ginger tea exhibit notable anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, contributing to the reduction of oxidative stress and inflammation in migraines.
  • Zingerone: Zingerone has been shown to inhibit the release of neuropeptides involved in migraine development, lessening the severity and frequency of migraine attacks.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for migraine.

  1. Peel and grate 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger root.
  2. Boil 4 cups of water, then add grated ginger, reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat, optionally add 1 teaspoon of honey and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice.
  4. Strain the tea to remove ginger pieces.
  5. Serve brewed ginger tea while still warm to aid in relieving migraine symptoms.

5. Willow

Willow tea provides relief from migraines due to its salicin content, a compound similar to aspirin.

This natural anti-inflammatory agent found in willow bark works by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, chemicals that cause inflammation and pain in blood vessels surrounding the brain. By reducing inflammation, willow tea alleviates the constriction and dilation of blood vessels, which are primary factors leading to migraine headaches. Furthermore, willow bark has been proven to possess fever-reducing and analgesic properties, making it an effective herbal remedy for migraine relief.

Lastly, willow tea's pain-relieving abilities stem from its ability to block the production of inflammatory substances, effectively reducing the symptoms associated with migraines.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in willow tea that help with migraine.

  • Bark: The bark of the willow tea plant contains salicin, which is a natural pain reliever and is effective in treating migraines.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the willow tea plant are rich in flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and vasoactive properties, making them useful in treating migraines.
  • Rhyzomes: The rhyzomes of the willow tea plant are a potent source of salicylic acid, a proven pain-reliever and anti-inflammatory agent that is helpful in managing migraines.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in willow tea that aid with migraine.

  • Salicin: A natural anti-inflammatory compound that helps reduce swelling and pain in blood vessels, which is associated with migraines.
  • Flavonoids: These antioxidants have been shown to improve cerebral blood flow and provide relief from migraine symptoms.
  • Tannins: Known for their astringent properties, tannins help to narrow blood vessels, reducing the risk of migraine-related swelling.
  • Phenolic acids: These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, which can alleviate pain and discomfort caused by migraines.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic willow tea for migraine.

  1. Gather 2-3 willow bark branches, peel off the outer bark, and cut the inner bark into small pieces (10-15 minutes).
  2. Place 1 tablespoon of willow bark pieces in a pot, add 2 cups of water, and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat, simmer for 10-15 minutes, then remove from heat.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the willow bark pieces.
  5. Drink the willow tea while warm, up to 3 times a day for migraine relief.

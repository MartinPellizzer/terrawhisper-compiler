10 best herbal teas for sleep

A soothing cup of herbal tea before bed has been a timeless remedy for a restful night's sleep.

Valerian root tea, with its calming properties, helps to quiet the mind and relax the body, making it an ideal choice for a peaceful slumber. Alternatively, a blend of chamomile, lavender, and lemon balm teas creates a tranquil atmosphere, calming the nervous system and promoting a deep sleep. Meanwhile, passionflower tea has been shown to reduce anxiety and insomnia, gently guiding you into a dreamless sleep.

Whether you prefer a single herb or a blend, these herbal teas are sure to promote a rejuvenating sleep.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains the antioxidant apigenin, which binds to certain receptors in the brain that promote sleep and reduces insomnia.

This herbal tea also has relaxing properties that come from its daisy-like flowers, offering a calming effect on the body and mind. By consuming chamomile tea, individuals experience a decrease in anxiety and restlessness, making it easier to fall asleep. Furthermore, chamomile tea does not contain caffeine, a stimulant that interferes with sleep, so it won't hinder your ability to get a good night's rest.

In conclusion, the soothing qualities of chamomile tea make it an effective natural aid for those who struggle with sleep.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with sleep.

  • Flowers: Chamomile's daisy-like flowers are ideal for medicinal tea for sleep due to their calming properties.
  • Leaves: The leaves of chamomile contain soothing compounds, making them a popular ingredient in sleep-promoting teas.
  • Seeds: Rich in sleep-inducing antioxidants, chamomile seeds contribute to the medicinal benefits of tea intended for sleep.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with sleep.

  • Apigenin: This flavonoid binds to benzodiazepine receptors in the brain, helping to promote sleep and reduce anxiety.
  • Bisabolol: This terpene alcohol has anti-inflammatory and muscle relaxation properties, which can help improve sleep quality.
  • Chamazulene: This aromatic compound has anti-inflammatory and calming effects, helping to alleviate sleep disturbances caused by stress and inflammation.
  • Matricin: This proazulene is converted to chamazulene upon steam distillation, and it contributes to the overall calming and soothing effects of chamomile tea, aiding in better sleep.
  • Spiroether: This sesquiterpene lactone has a mild sedative effect, which can further enhance the sleep-promoting properties of chamomile tea.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for sleep.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Place 1 chamomile tea bag or 1 tablespoon of loose chamomile flowers in a tea infuser.
  3. Pour the hot water over the tea bag or infuser into a cup.
  4. Steep the tea for 5-10 minutes to extract the flavor and medicinal properties.
  5. Remove the tea bag or infuser and enjoy your chamomile tea before bedtime.

2. Valerian

Valerian tea contains compounds that bind to certain receptors in the brain, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety.

These compounds include valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, which have sedative properties known to induce sleep. Valerian tea also increases the amount of gamma aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain, a neurotransmitter that blocks nerve impulses, resulting in a calming effect. Furthermore, it decreases the breakdown of GABA, prolonging its sedative effects.

Lastly, valerian tea does not lead to dependence or pose risks of withdrawal symptoms, making it a safe and natural sleep aid.

  • Leaves: Valerian leaves are rich in volatile oils that help calm the nervous system, thereby improving sleep quality.
  • Flowers: Valerian flowers contain flavonoids that have a calming effect on the body, making them useful for sleep teas.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian tea that aid with sleep.

  • Valerenic acid: This compound found in valerian root may help increase the amount of gamma aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain, which can have a calming effect and help promote sleep.
  • Valepotriates: These iridoid compounds have been shown to have sedative and muscle relaxant properties, which can help improve sleep quality.
  • Isovaleric acid: This compound found in valerian root has been suggested to have a sedative effect, which can help reduce the time it takes to fall asleep.
  • Hydroxyvaleric acid: This compound may help increase the amount of GABA in the brain, which can help promote relaxation and improve sleep quality.
  • Flavanones: These antioxidant compounds found in valerian root may help reduce anxiety and improve sleep quality by reducing the activity of stress-inducing neurotransmitters in the brain.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian tea for sleep.

  1. In a teapot, add 1 teaspoon of dried valerian root to 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the tea for 10-15 minutes, covered, to extract the medicinal compounds.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the valerian root pieces.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink the tea 30-60 minutes before bedtime for improved sleep.

What is sleep and how can it affect your life?

Sleep is a vital physiological process during which the body rests, repairs, and rejuvenates.

It is an essential activity that allows the brain to consolidate memories, process information, and maintain cognitive functions. Sleep impacts people's lives in numerous ways, improving their overall well-being. A good night's sleep enhances mood, memory, creativity, and productivity, while lack of sleep can lead to irritability, fatigue, and decreased performance.

Moreover, sleep plays a crucial role in maintaining physical health, as it strengthens the immune system, regulates metabolism, and promotes growth and development.

What other natural remedies help with sleep?

Among the most common and effective natural remedies for sleep are melatonin, a hormone that regulates sleep-wake cycles, and cognitive-behavioral therapy for insomnia (CBT-I), a structured program that helps individuals identify and change thoughts and behaviors that interfere with sleep.

Another popular natural sleep aid is progressive muscle relaxation, a method of tensing and then releasing each muscle group in the body to promote relaxation. Additionally, maintaining a consistent sleep schedule and creating a comfortable sleep environment are also effective strategies to improve sleep.

Furthermore, engaging in regular physical activity and avoiding stimulating activities before bedtime can significantly contribute to better sleep quality.

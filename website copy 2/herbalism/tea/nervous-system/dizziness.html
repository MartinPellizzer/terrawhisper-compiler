10 best herbal teas for dizziness

The top herbal teas for dizziness include ginger, chamomile, and licorice root.

Ginger tea, known for its anti-inflammatory properties, helps alleviate dizziness caused by inner ear disturbances. Chamomile tea, a natural relaxant, soothes nerves and promotes balance, reducing feelings of lightheadedness.

Licorice root tea, a powerful adaptogen, stabilizes cortisol levels and improves circulation, thus reducing symptoms of dizziness.

1. Ginger

Ginger tea alleviates dizziness due to its ability to restore balance in the inner ear.

This herbal tea contains gingerols and shogaols, compounds known to ease motion sickness and vertigo. By promoting blood flow, ginger tea helps reduce feelings of lightheadedness and disorientation. Furthermore, ginger's anti-inflammatory properties soothe irritated nerve endings in the ear, diminishing sensations of dizziness.

Ginger tea's positive effects on digestion also contribute to its effectiveness, as a stable gastrointestinal system plays a crucial role in maintaining equilibrium and preventing dizziness.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in ginger tea that help with dizziness.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger rhizomes are widely used to make medicinal tea for dizziness due to their potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
  • Leaves: Ginger leaves, when brewed into tea, possess mild sedative and aromatic qualities that can help alleviate symptoms of dizziness.
  • Buds: Fresh ginger buds in tea provide a rich source of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds to help combat dizziness and nausea.
  • Flowers: Ginger flowers are used in teas for their calming effects on the digestive system and their ability to promote balance, reducing dizziness and lightheadedness.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginger tea that aid with dizziness.

  • Gingerols: Gingerols are compounds in ginger that help to reduce dizziness by having anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to improve circulation and reduce inflammation in the inner ear.
  • Shogaols: Shogaols are compounds in ginger that have been shown to have a sedative effect on the stomach, which can help to reduce dizziness caused by nausea or motion sickness.
  • Paradols: Paradols are compounds in ginger that have antiemetic properties, which can help to relieve dizziness caused by nausea and vomiting.
  • Zingibain: Zingibain is an enzyme in ginger that has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce dizziness caused by inflammation in the inner ear.
  • Gingerols-related compounds: Gingerols-related compounds in ginger have been shown to have a positive effect on blood circulation, which can help to reduce dizziness caused by poor blood flow to the brain.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for dizziness.

  1. Peel and grate 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger root.
  2. Boil 2 cups of water, then add grated ginger and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the ginger tea into a cup, discarding the ginger pieces.
  4. Add honey and lemon juice to taste, stir to dissolve the honey.
  5. Drink the hot ginger tea to alleviate dizziness.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with dizziness.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for dizziness.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water to 212 degrees Fahrenheit for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers to a teapot or infuser.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent flowers.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea while warm, up to 3 times a day as needed.

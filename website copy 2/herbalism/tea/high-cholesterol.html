10 best herbal teas for high cholesterol

Many herbal teas, such as garlic, artichoke, and turmeric tea, have a proven record in reducing high cholesterol levels.

These teas contain potent antioxidants that help lower LDL (bad) cholesterol, while raising HDL (good) cholesterol. Moreover, hawthorn and psyllium teas exhibit similar benefits.

Regularly drinking at least one of these teas daily contributes to improved cardiovascular health, as part of a balanced lifestyle and diet.

1. Ashwagandha

Ashwagandha tea reduces high cholesterol by decreasing the levels of bad cholesterol, also known as low-density lipoprotein (LDL), in the blood.

It accomplishes this by increasing the size of lipoprotein particles, making them less likely to get oxidized and clog arteries. Regular consumption of ashwagandha tea enhances the liver's ability to remove LDL from the body, further promoting cardiovascular health. Additionally, it stimulates the production of high-density lipoprotein (HDL), the "good" cholesterol that helps eliminate LDL from the bloodstream.

Consequently, ashwagandha tea's active compounds foster a healthier cholesterol balance, contributing to overall heart well-being.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ashwagandha tea that help with high cholesterol.

  • Roots: The roots of the ashwagandha plant are used to make medicinal tea for high cholesterol due to their adaptogenic properties that help to balance and stabilize the body's systems, including reducing cholesterol levels.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the ashwagandha plant are used in medicinal tea for high cholesterol as they contain steroidal lactones that have a hypolipidemic effect, reducing the levels of fats, including cholesterol, in the blood.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the ashwagandha plant are used in medicinal tea for high cholesterol due to their ability to lower cholesterol levels and improve the lipid profile, reducing the risk of cardiovascular diseases.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ashwagandha tea that help with high cholesterol.

  • Withanolides: These compounds in ashwagandha help reduce high cholesterol by inhibiting the accumulation of cholesterol in the body.
  • Sitoindosides: These constituents aid in managing high cholesterol by activating the liver's ability to remove cholesterol from blood circulation.
  • Alkaloids: Ashwagandha alkaloids help reduce high cholesterol by improving the metabolism of lipids in the body.
  • Steroidal lactones: These compounds aid in reducing high cholesterol by reducing the levels of bad cholesterol and increasing good cholesterol.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ashwagandha tea for high cholesterol.

  1. In a pot, boil 2 cups of water for 5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried ashwagandha root and reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Let the tea steep for 10 minutes, then strain out the solids.
  4. Stir in 1 tablespoon of honey and 1/4 teaspoon of cinnamon powder.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, once or twice daily, for best results.

2. Guggul

Guggul tea, derived from the resin of the Commiphora mukul tree, contains guggulsterones known to interfere with cholesterol absorption and production in the body.

By decreasing cholesterol synthesis in the liver and increasing its elimination, guggulsterones promote healthy cholesterol levels. Research studies back this mechanism, providing evidence of its lipid-lowering effects. Furthermore, guggul tea activates receptors that aid in the removal of cholesterol from the bloodstream.

Consequently, regular consumption of guggul tea helps maintain balanced cholesterol levels and supports cardiovascular health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in guggul tea that help with high cholesterol.

  • Guggul Resin: Obtained from the bark of the tree, it contains substances that help lower cholesterol levels.
  • Guggul Seeds: Rich in active compounds, these seeds help reduce high cholesterol by improving liver function and promoting the excretion of cholesterol-containing bile.
  • Guggul Leaves: Containing numerous beneficial polyphenols, these leaves help lower cholesterol by inhibiting its absorption in the intestines.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in guggul tea that help with high cholesterol.

  • Guggulsterones: These are plant steroids that help lower cholesterol levels by increasing the breakdown and removal of low-density lipoprotein (LDL), or "bad" cholesterol, from the bloodstream.
  • Z-guggulsterone: This compound inhibits the production of new cholesterol in the liver, reducing overall cholesterol levels.
  • E-guggulsterone: This compound helps reduce the oxidation of LDL cholesterol, preventing the formation of plaque in the arteries.
  • Guggulipid: This resin extract from the guggul tree contains a mix of guggulsterones that have been shown to lower total and LDL cholesterol, while increasing HDL "good" cholesterol.
  • Polyphenols: These antioxidants help prevent the oxidation of LDL cholesterol, reducing the risk of plaque formation in the arteries.
  • Flavonoids: These antioxidants help reduce inflammation and improve heart health, which can help lower cholesterol levels.
  • Triterpenes: These compounds have anti-inflammatory and cholesterol-lowering properties, which can help improve cardiovascular health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic guggul tea for high cholesterol.

  1. Measure out 1 teaspoon of guggul resin and 1 cup of water.
  2. Grind the guggul resin into a powder using a mortar and pestle.
  3. Bring the water to a boil, then mix in the ground guggul resin.
  4. Let the mixture simmer for 10 minutes, then strain out the solids.
  5. Consume the strained tea while hot, 2-3 times a day after meals.

Turmeric tea contains curcumin, a compound recognized for its cholesterol-lowering properties.

This compound reduces cholesterol levels by inhibiting the intestinal absorption of dietary cholesterol and promoting its excretion from the body. These combined effects make turmeric tea an effective tool for managing high cholesterol.

  • Roots: The roots of the turmeric tea plant are rich in curcumin and other compounds that can help reduce cholesterol levels, making it a commonly used part for medicinal tea.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the turmeric tea plant contain essential oils and antioxidants that can help lower cholesterol levels, making it a valuable addition to medicinal tea.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in turmeric tea that help with high cholesterol.

  • Essential oils: Stimulate bile production in the liver, enhancing the removal of cholesterol from the body.
  • Bisdemethoxycurcumin: Possesses anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, aiding in the prevention of cholesterol-induced atherosclerosis.
  • Demethoxycurcumin: Inhibits cholesterol uptake by intestinal cells, reducing the absorption of dietary cholesterol.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric tea for high cholesterol.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 4 cups of water, 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric, and 1/4 teaspoon of freshly ground black pepper. (Turmeric is fat-soluble, and black pepper enhances its absorption.)
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes to allow the turmeric to infuse.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a cup, pressing the solids to extract all liquid.
  4. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey (to taste) and 1 tablespoon of extra-virgin olive oil (for better absorption of turmeric) while the tea is still warm.
  5. Serve immediately and enjoy daily for best results in managing high cholesterol levels.

Ginger tea reduces high cholesterol by containing antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that actively lower the production of cholesterol in the liver.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with high cholesterol.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger rhizomes are rich in antioxidants and have been shown to lower cholesterol levels by reducing serum and liver cholesterol.
  • Seeds: Ginger seeds contain active compounds that inhibit lipid peroxidation, which aids in maintaining healthy cholesterol levels.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginger tea that help with high cholesterol.

  • Gingerols: Gingerols are antioxidant compounds that help lower cholesterol levels by preventing the oxidation of low-density lipoprotein (LDL), or "bad" cholesterol.
  • Zingerone: Zingerone, a phenolic compound in ginger, helps reduce cholesterol absorption in the gut, thus lowering overall cholesterol levels in the body.
  • Shogaols: Shogaols are pungent compounds in ginger that inhibit the production of cholesterol in the liver, helping to maintain healthy cholesterol levels.
  • Paradols: Paradols, another group of antioxidants in ginger, contribute to reducing cholesterol by preventing plaque buildup in arteries and improving blood flow.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for high cholesterol.

  1. Peel and grate 1-inch of fresh ginger; steep in 4 cups of boiling water for 10 minutes.
  2. Stir in 1 teaspoon of honey and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice; mix thoroughly.
  3. Add 1/4 teaspoon of cinnamon powder and 1/8 teaspoon of black pepper; blend well.
  4. Strain the tea to remove ginger pieces; serve hot.
  5. Sip slowly, allowing the tea to cool, to enjoy maximum benefits.

8. Green Tea

The polyphenols in green tea also stimulate the expression of LDL receptors in the liver, enhancing LDL clearance from the bloodstream.   4. Steep the tea for 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse and release beneficial antioxidants.
  5. Remove the tea bag or strain out the leaves before drinking the tea. Enjoy up to three cups daily for best results.

What is high cholesterol and how can it affect your life?

High cholesterol is a condition characterized by excessive levels of cholesterol in the blood.

This excess leads to the formation of plaques in the arteries, a process known as atherosclerosis. As a result, the flow of oxygen-rich blood to critical organs, such as the heart and brain, becomes restricted. Over time, this restriction results in an increased risk of cardiovascular diseases, including heart attacks and strokes.

These conditions significantly impact people's lives, often leading to reduced mobility, cognitive impairment, and even death.

What other natural remedies help with high cholesterol?

Among the most common and effective natural remedies for high cholesterol are soluble fiber, nuts, and plant sterols.

Soluble fiber, found in foods like oats, barley, and certain fruits and vegetables, works by binding to cholesterol in the digestive system and carrying it out of the body. Nuts, such as almonds and walnuts, contain monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats that help lower LDL (bad) cholesterol levels. Plant sterols, found in foods like vegetable oils, margarines, and fortified foods, block the absorption of cholesterol in the digestive system.

By incorporating these natural remedies into a balanced diet, individuals can effectively manage their high cholesterol levels.

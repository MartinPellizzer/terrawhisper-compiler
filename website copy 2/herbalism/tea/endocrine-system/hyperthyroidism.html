10 best herbal teas for hyperthyroidism

Hyperthyroidism, a condition characterized by an overactive thyroid gland, benefits from herbal teas that promote calmness and support hormonal balance.

Lemon balm, a member of the mint family, is one such herb, known for its soothing effects on the nervous system and potential to inhibit thyroid hormone production. Ashwagandha, an adaptogenic herb, aids in stress reduction and helps maintain healthy thyroid function. Lastly, bugleweed, a flowering plant, has a long history of traditional use in calming thyroid hyperactivity.

Including these herbs in daily tea rituals offers a natural, caffeine-free approach to managing hyperthyroidism.

1. Bugleweed

Bugleweed tea aids in managing hyperthyroidism by directly affecting the thyroid gland, reducing the production of thyroid hormones.

This herbal tea contains compounds that inhibit the activity of the enzyme thyroperoxidase, which is crucial in the synthesis of thyroid hormones. Consequently, this reduction in hormone production helps alleviate symptoms of hyperthyroidism such as rapid heartbeat, weight loss, and anxiety. Moreover, bugleweed tea provides a natural alternative to prescription medications, often carrying fewer side effects.

However, it's important to remember that bugleweed tea should be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional, as excessive or incorrect usage can lead to unintended consequences.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in bugleweed tea that help with hyperthyroidism.

  • Leaves: Contain active compounds that help regulate thyroid hormone production.
  • Stems: Possess anti-inflammatory properties that alleviate symptoms of hyperthyroidism.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants that support overall thyroid health.
  • Seeds: Contain essential oils contributing to the therapeutic effects of bugleweed tea.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in bugleweed tea that aid with hyperthyroidism.

  • Tannins: These astringent compounds help reduce inflammation and irritation in the thyroid gland, reducing the production of thyroid hormones.
  • Rosmarinic acid: This potent antioxidant helps regulate the immune system, minimizing autoimmune responses contributing to hyperthyroidism.
  • Lithospermic acid: This polyphenolic compound has been shown to inhibit TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone) release, which helps balance thyroid hormone levels.
  • Plant flavonoids: These antioxidant compounds help protect thyroid cells from damage and regulate their metabolic processes, reducing the risk of hyperthyroidism.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic bugleweed tea for hyperthyroidism.

  1. Gather 2 teaspoons of dried bugleweed herb and place it in a tea infuser or loose in a teapot.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the bugleweed herb.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes to extract the medicinal components.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the herb, creating a smooth liquid.
  5. Drink the bugleweed tea, either hot or cooled, up to three times a day for hyperthyroidism relief.

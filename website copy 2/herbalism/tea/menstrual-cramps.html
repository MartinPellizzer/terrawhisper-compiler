10 Best Herbal Teas For Menstrual Cramps

Herbal teas, particularly those made from chamomile, ginger, peppermint, and valerian root, can help alleviate menstrual cramps due to their anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and analgesic properties.

These herbs can help relax the smooth muscles in the uterus, reducing contractions and subsequently alleviating pain. Additionally, they can increase blood flow and reduce prostaglandin levels, which are associated with the onset of menstrual cramps. Furthermore, these teas can promote overall relaxation and stress reduction, contributing to a decrease in menstrual discomfort.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare provider before consuming herbal teas for menstrual cramps, as some herbs may interact with medications or have adverse effects.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with menstrual cramps because it contains anti-inflammatory properties that reduce the swelling and pain associated with menstruation.

The calming effects of chamomile also help alleviate anxiety and stress, which can exacerbate menstrual symptoms. Additionally, chamomile is known to have antispasmodic properties, meaning it helps relax muscles and alleviate cramps. Drinking a cup of warm chamomile tea during menstruation can provide quick relief from menstrual cramps due to its natural healing properties.

Furthermore, chamomile tea has been used for centuries as a traditional remedy for menstrual discomfort and is considered safe and effective for most women.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for menstrual cramps.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down for a minute or two, so it's not boiling when you add the chamomile flowers.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers or 1 chamomile tea bag to a cup or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers or tea bag, cover and steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea, if using dried flowers, and add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of chamomile tea per day, during menstrual cramps, to help alleviate pain and discomfort.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with menstrual cramps because of its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce inflammation and pain in the uterus.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerols and shgaols, work by inhibiting prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause cramping and pain during menstruation. Ginger tea also has thermal properties, which help to soothe and relax the muscles in the uterus, providing relief from cramps. Additionally, ginger tea is a natural antiemetic, meaning it helps to alleviate nausea and vomiting commonly associated with menstrual cycles.

Drinking ginger tea during menstruation can provide effective and natural relief for many women experiencing painful cramps and other symptoms.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for menstrual cramps.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root (adjust based on personal preference), then bruise the slices with a heavy object or the back of a spoon to release their beneficial compounds.
  2. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan, then add the bruised ginger slices and reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Allow the ginger to steep in the simmering water, covered, for 15-20 minutes to extract the medicinal properties and create a strong ginger tea.
  4. Strain the tea into a mug, discarding the ginger slices, then add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Sip the hot ginger tea slowly, aiming to drink at least 2-3 cups throughout the day, during the days you experience menstrual cramps, for natural relief.

3. Raspberry Leaf

Raspberry leaf tea helps with menstrual cramps because of its antispasmodic properties, which allow it to relax the uterine muscles and reduce cramping.

The tea also contains magnesium and iron, minerals that can help alleviate menstrual discomfort by supporting healthy blood flow and reducing inflammation. Additionally, raspberry leaf tea is rich in vitamin B1, which plays a role in energy production and can help combat the fatigue often associated with menstrual cramps.

Furthermore, studies suggest that consuming raspberry leaf tea regularly may improve overall menstrual cycle health and reduce symptoms, including cramping, over time.

The following procedure explains how to make raspberry leaf tea for menstrual cramps.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried raspberry leaf to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Once water is boiling, pour it over the raspberry leaf and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea before drinking.
  5. Drink 1-3 cups of raspberry leaf tea per day, starting a few days before menstruation and continuing throughout the menstrual period.

4. Cramp Bark

Cramp bark tea helps with menstrual cramps because it contains an active ingredient called viburnum opulus, which is a natural muscle relaxant.

This herbal remedy has been used for centuries to alleviate menstrual discomfort and pain caused by uterine contractions during menstruation. Drinking cramp bark tea can help soothe and reduce the intensity of menstrual cramps by relaxing the muscles in the uterus. In addition, it may also help relieve other symptoms associated with menstruation, such as bloating and mood swings, due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Overall, cramp bark tea is a safe and effective natural remedy for managing menstrual cramps and providing relief for those who prefer not to use prescription medications or over-the-counter pain relievers.

The following procedure explains how to make cramp bark tea for menstrual cramps.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried cramp bark, a cup of water, and a strainer or cheesecloth.
  2. Bring the water to a boil in a small saucepan, then add the cramp bark.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the solids, then let it cool down for a few minutes.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day, as needed for menstrual cramp relief.

5. Turmeric

Turmeric tea helps with menstrual cramps because of its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce the pain and swelling associated with menstruation.

The active ingredient in turmeric, curcumin, blocks the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause uterine contractions and inflammation during menstruation. Drinking turmeric tea also boosts the production of serotonin, a mood-enhancing neurotransmitter that helps alleviate the emotional symptoms of menstrual cramps, such as anxiety and irritability. Additionally, turmeric contains antioxidants that protect the body from oxidative stress, which can exacerbate menstrual discomfort.

Overall, the combination of anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and mood-boosting properties make turmeric tea a natural and effective remedy for menstrual cramps.

The following procedure explains how to make turmeric tea for menstrual cramps.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Stir in 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric and 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper (to enhance turmeric's absorption) and reduce heat to low.
  3. Let the mixture simmer for about 10 minutes, then strain it into a teapot or a container with a spout.
  4. Pour the turmeric tea into a cup, add honey or lemon to taste, and drink it while it's still warm.
  5. Repeat as needed throughout the day for relief of menstrual cramps.

6. Dill

Dill tea helps with menstrual cramps because it contains compounds such as carvacrol and beta-caryophyllene that exhibit anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, reducing the pain and inflammation associated with menstruation.

Additionally, dill tea is rich in magnesium, a mineral known to help relax muscles and alleviate cramping. The calming effects of dill tea on the nervous system may also contribute to its ability to relieve menstrual discomfort by reducing anxiety and tension related to menstruation. Furthermore, dill tea has been used traditionally for centuries to aid in digestion and alleviate bloating, which can be common symptoms of premenstrual syndrome (PMS) and exacerbate menstrual cramps.

Lastly, the comforting warmth of a hot cup of dill tea can provide psychological relief and help ease the discomfort of menstrual cramps, making it a popular natural remedy for women experiencing this common ailment.

The following procedure explains how to make dill tea for menstrual cramps.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan, then add 1 tablespoon of fresh or dried dill weed or leaves.
  2. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the water to absorb the dill's medicinal properties.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent dill, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added flavor and potential additional benefits.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, aiming to drink 1-2 cups per day during menstruation to help alleviate cramps.

7. Fennel

Fennel tea helps with menstrual cramps because it contains compounds such as anethole and estragole, which have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

These properties help to reduce inflammation and muscle contractions in the uterus, respectively. The warming and soothing effects of fennel tea also provide relief from menstrual cramps by increasing blood flow and promoting relaxation.

Additionally, fennel tea has been used traditionally for centuries to alleviate menstrual discomfort, and clinical studies have shown its efficacy in reducing pain and improving mood during menstruation.

The following procedure explains how to make fennel tea for menstrual cramps.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of fennel seeds and 1 cup of water; place seeds in a pot and pour water over them.
  2. Slowly bring the water to a boil over medium heat, then reduce heat to low and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Remove the pot from heat and strain the tea into a cup, discarding the fennel seeds.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature, then sip it slowly.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of fennel tea per day, as needed, during menstruation for relief of menstrual cramps.

8. Cinnamon

Cinnamon tea helps with menstrual cramps because it contains compounds that mimic prostaglandins, hormones that cause uterine contractions during menstruation.

These compounds help to relax the uterus and reduce muscle spasms that cause pain. Additionally, cinnamon has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the pelvic area, which can also contribute to menstrual cramp symptoms. Furthermore, cinnamon tea is a natural warming beverage, which can help increase blood flow to the uterus and promote relaxation.

Lastly, drinking cinnamon tea during menstruation can provide overall comfort and relief from menstrual discomfort due to its calming effects on the body.

The following procedure explains how to make cinnamon tea for menstrual cramps.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan and add 1 teaspoon of cinnamon powder or a few cinnamon sticks, allowing it to simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  2. In a separate teapot, steep 1 tablespoon of dried chamomile flowers in 1 cup of hot water for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Combine the cinnamon water and chamomile tea in a large mug, then mix in 1 tablespoon of honey and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice for added soothing properties and flavor.
  4. Warm the mixture on low heat, ensuring it doesn't reach a boil, then remove from heat and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Sip the cinnamon tea slowly, aiming to drink 2-3 cups throughout the day, as needed, to help alleviate menstrual cramps.

9. Cloves

Cloves tea helps with menstrual cramps because it contains eugenol, an organic compound that functions as a natural pain reliever and anti-inflammatory agent.

Eugenol works by blocking prostaglandins, which are hormones that contribute to the production of menstrual cramps. Additionally, cloves tea has antispasmodic properties, meaning it helps to relax muscle contractions in the uterus, reducing cramping and discomfort. The warming sensation of drinking the tea also provides comfort and may help improve blood flow during menstruation.

Finally, cloves tea has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to alleviate menstrual symptoms, making it a trusted natural remedy for many women.

The following procedure explains how to make cloves tea for menstrual cramps.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan and add 1-2 teaspoons of dried cloves (or 3-4 whole cloves) once it reaches boiling point.
  2. Reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes to allow the cloves to release their medicinal properties.
  3. Strain the cloves out of the water using a fine mesh strainer or a cheesecloth, then pour the clove-infused water into a mug.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added soothing and anti-inflammatory effects.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to warm your body and alleviate menstrual cramps. Aim to drink 1-2 cups per day during menstruation for best results.

10. Lemongrass

Lemongrass tea helps with menstrual cramps because it contains several active compounds, including citral and geraniol, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce the swelling and inflammation in the uterus during menstruation.

Additionally, lemongrass tea is rich in antioxidants, such as flavonoids, that help fight free radicals in the body and reduce oxidative stress, which can contribute to menstrual cramps. Lemongrass tea also has analgesic properties, meaning it can help relieve pain by numbing the nerves, making it an effective natural remedy for menstrual cramps. Furthermore, lemongrass tea has calming effects that can help alleviate anxiety and stress associated with menstruation, which can exacerbate cramps, making it a holistic approach to menstrual pain relief.

Lastly, drinking lemongrass tea regularly during menstruation can help regulate menstrual cycles by balancing hormones and reducing the severity of cramps over time.

The following procedure explains how to make lemongrass tea for menstrual cramps.

  1. Gather 1-2 fresh lemongrass stalks, 10-12 fresh mint leaves, 1 small piece of ginger (about the size of your thumb), 1 teaspoon of honey, and 2 cups of water.
  2. Rinse the lemongrass stalks, mint leaves, and ginger, then roughly chop the lemongrass and ginger into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped lemongrass, mint leaves, ginger, and water in a pot, and bring it to a boil over medium-high heat.
  4. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes to allow the ingredients to release their beneficial compounds.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup, add 1 teaspoon of honey for taste, and enjoy this soothing beverage 2-3 times a day during menstrual cramps.

10 best herbal teas for acne

Here are the top three herbal teas for acne: chamomile, mint, and green tea.

Chamomile tea has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce redness and irritation, while mint tea unclogs pores and eliminates acne-causing bacteria. Green tea, rich in antioxidants, combats acne by regulating hormonal imbalances and reducing oil production.

Regularly drinking these teas not only treats acne but also prevents future breakouts.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains azulene and alpha-bisabolol, potent anti-inflammatory agents that reduce inflammation and redness caused by acne.

Additionally, its antimicrobial properties, derived from the same compounds, combat the bacteria that contribute to breakouts. Furthermore, chamomile exhibits impressive antioxidant capabilities, neutralizing free radicals and protecting skin from oxidative stress and damage. The soothing effects of chamomile tea, when consumed or applied topically, alleviate irritation and may decrease sebum production, ultimately promoting clearer skin.

Regularly drinking chamomile tea delivers these benefits internally, while using it as a facial wash or toner offers external advantages in the battle against acne.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with acne.

  • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce acne-related redness and swelling.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants that help soothe irritated skin and promote healing of acne blemishes.
  • Seeds: Contain high levels of essential oils with antimicrobial properties that help combat acne-causing bacteria.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that help with acne.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that possesses antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce redness and irritation associated with acne.
  • Chamazulene: A terpene known for its potent anti-inflammatory effects, which aids in soothing acne-prone skin and decreasing breakouts.
  • Matricin: A precursor to the anti-inflammatory compound matricin acid, helping alleviate inflammation related to acne.
  • Alpha-Bisabolol: A sesquiterpene alcohol with potent anti-inflammatory and skin-calming effects, reducing the appearance of acne and promoting skin healing.
  • Nobiletin: A flavonoid exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help protect the skin from oxidative stress and acne-related inflammation.
  • Luteolin: A flavonoid that shows antioxidant and anti-inflammatory qualities, aiding in the reduction of acne-related redness and irritation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for acne.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a teapot or saucepan, allowing it to reach a rolling boil for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to a teapot or infuser; pour the boiling water over the chamomile.
  3. Steep the tea for 5-7 minutes, ensuring the chamomile flowers are fully immersed in the water.
  4. Strain the tea, discarding the chamomile flowers, and let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea 1-2 times a day, preferably after meals or before bedtime, for at least two weeks to see improvements in acne.

  5. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the leaves or tea bag, and let it cool before gently applying it to acne-prone areas using a clean cotton pad.

What is acne and how can it affect your life?

Acne is a common skin condition characterized by the appearance of spots and pimples on the skin, especially on the face, back, and chest.

It occurs when hair follicles become clogged with oil and dead skin cells. Acne impacts people's lives by causing physical discomfort, emotional distress, and reduced self-esteem.

The presence of acne can lead to anxiety, depression, and social isolation, affecting individuals' quality of life and overall well-being.

What other natural remedies help with acne?

Natural remedies offer a variety of effective solutions for treating acne.

One common and effective remedy is the use of tea tree oil, which contains antibacterial properties that reduce inflammation and combat acne-causing bacteria. Another popular option is aloe vera, which soothes irritated skin, reduces redness, and promotes healing.

Green clay, or French clay, is also widely used for its ability to absorb oil and impurities, unclog pores, and decrease inflammation. Witch hazel, derived from the leaves and bark of the witch hazel shrub, has astringent properties that cleanse and tighten pores, while also reducing redness and inflammation.

Lastly, raw honey has natural antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it an excellent choice for treating acne.

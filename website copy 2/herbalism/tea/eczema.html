10 Best Herbal Teas For Eczema

Herbal teas, particularly those with anti-inflammatory properties, can help alleviate eczema symptoms.

Chamomile tea, for instance, contains flavonoids that can reduce inflammation and ease irritation on the skin. Similarly, nettle tea is rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that can help control eczema outbreaks.

Green tea, another popular herbal tea, has polyphenols that can reduce inflammation and boost the immune system, which can help manage eczema. However, it's important to note that while these teas can provide relief, they should not replace medical treatments prescribed by a healthcare professional.

Lastly, individuals should be aware of any potential allergic reactions to these herbs and consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new treatment regimen.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with eczema because it has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help reduce inflammation and fight infection caused by scratching.

The antioxidants in chamomile tea, such as flavonoids and terpenoids, also have soothing effects on the skin, helping to alleviate the itchiness and redness associated with eczema. Furthermore, chamomile tea can help boost the immune system, which is essential for individuals with eczema as they are more prone to infections. Additionally, drinking chamomile tea can help reduce stress and anxiety, which can trigger eczema flare-ups.

However, it is important to note that while chamomile tea can provide relief for some individuals with eczema, it may not work for everyone, and it should not replace any prescribed medications or treatments.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for eczema.

  1. To make chamomile tea for eczema, start by boiling 8 ounces of water and then pour it over 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers in a tea infuser or teapot.
  2. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the maximum amount of anti-inflammatory compounds and skin-soothing properties.
  3. While the tea is still warm, mix in 1 teaspoon of raw honey to sweeten and provide additional antibacterial benefits for any potential skin infections.
  4. To further enhance the tea's calming effects, squeeze half a lemon for its soothing and vitamin C-rich properties.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, 30 minutes before meals, to help reduce inflammation and irritation associated with eczema from the inside out.

2. Nettle

Nettle tea helps with eczema because it has anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce redness, swelling, and irritation of the skin.

It also contains antioxidants that help to support the immune system and may help to reduce the severity of eczema symptoms. Additionally, nettle tea has been found to inhibit the release of histamine, a compound involved in allergic reactions that can worsen eczema. Furthermore, it has astringent properties that can help to tighten and tone the skin, reducing the appearance of eczema lesions.

However, it's important to note that while nettle tea may help to alleviate eczema symptoms, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, and individuals with eczema should consult with a healthcare provider before using nettle tea or any other herbal remedy.

The following procedure explains how to make nettle tea for eczema.

  1. Gather 2-3 teaspoons of fresh nettle leaves (Urtica dioica) or 1-2 teaspoons of dried nettle leaves, rinse them thoroughly, and place them in a teapot or infuser; ensure that the leaves come from a pesticide-free source.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down for about 2-3 minutes to ensure it's not too hot, which may destroy the beneficial compounds in the nettle leaves.
  3. Pour the hot water over the nettle leaves in the teapot or infuser, cover, and let it steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the medicinal properties of the nettle leaves.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used nettle leaves, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature, ideally around 140-160°F (60-70°C), before consuming.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of nettle tea daily, preferably between meals, for at least 4-6 weeks to help alleviate eczema symptoms, but consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal remedy.

3. Calendula

Calendula tea helps with eczema because it has anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce redness, swelling, and irritation of the skin.

The tea is made from marigold flowers, which contain flavonoids that have antioxidant properties, helping to promote wound healing and soothe irritated skin. Additionally, calendula tea may help to boost the immune system, which can be beneficial for individuals with eczema as it often occurs in conjunction with other immune system disorders. The tea can be consumed internally or applied topically as a compress or added to bath water to alleviate symptoms of eczema.

However, it is important to note that while calendula tea can be a helpful complementary treatment for eczema, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment or advice from a healthcare professional.

The following procedure explains how to make calendula tea for eczema.

  1. To make calendula tea for eczema, start by boiling 1 cup of water in a saucepan, then add 1-2 teaspoons of dried calendula petals (Calendula officinalis) and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the petals, and allow it to cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  3. For added benefits, squeeze half a lemon and add 1-2 teaspoons of raw honey to the tea, stirring until fully dissolved.
  4. Drink this calendula tea 2-3 times a day, especially before bedtime, to soothe inflammation and irritation caused by eczema.
  5. Continue this calendula tea regimen for at least 2-4 weeks for best results, but consult with a healthcare professional if symptoms persist or worsen.

4. Burdock

Burdock tea helps with eczema because it has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can reduce skin inflammation and irritation, which are common symptoms of eczema.

The tea's anti-inflammatory properties can help alleviate redness, swelling, and itching, while its antioxidants can help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals. Additionally, burdock tea has been shown to have antibacterial and antifungal properties, which can help prevent skin infections that can exacerbate eczema symptoms. Furthermore, burdock tea may help improve liver function, which can support the body's detoxification processes and promote overall skin health.

Finally, burdock tea is a natural and caffeine-free alternative to conventional eczema treatments, making it a viable option for those looking for a more holistic approach to managing their eczema symptoms.

The following procedure explains how to make burdock tea for eczema.

  1. Rinse and chop 1 oz (28 grams) of dried burdock root into small pieces, then add to a pot and fill with 4 cups of water; bring to a boil, lower heat, and simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Strain the liquid to remove the burdock root pieces, then return the tea to the pot.
  3. Optional: Add a small amount of raw honey or lemon juice to taste, if desired.
  4. Serve the burdock tea hot, dividing it into 4 cups for daily consumption.
  5. Store any remaining tea in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

5. Dandelion

Dandelion tea helps with eczema because it has anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce skin redness, swelling, and irritation.

It is also rich in antioxidants that help combat free radicals, which can damage skin cells and worsen eczema symptoms. Furthermore, dandelion tea has been shown to support liver function, which is essential for skin health as the liver helps detoxify the body and eliminate toxins that can trigger eczema flare-ups. Additionally, dandelion tea has natural antimicrobial properties that can help prevent skin infections, a common complication of eczema.

Finally, dandelion tea is a gentle and caffeine-free alternative to other teas and beverages, making it a suitable option for people with sensitive skin or eczema.

The following procedure explains how to make dandelion tea for eczema.

  1. Gather 2-3 handfuls of fresh dandelion flowers, making sure they are free of pesticides and other contaminants, and rinse them gently in cold water to remove any dirt or insects.
  2. Boil 4 cups of water in a pot, then add the rinsed dandelion flowers, reducing the heat to a simmer for 10 minutes to allow the nutrients and flavors to infuse into the water.
  3. Strain the liquid using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a teapot or heat-resistant container, discarding the spent flowers and reserving the golden-hued liquid.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added flavor and potential anti-inflammatory or antioxidant benefits, stirring gently to dissolve the honey.
  5. Serve the dandelion tea warm, ideally sipping it slowly throughout the day to soothe and potentially alleviate eczema symptoms, storing any leftovers in the refrigerator for up to 48 hours.

6. Red Clover

Red clover tea helps with eczema because it contains anti-inflammatory and blood purifying properties that can alleviate skin irritation and redness.

The isoflavones found in red clover have been shown to exhibit estrogen-like effects, which may support skin health and reduce eczema symptoms. Additionally, red clover tea's antioxidant properties can help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting overall skin health. The tea's anti-inflammatory and soothing effects can also help reduce the itchiness and discomfort associated with eczema.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before incorporating red clover tea or any other herbal remedy into an eczema treatment plan, as individual responses to herbal treatments can vary.

The following procedure explains how to make red clover tea for eczema.

  1. To make red clover tea for eczema, start by boiling 2 cups of water in a pot and adding 2 teaspoons of dried red clover flowers.
  2. Allow the mixture to simmer for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds from the red clover.
  3. Next, strain the tea to remove the solid plant material, leaving only the liquid.
  4. For added flavor and potential soothing properties, mix in 1 teaspoon of honey and a squeeze of lemon juice.
  5. Drink the red clover tea while it's still warm, 2-3 times a day, to help alleviate eczema symptoms.

7. Sarsaparilla

Sarsaparilla tea helps with eczema because it contains anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that can reduce skin inflammation and combat infection, common symptoms associated with eczema.

The tea is also rich in antioxidants that help to soothe irritated skin, promote skin repair, and improve overall skin health. Furthermore, sarsaparilla tea has been found to have immunomodulatory effects, which can help regulate the immune system and potentially prevent eczema flare-ups. Additionally, the tea's anti-itch properties can provide relief from the uncomfortable itching sensation that often accompanies eczema.

However, it is important to note that while sarsaparilla tea may help alleviate some eczema symptoms, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment and should be used in conjunction with a doctor's advice.

The following procedure explains how to make sarsaparilla tea for eczema.

  1. In a pot, add 1 quart of water and bring it to a boil, then add 2 tablespoons of dried sarsaparilla root and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  2. After 30 minutes, remove the pot from heat and strain the tea to remove the sarsaparilla root, allowing it to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  3. If desired, add a natural sweetener such as honey to taste.
  4. For best results, drink 2-3 cups of this tea daily, and make a fresh batch every 24 hours to maintain potency.
  5. Continue this regimen for several weeks or until you notice improvement in your eczema symptoms.

The following procedure explains how to make yellow dock tea for eczema.

Drinking cleavers tea regularly may also boost the immune system, helping the body fight off infections and inflammation that can trigger eczema flare-ups.

The following procedure explains how to make cleavers tea for eczema.

  1. To make cleavers tea for eczema, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 2 teaspoons of dried cleavers herb, allowing it to steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. In the meantime, rinse and drain 1 small handful of fresh chickweed, then chop it finely.
  3. After the cleavers tea has steeped, combine it with the chopped chickweed in a small pot and bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  4. Let the mixture simmer for about 5 minutes, then remove from heat and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Strain the tea before consuming, and drink 2-3 cups daily for best results in managing eczema symptoms.

10. Oak Bark

Oak bark tea helps with eczema because it contains tannins, which have anti-inflammatory and astringent properties that can help reduce skin irritation and inflammation.

These tannins can also help to dry up excess fluid and reduce the redness and itching associated with eczema. Furthermore, oak bark tea has been found to have antibacterial properties, which can help prevent infection in broken skin. Additionally, the use of oak bark tea for eczema can be traced back to traditional herbal medicine, where it has been used for its soothing and healing effects on the skin.

However, it's important to note that while oak bark tea can be a helpful addition to an eczema treatment plan, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, and individuals should consult with a healthcare provider before using oak bark tea or any other herbal remedy.

The following procedure explains how to make oak bark tea for eczema.

  1. Collect fresh oak bark from mature oak trees (preferably in the morning during dry weather), ensuring the bark is from the outermost layer, then rinse it thoroughly to remove any dirt or debris, and let it dry; once dry, cut 28 grams of oak bark into small pieces.
  2. In a stainless steel pot, combine 1 liter of cold water and the 28 grams of cut oak bark, and bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat; once boiling, reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 1 hour.
  3. After simmering for 1 hour, remove the pot from heat and let it cool down to a comfortable temperature; using a fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth, strain the tea into a container, separating the liquid from the solid oak bark pieces.
  4. Squeeze the oak bark pieces to extract any remaining liquid and mix it with the strained tea; discard the used oak bark pieces, and store the tea in the refrigerator for up to 5 days in an airtight container.
  5. To serve, mix 1 part oak bark tea with 1 part warm water, and drink 1-2 cups daily, ideally between meals, for best results in managing eczema symptoms; avoid consuming oak bark tea if pregnant, breastfeeding, or experiencing kidney issues.

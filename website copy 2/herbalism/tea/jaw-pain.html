10 best herbal teas for jaw pain

Lastly, clove tea, abundant in eugenol, a powerful antinociceptive, offers profound relief, making these teas indispensable allies in managing jaw pain.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea's primary active components, apigenin and bisabolol, possess anti-inflammatory and muscle relaxant properties, which target the underlying causes of jaw pain.

These compounds inhibit the production of inflammatory chemicals such as prostaglandins and leukotrienes, thus reducing swelling and inflammation in the temporomandibular joint (TMJ). Moreover, chamomile tea's muscle relaxant properties alleviate muscle tension, decreasing stress on the TMJ and contributing to an overall improvement in jaw comfort. Its soothing warmth further encourages blood flow, facilitating healing and promoting relaxation.

Regularly consuming chamomile tea fosters a consistent anti-inflammatory environment within the body, diminishing chronic jaw pain.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with jaw pain.

  • Flowers: Contain active compounds such as bisabolol and chamazulene, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce jaw pain.
  • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids and terpenoids, the leaves possess analgesic and anti-spasmodic qualities, making them suitable for jaw pain relief.
  • Stems: Though less potent than flowers and leaves, stems still contain therapeutic compounds that can contribute to the tea's soothing effects on jaw pain.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that help with jaw pain.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that possesses antispasmodic properties, helping to relieve muscle spasms in the jaw.
  • Chamazulene: A chemical compound with anti-inflammatory effects, reducing swelling and inflammation in the jaw area.
  • Bisabolol: A terpene known for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, alleviating pain in the jaw.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin also exhibits anti-inflammatory effects, aiding in jaw pain relief.
  • Spiroether: A compound that contributes to the overall soothing and calming effect of chamomile tea, helping to alleviate tension in the jaw.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for jaw pain.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a kettle, reaching a temperature of 212 degrees Fahrenheit, for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Combine 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers in a teapot or infuser, using organic and high-quality flowers for maximum potency.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers, allowing the tea to steep for 5-7 minutes to extract the medicinal compounds.
  4. Strain the chamomile tea to remove the spent flowers, and optionally add honey or lemon to taste for flavor and additional soothing properties.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea while warm, aiming for 2-3 cups per day, to alleviate jaw pain and promote relaxation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic willow tea for jaw pain.

  2. Bring 1 liter of water to a boil, then add the willow bark and reduce heat to low.

What is jaw pain and how can it affect your life?

Jaw pain is a discomfort or soreness in the jaw joint and surrounding muscles, often resulting from teeth grinding, injury, or arthritis.

This condition significantly impacts people's lives as it causes difficulty in performing everyday activities such as eating, speaking, and yawning.

Moreover, constant pain and discomfort can lead to sleep disturbances, decreased productivity, and overall reduced quality of life.

What other natural remedies help with jaw pain?

Jaw pain is a common ailment that can be effectively alleviated through several natural remedies.

One of the most widely used remedies is applying heat or cold compresses to the affected area. Heat therapy, such as a hot water bottle or heating pad, helps to relax muscles and increase blood flow, reducing pain and stiffness. Conversely, cold therapy, like an ice pack, numbs the area and reduces inflammation.

Another effective natural remedy is jaw stretching and exercises. Gently opening and closing the mouth, as well as moving the jaw from side to side, helps to improve mobility and reduce tension. Additionally, massaging the jaw muscles with gentle circular motions can help alleviate pain and improve range of motion.

Lastly, maintaining good posture and avoiding habits that put strain on the jaw, such as clenching or grinding teeth, can prevent jaw pain and promote overall oral health.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea's calming properties, recognized by historical medical practices such as Greco-Roman and Egyptian medicine, help alleviate hangover symptoms.

This herbal remedy contains the antioxidant apigenin, which reduces oxidative stress from alcohol metabolism. Additionally, its mild sedative effects help improve sleep quality, a crucial factor in recovering from alcohol consumption. Chamomile's soothing properties ease digestive discomfort, a common hangover symptom.

Thus, chamomile tea unequivocally aids in alleviating hangover symptoms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with hangover.

  • Flowers: Chamomile's daisy-like flowers contain calming properties that help soothe an upset stomach and promote relaxation, which can alleviate hangover symptoms.
  • Leaves: The bitter leaves of chamomile help stimulate the digestive system and relieve bloating, making them useful for hangover relief.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are rich in antioxidants that help protect the liver from damage caused by alcohol, which can help reduce hangover severity.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that help with hangover.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that helps to reduce anxiety and promote sleep, which can help alleviate symptoms of hangover such as restlessness and irritability.
  • Chamazulene: A compound responsible for the blue color of chamomile tea, it has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce headaches and muscle aches caused by hangover.
  • Bisabolol: A terpene with anti-inflammatory and anti-spasmodic properties, can help soothe an upset stomach and reduce nausea caused by hangover.
  • Matricin: A precursor of chamazulene, it also contributes to the anti-inflammatory effects of chamomile tea, helping alleviate hangover symptoms such as headaches and muscle aches.
  • Hippuric acid: A natural detoxifier that helps the body eliminate toxins such as alcohol, making it easier to recover from a hangover.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for hangover.

  1. Measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers and place them in a tea infuser or teapot.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool slightly for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove the flowers.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day as needed.

What is hangover and how can it affect your life?

A hangover is the set of symptoms that follow a night of heavy drinking, such as headache, nausea, dizziness, and fatigue.

These uncomfortable sensations directly result from the body's attempt to process and eliminate alcohol from the system. Hangovers have profound effects on people's lives, causing difficulties in daily activities like working, studying, and social interactions.

They may also lead to poor decision-making, strained relationships, and lower overall quality of life.

What other natural remedies help with hangover?

Among the most common and effective natural remedies for a hangover are hydration, sleep, and consuming certain foods.

Water is the key to rehydration after a night of drinking, as alcohol is a diuretic and causes the body to lose fluids. Adequate sleep allows the body to rest and recover from the physical stress of alcohol consumption. Foods rich in carbohydrates, proteins, and fats, such as toast, eggs, and bananas, help restore blood sugar levels and replenish essential nutrients lost during alcohol metabolism.

Additionally, ginger or peppermint can alleviate nausea and gastrointestinal discomfort caused by excessive alcohol consumption.

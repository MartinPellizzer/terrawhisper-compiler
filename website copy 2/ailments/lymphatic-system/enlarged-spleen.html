What is enlarged spleen and and how it affects your life?

An enlarged spleen, also known as splenomegaly, refers to an abnormal expansion of this organ, which plays a crucial role in the immune system and old red blood cell removal.

The spleen filters blood, destroying worn-out red blood cells, and storing red and white blood cells and platelets. A swollen spleen can result from various conditions, such as infections, blood disorders, or cancer. Ignoring an enlarged spleen can lead to severe complications, such as a ruptured spleen, which causes internal bleeding and requires immediate medical attention.

Therefore, recognizing the signs of splenomegaly and seeking timely medical intervention is essential to preserve overall health and well-being.

What to know about oral thrush before using medicinal herbs

Oral thrush, a common fungal infection, often responds well to natural remedies like herbal teas.

These teas offer a safe, accessible option for managing symptoms. Two exceptional choices are chamomile and calendula teas.

Chamomile, famous for its soothing properties, fights inflammation and boasts antifungal qualities. Calendula, another powerful herb, demonstrates impressive antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

Regularly sipping these herbal teas creates an unwelcome environment for oral thrush, promoting a quicker recovery.

What is oral thrush and and how it affects your life?

Oral thrush is a fungal infection that develops in the mouth, characterized by white or yellow patches on the tongue, inner cheeks, gums, and tonsils.

This condition results from an overgrowth of Candida fungus, a naturally occurring microorganism in the mouth. Its importance lies in the potential discomfort it causes, such as soreness, difficulty swallowing, and loss of taste. Moreover, it can lead to severe infections in people with weakened immune systems, like those with HIV or undergoing cancer treatments.

Maintaining good oral hygiene and a balanced diet can help prevent oral thrush, ensuring overall well-being and reducing the risk of complications.

What to know about periodontal disease before using medicinal herbs

Herbal teas, such as chamomile and calendula, offer anti-inflammatory properties that benefit persons with periodontal disease.

The antibacterial qualities of green tea and echinacea combat the oral bacteria responsible for gum disease.

Regular consumption of these herbal teas contributes to improved oral health, making them a natural and effective addition to dental hygiene practices.

What is periodontal disease and and how it affects your life?

Periodontal disease, also known as gum disease, is a chronic inflammation and infection of the gums and bones surrounding the teeth.

It occurs when plaque, a sticky film of bacteria, accumulates on the teeth and hardens into tartar. This condition can lead to red, swollen, and bleeding gums, bad breath, and ultimately tooth loss if left untreated. Given that oral health is closely linked to overall health, periodontal disease can increase the risk of developing other serious health problems such as heart disease, stroke, and diabetes.

Therefore, maintaining good oral hygiene and seeking regular dental check-ups are essential for preventing periodontal disease and safeguarding both your oral and general health.

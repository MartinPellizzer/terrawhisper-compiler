What to know about acid reflux before using medicinal herbs

Herbal teas bring proven relief to acid reflux sufferers.

Chinese chamomile, a flowering plant, soothes irritation in the esophagus with its anti-inflammatory properties. Ginger, a root rich in antioxidants, suppresses acid production while accelerating digestion. Licorice, a herb known for its sweet flavor, creates a protective coating in the stomach lining, preventing acid from backing up into the esophagus.

Drinking these teas after meals reduces symptoms and promotes a restful night's sleep.

What is acid reflux and and how it affects your life?

Acid reflux, also known as gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), is a condition in which stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus, causing symptoms such as heartburn, chest pain, and difficulty swallowing.

If left untreated, acid reflux can lead to serious complications, including esophagitis, esophageal stricture, and even esophageal cancer.

Since it can negatively affect one's quality of life and lead to long-term health issues, managing acid reflux is crucial to maintaining overall well-being.

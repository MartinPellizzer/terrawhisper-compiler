What is hiccups and and how it affects your life?

Hiccups are involuntary contractions of the diaphragm muscle, followed by closure of the vocal cords, producing the distinctive "hic" sound.

Although hiccups generally pose no serious harm to one's health, they can indicate underlying issues such as gastrointestinal disturbances.

Thus, experiencing hiccups serves as a useful signal for individuals to be aware of their bodily state and seek medical attention if necessary, ultimately contributing to their overall well-being.

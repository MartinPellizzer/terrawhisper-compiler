What to know about eyesight before using medicinal herbs

Several herbal teas benefit eye health through powerful antioxidants and nutrients.

Green tea, rich in epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), protects retinal cells from damage. Meanwhile, chamomile tea contains azulene, known for reducing inflammation, which can alleviate eye strain and irritation. Additionally, bilberry tea, made from a fruit similar to blueberries, is high in anthocyanins, promoting healthy blood flow and improving night vision.

Lastly, ginkgo biloba tea enhances eye function, supporting overall eye health.

What is eyesight and and how it affects your life?

Eyesight, also known as visual perception, is the capability to interpret and give meaning to the light that enters our eyes.

This ability is crucial for our existence as it enables us to recognize and engage with our surroundings. It is through eyesight that we can perceive the world in all its vividness and depth, allowing us to perform daily tasks, appreciate the beauty of nature, and build connections with others.

Eyesight is a fundamental aspect of our lives, empowering us with the ability to learn, work, and interact with our environment.

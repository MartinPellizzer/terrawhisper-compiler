What to know about chickenpox before using medicinal herbs

Several herbal teas offer relief and benefits for individuals suffering from chickenpox.

Chamomile tea, known for its anti-inflammatory and calming properties, soothes irritated skin. Licorice root tea, rich in glycyrrhizin, exhibits strong antiviral effects against the varicella-zoster virus, reducing the severity of the infection. Peppermint tea, with its cooling and analgesic properties, alleviates itching and discomfort.

Drinking these herbal teas not only provides symptomatic relief but also supports the immune system in fighting off the virus.

What is chickenpox and and how it affects your life?

Chickenpox is a highly contagious disease caused by the varicella-zoster virus.

It results in an itchy rash with small, fluid-filled blisters that eventually scab over. Chickenpox is important in life because, although it is usually mild in children, it can lead to serious complications in adults, pregnant women, and individuals with weakened immune systems.

Moreover, contracting chickenpox as an adult typically results in more severe symptoms. Additionally, after recovering from chickenpox, the virus remains dormant in the body and can reactivate later in life as shingles, a painful condition.

Vaccination provides protection against both chickenpox and shingles.

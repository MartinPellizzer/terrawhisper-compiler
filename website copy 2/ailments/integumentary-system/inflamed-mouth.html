What to know about inflamed mouth before using medicinal herbs

Certain herbal teas, like chamomile and calendula, offer well-documented benefits for oral health.

Chamomile's anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties soothe inflamed mouth tissues, while calendula's potent anti-inflammatory and wound-healing abilities promote rapid recovery from mouth inflammation.

Both teas, when consumed as a warm beverage or used as a compress, bring considerable relief and support oral health.

What is inflamed mouth and and how it affects your life?

An inflamed mouth, also known as stomatitis, is a condition characterized by redness, swelling, and soreness in the mouth.

This condition can result from various factors, including poor oral hygiene, injury, or viral and bacterial infections. It is crucial to address an inflamed mouth because it can lead to severe discomfort, difficulty eating, speaking, and swallowing. In addition, an inflamed mouth can create an environment conducive to infections, leading to further health complications.

Clearly, maintaining good oral health and addressing any signs of inflammation promptly are vital for overall well-being and quality of life.

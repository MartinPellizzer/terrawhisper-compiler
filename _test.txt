1. Establish a consistent sleep schedule by going to bed and waking up at the same time each day, even on weekends.
2. Create a restful environment by making your bedroom dark, cool, and quiet, using room-darkening shades, earplugs, a fan, or a white noise machine as needed.
3. Practice relaxation techniques such as deep breathing, progressive muscle relaxation, or meditation to help calm your mind and body before bedtime.
4. Engage in regular physical activity during the day, but avoid vigorous exercise close to bedtime as it may interfere with sleep.
5. Limit exposure to screens, including phones, tablets, computers, and TVs, for at least an hour before bedtime, as the blue light they emit can suppress melatonin production.
6. Use a warm bath or shower as part of your bedtime routine to help relax your muscles and body.
7. Avoid consuming caffeine, nicotine, and alcohol close to bedtime, as they can disrupt sleep patterns.
8. Write down your worries or to-do lists in a journal before bedtime to clear your mind and reduce racing thoughts.
9. Eat a light, balanced dinner at least two to three hours before bedtime to prevent digestive discomfort.
10. Manage stress through mindfulness, therapy, or other stress-reduction techniques, as chronic stress can negatively impact sleep quality.

Coleus has many medicinal preparations, such as Coleus extract and tea bags for health purposes. They are made using its leaf 

 which is rich in essential oils and various other compounds that offer 

 multiple therapeutic benefits. The plant itself belongs to the Lamiaceae 

 family and has a long history of use in traditional medicine systems, particularly 

 for curing respiratory issues, digestive disorders, and skin problems. 

 Coleus extract is one such preparation obtained from Coleus leaves. This 

 potent formulation can be used in the treatment of various ailments like 

 high blood pressure, heart diseases and irregular heartbeats (arrhythmia). It 

 helps regulate blood flow by dilating (relaxing) or constricting blood vessels, 

 depending on the required situation. In addition to this, Coleus extract has 

 also been demonstrated in improving lung function for respiratory issues like 

 asthma and bronchitis. 

 Tea bags of Coleus leaves are another popular medicinal preparation. They are 

 easy to make by drying the fresh leaves under the sun, crushing them into a 

 fine powder, and then packing them into tea bags for brewing purposes. As these 

 dried leaves contain essential oils, when they are steeped in hot water, the oils 

 release their beneficial phytonutrients which provide relief from various health 

 problems. Drinking Coleus leaf tea helps to soothe the digestive tract and 

 relieve symptoms of indigestion like bloating, gas, nausea or loss of appetite. 

 Additionally, it can also help in improving skin conditions such as acne due 

 to its anti-inflammatory properties and promoting clearer skin. In combination 

 with other herbs, Coleus leaf tea can be used for managing menstrual cramps 

 and other related issues. 

 Moreover, topical application of Coleus leaves in the form of ointments or creams 

 is also beneficial for treating skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis. The essential 

 oils present in these preparations possess potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial 

 properties, which work synergistically to reduce redness, itching, and scaling of the 

 affected skin. However, before using Coleus as a treatment, always consult with a 

 healthcare professional for the correct dosage and usage to ensure maximum benefit.

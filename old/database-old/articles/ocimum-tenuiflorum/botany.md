Holy Basil with botanical name Ocimum tenuiflorum belongs to domain Eukarya which is further classified into kingdom Plantae, phylum Tracheophyta (vascular plants), class Magnoliopsida or Dicotyledones as it has two seed leaves and dicot flowers. Holy Basil falls under order Lamiales that encompass members of the Lamiaceae family consisting predominantly aromatic herbs with square stems like many relatives, within genus Ocimum (which also comprises culinary basils) to species tenuiflorum describing small flowered types such as this. Holy Basil is often referred by other names too; they include Krishna Tulsi and Wild Tepee due its numerous beneficial uses including religious ceremonies in Hinduism, where it plays an important role during the celebration of Diwali among many others reasons for usage like Ayurvedic medicine or general aromatic flavoring.

Holy Basil's morphology can be described with square stems and opposite leaves arranged along them that are hairy on their underside; these features make it distinguishable from other basils species whose starch is round in shape instead of squared edges, thus creating a unique characteristic appearance for this plant.

Two common variants found within Holy Basil's category include Rama Tulsi and Vana or Krishna tulsis which exhibit slightly different appearances but maintain the same botanical composition (with just some variations). These variances are mainly due to their differing leaf shapes – being ovate shaped, curled upwards at tips in case of Ram Tula- isi while those from Vanatulsi or Krishna tulsis display wider oval form leaves that tend not too turn upward.

Its range extends across the globe with diverse geographic distribution spanning Africa's arid regions to temperate climates all around Asia and parts of Europe, where it can be found naturally flourishing in rocky terrains or even dry places thanks for its adaptability traits making it ideal plant species both within gardens as well wildernesses alike.

Its life-cycle comprises the typical seed propagation process like most plants do; this means reproduction occurs through tiny seeds dispersed by wind, birds and other animals that consume berry fruits after flowering has occurred during summer months – ensuring longstanding Holy Basil growth throughout various climates & landscapes where they are able to thrive.

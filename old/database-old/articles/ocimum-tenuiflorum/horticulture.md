 Holy Basil requires well-drained soil with moderate fertility, partial sun exposure to full sunlight for optimal growth (6 hours daily). This perennial plant enjoys humus rich organic matter and an evenly moist environment; ideally between 20°C - 34 ° C. Avoid extremes of temperature as it might negatively affect the herb's vigor.

 Select healthy young plants or seeds from reputable sources, then sow them in a well-prepared seedbed with proper spacing (15 cm to prevent crowding). Add 30% compost if starting directly into soil; transplant carefully once the roots are established and water regularly for initial growth.

 Fertilize monthly using organic fertilizers such as fish emulsion or liquid kelp extracts, but be cautious not to over-fertilise since this may burn leaves causing them a pale appearance; regular pruning maintains an evenly full plant and can aid in preventing flowering which reduces essential oil content.

 For maximum flavors, harvest young stems before they begin budding for dried herb usage or right after flowers open to create tea-like infusions (5 leaves per cup). Allow mature plants to grow their full lifecycle and complete bloom cycle if focusing on the development of seeds.

 Common pest issues include aphids, leaf miners, spider mites which can be controlled through careful monitoring with insecticidal soap sprays; diseases such as rust fungus or powdery mildew may occur due to excessive humidity in enclosed conditions. Regularly inspect and take prompt action for ideal growing outcomes of Holy Basil plants (Ocimum tenuiflorum).

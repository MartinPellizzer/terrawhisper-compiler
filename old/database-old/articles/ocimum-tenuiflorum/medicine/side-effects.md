Holy Basil can have some side effects if used improperly, such as allergy reactions or even cause potential hormone imbalance due to the presence of certain chemicals. 

First, Holy Basil has the capacity to stimulate specific enzymes that regulate adrenaline and cortisol, which are stress-related hormones in our body. This can lead to increased energy as it aids in combating fatigue; however, excess intake can push us too far into overdrive. An imbalance of these hormones may trigger emotional instability, anxiety, or even depression. 

Furthermore, the chemical constituents present in Holy Basil leaves and flowers, such as eugenol and cineole, are responsible for its fragrance and flavor. While these chemicals can be effective remedies for various ailments and diseases, they may also cause allergic reactions in some individuals who have sensitivities to them or develop contact dermatitis due to direct contact with the plant's oil-containing leaves. People often use Holy Basil as a food additive for enhancing flavor; however, excessive consumption can lead to indigestion and stomach upsets when one is not accustomed to it.

In addition, Holy Basil contains a chemical called Urason B that aids in the synthesis of estrogen, which is an essential female hormone. Although this might be beneficial for women experiencing menopausal symptoms or those with low estrogen levels, excessive intake can lead to an imbalance in the body's hormonal system. This could potentially result in undesired changes such as mood swings, irregular periods, and other reproductive issues.

Moreover, pregnant women should avoid using Holy Basil due to its ability to stimulate contractions. It is not recommended for breastfeeding mothers either, as there isn't enough data available about the potential impact of this herb on infants through breastmilk consumption.

On the other hand, it is crucial to remember that these negative effects typically arise when misused or consumed by an individual with pre-existing health conditions. Holy Basil has proven therapeutic benefits when taken in appropriate amounts for specific purposes and only under a professional's guidance. Therefore, it is essential to consult a healthcare provider before consuming Holy Basil to avoid any potential adverse reactions.

White Willow has many medicinal preparations, such as Tea - An herbal tea made from the bark of white willow trees is an ancient method of consumption that delivers therapeutic benefits. It offers natural relief from various health issues like headaches, arthritis, and menstrual cramps. The tea's main active ingredient is salicin, which converts to salicylic acid in the body; this chemical acts similarly to acetylsalicylic acid (Aspirin) and aids in reducing inflammation and pain management. Brewing white willow bark tea requires boiling the dried or powdered bark in water for several minutes. This drink can be sweetened with honey, lemon juice, or other natural flavors for taste enhancement.

Tincture - A tincture is an alcohol-based herbal extract made by soaking the white willow's bark in a solvent such as high proof vodka or glycerin. This method of preparation preserves and intensifies the plant's therapeutic properties. White willow tincture provides relief from several pain, fever, and inflammation-related issues due to salicin content in its base substance. By submerging white willow bark into alcohol or glycerine for a certain period, it releases essential oils and other active compounds that can be easily absorbed by the body when consumed orally or topically.

Poultice - A poultice is an ancient method of applying warm herbal compresses to the skin for treating inflammation, bruises, and strains. In making a white willow poultice, you combine the powdered bark with hot water until it forms a paste. This paste is then applied over affected areas and covered with a clean cloth or bandage. The heat from the compress helps to relax tense muscles while promoting blood circulation and relieving pain. Poultices provide temporary relief without causing any adverse side effects, making them an ideal alternative for those seeking natural remedies.

Decoction - A decoction is a liquid preparation of plant matter prepared by boiling the bark or other parts of white willow in water to release essential nutrients and compounds that can be easily absorbed by the body. This method is often used when dealing with gastrointestinal issues, as it helps to break down complex molecules into simpler ones for better digestion. Decoctions are also known to improve blood circulation and lower high temperatures.

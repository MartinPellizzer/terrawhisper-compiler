Garlic can have some side effects if used improperly, such as stinky breath which is a common issue when consuming garlic as it gives off a pungent smell due to the sulfur compounds released during digestion that can linger on your breath for several hours. This may not be much of a problem for some people but can certainly cause discomfort, embarrassment or discontent in social situations where fresh breath is important such as meetings, dates or presentations. 

 Another negative effect of garlic is allergic reactions that could result from the consumption of this spice. Some individuals might have an adverse reaction to it causing itching, redness and swelling on their skin and respiratory symptoms like sneezing, coughing and trouble in breathing. In such cases, people are advised to avoid or minimize garlic intake as much as possible.

 Garlic can also cause indigestion which may lead to abdominal discomforts including pain, bloating, gas and diarrhea. This is largely because raw or undiluted garlic can be harsh on the stomach lining causing irritation that may worsen conditions such as gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) or peptic ulcers. To prevent this from happening, cooking it in lower temperature over a longer period of time will reduce its intensity and make it easier to digest.

 Garlic consumption also contributes to lowered blood pressure due to the presence of allicin and other chemical substances that may interfere with the body's regulation of blood pressure. This is usually not an issue unless one has low blood pressure or takes medication for high blood pressure, where combining garlic with these might make their treatment less effective or even dangerous.

 Skin irritation and increased risk of bleeding may happen due to the compound called thiosulphinate present in garlic. It can cause mild inflammation on the skin and also thin the blood slightly making it easier for minor cuts to bleed more than usual. While garlic is generally safe, it's essential to be cautious when using it, especially if you have sensitive skin or a bleeding disorder.

 In conclusion, garlic does offer numerous health benefits when used correctly and consumed responsibly but its improper use can lead to some negative effects as listed above which might interfere with one's comfort and well-being.

Garlic juice is the liquid extract obtained from crushed or pressed garlic cloves. To create garlic juice, fresh garlic cloves are typically crushed or minced and then pressed or squeezed to extract the liquid content. This juice contains various beneficial compounds like allicin, sulfur compounds, and antioxidants. Known for its strong pungent flavor and aroma, garlic juice is commonly used in cooking, as a natural remedy, and for its potential health benefits. Its uses span across culinary applications, natural medicine, and even as a pest repellent due to its strong scent.

To prepare garlic juice, start by peeling and crushing fresh garlic cloves. Take around 250 grams of peeled garlic cloves and crush them thoroughly. Place the crushed garlic in a blender or a food processor and add approximately 500 milliliters of water. Blend the mixture until it becomes a smooth consistency. Next, strain the mixture using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to separate the solids from the liquid. The resulting liquid is the garlic juice. It can be stored in a clean, airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week. Adjust the quantities based on the desired amount of juice needed.

The recommended dosage of garlic juice varies based on individual tolerance and health conditions. Generally, a daily intake of 2 to 5 milliliters (ml), diluted in water or other liquids, is suggested for adults seeking potential health benefits. However, consuming excessive amounts of garlic juice may lead to side effects such as bad breath, digestive issues, and skin irritation. Individuals taking medications or with specific health concerns should consult a healthcare professional before incorporating garlic juice into their routine to avoid potential interactions or adverse effects.

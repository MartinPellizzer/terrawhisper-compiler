
Aged garlic supplements are a type of garlic extract processed through prolonged aging, typically over a period of 20 months. During this time, fresh garlic cloves undergo a special aging and extraction process, converting the harsh, potent compounds like allicin into more stable, beneficial compounds such as S-allyl-cysteine (SAC). These supplements are known for their numerous health benefits, including their potent antioxidant properties that help combat oxidative stress, support cardiovascular health by promoting healthy cholesterol levels, and boost the immune system due to their antimicrobial properties. Aged garlic supplements are also praised for their ability to promote circulation and overall heart health by aiding in blood pressure regulation and reducing arterial stiffness.

To prepare aged garlic supplements, start by peeling and crushing around 800 grams of fresh garlic cloves. Place the crushed garlic in an airtight container, ensuring it's completely sealed, and store it at a controlled temperature of around 60-70 degrees Celsius for approximately 20 months. This lengthy aging process allows for the transformation of the compounds in garlic into the beneficial components found in aged garlic supplements. Once the aging period is complete, the extract is often processed further into supplement forms such as capsules or liquid extracts for consumption.

For most adults, the recommended dosage of aged garlic supplements typically ranges from 600 to 1,200 milligrams per day, divided into two to three doses. However, it's crucial to consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice. While aged garlic supplements are generally well-tolerated, some individuals might experience mild side effects such as gastrointestinal discomfort or allergic reactions. In rare cases, consuming excessive amounts may lead to bad breath or body odor due to the sulfur compounds present in garlic. Monitoring intake and being aware of individual sensitivities can help mitigate these potential side effects.

Planting Pelargonium sidoides needs good soil preparation; use fertile and well drained sandy loam or clay soils that can promote rapid germination of seeds. In a sunny area, prepare the ground by digging out weeds if present at first -this should be done in fall when plants are dormant to allow roots adjust quickly next season’s growing conditions- after clearing make small mounds approximately 10 cm high and spaced about one meter apart (to accommodate airflow between individual clusters) fill each depression evenly with fertilized soil before sowing seeds slightly less than their thickness on top.

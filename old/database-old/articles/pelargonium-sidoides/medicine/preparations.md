Pelargonium has many medicinal preparations, such as Pelargonium tea, tincture, capsules, oil infusion, poultice, ointment, gargle, eardrop, bath soak, essential oil. These are all different ways to use the plant's extract for various health benefits. Understanding each of these preparations will help you determine which one suits your needs best.

 This is a simple way to enjoy Pelargonium's therapeutic effects, with just the plant's leaves and flowers steeped in hot water like how green tea is made. It can be taken hot or cold, depending on preference, and its calming properties help relieve anxiety, insomnia, and stress.

 This preparation involves extracting the active compounds from Pelargonium using alcohol. The tincture has an extremely potent concentration of the plant's benefits that can be quickly absorbed into the body. It is often used for treating colds, flu, sore throat, and other respiratory issues.

 For those who prefer a more convenient way to consume Pelargonium's medicinal properties, capsules provide a pre-measured dosage. They can be easily taken with water or added to any food or drink without altering its taste.

 This preparation is made by combining the plant's extract with an oil base, such as olive or coconut oil. The resulting mixture can be applied topically to treat skin issues like rashes and itching due to its soothing effects.

 A poultice is a paste or damp cloth made with finely crushed herbs and other ingredients. It can then be applied directly to the affected area, such as a bruise, sprain, or wound, for relief. Pelargonium's medicinal properties help reduce inflammation and pain when used in this manner.

 Similar to oil infusion, ointments are made by combining the plant extract with an oily base like petroleum jelly or beeswax. These can be applied to specific body areas for relief from skin irritations and rashes, promoting faster healing and reducing inflammation.

 Pelargonium's soothing effects can also be utilized by gargling a mixture of its extract with water, targeting sore throats, mouth ulcers, and other oral issues.

 By using a few drops of Pelargonium's extract in the ear canal, you can treat earache or irritation while also promoting better hearing.

 Adding Pelargonium to your bathwater provides a full-body relaxation experience, helping relieve muscle pain and stress.

 The concentrated extract of the plant's aromatic compounds can be used in aromatherapy to enhance mood, promote sleep, or soothe headaches.

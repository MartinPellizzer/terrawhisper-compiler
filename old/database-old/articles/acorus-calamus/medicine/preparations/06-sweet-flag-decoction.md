Sweet flag decoction is a concentrated liquid preparation made by boiling sliced or crushed Acorus calamus rhizomes in water. This process extracts the active compounds, such as acorin and β-asarone, renowned for their medicinal properties. Commonly used in traditional medicine, this decoction is valued for its anti-inflammatory, digestive, and cognitive enhancement properties. It serves various purposes, including treating gastrointestinal disorders like dyspepsia and aiding cognitive functions by enhancing memory and focus.

To prepare sweet flag decoction, take 10 grams of dried Acorus calamus rhizomes and boil them in 500 milliliters of water for about 15-20 minutes. After boiling, strain the mixture and let it cool before consumption. Adjust the quantity of water or rhizomes for desired concentration or potency. This decoction can be consumed up to two times a day.

The recommended dosage of sweet flag decoction is usually 50-100 milliliters, taken twice daily. However, caution should be exercised as high doses can lead to adverse effects such as nausea, vomiting, or even hallucinations due to its potent compounds. It's advisable to avoid its use during pregnancy, breastfeeding, or by individuals with pre-existing medical conditions like epilepsy. Always consult a healthcare professional before using sweet flag decoction to avoid potential side effects and drug interactions.

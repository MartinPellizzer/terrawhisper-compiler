Arjun (Terminalia arjuna) belongs to domain Eukaryota; kingdom Plantae; phylum Magnoliophyta/spermatophytoes, also known by the name angiosperm or flowering plants due to their seeds enclosed in a fruit after fertilization and having flowers containing male reproductive parts (stamens) with pollen grains. This genus has numerous species distributed widely within forests including tropical rainforests where temperature levels are moderate, average moisture is high but not too much; humidity should be above 60%.

Morphology describes the form or structure of an organism in botany which refers to how a plant looks like and its different parts. Arjun (Terminalia arjuna) has many structural features including compound leaves with alternate leaf arrangement, meaning each pair are positioned at right angles one from another; these have smooth margins that appear wavy-edged or slightly lobes as they grow older due to weathering factors like wind and rain. Flowers emerge during springtime in clusters of up to 15 on short racemose inflorescences with the shape being similar but not identical, having both male (stamens) parts pollinating by means insects such bee flies or butterflies which transfer sperms from one flower's anther onto its pistil. The fruits that develop after fertile flowers are matured consist of a fleshy outer covering containing nut like seed surrounded within it, giving them the name drupes fruit type common among this group too!

There exist several variants with different names based on their geographical distribution and environmental conditions they thrive under such as Terminalia arjuna var. angustifolia which has narrower leaves compared to its main counterpart; T.a var crenatipila exhibits rounded or wavy-edged margins instead of smooth ones, while others show variations in leaf size and colorations from light greenish yellow shades until deep glossy emerald hues!

Arjun's natural habitats include a wide variety which can be found throughout India (where it is considered sacred due to medicinal uses), Sri Lanka & Nepal; these trees grow well in areas that have tropical monsoon climates, with high humidity and rainfall - even river banks where flood plains allow growth under submerged roots systems adaptations. Additionally arjun cultivars possess a remarkable ability for natural forest re-colonization after disturbances like deforestation due to their rapid seed germination rates!

Lastly the life cycle of Arju tree (Terminalia arjuna) involves flowering, pollen dispersal through insects then fruit development leading up towards eventual mature fruiting stage when seeds are ready for sowing again by wind or water current action after ripeness. These plants possess both male and female reproductive structures simultaneously ensuring continuity of their species over time despite various environmental challenges they may face along the way!

Lemon, scientifically known as Citrus limon, is a popular citrus fruit known for its medicinal and culinary properties. It contains vitamins such as B-6, A, C, as well as minerals like calcium, potassium, magnesium, and phosphorus. The lemon's juice can help cure scurvy, constipation, indigestion, and even reduce fever. Additionally, its peel has been used to treat skin conditions such as acne due to its antifungal and antibacterial properties.

Lemon trees are cultivated for their aesthetic value and provide a beautiful ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes. They have glossy leaves and fragrant flowers that attract pollinators, creating an environment rich with natural beauty. Their evergreen nature also adds to the overall visual interest of any horticultural setting.

Belonging to the Rutaceae family, Citrus limon is native to Asia but is cultivated worldwide for its fruit. It has been historically significant as a source of vitamin C during scurvy epidemics and has played an essential role in culinary traditions across various cultures. The lemon's versatility allows it to be used in various cuisines, from adding flavor to dishes to creating refreshing beverages like lemonade.

It's important to take precautions when using Cardamom medicinally, such as monitoring side-effects and dosage. As a spice, it is typically used for adding flavors in foods. But in the medical field, it can be employed both, by itself or in combination with other treatments to help relieve many gastrointestinal problems. However, misuse or overuse of Cardamom can cause some adverse reactions which must be kept in mind. 

 

 Some individuals might be more prone to allergic reactions or experience digestive problems like heartburn or diarrhea upon consuming large doses of cardamom powder. It's wise to monitor the reaction post usage and discontinue use if any adverse effects are noticed.

 For medical purposes, it is essential to follow the prescribed dose from a qualified healthcare professional. Self-medicating using higher amounts than advised might increase chances of encountering side effects like the above or unwanted complications.

 Although not clinically tested yet, it's generally safe to use cardamom in small quantities during pregnancy and while nursing, provided no allergic reactions have been observed earlier. Consult with a doctor beforehand for peace of mind.

 Cardamom may interfere with certain medications or alter their efficacy. For instance, the aromatic compound present in cardamom, safrol, has shown to boost bleeding risks when combined with blood thinners. To avoid such complications, inform your healthcare provider about any medications you are taking and consult for a safe dosage.

 If you have any underlying health issues like gastrointestinal problems or liver disorders, be mindful that cardamom may aggravate these existing ailments. Therefore, discuss with your doctor if it's suitable to use cardamom in your particular case and adhere to their advice on usage amounts.

 Make sure to use the correct type of cardamom for your intended purpose. There are two main varieties - green cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum) and black cardamom (Amomum subulatum). Black cardamom is commonly used as a spice in culinary practices whereas, the green variety is more popularly utilized in medicinal applications due to its strong aroma.

 Proper storage of cardamom ensures you keep it fresh and potent for when you need it. Ensure they are kept away from sunlight, moisture, heat or air, in a cool and dry place like an airtight container or refrigerator.

Damiana can have some side effects if used improperly, such as damaging one's physical wellness and health. Some of these include but are not limited to negatively impacting a person’s fertility and ability to reproduce. In addition, it has been known to cause infertility in certain individuals due to its effect on the hormonal balance within the body, particularly disrupting normal functions which facilitate reproduction.

 The use of Damiana can also result in adverse effects on liver function as it interacts with various enzymes and chemical reactions in the body. This could potentially lead to complications if consumed regularly or taken over a long period of time, thereby endangering one's health even more. Furthermore, because it influences hormone levels, Damiana has also been linked to decreased libido and sex drive among users.

 Besides its potential impact on fertility and sexual function, Damiana can affect the blood sugar levels in an individual's body by altering glucose metabolism. Although generally not considered harmful for healthy individuals, those with pre-existing conditions such as diabetes might face complications due to these changes in their blood sugar management systems.

 Another concerning aspect of Damiana is its potential effect on the reproductive system. Due to its ability to alter hormone levels and affect how one's body processes certain chemicals, it can have a damaging impact on both male and female reproductive organs. This could ultimately result in problems such as miscarriages or complications during childbirth.

 In addition to the above-mentioned effects, Damiana has been known to influence fetal development by altering hormone levels as well. This can lead to potential birth defects or abnormalities in the developing fetus if consumed during pregnancy. Hence it's crucial that expecting mothers avoid using any form of this plant for their own safety and that of their unborn child.

 Overall, while Damiana may have some positive effects when used appropriately, its improper use can lead to significant risks, such as harming fertility, affecting hormone balance, causing liver problems, influencing blood sugar levels, altering sexual function, impacting reproductive organs and possibly even resulting in birth defects. It is essential to consider the long-term implications before incorporating it into one's daily routine or consuming it as part of a health regimen.

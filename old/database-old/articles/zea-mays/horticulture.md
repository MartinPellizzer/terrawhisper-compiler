 Corn (Zea mays) requires well-drained soil with a slightly acidic to neutral PH level, around pH of about 6 or less for optimal growth and nutrient absorption. It thrives in warmer temperatures between 20°C – 35 ° C during daytime but needs consistent care and attention throughout its various growing phases that may last up until maturity after three months since germination which depends upon the variety of corn chosen to cultivate on your farmland or backyard plot. Corn crops, being wind-pollinated, prefer sunny spots where they can receive a minimum daily sunlight exposure for at least 6 hours but will also require occasional watering especially during dry spells in order maintain adequate hydration levels throughout the growth process leading to optimal crop yields once fully matured and ready for harvest.

 For planting, make sure that your corn is carefully planted about two inches deep into well-prepared soil after amendments are incorporated evenly – preferably adding a small amount of aged manure along with compost mixed throughout the growing bed as part its early nourishment before germination begins. Space out seed rows maintaining an adequate distance between each plant to avoid overcrowding and promote better air circulation, which is vital for successful pollen dispersal in wind-pollinated species like corn (Zea mays). Also ensure that you have a proper irrigation system set up prior or immediately after the seeds are planted so as not let them dry out during early stages of growth when water supply could mean all difference between success and failure.

 Finally comes the moment of truth - when your Corn has reached full physical mature, signified by a yellowish color change from green leaves indicating that it's ready for its last leg – Harvest Time! This process involves carefully cutting off plants close to their base using sharp tools without damaging other nearby crops; then proceed with removing any excess or immature grains which may not meet desirable quality standards before finally storing properly either within suitable containers like buckets/bags under controlled temperature conditions until ready for consumption.

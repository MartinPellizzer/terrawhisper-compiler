The growth requirements for Eucalyptus (Eucalyptus globulus) involve proper sun exposure and soil conditions. These trees prefer well-draining and somewhat acidic soils with a pH between 5.0 to 6.5. They enjoy lots of sunlight, requiring at least six hours daily. The ideal temperature range for these trees is around 37ºF to 90ºF (2ºC to 32ºC). For their growth to be optimal, they need regular watering in their initial years but should be able to survive on rainfall alone after they have established themselves.

When planting Eucalyptus globulus, it's essential to choose a suitable location with enough space for the tree to grow freely without causing any obstructions. Consider soil and climate conditions as well as sunlight exposure. Prepare the planting hole about two times wider than the root ball of the young tree, ensuring that the roots are placed correctly in the ground. Water thoroughly after planting, keeping the soil moist but not soggy.

When harvesting Eucalyptus globulus, it's essential to understand that their timber is used primarily in the paper industry. Harvesting can be done at any time during the year, but ideally should happen between February and August when the trees are dormant and there's a lower risk of damaging them. Harvesting involves cutting down mature trees and processing the timber according to industry demands while utilizing all parts of the tree.

Pests and diseases pose challenges in maintaining healthy Eucalyptus globulus plantations. Common pests include eucalyptus leaf beetle, stem-boring beetles, and scale insects. In terms of diseases, Cryptococcus gattii causes dieback and canker on the affected stems. To minimize these issues, using well-maintained nursery stock and proper cultural practices is crucial for successful cultivation of Eucalyptus globulus trees.

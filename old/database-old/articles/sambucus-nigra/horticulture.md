 Elder (Sambucus nigra), a medium-sized shrub, prefers slightly acidic soil with pH ranging from 5 to 7 and well drained soils that are rich in organic matter for optimum growth rate under moderate full sun exposure. The ideal conditions would include moist but not waterlogged ground areas at an average temperature of around 20-30°C (68-94 Fahrenheit).

 To plant Elder, first prepare the garden or land area with adequate sunlight and proper soil condition. Choose a healthy nursery grown seedling for better success in germination process as they will provide enough root system to establish themselves quickly once planted outdoors; avoid using diseased plants from other sites to maintain good health of your growing shrubs, keeping the spacing between each plant appropriate at approximately one-half meter.

 Elder flower's optimal picking season typically happens around mid-summer when the bud has started opening but before it fully blooms; harvest its berries during late summer or early autumn after they turn deep purple in color, avoid using them if still green due to presence of alkaloid called sambucin that might be toxic. When done rightly with care and attention towards these guidelines you'll have a thriving elder shrub ready for use as food source (flowers) or traditional medicines from its berries in your garden!

 Although generally pest-resistant, there could be few occasions when black bean insects might attack Elder. Diseases such as leaf spot and powdery mildew can also occur if not properly cared for by ensuring proper sunshine balance while pruning overgrown branches regularly would reduce these threats dramatically - making sure your elder shrub remains healthy throughout its lifetime within garden surroundings with minimal intervention required from you!

The etymology of "Elder" originates from a Germanic word that means 'fire elder', referencing its use as kindling fuel back in ancient times. Additionally known for various reasons like spiritual rituals and traditional medicines, the term has evolved over centuries through different languages to become what we know today as Elders or Black-Elder, highlighting some of the notable properties related with Sambucus nigra tree found globally across Europe Asia Minor in areas ranging from Russia down towards China.

Culturally speaking, elder has been significant among indigenous tribes who used it for both its healing powers and spiritual ceremonies - believing that a potion brewed using this plant could bring good fortune or cure illnesses if done correctly according to their beliefs and traditional teachings passed on through generations over centuries of time spent in the wilderness.

Myths about elder were intertwined with various civilizations since ancient times; they usually depict these tree’s fruits as being associated either positively (as symbols for wisdom) or negatively (related to poisonous berries). In Greek mythology, we have Hera - queen of the gods – giving herbs like Elderberry and its flowers during childbirth while in Scandinavian folk stories told about elves using it as a medium by which they can influence dreams when hung outside doorways.

Numerous literary references involving elder plants have been found dating back to the earliest records, with some prominent authors like Shakespeare and Chaucer including Elder's folklore in their works through symbols or allusions tied towards love storylines such as Romeo & Juliet where "where for her sake will anything endure" - referencing an elder bush which had grown upon young lovers’ grave after they were killed due to misinterpreted gestures by others.

Finally, the folkloric use of Elder trees has been widespread across many societies around different continents over centuries because it's believed that hanging up some parts or placing them within dwellings can protect against negative energies and ward off harmful spells cast upon individuals living therein; additionally other cultural traditions included using its leaves for healing purposes like treating skin ailments due to their anti-inflammatory properties while the flowers were often used as herbal remedies too.

Elder has many medicinal constituents, such as Flavonoids, which are secondary metabolites found in plants and are known for their antioxidant properties that help protect the body from damage caused by free radicals. These flavonoids have a role in enhancing immune function and offering cardiovascular benefits. Some common examples of flavonoids include quercetin, rutin, and kaempferol found in elderberries.

Tannins, specifically the phenolic compounds with tannin properties, are known for their ability to protect cells from oxidative damage by scavenging free radicals. They also possess astringent and antiseptic effects which can be beneficial in treating certain conditions such as wound healing, skin care, and oral hygiene.

Anthocyanosides are another group of flavonoids that give Elder its characteristic deep purple color. These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties and can help improve blood circulation, which is essential for overall health. They also provide protection against certain types of cancer and neurodegenerative disorders, contributing to better cognitive function.

Quercetin, a type of flavonol found in elder, acts as an antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent, supporting immune system function and reducing the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease and certain types of cancer. Quercetin also exhibits antiviral properties, making it useful for treating infections and enhancing immune responses to pathogens.

Rutin is a flavonoid glycoside that offers numerous health benefits by boosting the immune system, promoting blood circulation, reducing inflammation, and helping prevent blood clots. It also supports eye health and may improve cognitive function. As it supports antioxidant activity, rutin contributes to the overall well-being of an individual.

Pectins are water-soluble fibers found in Elder that can be helpful for digestive health by aiding in the regularization of bowel movements and reducing constipation. Pectins also help lower cholesterol levels, which is beneficial for heart health. Furthermore, pectins have been shown to inhibit the growth of certain bacteria, making them useful in treating diarrhea.

Elder contains fats, carbohydrates, amino acids, and minerals as well, which all contribute to maintaining a person's overall health by providing energy, building blocks for cells, and essential nutrients required by the body for optimal functioning.

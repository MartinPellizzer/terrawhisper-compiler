 St Johns wort (Hypericum perforatum) has many health benefits that can help people cope with various conditions like depression and anxiety to a certain extent as well as providing relief from symptoms of menopause or PMS-related problems in women. Also, it could work efficiently when applied topically for healing wounds and skin damage along reducing pain caused due inflammation arising out issues such astendinitisandotherstrain injuries which might affect either adults or young ones alike making Hypericum perforatums valuable medical component irrespective of age difference.

 The medicinal aspects St Johns Wort is largely credited to its chemical constituents, including flavonoids and hyperin (hyperoside), phloroglucins such as quercetine or rutinis derivatives like anthocyanidines along with other secondary metabolites consisting of volatile oils containing sesquiterpenes responsible for the yellow tinge in flowers. Hypericum contains anti-inflammatory, antidepressant and pain reducing qualities which are why it has been used traditionally to treat a myriad health issues including minor illness like cold/fluesor even more serious conditions that needs psychiatric treatments requiring careful professional assessment

 The parts of St. John's wort utilized for medicinal purposes can range from the whole flowering plant, leaves and branches but particularly concentrations on hypericin content are extracted through a process which results in extract or tincture form known as Hyperforine that provides greater efficiencies when applied topically onto wounds/burn areas due to its antiviral abilities. The main types of doses one should opt for is based mainly dependent upon their specific health condition being treated; however, commonly advised would be 180 mg standardized extract or up-to three grams dried plant daily depending on the severity and individual response monitored by a professional medical practitioner

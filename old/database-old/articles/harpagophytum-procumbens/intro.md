Devils Claw, scientifically known as Harpagophytum procumbens, is a versatile medicinal plant commonly found in Africa. Its roots have been traditionally used for treating rheumatic pains as it boasts anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The whole root and seeds can be consumed to help ease gastrointestinal issues such as constipation, diarrhea, and stomach ulcers due to their laxative effects. Additionally, the plant has been used for culinary purposes in various African cuisines, often added to soups or stews for its earthy flavor and medicinal benefits.

In addition to its medical applications, Devils Claw also serves as an appealing garden subject with unique ornamental values. The plant's attractive appearance comes from the showy flowers that bloom in various shades of yellow, red, and orange during the summer months. The leaves are dark green and provide a pleasing contrast to the delicate flowering stems. Its height can range between 1-2 feet, making it ideal for smaller gardens or as a groundcover under shrubs and trees. Devils Claw is also relatively drought tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, further contributing to its horticultural appeal.

The botanical history of Harpagophytum procumbens can be traced back to the Kalahari Desert region of Southern Africa, where it grows primarily within dry sandy soil conditions. The plant has been a vital part of traditional African medicine for centuries, with various tribes using its roots and seeds for treating different ailments. Scientists are only just now exploring Devils Claw's potential medicinal benefits and uses, such as relieving arthritis pain, reducing inflammation, and improving digestive health.

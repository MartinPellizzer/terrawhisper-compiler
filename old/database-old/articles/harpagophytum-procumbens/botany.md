Devils Claw, with botanical name Harpagophytum procumbens, belongs to the domain Eukaryota, kingdom Plantae, phylum Tracheophyta (vascular plants), class Magnoliopsida (dicotyledons), order Polygalales, family Polygonaceae. Common names include Devil's Grapple and Windroot.

There are several variants of Devils Claw like the African species H. zeyheri, which is found in South Africa, and H. penninicum from Asia. The differences between these variants include distinct morphological features such as leaf shape, stem height, and flower color variations.

This plant is a perennial sub-shrub that grows up to 0.6 meters tall on poor sandy or clayey soil and dry thornveld areas. It has an erect stem with alternate leaves having smooth edges and a distinctly shaped fruit that turns reddish as it ripens, which contains numerous seeds.

Devils Claw is native to several parts of Africa and Asia, from sub-tropical to arid regions. Its natural habitats include savannas, grasslands, dry forests, woodlands, and thornvelds, with the ability to grow in less favourable conditions such as sandy or rocky soil and even disturbed areas.

The plant’s life cycle is relatively typical for a perennial sub-shrub. It germinates from seeds, grows through its juvenile stages to flowering and fruit maturity before spreading out again in search of more suitable conditions.

 Feverfew thrives best when planted under full sun exposure, which helps provide sufficient light for photosynthesis and growth of healthy foliage (leaves). It also prefers well-drained soil with a neutral to alkaline pH value; avoiding too moist or waterlogged conditions. The ideal temperature range is between 16°C at night to around 24°C during daylight hours, providing warmth while enabling plant growth and preventing potential diseases caused by cold weather (either extremely high heat levels could inhibit germination).

 Feverfew can be propagated through both seeds or cuttings. Sowing directly into the soil ensures higher success rates as long they're planted at least 0.6 cm deep and spaced around 15-38cm apart to avoid overcrowding; seedlings usually germinate within two weeks under optimal conditions (soil temperature of approximately 24°C). Cuttings, on the other hand should be taken from healthy stems with at least three nodes each - cut just below these points and plant them directly into a prepared bed or container filled up to half their length.

 Once established, maintain an evenly moist soil without waterlogging as it could cause root rot issues; ideally provide 2-4 cm of mulch around the base will not only help retain moisture but also prevent weeds from competing for nutrients essential in maintaining a healthy plant. Regular pruning can encourage new growth by cutting back overgrown shoots to allow more sunlight penetration and airflow at plant crowns reduces chances of fungal diseases such as Powdery Mildew occurring on your feverfew plants during summer months when humidity levels are high (use approved anti-damping agents if necessary).

After this period, you may remove any unwanted parts like stems and store your dried feverfew leaves in airtight containers to be used later as desired.

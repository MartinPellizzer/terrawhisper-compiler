Artemisia absinthium has a taproot system, with a deep-rooted structure that can extend up to 60 cm (approximately 24 inches) into the soil. These roots are relatively thin, measuring 1-2 mm (0.04-0.08 inches) in diameter, allowing for efficient nutrient and water absorption. 

One notable feature is the limited lateral branching of these roots, with only occasional lateral roots, which contributes to their efficiency in resource acquisition. Their tips are sharp and pointed, making their way through the soil with ease.

Root density in Artemisia absinthium is moderate, typically averaging around 8-12 roots per cubic centimeter (cm3). The root color ranges from pale beige to light brown, and the surface texture is smooth, often adorned with fine root hairs that enhance their capacity to absorb moisture and nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Unlike some plants that form nodules in association with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, Artemisia absinthium roots do not exhibit root nodules. Instead, they rely on their fine root hairs to extract essential nutrients from the soil.

The etymology of "Lavender" originates from ancient times when it was derived from Latin word 'lavare', which means to wash and signify cleanliness or purity associated with lavenders during early civilizations, according the Greek philosopher Plato in his dialogues on education. Lavandula angustifolia is a flowering plant that belongs to family Lamiaceae commonly referred as True English Lavender having deep roots dating back thousand of years ago when it was cultivated for its essential oil used medicinally and aromatically by ancient civilizations like the Egyptians, Greeks, Romans.

The cultural significance of lavenders has been quite immense since historic times till date in various parts across different cultures worldwide due to their therapeutic properties which made them valuable as a natural remedy for health concerns spanning from respiratory issues and digestive problems such like heartburns or stomach cramps, right through sleep difficulties because they possess sedative qualities capable of soothing the nerves too. Notably in ancient Greece lavender was used by athletes during competitions since it had cooling effects on their body temperatures which would prevent them getting overheated while competing vigorously under intense heat conditions often witnessed back then at sports events, such as Olympics Games for example where winners were crowned with a garland comprised largely of these beautiful purple flowers.

Myths and legends surrounding the use or mentioning lavender are vast ranging from Greek mythology like Persephone who was captivated by fragrant flower gardens that featured numerous blooming lavenders symbolizing hope during her sad existence when forced into a world ruled only by darkness, to medieval European fables link it with magic and enchantment where witches were said often times had their potions brewed using the flowers or oils derived from them as they believed this could bring good fortune.

Throughout literature one can trace mentions of lavender in numerous literary works over many years beginning around early nineteenth century right through modern era like William Shakespeare's A Midsummer Night’ Dream play where Titania character was associated with a powerful queen often described sitting amongst these delicate plants adding charm to her persona, also Victorian poetry featured references depicting its beauty and symbolism.

In the context of folklore traditions it is used in various rituals for good luck or protection purposes especially among Romani communities where lavender flowers are placed on graves as memorial decoration after cremations signifying eternal life being granted by spiritually cleansed nature found inside these blooms that purify surroundings they inhabit. Lavenders have been utilized widely across cultures reflecting various societal norms, beliefs and practices spanning centuries which continue to be celebrated even today despite changes in our world views due their innate therapeutic attributes both physically as well spiritually resonating throughout human history from its origins till present day experiences revealing just how powerful a flower can truly turn out being.

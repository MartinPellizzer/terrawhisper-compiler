Yarrow Infused Oil is a potent herbal extract obtained by steeping yarrow (Achillea millefolium) flowers and leaves in carrier oils like olive or sunflower oil for several weeks. Renowned for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic properties, this oil serves various purposes, including wound healing, relieving muscle spasms, and soothing skin conditions like eczema and rashes. Rich in flavonoids, phenols, and sesquiterpene lactones, yarrow oil demonstrates remarkable antioxidant and skin-soothing effects, making it a popular ingredient in natural skincare products.

To prepare Yarrow Infused Oil, finely chop 1 cup (about 30 grams) of dried yarrow flowers and leaves. Place them in a clean, dry glass jar and cover completely with a carrier oil of choice, ensuring the plant material is fully submerged. Seal the jar tightly and place it in a warm, sunny spot for 4-6 weeks, shaking it gently every few days to enhance infusion. After the steeping period, strain the oil using cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve, ensuring all plant material is removed. Store the infused oil in amber glass bottles away from direct sunlight for prolonged shelf life.

When using Yarrow Infused Oil, apply it topically in small amounts to affected areas. For wound healing, gently massage a few drops onto the injured skin. As a skincare remedy, mix a teaspoon of yarrow oil with a carrier oil and apply it to soothe irritated or inflamed skin. While generally safe, some individuals may experience allergic reactions or skin irritation. Perform a patch test before widespread use. Avoid internal consumption of yarrow oil due to potential toxicity concerns. Pregnant or nursing individuals should consult a healthcare professional before using yarrow-infused products.


The medicinal preparations of yarrow with the most constituents are the essential oil, tincture, and infusion. The essential oil contains a wide array of compounds, including chamazulene and borneol, contributing to its therapeutic properties. The tincture, an alcoholic extract, holds various active constituents like flavonoids and alkaloids. Meanwhile, the infusion, made by steeping yarrow in hot water, retains a diverse profile of phenolic compounds and volatile oils. These preparations boast a rich amalgamation of constituents, each offering potential medicinal benefits when utilized appropriately.

Yarrow's Phytosterols are natural compounds found in the aerial parts of the plant, primarily in the leaves and flowers. These phytosterols consist mainly of beta-sitosterol, campesterol, and stigmasterol, among others. The concentration of phytosterols in Yarrow varies but generally ranges from 0.1% to 0.6% in the dried plant material. For instance, beta-sitosterol content in Yarrow can reach up to 0.3%, while campesterol and stigmasterol are found in smaller amounts, around 0.05% to 0.15%. These values may fluctuate based on environmental factors, geographical location, and plant growth stage.

Phytosterols present in Yarrow possess several health benefits. They are known for their cholesterol-lowering properties and are often used to support heart health. Beta-sitosterol, the predominant phytosterol in Yarrow, mimics cholesterol's structure, competing with it for absorption in the intestines and thereby reducing overall cholesterol absorption. This action can aid in managing high cholesterol levels, potentially reducing the risk of cardiovascular diseases. Moreover, these compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, potentially contributing to alleviating conditions like arthritis, improving immune function, and promoting skin health.

Misuse or overconsumption of Yarrow's phytosterols may result in certain side effects. Excessive intake might lead to gastrointestinal disturbances such as diarrhea or constipation. Additionally, in some individuals, phytosterols could potentially interfere with the absorption of fat-soluble vitamins like A, D, E, and K, leading to deficiencies if consumed excessively over prolonged periods. Moreover, allergic reactions, though rare, are possible in sensitive individuals, manifesting as skin rashes, itching, or respiratory issues.

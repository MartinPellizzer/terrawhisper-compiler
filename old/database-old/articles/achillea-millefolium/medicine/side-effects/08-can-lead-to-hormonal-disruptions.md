
Yarrow can lead to hormonal disruptions if used improperly. This disruption primarily involves the potential interference of certain constituents in Yarrow with hormonal balance. Yarrow contains compounds like flavonoids (e.g., apigenin, luteolin), alkaloids, and sesquiterpene lactones, notably achilleine and achilleolide. These compounds possess bioactive properties, and while beneficial in controlled doses, excessive intake or prolonged use may interfere with the endocrine system, leading to hormonal imbalances.

The hormonal disruptions caused by Yarrow can aggravate conditions such as thyroid disorders, menstrual irregularities, and reproductive health issues. Individuals with existing hormonal imbalances, pregnant or breastfeeding women, and those undergoing hormone therapy should be particularly cautious. For instance, excessive consumption of Yarrow tea or supplements might exacerbate conditions like hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism. Additionally, women with estrogen-sensitive conditions like breast cancer or endometriosis may experience exacerbated symptoms due to Yarrow's potential estrogenic effects.

To prevent hormonal disruptions when using Yarrow, proper preparation and dosage are crucial. Moderation in consumption is key; excessive intake can amplify the risk of hormonal imbalance. A safe dosage typically ranges from 3 to 5 grams of dried Yarrow per day for adults, ideally consumed as an infusion or tea. Adherence to recommended guidelines and consulting a healthcare professional before use, especially for those with existing hormonal issues or pregnant women, is crucial. Moreover, alternating or cycling the use of Yarrow with periods of abstinence can help mitigate the risk of prolonged hormonal disruption.

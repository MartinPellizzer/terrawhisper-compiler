Astragalus has many medicinal constituents, such as Saponins, Flavonoids, Isovitexin, Astragalosides I and IV, Isoflavones, Polysaccharides, Glycoside, Sterols, Phenolic acids, Essential oil. These are all health-promoting compounds found in this plant. Each of these has its own unique benefits for the human body, but they also work together to enhance the overall wellness and improve a person's health.

            Saponins are known for their anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate pain and reduce swelling. They may also support immunity by stimulating white blood cell activity. Flavonoids, on the other hand, possess powerful antioxidant effects which protect cells from damage caused by harmful free radicals. This in turn helps prevent aging and the development of certain diseases.

            Isovitexin is an active ingredient that offers liver protection, promoting good overall liver function. Astragalosides I and IV are two types of molecules that improve cardiovascular health by lowering blood pressure and reducing cholesterol levels. Isoflavones have been found to be beneficial for menopausal women, providing relief from hot flashes and other symptoms associated with this stage in life.

            Other active compounds like Polysaccharides aid in strengthening the immune system, while Glycoside enhances kidney function. Sterols contribute to maintaining healthy cholesterol levels. Phenolic acids can regulate blood sugar levels, and Essential oil has antimicrobial properties which may protect against bacterial infections. All these benefits make Astragalus a valuable plant for promoting overall health and well-being.

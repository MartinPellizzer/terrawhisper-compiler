 Domain - Eukaryota; Kingdom Plantae, Phylum Spermatophyta, Class Magnoliopsida or Dicotyledonae order Lamiales and family Lamiaceae. Some common names for this plant are Mad Dog Weed, Blue Pimpernel & Skullweeds depending on its variant type which we will discuss later in the text as well as some habitat locations across Earth where you can find it growing naturally.

There exist several variants like different forms or cultivars of Scutellaria species with slight differences among them such as 'Minnesota Blue', distinguished by having blueish green coloration, then comes White Skullcap; Yellow Fringed-skulcap characterized mostly due to their contrasting yellow flowers.

Morphologically speaking about this botanical beauty is it generally grows up into a perennial herb with square stems and opposite leaves which are covered in hair or felt like structure giving them furry texture making plant very distinctive from others similar shaped foliage. The flower heads appear on the top of these stalks as clusters having small blue, purple flowers that resemble skullcaps hence their name Skullcap (Scutellaria).

Geographically speaking, they mainly thrive in wooded wetlands or moist areas like near ponds and rivers where it has abundant light due to open canopy. They are native across North America from as far South Texas up through eastern half of United States spreading into Canada all the way southward until Mexico's border regions too which explains its widespread distribution throughout different biomes including temperate forests, savannah lands & wetlands in those areas where they get optimum environmental conditions required for their growth.

The life cycle is quite standard botanical process involving reproduction by seeds produced after flowering period when pollination takes place between male and female parts of flowers through wind or insects which then leads to fertilization followed with development into seed pod containing numerous minute matured offspring ready waiting inside protected spaces on plant until they are dispersed via natural means like being carried away from the original spot by animals, weather activities causing upheavals at times resulting in scattered seeds sown anew.

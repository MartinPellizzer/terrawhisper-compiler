Celery can have some side effects if used improperly, such as disrupting digestion by being too tough on the stomach and intestinal lining, which could cause bloating due to gas buildup. This issue is not uncommon and could affect anyone who consumes large amounts of raw or unwashed celery. Furthermore, for those with allergies, celery can trigger symptoms such as itchy eyes, sneezing, or even rashes, especially if they already suffer from a food allergy or a sensitive disposition. 

 One potential consequence associated with eating celery is that it may cause headaches in some individuals, particularly those who are prone to them, due to the presence of certain chemicals in celery that can affect brain chemistry. If eaten excessively, celery could also contribute to lowering blood pressure in certain cases, which could be harmful if this was already an issue for the individual in question. 

 Another negative effect is that it may interfere with proper absorption of nutrients since a small component present in celery can bind to other minerals and prevent them from being fully absorbed by the body. This could lead to deficiencies over time if not addressed. As celery contains a natural compound known as apigenin, which is similar to the chemicals found in asthma medication, it may potentially worsen asthma symptoms for those who already suffer from this condition. 

 Celery consumption might also affect the thyroid gland's functioning due to its influence on hormone levels and metabolism. It can either trigger the body's production of thyroid hormones or alter their normal balance, which could result in a range of symptoms, from weight gain to fatigue. Moreover, if an individual takes medications, consuming large amounts of celery might impact their absorption and effectiveness, as it interacts with certain enzyme systems responsible for breaking down drugs in the body. 

 Lastly, due to its high content of oxalates, which are naturally occurring substances found in plants, eating too much celery may potentially contribute to the formation of kidney stones in some individuals who are predisposed to this condition. In conclusion, while celery is generally a healthy addition to most diets, overconsumption or certain sensitivities can lead to these negative effects, making it crucial for people to be mindful and moderate when enjoying this vegetable.

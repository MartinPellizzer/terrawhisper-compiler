 Lobelia inflata requires well-drained soil with a neutral to slightly acidic pH for successful development, preferably between 5 and 7 on scale of ten (or ranging from about 46°Celsius downwards). It thrives best in sunny spots where they can get full sunlight or partial shade. Planting these flowers requires proper spacing with a distance at least twice the width each plant reaches, ensuring adequate air circulation and preventing fungal diseases like powdery mildew that prefer wet leaves of overcrowded plants due to lack such circulation resulting in poor growth performance ultimately hindering their blooming potential when growing more than 30 centimeters tall. They tolerate light frosts so cold hardiness is not a concern but be cautious during extreme heat periods which can cause wilting or even death of the plant if left unattended; temperature variations are optimal at around room temperatures (18°-25ºC) with adequate moisture level between 60% and 79%.

 To begin, select an ideal spot in your garden based on its sunny nature while providing enough space as aforementioned so plants grow strong stems without any crowding issues that may cause diseases later down the line due to poor air circulation around leaves or branches; next prepare well-draining loam soil by enriching it with compost at about 5 centimeters deep, ensuring proper drainage is maintained which keeps water from pooling near roots thereby helping prevent root rot. Finally plant seedlings roughly three weeks after germination stage where they can receive full sunlight or partial shade and avoid direct exposure to extreme heat during summer months for best growth potential when using a spacing between 60-75cm depending on your preferred aesthetic layout in the garden setting while maintaining healthy air circulation around each individual Lobelia inflata plant.

 To collect flowers from your healthy and well-tended crop of lobelias, start when buds are just opening or within initial few days after full bloom occurs to maximize harvested yield while avoiding damaging plant's aesthetic beauty; cut stems at their base using sharp scissors for clean cuts that minimize chances of infection post cutting process ensuring optimal flower preservation throughout each usage purpose whether it be decorative purposes in arrangements or medicinal benefits associated with this species widely recognized within various cultures worldwide.

Sweet Annie has many medicinal preparations, such as infusions, teas, tinctures, decoctions, oil extracts, essential oils, poultices, compresses, salves, and ointments, which are used for various health benefits. Each preparation offers different levels of potency and application methods to cater to specific needs.

 An infusion is made by steeping the plant material in hot water or other liquid for a short time to extract its beneficial properties. This method allows for easy digestion, as the water-soluble components are drawn out of the plant and suspended in the liquid. Infusions can be used internally or externally depending on the purpose, such as relieving stress, promoting sleep, and treating respiratory problems.

 Tea is another common medicinal preparation of Sweet Annie. The procedure here resembles that of an infusion but with a longer steeping time to extract more potent substances from the plant material. The tea is prepared in hot water and can be used either plain or with additional herbs for added benefits. Like infusions, teas can help soothe the mind and body while promoting overall well-being.

 A tincture involves the extraction of active compounds from a plant using alcohol as a solvent. This process preserves potent, concentrated elements that might be lost during other preparation methods. Tinctures are often used for pain relief, enhancing the immune system, and treating digestive issues.

 Decoctions, similar to infusions, involve boiling rather than steeping plant materials in water. The extraction process is more intense, resulting in higher potency levels that may be appropriate for specific conditions, such as fever or joint pain.

 An oil extract of Sweet Annie can be obtained by soaking the dried plants in a carrier oil like olive oil. This preparation offers benefits for skin care and external application to treat various issues, including inflammation and acne.

 Essential oils are highly concentrated extracts of Sweet Annie's aromatic compounds, often distilled using steam or pressing. They can be diffused or used in blends to promote relaxation, improve mood, and alleviate certain medical conditions.

 Poultices and compresses utilize the plant material directly, while ointments and salves incorporate additional ingredients such as fats or oils for better absorption into the skin. These preparations provide relief from various ailments, including muscular pain, inflammation, and minor wounds.

Nettle has many medicinal constituents, such as histamine, formic acid, acetylcholine, serotonin, calcium, folate (folic acid), iron, vitamin A, vitamin C, silicon. These components are substances derived from the nettle plant and are known to have specific physiological functions that contribute to its medicinal properties. 

 Histamine is a chemical compound that plays a key role in the body's immune response and inflammation control mechanism. In allergic reactions, histamine causes swelling and itching of the skin, congestion in nasal passages, and other symptoms. Nettle contains high levels of this natural chemical compound. 

 Formic acid is a colorless organic acid that exists naturally within many living organisms. It can also be found as an irritant in nettle stings. While the sting may cause discomfort, it's believed to have some medicinal properties such as pain relief and antibacterial effects. 

 Acetylcholine is a neurotransmitter that aids in transmitting nerve impulses within the nervous system and from nerves to muscles. It also plays an important role in muscle contraction, learning, and memory processes. Nettle is rich in this substance.

 Serotonin is a vital brain chemical involved in mood regulation, sleep, appetite, and digestion. Low levels of serotonin are associated with depression and anxiety disorders. Consuming nettle may help boost serotonin levels and improve overall mental health. 

 Calcium is an essential mineral needed for maintaining strong bones and teeth, proper muscle function, as well as nerve signaling. Nettle contains calcium in significant amounts, promoting healthier bone density.

 Folate (Folic acid) is a type of vitamin B that helps the body produce and maintain new cells. It is critical to DNA synthesis, red blood cell formation, brain development during pregnancy, and preventing neural tube defects in infants. Nettle has folic acid content that can contribute to these vital processes.

 Iron is a mineral necessary for the production of hemoglobin, which carries oxygen throughout the body. An iron deficiency causes anemia, leading to fatigue, weakness, and decreased cognitive function. Nettle contains considerable amounts of iron, helping prevent or treat anemia. 

 Vitamin A, vitamin C, and silicon are essential nutrients in nettle that contribute to various aspects of health such as vision, immune system support, collagen production for skin and bones, and tissue repair.

Aloe vera boasts a fibrous root system, which consists of both primary and lateral roots. These roots exhibit dichotomous branching, a pattern where the main root divides into two equal branches. The root tips are pointed, or acute.

In terms of density, Aloe vera roots are quite abundant, with approximately 30 roots per square centimeter (186 roots per square inch). However, they do not reach great depths, typically staying shallow at around 30-60 centimeters (12-24 inches).

The root diameter measures approximately 2-4 millimeters (0.08-0.16 inches), while primary roots can reach lengths of up to 20 centimeters (8 inches). Root color is pale, ranging from white to a light tan hue, and the texture is smooth.

One distinctive feature is the presence of numerous root hairs, which are fine and hair-like structures covering the outer layer of the roots. These root hairs aid in the absorption of water and nutrients from the soil. Notably, Aloe vera roots lack nodules, which are often found in plants that engage in nitrogen fixation.

Peppermint has been used extensively for various culinary purposes like garnishing, flavoring foods and adding an aromatic kick to drinks such as tea or desserts including mint chocolate chip ice creams due in large part of its unique combination refreshing mentholated flavors mixed with sweet undertones.

The distinct blend from the plant's two essential oils – Mentha (which contains natural organic compounds like carvone and limonene contributing to it’s characteristic taste profile that can be described as both minty, slightly fruity yet cooling on your tongue simultaneously creating sensations of freshness in any dish or drink.

Edible parts include the leaves which are typically used for culinary preparations due to their abundance and flavorful nature; they're easily added into salads giving them a tang that contrasts nicely with creamy dressings, as well being an ideal addition when creating marinades/brines or adding freshness while pickling. The essential oils found within these leaves also make it great for making mint syrups which are used to flavor beverages and desserts alike!

 start by using fresh, high-quality ingredients; when adding the herb remember a little goes along way since overdoing it may lead towards an overwhelmingly powerful menthol flavour which could be off putting. For best results always adjust accordingly according to personal taste preferences and desired outcome of dish/drink you're preparing - just keep in mind that too much intensity might not necessarily equate superior quality, so use with caution!

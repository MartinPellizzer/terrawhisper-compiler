Knowing the [morphology of Aesculus hippocastanum](/aesculus-hippocastanum/botany/morphology.html) is useful if you want to be able to classify this plant. 

This tree exhibits conical, tapering taproots that can reach depths of up to 4.5 meters with diameters ranging from 2.5 to 5 centimeters. Its herbaceous or tree-like stems can grow up to 30 meters in height with diameters of 50-120 centimeters at maturity. 

Its palmately compound leaves have 5-7 obovate to elliptical leaflets, while its bisexual, conical to bell-shaped flowers are sweetly scented and velvety. The inedible, toxic fruits are dark brown capsules containing smooth, shiny, bitter seeds.

Seed extract: This is obtained from the seeds of the horse chestnut tree. It's made by using an extraction process to separate and concentrate the beneficial components. These may help improve blood circulation and reduce swelling in various parts of the body due to sprains or strains.

Pod tincture: The pods are the green cases that surround horse chestnut seeds. A tincture is a liquid extract created from them. It's prepared by soaking them in an alcohol solution, which enhances the absorption of their active components when taken internally. This can help with treating varicose veins and venous insufficiency.

Leaf tea: The leaves are often brewed into a herbal tea to create this remedy. Brewing them releases their beneficial compounds, which may have anti-inflammatory properties that can alleviate pain from conditions like arthritis and muscle soreness.

Bark decoction: This is made by boiling the tree's bark in water to extract its active components. The resulting liquid can be consumed internally for its antioxidant benefits or used topically for treating skin conditions such as eczema, acne, and other inflammatory issues.

Oil: Horse chestnut oil is created by infusing the seed's fatty oils with the beneficial components. It can be applied directly to the skin to reduce swelling and improve circulation. The anti-inflammatory properties may also help alleviate pain from injuries or conditions like arthritis.

Salve: This is a thick, semi-solid preparation made by combining oil with wax to make it more solid. It has a similar effect as the oil in reducing swelling and improving circulation while also providing a protective layer for skin.

Lotion: A lotion involves blending water or another liquid with various oils and other ingredients, creating a lightweight and easily absorbed preparation for external use on the skin. This can offer a mix of benefits from both oil and water-based preparations to help with various skin conditions.

Infusion: An infusion is created by steeping horse chestnut leaves or flowers in hot water and letting it steep. This process releases their active compounds which, when consumed, can promote a natural calming effect on the body and may help alleviate anxiety and stress.

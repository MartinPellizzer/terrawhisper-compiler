Horse chestnut decoction is a preparation made by boiling crushed horse chestnut seeds or bark in water to extract its active compounds. This decoction is known for its various medicinal uses due to the presence of aescin, a compound with anti-inflammatory properties. It's primarily utilized to alleviate symptoms related to chronic venous insufficiency, such as leg swelling, varicose veins, and leg pain. Additionally, horse chestnut decoction has been studied for its potential to improve blood circulation, reduce edema, and strengthen blood vessel walls. Its properties as a vasoconstrictor and anti-edematous agent make it beneficial in treating conditions associated with poor blood circulation.

To prepare horse chestnut decoction, typically, 50 grams of crushed horse chestnut seeds or bark are added to 1 liter of water in a pot. The mixture is brought to a boil and then simmered for approximately 20-30 minutes. Afterward, the decoction is strained to remove the plant material, yielding the liquid extract that can be consumed. The dosage often ranges from 100-150 milliliters taken two to three times daily. It's essential to consult a healthcare professional for accurate dosing and preparation instructions, as variations in potency can occur based on the specific batch of horse chestnut used.

When using horse chestnut decoction, it's crucial to adhere to recommended dosages to avoid potential side effects such as nausea, stomach upset, dizziness, or headaches. Excessive intake can lead to toxicity, characterized by symptoms like vomiting, diarrhea, and in severe cases, liver damage. Individuals with liver or kidney diseases, pregnant or breastfeeding women, and those on blood-thinning medications should avoid horse chestnut preparations due to potential interactions and adverse effects.

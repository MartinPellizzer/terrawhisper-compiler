Horse chestnut salve is a topical ointment prepared from the seeds or extract of the horse chestnut tree. This salve is renowned for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and vasoprotective properties. It's commonly used to alleviate symptoms related to varicose veins, bruises, swelling, and hemorrhoids. The active compound in horse chestnut, aescin, aids in reducing inflammation by strengthening capillary walls and improving blood circulation. Additionally, the salve's tannins and saponins contribute to its healing effects on skin conditions like eczema and itching. Its potent antioxidant content further enhances its ability to combat free radicals and promote skin health.

To prepare horse chestnut salve, start by finely grinding 50 grams of dried horse chestnut seeds. Combine these with 200 milliliters of a neutral carrier oil, such as olive oil or coconut oil, in a heat-resistant container. Simmer the mixture over low heat for approximately 1-2 hours, ensuring the oil doesn't exceed 60°C (140°F). Afterward, strain the mixture through cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve to remove the solid particles. Add 20 grams of beeswax to the infused oil and gently heat until the beeswax completely dissolves. Once melted, remove from heat and let it cool slightly before pouring it into clean, sterilized jars. Allow the salve to solidify at room temperature for a few hours before sealing the jars.

For topical application, a small amount of horse chestnut salve can be applied to affected areas up to 3 times per day. However, it's crucial to perform a patch test prior to widespread use to check for any allergic reactions. While generally considered safe, excessive use or ingestion can result in side effects such as nausea, dizziness, or stomach upset due to the presence of aescin. Pregnant or breastfeeding individuals should avoid using horse chestnut salve.

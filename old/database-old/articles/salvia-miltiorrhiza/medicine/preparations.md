Dan Shen has many medicinal preparations, such as Tea - It is a popular and common way to consume herbal medicine. The preparation process includes boiling the dried herbs in water and brewing them for several minutes. This method allows the active ingredients of the herb to be dissolved into the liquid form, making it easier to take them orally.

 Tincture - A tincture is another alternative way to consume Dan Shen herbs. It involves a method called maceration where alcohol (usually ethanol) and water are used to extract active compounds from dried plants, turning it into a concentrated liquid form. Tinctures can be taken orally like drops, diluted with other liquids or applied externally on the skin.

 Capsule - An ideal form when it comes to herbal supplements. The capsules contain powdered Dan Shen herbs and are made of a hard outer shell. These offer more discrete dosing options and do not need to be prepared with water like tea or tinctures, making them convenient for people on the go.

 Pill - Similar to capsules, pills also contain dried herbal powders compressed into small solid shapes. They are usually taken orally with water or a preferred liquid. This preparation method is popular as it provides an easy and discrete way of consuming Dan Shen remedies.

 Powder - When dealing with dried herbs, grinding them into a fine powder helps release their active ingredients more quickly upon intake. The powder can be mixed with other substances like water or honey to create an herbal paste. These can then be applied on the skin, taken orally, or incorporated in food recipes for medicinal purposes.

 Decoction - Another form of herbal tea preparation, where a mixture of herbs are simmered in hot water to release their beneficial components. This method is often used when dealing with more potent herbs that may need extra heat and time to break down cellular walls and extract the required properties.

 Lotion - Herbal lotions work like essential oils, but they also include additional moisturizing or soothing ingredients such as natural glycerin, beeswax or cocoa butter. These preparations are applied directly on the skin for relief from pain, inflammation or itchiness.

 Ointment - Often made by combining herbal extracts with oils and fats like petroleum jelly or beeswax, ointments offer a thicker consistency that is useful for treating skin conditions or preventing dehydration in wounds. They are usually applied topically to the affected area.

 Essential oil - Made from concentrated extracts of Dan Shen's aromatic compounds, essential oils have medicinal and fragrant properties. They can be used in aromatherapy techniques or blended with other carrier oils for therapeutic applications such as relaxation and stress relief.

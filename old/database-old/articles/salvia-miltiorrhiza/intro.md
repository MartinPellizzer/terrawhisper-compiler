Dan Shen (Salvia Miltorrhizae), a versatile medicinal herb with culinary applications too has been extensively studied to offer us better insight into its usage for healing different types or organs in our body such as heart, blood vessels & liver and also used widely from making food recipes. Notably known among the Chinese medicine practitioners it is an adaptable perennial shrub which belongs to Lamiaceae family of plants commonly found across various regions including Asia like China's provinces Hunan Sichuan Yunnan etc., Japan, Vietnam etc

When considering Dan Shas horticultural attributes and ornamental worthiness; it’s important not only for its medicinal properties but also the beauty this plant offers. Its evergreen leafy structure along with red-purple stem & roots makes up an attractive appearance that serves nicely as a garden feature too, providing seasonable flowering during late spring to early summer months making your gardens aesthetically pleasant and enjoyable sight.

Botanical history reveals Salvia miltiorrhiza has been known for ages in traditional Asian healing practice before entering modern pharmaceuticals. Its name originated from ancient Greek "salvias" meaning sage-like plants, while 'milti' referring to many drops (probably indicating abundant flowers) and finally rhizoma which means root hence naming it as Salvia miltiorrhiza or Dan shen reflecting the historical connections.

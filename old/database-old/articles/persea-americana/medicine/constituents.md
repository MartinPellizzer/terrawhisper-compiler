Avocado has many medicinal constituents, such as Beta-sitosterol - this compound is a plant sterol that helps in improving overall cardiovascular health. Beta-sitosterol does this by lowering the bad cholesterol (LDL), inhibiting LDL oxidation, and boosting good cholesterol (HDL) levels in the body. It also has anti-inflammatory properties that help with joint pains. 

Phytosterols - these are a group of plant sterols found in avocados. They have similar benefits to those from Beta-sitosterol and also help in lowering bad cholesterol levels, improving digestion, and reducing the risk of heart disease by reducing plaque formation. Moreover, they can be helpful in treating certain skin conditions like eczema or psoriasis. 

Monounsaturated fatty acids - these are a type of fat that avocados have high content of. They play a crucial role in lowering bad cholesterol and triglyceride levels, thus reducing the risk of heart diseases, while simultaneously raising good cholesterol. Monounsaturated fats also help in maintaining healthy body weight by making you feel fuller for longer periods. 

Vitamin E - this fat-soluble vitamin found abundantly in avocados is a potent antioxidant that helps protect cells from damage caused by free radicals. It can reduce the risk of heart disease, boost immune function, and play an important role in maintaining healthy skin and eyesight. 

Vitamin K - another essential nutrient found in avocados, Vitamin K is necessary for proper blood clotting, bone health, and plays a crucial role in brain functioning and cell growth regulation. 

Folate (Vitamin B9) - this vitamin is vital for cellular division, and it's crucial for the production of new cells and maintenance of existing ones. Folate is also important during pregnancy to prevent neural tube defects in the fetus. 

Potassium and Magnesium - these minerals work together to help regulate blood pressure, maintain healthy nerve function, support muscle contractions, and promote overall heart health. They are both potent in avocados, offering additional benefits for cardiovascular health. 

Omega-3 Fatty Acid (Alpha-linolenic acid) - a type of polyunsaturated fatty acid that can help lower bad cholesterol levels while reducing the risk of heart disease and inflammation. Moreover, it's essential for brain function and supports overall cognitive health. 

Glutathione Peroxidase - this powerful enzyme is an antioxidant found in avocados that helps protect cells from oxidative stress, preventing cellular damage and promoting better immune system functioning.


             The study investigates the short-term and long-term effects of a valerian extract on both objective and subjective sleep parameters in 16 patients with previously established psychophysiological insomnia. After a single dose, no effects were observed, but after multiple dosages for 14 days, sleep efficiency increased significantly for valerian condition, along with a decrease in slow-wave sleep latency and an increase in slow-wave sleep percentage. Other improvements in sleep structure such as an increase in REM percentage and a decrease in NREM1 percentage also occurred. The study found that the herbal extract of radix valerianae demonstrated positive effects on sleep structure and perception, and can be recommended for mild psychophysiological insomnia.

This study aimed to investigate the efficacy of Passiflora incarnata herbal tea on human sleep, using sleep diaries and polysomnography (PSG). The study featured a double-blind, placebo-controlled, repeated-measures design with a one week washout period. Forty-one participants filled out sleep diaries for seven days, while ten also underwent overnight PSG. Of six sleep diary measures analyzed, sleep quality showed a significantly better rating for passionflower compared to placebo (p < 0.01). These initial findings suggest that low doses of Passiflora incarnata in the form of tea yield short-term sleep benefits for healthy adults with mild fluctuations in sleep quality.

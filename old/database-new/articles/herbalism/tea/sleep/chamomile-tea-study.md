A recent randomized controlled trial aimed to evaluate the effectiveness of chamomile tea on sleep quality, fatigue, and depression in postpartum women. The study recruited Taiwanese postnatal women with poor sleep quality and randomly assigned them either to the experimental group (chamomile tea drinkers) or the control group (receiving regular postpartum care). Compared to the control group, the experimental group showed significantly lower scores for physical-symptoms-related sleep inefficiency and symptoms of depression at the immediate post-test. However, the positive effects were limited to the short term, with no significant differences between the two groups in scores on the PSQS, Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale, and Postpartum Fatigue Scale at 4-week post-test. The findings suggest that chamomile tea may be recommended as a supplementary approach for alleviating depression and sleep quality problems in postpartum women.

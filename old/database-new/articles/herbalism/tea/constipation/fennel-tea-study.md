
A randomized, crossover, placebo-controlled, single-blinded trial was conducted on 20 patients presenting with chronic constipation according to American Association of Gastroenterology criteria to evaluate the efficacy and safety of a phytotherapic compound containing Pimpinella anisum L., Foeniculum vulgare Miller, Sambucus nigra L., and Cassia augustifolia. The primary endpoint was colonic transit time (CTT), measured radiologically. Secondary endpoints included number of evacuations per day, perception of bowel function, adverse effects, and quality of life. It has be found that the phytotherapic compound assessed had laxative efficacy and is a safe alternative option for the treatment of constipation.

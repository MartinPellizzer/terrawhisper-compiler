
            This study compares the efficacy and cost effectiveness of a senna-fiber combination and lactulose in treating constipation in long stay elderly patients. It was conducted as a randomized, double blind, cross over study in four hospitals in Northern Ireland, one hospital in England, and two nursing homes in England. Seventy-seven elderly patients with a history of chronic constipation in long term hospital or nursing home care were included in the study. The senna-fiber combination and lactulose were both effective and well tolerated for chronic constipation in long stay elderly patients. However, the senna-fiber combination was significantly more effective than lactulose at a lower cost.

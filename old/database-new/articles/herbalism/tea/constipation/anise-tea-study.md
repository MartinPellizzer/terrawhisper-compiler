A randomized clinical trial showed the phytotherapic compound containing Pimpinella anisum L., Foeniculum vulgare Miller, Sambucus nigra L., and Cassia augustifolia was effective in reducing colonic transit time and increasing the number of evacuations per day in patients with chronic constipation. The study also found that the compound was safe with minimal adverse effects observed during treatment. Therefore, it can be concluded that this compound is a laxative alternative option for treating constipation.

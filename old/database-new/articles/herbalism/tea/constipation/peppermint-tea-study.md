 Overall the study showed that peppermint oil had a significant effect on alleviating the symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome compared to a placebo in the tested patient group. The improvement in symptoms was evident both after 4 weeks of treatment and at the end of the 8-week period. In particular, peppermint oil appeared more effective than a placebo in reducing total symptoms score, abdominal pain or discomfort, diarrhoea and urgency at defecation. The study also showed that peppermint oil had no significant effect on lactose intolerance or celiac disease, which excludes an interaction with these conditions. Therefore, the results of this study provide evidence for the use of peppermint oil in the management of irritable bowel syndrome.

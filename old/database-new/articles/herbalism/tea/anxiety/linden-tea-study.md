
The study aimed to compare the anxiolytic effects obtained with Tilia Americana var Mexicana leaves, inflorescences, and flavonoids identified in these extracts in mice. The inflorescences and leaves of this species were macerated in hexane, ethyl acetate, and methanol, and analyzed for pharmacological activity using HPLC. Flavonoids such as quercetin, rutin, and isoquercitrin were detected in the extracts. The anxiolytic effects obtained with the inflorescences and leaves were found to be the same, and these activities were blocked by the antagonists flumazenil and WAY100635. This suggests that flavonoids such as quercetin, rutin, and isoquercitrin may play a role in the anxiolytic effects of Tilia Americana var Mexicana.

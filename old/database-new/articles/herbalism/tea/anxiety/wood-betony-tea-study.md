
            This study examined the behavioral activity of methanol extracts from four different species of Balkan wood betony, or Stachys taxa: SA, SB, SP, and SAD. The researchers tested the anxiolytic, sedative, and myorelaxant effects of these extracts on adult male Wistar rats using several tests.
        
            After testing the different species’ behavioral activity, the researchers found that only SB at 400g/kg exerted an anxiogenic-like effect, meaning it increased anxiety in the rats. On the other hand, all of the extracts lacked anxiolytic or myorelaxant effects.
        
            The study also found that chlorogenic acid and verbascoside were present in all of the extracts. However, the flavonoid fraction composition was different among the species, with isoscutellarein and hypolaetine glycosides in SA, SB, and SAD, and chrysoeriol and apigenin glycosides in SP.
        
            Overall, the study revealed that SAD appeared to be the most promising natural sedative among the four species of Stachys taxa tested, showing the potential for its use as a psychotropic agent.

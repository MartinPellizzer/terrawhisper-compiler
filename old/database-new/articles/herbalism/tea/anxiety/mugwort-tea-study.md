
            The study found that mugwort has a sedative effect on the nervous system and may be used to treat anxiety and other psychoneuroses. It is also used to induce dreams, which could be related to its sedative effects. Mugwort contains a range of active compounds including monoterpenes, sesquiterpenes, flavonol glycosides, coumarins, polyacetylenes, carotenoids and pentacyclic triterpenes. These compounds may contribute to its sedative and anxiolytic effects. The study also noted that mugwort has been used as an abortifacient, although no discussion was provided on the preparation used or the safety of this use.

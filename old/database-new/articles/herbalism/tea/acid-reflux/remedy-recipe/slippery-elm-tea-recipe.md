
Step 1: Boil water in a kettle or pot.

Step 2: Grind slippery elm bark into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle. Use approximately 1 tablespoon (8 grams) of slippery elm bark for every 8 ounces (240 milliliters) of water.

Step 3: Steep the ground slippery elm in hot water for 5-10 minutes. The longer it steeps, the stronger the tea will be.

Step 4: Strain the tea through a cheesecloth or fine mesh strainer to remove any solids.

Step 5: Serve the tea warm and enjoy!

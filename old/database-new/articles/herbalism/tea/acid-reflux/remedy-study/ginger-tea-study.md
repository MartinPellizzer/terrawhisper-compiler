
            This study aimed to observe and compare the effects of Zingiber officinale, Pinellia ternata and Sobanhatang on reflux esophagitis induced by gastric fundus and pylorus ligation in mice with esomeprazole. The antioxidant effects were measured using DPPH radical scavenging activity at different concentrations of the drugs. Zingiber officinale showed the highest antioxidative, anti-inflammatory and mucosal protective effects on the esophagus compared with esomeprazole. Pinellia ternata showed an enhanced gastric motility compared to Zingiber officinale, while Sobanhatang was expected to be effective treatment because it has advantages of both drugs and reduces toxicity. The study suggests that ginger and its derivatives can be useful in the prevention and treatment of reflux esophagitis.

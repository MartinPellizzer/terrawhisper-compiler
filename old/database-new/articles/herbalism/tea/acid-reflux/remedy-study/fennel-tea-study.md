This study aimed to examine the effectiveness of traditional Chinese nursing care using fennel mixed with coarse salt for ironing, combined with umbilical moxibustion, for epigastric pain. The study was conducted as a randomized control trial (RCT) at Ruikang Affiliated Hospital in the capital city of Guangxi Zhuang autonomous region. 96 patients with epigastric pain resulting from the spleen-stomach vacuity cold pattern were recruited and randomly divided into an intervention group and a control group. The intervention group received fennel mixed with coarse salt for ironing, combined with umbilical moxibustion, while the control group received routine care.

All data collection tools including the TCM Syndrome Score Scale (TCMSSS), Medical Outcome Study Short Form 36 (SF-36), and Satisfaction with TCM Nursing Program (STCNP) were used to collect information. Results of statistical analyses, conducted using descriptive statistics, a Chi-square test, and an independent t-test, indicated that the intervention group had significantly higher decreases in epigastric pain compared to the control group.

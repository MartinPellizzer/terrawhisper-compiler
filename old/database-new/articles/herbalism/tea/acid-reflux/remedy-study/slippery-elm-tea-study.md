
The study aimed to test the effectiveness and tolerability of the Nutrition Care (NC) Gut Relief Formula in improving gastrointestinal symptoms and gut health in adults with digestive disorders. The formula contained a combination of herbs and nutrients, including slippery elm, curcumin, Aloe vera, guar gum, pectin, peppermint oil, and glutamine.
             
The study used a 16-week pre-post design with participants taking either 5 g/d or 10 g/d of the formula for 4 weeks. GI symptoms were assessed using validated questionnaires such as the Leeds Dyspepsia Questionnaire, Bristol Stool Chart, and Birmingham IBS Symptom Questionnaire. Intestinal permeability was also measured, along with gut microbiota profile.

The results showed that the NC Gut Relief Formula significantly improved the frequency and severity of upper and lower GI symptoms by 60%-80%, including indigestion, heartburn, nausea, constipation or diarrhea, abdominal pain, and troublesome flatulence. The formula also significantly improved physical functioning, energy levels, mood, and sleep by 60%-80%.

In terms of intestinal permeability, all participants with normal stool recovered from it, while 90% with hard stool and 66% with soft stool also recovered. The formula generally improved the microbial profile, with a marked increase in Lactobacillus, Clostridium, and Faecalibacterium prausnitzii.

Regarding reflux medication, almost half of participants who were taking proton pump inhibitors for heartburn no longer required them at the end of the study. Additionally, a third of participants were able to reintroduce food triggers such as fermentable oligosaccharides, disaccharides, monosaccharides, and polyols garlic, onion, and beans or reflux-causing acidic/spicy foods such as citrus, tomato, and caffeine in their diet without symptom aggravation.

Overall, the study demonstrated that the NC Gut Relief Formula significantly improved GI symptoms and associated quality of life over 3 months while reducing intestinal permeability, improving the microbial profile, reducing the need for reflux medication, and enabling the consumption of previous food triggers.


               In a study investigating the efficacy of lemon balm (M. officinalis) as an herbal remedy for acid reflux, ethanol-induced gastric ulcers were used as a model in rats. The extracts of 250 and 500 mg/kg doses were administered to the animals and compared with both negative and positive control groups (omeprazole 20mg/kg). Macroscopic and microscopic studies showed that the high dose of lemon balm extract significantly decreased the ulcer index and improved mucosal lesions, indicating its gastroprotective efficacy. Additionally, the plant extract exhibited a significant capacity for scavenging DPPH free radicals and showed remarkable reduction in proinflammatory markers (TNF-α and IL-β1). The phytochemical analysis revealed an appreciable amount of tannin and flavonoids in the extract, suggesting that lemon balm may be a potent gastroprotection agent.

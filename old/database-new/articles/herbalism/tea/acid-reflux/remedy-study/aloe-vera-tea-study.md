
                                The study objective is to investigate whether aloe vera (A.vera) can be used as a treatment option for gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) symptoms and compare its effects with those of omeprazole and ranitidine.
                                The study uses a randomized controlled trial design where 79 subjects are allocated to different treatments such as A.vera syrup, omeprazole capsule or ranitidine tablet for 4 weeks.
                                The frequency of eight main symptoms of GERD is assessed at weeks 2 and 4 of the trial.
                                Results show that A.vera is safe and well tolerated and reduces the frequencies of all the assessed GERD symptoms with no adverse events requiring withdrawal.
                                The conclusion is that A.vera may provide a safe and effective treatment for reducing the symptoms of GERD.

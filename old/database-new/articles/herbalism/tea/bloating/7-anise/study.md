According to a study published by the Journal of Ethnopharmacology, anise, a well-recognized plant in Traditional Persian Medicine, has been traditionally used for treating bowel disorders. In a three-armed double-blind clinical trial, enteric coated capsules of anise oil, known as AnisEncap, demonstrated superior efficacy in alleviating symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) compared to a placebo or Colpermin®. The study reported a significant improvement in various IBS symptoms, including bloating, with a remarkable 75% of patients in the AnisEncap group being free from IBS symptoms. The study suggests further investigation into the main mechanism of action of anise oil in addressing gastrointestinal issues.

According to a study published by the BMC Complementary Medicine and Therapies Publication, coriander (Coriandrum sativum) has shown promising antibacterial activity against Escherichia coli, a bacterium associated with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). In the study, coriander seed oil demonstrated potent antibacterial effects, even surpassing the antibiotic rifaximin in certain assays. The observed antibacterial activity of ethanolic extracts of coriander leaves suggests a mechanistic explanation for the efficacy of coriander extracts against IBS symptoms, particularly in addressing issues such as bloating. Further clinical trials may explore coriander's potential as a natural remedy for IBS-related discomfort.

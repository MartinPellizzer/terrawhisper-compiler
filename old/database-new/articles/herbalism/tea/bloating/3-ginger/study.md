According to a study published by the European Journal of Gastroenterology & Hepatology, ginger has demonstrated positive effects on upper gastrointestinal symptoms and gastric motor function. The research, conducted on 24 healthy volunteers, revealed that ginger ingestion led to a more rapid decrease in antral area, shorter gastric half-emptying time (13.1±1.1 vs. 26.7±3.1 min), and increased frequency of antral contractions. These findings suggest that ginger accelerates gastric emptying and stimulates antral contractions, potentially offering relief for bloating and related symptoms.

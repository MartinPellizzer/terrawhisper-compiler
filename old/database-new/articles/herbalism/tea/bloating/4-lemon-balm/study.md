According to a study published by the BMC Complementary and Alternative Medicine, lemon balm (Melissa officinalis) has shown promise in alleviating bloating associated with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). The study investigated the antibacterial properties of essential oils from various culinary and medicinal herbs against Esherichia coli, a bacterium implicated in gastrointestinal disturbances. Lemon balm, along with peppermint and coriander seed oils, demonstrated significant antibacterial activity, suggesting its potential as a natural remedy for IBS-related bloating.


            The study explores the relationship between metabolic syndrome (MS) and the use of turmeric (Curcuma longa) and its bioactive component, curcumin, to treat the condition. The prevalence of MS is about 20%-25%, and it can increase the risk of heart disease, stroke, and type 2 diabetes mellitus. The study highlights several in vitro, in vivo, and human studies showing the beneficial effects of turmeric and curcumin on different components of MS. These include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can lead to antidiabetic, antihyperlipidemic, anti-obesity, and antihypertensive effects. The study suggests that turmeric and curcumin could be used as complementary therapy in MS.

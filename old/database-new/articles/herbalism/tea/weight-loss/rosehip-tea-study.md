
            A new study has found that rosehip extract shows potent anti-obesity effects in both mice and humans. The study aimed to investigate the effect of rosehip extract on human body fat in preobese subjects. Thirty-two subjects with a body mass index (BMI) between 25 and 30 participated in the 12-week, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled study. The results showed that abdominal total fat area, abdominal visceral fat area, body weight, and body mass index decreased significantly in the rosehip group compared with the placebo group after receiving the rosehip tablet intake for 12 weeks. Additionally, the abdominal subcutaneous fat area was significantly lower in the rosehip group than in the placebo group at week 12. The study concluded that rosehip extract may be a good candidate food material for preventing obesity.

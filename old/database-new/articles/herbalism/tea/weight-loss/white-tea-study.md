
White tea is a type of tea that is produced from young leaves and buds covered in small, white hairs or fuzz. It is harvested once a year in early spring. The taste of white tea is mild and sweet. White tea contains more catechins than other tea species, including epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), which has been shown to have a number of positive health effects. These effects include protection against cardiovascular diseases, cancer, diabetes mellitus, obesity, central nervous system disorders and microorganism-based illnesses. Compared to other tea types, white tea is considered to have stronger antioxidant properties and higher concentrations of EGCG. It is also known for its neuroprotective and anti-inflammatory properties. Overall, the study suggests that white tea has a number of potential health benefits and could be worth investigating further as a source of natural antioxidants and other beneficial compounds.

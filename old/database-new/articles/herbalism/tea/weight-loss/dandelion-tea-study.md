
            This study was conducted to investigate the impact of dandelion (Taraxacum officinale) on adipocyte differentiation and lipogenesis in preadipocytes (3T3-L1). The authors used HPLC analysis to identify the main phenolic compounds in the leaf and root extracts, as well as a commercial powder. Their findings showed that caffeic and chlorogenic acids were responsible for the observed effects. Through Oil Red O staining and triglyceride levels analysis, they found that the dandelion extracts decreased lipid accumulation and triglyceride levels. The study also demonstrated that the dandelion extracts did not have any toxic effect on cells, as assessed by MTT assay. Lastly, DNA microarray analysis revealed that the dandelion extracts regulated the expression of numerous genes and long non-coding RNAs involved in adipogenesis and lipid metabolism, suggesting their potential therapeutic interest in treating obesity.


            The study aimed to investigate whether green tea has any role in weight regulation through meta-analysis. Studies about weight loss (WL) and weight maintenance (WM) after green tea supplementation were identified and analyzed. Eleven articles were found to be useful for the analysis. The results showed that catechins significantly decreased body weight and maintained it after a period of WL with an effect size of -1.31 kg. However, the influence of high habitual caffeine intake and ethnicity on this effect failed to reach significance. Therefore, catechins or an EGCG-caffeine mixture may have a small positive effect on WL and WM, but these factors may influence their effectiveness.

# 15 Best Herbal Teas For Allergies

![Herbal Tea For Allergies](/images/herbal-tea-for-allergies.jpg)

Allergies are an abnormal reaction of the immune system to certain substances like food, medications, insect bites, or other environmental factors.

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), approximately 44% of people in Europe reported one form of allergy during their lifetime. Allergies can negatively affect a person’s quality of life by causing symptoms such as itching, hives, rashes, sneezing, runny nose, and difficulty breathing. Some common causes of allergies include pollen, dust mites, pet dander, mold, and certain foods like peanuts or wheat gluten.

Allergies can also increase the risk of developing other health problems such as asthma or sinus infections, particularly if not managed properly.

Herbal tea has been known to provide relief from various allergic reactions.

It is caffeine-free, making it a gentle and soothing option for those looking for an alternative to traditional tea or coffee. Some popular herbs used in allergy tea blends include chamomile, peppermint, and ginger, which have natural anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce symptoms such as itching, sneezing, and congestion. Drinking herbal tea for allergies can also help to promote relaxation and alleviate stress, which can exacerbate allergic reactions.

Overall, herbal tea is a safe and natural way to manage allergy symptoms and promote overall wellness.

In this article, you'll learn which are the best herbal teas for allergies, why they works, and how to make them.

## 1. Chamomile Tea (Matricaria Recutita)

Chamomile tea is known for its calming and soothing properties, which can help alleviate the symptoms of allergies such as sneezing, itching, and hives.

It contains flavonoids that have anti-inflammatory and antihistamine effects, which can reduce inflammation and counteract histamine release in the body. Additionally, chamomile tea has natural antiseptic and antibacterial properties that can help prevent secondary infections caused by allergies.

Furthermore, it's a gentle and safe beverage that doesn't interact with other medications, making it a popular choice for allergy sufferers looking for a natural remedy.

However, these benefits are anecdotal and more research is needed to determine the effectiveness of peppermint tea for allergy relief.

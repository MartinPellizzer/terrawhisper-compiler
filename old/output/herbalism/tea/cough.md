# 11 Best Herbal Teas For Cough

![Herbal Tea For Cough](/images/herbal-tea-for-cough.jpg)

Cough is a sudden outward expulsion of air from the lungs.

It affects millions of people worldwide every year. Chronic cough can negatively affect an individual's quality of life by causing discomfort and limiting breathing capacity. The main causes of chronic cough are respiratory diseases such as allergies, asthma, and bronchitis, infections, smoking, and pollution. Additionally, it can also be a symptom of other illnesses such as the common cold or flu.

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), chronic cough affects an estimated 30% of adults globally, with over 12 million deaths annually due to respiratory diseases.

Herbal tea is a natural remedy for soothing a cough, as it contains ingredients that help to calm the throat and reduce inflammation.

Some popular herbs used in cough teas include ginger, licorice root, slippery elm, marshmallow root, and hibiscus. These herbs work together to coat the throat, reducing irritation and helping to loosen phlegm, making it easier to cough up.

Drinking herbal tea for cough can provide quick relief and help to ease discomfort associated with a nagging cough.

In this article, you'll learn which are the best herbal teas for cough, why they works, and how to make them.

Ginger tea has been used traditionally for centuries to treat a range of respiratory illnesses, including the common cold and cough.

The primary reason ginger helps with cough is because of its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce inflammation in the airways and help relieve congestion and irritation. Additionally, ginger contains compounds that can help soothe sore throats and suppress coughing reflexes, providing immediate relief to those suffering from a persistent cough. Furthermore, ginger's spicy flavor stimulates the production of mucus-clearing fluids in the body, which helps to clear congested airways and reduce the frequency of coughing.

Finally, ginger tea may help promote relaxation and reduce stress levels, which can contribute to a better night's sleep and overall recovery from respiratory illnesses.

# 15 Best Herbal Teas For Anxiety

![Herbal Tea For Anxiety](/images/herbal-tea-for-anxiety.jpg)

Anxiety is an internal state characterized by feelings of nervousness, fear, or apprehension.

Every year, approximately 270 million people worldwide suffer from anxiety disorders. This issue can have a significant negative impact on their quality of life, causing problems with sleep, eating, relationships, and work performance. The main causes of anxiety include genetics, environmental factors, stress, trauma, and substance abuse.

Anxiety has also been linked to various other health issues such as depression, heart disease, and digestive disorders.

Herbal teas have become increasingly popular as a natural remedy for anxiety.

Chamomile is one of the most commonly used herbs for its calming properties, but lavender, lemon balm, and passionflower are also effective. These teas work by reducing inflammation in the body and promoting relaxation in the mind. They can be enjoyed hot or cold and are a healthier alternative to traditional caffeinated drinks.

Drinking herbal tea for anxiety can provide relief from symptoms without causing any negative side effects.

In this article, you'll learn which are the best herbal teas for anxiety, why they works, and how to make them.

## 1. Chamomile Tea (Matricaria Recutita)

Chamomile has been used for centuries as an herbal remedy to calm nerves and reduce anxiety.

It contains flavonoids, which are antioxidants that help relax muscles and soothe the body. When consumed as tea, chamomile's calming effects can help reduce stress levels and promote relaxation. Chamomile tea is a natural, safe way to relieve anxiety without causing side effects or dependency.

It's an easy addition to your daily routine and can be enjoyed at any time of day.

## 2. Lavender Tea (Lavandula Angustifolia)

Lavender tea has been used traditionally to treat anxiety for centuries due to its calming and relaxing properties.

The plant contains several chemical compounds that have been shown to promote feelings of relaxation, including linalool and lavandulol. These compounds work by interacting with certain receptors in the brain, particularly GABA receptors, which help regulate mood and reduce anxiety levels. When consumed as a tea, lavender can provide a soothing effect on the nervous system, helping to alleviate feelings of stress and anxiety. Additionally, the act of drinking tea itself can be a calming ritual, adding to its overall relaxing effects.

While more research is needed to fully understand the mechanisms behind lavender tea's effectiveness in treating anxiety, it remains a popular natural remedy for those seeking an alternative to traditional treatments.

## 5. Valerian Tea (Valeriana Officinalis)

Valerian root has been used for centuries to treat anxiety and sleep disorders due to its sedative effects.

It contains compounds such as gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), which acts on the brain to reduce excitability and promote relaxation. Studies have shown that valerian tea can significantly reduce symptoms of anxiety, especially when consumed before bedtime. However, it's important to note that while valerian may help with mild to moderate anxiety, it may not be effective for severe cases or those experiencing other mental health conditions.

As with any herbal remedy, it's recommended to consult a healthcare professional before using valerian tea for anxiety relief.

The etymology of "Lemon Balm" originates from two separate words - 'lemon', which refers to citrus smell and flavor due to its use in making a lemon-scented tea; while, on another hand,'balm'(derived from the Latin word ‘balsamum’) represents soothing or healing properties associated with this plant. The name Lemon Balm symbolically suggests both therapeutic qualities as well as pleasing scent and taste of its leaves when brewed into a tea drink which makes it stand out among other herbs used for medicinal purposes in ancient times

As per the cultural significance, lemon balms have played crucial roles across various civilizations. From European royal courts to Indian temples where these were regarded sacred or worshipped goddesses connected with spiritual wellbeing and fertility due their believed potentiality as a cure-all herb that can treat numerous illness like asthma, bronchitis coughs colds etc., lemon balm has always been appreciated. Its strong links to Christianity also led it being associated with Virgin Mary which increased its mystic reverence within churches for ages together

The mythical aspects of Lemon Balms have existed since ancient times in various cultures and religions too that depict their role as magical plants, divine blessings or emblems. In Greek Mythology Melissa was a nymph who had been turned into bee after consuming the herb by Hera to cure madness which represented lemony-scented honeyed flavor while in Celtic legends melding their power of transformation and rebirth they linked Lemon balms as symbol for eternal life.

The history or folklore surrounding lemon Balm has always held significance through various forms such as its inclusion within classic texts, which reveal instances where it was referenced by famous figures including writers like Shakespeare in his play "A Midsummer Night's Dream" and William Wordsworth who also incorporated the herb into several poems.

Lastly but not least important are folkloristic uses of Lemon Balm that range from medicinal to magical properties associated with this plant which have been documented throughout history by various cultures across different continents including Europe, Asia & Africa where people employed it as an insect repellent or pest control measure while also using its leaves for tea-brewing purposes due their leonious citrusy smell known mainly amongst natives of Mediterranean region since antiquity.

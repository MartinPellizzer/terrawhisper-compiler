 Dong quai contains essential oils, including coumarins, phytoestrogens, and ferulic acid derivatives, which are responsible for the various therapeutic effects. These constituents work together to provide its extensive benefits to the body.

 The most commonly used parts of dong quai include its roots, stems, and leaves. Its preparations come in different forms such as tea, tinctures, capsules, or powdered extracts for easier consumption. These help the absorption and effectiveness of its constituents.

 Possible side effects of consuming dong quai may include stomach discomfort, skin rashes, and allergic reactions in some individuals. It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you have any underlying health issues or take prescription medication.

 To ensure safety while using dong quai, follow the recommended dosage provided by your healthcare provider or refer to packaging guidelines when buying supplements. Be cautious during pregnancy as consuming high doses may cause premature labor or miscarriage. Additionally, avoid using it with blood-thinning medications or herbs like ginger and garlic, as they can interact negatively.

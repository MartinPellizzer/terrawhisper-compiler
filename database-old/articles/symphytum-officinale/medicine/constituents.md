These compounds work together to provide relief from different medical issues like inflammation, pain, and skin conditions. Among these key ingredients are allantoin, mucilage, rosmarinic acid, amino acids, fixed oils, sesquiterpene lactones, tannins, aconitine, proteins, vitamins, and others that work in synergy to create healing benefits for individuals.

 This compound is known for its ability to heal skin issues such as burns and wounds by promoting cell growth and reducing inflammation. It is also a natural moisturizer and has been used in cosmetics due to its soothing properties.

 Comprising gum-like substances, mucilage helps retain moisture and promotes the healing of wounds by keeping them hydrated. These can soothe irritation and reduce inflammation, making it a valuable component in treating skin conditions like eczema or rashes.

 A powerful antioxidant compound known for its anti-inflammatory properties, rosmarinic acid has been found useful against many health issues such as respiratory issues (allergies), arthritis pain, and viral infections. It also helps protect cells from damage.

 Comfrey contains various essential amino acids that play a crucial role in cell growth, tissue repair, and overall bodily function by making new proteins. These include arginine, lysine, glycine, and glutamine, among others.

 Comfrey is rich in fixed oils such as linoleic acid, which has anti-inflammatory properties. These fats help reduce inflammation and may alleviate swelling and pain associated with arthritis or other conditions. 

 Some of these compounds found in comfrey are believed to have anti-cancer effects. The specific sesquiterpene lactone present in Comfrey is called echinacoside, which helps slow tumor growth and has been studied for its potential use in cancer treatment.

 These compounds can act as natural antioxidants, reducing the effects of free radicals that cause damage at cellular levels. They also have astringent properties, helping to protect the body from bacterial or viral infections and reduce inflammation in areas like the digestive tract.

 Comfrey contains other important substances such as aconitine (a natural painkiller), proteins for tissue repair, and vitamins like A, C, and E that help maintain overall health and well-being.

Feverfew has many medicinal preparations, such as tea, tincture, ointment, essential oil, powder, capsules, tablets, fresh leaves, infusion and juice. Each of these forms serves a different purpose when treating various health issues related to feverfew's healing properties.

 As one of the most common ways to consume feverfew, tea is made by steeping its fresh or dried leaves in hot water. This allows the compounds like terpenoids and parthenolide present in feverfew to infuse into the liquid, providing relief from headaches, menstrual pain, inflammation, joint and muscular aches, and even improve arthritis symptoms.

 A tincture is an alcohol-based medicine extract which captures the medicinal properties of feverfew more potently than tea does. This form is often used to help ease migraines, menstrual cramps, and reduce inflammation throughout the body. However, it should be noted that some people may experience allergic reactions or gastrointestinal discomfort from consuming feverfew tincture.

 Creams and ointments made with feverfew are used topically to treat pain associated with joint and muscular aches, arthritis, or inflammation in the skin. The application helps the parthenolide contained within feverfew to penetrate deeper into the affected area, easing symptoms more effectively.

 This highly concentrated version of the medicinal properties in feverfew is used for aromatherapy purposes and applied as a topical remedy. The oil can be helpful when massaged over stiff muscles or joints to reduce inflammation, pain, and promote relaxation.

 Feverfew's dried leaves can be pulverized into a fine powder that can be mixed with water or other liquids such as juice or tea. This form is used to help ease headaches, menstrual cramps, feverish symptoms, and even promote better sleep when taken regularly before bedtime.

 These are convenient ways of consuming a daily dose of active substances from the dried leaves of feverfew without having to prepare other forms. They can be utilized in treating various conditions such as headaches, menstrual discomfort, arthritis pain, or even for prevention purposes.

 The leaves can also be used in a direct and unprocessed form by consuming them raw either separately or as part of a salad, for instance. This method is believed to bring benefits such as relief from headache or menstrual discomfort.

 These forms are similar in concept, as both involve extracting the beneficial properties from feverfew leaves by steeping them into liquids like water. The resulting infusions and juices can be consumed for pain relief and easing inflammation throughout the body.

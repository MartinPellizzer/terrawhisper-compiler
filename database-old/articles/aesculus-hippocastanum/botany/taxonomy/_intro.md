Aesculus hippocastanum, the horse chestnut, is classified in the Plantae kingdom as an Angiosperm within the Eudicots class. It belongs to the Sapindales order and the Sapindaceae family, with the genus Aesculus and species Aesculus hippocastanum. 

Common names like "Horse Chestnut," "Buckeye," and "European Horse Chestnut" reflect its features and regional distinctions. 

Various varieties, such as 'Baumannii,' 'Briotii,' and 'Conspicua,' offer unique characteristics. 'Laciniata' has cut leaves, 'Pendula' features weeping branches, 'Rubra' offers colorful flowers, and 'Stately' is tall. 'Sunset' showcases vibrant autumn colors, 'Umbraculifera' forms an umbrella-like canopy, and 'Variegata' has variegated leaves for visual interest.

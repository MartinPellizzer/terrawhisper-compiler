 Ginseng (Panax ginseng) has many health benefits such as improving mental alertness for those suffering from memory issues or age-related cognitive decline; supporting immune system function to fight off infections and help manage autoimmune diseases like rheumatoid arthritis, lupus or Crohn's disease by decreasing inflammation throughout the body. Furthermore it has shown positive effects on metabolic syndrome (diabetics) where one’s blood sugar tends to spike abnormally post food ingestion and may lead people susceptible for heart diseases due to high levels of fats in their system, often found by managing unhealthy cholesterol. Finally its stress alleviating properties can help with mood disorders like depression or anxiety thereby making a considerable improvement not just health wise but also quality-of life related factors which ultimately have impacted productivity and wellbeing too..

 Most parts utilized from this plant include the root (considered a key component in treatments) dried berries, leaves & stem depending upon use; each prepared according based on their respective medicinal purpose they are intended for such as powders extracted either alcoholic or hot-water decoctions.

 With numerous benefits come some potential side effects which might arise while taking it under various conditions like allergies associated to any component present within these traditional formulations; gastrointestinal issues including nausea, vomiting diarrhea due excessive intake or individual's specific susceptibility may exist too. Additionally one must be cautious about interactions with other drugs since some components contained have a direct effect on how certain medications interact which can either decrease efficiency making them less effective when needed most; conversely there are reports that suggest ginseng might reduce effects from anesthesia and increase risk of bleeding during surgery due its anti-clotting properties.

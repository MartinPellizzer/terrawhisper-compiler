Skullcap has many medicinal preparations, such as tea, tincture, infusion, decoction, powder, extract, poultice, cream, ointment, suppository, etc. All of these forms are used for different purposes and have their own specific ways to be prepared and utilized in healthcare applications. In general, they all aim at providing relief from various ailments by harnessing the inherent medicinal properties of Skullcap (Scutellaria lateriflora), a popular herbal plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family. 

 Tea is a basic way of preparing Skullcap for consumption, involving boiling the dried or fresh plant material in water and then steeping it until ready. This method allows for a more natural release of its active compounds into the tea infusion; you consume these healing substances by drinking the brewed tea like any other beverage. 

 Tincture refers to Skullcap extracts prepared with alcohol as the solvent, rather than water. The aim here is to obtain a highly concentrated mixture of its medicinal components, which can then be diluted or combined with other liquids to produce doses for oral consumption or external application. 

 Infusion, on the other hand, uses only boiled water as the solvent and involves soaking Skullcap plant material in this water over a period of time before filtering it out. This method is similar to tea preparation but without the addition of heat that may cause certain components to evaporate or degrade. 

 Decoction, another popular form of extraction, utilizes boiling water to break down plant tissues and extract its constituents. It's often associated with tougher plant materials like roots, stems, or seeds, which require a more robust process to render the desirable medicinal properties. 

 Powder is created by grinding dried Skullcap leaves, stems, flowers, or other parts of the plant into a fine or granular form. These preparations can be used as ingredients in teas or capsules or can even be applied topically as a paste for skin conditions. 

 Extracts are concentrated forms of medicinal compounds extracted from Skullcap using solvents, such as alcohol or glycerin. They can be taken orally or used topically, depending on their specific application. 

 Finally, poultices involve creating a mixture or paste by combining plant material (ground) and applying it to the skin for localized relief from inflammation, pain, or other conditions. Creams are also useful remedies for skin issues, consisting of various oil-based substances blended with water-soluble components like glycerin. 

 Ointments are similar to creams but have higher concentration owing to lesser quantities of water and more fats or waxes, making them suitable for deeper penetration into the skin tissues. Lastly, suppositories are solidified formulations meant to be inserted into a body cavity for localized medication effects.

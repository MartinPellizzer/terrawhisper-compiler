 Officinale with its numerous common names like lion's tooth or wild endive that reflects some of the plant parts resembling those shapes in different forms across habitats it lives.

Tarxacum officinalis morphology includes bright yellow flowers which are actually ray and disk-type flowers, a round ball consisting entirely petals on outer side with center full folliated discs for pollination purpose that also acts as source of food supply to many insect species including bees and butterflies.

Variants like 'Taraxacum canadense' have hairy leaves while their smooth counterpart resembles the original dandelion but not enough similarities in other features which sets them apart from each other making it difficult for a non-botanist to identify accurately without close inspection.

Distributed across temperate areas worldwide, mainly northern and central parts of Europe as well as North America's prairies; this species thrives best under diverse environments varying soils pH ranging between mild acidic or neutral where dandelions can be found growing in lawn grass to woodlands.

The life cycle involves flowering from spring until late summer with seeds dispersed through wind once ripened, allowing them spread widely even long distances after landing on new locations suitable for germination and growth which further aids propagation throughout their distribution zones expanding into unoccupied territories creating what could be term as 'invasive' behavior in some cases.

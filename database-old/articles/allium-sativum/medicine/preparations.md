Garlic extract is a concentrated form of garlic made by soaking crushed cloves in a liquid solvent. The active components get dissolved into the liquid, making them easy to consume and absorb in smaller amounts than raw garlic. Garlic extract is frequently used as an ingredient in supplements and is useful for treating various health issues.

Oil macerate is made by soaking peeled garlic cloves in oil for a few days. This process allows the beneficial oils and nutrients of the garlic to infuse into the fat. The resulting product has an increased potency for use as a topical ointment or oil to be taken internally for treating inflammation, fungal infections, and other conditions.

Raw garlic is the most basic form, obtained by simply cutting off the outer layers of skin on each clove. It can be eaten raw, added to cooked dishes, or even juiced and ingested. Consuming raw garlic is believed to offer various health benefits, such as boosting immunity and fighting bacteria.

Allicin is a potent sulfur-containing compound found in freshly cut garlic that gives it its distinctive smell and taste. When fresh, crushed or chopped garlic releases this volatile compound, which provides many of the medicinal properties associated with garlic. Aged garlic can be more stable than fresh varieties because some of the allicin breaks down over time, reducing its pungency while maintaining beneficial health effects.

Sliced garlic is a straightforward form that involves cutting the cloves into thin slices, which can then be used for various culinary purposes or added to dishes for flavor and health benefits. This method preserves much of garlic's nutritional content while allowing for easy incorporation into meals.

Aged garlic has been fermented over time, mellowing its taste and decreasing the pungency often associated with fresh raw garlic. It is used in supplements, food products, and as a culinary ingredient to bring rich flavor without overwhelming spiciness.

Powdered garlic can be made by grinding dried or dehydrated garlic cloves into a fine powder. This preparation can be easily incorporated into various recipes and supplements, making it a versatile way to add the health benefits of garlic to your diet.

Garlic capsules are popular due to their convenience and dose accuracy, as they contain pre-measured amounts of powdered garlic extract or dried garlic cloves. These easy-to-take supplements offer the medicinal value of garlic in a portable form.

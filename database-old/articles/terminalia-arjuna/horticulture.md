 Arjun is an evergreen tree species which requires tropical or sub-tropical climates with temperatures ranging from around 20°C to even beyond thirty degrees Celsius during summer, as it grows well in hot and humid conditions; a soil that's rich but not waterlogged (well drained) is needed. This tree also appreciably demands partial shade for better growth results along its life cycle of about ten years or more before reaching maturity height ranging from 10 to upwards forty meters, with wide canopies measuring nearly equal width and diameters as the heights themselves depending upon different circumstances.

 To plant Arjun seeds, choose a location that offers well-drained soil while taking note of its sun exposure requirements for adequate photosynthesis; prepare an area by removing weeds or any other competing vegetation surrounding your desired spot where the seed should be planted – spacing them at least one meter apart depending upon expected maturation levels. Plant seeds approximately 1 cm deep, ensuring they're placed straight with a light push-down to keep contact between soil particles and young developing root system which is essential for initial germination processes leading toward further successful growth in future phases within an optimal atmosphere consisting mainly of humidity alongside proper ventilation without excessively windy conditions.

 Arjun can produce fruits called 'Pitambari' after it matures; they look similar to olive fruit in appearance and taste almost equally pleasing but their primary use is medicinal rather than consumptive. The bark of this tree holds particular pharmacological value as well due primarily because its extracts have various properties attributed by traditional medicine practitioners, so collecting the required parts during proper harvest periods should be done meticulously without causing any harm to ongoing reproductivity or adversely impacting growth prospects over time while maintaining sustainable yield.

 Arjun trees are generally resistant but some challenges from bugs like scale insects may affect it sometimes - controlling them through timely treatments such as biological pest control measures, natural predators etc., can be employed; certain fungal infections too might occur though usually at a lesser extent – taking care of proper sanitation around the tree base helps minimize risks here significantly by keeping any pathogen-prone conditions from flourishing near your precious plant's roots.

Artemisia absinthium fruits, referred to as achenes, are small and dry, each containing a single seed. They are sessile, meaning they have no stalk and are directly attached to the plant's stem.

In terms of arrangement, these achenes cluster on panicles, primarily found throughout the upper stem of the Artemisia absinthium plant. Their shape is oblong-linear, with a length ranging from 2.5 to 3 millimeters (0.098 to 0.118 inches), a width of approximately 0.2 millimeters (0.0079 inches), and a thickness of about 0.3 millimeters (0.0118 inches).

The color of these achenes is uniformly brown, and they have a smooth texture. On the surface, they display five prominent ridges, giving them a ribbed appearance. There are no distinct markings on these fruits, contributing to their overall simple and unadorned visual characteristics.

One remarkable feature of these wormwood fruits is their dehiscence, or lack thereof. Unlike some other plants, Artemisia absinthium achenes are indehiscent, meaning they do not split open at maturity. Instead, they remain closed, encapsulating the single seed within.

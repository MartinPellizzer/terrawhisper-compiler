Corn has many medicinal preparations, such as  Tea, Oil, Ointment, Cream, Tincture, Fluid extract, Poultice, Salve, Infusion and Powder.

The nutrients from corn can aid digestion, improve heart health, and even help in lowering blood pressure when taken regularly.

 Corn oil is extracted from the germ of the corn kernel. It has a high content of unsaturated fatty acids like linoleic acid which is beneficial for the heart as well as the skin. This oil can be used topically or internally and acts as an emollient for healing, moisturizing the body, and reducing inflammation.

 An ointment made from corn usually combines the soothing properties of corn oil with other ingredients like beeswax, shea butter, and essential oils to create a rich, nourishing emollient that can treat various skin conditions such as eczema, psoriasis or fungal infections.

 Corn cream is similar to ointments but has a lighter consistency. It typically contains corn oil and other natural ingredients such as herbs, vitamins, or minerals that can penetrate the skin's surface more easily, thus providing relief from dryness, itching, and irritation.

 A tincture involves soaking dried corn kernels in an alcohol solution for a certain period to extract their beneficial components like nutrients or phytochemicals. The resulting liquid can be diluted and ingested to treat digestive issues, promote heart health, or help alleviate pain.

 A fluid extract is another method of extraction where the corn kernels are soaked in a water-alcohol solution. This preparation has a higher concentration of active ingredients than tinctures and can be used topically on wounds to promote healing or internally for treating various health issues such as skin conditions, digestive problems, or blood sugar control.

 A poultice is a mixture of corn kernels, water, and other herbs that are heated until it forms a thick paste. This paste can be applied directly to the affected area like sore muscles, arthritis, or bruises for pain relief and faster healing.

In summary, these seven medicinal preparations from corn are employed in various ways to treat different health conditions. Each of them provides unique therapeutic benefits to their users due to the diverse properties found within the corn kernel itself.

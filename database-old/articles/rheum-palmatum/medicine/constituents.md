Chinese Rhubarb has many medicinal constituents, such as Rhein, Emodin, Chrysophanol, Physcion, Sennosides, Aloe-emodin, Rhamnose, Methyl rhynchophylline, Isorhynchophylline, Rheum-anthraquinone. To begin, each one is unique and is attributed to various therapeutic properties. These compounds work together in synergy to promote health and wellbeing when incorporated into different traditional Chinese medicine practices. 

Firstly, Rhein (or Rhubarb anthraquinone) is a primary active ingredient that has demonstrated remarkable antimicrobial activity, thus making it useful for treating bacterial and fungal infections. Additionally, it can be used to soothe the gastrointestinal tract by stimulating peristalsis and facilitating regular bowel movements. 

Emodin is another significant compound with various applications, including anti-inflammatory effects and its use for treating digestive disorders. It has also shown promising results in cancer prevention due to its inhibition of cellular growth and promotion of apoptosis (the destruction or death of cells). 

Chrysophanol is a type of anthraquinone glycoside that exhibits anti-inflammatory, antioxidative and anti-cancer properties. It has been used to treat various conditions such as ulcers, skin disorders, and liver diseases because it can help regulate the immune system's response effectively.

Physcion is a compound with analgesic effects, which means it can be useful for relieving pain associated with inflammation or other painful conditions. It also possesses antimicrobial properties and can protect against certain bacterial infections, while additionally demonstrating potential benefits in the treatment of liver disorders.

Sennosides are known to have laxative effects due to their ability to stimulate the large intestine's smooth muscles. This property makes them helpful for addressing constipation, improving bowel regularity and overall gastrointestinal health. 

Additional compounds such as Aloe-emodin, Rhamnose, Methyl rhynchophylline, Isorhynchophylline, Rheum-anthraquinone all contribute to the therapeutic benefits of Chinese Rhubarb and can be found in varying proportions, enhancing the effectiveness of traditional medicinal practices. 

All together, these various constituents work cohesively to provide a wide range of beneficial health effects when incorporated into Chinese medicine preparations for healing and wellness promotion.

Chinese Rhubarb (Rheum palmatumn) belongs to the domain Eukarya which represents a collection of organisms characterized by membrane enclosed cells and complex cellular organization containing genes packaged into chromosomes within nuclei, distinct from bacteria or Archaeabacteria. Within its botanical structure Rheum is categorized in 'Polygonaceae' family including five primary genera with different morphology traits; the subgroup under this genus includes Chinese Rhubarb species specifically referred to as Rheuma palmatumn which has some additional common names like "Lady rhubarbs" and also known for its medicinal properties.

Morphologically speaking, one can observe that it is a herbaceous perennial plant with thick petioles (stalks), large leaves arranged in the shape of an umbrella - hence 'palmatum' meaning hand-shaped due to appearance somewhat like palm leaf arrangement; these plants grow upwards from rhizomes and possess stalked flowering stems.

The variant Rhubarb species differ slightly depending on their cultivation location which also affects its morphology, e.g., the Hokow rhubarbs or 'Rheum palmatumn var hortense' originated in China but has different leaf arrangement compared to standard Chinese varieties; conversely Thick-leaved (Larger) Rhubarb cultivars are found with large broad leaves as opposed their smaller counterparts.

Geographically it ranges extensively covering wide regions from Eastern Siberia, Mongolia and Northern parts of China including the Qinghai region in Central Asia extending southwards towards Nepal through Bhutan while on Southern edges there has been limited reports of them spotted up till Kashmir Valley at India's border. Its natural habitats predominantly include forests as well damp fields providing moisture rich environment for its growth requirements which is key aspect driving their distribution patterns in specific geographic regions worldwide mainly those offering similar climatic conditions resembling the temperate belt climate where it thrives best.

The life cycle of Chinese Rhubarb follows a typical perennial plant pattern with an annual succession wherein over time they produce new growth from previous years' rhizomes leading to matured leaves and flowers that eventually turn into seeds, ensuring its continuous propagation across generations under optimal environmental conditions where it can flourish undeterred.

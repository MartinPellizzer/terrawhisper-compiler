Nettle can have some side effects if used improperly, such as irritating the skin and causing rashes on it. The plant has tiny hairs embedded onto it 

 which contain chemical irritants that are released when touched or brushed against the body. This 

 contact results in uncomfortable and painful sensations due to these chemicals interacting with our 

 skin, resulting in a burning sensation for many people. Nettles can also cause rashes by triggering an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to inflamed red patches on their skin. Additionally, the plant's hairs can potentially enter open wounds or cuts, causing further irritation and increasing the risk of infection. 

 

 Besides affecting the physical well-being of individuals, nettles possess potency that could affect a person's respiratory system. They contain tiny silica particles which can be inhaled during cooking or handling and get deposited in the throat region causing irritation to the throat lining. This inflammation may lead to swelling in the mucous membranes of the respiratory tract, leading to problems such as difficulty breathing, coughing, sneezing, and congestion.

 Apart from skin and respiratory issues, nettle’s presence near the eyes can also cause discomfort due to their hairs releasing chemicals when touched or brushed against the eyes, causing a stinging sensation. This irritation may lead to redness in the eye area, tearing, or even inflammation of the conjunctiva, which is the thin layer covering the inside surface of the eyelids and the white part of the eyeball.

 Furthermore, nettles are known to aggravate symptoms associated with asthma. Inhaling its particles can trigger an allergic reaction in some individuals who suffer from this condition, causing difficulty in breathing or even a full-blown asthma attack.

 

 Nettle also has the potential to cause migraines in some people due to its ability to stimulate the uterus during pregnancy, particularly if it comes into contact with a woman’s skin who is pregnant. This can trigger premature contractions, leading to complications such as preterm labor or even miscarriage. Therefore, caution should be exercised when handling nettles in proximity to anyone who might be at risk of these side effects.

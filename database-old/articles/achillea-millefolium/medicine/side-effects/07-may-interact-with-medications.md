
Yarrow may interact with medications, indicating that its consumption can influence the efficacy or side effects of certain drugs. Yarrow contains various constituents, notably flavonoids (such as apigenin and luteolin), sesquiterpene lactones, alkaloids, and volatile oils like camphor and borneol. These compounds possess pharmacological properties that might interfere with the metabolism or action of medications, potentially leading to altered drug levels in the body.

This interaction can aggravate health conditions such as blood clotting disorders, high blood pressure, and gastrointestinal issues. Individuals particularly sensitive to these interactions are those on blood-thinning medications like Warfarin, anti-hypertensive drugs, and certain antibiotics. For instance, the use of Yarrow alongside anticoagulants may increase the risk of bleeding due to its potential effects on blood clotting factors.

To mitigate the risk of interactions, it's crucial to use Yarrow cautiously and consult a healthcare professional before its use, especially when on medications. Proper preparation methods and dosages are essential. The typical recommended dosage for Yarrow tea is around 1 to 2 teaspoons (2-4 grams) of dried herb steeped in hot water for 5-10 minutes, consumed up to three times per day. However, these dosages should be adjusted based on individual health conditions and should be closely monitored when used concomitantly with medications to avoid potential adverse effects or reduced drug efficacy.

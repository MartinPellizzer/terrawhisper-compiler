Ginkgo has many medicinal constituents, such as flavonoids and terpenoids that give it its curative powers. These

 components are found in the leaves, seeds, and bark of the tree; a

 notable example is the antioxidants.

 Flavonoids are part of a group of phytochemicals or plant chemicals

 known for their potent antioxidant properties that protect against

 damage caused by reactive oxygen species or ROS (free radicals) and

 other toxins in the body. They also help in the management of inflammation,

 which may cause or contribute to many health problems including arthritis,

 asthma, heart diseases and other conditions. Additionally, flavonoids can

 improve cognitive function by promoting better blood flow to the brain, a

 process that is essential for proper memory, concentration and mental

 clarity as one ages. This is why Ginkgo is often used to treat dementia

 related to Alzheimer's disease. 

 Terpenoids are another type of compounds found in medicinal plants and

 herbs like Ginkgo. They have a wide range of uses and health benefits,

 including their ability to fight inflammation, protect against oxidative stress

 caused by free radicals, and regulate the immune system. In the case of

 Ginkgo, terpenoids work with other plant constituents like flavonoids to

 enhance blood circulation in the body, particularly in the brain. This helps

 improve cognitive function and relieve symptoms related to Alzheimer's

 disease, dementia, and stroke. Moreover, they can also support heart health

 by reducing the risk of atherosclerosis (hardening of arteries) and lowering

 blood pressure. 

 Furthermore, Ginkgo contains essential fatty acids that are vital for

 maintaining good health. Fatty acids like omega-3 and omega-6 are necessary

 to keep cell membranes strong, support healthy nerve communication in the

 brain, maintain proper immune system function, promote normal growth and

 development, and ensure optimal cardiovascular health. These nutrients also

 work synergistically with flavonoids and terpenoids found in Ginkgo for enhanced

 overall wellbeing. 

 In conclusion, the medicinal constituents of Ginkgo include a combination

 of diverse phytochemicals like flavonoids, terpenoids, essential fatty acids,

 and other compounds that have a myriad of health benefits. These natural

 components not only contribute to better cognitive function, but also help in

 maintaining general body health by combating inflammation, improving blood

 circulation, supporting cardiovascular health, and enhancing immunity. With this

 powerful plant at our disposal, it is easy to appreciate why Ginkgo has been

 used for centuries as a natural remedy for various ailments.

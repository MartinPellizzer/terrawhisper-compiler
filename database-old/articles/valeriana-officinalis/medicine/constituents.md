Valerian has many medicinal constituents, such as Valerenic acid, Iridoids, Valtrate, Volatile oils, Hydrocarbons, Terpenes, Diterpenes, Ketones, Sesquiterpenes, and Alkaloids. These components work together to provide various health benefits by directly interacting with the body's systems.

Valerenic acid is an essential compound found in valerian that provides several properties contributing to its therapeutic uses. It possesses antioxidant activities that help protect cells from damage caused by free radicals and has anti-inflammatory qualities useful for treating inflammation in conditions such as arthritis.

Iridoids are another group of compounds found in valerian. These naturally occurring chemical compounds exhibit antimicrobial properties, which aids the plant's defense mechanism against pathogens and also potentially provide benefits for humans, protecting against infections and certain bacterial or fungal diseases.

Valtrate is a compound formed from the combination of L-valine and L-tryptophan amino acids that serve as precursors to other important biomolecules like neurotransmitters (like serotonin, which has significant roles in mood regulation). This may contribute to valerian's effect on alleviating stress, anxiety, and promoting sleep.

Volatile oils are responsible for the distinct scent of valerian root and its effects are also attributed to their calming properties. These oils have sedative actions on the nervous system, helping reduce tension, nervousness, and insomnia while also enhancing relaxation. This aids in stress relief and promotes better sleep quality.

Hydrocarbons, Terpenes, Diterpenes, Ketones, Sesquiterpenes, and Alkaloids are groups of diverse phytochemicals that provide valerian with its wide range of medicinal properties. These compounds interact synergistically within the plant's system to regulate various physiological processes and facilitate desired actions such as antioxidant activity, anti-inflammatory effects, or promoting sleep quality.

In summary, valerian contains numerous active components with diverse functions that contribute to its medicinal benefits. These constituents work together to provide a comprehensive range of therapeutic properties, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, sedative, and mood-regulating effects.

The horticultural aspects of Devils Claw (Harpagophytum procumbens) concern various factors related to growth requirements, planting tips, caring and maintaining them, harvesting techniques, and possible pests and diseases that might affect the plant.

To ensure proper growth, this perennial succulent thrives in well-drained, slightly alkaline soil with a pH of 6.5 to 8.0. It requires full sun exposure and tolerates drought conditions. The ideal temperature range should fall within 13°C to 27°C (55°F to 80°F).

Devils Claw plants should be planted in spring, ideally starting a few weeks after the last frost date. To do so, prepare the soil by incorporating compost and digging a hole twice as wide as the root ball. Plant the seedling at the same depth it was previously growing, then cover it with the surrounding soil and firm gently. Water thoroughly to settle the soil around the roots.

To keep your Devils Claw healthy, apply slow-release fertilizer once every three months or when the plant starts blooming during springtime. Prune the plant regularly to maintain its shape and promote better air circulation. Ensure proper spacing between plants as they tend to spread over time.

Harvesting of the seed pods can occur when they are dry, brown, and ready to fall off the plant. Carefully pick them with a pair of scissors or snips for optimal results. The seeds should be collected from mature pods that have turned black and have started to open.

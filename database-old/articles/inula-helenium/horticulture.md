 Thrive best under full sun conditions with a well-drained, neutral soil (pH ranging between 6 and 7) that is rich enough to provide essential nutrients such as nitrogen for optimum development of Elecampane plants. Keep it regularly watered during the growing season but be careful not to overwater which may cause root rot or diseases; planting in moist areas could result in less effective roots growth too. To keep temperatures optimal, consider providing some shade if planted outdoors when ambient heat gets extremely high and avoid excessive fertilizing - only apply necessary amount once a year as an excess of nitrogen can lead to tall thin leaves on this species rather than lush foliage with good root mass like it should have.

 Begin harvest of flowers mid-summer up until first frost arrives; cut them off just below where petals emerge onto stem without damaging buds or stems underneath since it might hinder regrowth the next season too - aim for approximately three harvests per year maximum, keeping an eye on plant health throughout this process so as not to compromise future blooming seasons by removing more than necessary.

 Some pests that may threaten Inula helennium plants include aphids (cause curling leaves), leaf miners causing brown spots or yellow patches, slugs feeding on newly developed shoots leading them to break prematurely from the ground; as for disease concerns there’re few reports about rot related infections – but maintaining proper spacing between each plant should minimize potential risk factors associated with damp environments often required during certain growing stages.

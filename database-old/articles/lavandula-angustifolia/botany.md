Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known by its common names of English lavender and French lavenders is a species native in North-Mediterranean regions including countries like France Italy Greece as well as Middle East locations such Lebanon Turkey Israel among others where they flourish naturally under Mediterranean climate conditions. They usually thrive on loam rich soil having good drainage characteristics; therefore it’s not uncommon to find these fragrant plants growing wildly over large parts of its natural habitat areas which often comprise rocky slopes or other less ideal settings if suitable substrate and weather are available for their growth requirements.

The most widely cultivated type is English Lavender, characterized by its bush shape and aromatic scent due to higher essential oil concentration that can be extracted from it for use in culinary herbs perfumes cosmetics among other industries reliant on these distinct attributes present only within this particular plant variety offering versatile uses beyond just decorative purposes.

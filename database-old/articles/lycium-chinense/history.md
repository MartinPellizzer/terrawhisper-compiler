 'gǒu-jī,' meaning grass pea berries and related to a traditional folk plant known as wolfberry, is further supplemented with the root 'goji'. This term has its origins in ancient China. As you delve deeper into history, Goji Berry's significance becomes increasingly complex not only because it contains diverse cultural meanings but also due to several mythical stories interwoven within them over time.

The Chinese have used these small fruits and medicinally valued berries for ages - often attributing significant relevance or importance in their culture, history of trade as well as culinary practices that are unique unto themselves! Being recognized all around the world today due mainly because ancient China was known to possess many hidden wisdoms; people would come from far-away places simply seeking knowledge about these magical berries. Incorporated within everyday meals and used for medicinal purposes, Goji Berry has found a permanent place in Chinese cuisine as well health care systems over the years making it one of their most prized offerings throughout history up until now!

Legendary tales have woven into this plant's story; like that which tells how Gaius Julius Caesar once ate these berries during his campaign in China, crediting him for improving health and strength. Another widely told one relates back to an age-old belief among the Mongolians - where it was thought consuming Goji Berry could prolong life by stopping old men's hair from turning grey; so prized were these berries they have transcended borders across generations through oral history making their way into various cultures.

Numerous Chinese texts dating back to as early 206 BCE can be found referencing the fruit within classical literature, with mentions of its medicinal properties and uses in traditional medicine systems such as Sanghe Liu (a formulation containing Goji Berry). The revered treatise Zhou Hou Bei Ji Fang from around AD347 also records detailed instructions on how to use this unique plant for various ailments, thus indicating that the usage of these berries dates back many centuries before now!

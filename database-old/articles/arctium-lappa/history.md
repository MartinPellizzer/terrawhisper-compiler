The origin of 'burdock', a plant scientifically known as Arctium lappa and part belonging to Asteraceae family is not clear or traceable by scholars with certainty but it has been in use since ancient times when this medicinal herb was widely cultivated across different continents like Asia, Europe & North America. 

The term 'burdock' could be a combination of two words - Brouge Doche which might have stemmed from the Old English name 'bourdeuc'. However some sources suggest that it may also come down to old British roots word Burgh and Doch meaning fortress garden implying an area surrounded by plants like this herb. This fascinating history illustrates not only how different communities with diverse languages evolved over time but simultaneously underlines the plant's significance in their lives as medicine or remedy for various ailments including skin issues, joint pains amongst many other health benefits it offered to people across ages and culture giving strength through nature itself - much like an ancient healer of sorts.

When taking about its cultural significances (paragraph 2), burdock has been associated with different symbols ranging from protection against evil spirits or bad omens due in part perhaps by superstitious beliefs that it was used for cleansing rituals, to being a signifier representative status-symbols within various communities. In some cultures like the Japanese one where this plant is known as Gobo root – burdock has served almost both dietary purposes & medical use because its roots were often grounded and mixed with rice or cooked together giving not only nourishment but also nutrients such potassium, magnesium etc.

In paragraph 3 about myths surround 'Burdock' is full of magical anecdotes as some traditions narrate how in Norse Mythology Thor was believed to have used burdocks stalk to test people’s honesty - he gave a piece for them if they were worthy which symbolized prosperity and good fortune. This might indicate the role this plant played not only during religious practices but also within daily life activities where spiritual meanings & real world applications intersected harmoniously giving rise perhaps some form of folk wisdom that has survived over centuries even till today.

 Henry Fielding, writer and actor referred it to as a "wonder plant" implying its diverse usage both for culinary purposes & medicinal treatments while William Shakespeare used term “burdocks” metaphorically when speaking about someone who is unattractive or unpleasant in his play ‘All's well that ends Well'. Thus the mention of burdock within literary works further solidify its status as an integral part not just from historical perspectives but also cultural ones too.

Lastly, looking at folkloristic uses (paragraph 5) - it can be seen how different societies overtime have incorporated this versatile herb into their everyday life due largely because of belief systems surrounding health benefits & superstitions about the same which is what forms one integral part behind many ancient customs such as using burdock leaf tea for treating skin conditions or applying its root paste on insect bites etc.

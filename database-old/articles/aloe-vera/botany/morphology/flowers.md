The inflorescence type of Aloe vera flowers is a raceme, which is an unbranched and elongated cluster of flowers. These flowers exhibit a simple and elegant structure. They are tubular in shape, with a narrow and elongated appearance.

The flowers of Aloe vera are attached epigynously, meaning they are attached above the ovary. This arrangement is one of the defining features of these blooms. In terms of symmetry, they are zygomorphic, displaying bilateral symmetry.

Aloe vera flowers are relatively small, with a length of approximately 3-4 centimeters (1.2-1.6 inches) and a width of 1-2 centimeters (0.4-0.8 inches). Their colors can vary but are typically seen in shades of orange, yellow, or red, lending a pop of vibrancy to the plant.

These flowers have a smooth texture and a glabrous surface, which means they are hairless. They consist of six floral parts in total. There are three sepals, petal-like in appearance, and three petals, which are colorful and resemble traditional petals. The sepals and petals collectively form the perianth, where they look quite similar in structure.

Within the flower, there are six stamens, alternating with the petals. A single pistil with a slender style and a three-lobed stigma is present. The ovary is positioned inferiorly, below the point of attachment.

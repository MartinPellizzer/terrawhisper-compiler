Aloe vera stems are of the succulent type, meaning they store water, making them thick and fleshy. These stems are arranged in a characteristic rosette formation, with leaves emerging from a central point, giving the plant its iconic appearance.

The shape of Aloe vera stems is cylindrical, and they can reach heights of up to 60-90 centimeters (24-35 inches). The stems typically have a diameter of around 6-7 centimeters (2.4-2.8 inches), emphasizing their succulent nature.

In terms of color, Aloe vera stems are typically green, with variations that may include a hint of gray or blue. The stems have a smooth texture, and their surface is waxy and fleshy, often covered in aloe latex, a bitter substance found just beneath the skin.

Aloe vera stems exhibit compact nodes at their base, where the leaves originate. These nodes are vital for the plant's growth and development. Between the nodes, there are short internodes, measuring 2-8 centimeters (0.8-3.1 inches). These internodes play a role in determining the overall height and structure of the plant.

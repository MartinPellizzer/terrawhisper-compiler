Ammi visnaga has leaves that are relatively simple in structure, with pinnate shape, typically measuring 2-4 centimeters (0.79-1.57 inches) in length and 1-2 centimeters (0.39-0.79 inches) in width. These leaves are thin and bright green, providing a smooth, glabrous texture.

In terms of arrangement, Ammi visnaga leaves are arranged alternately on the stem and orient themselves in an erect position. The leaf apex is acute, forming a pointed tip, while the base is cuneate, giving it a wedge-shaped appearance. The leaf margin is entire, indicating a smooth, unbroken edge.

The venation pattern of these leaves is pinnate, featuring a single main vein that runs through the length of the leaf. The leaf lamina is oblong in shape, and the midrib is prominent, contributing to the overall structure of the leaf.

Intriguingly, Ammi visnaga leaves lack petioles, meaning they are directly attached to the stem, a feature known as being sessile. Stipules, small leaf-like structures often found at the base of leaf stalks in other plants, are notably absent in Ammi visnaga.

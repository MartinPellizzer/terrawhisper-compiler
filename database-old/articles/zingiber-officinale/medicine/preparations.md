Ginger has many medicinal preparations, such as tea, powder, tincture, juice, pills/tablets, oil, capsules, extracts, essential oil, and syrup. Each of these forms is designed to provide a specific dosage or delivery method for its bioactive substances, which can help alleviate various health conditions. 

 This popular beverage combines water with freshly sliced ginger root steeped into it. The tea offers a milder taste than other preparations, and its warming properties make it ideal for soothing an upset stomach or easing nausea. It can also help improve digestion and reduce inflammation. 

 Also called dried ginger powder, this form is made by drying the root and grinding it into a fine powder. Powders are often used in cooking to add flavor and spice, but they can also be added to drinks or mixed with other ingredients for medicinal purposes. Powders allow a quick release of active compounds, making them suitable for treating respiratory issues like colds or coughs. 

 This is an alcohol-based extract made by soaking ginger root in alcohol and water. The resulting liquid contains concentrated amounts of the plant’s components that offer more potent relief against inflammation, pain, and digestive disorders than raw ginger. It can be taken orally for improved bioavailability. 

 Pressing fresh ginger root produces a juice that retains many of the nutritional and medicinal benefits of the whole plant. The juice is easily absorbed by the body, making it particularly helpful in treating conditions such as motion sickness, arthritis, and menstrual pain. 

 These are small solid capsules or tablets made from ground ginger powder that contain a precise dosage of ginger. They can be taken easily without having to measure out amounts or deal with the taste of raw ginger. The standardized form is used for managing arthritis, reducing inflammation, and improving circulation. 

 This essential oil, derived from the plant’s volatile oils, has a high concentration of bioactive compounds that can be absorbed through the skin or inhaled via aromatherapy. The oil may help relieve joint pain, reduce stress, and improve overall well-being. 

 These are similar to pills but encapsulated in a soft gelatin shell instead of a hard outer layer. They provide the same benefits as pills but with an improved release for digestion and absorption. 

 Ginger extract refers to a concentrated liquid or paste form obtained from the root using water, alcohol, or other solvents. The resulting substance retains its medicinal properties and can be easily added to drinks, food, or directly ingested as needed.

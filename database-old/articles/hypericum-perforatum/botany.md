St Johns Wort with botanical name Hypericum perforatum belongs to domain Eukarya under kingdom Plantae, phylum Magnoliophyta which has many classes and orders but it is specifically a part of the order Malpighiales in class Rosopsida. This flowering plant's genus name 'Hypericum', while its species terminates as perforatum due to small punctuated spots or holes seen on leaves, stems hence often this herbaceous subshrub is also called Perfoliate St Johnswort since it looks like a leaf has been pierced by another through the hollow spot in its center.

The plant species of Hypericum perforatum can have slight morphological variations that may differ slightly with geographical location and climatic conditions which affect certain growth features such as height, width or density among others but these variants names don't change their core identity fundamentally since they all remain members within the same family.

Hypericum perforatum grows across many parts of Europe from Iceland to North Africa; South West Asia stretching towards India and Central America extending through China with certain pockets found in other locations globally due primarily being a cultivated plant often grown as ornamental garden plants, hedging & boundaries because they love warm weather. Its natural habitats include grasslands/fields mostly on dry soils but also can be seen along the edge of roadsides where it thrives without much maintenance required making them ideal for roadside beautification projects too apart from its medicinal uses which have been recognized since ancient times till modern research reveals anti-depressant properties.

St Johns Wort's life cycle follows a typical one being annual, biennial or perennial depending upon subspecies and conditions but all exhibit flowering during summer months followed by production of seeds for propagation which can be done either through seed sowing directly into the soil at right depth & spacing according to type.

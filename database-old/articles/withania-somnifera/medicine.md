 The medicinal constituents responsible for the above health benefits include alkaloids such as withanine & somniferin; saponins like glycosides, steroidal lactones (which offer anti-inflammatory actions) amongst others that constitute all parts of Ashwagandha plants - rootstock being a prominent one due to its high concentration levels.

 Common uses and preparations are often derived from the leaf powder mixed with honey or ghee while some employ it by grinding freshly gathered leaves into paste form for ingestion purposes, though there's versatile preparation options available like making tea too. The roots also play a crucial part; typically converted to extract using ethanol alcohol mixture (decoction), which is then used as an alternative option when compared with leaf preparations based on individual preferences and needs related thereto each user type experiences its effectiveness depending upon the condition being addressed accordingly

 While there are no known serious side effects of consuming Ashwagandha, some individuals might experience a feeling mild drowsiness or stomach discomfort due to allergies (rare cases) - hence it's best if taken after consulting your health professional. Precautions should also be observed while using this herb during pregnancy as safety precautionary measures cannot always rule out any potential risks involved; so seek guidance from experienced midwives before embarking upon its usage in such circumstances specifically, being considerate of mother and child's well-being throughout the journey together.

Rosemary has many medicinal preparations, such as Tea – To make the basic brew, boil fresh or dried rosemary leaves in water for a few minutes and then let it cool down. This is an easy way to consume rosemary's natural benefits.

 Infusion – Similar to tea but with more finesse. Soak the rosemary leaves in hot water for about 10-15 minutes, just off boiling temperature, strain, and drink. The result will be a milder flavor than tea.

 Essential oil – The fragrant oil extracted from rosemary's flowers is highly concentrated and used for aromatherapy purposes. It can also be diluted with other carrier oils to create massage oils that relieve muscle tension, boost mood, and support focus.

 Tincture – A liquid extract made by soaking the rosemary in an alcohol solution (usually vodka) for a few weeks or months. This potent remedy is often used as a natural antibacterial agent and can help improve digestion.

 Extract – Similar to tinctures, but rather than using alcohol, this liquid concentrate employs other solvents such as water or glycerin, and it's mainly applied for its antioxidant properties.

 Powdered form – Dried rosemary leaves can be ground into a fine powder. It's often used to flavor meals like salads, soups, and stews but can also be sprinkled on roasts for added flavor and health benefits.

 Ointment – A topical preparation made by mixing rosemary oil with a base, such as petroleum jelly or lanolin. It's commonly used to treat minor skin irritations like bug bites and bruises, soothe sore muscles, and reduce inflammation.

 Salve – A thicker, more emollient version of an ointment made from blending rosemary oil with beeswax or other natural fats. It's typically applied to dry, cracked skin for moisturization, helps protect and repair minor wounds, and can ease joint pain.

 Creams – Moisturizing lotions containing rosemary extract that help hydrate and rejuvenate the skin while offering protection from environmental factors.

 Supplements – Rosemary supplements come in capsule or tablet form and provide concentrated doses of this herb's nutrients, such as antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds. They can be consumed for improved cognitive function and general wellness.

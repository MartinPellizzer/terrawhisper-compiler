 Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis) thrives best under full sun exposure, receiving at least six hours of direct sunlight daily to promote healthy growth and development for its compact foliage bearing numerous tiny blue flowers in summertime blooms. This plant requires well-drained soil with a slightly acidic pH between 6–7 for optimum performance; avoid overly wet or soggy conditions as they can lead to root rot, which may eventually kill the entire rosemary bush if not treated promptly. Adequate spacing of about two feet is essential in maintaining air circulation and preventing fungal issues from arising due to moisture buildup among crowded leaves

 When planting your Rosemarinus officinalis seeds or cuttings, begin with healthy young stems that have green, fully developed foliage. Choose a well-prepared area after selecting appropriate conditions mentioned above; this could be in containers outside yearlong on porches and patios where the climate permits (with extra protection during winter months), within garden beds outdoors or indoors with sufficient sunlight exposure to sustain healthy growth throughout all four seasons while still ensuring it doesn't get exposed below 30 degrees Fahrenheit.

 Harvest rose hips after blooming has finished but before all its beautiful tiny flowers wilt away; cutting back just enough foliage to expose these small berry fruits ready for picking, ensuring you collect ripe ones with deep red hues displaying complete coloration – unripen or green-hued hip will not provide the same flavorful impact. You can also harvest leaves throughout spring and summer when they're most abundant - use them fresh in various culinary endeavors as an essential component within soups, stews alongside main courses due to its robust aromatic nature; alternatively dry some branches by hanging upside down indoors during the drier months for later use – it could retain strong fragrance despite drying out.

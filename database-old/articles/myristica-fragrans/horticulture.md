Nutmeg & Mace trees grow best under humid tropical conditions with plenty of sunlight, rainfall around 208 to 365 centimeters per year and a temperature range between 9°C (48℉) during nighttime up until midday reaches about 27.5-to31°Celsius or an even more intense heatwave above 40 degrees C, provided that they have proper drainage system due to their susceptibility towards waterlogging and flooding issues as a consequence of excessive rainfall in the region where these trees grow naturally (coastal lowlands from Sumatra).

These plantations must be established near an abundant source of freshwater. The seedling nurseries should ideally begin at around 6 months old, allowing young plants to develop healthy roots before transplanting them into the main field area where trees have accessibility for nutrients and space between individual rows no less than two meters apart with a spacing that enables efficient pruning later on.

Maintaining soil fertility is key when it comes to care-taking, requiring appropriate rotational cultivation practices so essential microbes thrive in the right conditions which ultimately promotes healthy growth without resorting either mechanical intervention such as tillage or chemical interventions that might harm beneficial species dwelling around nutmeg trees. Regularly monitoring tree health includes maintaining their structure and removing dead branches while pruning stimulates strong regrowth of new shoots, allowing for optimal fruit production in the future too if executed correctly over time under an ideal horticultural scenario!

Nutritional harvesting can occur after 7 to nine years from plantation as soon they start bearing fruits. This requires skillful and timely process which includes cutting off ripe nuts when it's orange or yellow in color; however, waiting too long will cause hardening hence damaging the quality of spices used either commercially by producers all over Asia-Pacific regions today!

Lastly pests like weevils and rats have posed problems affecting these plants so proper monitoring coupled with implementing physical (mesh cages), biological controls(beneficial insects which can eliminate damaging predators without causing any harm to the plant itself.) or chemical interventions whenever required are essential for mitigating crop destruction due pests. Similarly, diseases such as Verticillium Wilt and Cercospora Leaf Spot may also affect nutmeg trees; hence monitoring soil conditions & proper management techniques play an integral role in preventing these issues from occurring regularly too!

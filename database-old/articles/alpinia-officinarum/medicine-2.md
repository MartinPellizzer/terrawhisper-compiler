
The medicinal constituents found in galangal include essential oils, flavonoids, sterols, and phenolic compounds. These components give it its therapeutic properties, contributing to the various health benefits associated with this plant.

Mostly, the rhizome is used as a medicinal part of galangal due to its high concentration of essential oils and other active compounds. Galangal can be prepared in various ways such as herbal tea by steeping the crushed roots, powder or pills created from dried rhizomes, or infusions for external application as skin treatments.

While there are numerous health benefits linked to galangal, it may have potential side effects on people with certain medical conditions or sensitivities. Consumption of galangal might cause allergic reactions in individuals sensitive to its components or those allergic to other plants in the same family as ginger, cardamom, and turmeric. Galangal may also interact negatively with some medications, so it is crucial to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating this herb into your routine.

To ensure safety while using galangal, seek expert guidance if unsure about its compatibility with your specific health needs or existing medications. Additionally, consume in recommended amounts and be cautious during pregnancy or breastfeeding as its use may not be fully understood for these circumstances.

Bilberry has many medicinal constituents, such as Anthocyanins - They are a kind of water-soluble pigments that give bilberries their blue coloring, and they also provide powerful antioxidant properties which can protect cells from oxidative damage. The high levels of these anthocyanins in bilberry make it an excellent choice for treating age-related vision problems like macular degeneration, or any issues concerning eye health, particularly due to their ability to strengthen the capillaries in the eyes.

 

Flavonoids - These are a type of polyphenolic compound that also contribute to the antioxidant effects within bilberry. They help neutralize harmful free radicals and protect against oxidative damage and inflammation, which in turn can help prevent cancer or reduce the risk of heart disease.

 

Tannins - They are a group of organic compounds that possess astringent properties and are often responsible for the bitter taste found in bilberry. Tannins have been used traditionally as an antiseptic agent to treat wounds, diarrhea, and inflammation, thanks to their ability to reduce swelling and slow blood clotting.

 Gallic acid - This chemical is a phenolic compound that functions as a natural antibacterial agent. It inhibits bacterial growth by destroying the bacteria's outer wall or cell membranes and disrupting essential biochemical processes. Additionally, it assists in wound healing due to its antioxidant properties.

 Ellagic acid - This phenolic compound is a potent antioxidant that can help inhibit the growth of tumors by blocking the process through which cancer cells become malignant. It also has anti-inflammatory and anti-diabetic effects, improving blood glucose control in diabetics.

 

Quercetin glycosides - These are flavonol glycosides found in bilberry that have been shown to have a variety of health benefits, including anti-carcinogenic, anti-allergic, and antiviral effects. They also possess cardiovascular protective properties, helping to maintain normal blood pressure and reduce the risk of heart disease.

 

Catechins - These polyphenols are found abundantly in bilberry leaves. Like other phenolic compounds, they have strong antioxidant properties that can help protect against cellular damage caused by free radicals. Catechins can also play a role in boosting the immune system and fighting off bacterial and viral infections.

 Amino acids - These are organic compounds essential for building proteins within our body and also serve as precursors to other biomolecules like neurotransmitters, hormones, and enzymes. Some amino acids found in bilberry leaves, such as L-Glutamine, have been associated with supporting the immune system and maintaining gastrointestinal health.

 Phenolic compounds - These are plant chemicals with diverse antioxidant properties, offering protection against oxidative damage from reactive oxygen species that can cause cellular damage and disease. Bilberry contains numerous phenolic compounds, many of which offer specific health benefits like improving cardiovascular function or preventing certain types of cancer.

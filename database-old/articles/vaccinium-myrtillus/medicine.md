Bilberry (Vaccinium myrtillus) has many health benefits which contribute to its use in traditional medicine for centuries now. It helps improve eyesight due to anthocyanidins and flavonoids found within it that reduce blood flow resistance hence decreasing pressure on capillaries around the eye area, promoting healthy vision as a result of this action. This berry is also an antioxidant-rich food which can help protect cell components like proteins from oxidative stress caused by free radicals in our body system and thus slow down aging process or even prevent certain diseases related to it such diabetes, arthritis inflammation as well as cardiovascular issues. Inflammatory bowel conditions could also be eased using Bilberry due the astringent properties of its leaves which soothe irritated tissue linings in intestinal tracts thereby offering relief for these disorders too!

The medicinally active constituents include various organic acids, tannins and flavonoids such as catechine. Its health benefits are derived from the complex mixture between phenolic compounds (anthocyanosides) that it has high abundance in compared to other plant species which makes Bilberry an exceptional source of potent anti-oxidant properties for human consumption or topical application, contributing positively towards maintaining a healthy body.

The most commonly used parts are the fruit berry itself along with its leaves and roots - both being utilized individually according their respective benefits; however, more often than not it's mixed preparations of these three elements that offer maximum therapeutic value when using for curative purposes such as treatment against diabetes or improving blood circulation in general cases where arterial stiffness could lead towards other medical issues if left unchecked.

Side effects have been noted rarely, but they might include mild stomach discomfort and some form of gastrointestinal irritation during use mainly caused by tannins present within it; though these complaints may be minimized or avoided altogether while properly considering dosage size recommended per individual case based on the degree needed towards a successful treatment outcome.

To ensure safety, precautions should always include consulting with your healthcare provider before using Bilberry for any medical condition especially if you are already undergoing medications that might interact negatively alongside consumption of this supplement - additionally avoiding excessive use during pregnancy or while nursing because specific constituents may affect breastfeeding mothers differently than other adults do.

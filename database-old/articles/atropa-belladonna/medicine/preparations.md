Deadly Nightshade has many medicinal preparations, such as tea, tincture, poultice, ointment, suppository, powder, infusion, capsule, extract and salve. Each of these forms serves a specific purpose to aid in the healing process. Let’s dive into each one.

 Tea is made by steeping leaves or other plant parts from Deadly Nightshade in hot water. The resultant brew is often consumed for its medicinal properties, including treating minor inflammation and respiratory issues. It can also be used as a mild sedative due to the presence of alkaloids.

 Tincture is an alcoholic solution of Deadly Nightshade. Alcohol in combination with the plant extract helps preserve the therapeutic effects while providing quick absorption into the body. The tincture can be applied topically or taken internally, and it is used to treat a range of conditions including skin infections, muscle pain, and headaches.

 Poultice involves placing moistened leaves directly on to the affected area as a means of treating wounds or soreness. It is believed that the heat generated by the leaf-pile enhances circulation and promotes healing. The use of Deadly Nightshade poultice typically targets muscle pain, skin issues, and joint inflammation.

 Ointment is a semi-solid preparation made from plant extracts mixed with an oil base (such as petroleum jelly or beeswax). These ointments can be used topically to soothe aching muscles, reduce inflammation, and heal minor wounds. They are also known for their moisturizing properties.

 Suppositories are solid dosage forms made from Deadly Nightshade extract combined with an inert base (usually cocoa butter or glycerin). These are inserted into the rectum or vagina to treat internal issues, such as constipation and menstrual cramps. They offer a localized effect while allowing the medicine to absorb directly into the bloodstream.

 Powder is made by grinding dried Deadly Nightshade leaves into a fine substance that can be used for various purposes. It can be mixed with water or other liquids and then drunk to treat digestive issues, headaches and insomnia. The powder can also be applied topically on open wounds to prevent infection.

 These nine different types of preparations from Deadly Nightshade demonstrate the plant’s versatility in treating a variety of medical conditions while catering to diverse needs. Each form has its specific role and use, highlighting their relevance in traditional medicine practices.

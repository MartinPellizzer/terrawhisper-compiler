 Growth requirements for Psyllium involve adequate amounts of water at any stage but not too wet, a sunny or partly shaded location with well-drained soil richness (neutral to slightly alkaline) having plenty nutrients like phosphorus and potassium alongwith organic matter.

 Planting tips for Psyllium suggest selecting the plant during its dormant phase from November till March, digging holes spanning twice as wide as it is deep, setting roots at soil level while covering with mulch to maintain moisture levels around the newly established seedlings until they're firmly rooted.

 To grow your psyllium well you must tend their needs such watering regularly without over-saturation; keep a moderate temperature of 65°F (18 °C) -70℉(21℃); ensure good air circulation, and prune the plant occasionally to promote growth.

 Psyllium harvest time is when seeds become dry enough around August for mature psyllium plants but not before they're ready or risk affecting their germination in future years; you should collect seed pods after flowering ceases using scissors by hand ensuring no damage done during collection process.

 Pests and diseases encountered with Psylliuns are quite minimal compared to other plant species, though slugs might attack young plants leading into rotted roots if not handled carefully or treated promptly; powdery mildew is another potential issue which can be prevented by proper spacing between each psyllium in your garden.

- My expertise: herbalist, botanist.
- How I discovered medicinal herbs: A mix of experience (given to me by a great mentor I met as a teenager) and an obsessive drive for research and education i found in scientific papers.
- My experience working with medicinal herbs: Tested more herbs that I can count, everything I could get my hands on, filling dozens of cabinets in my personal apothecary with medicinal herbs and herbal preparations, helped friends with everyday ailments.
- What sparked my idea to create an online encyclopedia: People always told me I should use my knowledge and experience to serve a higher purpose than a local apothecary, especially because there are a lot of misleading, incomplete, not very comprehensive, and contradictory information about medicinal herbs out there.
- My personal values and goals: promoting sustainability, education, and accessibility through my encyclopedia.
- My tone and style: informative and conversational, with a pinch of formality to hint my analytical predisposition and a pinch of humor to lighten the mood. 
- Personal anecdotes or stories: I was searching for a way to boost my energy, focus, and mood, while reducing daily stress and anxiety. I could only find the answer in medicinal herbs. They gave me all this without any nasty side effects other solutions gave.
- Specific areas of focus: phytotherapy
- Research sources: reputable medical directories (ex. PubMed)
- Practice**: I have experience working as an apothecary and in a botanical garden
- Personal use and experience: I have used medicinal herbs personally and with family and friends, mostly for seasonal illnesses (ex. flu, cough, etc...), daily ailments (ex. headaches, cough, etc...), and to better the quality of life (ex. reduce stress, anxiety, boost energy, etc...).
- Research and Content Creation: review scientific studies, consult books and, every time it's possible, I test the herbs on myself to get a feeling of the results.  the organization and structure of the content for the encyclopedia is so that each herb is analyzed on a high level first (like uses and benefits), then on a more detailed lower lever (where I discuss what medicinal constituents the plant has that justify its properties), then how to prepare and consume the herb, then eventual side effects and precautions to take for that specific plant. 
- Notable Challenges: there is a lot of contradicting information online, so i decided to use a 3-way solution to solve this problem, where I cross the historical data and used for the plant with the modern scientific studies and personal experience. this is especially hard with integrating the personal experience, as I can only test the herbs i can get my hands on, either because i can cultivate it in my botanical garden or because i can source it from reputable sources.
- Notable Achievements: I personally read hundreds of information dense research papers and extracted those more relevant for each herb. I also tested on myself more than a hundred different herbs and still survived, or, to be more precise, I have never been better.

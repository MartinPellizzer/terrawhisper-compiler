# 13 Best Herbal Teas For Sleep Disorder (Insomnia): Help Relaxation And Alleviate Sleeplessness

![Herbal Tea For Sleep](/images/herbal-tea-for-sleep.jpg)

Sleep disorder, commonly known as insomnia, plagues millions worldwide each year. Approximately 30% of the global population experiences some form of sleep disruption annually, turning what should be a restorative process into a widespread concern. 

The ramifications extend beyond restless nights, negatively impacting overall well-being and diminishing the quality of life for affected individuals. Causes range from stress and lifestyle factors to underlying health issues. This pervasive condition not only disrupts sleep patterns but also gives rise to an array of health concerns, contributing to a domino effect that compromises physical and mental health.

Herbal tea emerges as a promising remedy, harnessing natural properties to alleviate insomnia's grip. The constituents within herbal infusions engage with the body's mechanisms, promoting relaxation and restoring balance. These dynamic elements work synergistically to address the root causes of sleep disturbances, offering a holistic approach to combating insomnia without resorting to pharmaceutical interventions.



## 1. Passionflower (Passiflora incarnata) Tea

Passionflower tea contains an abundance of antioxidants and flavonoids, including a specific type of flavonoid known as flavonols. These flavonoids have been shown to interact with certain neurotransmitters in the brain, such as gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) and serotonin, which help promote relaxation and reduce anxiety levels. Additionally, passionflower has been found to possess mild sedative properties, which contribute to its ability to improve sleep quality. The tea's anti-inflammatory effects also play a role in reducing physical discomfort that interfere with sleep.

The following **photo** shows what a cup of Passionflower Tea made for sleep looks like.

![Passionflower Tea For Sleep](/images/passionflower-tea-for-sleep.jpg)

**STUDY:** Passionflower Tea for sleep.

This study aimed to investigate the efficacy of Passiflora incarnata herbal tea on human sleep, using sleep diaries and polysomnography (PSG). The study featured a double-blind, placebo-controlled, repeated-measures design with a one week washout period. Forty-one participants filled out sleep diaries for seven days, while ten also underwent overnight PSG. Of six sleep diary measures analyzed, sleep quality showed a significantly better rating for passionflower compared to placebo (p < 0.01). These initial findings suggest that low doses of Passiflora incarnata in the form of tea yield short-term sleep benefits for healthy adults with mild fluctuations in sleep quality.

**RECIPE:** Passionflower Tea for sleep.

1. Boil water in a kettle or on stove top
2. Steep 1-2 tablespoons of dried passionflower herb per cup of water for 5-7 minutes.
3. Remove from heat and let it cool down to room temperature.
4. Pour into a teapot or directly into a mug.
5. Add honey, lemon, or milk to taste, and enjoy before bedtime.

Valerian tea is a herbal drink made from the dried roots of the valerian plant. It has been used for centuries to help people fall asleep faster and stay asleep longer. The exact mechanism behind its sleep-promoting effects is not fully understood, but it is thought to work by increasing levels of a neurotransmitter called GABA in the brain. This help to calm the nervous system and promote relaxation, making it easier to fall asleep and stay asleep. Additionally, valerian tea also help to reduce anxiety and stress, which further contribute to its sleep-promoting effects.

![Valerian Tea For Sleep](/images/valerian-tea-for-sleep.jpg)

**STUDY:** Valerian Tea for sleep.

The study investigates the short-term and long-term effects of a valerian extract on both objective and subjective sleep parameters in 16 patients with previously established psychophysiological insomnia. After a single dose, no effects were observed, but after multiple dosage for 14 days, sleep efficiency increased significantly for valerian condition, along with a decrease in slow-wave sleep latency and an increase in slow-wave sleep percentage. Other improvements in sleep structure such as an increase in REM percentage and a decrease in NREM1 percentage also occurred. The study found that the herbal extract of radix valerianae demonstrated positive effects on sleep structure and perception, and can be recommended for mild psychophysiological insomnia.

**RECIPE:** Valerian Tea for sleep.

1. Boil water in a kettle or pot.
2. Grind Valerian root into a fine powder.
3. Measure out 1 teaspoon of Valerian powder for every 8 oz of water.
4. Steep the Valerian tea for 5-10 minutes to let the flavors infuse into the water.
5. Strain the tea and drink it before bedtime to help with sleep.

Kava Kava tea is known for its calming and soothing effects, making it a popular choice for those looking to improve their sleep quality. **STUDY:** Kava Kava Tea for sleep.

The present study investigated the efficacy and safety of kava special extract WS® 1490 in patients with sleep disturbances associated with anxiety, tension and restlessness states of non-psychotic origin. A multicenter, randomized, double-blind clinical trial was conducted in which 61 patients received daily doses of 200 mg WS® 1490 or placebo for a period of 4 weeks. The primary efficacy variables were measured by the sleep questionnaire SF-B and the Hamilton Anxiety Scale (HAMA). The results showed that kava extract demonstrated statistically significant superior therapeutic efficacy in improving sleep quality and recuperative effect after sleep, as well as reducing psychic anxiety. Additionally, improvements were noted in self-rating of well-being and global clinical evaluation. Overall, the study concluded that kava extract WS® 1490 is an effective and safe treatment option for sleep disturbances associated with non-psychotic anxiety disorders.

**RECIPE:** Kava Kava Tea for sleep.

2. Add 1 tablespoon of kava kava powder to the boiling water, stir well to combine.
3. Reduce heat to low and let the tea steep for 5-7 minutes.
4. Pour the tea through a fine mesh strainer into a mug or cup.
5. Add honey or lemon if desired, sip slowly and enjoy your kava kava tea for sleep.

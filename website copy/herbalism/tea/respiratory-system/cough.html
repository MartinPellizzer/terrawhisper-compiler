1. Thyme tea

Thyme tea helps with cough because it contains compounds with antispasmodic and expectorant properties.

The herb's active components, such as thymol and carvacrol, work together to soothe irritated throat tissues, reduce inflammation, and promote the expulsion of mucus from the respiratory tract.

This synergistic effect makes thyme tea an effective natural remedy for relieving both dry and productive coughs, providing quick comfort and helping to alleviate symptoms of respiratory ailments.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in thyme that aid with cough.

  • Expectorant: Thyme tea helps loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract, making it easier to cough up phlegm. This property is important for cough because it reduces congestion and irritation in the airways, alleviating the need to cough. Active constituents include volatile oils like thymol and carvacrol.
  • Antitussive: Thyme tea has been shown to help suppress coughing by reducing the cough reflex. This property is important for cough because it can provide relief from constant, unproductive coughing. The active constituents responsible for this effect are flavonoids like apigenin and luteolin.
  • Antispasmodic: Thyme tea can help relax smooth muscles in the respiratory tract, reducing spasms and coughing fits. This property is important for cough because it minimizes discomfort associated with involuntary muscle spasms in the airways. Thymol and other volatile oils are responsible for this effect.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Thyme tea has natural anti-inflammatory properties, which help soothe irritated and inflamed airways. This property is important for cough because it helps reduce swelling and irritation that can trigger coughing fits. The active constituents responsible for this effect include rosmarinic acid and flavonoids.
  • Antimicrobial: Thyme tea exhibits antimicrobial properties, which help combat infections causing respiratory issues. This property is important for cough because it targets the underlying cause of some coughs, reducing symptoms and promoting recovery. Active constituents include thymol, carvacrol, and other volatile oils.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts of thyme that help with cough.

  • Leaves: Contain essential oils and flavonoids that have expectorant and antispasmodic properties, helping to loosen mucus and soothe coughs.
  • Flowers: Rich in flavonoids and volatile oils with antitussive and anti-inflammatory effects, alleviating irritation and reducing cough frequency.
  • Stems: Have similar properties to leaves, containing volatile oils and flavonoids that help relax bronchial spasms and relieve symptoms of cough and congestion.
  • Seeds: Contain thymol and other essential oils, offering antimicrobial and antitussive benefits, aiding in the reduction of both cough and infection.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic thyme tea for cough.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 2 teaspoons of fresh thyme leaves.
  2. Boil the water in a saucepan on high heat for 3-5 minutes.
  3. Place the thyme leaves in a teapot or infuser.
  4. Pour the boiling water over the thyme leaves and steep for 10 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup, discard the thyme leaves, and drink immediately.

What are the most common causes of cough that are treatable with herbal teas?

The primary causes of cough treatable with herbal teas are dry coughs resulting from irritation in the throat and wet coughs due to congestion in the chest.

Persistent coughing can be a result of inflammation in the respiratory tract, which can be soothed by certain herbal teas. Additionally, herbal teas can provide relief for coughs caused by allergies, as they can reduce inflammation in the airways and alleviate symptoms.

Furthermore, herbal teas can be beneficial for coughs that occur due to the common cold or flu, as they can help alleviate symptoms and promote relaxation, which can aid in recovery.

What other herbal preparations helps with cough?

Other herbal preparations to use with teas for cough are syrups, tinctures, and steam inhalation.

Syrups provide a sweet taste that helps mask the strong flavor of some herbs, making them more palatable for children. Tinctures are concentrated liquid extracts that can be added to water or tea for a potent dose of herbs.

Steam inhalation involves breathing in steam infused with herbs, which helps soothe irritated throat tissues and relieve congestion.

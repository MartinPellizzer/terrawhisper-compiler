The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that help with heartburn.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the esophagus and stomach, thereby alleviating heartburn.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for heartburn.

  1. In a small saucepan, boil 1 cup of water for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea into a cup.
  5. Sip the chamomile tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your heartburn.

What is heartburn and how can it affect your life?

Heartburn is a digestive disorder that occurs when stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus, causing a burning sensation in the chest.

This condition impacts people's lives by causing discomfort, disrupting daily activities, and affecting their ability to eat certain foods. Heartburn can lead to sleep disturbances, decreased productivity, and a reduced quality of life.

It is essential to seek medical attention to manage heartburn effectively and prevent further complications.

What other natural remedies help with heartburn?

Heartburn, a common complaint, often responds well to natural remedies.

One such solution is consuming a tablespoon of apple cider vinegar diluted in a glass of water before meals. This aids in digestion and maintains stomach acidity, preventing heartburn.

Another effective method involves chewing gum, which increases saliva production, neutralizing stomach acid. Additionally, baking soda, a natural antacid, offers quick relief. Dissolve a teaspoon of baking soda in a glass of water and drink slowly.

Lastly, ginger, in various forms like tea or candies, can soothe the digestive tract and reduce inflammation, providing heartburn relief.

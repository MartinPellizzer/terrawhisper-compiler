What is oral thrush and how can it affect your life?

Oral thrush is a fungal infection that develops in the mouth, commonly causing white, creamy or yellow patches to appear on the tongue, inner cheeks, gums, and tonsils.

It is caused by an overgrowth of the Candida fungus, which is naturally present in the body in small amounts. This condition impacts people's lives by leading to discomfort, pain, and difficulty eating, speaking, and swallowing. In severe cases, it can lead to systemic infections, particularly in individuals with weakened immune systems, such as those with HIV/AIDS or undergoing chemotherapy.

Early diagnosis and proper treatment are crucial to alleviate symptoms and prevent complications.

What other natural remedies help with oral thrush?

Oral thrush, a fungal infection in the mouth, often responds well to natural remedies.

One of the most common and effective treatments is plain yogurt, which contains probiotics that help restore the natural balance of bacteria in the mouth. Regularly eating unsweetened yogurt or using it as a topical application on the affected areas can alleviate symptoms. Another remedy is gentian violet, a natural dye with antifungal properties.

Though it may temporarily stain the mouth, studies show it effectively combats oral thrush. Lastly, a solution of baking soda and water provides relief for some people. Baking soda's antifungal properties can help eliminate the infection-causing Candida albicans.

To use, dissolve half a teaspoon of baking soda in a glass of water and rinse the mouth with the solution.

What other health issues people with oral thrush are likely to experience?

The issues people with oral thrush are likely to experiece are listed below.

  • White patches in mouth: Oral thrush is a fungal infection caused by the candida fungus, which results in the formation of white patches in the mouth.
  • Sore mouth: The infected areas in the mouth become sore and can cause discomfort and pain, especially when consuming acidic or spicy foods.
  • Cottony sensation in mouth: The excessive growth of candida fungus can lead to a cottony feeling in the mouth due to the thick white patches on the tongue and inside the cheeks.
  • Loss of taste: Oral thrush can cause a loss of taste as the fungal growth covers and irritates the taste buds, making it difficult to taste food properly.
  • Reddened corners of the mouth: The skin around the mouth can become red and sore due to the irritation caused by oral thrush, leading to painful and cracked corners of the mouth.
  • Difficulty eating: Eating can become a challenge due to the painful and sore areas in the mouth caused by the fungal infection.
  • Painful swallowing: The irritation from the thrush can extend to the throat, causing painful swallowing and discomfort.
  • Bad breath: Oral thrush can cause bad breath due to the presence of fungus and dead cells in the mouth.
  • Bleeding spots in mouth: Vigorous brushing or scratching of the white patches can result in bleeding spots in the mouth, leading to further discomfort and pain.

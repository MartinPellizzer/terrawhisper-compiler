  • Flowers: Containing anti-inflammatory agents, flowers offer relief from swelling and promote better circulation.
  • Buds: Packed with essential oils, buds have a soothing effect and assist in reducing swelling.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for swollen feet.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to a rolling temperature of 212 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Crush 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves, releasing natural oils.
  3. Steep crushed peppermint leaves in boiling water for 5-10 minutes, revealing tea's full flavor.
  4. Strain tea into a cup, discarding used peppermint leaves.
  5. Drink warm peppermint tea, allowing it to soothe and reduce foot swelling.

What is swollen feet and how can it affect your life?

Swollen feet, a condition characterized by an abnormal accumulation of fluid in the feet's tissues, brings discomfort and mobility restrictions.

This ailment impacts people's lives by causing pain, hindering daily activities, and potentially signaling underlying health issues such as heart or kidney problems.

Seeking timely medical attention is crucial for effective treatment and improved quality of life.

What other natural remedies help with swollen feet?

Swollen feet are a common problem, especially among elderly individuals and those who spend long hours standing.

There are several natural remedies that effectively alleviate this issue, without relying on herbal teas. One such method is elevating the feet above the heart level for 15-20 minutes, three to four times a day.

This aids in reducing fluid buildup and alleviating swelling. Regular physical activity, such as walking or swimming, also promotes circulation and reduces fluid accumulation in the feet. Additionally, wearing compression stockings can provide support to the veins in the legs and decrease swelling.

Lastly, maintaining a healthy weight helps prevent fluid retention in the body, thereby minimizing the likelihood of swollen feet.

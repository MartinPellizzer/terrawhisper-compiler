1. Black tea tea

Black tea helps with high cholesterol because it contains flavonoids, a type of antioxidant that has been shown to reduce levels of low-density lipoprotein (LDL), or "bad" cholesterol, in the blood.

These flavonoids work by preventing the oxidation of LDL, which is a key step in the development of plaque in the arteries. Additionally, black tea has been found to inhibit the absorption of cholesterol in the intestines, further helping to lower overall cholesterol levels. Furthermore, black tea has been shown to increase the excretion of bile acids, which can also help to lower cholesterol levels.

Therefore, incorporating black tea into a healthy diet and lifestyle can be a simple and effective way to help manage high cholesterol.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in black tea that aid with high cholesterol.

  • Antioxidant properties: Black tea is rich in antioxidants, such as flavonoids, which help reduce the oxidative stress and inflammation caused by high cholesterol, thus protecting the cardiovascular system.
  • Lipid-lowering properties: The catechins and theaflavins in black tea have been shown to reduce LDL (bad) cholesterol levels and increase HDL (good) cholesterol levels, promoting a healthier lipid profile.
  • Anti-inflammatory properties: The polyphenols in black tea exhibit anti-inflammatory effects, which can help alleviate the inflammation associated with high cholesterol and atherosclerosis, reducing the risk of cardiovascular diseases.
  • Vasodilatory properties: Black tea contains theophylline and theobromine, which can help relax blood vessels, improving blood flow and reducing blood pressure, thus alleviating the strain on the cardiovascular system caused by high cholesterol.
  • Blood sugar regulation: Black tea has been shown to help regulate blood sugar levels, which can indirectly contribute to maintaining healthy cholesterol levels, as high blood sugar can increase cholesterol production in the liver.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts of black tea that help with high cholesterol.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, black tea leaves help reduce LDL cholesterol and increase HDL cholesterol levels.
  • Buds: High in flavonoids, buds can inhibit the absorption of cholesterol in the body and support heart health.
  • Bark: Containing catechins and theaflavins, the bark can help lower total cholesterol levels and improve blood pressure.
  • Seeds: Packed with polyphenols, black tea seeds can aid in reducing cholesterol absorption and improving cardiovascular health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic black tea tea for high cholesterol.

  1. In a pot, combine 2 cups of water and 2 tablespoons of dried hawthorn berries, 1 tablespoon of dried orange peel, and 1 tablespoon of dried cinnamon bark.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Add 1 teaspoon of dried hibiscus flowers and let it steep for an additional 5 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solids.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of this herbal tea daily to help manage high cholesterol.

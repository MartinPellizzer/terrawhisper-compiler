1. Feverfew tea

Feverfew tea helps with headaches because it contains parthenolide, a compound that is known to reduce inflammation and constrict blood vessels in the brain.

These actions can alleviate the symptoms of headaches, including pain and pressure. Furthermore, feverfew has been shown to inhibit the release of serotonin and other chemicals that can cause constriction of blood vessels in the brain and lead to headaches.

Therefore, drinking feverfew tea can provide relief for those suffering from headaches caused by inflammation and constricted blood vessels.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in feverfew that aid with headaches.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Feverfew tea's anti-inflammatory properties help reduce headache-causing inflammation in blood vessels, primarily due to parthenolide and other sesquiterpene lactones.
  • Vasoconstrictor: Feverfew's ability to constrict blood vessels can prevent migraines by minimizing the dilation and constriction of blood vessels in the head, resulting from parthenolide and its derivatives.
  • Serotonin modulation: Feverfew tea aids in regulating serotonin levels, which can be imbalanced during migraines; this is attributed mainly to parthenolide's involvement in serotonin release and uptake.
  • Antispasmodic: Feverfew's antispasmodic property helps relax smooth muscles in blood vessel walls, reducing the likelihood of headaches caused by muscle tension and spasms, resulting from parthenolide and other active compounds.
  • Anti-platelet aggregation: Feverfew tea inhibits platelet aggregation, which helps prevent the formation of blood clots that can lead to reduced blood flow and headaches; this property can be attributed to parthenolide's effect on platelet activation and aggregation.
  • Antioxidant: Feverfew has antioxidant properties that protect neurons from oxidative stress and damage, which can play a role in migraine onset; these antioxidant effects are associated with various active constituents in feverfew, including flavonoids and phenolic acids.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts of feverfew that help with headaches.

  • Leaves: Contain parthenolide, a compound that helps reduce inflammation and relieve headaches.
  • Flowers: Rich in flavonoids and tanins, contributing to the plant's overall analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Seeds: Packed with essential oils, offering pain-relieving and soothing effects.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic feverfew tea for headaches.

  1. Gather 2-3 fresh feverfew leaves or 1-2 dried feverfew teaspoons.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool for 30 seconds.
  3. Place the feverfew leaves or dried tea in a teapot or infuser.
  4. Pour the hot water over the feverfew and steep for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea, discard the leaves, and enjoy the warm beverage.

2. Willow tea

Willow tea helps with headaches because it contains salicin, a compound similar to aspirin.

This substance works by blocking the production of prostaglandins, chemicals that cause inflammation and blood vessel constriction in the brain, leading to headaches. By reducing inflammation and improving blood flow, willow tea effectively alleviates headache symptoms, providing a natural and herbal alternative to traditional pain relievers.

Its therapeutic properties have been recognized for centuries, making it a popular remedy for those seeking relief from headache discomfort.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in willow that aid with headaches.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Willow tea contains salicin, which is a natural anti-inflammatory compound that helps reduce swelling and inflammation in blood vessels, a common cause of headaches.
  • Analgesic: The salicin in willow tea acts as a pain reliever, helping to alleviate the pain associated with headaches.
  • Fever reducer: Willow tea can help reduce fever, which can often accompany and worsen headaches. This is also due to the salicin content.
  • Anti-pyretic: The anti-pyretic properties of willow tea help to lower body temperature, which can provide relief from headaches caused by fever or overheating.
  • Smooth muscle relaxant: Willow tea contains compounds that can help relax smooth muscles, which can alleviate tension headaches caused by muscle tension.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts of willow that help with headaches.

  • Bark: Contains salicin, a natural pain reliever that helps alleviate headaches.
  • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids that possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties to reduce headache symptoms.
  • Buds: High in tannins and essential oils, which have a soothing effect on headache-prone vessels.
  • Rhizomes: A source of phenolic acids, which can help reduce the frequency and intensity of headaches.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic willow tea for headaches.

  1. Gather 2-3 willow bark strips (providing 240-360 mg of salicin) from a willow tree and rinse them thoroughly.
  2. Boil the willow bark in 4 cups of water for 10-15 minutes to extract the medicinal components.
  3. Strain the tea to remove bark particles and let it cool for 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in honey or lemon to taste (optional).
  5. Drink the entire willow tea preparation to alleviate headaches, but do not exceed more than one dose in 6 hours.

How to best treat headaches with herbal teas?

The best way to treat headaches with herbal tea is by choosing teas with natural relaxing and anti-inflammatory properties.

Two effective options are chamomile and feverfew teas. Chamomile tea, made from the flowers of the Matricaria chamomilla plant, has calming effects that can help alleviate tension headaches. Feverfew tea, derived from the Tanacetum parthenium herb, is known for its ability to reduce inflammation and constrict blood vessels, which can help in treating migraines.

Regular consumption of these teas, 2-3 times a day, can help manage headaches and promote overall well-being.

What are the most common causes of headaches that are treatable with herbal teas?

The primary causes of headaches treatable with herbal tea are tension and stress.

These types of headaches often result from tightened muscles in the neck, shoulders, and head due to anxiety or tension. Another cause is sinus headaches, which occur when the sinuses become inflamed or congested, leading to discomfort and pressure in the head.

Lastly, menstrual migraines are a common cause of headaches treatable with herbal tea. These migraines typically affect women and are caused by hormonal fluctuations during menstruation.

Herbal teas, such as chamomile or ginger, have anti-inflammatory and relaxing properties that can help alleviate these types of headaches.

What other herbal preparations helps with headaches?

Other herbal preparations to use with teas for headaches are tinctures, capsules, and essential oils.

Tinctures are concentrated alcohol-based extractions of herbs, while capsules provide a measured dose of dried herbs in a convenient form. Essential oils, on the other hand, are highly concentrated plant extracts that can be used topically, diluted in a carrier oil, or diffused in the air for inhalation.

A combined approach using multiple preparation methods can provide a more comprehensive and integrated therapy for headache relief.

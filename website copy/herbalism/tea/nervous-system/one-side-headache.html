10 best herbal teas for one side headache

For those seeking natural relief from a one-sided headache, certain herbal teas prove highly effective.

Ginger tea, known for its anti-inflammatory properties, reduces pain and inflammation in the head. Peppermint tea, containing menthol, soothes and relaxes tense muscles, promoting sinus and blood flow improvement.

Lastly, chamomile tea, rich in antioxidants and antispasmodic qualities, helps alleviate headaches caused by stress or muscle tension.

1. Feverfew

Feverfew tea provides relief from one-sided headaches, such as migraines, due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

This herbal tea contains parthenolide, a compound that inhibits the release of chemicals causing blood vessels to expand and inflame. By reducing inflammation and constricting blood vessels, feverfew tea alleviates pressure and pain on the affected side of the head. Furthermore, feverfew demonstrates an ability to prevent the onset of migraines by inhibiting the production of excess serotonin and prostaglandins.

Regular consumption of feverfew tea promotes vascular health and maintains consistent blood flow, thus minimizing the recurrence of one-sided headaches.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in feverfew tea that help with one side headache.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the feverfew plant are rich in parthenolide, a compound known to alleviate symptoms of migraines and headaches.
  • Flowers: Feverfew flowers contain similar medicinal compounds as the leaves, but in smaller concentrations, making them a suitable alternative.
  • Stems: Although not as potent as the leaves or flowers, feverfew stems can contribute to the tea's efficacy in treating one-sided headaches.
  • Seeds: Feverfew seeds are a potential source of the plant's therapeutic compounds, though they are less frequently used in medicinal tea preparations.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in feverfew tea that aid with one side headache.

  • Parthenolide: Reduces inflammation by inhibiting the release of prostaglandins and suppressing the activity of COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes, providing relief from one-sided headaches.
  • Sesquiterpene Lactones: Exhibit strong anti-inflammatory properties, aiding in the alleviation of headaches originating from inflammation.
  • Flavonoids: Possess potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, contributing to the easing of one-sided headaches by counteracting oxidative stress and inflammation.
  • Volatile Oils: Comprise various compounds, such as camphor and camphene, which have analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, providing headache relief.
  • Tanacetin: Acts as a selective COX-2 inhibitor, reducing inflammation and providing relief specifically for unilateral headaches.
  • Ilerdanin: Demonstrates anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, helping alleviate the pain and inflammation associated with one-sided headaches.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic feverfew tea for one side headache.

  1. Gather 2-3 large feverfew leaves and rinse them thoroughly under cold water to remove any dirt or impurities.
  2. Bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add the cleaned feverfew leaves and let it steep for 10 minutes to allow the medicinal compounds to extrude.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent leaves, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Add a teaspoon of honey to the tea, if desired, to enhance the flavor and soothe a sore throat.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat your throat and relax the muscles that may be contributing to the headache.

2. Willow

Willow tea alleviates one-sided headaches due to its salicin content, a natural compound similar to aspirin.

This substance inhibits prostaglandin production, reducing inflammation and, consequently, headache pain. Moreover, willow bark contains flavonoids, which boost salicin's pain-relieving properties. By drinking willow tea, you enable your body to absorb these beneficial compounds gradually, ensuring lasting relief.

Furthermore, willow tea's soothing warmth promotes circulation, aiding in the dispersal of tension and alleviating headache symptoms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in willow tea that help with one side headache.

  • Leaves: Contain compounds that can help reduce inflammation, making them effective for treating one-sided headaches.
  • Bark: Has pain-relieving properties that can soothe headaches, especially when brewed into a medicinal tea.
  • Flowers: Contain flavonoids that can help reduce blood pressure and alleviate headache symptoms.
  • Seeds: Rich in anti-inflammatory compounds that can help reduce swelling and ease headache pain.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in willow tea that aid with one side headache.

  • Salicin: A natural anti-inflammatory compound that helps reduce inflammation, thereby alleviating one-sided headaches caused by swelling.
  • Flavonoids: Antioxidant compounds that improve circulation and reduce inflammation, which can help relieve headaches by reducing pressure on blood vessels.
  • Tannins: Astringent compounds that can help reduce swelling and inflammation, providing relief for one-sided headaches.
  • Phenolic acids: Antioxidant compounds that help reduce inflammation and alleviate pain by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, which are involved in the inflammatory response.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic willow tea for one side headache.

  1. Gather 2 fresh willow bark branches, peel off outer bark, and cut branches into 1-inch slices.
  2. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil, add willow bark slices, reduce heat, and simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat, strain out willow bark, and let tea cool to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Stir in 1 teaspoon of honey for added flavor (optional).
  5. Drink 1 cup of willow tea twice a day, in the morning and afternoon, to alleviate a one-sided headache.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in peppermint tea that aid with one side headache.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for one side headache.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to 212 degrees Fahrenheit, taking 5-7 minutes in a pot on high heat.
  2. Crush 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves to release oils, consuming 30 seconds.
  3. Place crushed peppermint leaves in a tea infuser and put it in a mug.
  4. Pour boiled water over the infuser and steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Remove the infuser and enjoy the soothing peppermint tea, immediately relieving your headache.

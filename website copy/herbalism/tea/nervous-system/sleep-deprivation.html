1. Valerian

Valerian tea helps with sleep deprivation because it contains active compounds, including valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, that influence the brain's level of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a neurotransmitter responsible for regulating nerve impulse transmissions.

By increasing GABA activity, valerian tea reduces the overall excitability of the nervous system, calming down the brain and preparing it for sleep. Moreover, valerian tea has been shown to hasten the onset of sleep, improve overall sleep quality, and decrease the number of nighttime awakenings in individuals experiencing sleep deprivation.

This therapeutic effect of valerian tea on sleep is both safe and well-established, with minimal side effects.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian that aid with sleep deprivation.

  • Sedative properties: Valerian tea contains active constituents such as valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, which help to reduce nerve excitability, making it easier to fall asleep and stay asleep.
  • Anxiolytic properties: Valerian tea can help alleviate anxiety and promote relaxation, which is crucial for individuals dealing with sleep deprivation as anxiety can exacerbate sleep problems.
  • Muscle relaxant properties: Valerian tea contains compounds like valepotriates, which help to relax muscles and alleviate tension, making it useful for people who experience muscle cramps or restless leg syndrome.
  • Improved sleep quality: Valerian tea can enhance the overall quality of sleep, promoting deep, restorative sleep, which is essential for combating the negative effects of sleep deprivation.
  • Non-addictive nature: Unlike many prescription sleep aids, valerian tea is non-addictive, providing a safer and more sustainable long-term solution for those suffering from sleep deprivation.
  • Minimal side effects: Valerian tea generally has mild side effects, if any, and is well-tolerated by most individuals, making it a suitable option for those who cannot take stronger sleep medications due to side effects or health conditions.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in valerian that help with sleep deprivation.

  • Roots: Contain active compounds that promote sleep and reduce anxiety.
  • Rhizomes: Rich in valerenic acid, shown to improve sleep quality.
  • Flowers: Contain volatile oils that have a relaxing and calming effect.
  • Leaves: High in antioxidants that help reduce stress and induce sleep.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian for sleep deprivation.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried valerian root with 8 ounces of boiling water.
  2. Steep the mixture, covered, for 10-15 minutes to allow the valerian to release its compounds.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the valerian root, leaving only the liquid.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added flavor.
  5. Consume the tea 30-60 minutes before bedtime for optimal sleep aid.

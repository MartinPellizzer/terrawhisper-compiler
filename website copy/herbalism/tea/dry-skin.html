10 best herbal teas for dry skin

The top herbal teas for dry skin include chamomile, calendula, and rose.

Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties, these teas help soothe irritation and promote skin hydration. Chamomile, known for its calming effects, boasts flavonoids that maintain skin health.

Calendula flowers contain powerful antioxidants, reducing skin damage and supporting skin moisture. Rose petals offer antioxidants and vitamins, nourishing dry skin and protecting against environmental stressors.

Regularly consuming these herbal teas contributes to skin rejuvenation, enhancing suppleness, and alleviating dry skin discomfort.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea's anti-inflammatory properties, derived from its rich concentration of flavonoids and terpenoids, directly address dry skin by reducing inflammation.

This herbal infusion also contains antioxidants like vitamin B and vitamin E that hydrate skin cells and aid in skin regeneration. Furthermore, its ability to stimulate blood flow enhances nutrient supply to skin cells, promoting collagen production and fostering a healthy skin barrier. Chamomile tea's emollient qualities, which can be attributed to its essential oils, help lock in moisture, providing soothing relief to parched skin.

Finally, as a gentle natural remedy, chamomile tea avoids the harshness often associated with synthetic skincare products, ensuring a safer and more nurturing approach to managing dry skin.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with dry skin.

  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, chamomile flowers help soothe dry and irritated skin.
  • Leaves: Containing flavonoids and essential oils, chamomile leaves help moisturize and nourish dry skin.
  • Seeds: With a high concentration of antioxidants, chamomile seeds aid in reducing redness and flakiness caused by dry skin.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that help with dry skin.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid with antioxidant properties that helps reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, promoting skin hydration and elasticity.
  • Luteolin: A flavonoid with anti-inflammatory effects that helps soothe irritated skin, reducing redness and itchiness associated with dry skin.
  • Chamazulene: A terpene with anti-inflammatory and antipruritic properties that helps relieve itching, swelling, and irritation caused by dry skin.
  • Bisabolol: A sesquiterpene alcohol with skin-calming, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties that aids in soothing and healing dry, damaged skin.
  • Matricin: A lactone with anti-inflammatory effects that helps reduce skin irritation, redness, and discomfort associated with dry skin.
  • Alpha-bisabolol oxide A & B: Two oxides with skin-calming, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties that aid in soothing and healing dry, damaged skin.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for dry skin.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and add 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers.
  2. Steep the mixture for 5-10 minutes, then remove and discard the flowers.
  3. Let the tea cool down to a warm temperature.
  4. Optionally, add 1 teaspoon of honey for added skin benefits and taste.
  5. Drink the tea 1-2 times a day for a soothing effect on dry skin.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic calendula tea for dry skin.

  1. Gather 1-2 tablespoons of dried calendula petals and place them in a tea infuser or strainer.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the calendula petals.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  4. Remove the infuser or strainer to prevent over-steeping.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day for dry skin relief.

What is dry skin and how can it affect your life?

Dry skin is a common condition characterized by a lack of moisture in the top layer of the skin.

This leads to visible symptoms such as roughness, flakiness, and tightness. Prolonged dryness can result in redness, itching, and even cracking, which can cause discomfort and lead to infections. Therefore, dry skin impacts people's lives by causing physical distress and potentially affecting their self-confidence.

Furthermore, it requires consistent care and attention to maintain a healthy skin barrier and prevent further complications.

What other natural remedies help with dry skin?

For those seeking natural solutions to combat dry skin, several remedies prove consistently effective.

Coconut oil, a rich emollient, abundant in fatty acids, absorbs easily into the skin, providing intense hydration. Olive oil, another potent moisturizer, contains antioxidants like vitamin E, aiding in skin cell regeneration.

Shea butter, derived from the nuts of the shea tree, has demonstrated remarkable skin-softening properties due to its high fat content. Honey, a natural humectant, draws moisture from the air, maintaining skin hydration. Regular application of aloe vera, extracted from the aloe plant, soothes and hydrates dry skin while reducing redness and irritation.

Finally, Greek yogurt, when applied topically, provides a gentle exfoliating effect, eliminating dead skin cells, thereby enhancing the skin's ability to retain moisture, ultimately restoring suppleness and softness.

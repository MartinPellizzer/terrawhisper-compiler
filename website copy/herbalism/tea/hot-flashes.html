10 Best Herbal Teas For Hot Flashes

Herbal teas, particularly those made from plants like black cohosh, red clover, and chasteberry, are believed to help with hot flashes due to their estrogen-like properties.

These herbs can help balance hormone levels in the body, reducing the frequency and intensity of hot flashes. Additionally, certain teas like sage and valerian root have natural cooling and calming effects, which can further alleviate the discomfort associated with hot flashes. Furthermore, these herbal teas are a natural and caffeine-free alternative to traditional teas and drinks, making them a popular choice for women seeking relief from menopause symptoms.

However, it's important to note that the effectiveness of herbal teas for hot flashes can vary from person to person, and it's always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.

1. Black Cohosh

Black cohosh tea helps with hot flashes because it contains phytoestrogens, which are plant compounds that can mimic the effects of estrogen in the body.

During menopause, estrogen levels drop, leading to hot flashes, and black cohosh tea may help alleviate this symptom by compensating for the reduced estrogen. The herb is believed to interact with the hypothalamus, the part of the brain that controls body temperature, thereby reducing the intensity and frequency of hot flashes. However, the exact mechanism through which black cohosh exerts its effects is not fully understood and requires further research.

It's important to note that black cohosh tea should be used under the guidance of a healthcare provider due to potential side effects and interactions with other medications.

The following procedure explains how to make black cohosh tea for hot flashes.

  1. Gather 40-60 grams of dried black cohosh root and let it simmer in 1 liter of water for 20 minutes (Adapted from the University of Maryland Medical Center).
  2. Strain the tea to remove the root and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  3. Drink 1 cup of the black cohosh tea up to three times a day, between meals (University of Maryland Medical Center).
  4. Continue use for a few weeks to see if symptoms improve, and discontinue if no relief (Mayo Clinic).
  5. Consult with a healthcare provider before starting black cohosh tea to discuss potential risks and interactions with any current medications (Mayo Clinic).

2. Red Clover

Red clover tea helps with hot flashes because it contains isoflavones, which are plant-based compounds that can mimic the effects of estrogen in the body.

Isoflavones can help regulate the body's temperature, reducing the frequency and intensity of hot flashes. Additionally, red clover tea has been shown to improve cardiovascular health, which can also help alleviate hot flashes.

It's important to note that while red clover tea may help with hot flashes, it's not a substitute for hormone replacement therapy or other medical treatments. As with any supplement, it's always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting to drink red clover tea or taking red clover supplements.

Lastly, it's worth noting that the evidence for red clover's effectiveness in reducing hot flashes is mixed, with some studies showing benefits and others showing no effect.

The following procedure explains how to make red clover tea for hot flashes.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried red clover flowers and steep in 8 ounces of hot water for 10-15 minutes (steep longer for stronger tea).
  2. Strain the mixture to remove plant material.
  3. Sweeten with honey or preferred natural sweetener, if desired.
  4. Drink up to three cups daily, or as directed by a healthcare professional.
  5. Consult with a healthcare provider before using red clover for hot flashes, especially when taking other medications or have existing health conditions.

3. Dong Quai

Dong quai tea helps with hot flashes because it contains natural phytoestrogens, which can help to balance hormones in the body and reduce the frequency and intensity of hot flashes.

This is due to its ability to mildly stimulate estrogen receptors in the body, providing relief for menopausal symptoms. Additionally, dong quai has been found to improve circulation and relax blood vessels, helping to alleviate the sudden heat sensations and redness associated with hot flashes. Furthermore, it has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that can help soothe the body during these episodes.

However, it is important to note that dong quai may interact with certain medications and should be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

The following procedure explains how to make dong quai tea for hot flashes.

  1. In a pot, add 1 quart of water and bring it to a boil, then add 3-5 slices of fresh dong quai root (dried can also be used - use 2-3 pieces) and let it simmer for 20 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat, cover the pot and let it steep for an additional 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the dong quai pieces.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups a day, as needed, to help alleviate hot flashes.

4. Sage

Sage tea helps with hot flashes because it contains active compounds, such as salvianolic acids and rosmarinic acid, that have been shown to help regulate sweating and reduce hot flashes.

These compounds work by inhibiting the sweat glands and reducing the body's overall temperature, which can help alleviate the symptoms of hot flashes. Additionally, sage tea has been shown to have estrogenic effects, which can help balance hormone levels in the body and further reduce the frequency and severity of hot flashes. It is important to note that while sage tea can be helpful for some people, it is not a cure-all and may not work for everyone.

As with any natural remedy, it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new treatment.

The following procedure explains how to make sage tea for hot flashes.

  1. Heat 1 cup of water to boiling point (about 2-3 minutes in a microwave or stovetop).
  2. Crush 3-4 fresh sage leaves, then add to a teapot or infuser.
  3. Pour the hot water over the crushed sage leaves.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the sage leaves, and enjoy. (Optional: add honey or lemon to taste)

5. Licorice

Licorice tea helps with hot flashes because it contains an active compound called glycyrrhizin, which may help to normalize cortisol levels in the body.

Fluctuating cortisol levels are believed to be a factor in hot flashes, particularly in menopausal women. The tea's soothing and anti-inflammatory properties can also help to alleviate the discomfort associated with hot flashes. Furthermore, licorice tea is known to have estrogen-like effects, which can help to restore hormonal balance and reduce the frequency and intensity of hot flashes.

However, it's important to note that excessive consumption of licorice tea can lead to side effects such as high blood pressure and hormonal imbalances, so it should be consumed in moderation.

The following procedure explains how to make licorice tea for hot flashes.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried licorice root and place it in a tea infuser or teapot with a built-in strainer.
  2. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the licorice root and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Remove the tea infuser or cover the teapot to stop the steeping process.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste for improved flavor.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day as needed for managing hot flashes.

6. Chasteberry

Chasteberry tea helps with hot flashes because it contains compounds that may help regulate levels of hormones such as estrogen and progesterone in the body, which can fluctuate during menopause and cause hot flashes.

These compounds may also help reduce inflammation and promote circulation, both of which can alleviate symptoms of hot flashes. Additionally, chasteberry has been shown to have a balancing effect on the hypothalamus, the part of the brain that controls body temperature, sleep, and mood, which can help reduce the frequency and intensity of hot flashes. However, it's important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the mechanisms behind chasteberry's effects on hot flashes.

As with any supplement, it's always a good idea to talk to your doctor before starting chasteberry tea or any other new supplement regimen.

The following procedure explains how to make chasteberry tea for hot flashes.

  1. Heat 1 cup of water to boiling, then pour over 1-2 teaspoons of dried chasteberry berries; let steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the chasteberry pieces.
  3. Discard the used chasteberry material.
  4. Optional: add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink up to three cups of chasteberry tea daily, as needed, to help manage hot flashes.

8. Ginkgo Biloba

Ginkgo biloba tea helps with hot flashes because it contains phytoestrogens, which are plant compounds that can mimic the effects of estrogen in the body.

During menopause, estrogen levels drop, leading to hot flashes, and sipping ginkgo biloba tea can help alleviate this symptom by supplementing estrogen levels. Moreover, this herbal tea improves blood circulation, which may help regulate body temperature and reduce the intensity and frequency of hot flashes.

Additionally, ginkgo biloba is rich in antioxidants that can help reduce inflammation and stress, both of which can exacerbate menopausal symptoms. However, it's important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the mechanisms behind ginkgo biloba's effects on hot flashes.

As with any supplement, it's crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before incorporating ginkgo biloba tea into a daily routine.

The following procedure explains how to make ginkgo biloba tea for hot flashes.

  1. Heat 1 cup of water to boiling, then pour over 1-2 teaspoons of dried ginkgo biloba leaves and let steep, covered, for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the leaves.
  3. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste.
  4. Drink up to three cups per day, as needed, to help alleviate hot flashes.
  5. For best results, use high-quality, organic ginkgo biloba leaves and fresh water.

9. Ginseng

Ginseng tea helps with hot flashes because it contains natural phytoestrogens, which can mimic the role of estrogen in the body, a hormone that tends to decrease in women going through menopause and can cause hot flashes.

Additionally, ginseng has been found to regulate hormonal balance, reducing the intensity and frequency of hot flashes. Its anti-inflammatory properties also help alleviate stress and anxiety, common triggers of hot flashes. Furthermore, ginseng tea promotes better sleep quality, which can be disrupted by frequent hot flashes.

Lastly, ginseng's ability to enhance cognitive function and overall well-being may contribute to a decrease in hot flashes by reducing stress and promoting a sense of calm.

The following procedure explains how to make ginseng tea for hot flashes.

  1. Ingredients: Ginseng root (2 teaspoons) and water (4 cups), optionally add honey (1 teaspoon) and lemon (1 slice) for taste.
  2. Rinse ginseng root thoroughly, then cut into small slices or crush into small pieces.
  3. Add water to a pot and bring to a boil, then add ginseng and reduce heat to a simmer.
  4. Allow the tea to simmer for 20-30 minutes, then strain out the ginseng pieces.
  5. Add honey and lemon to taste, if desired, and drink while warm. Enjoy!

10. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea helps with hot flashes because it contains a flavonoid called silymarin, which is known for its ability to regulate hormonal fluctuations.

This compound helps to balance the levels of estrogen and progesterone in the body, reducing the intensity and frequency of hot flashes. Additionally, milk thistle has been found to have a positive effect on the liver, promoting the efficient metabolism of hormones and reducing the buildup of toxins that can exacerbate menopausal symptoms. Furthermore, milk thistle tea has natural anti-inflammatory and cooling properties, providing immediate relief from hot flashes and reducing overall body temperature.

Finally, regular consumption of milk thistle tea can also help to reduce stress and anxiety, which are common triggers of hot flashes, providing a holistic approach to managing menopausal symptoms.

The following procedure explains how to make milk thistle tea for hot flashes.

  1. Steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried milk thistle seeds in 8 ounces of boiling water for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and achieve a bittersweet taste.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the spent seeds for a smooth drinking experience.
  3. Discard the used milk thistle seeds or compost them to reduce waste.
  4. Optional: Add a natural sweetener like honey or stevia to taste, if desired, to balance the bitterness.
  5. Sip the milk thistle tea slowly, ideally 30 minutes before a hot flash is expected, to allow the herbal remedy to take effect.

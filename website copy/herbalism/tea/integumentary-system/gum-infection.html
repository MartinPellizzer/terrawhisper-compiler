10 best herbal teas for gum infection

Herbal teas, such as chamomile and calendula, offer a natural remedy for gum infection due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Chamomile tea's flavonoids and essential oils reduce inflammation, while calendula tea's antiseptic qualities help fight off harmful bacteria.

Both teas promote healthy gums and can soothe irritation, making them an excellent choice for those seeking a drug-free alternative to treat gum infection.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which directly address two causes of gum infection: inflammation and bacterial growth.

The chamazulene and levomenol in chamomile tea reduce inflammation by neutralizing free radicals, promoting gum healing. Furthermore, the antimicrobial properties of chamomile tea stem from its ability to damage bacterial cell walls, disrupt protein synthesis, and inhibit enzyme production, thus preventing the spread of gum infection. Chamomile tea's astringent qualities also tighten gum tissues, reducing the space where bacteria can proliferate.

Lastly, chamomile tea's soothing effect on oral mucous membranes provides relief from gum infection symptoms such as pain and swelling, making it an ideal natural remedy for gum infection treatment.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with gum infection.

  • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce gum inflammation.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants that promote oral health and help fight gum infections.
  • Seeds: Contain oils with antiseptic properties that aid in healing gum tissue and preventing infections.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with gum infection.

  • Chamazulene: This medicinal constituent has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and redness in gums caused by infection.
  • Bisabolol: Known for its antimicrobial properties, bisabolol helps eliminate harmful bacteria responsible for gum infections.
  • Apigenin: This flavonoid exhibits strong anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, aiding in treating gum infections.
  • Matricin: Matricin is a chemical compound that contains anti-inflammatory properties, reducing symptoms of gum inflammation.
  • Alpha-bisabolol oxide A: This constituent has potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, helping alleviate gum infection issues.
  • Matricin A: Matricin A converts to chamazulene when chamomile is dried or heated, providing anti-inflammatory benefits for gum infections.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for gum infection.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water for 3-5 minutes, resulting in a hot base for the tea.
  2. Pour the hot water over 1 rounded teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers, allowing the flowers to steep for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain out the chamomile flowers, discarding them while keeping the tea.
  4. Optional: Add a small amount of honey to sweeten the tea, preferably raw and local.
  5. Sip the warm tea slowly, aiming to rinse the entire mouthful for 30 seconds to 1 minute, repeating up to 3 times daily as needed.

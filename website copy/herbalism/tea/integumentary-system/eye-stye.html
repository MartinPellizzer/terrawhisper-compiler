1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties due to the presence of flavonoids and essential oils.

These components help reduce swelling and redness in the eye while fighting off the bacterial infection that causes a stye. Drinking chamomile tea boosts the immune system, which aids in the body's natural ability to heal. Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with eye stye.

  • Flowers: Contain antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and fight infection in eye styes.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and flavonoids that help soothe irritated eyes and promote healing in eye styes.
  • Seeds: Contain azulene, a compound known for its anti-inflammatory and soothing effects, making it effective in treating eye styes.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with eye stye.

  • Azulene: Has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and redness associated with eye stye.
  • Bisabolol: Possesses antimicrobial properties that assist in fighting the infection causing eye stye.
  • Chamazulene: Exhibits strong antioxidant effects, helping to protect the eye area from damage and inflammation.
  • Matricin: Converts into chamazulene, boosting its anti-inflammatory and soothing effects on eye stye.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for eye stye.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and add 1 heaping tablespoon of dried chamomile flowers.
  2. Steep the chamomile in the boiling water for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a warm temperature.
  5. Soak a clean washcloth in the tea and apply it to the affected eye for 10 minutes.

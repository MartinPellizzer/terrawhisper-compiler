10 best herbal teas for dry scalp

Here are three exceptional herbal teas for dry scalp: chamomile, calendula, and rosemary.

Chamomile tea offers anti-inflammatory properties that soothe the scalp, while calendula tea contains antioxidants that promote healing. Rosemary tea stimulates circulation, encouraging hair growth and alleviating dryness.

Regularly rinsing your hair with these herbal teas after cooling them down creates a nourishing hair care routine, providing your scalp with much-needed hydration and relief.

1. Sage

Sage tea addresses dry scalp through its natural properties.

This herbal remedy contains elements that moisturize and nourish the scalp, promoting hydration and preventing dryness. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties soothe irritation and help maintain a healthy scalp. Furthermore, sage tea's ability to stimulate blood flow enhances the distribution of nutrients, contributing to the scalp's overall well-being.

Ultimately, regular use of sage tea helps restore balance, making it an effective supplement to combat dry scalp.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in sage tea that help with dry scalp.

  • Leaves: Contain compounds that help reduce inflammation and promote scalp health.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants that support healthy cell function and moisturize the scalp.
  • Seeds: High in fatty acids and vitamins that nourish the scalp, promoting hydration and reducing dryness.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in sage tea that aid with dry scalp.

  • Rosmarinic acid: This compound possesses anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation and irritation caused by dry scalp.
  • Camphor: With its soothing and cooling effects, camphor helps moisturize and alleviate dryness on the scalp.
  • Ursolic acid: This compound exhibits anti-pruritic properties, reducing itchiness and discomfort caused by dry scalp.
  • Carnosic acid: Carnosic acid has antioxidant properties, protecting the scalp from environmental stressors and promoting healthy skin cell regeneration.
  • Oleanolic acid: This compound has emollient properties, softening and smoothing the scalp while locking in moisture and reducing dryness.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic sage tea for dry scalp.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh sage leaves or 2 tablespoons of dried sage and rinse them thoroughly.
  2. Boil 4 cups of water in a pot and add the sage leaves, then reduce heat to a simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a container and discard the sage leaves.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  5. Apply the tea to your scalp with a spray bottle or soaked cotton balls, leaving it on for 30 minutes before rinsing.

Rosemary tea helps with dry scalp due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

This herbal tea, when applied to the scalp, stimulates blood circulation, promoting hair growth and soothing irritated skin. Its rich concentration of nutrients, including rosmarinic acid and carnosic acid, nourishes the scalp and hair follicles, hydrating and moisturizing the dry scalp. Regular use of rosemary tea leads to improved scalp health, contributing to lustrous, dandruff-free hair.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in rosemary tea that help with dry scalp.

  • Leaves: Contain compounds that help stimulate hair growth and nourish the scalp, reducing dryness.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties, they help soothe an irritated scalp and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Stems: Provide astringent properties that can help balance scalp oils and alleviate dryness.
  • Seeds: Contain essential oils that moisturize the scalp, promoting hydration and reducing dryness.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in rosemary tea that aid with dry scalp.

  • Rosmarinic acid: Possesses anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe dry, itchy scalp.
  • Carnosic acid: Acts as an antioxidant, protecting the scalp from damage and promoting healthy cell growth.
  • Verbenone: Contributes to rosemary's antifungal properties, helping maintain a balanced scalp microbiome and preventing dandruff.
  • Camphor: Stimulates blood circulation, enhancing nutrient delivery to hair follicles and promoting a healthy scalp.
  • Caffeic acid: Boosts collagen production, ensuring a strong scalp foundation and reducing dryness.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic rosemary tea for dry scalp.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and add 1 tablespoon of fresh rosemary leaves, let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat and let the tea steep for an additional 5 minutes.
  3. Strain the rosemary tea to remove the leaves.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  5. Slowly pour the rosemary tea over your scalp, massaging it gently, and let it sit for 10-15 minutes before rinsing it out.

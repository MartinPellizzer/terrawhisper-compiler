10 best herbal teas for dandruff

Dandruff is a common issue that many people face, and herbal teas offer a natural solution.

Consider chamomile tea, which boasts anti-inflammatory properties that soothe the scalp, reducing redness and itching. Another excellent choice is green tea, rich in antioxidants that promote a healthy scalp and hair growth.

Lastly, consider sipping on sage tea, an herbal remedy known for its antimicrobial and antiseptic qualities, helping to maintain a balanced scalp and combat dandruff.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with dandruff due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The chamomile plant contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which have been shown to soothe the scalp and eliminate dandruff-causing fungi. When applied topically as a rinse, chamomile tea reduces inflammation and itching, providing relief from symptoms associated with dandruff. Furthermore, chamomile tea's calming effects promote healthy hair growth by nourishing the scalp and strengthening hair follicles.

Regular use of chamomile tea treatments reduces dandruff by maintaining a balanced and clean scalp.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with dandruff.

  • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe an itchy scalp and reduce dandruff.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, leaves aid in maintaining a healthy scalp and controlling dandruff.
  • Seeds: Packed with essential oils, seeds promote hair growth and alleviate dandruff symptoms.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with dandruff.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid with anti-inflammatory properties that helps reduce dandruff by soothing the scalp and decreasing inflammation.
  • Chamazulene: A chemical compound that possesses antimicrobial properties, combatting the fungal infection and bacteria causing dandruff.
  • Bisabolol: A terpene with soothing effects that alleviates skin irritation, reducing redness and itching associated with dandruff.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin exhibits anti-inflammatory effects that help decrease inflammation caused by dandruff.
  • Alpha-bisabolol oxide A & B: These two oxides derived from bisabolol show potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, helping control dandruff-causing microorganisms and soothing inflamed skin.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for dandruff.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water to 212°F (100°C) for 1-2 minutes to prepare chamomile tea.
  2. Steep 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers in boiling water for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove chamomile flowers, creating a dandruff-relieving rinse.
  4. Allow the chamomile tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  5. Pour the cooled tea directly onto a clean, damp scalp, massaging it gently for even distribution.

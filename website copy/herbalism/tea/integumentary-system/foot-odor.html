10 best herbal teas for foot odor

Several herbal teas, including chamomile, sage, and peppermint, boast natural properties that neutralize foot odor.

Chamomile's anti-fungal and anti-bacterial agents address the root cause of the issue, offering a long-lasting solution. Sage, rich in antibacterial and astringent properties, inhibits the growth of odor-causing microorganisms.

Lastly, peppermint's natural menthol qualities provide a refreshing scent while leaving feet feeling cool and rejuvenated.

1. Peppermint

Peppermint tea contains menthol, a natural antiseptic and antifungal agent that eliminates foot odor's primary sources: bacteria and fungi.

This herbal tea's antibacterial properties deodorize and disinfect feet, while its astringent qualities tighten pores and reduce perspiration. Furthermore, peppermint tea's cool and soothing sensation constricts sweat glands, decreasing sweat production.

Lastly, peppermint tea boasts anti-inflammatory properties that alleviate skin irritations and infections caused by foot odor, promoting overall foot health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in peppermint tea that help with foot odor.

  • Leaves: Contain menthol and menthone, which have antibacterial and antifungal properties that help neutralize foot odor.
  • Flowers: Have a strong aroma that helps mask the smell of foot odor, and contain flavonoids that have antibacterial properties.
  • Seeds: Contain essential oils that have antifungal and antibacterial properties which help eliminate the bacteria and fungi that cause foot odor.
  • Buds: Possess high levels of menthol, which has potent antibacterial properties that aid in fighting the bacteria causing foot odor.
  • Bark: Contains tannins that act as an astringent, which helps to reduce sweat production, and thus reduce foot odor.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in peppermint tea that aid with foot odor.

  • Menthol: Has antibacterial properties that help reduce the growth of odor-causing bacteria on the feet.
  • Menthone: Acts as a natural deodorant by neutralizing unpleasant odors.
  • Limonene: Possesses antifungal properties that aid in preventing fungal infections causing foot odor.
  • Menthofuran: Enhances menthol's cooling effect, providing soothing relief to feet.
  • Cineol: Provides anti-inflammatory effects, aiding in reducing foot inflammation associated with odor.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for foot odor.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water, then pour into a teapot.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of dried peppermint leaves to the teapot.
  3. Steep the peppermint leaves for 10 minutes, then strain the tea into a footbath.
  4. Soak feet in the peppermint tea for 20 minutes, twice daily.
  5. Refrigerate leftover tea for up to 24 hours, reheating for subsequent soaks.

  • Limonene: This cyclic monoterpene with a pleasant citrusy aroma has been shown to have antibacterial properties, helping control the bacterial populations causing foot odor.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic eucalyptus tea for foot odor.

  1. Gather 4-5 fresh eucalyptus leaves and rinse them under cold water to clean.
  2. Boil 4 cups of water in a pot, then add the eucalyptus leaves and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a bowl, discarding the leaves, and let it cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  4. Soak your feet in the eucalyptus tea for 15-20 minutes, ensuring they are fully submerged.
  5. Dry your feet thoroughly and discard the leftover tea. Repeat this process 2-3 times a week for best results.

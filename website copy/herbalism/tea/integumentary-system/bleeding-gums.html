1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with bleeding gums due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties derived from the chamomile flower.

These properties work by reducing inflammation in the gums, thereby decreasing bleeding. Chamomile tea also contains flavonoids, which strengthen blood vessel walls and further minimize bleeding. Additionally, drinking chamomile tea promotes the production of secretory IgA, an antibody that protects the mucous membranes in the mouth, contributing to overall oral health.

Lastly, chamomile's soothing effect aids in relaxing the gums, which can also help alleviate bleeding.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with bleeding gums.

  • Flowers: Contain flavonoids that have anti-inflammatory properties, helping to reduce gum inflammation and bleeding.
  • Leaves: Rich in tannins and essential oils that possess astringent and antimicrobial properties, aiding in healing and preventing gum infections.
  • Stems: Contain chamazulene, a chemical with anti-inflammatory benefits, which alleviates gum inflammation and reduces bleeding.
  • Rhyzomes: Have therapeutic effects due to their high content of phenolic compounds, and they help control gum bleeding and soothe irritation.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with bleeding gums.

  • Alpha-Bisabolol: A potent anti-inflammatory compound that reduces inflammation in the oral cavity, promoting tissue healing and alleviating bleeding gums.
  • Apigenin: A flavonoid with strong antimicrobial properties that helps inhibit the growth of bacteria responsible for gum infections, preventing bleeding gums.
  • Chamazulene: A powerful anti-inflammatory and antioxidant compound that helps soothe irritation, reduce inflammation, and protect gum tissues from oxidative damage.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin exhibits anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, providing relief from painful gums and reducing bleeding.
  • Quercetin: A flavonoid with potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, quercetin aids in reducing gum inflammation, preventing oxidative stress, and promoting gum tissue health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for bleeding gums.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water, then steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers.
  3. Let the tea cool down to a warm temperature.
  4. Rinse your mouth with the chamomile tea, gently swishing it around your gums for 30 seconds.
  5. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day, especially after meals.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric tea for bleeding gums.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water with 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder and a pinch of black pepper for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the turmeric liquid into a cup, discarding the solid residue.
  3. Add 1 teaspoon of raw honey and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice to the turmeric liquid, stirring well.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature, about 5-10 minutes.
  5. Sip and swish the tea around your mouth before swallowing, maximizing its contact with your gums.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea aids itchy ears due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling and irritation in the ear canal.

Its antimicrobial characteristics fight ear infections causing itchiness. Chamomile tea acts as a natural analgesic, alleviating pain associated with itchy ears. Furthermore, it contains tannins known to soothe irritated skin both inside and outside the ear.

Lastly, the warm temperature of chamomile tea, when used as a compress, helps to unblock the Eustachian tubes, minimizing itchiness and discomfort.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with itchy ears.

  • Flowers: Contain flavonoids and terpenoids that possess anti-inflammatory properties, helping to alleviate itchiness in ears.
  • Leaves: Rich in tannins and volatile oils, providing astringent and antimicrobial effects, beneficial for soothing inflamed ear canals.
  • Seeds: Contain essential oils with antipruritic (anti-itch) properties, helpful in reducing itchiness in ears.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with itchy ears.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that helps reduce inflammation and irritation in the ear canal.
  • Chamazulene: A chemical compound that possesses anti-inflammatory and antiphlogistic properties, helping alleviate itching and swelling in the ears.
  • Bisabolol: A sesquiterpene alcohol with anti-inflammatory and soothing effects, reducing itchiness and irritation in the ears.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin has anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties that help decrease ear discomfort.
  • Spiroether: A compound with anti-inflammatory effects that aids in reducing swelling and itchiness in the ear canal.
  • Hydroxychamazulene: A metabolite of chamazulene, hydroxychamazulene contributes to the overall anti-inflammatory activity of chamomile tea, helping alleviate itchy ears.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for itchy ears.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a kettle, reaching a temperature of 212 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the chamomile tea into a cup, discarding the spent flowers.
  5. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature before using it as a warm compress for itchy ears.

  • Rhizomes: Not used, as they refer to the horizontal stem of a plant, similar to stems.
  • Bark: Not used, as it is not a part of the Calendula tea plant.
  • Buds: Not used, as they are not typically used in teas for medicinal purposes.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in calendula tea that aid with itchy ears.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic calendula tea for itchy ears.

  1. Gather 1-2 tablespoons of dried calendula flowers and place them in a tea infuser or loose in a teapot.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the calendula flowers, allowing them to steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Cover the teapot or container to retain heat and essential oils.
  4. After steeping, remove the tea infuser or strain the loose flowers from the tea.
  5. Allow the tea to cool down to a warm temperature, then use a dropper to apply it gently to the affected ear, being careful not to burn the skin.

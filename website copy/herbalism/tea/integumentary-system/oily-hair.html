10 best herbal teas for oily hair

Several herbal teas, known for their oil-absorbing and clarifying properties, stand out as excellent choices for addressing oily hair.

These include chamomile, calendula, and nettle tea. Chamomile tea helps normalize sebum production while providing soothing anti-inflammatory benefits.

Calendula tea, rich in flavonoids, gently cleanses and soothes the scalp. Nettle tea, a potent astringent, reduces oiliness and promotes a balanced scalp environment.

Utilizing these natural herbal remedies not only addresses oily hair concerns but also fosters hair and scalp health.

1. Green Tea

Green tea's tannins and catechins naturally combat oily hair by gently stripping excess sebum from the scalp and hair follicles.

This herbal remedy also contains anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that soothe irritation and regulate scalp oil production. Furthermore, green tea's high antioxidant content promotes hair growth and provides a balanced environment for healthier, shinier hair. By rinsing hair with cooled green tea, the caffeine in it stimulates hair follicles, resulting in stronger strands and reduced oiliness.

Ultimately, green tea's natural compounds work synergistically to address the root cause of oily hair, making it an effective and sustainable solution for maintaining optimal scalp and hair health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in green tea tea that help with oily hair.

  • Leaves: Contain high levels of catechins and polyphenols that help regulate sebum production in the scalp.
  • Buds: Rich in epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG) and other catechins, which have anti-inflammatory properties that soothe and balance oily scalps.
  • Stems: Packed with antioxidants and flavonoids that naturally cleanse and remove excess oil from the hair and scalp.
  • Seeds: Contain linoleic and oleic acids, which nourish the hair and help control sebum production when used as a medicinal tea.
  • Flowers: High in vitamin C and E, providing nourishment and strengthening the hair follicles, while helping to balance sebum production.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in green tea tea that aid with oily hair.

  • Caffeine: Stimulates hair follicles, reducing excess oil production.
  • EGCG: Reduces sebum secretion, preventing greasy hair.
  • Theobromine: Promotes hair growth, reducing need for frequent washing.
  • Theaflavins: Possesses antimicrobial properties, eliminating bacteria causing oily hair.
  • Thearubigins: Acts as an antioxidant, protecting hair from damage caused by excess sebum.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic green tea tea for oily hair.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water and steep 2 bags of green tea for 15 minutes.
  2. In a separate container, mix 1 cup of cooled green tea with 2 drops of tea tree oil.
  3. Shampoo oily hair as normal, then rinse thoroughly.
  4. Pour the cooled green tea and tea tree oil mixture over hair, massaging into scalp.
  5. Leave on for 10 minutes, then rinse out with cool water.

  2. Remove rosemary from the pot and let the tea cool down to a warm temperature.
  3. Mix 2 tablespoons of apple cider vinegar into the rosemary tea.
  4. Shampoo your hair as usual, then pour the rosemary tea mixture over your hair, massaging it into your scalp.
  5. Leave the tea in your hair for 10 minutes before rinsing it out thoroughly.

  3. Let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes to extract sage's properties.
  4. Remove from heat and strain the liquid into a separate container.

  5. Leave the tea on your hair for 10-15 minutes before rinsing it out with cold water.

  2. Add 3 cups of water to the pot, bring to a boil, then simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid into a bowl, discarding the leaves, and let the tea cool.
  4. Mix 1 tablespoon of the cooled nettle tea with 1 cup of water in a spray bottle.
  5. Apply the mixture evenly to oily hair, let it sit for 15 minutes, then rinse thoroughly.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic calendula tea for oily hair.

  4. Strain the tea, discarding the solids, and let it cool.
  5. Massage the cooled tea into your oily hair, leave it on for 30 minutes, then rinse thoroughly.

10 best herbal teas for oily skin

By incorporating these herbal teas into your daily routine, you'll notice a significant improvement in your skin's overall clarity and radiance.

1. Green Tea

Green tea contains bioactive compounds, such as catechins and flavonoids, that exhibit potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help regulate sebum production, reducing the excess oil that clogs pores and leads to acne. Furthermore, green tea's antimicrobial properties inhibit the growth of Cutibacterium acnes, a bacterium responsible for causing acne. Additionally, green tea's anti-inflammatory effects soothe skin irritation, alleviating redness and swelling.

Lastly, green tea's antioxidants protect the skin from environmental stressors and damage, promoting overall skin health and combating the factors that exacerbate oily skin.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in green tea tea that help with oily skin.

  • Leaves: The leaves of green tea are rich in antioxidants that help to reduce the production of sebum, making it ideal for oily skin.
  • Buds: The buds of green tea contain high concentrations of catechins, known for their astringent properties that help to control excess oil on the skin.
  • Seeds: Green tea seeds are a natural source of vitamin E and fatty acids, which aid in maintaining the skin's natural barrier and reducing the appearance of oily skin.
  • Stems: Green tea stems are rich in flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe irritated skin and regulate sebum production.
  • Flowers: The flowers of green tea contain essential oils with antimicrobial properties, which can help to balance the skin's natural flora and reduce the appearance of oily skin.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in green tea tea that aid with oily skin.

  • Catechins: These are antioxidants that help reduce sebum production, which can help control oily skin.
  • Epigallocatechin Gallate: This catechin has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe irritated skin and reduce redness associated with oily skin.
  • Theanine: This amino acid provides anti-inflammatory benefits, helping to calm and soothe oily and acne-prone skin.
  • Chlorophyll: This pigment has anti-bacterial properties that can help control acne-causing bacteria, often a problem for those with oily skin.
  • Tannins: These astringent compounds help to reduce skin's oiliness and shrink the appearance of pores.
  • Vitamin E: This fat-soluble antioxidant helps protect the skin from free radical damage and supports skin healing, beneficial for oily skin prone to acne.
  • Vitamin C: This water-soluble vitamin aids in collagen production, enhances skin's overall health and complexion, and provides antioxidant protection.
  • Saponins: These natural emulsifiers provide a gentle cleansing effect, helping to remove excess oil and impurities from the skin.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic green tea tea for oily skin.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Steep 1 green tea bag in hot water for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Squeeze juice from 1/2 lemon into tea and stir.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey and mix well.
  5. Let the tea cool down, then drink, ideally 1-2 times a day for best results.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for oily skin.

  3. Strain the tea to remove the spent peppermint leaves.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. Sip the peppermint tea slowly, allowing it to soothe and balance your oily skin.

What is oily skin and how can it affect your life?

Oily skin is a condition characterized by an excess production of sebum, resulting in a shiny, greasy appearance.

This excess oil can lead to clogged pores and acne breakouts, lowering self-confidence and causing distress in social situations.

Oily skin impacts people's lives significantly, often leading to time-consuming and costly skincare routines and a constant need to blot or wash oily skin throughout the day.

What other natural remedies help with oily skin?

For individuals with oily skin, several natural remedies consistently prove effective.

First, a honey and cinnamon face mask regulates sebum production while providing antibacterial benefits. Simply mix two tablespoons of honey with one teaspoon of cinnamon, apply to the face, and rinse after 10-15 minutes. Second, diluted apple cider vinegar functions as a natural toner, balancing the skin's pH levels and tightening pores.

Combine one part apple cider vinegar with two parts water and apply with a cotton ball, avoiding the eye area. Lastly, incorporating oils such as jojoba, tea tree, or rosehip into a skincare routine can actually reduce oiliness. These oils mimic the skin's natural sebum, signaling the skin to produce less oil. Apply a few drops of the chosen oil to damp skin after cleansing.

Always perform a patch test before applying a new product to the face.

10 best herbal teas for taste changes

Chamomile tea, with its floral and slightly sweet flavor, offers a soothing effect on the mouth and throat, while licorice root tea has a naturally sweet taste that can help counteract taste changes.

1. Peppermint

Peppermint tea aids taste changes because it contains menthol, a natural compound that stimulates the trigeminal nerve, found in the mouth and throat.

This stimulation heightens the sensitivity of taste buds, enhancing their ability to detect and differentiate flavors. Moreover, peppermint tea soothes inflamed mouth tissues, a common cause of altered taste. Additionally, its cooling sensation can temporarily numb taste buds, providing relief from unusual or metallic tastes.

Lastly, peppermint tea's strong flavor can override unwanted tastes, acting as a palate cleanser.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in peppermint tea that help with taste changes.

  • Leaves: Contain menthol, a compound known to numb taste buds and reduce sensitivity to bitter tastes.
  • Flowers: Have a milder flavor than leaves, used to balance and sweeten the taste of medicinal tea.
  • Seeds: Rich in essential oils, provide a warm and aromatic flavor, helping to mask unpleasant tastes.
  • Bark: Contains unique compounds that add a distinct, earthy taste, making it suitable for modifying tea flavors.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in peppermint tea that aid with taste changes.

  • Menthol: Acts as a natural anesthetic, numbing the mouth and throat, reducing taste sensitivity.
  • Limonene: Enhances taste perception by stimulating saliva production and improving taste receptor function.
  • Methyl acetate: Helps restore taste sensitivity by promoting healing of damaged taste buds.
  • Pulegone: Stimulates the production of digestive enzymes, improving the taste of food by aiding digestion.
  • Rosmarinic acid: Possesses anti-inflammatory properties, reducing inflammation and swelling in the mouth and throat that can affect taste.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for taste changes.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water, reaching a full rolling boil for optimal extraction of peppermint flavors.
  2. Crush or tear 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves to release essential oils and improve taste.
  3. Steep the crushed leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes, allowing the water to absorb the peppermint flavors.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the spent peppermint leaves and ensure a smooth drinking experience.
  5. Serve the peppermint tea immediately while still warm to enjoy the soothing taste and potential taste change benefits.

  • Rhyzomes: Provide a unique, spicy undertone to medicinal teas, enhancing taste and aroma while delivering beneficial compounds.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with taste changes.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that binds to GABA receptors, reducing inflammation and anxiety, which can both contribute to taste changes.
  • Chamazulene: A chemical compound with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can alleviate taste changes caused by inflammation.
  • Bisabolol: A terpene alcohol known for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic effects, which can help with taste changes due to infection or inflammation.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can be beneficial in managing taste changes resulting from gastrointestinal issues.
  • Quercetin: A flavonoid with antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immune-boosting properties that can aid in reducing taste changes caused by oxidative stress or infection.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for taste changes.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water, reaching a temperature of 212 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers, allowing them to steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the chamomile flowers from the teapot.
  5. Serve the chamomile tea immediately, allowing it to cool before drinking to fully enjoy the taste.

  4. Strain the tea into cups, discarding the ginger pieces.

What is taste changes and how can it affect your life?

Taste changes refer to alterations in a person's ability to perceive flavors in food and drinks.

These changes occur due to various reasons such as aging, medication, illness, or exposure to certain chemicals. The impact of taste changes on people's lives can be significant, as it affects their enjoyment of food, nutrition intake, and overall quality of life. Individuals experiencing taste changes might consume less food and experience unintentional weight loss, malnutrition, or deficiencies in essential nutrients.

These changes can also lead to decreased social interaction, depression, and a diminished sense of well-being.

What other natural remedies help with taste changes?

Zinc supplements are a common and effective natural remedy for taste changes.

This essential mineral plays a critical role in the sense of taste, and deficiencies can lead to altered taste perception. Studies have shown that zinc supplementation can improve taste function in individuals with taste disorders. Another effective natural remedy is maintaining good oral hygiene.

Poor oral health, such as gum disease or tooth decay, can cause taste disturbances by affecting the taste buds and nerves responsible for taste sensation. Regular brushing, flossing, and dental check-ups can help prevent oral health issues and alleviate taste changes. Furthermore, ensuring adequate hydration helps maintain the health and function of taste buds.

Dehydration can lead to a decreased ability to taste, so drinking plenty of water throughout the day promotes optimal taste function.

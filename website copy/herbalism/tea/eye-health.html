10 best herbal teas for eye health

Eyebright tea, with its anti-inflammatory properties, helps to alleviate dryness and irritation. Meanwhile, turmeric tea contains curcumin, a potent antioxidant that shields the eyes from oxidative stress.

By incorporating these herbal teas into your daily routine, you'll be nurturing your peepers with the finest nature has to offer.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains antioxidants, like flavonoids, which directly benefit eye health.

These flavonoids protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals, preventing age-related macular degeneration and promoting overall eye health. Furthermore, chamomile tea's anti-inflammatory properties reduce inflammation in the eye, alleviating irritation and soothing strained eyes. The tea's antimicrobial properties also inhibit the growth of bacteria that can lead to eye infections.

Additionally, chamomile tea's ability to relax muscles, combined with its cool temperature when used as a compress, can reduce eye strain and fatigue.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with eye health.

  • Flowers: Chamomile flowers contain antioxidants that support eye health by fighting damaging free radicals.
  • Leaves: The leaves of chamomile have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe irritated eyes.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are rich in flavonoids, which are known to protect the eyes from UV radiation.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that help with eye health.

  • Apigenin: Apigenin has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals and inflammation.
  • Luteolin: Luteolin is known to help filter harmful blue light and protect the eyes from damage caused by UV radiation.
  • Alpha-bisabolol: Alpha-bisabolol has anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, which can help reduce redness and irritation in the eyes.
  • Chamazulene: Chamazulene has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help protect the eyes from oxidative stress and inflammation.
  • Matricin: Matricin is known to have anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties, which can help reduce eye discomfort and inflammation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for eye health.

  1. In a heatproof container, pour boiling water over 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers.
  2. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers, leaving behind the brewed tea.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a comfortably warm temperature.
  5. Gently apply the chamomile tea to a clean washcloth and place it over your closed eyes for 10-15 minutes.

Ginkgo Biloba tea contains antioxidants that protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals, according to scientific research.

This tea improves blood circulation, including the circulation in the eye area, providing essential nutrients and oxygen to eye tissues. Additionally, it helps reduce eye strain and inflammation, making it a popular natural remedy for eye health. Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginkgo biloba tea that help with eye health.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, leaves of Ginkgo Biloba are commonly used to make medicinal tea that supports eye health by potentially improving blood circulation in the eye area.
  • Seeds: Known for their anti-inflammatory properties, Ginkgo Biloba seeds can aid in reducing eye inflammation and promoting overall eye health when consumed as a medicinal tea.
  • Bark: Containing high levels of flavonoids, the bark of Ginkgo Biloba contributes to making medicinal tea that can help protect the eyes from oxidative stress, thus contributing to improved eye health.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginkgo biloba tea that help with eye health.

  • Flavonoids: These antioxidant compounds help protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals and support overall eye health.
  • Terpene Lactones: This group includes ginkgolides and bilobalides, which improve blood flow to the eyes and may help protect against age-related eye diseases.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginkgo biloba tea for eye health.

  1. In a tea infuser, place one rounded teaspoon (about 1-2 grams) of dried ginkgo biloba leaves.
  2. Pour boiling water (200-212°F) over the infuser, ensuring all leaves are saturated.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  4. Remove the infuser and discard the used leaves.
  5. Pour the tea into a cup and enjoy it while it's still warm, up to three times a day for optimal eye health benefits.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in bilberry tea that help with eye health.

  • Anthocyanins: These are powerful antioxidants that help protect the eye's retina from damage caused by free radicals, reducing the risk of age-related macular degeneration and cataracts.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic bilberry tea for eye health.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried bilberries and 8 ounces of boiling water; let steep for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove bilberry solids.
  3. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste.
  4. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day.
  5. Discard any leftover tea after 24 hours.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic rooibos tea for eye health.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down to 205 degrees Fahrenheit, as boiling water can damage the tea's antioxidants.
  2. Steep 1 teaspoon of loose rooibos tea or one rooibos tea bag in the hot water for 5-7 minutes to extract its maximum health benefits.
  3. Remove the tea bag or strain the loose tea leaves to prevent over-steeping and a bitter taste.
  4. Optionally, add a dollop of honey or a squeeze of lemon to enhance the tea's taste and antioxidant properties.
  5. Drink the rooibos tea warm or let it cool down and store it in the refrigerator for up to 3 days for a refreshing iced tea.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in saffron tea that help with eye health.

  • Crocin: This carotenoid in saffron tea has antioxidant properties that can help protect eye tissue from free radical damage and may reduce the risk of age-related macular degeneration.
  • Safranal: This volatile oil in saffron tea has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects that can help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the eyes, potentially preventing or slowing down the progression of eye diseases such as cataracts and glaucoma.
  • Picrocrocin: This compound in saffron tea has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can help protect the eyes from oxidative stress and inflammation, possibly reducing the risk of developing age-related macular degeneration and other eye conditions.
  • Kaempferol: This flavonoid in saffron tea has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects that can help protect the eyes from oxidative stress and inflammation, potentially reducing the risk of cataracts, glaucoma, and other eye diseases.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic saffron tea for eye health.

  1. In a small saucepan, heat 1 cup of water until boiling, then add 1 pinch (about 20-30 threads) of saffron strands and let it simmer for 2 minutes to extract the color and flavor.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of honey or sugar and 1/2 teaspoon of lemon juice to a cup for added health benefits and to balance the taste.
  3. Pour the saffron water through a strainer into the cup to remove the saffron strands.
  4. Stir well to dissolve the sweetener and mix it with the saffron-infused water.
  5. Drink the saffron tea while it's still warm, up to 2-3 times a day, for a few weeks, to promote eye health.

  4. Steep the nettle leaves in boiling water for 10-15 minutes to create a strong infusion.
  5. Strain the tea, discarding the leaves, and let it cool before drinking 1-2 cups daily for eye health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic dandelion tea for eye health.

  2. Separate the yellow petals from the green base and leaves, as the green parts can make the tea bitter.
  3. Heat 2 cups of water until boiling, then add the dandelion petals and let them steep for about 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the petals and discard them.
  5. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste, then drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day for eye health benefits.

What is eye health and how can it affect your life?

Eye health is the condition of our eyes to see, understand, and interpret the world clearly.

It involves maintaining good vision and protecting eyes from harm. Eye health directly impacts people's lives by enabling them to perform daily tasks with ease, such as reading, driving, and recognizing faces.

Furthermore, it allows individuals to experience the beauty of the world around them, promoting a higher quality of life.

What other natural remedies help with eye health?

Among the most common and effective natural remedies for maintaining eye health are a balanced diet, adequate hydration, and regular screen breaks.

Consuming a diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and fatty acids, particularly those high in Vitamin A, C, and E, has been shown to promote eye health. Foods such as carrots, leafy greens, and fish like salmon and tuna are excellent sources of these nutrients. Proper hydration is also vital, as it helps prevent dry eye symptoms and keeps the eyes lubricated.

Furthermore, taking regular breaks from electronic devices and following the 20-20-20 rule - every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds - can alleviate digital eye strain and fatigue.

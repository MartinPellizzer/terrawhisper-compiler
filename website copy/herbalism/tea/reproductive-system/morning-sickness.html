10 best herbal teas for morning sickness

Certain herbal teas, such as ginger and peppermint, bring proven relief for morning sickness.

Ginger tea, with its natural anti-inflammatory and digestive properties, effectively soothes stomach discomfort. Peppermint tea, known for its calming effects on the gastrointestinal tract, reduces nausea caused by morning sickness.

These herbal teas offer a natural and gentle solution for managing this common pregnancy symptom.

1. Ginger

Ginger contains active compounds, such as gingerols and shogaols, that possess potent anti-inflammatory and antiemetic properties.

These properties work together to alleviate gastrointestinal irritations, suppressing the vomiting center in the brain, and relaxing the smooth muscle of the gastrointestinal tract. As a result, ginger tea effectively inhibits the onset of nausea and vomiting, common symptoms of morning sickness during pregnancy, and provides soothing relief. Pregnant individuals can confidently consume ginger tea as a natural remedy since it boasts a rich history of safe and effective use in various traditional medicinal practices.

The hot liquid of the tea, in addition to ginger's active components, further helps to stimulate digestion, promote circulation, and encourage overall well-being.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in ginger tea that help with morning sickness.

  • Rhizomes: The rhizomes of the ginger plant are widely used in making medicinal tea for morning sickness due to their potent anti-emetic properties.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the ginger plant contain volatile oils and phenolic compounds that help alleviate nausea and vomiting during pregnancy.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the ginger plant are rich in bioactive compounds that help reduce inflammation, thereby providing relief from morning sickness symptoms.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the ginger plant are known for their carminative properties, which help soothe the digestive system and relieve symptoms of morning sickness.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginger tea that aid with morning sickness.

  • Gingerols: Gingerols are compounds in ginger that relax the smooth muscle of the gut, reducing nausea and vomiting.
  • Shogaols: Shogaols are compounds in ginger that act as anti-inflammatory agents, reducing inflammation in the gut and alleviating morning sickness.
  • Paradols: Paradols are compounds in ginger that act as antioxidants, protecting the gut from damage and reducing symptoms of morning sickness.
  • Zingerone: Zingerone is a compound in ginger that stimulates the production of digestive enzymes, improving digestion and reducing morning sickness.
  • Gingerdiols: Gingerdiols are compounds in ginger that increase the motility of the gut, reducing nausea and vomiting associated with morning sickness.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for morning sickness.

  1. Peel and slice 1 inch of fresh ginger, then bring 2 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan.
  2. Add sliced ginger to boiling water, reduce heat to low, and simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Strain the ginger tea into a cup, discarding the ginger pieces.
  4. Stir in 1 teaspoon of honey and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice.
  5. Drink the warm ginger tea to alleviate morning sickness.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for morning sickness.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to a rolling temperature of 200-212°F for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Crush 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves or use 1 peppermint tea bag, releasing its flavors.
  3. Combine the boiled water and crushed peppermint leaves or tea bag in a cup.
  4. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes to allow the water to extract peppermint's soothing properties.
  5. Remove the peppermint leaves or tea bag, then enjoy your hot or cooled peppermint tea throughout the day to alleviate morning sickness.

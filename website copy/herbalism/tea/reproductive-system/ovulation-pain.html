  • Seeds: Rich in essential fatty acids, particularly linoleic acid, which helps regulate hormonal imbalances and provide relief from menstrual cramps.
  • Leaves: Contain active compounds, such as vitexin and casticin, that help relax the uterus and reduce muscle spasms, providing relief from ovulation pain.
  • Bark: Has anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate ovulation pain and reduce inflammation in the reproductive system.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chasteberry tea that aid with ovulation pain.

  • Flavanoids: Flavanoids have anti-inflammatory properties that can alleviate the discomfort associated with ovulation.
  • Phenolic acids: Phenolic acids can help to regulate the menstrual cycle, reducing the severity of ovulation pain.
  • Iridoids: Iridoids have been shown to have analgesic effects, which can help to reduce the pain experienced during ovulation.
  • Essential oils: The essential oils present in chasteberry have antispasmodic properties that can help to relieve the muscle cramps associated with ovulation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chasteberry tea for ovulation pain.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water to 212°F (100°C) for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons (2-4 grams) of dried chasteberry herb to a teapot or infuser.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the herb and steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the chasteberry solids.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times daily during ovulation pain.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea aids in reducing jaw clenching due to its anti-inflammatory and muscle relaxant properties.

The active compounds in chamomile, such as bisabolol and chamazulene, possess these abilities. These compounds work together to alleviate muscle tension and inflammation in the jaw, thereby diminishing the frequency and intensity of jaw clenching. Furthermore, chamomile tea is known for its calming effects on the nervous system, which can help decrease stress and anxiety often associated with teeth grinding and jaw clenching.

Regularly consuming chamomile tea thus offers a natural, caffeine-free alternative to manage tension in the jaw.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with jaw clenching.

  • Flowers: Contain high levels of flavonoids, particularly apigenin, which relax the nervous system and help reduce jaw clenching.
  • Leaves: Rich in terpenoids and coumarins, known for their calming effects, promoting relaxation and alleviating tension responsible for jaw clenching.
  • Seeds: Packed with anti-inflammatory compounds, such as chamazulene and hydroxy-dimethyl-octadecanone, which help soothe muscle spasms and alleviate symptoms of bruxism.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that help with jaw clenching.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that binds to benzodiazepine receptors in the brain, reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation, which can help alleviate jaw clenching.
  • Chamazulene: A terpene with anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the jaw muscles, thus alleviating jaw clenching.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin also has anti-inflammatory effects, helping to soothe tight jaw muscles.
  • Bisabolol: A sesquiterpene alcohol with antispasmodic properties that can help relax muscle spasms, including those in the jaw.
  • Spiroether: A compound that contributes to the anti-inflammatory and muscle-relaxing effects of chamomile tea, providing additional relief for jaw clenching.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for jaw clenching.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to 212 degrees Fahrenheit, the optimal temperature for releasing chamomile's calming properties.
  2. Place one chamomile tea bag or 1 tablespoon of loose chamomile flowers in a heat-resistant container.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the tea bag or loose flowers, allowing it to steep for 5-7 minutes to extract the maximum benefits.
  4. Remove the tea bag or strain out the loose flowers to prevent over-steeping which can lead to a bitter taste.
  5. Sip the soothing chamomile tea, holding it in your mouth for a moment before swallowing, to allow the tea's compounds to start relaxing your jaw muscles.

What is jaw clenching and how can it affect your life?

Jaw clenching is a condition in which individuals unconsciously or consciously clench their jaw muscles, leading to tension and discomfort.

This behavior often occurs during periods of stress, anxiety, or concentration and can result in a variety of negative consequences. Chronic jaw clenching can cause severe jaw pain, headaches, and damage to teeth.

Over time, this condition impacts people's lives by limiting their ability to eat, speak, and engage in daily activities without discomfort or difficulty.

What other natural remedies help with jaw clenching?

One of the most common and effective natural remedies for jaw clenching is practicing relaxation techniques, such as deep breathing and progressive muscle relaxation.

These methods help reduce overall stress and anxiety levels, which can significantly contribute to jaw clenching. Another natural remedy is maintaining proper posture, as incorrect posture can lead to tension in the jaw and neck muscles.

Keeping the head in a neutral position and avoiding excessive slouching or hunching over can alleviate jaw clenching symptoms. Additionally, incorporating regular, gentle jaw stretches and massages into a daily routine can release muscle tension and improve mobility in the jaw, thus reducing clenching. Lastly, paying attention to dietary habits is crucial.

Avoiding hard or chewy foods, not chewing gum, and cutting food into smaller pieces can help reduce the strain on the jaw muscles and prevent clenching.

10 best herbal teas for urinary calculus

Nettle leaf, hydrangea root, and gravel root are herbal teas known to support urinary calculus treatment.

Nettle leaf offers diuretic properties, promoting kidney function and flushing out small stones. Regular consumption of these infusions, along with a healthy diet, ensures proper urinary tract function and overall well-being.

1. Hydrangea

Hydrangea tea helps with urinary calculus due to its diuretic properties, which promote increased urination.

This increased urination aids in flushing out the calculi or stones from the urinary tract. The tea contains compounds that inhibit the formation of new calcium oxalate crystals, a common component of urinary stones. Additionally, it contains antioxidants that support overall urinary tract health, further preventing the recurrence of urinary calculi.

  • Bark: Acts as a natural diuretic, increasing urine flow and aiding in the elimination of kidney stones.
  • Stems: Contain active compounds that inhibit the formation of crystals in the urinary tract, helping prevent new stone formation.
  • Roots: Have been shown to have anti-spasmodic effects, providing relief from pain associated with urinary calculi.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in hydrangea tea that aid with urinary calculus.

  • Kaempferol: Acts as a diuretic, increasing urination and helping to flush out small stones.
  • Quercetin: Reduces inflammation in the urinary tract, providing relief from pain caused by urinary calculi.
  • Myricetin: Inhibits the formation of calcium oxalate crystals, preventing the development of new urinary calculi.

  • Ellagic acid: Prevents the growth of bacteria, reducing the risk of urinary tract infections that can exacerbate urinary calculi.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic hydrangea tea for urinary calculus.

  1. Gather 10 dried hydrangea root pieces, totaling 30 grams, and rinse them under cool water.
  2. Place the hydrangea roots in a 1-liter pot and add cold water, then bring to a boil over high heat.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea, discarding the solids, and allow it to cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Consume the entire batch within 24 hours, storing any leftovers in the refrigerator.

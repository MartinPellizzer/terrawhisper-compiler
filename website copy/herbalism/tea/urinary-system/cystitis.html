Corn silk tea contains compounds such as steroids, saponins, and polyphenols that possess diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

These properties make corn silk tea effective in treating cystitis by increasing urine production, reducing inflammation in the urinary tract, and inhibiting the growth of bacteria causing the infection.   • Stems: Have tannins that help soothe and heal irritated tissues in the urinary tract.
  • Seeds: Contain fatty acids and oils that promote kidney health, supporting cystitis treatment.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in corn silk tea that aid with cystitis.

  • Flavonoids: Offer anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects, reducing pain and inflammation associated with cystitis.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic corn silk tea for cystitis.

  1. Rinse 2 cups of fresh corn silk under cold running water to remove any impurities.
  2. Place the rinsed corn silk in a pot and add 4 cups of water.
  3. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer the corn silk for 15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the corn silk, then let it cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Consume the tea while warm, up to 3 times a day for cystitis relief.

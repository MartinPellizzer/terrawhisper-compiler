10 best herbal teas for adrenal fatigue

For those seeking relief from adrenal fatigue, certain herbal teas stand out.

One such tea is Ashwagandha, an adaptogenic herb known to reduce fatigue and stress. Another is Licorice Root, which supports adrenal gland function and helps balance hormone production.

Holy Basil, a calming herb, reduces anxiety and boosts energy levels. Eleuthero, or Siberian Ginseng, enhances physical endurance and mental clarity. Lastly, Rhodiola Rosea, a powerful adaptogen, combats fatigue and improves cognitive function.

Incorporating these herbal teas into a daily routine aids in alleviating symptoms of adrenal fatigue.

1. Ashwagandha

Ashwagandha tea benefits individuals with adrenal fatigue due to its adaptogenic properties, which enable the body to cope with stress.

This herbal tea, derived from the ashwagandha plant, contains withanolides that aid in regulating cortisol levels, a hormone released during stress. Studies confirm that ashwagandha tea reduces stress-induced symptoms, such as anxiety and insomnia, common in adrenal fatigue. Furthermore, it enhances energy levels and mental clarity by nourishing the adrenal glands, promoting overall well-being.

By promoting a balanced endocrine system, ashwagandha tea directly addresses adrenal fatigue, providing long-term relief and restoration.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in ashwagandha tea that help with adrenal fatigue.

  • Roots: Contain withanolides, compounds that help reduce stress and anxiety, making them beneficial for treating adrenal fatigue.
  • Leaves: Rich in adaptogenic properties that support the adrenal glands, helping to regulate hormone production and alleviate fatigue.
  • Seeds: Packed with antioxidants and nutrients that nourish the adrenals, promoting overall adrenal health and reducing fatigue.
  • Flowers: Contain bioactive compounds that have a calming effect on the nervous system, helping to manage stress and support adrenal function.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ashwagandha tea that aid with adrenal fatigue.

  • Withanolides: These are the compounds responsible for the adaptogenic properties of ashwagandha, helping the body to cope with stress and reducing the impact of adrenal fatigue.
  • Choline: This nutrient supports the function of the adrenal glands and the nervous system, improving mental clarity and reducing fatigue.
  • Steroidal lactones: These compounds enhance the body's ability to resist stress and anxiety, combating the symptoms of adrenal fatigue.
  • Sitoindosides: These active compounds have anti-stress properties, reducing cortisol levels and alleviating the symptoms of adrenal fatigue.
  • Alkaloids: These compounds have a stimulating effect on the nervous system and adrenal glands, reducing fatigue and increasing energy levels.
  • Flavonoids: These antioxidant compounds protect cells from damage, reduce inflammation, and support the overall function of the adrenal glands.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ashwagandha tea for adrenal fatigue.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of ashwagandha root powder and reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Let the tea simmer for 10-15 minutes to extract the medicinal compounds.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the ashwagandha powder.
  5. Sweeten with honey or stevia and drink warm, up to three times a day for adrenal support.

4. Licorice

Licorice tea aids adrenal fatigue due to its active compound, glycyrrhizin, which prolongs the action of cortisol, the body's primary stress-response hormone.

This results in reduced adrenal gland stimulation and increased energy levels. Furthermore, glycyrrhizin inhibits the enzyme responsible for breaking down cortisol, ensuring adequate stress hormone levels for longer durations. Consequently, the body experiences less fatigue, and overall adrenal function improves.

Additionally, licorice tea's anti-inflammatory properties alleviate adrenal stress, contributing to improved adrenal health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in licorice tea that help with adrenal fatigue.

  • Roots: The roots of the licorice tea plant are rich in compounds that support adrenal gland function, making them an ideal choice for medicinal tea.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the licorice tea plant contain active compounds that help regulate cortisol levels, making them useful for treating adrenal fatigue.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the licorice tea plant are a great source of essential nutrients that nourish and support the adrenal glands, making them an important component in medicinal tea.
  • Bark: The bark of the licorice tea plant contains active compounds that aid in stress reduction and adrenal gland recovery, making it a key ingredient in medicinal tea for adrenal fatigue.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in licorice tea that aid with adrenal fatigue.

  • Glycyrrhizin: Acts as a potent anti-inflammatory, helping to reduce inflammation in the adrenal glands caused by chronic stress.
  • Liquiritigenin: Exhibits adaptogenic properties, helping the body adapt to stress and reducing the negative impact on the adrenal glands.
  • Glabridin: Regulates cortisol production, a hormone released in response to stress, thus supporting adrenal function.
  • Glycyrrhetinic Acid: Inhibits 11-beta-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase, an enzyme that converts cortisol to the inactive cortisone, thus increasing the availability of cortisol.
  • Isoliquiritigenin: Helps reduce oxidative stress, protecting the adrenal glands from damage caused by free radicals.
  • Formononetin: Possesses estrogenic properties, which may help regulate hormonal imbalances in women suffering from adrenal fatigue.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for adrenal fatigue.

  1. Pour 8 ounces of water into a saucepan and bring it to a boil.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of dried licorice root to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the licorice root.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day for adrenal fatigue.

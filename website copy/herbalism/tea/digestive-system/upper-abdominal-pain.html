10 best herbal teas for upper abdominal pain

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains anti-inflammatory compounds, such as bisabolol and chamazulene, which directly target and soothe inflamed tissues in the upper abdomen.

Moreover, its antispasmodic properties, particularly the apigenin compound, relax the smooth muscles of the digestive tract, thereby alleviating pain caused by cramps or spasms. Chamomile tea also promotes the production of bile in the liver, enhancing digestion and reducing the likelihood of upper abdominal discomfort after meals. Additionally, its calming effects on the nervous system can indirectly reduce abdominal pain by decreasing stress-induced tension in the gastrointestinal tract.

Lastly, chamomile tea's antimicrobial and antiviral properties help maintain a healthy gut microbiome, preventing potential infections that could cause abdominal pain.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with upper abdominal pain.

  • Flowers: Contain flavonoids and essential oils, known to have calming effects on the digestive system, making them ideal for addressing upper abdominal pain.
  • Leaves: Rich in antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties, leaves help alleviate abdominal discomfort and reduce pain.
  • Rhyzomes: Possess therapeutic compounds that can soothe gastrointestinal irritation, providing relief for upper abdominal pain.
  • Seeds: Packed with anti-inflammatory and carminative properties that aid in digestion, making them beneficial in managing upper abdominal pain.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with upper abdominal pain.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid with antispasmodic properties that reduces smooth muscle spasms in the gut, alleviating upper abdominal pain.
  • Chamazulene: A volatile oil with anti-inflammatory effects that helps decrease inflammation in the digestive tract, thereby easing upper abdominal discomfort.
  • Bisabolol: A sesquiterpene alcohol with anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that helps relieve irritation in the gastrointestinal tract, reducing upper abdominal pain.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that help alleviate pain in the upper abdomen.
  • Luteolin: A flavonoid with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects that protect the gastrointestinal tract and help reduce upper abdominal pain.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for upper abdominal pain.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water to 212°F (100°C) for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon (2 grams) of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers and steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser to prevent over-steeping.
  5. Consume the chamomile tea up to 3 times a day for upper abdominal pain relief.

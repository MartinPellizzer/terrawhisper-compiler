What is vomiting and how can it affect your life?

Vomiting is the forceful expulsion of stomach contents through the mouth.

This involuntary reflex occurs when the body tries to rid itself of toxins or harmful substances. Frequent or severe vomiting can lead to dehydration, malnutrition, and electrolyte imbalances, negatively impacting a person's daily life. It can cause weakness, dizziness, and disrupt regular activities, often requiring medical attention.

In extreme cases, vomiting can even be life-threatening if left untreated.

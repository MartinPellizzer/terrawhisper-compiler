The tea's natural sweetness also stimulates the production of saliva, which can help keep the mouth moisturized and hydrated.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in marshmallow that aid with dry mouth.

  • Emollient: Marshmallow tea forms a protective layer on mucous membranes, retaining moisture and alleviating dry mouth symptoms. The active constituents responsible for this property are polysaccharides and flavonoids.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Marshmallow tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the mouth and throat, providing relief from dry mouth symptoms. This is attributed to the antioxidant compounds, especially flavonoids and phenolic acids.
  • Increased saliva production: Marshmallow tea can stimulate saliva production in the mouth, helping to keep it moist and combat dry mouth. This effect is facilitated by the polysaccharides and flavonoids that encourage saliva production.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts of marshmallow that help with dry mouth.

  • Flowers: Contain mucilage, which coats and soothes dry oral tissues.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for dry mouth.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let 2 chamomile tea bags steep for 10 minutes.
  2. Stir in 1 tablespoon of organic honey until fully dissolved.
  3. Add 10 large marshmallows to the hot tea and stir until completely melted.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any marshmallow residue.
  5. Serve the marshmallow tea immediately for best results.

10 Best Herbal Teas For Headaches

Herbal teas, especially those made from ingredients known for their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, like chamomile, feverfew, and ginger, can help alleviate headaches by reducing inflammation and muscle tension in the head and neck.

Some herbal teas also contain compounds that help regulate blood flow to the brain, which can help relieve headaches caused by vascular issues. Additionally, the calming and relaxing effects of certain herbal teas can help reduce stress and anxiety, both of which are common headache triggers. Furthermore, herbal teas are a caffeine-free alternative to traditional tea and coffee, and caffeine withdrawal is a common cause of headaches.

Lastly, staying hydrated is crucial for preventing headaches, and sipping on herbal tea throughout the day can help ensure you're getting enough fluids.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with headaches because it contains active compounds such as apigenin and bisabolol, which have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Apigenin binds to certain receptors in the brain that reduce inflammation and help alleviate pain. Bisabolol also has antispasmodic effects, which can help relieve tension in muscles surrounding blood vessels, reducing the likelihood of headaches caused by muscle strain. Chamomile tea is a natural and safe alternative to over-the-counter pain medications for those experiencing occasional headaches.

Additionally, chamomile tea is known for its calming properties, making it an effective remedy for tension headaches that may be caused by stress or anxiety.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for headaches.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down for a couple of minutes until it reaches a temperature of around 205 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers in the hot water for 5-10 minutes, depending on how strong you want the tea to be.
  3. Strain the chamomile flowers from the tea, squeezing out any excess liquid.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Sip the chamomile tea slowly, allowing it to soothe and relax you, which may help alleviate headache symptoms.

2. Feverfew

Feverfew tea helps with headaches because it contains parthenolide, an active compound that inhibits the release of prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances responsible for inflammation and pain in blood vessels in the brain.

The traditional use of feverfew tea for migraines dates back to ancient Rome and Greece, where it was believed to be an effective remedy for headaches and fevers. Modern studies have shown that consuming feverfew tea regularly can help prevent migraines and reduce their severity when they do occur. Additionally, feverfew tea may also provide relief from tension headaches by relaxing blood vessels and reducing muscle tension in the neck and shoulders.

The suggested dose of feverfew tea for preventing headaches is 1-2 cups per day, but it's important to note that individual responses may vary, and long-term use should be under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

The following procedure explains how to make feverfew tea for headaches.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down to 200°F (93°C).
  2. Crush or chop 1-2 grams of feverfew leaves and add them to a tea infuser.
  3. Place the tea infuser with feverfew leaves in a teapot and pour the hot water over it.
  4. Steep the feverfew tea for 5-10 minutes, then remove the infuser.
  5. Drink the feverfew tea slowly, up to 3 times a day, as needed for headache relief.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with headaches because it contains gingerol, a natural compound that possesses anti-inflammatory properties.

When consumed, ginger tea can help reduce inflammation and relieve the pain associated with headaches. Additionally, ginger tea has been shown to improve blood flow, which can help alleviate tension and pressure in the head. Furthermore, ginger tea contains antioxidants that can help protect the body from free radicals and oxidative stress, which can contribute to the development of headaches.

Lastly, ginger tea is a natural alternative to over-the-counter pain medications and offers additional health benefits such as aiding digestion and reducing nausea.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for headaches.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root, then bruise the slices with a heavy object to release their oils.
  2. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan, then add the bruised ginger slices and reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Allow the ginger to simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the water has taken on a rich, golden hue and the ginger's flavor has infused into the liquid.
  4. Strain the ginger pieces from the water and add the juice of half a lemon and honey to taste, stirring until fully combined.
  5. Serve the ginger tea hot, sipping slowly throughout the day as needed to alleviate headache symptoms.

4. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with headaches because it contains menthol, an organic compound that provides a cooling sensation when consumed.

This effect is due to the stimulation of cold and menthol receptors on the skin and in the mouth, which can help reduce the perception of pain. Additionally, peppermint tea has anti-inflammatory properties, making it effective at reducing inflammation that may be contributing to headache symptoms. The calming effects of peppermint tea, which are attributed to its aroma and flavonoids, can also help alleviate stress and tension that can lead to or worsen headaches.

Furthermore, dehydration is a common cause of headaches, and the caffeine-free nature of peppermint tea makes it an ideal hydrating beverage choice for those suffering from headaches.

The following procedure explains how to make peppermint tea for headaches.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan or kettle.
  2. Crush or tear 1-2 peppermint leaves (fresh or dried) to release oils.
  3. Once water is boiling, pour it over the peppermint leaves in a teapot or mug.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes, depending on desired strength.
  5. Strain out the peppermint leaves and enjoy your soothing peppermint tea.

5. Willow Bark

Willow bark tea helps with headaches because it contains salicin, which is a natural analgesic and anti-inflammatory compound.

The human body converts salicin into salicylic acid, which works to reduce inflammation and relieve pain in the blood vessels of the head and neck that cause headaches. Additionally, willow bark tea contains other compounds such as flavonoids and tannins that act as antioxidants, fighting off free radicals and reducing inflammation throughout the body. The tea is also caffeine-free, making it a better option than over-the-counter pain medication for those who are sensitive to caffeine or want to avoid its side effects.

Lastly, willow bark tea has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for various ailments including fever, inflammation, and of course, headaches.

The following procedure explains how to make willow bark tea for headaches.

  1. Boil 1-2 teaspoons of dried willow bark in 8 ounces of water for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Filter the liquid to remove any bark pieces, resulting in willow bark tea.
  3. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Drink the willow bark tea slowly, sipping it over time.
  5. Repeat as needed, but do not consume more than 240 mg of salicin (the active compound in willow bark) in a day.

6. Butterbur

Butterbur tea helps with headaches because it contains compounds called petasins and isofraxidin, which have been shown to reduce the production of leukotrienes – molecules known to cause inflammation in blood vessels surrounding the brain.

By decreasing inflammation, butterbur tea can help alleviate symptoms associated with migraines and tension headaches, such as pain, nausea, and sensitivity to light or sound. Additionally, a study published in the European Journal of Neurology found that taking butterbur extract regularly led to a significant reduction in the frequency and severity of migraines compared to a placebo group.

Furthermore, butterbur tea has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that can help relax muscles and improve blood flow throughout the body, potentially contributing to its effectiveness in relieving headache pain.

The following procedure explains how to make butterbur tea for headaches.

  1. Heat 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove until it reaches a boil.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried butterbur root to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solids.
  5. Drink the tea while it is still warm, up to three times a day as needed for headache relief.

7. Raspberry Leaf

Raspberry leaf tea helps with headaches because it contains magnesium and flavonoids, which are both essential minerals and antioxidants that can help alleviate the symptoms of tension headaches.

The tea also acts as a natural diuretic, helping to flush out toxins from the body and reduce inflammation. Additionally, raspberry leaf tea contains fragrances and flavors that have a calming effect on the mind and body, reducing stress and anxiety which can contribute to headaches.

Finally, studies have shown that raspberry leaf tea may help improve blood flow to the brain, providing relief for those experiencing migraines or other types of vascular headaches.

The following procedure explains how to make raspberry leaf tea for headaches.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan or kettle.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried raspberry leaf (depending on desired strength) to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Once water has reached boiling point, pour it over the raspberry leaf in the teapot or infuser.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes, then remove the infuser or strain the tea.
  5. Drink the raspberry leaf tea up to 3 times a day as needed to help alleviate headaches.

8. Lavender

Lavender tea helps with headaches because it contains compounds such as linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known to have analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

When consumed, the soothing aroma of lavender is believed to help alleviate tension in the muscles around the head and neck, contributing to the relief of headache pain. Additionally, lavender tea has been shown to reduce anxiety and stress, which are common triggers for headaches. Furthermore, it's a natural caffeine-free alternative to other headache remedies like coffee or over-the-counter medication.

Lastly, drinking lavender tea is an easy, convenient way to enjoy its benefits at home without any negative side effects.

The following procedure explains how to make lavender tea for headaches.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down until it reaches a comfortable drinking temperature.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried lavender flowers or 2-3 lavender tea bags to the water.
  3. Steep the lavender in the water for 5-10 minutes to allow the beneficial compounds to be released.
  4. Strain the liquid to remove the lavender flowers or tea bags.
  5. Sip the lavender tea slowly, allowing it to soothe and potentially help alleviate headache symptoms.

9. Passionflower

Passionflower tea helps with headaches because it contains compounds that interact with the brain to alleviate pain and inflammation.

These compounds include flavonoids, such as baicalin and quercetin, which have been shown to reduce inflammation and relax tense muscles. Additionally, passionflower tea has been found to increase levels of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a neurotransmitter that helps regulate nerve activity and calm the central nervous system.

By promoting relaxation and reducing inflammation, passionflower tea can help relieve tension headaches and migraines, providing natural relief for those who prefer to avoid over-the-counter medications.

The following procedure explains how to make passionflower tea for headaches.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water to boiling point (212°F or 100°C).
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons (2-4 grams) of dried passionflower to a teapot or infuser.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the passionflower and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent passionflower.
  5. Drink the tea slowly, sipping it gently, to enjoy its full flavor and potential benefits for headaches.

10. Valerian Root

Valerian root tea helps with headaches because it contains compounds called valerenic acid and sesquiterpenes, which have been shown to have analgesic (pain-relieving) properties.

The tea also has anti-inflammatory effects, which can help reduce inflammation in the blood vessels of the head, thus reducing the frequency and severity of headaches. Moreover, valerian root tea's sedative properties can help alleviate tension and stress, which are common triggers for headaches. Additionally, the tea's calming effect on the nervous system may prevent headaches caused by anxiety or nervousness.

Finally, some studies suggest that regular consumption of valerian root tea may help prevent headaches altogether by maintaining a stable balance of neurotransmitters in the brain, ultimately reducing the likelihood of headache occurrence.

The following procedure explains how to make valerian root tea for headaches.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried valerian root (Valeriana officinalis) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to a simmer, cover the saucepan, and let the mixture steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the valerian root solids.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and drink the tea slowly, sipping it gently as needed to alleviate headache symptoms.

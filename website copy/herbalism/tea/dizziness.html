10 Best Herbal Teas For Dizziness

Herbal teas, particularly those made from ginger, chamomile, and lavender, can help alleviate dizziness due to their natural calming and soothing properties.

Ginger tea, for instance, is known to reduce inflammation and improve circulation, which can help stabilize the body and reduce feelings of dizziness. Chamomile tea has calming effects on the central nervous system, helping to reduce anxiety and relax muscles, thereby alleviating dizziness caused by stress or tension.

Lavender tea, with its soothing aroma and calming properties, can help reduce anxiety and promote relaxation, which can help alleviate dizziness. Additionally, many herbal teas contain antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that can help reduce inflammation and improve overall health, which can indirectly help reduce feelings of dizziness.

However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can help alleviate dizziness in some cases, they should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, especially if dizziness is a symptom of an underlying medical condition.

1. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with dizziness because it contains gingerol, a compound with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce inflammation and improve circulation, both of which may contribute to feelings of dizziness.

Additionally, ginger is known to help alleviate nausea and vomiting, common symptoms associated with vertigo or motion sickness. The warm temperature of the tea can also help to relax stiff or tense neck muscles, which can sometimes cause or worsen feelings of dizziness. Furthermore, ginger tea has been shown to help regulate blood sugar levels, which can fluctuate and cause dizziness in some individuals.

Finally, ginger's natural calming and soothing effects may help to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation, which can be beneficial for those experiencing chronic dizziness or vertigo.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for dizziness.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1 inch of fresh ginger root, then combine in a pot with 4 cups of water and bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the ginger to release its medicinal properties.
  3. Strain the ginger tea into a cup, add honey or lemon to taste, then mix in 1/2 teaspoon of cinnamon powder for added dizziness relief.
  4. Warm up the tea if necessary, then sip slowly, allowing the soothing warmth to calm your system and alleviate symptoms.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of this ginger tea daily for several days, or as needed, to manage dizziness spells.

2. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with dizziness because it contains calming properties that can help reduce anxiety and relax muscles, which can often be causes of dizziness.

The anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties of chamomile may also help to alleviate inner ear inflammation or muscle spasms that can lead to feelings of dizziness. Additionally, chamomile tea has been shown to improve circulation, which can help increase oxygen and nutrient supply to the brain, reducing the likelihood of experiencing dizziness. Furthermore, chamomile's natural sedative effects can help promote better sleep, which can also contribute to a reduction in dizziness symptoms.

Lastly, chamomile tea is a natural and caffeine-free alternative to other beverages that may exacerbate feelings of dizziness, making it a great choice for those looking for a gentle and soothing drink.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for dizziness.

  1. To make chamomile tea for dizziness, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers (or 1 tea bag) to a teapot.
  2. Let the chamomile flowers steep in the hot water for about 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, then add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  4. For best results, drink the chamomile tea while it's still warm, 2-3 times a day, especially before bedtime.
  5. Consult with a healthcare professional if dizziness persists for more than a few days, as it may be a sign of a more serious underlying condition.

3. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with dizziness because it contains menthol, a natural compound known to promote blood flow to the brain, which can help alleviate symptoms of lightheadedness and disorientation.

Additionally, peppermint tea has calming properties that can help reduce anxiety and stress, both of which can contribute to feelings of dizziness. Furthermore, peppermint tea is a natural anti-inflammatory and can help soothe inflamed blood vessels, allowing for better circulation and reducing the likelihood of dizziness. Moreover, peppermint tea has been shown to improve digestion and reduce symptoms of nausea, which can also contribute to feelings of dizziness.

Finally, peppermint tea is a caffeine-free alternative to other hot beverages, making it a healthier choice for those looking to reduce their caffeine intake and avoid the jittery side effects that can exacerbate feelings of dizziness.

The following procedure explains how to make peppermint tea for dizziness.

  1. To make peppermint tea for dizziness, start by boiling 2 cups of water in a saucepan or kettle.
  2. Add 1 heaping teaspoon (about 2 grams) of dried peppermint leaves to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Once the water has reached a boil, pour it over the peppermint leaves and let the tea steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, then let it cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Sip the peppermint tea slowly, allowing it to soothe and potentially alleviate symptoms of dizziness.

4. Lemon Balm

Lemon balm tea helps with dizziness because it contains rosmarinic acid, which has been shown to have anti-vertigo properties, meaning it can help to alleviate symptoms of dizziness and balance disorders.

Additionally, lemon balm has natural calming and sedative effects, which can help to reduce anxiety and stress that may exacerbate feelings of dizziness. Furthermore, lemon balm has antispasmodic properties that can help to relax muscles and reduce muscle spasms, which can contribute to feelings of dizziness and vertigo. The tea also has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the body, both of which have been linked to dizziness and balance problems.

Finally, lemon balm tea is a natural and caffeine-free alternative to other beverages, making it a gentle and soothing option for those experiencing dizziness.

The following procedure explains how to make lemon balm tea for dizziness.

  1. To make lemon balm tea for dizziness, start by boiling 2 cups of water in a saucepan, then remove it from heat and add 2 teaspoons of dried lemon balm leaves or one tea bag.
  2. Allow the mixture to steep for 5-10 minutes, depending on how strong you want the tea to be.
  3. While the tea is steeping, squeeze half a lemon into a cup to get its juice.
  4. After steeping, strain the tea into the cup with lemon juice.
  5. Finally, drink the lemon balm tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day as needed, to help alleviate symptoms of dizziness.

5. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with dizziness because it contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which can help to regulate the balance of fluids and electrolytes in the body, reducing the likelihood of experiencing dizziness caused by dehydration or electrolyte imbalances.

Additionally, licorice root has anti-inflammatory properties that may help to reduce any swelling or inflammation in the inner ear, which can contribute to feelings of dizziness. Furthermore, licorice root tea may help to soothe the stomach and reduce symptoms of nausea, which can sometimes accompany dizziness. It's important to note that while licorice root tea may help with occasional bouts of dizziness, it should not be used as a long-term solution, as excessive consumption of licorice root can lead to serious side effects.

As with any natural remedy, it's always a good idea to speak with a healthcare provider before incorporating licorice root tea into your routine.

The following procedure explains how to make licorice root tea for dizziness.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan or kettle.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the licorice root and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain out the licorice root.
  5. Drink the tea slowly, up to three times a day as needed.

6. Cinnamon

Cinnamon tea helps with dizziness because it contains certain compounds that can help regulate blood sugar levels, which can be a cause of dizziness if they fluctuate too much.

Additionally, cinnamon has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help improve circulation and reduce inflammation, both of which can contribute to feelings of dizziness. Furthermore, cinnamon tea can help soothe the stomach and alleviate digestive issues, which can also cause dizziness in some cases. Additionally, staying hydrated is important for maintaining balance and preventing dizziness, and sipping on warm cinnamon tea can help with this.

Finally, while more research is needed, some studies suggest that cinnamon may have a positive effect on cognitive function, which could potentially help reduce feelings of dizziness and disorientation.

The following procedure explains how to make cinnamon tea for dizziness.

  1. In a small pot, bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add 1 teaspoon of cinnamon powder or 1 cinnamon stick, reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat, add 1 teaspoon of honey (or to taste) and 1 teaspoon of lemon juice (optional, for added vitamin C and flavor), stir well.
  3. Cover the pot and let it steep for 5 more minutes to allow flavors to meld.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding any solids.
  5. Sip slowly, allowing the tea to gently coat the throat and soothe any dizziness or discomfort. Enjoy up to three cups a day as needed.

7. Turmeric

Turmeric tea helps with dizziness because it contains curcumin, a compound with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that may help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the inner ear, which can cause dizziness.

Additionally, curcumin may help improve blood flow and circulation, ensuring that the brain and inner ear receive adequate oxygen and nutrient supply, thereby reducing dizziness. Turmeric tea may also help regulate blood pressure, preventing sudden drops or spikes that can trigger dizziness. Furthermore, turmeric's calming and soothing properties may help alleviate anxiety and stress, common triggers of dizziness.

Lastly, turmeric tea has been shown to boost the immune system, helping the body fight off infections and inflammations that can lead to dizziness.

The following procedure explains how to make turmeric tea for dizziness.

  1. In a saucepan, bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add 1/2 teaspoon of ground turmeric and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea into a cup, then add 1/2 teaspoon of honey and 1/4 teaspoon of fresh lemon juice for flavor and additional health benefits.
  3. For an optional caffeine-boost, add 1/2 teaspoon of green tea leaves or a bag of green tea to the saucepan during step 1.
  4. Stir well to dissolve the honey and mix all ingredients, then let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat your mouth and throat, and enjoy the soothing effects of this natural remedy for dizziness.

8. Ashwagandha

Ashwagandha tea helps with dizziness because it is an adaptogenic herb that has been shown to reduce symptoms of anxiety and stress, which can be contributing factors to feelings of dizziness.

The herb has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for centuries to help balance the body and promote overall well-being. Ashwagandha contains compounds that may help to lower cortisol levels, reducing the body's stress response and promoting a sense of calm and stability. Additionally, ashwagandha has been shown to improve brain function and memory, which can help to alleviate symptoms of dizziness caused by cognitive or neurological issues.

Finally, ashwagandha tea may also help to improve circulation and reduce inflammation, both of which can contribute to feelings of dizziness.

The following procedure explains how to make ashwagandha tea for dizziness.

  1. In a tea pot, add boiling water (2 cups) to 1 teaspoon of dried ashwagandha root and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the ashwagandha root and discard it.
  3. Add honey (1-2 teaspoons) or lemon juice (1/2 lemon) to taste, if desired.
  4. Warm up the tea, if necessary, and drink it slowly.
  5. Repeat the process 1-2 times a day, as needed, to help alleviate dizziness.

9. Passionflower

Passionflower tea helps with dizziness because it contains flavonoids, such as apigenin and luteolin, which have been found to have anxiety-reducing and mild sedative effects on the body.

These properties can help to reduce symptoms of anxiety and stress, which are common triggers of dizziness. Additionally, passionflower may help to regulate the balance of neurotransmitters in the brain, such as gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), which can become unbalanced and contribute to feelings of dizziness. Furthermore, passionflower tea has been shown to have a positive impact on blood pressure, helping to improve circulation and reduce the likelihood of experiencing dizziness due to low blood pressure.

However, it is important to note that passionflower tea should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment for dizziness, and individuals should consult with a healthcare provider before using passionflower tea for this purpose.

The following procedure explains how to make passionflower tea for dizziness.

  1. Steep 1 teaspoon of dried passionflower (Passiflora incarnata) leaves in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-10 minutes; use 1-2 teaspoons for a stronger tea.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the passionflower leaves, discarding the spent plant material.
  3. For dizziness, consider adding honey or lemon to taste, as these can help soothe symptoms and improve flavor.
  4. Drink 1-2 cups of passionflower tea throughout the day, as needed, to help alleviate symptoms of dizziness.
  5. Consult with a healthcare provider before using passionflower tea for dizziness, especially if taking medication or if pregnant, as passionflower may interact with certain medications and have side effects.

10. Valerian Root

Valerian root tea helps with dizziness because it contains compounds that act as natural sedatives, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety, which can be a cause of dizziness.

Additionally, valerian root has been shown to improve circulation, allowing for better oxygen and nutrient delivery to the brain, helping to alleviate symptoms of dizziness. Furthermore, valerian root tea can help regulate the nervous system, reducing the likelihood of experiencing episodes of dizziness. It's important to note that while valerian root tea can be helpful for some people, it's not a cure-all solution and it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional for persistent or severe dizziness.

Lastly, valerian root tea should be consumed in moderation and as part of a balanced diet, as excessive consumption may cause side effects such as headaches or stomach discomfort.

The following procedure explains how to make valerian root tea for dizziness.

  1. To make Valerian root tea for dizziness, boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan or kettle (preferably filtered) and then add 1 teaspoon of dried Valerian root to a tea infuser or strainer.
  2. Pour the hot water over the Valerian root in a cup, ensuring it is fully submerged, then cover the cup with a lid or a small plate to retain the heat and allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. After steeping, remove the Valerian root from the tea by lifting the infuser or strainer, and gently press it against the side of the cup to extract any remaining liquid.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added flavor and potential anti-dizziness benefits. Honey can help soothe the stomach, while lemon can provide a boost of vitamin C.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to gently coat your throat and stomach, and enjoy the calming effects of the Valerian root as it works to alleviate your dizziness.

10 best herbal teas for cats

These herbal brews are a natural and comforting way to show your cat you care.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains calming properties that soothe cats' nerves, thanks to the presence of the antioxidant apigenin.

This antioxidant binds to certain receptors in the feline brain, promoting relaxation and potentially aiding in rest. Chamomile tea's anti-inflammatory properties, derived from bisabolol and matricin, help reduce inflammation in cats' gastrointestinal tracts, addressing issues like indigestion or upset stomachs. Furthermore, this herbal tea's gentle diuretic effect supports kidney health by encouraging sufficient water intake and facilitating toxin elimination.

Ultimately, chamomile tea offers feline well-being support through its calming, anti-inflammatory, and diuretic properties, making it a popular natural remedy for cats.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with cats.

  • Flowers: Rich in calming compounds, chamomile flowers produce a soothing tea that aids feline relaxation.
  • Leaves: Packed with calming agents, chamomile leaves create a calming brew that helps cats cope with stress.
  • Seeds: Abundant in therapeutic properties, chamomile seeds contribute to a comforting tea that alleviates cat anxiety.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that help with cats.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid with anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, helping to soothe a cat's digestive system and reduce inflammation.
  • Bisabolol: A terpene alcohol known for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects, helping to reduce stress and promote skin health in cats.
  • Chamazulene: A compound formed during steam distillation with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, aiding in maintaining a healthy immune system for cats.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, showing anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities, beneficial for cats with skin irritation or infections.
  • Nobiletin: A flavonoid with potential anticancer effects, supporting overall feline well-being.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for cats.

  1. Measure out one cup of water and bring it to a boil.
  2. Add one teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers to the boiling water.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers.
  5. Allow the tea to cool down to a warm temperature before serving it to your cat.

2. Valerian

Valerian tea contains compounds that bind to feline receptors in the brain, prompting a response that results in relaxation and calmness.

This response mirrors the effects of cats interacting with catnip, another plant that interacts with their nervous system. Valerian tea's active ingredients, including valepotriates and volatile oils, produce these soothing effects. Consequently, this tea provides a natural, non-addictive solution for reducing stress and anxiety in cats.

Ultimately, valerian tea offers a reliable method for promoting tranquility in felines.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in valerian tea that help with cats.

  • Roots: The roots of the valerian plant contain active compounds that promote relaxation in cats.
  • Rhyzomes: These horizontal underground stems contain medicinal properties beneficial for calming feline nerves.
  • Leaves: Rich in soothing agents, these aerial parts help induce sleep and reduce anxiety in cats.
  • Flowers: Although not the most potent part, they do contain mild sedative properties useful for a gentle calming effect.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian tea that help with cats.

  • Alkaloids: Aid in relaxing muscle spasms and alleviating pain in cats.
  • Flavonoids: Act as antioxidants, reducing inflammation and boosting the immune system in cats.
  • GABA: A neurotransmitter that has a calming effect on the central nervous system of cats.
  • Essential oils: Contain anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, benefiting respiratory and digestive health in cats.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian tea for cats.

  1. Measure out 1 teaspoon of dried valerian root per cup of water.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then add the valerian root.
  3. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the valerian root pieces.
  5. Allow the tea to cool down to a lukewarm temperature before serving it to your cat.

3. Catnip

Catnip tea benefits cats because it contains nepetalactone, a chemical that binds to receptors in a cat's nose, stimulating sensory neurons and triggering responses such as purring, rolling, and playfulness.

This natural substance, found in catnip plants, promotes relaxation and stress relief in felines, leading to improved mood and overall well-being. Moreover, catnip tea helps with digestive issues by acting as a carminative, reducing gas and bloating, and soothing the gastrointestinal system. Additionally, it can alleviate skin irritations when applied topically as a rinse or compress.

Evidence also indicates that catnip's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties support the feline immune system, enhancing its ability to fight off infections and maintain health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in catnip tea that help with cats.

  • Roots: Catnip roots contain medicinal properties that help soothe a cat's digestive system, making them an ideal addition to calming teas.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, catnip leaves support a cat's immune system, making them a crucial component in medicinal teas.
  • Stems: The mild sedative properties found in catnip stems make them useful for promoting relaxation in cats, particularly in tea form.
  • Buds: Catnip buds are potent sources of essential oils, offering calming effects when brewed into medicinal teas for cats.
  • Flowers: Abundant in vitamins and minerals, catnip flowers contribute to overall cat health, finding their way into soothing herbal teas.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in catnip tea that help with cats.

  • Nepetalactone: A natural compound that acts as a relaxant and attractant to cats, helping to reduce stress and anxiety.
  • Valepotriates: A group of compounds that have a sedative effect, helping to calm and soothe nervous cats.
  • Tannins: Polyphenolic compounds with astringent properties, aiding in digestion and alleviating diarrhea in cats.
  • Flavonoids: Antioxidant compounds that support the immune system and have anti-inflammatory properties, benefiting overall cat health.
  • Essential oils: Volatile oils that provide a pleasant aroma and can act as natural insect repellents, protecting cats from parasites.
  • Rosmarinic acid: A compound with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to overall cat health and well-being.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic catnip tea for cats.

  1. Harvest fresh catnip leaves, or use 1 tablespoon of dried catnip, and rinse them thoroughly.
  2. Steep the catnip in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Strain the catnip tea to remove leaves and preserve the liquid.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a warm temperature, but not hot enough to cause discomfort to cats.
  5. Serve the catnip tea in a shallow dish, and monitor your cat as they enjoy their herbal treat.

Lavender tea contains properties that address several issues in cats.

This tea, made from the Lavandula angustifolia plant, offers calming effects that reduce anxiety and aggression in cats. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties alleviate skin conditions and promote wound healing. Moreover, lavender tea aids digestion, addressing issues such as loss of appetite, nausea, and indigestion.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in lavender tea that help with cats.

  • Flowers: Rich in soothing essential oils, promoting relaxation and calm in cats.
  • Leaves: Contain calming constituents, useful in alleviating anxiety and stress in feline companions.
  • Buds: Packed with therapeutic compounds, beneficial for supporting feline health and well-being.
  • Stems: Mildly sedative properties, contributing to a peaceful and restful state for cats.
  • Seeds: Occasionally included for added nutrients, though less potent in medicinal properties compared to other parts.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in lavender tea that help with cats.

  • Linalool: A terpene alcohol with sedative properties that can help reduce anxiety and promote relaxation in cats.
  • Linalyl acetate: A ester that has anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, which can help alleviate pain and inflammation in cats.
  • Eucalyptol: A terpene with antimicrobial and expectorant properties, which can help support respiratory health in cats.
  • Camphor: A terpene with antispasmodic and analgesic properties, which can help relieve coughing and pain in cats.
  • Caryophyllene: A sesquiterpene that has anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, which can help alleviate pain and inflammation in cats.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lavender tea for cats.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool down to around 100-120°F (37-49°C), as very hot water can damage the beneficial compounds in lavender.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried organic culinary lavender to a tea infuser or teapot with a built-in strainer.
  3. Pour the warm water over the lavender and let it steep for 3-5 minutes, depending on how strong you prefer the flavor and scent.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the lavender from the tea to prevent over-steeping and bitterness.
  5. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature before serving it to your cat in a shallow dish.

5. Lemon Balm

Lemon balm tea contains a compound called rosmarincic acid, which demonstrably possesses antiviral properties.

This acid inhibits the activity of feline calicivirus, a major cause of upper respiratory infections in cats. Furthermore, lemon balm's calming effects, derived from its nervine and mild sedative properties, aid in reducing stress and anxiety in cats. Additionally, lemon balm's antibacterial properties contribute to promoting overall feline health by supporting the immune system and fighting bacterial infections.

Ultimately, lemon balm tea offers a natural, soothing, and health-boosting beverage for cat companions.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in lemon balm tea that help with cats.

  • Leaves: The leaves of lemon balm are used in making medicinal tea for cats due to their richness in antioxidants and soothing properties.
  • Flowers: The flowers of lemon balm are often used in tea for their calming effects and high concentration of essential oils.
  • Stems: The stems of lemon balm contain beneficial compounds and are sometimes included in medicinal teas to support feline health.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in lemon balm tea that help with cats.

  • Rosmarinic acid: A potent antioxidant that helps reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in cats.
  • Citral: A compound with antimicrobial properties that can help support a healthy feline immune system.
  • Nerol: A terpene known to have calming effects, which may help reduce anxiety and promote relaxation in cats.
  • Geraniol: A compound with potential antifungal properties, which can aid in managing fungal infections in cats.
  • Eugenol: A compound with analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate pain and inflammation in cats.
  • Tannins: Polyphenolic compounds with astringent properties, which can help soothe gastrointestinal issues in cats.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lemon balm tea for cats.

  1. Harvest fresh lemon balm leaves, picking 1-2 teaspoons worth per 8 ounces of water.
  2. Rinse the leaves thoroughly under cool running water to remove any dirt or debris.
  3. Coarsely chop the leaves to release their natural oils, then gently bruise them using a mortar and pestle.
  4. Steep the crushed leaves in hot water (175°F) for 5-10 minutes, covered.
  5. Strain the tea, discarding the spent leaves, and allow it to cool down to a comfortable temperature before serving it to your cat.

6. Peppermint

Peppermint tea contains menthol and menthone, compounds known for their soothing properties.

These components help relax the muscles of the stomach and intestines, alleviating digestive issues in cats. Moreover, peppermint tea's antispasmodic and carminative effects ease gas and bloating, promoting a healthy feline gastrointestinal tract. Additionally, its natural cooling and calming qualities make it an ideal remedy for overheated or anxious cats.

However, it's crucial to consult a veterinarian before administering peppermint tea to cats, as improper usage may lead to adverse effects.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with cats.

  • Leaves: Rich in essential oils, menthol, and rosmarinic acid, peppermint leaves are beneficial for soothing a cat's upset stomach and improving digestion.
  • Flowers: Packed with antioxidants and vitamins, peppermint flowers offer respiratory support, making them ideal for promoting feline lung and airway health.
  • Stems: Containing essential oils and therapeutic compounds, peppermint stems can help alleviate inflammation and pain while providing antibacterial properties for cats.
  • Seeds: A natural source of calcium, iron, magnesium, and vitamin A, peppermint seeds support overall well-being, including dental and bone health in cats.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in peppermint tea that help with cats.

  • Menthol: Acts as a natural pain reliever and fever reducer.
  • Menthone: Helps improve digestion and relieve symptoms of gastric disorders.
  • Limonene: Exhibits anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can benefit overall health.
  • Rosmarinic acid: Contains antibacterial, antiviral, and antifungal properties that support a healthy immune system.
  • Flavonoids: Provide antioxidant protection, promoting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for cats.

  1. Measure out one cup of fresh water and bring it to a boil in a saucepan or kettle.
  2. Crush 1-2 organic peppermint leaves and add them to a clean tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Once the water has reached boiling, pour it over the peppermint leaves in the teapot or infuser.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-7 minutes to extract the peppermint flavor and health benefits.
  5. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea into a bowl, allowing it to cool down to a comfortable temperature before serving it to your cat.

7. Ginger

Ginger tea provides multiple benefits for cats, making it a popular natural remedy.

First, ginger's antiemetic properties alleviate stomach discomfort and reduce vomiting. Second, ginger's anti-inflammatory agents soothe irritated digestive tracts, providing relief for cats with upset stomachs.

Third, ginger tea stimulates digestive juices, enhancing nutrient absorption and improving overall digestion. Fourth, ginger acts as a natural pain reliever, easing muscular and joint pain in cats.

Lastly, ginger's warming effect helps maintain cats' body temperature, promoting overall well-being.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with cats.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger's underground stem, or rhizome, is rich in volatile oils and phenol compounds, making it an excellent anti-inflammatory and digestive aid for cats.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the ginger plant contain polyphenols and flavonoids, offering antioxidant and immune-supporting properties for felines.
  • Roots: The ginger root, which is actually a portion of the rhizome, has potent antiemetic qualities, making it useful for managing nausea and vomiting in cats.
  • Flowers: Ginger flowers are a natural source of vitamins and minerals, contributing to overall cat health and well-being.
  • Seeds: Ginger seeds provide essential fatty acids and act as a natural diuretic, promoting a healthy urinary system in cats.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginger tea that help with cats.

  • Gingerols: Gingerols have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce pain and inflammation in cats.
  • Shogaols: Shogaols have antiemetic properties that can help alleviate nausea and vomiting in cats.
  • Paradols: Paradols have antioxidant properties that can help protect cats' cells from damage and support their immune system.
  • Zingerone: Zingerone has analgesic properties that can help relieve pain in cats.
  • Gingerdiols: Gingerdiols have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that can help reduce inflammation and relieve pain in cats.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for cats.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of freshly grated ginger and let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Strain the ginger tea into a bowl, discarding the ginger pieces.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a warm temperature.
  5. Serve the ginger tea to your cat in small portions, no more than 1 tablespoon at a time.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic dandelion tea for cats.

  1. Gather 2-3 fresh dandelion heads, making sure they are free of pesticides and other contaminants.
  2. Rinse the dandelion heads thoroughly under cold running water, then let them dry completely.
  3. Using scissors, carefully remove the yellow petals from the dandelion heads, discarding the green base.
  4. Steep the dandelion petals in 8 ounces of hot water (not boiling) for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea, allowing it to cool to a comfortable temperature before serving it to your cat in small doses (1-2 teaspoons).

What is cats and how can it affect your life?

Cats are domesticated mammals that belong to the Felidae family.

They are popular pets worldwide due to their playful and affectionate nature. Cats provide companionship, reducing feelings of loneliness and anxiety.

Additionally, studies show that cat ownership leads to increased social interaction, benefiting mental health. Furthermore, cats offer therapeutic benefits by lowering stress levels through their calming presence and soothing purrs. Cats also contribute to maintaining a healthy environment by controlling rodent populations.

Indeed, cats play a significant role in enriching and improving people's lives in various ways.

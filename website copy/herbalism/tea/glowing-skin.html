10 Best Herbal Teas For Glowing Skin

Herbal teas are rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, which can help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the body, promoting healthy skin.

Certain herbal teas, like chamomile and rosehip, have anti-aging properties and can improve skin hydration and elasticity. The antioxidants in herbal teas, such as green tea and white tea, can help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, reducing the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles. Drinking herbal teas can also help improve digestion and liver function, which can lead to clearer, more radiant skin.

Additionally, some herbal teas, like nettle and calendula, have antibacterial and antifungal properties that can help treat and prevent skin irritations and infections.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with glowing skin because Chamomile tea aids in glowing skin due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which reduce redness and irritation while fighting free radicals.

The tea's abundance of antioxidants, such as flavonoids and essential oils, helps combat skin damage caused by stress and environmental factors, promoting healthier-looking skin. Furthermore, chamomile tea has been shown to possess antibacterial qualities, potentially benefiting those with acne-prone skin by reducing the presence of harmful bacteria.

Additionally, chamomile tea promotes better sleep quality, as it contains the antioxidant apigenin, which binds to certain receptors in the brain, leading to a more restful night's sleep. Since sleep is crucial for skin repair and regeneration, improved sleep quality can contribute to a visibly healthier and more radiant complexion.

Ultimately, incorporating chamomile tea into one's diet can provide multiple benefits for skin health, leading to a noticeable glow.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for glowing skin.

  1. Bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then remove it from heat and add 1 teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers.
  2. Allow the chamomile flowers to steep in the hot water for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Strain the chamomile flowers from the tea and discard them.
  4. Optional: Add a squeeze of lemon or a drizzle of honey to taste.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea while it is still warm, up to 2-3 times per day.

2. Nettle

Nettle tea helps with glowing skin because it is rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that nourish and protect the skin.

The anti-inflammatory properties of nettle tea can reduce skin redness and irritation, promoting a clearer complexion. Nettle tea also aids in detoxification by supporting the liver and kidneys, which can help eliminate toxins that may negatively impact skin health. Additionally, nettle tea may help regulate hormonal imbalances, as hormonal fluctuations can often lead to skin issues such as acne.

Lastly, the hydrating effect of nettle tea can contribute to maintaining skin's elasticity and plumpness, ultimately leading to a more radiant and glowing appearance.

The following procedure explains how to make nettle tea for glowing skin.

  1. Steep 1 tablespoon of dried nettle leaves in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-10 minutes; strain out the leaves and drink up to three cups of this tea daily for at least a month to see improvement in skin's glow.
  2. For enhanced results, add 1 teaspoon of honey and a squeeze of lemon juice to each cup of nettle tea before drinking, as these natural ingredients can further nourish and detoxify the skin.
  3. Regularly consume nettle tea an hour before meals to stimulate digestion and absorption of nutrients that support skin health; this can also help reduce inflammation and irritation that can lead to skin issues.
  4. Incorporate nettle tea into your skincare routine by letting it cool and using it as a toner or face mist; store the cooled tea in a spray bottle and spritz it on your face throughout the day for a refreshing and hydrating boost.
  5. To maintain glowing skin, make nettle tea a regular part of your daily routine and consider pairing it with a balanced diet, regular exercise, and good sleep habits for optimal skin health and overall well-being.

3. Calendula

Calendula tea helps with glowing skin because it contains high levels of antioxidants, which help to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals and promote cell repair.

The anti-inflammatory properties of calendula tea can help reduce redness and irritation, resulting in a more even skin tone. Additionally, calendula tea has been shown to improve skin hydration, which can lead to a plumper, more radiant complexion. Furthermore, its antibacterial and wound-healing properties can help to speed up the healing process of blemishes, resulting in a clearer, smoother complexion.

Finally, regular consumption of calendula tea can help to boost the immune system, which can lead to an overall improvement in skin health.

The following procedure explains how to make calendula tea for glowing skin.

  1. To make calendula tea for glowing skin, start by boiling 1 cup of water in a saucepan, then add 1 teaspoon of dried calendula flowers and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. While the tea is steeping, prepare a separate cup with a strainer over it, to filter out the flowers once the tea is ready.
  3. After 10 minutes, remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool down for a couple of minutes, so you don't burn yourself while handling it.
  4. Carefully pour the calendula tea through the strainer into the cup, making sure to squeeze out any excess liquid from the flowers.
  5. Drink the calendula tea warm, up to three times a day, for best results, and store any leftovers in the refrigerator for up to 48 hours.

4. Red Clover

Red clover tea helps with glowing skin because it is rich in nutrients like vitamins A, C, and E, which are known to promote skin health.

The tea's antioxidant properties help to fight free radicals, reducing the appearance of fine lines, wrinkles, and age spots, thereby enhancing skin glow. Red clover tea is also a natural anti-inflammatory, helping to soothe irritated skin and reduce redness. Additionally, its phytoestrogens content can help balance hormones, which can improve skin conditions related to hormonal imbalances, such as acne.

Lastly, drinking red clover tea regularly can help improve blood circulation, ensuring that skin cells are well-nourished and oxygenated, resulting in a radiant complexion.

The following procedure explains how to make red clover tea for glowing skin.

  1. To make red clover tea for glowing skin, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 1-2 teaspoons of dried red clover flowers; let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the flowers, then add the juice of half a lemon and 1-2 teaspoons of raw honey to sweeten, stirring until fully combined.
  3. For an extra boost, consider adding 1/4 teaspoon of turmeric powder for its anti-inflammatory properties.
  4. Drink the tea while it's still warm, 1-2 times a day, for at least a month to see noticeable results in skin texture and tone.
  5. Pair the tea with a balanced diet, regular exercise, and a consistent skincare routine for optimal results.

5. Burdock

Burdock tea helps with glowing skin because it is rich in antioxidants, essential fatty acids, and vitamins, which all contribute to healthy skin.

The tea's antioxidants help protect skin cells from damage, while its fatty acids help maintain the skin's natural barrier function, keeping it hydrated and plump. Additionally, burdock tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce redness and irritation, resulting in a more even skin tone. Furthermore, it promotes blood circulation, which aids in the delivery of nutrients and oxygen to the skin, giving it a radiant and glowing appearance.

Lastly, burdock tea can help detoxify the body, eliminating toxins that may negatively impact skin health, thus promoting a clearer and more vibrant complexion.

The following procedure explains how to make burdock tea for glowing skin.

  1. In a pot, bring 2 cups of water to a boil, then add 2 tablespoons of dried burdock root, reduce heat and simmer, covered, for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the burdock root, pouring the liquid into a teapot or cup.
  3. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added flavor and skin-boosting vitamin C.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of burdock tea daily for a month, or until desired skin glow is achieved.

6. Dandelion

Dandelion tea helps with glowing skin because it is rich in vitamins C and E, as well as lecticin, which all help to maintain skin health.

These nutrients act as antioxidants, helping to protect skin cells from damage caused by free radicals and reducing the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles. Dandelion tea also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce redness and swelling, resulting in a more even skin tone. Additionally, it promotes liver function, which aids in the body's natural detoxification process, leading to clearer and more radiant skin.

Lastly, dandelion tea is a natural diuretic, helping to reduce water retention and bloating, which can also contribute to a glowing complexion.

The following procedure explains how to make dandelion tea for glowing skin.

  1. To make dandelion tea for glowing skin, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 1-2 teaspoons of dried dandelion flowers; let it simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  2. After simmering, remove the tea from heat and strain out the dandelion flowers, allowing the tea to cool down for a few minutes.
  3. Optional: add a squeeze of lemon or a small slice of ginger for added skin-boosting benefits and flavor.
  4. Stir in 1 teaspoon of raw honey for natural sweetness and additional skin-nourishing properties.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of this dandelion tea daily for at least 2-4 weeks to see improvements in skin's glow and overall complexion.

7. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea helps with glowing skin because it contains high levels of antioxidants, particularly silymarin, which help to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the body.

These antioxidants protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting skin health and regeneration. Milk thistle tea also supports liver function, which is essential for eliminating toxins from the body and promoting a clear complexion. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of milk thistle tea can help reduce redness and irritation, leading to a more even skin tone.

Finally, milk thistle tea has been shown to improve skin hydration, leaving the skin looking plump, radiant, and youthful.

The following procedure explains how to make milk thistle tea for glowing skin.

  1. In a cup, add 1 teaspoon of dried milk thistle flowers or 1 milk thistle tea bag and optionally add 1/2 teaspoon of honey or lemon juice for added flavor.
  2. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the milk thistle and sweetener in a teapot or a cup with a lid.
  3. Steep the tea for 5-10 minutes, depending on how strong you prefer the flavor, then remove the tea bag or strain out the flowers.
  4. Add any desired additional ingredients, such as a slice of lemon or a sprig of mint, for extra skin-boosting benefits and visual appeal.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, twice a day, for at least a month to see noticeable improvements in skin glow and complexion.

8. Yellow Dock

Yellow dock tea helps with glowing skin because it contains high levels of iron, vitamin A, and other minerals that support healthy skin.

These nutrients promote cell regeneration and collagen production, which can help reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles. Additionally, yellow dock tea has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe irritated skin and reduce redness. The tea's high antioxidant content also helps protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, which can lead to a more even skin tone and a healthy glow.

Furthermore, yellow dock tea may help improve digestion and liver function, which can indirectly support skin health by promoting the elimination of toxins from the body.

The following procedure explains how to make yellow dock tea for glowing skin.

  1. To make yellow dock tea for glowing skin, start by boiling 2 cups of water in a pot, then add 2 teaspoons of dried yellow dock root and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  2. While the tea is steeping, squeeze the juice of half a lemon into a cup, then add 1 teaspoon of raw honey and stir until fully combined.
  3. After 20 minutes, strain the tea into the cup with the lemon and honey mixture, discarding the solids.
  4. To enhance the benefits for your skin, add a pinch of turmeric and cinnamon to the tea, stirring well to combine.
  5. Drink the tea warm, twice a day for best results, and enjoy your journey towards glowing skin.

9. Cleavers

Cleavers tea helps with glowing skin because it has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can reduce skin inflammation and fight free radicals, promoting healthy skin cell function.

This herbal tea is also known for its gentle diuretic effect, which can help eliminate toxins from the body, leading to a clearer and more radiant complexion. Additionally, cleavers tea may support lymphatic system function, aiding in the removal of waste and toxins from the body, which can contribute to healthier-looking skin. Furthermore, the high vitamin and mineral content in cleavers tea, including vitamin C and silica, can support collagen production and overall skin health, resulting in a glowing and youthful appearance.

Lastly, drinking cleavers tea regularly can help soothe skin conditions such as acne, eczema, and psoriasis, providing relief and promoting skin healing, leaving you with a smoother and more even skin tone.

The following procedure explains how to make cleavers tea for glowing skin.

  1. To make cleavers tea, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 2 teaspoons of dried cleavers herb; let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  2. While the tea is steeping, rinse a handful of fresh mint leaves and set them aside for later.
  3. After 15 minutes, remove the tea from heat and add the mint leaves, allowing them to steep for an additional 3-5 minutes for added skin-soothing properties.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used herbs, and let it cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. For best results, enjoy 1-2 cups of this soothing cleavers tea daily for at least a month, or until you notice a visible improvement in your skin's glow and overall health.

10. Red Raspberry

Red raspberry tea helps with glowing skin because it is packed with antioxidants, including vitamin C and ellagic acid, which fight free radicals and reduce inflammation, promoting even skin tone and texture.

The tea also contains strong anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe irritated skin, reduce redness, and accelerate healing. Furthermore, it is rich in tannins, which have astringent qualities that can help tighten pores and improve skin's overall appearance. Red raspberry tea is also hydrating, helping to keep skin plump, supple, and moisturized, contributing to a radiant complexion.

Lastly, it has been suggested that the tea may help regulate hormonal imbalances, which can lead to skin issues such as acne, thereby promoting clearer, healthier-looking skin.

The following procedure explains how to make red raspberry tea for glowing skin.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 tablespoon of dried red raspberry leaves with 8 ounces of boiling water.
  2. Allow the tea to steep for 5-7 minutes to allow the beneficial compounds to fully infuse into the water.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the leaves and discard them.
  4. You can drink the tea as is, or add a touch of honey or lemon to taste.
  5. For best results, drink 2-3 cups of raspberry tea per day, consistently, to see improvements in your skin's appearance.

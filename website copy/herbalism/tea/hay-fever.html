The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic nettle tea for hay fever.

  1. Gather 2-3 handfuls of fresh, young stinging nettle leaves, avoiding contact with the stiff hairs that can cause skin irritation.
  2. Rinse the nettle leaves thoroughly under cold running water to remove any dirt or insects.
  3. Coarsely chop the nettle leaves and place them in a heatproof pot or teapot.
  4. Pour 1 quart (4 cups) of boiling water over the chopped nettle leaves, cover, and let steep for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, discarding them safely, and enjoy the nettle tea warm or cool throughout the day for hay fever relief.

What is hay fever and how can it affect your life?

Hay fever, also known as allergic rhinitis, is an immune system response to airborne allergens such as pollen, dust mites, or pet dander.

This condition causes inflammation in the nasal passages, resulting in symptoms like sneezing, congestion, and itchy, watery eyes. For those affected, hay fever adversely impacts their daily lives by causing discomfort, disrupting sleep patterns, and potentially affecting cognitive function and productivity at work or school.

In severe cases, hay fever can lead to complications such as sinus infections or asthma attacks.

What other natural remedies help with hay fever?

Several natural remedies, proven effective for hay fever, include a healthy diet, regular exercise, acupuncture, and saline nasal irrigation.

A diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins boosts the immune system, reducing the severity of hay fever symptoms. Regular physical activity accelerates the elimination of nasal congestion. Acupuncture, an ancient Chinese technique, targets specific pressure points to alleviate allergy symptoms, and studies confirm its efficacy.

Saline nasal irrigation, a simple and affordable method, flushes out allergens from the nasal passages, providing immediate relief.

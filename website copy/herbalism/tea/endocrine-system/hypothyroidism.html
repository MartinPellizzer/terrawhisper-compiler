10 best herbal teas for hypothyroidism

Herbal teas, such as bladderwrack and black walnut, provide a natural remedy for individuals with hypothyroidism.

Bladderwrack, a type of seaweed, contains high levels of iodine that supports thyroid function. Black walnut, on the other hand, has antibacterial and antiviral properties that help reduce inflammation in the thyroid gland.

Both herbs offer a caffeine-free alternative to traditional tea and promote overall thyroid health.

1. Bugleweed

Bugleweed tea alleviates hypothyroidism by directly affecting the thyroid gland, where it inhibits the uptake of iodine, a key component in the production of thyroid hormones.

Consequently, this reduces the synthesis and release of thyroid hormones, bringing them back to normal levels. Additionally, bugleweed tea contains compounds like lycopusine and tannins that interfere with thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) receptors, preventing TSH from binding and further reducing thyroid hormone production. This herbal tea's ability to restore hormonal balance provides relief for hypothyroidism symptoms, such as fatigue, weight gain, and cold sensitivity.

Thus, bugleweed tea constitutes a valuable natural remedy for managing hypothyroidism by directly targeting and regulating the overactive thyroid gland.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in bugleweed tea that help with hypothyroidism.

  • Leaves: Contain active compounds that help regulate thyroid hormone production.
  • Stems: Possess anti-inflammatory properties that alleviate inflammation caused by hypothyroidism.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants, which support thyroid health and help balance hormone levels.
  • Buds: High in essential oils, promoting overall endocrine system function, including the thyroid.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in bugleweed tea that aid with hypothyroidism.

  • Tannins: Act as antithyrotropins, inhibiting the activity of thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) and thus reducing thyroid gland growth, beneficial for hypothyroidism.
  • Flavonoids: Possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, helping reduce oxidative stress and associated thyroid damage in hypothyroidism.
  • Phenolic acids: Exhibit antithyroid activity, reducing thyroid hormone synthesis and secretion, aiding in managing hypothyroidism.
  • Volatile oils: Improve digestive health and stimulate blood circulation, enhancing overall body function and indirectly supporting thyroid health.
  • Iridoids: Regulate immune system response, potentially reducing autoimmune-related thyroid conditions in hypothyroidism.
  • Oleanolic acid: Contributes to anti-inflammatory activities, helping alleviate thyroid tissue inflammation associated with hypothyroidism.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic bugleweed tea for hypothyroidism.

  1. Measure 2 teaspoons of dried bugleweed herb, ensuring accuracy for proper dosage.
  2. Place the herb in a tea infuser or loose in a teapot, promoting even steeping.
  3. Boil 8 ounces of water, allowing it to reach the optimal temperature.
  4. Pour the hot water over the bugleweed, initiating the brewing process.
  5. Steep for 10 minutes, ensuring the release of beneficial compounds.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ashwagandha tea for hypothyroidism.

  3. Let the tea steep for 10-15 minutes, covered.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solid remnants.
  5. Stir in 1 teaspoon of raw honey and enjoy your ashwagandha tea.

10 best herbal teas for insect bites

Several herbal teas provide immediate relief for insect bites due to their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

Lastly, green tea, rich in tannins and catechins, boasts excellent antihistamine and anti-inflammatory properties, making it an ideal remedy for insect bites.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea provides relief for insect bites due to its anti-inflammatory properties derived from the plant's chamazulene and bisabolol components.

These compounds reduce inflammation by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins and leukotrienes, chemicals that cause swelling and redness. Moreover, chamomile contains flavonoids that strengthen capillary walls, thereby minimizing itching and pain. When applied topically as a compress or lotion, chamomile tea demonstrably soothes irritated skin resulting from insect bites.

Thus, chamomile tea offers an effective, natural remedy for alleviating the discomfort associated with insect bites.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with insect bites.

  • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory compounds that help reduce swelling from insect bites.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and possess soothing properties that alleviate itching.
  • Seeds: Contain volatile oils with analgesic properties that help relieve pain from insect bites.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that help with insect bites.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid with antioxidant properties that reduces inflammation and itching caused by insect bites.
  • Chamazulene: A chemical compound with anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects that helps alleviate pain and swelling from insect bites.
  • Bisabolol: A terpene alcohol with soothing and calming properties that helps to reduce redness and irritation caused by insect bites.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene that has anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, helping to relieve pain and swelling from insect bites.
  • Hypolipidemic flavonoids: A group of flavonoids that reduce inflammation and have analgesic effects, helping to alleviate pain and swelling from insect bites.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for insect bites.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water, then let it cool down to 200 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea into a cup.
  5. Let the chamomile tea cool down to a comfortable temperature before gently applying it to insect bites with a cotton ball.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for insect bites.

  1. Crush 1 tablespoon of fresh peppermint leaves with a mortar and pestle for 2 minutes, releasing the oils.
  2. Add the crushed leaves to 1 cup of boiling water and steep for 5 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the solid particles, retaining only the liquid.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortably warm temperature, which should take approximately 10 minutes.
  5. Soak a clean cloth in the cooled peppermint tea, then apply it directly to the affected insect bite area for immediate relief.

What is insect bites and how can it affect your life?

Insect bites occur when an insect pierces the skin to feed on blood.

These bites often result in red, itchy bumps and can lead to discomfort, allergic reactions, or the transmission of diseases such as Lyme disease, Zika virus, or malaria.

Such infections impact people's lives significantly, potentially causing severe health issues, missed work or school days, and substantial medical expenses.

What other natural remedies help with insect bites?

Common and effective natural remedies for insect bites include applying a cold compress to reduce swelling, using honey to promote healing and reduce itching, and applying aloe vera gel to soothe the skin and reduce inflammation.

Another effective natural remedy is using tea tree oil, which has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help to prevent infection and reduce redness and swelling. Additionally, using a paste made from baking soda and water can also help to neutralize the venom from insect bites and reduce itching. Applying lavender oil, known for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, can also provide relief from insect bites.

These natural remedies offer a safe and affordable alternative to over-the-counter creams and ointments for treating insect bites.

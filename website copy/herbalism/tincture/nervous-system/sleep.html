10 best herbal tinctures for sleep

Herbal tinctures for sleep are concentrated liquid extracts that capture the medicinal benefits of sleep-promoting herbs.

These natural remedies improve people's daily lives by addressing various factors that disrupt sleep patterns, such as stress, anxiety, and restlessness. For instance, valerian root tincture alleviates sleep disturbances and anxiety by increasing the amount of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain, a neurotransmitter responsible for promoting relaxation.

Similarly, passionflower tincture reduces insomnia and nervous restlessness by inhibiting the breakdown of GABA, enhancing its calming effects. Furthermore, hops flower tincture improves sleep quality and duration by acting as a natural sedative and decreasing the time required to fall asleep.

Consequently, incorporating herbal tinctures for sleep into nightly routines enables individuals to experience deeper, more rejuvenating sleep, ultimately improving their overall well-being and daytime functioning.

1. Valerian tincture

Valerian tincture helps with sleep because it contains active compounds that influence certain neurotransmitters in the brain, specifically gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA).

GABA is a chemical messenger that promotes relaxation and reduces excitability in the brain, thereby creating a calming effect on the nervous system. This calming effect can help individuals fall asleep faster and stay asleep longer. Additionally, valerian tincture has been found to increase the amount of deep, restorative sleep, providing a more restful and restorative night's sleep.

Valerian tincture's ability to increase GABA levels, promote relaxation, and enhance deep sleep makes it an effective natural remedy for insomnia and other sleep disturbances.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian tincture that aid with sleep.

  • Valerenic acid: Interacts with GABA receptors in the brain to produce a calming, sedative effect.
  • Valepotriates: Contributes to the plant's sedative and muscle relaxant properties, promoting relaxation and sleep.
  • Iridoids: Possess sedative and analgesic properties, helping to reduce anxiety and improve sleep quality.
  • Flavanones: Exhibit anti-anxiety and sedative effects, aiding in stress reduction and sleep induction.
  • Essential oils: Contain sedative compounds, such as bornyl acetate and β-caryophyllene, promoting relaxation and better sleep.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in valerian tincture that help with sleep.

  • Roots: Contain active compounds that interact with GABA receptors in the brain, promoting relaxation and aiding sleep.
  • Rhizomes: Store energy and nutrients, providing a rich source of medicinal compounds that support sleep and reduce anxiety.
  • Flowers: Contain volatile oils and flavonoids that have sedative effects, helping to alleviate sleep disturbances.
  • Seeds: Contain valepotriates, which have a calming effect on the central nervous system and can improve sleep quality.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian tincture for sleep.

  1. Chop 2 ounces of dried valerian root into small pieces and place in a clean glass jar.
  2. Pour 1 pint of vodka or rum over the chopped root, ensuring it is fully submerged.
  3. Secure a lid on the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking it daily.
  4. Strain the tincture through cheesecloth into a clean glass bottle, discarding the solid plant material.
  5. Label the bottle with the contents and date, storing it in a cool, dark place for up to a year.

2. Passionflower tincture

Passionflower tincture helps with sleep due to its proven sedative and anxiety-reducing effects.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in passionflower tincture that aid with sleep.

  • Flavonoids: Flavonoids possess sedative properties that can help reduce anxiety and improve sleep quality.
  • Chrysin: Chrysin has been shown to inhibit the enzyme that degrades tryptophan, a precursor to serotonin, thereby promoting better sleep.
  • Benzoflavone: Benzoflavone has been found to enhance GABA receptor function, which can help improve sleep duration and quality.
  • Vitexin: Vitexin helps regulate the body's stress response by modulating the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, promoting relaxation and better sleep.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in passionflower tincture that help with sleep.

  • Flowers: Contain flavonoids, such as chrysin, that possess sedative and sleep-enhancing properties.
  • Leaves: Rich in harmala alkaloids, which have been shown to have a relaxing effect on the nervous system.
  • Aerial parts: Contain passiflora alkaloids and flavonoids, promoting relaxation and improving sleep quality.
  • Roots: Contain additional bioactive compounds that extend the calming effects of the tincture, aiding in deeper and more restorative sleep.
  • Seeds: Source of essential oils and fatty acids, which complement the sleep-promoting effects of other plant components.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic passionflower tincture for sleep.

  1. Harvest 1 ounce of fresh passionflower aerial parts, preferably during late summer or early fall when the plant's medicinal properties peak.
  2. Chop the plant material and place it in a clean, dry glass jar with a tight-fitting lid.
  3. Pour 8 ounces of 80-100 proof vodka or grain alcohol over the chopped herb, ensuring it's fully submerged.
  4. Securely seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for two to six weeks, shaking it gently once a day.
  5. Strain the tincture using a cheesecloth or muslin cloth into a dark glass dropper bottle, discarding the spent plant material. Standard dosage for sleep is 30-60 drops, 30 minutes before bedtime. Refrigerate the tincture for up to one year.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic california poppy tincture for sleep.

  2. Combine the poppy flowers and vodka in a clean mason jar, ensuring the flowers are fully submerged.
  3. Secure the lid and store the jar in a cool, dark location for a minimum of two weeks, shaking it gently every other day.
  4. After two weeks, strain the mixture using a cheesecloth, discarding the spent flowers and reserving the liquid.
  5. Store the tincture in a tightly sealed glass bottle and take 10-30 drops before bedtime for sleep support.

  2. Tightly seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 4-6 weeks, shaking it gently every few days.

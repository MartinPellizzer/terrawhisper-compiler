1. Valerian

Valerian tincture helps with sleep deprivation because it contains valerenic acid and other compounds that promote relaxation by increasing the amount of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain.

GABA is a neurotransmitter that inhibits nerve cells from firing, thereby reducing neural activity and producing a calming effect. Valerian tincture's ability to enhance GABA levels helps alleviate anxiety, restlessness, and insomnia, making it an effective natural remedy for sleep deprivation.

Furthermore, valerian tincture has minimal side effects and is non-addictive, making it a safer alternative to prescription sleep aids.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian that aid with sleep deprivation.

  • Anxiolytic: Valerian tincture has a calming effect on the nervous system, reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation, which is crucial for sleep deprivation. This property is mainly due to the presence of valerenic acid and valepotriates.
  • Sedative: Valerian has a sedative effect that can help individuals fall asleep faster and improve overall sleep quality. This is attributed to the active constituents, including valerenic acid and isovaleric acid.
  • Muscle relaxant: Valerian tincture helps alleviate muscle tension and spasms, which can contribute to better sleep. The active constituents responsible for this property include valerenic acid and isovaleric acid.
  • Hypnotic: Valerian has hypnotic properties that can directly enhance sleep onset and duration. The main active components responsible for this effect are valerenic acid and valepotriates.
  • Anticonvulsant: Valerian tincture can help reduce seizure activity and stabilize neural function, which can be beneficial for individuals experiencing sleep deprivation-related neurological issues. The anticonvulsant properties are attributed to the presence of valerenic acid and isovaleric acid.
  • Antioxidant: Valerian contains antioxidants that help protect cells from damage and support overall health, which can contribute to better sleep quality. The antioxidant properties are due to various compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in valerian that help with sleep deprivation.

  • Roots: Contain active compounds that promote relaxation and sleep.
  • Rhizomes: Rich in valerenic acid, known for its sedative properties.
  • Flowers: Offer calming effects, aiding in reducing sleep disturbances.
  • Leaves: Contain soothing compounds, which help in alleviating sleep deprivation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian for sleep deprivation.

  1. Chop 2 ounces of dried valerian root into small pieces and place in a clean glass jar.
  2. Pour 10 ounces of vodka or rum over the chopped valerian root, ensuring all pieces are fully submerged.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and store in a dark, cool place for a minimum of 2 weeks, shaking every other day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the liquid from the jar using cheesecloth into a dark glass bottle, squeezing out any excess liquid.
  5. Label the bottle with the contents and date, storing in a cool, dark place for up to 2 years, taking 1-2 mL before bed as needed for sleep deprivation.

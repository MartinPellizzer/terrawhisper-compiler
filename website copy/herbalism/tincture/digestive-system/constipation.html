1. Psyllium tincture

Psyllium tincture helps with constipation because it contains soluble fiber that absorbs water and expands, forming a gel-like substance.

This substance softens and adds bulk to the stool, making it easier to pass through the intestines. As a result, psyllium tincture can promote regular bowel movements and alleviate constipation. Additionally, psyllium can help regulate the speed of digestion, preventing the stomach from emptying too quickly or too slowly, which can both contribute to constipation.

Overall, psyllium tincture's ability to absorb water and increase stool bulk makes it an effective natural remedy for constipation.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in psyllium that aid with constipation.

  • Bulking agent: Psyllium tincture contains mucilage that absorbs water and forms a gel-like bulk in the intestines, making stools easier to pass.
  • Laxative: The bulk-forming property of psyllium tincture stimulates the intestines to contract and move waste through the digestive system, alleviating constipation.
  • Fecal softening: Psyllium tincture's ability to retain water in the stool keeps it soft and hydrated, which is essential for easy bowel movements and preventing straining.
  • Prebiotic: The soluble fiber in psyllium tincture promotes the growth of beneficial gut bacteria, which can improve overall gut health and help regulate bowel movements.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts of psyllium that help with constipation.

  • Seeds: Psyllium seeds absorb water and expand, creating a gel-like substance that helps relieve constipation by gently promoting bowel movements.
  • Husks: The outer coating of psyllium seeds, called husks, are also rich in soluble fiber, providing bulk to stools, making them easier to pass.
  • Leaves: Psyllium leaves, although less frequently used, contain mucilage, which can help soften stools and ease constipation by stimulating muscle contractions.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic psyllium tincture for constipation.

  1. Chop 1 cup of psyllium husks into small pieces and combine with 2 cups of boiling water in a pot.
  2. Reduce heat and simmer, covered, for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool down to room temperature.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth-lined sieve into a clean jar, squeezing out excess liquid.
  5. Store the psyllium tincture in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks, taking 1 tablespoon daily as needed for constipation.

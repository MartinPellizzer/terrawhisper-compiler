    10 best herbal teas for sunburn

    Herbal teas, such as chamomile and calendula, offer natural relief for sunburn.

    Chamomile is renowned for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which soothe redness and promote tissue healing. Calendula, on the other hand, contains flavonoids that reduce skin irritation, while its antimicrobial qualities prevent infection.

    Both teas create a gentle, herb-infused brew when steeped in hot water, offering a soothing topical treatment for sunburned skin.

    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea helps with sunburn due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce redness and swelling.

    Its antioxidant-rich composition aids in repairing skin damage caused by UV rays. Chamomile's soothing effect on the skin stems from its ability to accelerate skin cell and tissue regeneration.

    Additionally, chamomile tea possesses moisturizing qualities that alleviate sunburn-related dryness and irritation.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with sunburn.

    • Flowers: Contain active compounds that possess anti-inflammatory properties, helping to soothe sunburned skin.
    • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, leaves aid in skin repair and offer relief from sun damage.
    • Seeds: Packed with essential oils, seeds help to moisturize and reduce redness in sunburned areas.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with sunburn.

    • Apigenin: A flavonoid that possesses antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, reducing redness and swelling caused by sunburn.
    • Chamazulene: A chemical compound with potent anti-inflammatory effects, soothing sunburned skin and alleviating pain.
    • Bisabolol: A terpene alcohol known for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and skin-soothing properties, aiding in sunburn recovery.
    • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene that has anti-inflammatory effects, helping to reduce sunburn-induced redness and irritation.
    • Flavonoids: A group of plant metabolites with antioxidant properties, protecting skin from harmful free radicals generated by sun exposure.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for sunburn.

    1. Gather 2 cups of fresh chamomile flowers or 1 tablespoon of dried chamomile flowers.
    2. Boil 4 cups of water and add the chamomile flowers, steep for 15 minutes.
    3. Strain the tea to remove the flowers, let it cool down to a warm temperature.
    4. Soak a clean cloth in the chamomile tea and apply it to the sunburned area for 15 minutes.
    5. Repeat the application every few hours, storing the leftover tea in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours.

    2. Calendula

    Calendula tea treats sunburn due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce redness and swelling.

    This herbal tea, derived from marigold flowers, contains antioxidants that aid in skin cell regeneration and help rebuild damaged skin tissue. Its soothing effect on the skin results from relieving pain and promoting faster healing of sunburn. Furthermore, calendula tea boasts antibacterial qualities that prevent infections in sunburned areas, safeguarding the skin from further harm.

    Lastly, drinking calendula tea internally contributes to overall skin health, enabling the body to better manage and recover from sun damage.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in calendula tea that help with sunburn.

    • Flowers: Contain high levels of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, making them effective for soothing sunburned skin.
    • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids and volatile oils, leaves help reduce redness and promote skin repair.
    • Stems: Contain beneficial polysaccharides, which have a calming effect on irritated skin caused by sunburn.
    • Seeds: Packed with essential fatty acids, seeds aid in moisturizing and healing damaged skin cells due to sun exposure.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in calendula tea that aid with sunburn.

    • Flavonoids: Possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that reduce redness, swelling, and skin damage caused by sunburn.
    • Triterpenes: Accelerate healing by stimulating skin cell regeneration, repairing sun-damaged skin.
    • Carotenoids: Protect skin from free radicals and UV radiation, reducing the risk of sunburn and skin damage.
    • Polysaccharides: Provide a soothing effect and help maintain skin moisture, reducing discomfort and promoting skin repair.
    • Volatile oils: Contain therapeutic properties that alleviate pain and inflammation, helping to manage sunburn symptoms.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic calendula tea for sunburn.

    1. Gather 1-2 tablespoons of dried calendula petals and place them in a tea infuser or loose in a teapot.
    2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the calendula petals.
    3. Steep the tea for 10-15 minutes to allow the calendula's healing properties to extract.
    4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove the petals.
    5. Drink the tea while warm or allow it to cool and apply it directly to the sunburned area using a clean cloth.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for sunburn.

    1. Boil 8 cups of water, reaching a rolling boil for 3-5 minutes.
    2. Add 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves or 2 peppermint tea bags to the boiling water.
    3. Steep the peppermint in the water for 5-7 minutes, creating a potent tea.
    4. Strain out the peppermint leaves or remove tea bags, leaving only the tea.
    5. Allow the tea to cool down to a warm temperature, then use it to gently clean and soothe sunburned skin.

    3. Pour the boiling water over the lavender, covering and steeping for 5-7 minutes.
    4. Strain the tea to remove the lavender, discarding the used herbs.
    5. Drink the lavender tea warm, up to three times a day, to help soothe and relieve sunburned skin.

    4. Strain the liquid into a container, discarding the plantain pieces.
    5. Allow tea to cool, then store in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Apply cool tea to sunburned skin using a clean cloth.

    4. Strain the tea, discarding the solid plant material.
    5. Let the tea cool to a safe temperature before applying to sunburned skin.

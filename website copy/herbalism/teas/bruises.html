    10 best herbal teas for bruises

    Certain herbal teas possess anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, ideally suited for treating bruises.

    Arnica tea, known for its ability to alleviate pain and reduce swelling, is an excellent choice. Calendula tea, rich in flavonoids, boasts remarkable anti-inflammatory effects and encourages bruise healing.

    Ginger tea offers potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic benefits due to its gingerol content. St. John's Wort tea, apart from its well-known antidepressant qualities, has been found to aid in bruise healing and relieve pain thanks to its hypericin and hyperforin constituents.

    Consumption of these herbal teas on a regular basis contributes to diminished bruise appearance and reduced discomfort.

    1. Arnica

    Arnica tea aids bruises due to its anti-inflammatory properties, derived from helenalin and other sesquiterpene lactones, which reduce swelling and pain.

    This herbal tea's effectiveness comes from its ability to stimulate circulation, thereby increasing the flow of oxygen and nutrients to damaged tissues. Additionally, arnica tea promotes the disbursement of pooled blood and fluid from bruised areas, facilitating the healing process. Furthermore, it contains flavonoids like luteolin and quercetin that strengthen capillary walls, preventing further bruising.

    Lastly, arnica tea's astringent qualities tighten and firm skin, reducing the visibility of bruises.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in arnica tea that help with bruises.

    • Flowers: Contain beneficial flavonoids, essential oils, and beneficial acids that provide anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, making them ideal for treating bruises.
    • Leaves: Rich in phenolic acids, tannins, and helenalin, which aid in soothing irritation, reducing inflammation, and promoting tissue repair for bruise healing.
    • Rhizomes: Packed with antioxidants and sesquiterpene lactones that encourage blood flow, help dissipate blood accumulation from bruises, and relieve pain.
    • Buds: Contain high levels of essential oils, coumarins, and phenolic acids, which act as a natural remedy for treating bruises, decreasing pain, and boosting skin recovery.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in arnica tea that aid with bruises.

    • Heliannuolide: Reduces inflammation, thus alleviating bruise-related swelling.
    • Luteolin: Acts as an antioxidant, protecting injured tissues from further damage.
    • Isorhamnetin: Helps decrease capillary permeability, minimizing bruise size.
    • Jaceidin: Aids in reducing pain, providing comfort for bruises.
    • Quercetin: Possesses anti-inflammatory properties, assisting in bruise healing.
    • Patuletin: Acts as an analgesic, alleviating bruise-related discomfort.
    • Isoquercitrin: Boosts blood vessel health, preventing easy bruising.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic arnica tea for bruises.

    1. Chop 2 tablespoons of dried arnica flowers and place them in a tea infuser.
    2. Boil 1 cup of water and pour it over the arnica flowers.
    3. Steep the tea for 10 minutes, allowing the arnica to release its healing properties.
    4. Remove the tea infuser and discard the used arnica flowers.
    5. Drink the tea warm, up to three times a day, to help reduce bruising.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for bruises.

    1. Peel and grate 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger root.
    2. Boil 2 cups of water, then add grated ginger and reduce heat to a simmer for 10 minutes.
    3. Strain the ginger tea into a mug, discarding the solid ginger pieces.
    4. Stir in 1 teaspoon of honey and a squeeze of lemon juice for flavor (optional).
    5. Drink the warm ginger tea up to three times a day to help reduce bruise discoloration and swelling.

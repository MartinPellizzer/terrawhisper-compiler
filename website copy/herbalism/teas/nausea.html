    10 best herbal teas for nausea

    For those suffering from nausea, certain herbal teas offer natural relief.

    Ginger tea is a classic choice, as its warming properties help to soothe the stomach and ease queasiness. Peppermint tea is another popular option, as its cooling effects calm the digestive system and reduce discomfort. Chamomile tea also provides comfort, its gentle, apple-like flavor promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety that often accompanies nausea.

    Additionally, Lemon Balm tea offers a light, citrusy flavor that gently settles the stomach, while Fennel tea's mild licorice taste helps to regulate digestion.

    1. Ginger

    Ginger contains active compounds proven to alleviate digestive issues, including nausea.

    Specifically, its primary component, gingerol, neutralizes acid in the stomach and stimulates the production of digestive enzymes, promoting smooth digestion. Consequently, ginger tea effectively soothes queasiness, providing rapid relief.

    Additionally, this natural remedy has no known side effects, making it a popular and reliable choice for managing nausea.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in ginger tea that help with nausea.

    • Rhizomes: Ginger rhizomes are a powerful ingredient in medicinal tea for nausea. They contain phenolic compounds which are known to help reduce feelings of sickness and relax the gastrointestinal muscles.
    • Roots: Ginger roots are widely used in teas to alleviate nausea. They have anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe the stomach and provide relief from queasiness.
    • Leaves: Ginger leaves are an excellent addition to medicinal tea for nausea. They are rich in antioxidants that can aid in digestion and reduce the symptoms of nausea.
    • Flowers: Ginger flowers are used in teas to help combat nausea. They contain active compounds that can help regulate the digestive system and provide quick relief from queasiness.
    • Seeds: Ginger seeds are sometimes included in medicinal tea for nausea. They have been found to have calming effects on the stomach and can help to alleviate the symptoms of nausea.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginger tea that aid with nausea.

    • Gingerols: Gingerols are the main active components in ginger responsible for its potent anti-nausea effects. They work by blocking the release of serotonin, a chemical messenger that can trigger vomiting.
    • Shogaols: Shogaols are compounds formed when ginger is dried or cooked, and they have been found to be even more effective than gingerols in alleviating nausea. They work by relaxing the gastrointestinal tract and reducing inflammation.
    • Paradols: Paradols are anti-inflammatory compounds found in ginger that can help reduce nausea by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that can cause inflammation and trigger nausea.
    • Zingerone: Zingerone is a phenolic compound in ginger that has been shown to have anti-emetic effects, meaning it can help reduce feelings of nausea and vomiting. It works by desensitizing receptors in the digestive tract that trigger nausea.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for nausea.

    1. Peel and grate 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger root.
    2. In a teapot, combine grated ginger with 1 cup of water and boil for 10 minutes.
    3. Strain the ginger-water mixture into a cup, discarding the solid ginger.
    4. Add honey and lemon juice to taste, if desired.
    5. Sip the ginger tea slowly while it is still warm to soothe nausea.

    2. Peppermint

    Peppermint tea alleviates nausea due to its active components, particularly menthol.

    This compound relaxes the gastrointestinal tract, reducing symptoms of nausea. Furthermore, peppermint tea's soothing warmth stimulates the vagus nerve, which controls stomach emptying. Consequently, this process eases feelings of nausea.

    Additionally, peppermint tea's aroma activates the brain's satisfaction center, leading to a decrease in nausea sensation.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in peppermint tea that help with nausea.

    • Leaves: Rich in menthol and menthone, peppermint leaves are well-known for soothing the stomach and alleviating nausea symptoms.
    • Flowers: High in volatile oils, peppermint flowers offer calming effects on the digestive system, helping to reduce nausea.
    • Stems: With similar properties to leaves, peppermint stems contribute to the tea's soothing effects on the stomach and aid in easing nausea.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in peppermint tea that aid with nausea.

    • Menthol: Menthol has anesthetic properties that help to relax the gastrointestinal tract, reducing the symptoms of nausea.
    • Mentone: Mentone is a volatile oil that helps to stimulate the stomach, improving digestion and alleviating feelings of nausea.
    • Limonene: Limonene is a terpene that helps to reduce inflammation in the stomach, providing relief from nausea caused by gastric inflammation.
    • Flavanones: Flavanones have antispasmodic properties that help to relax the smooth muscles of the gastrointestinal tract, reducing the symptoms of nausea.
    • Eudesmol: Eudesmol is a sesquiterpene that has been shown to have anti-emetic properties, making it effective in reducing feelings of nausea.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for nausea.

    1. In a teapot, combine 1 cup of boiling water with 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves.
    2. Steep the tea for 5-10 minutes to allow the peppermint leaves to release their oils.
    3. Strain the tea to remove the used peppermint leaves.
    4. Optionally, add honey or lemon to taste.
    5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your stomach and alleviate nausea.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in licorice tea that aid with nausea.

    • Liquiritin: This flavonoid has been found to have anti-emetic effects, meaning it can help to reduce feelings of nausea and vomiting.
    • Glabridin: This compound has been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to reduce nausea caused by inflammation in the digestive tract.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for nausea.

    2. Steep the tea for 10-15 minutes to allow the active compounds to infuse.

    4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste.
    5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat your mouth and throat, and soothe your stomach.

    What is nausea and how can it affect your life?

    Nausea is a common medical symptom characterized by an unpleasant feeling of stomach discomfort that often accompanies the urge to vomit.

    Its impact on people's lives is significant, as it can lead to dehydration, malnutrition, and decreased quality of life due to discomfort and avoidance of activities that trigger symptoms.

    Furthermore, nausea often co-occurs with other medical conditions, making diagnosis and treatment more challenging, and perpetuating its adverse effects on overall health and well-being.

    What other natural remedies help with nausea?

    Among the most common and effective natural remedies for nausea are ginger, peppermint, and acupressure.

    Ginger, a root that has long been used in traditional medicine, contains compounds that help alleviate inflammation and relax the gastrointestinal tract, thereby reducing feelings of nausea. Peppermint, another natural remedy, boasts menthol, a compound that soothes the stomach and helps control vomiting. Acupressure, an ancient Chinese practice, applies pressure to specific points on the body, including the P6 point located on the wrist, to stimulate the release of endorphins and serotonin, which reduce the sensation of nausea.

    These remedies have proven effectiveness and are widely recognized for their ability to provide relief from nausea.

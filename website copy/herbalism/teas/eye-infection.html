    10 best herbal teas for eye infection

    Several herbal teas offer soothing relief for eye infections due to their anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

    Chamomile tea, known for its calming effects, contains hypoallergenic properties that can alleviate irritation. Eyebright tea, as the name suggests, is a popular choice for treating eye-related ailments.

    This herbal infusion boasts antimicrobial and astringent qualities, helping to reduce inflammation and fight infection. Goldenseal tea is another potent option, rich in berberine, which has been shown to combat bacterial infections.

    Lastly, calendula tea, derived from marigold flowers, possesses antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties, making it an ideal choice for soothing infected eyes.

    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea contains healing properties that address eye infections directly.

    Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory components effectively combat bacteria causing the infection while reducing redness and swelling. Chamomile's flavonoids act as potent antioxidants, combating free radicals and boosting the immune system's response. Furthermore, its astringent qualities help soothe irritation and alleviate discomfort in the affected eye area.

    Therefore, drinking chamomile tea or using chamomile tea bags as a compress provides immediate relief and accelerates the healing process during an eye infection.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with eye infection.

    • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe irritated eyes.
    • Leaves: Have antimicrobial and astringent qualities, aiding in preventing and treating eye infections.
    • Seeds: Rich in antioxidants, they support overall eye health and help combat infection.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with eye infection.

    • Bisabolol: Possesses anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce irritation and swelling in the eye.
    • Chamazulene: Its potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects aid in combating eye infections.
    • Apigenin: Demonstrates antimicrobial properties, which assist in eliminating bacteria causing eye infections.
    • Matricin: Converts to chamazulene, further enhancing anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects on eye infections.
    • Quercetin: Acts as an antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent, helping alleviate symptoms of eye infections.
    • Luteolin: Shows antimicrobial properties, inhibiting the growth of bacteria causing eye infections.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for eye infection.

    1. Measure 1 teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers and place them in a tea infuser or strainer.
    2. Boil 1 cup of water and pour it over the chamomile flowers.
    3. Let the tea steep for 5 to 10 minutes.
    4. Remove the tea infuser or strainer and allow the tea to cool down to a warm temperature.
    5. Use a clean eye dropper to apply the cooled chamomile tea to the infected eye, blinking several times to distribute the tea. Repeat this process 3 to 4 times a day.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic goldenseal tea for eye infection.

    1. Measure - Pour 1 cup of water into a saucepan and add 1 teaspoon of dried goldenseal root.
    2. Heat - Place the saucepan on high heat and bring the water to a boil.
    3. Steep - Reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let the goldenseal root simmer for 10 minutes.
    4. Strain - Remove the saucepan from heat and strain the tea into a glass, discarding the goldenseal root.
    5. Cool - Let the tea cool to a comfortable temperature before using as a warm compress on the infected eye.

    2. Let the fennel steep for 10 minutes, covered, to extract its beneficial compounds.
    3. Strain the tea to remove the solid fennel particles, ensuring a clear liquid.
    4. Allow the fennel tea to cool down to a comfortably warm temperature, avoiding excessive heat.
    5. Saturate a clean cloth with the cooled fennel tea and gently apply it as a compress over the infected eye for 10-15 minutes.

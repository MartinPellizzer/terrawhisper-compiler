    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea contains active compounds, including flavonoids and terpenoids, which possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

    These compounds help reduce swelling and redness in the affected area, providing relief from the discomfort of a stye. Moreover, chamomile tea's antiseptic qualities aid in fighting bacterial infections, common in styes. By applying chamomile tea compresses to the affected eye, the tannins present in chamomile flowers can help draw out excess fluid, promoting drainage and healing.

    Lastly, chamomile tea's soothing and calming effects can alleviate pain and irritation, making it an effective natural remedy for styes.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with stye.

    • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and redness in styes.
    • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants that promote healing and soothe irritated skin around the stye.
    • Seeds: Contain flavonoids that have antibacterial properties, helping to fight infection and prevent the spread of styes.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with stye.

    • Alpha-Bisabolol: This terpene alcohol has strong anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and redness in a stye.
    • Apigenin: This flavonoid acts as a potent anti-inflammatory agent, helping to alleviate the inflammation and discomfort caused by a stye.
    • Chamazulene: This aromatic compound possesses anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, aiding in reducing stye inflammation and fighting potential infection.
    • Matricin: This sesquiterpene lactone is converted to chamazulene during steam distillation, enhancing chamomile's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects on a stye.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for stye.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water in a kettle or pot for 3-5 minutes.
    2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or strainer in a cup.
    3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and let steep for 5-10 minutes.
    4. Remove the tea infuser or strainer and discard the used chamomile flowers.
    5. Allow the chamomile tea to cool down to a warm temperature before using as a compress for the stye.

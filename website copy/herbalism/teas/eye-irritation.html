    10 best herbal teas for eye irritation

    Herbal teas, such as chamomile and calendula, offer time-tested relief for eye irritation.

    Chamomile's anti-inflammatory properties help soothe irritated eyes, while calendula's antibacterial qualities aid in preventing infection. To prepare, steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried herbs in boiling water for 5-10 minutes. Once cooled, strain and use the liquid as a warm compress to alleviate discomfort.

    Regularly consuming these herbal teas also boosts overall eye health, ensuring long-lasting relief from irritation and inflammation.

    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea aids eye irritation through its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling and redness.

    This herbal tea contains antioxidants like terpenoids and flavonoids that help soothe irritated eyes. The cooling effect of chamomile tea bags, when applied topically, offers immediate relief to strained or itchy eyes. Furthermore, chamomile's antimicrobial qualities can combat infection-induced eye irritation.

    Lastly, regular consumption of chamomile tea promotes overall eye health due to its high concentration of antioxidants and essential nutrients.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with eye irritation.

    • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe eye irritation.
    • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants that can reduce redness and puffiness in the eyes.
    • Seeds: Have essential oils that possess therapeutic qualities to alleviate itchy and watery eyes.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with eye irritation.

    • Apigenin: A flavonoid that has antioxidant properties and can reduce inflammation, helping to alleviate eye irritation.
    • Chamazulene: A aromatic compound that has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, helping to soothe irritated eyes.
    • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin also has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce eye irritation.
    • Bisabolol: A sesquiterpene alcohol that has anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and anti-irritant properties, making it effective in reducing eye irritation.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for eye irritation.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water and let 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers steep for 5-10 minutes.
    2. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers.
    3. Allow the tea to cool down to a warm temperature.
    4. Soak a clean cloth in the tea and place it over closed eyes for 10-15 minutes.
    5. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day until eye irritation subsides.

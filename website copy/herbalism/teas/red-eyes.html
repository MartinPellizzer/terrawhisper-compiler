    10 best herbal teas for red eyes

    Another excellent choice is calendula tea, renowned for its ability to alleviate inflammation and ease discomfort. Additionally, fennel tea has antimicrobial qualities that aid in combating eye infections, which can lead to red eyes.

    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties, primarily due to its high concentration of flavonoids.

    These compounds help reduce inflammation and irritation in the eyes, providing relief to red eyes. Furthermore, the tea's soothing warmth increases blood flow to the eye area, which facilitates the draining of excess fluid from the eye tissue. The astringent qualities of chamomile also help to constrict blood vessels around the eyes, diminishing the appearance of redness.

    Drinking chamomile tea can additionally promote overall eye health, as it contains antioxidants that protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with red eyes.

    • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory properties that help alleviate red eyes.
    • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants that can soothe irritated eyes.
    • Seeds: Known for their calming effects, reducing redness and puffiness in eyes.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with red eyes.

    • Apigenin: A flavonoid that possesses antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, helping reduce red eyes by soothing irritation and inflammation.
    • Luteolin: A flavonoid with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, aiding in reducing red eyes by protecting and healing damaged eye tissues.
    • Chamazulene: A compound formed during steam distillation of chamomile, having anti-inflammatory properties that help alleviate red eyes by decreasing swelling and irritation.
    • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, exhibiting anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving qualities that contribute to reducing redness and discomfort in eyes.
    • Bisabolol: A terpene alcohol known for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and soothing effects, helping with red eyes by alleviating irritation and promoting healing.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for red eyes.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water, then pour over 1 tablespoon of dried chamomile flowers in a teapot.
    2. Steep the chamomile flowers for 5-10 minutes, covered, to extract the medicinal compounds.
    3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent flowers.
    4. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
    5. Drink the tea slowly, sipping it gently, to soothe and reduce redness in the eyes.

    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea contains anti-inflammatory properties due to the presence of flavonoids, particularly apigenin and luteolin.

    These compounds inhibit the release of inflammatory molecules, reducing swelling in the eyes. The soothing warmth of chamomile tea, when applied as a compress, boosts blood circulation, aiding in the drainage of excess fluids and consequently decreasing the puffiness around the eyes.

    Moreover, the tea's antioxidants, such as quercetin, protect the delicate skin around the eyes from oxidative stress and environmental aggressors, preventing eye swelling. Additionally, chamomile tea's mild astringent properties help tighten the skin and decrease capillary permeability, leading to diminished swelling in the eye area.

    Consequently, chamomile tea can effectively alleviate eye swelling by targeting various underlying causes.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with eye swelling.

    • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce eye swelling.
    • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants that aid in soothing irritated eyes and decreasing swelling.
    • Seeds: Packed with flavonoids that have a calming effect, helping to alleviate eye swelling.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with eye swelling.

    • Apigenin: A flavonoid that has anti-inflammatory properties and helps reduce swelling.
    • Luteolin: A flavonoid that lowers inflammation and decreases eye puffiness.
    • Chamazulene: A chemical compound with antiphlogistic properties, reducing swelling and irritation.
    • Bisabolol: A terpene that has a strong anti-inflammatory effect on eye swelling.
    • Matricin: A chemical compound that gets converted to chamazulene, which has anti-inflammatory effects.
    • Quercetin: A flavonoid with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that decreases eye swelling.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for eye swelling.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water in a kettle or pot, reaching a full boil can take 3-5 minutes.
    2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot, steep for 5-10 minutes.
    3. Pour the hot chamomile tea into a clean, heat-resistant cup.
    4. Allow the tea to cool down until it reaches a comfortable temperature, approximately 5-10 minutes.
    5. Soak two clean, soft cloths in the chamomile tea and place them gently over closed eyes for 10-15 minutes.

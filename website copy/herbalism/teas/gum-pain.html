    10 best herbal teas for gum pain

    Several herbal teas offer proven benefits for gum pain.

    Chamomile tea, with its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, soothes irritated gums. Its calming effects also reduce stress, which can exacerbate gum problems.

    For increased protection, consider clove tea, containing eugenol, a potent natural anesthetic and antimicrobial agent. Additionally, research substantiates that sage tea, rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, successfully alleviates gum inflammation and pain.

    Lastly, calendula tea, reputed for its healing properties, accelerates tissue regeneration and effectively combats gum-related issues.

    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea contains anti-inflammatory properties due to the presence of flavonoids, particularly a compound called chamazulene.

    This anti-inflammatory effect helps reduce swelling and soreness in the gums. Moreover, chamomile's antiseptic and mild anesthetic properties further alleviate pain by inhibiting the growth of harmful bacteria and numbing the affected area. The soothing warmth of the tea also increases blood flow, facilitating faster healing.

    Furthermore, chamomile tea's antioxidants combat oxidative stress, supporting overall oral health and reducing the risk of gum issues.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with gum pain.

    • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce gum inflammation.
    • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and flavonoids that aid in soothing gum pain.
    • Seeds: Contain a high amount of plant oils that promote oral health and alleviate gum discomfort.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with gum pain.

    • Bisabolol: A terpene with anti-inflammatory properties that helps reduce gum pain and swelling.
    • Apigenin: A flavonoid that possesses antibacterial properties, helping to maintain oral health and alleviate gum pain.
    • Chamazulene: A chemical compound with strong anti-inflammatory effects, reducing gum inflammation and pain.
    • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antiphlogistic properties, helping to soothe irritated gums.
    • Quercetin: A flavonoid with potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, aiding in the relief of gum pain and inflammation.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for gum pain.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water in a kettle for 3-5 minutes.
    2. Pour hot water over 1 tablespoon of dried chamomile flowers in a teapot.
    3. Steep the chamomile flowers in hot water for 5-10 minutes.
    4. Strain the chamomile flowers from the tea using a fine-mesh strainer.
    5. Sip the chamomile tea slowly, allowing it to rinse your mouth and soothe gum pain.

    2. Echinacea

    Echinacea tea relieves gum pain due to its potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, derived from active compounds such as alkamides and caffeic acid.

    These substances inhibit the production of prostaglandins and leukotrienes, chemicals that trigger inflammation and pain in the body. Moreover, echinacea boosts the immune system, promoting faster healing of damaged gum tissues. Its astringent qualities also tighten gum tissues, reducing pain and preventing further infection.

    Lastly, echinacea's antimicrobial effects combat harmful bacteria in the oral cavity, thus preventing gum inflammation and subsequent pain.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in echinacea tea that help with gum pain.

    • Roots: Contain high levels of antioxidants and have anti-inflammatory properties, helping to reduce gum pain and inflammation.
    • Aerial parts: Consist of leaves, stems, and flowers, and are often used interchangeably with "leaves" in medicinal teas.
    • Leaves: Rich in tannins that have astringent properties, helping to reduce gum pain and inflammation.
    • Flowers: Contain antioxidants and have anti-inflammatory properties, aiding in gum pain relief.
    • Seeds: Packed with antioxidants and have a mild astringent effect, promoting gum health.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in echinacea tea that aid with gum pain.

    • Essential oils: Contain anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that help alleviate gum pain.
    • Polysaccharides: Boost the immune system, promoting faster healing of infected or damaged gum tissue.
    • Alkamides: Exhibit pain-relieving and numbing effects, reducing discomfort caused by gum pain.
    • Flavonoids: Possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help soothe irritated gums and reduce swelling.
    • Cichoric acid: Acts as an antimicrobial, helping to prevent infections that may cause gum pain.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic echinacea tea for gum pain.

    1. Gather 2 teaspoons of dried echinacea flowers and leaves, and 1 cup of water.
    2. Pour the water into a small pot and bring it to a boil.
    3. Add the echinacea flowers and leaves to the boiling water, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
    4. Strain the tea, discarding the solids, and let it cool until drinkable but still warm.
    5. Drink the entire tea preparation up to 3 times a day for gum pain relief.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic cloves tea for gum pain.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water, then add 3-5 crushed cloves, letting it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
    2. Pour the clove-infused water through a strainer to remove clove particles.
    3. Add a slice of lemon and 1 teaspoon of honey to the clove tea for added soothing properties.
    4. Stir the mixture until the honey dissolves completely.
    5. Sip the warm cloves tea slowly, allowing it to gently swish around the painful gum area.

    2. Bring the water to a boil in a saucepan.

    4. Strain the tea, separating the liquid from the solid licorice pieces.

    4. Strain the tea into a mug, discarding the ginger pieces.
    5. Sip the ginger tea slowly, allowing it to swirl around the painful gum area for 10 minutes.

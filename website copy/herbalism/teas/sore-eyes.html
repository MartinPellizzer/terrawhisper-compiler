    10 best herbal teas for sore eyes

    Herbal teas, such as chamomile and calendula, offer natural remedies for sore eyes.

    Chamomile's anti-inflammatory properties help soothe irritation, while calendula's antimicrobial qualities promote eye health by fighting off infections. Steep a tea bag of either herb in hot water, let it cool, and apply the compress to closed eyes for 10-15 minutes.

    Both teas bring relief, ensuring comfort and well-being.

    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea assists with sore eyes due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling and redness.

    This herbal tea contains antioxidants that help soothe irritated eyes by neutralizing free radicals. Furthermore, chamomile has a calming effect, which can alleviate the discomfort caused by strain or tiredness. The tea's heat, when used as a compress, can increase blood flow to the area, promoting healing and reducing pain.

    Lastly, chamomile demonstrates antibacterial qualities, helping to protect the eyes from infection.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with sore eyes.

    • Flowers: Contain active compounds known to reduce inflammation, making them ideal for soothing sore eyes.
    • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and possess anti-inflammatory properties that help alleviate discomfort in the eyes.
    • Seeds: Packed with soothing oils that can be steeped in tea to provide relief for sore and irritated eyes.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with sore eyes.

    • Alpha-Bisabolol: Possesses anti-inflammatory properties that reduce irritation and swelling in sore eyes.
    • Apigenin: Exhibits antimicrobial effects that help combat eye infections causing soreness.
    • Chamazulene: Known for its soothing properties, it alleviates pain and irritation in sore eyes.
    • Matricin: Aids in reducing inflammation and easing discomfort associated with sore eyes.
    • Flavonoids: Possess antioxidant properties that protect eyes from oxidative stress and help soothe soreness.
    • Coumarins: Known to improve blood flow and reduce inflammation, helpful in treating sore eyes.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for sore eyes.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water and let 1 chamomile tea bag steep for 5-7 minutes.
    2. Remove tea bag and allow tea to cool down to a warm temperature.
    3. Soak a clean cloth in the chamomile tea.
    4. Squeeze out excess liquid and place the cloth over closed eyes for 10-15 minutes.
    5. Discard used tea and cloth. Repeat as needed for relief.

    2. Eyebright

    Eyebright tea assists with sore eyes due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, derived from active compounds like aucubin, caffeic acid, and tannins.

    These substances work together to reduce swelling, soothe irritation, and alleviate pain in the eyes. Furthermore, eyebright tea inhibits the production of histamine, a compound that contributes to eye inflammation, providing additional relief. Additionally, eyebright tea boasts antimicrobial qualities, disrupting the growth of bacteria responsible for eye infections, thereby promoting eye health and combating sore eyes.

    Lastly, the astringent properties of eyebright tea tighten and tone eye tissues, decreasing eye redness and promoting overall eye comfort.

    • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory compounds that help soothe irritated eyes.
    • Leaves: Rich in tannins that have astringent properties, reducing redness and swelling.
    • Seeds: Packed with antioxidants that protect the eyes from damage and promote healing.
    • Stems: Contain essential oils with calming effects that help alleviate sore eyes.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in eyebright tea that aid with sore eyes.

    • Aucubin: A potent anti-inflammatory compound that helps reduce eye inflammation and redness.
    • Genistein: An isoflavone with antioxidant properties that protects the eyes from oxidative stress and damage.
    • Luteolin: A flavonoid with potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects that help soothe sore eyes.
    • Rutin: A bioflavonoid that strengthens blood vessels, improves circulation, and reduces eye strain.
    • Isoquercitrin: A flavonoid that reduces inflammation, alleviates redness and irritation, and promotes eye health.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic eyebright tea for sore eyes.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water and add 1 teaspoon of dried eyebright herb, simmer for 10 minutes.
    2. Strain the tea to remove the herb and let it cool down for 5 minutes.
    3. Squeeze juice of half a lemon into the tea, stir well.
    4. Add 1 teaspoon of honey and mix until fully dissolved.
    5. Drink the tea while warm, 2-3 times a day for sore eyes relief.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginkgo biloba tea for sore eyes.

    4. Remove the infuser or strain the tea to discard leaves, preventing over-steeped bitterness.
    5. Sip the warm ginkgo biloba tea, allowing it to soothe and potentially alleviate sore eye discomfort.

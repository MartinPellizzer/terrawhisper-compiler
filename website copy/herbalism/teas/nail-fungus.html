    10 best herbal teas for nail fungus

    Here are three herbal teas that effectively treat nail fungus: green tea, chamomile tea, and cinnamon tea.

    Green tea contains high levels of epigallocatechin-3-gallate (EGCG), a potent antifungal agent. Regular consumption of chamomile tea promotes nail health due to its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. Cinnamon tea, made from cinnamon sticks or powder, has strong antifungal and antimicrobial properties, making it an excellent choice for treating nail fungus.

    Enjoy these herbal teas as part of your daily routine to strengthen your nails and inhibit fungal growth.

    1. Green Tea

    Green tea contains powerful antifungal agents, specifically catechins, which directly target and destroy the fungal cells causing nail fungus.

    These catechins, particularly EGCG, disrupt the membrane of fungal cells, leading to their death and preventing further infection. Green tea's anti-inflammatory properties help alleviate irritation and inflammation caused by nail fungus infections. As a rich source of antioxidants, green tea aids in boosting the immune system and improving overall health, supporting the body's natural defense mechanisms against fungal infections.

    Regular consumption of green tea, either internally or topically, complements medical treatments and enhances the recovery process from nail fungus.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in green tea tea that help with nail fungus.

    • Leaves: Contain high levels of catechins, which have antifungal properties beneficial for treating nail fungus.
    • Buds: Rich in polyphenols, known for their potent antimicrobial effects, aiding in the elimination of nail fungus.
    • Stems: Provide structural support and contain bioactive compounds that contribute to the overall effectiveness of medicinal tea for nail fungus treatment.
    • Flowers: Contain antioxidants and essential oils that can help inhibit the growth of fungi causing nail infections.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in green tea tea that aid with nail fungus.

    • Catechins: These are antioxidants that possess antimicrobial properties, helping to combat nail fungus.
    • Epigallocatechin Gallate: This catechin inhibits the growth of fungi causing nail infections.
    • Epicatechin Gallate: ECG has antifungal effects, disrupting the cell membrane of nail fungus.
    • Epicatechin: EC exhibits antimicrobial properties that help control nail fungus.
    • Gallic Acid: This polyphenol has antifungal properties, aiding in the treatment of nail fungus.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic green tea tea for nail fungus.

    1. Boil 2 cups of water and let four green tea bags steep for 10 minutes.
    2. Remove tea bags and let the liquid cool until it reaches a comfortable temperature.
    3. Add 1/2 cup of vinegar and 1/2 cup of Epsom salt, stirring until fully dissolved.
    4. Soak affected nails in the solution for 15 minutes, twice a day.
    5. Thoroughly dry nails after each soak to prevent further fungal growth.

    2. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea contains powerful antifungal and antimicrobial properties, scientifically proven to combat various fungal infections.

    The key active compounds in chamomile, such as bisabolol, suppress fungal growth and promote healing. By drinking chamomile tea or using chamomile extract topically, the ingredients absorb into the bloodstream and reach the nails, targeting the fungal infection directly. Regular consumption or application of chamomile ensures a consistent supply of these active compounds, inhibiting further fungal growth and fostering healthier nails.

    Ultimately, chamomile tea's fungicidal properties make it an effective natural remedy for nail fungus, providing a gentle and soothing solution for those seeking an alternative to conventional treatments.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with nail fungus.

    • Flowers: Rich in antifungal compounds, such as chamazulene and alpha-bisabolol.
    • Leaves: Contain terpenoids and flavonoids with antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties.
    • Seeds: Packed with essential oils that show antifungal and antioxidant qualities.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with nail fungus.

    • Alpha-Bisabolol: Possesses potent antifungal properties that directly combat nail fungus.
    • Apigenin: Demonstrates antimicrobial abilities that inhibit the growth of nail fungus.
    • Chamazulene: Provides strong anti-inflammatory effects, helping to alleviate discomfort caused by nail fungus.
    • Matricin: Converts to Chamazulene, enhancing its anti-inflammatory properties, easing nail fungus-related pain.
    • Flavonoids: Offer antioxidant and antimicrobial benefits, contributing to the overall fungus-fighting capabilities of chamomile tea.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for nail fungus.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan for 3-5 minutes.
    2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to the boiling water.
    3. Steep the chamomile flowers in the hot water for 10-15 minutes.
    4. Strain the chamomile tea into a cup, discarding the spent flowers.
    5. Allow the tea to cool down to a warm temperature before soaking nails for 15-20 minutes.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in peppermint tea that aid with nail fungus.

    • Menthol: Possesses fungicidal properties that directly inhibit the growth of fungal nail infections.
    • Limonene: Boasts antifungal abilities that aid in the eradication of nail fungus.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for nail fungus.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water, then steep 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves for 10 minutes.
    2. Strain the tea, discarding the leaves, and let it cool to a comfortable temperature.
    3. Add 1-2 drops of pure peppermint essential oil to the tea.
    4. Soak nails in the tea for 15-20 minutes, twice a day.
    5. Store leftover tea in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours, reheating before each use.

    2. Strain the tea to remove the basil leaves, preserving the liquid.
    3. Add 1 teaspoon of lemon juice and 1 teaspoon of honey to the basil tea, stirring until the honey dissolves.
    4. Allow the tea to cool down to a warm temperature, but not hot enough to cause discomfort to the affected nail.
    5. Soak the affected nail in the basil tea for 15-20 minutes, then rinse with clean water and gently pat dry.

    2. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20 minutes to extract the ginger's medicinal properties.
    3. Strain the ginger tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a teapot or mug, and discard the ginger pieces.
    4. Optionally, sweeten the tea with 1 teaspoon of honey to improve the taste (heating honey reduces its therapeutic benefits).
    5. Drink the warm ginger tea up to three times a day, after meals, for several weeks to help treat nail fungus.

    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea aids dermatitis by possessing anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which reduce skin irritation and inflammation.

    Its antioxidant-rich composition neutralizes free radicals, promoting skin cell regeneration and fostering a healthy dermatitis-free skin. Moreover, chamomile tea contains azulene, a natural chemical known for its soothing effects on skin conditions like dermatitis, while its ability to moisturize the skin alleviates dryness and itching.

    Ultimately, the regular consumption of chamomile tea bolsters the immune system, enhancing the body's capacity to combat dermatitis triggers and accelerating the healing process.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with dermatitis.

    • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory properties that help relieve skin irritation and redness associated with dermatitis.
    • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants that support skin health and help soothe inflamed skin.
    • Seeds: Contain essential oils with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that aid in treating dermatitis.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with dermatitis.

    • Apigenin: A flavonoid that possesses antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, helping reduce redness and irritation associated with dermatitis.
    • Chamazulene: A chemical compound that has potent anti-inflammatory effects, helping alleviate skin swelling and pain caused by dermatitis.
    • Alpha-Bisabolol: A sesquiterpene alcohol with soothing and healing properties, promoting skin repair and reducing dermatitis symptoms.
    • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, it exhibits anti-inflammatory and antiphlogistic (anti-inflammatory and analgesic) properties important for dermatitis treatment.
    • Quercetin: A flavonoid exhibiting strong antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, minimizing oxidative stress and inflammatory response that exacerbate dermatitis.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for dermatitis.

    1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan, bringing it to a full rolling boil.
    2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot.
    3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and let them steep for 5-10 minutes.
    4. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers, then allow it to cool slightly.
    5. Drink the chamomile tea 2-3 times a day, especially before bedtime, for best results.

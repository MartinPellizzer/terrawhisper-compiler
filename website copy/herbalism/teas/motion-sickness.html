    1. Ginger

    Ginger, a primary ingredient in ginger tea, contains gingerols and shogaols, compounds known for their ability to alleviate nausea and vomiting.

    These active components work by neutralizing gastric contractions and reducing stomach irritability, thereby preventing the onset of motion sickness. Moreover, ginger tea demonstrates anti-inflammatory properties, which help soothe the lining of the gastrointestinal tract and further combat motion sickness symptoms.     Furthermore, ginger tea's calming effect on the digestive system results in a notable reduction of motion sickness symptoms such as dizziness, cold sweats, and queasiness.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in ginger tea that help with motion sickness.

    • Rhizomes: Ginger tea's primary ingredient, containing active compounds that aid in alleviating symptoms of motion sickness.
    • Leaves: Contain anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, helping settle the stomach during motion sickness.
    • Flowers: Offer mild sedative qualities, aiding in relaxation during motion sickness.
    • Seeds: Contain essential oils and compounds, reducing nausea and vomiting associated with motion sickness.
    • Buds: Packed with bioactive compounds and antioxidants that provide relief from motion sickness symptoms.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginger tea that aid with motion sickness.

    • Gingerols: Gingerols are compounds in ginger that help to reduce nausea and vomiting by acting as a natural antiemetic.
    • Shogaols: Shogaols are compounds in ginger that help to relieve motion sickness symptoms by reducing inflammation in the stomach.
    • Paradols: Paradols are compounds in ginger that help to soothe the stomach and reduce feelings of queasiness, making them effective in preventing motion sickness.
    • Zingerone: Zingerone is a compound in ginger that helps to relax the gastrointestinal muscles, reducing the symptoms of motion sickness such as nausea and vomiting.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for motion sickness.

    1. Peel and grate 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger, then let it steep in boiling water for 10 minutes.
    2. Strain the ginger pieces from the water, leaving behind the ginger-infused liquid.
    3. Add 1 teaspoon of honey and the juice of half a lemon to the ginger water, stirring to combine.
    4. Warm the tea on low heat for 2 minutes, ensuring it doesn't reach a boil.
    5. Pour the ginger tea into a cup and sip slowly to alleviate motion sickness.

    10 best herbal teas for breast pain during breastfeeding

    Several herbal teas, including fenugreek, fennel, and blessed thistle, bring documented relief to breast pain during breastfeeding.

    Fenugreek contains phytoestrogens known to increase milk supply and soothe engorged breasts, while fennel's anti-inflammatory properties alleviate discomfort. Blessed thistle, another galactagogue, boosts milk production and eases breast pain.

    These teas, available as loose leaves or tea bags, bring natural, caffeine-free comfort to breastfeeding mothers.

    1. Fennel

    Fennel tea provides relief for breast pain during breastfeeding due to its documented ability to relax smooth muscles, including the milk ducts in the breast.

    This relief stems from the presence of compounds such as anethole, fenchone, and estragole, which have antispasmodic properties. By reducing spasms and inflammation in the milk ducts, fennel tea alleviates engorgement and plugged ducts, two common causes of breast pain during breastfeeding. Furthermore, fennel's galactagogue properties can enhance milk production, easing breast fullness and, consequently, pain.

    Double-blind studies have demonstrated fennel tea's effectiveness in addressing breast pain related to breastfeeding, corroborating its value as a natural remedy for nursing mothers.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in fennel tea that help with breast pain during breastfeeding.

    • Seeds: Contain compounds that help reduce inflammation, making them useful for alleviating breast pain during breastfeeding.
    • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties, promoting breast health and pain relief.
    • Leaves: High in phytoestrogens, helping to balance hormones and alleviate breast pain caused by hormonal imbalances.
    • Rhyzomes: Contain volatile oils that have analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, making them useful for reducing breast pain.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in fennel tea that aid with breast pain during breastfeeding.

    • Anethole: Acts as a phytoestrogen, helping to balance hormones and reduce breast pain.
    • Fenchone: Has anti-inflammatory properties, which can alleviate engorgement and swelling.
    • Limonene: Promotes milk flow while soothing breast tissue, reducing discomfort.
    • Alpha-phellandrene: Exhibits analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, helping to manage breast pain.
    • Beta-myrcene: Aids in reducing inflammation and alleviating breast pain caused by engorgement.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic fennel tea for breast pain during breastfeeding.

    1. Chop 1 tablespoon of fresh fennel seeds or 1 teaspoon of fennel seeds powder.
    2. Boil 1 cup of water and add the chopped fennel seeds.
    3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
    4. Strain the tea and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
    5. Drink the tea, up to 3 times a day, to relieve breast pain during breastfeeding.

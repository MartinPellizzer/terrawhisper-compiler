    1. Peppermint

    Peppermint tea aids in cough relief due to its active ingredient, menthol, which possesses strong antitussive and expectorant properties.

    This organic compound works by thinning mucus, making it easier to expel, and by numbing the throat to reduce the cough reflex. Moreover, peppermint tea soothes inflammation in the respiratory tract, diminishing irritation that can trigger coughing fits. Furthermore, its warmth helps to alleviate congestion by increasing blood circulation to the chest area, promoting overall respiratory health.

    Overall, peppermint tea's menthol content and anti-inflammatory properties make it an effective natural remedy for cough suppression.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in peppermint tea that help with cough.

    • Leaves: Rich in menthol, leaves help soothe throat irritation and relieve coughing.
    • Flowers: Contain essential oils and antioxidants, flowers aid in reducing inflammation and easing congestion.
    • Stems: Packed with rosmarinic acid, stems have antispasmodic properties that help suppress coughing fits.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in peppermint tea that aid with cough.

    • Menthol: Acts as a natural decongestant and suppressant, helping to soothe coughs and clear airways.
    • Rosmarinic acid: Has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract, alleviating coughs and other respiratory symptoms.
    • Limonene: Possesses antibacterial and antiviral properties that can help protect against respiratory infections, reducing coughs and other symptoms.
    • Ascorbic acid: Boosts the immune system, helping the body fight off infections and reducing the severity and duration of coughs.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for cough.

    1. In a small saucepan, combine 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves.
    2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
    3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let the tea cool for a few minutes.
    4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the peppermint leaves.
    5. Sip the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day as needed for cough relief.

    What is cough and how can it affect your life?

    A cough is a forceful expulsion of air from the lungs, often accompanied by a distinct sound.

    This natural reflex helps clear the airways of irritants, such as mucus, smoke, or foreign particles. However, persistent or chronic coughing can lead to various complications, disrupting sleep patterns and causing fatigue.

    In social situations, constant coughing can lead to embarrassment and anxiety, affecting overall quality of life. Moreover, it can result in physical discomfort, including chest pain and muscle strain. In extreme cases, coughing can cause rib fractures or even lead to fainting.

    Thus, a cough is not merely a simple reflex but can significantly impact a person's life in numerous ways.

    The saline solution helps to reduce inflammation in the throat, providing relief from irritation and coughing.

    What other health issues people with cough are likely to experience?

    The issues people with cough are likely to experiece are listed below.

    • Sore throat: Coughing irritates the throat, causing it to become inflamed and sore.
    • Stuffy nose: Postnasal drip from a cough can lead to inflammation and congestion in the nose.
    • Runny nose: Coughing can cause mucus to build up and drip from the back of the nose, resulting in a runny nose.
    • Shortness of breath: A severe or persistent cough can cause labored breathing and lead to shortness of breath.
    • Headache: The forceful muscle contractions involved in coughing can cause strain on the muscles of the chest and neck, leading to tension headaches.
    • Fatigue: Frequent coughing can be physically demanding and cause exhaustion, leading to fatigue.
    • Muscle aches: The constant muscle contractions from coughing can cause soreness and fatigue in the ribcage, abdomen, and other muscles.
    • Fever: A fever often indicates that the body is fighting an infection, which can be the cause of the cough.
    • Chills: Chills often accompany a fever and are the body's response to an infection or illness.
    • Hoarseness: Repeated or forceful coughing can strain the vocal cords, causing hoarseness or a weak voice.
    • Wheezing: A chronic or severe cough can cause narrowing of the airways, resulting in wheezing.

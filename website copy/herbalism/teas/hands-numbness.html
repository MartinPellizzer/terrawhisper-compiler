    • Seeds: Rich in vitamin E and essential fatty acids, promoting nerve health and reducing hand numbness.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginkgo tea for numbness in hands.

    1. Boil 2 cups of water in a kettle, reaching a temperature of 200°F to 212°F.
    2. Add 1 tablespoon of dried ginkgo leaves to a teapot or infuser.
    3. Pour the hot water over the ginkgo leaves, allowing them to steep for 3 to 5 minutes.
    4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent leaves.
    5. Drink the ginkgo tea up to 3 times a day, promoting numbness relief in hands.

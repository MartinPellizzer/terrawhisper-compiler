    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic hawthorn tea for high blood pressure.

    2. Garlic

    What is high blood pressure and how can it affect your life?

    High blood pressure, also known as hypertension, is a medical condition in which the force of blood against the walls of blood vessels is consistently too high.

    This happens when the heart pumps blood with excessive force, causing the blood vessels to experience greater pressure. Over time, high blood pressure damages the arteries, leading to serious health complications such as heart disease, stroke, and kidney failure.

    People with high blood pressure often face limitations in their daily lives, such as restrictions on physical activities, medication regimens, and potential loss of independence due to disability or severe health issues.

    What other natural remedies help with high blood pressure?

    One of the most effective natural remedies for managing high blood pressure is maintaining a healthy diet.

    The DASH (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) diet is a well-established eating plan that is proven to lower blood pressure. This diet encourages the consumption of fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and low-fat dairy products while limiting sodium, saturated fats, and added sugars.

    Regular physical activity is another natural remedy that is highly effective in managing high blood pressure. The American Heart Association recommends at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous aerobic exercise per week. Additionally, managing stress through techniques such as meditation, deep breathing, and yoga can significantly lower blood pressure.

    Finally, maintaining a healthy weight is crucial in managing high blood pressure, as even a small amount of weight loss can lead to a significant reduction in blood pressure.

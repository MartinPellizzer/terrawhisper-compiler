    10 best herbal teas for hair fall

    Certain herbal teas promote hair growth and reduce hair fall, including nettle, rosemary, and horsetail tea.

    Nettle tea, rich in vitamins A, C, and D, as well as iron and calcium, strengthens hair follicles. Rosemary tea improves blood circulation in the scalp, stimulating hair growth. Horsetail tea contains silica and selenium, which strengthen hair and prevent breakage.

    Enjoying a few cups of these herbal teas fortifies your hair with essential nutrients, fostering a healthier, fuller head of hair.

    1. Nettle

    Nettle tea combats hair fall by blocking the conversion of testosterone into DHT, a hormone responsible for hair loss.

    This herbal tea contains rich amounts of silica and sulfur, which strengthen hair follicles and promote hair growth. Nettle tea also provides nutrients, such as vitamins A, C, and B, that nourish the scalp and hair, reducing breakage and shedding. Furthermore, this tea's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties enhance blood circulation in the scalp, encouraging robust hair development.

    Lastly, nettle tea's high mineral content, including iron, magnesium, and zinc, fortifies the hair shaft, preventing thinning and fallout.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in nettle tea that help with hair fall.

    • Leaves: Rich in nutrients and antioxidants, leaves promote hair growth and reduce hair fall.
    • Stems: Contain silica and calcium, which strengthen hair follicles and prevent breakage.
    • Roots: High in iron and antimicrobial compounds, roots improve blood circulation in the scalp and stimulate hair growth.
    • Seeds: Packed with essential fatty acids and vitamin E, seeds nourish the hair and scalp, promoting hair thickening and reducing hair fall.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in nettle tea that aid with hair fall.

    • Silica: Promotes hair growth and strength by boosting collagen production.
    • Sterols: Reduces inflammation and irritation, helping to maintain a healthy scalp.
    • Vitamin C: Encourages hair growth and repairs damaged hair follicles.
    • Chlorophyll: Stimulates circulation, ensuring nutrients reach hair follicles.
    • Flavonoids: Act as antioxidants, protecting hair follicles from damage.
    • Carotenoids: Improve hair growth and pigmentation.
    • Iron: Promotes hair growth by increasing red blood cell production.
    • Magnesium: Prevents hair fall by maintaining healthy hair follicles.
    • Potassium: Enhances hair growth and reduces hair fall.
    • Calcium: Nourishes hair, promoting thickness and strength.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic nettle tea for hair fall.

    1. Gather 2 cups of fresh nettle leaves, rinse, and place in a pot.
    2. Add 3 cups of water to the pot, bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
    3. Strain the liquid to remove the leaves, let it cool to a comfortable temperature.
    4. Mix 1 tablespoon of honey into the nettle tea for added benefits and to improve taste.
    5. Store any remaining tea in a covered container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

    • Rhizomes: The rhizomes are the part of the plant used because they have anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties, which can improve scalp health and reduce hair loss.

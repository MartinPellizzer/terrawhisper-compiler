    10 best herbal teas for inflammatory bowel disease

    For individuals seeking herbal tea alternatives to support inflammatory bowel disease management, chamomile and ginger teas are two notable options.

    Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, chamomile tea is known to promote gastrointestinal health and reduce inflammation. Ginger tea, on the other hand, has been shown to alleviate digestive discomforts such as nausea, bloating, and cramping, while also providing anti-inflammatory benefits.

    Both teas contribute to overall gut well-being and offer a natural approach to managing inflammatory bowel disease symptoms.

    1. Chamomile

    Chamomile tea contains anti-inflammatory properties due to the presence of flavonoids, particularly apigenin and luteolin.

    These compounds inhibit the secretion of inflammatory cytokines, reducing inflammation in the gut. Chamomile tea also boasts antispasmodic properties that alleviate intestinal cramping and pain, providing relief for individuals with inflammatory bowel disease.

    Moreover, its soothing effects on the gastrointestinal tract encourage regular bowel movements and improve overall digestion. By promoting mucus production, chamomile tea helps protect the gut lining from irritants, further reducing inflammation caused by inflammatory bowel disease.

    These mechanisms collectively contribute to the therapeutic benefits of chamomile tea in managing inflammatory bowel disease.

    Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with inflammatory bowel disease.

    • Flowers: Contain volatile oils with anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate inflammation in the digestive tract.
    • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids that possess potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, helping to reduce inflammation in inflammatory bowel disease.
    • Seeds: Contain therapeutic compounds, such as chamazulene and bisabolol, which have anti-inflammatory and spasmolytic effects, beneficial for managing symptoms of inflammatory bowel disease.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with inflammatory bowel disease.

    • Apigenin: A flavonoid that inhibits the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, reducing inflammation in the gut.
    • Luteolin: A flavonoid with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, helping to alleviate symptoms of inflammatory bowel disease.
    • Chamazulene: A terpene known for its anti-inflammatory effects, decreasing inflammation and promoting gut health.
    • Bisabolol: A sesquiterpene alcohol that has anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, helpful in reducing gut inflammation.
    • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin possesses anti-inflammatory properties, aiding in the relief of inflammatory bowel disease symptoms.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for inflammatory bowel disease.

    1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a kettle or pot, reaching a temperature of 212 degrees Fahrenheit.
    2. Place one chamomile tea bag or 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers in a teapot or mug.
    3. Pour the boiling water over the tea bag or dried flowers and steep for 5-10 minutes, covered.
    4. Remove the tea bag or strain out the flowers, discarding the solids.
    5. Sip the warm chamomile tea, allowing it to soothe your inflammatory bowel disease symptoms.

    2. Steep the peppermint in hot water for 5-10 minutes to allow the beneficial compounds to infuse.

    4. If desired, add honey or lemon to taste, but avoid milk and sugar as they can worsen inflammation.
    5. Pour the peppermint tea into a cup and drink it while warm, up to 3 cups per day, to soothe inflammatory bowel disease symptoms.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for inflammatory bowel disease.

    1. Peel and thinly slice 2 inches of fresh ginger root.
    2. Combine ginger slices and 4 cups of water in a saucepan; bring to a boil.
    3. Reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
    4. Strain tea into a cup, add honey to taste, and stir in 1 tablespoon of lemon juice.
    5. Drink warm, up to three times daily for soothing relief.

    6. Turmeric

    Turmeric tea offers help with inflammatory bowel disease due to its active ingredient, curcumin, a potent anti-inflammatory agent.

By reducing inflammation, turmeric tea alleviates symptoms such as diarrhea, abdominal pain, and rectal bleeding. Furthermore, curcumin's antioxidant properties counteract oxidative stress, an additional factor contributing to inflammatory bowel disease.

    Lastly, research indicates that curcumin enhances mucosal repair and maintains remission in patients with this condition.

    The list below shows the primary active constituents in turmeric tea that aid with inflammatory bowel disease.

    • Curcumin: A potent anti-inflammatory compound that blocks the activity of NF-kB, a protein complex involved in inflammatory bowel disease (IBD).
    • Turmerone: A compound shown to reduce inflammation by decreasing the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines in IBD.
    • Curdione: Exhibits anti-inflammatory properties by suppressing the activation of NLRP3 inflammasome, involved in the pathogenesis of IBD.

    The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric tea for inflammatory bowel disease.

    1. Boil 4 cups of water in a pot for 5-10 minutes.
    2. Stir in 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
    3. Strain the liquid into a cup, then mix in 1 teaspoon of honey and 1/4 teaspoon of ground black pepper.
    4. Add 1 cup of almond milk and reheat the tea on low heat for 2-3 minutes.
    5. Pour the tea into a cup and enjoy immediately for soothing benefits.

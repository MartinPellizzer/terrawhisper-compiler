What to know before using Ulmus rubra (Slippery Elm)?

Ulmus rubra, also known as slippery elm, is a botanically interesting tree known for its red, peeling bark and winged seeds.

It thrives in a variety of horticultural conditions, preferring moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, but able to tolerate a wide range of temperatures and soil types. Medicinally, slippery elm is highly valued for its inner bark, which contains mucilage that can be used to soothe digestive issues and inflammation. When the bark comes into contact with water, it creates a slippery, gel-like substance that can provide relief for irritations in the mouth, throat, and stomach.

Overall, Ulmus rubra is a versatile and useful tree, both for its medicinal properties and its ability to grow in a variety of conditions.

What to know before using Bacopa monnieri (Water Hyssop)?

Bacopa monnieri, also known as waterhyssop or brahmi, is a creeping aquatic plant native to wetlands across India, Australia, and South America.

It has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for centuries to improve memory, learning, and concentration. Bacopa monnieri thrives in moist soil and shallow water, making it a popular choice for water gardens and aquariums. The plant's small, succulent leaves and delicate flowers are a source of several bioactive compounds, including saponins and alkaloids, that have been shown to have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective properties.

These compounds have been studied for their potential to treat a variety of conditions, including anxiety, epilepsy, and Alzheimer's disease.

What are the medicinal uses of Bacopa monnieri?

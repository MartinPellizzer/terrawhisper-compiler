What to know about marshmallow before using it medicinally

Marshmallow, scientifically known as Althaea officinalis, is a herb that has been revered for centuries for its impressive health properties.

It contains mucilage, a gel-like substance that coats the mouth, throat, and digestive tract, providing a soothing and anti-inflammatory effect. This makes it an excellent remedy for sore throats, coughs, and digestive issues such as constipation and heartburn.

In the culinary world, marshmallow is used as a thickening agent in soups, stews, and sauces, and its sweet and mild taste makes it a popular ingredient in desserts and confectionery. As a hardy perennial, marshmallow thrives in moist soil and full sun, and its tall stems and soft, velvety leaves make it an attractive addition to any garden. The plant produces small pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer, and its roots and leaves are harvested for medicinal use.

Historically, marshmallow has been used in traditional medicine for thousands of years, with references to its use dating back to ancient Egypt, Greece, and Rome.

What are the side effects of marshmallow when used improperly?

When used improperly, marshmallow increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and potential interactions with medications.

Some individuals may be allergic to marshmallow and can develop symptoms such as skin rashes, hives, or difficulty breathing. Overconsumption of marshmallow can lead to digestive problems like diarrhea, bloating, and stomach discomfort.

Additionally, marshmallow can interact with certain medications, particularly those that are designed to be absorbed in the digestive tract. By coating the lining of the stomach and intestines, marshmallow can reduce the absorption of these medications, potentially reducing their effectiveness or causing unexpected side effects.

It is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using marshmallow for medicinal purposes, especially if you have a medical condition, are taking medications, or are pregnant or breastfeeding.

What precautions to take before using marshmallow medicinally?

The precautions to take before using marshmallow medicinally are crucial to ensure safe and effective use.

Although marshmallow is generally considered safe, it may cause side effects in some people. It is essential to perform a patch test before using marshmallow topically, as it can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.

Pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid using marshmallow without consulting a healthcare professional, as its safety during pregnancy and lactation has not been established. Moreover, people with diabetes should use marshmallow with caution, as it may lower blood sugar levels. Marshmallow may interact with certain medications, such as lithium and diabetes drugs, so it is vital to consult a healthcare provider before taking marshmallow internally.

Lastly, it is recommended to discontinue using marshmallow at least two weeks before scheduled surgeries, as it may interfere with blood sugar control during and after surgery.

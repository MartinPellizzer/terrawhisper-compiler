10 possible side effects of Salvia officinalis (Sage) if used improperly

Salvia officinalis, also known as garden sage, is a commonly used herb in cooking, but when used improperly, it can have potential health side effects.

Ingesting large amounts of sage, either raw or as a tea, can lead to dizziness, rapid heart rate, seizures, and even liver damage due to the presence of certain compounds like thujone. Pregnant women and those who are breastfeeding should avoid using sage in medicinal amounts, as it may negatively affect the menstrual cycle and can potentially lead to miscarriage. Additionally, topical use of sage essential oil may cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals.

It is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new herbal remedies and to use them in moderation.

1. Upset Stomach

Upset stomach caused by Salvia Officinalis refers to a disturbance in the normal functioning of the stomach, resulting in discomfort, pain, n burning sensation in the abdominal area.

The main active constituents of Salvia Officinalis that can cause this side effect are volatile oils, including thujone, camphor, and cineole. The medicinal preparations of Salvia Officinalis that can cause this side effect if misused include teas, capsules, and essential oils. People who are more likely to experience this side effect are those with a history of gastrointestinal issues, such as acid reflux, gastritis, or ulcers.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to use Salvia Officinalis in moderation, follow the recommended dosage, and seek the guidance of a healthcare professional before using this herb, especially for extended periods or in high doses.

2. Dry Mouth

Dry mouth caused by Salvia Officinalis refers to the decreased production of saliva in the mouth, leading to a dry, parched, and sometimes uncomfortable feeling.

This herb contains active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and diterpenes, which can cause dry mouth when misused. The main medicinal preparations of Salvia officinalis that can cause dry mouth if misused include teas, essential oils, and supplements. People who are more likely to experience dry mouth after using Salvia officinalis include those who consume large amounts, those who consume it frequently, and those who have a history of dry mouth.

To avoid dry mouth when using Salvia officinalis, it is recommended to stay hydrated, avoid consuming large amounts, and discontinue use if dry mouth persists.

3. Throat Irritation

Throat irritation caused by Salvia Officinalis refers to a dry, scratchy, or painful sensation in the throat that may result from the use of this herb.

The main active constituents of sage that can cause this side effect are volatile oils, flavonoids, and tannins. These constituents can cause throat irritation when sage is used in medicinal preparations such as teas, extracts, and essential oils, especially if misused or used in large amounts.

People who are more likely to experience throat irritation from sage are those who are sensitive or allergic to its active constituents, have a history of respiratory conditions or irritation, or use it for prolonged periods or in high doses. To avoid throat irritation, it is important to use sage in moderation, follow the recommended dosages, and avoid using it for extended periods.

It is also advisable to consult with a healthcare professional before using sage, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or who are taking medications.

4. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Salvia Officinalis refers to a feeling of lightheadedness, unsteadiness, or vertigo that can occur after using this herb.

This side effect can be attributed mainly to the active constituents of sage, such as thujone and camphor, which can cause central nervous system stimulation and lead to symptoms of dizziness. Medicinal preparations of sage, including essential oils, teas, and supplements, can cause this side effect if misused or taken in large amounts.

Individuals who are more likely to experience dizziness from sage are those with a history of motion sickness, inner ear disorders, or those who are taking certain medications, such as blood pressure drugs or sedatives. To avoid this side effect, it is essential to use sage in moderation and follow the recommended dosages.

It is also advisable to consult with a healthcare provider before using sage, especially for those who have pre-existing medical conditions or are taking medications.

5. Rash Or Skin Irritation

Rash or skin irritation caused by Salvia Officinalis refers to a skin reaction that can occur after coming into contact with the plant or its preparations.

The main active constituents in Salvia Officinalis that can cause this side effect are volatile oils, flavonoids, and tannins. This reaction is most commonly seen with topical use of sage preparations such as salves, ointments, or essential oils. People who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with sensitive skin, existing skin conditions, or those who use the plant in large amounts or for extended periods of time.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow recommended dosages, perform a patch test before applying sage to the skin, and seek advice from a healthcare professional before using Salvia Officinalis if you have any pre-existing skin conditions or sensitivities.

6. Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes caused by Salvia Officinalis refers to the herb's ability to alter the levels of hormones in the body.

This is due to the presence of certain active constituents in sage, including volatile oils, diterpenes, and flavonoids. The main medicinal preparations of sage that can cause this side effect if misused include dried leaves for teas and extracts, and essential oils for topical use. People who are more likely to experience hormonal changes from using Salvia Officinalis include those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a history of hormone-sensitive conditions such as breast cancer.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow recommended dosages and usage instructions carefully, and to consult with a healthcare provider before using sage if you have any pre-existing hormone-related health conditions.

7. Seizures

Seizures caused by Salvia Officinalis refers to the onset of abnormal electrical activity in the brain that can result in a physical convulsion, muscle spasms, and loss of consciousness.

This side effect is believed to be primarily caused by the diterpenoid compounds present in sage, such as a-thujone and b-thujone. Medicinal preparations of sage, when used in large doses or misused, can increase the risk of developing seizures. Individuals with a history of seizure disorders, pregnant women, and those with sensitive nervous systems are more likely to experience this side effect.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to use sage in moderate amounts, avoid misuse, and consult with a healthcare professional before using sage if you have a history of seizure disorders, are pregnant, or have a sensitive nervous system.

8. Confusion

Confusion caused by Salvia Officinalis refers to a state of mental bewilderment and disorientation that can occur due to the misuse or overuse of this herb.

The main active constituents in Salvia officinalis that can cause this side effect are volatile oils, diterpenes, and flavonoids. The main medicinal preparations of Salvia officinalis that can cause confusion if misused include essential oil, dried leaves, and tinctures. People who are more likely to experience this side effect are those with a history of mental illness, pregnant or nursing women, and individuals who take medication that affects the central nervous system.

To avoid confusion when using Salvia officinalis, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage, consult with a healthcare professional before using it, and avoid using it in combination with other substances that affect the central nervous system.

9. Slowed Heart Rate

Slowed heart rate caused by Salvia Officinalis refers to a decrease in the number of heartbeats per minute that can potentially occur with the use of this herb.

The main active constituents in Salvia Officinalis that can cause this side effect include various diterpenoids, such as carnosol, carnosic acid, and rosmanol. These constituents can have a relaxing effect on the heart, leading to a slowed heart rate.

Medicinal preparations of Salvia Officinalis that can cause this side effect if misused include teas, tinctures, and essential oils made from the leaves of the plant. People who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with pre-existing heart conditions or those taking medications that can interact with Salvia Officinalis, such as blood pressure medications. To avoid this side effect, it is important to use Salvia Officinalis under the guidance of a healthcare professional and to start with a low dose, gradually increasing as tolerated.

Additionally, it is important to avoid taking Salvia Officinalis with other medications that can affect heart rate, such as beta blockers or calcium channel blockers, and to avoid consuming large amounts of the herb.

10. Low Blood Pressure

Low blood pressure caused by Salvia Officinalis refers to an abnormally low level of blood pressure in the body that can occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents in Salvia Officinalis that can cause low blood pressure include volatile oils, diterpenes, and flavonoids. These constituents can lead to a decrease in blood pressure when taken in large amounts or over an extended period of time.

The main medicinal preparations of Salvia Officinalis that can cause low blood pressure include sage tea, tinctures, and essential oils. People who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with low blood pressure or those taking medication to lower their blood pressure.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to use Salvia Officinalis in moderation and to consult with a healthcare provider before using it, particularly if you have a history of low blood pressure or are taking medication for high blood pressure.

What to know before using Cypripedium pubescens (Lady'S Slipper)?

Cypripedium pubescens, also known as the yellow lady's slipper or moccasin flower, is a rare species of orchid found in North America.

It has been traditionally used by Native American tribes for medicinal purposes, including treating digestive disorders, nervous conditions, and skin problems. This plant is characterized by its yellow, slipper-shaped pouch and a cluster of white, petal-like sepals. It thrives in well-drained, humus-rich soil in partial shade, and requires specific mycorrhizal fungi to survive.

Cultivating this orchid can be challenging due to its specific growing conditions and slow growth rate, making it a prized addition to any horticultural collection.

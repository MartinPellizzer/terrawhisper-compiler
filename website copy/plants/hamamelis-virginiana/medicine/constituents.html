10 Best Active Constituents of Hamamelis virginiana (Witch Hazel)

Hamamelis virginiana, also known as witch hazel, contains various active constituents that are beneficial for medicinal purposes.

The primary active components include tannins, volatile oils, resin, and flavonoids. Tannins are the most prominent and are known for their astringent properties, which can help to tighten and protect skin.

Volatile oils, such as eugenol and hexenol, have anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, making them useful for soothing irritated skin and reducing pain. Resin, another active constituent, has been shown to have antimicrobial properties, which can help to protect the skin from infection.

Lastly, flavonoids, a type of antioxidant, are also present in witch hazel and can help to reduce damage caused by free radicals, promoting overall skin health.

1. Tannins

The tannins contained in Hamamelis Virginiana are a type of polyphenolic biomolecule that can bind to proteins and various organic compounds, creating insoluble complexes.

These tannins have astringent, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, making them the main medicinal components of witch hazel. Witch hazel tannins help relieve various health conditions, such as skin irritations, inflammation, and minor wounds, by constricting blood vessels, reducing swelling, and fighting free radicals. The main parts of the Hamamelis virginiana plant that contain these beneficial tannins are the bark, leaves, and twigs.

However, if used improperly or in excess, witch hazel tannins can cause skin irritation, dryness, and allergic reactions, so it is essential to follow recommended dosages and usage guidelines.

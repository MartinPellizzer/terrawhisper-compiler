What to know before using Pterocarpus marsupium (Kino)?

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian Kino or Venga, is a species of tree that is native to India and Sri Lanka.

It is a large, deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a buttressed trunk and a spreading canopy. The tree has a wide range of medicinal properties, and has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for centuries.

The bark and heartwood of the tree contain compounds that have been shown to have anti-diabetic, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties. Pterocarpus marsupium prefers warm, tropical climates and grows best in well-drained, sandy soil. It is a hardy tree that can tolerate some shade, but prefers full sun.

The tree produces a pod-like fruit, and its leaves are used as a source of fodder for livestock.

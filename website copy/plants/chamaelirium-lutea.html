What to know before using Chamaelirium lutea (Helonias)?

Chamaelirium lutea, also known as false unicorn root, is a plant native to North America that has been used for medicinal purposes by indigenous peoples for centuries.

It is valued for its ability to regulate menstrual cycles and support pregnancy, and has also been used to treat a variety of other ailments including digestive issues and fever. Chamaelirium lutea prefers rich, well-drained soil and partial shade, and can be found in woodland areas in the eastern and central United States. The plant grows between 2-4 feet tall and has a thick, yellow taproot, from which the medicinal properties are derived.

The leaves of Chamaelirium lutea are compound, with 3-5 leaflets, and the small, greenish-white flowers bloom in the late spring and early summer.

What are the medicinal uses of Chamaelirium lutea?

Chamaelirium lutea has several medicinal uses, such as treating women's reproductive system disorders, including ovarian cysts, menstrual irregularities, and infertility.

What to know before using Gymnema sylvestre (Gymnema)?

Gymnema sylvestre, a woody climbing shrub native to the tropical forests of India and Africa, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to its numerous health benefits.

This plant, also known as "sugar destroyer," contains gymnemic acids, which have been shown to help regulate blood sugar levels by suppressing the absorption of sugar in the intestines and increasing insulin production in the pancreas. Gymnema sylvestre thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade to grow.

Its botanical characteristics include small, yellow flowers and compound, ovate-shaped leaves that have a bitter taste.

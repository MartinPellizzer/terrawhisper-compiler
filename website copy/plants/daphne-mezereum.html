What to know before using Daphne mezereum (Mezereon)?

Daphne mezereum, also known as spurge laurel, is a shrub that is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is known for its medicinal properties, including the ability to treat skin conditions and relieve pain, due to the presence of mezerein and other compounds in its bark and berries. In terms of horticultural conditions, Daphne mezereum prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and it is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive pink and white flowers. From a botanical perspective, Daphne mezereum is a member of the Thymelaeaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, bright berries.

It is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic if ingested and should be handled with care.

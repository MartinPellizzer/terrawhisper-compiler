What to know before using Elymus repens (Quackgrass)?

Elymus repens, also known as couch grass, is a perennial herb with medicinal properties.

It contains various compounds with potential health benefits, such as steroidal saponins and alkaloids, and has been used in traditional medicine to treat a range of conditions, including inflammation and gastrointestinal disorders. In horticultural settings, Elymus repens is often considered an invasive weed due to its rapid spread through underground stolons.

It thrives in a variety of soils and climates, making it a ubiquitous presence in many parts of the world. Botanically, Elymus repens is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Each flower produces a single seed, which is encased in a tiny, hardened fruit.

What to know before using Lawsonia inermis (Henna)?

Lawsonia inermis, also known as henna, is a small shrub or small tree that is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Northern Australia.

It is well-known for its medicinal properties, including its ability to treat various skin conditions, such as eczema and dermatitis, as well as its use as a natural hair dye and conditioner. Lawsonia inermis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it can tolerate a wide range of temperatures, making it a popular choice for horticulturalists and gardeners. The plant produces fragrant white or pink flowers, and its leaves and twigs are used to extract the natural dye known as henna.

This dye is commonly used for body art, textile dyeing, and cosmetic purposes, and it has been used for thousands of years for its medicinal and cultural significance.

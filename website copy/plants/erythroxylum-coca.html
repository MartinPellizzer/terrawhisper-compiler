What to know before using Erythroxylum coca (Coca)?

Erythroxylum coca, commonly known as the coca plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is renowned for its medicinal properties, including its ability to alleviate altitude sickness, suppress hunger, and enhance physical endurance. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial sunlight, often found growing at elevations between 500 and 2000 meters. Erythroxylum coca is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves, which contain small amounts of cocaine alkaloids, and its delicate white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Despite its controversial reputation due to the illegal use of its derivatives, the coca plant has a long history of traditional medicinal use and cultural significance in Andean communities.

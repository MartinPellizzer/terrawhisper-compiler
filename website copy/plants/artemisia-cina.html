What to know before using Artemisia cina (Levant Wormwood)?

Artemisia cina, also known as wormwood or southernwood, is a plant with a rich history of medicinal use.

Its roots and upper parts are used in herbal medicine to treat various ailments, including intestinal worms, fever, and digestive issues. Artemisia cina thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it can grow up to 3 feet tall. This perennial herb is characterized by its fine, feathery leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Its aromatic foliage releases a strong, pungent scent when crushed, making it a popular choice for potpourri and sachets.

What to know before using Cardamine hirsuta (Common Bittercress)?

Cardamine hirsuta, also known as hairy bittercress, is a plant with medicinal properties that include the ability to act as a natural diuretic and expectorant.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. This plant is easily identified by its small, white flowers and long, slender seed pods, which contain numerous tiny seeds that are expelled with force when the pods are ripe.

The leaves of Cardamine hirsuta are green and slightly hairy, and they have a pungent, spicy flavor that is often used in salads and as a garnish.

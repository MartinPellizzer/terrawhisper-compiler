What to know before using Teucrium scorodonia (Wood Germander)?

Teucrium scorodonia, also known as wood sage, is a perennial herb with a variety of medicinal properties.

It contains tannins, flavonoids, and essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine to treat digestive disorders, skin conditions, and respiratory ailments. Teucrium scorodonia prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can grow up to 90 cm tall with ovate, wrinkled, and toothed leaves.

Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in loose spikes during the summer, and it is native to Europe and western Asia.

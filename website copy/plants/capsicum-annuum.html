What to know before using Capsicum annuum (Cayenne)?

Capsicum annuum, commonly known as bell peppers or sweet peppers, is a species of pepper from the Solanaceae family that has been cultivated for thousands of years for its edible fruit.

This plant is native to Central and South America and is now grown in many parts of the world. Capsicum annuum is a warm-season annual that requires full sun and well-drained soil with a pH between 6.0 and 6.8.

It is a versatile crop that can be grown in a variety of horticultural conditions, including in-ground gardens, raised beds, and containers. Medicinally, Capsicum annuum has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and circulatory properties.

The fruit of Capsicum annuum contains capsaicin, a compound that has been shown to have pain-relieving effects and may help to reduce inflammation.

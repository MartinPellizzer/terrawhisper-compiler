What to know before using Nepeta cataria (Catnip)?

Nepeta cataria, commonly known as catnip, is a herb in the mint family that is native to Europe and Asia but has since been naturalized in many parts of the world.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it can grow up to three feet tall with a spread of up to 4 feet. The plant features gray-green, serrated leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically white with purple spots.

Medicinally, Nepeta cataria is best known for its psychoactive effects on cats, but it also has a long history of use in human medicine. It has been used to treat a variety of ailments, including anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues.

The active compounds in catnip, known as nepetalactones, are believed to interact with the feline nervous system to produce a state of mild euphoria, while in humans, it has been used as a mild sedative and digestive aid.

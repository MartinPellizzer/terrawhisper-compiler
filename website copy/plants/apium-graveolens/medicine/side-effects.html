10 possible side effects of Apium graveolens (Celery) if used improperly

Apium graveolens, also known as celery, is a plant that is generally considered safe when used appropriately.

However, if used improperly, it can potentially cause a number of adverse health effects. First, consuming large amounts of celery in its raw form can lead to digestive issues such as bloating, gas, and diarrhea.

Additionally, celery contains high levels of potassium, which can be harmful to individuals with kidney problems or those taking medications that affect potassium levels. Furthermore, celery seeds and oil, which are sometimes used in supplements, can cause skin irritation and sensitivity to sunlight, leading to rashes and blisters. In rare cases, excessive consumption of celery has been linked to low blood pressure and photosensitivity.

Finally, it is important to note that celery is often grown with the use of pesticides, so those with allergies or sensitivities to these chemicals should exercise caution when consuming large amounts of celery or celery-based products.

1. Contact Dermatitis

Contact dermatitis caused by Apium Graveolens refers to a skin reaction that occurs when the body comes into contact with the plant.

This reaction is often caused by the plant's active constituents, such as furanocoumarins and psoralens, which can cause skin irritation and inflammation. Medicinal preparations such as celery seeds and oils, when misused or used in excess, can also lead to this side effect.

People who are more likely to experience contact dermatitis from celery include those with a history of allergies, sensitive skin, and those who are frequently exposed to the plant. To avoid this side effect, it is important to use celery and its preparations in moderation, and to avoid contact with the skin.

If contact does occur, it is recommended to wash the area thoroughly and seek medical attention if symptoms persist.

2. Allergic Reactions

Allergic reactions caused by Apium Graveolens refer to the unpleasant and potentially dangerous immune response that occurs in certain individuals upon exposure to celery or its derivatives.

The primary volatile oils and organic compounds responsible for this hypersensitivity include phenolic acids, flavenoids, and several aldehydes. These components are often found in celery seeds, leaves, and stems, making any medicinal preparations made from these parts of the plant potentially hazardous if misused.

Individuals with a known allergy to mugwort, birch pollen, or chamomile are at a higher risk of experiencing an adverse reaction to celery. To avoid allergic reactions, individuals should exercise caution when consuming celery or using celery-based products, including supplements and teas.

It is essential to follow dosage instructions carefully, perform a patch test before applying celery-based topical remedies, and seek immediate medical attention if any signs of an allergic reaction, such as skin rashes, swelling, or difficulty breathing, occur.

3. Phototoxicity

Phototoxicity caused by Apium Graveolens refers to a skin reaction characterized by redness, pain, and inflammation after exposure to sunlight.

This reaction is triggered by the presence of furocoumarin compounds, such as psoralen and bergapten, in celery. These compounds are found in all parts of the celery plant, but are most concentrated in the stems.

Medicinal preparations, such as celery seed extracts and essential oils, can cause phototoxicity if misused or used in large quantities. Individuals with fair skin or those who are taking medications that increase photosensitivity are at a higher risk of experiencing phototoxicity from celery.

To avoid phototoxicity, it is recommended to limit exposure to sunlight after handling celery or using celery-based products, wear protective clothing and sunscreen, and avoid using celery-based products if taking medications that increase photosensitivity.

4. Hypotension

Hypotension caused by Apium Graveolens refers to abnormally low blood pressure that can occur due to the consumption of this plant.

The main active constituents in celery that can cause this side effect are a group of compounds called coumarins, which have been shown to have blood pressure-lowering effects. Hypotension can be a side effect of medicinal preparations such as celery seed extracts or oils, especially when taken in large amounts or over an extended period of time.

Individuals who are more likely to experience this side effect include those who already have low blood pressure, are taking medication for high blood pressure, or have a history of fainting or dizziness. To avoid this side effect, it is important to use celery in moderation and in accordance with recommended dosages.

If you have a health condition or are taking medication, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using celery as a natural remedy.

5. Epoysis

Eposysis caused by Apium Graveolens refers to the condition where there is a loss of tissue strength and elasticity.

This can occur due to the presence of certain active constituents in celery, including furanocoumarins and psoralens. These compounds can cause photo-sensitivity in some individuals, leading to eposysis.

Medicinal preparations that have a high concentration of these active constituents, such as celery seed extracts and essential oils, are more likely to cause this side effect if misused. People with a history of photo-sensitivity or those who consume celery as a regular part of their diet are more likely to experience eposysis. To avoid this side effect, it is important to use celery and its derivatives in moderation and to avoid exposure to sunlight or UV rays after consumption.

If you experience any symptoms of eposysis, such as skin irritation or inflammation, it is recommended to discontinue use and consult with a healthcare professional.

6. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Apium Graveolens refers to an increased frequency and loosening of stools due to the consumption of this plant.

The main active constituents of celery that can cause diarrhea are volatile oils, furanocoumarins, and polyacetylenes. These constituents can stimulate the gastrointestinal tract and increase motility, leading to loose stools.

Medicinal preparations of celery, such as juice, extracts, and supplements, can cause diarrhea if misused or consumed in large quantities. People who are more likely to experience diarrhea from celery include those with irritable bowel syndrome, inflammatory bowel disease, or other gastrointestinal disorders. To avoid diarrhea, it is important to consume celery in moderation, start with small doses, and increase gradually.

It is also recommended to drink plenty of fluids and consult with a healthcare professional before using celery as a medicine.

8. Increased Menstrual Bleeding

Increased menstrual bleeding caused by Apium Graveolens refers to an excessively heavy flow during menstruation that may occur as a side effect of using this plant.

The main active constituents in celery that can cause this side effect are volatile oils, flavonoids, and coumarins. This side effect is most commonly associated with the misuse of celery in medicinal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, or extracts. Individuals who are more likely to experience this side effect include those who are sensitive to hormonal changes or have a history of heavy menstrual bleeding.

To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to use celery in moderation, follow recommended dosages, and consult with a healthcare provider before using celery as a medicinal remedy.

9. Interference With Anticoagulants

Interference with anticoagulants caused by Apium Graveolens refers to the herb's ability to interact with anticoagulant medications, affecting their ability to prevent blood clotting.

The main active constituents of celery that can cause this side effect are coumarins and apiin. The essential oil of celery, as well as its seeds and roots, contains these compounds.

People who are taking anticoagulant medications, have a history of bleeding disorders, or are undergoing surgery are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to inform healthcare providers about celery consumption, especially when taking anticoagulant medications.

Additionally, excessive consumption of celery should be avoided, and it is recommended to consult with healthcare professionals before using celery as a medicinal preparation.

10. Potential For False Positive Drug Tests

Potential for false positive drug tests caused by Apium Graveolens refers to the risk of individuals testing positive for certain drugs, such as marijuana, when they have not actually consumed them.

This occurs due to the presence of certain compounds in celery that are similar in structure to those found in drugs. The main active constituents in celery that can cause this side effect are a group of chemicals called furanocoumarins.

These compounds can show up in drug tests and be mistaken for drug metabolites. Medicinal preparations that can cause this side effect if misused include celery juice, celery seed extract, and celery essential oil. People who are more likely to experience this side effect are those who consume large amounts of celery or celery products, have a high metabolism, or consume celery in conjunction with other substances that affect drug metabolism.

To avoid this side effect, individuals should consume celery in moderation, inform healthcare providers and drug testers of celery consumption, and consider alternative herbs and spices in meal preparation.

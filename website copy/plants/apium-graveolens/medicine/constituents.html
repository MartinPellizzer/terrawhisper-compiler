10 Best Active Constituents of Apium graveolens (Celery)

Apium graveolens, also known as celery, has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.

The active constituents of celery include volatile oils, flavonoids, and coumarins. The volatile oils, such as d-limonene and selinene, have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Flavonoids like apigenin and luteolin have been shown to have potential anticancer effects. Coumarins, such as bergapten and isopimpinellin, have been studied for their anticoagulant and anti-tumor properties.

These active constituents contribute to the medicinal uses of celery, such as reducing inflammation, providing antioxidant support, and potentially aiding in cancer prevention and treatment.

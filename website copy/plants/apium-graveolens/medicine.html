Apium graveolens (Celery) Medicinal Guide

Apium graveolens, also known as celery, has a variety of medicinal benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and diuretic properties.

The constituents of celery include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils such as sedanolide and butylphthalide. Preparations of celery for medicinal use can be made from the seeds, roots, and leaves in the form of teas, tinctures, capsules, and essential oils.

Potential side effects of consuming large amounts of celery include increased sun sensitivity and allergic reactions. Precautions should be taken when using celery as a medicine, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have certain medical conditions such as kidney disease or low blood pressure.

It is always recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new medicinal herb.

What are the possible side effects of using Apium graveolens (Celery) for medicinal purposes?

Apium graveolens can have side effects if used improperly, such as contact dermatitis, which is skin irritation that occurs after coming into contact with celery.

Additionally, some people may experience allergic reactions, including skin rashes, headaches, and respiratory issues. Phototoxicity, or skin sensitivity to sunlight, is another potential side effect of celery.

Furthermore, consuming large amounts of celery can lead to hypotension, diarrhea, and gastrointestinal disturbances. Moreover, celery may interfere with anticoagulants, increase menstrual bleeding, and cause false positive drug test results.

Therefore, it is important to use celery in moderation and consult with a healthcare provider if any adverse effects occur.

The following list shows the possible side effects of Apium graveolens.

  • Contact Dermatitis: Apium graveolens may cause contact dermatitis due to its potential allergic properties and improper use, such as ingesting or applying large amounts directly to the skin without prior sensitization testing or proper dilution.
  • Allergic Reactions: Apium graveolens may cause allergic reactions when it is consumed or used topically due to its ability to cross-react with other plants in the Apiaceae family, leading to unintended exposure and potential sensitization for individuals who are allergic to those plants.
  • Phototoxicity: Apium graveolens may cause phototoxicity when it's consumed in large quantities and applied topically prior to sun exposure due to the presence of furanocoumarins, which enhance skin's susceptibility to UV radiation and increase the risk of burns and blisters.
  • Hypotension: Apium graveolens may cause hypotension if ingested in large quantities or used topically on a damaged skin area due to its potent vasodilatory effects and beta-blocking properties, which can lead to excessive blood vessel relaxation and decreased blood pressure regulation.
  • Epoysis: Apium graveolens may cause epoysis if it is not properly identified, harvested, or prepared, leading to ingestion of its oil containing farnesol which can induce a strong allergic reaction resulting in the sudden release of a milky fluid from breast ducts.
  • Diarrhea: Apium graveolens may cause diarrhea if consumed in large quantities or used as a purgative due to its strong stimulant effect on the gastrointestinal tract, increasing intestinal motility and secretion of digestive fluids beyond normal levels.
  • Gastrointestinal Disturbances: Apium graveolens may cause gastrointestinal disturbances if ingested in large quantities or taken without proper preparation, as it contains compounds such as thioanisole and isothiocyanates which can stimulate the production of gastric juices and increase intestinal permeability.
  • Increased Menstrual Bleeding: Apium graveolens may cause increased menstrual bleeding due to its estrogenic properties, which can stimulate uterine contractions and thicken the endometrial lining, leading to heavier menstruation in susceptible individuals.
  • Interference With Anticoagulants: Apium graveolens may cause interference with anticoagulants due to the presence of a compound called thiophenone glycoside, which inhibits the activity of vitamin K epoxide reductase, reducing the ability for the liver to synthesize functional vitamin K-dependent clotting factors and interfering with warfarin's therapeutic effect.
  • Potential For False Positive Drug Tests: Apium graveolens may cause potential for false positive drug tests due to the presence of thiocyanates, which are naturally occurring compounds that can metabolize into compounds similar to those found in certain prescription medications, leading to a mismatch in lab results and potential misdiagnosis or negative consequences.

What are the precautions to take when using Apium graveolens (Celery) medicinally?

It's important to take some precautions when using Apium graveolens for medicinal purposes, such as consulting a healthcare professional before use, as they can provide guidance based on your medical history and current medications.

Pregnant women should avoid using it, as it may stimulate menstruation and potentially lead to miscarriage. Moderation is key, even with natural remedies, so overuse should be avoided.

Monitoring blood pressure and discontinuing use if an allergic reaction occurs are crucial for maintaining safety. Additionally, those with kidney disease, diabetes, or who are using it as their sole treatment should exercise caution, as Apium graveolens may interact with these conditions or treatments.

Finally, thoroughly washing the leaves and storing them away from sunlight can help ensure the potency and safety of the remedy.

The following list shows the precautions to take when using Apium graveolens.

  • Consult Healthcare Professional: Consult healthcare professional before consuming Apium graveolens, as it contains compounds that may interact with certain medications and have potential side effects when taken in large doses or for extended periods.
  • Avoid During Pregnancy: Avoid during pregnancy due to potential hormonal effects on the developing fetus that could disrupt its endocrine system, affecting reproductive development and overall growth.
  • Use In Moderation: Use in moderation when consuming Apium graveolens due to its potential for causing contact dermatitis, respiratory irritation, and hormonal disruptions upon prolonged exposure or excessive intake.
  • Monitor Blood Pressure: Monitor blood pressure when using Apium graveolens due to its potential impact on cardiovascular function, which may lead to hypotension or hypertension depending on individual sensitivity and dosage.
  • Discontinue If Allergic Reaction: Discontinue if allergic reaction occurs due to Apium graveolens because an allergic response can escalate rapidly and lead to serious health consequences such as difficulty breathing, swelling of the face or throat, and potentially life-threatening anaphylaxis.
  • Avoid With Kidney Disease: Avoid with kidney disease due to its diuretic and antioxidant properties, which may increase urine production and exacerbate kidney damage in individuals already suffering from the condition.
  • Avoid With Diabetes: Avoid with diabetes due to its potential blood sugar lowering effects, which if not monitored or managed properly, could lead to hypoglycemia and complications in individuals with diabetes.
  • Do Not Use As Sole Treatment: Do not use as sole treatment , applyingApium graveolens topically or internally can lead to significant improvements in skin conditions and overall health; however, it should not be the only remedy used due to potential limitations and interactions with other conditions and treatments, possibly resulting in incomplete relief or even adverse effects if not used appropriately.
  • Wash Leaves Thoroughly: Wash leaves thoroughly before consuming Apium graveolens to remove potential contaminants such as pesticides, bacteria and debris, ensuring food safety and preventing potential health risks.
  • Store Away From Sunlight: Store away from sunlight to prevent the essential oils in Apium graveolens (Celery) from oxidizing, leading to loss of aroma, flavor, and potential health benefits.

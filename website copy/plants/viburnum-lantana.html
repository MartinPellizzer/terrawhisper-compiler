What to know before using Viburnum lantana (Mealytree)?

Viburnum lantana, also known as wayfaring tree, is a versatile shrub valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in the spring, followed by clusters of blue-black berries that attract birds and other wildlife.

As a medicinal plant, Viburnum lantana has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including fever, inflammation, and diarrhea. Its bark and berries contain several bioactive compounds, such as tannins and saponins, that are believed to have therapeutic effects.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant can be toxic if ingested in large quantities, and it should be used with caution.

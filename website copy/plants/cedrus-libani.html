What to know before using Cedrus libani (Cedar)?

Cedrus libani, also known as the Lebanon cedar, is a species of tree with notable medicinal properties.

The essential oil derived from its wood contains cedrol, which has been found to have anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and insecticidal properties. This evergreen tree, which is native to the mountains of the eastern Mediterranean region, thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH. Cedrus libani can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a lifespan of over a thousand years.

Its needle-like leaves are arranged in a spiral on the shoots, and its cones are barrel-shaped, releasing winged seeds when mature.

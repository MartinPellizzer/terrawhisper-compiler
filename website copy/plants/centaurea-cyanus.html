What to know before using Centaurea cyanus (Cornflower)?

Its flowers, which range in color from bright blue to soft lavender, contain high levels of antioxidants and have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties. In horticulture, Centaurea cyanus is a hardy annual that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and can be grown easily from seed.

The plant typically reaches a height of 2-3 feet and produces delicate, feathery leaves and striking, daisy-like flowers. When it comes to botanical characteristics, Centaurea cyanus is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to Europe.

Its flowers are actually composed of many small florets, and its seeds are contained in a distinctive, spiky fruit head.

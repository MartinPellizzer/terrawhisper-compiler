What to know before using Sorbus aucuparia (Eastern Mountain Ash)?

Sorbus aucuparia, also known as European rowan or mountain ash, is a species of tree with a number of medicinal properties.

Its berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in herbal medicine to treat various ailments, including diarrhea, high blood pressure, and inflammation. In horticultural settings, Sorbus aucuparia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and can reach heights of up to 15 meters.

Botanically, the tree is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the early summer, followed by the appearance of bright red berries in the fall.

What are the medicinal uses of Sorbus aucuparia?

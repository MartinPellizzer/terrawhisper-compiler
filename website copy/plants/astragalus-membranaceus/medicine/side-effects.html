10 possible side effects of Astragalus membranaceus (Astragalus) if used improperly

Astragalus membranaceus, a plant commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine, has been suggested to have various health benefits, such as boosting the immune system and improving resistance to inflammation and stress.

However, when used improperly or in excessive amounts, it can cause negative side effects. These can include symptoms such as diarrhea, stomach upset, and dehydration.

Moreover, Astragalus membranaceus can interact with certain medications, such as immunosuppressants and anti-hypertensive drugs, leading to adverse effects and hindering the effectiveness of the medications. Pregnant or breastfeeding women, and individuals with autoimmune diseases or conditions affecting the blood sugar levels, should avoid using Astragalus membranaceus due to the potential risks and complications.

Consulting a healthcare professional is crucial before starting to use Astragalus membranaceus to ensure safe and effective application.

1. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Astragalus Membranaceus refers to a gastrointestinal side effect characterized by loose, watery stools that occurs upon ingesting this herb.

The main active constituents of Astragalus Membranaceus that can cause diarrhea are saponins and polysaccharides. This side effect is most likely to occur with the misuse of Astragalus Membranaceus medicinal preparations such as decoctions, capsules, and powders. Individuals with a history of gastrointestinal sensitivity, inflammatory bowel disease, or those taking medications that affect digestion are more likely to experience diarrhea after using Astragalus Membranaceus.

To avoid diarrhea, it is important to follow the recommended dosage, start with a low dose and gradually increase, and consult a healthcare professional before using Astragalus Membranaceus if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking medication.

2. Nausea

Nausea caused by Astragalus Membranaceus refers to the feeling of discomfort and distress in the upper stomach that precedes vomiting.

This side effect can be attributed to the main active constituents of astragalus, which include saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides. These compounds can stimulate the digestive system and cause irritation, leading to nausea. The main medicinal preparations that can cause this side effect if misused include astragalus decoctions, tinctures, and capsules.

Individuals who are more likely to experience nausea from astragalus include those with sensitive stomachs, digestive disorders, or those who consume high doses of astragalus. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with small doses of astragalus and gradually increase the dosage over time. Additionally, taking astragalus with meals or using enteric-coated capsules can help reduce the risk of digestive irritation and nausea.

It is also important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare professional before using astragalus, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or those taking medication.

3. Vomiting

Vomiting caused by Astragalus Membranaceus refers to the unpleasant symptom of forcefully expelling stomach contents through the mouth.

This side effect is thought to be caused mainly by the saponins and alkaloids present in astragalus. The medicinal preparations that can cause vomiting if misused include decoctions, capsules, and powdered forms of the plant.

Individuals with sensitive stomachs or those who consume large amounts of astragalus are more likely to experience vomiting. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with small doses of astragalus and gradually increase over time, and to avoid consuming it on an empty stomach.

Additionally, it is important to seek advice from a healthcare professional before taking astragalus, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or those who are taking other medications.

4. Stomach Upset

Stomach upset caused by Astragalus Membranaceus refers to a range of digestive symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, bloating, and abdominal cramps that may occur after using this herb.

The main active constituents of astragalus that can cause stomach upset include saponins, polysaccharides, and flavonoids. These compounds can irritate the digestive lining, leading to stomach discomfort.

Medicinal preparations of astragalus, such as decoctions, capsules, and powders, can cause stomach upset if misused or taken in large quantities. People who are more likely to experience stomach upset from astragalus include those with a history of gastrointestinal disorders, such as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) or inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), and those taking medications that affect the digestive system. To avoid stomach upset, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage and not exceed the maximum daily limit of astragalus.

It is also advisable to consult a healthcare provider before taking astragalus, especially if you have a medical condition or are taking medication.

5. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Astragalus Membranaceus refers to a spinning or lightheaded sensation that may occur in some individuals who use this herb.

This side effect can be attributed mainly to certain active constituents in astragalus, including saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which can have a mildly hypotensive effect on blood pressure. Medicinal preparations of astragalus that can cause dizziness if misused include decoctions, capsules, and tinctures. Elderly individuals, those with low blood pressure, and people prone to fainting are more likely to experience dizziness after using astragalus.

To avoid this side effect, it's crucial to follow the recommended dosage and usage guidelines carefully and consult with a healthcare provider before taking astragalus, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking any medications.

6. Dry Mouth

Dry mouth caused by Astragalus Membranaceus refers to the subjective feeling of dryness in the oral cavity, which can be accompanied by difficulty speaking, chewing, and swallowing.

This side effect is primarily caused by the saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides present in astragalus, which can interact with the mucous membranes in the mouth and reduce saliva production. Decoctions, powdered extracts, and capsule forms of astragalus are the most common medicinal preparations that can cause dry mouth if misused or overused.

Individuals who are sensitive to the active constituents of astragalus or have a dry mouth condition prior to taking the herb are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid dry mouth caused by astragalus, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase it, stay properly hydrated, and avoid using the herb for an extended period of time.

Additionally, it is advised to consult a healthcare provider before using astragalus for medicinal purposes.

7. Rash

Rash caused by Astragalus Membranaceus refers to a skin irritation that can occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents of Astragalus Membranaceus that can cause this side effect are saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides. The main medicinal preparations that can cause this side effect if misused include Astragalus membranaceus root extract, Astragalus membranaceus tea, and Astragalus membranaceus tincture.

Individuals who are more likely to experience this side effect are those with allergies to legumes, those with autoimmune diseases, and those taking immunosuppressive drugs. To avoid this side effect, it is important to start with a low dose and gradually increase, avoid using Astragalus membranaceus with other herbs that may cause rash, and discontinue use if a rash appears.

It is also recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using Astragalus membranaceus, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or those taking medication.

8. Itching

Itching caused by Astragalus Membranaceus refers to the sensation of irritation or pruritus that may occur in the skin or mucous membranes after using this herb.

The main active constituents in Astragalus Membranaceus that can cause this side effect are saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides. This side effect is most commonly associated with medicinal preparations such as decoctions, capsules, and powdered extracts of the root.

Individuals who are more likely to experience itching after using astragalus include those with sensitive skin or allergies, as well as those who use the herb in large amounts or over a long period of time. To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow recommended dosages and usage guidelines, start with a low dose and gradually increase, and discontinue use if itching or other adverse reactions occur.

It is also advisable to consult with a healthcare provider before taking astragalus, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or who are taking other medications.

9. Joint Pain

Joint pain caused by Astragalus Membranaceus refers to discomfort or pain in the joints that may occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents in Astragalus Membranaceus that can cause joint pain include saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides. This side effect is most commonly associated with medicinal preparations such as decoctions, capsules, and powdered forms of the root.

Individuals who are more likely to experience joint pain from using Astragalus Membranaceus include those with weakened immune systems, autoimmune disorders, or a history of joint-related conditions. To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow recommended dosages, consult with a healthcare provider before use, and monitor for any signs of joint discomfort or inflammation.

Additionally, it is advisable to gradually increase the dosage and avoid using astragalus in combination with other herbs or medications that may exacerbate joint pain.

10. Low Blood Pressure

Low blood pressure caused by Astragalus Membranaceus refers to an condition where the blood pressure in the arteries is lower than normal, leading to inadequate blood supply to the body's organs.

This side effect can be attributed to the main active constituents of astragalus, including saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which can dilate blood vessels and increase blood flow. The main medicinal preparations of astragalus that can cause this side effect if misused include decoctions, capsules, and powdered forms.

Individuals who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with naturally low blood pressure, those taking medication for high blood pressure, and those with heart conditions. To avoid this side effect, it is important to use astragalus under the guidance of a healthcare professional, start with a low dose, and monitor blood pressure regularly.

Additionally, it is crucial to avoid using astragalus with other medications that lower blood pressure, such as diuretics and ACE inhibitors.

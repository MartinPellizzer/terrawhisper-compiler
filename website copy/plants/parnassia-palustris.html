What to know before using Parnassia palustris (Bog Star)?

Parnassia palustris, also known as Grass of Parnassus, is a perennial plant with medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including skin diseases, inflammation, and digestive disorders, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and astringent properties. In horticultural conditions, Parnassia palustris prefers damp, acidic soil and partial shade, making it suitable for bog gardens or the edges of ponds. The plant grows to a height of 10-30 cm and blooms from July to September, producing white, star-shaped flowers with distinctive pink or green veins.

Its narrow, grass-like leaves form a basal rosette, and the stems are slender and delicate, adding a delicate touch to any garden.

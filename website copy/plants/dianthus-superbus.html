What to know before using Dianthus superbus (Fringed Pink)?

Dianthus superbus, also known as the superb pink, is a herbaceous perennial plant with medicinal properties.

Its roots and flowers contain tannins, resins, and essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine to treat digestive issues and skin conditions. In horticultural settings, Dianthus superbus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, producing showy blooms in shades of pink and red from early summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 2-3 feet, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, five-petaled flowers that are popular in cut flower arrangements.

Botanically, Dianthus superbus is distinguished by its serrated leaf margins and notched petals, which are often marked with darker pink or red stripes.

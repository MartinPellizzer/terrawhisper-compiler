What to know before using Anthriscus cerefolium (Chervil)?

Anthriscus cerefolium, also known as cow parsley or chervil, is a delicate herb with a mild, anise-like flavor that is a staple in French and German cuisine.

It boasts a variety of medicinal properties, including being an anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and digestive aid. This herb thrives in well-drained soil with partial shade and requires consistent moisture. Anthriscus cerefolium is a cool-season annual that grows to about 2 feet tall, with finely divided, bright green leaves and small white flowers arranged in umbels.

It is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic when consumed in large quantities, and it should be used in moderation.

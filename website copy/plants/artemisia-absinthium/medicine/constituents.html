10 Best Active Constituents of Artemisia absinthium (Wormwood)

Artemisia absinthium, also known as wormwood, contains various active constituents that are used for medicinal purposes.

The most notable of these constituents is a terpene called thujone, which has been found to have antiparasitic, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. Wormwood also contains flavonoids such as quercetin and luteolin, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Additionally, the plant contains phenolic acids, tannins, and essential oils, which contribute to its bitter taste and have been used in traditional medicine to stimulate digestion and appetite.

However, it is important to note that wormwood should be used with caution, as excessive consumption can have toxic effects, including seizures and kidney damage.

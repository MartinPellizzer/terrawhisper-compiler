Artemisia absinthium (Wormwood) Medicinal Guide

Artemisia absinthium, also known as wormwood, has a variety of medicinal benefits including acting as a vermifuge to treat intestinal worms, reducing fevers, and alleviating symptoms of dyspepsia.

The constituents of wormwood include absinthin, anabsinthin, and essential oils such as thujone, pinene, and limonene. Preparations of Artemisia absinthium can be made through infusions, decoctions, or tinctures of the leaves and flowers.

However, it is important to be aware of the potential side effects, which can include stomach irritation, restlessness, tremors, and seizures. Additionally, Artemisia absinthium should be used with caution in pregnant women, those with a history of seizures, or those taking medication that may interact with the herb.

It is always recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new herbal remedy.

What are the active constituents of Artemisia absinthium (Wormwood)?

Artemisia absinthium has several active constituents that include Alpha Thujone and Beta Thujone, which are responsible for its bitter taste and have been found to have psychoactive effects.

Artemisinin is another important constituent, known for its antimalarial properties and potential as an anticancer agent.

The plant also contains various terpenes and phenols, such as Eugenol, Thujyl alcohol, Artemisia Ketone, Camphene, Limonene, Myrcene, Alpha Pinene, Beta Pinene, Caryophyllene, Cadinene, Cineole, and Farnesene, which contribute to its aroma and have been found to have various medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic effects.

The following list shows the active constituents of Artemisia absinthium.

  • 1. Alpha Thujone: A terpene with reported psychoactive effects, believed to contribute to the bitter flavor and potential medicinal properties of wormwood.
  • 2. Beta Thujone: A terpene found in wormwood, known for its bitter taste and believed to have medicinal properties, but potentially toxic in large amounts.
  • 3. Artemisinin: A sesquiterpene lactone with antimalarial and potential anticancer properties, found in high concentrations in sweet wormwood (Artemisia annua), but also present in lesser amounts in wormwood (Artemisia absinthium).
  • 4. Eugenol: A phenolic compound with analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties, found in the essential oil of wormwood.
  • 5. Thujyl alcohol: A terpene alcohol found in wormwood, known for its bitter taste and believed to have medicinal properties.
  • 6. Artemisia Ketone: A sesquiterpene ketone found in wormwood, known for its bitter taste and believed to have medicinal properties.
  • 7. Camphene: A bicyclic terpene with a pungent odor, found in small amounts in wormwood, and believed to have potential medicinal properties.
  • 8. Limonene: A cyclic terpene hydrocarbon found in small amounts in wormwood, known for its citrusy aroma and potential medicinal properties.
  • 9. Myrcene: A monoterpene with a musky, earthy aroma, found in small amounts in wormwood, and believed to have potential medicinal properties.
  • 10. Alpha Pinene: A bicyclic monoterpene with a piney aroma, found in small amounts in wormwood, and believed to have potential medicinal properties.

What are the medicinal preparations of Artemisia absinthium (Wormwood)?

Artemisia absinthium has many medicinal preparations, such as infusion, tincture, decoction, fluid extract, dry extract, powder, capsules, oil, ointment, cream, liniment, mouthwash, gargle, suppositories, bath additive.

A tincture is a concentrated alcohol extract of wormwood that can be taken orally for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. A decoction is a strong tea made by simmering wormwood roots or leaves for a longer period, which can be used to treat respiratory infections and fevers. Fluid and dry extracts are concentrated forms of wormwood that can be taken orally, with the former being a liquid and the latter a powder.

Powder and capsules are also oral forms of wormwood, with the powder often mixed with water or other liquids before consumption. Mouthwash and gargle made from wormwood can be used to treat mouth ulcers, throat infections, and gum diseases. Suppositories containing wormwood can be used to treat hemorrhoids and other rectal issues.

Finally, bath additive made from wormwood can be used to soothe sore muscles and promote relaxation.

What are the possible side effects of using Artemisia absinthium (Wormwood) for medicinal purposes?

Artemisia absinthium can have side effects if used improperly, such as inducing nausea, vomiting, and dizziness.

Prolonged use or excessive intake may lead to further complications, including diarrhea, stomach cramps, and fatigue. Additionally, headaches, sleep problems, and confusion can occur. In severe cases, it can cause depression, hallucinations, and other neurological symptoms.

It is crucial to use this herb responsibly and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

The following list shows the possible side effects of Artemisia absinthium.

  • 1. Nausea: Can cause discomfort in the stomach and urge to vomit.
  • 2. Vomiting: Can result in forceful expulsion of stomach contents.
  • 3. Dizziness: May cause a sensation of lightheadedness or unsteadiness.
  • 4. Diarrhea: Can lead to frequent and watery bowel movements.
  • 5. Stomach cramps: Can result in abdominal pain and discomfort.
  • 6. Fatigue: Can cause a feeling of tiredness or lack of energy.
  • 7. Headache: Can lead to a pulsating pain in the head.
  • 8. Sleep problems: May result in difficulty falling or staying asleep.
  • 9. Confusion: Can cause a state of bewilderment or lack of clarity.
  • 10. Depression: Can result in feelings of sadness, hopelessness, or loss of interest in activities.

What are the precautions to take when using Artemisia absinthium (Wormwood) medicinally?

It's important to take some precautions when using Artemisia absinthium for medicinal purposes, such as avoiding excessive consumption and limiting use to recommended dosages to prevent potential toxicity.

Additionally, it's crucial to consult a healthcare provider first to ensure it's safe for your individual health situation, especially for pregnant women, breastfeeding mothers, and people with epilepsy or on anticoagulant medication. Furthermore, Artemisia absinthium should be discontinued if an allergic reaction occurs, and users should limit exposure to sunlight, refrain from mixing it with alcohol, and discontinue use if dizziness or headaches occur.

Lastly, it's important to note that Artemisia absinthium should not be used in children under 2 years, before surgery, or in large quantities, and its use should be limited to two weeks.

The following list shows the precautions to take when using Artemisia absinthium.

  • 1. Avoid excessive consumption: Drinking too much Artemisia absinthium tea or extract can lead to unpleasant side effects such as stomach upset, dizziness, and seizures.
  • 2. Consult a healthcare provider first: Before using Artemisia absinthium for medicinal purposes, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional to ensure it is safe and appropriate for your specific health condition.
  • 3. Limit use to two weeks: Prolonged use of Artemisia absinthium can lead to serious side effects, so it's crucial to limit its use to no more than two weeks at a time.
  • 4. Do not use during pregnancy: Artemisia absinthium can stimulate uterine contractions and is therefore not recommended for use during pregnancy.
  • 5. Avoid if breastfeeding: Artemisia absinthium can pass into breast milk and may have adverse effects on the baby, so it's best to avoid using it while breastfeeding.
  • 6. Refrain from use if epileptic: Artemisia absinthium can lower the seizure threshold and should be avoided by people with a history of seizures or epilepsy.
  • 7. Do not mix with alcohol: Consuming Artemisia absinthium with alcohol can increase the risk of unpleasant side effects, such as nausea, dizziness, and sedation.
  • 8. Discontinue if allergic reaction occurs: If you experience an allergic reaction, such as hives, swelling, or difficulty breathing, after using Artemisia absinthium, discontinue use immediately and seek medical attention.
  • 9. Limit exposure to sunlight: Artemisia absinthium can increase photosensitivity, so it's essential to limit exposure to sunlight and wear protective clothing when using this herb.
  • 10. Do not use in children under 2 years: Artemisia absinthium is not recommended for use in children under 2 years of age due to the risk of serious side effects.

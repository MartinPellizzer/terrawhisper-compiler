What to know before using Dryopteris filix mas (Male Fern)?

Dryopteris filix-mas, also known as the male fern, is a fern native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

Its rhizome has been used in herbal medicine for its vermifugal and anti-inflammatory properties. It contains a compound called filicin, which is toxic to certain parasites and has been used to treat intestinal infections and expel tapeworms.

In horticulture, Dryopteris filix-mas prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade. It is a hardy plant that can tolerate a wide range of conditions, making it a popular choice for gardeners looking for a low-maintenance fern. Botanically, Dryopteris filix-mas is characterized by its long, lance-shaped fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length.

Its sori, or spore cases, are arranged in two rows along the undersides of the fronds, giving it a distinctive appearance.

10 Best Active Constituents of Arctium lappa (Burdock)

Arctium lappa, commonly known as burdock, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to its active constituents.

The most notable of these constituents include phenolic acids, quinic acid, and chlorogenic acid, which have been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Additionally, burdock contains polyacetylenes, such as dehydroadonitol and lappol, which have been found to have cytotoxic activity against certain types of cancer cells. The plant also contains significant amounts of inulin, a type of dietary fiber that has been linked to improved gut health and immune function.

Furthermore, burdock contains various essential vitamins and minerals, such as vitamin C, provitamin B-complex, and iron, which contribute to its overall medicinal value.

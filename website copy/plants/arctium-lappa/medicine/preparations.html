Arctium lappa, also known as greater burdock, is a plant that has been used in medicinal preparations for centuries.

Its roots and seeds are commonly used to make tisanes, which are herbal teas that are believed to have diuretic and digestive properties. The roots can also be made into a tincture, which is an alcohol-based extract, and is used to treat skin conditions such as acne, eczema, and psoriasis.

Additionally, some studies suggest that Arctium lappa may have anti-inflammatory and blood sugar-lowering effects. However, it is important to note that more research is needed to confirm these potential health benefits and to establish appropriate dosages.

As with any natural remedy, it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new treatment.

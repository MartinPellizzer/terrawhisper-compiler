It possesses medicinal properties, including its use as a stimulant laxative, a treatment for skin conditions such as eczema and psoriasis, and as a remedy for fever and rheumatism. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, and can grow up to 3 feet tall with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are rough to the touch.

Stillingia sylvatica's yellow, star-shaped flowers bloom in the summer, followed by the appearance of small, brown seed capsules.

What to know before using Conyza canadensis (Canadian Fleabane)?

Conyza canadensis, also known as Canadian fleabane or horseweed, is a plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine as a treatment for a range of ailments, including respiratory issues, fever, and wounds. The plant contains several beneficial compounds, such as phenolic acids and flavonoids, which are believed to be responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Conyza canadensis is a hardy plant that can thrive in a wide range of environments. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as those along roadsides and fields, and can tolerate both drought and poor soil quality. From a botanical perspective, Conyza canadensis is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, daisy-like flowers.

The plant can grow to be several feet tall and has a tendency to spread rapidly, making it a common weed in many areas.

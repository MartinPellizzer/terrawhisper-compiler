What to know before using Fucus vesiculosus (Bladderwrack)?

Fucus vesiculosus, also known as bladderwrack, is a type of brown seaweed that grows in cooler waters in the northern and western regions of the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.

It has a long history of use in traditional medicine, particularly in the treatment of thyroid conditions, due to its high iodine content which can help to regulate hormone production. Fucus vesiculosus prefers rocky shores and moderately exposed areas, where it can anchor itself to rocks with its holdfasts. It has a distinctive appearance, with flat, branching fronds that can grow up to two feet long and contain small, gas-filled bladders that help the seaweed to float.

In addition to its medicinal uses, Fucus vesiculosus is also used as a dietary supplement and as a natural fertilizer in horticulture, where it can help to improve soil structure and provide essential nutrients to plants.

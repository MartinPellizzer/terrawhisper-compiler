What to know before using Lysimachia nummularia (Creeping-Jenny)?

Lysimachia nummularia, also known as creeping Jenny, is a perennial plant with charming yellow flowers and rounded, bright green leaves.

It thrives in moist, fertile soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it an excellent ground cover option. Medicinally, creeping Jenny has been used in traditional medicine to treat wounds and skin irritations, as it contains tannins and flavonoids with anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.

This plant is also known for its creeping habit, as it roots at nodes, allowing it to form dense mats, and it can even thrive in wet soil or shallow water.

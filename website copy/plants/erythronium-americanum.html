What to know before using Erythronium americanum (Trout Lily)?

Erythronium americanum, also known as trout lily, is a botanical treasure native to North America's rich woodland habitats.

Its bell-shaped, yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring, are a delightful sight, each sitting atop a slender stem surrounded by whorls of speckled, mottled leaves. This plant possesses medicinal properties, with its roots and bulbs having been used traditionally by Native American communities to treat a variety of ailments, including wounds, skin conditions, and digestive issues. In horticultural settings, trout lilies thrive in well-drained, humus-rich soil with partial to full shade, mimicking their natural woodland environment.

These bulbs can be propagated through division during their dormancy in late summer, and they make excellent additions to shady gardens and naturalized landscapes.

What to know before using Serenoa repens (Saw Palmetto)?

Serenoa repens, also known as saw palmetto, is a small species of palm tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is well-known for its medicinal properties, particularly in the treatment of benign prostatic hyperplasia, or enlarged prostate, due to its ability to inhibit the conversion of testosterone to dihydrotestosterone. Serenoa repens thrives in sandy and well-drained soils, often found in coastal areas, dunes, and sandhills. This slow-growing plant can reach up to 10 feet in height, producing a dense canopy of fan-shaped fronds and small, white flowers that give way to yellowish-black berries.

The berries are rich in fatty acids and phytosterols, which are believed to contribute to the plant's medicinal benefits.

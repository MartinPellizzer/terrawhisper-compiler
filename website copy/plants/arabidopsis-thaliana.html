What to know before using Arabidopsis thaliana (Mouse-Ear Cress)?

Arabidopsis thaliana, a small flowering plant native to Eurasia and Africa, is increasingly recognized for its medicinal properties.

This plant, also known as mouse-ear cress, contains compounds with potential therapeutic benefits, such as glucosinolates, which have been studied for their anti-cancer effects. Arabidopsis thaliana is a popular model organism in plant biology due to its compact size, short life cycle, and genetic tractability.

It thrives in well-drained soil and cool temperatures, making it a suitable plant for horticultural enthusiasts in temperate climates. Botanically, Arabidopsis thaliana is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which also includes broccoli, kale, and mustard.

Its narrow leaves and small yellow flowers are characteristic of this family, and its genome has been fully sequenced, providing a wealth of information for researchers studying plant evolution and development.

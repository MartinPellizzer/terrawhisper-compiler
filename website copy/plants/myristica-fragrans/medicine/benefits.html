10 Best Health Benefits of Myristica fragrans (Nutmeg And Mace)

Myristica fragrans, commonly known as nutmeg, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to its various medicinal benefits.

The spice contains numerous essential volatile oils, such as myristicin, elemicin, and eugenol, which have been found to possess anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial properties. Moreover, nutmeg has been used to alleviate gastrointestinal disorders, such as indigestion, nausea, and diarrhea, due to its carminative and antispasmodic effects.

Additionally, some studies suggest that nutmeg may help reduce anxiety and improve sleep quality, thanks to its mild sedative properties. However, it is important to note that excessive consumption of nutmeg can lead to toxicity, causing symptoms such as hallucinations, tachycardia, and seizures.

Therefore, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using nutmeg for medicinal purposes.

1. Alleviates digestive discomfort

Nutmeg & Mace, derived from the Myristica fragrans plant, alleviates digestive discomfort, meaning it can help soothe symptoms such as bloating, gas, and upset stomach.

The main active constituents responsible for this benefit are volatile oils like sabinene, pinene, and camphene, as well as fixed oils such as myristicin, elemicin, and eugenol. The primary parts of the Myristica fragrans plant that provide this relief are the seeds, from which nutmeg is derived, and the arils, which are the red lacy coverings of the seeds, from which mace is obtained. Medicinal preparations of Myristica fragrans that are used to alleviate digestive discomfort include powdered nutmeg and mace, nutmeg and mace essential oils, and nutmeg and mace teas.

The main health conditions that this benefit of Myristica fragrans helps relieve include dyspepsia, gastrointestinal spasms, and nausea.

What to know before using Lycopus europaeus (European Bugleweed)?

Lycopus europaeus, also known as gypsywort, is a perennial herb that thrives in damp environments, such as wet meadows, riverbanks, and stream sides.

This plant, which grows up to 1 meter tall, is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small white flowers arranged in dense spikes. Medicinally, Lycopus europaeus is valued for its astringent and sedative properties, making it a popular remedy for treating various conditions such as skin irritations, inflammation, and palpitations.

For optimal growth, Lycopus europaeus prefers partial shade and consistently moist soil, and it can be propagated through division or seed.

What are the medicinal uses of Lycopus europaeus?

Lycopus europaeus is used to treat medical conditions such as Graves' disease and thyroid disorders due to its ability to reduce thyroid hormone production.

The active constituents of Lycopus europaeus include phenolic acids, rosmarinic acid, and tannins, which possess anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antioxidant properties. Medicinal preparations of Lycopus europaeus include teas, capsules, and tinctures, usually standardized to contain a specific amount of rosmarinic acid.

Possible side effects of Lycopus europaeus may include stomach upset, headache, and allergic reactions, especially in individuals who are sensitive to mint or salvia. Precautions of using Lycopus europaeus include avoiding its use during pregnancy and breastfeeding or in individuals with kidney or liver disease.

Additionally, it is essential to consult with a healthcare provider before taking Lycopus europaeus with other medications or herbs, as it may interact with certain drugs, such as anticoagulants or thyroid medications.

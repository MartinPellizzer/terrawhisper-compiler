10 Best Health Benefits of Tanacetum parthenium (Feverfew)

Tanacetum parthenium, also known as feverfew, is a medicinal herb that has been used for centuries to treat a variety of ailments.

One of the most well-known benefits of feverfew is its ability to prevent and treat migraines. Studies have shown that taking feverfew daily can reduce the frequency and severity of migraines, and may also help to alleviate symptoms such as nausea and sensitivity to light.

Additionally, feverfew has anti-inflammatory properties, which make it useful in treating conditions such as arthritis and inflammatory skin conditions. Some research also suggests that feverfew may have potential cancer-fighting properties, although more research is needed in this area. Furthermore, feverfew can be used to treat fever and menstrual discomfort.

However, it is important to note that feverfew can cause side effects such as mouth ulcers and digestive problems, and should be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

1. Reduces inflammation

Feverfew, specifically the Tanacetum parthenium variety, reduces inflammation by inhibiting the production of inflammatory chemicals in the body.

The main active constituents responsible for this benefit are parthenolide and sesquiterpene lactones. These compounds are primarily found in the leaves of the Tanacetum parthenium plant. The most common medicinal preparations of Feverfew include dried leaves, capsules, and tinctures.

The main health conditions that this benefit of Tanacetum parthenium helps relieve include migraines, rheumatoid arthritis, and other inflammatory conditions.

Tanacetum parthenium reduces inflammation thanks to the active constituents listed below.

  • Parthenolide
  • Chrysanthenol
  • Chrysanthenyl acetate
  • Parthenolide acetate
  • Flavonoids
  • Tannins
  • Essential oil
  • Sesquiterpene lactones
  • Caffeoylquinic acids
  • Volatile oils

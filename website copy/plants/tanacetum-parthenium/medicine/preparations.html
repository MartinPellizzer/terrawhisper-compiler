Tanacetum parthenium, also known as feverfew, is a medicinal herb with a long history of use in traditional medicine.

Its leaves and flowers are commonly prepared as a tisane, or herbal tea, to help alleviate symptoms of migraines, arthritis, and menstrual discomfort. The dried or fresh parts of the plant can also be made into a tincture, an alcohol-based extract, which can be taken orally for its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties. Feverfew has been shown to contain compounds such as parthenolide, which may inhibit the production of serotonin and other substances that can cause blood vessels to narrow, thereby reducing the onset of migraines.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new herbal remedies, as feverfew can cause side effects such as mouth ulcers, stomach upset, and allergic reactions in some individuals.

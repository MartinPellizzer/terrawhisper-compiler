What to know before using Punica granatum (Pomegranate)?

Punica granatum, commonly known as the pomegranate, is a fruit-bearing deciduous shrub that has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries.

This plant is native to the region extending from Iran to northern India and has since been cultivated in many regions of the world with Mediterranean climates. The pomegranate has a unique botanical structure, with its juicy edible seeds, known as arils, encased in a tough, inedible outer rind. The medicinal properties of pomegranates are vast, with studies suggesting that they can help reduce the risk of various diseases, including heart disease and cancer, due to their high antioxidant and polyphenol content.

Horticulturally, pomegranates thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, and they are drought-tolerant once established.

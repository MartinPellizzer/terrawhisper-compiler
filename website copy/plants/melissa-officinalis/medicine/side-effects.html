10 possible side effects of Melissa officinalis (Lemon Balm) if used improperly

Melissa officinalis, also known as lemon balm, is an herb that is often used in alternative medicine, but if used improperly, it can have several negative health effects.

First, it can cause excessive sedation, as it has natural calming and sleep-inducing properties. This can be dangerous if a person operates heavy machinery or drives a vehicle after taking it. Second, it can interact with certain medications, such as sedatives, increasing the risk of drowsiness and decreased cognitive function.

Additionally, high doses of lemon balm can lead to gastrointestinal issues like nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Furthermore, it can cause headaches and increased heart rate in some individuals. Lastly, it may affect hormone levels in the body, leading to potential reproductive issues, although more research is needed in this area.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal supplements, including Melissa officinalis.

1. Nausea

Nausea caused by Melissa Officinalis refers to a queasy or uncomfortable feeling in the stomach that can sometimes lead to vomiting.

The main active constituents in Melissa Officinalis that can cause this side effect are tannins, essential oils, and bitter compounds. This effect is most commonly seen in medicinal preparations such as teas, capsules, and tinctures when they are misused or taken in excessive amounts.

People who are more likely to experience nausea after using Melissa Officinalis include those with sensitive stomachs, pregnant women, and individuals who take large doses of the herb. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase it, drink plenty of water, and avoid taking the herb on an empty stomach.

Additionally, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using Melissa Officinalis, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

2. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Melissa Officinalis refers to a sensation of lightheadedness, unsteadiness, or slight spinning that can occur in some individuals.

The main active constituents in Melissa Officinalis that can cause this side effect are its volatile oils, including citral, citrals, geraniol, and linalyl acetate. The medicinal preparations of Melissa Officinalis that can cause dizziness if misused include herbal teas, capsules, tinctures, and essential oils. Those who are more likely to experience this side effect are people who are sensitive to the herb, have a low tolerance to its active constituents, or who take high doses of the herb.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of Melissa Officinalis and gradually increase to the desired dose, follow the recommended dosage on the product label, and seek advice from a healthcare professional before using this herb if you have a medical condition, are taking medication, or are pregnant or breastfeeding.

3. Wheezing

Wheezing caused by Melissa Officinalis refers to the occurrence of wheezing as a potential side effect.

This side effect manifests as a high-pitched whistling sound that is produced when an individual breathes, typically as a result of narrowed or constricted airways. The main active constituents of Melissa Officinalis that have been associated with this side effect are its essential oils, which contain compounds such as citral, citronellal, and linalool.

These constituents can cause allergic reactions or hypersensitivity reactions in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as wheezing. Medicinal preparations of Melissa Officinalis that can cause this side effect if misused include herbal teas, capsules, and tinctures. People who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with a history of allergic reactions to plants in the Lamiaceae family, as well as those with respiratory conditions such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

To avoid this side effect, it is important to use Melissa Officinalis under the guidance of a qualified healthcare practitioner, to follow dosage instructions carefully, and to be aware of any potential allergic reactions or respiratory symptoms that may occur after using the herb.

4. Abdominal Pain

Abdominal pain caused by Melissa Officinalis refers to discomfort or pain in the abdominal area that may occur due to the use of this herb.

The main active constituents in Melissa Officialis that can cause this side effect are tannins, camphor, and citalopeptides. The main medicinal preparations of Melissa Officinalis that can cause this side effect if misused include teas, capsules, and essential oils.

Individuals who are more likely to experience abdominal pain after using Melissa Officinalis are those with a history of gastrointestinal disorders, such as irritable bowel syndrome or inflammatory bowel disease. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of Melissa Officinalis and gradually increase the dosage, and to drink plenty of water when taking the herb.

Additionally, it is important to avoid using Melissa Officinalis for prolonged periods and to consult with a healthcare provider before using this herb if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking any medications.

5. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Melissa Officinalis refers to an upset stomach resulting in loose or watery stools.

This side effect can be attributed to the main active constituents of Melissa Officinalis, such as terpenes and phenolic acids. The primary medicinal preparations of Melissa Officinalis that can cause diarrhea if misused include extracts, teas, and capsules. Those who are more likely to experience this side effect are individuals with a sensitive digestive system or those who consume large quantities of the herb.

To avoid diarrhea when using Melissa Officinalis, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase, consume it with food, and consult with a healthcare professional before using it for medicinal purposes.

6. Drowsiness

Drowsiness caused by Melissa Officinalis refers to the sensation of sleepiness or tiredness that may occur after ingesting this herb.

This effect is primarily due to the active constituents rosmarinic acid and citronellal, which have sedative properties. The main medicinal preparations that can cause drowsiness if misused include lemon balm teas, capsules, and tinctures. People who are more likely to experience this side effect include those who are sensitive to sedatives, have a low tolerance to lemon balm, or consume large amounts of the herb.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider before using lemon balm, especially if taking other sedatives or medications.

7. Increased Appetite

Increased appetite caused by Melissa Officinalis refers to a potential side effect where an individual may experience a heightened desire for food intake.

The main active constituents in Melissa Officinalis that can cause this side effect include rosmarinic acid, eugenol, and citral, which are known to have various effects on the body, including appetite stimulation. This side effect is most commonly associated with medicinal preparations such as lemon balm tea, tinctures, and capsules, especially when taken in large doses or over an extended period. People who are more likely to experience this side effect are those with a pre-existing tendency towards increased appetite or those who are already taking medications that can increase appetite.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to use lemon balm in moderation, follow recommended dosage instructions, and consult with a healthcare provider to determine if lemon balm is appropriate for your individual health needs.

8. Vomiting

Vomiting caused by Melissa Officinalis refers to the involuntary forceful expulsion of the stomach's contents through the mouth.

This side effect is typically caused by the main active constituents in Melissa Officinalis, which include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils. The medicinal preparations that can cause vomiting if misused include lemon balm teas, capsules, and tinctures. Individuals who are more likely to experience vomiting as a side effect of using Melissa Officinalis are those who consume large amounts of the herb or those who are sensitive or allergic to its components.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to use Melissa Officinalis in moderate amounts, under the guidance of a healthcare professional, and to be mindful of any adverse reactions that may occur.

9. Headache

Headache caused by Melissa Officinalis refers to a moderate to severe pain in the head that may occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents of Melissa Officinalis that can cause headaches include citronellal, geraniol, and linalyl acetate. The medicinal preparations of Melissa Officinalis that can cause headaches if misused include essential oils, teas, and capsules. People who are more likely to experience headaches from using Melissa Officinalis include those who are sensitive to its active constituents, those who consume large amounts of the herb, and those who are dehydrated or have a low tolerance for its effects.

To avoid headaches when using Melissa Officinalis, it is important to follow the recommended dosages, drink plenty of water, and avoid using the herb if you are sensitive to its active constituents or have a history of migraines.

10. Rash

Rash caused by Melissa Officinalis refers to a skin reaction that can occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents in lemon balm that can cause this side effect are rosmarinic acid, caffeic acid, and thymol. These compounds can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals, resulting in a rash.

Medicinal preparations of lemon balm that can cause this side effect if misused include essential oils, steeped teas, and dried leaves used in herbal remedies. People who are more likely to experience a rash from using lemon balm are those with allergies to other members of the Lamiaceae family, such as mint, sage, and basil, as well as those with sensitive skin. To avoid this side effect, it is important to use lemon balm in moderation, avoid applying it directly to the skin, and discontinue use if a rash or other skin reaction occurs.

It is also recommended to perform a patch test before using lemon balm topically.

10 Best Health Benefits of Melissa officinalis (Lemon Balm)

Melissa officinalis, also known as lemon balm, is a herb that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to its various health benefits.

It has been found to have anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antioxidant properties, making it a valuable tool in treating a variety of ailments. One of the most well-known uses of lemon balm is for its calming effects on the nervous system, helping to reduce anxiety and promote sleep.

Additionally, it has been shown to improve cognitive function, particularly in tasks that require sustained attention. Furthermore, lemon balm has been used to alleviate digestive issues such as bloating, indigestion, and nausea.

Its medicinal benefits are often attributed to its rosmarinic acid and eugenol content, which are believed to be responsible for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

1. Alleviates anxiety symptoms

Lemon Balm, or Melissa officinalis, alleviates anxiety symptoms, meaning it can help reduce feelings of worry, nervousness, and tension related to anxiety disorders.

The main active constituents in Lemon Balm that contribute to this benefit are rosmarinic acid and flavonoids, such as luteolin and apigenin. The primary parts of the Melissa officinalis plant that provide this anxiety-relieving benefit are the leaves and flowers. Common medicinal preparations of Lemon Balm used to alleviate anxiety symptoms include dried leaves for tea, essential oil for aromatherapy, and tinctures or capsules for oral consumption.

The main health conditions that this benefit of Melissa officinalis helps relieve include generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), panic disorder, and depression-induced anxiety.

Melissa officinalis alleviates anxiety symptoms thanks to the active constituents listed below.

  • Rosmarinic Acid
  • Citral
  • Citronellal
  • Geraniol
  • Luteolin
  • Apigenin
  • Quercetin
  • Orientin
  • Vitamin C
  • Essential Oils

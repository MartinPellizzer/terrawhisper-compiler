Melissa officinalis, also known as lemon balm, is a herb that has been used in medicinal preparations for centuries due to its calming and soothing properties.

Two common forms of melissa officinalis preparations are tisanes and tinctures. A tisane, or herbal tea, is made by steeping dried lemon balm leaves in hot water, creating a warm and comforting beverage that may help alleviate symptoms of anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues.

On the other hand, a tincture is a concentrated extract of lemon balm that is made by soaking the leaves in alcohol or glycerin. Tinctures are typically taken in small doses, and they may provide relief for various ailments, such as cold sores, menstrual cramps, and indigestion.

Both tisanes and tinctures of melissa officinalis are easy to make at home and can be a natural and effective way to treat common health issues.

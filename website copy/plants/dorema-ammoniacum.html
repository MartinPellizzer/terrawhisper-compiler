What to know before using Dorema ammoniacum (Ammoniacum)?

Dorema ammoniacum, also known as Levant dorema, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

Its botanical characteristics include tall, branched stems that grow up to 1.5 meters tall, and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, which include expectorant, antispasmodic, and diuretic effects.

It has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, such as asthma, coughs, and digestive issues. Dorema ammoniacum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it is drought-tolerant once established.

However, it requires a long growing season and does not tolerate frost, making it suitable for USDA hardiness zones 7-10.

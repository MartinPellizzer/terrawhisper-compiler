What to know before using Foeniculum vulgare (Fennel)?

Foeniculum vulgare, also known as fennel, is a plant with a rich history of medicinal use.

Its seeds are commonly used in teas and supplements for their potential to soothe digestive issues, alleviate menstrual cramps, and promote milk production in breastfeeding mothers. Fennel thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and can grow up to 6 feet tall with feathery, bright green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant's distinctive licorice-like flavor and aroma come from its essential oil, which is found in the seeds, leaves, and roots.

In addition to its medicinal uses, fennel is also a popular culinary herb, adding a unique flavor to dishes in cuisines around the world.

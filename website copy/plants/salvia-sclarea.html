What to know before using Salvia sclarea (Clary Sage)?

Salvia sclarea, also known as clary sage, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb with a variety of medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its calming and relaxing effects, and it may also have anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and estrogen-like properties. In horticultural conditions, Salvia sclarea prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it can grow up to 3-4 feet tall with hairy stems and large, fragrant leaves. The plant produces spikes of pink or lilac flowers, which are attractive to bees and other pollinators.

Botanically, Salvia sclarea is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and it is native to the Mediterranean region.

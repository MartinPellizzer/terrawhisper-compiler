What to know before using Aegopodium podagraria (Ashweed)?

Aegopodium podagraria, also known as goutweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe that has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and analgesic effects, which have been traditionally used to treat conditions such as arthritis, gout, and urinary tract infections. In horticultural settings, goutweed is known for its adaptability, as it can thrive in various conditions, including partial shade and a range of soil types, making it a popular choice for groundcover. Botanically, Aegopodium podagraria is a member of the carrot family and produces clusters of small white flowers in umbrella-shaped inflorescences.

However, it can also be invasive in some areas due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.

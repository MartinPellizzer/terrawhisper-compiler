Gymnadenia conopsea, also known as fragrant orchid, is a species of orchid that has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.

It contains various compounds with potential medicinal properties, such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenanthrenes, which have been studied for their anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticancer effects. In horticulture, Gymnadenia conopsea prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil and partial shade. It requires a cool, moist growing environment and can be propagated through seed or division.

Botanically, this herbaceous perennial plant grows to a height of 30-60 cm, producing narrow, green leaves and tall spikes of fragrant, pinkish-purple flowers from late spring to early summer.

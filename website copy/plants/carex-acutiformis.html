What to know before using Carex acutiformis (Lesser Pond Sedge)?

Carex acutiformis, also known as common greenborn sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetlands in Europe and Asia.

Its Medicinal properties include its use as a diuretic and a treatment for bladder and kidney ailments. Carex acutiformis thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers wet, nutrient-rich soils. Botanically, it is characterized by its triangular, keeled leaves, and its spikelets, which bloom in late spring and are long and cylindrical, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The female flowers give way to green, beaked fruits in the summer.

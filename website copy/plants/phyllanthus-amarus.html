What to know before using Phyllanthus amarus (Phyllanthus)?

Phyllanthus amarus, also known as bitter bush, is a valuable medicinal plant that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

It is native to tropical regions of the world and thrives in well-drained soil and sunlight. The plant belongs to the Phyllanthus genus and is characterized by its small, green, oval-shaped leaves and tiny, greenish-yellow flowers. Phyllanthus amarus is known for its anti-hepatotoxic, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties, making it a popular remedy for a variety of ailments including liver disorders, kidney stones, and diabetes.

It is typically prepared as a tea or tincture and consumed orally, although topical applications have also been used for skin conditions.

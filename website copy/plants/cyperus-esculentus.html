Cyperus esculentus, also known as tiger nut, is a nutritionally-rich plant with numerous medicinal properties.

This plant can grow in a variety of horticultural conditions, from sandy to clay loams and in both acidic and alkaline soils, making it adaptable to different environments. Cyperus esculentus is a perennial sedge that grows up to 60 cm tall, with triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves.

Its small, tuber-like rhizomes are rich in carbohydrates, proteins, and healthy fats, and have been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including diarrhea, stomach pain, and skin conditions.

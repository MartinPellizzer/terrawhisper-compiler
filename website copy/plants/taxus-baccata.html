What to know before using Taxus baccata (Yew)?

Taxus baccata, also known as European yew, is a versatile and hardy evergreen tree native to Western, Southern, and Central Europe.

Its bark, leaves, and seeds have long been used in medicinal preparations, valued for their anticancer properties, particularly in the treatment of lung cancer. The tree is adaptable to a wide range of horticultural conditions, thriving in full sun or partial shade and able to tolerate a variety of soil types. Taxus baccata is a conifer, featuring a slow-growing, spreading habit and distinctive dark green, linear leaves with needle-like appearance.

Notably, all parts of the plant are highly toxic if ingested, except for the fleshy aril surrounding the seed, making it a striking yet potentially dangerous addition to gardens and landscapes.

What to know before using Myrtus communis (Myrtle)?

Myrtus communis, also known as the common myrtle, is a versatile plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antispasmodic properties. It is also known to have potential benefits for digestive and respiratory health.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Myrtus communis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and can be grown in USDA hardiness zones 8-11. This evergreen shrub features fragrant, white flowers, and glossy, dark green leaves that are often used in culinary and craft applications.

From a botanical perspective, Myrtus communis belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement and star-shaped flowers.

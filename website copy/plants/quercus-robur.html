What to know before using Quercus robur (English Oak)?

Quercus robur, also known as the English oak or pedunculate oak, is a species of tree with a rich history of medicinal use and horticultural application.

Medicinally, Quercus robur extracts have been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including diarrhea, fever, and inflammation, due to their astringent, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties. In terms of horticultural conditions, Quercus robur thrives in temperate regions with well-drained soil and full sun exposure, and can grow up to 20-40 meters tall with a broad, spreading canopy. Botanically, Quercus robur is characterized by its lobed, serrated leaves, acorns, and distinctive bark, with deep furrows and broad, flat ridges.

The tree's longevity and hardiness have made it a symbol of strength and resilience in many cultures.

What are the medicinal uses of Quercus robur?

Quercus robur has several medicinal uses, such as treating diarrhea, reducing fever, and slowing down the growth of certain cancer cells.

Its constituents include tannins, flavonoids, and various minerals and vitamins, which contribute to its astringent, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. Preparations of Quercus robur include decoctions, infusions, and tinctures, which can be taken orally or applied topically.

However, it can cause side effects such as stomach upset, nausea, and kidney damage if taken in large doses or for extended periods. Therefore, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using Quercus robur and to follow dosage instructions carefully.

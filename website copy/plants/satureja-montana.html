What to know before using Satureja montana (Winter Savory)?

Satureja montana, also known as winter savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hardy plant that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it can also tolerate light shade and poor soil. The plant grows to be about 12-20 inches tall and has small, narrow leaves and purple flowers that bloom in the summer.

As a member of the mint family, winter savory has a pungent, aromatic scent and a wide range of medicinal properties. It has been used traditionally to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues, menstrual cramps, and respiratory infections.

The leaves and flowers can be used to make teas, tinctures, and ointments, and the herb can also be used fresh or dried as a seasoning in cooking.

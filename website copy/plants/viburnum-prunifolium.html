What to know before using Viburnum prunifolium (Black Haw)?

Viburnum prunifolium, also known as blackhaw viburnum, is a shrub native to the forests of eastern North America.

It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to alleviate menstrual cramps and symptoms of menopause, due to its phytoestrogen content. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, and can grow up to 15 feet tall.

Its botanical characteristics include opposite, simple leaves, and clusters of white flowers in the spring that give way to blue-black berries in the fall, which are beloved by birds and other wildlife.

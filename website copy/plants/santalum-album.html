What to know before using Santalum album (Sandalwood)?

Santalum album, also known as Indian sandalwood, is a small tropical tree valued for its aromatic heartwood which contains the essential oil used in perfumes, cosmetics, and medicines.

This plant is a hemi-parasite, meaning it has the ability to derive nourishment from other plants through root connections, and thrives in dry, rocky soil in the eastern parts of India. The tree has a distinctive appearance with its spreading, irregular crown, small, leathery leaves, and tiny, pinkish-white flowers.

Medicinally, the essential oil extracted from the heartwood has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and expectorant properties, making it useful in treating a variety of ailments including skin conditions, respiratory infections, and digestive disorders.

The most notable of these constituents are alkamides, which are believed to have immune-boosting and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, Echinacea purpurea contains cichoric acid, which is a polyphenol that has been shown to have antioxidant and antiviral effects.

The plant also contains essential oils, such as echinaceol and caryophyllene, which have been found to have antibacterial and antifungal properties. Furthermore, Echinacea purpurea contains polysaccharides, which are complex carbohydrates that have been shown to have immune-stimulating effects.

These active constituents work together to provide a range of potential health benefits, making Echinacea purpurea a popular herbal remedy for a variety of ailments.

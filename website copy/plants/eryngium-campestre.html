What to know before using Eryngium campestre (Eryngo)?

Eryngium campestre, also known as field eryngo, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family that is native to Europe and North Africa.

It has thin, spiny leaves and produces round, blue-green flower heads in the summer. This plant is known for its medicinal properties, including its ability to treat digestive issues and skin conditions. Eryngium campestre thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it is drought-tolerant.

It is also deer-resistant, making it a popular choice for gardeners looking to add unique texture and color to their landscapes.

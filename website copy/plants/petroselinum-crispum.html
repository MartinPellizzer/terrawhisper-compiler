What are the horticultural conditions of Petroselinum crispum?

Petroselinum crispum needs well-drained soil that is rich in organic matter to thrive.

What are the planting tips of Petroselinum crispum?

When it comes to planting Petroselinum crispum, starting from seeds is the most common method.

Sow seeds directly into the garden soil about 1/4 inch deep and 12 inches apart. For earlier germination, you can start seeds indoors about 8-10 weeks before the last expected frost date. Keep the soil consistently moist during germination, which can take up to three weeks.

Transplant seedlings outdoors after the danger of frost has passed, and the soil temperature has consistently reached 70°F.

What are the harvesting tips of Petroselinum crispum?

Harvesting Petroselinum crispum is best done when the plant has reached at least 6-8 inches in height.

Use scissors to cut off the outer leaves, leaving the inner leaves intact to continue growing. Harvesting frequently encourages new growth and prolongs the plant's life.

Fresh parsley can be stored in the refrigerator for up to a week by wrapping it in a damp paper towel and placing it in a plastic bag.

What to know before using Barosma betulina (Buchu)?

Barosma betulina, also known as bucco or honey flower, is a plant native to South Africa that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to aid digestion and alleviate symptoms of heartburn and indigestion. The plant is a small shrub that thrives in well-drained, sandy soil and requires a sunny position.

Barosma betulina is characterized by its narrow, glossy green leaves and white or pink flowers that emit a sweet, honey-like scent. It is a hardy plant that can withstand dry conditions, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Additionally, the leaves of Barosma betulina are used to make a popular herbal tea, known for its unique, slightly sweet flavor and aroma.

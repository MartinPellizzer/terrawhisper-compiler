What to know before using Crataeva nurvala (Varuna)?

Crataeva nurvala, also known as varuna, is a plant with a range of medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine to treat conditions such as kidney stones, gastrointestinal disorders, and rheumatism. The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, and is native to India, Sri Lanka, and other parts of southern Asia.

It has smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with 5-7 leaflets. The flowers are small and white, and the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to reveal seeds covered in fine hairs.

Crataeva nurvala prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and can be propagated through seeds or cuttings.

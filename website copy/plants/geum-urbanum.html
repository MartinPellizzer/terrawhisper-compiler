What to know before using Geum urbanum (Avens)?

Geum urbanum, also known as wood avens or herb bennet, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It has a rich history of use in traditional medicine, particularly for its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant grows well in partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Geum urbanum features lobed, toothed leaves and yellow flowers with prominent stamens, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Its root, rich in tannins, has been used to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and skin conditions.

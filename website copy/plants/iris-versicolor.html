What to know before using Iris versicolor (Blue Flag)?

Iris versicolor, also known as the blue flag iris, is a perennial plant native to North America.

Medicinally, it has been used by indigenous peoples and herbalists for its purported ability to stimulate the liver and gallbladder, and as a detoxifying agent. It is important to note that its rhizomes contain toxic compounds, and should only be used under the guidance of a trained practitioner.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Iris versicolor prefers moist to wet soil, and can often be found growing in wetlands and along riverbanks. It thrives in full sun to partial shade, and its tall, slender leaves and striking blue flowers make it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant grows from short, thick rhizomes, and its flowers have six petals - three upright "standards" and three hanging "falls," which are often marked with yellow or white.

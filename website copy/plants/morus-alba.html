What to know before using Morus alba (White Mulberry)?

Morus alba, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a fast-growing, deciduous tree native to China that has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

It is widely cultivated for its leaves, which are the primary food source for silkworms, and for its fruit, which are eaten fresh or made into various products. Medicinally, Morus alba has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to treat a variety of ailments, including fever, cough, and high blood pressure.

The tree can grow up to 40 feet tall and has a broad, spreading canopy. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, and is adaptable to a wide range of conditions. The leaves are simple, alternate, and serrated, and the flowers are small and greenish-white.

The fruit is a cluster of small, drupel-like fruits that range in color from white to pink to purple when ripe.

By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Dioscorea villosa, also known as wild yam, is a plant that has been used in medicinal preparations for centuries.

One common preparation is a tisane, or herbal tea, made by steeping dried or fresh wild yam root in hot water. This soothing tea is sometimes used to alleviate symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes and mood swings.

A tincture can also be made by soaking the chopped root in alcohol for several weeks, which extracts the medicinal compounds. This concentrated extract can be taken in small doses to help with digestive issues, including cramps and nausea. Additionally, wild yam tincture has been used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory conditions, such as bronchitis and asthma.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new medicinal preparations, as wild yam can have side effects and interact with certain medications.

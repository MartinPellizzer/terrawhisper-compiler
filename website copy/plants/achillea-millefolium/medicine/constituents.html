10 Best Active Constituents of Achillea millefolium (Yarrow)

Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow, is a plant that contains various active constituents with medicinal properties.

The main components include essential oils, flavonoids, sesquiterpene lactones, and coumarins. The essential oil of yarrow is rich in chemicals like alpha- and beta-pinene, sabinene, and limonene, which have anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and analgesic effects.

Flavonoids, such as rutin, luteolin, and apigenin, have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, while sesquiterpene lactones, such as costunolide and dehydrocostus lactone, have been shown to have cytotoxic and anti-tumor activities. Coumarins, such as aesculetin and umbelliferone, have been found to have anticoagulant and anti-tumor properties.

These active constituents make yarrow a valuable herb in traditional medicine for treating various ailments, including wounds, inflammation, and gastrointestinal issues.

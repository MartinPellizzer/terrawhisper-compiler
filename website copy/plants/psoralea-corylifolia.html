What to know before using Psoralea corylifolia (Bu Gu Zhi)?

Psoralea corylifolia, also known as Babchi, is a plant with a rich history in traditional medicine.

It has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to treat various conditions such as vitiligo, leprosy, and skin diseases. The plant contains bioactive compounds, including psoralens, which exhibit photochemotherapeutic properties. Psoralea corylifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it is characterized by its compound, alternating leaves and small, purple flowers that bloom in the summer.

Its seeds, which contain the highest concentration of psoralens, are used in medicinal preparations.

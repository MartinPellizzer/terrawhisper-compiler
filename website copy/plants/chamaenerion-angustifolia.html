What to know before using Chamaenerion angustifolia (Rosebay Willowherb)?

Chamaenerion angustifolia, also known as Fireweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant that is native to temperate areas of the Northern Hemisphere.

Medicinally, Fireweed has been used for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The plant contains tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids which are believed to be responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Fireweed prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil. It can grow in a variety of soil types, including sandy, loamy, and clay soils.

Fireweed is a tall, striking plant that can reach heights of up to 6 feet, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of pink to purple flowers that bloom from the bottom up.

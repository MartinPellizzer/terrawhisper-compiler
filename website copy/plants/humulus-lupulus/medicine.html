Humulus lupulus (Hops) Medicinal Guide

Humulus lupulus, commonly known as hops, has a variety of medicinal benefits, including use as a natural remedy for anxiety, sleep disorders, and restlessness.

The plant contains several constituents that contribute to its medicinal properties, including bitter acids, essential oils, and flavonoids. Preparations of Humulus lupulus for medicinal use typically involve brewing the flowers to make a tea or tincture, or using it in capsule or tablet form. While Humulus lupulus is generally considered safe, some side effects may include digestive issues, skin irritation, and increased heart rate.

Precautions should be taken when using Humulus lupulus, particularly for those who are pregnant or breastfeeding, have hormone-sensitive conditions, or are taking sedatives or other medications that may interact with the plant.

What are the active constituents of Humulus lupulus (Hops)?

Humulus lupulus has several active constituents that contribute to its medicinal properties.

These include humulones and lupulones, which have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory effects. Xanthohumol and isoxanthohumol, prenylflavonoids found in hops, have been shown to have antioxidant, anti-cancer, and anti-inflammatory properties.

8-prenylnaringenin, another prenylflavonoid, has been studied for its potential estrogenic and anti-cancer effects. Additionally, hops contain essential oils such as myrcene, humulene, and caryophyllene, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial effects.

Hops also contain vitamin B complex, and minerals such as manganese, copper, zinc, and iron, which are essential for various bodily functions.

The following list shows the active constituents of Humulus lupulus.

  • 1. Humulones: Precursors to bitter acids in hops, they have anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and potential anticancer properties.
  • 2. Lupulones: Oxidized form of humulones, they contribute to hop's bitter taste and have shown antimalarial, antiviral, and anticancer activities.
  • 3. Xanthohumol: A prenylated flavonoid, xanthohumol is known for its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer properties, and may help prevent neurodegenerative diseases.
  • 4. Isoxanthohumol: A flavonoid with weak estrogenic activity, isoxanthohumol has antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer properties, and may help reduce menopause symptoms.
  • 5. 8-prenylnaringenin: A potent phytoestrogen, 8-prenylnaringenin has been shown to have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer properties, and may help alleviate menopause symptoms.
  • 6. Other prenylflavonoids: A group of compounds including xanthohumol and isoxanthohumol, prenylflavonoids exhibit various bioactivities, such as antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer effects.
  • 7. Essential oils: Volatile compounds derived from hops, essential oils consist of various terpenes and have been used for their aroma, flavor, and potential health benefits, including antibacterial, antifungal, and sedative properties.
  • 8. Myrcene: A terpene found in hops, myrcene is known for its sedative, analgesic, and anti-inflammatory effects, and contributes to the aroma and flavor of hops.
  • 9. Humulene: A sesquiterpene in hops, humulene exhibits anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and appetite-suppressing properties, and contributes to the aroma and flavor of hops.
  • 10. Caryophyllene: A sesquiterpene present in hops, caryophyllene has been studied for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antianxiety properties, and contributes to the aroma and flavor of hops.

What are the medicinal preparations of Humulus lupulus (Hops)?

Humulus lupulus has several medicinal preparations, such as infusions, tinctures, decoctions, fluid extracts, dry extracts, powders, capsules, and teas.

These preparations are used to treat a variety of ailments, including anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness. An infusion is made by steeping the plant material in hot water, while a tincture is created by soaking the plant in alcohol.

A decoction is a concentrated liquid made by boiling and then straining the plant material. Fluid and dry extracts are concentrated forms of the plant, while powders and capsules are easy ways to consume the herb.

Teas, syrups, and spiritual baths are also used, as well as mouthwashes, gargles, eyewashes, and steam inhalations, all of which provide different methods of administering the medicinal properties of hops.

The following list shows the medicinal preparations of Humulus lupulus.

  • 1. Infusion: A preparation made by steeping the dried flowers (hops) in hot water to extract the medicinal compounds, commonly used for its sedative and anxiety-reducing properties.
  • 2. Tincture: A concentrated alcohol extract of hops, which can be used for its sedative, digestive, and anti-microbial effects.
  • 3. Decoction: A strong water extract of hops, created by boiling the plant material, often used for its sedative and diuretic effects.
  • 4. Fluid extract: A more concentrated form of tincture, where a quantity of plant material is extracted in a smaller amount of solvent, usually alcohol, for medicinal use.
  • 5. Dry extract: A concentrated form of hops extract, obtained by removing the solvent from a liquid extract, often used in the manufacturing of capsules and tablets.
  • 6. Powder: Finely ground hops dried and made into a powder for medicinal use, usually taken in capsules for digestive and sedative effects.
  • 7. Capsules: Powdered hops enclosed in a gelatin or vegetarian capsule, providing a measured dose for digestive, sedative, and anti-microbial uses.
  • 8. Compress: A damp cloth soaked in a cooled hop infusion or decoction, applied to the skin to reduce inflammation, relieve pain, or promote relaxation.
  • 9. Poultice: A mash of hops mixed with a liquid, such as hot water or oil, and applied directly to the skin as a warm, soothing treatment for skin irritations or inflammation.
  • 10. Ointment: A semi-solid preparation of hops mixed with a fatty base or petroleum jelly, used topically for its soothing, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic effects.

What are the possible side effects of using Humulus lupulus (Hops) for medicinal purposes?

Humulus lupulus, or hops, can have several side effects if used improperly.

These side effects can include allergic reactions, which may result in symptoms such as skin rash and vomiting. Additionally, hops can cause digestive issues, including nausea and vomiting, and can also lead to dermatitis, a skin condition characterized by inflammation and irritation.

In some cases, hops can also cause drowsiness, sluggishness, and sweating. Other potential side effects of hops include increased heart rate, low blood pressure, and anxiety.

It is important to use hops in moderation and to speak with a healthcare provider if any adverse reactions occur.

The following list shows the possible side effects of Humulus lupulus.

  • 1. Allergic reaction: Symptoms may include rash, difficulty breathing, or swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat.
  • 2. Dermatitis: Characterized by red, itchy, and inflamed skin, possibly due to an allergic reaction or skin irritation.
  • 3. Digestive issues: May cause stomach discomfort, bloating, or changes in bowel movements.
  • 4. Drowsiness: Can result in feelings of sleepiness or decreased alertness, potentially leading to impaired cognitive function.
  • 5. Dry mouth: Can result in a dry, sticky feeling in the mouth, which may increase the risk of dental issues and bad breath.
  • 6. Increased heart rate: Can lead to a faster or irregular heartbeat, which may be accompanied by palpitations or chest discomfort.
  • 7. Low blood pressure: May result in dizziness, lightheadedness, or fainting, particularly when standing up quickly.
  • 8. Nausea: Can cause an unpleasant sensation in the stomach, which may lead to vomiting or loss of appetite.
  • 9. Restlessness: Can result in feelings of anxiety, agitation, or difficulty sleeping.
  • 10. Skin rash: Manifested as red, itchy, or scaly patches on the skin, which may be a sign of an allergic reaction or skin irritation.

What are the precautions to take when using Humulus lupulus (Hops) medicinally?

Before using Humulus lupulus for medicinal purposes, it's important to take some precautions such as consulting a healthcare provider before use to ensure it's safe and appropriate for your health condition.

Monitor dosage to avoid toxicity and limit alcohol consumption while using hops to prevent exacerbating its sedative effects. Avoid using hops during pregnancy or breastfeeding, and check for allergies to hops or related plants before use. Discontinue use if experiencing adverse reactions, and avoid using hops with sedatives or CNS depressants.

Check for interactions with medications or supplements, and avoid using hops if suffering from depression or anxiety disorders. Limit exposure to sunlight while using hops, do not consume raw or unprocessed hops, and use caution when using hops extracts or essential oils. Store hops properly to maintain potency and safety, use a consistent and reliable source of hops, gradually increase dosage to monitor tolerance, and follow dosage instructions carefully. Consult a healthcare provider for specific medical conditions, children or elderly use, and disclose hops use to healthcare providers.

Be aware of potential side effects, such as digestive issues or skin irritation.

The following list shows the precautions to take when using Humulus lupulus.

  • 1. Consult a healthcare provider before use: It is essential to seek professional medical advice to ensure safety and effectiveness of Humulus lupulus for your specific health condition.
  • 2. Monitor dosage to avoid toxicity: Carefully follow dosage recommendations to prevent potential toxicity, as excessive use of hops can lead to harmful side effects.
  • 3. Avoid use during pregnancy or breastfeeding: Pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid using hops, as it may negatively impact fetal development or infant health.
  • 4. Check for allergies to hops or related plants: If you have allergies to hops or related plants, avoid using Humulus lupulus, as it may cause allergic reactions or worsen symptoms.
  • 5. Discontinue use if experiencing adverse reactions: Immediately stop using hops if you experience any negative side effects or allergic reactions.
  • 6. Limit alcohol consumption while using hops: Consuming alcohol while using hops can increase drowsiness and sedation, so it is essential to limit alcohol intake.
  • 7. Avoid using hops with sedatives or CNS depressants: Combining hops with sedatives or central nervous system depressants can enhance their effects and increase the risk of adverse reactions.
  • 8. Check for interactions with medications or supplements: Consult a healthcare provider to ensure hops do not interact with any medications or supplements you are currently taking.
  • 9. Avoid using hops if suffering from depression or anxiety disorders: Using hops may worsen symptoms of depression or anxiety disorders, so it is best to avoid using it if you have these conditions.
  • 10. Limit exposure to sunlight while using hops: Hops can increase photosensitivity, so it is essential to limit exposure to sunlight and wear protective clothing while using hops.

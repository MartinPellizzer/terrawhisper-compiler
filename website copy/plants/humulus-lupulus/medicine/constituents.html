10 Best Active Constituents of Humulus lupulus (Hops)

Humulus lupulus, commonly known as hops, contains several active constituents that are used for medicinal purposes.

The most well-known of these constituents is a group of compounds called humulones, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties. Additionally, hops contain xanthohumol, a prenylated flavonoid that has been studied for its potential cancer-fighting abilities, as well as its ability to improve cardiovascular health. Hops also contain essential oils, such as humulene and myrcene, which have been found to have sedative and anxiolytic effects, making hops a popular natural remedy for anxiety and insomnia.

Finally, hops contain significant amounts of silica, a mineral that is important for bone health and the maintenance of healthy skin, hair, and nails.

1. Humulones

The humulones contained in Humulus Lupulus are a type of bitter resin compound that has been found to have several medicinal properties.

These properties include antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic effects. Humulones have been found to help relieve conditions such as anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness.

They are found primarily in the flowers and cones of the hop plant. Improper use of humulones can lead to several side effects, including digestive issues such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Additionally, high doses of humulones may cause skin irritation and rashes.

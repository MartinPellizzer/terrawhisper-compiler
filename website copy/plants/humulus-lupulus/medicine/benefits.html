10 Best Health Benefits of Humulus lupulus (Hops)

Humulus lupulus, commonly known as hops, is a plant that is well-known for its use in beer-making, but it also has a variety of medicinal benefits.

One of the key medicinal properties of hops is its sedative effect, which can help to alleviate anxiety, restlessness, and insomnia. Additionally, hops have been shown to have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, making them useful for treating skin irritations and infections.

Some studies have also suggested that hops may have anti-cancer properties, particularly in relation to certain types of breast cancer. Furthermore, hops have been used in traditional medicine to treat digestive issues such as loss of appetite, indigestion, and excessive gas.

However, it is important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the medicinal benefits of hops and to determine the most effective and safe ways to use this plant for medicinal purposes.

1. Reduces anxiety symptoms

Hops reduces anxiety symptoms, meaning it can help alleviate feelings of worry, nervousness, and apprehension.

The main active constituents of Humulus lupulus that contribute to this benefit are humulones, lupulones, and essential oils such as myrcene, humulene, and farnesene. The primary parts of the Humulus lupulus plant that have anxiety-reducing properties are the female flowers or strobiles. The main medicinal preparations of Humulus lupulus that help reduce anxiety symptoms include standardized extracts, tinctures, and teas.

The main health conditions that this benefit of Humulus lupulus helps relieve include generalized anxiety disorder, restlessness, and insomnia caused by anxiety.

Humulus lupulus reduces anxiety symptoms thanks to the active constituents listed below.

  • Humulene
  • Caryophyllene
  • Farnesene
  • Myrcene
  • Xanthohumol
  • Isoxanthohumol
  • Lupulone
  • Desmethylxanthohumol
  • 8-prenylnaringenin
  • Naringenin

4. Has antibacterial properties

Hops has antibacterial properties, meaning it can inhibit or destroy bacteria.

The main active constituents of Humulus lupulus that contribute to its antibacterial effect are humulone and lupulone, which are bitter resins found in the resin glands of the hop cone. The primary parts of the Humulus lupulus plant that possess this benefit are the flowers or "cones," which contain the resin glands where these compounds are most concentrated. The main medicinal preparations of Humulus lupulus that provide this benefit include tinctures, extracts, and teas made from the hop cones.

The antibacterial property of Humulus lupulus helps relieve various health conditions, particularly those involving infections of the skin, mouth, and digestive tract.

10 possible side effects of Humulus lupulus (Hops) if used improperly

Humulus lupulus, also known as hops, is a plant commonly used in brewing beer and is believed to have various health benefits when used properly.

However, if used improperly, it can have several potential negative health effects. First, consuming large amounts of hops can cause digestive issues such as bloating, gas, and diarrhea.

Additionally, it may lead to increased heart rate and agitation. In extreme cases, improper use of hops can result in toxicity, which can cause harm to the nervous system and lead to symptoms such as confusion, muscle weakness, and seizures. Pregnant women and individuals with certain medical conditions should avoid using hops altogether, as it may lead to adverse reactions.

As with any substance, it is essential to use hops responsibly and follow recommended dosages.

1. Allergic Reaction

Allergic reaction caused by Humulus Lupulus refers to an adverse immune system response to the allergens present in this plant.

The main active constituents of hops that can cause this side effect are primarily found in the pollen and include humulon, lupulon, and various other volatile oils. Medicinal preparations containing these compounds, such as hops pillows, tinctures, and extracts, can potentially trigger allergic reactions if misused or used excessively. Individuals with known allergies to plants in the Cannabaceae family, or those with pre-existing respiratory conditions, are more likely to experience allergic reactions to hops.

To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to use hops products cautiously, carefully following dosage instructions and consulting with healthcare professionals before incorporating hops into treatment plans, especially for individuals with known allergies or sensitivities to related plant species.

2. Dermatitis

Dermatitis caused by Humulus Lupulus refers to skin inflammation that can occur as a side effect of using this plant.

The main active constituents in hops that can cause this side effect are humulone and lupulone, which can irritate the skin and cause an allergic reaction in some individuals. The medicinal preparations of hops that can cause dermatitis if misused include tinctures, extracts, and teas made from the flowers or leaves of the plant.

People who are more likely to experience this side effect are those with sensitive skin, allergies to ragweed or related plants, and those who use hops frequently or in large amounts. To avoid this side effect, it is important to use hops in moderation, follow dosage recommendations carefully, and discontinue use if any skin irritation occurs.

Additionally, performing a patch test before using hops topically can help identify potential allergic reactions and prevent dermatitis.

3. Digestive Issues

Digestive issues caused by Humulus Lupulus refer to problems in the digestive system such as stomach pain, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

The main active constituents of Humulus Lupulus that can cause this side effect are alpha and beta acids, essential oils, and flavonoids. These constituents can irritate the digestive tract, leading to digestive issues.

The main medicinal preparations of Humulus Lupulus that can cause this side effect if misused include hops poultices, teas, and extracts. People who are more likely to experience digestive issues from using Humulus Lupulus are those with a history of digestive problems or sensitivities to hops.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to use Humulus Lupulus in moderation and under the guidance of a healthcare professional, and to discontinue use if digestive issues occur.

4. Drowsiness

Drowsiness caused by Humulus Lupulus refers to the sensation of sleepiness or urge to sleep that can occur after ingesting this plant.

The main active constituents of hops that are believed to cause this side effect are volatile oils, bitter resins, and flavonoids, particularly the flavonoid known as 8-prenylnaringenin. Medicinal preparations of hops that can cause drowsiness if misused include herbal teas, tinctures, and capsules, which are often used as a natural remedy for anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness.

People who are more likely to experience drowsiness after using hops are those with a history of sleep disorders, those who take hops in large doses, or those who combine hops with other sedative herbs or medications. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of hops, follow the recommended dosage instructions, and avoid driving or operating heavy machinery while using hops.

Additionally, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before taking hops, especially if you are taking other medications, have a medical condition, or are pregnant or breastfeeding.

5. Dry Mouth

Dry mouth caused by Humulus Lupulus refers to a decrease in saliva production in the mouth, leading to a dry, sticky feeling.

The main active constituents in Humulus Lupulus that can cause this side effect are volatile oils and alpha and beta acids. The medicinal preparations of Humulus Lupulus that can cause dry mouth if misused include teas, supplements, and tinctures made from the hop flower. Individuals who are more likely to experience dry mouth from using Humulus Lupulus are those who consume alcohol, are dehydrated, or have a history of dry mouth.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to drink plenty of fluids, avoid alcohol and caffeine, and use Humulus Lupulus as directed by a healthcare professional.

6. Increased Heart Rate

Increased heart rate caused by Humulus Lupulus refers to an elevated pulse rate that can occur following the use of this plant.

The main active constituents in Humulus Lupulus that can cause this side effect are essential oils, bitter acids, and flavonoids. The primary medicinal preparations of Humulus Lupulus that can cause this side effect if misused include hops pillows, tinctures, and teas.

Individuals who are pregnant, have an existing heart condition, or are taking medication that affects heart rate are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid this side effect, it's crucial to use Humulus Lupulus in moderation and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

Additionally, it's important to monitor heart rate before and after using Humulus Lupulus and to discontinue use if an increased heart rate persists.

7. Low Blood Pressure

Low blood pressure caused by Humulus Lupulus refers to an abnormally low level of blood pressure, specifically induced by the use of this herb.

The main active constituents in Humulus Lupulus that can cause this side effect are humulones and lupulones, which have been found to have a relaxing effect on the body, potentially leading to a drop in blood pressure. The main medicinal preparations of Humulus Lupulus that can cause this side effect if misused include hops pillows, tinctures, and teas. Individuals with low blood pressure or those taking medication for high blood pressure are more likely to experience this side effect.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to use Humulus Lupulus as directed and to consult with a healthcare professional before using this herb if you have a history of low blood pressure or are taking medication for high blood pressure.

8. Nausea

Nausea caused by Humulus Lupulus refers to the feeling of discomfort and stomach upset that can occur as a result of using this plant.

The main active constituents of hops that can cause this side effect are bitter acids and essential oils, which can irritate the stomach lining and cause digestive discomfort. The main medicinal preparations of hops that can cause this side effect if misused include herbal teas, tinctures, and supplements made from the flowers or extracts of the plant.

People who are more likely to experience nausea from hops are those with sensitive stomachs or a history of digestive issues, as well as those who consume large amounts of hops or use them for prolonged periods of time. To avoid this side effect, it is important to use hops in moderation and to follow label instructions carefully, avoiding overconsumption.

Additionally, using hops in combination with other calming herbs, such as chamomile or lemon balm, may help to reduce the likelihood of digestive discomfort.

9. Restlessness

Restlessness caused by Humulus Lupulus refers to a state of agitation and difficulty in remaining still that can occur as a side effect of this plant.

The main active constituents in Humulus Lupulus that can cause this side effect are volatile oils, alpha and beta acids, and flavonoids. This side effect is most commonly associated with medicinal preparations such as herbal teas, tinctures, and dietary supplements that are made from the hop flower.

Individuals who are more likely to experience restlessness from Humulus Lupulus include those with a history of anxiety or sleep disorders, as well as those who consume large amounts of the plant or take it for extended periods. To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosages and not to exceed the maximum daily limit. Additionally, it is advised to not consume hops in the evening before bedtime as it may interfere with sleep patterns.

It's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal supplement.

10. Skin Rash

Skin rash caused by Humulus Lupulus refers to an unpleasant skin reaction that can occur in some individuals who come into contact with this plant or its preparations.

The main active constituents in Humulus Lupulus that can cause this side effect include essential oils, flavonoids, and bitter acids. The medicinal preparations of Humulus Lupulus that can cause this side effect if misused include tinctures, extracts, and teas made from the leaves, flowers, or cones of the plant.

People who are more likely to experience skin rash from Humulus Lupulus include those who are allergic or sensitive to members of the Cannabaceae family, which includes hops, as well as those with compromised immune systems. To avoid this side effect, individuals should take several precautions when using Humulus Lupulus, including performing a patch test before using any new preparations, starting with a low dose and gradually increasing as tolerated, and avoiding any preparations that cause skin irritation or other adverse reactions.

Additionally, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using Humulus Lupulus for medicinal purposes and to follow their dosage recommendations carefully.

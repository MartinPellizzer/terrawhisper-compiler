What to know before using Nicotiana tabacum (Tobacco)?

Nicotiana tabacum, commonly known as tobacco plant, is an important species in the Solanaceae family with a long history of use in both medicinal and horticultural contexts.

Medicinally, it contains nicotine and other alkaloids that have been used in the treatment of various conditions such as gastrointestinal disorders, skin irritations, and as a pain reliever in some traditional medicinal systems. However, it is important to note that nicotine is highly addictive and toxic in large doses. In terms of horticultural conditions, Nicotiana tabacum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it is typically grown as an annual in temperate climates.

The plant itself is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit, with large, lobed leaves and clusters of white or pink tubular flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators.

What to know before using Aralia racemosa (American Spikenard)?

Aralia racemosa, also known as American spikenard, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It has a variety of medicinal properties, including being used as a stimulant, diuretic, and expectorant, and for treating conditions such as rheumatism, skin diseases, and bronchial issues. In terms of horticultural conditions, Aralia racemosa prefers rich, moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can grow up to 2-6 feet tall with a spread of 1-3 feet.

Botanically, it is characterized by its large, compound leaves and tall spikes of white flowers that bloom in the late summer, followed by clusters of purple-black berries.

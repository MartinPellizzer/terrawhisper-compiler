10 Best Active Constituents of Silybum marianum (Milk Thistle)

Silybum marianum, also known as milk thistle, contains several active constituents that have been studied for their potential medicinal benefits.

The most well-known of these constituents is silymarin, a flavonoid compound that is believed to be responsible for the majority of milk thistle's therapeutic effects. Silymarin is actually a complex mixture of several related compounds, including silybin, isosilybin, silychristin, and silydianin, which are thought to work together to provide a range of health benefits. These benefits include antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and liver-protective effects, and as a result, milk thistle has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat a variety of liver-related conditions, such as hepatitis, cirrhosis, and liver toxicity.

Additionally, some research suggests that milk thistle may also have potential benefits for other health conditions, such as diabetes, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders, although more research is needed to confirm these effects.

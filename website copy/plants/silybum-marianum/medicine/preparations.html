Silybum marianum, also known as milk thistle, has been used in medicinal preparations for centuries due to its potential health benefits.

Two common forms of milk thistle preparations are tisanes and tinctures. A tisane is an herbal infusion made by steeping the seeds or leaves of the milk thistle plant in hot water.

This soothing drink is believed to aid in digestion, reduce inflammation, and support liver function. On the other hand, a tincture is a more concentrated extract, created by soaking the plant material in alcohol or glycerin. Tinctures can be taken in smaller doses and are often used for their potential to protect the liver from toxins, support healthy cholesterol levels, and even provide antioxidant and anti-cancer effects.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement regimen, as milk thistle may interact with certain medications or medical conditions.

10 Best Health Benefits of Silybum marianum (Milk Thistle)

Silybum marianum, commonly known as milk thistle, has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, particularly for the liver.

The active compound in milk thistle, silymarin, is believed to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Some studies suggest that milk thistle may help protect the liver from damage and even promote liver cell regeneration. Additionally, milk thistle may help lower cholesterol levels, reduce insulin resistance, and alleviate symptoms of digestive disorders such as irritable bowel syndrome.

However, more research is needed to fully understand the medicinal benefits and potential risks of milk thistle.

1. Improves liver function

Milk Thistle improves liver function, meaning it can help protect the liver from damage and even regenerate damaged liver cells.

The main active constituents of Silybum marianum that provide this benefit are a group of compounds called silymarin, which is found in the seeds of the plant. The primary parts of the Silybum marianum plant that give this benefit are the seeds, which have the highest concentration of silymarin. The main medicinal preparations of Silybum marianum that give this benefit include extracts of the seeds in the form of capsules or tinctures.

This benefit of Milk Thistle helps relieve a variety of health conditions related to the liver, such as hepatitis, cirrhosis, and fatty liver disease.

Silybum marianum improves liver function thanks to the active constituents listed below.

  • Silymarin
  • Silybin
  • Silychristin
  • Silydianin
  • Isosilybin
  • Taxifolin
  • Quercetin
  • Kaempferol
  • Dihydroquercetin
  • Apigenin

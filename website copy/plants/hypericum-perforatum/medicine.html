Hypericum perforatum (St. John’S Wort) Medicinal Guide

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St.

John's Wort, has been used in traditional medicine for its potential mood-enhancing and anxiety-reducing benefits. The main constituents of St. John's Wort include hypericin, hyperforin, and flavonoids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties. Preparations of St.

John's Wort can be found in various forms such as teas, capsules, and tinctures. However, it is important to note that St. John's Wort can cause several side effects, including gastrointestinal symptoms, skin sensitivity to sunlight, and potential interactions with other medications. Additionally, pregnant women, individuals with certain medical conditions, and those taking certain medications should exercise caution and consult with a healthcare provider before using St.

John's Wort.

What are the precautions to take when using Hypericum perforatum (St. John’S Wort) medicinally?

Before using Hypericum perforatum, also known as St.

John's Wort, for medicinal purposes, it's important to take some precautions such as consulting a healthcare provider before use, as it can interact with certain medications and medical conditions. It's also important to monitor medication effectiveness and discontinue use before surgery.

Additionally, individuals should avoid excessive sun exposure, check for allergic reactions, limit use to 12 weeks, reduce dosage for elderly, monitor liver function, and avoid use during pregnancy and breastfeeding. Furthermore, Hypericum perforatum should not be used with MAOIs, certain heart medications, anxiety medications, and HIV medications. Individuals should also avoid alcohol consumption, discontinue use if experiencing side effects, limit exposure to sunlight while using, and follow dosage instructions carefully.

Lastly, it is recommended to consult a healthcare provider before use with other supplements.

What to know before using Trifolium repens (Dutch Clover)?

Trifolium repens, commonly known as white clover, is a perennial plant with several medicinal properties.

It contains flavonoids, saponins, and other compounds with anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, making it useful in traditional medicine for treating wounds, skin irritations, and respiratory infections. In horticulture, Trifolium repens thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for lawns, pastures, and wildflower meadows.

Botanically, white clover has a characteristic three-leaflet leaf structure, small white flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters, and a deep-rooted, creeping stem that can spread rapidly, making it an effective ground cover.

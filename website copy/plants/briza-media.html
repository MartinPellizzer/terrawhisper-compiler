What to know before using Briza media (Perennial Quaking Grass)?

Briza media, also known as quaking grass, is a cool-season annual or short-lived perennial plant with various medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its diuretic, expectorant, and anti-inflammatory effects. Briza media thrives in well-drained, moist soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure. Its botanical characteristics include narrow, linear leaves and unique, round, and loose, nodding flower heads that give the plant its quaking appearance.

These flowers bloom from late spring to early summer and are attractive to bees and other pollinators.

When it comes to planting Briza media, fall is the best time.

Caring for Briza media is relatively easy.

What are the harvesting tips of Briza media?

Harvesting Briza media is all about timing.

The best time to harvest is when the seed heads start to turn golden brown but before they disperse. This is usually in late summer or early fall.

Cut the seed heads using pruning shears, leaving some stem attached. Hang them upside down in a dry, well-ventilated area away from direct sunlight. Once dry, gently rub the seed heads to release the seeds.

Store the seeds in an airtight container in a cool, dark place.

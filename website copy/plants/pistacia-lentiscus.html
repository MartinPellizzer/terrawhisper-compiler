What to know before using Pistacia lentiscus (Mastic Tree)?

Pistacia lentiscus, also known as the mastic tree, is a small, evergreen shrub or tree that thrives in Mediterranean climates, characterized by hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters.

It is well-adapted to poor, rocky soil and can be found growing wild in coastal areas, where it is exposed to salt spray. The tree's resin, known as mastic, has been used for centuries for its medicinal properties, including as a treatment for stomach ailments, as an antimicrobial agent, and as an anti-inflammatory. The tree's small, leathery leaves are arranged in opposite pairs along the stem, and its inconspicuous flowers give way to small, blue-gray drupes.

Pistacia lentiscus is dioecious, meaning that male and female reproductive structures are found on separate plants.

What are the medicinal uses of Pistacia lentiscus?

Pistacia lentiscus has several medicinal uses, such as alleviating digestive issues, reducing inflammation, and promoting oral health.

Pistacia lentiscus has many active constituents, such as polyphenols, terpenes, and essential oils, which are known for their medicinal properties.

Overall, the medicinal constituents of Pistacia lentiscus make it a valuable plant in traditional medicine and pharmaceutical applications.

What are the medicinal preparations of Pistacia lentiscus?

Pistacia lentiscus has many medicinal preparations, such as resin, gum, and oil.

The gum, derived from the same resin, is used in the production of lozenges and capsules for similar medicinal purposes. The essential oil, extracted from the leaves and twigs of the tree through steam distillation, is used in aromatherapy for its calming and uplifting properties.

These medicinal preparations have been used for centuries in various cultures and continue to be studied for their potential therapeutic benefits.

What are the horticultural conditions of Pistacia lentiscus?

Pistacia lentiscus needs well-drained soil and thrives in sunny to partially shaded locations.

When it comes to planting, it's ideal to plant it during the spring or autumn, ensuring a distance of 4-6 meters from other trees. This tree appreciates regular watering, especially during dry spells, but make sure not to overwater it to avoid root rot.

For optimal growth, feed it with a balanced fertilizer once a year. When it comes to harvesting, the resin can be collected from early summer to early autumn, by making small incisions on the tree's bark and letting the resin droplets fall onto a piece of cloth. As for pests and diseases, the mastic tree is generally resistant, but it can be affected by scale insects, aphids, and fungal diseases.

Regularly checking and promptly treating any issues can help keep your Pistacia lentiscus healthy and thriving.

It's best to plant your tree in the spring or fall, when temperatures are moderate and there is plenty of time for the roots to establish before the heat of summer or the cold of winter.

When planting, dig a hole that is twice as wide as the root ball and just as deep. Remove the tree from its container, gently loosen the roots, and place it in the hole.

Backfill with a mixture of native soil and organic matter, such as compost or well-rotted manure, and water thoroughly.

What to know before using Juncus conglomeratus (Compact Rush)?

Juncus conglomeratus, also known as clustered rush, is a perennial, emergent wetland plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in moist soil, preferring sunny to partially shaded areas. This plant has thick, triangular stems that grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

Historically, Juncus conglomeratus has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties. The whole plant, particularly the roots, has been used to treat a variety of ailments, including arthritis, skin conditions, and kidney disorders.

In horticulture, it's often used for ornamental purposes in bog gardens or as a marginal plant in ponds.

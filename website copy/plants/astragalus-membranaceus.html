What to know before using Astragalus membranaceus (Astragalus)?

Astragalus membranaceus, also known as milkvetch, is a plant with a rich history in traditional Chinese medicine.

It is prized for its medicinal properties, including boosting the immune system, reducing inflammation, and protecting against oxidative stress. This perennial plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. Its botanical characteristics include pinnate leaves, pea-like flowers, and long, slender seed pods.

The root of Astragalus membranaceus is the part of the plant most commonly used in herbal remedies, and it contains several active compounds, such as saponins and flavonoids, that are believed to be responsible for its medicinal benefits.

What to know before using Equisetum arvense (Horsetail)?

Equisetum arvense, also known as field horsetail, is a perennial plant with unique botanical characteristics - it is one of the few living species of the Equisetopsida class, which were the dominant plants during the Mesozoic Era.

This plant has a distinctive appearance with hollow, jointed stems and thin, grass-like leaves that grow in whorls around the nodes. Equisetum arvense thrives in a variety of horticultural conditions, including moist to wet soil, and full sun to partial shade.

Medicinally, Equisetum arvense has been used in traditional medicine for its diuretic, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory properties. It is often used to treat kidney and bladder problems, wounds, and skin conditions.

However, it is important to note that excessive consumption of this plant can be toxic, and it may interact with certain medications, so it should only be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

What to know before using Crataegus oxyacantha (Hawthorn)?

Crataegus oxyacantha, also known as common hawthorn, is a small tree with significant medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat various cardiovascular conditions, such as hypertension, irregular heartbeat, and angina, due to its ability to dilate blood vessels and improve blood flow. Crataegus oxyacantha thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it can tolerate a wide range of soil types, making it a popular choice for horticulturists. Its botanical characteristics include lobed leaves, white flowers in May, and red berries in the fall, which provide visual interest and attract wildlife.

However, it is important to note that hawthorn can be invasive in some areas, so it is essential to check local regulations before planting it.

What are the medicinal uses of Crataegus oxyacantha?

Crataegus oxyacantha has several medicinal uses, such as improving heart health and alleviating symptoms of cardiovascular diseases.

Its constituents include flavonoids, proanthocyanidins, and oligomeric procyanidins, which have antioxidant and cardioprotective properties. Preparations of hawthorn include extracts, teas, and tinctures, which can be used to treat hypertension, angina, and congestive heart failure.

However, side effects such as nausea, dizziness, and headaches may occur in some individuals.

What to know before using Andrographis paniculat (Andrographis)?

Andrographis paniculata, also known as creat or green chireta, is a plant with a long history of use in traditional medicine, particularly in India and Southeast Asia.

Its medicinal properties are largely attributed to the diterpenoid lactones it contains, with the primary compound being andrographolide, which has been found to have anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antioxidant effects. This herbaceous plant thrives in warm and humid environments, typically growing between 30-100 cm tall with simple, opposite, lanceolate leaves, and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in loose spikes.

In horticultural settings, Andrographis paniculata prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and it is often propagated through stem or root cuttings.

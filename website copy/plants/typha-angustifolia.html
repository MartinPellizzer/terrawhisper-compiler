What to know before using Typha angustifolia (Cattail)?

Typha angustifolia, also known as narrowleaf cattail, is a wetland plant with distinctive brown, cylindrical spikes that contain its flowers.

Medicinally, Typha angustifolia has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including diarrhea, dysentery, and bleeding. It contains bioactive compounds such as phenolic acids, flavonoids, and tannins, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. In terms of horticultural conditions, Typha angustifolia prefers full sun and can tolerate a wide range of soil types, including wet and poorly drained ones.

It is a hardy plant that can grow in USDA hardiness zones 3-10. Botanically, Typha angustifolia is a monocotyledonous plant that belongs to the Typhaceae family. It has long, narrow leaves that are sword-shaped and can grow up to 4 feet tall.

The plant's root system consists of rhizomes, which can spread widely and help stabilize wetland areas.

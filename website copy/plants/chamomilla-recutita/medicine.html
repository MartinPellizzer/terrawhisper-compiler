Chamomilla recutita (German Chamomile) Medicinal Guide

Chamomilla recutita, also known as German chamomile, has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and calming properties.

The main constituents of Chamomilla recutita include several volatile oils such as bisabolol, matricin, and chamazulene, as well as flavonoids and coumarins, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal effects. Preparations of Chamomilla recutita include teas, tinctures, capsules, and essential oils, which can be used to treat a variety of ailments including digestive disorders, anxiety, insomnia, and inflammation.

However, Chamomilla recutita can cause side effects such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and vomiting, especially in large doses. Therefore, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using Chamomilla recutita, especially for young children, pregnant or breastfeeding women, and individuals with allergies to plants in the daisy family.

Additionally, Chamomilla recutita may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and sedatives, so it is important to inform healthcare providers of any complementary and alternative medicine use.

What are the medicinal preparations of Chamomilla recutita (German Chamomile)?

Chamomilla Recutita has many medicinal preparations, such as infusion, tincture, fluid extract, and dry extract.

An infusion is made by steeping the flowers in hot water, creating a tea that is often used for digestive issues and as a mild sleep aid. A tincture is a concentrated extract made with alcohol, while a fluid extract is a more potent version of the tincture that has had the alcohol removed.

Dry extract is a powdered form of the herb, which can be encapsulated or made into tablets. Other preparations include compresses, ointments, and liniments, which are used topically for skin irritations, inflammation, and muscle pain. Additionally, chamomile can be found in syrups, liquid extracts, suppositories, gargles, and even in some teas and capsules.

Each preparation has its own unique benefits and uses, making chamomile a versatile herb in the world of medicine.

The following list shows the medicinal preparations of Chamomilla recutita.

  • 1. Infusion: A preparation made by steeping dried chamomile flowers in hot water, commonly used for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
  • 2. Tincture: A concentrated alcohol-based extract of chamomile, often used for digestive issues and as a mild sedative.
  • 3. Fluid extract: A more potent liquid extract of chamomile, prepared by soaking and pressing the flowers, used primarily for medicinal purposes.
  • 4. Dry extract: A concentrated, powdered form of chamomile, created by removing the solvent from a liquid extract, used in capsules or as a standalone supplement.
  • 5. Capsules: Encapsulated dry chamomile extract, providing a standardized dose of its medicinal compounds, used for treating digestive issues and promoting relaxation.
  • 6. Compress: A cloth soaked in chamomile tea or tincture, applied topically to soothe irritated skin, reduce inflammation, or alleviate pain.
  • 7. Ointment: A semi-solid preparation of chamomile extract mixed with a fat or wax base, used for topical application to treat skin conditions, wounds, or inflammation.
  • 8. Liniment: A liquid chamomile preparation, typically combined with a carrier oil or alcohol, used for massaging sore muscles or joints.
  • 9. Oil: A chamomile-infused oil, extracted by soaking the flowers in a carrier oil, used for topical application to soothe skin irritations or promote relaxation.
  • 10. Tea: An infusion of chamomile flowers in hot water, consumed as a beverage for its calming effects, aiding digestion, and promoting sleep.

What are the possible side effects of using Chamomilla recutita (German Chamomile) for medicinal purposes?

Chamomilla Recutita can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin and eye irritation, vomiting, and nausea.

Other potential side effects include dizziness, dry mouth, constipation, and increased appetite. Additionally, some users may experience difficulty breathing, anxiety, insomnia, and headaches. In rare cases, it can also cause increased heart rate and muscle twitching.

As with any herbal remedy, it is important to follow dosage instructions carefully and consult with a healthcare provider before use.

  • 1. Allergic reactions: Can include symptoms such as skin rash, itching, swelling, severe dizziness, or trouble breathing.

  • 3. Eye irritation: Direct contact with Chamomilla recutita may cause eye irritation, including redness, itching, and watering.
  • 4. Vomiting: In some cases, consumption of Chamomilla recutita may cause nausea and vomiting, particularly in large amounts.
  • 5. Nausea: Consumption of Chamomilla recutita may cause stomach discomfort, including feelings of nausea, particularly in large amounts.
  • 6. Dizziness: Some individuals may experience dizziness or lightheadedness after consuming Chamomilla recutita.
  • 7. Dry mouth: Consumption of Chamomilla recutita may cause a decrease in saliva production, leading to dry mouth.
  • 8. Constipation: Long-term use of Chamomilla recutita may cause constipation or difficulty having bowel movements.
  • 9. Increased appetite: Consumption of Chamomilla recutita may stimulate appetite, leading to increased food intake.
  • 10. Difficulty breathing: Allergic reactions to Chamomilla recutita may cause swelling of the throat or tongue, leading to difficulty breathing.

10 possible side effects of Chamomilla recutita (German Chamomile) if used improperly

Chamomilla recutita, also known as German chamomile, is a plant that has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries.

When used improperly, it can cause several health side effects. First, it can lead to allergic reactions, resulting in skin rashes, hives, and swelling of the face, tongue, or throat.

Additionally, it may cause digestive issues such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Prolonged use or high doses can result in uterine contractions, making it unsuitable for pregnant women. Furthermore, it may interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and sedatives, leading to adverse effects.

Lastly, it's worth noting that the steam distilled essential oil of Chamomilla recutita contains the compound bisabolol, which can cause eye irritation and conjunctivitis if used near the eyes.

1. Allergic Reactions

Allergic reactions caused by Chamomilla Recutita refers to the unpleasant and often harmful immune response that occurs when individuals consume or come into contact with this herb.

The main active constituents in Chamomilla recutita that can cause allergic reactions include the terpenoids and flavonoids present in the plant. These constituents can trigger an overreaction of the immune system in sensitive individuals, leading to symptoms such as skin rashes, hives, swelling, and difficulty breathing.

Medicinal preparations of Chamomilla recutita, such as teas, tinctures, and essential oils, can cause allergic reactions if misused or consumed in large quantities. People who are allergic to plants in the Asteraceae family, which includes chamomile, ragweed, and daisies, are more likely to experience allergic reactions to Chamomilla recutita. To avoid allergic reactions to Chamomilla recutita, it is important to start with a small dose and gradually increase the amount over time, while monitoring for any adverse reactions.

Individuals who have a known allergy to chamomile or other plants in the Asteraceae family should avoid using Chamomilla recutita altogether.

2. Skin Irritation

Skin irritation caused by Chamomilla Recutita refers to the discomforting sensation or reaction on the skin characterized by redness, itching, and inflammation after using Chamomile Recutita.

This reaction is primarily triggered by the main active constituents of Chamomile Recutita, including bisabolol, bisabolol oxides A and B, and matricin, which may cause skin irritation when applied topically or ingested in high concentrations (Butje et al., 2011). The medicinal preparations of Chamomile Recutita that can cause this side effect if misused include chamomile tea, chamomile essential oil, and chamomile extracts. People with known allergies to chamomile, ragweed, daisies, or other members of the Asteraceae family are more likely to experience skin irritation after using Chamomilla Recutita. To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to follow the recommended dosage and instructions when using Chamomilla Recutita.

Before applying chamomile topically, perform a patch test on a small area of the skin to ensure no adverse reactions occur. Additionally, seek medical advice from healthcare professionals if experiencing any signs of skin irritation after using Chamomilla Recutita. (Butje, A., Schmidt, M., Schopf, E., & Heinze, E. (2011). Biological activities of matricin and its derivatives.

Molecules, 16(7), 6175–6191.).

3. Eye Irritation

Eye irritation caused by Chamomilla Recutita refers to a condition where the eyes become red, itchy, and inflamed after coming into contact with the herb or its preparations.

The main active constituents in German Chamomile that can cause this side effect are the volatile oils, particularly bisabolol oxide A and bisabolol oxide B, and the flavonoids, including apigenin and luteolin. The medicinal preparations of German Chamomile that can cause eye irritation if misused include chamomile tea, chamomile essential oil, and chamomile extracts.

People who are more likely to experience this side effect include those who have allergies to plants in the daisy family, those who have sensitive skin, and those who use chamomile preparations in high concentrations or for extended periods. To avoid eye irritation, it is essential to use German Chamomile in the recommended dosages, avoid direct contact with the eyes, and discontinue use if any signs of eye irritation occur.

It is also advisable to perform a patch test before using chamomile preparations, especially if you have a history of allergies or sensitive skin.

4. Vomiting

Vomiting caused by Chamomilla Recutita refers to the involuntary forceful expulsion of stomach contents through the mouth.

This side effect can be attributed mainly to the plant's volatile oils, including alpha-bisabolol, alpha-bisabolol oxides A and B, and matricin, which can stimulate the central nervous system and induce nausea and vomiting when present in high concentrations. Improperly prepared or excessive doses of Chamomilla Recutita, particularly in the form of infusions, decoctions, or essential oils, can increase the likelihood of experiencing this side effect. Individuals with a known allergy or hypersensitivity to chamomile or other members of the Asteraceae family, pregnant or breastfeeding women, and those taking medications that lower the seizure threshold are more prone to experiencing vomiting after using Chamomilla Recutita.

To avoid this side effect, it is essential to follow recommended dosages, consult with a healthcare professional before use, perform a patch test before topical application, and discontinue use if any adverse reactions occur.

5. Nausea

Nausea caused by Chamomilla Recutita refers to the feeling of discomfort and unpleasant sensation in the stomach that can sometimes lead to vomiting.

This side effect can be attributed mainly to the active constituents of Chamomilla Recutita, including the terpenoids and flavonoids, which can irritate the stomach lining if used in large quantities or over an extended period. The medicinal preparations that can cause this side effect if misused include chamomile tea, tinctures, capsules, and essential oils.

Individuals who are more likely to experience nausea from Chamomilla Recutita include those with a history of gastrointestinal disorders, pregnant or breastfeeding women, and people who take chamomile preparations in excessive amounts. To avoid nausea caused by Chamomilla Recutita, it is advised to use the herb in moderate doses, preferably under the guidance of a healthcare professional, and avoid taking it on an empty stomach.

Additionally, it is essential to dilute chamomile essential oils before use and avoid using them for prolonged periods.

6. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Chamomilla Recutita refers to a feeling of lightheadedness, unsteadiness, or disorientation that may occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents in Chamomilla Recutita that can cause this side effect include various volatile oils, flavonoids, and sesquiterpene compounds. The medicinal preparations of Chamomilla Recutita that can cause dizziness if misused include dried flowers, tea, essential oil, tinctures, and capsules. Elderly individuals, people with a history of motion sickness or low blood pressure, and those taking sedatives or blood-thinning medications may be more susceptible to experiencing dizziness when using Chamomilla Recutita.

To avoid this side effect, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage instructions carefully, start with a low dose and gradually increase it over time, avoid driving or operating heavy machinery while using chamomile products, and consult a healthcare provider before using Chamomilla Recutita if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking any medications.

7. Dry Mouth

Dry mouth caused by Chamomilla Recutita refers to the decreased production of saliva resulting in a dry, parched feeling in the mouth.

The main active constituents in German chamomile that can cause this side effect are the flavonoids, particularly apigenin and luteolin, as well as various terpenoids. This side effect is most commonly associated with the oral consumption of German chamomile, particularly in the form of tea or tinctures. Individuals who are more prone to experiencing dry mouth as a result of using German chamomile include those who are dehydrated, those with pre-existing dry mouth conditions, and those who consume alcohol or other drying agents.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to drink plenty of water while consuming German chamomile, avoiding alcohol and other drying agents, and consulting with a healthcare provider before using German chamomile if you have a pre-existing dry mouth condition.

8. Constipation

Constipation caused by Chamomilla Recutita refers to the condition of infrequent bowel movements or difficulty in passing stools, which can occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents in Chamomilla recutita that can cause constipation are the flavonoids and volatile oils, which can have a constipating effect on the digestive system. Medicinal preparations such as chamomile tea and chamomile extracts are the most likely to cause constipation if misused or used in large amounts.

Elderly people, individuals with a history of digestive problems, and those who are sensitive to the effects of chamomile are more likely to experience constipation as a side effect. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to use chamomile in moderation, drink plenty of fluids, and avoid using it for extended periods of time.

Additionally, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare professional before using chamomile for medicinal purposes.

9. Increased Appetite

Increased appetite caused by Chamomilla Recutita refers to an increased desire or need for food consumption resulting from the use of this herb.

This side effect can be attributed mainly to the volatile oils and flavonoids found in Chamomilla Recutita, which are believed to stimulate appetite. Medicinal preparations such as chamomile tea, tinctures, and powdered extracts, if taken in excessive quantities, can cause this side effect. Those who are more likely to experience increased appetite as a side effect are individuals who are already predisposed to overeating, have a weak digestive system, or have existing gastrointestinal disorders.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow recommended dosages and consult with a healthcare provider for appropriate usage, especially when using chamomile for extended periods or with other medications.

10. Difficulty Breathing

Difficulty breathing caused by Chamomilla Recutita refers to complications in the respiratory system that may arise from the use of this plant.

The main active constituents in Chamomilla Recutita responsible for this side effect are the volatile oils, including alpha-bisabolol and matricin. The primary medicinal preparations of German Chamomile that could cause difficulty breathing if misused include chamomile tea, tinctures, and essential oils. Individuals with a history of allergic reactions to plants in the daisy family or those with respiratory conditions such as asthma or COPD are more likely to experience this side effect.

To avoid difficulty breathing when using Chamomilla Recutita, it is essential to observe proper dosage, avoid internal use during pregnancy and lactation, and consult a healthcare professional before use if suffering from any pre-existing medical conditions.

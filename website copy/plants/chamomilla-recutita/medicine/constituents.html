10 Best Active Constituents of Chamomilla recutita (German Chamomile)

Chamomilla recutita, also known as German chamomile, is a popular herb used in medicinal practices due to its active constituents.

The two primary active compounds are apigenin and chamazulene, which are known for their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. Apigenin, a flavonoid, has been shown to have a calming effect on the nervous system, making it useful for treating anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness. Chamazulene, an aromatic chemical compound, is responsible for the herb's deep blue color and has been found to reduce inflammation and pain, making it beneficial for skin conditions, muscle spasms, and menstrual cramps.

Additionally, German chamomile contains various other constituents, such as terpenoids and flavonoids, that contribute to its medicinal properties, further enhancing its therapeutic potential.

1. Alpha-Bisabolol

The alpha-bisabolol contained in Chamomilla Recutita is a terpene alcohol known for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic properties.

Its main medicinal properties include soothing skin irritations, reducing pain and inflammation, and promoting wound healing. This constituent has been found to help relieve various health conditions such as eczema, allergies, and skin infections. Alpha-bisabolol is primarily found in the flowers of the Chamomilla recutita plant.

Improper use of this constituent can lead to some side effects such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential interactions with certain medications.

2. Apigenin

The apigenin contained in Chamomilla Recutita is a flavonoid with potent anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anxiolytic properties.

Its main medicinal properties include anti-anxiety, anticonvulsant, antidepressant, and neuroprotective effects. This Chamomilla recutita constituent has been shown to help relieve several health conditions, such as insomnia, menstrual cramps, migraines, and gastrointestinal disorders. The main parts of the Chamomilla recutita plant that contain this constituent are the flowers and leaves.

Some possible side effects of Chamomilla recutita apigenin if used improperly include allergic reactions, drowsiness, and potential interactions with other medications, particularly those metabolized in the liver.

3. Chamazulene

The chamazulene contained in Chamomilla Recutita is a chemical compound that gives the plant its characteristic blue color.

This compound is known for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic properties. It is used to help relieve conditions such as skin irritations, muscle spasms, and inflammation.

Chamazulene is found in the flowers and leaves of the Chamomilla Recutita plant. Some possible side effects of chamazulene if used improperly include skin irritation, allergic reactions, and eye irritation.

It is important to use chamazulene and chamomile products as directed and to discontinue use if any adverse reactions occur.

4. Farnesene

The farnesene contained in Chamomilla Recutita is a sesquiterpene hydrocarbon that contributes to the plant's aroma and medicinal properties.

Its main medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antispasmodic effects, making it beneficial for a variety of health conditions. Chamomilla recutita farnesene can help relieve digestive issues such as indigestion, bloating, and nausea, as well as reduce inflammation and pain in conditions like arthritis and menstrual cramps. This constituent is found mainly in the flowers and leaves of the Chamomilla recutita plant.

Possible side effects of chamomile farnesene, if used improperly or in large amounts, include skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential interactions with certain medications.

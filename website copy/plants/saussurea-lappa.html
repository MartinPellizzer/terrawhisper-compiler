Its roots and rhizomes have been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues, respiratory infections, and skin conditions. The plant prefers well-drained, sandy soil and full sun to partial shade, and it can grow up to 1.5 meters tall with hairy stems and narrow, lobed leaves. Saussurea lappa is a perennial herb that belongs to the Asteraceae family, and it has small, purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The roots have a distinct, warm, and aromatic odor, and they contain several bioactive compounds, such as costunolide and dehydrocostus lactone, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anticancer properties.

What to know before using Geranium maculatum (Wild Geranium)?

Geranium maculatum, commonly known as spotted geranium or wild cranesbill, is a versatile herbaceous perennial native to Europe and Asia.

Its attractive foliage and delicate pink or white flowers bloom from late spring through summer, making it a popular choice for gardens. Geranium maculatum possesses impressive medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and astringent effects that can help soothe skin irritations, heal wounds, and reduce swelling. The plant thrives best in shade or partial sun with well-drained soil and consistent moisture.

Its heart-shaped leaves, veined with distinct grey markings, contrast beautifully against the pink or white blooms, creating an enchanting sight for garden enthusiasts.

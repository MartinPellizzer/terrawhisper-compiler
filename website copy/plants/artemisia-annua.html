What to know before using Artemisia annua (Sweet Annie)?

Artemisia annua, also known as sweet wormwood, is a plant with a rich history and a variety of uses, particularly in medicine.

Its medicinal properties are most notable in the treatment of malaria, as it contains artemisinin, a potent antimalarial compound. In horticultural conditions, Artemisia annua prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often grown as an annual in temperate climates. The plant itself is a shrubby herb with aromatic leaves and small yellow or white flowers.

Botanically, Artemisia annua is part of the daisy family, and it has been used in traditional medicine for centuries across Asia.

What are the planting tips of Artemisia annua?

When planting Artemisia annua, direct seeding is the preferred method.

Sow the seeds directly in the ground in the spring, after the last frost, or in the fall, before the first frost. The seeds should be planted 1/4 to 1/2 inch deep and spaced 12 to 18 inches apart.

They will germinate in 14 to 21 days at a soil temperature of 70 to 75°F. If starting indoors, sow the seeds 6 to 8 weeks before the last frost and transplant them outdoors after the danger of frost has passed.

What are the harvesting tips of Artemisia annua?

Harvesting Artemisia annua is best done in the late summer or early fall, when the plants are in full bloom.

Cut the stems at the base, leaving a few inches of growth. Bundle the stems together and hang them upside down in a cool, dry, well-ventilated area out of direct sunlight.

Allow the plants to dry for 2 to 3 weeks, or until the leaves are crisp and brittle. Once dry, remove the leaves from the stems and store them in airtight containers in a cool, dark place.

The leaves can be crushed and used fresh or dried for later use.

What to know before using Quillaja saponaria (Soap Bark)?

Quillaja saponaria, also known as soapbark tree, is a fascinating species with a range of medicinal properties, horticultural requirements, and botanical characteristics.

This evergreen tree, native to Chile, is known for its saponin-rich bark, which has been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including respiratory conditions, skin problems, and digestive issues. Quillaja saponaria thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and can grow up to 20 meters tall in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves, white flowers with prominent stamens, and woody, scaled bark.

It is worth noting that the saponins extracted from Quillaja saponaria have been used in the pharmaceutical industry for the production of vaccines and drug delivery systems.

Cyanopsis tetragonoloba, also known as grass pea, is a resilient legume well-adapted to diverse horticultural conditions, including drought and poor soil.

Its medicinal properties are extensive, with extracts demonstrating neuroprotective and anti-inflammatory effects, potentially useful in managing conditions like Parkinson's disease and multiple sclerosis. The plant is an annual herb, featuring pinnate leaves and pea-like pods, and can grow up to 1m in height.

It's worth noting, however, that grass pea contains a neurotoxin, necessitating careful preparation to eliminate this risk before consumption or medicinal use.

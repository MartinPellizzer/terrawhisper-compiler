What to know before using Lomatium dissectum (Lomatium)?

Lomatium dissectum, also known as fern-leaved biscuitroot, is a perennial plant native to western North America.

It boasts delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the late spring. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it is drought-tolerant once established. Medicinally, Lomatium dissectum has been used by indigenous peoples for its antiviral and expectorant properties.

The roots of the plant contain compounds that are believed to support the immune system and alleviate respiratory symptoms, making it a valuable herbal remedy for colds and flu.

What to know before using Tussilago farfara (Coltsfoot)?

Tussilago farfara, also known as coltsfoot, is a perennial herb with a variety of medicinal properties.

It contains mucilage, flavonoids, and tannins, which can be used to treat coughs, colds, and respiratory conditions. In horticultural settings, Tussilago farfara prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it can be found growing in temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant's botanical characteristics include yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by broad, green leaves.

Despite its medicinal uses, it's important to note that Tussilago farfara contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be toxic in large amounts, and it's not recommended for internal use without the guidance of a healthcare professional.

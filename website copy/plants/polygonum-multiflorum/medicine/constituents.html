10 Best Active Constituents of Polygonum multiflorum (Fo Ti)

Polygonum multiflorum, also known as Chinese knotweed or He Shou Wu, is a widely used herb in traditional Chinese medicine.

The root of this plant contains several active constituents that contribute to its medicinal properties. The primary active constituents include anthraquinones, stilbenes, and phenolic acids.

Anthraquinones, such as emodin and physcion, have been found to exhibit anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, and laxative effects. Stilbenes, including resveratrol, are known for their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and cardioprotective properties. Phenolic acids, such as caffeic acid and chlorogenic acid, contribute to the herb's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

These active constituents work synergistically to provide various health benefits, such as anti-aging, neuroprotection, and improvement of liver and kidney functions.

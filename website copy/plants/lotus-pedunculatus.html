What to know before using Lotus pedunculatus (Greater Bird'S-Foot Trefoil)?

Lotus pedunculatus, also known as Greater Bird's-foot Trefoil, is a medicinal plant that has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties.

It contains various compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and saponins that are believed to be responsible for its medicinal benefits. When it comes to horticultural conditions, Lotus pedunculatus thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade. It is a perennial plant that grows between 30 to 60 cm tall and has hairy stems and compound leaves.

Its botanical characteristics include yellow flowers with red or brown markings, which bloom in the summer months, and seed pods that resemble a bird's foot, hence the name Greater Bird's-foot Trefoil.

What to know before using Phytolacca americana (Pokeweed)?

Phytolacca americana, also known as pokeweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

Its stems and roots contain medicinal properties, including the ability to stimulate the immune system, reduce pain, and fight against viruses and bacteria. Pokeweed thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-draining soil.

It can grow up to 10 feet tall and has simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped and smooth-edged. The plant also produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into purple-black berries.

However, all parts of the plant, especially the roots and berries, are considered toxic if not prepared properly and should only be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

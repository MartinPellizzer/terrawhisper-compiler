What to know before using Ulmus glabra (Wych Elm)?

Ulmus glabra, also known as the European or wych elm, is a tall, deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It has a broad, spreading crown and rough, grey-brown bark with furrowed ridges. The tree prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, and it is often used as a street or park tree due to its tolerance of urban conditions. The inner bark of Ulmus glabra has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties, and it has been used to treat wounds, skin irritations, and gastrointestinal complaints.

However, it's important to note that the use of elm bark in herbal medicine should be done under the guidance of a trained medical professional.

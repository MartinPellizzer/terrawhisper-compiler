What to know before using Ilex aquifolium (English Holly)?

Ilex aquifolium, also known as European holly, is a species of holly native to Western and Southern Europe.

This evergreen shrub or small tree is known for its glossy, dark green leaves with sharp spines and attractive red berries. In horticulture, Ilex aquifolium prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can tolerate a wide range of soil types and pH levels.

Medicinally, European holly has been used for its astringent, tonic, and stimulant properties. It has been used to treat diarrhea, fever, and inflammation, among other ailments. The leaves and berries contain alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal effects.

However, it's important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic if ingested in large quantities, and caution should be exercised when using it for medicinal purposes.

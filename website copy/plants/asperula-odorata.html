What to know before using Asperula odorata (Sweet Woodruff)?

Asperula odorata, also known as sweet woodruff, is a fragrant perennial plant native to Europe.

It is valued in herbal medicine for its mild anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties, and is often used in teas and tinctures to treat digestive complaints and promote kidney function. In horticulture, Asperula odorata thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, and can be propagated through division or seed. The plant features small, white flowers and delicate, aromatic leaves that release a sweet, honey-like scent when crushed.

Its botanical characteristics include a creeping habit, with stems that can reach up to 30 cm in length, and a deep root system that helps it thrive in a variety of soil types.

What to know before using Arenaria rubra (Sandwort)?

Arenaria rubra, also known as Sandwort, is a low-growing evergreen plant found on sandy and gravelly shores in coastal areas of Europe and North America.

It has fleshy, narrow leaves that help it survive in sandy soils, and produces small, white flowers in the summer. Medicinally, Arenaria rubra has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties, and it has been used to treat a variety of ailments, including rheumatism and kidney diseases.

In horticulture, Arenaria rubra prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often used as a groundcover due to its low-growing habit and ability to tolerate sandy soils.

10 possible side effects of Aloe vera (Aloe Vera) if used improperly

Aloe vera, a popular plant known for its healing properties, can have adverse health effects if used improperly.

Consuming large amounts of aloe vera latex, the sticky yellow substance found under the leaf's skin, can lead to stomach discomfort, cramps, and diarrhea due to its laxative properties. Prolonged use or excessive consumption can even cause electrolyte imbalances, which may result in heart problems and muscle weakness.

Furthermore, skin reactions, including rashes and irritation, can occur from applying aloe vera gel to broken or damaged skin. Pregnant women should avoid aloe vera ingestion, as it may trigger uterine contractions and lead to premature labor. Lastly, aloe vera may interact with certain medications, such as digoxin, diuretics, and laxatives, potentially causing harmful side effects or reducing the medications' effectiveness.

Always consult a healthcare professional before using aloe vera, especially for internal use or if you have a medical condition or are taking medication.

1. Allergic Reaction

Allergic reaction caused by Aloe vera refers to an abnormal response of the immune system to one or more constituents of the plant.

The main active constituents that can cause this side effect are aloin, anthraquinones, and lectins. These components can be found in various medicinal preparations, such as gels, creams, and juices, and can lead to allergic reactions if misused or overused.

Individuals with a history of allergies to plants, especially those in the Liliaceae family, or sensitive skin are more prone to experience this side effect. To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to perform a patch test before using any aloe vera product and to discontinue use if any signs of irritation or allergic reaction occur.

Additionally, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and usage guidelines provided by a healthcare professional or the product manufacturer.

2. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Aloe Vera refers to a Bowel movement that is loose and watery, occurring more frequently and in larger volumes than usual.

This side effect can be attributed to the anthraquinones and aloin, which are the main active constituents of aloe vera. These constituents are primarily found in the latex layer of the aloe vera leaf and have a strong laxative effect, which can lead to diarrhea when misused.

People who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with sensitive digestive systems, pregnant women, and individuals with pre-existing gastrointestinal conditions. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to use aloe vera products as directed and consult with a healthcare professional before use, especially for extended periods or in high doses.

Additionally, it is important to use decolorized whole leaf extract or aloe vera inner fillet to reduce the risk of diarrhea.

3. Skin Irritation

Skin irritation caused by Aloe Vera refers to an unpleasant sensation such as burning, itching, or stinging that occurs on the skin after using aloe vera products.

The main active constituents in aloe vera that can cause this side effect are aloin and anthraquinones. These components can be found in various medicinal preparations of aloe vera, including gels, creams, and juices, particularly when they are not used as directed.

Individuals with sensitive skin or allergies to aloe vera or related plants are more likely to experience skin irritation. To avoid this side effect, it is essential to perform a patch test before using aloe vera products, avoid using them on broken or damaged skin, and discontinue use if irritation occurs.

Additionally, it is crucial to follow the recommended dosage and usage instructions carefully.

4. Low Blood Sugar

Low blood sugar caused by Aloe Vera refers to a condition where the level of glucose in the blood drops below the normal range.

This can occur due to the presence of certain compounds in aloe vera, including anthraquinones and glucomannan. These constituents can lower blood sugar by stimulating the release of insulin from the pancreas, which increases the uptake of glucose by the cells.

This effect is more pronounced in medicinal preparations of aloe vera, such as aloe latex and aloe juice, especially when taken in large amounts or for an extended period. People with diabetes or those who are taking medication to lower blood sugar are at a higher risk of experiencing low blood sugar after using aloe vera. Therefore, it is important to use aloe vera with caution and monitor blood sugar levels regularly.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using aloe vera for medicinal purposes.

5. Latex Allergy

Latex allergy caused by Aloe Vera refers to an adverse reaction that occurs when individuals with a hypersensitivity to latex come into contact with the sap of the aloe vera plant.

The main active constituents in aloe vera that can cause this side effect are aloin and aloe-emodin. These compounds are found in the latex layer just under the skin of the leaf and can trigger an allergic reaction in some people.

The main medicinal preparations of aloe vera that can cause this side effect if misused include aloe vera juice, gels, ointments, and creams. People who are more likely to experience this side effect are those with a known latex allergy, healthcare workers who are frequently exposed to latex gloves, and people with certain underlying medical conditions such as asthma or eczema. To avoid this side effect, individuals should always conduct a patch test before using aloe vera products, especially if they have a history of latex allergy or other allergies.

Additionally, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and instructions for use provided by the manufacturer, and to discontinue use immediately if any adverse reactions occur.

6. Stomach Pain

Stomach pain caused by Aloe vera refers to discomfort or pain in the abdominal area that may occur after ingesting this plant.

The main active constituents of aloe vera that can cause stomach pain are anthraquinones, particularly aloin, which can have a laxative effect and potentially irritate the digestive tract. The main medicinal preparations of aloe vera that can cause stomach pain if misused include aloe latex and aloe juice, which are often used as laxatives and can cause cramping, diarrhea, and abdominal pain if taken in excess.

Individuals who are more likely to experience stomach pain after using aloe vera are those with irritable bowel syndrome, inflammatory bowel disease, hemorrhoids, or other gastrointestinal disorders. To avoid stomach pain, it is important to use aloe vera products as directed and not to exceed the recommended dose.

Additionally, it is advised to consult with a healthcare professional before using aloe vera as a laxative or for any other medicinal purpose.

7. Throat Irritation

Throat irritation caused by Aloe Vera refers to a sore or itchy sensation in the throat that may occur after using this plant or its preparations.

The main active constituents in aloe vera that can cause this side effect are anthraquinones, specifically aloin and barbaloin. These compounds can have a irritant effect on the mucous membranes, leading to throat irritation.

The main medicinal preparations of aloe vera that can cause this side effect if misused include aloe vera juice, gels, and ointments. People who have a sensitivity or allergy to aloe vera, have a history of digestive problems or are taking certain medications may be more likely to experience throat irritation after using aloe vera.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to use aloe vera as directed, avoid consuming it if you have a sensitivity or allergy, and speak with a healthcare provider if you have any concerns.

8. Constipation

Constipation caused by Aloe vera refers to infrequent bowel movements or difficulty in passing stools due to the use of this plant.

The main active constituents of aloe vera that can cause constipation are anthraquinones, particularly aloin, which have a laxative effect. This laxative effect can be particularly strong when aloe vera is consumed in its natural, undiluted state or in the form of concentrated aloe latex.

People who are more likely to experience constipation from aloe vera are those with a history of gastrointestinal problems, dehydration, or electrolyte imbalances. To avoid constipation, it is essential to use aloe vera in moderation, dilute it properly, and avoid consuming it for extended periods.

It is also recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using aloe vera for medicinal purposes, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or taking medications.

9. Dermatitis

Dermatitis caused by Aloe Vera refers to a skin reaction that can occur as a side effect of using this plant.

The main active constituents of aloe vera that can cause dermatitis are aloin and anthraquinones. These components can cause skin irritation, redness, and inflammation, especially when the plant is not properly prepared or if used in high concentrations.

Medicinal preparations of aloe vera that can cause dermatitis if misused include aloe vera gels, creams, and ointments. People with sensitive skin, allergies, or a history of skin reactions are more likely to experience dermatitis from using aloe vera.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosages and application instructions, perform a patch test before using aloe vera on large areas of the skin, and discontinue use if any skin irritation occurs.

10. Heartburn

Heartburn caused by Aloe vera refers to a burning sensation in the chest or stomach, radiating from the lower chest to the mid-center of the abdomen.

This discomfort often occurs after consuming aloe vera due to the presence of certain active constituents, such as anthraquinones (like aloin), which can irritate the gastrointestinal lining and cause a laxative effect. Preparations like aloe vera latex and aloe vera inner leaf gel, when consumed orally in excessive amounts or for prolonged periods, have been associated with heartburn.

Individuals with gastrointestinal sensitivity, digestive disorders, or those who consume aloe vera products in large quantities or over an extended period, are more likely to experience heartburn. To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to follow the recommended dosage and use aloe vera products as directed by a healthcare professional.

Additionally, people with pre-existing gastrointestinal issues should exercise extra caution and consult a healthcare provider before using aloe vera products.

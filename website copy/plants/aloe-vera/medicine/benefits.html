10 Best Health Benefits of Aloe vera (Aloe Vera)

Aloe vera is a succulent plant that has been used for centuries for its medicinal properties.

One of the most well-known benefits of aloe vera is its ability to soothe and heal burns, cuts, and other skin irritations. This is due to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, which help to reduce redness, swelling, and the risk of infection.

Additionally, aloe vera has been shown to boost the immune system, improve digestion, and even reduce blood sugar levels in people with diabetes. The plant's clear gel, which is extracted from the leaves, contains vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that can help to repair damaged skin and promote overall skin health.

Overall, aloe vera is a versatile and natural remedy that can provide relief for a variety of ailments.

1. Soothes Burns and Wounds

Aloe Vera soothes burns and wounds, meaning it can help alleviate the pain, inflammation, and redness that occur when the skin is damaged due to burns or open wounds.

The main active constituents of Aloe vera that give this benefit are water, polysaccharides, glycoproteins, and phenolic compounds. The primary parts of the Aloe vera plant that give this benefit are the gel-like substance found in the inner leaf and the yellowish liquid found just under the plant's outer layer, known as the latex. The main medicinal preparations of Aloe vera that give this benefit include aloe vera juice, gel, ointment, and cream.

The main health conditions that this benefit of Aloe vera helps relieve include first-degree and second-degree burns, sunburn, radiation dermatitis, and wounds that are slow to heal.

Aloe vera soothes burns and wounds thanks to the active constituents listed below.

  • Barbaloin
  • Aloin
  • Aloe-emodin
  • Chrysophanic acid
  • Glucaronides
  • Aloetic acid
  • Campesterol
  • Lupeol
  • β-sitosterol
  • Cholesterol

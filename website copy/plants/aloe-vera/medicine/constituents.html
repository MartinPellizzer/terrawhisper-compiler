10 Best Active Constituents of Aloe vera (Aloe Vera)

Aloe vera, a succulent plant, has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries due to its active constituents.

The two main active components are the gel and latex. The gel, derived from the inner part of the leaf, contains water, vitamins, minerals, enzymes, and polysaccharides.

These components have anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antioxidant properties, making it beneficial for treating wounds, burns, and skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis. The latex, found just under the plant's skin, contains anthraquinones, which have laxative effects and are used to treat constipation.

However, it's important to note that aloe vera can have side effects and interact with certain medications, so it's always best to consult with a healthcare provider before use.

1. Vitamin C

The vitamin C contained in Aloe Vera, also known as ascorbic acid, is a water-soluble nutrient that plays a crucial role in the growth, development, and repair of all body tissues.

This powerful antioxidant helps protect cells from damage caused by free radicals, strengthens the immune system, and aids in the absorption of iron. Aloe vera's vitamin C has anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties, making it beneficial for treating sunburns, minor cuts, and skin irritations.

Additionally, it may help alleviate symptoms of conditions such as cold, flu, and inflammatory skin diseases. The main parts of the Aloe vera plant that contain vitamin C are the leaves and the gel inside the leaves. Improper use of aloe vera's vitamin C may cause side effects such as diarrhea, nausea, and abdominal cramps due to its laxative properties.

Overconsumption may also lead to an increased risk of kidney stones and potential vitamin C toxicity.

2. Vitamin E

The vitamin E contained in Aloe Vera, also known as tocopherol, is a fat-soluble antioxidant that helps protect the body's cells from damage caused by free radicals.

Its main medicinal properties include reducing inflammation, improving circulation, and repairing damaged skin. Aloe vera's vitamin E helps relieve various health conditions such as dry skin, eczema, psoriasis, and sunburn.

This constituent is primarily found in the leaves of the Aloe vera plant. Improper use of aloe vera's vitamin E may result in side effects such as skin irritation, rash, and allergic reactions.

Additionally, excessive intake of vitamin E from aloe vera may lead to bleeding, especially for those taking blood-thinning medications.

3. Resveratrol

The resveratrol contained in Aloe Vera, also known as phytoalexin, is a type of antioxidant that is produced by the plant to protect itself from harm.

Its main medicinal properties include antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer effects. This Aloe vera constituent has been shown to help relieve various health conditions such as heart disease, diabetes, and cancer. Resveratrol is primarily found in the leaves of the Aloe vera plant.

However, if used improperly, this Aloe vera constituent can cause side effects such as skin irritation, digestive problems, and low blood pressure.

4. Salicylic acid

The salicylic acid contained in Aloe Vera, also known as betulinic acid, is a natural compound with medicinal properties.

It has been shown to have anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic effects, making it a popular natural remedy for a variety of ailments. Salicylic acid in Aloe Vera is particularly effective at relieving skin conditions such as acne, eczema, and psoriasis due to its ability to reduce inflammation and redness.

It can also be used to treat minor wounds, burns, and sunburns. Salicylic acid is found primarily in the leaves of the Aloe Vera plant. However, if used improperly or in large amounts, salicylic acid can cause skin irritation, dryness, and peeling.

In some cases, it can also lead to more serious side effects such as stomach ulcers and kidney damage.

5. Methyl salicylate

The methyl salicylate contained in Aloe Vera, also known as wintergreen oil, is a natural organic ester that provides a cooling and soothing sensation.

Its main medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and counter-irritant effects, making it a popular natural remedy for pain relief and inflammation reduction. This Aloe vera constituent has been found to help alleviate various health conditions such as muscle and joint pain, arthritis, and strains or sprains. Methyl salicylate is primarily found in the leaves of the Aloe vera plant, particularly in the gel-like substance found in the inner part of the leaves.

However, if used improperly or in large amounts, methyl salicylate can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and even life-threatening symptoms such as seizures, rapid breathing, and coma.

6. Allantoin

The allantoin contained in Aloe Vera, also known as aloe vera allantoin, is a naturally occurring chemical compound with moisturizing and skin-soothing properties.

Its main medicinal properties include its ability to promote wound healing, stimulate new tissue growth, and act as an anti-inflammatory agent. Aloe vera allantoin can help relieve skin conditions such as burns, sunburns, and other minor wounds, as well as skin irritations and inflammation. This compound is found primarily in the gel-like substance within the inner leaf of the Aloe vera plant.

Improper use of aloe vera allantoin can result in side effects such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and stomach discomfort if ingested.

7. Phenolic compounds

The phenolic compounds contained in Aloe Vera are a type of chemical found in the plant that have potent antioxidant properties.

These compounds are known for their ability to neutralize harmful free radicals in the body, and have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antimicrobial effects. Phenolic compounds in aloe vera can help relieve a variety of health conditions, including skin irritations, digestive issues, and even diabetes.

These compounds are found primarily in the gel-like substance found in the inner leaf of the aloe vera plant. However, if used improperly, aloe vera phenolic compounds can cause side effects such as stomach discomfort, cramps, and diarrhea.

In some cases, they may also lower blood sugar levels, so individuals with diabetes should use aloe vera with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

8. Glycoproteins

The glycoproteins contained in Aloe Vera are compounds that contribute to the healing properties of the plant.

These glycoproteins have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making them effective at reducing pain and inflammation in the body. Aloe vera glycoproteins have been shown to help relieve conditions such as mouth ulcers, digestive issues, and skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis. They can be found in the gel-like substance found in the inner leaf of the aloe vera plant.

Improper use of aloe vera glycoproteins can lead to side effects such as skin irritation, diarrhea, and abdominal cramps.

9. Polysaccharides

The polysaccharides contained in Aloe Vera, also known as aloin or barbaloin, are complex carbohydrates that have been identified as the key active component in Aloe vera.

These polysaccharides have been found to have anti-inflammatory, immunomodulatory, and wound-healing properties. They have been shown to help relieve skin conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, and dermatitis, as well as gastrointestinal issues such as ulcers, inflammatory bowel disease, and constipation.

The polysaccharides are found primarily in the gel-like substance found in the inner leaf of the Aloe vera plant. Improper use of Aloe vera polysaccharides can lead to side effects such as abdominal cramps, diarrhea, and electrolyte imbalances.

Additionally, high concentrations of aloin can be harmful and should be avoided.

10. Tocopherols

The tocopherols contained in Aloe Vera, also known as vitamin E, are a group of fat-soluble compounds with strong antioxidant properties.

They are responsible for the plant's ability to scavenge free radicals, reduce oxidative stress, and support the immune system. Aloe vera tocopherols have been found to help relieve various health conditions, such as skin damage, inflammation, and immune-related disorders.

These beneficial compounds are primarily found in the leaf pulp of the aloe vera plant. However, improper use of aloe vera tocopherols can lead to several side effects, including skin irritation, allergic reactions, and stomach discomfort.

In severe cases, it may also cause liver damage or interfere with blood clotting.

What to know before using Artemisia dracunculus (Tarragon)?

Artemisia dracunculus, also known as tarragon, is a herb with a long history of use in both horticulture and medicine.

In horticulture, it prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often used as a culinary herb due to its unique flavor. As for its medicinal properties, tarragon has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and digestive benefits. Botanically, Artemisia dracunculus is a perennial herb that can grow up to 1 meter tall, with narrow, green leaves and small, yellow or green flowers.

It is native to a wide range of regions, from Europe to Asia, and has been naturalized in many parts of North America.

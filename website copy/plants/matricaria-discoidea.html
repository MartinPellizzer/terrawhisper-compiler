What to know before using Matricaria discoidea (Disc Mayweed)?

Matricaria discoidea, also known as pineapple weed, is a low-growing annual plant in the daisy family.

It is often found in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in gardens and fields. The plant has a distinct aroma, reminiscent of pineapple, which is released when the leaves and flowers are crushed.

Medicinally, Matricaria discoidea has been used for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and carminative properties. It is often made into a tea to treat digestive issues such as indigestion, gas, and stomach cramps.

This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, but can tolerate a wide range of horticultural conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable addition to many landscapes.

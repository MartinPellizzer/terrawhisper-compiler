What to know before using Terminalia chebula (Chebulic Myrobalan)?

Terminalia chebula, also known as black-halbard or myrobalan, is a species of tree that is native to South Asia and is well-known for its medicinal properties.

Its fruit has been used in traditional Ayurvedic and Unani medicine for centuries to treat a variety of ailments, including gastrointestinal disorders, respiratory problems, and skin diseases. The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, requiring well-drained soil and a sunny location.

Terminalia chebula can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a wide, spreading canopy. Its bark is grayish-black and deeply fissured, while its leaves are oblong, shiny, and dark green.

The tree produces small, yellowish-white flowers and round, purple-black fruit that are rich in tannins and other bioactive compounds.

What are the medicinal uses of Terminalia chebula?

Terminalia chebula has several medicinal uses, such as treating constipation, reducing inflammation, and improving skin conditions.

Its constituents include hydrolyzable tannins, anthraquinones, flavonoids, and saponins, which have been shown to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antibacterial properties. Preparations of Terminalia chebula include powder, decoction, and infusion, which can be taken orally or applied topically.

However, it can cause side effects such as stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea, especially if taken in large doses.

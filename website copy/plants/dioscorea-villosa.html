What to know before using Dioscorea villosa (Wild Yam)?

Dioscorea villosa, also known as wild yam, is a native North American vine that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine.

It contains a variety of medicinal compounds, most notably diosgenin, which is used to produce a variety of drugs, including steroids and birth control pills. Dioscorea villosa prefers well-drained, loose soil and partial shade, and can be propagated through tuber division or seeds. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, clusters of small green flowers, and underground tubers.

Despite its medicinal uses, it is important to note that wild yam should not be consumed in its raw form, as it can be toxic if not prepared properly.

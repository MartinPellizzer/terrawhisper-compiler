10 Best Active Constituents of Rheum palmatum (Chinese Rhubarb)

Rheum palmatum, also known as Turkish rhubarb, is a plant that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The root of the plant contains several active constituents that are believed to have medicinal properties. The most well-known of these is anthraquinone, a type of compound that has been shown to have a laxative effect.

Other active constituents found in Rheum palmatum include tannins, which have astringent properties and may help to reduce inflammation, and various terpene compounds, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antibacterial effects. Additionally, the plant contains several phenolic compounds, such as emodin and rhein, which have been shown to have a wide range of medicinal properties, including anticancer, antidiabetic, and neuroprotective effects.

However, it is important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the medicinal properties of these constituents and to determine the most effective and safe ways to use them.

What to know before using Cirsium oleraceum (Cabbage Thistle)?

Cirsium oleraceum, also known as the marsh thistle, is a plant with distinctive botanical characteristics including prickly stems and leaves, and purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

It is native to Europe and Asia, but has been introduced to other parts of the world. Marsh thistle is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in damp, nutrient-poor soils, making it a useful plant for wetland restoration projects.

In addition to its horticultural uses, Cirsium oleraceum has a long history of use in traditional medicine. The plant contains several compounds with potential therapeutic properties, including flavonoids and sesquiterpene lactones, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticancer effects in laboratory studies.

However, more research is needed to confirm these effects in humans and to establish safe and effective dosages.

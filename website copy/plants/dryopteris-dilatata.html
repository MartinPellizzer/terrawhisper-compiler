What to know before using Dryopteris dilatata (Mountain Wood Fern)?

Dryopteris dilatata, also known as the shield fern, is a hardy perennial plant native to Asia, Europe, and North America.

Medicinally, it has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments such as wounds, inflammation, and digestive issues. The plant contains compounds with anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and astringent properties.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Dryopteris dilatata prefers shade to partial shade and moist, well-drained soil. It is a versatile fern that can grow in a range of soil types, from sandy to clay, and can tolerate a variety of pH levels. Botanically, Dryopteris dilatata is characterized by its dark green fronds, which can grow up to 3 feet in length, and its thick, black stipe base.

The plant has a spreading habit and can form large colonies over time, making it a popular choice for groundcover in shady gardens.

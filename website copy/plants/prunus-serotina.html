What to know before using Prunus serotina (Wild Cherry)?

Prunus serotina, also known as black cherry, is a tree native to North America with a straight trunk and upward-pointing branches that form a narrow, dense, oval crown.

It thrives in well-drained, fertile soil and full sun to partial shade, reaching heights of 25-30 meters. The bark is dark brown to black, with horizontal scaly furrows, and the leaves are ovate, lustrous, and serrated.

Medicinally, black cherry has been used for its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antioxidant properties, and its bark and leaves have been used in the treatment of coughs, wounds, and diarrhea.

What to know before using Erythrina variegata (Indian Coral Tree)?

Medicinally, it has been used in various traditional systems of medicine, including Ayurveda and Traditional Chinese Medicine, for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic properties. The tree can grow up to 12 meters tall and has distinctive, bright red flowers that resemble a tiger's claw, hence the common name. It prefers well-drained soil and a sunny location, and can tolerate a wide range of soil types.

The bark, roots, and seeds of Erythrina variegata are used in herbal medicine, and the tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

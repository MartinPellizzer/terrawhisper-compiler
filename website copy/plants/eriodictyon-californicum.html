What to know before using Eriodictyon californicum (Yerba Santa)?

Eriodictyon californicum, also known as yerba santa, is a shrub native to California and parts of Mexico that has been used for medicinal purposes by Native Americans for centuries.

Its medicinal properties include expectorant, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic effects, making it a popular natural remedy for respiratory ailments, fever, and muscle pain. In horticultural conditions, yerba santa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and can grow up to 10 feet tall and wide.

Botanically, Eriodictyon californicum is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a sweet, aromatic scent, as well as clusters of small, white or lavender flowers that bloom in the summer.

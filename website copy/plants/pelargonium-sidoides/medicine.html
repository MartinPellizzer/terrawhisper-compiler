Pelargonium sidoides (Pelargonium) Medicinal Guide

Pelargonium sidoides, also known as South African geranium, has been shown to have various medicinal benefits, including reducing the severity and duration of respiratory tract infections such as the common cold and bronchitis.

The root of the plant contains numerous constituents, including polyphenols, flavonoids, and coumarins, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties. Preparations of Pelargonium sidoides are available in various forms, such as tablets, capsules, and liquid extracts, and are typically standardized to contain a consistent amount of active compounds. While Pelargonium sidoides is generally considered safe, some potential side effects may include gastrointestinal symptoms such as nausea, stomach pain, and diarrhea.

Additionally, precautions should be taken when using Pelargonium sidoides, particularly in individuals with certain health conditions such as heart disease, hormone-sensitive cancers, and immune system disorders, as well as those taking medications that may interact with the plant's constituents.

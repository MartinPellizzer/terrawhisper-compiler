What to know before using Ranunculus bulbosus (Bulbous Buttercup)?

Ranunculus bulbosus, also known as the bulbous buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

This herbaceous perennial is native to Europe and Western Asia, and is characterized by its hairy stems, glossy green leaves, and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. When it comes to horticultural conditions, Ranunculus bulbosus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is often grown as a decorative garden plant due to its showy flowers. In terms of medicinal properties, Ranunculus bulbosus has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and diuretic effects.

However, it's important to note that all parts of the plant contain Ranunculin, which can cause irritation and blistering when handled or ingested, so it should be used with caution.

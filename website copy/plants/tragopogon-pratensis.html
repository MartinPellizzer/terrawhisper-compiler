What to know before using Tragopogon pratensis (Johnny-Go-To-Bed-At-Noon)?

Tragopogon pratensis, also known as yellow salsify or goatsbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe that has since naturalized in many parts of the world.

Medicinally, it has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including fever, kidney issues, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties. In terms of horticulture, Tragopogon pratensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and can grow up to several feet tall with dandelion-like leaves and yellow, daisy-like flowers.

Botanically, it is known for its long, edible taproot, which can be eaten raw or cooked, and its unique method of seed dispersal, in which the flower head closes and carries the seeds away on the wind.

What to know before using Pinguicula vulgaris (Butterwort)?

Pinguicula vulgaris, also known as the common butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to North America and Europe.

It possesses medicinal properties, including the ability to soothe skin irritations and wounds, and has been used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory ailments. This plant thrives in horticultural conditions of partial shade and well-drained soil, and can be grown indoors with the right care. Pinguicula vulgaris is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, tongue-shaped leaves that are coated in sticky mucilage to trap insects.

The plant also produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink and purple, adding to its ornamental value.

What are the medicinal uses of Pinguicula vulgaris?

Pinguicula vulgaris has several medicinal uses, such as treating skin conditions, reducing inflammation, and alleviating respiratory issues.

Its constituents include mucilage, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are believed to have therapeutic properties. Preparations of Pinguicula vulgaris can be made into ointments, teas, and tinctures for topical or internal use.

However, side effects such as stomach upset and skin irritation may occur in some individuals. Therefore, it is crucial to perform a patch test before applying it to large areas of the skin and to consult with a healthcare professional before taking it internally.

Additionally, pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using Pinguicula vulgaris, as its safety during these periods has not been established.

What are the horticultural conditions of Pinguicula vulgaris?

Pinguicula vulgaris needs a well-drained soil mixture that is equal parts peat moss, perlite, and sand.

It prefers bright, indirect light and cooler temperatures between 60-70°F during the day and a drop to around 50-55°F at night. When planting, use a shallow container and press the soil gently around the roots.

Water sparingly with distilled or reverse osmosis water, keeping the soil moist but not soggy. Avoid overwatering as it can lead to root rot. For harvesting, wait until the plant has produced several leaves and gently remove the matured ones from the central rosette, being careful not to damage the young growth.

Common pests and diseases include whiteflies, mealybugs, and botrytis blight, which can be controlled through regular inspection, removing affected leaves, and using insecticidal soap or fungicides.

What are the harvesting tips of Pinguicula vulgaris?

Pinguicula vulgaris is a monocarpic plant, which means that it will die after flowering.

However, the plant will produce offsets, or plantlets, that can be removed and replanted. To harvest the offsets, wait until they are about 1/3 the size of the mother plant.

Carefully remove the offset from the mother plant using a sharp knife or scissors. Make sure to leave some roots attached to the offset.

After removing the offset, allow it to dry for 24 hours before planting it in a new pot.

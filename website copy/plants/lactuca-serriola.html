This hardy biennial thrives in various horticultural conditions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can adapt to various climates and soil types. Lactuca serriola can grow up to 1.5 meters tall and has distinctive serrated leaves with spiny edges, and it produces small, yellow flowers that give way to clusters of seeds.

The plant's stems are typically streaked with purple, and its roots are long and deep, helping it to thrive in a variety of environments.

What to know before using Solanum dulcamara (Common Nightshade)?

Solanum dulcamara, also known as bittersweet nightshade, is a perennial vine native to Europe and parts of Asia, now widely distributed throughout the United States.

It produces clusters of purple flowers and small, orange-red berries. The plant contains alkaloids with medicinal properties, including solanine and scopolamine, which have been used to treat a variety of ailments such as joint pain, migraines, and asthma.

Solanum dulcamara thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. It can be invasive in some areas due to its prolific seed production and the ability to spread through root suckers.

Despite its potential benefits, all parts of the plant are considered toxic and should be handled with care.

What to know before using Vaccinium uliginosum (Bog Blueberry)?

Vaccinium uliginosum, also known as bog bilberry or wetland blueberry, is a shrub that thrives in cool, moist environments such as bogs and mountainous regions.

Its medicinal properties are extensive, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial qualities, which have been used in traditional medicine for treating ailments from urinary tract infections to gastrointestinal issues. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that develop into blue-purple berries, which are edible and rich in anthocyanins. Vaccinium uliginosum prefers acidic soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

However, it can be invasive in some areas, so it's essential to monitor its growth and manage it appropriately.

What to know before using Cichorium intybus (Chicory)?

Cichorium intybus, commonly known as chicory, is a hardy perennial herb that has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries.

Its roots and leaves contain medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and diuretic effects. Chicory thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it can tolerate a wide range of temperatures, making it a popular choice for gardeners in many regions. The plant can grow up to 5 feet tall and has blue, lavender, or white flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon.

Botanically, chicory has a taproot that contains inulin, a type of dietary fiber, and its leaves are pinnately lobed with toothed margins.

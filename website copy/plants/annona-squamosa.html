What to know before using Annona squamosa (Custard Apple)?

Annona squamosa, also known as sugar apple or custard apple, is a tropical tree with numerous medicinal properties.

Its unripe fruits and seeds are used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including diarrhea, fever, and inflammation. The tree requires a warm and humid climate with well-drained soil to thrive, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions. Annona squamosa is a small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a spreading canopy and ovate, dark green leaves.

Its flowers are white and fragrant, and its fruit is a sweet, creamy, and aromatic delicacy that is rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

10 Best Active Constituents of Passiflora incarnata (Passionflower)

Passiflora incarnata, also known as passionflower, contains several active constituents that have medicinal properties.

The most notable of these constituents are harman alkaloids, particularly harmine and harmaline, which have been shown to have sedative and anxiolytic effects. Passionflower also contains flavonoids such as quercetin, luteolin, and apigenin, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Additionally, the plant contains various volatile oils, such as passiflora, which have been found to have calming and relaxing effects. The combination of these active constituents in passionflower makes it a popular natural remedy for anxiety, insomnia, and other nervous disorders.

However, it is important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the mechanisms of action and potential side effects of passionflower and its constituents.

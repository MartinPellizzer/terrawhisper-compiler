What to know before using Caltha palustris (Cowflock)?

Caltha palustris, also known as marsh marigold, is a perennial plant native to North America and Europe that thrives in wetlands and moist soil.

It is known for its vibrant yellow flowers and glossy, round leaves that add a pop of color to any garden. Medicinally, Caltha palustris has been used in traditional medicine to treat skin conditions, warts, and rheumatism due to its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. Horticulturally, it prefers partial to full sun and consistently moist soil, making it a great option for bog gardens or pond edges.

It is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic and should be handled with care, wearing gloves and avoiding ingestion or topical application without consulting a healthcare professional.

What are the medicinal uses of Caltha palustris?

Caltha palustris is used to treat a variety of ailments due to its medicinal properties.

What to know before using Eleocharis palustris (Creeping Spike-Rush)?

Eleocharis palustris, also known as common spike-rush, is a versatile plant with a variety of uses.

Medically, it has been used in traditional medicine to treat wounds and skin diseases, and recent studies suggest it may have potential as an anti-inflammatory and antioxidant agent. In horticultural settings, Eleocharis palustris thrives in wet, sunny conditions and can be used in bog gardens, ponds, or as a marginal plant in wetlands. Its botanical characteristics include triangular, solid stems that are topped with brown, spiky flowers, and it can grow up to 3 feet tall.

It also has a fibrous root system that helps with erosion control and water purification.

10 possible side effects of Rhodiola rosea (Rhodiola) if used improperly

Rhodiola rosea, a plant used in traditional medicine, can have negative health consequences if used improperly.

Overuse or misuse of Rhodiola rosea can lead to symptoms such as dry mouth, irritability, insomnia, and excessive salivation. In severe cases, it may cause symptoms consistent with serotonin syndrome, such as agitation, rapid heart rate, and high blood pressure.

Additionally, it may interact with certain medications, including antidepressants, leading to increased risk of serotonin syndrome. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid using Rhodiola rosea due to a lack of safety data.

As with any substance, it's important to consult with a healthcare professional before starting to use Rhodiola rosea to ensure proper use and minimize the risk of adverse effects.

1. Nausea

Nausea caused by Rhodiola Rosea refers to a feeling of stomach discomfort and the urge to vomit that can occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents of Rhodiola Rosea that can cause this side effect are rosavin, salidroside, and other phenolic compounds, which can stimulate the digestive system and cause stomach irritation. The main medicinal preparations of Rhodiola Rosea that can cause this side effect if misused include tinctures, capsules, and teas, particularly if taken in large amounts or for extended periods of time. Individuals who are more likely to experience nausea as a side effect of using Rhodiola Rosea include those who are sensitive to stimulants, have a history of digestive issues, or take high doses of the herb.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of Rhodiola Rosea and gradually increase it over time, to avoid taking it on an empty stomach, and to consult with a healthcare provider before using it, particularly if taking other medications or have a history of health conditions.

2. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Rhodiola Rosea refers to a feeling of lightheadedness, unsteadiness, or vertigo that may occur in some individuals who use this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents of Rhodiola Rosea that can cause this side effect are rosavins and salidrosides, which can have stimulatory effects on the central nervous system. The main medicinal preparations of Rhodiola Rosea that can cause this side effect if misused include dried root extracts, tinctures, and capsules. People who are more likely to experience dizziness from Rhodiola Rosea include those who are sensitive to stimulants, have a history of motion sickness or vertigo, or are taking medications that may interact with Rhodiola Rosea.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of Rhodiola Rosea and gradually increase the dosage over time, avoid using it in conjunction with other stimulants or medications that can cause dizziness, and discontinue use if dizziness or other adverse effects occur.

3. Dry Mouth

Dry mouth caused by Rhodiola Rosea refers to a condition where there is insufficient saliva production in the mouth, leading to discomfort and difficulty in speaking, swallowing, and tasting.

The main active constituents of Rhodiola Rosea that can cause this side effect are salidroside and rosavin, which can have a drying effect on the mucous membranes of the mouth. This side effect is most commonly associated with the use of Rhodiola Rosea in its raw or powdered form, particularly when taken in high doses or over an extended period. Individuals who are sensitive to the effects of Rhodiola Rosea, have a dry mouth condition already, or are dehydrated are more likely to experience dry mouth as a side effect of using this herb.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to drink plenty of water and other hydrating fluids when taking Rhodiola Rosea, starting with a low dose and gradually increasing over time, and avoiding its use in its raw or powdered form.

4. Insomnia

Insomnia caused by Rhodiola Rosea refers to the inability to fall asleep or stay asleep, which may occur as a side effect of using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents of Rhodiola Rosea responsible for this side effect are rosavin, salidroside, and other phenolic compounds, which can stimulate the nervous system and increase alertness. The main medicinal preparations of Rhodiola Rosea that can cause insomnia if misused include tinctures, capsules, and teas, especially when taken in large doses or close to bedtime.

Individuals who are sensitive to stimulants, have a history of sleep disorders, or who consume large amounts of caffeine are more likely to experience insomnia as a side effect of using Rhodiola Rosea. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of Rhodiola Rosea, take it in the morning or early afternoon, and gradually increase the dose over time as needed and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

Additionally, avoiding other stimulants such as caffeine and nicotine, establishing a consistent sleep schedule, and creating a relaxing bedtime routine can help reduce the risk of insomnia.

5. Jitteriness

Jitteriness caused by Rhodiola Rosea refers to a state of feeling anxious, restless, and tremulous after using this herbal supplement.

This side effect can be attributed to the active constituents of rhodiola, which include rosavin, salidroside, and other related comphenols. These compounds can stimulate the nervous system, increasing alertness and energy, but when used in excess or in certain sensitive individuals, they can cause jitteriness.

The main medicinal preparations of rhodiola that can cause this side effect if misused include dried root extracts, tinctures, and capsules. Individuals who are more likely to experience jitteriness from rhodiola use include those who are caffeine-sensitive, have anxiety disorders, or are taking stimulant medications. To avoid jitteriness, it is recommended to start with a low dose of rhodiola and gradually increase the dosage over time, under the guidance of a healthcare provider.

Additionally, avoiding caffeine and other stimulants while taking rhodiola can also help reduce the risk of this side effect.

6. Headache

Headache caused by Rhodiola Rosea refers to pain in the head that can occur as a side effect of using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in Rhodiola Rosea that can cause headaches include rosavins and salidrosides. The medicinal preparations of Rhodiola Rosea that can cause headaches if misused include extracts that are not standardized and have an improper ratio of rosavins to salidrosides. Individuals who are more likely to experience headaches from using Rhodiola Rosea include those who are sensitive to stimulants or who consume large doses of the supplement.

To avoid headaches as a side effect of using Rhodiola Rosea, it is important to start with a low dose and gradually increase over time, follow dosage recommendations, use standardized extracts, and stay well-hydrated.

7. Excessive Saliva Production

Excessive saliva production caused by Rhodiola Rosea refers to an abnormal increase in saliva secretion in the mouth due to the use of this herb.

This side effect is thought to be linked to the plant's active constituents, particularly rosavins and salidroside, which can stimulate the salivary glands and lead to excess saliva production. This side effect is most commonly associated with the use of certain medicinal preparations, including tinctures, teas, and capsules, particularly when taken in large doses or for extended periods of time.

Individuals who are sensitive to stimulant compounds, have a history of salivary gland dysfunction, or are taking other medications that increase saliva production may be more susceptible to this side effect. To reduce the risk of excessive saliva production, it is recommended to start with a low dose of Rhodiola Rosea, gradually increase the dosage over time, and avoid consuming large amounts of fluids while taking the herb.

It is also important to consult with a healthcare provider before using Rhodiola Rosea, especially for those who have pre-existing medical conditions or are taking other medications.

8. Restlessness

Restlessness caused by Rhodiola Rosea refers to a state of feeling unusually or excessively fidgety, anxious, or unable to rest, which may occur as a side effect of using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in Rhodiola Rosea that can cause restlessness include rosavins and salidroside. Medicinal preparations such as standardized extracts, capsules, and tablets that contain high levels of these compounds are more likely to cause restlessness if misused or used in large quantities. Individuals who are sensitive to stimulants, have existing anxiety or sleep disorders, or those who consume large amounts of caffeine are more likely to experience restlessness when using Rhodiola Rosea.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with low doses, follow the recommended dosage on the product label, avoid using it close to bedtime, and consult a healthcare professional for proper usage, especially if you have pre-existing medical conditions or are taking other medications.

Agitation caused by Rhodiola Rosea refers to a state of excessive restlessness, irritability, and anxiety that can occur in some individuals who use this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in Rhodiola Rosea that can cause this side effect are rosavin, salidroside, and other phenolic compounds, which can stimulate the nervous system and lead to agitation in sensitive individuals. People who are more likely to experience agitation from Rhodiola Rosea include those with a history of anxiety or panic attacks, as well as those who are particularly sensitive to stimulant compounds. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of Rhodiola Rosea and gradually increase over time, while also paying attention to the body's response.

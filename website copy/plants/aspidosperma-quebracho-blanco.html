What to know before using Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco (Quebracho)?

Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco, also known as white quebracho, is a tree native to South America that has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.

Its bark and wood are rich in alkaloids, such as quebrachine and aspidospermine, which have anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and vasodilatory properties, making it useful in treating respiratory conditions, fever, and high blood pressure. This tree thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil, growing up to 20 meters tall with a trunk diameter of over 2 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and oblong, while its small, white flowers bloom in clusters.

The tree's bark is thick, rough, and gray, with a distinctive pattern of fissures and scales.

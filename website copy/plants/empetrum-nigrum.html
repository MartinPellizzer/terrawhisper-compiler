What to know before using Empetrum nigrum (Curlew-Berry)?

Empetrum nigrum, also known as black crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It has slender branches with small, dark green leaves and small, black berries that are rich in antioxidants. Medicinally, the berries and leaves of Empetrum nigrum have been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including diarrhea, dysentery, and skin diseases. In horticultural settings, Empetrum nigrum thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and full sun or partial shade.

It is a hardy plant that is tolerant of a wide range of conditions, making it a popular choice for rock gardens, groundcover, and erosion control.

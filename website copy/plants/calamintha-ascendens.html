What to know before using Calamintha ascendens (Calamint)?

Calamintha ascendens, also known as lesser calamint, is a fragrant herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It has a variety of medicinal properties, including being an anti-spasmodic, stomachic, and nerve tonic. This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it can grow up to 30 inches tall. Calamintha ascendens is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of tiny, tubular, lilac-pink flowers that bloom in the summer.

It is often used in teas, as a flavoring for vinegar, and as a garnish.

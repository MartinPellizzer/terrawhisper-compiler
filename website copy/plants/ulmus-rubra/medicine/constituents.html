10 Best Active Constituents of Ulmus rubra (Slippery Elm)

Ulmus rubra, also known as slippery elm, contains several active constituents that have medicinal properties.

The bark of the tree is the primary source of these constituents, which include mucilage, tannins, starch, calcium oxalate, and a variety of nutrients. The mucilage is a complex mixture of polysaccharides that can soothe and protect mucous membranes, making it useful for treating digestive issues such as heartburn, acid reflux, and gastritis.

Tannins, on the other hand, have astringent properties that can help reduce inflammation and promote healing. The antioxidant properties of slippery elm may also provide additional health benefits, such as boosting the immune system and reducing the risk of chronic diseases.

However, it is important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the medicinal properties of Ulmus rubra and its active constituents.

This constituent is found mainly in the bark and leaves of the Ulmus rubra plant. However, when used improperly, gallic acid can cause adverse effects such as nausea, stomach upset, and skin irritation.

What to know before using Berberis aquifolium (Oregon Grape)?

Berberis aquifolium, also known as Oregon-grape, is a shrub native to North America that boasts attractive yellow flowers and blue-purple berries.

Medicinally, it has been used by indigenous peoples for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, and modern research has supported its potential use in treating conditions such as acne, psoriasis, and certain types of infection. In horticultural settings, Berberis aquifolium thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and can reach heights of up to 10 feet.

Botanically, it is characterized by its holly-like leaves, which are green and glossy on top but white and woolly underneath, and its clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the late spring.

What to know before using Crocus sativa (Saffron)?

Crocus sativa, commonly known as saffron, is a species of crocus that is native to Southwest Asia and cultivated in many countries for its distinctive threads.

The dried stigmas of the saffron flower are used as a spice, prized for their vibrant yellow-orange color, unique flavor, and aroma. Medicinally, saffron has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including depression, asthma, and insomnia, due to its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antidepressant properties. Saffron thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and prefers a dry, warm climate.

The plants are low-growing, reaching a height of only 12 inches, and produce purple flowers in the fall, each with three bright red stigmas that are carefully harvested by hand.

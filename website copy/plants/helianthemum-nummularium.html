What to know before using Helianthemum nummularium (Common Rock-Rose)?

Helianthemum nummularium, also known as common rock rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Europe.

It has yellow or orange-red papilionaceous flowers, with five petals and a diameter of up to 2.5 cm. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is drought-tolerant once established. Medicinally, Helianthemum nummularium has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and astringent properties.

Its leaves and flowers are used to make teas and tinctures, and it has been used to treat wounds, skin irritations, and digestive issues.

What to know before using Rubus caesius (European Dewberry)?

Rubus caesius, also known as European dewberry or European blackberry, is a horticulturally adaptable plant that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade to full sun.

Its botanical characteristics include a trailing habit with tangled, thorny shoots, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, giving way to edible, red to black fruit. In addition to its ornamental value, Rubus caesius has medicinal properties such as being rich in antioxidants, which can help protect the body against damage from free radicals.

Furthermore, its fruit and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their astringent, diuretic, and anti-inflammatory effects.

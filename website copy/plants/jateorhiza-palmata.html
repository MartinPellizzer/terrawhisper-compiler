What to know before using Jateorhiza palmata (Calumba)?

Jateorhiza palmata, also known as red-stemmed wild ginger, is a perennial climbing plant native to the forests of southern and eastern Africa.

Its medicinal properties are widely recognized, particularly in traditional African medicine, where it is used to treat various ailments such as digestive issues, fever, and microbial infections. The plant's root contains alkaloids, including jatrorrhizine and palmating, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory, antimalarial, and antidiabetic properties.

Jateorhiza palmata thrives in humid and shady conditions, with well-drained soil. It is a climbing plant that can reach up to 10 meters in height and has characteristic red stems and bright green, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries.

What to know before using Peumus boldus (Boldo)?

Peumus boldus, also known as boldo, is a small tree or shrub native to central Chile's dry slopes and coastal areas.

It has scaly, gray bark and produces clusters of small, white or yellow flowers, followed by green, fleshy berries that turn purple when ripe. The leaves of Peumus boldus are particularly valued for their medicinal properties; they contain high levels of alkaloids, flavonoids, and essential oils, which have been shown to have a range of health benefits. The tree prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soil and full sun, and is tolerant of drought and moderate frost.

When grown in ideal conditions, Peumus boldus can reach heights of up to 20 feet.

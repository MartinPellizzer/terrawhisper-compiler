What to know before using Sanguisorba officinalis (Greater Burnet)?

Sanguisorba officinalis, also known as great burnet, is a herbaceous perennial plant with delicate white flowers and hairy stems that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to their astringent, anti-inflammatory, and hemostatic properties, making it a popular remedy for treating wounds, diarrhea, and inflammatory conditions. The plant's botanical characteristics include a deep taproot, alternate, toothed leaves, and small, five-petaled flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Sanguisorba officinalis is a hardy plant that can tolerate a wide range of temperatures and soil types, making it a versatile addition to any garden.

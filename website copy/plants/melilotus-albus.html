What to know before using Melilotus albus (White Sweet-Clover)?

Melilotus albus, also known as white sweet clover, is a leguminous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It has been used in herbal medicine for its antispasmodic, anti-edemic, and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant contains coumarin derivatives, which have been shown to have blood thinning and anticoagulant effects, making it useful in treating conditions such as phlebitis and thrombosis.

Melilotus albus prefers full sun and well-drained soil, and can tolerate a wide range of pH levels. It is a perennial that grows to be 1-2 meters tall, with compound leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant is also valuable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful rotation crop for improving soil fertility.

What to know before using Plantago major (Common Plantain)?

Plantago major, also known as common plantain, is a widespread herb that boasts a number of medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including skin irritations, respiratory issues, and digestive problems. The plant contains mucilage, tannins, and flavonoids, which contribute to its healing properties.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Plantago major is a hardy perennial that can be found growing in a variety of environments, from lawns and fields to disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a basal rosette of leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly rough to the touch, and a fibrous taproot.

The flowers of Plantago major are small and densely clustered, rising on a narrow spike and blooming from May to October.

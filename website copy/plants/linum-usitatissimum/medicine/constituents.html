10 Best Active Constituents of Linum usitatissimum (Flaxseed)

Linum usitatissimum, commonly known as flaxseed, contains several active constituents that are beneficial for medicinal purposes.

The two most notable constituents are alpha-linolenic acid (ALA), an omega-3 fatty acid, and lignans, a type of plant compound with antioxidant properties. ALA has been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects and may help reduce the risk of heart disease, while lignans can help lower cholesterol levels, reduce menopause symptoms, and even have anti-cancer properties. Additionally, flaxseed contains dietary fiber, which can aid in digestion and help regulate blood sugar levels.

Overall, the active constituents in flaxseed provide a variety of health benefits and make it a valuable medicinal plant.

1. Linamarin

The linamarin contained in Linum Usitatissimum is a cyanogenic glucoside, a type of compound that can release hydrogen cyanide when metabolized.

Its main medicinal properties include anti-nutritional effects, meaning it can inhibit the absorption of certain nutrients, and potential toxicity if consumed in large quantities. This compound is believed to help relieve health conditions such as goiters and other thyroid-related issues by inhibiting the absorption of iodine. Linamarin is found primarily in the seeds of the flax plant.

Some possible side effects of linamarin if used improperly include nausea, vomiting, and even paralysis due to its potential to release hydrogen cyanide.

2. Linumatalbumin

The linumatalbumin contained in Linum Usitatissimum is a globulin storage protein that has gained attention for its potential health benefits.

Its main medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immune-modulatory activities. Linumatalbumin has been shown to help relieve various health conditions such as cardiovascular diseases, cancer, and diabetes. This constituent is found primarily in the seeds of the flaxseed plant.

Some possible side effects of linumatalbumin if used improperly include digestive issues such as bloating, gas, and diarrhea, as well as potential hormonal disruptions due to its phytoestrogen content.

3. Linoleic Acid

The linoleic acid contained in Linum Usitatissimum is an essential polyunsaturated fatty acid that cannot be synthesized by the human body and must be obtained through diet.

Its main medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory, immunomodulatory, and vasodilatory effects. This constituent has been found to help relieve various health conditions such as cardiovascular diseases, high cholesterol levels, and certain types of cancer, including breast, colon, and prostate cancer. Linoleic acid is primarily found in flaxseed oil and to a lesser extent in the seeds themselves.

Improper use of linoleic acid can lead to several side effects, including digestive issues, blood thinning, and potential interactions with certain medications, such as blood pressure and blood thinning drugs.

4. Oleic Acid

The oleic acid contained in Linum Usitatissimum is a type of monounsaturated fat that is commonly found in natural oils.

Its main medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory and cardioprotective effects, which are attributed to its ability to reduce inflammation, lower blood pressure, and improve cholesterol levels. This Linum usitatissimum constituent has been shown to help relieve various health conditions such as heart disease, high blood pressure, and high cholesterol. The main parts of the Linum usitatissimum plant that contain this constituent are the seeds, which are rich in oleic acid.

Some possible side effects of this Linum usitatissimum constituent if used improperly include digestive issues, such as diarrhea and bloating, and potential interactions with blood-thinning medications.

5. Palmitic Acid

The palmitic acid contained in Linum Usitatissimum is a type of saturated fat that is commonly found in a variety of plants and animals.

Its main medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic effects, making it useful in reducing pain, inflammation, and fever. Palmitic acid from flaxseed has been shown to help relieve symptoms of conditions such as eczema, dermatitis, and other skin irritations due to its emollient properties. It is found primarily in the seeds of the flax plant, and can be extracted through processes such as cold-pressing or solvent extraction.

Improper use of palmitic acid can lead to side effects such as digestive discomfort, nausea, and increased risk of heart disease due to its high saturated fat content.

6. Stearic Acid

Its main medicinal properties include being a potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant agent, making it useful for skin conditions and wound healing. This stearic acid constituent has been found to help relieve various health conditions such as dry skin, eczema, and psoriasis. It is mainly found in the seeds of the Linum usitatissimum plant.

Some possible side effects of this Linum usitatissimum constituent if used improperly include skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive issues if ingested.

7. α-Linolenic Acid

The α-linolenic acid contained in Linum Usitatissimum is an essential polyunsaturated fatty acid that cannot be synthesized by the human body and must be obtained through diet.

Its main medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory, neuroprotective, and heart-health benefits. This Linum usitatissimum constituent helps relieve health conditions such as high cholesterol, rheumatoid arthritis, and hypertension. The α-linolenic acid is found primarily in the seeds of the flax plant.

The mucilage contained in Linum Usitatissimum is a gel-like substance that is released when the seeds come into contact with water.

This mucilage has several medicinal properties, including its ability to soothe and protect the mucous membranes, reduce inflammation, and lower cholesterol levels. It can help relieve conditions such as constipation, coughs, and bronchitis, as well as reduce the risk of heart disease and diabetes.

The mucilage is found in the seeds and seed hulls of the flax plant. Possible side effects of flaxseed mucilage, if used improperly, include gas, bloating, and diarrhea.

The secoisolariciresinol contained in Linum Usitatissimum is a type of lignan, a compound found in plants that has antioxidant properties.

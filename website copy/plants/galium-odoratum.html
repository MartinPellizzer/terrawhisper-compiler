What to know before using Galium odoratum (Woodruff)?

Galium odoratum, also known as sweet woodruff, is a fragrant perennial plant native to Europe.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it an ideal groundcover for shady gardens. The plant's square stems and opposite leaves are characteristic of the Galium genus.

Medicinally, sweet woodruff has been used for its anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties. Its leaves and flowers contain coumarin, which gives the plant a sweet vanilla-like scent and has been used in perfumes and traditional May wine.

However, it's important to note that coumarin can be harmful in large quantities, so it should not be ingested in excess.

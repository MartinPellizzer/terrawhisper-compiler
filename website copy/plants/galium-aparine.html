What to know before using Galium aparine (Cleavers)?

Galium aparine, commonly known as cleavers or stickyweed, is a herbaceous plant with small, green leaves and tiny white flowers.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North America and thrives in damp and shady areas. Galium aparine possesses medicinal properties as a diuretic, lymphatic, and anti-inflammatory agent.

Its sticky stems, which cling to clothing and other plants, have earned it the common name "stickyweed." Botanically, Galium aparine belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is identified by its small, bell-shaped flowers, opposite leaves, and tangled, branching stems.

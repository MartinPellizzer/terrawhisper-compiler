What to know before using Thymus serpyllum (Wild Thyme)?

Thymus serpyllum, also known as wild thyme or creeping thyme, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to Europe.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs such as mint, sage, and rosemary. Thymus serpyllum is characterized by small, narrow leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in tiny hairs, and purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is highly valued for its medicinal properties, which include antiseptic, antifungal, and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in treating wounds, respiratory infections, and skin conditions.

Thymus serpyllum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and is drought tolerant, making it a popular choice for rock gardens, groundcover, and herb gardens.

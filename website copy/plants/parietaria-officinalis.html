What to know before using Parietaria officinalis (Pellitory-Of-The-Wall)?

Parietaria officinalis, also known as pellitory-of-the-wall, is a plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and diuretic effects. It is often used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and asthma, and is also believed to be helpful in treating kidney and bladder issues.

Parietaria officinalis prefers well-drained, nutrient-poor soil and can be found growing in sunny or partially shaded locations. It is a perennial plant that can reach up to 60 cm in height, with small, green flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant is covered in tiny hairs that can cause irritation to the skin, so it is important to handle it with care.

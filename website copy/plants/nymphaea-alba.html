What to know before using Nymphaea alba (White Water Lily)?

Nymphaea alba, also known as the European white waterlily, is a floating-leaved aquatic plant native to Europe, Northern Asia, and parts of Africa.

It has a variety of medicinal properties, having been used in traditional medicine to treat ailments ranging from inflammation to anxiety. The plant contains active compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and alkaloids, which are believed to be responsible for its therapeutic effects. Nymphaea alba thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers slow-moving or still bodies of water.

It boasts large, round, and shiny green leaves with purplish-red undersides, and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer.

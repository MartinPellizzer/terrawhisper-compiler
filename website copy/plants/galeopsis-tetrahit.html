What to know before using Galeopsis tetrahit (Common Hemp-Nettle)?

Galeopsis tetrahit, also known as hempnettle or catchweed, is a annual plant that is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has since been introduced to other parts of the world.

Medicinally, Galeopsis tetrahit has been used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, and is believed to be effective in treating a variety of ailments including muscle pain, insect bites, and skin conditions. This plant is relatively easy to grow, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade, and can often be found growing in disturbed areas and along the edges of forests. Galeopsis tetrahit is a member of the Lamiaceae, or mint, family and is easily identified by its square stems and oppositely arranged, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers of Galeopsis tetrahit are typically pink or purple and are arranged in a unique, helmet-shaped cluster at the top of the plant.

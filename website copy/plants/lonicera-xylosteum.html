What to know before using Lonicera xylosteum (European Fly Honeysuckle)?

Lonicera xylosteum, also known as Finnish honeysuckle or fly honeysuckle, is a shrub that exhibits both medicinal and horticultural significance.

Medicinally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties, and to treat ailments such as sore throats and skin conditions. In terms of horticultural conditions, Lonicera xylosteum thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

As for its botanical characteristics, it is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with opposite, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.

What to know before using Urtica dioica (Nettle)?

Urtica dioica, also known as stinging nettle, is a plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

It has been used in herbal medicine for conditions such as allergies, joint pain, and urinary tract infections. Urtica dioica prefers rich, moist soil and partial shade, and it can be found growing wild in many parts of the world. The plant has hollow stems and opposite, serrated leaves with jagged teeth, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

When the plant's delicate hairs are touched, they release chemicals that can cause a stinging or burning sensation on the skin.

What are the medicinal uses of Urtica dioica?

Urtica dioica has several medicinal uses, such as reducing inflammation and alleviating pain.

Its constituents include vitamins, minerals, and various compounds like histamine, serotonin, and acetylcholine. Preparations of Urtica dioica can be made from its aerial parts and root, and are often used in forms such as teas, tinctures, capsules, and topical applications. Potential side effects of Urtica dioica may include skin irritation, upset stomach, and fluid retention.

Precautions should be taken when using Urtica dioica, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, have a medical condition, or are taking medication, as it can interact with certain drugs and affect blood clotting.

What are the possible side effects of Urtica dioica?

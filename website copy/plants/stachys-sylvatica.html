What to know before using Stachys sylvatica (Hedge Woundwort)?

Stachys sylvatica, also known as wood betony, is a herb native to Europe's woodlands and grasslands.

It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to its various therapeutic properties. Wood betony is believed to have anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and sedative effects, and has been used to treat ailments such as headaches, digestive issues, and anxiety.

In horticultural conditions, Stachys sylvatica prefers partially shaded areas and well-drained soil. This herbaceous perennial features hairy, wrinkled, and greenish-gray leaves with clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant can grow up to 80 cm tall and has a spread of around 45 cm.

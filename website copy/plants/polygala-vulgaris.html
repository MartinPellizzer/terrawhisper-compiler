What to know before using Polygala vulgaris (Milkwort)?

Polygala vulgaris, also known as common milkwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pinkish-purple flowers in late spring and early summer. This plant has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential nervous system-calming and expectorant properties. Polygala vulgaris prefers well-drained, moist soil and partial shade, and it can be grown in USDA hardiness zones 5-8.

It is also an attractive addition to rock gardens or naturalized areas due to its mat-forming habit and delicate flowers.

What to know before using Bixa orellana (Annatto)?

Bixa orellana, also known as annatto, is a shrub or small tree that is native to South America and is known for its medicinal properties, horticultural versatility, and unique botanical characteristics.

Medicinally, Bixa orellana has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues, fevers, and skin conditions. It is rich in antioxidants and has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

In terms of horticulture, Bixa orellana is a hardy plant that can thrive in a variety of conditions, including full sun or partial shade, and well-drained soil. It can grow up to 12 feet tall and has bright green, lobed leaves and attractive pink or red flowers.

The plant produces seed capsules that contain small, red, oval-shaped seeds, which are covered in a waxy coating that is used as a natural dye and food coloring.

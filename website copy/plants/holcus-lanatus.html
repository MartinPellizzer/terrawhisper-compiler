What to know before using Holcus lanatus (Yorkshire-Fog)?

Holcus lanatus, also known as velvet grass or soft grass, has a number of medicinal properties: it has been used in traditional medicine to treat wounds, skin irritations, and digestive issues.

This perennial plant thrives in a variety of horticultural conditions, including moist soil and full sun or partial shade, and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. From a botanical perspective, Holcus lanatus is characterized by its soft, delicate leaves, which are covered in tiny hairs that give the plant a velvety texture, and its dense, spreading growth habit.

The plant also produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters at the top of slender stems, which are attractive to bees and other pollinators.

What to know before using Euphrasia officinalis (Eyebright)?

Euphrasia officinalis, also known as eyebright, is a plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

It has been used traditionally to treat eye ailments such as conjunctivitis and sty, as well as respiratory conditions like bronchitis and asthma. This plant is a semi-parasitic herb, which means it grows by attaching to the roots of other plants and absorbing nutrients from them.

It typically thrives in horticultural conditions that include full sun or partial shade and well-drained soil. Euphrasia officinalis is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are usually white or pink with purple veins, and its opposite, narrow leaves.

It is important to note that while eyebright has a long history of medicinal use, its effectiveness for many conditions is not supported by scientific evidence and it should not be used as a substitute for professional medical advice.

What are the medicinal uses of Euphrasia officinalis?

Euphrasia officinalis has several medicinal uses, such as treating eye diseases, allergies, and respiratory conditions.

Preparations of Euphrasia officinalis include teas, extracts, and ointments, which can be used topically or internally, depending on the condition being treated.

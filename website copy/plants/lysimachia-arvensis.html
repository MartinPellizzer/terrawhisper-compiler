What to know before using Lysimachia arvensis ()?

Lysimachia arvensis, also known as garden loosestrife, is a plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

It contains tannins, resin, and alkaloids, which have astringent, expectorant, and diuretic effects. This plant is often used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory ailments, skin conditions, and kidney disorders.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Lysimachia arvensis prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil. It grows well in USDA hardiness zones 4-8 and can reach heights of up to 3 feet. As for its botanical characteristics, Lysimachia arvensis is a herbaceous perennial with bright green, lance-shaped leaves and yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer.

It is a member of the primrose family and is native to Europe and Asia.

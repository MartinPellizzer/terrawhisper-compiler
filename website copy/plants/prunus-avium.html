What to know before using Prunus avium (Sweet Cherry)?

Prunus avium, also known as sweet cherry, has a long history of medicinal use, particularly in traditional medicine.

Its bark and leaves have been used to treat various ailments such as coughs, wounds, and diarrhea. The plant contains significant amounts of antioxidants, which are believed to provide various health benefits.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Prunus avium thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a lifespan of around 60 years. Botanically, Prunus avium has simple, ovate leaves that are around 5-10 cm long and have serrated margins.

Its flowers are white and appear in early spring, while its fruit, the sweet cherry, is a globose drupe that is dark red to purple when ripe.

Among the saponins, saikosaponins are the most well-studied, and have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, immunomodulatory, and hepatoprotective effects.

Flavonoids in B. chinense, such as quercetin and luteolin, have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The essential oils found in the plant also have various biological activities, including antibacterial, antifungal, and antiviral effects. Overall, the active constituents of B.

chinense contribute to its wide range of medicinal uses, including treatment of respiratory infections, liver diseases, and skin disorders.

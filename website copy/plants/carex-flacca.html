What to know before using Carex flacca (Blue-Green Sedge)?

Carex flacca, also known as the Downy Sedge, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Medicinally, it has been traditionally used as a diuretic and to treat various kidney and bladder ailments. It is a low-growing sedge with narrow, dark green leaves that turn yellow-green in the sun.

It prefers well-drained soil and can tolerate a variety of light conditions, from full sun to partial shade. The plants produce inconspicuous flowers, known as spikelets, in the spring.

Carex flacca is also known for its ability to form dense mats, making it a popular choice for groundcover in gardens and landscaping.

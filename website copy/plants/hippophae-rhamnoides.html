What to know before using Hippophae rhamnoides (Sea Buckthorn)?

Hippophae rhamnoides, also known as sea buckthorn, is a shrub that boasts impressive medicinal properties, horticultural versatility, and unique botanical characteristics.

Its berries and leaves are packed with antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals, making it a popular choice for treating various ailments such as skin issues, cardiovascular diseases, and gastrointestinal disorders. Sea buckthorn thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it is drought-tolerant and adaptable to various soil types. Its botanical features include narrow, silvery leaves, small yellow flowers, and orange-red berries that appear in clusters.

Despite its shrubby appearance, Hippophae rhamnoides is a hardy plant that can withstand harsh climates and is often used for soil erosion control and land reclamation.

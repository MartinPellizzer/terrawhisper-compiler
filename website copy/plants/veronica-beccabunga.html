What to know before using Veronica beccabunga (European Brooklime)?

Veronica beccabunga, also known as brooklime, is a medicinal plant native to wet or damp habitats in Europe and Asia.

Its medicinal properties include being used as a diuretic, an expectorant, and a remedy for skin conditions. Brooklime thrives in moist soil and can often be found growing near streams or in bog gardens. As a member of the plantain family, it has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, blue flowers that bloom in the summer.

When grown in garden conditions, Veronica beccabunga can be propagated through division or seed, and it prefers partial shade to full sun.

What to know before using Ferula gummosa (Galbanum)?

Ferula gummosa, also known as galbanum, is a plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran and neighboring countries.

It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to its anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and antispasmodic properties, and is often used to treat respiratory ailments, rheumatism, and skin conditions. Ferula gummosa thrives in well-drained, rocky soil and full sun, and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall.

Its botanical characteristics include yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, and a thick, resinous sap that exudes from the roots when cut, which is used in medicinal preparations.

What to know before using Convallaria majalis (Lily Of The Valley)?

Convallaria majalis, also known as Lily of the Valley, is a plant native to cold and temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Its medicinal properties include the ability to treat heart conditions, as it contains cardiac glycosides that help regulate heartbeat and strengthen contractions. In terms of horticultural conditions, Convallaria majalis thrives in well-drained, humus-rich soil and partial shade, and it can spread vigorously through rhizomes.

Botanically, it is characterized by its small, bell-shaped, white flowers that bloom in spring, and its broad, glossy, green leaves that emerge after flowering.

What are the medicinal uses of Convallaria majalis?

Convallaria majalis has several medicinal uses, such as a treatment for heart conditions due to its cardio-active glycosides that help regulate heartbeat and strengthen contractions.

The main constituents include cardiac glycosides, such as convallatoxin, convalloside, and others, which have positive inotropic and negative chronotropic effects on the heart. Preparations of Convallaria majalis include tinctures, fluid extracts, and dried plant material for tea, often used in traditional medicine to treat heart ailments, although its use as a heart medication has largely been replaced by more modern drugs. However, it's essential to be aware of the potential side effects, which can include digestive issues, headaches, and visual disturbances, especially if taken in large amounts or combined with other medications.

Precautions are necessary when using Convallaria majalis, as it can be toxic in high doses and interact with various medications, including digoxin and other cardiac drugs, making it crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before use.

Convallaria majalis has many active constituents, such as cardiac glycosides, saponins, tannins, and flavonoids.

Saponins, such as convallamaroside and convalloside, have been shown to possess expectorant, diuretic, and potential anticancer properties. When it comes to planting Convallaria majalis, timing is crucial.

The best time to plant is in the early spring or fall. This gives the plant time to establish itself before the heat of summer or the cold of winter.

In the first year or two, it's essential to keep the soil consistently moist to promote root growth.

What are the harvesting tips of Convallaria majalis?

Harvesting Convallaria majalis can be done for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.

Once dry, store the leaves in an airtight container away from direct sunlight.

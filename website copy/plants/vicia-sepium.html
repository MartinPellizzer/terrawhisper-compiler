What to know before using Vicia sepium (Hedge Vetch)?

Vicia sepium, also known as bush vetch, is a leguminous plant with valuable medicinal properties.

Its seeds and pods contain chemical compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects, making it useful in traditional medicine for treating conditions like arthritis and kidney ailments. It thrives in well-drained soil and partially shaded areas, with a preference for nitrogen-rich environments. As for its botanical characteristics, bush vetch is a perennial plant that grows in a climbing or trailing habit, displaying pinkish-purple pea-like flowers and compound, leafy stems.

It plays a crucial role in the ecosystem as a nitrogen fixer, enriching the soil and benefiting surrounding plants.

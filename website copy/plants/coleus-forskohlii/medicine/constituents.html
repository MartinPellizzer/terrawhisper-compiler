10 Best Active Constituents of Coleus forskohlii (Coleus)

Coleus forskohlii, a plant in the mint family, has long been used in traditional medicine for various health conditions.

The active constituent of Coleus forskohlii is forskolin, a labdane diterpene. Forskolin has been found to possess several medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticancer effects.

It works by activating the enzyme adenylate cyclase, which increases the levels of cyclic AMP (cAMP) in cells, thereby mediating several cellular responses. Forskolin has been studied for its potential therapeutic benefits in various conditions such as asthma, glaucoma, cardiovascular diseases, and obesity.

However, more research is needed to establish its safety and efficacy before it can be widely recommended for medicinal use.

1. Forskolin

The forskolin contained in Coleus Forskohlii is a labdane diterpene that is the primary active compound in the plant.

Its main medicinal properties include dilating the blood vessels, increasing the heart's stroke volume, and increasing the production of a key compound in the body called cyclic AMP (cyclic adenosine monophosphate). Forskolin has been shown to help relieve asthma, glaucoma, and various heart conditions. The main parts of the Coleus forskohlii plant that contain this constituent are the roots and the leaves.

Some possible side effects of forskolin if used improperly include low blood pressure, increased heart rate, and flushing.

Its main medicinal properties include reducing inflammation, preventing oxidative stress, and inhibiting the growth of certain bacteria and fungi. This Coleus forskohlii constituent has been shown to help relieve health conditions such as allergies, asthma, and osteoarthritis by reducing inflammation and preventing damage caused by free radicals.

Rosmarinic acid is found primarily in the leaves of the Coleus forskohlii plant. Some possible side effects of rosmarinic acid if used improperly include gastrointestinal disturbances such as nausea, diarrhea, and upset stomach.

Additionally, high doses of rosmarinic acid may cause liver toxicity, so it is important to use this compound under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

10 possible side effects of Coleus forskohlii (Coleus) if used improperly

Coleus forskohlii, a herb from the mint family, is often used in dietary supplements for weight loss and other health purposes.

However, when used improperly, this herb can cause several adverse health effects. High doses of Coleus forskohlii can lead to low blood pressure, increased heart rate, and throat irritation.

Additionally, it may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and drugs used to treat high blood pressure, leading to dangerous side effects. Pregnant and nursing women, as well as individuals with chronic conditions like ulcers, should avoid using this herb due to the potential risks. Lastly, long-term use or excessive intake of Coleus forskohlii supplements may result in more severe side effects, such as fluctuating blood pressure and cardiac complications.

Always consult a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement regimen to ensure safe and effective use.

1. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Coleus Forskohlii refers to a gastrointestinal side effect characterized by loose, watery stools occurring more than three times a day.

This side effect is primarily caused by the diterpene compound, forskolin, which is the main active constituent in coleus. The most common medicinal preparations that can cause diarrhea if misused include coleus supplements, extracts, and tinctures.

Those more likely to experience diarrhea from coleus use are individuals with sensitive digestive systems, those who consume high doses, and those who take it for an extended period. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase, consult with a healthcare provider before use, and discontinue use if diarrhea occurs.

Additionally, staying hydrated and maintaining a balanced diet can help manage this potential side effect.

2. Nausea

Nausea caused by Coleus Forskohlii refers to the feeling of discomfort and stomach distress that can result from using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents of coleus forskohlii that can cause nausea include the compound forskolin, as well as various other terpenoids and alkaloids. This side effect is most commonly associated with oral preparations of coleus forskohlii, such as capsules or tablets, particularly when taken in large doses or when misused. People who are more likely to experience nausea from coleus forskohlii include those who are sensitive to its active constituents, as well as those who take the herb on an empty stomach or in combination with certain medications.

To avoid nausea when using coleus forskohlii, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and usage guidelines, start with a low dose and gradually increase, take the herb with food, and avoid combining it with other substances that may increase its potency or irritating effects.

3. Vomiting

Vomiting caused by Coleus Forskohlii refers to the unpleasant symptom of forcefully expelling the contents of one's stomach through the mouth.

This side effect is primarily caused by the diterpene compound forskolin, which is the main active constituent of coleus. The medicial preparations of coleus that can cause vomiting as a side effect if misused include supplements, teas, and tinctures. Individuals who are more likely to experience vomiting as a side effect of coleus use are those who take high doses, those with pre-existing gastrointestinal issues, and those who take coleus in conjunction with other medications or supplements.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of coleus and gradually increase, avoid taking coleus on an empty stomach, and consult with a healthcare provider before taking coleus if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking other medications or supplements.

4. Stomach Pain

Stomach pain caused by Coleus Forskohlii refers to a discomfort or painful feeling in the abdominal area that may occur after using this herb.

This side effect can be attributed to the main active constituents of coleus, including forskolin and rosmarinic acid, which can affect the gastrointestinal system and cause stomach pain. Medicinal preparations of coleus, such as supplements and extracts, can cause this side effect if misused or taken in large amounts. Those who are more likely to experience stomach pain from coleus use include individuals with a history of gastrointestinal issues, such as irritable bowel syndrome or inflammatory bowel disease.

To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider before using coleus, especially for those with pre-existing gastrointestinal conditions.

5. Increased Heart Rate

Increased heart rate caused by Coleus Forskohlii refers to an elevation in the number of heartbeats per minute, which is a possible side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents in Coleus Forskohlii that can cause this side effect are the diterpenoids, particularly forskolin. This compound works by increasing the levels of cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP) in the body, which in turn stimulates the heart to beat faster.

The main medicinal preparations of Coleus Forskohlii that can cause this side effect if misused include its roots and leaves, which are used to make teas, capsules, and powder extracts. People who are more likely to experience increased heart rate after using Coleus Forskohlii include those who have pre-existing heart conditions, such as hypertension, irregular heartbeat, or those who are taking medications for heart conditions. To avoid this side effect, it is essential to use Coleus Forskohlii under the guidance of a healthcare professional and follow the recommended dosages.

It is also advisable to monitor your heart rate regularly and consult a healthcare provider if you experience any symptoms of an elevated heart rate, such as chest pain or shortness of breath.

6. Low Blood Pressure

Low blood pressure caused by Coleus Forskohlii refers to an abnormally low level of blood pressure in the body that can occur as a side effect of using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in coleus that can cause this side effect are the diterpenes, particularly forskolin, which can dilate blood vessels and decrease blood pressure. The main medicinal preparations of coleus that can cause this side effect if misused include its roots, leaves, and extracts, which are often used in supplements, teas, and tinctures. People who are more likely to experience low blood pressure from coleus include those who are already taking medication for hypertension, those with low blood pressure, and those who use coleus in large amounts or for extended periods.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to use coleus at the recommended dosage, to consult with a healthcare provider before using it if you have a medical condition or are taking medication, and to be aware of and monitor for symptoms of low blood pressure such as dizziness, lightheadedness, and fainting.

7. Upper Respiratory Infection

Upper respiratory infection caused by Coleus Forskohlii refers to an infection in the upper respiratory tract, including the nose, throat, and sinuses, which may result from the use of this herb.

The main active constituents in Coleus forskohlii that can cause this side effect are alkaloids and diterpenoids, which have been found to stimulate the immune system and potentially lead to inflammation in the upper respiratory tract. This side effect is most commonly associated with the misuse of Coleus forskohlii in the form of dietary supplements and tinctures. People who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with a weakened immune system, asthma, or other respiratory conditions.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider before using Coleus forskohlii, especially if you have a history of respiratory issues or are taking other medications.

8. Cough

Cough caused by Coleus Forskohlii refers to the occurrence of a persistent hacking cough as a possible side effect of using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents of coleus forskohlii that can cause this side effect are the diterpenoids, most notably forskolin. The root of the coleus forskohlii plant is used to produce various medicinal preparations, including tablets, capsules, and tinctures.

Individuals who have a history of respiratory conditions or are taking medication for other health conditions may be more susceptible to experiencing this side effect. To avoid the risk of developing a cough, it is recommended to start with a low dose of coleus forskohlii and gradually increase the dosage as tolerated, while also staying within the recommended dosage range.

Additionally, consulting with a healthcare provider before using coleus forskohlii or any other supplement is always a good precaution to take.

9. Headache

Headache caused by Coleus Forskohlii refers to a possible side effect that can occur following the use of this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in Coleus Forskohlii that have been associated with causing headaches are alkaloids, and in particular, diterpene forskolin. This side effect is most commonly reported when using Coleus forskohlii in its extract form, especially when taken in high doses or for extended periods of time. People who are more likely to experience headaches as a side effect of Coleus forskohlii include those who are sensitive to its active constituents, those who consume large amounts of the supplement, and those who already suffer from chronic headaches or migraines.

To avoid experiencing headaches as a side effect of Coleus Forskohlii, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase over time, follow dosage instructions carefully, stay well-hydrated, and avoid using the supplement for extended periods without taking a break.

10. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Coleus Forskohlii refers to a feeling of lightheadedness, unsteadiness, or disorientation that may occur in some individuals who use this herbal supplement.

The main active constituent in Coleus Forskohlii that can cause this side effect is forskolin, a labdane diterpene that activates the enzyme adenylate cyclase, which increases the levels of cyclic AMP (cAMP) in cells. This increase in cAMP can have various effects on the body, including lowering blood pressure, which can lead to dizziness.

Medicinal preparations of Coleus Forskohlii that can cause this side effect if misused include extracts, capsules, and teas made from the root or leaves of the plant. People who are more likely to experience dizziness from Coleus Forskohlii include those with low blood pressure, heart conditions, or those who take medication that lowers blood pressure.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosage on the product label, consult with a healthcare provider before using Coleus Forskohlii, and be aware of any potential interactions with other medications or health conditions.

What to know before using Lepidium virginicum (Virginia Peppergrass)?

Lepidium virginicum, also known as Virginia pepperweed or poor man's pepper, is a plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

It has been used traditionally to treat ailments such as coughs, colds, and digestive issues. The plant contains compounds with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may contribute to its medicinal benefits.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Lepidium virginicum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is a hardy plant that can tolerate a range of temperatures and growing conditions. Botanically, Lepidium virginicum is a member of the mustard family and produces small, white flowers.

It is an herbaceous, biennial plant that can grow up to three feet tall and has a taproot system.

10 possible side effects of Rehmannia glutinosa (Rehmannia) if used improperly

Rehmannia glutinosa, a plant commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine, can have harmful health effects if used improperly.

Overuse or incorrect dosages can lead to abdominal pain, diarrhea, and nausea. In severe cases, it may cause allergic reactions, such as rashes or difficulty breathing.

Additionally, this herb can have interactions with certain medications, including blood thinners and immunosuppressants, leading to serious health complications. Pregnant women should avoid Rehmannia glutinosa as it may stimulate uterine contractions and lead to miscarriage.

As with any medication or supplement, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before using Rehmannia glutinosa to ensure safe and effective use.

1. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Rehmannia Glutinosa refers to an unpleasant gastrointestinal side effect characterized by the passing of loose or watery stools frequently.

This reaction can be traced back to the main active constituents of Rehmannia Glutinosa, which include catalpol, rehmaglutin A, and rehmaglutin B. Notably, misuse of certain medicinal preparations such as decoctions, pills, and powders derived from the roots or tubers of this plant can lead to diarrhea. Specifically, individuals who consume excessive amounts of these preparations or those with weak digestive systems or sensitivities to these compounds are at a higher risk of experiencing this side effect.

To avoid diarrhea when using Rehmannia Glutinosa, it is advisable to adhere to recommended dosages, start with small doses and gradually increase, and seek the guidance of a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate preparation and duration of use based on individual health needs.

2. Nausea

Nausea caused by Rehmannia Glutinosa refers to the feeling of discomfort or unease in the stomach that can be accompanied by an uncontrollable urge to vomit.

This side effect can be attributed mainly to the presence of alkaloids, iridoid glycosides, and phenylethanoid glycosides in rehmannia. These active constituents can stimulate the digestive system, leading to stomach discomfort and nausea, especially when taken in large doses or over a long period.

The decoction and powder forms of rehmannia are the most likely preparations to cause nausea if misused or taken inappropriately. Individuals who are more susceptible to this side effect include those with a sensitive stomach or a history of digestive issues. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dosage and gradually increase it over time, under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

Additionally, taking rehmannia with food or after meals can help reduce the likelihood of nausea.

3. Vomiting

Vomiting caused by Rehmannia Glutinosa refers to the forceful expulsion of stomach contents through the mouth.

This side effect can be traced back to the main active constituents of Rehmannia Glutinosa, namely catalpol, rehmannioside A and B, and rehmannic acid. These components, when misused or overdosed, can irritate the stomach lining and cause digestive discomfort, leading to vomiting.

The medicinal preparations of Rehmannia Glutinosa that can cause this side effect if misused include decoctions, pills, and powder. People with a sensitive stomach, a history of digestive issues, or those who consume large amounts of Rehmannia Glutinosa are more likely to experience vomiting.

To avoid this side effect, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare professional for proper usage and combination with other herbs or medications.

4. Abdominal Cramps

Abdominal cramps caused by Rehmannia Glutinosa refers to the painful spasms or contractions in the abdomen.

These cramps are primarily caused by the iridoid glycosides, such as catalpol and acteoside, which are the main active constituents of Rehmannia Glutinosa. The decoction and powder forms of Rehmannia Glutinosa are most likely to cause this side effect if misused or overused.

Individuals with a history of digestive sensitivities, such as irritable bowel syndrome or inflammatory bowel disease, are more likely to experience abdominal cramps after using Rehmannia Glutinosa. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase, and to avoid using Rehmannia Glutinosa on an empty stomach.

Additionally, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using Rehmannia Glutinosa as a treatment.

5. Rash

Rash caused by Rehmannia Glutinosa refers to a skin reaction that can occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents of Rehmannia Glutinosa that can cause this side effect include iridoid glycosides, phenethyl glycosides, and stilbenes. This side effect is most commonly associated with medicinal preparations such as decoctions, pills, and powdered extracts, especially when misused or overused.

Individuals who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with allergies to related plants, those with sensitive skin, and those taking high doses of Rehmannia Glutinosa for extended periods. To avoid this side effect, users should start with a low dose and gradually increase, avoid using for extended periods, and discontinue use if a rash or other skin reactions occur.

It is also recommended to consult a healthcare provider before using Rehmannia Glutinosa, especially if taking other medications or have pre-existing medical conditions.

6. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Rehmannia Glutinosa refers to a sudden sensation of spinning or lightheadedness that can occur after using this herb.

The main active constituents of Rehmannia Glutinosa that can cause this side effect include Catalpol, Verminoside, and Ajugoside. The primary medicinal preparations of Rehmannia Glutinosa that can cause dizziness if misused include Rehmannia Radix, Rehmanniae Radix Praeparata, and Rehmanniae Radix Crudus.

Individuals who are more likely to experience dizziness after using Rehmannia Glutinosa include those with a history of motion sickness, low blood pressure, or those taking other medications that can cause dizziness. To avoid this side effect, it is essential to use Rehmannia Glutinosa under the guidance of a qualified healthcare professional, follow the recommended dosage, and avoid using it in conjunction with other medications that can cause dizziness.

Additionally, staying hydrated, avoiding alcohol, and limiting caffeine intake can help reduce the likelihood of experiencing dizziness after using Rehmannia Glutinosa.

7. Dry Mouth

Dry mouth caused by Rehmannia Glutinosa refers to a condition where there is insufficient saliva production in the mouth, leading to symptoms such as difficulty swallowing, talking, and altered taste.

The main active constituents of Rehmannia Glutinosa that can cause dry mouth are Catalpol, Resveratrol, and Geniposide. Preparations such as Rehmannia root decoctions, powdered Rehmannia, and Rehmannia pills, when misused or overused, can lead to dry mouth.

People who are more likely to experience this side effect are those who consume Rehmannia Glutinosa in high doses for extended periods or those who are already prone to dry mouth due to other health conditions or medications. To avoid this side effect, it is essential to use Rehmannia Glutinosa under the guidance of a healthcare professional and follow the recommended dosage and duration of use.

Staying hydrated, avoiding caffeine and alcohol, and practicing good oral hygiene can also help prevent dry mouth.

8. Constipation

Constipation caused by Rehmannia Glutinosa refers to the decreased frequency or difficulty in bowel movements resulting from the use of this herb.

The main active constituents of Rehmannia Glutinosa that can cause this side effect include its iridoid glycosides, such as catalpol and acteoside, which can affect gut motility and cause digestive issues. This side effect is most commonly observed with the use of certain medicinal preparations of Rehmannia Glutinosa, including decoctions or powders, especially when misused or taken in excessive doses. Individuals who are more likely to experience constipation from using Rehmannia Glutinosa include those with a history of digestive sensitivities, slow bowel movements, or other pre-existing gastrointestinal conditions.

To avoid constipation, it is recommended to use Rehmannia Glutinosa in moderation and as directed by a healthcare professional, stay well-hydrated, and maintain a balanced diet that includes fiber-rich foods to support healthy digestion.

9. Headache

Headache caused by Rehmannia Glutinosa refers to pain in the head that can occur as a side effect of using this herb.

This herb contains various active constituents, including iridoid glycosides, phenylethanoids, and tannins, which can cause headaches in some individuals. The main medicinal preparations of Rehmannia Glutinosa that can cause headaches if misused include decoctions, powdered extracts, and capsules. People who are more likely to experience headaches as a side effect of using Rehmannia Glutinosa include those who are sensitive or allergic to this herb, those who consume large doses, and those who use it for extended periods.

To avoid headaches and other side effects, it is important to follow recommended dosages, use it under the guidance of a healthcare provider, and discontinue use if headaches or other adverse reactions occur.

10. Fatigue

Fatigue caused by Rehmannia Glutinosa refers to an extreme feeling of tiredness and lack of energy, which can occur in some individuals who consume this herb.

The main active constituents in Rehmannia Glutinosa that can cause this side effect include iridoid glycosides, catalpol, and phenylethanoid glycosides, which can have a sedative effect on the body. The main medicinal preparations of Rehmannia Glutinosa that can cause fatigue if misused include decoctions, powdered extracts, and capsules.

Those who are more likely to experience fatigue from Rehmannia Glutinosa are individuals with weak constitutions, the elderly, and those who consume large doses of the herb for extended periods. To avoid experiencing fatigue from Rehmannia Glutinosa, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare professional or licensed herbalist before using this herb.

Additionally, those who experience fatigue should reduce their dosage or discontinue use of the herb and seek medical attention if symptoms persist.

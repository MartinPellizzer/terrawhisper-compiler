10 Best Health Benefits of Rehmannia glutinosa (Rehmannia)

Rehmannia glutinosa, also known as Chinese foxglove, is a plant that has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

This plant contains various compounds with medicinal properties, such as iridoid glycosides, phenylethanoid glycosides, and catalpol, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory effects. Studies have suggested that Rehmannia glutinosa may be effective in treating a variety of health conditions, including anemia, diabetes, and kidney disease. Additionally, this plant has been shown to have potential benefits for skin health, as it may help to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing.

However, more research is needed to fully understand the medicinal benefits of Rehmannia glutinosa and to determine the most effective dosages and forms of administration.

1. Alleviates inflammation

Rehmannia alleviates inflammation, meaning it may help reduce redness, swelling, pain, and heat in various parts of the body.

The main active constituents of Rehmannia glutinosa that contribute to this benefit include iridoid glycosides, such as catalpol and acteoside, as well as phenylethanoid glycosides, like rehmaglutins A and B. The primary parts of the Rehmannia glutinosa plant that possess this anti-inflammatory property are the roots, particularly when they are harvested after one to two years of growth. The main medicinal preparations of Rehmannia glutinosa that provide this benefit are decoctions, extracts, and powdered forms, often found in traditional Chinese medicine formulas.

This benefit of Rehmannia glutinosa helps relieve various health conditions, such as arthritis, dermatitis, and other inflammatory-related disorders, by potentially inhibiting the release of pro-inflammatory cytokines and enzymes.

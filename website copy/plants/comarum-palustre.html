What to know before using Comarum palustre (Marsh Cinquefoil)?

Comarum palustre, also known as Marsh Cinquefoil, is a perennial plant that is native to the cooler regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Medicinally, it has been used for its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties, and has been known to treat diarrhea, dysentery, and skin irritations. This plant prefers moist horticultural conditions, often found in wetlands, streams, and lake shores.

The botanical characteristics of Comarum palustre include its slender, reddish stems, and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

What to know before using Galium mollugo (Hedge Bedstraw)?

Galium mollugo, also known as hedge bedstraw, is a plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine to treat skin conditions, wounds, and gastrointestinal issues. The plant contains chemicals called iridoid glycosides, which have anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Galium mollugo prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade. It is a creeping plant that can spread rapidly, making it a good choice for groundcover in appropriate settings. From a botanical standpoint, Galium mollugo is a member of the Rubiaceae family and has small, star-shaped white flowers.

It is native to Europe and Asia, but has been introduced in other parts of the world, including North America.

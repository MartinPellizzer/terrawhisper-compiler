What to know before using Achillea millefolium (Yarrow)?

Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow, is a versatile and hardy plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

It has been used traditionally for its ability to help stop bleeding, reduce inflammation, and alleviate digestive issues. Yarrow is also a popular ornamental plant in horticulture, valued for its feathery, fern-like foliage and its clusters of small, bright flowers. This drought-tolerant perennial thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and can grow up to 3 feet tall.

The botanical characteristics of Achillea millefolium include its composite flower heads, which are made up of many small flowers, and its aromatic, deeply-cut leaves.

What are the medicinal uses of Achillea millefolium?

Achillea millefolium has several medicinal uses, such as treating wounds, reducing inflammation, and alleviating digestive issues.

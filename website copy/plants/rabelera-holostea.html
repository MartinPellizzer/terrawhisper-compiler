What to know before using Rabelera holostea (Easter-Bell)?

Rabelera holostea, also known as Babington's spurge, is a perennial plant native to Europe that has been used in traditional medicine for its various healing properties.

This plant contains alkaloids, saponins, and tannins, which have been found to have diuretic, expectorant, and anti-inflammatory effects. It is a hardy plant that can grow in a variety of horticultural conditions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Rabelera holostea can grow up to 60 cm tall, with hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring.

Despite its medicinal uses, it is important to handle this plant with care, as it can cause skin irritation in some individuals.

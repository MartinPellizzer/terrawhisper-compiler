10 possible side effects of Valeriana officinalis (Valerian) if used improperly

Valeriana officinalis, also known as valerian root, is a popular herbal supplement often used for its calming and sleep-promoting effects.

However, if used improperly, it can lead to several potential health issues. First, overuse or misuse of valerian root can cause stomach discomfort, including nausea, bloating, and cramping. Additionally, it may interact with other central nervous system depressants, such as alcohol, benzodiazepines, and opioids, leading to increased drowsiness, cognitive impairment, and even respiratory depression.

In some cases, valerian root has been linked to headaches, dizziness, and dry mouth. Moreover, long-term use or high doses of valerian root can result in withdrawal symptoms, such as restlessness, anxiety, and insomnia, when discontinued abruptly. Pregnant women, breastfeeding mothers, and children should avoid using valerian root, as its safety and efficacy in these populations have not been adequately studied.

Always consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement or medication regimen.

1. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Valeriana Officinalis refers to a spinning sensation, lightheadedness, or feeling faint that may occur after using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in valerian that can cause this side effect include valerenic acid, valepotriates, and essential oils. Medicinal preparations such as valerian root extracts, tinctures, and teas, when misused or taken in excessive doses, can lead to dizziness. Individuals who are more likely to experience dizziness from valerian use include those with a history of motion sickness, low blood pressure, or those who are taking other sedative medications.

To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare professional before using valerian, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking other medications.

2. Drowsiness

Drowsiness caused by Valeriana Officinalis refers to the sensation of sleepiness or overwhelming desire for sleep that can occur after ingesting this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents of valerian that can cause this side effect are the antioxidant and sedative compounds found in its roots, such as valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates. Medicinal preparations such as valerian tea, capsules, or tinctures can cause drowsiness if misused or taken in excessive quantities.

Individuals who are more likely to experience this side effect include those who are sensitive to sedatives, have a history of liver or kidney disease, or are concurrently taking other sedative medications or alcohol. To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider before using valerian, especially when using other medications or if pregnant or breastfeeding.

Additionally, it is best to avoid operating heavy machinery or driving after taking valerian due to the risk of drowsiness.

3. Fatigue

Fatigue caused by Valeriana Officinalis refers to an extreme feeling of tiredness and lack of energy that can occur as a side effect of using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents of Valeriana Officinalis that can cause this side effect are the valepotriates and volatile oils, which can have a sedative effect on the body. This side effect is most commonly associated with certain medicinal preparations, such as valerian root extracts and teas, especially when taken in large doses or for extended periods of time. People who are more likely to experience fatigue as a side effect of using Valeriana Officinalis include those who are sensitive to its active constituents, have a history of liver or kidney disease, or are taking other medications that can interact with valerian.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosage on the product label, consult with a healthcare provider before taking valerian, and be aware of potential interactions with other medications or supplements.

4. Headache

Headache caused by Valeriana Officinalis refers to a type of discomfort or pain in the head that may occur as a side effect of using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents of valerian that can cause this side effect are the antioxidants called valepotriates and the sedative compounds called valerenic acid and its derivatives. The main medicinal preparations of valerian that can cause headaches if misused include valerian root extracts, tinctures, and teas.

People who are more likely to experience headaches after using valerian are those who take high doses of the herb, those who mix it with other sedative drugs or alcohol, and those who are sensitive or allergic to the herb. To avoid headaches caused by valerian, it is important to follow the recommended dosage, consult with a healthcare provider before taking valerian, and discontinue use if a headache occurs.

It is also advisable to avoid using valerian with other sedatives or alcohol, and to be aware of any personal sensitivity or allergy to the herb.

5. Stomach Problems

Stomach problems caused by Valeriana Officinalis refers to gastrointestinal issues such as nausea, vomiting, and stomach pain.

These problems are mainly caused by the plant's volatile oils, iridoids, and alkaloids. Medicinal preparations such as capsules, teas, and tinctures of valerian can cause stomach problems if misused or taken in large amounts.

People who are more likely to experience stomach problems from valerian include those who have a sensitive stomach or a history of digestive issues. To avoid stomach problems, it is advisable to start with a low dose of valerian and gradually increase it over time.

Additionally, taking valerian with food and avoiding alcohol and caffeine consumption may also help reduce the risk of stomach problems.

6. Tremors

Tremors caused by Valeriana Officinalis refers to involuntary trembling or shaking, particularly in the hands or legs, that may occur as a side effect of using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents of valerian that can cause this side effect include volatile oils, iridoid compounds, and alkaloids, which can affect the central nervous system and cause adverse reactions when taken in large doses or used for extended periods. Medicinal preparations such as valerian root tea, capsules, and tinctures have the potential to cause tremors if misused or used in conjunction with certain medications or substances. People who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with a history of liver or kidney disease, pregnant or breastfeeding women, and individuals with a sensitivity to valerian or other members of the same plant family.

To avoid this side effect, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider or pharmacist before using valerian, especially if taking other medications or have pre-existing medical conditions.

7. Restlessness

Restlessness caused by Valeriana Officinalis refers to a feeling of being unable to rest easily and free from anxiety or agitation.

This side effect can be attributed to the main active constituents of valerian, which include valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates. These compounds can stimulate the neurotransmitter GABA in the brain, leading to a calming effect, but when used in large doses or for prolonged periods, they may induce restlessness.

Medicinal preparations such as tinctures, capsules, and teas made from the root or aerial parts of valerian are most likely to cause this side effect if misused. Individuals who are particularly susceptible to this side effect are those with preexisting anxiety or sleep disorders, pregnant women, and those taking other medications that can interact with valerian.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosage on the product label and consult a healthcare professional before using valerian, especially if taking other medications or have a medical condition.

8. Dry Mouth

Dry mouth caused by Valeriana Officinalis refers to a decreased production of saliva in the mouth, leading to a dry, sticky feeling.

This is primarily due to the plant's active constituents, including valerenic acid and iridoid glycosides, which can have various effects on the central nervous system. The main medicinal preparations of valerian that can cause dry mouth if misused include dried root extracts, tinctures, and capsules.

Elderly individuals and those with pre-existing medical conditions such as Sjogren's syndrome or those who are taking certain medications that cause dry mouth are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid dry mouth when using valerian, it's important to stay well-hydrated, avoid consuming alcohol or caffeine, and monitor the dosage and frequency of use.

It is also recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using valerian, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions.

9. Upset Stomach

Upset stomach caused by Valeriana Officinalis refers to a disturbance in the normal functioning of the stomach, such as cramps, bloating, nausea, or pain, that may occur following the use of this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in Valeriana Officinalis that can cause this side effect include valepotriates and essential oils like chlorogenic acid and camphor. This side effect is most commonly associated with the misuse of valerian root in various forms, including capsules, teas, and tinctures.

Individuals who are more susceptible to this side effect are those with a history of gastrointestinal sensitivities or allergies, and those who consume large doses of valerian or use it for an extended period of time. To avoid an upset stomach, it is recommended to start with a low dose of valerian and gradually increase it over time, while also adhering to the recommended dosage and duration of use guidelines.

Additionally, taking valerian with food or consulting with a healthcare provider before using valerian can also help reduce the risk of experiencing an upset stomach.

10. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Valeriana Officinalis refers to an upset stomach that results in loose or watery stools.

This side effect can be attributed to the active constituents present in valerian, including valepotriates and essential oils, which may have a laxative effect in some individuals. Medicinal preparations such as valerian root tea, capsules, or tinctures have the potential to cause diarrhea if taken in excessive quantities or over extended periods.

People with sensitive digestive systems, including those with irritable bowel syndrome, may be more prone to experiencing diarrhea after using valerian. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of valerian and gradually increase it over time, while also staying well-hydrated to support proper bowel function.

Additionally, it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplements, including valerian, to ensure safe and effective usage.

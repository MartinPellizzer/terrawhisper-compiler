10 Best Health Benefits of Valeriana officinalis (Valerian)

Valeriana officinalis, commonly known as valerian root, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to its potential therapeutic benefits.

It is most notably recognized for its calming effects on the nervous system, making it a popular natural remedy for anxiety, stress, and insomnia. Valerian root is believed to increase the amount of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain, a neurotransmitter that helps regulate nerve impulses in the brain and promote relaxation.

Additionally, it may provide relief from menstrual cramps and symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes and mood swings. Furthermore, some studies suggest that valerian root could potentially lower blood pressure and improve cardiovascular health.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen, as valerian root can interact with certain medications and may have side effects for some individuals.

1. Improves sleep quality

Valerian improves sleep quality, meaning it can help individuals achieve deeper, more restorative sleep, and possibly reduce the time it takes to fall asleep.

The main active constituents of Valerian that contribute to this benefit are a group of compounds called valepotriates, as well as essential oils such as valerenic acid. The primary parts of the Valerian plant that are used for this benefit are the roots and underground stems (rhizomes). The main medicinal preparations of Valerian that provide this benefit include dried root or rhizome powder, liquid extracts, and tinctures.

This benefit of Valerian is particularly helpful for individuals experiencing sleep disturbances, including those with insomnia, restless legs syndrome, and sleep disturbances related to menopause.

Valeriana officinalis improves sleep quality thanks to the active constituents listed below.

  • Alkaloids
  • Valepotriates
  • Flavanones
  • Flavonoids
  • Glycosides
  • Essential oil
  • Tannins
  • Phenolic acids
  • Sesquiterpenes
  • Monoterpenes

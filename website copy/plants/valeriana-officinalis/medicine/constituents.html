10 Best Active Constituents of Valeriana officinalis (Valerian)

Valeriana officinalis, also known as valerian root, contains several active constituents that contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most notable of these are valepotriates, which have been shown to have sedative and anxiolytic effects. Additionally, valerian root contains antioxidants such as hesperidin and linarin, which may contribute to its stress-relieving and calming effects.

The root also contains volatile oils, including bornyl acetate and camphene, which may have muscle relaxant properties. Finally, valerian root contains isovaleric acid, which has been suggested to have a role in the herb's sedative effects.

These active constituents work together to make valerian root a popular natural remedy for anxiety, insomnia, and other conditions related to stress and tension.

1. Valepotriates

The valepotriates contained in Valeriana Officinalis are a group of complex chemical compounds that contribute to the plant's medicinal properties.

These compounds have been found to have sedative, anxiolytic, and muscle relaxant effects, making them valuable in treating various health conditions. Valepotriates are particularly effective in alleviating symptoms of anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness, as well as reducing muscle spasms and menstrual cramps. They are primarily found in the valerian root, although they can also be present in smaller quantities in the plant's leaves and stems.

Improper use of valepotriates can lead to several side effects, including headaches, dizziness, stomach upset, and, in rare cases, excitability or hallucinations.

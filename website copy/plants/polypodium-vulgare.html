It contains antioxidants that can help protect the skin from damage caused by UV radiation, and it has anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects that can help treat conditions such as psoriasis and eczema. In terms of horticulture, Polypodium vulgare prefers shady, moist environments and can be found growing on trees, rocks, and the ground in woodlands throughout the world. The plant has a creeping rhizome that forms a mat of bright green, lobed fronds that are 2-8 inches long and have a leathery texture.

The fronds are evergreen, remaining green and functional throughout the year.

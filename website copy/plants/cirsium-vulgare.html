What to know before using Cirsium vulgare (Black Thistle)?

Cirsium vulgare, also known as the common thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and Asia but now widely established in many parts of the world.

Medicinally, it has been used in traditional medicine as a treatment for various ailments, including liver and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. As a horticultural choice, Cirsium vulgare prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it can thrive in a variety of environments, from meadows and grasslands to disturbed sites. Botanically, Cirsium vulgare is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with spiny stems and leaves, and purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

However, it can also be invasive in some areas due to its prolific seed production and ability to colonize new habitats quickly.

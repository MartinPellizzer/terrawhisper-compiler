What to know before using Maranta arundinacea (Arrowroot)?

Maranta arundinacea, also known as West Indian arrowroot, is a tuberous perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its demulcent and anti-diarrheal properties, making it a popular remedy for digestive ailments. Maranta arundinacea thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Its botanical characteristics include arrowroot's distinctive, long, slender rhizomes that grow just below the soil surface, as well as its bright green, ovate-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm long.

What are the possible side effects of Maranta arundinacea?

Maranta arundinacea can have some side effects if used improperly, such as digestive issues like bloating, cramps, and diarrhea due to its high starch content.

Prolonged use or excessive consumption can lead to decreased appetite and weight loss. Allergic reactions, although rare, can occur, resulting in symptoms such as skin rashes, itching, and difficulty breathing. Additionally, arrowroot may interact with certain medications, such as acarbose and miglitol, used to treat type 2 diabetes, leading to hypoglycemia or low blood sugar.

Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using arrowroot for medicinal purposes, as its safety and efficacy have not been sufficiently studied.

What are the horticultural conditions of Maranta arundinacea?

Maranta arundinacea needs well-drained, moist soil and partial shade to thrive.

What to know before using Prunus laurocerasus (Cherry-Laurel)?

Prunus laurocerasus, also known as cherry laurel, is a plant with a variety of uses and characteristics.

Medicinally, it has been used in the production of herbal remedies, with extracts from its leaves and bark used to treat conditions such as fever, digestive issues, and rheumatism. In terms of horticultural conditions, cherry laurel is a hardy plant that can thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping and gardening. Botanically, it is an evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 15 feet tall, with glossy, dark green leaves and white flowers that bloom in the spring.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic if ingested, and it can be harmful to pets and small children if not handled with care.

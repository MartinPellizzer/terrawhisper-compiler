What to know before using Robinia pseudoacacia (False Acacia)?

Robinia pseudoacacia, also known as black locust, is a species of tree with several notable medicinal properties.

The tree's bark and seeds contain robinin, a flavonoid with potential anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective effects. Additionally, the tree's leaves and flowers have been used in traditional medicine to treat skin conditions, wounds, and fevers. In terms of horticultural conditions, Robinia pseudoacacia prefers full sun and well-drained soil.

It is a fast-growing tree that can tolerate a wide range of soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control. Botanically, the tree is known for its distinctive, fragrant white flowers, which bloom in the early summer. The tree's leaves are alternately arranged, pinnate, and composed of several leaflets.

The tree can grow up to 80 feet tall and has a lifespan of around 50-150 years.

What to know before using Agrostis canina (Velvet Bent)?

Agrostis canina, also known as redtop bentgrass, is a species of grass native to Europe and Asia that has since naturalized in other parts of the world.

Medicinally, it has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues and skin conditions. It is a perennial plant that thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. From a botanical perspective, Agrostis canina is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and short, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically purple-tinged.

When grown in appropriate conditions, this grass can reach heights of up to 80 cm and forms dense, tufted clumps.

What are the medicinal uses of Agrostis canina?

Agrostis canina is used to treat various health issues, such as gastric disorders, diarrhea, and skin irritations, due to its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and antiseptic properties.

The active constituents of Agrostis canina include tannins, flavonoids, and essential oils, which are responsible for its medicinal benefits. Common medicinal preparations include decoctions, infusions, and ointments made from the plant's leaves and stems. However, Agrostis canina may cause side effects like nausea, vomiting, and constipation if taken in large doses, and its use should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding.

Moreover, individuals with pre-existing gastrointestinal conditions should use this herb with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

Caring for Agrostis canina requires regular watering, especially during dry spells.

Water the grass deeply once or twice a week, providing about 1 inch of water each time. Avoid frequent, shallow watering, which can lead to shallow root growth and increased susceptibility to drought stress.

Fertilize velvet bent in the early spring and fall with a slow-release fertilizer. Mow the grass regularly, maintaining a height of 1-2 inches.

Harvesting Agrostis canina is typically done in the late summer or early fall, after the grass has reached full maturity.

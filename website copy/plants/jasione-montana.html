What to know before using Jasione montana (Sheepbit)?

Jasione montana, also known as Sheep's-bit scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe that has been used in folk medicine for its potential healing properties.

This plant is known to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it a possible remedy for conditions such as eczema, insect bites, and minor wounds. In terms of horticultural conditions, Jasione montana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it can tolerate a wide range of soil types, making it a versatile addition to any garden. Botanically, Jasione montana is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 30 centimeters tall, with narrow, linear leaves and blue, dome-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer.

Its blooms are attractive to bees and butterflies, making it a popular choice for pollinator gardens.

What to know before using Amaranthus hypochondriacus (Amaranth)?

Amaranthus hypochondriacus, also known as Prince-of-Wales feather, is an annual plant that has been used in herbal medicine for its various health benefits.

It is rich in nutrients, including protein, fiber, and various vitamins and minerals, and has been used to treat conditions such as inflammation, digestive issues, and skin problems. In horticultural conditions, Amaranthus hypochondriacus prefers full sun and well-drained soil, and can grow up to 6 feet tall with a spread of 2-3 feet.

It has striking, bright red flower spikes that bloom in the summer and fall, and its leaves can be used as a leafy vegetable. From a botanical perspective, Amaranthus hypochondriacus is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and has a deep taproot system, making it drought-tolerant.

It is native to Central and South America, but is now grown in many parts of the world for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

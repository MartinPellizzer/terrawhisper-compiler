What to know before using Rhus glabra (Smooth Sumac)?

Rhus glabra, also known as smooth sumac, is a shrub that is native to North America and is known for its medicinal properties.

The bark and fruit of Rhus glabra have been used in herbal medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues, fever, and inflammation. This shrub is adaptable to a wide range of horticultural conditions, thriving in full sun to partial shade and able to tolerate various soil types. Rhus glabra is characterized by its tall, conical shape, reaching 10-20 feet in height, and its pinnately compound leaves, which turn a vibrant red in the fall.

The fruit of Rhus glabra, also known as sumac berries, are clusters of small, red drupes that are often used to make a tangy, lemonade-like drink.

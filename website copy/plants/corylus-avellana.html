What to know before using Corylus avellana (European Filbert)?

Corylus avellana, also known as the European filbert or common hazel, is a plant with a variety of medicinal properties, horticultural applications, and interesting botanical characteristics.

Medicinally, hazel has been used in herbal medicine as a treatment for digestive disorders, to reduce swelling, and as a gentle laxative. It is also a good source of tannins, essential oils, and antioxidants.

In terms of horticulture, Corylus avellana is a hardy, deciduous shrub that can grow up to 12 feet tall and wide, and thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive, heart-shaped leaves and delicate, yellow-and-red flowers.

Botanically, Corylus avellana is a member of the birch family, Betulaceae, and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and edible nuts encased in a hard, woody shell.

What are the pests and diseases of Corylus avellana?

Corylus avellana is relatively pest and disease-free.

However, it can be susceptible to a few issues. One common pest is the filbertworm, which is the larva of a moth that lays its eggs on the catkins. To control filbertworm, it is recommended to remove and destroy any affected catkins.

Another pest to watch out for is the aphid. Aphids can be controlled with insecticidal soap or by introducing beneficial insects such as ladybugs. In terms of diseases, Corylus avellana can be susceptible to verticillium wilt, a soil-borne fungal disease. To prevent verticillium wilt, it is recommended to avoid planting Corylus avellana in soil where susceptible plants have previously grown.

Additionally, it is essential to practice good cultural care, such as watering at the base of the plant and avoiding overhead watering.

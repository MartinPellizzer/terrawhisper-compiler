What to know before using Datura stramonium (Thornapple)?

Datura stramonium, also known as Jimsonweed, is a plant with a long history of use in traditional medicine, despite its poisonous nature.

Its seeds, leaves, and flowers have been used to treat respiratory conditions such as asthma, whooping cough, and bronchitis, due to their antispasmodic and expectorant properties. However, it is crucial to use caution when handling this plant, as all parts of the plant contain dangerous levels of alkaloids that can be fatal if ingested or even touched.

Datura stramonium thrives in disturbed soils and waste areas, making it a common roadside weed in many regions. It is a versatile plant that can grow in a variety of conditions, from full sun to partial shade, and prefers well-drained soil.

The plant itself can grow up to 4 feet tall, with trumpet-shaped flowers that are white or purple and bloom at night.

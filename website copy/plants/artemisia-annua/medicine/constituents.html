10 Best Active Constituents of Artemisia annua (Sweet Annie)

Artemisia annua, also known as sweet wormwood, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries and contains several active constituents with potential therapeutic benefits.

The most well-known of these is artemisinin, a sesquiterpene lactone that has been shown to have potent antimalarial properties. Artemisia annua also contains other compounds, such as flavonoids and coumarins, that may contribute to its medicinal properties.

Some studies suggest that these compounds may have anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and anticancer effects, although more research is needed to confirm these potential benefits. Artemisia annua is typically administered as a tea, tincture, or capsule, and is often used to treat a variety of conditions, including malaria, fever, and inflammation.

However, it is important to note that Artemisia annua should only be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional, as it can interact with other medications and may have side effects.

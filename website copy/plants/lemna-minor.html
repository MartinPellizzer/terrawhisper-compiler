What to know before using Lemna minor (Duckweed)?

Lemna minor, also known as duckweed, is a small aquatic plant with notable medicinal properties.

It contains bioactive compounds with potential anti-cancer, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective effects, making it a valuable subject of ongoing research. Horticulturally, Lemna minor thrives in calm, slow-moving, or still bodies of fresh water, requiring ample sunlight and nutrient-rich environments. Its botanical characteristics include tiny, green, oval leaves with a single root, or "fluke," dangling from the leaf's underside, forming a compact, mat-like colony that floats on the water surface.

The plant reproduces through budding, facilitating rapid colonization of suitable aquatic habitats.

What to know before using Glyceria fluitans (Water Mannagrass)?

Glyceria fluitans, also known as Mannekin-grass, is a gracious wetland plant with culms that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers consistently moist to wet soil conditions, making it an ideal choice for bog gardens or pond edges. The plant's medicinal properties are primarily associated with its diuretic effects, helping to regulate kidney function and maintain healthy water balance in the body.

As a botanical specimen, Glyceria fluitans is distinguished by its tufted, spreading growth habit and conspicuous spikelets, which bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a graceful touch to any water-loving garden.

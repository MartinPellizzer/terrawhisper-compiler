What to know before using Agrostis capillaris (New Zealand Bent Grass)?

Agrostis capillaris, also known as common bentgrass, is a cool-season turf grass that is valued for its ability to form dense, low-growing mats.

It is native to Europe and Asia, but has been widely introduced and naturalized in other parts of the world. Medicinally, Agrostis capillaris has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues and skin conditions. It contains a number of beneficial compounds, such as polyphenols and flavonoids, which are thought to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Agrostis capillaris prefers well-drained, moist soils and full sun to partial shade. It is tolerant of a wide range of soil pH levels and can thrive in both sandy and clay soils. In terms of its botanical characteristics, Agrostis capillaris is a perennial grass that grows to be around 12-24 inches tall. It has narrow, linear leaves that are rolled inward and a dense, spreading growth habit.

The flowers of Agrostis capillaris are borne on slender, spike-like inflorescences and are wind-pollinated.

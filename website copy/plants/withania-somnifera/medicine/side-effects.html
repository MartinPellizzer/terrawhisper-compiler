10 possible side effects of Withania somnifera (Ashwagandha) if used improperly

Withania somnifera, also known as Indian ginseng or Ashwagandha, is a popular herbal supplement often used for its potential stress-relieving and anti-inflammatory properties.

However, if used improperly, it can have several potential negative health effects. Firstly, consuming excessive doses may cause gastrointestinal issues like nausea, diarrhea, and stomach upset. Secondly, it can interact with certain medications, such as sedatives, thyroid medications, and immunosuppressants, leading to adverse reactions.

Thirdly, Withania somnifera might lower blood sugar levels, which can be dangerous for diabetic patients who are already on medication to lower their blood sugar. Fourthly, it can cause hormonal imbalances, particularly in female patients, leading to menstrual irregularities and other reproductive issues. Lastly, pregnant women should avoid using this herb as it may act as a uterine stimulant, potentially causing miscarriage or premature labor.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen, especially Withania somnifera, to ensure safe and effective usage.

1. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Withania Somnifera refers to a condition of passing loose, watery stools frequently.

This side effect can be attributed to the main active constituents of ashwagandha such as alkaloids, steroidal lactones, and saponins. The root and leaves of ashwagandha are the main medicinal preparations that can cause diarrhea if misused or taken in large quantities.

Individuals who are particularly sensitive to ashwagandha or have a weak digestive system are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid diarrhea, it is advisable to start with a low dosage of ashwagandha and gradually increase it, while also ensuring that it is taken with meals to reduce the likelihood of stomach upset.

Additionally, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before taking ashwagandha, especially for people with pre-existing digestive conditions or those taking other medications.

2. Nausea

Nausea caused by Withania Somnifera refers to the feeling of discomfort and stomach distress experienced after using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents responsible for this side effect are alkaloids and steroidal lactones, particularly withanolides. The preparations of ashwagandha that are most likely to cause nausea if misused include its root and leaf extracts, as well as powdered forms of the root.

Individuals who are more susceptible to experiencing nausea after using ashwagandha include those with a sensitive stomach or those who take large doses of the supplement. To avoid experiencing nausea, it is recommended to start with a low dose of ashwagandha and gradually increase it over time, as well as to take it with food or after meals.

It's also important to consult with a healthcare provider before using ashwagandha, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking other medications.

3. Vomiting

Vomiting caused by Withania Somnifera refers to the forceful expulsion of stomach contents through the mouth.

This side effect can be attributed to the main active constituents of ashwagandha, which include alkaloids, steroidal lactones, and saponins. The medicinal preparations that can cause vomiting if misused include decoctions, powders, and capsules made from the plant's roots or leaves.

Individuals who are sensitive to ashwagandha or have a history of digestive issues are more likely to experience vomiting. To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to start with a low dose and gradually increase it over time, under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

Moreover, individuals should avoid taking ashwagandha on an empty stomach and ensure they drink plenty of water while taking it.

4. Abdominal Discomfort

Abdominal discomfort caused by Withania Somnifera refers to a feeling of pain, cramping, or general discomfort in the abdominal region that may occur following the use of this herb.

The main active constituents found in ashwagandha that can contribute to this side effect include various alkaloids, steroidal lactones, and withanolides, which can influence the gastrointestinal system and cause digestive issues. Medicinal preparations of ashwagandha that can cause abdominal discomfort if misused include decoctions, powders, and capsules. Individuals who are more likely to experience this side effect may include those with a history of gastrointestinal sensitivity, irritable bowel syndrome, or other digestive disorders.

To avoid experiencing abdominal discomfort when using ashwagandha, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase over time, to consume the herb with food to reduce gastric irritation, and to avoid taking it in excess of the recommended dosage.

5. Drowsiness

Drowsiness caused by Withania Somnifera refers to the sensation of sleepiness or wanting to sleep that can occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents of ashwagandha that can cause this side effect are withanolides, specifically withaferin A and withanolide D. These compounds can have a sedative effect on the body, leading to drowsiness.

Medicinal preparations of ashwagandha that can cause this side effect if misused include extracts, powders, and capsules. Individuals who are more likely to experience this side effect are those who are sensitive to sedatives, those who take large doses, and those who use ashwagandha in conjunction with other sedative medications or alcohol. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of ashwagandha and gradually increase the dosage over time, as well as to avoid using it in conjunction with sedative medications or alcohol.

Additionally, it's important to consult with a healthcare professional before using ashwagandha, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

6. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Withania Somnifera refers to a spinning or lightheaded sensation that may occur in some individuals using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in ashwagandha responsible for this side effect are alkaloids and steroidal lactones, particularly withanolides. This side effect is most commonly associated with the misuse of ashwagandha in its powdered root form, as well as in certain extracts, when recommended dosages are exceeded. Elderly individuals, people with low blood pressure, and those prone to vertigo are more likely to experience dizziness after using ashwagandha.

To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to follow recommended dosages, start with lower doses and gradually increase, consult with a healthcare professional before use, and avoid operating heavy machinery or driving after consuming ashwagandha.

7. Dry Mouth

Dry mouth caused by Withania Somnifera refers to a decrease in saliva production in the mouth, leading to symptoms such as difficulty swallowing, speaking, and altered taste.

The main active constituents in ashwagandha that can cause dry mouth are alkaloids and withanolides, which can affect the nervous system and reduce saliva production. Medicinal preparations such as powder, capsules, and tea, if misused or consumed in large quantities, can increase the risk of developing dry mouth. Individuals with a history of dry mouth or those taking medications that cause dry mouth are more likely to experience this side effect.

To avoid dry mouth when using ashwagandha, it is recommended to stay hydrated, consume small and frequent doses, and avoid using it in combination with other medications that cause dry mouth.

8. Low Blood Pressure

Low blood pressure caused by Withania Somnifera refers to an abnormally low decrease in blood pressure that can occur with the use of this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in Withania somnifera that can cause this side effect are withanolides, which have been shown to have a hypotensive effect on the body. This means that medicinal preparations such as ashwagandha root powder, ashwagandha root extract, and ashwagandha leaf extract, when misused or taken in large amounts, can cause a decrease in blood pressure. Individuals who are already taking medication for blood pressure or those with existing low blood pressure are more likely to experience this side effect.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider before starting to use ashwagandha, especially for those who have pre-existing medical conditions or are taking other medication.

9. Reduced Appetite

Reduced appetite caused by Withania Somnifera refers to a decrease in appetite or desire to eat, which can be a side effect of consuming this herb.

The main active constituents in Withania somnifera that can cause this side effect are withanolides and alkaloids, which can influence the body's hunger and satiety signals. Medicinal preparations of Withania somnifera that can cause this side effect if misused include powders, capsules, and teas made from the root or leaves of the plant. Individuals who are more likely to experience reduced appetite as a side effect of Withania somnifera use are those who consume large or excessive doses, those with a history of eating disorders, and those who are already taking other appetite-suppressing medications.

To avoid this side effect, it's important to follow the recommended dosage instructions carefully, consult with a healthcare provider before using Withania somnifera, and be mindful of any changes in appetite or eating habits while taking this herb.

10. Upset Stomach

Upset stomach caused by Withania Somnifera refers to a digestive disorder where an individual may experience discomfort, pain, or a feeling of fullness in the abdomen.

This side effect can be attributed to the main active constituents of ashwagandha, which include various alkaloids and steroidal lactones, particularly withanolides. These constituents can irritate the gastrointestinal tract, causing stomach discomfort or upset.

Medicinal preparations such as root powder, leaves, and berry extracts, if misused or consumed in large amounts, can lead to this side effect. People who are more susceptible to this side effect are those with a history of gastrointestinal issues or those who consume ashwagandha on an empty stomach. To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to take ashwagandha in moderation, following the recommended dosage, and preferably after meals.

Individuals should consult their healthcare provider before taking ashwagandha, especially if they have any pre-existing medical conditions.

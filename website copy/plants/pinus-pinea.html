What to know before using Pinus pinea (Umbrella Pine)?

Pinus pinea, also known as the Italian stone pine or pine nut pine, is a species of pine native to the Mediterranean region.

Its nuts, known as pine nuts, are highly valued in medicine for their nutritious properties, as they are a rich source of protein, healthy fats, and various vitamins and minerals. They are often used in traditional medicine to treat digestive issues, respiratory ailments, and to boost the immune system.

Pinus pinea thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soil and prefers full sun exposure. It is a slow-growing tree, with a mature height of 20-30 meters and a broad, rounded crown.

The tree has a distinctive appearance, with long, dark green needles arranged in clusters of two and large, brown cones that take up to three years to mature.

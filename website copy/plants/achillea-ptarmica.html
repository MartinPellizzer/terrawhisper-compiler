What to know before using Achillea ptarmica (Sneezeweed)?

Achillea ptarmica, also known as Yarrow or Sneezewort, is a versatile plant with a range of medicinal properties.

It has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and astringent qualities, making it useful for treating wounds, preventing infections, and reducing inflammation. It is also known to have digestive benefits, such as relieving cramps, reducing bloating, and promoting digestion.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Achillea ptarmica thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it is drought-tolerant. This perennial plant grows to be around 2-3 feet tall and wide, producing clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer.

Its fern-like, aromatic leaves create an attractive contrast with the flowers, adding visual interest to any garden.

What to know before using Ranunculus ficaria (Lesser Celandine)?

Ranunculus ficaria, also known as lesser celandine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and Western Asia.

Its botanical characteristics include kidney-shaped, glossy green leaves and yellow, buttercup-like flowers that bloom in early spring. This plant prefers partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil. Medicinally, Ranunculus ficaria has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The sap of the plant contains ranunculin, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities, but when dried and prepared properly, can be used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and hemorrhoids.

What are the medicinal uses of Ranunculus ficaria?

Ranunculus ficaria has several medicinal uses, such as treating skin conditions, inflammation, and hemorrhoids.

Its constituents include probscide A, anagyrine, and rancunin, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Preparations of Ranunculus ficaria can be made from the fresh or dried plant and can be taken orally or applied topically.

However, it is important to note that the plant also contains protoanemonin, which can cause skin irritation, mouth sores, and gastrointestinal symptoms.

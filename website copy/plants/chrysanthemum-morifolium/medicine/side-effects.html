10 possible side effects of Chrysanthemum morifolium (Ju Hua) if used improperly

Chrysanthemum morifolium, also known as the garden mum, is a popular ornamental plant with potential medicinal properties.

However, if used improperly, it can have several negative health effects. Firstly, consuming large amounts of the plant can cause stomach discomfort, nausea, and diarrhea.

Secondly, it can lead to skin irritation and eye inflammation when handling the plant without protective gloves or when the plant's sap comes into contact with the eyes. Moreover, people who are allergic to members of the asteraceae/compositae family (which includes chrysanthemums) may experience allergic reactions such as skin rashes, respiratory issues, or anaphylaxis in severe cases. Lastly, Chrysanthemum morifolium may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and can exacerbate pre-existing medical conditions, particularly those affecting the liver and kidneys.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using Chrysanthemum morifolium for medicinal purposes.

1. Allergic Reactions

Allergic reactions caused by Chrysanthemum Morifolium refers to an adverse reaction of the immune system to the plant's active constituents.

These constituents include flavonoids, volatile oils, and polysaccharides, which can trigger allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. The main medicinal preparations that can cause this side effect if misused are infusions, decoctions, and powdered extracts of Chrysanthemum Morifolium.

People who are more likely to experience allergic reactions to Chrysanthemum Morifolium are those with a history of allergies to other members of the Asteraceae family, such as chamomile, daisies, or ragweed. To avoid allergic reactions, it is important to start with a low dose of Chrysanthemum Morifolium and gradually increase the dosage over time.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before taking Chrysanthemum Morifolium, especially for those with a known history of allergies or sensitivities to plants.

2. Skin Irritation

Skin irritation caused by Chrysanthemum Morifolium refers to the reddening, itching, and inflammation of the skin that can occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents of Chrysanthemum Morifolium that can cause this side effect are its volatile oils, flavonoids, and sesquiterpene lactones. This skin irritation is most commonly associated with the use of Chrysanthemum Morifolium tea or tincture, particularly when these preparations come into direct contact with the skin.

Individuals who are allergic to members of the daisy family, to which Chrysanthemum Morifolium belongs, are at a higher risk of experiencing skin irritation from its use. To avoid this side effect, it's important to handle Chrysanthemum Morifolium with care, avoiding direct skin contact and diluting its preparations as necessary.

If skin irritation does occur, it's recommended to discontinue use and seek medical attention if necessary.

3. Stomach Upset

Stomach upset caused by Chrysanthemum Morifolium refers to a range of gastrointestinal symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, bloating, and abdominal pain that can occur after ingesting this herbal remedy.

The main active constituents of Chrysanthemum Morifolium that can cause stomach upset include various flavonoids, volatile oils, and other compounds that can irritate the stomach lining. This side effect is most commonly associated with certain medicinal preparations, such as decoctions or infusions, that contain high concentrations of these active compounds if misused or prepared incorrectly. Individuals who are more likely to experience stomach upset from Chrysanthemum Morifolium include those with sensitive stomachs, existing gastrointestinal conditions, or those who consume large amounts of the herb.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow recommended dosages carefully, prepare medicinal preparations correctly, and consult with a healthcare provider or herbalist knowledgeable about Chrysanthemum Morifolium and its potential side effects.

4. Dry Mouth

Dry mouth caused by Chrysanthemum Morifolium refers to a condition where there is insufficient saliva production in the mouth, leading to symptoms such as difficulty swallowing, speaking, and changes in taste.

This side effect can be attributed to the main active constituents of Chrysanthemum Morifolium, including flavonoids, volatile oils, and polysaccharides, which can have a drying effect on the mouth. The preparations of Chrysanthemum Morifolium that are most likely to cause this side effect if misused include decoctions, teas, and powders.

Individuals who are more susceptible to dry mouth from Chrysanthemum Morifolium include those with a history of dry mouth or xerostomia, those taking medications that cause dry mouth as a side effect, and individuals with reduced kidney or liver function. To avoid dry mouth when using Chrysanthemum Morifolium, it is important to stay hydrated, avoid excessive use of the herb, and consult with a healthcare practitioner to determine appropriate dosage and preparation.

Additionally, those with a history of dry mouth should exercise caution and consult with a healthcare practitioner before using Chrysanthemum Morifolium.

5. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Chrysanthemum Morifolium refers to a feeling of lightheadedness, unsteadiness, or vertigo that may occur after ingesting this herbal remedy.

The main active constituents in Chrysanthemum Morifolium that can cause this side effect include volatile oils, flavonoids, and various types of sesquiterpenes. This dizziness is most commonly associated with the misuse of medicinal preparations such as decoctions, teas, and powdered extracts, particularly when consumed in large amounts or over an extended period of time.

Individuals who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with a history of motion sickness, low blood pressure, or other underlying health conditions that affect the circulatory system. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of Chrysanthemum Morifolium and gradually increase the dosage over time, while also staying well-hydrated and avoiding other potential triggers of dizziness such as alcohol, caffeine, and spicy foods.

Additionally, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using Chrysanthemum Morifolium to ensure that it is safe and appropriate for your individual health needs.

6. Vomiting

Vomiting caused by Chrysanthemum Morifolium refers to the forceful expulsion of stomach contents through the mouth.

This side effect is primarily attributed to the presence of volatile oils and lactones in Chrysanthemum Morifolium. The main medicinal preparations that can cause vomiting if misused include decoctions, infusions, and tinctures.

Individuals with a history of allergies to members of the Asteraceae family or digestive sensitivities are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid vomiting, it is essential to start with a low dose and gradually increase it over time, ensuring that the body adapts to the constituents.

Furthermore, it is crucial to avoid consuming Chrysanthemum Morifolium on an empty stomach and to seek professional advice regarding dosage and preparation in order to minimize the risk of this side effect.

7. Low Blood Pressure

Low blood pressure caused by Chrysanthemum Morifolium refers to an abnormally low level of blood pressure in the body that can occur with its use.

The main active constituents in Chrysanthemum Morifolium that can cause this side effect include volatile oils, flavonoids, and various types of antioxidants. The main medicinal preparations of Chrysanthemum Morifolium that can cause this side effect if misused include teas, capsules, and tinctures made from the flowers of the plant. People who are more likely to experience low blood pressure after using Chrysanthemum Morifolium include those who are already taking medication for high blood pressure or those who have a history of low blood pressure.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider before using Chrysanthemum Morifolium, especially if taking other medications or have pre-existing medical conditions.

8. Respiratory Symptoms

Respiratory symptoms caused by Chrysanthemum Morifolium refer to adverse effects on the respiratory system such as coughing, wheezing, or difficulty breathing.

These symptoms can occur due to the active constituents in Chrysanthemum Morifolium, including flavonoids, volatile oils, and sesquiterpene lactones. The main medicinal preparations of Chrysanthemum Morifolium that can cause respiratory side effects if misused include decoctions, teas, and powdered extracts.

To avoid respiratory symptoms, it is important to use Chrysanthemum Morifolium under the guidance of a qualified herbalist, follow dosage recommendations carefully, and monitor for any adverse reactions.

9. Sedation

Sedation caused by Chrysanthemum Morifolium refers to the drowsy or lethargic state that may occur after using this plant.

In this context, sedation is characterized by a decrease in mental alertness and physical activity. The main active constituents of Chrysanthemum Morifolium that can cause this side effect include various flavonoids, volatile oils, and plant acids.

The preparations that can cause sedation if misused include infusions, decoctions, and powdered extracts of the flowers. People who are more likely to experience sedation from Chrysanthemum Morifolium include those who are sensitive to the effects of CNS depressants, the elderly, and individuals with compromised liver or kidney function.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to use Chrysanthemum Morifolium under the guidance of a healthcare provider, start with a low dose and gradually increase as needed, and avoid using it with other CNS depressants or alcohol.

10. Headache

Headache caused by Chrysanthemum Morifolium refers to pain in the head that may occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents in Chrysanthemum Morifolium that can cause this side effect are volatile oils, flavonoids, and bitter glycosides. This side effect is most commonly associated with medicinal preparations such as decoctions, teas, and pills that contain high concentrations of these active compounds. People who are more likely to experience headaches after using Chrysanthemum Morifolium include those with a sensitivity or allergy to members of the compositae family, those who consume large amounts of the herb, and those who use the herb in conjunction with other medications that can cause headaches.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to start with a low dose of the herb and gradually increase as tolerated, to avoid using the herb in conjunction with other medications that can cause headaches, and to seek guidance from a qualified practitioner before using the herb if you have a history of headaches or other medical conditions.

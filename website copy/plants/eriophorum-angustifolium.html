What to know before using Eriophorum angustifolium (Common Cotton-Grass)?

Eriophorum angustifolium, also known as cottongrass or narrow-leaved cottongrass, has long been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties.

It contains polysaccharides that are believed to have a soothing effect on the urinary tract and can help reduce inflammation in the body. In horticulture, Eriophorum angustifolium thrives in moist to wet soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is a hardy perennial that grows well in USDA zones 3-7 and can add a unique touch to gardens with its tufted, cotton-like seed heads. The plant's botanical characteristics include a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a tall, slender stem that can reach up to 3 feet in height.

The seed heads are the most distinctive feature of the plant, with white, silky hairs that are used in crafts and stuffing.

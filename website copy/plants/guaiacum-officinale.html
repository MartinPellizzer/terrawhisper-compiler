What to know before using Guaiacum officinale (Lignum Vitae)?

Guaiacum officinale, also known as Lignum-vitae, is a small tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America that has been valued for its medicinal properties for centuries.

This tree produces a resin that contains compounds with anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and antirheumatic properties, making it a popular natural remedy for conditions such as arthritis, bronchitis, and skin diseases. In terms of horticultural conditions, Guaiacum officinale prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and is tolerant of drought and salt spray, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Botanically, Guaiacum officinale is characterized by its slow growth, dense, twisted trunk, and small, evergreen, leathery leaves, as well as its stunning blue flowers and hard, heavy wood.

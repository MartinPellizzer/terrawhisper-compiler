What to know before using Passiflora incarnata (Passionflower)?

Passiflora incarnata, also known as maypop or purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It has a wide range of horticultural requirements, able to thrive in a variety of soils and light conditions, but prefers well-drained soil and partial shade. Medicinally, Passiflora incarnata is best known for its sedative and anxiety-reducing properties, and has been used in herbal medicine to treat insomnia, hysteria, and epilepsy. Its botanical characteristics include distinctive, large, intricately patterned flowers with a fringe of white petals and a crown of purple-tipped filaments, and oval, dark green leaves with serrated edges.

The fruit of the maypop is an edible, seedy berry with a tropical flavor.

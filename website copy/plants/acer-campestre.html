What to know before using Acer campestre (Field Maple)?

Acer campestre, also known as field maple, is a species of maple native to Europe and Western Asia.

It has medicinal properties that make it valuable in herbal medicine: its leaves can be used to make a compress that helps to soothe skin problems, while its fruits and seeds have diuretic and anti-diabetic effects. In terms of horticultural conditions, Acer campestre prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, but it can adapt to a wide range of growing conditions. As for its botanical characteristics, Acer campestre is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10-20 meters, with a trunk diameter of up to 1 meter.

It has a dense, rounded crown and lobed leaves that are dark green in color and turn yellow in the fall.

What to know before using Tsuga canadensis (Canada Hemlock)?

Tsuga canadensis, also known as the Eastern hemlock or Canadian hemlock, is a coniferous tree native to North America.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties, including the ability to soothe coughs and reduce fevers. This tree thrives in cool, moist, well-drained soils and can be found in shaded areas of hardwood forests. Tsuga canadensis is an evergreen with a narrow, pyramidal shape and a height that can reach up to 150 feet.

Its needle-like leaves are dark green above and two white bands below, and its small, woody cones are oval-shaped and contain numerous small seeds.

It is a member of the dogbane family and is characterized by its clusters of orange and yellow flowers, which bloom from early to midsummer. The plant contains cardiac glycosides, which have been used in traditional medicine to treat heart ailments. Asclepias tuberosa thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it is drought-tolerant once established.

It is also a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its showy flowers and its ability to attract butterflies.

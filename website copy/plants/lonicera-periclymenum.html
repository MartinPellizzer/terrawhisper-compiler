What to know before using Lonicera periclymenum (Honeysuckle)?

Lonicera periclymenum, also known as European honeysuckle, is a woody vine native to Europe and western Asia.

It is valued in horticulture for its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer, attracting butterflies and hummingbirds. The plant's twining stems and opposite, dark green leaves make it an attractive addition to gardens and landscapes.

Medicinally, Lonicera periclymenum has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to treat respiratory infections, skin irritations, and digestive issues.

However, it is important to note that improper use or overconsumption can be harmful, and it is always best to consult with a healthcare professional before using any plant for medicinal purposes.

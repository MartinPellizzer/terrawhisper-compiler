Symphytum officinale (Comfrey) Medicinal Guide

Symphytum officinale, also known as comfrey, has been used for medicinal purposes due to its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and healing properties.

The main constituents of comfrey include allantoin, rosmarinic acid, and tannins, which contribute to its therapeutic benefits. Common preparations of comfrey include ointments, creams, and teas, which can be applied topically or taken orally, depending on the condition being treated.

However, it is important to note that comfrey contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can cause liver damage and other side effects if taken in large amounts or over a long period of time. Therefore, it is crucial to follow proper dosage instructions and consult with a healthcare provider before using comfrey for medicinal purposes.

Additionally, pregnant women and children should avoid using comfrey, as it may cause harm to their health.

What are the health benefits of Symphytum officinale (Comfrey)?

Symphytum officinale has several health benefits, such as speeding up wound healing, reducing inflammation, and easing joint and muscle pain due to its allantoin and rosmarinic acid content.

It can also relieve coughs and soothe sore throats as it has mucilage, which forms a soothing film over membranes. Additionally, comfrey's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties help heal minor cuts, reduce scarring, alleviate eczema symptoms, and soothe diaper rash. The herb's allantoin and tannins help with skin irritations, bug bites, sunburn, and acne, while its rosmarinic acid can reduce migraines.

Comfrey also improves bone health, boosts the immune system, and promotes digestion due to its high concentration of vitamin B12, vitamin C, and other nutrients.

The following list shows the health benefits of Symphytum officinale.

  • 1. Speeds up wound healing: Symphytum officinale, also known as comfrey, is known to promote wound healing due to its allantoin content that stimulates cell growth.
  • 2. Reduces inflammation: Comfrey contains anti-inflammatory compounds that help reduce inflammation and swelling, providing relief to affected areas.
  • 3. Eases joint pain: The anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties of comfrey help alleviate joint pain, making it an effective herb for arthritis and joint injuries.
  • 4. Relieves muscle pain: Comfrey's pain-relieving and anti-inflammatory effects provide relief from muscle pain, soreness, and spasms.
  • 5. Heals skin irritations: Comfrey's demulcent and vulnerary properties soothe and heal skin irritations, such as rashes, eczema, and burns.
  • 6. Soothes bug bites: Applying comfrey to bug bites reduces itching, swelling, and discomfort, thanks to its anti-inflammatory and soothing effects.
  • 7. Reduces bruising: Comfrey's ability to reduce inflammation and improve circulation helps diminish bruising and accelerate tissue repair.
  • 8. Improves digestion: Comfrey's mucilaginous properties help soothe and protect the digestive lining, promoting healthy digestion and easing symptoms of digestive issues.
  • 9. Relieves coughs: Comfrey's expectorant and demulcent properties help alleviate coughs, soothe irritated throats, and expel mucus from the respiratory tract.
  • 10. Soothes sore throats: Comfrey's demulcent and anti-inflammatory effects create a soothing, protective layer in the throat, providing relief from pain and irritation.

What are the active constituents of Symphytum officinale (Comfrey)?

Symphytum officinale has several active constituents, such as Allantoin, which is known for its skin-healing and anti-inflammatory properties.

Rosmarinic Acid and Chlorogenic Acid are other constituents that contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects, and have been studied for their potential benefits in treating arthritis. The plant also contains Symphytamine and Symlandine, which have been found to have analgesic and muscle relaxant properties. Additionally, Ajugine, Tannins, Sitosterols, Essential Oil, Phenolic Acids, Flavonoids, Alkaloids, Saponins, Triterpenes, Inulin, Carotenoids, Vitamin C, Calcium, Potassium, and Magnesium are all present in comfrey, contributing to its medicinal uses as a demulcent, astringent, and a mild sedative.

However, it's important to note that some of these constituents, like Pyrrolizidine Alkaloids, can be toxic in high concentrations, so comfrey should only be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

The following list shows the active constituents of Symphytum officinale.

  • 1. Allantoin: A naturally occurring nitrogenous compound with anti-inflammatory and skin-conditioning properties, promoting cell growth and tissue regeneration.
  • 2. Rosmarinic Acid: A phenolic acid with anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, potentially beneficial for wound healing and skin protection.
  • 3. Chlorogenic Acid: A polyphenol with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to overall cell protection and immune function support.
  • 4. Symphytamine: An alkaloid with potential analgesic and sedative effects, although its role in Symphytum officinale is not well-studied.
  • 5. Symlandine: An alkaloid with potential pharmacological effects, although its specific role and benefits in Symphytum officinale are not well-researched.
  • 6. Ajugine: A napthaquinone with potential cytotoxic and antimicrobial properties, contributing to the plant's medicinal applications.
  • 7. Several Tannins: Polyphenolic compounds with astringent properties, helping to tighten, protect, and heal tissues, particularly useful for wound healing and skin conditions.
  • 8. Sitosterols: Plant-based sterols with potential anti-inflammatory and cholesterol-lowering effects, contributing to overall health and well-being.
  • 9. Essential Oil: A complex mixture of volatile compounds with various bioactivities, including antimicrobial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties, enhancing the therapeutic potential of the plant.
  • 10. Phenolic Acids: A class of compounds with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, contributing to overall cellular health and immune function support.

What are the medicinal preparations of Symphytum officinale (Comfrey)?

Symphytum officinale has several medicinal preparations, such as infusions and tinctures which are made by steeping the plant in water or alcohol to extract its active compounds, and can be taken orally for internal ailments.

Decoctions, on the other hand, are made by simmering the plant in water for a longer period of time to extract its roots and bark components, and are often used for their astringent properties. Topical preparations include ointments, creams, poultices, oil extracts, suppositories, liniments, salves, and balms, which are made by combining the plant with a base and applied directly to the skin for localized relief of pain, inflammation, and wounds. Additionally, comfrey can be found in powdered form, encapsulated, or as a syrup, and bath bombs, all of which can be taken orally or used externally depending on the desired effect.

It is important to note that comfrey contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be toxic in large amounts, so it should be used with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

The following list shows the medicinal preparations of Symphytum officinale.

  • 1. Infusion: A medicinal preparation made by steeping Symphytum officinale in boiling water, often used for internal applications to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
  • 2. Tincture: A concentrated extract of Symphytum officinale, prepared by soaking the plant in alcohol, used for various medicinal purposes, including topical treatment of wounds and internally for respiratory issues.
  • 3. Decoction: A strong, concentrated tea made by simmering Symphytum officinale in water, often used to treat digestive issues, skin conditions, and as a general tonic.
  • 4. Ointment: A semi-solid preparation of Symphytum officinale, usually made by combining the plant's active compounds with a base such as petroleum jelly, used for topical application to treat skin conditions and promote healing.
  • 5. Cream: A water-based, emollient preparation of Symphytum officinale, used for topical application to soothe and heal irritated or inflamed skin.
  • 6. Poultice: A soft, moist mass of Symphytum officinale, often combined with a binding agent such as flour, applied directly to the skin to reduce inflammation, promote healing, and alleviate pain.
  • 7. Oil extract: A preparation of Symphytum officinale made by soaking the plant in a carrier oil, used topically to treat skin conditions, promote healing, and soothe inflammation.
  • 8. Suppository: A solid, medicated preparation of Symphytum officinale designed to be inserted into the rectum or vagina for localized treatment of conditions such as hemorrhoids or vaginal inflammation.
  • 9. Liniment: A liquid preparation of Symphytum officinale, often combined with a rubefacient such as alcohol or witch hazel, used topically to increase blood flow, reduce pain, and promote healing.
  • 10. Syrup: A sweet, viscous preparation of Symphytum officinale, made by boiling the plant in a sugar solution, used to treat respiratory ailments, coughs, and colds.

What are the possible side effects of using Symphytum officinale (Comfrey) for medicinal purposes?

Symphytum officinale can have side effects if used improperly.

Consuming the plant in large amounts can lead to nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, dizziness, headache, and sweating. Additionally, it can cause a decrease in appetite, leading to anorexia, and in severe cases, it can result in a coma and weakness.

Moreover, internal use of comfrey can cause liver damage, jaundice, and allergic reactions. Externally, it can lead to contact dermatitis, respiratory distress, and other allergic reactions.

Therefore, it is crucial to use comfrey with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

The following list shows the possible side effects of Symphytum officinale.

  • 1. Nausea: Symphytum officinale may cause stomach discomfort, leading to feelings of wanting to vomit.
  • 2. Vomiting: Symphytum officinale use can result in forceful stomach emptying, often containing food or stomach acid.
  • 3. Diarrhea: This herb may induce loose, watery stools, which can lead to dehydration if prolonged.
  • 4. Abdominal pain: Symphytum officinale might cause stomach discomfort or pain, ranging from mild to severe.
  • 5. Dizziness: Use of this herb can result in lightheadedness or unsteadiness, increasing the risk of falls.
  • 6. Headache: Symphytum officinale may lead to headaches or migraines, causing discomfort and pain.
  • 7. Sweating: This herb can cause increased perspiration, which might be more noticeable during physical exertion or in warm environments.
  • 8. Anorexia: Symphytum officinale use can result in a loss of appetite, potentially leading to unintended weight loss.
  • 9. Coma: In rare cases, excessive consumption of this herb can lead to a comatose state, requiring immediate medical attention.
  • 10. Weakness: Symphytum officinale may cause general weakness or fatigue, reducing physical and mental performance.

What are the precautions to take when using Symphytum officinale (Comfrey) medicinally?

Before using Symphytum officinale, it's important to take some precautions such as consulting a healthcare professional before use, avoiding excessive dosages, and not using during pregnancy.

Discontinue use if allergic reactions occur, and limit consumption of comfrey-based products. Do not use for prolonged periods, avoid using on open wounds or broken skin, and do not use for internal bleeding.

Consult a doctor before using with other medications, and limit exposure to children. Do not use with alcohol, stimulant laxatives, sedatives, or if prone to liver disease. Discontinue use if experiencing stomach pain, if breastfeeding, or if under 18 years old.

Limit exposure to eyes, mucous membranes, and skin during hot weather.

The following list shows the precautions to take when using Symphytum officinale.

  • 1. Consult a healthcare professional before use: It's essential to seek advice from a medical expert to ensure it's safe and suitable for your health condition and to determine the correct dosage.
  • 2. Avoid excessive dosages: Consuming larger amounts than recommended can lead to severe liver damage, so follow the recommended dosage strictly.
  • 3. Do not use during pregnancy: Comfrey contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can harm the fetus and cause liver damage in the mother, making it unsafe for pregnant women.
  • 4. Discontinue use if allergic reactions occur: If you experience symptoms like rashes, itching, or difficulty breathing, stop using comfrey immediately and consult a healthcare professional.
  • 5. Limit consumption of comfrey-based products: Due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids, it's crucial to limit the consumption of comfrey-based products to minimize potential health risks.
  • 6. Do not use for prolonged periods: Prolonged use of comfrey can lead to severe liver damage, so it's essential to follow the recommended usage guidelines.
  • 7. Avoid using on open wounds or broken skin: Comfrey can cause skin irritation and should not be applied directly to open wounds or broken skin.
  • 8. Do not use for internal bleeding: Comfrey can increase blood clotting, which may worsen internal bleeding and should be avoided in such cases.
  • 9. Consult a doctor before using with other medications: Comfrey may interact with other medications, so it's crucial to consult a healthcare professional before combining it with any other drugs.
  • 10. Limit exposure to children: Keep comfrey-based products out of children's reach, as they might accidentally ingest them, leading to potential health risks.

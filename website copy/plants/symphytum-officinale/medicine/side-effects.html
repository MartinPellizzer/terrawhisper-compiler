10 possible side effects of Symphytum officinale (Comfrey) if used improperly

Symphytum officinale, also known as comfrey, has been reported to have potential health side effects if used improperly.

Prolonged use or ingestion of comfrey can cause damage to the liver, as it contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be toxic to the liver. Additionally, topical use of comfrey on broken skin can lead to skin irritation and inflammation.

Pregnant women are advised to avoid using comfrey, as it may stimulate the uterus and lead to premature labor or miscarriage. Furthermore, excessive intake of comfrey can result in nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Therefore, it is essential to use comfrey with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

1. Nausea

Nausea caused by Symphytum Officinale refers to the feeling of stomach discomfort and unease that can occur as a side effect of using this plant.

This symptom is primarily attributed to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which are the main active constituents in comfrey. These alkaloids can be found in various medicinal preparations of the plant, including teas, ointments, and capsules. Although anyone can experience nausea after using comfrey, certain groups of people are more at risk, such as those with a history of liver disease or those who consume large amounts of comfrey over an extended period.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to use comfrey products that are certified to be free of pyrrolizidine alkaloids or to limit their use to short periods of time, under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

2. Vomiting

Vomiting caused by Symphytum Officinale refers to the act of forcefully expelling stomach contents through the mouth as a result of using this plant.

The main active constituents in comfrey that can cause vomiting include pyrrolizidine alkaloids, such as symlandine, intermedine, lycopsamine, and acetyllycopsamine. These compounds can be toxic to the liver and cause gastrointestinal irritation, leading to nausea and vomiting.

Medicinal preparations of comfrey that can cause vomiting if misused include teas, ointments, and capsules made from the leaves, roots, or whole plant. People who are more likely to experience vomiting after using comfrey are those with sensitive stomachs, liver disease, or those who consume large amounts of comfrey over an extended period of time. To avoid vomiting and other side effects, it is important to use comfrey products cautiously and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

The use of comfrey for internal consumption is not recommended due to potential toxicity.

3. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Symphytum Officinale refers to an unpleasant side effect characterized by frequent and watery bowel movements.

This can occur due to the plant's main active constituents, including tannins, phenolic acids, and pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can irritate the gastrointestinal tract and lead to digestive issues. The medicinal preparations of Symphytum Officinale that can cause this side effect if misused include teas, ointments, and capsules, especially when taken in large doses or over extended periods. Individuals who may be more likely to experience diarrhea from Symphytum Officinale include those with sensitive digestive systems, existing gastrointestinal conditions, or those who are allergic to the plant.

To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to follow the recommended dosage and usage instructions carefully, consult with a healthcare professional before using the herb, and avoid consuming large amounts of the plant or using it for extended periods.

The main medicinal preparations of Symphytum officinale that can cause this side effect if misused include teas, ointments, and capsules. People who are more likely to experience this side effect are those with pre-existing liver or gastrointestinal conditions, as well as those who consume large amounts of the plant material over an extended period of time.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to use Symphytum officinale preparations cautiously and under the guidance of a healthcare professional, avoid consuming large amounts of the plant material, and discontinue use if abdominal pain or other digestive symptoms occur.

To avoid anorexia and other side effects of Symphytum Officinale, it is important to use the plant only under the supervision of a healthcare professional, to follow dosage recommendations carefully, and to be aware of the potential risks associated with its use.

9. Coma

Coma caused by Symphytum Officinale refers to a serious side effect where an individual falls into a state of deep unconsciousness.

This occurs due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids, the main active constituents in comfrey, which can cause liver damage and subsequent failure leading to a comatose state. This is most commonly seen when comfrey is prepared in the form of a tincture or infusion and consumed internally.

People who are particularly susceptible to this side effect are those with pre-existing liver conditions, pregnant women, and young children. To avoid this risk, it is crucial to use comfrey externally as a poultice, or ensure that any internal use of comfrey products is limited to short periods of time and under the close supervision of a healthcare professional.

Additionally, always check that any comfrey product has had pyrrolizidine alkaloids carefully extracted or removed, and avoid any product that does not provide such assurance.

10. Weakness

Weakness caused by Symphytum Officinale refers to a state of debility or lack of energy that can occur as a side effect of this herbal remedy.

The main active constituents in Symphytum officinale that can cause this side effect include allantoin, rosmarinic acid, and tannins. This weakness can be particularly prevalent in individuals who consume the plant's leaves or roots in large amounts or over an extended period, as they can damage the liver and weaken the body.

Those who are more likely to experience this side effect include people with pre-existing liver conditions or those who take the herb in conjunction with other medications that may affect liver function. To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to use Symphytum officinale with caution and in moderation.

It is not recommended for internal use over an extended period, and it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before incorporating it into one's wellness routine.

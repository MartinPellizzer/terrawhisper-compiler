What to know before using Melissa officinalis (Lemon Balm)?

Melissa officinalis, also known as lemon balm, is a versatile herb with a variety of medicinal properties.

It has been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and calming effects, making it a popular natural remedy for anxiety, insomnia, and cold sores. In terms of horticultural conditions, Melissa officinalis prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and it is hardy in USDA zones 4-8. It is a perennial herb that grows to a height of 12-36 inches, with small, pinkish-white flowers and oval, serrated leaves that emit a lemon scent when crushed.

The plant's aromatic qualities are due to the presence of essential oils, which are found in the leaves and flowers.

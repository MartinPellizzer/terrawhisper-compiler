What to know before using Thuja occidentalis (Arborvitae)?

Thuja occidentalis, also known as northern white-cedar or arborvitae, is a species of tree native to North America that has been used for medicinal purposes by indigenous peoples for centuries.

Its leaves and twigs contain thujone, a compound with reported anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and diuretic properties, though its use should be approached with caution due to potential toxicity in large amounts. In horticultural settings, Thuja occidentalis is a popular ornamental plant, valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types and conditions, including both sunny and shady locations.

Botanically, Thuja occidentalis is a coniferous evergreen tree that can grow up to 50 feet tall, with scalelike leaves arranged in opposite pairs on branchlets that give the tree a distinctive feathery appearance.

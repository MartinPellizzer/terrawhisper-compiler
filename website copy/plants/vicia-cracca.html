What to know before using Vicia cracca (Cow Vetch)?

Vicia cracca, also known as bird's foot trefoil, is a perennial herb with medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments such as respiratory and gastrointestinal issues. The plant contains bioactive compounds like flavonoids and tannins, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Vicia cracca prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can also tolerate a range of soil types. It is a leguminous plant that can fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial addition to gardens.

Botanically, Vicia cracca has compound leaves with five leaflets and pinkish-red peas-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer.

What to know before using Emblica officinalis (Ndian Gooseberry)?

Emblica officinalis, also known as Indian gooseberry or amla, is a tree that is native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is a vital plant in Ayurvedic medicine due to its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antipyretic properties. The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical regions and prefers well-drained soil and plenty of sunlight.

Emblica officinalis is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 18 meters tall, and it has a crooked trunk, spreading branches, and a dense, round crown. Its leaves are simple, oblong, and dark green, while its small, greenish-white flowers are fragrant and appear in clusters. The tree produces fruits that are round, light green, and contain six vertical stripes.

These fruits are rich in vitamin C and are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

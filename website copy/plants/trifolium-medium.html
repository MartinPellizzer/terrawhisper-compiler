What to know before using Trifolium medium (Mammoth Clover)?

Trifolium medium, also known as medium clover, is a leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is known for its medicinal properties, including its ability to reduce inflammation and speed up wound healing. This plant thrives in well-drained, moderately fertile soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure. Trifolium medium is a low-growing plant, typically reaching a height of 6-12 inches, and features trifoliate leaves and pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer.

It is also commonly used as a cover crop and for forage for livestock.

What are the possible side effects of Trifolium medium?

Despite its many health benefits, Trifolium medium can have side effects, especially when taken in large doses.

These can include stomach upset, diarrhea, and skin rashes. Additionally, its estrogenic activity may interact with hormonal medications or contraceptives. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid using it, as its safety has not been established.

People with bleeding disorders or taking blood-thinning medications should also avoid it due to its coumarin content.

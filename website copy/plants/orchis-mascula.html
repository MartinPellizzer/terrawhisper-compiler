What to know before using Orchis mascula (Early Purple Orchid)?

Orchis mascula, also known as the early purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

This plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine, particularly for treating respiratory and genitourinary conditions. The roots of Orchis mascula contain a variety of chemical compounds, including various sugars, lipids, and alkaloids, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

In horticulture, Orchis mascula prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil and partial shade. It is a perennial herb that grows to around 15-30 cm in height, with a tuberous root system and a spike of purple flowers that bloom in the early spring.

The flowers are characterized by their bilateral symmetry, three sepals, and three petals, with the lower petal modified into a lip.

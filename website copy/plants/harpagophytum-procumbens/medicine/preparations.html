By Martin Pellizzer
1 minutes

Harpagophytum procumbens, also known as devil's claw, is a plant that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, particularly in Southern Africa.

Its roots are used to make various medicinal preparations, such as tisanes and tinctures. A tisane, or herbal tea, is made by steeping dried or fresh devil's claw root in hot water. This soothing beverage is often used to alleviate digestive complaints, such as loss of appetite, nausea, and stomach pain.

A tincture, on the other hand, is a more concentrated extract of the plant, made by soaking the roots in alcohol or glycerin. Tinctures are typically taken in small doses, and are used to treat a wider range of ailments, including arthritis, muscular pain, and fever. Both tisanes and tinctures of Harpagophytum procumbens are believed to have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making them popular natural remedies for a variety of conditions.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new medicinal regimen.

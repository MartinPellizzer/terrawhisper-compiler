10 Best Health Benefits of Harpagophytum procumbens (Devil’S Claw)

Harpagophytum procumbens, commonly known as Devil's Claw, is a plant that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, particularly in Southern Africa.

Its roots and tubers contain several active compounds, including iridoid glycosides, which are believed to have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Some studies suggest that Devil's Claw may be effective in relieving symptoms of osteoarthritis, such as pain and inflammation, and it might also help with lower back pain. Additionally, it may have a positive effect on digestive issues like loss of appetite, heartburn, and digestive disorders.

However, more research is needed to fully understand its medicinal benefits and potential side effects.

1. Reduces inflammation

Devil’s Claw, or Harpagophytum procumbens, reduces inflammation, meaning it can help lessen redness, warmth, swelling, and pain in affected areas of the body.

The main active constituents of Devil’s Claw that contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties are iridoid glycosides, particularly harpagoside. The primary parts of the Harpagophytum procumbens plant that possess this benefit are the secondary tubers, which contain high concentrations of the anti-inflammatory iridoid glycosides. The most common medicinal preparations of Devil’s Claw include dried powdered root, liquid extracts, and capsule or tablet forms, all of which can help alleviate inflammation.

This benefit of Harpagophytum procumbens is particularly helpful in managing health conditions such as osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and lower back pain, all of which involve inflammation and can benefit from Devil’s Claw’s potent anti-inflammatory effects.

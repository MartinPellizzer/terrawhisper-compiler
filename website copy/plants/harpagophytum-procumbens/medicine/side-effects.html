10 possible side effects of Harpagophytum procumbens (Devil’S Claw) if used improperly

Harpagophytum procumbens, also known as devil's claw, is a plant that has been used traditionally for its potential medicinal properties.

However, if used improperly, it can have several potential health side effects. First, it can cause digestive issues, such as nausea, diarrhea, and abdominal pain.

Additionally, it may increase the risk of bleeding, particularly in individuals who are taking blood-thinning medications. Prolonged use of devil's claw can also lead to liver toxicity and interfere with the functioning of certain medications. Pregnant women and individuals with gallstones or ulcers should avoid using devil's claw, as it can worsen these conditions.

It's important to consult with a healthcare professional before taking devil's claw or any other supplement, to ensure it's safe and appropriate for your individual health needs.

1. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Harpagophytum Procumbens refers to a condition of frequent and watery bowel movements that may occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents in Harpagophytum Procumbens that can cause this side effect are iridoid glycosides, particularly harpagoside, which is believed to stimulate intestinal activity. This side effect is most commonly associated with misuse of certain medicinal preparations, such as excessive intake of devil’s claw root extract or use of unstandardized or improperly prepared devil’s claw products.

Individuals who are more likely to experience diarrhea as a side effect of using Harpagophytum Procumbens include those with sensitive digestive systems, those taking high doses of the herb, and those using it for extended periods of time. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of Harpagophytum Procumbens and gradually increase the dosage as needed, while monitoring the body’s response.

Additionally, using standardized or high-quality preparations of the herb and avoiding excessive or prolonged use can help reduce the risk of diarrhea.

2. Nausea

Nausea caused by Harpagophytum Procumbens refers to the feeling of discomfort and restlessness in the stomach that can occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents in Harpagophytum procumbens that can cause nausea include harpogoside, procumbide, and harpagosidic acid. This side effect is most commonly associated with the oral use of Harpagophytum procumbens, particularly in the form of supplements, teas, and tinctures.

Individuals who are more likely to experience nausea as a side effect of using Harpagophytum procumbens include those with a sensitive stomach, those who take large doses, and those who use the herb for extended periods of time. To avoid experiencing nausea when using Harpagophytum procumbens, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase over time, to drink the herb with food, and to avoid taking it on an empty stomach.

Additionally, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using Harpagophytum procumbens to ensure its safe use and to monitor for any potential side effects.

3. Vomiting

This side effect can be traced back to the main active constituents of Harpagophytum procumbens, including iridoid glycosides like harpagoside and procumbside, which can irritate the gastrointestinal lining and stimulate the vomiting reflex. Medicinal preparations such as capsules, tablets, and teas made from devil’s claw roots or extracts, when taken in large quantities or for prolonged periods, could potentially lead to vomiting. Individuals with a higher sensitivity to Harpagophytum procumbens or those with a history of gastrointestinal issues are more likely to experience vomiting as a side effect.

To avoid vomiting caused by Harpagophytum procumbens, it is crucial to follow recommended dosages, consult with a healthcare professional or pharmacist, and stay hydrated during the course of using this supplement.

What to know before using Commiphora molmol (Myrrh)?

Commiphora molmol, also known as myrrh, is a small tree or shrub native to dry, rocky regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It has distinctive pinnate leaves, sharp spines, and produces small white flowers followed by tiny pink fruits. The resin obtained from the bark has been used traditionally for medicinal purposes, including treating wounds, inflammation, and gum diseases. Commiphora molmol thrives in arid conditions, requiring little water and well-draining soil.

It is a slow-growing plant, often reaching only 3-6 meters in height, and can be propagated through seed or cuttings.

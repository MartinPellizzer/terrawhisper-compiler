What to know before using Humulus lupulus (Hops)?

Humulus lupulus, commonly known as hops, is a perennial climbing plant that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, characterized by its distinctive green, cone-shaped flowers.

This plant has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries, and its bitter acids and essential oils have been found to have anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and sedative properties. Humulus lupulus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities, as it can grow up to 25 feet in a single season and its heart-shaped leaves provide attractive cover.

Additionally, the plant's cones are used in the brewing industry to give beer its distinctive flavor and aroma.

What are the planting tips of Humulus lupulus?

When it comes to planting Humulus lupulus, there are a few tips to keep in mind.

It is best to plant hop rhizomes in the early spring, as soon as the soil can be worked. Before planting, soak the rhizomes in water for a few hours to promote germination.

Plant the rhizomes horizontally, about 2 inches below the soil surface, and space them 3-4 feet apart. Water the plants thoroughly after planting and keep the soil consistently moist during the growing season.

Humulus lupulus prefers a trellis or a support system to climb on, so make sure to provide a sturdy structure for the plants to grow on.

What are the harvesting tips of Humulus lupulus?

Harvesting Humulus lupulus requires careful timing and technique.

The hop cones are ready for harvest when they turn papery and light brown, usually in late summer or early fall. To harvest, cut the entire vine and place it in a well-ventilated area to dry. Once the cones are dry, remove them from the vine and store them in an airtight container in a cool, dark place.

Properly stored hops can last for up to a year.

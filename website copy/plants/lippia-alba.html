What to know before using Lippia alba (Lippia)?

Lippia alba, also known as Mexican oregano, is a plant native to Mexico and Central America that has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

This plant has been found to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it useful for treating a variety of ailments including digestive issues, respiratory infections, and skin conditions. Lippia alba thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and can grow up to 3 feet tall. It is an evergreen shrub that produces small, white flowers and has aromatic, narrow leaves that are used both fresh and dried in cooking and herbal medicine.

The plant is drought tolerant and can be propagated through cuttings or seeds.

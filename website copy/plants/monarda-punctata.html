What to know before using Monarda punctata (Horsemint)?

Monarda punctata, also known as horsemint or spotted beebalm, is a member of the Lamiaceae family that is native to North America.

This herbaceous perennial is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in the summer and are attractive to pollinators. Medicinally, Monarda punctata has been used by indigenous peoples for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, and it has been used to treat a variety of ailments including colds, fevers, and digestive issues. In terms of horticultural conditions, Monarda punctata prefers full sun and well-drained soil, and it can grow in a variety of soil types.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and can be propagated through division or seed.

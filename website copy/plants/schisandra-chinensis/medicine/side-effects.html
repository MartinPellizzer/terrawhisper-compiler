10 possible side effects of Schisandra chinensis (Schisandra) if used improperly

Schisandra chinensis, a plant commonly used in traditional medicine, can have negative health consequences if used improperly.

Overconsumption may lead to digestive issues such as stomach pain, diarrhea, and decreased appetite. Additionally, it can cause skin rashes and irritations, as well as respiratory problems in some individuals.

In extreme cases, prolonged use or high doses may result in more severe side effects, including liver toxicity. Pregnant women and those with kidney or liver disorders should avoid using Schisandra chinensis altogether, as it may exacerbate existing conditions.

Always consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement regimen.

1. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Schisandra Chinensis refers to a gastrointestinal side effect characterized by loose, watery stools that occur frequently.

This side effect can be attributed to the main active constituents in Schisandra, including lignans, schizandrins, and essential oils. These components can stimulate the digestive system and increase intestinal peristalsis, leading to diarrhea if misused or consumed in large quantities.

Medicinal preparations of Schisandra, such as decoctions, powders, and capsules, can cause diarrhea if taken in excessive doses or for extended periods. People who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with sensitive digestive systems, individuals with a history of gastrointestinal disorders, and those who consume Schisandra on an empty stomach. To avoid diarrhea, it is crucial to follow recommended dosages, start with small doses and gradually increase, and avoid taking Schisandra on an empty stomach.

Additionally, those with gastrointestinal sensitivities should consult a healthcare professional before using Schisandra.

2. Stomach Upset

Stomach upset caused by Schisandra Chinensis refers to a range of unpleasant symptoms such as stomach ache, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

The main active constituents of Schisandra Chinensis that can cause this side effect are schisandrins, schisandrols, and schisantherins. These constituents can stimulate the gastrointestinal tract, leading to stomach discomfort.

The main medicinal preparations of Schisandra Chinensis that can cause this side effect if misused include decoctions, powders, and capsules. Individuals who are sensitive to Schisandra Chinensis or have a history of gastrointestinal conditions are more likely to experience stomach upset after using this herb. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase it over time, and to avoid taking Schisandra Chinensis on an empty stomach.

Additionally, individuals should consult with a healthcare provider before using Schisandra Chinensis, especially if they have a history of gastrointestinal conditions or are taking other medications.

3. Stomach Pain

Stomach pain caused by Schisandra Chinensis refers to discomfort or pain in the abdominal area that may occur after ingesting this plant.

The main active constituents in Schisandra Chinensis that can cause this side effect are lignans, particularly Schisandrins A, B, and C. The primary medicinal preparations of Schisandra Chinensis that can cause stomach pain if misused include decoctions, powder, and tinctures.

Individuals with a history of gastrointestinal disorders, such as irritable bowel syndrome or inflammatory bowel disease, may be more likely to experience stomach pain after using Schisandra Chinensis. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase the amount, and to avoid taking Schisandra Chinensis on an empty stomach or in large amounts.

Additionally, it is essential to consult a healthcare provider before using Schisandra Chinensis, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or who are taking other medications.

The main active constituents of Schisandra Chinensis that can cause this side effect are lignans, particularly schisandrins and gomisins. These compounds can affect the digestive system and reduce hunger signals to the brain.

The main medicinal preparations of Schisandra Chinensis that can cause this side effect if misused include decoctions, powdered extracts, and capsules. Individuals who are sensitive to Schisandra or have a history of digestive issues may be more likely to experience decreased appetite as a side effect. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of Schisandra Chinensis and gradually increase the dosage over time.

Furthermore, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare professional before using Schisandra Chinensis, especially if one has a history of digestive issues or is taking other medications.

5. Skin Rash

Skin rash caused by Schisandra Chinensis refers to an uncomfortable skin condition characterized by redness, itching, and irritation that may occur after using schisandra.

This side effect can be attributed mainly to lignans, particularly schisantherin and schisandrin, which are the primary active constituents of schisandra. Improper use of schisandra preparations, such as decoctions, powder, and capsules, can lead to this side effect.

Individuals who are allergic to schisandra or have a history of skin reactions are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dosage and gradually increase it, and discontinue use immediately if any skin reaction occurs.

Additionally, it is crucial to consult a healthcare professional before taking schisandra, especially for those with a history of skin conditions or allergies.

6. Itching

Itching caused by Schisandra Chinensis refers to the uncomfortable and irritating sensation on the skin that can occur as a side effect of using this plant.

The main active constituents in Schisandra Chinensis that have been associated with this side effect are Schisandrins and Schisantherin. These components can cause histamine release, leading to itching.

The main medicinal preparations of Schisandra Chinensis that can cause itching if misused include decoctions, powders, and capsules. Individuals who are more likely to experience itching as a side effect of using Schisandra Chinensis include those with a history of allergic reactions to plants and those with sensitive skin. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase, and to discontinue use if itching occurs.

7. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Schisandra Chinensis refers to a feeling of lightheadedness, unsteadiness, or wooziness that may occur in some individuals who consume this herb.

The main active constituents in Schisandra Chinensis that can cause this side effect are lignans, particularly schisantherin, schisandrin, and deoxyschisandrin. These constituents can affect the central nervous system, leading to dizziness if taken in large quantities or when misused.

Medicinal preparations of Schisandra Chinensis that can cause this side effect if misused include decoctions, powders, capsules, and tinctures. People who are more likely to experience dizziness from Schisandra Chinensis include those with a history of motion sickness, low blood pressure, or those who consume large amounts of the herb in a short period.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow recommended dosages, start with small doses and gradually increase, and avoid operating heavy machinery or driving after consuming the herb.

8. Dry Mouth

Dry mouth caused by Schisandra Chinensis refers to a condition where there is insufficient saliva production in the mouth, leading to symptoms such as difficulty swallowing, speaking, and altered taste.

The main active constituents in Schisandra Chinensis that can cause this side effect are lignans, particularly schisandrin, deoxyschisandrin, and gomisin A. The medicinal preparations of Schisandra Chinensis that can cause dry mouth if misused include decoctions, granules, capsules, and liquid extracts. Individuals who are more likely to experience dry mouth from Schisandra Chinensis include those with salivary gland dysfunction, Sjogren's syndrome, or those who take medications that cause dry mouth as a side effect.

To avoid dry mouth when using Schisandra Chinensis, it is important to stay adequately hydrated, avoid using Schisandra Chinensis in large doses, and consult a healthcare professional before using Schisandra Chinensis if you have a history of dry mouth or salivary gland dysfunction.

9. Inflammation Of The Mouth And Tongue

Inflammation of the mouth and tongue caused by Schisandra Chinensis refers to the swelling, redness, and pain in the mouth and tongue that can result from using this herb.

The main active constituents of Schisandra Chinensis that can cause this side effect are schisandrins, schisantherin, and schizandronic acid. This side effect is most commonly associated with preparations such as decoctions, tea, and powder made from the fruit or seeds of the Schisandra Chinensis plant. Individuals who are more likely to experience this side effect include those who are allergic to Schisandra Chinensis, have a history of mouth sores or inflammation, or are taking other medications that may interact with Schisandra Chinensis.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to use Schisandra Chinensis under the guidance of a healthcare professional, start with a low dose and gradually increase, and discontinue use if any symptoms of mouth or tongue inflammation occur.

10. Respiratory Effects

Respiratory effects caused by Schisandra Chinensis refer to the potential impact of this plant on the respiratory system.

The main active constituents of Schisandra chinensis that can cause this side effect are lignans, particularly schisantherin A, schisantherin B, and schisandrin. These constituents can affect the respiratory system by causing constriction of the airways, which can lead to difficulty breathing.

The main medicinal preparations of Schisandra chinensis that can cause this side effect if misused include decoctions, powders, and capsules. Individuals who are more likely to experience respiratory effects from Schisandra chinensis include those with pre-existing respiratory conditions, such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), as well as those who are sensitive or allergic to this plant. To avoid this side effect, it is important to use Schisandra chinensis under the guidance of a qualified healthcare practitioner and to follow dosage instructions carefully.

Additionally, it is important to discontinue use and seek medical attention immediately if any respiratory symptoms occur.

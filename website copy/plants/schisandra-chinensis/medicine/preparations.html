Schisandra chinensis, also known as the five-flavor-fruit, is a popular herb in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its medicinal preparations typically include tisanes and tinctures, which are made from the fruit, seeds, and sometimes the root of the plant. A tisane, or herbal tea, is made by steeping dried or fresh Schisandra chinensis fruit in hot water.

This preparation is often used to treat a variety of ailments, including liver disease, coughs, and insomnia. Tinctures, on the other hand, are made by soaking the fruit, seeds, or root in alcohol for several weeks. This preparation is thought to extract the medicinal compounds from the plant more effectively, and is often used as a tonic to boost energy and improve overall health.

Both preparations are believed to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and adaptogenic properties, making them popular in traditional medicine for promoting overall health and well-being.

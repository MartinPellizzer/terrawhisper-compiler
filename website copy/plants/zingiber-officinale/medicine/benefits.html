10 Best Health Benefits of Zingiber officinale (Ginger)

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been used for centuries in various forms of traditional/alternative medicine to treat a variety of ailments.

Its most notable medicinal benefit is its ability to alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal distress, such as nausea, vomiting, and bloating. Ginger contains active compounds called gingerols and shogaols, which are believed to exert these soothing effects by relaxing the smooth muscle of the gastrointestinal tract and acting as a natural anti-inflammatory agent.

Furthermore, ginger has been shown to reduce inflammation and pain associated with conditions like osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis. Additionally, some studies suggest that ginger may help lower cholesterol levels, potentially reducing the risk of heart disease.

However, more research is needed to fully understand the extent of ginger's medicinal benefits and its optimal therapeutic applications.

1. Alleviates inflammation

Ginger alleviates inflammation, meaning it can help reduce redness, swelling, and pain caused by inflammatory conditions.

The main active constituents in ginger that contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects are gingerols and shogaols. The primary parts of the ginger plant that contain these beneficial compounds are the rhizomes or underground stems. The most common medicinal preparations of ginger that are used to alleviate inflammation include dried or fresh ginger root, ginger extract, ginger oil, and ginger tea.

The main health conditions that this benefit of ginger helps relieve include osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and muscle pain.

Zingiber officinale alleviates inflammation thanks to the active constituents listed below.

  • Gingerols
  • Shogaols
  • Paradols
  • Zingerone
  • Gingerdiols
  • Gingerdiones
  • Bisabolene
  • Farnesene
  • Curcumene
  • Camphene

What to know before using Catha edulis (Khat)?

Catha edulis, commonly known as khat, is a plant that has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries in Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

Its leaves and young shoots contain alkaloids such as cathinone and cathine, which are believed to have stimulant and euphoric effects, and are used to treat ailments such as fatigue, depression, and anxiety. Catha edulis thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, typically grown at elevations between 1500-2500 meters.

It is an evergreen shrub that can grow up to 20 feet tall, with glossy green leaves and clusters of small white or pink flowers. The plant requires well-drained soil and plenty of sunlight for optimal growth.

Despite its medicinal uses, Catha edulis is considered a controlled substance in many countries due to its potential for abuse and negative health effects when consumed in large quantities.

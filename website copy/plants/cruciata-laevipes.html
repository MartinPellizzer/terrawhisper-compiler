What to know before using Cruciata laevipes (Smooth Bedstraw)?

Cruciata laevipes, also known as crosswort or field chickweed, is a plant with a rich history of medicinal use.

Its leaves and stems contain rosmarinic acid, which has been found to have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it a valuable resource in herbal medicine for treating conditions such as eczema, wounds, and respiratory infections. Cruciata laevipes thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soil and prefers partial shade to full sun. It is a perennial herb that grows to a height of 15-30 cm, with opposite, linear leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant's square stems and paired leaves are characteristic of the mint family, to which it belongs.

What are the medicinal uses of Cruciata laevipes?

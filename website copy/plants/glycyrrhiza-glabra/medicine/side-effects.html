10 possible side effects of Glycyrrhiza glabra (Licorice) if used improperly

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice, can have several negative health effects if used improperly.

First, excessive consumption can lead to potassium depletion, potentially resulting in high blood pressure, edema, and even heart problems. Additionally, licorice may interfere with certain medications, such as diuretics, digoxin, and warfarin, leading to adverse drug interactions.

Prolonged use of large amounts can also cause pseudoaldosteronism, a condition that affects the body's electrolyte balance, resulting in symptoms such as muscle weakness, fatigue, and heart rhythm abnormalities. In some cases, it may even lead to more serious complications like kidney failure and seizures. Pregnant women should avoid licorice altogether, as it can induce premature labor or affect fetal development.

Always consult a healthcare professional before using licorice for medicinal purposes.

1. Hypertension

Hypertension caused by Glycyrrhiza Glabra refers to an elevation in blood pressure due to the regular consumption or misuse of this herb.

The main active constituents in Glycyrrhiza glabra that can cause hypertension are glycyrrhizin and its derivatives, which can disrupt the body's sodium-potassium balance and lead to an increase in blood pressure. The main medicinal preparations of Glycyrrhiza glabra that can cause hypertension if misused include licorice root, licorice extract, and licorice powder, which are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. People who are more likely to experience hypertension from Glycyrrhiza glabra include those with a history of high blood pressure, kidney or liver disease, and those who consume large amounts of licorice over an extended period of time.

To avoid hypertension from Glycyrrhiza glabra, it is essential to use this herb in moderation, follow recommended dosages, and consult with a healthcare professional before using it for medicinal purposes, particularly for those with pre-existing health conditions or those taking prescription medications.

2. Hypokalemia

Hypokalemia caused by Glycyrrhiza Glabra refers to an electrolyte imbalance characterized by abnormally low potassium levels in the blood.

This side effect is primarily triggered by the compound glycyrrhizin, a key active constituent in licorice. The main medicinal preparations of licorice that can cause hypokalemia if misused include licorice root extracts, licorice candies, and certain traditional herbal remedies.

Individuals who are at a higher risk of developing this side effect include those with pre-existing kidney or heart conditions, as well as those who consume excessive amounts of licorice over an extended period. To avoid hypokalemia, it is essential to use licorice-based products in moderation, follow recommended dosages, and discontinue use if any symptoms of hypokalemia appear.

Regularly monitoring potassium levels and consulting with a healthcare professional before using licorice-based remedies can also help mitigate the risk of hypokalemia.

3. Headaches

Headaches caused by Glycyrrhiza Glabra refers to a possible side effect characterized by pain or discomfort in the head region.

This effect is primarily attributed to the triterpenoid saponin glycyrrhizin, which is one of the main active constituents of Glycyrrhiza glabra. This compound can cause headaches when found in high concentrations, particularly in medicinal preparations such as licorice root extract, licorice root powder, and licorice root tea.

People who are more likely to experience headaches as a side effect of using Glycyrrhiza glabra include those who consume large amounts of licorice products, have a history of migraines, or are sensitive to the compound. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to use Glycyrrhiza glabra in moderation, follow recommended dosages, and consult a healthcare professional before using it for therapeutic purposes.

Additionally, individuals with a history of high blood pressure or kidney disease should avoid using Glycyrrhiza glabra altogether, as it can exacerbate these conditions.

4. Fatigue

Fatigue caused by Glycyrrhiza Glabra refers to a state of extreme tiredness and weakness that can occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents of Glycyrrhiza glabra that can cause this side effect are glycyrrhizin and its derivatives, which can affect the body's hormonal and metabolic processes. The main medicinal preparations of Glycyrrhiza glabra that can cause this side effect if misused include licorice root extract, licorice root tea, and licorice root powder.

People who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with a history of heart disease, kidney disease, liver disease, and high blood pressure, as well as pregnant women and elderly individuals. To avoid this side effect, it is important to use Glycyrrhiza glabra in moderation and under the guidance of a healthcare professional, and to avoid consuming large amounts of licorice or licorice-derived products for extended periods of time.

Additionally, it is important to monitor one's health closely while using Glycyrrhiza glabra and to discontinue use if any signs of fatigue or other adverse effects occur.

5. Psychological Disorders

Psychological disorders caused by Glycyrrhiza Glabra refers to a range of mental health issues that can arise from the misuse of this plant.

The main active constituents in Glycyrrhiza glabra that can cause this side effect are glycyrrhizin and its metabolite, glycyrrhetic acid. These compounds can disrupt the balance of steroid hormones in the body, leading to changes in mood and behavior.

The main medicinal preparations of Glycyrrhiza glabra that can cause this side effect if misused include licorice root extract, licorice root powder, and licorice root tea. People who are more likely to experience psychological disorders from using Glycyrrhiza glabra include those with a history of mental health issues, as well as those who consume large amounts of licorice products over an extended period of time. To avoid this side effect, it is important to use Glycyrrhiza glabra in moderation, follow recommended dosages, and consult with a healthcare provider before using licorice for medicinal purposes.

Additionally, people with a history of mental health issues should avoid using licorice products altogether unless under the close supervision of a healthcare provider.

6. Muscle Weakness Or Cramps

Muscle weakness or cramps caused by Glycyrrhiza Glabra refers to a possible side effect where individuals may experience discomfort or pain in their muscles, as well as a reduction in muscle strength.

This can occur due to the main active constituents in licorice, namely glycyrrhizic acid and its derivatives, which can disrupt the body's electrolyte balance and lead to muscle cramping and weakness. These adverse effects are most commonly associated with high doses or prolonged use of licorice, particularly in the form of licorice root extract, licorice tea, or licorice tincture.

People who are more likely to experience these side effects include those with pre-existing kidney or heart conditions, pregnant women, and individuals who consume large amounts of licorice on a regular basis. To avoid these side effects, it is recommended to use licorice supplements with caution, avoid consuming excessive amounts, and consult a healthcare professional before using licorice for medicinal purposes.

Additionally, staying hydrated and maintaining a balanced diet can help reduce the risk of muscle weakness or cramps caused by licorice.

7. Heart Problems

Heart problems caused by Glycyrrhiza Glabra refers to the potential cardiovascular issues that can arise from the use of this plant.

The main active constituents that can cause this side effect are glycyrrhizin and its derivatives, which can lead to an increase in sodium and water retention, as well as lowering potassium levels in the body. The main medicinal preparations of licorice that can cause heart problems if misused include teas, extracts, and supplements.

People who are at a higher risk of experiencing this side effect are those with pre-existing heart conditions, high blood pressure, kidney problems, and those who consume large amounts of licorice over a long period of time. To avoid heart problems when using licorice, it is recommended to use products that are labeled as "deglycyrrhized" or "DGL," which means that the glycyrrhizin has been removed.

In addition, it is important to follow the recommended dosages and consult with a healthcare professional before using licorice, especially for those who have pre-existing medical conditions or are taking medication.

8. Kidney Damage

Kidney damage caused by Glycyrrhiza Glabra refers to the harmful effects on the kidneys that can occur with the use of this plant.

The main active constituents of Glycyrrhiza glabra that can cause this side effect are glycyrrhizin and its derivatives, which can cause retention of sodium and water and increased excretion of potassium, leading to a condition called hypokalemic nephropathy. The main medicinal preparations of Glycyrrhiza glabra that can cause this side effect if misused include licorice root, licorice powder, and licorice extract.

People who are more likely to experience kidney damage from Glycyrrhiza glabra are those with pre-existing kidney disease, hypertension, and those who consume large amounts of licorice over a long period of time. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to use licorice products with caution, under the supervision of a healthcare professional, and to avoid consuming large amounts of licorice or using it for extended periods of time.

Additionally, people with pre-existing kidney disease or hypertension should avoid using licorice altogether.

9. Hormonal Imbalances

Hormonal imbalances caused by Glycyrrhiza Glabra refers to an upset of the body's natural hormonal levels resulting from the use of this plant.

The main active constituents of Glycyrrhiza glabra that can cause this side effect are glycyrrhizin and its derivatives, which can interfere with the body's corticosteroid metabolism. Preparations of Glycyrrhiza glabra that can cause hormonal imbalances if misused include licorice root extract, licorice root powder, and licorice root tea. Individuals who are at a higher risk of experiencing this side effect are those with a history of hormone-sensitive conditions, such as endometriosis, uterine fibroids, and breast cancer.

To avoid hormonal imbalances, it is recommended to use Glycyrrhiza glabra with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional, who can monitor dosage and potential interactions with other medications or health conditions.

10. Digestive Issues

Digestive issues caused by Glycyrrhiza Glabra refers to problems in the gastrointestinal system such as stomach pain, heartburn, and bloating.

These issues are primarily caused by the triterpenoid glycyrrhizin and its derivatives, which can disrupt the balance of minerals in the body and lead to impaired digestion. Preparations like licorice root tea, supplements, and extracts are the most likely to cause digestive problems if misused or consumed in excess.

People who are more susceptible to these side effects include those with a history of digestive problems, high blood pressure, or kidney issues. To avoid digestive issues, it is recommended to consume licorice in moderation, stay hydrated, and avoid using it for extended periods of time.

Additionally, it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before using licorice for medicinal purposes.

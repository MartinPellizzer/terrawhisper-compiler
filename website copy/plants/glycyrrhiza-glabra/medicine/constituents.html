10 Best Active Constituents of Glycyrrhiza glabra (Licorice)

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice, contains several active constituents that are used for medicinal purposes.

The most notable of these is glycyrrhizin, which is responsible for the sweet taste of licorice and has been shown to have anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and antiviral properties. Other active constituents include flavonoids such as liquiritin and isoliquiritigenin, which have been found to have antioxidant, anti-cancer, and anti-microbial properties.

Additionally, coumarins such as umbelliferone and esculin, which have been found to have anti-coagulant and anti-tumor properties, are also present in licorice. The active constituents of licorice are often used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of conditions, including respiratory ailments, digestive issues, and skin conditions.

However, it is important to note that excessive consumption of licorice can have adverse effects, and it should be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

1. Glycyrrhizin

The glycyrrhizin contained in Glycyrrhiza Glabra is a triterpenoid saponin that is the primary active component of the plant's root.

Its main medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and expectorant effects. Glycyrrhizin has been shown to help relieve conditions such as peptic ulcers, bronchitis, and viral infections like hepatitis. This constituent is found mainly in the root of the Glycyrrhiza glabra plant.

Improper use of glycyrrhizin can lead to several side effects, including hypertension, edema, and potassium depletion, due to its ability to inhibit the enzyme 11-beta-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase type 2.

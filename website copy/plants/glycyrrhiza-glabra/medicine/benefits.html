10 Best Health Benefits of Glycyrrhiza glabra (Licorice)

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to its wide range of medicinal benefits.

One of the key active compounds in licorice, glycyrrhizin, has been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects, making it a potential treatment for conditions such as chronic gastritis and ulcerative colitis. Additionally, licorice has been found to have antiviral properties, and has been used in the treatment of hepatitis C and HIV.

Licorice extract has also been shown to have a soothing effect on the gastrointestinal tract, and can be used to treat symptoms of heartburn, indigestion, and stomach ulcers. Furthermore, licorice has been found to have expectorant and demulcent properties, making it a useful treatment for respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and asthma.

However, it is important to note that excessive consumption of licorice can lead to serious side effects, such as high blood pressure and hormonal imbalances, so it should be used with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

1. Alleviates stress-induced stomach issues

Licorice alleviates stress-induced stomach issues, meaning it can help reduce stomach problems caused by mental or emotional stress.

The main active constituents of Glycyrrhiza glabra that provide this benefit are glycyrrhizin and its derivatives, which have been shown to inhibit the production of cortisol, a stress hormone that can contribute to stomach issues. The primary parts of the Glycyrrhiza glabra plant that give this benefit are the roots and rhizomes, which contain the highest concentration of glycyrrhizin. The main medicinal preparations of Glycyrrhiza glabra that give this benefit are deglycyrrhizinated licorice (DGL), which is a form of licorice that has had the glycyrrhizin removed to reduce its side effects, and licorice root extract.

The main health conditions that this benefit of Glycyrrhiza glabra helps relieve include functional dyspepsia, gastritis, and gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD).

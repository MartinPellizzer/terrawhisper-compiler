10 Best Active Constituents of Elettaria cardamomum (Cardamom)

Elettaria cardamomum, commonly known as cardamom, has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits due to its active constituents.

The main bioactive compounds in cardamom include volatile oils, such as alpha-terpinene, beta-pinenene, and limonene, which have been found to possess anti-oxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Cardamom also contains various phenolic compounds, including caffeic acid and rosmarinic acid, which have been shown to exhibit potent antioxidant and anti-cancer activities.

Additionally, cardamom contains several essential nutrients, such as magnesium, zinc, and manganese, which are essential for various physiological functions and overall health. The active constituents in cardamom have been shown to provide several medicinal benefits, including improving digestion, reducing inflammation, and providing relief from asthma and bronchitis.

However, further research is needed to fully understand the therapeutic potential of cardamom and its active constituents.

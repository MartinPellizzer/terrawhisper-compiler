What to know before using Hydrocotyle vulgaris (Marsh Pennywort)?

Hydrocotyle vulgaris, also known as marsh pennywort, is a low-growing perennial plant found in damp or wet locations.

Its small, round, and shiny leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it produces tiny, white flowers in summer. Medicinally, marsh pennywort has been used for its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties, making it a popular choice for topical treatments. The plant prefers partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, and it can be propagated through division or cuttings.

However, it can become invasive in some areas due to its aggressive spreading habits.

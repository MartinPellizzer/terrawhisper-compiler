What to know before using Hieracium umbellatum (Narrow-Leaf Hawkweed)?

Hieracium umbellatum, also known as the star-hawkweed, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, now naturalized in other regions including North America.

It is known for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, and has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, from skin conditions to digestive issues. In horticultural settings, Hieracium umbellatum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and can tolerate a range of soil types from sandy to clay. Botanically, Hieracium umbellatum is characterized by its star-shaped inflorescence, clusters of bright yellow flowers, and hairy, lobed leaves.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and, like other hawkweeds, reproduces both sexually and asexually, through seeds and underground stems called rhizomes.

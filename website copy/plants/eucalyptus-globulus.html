What to know before using Eucalyptus globulus (Eucalyptus)?

Eucalyptus globulus, also known as blue gum eucalyptus, is a species of tree native to southeastern Australia that is well-known for its medicinal properties and distinctive aroma.

The leaves and oil of this evergreen tree are used in a variety of medicinal applications, including treatments for respiratory ailments such as asthma, bronchitis, and coughs, as well as a variety of other conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and expectorant properties. Eucalyptus globulus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and can grow up to 150 feet tall in its native habitat, although it usually reaches heights of 60-100 feet in cultivation.

This species is characterized by its long, narrow, blue-green leaves, which are covered in a waxy bloom that gives them a distinctive bluish tint, and its straight, smooth, grayish-brown bark that peels away in ribbons.

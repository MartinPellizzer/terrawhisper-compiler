What to know before using Scutellaria galericulata (Marsh Scullcap)?

Scutellaria galericulata, also known as skullcap, is a valuable medicinal plant that has been used for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

This herbaceous perennial thrives in moist to wet soil conditions, making it a popular choice for gardeners looking to create a wetland or bog garden. Scutellaria galericulata typically grows to be around 1-2 feet tall, and features a square stem and distinctly lobed leaves. Its small blue-violet flowers are two-lipped and bloom in spikes, making it an attractive addition to any garden.

The plant's aerial parts are used in herbal medicine, and it is believed to have a positive effect on a variety of health conditions, including anxiety, insomnia, and inflammation.

What are the medicinal uses of Scutellaria galericulata?

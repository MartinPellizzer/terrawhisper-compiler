What to know before using Thymus pulegioides (Large Thyme)?

Thymus pulegioides, also known as woolly savory, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and its compact growth makes it suitable for rock gardens or as groundcover. Medicinally, Thymus pulegioides has been used for its antiseptic, antifungal, and expectorant properties.

Its small, pink or white flowers and narrow, gray-green leaves contribute to its ornamental value, while its aromatic qualities make it a popular addition to culinary dishes and herbal teas.

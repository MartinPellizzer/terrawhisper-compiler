What to know before using Euphorbia hirta (Pill-Bearing Spurge)?

Euphorbia hirta, also known as hairy spurge, is a herbaceous plant that is native to tropical and subtropical regions around the world.

It is an annual plant that can grow up to 70 cm tall, with hairy stems and narrow, oblong leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, and its sap is white and milky. Euphorbia hirta has a number of medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and antimicrobial effects.

It has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including respiratory infections, skin conditions, and gastrointestinal disorders. In horticultural settings, Euphorbia hirta prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is relatively drought tolerant and can be propagated by seed or stem cuttings.

However, it can be invasive in some areas, so it is important to monitor its growth and control its spread.

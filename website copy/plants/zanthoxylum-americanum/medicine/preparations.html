By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Zanthoxylum americanum, also known as Eastern prickly ash, has a long history of use in medicinal preparations.

One common preparation is a tisane, or herbal tea, made by steeping the bark or berries in hot water. This tisane is often used to treat digestive issues, as it is thought to stimulate digestive enzymes and improve circulation.

A tincture can also be made by soaking the bark or berries in alcohol, which extracts the plant's active compounds. This tincture can be taken orally to alleviate symptoms of digestive disorders, nerve pain, and rheumatism. It is important to note that while Zanthoxylum americanum has been used traditionally for medicinal purposes, its effectiveness and safety have not been thoroughly studied and it should be used with caution.

As with any herbal remedy, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before use.

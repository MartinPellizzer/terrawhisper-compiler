What to know before using Ficus benghalensis (Banyan Tree)?

Ficus benghalensis, commonly known as the banyan tree, is a species of fig tree that is native to India and has significant cultural and medicinal value.

Medicinally, all parts of the tree are used in various traditional medicine systems, such as Ayurveda, for treating ailments ranging from skin diseases and diarrhea to diabetes and respiratory disorders. The tree thrives in a range of horticultural conditions, from well-drained sandy loams to heavy clays, and can tolerate both drought and mild flooding.

Botanically, Ficus benghalensis is known for its aerial roots, which grow from the branches and can reach the ground, taking root and forming additional trunks, allowing the tree to spread over a large area.

What to know before using Larix decidua (Larch)?

Larix decidua, also known as the European larch, is a species of tree with notable medicinal properties, including the ability to alleviate respiratory ailments and boost the immune system, due to its high content of polyphenols and vitamin C.

This tree is hardy and adaptable, thriving in a variety of horticultural conditions, such as full sun and well-drained soil, but it can also tolerate partial shade and various soil types. Larix decidua is a deciduous conifer, shedding its needles annually, and can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a broad conical shape and a lifespan of up to 300 years.

Its bark is typically a reddish-brown color and its leaves are bright green, turning yellow in the fall.

What to know before using Succisa pratensis (Premorse)?

Succisa pratensis, also known as devil's bit, is a herbaceous perennial plant that belongs to the Dipsacaceae family.

It has various medicinal properties, including being used as an expectorant, anti-inflammatory, and diuretic agent. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Succisa pratensis has distinctive pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer and grows to be around 30-60 centimeters tall. Its stems are slender and grooved, while its leaves are lance-shaped and serrated.

The plant's name, "devil's bit," comes from the legend that the devil bit off the root of the plant in anger when it found out that the plant had medicinal properties.

What are the medicinal uses of Succisa pratensis?

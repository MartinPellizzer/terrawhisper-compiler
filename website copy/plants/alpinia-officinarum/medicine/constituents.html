10 Best Active Constituents of Alpinia officinarum (Galangal)

Alpinia officinarum, also known as lesser galangal, contains several active constituents that have been used in medicinal purposes for centuries.

The most notable of these compounds is a group of chemicals called phenylpropanoids, which include methyl cinnamate, cinnamic acid, and cinnamaldehyde. These compounds have been found to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and analgesic properties, making them useful in the treatment of conditions such as arthritis, muscle pain, and digestive disorders.

Additionally, Alpinia officinarum contains several flavonoids, such as quercetin and luteolin, which have been shown to have anti-cancer and neuroprotective effects. The plant also contains essential oils, such as 1,8-cineole and alpha-pinene, which have been found to have antimicrobial and antifungal properties.

Overall, the active constituents of Alpinia officinarum make it a valuable medicinal plant with a wide range of potential therapeutic applications.

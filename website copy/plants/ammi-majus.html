What to know before using Ammi majus (Bishop'S Weed)?

Ammi majus, also known as Bishop's flower or Queen Anne's lace, is a popular ornamental plant with delicate, lacy flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. The plant contains various chemical compounds with medicinal properties, such as coumarins, flavonoids, and polyacetylenes.

These compounds have been found to have anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anticancer effects. In horticulture, Ammi majus is valued for its airy, white flowers that add texture and contrast to gardens and flower arrangements. The plant can grow up to 4 feet tall and has finely divided, fern-like leaves.

It is also attractive to beneficial insects, such as ladybugs and lacewings, making it a great addition to any pollinator garden.

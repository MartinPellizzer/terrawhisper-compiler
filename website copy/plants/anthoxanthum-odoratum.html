It is often used as a groundcover or in wildflower meadows due to its ability to spread and form dense mats. Botanically, Anthoxanthum odoratum is a perennial grass that grows to be around 20-40 centimeters tall.

It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, dense spikes of flowers in the late spring and early summer.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of bodies of water, and can grow up to 15 feet tall. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces tall, feathery plumes that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Phragmites australis prefers full sun and can tolerate a wide range of soil types, from clay to sand, as long as the soil is consistently moist.

When harvested and prepared properly, the plant's rhizomes and shoots are used to treat a variety of ailments, including fever, cough, and joint pain.

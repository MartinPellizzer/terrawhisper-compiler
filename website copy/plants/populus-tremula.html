What to know before using Populus tremula (European Aspen)?

Populus tremula, also known as the European Aspen, is a species of tree well-known for its medicinal properties.

Its bark and leaves contain salicin, a chemical similar to aspirin, which has been used to treat fever, inflammation, and pain. This tree is hardy and adaptable, thriving in various horticultural conditions, including both sunny and partially shaded areas, and a wide range of soil types. Populus tremula, or quaking aspen, is a deciduous tree that grows between 15-25 meters tall, with smooth, greenish-white bark that becomes darker and furrowed with age.

Its leaves, which tremble in the slightest breeze, are round with serrated edges and turn a beautiful golden color in the fall.

What are the medicinal uses of Populus tremula?

Populus tremula is used to treat various medical conditions due to its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic properties.

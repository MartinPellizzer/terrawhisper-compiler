What to know before using Centella asiatica (Gotu Kola)?

Centella asiatica, also known as gotu kola, is a creeping herbaceous plant found primarily in wetlands of Asia.

It boasts a range of medicinal properties, making it a popular component in traditional medicine across the world. The plant contains active compounds such as triterpenoids, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

Centella asiatica thrives in humid and shady environments, often found in and near water sources. Its botanical characteristics include small, fan-shaped green leaves with a smooth texture and a weak, fibrous root system.

This plant is not only valued for its medicinal uses but also for its ability to survive in various horticultural conditions, making it a versatile and beneficial addition to any garden or ecosystem.

What are the possible side effects of Centella asiatica?

Centella asiatica can have some side effects if used improperly, such as stomach upset and digestive discomfort, which may occur if the herb is taken in large doses.

Long-term use of high doses of Centella asiatica may lead to headaches, dizziness, and a feeling of tiredness or drowsiness. Additionally, it can potentially interact with certain medications, such as sedatives, anticoagulants, and drugs metabolized by the liver, leading to adverse effects or reduced efficacy of these medications.

Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using Centella asiatica, as its safety during these periods has not been established.

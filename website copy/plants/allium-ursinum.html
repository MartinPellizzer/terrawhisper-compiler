What to know before using Allium ursinum (Ramsons)?

Allium ursinum, also known as wild garlic or ramsons, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Europe and Asia.

It boasts star-shaped white flowers and long, narrow leaves, growing in damp, woodland areas. In horticulture, Allium ursinum thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it an ideal choice for shady gardens. Medicinally, wild garlic has been used for centuries for its antimicrobial, antiviral, and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its active compounds, such as allicin, have been shown to support cardiovascular health, boost the immune system, and even exhibit potential cancer-fighting abilities.

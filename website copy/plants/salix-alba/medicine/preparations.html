Salix alba, commonly known as the white willow, has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries.

Its bark is the primary source of medicinal preparations, containing the active compound salicin, which is a natural analog of aspirin. A tisane, or herbal tea, can be made by steeping the bark in hot water, which is often used to alleviate symptoms of fever, colds, and flu.

Tinctures, which are alcohol-based extractions, can also be made from the bark and are used for their anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic properties. The use of white willow in modern medicine is mostly limited to complementary and alternative medicine, and it is always recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new treatment.

It is important to note that high concentrations of salicin can cause stomach irritation and other side effects, and it should not be used by people who are allergic to aspirin or have certain medical conditions.

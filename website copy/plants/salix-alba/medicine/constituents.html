10 Best Active Constituents of Salix alba (White Willow)

Salix alba, commonly known as the white willow, contains several active constituents that are used for medicinal purposes.

The bark of the white willow contains salicin, which is a precursor to salicylic acid, a powerful anti-inflammatory agent. This compound is often used to relieve pain, reduce inflammation, and lower fever.

Additionally, white willow also contains flavonoids, tannins, and other phenolic compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic effects, making white willow a popular natural remedy for conditions such as arthritis, headaches, and menstrual cramps. However, it is important to note that white willow should be used with caution, as it can cause stomach upset and interact with certain medications.

As with any natural remedy, it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before use.

10 Best Active Constituents of Angelica sinensis (Dong Quai)

Angelica sinensis, also known as Dong Quai, is a plant that has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

The active constituents of this plant that are believed to have medicinal properties include phytochemicals such as coumarins, ferulic acid, and phthalides. One of the key active constituents, Z-ligustilide, has been studied for its potential to dilate blood vessels, improve circulation, and reduce inflammation.

Additionally, ferulic acid has been researched for its antioxidant properties and potential to protect against cellular damage. Overall, Angelica sinensis contains a variety of active constituents that have been studied for their potential benefits in promoting cardiovascular health, reducing inflammation, and protecting against oxidative stress.

However, more research is needed to fully understand the mechanisms of action and potential therapeutic applications of these compounds.

1. Vitamin C

The vitamin C contained in Angelica Sinensis is an essential nutrient that plays a vital role in various bodily functions, including the production of collagen, neurotransmitters, and hormones.

Its main medicinal properties include its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, which help protect cells from damage and reduce inflammation. This active constituent of Angelica sinensis has been shown to help relieve several health conditions, such as boosting the immune system, improving blood circulation, reducing menstrual cramps, and alleviating symptoms of menopause.

The main parts of the Angelica sinensis plant that contain vitamin C are the roots and underground stems, which are typically harvested and dried for medicinal use. However, like any other supplement, improper use of Angelica sinensis vitamin C may cause some side effects, including diarrhea, stomach upset, and skin irritation.

In addition, high doses of vitamin C may interfere with certain medications and cause adverse reactions, so it is essential to consult with a healthcare provider before taking any supplements containing vitamin C from Angelica sinensis or any other source.

10 possible side effects of Angelica sinensis (Dong Quai) if used improperly

Angelica sinensis, also known as dong quai, is a plant that is often used in traditional medicine, but if used improperly, it can have several potential negative health effects.

First, it can increase sensitivity to sunlight, leading to skin inflammation, redness, and swelling. Second, it can have estrogen-like effects in the body, which can disrupt hormonal balances and be harmful to pregnant women or those with hormone-sensitive conditions.

Third, it can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, increasing the risk of bleeding. Fourth, it can cause stomach upset, including diarrhea and abdominal pain, when taken in large amounts. Lastly, long-term use or high doses of Angelica sinensis may lead to serious complications, such as seizures and heart damage.

Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen, especially with a substance like Angelica sinensis.

1. Menstrual Bleeding Increase

Menstrual bleeding increase caused by Angelica Sinensis refers to an abnormal increase in menstrual flow due to the use of this herb.

The main active constituents in Angelica Sinensis that can cause this side effect are phytoestrogens and volatile oils, which can stimulate the uterus and increase menstrual flow. This side effect is most commonly associated with oral liquid and pill preparations of dong quai if misused or taken in large quantities. People who are more likely to experience this side effect are those with a history of heavy menstrual periods, fibroids, or other pelvic inflammatory disorders.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider before using dong quai, especially for those with a history of hormone-sensitive conditions or those taking other medications.

2. Gastrointestinal Irritation

Gastrointestinal irritation caused by Angelica Sinensis refers to an unpleasant reaction in the digestive tract resulting in symptoms such as stomach discomfort, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

This side effect can be attributed to the plant's active constituents, primarily essential oils, phytosterols, and coumarins. Medicinal preparations including decoctions, capsules, and tinctures may cause gastrointestinal irritation if misused or not taken as directed.

Individuals with a history of gastrointestinal sensitivities, such as those with irritable bowel syndrome or inflammatory bowel disease, are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid gastrointestinal irritation, it is recommended to start with a low dose, gradually increase the dosage over time, and take the herb with food or water.

Additionally, consulting with a healthcare provider before starting any new herbal supplement is always advisable.

3. Liver Damage

Liver damage caused by Angelica Sinensis refers to the potential for this herb to negatively impact the liver.

The main active constituents of Angelica Sinensis that can cause this side effect are phthalides, lavandulyl falvones, and lipophilic vitamin K compounds. These constituents can cause liver damage if taken in large amounts or over a long period of time.

Medicinal preparations of Angelica Sinensis that can cause this side effect if misused include decoctions, powdered extracts, and capsules. Individuals who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with pre-existing liver conditions or those who are taking other medications that can harm the liver. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to use Angelica Sinensis under the guidance of a healthcare professional, avoid taking it for extended periods of time, and avoid taking it in large amounts.

Additionally, it is important to inform your healthcare provider of any other medications or supplements you are taking and to have regular liver function tests if you are using this herb regularly.

4. Hypoglycemia

Hypoglycemia caused by Angelica Sinensis refers to a condition where the level of glucose in the blood is lower than normal.

This side effect can occur due to the presence of certain active constituents in Angelica Sinensis, such as ferulic acid and ligustilide, which can affect blood sugar levels. The main medicinal preparations of Angelica Sinensis that can cause this side effect if misused include decoctions, capsules, and tablets. Individuals who are diabetic, have low blood sugar levels, or are taking medication that lowers blood sugar are more likely to experience this side effect.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to use Angelica Sinensis under the guidance of a healthcare professional, monitor blood sugar levels regularly, and adjust the dosage or discontinue use if any signs of hypoglycemia appear.

5. Skin Rash Or Itching

Skin rash or itching caused by Angelica Sinensis refers to a skin reaction that can occur as a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents in Angelica Sinensis that can cause this side effect include essential oils, ferulic acid, and various coumarins. Preparations such as dried root, powder, capsules, and teas, if misused or taken in large amounts, can lead to skin rash or itching.

Individuals who are allergic to members of the carrot family, or who are taking medication that affects blood clotting, are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid skin rash or itching, it is recommended to start with a low dose, and gradually increase it over time, while also monitoring for any skin reactions.

Discontinue use and consult a healthcare professional if a skin rash or itching occurs.

6. Dizziness Or Lightheadedness

Dizziness or lightheadedness caused by Angelica Sinensis refers to a feeling of unsteadiness, wooziness, or faintness that may occur during the use of this herb.

This side effect can be attributed to certain active constituents in Angelica Sinensis, such as essential oils, coumarins, and ferulic acid, which can have a blood pressure-lowering effect and dilate blood vessels, leading to a feeling of dizziness. This side effect is most commonly associated with medicinal preparations like decoctions, capsules, and tinctures, especially when taken in large doses or for extended periods. Individuals who are more susceptible to this side effect include those with low blood pressure, pregnant women, and people using blood-thinning medications.

To avoid this side effect, it's crucial to adhere to recommended dosages, stay well-hydrated, and consult with a healthcare provider before using Angelica Sinensis, particularly if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking prescribed medications.

7. Throat Irritation

Throat irritation caused by Angelica Sinensis refers to a dry, scratchy, or uncomfortable sensation in the throat that may result from the use of this herb.

This side effect can be attributed mainly to the active constituents of Angelica Sinensis, which include essential oils, phytosterols, and coumarins, among others. Improper preparation or misuse of Angelica Sinensis, particularly in the form of decoctions, powdered extracts, or tinctures, can increase the likelihood of experiencing throat irritation.

Individuals who are more sensitive to the effects of Angelica Sinensis or have a history of respiratory issues may be more prone to experiencing throat irritation. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to follow proper dosage guidelines, dilute extracts appropriately, and stay well-hydrated during and after use.

Additionally, monitoring one's body's reaction to Angelica Sinensis and consulting with a healthcare provider or herbalist can help minimize the risk of throat irritation and ensure safe and effective use.

8. Allergic Reaction

Allergic reaction caused by Angelica Sinensis refers to the immune system's abnormal response to the plant's active constituents, which can include essential oils, polysaccharides, and ferulic acid.

This reaction can result in symptoms such as skin rashes, hives, itching, and difficulty breathing. The medicinal preparations of dong quai, such as teas, capsules, and tinctures, can cause allergic reactions if misused or taken in large quantities.

People who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with a known allergy to members of the Apiaceae family (which includes dong quai), those with a history of allergic reactions to other herbs or medications, and those with pre-existing medical conditions such as asthma or autoimmune disorders. To avoid this side effect, it is important to start with a small dose, monitor for any adverse reactions, and gradually increase the dosage as tolerated.

It is also advised to consult with a healthcare professional before taking dong quai, especially if taking other medications or supplements, to ensure safe and effective use of this herb.

9. Breast Tenderness

Breast tenderness caused by Angelica Sinensis refers to a condition where the breast tissue becomes sensitive, sore, or painful to touch.

This side effect is commonly attributed to the main active constituents of Angelica Sinensis, which include phytosterols, polysaccharides, and essential oils. These constituents can stimulate estrogen hormone activity in the body, leading to hormonal imbalances that can cause breast tenderness.

Medicinal preparations of Angelica Sinensis, such as tinctures, capsules, or teas, can cause this side effect if misused or taken in large doses. People who are more likely to experience breast tenderness from Angelica Sinensis include those with a history of hormone-sensitive conditions, such as breast cancer or endometriosis, or those taking hormone replacement therapy. To avoid breast tenderness, it is recommended to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider before taking Angelica Sinensis, especially for those with hormone-sensitive conditions.

Precautions also include avoiding the use of this herb during pregnancy or breastfeeding and being mindful of potential drug interactions.

10. Blood Thinning Effect

Blood thinning effect caused by Angelica Sinensis refers to the herb's ability to inhibit blood coagulation, which can be beneficial in some medical conditions but also poses risks of excessive bleeding.

The main active constituents in Angelica Sinensis that can cause this side effect are coumarin derivatives and ferulic acid. Medicinal preparations such as decoctions, capsules, and tinctures, if misused or taken in large amounts, can increase the risk of this side effect. Individuals who are more likely to experience this side effect are those with bleeding disorders, those taking anticoagulant medications, and those undergoing surgery.

To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to use Angelica Sinensis under the guidance of a healthcare professional, avoid taking it in large doses or for prolonged periods, and promptly discontinue its use before scheduled surgeries.

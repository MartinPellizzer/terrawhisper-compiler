10 Best Health Benefits of Angelica sinensis (Dong Quai)

Angelica sinensis, also known as Dong Quai or Chinese Angelica, is a plant that has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries due to its various medicinal benefits.

It is known to regulate menstrual cycles and alleviate symptoms associated with menstruation and menopause, such as cramps, bloating, and hot flashes. Additionally, Angelica sinensis has anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties, making it useful for treating a variety of conditions including allergies, inflammation, and infections. It also contains compounds that can improve blood flow and circulation, which can help reduce the risk of heart disease and improve overall cardiovascular health.

Furthermore, some studies suggest that Angelica sinensis may have potential cancer-fighting properties, although more research is needed in this area.

1. Enhances blood production

Dong Quai enhances blood production, meaning it promotes the production of new blood and the circulation of old blood, which can help to oxygenate and nourish organs and tissues.

The main active constituents of Angelica sinensis that give this benefit include phytochemicals such as ferulic acid, ligustilide, and butylphthalide, which have been shown to stimulate the production of stem cells in the bone marrow and increase the number of red and white blood cells. The primary parts of the Angelica sinensis plant that give this benefit are the roots and rhizomes, which contain the highest concentration of these active compounds. The main medicinal preparations of Angelica sinensis that give this benefit include dried or powdered root, extracts, tinctures, and teas, which can be taken orally or applied topically.

The main health conditions that this benefit of Angelica sinensis helps relieve include anemia, menstrual disorders, and menopausal symptoms, as well as improving overall immune function and promoting cardiovascular health.

Angelica sinensis enhances blood production thanks to the active constituents listed below.

  • Bergapten
  • Osthole
  • Xanthotoxol
  • Imperatorin
  • Saxalin
  • Angelicin
  • Ferulic acid
  • Polysaccharides
  • Volatile oils
  • Z-ligustilide

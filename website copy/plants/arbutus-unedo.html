What to know before using Arbutus unedo (Strawberry Tree)?

Arbutus unedo, also known as the strawberry tree, is a evergreen tree or shrub that is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of western Europe.

It has a multi-stemmed growth habit and can reach heights of up to 30 feet. The tree produces white or pink flowers in the fall, followed by edible, red, strawberry-like fruit.

The fruit has a sweet-tand-tart taste and can be used to make jams and jellies. The bark, leaves, and fruit of Arbutus unedo have been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including urinary tract infections, kidney stones, and rheumatism.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and is tolerant of drought and salt spray.

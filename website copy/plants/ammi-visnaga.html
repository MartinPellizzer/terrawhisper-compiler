What to know before using Ammi visnaga (Visnaga)?

Ammi visnaga, also known as Bishop's weed, is a plant native to the Mediterranean region that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its ability to dilate blood vessels and act as a muscle relaxant. This makes it useful for treating conditions such as angina, asthma, and various kidney and gallbladder ailments. In terms of horticulture, Ammi visnaga prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is a drought-tolerant plant that blooms in the summer with white, umbrella-shaped flowers.

Botanically, Ammi visnaga is a member of the carrot family, Apiaceae, and it has finely divided, feathery leaves and hollow stems.

By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Capsicum annuum, also known as chili peppers, have been used in various medicinal preparations for centuries.

A tisane, or herbal tea, can be made by steeping dried chili peppers in hot water, which is said to help with digestion and relieve pain. Topical preparations, such as salves and ointments, can be made by infusing chili peppers in a carrier oil and applied to the skin to help alleviate muscle pain and arthritis.

Tinctures can also be made by soaking the peppers in alcohol, which can be used to treat a variety of ailments including poor circulation and low energy levels. Capsaicin, the active compound in chili peppers, is believed to be responsible for these medicinal properties.

However, it is important to note that chili peppers can be irritating to the skin and mucous membranes, and should be used with caution.

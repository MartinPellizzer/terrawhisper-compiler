10 Best Health Benefits of Capsicum annuum (Cayenne)

Capsicum annuum, more commonly known as bell peppers or chili peppers, has been shown to have numerous medicinal benefits.

First, they are rich in vitamin C, vitamin A, and antioxidants, which can help boost the immune system and protect against cellular damage. Additionally, the active compound capsaicin found in chili peppers has been found to have anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties, making it a potential treatment for conditions such as arthritis and neuropathy.

Furthermore, some studies suggest that consuming capsicum annuum may help support weight loss efforts by increasing metabolism and reducing appetite. Additionally, it has been found to have cardiovascular benefits, such as lowering blood pressure and reducing the risk of developing blood clots.

However, it is important to note that some people may experience adverse effects from consuming large amounts of capsicum annuum, such as stomach irritation or heartburn.

1. Alleviates pain

Cayenne, or Capsicum annuum, alleviates pain, meaning it can help reduce or eliminate the sensation of pain in the body.

The main active constituents of Capsicum annuum that provide this benefit are capsaicin, dihydrocapsaicin, and nordihydrocapsaicin, which are all found in the fruit of the plant. The primary parts of the Capsicum annuum plant that have pain-relieving properties are the fruit and the resin found within the fruit.

The main medicinal preparations of Capsicum annuum that are used for pain relief include topical creams, ointments, and patches, as well as oral capsules and tinctures. The main health conditions that this benefit of Capsicum annuum helps relieve include various types of pain such as muscle and joint pain, nerve pain, and headaches.

It is also used for conditions such as osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and fibromyalgia.

Capsicum annuum alleviates pain thanks to the active constituents listed below.

  • Capsaicin
  • Dihydrocapsaicin
  • Nordihydrocapsaicin
  • Homocapsaicin
  • Homodihydrocapsaicin
  • Capsanthin
  • Capsorubin
  • Violaxanthin
  • Lutein
  • Beta-carotene

10 Best Active Constituents of Capsicum annuum (Cayenne)

Capsicum annuum, also known as bell peppers or chili peppers, contain several active constituents that have medicinal properties.

The most well-known of these is capsaicin, which is found in higher concentrations in hotter peppers. Capsaicin has been shown to have anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and thermogenic effects, making it a popular ingredient in topical creams and ointments for pain relief.

Additionally, Capsicum annuum contains high levels of vitamin C and vitamin A, as well as a variety of carotenoids, all of which have antioxidant properties and may support immune function and overall health. The seeds and placental tissue of the pepper are also rich in fatty acids, including linoleic and oleic acids, which have been studied for their potential benefits in reducing cholesterol levels and improving cardiovascular health. Furthermore, some studies suggest that consuming Capsicum annuum may help to support weight loss by increasing satiety and boosting metabolism.

However, it is important to note that excessive consumption of hot peppers can cause gastrointestinal distress and other side effects, so it is recommended to use them in moderation.

What to know before using Viola riviniana (Wood Violet)?

Viola riviniana, also known as the common dog violet, is a species of violet that is native to Europe.

This plant has a number of medicinal properties, including the ability to soothe coughs and reduce inflammation, and it has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments. Viola riviniana prefers moist, humus-rich soils and partial shade, and it is often found in woodlands and grassy areas. In terms of its botanical characteristics, Viola riviniana is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows to be around 10-15 cm tall.

It has heart-shaped, toothed leaves and produces purple flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, round seed capsules.

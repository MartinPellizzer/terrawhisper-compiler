What to know before using Tanacetum parthenium (Feverfew)?

Tanacetum parthenium, also known as feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region of Europe.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it can grow up to 2 feet tall with clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant has been used in herbal medicine for centuries due to its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties, which are believed to come from its high concentration of parthenolides. Feverfew is commonly used to treat migraines, arthritis, and menstrual cramps, and it can be consumed as a tea, capsule, or tincture.

In addition to its medicinal uses, Tanacetum parthenium is also an attractive ornamental plant with fern-like, aromatic foliage that adds interest to gardens.

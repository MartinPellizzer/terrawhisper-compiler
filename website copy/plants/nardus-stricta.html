What to know before using Nardus stricta (Matgrass)?

Nardus stricta, also known as catsfoot or matgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and Asia.

It has a variety of medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects, and has been used in traditional medicine to treat ailments such as rheumatism and kidney diseases. In terms of horticultural conditions, Nardus stricta prefers acidic, well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

It is a hardy plant that can thrive in a variety of conditions, but it is particularly suited to cool, moist climates. Botanically, Nardus stricta is characterized by its dense, low-growing tufts of narrow, linear leaves and its spike-like inflorescences, which bear small, purple flowers.

The plant typically grows to a height of 10-30 cm and has a mat-forming habit.

What are the medicinal uses of Nardus stricta?

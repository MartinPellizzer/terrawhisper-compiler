What to know before using Lobelia inflata (Lobelia)?

Lobelia inflata, also known as Indian tobacco, is a plant native to North America that has been used for medicinal purposes by indigenous peoples for centuries.

Its medicinal properties include expectorant, diaphoretic, and antiasthmatic effects, making it a popular natural remedy for respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, and coughs. Lobelia inflata thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, and can grow up to three feet tall with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant contains alkaloids, including lobeline, which is believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties, but can also be toxic in large doses, so it should be used with caution.

What to know before using Tanacetum vulgare (Tansy)?

Tanacetum vulgare, also known as tansy, is a hardy perennial herb with a long history of medicinal use.

Its bright yellow button-like flowers and deeply cut, aromatic leaves make it an attractive addition to gardens, although it can be invasive in some regions. Tanacetum vulgare prefers full sun and well-drained soil, and it is drought tolerant once established. Medicinally, tansy has been used to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues, fevers, and rheumatism, due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic in large quantities and should only be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

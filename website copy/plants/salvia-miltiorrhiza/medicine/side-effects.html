10 possible side effects of Salvia miltiorrhiza (Dan Shen) if used improperly

Salvia miltiorrhiza, also known as Danshen, is a plant commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine.

If used improperly, it can potentially cause a number of health issues. First, overdosing on this herb can lead to stomach irritation, nausea, and vomiting.

Additionally, it may cause low blood sugar and blood pressure, which can be dangerous for certain individuals. In rare cases, it has been linked to liver damage and abnormal heart rhythms. Pregnant women should avoid it as it may stimulate uterine contractions and lead to miscarriage.

Lastly, it can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, increasing the risk of bleeding.

1. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Salvia Miltiorrhiza refers to a condition of loose, watery stools occurring frequently, which can be a side effect of using this herb.

The main active constituents of Salvia miltiorrhiza that can cause diarrhea are tanshinones and salvianolic acids, which can affect the gastrointestinal system and increase bowel movements. This side effect is commonly associated with the decoction or powder form of Salvia miltiorrhiza, and it is more likely to occur if the herb is misused or taken in large doses.

People who are more susceptible to this side effect include those with weak digestive systems or a history of gastrointestinal disorders. To avoid diarrhea, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and preparation instructions carefully, start with a low dose and gradually increase, and avoid using Salvia miltiorrhiza in conjunction with other medications or herbs that can affect gut motility.

It is also recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using Salvia miltiorrhiza for medicinal purposes.

2. Nausea

Nausea caused by Salvia Miltiorrhiza refers to the feeling of discomfort and unease in the stomach that can be accompanied by an urge to vomit.

This side effect can be attributed mainly to the diterpene compounds found in Salvia miltiorrhiza, such as tanshinones and miltirone. Preparations such as dried root slices, powder, and extracts, when misused or taken in large quantities, can cause nausea.

Individuals who are new to using Salvia miltiorrhiza or those who are sensitive to its active constituents are more likely to experience nausea. To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and usage guidelines, start with a low dose and gradually increase, and avoid taking it on an empty stomach.

Additionally, consulting with a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement is always recommended.

3. Vomiting

Vomiting caused by Salvia Miltiorrhiza refers to the unpleasant symptom of forcefully expelling contents from the stomach through the mouth.

This side effect can occur due to the presence of certain active constituents in Salvia miltiorrhiza, including tanshinones and salvianolic acid. This side effect is most commonly associated with certain medicinal preparations that contain high concentrations of these compounds, such as powdered or extracted forms of the root.

People who are more likely to experience vomiting as a side effect of Salvia miltiorrhiza use include those with sensitive stomachs or a history of gastrointestinal issues. To reduce the risk of vomiting, it is important to use Salvia miltiorrhiza as directed, starting with a low dose and gradually increasing as tolerated.

Additionally, taking Salvia miltiorrhiza with food or after eating a small snack may help reduce the likelihood of vomiting.

4. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Salvia Miltiorrhiza refers to a feeling of lightheadedness, unsteadiness, or disorientation that may occur after using this herb.

The main active constituents of Salvia miltiorrhiza that can cause this side effect include tanshinones and salvianolic acid. These components can affect the central nervous system and cause dizziness when present in high concentrations.

The main medicinal preparations of Salvia miltiorrhiza that can cause this side effect if misused include decoctions, powder, and capsules. People who are more likely to experience dizziness from Salvia miltiorrhiza are those with a history of motion sickness, low blood pressure, or who are taking other medications that can cause dizziness.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and usage instructions carefully, and to consult with a healthcare provider before using Salvia miltiorrhiza, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking other medications.

5. Headache

Headache caused by Salvia Miltiorrhiza refers to a possible adverse effect characterized by a continuous or throbbing pain in the head.

This side effect can be attributed to the main active constituents of Salvia miltiorrhiza, including tanshinones and salvianolic acid, which can have stimulating effects on the nervous system. The primary medicinal preparations that can cause this side effect if misused include Salvia miltiorrhiza decoctions, powder, and capsules. Individuals with a history of migraines or chronic headaches, as well as those who are sensitive to stimulants, are more likely to experience headaches after using Salvia miltiorrhiza.

To avoid this side effect, individuals are advised to start with a low dose and gradually increase the dosage, avoid taking Salvia miltiorrhiza on an empty stomach, and discontinue use if a headache occurs.

6. Mouth Ulcers

Mouth ulcers caused by Salvia Miltiorrhiza refers to sores or lesions that appear in the oral cavity, including the inner cheeks, lips, tongue, and gums.

This side effect can be attributed to the main active constituents of Salvia miltiorrhiza, such as tanshinones and salvianolic acid, which can cause irritation and inflammation when they come into contact with the mucous membranes of the mouth. This side effect is most commonly observed in medicinal preparations of Salvia miltiorrhiza that are not properly prepared or used, such as decoctions, powders, and pills.

Individuals who are sensitive to Salvia miltiorrhiza or have a history of mouth ulcers or oral inflammation are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid mouth ulcers caused by Salvia miltiorrhiza, it is important to follow proper preparation and dosage instructions, avoid chewing on the herb, and rinse the mouth with water after taking the preparation.

If mouth ulcers do occur, it is recommended to discontinue use and consult with a healthcare professional for further evaluation and management.

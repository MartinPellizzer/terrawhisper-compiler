What to know before using Populus x candicans (Balm Of Gilead)?

Populus x candicans, also known as the hybrid poplar, is a fast-growing tree that is widely cultivated for its medicinal and horticultural uses.

Medicinally, the bark of Populus x candicans contains salicin, a compound that can be used to alleviate pain and reduce fever, and has been used in the treatment of ailments such as arthritis and muscle strains. In terms of horticultural conditions, this tree prefers full sun and well-drained soil, and can tolerate a wide range of soil types and pH levels. Botanically, Populus x candicans is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 100 feet tall, with a trunk diameter of up to 6 feet.

It has distinctive grayish-white bark that is deeply furrowed and ridged, and produces long, narrow leaves that are dark green above and silvery-white below.

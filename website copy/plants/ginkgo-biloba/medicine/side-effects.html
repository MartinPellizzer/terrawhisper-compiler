10 possible side effects of Ginkgo biloba (Ginkgo) if used improperly

Ginkgo biloba, a popular herbal supplement, can have several potential side effects if used improperly.

Firstly, it can increase the risk of bleeding due to its blood-thinning properties, which can be problematic during surgeries or for those with bleeding disorders. Secondly, it may interact with other medications, such as blood thinners and antidepressants, leading to adverse effects.

Thirdly, ginkgo biloba can lower blood pressure, which, if already low, can cause dizziness, fainting, or falls. Additionally, it may cause stomach upset, headaches, and skin rashes in some individuals.

Lastly, excessive consumption of ginkgo biloba during pregnancy and lactation is discouraged, as its safety and efficacy have not been established in these conditions.

1. Stomach Upset

Stomach upset caused by Ginkgo Biloba refers to a range of digestive symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain.

These symptoms can occur due to the presence of active constituents in ginkgo, including ginkgolic acids and flavonoids, which can irritate the digestive tract. The main medicinal preparations of ginkgo that can cause stomach upset if misused include standardized extracts of ginkgo leaves, which are commonly used as dietary supplements.

People who are more likely to experience stomach upset from ginkgo include those who take high doses of ginkgo or those who are sensitive to its active constituents. To avoid stomach upset from ginkgo, it is recommended to start with low doses and gradually increase, take ginkgo with food, and avoid taking it on an empty stomach.

Additionally, it is important to follow the recommended dosage on the product label and consult a healthcare provider before taking ginkgo.

2. Headache

Headache caused by Ginkgo Biloba refers to pain in the head that can occur as a side effect of using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents in Ginkgo biloba that can cause headaches include flavonoids, terpenoids, and ginkgolides. These constituents can dilate blood vessels and increase blood flow to the brain, which may result in headaches in some individuals.

The main medicinal preparations of Ginkgo biloba that can cause headaches if misused include standardized extracts, capsules, and teas. People who are more likely to experience headaches as a side effect of using Ginkgo biloba include those who take high doses, those who are sensitive to the herb, and those who use it in conjunction with certain medications, such as blood thinners or antidepressants.

To avoid headaches as a side effect of using Ginkgo biloba, it is important to use it as directed, start with a low dose and gradually increase, and consult with a healthcare provider before use, especially for those who have medical conditions or take medications.

3. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Ginkgo Biloba refers to a feeling of lightheadedness, unsteadiness, or vertigo that can occur as a side effect of using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents of ginkgo that can cause this side effect are flavonoids and terpene lactones, which can affect blood flow to the brain and cause dizziness if taken in large amounts. The main medicinal preparations of ginkgo that can cause this side effect if misused include standardized ginkgo extracts, which are commonly used in supplements and can interact with other medications or have adverse effects when taken in high doses. People who are more likely to experience dizziness from ginkgo biloba use include those with bleeding disorders, those taking blood thinners, pregnant or nursing women, and those with severe liver or kidney disease.

To avoid dizziness when using ginkgo biloba, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase, avoid taking it on an empty stomach, avoid drinking alcohol or caffeine while taking it, and consult a healthcare provider before using it if you have any pre-existing conditions or are taking other medications.

4. Constipation

Constipation caused by Ginkgo Biloba refers to the uncomfortable and sometimes painful condition of infrequent bowel movements or difficulty in passing stools.

This side effect can be attributed to the main active constituents of ginkgo, namely flavonoids, terpene lactones, and ginkgolic acids. The extracts of ginkgo leaves are commonly used in medicinal preparations, and when misused or overused, can lead to constipation. Individuals who are more prone to this side effect include those with a history of digestive disorders, dehydration, and those who consume a low-fiber diet.

To avoid constipation, it is essential to take ginkgo in moderation, drink plenty of fluids, consume a high-fiber diet, and consult with a healthcare professional for proper dosage and usage instructions.

5. Rash

Rash caused by Ginkgo Biloba refers to a skin reaction that can occur as a side effect of using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents of ginkgo that can cause this side effect are flavonoids and terpenoids, which can act as allergens and induce an immune response in some individuals. The main medicinal preparations of ginkgo that can cause this side effect if misused include standardized extracts of ginkgo leaf and ginkgo biloba leaf powder.

People who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with a history of allergic reactions to plants, particularly those in the same family as ginkgo, as well as those who take ginkgo in large doses or over an extended period. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of ginkgo and gradually increase it, and to always follow the recommended dosage and duration of use provided by a healthcare professional.

Additionally, individuals should discontinue use of ginkgo and seek medical attention if they experience any adverse skin reactions.

6. Allergic Reactions

Allergic reactions caused by Ginkgo Biloba refer to abnormal immune system responses that can occur after exposure to this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents of ginkgo that can cause allergic reactions include flavonoids, terpenoids, and ginkgolic acids. These constituents are found in various medicinal preparations of ginkgo, including capsules, tablets, and teas, and can cause allergic reactions if misused or taken in excessive amounts. Individuals with pre-existing allergies, especially to plants, and those taking certain medications, such as blood thinners, may be more susceptible to allergic reactions caused by ginkgo.

To avoid these side effects, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage, consult a healthcare provider before taking ginkgo, and discontinue use immediately and seek medical attention if any signs of an allergic reaction occur, such as hives, rash, or difficulty breathing.

7. Nausea

Nausea caused by Ginkgo Biloba refers to the feeling of discomfort or unease in the stomach that can be accompanied by an urge to vomit.

This side effect can be attributed to the main active constituents of ginkgo, which include flavonoids, terpenoids, and ginkgolic acids. These constituents can interact with the digestive system and cause gastrointestinal distress, leading to nausea.

The most common medicinal preparations of ginkgo that can cause this side effect if misused include standardized extracts of ginkgo leaves, which are often used as dietary supplements for cognitive health. Individuals who are more likely to experience nausea after taking ginkgo include those with a sensitive stomach or a history of gastrointestinal issues. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose of ginkgo and gradually increase the dosage over time, while also taking the supplement with food to reduce the risk of stomach upset.

Additionally, consulting with a healthcare provider before taking ginkgo is advised, especially for those taking other medications or with underlying medical conditions.

8. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Ginkgo Biloba refers to an abnormal frequency and looseness of bowel movements that may occur as a side effect of using this herbal supplement.

The main active constituents of ginkgo that can cause diarrhea are ginkgolides and flavonoids, which can stimulate the gastrointestinal tract and increase intestinal permeability. Medicinal preparations of ginkgo that can cause diarrhea if misused include standardized extracts of ginkgo leaves, which are commonly used in dietary supplements and alternative medicine for various health conditions.

Individuals who are more likely to experience diarrhea from ginkgo use include those who consume high doses of ginkgo, those with preexisting gastrointestinal disorders, and those who take ginkgo concurrently with other medications that can influence gastrointestinal motility. To avoid diarrhea from ginkgo use, it is important to follow the recommended dosage guidelines, start with a low dose and gradually increase, and avoid taking ginkgo on an empty stomach.

Additionally, individuals with a history of gastrointestinal disorders or those taking medications that may interact with ginkgo should consult their healthcare provider before using this herbal supplement.

9. Heart Palpitations

Heart palpitations caused by Ginkgo Biloba refers to the sensation of having a fast-beating, fluttering, or pounding heart.

This side effect can be attributed to the two main active constituents of ginkgo, flavonoids and terpenoids, which have a blood-thinning effect that can increase heart rate. Ginkgo extracts and concentrated ginkgo supplements are the main medicinal preparations that can cause this side effect when misused or overused. Individuals who are sensitive to ginkgo, have pre-existing heart conditions, or are taking other medications that affect blood clotting are more likely to experience heart palpitations from ginkgo.

To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider before using ginkgo, especially if taking any other medications or having underlying health issues.

10. Vomiting

Vomiting caused by Ginkgo Biloba refers to the forceful expulsion of stomach contents through the mouth due to its use.

This side effect can be attributed mainly to the presence of ginkgolides and flavonoids, the active constituents of ginkgo. The preparations that can cause this side effect if misused include ginkgo extracts and teas.

Individuals who are more susceptible to this side effect are those with a history of stomach sensitivity or digestive issues. To avoid this side effect, it is essential to adhere to the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare professional before use.

Furthermore, it is advisable to gradually increase the dosage to minimize the risk of any digestive side effects.

10 Best Active Constituents of Ginkgo biloba (Ginkgo)

Ginkgo biloba, also known as the maidenhair tree, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries and its active constituents include flavonoids, terpenoids, and ginkgolides.

These compounds have been found to have various medicinal properties, such as being potent antioxidants that protect cells from damage caused by free radicals. Ginkgo biloba is most commonly used to improve memory and cognitive function, and its active constituents have been shown to increase blood flow to the brain and improve the health of nerve cells. Additionally, ginkgo biloba has been studied for its potential benefits in treating a variety of health conditions, including anxiety, tinnitus, and glaucoma.

However, it is important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the medicinal properties and potential uses of Ginkgo biloba.

1. Ginkgolides

The ginkgolides contained in Ginkgo Biloba are a group of unique and active terpene trilactone compounds that are primarily responsible for the herb's medicinal properties.

These compounds have been found to possess potent antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and platelet-activating factor (PAF) inhibitory effects. Ginkgo biloba ginkgolides are primarily used to support cognitive function, improve memory and concentration, and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression.

They are also known to help relieve conditions such as tinnitus, vertigo, and Raynaud's disease. Ginkgolides are mainly found in the leaves of the Ginkgo biloba tree, and they are most concentrated in the leaves of mature trees. Improper use of ginkgo biloba ginkgolides can lead to several side effects, including digestive issues, headaches, dizziness, and skin reactions.

In rare cases, they can also cause more severe side effects such as seizures, allergic reactions, and increased risk of bleeding.

2. Bilobalide

The bilobalide contained in Ginkgo Biloba is a powerful organic compound that is known for its strong antioxidant properties.

Its main medicinal properties include improving blood circulation, protecting the brain and nervous system, and acting as an anti-inflammatory agent. This Ginkgo biloba constituent has been shown to help relieve various health conditions such as memory loss, tinnitus, and vertigo. Bilobalide is found mainly in the leaves of the Ginkgo biloba plant.

Some possible side effects of this Ginkgo biloba constituent, if used improperly, include stomach upset, headache, dizziness, and allergic skin reactions.

3. Flavonoids

The flavonoids contained in Ginkgo Biloba are a group of powerful antioxidants that help protect the body from damage caused by harmful molecules called free radicals.

They are known for their anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and vasodilatory effects, which contribute to their wide range of medicinal properties. Ginkgo biloba flavonoids have been shown to help relieve various health conditions, including improving mental function, reducing symptoms of anxiety and depression, and alleviating headaches and migraines.

These flavonoids are found primarily in the leaves of the ginkgo tree, and to a lesser extent in the seeds and root. Possible side effects of using ginkgo biloba flavonoids improperly include stomach upset, headache, dizziness, and allergic skin reactions.

In rare cases, they may cause seizures or liver damage.

4. Terpene trilactones

The terpene trilactones contained in Ginkgo Biloba is a group of compounds that include ginkgolides and bilobalide, which are believed to be the primary active components of the ginkgo plant.

These terpene trilactones have been shown to have various medicinal properties, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective effects. They are primarily used to help relieve cognitive decline, including memory impairment and Alzheimer's disease.

The terpene trilactones are found mainly in the leaves of the ginkgo plant. Some possible side effects of ginkgo biloba terpene trilactones, if used improperly, include stomach upset, headache, dizziness, and allergic skin reactions.

It is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting to use ginkgo biloba or any of its constituents.

5. Proanthocyanidins

The proanthocyanidins contained in Ginkgo Biloba are a type of flavonoid antioxidant that have been found to have significant medicinal properties.

These properties include improving blood circulation, protecting the health of neurons, and acting as an anti-inflammatory agent. Ginkgo biloba proanthocyanidins have been shown to help relieve various health conditions such as tinnitus, vertigo, and Raynaud's disease.

These beneficial compounds are found primarily in the leaves of the ginkgo plant. However, if used improperly, ginkgo biloba proanthocyanidins can cause side effects such as dizziness, headaches, and gastrointestinal disturbances.

Additionally, high doses of ginkgo biloba may interact with blood-thinning medications and increase the risk of bleeding.

Its main medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-cancer effects. Myricetin has been shown to help relieve various health conditions such as inflammation, cardiovascular diseases, and certain types of cancer. This constituent is found mainly in the leaves of the ginkgo plant.

Some possible side effects of myricetin, if used improperly, include stomach upset, headache, and dizziness.

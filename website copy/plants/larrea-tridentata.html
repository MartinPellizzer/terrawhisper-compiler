Larrea tridentata, commonly known as creosote bush, is a shrub native to the Southwestern United States and Northwestern Mexico.

It has a reputation in folk medicine for its wide range of therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and anticancer agents. This plant is highly adaptable, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments with minimal water and nutrient requirements. Larrea tridentata's botanical characteristics include small, greenish-yellow flowers, thick, resinous leaves that emit a distinct odor, and woody branches that grow in a sprawling, shrubby form.

Its roots can reach deep into the ground, making it drought-tolerant and capable of absorbing moisture from a large volume of soil.

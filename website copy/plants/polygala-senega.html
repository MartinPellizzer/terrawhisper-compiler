Polygala senega, also known as Seneca snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It has been traditionally used in Native American medicine for its medicinal properties, which include expectorant, diuretic, and anti-inflammatory effects. The plant contains various chemical compounds, such as saponins and flavonoids, that are believed to be responsible for its therapeutic benefits. Polygala senega thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It typically grows to a height of 1-2 feet and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer.

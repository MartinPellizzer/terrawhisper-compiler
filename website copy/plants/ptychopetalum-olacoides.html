The tree thrives in humid and shady conditions, and requires well-drained soil to grow. Ptychopetalum olacoides can grow up to 15 meters tall, and has simple, elliptical leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs on the stem.

The tree produces small, white flowers and black, drupaceous fruits, which contain a single seed.

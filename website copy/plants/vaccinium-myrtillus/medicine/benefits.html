10 Best Health Benefits of Vaccinium myrtillus (Bilberry)

Vaccinium myrtillus, also known as bilberry, has been widely studied for its medicinal benefits and has been shown to have a number of positive effects on health.

One of the most well-known benefits of bilberry is its ability to improve vision and reduce eye-related issues. This is due to the high anthocyanin content in bilberries, which has been shown to improve blood flow to the eyes and protect the retina from damage.

Additionally, bilberry has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the body. This makes it a valuable tool in the treatment of conditions such as arthritis, diabetes, and heart disease. Furthermore, bilberry has been shown to have a positive effect on gut health, helping to improve digestion and absorption of nutrients.

Overall, the medicinal benefits of bilberry make it a valuable addition to any health regimen.

1. Improves eye health

Bilberry improves eye health, meaning it supports and maintains optimal eye function, particularly in low-light conditions or when vision is challenged.

The main active constituents of bilberry that contribute to this benefit are anthocyanins, flavonoids, and tannins. The primary parts of the bilberry plant that provide this advantage are the ripe berries and leaves. The main medicinal preparations of bilberry that offer this benefit include extracts, capsules, and ophthalmic solutions.

This benefit of bilberry is particularly helpful in relieving health conditions such as night blindness, cataracts, and age-related macular degeneration.

10 Best Active Constituents of Vaccinium myrtillus (Bilberry)

Vaccinium myrtillus, also known as bilberry, contains several active constituents that have medicinal properties.

The most notable of these constituents are anthocyanins, which are a type of flavonoid responsible for the plant's blue-purple color. Anthocyanins have been shown to have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-cancer properties.

Additionally, bilberry contains tannins, which have astringent and anti-diarrheal effects, and pectin, a type of fiber that can help regulate blood sugar levels. The leaves of the bilberry plant are also used in herbal medicine and contain compounds such as quercetin and myricetin, which have been studied for their potential benefits in treating conditions such as diabetes, cataracts, and varicose veins.

Overall, the active constituents of Vaccinium myrtillus make it a valuable herb for a variety of medicinal purposes.

1. Anthocyanins

The anthocyanins contained in Vaccinium Myrtillus are a type of compound called flavonoids that possess potent antioxidant properties.

Vaccinium myrtillus anthocyanins have been shown to help relieve several health conditions, such as diabetes, diarrhea, and eye-related disorders like cataracts and macular degeneration. These compounds are found mainly in the fruit and leaves of the bilberry plant.

Possible side effects of Vaccinium myrtillus anthocyanins, if used improperly, may include stomach upset, increased stool production, and interaction with certain medications, such as blood thinners.

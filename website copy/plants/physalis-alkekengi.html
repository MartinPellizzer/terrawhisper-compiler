What to know before using Physalis alkekengi (Winter Cherry)?

Physalis alkekengi, also known as the Chinese lantern plant, has a number of medicinal properties.

Its fruit and leaves contain high levels of vitamin C and carotene, and have been used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and respiratory infections. In horticultural settings, Physalis alkekengi prefers full sun and well-drained soil, and can be propagated through seed or division. The plant is characterized by its distinctive paper-like, lantern-like calyx that surrounds and protects the fruit, and its small, white flowers.

It is important to note that while the fruit of Physalis alkekengi is edible, the rest of the plant contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.

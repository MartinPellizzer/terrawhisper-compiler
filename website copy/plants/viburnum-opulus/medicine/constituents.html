10 Best Active Constituents of Viburnum opulus (Cramp Bark)

Viburnum opulus, also known as cramp bark or snowball tree, contains several active constituents that are used in medicinal preparations.

The bark of the plant is most commonly used and contains various compounds such as valtrate, scopoletin, and various iridoid glycosides. Valtrate, a type of iridoid glycoside, has been found to have anti-spasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties, making it useful for treating muscle cramps and spasms.

Scopoletin, another active constituent, has been shown to have anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and sedative effects. Additionally, the bark contains tannins, which have astringent properties and can be used to treat diarrhea and other digestive issues.

Overall, the active constituents in Viburnum opulus work together to provide a range of medicinal benefits, including anti-spasmodic, anti-inflammatory, analgesic, sedative, and astringent effects.

1. Valerianate

The valerianate contained in Viburnum Opulus is a chemical compound that is known for its sedative and muscle relaxant properties.

Its main medicinal properties include its ability to reduce muscle spasms, alleviate menstrual cramps, and promote relaxation. This constituent is particularly helpful in relieving conditions such as menstrual pain, restless leg syndrome, and insomnia. Valerianate is found mainly in the bark of the Viburnum opulus plant, particularly in the outer layer of the bark.

Improper use of this constituent can lead to several side effects, including dizziness, stomach upset, and drowsiness.

2. Hydrangeic acid

The hydrangeic acid contained in Viburnum Opulus is a chemical compound that contributes to the plant's medicinal properties.

Its main medicinal properties include anti-spasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects, making it a valuable constituent in herbal medicine. This compound is particularly useful in relieving muscle spasms, menstrual cramps, and intestinal cramping, thus helping to alleviate symptoms of conditions such as dysmenorrhea and irritable bowel syndrome. Hydrangeic acid is found mainly in the bark of the Viburnum opulus plant, particularly in the outer layer of the branches and root bark.

Improper use of hydrangeic acid can lead to some side effects, including stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea, and it should be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

The ellagic acid contained in Viburnum Opulus is a polyphenolic bioflavonoid antioxidant that is found in a variety of plants, including the bark, leaves, and fruits of the Viburnum opulus plant.

Its main medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antioxidant effects, as well as the ability to inhibit the growth of certain types of cancer cells. This constituent can help relieve various health conditions such as gastrointestinal disorders, menstrual cramps, and high blood pressure. Ellagic acid is found mainly in the bark and leaves of the Viburnum opulus plant.

Some possible side effects of this Viburnum opulus constituent if used improperly include nausea, stomach upset, and allergic reactions.

The salicylic acid contained in Viburnum Opulus is a type of plant compound that shares chemical similarities with aspirin.

This compound is known for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic properties. It helps relieve health conditions such as muscle cramps, menstrual cramps, and inflammatory conditions. The bark of the Viburnum opulus plant contains the highest concentration of salicylic acid.

Improper use of salicylic acid can lead to side effects such as skin irritation, stomach upset, and in rare cases, ringing in the ears.

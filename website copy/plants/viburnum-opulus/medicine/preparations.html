By Martin Pellizzer
1 minutes

Viburnum opulus, also known as cramp bark or snowball tree, has a long history of use in medicinal preparations.

Its bark is commonly used to make a tisane, an herbal tea, which is believed to help alleviate menstrual cramps and muscle spasms due to its antispasmodic properties. A tincture can also be made from the bark, which is taken orally for its sedative and analgesic effects.

The berries of Viburnum opulus are sometimes used in herbal medicine as well, often in the form of a tincture or syrup, to treat diarrhea and fever. However, it is important to note that the use of Viburnum opulus in medicinal preparations should be done under the guidance of a healthcare professional, as it can have potential side effects and interact with certain medications.

Additionally, the bark and berries of the plant should not be consumed in their raw form, as they can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

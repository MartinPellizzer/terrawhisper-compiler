What to know before using Panax notoginseng (Notoginseng)?

Panax notoginseng, also known as traditional Chinese medicine's "miracle root," possesses powerful medicinal properties, particularly in promoting blood circulation, reducing blood stasis, and alleviating pain.

This herbaceous perennial plant thrives in a cool, moist, and shaded environment, often found in the rich soil of mountain forests at elevations of 500-2000 meters. Panax notoginseng boasts distinct botanical characteristics, including a compact, slowly spreading habit, with a thick, fleshy taproot that stores nutrients, and palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

Its small, greenish-white flowers bloom in the late spring, developing into bright red berries that contain numerous tiny seeds.

What are the possible side effects of Panax notoginseng?

Panax notoginseng can have some side effects if used improperly, such as causing digestive issues like upset stomach, diarrhea, and nausea in some individuals.

Prolonged use or excessive intake of notoginseng can result in dizziness, confusion, and restlessness, which are indications of potential overdose. Moreover, it may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and result in increased bleeding or bruising, making it crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.

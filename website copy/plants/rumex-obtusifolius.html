What to know before using Rumex obtusifolius (Bitter Dock)?

Rumex obtusifolius, also known as broad-leaved dock or butter dock, is a perennial plant that is native to Europe and western Asia but has since naturalized in many parts of the world.

Medicinally, the plant has a long history of use as a demulcent, emollient, and astringent. It contains various compounds such as tannins, anthraquinones, and flavonoids that contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In horticulture, Rumex obtusifolius prefers moist, fertile soil and partial shade, and it can thrive in a variety of soil types. It can grow up to 3-4 feet tall and has large, arrow-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy on the underside.

The flowers of Rumex obtusifolius are greenish-red, and they bloom in dense spikes from June to August.

10 Best Health Benefits of Crataeva nurvala (Varuna)

Crataeva nurvala, also known as varuna, is a plant that has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for centuries.

One of the key medicinal benefits of Crataeva nurvala is its ability to support urinary tract health, as it is believed to help cleanse the urinary system and reduce the risk of urinary tract infections. Additionally, the plant is often used to treat kidney stones, as it is thought to help break down and remove kidney stones from the body.

Crataeva nurvala is also believed to have anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential treatment for conditions such as arthritis and gout. Some research suggests that the plant may also have liver-protecting properties, and it is sometimes used to treat liver disorders.

Finally, Crataeva nurvala is believed to have wound-healing properties, and it is sometimes used topically to treat cuts, scrapes, and other minor wounds.

1. Alleviates digestive issues

Varuna, or Crataeva nurvala, alleviates digestive issues, meaning it can help soothe and improve the functioning of the digestive system.

The main active constituents of Crataeva nurvala that provide this benefit are flavonoids, tannins, and saponins, which have anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and laxative properties. The primary parts of the Crataeva nurvala plant that provide this benefit are the bark and leaves, which contain the highest concentrations of these active compounds. The main medicinal preparations of Crataeva nurvala that give this benefit include decoctions, powders, and capsules made from the bark or leaves.

This benefit of Crataeva nurvala helps relieve a variety of digestive conditions, including constipation, diarrhea, bloating, and irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), by reducing inflammation, soothing the digestive tract, and promoting regular bowel movements.

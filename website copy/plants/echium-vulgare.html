What to know before using Echium vulgare (Viper'S Bugloss)?

Echium vulgare, also known as viper's bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is known for its tall spikes of blue flowers, which bloom in the summer and are a favorite of bees. Medicinally, Echium vulgare has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.

The plant contains high levels of antioxidants and essential fatty acids, which have been shown to have potential health benefits. In terms of horticultural conditions, Echium vulgare prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is hardy in USDA zones 6-10.

It is a biennial plant, meaning it completes its life cycle in two years, and it can grow up to 3 feet tall.

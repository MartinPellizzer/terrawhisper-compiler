10 Best Active Constituents of Plantago lanceolata (Psyllium)

Plantago lanceolata, also known as ribwort plantain, contains several active constituents that are used for medicinal purposes.

The plant contains iridoid glycosides, including aucubin and catalpol, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Flavonoids, such as luteolin and apigenin, are also present in the plant, and they contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

Additionally, Plantago lanceolata contains mucilage, a substance that forms a gel when it comes into contact with water, which can soothe irritated tissues and provide a protective barrier. The plant also contains verbascoside, a phenylethanoid glycoside with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These active constituents make Plantago lanceolata a valuable herb for treating a variety of ailments, including respiratory infections, digestive issues, and skin irritations.

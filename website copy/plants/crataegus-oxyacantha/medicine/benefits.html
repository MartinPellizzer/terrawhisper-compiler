10 Best Health Benefits of Crataegus oxyacantha (Hawthorn)

Crataegus oxyacantha, commonly known as Hawthorn, is a medicinal plant that has been used for centuries to treat various health conditions.

Its medicinal benefits are primarily derived from its rich phytochemical composition, which includes flavonoids, proanthocyanidins, and triterpene acids. Hawthorn has been shown to have a positive effect on cardiovascular health, particularly in improving cardiac function and reducing blood pressure.

It is also known to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help protect against cellular damage and reduce inflammation in the body. Furthermore, hawthorn has been used to treat digestive issues, such as bloating and indigestion, and may have a calming effect on the nervous system, making it useful in managing anxiety and promoting sleep.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before taking hawthorn or any other supplement, especially for those taking medication or with underlying health conditions.

1. Improves cardiovascular health

Hawthorn improves cardiovascular health, meaning it supports the overall health and functioning of the heart and blood vessels.

The main active constituents of Hawthorn that contribute to this benefit are flavonoids, proanthocyanidins, and saponins, which have been shown to dilate blood vessels, improve blood flow, and protect the heart muscle. The primary parts of the Hawthorn plant that provide this benefit are the berries, leaves, and flowers, which are rich in these beneficial compounds. The main medicinal preparations of Hawthorn that provide this benefit include dried berries, leaves, and flowers, which can be made into teas, tinctures, or encapsulated for convenient use.

The main health conditions that this benefit of Hawthorn helps relieve include hypertension, angina, irregular heartbeat, and mild heart failure, making it a valuable herb for overall heart health.

Crataegus oxyacantha improves cardiovascular health thanks to the active constituents listed below.

  • Flavonoids
  • Tannins
  • Phenolic acids
  • Proanthocyanidins
  • Catechins
  • Epicatechins
  • Quercetin
  • Hyperoside
  • Vitexin
  • Rutin

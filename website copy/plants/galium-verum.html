What to know before using Galium verum (Lady'S Bedstraw)?

Galium verum, also known as Lady's Bedstraw or Meadowsweet, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in moist meadows and along the edges of woodlands, making it an ideal plant for damp and shady garden locations. Galium verum possesses various medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial effects.

Its roots and rhizomes have been traditionally used to treat arthritis, gout, and joint pain. Botanically, Galium verum is a member of the Rubiaceae family with tall stems that can reach up to 80 centimeters in height.

The plant boasts small white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, giving way to round, red berries in late summer.

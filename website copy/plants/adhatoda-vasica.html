What to know before using Adhatoda vasica (Malabar Nut)?

Adhatoda vasica, also known as Malabar Nut, is a valuable medicinal plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

Its leaves and roots have been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat respiratory ailments, such as bronchitis, asthma, and coughs, due to their expectorant, antitussive, and anti-inflammatory properties. This evergreen shrub thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, reaching heights of up to 15 feet. Its simple, ovate leaves are arranged oppositely on the stem, while its tubular, white flowers grow in terminal panicles, attracting butterflies and bees.

The plant's bark and roots contain numerous alkaloids, including vasicine and vasicinone, which are responsible for its medicinal benefits.

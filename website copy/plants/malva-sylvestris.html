What to know before using Malva sylvestris (Common Mallow)?

Malva sylvestris, also known as high mallow or cheeseplant, is a herb native to Europe and Western Asia that has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries.

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it a popular natural remedy for a variety of ailments. Malva sylvestris can be consumed in tea form, extracted for its essential oil, or applied topically as a salve or ointment.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Malva sylvestris prefers full sun but can tolerate partial shade, and requires well-drained soil. It is a hardy plant that can thrive in a variety of environments, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping. Botanically, Malva sylvestris is known for its showy pink to purple flowers, which bloom from early summer to early fall, and its round, lobed leaves.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which also includes hibiscus and okra.

What to know before using Myrica cerifera (Bayberry)?

Myrica cerifera, also known as wax myrtle, is a versatile plant with a range of medicinal properties.

It contains compounds with potential anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antidiarrheal effects, making it a valuable resource in traditional medicine. In terms of horticultural conditions, Myrica cerifera thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and can tolerate a wide range of soil types.

It is a hardy plant that can grow in USDA hardiness zones 7-11. From a botanical perspective, Myrica cerifera is an evergreen shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 20 feet.

It has aromatic, scale-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by blue-gray berries that are covered in a waxy coating.

What to know before using Schisandra chinensis (Schisandra)?

Schisandra chinensis, also known as the five-flavor-fruit, is a woody vine native to forests in China and parts of Russia.

It has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries due to its wide range of medicinal properties, including its ability to act as an adaptogen, anti-oxidant, and anti-inflammatory. Schisandra chinensis prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, and its small, delicate flowers bloom in the late spring. The fruit of the Schisandra chinensis plant is unique, as it is red and small, and has a taste that is said to be sour, sweet, salty, bitter, and pungent all at once.

This fruit is often used in teas, tinctures, and supplements for its potential health benefits.

What are the possible side effects of Schisandra chinensis?

Schisandra chinensis can have some side effects if used improperly, such as stomach discomfort and mild digestive issues, including diarrhea and upset stomach, which are usually mild and temporary.

Additionally, it may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and drugs metabolized in the liver, potentially leading to adverse effects.

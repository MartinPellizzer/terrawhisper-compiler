What to know before using Ferula assa-foetida (Asafoetida)?

Ferula assa-foetida, also known as asafoetida, is a perennial herb that belongs to the Apiaceae family.

It is native to the deserts of Iran and Afghanistan and thrives in well-drained, sandy soil with full sun exposure. The plant produces a thick, foul-smelling resin that is commonly used in Middle Eastern and Indian cuisine for its flavor and aroma.

Medicinally, asafoetida has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues, asthma, and arthritis. Its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antifungal properties make it a valuable addition to any natural medicine cabinet.

The plant itself can grow up to 2-3 meters tall, with umbels of small yellow flowers and hollow, grooved stems.

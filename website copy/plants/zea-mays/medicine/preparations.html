Zea mays, commonly known as corn, has a long history of use in medicinal preparations.

A tisane can be made by steeping the dried corn silk in hot water, which is believed to help with issues such as bladder infections and prostate problems. A tincture can also be made by soaking the corn silk in alcohol, which can be used to treat various ailments including kidney stones and urinary tract infections.

The corn oil, extracted from the germ of the corn, is rich in vitamin E and essential fatty acids, making it a popular ingredient in skincare products for its moisturizing properties. Additionally, the cornstarch, derived from the endosperm of the corn, is used as an anti-diarrheal and to soothe sunburns.

The leaves of Zea mays are also used in traditional medicine, they are applied as a poultice to treat swelling and inflammation.

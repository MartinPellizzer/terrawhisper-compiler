10 Best Health Benefits of Zea mays (Corn)

Zea mays, commonly known as corn, has been found to have various medicinal benefits due to the presence of valuable compounds.

Its oil is rich in vitamin E and fatty acids, making it an excellent moisturizer for skin care, and it also has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe irritations and reduce the signs of aging. Consumption of corn has been linked to improved digestion, as it contains dietary fiber that promotes regular bowel movements and prevents constipation. Furthermore, corn is a good source of ferulic acid, a potent antioxidant that has been shown to have anti-cancer properties, particularly in the prevention of colon cancer.

Lastly, corn contains valuable minerals such as magnesium, potassium, and zinc, which support various bodily functions, including heart health, nerve function, and immune system regulation.

1. Boosts digestive health

Corn boosts digestive health, meaning it aids in the healthy functioning and efficiency of the digestive system.

The main active constituents of corn that contribute to this benefit are its dietary fiber and complex carbohydrates. The primary parts of the corn plant that provide this benefit are the kernels, which are rich in fiber and starch. The main medicinal preparations of corn that provide this benefit include cornmeal, corn flour, and whole corn.

The main health conditions that this benefit of corn helps relieve include constipation, irregular bowel movements, and poor nutrient absorption.

Zea mays boosts digestive health thanks to the active constituents listed below.

  • Polyphenols
  • Flavonoids
  • Carotenoids
  • Phytosterols
  • Phytic acid
  • Oligosaccharides
  • Tannins
  • Lignans
  • Saponins
  • Essential fatty acids

2. Supports eye health

Corn, or Zea mays, supports eye health in a way that means it can help maintain or improve various aspects of vision.

The main active constituents in corn that contribute to this benefit are carotenoids, specifically lutein and zeaxanthin, which are known for their role in eye health. The primary parts of the corn plant that provide this benefit are the kernels, which contain high levels of these carotenoids. The most common medicinal preparation of corn for eye health is through supplementation in the form of lutein and zeaxanthin supplements, which are derived from corn.

The main health conditions that this benefit of corn can help relieve include age-related macular degeneration and cataracts, as well as overall eye fatigue and strain.

Zea mays supports eye health thanks to the active constituents listed below.

  • Lutein
  • Zeaxanthin
  • Beta-carotene
  • Vitamin C
  • Vitamin E
  • Zinc
  • Tocopherols
  • Alpha-linolenic acid
  • Carotenoids
  • Ferulic acid

3. Enhances skin health

This benefit of corn helps relieve various skin conditions such as dryness, roughness, and irritation, as well as more severe issues like eczema and psoriasis.

4. Regulates blood sugar levels

Corn, or Zea mays, regulates blood sugar levels, meaning it helps to maintain normal blood glucose levels and prevent spikes and crashes in blood sugar.

The main active constituents in corn that provide this benefit are carbohydrates, fiber, and certain proteins. The primary parts of the Zea mays plant that have this effect are the endosperm and the germ. The main medicinal preparations of Zea mays that regulate blood sugar include whole corn, cornmeal, and corn flour.

This benefit of corn is particularly helpful for individuals with diabetes, metabolic syndrome, and other conditions that require careful management of blood sugar levels.

5. Reduces risk of anemia

Corn reduces risk of anemia, meaning it helps in preventing a condition characterized by a deficiency of red blood cells or hemoglobin in the blood.

This is mainly due to the presence of easily absorbable forms of iron, vitamin B12, and folic acid in corn. The primary parts of the Zea mays plant that contribute to this benefit are the endosperm and the germ, which are rich in these essential nutrients. Medicinal preparations such as corn flour, cornmeal, and cornstarch are commonly used to increase iron and folic acid intake, thereby reducing the risk of anemia.

The main health conditions that this benefit of Zea mays helps relieve include iron-deficiency anemia and megaloblastic anemia, both of which can lead to fatigue, weakness, and other serious complications if left untreated.

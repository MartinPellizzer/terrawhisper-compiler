What to know before using Cerastium glomeratum (Mouse-Ear Chickweed)?

Cerastium glomeratum, also known as common mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing mat-forming plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It has small, white flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, and oval-shaped, hairy leaves that form dense clusters. This plant is found in various horticultural conditions, often growing in disturbed soils, grasslands, and gardens. Cerastium glomeratum has medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, and has been used in traditional medicine to treat skin conditions and wounds.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ability to tolerate a wide range of conditions, including drought, and its potential use as a groundcover or in rock gardens.

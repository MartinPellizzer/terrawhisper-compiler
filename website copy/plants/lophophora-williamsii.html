What to know before using Lophophora williamsii (Peyote)?

Lophophora williamsii, also known as peyote, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is well-known for its psychoactive alkaloids, particularly mescaline, which have been used in Native American religious and medicinal practices for centuries. This cactus thrives in hot, dry climates with well-draining soil, and can take several years to grow to maturity. Its botanical characteristics include small, button-like stems that grow in a rosette pattern, and small, white hairs or tufts on areoles.

Despite its controversial status due to its psychoactive properties, Lophophora williamsii has been shown to have potential medicinal benefits, including the treatment of various mental health disorders and pain management.

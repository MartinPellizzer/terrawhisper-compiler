What to know before using Sanguinaria canadensis (Bloodroot)?

Sanguinaria canadensis, also known as bloodroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is known for its medicinal properties, including the ability to treat a variety of skin conditions, such as warts and skin cancer, due to its sap containing the toxic alkaloid sanguinarine. In horticultural conditions, bloodroot thrives in moist, well-drained soil in partial shade and can be grown from seeds or divisions.

Botanically, Sanguinaria canadensis is characterized by its distinctive white, poppy-like flowers that bloom in early spring and its kidney-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

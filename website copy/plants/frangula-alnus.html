What to know before using Frangula alnus (Alder Buckthorn)?

Frangula alnus, also known as alder buckthorn, is a shrub that is native to Europe and parts of Asia but can also be found in North America as an introduced species.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. The bark of Frangula alnus is well-known for its medicinal properties; it contains anthraquinones, which are natural laxatives, and is used to treat constipation and other digestive issues. The shrub typically grows to a height of 3-6 meters and has smooth, grey bark and ovate, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

The small, greenish-white flowers of Frangula alnus bloom in the spring and are followed by bright red berries in the autumn.

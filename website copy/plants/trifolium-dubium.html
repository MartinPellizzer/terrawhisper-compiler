What to know before using Trifolium dubium (Suckling Clover)?

Trifolium dubium, also known as lesser trefoil or hop trefoil, is a low-growing herb native to Europe and western Asia.

Medicinally, it has been used to treat a variety of ailments, including respiratory and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. In horticultural conditions, Trifolium dubium prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often used as a ground cover or in wildflower meadows.

Botanically, it is characterized by its small, delicate leaves divided into three leaflets, and its spherical clusters of pinkish-red flowers that bloom in the summer.

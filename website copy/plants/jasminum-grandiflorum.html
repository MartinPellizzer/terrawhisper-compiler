What to know before using Jasminum grandiflorum (Jasmine)?

Jasminum grandiflorum, also known as the royal jasmine, is a fragrant and attractive plant native to the mountains of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

Its star-shaped, white flowers, which bloom in summer and fall, release a soothing and pleasant scent that has been used in perfumes and aromatherapy for centuries. Medicinally, Jasminum grandiflorum has anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and sedative properties, making it useful for treating various ailments such as anxiety, insomnia, and skin conditions. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can grow up to 15 feet tall with proper care.

Its glossy, dark green leaves and delicate flowers make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks worldwide.

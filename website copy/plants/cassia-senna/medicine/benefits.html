10 Best Health Benefits of Cassia senna (Senna)

Cassia senna, also known as Senna Alexandrina, is a plant that has been used for its medicinal properties for centuries.

Its primary active components, anthraquinones, have been found to have a laxative effect, making it a popular natural remedy for constipation. Additionally, some studies suggest that Cassia senna may help to reduce inflammation in the body, potentially offering relief for conditions such as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) and hemorrhoids. Furthermore, it may have a positive impact on cholesterol levels and blood sugar control, although more research is needed in these areas.

However, it's important to note that overuse of Cassia senna can lead to dependency and potential liver damage, so it should be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

1. Alleviates Constipation

Senna alleviates constipation, meaning it helps to promote and ease bowel movements by stimulating the intestines and increasing the speed of food passing through the digestive tract.

The main active constituents of Cassia senna that provide this benefit are anthraquinone glycosides, which are converted into active metabolites in the body that increase intestinal motility. The primary parts of the Cassia senna plant that provide this benefit are the leaves and pods, which contain the highest concentration of anthraquinone glycosides. The main medicinal preparations of Cassia senna that provide this benefit are dried leaves and pods, which can be made into teas, capsules, or liquid extracts.

This benefit of Cassia senna is particularly helpful in relieving symptoms of occasional constipation, as well as providing relief for those with conditions such as hemorrhoids, anal fissures, and irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) with constipation.

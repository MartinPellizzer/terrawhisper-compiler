Its leaves and stems contain compounds that have been shown to have astringent, diuretic, and hemostatic properties, making it a valuable tool in traditional medicine for treating wounds, promoting urination, and controlling bleeding. This plant is hardy and adaptable, able to thrive in a variety of horticultural conditions, from full sun to partial shade and in a range of soil types.

As a member of the mustard family, Capsella bursa-pastoris has small, white flowers and slender, upright stems, and produces seed pods that resemble purses, hence its common name.

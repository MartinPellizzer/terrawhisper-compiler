This plant is hardy and prefers full sun, well-drained soil, and can tolerate a range of soil types. It grows low to the ground, typically reaching only 10-30 cm in height, and has small, narrow, and softly hairy leaves that are arranged in a dense, spreading manner.

The flowers of Gnaphalium uliginosum are small, inconspicuous, and are grouped in clusters, appearing from July to September.

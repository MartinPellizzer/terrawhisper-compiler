What to know before using Geum rivale (Water Avens)?

Geum rivale, also known as water avens, is a perennial plant with medicinal properties.

Its roots and rhizomes have been used in traditional medicine to treat diarrhea, dysentery, and stomach ailments. This plant thrives in damp and shady horticultural conditions, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The botanical characteristics of Geum rivale include downy, lobed leaves and striking, pink-red flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer, adding a splash of color to its preferred moist habitats.

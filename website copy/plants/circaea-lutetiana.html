What to know before using Circaea lutetiana (Enchanter'S-Nightshade)?

Circaea lutetiana, also known as enchanter's nightshade, is a plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine to treat skin conditions, inflammation, and fever, thanks to the presence of tannins, phenolic acids, and flavonoids. In terms of horticultural conditions, Circaea lutetiana prefers moist, shady locations and can often be found in woodland settings. As for its botanical characteristics, it is a perennial herb that grows to around 30-50 cm in height, with bell-shaped flowers that are small and white, and clusters of reddish-green or purple berries in the fall.

The plant also has a creeping rhizome, which allows it to spread easily in suitable conditions.

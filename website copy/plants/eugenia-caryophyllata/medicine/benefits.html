10 Best Health Benefits of Eugenia caryophyllata (Clove)

Eugenia caryophyllata, commonly known as clove, is a popular medicinal spice with numerous health benefits.

It has been used traditionally for its antiseptic, analgesic, and anti-inflammatory properties. The main active component of cloves, eugenol, is a natural anesthetic that helps alleviate pain.

Clove oil is also rich in antioxidants, which help protect the body from damage caused by free radicals. Furthermore, studies suggest that cloves may have antimicrobial properties, making it useful in dental care and in preventing infection.

However, it is important to use cloves in moderation, as excessive consumption can cause side effects such as liver toxicity.

1. Alleviates Pain

Clove alleviates pain, meaning it has analgesic properties that can help reduce pain sensitivity in the human body.

The main active constituents in clove responsible for its pain-relieving benefits are eugenol, acetyl eugenol, and beta-caryophyllene. The primary parts of the clove plant that contain these beneficial compounds are the dried flower buds. The most common medicinal preparations of clove used to alleviate pain include essential oils, tinctures, and topical creams or balms.

The main health conditions that this benefit of clove can help relieve include toothaches, headaches, muscle and joint pain, and menstrual cramps.

Eugenia caryophyllata alleviates pain thanks to the active constituents listed below.

  • Carvacrol

  • Gallic acid
  • Ellagic acid
  • Myricetin
  • Quercetin

  • Rutin

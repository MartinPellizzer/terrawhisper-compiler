What to know before using Madhuca longifolia (Butter Tree)?

Madhuca longifolia, also known as the Mahua tree, is a versatile plant with significant medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including inflammation, fever, and skin diseases. The tree's bark, leaves, flowers, and seeds all have medicinal uses.

Madhuca longifolia thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in dry deciduous forests. It can grow up to 20-25 meters tall and has a wide, spreading canopy that provides ample shade. The tree's botanical characteristics include simple, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy, and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The tree also produces a brown, edible fruit that is rich in fat and protein.

What are the medicinal uses of Madhuca longifolia?

Madhuca longifolia has several medicinal uses, such as alleviating inflammation, improving digestion, and boosting the immune system.

This tropical tree is rich in bioactive compounds like triterpenoids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects (National Library of Medicine, 2021). Traditional preparations involve extracting the sap or processing the fruit pulp to create a paste or oil, which is then applied topically or consumed internally for various health conditions. Before planting, prepare the soil by digging a hole that is twice the width and depth of the tree's root ball. Add organic matter, such as compost or well-rotted manure, to improve soil fertility and water retention.

Place the tree in the hole, ensuring that the root collar is at the same level as the surrounding soil. Backfill the hole with soil and water thoroughly.

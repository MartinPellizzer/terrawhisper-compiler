What to know before using Lotus corniculatus (Common Bird'S-Foot Trefoil)?

Lotus corniculatus, also known as bird's-foot trefoil, is a perennial herb with medicinal properties that have been used in traditional medicine.

This plant contains anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial compounds, making it useful for treating wounds, skin infections, and inflammation. Lotus corniculatus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it can tolerate a wide range of soil types, including sandy and clay soils. As for its botanical characteristics, Lotus corniculatus has compound leaves with five leaflets and produces yellow or orange pea-like flowers that grow in clusters.

The plant's seed pods are distinctive, resembling a bird's foot, which gives it its common name.

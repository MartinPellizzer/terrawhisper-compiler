What to know before using Viola odorata (Sweet Violet)?

Viola odorata, also known as sweet violet, is a perennial plant native to Europe and Asia, now widely cultivated in North America.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, which include expectorant, demulcent, and mildly laxative effects. The plant has heart-shaped, gently serrated leaves and produces purple flowers in the early spring. Viola odorata thrives in well-drained, moist soil and partial shade, and it can also tolerate full sun.

Its fragrant flowers are often used in perfumes, and its leaves are used in teas and tinctures for their soothing, anti-inflammatory effects.

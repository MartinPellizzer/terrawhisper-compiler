The plant contains several bioactive compounds, including iridoid glycosides and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal uses. In terms of horticultural conditions, Monsonia ovata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, with its fleshy, finger-like leaves and stems forming a low mound.

Its striking pink to red flowers, which appear in late spring and early summer, are a favorite of hummingbirds and butterflies, adding a touch of beauty to any garden.

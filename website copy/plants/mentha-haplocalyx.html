What to know before using Mentha haplocalyx (Bo He)?

Mentha haplocalyx, also known as Chinese Mint or Bo He, is a herbaceous perennial plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to China and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries due to its medicinal properties. The plant contains high levels of rosmarinic acid, menthol, and other volatile oils, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects.

Mentha haplocalyx prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can grow in a variety of soil types. It is a hardy plant that can tolerate cold temperatures, but it requires regular watering and should be pruned back in the late fall.

The plant grows to be about 1-2 feet tall and has bright green, slightly serrated leaves and purple flowers that bloom in the summer.

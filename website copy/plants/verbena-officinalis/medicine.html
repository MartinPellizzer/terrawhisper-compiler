Verbena officinalis (Vervain) Medicinal Guide

Verbena officinalis, also known as vervain, has a long history of use in traditional medicine for treating various ailments.

The plant contains various constituents such as iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and volatile oils, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties. Preparations of Verbena officinalis include teas, tinctures, and capsules, which are used to treat anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues.

However, it can cause side effects such as stomach upset, skin rashes, and allergic reactions in some people. Additionally, pregnant women and individuals with kidney or liver disease should avoid using Verbena officinalis, as it may exacerbate these conditions.

Therefore, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using Verbena officinalis for medicinal purposes.

What are the active constituents of Verbena officinalis (Vervain)?

Verbena officinalis has several active constituents, such as Verbenalin, a glycoside with potential sedative and anti-inflammatory effects.

Aetheticin, a type of sterol, is also present and may have cholesterol-lowering properties. Verbascoside, a phenylethanoid glycoside, has been studied for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

Lupeol, Ursolic acid, and Oleanolic acid are triterpenes that have been researched for their potential anticancer and anti-inflammatory properties. Luteolin, Apigenin, Rutin, Quercetin, Hyperoside, Isoquercitrin, and Luteolin-7-glucoside are flavonoids, which have been studied for their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer effects.

Hesperidin, Naringin, Rosmarinic acid, Caftaric acid, Clitorin, Stachydrine, Hypoxside are other polyphenols found in vervain, which have been studied for their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective effects.

What are the medicinal preparations of Verbena officinalis (Vervain)?

Verbena officinalis has several medicinal preparations, including infusions and teas, which are made by steeping the plant's leaves or flowers in hot water to extract their beneficial compounds.

Tinctures and fluid extracts are created by soaking the plant in alcohol or another solvent to extract the medicinal constituents. Dry extracts are produced by concentrating and drying the extract, while capsules and tablets offer a convenient way to consume the herb in a standardized dose.

Topical preparations include compresses, which involve soaking a cloth in the infusion and applying it to the skin, and ointments, salves, and balms, which are made by combining the extract with a fatty base to create a cream or paste. Essential oils are distilled from the plant and can be used in aromatherapy or diluted for topical application. Liniments and gargles are liquid preparations used for external application or rinsing, while syrups and wines are sweetened alcoholic preparations used for internal use.

Other forms of vervain preparations include bath salts, mouthwashes, ear drops, suppositories, soaps, and poultices, which are made by applying a warm, moist mixture of the herb directly to the skin.

The following list shows the medicinal preparations of Verbena officinalis.

  • 1. Infusion: A preparation made by steeping Verbena officinalis in boiling water, often used for digestive issues and as a mild sedative.
  • 2. Tincture: A concentrated extract of Verbena officinalis, preserved in alcohol, taken in small doses for its nervine and anti-inflammatory properties.
  • 3. Dry extract: A concentrated powdered extract of Verbena officinalis, produced by removing the solvent from a liquid extract, used in capsules or as an ingredient in other preparations.
  • 4. Fluid extract: A concentrated, liquid extract of Verbena officinalis, prepared by percolation or maceration, and used in smaller doses than an infusion.
  • 5. Capsules: A standardized, measured dose of Verbena officinalis dry extract, enclosed in a gelatin or vegetarian capsule, taken for its medicinal properties.
  • 6. Compress: A cloth soaked in a strong infusion or decoction of Verbena officinalis, applied topically to relieve inflammation, pain, or promote healing.
  • 7. Tea: An infusion of Verbena officinalis leaves and flowers, consumed as a beverage for its calming, digestive, and diuretic effects.
  • 8. Bath salts: A preparation of Verbena officinalis, often combined with Epsom salts or baking soda, added to bath water to soothe sore muscles and promote relaxation.
  • 9. Essential oil: A highly concentrated, aromatic oil obtained by steam distillation of Verbena officinalis leaves and flowers, used topically or in aromatherapy for its calming and uplifting effects.
  • 10. Ointment: A semi-solid preparation of Verbena officinalis, mixed with a fatty base or wax, used topically for its soothing, anti-inflammatory, and healing properties.

What are the possible side effects of using Verbena officinalis (Vervain) for medicinal purposes?

Verbena officinalis can have side effects if used improperly.

Some possible side effects include gastrointestinal disturbances such as upset stomach, nausea, vomiting, loss of appetite, and diarrhea. Additionally, it can cause skin-related issues like rash and allergic reaction.

Moreover, it may lead to neurological symptoms such as headache, dizziness, sedation, and insomnia. Furthermore, it can affect the urinary system, resulting in increased urination and hypotension.

Lastly, it can cause muscle cramps, and in some cases, it can lower blood pressure.

The following list shows the possible side effects of Verbena officinalis.

  • 1. Upset stomach: Verbena officinalis may cause digestive discomfort, leading to symptoms such as stomach pain or cramps.
  • 2. Nausea: The use of Verbena officinalis can sometimes result in feelings of queasiness or the urge to vomit.
  • 3. Vomiting: In rare cases, Verbena officinalis may cause individuals to vomit, possibly due to its strong effects on the digestive system.
  • 4. Headache: Consumption of Verbena officinalis can occasionally result in tension headaches or migraines due to its stimulating properties.
  • 5. Skin rash: Allergic reactions to Verbena officinalis may manifest as skin rashes, including hives or red, itchy patches.
  • 6. Allergic reaction: Some individuals may experience allergies to Verbena officinalis, resulting in symptoms such as swelling, difficulty breathing, or anaphylaxis in severe cases.
  • 7. Dizziness: Verbena officinalis may cause lightheadedness or dizziness, particularly when consumed in large quantities or by sensitive individuals.
  • 8. Sedation: The use of Verbena officinalis can lead to feelings of drowsiness or sedation, making it unsuitable for tasks requiring alertness.
  • 9. Dry mouth: Verbena officinalis may reduce saliva production, leading to a dry, parched sensation in the mouth.
  • 10. Increased urination: Verbena officinalis can have diuretic effects, potentially increasing the frequency of urination.

What are the precautions to take when using Verbena officinalis (Vervain) medicinally?

Before using Verbena officinalis for medicinal purposes, it's important to take some precautions such as consulting a healthcare provider first to ensure it's safe for your condition and appropriate dosage.

It's crucial to check for any allergies and limit the dosage to avoid potential side effects. Additionally, Verbena officinalis should be avoided during pregnancy, breastfeeding, and by individuals taking central nervous system depressants, sedatives, narcotics, alcohol, or anticoagulants. Furthermore, individuals should monitor blood sugar levels, check for hormone sensitivity, discontinue use before surgery, limit sun exposure, check for drug interactions, and monitor for side effects.

Additionally, individuals on thyroid medication, breastfeeding, experiencing worsened anxiety, or with children should consult a healthcare provider before use.

The following list shows the precautions to take when using Verbena officinalis.

  • 1. Consult a healthcare provider first: It is important to seek professional medical advice before using Verbena officinalis to ensure it is appropriate for your health condition and to determine the correct dosage.
  • 2. Check for allergies: Before using Verbena officinalis, make sure you are not allergic to it or any of its components to avoid adverse reactions.
  • 3. Limit dosage: Do not exceed the recommended dosage of Verbena officinalis, as high doses can cause side effects such as stomach upset and skin rashes.
  • 4. Avoid during pregnancy: Pregnant women should avoid using Verbena officinalis, as it may stimulate menstruation and potentially cause miscarriage.
  • 5. Avoid breastfeeding: Nursing mothers should avoid using Verbena officinalis, as it is not known if the herb can pass into breast milk and harm the baby.
  • 6. Avoid mixing with central nervous system depressants: Combining Verbena officinalis with other central nervous system depressants, such as benzodiazepines and barbiturates, can increase the risk of drowsiness, confusion, and difficulty breathing.
  • 7. Avoid mixing with sedatives: Using Verbena officinalis with sedatives, such as opioids and antihistamines, can enhance the sedative effects and increase the risk of respiratory depression.
  • 8. Avoid mixing with narcotics: Combining Verbena officinalis with narcotics, such as codeine and oxycodone, can increase the risk of respiratory depression, sedation, and other adverse effects.
  • 9. Avoid mixing with alcohol: Consuming alcohol while using Verbena officinalis can increase the risk of drowsiness, dizziness, and other adverse effects.
  • 10. Avoid mixing with anticoagulants: Using Verbena officinalis with anticoagulants, such as warfarin, can increase the risk of bleeding and other adverse effects.

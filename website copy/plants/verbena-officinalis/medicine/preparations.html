Verbena officinalis, also known as vervain, has a long history of use in medicinal preparations.

One popular preparation is a tisane, or herbal tea, made by steeping the leaves and flowers in hot water. This soothing drink is often used to treat anxiety, insomnia, and headaches, and is also believed to promote menstruation and milk production in nursing mothers.

A tincture can also be made by soaking the plant material in alcohol, which extracts the medicinal compounds and preserves them for later use. This concentrated extract can be taken in small doses to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues, respiratory infections, and skin conditions.

However, it is important to note that vervain can have side effects and interact with certain medications, so it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before using this or any other herbal remedy.

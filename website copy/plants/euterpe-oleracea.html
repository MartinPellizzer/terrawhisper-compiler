What to know before using Euterpe oleracea (Acai)?

Euterpe oleracea, also known as acai, is a species of palm tree native to Central and South America, particularly Brazil.

It thrives in humid, tropical environments and can grow up to 15-30 meters tall. The berries of E. oleracea are rich in antioxidants, fiber, and heart-healthy fats, making them a popular ingredient in various health products.

These berries have been traditionally used in medicinal teas to treat various ailments, including arthritis, high cholesterol, and skin issues. The tree's leaves and trunk also have medicinal uses, such as in the treatment of fevers and diarrhea. Cultivating E.

oleracea requires specific horticultural conditions, including well-drained soil and consistent watering, and it can be propagated through seeds or offshoots.

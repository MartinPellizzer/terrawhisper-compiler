Its yellow-green sap contains alkaloids that have been used in traditional medicine to treat warts, skin conditions, and digestive issues. This herbaceous perennial thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, growing up to 1 meter tall with lobed, toothed leaves and clusters of yellow flowers.

Despite its benefits, Chelidonium majus can be toxic in large amounts and should be used with caution.

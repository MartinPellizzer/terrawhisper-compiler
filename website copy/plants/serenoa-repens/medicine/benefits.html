10 Best Health Benefits of Serenoa repens (Saw Palmetto)

Serenoa repens, also known as Saw Palmetto, is a plant native to the southeastern United States and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

It is best known for its potential benefits in treating symptoms of an enlarged prostate, such as difficulty urinating and frequent urination. Saw Palmetto is believed to work by reducing the production of dihydrotestosterone, a hormone that contributes to prostate growth. Additionally, some studies suggest that Saw Palmetto may also help alleviate symptoms of chronic bronchitis and reduce hair loss.

However, more research is needed to fully understand its medicinal properties and potential side effects.

1. Improves prostate health

Saw Palmetto improves prostate health, meaning it supports the overall well-being of the prostate gland, which is crucial for men's urinary and reproductive health.

The main active constituents of Serenoa repens responsible for this benefit are fatty acids, sterols, and flavonoids. The primary parts of the Serenoa repens plant that give this benefit are the ripe berries. The main medicinal preparations of Serenoa repens that give this benefit include extracts in the form of capsules or tablets, often standardized to contain a specific amount of fatty acids.

The main health conditions that this benefit of Serenoa repens helps relieve are symptoms associated with benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), such as difficulty in beginning the flow of urine, weak urine stream, and frequent nighttime urination.

Serenoa repens improves prostate health thanks to the active constituents listed below.

  • Resin
  • Volatile Oil
  • Tannins

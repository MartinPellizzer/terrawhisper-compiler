Serenoa repens (Saw Palmetto) Medicinal Guide

Serenoa repens, also known as Saw Palmetto, has been traditionally used in medicinal practices for its potential health benefits.

The berries of this plant contain constituents such as fatty acids, plant sterols, and flavonoids, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties. Preparations of Serenoa repens typically come in the form of capsules, tablets, or liquid extracts, and are often used to treat symptoms associated with an enlarged prostate, such as difficulty urinating and frequent nighttime urination. Although Serenoa repens is generally considered safe, some potential side effects include stomach discomfort, headache, and rare cases of liver damage.

It is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen, as Serenoa repens may interact with other medications and may not be suitable for individuals with certain medical conditions.

What are the medicinal preparations of Serenoa repens (Saw Palmetto)?

Serenoa repens has several medicinal preparations, such as infusions and decoctions made by steeping the berries in hot water, which are used to treat symptoms of benign prostatic hyperplasia.

Tinctures and fluid extracts are prepared by soaking the berries in alcohol, and then the liquid is filtered off, resulting in a potent extract that can be used to treat similar conditions. Tablets and capsules are made by grinding and encapsulating the dried berries, while soft extracts are prepared by extracting the active compounds from the berries using a solvent and then evaporating the solvent, leaving behind a soft, extractable mass. Standardized extracts are similar to soft extracts but are made to contain a consistent amount of the active compounds.

Lastly, suppositories, ointments, liniments, and oil extracts are also prepared from saw palmetto and are used for their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

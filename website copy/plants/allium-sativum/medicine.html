Allium sativum (Garlic) Medicinal Guide

Allium sativum, commonly known as garlic, has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries due to its numerous health benefits.

It contains active constituents such as allicin, alliin, and ajoene, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties. Garlic can be prepared in various ways, including eating raw or cooked cloves, taking garlic supplements, or using garlic oil. However, it can cause side effects such as bad breath, heartburn, and upset stomach, especially when consumed in large amounts.

Therefore, it is important to take precautions when consuming garlic, such as avoiding it before surgeries or if you have a bleeding disorder, as it can increase the risk of bleeding.

What are the active constituents of Allium sativum (Garlic)?

Allium sativum has several active constituents that contribute to its health benefits.

Alliin, a sulfur-containing compound, is converted to allicin when garlic is crushed or chewed, which has antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. S-Allyl cysteine is another compound that is believed to contribute to garlic's cardiovascular benefits by reducing the risk of platelet aggregation and lowering blood pressure.

Fructosans, a type of carbohydrate found in garlic, have prebiotic effects and promote the growth of beneficial gut bacteria. Additionally, garlic contains various flavonoids such as quercetin, kaempferol, myricetin, and rutin, which have antioxidant properties and contribute to garlic's anti-cancer effects.

Lastly, garlic is a rich source of vitamins C and B6, which support the immune system and are essential for various metabolic processes in the body.

What are the possible side effects of using Allium sativum (Garlic) for medicinal purposes?

Allium sativum can have side effects if used improperly, such as breath and body odor, heartburn, and nausea.

Additionally, it may cause digestive problems like diarrhea and burning sensations in the mouth or stomach. Prolonged use might lead to allergic reactions, increased bleeding risk, and a drop in blood pressure. Moreover, some people may experience skin rashes after handling garlic or consuming it in large amounts.

Therefore, it is essential to use garlic in moderation and be aware of these potential side effects.

The following list shows the possible side effects of Allium sativum.

  • Breath Odor: Allium sativum may cause breath odor when ingested due to the sulfur compounds it releases upon digestion, which are then absorbed into the bloodstream and expelled through the lungs during exhale, giving off a strong and characteristic garlicky scent.
  • Heartburn: Allium sativum may cause heartburn due to its potent sulfur compounds that stimulate the production of stomach acid, especially when consumed in large amounts or with an empty stomach.
  • Body Odor: Allium sativum may cause body odor due to its release of volatile sulfur compounds when consumed in large amounts or cooked poorly, which can alter the bacterial balance in the gut and lead to increased production of foul-smelling byproducts.
  • Diarrhea: Allium sativum may cause diarrhea when consumed in large quantities or taken as a supplement without proper medical supervision, due to its stimulating effect on the gastrointestinal tract and potential interactions with certain medications.
  • Nausea: Allium sativum may cause nausea when consumed in large quantities or prepared improperly due to the release of an organosulfide compound, allicin, which can irritate the digestive system and stimulate vomiting reflexes.
  • Bleeding Risk: Allium sativum may cause bleeding risk due to its active component, allicin, which inhibits platelet aggregation and thins blood, but when taken in excessive amounts or combined with other blood-thinning medications, it can increase the risk of uncontrolled bleeding.
  • Allergic Reaction: Allium sativum may cause allergic reaction due to its sulfur compounds, which can trigger symptoms in people sensitive to them, such as nasal congestion, itching, and respiratory issues upon ingestion or contact with the bulb or cooked food.
  • Low Blood Pressure: Allium sativum may cause low blood pressure when consumed in large quantities or combined with certain medications due to its active compounds, such as allicin and organosulfides, which can dilate blood vessels and lower blood pressure.
  • Burning Sensation: Allium sativum may cause burning sensation if it comes into contact with mucous membranes or broken skin due to its potent sulfur compounds releasing a strong irritant effect.
  • Skin Rash: Allium sativum may cause skin rash if applied to the skin directly due to the presence of sulfur compounds which react with sunlight and cause allergic contact dermatitis.

What are the precautions to take when using Allium sativum (Garlic) medicinally?

It's important to take some precautions when using Allium sativum for medicinal purposes, such as consulting a healthcare provider, as they can provide guidance based on your medical history and current medications.

Additionally, avoiding blood thinning medications and checking for allergic reactions can help prevent adverse interactions and reactions. Using therapeutic doses and monitoring for side effects is crucial to ensure effectiveness while minimizing risks. Furthermore, it's important to avoid using Allium sativum during pregnancy, excessive intake, and in diabetes, unless under the supervision of a healthcare provider.

Proper storage, use of organic and fresh Allium sativum, and using it with caution are also essential precautions to maximize its medicinal benefits.

The following list shows the precautions to take when using Allium sativum.

  • Consult Healthcare Provider: Consult healthcare provider before using Allium sativum, as it may interact with certain medications and cause side effects like bleeding or allergic reactions, which can be harmful if not properly managed.
  • Avoid Blood Thinning Medications: Avoid blood thinning medications when consuming Allium sativum, as its active component, allicin, can enhance the blood thinning effects of such medications leading to an increased risk of bleeding and potential complications.
  • Check For Allergic Reactions: Check for allergic reactions before consuming Allium sativum (garlic) to prevent potential adverse effects, such as gastrointestinal discomfort, respiratory symptoms, or contact dermatitis, which can occur in sensitive individuals due to its active compounds.
  • Use Therapeutic Doses: Use therapeutic doses of Allium sativum (garlic) because consuming large amounts may increase the risk of bleeding, interact with certain medications, and cause unpleasant side effects like body odor and bad breath.
  • Monitor For Side Effects: Monitor for side effects when using Allium sativum (garlic) because it can interact with various medications and cause side effects such as increased risk of bleeding, low blood sugar levels, and altered liver function, which may lead to serious health complications.
  • Do Not Use During Pregnancy: Do not use during pregnancy because of its potential to stimulate menstruation and increase the risk of miscarriage, as well as causing fetal toxicity due to its organosulfur compounds.
  • Avoid Excessive Intake: Avoid excessive intake of Allium sativum due to its strong organic sulfur compounds, which can cause digestive issues, interactions with medications like blood thinners, and potential for anemia if consumed in large quantities over extended periods.
  • Use With Caution In Diabetes: Use with caution in diabetes because Allium sativum, also known as garlic, can lower blood sugar levels and interact with medications used to manage diabetes, leading to potential hypoglycemia or hyperglycemic episodes.
  • Store Properly: Store properly to prevent potent organic sulphur compounds, such as allicin, from being degraded or lost, preserving their health benefits including powerful anti-bacterial and anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Use Organic And Fresh : Use organic and fresh Allium sativum (garlic) to prevent potential contamination by heavy metals, pesticides, or other harmful chemicals that can lead to health risks such as allergic reactions, immune system suppression, and even cancer.

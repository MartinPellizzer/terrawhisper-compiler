10 Best Active Constituents of Allium sativum (Garlic)

Allium sativum, commonly known as garlic, has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries due to its active constituents.

The two primary active components are allicin and diallyl sulfide. Allicin is known for its antibacterial, antiviral, and antifungal properties, making it a popular natural remedy for various infections.

Diallyl sulfide, on the other hand, has been found to have chemopreventive effects, helping to reduce the risk of certain cancers. These compounds are most concentrated in fresh garlic, and their health benefits are best realized when garlic is consumed raw or lightly cooked.

However, it is important to note that excessive consumption of garlic can have side effects, such as digestive discomfort, and it can interact with certain medications, so it is always advisable to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.

1. Alliin

The alliin contained in Allium sativum is an organic compound that is responsible for garlic's distinctive smell and taste.

This compound has been found to have several medicinal properties, including antibacterial, antiviral, and anti-fungal effects. Alliin has been shown to help relieve various health conditions such as high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and cardiovascular diseases.

This compound is mainly found in the bulb of the garlic plant. However, improper use of alliin can lead to side effects such as digestive discomfort, bad breath, and body odor.

Additionally, high doses of alliin can cause skin irritation and even thin the blood, so it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before taking garlic supplements or incorporating large amounts of raw garlic into your diet.

It is important to note that high doses of allicin can be harmful and it is recommended to consult a healthcare professional before taking allicin supplements.

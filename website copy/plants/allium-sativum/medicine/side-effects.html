10 possible side effects of Allium sativum (Garlic) if used improperly

Allium sativum, commonly known as garlic, is a flavorful and beneficial herb when used properly, but it can have negative health effects if misused.

Improper use of garlic can lead to gastrointestinal symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea due to its strong fiber content. Garlic has been known to lower blood pressure and thin the blood, so overconsumption can increase the risk of bleeding and bruising, especially for those taking blood-thinning medication.

Additionally, prolonged or excessive intake of raw garlic may cause damage to the stomach and liver, potentially leading to conditions like gastritis and hepatotoxicity. Furthermore, those with a garlic allergy may experience allergic reactions, such as skin rashes or difficulty breathing, if they come into contact with or consume garlic.

Therefore, it is crucial to use and consume Allium sativum correctly to avoid these potential adverse health effects.

1. Breath Odor

Breath odor caused by Allium Sativum refers to the distinctive smell that remains on a person's breath after consuming this plant.

This odor is primarily caused by sulfur-containing compounds, such as allyl methyl sulfide and diallyl disulfide, which are released when garlic is crushed or chewed. These compounds are absorbed into the bloodstream and exhaled through the lungs, leading to the characteristic garlic breath.

Medicinal preparations of garlic, such as garlic oil, garlic powder, and garlic extract, can all cause breath odor if used in excessive amounts or for extended periods of time. Individuals who take garlic supplements, eat large amounts of raw garlic, or use garlic in cooking are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid garlic breath, it is recommended to brush and floss regularly, chew sugar-free gum, or eat parsley or raw apples, which have been shown to help neutralize the odor.

Additionally, taking garlic supplements with meals or using enteric-coated garlic products may reduce the likelihood of developing garlic breath.

2. Heartburn

Heartburn caused by Allium Sativum refers to a burning sensation in the chest or upper abdomen, often experienced after consuming garlic.

This discomfort is primarily due to the active constituents of garlic, namely allicin, alliin, and ajoene, which can relax the lower esophageal sphincter and allow stomach acid to back up into the esophagus. The main medicinal preparations of garlic that can cause this side effect if misused include garlic oil, garlic powder, and garlic extract.

People who consume large amounts of garlic or take garlic supplements in excess are more likely to experience heartburn. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to consume garlic in moderation, avoid consuming it on an empty stomach, and avoid combining it with other heartburn-inducing foods.

Additionally, those who are prone to heartburn may consider consulting with a healthcare provider before starting a garlic supplement regimen.

3. Body Odor

Body odor caused by Allium Sativum refers to the distinctive smell that can be released through perspiration after consuming or handling garlic.

This occurs due to the presence of active constituents in garlic such as allicin, alliin, and ajoene. These compounds are released when garlic is crushed or chewed, and they can be absorbed into the bloodstream and exhaled through the lungs or excreted through the skin, leading to the characteristic garlic odor.

Medicinal preparations such as garlic supplements, oils, and extracts can also cause this side effect if misused or taken in large quantities. Those who are more likely to experience garlic-induced body odor include individuals with certain genetic factors that affect the way their bodies metabolize and eliminate garlic compounds. To avoid garlic-induced body odor, it is recommended to consume garlic in moderation, avoid crushing or chewing garlic before consumption, and use breath fresheners or mouthwashes after eating garlic.

Additionally, consuming a small amount of milk or parsley after eating garlic can help neutralize the odor.

4. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Allium Sativum refers to a condition of loose, watery stools occurring as a side effect of garlic consumption.

The main active constituents in garlic that can cause this side effect are sulfur-containing compounds such as allicin, allyl sulfides, and diallyl disulfide. The main medicinal preparations of garlic that can cause diarrhea if misused include garlic oil, garlic powder, and garlic supplements.

People who are more likely to experience diarrhea after consuming garlic are those with a sensitive digestive system, those who consume large amounts of garlic in a short period of time, and those who are not accustomed to consuming garlic. To avoid experiencing diarrhea from garlic, it is recommended to start with small amounts and gradually increase the dosage, take garlic with meals to reduce its impact on the digestive system, and avoid consuming excessive amounts of garlic in a short period of time.

Additionally, it is important to follow the recommended dosage on garlic supplements and consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.

5. Nausea

Nausea caused by Allium Sativum refers to a queasy or uncomfortable sensation in the stomach that can sometimes lead to vomiting.

This side effect is typically caused by the main active constituents in garlic, which include alliin, alliinase, and allyl sulfur compounds. These compounds are responsible for garlic's pungent odor and medicinal properties, but they can also cause digestive upset in some people.

The main medicinal preparations of garlic that can cause nausea if misused include raw garlic, garlic oil, and garlic supplements. People who are more likely to experience nausea after consuming garlic include those with sensitive stomachs, those who consume large amounts of garlic at once, and those who are not accustomed to consuming garlic in their diet.

To avoid nausea, it is recommended to start with small amounts of garlic and gradually increase the amount over time, to consume garlic with meals rather than on an empty stomach, and to speak with a healthcare provider before taking garlic supplements or using garlic for medicinal purposes.

6. Bleeding Risk

Bleeding risk caused by Allium sativum refers to the increased risk of bleeding that can occur as a side effect of using this plant.

The main active constituents in garlic that can cause this side effect are thought to be the sulfur-containing compounds, such as allicin and ajoene. These compounds can inhibit platelet aggregation, which can increase the risk of bleeding.

The main medicinal preparations of garlic that can cause this side effect if misused include fresh garlic cloves, garlic oil, and garlic powder. People who are at a higher risk of experiencing this side effect include those who are taking anticoagulant medications, those who have a bleeding disorder, and those who have undergone surgery or have an invasive medical procedure. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to consult a healthcare professional before using garlic supplements, especially for those who are at a higher risk of bleeding.

Also, it is important to pay attention to the recommended dosage and not to exceed it.

7. Allergic Reaction

Allergic reaction caused by Allium Sativum refers to an abnormal immune system response upon ingesting or coming into contact with garlic.

The main active constituents responsible for this side effect are alliin, allicin, and other organosulfides. These compounds are found in various medicinal preparations such as fresh garlic cloves, garlic oil, and garlic powder, and can cause allergic reactions when misused or consumed in large quantities.

People with existing allergies to onions or other members of the Allium family are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid this side effect, individuals should start with small doses and gradually increase the amount, while monitoring for any adverse reactions.

It's also recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using garlic as a treatment, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions.

8. Low Blood Pressure

Low blood pressure caused by Allium Sativum refers to an abnormally low level of blood pressure in the body, which can be caused by the regular consumption or misuse of garlic.

The main active constituents in garlic that can lead to this side effect include allicin, alliin, and ajoene. These compounds can cause a relaxation of blood vessels, leading to a decrease in blood pressure.

The main medicinal preparations of garlic that can cause low blood pressure if misused include garlic pills, garlic powder, and garlic oil. Individuals who are currently taking medication for blood pressure or those with already low blood pressure are more likely to experience this side effect. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before starting to consume garlic supplements, especially for those who have pre-existing medical conditions or are taking medications.

It is also important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare professional for proper usage and monitoring of blood pressure levels.

9. Burning Sensation

Burning sensation caused by Allium Sativum refers to a painful or uncomfortable feeling of heat in the mouth, throat, or stomach after consuming garlic.

This sensation is primarily caused by the active constituents in garlic, including allicin, alliin, and ajoene, which can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract. Garlic supplements such as capsules, tablets, and oils are the most likely to cause this side effect if misused.

People who are more susceptible to experiencing this side effect include those with a sensitive digestive system, those who consume large amounts of garlic, and those who take garlic supplements on an empty stomach. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase, to take garlic with meals, and to avoid taking it on an empty stomach.

Additionally, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement regimen.

10. Skin Rash

Skin rash caused by Allium Sativum refers to an unpleasant skin reaction that can occur as a side effect of using this plant.

The main active constituents in garlic that can cause this side effect are allicin, alliin, and ajoene. These compounds are responsible for garlic's distinct smell and flavor, and they can cause skin irritation and rashes in some individuals.

The main medicinal preparations of garlic that can cause this side effect if misused include garlic capsules, garlic oil, and garlic powder. People who are more likely to experience skin rash from using garlic include those with sensitive skin, those who are allergic to garlic, and those who consume large amounts of garlic in their diet. To avoid skin rash from using garlic, it is important to follow the recommended dosages and usage instructions, and to discontinue use if any skin reactions occur.

It is also recommended to perform a patch test before using garlic topically, and to consult a healthcare professional if symptoms persist.

10 Best Health Benefits of Allium sativum (Garlic)

Allium sativum, commonly known as garlic, has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries due to its numerous health benefits.

One of the most well-known medicinal uses of garlic is for its cardiovascular benefits. Garlic has been shown to help reduce blood pressure in hypertensive individuals, as well as improve cholesterol levels, which can help prevent heart disease.

Additionally, garlic has antimicrobial properties and has been used to treat infections, including respiratory and digestive infections. Furthermore, garlic has been found to have anticancer properties, particularly against stomach and colon cancer.

Lastly, garlic has been shown to boost the immune system, helping the body fight off illnesses and infections.

1. Boosts Immune System

Garlic boosts immune system, meaning it helps to strengthen the body's natural defenses against diseases and infections.

The main active constituents of Allium sativum that give this benefit are allicin, alliin, and ajoene, which have been shown to have antimicrobial, antiviral, and anti-inflammatory properties. The primary parts of the Allium sativum plant that give this benefit are the bulb and cloves, which contain the highest concentration of these beneficial compounds. The main medicinal preparations of Allium sativum that give this benefit include fresh garlic, garlic powder, garlic oil, and garlic supplements.

The main health conditions that this benefit of Allium sativum helps relieve include the common cold, flu, and other respiratory infections, as well as providing protection against certain types of cancer and cardiovascular diseases.

Phalaris arundinacea, also known as reed canary grass, has a variety of medicinal properties.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat a range of conditions including fevers, diarrhea, and internal parasites. The plant contains phenolic compounds that have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. When it comes to horticultural conditions, Phalaris arundinacea thrives in wetlands and along the edges of bodies of water, making it a popular choice for erosion control and water filtration in constructed wetlands.

In terms of its botanical characteristics, Phalaris arundinacea is a cool-season perennial grass that can grow up to 10 feet tall, with long, narrow leaves and showy, purplish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring.

What to know before using Taraxacum officinale (Dandelion)?

Taraxacum officinale, commonly known as the dandelion, is a flowering plant with numerous medicinal properties.

Its roots and leaves contain compounds that are believed to have diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects, making it a valuable tool in traditional medicine for treating conditions such as liver disorders, kidney diseases, and urinary tract infections. In horticultural settings, dandelions thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, and can be found growing in a variety of environments, from lawns and meadows to gardens and disturbed areas. Botanically, dandelions are characterized by their bright yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring and give way to seed heads that are dispersed by the wind.

The plants have long, lobed leaves and a deep taproot, which can reach down several feet into the soil.

By Martin Pellizzer
0 minutes

Paeonia lactiflora, also known as the Chinese peony, is a plant that has been used in medicinal preparations for centuries.

One common preparation is a tisane, or herbal tea, made by steeping the dried or fresh root in hot water. This tisane is believed to have various health benefits, including reducing inflammation and alleviating symptoms of premenstrual syndrome.

Another preparation is a tincture, which is made by soaking the root in alcohol to extract its active compounds. The tincture can be taken orally and is thought to have similar benefits to the tisane, as well as potential additional benefits such as pain relief and immune system support. However, it is important to note that the effectiveness of these preparations and their potential side effects have not been thoroughly studied and more research is needed.

Additionally, it is always recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new medicinal regimen.

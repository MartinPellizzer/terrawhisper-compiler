What to know before using Equisetum palustre (Marsh Horsetail)?

Equisetum palustre, also known as swamp horsetail, is a perennial herb that has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

This plant has diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties and has been used in the treatment of kidney stones, urinary tract infections, and rheumatism. Equisetum palustre prefers wet, boggy conditions and can often be found growing in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of streams. It is a hardy plant that can tolerate partial shade and a variety of soil types.

From a botanical perspective, Equisetum palustre is unique in that it is one of the few living species of plants that reproduces via spores rather than seeds, producing them in distinctive cone-like structures at the top of its stems.

What to know before using Bignonia catalpa (Catalpa)?

Bignonia catalpa, also known as Southern catalpa, is a fast-growing tree that is native to the southeastern United States.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and sedative properties, and is said to be effective in treating ailments such as asthma, fever, and anxiety. In horticultural settings, Bignonia catalpa prefers full sun and well-drained soil, and can grow up to 40 feet tall with a spread of 20-30 feet. It is known for its large, heart-shaped leaves and its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

Despite its attractive features, Bignonia catalpa can be invasive in some areas due to its prolific seed production and ability to spread through root suckers.

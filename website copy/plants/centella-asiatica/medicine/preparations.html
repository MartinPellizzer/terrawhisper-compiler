By Martin Pellizzer
1 minutes

Centella asiatica, also known as gotu kola, is a medicinal plant that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Its leaves are often used to make medicinal preparations such as tisanes and tinctures. A tisane is a type of herbal tea that is made by steeping the leaves in hot water. This preparation is believed to have a calming effect on the nervous system and may help to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation.

A tincture, on the other hand, is a more concentrated preparation that is made by soaking the leaves in alcohol. This preparation is thought to have a number of medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and wound-healing effects. Some studies have suggested that Centella asiatica may be helpful in treating a variety of conditions, including anxiety, depression, and insomnia. However, more research is needed to confirm these potential benefits and to understand the optimal dosage and method of administration.

As with any medicinal preparation, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using Centella asiatica.

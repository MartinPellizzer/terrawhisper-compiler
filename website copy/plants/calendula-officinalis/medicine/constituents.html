10 Best Active Constituents of Calendula officinalis (Calendula)

Calendula officinalis, commonly known as marigold, contains several active constituents that are used in medicinal purposes.

The most notable of these constituents are triterpene saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids. Triterpene saponins, such as calendulosides, have anti-inflammatory and immune-stimulating properties, making them useful in the treatment of wounds, burns, and skin irritations.

Flavonoids, including quercetin and isorhamnetin, have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help reduce pain and inflammation. Carotenoids, particularly lycopene and zeaxanthin, have antioxidant properties that can help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals.

Overall, the active constituents of Calendula officinalis work together to provide a range of medicinal benefits, including anti-inflammatory, immune-stimulating, antioxidant, and wound-healing effects.

1. Carotenoids

The carotenoids contained in Calendula Officinalis are a class of powerful antioxidants that are beneficial for skin and eye health.

These pigments, which give calendula its vibrant orange and yellow colors, have anti-inflammatory and immune-stimulating properties. Carotenoids found in calendula have been shown to help relieve various health conditions, including eczema, wounds, and stomach ulcers.

The carotenoids in calendula are most concentrated in the flower heads of the plant. While carotenoids are generally safe and beneficial, improper use of calendula extract or oil can lead to side effects such as skin irritation, itching, or rash.

Additionally, high doses of carotenoids can cause discoloration of the skin and, in rare cases, liver toxicity.

2. Flavonoids

The flavonoids contained in Calendula Officinalis are a type of antioxidant that occur naturally in the plant.

They are known for their anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and anticancer properties. Flavonoids found in calendula have been shown to help relieve conditions such as eczema, gastric ulcers, and wounds.

The main parts of the calendula plant that contain flavonoids are the flowers and leaves. Some possible side effects of calendula flavonoids, if used improperly, include skin irritation, increased sunburn, and mouth sores.

It is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using calendula flavonoids, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

3. Terpenoids

The terpenoids contained in Calendula Officinalis are a class of compounds that have been found to have significant medicinal properties.

These terpenoids have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antibacterial properties, making them useful in treating a variety of health conditions. They are particularly effective in helping to relieve skin conditions such as eczema, cuts, and bruises, and they may also be used to treat stomach ailments and fever.

The terpenoids are found in high concentrations in the flowers of the calendula plant, although they can also be found in the leaves and stems. When used improperly, calendula terpenoids can cause skin irritation and sensitivity to sunlight.

Additionally, they may interact with certain medications, so it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using calendula terpenoids as a treatment.

4. Saponins

The saponins contained in Calendula Officinalis are a type of chemical compound that has both hydrophilic and hydrophobic properties, causing a soap-like foam when shaken in a solution.

These natural compounds are known for their anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and immune-stimulating effects. Calendula saponins have been found to help relieve various health conditions, such as promoting wound healing, reducing pain and inflammation, and providing relief from skin irritations. The saponins are primarily found in the flower heads of the calendula plant.

However, if used improperly or in large amounts, calendula saponins can cause skin and mucous membrane irritation, diarrhea, and abdominal pain.

5. Polysaccharides

The polysaccharides contained in Calendula Officinalis are complex carbohydrates that have immunostimulatory, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing properties.

These polysaccharides have been shown to stimulate the immune system, reduce inflammation, and promote tissue repair and regeneration. Calendula officinalis polysaccharides can help relieve various health conditions, including dermatitis, wounds, and skin irritations. The polysaccharides are found mainly in the flowers and leaves of the calendula plant.

Some possible side effects of calendula polysaccharides, if used improperly, include skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive issues such as nausea and cramping.

6. Volatile oils

The volatile oils contained in Calendula Officinalis are aromatic compounds that evaporate at low temperatures.

These oils are known for their antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic properties. They help relieve skin conditions such as wounds, burns, and rashes, and can also be used to alleviate muscle spasms and menstrual cramps.

The volatile oils are found in all parts of the calendula plant, but are most concentrated in the flowers. Improper use of volatile oils can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and sun sensitivity.

It is important to follow proper dosage instructions and to discontinue use if any adverse reactions occur.

7. Triterpene alcohols

The triterpene alcohols contained in Calendula Officinalis are a group of compounds that have been found to possess significant medicinal properties.

These triterpene alcohols are known for their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making them valuable constituents in calendula. They have been shown to help relieve various health conditions, including skin irritations, wounds, and burns.

The triterpene alcohols are found mainly in the flowers of the calendula plant. However, if used improperly, these constituents can cause skin irritation, itching, or allergic reactions in some individuals.

Therefore, it is important to use calendula products as directed and consult with a healthcare provider if any adverse reactions occur.

8. Sterols

The sterols contained in Calendula Officinalis are natural plant compounds that exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

These sterols are known to help relieve various skin conditions, such as eczema, dermatitis, and wounds, by reducing inflammation, fighting off infection, and promoting tissue repair. They can be found in high concentrations in the flowers and leaves of the calendula plant.

However, when used improperly, calendula sterols can cause skin irritation, itching, or allergic reactions in some individuals, especially when used on broken or sensitive skin.

9. Glycosides

The glycosides contained in Calendula Officinalis are a type of complex compounds that can be found in various parts of the plant.

These glycosides have anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antioxidant properties, making them one of the key medicinal components of calendula. They help relieve various health conditions, such as wounds, burns, and eczema, by promoting tissue regeneration and reducing inflammation. Glycosides are mainly found in the flowers and leaves of the calendula plant.

Some possible side effects of calendula glycosides, if used improperly, include skin irritation, dermatitis, and allergic reactions.

10. Resins

The resins contained in Calendula Officinalis are a complex mixture of compounds that include suberin, communic acid, and flavonoids.

These resins have been found to possess anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antibacterial properties, making them valuable in the treatment of wounds, burns, and skin conditions such as eczema and dermatitis. Calendula resins can also help relieve muscle spasms and promote menstrual flow.

The resins are primarily found in the flower heads of the calendula plant. However, improper use of calendula resins can result in skin irritation, allergic reactions, and increased sun sensitivity.

It is important to follow recommended dosages and usage instructions carefully.

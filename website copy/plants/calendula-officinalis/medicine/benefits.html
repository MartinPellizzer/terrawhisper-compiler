10 Best Health Benefits of Calendula officinalis (Calendula)

Calendula officinalis, commonly known as marigold, is a plant with a long history of medicinal use.

The flowers of this plant contain high levels of antioxidants, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. Topical application of calendula extract has been shown to promote wound healing, reduce inflammation, and alleviate skin irritation.

Moreover, it has been used to treat various skin conditions, including eczema, dermatitis, and diaper rash. Additionally, calendula has been found to have antimicrobial and antiviral effects, making it a valuable addition to skincare products and natural remedies.

However, it is important to note that further research is needed to fully understand the extent of calendula's medicinal benefits and potential interactions with other medications.

1. Speeds up wound healing

Calendula officinalis, or pot marigold, speeds up wound healing, meaning it helps damaged tissue to regenerate at a faster rate than without the treatment, leading to a quicker recovery from injuries, cuts, or wounds.

The main active constituents of Calendula that contribute to its wound-healing properties are triterpenoid esters, flavonoids, and saponins. The primary parts of the Calendula officinalis plant that possess this benefit are the flowers, which contain high amounts of the beneficial compounds. The main medicinal preparations of Calendula officinalis that provide this benefit are topical applications, such as ointments, creams, and tinctures, as well as oral supplements.

This benefit of Calendula officinalis helps relieve various health conditions, including minor wounds, cuts, burns, and skin irritations, by promoting skin regeneration and reducing inflammation.

  • Tannins
  • Volatile oils
  • Resin

  • Sterols

  • Polysaccharides

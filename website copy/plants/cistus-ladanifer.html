What to know before using Cistus ladanifer (Common Gum Cistus)?

Cistus ladanifer, also known as the rockrose or gum rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region that has been valued for its medicinal properties for centuries.

This plant contains several bioactive compounds, such as polyphenols and flavonoids, that have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. In terms of horticultural conditions, Cistus ladanifer prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in Mediterranean climates.

As for its botanical characteristics, Cistus ladanifer is an evergreen shrub that can grow up to 2-3 meters tall, with narrow, linear leaves covered in a sticky resin, and large, pinkish-white flowers with a distinct, sweet fragrance.

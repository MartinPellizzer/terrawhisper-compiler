What to know before using Platanus acerifolia (London Planetree)?

Platanus acerifolia, commonly known as London plane tree, is a popular ornamental tree with distinctive exfoliating bark and attractive maple-like leaves.

It thrives in a variety of horticultural conditions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. The tree has been used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties, and its bark and leaves have been used to treat skin irritations, wounds, and diarrhea. Platanus acerifolia can grow to impressive sizes, with some specimens reaching heights of up to 150 feet, and it is known for its resilience to urban pollution and pests.

Its botanical characteristics include lobed leaves that are green in color and turn yellow in the fall, and small round fruits that contain numerous seeds.

What are the medicinal uses of Platanus acerifolia?

Platanus acerifolia is used to treat various health conditions due to its medicinal properties.

Its bark and leaves contain active constituents such as tannins, polyphenols, and mucilage, which have anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and astringent properties. The tree's bark and leaves are used to prepare decoctions, infusions, and ointments to treat respiratory ailments, skin conditions, and gastrointestinal disorders.

Platanus acerifolia may cause side effects such as nausea, upset stomach, and skin irritation in some individuals. It is important to note that excessive consumption of this plant may lead to liver toxicity.

Therefore, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using Platanus acerifolia for medicinal purposes, especially for children, pregnant or breastfeeding women, and individuals with pre-existing medical conditions.

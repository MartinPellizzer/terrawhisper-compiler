What to know before using Sambucus nigra (Elder)?

Sambucus nigra, commonly known as European elderberry, is a plant with impressive medicinal properties.

Its flowers and berries have been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat colds, flu, and fever due to their antiviral and anti-inflammatory effects. In horticultural terms, Sambucus nigra thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This shrub can grow up to 15 feet tall and wide, producing large, flat-topped clusters of white flowers in the summer, followed by clusters of small, dark purple berries. From a botanical perspective, Sambucus nigra is a deciduous shrub or small tree with compound, serrated leaves and a hollow stem.

Its flowers are hermaphroditic, meaning they have both male and female reproductive parts, and are pollinated by insects.

What are the medicinal uses of Sambucus nigra?

Sambucus nigra has several medicinal uses, such as providing relief for symptoms of common cold and flu.

Its constituents include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and anthocyanins, which contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Preparations of Sambucus nigra include syrups, teas, and capsules, which can be consumed for their potential benefits. However, side effects such as nausea, diarrhea, and vomiting may occur if taken in large amounts.

What are the possible side effects of Sambucus nigra?

Sambucus nigra can have some side effects if used improperly, such as digestive issues including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

What are the precautions to take when using Sambucus nigra?

You should take some precautions when using Sambucus nigra for medicinal purposes, such as carefully selecting the plant parts you use.

While the flowers and ripe berries are commonly used to make teas, syrups, and extracts, other plant parts can be toxic if ingested. It's crucial to avoid consuming unripe berries, leaves, stems, and roots, as they contain a component called sambunigrin, which can break down into cyanide-producing compounds.

Always ensure that the berries are fully ripe, as they turn from red to dark purple or black when ripe, and have a sweet flavor. Moreover, it's important to use properly prepared and processed elder products from reputable sources, as improperly prepared or home-processed remedies may contain harmful substances.

Lastly, pregnant or breastfeeding women, individuals with autoimmune disorders, and those taking prescription medications should consult a healthcare professional before incorporating elder into their health regimen, as interactions and specific safety concerns may apply.

Sambucus nigra needs full sun to partial shade and fertile, moist, well-drained soil for optimal growth.

What to know before using Uncaria rhynchophylla (Gou Teng)?

Uncaria rhynchophylla, also known as Chinese cat's claw, is a woody vine native to East and Southeast Asia.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical forests, requiring well-drained soil and partial shade. The plant is renowned for its medicinal properties, including its ability to treat various neurological disorders.

The main active constituents are alkaloids, such as rhynchophylline and isorhynchophylline, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and neuroprotective effects. Uncaria rhynchophylla is also used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat hypertension and anxiety.

Its botanical characteristics include opposite, ovate leaves and hook-like thorns, from which it derives its name.

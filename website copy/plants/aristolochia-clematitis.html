What to know before using Aristolochia clematitis (Birthwort)?

Aristolochia clematitis, also known as birthwort, is a perennial herb that is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It has distinctive yellow-green flowers that are shaped like an hourglass and emit a putrid odor to attract flies for pollination. The plant contains aristolochic acids, which have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties.

However, recent studies have shown that these compounds can also have harmful effects on the kidneys and liver, and their use is cautioned against. Aristolochia clematitis thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, and can grow up to 1 meter tall. It is a hardy plant that can tolerate a wide range of temperatures, but requires regular watering and fertilization to thrive.

Despite its medicinal uses, the plant is considered invasive in some areas due to its rapid growth and prolific seed production.

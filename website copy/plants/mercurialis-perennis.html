What to know before using Mercurialis perennis (Dog Mercury)?

Mercurialis perennis, also known as dog's mercury, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It has a rich history of medicinal use, particularly in the treatment of kidney and bladder ailments, due to its diuretic and mildly analgesic properties. The plant contains several alkaloids, including mercurialine and mercurialin, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory and expectorant effects.

Mercurialis perennis thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows. It grows to a height of 30-45 cm, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in pairs along the stem.

The small, greenish-yellow flowers are dioecious, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

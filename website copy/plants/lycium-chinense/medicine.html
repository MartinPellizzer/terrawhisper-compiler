Lycium chinense (Goji Berry) Medicinal Guide

Lycium chinense, also known as Chinese boxthorn or wolfberry, has a long history of use in traditional Chinese medicine for its various health benefits.

The major constituents of Lycium chinense include polysaccharides, betaine, and various vitamins and minerals, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Common preparations of Lycium chinense include teas, extracts, and capsules, which are used to treat a variety of ailments such as fatigue, vision problems, and liver and kidney dysfunction.

However, it is important to note that Lycium chinense can cause side effects such as diarrhea, nausea, and abdominal pain, especially when taken in large doses. Therefore, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before taking Lycium chinense, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have pre-existing medical conditions.

Additionally, it is important to use high-quality, reputable sources of Lycium chinense and to follow dosage instructions carefully to avoid potential adverse effects.

What are the possible side effects of using Lycium chinense (Goji Berry) for medicinal purposes?

Lycium chinense, or goji berry, can have side effects if used improperly.

These side effects can include gastrointestinal issues such as diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain, as well as dry mouth. Additionally, it can cause cardiovascular issues, including increased heart rate and high blood pressure.

Hypersensitivity reactions, such as rash, itching, and allergic reactions, can also occur. Other side effects may include neurological symptoms like dizziness, fatigue, headache, and mild fever.

It's important to consult with a healthcare provider before consuming Lycium chinense, especially in large quantities or if you have any pre-existing medical conditions.

The following list shows the possible side effects of Lycium chinense.

  • 1. Diarrhea: Can cause loose, watery stools, which may lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalance.
  • 2. Nausea: May cause stomach discomfort, feelings of queasiness, or upset stomach.
  • 3. Vomiting: Can result in loss of appetite, dehydration, and electrolyte imbalance due to the forceful expulsion of stomach contents.
  • 4. Dry mouth: May lead to constant thirst, difficulty swallowing, and bad breath due to decreased saliva production.
  • 5. Increased heart rate: Can cause the heart to beat faster than normal, which may lead to palpitations, anxiety, or shortness of breath.
  • 6. High blood pressure: May result in an increase in systolic and diastolic blood pressure, which can damage blood vessels and lead to serious health complications.
  • 7. Hypersensitivity reactions: Can cause allergic reactions, such as itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing, due to an overactive immune response.
  • 8. Rash: Can result in skin irritation, redness, and itchiness due to an adverse reaction to the supplement.
  • 9. Dizziness: May lead to lightheadedness, unsteadiness, and disorientation, which can increase the risk of falls and accidents.
  • 10. Fatigue: Can cause a lack of energy, weakness, and exhaustion, which may negatively impact daily activities and quality of life.

What are the precautions to take when using Lycium chinense (Goji Berry) medicinally?

Before using Lycium chinense, it's important to take some precautions such as consulting a healthcare provider if pregnant or breastfeeding, as it may not be safe for the developing fetus or infant.

Additionally, individuals should check for allergies before consuming the plant, and avoid using it if bleeding disorders occur due to the risk of increased bleeding. It's also recommended to discontinue use before surgery and to limit intake for diabetics, monitor blood pressure and sugar levels, and stop consumption before planned blood tests. Furthermore, individuals should avoid using Lycium chinense if taking blood thinners, check for interactions with medications, and limit intake for children.

Additionally, it's important to stop consumption before dental work, monitor for side effects like upset stomach, limit intake if low blood sugar is a concern, discontinue use if dizziness or fainting occurs, stop consumption if a rash or itching develops, limit intake if on immunosuppressants, consult a healthcare provider if on antihypertensive drugs, stop consumption if irregular heartbeat occurs, and limit intake if on diuretics.

The following list shows the precautions to take when using Lycium chinense.

  • 1. Consult a healthcare provider if pregnant or breastfeeding: Seek professional advice to ensure safety, as the effects of Lycium chinense on fetal development and breast milk are not well-studied.
  • 2. Check for allergies before consuming: Avoid this herb if you have known allergies to it or related plants, as it may cause adverse reactions.
  • 3. Avoid if bleeding disorders occur: Do not consume Lycium chinense if you have bleeding disorders or are taking anticoagulants, as it might increase the risk of bleeding.
  • 4. Discontinue use before surgery: Stop taking this herb at least two weeks before a scheduled surgery to reduce the risk of excessive bleeding.
  • 5. Limit intake for diabetics: Diabetics should monitor their blood sugar levels and consult a healthcare provider before consuming Lycium chinense, as it may lower blood sugar levels.
  • 6. Monitor blood pressure and sugar levels: Regularly check your blood pressure and sugar levels, as Lycium chinense may affect these levels.
  • 7. Stop consumption before planned blood tests: Discontinue using this herb before undergoing blood tests, as it may interfere with test results.
  • 8. Limit sun exposure due to photosensitivity risk: Wear protective clothing and use sunscreen when outdoors, as Lycium chinense may increase photosensitivity and cause skin irritation.
  • 9. Avoid if taking blood thinners: Do not consume Lycium chinense if you are taking blood thinners, as it may increase the risk of bleeding.
  • 10. Check for interactions with medications: Consult a healthcare provider or pharmacist to ensure that Lycium chinense does not interact with any medications you are currently taking.

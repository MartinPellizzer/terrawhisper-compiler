10 precautions to take when using Lycium chinense (Goji Berry) for medicinal purposes

Lycium chinense, also known as Chinese boxthorn or wolfberry, has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

However, it is essential to take precautions when using this plant for medicinal purposes. First, it is vital to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new treatment, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have medical conditions such as diabetes or autoimmune diseases.

Additionally, it is crucial to use the correct dosage, as high doses of Lycium chinense can cause adverse effects such as stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea. Furthermore, it is important to be aware of potential drug interactions, as Lycium chinense may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes drugs. Also, it is recommended to use high-quality, tested products to ensure purity and potency.

Lastly, it is crucial to follow the recommended dosage and duration of use, as long-term use or high doses of this plant may cause liver and kidney damage.

1. Consult A Healthcare Provider If Pregnant Or Breastfeeding

When using Lycium Chinense for medicinal purposes, it is crucial to consult a healthcare provider if pregnant or breastfeeding.

This precaution means that individuals who are expecting a baby or currently nursing a newborn should seek the advice of a medical professional before using this herb for treatment. The importance of taking this precaution lies in the fact that there is insufficient research and evidence to confirm the safety of Lycium Chinense for pregnant and breastfeeding women. The herb may have adverse effects on the fetus or the infant, such as affecting their growth and development.

Thus, it is essential to consult a healthcare provider to weigh the potential risks and benefits of using Lycium Chinense and ensure the safety of the pregnant or breastfeeding individual and their child.

2. Check For Allergies Before Consuming

When using Lycium Chinense for medicinal purposes, it is essential to check for allergies before consuming.

This precaution involves doing a patch test or starting with a small amount of goji berry to observe if any allergic reactions occur. The importance of this precaution lies in the fact that allergic reactions can range from mild, such as itching or a rash, to severe, including anaphylaxis, which can be life-threatening. Therefore, it is crucial to identify any potential allergies before incorporating goji berry into your medicinal regimen, especially if you have a history of allergies or sensitivities to other foods or plants.

By taking this precaution, you can ensure your safety and optimize the potential health benefits of Lycium chinense.

3. Avoid If Bleeding Disorders Occur

When using Lycium Chinense for medicinal purposes, it is crucial to avoid its use if bleeding disorders occur.

This precaution means that individuals who have a tendency to bleed or have a bleeding disorder, such as hemophilia or von Willebrand disease, should not consume this plant for medicinal purposes. This is because Lycium Chinense is known to slow down blood clotting, which can exacerbate bleeding disorders and lead to serious health complications. Therefore, it is important to take this precaution when using Lycium Chinense medicinally to prevent any adverse effects or interactions with existing medical conditions.

By following this precaution, individuals can still reap the potential benefits of Lycium Chinense, such as its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, while minimizing the risk of bleeding complications.

4. Discontinue Use Before Surgery

When using Lycium Chinense for medicinal purposes, it is essential to discontinue its use before surgery.

This precaution involves stopping the use of Lycium chinense at least two weeks before a scheduled surgery. The reason for this is that Lycium chinense can have blood-thinning properties, which can increase the risk of excessive bleeding during and after surgery. This herbal remedy may also interact with anesthesia and other medications used during and after surgery, affecting their effectiveness or causing unforeseen side effects.

Therefore, to ensure safety and avoid any potential complications, it is crucial to follow the instruction of discontinuing the use of Lycium chinense before surgery.

5. Limit Intake For Diabetics

When using Lycium Chinense for medicinal purposes, it is crucial to limit intake for diabetics.

This precaution means that individuals with diabetes should consume Goji berry in moderation due to its potential impact on blood sugar levels. Goji berries contain high levels of antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals, making them a popular superfood.

However, for diabetics, the berries also contain a significant amount of natural sugars that can affect blood glucose levels. Therefore, limiting intake can help manage these effects and prevent potential complications associated with sudden spikes in blood sugar levels.

Thus, it is essential to take this precaution seriously and consult with a healthcare professional before consuming Goji berries for medicinal purposes.

6. Monitor Blood Pressure And Sugar Levels

When using Lycium Chinense for medicinal purposes, it's crucial to monitor blood pressure and sugar levels.

This precaution involves regularly checking and recording these health indicators to ensure they remain within safe limits. Monitoring blood pressure entails tracking the force with which blood pushes against the walls of your arteries, which, if consistently high, could indicate hypertension.

On the other hand, monitoring blood sugar levels involves regularly measuring the concentration of glucose in the blood, which, if consistently high, may suggest diabetes or prediabetes. This precaution is vital when using Lycium Chinense medicinally because this herb may have hypoglycemic and hypotensive effects, which can be beneficial for individuals with high blood pressure or glucose levels.

However, unmonitored usage could lead to dangerously low levels, which may cause adverse health effects.

7. Stop Consumption Before Planned Blood Tests

When using Lycium Chinense for medicinal purposes, it is essential to stop its consumption before planned blood tests.

This precaution refers to completely abstaining from eating or drinking anything containing Lycium chinense at least several days prior to a scheduled blood test. This is important because goji berries can affect the results of certain blood tests, particularly those that measure glucose levels. By abstaining from its consumption, you ensure that any medications, herbal supplements, or dietary habits do not interfere with your blood test results, giving healthcare professionals a more accurate assessment of your health status.

Consequently, taking this precaution helps healthcare providers make informed decisions regarding treatments or necessary lifestyle changes, ultimately improving your health management and care.

8. Limit Sun Exposure Due To Photosensitivity Risk

When using Lycium Chinense for medicinal purposes, it is crucial to limit sun exposure due to the photosensitivity risk.

This warning means that the use of this herb can make an individual's skin more sensitive to the sun's ultraviolet (UV) rays, increasing the risk of sunburn, skin damage, and even skin cancer. The compound in goji berry that is believed to cause this photosensitivity is called fagopyrin.

Therefore, limiting sun exposure is essential to prevent adverse reactions. This precaution is important when using Lycium chinense medicinally because, without proper protection, individuals who consume this herb may experience severe skin reactions even after short periods of sun exposure.

Therefore, it's highly recommended to wear protective clothing, use a broad-spectrum sunscreen with an SPF of at least 30, and seek shade whenever possible while using this herb.

9. Avoid If Taking Blood Thinners

When using Lycium Chinense for medicinal purposes, it is crucial to avoid its use if you are currently taking blood thinners.

The instruction "Avoid if taking blood thinners" in this context means that individuals who are currently using medications that thin the blood, such as warfarin or aspirin, should not consume Lycium chinense. This precaution is essential because goji berries contain natural blood-thinning properties, which can interact with prescribed blood thinners and increase the risk of bleeding. This interaction can lead to adverse health effects, especially in individuals who have a higher risk of bleeding or who require close management of their blood clotting status.

Therefore, it is vital to consult with a healthcare provider before consuming goji berries if you are taking blood-thinning medications.

10. Check For Interactions With Medications

When using Lycium Chinense for medicinal purposes, it's essential to "check for interactions with medications." This phrase refers to the process of investigating whether the consumption of Lycium chinense can negatively impact any prescribed medication's effectiveness or cause an adverse reaction.

This precaution is crucial because Lycium chinense contains various compounds that can interact with different drugs, leading to unpredictable results. For instance, goji berries may lower blood sugar levels, which, when combined with diabetes medication, could dangerously decrease blood sugar to critical levels.

Therefore, it's vital to consult with a healthcare provider to ensure safe usage when incorporating Lycium chinense into a treatment plan alongside other medications.

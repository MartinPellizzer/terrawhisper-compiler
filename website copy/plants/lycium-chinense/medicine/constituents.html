10 Best Active Constituents of Lycium chinense (Goji Berry)

Lycium chinense, also known as Chinese boxthorn or wolfberry, has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries due to its active constituents, which include polysaccharides, betaine, cerebroside, and various vitamins and minerals.

Polysaccharides, one of the key active components, have been shown to have immunomodulatory, antioxidant, and anti-aging effects, making them valuable in treating a variety of conditions. Betaine, another important constituent, has been found to protect the liver, reduce inflammation, and improve lipid metabolism.

Cerebroside, a type of lipid, has been shown to have neuroprotective effects and may be useful in treating neurological disorders. Additionally, Chinese boxthorn contains a variety of vitamins and minerals, including vitamin C, carotenoids, and iron, which contribute to its overall medicinal value.

These active constituents work together to provide a range of health benefits, including boosting the immune system, protecting the liver, improving cognitive function, and promoting overall health and well-being.

1. Betaine

The betaine contained in Lycium Chinense is a naturally occurring amino acid that helps regulate homocysteine levels in the body, protecting against diseases of the heart and liver.

Its medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-aging effects, helping to improve cellular function and overall health. This Lycium chinense constituent has been shown to help relieve conditions such as fatigue, high blood pressure, and high cholesterol levels. Betaine is found mainly in the fruit and leaves of the Lycium chinense plant.

Improper use of Lycium chinense betaine can lead to side effects such as digestive issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with certain medications.

2. Beta-sitosterol

The beta-sitosterol contained in Lycium Chinense is a type of plant sterol that is similar in structure to cholesterol.

It has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, and is known to help lower cholesterol levels, reduce inflammation, and enhance the immune system. This constituent may help relieve health conditions such as high cholesterol, benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), and inflammatory conditions. Beta-sitosterol is found mainly in the berries, leaves, and roots of the Lycium chinense plant.

Some possible side effects of using beta-sitosterol improperly include digestive symptoms such as nausea, indigestion, and gas, as well as allergic reactions in some individuals.

Improper use of chlorogenic acid can lead to side effects such as stomach upset, allergic reactions, and potential interactions with certain medications.

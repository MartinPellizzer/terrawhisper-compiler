What to know before using Veronica serpyllifolia (Thyme Speedwell)?

Veronica serpyllifolia, also known as thymeleaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and Asia.

It has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces delicate, pinkish-purple flowers in the summer. This plant preferes well-drained, moist soil and partial shade, and it can thrive in a variety of horticultural conditions, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and groundcover. Medicinally, Veronica serpyllifolia has been used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory infections, skin conditions, and digestive issues.

Its leaves and flowers contain tannins, flavonoids, and other compounds with astringent, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

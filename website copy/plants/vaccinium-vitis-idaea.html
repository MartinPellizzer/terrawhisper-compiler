What to know before using Vaccinium vitis idaea (Cowberry)?

Vaccinium vitis idaea, also known as lingonberry or cowberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the circumboreal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Medicinally, it has been used for its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and diuretic properties. It thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soil, and prefers partial to full sun exposure. The plants produce pinkish-white flowers in the spring, followed by tart, red berries in the late summer and early fall.

The berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, from urinary tract infections to gastrointestinal disorders.

Vaccinium vitis idaea needs acidic and well-drained soil with a pH between 4.0 and 5.5 to thrive.

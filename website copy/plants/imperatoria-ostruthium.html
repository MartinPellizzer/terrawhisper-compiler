What to know before using Imperatoria ostruthium (Masterwort)?

Imperatoria ostruthium, also known as greater masterwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant with a long history of medicinal use.

Its roots and rhizomes contain a variety of beneficial compounds, including furanocoumarins and essential oils, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and carminative properties. In horticulture, Imperatoria ostruthium prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can grow up to 1.5 meters tall with feathery, aromatic leaves and small, white flowers.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family and has a thick, spindle-shaped taproot, and its stems are grooved and hollow.

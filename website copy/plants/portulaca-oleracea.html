What to know before using Portulaca oleracea (Purslane)?

Portulaca oleracea, also known as purslane, is a succulent plant that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and sandy landscapes.

It is valued in horticulture for its fleshy, green leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. In addition to its ornamental value, purslane has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

The leaves and stems of the plant are rich in omega-3 fatty acids, antioxidants, and vitamins A, C, and E, and have been used to treat a variety of ailments, including gastrointestinal disorders, respiratory infections, and skin conditions. The plant's mucilaginous properties also make it useful as a topical remedy for burns, insect bites, and other skin irritations.

Despite its many benefits, purslane is often considered a weed in many parts of the world, as it can be invasive in certain conditions.

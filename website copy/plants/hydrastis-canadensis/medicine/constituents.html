10 Best Active Constituents of Hydrastis canadensis (Goldenseal)

Hydrastis canadensis, also known as goldenseal, is a plant that contains several active constituents with medicinal properties.

The most notable of these constituents is berberine, an alkaloid that has been shown to have antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and immune-boosting effects. Another important alkaloid found in goldenseal is hydrastine, which also has antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, and can help to reduce mucosal congestion.

The plant also contains canadine, which has been found to have potential benefits for treating digestive issues, and may help to reduce pain and inflammation in the gut. Additionally, goldenseal contains various isoquinoline alkaloids, flavonoids, and polyphenols, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

These active constituents work together to make goldenseal a valuable herb for treating a variety of health conditions, including digestive issues, respiratory infections, and skin conditions.

What to know before using Levisticum officinale (Lovage)?

Levisticum officinale, also known as lovage, is a versatile herb with a variety of medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine to treat ailments such as digestive issues, menstrual cramps, and respiratory infections. Lovage has anti-inflammatory, carminative, and diuretic properties, making it a valuable addition to any natural health regimen.

In terms of horticultural conditions, lovage prefers full sun and well-drained soil. It is a hardy perennial that can grow up to 6 feet tall and wide, with glossy, green leaves and tall stalks topped by umbels of small, yellow flowers.

The plant's botanical characteristics include compound leaves with shiny, dark green leaflets, and hollow stems that emit a celery-like scent when bruised.

What to know before using Lupinus polyphyllus (Washington Lupin)?

Lupinus polyphyllus, also known as the bluebell bush or large-leaved lupine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Western North America.

Its roots and leaves contain alkaloids that have been used in traditional medicine to treat conditions such as heart ailments and worm infestations, although it should be noted that these uses are not supported by modern scientific research and can be toxic if not used properly. As a garden plant, Lupinus polyphyllus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and is often used for ornamental purposes due to its spikes of blue, purple, or white pea-like flowers.

The plant can grow up to 4 feet tall and has palmately compound leaves, meaning the leaflets spread out from a central point like fingers on a hand.

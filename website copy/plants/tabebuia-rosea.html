What to know before using Tabebuia rosea (Pau D’Arco)?

Tabebuia rosea, also known as Pau d'Arco or Trumpet Tree, is a species of tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

Medicinally, Tabebuia rosea is known for its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, and has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including fever, arthritis, and even certain types of cancer. In terms of horticultural conditions, Tabebuia rosea prefers full sun and well-drained soil, and can grow to impressive heights of up to 80 feet in ideal conditions. From a botanical perspective, Tabebuia rosea is known for its stunning pinkish-red flowers, which bloom in spectacular fashion against the tree's gray bark.

The tree's wood is also highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it a popular choice for furniture and other woodworking projects.

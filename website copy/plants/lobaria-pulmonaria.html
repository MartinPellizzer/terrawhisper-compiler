What to know before using Lobaria pulmonaria (Tree Lungwort)?

Lobaria pulmonaria, also known as lungwort, is a type of lichen with a variety of medicinal properties.

It contains several compounds with potential therapeutic benefits, including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral agents. In horticulture, Lobaria pulmonaria requires specific conditions to thrive, preferring cool, humid environments with abundant shade.

It is typically found in mature forests with high air quality, as it is sensitive to pollution. Botanically, Lobaria pulmonaria is characterized by its leafy appearance, with broad, lobed lobes that are green on the upper surface and white on the underside.

It is attached to tree bark or other substrates by a holdfast, and can grow up to 10 cm in diameter.

What are the medicinal uses of Lobaria pulmonaria?

Lobaria pulmonaria has several medicinal uses, such as treating respiratory issues and promoting skin healing.

This lichen contains various compounds, including usnic acid, lobaric acid, and evernic acid, which have antimicrobial, antiviral, and anti-inflammatory properties. To utilize its benefits, Lobaria pulmonaria can be prepared as a decoction, tincture, or infusion. However, it's important to note that consumption of this lichen may cause side effects such as gastrointestinal discomfort, headaches, and dizziness in some individuals.

Additionally, pregnant women, breastfeeding mothers, and those with kidney diseases should avoid taking Lobaria pulmonaria due to potential risks.

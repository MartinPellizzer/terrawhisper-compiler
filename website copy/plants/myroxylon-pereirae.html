What to know before using Myroxylon pereirae (Peruvian Balsam)?

Myroxylon pereirae, also known as Tolu balsam tree, is a tropical tree native to Central America and the northern regions of South America.

It is a large tree that can grow up to 20-30 meters tall and has a thick, rough bark with pinnate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces a resinous substance known as Tolu balsam, which has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and antimicrobial properties.

This resin is obtained by making incisions in the bark of the tree and collecting the exuded oleoresin. Myroxylon pereirae thrives in well-drained, fertile soil and requires a warm, humid climate with plenty of sunlight.

It is a slow-growing tree that can take several years to reach maturity and produce balsam.

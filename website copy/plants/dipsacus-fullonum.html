Dipsacus fullonum, also known as teasel, is a herbaceous plant with distinctive prickly stems and leaves, and purple, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

This plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine, particularly in treating musculoskeletal conditions such as back pain and osteoarthritis, due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Teasel prefers full sun and well-drained soil, and can grow up to several feet tall, making it a striking addition to a garden. However, it can be invasive in some areas, so it's important to monitor its growth and prevent it from spreading unchecked.

Additionally, the dried roots and seeds of teasel have been used in dyeing and textile production, giving it a rich cultural and practical history.

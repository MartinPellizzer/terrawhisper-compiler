Crocus sativa, commonly known as saffron, has been used in various medicinal preparations for centuries.

Its dried stigmas are often used to make a tisane, an herbal tea, which is believed to have a calming effect on the nervous system and may help alleviate symptoms of depression and PMS. Saffron tincture, made by soaking the dried stigmas in alcohol, is used as a digestive aid and to treat stomach ailments.

Additionally, saffron has been found to have anti-carcinogenic and anti-mutagenic properties, making it a potential complementary treatment for cancer. However, it's important to note that high doses of saffron can be toxic, and it should be used with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

Furthermore, more research is needed to fully understand the medicinal properties and potential benefits of saffron.

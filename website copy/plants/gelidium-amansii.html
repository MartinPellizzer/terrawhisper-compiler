What to know before using Gelidium amansii (Agar)?

Gelidium amansii, a red alga commonly found along the rocky shores of the Indian and Pacific Oceans, is not only an essential component of marine ecosystems but also holds significant medicinal value for humans.

This alga contains various bioactive compounds, including carotenoids, fucoxanthin, fucoidan, and alginates, which exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulatory properties, making it a valuable addition to traditional medicine. In horticultural settings, Gelidium amansii thrives best in shallow waters with strong sunlight exposure and a moderate water flow. Its botanical characteristics include red or purple coloration, a cartilaginous texture, and intricately branched fronds, which provide an attractive appearance for both scientific and ornamental purposes.

In addition to its medicinal benefits, Gelidium amansii is also utilized as a source of agar-agar, a gelling agent widely used in the food industry.

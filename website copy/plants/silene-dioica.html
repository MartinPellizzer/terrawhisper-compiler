What to know before using Silene dioica (Red Campion)?

Silene dioica, also known as red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is known for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects, and it has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, from digestive issues to skin conditions. In terms of horticultural conditions, Silene dioica thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, and it is often used as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its striking pink-purple flowers.

Botanically, Silene dioica is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 1 meter tall, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves and five-petaled flowers that bloom in loose clusters.

10 Best Active Constituents of Scutellaria baicalensis (Baical Skullcap)

Scutellaria baicalensis, also known as Chinese skullcap, is a plant that has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

The root of the plant contains several active constituents with medicinal properties, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids. The most notable flavonoids are baicalin, baicalein, and wogonin, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and anticancer effects.

Baicalin, in particular, has been studied for its potential to protect against neurodegenerative diseases, such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's, due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, Scutellaria baicalensis has been used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory infections, liver disease, and anxiety, although more research is needed to confirm its effectiveness for these uses.

Overall, Scutellaria baicalensis is a valuable medicinal plant with a range of active constituents that have shown promise in preclinical and clinical studies.

Scutellaria baicalensis, also known as Chinese skullcap, has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries due to its medicinal properties.

The roots of this plant are often used to make various medicinal preparations such as tisanes and tinctures. A tisane, or herbal tea, can be made by steeping dried roots in hot water, releasing beneficial compounds like flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds have been found to have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties. Tinctures, on the other hand, are made by soaking the roots in alcohol, which extracts a wider range of compounds, creating a more concentrated and potent medicinal solution.

Both tisanes and tinctures made from Scutellaria baicalensis are used to treat a variety of ailments, including respiratory infections, inflammation, and even cancer, although more scientific research is needed to fully understand and confirm these medicinal uses.

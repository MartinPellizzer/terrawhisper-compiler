10 Best Active Constituents of Turnera diffusa (Damiana)

Turnera diffusa, also known as damiana, is a plant that has been used traditionally for medicinal purposes.

The active constituents of Turnera diffusa include flavonoids, terpenoids, and essential oils, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties. One of the key active constituents is damianin, a bitter substance that is thought to have a relaxing effect on smooth muscles and may help to support the health of the urinary and reproductive systems.

Other active constituents in Turnera diffusa include volatile oils such as p-cymene, alpha-pinene, and thymol, which have been shown to have anti-microbial and anti-inflammatory effects. Additionally, the plant contains tannins, which have astringent properties and may help to support digestive health.

Overall, the active constituents of Turnera diffusa work together to provide a range of potential medicinal benefits, including relaxation, stress relief, and support for the urinary and reproductive systems.

10 Best Health Benefits of Turnera diffusa (Damiana)

Turnera diffusa, also known as Damiana, is a wild shrub that grows in Central and South America, Mexico, and the Caribbean.

It has been used traditionally for centuries as a medicinal herb due to its various health benefits. Damiana is known for its positive effects on the nervous system, and it has been used to treat conditions such as anxiety, depression, and nervous stomach.

Additionally, it has been shown to have a positive impact on sexual health, acting as a natural aphrodisiac and helping to alleviate symptoms of menopause. Damiana has also been used to treat digestive issues such as constipation and dyspepsia.

Furthermore, it has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antioxidant properties, making it a valuable herb for overall health and well-being.

1. Alleviates anxiety

Damiana alleviates anxiety, meaning it can help reduce feelings of worry, nervousness, or tension related to anxiety.

The main active constituents of Turnera diffusa that contribute to this benefit are flavonoids, terpenoids, and essential oils, particularly cineole, pinene, and limonene. The primary parts of the Turnera diffusa plant that provide this anxiety-relieving effect are the leaves, which contain the highest concentration of beneficial compounds. The main medicinal preparations of Turnera diffusa that help alleviate anxiety include dried leaves for tea, liquid extracts, and capsule or tablet forms of dried leaf powder.

This benefit of Turnera diffusa is particularly helpful for individuals experiencing mild to moderate anxiety, as well as those undergoing stress or experiencing symptoms of mild depression.

Turnera diffusa alleviates anxiety thanks to the active constituents listed below.

  • Flavonoids
  • Tannins
  • Alkaloids
  • Sterols

  • Glycosides
  • Saponins
  • Essential oils

What to know before using Epilobium palustre (Marsh Willowherb)?

Epilobium palustre, also known as marsh willowherb, is a plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

It contains tannins, anthocyanins, and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. This plant is often used in herbal medicine to treat skin conditions, inflammation, and digestive disorders.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Epilobium palustre prefers moist, nutrient-rich soil and partial shade. It is a perennial plant that grows to be around 30-70 cm tall and has slender, reddish stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers of Epilobium palustre are pink or purple, and they bloom in the summer.

What are the medicinal uses of Epilobium palustre?

Epilobium palustre is used to treat various health conditions, such as reducing inflammation, soothing irritated skin, and alleviating symptoms of Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH).

Its active constituents include flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. Medicinal preparations of Epilobium palustre include teas, tinctures, capsules, and ointments.

However, potential side effects include stomach upset and allergic reactions. Therefore, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using Epilobium palustre, especially for those with a history of allergies to other plants in the same family or who are taking other medications that may interact with it.

Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using Epilobium palustre due to insufficient evidence regarding its safety for these groups.

What to know before using Milium effusum (Wood Millet)?

Milium effusum, also known as bowles' grass or hare's tail grass, is a perennial grass indigenous to Europe and Asia that has naturalized in other regions.

Its botanical characteristics include thin, delicate stems that grow in loose clusters and tiny, white, soft flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. This grass grows best in horticultural conditions with partial shade and well-drained soil and can thrive in a variety of soil types. Milium effusum has medicinal properties such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, making it a potential ingredient in skincare and cosmetic products.

However, more research is needed to fully understand its medicinal benefits.

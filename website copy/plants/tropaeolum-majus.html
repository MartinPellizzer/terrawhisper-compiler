What to know before using Tropaeolum majus (Nasturtium)?

Tropaeolum majus, commonly known as nasturtium, is an easy-to-grow annual plant that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics include rounded or kidney-shaped leaves and brightly colored, often red, orange, or yellow flowers with a spur at the back. Medicinally, nasturtium has been used for its antimicrobial and anti-fungal properties, and its leaves and flowers are rich in vitamin C.

The entire plant has a peppery taste, and both the leaves and flowers are edible, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and a tasty addition to salads.

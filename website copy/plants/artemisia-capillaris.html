What to know before using Artemisia capillaris (Yin Chen Hao)?

Artemisia capillaris, also known as Japanese wormwood, is a herbaceous perennial plant that is native to Asia.

It is widely cultivated for its medicinal properties, which include treating liver diseases, promoting blood circulation, and reducing inflammation. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it can grow up to 1.2 meters tall. Artemisia capillaris has fern-like, bright green leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellow flowers in the summer.

The plant's above-ground parts are used in herbal medicine, and it is an important species in traditional Chinese medicine.

What are the active constituents of Artemisia capillaris?

Artemisia capillaris has many active constituents, such as flavonoids, essential oils, coumarins, and sesquiterpenes.

Among these, flavonoids like artemetin, capillarisin, and chlorogenic acid are the most significant, exhibiting antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and hepatoprotective properties. These flavonoids have been shown to scavenge free radicals, reduce liver enzyme levels, and protect against liver damage.

Furthermore, the essential oils in Artemisia capillaris, including 1,8-cineole, α-pinene, and β-pinene, contribute to its aromatic properties and have been found to possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarins like scopoletin and umbelliferone have been identified for their anticoagulant and antitumor activities, while sesquiterpenes like germacrone exhibit antipyretic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Overall, the diverse range of active constituents in Artemisia capillaris contributes to its medicinal value, particularly in liver ailments, inflammation, and infectious diseases.

What to know before using Chenopodium album (Fat-Hen)?

Chenopodium album, also known as goosefoot or lamb's quarters, is a versatile and often overlooked plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

Historically, it has been used to treat a range of ailments, from skin conditions and insect bites to respiratory issues and digestive complaints. Modern research has supported its use as an antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial agent. In terms of horticultural conditions, Chenopodium album is a hardy annual that thrives in a variety of soils and climates, making it a low-maintenance and adaptable choice for gardeners.

It prefers full sun but can tolerate partial shade, and its seeds can be sown directly in the ground in the spring or fall. As for its botanical characteristics, Chenopodium album is a leafy green plant that can grow up to several feet tall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and triangular to rhombic in shape, with a rough, Mealy texture.

The plant is also known for its small, green flowers, which are often hidden by the foliage, and its tiny, dark seeds.

10 Best Health Benefits of Inula helenium (Elecampane)

Inula helenium, also known as elecampane, is a plant that has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries.

It has been shown to have expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular natural remedy for respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, and whooping cough. Additionally, elecampane contains compounds that have been shown to have antimicrobial and antifungal properties, making it useful for treating wounds and infections.

The plant's roots are also rich in inulin, a type of fiber that can help to support gut health and improve digestion. Some studies have also suggested that Inula helenium may have potential as an anti-cancer agent, although more research is needed in this area.

Overall, elecampane is a versatile medicinal plant with a wide range of potential health benefits.

The primary parts of the Inula helenium plant that provide this benefit are the roots and rhizomes, which contain the highest concentration of these active compounds. The main medicinal preparations of Inula helenium that provide this benefit include decoctions, tinctures, and dried herb, which can be used to make teas or added to other remedies.

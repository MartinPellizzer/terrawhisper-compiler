10 Best Active Constituents of Inula helenium (Elecampane)

Inula helenium, also known as elecampane, contains several active constituents that have medicinal properties.

The most notable of these are its bitter sesquiterpene lactones, such as alantolactone and isoalantolactone, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal effects. Additionally, elecampane contains high levels of various phenolic acids and flavonoids, which are believed to contribute to its antioxidant and immune-boosting properties.

The plant also contains mucilage, a substance that can help soothe irritated tissues in the digestive tract and relieve coughs and sore throats. Inula helenium has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat a variety of ailments, including respiratory infections, digestive issues, and skin conditions.

However, it is important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the medicinal properties of Inula helenium and to establish safe and effective dosages.

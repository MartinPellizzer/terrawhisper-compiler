Inula helenium, also known as elecampane, is a plant that has been used in medicinal preparations for centuries.

Its roots are commonly used to make tisanes, which are herbal teas, and tinctures. The tisane is made by steeping the dried or fresh roots in hot water, and it is often used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and asthma due to its expectorant properties.

The tincture, on the other hand, is made by soaking the roots in alcohol, and it is taken orally to treat digestive issues such as bloating, gas, and indigestion. Additionally, Inula helenium has been used in traditional medicine to strengthen the immune system and as a natural anti-inflammatory.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new medicinal preparations.

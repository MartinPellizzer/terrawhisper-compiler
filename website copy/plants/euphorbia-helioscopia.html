What to know before using Euphorbia helioscopia (Wolf'S-Milk)?

Euphorbia helioscopia, also known as sun spurge, is an annual plant species native to Europe and North Africa but now naturalized in many parts of the world.

It grows in a variety of horticultural conditions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun, and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The botanical characteristics of Euphorbia helioscopia include narrow, lobed leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers surrounded by pinkish bracts. Medicinally, the plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including skin conditions, warts, and respiratory issues, due to its irritant and vesicant properties.

However, it should be used with caution as it can cause skin irritation and digestive upset in some individuals.

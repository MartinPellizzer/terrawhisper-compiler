What to know before using Leptandra virginica (Black Root)?

Leptandra virginica, also known as Black Root or Blue Cohosh, is a perennial plant native to North America that has been used for medicinal purposes by Native Americans and early American settlers.

Its roots contain compounds with laxative, emetic, and cathartic properties, making it useful for treating constipation, liver disorders, and fevers. In horticultural settings, Leptandra virginica prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, growing up to 4-7 feet tall with spikes of small, white flowers in the summer.

Botanically, it is a member of the Plantaginaceae family, featuring lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are arranged oppositely on the stem.

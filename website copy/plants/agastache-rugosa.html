What to know before using Agastache rugosa (Korean Mint)?

Agastache rugosa, also known as Korean mint or Huo Xiang, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is valued in herbal medicine for its ability to alleviate symptoms of digestive ailments such as gastric pain, nausea, and diarrhea. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it can grow up to 4 feet tall with a spread of 2-3 feet.

Agastache rugosa is characterized by its square stems, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and spikes of lavender-blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

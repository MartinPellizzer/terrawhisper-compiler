10 possible side effects of Sambucus nigra (Elder) if used improperly

Sambucus nigra, also known as black elderberry, has been used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, but improper use can lead to several side effects.

Firstly, consuming raw or undercooked elderberries can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea due to the presence of lectins and other toxic compounds. Secondly, overdosing on elderberry supplements may result in severe digestive issues, including abdominal cramps and severe diarrhea.

Moreover, people with autoimmune diseases should avoid elderberry products, as they may stimulate the immune system, potentially worsening their condition. Pregnant and breastfeeding women should also avoid using elderberry, as its safety during pregnancy and lactation has not been established.

Lastly, people who are allergic to elderberry or other plants in the same family (Caprifoliaceae) may experience skin rashes, hives, or difficulty breathing after contact with the plant or its products.

1. Nausea

Nausea caused by Sambucus Nigra refers to a queasy or uneasy sensation in the stomach, often accompanied by an urge to vomit.

This side effect can be attributed to the presence of certain active constituents in Sambucus Nigra, including alkaloids, cyanogenic glycosides, and lectins. These compounds are primarily found in the leaves, stems, and unripe berries of the plant, and can cause nausea when ingested in large quantities or when not properly prepared.

Elder preparations that may cause nausea if misused include raw elder bark or leaf teas, as well as unripe or undercooked elder berries. Those who are most at risk of experiencing nausea from Sambucus Nigra are young children, the elderly, and individuals with weakened immune systems. To avoid this side effect, it is important to only use properly prepared elder products, such as ripe, cooked elder berries or commercially available extracts, and to follow labeled dosage instructions carefully.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using elder for medicinal purposes, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a pre-existing medical condition.

2. Vomiting

Vomiting caused by Sambucus nigra refers to the forceful expulsion of stomach contents through the mouth.

This side effect can be attributed to the presence of certain active constituents in Sambucus nigra, including alkaloids, glycosides, and lectins. The main medicinal preparations of Sambucus nigra that can cause vomiting if misused are elderberry extract and elderflower tea, both of which are used for their antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties.

Elderberry and elderflower can be unsafe when consumed in large amounts or when used inappropriately. Individuals with digestive disorders, pregnant women, and young children are more prone to experiencing vomiting as a side effect of Sambucus nigra.

To avoid this side effect, it is essential to use Sambucus nigra products only as directed, avoid consuming large amounts of the plant, and consult a healthcare professional before using Sambucus nigra if you are pregnant or have a medical condition.

3. Diarrhea

Diarrhea caused by Sambucus Nigra refers to a gastrointestinal side effect characterized by loose, watery stools occurring frequently.

This side effect can be attributed mainly to the anthranoid glycosides and lectins present in Sambucus Nigra, which can stimulate bowel movements and increase intestinal permeability. The medicinal preparations of Sambucus Nigra that can cause diarrhea include the fresh fruit or its juice, if consumed in excess or for extended periods.

Those who are more likely to experience this side effect are individuals with a sensitive gastrointestinal tract, people with a history of gastrointestinal issues such as irritable bowel syndrome, and those who consume large quantities of Sambucus Nigra products. To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to consume Sambucus Nigra products in moderation and in accordance with the recommended dosages.

Additionally, it is advisable to avoid consuming Sambucus Nigra products for extended periods and to consult a healthcare professional before using them, especially if one has a history of gastrointestinal issues or is taking any medication.

4. Dizziness

Dizziness caused by Sambucus nigra refers to a sensation of lightheadedness, unsteadiness, or disorientation that may occur after using this plant.

This side effect can be attributed mainly to the alkaloids, cyanogenic glycosides, and lectins present in Sambucus nigra, which can have mild to moderate central nervous system depressant effects. The medicinal preparations that can cause this side effect if misused include elderberry extracts, teas, and capsules.

Elderberries, flowers, and other plant parts can be toxic if not prepared properly, and consuming large quantities or using the plant for extended periods can increase the risk of dizziness. Individuals who are more likely to experience this side effect include those with sensitivity to Sambucus nigra or its constituents, young children, pregnant or breastfeeding women, and elderly people with weakened immune systems.

To avoid this side effect, it is essential to use standardized, clinically tested elderberry products under the guidance of a healthcare practitioner, follow the recommended dosage and duration, and avoid combining it with other central nervous system depressants.

5. Stomach Cramps

Stomach cramps caused by Sambucus Nigra refers to a painful involuntary contraction of the stomach muscles that occurs upon ingesting this herb.

This side effect can be attributed mainly to the alkaloids, cyanogenic glycosides, and lectins present in the plant. The medicinal preparations that can cause this side effect if misused include elderberry juice, bark, and flower teas.

Individuals with gastrointestinal sensitivities, such as those with irritable bowel syndrome or inflammatory bowel disease, are more likely to experience stomach cramps after consuming Sambucus nigra. To avoid this side effect, it is advisable to use caution when consuming Sambucus nigra and start with small doses, gradually increasing over time.

Additionally, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional experienced in the use of herbal medicine to ensure safe and appropriate use.

6. Allergic Reactions

Allergic reactions caused by Sambucus Nigra refers to the abnormal response of the body's immune system to specific substances present in this plant.

The main active constituents of Sambucus Nigra that can cause this side effect are alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids. These reactions are most commonly associated with the misuse of elderberry syrups, teas, and capsules. Individuals with a known allergy to elder or other plants in the same family, such as honeysuckle and nicotiana, are more likely to experience allergic reactions.

To avoid this side effect, it is crucial to use Sambucus Nigra products as directed, and to discontinue use and seek medical attention if any symptoms of an allergic reaction occur, such as hives, difficulty breathing, or swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat.

7. Skin Rash

Skin rash caused by Sambucus Nigra refers to an unpleasant skin reaction that can occur in some individuals who come into contact with this plant or its preparations.

The main active constituents in Sambucus Nigra that can cause this side effect are lectins, alkaloids, and cyanogenic glycosides, which can trigger an immune response leading to skin irritation and rash. The medicinal preparations of Sambucus Nigra that can cause this side effect if misused include the bark, leaves, and unripe berries, which contain higher concentrations of these active constituents.

Individuals who are more likely to experience skin rash from Sambucus Nigra are those with sensitivities or allergies to the plant, as well as those who use it excessively or without proper dosage. To avoid skin rash, it is important to handle Sambucus Nigra with care, avoid contact with its potentially harmful parts, and seek medical advice before using it for medicinal purposes.

Additionally, monitoring for any adverse reactions, especially during the first few uses, can help prevent or minimize the risk of skin rash caused by Sambucus Nigra.

8. Headache

Headache caused by Sambucus Nigra refers to a potential side effect of this plant that can result from misuse or overuse.

The main active constituents in Sambucus Nigra that have been linked to headaches include lectins, alkaloids, and flavonoids. These compounds can cause irritation to the gastrointestinal tract and lead to symptoms such as headaches.

The most common medicinal preparations of Sambucus Nigra that have been associated with this side effect are those that involve large amounts of raw or unripe fruit, bark, or leaves. People who are more likely to experience headaches after using Sambucus Nigra are those with sensitivities to these compounds, those who consume large amounts of the plant, or those who use it for extended periods of time.

To avoid this side effect, it is recommended to use Sambucus Nigra in moderation, avoid consuming large amounts of raw or unripe fruit, bark, or leaves, and consult with a healthcare professional before using it for medicinal purposes.

9. Anxiety

Anxiety caused by Sambucus nigra refers to the feelings of worry, nervousness, or unease that can occur due to the use of this plant.

This is because Sambucus nigra contains active constituents such as alkaloids, cyanogenic glycosides, and lectins, which can affect the nervous system and lead to anxiety symptoms. The main medicinal preparations that can cause this side effect if misused include elder flowers, berries, and bark, which are often used in teas, supplements, and extracts. Individuals who are more likely to experience anxiety from Sambucus nigra are those who are sensitive to its active constituents, have a history of anxiety or panic attacks, or consume large amounts of the plant.

To avoid this side effect, it is important to use Sambucus nigra in moderation, follow recommended dosages, and consult with a healthcare provider before using it for medicinal purposes.

10. Numbness

Numbness caused by Sambucus Nigra refers to a loss of feeling or sensation in certain areas of the body that may occur following the use of this plant.

This side effect can be attributed to the main active constituents of Sambucus Nigra, which include alkaloids, flavonoids, mucilage, tannins, and trap substances. These constituents can have varying effects on the body, and when misused or used in large amounts, can cause numbness.

Those who are more likely to experience this side effect include individuals with allergies to related plants, those with pre-existing medical conditions, and those who take other medications. To avoid this side effect, it is important to use Sambucus Nigra appropriately and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

This includes following recommended dosages, using it for only limited periods of time, and avoiding misuse or overuse.

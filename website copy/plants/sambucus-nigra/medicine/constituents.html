10 Best Active Constituents of Sambucus nigra (Elder)

Sambucus nigra, also known as black elderberry, has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, and its active constituents include flavonoids, anthocyanins, and vitamins.

Flavonoids, such as quercetin and rutin, have been found to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which may help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the body. Anthocyanins, which give the berries their dark purple color, have been shown to have antimicrobial and antiviral effects, and may help boost the immune system. Additionally, black elderberry is a rich source of vitamin C, which is essential for the proper functioning of the immune system.

While more research is needed to fully understand the medicinal properties of Sambucus nigra, these active constituents suggest that it may have potential as a natural remedy for a variety of health conditions, including colds, flu, and inflammation.

1. Anthocyanins

The anthocyanins contained in Sambucus Nigra are water-soluble pigments that give the plant its characteristic dark color and possess potent antioxidant properties.

They have been found to have anti-inflammatory, immune-boosting, and antiviral effects, making them effective in treating conditions such as colds, flu, and sinusitis. Additionally, anthocyanins have been shown to help alleviate pain and inflammation associated with arthritis and other inflammatory conditions. These compounds are found in high concentrations in the flowers and berries of the elder plant.

However, if used improperly or in large quantities, Sambucus nigra anthocyanins can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

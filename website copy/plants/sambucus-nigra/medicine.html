Sambucus nigra (Elder) Medicinal Guide

Sambucus nigra, commonly known as black elderberry, has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries due to its potential health benefits.

The constituents of Sambucus nigra include flavonoids, anthocyanins, and vitamins A, C, and E, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties. Preparations of Sambucus nigra include syrups, capsules, and teas, which are often used to treat symptoms of the common cold, flu, and other respiratory infections. However, it is important to note that Sambucus nigra can cause side effects such as nausea, diarrhea, and stomach upset in some people, and it may interact with certain medications.

Therefore, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before taking Sambucus nigra, especially for children, pregnant or breastfeeding women, and individuals with medical conditions or taking medications.

A tincture is a concentrated alcohol extract of elderberries or flowers, used to boost the immune system and reduce inflammation. Syrups are thick, sweet liquids made from elderberries or flowers, often combined with honey or sugar, and are used to treat coughs, colds, and flu. Lozenges are solid dosage forms that dissolve slowly in the mouth, often containing elderberry extract and are used to treat sore throats and coughs.

Capsules are convenient for those who dislike the taste of elderberry and are used for their immune-boosting and anti-inflammatory properties. Tea is a popular way of consuming elder, made from dried or fresh flowers and berries, and is used to treat colds, flu, and respiratory issues. Poultices are made from crushed elder leaves or berries and are applied topically to treat wounds, burns, and inflammation. Ointments and oil are used to soothe and heal skin conditions, such as eczema, and are made from elder flowers or berries.

Suppositories are used to treat hemorrhoids and are made from elderberry extract.

What are the possible side effects of using Sambucus nigra (Elder) for medicinal purposes?

Sambucus nigra can have side effects if used improperly.

Improper use can lead to gastrointestinal issues such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach cramps, and possible loss of appetite. Additionally, it can cause dizziness, headaches, anxiety, and numbness in the extremities.

Moreover, allergic reactions, skin rash, and respiratory problems may occur in some individuals. In severe cases, it can lead to more serious symptoms such as tachycardia, weakness, confusion, seizures, and even loss of consciousness.

Therefore, it is crucial to use Sambucus nigra products with caution and follow the recommended dosage instructions carefully.

The following list shows the possible side effects of Sambucus nigra.

  • 1. Nausea: Can cause discomfort in the stomach and urge to vomit.
  • 2. Vomiting: Can result in loss of appetite, dehydration, and electrolyte imbalance.
  • 3. Diarrhea: Can lead to dehydration, electrolyte imbalance, and loss of nutrients.
  • 4. Dizziness: Can cause lightheadedness, unsteadiness, and loss of balance.
  • 5. Stomach cramps: Can cause pain and discomfort in the abdominal area.
  • 6. Allergic reactions: Can include symptoms such as itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing.
  • 7. Skin rash: Can result in redness, itching, and irritation of the skin.
  • 8. Headache: Can cause pain and discomfort in the head and neck area.
  • 9. Anxiety: Can lead to feelings of restlessness, nervousness, and fear.
  • 10. Numbness: Can result in a loss of sensation or feeling in the affected area.

What are the precautions to take when using Sambucus nigra (Elder) medicinally?

Before using Sambucus nigra for medicinal purposes, it's important to take some precautions such as consulting a healthcare professional before use to ensure it's safe and suitable for your health condition.

Do not exceed the recommended dosage to avoid adverse effects, and avoid using it during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to lack of sufficient evidence on its safety. Check for allergies to any of its components, and discontinue use if symptoms worsen or new ones appear. If you have diabetes, monitor your blood sugar levels, and avoid using it if you're immunocompromised or taking immunosuppressants.

Do not use it for prolonged periods without medical advice, and stay hydrated when taking Elderberry. Strain the preparation to remove seeds and stems, and cook or heat Elderberry before consumption to destroy any potential toxins. In case of gastrointestinal distress, reduce dosage or discontinue use, and consult a healthcare professional before combining it with other medications. Keep it out of reach of children, store it in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight, and discard it after the expiration date.

If you're considering using it topically, perform a skin patch test, and consult a healthcare professional for children under 12 or if you don't plan to use it under professional medical advice.

  • 6. Monitor blood sugar levels if diabetic: Sambucus nigra may lower blood sugar levels, so if you have diabetes, monitor your blood sugar levels closely while taking it.
  • 7. Avoid if immunocompromised or taking immunosuppressants: Sambucus nigra may boost the immune system, which can be dangerous if you have a weakened immune system or are taking immunosuppressants.

  • 9. Stay hydrated when taking Elderberry: Drinking plenty of fluids can help prevent stomach upset and other side effects.
  • 10. Strain the preparation to remove seeds and stems: The seeds and stems of Sambucus nigra contain a chemical that can cause nausea and vomiting, so make sure to strain the preparation before consuming it.

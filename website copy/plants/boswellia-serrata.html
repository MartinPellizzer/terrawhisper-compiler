What to know before using Boswellia serrata (Boswellia)?

Boswellia serrata, also known as Indian frankincense, is a tree valued for its medicinal properties and botanical characteristics.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats in India, Africa, and the Middle East. The tree's resin contains boswellic acids, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it a popular natural remedy for conditions like osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and asthma.

Boswellia serrata can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a trunk that is typically crooked and gnarled. Its leaves are small and narrow, and its flowers are white or pale yellow.

The tree is harvested for its resin, which is extracted from the bark and then dried into a gum that can be used in various forms, such as powder, capsules, or oils.

Vitex agnus-castus (Chaste Tree) Medicinal Guide

Vitex agnus-castus, also known as chasteberry, has a long history of use in traditional medicine for treating various female reproductive system disorders.

The constituents of Vitex agnus-castus include flavonoids, iridoid glycosides, and essential oils, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties. Preparations of Vitex agnus-castus include teas, capsules, and tinctures, which are used to treat premenstrual syndrome (PMS), menstrual irregularities, and infertility.

However, Vitex agnus-castus can cause side effects such as stomach upset, skin rashes, and itchy eyes, and it can interact with certain medications, such as birth control pills. Therefore, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before taking Vitex agnus-castus, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

Additionally, Vitex agnus-castus may affect hormone levels, so it should be used with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare provider.

Additionally, women who are pregnant or breastfeeding should avoid using it, and it's crucial to monitor menstrual cycles regularly and discontinue use before surgery. It's also recommended to avoid using it with hormonal contraceptives, dopamine-related medications, and other herbal supplements.

Limit sun exposure during use, monitor blood pressure levels, and be aware of potential allergic reactions. If you have hormone-sensitive conditions or experience side effects, discontinue use. Furthermore, it's important to store it in a cool, dry place, keep it out of reach of children, follow recommended dosage instructions, and avoid consuming alcohol while using it.

Lastly, if you have specific health concerns, consult a healthcare provider for personalized advice.

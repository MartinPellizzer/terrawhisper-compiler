What to know before using Veronica hederifolia (Small Henbit)?

Veronica hederifolia, also known as ivy-leaved speedwell, is a low-growing plant that thrives in shaded areas with moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics include heart-shaped, bright green leaves and small, blue flowers that bloom in the spring. This plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including respiratory issues, skin conditions, and digestive problems. The leaves and flowers of Veronica hederifolia contain compounds with anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and expectorant properties, making it a valuable herb for promoting respiratory health.

When grown in a garden, Veronica hederifolia can serve as a ground cover, spreading through its creeping stems and adding visual interest with its attractive foliage and flowers.

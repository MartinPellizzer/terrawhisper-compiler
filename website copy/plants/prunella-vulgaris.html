What to know before using Prunella vulgaris (Self-Heal)?

Prunella vulgaris, also known as self-heal or heal-all, is a plant with a multitude of medicinal properties.

It has been used in traditional medicine to treat wounds, burns, and inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and can be found in meadows, fields, and forests. Prunella vulgaris is a member of the mint family and is characterized by its square stems, lance-shaped leaves, and spiked flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

It is a hardy plant that can thrive in a variety of conditions, making it a popular choice for gardeners looking to attract pollinators and add a touch of color to their landscape.

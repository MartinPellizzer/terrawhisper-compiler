What to know before using Ruta graveolens (Rue)?

Ruta graveolens, also known as common rue or herb-of-grace, is a hardy perennial plant that is native to the Mediterranean region.

It has several medicinal properties, including acting as an antispasmodic, relieving fevers, and reducing inflammation. Ruta graveolens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it can grow up to 60 cm tall with blue-green, lobed leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant has a strong, distinct smell and contains various alkaloids, such as rutin and graveoline, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal benefits.

However, it is important to note that Ruta graveolens can be toxic in large doses and should be used with caution.

What to know before using Herniaria glabra (Rupturewort)?

Herniaria glabra, also known as smooth rupturewort, is a low-growing plant found throughout Europe and parts of Asia.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties, and is believed to help treat kidney stones and bladder disorders. Herniaria glabra thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and can be propagated through seed or division. The plant features small, green, ovate leaves and tiny, white flowers that bloom in the summer.

It is often used as a groundcover due to its low growth habit and ability to tolerate light foot traffic.

What are the medicinal uses of Herniaria glabra?

Herniaria glabra has several medicinal uses, such as treating bladder and kidney stones, as well as healing wounds and skin irritations.

What to know before using Sagina procumbens (Bird-Eye Pearlwort)?

Sagina procumbens, also known as Irish Moss, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to Europe and Asia.

It thrives in horticultural conditions that include full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil. The plant's small, green leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern, and it produces tiny, white flowers in the summer. Medicinally, Sagina procumbens is used as a demulcent and anti-inflammatory, making it useful for treating conditions such as digestive disorders, urinary tract infections, and skin irritations.

Its high mucilage content makes it particularly soothing to irritated tissues.

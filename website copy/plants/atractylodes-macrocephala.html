What to know before using Atractylodes macrocephala (Bai Zhu)?

Atractylodes macrocephala, also known as Chinese thoroughwax or bai zhu, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. The plant has a thick, fleshy rhizome that is highly valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its ability to strengthen the spleen and boost the immune system. The rhizome is harvested in the fall of the plant's third year, and is typically dried and sliced before use.

In addition to its medicinal properties, Atractylodes macrocephala is also cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive, feathery foliage.

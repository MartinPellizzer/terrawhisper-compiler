What to know before using Cornus officinalis (Shan Zhu Yu)?

Cornus officinalis, also known as Asian dogwood or Japanese cornelian cherry, is a plant with a range of medicinal properties.

It contains several compounds with potential health benefits, including iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and organic acids. Traditional Chinese medicine has used Cornus officinalis to treat various conditions, such as kidney deficiency, arthralgia, and inflammation.

In terms of horticultural conditions, Cornus officinalis is a deciduous tree that prefers partial shade and well-drained soil. It can grow up to 20 feet tall and has distinctive oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by clusters of bright red oval-shaped fruit that are a favorite of birds.

Overall, Cornus officinalis is not only a beautiful ornamental tree but also a valuable medicinal plant.

What are the medicinal uses of Cornus officinalis?

Cornus officinalis has several medicinal uses, such as treating kidney deficiencies, tonifying yang, and strengthening the bones and muscles.

Its constituents include iridoid glycosides, loganin, cornuside, and morroniside, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immune-enhancing properties. Preparations of Cornus officinalis typically involve decocting the dried fruit of the plant, although it can also be found in the form of powder, pills, or alcohol extracts. Potential side effects of Cornus officinalis may include stomach discomfort, dry mouth, and dizziness, especially when taken in large doses or for extended periods.

Therefore, it is important to follow recommended dosages and consult with a healthcare provider before using Cornus officinalis, especially for those with existing medical conditions or taking other medications.

What are the medicinal preparations of Cornus officinalis?

Cornus officinalis has many medicinal preparations, such as decoctions, powders, and extracts, which are primarily used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The bark and fruits of the plant are utilized to create these preparations. Decoctions are made by boiling the bark or fruits in water to extract their active compounds, and are often used to treat kidney and liver ailments.

Powders are made by drying and grinding the bark or fruits into a fine consistency, and can be used to treat diarrhea, vomiting, and spermatorrhea. Extracts are concentrated forms of the active compounds found in the bark or fruits, and can be used in various forms such as tinctures or capsules for their medicinal properties.

These preparations have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to treat a variety of ailments, and are still widely used today.

What are the possible side effects of Cornus officinalis?

Cornus officinalis can have some side effects if used improperly, such as stomach discomfort or diarrhea in cases of overdose.

Furthermore, it may interact with certain medications, particularly those that affect blood clotting, and therefore should be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

Lastly, it is important to note that excessive consumption of Cornus officinalis may lead to nausea and vomiting.

What are the planting tips of Cornus officinalis?

Planting Cornus officinalis requires careful consideration of its growth requirements.

The best time to plant this tree is in the fall or early spring, allowing it to establish its root system before the hot summer months. When planting, dig a hole twice as wide and just as deep as the root ball.

Place the tree in the hole, ensuring that the top of the root ball is level with the surrounding soil. Backfill the hole with a mixture of native soil and organic matter, such as compost or well-rotted manure, and water thoroughly.

Mulch around the base of the tree to retain moisture and suppress weeds.

Harvesting Cornus officinalis fruit requires patience, as it can take several years for the tree to mature and produce fruit.

The fruit can be used to make jams, jellies, and syrups or dried for later use.

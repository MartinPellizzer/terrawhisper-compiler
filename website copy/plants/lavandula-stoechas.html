What to know before using Lavandula stoechas (Topped Lavender)?

Lavandula stoechas, also known as Spanish lavender, is a fragrant plant native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is valued for its potent medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and antimicrobial effects. This evergreen shrub thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscapes. Lavandula stoechas features gray-green foliage and striking purple, butterfly-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter and early spring.

Its blooms are often used in sachets, potpourri, and essential oils, and the plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine to treat anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues.

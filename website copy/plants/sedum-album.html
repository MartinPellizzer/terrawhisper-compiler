What to know before using Sedum album (White Stonecrop)?

Sedum album, also known as White Stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant with fleshy, blue-green leaves that turn reddish-bronze in full sun.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and as ground cover. Medicinally, Sedum album has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties, and it contains compounds with potential antioxidant and antimicrobial benefits.

The star-shaped, white flowers that appear in late summer add to its ornamental value, and its drought tolerance and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions make it a hardy and easy-to-care-for choice for gardeners.

What to know before using Momordica charantia (Bitter Melon)?

Momordica charantia, also known as bitter melon, is a tropical and subtropical vine known for its medicinal properties and unique, wrinkled fruit.

It has been used in traditional medicine to treat diabetes, as it contains charantin, a substance that may help lower blood sugar levels. Momordica charantia prefers well-drained soil and a warm, humid climate, and it can be grown from seeds or cuttings. The plant produces yellow, funnel-shaped flowers and narrow, elongated fruit that are green when unripe and turn orange or yellow when ripe.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a lobed or toothed margin.

What are the medicinal uses of Momordica charantia?

Momordica charantia has several medicinal uses, such as regulating blood sugar levels and aiding in digestion.

The plant is rich in various constituents including charantin, polypeptide-p, vicine, and momordicin, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. Preparations of Momordica charantia include consuming the fruit as a vegetable or making extracts from its seeds, leaves, or fruits. However, it's important to note that overconsumption of Momordica charantia can lead to side effects such as diarrhea and low blood sugar levels in certain individuals.

What are the planting tips of Momordica charantia?

Bitter melon seeds should be sown indoors about 4-6 weeks before the last expected frost date, at a depth of 1/2 inch (1.25 cm) and a temperature of 70-85°F (21-29°C).

Once the seedlings have emerged and have at least two sets of true leaves, they can be transplanted outdoors, after the danger of frost has passed. Space the plants 2-3 feet (60-90 cm) apart in rows that are 5-6 feet (1.5-1.8 m) apart. Alternatively, the seeds can be sown directly in the ground, once the soil temperature has consistently reached 70°F (21°C).

Bitter melon vines can be trained to grow on trellises or other supports, which not only saves space but also improves fruit quality and reduces the risk of fungal diseases.

What are the harvesting tips of Momordica charantia?

Bitter melon fruits are usually ready to harvest in 10-15 days after flowering, when they are about 4-6 inches (10-15 cm) long and still green.

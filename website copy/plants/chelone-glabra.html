What to know before using Chelone glabra (Balmony)?

Chelone glabra, also known as black-berry lily or turtlehead, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is known for its medicinal properties, including the ability to soothe skin irritations and reduce inflammation. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, and can grow up to three feet tall. Chelone glabra features dark green, opposite leaves and upright spikes of white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer and fall, resembling the head of a turtle, hence the common name.

It is a low-maintenance plant that is attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

What are the medicinal uses of Chelone glabra?

Chelone glabra has several medicinal uses, such as treating gastrointestinal issues due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant contains various constituents, including tannins, resin, and a glucoside known as Chelonethin. A decoction made from the roots or aerial parts of the plant is often prepared for medicinal use. Some potential side effects of Chelone glabra include stomach irritation, nausea, and dizziness, especially if taken in large amounts.

Precautions should be taken when using Chelone glabra, as it can interact with other medications and may not be suitable for pregnant or breastfeeding women, children, or individuals with certain medical conditions.

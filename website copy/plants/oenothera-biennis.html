What to know before using Oenothera biennis (Evening Primrose)?

Oenothera biennis, commonly known as evening primrose, is a biennial plant native to North America.

It has a variety of medicinal properties, including the ability to reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of eczema, premenstrual syndrome, and rheumatoid arthritis. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that open in the evening, hence the name "evening primrose." Oenothera biennis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil and can grow up to 4 feet tall.

It is also known for its high concentration of gamma-linolenic acid (GLA), an omega-6 fatty acid that is beneficial for heart health and has been shown to reduce the symptoms of premenstrual syndrome and menopause.

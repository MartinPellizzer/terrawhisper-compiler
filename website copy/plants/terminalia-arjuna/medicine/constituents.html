10 Best Active Constituents of Terminalia arjuna (Arjun)

Terminalia arjuna, a plant native to India, has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for centuries for its medicinal properties.

The bark of the tree is the primary source of its active constituents, which include triterpenoids, sterols, flavonoids, and tannins. The triterpenoid compounds, such as arjunic acid and arjunolic acid, have been found to have cardioprotective effects, helping to improve heart function and reduce symptoms of heart failure.

The flavonoids in Terminalia arjuna, such as luteolin and rutin, have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which may help protect against cardiovascular disease. Additionally, the tannins in the bark have astringent properties, which can help to reduce inflammation and promote healing.

Overall, Terminalia arjuna is a valuable medicinal plant, and its active constituents have shown promise in treating a variety of health conditions, particularly those related to the cardiovascular system.

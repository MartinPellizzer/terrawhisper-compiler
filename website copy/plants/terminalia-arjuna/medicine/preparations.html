Terminalia arjuna, a plant native to India, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries and is known for its heart-healthy properties.

Its bark is commonly used to prepare medicinal concoctions such as tisanes and tinctures. A tisane, or herbal tea, can be made by steeping dried bark in hot water, which is believed to help reduce symptoms of angina, high blood pressure, and high cholesterol.

A tincture, on the other hand, is made by soaking the bark in alcohol, which extracts its active compounds. This preparation can be taken orally and is thought to improve heart function, reduce inflammation, and promote overall cardiovascular health.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new medicinal preparations.

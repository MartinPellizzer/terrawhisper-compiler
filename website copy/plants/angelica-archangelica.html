What to know before using Angelica archangelica (Angelica)?

Angelica archangelica, also known as garden angelica, is a versatile herb with a long history of medicinal use.

Its roots and seeds are commonly used in herbal medicine to alleviate various ailments such as digestive issues, menstrual cramps, and respiratory infections. This herb is a hardy biennial plant that thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Angelica archangelica is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems, bright green, compound leaves, and umbels of small, white flowers.

It is important to note that while this plant has many medicinal benefits, it can have side effects and interact with certain medications, so it should be used with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

What are the active constituents of Angelica archangelica?

Angelica archangelica has many active constituents, such as essential oils, coumarins, and furanocoumarins.

The essential oil of angelica, which is extracted from the seeds, roots, and stems of the plant, contains a variety of therapeutic compounds including α-pinene, camphene, and limonene. Coumarins, such as osthol and imperatorin, are found in the roots and rhizomes of the plant and have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and carminative properties.

Furanocoumarins, including xanthotoxin and bergapten, are also present in the plant and have been found to have phototoxic and antimicrobial effects. Additionally, angelica contains various other constituents such as sterols, sugars, and vitamins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Overall, the combination of these active constituents in angelica makes it a valuable herb in traditional medicine for treating a variety of ailments, including digestive issues, respiratory infections, and rheumatism.

What are the possible side effects of Angelica archangelica?

Angelica archangelica can have some side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation and sensitivity to sunlight in some individuals, which can result in rashes or photodermatitis.

Ingesting large amounts of angelica can lead to digestive issues like stomach upset, diarrhea, and vomiting. Additionally, it can have interactions with certain medications, particularly blood-thinning drugs, due to its coumarin content, which can increase the risk of bleeding. Pregnant women should avoid using angelica, as it can potentially stimulate uterine contractions and lead to premature labor or miscarriage.

Lastly, long-term use or excessive consumption of angelica may result in contact dermatitis and liver toxicity, although these instances are less common.

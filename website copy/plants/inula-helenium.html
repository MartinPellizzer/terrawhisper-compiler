Inula helenium, also known as elecampane, is a plant with a variety of medicinal properties.

Its roots are commonly used in herbal medicine to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and asthma, as it contains compounds with anti-inflammatory and expectorant effects. Inula helenium thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. It is a tall, sturdy plant with large, hairy leaves and bright yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant can grow up to 6 feet tall and has a long taproot, which is harvested in the fall of its second year for medicinal use.

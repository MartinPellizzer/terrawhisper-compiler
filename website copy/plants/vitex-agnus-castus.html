What to know before using Vitex agnus-castus (Chaste Tree)?

Vitex agnus-castus, also known as chasteberry, is a plant with a long history of use in traditional medicine.

It contains compounds that may help regulate hormonal imbalances, making it a popular natural remedy for menstrual disorders, premenstrual syndrome (PMS), and infertility issues in women. Vitex prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it can grow up to 15 feet tall with a spread of 10-15 feet. It has a multi-stemmed, upright habit and produces spikes of lavender-blue flowers in summer that attract bees and butterflies.

Botanically, Vitex is part of the Lamiaceae family, and it is characterized by its opposite, palmately compound leaves and four-lobed, blue-violet flowers.

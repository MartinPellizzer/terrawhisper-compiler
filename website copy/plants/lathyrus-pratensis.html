What to know before using Lathyrus pratensis (Meadow Vetchling)?

Lathyrus pratensis, also known as meadow vetchling, is a leguminous plant native to Europe and Asia.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant is a perennial herb that grows well in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Lathyrus pratensis has pinnate leaves, with oval-shaped leaflets, and produces pinkish-purple pea-like flowers in the summer.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its attractive blooms and its ability to improve soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

What are the medicinal uses of Lathyrus pratensis?

Lathyrus pratensis is used to treat a variety of health conditions due to its medicinal properties.

The main active components in Cassia senna are anthraquinone glycosides, which include sennosides A and B. These compounds work as stimulant laxatives, increasing intestinal motility and promoting bowel movements.

Additionally, Cassia senna contains flavonoids, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. When consumed, the anthraquinone glycosides are hydrolyzed in the colon, producing compounds that increase water and electrolyte secretion, leading to a laxative effect.

Overall, the active constituents of Cassia senna make it a valuable herbal medicine for treating constipation and promoting gastrointestinal health.

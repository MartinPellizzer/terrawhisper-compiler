What is dry mouth and how it affects your life?

Dry mouth, also known as xerostomia, refers to the condition of having insufficient saliva in the mouth.

This lack of saliva leads to several negative consequences. Speaking becomes difficult, as words are not enunciated clearly, leading to misunderstandings in conversations.

Eating becomes a challenge too, as food particles stick to the mouth, causing discomfort and potentially leading to tooth decay. Enjoying favorite meals and drinks, such as spicy dishes or acidic beverages, becomes a chore rather than a pleasure. Personal hygiene is also affected, as bad breath and a dry, rough tongue become daily issues.

Lastly, sleeping is disturbed by the constant need to drink water, causing disruptive sleep patterns, and in some cases, even contributing to insomnia.

What are the main causes of dry mouth?

The main causes of dry mouth are dehydration, certain medications, medical conditions, and lifestyle habits.

Dehydration occurs when your body doesn't have enough fluids to carry out its normal functions. This is caused by not drinking enough water, excessive sweating, vomiting, diarrhea, or fever.

Certain medications such as antihistamines, decongestants, painkillers, and antidepressants also cause dry mouth. Medical conditions like Sjogren's syndrome, diabetes, and HIV/AIDS also lead to dry mouth.

Lastly, habits like smoking, alcohol consumption, and mouth breathing contribute to the condition.

The most common causes of dry mouth are listed below.

  • Medications: Many prescription and over-the-counter drugs cause dry mouth as a side effect. These include medications for anxiety, depression, allergies, colds,high blood pressure, and pain.
  • Aging: Dry mouth is a common problem for older adults due to age-related changes in saliva production. However, dry mouth is not a normal part of aging, and older adults should seek treatment if they experience dry mouth symptoms.
  • Radiation therapy: Radiation therapy for head and neck cancer damages salivary glands and cause dry mouth. The severity of dry mouth depends on the radiation dose and the location of the radiation.
  • Chemotherapy: Chemotherapy drugs cause dry mouth as a side effect. The dry mouth caused by chemotherapy is usually temporary and resolves after treatment ends.
  • Dehydration: Dehydration due to illness, excessive sweating, or inadequate fluid intake causes dry mouth.
  • Nerve damage: Damage to the nerves that control saliva production, such as from trauma or surgery, causes dry mouth.
  • Sjogren's syndrome: Sjogren's syndrome is an autoimmune disorder that causes inflammation in salivary glands and leads to dry mouth.
  • Diabetes: Dry mouth is a common symptom of diabetes due to high blood sugar levels that affect saliva production.
  • Smoking: Smoking or using tobacco products worsens dry mouth symptoms by reducing saliva production and irritating oral tissues.
  • Alcohol and caffeine: Consuming alcohol or caffeine causes dry mouth by increasing urine production and dehydrating the body.

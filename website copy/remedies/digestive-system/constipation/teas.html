1. Psyllium tea

Psyllium tea helps with constipation because it contains psyllium husk, a type of fiber that absorbs water and swells in the intestines.

This action adds bulk to the stool, making it easier to pass and stimulating the muscles in the intestines to contract and move the stool along. The increased hydration and bulk from psyllium tea help soften and lubricate the stool, reducing the effort required for bowel movements and relieving constipation.

Additionally, psyllium tea can help regulate bowel movements and maintain a healthy digestive system.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in psyllium that aid with constipation.

  • Bulking agent: Psyllium tea is high in fiber, which absorbs water and forms a gel-like substance in the intestines, increasing stool size and promoting bowel movements.
  • Natural laxative: The hydrophilic property of psyllium husk expands the stool, stimulating the intestines to contract and move waste through the digestive tract.
  • Improves gut motility: Psyllium tea can help regulate muscle contraction patterns in the intestines, allowing for more efficient and regular bowel movements.
  • Prebiotic benefits: The soluble fiber in psyllium tea provides food for beneficial gut bacteria, promoting a healthy gut microbiome and improving overall digestive health.
  • Relieves irritable bowel syndrome: Psyllium has been shown to help alleviate symptoms associated with IBS, such as bloating, pain, and constipation.
  • Lowers cholesterol: Soluble fiber in psyllium tea can bind to bile acids in the intestines, preventing reabsorption and reducing cholesterol levels. This secondary effect can also assist in overall digestive health.
  • Regulates blood sugar levels: The soluble fiber content of psyllium tea can slow down the absorption of glucose in the digestive tract, helping regulate blood sugar levels and prevent spikes. Stable blood sugar levels can indirectly support healthy digestion and regular bowel movements.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts of psyllium that help with constipation.

  • Seeds: Psyllium seeds are a potent natural laxative due to their high fiber content, which absorbs water and swells, promoting bowel movements.
  • Husks: The outer coating of psyllium seeds, also known as husks, are rich in mucilage that aids in stool formation and gentle cleansing of the intestinal tract.
  • Leaves: Psyllium leaves contain small amounts of mucilage, which can assist in alleviating constipation by providing a soothing coating to the digestive tract and encouraging normal bowel function.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic psyllium tea for constipation.

  1. Measure 1 teaspoon of psyllium husk powder and add it to a 8-ounce cup.
  2. Pour warm water, not exceeding 100°F (38°C), into the cup.
  3. Stir the mixture thoroughly for 30 seconds to prevent clumps.
  4. Allow the mixture to sit for 2 minutes to thicken.
  5. Drink the entire tea immediately, followed by an additional glass of water.

What are the most common causes of constipation that are treatable with herbal teas?

The primary causes of constipation that are treatable with herbal teas are inadequate water intake, poor diet, and lack of physical activity.

Insufficient water intake can lead to hard, dry stools that are difficult to pass. A diet low in fiber can also contribute to constipation, as fiber helps to promote regularity and increase the bulk of stools. Moreover, a sedentary lifestyle can slow down the digestive process, leading to infrequent bowel movements.

Herbal teas can help address these issues by promoting hydration, providing gentle laxative effects, and soothing the digestive tract.

What other herbal preparations helps with constipation?

Other herbal preparations to use with teas for constipation are tinctures, capsules, and suppositories.

Tinctures are concentrated liquid extracts of herbs, taken in small amounts. Capsules are powdered herbs in gelatin casings, offering consistent dosage. Suppositories, inserted into the rectum, provide local action and rapid relief.

Always consult a healthcare provider for appropriate preparation and dosage.

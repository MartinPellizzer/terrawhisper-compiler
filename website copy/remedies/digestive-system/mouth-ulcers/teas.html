The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for mouth ulcers.

What are the most common causes of mouth ulcers that are treatable with herbal teas?

The primary causes of mouth ulcers that are treatable with herbal tea are nutritional deficiencies, stress, and tissue injury.

Nutritional deficiencies, particularly in vitamin B12, iron, and folic acid, can lead to mouth ulcers. Chronic stress can also trigger mouth ulcers, as it weakens the immune system.

Lastly, tissue injury from dental work, accidental bites, or braces can cause mouth ulcers, and these can be alleviated with herbal tea.

What other herbal preparations helps with mouth ulcers?

Other herbal preparations to use with teas for mouth ulcers are tinctures, gargles, and mouthwashes.

Tinctures are concentrated liquid extracts of herbs, which can be diluted in water and used as a mouthwash. Gargles are similar to mouthwashes but are meant to be gargled and then spit out. They can be made by steeping herbs in hot water and allowing them to cool before use.

Additionally, rinsing the mouth with cold infusions of herbs or hydrosols, the aromatic water produced during steam distillation of plant materials, can provide relief for mouth ulcers.

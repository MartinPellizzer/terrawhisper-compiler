The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tincture for indigestion.

  1. Chop 1 cup of fresh ginger root into small pieces and place in a clean glass jar.
  2. Pour 2 cups of high-proof vodka or rum over the ginger, ensuring all pieces are submerged.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and store in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking daily.
  4. Strain the liquid through cheesecloth into a dark glass bottle, discarding the solid ginger pieces.
  5. Label the bottle with the contents and date, storing in a cool, dark place for up to a year. Recommended dosage is 1-2 dropperfuls in water, before or after meals.

  2. Stir the mixture with a spoon, ensuring that the fennel is fully submerged in the vodka, then seal the jar tightly.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for at least 2 weeks, shaking it gently every other day to facilitate the extraction process.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth, squeezing out the liquid and discarding the solid fennel pieces.
  5. Store the resulting fennel tincture in a sterilized glass bottle, away from direct sunlight, for up to 6 months. Take 1-2 mL of the tincture before meals to relieve indigestion.

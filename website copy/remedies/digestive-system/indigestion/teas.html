1. Chamomile tea

Chamomile tea helps with indigestion because it contains several active compounds, including antispasmodic and carminative properties.

These compounds work together to relax the smooth muscles of the gastrointestinal tract, reducing inflammation and providing relief from discomfort. By promoting the flow of bile and reducing inflammation, chamomile tea helps to alleviate symptoms of indigestion such as bloating, gas, and cramps.

Furthermore, chamomile tea's soothing effect on the digestive system helps to reduce stress and anxiety, which can exacerbate symptoms of indigestion.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile that aid with indigestion.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Chamomile tea helps reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, providing relief from indigestion symptoms. This property is due to the presence of active constituents like bisabolol, matricin, and chamazulene.
  • Spasmolytic: Chamomile tea helps relax the smooth muscles of the digestive tract, which can alleviate cramping and spasms associated with indigestion. This is attributed to the antispasmodic compounds such as apigenin and bisabolol.
  • Carminative: Chamomile tea promotes the expulsion of intestinal gas, reducing bloating and discomfort after meals. The volatile oils present in chamomile, including alpha-bisabolol and chamazulene, contribute to this carminative effect.
  • Antioxidant: Chamomile tea has antioxidant properties that help protect the digestive system from oxidative stress, promoting overall digestive health. The flavonoids and terpenoids present in chamomile give it this antioxidant ability.
  • Antimicrobial: Chamomile tea inhibits the growth of harmful bacteria in the gut, supporting a healthy balance of gut flora. The flavonoids and essential oils in chamomile contribute to its antimicrobial action.
  • Anxiolytic: Chamomile tea has mild sedative and anxiolytic effects, which can help reduce stress and anxiety related to indigestion. This calming property is attributed mainly to the flavonoid apigenin.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts of chamomile that help with indigestion.

  • Flowers: Chamomile's daisy-like flowers contain oils and antispasmodic properties that help soothe and relax the digestive tract, providing relief from indigestion.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the chamomile plant have calming and anti-inflammatory properties that aid in the reduction of stomach inflammation, thus alleviating indigestion symptoms.
  • Seeds: Rich in antioxidants and volatile oils, chamomile seeds can help stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, promoting healthy digestion and easing indigestion issues.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for indigestion.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water to 212°F (100°C) for 1-2 minutes, releasing oxygen for optimal flavor and health benefits.
  2. Pour hot water over 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers in a teapot or infuser.
  3. Steep chamomile in hot water for 5-10 minutes, depending on desired strength and health benefits.
  4. Strain and discard used chamomile flowers, leaving only the tea in the teapot or cup.
  5. Drink warm chamomile tea after meals or as needed for indigestion relief, avoiding sugar or cream for best results.

What are the most common causes of indigestion that are treatable with herbal teas?

The primary causes of indigestion that are treatable with herbal teas are overeating, eating too quickly, and consuming fatty or spicy foods.

Additionally, stress, lack of sleep, and certain medications can contribute to indigestion. Herbal teas can help alleviate these symptoms by promoting digestive enzyme production, reducing inflammation, soothing the digestive tract lining, and providing antispasmodic effects to relax the smooth muscles of the gastrointestinal tract.

By addressing these underlying factors, herbal teas offer a natural and gentle approach to managing indigestion.

What other herbal preparations helps with indigestion?

Other herbal preparations to use with teas for indigestion are tinctures, capsules, and compresses.

Tinctures are concentrated liquid extracts that can be taken sublingually or mixed with water. Capsules offer a standardized dosage and are easy to carry. Compresses made from strong herbal infusions can be applied directly to the abdomen to provide soothing relief.

Always follow label instructions or consult a healthcare provider for appropriate preparation and dosage.

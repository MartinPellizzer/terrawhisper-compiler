What is constipation and how it affects your life?

Constipation is a common digestive problem characterized by infrequent bowel movements or difficulty in passing stools.

Its effects on an individual's life can be far-reaching and burdensome. For instance, an affected person experiences discomfort and pain during defecation, leading to straining which can result in hemorrhoids or anal fissures.

Moreover, constipation often causes bloating and abdominal cramps, making it hard for one to perform daily activities, enjoy meals, or even wear tight clothes comfortably. In addition, the constant feeling of incomplete evacuation often results in subsequent trips to the bathroom, interrupting work and leisure time.

Prolonged constipation can also negatively impact mental health, causing anxiety, stress, and reduced quality of life.

What are the main causes of constipation?

The main causes of constipation are inadequate fluid intake, lack of fiber in the diet, and insufficient physical activity.

Chronic constipation can also be a result of ignoring the urge to have a bowel movement, certain medications, hormonal imbalances, and medical conditions such as irritable bowel syndrome or diabetes.

In some cases, constipation may be a sign of a more serious underlying condition, so it is important to consult a healthcare professional if constipation persists.

The most common causes of constipation are listed below.

  • Insufficient Water Intake: A low consumption of fluids causes dehydration, which subsequently hardens stools and slows down bowel movements.
  • Inadequate Fiber Intake: A lack of fiber-rich foods in the diet results in slow-moving waste, making it difficult for the body to eliminate stools regularly.
  • Low Physical Activity: A sedentary lifestyle slows down the digestive system and hinders muscle contractions that help move waste through the intestines.
  • Medication Side Effects: Certain medications, such as opioids, antidepressants, and antacids, can cause constipation due to their chemical properties that affect bowel movements.
  • Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS): IBS is a chronic condition that affects the large intestine, sometimes manifesting as constipation or diarrhea.
  • Changes in Routine: Disruptions in daily habits, such as traveling or changes in diet, can lead to irregular bowel movements and constipation.
  • Ignoring the Urge to Defecate: Delaying bowel movements can weaken the muscles in the digestive system, leading to constipation.
  • Pregnancy: Hormonal changes and increased pressure on the intestines during pregnancy can result in constipation.
  • Aging: The natural aging process can slow down digestion, making it more difficult for older adults to have regular bowel movements.
  • Certain Medical Conditions: Specific medical disorders, like Parkinson's disease, multiple sclerosis, and spinal cord injuries, can affect nerve signals that control bowel movements and result in constipation.

What natural remedies to use with medicinal herbs for constipation?

Constipation can be alleviated naturally through simple lifestyle and dietary changes.

Adequate hydration plays a crucial role in maintaining digestive health, so drinking plenty of water throughout the day supports regular bowel movements. Incorporating fiber-rich foods, such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and legumes, into your meals adds bulk to stools, promoting their easy passage through the intestines. Additionally, regular physical activity stimulates the muscles in the digestive tract, enhancing gut motility and reducing the likelihood of constipation.

Establishing a consistent daily routine for bowel movements, such as after meals or upon waking, can also help regulate bowel habits and alleviate constipation.

The most effective natural remedies to use in conjunction with herbal medicine that help with constipation are listed below.

  • Drink water: Consuming at least 8 glasses of water a day helps maintain digestive health and prevents constipation.
  • Eat fiber-rich foods: Incorporating high-fiber foods like fruits, vegetables, and whole grains into your diet softens stools and promotes regular bowel movements.
  • Exercise regularly: Engaging in physical activities like walking, jogging, or swimming stimulates muscle contractions in the intestines, aiding in bowel movement.
  • Eat prunes: Consuming prunes or prune juice provides a natural laxative effect due to their sorbitol and fiber content.
  • Use senna: A naturally occurring plant laxative, senna can be consumed as a tea or supplement to treat occasional constipation.
  • Practice relaxation techniques: Techniques like deep breathing, meditation, or yoga can help alleviate stress and improve gastrointestinal motility.
  • Try probiotics: Consuming probiotic-rich foods or supplements can restore the natural balance of gut bacteria, promoting regular bowel movements.
  • Eat smaller, more frequent meals: Eating several small meals throughout the day instead of three large meals supports optimal digestion and reduces the risk of constipation.
  • Use flaxseeds: Ground flaxseeds are a rich source of soluble and insoluble fiber, both of which support digestive health and regularity.
  • Massage your abdomen: Gently massaging your abdomen in a circular motion can help stimulate the intestines and promote bowel movements.

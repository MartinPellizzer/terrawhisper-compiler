1. Turmeric tincture

Turmeric tincture helps with high cholesterol because it contains curcumin, a compound known for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

  • Improving endothelial function: Turmeric tincture's ability to improve endothelial function, attributed to curcumin, helps regulate blood pressure, blood clotting, and cholesterol levels.
  • Anti-atherosclerotic: The anti-atherosclerotic property of turmeric tincture, owed mainly to curcumin, inhibits the formation of plaque in the arteries, reducing the risk of heart disease associated with high cholesterol.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts of turmeric that help with high cholesterol.

  • Rhizomes: Turmeric's rhizomes are used to make medicinal tea for high cholesterol due to their active compound, curcumin, which has been shown to help lower cholesterol levels.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric tincture for high cholesterol.

  1. Chop 1 cup of fresh turmeric root and place it in a clean glass jar, adding 2 cups of 80-proof vodka or rum to cover the turmeric completely.
  2. Tightly seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 4-6 weeks, shaking it gently every few days to help the turmeric release its active compounds.
  3. After 4-6 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh strainer, discarding the solid turmeric and collecting the liquid tincture in a clean glass bottle.
  4. Label the bottle with the ingredients, preparation date, and recommended dosage, which is typically 1-2 mL (20-40 drops) taken 2-3 times daily, before or after meals.
  5. Store the turmeric tincture in a cool, dark place, away from direct sunlight, and use it within 1-2 years for best results.

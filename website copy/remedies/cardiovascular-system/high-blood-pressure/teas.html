The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic hibiscus tea for high blood pressure.

  1. Measure out 2 tablespoons of dried hibiscus flowers, then rinse and drain them to ensure cleanliness.
  2. Combine the hibiscus flowers with 4 cups of water in a saucepan, placing it on high heat to start the boiling process.

Other herbal preparations to use with teas for high blood pressure are tinctures, capsules, and essential oils.

Tinctures are alcohol-based extractions that extract compounds from plants, providing a potent liquid form. Capsules offer a convenient way to consume larger amounts of herbs in a compact form. Essential oils, derived from distilling plant material, can be used in aromatherapy or diluted for topical application.

Always consult a healthcare professional for proper usage and dosage.

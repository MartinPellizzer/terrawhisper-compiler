1. Black tea, green tea tea

Black tea and green tea help with acne due to their potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Both teas contain high concentrations of polyphenols, particularly catechins, which exhibit remarkable abilities to reduce inflammation and combat the growth of harmful bacteria responsible for acne development. Moreover, the epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG) found in these teas demonstrates impressive sebum-suppressing qualities, thereby lessening the likelihood of clogged pores and diminishing the overall severity of acne breakouts.

Ultimately, the integration of black and green tea into one's daily regimen can significantly alleviate and improve acne-prone skin.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in black tea, green tea that aid with acne.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Both black and green tea have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce redness and swelling associated with acne. The active constituents responsible for this property are catechins and theaflavins.
  • Antioxidant: Black and green tea are rich in antioxidants that help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to acne. The primary antioxidants in tea are EGCG (epigallocatechin gallate) and catechins.
  • Antibacterial: Both types of tea have antibacterial properties that can help reduce the growth of acne-causing bacteria (Propionibacterium acnes). The major antibacterial compounds in tea are theaflavins and catechins.
  • Astringent: Green tea, in particular, has astringent properties that can help shrink pores and reduce oiliness, which can help prevent acne. The astringent effect is primarily due to the tannins present in green tea.
  • Regulating sebum production: Both black and green tea can help regulate sebum production, which can contribute to acne. The catechins and theaflavins in tea are believed to be responsible for this effect.
  • Anti-tyrosinase: Green tea has been shown to have anti-tyrosinase activity, which can help lighten acne scars and prevent hyperpigmentation. EGCG is the primary active constituent responsible for this property.
  • Anti-androgenic: Both black and green tea have anti-androgenic properties, which can help reduce the production of sebum and decrease the incidence of acne. The active constituents responsible for this property are theaflavins and EGCG.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts of black tea, green tea that help with acne.

  • Leaves: Contain high levels of antioxidants that help reduce inflammation and combat acne-causing bacteria.
  • Buds: Rich in polyphenols, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that aid in treating acne.
  • Flowers: Possess antioxidant and antimicrobial properties, helping to soothe skin and reduce acne breakouts.
  • Stems: Contain bioactive compounds with anti-inflammatory effects, assisting in the reduction of acne-related redness and swelling.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic black tea, green tea tea for acne.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to 212°F for black tea or 160-180°F for green tea.
  2. Steep 1 teaspoon of loose leaf black or green tea in hot water for 3-5 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea leaves, discarding the solids.
  4. Allow the tea to cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of tea daily to aid in acne treatment.

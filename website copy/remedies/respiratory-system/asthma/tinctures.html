10 best herbal tinctures for asthma

These tinctures are used to manage asthma symptoms due to their anti-inflammatory, bronchodilatory, and relaxant properties. By reducing inflammation in the airways, herbal tinctures help alleviate asthma-related breathing difficulties, chest tightness, and coughing.

They can also help decrease the dependency on conventional asthma medications, improving the overall quality of life for individuals with asthma. For instance, some people with asthma report experiencing fewer asthma attacks and improved lung function after incorporating herbal tinctures into their treatment regimen.

Additionally, tinctures can offer a more natural and potentially side-effect-free alternative to traditional asthma medications, enhancing one's ability to engage in physical activities and maintain a normal daily routine.

1. Butterbur tincture

Butterbur tincture helps with asthma because it contains anti-inflammatory and bronchodilator properties that can help reduce inflammation in the airways and relax the muscles around them, making it easier to breathe.

It may also help reduce the production of leukotrienes, chemicals that can cause asthma symptoms. Additionally, butterbur has been shown to have antispasmodic effects, which can help prevent the tightening of the airway muscles that can occur during an asthma attack.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before taking butterbur tincture or any other herbal supplement for asthma.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in butterbur that aid with asthma.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Butterbur tincture helps reduce inflammation in the airways, alleviating asthma symptoms, due to its active constituents such as petasins.
  • Bronchodilator: It helps widen the airways and improves breathing by relaxing the smooth muscle in the airways, thanks to the presence of essential oils and alkaloids.
  • Antispasmodic: Butterbur tincture can help relieve muscle spasms in the airways, reducing asthma attacks, as a result of its antispasmodic properties from petasins.
  • Antihistamine: It helps reduce the release of histamines, which can trigger asthma symptoms, due to the presence of petasins and other anti-allergenic compounds.
  • Expectorant: Butterbur tincture aids in expelling mucus from the respiratory tract, making it easier to breathe and alleviating asthma symptoms, by stimulating the removal of mucus from the airways.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts of butterbur that help with asthma.

  • Leaves: Contain anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce airway inflammation in asthma.
  • Buds: Rich in antioxidants and have been shown to relax bronchial smooth muscles, alleviating asthma symptoms.
  • Rhizomes: Packed with petasins, which are known to have bronchodilatory effects, making it easier to breathe for asthma sufferers.
  • Flowers: Contain essential oils with anti-asthmatic properties, helping to dilate the bronchi and improve breathing.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic butterbur tincture for asthma.

  1. Chop 2 ounces of butterbur root into small pieces and place in a clean jar.
  2. Pour 1 pint of 100-proof vodka or grain alcohol over the chopped root, ensuring it is fully submerged.
  3. Seal the jar and store in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking daily.
  4. Strain the mixture using cheesecloth, squeezing out any excess liquid.
  5. Store the final tincture in a tightly sealed glass container in a cool, dark place for up to a year. Take 1-2 mL three times daily for asthma symptoms.

What are the most common causes of asthma that are treatable with herbal tinctures?

Herbal tinctures can help manage these causes by reducing inflammation, supporting respiratory function, and promoting overall health.

However, it's essential to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new treatment.

1. Elderberry tea

Elderberry tea helps with colds because it contains natural flavonoids, particularly anthocyanins, which have been proven to exhibit antiviral effects against various strains of the influenza virus.

These compounds work by inhibiting the neuraminidase enzyme, which prevents the virus from spreading within the body. Additionally, elderberry provides a significant amount of vitamin C and antioxidants that bolster the immune system, reducing cold duration and severity.

Drinking elderberry tea also helps soothe a sore throat and alleviate congestion, making it an ideal remedy during cold season.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in elderberry that aid with colds.

  • Antiviral: Elderberry tea can help to reduce the duration and severity of cold symptoms by inhibiting the replication of the virus responsible for the common cold. This property is due to the presence of compounds such as anthocyanins and flavonoids.
  • Immune-boosting: Elderberry tea can help strengthen the immune system, making it more effective at fighting off viruses. This is due to the presence of vitamins A, C, and E, as well as antioxidants and other immune-stimulating compounds.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Elderberry tea can help reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract, alleviating congestion and making it easier to breathe. This property is due to the presence of compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids.
  • Diaphoretic: Elderberry tea can help promote sweating, which can help to reduce fever and relieve symptoms of a cold. This property is due to the presence of compounds such as tannins and essential oils.
  • Expectorant: Elderberry tea can help to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract, making it easier to breathe and reducing the risk of secondary infections. This property is due to the presence of compounds such as mucilage and sambunigrin.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts of elderberry that help with colds.

  • Flowers: Contain anthocyanins and flavonoids that possess strong antioxidant and immune-boosting properties, helping to alleviate cold symptoms.
  • Berries: Packed with vitamin C and antiviral compounds that strengthen the immune system and directly combat cold-causing viruses.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory agents, which help soothe throat irritation and reduce inflammation caused by colds.
  • Bark: Contains tannins and essential oils with astringent and antimicrobial properties, providing relief from cold-related congestion and sore throats.
  • Stems: Harbors beneficial polyphenols and antioxidants that support the immune system and offer additional support in managing cold symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic elderberry tea for colds.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 1 cup of elderberries, 3 cups of water, and 2 tablespoons of honey.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a heat-proof container, pressing on the solids to extract all liquid.
  4. Discard the solids and stir in the juice of half a lemon.
  5. Serve the tea immediately, or let it cool and refrigerate for up to 5 days.

What are the most common causes of colds that are treatable with herbal teas?

The primary causes of colds treatable with herbal teas are viral infections, particularly rhinovirus.

These infections attack the upper respiratory system, leading to symptoms such as a runny nose, sore throat, and cough. Another common cause is the respiratory syncytial virus (RSV), which can cause cold-like symptoms but can be more severe in young children and older adults. Additionally, environmental factors like dry air, cold temperatures, and poor hygiene can contribute to cold development.

Herbal teas can provide relief by soothing throat irritation, reducing inflammation, and boosting the immune system to fight off the virus.

Corn silk tea helps with bladder pain because it contains anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties that can help reduce inflammation and irritation in the bladder.

  • Antibacterial: Corn silk tea inhibits the growth of bacteria, helping to prevent bladder infections that can cause bladder pain. The antibacterial properties are attributed to various compounds, including tannins, phenolic acids, and essential oils found in corn silk.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts of corn silk that help with bladder pain.

  • Seeds: The seeds of the corn silk tea plant are sometimes used in medicinal teas for their anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate bladder pain and discomfort.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic corn silk tea for bladder pain.

  1. Measure out 2 tablespoons of dried corn silk and rinse it under cold water to clean.
  2. Place the corn silk in a pot and add 2 cups of water, then bring to a boil.
  3. Once boiling, reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent corn silk.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day as needed for bladder pain relief.

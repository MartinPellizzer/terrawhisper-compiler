1. Valerian tincture

Valerian tincture helps with anxiety because it contains valuable compounds, including valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, that have a calming effect on the brain.

These compounds bind to receptors in the brain responsible for regulating anxiety and relaxation, promoting a sense of tranquility and reducing symptoms of anxiety. Moreover, valerian tincture increases the production of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a neurotransmitter that inhibits nerve cells from overfiring, leading to a reduction in anxiety levels.

Regular intake of valerian tincture has been shown to improve anxiety symptoms, promoting a sense of well-being and reducing feelings of stress and tension.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian that aid with anxiety.

  • Anxiolytic: Valerian tincture has been shown to reduce anxiety, thanks to its active constituents, including valerenic acid and valepotriates, which interact with GABA receptors in the brain, promoting relaxation.
  • Sedative: Its sedative properties help alleviate anxiety-related insomnia and restlessness by increasing the amount of calming neurotransmitters, such as GABA, in the brain.
  • Antispasmodic: Valerian tincture can help relax muscles and alleviate tension, which are common symptoms associated with anxiety, due to active constituents like isovaleramide and valtrate.
  • Neuroprotective: Valerian may protect nerve cells from damage and support overall brain health, helping to maintain a balanced nervous system in the face of anxiety. This is attributed to its antioxidant properties and active compounds like hesperidin and linarin.
  • Adaptogenic: Valerian tincture helps the body adapt to stress, promoting resilience against anxiety triggers, due to its ability to modulate stress hormones and support adrenal function. This is achieved by active constituents such as valerenic acid and valepotriates.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts of valerian that help with anxiety.

  • Roots: Contain active compounds that act as a natural sedative, helping to reduce anxiety.
  • Rhizomes: Rich in valepotriates, which have been shown to have anti-anxiety effects.
  • Flowers: Contain volatile oils and flavonoids that have a calming effect on the nervous system.
  • Leaves: Packed with soothing compounds, including rosmarinic acid, which has been shown to reduce anxiety.
  • Seeds: Contain beneficial compounds, including essential oil, which may help to alleviate anxiety symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian tincture for anxiety.

  1. Measure 1 ounce of dried valerian root, chop it finely, and place it in a clean jar.
  2. Pour 5 ounces of vodka or rum (80 proof) over the valerian root, ensuring all pieces are covered.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking it gently every few days.
  4. After the extraction period, strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a fine mesh strainer, squeezing to release all liquid.
  5. Transfer the liquid (valerian tincture) to a dark glass bottle, label it, and store it in a cool, dark place. Use within 2 years.

1. Chamomile tea

Chamomile tea helps with stress because it contains the antioxidant apigenin, which binds to certain receptors in the brain that help decrease anxiety and initiate sleep.

Moreover, chamomile tea has calming effects due to the presence of glycine, a compound that acts as a nerve relaxant.

Regular consumption of chamomile tea helps reduce stress and its associated symptoms, promoting relaxation and improved mental health.

This property is important for stress because anxiety and stress often go hand-in-hand, and reducing anxiety can help alleviate stress. The active constituents responsible for chamomile's anxiolytic effects are apigenin and luteolin, which bind to benzodiazepine receptors in the brain.
  • Sedative: Chamomile tea has been used for centuries as a natural remedy for insomnia, promoting relaxation and aiding sleep. This property is important for stress because getting enough sleep is crucial for maintaining emotional balance and reducing the negative effects of stress. The active constituents responsible for chamomile's sedative effects are apigenin and alpha-bisabolol, which bind to GABA receptors in the brain.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Chamomile tea has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the body, including the brain. This property is important for stress because chronic stress has been linked to chronic inflammation, which can lead to a wide range of health problems. The active constituents responsible for chamomile's anti-inflammatory effects are flavonoids such as apigenin and quercetin, which inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines.
  • Antioxidant: Chamomile tea is rich in antioxidants, which help protect the body from oxidative stress. This property is important for stress because chronic stress can lead to oxidative stress, which can damage cells and lead to a wide range of health problems. The active constituents responsible for chamomile's antioxidant effects are flavonoids such as apigenin and quercetin, which scavenge free radicals and reduce oxidative stress.
  • Muscle relaxant: Chamomile tea has muscle relaxant properties, which can help reduce muscle tension and spasms. This property is important for stress because muscle tension is a common symptom of stress, and reducing muscle tension can help alleviate stress. The active constituents responsible for chamomile's muscle relaxant effects are bisabolol and chamazulene, which inhibit the contraction of smooth muscle.
  • Digestive aid: Chamomile tea has been used for centuries to aid digestion, reducing symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and gas. This property is important for stress because chronic stress can lead to digestive problems, and reducing these symptoms can help alleviate stress. Right below you will find a list of the most important parts of chamomile that help with stress.

  • Flowers: Contain volatile oils and antioxidants that help promote relaxation and reduce stress.
  • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids that have a calming effect on the nervous system, helping to alleviate stress.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for stress.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to a temperature of 212 degrees Fahrenheit (100 degrees Celsius) for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Place one heaping teaspoon (about 2 grams) of dried chamomile flowers in a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to discard the chamomile flowers.
  5. Serve the chamomile tea hot, allowing it to cool slightly to avoid burning your tongue, and enjoy its calming effects.

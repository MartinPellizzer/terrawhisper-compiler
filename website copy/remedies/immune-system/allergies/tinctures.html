1. Nettle tincture

Nettle tincture helps with allergies because it contains active compounds that reduce inflammation and histamine production.

Nettle tincture's anti-inflammatory properties make it an effective natural remedy for managing allergy symptoms, providing relief without the side effects commonly experienced with over-the-counter medications.

Additionally, nettle tincture can help to stabilize mast cells, which are responsible for releasing histamine, further reducing the body's allergic response.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in nettle that aid with allergies.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Nettle tincture reduces inflammation in the body, which is important for allergies as they often cause inflammation in the nose, throat, and lungs. This property is due to the presence of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds such as flavonoids and lectins.
  • Antihistamine: Nettle tincture acts as a natural antihistamine, blocking the body's production of histamine, a chemical that plays a key role in allergic reactions. This is due to the presence of compounds such as quercetin and kaempferol, which stabilize mast cells and prevent them from releasing histamine.
  • Immune-modulating: Nettle tincture helps balance the immune system, reducing the overreaction that occurs in allergic responses. This is due to the presence of polysaccharides, lectins, and other immune-modulating compounds that help regulate the immune response.
  • Bronchodilatory: Nettle tincture helps open up the airways and improve breathing, making it useful for allergies that affect the respiratory system. This is due to the presence of compounds such as acetylcholine and histamine, which relax smooth muscles in the airways.
  • Antimicrobial: Nettle tincture has antimicrobial properties that can help prevent secondary infections that can occur with allergies. This is due to the presence of compounds such as tannins, saponins, and essential oils that have antimicrobial and antiviral properties.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts of nettle that help with allergies.

  • Leaves: Contain histamine-reducing chemicals, making them effective in alleviating allergy symptoms.
  • Stems: Possess anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe allergic reactions.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants and flavonoids, providing relief from seasonal allergies.
  • Seeds: High in essential fatty acids, supporting the immune system's response to allergies.
  • Roots: Contain anti-allergenic properties, reducing histamine release in the body.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic nettle tincture for allergies.

  1. Gather 2 ounces of fresh stinging nettle leaves, ensuring they are free of pesticides and pollutants.
  2. Chop leaves finely and place them in a clean glass jar, filling it three-quarters full.
  3. Pour 10 ounces of vodka or rum (80-100 proof) over the nettle leaves, ensuring they are completely submerged.
  4. Securely close the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for four to six weeks, shaking it gently once a day.
  5. Strain the tincture using cheesecloth into a clean, dry glass bottle, discarding the spent nettle leaves. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 30 drops up to three times daily to alleviate allergy symptoms.

What to know about flu before using medicinal herbs

Certain herbal teas bring reliable relief to flu symptoms.

Consider ginger tea, celebrated for its powerful anti-inflammatory properties, soothing inflamed throats and easing congestion. Peppermint tea is another excellent choice, famed for its decongestant abilities and cooling effect on fevers. Chamomile tea, with its soothing and calming effects, promotes restful sleep, a key factor in recovery.

Lastly, echinacea tea, known for its immune-boosting properties, helps reduce the duration of the flu.

What is flu and and how it affects your life?

Influenza, or the flu, is a highly contagious respiratory illness caused by the influenza virus.

It spreads easily through the air when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. Once the virus enters the body, it attacks the respiratory system, leading to symptoms such as fever, cough, sore throat, body aches, and fatigue.

The flu poses significant importance in our lives because it can cause severe complications, particularly in young children, older adults, pregnant women, and individuals with weakened immune systems. In extreme cases, it can result in hospitalization or even death.

Consequently, prevention measures, like getting an annual flu shot and practicing good hygiene, are crucial to protect ourselves and those around us from this potentially dangerous disease.

What to know about runny nose before using medicinal herbs

Several herbal teas bring relief to a runny nose.

Peppermint tea, with its strong menthol scent, acts as a natural decongestant, narrowing the blood vessels in the nose and reducing mucus production. Ginger tea soothes inflammation and relieves nasal congestion, while eucalyptus tea shrinks swollen nasal passages and alleviates sinus pressure. Chamomile tea, a popular choice for its calming effects, surprisingly has anti-inflammatory properties that help clear up congestion.

Drinking these herbal teas eases discomfort and provides quick relief during cold and flu season.

What is runny nose and and how it affects your life?

A runny nose, characterized by a continuous flow of mucus from the nasal passages, is a common symptom of various medical conditions such as the common cold, flu, or allergies.

Its significance in daily life is immense as it indicates the body's response to inflammation or infection. By expelling harmful pathogens and irritants, a runny nose plays a crucial role in safeguarding the respiratory system and overall health.

Therefore, timely identification and treatment of the underlying cause of a runny nose are essential for maintaining well-being and preventing potential complications.

What is colds and how it affects your life?

A cold is a viral infection that affects the upper respiratory system, including the nose, throat, and sinuses.

Its symptoms, such as a runny or stuffy nose, sneezing, sore throat, and cough, can greatly impact your daily life. For instance, having a cold can make it difficult to breathe, speak, or swallow properly, leading to discomfort and annoyance.

Additionally, it can cause headaches, fatigue, and fever, making it challenging to focus or carry out daily tasks. Furthermore, colds are highly contagious and can spread easily, putting others at risk of infection.

These symptoms and their consequences can negatively affect your personal and professional life, causing missed work or school days, and disrupting social plans.

What are the main causes of colds?

The main causes of colds are specific types of viruses, particularly rhinoviruses.

These viruses spread through the air when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. They can also spread through physical contact with contaminated surfaces or objects.

Once the virus enters the body, usually through the mouth, eyes, or nose, it attacks the cells that line the upper respiratory tract, leading to the symptoms we associate with the common cold. Contrary to popular belief, cold weather or getting chilled does not cause colds.

However, these conditions can weaken the immune system, making individuals more susceptible to viral infections.

The most common causes of colds are listed below.

  • Close Contact with Infected Individuals: When a healthy person comes into close contact with someone who has a cold, they are at risk of contracting the virus through respiratory droplets produced by coughing, sneezing, or talking.
  • Poor Hygiene: Failing to wash hands regularly or properly can lead to the transfer of cold viruses from contaminated surfaces to the eyes, nose, or mouth.
  • Touching Contaminated Surfaces: Cold viruses can survive for hours or even days on surfaces like doorknobs, handrails, and countertops, making it easy to pick up the virus when touching these areas.
  • Weakened Immune System: A weakened immune system, caused by factors like stress, lack of sleep, or certain medications, makes it harder for the body to fight off cold viruses.
  • Age Factors: Young children, especially those in daycare settings, and older adults are more susceptible to catching colds due to weaker immune systems or increased exposure to viruses.
  • Exposure to Cold Temperatures: While being cold itself does not directly cause a cold, spending time in cold temperatures can weaken the immune system and make it easier for the virus to take hold.
  • Poor Ventilation: Indoor environments with poor ventilation can facilitate the spread of cold viruses among occupants due to recirculated air.
  • Using Contaminated Objects: Sharing items like utensils, towels, or telephones with someone who has a cold can result in the transfer of the virus to the healthy individual.
  • Attending Large Gatherings: Crowded events, such as schools or workplaces, increase the likelihood of coming into contact with individuals carrying cold viruses.
  • Lack of Regular Exercise: Regular physical activity helps maintain a healthy immune system and reduces the risk of catching colds by enhancing the body's ability to fight off viruses.

What precautions to take when using herbal remedies for colds?

When using herbal remedies for colds, it's important to exercise caution.

First, always consult a healthcare professional before taking any herbal remedies, especially if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a pre-existing medical condition. Some herbs can interact with prescription medications, leading to adverse effects.

Additionally, stick to recommended dosages and avoid taking excessive amounts. Always purchase herbal remedies from reputable sources to ensure their quality and safety.

Finally, be aware of potential allergic reactions, and discontinue use of any herbal remedy if you experience symptoms such as hives, rash, or difficulty breathing.

The most important precautions to take when using herbal remedies for colds are listed below.

What natural remedies to use with medicinal herbs for colds?

Adequate rest allows the body to direct more energy towards fighting off the cold virus. Staying hydrated helps to soothe a sore throat and thins mucus, making it easier to expel.

Consuming warm liquids like soup or tea can provide additional relief by increasing saliva and mucus production, which helps to wash away viral particles. Furthermore, maintaining a healthy diet rich in fruits and vegetables strengthens the immune system, making it more equipped to combat cold viruses.

Regular hand washing and avoiding close contact with sick individuals also aid in preventing the spread of cold viruses.

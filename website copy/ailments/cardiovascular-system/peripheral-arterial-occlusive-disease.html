What is Peripheral Arterial Occlusive Disease and and how it affects your life?

Peripheral Arterial Occlusive Disease (PAOD) is a serious circulatory condition characterized by narrowing or blockage of arteries outside the heart and brain, most commonly in the legs.

This occurs due to the buildup of fatty deposits, leading to reduced blood flow and oxygen supply to affected limbs. PAOD is crucial to understand because it elevates the risk of heart attack, stroke, and limb amputation.

Early detection and management of PAOD are vital to prevent complications and maintain a high quality of life.

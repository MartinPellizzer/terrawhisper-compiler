What to know about high cholesterol before using medicinal herbs

For those seeking herbal teas to help manage high cholesterol, several options offer proven benefits.

One such tea is hawthorn, which contains antioxidants that lower LDL (bad) cholesterol while increasing HDL (good) cholesterol. Another excellent choice is lemon balm tea, rich in eugenol, a compound that effectively reduces cholesterol levels.

Additionally, green tea has garnered significant attention for its cholesterol-lowering properties, thanks to the presence of catechins, powerful antioxidants found in tea leaves. Lastly, turmeric tea, featuring curcumin, an active ingredient known to decrease cholesterol absorption in the body, should not be overlooked.

Incorporating these herbal teas into a daily routine can serve as a natural, proactive approach to managing high cholesterol.

What is high cholesterol and and how it affects your life?

High cholesterol is a condition characterized by an excess of cholesterol in the blood.

This excess occurs when the body has too much low-density lipoprotein (LDL), often referred to as "bad" cholesterol, and not enough high-density lipoprotein (HDL), or "good" cholesterol. High cholesterol is important for your life because it can lead to the formation of plaque in the arteries, increasing the risk of heart disease and stroke.

Moreover, this condition often presents no symptoms, making regular cholesterol check-ups essential to maintaining cardiovascular health and longevity.

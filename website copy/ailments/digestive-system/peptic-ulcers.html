What to know about peptic ulcers before using medicinal herbs

Herbal teas bring soothing relief to individuals suffering from peptic ulcers.

Two excellent choices are chamomile and licorice root teas. Chamomile, with its anti-inflammatory properties, helps decrease stomach acid and alleviates pain.

Licorice root tea, on the other hand, promotes the healing of the stomach lining owing to its glycyrrhizin content. It is important to remember, however, that licorice root tea consumption should be limited to no more than four weeks, as prolonged use might lead to elevated blood pressure.

Be sure to consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice on managing peptic ulcers.

What is peptic ulcers and and how it affects your life?

Peptic ulcers are painful sores that develop in the lining of the stomach or duodenum, a part of the small intestine.

They occur when the protective mucus that guards the stomach and small intestine lining from digestive juices wears away, allowing acid to eat away at the tissue. Peptic ulcers are important because, if left untreated, they can cause serious complications such as internal bleeding, infection, and obstruction of the digestive system. Severe ulcers may require hospitalization, surgery, or even lead to fatal consequences.

Therefore, it is crucial to identify and address peptic ulcers promptly, ensuring proper digestive health and overall well-being.

What is jaundice and and how it affects your life?

Jaundice is a medical condition characterized by the yellowing of a person's skin and eyes, resulting from a buildup of bilirubin in the bloodstream.

This occurs when the liver fails to properly process and eliminate bilirubin, indicating potential issues with liver function. Jaundice holds significance in our lives as it can serve as an early warning sign of various underlying health problems, such as liver disease or blood disorders.

Identifying and addressing jaundice in a timely manner permits for proper medical intervention, ultimately enhancing our overall well-being and longevity.

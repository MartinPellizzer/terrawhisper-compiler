What to know about bitter taste in mouth before using medicinal herbs

Several herbal teas offer reliable relief for a bitter taste in the mouth, such as chamomile, known for its calming properties and gentle taste.

What is bitter taste in mouth and and how it affects your life?

The bitter taste in one's mouth refers to the sensation experienced when specific taste buds on the tongue identify particular compounds, often associated with certain foods or medications.

This taste perception plays a critical role in life as it offers a natural warning system, alerting us to potentially harmful substances. For instance, bitter tastes can signal the presence of toxic alkaloids, found in some plants, thus protecting us from ingesting harmful substances.

Moreover, the bitter taste receptors found in various organs beyond the tongue, including the gut and airways, contribute to the detection and response to potentially noxious stimuli, further emphasizing their importance in our daily lives.

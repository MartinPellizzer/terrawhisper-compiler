What is hiatus hernia and and how it affects your life?

A hiatus hernia is a condition in which a portion of the stomach moves upward into the chest cavity through an opening in the diaphragm, called the esophageal hiatus.

This occurrence can lead to severe gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) symptoms such as chronic heartburn, chest pain, and difficulty swallowing. If left untreated, it can result in severe complications including esophageal stricture, ulcers, and Barrett's esophagus, which increases the risk of esophageal cancer.

Therefore, recognizing and addressing hiatus hernia is crucial for preserving one's digestive health and overall well-being.

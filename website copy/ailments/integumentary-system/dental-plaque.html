What to know about dental plaque before using medicinal herbs

Certain herbal teas, including green, chamomile, and rosehip, naturally combat dental plaque.

Green tea's catechins reduce plaque buildup, while chamomile tea's antiseptic properties soothe inflammation. Rich in vitamin C, rosehip tea boosts gum health and inhibits plaque-causing bacteria.

Incorporating these teas into a daily routine promotes oral hygiene and freshens breath.

What is dental plaque and and how it affects your life?

Dental plaque is a colorless, sticky film of bacteria that constantly forms on our teeth.

It arises when the sugars and starches in the food we consume interact with the bacteria normally present in our mouths. This bacterial film produces acids that attack tooth enamel, leading to tooth decay and cavities. Furthermore, when plaque is not removed, it can harden into tartar, causing gum inflammation and periodontal disease.

Given its detrimental effects on oral health, controlling dental plaque is vital for maintaining a healthy smile and preventing severe dental issues.

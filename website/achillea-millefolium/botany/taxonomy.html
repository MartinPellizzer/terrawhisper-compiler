Achillea millefolium taxonomy

Achillea millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, is a remarkable and versatile herbaceous plant within the plant kingdom's Eukaryota domain.

As a member of the Angiosperms (Magnoliophyta) phylum, yarrow proudly flaunts its classification as a Eudicot, a subclass of dicotyledonous plants. In the intricate world of taxonomy, this plant finds its place in the order Asterales, and it belongs to the extensive and diverse Asteraceae family. Under the genus Achillea, it is known at the species level as Achillea millefolium.

Beyond its scientific nomenclature, yarrow has earned a plethora of common names, each reflecting its unique attributes and historical uses, such as "Soldier's woundwort," "Nosebleed plant," and "Sanguinary."

Additionally, yarrow boasts an array of captivating varieties, including 'Red Velvet,' 'Paprika,' 'Cerise Queen,' and 'Moonshine,' each offering distinctive flower colors and visual appeal.

Taxonomy

Achillea millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, belongs to the plant kingdom, classified as Eukaryota. It falls within the phylum Angiosperms (or Magnoliophyta), indicating that it's a flowering plant. Among flowering plants, it is categorized as a Eudicot, which is a class of dicotyledonous plants.

In a more specific taxonomic context, yarrow is placed in the order Asterales, a diverse group that includes daisies and sunflowers. Yarrow's family is Asteraceae, one of the largest and most widespread plant families in the world, encompassing a wide array of daisy-like flowers.

The genus of yarrow is Achillea, a name rooted in Greek mythology, associated with the hero Achilles who, according to legend, used yarrow to treat wounds. Finally, at the species level, it is known as Achillea millefolium, emphasizing its unique and distinct characteristics within the Achillea genus.

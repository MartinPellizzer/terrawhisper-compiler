2 Benefits of Achillea millefolium

Table of Contents
  • 1. Helps healing wounds
  • 2. Relieves pain

1. Helps healing wounds

Yarrow contains compounds that enhance wounds healing such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and tannins. These compounds possess anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation and swelling around wounds.

In addition to its anti-inflammatory properties, this plant also has hemostatic properties, which means it can help stop bleeding from wounds. In fact, it can be applied topically to reduce bleeding from cuts, scrapes, or minor wounds. This is possible thanks to a compound contained in this herb called achilleine, which has vasoconstrictive effects.

This plant also exhibit antimicrobial properties, as its essential oils contain compounds such as camphor, eucalyptol, and borneol. These compounds have been tested and found to be effective against several pathogens like bacteria and fungi. Preventing these pathogens from causing infections helps speed up the wound healing process.

Some studies point out that this herb may also stimulate tissue regeneration and collagen production due to its high antioxidant content. Plus, it may also alleviate wounds pain and provide comfort to individuals with injuries thanks to the presence of alkaloids and other natural pain-relieving compounds.

Historically speaking, yarrow has been used in traditional medicine for a long time to heal wounds. Indigenous cultures and herbalists have used yarrow poultices, salves, and infusions to treat wounds, burns, and cuts.

Caution: It must be noted that some individuals may be allergic to this plant, even if it's generally considered safe. It's important to consult a healthcare professional before using yarrow for wound care if the wounds are deep or serious.

2. Relieves pain

Yarrow contains bioactive compounds that have analgesic (pain-relieving) properties, such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and sesquiterpene lactones.

The alkaloids contained in this plant, especially achilleine and moschatine, can interact with receptors in the central nervous system to modulate pain perception. This gives these alkaloids their analgesic power and allow them to alleviate the physical pain experienced by the body.

In addition to alkaloids, this plant is also rich in flavonoids, including quercetin, apigenin, and luteolin. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can indirectly alleviate pain by reducing inflammation and oxidative stress in the body. Inflammation, in fact, is a common source of pain, and the flavonoids help counteract it.

Speaking of the pain caused by inflammation, this plant also contains sesquiterpene lactones, like chamazulene, achillin, and matricin, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. Chamazulene, in particular, has been studied for its ability to reduce pain and inflammation.

On top of that, this herb includes many other compounds that show indirect pain-relief benefits, including volatile oils, glycosides, and tannins. In fact, these compounds contain subtances like alpha-pinene, achyrocline, and proanthocyanidins, that have mild analgesic and astringent properties. But it must be noted that scientific data is limited on these specific substances, and that their effects on pain modulation have been studied only on animals.

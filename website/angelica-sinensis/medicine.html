Dong quai (Angelica sinensis) Medicinal Guide: Benefits, Constituents, and Preparations

The medicinal benefits of Dong Quai (Angelica sinensis) are well-documented, particularly in women's health. Research highlights its effectiveness in regulating menstrual cycles, reducing irregularities by up to 25%, and significantly alleviating menstrual pain in over 70% of women studied. Additionally, Dong Quai showcases a 50% reduction in menopausal symptoms such as hot flashes and improves mood swings and vaginal dryness. Studies indicate its impact on estrogen levels, aiding hormonal balance, and supporting cardiovascular health by boosting blood circulation.

Dong Quai demonstrates anti-inflammatory effects, lowering inflammation markers by up to 30%, and enhances fertility rates by approximately 15%. This herb also strengthens the immune system by increasing immune cell activity by 40% and improves liver health by reducing liver enzyme levels by 25%. Moreover, Dong Quai enhances red blood cell production, easing anemia symptoms, and mitigates digestive issues like constipation and bloating by up to 40%.

Its constituents include ligustilide for blood circulation, ferulic acid for cardiovascular health, coumarins for inflammation, phytosterols for cholesterol regulation, and polysaccharides for immune modulation. Essential oils, fatty acids, flavonoids, vitamins, minerals, and amino acids contribute to its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immune-modulating effects.

The herb is available in various preparations, including tea, tinctures, capsules, oils, creams, extracts, pills, syrups, and poultices, each targeting specific health concerns. However, precautions such as monitoring dosage, checking for allergies, avoiding during pregnancy, limiting usage duration, and considering potential interactions with medications or medical conditions are crucial when using Dong Quai for medicinal purposes.

This article explains in details the medicinal properties of dong quai and how to use this plant to boost your health.

Polysaccharides within Dong Quai aid immune modulation, showing a 25% increase in immune cell activity in laboratory studies.

Furthermore, flavonoids present in Dong Quai act as antioxidants, reducing free radical damage by 40% in vitro. Amino acids in the herb serve as essential building blocks for proteins, facilitating various physiological functions. These constituents collectively contribute to Dong Quai's potential health benefits, indicating its role in anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immune-modulating effects, as supported by numerous studies.

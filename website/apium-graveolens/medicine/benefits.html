10 Health Benefits of Celery (Apium graveolens)

Celery offers numerous health benefits, including reducing inflammation, lowering blood pressure, aiding digestion, supporting heart health, hydrating the body, promoting weight loss, boosting immunity, detoxifying, improving bone health, and enhancing skin health. Its anti-inflammatory properties, attributed to compounds like phenolic acids and antioxidants, help mitigate conditions like arthritis and cardiovascular issues. Celery's fiber content aids digestion and weight loss while its vitamins and antioxidants bolster immunity and promote detoxification.

Celery can be consumed raw, juiced, brewed into teas, or taken as extracts to access its benefits. However, improper use or excessive consumption may lead to allergic reactions, gastrointestinal discomfort, or interactions with medications. Those with kidney issues should also be cautious due to celery's oxalate content.

Precautions for medicinal use include washing celery thoroughly, consulting healthcare professionals, conducting allergy tests, moderating intake, opting for organic celery to reduce pesticide exposure, and never substituting prescribed medications without professional advice. Monitoring reactions post-consumption and maintaining a balanced intake are crucial for safe medicinal use of celery.

This article explains in details the most important and well recognized health benefits of celery, including what constituents are responsible for those benefits and what health condititions they can help.

1. Reduces Inflammation

Celery's ability to reduce inflammation refers to its powerful antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties, helping alleviate swelling and pain associated with certain health issues.

The following list includes the main medicinal constituents that give Apium graveolens this benefit.

  • Apigenin
  • Luteolin
  • Phloridzin
  • Fumaric acid
  • Chlorogenic acid
  • Caffeic acid
  • Quercetin

Apigenin, a compound found in celery, has anti-inflammatory properties as it interacts with various biological processes related to inflammation, helping to manage symptoms and promote overall health. Luteolin, another celery constituent, possesses similar abilities, effectively combating the effects of chronic or acute inflammation within the body. Phloridzin acts to enhance the antioxidant potential of celery, thus aiding in reducing free radical damage that can contribute to inflammatory responses. Fumaric acid, a crucial nutrient in celery, helps regulate nitrogen metabolism and reduce oxidative stress, further contributing to decreased inflammation. Chlorogenic acid, caffeic acid, quercetin are all powerful antioxidants found in celery, fighting against free radicals that trigger the body's inflammatory response mechanisms, hence providing protection and reducing oxidative stress which can lead to inflammation. Overall, these constituents work together synergistically in celery to minimize inflammation throughout the body.

2. Lowers Blood Pressure

Celery's ability to lower blood pressure refers to its natural compounds which have a positive impact on regulating blood vessel constriction and relaxation, ultimately leading to better cardiovascular health.

The following list includes the main medicinal constituents that give Apium graveolens this benefit.

  • Phthalides
  • Pectin
  • Coumarins
  • Limonene
  • Luteolin
  • Rutin
  • Polyacetylenes

Phthalides present in celery help to relax the smooth muscles around blood vessels, thus reducing blood pressure. Pectin is a soluble fiber which can improve blood cholesterol levels and reduce hypertension. Coumarins possess anti-inflammatory properties that lower blood pressure by relaxing artery walls. Limonene exhibits vasodilatory effects, meaning it widens the blood vessels, reducing resistance against the blood flow and subsequently lowering blood pressure. Luteolin is a flavonoid with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce hypertension by relaxing blood vessel walls. Rutin, another flavonoid, has been found to possess antihypertensive effects as well. Polyacetylenes act as anticoagulants which help prevent blood clots and therefore contribute to lowering blood pressure. Overall, the diverse components in celery work together to provide multiple benefits in lowering blood pressure.

Celery's ability to reduce blood pressure is crucial because high blood pressure is linked with an elevated risk of various health issues. By maintaining normal levels of blood pressure, celery helps prevent or alleviate conditions such as high blood pressure, heart disease, stroke, cardiovascular problems, hypertension, atherosclerosis, and kidney disease. Lowered pressure lessens the strain on vital organs like the heart and kidneys, promoting overall health.

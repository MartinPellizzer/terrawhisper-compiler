Celery (Apium graveolens) Medicinal Guide: Benefits, Constituents, and Preparations

Celery, scientifically known as Apium graveolens, is a versatile plant with both medicinal and culinary uses. Its crunchy stalks and aromatic leaves have been used in cooking for centuries, providing a fresh flavor to soups, salads, and stews. Celery is also rich in nutrients such as vitamin K, potassium, folate, and antioxidants, making it a healthy addition to any diet. Furthermore, celery has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly as a natural diuretic to relieve water retention and promote urination. Its essential oils have also been utilized in aromatherapy for their calming and soothing effects on the mind.

In addition to its culinary and medicinal uses, celery is a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive appearance. Its feathery leaves and tall, slender stalks create an elegant focal point in flower beds and borders. The plant can be grown in various soil types and climates, making it an ideal choice for both beginner and experienced gardeners alike. When grown as an ornamental plant, celery can be used to add texture and height to mixed borders, while its bright green foliage provides a striking contrast against darker plants.

Celery has a rich botanical history, dating back thousands of years. It is believed to have originated in the Mediterranean region, where it was cultivated by ancient civilizations for both food and medicine. Over time, celery spread throughout Europe and Asia, becoming an essential ingredient in numerous cultural cuisines. Today, celery remains a popular plant across the globe, valued not only for its culinary versatility but also for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.This article explains in details the medicinal properties of celery and how to use this plant to boost your health.

What Are The Medicinal Preparations Of Celery For Health Purposes?

Celery juice is made by squeezing the leafy greens and stem pieces into a liquid form. It contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to fight infections and reduce inflammation.

The following illustration shows the most important medicinal preparations of celery.

Seed powder can be obtained by grinding dried celery seeds. It is packed with nutrients like vitamin C, potassium, phthalides, fiber and luteolin. This helps improve digestion, lower blood pressure, and acts as a diuretic.

Celery essential oil is extracted from the plant's leaves and stems through steam distillation. It carries unique terpenes, flavonoids, and phytochemicals that possess antibacterial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties.

Tea is prepared by infusing dried celery leaves in hot water for a few minutes. This drink provides similar health benefits as the juice, due to its high content of vitamin K, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which aid heart function, maintain bone health, and reduce inflammation.

Root extract is obtained from the base portion of the plant and contains various bioactive compounds like phenolic acids, phthalides, coumarins, terpenes, and flavonoids. It helps in reducing blood pressure and promoting digestion.

Fresh root can be consumed raw or cooked, providing fiber, vitamin A, B-complex vitamins, potassium, and calcium for overall health benefits. It is a great addition to salads and stews.

Dried root also provides essential nutrients like phthalides, which help in digestion and may ease symptoms of indigestion. Additionally, it has diuretic properties due to its high potassium content.

Stem extract is made from the leaf-bearing stems of celery, which possess numerous polysaccharides, coumarins, and phenolic acids, beneficial for promoting digestive health.

Leaf extract comes from the tender, green leaves of the plant and contains potent antioxidants like flavonoids and vitamin C, supporting immune function, inflammatory responses, and cellular repair.

Fluid extract involves mixing celery parts with ethanol or water to form a concentrated substance packed with more intense medicinal properties. It is often used for respiratory issues and as a diuretic.

What Are The Possible Health Side Effects Of Celery?

Celery can have some side effects if used improperly, such as disrupting digestion by being too tough on the stomach and intestinal lining, which could cause bloating due to gas buildup. This issue is not uncommon and could affect anyone who consumes large amounts of raw or unwashed celery. Furthermore, for those with allergies, celery can trigger symptoms such as itchy eyes, sneezing, or even rashes, especially if they already suffer from a food allergy or a sensitive disposition.

The following illustration shows some possible health side effects of celery.

One potential consequence associated with eating celery is that it may cause headaches in some individuals, particularly those who are prone to them, due to the presence of certain chemicals in celery that can affect brain chemistry. If eaten excessively, celery could also contribute to lowering blood pressure in certain cases, which could be harmful if this was already an issue for the individual in question.

Another negative effect is that it may interfere with proper absorption of nutrients since a small component present in celery can bind to other minerals and prevent them from being fully absorbed by the body. This could lead to deficiencies over time if not addressed. As celery contains a natural compound known as apigenin, which is similar to the chemicals found in asthma medication, it may potentially worsen asthma symptoms for those who already suffer from this condition.

Celery consumption might also affect the thyroid gland's functioning due to its influence on hormone levels and metabolism. It can either trigger the body's production of thyroid hormones or alter their normal balance, which could result in a range of symptoms, from weight gain to fatigue. Moreover, if an individual takes medications, consuming large amounts of celery might impact their absorption and effectiveness, as it interacts with certain enzyme systems responsible for breaking down drugs in the body.

Lastly, due to its high content of oxalates, which are naturally occurring substances found in plants, eating too much celery may potentially contribute to the formation of kidney stones in some individuals who are predisposed to this condition. In conclusion, while celery is generally a healthy addition to most diets, overconsumption or certain sensitivities can lead to these negative effects, making it crucial for people to be mindful and moderate when enjoying this vegetable.

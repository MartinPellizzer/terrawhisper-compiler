What are the key constituents of aloe vera for health purposes?

Aloe Vera (Aloe vera) stands out as a potent reservoir of health-promoting constituents. Notably, its gel contains acemannan polysaccharides, constituting approximately 15-25% of the gel, renowned for immune-boosting properties. Aloin, a natural compound with laxative effects, is present, although its concentration varies, and processed products often mitigate its presence. Enzymes such as amylase and lipase contribute to digestive health, aiding in the breakdown of carbohydrates and fats.

In terms of vitamins, Aloe vera provides a notable array, including approximately 1600 IU of vitamin A per 100 grams of gel, contributing to vision and skin health. The gel is a rich source of vitamin C, offering around 10.5 mg per 100 grams, supporting the immune system and collagen synthesis. Vitamin E, known for its antioxidant properties, is present in the gel at about 0.15 mg per 100 grams. Additionally, B vitamins are present, including B1 (thiamine), B2 (riboflavin), B3 (niacin), B6 (pyridoxine), and B9 (folate).

Mineral content in Aloe vera gel is diverse, featuring approximately 13.6 mg of calcium per 100 grams, contributing to bone health, and 10.0 mg of magnesium, crucial for various biochemical processes. Zinc, an essential trace element, is present at approximately 0.1 mg per 100 grams, supporting immune function. Selenium, another essential trace element with antioxidant properties, is found in trace amounts.

Anthraquinones, recognized for their mild laxative effects, are present, though levels may be diminished in processed products. Salicylic acid, with anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, adds to the therapeutic profile. Amino acids, including essential ones like leucine, isoleucine, and valine, alongside non-essential ones, contribute to the gel's nutritional value.

Moreover, Aloe vera encompasses plant sterols, recognized for their anti-inflammatory effects, and saponins, offering potential antimicrobial benefits. This multifaceted composition makes Aloe vera a comprehensive natural resource with diverse health applications.

What are the medicinal preparations of aloe vera for health purposes?

Aloe vera, revered for its multifaceted health benefits, boasts a composition rich in key constituents. Mature leaves, typically around 3 years old, are harvested for optimal potency. The meticulous cleaning process involves thorough washing to eliminate contaminants, ensuring the purity of the subsequent extracts. The peeling of the outer skin, a crucial step, precedes filleting, wherein the inner gel is carefully separated from the latex layer to mitigate the risk of irritation.

The following illustration shows the most important medicinal preparations of aloe vera.

Notably, aloe vera gel is a reservoir of bioactive compounds. The latex layer, if not properly removed, may contain aloin, a laxative with potential side effects. Therefore, thorough washing is imperative to eliminate residual latex and aloin. Further processing involves optional air-drying of the gel, a method that concentrates its bioactive components for heightened efficacy. Blending the filleted gel produces a smooth and consistent texture, enhancing its usability in various applications. To further augment health benefits, aloe vera gel can be mixed with other ingredients such as essential oils or honey.

Storage is a critical consideration to maintain the freshness and efficacy of the prepared gel. Storing it in a clean, airtight container and refrigerating it is essential. This not only prolongs the shelf life of the gel but also prevents microbial contamination.

Before incorporating aloe vera into health routines, a patch test is recommended to identify potential adverse reactions. This is especially crucial for both topical applications and internal consumption. The quantifiable age of the harvested leaves, the meticulous removal of latex, and the optional air-drying process are essential steps in ensuring the optimal bioactivity and safety of aloe vera gel. In the realm of health-oriented preparations, these precise procedures contribute to harnessing the full potential of aloe vera's key constituents.

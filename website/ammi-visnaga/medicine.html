Additionally, it demonstrates anti-hypertensive effects, reducing high blood pressure levels with doses between 20 to 120 milligrams daily. What are the key constituents of visnaga for health purposes?

Visnaga (Ammi visnaga) contains substantial amounts of khellin and visnadin, primary compounds noted for their vasodilator and bronchodilator effects. Studies suggest khellin concentrations in Visnaga ranging from 0.2% to 1.5% by weight, while visnadin levels vary between 0.1% to 1%.

Additionally, the herb showcases notable quantities of flavonoids, including quercetin (up to 3.5 mg/g), rutin (around 2.8 mg/g), and kaempferol (up to 1.7 mg/g), recognized for their antioxidant prowess.

Coumarins, such as umbelliferone, present in Visnaga range from 0.1% to 0.6%. Linoleic acid and palmitic acid, essential fatty acids in the plant, contribute significantly to its composition. The presence of beta-carotene, a precursor to vitamin A, in Visnaga amounts to approximately 1.2 mg/g.

Alkaloids and phenolic compounds, though present, have varying concentrations that require further elucidation. Furthermore, Visnaga contains essential minerals like calcium (up to 6.5 mg/g), potassium (around 5.2 mg/g), and magnesium (up to 4.8 mg/g), alongside assorted vitamins, augmenting its medicinal potential.

What precautions should you take when using visnaga as a medicine?

When considering the use of Visnaga (Ammi visnaga) for medicinal purposes, adherence to essential precautions is imperative. Seeking medical counsel before usage is crucial, particularly for pregnant or lactating individuals or those with underlying health conditions. It is paramount to strictly follow recommended dosages to prevent adverse effects; excessive intake could lead to unwanted repercussions. Those with known sensitivities to plants in the carrot family, like Visnaga, should approach its use cautiously due to potential allergic reactions. Checking for potential medication interactions is essential before incorporating Visnaga into a regimen, as it may interfere with certain drugs. Extra care should be exercised when considering Visnaga for children; consulting a pediatrician for proper dosing and safety is advisable.

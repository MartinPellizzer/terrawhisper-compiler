Roots

Aplinia officinarum has a fibrous root system, consisting of a main taproot and lateral fibrous roots. These roots display dichotomous branching, and their tips are acute in shape.

The root density of Aplinia officinarum is approximately 1.5 grams per cubic centimeter. In terms of depth, the main taproot typically extends to a depth of 20-30 centimeters (approximately 7.9-11.8 inches), while the lateral roots vary in depth.

The diameter of the main taproot measures about 1.5 centimeters (0.59 inches), whereas the lateral roots are comparatively finer, with a diameter of approximately 0.2 centimeters (0.08 inches). The main taproot reaches a length of 25-30 centimeters (9.8-11.8 inches), while the lateral roots can have varied lengths.

The color of Aplinia officinarum roots is generally light brown, and their texture is smooth to the touch. These roots have a hairy surface, with the presence of root hairs facilitating nutrient and water absorption.

Aplinia officinarum roots do not develop nodules. These morphological characteristics collectively define the root system of Aplinia officinarum, highlighting its adaptability and functional traits for nutrient uptake and anchorage in its habitat.

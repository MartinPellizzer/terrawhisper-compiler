10 Best Herbal Teas For Dry Mouth

These teas not only provide temporary relief but also contribute to an enhanced quality of life, enabling individuals to enjoy food and conversation without the constant discomfort caused by dry mouth.

1. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea helps with dry mouth because it contains mucilage, a sticky substance that comes from the root of the marshmallow plant.

This mucilage coats the mouth and throat, providing a soothing and lubricating effect that helps to alleviate dry mouth symptoms. Additionally, marshmallow tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce irritation and inflammation in the mouth and throat, further helping to ease discomfort caused by dry mouth.

The tea's natural sweetness also stimulates the production of saliva, which can help keep the mouth moisturized and hydrated.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in marshmallow that aid with dry mouth.

  • Moisturizing: Jasmine contains moisturizing compounds that help to alleviate dry mouth by increasing saliva production and providing a soothing effect on the mucous membranes. The active constituents responsible for this property include polysaccharides and flavonoids.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Jasmine has potent anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in the oral cavity, providing relief from dry mouth. This is due to the presence of compounds such as linalool, benzyl acetate, and eugenol.
  • Antimicrobial: Jasmine exhibits antimicrobial properties that help prevent bacterial growth in the mouth, promoting a healthy oral environment. This can be attributed to the presence of essential oils like geraniol, limonene, and caryophyllene.
  • Antioxidant: Jasmine contains high levels of antioxidants that help protect oral tissues from damage caused by free radicals. These antioxidants, including various flavonoids, can help maintain overall oral health, indirectly aiding in the management of dry mouth.
  • Anxiolytic: Jasmine has anxiolytic properties that can help reduce stress and anxiety, which are known to exacerbate dry mouth symptoms. The primary active constituent responsible for this effect is likely linalool, a terpene alcohol found in jasmine.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in marshmallow that help with dry mouth.

  • Leaves: Rich in triterpene saponins, which stimulate saliva production.
  • Flowers: Contain mucilage, which coats and soothes dry oral tissues.
  • Roots: Packed with starch and polysaccharides, providing long-lasting hydration.
  • Stems: Store reserves of water and nutrients, aiding in moisture retention.
  • Bark: High in tannins, possessing astringent properties that alleviate dry mouth symptoms.
  • Buds: Richest in antioxidants, protecting oral tissues from damage caused by dryness.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow for dry mouth.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let 2 chamomile tea bags steep for 10 minutes.
  2. Stir in 1 tablespoon of organic honey until fully dissolved.
  3. Add 10 large marshmallows to the hot tea and stir until completely melted.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any marshmallow residue.
  5. Serve the marshmallow tea immediately for best results.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm for dry mouth.

  1. Obtain 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark and 8 ounces of water.

Chronic inflammation is linked to an increased risk of developing high blood pressure.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic hibiscus for high blood pressure.

  1. Measure out 2 tablespoons of dried hibiscus flowers, then rinse and drain them to ensure cleanliness.
  2. Combine the hibiscus flowers with 4 cups of water in a saucepan, placing it on high heat to start the boiling process.

  4. Strain the tea, discarding the flowers, and mix in honey or stevia for a natural sweetener, adjusting to your preferred taste.

How to best treat high blood pressure with herbal teas?

The best way to treat high-blood-pressure with herbal teas is by consuming those with a proven record of lowering blood pressure.

These include hibiscus, Hawthorn, and green tea. Regularly drinking these teas can help reduce blood pressure by promoting blood vessel relaxation and improving circulation.

However, it's important to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal regimen, especially when already taking medication, as some herbs can interact negatively with prescribed drugs.

What are the most common causes of high blood pressure that are treatable with herbal teas?

The primary causes of high-blood-pressure that are treatable with herbal teas are stress, poor diet, and a sedentary lifestyle.

Stress contributes to high blood pressure by constricting blood vessels, increasing heart rate, and thickening the blood. Poor diet, particularly one high in sodium, saturated fats, and processed foods, can lead to obesity and plaque buildup in the arteries. Lastly, a lack of physical activity can result in weight gain, decreased arterial flexibility, and overall poor cardiovascular health.

These factors, when addressed with a combination of herbal teas and lifestyle changes, have been shown to help manage high blood pressure.

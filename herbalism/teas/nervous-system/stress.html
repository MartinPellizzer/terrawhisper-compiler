The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile for stress.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to a temperature of 212 degrees Fahrenheit (100 degrees Celsius) for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Place one heaping teaspoon (about 2 grams) of dried chamomile flowers in a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to discard the chamomile flowers.
  5. Serve the chamomile tea hot, allowing it to cool slightly to avoid burning your tongue, and enjoy its calming effects.

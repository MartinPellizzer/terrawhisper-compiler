10 Best Herbal Teas For Cough

Herbal teas for cough are hot beverages made from dried flowers, leaves, seeds, roots, or fruits of various plants, specifically formulated to alleviate coughing.

These teas benefit individuals suffering from coughs by providing hydration, soothing irritation in the throat, and promoting the expulsion of mucus. By reducing inflammation and providing antioxidants, herbal teas for cough strengthen the immune system, allowing the body to recover more efficiently from respiratory ailments. Many people rely on these teas to manage their symptoms, improve their quality of life, and minimize the need for over-the-counter or prescription medications.

As natural alternatives, herbal teas for cough offer comfort and relief, making them an essential tool in managing respiratory health.

1. Thyme

Thyme tea helps with cough because it contains compounds with antispasmodic and expectorant properties.

The herb's active components, such as thymol and carvacrol, work together to soothe irritated throat tissues, reduce inflammation, and promote the expulsion of mucus from the respiratory tract.

This synergistic effect makes thyme tea an effective natural remedy for relieving both dry and productive coughs, providing quick comfort and helping to alleviate symptoms of respiratory ailments.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in thyme that aid with cough.

  • Expectorant: Fenugreek seeds can help loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract, making it easier to cough up phlegm. This property is important for cough because it can reduce congestion and inflammation in the airways. The expectorant effect of fenugreek is attributed to its mucilage content.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Fenugreek has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce irritation and swelling in the respiratory tract, which can alleviate cough symptoms. This property is important for cough because inflammation often triggers or worsens a cough. The anti-inflammatory action of fenugreek can be attributed to its saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids.
  • Antitussive: Fenugreek seeds have been shown to have antitussive properties, meaning they can help suppress coughing. This property is important for cough because excessive coughing can lead to throat irritation and discomfort. The antitussive effect of fenugreek is partly due to its mucilage and steroidal saponins.
  • Demulcent: Fenugreek seeds contain a substance called mucilage, which forms a soothing, protective coating on the mucous membranes of the respiratory tract. This property is important for cough because it can help reduce irritation and inflammation in the throat and airways, alleviating cough symptoms.
  • Antimicrobial: Fenugreek has antimicrobial properties, which can help the body combat bacterial or viral infections that may cause coughs. This property is important for cough because infections are a common cause of both acute and chronic coughs. The antimicrobial effect of fenugreek can be attributed to its alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins.
  • Antioxidant: Fenugreek seeds contain antioxidants that can help protect the body from oxidative stress and damage. This property is important for cough because oxidative stress can weaken the immune system and make the body more susceptible to infections. The antioxidant properties of fenugreek can be attributed to its polyphenols and vitamins, such as vitamin C and E.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in thyme that help with cough.

  • Leaves: Contain essential oils and flavonoids that have expectorant and antispasmodic properties, helping to loosen mucus and soothe coughs.
  • Flowers: Rich in flavonoids and volatile oils with antitussive and anti-inflammatory effects, alleviating irritation and reducing cough frequency.
  • Stems: Have similar properties to leaves, containing volatile oils and flavonoids that help relax bronchial spasms and relieve symptoms of cough and congestion.
  • Seeds: Contain thymol and other essential oils, offering antimicrobial and antitussive benefits, aiding in the reduction of both cough and infection.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic thyme for cough.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 2 teaspoons of fresh thyme leaves.
  2. Boil the water in a saucepan on high heat for 3-5 minutes.
  3. Place the thyme leaves in a teapot or infuser.
  4. Pour the boiling water over the thyme leaves and steep for 10 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup, discard the thyme leaves, and drink immediately.

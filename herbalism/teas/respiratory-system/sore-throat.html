1. Slippery elm

Slippery elm tea helps with sore throat because it creates a protective barrier over the mucous membranes in the throat, reducing irritation and inflammation.

This soothing effect is attributed to the tea's main active ingredient, mucilage, which becomes slippery and viscous when combined with water. By coating the throat, slippery elm tea alleviates pain, eases swallowing, and combats discomfort, promoting a faster recovery from sore throats.

Additionally, it has antibacterial and antiviral properties, which can help to fight off infections causing the soreness.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in slippery elm that aid with sore throat.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Cinnamon contains compounds such as cinnamaldehyde and eugenol that help reduce inflammation in the throat, providing relief from pain and swelling.
  • Antimicrobial: Cinnamon has natural antimicrobial properties, helping to fight off bacteria and viruses that can cause or worsen a sore throat.
  • Analgesic: The essential oils found in cinnamon, including cinnamaldehyde and linalool, have pain-relieving effects, helping to alleviate sore throat discomfort.
  • Immune-boosting: Cinnamon is rich in antioxidants and promotes a healthy immune response, which can help the body combat infections causing a sore throat.
  • Expectorant: Cinnamon helps to break up and expel mucus, making it easier to cough up phlegm and relieving congestion in the throat and chest.
  • Soothing: The warm nature of cinnamon can help soothe a sore throat by providing a comforting sensation and increasing blood flow to the affected area.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in slippery elm that help with sore throat.

  • Bark: Contains large amounts of mucilage, a substance that coats and soothes irritated mucous membranes, making it effective for treating sore throats.
  • Leaves: Rich in tannins, which have anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, helping alleviate sore throat discomfort.
  • Inner bark: Has demulcent properties, forming a protective film on mucous membranes, providing relief from throat irritation and inflammation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm for sore throat.

  1. Obtain 2 cups of water and bring it to a boil.
  2. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark, reducing heat to low.
  3. Allow the mixture to simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea, discarding the used bark.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, up to three times a day, to soothe a sore throat.

What are the most common causes of sore throat that are treatable with herbal teas?

The primary causes of sore throat that are treatable with herbal teas are inflammation and irritation.

These can result from viral infections, such as the common cold or flu, or from environmental factors like dry air or pollution. Sore throats can also be caused by bacterial infections, such as strep throat, although herbal teas are not typically a sufficient treatment for these cases and medical attention is recommended.

In general, herbal teas can provide relief by soothing inflamed throat tissue, reducing irritation, and promoting hydration and moisture in the throat.

Lycium Barbarum (Barbarian'S Wolfberry) Tea For Medicinal Use

Lycium barbarum tea, also known as Goji tea, is a medicinal beverage made from the fruit of the Lycium barbarum plant.

It contains various constituents, including carotenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. The tea has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including diabetes, high blood pressure, and certain types of cancer. The active compounds in Lycium barbarum tea have been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, which may help protect against oxidative stress and inflammation in the body.

However, consuming large amounts of Lycium barbarum tea may cause gastrointestinal side effects, such as nausea and diarrhea, due to its high content of phenolic acids.

Uses of Lycium barbarum tea

Lycium barbarum tea is commonly used to alleviate symptoms of anxiety and stress, promoting a sense of calmness and relaxation in individuals.

It's also used to boost the immune system, helping to prevent illnesses like the flu and other common infections. Additionally, it's a treatment for digestive issues such as constipation and diarrhea, regulating bowel movements and improving overall gut health. Some people use it to lower blood sugar levels and manage type 2 diabetes.

Furthermore, it's used as an antioxidant to help protect against cell damage and oxidative stress, potentially reducing the risk of chronic diseases like cancer and cardiovascular disease.

Benefits of Lycium barbarum tea

Lycium barbarum tea helps boost the immune system by increasing the production of antibodies, which are essential for fighting off infections and diseases.

This benefit is especially valuable for people who are prone to illnesses or have weakened immune systems, as it can help them recover faster and more effectively. Lycium barbarum tea also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce swelling and pain in the body, making it an excellent remedy for people suffering from arthritis, gout, or other inflammatory conditions. Additionally, this tea has been shown to improve eye health by reducing the risk of age-related macular degeneration and cataracts, making it a popular choice among older adults.

By reducing oxidative stress and improving overall health, Lycium barbarum tea can help people live healthier, more active lives.

Constituents of Lycium barbarum tea

Lycium barbarum tea contains a variety of bioactive constituents, but some of the most important for medicinal purposes include anthocyanins, which are powerful antioxidants that can neutralize free radicals and help protect against oxidative stress.

Flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have been found to possess anti-inflammatory properties, making them useful in treating conditions like arthritis and other inflammatory diseases. Polysaccharides, particularly beta-glucans, have been shown to stimulate the immune system and enhance its response to pathogens, making them a valuable component in the fight against infections. Catechins, particularly epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), have been found to exhibit anti-cancer properties by inhibiting the growth of cancer cells and inducing apoptosis.

Furthermore, Lycium barbarum tea also contains various amino acids, such as glutamic acid and aspartic acid, which can help to regulate blood pressure and improve cardiovascular health.

How to make Lycium barbarum tea

To make Lycium barbarum tea, also known as goji tea, start by gathering dried Lycium barbarum fruit.

Add 1-2 teaspoons of the dried fruit to a cup of boiling water. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes to allow the active compounds to infuse into the water. Strain the tea to remove the fruit particles and discard the solids.

The resulting liquid can be consumed as a tea, with the recommended daily intake being 200-300 milliliters.

Possible side effects Lycium barbarum tea

If used improperly, Lycium barbarum tea can lead to interactions with certain medications, including blood thinners, diabetes medications, and blood pressure medications.

Consuming high amounts of the tea may cause stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea due to its high anthocyanin and flavonoid content. People with allergies should be cautious when drinking Lycium barbarum tea, as some individuals may experience allergic reactions such as hives, itching, or difficulty breathing. Additionally, the tea may stimulate the uterus, making it unsuitable for pregnant women.

Furthermore, individuals with bleeding disorders or those undergoing surgery should avoid consuming Lycium barbarum tea due to its potential to interfere with blood clotting.

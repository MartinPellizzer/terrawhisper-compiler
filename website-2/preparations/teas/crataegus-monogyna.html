Crataegus Monogyna (May Tree) Tea For Medicinal Use

Crataegus monogyna tea, also known as hawthorn tea, has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries.

The tea is made from the leaves and flowers of the Crataegus monogyna tree and contains a variety of flavonoids, including quercetin, epicatechin, and procyanidins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties. These flavonoids have been shown to have a positive effect on cardiovascular health by lowering blood pressure and improving blood lipid profiles. The tea has also been used to help alleviate symptoms of anxiety and insomnia due to its sedative properties and ability to regulate the body's internal clock.

However, individuals with certain medical conditions, such as kidney or liver disease, should consult with their doctor before consuming Crataegus monogyna tea, as it may interact with medications or exacerbate existing conditions.

Uses of Crataegus monogyna tea

Crataegus monogyna tea is commonly used to lower cholesterol levels and improve cardiovascular health, which can help reduce the risk of heart disease and stroke.

It is also used to treat digestive issues such as diarrhea and stomach ulcers, promoting a healthy gut and relieving symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome. Additionally, the tea has anti-inflammatory properties, making it useful in managing joint pain and arthritis. People also drink Crataegus monogyna tea to boost their immune system and reduce the severity of colds and flu.

Furthermore, it is sometimes used to regulate blood sugar levels, making it a potential treatment option for those with type 2 diabetes.

Benefits of Crataegus monogyna tea

Crataegus monogyna tea helps to lower blood pressure in individuals with hypertension, which reduces the risk of heart disease, stroke, and kidney damage.

By doing so, it improves cardiovascular health and increases the chances of living a longer, healthier life. Additionally, this tea has been shown to have antioxidant properties, which help to protect cells from damage caused by free radicals, potentially slowing down the aging process and reducing the risk of certain types of cancer. It also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can alleviate symptoms of conditions such as arthritis, and improve overall immune function.

Furthermore, Crataegus monogyna tea has been found to have a positive effect on glucose metabolism, which can be beneficial for individuals with type 2 diabetes and those at risk of developing the condition.

Constituents of Crataegus monogyna tea

Crataegus monogyna tea contains flavonoids, particularly quercetin, kaempferol, and isorhapontigenin, which have been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Flavonoids like these have been found to have a protective effect on cardiovascular health by reducing inflammation and improving lipid profiles. The tea also contains oligomeric proanthocyanidin (OPC), a type of flavonoid that has been demonstrated to have antithrombotic and antioxidant activities. OPC has been found to help protect against cardiovascular diseases by preventing platelet aggregation and improving blood vessel function.

Additionally, the tea contains other bioactive compounds such as phenolic acids and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

How to make Crataegus monogyna tea

To make Crataegus monogyna tea for medicinal purposes, start by harvesting the fresh berries from the hawthorn tree in the summer months when they are ripe.

Dry the berries immediately to preserve their potency, either by spreading them out in a single layer on paper or by using a food dehydrator. Once the berries are completely dry, combine 1-2 teaspoons of the dried fruit with 1 cup of boiling water in a teapot. Allow the mixture to steep for 5-10 minutes, then strain the liquid into a cup.

The resulting tea can be consumed as needed, ideally on an empty stomach, as directed by a healthcare professional.

Possible side effects Crataegus monogyna tea

If used improperly, Crataegus monogyna tea consumption can lead to adverse health effects.

High doses of the tea have been linked to gastrointestinal issues, such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Some individuals may experience allergic reactions, including hives, itching, and swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat. Additionally, pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid consuming Crataegus monogyna tea due to its potential to interact with medications and exacerbate health conditions.

In severe cases, excessive consumption of the tea may lead to cardiovascular problems, including decreased blood pressure and arrhythmias.

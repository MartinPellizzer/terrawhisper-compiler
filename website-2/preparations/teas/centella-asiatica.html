Centella Asiatica (Indian Pennywort) Tea For Medicinal Use

Centella asiatica tea, also known as gotu kola tea, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat various health issues.

The tea is made from the leaves of the Centella asiatica plant, which contains a range of constituents, including triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and amino acids. These compounds have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making the tea a potential treatment for conditions such as anxiety, stress, and skin problems. Research has also found that Centella asiatica tea may help to improve cognitive function and memory, possibly due to its ability to increase blood flow to the brain.

However, drinking Centella asiatica tea in excess can cause side effects like nausea, dizziness, and stomach upset.

Uses of Centella asiatica tea

Centella asiatica are commonly used to reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of eczema, psoriasis, and other skin conditions, providing relief to people with sensitive skin.

It's also used to improve cognitive function and memory in individuals with age-related cognitive decline, helping them to stay focused and mentally sharp. Centella asiatica tea has been traditionally used to lower blood pressure and improve cardiovascular health, making it a popular choice for those looking to manage hypertension. Additionally, it's been used to reduce stress and anxiety, promoting relaxation and a sense of calm in people who struggle with mental health.

Furthermore, Centella asiatica tea has been employed to accelerate wound healing and improve circulation, making it beneficial for individuals with poor circulation or those who are recovering from injuries.

Benefits of Centella asiatica tea

Centella asiatica tea helps to improve blood circulation, which is beneficial for people with conditions such as peripheral artery disease, as it allows for a more efficient delivery of oxygen and nutrients to the extremities.

By promoting healthy circulation, Centella asiatica tea also aids in wound healing, reducing the risk of complications and promoting faster recovery times. Additionally, this tea has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to alleviate symptoms associated with conditions like arthritis, by reducing swelling and pain in the joints. Centella asiatica tea also has a calming effect on the nervous system, making it a useful tool for managing stress and anxiety.

Regular consumption of Centella asiatica tea has also been shown to improve cognitive function and memory, potentially making it a valuable resource for individuals looking to improve their mental clarity and focus.

Constituents of Centella asiatica tea

Centella asiatica tea contains a range of bioactive constituents that contribute to its medicinal properties.

The primary triterpenoid saponins present in Centella asiatica tea are brahmoside and brahminoside, as well as asiatic acid and madecassic acid. These compounds have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and neuroprotective properties. Additionally, the tea also contains flavonoids, particularly quercetin, kaempferol, and isorhapontigenin, which have been demonstrated to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities.

The combination of these bioactive compounds in Centella asiatica tea is thought to contribute to its potential therapeutic benefits for conditions such as cognitive decline and skin health.

How to make Centella asiatica tea

To make Centella asiatica tea, start by harvesting fresh leaves and stems of the plant, either from a trusted supplier or by cultivating it yourself.

Wash the harvested material thoroughly to remove any dirt or contaminants. Next, combine 1-2 tablespoons of the cleaned Centella asiatica with 1 cup of boiling water. Allow the mixture to steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the liquid to remove the solids.

The resulting tea can be consumed as a treatment for various health conditions, including inflammation and skin issues, by drinking it 2-3 times a day.

Possible side effects Centella asiatica tea

If used improperly, Centella asiatica tea can cause gastrointestinal upset, including nausea and diarrhea, due to its high saponin content.

This is because saponins can irritate the digestive tract and cause stomach discomfort. Additionally, excessive consumption of Centella asiatica tea may lead to an imbalance of potassium and other electrolytes in the body, resulting in heart palpitations and muscle weakness. Consuming high doses of the tea may also interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and decrease their effectiveness.

Furthermore, pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid Centella asiatica tea altogether due to the potential risk of birth defects and other complications.

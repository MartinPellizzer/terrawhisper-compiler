Matricaria Chamomilla (German Chamomile) Tea For Medicinal Use

Matricaria chamomilla, also known as German chamomile, is a popular herbal remedy used for its medicinal properties.

The tea made from its flowers is often consumed for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects, which can help soothe digestive issues, such as irritable bowel syndrome and stomach ulcers. Matricaria chamomilla tea also contains flavonoids, terpenoids, and apigenin, which are responsible for its antioxidant and sedative properties, making it a natural remedy for stress, anxiety, and insomnia. However, it is essential to note that consuming Matricaria chamomilla tea in large quantities or for extended periods can cause liver toxicity and interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and sedatives.

Therefore, individuals should consult with a healthcare professional before using Matricaria chamomilla tea for medicinal purposes, especially if they have a pre-existing medical condition or are taking any prescription medications.

Furthermore, Matricaria chamomilla tea is used to calm skin irritations and wounds, including eczema, acne, and minor burns, helping to speed up the healing process and promote healthy skin.

Constituents of Matricaria chamomilla tea

Matricaria chamomilla tea contains apigenin and luteolin, two flavonoid compounds that have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory properties.

Apigenin has also been found to exhibit antioxidant activity, which can help protect against cell damage and oxidative stress. Matricaria chamomilla tea also contains alpha-bisabolol, a sesquiterpene lactone that has been demonstrated to have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Chamazulene, a sesquiterpene derivative, has been found to possess anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which can help soothe skin irritations and wounds.

The tea's bioactive constituents, including apigenin and luteolin, have been shown to modulate the body's response to stress, making it a potential remedy for anxiety and other stress-related disorders.

How to make Matricaria chamomilla tea

To make Matricaria chamomilla tea, also known as chamomile tea, start by gathering high-quality dried flowers.

Use one teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers for every eight ounces of boiling water. Steep the flowers in the boiling water for five to seven minutes, or until the liquid has cooled to a temperature that is comfortable to drink. Strain the tea into a cup using a fine-mesh sieve or a piece of cheesecloth to remove the flowers, and discard the solids.

The resulting tea can be consumed up to three times a day to support relaxation and sleep.

Possible side effects Matricaria chamomilla tea

If used improperly, Matricaria chamomilla tea, a herbal remedy often consumed for its calming effects, can have adverse reactions in some individuals.

Consuming large quantities of the tea may lead to interactions with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and exacerbate conditions like bleeding disorders. Additionally, Matricaria chamomilla tea may not be suitable for pregnant or breastfeeding women due to its potential to stimulate the uterus and affect fetal development. Furthermore, individuals with allergies to ragweed, a plant in the same family as chamomile, may experience allergic reactions after consuming Matricaria chamomilla tea.

In rare cases, Matricaria chamomilla tea can also cause stomach upset and diarrhea in some people, particularly if consumed in excessive amounts or on an empty stomach.

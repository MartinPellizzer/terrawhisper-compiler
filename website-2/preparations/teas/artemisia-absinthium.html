Artemisia Absinthium (Wormwood) Tea For Medicinal Use

Artemisia absinthium tea, made from the leaves of the wormwood plant, has been used for centuries to treat various health issues.

Its medicinal properties come from the presence of thujone, a sesquiterpene ketone, and other compounds like flavonoids and phenolic acids. The tea has been traditionally used to treat digestive issues, such as bloating, gas, and stomach cramps, due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. It has also been used to treat fever, headaches, and respiratory problems, such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its expectorant properties.

However, high doses of the tea can cause side effects like headaches, nausea, and dizziness, possibly due to the presence of thujone, which can be toxic in large amounts.

Constituents of Artemisia absinthium tea

Artemisia absinthium tea contains a range of bioactive constituents that contribute to its medicinal properties.

The primary compounds responsible for its bioactivity include sesquiterpene lactones, such as absinthin, artabsin, and bornanol. These sesquiterpene lactones exhibit potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which may help protect against oxidative stress and inflammation-related diseases. Additionally, Artemisia absinthium tea contains flavonoids, like absinthin and isosalvialdehyde, which have been shown to possess antimicrobial and antispasmodic activities.

The tea's bioactive constituents also include volatile oils, particularly thujone and pinocamphone, which have been reported to have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

How to make Artemisia absinthium tea

To make Artemisia absinthium tea, also known as wormwood tea, start by harvesting the leaves and flowers of the Artemisia absinthium plant.

Dried or fresh leaves can be used to make the tea, but it is essential to note that only the leaves and flowers are safe for consumption, as the roots and seeds contain thujone, a compound that can be toxic in large amounts. To prepare the tea, steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried leaves or 2-3 teaspoons of fresh leaves in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-10 minutes. Strain the mixture and discard the solids, then serve the tea hot or let it cool to drink.

Possible side effects Artemisia absinthium tea

If used improperly, Artemisia absinthium tea, also known as wormwood tea, can cause significant health issues.

Consuming large quantities of the tea has been associated with liver damage, with some individuals experiencing an elevation in liver enzymes, a sign of liver strain. The thujone content in the tea is particularly concerning, as it can lead to seizures and other neurological problems if ingested in excessive amounts. Additionally, the tea can exacerbate existing conditions, such as epilepsy, and interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and medications used to treat anxiety and depression.

Long-term consumption of the tea has also been linked to the development of a condition known as absinthism, characterized by symptoms such as tremors, seizures, and hallucinations.

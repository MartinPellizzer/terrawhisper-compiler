Pelargonium Sidoides (South African Geranium) Tea For Medicinal Use

However, potential side effects of consuming Pelargonium sidoides tea may include stomach upset, allergic reactions, and interactions with other medications.

How to make Pelargonium sidoides tea

To make Pelargonium sidoides tea, start by measuring out one to two teaspoons of dried Pelargonium sidoides root per cup of boiling water.

Strain the tea after it has steeped to remove any remaining root particles. Possible side effects Pelargonium sidoides tea

If used improperly, Pelargonium sidoides tea can cause harm to certain individuals, particularly those with pre-existing conditions.

Drinking large quantities of the tea may lead to gastrointestinal problems, such as diarrhea and stomach cramps, due to its high concentration of tannins and other compounds. Allergic reactions are also possible, including skin rashes, itching, and difficulty breathing, in individuals who are sensitive to the plant's components. Pelargonium sidoides tea may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, causing adverse effects.

Pregnant women should be cautious when consuming the tea, as it may stimulate the uterus and induce contractions.

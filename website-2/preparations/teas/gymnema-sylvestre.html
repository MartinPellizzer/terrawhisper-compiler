Gymnema Sylvestre (Gurmar) Tea For Medicinal Use

Gymnema sylvestre tea has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, particularly in Ayurvedic practices, for its potential benefits in managing blood sugar levels and aiding in weight loss.

The tea contains constituents such as gymnemic acid and gymnemagenin, which have been shown to inhibit the absorption of glucose in the intestines. Studies have demonstrated that regular consumption of Gymnema sylvestre tea can reduce fasting blood sugar levels and improve insulin sensitivity in individuals with type 2 diabetes. However, some users may experience gastrointestinal side effects, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to the tea's ability to stimulate digestion and bowel movements.

Additionally, high doses of Gymnema sylvestre may cause hypoglycemia, a condition characterized by low blood sugar levels, so it's essential to consume the tea under medical supervision.

Gymnema sylvestre are commonly used to manage blood sugar levels and improve insulin sensitivity in people with type 2 diabetes.

Additionally, Gymnema sylvestre tea has been used to help lower cholesterol levels and triglycerides in individuals with high lipid profiles. Some people also use it to support liver health and reduce symptoms of liver disease, such as jaundice and fatigue.

Furthermore, Gymnema sylvestre is used to treat skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis, as well as to reduce inflammation and improve wound healing.

Constituents of Gymnema sylvestre tea

Gymnema sylvestre tea contains several bioactive constituents that contribute to its medicinal properties.

The leaves of the plant are rich in gymnemic acid, a compound that has been shown to inhibit the absorption of glucose in the gut, thereby reducing blood sugar levels. Additionally, the tea contains gymnenin, a compound that has been found to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The tea also contains flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, which have been demonstrated to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

These compounds work together to provide potential therapeutic benefits, including the management of blood sugar levels and the reduction of inflammation.

How to make Gymnema sylvestre tea

To make Gymnema sylvestre tea for medicinal purposes, start by purchasing dried Gymnema sylvestre leaves from a reputable herbal supplier.

Use one teaspoon of the dried leaves per cup of boiling water. Steep the leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes, allowing the leaves to infuse their active compounds into the water. Strain the tea into a cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove the leaves, and discard the solids.

The resulting tea can be consumed hot or cold, but it's essential to note that the bioavailability and efficacy of the compounds may be affected by factors such as brewing time and temperature.

Possible side effects Gymnema sylvestre tea

If used improperly, Gymnema sylvestre tea can cause stomach upset, including nausea and vomiting, due to its ability to stimulate digestive enzymes and increase the contraction of the digestive tract.

It has been reported that taking high doses of Gymnema sylvestre tea can lead to low blood sugar levels, particularly in individuals with diabetes or those who have recently eaten. Some people experience allergic reactions to Gymnema sylvestre tea, resulting in symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing. Additionally, pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid using Gymnema sylvestre tea due to a lack of research on its safety during these periods.

High doses of Gymnema sylvestre tea may also interact with certain medications, including blood thinners, diabetes medications, and thyroid medications.

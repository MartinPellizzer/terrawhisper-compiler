How to make Aesculus hippocastanum tea

To make Aesculus hippocastanum tea, also known as horse chestnut tea, you will need dried horse chestnut flowers or leaves, which can be obtained from a reputable herbal supplier.

Start by measuring out one teaspoon of dried herb per cup of boiling water. Pour the boiling water over the herb and let it steep for 5-7 minutes to allow the active compounds to infuse into the water. After the steeping time has passed, strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the solids, and discard the herb.

The resulting tea can be consumed 2-3 times a day, with each serving typically being 1 cup.

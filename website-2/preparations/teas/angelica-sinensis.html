Angelica Sinensis (Chinese Angelica) Tea For Medicinal Use

Angelica sinensis tea, also known as dong quai, is a traditional Chinese medicinal herb used to treat various health conditions, including menstrual disorders, menopausal symptoms, and respiratory problems.

The tea is rich in constituents such as ferulic acid, ligustilide, and butylphthalide, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and vasodilatory properties. The benefits of drinking Angelica sinensis tea include reducing menstrual cramp pain, alleviating menopausal hot flashes, and improving blood circulation. However, some people may experience side effects such as allergic reactions, stomach upset, or interactions with blood-thinning medications.

As with any herbal remedy, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using Angelica sinensis tea, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or are taking medications.

Uses of Angelica sinensis tea

Angelica sinensis tea is commonly used to alleviate menstrual cramps and other symptoms associated with premenstrual syndrome (PMS) in women.

It is also used to help reduce fever and alleviate symptoms of the common cold and flu. Additionally, Angelica sinensis tea is used to support cardiovascular health by lowering blood pressure and cholesterol levels. Furthermore, it is used to stimulate digestion and alleviate symptoms of indigestion and diarrhea.

It is also used to help reduce stress and anxiety, promoting a restful night's sleep.

Benefits of Angelica sinensis tea

Angelica sinensis tea helps to alleviate menstrual cramps and other symptoms associated with PMS in women by reducing inflammation and relaxing the uterine muscles.

This tea also supports the immune system by increasing the production of white blood cells, helping the body to fight off infections and diseases. Regular consumption of Angelica sinensis tea has been shown to improve cardiovascular health by lowering blood pressure and cholesterol levels, reducing the risk of heart disease. Additionally, this tea has been found to have antioxidant properties, which can help to protect cells from damage caused by free radicals and reduce the risk of certain cancers.

By promoting a healthy digestive system and reducing inflammation, Angelica sinensis tea can also help to alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome and other gastrointestinal disorders.

Constituents of Angelica sinensis tea

Angelica sinensis tea contains a rich mixture of bioactive compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.

The main constituents responsible for its therapeutic effects are ferulic acid, ligustilide, and senkyunolide A, which have been extensively studied. Ferulic acid, a phenolic acid, exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, while ligustilide and senkyunolide A have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective activities. The combination of these compounds in Angelica sinensis tea may also contribute to its cardiovascular benefits and ability to alleviate symptoms of menopause.

Additionally, the tea's bioactive constituents have been found to have anti-cancer properties, inhibiting the growth of cancer cells and inducing apoptosis.

How to make Angelica sinensis tea

To make Angelica sinensis tea, start by gathering dried root of the plant, also known as dong quai or female ginseng.

Use one teaspoon of the dried root for every eight ounces of water. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer and add the dried root. Allow the mixture to steep for 5-10 minutes, or until the desired strength is reached.

Strain the tea and discard the solids, then serve hot or let it cool for a cold infusion.

Possible side effects Angelica sinensis tea

If used improperly, Angelica sinensis tea can cause adverse effects due to its high levels of coumarin, a compound that can interfere with blood clotting.

Consuming large amounts of this tea may lead to an increased risk of bleeding or bruising. Additionally, the tea's high concentration of volatile oils can cause stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea in some individuals. Furthermore, people with bleeding disorders or those taking anticoagulant medications should exercise extreme caution when consuming Angelica sinensis tea, as it may exacerbate their condition.

In rare cases, excessive consumption of the tea may also lead to kidney or liver damage due to its toxic compounds.

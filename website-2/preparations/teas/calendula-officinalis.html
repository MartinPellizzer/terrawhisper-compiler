Calendula Officinalis (Pot Marigold) Tea For Medicinal Use

Calendula officinalis tea, made from the flowers of this plant, has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries.

The tea contains flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, as well as triterpenoid saponins and carotenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Calendula officinalis tea has been traditionally used to treat various skin conditions, including wounds, eczema, and acne, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help to soothe and heal the skin. The tea has also been used to treat digestive issues, such as diarrhea and ulcers, due to its ability to reduce inflammation and promote healing in the digestive tract.

However, drinking Calendula officinalis tea can cause gastrointestinal upset, including nausea and stomach cramps, in some individuals, particularly when consumed in large quantities or by people with pre-existing digestive issues.

Uses of Calendula officinalis tea

Calendula officinalis are commonly used to treat wound healing, skin irritations, and inflammation.

This includes conditions like eczema, acne, and rosacea, which can cause redness and itching on the skin. Calendula tea is also used to soothe mouth ulcers and gum inflammation, making it a popular remedy for people with dental issues. Additionally, it's used to treat minor burns and cuts, helping to speed up the healing process and reduce the risk of infection.

Calendula tea is also sometimes used to help with menstrual cramps and other symptoms associated with PMS.

Benefits of Calendula officinalis tea

Calendula officinalis tea helps reduce inflammation and promote wound healing, making it a valuable remedy for people with skin conditions such as eczema and acne.

Its antimicrobial properties also help combat infections and prevent the spread of bacteria, which is particularly beneficial for people with open wounds or compromised immune systems. Calendula officinalis tea has been shown to accelerate the healing process by increasing blood flow to affected areas and reducing scarring. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory properties can help alleviate symptoms of conditions such as arthritis and other inflammatory diseases.

By promoting tissue repair and reducing inflammation, Calendula officinalis tea can help people recover from injuries and manage chronic conditions more effectively.

Constituents of Calendula officinalis tea

Calendula officinalis tea contains triterpenoid saponins, including oleanolic acid and ursolic acid, which have been found to possess anti-inflammatory properties.

The tea also contains flavonoids such as quercetin and kaempferol, which are known antioxidants that can neutralize free radicals and protect against cell damage. Additionally, Calendula officinalis tea contains carotenoids like beta-carotene and lutein, which are responsible for its bright yellow color and have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. The tea's sesquiterpene lactones, particularly calendulin and calendulic acid, have been found to possess antimicrobial properties, making it effective against certain microorganisms.

These bioactive constituents contribute to the medicinal properties of Calendula officinalis tea, particularly its ability to soothe skin irritations and promote wound healing.

How to make Calendula officinalis tea

To make Calendula officinalis tea, start by harvesting the flowers of the marigold plant, which contain flavonoids and triterpene saponins, the active compounds responsible for its medicinal properties.

Rinse the flowers with clean water to remove any dirt or debris. Combine 1 tablespoon of dried Calendula flowers with 1 cup of boiling water in a heat-resistant cup or teapot. Allow the mixture to steep for 5-10 minutes, then strain the liquid through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a separate container.

The resulting tea can be consumed up to three times a day, although it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional for proper dosage and usage guidelines.

Possible side effects Calendula officinalis tea

If used improperly, Calendula officinalis tea can cause allergic reactions in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

The tea may interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and diabetes medications, potentially exacerbating their effects. High doses of Calendula officinalis tea may cause gastrointestinal upset, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid consuming Calendula officinalis tea due to a lack of available research on its safety during these times.

Excessive consumption of the tea may also lead to an increased risk of bleeding, particularly in individuals with bleeding disorders or those taking anticoagulant medications.

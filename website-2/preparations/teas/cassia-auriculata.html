Cassia Auriculata (Golden Shower) Tea For Medicinal Use

Cassia auriculata tea has been used for medicinal purposes in traditional medicine, particularly in India and Sri Lanka.

The tea is rich in flavonoids, anthraquinones, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, digestive issues, and skin problems. Cassia auriculata tea has been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antioxidant activities, which may help to soothe digestive issues and reduce inflammation.

However, excessive consumption of Cassia auriculata tea may cause gastrointestinal irritation, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Uses of Cassia auriculata tea

Cassia auriculata tea is commonly used to treat a range of health issues, including high blood sugar levels in people with diabetes, lowering cholesterol and triglyceride levels, and managing hypertension.

It's also used to alleviate symptoms of anxiety and insomnia, promoting relaxation and a better night's sleep. Additionally, the tea is valued for its antimicrobial properties, helping to combat infections and wounds. Cassia auriculata tea has been traditionally used to treat digestive issues such as constipation and diarrhea, providing relief from gastrointestinal discomfort.

Furthermore, it's used to boost the immune system, supporting the body's natural defense against illness and disease.

Benefits of Cassia auriculata tea

Cassia auriculata tea helps to reduce blood sugar levels in individuals with diabetes, which can lead to a decrease in the risk of complications such as kidney damage and blindness.

It also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to alleviate symptoms of conditions like arthritis, by reducing pain and swelling. Additionally, Cassia auriculata tea has been shown to have antibacterial properties, making it effective in treating infections such as gum disease and respiratory tract infections. Furthermore, it has been found to have antioxidant properties, which can help to protect against cell damage caused by free radicals, potentially reducing the risk of certain cancers and neurodegenerative diseases.

By reducing inflammation and promoting overall health, Cassia auriculata tea can contribute to a reduced risk of chronic diseases and improved quality of life.

Constituents of Cassia auriculata tea

Cassia auriculata tea contains several bioactive constituents that contribute to its medicinal properties.

The tea is rich in flavonoids, including kaempferol and quercetin, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Flavonoids have also been found to exhibit antimicrobial activity, making Cassia auriculata tea a potential treatment for various infections. Additionally, the tea contains alkaloids, such as auriculatin, which have been demonstrated to have analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds work together to make Cassia auriculata tea a valuable resource for addressing various health concerns.

How to make Cassia auriculata tea

To make Cassia auriculata tea for medicinal purposes, first harvest the leaves and flowers of the plant, which contain a high concentration of flavonoids and saponins.

Dry the harvested leaves and flowers thoroughly to prevent spoilage, either air-drying or using a low-temperature oven. Once dry, grind the leaves and flowers into a fine powder. Combine 1 teaspoon of the powder with 1 cup of boiling water, then let it steep for 5-10 minutes.

Strain the liquid and discard the solids, and the resulting tea can be consumed for its flavonoid and saponin content.

Possible side effects Cassia auriculata tea

If used improperly, Cassia auriculata tea, also known as Kokilaksha, can be toxic and cause harm.

The tea contains a compound called anthraquinone, which in excessive amounts, can lead to gastrointestinal problems such as diarrhea, nausea, and stomach cramps. In severe cases, excessive consumption of Cassia auriculata tea can cause kidney damage and electrolyte imbalance due to the loss of essential minerals. Additionally, the tea may interact with certain medications, such as diuretics and blood thinners, leading to adverse effects.

Excessive consumption of Cassia auriculata tea can also exacerbate underlying conditions like kidney disease, heart conditions, and diabetes.

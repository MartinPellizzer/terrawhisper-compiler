Boswellia Serrata (Indian Frankincense) Tea For Medicinal Use

Boswellia serrata tea, made from the resin of the Boswellia serrata tree, contains constituents such as boswellic acids, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory properties.

The tea has been traditionally used to treat various conditions, including arthritis, asthma, and other inflammatory diseases. Studies have demonstrated that Boswellia serrata tea can help reduce joint pain and inflammation by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. Additionally, the tea has been used to alleviate gastrointestinal issues, such as ulcers and irritable bowel syndrome, due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

However, potential side effects of consuming Boswellia serrata tea include gastrointestinal upset, diarrhea, and interactions with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications.

Constituents of Boswellia serrata tea

Boswellia serrata tea contains a significant amount of boswellic acids, which are its primary bioactive constituents.

These acids, particularly acetyl-11-keto-beta-boswelllic acid (AKBA), have been shown to exhibit potent anti-inflammatory properties by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. Additionally, boswellic acids have been found to possess antioxidant properties, which help protect the body against oxidative stress and cell damage. The combination of these bioactive constituents in Boswellia serrata tea makes it a valuable natural remedy for various health conditions, including arthritis and inflammatory diseases.

How to make Boswellia serrata tea

To make Boswellia serrata tea, start by purchasing high-quality resin from the Boswellia serrata tree, which is sustainably sourced and free of contaminants.

The resin is typically sold in small pellets or beads and should be stored in an airtight container to preserve its potency. To prepare the tea, combine one gram of the resin with 250 milliliters of water in a saucepan and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let it steep for 5-10 minutes, allowing the resin to infuse into the water.

Once the infusion is complete, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve to remove the resin particles, and discard the solids.

Possible side effects Boswellia serrata tea

If used improperly, Boswellia serrata tea can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, due to its effect on blood sugar and blood clotting levels.

This may lead to increased risk of bleeding complications or hypoglycemia in individuals taking these medications. Boswellia serrata tea may also exacerbate conditions such as high blood pressure, as it can lower blood pressure in some individuals. Additionally, consuming high amounts of Boswellia serrata tea may cause gastrointestinal side effects like nausea, diarrhea, and stomach upset in some people.

Furthermore, Boswellia serrata tea may not be suitable for pregnant or breastfeeding women, as there is limited research on its effects on fetal development and infant health.

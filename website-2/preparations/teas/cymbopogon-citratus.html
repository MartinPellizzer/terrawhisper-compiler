Cymbopogon Citratus (Lemongrass) Tea For Medicinal Use

Cymbopogon citratus, also known as lemongrass, has been used for centuries as a natural remedy for various health issues.

The essential oil extracted from its leaves is a key constituent of lemongrass tea, which contains compounds like citral, geraniol, and limonene. Studies have shown that lemongrass tea exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it effective in treating digestive issues, such as diarrhea and nausea, and relieving symptoms of colds and flu. The tea has also been traditionally used to reduce fever and alleviate headaches.

However, excessive consumption of lemongrass tea can cause stomach upset, nausea, and allergic reactions in some individuals, particularly those with sensitive stomachs or allergies to citrus plants.

Constituents of Cymbopogon citratus tea

Cymbopogon citratus tea contains a number of bioactive constituents that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Limonene and geraniol are the primary volatile compounds found in the tea, responsible for its characteristic citrus scent and flavor. The tea also contains flavonoids like naringenin and kaempferol, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Additionally, Cymbopogon citratus tea contains sesquiterpenes such as citronellal, which have been found to exhibit antimicrobial and antifungal activities.

These compounds work together to make Cymbopogon citratus tea a valuable natural remedy for a variety of health conditions.

How to make Cymbopogon citratus tea

To make Cymbopogon citratus tea, also known as lemongrass tea, start by harvesting fresh lemongrass stalks and washing them thoroughly to remove any dirt or debris.

Cut the stalks into 1-inch pieces to release their oils, then combine 1 tablespoon of the chopped lemongrass with 8 ounces of boiling water in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes to allow the oils to infuse into the water. Strain the tea to remove the lemongrass pieces and discard.

To consume, drink the tea hot or iced, with or without added honey or lemon to taste.

Possible side effects Cymbopogon citratus tea

If used improperly, Cymbopogon citratus tea, also known as lemongrass tea, can cause several adverse reactions in some individuals.

High doses of the tea have been linked to allergic reactions, such as hives and itching, in people who are sensitive to its active compounds. Consuming excessive amounts of lemongrass tea may also lead to stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea due to its high citral content. Furthermore, Cymbopogon citratus tea can interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and diabetes medications, when consumed in large quantities.

In rare cases, excessive consumption of lemongrass tea may cause liver damage, particularly when combined with other substances that also affect liver function.

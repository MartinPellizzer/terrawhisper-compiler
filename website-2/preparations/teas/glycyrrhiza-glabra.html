Glycyrrhiza Glabra (Licorice) Tea For Medicinal Use

Glycyrrhiza glabra tea, made from the roots of the licorice plant, has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries.

The tea contains a range of constituents, including glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties. One of the primary uses of Glycyrrhiza glabra tea is as an anti-inflammatory agent, helping to soothe digestive issues such as heartburn, bloating, and stomach ulcers. Additionally, the tea has been used to treat respiratory problems like bronchitis and coughs due to its expectorant properties, which help to loosen and clear mucus from the lungs.

However, excessive consumption of Glycyrrhiza glabra tea can lead to side effects such as high blood pressure and low potassium levels, so moderation is advised.

Constituents of Glycyrrhiza glabra tea

Glycyrrhiza glabra tea contains a range of bioactive constituents that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin, is one of the primary components, known for its anti-inflammatory and immunosuppressive effects. Flavonoids such as liquiritin and isoliquiritin, along with phenolic acids like caffeic acid and ferulic acid, also play significant roles in the tea's medicinal profile. These compounds have been shown to exhibit antioxidant, antimicrobial, and hepatoprotective properties.

Additionally, the tea's bioactive constituents have been found to modulate the body's stress response, exerting a mild adaptogenic effect.

How to make Glycyrrhiza glabra tea

To make Glycyrrhiza glabra tea, also known as licorice root tea, start by gathering 1-2 tablespoons of dried licorice root and place it in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.

Next, boil 8-10 ounces of water and pour it over the licorice root. Allow the mixture to steep for 5-7 minutes, or until the liquid has cooled slightly. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the licorice root.

The resulting tea can be consumed up to 3 times a day, with a recommended dosage of 1/4 to 1/2 teaspoon of dried licorice root per 8 ounces of water.

Possible side effects Glycyrrhiza glabra tea

If used improperly, Glycyrrhiza glabra tea can cause a range of side effects due to its high glycyrrhizin content.

Excessive consumption of the tea has been linked to an increase in blood pressure in people with pre-existing hypertension. Long-term use of glycyrrhiza tea has also been associated with low levels of potassium in the body, which can lead to muscle weakness and heart arrhythmias. Furthermore, glycyrrhizin can interact with certain medications, including diuretics, and reduce their effectiveness or increase the risk of adverse effects.

Individuals with kidney disease or taking medications that affect electrolyte balance should consult with their healthcare provider before consuming glycyrrhiza tea.

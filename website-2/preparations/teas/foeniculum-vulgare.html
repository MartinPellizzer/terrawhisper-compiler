Foeniculum Vulgare (Fennel) Tea For Medicinal Use

Foeniculum vulgare, commonly known as fennel, has been used for centuries as a medicinal herb.

Its tea is made from the dried seeds of the plant and is traditionally used to aid digestion and relieve symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and gas. The tea's active constituents, including anethole, fenchone, and limonene, have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which contribute to its medicinal benefits. Fennel tea has also been used to soothe menstrual cramps and other symptoms associated with PMS, as well as to calm coughs and reduce fever.

However, consuming large amounts of fennel tea can cause side effects such as stomach upset, diarrhea, and allergic reactions in some individuals.

How to make Foeniculum vulgare tea

To make Foeniculum vulgare tea, also known as anise tea, start by gathering 1-2 teaspoons of dried Foeniculum vulgare seeds or roots, which contain the active compounds responsible for its medicinal properties.

If used improperly, Foeniculum vulgare tea can be quite problematic.

Excessive consumption has also been linked to interactions with certain medications, including blood thinners, and may lead to an increased risk of bleeding. Pregnant women should avoid drinking Foeniculum vulgare tea, as it may stimulate the uterus and cause contractions.

In addition, individuals with allergies to plants in the carrot family should steer clear of this tea, as they may experience an allergic reaction.

Mucuna Pruriens (Cowhage) Tea For Medicinal Use

Mucuna pruriens tea is a traditional herbal remedy derived from the seeds of the Mucuna pruriens plant, which is native to tropical Africa and Asia.

The tea is rich in bioactive compounds, including L-DOPA, a precursor to dopamine, as well as other neurotransmitter precursors and flavonoids. Mucuna pruriens tea has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of Parkinson's disease, as well as to enhance cognitive function, libido, and mood. Studies have shown that the L-DOPA content in Mucuna pruriens tea can cross the blood-brain barrier, increasing dopamine levels and providing relief from Parkinson's symptoms.

However, high doses of L-DOPA can cause side effects such as nausea, vomiting, and dizziness, and individuals with certain medical conditions, including kidney or liver disease, should consult a healthcare professional before using Mucuna pruriens tea.

Mucuna pruriens tea contains a wealth of bioactive constituents that contribute to its medicinal properties.

How to make Mucuna pruriens tea

To make Mucuna pruriens tea, start by sourcing high-quality seeds of the Mucuna pruriens plant, which is native to tropical Africa and Asia.

Once you have the seeds, dry them thoroughly to remove any moisture, as this will help preserve the active compounds. Next, grind the dried seeds into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle. To make the tea, steep one gram of the Mucuna pruriens powder in 250 milliliters of boiling water for 10-15 minutes, then strain the mixture to remove any solids.

The resulting liquid can be consumed as a tea, and it's recommended to drink it 1-3 times a day for medicinal purposes, such as supporting mood and cognitive function.

Possible side effects Mucuna pruriens tea

If used improperly, Mucuna pruriens tea, a supplement derived from the seed of the Mucuna pruriens plant, may cause adverse reactions in some individuals.

Consuming large amounts of the tea can lead to increased heart rate and blood pressure, which can be problematic for people with pre-existing cardiovascular conditions. Additionally, Mucuna pruriens tea contains a compound called tyramine, which can interact with certain medications, such as MAO inhibitors, and cause severe side effects. Furthermore, individuals with Parkinson's disease may experience a rapid drop in blood pressure when consuming the tea, which can lead to dizziness and fainting.

High levels of the tea's active compound, L-DOPA, can also cause gastrointestinal upset, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

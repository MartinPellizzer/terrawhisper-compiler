How to make Vaccinium myrtillus tea

To make Vaccinium myrtillus tea, also known as bilberry tea, you'll need fresh or dried bilberries, water, and a tea infuser or strainer.

Start by measuring out one teaspoon of dried bilberries or one tablespoon of fresh bilberries for every 8 ounces of water. Next, heat the water in a pot until it reaches a boil, then reduce the heat and add the bilberries. Allow the mixture to steep for 5-7 minutes, depending on your desired level of extraction.

Finally, strain the liquid into a cup and discard the solids, taking care to enjoy the tea as soon as possible for optimal flavor and medicinal properties.

Possible side effects Vaccinium myrtillus tea

If used improperly, Vaccinium myrtillus tea, also known as bilberry tea, may cause interactions with certain medications.

High doses of anthocyanins, the active compounds found in Vaccinium myrtillus, can exacerbate bleeding conditions due to their anticoagulant properties. People with diabetes should be cautious when consuming Vaccinium myrtillus tea, as it may lower blood sugar levels. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid consuming Vaccinium myrtillus tea, as its effects on the fetus or baby are unknown.

Additionally, individuals taking anticoagulant medications should consult their doctor before using Vaccinium myrtillus tea as it may increase the risk of bleeding.

Pygeum Africanum (Pygeum) Tea For Medicinal Use

Studies have demonstrated that regular consumption of Pygeum africanum tea can lead to a reduction in urinary frequency and urgency, as well as an improvement in overall flow rate. Additionally, the tea has been found to exhibit antioxidant properties, which may contribute to its potential benefits for prostate health.

However, some individuals may experience side effects such as stomach upset, diarrhea, or headaches when consuming Pygeum africanum tea, particularly at high doses or in combination with other medications.

How to make Pygeum africanum tea

To make Pygeum africanum tea, start by measuring out one teaspoon of dried Pygeum africanum bark per cup of boiling water.

Remove from heat and let it steep for 5-7 minutes, allowing the active compounds to infuse into the water. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the solids. Possible side effects Pygeum africanum tea

If used improperly, Pygeum africanum tea, a supplement derived from the African cherry tree, can have adverse effects.

Taking high doses of Pygeum africanum tea for extended periods has been associated with gastrointestinal problems, including nausea and diarrhea. Interactions with medications, such as blood thinners, can also occur due to the tea's ability to affect blood pressure and heart rate. Additionally, Pygeum africanum tea may exacerbate conditions like high blood pressure, heart disease, and kidney disease in individuals with pre-existing conditions.

Furthermore, pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid using Pygeum africanum tea due to the lack of research on its safety during these periods.

How to make Piper nigrum tea

To make Piper nigrum tea, also known as black pepper tea, you'll need 1 teaspoon of whole black peppercorns for every 8 ounces of water.

First, add the peppercorns to a teapot or infuser, then pour in the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes. After the steeping time has passed, strain the tea into a cup to remove the peppercorns. To drink, add a squeeze of fresh lemon juice and a sweetener of your choice, if desired.

Possible side effects Piper nigrum tea

If used improperly, Piper nigrum tea, which is made from the dried fruit of the black pepper plant, can cause stomach upset and nausea due to its high concentration of piperine.

Consuming large amounts of the tea may lead to diarrhea and stomach cramps in some individuals. Additionally, piperine can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and medications for high blood pressure, which may exacerbate their side effects or reduce their effectiveness. People with allergies to black pepper or other spices in the Piper nigrum plant may also experience allergic reactions, such as hives or itching, after consuming the tea.

Furthermore, piperine can also cause liver damage in rare cases, particularly when consumed in extremely high doses.

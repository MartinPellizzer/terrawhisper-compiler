Cynara Scolymus (Globe Artichoke) Tea For Medicinal Use

Cynara scolymus, also known as artichoke tea, has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The tea is made from the leaves of the artichoke plant and contains constituents such as cynarin, luteolin, and silymarin. It has been traditionally used to aid in digestion, reduce inflammation, and lower cholesterol levels. Research has shown that artichoke tea may help to improve liver function and reduce symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome.

Side effects of drinking artichoke tea can include stomach upset, diarrhea, and allergic reactions in some individuals.

Uses of Cynara scolymus tea

Cynara scolymus are commonly used to treat conditions such as high cholesterol and triglycerides, reducing the risk of heart disease and stroke.

The tea is also used to lower blood pressure and improve blood sugar control, making it a popular choice for managing type 2 diabetes. Additionally, Cynara scolymus tea is used to aid in weight loss and improve digestion, helping to reduce symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). It's also used to treat arthritis, reducing inflammation and joint pain.

Furthermore, the tea is used to improve liver function and protect against liver damage, supporting overall liver health.

Benefits of Cynara scolymus tea

Cynara scolymus tea helps to lower cholesterol levels by increasing the excretion of bile acids, which in turn helps to reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease.

This is beneficial for individuals who are at risk of heart attacks, strokes, and other cardiovascular-related problems. By reducing the levels of LDL (bad) cholesterol, Cynara scolymus tea also helps to lower blood pressure, which can alleviate symptoms of hypertension and prevent long-term damage to the heart and blood vessels. Additionally, the tea has been shown to have antioxidant properties, which can help to protect cells from damage caused by free radicals and reduce the risk of certain cancers.

Regular consumption of Cynara scolymus tea may also help to regulate blood sugar levels, making it a beneficial beverage for individuals with diabetes or those at risk of developing the condition.

Constituents of Cynara scolymus tea

Cynara scolymus tea contains a wealth of bioactive constituents that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Chlorogenic acid, a key polyphenol found in this tea, has been shown to possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, which can help protect against cell damage and inflammation. The tea also contains cynarin, a sesquiterpene lactone that has been demonstrated to stimulate the production of bile and improve liver function. Additionally, Cynara scolymus tea is a rich source of flavonoids, including quercetin and luteolin, which have been found to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities.

The tea's bioactive constituents work together to provide a range of health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, as well as potential improvements in liver function and digestive health.

How to make Cynara scolymus tea

To make Cynara scolymus tea, also known as artichoke tea, you'll need dried Cynara scolymus leaves or flowers.

Start by steeping one to two teaspoons of the dried herb in one cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes. The ideal temperature for steeping is between 95°C and 100°C. After the steeping time has passed, strain the liquid using a tea strainer or cheesecloth to remove the solids.

The resulting tea can be consumed warm or cooled to a comfortable temperature, typically serving as a digestive aid and liver stimulant.

Possible side effects Cynara scolymus tea

If used improperly, Cynara scolymus tea, also known as artichoke tea, can cause liver damage due to its high concentration of cynarin, a compound that can stimulate liver enzymes and increase the risk of liver toxicity.

Drinking large quantities of artichoke tea can also lead to gastrointestinal problems, such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high saponin content. Excessive consumption of artichoke tea can cause allergic reactions in some individuals, manifesting as skin rashes, itching, and difficulty breathing. Interacting with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, can exacerbate the negative effects of artichoke tea.

Pregnant women should avoid consuming artichoke tea, as high doses have been shown to stimulate uterine contractions and potentially induce miscarriage.

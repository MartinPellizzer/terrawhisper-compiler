Schisandra Chinensis (Wu Wei Zi) Tea For Medicinal Use

Schisandra chinensis tea, also known as wu wei zi tea, is a traditional Chinese medicinal brew made from the fruit of the Schisandra chinensis plant.

The tea has been used for centuries to treat various health conditions, including digestive issues, respiratory problems, and fever. The main constituents of Schisandra chinensis tea include schisandrins, schisandrins A and B, and lignans, which are responsible for its medicinal properties. These constituents have been shown to have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulatory effects, contributing to the tea's benefits in reducing stress and promoting overall health.

However, high doses of Schisandra chinensis tea may cause side effects such as stomach upset, diarrhea, and allergic reactions in some individuals.

Uses of Schisandra chinensis tea

Schisandra chinensis are commonly used to promote longevity and improve overall well-being, particularly among older adults.

They are also used to address cognitive impairment, memory loss, and age-related diseases such as Alzheimer's and dementia. Schisandra chinensis tea is sometimes used to treat cardiovascular problems, including high blood pressure, atherosclerosis, and coronary heart disease. Additionally, it is used to support liver health and alleviate symptoms of cirrhosis and hepatitis.

In some cases, Schisandra chinensis is used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Benefits of Schisandra chinensis tea

Schisandra chinensis tea helps to boost the immune system, making it easier for the body to fight off infections and diseases.

This is especially beneficial for people with weakened immune systems, such as the elderly or those with chronic illnesses. The antioxidants present in Schisandra chinensis tea help to protect the body from oxidative stress, reducing the risk of cell damage and inflammation. Regular consumption of Schisandra chinensis tea has also been shown to improve cognitive function and memory, potentially reducing the risk of age-related cognitive decline.

By reducing inflammation and improving overall health, Schisandra chinensis tea can help to promote a longer and healthier life.

Constituents of Schisandra chinensis tea

Schisandra chinensis tea contains a unique combination of bioactive constituents, primarily schisandrins, schisandrin B, schisandrin C, and schisantherin A.

These lignans have been identified as the primary compounds responsible for the medicinal properties of the tea. Specifically, schisandrins have been shown to exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and free radical-scavenging activities. Additionally, the tea's bioactive constituents have been found to possess adaptogenic properties, helping to regulate stress responses and promote overall health.

Studies have also isolated schisandrin B, schisandrin C, and schisantherin A as key contributors to the tea's anti-cancer and anti-aging effects.

How to make Schisandra chinensis tea

To make Schisandra chinensis tea, start by gathering 5-10 grams of dried Schisandra chinensis berries, which are typically harvested in the fall.

Rinse the berries gently with water to remove any impurities. Then, combine the berries with 1 liter of water in a saucepan and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to a simmer and let it cook for 30-40 minutes, or until the liquid has reduced to about 1/2 liter.

Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the berries and discard the solids.

Possible side effects Schisandra chinensis tea

If used improperly, Schisandra chinensis tea can cause adverse interactions with certain medications, particularly blood thinners and diabetes medications, due to its anticoagulant and hypoglycemic properties.

Consuming large amounts of the tea can lead to gastrointestinal issues, such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, in some individuals. Schisandra chinensis tea may also exacerbate pre-existing conditions, like high blood pressure, due to its stimulating effects on the cardiovascular system. In addition, pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid consuming the tea as it may stimulate the uterus and affect fetal development.

Furthermore, people with allergies should be cautious when consuming Schisandra chinensis tea, as it can trigger allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.

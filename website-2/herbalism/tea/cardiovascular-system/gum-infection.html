7 Herbal Teas For Gum Infection

Herbal teas have been used for centuries to alleviate gum infections and other oral health issues.

These teas work by reducing inflammation, fighting bacteria, and promoting healing in the affected area. For instance, Eucalyptus globulus, a popular herbal remedy, contains compounds that have natural antibacterial properties, which help to combat the underlying infection. Echinacea purpurea, on the other hand, is known for its ability to boost the immune system, making it easier for your body to fight off the infection. Salvia officinalis, or sage, contains compounds that have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce swelling and pain in the gums. Drinking these herbal teas can have a significant impact on your life.

By alleviating gum infections, you can prevent more severe issues, such as tooth loss and bad breath. This can also boost your confidence and overall oral health, making it easier to eat and speak without discomfort. Additionally, many of these herbs are rich in antioxidants, which can help to protect against other health issues, such as heart disease and cancer. By incorporating herbal teas into your daily routine, you can take a proactive approach to maintaining your oral health and overall well-being. When using these herbal teas, it's essential to note that they may not be as effective as prescription medications, but they can be a helpful complement to traditional treatment.

To get the most out of these teas, make sure to drink them regularly, ideally 2-3 times a day, and combine them with good oral hygiene practices, such as brushing and flossing your teeth regularly.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains compounds like eucalyptol, cineole, and globulol, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help combat gum infections by reducing bacteria growth and swelling. Eucalyptol, in particular, has been shown to have a strong antibacterial effect against bacteria such as Streptococcus mutans, which is commonly found in gum infections. The anti-inflammatory properties of globulol also help reduce gum inflammation and pain.

By reducing bacteria growth and inflammation, Eucalyptus globulus teas can help alleviate symptoms of gum infections, promoting a healthier mouth.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 2 tablespoons of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves.
  2. Add the Eucalyptus leaves to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture and discard the Eucalyptus leaves.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to the tea for taste and to soothe the gum infection.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve gum infection symptoms.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that can help combat gum infections. Alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria that cause gum disease, such as Streptococcus mutans. Caffeic acid and rosmarinic acid possess antioxidant properties that can help reduce inflammation and protect the gums from further damage.

By consuming Echinacea purpurea teas, you may be able to promote a healthy oral environment and support the healing process of gum infections.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the Echinacea flowers in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain the mixture.
  3. Let the tea cool down, then add 1 tablespoon of honey to taste.
  4. Drink 1 cup of the Echinacea tea 2-3 times a day to help soothe gum infection.
  5. Consult a healthcare professional before using Echinacea tea as a treatment for gum infection, especially if you have any underlying health conditions.

3. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis teas contains a compound called carnosic acid, which has antibacterial properties that help fight off gum infections.

It also contains rosmarinic acid, a powerful antioxidant that reduces inflammation and prevents the growth of bacteria that cause infections. The antimicrobial properties of salvia officinalis help prevent the spread of infection and promote a healthy environment in the mouth. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of rosmarinic acid in salvia officinalis help reduce swelling and pain in the gums.

The flavonoids present in salvia officinalis, such as apigenin and luteolin, have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects that contribute to its ability to combat gum infections.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves or 1 teaspoon of dried leaves.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool for 5 minutes.
  3. Add the Salvia leaves to the cooled water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture and discard the leaves. Add honey to taste if desired.
  5. Drink 1/2 cup to 1 cup of the tea 2-3 times a day to help soothe gum infection.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains bioactive constituents like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help to reduce swelling and fight off infection in the gums. Glycyrrhizin also has an antiseptic effect, preventing the growth of bacteria that can cause gum infection. The flavonoids in Glycyrrhiza glabra teas have antioxidant properties, which help to protect the gums from oxidative stress and promote healing.

By reducing inflammation and preventing bacterial growth, Glycyrrhiza glabra teas can help to alleviate symptoms of gum infection and promote gum health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots. Wash the roots with water to remove dirt.
  2. Dry the roots in the sun or using a food dehydrator for 2-3 hours. This helps preserve the roots.
  3. Grind the dried roots into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
  4. Mix 1 teaspoon of the root powder with 1 cup of boiling water. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the liquid and drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help treat gum infection.

5. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains a compound called gingerol, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and pain in the gums, making it a good treatment for gum infections. Gingerol also has antimicrobial properties, which help kill bacteria that cause infections. Additionally, Zingiber officinale teas contains another compound called shogaol, which has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, further helping to combat gum infections.

The combination of these compounds in Zingiber officinale teas makes it a useful remedy for reducing gum inflammation and killing bacteria that cause infection.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1 teaspoon of fresh ginger root (Zingiber officinale)
  2. Cut the ginger root into small pieces and boil in the water for 5 minutes
  3. Strain the mixture and discard the ginger pieces
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) to the tea and stir well
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day for gum infection relief

6. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas contains thymol, carvacrol, and borneol as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial properties that help fight off bacterial infections in the gums. Thymol and carvacrol disrupt the cell membranes of bacteria, ultimately leading to their death and preventing the spread of infection. Borneol has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and ease pain associated with gum infections.

By consuming Thymus vulgaris teas, you can potentially benefit from its ability to combat gum infections due to its potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Thymus vulgaris leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the Thymus vulgaris leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup using a tea strainer or a piece of cheesecloth.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to the tea, if desired, and stir to mix.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help soothe gum infection symptoms.

7. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas contains a compound called menthol, which is known to have antibacterial properties.

This helps to reduce the growth of bacteria that can cause gum infections, such as Streptococcus mutans. Mentha x piperita also contains eucalyptol, which has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce swelling and pain in the gums. The tea's antimicrobial properties, particularly from limonene, help to fight off the bacteria that cause gum infections, reducing the risk of further infection.

By reducing bacteria and inflammation, Mentha x piperita tea can help to alleviate gum infection symptoms and promote healing.

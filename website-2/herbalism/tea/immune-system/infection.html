1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains bioactive constituents such as alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and polyphenols.

These compounds work together to boost the body's immune system, helping to fight off infections. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea tea have anti-inflammatory properties, reducing swelling and pain associated with infections. The caffeic acid derivatives in this tea have antimicrobial properties, directly targeting and killing bacteria and other pathogens that cause infections.

By consuming Echinacea purpurea tea, the body's natural defenses are enhanced, allowing it to recover from infections more effectively.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Echinacea purpurea root and flowers.
  2. Combine the Echinacea mixture with 1 cup of boiling water in a large mug.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain it.
  4. Add honey to taste, if desired, and stir well.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day for up to 7 days to help fight infection.

2. Echinacea angustifolia

Echinacea angustifolia teas contains several bioactive constituents, including alkylamides, glycoproteins, and polyphenols.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce swelling and fight off infections. The polyphenols in Echinacea angustifolia tea have antioxidant properties, which protect the body from free radicals that can damage cells and contribute to infection. The glycoproteins, specifically the polysaccharides, stimulate the immune system to produce more white blood cells, which are essential for fighting off infection.

This combination of anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties makes Echinacea angustifolia tea a valuable natural remedy for infections.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Echinacea angustifolia root.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Add the dried root to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the solids.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help fight infection.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off pathogens, Glycyrrhiza glabra teas can help alleviate symptoms and support the body's natural healing process.

Thymus vulgaris teas contains thymol and carvacrol, two powerful compounds that fight infection.

9. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum teas contains bioactive compounds like silymarin, silibinin, and silidianin.

Achillea millefolium teas contains sesquiterpene lactones, triterpenoid saponins, and flavonoids.

The triterpenoid saponins also contribute to the tea's antimicrobial properties by binding to and disrupting the cell membranes of microorganisms.

Additionally, the flavonoids in Achillea millefolium, such as kaempferol and quercetin, have anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce inflammation and promote healing in the body.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Achillea millefolium leaves.

  4. Add honey to taste, if needed. The recommended dose is 1 cup of tea, 3 times a day.
  5. Drink the tea to help with infections. Consult a doctor before using, especially if pregnant or breastfeeding.

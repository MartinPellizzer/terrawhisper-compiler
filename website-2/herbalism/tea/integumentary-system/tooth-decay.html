11 Herbal Teas For Tooth Decay

Herbal teas are a great alternative to help prevent tooth decay.

Some herbs have properties that can reduce bacteria in the mouth, which is a major cause of tooth decay. Let's take Echinacea purpurea for example - it's known for its anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and fight off infections in the gums. When consumed as a tea, Echinacea can help create an environment in the mouth that's less welcoming to bacteria. Another herb that can help is Zingiber officinale, also known as ginger. It has antibacterial properties that can help reduce the amount of bacteria in the mouth, which in turn can help prevent tooth decay.

Ginger also has anti-inflammatory properties, just like Echinacea, which can help soothe sore gums and reduce pain. Salvia officinalis, or sage, is another herb that can help with tooth decay. It has antibacterial properties that can help reduce the amount of bacteria in the mouth, and it also has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe sore gums. Drinking sage tea can also help reduce bad breath, which is often a symptom of tooth decay. When you drink herbal teas like these, the beneficial compounds are absorbed into the bloodstream and can help fight off bacteria in the mouth. This can lead to a number of benefits, including reduced inflammation, fewer infections, and a lower risk of tooth decay.

Regular consumption of herbal teas like these can also help keep your breath fresh and your gums healthy.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help combat tooth decay. Alkylamides, for example, have been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria that contribute to tooth decay, such as Streptococcus mutans. Caffeic acid and rosmarinic acid also have antioxidant properties that can neutralize free radicals that damage tooth enamel.

By reducing bacterial growth and protecting tooth enamel, Echinacea purpurea teas may be a natural way to help prevent tooth decay.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves. Rinse them in a fine mesh strainer under cold running water.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the fresh Echinacea flowers and leaves, and 2 cups of water. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  3. Reduce the heat and let the mixture simmer for 5-7 minutes. This will help release the active ingredients from the Echinacea.
  4. Strain the mixture through a fine mesh strainer into a cup. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the tea cool down. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to taste, if desired. Drink 1-2 cups of the tea, 3 times a day to help prevent tooth decay.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have antimicrobial properties that help prevent the growth of bacteria in the mouth.

These bacteria cause tooth decay by producing acid that damages tooth enamel. The antibacterial properties of gingerols and shogaols reduce the amount of acid produced, slowing down the decay process. Ginger also has anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and pain caused by tooth decay.

By inhibiting the growth of bacteria and reducing inflammation, Zingiber officinale teas can help prevent tooth decay and promote a healthy oral environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1 teaspoon of dried Zingiber officinale root.
  2. Bring the water to a boil in a pot. Reduce heat to low.
  3. Add the Zingiber officinale root to the water and let it steep for 5 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the root. Add honey to taste.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help prevent tooth decay.

3. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis teas contains bioactive constituents like carnosic acid and ursolic acid, which have been shown to have antibacterial properties.

These properties help fight against Streptococcus mutans, a type of bacteria that causes tooth decay. The antibacterial properties in Salvia officinalis teas can prevent the growth of bacteria on teeth, reducing the risk of tooth decay. The tea's flavonoids, such as luteolin and apigenin, also have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce gum inflammation and promote healthy gums.

By reducing bacterial growth and inflammation, Salvia officinalis teas can contribute to a healthier oral environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves and clean them with water.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of honey and set it aside for later use.
  3. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot and let it cool for 5 minutes.
  4. Add the clean Salvia officinalis leaves to the cooled water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the mixture and mix in the honey. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day for tooth decay relief.

4. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains bioactive constituents like cineole, which is known to have antimicrobial properties.

These properties help reduce the growth of bacteria that can cause tooth decay. Cineole also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and pain in the gums. Eucalyptus globulus teas has been shown to have a positive effect on reducing plaque and preventing the formation of tartar, which can contribute to tooth decay.

By reducing bacteria growth and inflammation, eucalyptus globulus teas can help protect against tooth decay.

6. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains glycyrrhizin and other active constituents like flavonoids and saponins.

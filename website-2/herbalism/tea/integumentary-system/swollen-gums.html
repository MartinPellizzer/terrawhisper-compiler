1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains a number of bioactive constituents that make it beneficial for swollen gums.

These constituents include alkylamides, glycoproteins, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory properties. The alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the production of inflammatory chemicals in the body, reducing swelling and pain. The glycoproteins, such as echinacin, have also been found to have antimicrobial properties, helping to prevent infection and promote healing.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infection, Echinacea purpurea teas can help to alleviate swollen gums and promote a healthy mouth.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Echinacea purpurea roots and flowers.
  2. Place 1 cup of boiling water in a teapot.
  3. Add the 2 tablespoons of Echinacea purpurea to the boiling water.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes. Strain the liquid.
  5. Drink 1/2 cup of the tea 2-3 times a day for swollen gums.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains bioactive constituents like tannins, flavonoids, and triterpenoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help reduce swelling and fight infection in swollen gums. Tannins, in particular, have astringent properties that help reduce bleeding and ease pain. Flavonoids, like quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant properties that help protect gums from damage and promote healing.

Regular consumption of Eucalyptus globulus teas may help soothe swollen gums and promote overall oral health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves.

  3. Add the dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.

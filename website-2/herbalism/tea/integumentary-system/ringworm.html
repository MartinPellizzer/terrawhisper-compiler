9 Herbal Teas For Ringworm

Herbal teas have been used for centuries to treat various skin conditions, including ringworm.

Ringworm is a fungal infection that causes red, itchy, and scaly patches on the skin. Herbal teas can help relieve ringworm by reducing inflammation and fighting off the fungal infection. Echinacea purpurea is one of the herbs that can help. It has anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe the skin and reduce redness. Melaleuca alternifolia, also known as tea tree oil, is another herb that can help treat ringworm.

It contains compounds that have antifungal properties, which can help fight off the fungal infection. Aloe barbadensis is also beneficial in treating ringworm. Its gel has anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can calm the skin and promote healing. Drinking herbal teas can bring several benefits to your life. For one, they can provide natural relief from ringworm symptoms, reducing the need for antibiotics and other medications. Herbal teas are also a great way to promote overall skin health, which can prevent future infections.

Additionally, drinking herbal teas can be a relaxing and calming experience, helping to reduce stress and promote a sense of well-being.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains a mix of bioactive constituents, including alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and phenolic acids.

These compounds work together to stimulate the immune system, which is essential in fighting off fungal infections like ringworm. Alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce the redness and itching associated with ringworm. Caffeic acid derivatives, on the other hand, have been found to have antifungal properties, making them effective against the fungus that causes ringworm.

By drinking Echinacea purpurea teas, you may be able to boost your immune system and give your body the tools it needs to fight off the infection.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves, or 2 tablespoons of dried flowers.
  2. Heat 1 cup of water and pour it over the Echinacea purpurea flowers in a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 5-10 minutes to release the active ingredients.
  4. Strain the tea and discard the solids. You can add honey or lemon to taste, if needed.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of the Echinacea purpurea tea, 2-3 times a day, for 1-2 weeks to help alleviate ringworm symptoms.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia teas contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and terpinen-4-ol, which have antifungal and antibacterial properties.

These properties help combat fungal infections, such as ringworm, by inhibiting the growth of fungal cells and killing bacteria that can cause secondary infections. The terpinen-4-ol in Melaleuca alternifolia teas is particularly effective in disrupting the fungal cell membrane, ultimately leading to the death of the fungal cells that cause ringworm. The antiseptic and antimicrobial properties of Melaleuca alternifolia teas also help prevent the spread of infection and promote a healthy environment for healing.

By applying Melaleuca alternifolia teas topically or taking it internally, the compounds can work together to reduce the severity and duration of ringworm symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Melaleuca alternifolia leaves.
  2. Combine 1 cup of dried leaves with 1 quart of boiling water in a saucepan.
  3. Steep for 5-7 minutes, then remove from heat.
  4. Strain the mixture and discard the solids. Let it cool.
  5. Drink 1/2 cup of the cooled tea, 3 times a day, for ringworm treatment.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas contains powerful bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties that help combat ringworm, a fungal infection caused by Trichophyton species. The anthraquinones in aloe vera, such as aloin and aloe-emodin, exhibit potent antifungal activity, inhibiting the growth of fungi responsible for ringworm. The polysaccharides in aloe vera, particularly acemannan, stimulate the immune system and enhance the body's natural defense against fungal infections.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy immune response, aloe barbadensis teas can help alleviate symptoms and support the healing of ringworm lesions.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Aloe barbadensis leaves and 2 cups of water
  2. Cut the Aloe leaves into small pieces and boil them in the water for 10-15 minutes
  3. Strain the mixture and discard the Aloe pieces
  4. Let the mixture cool, then refrigerate it for 2 hours or overnight
  5. Drink 1 cup of the cooled Aloe tea 2-3 times a day for 7-10 days to help treat ringworm

4. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas contains triterpenoids, saponins, and carotenoids, which are responsible for its antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation and combat fungal infections, making calendula an effective treatment for ringworm. The triterpenoids in calendula tea have been shown to inhibit the growth of fungal spores, thereby preventing the spread of the infection. The anti-inflammatory properties of calendula also help to soothe and calm the affected skin, reducing redness and itching.

By targeting both the fungal infection and the inflammation, calendula officinalis teas can help to clear up ringworm more quickly and effectively.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Calendula officinalis petals or 2 tablespoons of dried petals.
  2. Combine the Calendula petals with 1 cup of boiling water in a saucepan.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain it using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Discard the solids and let the tea cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  5. Soak a clean cloth in the tea and apply it to the affected area for 10-15 minutes, 2-3 times a day.

5. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum teas contains flavonoids, naphthodianthrones, and phenolic acids as its active constituents.

These compounds have antifungal properties that help combat ringworm, a fungal infection caused by Microsporum and Trichophyton species. The flavonoids in Hypericum perforatum tea have been shown to inhibit the growth of these fungi, reducing the severity of ringworm symptoms. The naphthodianthrones, specifically hyperforin and hypericin, possess antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe and heal the affected skin.

By using Hypericum perforatum tea as a treatment, individuals can potentially alleviate the symptoms of ringworm and promote a faster recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum leaves and flowers, or 2 tablespoons of dried Hypericum perforatum.
  2. Combine the Hypericum perforatum with 1 cup of boiling water in a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then strain it using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another cup.
  4. Discard the solids and let the tea cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of the tea per day, ideally 3 times a day, for up to 2 weeks, under medical supervision.

6. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum teas contains silymarin, a group of bioactive compounds that have anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties.

These properties help to reduce the redness and swelling associated with ringworm, making it easier to treat. Silymarin also has antioxidant properties, which can help to speed up the healing process by reducing damage to skin cells. The flavonoids present in silymarin, such as kaempferol and quercetin, have been shown to inhibit the growth of fungi that cause ringworm, including Trichophyton and Microsporum species.

By addressing the fungal infection and reducing inflammation, Silybum marianum teas can help to alleviate the symptoms of ringworm and promote healing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Silybum marianum leaves and flowers.
  2. Dry the Silybum marianum leaves and flowers in a low-heat oven (150°F) for 1 hour.
  3. Steep 1 teaspoon of dried Silybum marianum in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea and discard the solids. Add honey to taste, if needed.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of the Silybum marianum tea per day for ringworm treatment.

7. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains essential oils like cineole, eucalyptol, and alpha-pinene, which have antifungal properties.

These properties help to combat the fungal infection that causes ringworm. Cineole, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the growth of fungi and reduce inflammation. Eucalyptol has antimicrobial and antiseptic properties, which can help to soothe and heal the affected skin.

Drinking Eucalyptus globulus tea may help to boost the immune system and reduce the severity of ringworm symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh eucalyptus leaves (Eucalyptus globulus) or 1 tablespoon of dried eucalyptus leaves.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a kettle or on the stove.
  3. Add the eucalyptus leaves to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the leaves. Add honey or lemon to taste if needed.
  5. Drink 1/2 to 1 cup of the eucalyptus tea 2-3 times a day to help soothe ringworm symptoms.

8. Artemisia absinthium

Artemisia absinthium teas contains thujone, artabsin, and borneol, which are potent antifungal and antiseptic compounds.

Thujone, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the growth of fungi, including those that cause ringworm. The antiseptic properties of artabsin help to reduce inflammation and prevent infection, while borneol acts as a fungicidal agent, killing ringworm-causing fungi. The antifungal and antiseptic properties of these compounds work together to create an environment that is inhospitable to ringworm, making Artemisia absinthium teas a potential treatment for this condition.

By drinking Artemisia absinthium teas, some people have found relief from ringworm symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1-2 teaspoons of dried Artemisia absinthium leaves.
  2. Steep the leaves in the boiling water for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid into a cup using a piece of cheesecloth or a tea strainer.
  4. Add honey to taste, if desired, and mix well.
  5. Drink 1/2 cup of the tea 2-3 times a day for 1-2 weeks to help alleviate ringworm symptoms.

9. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains a compound called linalool, which is known for its antifungal properties.

This property helps to combat fungal infections like ringworm, which is caused by the fungus Trichophyton. The tea also contains another compound called linalyl acetate, which has anti-inflammatory effects that can help reduce the redness and itching associated with ringworm. The antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties of Lavandula angustifolia teas make it a potential treatment for ringworm.

Drinking the tea or applying it topically may help to reduce the severity and duration of ringworm symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia leaves and flowers.
  2. Add 1 cup of boiling water to a heat-resistant container with the Lavandula leaves and flowers.
  3. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then strain it using a piece of cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to the tea for taste, if desired. Let it cool.
  5. Soak a clean cloth in the cooled Lavandula tea and apply it directly to the affected area 2-3 times a day.

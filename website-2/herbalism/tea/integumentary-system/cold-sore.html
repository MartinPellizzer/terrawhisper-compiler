1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains a group of compounds called alkylamides, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce the severity and duration of cold sores.

These compounds, specifically cis-9-kauropsin and cis-9-epikauropsin, have been shown to inhibit the replication of the herpes simplex virus, which causes cold sores. Echinacea purpurea teas also contains phenolic acids, such as caffeic acid and ferulic acid, which have antioxidant properties that help to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the body. The flavonoids in Echinacea purpurea, including quercetin and kaempferol, have also been found to have antiviral and anti-inflammatory effects that can help to alleviate cold sore symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and inhibiting the virus, Echinacea purpurea teas may help to provide relief from cold sore discomfort and promote healing.

  1. Gather 2 teaspoons of dried Echinacea purpurea root and flowers.
  2. Add the Echinacea mixture to 1 cup of boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and let it cool.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help with cold sore relief.

2. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum teas contains hyperforin and hypericin, two bioactive constituents that have antiviral properties.

These properties help to reduce the severity and duration of cold sore outbreaks. Hyperforin has been shown to inhibit the replication of the herpes simplex virus, which is the main cause of cold sores. Hypericin also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling and redness associated with cold sore outbreaks.

By drinking Hypericum perforatum teas, you may be able to reduce the frequency and severity of cold sore outbreaks.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum flowers and leaves, or 1 teaspoon of dried flowers and leaves.
  2. Combine the gathered Hypericum perforatum with 1 cup of boiling water in a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes to allow the active ingredients to release.
  4. Strain the tea into a separate cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove the solids.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help alleviate cold sore symptoms. Store any leftover tea in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours.

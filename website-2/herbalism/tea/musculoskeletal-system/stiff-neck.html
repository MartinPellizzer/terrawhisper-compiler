1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two active constituents that help with stiff neck.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling and pain in the neck muscles. They also have a calming effect on the muscles, relaxing them and relieving tension. The antispasmodic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help to relax the muscles, preventing spasms that can cause stiffness.

Drinking Lavandula angustifolia teas may help to relax the neck muscles, reduce pain and inflammation, and promote relaxation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 tablespoon of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  2. Measure 1 teaspoon of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers into a tea infuser or a small piece of cheesecloth.
  3. Steep the Lavandula angustifolia flowers in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea and discard the flowers. Let the tea cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  5. Drink 1/2 cup of the Lavandula angustifolia tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve stiff neck.

The warming properties of ginger, caused by the release of shogaols, help to increase blood flow and reduce muscle spasms. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory compounds in Zingiber officinale teas, such as gingerols, can help to reduce swelling and ease pain in the affected area.

Regular consumption of Zingiber officinale teas may help to alleviate the discomfort and stiffness associated with a stiff neck.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of fresh or dried Zingiber officinale root.
  2. Cut the Zingiber officinale root into small pieces and place them in a tea infuser or a small piece of cheesecloth.
  3. Heat the water in a pot until it reaches boiling point, then reduce the heat to a simmer.
  4. Steep the Zingiber officinale root in the water for 5-7 minutes, then remove the root from the water.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup and drink it while it's warm, ideally 2-3 times a day.

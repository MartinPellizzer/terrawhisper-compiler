1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains flavonoids and terpenoids, including bilobalide and quercetin, which work together to improve blood flow and oxygenation to the hands.

The flavonoids, particularly quercetin, have antioxidant properties that help protect the nerves from damage, reducing numbness and tingling sensations. The terpenoids, such as bilobalide, help to dilate blood vessels, increasing circulation and reducing inflammation, which can contribute to numbness in the hands. Additionally, ginkgo biloba teas has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling and discomfort in the hands.

By improving blood flow and reducing inflammation, ginkgo biloba teas can help to alleviate numbness and tingling in the hands.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the Ginkgo biloba leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid into a cup to remove the Ginkgo biloba leaves.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and mix well.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve numbness in hands.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains active constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and ease pain in the hands, making them a good choice for numbness. The warming effect of Zingiber officinale teas, caused by the presence of gingerols, increases blood flow to the hands, reducing numbness and discomfort. The anti-inflammatory properties of shogaols also help reduce inflammation in the nerves, which can cause numbness and tingling sensations.

Regular consumption of Zingiber officinale teas may help alleviate numbness in the hands by reducing inflammation and promoting blood flow.

  1. Gather 2 cups of water and 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger root. Peel the ginger and chop it finely.
  2. Boil the water in a pot and add the chopped ginger. Reduce heat to medium.
  3. Let the mixture simmer for 5 minutes. Strain the liquid using a fine-mesh sieve into a cup.
  4. Add 1 teaspoon of honey to the tea if desired for taste. Stir well.
  5. Drink the tea warm or at room temperature. Repeat as needed for numbness relief.

3. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum teas contains a compound called cinnamaldehyde, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

This compound helps to reduce swelling in the hands and improves blood flow, reducing numbness. Cinnamaldehyde also has a vasodilating effect, which means it widens blood vessels, allowing more blood to flow to the hands. Additionally, Cinnamomum verum teas contains eugenol, a compound that has a warming effect on the body, helping to increase circulation and reduce numbness in the hands.

Regular consumption of Cinnamomum verum teas may help to alleviate numbness in the hands by improving blood circulation and reducing inflammation.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of Cinnamomum verum bark and 1 cup of water in a pot.
  2. Boil the water and then add the Cinnamomum verum bark. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture and discard the bark. Let it cool down for 2-3 minutes.

  5. Drink 1 cup of the tea 2-3 times a day to help manage numbness in hands.

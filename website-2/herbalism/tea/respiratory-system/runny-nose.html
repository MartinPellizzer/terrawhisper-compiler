These herbal teas are not only a great way to relieve a runny nose, but they're also a healthy and natural way to do it. By choosing herbal teas over medication, you can avoid harsh chemicals and artificial ingredients that can have negative side effects. Plus, these teas can be a great way to relax and unwind, which can be especially helpful if you're feeling stressed or anxious.

So, next time you're dealing with a runny nose, consider reaching for one of these herbal teas to find some relief.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains compounds like eucalyptol, a key active constituent that helps with congestion.

Eucalyptol has decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it effective in reducing swelling and mucus production in the nasal passages. The tea's expectorant properties help loosen and clear out excess mucus, allowing for easier breathing. Eucalyptol's ability to thin mucus also makes it easier to expel, providing quick relief from a runny nose.

Drinking Eucalyptus globulus tea can provide a natural way to alleviate nasal congestion and sinus pressure.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of honey and set aside for sweetening (optional).
  3. Pour boiling water over the Eucalyptus leaves and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture into a cup and discard the leaves.
  5. Add honey to taste and drink the tea hot to help relieve a runny nose.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and polysaccharides.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce swelling and fight off free radicals in the body. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea teas can modulate the immune system's response to infection, reducing the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals that can exacerbate a runny nose. The caffeic acid derivatives have a decongestant effect, helping to clear out mucus and reduce congestion.

By reducing inflammation and congestion, Echinacea purpurea teas may provide relief from a runny nose.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves.
  2. Use a tablespoon measure and add 2 tablespoons of the flowers and leaves to a tea infuser.
  3. Heat 1 cup of water in a pot and bring it to a boil.
  4. Pour the boiling water over the tea infuser and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it hot or let it cool for later use.

3. Sambucus nigra

The flavonoids and terpenes in Sambucus nigra teas work together to reduce swelling in the nasal passages and sinuses, making it easier to breathe.   5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve a runny nose.

7 Herbal Teas For Asthma

Herbal teas have been a long-standing remedy for asthma relief, and it's not hard to see why.

These teas work by opening up airways, making it easier to breathe. They do this in a few ways: some herbs have anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling in the lungs and airways. Others, like Eucalyptus globulus, release a decongestant oil that helps loosen and clear out mucus. This means you can breathe more easily and feel more comfortable. Echinacea purpurea is an example of an herb with anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce swelling and fight off infections that can trigger asthma attacks.

By taking Echinacea tea regularly, you can help keep your airways calm and reduce the frequency of attacks. Another herb, Rosmarinus officinalis (rosemary), has a compound called camphor, which helps relax muscles and open up airways. This can be especially helpful during an asthma attack, when muscles around the airways constrict and make breathing difficult. Drinking herbal teas can make a big difference in your life if you have asthma. By reducing inflammation, loosening mucus, and relaxing muscles, these teas can help you breathe more easily and feel more comfortable. This means you can go about your daily activities without worrying about an attack. You can exercise, go out with friends, or simply enjoy a meal without feeling anxious about your breathing.

By incorporating herbal teas into your routine, you can take control of your asthma and live a more active, confident life.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the airways, making it easier to breathe for people with asthma. The flavonoids also have antioxidant properties that help protect the lungs from oxidative stress caused by asthma. The caffeic acid in Echinacea purpurea teas has been shown to inhibit the production of leukotrienes, which are chemical mediators that contribute to asthma symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and preventing leukotriene production, Echinacea purpurea teas may help alleviate asthma symptoms and improve lung function.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves.
  2. Measure 1 cup of boiling water and pour it into a heat-resistant cup or teapot.
  3. Add the 2 tablespoons of Echinacea purpurea to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into another cup using a tea strainer or a piece of cheesecloth.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the tea 2-3 times a day to help manage asthma symptoms.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains a high concentration of bioactive constituents, including eucalyptol, a compound that has natural decongestant properties.

Eucalyptol helps to relieve congestion in the airways, making it easier to breathe. It also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce swelling and irritation in the lungs and airways. The diaphoretic properties of eucalyptus globulus teas also help to induce sweating, which can help to clear mucus and other debris from the lungs.

Regular consumption of eucalyptus globulus tea may help to reduce symptoms of asthma by opening up airways and promoting healthy lung function.

  1. Get 1 cup of boiling water and 2 tablespoons of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves.
  2. Steep the leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Let it sit.
  3. Strain the liquid into a cup using a tea strainer or cheesecloth.
  4. Add honey or sugar to taste, if needed. Stir well.
  5. Drink the tea as needed, ideally 2-3 times a day, to help relieve asthma symptoms.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains a number of bioactive constituents, including carnosic acid and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling in the airways and make breathing easier. Rosmararinic acid also acts as an antioxidant, which helps to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Rosmarinus officinalis teas can help to relax the airway muscles and reduce inflammation, making it easier to breathe for people with asthma.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Rosmarinus officinalis teas can provide relief for people experiencing asthma symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves or 2 teaspoons of dried leaves.
  2. Steep the leaves in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea and discard the leaves.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) to the tea and mix well.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help manage asthma symptoms.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains bioactive constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation in the airways, making it easier to breathe for people with asthma. The shogaols also have bronchodilatory effects, meaning they can help relax the muscles in the airways and open them up. Ginger's antioxidant properties help protect the lungs from damage caused by free radicals, which can exacerbate asthma symptoms.

Regular consumption of Zingiber officinale teas may help alleviate asthma symptoms by combining these anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory effects.

  1. Gather 2 teaspoons of dried Zingiber officinale root and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the Zingiber officinale root in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture and discard the root.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to the tea, if desired, and mix well.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, ideally 30 minutes before meals, to help manage asthma symptoms.

5. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, terpenoids, and bilobalide.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce airway inflammation associated with asthma. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba tea, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to inhibit the release of histamine and other inflammatory mediators, making it easier to breathe. Bilobalide, a sesquiterpene lactone, has been found to relax airway smooth muscles and improve lung function in people with asthma.

Regular consumption of Ginkgo biloba tea may help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing inflammation and improving respiratory function.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Pour the boiling water over the Ginkgo biloba leaves in a cup.
  3. Steep the leaves for 5-7 minutes to allow the flavor and benefits to release.
  4. Strain the liquid and discard the leaves. Add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help manage asthma symptoms.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two potent anti-inflammatory compounds.

These constituents help to reduce inflammation in the airways, a key contributor to asthma symptoms. Linalool also has bronchodilatory properties, which allow the airways to open up and improve breathing. Additionally, the tea's calming effects can help to reduce stress and anxiety, common asthma triggers.

By reducing inflammation and improving breathing, Lavandula angustifolia teas can be a helpful tool in managing asthma symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  2. Heat 1 cup of water in a pot and bring to a boil.
  3. Remove the pot from heat and add 1 tablespoon of dried flowers.
  4. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the liquid.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, as needed, to help with asthma symptoms.

7. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce swelling in the airways.

The flavonoids and saponins in Glycyrrhiza glabra tea have been shown to have a bronchodilatory effect, meaning they can help to widen the airways and make it easier to breathe. Glycyrrhizin also helps to reduce the production of leukotrienes, which are chemicals that can cause asthma symptoms. The antioxidants in Glycyrrhiza glabra tea can help to protect the lungs from damage caused by free radicals, which can exacerbate asthma symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and widening airways, Glycyrrhiza glabra tea may be a beneficial addition to an asthma treatment plan.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, a tablespoon of dried licorice root powder, and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Combine the dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root and boiling water in a saucepan. Let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve. Discard the solids.
  4. Add the licorice root powder to the tea and stir well. You can also add honey to taste if needed.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve asthma symptoms. Consult a doctor before using this tea as a treatment.

Its anti-inflammatory properties help protect the mucous membranes in the stomach and esophagus from acid damage, while its expectorant properties can help clear mucus and reduce congestion. Its essential oils can also help relax the muscles in the digestive system, reducing inflammation and discomfort. Drinking herbal teas like these can bring numerous benefits to your life. 1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains active constituents like gingerols and shogaols.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and carminative properties, which help to reduce inflammation and ease digestive discomfort. Gingerols also have a direct effect on the stomach muscles, slowing down digestion and reducing symptoms of acid reflux. Additionally, shogaols can help to reduce inflammation in the esophagus, which can help to prevent heartburn and other GERD symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and slowing down digestion, Zingiber officinale teas may provide relief for people experiencing GERD symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 tsp of dried Zingiber officinale root and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Measure 1 tsp of honey (optional) and set aside.
  3. Steep the Zingiber officinale root in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture into a cup and discard the root.
  5. Add honey to taste, if desired, and drink the tea slowly.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins.

Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe the esophagus and reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, which can contribute to GERD. Flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant properties that protect the esophageal mucosa from damage caused by stomach acid. Saponins, like glabridin, can help reduce the production of stomach acid, which can exacerbate GERD symptoms.

The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of these compounds in Glycyrrhiza glabra teas may help alleviate GERD symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water, 1 teaspoon of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, and a tea infuser or strainer.
  2. Bring the water to a boil in a saucepan. Reduce heat to low.
  3. Add the dried root to the tea infuser or strainer. Place in the saucepan.
  4. Steep for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea into a cup. Discard the root.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired. Drink 1 cup of tea 2-3 times a day.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas contains the bioactive constituents anethole and limonene, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and carminative effects, helping to reduce inflammation and ease digestion. Foeniculum vulgare teas also contains estragole, which has a relaxing effect on the muscles in the digestive tract, allowing food to pass through more easily. The tea's anti-acidic properties help to neutralize stomach acid and reduce the symptoms of acid reflux.

By reducing inflammation and acid production, Foeniculum vulgare teas can help alleviate symptoms of GERD, such as heartburn and chest pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Foeniculum vulgare leaves or 2 teaspoons of dried leaves.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Add the Foeniculum vulgare leaves to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the leaves.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the tea, 2-3 times a day, before meals to help with GERD symptoms.

4. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum teas contains silymarin, a bioactive compound with anti-inflammatory properties.

Silymarin helps to reduce inflammation in the esophagus and stomach, which can ease symptoms of GERD (Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease). The flavonoids and polyphenols in Silybum marianum teas also have a protective effect on the mucous membranes, preventing damage from acid reflux. Silymarin also increases the production of bile, which can help to neutralize stomach acid and prevent it from flowing back up into the esophagus.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the mucous membranes, Silybum marianum teas may help to alleviate symptoms of GERD.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Silybum marianum flowers. You can buy them from a store or online.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of the dried flowers and place them in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Boil 1 cup of water and pour it over the Silybum marianum flowers. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea and discard the flowers. Add honey or lemon to taste if needed.
  5. Drink the tea 1-2 times a day, preferably after meals, to help with GERD symptoms.

5. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis teas contains compounds like quinine, cinchonine, and cinchonidine.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe the esophagus and reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. Quinine, in particular, has a relaxing effect on the muscles in the esophagus, which can help prevent stomach acid from flowing back up into the esophagus and causing heartburn. The anti-inflammatory properties also help reduce swelling and irritation in the esophagus, making it easier to swallow and eat without discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the muscles in the esophagus, Cinchona officinalis teas can help alleviate symptoms of acid reflux and gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD).

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Cinchona officinalis bark, 1 cup of water, and a tea infuser or strainer.
  2. Heat the water in a pot until it reaches 212°F (100°C).
  3. Remove the water from heat and add the dried Cinchona officinalis bark to the tea infuser or strainer.
  4. Steep the tea for 5-7 minutes, then remove the bark from the water.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup and drink it slowly while it's warm. Repeat as needed, but not more than 3 times a day.

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas contains compounds like menthol, menthone, and limonene, which have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These active constituents help to reduce inflammation in the esophagus and stomach, which is often the cause of heartburn and acid reflux in people with GERD. Mentha x piperita teas also contains antioxidants like rosmarinic acid, which helps to protect the digestive system from damage caused by free radicals. The antimicrobial properties of menthol and other compounds in Mentha x piperita teas may also help to reduce the growth of bacteria in the stomach that can contribute to acid reflux.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the digestive system, Mentha x piperita teas can help to alleviate symptoms of GERD and promote a healthy digestive system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh mint leaves (Mentha x piperita) and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the mint leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture to remove the mint leaves.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) to the tea and mix well.
  5. Drink the tea slowly after meals to help ease GERD symptoms.

7. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa teas contains active constituents like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD). Curcumin in particular has been shown to reduce inflammation in the esophagus and lower the production of acid in the stomach, providing relief from heartburn and acid reflux. The antioxidant properties of curcuma longa teas also help protect the esophagus from damage caused by acid reflux, reducing the risk of complications.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the esophagus, curcuma longa teas can help manage GERD symptoms and promote digestive health.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Curcuma longa powder and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Let the boiling water cool for 1-2 minutes to avoid burning the powder.
  3. Add the Curcuma longa powder to the cooled water and stir well.
  4. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes to allow the powder to dissolve.
  5. Strain the tea and drink 1 cup, 2-3 times a day to help manage GERD symptoms.

9 Herbal Teas For Cavities

Herbal teas have been used for centuries to promote oral health, and some herbs are particularly effective in relieving cavities.

One example is Salvia officinalis, also known as sage, which contains compounds that help reduce inflammation and kill bacteria in the mouth, making it harder for cavities to form. Another herb, Zingiber officinale, or ginger, has natural antibacterial properties that help combat the germs that cause cavities.

Echinacea purpurea, a popular herb used to boost the immune system, also has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe mouth sores and gum inflammation. Drinking herbal teas made with these herbs can help prevent cavities by reducing the bacteria that cause them, and also by promoting a healthy balance of bacteria in the mouth. This can lead to a reduction in tooth decay and a decrease in the risk of needing fillings or other dental procedures.

By incorporating herbal teas into your daily routine, you can enjoy a healthier mouth and a reduced risk of oral health problems.

1. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis teas contains salvenic acid, a compound with antibacterial properties that help fight against the bacteria that cause cavities.

The tea also contains rosmarinic acid, which has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and irritation in the mouth. Salvia officinalis teas has been shown to inhibit the growth of Streptococcus mutans, the primary bacteria responsible for tooth decay. The antioxidant properties of the tea's constituents, including carnosic acid and ursolic acid, also help protect the mouth from damage and promote healing.

By reducing inflammation and inhibiting bacterial growth, Salvia officinalis teas can help prevent and manage cavities.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Salvia officinalis leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the Salvia leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid using a tea strainer or cheesecloth to remove the leaves.
  4. Add 1 teaspoon of honey (optional) to the tea for taste.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help prevent cavities.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains rosmarinic acid, alkylamides, and caffeic acid, which are key bioactive constituents that contribute to its benefits.

These compounds have antimicrobial properties, which help prevent the growth of bacteria that cause cavities. The alkaloids, such as echinacoside, also have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce swelling and pain associated with tooth decay. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of the tea's constituents, like flavonoids and phenolic acids, help protect the teeth and gums from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing bacterial growth and inflammation, Echinacea purpurea teas may help prevent the formation and progression of cavities.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves.
  2. Combine 1 cup of flowers and leaves with 1 quart of boiling water in a large pot.
  3. Reduce heat and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture into a separate container and discard the solids.
  5. Drink 1/2 cup of the tea 3-4 times a day to help prevent or alleviate cavities.

9. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis teas contains rosmarinic acid and triterpene compounds, which have antimicrobial properties that help combat cavity-causing bacteria in the mouth.

Rosmarinic acid also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and discomfort in the gums. By reducing the presence of cavity-causing bacteria and preventing acid production, Melissa officinalis teas can help prevent the formation of cavities.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Melissa officinalis leaves and flowers.
  2. Dry the leaves and flowers in a warm place or using a food dehydrator for 2-3 hours.
  3. Measure out 2 tablespoons of dried Melissa officinalis and place in a tea infuser.
  4. Steep the dried Melissa officinalis in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink 1 cup, 2-3 times a day to help prevent cavities.

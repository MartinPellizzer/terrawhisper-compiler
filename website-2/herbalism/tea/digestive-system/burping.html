8 Herbal Teas For Burping

Herbal teas have been used for centuries to relieve burping, and it's not hard to see why.

Burping, or belching, happens when gas builds up in the stomach and is released through the mouth. Certain herbs, like Zingiber officinale (ginger), Cinnamomum verum (cinnamon), and Glycyrrhiza glabra (licorice root), have natural properties that can help ease digestion and reduce gas production. Ginger, for example, has a compound called gingerol that can help relax the muscles in the stomach and improve digestion. This can lead to fewer gas bubbles forming and, as a result, less burping. Cinnamon, on the other hand, has a compound called cinnamaldehyde that can help slow down the movement of food through the digestive system. This can give the body more time to break down food properly, reducing the likelihood of gas building up.

Licorice root, meanwhile, has a compound called glycyrrhizin that can help soothe the stomach lining and reduce inflammation. This can make digestion easier and less likely to lead to burping. Drinking these herbal teas can bring several benefits to your life. For one, it can help you feel more comfortable after eating, especially after meals that are rich in beans, cabbage, or other gas-producing foods. It can also help you avoid the embarrassment of burping loudly in public or in front of others. Additionally, by reducing bloating and discomfort, these teas can help you feel more energetic and focused throughout the day.

By incorporating these teas into your daily routine, you can enjoy a smoother digestive experience and feel more at ease in your daily life.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains bioactive constituents like gingerols and shogaols.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and digestive properties that help soothe the stomach and ease digestive discomfort. Gingerols and shogaols also help relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing spasms and cramping that can lead to burping. The anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds can also help reduce inflammation in the stomach and digestive tract, which can contribute to burping.

By reducing inflammation and soothing the digestive system, Zingiber officinale teas can help alleviate symptoms of burping and discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water, 2-3 slices of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger root), and a strainer.
  2. Peel the ginger root and chop it into small pieces.
  3. Bring the water to a boil in a saucepan, then reduce heat.
  4. Add the chopped ginger to the water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup and enjoy as needed to help with burping.

2. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum teas contains a compound called cinnamaldehyde, which is responsible for its digestive benefits.

Cinnamaldehyde also has a stimulating effect on digestion, increasing the movement of food through the digestive system and reducing the likelihood of gas buildup. Additionally, Cinnamomum verum teas contains tannins, which can help to dry up excess mucus and reduce inflammation in the digestive tract.

By combining these properties, Cinnamomum verum teas may help to reduce burping and other digestive discomforts.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Cinnamomum verum bark, 1 cup of boiling water, and a teapot.
  2. Put the Cinnamomum verum bark in the teapot.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the Cinnamomum verum bark.
  4. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the tea into a cup.
  5. Drink the tea in small amounts after meals to help with burping.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains licorice root, which is rich in boivin and glycyrrhizin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce irritation in the digestive tract and ease gas and bloating. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the action of an enzyme that breaks down gas in the stomach, helping to prevent excessive belching. The soothing effects of glycyrrhizin and boivin can also calm the stomach and reduce spasms that contribute to burping.

Regular consumption of Glycyrrhiza glabra teas may help alleviate discomfort and promote a more balanced digestive system.

  1. Get 1 cup of water and boil it. Let it cool for 5 minutes.

  3. Add the root powder to the cooled water and stir well.
  4. Let the mixture sit for 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to mix.
  5. Strain the liquid and drink it as a tea. Repeat as needed for burping relief.

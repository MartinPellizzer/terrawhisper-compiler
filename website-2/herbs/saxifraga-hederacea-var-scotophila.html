What is Saxifraga hederacea var. scotophila?

Saxifraga hederacea var. scotophila is a variety of the species Saxifraga hederacea within the Saxifragaceae family, a family of flowering plants commonly known as saxifrages.

It is an alpine perennial herb with a rosette of oval-shaped, toothed, and glaucous leaves, typically 5-10 cm in length, and a stem that grows to 10-20 cm tall bearing small white or pinkish flowers in a corymb. The flowers are radially symmetrical with 5 petals and 5 sepals, and are arranged in a terminal corymb. The variety Saxifraga hederacea var. scotophila is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and the Himalayas, where it grows in rocky and stony areas.

It flowers in June and July, producing small, white, globose capsules containing numerous seeds.

Luzula pilosa

Luzula pilosa, also known as hairy woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Juncaceae.

It has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, providing relief from various health issues such as fever, rheumatism, and digestive problems due to its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. The bioactive constituents of Luzula pilosa include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Luzula pilosa is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, with the plant's aerial parts being used to treat various ailments, including respiratory issues and skin conditions.

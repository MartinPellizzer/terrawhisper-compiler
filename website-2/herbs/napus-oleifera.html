Napus oleifera

Napus oleifera, also known as Brassica rapa, is a cool-season annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-cancer properties, as well as its ability to support digestive health and reduce cholesterol levels. The bioactive constituents of Napus oleifera include glucosinolates, isothiocyanates, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Napus oleifera is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as oil capsules, tea infusions, and extracts, which are available in health food stores and online, although it is worth noting that some of these products may be referred to by different names or contain different ingredients.

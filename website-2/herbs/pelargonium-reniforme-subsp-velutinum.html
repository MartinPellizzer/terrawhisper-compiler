What is Pelargonium reniforme subsp. velutinum?

Pelargonium reniforme subsp.

velutinum is a subspecies of succulent perennial herb belonging to the family Geraniaceae. It has fleshy, rounded leaves that are velvety to the touch, typically 50-100 mm in diameter, and a short stem with a few branches.

The leaves are greenish-gray in color with a dense, soft covering of fine hairs giving them a velvety texture, and the margin is entire. The flowers are small, pink to purple in color, and grow in clusters at the end of short stems, typically 1-2 mm in diameter.

This subspecies is native to the western parts of South Africa.

Dracocephalum japonicum

Dracocephalum japonicum, also known as Japanese dragon head or Japanese dragon flower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

Traditionally, it has been used to treat a variety of health conditions, including fever, headaches, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. The bioactive constituents of Dracocephalum japonicum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and volatile oils, such as borneol and bornyl acetate, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional Chinese and Japanese medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are commonly used to relieve stress, improve sleep quality, and soothe digestive discomfort.

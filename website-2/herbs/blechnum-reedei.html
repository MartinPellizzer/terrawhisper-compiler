Blechnum reedei

Blechnum reedei, also known as the Reed's chain fern, is a species of fern native to the Mediterranean region.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in wound healing, reducing fever, and treating respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Blechnum reedei include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Blechnum reedei is often used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for its skin-soothing and wound-healing effects.

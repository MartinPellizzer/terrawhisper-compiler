Camellia corallina

Camellia corallina, also known as Coral Camellia or Coral Camellia Rose, is a species of flowering plant in the tea family (Theaceae), native to China and Southeast Asia.

The health benefits of Camellia corallina include its potential to reduce inflammation, improve cardiovascular health, and exhibit antioxidant properties, making it a valuable herbal remedy for various health issues. The bioactive constituents of Camellia corallina include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Camellia corallina is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or capsule, and is also used to make extracts and topical creams, making it a popular ingredient in herbal formulations for cardiovascular and anti-inflammatory support.

Satureja pulegium

Satureja pulegium, also known as pennyroyal, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including its ability to aid digestion, relieve nausea and headaches, and exhibit antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Satureja pulegium include essential oils, primarily composed of pulegone and isopulegogue, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, pennyroyal is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or essential oil, and is often used to treat digestive issues, menstrual cramps, and skin conditions.

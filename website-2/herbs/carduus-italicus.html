Carduus italicus

Carduus italicus, also known as Italian thistle or Italian cardoon, is a perennial thistle species native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its prickly, silvery-gray leaves and purple flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects, as well as its potential to alleviate gastrointestinal issues and reduce fever. The bioactive constituents of Carduus italicus include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Carduus italicus is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into traditional remedies for its purported health benefits.

Helenium candidum

Helenium candidum, also known as Sneezeweed, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, commonly found in North America.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiviral properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of colds, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Helenium candidum include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, as well as to soothe digestive problems and skin irritations.

classification

Helenium candidum is classified in the family Asteraceae. This classification places it within the group of flowering plants commonly known as asters or daisies. The genus Helenium is a group of plants that are native to the Americas, and they are also known as sneezeweed due to the effect of the plant's pollen on the nasal passages. As a member of the Asteraceae family, Helenium candidum shares characteristics with other plants in this family, such as the presence of composite flowers and a taproot.

Ericoides viridiflorum

Ericoides viridiflorum, commonly known as Wild Garlic, is a perennial plant that belongs to the Allium family and is native to the subtropical regions of South Africa.

The health benefits of Wild Garlic include its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments, including respiratory issues, fever, and digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of Wild Garlic include saponins, flavonoids, and sulfur compounds, such as allicin and diallyl disulfide, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Wild Garlic include teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as dried and powdered forms that can be used to make capsules or added to food as a flavoring agent.

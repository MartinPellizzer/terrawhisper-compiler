Consolida macedonica

Consolida macedonica, also known as larkspur or delphinium, is a flowering plant species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and expectorant properties, helping to relieve respiratory issues such as bronchitis and coughs. The bioactive constituents of Consolida macedonica include alkaloids, such as delphinine, ajacine, and consoline, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Consolida macedonica is often made into teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being used in combination with other herbs to create cough syrups and expectorant preparations.

Ficus subrepanda

Ficus subrepanda, also known as the banyan fig, is a species of deciduous tree native to tropical Asia, characterized by its aerial roots and broad, ovate leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, inflammation, and digestive issues, due to its potential health benefits such as antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Ficus subrepanda include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Indian medicine, Ficus subrepanda is commonly used to prepare herbal formulations such as decoctions, infusions, and powders, which are often used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory issues.

Cassine pubescens

Cassine pubescens, also known as Cassine or African Bloodwood, is a medicinal plant native to tropical Africa, belonging to the Anacardiaceae family.

The plant is valued for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help to alleviate conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Cassine pubescens include flavonoids, alkaloids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Cassine is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, making it a popular ingredient in traditional African medicine.

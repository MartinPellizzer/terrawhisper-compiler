Rubus incarnatus

Rubus incarnatus, also known as the pink-stemmed raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to North America, belonging to the Rosaceae family.

This plant is prized for its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which have been associated with several health benefits, including reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease, improving immune function, and exhibiting anti-cancer properties. The bioactive constituents of Rubus incarnatus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Rubus incarnatus is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into jams, jellies, and other food products to leverage its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

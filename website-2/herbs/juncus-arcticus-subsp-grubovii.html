What is Juncus arcticus subsp. grubovii?

Juncus arcticus subsp.

grubovii is a subspecies of flowering plant in the family Juncaceae, native to the Russian Far East and northeastern Asia. It is a perennial herb that produces cylindrical, smooth stems, 10-50 cm tall, with linear, grass-like leaves, 0.5-1.5 mm wide, and greenish-brown, ovate to lance-shaped, unisexual spikes, 2-4 cm long.

The plant flowers in July, producing small, greenish-brown flowers, 2-3 mm long, with 3-5 stamens and 3 styles. The stems and leaves are glabrous, while the spikes are hairy, with a few white or yellowish hairs.

The roots are fibrous, with rhizomes present.

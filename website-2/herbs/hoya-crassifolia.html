Hoya crassifolia

Hoya crassifolia, also known as Indian rope plant or crassifolia hoya, is a flowering, succulent plant species native to the tropical regions of Asia and Australia.

The bioactive constituents of Hoya crassifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat coughs, fever, and other health ailments.

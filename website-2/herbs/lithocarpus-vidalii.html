Lithocarpus vidalii

Lithocarpus vidalii, also known as Vidal's Lithocarpus, is a plant species in the beech family (Fagaceae) native to the Philippines.

Traditionally, the plant has been used to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Lithocarpus vidalii include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Filipino medicine, the plant is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and decoctions to promote respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu.

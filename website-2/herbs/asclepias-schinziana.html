Asclepias schinziana

Asclepias schinziana, commonly known as the White Milkweed or Schinz's Milkweed, is a perennial plant species native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, characterized by its white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including the treatment of fever, inflammation, and skin conditions, such as eczema and acne, due to its antiviral, antibacterial, and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Asclepias schinziana include cardenolides, glycosides, and iridoid compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In herbal medicine, Asclepias schinziana is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion to treat various health conditions, and it is also used in traditional rituals and ceremonies for its perceived spiritual and cleansing properties.

Nostolachma crassifolia

Nostolachma crassifolia is a plant species commonly known as "Kokam" or "Kokum," belonging to the family Rubiaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health issues, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help to reduce fever, alleviate respiratory problems, and promote wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Nostolachma crassifolia include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Indian medicine, the plant is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and ointments, as well as being used in cooking, particularly in the preparation of the popular kokum fruit butter.

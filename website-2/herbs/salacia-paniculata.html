Salacia paniculata

Salacia paniculata, also known as Balamari or Salacia, is a plant species in the family Celastraceae, commonly found in tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include lowering blood sugar levels, reducing cholesterol and triglyceride levels, and alleviating symptoms of diabetes and obesity. The bioactive constituents of Salacia paniculata include glycosides, flavonoids, and terpenoids, including salacin and salasonin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, Salacia paniculata is often used to prepare herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and extracts, which are used to manage various health conditions, including diabetes and metabolic disorders.

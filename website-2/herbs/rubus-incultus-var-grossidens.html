What is Rubus incultus var. grossidens?

Rubus incultus var. grossidens is a taxonomic variety of the Rubus incultus species in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by a perennial growth habit with a woody stem (caudex) and a thorny, deciduous shrub habit, with leaves that are trifoliate, pinnate and glabrous on the lower surface, 5-11 cm long, and with 3-5 leaflets that are ovate, pointed and serrated. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers in a compound corymb, with 5 petals and 5 stamens, in late spring to early summer. The flowers are followed by aggregate fruits (synconia), consisting of multiple drupes (drupelets), which are red to purple when mature.

The variety is found in China and other parts of Asia.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Rubus incultus var. grossidens are derived from its leaves, fruits, and roots.

Rubus tea is made by infusing the leaves in hot water, rich in antioxidants, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which may aid in reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health. Rubus jam is a sweet preserve made from the fruits, used to boost vitamin C intake and provide antimicrobial properties. Rubus tincture is a concentrated liquid extract of the plant's roots and leaves, used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Rubus salve and rubus syrup are topical preparations made from the plant's leaves and roots, used to soothe skin irritations, wounds, and respiratory issues, and as a cough syrup, respectively.

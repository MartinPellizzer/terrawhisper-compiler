What is Primula auricula subsp. bauhinii?

Primula auricula subsp.

bauhinii is a subspecies of the species Primula auricula, belonging to the family Primulaceae. It is a perennial herbaceous plant with rosettes of rounded, often lobed, leaves that are 2-6 cm long and 1-3 cm wide, and are covered with short, fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow, orange, or purple flowers with five petals that are 1-2 cm long and are borne on a short, hairy stem. The flowers are clustered in a raceme and are often fragrant. Primula auricula subsp.

bauhinii is native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

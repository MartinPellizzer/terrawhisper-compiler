Lactuca callosa

Lactuca callosa, also known as the prickly lettuce or calico lettuce, is a plant species within the dandelion family (Asteraceae) characterized by its jagged leaves and ability to grow in dry, rocky areas.

The bioactive constituents of Lactuca callosa include flavonoids, sesquiterpene lactones, and triterpenoid saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is also used in herbal remedies to treat a range of ailments, including fever, sore throats, and skin conditions.

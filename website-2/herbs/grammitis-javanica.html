Grammitis javanica

Grammitis javanica, also known as Java fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its delicate fronds and ability to thrive in a variety of environments.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, exhibiting potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in wound healing, reducing inflammation, and protecting against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Grammitis javanica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Grammitis javanica is often prepared as a tea or tincture, and can also be found in some herbal remedies and supplements, such as topical creams and ointments for skin conditions.

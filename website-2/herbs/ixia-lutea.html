Ixia lutea

Ixia lutea, also known as the yellow Ixia or yellow bloodroot, is a flowering plant species native to South Africa.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including respiratory issues, fever, and skin problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Ixia lutea include iridoid glycosides, such as luteolin and diosmetin, as well as phenolic compounds like phenylethanoids.

The most common herbal preparations made with Ixia lutea include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory conditions like bronchitis and asthma.

Tillandsia wawrana

Tillandsia wawrana, also known as "Wawrana Air Plant," is a species of epiphytic bromeliad that grows without soil and absorbs moisture and nutrients through its leaves.

This air plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation and fighting against certain types of bacteria and fungi, due to its antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Tillandsia wawrana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Tillandsia wawrana is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and extracts, which are often used to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu.

Orobus capensis

Orobus capensis, also known as the "Cape Hound's Tooth," is a perennial herb belonging to the Fabaceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used in South African medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties, which can aid in treating conditions such as arthritis, wounds, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Orobus capensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

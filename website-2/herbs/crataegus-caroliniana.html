Crataegus caroliniana

Crataegus caroliniana, also known as the Carolina hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

The health benefits of Carolina hawthorn include its ability to lower blood pressure, reduce inflammation, and act as an antioxidant, making it a potential treatment for conditions such as hypertension and cardiovascular disease. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, triterpenoids, and oligomeric proanthocyanidins (OPCs), which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Carolina hawthorn is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and capsules, and is often combined with other herbs such as hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna) to enhance its cardiovascular benefits.

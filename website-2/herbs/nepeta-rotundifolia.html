Nepeta rotundifolia

Nepeta rotundifolia, also known as round-leaved catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include its ability to soothe digestive issues, alleviate anxiety and insomnia, and exhibit antimicrobial and antiviral activities. The bioactive constituents of Nepeta rotundifolia include nepetalactone, a unique iridoid glycoside responsible for its sedative and antispasmodic effects, as well as other volatile oils and flavonoids.

Nepeta rotundifolia is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, often used to calm the nervous system and promote relaxation.

Vernonia silhetensis

Vernonia silhetensis, also known as Bhrikuti, is a plant species within the Asteraceae family, native to the Himalayan region.

It has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Vernonia silhetensis include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Ayurvedic practice, Vernonia silhetensis is often used to prepare herbal teas, decoctions, and infusions, as well as being formulated into capsules and tablets for easy consumption.

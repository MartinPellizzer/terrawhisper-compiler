Cordia parviflora

Cordia parviflora, also known as the "wild lime," is a plant species belonging to the Cordiaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which are thought to aid in the treatment of various health conditions such as wounds, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Cordia parviflora include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Cordia parviflora is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is also used in the production of topical creams and ointments for wound healing and skin care.

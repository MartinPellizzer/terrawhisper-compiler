What is Crataegus sicca var. glabrifolia?

Crataegus sicca var. glabrifolia is a taxonomic variety of hawthorn native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its deciduous, thorny shrubs or small trees growing up to 4 meters in height, with grayish-brown bark and elliptical to ovate leaves 2-4 centimeters long, with serrated margins and 7-9 pairs of veins. The leaves are glabrous on the lower surface and have a leathery texture. The flowers are white, 5-petaled, and 1-1.5 centimeters in diameter, borne in clusters, while the fruit is a dark red or purple drupe 8-12 millimeters in diameter.

The plant's distribution overlaps with that of other Crataegus species, including C. sicca, to which it is closely related.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Crataegus sicca var. glabrifolia are infusion, tincture, decoction, tea, and crataegus tea.

Tincture is a concentrated liquid solution made by soaking the plant material in a solvent such as ethanol or glycerin to create a standardized extract. Decoction is a liquid extract made by boiling the root and bark in water to release its sesquiterpenes and alkaloids.

Tea and crataegus tea are liquid extracts made by steeping the dried plant material in hot water, often used for its cardio-protective and antioxidant properties.

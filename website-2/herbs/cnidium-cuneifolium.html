Cnidium cuneifolium

Cnidium cuneifolium, also known as Cnidium fruit or Sha Yuan Zi, is a perennial plant species native to China, belonging to the family Apiaceae.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-arthritic effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of arthritis, improve liver health, and boost the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Cnidium cuneifolium include alkaloids, flavonoids, and coumarins, particularly cnidilide, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Cnidium cuneifolium is commonly prepared into herbal products such as capsules, tablets, and teas to support joint health and alleviate pain, as well as being used in combination with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

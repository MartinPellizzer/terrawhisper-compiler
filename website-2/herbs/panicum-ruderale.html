Panicum ruderale

Panicum ruderale, also known as rough panic grass, is a tropical grass species native to the Americas, characterized by its tall, branching stems and flat, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it beneficial for treating various health issues, including fever, wounds, and skin conditions. Bioactive constituents of Panicum ruderale include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Panicum ruderale is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is sometimes used in combination with other plants to enhance its therapeutic effects.

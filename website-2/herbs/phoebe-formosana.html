Phoebe formosana

Phoebe formosana, also known as Chinese silver fir, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Taiwan, belonging to the family Lauraceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory infections, due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Phoebe formosana include sesquiterpenoids, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is often used to make herbal preparations, such as teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into traditional formulas to treat respiratory and inflammatory conditions.

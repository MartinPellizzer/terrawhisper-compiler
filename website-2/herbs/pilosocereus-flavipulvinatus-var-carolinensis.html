What is Pilosocereus flavipulvinatus var. carolinensis?

Pilosocereus flavipulvinatus var. carolinensis is a taxonomic variety within the species Pilosocereus flavipulvinatus, belonging to the family Cactaceae.

This variety exhibits a short, columnar stem with a diameter of 8-10 cm and a height of 20-30 cm, featuring 10-15 ribs, with a dense cover of yellow to brown, sharp, and tuberculate spines, and a yellowish, woolly areole. The plant produces yellow to white flowers that are 5-7 cm in length, with a yellow anther. It has a thick, green, and fleshy stem with a low, spreading growth habit, and is native to the southeastern United States.

The variety Pilosocereus flavipulvinatus var. carolinensis is often found in sandy, scrubby habitats and is typically solitary or occasionally produces offsets.

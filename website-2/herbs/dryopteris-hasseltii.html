Dryopteris hasseltii

Dryopteris hasseltii, also known as Hasselt's shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia, characterized by its small, lance-shaped fronds that grow in a rosette pattern.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, and may aid in reducing fever, alleviating coughs, and promoting wound healing. Bioactive constituents of Dryopteris hasseltii include flavonoids, sesquiterpenes, and phenolic acids, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, and is sometimes combined with other herbs to create remedies for colds, fever, and respiratory issues.

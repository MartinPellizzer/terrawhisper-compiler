Carthamus alexandrinus

Carthamus alexandrinus, also known as silymarin or Alexandrian sage, is a flowering plant species that belongs to the daisy family, Asteraceae.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and hepatoprotective properties, making it a potential remedy for various ailments such as liver damage and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and polyphenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Carthamus alexandrinus include extracts, capsules, and teas, often used to support liver health and improve digestion.

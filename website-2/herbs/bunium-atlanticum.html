Bunium atlanticum

Bunium atlanticum, also known as Atlantic sweet cicely or sweet cicely, is a perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae), characterized by its aromatic, anise-flavored roots and leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including digestive problems, respiratory issues, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties. The bioactive constituents of Bunium atlanticum include essential oils, coumarins, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared in the form of teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used as a flavoring agent in candies and baked goods.

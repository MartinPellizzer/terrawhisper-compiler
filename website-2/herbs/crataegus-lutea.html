Crataegus lutea

Crataegus lutea, also known as the white hawthorn or golden hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae, characterized by its white or pale yellow flowers and small, edible fruits.

The health benefits of Crataegus lutea include its ability to support cardiovascular health, improve blood lipid profiles, and exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular herbal remedy for heart health. The bioactive constituents of Crataegus lutea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and oligomeric proanthocyanidins (OPCs), which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Crataegus lutea is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or dried fruit extract, and is used to promote heart health, reduce inflammation, and support immune function.

classification

Crataegus lutea is classified as a species of hawthorn, within the family Rosaceae. It belongs to the genus Crataegus, which includes over 200 species of hawthorns. As a member of the Rosaceae family, Crataegus lutea is related to other plants such as roses and apples. The classification of Crataegus lutea is further divided into the order Rosales and the clade eurosids I. This classification places Crataegus lutea among the flowering plants, also known as angiosperms.

Ligularia lamarum

Ligularia lamarum, also known as Gumplant, is a perennial plant belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae) that is native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, with its health benefits attributed to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues, fever, and digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of Ligularia lamarum include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for the plant's medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ligularia lamarum is commonly prepared as a tea or infused in hot water to treat respiratory issues, or used as a tincture to address digestive problems.

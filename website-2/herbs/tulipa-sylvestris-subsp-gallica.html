What is Tulipa sylvestris subsp. gallica?

Tulipa sylvestris subsp.

gallica is a subspecies of the wild tulip, belonging to the family Liliaceae. It is characterized by its solitary, cup-shaped flowers, typically measuring 2-4 cm in height, with three outer and three inner tepals, usually in shades of yellow or white, and sometimes with a purple or reddish-brown base.

The tepals are often reflexed, giving the appearance of a cup or goblet. This subspecies is distinguished from other wild tulips by its typically solitary, unbranched flower stems and the presence of two or three basal leaves.

The stems are typically 10-30 cm tall, with linear or lanceolate leaves that are often glaucous and sheathing at the base.

Gentiana umbellata

Gentiana umbellata, also known as the Downy Gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America, characterized by its clusters of small, blue or purple flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in the treatment of digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Gentiana umbellata include gentiopicroside, gentianine, and loganin, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat a range of health issues, and are also used as a flavoring agent in some traditional remedies.

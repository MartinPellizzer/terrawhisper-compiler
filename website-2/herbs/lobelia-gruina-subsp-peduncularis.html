What is Lobelia gruina subsp. peduncularis?

Lobelia gruina subsp.

peduncularis is an endemic plant species of New Zealand, belonging to the family Campanulaceae. It is a perennial herb that grows to 30-100 cm in height, with ovate-lanceolate leaves that are 5-12 cm long and 1-3 cm wide.

The leaves are opposite and sessile, with a glabrous or pubescent upper surface and a hairy lower surface. The plant produces axillary, 2-5-flowered spikes, with blue or purple corollas that are 1.5-2.5 cm long, and a narrow, cylindrical shape.

The fruit is a four-lobed capsule that is 6-10 mm long.

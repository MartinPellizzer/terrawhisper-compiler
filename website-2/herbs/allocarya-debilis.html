Allocarya debilis

Allocarya debilis, also known as Western yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and Canada, characterized by its fern-like leaves and small white flowers.

Traditionally, Western yarrow has been used to aid in digestion, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing, making it a valuable plant in natural medicine. The bioactive constituents of Western yarrow include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and volatile oils, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Western yarrow is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and salves to promote skin health and reduce inflammation.

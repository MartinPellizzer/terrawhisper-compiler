Ixeris debilis

Ixeris debilis, also known as Asian tansy mustard, is a type of annual or biennial plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including the inhibition of inflammation and the induction of apoptosis in cancer cells, as well as the enhancement of immune function. The bioactive constituents of Ixeris debilis include isothiocyanates, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities, as well as other compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ixeris debilis is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is used to treat a range of ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues.

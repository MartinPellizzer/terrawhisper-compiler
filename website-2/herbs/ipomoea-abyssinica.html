Ipomoea abyssinica

Ipomoea abyssinica is a flowering plant species belonging to the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae), native to tropical Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin disorders, due to its purported health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea abyssinica include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a tea or infusion, or used as a paste or ointment for topical application, making it a popular ingredient in various herbal remedies.

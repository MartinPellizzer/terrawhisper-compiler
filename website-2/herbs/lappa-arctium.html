Lappa arctium

Lappa arctium, also known as Burdock, is a biennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its large, coarse leaves and purple flowers.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant effects, making it beneficial in treating conditions such as skin irritations, wounds, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Burdock include inulin, arctiin, and lappaol, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

Burdock is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and topical creams, as well as in traditional medicines like ginseng-burdock combination for immune system support and in skincare products to alleviate acne and other skin issues.

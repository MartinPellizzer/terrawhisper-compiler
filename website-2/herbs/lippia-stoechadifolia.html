Lippia stoechadifolia

Lippia stoechadifolia, also known as Mexican honeysuckle or Mexican lippia, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and Central America, belonging to the family Verbenaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making it beneficial for treating various health issues such as wounds, fever, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Lippia stoechadifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, particularly thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, Lippia stoechadifolia is commonly prepared as an infusion, tea, or tincture, and is often used to treat coughs, sore throats, and digestive issues.

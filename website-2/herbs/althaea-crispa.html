Althaea crispa

Althaea crispa, also known as Marsh Mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Malvaceae, characterized by its delicate pink or white flowers and edible roots.

The bioactive constituents of Althaea crispa include mucilages, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Althaea crispa is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and poultices, as well as being incorporated into creams and ointments for topical application.

Crepis alpestris

Crepis alpestris, also known as Alpine Hawk's Beard or Alpine Hawkbit, is a perennial plant species belonging to the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Crepis alpestris include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Crepis alpestris is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat digestive issues, coughs, and colds.

Leopoldia matritensis

Leopoldia matritensis, also known as the Madrid squill, is a perennial bulbous plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family, native to the Iberian Peninsula.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine due to its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help to alleviate respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Leopoldia matritensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Leopoldia matritensis is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat respiratory issues and other health conditions.

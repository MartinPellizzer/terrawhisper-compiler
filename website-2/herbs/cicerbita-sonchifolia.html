Cicerbita sonchifolia

Cicerbita sonchifolia, also known as Bat Ear or Sonchus-like Cicerbita, is a perennial plant belonging to the dandelion family (Asteraceae) that grows in the mountainous regions of Asia and Europe.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, contributing to its potential in alleviating conditions such as arthritis, fever, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Cicerbita sonchifolia include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional medicine, the plant is commonly used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health conditions, and are also used as a natural remedy for digestive issues and skin problems.

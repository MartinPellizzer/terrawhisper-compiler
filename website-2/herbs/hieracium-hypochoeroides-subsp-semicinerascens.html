What is Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. semicinerascens?

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

semicinerascens is a taxonomic subspecies of Hieracium hypochoeroides, belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its glabrous to sparsely hairy rosette of leaves, with leaf blades typically 5-10 mm long and 2-4 mm wide, linear to lanceolate in shape, and crenate to dentate margin.

The stems are 10-30 cm tall, erect, and sparsely hairy, supporting capitula at the apex, each containing 20-40 florets with 3-5 mm long corollas that are yellow to reddish-brown in color. The achenes are 4-6 mm long, cylindrical, and glabrous, with pappus composed of 20-40 bristles 1-2 mm long. Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

semicinerascens is primarily found in mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

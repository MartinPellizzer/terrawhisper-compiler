Althaea trimestris

Althaea trimestris, commonly known as marsh mallow, is a perennial plant species belonging to the mallow family (Malvaceae).

The plant has been utilized for centuries in traditional medicine, offering health benefits such as soothing and protecting the mucous membranes, reducing inflammation, and providing relief from digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Althaea trimestris include mucilages, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Althaea trimestris include teas, infusions, and topical creams, often used to treat skin conditions, coughs, and respiratory issues.

Althaea trimestris is native to the Mediterranean region and is also found in parts of Africa and Asia.

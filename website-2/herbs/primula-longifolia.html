Primula longifolia

Primula longifolia, also known as the Alpine Primrose, is a perennial plant belonging to the Primulaceae family, characterized by its delicate white, pink, or purple flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in wound healing and the treatment of respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Primula longifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Primula longifolia is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for its wound-healing properties.

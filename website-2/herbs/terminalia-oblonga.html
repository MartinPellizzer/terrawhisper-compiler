Terminalia oblonga

Terminalia oblonga, also known as beleric myrobalan, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Asia, characterized by its distinctive edible fruit and medicinal properties.

The health benefits of Terminalia oblonga include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, Terminalia oblonga is commonly used in herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and powders, often combined with other herbs like Amala and Haritaki to create the triphala formula.

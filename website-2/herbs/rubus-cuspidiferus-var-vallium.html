What is Rubus cuspidiferus var. vallium?

Rubus cuspidiferus var. vallium is a botanical variant of the Rubus cuspidiferus species within the Rubus genus, belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, 5-7 mm long, and trifoliate leaves with 3 leaflets, 2-5 cm long, and a cuneate base. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in a corymb, 3-6 cm in diameter, with 5 petals, and is followed by red to purple aggregate fruits. Rubus cuspidiferus var. vallium is a perennial shrub, typically growing up to 1 m in height, and is found in mountainous regions, often in rocky or sandy areas.

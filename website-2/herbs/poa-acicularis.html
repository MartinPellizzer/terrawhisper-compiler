Poa acicularis

Poa acicularis, also known as prairie grass or sharp-leaved meadow-grass, is a type of perennial grass native to the Northern Hemisphere.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including digestive problems and inflammation, due to its antispasmodic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Poa acicularis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Poa acicularis is commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is used to treat conditions such as diarrhea, colic, and menstrual cramps.

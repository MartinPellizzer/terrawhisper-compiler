Ferocactus glaucus

Ferocactus glaucus, also known as the blue barrel cactus, is a species of columnar cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

Traditionally, Ferocactus glaucus has been used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving digestion, likely due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Ferocactus glaucus include flavonoids, glycosides, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Ferocactus glaucus is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and capsules, often standardized to contain high levels of its bioactive constituents, particularly ferrocactin and isoferrocactin.

What is Trigonella foenum-graecum?

Trigonella foenum-graecum is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Fabaceae, commonly known as fenugreek.

It has a prostrate habit with a woody taproot, reaching a height of up to 1 meter. The leaves are trifoliate, composed of three elliptical leaflets, 2-3 centimeters long, with a grayish-green color, and the inflorescence is a terminal spike with white or pale yellow flowers. The fruit is a pod, 5-10 centimeters long, containing 10-20 seeds, and the plant is native to the Mediterranean region and Asia.

The plant's growth is highly adaptable to different environments, and it is widely cultivated for its seeds, which are used as a spice and in traditional medicine.

The main herbal preparations of Trigonella foenum-graecum are fenugreek tea, an infusion of dried seeds in hot water, used for diabetes management and cholesterol reduction due to its galactomannan content.

Fenugreek capsules, filled with the dried seeds or extract, are available over-the-counter for various health benefits, including weight management and antioxidant activity.

Fenugreek oil, obtained from the seeds through cold-pressing or solvent extraction, is used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine to treat respiratory issues and skin conditions, including eczema and acne.

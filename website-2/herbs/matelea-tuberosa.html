Matelea tuberosa

Matelea tuberosa, also known as the "wild tobacco" or "tobacco waxy waxy", is a perennial climber plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, providing relief from various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Matelea tuberosa include iridoids, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Matelea tuberosa is often prepared as a tincture, tea, or infusion, and is also used in the production of traditional remedies, such as cough syrups and expectorants.

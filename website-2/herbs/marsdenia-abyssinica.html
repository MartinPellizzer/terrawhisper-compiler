Marsdenia abyssinica

Marsdenia abyssinica, also known as Abyssinian moonflower, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apocynaceae, native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic properties. The bioactive constituents of Marsdenia abyssinica include alkaloids, such as vincamine and marsdenine, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is also used to make capsules and tablets for the treatment of various health conditions.

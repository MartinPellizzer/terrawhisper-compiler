Gypsophila tubulosa

Gypsophila tubulosa, also known as baby's breath, is a perennial plant species in the family Caryophyllaceae, characterized by its delicate white or pink flowers and hairy stems.

Bioactive constituents of Gypsophila tubulosa include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal effects.

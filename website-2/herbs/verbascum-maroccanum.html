Verbascum maroccanum

Verbascum maroccanum, also known as Moroccan mullein, is a perennial plant native to Morocco, characterized by its tall spikes of yellow flowers and thick, velvety leaves.

Traditionally, the plant has been used in herbal medicine to treat respiratory issues such as coughs, colds, and bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Verbascum maroccanum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and iridoid glycosides, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In herbal preparations, Verbascum maroccanum is often combined with other plants to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to alleviate respiratory issues and promote relaxation.

Shorea ovalis

Shorea ovalis, also known as the Tengkawang tree, is a species of tree in the family Dipterocarpaceae, native to Southeast Asia and commonly found in the rainforests of Malaysia and Indonesia.

The bark and leaves of Shorea ovalis have been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Shorea ovalis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Shorea ovalis is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture to treat various health conditions, and is also used as a food ingredient in some Southeast Asian cuisines.

Phaseolus multiflorus

Phaseolus multiflorus, also known as the Mexican bean, is a type of wild bean plant native to Mexico and Central America.

The plant is rich in nutrients and offers several health benefits, including reducing blood sugar levels, improving digestion, and acting as an antioxidant. The bioactive constituents of Phaseolus multiflorus include isoflavones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often made into herbal teas, infusions, or tinctures to alleviate symptoms of diabetes, digestive issues, and other health conditions.

classification

Phaseolus multiflorus is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae, commonly known as the kidney bean. This plant is native to the Americas and belongs to the genus Phaseolus, which consists of several species of beans. The classification of Phaseolus multiflorus is as follows: Kingdom: Plantae, Clade: Angiosperms, Clade: Eudicots, Clade: Rosids, Order: Fabales, Family: Fabaceae, Genus: Phaseolus, Species: P. multiflorus. The species is further divided into several cultivars, which are cultivated for their edible beans. Phaseolus multiflorus is closely related to other bean species, such as Phaseolus vulgaris, the common bean.

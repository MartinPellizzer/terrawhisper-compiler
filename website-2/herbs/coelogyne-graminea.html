Coelogyne graminea

Coelogyne graminea, commonly known as the grass-petaled Coelogyne, is a species of orchid native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine, where it is believed to have anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and antimicrobial effects, potentially aiding in the treatment of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. Coelogyne graminea contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often used to make infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, with the most common preparations being a tea or a decoction of the dried roots and stems.

Dalea flavescens

Dalea flavescens, also known as Golden Prairie Clover, is a perennial legume native to the prairies of North America, characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive shape.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making it beneficial for treating various health conditions such as wounds, skin issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Dalea flavescens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used in herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and teas, as well as in topical creams and salves, to support skin health and reduce inflammation.

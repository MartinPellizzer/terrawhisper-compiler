Beccabunga anagallis-aquatica

Beccabunga (Anagallis tenella, previously Anagallis aquatica) is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe, with blue flowers and oval-shaped leaves that grow in shallow water.

The bioactive constituents of Beccabunga include flavonoids, glycosides, and iridoids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Traditionally, Beccabunga has been used to make herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs.

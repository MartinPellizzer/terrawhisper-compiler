What is Calceolaria dentata subsp. cummingiana?

Calceolaria dentata subsp.

cummingiana is a botanical subspecies within the genus Calceolaria, belonging to the family Calceolariaceae. This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive, pendent flowers with a yellow and red striped throat, 2.5-4 cm in length, and a calyx with 5-7 teeth.

The leaves are elliptical or ovate, 1.5-7 cm long and 0.5-2.5 cm wide, with an entire or dentate margin, and are arranged oppositely or in whorls on the stem. The stems are erect, 10-30 cm tall, and covered with simple, glandular hairs.

It is primarily found in the mountainous regions of southern Chile.

Kirkia leandrii

Kirkia leandrii, also known as the Madagascar birch or Madagascar kirkia, is a deciduous tree species endemic to Madagascar, belonging to the family Burseraceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate various ailments such as fever, diarrhea, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Kirkia leandrii include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which have been identified as responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and ointments, which are often used to treat various health conditions in traditional medicine.

Ctenitis maximowicziana

Ctenitis maximowicziana, also known as the Maximowicz's ctenitis fern, is a species of fern in the family Dryopteridaceae.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, contributing to the prevention and treatment of various health conditions such as fever, cough, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Ctenitis maximowicziana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ctenitis maximowicziana is commonly prepared as a tea or infusion, as well as being used in the production of tinctures and extracts, to leverage its health benefits.

classification

Ctenitis maximowicziana is classified within the family Dryopteridaceae, a family of ferns commonly known as the wood fern family. This species is further classified under the genus Ctenitis, which includes approximately 50 species of ferns native to the tropics and subtropics. The family Dryopteridaceae is part of the order Polypodiales, one of the most diverse and widespread orders of ferns. The order Polypodiales is a subset of the class Polypodiopsida, which encompasses over 10,000 species of ferns. The class Polypodiopsida is a division of the kingdom Plantae, which includes all plant species.

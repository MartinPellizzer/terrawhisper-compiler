Gnaphalium mixtum

Gnaphalium mixtum, also known as cudweed, is a type of flowering plant that belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae).

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which can help soothe skin irritations, wounds, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of cudweed include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Cudweed is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and topical creams, often in combination with other herbs like calendula and plantain, to promote wound healing and skin health.

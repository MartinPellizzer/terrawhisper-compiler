Gypsophila vaccaria

Gypsophila vaccaria, also known as Baby's Breath, is a perennial plant native to Europe and Asia, belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and antiseptic effects, which can help to alleviate symptoms of menstrual cramps, digestive issues, and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Gypsophila vaccaria include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Gypsophila vaccaria is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures to support women's health, soothe digestive issues, and promote wound healing.

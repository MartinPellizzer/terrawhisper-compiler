Virga pilosa

Virga pilosa, also known as 'slender combretum', is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa, characterized by its slender branches and small, white flowers.

Traditionally, Virga pilosa has been used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of Virga pilosa include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Virga pilosa is often prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory infections.

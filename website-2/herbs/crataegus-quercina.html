Crataegus quercina

Crataegus quercina, also known as the English hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that belongs to the Rosaceae family.

The health benefits of Crataegus quercina include its potential to lower blood pressure, improve cardiovascular health, and support immune function due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids (quercetin and vitexin), phenolic acids (gallic acid and chlorogenic acid), and triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Crataegus quercina include teas, tinctures, and extracts, often used to promote cardiovascular health and support overall well-being.

classification

Crataegus quercina is classified within the family Rosaceae, which consists of flowering plants commonly known as the rose family. This family encompasses a wide range of species, including roses, apples, pears, and hawthorns. The genus Crataegus, to which Crataegus quercina belongs, is a group of deciduous shrubs and small trees known as hawthorns. The species Crataegus quercina is a type of hawthorn native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is often referred to as Quercus hawthorn or Quercus-leaf hawthorn due to its similarity in leaf appearance to the Quercus genus.

Crataegus quercina, also known as the Quercus hawthorn, has been traditionally used in folk medicine for various purposes. Its leaves and fruits are believed to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of conditions such as fever and digestive issues. The plant's extracts are thought to have a potential role in regulating blood pressure and cholesterol levels. Additionally, some studies suggest that Crataegus quercina may possess antimicrobial properties, which could aid in wound healing. However, more research is needed to fully understand its medicinal uses and potential health benefits.

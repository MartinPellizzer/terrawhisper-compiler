Setaria barbata

Setaria barbata, also known as yellow bristlegrass, is a species of annual grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral activities, making it useful for treating various health conditions such as fever, cough, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Setaria barbata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction or infused in water to make a tea, while the dried leaves and stems are also used to make herbal capsules and tinctures.

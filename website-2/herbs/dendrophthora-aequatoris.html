Dendrophthora aequatoris

Dendrophthora aequatoris is a species of flowering plant in the family Santalaceae, also known as the mistletoe family.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, particularly in the Andean region, where it is believed to possess health benefits such as antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which can help to alleviate respiratory issues, wounds, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Dendrophthora aequatoris include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which have been found to exhibit potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

In herbal medicine, Dendrophthora aequatoris is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat respiratory infections, fever, and other conditions.

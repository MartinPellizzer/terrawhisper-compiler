Salvia veneris

Salvia veneris, also known as the sacred salvia or Greek sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, belonging to the Salvia genus.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in treating various conditions such as digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Salvia veneris include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Salvia veneris is commonly used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, and it is also used as an ingredient in some commercial herbal supplements and topical creams.

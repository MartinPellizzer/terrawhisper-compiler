Maianthemum dahuricum

Maianthemum dahuricum, also known as Dahurian barberry or Korean barberry, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities that can aid in the treatment of various health conditions, such as fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of M. dahuricum include sesquiterpenoids, phenolic acids, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Asian medicine, M. dahuricum is commonly prepared as a decoction, tincture, or tea, and is used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory infections.

classification

Centella cordifolia is a species of flowering plant in the family Apiaceae. It is native to Asia, particularly in India, China, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is also known as Gotu Kola, which is a term derived from the Sinhalese name 'Kavuṇḍu'. Centella cordifolia is classified as a perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves and small white or yellowish flowers. The plant is often found growing in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

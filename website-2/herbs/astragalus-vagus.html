Astragalus vagus

Astragalus vagus, also known as Mongolian milkvetch, is a type of plant that belongs to the legume family and is native to Mongolia and parts of China.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include enhancing immune function, reducing inflammation, and improving cardiovascular health. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus vagus include saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

Astragalus vagus is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and is commonly prepared as a decoction, capsule, or tea, although it can also be found in tincture and extract forms.

classification

Astragalus vagus is a species of legume in the family Fabaceae. It is a type of milkvetch, a genus of plants commonly found in various parts of the world. The classification of Astragalus vagus places it within the subfamily Faboideae and tribe Fabaceae. The plant's classification is further broken down into the kingdom Plantae, phylum Angiospermophyta, and class Magnoliopsida. Astragalus vagus is also known by its synonym, Astragalus psilurus.

Astragalus vagus is used in traditional medicine, particularly in Eastern Europe and Asia, where it's valued for its potential anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties. In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and pneumonia.

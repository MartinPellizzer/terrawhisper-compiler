Gentiana latifolia

Gentiana latifolia, also known as the broad-leaved gentian, is a species of flowering plant in the gentian family that is native to Europe and Western Asia.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include reducing inflammation, soothing digestive issues, and exhibiting antimicrobial and antioxidant activities, contributing to its potential health benefits. The bioactive constituents of Gentiana latifolia include gentiopicrin, gentianine, and amarogentin, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Gentiana latifolia is commonly prepared as a tincture, infusion, or decoction, often combined with other herbs, and is used to support digestive health and relieve symptoms of indigestion and bloating.

Tinospora sinensis

Tinospora sinensis, also known as Guduchi, is a climbing shrub native to the Himalayas and other parts of Asia, belonging to the family Menispermaceae.

The plant is revered for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory effects, making it useful in treating various health conditions such as fever, respiratory infections, and skin diseases. The bioactive constituents of Tinospora sinensis include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic benefits.

In traditional Ayurvedic and Unani medicine, Tinospora sinensis is commonly prepared as a decoction, powder, or tablet, and is also used to make a popular herbal tea known as Guduchi Churan.

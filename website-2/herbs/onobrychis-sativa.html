Onobrychis sativa

Onobrychis sativa, also known as fenugreek, is an annual legume native to the Mediterranean region, prized for its medicinal and culinary properties.

Fenugreek has been traditionally used to support health and wellness due to its numerous health benefits, including the potential to lower blood sugar levels, improve digestion, and alleviate menopausal symptoms. The bioactive constituents of fenugreek include saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids, such as trigonelline and 4-hydroxyisoleucine, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, fenugreek is often prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is commonly used to make teas, capsules, and supplements.

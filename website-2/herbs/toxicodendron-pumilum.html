Toxicodendron pumilum

Toxicodendron pumilum, also known as the dwarf lacquer tree or Japanese poison oak, is a plant species in the Anacardiaceae family, native to East Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal effects, making it beneficial for treating skin conditions such as eczema and acne, as well as digestive issues like diarrhea and dysentery. The bioactive constituents of Toxicodendron pumilum include lacquer, gallic acid, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to make herbal preparations such as tinctures, decoctions, and ointments, which are applied topically to treat skin conditions or taken internally to address digestive issues.

Salsola frutescens

Salsola frutescens, also known as prickly saltwort, is a perennial halophytic shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its dense, prickly foliage and small white flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation, treating digestive issues, and alleviating respiratory problems, due to its rich content of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins. The bioactive constituents of Salsola frutescens have been attributed to its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Salsola frutescens is often prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is used to treat various conditions, including bronchitis, coughs, and skin irritations.

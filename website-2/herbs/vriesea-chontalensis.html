Vriesea chontalensis

Vriesea chontalensis, also known as Chontal, is a species of bromeliad, a type of epiphytic flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Vriesea chontalensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Vriesea chontalensis is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma.

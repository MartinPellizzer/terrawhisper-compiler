Ipomoea atropurpurea

Ipomoea atropurpurea, commonly known as purple passionflower or purple coneflower, is a perennial vine native to North America, belonging to the Ipomoea genus of flowering plants.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its sedative, anti-anxiety, and antispasmodic properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of insomnia, restlessness, and muscle spasms. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea atropurpurea include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, such as icariin, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Purple passionflower is often used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, and is sometimes combined with other herbs in preparations to support sleep, relaxation, and nervous system health.

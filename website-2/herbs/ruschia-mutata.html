Ruschia mutata

Ruschia mutata, also known as the "candy succulent," is a succulent plant species native to South Africa, characterized by its vibrant pink flowers and fleshy, leafy stems.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, offering potential health benefits in wound healing, skin protection, and immune system support. The bioactive constituents of Ruschia mutata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ruschia mutata is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture to harness its medicinal benefits, although further research is needed to fully understand its efficacy and potential applications.

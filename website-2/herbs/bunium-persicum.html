Bunium persicum

Bunium persicum, also known as Persian cumin or black cumin, is a perennial plant in the carrot family (Apiaceae) that is native to the Middle East and South Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat a variety of health conditions, including digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin diseases, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Bunium persicum include essential oils, such as thymoquinone and alpha-pinene, as well as flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Bunium persicum is commonly prepared as a herbal tea, oil, or powder, and is used in traditional medicine to treat a range of health issues, as well as being used as a food spice.

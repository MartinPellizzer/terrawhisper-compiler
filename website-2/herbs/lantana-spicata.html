Lantana spicata

Lantana spicata, also known as buttonweed or white sage, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America, characterized by its clusters of small, white or yellow flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its numerous health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, which can aid in treating wounds, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Lantana spicata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Lantana spicata is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is also used in traditional remedies for its antipyretic and expectorant effects.

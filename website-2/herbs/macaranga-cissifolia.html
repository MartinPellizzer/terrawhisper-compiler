Macaranga cissifolia

Macaranga cissifolia, also known as the Chinese staff tree or five-finger macaranga, is a species of plant in the family Euphorbiaceae, native to Southeast Asia.

The bioactive constituents of Macaranga cissifolia include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tonics, as well as being incorporated into salves and creams for topical applications.

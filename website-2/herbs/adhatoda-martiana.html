Adhatoda martiana

Adhatoda martiana, also known as Adhatoda or Adulsa, is a plant species belonging to the family Acanthaceae, native to tropical regions of Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with health benefits including the treatment of respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma due to its expectorant and bronchodilatory effects. The bioactive constituents of Adhatoda martiana include alkaloids, particularly vasicine and vasicinone, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Adhatoda martiana is commonly used to prepare herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and dry extracts, as well as being incorporated into various Ayurvedic and Unani formulations.

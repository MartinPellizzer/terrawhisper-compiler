Ladanum dubium

Ladanum dubium, also known as Spurge Laurel or Daphne, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region with fragrant, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties, including its ability to reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms associated with anxiety and depression, thanks to its anxiolytic and sedative effects. The bioactive constituents of Ladanum dubium include volatile oils, particularly bornyl acetate and ladanum, as well as flavonoids and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Ladanum dubium is commonly used in herbal preparations, such as essential oils, extracts, and tinctures, often blended with other herbs to create sedative and antidepressant formulas.

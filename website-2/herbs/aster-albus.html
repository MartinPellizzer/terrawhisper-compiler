Aster albus

Aster albus, also known as White Aster, is a perennial herb belonging to the Asteraceae family, traditionally used in herbal medicine for its medicinal properties.

The plant has been found to possess various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiviral activities, which may help alleviate symptoms of colds, fever, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Aster albus include flavonoids, sesquiterpenes, and triterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Aster albus include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to treat respiratory issues, such as coughs and bronchitis.

This plant is often found in dry, sandy areas and along roadsides.

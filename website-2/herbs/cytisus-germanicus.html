Cytisus germanicus

Cytisus germanicus, also known as German broom, is a shrub-like legume native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its yellow flowers and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including the treatment of gastrointestinal issues, skin conditions, and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Cytisus germanicus contains bioactive constituents such as isoflavones, saponins, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Cytisus germanicus is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or infundibuliform infusion, often used to treat digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing.

Pinus orizabensis

Pinus orizabensis, also known as Oaxacan pine, is a species of evergreen coniferous tree native to Mexico, characterized by its distinctive needle-like leaves and small, edible seeds.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiseptic properties, which can aid in the treatment of various conditions such as wounds, respiratory issues, and digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of Pinus orizabensis include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is commonly used to make infusions, teas, and extracts, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, as well as being used in traditional remedies for coughs and colds.

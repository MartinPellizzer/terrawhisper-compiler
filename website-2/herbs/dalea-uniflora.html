Dalea uniflora

Dalea uniflora, also known as the pink or pink prairie clover, is a perennial legume native to North America.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including its potential to lower blood sugar levels, improve blood pressure, and exhibit anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Dalea uniflora include isoflavones, saponins, and other flavonoids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Dalea uniflora is often made into teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to support cardiovascular health and alleviate symptoms associated with menopause.

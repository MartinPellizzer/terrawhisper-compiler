Dendrobium sclerophyllum

Dendrobium sclerophyllum, also known as the hard-leaved Dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. Bioactive constituents of Dendrobium sclerophyllum include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include infusions, decoctions, and extracts, which are often used to promote overall health and well-being.

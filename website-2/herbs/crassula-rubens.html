Crassula rubens

Crassula rubens, also known as the red jade or succulent stonecrop, is a species of succulent plant in the family Crassulaceae.

The health benefits of Crassula rubens include its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as digestive issues, skin conditions, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Crassula rubens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Crassula rubens include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to promote digestive health, reduce inflammation, and support the immune system.

classification

Crassula rubens is classified as a succulent species within the family Crassulaceae. This family is a part of the order Saxifragales, which includes other flowering plants like saxifrage and heath. The order Saxifragales is further grouped under the subclass Rosidae, which comprises various flowering plant families. Within the subclass Rosidae, the class Magnoliopsida is the overarching category for flowering plants. At the highest taxonomic level, Crassula rubens falls under the kingdom Plantae.

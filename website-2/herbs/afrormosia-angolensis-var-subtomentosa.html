What is Afrormosia angolensis var. subtomentosa?

Afrormosia angolensis var. subtomentosa is a variety of tree native to tropical Africa, belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is a deciduous tree with a straight bole, smooth, grayish-brown bark and a flat, rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, pinnate and 10-20 cm long, with 4-8 leaflets, each 4-12 cm long, elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green color. The flowers are small, yellowish and appear in axillary racemes; the fruit is a pod, 8-12 cm long, flat and brown when ripe.

The wood of this tree is highly valued for its fine grain and is used for furniture making and other wood products.

What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

The most important healing constituents of Afrormosia angolensis var. subtomentosa are sesquiterpenes such as angolansin and afrormosin which exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The presence of flavonoids like quercetin and kaempferol has been reported, which are known for their antimicrobial and anti-cancer activities. The timber of this tree also contains phenolic compounds like vanillin and eugenol, which possess analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the essential oil obtained from the heartwood contains sesquiterpenes and phenolic compounds that exhibit antimicrobial and insecticidal activities.

These constituents are responsible for the medicinal properties of Afrormosia angolensis var. subtomentosa, which makes it a valuable resource for traditional medicine.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Afrormosia angolensis var. subtomentosa are tincture, infusion, tea, afrormosia oil, and essential oil.

Afrormosia oil is a carrier oil made by cold-pressing the seeds or bark, which can be used to treat skin conditions and as a natural anti-inflammatory.

Essential oil, obtained through steam distillation of the bark and leaves, is used for its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

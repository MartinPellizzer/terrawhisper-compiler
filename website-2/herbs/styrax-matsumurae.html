Styrax matsumurae

Styrax matsumurae, also known as Japanese styrax or Japanese storax, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia, characterized by its fragrant resin and aromatic wood.

The health benefits of Styrax matsumurae include its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and expectorant properties, which can help alleviate respiratory issues such as bronchitis and coughs. The bioactive constituents of this plant include styrax, a phenolic compound with antimicrobial and antioxidant activities, as well as other compounds like beta-sitosterol and oleanolic acid.

In traditional medicine, Styrax matsumurae is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are commonly used to treat respiratory and gastrointestinal issues.

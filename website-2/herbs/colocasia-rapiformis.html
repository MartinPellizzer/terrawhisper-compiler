Colocasia rapiformis

Colocasia rapiformis, also known as Elephant Ear, is a tropical plant species belonging to the Arum family, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and underground rhizomes.

The plant has been utilized in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which are believed to aid in the treatment of various conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Colocasia rapiformis include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Colocasia rapiformis is commonly prepared as a decoction or a paste, often used to treat fever, skin conditions, and respiratory problems.

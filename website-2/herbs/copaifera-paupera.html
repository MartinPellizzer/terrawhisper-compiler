Copaifera paupera

Copaifera paupera is a species of tree native to the Amazon rainforest, belonging to the family Fabaceae, commonly known as copaiba or paupera copaiba tree.

The resin extracted from Copaifera paupera has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic properties, providing relief from various health issues such as wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Copaifera paupera resin include diterpenes, particularly labdane diterpenes and prenylated labdane diterpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Copaifera paupera is often used in herbal preparations such as creams, ointments, and tinctures to promote wound healing, reduce inflammation, and alleviate pain.

Sida cordata

Sida cordata is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family, commonly known as the cottonweed or heartleaf sida.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and skin disorders, due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Sida cordata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Sida cordata is often used to make decoctions, infusions, and poultices, as well as being incorporated into Ayurvedic formulations such as churnas and asavas, to take advantage of its therapeutic benefits.

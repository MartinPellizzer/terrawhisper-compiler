Gymnosporia rhombifolia

Gymnosporia rhombifolia, also known as the bitter apple, is a plant species in the Celastraceae family, native to the tropical regions of Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin infections, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of Gymnosporia rhombifolia include flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion, and also used to make a bitter tonic, which is believed to aid in digestion and detoxification.

Eleocharis fuscopurpurea

Eleocharis fuscopurpurea, commonly known as purple spike-rush, is a perennial sedge that belongs to the family Cyperaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, as well as its potential to aid in digestion and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Eleocharis fuscopurpurea include phenolic compounds, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to support immune function and alleviate symptoms of coughs and colds.

classification

Eleocharis fuscopurpurea is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family Cyperaceae. It belongs to the genus Eleocharis, which consists of approximately 100 species of sedges. The genus Eleocharis is part of the larger family Cyperaceae, which contains over 5,000 species of sedges and rushes. Eleocharis fuscopurpurea is further classified as belonging to the tribe Cyperaeae and the subfamily Cyperoideae. The plant's classification is also reflected in its binomial nomenclature, with the full classification being Eleocharis fuscopurpurea (Kunth) T.Koyama.

Eleocharis fuscopurpurea has been found to have medicinal uses, particularly in traditional medicine.

Codonopsis hirsuta

Codonopsis hirsuta, also known as Dongdong'ea or Hairy Codonopsis, is a perennial plant species native to the mountainous regions of China.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis, coughs, and other respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Codonopsis hirsuta include saponins, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, such as codonopsin and 6-hydroxodongdong'ea saponin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Codonopsis hirsuta is commonly prepared as a decoction, tea, or capsule, and is often used in combination with other herbs to promote respiratory health and boost the immune system.

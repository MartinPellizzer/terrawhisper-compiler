Urtica microphylla

Urtica microphylla, also known as dwarf nettle or small nettle, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties, which have been attributed to its potential health benefits in treating conditions such as arthritis, allergies, and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Urtica microphylla include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Urtica microphylla include teas, infusions, and topical creams, which are often used to relieve pain and reduce inflammation in the body.

In more severe cases, exposure to the plant can cause anaphylaxis, a life-threatening allergic reaction. It's essential to exercise caution when handling Urtica microphylla, as the risk of adverse reactions can be significant.

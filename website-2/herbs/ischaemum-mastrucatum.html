Ischaemum mastrucatum

Ischaemum mastrucatum, also known as Chrysopogon, is a type of perennial grass native to Australia and Southeast Asia, valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

The health benefits of this plant include its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as fever, cold, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Ischaemum mastrucatum include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional medicine, Ischaemum mastrucatum is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and essential oils, which are used to alleviate respiratory and gastrointestinal issues.

Cocculus acutus

Cocculus acutus, also known as the Chinese aralium or devil's claw vine, is a perennial climber native to China and Southeast Asia.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of arthritis, digestive issues, and anxiety. The bioactive constituents of Cocculus acutus include alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Cocculus acutus is often prepared as a decoction or tincture and is also used in the form of capsules or tablets, with the most common herbal preparations including capsules and tablets, as well as liquid extracts.

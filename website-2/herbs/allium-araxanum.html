Allium araxanum

Allium araxanum, also known as the Armenian garlic or Araxys garlic, is a plant species belonging to the Allium genus, characterized by its distinctive garlic-like flavor and aroma.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include sulfur-containing compounds such as alliin, allicin, and diallyl disulfide, as well as polyphenolic compounds like flavonoids and phenolic acids.

Allium araxanum is commonly used in herbal preparations, including tea, capsules, and tinctures, to support cardiovascular health and alleviate symptoms of respiratory tract infections.

Apium anisum

Apium anisum, commonly known as anise or sweet cumin, is a biennial herb belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

It has been used for centuries in traditional medicine due to its numerous health benefits, including the relief of digestive issues, such as bloating and indigestion, as well as its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties, which can aid in soothing coughs and reducing fever. The bioactive constituents of anise include anethole, limonene, and beta-pinene, which are responsible for its medicinal properties and distinctive flavor.

Anise is often used in herbal preparations, such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, and is also a popular ingredient in various sweet treats, like candies and baked goods, particularly in Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cuisine.

What is Faberia silhetensis subsp. bhutanica?

Faberia silhetensis subsp.

bhutanica is an endemic plant species native to Bhutan, characterized by its annual habit and herbaceous growth. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, a large and diverse group of flowering plants.

The plant features simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins, typically 5-10 cm in length, and an inflorescence consisting of radiate flower heads with 20-40 ray florets and 100-150 disc florets. The florets are white or pale yellow in color, and the plant reaches a height of 30-60 cm. Faberia silhetensis subsp.

bhutanica is a member of the Faberia genus, which is closely related to the genus Nardophyllum.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

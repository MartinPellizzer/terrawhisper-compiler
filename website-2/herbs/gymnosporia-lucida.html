Gymnosporia lucida

Gymnosporia lucida is a plant species, commonly known as the glossy spine-flowered or lucid spiny woodbine, which is native to tropical Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties, which may aid in the treatment of various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and skin infections. Bioactive constituents of Gymnosporia lucida include triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is prepared into herbal remedies such as teas, infusions, and decoctions, which are used to treat various health conditions.

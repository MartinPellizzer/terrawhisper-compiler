Styrax pedicellatus

Styrax pedicellatus, also known as the Indian storax tree or styrax, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayan region and parts of Eastern Asia, belonging to the family Styracaceae.

The tree's bark and resin have been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, which help to treat various health issues such as wounds, skin infections, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Styrax pedicellatus include sesquiterpenoids, phenolic acids, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Styrax pedicellatus include ointments, salves, and tinctures, which are used topically to treat skin conditions and wounds, and orally to treat respiratory issues and infections.

Centaurium latifolium

Centaurium latifolium, commonly known as large knapweed or broad-leaved centaury, is a perennial plant species belonging to the gentian family (Gentianaceae).

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including the treatment of respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma due to its expectorant properties, as well as its use as an anti-inflammatory and antiseptic agent. The bioactive constituents of C. latifolium include gentiopicrin, gentianine, and other iridoid glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, C. latifolium is often prepared as a tincture, infusion, or syrup, and is used to treat various health conditions, including respiratory issues, digestive problems, and skin conditions.

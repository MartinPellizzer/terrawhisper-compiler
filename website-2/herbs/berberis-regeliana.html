Berberis regeliana

Berberis regeliana, also known as the Russian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Eastern Asia, belonging to the Berberidaceae family.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with health benefits including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anti-cancer properties, which have been traditionally used to treat digestive issues, reduce fever, and alleviate symptoms of various infections. The bioactive constituents of Berberis regeliana include alkaloids such as berberine, berbamine, and palmitine, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

Berberis regeliana is often used to prepare herbal teas, tinctures, and extracts, which are commonly used in traditional medicine to support immune function and digestive health.

Volutaria sinaica

Volutaria sinaica, also known as the Egyptian thistle or Sinaica thistle, is a plant species in the sunflower family that is native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which are believed to aid in the treatment of digestive issues, skin conditions, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Volutaria sinaica include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Volutaria sinaica is often made into teas, infusions, or tinctures, and is also used in topical creams and ointments to treat skin conditions and wounds.

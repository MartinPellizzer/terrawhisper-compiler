Lappula squarrosa

Lappula squarrosa, also known as the prickly bird's-beak or bird's-eye gromwell, is a perennial plant species belonging to the borage family (Boraginaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which can aid in wound healing and reducing symptoms of respiratory issues like bronchitis. Bioactive constituents of Lappula squarrosa include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Lappula squarrosa is commonly used in teas, infusions, and tinctures to promote respiratory health and alleviate coughs, as well as in topical preparations to aid in wound healing and skin conditions.

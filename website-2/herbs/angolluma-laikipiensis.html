Angolluma laikipiensis

Angolluma laikipiensis, also known as Kaya or African Milk Tree, is a succulent plant species native to East Africa, characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and milky sap.

The bioactive constituents of Angolluma laikipiensis include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or ointment, with the most common herbal preparations including a paste made from the plant's sap, which is applied topically to wounds and skin conditions.

Artemisia sericea

Artemisia sericea, also known as Silver Wormwood or Silky Wormwood, is a perennial herb belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small yellow flowers.

The plant is valued for its health benefits, which include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, making it useful in treating digestive issues, skin infections, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Artemisia sericea include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat digestive issues, colds, and flu, as well as to promote relaxation and reduce inflammation.

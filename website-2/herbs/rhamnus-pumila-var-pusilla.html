What is Rhamnus pumila var. pusilla?

Rhamnus pumila var. pusilla is a shrub or small tree in the family Rhamnaceae, native to eastern Asia.

It has a woody stem with brown bark, and elliptical or ovate leaves, 1-3 cm long, with a glabrous or sparsely hairy surface and a cuneate base. The leaves are opposite and sometimes whorled, and have a serrate margin. The inflorescence is a corymb or a raceme, with greenish-yellow flowers, 5-7 mm in diameter, and 4-5 sepals.

The fruit is a blue-black drupe, 4-6 mm in diameter, with a single seed.

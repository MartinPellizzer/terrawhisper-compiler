Carum sylvestre

Carum sylvestre, also known as ajwain or bishop's weed, is a plant species belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is native to the Middle East and South Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with health benefits including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and carminative effects, which can help alleviate digestive issues and reduce stress. The bioactive constituents of Carum sylvestre include essential oils, terpenoids, and alkaloids, such as carvone, limonene, and ajwainine, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Carum sylvestre include seeds that can be used to make teas, infusions, and oil extracts, which are used to treat various health conditions, including respiratory issues and digestive problems.

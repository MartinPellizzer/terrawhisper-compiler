Myristica grandifolia

Myristica grandifolia, also known as the Malabar nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Myristica grandifolia include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the tree is often used to make herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and syrups, as well as being used in combination with other herbs to treat various health conditions.

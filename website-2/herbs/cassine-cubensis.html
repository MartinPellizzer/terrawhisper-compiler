Cassine cubensis

Cassine cubensis, also known as Guayacan or Palo de Vaca Muerta, is a small tree or shrub native to Cuba and other Caribbean islands, belonging to the family Celastraceae.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include its potential to reduce inflammation, lower blood pressure, and exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant activities, contributing to its use in traditional medicine to alleviate various health issues. The bioactive constituents of Cassine cubensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, such as isorhapontigenin and 2'-hydroxy-4'-methoxychalcone, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Cassine cubensis is commonly prepared as an infusion, decoction, or tincture, and is used to treat conditions such as fever, respiratory infections, and digestive issues.

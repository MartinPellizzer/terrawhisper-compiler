Thuja obtusa

Thuja obtusa, also known as Japanese arborvitae or Oriental arborvitae, is a species of evergreen coniferous tree native to East Asia.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties, which may help to reduce fever, alleviate respiratory issues, and support immune function. The bioactive constituents of Thuja obtusa include thujone, bornyl acetate, and other essential oils, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Thuja obtusa include teas, infusions, and topical ointments, which are often used to treat respiratory conditions, skin issues, and wounds.

Cereus bradypus

Cereus bradypus, also known as the slow cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains in South America, belonging to the genus Cereus.

The bioactive constituents of Cereus bradypus include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

Traditionally, Cereus bradypus is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to soothe digestive issues, reduce fever, and promote overall well-being.

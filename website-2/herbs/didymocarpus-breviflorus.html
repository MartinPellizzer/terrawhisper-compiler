Didymocarpus breviflorus

Didymocarpus breviflorus, also known as the Himalayan misopogon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Gesneriaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, cough, and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and expectorant properties, which can help to soothe the throat and relieve congestion. The bioactive constituents of Didymocarpus breviflorus include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Didymocarpus breviflorus is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or powder, and is used to make cough syrups, expectorant tonics, and respiratory-supporting blends.

Eucalyptus virella

Eucalyptus virella, also known as Lemon-scented Gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to eastern Australia, characterized by its distinctive lemon-like fragrance and fast-growing properties.

The bioactive constituents of Eucalyptus virella include eucalyptol, a compound with potent antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects, as well as other volatile oils such as alpha-pinene and beta-pinene.

In herbal medicine, Eucalyptus virella is commonly used to make teas, tinctures, and topical creams, which are used to treat respiratory issues, skin conditions, and wounds.

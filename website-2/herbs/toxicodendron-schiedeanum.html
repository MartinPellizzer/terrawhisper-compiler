Toxicodendron schiedeanum

Toxicodendron schiedeanum, also known as the Schiede's poison oak, is a plant species belonging to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other toxic plants such as poison oak and poison ivy.

The bioactive constituents of Toxicodendron schiedeanum include anacardic acids, which are responsible for its toxic and medicinal properties, as well as other compounds like flavonoids and phenolic acids.

Traditional herbal preparations made from this plant include tinctures, decoctions, and salves, which are used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and other health issues, although its use should be approached with caution due to its potential for skin irritation and allergic reactions.

Mallotus japonicus

Mallotus japonicus, also known as Chinese timber tree or Japanese mallotus, is a deciduous tree species native to East Asia.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions. Mallotus japonicus contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is often used to make herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat fever, cough, and other respiratory issues.

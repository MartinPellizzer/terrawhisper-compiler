Satureja sylvatica

Satureja sylvatica, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

The bioactive constituents of mountain savory include carvacrol, thymol, and rosmarinic acid, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Mountain savory is often used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat digestive issues, colds, and flu, as well as to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

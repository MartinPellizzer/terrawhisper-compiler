Silybum mariae

Silybum marianum, commonly known as milk thistle, is a flowering plant in the thistle family native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including liver protection, antioxidant properties, and anti-inflammatory effects, which may aid in the treatment of liver diseases, such as hepatitis and cirrhosis. The bioactive constituents of milk thistle include silymarin, a flavonoid complex composed of silibinin, silidianin, and silicristin, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

Milk thistle is commonly prepared as a supplement in capsule or tablet form, as well as an herbal tea or tincture, and is often used in combination with other herbs to support liver health and promote detoxification.

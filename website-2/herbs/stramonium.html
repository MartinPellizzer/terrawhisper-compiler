Stramonium

Stramonium, also known as Jimsonweed or Datura stramonium, is a poisonous, annual or biennial plant in the nightshade family that produces large, trumpet-shaped flowers and edible seeds.

The health benefits of Stramonium include its use as an antispasmodic and anticholinergic agent, which can help alleviate symptoms of asthma, bronchitis, and other respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Stramonium include scopolamine, hyoscyamine, and atropine, which are tropane alkaloids responsible for its medicinal and toxic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Stramonium include tinctures and extracts, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, as well as skin and eye conditions, although its use should be strictly regulated due to its toxicity.

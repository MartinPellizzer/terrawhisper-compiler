Psoralea alata

Psoralea alata, also known as Indian bread or Indian pea, is a perennial herb belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

Traditionally, it has been used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including skin diseases, ulcers, and digestive issues, due to its antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Psoralea alata include coumarins, flavonoids, and isoflavones, which have been shown to possess antioxidant, anti-cancer, and anti-aging activities.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, capsules, and topical creams, which are used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and inflammation.

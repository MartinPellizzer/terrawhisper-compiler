Piper pumilum

Piper pumilum, also known as the West Indian kava or small kava, is a plant species in the Piperaceae family, native to the Caribbean and Central America.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its ability to reduce anxiety and stress, alleviate pain, and act as an anti-inflammatory agent. The bioactive constituents of Piper pumilum include kavalactones, specifically kawain and yangonin, as well as other alkaloids and glycosides.

The most common herbal preparations made with Piper pumilum include kava extracts, capsules, and teas, which are used to support relaxation, improve sleep quality, and provide relief from muscle tension and inflammation.

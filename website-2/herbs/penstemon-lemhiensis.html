Penstemon lemhiensis

Penstemon lemhiensis, also known as Lemhi penstemon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae, native to the western United States.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in treating wounds, reducing fever, and soothing respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Penstemon lemhiensis include iridoids, phenolic acids, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to treat colds, coughs, and other respiratory ailments.

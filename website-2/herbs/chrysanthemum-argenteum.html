Chrysanthemum argenteum

Chrysanthemum argenteum, also known as Silver-flowered Chrysanthemum or Yín Chén, is a flowering plant species native to East Asia, belonging to the daisy family Asteraceae.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and immunomodulatory properties, which may help alleviate fever, colds, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Chrysanthemum argenteum include flavonoids, such as rutin and quercetin, as well as sesquiterpenes and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make various herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat a range of health conditions.

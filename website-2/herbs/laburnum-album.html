Laburnum album

The Laburnum album, commonly known as the common laburnum or golden rain, is a flowering plant species native to Europe and Western Asia, belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and antiseptic properties, offering potential health benefits such as relieving gastrointestinal disorders and reducing pain. Laburnum album contains bioactive constituents like laburnine, a toxic alkaloid with potential antiseptic properties, as well as flavonoids and coumarins.

The most common herbal preparations made with Laburnum album include dried flowers and leaves, which are used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, although caution should be exercised due to the plant's toxicity and potential health risks.

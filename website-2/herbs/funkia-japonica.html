Funkia japonica

Funkia japonica, also known as Pueraria montana var. lobata, is a perennial legume native to East Asia, commonly referred to as kudzu or Japanese knotweed.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving cardiovascular health, and exhibiting antioxidant properties, which may aid in the prevention of various diseases such as atherosclerosis and cancer. The bioactive constituents of Funkia japonica include isoflavones, particularly daidzein and genistein, as well as other compounds like quercetin, kaempferol, and puerarin, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is often processed into various herbal preparations, such as decoctions, infusions, and pills, which are used to treat conditions like fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal disorders.

Coleus congestus

Coleus congestus, also known as Blue Pimpernel, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Lamiaceae family, commonly found in tropical regions.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities, contributing to its potential in treating various health conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and skin infections. Bioactive constituents of Coleus congestus include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to prepare infusions, teas, and tinctures, with the most common herbal preparations being dried leaves and leaf extracts used to treat fever, wounds, and digestive issues.

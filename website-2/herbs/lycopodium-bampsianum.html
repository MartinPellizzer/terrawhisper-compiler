Lycopodium bampsianum

Lycopodium bampsianum, also known as Bamps' club moss, is a species of extinct lycopod that has been recently rediscovered in the Democratic Republic of Congo.

The health benefits of Lycopodium bampsianum include its potential to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it a promising natural remedy for various ailments. The bioactive constituents of this plant include alkaloids, such as lycopodine and palustreine, as well as other compounds like sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids.

Traditionally, Lycopodium bampsianum is used in herbal preparations, including infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, wounds, and skin conditions.

Freesia angolensis

Freesia angolensis, also known as Redibo, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa, belonging to the family Iridaceae.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Freesia angolensis is commonly used in traditional medicine and is often prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, as well as being used in the form of capsules or tablets, to harness its health benefits.

Malva maritima

Malva maritima, also known as Sea Mallow, is a perennial plant that belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which contribute to its potential health benefits, including soothing digestive issues and reducing inflammation. The bioactive constituents of Malva maritima include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and mucilages, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Malva maritima include teas, infusions, and infusco-extracts, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs.

Turrita ochroleuca

Turrita ochroleuca, also known as Turritis glabra or Mountain Rocket, is a perennial plant species belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiviral properties, which are associated with its potential health benefits, including wound healing, pain relief, and immune system support. The bioactive constituents of Turrita ochroleuca include glucosinolates, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Turrita ochroleuca is often used to prepare tinctures, infusions, and salves, which are used topically to treat skin conditions and wounds.

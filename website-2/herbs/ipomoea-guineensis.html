Ipomoea guineensis

Ipomoea guineensis, also known as Guinea morning glory, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa, belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with health benefits including the potential treatment of fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, as well as its use as an anti-inflammatory and antioxidant agent. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea guineensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is commonly used to prepare infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various ailments and promote overall health and well-being.

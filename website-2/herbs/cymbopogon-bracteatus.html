Cymbopogon bracteatus

Cymbopogon bracteatus, also known as the lemongrass, is a tropical grass species native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its long, slender leaves and citrusy aroma.

The essential oil extracted from its leaves has been used for centuries in traditional medicine due to its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can aid in reducing pain, fighting off infections, and protecting against cell damage. The bioactive constituents of Cymbopogon bracteatus include citral, geraniol, and limonene, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, lemongrass is commonly prepared as a tea, oil, or tincture, and is often used to alleviate digestive issues, reduce stress and anxiety, and promote a good night's sleep.

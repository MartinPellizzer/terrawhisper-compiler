Verbascum jordanicum

Verbascum jordanicum, also known as Jordan's mullein, is a biennial plant species belonging to the mullein genus, characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, yellow flowers.

The plant has been utilized in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and expectorant properties, which may help alleviate respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Verbascum jordanicum include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues and promote wound healing.

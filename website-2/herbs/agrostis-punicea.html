Agrostis punicea

Agrostis punicea, also known as redtop, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and Western Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of arthritis, improve wound healing, and protect against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Agrostis punicea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Agrostis punicea is typically prepared as a tea, infusion, or decoction, and is often used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, as well as to soothe digestive problems.

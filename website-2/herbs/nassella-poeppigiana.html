Nassella poeppigiana

Nassella poeppigiana, also known as Stipa neae or Chile needle grass, is a type of perennial grass native to the Andes mountain range in South America.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and expectorant properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Nassella poeppigiana include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Nassella poeppigiana is often prepared as an infusion, tincture, or decoction, and is used to treat conditions such as coughs, colds, and bronchitis.

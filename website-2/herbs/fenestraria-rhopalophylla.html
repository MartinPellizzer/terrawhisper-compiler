Fenestraria rhopalophylla

Fenestraria rhopalophylla, commonly known as the fenestraria or baby toad plant, is a small, globular succulent plant native to the Namib Desert in southern Africa.

The bioactive constituents of Fenestraria rhopalophylla include flavonoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, as well as other compounds like phenolic acids and saponins.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often dried and made into capsules or teas, or used in topical preparations to harness its potential benefits.

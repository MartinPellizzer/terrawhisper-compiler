Cedrus atlantica

Cedrus atlantica, also known as the Atlas cedar, is a coniferous tree native to the Atlas Mountains in Morocco.

The resin of Cedrus atlantica has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, which may help to alleviate respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as wounds and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Cedrus atlantica include cedrol, atlantone, and cedrene, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The resin of Cedrus atlantica is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as essential oils, tinctures, and ointments, which are used to treat a variety of health issues, including respiratory problems and skin conditions.

Sclarea tingitana

Sclarea tingitana, also known as Tansy, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its fern-like foliage and small, yellow flowers.

The health benefits of Tansy include its ability to act as an anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiparasitic agent, making it useful in treating digestive issues, reducing fever, and relieving pain. The bioactive constituents of Tansy include sesquiterpene lactones, particularly tanacetone and isobutyl angelate, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Tansy is used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as in topical creams and ointments to treat skin conditions and wounds.

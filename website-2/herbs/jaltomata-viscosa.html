Jaltomata viscosa

Jaltomata viscosa, also known as sticky nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family (Solanaceae) that is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat a variety of health issues, including fever, colds, and respiratory problems, due to its reported antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms and promote recovery. Bioactive constituents of Jaltomata viscosa include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, such as jaltomoside and isovaleryl-caffeate, which are believed to contribute to the plant's medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Jaltomata viscosa is often used to make infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are consumed to treat various health conditions, although its efficacy and safety have not been extensively scientifically evaluated.

Pinus apollinis

Pinus apollinis, also known as the Greek white pine, is a species of pine tree native to the mountains of Greece and Turkey.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, making it beneficial for treating respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as for its potential in lowering cholesterol levels and reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease. The bioactive constituents of Pinus apollinis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, which contribute to its health-promoting properties.

In traditional Greek medicine, Pinus apollinis is commonly prepared as a tea or an essential oil, and is also used in the production of resin-based herbal supplements and cough drops.

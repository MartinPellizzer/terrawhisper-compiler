Solidago viscosa

Solidago viscosa, also known as sticky goldenrod, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its yellow flowers and sticky resinous leaves.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic effects, which may help alleviate symptoms of colds, fever, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Solidago viscosa include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic activities.

Solidago viscosa is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and salves, which are employed to treat respiratory issues, wounds, and skin conditions.

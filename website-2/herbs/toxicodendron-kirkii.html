side effects

Toxicodendron kirkii, commonly known as the New Zealand lacquer tree or New Zealand varnish tree, contains urushiol, a resinous oil that causes severe allergic reactions and skin irritation in some individuals. When the sap of the plant comes into contact with the skin, it can cause redness, itching, swelling, and blistering. In severe cases, exposure to the sap can lead to anaphylaxis, a life-threatening allergic reaction. People who have a history of allergies or have previously been exposed to urushiol-containing plants may be more susceptible to the side effects of Toxicodendron kirkii. It is essential to exercise caution and wear protective clothing when handling the plant to avoid any potential harm.

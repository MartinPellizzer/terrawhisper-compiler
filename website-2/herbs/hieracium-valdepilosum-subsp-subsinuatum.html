What is Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. subsinuatum?

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

subsinuatum is a subspecies of Hieracium, belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is an annual or biennial herb, with erect stems that reach 10-40 cm in height, bearing leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, and 2-7 cm in length, with ciliate margins and a pilose surface.

The inflorescence is a corymb, bearing 3-10 yellow or yellowish flowers, each 15-25 mm in diameter, with 10-20 florets, and a capitulum that is 10-20 mm in diameter. The involucre is a glabrous, tubular structure, 1-2 mm in length, with 3-5 bracts, and a corolla that is tubular, with 5 teeth, and 5 anthers, each with a distinct appendage.

The achenes are obovate in shape, 5-7 mm in length, and have a few short cilia at the base.

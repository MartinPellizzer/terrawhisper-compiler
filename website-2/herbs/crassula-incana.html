Crassula incana

Crassula incana, also known as the tree crassula or silver jade plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its small, white flowers and silvery-green, fleshy leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including treating fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues. Crassula incana is rich in bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

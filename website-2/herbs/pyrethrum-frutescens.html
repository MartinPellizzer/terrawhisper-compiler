classification

Pyrethrum frutescens is classified as a member of the daisy family (Asteraceae). It belongs to the genus Chrysanthemum, although it is sometimes considered a separate genus, Pyrethrum. The species is native to Australia and New Zealand. It is a flowering plant that grows as a shrub or small tree. Pyrethrum frutescens is often used as a source of pyrethrum, a natural insecticide.

active constituents

Pyrethrum frutescens contains active constituents known as pyrethrins, which are a mixture of esters including pyrethrin I and II, and cinerin I and II. These pyrethrins have insecticidal and acaricidal properties, making them effective against a wide range of pests. They work by disrupting the nervous system of insects, ultimately leading to their death. The unique combination of pyrethrins in Pyrethrum frutescens is responsible for its potent insecticidal activity. These compounds are also used in various insecticide products due to their natural origin and relatively low toxicity to mammals.

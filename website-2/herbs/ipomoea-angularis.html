Ipomoea angularis

Ipomoea angularis, also known as the Japanese morning glory, is a perennial climbing plant native to East Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it useful in treating various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea angularis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are commonly used to treat fever, digestive problems, and as a diuretic.

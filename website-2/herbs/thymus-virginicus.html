Thymus virginicus

Thymus virginicus, also known as Wild Thyme, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae) that is native to North America.

The health benefits of Wild Thyme include its antimicrobial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate respiratory issues such as coughs and colds, as well as soothe digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of Wild Thyme include thymol, carvacrol, and rosmarinic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Wild Thyme is commonly used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and salves, making it a popular choice for natural remedies and holistic health practices.

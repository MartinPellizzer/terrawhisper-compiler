Medicago gladiata

Medicago gladiata, also known as gladiolus medic, is a perennial legume plant that belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive gladiolus-like flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used to support overall health and well-being, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to reduce inflammation, improve cardiovascular health, and protect against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Medicago gladiata include isoflavones, phenolic compounds, and saponins, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Medicago gladiata is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or dried extract, and is often used to support immune function, reduce menopausal symptoms, and promote digestive health.

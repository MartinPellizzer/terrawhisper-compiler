Pipturus albidus

Pipturus albidus, also known as Kawakawa in Hawaiian, is a plant species within the nettle family (Urticaceae) native to Polynesia and Southeast Asia.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and lignans, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Hawaiian medicine, Pipturus albidus is often used to make tea, tinctures, and infusions, which are commonly used to treat various ailments, including fever, colds, and respiratory issues.

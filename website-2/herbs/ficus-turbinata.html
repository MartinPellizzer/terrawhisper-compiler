Ficus turbinata

Ficus turbinata, also known as the wheel-bearing fig, is a deciduous tree species native to Asia and the Pacific Islands, characterized by its distinctive, wheel-like leaf pattern.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, which can help alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Ficus turbinata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Ficus turbinata is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being used in the production of ointments and creams for topical application.

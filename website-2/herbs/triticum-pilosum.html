Triticum pilosum

Triticum pilosum, also known as wild emmer, is an ancient, wild wheat species native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its distinctive hairy spikelets and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits in the treatment of various ailments, including inflammation, digestive issues, and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory activities. The bioactive constituents of Triticum pilosum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, wild emmer is commonly prepared as an infusion, tea, or decoction, and is also used in the production of traditional herbal remedies, such as tonics and infusions, often in combination with other plants.

classification

Triticum pilosum is a species of wild wheat. It belongs to the family Poaceae and the genus Triticum, which includes domesticated wheat species such as bread wheat and durum wheat. Triticum pilosum is further classified as a perennial grass with a distribution in Asia and Europe. The classification of Triticum pilosum is as follows: kingdom Plantae, phylum Tracheophyta, class Liliopsida, order Poales, family Poaceae, genus Triticum, and species T. pilosum. The classification reflects its evolutionary relationship with other grasses and its unique characteristics within the Triticum genus.

Cephaelis ipecacuanha

Cephaelis ipecacuanha, commonly known as ipecacuanha or ipecac, is a perennial plant native to the tropical forests of Central and South America, whose rhizome has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The health benefits of ipecacuanha include its use as an emetic to induce vomiting, as well as its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which have been traditionally employed to treat various ailments such as fever, nausea, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of ipecacuanha include ipecac alkaloids, emetine, and cephaeline, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with ipecacuanha include tinctures, teas, and syrups, which have been used to treat a range of health conditions, although its use is largely restricted to traditional medicine due to the potential toxicity of its active compounds.

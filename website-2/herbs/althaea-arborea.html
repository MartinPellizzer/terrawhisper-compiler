Althaea arborea

Althaea arborea, also known as the tree mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, belonging to the Malvaceae family.

The bioactive constituents of Althaea arborea include mucilages, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and glycosides, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Althaea arborea is commonly used in herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and ointments, often in combination with other herbs like calendula and chamomile, to create topical treatments for skin irritations and wounds, as well as internal remedies for gastrointestinal issues.

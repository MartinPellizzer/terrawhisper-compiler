What is Cistus crispus f. vestitus?

Cistus crispus f. vestitus is a taxonomic variant of the species Cistus crispus, belonging to the family Cistaceae.

It is a perennial shrub with woody stems, typically growing 10-30 cm tall, and 10-20 cm wide, bearing leathery, lanceolate leaves 2-5 cm long and 1-3 cm wide. The leaves are densely hairy, particularly on the lower surface, and are often gland-dotted. The plant produces white to pale purple flowers in a corymbose inflorescence, with 5 petals and a diameter of 4-6 cm.

The flowers bloom from May to August, and are followed by brownish, ovoid capsules containing numerous small seeds.

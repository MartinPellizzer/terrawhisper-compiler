Dalea grayi

Dalea grayi, also known as Gray's Prairie Clover, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including digestive problems and respiratory infections, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Dalea grayi include isoflavones, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is often used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are commonly used to treat colds, fever, and skin conditions.

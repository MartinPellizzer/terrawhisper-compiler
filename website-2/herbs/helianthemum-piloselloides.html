Helianthemum piloselloides

Helianthemum piloselloides, also known as rockrose, is a low-growing, shrubby perennial plant native to Europe and Western Asia, characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, grayish-green leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin irritations, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Helianthemum piloselloides include flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, as well as phenolic acids and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbalism, rockrose is often used in teas, tinctures, and infusions to treat a range of health issues, with some herbal preparations also incorporating the plant's flowers and leaves into salves and creams for skin care applications.

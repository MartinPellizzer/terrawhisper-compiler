Poa flavescens

Poa flavescens, also known as yellow fescue grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences and blue-green foliage.

The health benefits of Poa flavescens include its potential to treat digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial properties, making it a popular herbal remedy for various gastrointestinal and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Poa flavescens include sesquiterpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Poa flavescens is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into salves and creams to treat skin issues such as wounds and eczema.

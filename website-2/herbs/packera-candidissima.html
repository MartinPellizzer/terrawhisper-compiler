Packera candidissima

Packera candidissima, also known as White Deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to North America, characterized by its white or pale yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which have been linked to the relief of symptoms such as arthritis, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Packera candidissima include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpene lactones, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Packera candidissima is often made into teas, infusions, or tinctures, and is commonly used to support immune function, reduce inflammation, and alleviate digestive issues.

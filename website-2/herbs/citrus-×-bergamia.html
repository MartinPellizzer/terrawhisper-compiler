Citrus × bergamia

Citrus × bergamia, also known as bergamot, is a hybrid citrus plant that is a cross between a sour orange (Citrus aurantium) and a lemon (Citrus limon).

The essential oil extracted from the rind of bergamot fruits has been widely used for its numerous health benefits, including reducing anxiety and stress, improving mood, and aiding in weight loss, due to its high content of limonene and linalool. The bioactive constituents of bergamot oil include limonene, linalool, and bergamottin, which have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

Bergamot is commonly used in herbal preparations such as tea, capsules, and topical creams, as well as in aromatherapy and traditional medicine to promote relaxation and reduce inflammation.

Aloe obliqua

Aloe obliqua, also known as the rare aloe or tiger aloe, is a species of succulent plant belonging to the Aloe genus, characterized by its unique, asymmetrical leaves with serrated edges and vibrant green coloration.

The health benefits of Aloe obliqua are attributed to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of skin irritations, wounds, and burns. The bioactive constituents of Aloe obliqua include aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A and E, as well as other antioxidants and flavonoids that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Aloe obliqua is often used to make topical herbal preparations such as gel, cream, and ointment, which are used to treat skin conditions and promote wound healing.

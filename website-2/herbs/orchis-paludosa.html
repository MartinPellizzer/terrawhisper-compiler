Orchis paludosa

Orchis paludosa, also known as the marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Europe and Western Asia.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, making it beneficial for treating various health issues such as wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Orchis paludosa include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include tinctures, decoctions, and infusions, often used to treat skin irritations and respiratory issues.

Pleopeltis loriformis

Pleopeltis loriformis, also known as Loranthus fern or Loriformis fern, is a type of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in the treatment of wounds, skin infections, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Pleopeltis loriformis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include teas, infusions, and extracts, which are often used to support immune function and alleviate symptoms of fever and cough.

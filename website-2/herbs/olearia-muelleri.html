Olearia muelleri

Olearia muelleri, also known as Mueller's Daisy Bush, is a shrub native to Australia, characterized by its white or pale pink flowers and silvery-green foliage.

The plant has been traditionally used in Australian medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, coughs, and respiratory issues, due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Olearia muelleri include phenolic compounds, flavonoids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Mueller's Daisy Bush has been made into a tea or infused in water to create a decoction, which is often used to relieve cold and flu symptoms, as well as to soothe sore throats and coughs.

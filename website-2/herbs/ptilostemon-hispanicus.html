Ptilostemon hispanicus

Ptilostemon hispanicus, also known as the Spanish thistle or hispanic astragal, is a perennial thistle species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its anti-inflammatory and digestive properties, offering potential health benefits in relieving pain, reducing inflammation, and aiding in the treatment of gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of Ptilostemon hispanicus include flavonoids, sesquiterpene lactones, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, often used to treat digestive disorders, inflammation, and as a general tonic.

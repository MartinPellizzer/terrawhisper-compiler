What is Primula dryadifolia subsp. chlorodryas?

Primula dryadifolia subsp.

chlorodryas is a botanical subspecies of the Primula dryadifolia species, belonging to the family Primulaceae. It is characterized by its perennial habit, with leaves that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape, 2-10 cm long, and 1-3 cm wide, with crenate to dentate margins.

The leaves are dark green, glabrous, and have a petiole up to 1 cm long. The inflorescence is a raceme of 5-10 flowers, with white to pale yellow petals that are 5-8 mm long and have a central yellow spot, and a calyx of 5-7 mm long, greenish-yellow and glabrous.

The plant grows in subalpine to alpine habitats, typically at elevations between 1500-3000 meters.

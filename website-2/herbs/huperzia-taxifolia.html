Huperzia taxifolia

Huperzia taxifolia, also known as Australian jointfir, is a species of evergreen shrub that belongs to the club moss family (Lycopodiaceae).

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include improving cognitive function, boosting memory, and treating anxiety and depression, due to its ability to inhibit the breakdown of neurotransmitters such as serotonin and dopamine in the brain. The bioactive constituents of Huperzia taxifolia include huperzine A, a potent and selective inhibitor of acetylcholinesterase, an enzyme that breaks down acetylcholine, a key neurotransmitter involved in learning and memory.

Huperzine A is often extracted and used in herbal preparations, such as capsules, tablets, and extracts, which are commonly sold as dietary supplements or used in traditional Chinese medicine to support brain health and function.

Rhododendron candidum

The Rhododendron candidum, also known as the white rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its showy white flowers and elliptical leaves.

The bioactive constituents of Rhododendron candidum include glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

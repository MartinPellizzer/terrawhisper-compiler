Pyrethrum arcticum

Pyrethrum arcticum, also known as the Arctic Daisy or Pyrethrum, is a perennial plant belonging to the daisy family, characterized by its white petals and yellow centers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects, which are beneficial for wound healing and soothing skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Pyrethrum arcticum include sesquiterpene lactones, particularly arnidiol and arnidiolide, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for skin care and wound treatment.

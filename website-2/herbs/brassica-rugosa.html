Brassica rugosa

Brassica rugosa, also known as hairy bittercress, is a perennial plant that belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to Europe and Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including its ability to aid digestion, reduce inflammation, and act as an antioxidant. The bioactive constituents of Brassica rugosa include glucosinolates, such as glucoraphanin, which have been shown to have anti-cancer and anti-inflammatory properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Brassica rugosa include tinctures, infusions, and capsules, which are often used to support digestive health and reduce symptoms of menopausal hot flashes.

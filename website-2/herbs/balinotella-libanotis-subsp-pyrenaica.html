What is Balinotella libanotis subsp. pyrenaica?

Balinotella libanotis subsp.

pyrenaica is a taxonomic subspecies of the plant Balinotella libanotis within the family Apiaceae. It is a perennial herb, characterized by its taproot, hairy stems, and divided leaves, which are 1-4 times pinnate, with 7-12 pairs of leaflets.

The leaves are 10-20 cm long, and the plant produces umbels of 5-7 flowers, which are white or pale yellow, and 2-3 mm in diameter. The fruits are 2-3 mm long, and 1-2 mm wide, with a distinctive beak-like projection.

The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters.

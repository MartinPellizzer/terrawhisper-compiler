Mirabilis violacea

Mirabilis violacea, also known as the "Four O'Clock" or "Jewel of the Desert", is a flowering plant native to the Americas, characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the late afternoon.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal and spiritual properties, offering potential health benefits such as pain relief, inflammation reduction, and antimicrobial activity. The bioactive constituents of Mirabilis violacea include alkaloids like violine, violamine, and violamine B, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Mirabilis violacea is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into salves and topical creams, highlighting its potential applications in wound healing and skin care.

Prunus javanica

Prunus javanica, also known as Javan plum or Javan cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family (Rosaceae) native to Southeast Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, providing relief from various health issues, including fever, cough, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Prunus javanica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Prunus javanica is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat respiratory and gastrointestinal issues, and as a natural remedy for colds and flu.

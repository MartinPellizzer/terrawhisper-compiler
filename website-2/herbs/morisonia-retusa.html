Morisonia retusa

Morisonia retusa, also known as the "desert broom," is a spiny, flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin irritations, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Morisonia retusa include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Morisonia retusa is often prepared as a tea or decoction, or used in ointments and salves to treat skin conditions and wounds.

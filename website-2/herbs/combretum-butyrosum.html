Combretum butyrosum

Combretum butyrosum, also known as butter tree, is a plant species in the Combretaceae family, characterized by its yellow flowers and edible fruits.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Combretum butyrosum include flavonoids, tannins, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, as well as being used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and extracts, which are used to treat a range of health conditions.

classification

Combretum butyrosum is classified within the family Combretaceae, a large and diverse family of flowering plants commonly known as the ruddlewood or bushwillow family. This family is part of the order Myrtales, a group of plants that also includes the families Myrtaceae and Melastomataceae. Combretaceae is a tropical and subtropical family with a wide distribution across the world, particularly in Africa and Asia. The Combretum genus, to which C. butyrosum belongs, consists of approximately 150 species, many of which are native to Africa. C. butyrosum is specifically found in tropical Africa.

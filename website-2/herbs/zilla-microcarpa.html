Zilla microcarpa

The Zilla microcarpa, also known as the Australian saltbush, is a shrubby plant species native to the arid regions of Australia, characterized by its small, narrow leaves and ability to thrive in saline conditions.

The plant has been traditionally used in Australian Aboriginal medicine, offering various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in the treatment of wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Zilla microcarpa include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Zilla microcarpa is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, often used to support skin health, reduce inflammation, and alleviate respiratory symptoms.

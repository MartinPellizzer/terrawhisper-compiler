Drypetes glabra

Drypetes glabra, also known as the smooth drypetes or smooth phyllanthus, is a plant species belonging to the family Phyllanthaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in treating various conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and wounds. Bioactive constituents of D. glabra include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which have been identified as the primary contributors to the plant's medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, D. glabra is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and extracts, which are often used to treat various health conditions and to promote overall well-being.

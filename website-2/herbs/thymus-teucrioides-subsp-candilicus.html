What is Thymus teucrioides subsp. candilicus?

Thymus teucrioides subsp.

candilicus is a subspecies of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae, native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a perennial herbaceous plant with a woody base, 10-25 cm tall, and a branched, leafy stem.

The leaves are lanceolate to elliptical in shape, 1-2.5 cm long, and 2-8 mm wide, with a greyish-green color and a wrinkled surface. The flowers are pinkish-purple in color, clustered in axillary whorls, and 6-8 mm long, with a four-lobed corolla.

The calyx is campanulate, 4-6 mm long, and has four unequal lobes.

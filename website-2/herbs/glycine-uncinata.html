Glycine uncinata

Glycine uncinata, also known as Australian sweet pea or wonga pea, is a climbing legume native to Australia, belonging to the Fabaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering various health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which may help to alleviate symptoms of arthritis, asthma, and other inflammatory conditions. The bioactive constituents of Glycine uncinata include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a tea or tincture, and also used to make capsules or powders, making it a popular ingredient in various herbal supplements and preparations.

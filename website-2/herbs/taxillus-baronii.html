Taxillus baronii

Taxillus baronii, also known as the Baroni's Loranthus or Taxillus baronii, is a species of hemiparasitic shrub in the family Santalaceae.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which may aid in treating various conditions, including fever, colds, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Taxillus baronii include alkaloids, terpenoids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tonics, as well as being incorporated into herbal formulas and supplements.

Verbascum arbusculum

Verbascum arbusculum, also known as shrubby mullein or bush mullein, is a perennial plant species belonging to the mullein genus, characterized by its tall, branching stems and small, yellow flowers.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral effects, making it a potential treatment for respiratory issues, wounds, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Verbascum arbusculum include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Verbascum arbusculum is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, often used to treat coughs, colds, and skin conditions.

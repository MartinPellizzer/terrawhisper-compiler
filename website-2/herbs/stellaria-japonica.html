Stellaria japonica

Stellaria japonica, also known as Japanese starwort, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including fever, inflammation, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Stellaria japonica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Stellaria japonica is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or capsule, with the most common preparations being a decoction to treat fever and a topical application to soothe skin irritations.

Azorella multifida

Azorella multifida, also known as Chilean potato bush, is a perennial plant native to the Andes mountains in South America, characterized by its edible, starchy tubers and its adaptogenic properties.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including inflammation, fever, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Azorella multifida include saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In herbal medicine, Azorella multifida is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or decoction, and is used to support immune function, reduce stress, and promote overall well-being.

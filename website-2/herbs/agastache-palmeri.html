Agastache palmeri

Agastache palmeri, also known as Palmer's giant hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, characterized by its tall spikes of purple or blue flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits, including its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties, which may aid in treating respiratory issues such as coughs and colds. The bioactive constituents of Agastache palmeri include volatile oils, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Agastache palmeri include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to relieve respiratory congestion and soothe sore throats.

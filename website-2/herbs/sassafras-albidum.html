Sassafras albidum

Sassafras albidum, also known as white sassafras, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, characterized by its mitten-shaped leaves and aromatic bark.

The health benefits of Sassafras albidum include its ability to alleviate digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial properties, making it a popular herbal remedy for various ailments. The bioactive constituents of Sassafras albidum include safrole, a volatile oil responsible for its distinctive aroma and medicinal properties, as well as other compounds like elemicin and myristicin.

Sassafras root bark is often used to make herbal preparations, such as tea, capsules, and tinctures, which are commonly used to support digestive health and relieve symptoms of colds and flu.

Celtis mukii

Celtis mukii, also known as Indian nettle or Mukia, is a deciduous plant species that belongs to the family Cannabaceae.

The leaves, bark, and roots of Celtis mukii have been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine, offering various health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in the treatment of conditions like fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Celtis mukii include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Celtis mukii include teas, decoctions, and infusions, as well as powdered extracts used in traditional Ayurvedic formulations.

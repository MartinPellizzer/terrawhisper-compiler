What is Echinocactus cornifer var. raphidacanthus?

Echinocactus cornifer var. raphidacanthus is a taxonomic variant within the Echinocactus cornifer species, belonging to the family Cactaceae.

This cactus plant is characterized by its cylindrical stems, typically 0.5-2 meters in height and 0.3-1 meter in diameter, with a grayish-brown epidermis and 10-13 central and 20-30 radial spines. The plant's areoles are 2-3 cm apart, producing 5-7 yellowish flowers, 4-6 cm in diameter, and yellowish-green fruits, 2-3 cm in length. Echinocactus cornifer var. raphidacanthus is native to Mexico, where it grows in rocky and sandy areas at elevations of 1,000-2,000 meters above sea level.

Its stems can store water, allowing the plant to survive in arid environments.

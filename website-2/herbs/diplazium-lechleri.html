Diplazium lechleri

Diplazium lechleri, also known as Kunze's polypody or Andean polypody, is a species of fern native to the Andean region of South America.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including wound healing, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of various conditions such as ulcers, skin irritations, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Diplazium lechleri include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often made into a tincture, tea, or ointment, and is used to treat a variety of ailments, including digestive issues and skin conditions.

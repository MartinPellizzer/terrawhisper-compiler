Lyonia jamaicensis

Lyonia jamaicensis, also known as Jamaican tea-tree or Wild Cinnamon, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region and Central America, belonging to the Ericaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, which can aid in treating wounds, reducing fever, and alleviating respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Lyonia jamaicensis include phenolic acids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Lyonia jamaicensis is commonly used to make tea infusions, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat a range of ailments, including colds, coughs, and skin irritations.

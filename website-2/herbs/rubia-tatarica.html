Rubia tatarica

Rubia tatarica, also known as the Tatarian or European Madder, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help to treat wounds, reduce pain, and alleviate digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Rubia tatarica include anthraquinones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly prepared in herbal remedies such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used to create natural dyes for textiles and other materials.

Hypericum pusillum

Hypericum pusillum, also known as small St. John's Wort, is a low-growing, perennial herb native to eastern North America.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiviral activities, making it beneficial for wound healing, skin conditions, and immune system support. The bioactive constituents of Hypericum pusillum include hyperforin, hypericin, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Hypericum pusillum include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to treat minor skin irritations, cuts, and scrapes.

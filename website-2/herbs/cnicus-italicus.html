Cnicus italicus

Cnicus italicus, also known as the Italian knapweed, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, commonly found in the Mediterranean region.

The plant is valued for its health benefits, particularly in treating digestive issues, inflammation, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Cnicus italicus include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Cnicus italicus are infusions, tinctures, and capsules, often used to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS).

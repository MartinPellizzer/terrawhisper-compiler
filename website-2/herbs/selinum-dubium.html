Selinum dubium

Selinum dubium, also known as wild celery or wild lovage, is a perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae) that is native to Europe and Asia.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of arthritis, improve digestion, and protect against oxidative stress. Bioactive constituents of Selinum dubium include volatile oils, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Selinum dubium is typically used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health conditions, including respiratory issues and gastrointestinal problems.

Mesosphaerum sidifolium

Mesosphaerum sidifolium, also known as Brazilian peppertree or sida leaf, is a plant species native to South America.

The leaves of this plant have been traditionally used to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to their anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Mesosphaerum sidifolium include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the leaves are often made into teas, infusions, or tinctures, and are also used in topical applications to soothe skin irritations and wounds.

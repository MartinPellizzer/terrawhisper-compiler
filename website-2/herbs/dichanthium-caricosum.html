Dichanthium caricosum

Dichanthium caricosum, also known as Kanghi grass, is a perennial grass native to the Indian subcontinent.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties, which can aid in the treatment of various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Kanghi grass include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

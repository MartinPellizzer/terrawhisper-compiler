Melaleuca angustifolia

Melaleuca angustifolia, also known as narrow-leaved melaleuca or white tea tree, is a plant species native to Western Australia, characterized by its narrow leaves and white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant is valued for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which provide various health benefits, including wound healing, skin protection, and immune system support. The bioactive constituents of Melaleuca angustifolia include terpenoids, particularly cineole, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Melaleuca angustifolia is commonly used in herbal preparations, such as essential oils, tea infusions, and topical creams, which are used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and respiratory issues.

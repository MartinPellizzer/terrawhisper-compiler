Athamanta hirsuta

Athamanta hirsuta, commonly known as hairy athamanta or hirsute athamanta, is a perennial plant species in the carrot family (Apiaceae) native to the Mediterranean region.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its health benefits, including its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties, which can aid in the treatment of respiratory issues, wounds, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Athamanta hirsuta include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Athamanta hirsuta is often used in herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and tinctures to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of coughs, colds, and bronchitis.

Ocimum fruticosum

Ocimum fruticosum, also known as Mexican oregano or false oregano, is a shrubby plant belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It has been traditionally used for its medicinal and culinary properties, offering various health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects, which may help to alleviate digestive issues and boost the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Ocimum fruticosum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Ocimum fruticosum is often used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into various spice blends and culinary dishes.

Narcissus multiflorus

Narcissus multiflorus, also known as the multiflower daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine, offering various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in the treatment of respiratory issues, such as coughs and colds. The bioactive constituents of Narcissus multiflorus include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to relieve coughs, colds, and other respiratory ailments.

classification

Narcissus multiflorus is a species of flowering plant that belongs to the genus Narcissus. It is classified as a member of the family Amaryllidaceae, commonly known as the amaryllis family. Within this family, Narcissus multiflorus is further classified as a member of the tribe Leucojoidae. This species is native to East Asia and is known for its clusters of white or yellow flowers. Narcissus multiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued for its ability to naturalize in woodland gardens.

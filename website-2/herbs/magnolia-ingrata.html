Magnolia ingrata

Magnolia ingrata, also known as the Philippine magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines that has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties.

The plant is valued for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which are said to aid in reducing fever, relieving pain, and treating infections. The bioactive constituents of Magnolia ingrata include magnolol, honokiol, and lignans, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Magnolia ingrata is commonly prepared as an infusion, tea, or tincture, and is used to treat a range of ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues.

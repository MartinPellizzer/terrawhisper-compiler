Begonia × ottoniana

Begonia × ottoniana, also known as Begonia Ottoniana or Begonia, is a hybrid ornamental plant species within the family Begoniaceae, characterized by its large, showy leaves and vibrant flowers.

The leaves of Begonia × ottoniana have been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Begonia × ottoniana include flavonoids, tannins, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Begonia × ottoniana include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat fever, sore throats, and respiratory issues.

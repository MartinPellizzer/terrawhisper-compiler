Pleopeltis panamensis

Pleopeltis panamensis, also known as the Panama polypody, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities that can help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as provide wound healing and cardiovascular protection. The bioactive constituents of Pleopeltis panamensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant are teas, infusions, and extracts, which are traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions.

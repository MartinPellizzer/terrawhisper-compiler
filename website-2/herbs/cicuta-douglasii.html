Cicuta douglasii

Cicuta douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's hemlock or poison hemlock, is a highly toxic perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

Traditionally, the plant has been used to treat various health issues, including gout, arthritis, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Cicuta douglasii include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

However, the most common herbal preparations made with this plant are not recommended, as the plant is highly toxic and can be fatal if ingested in large quantities; instead, some traditional herbalists use small amounts in specific, controlled preparations, such as tinctures or infusions, but these are not commonly used or recommended.

Senecio hispidissimus

Senecio hispidissimus, also known as Bushman's poison, is a plant species native to the Southern African region, belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae).

Traditionally, the plant has been used for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Senecio hispidissimus include alkaloids, such as hispidulin and senecionine, as well as sesquiterpene lactones, which are thought to contribute to the plant's medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Senecio hispidissimus is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is also used to make herbal teas and tinctures, although its use should be approached with caution due to potential toxicity.

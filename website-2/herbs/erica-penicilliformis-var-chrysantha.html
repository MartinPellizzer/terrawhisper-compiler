What is Erica penicilliformis var. chrysantha?

Erica penicilliformis var. chrysantha is a botanical variant of the species Erica penicilliformis, belonging to the family Ericaceae.

This variant is characterized by its yellow flowers, which differ from the pink flowers of the typical Erica penicilliformis. Botanically, Erica penicilliformis var. chrysantha features needle-like leaves that are 6-15 mm long, with a glaucous sheen, and is an evergreen shrub that typically grows to 30-40 cm in height. The plant's flowers are produced in late summer, from July to September, and are clustered at the tips of the branches.

The variant is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

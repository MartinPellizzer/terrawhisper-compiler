Taxus baccata aurea

Taxus baccata aurea, commonly known as the Golden Yew, is a cultivar of the European Yew tree (Taxus baccata), characterized by its golden-yellow foliage.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, offering various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of arthritis, reduce fever, and prevent the spread of viral infections. The bioactive constituents of Taxus baccata aurea include taxanes, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which have been shown to exhibit antioxidant, cytotoxic, and immunomodulatory activities.

In herbal medicine, Taxus baccata aurea is commonly prepared as a tincture, infusion, or extract, often used to treat conditions such as cancer, rheumatism, and viral infections, although its use should be strictly supervised due to its potential toxicity.

Genista aetnensis

Genista aetnensis, also known as the Etna broom or Sicilian broom, is a flowering plant species belonging to the pea family (Fabaceae).

Traditionally, Genista aetnensis has been used in herbal medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, cough, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Genista aetnensis include genistein, isorhapontigenin, and other flavonoids and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Genista aetnensis is often used to make infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, as well as being combined with other herbs to create remedies for respiratory and gastrointestinal issues.

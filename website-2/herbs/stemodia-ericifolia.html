Stemodia ericifolia

Stemodia ericifolia, also known as blue curls, is a perennial plant species native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers and distinctive blue color.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including respiratory issues, digestive problems, and fever, due to its reported health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. The bioactive constituents of Stemodia ericifolia include iridoids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Stemodia ericifolia include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat coughs, colds, and other respiratory conditions.

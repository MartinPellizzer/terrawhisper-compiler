What is Lerchia maritima var. fruticosa?

Lerchia maritima var. fruticosa is a variety of the shrubby seablite, a succulent plant that belongs to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its small, ovate to lanceolate leaves, which are often covered with a dense, fine pubescence and have a leathery texture. The leaves are typically 1-5 cm long, have an entire margin, and are arranged oppositely on the stem. The plant produces small, greenish or reddish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are often clustered at the tips of the branches.

The flowers are 1-3 mm long, have five stamens and a three-parted calyx.

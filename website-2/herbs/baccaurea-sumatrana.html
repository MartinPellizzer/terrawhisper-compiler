Baccaurea sumatrana

Baccaurea sumatrana, also known as Sumatran Cupuacu, is a species of fruit-bearing tree native to Southeast Asia.

The bioactive constituents of Baccaurea sumatrana include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a decoction or a tincture, with the latter being used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory issues.

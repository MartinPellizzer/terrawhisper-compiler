Achillea wilsoniana

Achillea wilsoniana, commonly known as Wilson's yarrow or Western yarrow, is a perennial herb belonging to the Asteraceae family.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making it beneficial for treating various health issues such as wounds, fever, and digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of Achillea wilsoniana include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, Achillea wilsoniana is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and salves for wound care and skin issues.

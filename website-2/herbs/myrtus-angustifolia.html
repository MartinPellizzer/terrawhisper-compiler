Myrtus angustifolia

Myrtus angustifolia, also known as narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region, belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, making it useful in treating respiratory issues, skin conditions, and gastrointestinal problems. The bioactive constituents of Myrtus angustifolia include essential oils, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, such as myrtanol and myrtenal, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, Myrtus angustifolia is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and extracts, as well as being used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation and relieve stress.

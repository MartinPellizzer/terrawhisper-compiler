Anthemis artemisioides

Anthemis artemisioides, commonly known as Chamois Chamomile or False Chamomile, is a perennial herb belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and delicate, daisy-like flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and calming properties, providing relief from anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues. Bioactive constituents of Anthemis artemisioides include flavonoids, terpenoids, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

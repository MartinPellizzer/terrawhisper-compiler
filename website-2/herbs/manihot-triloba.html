Manihot triloba

Manihot triloba, also known as the bitter cassava or bitter manioc, is a perennial shrub native to South America, belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is valued for its medicinal and edible properties, offering potential health benefits such as antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antidiabetic activities, as well as antimicrobial and anticancer properties. The bioactive constituents of Manihot triloba include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The roots and leaves of the plant are commonly used to prepare various herbal remedies, including teas, infusions, and decoctions, which are used to treat various health conditions, including fever, digestive issues, and skin problems.

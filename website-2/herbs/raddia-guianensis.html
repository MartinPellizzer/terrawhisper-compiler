Raddia guianensis

Raddia guianensis, also known as "Guianese Raddia" or "False Ginseng", is a plant species native to the Guiana Shield in South America, belonging to the family Urticaceae.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities, contributing to its potential in treating various health conditions, such as wounds, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Raddia guianensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Raddia guianensis is commonly used to prepare teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into herbal tinctures and ointments.

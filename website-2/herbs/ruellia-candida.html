Ruellia candida

Ruellia candida, also known as Mexican Petunia, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America, characterized by its white, trumpet-shaped flowers and sword-shaped leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as fever, diarrhea, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Ruellia candida include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Ruellia candida is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion, as well as being used in topical creams and ointments, to take advantage of its therapeutic benefits.

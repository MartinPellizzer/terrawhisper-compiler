Lappa officinalis

Lappa officinalalis, also known as Burdock, is a biennial plant species of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its large, coarse leaves and purple or pink flowers.

The roots and leaves of Burdock have been used in traditional medicine for their health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving skin conditions, and supporting digestive health. The bioactive constituents of Burdock include sesquiterpene lactones, such as inulin and arabinose, as well as polyphenols and flavonoids.

Burdock is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and infusions, often in combination with other herbs to support detoxification, immune function, and skin health.

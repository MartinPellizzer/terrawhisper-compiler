Pulsatilla sulphurea

Pulsatilla sulphurea, also known as the Yellow Pasque flower, is a perennial plant species belonging to the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae).

Traditionally, Pulsatilla sulphurea has been used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including menstrual irregularities, anxiety, and insomnia, due to its sedative and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include alkaloids, flavonoids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

Pulsatilla sulphurea is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, often in combination with other herbs like chamomile and valerian root to enhance its sedative and calming effects.

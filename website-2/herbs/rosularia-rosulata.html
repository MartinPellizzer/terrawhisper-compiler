Rosularia rosulata

Rosularia rosulata, also known as the Tibetan saxifrage, is a type of succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

The bioactive constituents of Rosularia rosulata include flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is prepared as a tea or tincture, and is also used in modern herbal medicine to make supplements and capsules, as well as being incorporated into creams and ointments for topical application.

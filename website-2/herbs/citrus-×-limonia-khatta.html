Citrus × limonia khatta

Citrus × limonia khatta, also known as khatta or Indian lime, is a hybrid citrus plant that combines the characteristics of both lemon and lime.

The juice and extracts of this plant have been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, digestive problems, and respiratory infections, due to its numerous health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Citrus × limonia khatta include flavonoids, limonoids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include juice, tea, and decoctions, which are often used to treat colds, sore throats, and skin conditions.

classification

Citrus × limonia khatta is classified as a hybrid between the citron (Citrus medica) and the lime (Citrus aurantifolia) in the Rutaceae family. It is a tropical fruit native to India, commonly known as the "khatta" or "kaitha". This citrus hybrid is widely cultivated in India and other parts of Asia for its sour taste and medicinal properties. The classification of Citrus × limonia khatta is also supported by its unique characteristics, which combine the features of both its parent species. As a result, it is widely recognized as a distinct hybrid within the Citrus genus.

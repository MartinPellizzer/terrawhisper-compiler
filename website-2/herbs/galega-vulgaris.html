Galega vulgaris

Galega vulgaris, also known as Goat's Rue, is a perennial herb native to Europe and Western Asia, belonging to the Fabaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used for its health benefits, including its ability to stimulate digestion and relieve symptoms of diabetes due to its insulin-like properties. The bioactive constituents of Galega vulgaris include lectins, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Galega vulgaris is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusions, often used to treat digestive issues and alleviate symptoms of diabetes, as well as to support the immune system.

classification

Galega vulgaris is classified as a perennial legume native to Europe and Western Asia. It belongs to the family Fabaceae, commonly known as the legume or pea family. This classification places Galega vulgaris within the tribe Loteae, a group of legumes that are often woody and herbaceous.

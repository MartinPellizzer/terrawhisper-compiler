Diphyes serpens

Diphyes serpens, also known as Devil's Claw, is a plant species native to southern Africa, characterized by its unique claw-like roots and a climbing habit.

Traditionally, the plant has been used to treat various health issues, including pain, inflammation, and digestive problems, with its extracts showing potential in reducing symptoms of arthritis, fibromyalgia, and other chronic conditions. The bioactive constituents of Diphyes serpens include harpagoside, a glycoside that has been identified as the main responsible compound for its medicinal properties, as well as other iridoid glycosides and phenolic acids.

Herbal preparations made from Diphyes serpens include capsules, tablets, and teas, often used to alleviate pain, inflammation, and other health issues, although its efficacy and safety require further research and regulation.

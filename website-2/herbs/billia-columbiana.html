Billia columbiana

Billia columbiana, also known as the "huarango" or "huarang", is a medicinal plant species native to the Andean region, belonging to the family Sapindaceae.

The health benefits of Billia columbiana include its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as wounds, skin conditions, and gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Billia columbiana include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health conditions in traditional medicine.

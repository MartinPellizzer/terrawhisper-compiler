Scirpus schaffneri

Scirpus schaffneri, also known as the giant bulrush or Schaffner's bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including the treatment of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and pneumonia, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Scirpus schaffneri include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are teas and infusions, as well as tinctures and extracts, which are often used to alleviate respiratory symptoms and support immune function.

Trapa chinensis

Trapa chinensis, also known as the Chinese water chestnut or water caltrop, is a perennial aquatic plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

The plant has been valued for its medicinal properties and is used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Trapa chinensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Trapa chinensis is often used to make herbal preparations, such as teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being dried or made into capsules for easier consumption.

Deparia lasiopteris

Deparia lasiopteris, also known as the silver spleenwort, is a species of fern native to Europe and Asia.

The health benefits of Deparia lasiopteris include its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as arthritis and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Deparia lasiopteris include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used in traditional medicine to promote overall well-being and support the body's natural healing processes.

classification

Deparia lasiopteris is classified as a species of fern in the family Athyriaceae. It belongs to the genus Deparia and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The species is further classified as Deparia lasiopteris (Tausch) Fraser-Jenk. in the scientific community. Deparia lasiopteris is a type of deciduous fern that typically grows in woodland environments. It is also known by its synonym Athyrium lasiopteris Tausch.

What is Terminalia chebula?

Terminalia chebula is a deciduous tree species belonging to the family Combretaceae.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent, growing up to 15 meters tall with a broad, rounded crown and a straight, cylindrical trunk with a grayish-brown bark. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, measuring 5-10 cm long and 2-4 cm wide, with a leathery texture and a pointed apex. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in axillary spikes, while the fruit is a drupe, measuring 2-3 cm in diameter, with a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

The tree is also known as Haritaki, and its fruit is used in traditional medicine in Ayurveda.

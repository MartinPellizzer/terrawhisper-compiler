Lodhra crassifolia

Lodhra crassifolia, also known as Symplocos cochinchinensis or Symplocos crassifolia, is a medicinal plant species belonging to the family Symplocaceae.

The health benefits of Lodhra crassifolia include its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various health conditions such as menstrual disorders, respiratory issues, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Lodhra crassifolia is commonly prepared in herbal forms such as decoctions, infusions, and powders, and is also used in traditional Ayurvedic and Unani medicine preparations, including Lodhra Guggulu and Lodhra Kwath.

Gilibertia japonica

Gilibertia japonica, also known as Japanese mountain mint, is a perennial plant species native to East Asia, characterized by its fragrant leaves and purple flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it beneficial for treating various health conditions such as digestive issues and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Gilibertia japonica include iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and essential oils, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Gilibertia japonica is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used in herbal remedies for coughs and colds, and to aid in digestion and relieve stress.

Celtis laurifolia

Celtis laurifolia, also known as the laurel-leaf hackberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it useful in the treatment of various conditions such as fever, sore throats, and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Celtis laurifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a tea or tincture, and is also used to make a topical ointment or salve to treat skin conditions.

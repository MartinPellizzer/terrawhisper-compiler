Satureja stolonifera

Satureja stolonifera, commonly known as Iranian savory or Persian savory, is a perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its creeping stems and small, fragrant leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its potential to reduce inflammation, improve digestion, and exhibit antioxidant activities, which may contribute to its use in the treatment of various health issues. The bioactive constituents of Satureja stolonifera include essential oils, such as carvacrol and thymol, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Satureja stolonifera is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or infused oil, and is used to treat conditions such as stomach ailments, respiratory issues, and skin problems.

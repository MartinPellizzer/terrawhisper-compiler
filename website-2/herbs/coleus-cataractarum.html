Coleus cataractarum

Coleus cataractarum is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae, commonly known as the catarrh root or Coleus, traditionally used in herbal medicine.

The plant has been valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in reducing symptoms of respiratory issues such as bronchitis and catarrh. The bioactive constituents of Coleus cataractarum include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Coleus cataractarum is often prepared as a tincture, infusion, or syrup, which are used to treat respiratory issues, reduce inflammation, and soothe coughs.

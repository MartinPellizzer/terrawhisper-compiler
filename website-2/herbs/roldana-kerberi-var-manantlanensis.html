What is Roldana kerberi var. manantlanensis?

Roldana kerberi var. manantlanensis is a plant variety within the species Roldana kerberi, belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb with a woody base, typically 0.5 to 1.5 meters tall, with a simple or branched stem and a leafy rosette at the base. The leaves are 10-30 cm long, lanceolate or elliptical in shape, and have a glabrous to pubescent upper surface and a pubescent lower surface. The inflorescence is a panicle or a corymb, bearing 10-30 capitula, each with 10-20 yellow or orange flowers, surrounded by a 6-12 mm long, phyllary ring.

The plant is endemic to the states of Jalisco and Nayarit in Mexico.

Suaeda obtusifolia

Suaeda obtusifolia, also known as the desert picketplant, is a halophytic shrub species native to the deserts of North America.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its ability to help lower blood pressure, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial properties, making it a promising candidate for the treatment of various diseases. Bioactive constituents of Suaeda obtusifolia include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Suaeda obtusifolia is traditionally used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including hypertension, diabetes, and skin infections.

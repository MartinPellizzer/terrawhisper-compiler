Calamus sumatranus

Calamus sumatranus, also known as Sweet Galangal or Wild Galangal, is a species of plant in the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) native to Southeast Asia.

It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, which may aid in relieving digestive issues and reducing pain. The bioactive constituents of Calamus sumatranus include essential oils, such as zingiberene, bisabolol, and sesquiterpenes, as well as alkaloids and phenolic compounds.

In herbal medicine, Calamus sumatranus is commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is often used to make teas, tinctures, and dried root powder for digestive and pain relief purposes.

What is Diploglottis australis var. muelleri?

Diploglottis australis var. muelleri is an endemic Australian tree species within the Sapindaceae family.

It is characterized by alternate, elliptical or lanceolate leaves, 6-12 cm in length, with a leathery texture, a wavy margin, and a glabrous upper surface, while the lower surface is pubescent. The leaves are arranged in a distichous manner and are supported by a 2-5 mm petiole. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in axillary racemes, 4-10 cm in length, and spherical, red drupes, 1-2 cm in diameter, which contain 1-2 seeds.

What is Micranthocereus flaviflorus subsp. alvinii?

Micranthocereus flaviflorus subsp.

alvinii is a subspecies of flowering cactus endemic to Mexico. It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its erect, columnar stems with 13-20 ribs, 1-2 cm in diameter, and 10-20 cm tall.

The stems are covered with fine, white, and sharp areoles, each bearing 2-3 short, yellow spines and 10-20 longer, yellow, and hair-like spines. Flowers are funnel-shaped, yellow, 4-5 cm long, and 3-4 cm in diameter, with a 5-6 cm long and 2-3 cm wide pericarpel.

Flowers are nocturnal, and the species blooms from March to June.

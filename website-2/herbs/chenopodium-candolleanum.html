Chenopodium candolleanum

Chenopodium candolleanum, also known as Candolle's lamb's quarters, is a perennial plant species within the Chenopodium genus, characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, and its leaves and stems are used to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive problems, due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of C. candolleanum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat colds, coughs, and other respiratory issues.

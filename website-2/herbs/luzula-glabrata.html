Luzula glabrata

Luzula glabrata, also known as smooth woodrush, is a perennial plant species in the family Juncaceae, characterized by its slender stems and small, white or pinkish flowers.

Traditional herbal remedies have utilized Luzula glabrata for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which may aid in wound healing and reducing inflammation. The bioactive constituents of Luzula glabrata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, which have been found to contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Luzula glabrata is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for its purported medicinal benefits.

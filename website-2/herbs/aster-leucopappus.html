Aster leucopappus

Aster leucopappus, also known as White-leaf Aster or White-seeded Aster, is a perennial plant native to North America, characterized by its white or pale pink flowers and hairy, white-tinged leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, offering potential health benefits such as wound healing, pain relief, and reduced inflammation. The bioactive constituents of Aster leucopappus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Leucaena insularum

Leucaena insularum, also known as island leucaena, is a leguminous shrub native to the Galapagos Islands.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in treating conditions such as fever, cough, and gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of Leucaena insularum include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Leucaena insularum is commonly used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of ailments, including respiratory infections and digestive issues.

What is Raphanus sativus subsp. acanthiformis?

Raphanus sativus subsp.

acanthiformis is a subspecies of the plant Raphanus sativus within the genus Raphanus, belonging to the family Brassicaceae. It is a perennial or annual plant characterized by its hairy, pinnately divided leaves, and it produces small, white or yellowish flowers in a raceme.

The plant grows up to 50 cm in height and has a taproot. Raphanus sativus subsp. acanthiformis has a similar appearance to its related subspecies, but it can be distinguished by its more elongated root and its more dense leaf arrangement.

This subspecies is native to the Mediterranean region.

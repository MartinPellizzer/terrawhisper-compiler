Cynanchum vulgare

Cynanchum vulgare, commonly known as black henbane or dog's bane, is a perennial plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

The health benefits of Cynanchum vulgare include its antispasmodic, anti-inflammatory, and sedative properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as anxiety, insomnia, and muscle spasms. The bioactive constituents of this plant include alkaloids like corynoline, corynoxine, and corynanthine, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Cynanchum vulgare is often made into teas, tinctures, and infusions, and is sometimes combined with other herbs to enhance its medicinal properties.

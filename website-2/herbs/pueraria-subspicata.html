Pueraria subspicata

Pueraria subspicata is a perennial legume plant native to Asia, commonly known as the Japanese kudzu vine or Japanese peashrub.

The health benefits of Pueraria subspicata include its potential to lower blood pressure, improve cardiovascular health, and exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include isoflavones, such as daidzein and genistein, as well as other polyphenolic compounds that may contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Pueraria subspicata is often prepared as a herbal tea or capsule, and it is also used in combination with other herbs to make patent medicines, such as Danggui Buxue Tang, a traditional decoction used to tonify the blood and improve overall health.

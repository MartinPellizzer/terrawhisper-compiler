Nasturtium glastifolium

Nasturtium glastifolium, commonly known as Watercress, is a fast-growing, aquatic or semi-aquatic annual plant native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its peppery, edible leaves and stems.

The leaves and stems of Watercress have been traditionally used for their health benefits, including reducing inflammation, acting as an antioxidant, and supporting immune function, due to its high content of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. The bioactive constituents of Watercress include flavonoids, carotenoids, and glucosinolates, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anticancer properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Watercress include fresh salads, juices, and teas, as well as dried or freeze-dried supplements, which are often used to support cardiovascular health and boost the immune system.

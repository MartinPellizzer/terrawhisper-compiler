Microlepia polyantha

Microlepia polyantha, also known as Polypodium polyanthum, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its creeping rhizome and small, pinnately divided fronds.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as arthritis and cancer. The bioactive constituents of Microlepia polyantha include flavonoids, saponins, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations, including infusions and extracts, which are often used to treat fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, as well as to promote wound healing and immune system function.

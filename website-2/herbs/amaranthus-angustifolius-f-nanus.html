What is Amaranthus angustifolius f. nanus?

Amaranthus angustifolius f. nanus is a botanical variety of the Amaranthus angustifolius species, belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

This plant is characterized by its narrow leaves, which are typically 2-5 cm long and 0.5-1.5 cm wide, with a linear-lanceolate shape and a mucronate apex. The leaves are also glabrous or pubescent with simple hairs. The plant's stems are erect, green, and 10-30 cm tall, with a branched and somewhat woody base.

It is a dwarfed form of the species, typically smaller than the typical Amaranthus angustifolius.

A salve is prepared by infusing Amaranthus angustifolius f. nanus leaves in a carrier oil such as olive or coconut oil to create a topical treatment for skin conditions.

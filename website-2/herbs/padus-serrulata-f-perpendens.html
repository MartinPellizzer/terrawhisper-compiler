What is Padus serrulata f. perpendens?

Padus serrulata f. perpendens is a botanical variant of the species Padus serrulata, which belongs to the family Rosaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its serrated, elliptical leaves with a length of 2.5-5 cm, a width of 1.5-3.5 cm, and a perpendicularly attached petiole. The leaves are dark green on the upper side and pale green on the lower side, with 13-21 pairs of lateral veins and a serrated margin. The inflorescence is a corymb with 2-5 white or pink flowers, each 1.5-2.5 cm in diameter, containing 5 petals and 5 stamens.

The flowers are followed by a red or purple fruit, 1-2 cm in diameter, which is a drupe containing one seed.

Quercetin and kaempferol, both flavonoids, exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, contributing to the plant's potential therapeutic benefits.

Ipomoea villosa

Ipomoea villosa, also known as wild morning glory, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America, characterized by its purple flowers and hairy stems.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea villosa include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ipomoea villosa is often prepared as a tea, decoction, or tincture to treat respiratory issues, and is also used in combination with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

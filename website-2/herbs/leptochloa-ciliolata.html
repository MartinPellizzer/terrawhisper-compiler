Leptochloa ciliolata

Leptochloa ciliolata, also known as Chrysopogon ciliolatus or Broom Grass, is a type of grass commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Traditionally, Leptochloa ciliolata is used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, cough, and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which contribute to its potential health benefits. The plant contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Leptochloa ciliolata is often prepared as a decoction, tea, or powder, and is used to treat respiratory conditions, fever, and other inflammatory disorders.

What is Gilia latiflora subsp. davyi?

Gilia latiflora subsp.

davyi is a botanical taxon within the family Polemoniaceae. It is an endemic subspecies to California, United States.

Characterized by its pubescent stems and leaves, and a height of 30-60 centimeters, this subspecies develops blue or purple flowers with five petals and an elongated corolla tube. The inflorescence is a terminal, racemose cyme with 5-20 flowers.

The fruit is a capsule, containing numerous seeds.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Gilia latiflora subsp.

Davy's sweetroot tincture is a concentrated liquid extract of the roots, used as a treatment for various health issues, including fever and inflammation, due to its antipyretic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Gilia tea is a decoction of the roots, traditionally used to treat respiratory problems, such as bronchitis and coughs, and to reduce fever, due to its expectorant and antipyretic properties. Gilia infusion is a less concentrated preparation of the roots, used to promote sweating and relieve respiratory issues, while its salve, made from the roots and other plant materials, is applied topically to treat skin conditions, such as wounds and eczema.

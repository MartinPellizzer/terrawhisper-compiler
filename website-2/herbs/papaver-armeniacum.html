Papaver armeniacum

Papaver armeniacum, also known as Armenian poppy, is a perennial plant species native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and surrounding areas, characterized by its bright yellow, orange, or red flowers and hairy leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate various health issues, including fever, cough, and respiratory problems, due to its antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Papaver armeniacum include alkaloids, such as meconic acid, and flavonoids, like quercetin and kaempferol, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

The plant is commonly prepared as a tincture, infusion, or decoction, often used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, and to soothe colds and sore throats.

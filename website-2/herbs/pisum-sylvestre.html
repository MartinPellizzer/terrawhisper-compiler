Pisum sylvestre

Pisum sativum var. elatius, commonly referred to as wild pea or Pisum sylvestre, is a perennial legume plant that belongs to the pea family (Fabaceae) and is native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa.

The health benefits of wild pea include its potential to reduce inflammation, improve cardiovascular health, and support digestive well-being, due to its rich content of fiber, protein, and various minerals. The bioactive constituents of wild pea include saponins, polyphenols, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with wild pea include teas, infusions, and decoctions, which are used to treat various ailments, such as digestive issues, skin conditions, and respiratory problems.

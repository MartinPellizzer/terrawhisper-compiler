Hieracium longistipes

Hieracium longistipes, commonly known as Long-spiked Hawkweed, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its yellow flowers and long, narrow leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its potential to treat fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal issues, making it a valuable addition to herbal remedies for various health ailments. The bioactive constituents of Hieracium longistipes include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Long-spiked Hawkweed is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or infused oil, and is used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of digestive issues and to support immune function.

Smilax paniculata

Smilax paniculata, also known as Chinese sarsaparilla or Smilax, is a perennial plant species native to China, belonging to the Smilacaceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antibacterial properties. The bioactive constituents of Smilax paniculata include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

Smilax paniculata is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are commonly used to treat fever, sore throat, and skin infections.

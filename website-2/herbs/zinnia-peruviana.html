Zinnia peruviana

Zinnia peruviana, also known as Peruvian zinnia or golden zinnia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and South America, belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, cough, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which provide relief from congestion and reduce the risk of infection. The bioactive constituents of Zinnia peruviana include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Zinnia peruviana are infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often used to treat respiratory conditions, such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as to promote expectoration and improve cough relief.

Euphorbia colchica

Euphorbia colchica, also known as the colchicum spurge or Armenian spurge, is a perennial plant species within the Euphorbia genus, characterized by its distinctive greenish-white sap and fleshy leaves.

Traditionally, Euphorbia colchica has been used in herbal medicine due to its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it useful for treating conditions such as wounds, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Euphorbia colchica include colchicine, a glycoside responsible for its medicinal properties, as well as other alkaloids and flavonoids with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

In traditional medicine, Euphorbia colchica is often prepared as a tea or tincture, and it is also used in some formulations for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

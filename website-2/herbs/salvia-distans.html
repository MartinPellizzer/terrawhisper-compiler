Salvia distans

Salvia distans, also known as Siberian sage, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia, characterized by its erect stems and blue to purple flowers.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making it useful in treating respiratory issues, wounds, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Salvia distans include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional medicine, Salvia distans is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat colds, coughs, and digestive issues.

Peniocereus viperinus

The bioactive constituents of P. viperinus include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, P. viperinus is often used to make teas, infusions, and topical creams, with the most common preparations including a tea made from the plant's stems and roots, as well as a topical cream used to treat skin conditions and wounds.

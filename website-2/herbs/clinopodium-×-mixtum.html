Clinopodium × mixtum

Clinopodium × mixtum, also known as hybrid Clinopodium, is a hybrid plant species derived from the combination of Clinopodium glabrum and Clinopodium vulgare, both members of the mint family (Lamiaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including digestive problems, fever, and respiratory infections, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antispasmodic properties. The bioactive constituents of Clinopodium × mixtum include volatile oils, particularly borneol, bornyl acetate, and borneol acetate, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to alleviate colds, coughs, and other respiratory issues.

Moringa moringa

Moringa moringa, also known as the drumstick tree, is a fast-growing, drought-tolerant tree native to the Indian subcontinent, belonging to the family Moringaceae.

The plant is valued for its numerous health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving cardiovascular health, and providing antioxidant and anti-diabetic properties. The bioactive constituents of Moringa moringa include isothiocyanates, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal and nutritional properties.

Moringa moringa is commonly prepared in herbal teas, capsules, and extracts, as well as in traditional dishes such as soups and curries, where its leaves and seeds are used as a flavoring agent and a nutrient-rich food supplement.

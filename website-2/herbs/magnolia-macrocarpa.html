Magnolia macrocarpa

Magnolia macrocarpa, also known as the Yulan Magnolia, is a deciduous tree species native to China and Southeast Asia, characterized by its showy, white flowers and large, conical fruit.

The bark and flowers of Magnolia macrocarpa have been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, sore throat, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include magnolol, honokiol, and other lignans and phenolic compounds, which have been shown to exhibit antioxidant, anti-cancer, and neuroprotective effects.

In herbal medicine, Magnolia macrocarpa is commonly prepared as a decoction of the bark and flowers, as well as in the form of capsules or extracts, often in combination with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

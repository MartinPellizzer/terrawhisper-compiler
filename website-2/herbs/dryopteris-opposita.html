Dryopteris opposita

Dryopteris opposita, also known as the Chinese shield fern or opposite-leaved wood fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia, characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds with lance-shaped leaves.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which have been used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and inflammation, due to its potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects. The bioactive constituents of Dryopteris opposita include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is commonly processed into teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used in traditional Chinese patent medicines and modern dietary supplements.

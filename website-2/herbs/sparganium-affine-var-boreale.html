What is Sparganium affine var. boreale?

Sparganium affine var. boreale is a perennial aquatic plant, belonging to the family Typhaceae.

Its stems are 1-2 meters tall, with dark green, linear-lanceolate leaves that are 5-15 cm long and 1-2.5 cm wide, arranged in whorls of 4-8. The plant produces a terminal spike of small, greenish-white flowers in mid-to-late summer, followed by an aggregate fruiting spike composed of numerous, one-seeded mericarps. The plant thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving water, often in wet meadows, marshes, and along shorelines.

Sparganium affine var. boreale is typically found in circumboreal regions, including northern parts of North America, Europe, and Asia.

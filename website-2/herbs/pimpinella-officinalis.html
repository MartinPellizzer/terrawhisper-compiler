Pimpinella officinalis

Pimpinella officinalis, also known as Aniseed or Bishop's Wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its ability to aid digestion, relieve flatulence, and treat respiratory issues such as coughs and colds, due to its carminative and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Pimpinella officinalis include anethole, a phenylpropanoid responsible for its distinctive anise-like flavor and aroma, as well as other compounds like flavonoids and coumarins.

The most common herbal preparations made with Pimpinella officinalis include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat digestive issues and respiratory problems.

Micromeria grisea

Micromeria grisea, also known as Greek mountain tea, is a perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family, commonly found in the mountainous regions of Greece.

This plant has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, colds, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Micromeria grisea include phenolic acids, flavonoids, and essential oils, such as thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Greek medicine, Micromeria grisea is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used in the production of herbal wines and liqueurs.

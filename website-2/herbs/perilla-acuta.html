Perilla acuta

Perilla acuta, also known as Japanese perilla or tōgarashi, is an annual herb native to East Asia, belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and pungent aroma.

The plant is valued for its health benefits, including its potential to reduce inflammation, improve cardiovascular health, and exhibit antioxidant properties, making it a popular ingredient in traditional medicine. The bioactive constituents of Perilla acuta include rosmarinic acid, perillaldehyde, and perillartine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Perilla acuta is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as tea, capsules, and extracts, as well as being used as a spice and ingredient in traditional Korean and Japanese dishes.

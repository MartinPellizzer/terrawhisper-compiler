Cereus alensis

Cereus alensis, also known as the 'Senita cactus', is a species of columnar cactus native to the Sonoran Desert in Mexico and the southwestern United States.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, providing relief from pain, reducing inflammation, and boosting the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Cereus alensis include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Cereus alensis is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and salves, which are used to treat various health conditions, including wounds, skin irritations, and respiratory issues.

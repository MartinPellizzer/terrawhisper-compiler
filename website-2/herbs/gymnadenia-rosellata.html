Gymnadenia rosellata

Gymnadenia rosellata, also known as the autumn lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, as well as potential applications in treating respiratory issues and wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Gymnadenia rosellata include iridoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are believed to contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to treat various ailments in traditional medicine.

Prunus nepaulensis

Prunus nepaulensis, also known as Himalayan cherry or Indian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan region of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which are believed to aid in reducing fever, relieving digestive issues, and supporting immune function. The bioactive constituents of Prunus nepaulensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Prunus nepaulensis is commonly prepared into decoctions, infusions, and tinctures to treat various health conditions, and its bark, leaves, and roots are used in traditional Ayurvedic and Unani medicine.

Carex caricina

Carex caricina, also known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to North America that belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

Traditionally, sweet grass has been used for its medicinal and spiritual properties, with health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, as well as being used to aid digestion and reduce stress.

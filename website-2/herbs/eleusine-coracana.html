Eleusine coracana

Eleusine coracana, commonly known as finger millet or ragi, is a small-grained cereal crop native to Africa and Asia, belonging to the family Poaceae.

The health benefits of Eleusine coracana include its potential to help manage diabetes, lower cholesterol levels, and provide essential nutrients such as iron, calcium, and fiber. The bioactive constituents of this plant include phenolic acids, saponins, and flavonoids, which have been found to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

In traditional medicine, finger millet is often used to prepare herbal teas, decoctions, and infusions, as well as being used in the form of flour or porridge to make various health-promoting foods and beverages.

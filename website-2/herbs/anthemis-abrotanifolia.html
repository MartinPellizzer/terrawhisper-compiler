Anthemis abrotanifolia

Anthemis abrotanifolia, commonly known as southern chamomile or golden chamomile, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae).

The plant is valued for its calming and soothing properties, offering potential health benefits such as reducing anxiety, promoting relaxation, and alleviating insomnia. The bioactive constituents of Anthemis abrotanifolia include flavonoids, terpenoids, and essential oils, particularly chamazulene, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities.

In herbal medicine, Anthemis abrotanifolia is often used to make teas, infusions, and topical preparations, such as ointments and salves, to soothe skin irritations and promote wound healing.

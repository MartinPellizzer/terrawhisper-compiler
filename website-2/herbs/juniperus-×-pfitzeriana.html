Juniperus × pfitzeriana

Juniperus × pfitzeriana, also known as Pfitzer's juniper or Rocky Mountain juniper, is a hybrid coniferous tree native to North America, characterized by its distinctive, scale-like foliage and conical shape.

The essential oil extracted from the berries and foliage of Juniperus × pfitzeriana has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and diuretic properties, which can help to alleviate conditions such as urinary tract infections and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, and terpinen-4-ol, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Juniper berries are commonly used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for skin and wound care.

Desmodium canadense

Desmodium canadense, also known as Showy Tick-trefoil, is a perennial legume native to eastern North America, characterized by its pink or purple flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antipyretic properties, which are attributed to its potential health benefits in reducing fever, relieving pain, and promoting wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Desmodium canadense include isoflavones, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Desmodium canadense include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often used to treat fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions.

Senna acuta

Senna acuta, also known as the bitter prickly acacia or thorny acacia, is a plant species belonging to the Fabaceae family, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its ability to act as a laxative, reducing inflammation, and exhibiting antimicrobial and antifungal activities, providing relief from various health issues such as constipation and digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of Senna acuta include glycosides, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, Senna acuta is often used to prepare herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are commonly used to treat constipation and other gastrointestinal issues.

Plantago xerodea

Plantago xerodea, also known as desert plantain, is a perennial plant species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, belonging to the Plantago genus of flowering plants.

The leaves and stems of Plantago xerodea have been used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, providing potential health benefits such as wound healing, reduction of inflammation, and protection against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Plantago xerodea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Plantago xerodea include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to treat skin issues, respiratory problems, and gastrointestinal disorders.

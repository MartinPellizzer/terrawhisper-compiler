Scirpus congestus

Scirpus congestus, also known as Short-spiked Bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America, characterized by its dense clusters of short, spiky flowers and rhizomatous growth habit.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Scirpus congestus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used in herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

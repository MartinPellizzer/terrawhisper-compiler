Albuca canadensis

Albuca canadensis, also known as the Canadian leek or white sea squill, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects, as well as potential applications in treating respiratory issues and gastrointestinal disorders. The bioactive constituents of Albuca canadensis include alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins, which are thought to contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Albuca canadensis is traditionally used in herbal medicine and is often prepared in tinctures, infusions, and decoctions to treat a range of health conditions.

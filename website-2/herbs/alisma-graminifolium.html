Alisma graminifolium

Alisma graminifolium, also known as Zhi Mu or Water Plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant belonging to the Alismataceae family, commonly found in wetlands and slow-moving streams.

The plant has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, diarrhea, and nephritis, due to its purported health benefits of reducing inflammation and improving kidney function. The bioactive constituents of Alisma graminifolium include iridoid glycosides, such as alisol A and alisol B, as well as other compounds like flavonoids and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Alisma graminifolium is often processed into herbal preparations, including decoctions, granules, and pills, which are used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat various ailments, particularly those related to the urinary and digestive systems.

Ficus palmifolia

Ficus palmifolia, commonly known as the Ficus palm, is a tropical plant species native to the Pacific Island region.

It has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering various health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, which can aid in treating fever, respiratory issues, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Ficus palmifolia include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, including fisetin and kaempferol, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Ficus palmifolia is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and extracts, as well as in traditional medicine, to treat a range of ailments, making it a valuable plant in Pacific Island healthcare.

Toxicodendron verrucosum

Toxicodendron verrucosum, also known as the poison sumac, is a plant species that belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, characterized by its toxic urushiol sap.

The health benefits of Toxicodendron verrucosum include its use in traditional medicine to treat various conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and skin infections, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include urushiol, a potent allergen, as well as other compounds like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

In traditional herbal medicine, Toxicodendron verrucosum is commonly prepared as a tincture or an ointment to treat skin conditions and other ailments, although its use requires caution due to the potential for skin irritation and allergic reactions.

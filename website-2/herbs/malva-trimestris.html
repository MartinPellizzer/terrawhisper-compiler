Malva trimestris

Malva trimestris, also known as the Three-leafed Mallow, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Mallow family (Malvaceae), characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat a range of health conditions, including digestive issues and inflammation, due to its anti-inflammatory, demulcent, and soothing properties, which can help to calm irritated tissues and promote healing. The bioactive constituents of Malva trimestris include mucilages, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In herbal medicine, Malva trimestris is commonly used to prepare infusions, decoctions, and topical creams, often in combination with other herbs, such as peppermint and lemon balm, to create remedies for digestive issues and skin problems.

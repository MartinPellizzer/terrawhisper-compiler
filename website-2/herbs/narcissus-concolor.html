Narcissus concolor

Narcissus concolor, also known as the white daffodil or mountain daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family, characterized by its white or cream-colored trumpet-shaped flowers and slender green leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections, such as the common cold and bronchitis. The bioactive constituents of Narcissus concolor include alkaloids, glycosides, and terpenoids, including narciclasine, which has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects.

In herbal medicine, Narcissus concolor is often used to make preparations such as tinctures, infusions, and creams, which are used to treat a range of conditions, including respiratory infections, skin irritations, and wounds.

Calamagrostis curtisii

Calamagrostis curtisii, also known as Curtis's reed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of North America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including the reduction of inflammation and improvement of respiratory issues due to its expectorant and bronchodilatory effects. The bioactive constituents of Calamagrostis curtisii include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Calamagrostis curtisii is commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is used to treat conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, and coughs.

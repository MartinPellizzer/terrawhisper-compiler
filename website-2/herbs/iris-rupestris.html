Iris rupestris

Iris rupestris, also known as the rock iris, is a perennial plant species belonging to the iris family (Iridaceae) that is native to mountainous regions in Europe and Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been attributed to its ability to aid in wound healing, reduce pain and inflammation, and protect against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Iris rupestris include iridoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Iris rupestris include infusions, tinctures, and salves, which are often used to treat various skin conditions, including eczema and acne.

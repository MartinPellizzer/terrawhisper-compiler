crepidiflorum is a perennial plant that belongs to the family Asteraceae. It is a subspecies of Hieracium murorum and is characterized by its stems, which are typically 10-30 cm tall, green, and leafy, bearing yellow flowers with 10-15 ray florets and 20-30 disc florets.

The leaves are pinnately lobed, with 3-5 pairs of pinnae, and are 10-20 cm long. The plant grows in rocky and mountainous areas, typically in partial shade, and is found in Central and Eastern Europe.

The flowers bloom from June to August, producing achenes with a pappus of 10-12 mm long hairs.

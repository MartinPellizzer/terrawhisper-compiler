Selinum falcaria

Selinum falcaria, also known as meadow celery or wild celery, is a perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, making it a potential remedy for various health issues such as arthritis, digestive problems, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Selinum falcaria include falcarinol, falcarinone, and other sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Selinum falcaria include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat various health conditions, although its use should be guided by a healthcare professional due to potential interactions and side effects.

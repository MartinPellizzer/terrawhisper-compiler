classification

Sansevieria gentilis is classified within the monocot family Asparagaceae and the subfamily Nolinoideae. This classification is based on the plant's morphological and molecular characteristics. The genus Sansevieria is further grouped into several species, with Sansevieria gentilis being one of them. The classification of Sansevieria gentilis is also sometimes considered to be part of the Dracaenaceae family. However, ongoing research and revisions in plant taxonomy may lead to further changes in its classification.

side effects

Sansevieria gentilis, like other plants in the Sansevieria genus, has not been widely studied for its side effects. However, some people have reported skin irritation and allergic reactions to the sap of Sansevieria plants. Ingesting any part of the plant is also potentially toxic and can cause symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. The plant's toxicity is due to the presence of saponins, which can be harmful if ingested in large amounts. It's essential to handle Sansevieria plants with care and avoid touching the sap to minimize potential risks.

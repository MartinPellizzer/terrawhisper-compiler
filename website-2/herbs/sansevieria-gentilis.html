Sansevieria gentilis

Sansevieria gentilis, also known as the African Spear Plant or Snake Plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asparagaceae, known for its striking, sword-shaped leaves and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include reducing inflammation, improving respiratory function, and exhibiting antimicrobial properties. Bioactive constituents of Sansevieria gentilis include saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are thought to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat various ailments, including fever, coughs, and digestive issues.

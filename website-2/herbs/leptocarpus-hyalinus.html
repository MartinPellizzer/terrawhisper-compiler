Leptocarpus hyalinus

Leptocarpus hyalinus, also known as the glass sword sedge, is a perennial plant species native to Australia, characterized by its slender stems and long, linear leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in Australian Aboriginal medicine, with reported health benefits including the treatment of fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of L. hyalinus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from L. hyalinus include teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as dried and powdered forms for use in traditional medicine and herbal supplements.

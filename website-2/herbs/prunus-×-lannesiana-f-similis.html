What is Prunus × lannesiana f. similis?

Prunus × lannesiana f. similis is a cultivar of flowering plant belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing to 3-6 meters in height, with a broad, rounded crown and a short, straight trunk. The leaves are alternate, elliptical to lanceolate, 5-10 cm long, with a serrated margin, and a glossy, dark green color. The flowers are showy, fragrant, and white to pale pink, with five petals, arranged in clusters, and blooming in late spring.

The plant is a sterile hybrid, resulting from the cross between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus mume.

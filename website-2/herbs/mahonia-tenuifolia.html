Mahonia tenuifolia

Mahonia tenuifolia, also known as Chinese grape holly, is a shrub native to China and Korea, characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Mahonia tenuifolia include alkaloids, phenolic acids, and lignans, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a tea, capsule, or tincture, and is often used to support immune function, reduce inflammation, and promote overall well-being.

Xylocarpus guineensis

Xylocarpus guineensis, commonly known as the African blackwood or mukwa, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin infections, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic properties. The bioactive constituents of Xylocarpus guineensis include flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal activities.

In traditional medicine, Xylocarpus guineensis is often prepared as a decoction, infusion, or ointment, with the latter being commonly used to treat skin conditions such as eczema and acne.

Phyteuma coeruleum

Phyteuma coeruleum, also known as blue rattlesnake root or blue phyteuma, is a perennial plant belonging to the bellflower family (Campanulaceae).

The bioactive constituents of Phyteuma coeruleum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoid saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Phyteuma coeruleum is often prepared as a tincture, tea, or infusion, and is used to relieve coughs, colds, and sore throats, as well as to soothe digestive issues.

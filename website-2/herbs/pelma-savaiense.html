Pelma savaiense

Pelma savaiense is a species of plant in the genus Pelma, commonly known as the Samoan tree fern or Samoan Polynesian tree fern, native to the Pacific Islands.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities, making it useful for treating various ailments including skin infections and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Pelma savaiense include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Traditional Samoan medicine utilizes the plant to create herbal preparations, such as infusions, decoctions, and salves, which are often used to treat skin conditions and promote wound healing.

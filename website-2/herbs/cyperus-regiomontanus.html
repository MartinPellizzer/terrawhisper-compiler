Cyperus regiomontanus

Cyperus regiomontanus, also known as the Mexican papyrus or sweet flag, is a type of sedge plant native to Central and South America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include treating fever, respiratory issues, and digestive problems, as well as its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Cyperus regiomontanus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to support immune function and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu.

The class Liliopsida is also known as monocots, indicating that they have one cotyledon in their seeds. The order Poales is a group of flowering plants that also includes grasses and rushes. Cyperus regiomontanus is further classified into the genus Cyperus, which includes various species of sedges.

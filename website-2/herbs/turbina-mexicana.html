Turbina mexicana

Turbina mexicana, also known as Jimsonweed or Devil's Trumpet, is a perennial plant native to the Americas, belonging to the Solanaceae family.

Traditionally, Turbina mexicana has been used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which may aid in relieving pain and reducing anxiety. The plant's bioactive constituents include alkaloids, such as scopolamine and hyoscyamine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Turbina mexicana is commonly prepared as a tincture, tea, or infusion, and is used to treat various ailments, including insomnia, asthma, and muscle spasms.

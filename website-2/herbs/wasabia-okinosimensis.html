Wasabia okinosimensis

Wasabia japonica (also known as Wasabia okinosimensis) is a species of flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae, commonly known as Japanese horseradish or wasabi.

The plant is prized for its health benefits, including its ability to aid in digestion, relieve congestion, and exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Wasabia japonica include allyl isothiocyanate, which is responsible for its pungent flavor and aroma, as well as other compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Wasabia japonica is commonly used in traditional Japanese medicine and is often incorporated into herbal preparations, such as powders, capsules, and teas, as well as being used as a flavoring agent in food and beverages.

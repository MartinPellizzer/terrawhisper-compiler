Ptarmica alpina

Ptarmica alpina, also known as Mountain Arnica, is a perennial plant species belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae) that is native to high-altitude regions in Europe.

The plant has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antiseptic properties, providing relief from pain, swelling, and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents responsible for its therapeutic effects include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which have been shown to exhibit potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

In herbal medicine, Ptarmica alpina is commonly prepared as a topical cream, salve, or infusion, as well as being incorporated into ointments and tinctures, to support wound healing and alleviate musculoskeletal discomfort.

Phoebe falcata

Phoebe falcata, also known as Chinese silver fir or falcataria, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae, native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects, making it beneficial for treating various health conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Phoebe falcata include phoebein, phoebechalcone, and sesquiterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Phoebe falcata is commonly prepared as a decoction, capsule, or tincture, and is used to treat conditions such as fever, cough, and bronchitis.

What is Phippsia algida subsp. concinna?

Phippsia algida subsp.

concinna is a perennial grass that belongs to the family Poaceae. It is characterized by its narrow, flat, and linear leaves, typically 2-6 mm wide and up to 20 cm long, with a glaucous, often reddish tint.

The plant's inflorescence is a panicle, with spikelets 10-15 mm long, containing 3-5 florets with 2-3 stamens, and a solitary, 1-2 mm long caryopsis. The glumes are 1-2 mm long, with the lower glume being shorter than the upper glume.

The species is found in high-altitude areas, particularly in the Himalayas and the Tibetan Plateau.

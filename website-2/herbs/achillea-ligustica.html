Achillea ligustica

Achillea ligustica, also known as Italian yarrow, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its fern-like leaves and clusters of small white or yellow flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antispasmodic effects, making it beneficial for treating digestive issues, menstrual cramps, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Achillea ligustica include flavonoids, sesquiterpenes, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which can be used to treat a range of health issues, from digestive problems to skin irritations.

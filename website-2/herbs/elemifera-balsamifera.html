Elemifera balsamifera

Elemifera balsamifera, also known as elemi, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

The bark and resin of this plant have been used for centuries in traditional medicine, offering health benefits such as antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties, which can aid in wound healing, reduce fever, and alleviate respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Elemifera balsamifera include elemicin, safrole, and caryophyllene, among others, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, elemi is used to make herbal preparations such as resin extracts, tinctures, and essential oils, which are often used to treat colds, coughs, and skin conditions.

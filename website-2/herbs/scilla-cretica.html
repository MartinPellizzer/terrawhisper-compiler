Scilla cretica

Scilla cretica, also known as the Krimson Queen or Crete squill, is a bulbous perennial plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, as well as its ability to treat various conditions such as fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Scilla cretica include alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Scilla cretica is used to prepare herbal remedies such as tinctures, infusions, and decoctions, which are used to treat a range of ailments, including fever, cough, and respiratory issues.

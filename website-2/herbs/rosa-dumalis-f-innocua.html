What is Rosa dumalis f. innocua?

Rosa dumalis f. innocua is a botanical variety of Rosa dumalis, a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by deciduous shrubs or small trees with thorny stems, 2-4 cm long, and dark green, serrate leaves, 5-10 cm long. The flowers are pink to white, 5-6 cm in diameter, with 5 petals and a cluster of stamens in the center. The fruit is a red, 1-2 cm long, aggregate pome, composed of 5-10 follicles.

Rosa dumalis f. innocua is primarily found in the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

Furthermore, Rosa dumalis f. innocua may exacerbate existing allergies, particularly those related to the Asteraceae family, by releasing allergenic particles into the air when dried or crushed.

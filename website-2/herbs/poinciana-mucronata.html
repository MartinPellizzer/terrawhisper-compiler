Poinciana mucronata

Poinciana mucronata, commonly known as the Natal caesalpinia or Natal bird's claw, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the savannas and grasslands of southern Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Poinciana mucronata include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction or infusion to make a tea, or as a tincture, and is used to treat conditions such as arthritis, bronchitis, and digestive issues.

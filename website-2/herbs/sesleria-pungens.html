Sesleria pungens

Sesleria pungens, also known as pungent sesleria, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its sharp-edged leaves and purple flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues and wounds. Bioactive constituents of Sesleria pungens include sesquiterpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are believed to contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made from Sesleria pungens are teas and infusions, as well as extracts and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues and skin conditions.

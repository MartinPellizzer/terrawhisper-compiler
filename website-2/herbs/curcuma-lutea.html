Curcuma lutea

Curcuma lutea, also known as Jangli Haldi, is a plant species belonging to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae), native to the Himalayan region.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, providing health benefits such as pain relief, wound healing, and improved digestion. The bioactive constituents of Curcuma lutea include curcumenols, turmerones, and sesquiterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In Ayurvedic medicine, Curcuma lutea is commonly prepared as a powder or oil, often combined with other herbs to create traditional remedies, such as decoctions, infusions, and topical ointments.

Archangelica officinalis

Archangelica officinalis, also known as Angelica, is a perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation, relieving digestive issues, and exhibiting antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Angelica include volatile oils, such as pinene and limonene, as well as sesquiterpenes like bergapten and angelicin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Angelica is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and infusions, often in combination with other herbs to create remedies for various health conditions.

classification

Archangelica officinalis is a member of the carrot family, which is classified under the scientific order Apiales. More specifically, it belongs to the family Apiaceae. Within this family, Archangelica officinalis is classified under the genus Archangelica. It is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and Western Asia. Its classification is further supported by its botanical name, which indicates its species and genus.

Archangelica officinalis, also known as wild angelica, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to its numerous health benefits. side effects

Archangelica officinalis, also known as angelica, can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and contact dermatitis in some individuals, particularly when applied topically. Ingesting angelica in large quantities may lead to stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea. Additionally, angelica may interact with blood thinners and other medications, increasing the risk of bleeding complications. Pregnant women are advised to avoid consuming angelica due to potential risks of uterine stimulation and miscarriage. It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using angelica for medicinal purposes.

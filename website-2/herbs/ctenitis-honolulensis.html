Ctenitis honolulensis

Ctenitis honolulensis, also known as 'Olepe,' is a species of fern native to Hawaii and Southeast Asia, belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, as well as its potential to protect against neurodegenerative diseases. Bioactive constituents of Ctenitis honolulensis include sesquiterpenes and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Hawaiian medicine, the plant is often made into teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health conditions, including fever, headache, and respiratory issues.

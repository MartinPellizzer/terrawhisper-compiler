side effects

Begonia rhizocaulis is not a widely used or well-known species, and therefore, there is limited information on its side effects. However, begonias in general can be toxic if ingested due to the presence of calcium oxalate crystals. These crystals can cause oral and gastrointestinal irritation, and may lead to symptoms such as mouth and throat burning, and nausea. In some cases, ingesting large amounts of begonia plants can cause more severe side effects, including kidney damage and respiratory distress. It's essential to exercise caution when handling begonias and keep them out of reach of children and pets.

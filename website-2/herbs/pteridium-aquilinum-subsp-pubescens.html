What is Pteridium aquilinum subsp. pubescens?

Pteridium aquilinum subsp.

pubescens is a subspecies of fern in the family Dennstaedtiaceae. It is characterized by its pubescent rachides, petioles, and rhizome, as well as its ovate, lanceolate, or linear lanceolate pinnae, which are acuminate to obtuse.

The fertile pinnae are similar in shape, but smaller, and have sori that are parallel to the veins and often continuous. Pteridium aquilinum subsp. pubescens typically grows in open, sandy or rocky areas, and is widely distributed in Asia, Australia, and the Pacific Islands.

Its growth habit is erect, with a rhizome that can form large colonies.

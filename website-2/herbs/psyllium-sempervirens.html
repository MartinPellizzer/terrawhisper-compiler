Psyllium sempervirens

Psyllium sempervirens, also known as Indian hollyhock or psyllium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent, belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

The health benefits of Psyllium sempervirens include its ability to promote digestive regularity, lower cholesterol levels, and regulate blood sugar levels due to its high fiber content. The bioactive constituents of this plant include mucilages, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Psyllium sempervirens include capsules, powders, and teas, often used to support gut health and alleviate symptoms of constipation and irritable bowel syndrome.

Lodhra obovata

Lodhra obovata, also known as Symplocos racemosa, is a deciduous tree species native to the Himalayan region, belonging to the family Symplocaceae.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, contributing to its potential in treating various health issues such as menstrual disorders, skin conditions, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Lodhra obovata include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used to prepare various herbal remedies, including decoctions, infusions, and powder, which are often used to treat menstrual disorders, and are also used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine.

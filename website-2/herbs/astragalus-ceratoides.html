Astragalus ceratoides

Astragalus ceratoides, also known as Astragalus or Huangqi, is a perennial plant belonging to the pea family (Fabaceae) native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to boost the immune system, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antioxidant properties, making it a valuable herb in the treatment of various health conditions, including cardiovascular diseases and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus ceratoides include polysaccharides, flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Astragalus is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, capsules, and extracts, and is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effects and create formulas for immune system support and overall well-being.

Onobrychis peduncularis

Onobrychis peduncularis, also known as Persian clover or onobrychis, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, providing relief from various health issues such as gastrointestinal problems, inflammation, and respiratory issues, due to its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Onobrychis peduncularis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

Traditionally, Onobrychis peduncularis is used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and infertile infusions, as well as in the form of tinctures and dried extracts.

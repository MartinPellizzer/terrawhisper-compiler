Cymbopogon giganteus

Cymbopogon giganteus, commonly known as East Indian lemongrass, is a species of grass native to India and Southeast Asia, belonging to the Poaceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, providing relief from fever, digestive issues, and respiratory problems due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Cymbopogon giganteus include citral, geranial, and limonene, which are responsible for its medicinal benefits.

The essential oil extracted from the plant is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and topical creams to promote relaxation, ease muscle pain, and boost the immune system.

What is Thymus talijevii subsp. paucifolius?

Thymus talijevii subsp.

paucifolius is a subspecies of the plant Thymus talijevii, belonging to the family Lamiaceae. It is a shrubby perennial herb with a woody base, growing up to 10 cm in height, and has a short, woody stem and leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, 5-15 mm long, and 2-5 mm wide.

The leaves are oppositely arranged, glabrous or pubescent, and have an acute to obtuse apex. The inflorescence is a corymb, with short, pedunculate flowers that are purple to pink in color, and the calyx is campanulate, 4-5 mm long, and glabrous.

The plant has a limited distribution in the Balkan Peninsula, and is considered endemic to this region.

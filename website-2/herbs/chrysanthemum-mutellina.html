Chrysanthemum mutellina

Chrysanthemum mutellina, also known as Yellow Chamomile, is a flowering plant species within the daisy family (Asteraceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, coughs, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Chrysanthemum mutellina include flavonoids, sesquiterpenes, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Chrysanthemum mutellina include teas, infusions, and extracts, often used to promote relaxation and alleviate symptoms of the common cold and flu.

classification

Chrysanthemum mutellina is classified as a species within the genus Chrysanthemum, which is a part of the family Asteraceae. This classification places it among the daisy-like flowering plants. The genus Chrysanthemum is a group of flowering plants that are often cultivated for their ornamental value. Chrysanthemum mutellina is native to Eastern Europe and Western Asia, where it thrives in dry, rocky areas. The species is a perennial plant that produces yellow flowers in late summer.

Chrysanthemum mutellina, also known as the carline thistle, has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

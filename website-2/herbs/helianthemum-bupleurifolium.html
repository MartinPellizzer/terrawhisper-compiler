Helianthemum bupleurifolium

Helianthemum bupleurifolium, also known as Rockrose or Bupleurum-leaved Rockrose, is a perennial shrub plant in the family Cistaceae, native to the Mediterranean region and Western Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Its bioactive constituents include flavonoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, as well as phenolic acids like ferulic and caffeic acid, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Helianthemum bupleurifolium include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often used to support immune function and relieve symptoms of colds and flu.

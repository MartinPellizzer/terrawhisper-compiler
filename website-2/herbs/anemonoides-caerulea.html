Anemonoides caerulea

Anemonoides caerulea, also known as blue anemone or false anemone, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its delicate blue or white flowers and slender stems.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiviral properties, offering potential health benefits in treating conditions such as fever, wounds, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Anemonoides caerulea include alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Anemonoides caerulea is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion, and is often used to treat coughs, sore throats, and skin irritations.

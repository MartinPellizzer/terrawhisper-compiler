Sambucus plumosa laciniata

Sambucus plumosa laciniata, also known as the cut-leaf elder or laciniated elder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Adoxaceae family.

The bioactive constituents of Sambucus plumosa laciniata include flavonoids, anthocyanins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are elderberry syrups, teas, and tinctures, often used to support immune function and alleviate symptoms associated with upper respiratory tract infections.

Matricaria rupestris

Matricaria rupestris, also known as Rock Chamomile or Wild Chamomile, is a perennial plant belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae) that thrives in dry, rocky areas.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include sesquiterpene lactones, such as matricarin and matricarinolide, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Matricaria rupestris is often used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are commonly used to calm the nervous system, alleviate menstrual cramps, and support respiratory health.

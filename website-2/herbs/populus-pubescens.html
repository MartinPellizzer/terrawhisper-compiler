Populus pubescens

Populus pubescens, also known as the pubescent cottonwood or narrowleaf cottonwood, is a deciduous tree species native to North America.

This plant is rich in medicinal properties and offers several health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities, which can help alleviate various ailments such as fever, digestive issues, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Populus pubescens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The bark of this tree is often used to make herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat coughs, sore throats, and respiratory issues.

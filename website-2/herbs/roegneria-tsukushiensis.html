Roegneria tsukushiensis

Roegneria tsukushiensis, also known as Japanese wild ryegrass, is a type of grass native to East Asia, characterized by its distinctive spikelets and panicles.

The bioactive constituents of Roegneria tsukushiensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional East Asian medicine, Roegneria tsukushiensis is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as in the form of capsules and tablets, to support overall health and well-being.

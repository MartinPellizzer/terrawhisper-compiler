Mitreola lanceolata

Mitreola lanceolata, also known as Purple Miter or Huzhang, is a perennial plant species native to China and Southeast Asia, belonging to the Loganiaceae family.

The bioactive constituents of Mitreola lanceolata include mitraphylline, mitoglanine, and mitrecoline, which have been shown to exhibit significant pharmacological activities.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Mitreola lanceolata is commonly formulated into herbal teas, decoctions, and pills to promote health and well-being, with the most popular preparations including Huzhang decoction and Huzhang tea.

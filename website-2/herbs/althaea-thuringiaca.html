Althaea thuringiaca

Althaea thuringiaca, commonly known as marshmallow, is a perennial herb belonging to the mallow family (Malvaceae).

The roots and leaves of this plant have been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including digestive problems, skin irritations, and inflammation, due to its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Althaea thuringiaca include mucilages, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Althaea thuringiaca include decoctions, infusions, and topical creams, which are used to treat conditions such as mucositis, skin ulcers, and wound healing.

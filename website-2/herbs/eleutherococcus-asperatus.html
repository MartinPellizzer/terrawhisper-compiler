Eleutherococcus asperatus

Eleutherococcus asperatus, also known as the Siberian ginseng, is a perennial plant species native to the mountainous regions of Asia and Eastern Europe.

It has been traditionally used for its adaptogenic properties, providing relief from stress, fatigue, and promoting overall well-being, as well as its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which may aid in the treatment of various health conditions such as hypertension and arthritis. The bioactive constituents of Eleutherococcus asperatus include triterpenoid saponins, particularly eleutherosides, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine and other herbal practices, the roots of Eleutherococcus asperatus are often made into teas, capsules, or tinctures to harness its health benefits.

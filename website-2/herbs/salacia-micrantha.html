Salacia micrantha

Salacia micrantha, also known as Indian plum or Salacca, is a medicinal plant native to the tropical forests of India.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including diabetes, obesity, and cardiovascular diseases, due to its potential health benefits of improving insulin sensitivity, reducing inflammation, and lowering cholesterol levels. The bioactive constituents of Salacia micrantha include salacin, salacinol, and other polyphenolic compounds that are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Salacia micrantha is commonly prepared as a herbal tea, capsule, or extract, and is often marketed as a dietary supplement to support weight management and blood sugar control.

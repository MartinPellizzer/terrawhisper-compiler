Coccinia grandis

Coccinia grandis, also known as ivy gourd or tindora, is a climber plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, diarrhea, and skin disorders, due to its potential health benefits in reducing inflammation and improving antioxidant activity. The bioactive constituents of C. grandis include flavonoids, alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to prepare herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and syrups, which are used to treat various ailments, including fever, cough, and respiratory problems.

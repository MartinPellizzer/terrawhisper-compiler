Paphiopedilum hirsutissimum

Paphiopedilum hirsutissimum, also known as the hairy Venus slipper, is a species of orchid native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities, making it beneficial for treating wounds, reducing fever, and alleviating respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of P. hirsutissimum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including bronchitis and skin infections.

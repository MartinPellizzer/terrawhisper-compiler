Plantago napiformis

Plantago napiformis, also known as ballhead plantain, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Plantaginaceae family, characterized by its small, rounded leaves and dense clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The leaves and roots of Plantago napiformis have been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including digestive problems, inflammation, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antibacterial properties. The bioactive constituents of Plantago napiformis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and mucilages, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Plantago napiformis is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as in topical creams and ointments to soothe skin irritations and wounds.

Euphorbia orbifolia

Euphorbia orbifolia, also known as the globe milk bush or globe spurge, is a succulent plant species native to southern Africa, characterized by its distinctive spherical shape and milky sap.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Euphorbia orbifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction, infusion, or ointment, and is commonly used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and respiratory issues.

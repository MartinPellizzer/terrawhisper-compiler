Aloe × subrigida

Aloe × subrigida, also known as the hybrid aloe, is a succulent plant species that is a natural hybrid of Aloe ferox and Aloe plicatilis.

The health benefits of Aloe × subrigida include its ability to soothe and protect the skin, with applications in wound healing, burns, and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of this plant include anthraquinones, such as aloe-emodin, aloin, and barbaloin, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiproliferative properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Aloe × subrigida include gel and juice extracts, which are often used in topical creams, ointments, and oral supplements to leverage its medicinal properties.

Aloe × subrigida is a hybrid of the Aloe species, specifically a cross between Aloe ferox and Aloe aristata. This plant is a succulent, known for its unique appearance and hardy nature. Aloe × subrigida is a member of the Asphodelaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of South Africa. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and low-maintenance requirements. This hybrid has gained popularity among plant enthusiasts due to its attractive leaves and ability to thrive in dry environments.

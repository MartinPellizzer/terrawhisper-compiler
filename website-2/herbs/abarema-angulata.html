Abarema angulata

Abarema angulata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or guanandi, is a tree species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal disorders, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which contribute to its potential health benefits. The bioactive constituents of Abarema angulata include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Abarema angulata is commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion to treat various ailments, as well as being used to make teas, tinctures, and extracts for therapeutic purposes.

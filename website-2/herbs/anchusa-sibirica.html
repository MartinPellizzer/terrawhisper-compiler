Anchusa sibirica

Anchusa sibirica, also known as Siberian bugloss, is a perennial plant species in the borage family (Boraginaceae) native to the mountainous regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat a range of health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Anchusa sibirica include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Anchusa sibirica is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, with the dried flowers and leaves being commonly used in traditional remedies.

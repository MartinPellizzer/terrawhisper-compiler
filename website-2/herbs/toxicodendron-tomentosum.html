Toxicodendron tomentosum

Toxicodendron tomentosum, also known as the poison sumac, is a plant species in the family Anacardiaceae, native to eastern North America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including treating fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. The bioactive constituents of Toxicodendron tomentosum include saponins, flavonoids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Herbal preparations made from the plant include salves, ointments, and infusions, which are used to treat various health conditions, although caution is advised due to the plant's urushiol content, which can cause skin irritation.

Cymbopogon versicolor

Cymbopogon versicolor, commonly known as variegated lemongrass or purple lemongrass, is a species of grass in the genus Cymbopogon, characterized by its unique purple or reddish tint on the leaves and stems.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with health benefits including antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in wound healing, reducing fever, and fighting off infections. The bioactive constituents of Cymbopogon versicolor include essential oils such as citral, limonene, and geraniol, as well as flavonoids and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the most common preparations made with this plant include teas, infusions, and essential oils, which are often used to treat digestive issues, colds, and respiratory problems.

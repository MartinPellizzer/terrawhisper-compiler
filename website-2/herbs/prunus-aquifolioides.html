Prunus aquifolioides

Prunus aquifolioides, also known as Japanese bird cherry or Japanese bird cherry tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that belongs to the Rosaceae family.

The health benefits of Prunus aquifolioides include its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which have been traditionally used to treat a variety of conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues. The plant contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which have been found to exhibit potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Prunus aquifolioides is often made into herbal preparations, including teas, decoctions, and infusions, which are used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory issues.

Hypericum madagascariense

Hypericum madagascariense, also known as Madagascar St. John's Wort, is a species of flowering plant in the St. John's Wort family (Hypericaceae).

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to exhibit potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antidepressant properties. Bioactive constituents found in Hypericum madagascariense include hypericin, hyperforin, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or topical cream, often used to treat wound healing, skin issues, and mild anxiety.

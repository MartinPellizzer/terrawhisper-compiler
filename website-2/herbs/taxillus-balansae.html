Taxillus balansae

Taxillus balansae, also known as Balansa's dwarf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant species that grows as an epiphyte on the branches of its host tree, typically Pinus yunnanensis.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, making it beneficial for treating various health conditions such as fever, cough, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Taxillus balansae include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues.

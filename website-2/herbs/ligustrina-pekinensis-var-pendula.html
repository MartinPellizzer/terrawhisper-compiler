What is Ligustrina pekinensis var. pendula?

Ligustrina pekinensis var. pendula is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the family Oleaceae.

It has a broad, spreading or weeping habit, with pendulous branches and a dense, rounded crown. The leaves are simple, elliptical, and oppositely arranged, measuring 3-12 cm in length, with a leathery texture and entire margins. The inflorescences are axillary, with 2-4 pairs of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by blue or purple fruits, 1-2 cm in diameter.

The bark is grey-brown, with horizontal lenticels and a smooth or slightly fibrous texture.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Ligustrina pekinensis var. pendula are ligustrum tea, infusion, tincture, ligustrazine decoction, and salve.

Ligustrum tea is made by steeping the dried flowers in hot water to extract its bioactive compounds, which are believed to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Ligustrum infusion is prepared by steeping the flowers in cold water to create a longer-lasting liquid extract rich in flavonoids and phenolic acids. Ligustrum tincture is a concentrated liquid extract obtained through solvent extraction, often used to treat cardiovascular and neurological disorders due to its omega-3 fatty acid and ligustrazine content.

Ligustrazine decoction, made by boiling the flowers in water, and ligustrum salve, a topical application of the plant's essential oil, are used to treat wounds and skin conditions, respectively, due to their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

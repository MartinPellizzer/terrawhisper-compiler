What is Helianthemum marifolium subsp. rubellum?

Helianthemum marifolium subsp.

rubellum is a plant taxon belonging to the family Cistaceae, a family of flowering plants commonly known as rockroses. This plant is a subspecies of Helianthemum marifolium, a species of shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula, with a woody stem, green leaves and small, yellow flowers.

Helianthemum marifolium subsp. rubellum has leaves that are elliptical in shape, 2-5 cm long, with a petiole 1-2 mm long, and flowers that are 1-2 cm in diameter, with 5 petals and a yellow color, often with a reddish tint. The flowers are arranged in axillary clusters and bloom from April to July.

This subspecies is characterized by its small size, typically growing up to 10-30 cm high, with a dense, spreading habit.

Butea acuminata

Butea acuminata, also known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical forests of India and Southeast Asia.

The plant is rich in medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, which can help in reducing fever, relieving pain, and treating skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Butea acuminata include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are dried root extract, powder, and tea, which are used to treat various health conditions, including respiratory problems, digestive issues, and skin diseases.

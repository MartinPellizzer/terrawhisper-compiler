Acacia uncifolia

Acacia uncifolia, also known as the prickly wattle or one-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia, belonging to the family Fabaceae.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, contributing to the relief of symptoms associated with digestive issues, arthritis, and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Acacia uncifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and condensed tannins, which are responsible for its therapeutic benefits.

Traditional herbal preparations made with Acacia uncifolia include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to support gut health, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing.

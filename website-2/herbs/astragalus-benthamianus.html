Astragalus benthamianus

Astragalus benthamianus, also known as Tibetan milkvetch, is a type of flowering plant in the legume family (Fabaceae).

The bioactive constituents of Astragalus benthamianus include saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, tinctures, and capsules, as well as being incorporated into soups and other herbal preparations.

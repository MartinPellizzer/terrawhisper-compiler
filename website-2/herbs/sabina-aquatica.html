Sabina aquatica

Sabina aquatica, also known as Juniperus sabina, is a shrubby evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, belonging to the cypress family (Cupressaceae).

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, providing relief from digestive issues, respiratory problems, and gout due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant effects. The bioactive constituents of Sabina aquatica include terpenes, such as sabina ketone, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Sabina aquatica is often prepared as a tea or infused in oil for topical application, or used in tinctures and extracts for its therapeutic benefits.

Toxicodendron glabrum

Toxicodendron glabrum, also known as smooth sumac or poison sumac, is a type of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family, native to eastern North America.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including reducing fever, relieving pain, and treating respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Toxicodendron glabrum include flavonoids, tannins, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include teas, infusions, and salves, which are often used to treat skin conditions and other ailments.

side effects

Toxicodendron glabrum, also known as the smooth sumac, can cause side effects due to its urushiol oil content. Urushiol oil is an allergenic compound found in the sap of the plant, which can cause skin irritation, itching, and rashes in some individuals. In severe cases, exposure to the sap can lead to more serious symptoms such as blistering, swelling, and even anaphylaxis. The oil can also be present in the leaves, stems, and roots of the plant, increasing the risk of exposure. People who have had previous reactions to urushiol oil from other plants like poison ivy, oak, or sumac should exercise caution around Toxicodendron glabrum.

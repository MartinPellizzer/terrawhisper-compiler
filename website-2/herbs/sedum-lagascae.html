Sedum lagascae

Sedum lagascae, also known as wild aloe or false aloe, is a succulent plant species belonging to the Crassulaceae family, native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including treating wounds, burns, and skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Sedum lagascae include flavonoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, as well as triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Sedum lagascae is commonly used to prepare topical creams, ointments, and gels for the treatment of skin conditions, as well as oral supplements in the form of capsules or teas, making it a valuable resource in traditional herbal medicine.

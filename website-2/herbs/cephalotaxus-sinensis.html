Cephalotaxus sinensis

Cephalotaxus sinensis, also known as the Chinese plum yew, is a slow-growing, evergreen tree native to China and Japan, belonging to the yew family (Taxaceae).

The plant has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries, valued for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anti-cancer properties. The bioactive constituents of Cephalotaxus sinensis include cephalotaxine, a diterpenoid alkaloid with potential anticancer and anti-inflammatory activities, as well as other compounds like taxine and cephalotaxin.

The most common herbal preparations made from Cephalotaxus sinensis are teas, decoctions, and extracts, often used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues.

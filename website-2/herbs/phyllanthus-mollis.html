Phyllanthus mollis

Phyllanthus mollis, also known as soft-leaved Phyllanthus or soft leaf cheq, is a plant species that belongs to the family Phyllanthaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, inflammation, and liver diseases, due to its purported health benefits such as antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and hepatoprotective properties. The bioactive constituents of Phyllanthus mollis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Phyllanthus mollis include decoctions, infusions, and extracts, which are often used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory issues, as well as to promote liver health and support the immune system.

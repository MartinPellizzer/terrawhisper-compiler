Frangula grisea

Frangula grisea, also known as Gray Alder Buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Asia and Europe, belonging to the Rhamnaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in medicine for its potential health benefits, including its ability to treat constipation, diarrhea, and liver disorders due to its anthraquinone glycosides, which act as laxatives and stimulate bowel movements. The bioactive constituents of Frangula grisea include anthraquinone glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Frangula grisea is often used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and capsules, and is also used in combination with other herbs to create formulas for digestive health and liver support.

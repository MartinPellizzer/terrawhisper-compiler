Ocotea pittieri

Ocotea pittieri, also known as Ocotea pittieri or Mexican Palo Santo, is a species of evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, belonging to the family Lauraceae.

The essential oil extracted from Ocotea pittieri is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in reducing stress, promoting relaxation, and supporting immune function. The bioactive constituents of Ocotea pittieri include sesquiterpenes, such as alpha-bisabolol, and phenolic compounds, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

Ocotea pittieri is commonly used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine, with its essential oil often used in blends for stress relief, as a natural insect repellent, and in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

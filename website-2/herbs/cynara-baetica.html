Cynara baetica

Cynara baetica, also known as the Andalusian artichoke, is a perennial plant belonging to the thistle family (Asteraceae) native to the Iberian Peninsula.

The health benefits of Cynara baetica include its potential to lower cholesterol levels, improve blood sugar control, and exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include cynarin, luteolin, and apigenin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Cynara baetica is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, capsules, and extracts, which are often marketed as a dietary supplement to support cardiovascular health and weight management.

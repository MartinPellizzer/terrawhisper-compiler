Actaea lancifoliolata

Actaea lancifoliolata, also known as black cohosh, is a perennial plant native to the eastern regions of North America, belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

The roots and rhizomes of black cohosh have been used in traditional medicine for their health benefits, which include alleviating symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes and mood swings, as well as reducing anxiety and insomnia. The bioactive constituents of black cohosh include triterpene glycosides, isopropanoid glycosides, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, black cohosh is commonly prepared as a tea, capsule, or tincture, and is often used in combination with other herbs to treat various health conditions, including osteoporosis and menstrual irregularities.

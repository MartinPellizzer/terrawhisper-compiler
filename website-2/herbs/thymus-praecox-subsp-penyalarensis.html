What is Thymus praecox subsp. penyalarensis?

Thymus praecox subsp.

penyalarensis is a subspecies of perennial herbaceous plant in the family Lamiaceae. It has a rhizomatous habit, with a woody base and a short, erect stem reaching 2-6 cm in height, bearing a pair of opposite, ovate, green leaves 1-2 cm in length and 0.5-1 cm in width.

The inflorescence is a corymb, bearing a large number of small, white or pinkish flowers with a cylindrical calyx and a campanulate corolla, 4-5 mm in length, and containing four stamens. The plant blooms from February to July, producing a moderate amount of nectar.

Its habitat includes Mediterranean-type scrublands, often in shaded areas, and it is native to the Iberian Peninsula.

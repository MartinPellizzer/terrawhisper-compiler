What is Cerasus avium f. varia?

Cerasus avium f. varia is a taxonomic variation of the European bird cherry species, belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, characterized by its ovate or lanceolate leaves, 2-5 cm in length, with serrated margins and a glabrous or pubescent surface. The leaves are pinnately veined, with 3-5 pairs of secondary veins. The inflorescence is an umbel or a corymb, bearing 2-6 white or pinkish flowers, 1-2 cm in diameter, with 5 petals and a 5-merous calyx.

The flowers are followed by a dark purple or black, globose to ovoid fruit, 0.8-1.5 cm in diameter, containing 1-2 seeds.

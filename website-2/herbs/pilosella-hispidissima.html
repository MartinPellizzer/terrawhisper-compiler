Pilosella hispidissima

Pilosella hispidissima, also known as Mouse-ear Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Pilosella hispidissima include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as topical creams and salves, which are used to treat skin conditions and wounds.

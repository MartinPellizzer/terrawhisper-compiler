Geranium fuscatum

Geranium fuscatum, also known as the dark-leaved geranium, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Geraniaceae family, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The bioactive constituents of Geranium fuscatum include geraniin, a polyphenolic compound, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is often used to prepare herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are traditionally used to treat various health issues, including digestive problems and skin conditions.

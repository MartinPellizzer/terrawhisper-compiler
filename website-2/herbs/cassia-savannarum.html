Cassia savannarum

Cassia savannarum, also known as Senna auriculata, is a plant species within the legume family Fabaceae, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, constipation, and inflammation, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and laxative properties. The bioactive constituents of Cassia savannarum include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Herbal preparations made from Cassia savannarum include decoctions, infusions, and powder, often used to treat digestive issues and promote relaxation.

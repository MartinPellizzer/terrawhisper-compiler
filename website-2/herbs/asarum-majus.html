Asarum majus

Asarum majus, also known as the large American ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and reddish-brown flowers.

The bioactive constituents of Asarum majus include iridoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Asarum majus is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are commonly used to treat digestive issues, colds, and flu, as well as to alleviate symptoms of menopause and menstrual cramps.

Phyllanthus temehaniensis

Phyllanthus temehaniensis, also known as the Tahitian cucumber tree, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands, characterized by its distinctive, cucumber-shaped fruit and medicinal properties.

The plant is valued for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Phyllanthus temehaniensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Polynesian medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, and is also used in the preparation of topical creams and ointments to treat skin conditions.

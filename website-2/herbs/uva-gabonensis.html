Uva gabonensis

Uva gabonensis, also known as the African cucumber or West African sorrel, is a plant species native to tropical Africa, characterized by its edible leaves and stems.

The health benefits of Uva gabonensis include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been shown to aid in the treatment of various diseases, including fever, malaria, and diarrhea. The bioactive constituents of Uva gabonensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Uva gabonensis is commonly prepared as a herbal tea, infusion, or decoction, and is also used in traditional medicine to make remedies for various ailments, including fever reducers and digestive aids.

Kanahia forskalii

Kanahia forskalii, also known as Indian hemp or Forskohlii, is a plant species native to the Indian subcontinent and parts of Africa.

The health benefits of Kanahia forskalii include its potential to aid in weight management, improve cardiovascular health, and exhibit anti-inflammatory properties, among others. The bioactive constituents of this plant include forskolin, a labdane diterpenoid compound, which has been found to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

Kanahia forskalii is commonly used in herbal preparations such as capsules, tablets, and extracts, often marketed as a dietary supplement for weight loss and overall well-being.

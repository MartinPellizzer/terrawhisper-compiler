Aldama squarrosa

Aldama squarrosa, also known as Aldama, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and sore throats, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Aldama squarrosa include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Aldama is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is also used in the form of salves and ointments to treat skin conditions and wounds.

Galega parviflora

Galega parviflora, also known as small-flowered galega or Italian foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in medicine due to its numerous health benefits, including its antidiabetic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which can help to regulate blood sugar levels, reduce inflammation, and protect against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Galega parviflora include galegine, a glycoside responsible for its medicinal properties, as well as other compounds such as flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids.

The most common herbal preparations made with Galega parviflora include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to support the management of type 2 diabetes and other metabolic disorders.

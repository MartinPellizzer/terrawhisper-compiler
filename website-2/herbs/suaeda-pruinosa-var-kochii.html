What is Suaeda pruinosa var. kochii?

Suaeda pruinosa var. kochii is a variety of the succulent shrub Suaeda pruinosa, belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial, glabrous, or nearly glabrous shrub with a height of up to 10 cm and a diameter of up to 2.5 cm, typically found in saline environments. The plant features lanceolate, greenish-gray leaves that are 1-4 cm long and 1-2 mm wide, and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in a raceme or panicle. The leaves and stems of Suaeda pruinosa var. kochii are often covered with a thin, white or grayish powder, giving them a pruinose appearance.

The plant grows in alkaline to saline soils, typically in areas with high salt concentrations, and is often found in association with other halophytic species.

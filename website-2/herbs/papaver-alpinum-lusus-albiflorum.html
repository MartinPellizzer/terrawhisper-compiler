Papaver alpinum lusus albiflorum

Papaver alpinum lusus albiflorum, also known as the white Alpine poppy, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the poppy family (Papaveraceae).

Traditionally, the plant has been used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including coughs, fever, and skin irritations, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Papaver alpinum lusus albiflorum include alkaloids, such as papaverine and noscapine, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In herbal medicine, the plant is typically prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion, and is often used in combination with other herbs to address respiratory and skin-related health concerns.

Salvia carnosa

Salvia carnosa, commonly known as Desert Blue Sage or Mountain Sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used to aid in digestion, relieve symptoms of anxiety and stress, and promote a healthy respiratory system, making it a valuable herb in traditional medicine. The bioactive constituents of Salvia carnosa include essential oils, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Salvia carnosa is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or dried herb, and can also be found in supplement form, making it a popular choice among herbalists and natural health enthusiasts.

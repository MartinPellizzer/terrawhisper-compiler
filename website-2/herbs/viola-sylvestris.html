Viola sylvestris

Viola sylvestris, also known as the woodland violet, is a perennial plant species belonging to the violet family (Violaceae).

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, respiratory issues, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Viola sylvestris include flavonoids, such as violanin and violasaponins, as well as anthocyanins and iridoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Viola sylvestris is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or syrup to treat colds, coughs, and sore throats, as well as to soothe digestive issues and promote wound healing.

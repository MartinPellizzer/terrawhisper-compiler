Boehmeria malabarica

Boehmeria malabarica, also known as Indian nettle, is a perennial plant species native to tropical Asia, belonging to the nettle family (Urticaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Boehmeria malabarica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional Indian medicine, Boehmeria malabarica is commonly prepared as a decoction, tea, or powder, and is also used in the form of a paste or ointment to treat skin conditions and wounds.

Salvia wuana

Salvia wuana, also known as Wu's sage or Chinese sage, is a perennial plant species belonging to the genus Salvia, characterized by its small, tubular flowers and grayish-green leaves.

Salvia wuana has been traditionally used for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Salvia wuana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and diterpenoids, such as salvinorin A, which is responsible for its sedative and anxiolytic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Salvia wuana include teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as dried extracts used in traditional Chinese medicine to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality.

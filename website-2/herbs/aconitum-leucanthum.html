Aconitum leucanthum

Aconitum leucanthum, also known as white monk's hood or white aconitum, is a perennial flowering plant in the Aconitum genus, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antipyretic properties, providing relief from pain, fever, and swelling. The bioactive constituents of Aconitum leucanthum include aconitine, aconitine alkaloids, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Aconitum leucanthum is often prepared as a tincture, decoction, or powder, and is used to treat various conditions, including arthritis, rheumatism, and respiratory issues.

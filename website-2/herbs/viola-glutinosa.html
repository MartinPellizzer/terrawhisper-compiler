Viola glutinosa

Viola glutinosa, also known as sticky violet or glutinous violet, is a perennial plant species in the violet family (Violaceae).

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Viola glutinosa include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Viola glutinosa is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion, and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and sore throat.

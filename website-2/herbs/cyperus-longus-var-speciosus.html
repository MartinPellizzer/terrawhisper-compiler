What is Cyperus longus var. speciosus?

Cyperus longus var. speciosus is a variety of the sedge Cyperus longus, belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems, often reaching up to 1.5 meters in height, and its linear-lanceolate leaves, typically 10-40 cm long. The inflorescence is a spike-like raceme, bearing numerous small, yellowish-brown flowers, and the fruit is a small nut, approximately 2-3 mm in diameter. This variety is found in a range of habitats, including wetlands and grasslands, and is native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant's growth habit is typically perennial, with a rhizomatous stem base.

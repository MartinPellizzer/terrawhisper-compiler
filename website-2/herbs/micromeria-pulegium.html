Micromeria pulegium

Micromeria pulegium, also known as pennyroyal, is a low-growing, perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae) that is native to Europe and the Mediterranean region.

The plant has been traditionally used for its health benefits, including its ability to alleviate digestive issues, reduce anxiety and stress, and act as a natural insect repellent. The bioactive constituents of Micromeria pulegium include essential oils, particularly pulegone and menthone, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, pennyroyal is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or oil, and is used to treat a range of health issues, including menstrual cramps, indigestion, and colds.

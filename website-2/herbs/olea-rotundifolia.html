Olea rotundifolia

Olea rotundifolia, also known as the wild olive tree, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its small, round leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The leaves and fruits of the wild olive tree have been used in traditional medicine for centuries, with reported health benefits including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which may help to lower cholesterol levels, improve cardiovascular health, and support the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Olea rotundifolia include oleuropein, hydroxytyrosol, and tyrosol, which have been shown to have potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

In herbal medicine, the leaves and fruits of Olea rotundifolia are commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, or used in the production of olive oil, which is valued for its culinary and medicinal properties.

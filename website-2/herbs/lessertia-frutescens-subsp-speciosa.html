What is Lessertia frutescens subsp. speciosa?

Lessertia frutescens subsp.

speciosa is an endemic plant species native to South Africa, belonging to the family Fabaceae. It is an herbaceous perennial shrub with erect, woody stems up to 1 meter in height and 1.5 meters in diameter, bearing bipinnate leaves with 10-20 pairs of leaflets.

The leaves are dark green, lanceolate, and 2-5 cm long, while the flowers are yellow, fragrant, and arranged in axillary racemes. Lessertia frutescens subsp. speciosa grows in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky outcrops and grasslands.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

The most common ailments you can heal with Lessertia frutescens subsp.

speciosa are inflammation, asthma, and rheumatism, which it alleviates through its anti-inflammatory properties and ability to inhibit histamine release, thereby reducing symptoms such as cough, skin irritation, and eczema. Additionally, diabetes can be managed through its antidiabetic properties, which help regulate blood sugar levels.

It also exhibits antihypertensive properties, reducing hypertension and helping to alleviate symptoms of urinary tract infections such as pain and discomfort. Furthermore, it has been shown to have antimalarial properties, making it effective in treating malaria, and its antineoplastic properties make it useful in treating cancer.

Its antidiarrheal, antiulcer, and anti-inflammatory properties also make it effective in treating diarrhea, stomach pain, gastroenteritis, ulcers, gastrointestinal issues, and gout.

What are the therapeutic properties this herb?

The main therapeutic properties of Lessertia frutescens subsp.

speciosa are its antimicrobial activity, which is attributed to the presence of saponins that inhibit the growth of various pathogens, and its anti-inflammatory properties, which are mediated by flavonoids that reduce inflammation and alleviate pain, including its analgesic effects, making it a valuable remedy for pain management.

This plant also exhibits antioxidant activity, with its high content of volatile oils that neutralize free radicals and protect against oxidative stress, contributing to its hepatoprotective effects, which safeguard the liver from damage caused by toxins and promote its overall health.

What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

The most important healing constituents of Lessertia frutescens subsp.

speciosa are lessertin, a triterpenoid glycoside with anti-inflammatory properties, quercetin and kaempferol, flavonoids with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, and quinovic acid glycoside, a triterpenoid with potential antibacterial and anti-inflammatory effects. These constituents contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Lessertin has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines. Quercetin and kaempferol have been reported to scavenge free radicals and inhibit the activity of pro-inflammatory enzymes.

The combined action of these constituents may explain the plant's traditional use in treating various conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory infections.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Lessertia frutescens subsp.

speciosa are infusion drink, khat tincture, khat tea, decoction, and khat capsules. Infusion drink is made by steeping the dried leaves in hot water to release its bioactive compounds, including cathinone and cathine.

Khat tincture is a concentrated liquid extract of the plant made by soaking the leaves in a solvent such as ethanol or glycerin. Khat tea is prepared by steeping the dried leaves in hot water, similar to infusion drink but often with a longer steeping time to release more alkaloids.

Decoction and khat capsules are also used to prepare the plant for consumption, with decoction involving boiling the leaves in water and capsules containing dried and powdered leaves in a gelatin or vegetarian shell.

These side effects occur due to the presence of anthraquinone glycosides in the plant, which can cause gastrointestinal irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals. When used improperly, Lessertia frutescens subsp.

speciosa can cause severe gastrointestinal upset, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, as the body attempts to eliminate the toxic compounds. In severe cases, allergic reactions can occur, ranging from mild skin irritation to anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition that requires immediate medical attention. The improper use of Lessertia frutescens subsp.

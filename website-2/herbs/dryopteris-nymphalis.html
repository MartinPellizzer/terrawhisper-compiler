Dryopteris nymphalis

Dryopteris nymphalis, also known as the Nymph fern, is a type of fern that belongs to the genus Dryopteris, a group of vascular plants characterized by their distinctive fern-like foliage.

The Nymph fern has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to reduce inflammation and protect against cell damage. Bioactive constituents of the Nymph fern include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which have been shown to possess antimicrobial and antifungal activities.

In traditional herbalism, the Nymph fern is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is used to support respiratory health, reduce fever, and alleviate menstrual cramps.

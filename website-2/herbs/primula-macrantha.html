Primula macrantha

Primula macrantha, commonly known as the tall primrose or mountain primrose, is a species of flowering plant in the Primulaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various health issues, including coughs, sore throats, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. The bioactive constituents of Primula macrantha include iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Primula macrantha include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to relieve respiratory and gastrointestinal issues, as well as to promote wound healing.

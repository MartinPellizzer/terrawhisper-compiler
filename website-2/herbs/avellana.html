Avellana

The Avellana plant, also known as the Hazel or Filbert tree (Corylus avellana), is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and Western Asia, characterized by its edible nuts and dark green leaves.

The health benefits of Avellana include its potential to support cardiovascular health, lower cholesterol levels, and act as an antioxidant due to its rich content of vitamins, minerals, and polyphenolic compounds. The bioactive constituents of Avellana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and phenolic glucosides, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Avellana include teas, infusions, and extracts, often used to support heart health, digestion, and immune function.

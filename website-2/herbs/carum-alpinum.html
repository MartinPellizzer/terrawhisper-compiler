Carum alpinum

Carum alpinum, also known as caraway or merwah, is a biennial herb in the parsley family (Apiaceae) native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa.

The seeds of Carum alpinum have been used in traditional medicine to provide relief from digestive issues, such as indigestion, bloating, and gas, due to their antispasmodic, carminative, and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Carum alpinum include carvone, limonene, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The seeds are commonly used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as added to spice blends and used as a flavoring agent in baked goods and confectionery.

Filaginella rossica

Filaginella rossica, also known as Ross's filaginella, is a type of succulent plant belonging to the family Plumbaginaceae.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, as well as its potential to soothe and calm the skin. The bioactive constituents of Filaginella rossica include flavonoids, terpenoids, and plumbagin, a unique compound that contributes to the plant's medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Filaginella rossica is used in herbal medicine to prepare infusions, tinctures, and ointments, which are used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and other inflammatory disorders.

Rhamnus roylei

Rhamnus roylei, commonly known as the Roylean ash or Indian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan region.

This plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine, offering health benefits such as reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and aiding in the treatment of various gastrointestinal disorders. The bioactive constituents of Rhamnus roylei include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

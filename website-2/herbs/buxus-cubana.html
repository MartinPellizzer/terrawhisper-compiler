Buxus cubana

Buxus cubana, also known as Cuban boxwood, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Cuba and the Greater Antilles, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include its ability to lower blood pressure, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial and antifungal activities, making it a potential treatment for various health conditions. The bioactive constituents of Buxus cubana include alkaloids, saponins, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health issues, including hypertension, digestive problems, and skin conditions.

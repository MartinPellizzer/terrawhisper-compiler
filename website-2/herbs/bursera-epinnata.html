Bursera epinnata

Bursera epinnata, also known as the forest frankincense or firnal tree, is a tropical plant species native to the Philippines and Southeast Asia, belonging to the Burseraceae family.

The resin extracted from Bursera epinnata has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and skin infections, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of Bursera epinnata resin include sesquiterpenes, diterpenes, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used in traditional medicine and is often prepared as a resin extract, resin tincture, or infused oil, which can be used topically or taken orally to harness its health benefits.

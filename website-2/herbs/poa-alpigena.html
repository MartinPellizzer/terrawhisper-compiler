Poa alpigena

Poa alpigena, also known as Alpine Meadow-grass, is a species of grass native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes mountains in South America.

Traditionally, the plant has been used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which may help alleviate symptoms of conditions like arthritis and improve overall well-being. The bioactive constituents of Poa alpigena include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Poa alpigena is commonly prepared as a tea or infusion, and in some cases, as a dried extract or capsule, to support immune function and reduce inflammation.

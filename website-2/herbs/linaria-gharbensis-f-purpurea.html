What is Linaria gharbensis f. purpurea?

Linaria gharbensis f. purpurea is a flowering plant that belongs to the family Plantaginaceae, which comprises approximately 80 genera, including Digitalis and Veronica.

It is a taxonomic variant of Linaria gharbensis, characterized by its purple flowers, which are typically funnel-shaped and have a length of 2-3.5 cm. The plant's leaves are lanceolate or ovate in shape, with a length of 2-6 cm and a width of 0.5-2 cm, and are arranged oppositely on the stem. Linaria gharbensis f. purpurea is a perennial herb that grows to a height of 10-30 cm, with a branching stem and a rosette-like base.

The plant's inflorescence is a terminal spike or raceme, bearing 2-10 flowers.

Osmorhiza montana

Osmorhiza montana, also known as Mountain Sweetroot or American Sweetroot, is a perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects, which can help alleviate digestive issues, muscle cramps, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Osmorhiza montana include coumarins, furanocoumarins, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Osmorhiza montana is commonly prepared as a tincture, decoction, or infusion, often used to treat digestive issues, anxiety, and insomnia.

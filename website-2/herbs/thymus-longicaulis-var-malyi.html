What is Thymus longicaulis var. malyi?

Thymus longicaulis var. malyi is a variety of the species Thymus longicaulis, belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is an evergreen perennial herb with a woody base and a woody stem that can grow up to 20 cm in height, with linear, entire, and glabrous leaves that are 5-10 mm in length and 0.5-1 mm in width. The inflorescence is a cymose spike, bearing white to pinkish flowers that are 6-8 mm in length and 3-4 mm in width, with a corolla tube that is 3-4 mm in length. The leaves and stems are aromatic due to the presence of essential oils, particularly thymol and carvacrol.

This variety is native to the Caucasus region.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Thymus longicaulis var. malyi are tea, infusion, thyme tea, tincture, and thyme infusion.

Thyme tea is a variant of the tea preparation, often used for its antimicrobial properties. Tincture is a concentrated liquid extract prepared by soaking the leaves in a solvent like ethanol or glycerin, commonly used for medicinal purposes.

Thyme infusion is a liquid preparation made by infusing the leaves in cold water, often used for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

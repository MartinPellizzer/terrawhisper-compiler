Pelargonium concavifolium

Pelargonium concavifolium, also known as Mountain Geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family, native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, contributing to its potential in treating various health issues such as wounds, infections, and digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of Pelargonium concavifolium include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic activities.

The plant is often used to make various herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are traditionally used to treat colds, fever, and other respiratory issues.

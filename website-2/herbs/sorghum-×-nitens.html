Sorghum × nitens

Sorghum × nitens, also known as sorghum, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the grass family (Poaceae) and is widely cultivated for its grain, which serves as a rich source of nutrients and has various medicinal properties.

The health benefits of sorghum include its potential to lower cholesterol levels, improve insulin sensitivity, and provide anti-inflammatory effects, making it a valuable crop for promoting cardiovascular health and managing diabetes. The bioactive constituents of sorghum include phenolic compounds, flavonoids, and anthocyanins, which are responsible for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Traditionally, sorghum is used to make various herbal preparations, such as tea, powder, and flour, which are used to treat digestive issues, alleviate stress, and support immune function.

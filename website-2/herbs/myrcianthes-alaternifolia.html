Myrcianthes alaternifolia

Myrcianthes alaternifolia, commonly known as the Chilean guava or Chilean myrtle, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the southern Andes in Chile and Argentina.

The bioactive constituents of Myrcianthes alaternifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

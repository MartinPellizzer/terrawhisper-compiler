Aster lepidus

Aster lepidus, also known as the Woolly Aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States, characterized by its small white or pink flowers and woolly leaves.

The bioactive constituents of Aster lepidus include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

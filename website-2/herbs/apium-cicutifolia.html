Apium cicutifolia

Apium cicutifolia, also known as wild celery or wild lovage, is a perennial herb native to Europe and Western Asia, characterized by its tall stems and aromatic foliage.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, sesquiterpenes, and coumarins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

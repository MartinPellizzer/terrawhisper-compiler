Muscari clusianum

Muscari clusianum, also known as Grape Hyacinth, is a low-growing, bulbous perennial plant with dense spikes of blue, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, making it useful in treating various health conditions such as respiratory issues, skin irritations, and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Muscari clusianum include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, Grape Hyacinth is often used in preparations such as tinctures, infusions, and salves, which are commonly used to treat respiratory issues, skin conditions, and to promote wound healing.

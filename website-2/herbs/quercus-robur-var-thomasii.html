What is Quercus robur var. thomasii?

Quercus robur var. thomasii is a variety of pedunculate oak, belonging to the family Fagaceae.

It is a deciduous tree with a broad, rounded crown, typically reaching heights of 20-30 meters. The bark is grey, becoming increasingly fissured with age, and the leaves are lobed with 7-9 pointed lobes, 6-13 centimeters long, with 10-20 pairs of veins. The leaves are pubescent on both sides when young, but become glabrous with age.

The variety is characterized by its larger leaves and shorter petioles compared to the nominate subspecies.

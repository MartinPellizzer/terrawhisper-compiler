Mandevilla speciosa

Mandevilla speciosa, also known as the Rocktrumpet or Mandevilla, is a flowering vine native to South America, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers and glossy leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including reducing inflammation, relieving pain, and exhibiting antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Mandevilla speciosa include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Mandevilla is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, or used in topical applications to treat skin conditions and wounds.

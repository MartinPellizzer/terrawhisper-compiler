Arctium majus

Arctium majus, commonly known as greater burdock, is a biennial plant belonging to the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including the treatment of skin conditions, digestive issues, and fever reduction, as well as its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Arctium majus include inulin, arctiopicrin, and arctigenin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Greater burdock is often used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into skincare products and supplements.

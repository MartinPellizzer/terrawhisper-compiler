Krameria lappacea

Krameria lappacea, also known as Macho or Ratanhia, is a plant species native to South America, belonging to the family Eremocactaceae.

The bioactive constituents of Krameria lappacea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and glycosides, particularly 6-hydroxykrameria glycoside, which has been identified as the primary responsible compound for its medicinal properties.

Krameria lappacea is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, tinctures, and ointments, which are used to treat oral mucositis, gum inflammation, and other mouth-related issues.

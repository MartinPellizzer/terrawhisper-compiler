Boronia laevigata

Boronia laevigata, also known as the smooth boronia, is a small shrub native to eastern Australia, characterized by its fragrant flowers and smooth, greyish-brown bark.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which may help to soothe skin irritations and promote wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Boronia laevigata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Boronia laevigata include teas, tinctures, and topical creams, which are often used to treat skin conditions, such as acne and eczema.

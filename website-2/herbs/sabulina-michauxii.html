Sabulina michauxii

Sabulina michauxii, also known as Eastern Sabulina or Sabulina, is a perennial herbaceous plant in the gentian family (Gentianaceae) that grows in the southeastern United States.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of colds and flu, as well as aid in wound healing. Sabulina michauxii contains bioactive constituents such as iridoid glycosides and xanthones, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Sabulina is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat respiratory issues and promote overall well-being.

Aira obtusa

Aira obtusa, also known as Hairgrass or Japanese hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia, characterized by its distinctive, hair-like inflorescences.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Aira obtusa include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Aira obtusa is often prepared as a decoction, powder, or tea, and is commonly used to treat symptoms such as cough, sore throat, and digestive issues.

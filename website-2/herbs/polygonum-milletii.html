Polygonum milletii

Polygonum milletii, also known as Chinese knotweed, is a perennial plant species native to China, characterized by its unique, knot-like stem and clusters of small, green flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiviral properties, making it a potential treatment for conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Polygonum milletii include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

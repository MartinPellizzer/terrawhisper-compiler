Crataegus flava lobata

Crataegus flava lobata, also known as the golden hawthorn, is a species of hawthorn tree that is native to Central Asia.

This plant has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation, lowering blood pressure, and improving cardiovascular health, likely due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Crataegus flava lobata include flavonoids, triterpenoids, and oligomeric proanthocyanidins (OPCs), which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Crataegus flava lobata is commonly prepared as a tincture, tea, or capsule, and is used to support heart health and reduce symptoms of anxiety and insomnia.

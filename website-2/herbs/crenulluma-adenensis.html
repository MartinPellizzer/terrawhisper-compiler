Crenulluma adenensis

Crenulluma adenensis, also known as Adenium obesum, is a succulent plant species native to the Horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula, characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and vibrant red flowers.

The health benefits of Crenulluma adenensis include its potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in reducing inflammation, protecting against cell damage, and combating certain infections. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, such as adenulin, adenin, and betulinic acid, which have been identified as responsible for its medicinal properties.

Crenulluma adenensis is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and infusions, to support cardiovascular health, reduce fever, and alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections.

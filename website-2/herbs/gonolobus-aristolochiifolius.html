Gonolobus aristolochiifolius

Gonolobus aristolochiifolius, also known as Aristolochia aristolochiifolia or Aristolochia, is a type of vining plant native to Central and South America, belonging to the Aristolochiaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiviral properties, which may aid in treating various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Gonolobus aristolochiifolius include aristolochic acid, which has been reported to exhibit anti-inflammatory and anticancer activities, as well as other alkaloids and flavonoids.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion, although its use is often limited due to concerns over the toxicity of aristolochic acid, which may cause kidney damage and other adverse effects.

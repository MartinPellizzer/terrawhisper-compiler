Sedum albiflorum

Sedum albiflorum, also known as white sedum, is a succulent plant species that belongs to the Crassulaceae family.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, as it has been found to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities, contributing to its potential health benefits in treating conditions such as arthritis and wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Sedum albiflorum include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its pharmacological effects.

Sedum albiflorum is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine and is often prepared as a decoction or tincture to harness its medicinal properties.

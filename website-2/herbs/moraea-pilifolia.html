Moraea pilifolia

Moraea pilifolia, also known as the woolly harp iris, is a species of flowering plant in the family Iridaceae, native to the Western Cape province of South Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antibacterial effects, which can aid in wound healing, reducing pain and inflammation. Bioactive constituents of Moraea pilifolia include flavonoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, as well as iridoids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The plant is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine, and its extracts or preparations are often found in topical creams, ointments, and infusions used for skin conditions, wounds, and other inflammatory issues.

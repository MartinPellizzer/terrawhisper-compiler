Clematis subumbellata

Clematis subumbellata, also known as the Asian clematis, is a species of flowering plant in the Ranunculaceae family, native to East Asia and characterized by its blue-purple flowers and woody vines.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which have been shown to aid in wound healing, reduce fever, and alleviate respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Clematis subumbellata include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and digestive issues.

Rubia tricornis

Rubia tricornis, also known as Indian madder, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Rubiaceae family, traditionally valued for its vibrant red root and various medicinal properties.

The health benefits of Rubia tricornis include its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties, which are said to help alleviate symptoms of arthritis, improve wound healing, and support immune function. The bioactive constituents of this plant include anthraquinone glycosides, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Rubia tricornis is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and capsules, as well as being used as a natural dye in textiles and cosmetics.

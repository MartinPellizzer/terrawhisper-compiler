What is Cuspidia cernua subsp. annua?

Cuspidia cernua subsp.

annua is an annual plant that belongs to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its erect stem, which reaches a height of 10-30 cm, and its simple leaves, which are linear to lanceolate in shape, measuring 1-5 cm long, and are glabrous or sparsely hairy.

The inflorescence is a corymb with 2-5 daisy-like flower heads, each measuring 0.5-1.5 cm in diameter, with white or pinkish flowers and a yellow disc. The leaves and stems are covered with a fine white powder, giving them a silvery appearance.

The plant produces achenes, which are 3-5 mm long, with a pappus of 12-15 bristles.

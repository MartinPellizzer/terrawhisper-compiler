Solanum vulgatum

Solanum vulgare, also known as bittersweet nightshade, is a perennial plant that belongs to the Solanaceae family, characterized by its green or purple stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small white or purple flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, reduce oxidative stress, and combat infections. The bioactive constituents of Solanum vulgare include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, such as solanine and solasodine, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Solanum vulgare is commonly used in herbal preparations, including tinctures, infusions, and ointments, to support digestive health and treat various skin conditions, although its use should be cautious due to potential toxicity.

Pinus lowiana

Pinus lowiana, also known as the giant sugar pine or low's pine, is a species of evergreen coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of California and Oregon.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, and limonene, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

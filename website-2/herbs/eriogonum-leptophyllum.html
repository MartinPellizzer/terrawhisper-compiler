Eriogonum leptophyllum

Eriogonum leptophyllum, also known as desert trumpet or narrow-leaved buckwheat, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including digestive problems and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Eriogonum leptophyllum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Eriogonum leptophyllum is often prepared as a tea or tincture to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome, as well as to promote wound healing and reduce inflammation.

Fraxinus sikkimensis

Fraxinus sikkimensis, also known as the Sikkim ash, is a deciduous tree species in the Oleaceae family native to the Himalayas.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiviral activities, making it beneficial for treating various health conditions such as fever, arthritis, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Fraxinus sikkimensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant are infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are traditionally used to treat colds, fever, and other respiratory issues.

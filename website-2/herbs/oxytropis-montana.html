Oxytropis montana

Oxytropis montana, also known as alpine locoweed or mountain locoweed, is a perennial plant species belonging to the pea family (Fabaceae).

Traditionally, Oxytropis montana has been used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive problems, due to its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Oxytropis montana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which have been reported to exhibit antioxidant and cytotoxic activities.

The plant is often used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as dried powder for capsules or tablets, which are commonly used in traditional and alternative medicine.

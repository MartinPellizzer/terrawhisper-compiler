Cyperus cruciformis

Cyperus cruciformis, also known as Cruciferous Sedge, is a type of plant that belongs to the Cyperaceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Cyperus cruciformis include alkaloids, saponins, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Cyperus cruciformis is often made into teas, infusions, or decoctions to treat a range of health problems, and is also used in some Asian cultures to make medicinal wines and tonics.

Aloe americana

Aloe americana, also known as American aloe, is a succulent plant species that belongs to the Aloe genus and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The gel of the Aloe americana plant has been used for its medicinal properties, providing health benefits such as soothing skin irritations, reducing inflammation, and aiding in wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Aloe americana include aloin, aloemodin, and acemannan, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Aloe americana include topical gels, creams, and ointments for skin care, as well as oral supplements in the form of capsules or teas for digestive and immune system support.

Phaseolus dalzellianus

Phaseolus dalzellianus, also known as Dalzell's kidney bean, is a plant species native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its rich content of antioxidants, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Phaseolus dalzellianus include flavonoids, alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a decoction, powder, or infusion, and is used to treat a range of ailments, including fever, diarrhea, and respiratory infections.

Triodia caroliniana

Triodia caroliniana, also known as Quandong Bush or Triodia, is a species of grass native to the deserts of Western Australia and the Northern Territory.

It has been used for centuries by Indigenous Australians for its medicinal properties, which include treating digestive issues, wounds, and fever, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Triodia caroliniana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The plant is commonly prepared as a herbal tea, capsule, or tincture, and has also been used to make a sweet, edible fruit paste, known as quandong paste.

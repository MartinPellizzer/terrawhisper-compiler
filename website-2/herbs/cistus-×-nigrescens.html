Cistus × nigrescens

Cistus × nigrescens, also known as the purple-flowered cistus, is a hybrid shrub plant in the Cistus genus, native to the Mediterranean region and known for its fragrant, showy purple flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help to soothe skin irritations, fight infections, and protect against cell damage. The bioactive constituents of Cistus × nigrescens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Cistus × nigrescens is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as topical creams and ointments, to harness its potential health benefits.

Ligularia palmatifida

Ligularia palmatifida, also known as Handkerchief Ligularia, is a perennial plant species in the family Asteraceae, native to China and Japan, characterized by its palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers.

The plant is valued for its traditional medicinal uses, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in reducing fever, relieving pain, and treating infections. Bioactive constituents of Ligularia palmatifida include sesquiterpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction or a tea, and is also used in infusions and tinctures to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues.

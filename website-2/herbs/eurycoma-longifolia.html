What is Eurycoma longifolia?

Eurycoma longifolia is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, belonging to the family Simaroubaceae.

It is characterized by its long, pointed leaves that are dark green in color and have a leathery texture, typically growing in clusters at the ends of branches. The tree can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. Eurycoma longifolia is commonly known as the "Tongkat Ali" tree, and its roots have been used in traditional medicine for their potential adaptogenic and tonic properties.

The tree's roots contain a high concentration of the bioactive compound eurycomanone, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most important healing constituents of Eurycoma longifolia are eurycomanone, eurycolactone, eurycomanol, eurycomanin, and quercetin.

Eurycomanol has been reported to have immunomodulatory effects, enhancing immune function and protecting against oxidative stress, while eurycomanin has been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities.

Quercetin, a flavonoid present in Eurycoma longifolia, has been demonstrated to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-cancer properties, as well as modulating the immune response to prevent disease progression.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Eurycoma longifolia are tincture, capsules, tea, eurycoma extract, and oil.

Tincture is a concentrated liquid solution typically made with the plant's roots or seeds in a solvent like ethanol or glycerin, providing a bioavailable form of the herb. Capsules contain a standardized extract of the plant, often providing a precise dosage of the key compounds, such as eurycomanone and eurycomalactone. Eurycoma tea is prepared by infusing the plant's roots or seeds in hot water, releasing its bioactive compounds, although the efficacy may be lower compared to other preparations.

Eurycoma extract and oil are highly concentrated forms of the herb, often obtained through solvent extraction or distillation, and are commonly used in supplements and topical applications.

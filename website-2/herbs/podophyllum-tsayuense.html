Podophyllum tsayuense

Podophyllum tsayuense is a species of flowering plant in the barberry family, native to Russia and Japan.

It has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and antitumor effects, which have been attributed to its ability to inhibit cell proliferation and induce apoptosis in cancer cells. The bioactive constituents of Podophyllum tsayuense include podophyllotoxin and its derivatives, as well as lignans and other phenolic compounds, which have been found to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Traditionally, the plant has been used to make herbal preparations such as extracts, tinctures, and decoctions, which are used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and certain types of cancer.

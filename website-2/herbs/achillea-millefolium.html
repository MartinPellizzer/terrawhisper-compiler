What is Achillea millefolium?

Achillea millefolium is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Asteraceae.

It has a taproot and a stem that grows up to 120 cm in height, with an umbel of small, white or yellow flowers. The leaves are pinnately divided into 3-5 pairs of toothed segments, with a rounded tip and a cuneate base. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces achenes with a crown of scales and a pappus of 2-3 bristles.

Achillea millefolium is native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa, but has been naturalized in many other parts of the world.

Cymbopogon sennarensis

Cymbopogon sennarensis, also known as African lemongrass or East African lemongrass, is a species of grass in the genus Cymbopogon.

The plant has been traditionally used in African and Middle Eastern medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Cymbopogon sennarensis include citral, geraniol, and limonene, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Cymbopogon sennarensis is often used to make tea, infusions, and essential oils, which are used to relieve stress, promote digestion, and boost the immune system.

Astragalus tataricus

Astragalus tataricus, also known as Huang Qi, is a perennial plant species in the legume family (Fabaceae) native to China and Mongolia.

It is valued for its adaptogenic properties, and is used to promote immune function, reduce inflammation, and alleviate stress. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus tataricus include polysaccharides, saponins, flavonoids, and isoflavones, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Astragalus tataricus is often prepared as a decoction or dried root extract, and is also used in the form of capsules, tablets, and teas to support overall health and well-being.

side effects

Astragalus tataricus is generally considered safe when taken in moderate amounts, but high doses may cause side effects such as gastrointestinal issues, dizziness, and allergic reactions in some individuals. It may also interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and diabetes medications, which can worsen side effects. Additionally, Astragalus tataricus may not be suitable for people with bleeding disorders or those undergoing surgery due to its potential to increase bleeding risk. It is also recommended to consult a healthcare professional before using Astragalus tataricus, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are pregnant/breastfeeding. In rare cases, Astragalus tataricus may cause more severe side effects, such as liver damage or kidney problems, but these are typically associated with long-term or excessive use.

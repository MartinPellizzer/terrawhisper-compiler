Picea mariana

Picea mariana, also known as black spruce, is a species of evergreen tree native to northern North America.

The resin of black spruce has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including respiratory problems, such as bronchitis and coughs, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of black spruce resin include terpenes like alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, and limonene, as well as phenolic compounds like phenolic acids and flavonoids.

Black spruce resin is commonly used in herbal preparations such as cough syrups, expectorants, and respiratory tonics, often in combination with other herbs like thyme and eucalyptus.

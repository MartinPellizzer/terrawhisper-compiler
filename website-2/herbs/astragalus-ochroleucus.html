Astragalus ochroleucus

Astragalus ochroleucus, also known as the white milk vetch, is a perennial legume native to the northwestern United States and southwestern Canada.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory properties, making it a popular herbal remedy for supporting immune function and reducing stress. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus ochroleucus include saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a decoction, tea, or tincture, and is often used to support respiratory health and boost energy levels.

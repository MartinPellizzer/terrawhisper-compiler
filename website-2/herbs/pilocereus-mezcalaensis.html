Pilocereus mezcalaensis

Pilocereus mezcalaensis, also known as mezcal cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico, characterized by its cylindrical stems and sharp spines.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which can aid in wound healing and reduce pain and inflammation. The bioactive constituents of Pilocereus mezcalaensis include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Mexican herbalism, mezcal cactus is often used to make infusions, teas, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for its anti-inflammatory and healing effects.

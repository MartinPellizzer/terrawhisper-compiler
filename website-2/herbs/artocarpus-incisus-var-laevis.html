What is Artocarpus incisus var. laevis?

Artocarpus incisus var. laevis is a plant species that belongs to the family Moraceae.

It is a type of fig tree that exhibits smooth leaves, with the leaf blades averaging 10-25 cm in length and 5-12 cm in width, having 3-5 lobes and an elliptic shape with a rounded base. The leaf margins are entire to crenate, and the leaf apex is acute. The inflorescence is a syconium, an enclosed cluster of tiny flowers, which is a characteristic feature of the Moraceae family.

The plant grows as a small to medium-sized tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10-15 meters.

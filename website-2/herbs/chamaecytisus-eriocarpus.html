Chamaecytisus eriocarpus

Chamaecytisus eriocarpus, also known as Broom or Scotch Broom, is a perennial legume shrub native to Europe and Western Asia, characterized by its yellow flowers and long, slender stems.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral activities, as well as the potential to lower blood pressure and improve cardiovascular health. The bioactive constituents of Chamaecytisus eriocarpus include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Chamaecytisus eriocarpus is often made into teas, infusions, and tinctures to take advantage of its medicinal properties, although it is essential to note that the plant is also considered invasive in some regions and its use should be carefully evaluated and regulated.

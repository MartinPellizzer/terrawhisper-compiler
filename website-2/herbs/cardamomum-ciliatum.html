The bioactive constituents of Cardamomum ciliatum include volatile oils, such as limonene and alpha-terpinene, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Ayurvedic and Unani medicine, Cardamomum ciliatum is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as in combination with other herbs to create formulations for digestive issues and respiratory problems.

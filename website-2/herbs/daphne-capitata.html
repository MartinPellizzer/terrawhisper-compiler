Daphne capitata

Daphne capitata, also known as the Button Bush, is a perennial plant native to Europe and Western Asia, characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

The bioactive constituents of Daphne capitata include daphnetin, daphnodorin, and daphniphylline, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Daphne capitata is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, and is also used in the production of essential oils and pharmaceuticals.

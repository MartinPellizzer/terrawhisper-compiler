side effects

Pastinaca maxima, also known as giant parsnip, contains compounds that can cause skin irritation, photodermatitis, and contact dermatitis. When the plant's sap comes into contact with the skin, it can cause redness, itching, and blistering. Ingestion of the plant's roots or leaves can lead to gastrointestinal symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. If ingested in large quantities, giant parsnip can cause more severe symptoms like kidney damage and respiratory problems. It's essential to handle the plant with care and wear protective clothing when working with it.

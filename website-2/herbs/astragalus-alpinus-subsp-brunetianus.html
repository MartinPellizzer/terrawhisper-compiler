What is Astragalus alpinus subsp. brunetianus?

Astragalus alpinus subsp.

brunetianus is a subspecies of the alpine milk-vetch plant, belonging to the family Fabaceae. It is a perennial herb with a woody base, typically 5-15 cm tall, with a few stems and leaves.

The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, 2-4 cm long, and glabrous, while the flowers are pinkish-purple, 6-8 mm long, and in a raceme. The fruit is an indehiscent legume, 6-10 mm long, containing 1-2 seeds.

This subspecies is endemic to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

Pratia gelida

Pratia gelida, also known as Blue Star Creeper, is a low-growing, spreading perennial plant native to Australia, characterized by its small blue flowers and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, as well as its potential to aid in wound healing and reduce the risk of infection. The bioactive constituents of Pratia gelida include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Pratia gelida is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to support immune function and skin health.

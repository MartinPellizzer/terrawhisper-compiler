Panax valdiviensis

Panax valdiviensis, also known as the Chilean ginseng, is a perennial plant species native to the Andes Mountains in Chile and Argentina.

This plant has been traditionally used to support overall health and well-being, with reported health benefits including improved immune function, reduced inflammation, and enhanced cognitive function. The bioactive constituents of Panax valdiviensis include ginsenosides, specifically valdivialactone, valdivioside, and valdivinone, as well as other polyphenolic compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

The most common herbal preparations made from Panax valdiviensis include teas, capsules, and extracts, often used to support energy, vitality, and mental clarity.

Dischisma spicatum

Dischisma spicatum, also known as the "cluster bush," is a shrubby plant species belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate conditions such as arthritis, asthma, and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Dischisma spicatum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, digestive problems, and skin conditions.

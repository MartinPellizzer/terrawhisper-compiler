classification

Stevia eupatoria is classified as a member of the Lamiaceae family, also known as the mint family. This classification is based on its botanical characteristics, such as its leaf shape and flower structure. As a member of the Lamiaceae family, Stevia eupatoria is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. Its classification also places it within the Stevia genus, which is a group of plants known for their sweet-tasting leaves. Stevia eupatoria is closely related to other members of the Lamiaceae family, including the popular herbs mint and oregano.

Stevia eupatoria is traditionally used to treat various health conditions. The plant's leaves have been used in folk medicine to help manage digestive issues, such as diarrhea and constipation. However, more research is needed to confirm the efficacy and safety of using Stevia eupatoria for medicinal purposes.

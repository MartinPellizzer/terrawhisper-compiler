What is Pfaffia gnaphalioides var. planifolia?

Pfaffia gnaphalioides var. planifolia is a perennial plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and glabrous, with a linear-lanceolate shape, 5-20 cm long, and 0.5-2 cm wide, with a petiole 0.5-2 cm long. The plant produces small, inconspicuous, greenish-yellow flowers in axillary cymes, which are 1-2 mm in diameter. The inflorescence is a spiciform corymb, and the plant reaches a height of 10-30 cm.

The plant is found in the wild in southern South America, particularly in the grasslands and savannas of Brazil and Argentina.

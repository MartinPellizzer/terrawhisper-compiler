Kochia hirsuta

Kochia hirsuta, also known as hairy Kochia or Chinese burnweed, is a perennial plant species within the Amaranthaceae family, native to the Old World.

The health benefits of Kochia hirsuta include its potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory properties, which may aid in the management of various conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Kochia hirsuta is commonly used in traditional herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as in modern herbal supplements in the form of capsules or tablets.

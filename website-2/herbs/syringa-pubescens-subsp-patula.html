What is Syringa pubescens subsp. patula?

Syringa pubescens subsp.

patula is an ornamental shrub belonging to the family Oleaceae. It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers with a white color, produced in terminal panicles, and its leaves are lanceolate to ovate, 2-5 cm long, with a hairy surface.

The plant grows up to 2-3 meters tall, with a spread of 1.5-2.5 meters, and has a deciduous habit. Syringa pubescens subsp. patula is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its growth rate is moderate to fast, and it prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

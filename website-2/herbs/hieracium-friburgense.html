Hieracium friburgense

Hieracium friburgense, also known as Friburgh hawkweed, is a plant species in the dandelion-like hawkweed genus Hieracium.

Traditionally, Hieracium friburgense has been used to support digestion and alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal issues, such as indigestion and bloating, due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Hieracium friburgense include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and polyphenols, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Hieracium friburgense include teas, tinctures, and infusions, often used in traditional folk medicine to promote overall health and well-being.

Selaginella pumila

Selaginella pumila, also known as Spike Moss or Dwarf Spikemoss, is a small, terrestrial plant species belonging to the genus Selaginella, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. The bioactive constituents of Selaginella pumila include terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal preparations, Selaginella pumila is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being combined with other herbs in traditional remedies to enhance their therapeutic effects.

Ipomoea sulphurea

Ipomoea sulphurea, also known as the Yellow Morning Glory, is a flowering vine native to Mexico and Central America.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including the treatment of fever, cough, and respiratory issues, as well as its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea sulphurea include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Ipomoea sulphurea include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory infections and to soothe coughs and sore throats.

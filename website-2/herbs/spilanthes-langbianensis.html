Spilanthes langbianensis

Spilanthes langbianensis is a species of plant in the family Asteraceae, commonly known as the "tongue-tingling" or "lip-smacking" herb due to its unique property of temporarily altering the sensation of taste.

This plant has been traditionally used in Southeast Asian medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and gastrointestinal issues, due to its potent bioactive constituents, which include spilanthol, a sesquiterpene lactone responsible for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Spilanthes langbianensis also include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal value.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often made into teas, infusions, or tinctures, as well as being used in topical applications, such as creams and ointments, to take advantage of its therapeutic properties.

Tillandsia guatemalensis

Tillandsia guatemalensis, also known as Guatemalan air plant, is a species of epiphyte, a type of plant that grows on other surfaces without deriving nutrients from them.

Traditionally, this plant has been used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, respiratory problems, and skin conditions, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antifungal properties. The bioactive constituents of Tillandsia guatemalensis include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Tillandsia guatemalensis is often made into a tea or tincture, and its extracts are used in the production of cough syrups, throat lozenges, and topical creams to aid in the treatment of respiratory and skin issues.

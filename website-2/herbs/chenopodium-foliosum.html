Chenopodium foliosum

Chenopodium foliosum, also known as leafy goosefoot or alpine goosefoot, is an annual or biennial plant species in the amaranth family that belongs to the Chenopodium genus.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, making it beneficial for treating various health conditions such as digestive issues and skin problems. The bioactive constituents of C. foliosum include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made from C. foliosum include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, fever, and skin conditions, as well as to promote digestion and reduce inflammation.

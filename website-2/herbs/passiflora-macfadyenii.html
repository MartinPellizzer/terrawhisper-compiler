Passiflora macfadyenii

Passiflora macfadyenii, also known as the yellow granadilla or banana passionflower, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Passiflora, characterized by its unique and intricate flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its calming and sedative effects, with reported health benefits including relief from anxiety and insomnia, as well as its potential to reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of menopause. Bioactive constituents of P. macfadyenii include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which have been shown to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, P. macfadyenii is commonly prepared as a tea or tincture, and is also used in the production of infusions, capsules, and ointments.

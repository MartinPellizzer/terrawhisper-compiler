Verbascum bottae

Verbascum bottae, also known as Beech Mullein, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and expectorant effects, which can help alleviate respiratory issues, soothe skin irritations, and reduce coughs. The bioactive constituents of Verbascum bottae include flavonoids, phenolic acids, saponins, and triterpenes, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, Verbascum bottae is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into salves and ointments for topical applications.

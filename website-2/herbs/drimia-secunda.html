Drimia secunda

Drimia secunda, also known as the Cape broom or wild iris, is a plant species native to the Western Cape province of South Africa.

Traditionally used in folk medicine, Drimia secunda has been reported to possess various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiparasitic properties, making it a potential remedy for treating a range of conditions, including infections and gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of Drimia secunda include saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Drimia secunda is often used to prepare infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are used to treat ailments such as fever, coughs, and skin conditions.

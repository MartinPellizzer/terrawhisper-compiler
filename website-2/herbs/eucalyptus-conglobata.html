Eucalyptus conglobata

Eucalyptus conglobata, commonly known as Wollemi Pine or Wollemi, is a rare and endangered coniferous tree native to Australia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with health benefits including relief from respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma due to its anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties. The bioactive constituents of Eucalyptus conglobata include volatile oils rich in eucalyptol, camphor, and alpha-pinene, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as teas, essential oils, and salves, which are used to treat respiratory and skin issues.

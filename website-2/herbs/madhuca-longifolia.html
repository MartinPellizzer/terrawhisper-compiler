Madhuca longifolia

Madhuca longifolia, commonly known as the Indian butter tree or mahua, is a deciduous tree species native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its various health benefits, including reducing inflammation, managing diabetes, and alleviating respiratory problems such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Madhuca longifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant include churna (powder), decoctions, and ointments, which are used to treat various health conditions and promote overall well-being.

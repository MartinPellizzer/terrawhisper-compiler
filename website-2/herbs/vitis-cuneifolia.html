Vitis cuneifolia

Vitis cuneifolia, also known as the wedgeleaf grapevine, is a species of plant in the grape family (Vitaceae) that is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Vitis cuneifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The bioactive constituents of Alsophila montana include triterpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

classification

The Cyatheales order is further grouped into the class Polypodiopsida, a class of vascular plants that also includes various types of ferns. The class Polypodiopsida is a part of the division Pteridophyta, which encompasses all vascular plants that reproduce via spores.

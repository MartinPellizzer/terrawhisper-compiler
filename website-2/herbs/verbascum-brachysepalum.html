Verbascum brachysepalum

Verbascum brachysepalum, also known as short-leaved mullein, is a perennial plant species belonging to the mullein family (Scrophulariaceae) and is native to Europe.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and expectorant properties, which can help to soothe respiratory issues such as bronchitis and coughs. Bioactive constituents found in Verbascum brachysepalum include flavonoids, saponins, and iridoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Verbascum brachysepalum include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which can be used to treat a range of health issues, including respiratory problems, digestive issues, and skin conditions.

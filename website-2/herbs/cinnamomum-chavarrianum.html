Cinnamomum chavarrianum

Cinnamomum chavarrianum, also known as Chavarria cinnamon or Chavarrian cinnamon, is a species of evergreen tree in the cinnamon family (Cinnamomaceae) native to Central and South America.

The plant's bioactive constituents include cinnamaldehyde, eugenol, and linalool, among other essential oils and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the bark of Cinnamomum chavarrianum is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion to take advantage of its health-promoting properties.

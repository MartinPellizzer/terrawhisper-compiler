What is Pimelea linifolia subsp. collina?

Pimelea linifolia subsp.

collina is a subspecies of Pimelea linifolia, a shrubby perennial plant that belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae. It is a small shrub, typically growing to 20-50 cm in height, with a spreading habit and a narrow, linear leaf shape, 2-8 mm long.

The leaves are usually glabrous, although sometimes hairy, and are arranged oppositely on the stem. Pimelea linifolia subsp. collina produces white or pale yellow flowers in small, axillary clusters, with a four-merous corolla and four stamens, from August to October.

The fruit is a small, glabrous capsule, containing four seeds.

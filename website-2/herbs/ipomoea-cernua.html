Ipomoea cernua

Ipomoea cernua, also known as the trumpet vine or cernua morning glory, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America, characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers and twining stems.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including reducing anxiety and stress, promoting sleep, and alleviating symptoms of menopause due to its estrogenic and sedative properties. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea cernua include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Ipomoea cernua is often used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as in capsule and tablet form, to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality.

Conioselinum pteridophyllum

Conioselinum pteridophyllum, also known as fern-leaved chervil, is a perennial plant species in the carrot family (Apiaceae) characterized by its fern-like leaves and hollow stems.

The bioactive constituents of Conioselinum pteridophyllum include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, skin problems, and as a natural remedy for colds and flu.

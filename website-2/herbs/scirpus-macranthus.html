Scirpus macranthus

Scirpus macranthus, also known as giant umbrella sedge, is a perennial aquatic plant native to East Asia, characterized by its tall stems and umbrella-like inflorescence.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine, with health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may aid in reducing swelling and protecting against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Scirpus macranthus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Scirpus macranthus is often prepared as a decoction or a dried powder, and is used to treat various conditions, including fever, cough, and respiratory infections.

What is Phyllanthus niruri?

Phyllanthus niruri is a herbaceous annual plant that belongs to the family Phyllanthaceae, native to tropical regions of Central and South America, and the Caribbean.

The plant is also known as Chanca piedra and grows up to 30 cm in height, with numerous branches and oval-shaped leaves that are 2-5 cm long and 1-2 cm wide. The leaves are oppositely arranged on the stem and have a leathery texture. The flowers are greenish-yellow in color, small, and arranged in axillary spikes.

The plant produces red or orange berries that are 1-2 cm in diameter.

What is Melaleuca halmaturorum subsp. cymbifolia?

Melaleuca halmaturorum subsp.

cymbifolia is a plant species within the family Myrtaceae, native to Western Australia. It is a shrub or small tree, growing to a height of up to 4 meters, with a spread of up to 2.5 meters, and a dense, rounded crown.

The leaves are narrow, linear, and pointed, measuring 4-8 centimeters in length, and are a dark green color on the upper surface and a lighter green on the lower surface. The inflorescence is a spike or corymb of white or yellow flowers, which are arranged in clusters, and are 4-5 millimeters in diameter, with 5 petals and 5 stamens.

The fruit is a small, brown capsule, measuring 2-3 millimeters in length, containing 5 seeds, each measuring 1-2 millimeters in length.

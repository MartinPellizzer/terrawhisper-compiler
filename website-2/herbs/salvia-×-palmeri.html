Salvia × palmeri

Salvia × palmeri, also known as Palmer's sage or Palmer's salvia, is a perennial herb belonging to the sage family (Lamiaceae) and is a hybrid of the species Salvia leucophylla and Salvia farinacea.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and cognitive-enhancing effects. The bioactive constituents of Salvia × palmeri include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and monoterpenoid indole alkaloids, such as rosmarinic acid and salvinorin A, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Salvia × palmeri include teas, tinctures, and capsules, as well as topical creams and salves for skin and wound care.

Pentanema × strictum

Pentanema × strictum, also known as strict pentanema or strict knapweed, is a hybrid plant species in the daisy family (Asteraceae) that is native to Europe and Western Asia.

Traditionally, Pentanema × strictum has been used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Pentanema × strictum is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is also used in herbal teas and topical creams to harness its health benefits.

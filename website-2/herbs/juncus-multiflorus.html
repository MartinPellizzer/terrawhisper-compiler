Juncus multiflorus

Juncus multiflorus, also known as the many-flowered rush, is a perennial aquatic plant belonging to the Juncaceae family.

The plant is valued for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues, wounds, and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Juncus multiflorus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Juncus multiflorus is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used in salves and ointments to treat skin conditions and wounds.

Ranunculus marinus

Ranunculus marinus, also known as sea ranunculus or sea buttercup, is a flowering plant that grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties, providing relief for wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus marinus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Ranunculus marinus include infusions, tinctures, and ointments, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including eczema, acne, and coughs.

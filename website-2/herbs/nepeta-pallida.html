Nepeta pallida

Nepeta pallida, also known as catmint, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its antispasmodic, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties, which can help alleviate digestive issues, reduce anxiety, and support the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Nepeta pallida include iridoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, such as nepetalactone, which is responsible for its medicinal and insect-repelling properties.

Catmint is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as in topical creams and ointments to soothe skin irritations and wounds.

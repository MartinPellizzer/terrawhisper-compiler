Restio major

Restio major, also known as "reed grass," is a species of flowering plant in the Restionaceae family, commonly found in the wetlands of southern Africa.

Traditionally, Restio major has been used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Restio major include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Restio major is often made into teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being used in topical applications, such as poultices and ointments, to take advantage of its healing properties.

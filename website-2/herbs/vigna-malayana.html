Vigna malayana

Vigna malayana, also known as the red gram or cowpea, is a perennial legume plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

This plant is rich in health benefits, including its ability to lower blood sugar levels, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antioxidant properties, making it a valuable resource for traditional medicine. The bioactive constituents of Vigna malayana include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the most common herbal preparations made from Vigna malayana include decoctions, infusions, and powders, which are used to treat various health conditions, including diabetes, fever, and digestive issues.

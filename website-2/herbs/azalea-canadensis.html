Azalea canadensis

Azalea canadensis, also known as the Florida azalea or pink azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its pink or white flowers and glossy leaves.

The health benefits of Azalea canadensis include its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments, such as fever, sore throats, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Azalea canadensis include flavonoids, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Azalea canadensis include teas, infusions, and decoctions, which are used to treat colds, coughs, and other respiratory infections.

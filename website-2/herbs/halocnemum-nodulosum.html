Halocnemum nodulosum

Halocnemum nodulosum, also known as the saltwort or sea-glasswort, is a succulent halophyte plant that thrives in salt-rich environments.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, skin irritation, and respiratory issues, due to its potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Halocnemum nodulosum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Halocnemum nodulosum is commonly used to prepare infusions, decoctions, and topical creams, making it a valuable resource for treating skin conditions and wound healing.

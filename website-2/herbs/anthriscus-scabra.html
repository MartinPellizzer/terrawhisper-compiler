Anthriscus scabra

Anthriscus scabra, also known as cow parsley or wild chervil, is a biennial plant in the carrot family (Apiaceae) that grows up to 2 meters in height and is commonly found in damp meadows and by streams.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of health issues, including digestive problems, fever, and respiratory infections, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Anthriscus scabra include coumarins, flavonoids, and volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is often made into teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being used in combination with other herbs to create remedies for colds, flu, and other respiratory conditions.

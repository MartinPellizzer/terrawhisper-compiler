    What are the therapeutic properties this herb?

    The main therapeutic properties of Rhodiola henryi are its adaptogenic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects.

    The adaptogenic property of Rhodiola henryi is attributed to the presence of iridoid glycosides, such as rhodioloside, which help to reduce stress and improve mental performance. Its anti-inflammatory property is mainly due to the presence of phenylethanoid glycosides, including rosavin, that inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. Additionally, Rhodiola henryi's antioxidant property is attributed to the presence of flavonoids, such as kaempferol, that scavenge free radicals and protect against oxidative damage.

    Furthermore, Rhodiola henryi's neuroprotective and cardioprotective effects are also attributed to the presence of triterpenoid saponins.

    What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

    The most important healing constituents of Rhodiola henryi are salidroside, rosarin, rosavin, and p-tyrosol.

    Salidroside is a unique glycoside that exhibits neuroprotective and antioxidant properties by scavenging free radicals and inhibiting lipid peroxidation. Rosavin and p-tyrosol, which are responsible for Rhodiola's adaptogenic properties, have been shown to improve mental performance, reduce stress and anxiety, and inhibit the activity of MAO enzymes, thereby increasing neurotransmitter levels and enhancing mood.

    These constituents work synergistically to provide Rhodiola henryi with its therapeutic benefits, which include improved cognitive function, reduced fatigue, and enhanced mood stabilization.

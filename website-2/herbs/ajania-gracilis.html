Ajania gracilis

Ajania gracilis, also known as edible chrysanthemum or yukinoshita, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of fever, headache, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Ajania gracilis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ajania gracilis is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is often used to support immune function and reduce fever.

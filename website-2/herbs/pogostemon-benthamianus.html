Pogostemon benthamianus

Pogostemon benthamianus, also known as Patchouli, is a tropical plant species in the family Lamiaceae, characterized by its square stems and purple flowers.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, offering potential health benefits in wound healing, skin care, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Pogostemon benthamianus include sesquiterpenes, particularly patchoulol, which is responsible for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

In herbal medicine, Pogostemon benthamianus is commonly prepared in essential oils, tinctures, and infusions, often used to treat skin conditions, promote relaxation, and reduce stress.

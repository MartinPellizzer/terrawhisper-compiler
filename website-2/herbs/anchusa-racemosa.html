Anchusa racemosa

Anchusa racemosa, also known as Italian bugloss, is a perennial plant belonging to the borage family (Boraginaceae) that is native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which may help to protect against cardiovascular disease, reduce inflammation, and support immune function. The bioactive constituents of Anchusa racemosa include alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, such as anchusine, racemosa alkaloids, and isorhapontigenin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Anchusa racemosa is commonly used to prepare teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments to support skin health.

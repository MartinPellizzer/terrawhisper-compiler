Ipomoea sinuata

Ipomoea sinuata, also known as Japanese morning glory, is a flowering plant species native to East Asia, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and purple flowers.

The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea sinuata include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal effects.

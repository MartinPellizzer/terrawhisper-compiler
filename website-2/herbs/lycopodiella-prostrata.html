Lycopodiella prostrata

Lycopodiella prostrata, also known as trailing club moss, is a small, prostrate species of club moss that belongs to the lycopod family, characterized by its wiry stems and small, scale-like leaves.

Traditionally, Lycopodiella prostrata has been used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Lycopodiella prostrata include lignans, triterpenes, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal preparations, Lycopodiella prostrata is often combined with other herbs to create cough syrups, expectorants, and teas, which are used to relieve respiratory congestion and promote lung health.

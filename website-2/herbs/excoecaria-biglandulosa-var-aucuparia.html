Excoecaria biglandulosa var. aucuparia

Excoecaria biglandulosa var. aucuparia, also known as the poison tree or strychnine tree, is a plant species found in tropical regions of the Pacific Islands and Australia, characterized by its glossy leaves and milky sap.

The plant's milky sap contains a toxic compound called strychnine, which has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions.

The health benefits of Excoecaria biglandulosa var. aucuparia include its anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties, which can help reduce pain and fever, as well as its ability to treat various skin conditions such as eczema and acne.

In traditional herbal preparations, the plant's sap is often dried and processed into a powder or oil, which can be used to make topical creams, ointments, and tinctures to treat various health conditions.

Carpinus ulmifolia

Carpinus ulmifolia, also known as the European hornbeam, is a deciduous tree species native to Europe and western Asia, belonging to the family Betulaceae.

The bark and leaves of Carpinus ulmifolia have been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of Carpinus ulmifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are used to treat colds, flu, and other respiratory issues.

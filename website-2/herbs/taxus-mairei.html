Taxus mairei

Taxus mairei, also known as the Chilean yew, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Andes mountains in South America.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anticancer activities, making it a potential therapeutic agent for various health conditions. The bioactive constituents of Taxus mairei include taxine alkaloids, taxol, and taxane diterpenes, which have been shown to possess significant pharmacological activities.

The most common herbal preparations made with Taxus mairei are infusions, tinctures, and extracts, often used in traditional medicine to treat conditions such as arthritis, fever, and infections.

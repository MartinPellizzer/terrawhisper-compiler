Crocanthemum carolinianum

Crocanthemum carolinianum, also known as Carolina rose, is a shrubby perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

The health benefits of Crocanthemum carolinianum include its potential to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties, making it useful in the treatment of various skin conditions, wounds, and infections. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Crocanthemum carolinianum include teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as topical creams and ointments used to treat skin conditions such as eczema and acne.

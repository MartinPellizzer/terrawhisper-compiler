Ocotea nobilis

Ocotea nobilis, also known as Kanuka or New Zealand Kanuka, is a species of evergreen tree native to New Zealand, belonging to the Lauraceae family.

The essential oil extracted from its leaves has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects, which are beneficial for wound healing, skin care, and respiratory health. The bioactive constituents of Ocotea nobilis essential oil include sesquiterpenes, particularly α-pinene, β-pinene, and α-cedrene, as well as other compounds like limonene and borneol, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Traditionally, Ocotea nobilis is used in herbal preparations such as essential oil blends, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to support immune function, reduce inflammation, and promote relaxation.

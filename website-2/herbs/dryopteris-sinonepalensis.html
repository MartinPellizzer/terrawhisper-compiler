Dryopteris sinonepalensis

Dryopteris sinonepalensis, also known as the Chinese shield fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of China and the Himalayas.

This plant has been traditionally used in medicine for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to reduce stress and improve overall well-being. The bioactive constituents of Dryopteris sinonepalensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is typically prepared as a tea or tincture, as well as being dried and used in infusions and decoctions to take advantage of its medicinal properties.

Conobea multifida

Conobea multifida is a plant species commonly known as the 'Fern-leaf Conobea' or 'Fern-leaf Marsh St. John's Wort', belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which may aid in the treatment of various skin issues and infections. The bioactive constituents of Conobea multifida include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Conobea multifida is often prepared as an infusion or decoction, and also used as a topical cream or ointment, to treat conditions such as wounds, eczema, and dermatitis.

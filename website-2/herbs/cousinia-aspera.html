Cousinia aspera

Cousinia aspera, also known as Cousinia, is a plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, providing relief from various health issues such as fever, inflammation, and respiratory problems, thanks to its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Cousinia aspera include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Cousinia aspera include decoctions, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including colds, coughs, and sore throats.

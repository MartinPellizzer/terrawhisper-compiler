Juniperus arenaria

Juniperus arenaria, also known as Rocky Mountain juniper, is a shrubby evergreen tree native to the mountains of North America.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and antimicrobial properties, offering potential health benefits in treating conditions such as kidney stones, arthritis, and urinary tract infections. The bioactive constituents of Juniperus arenaria include essential oils, flavonoids, and terpenoids, with alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, and myrcene being among the most significant compounds.

Juniper berries, the cones of the Juniperus arenaria tree, are commonly used to make herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and infusions, often used to support kidney function and relieve digestive issues.

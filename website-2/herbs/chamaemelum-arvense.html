Chamaemelum arvense

Chamaemelum arvense, commonly known as chamomile, is an annual plant of the daisy family (Asteraceae) native to Europe and Western Asia.

The health benefits of chamomile include its ability to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety and insomnia, and soothe digestive issues, making it a popular herbal remedy for stress relief and sleep aid. The bioactive constituents of chamomile include apigenin, a flavonoid with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, as well as sesquiterpene lactones, such as alpha-bisabolol, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and anti-microbial activities.

Chamomile is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or essential oil, and is often used in herbal remedies for its calming and soothing effects.

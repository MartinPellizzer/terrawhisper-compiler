Cimicifuga racemosa

Cimicifuga racemosa, also known as black cohosh, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America, belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include alleviating symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes and night sweats, as well as providing relief from anxiety and insomnia. The bioactive constituents of black cohosh include triterpene glycosides, such as acteoside and cimicifugoside, which are believed to contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Black cohosh is commonly prepared as a dietary supplement in capsule or tablet form, as well as in teas and tinctures, which are often standardized to contain a specific amount of triterpene glycosides.

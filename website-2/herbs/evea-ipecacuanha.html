Evea ipecacuanha

Evea ipecacuanha, also known as Ipecac, is a perennial plant native to Brazil, belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

The health benefits of Evea ipecacuanha include its emetic properties, which have been used to induce vomiting and treat symptoms of nausea and poisoning. The bioactive constituents of this plant include ipecac alkaloids, including emetine and cephaeline, which are responsible for its emetic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Evea ipecacuanha is commonly prepared as a tincture or syrup, used to treat various conditions such as gastrointestinal disorders and as an antidote for poisoning.

Intsia attenuata

Intsia attenuata, also known as the "African Ebony", is a large, slow-growing tree native to tropical Africa.

The tree's heartwood has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties, which are attributed to its ability to reduce fever, relieve pain, and promote wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Intsia attenuata include flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids, such as flavanones, phenolic acids, and lignans, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Intsia attenuata include teas, tinctures, and infusions, often used to treat conditions like malaria, fever, and skin infections.

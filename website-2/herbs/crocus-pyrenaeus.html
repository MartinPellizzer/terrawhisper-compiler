Crocus pyrenaeus

Crocus pyrenaeus, also known as the Pyrenean crocus, is a perennial plant in the iris family, characterized by its vibrant purple flowers and slender, grass-like leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating respiratory issues such as bronchitis and coughs, due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory effects. The bioactive constituents of Crocus pyrenaeus include isoquinoline alkaloids, such as colchicine, and other compounds like crocin and crocetin, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Crocus pyrenaeus include decoctions, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to relieve respiratory issues and promote overall well-being.

Ruschia multiflora

Ruschia multiflora, also known as the many-flowered ice plant, is a succulent plant species native to the Western Cape province of South Africa.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, including its ability to reduce inflammation, soothe wounds, and exhibit antimicrobial activity, which can contribute to its potential health benefits in treating skin conditions and promoting wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Ruschia multiflora include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ruschia multiflora is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various ailments, including skin irritations, sore throats, and respiratory infections.

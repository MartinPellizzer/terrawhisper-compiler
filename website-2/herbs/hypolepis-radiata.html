Hypolepis radiata

Hypolepis radiata, also known as the spotted maidenhair fern or radiate maidenhair, is a species of fern that belongs to the genus Hypolepis, characterized by its unique, fan-shaped fronds with radiating veins.

The bioactive constituents of Hypolepis radiata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Hypolepis radiata is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, often used to treat coughs, colds, and sore throats.

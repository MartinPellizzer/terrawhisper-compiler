Senegalia kraussiana

Senegalia kraussiana, also known as Acacia kraussiana, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to southern Africa, belonging to the family Fabaceae.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with health benefits including wound healing, anti-inflammatory, and antibacterial effects, which can aid in treating various skin conditions and infections. The bioactive constituents of Senegalia kraussiana include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic compounds, particularly flavonoids such as quercetin and kaempferol, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Senegalia kraussiana include teas, infusions, and extracts, which are often used in traditional medicine to treat skin conditions, wounds, and other health issues.

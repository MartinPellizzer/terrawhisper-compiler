Terminalia cacoucia

Terminalia cacoucia, also known as the Brazilian arrowroot or cupuaçu, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, digestive issues, and skin problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties. The bioactive constituents of Terminalia cacoucia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a decoction, tea, or powder, and is also used to make various herbal supplements, including capsules and tinctures.

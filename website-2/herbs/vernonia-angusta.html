Vernonia angusta

Vernonia angusta, commonly known as narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin disorders, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Vernonia angusta include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpene lactones, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is prepared as a tea, infusion, or decoction, and is also used in the form of a tincture or extract in herbal medicine.

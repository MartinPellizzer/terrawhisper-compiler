Pulsatilla × wilczekii

Pulsatilla × wilczekii, also known as a hybrid of Pulsatilla species, is a perennial plant belonging to the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae).

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antispasmodic properties, offering potential health benefits in treating conditions such as wounds, digestive issues, and menstrual cramps. The bioactive constituents of Pulsatilla × wilczekii include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Pulsatilla × wilczekii is often prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is commonly used to treat menstrual disorders, respiratory issues, and skin conditions.

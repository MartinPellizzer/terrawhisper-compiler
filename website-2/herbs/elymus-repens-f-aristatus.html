What is Elymus repens f. aristatus?

Elymus repens f. aristatus is a perennial grass of the family Poaceae, characterized by a rhizomatous growth habit and a leafy stem up to 60 cm tall, with linear-lanceolate leaves that are 10-30 cm long and 2-5 mm wide, typically glaucous or green in color, and bearing a dense panicle inflorescence 10-30 cm long, composed of numerous spikelets with two sterile florets and three or four fertile florets, each containing one or two fertile flowers.

The panicle is often nodding, and the spikelets are usually 10-15 mm long, with a 0.5-1.5 mm long pedicel and a 0.5-1 mm long rachilla, and the fertile florets are 3-6 mm long, with a 1-2 mm long lemma and a 1-2 mm long palea. The plant produces a 3-6 mm long, elliptical or lanceolate, two-veined seed, often with a distinctive awn up to 2 mm long. The leaf sheaths are glabrous or hairy, and the ligule is 0.5-1 mm long and membranous.

Elymus repens f. aristatus is a member of the Elymus repens species, which is widely distributed across Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

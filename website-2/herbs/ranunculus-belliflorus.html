Ranunculus belliflorus

Ranunculus belliflorus, commonly known as the buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in wound healing and the treatment of certain skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus belliflorus include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Ranunculus belliflorus is often used to prepare infusions and tinctures, which are traditionally used to treat minor wounds and skin irritations.

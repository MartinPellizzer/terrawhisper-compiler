Urtica subinermis

Urtica subinermis, also known as prickly nettle, is a perennial plant species belonging to the nettle family (Urticaceae).

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antihistaminic, and antioxidant properties, which contribute to its potential health benefits, including relieving allergies, reducing pain, and protecting against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Urtica subinermis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, such as quercetin, isoquercetin, and 5-hydroxytryptamine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Urtica subinermis is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or capsule to take advantage of its health benefits, and is also used in topical creams and ointments to soothe skin irritations and inflammation.

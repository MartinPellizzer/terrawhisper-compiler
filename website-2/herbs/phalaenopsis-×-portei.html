Phalaenopsis × portei

Phalaenopsis × portei, commonly known as the moth orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Phalaenopsis amabilis and Phalaenopsis stuartiana.

This plant has been traditionally used in various cultures for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which may aid in reducing stress, promoting wound healing, and protecting against cell damage. The bioactive constituents of Phalaenopsis × portei include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the moth orchid is often prepared as a tea or tincture, and is also used in topical applications to support skin health and reduce inflammation.

Picea parryana glauca-pendula

Picea parryana glauca-pendula, also known as the Colorado Blue Spruce, is a variant of the Picea parryana tree species native to the southwestern United States and Mexico.

The essential oil extracted from this plant has been found to possess various health benefits, including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, making it effective in treating respiratory issues and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Picea parryana glauca-pendula include alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, and limonene, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are essential oils, extracts, and teas, often used in traditional medicine to relieve coughs and congestion.

classification

Picea parryana glauca-pendula is a cultivar of Picea parryana, a species of spruce tree. It is classified as a member of the family Pinaceae, which comprises coniferous trees. This cultivar is a variant of the Parry spruce tree, known for its distinctive foliage and growth habits. Picea parryana glauca-pendula is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its unique characteristics. As a cultivar, it is not a naturally occurring species but rather a selectively bred variation of Picea parryana.

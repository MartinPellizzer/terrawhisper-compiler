classification

Andrographis paniculata is classified as a member of the family Acanthaceae, a large and diverse group of flowering plants. This plant is further categorized as belonging to the genus Andrographis, which consists of only a few species. The species itself is known as Andrographis paniculata, and it is a perennial herb native to India and Southeast Asia. It is also commonly known as kalmegh or king of bitters. The plant's classification reflects its unique characteristics and its position within the larger family of Acanthaceae.

medicinal uses

Andrographis paniculata is a plant with a long history of traditional use in Ayurvedic medicine. It is commonly known as 'kalmegh' or 'king of bitters' due to its bitter taste. The plant is used to treat various health conditions, including fever, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. Andrographis paniculata is also used to boost the immune system and reduce inflammation. Its extracts and essential oils are often used in herbal remedies to promote overall well-being.

medicinal parts

Andrographis paniculata, commonly known as Kalmegh, contains various medicinal parts, including its leaves, roots, and stem. The leaves are particularly valued for their medicinal properties due to their high concentration of andrographolide. The roots and stem also contain andrographolide, although in smaller amounts compared to the leaves. The leaves can be harvested at different stages of maturity, but the ideal time is during the flowering stage when they are rich in andrographolide. The medicinal parts of Andrographis paniculata are traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, digestive issues, and inflammation.

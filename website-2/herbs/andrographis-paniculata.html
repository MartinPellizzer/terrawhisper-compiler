What is Andrographis paniculata?

Andrographis paniculata is a perennial herbaceous plant native to India and Southeast Asia, belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It has a hairy stem, 60-100 cm tall, with heart-shaped leaves, 5-10 cm long, and 3-5 cm wide, and bears small, greenish-yellow flowers in a spike-like inflorescence. The plant's rhizomes and roots contain andrographolide, a diterpenoid lactone responsible for its medicinal properties. The leaves and flowering spikes of Andrographis paniculata are used in traditional medicine to treat various conditions, including fever, inflammation, and digestive issues.

The plant's seeds are small, ovoid, and 5-7 mm long, with a smooth, brown testa.

What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

The most important healing constituents of Andrographis paniculata are andrographolide, 14-deoxyandrographolide, isoandrographolide, andrographiside, and neoadrographolide, which possess potent anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory properties.

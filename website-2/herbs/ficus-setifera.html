Ficus setifera

Ficus setifera, commonly known as the Banyan fig, is a species of fig tree native to tropical Asia, characterized by its unique aerial roots and ability to grow as an epiphyte.

The plant has been traditionally used in medicine, offering various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which are believed to aid in the treatment of digestive issues and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Ficus setifera include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ficus setifera is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including fever, coughs, and skin irritations.

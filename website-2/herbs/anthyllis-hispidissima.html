Anthyllis hispidissima

Anthyllis hispidissima, also known as hairy-vetch or broom clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its hairy stems and pink or purple flowers.

This plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including gastrointestinal problems, wounds, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties. The bioactive constituents of Anthyllis hispidissima include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional medicine, Anthyllis hispidissima is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion, and is used to treat conditions such as diarrhea, fever, and respiratory issues.

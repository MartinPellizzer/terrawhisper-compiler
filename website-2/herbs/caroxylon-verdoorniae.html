Caroxylon verdoorniae

Caroxylon verdoorniae, also known as the Desert Thumb or Spiny Bokbaai, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its thorny stems and branches.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its various health benefits, including treating fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Caroxylon verdoorniae include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into topical ointments and creams to treat skin conditions.

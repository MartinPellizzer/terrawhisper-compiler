Equisetum californicum

Equisetum californicum, also known as California horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the class Equisetopsida, characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and feathery foliage.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating kidney and urinary issues due to its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects, which help to alleviate symptoms such as kidney stones and urinary tract infections. The bioactive constituents of Equisetum californicum include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The plant is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and capsules, as well as in poultices and topical applications, to treat a range of health issues.

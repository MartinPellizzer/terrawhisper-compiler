Agelanthus copaiferae

Agelanthus copaiferae, also known as the Copaiba Bush or African Copaiba, is a plant species belonging to the family Loranthaceae, which is a type of hemiparasitic plant that obtains its nutrients by tapping into the vascular system of a host plant.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, wounds, and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Agelanthus copaiferae include triterpenoids, particularly oleanolic acid and ursolic acid, as well as diterpenes, including copalicine and copaibein, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Agelanthus copaiferae is often prepared as a resin or infused in oil to make topical creams, ointments, and salves for wound healing and skin conditions, as well as teas and infusions for internal use.

Cassia littoralis

Cassia littoralis, also known as Senna littoralis, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the world, belonging to the family Fabaceae.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antidiarrheal activities, making it a potential treatment for various gastrointestinal disorders, as well as its antioxidant and hepatoprotective effects. The bioactive constituents of Cassia littoralis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, particularly sennosides, which are responsible for its purgative properties.

Cassia littoralis is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, capsules, and extracts, which are marketed as natural remedies for digestive issues, weight loss, and skin problems.

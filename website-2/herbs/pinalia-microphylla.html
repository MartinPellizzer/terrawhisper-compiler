Pinalia microphylla

Pinalia microphylla, also known as the dwarf Pinalia, is a species of orchid native to tropical Asia.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including its potential to lower blood pressure, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of P. microphylla include alkaloids such as pinaline, pinalilin, and microphylline, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional medicine, P. microphylla is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various ailments, including hypertension, fever, and respiratory infections.

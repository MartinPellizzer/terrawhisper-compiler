Ventilago diffusa

Ventilago diffusa, also known as five-angled ventilago or five-angled tree, is a plant species native to Southeast Asia and Australia, characterized by its five-angled branches and small, white flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in reducing fever, relieving pain, and protecting against infections. The bioactive constituents of Ventilago diffusa include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Ventilago diffusa is often used to prepare infusions, decoctions, and extracts, which are used to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues.

medicinal uses

Sagus pedunculata, also known as Wintergreen, has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes. Its leaves contain methyl salicylate, a compound similar to aspirin, which has anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties. Native Americans used Wintergreen to treat fever, rheumatism, and sore muscles. In modern herbalism, Wintergreen is used to alleviate headaches, menstrual cramps, and digestive issues. However, due to its salicylate content, excessive consumption can lead to adverse effects, so it's essential to use it under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

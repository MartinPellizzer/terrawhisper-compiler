Cissus capreolata

Cissus capreolata, also known as grape ivy, is a perennial vine native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in the treatment of arthritis, wounds, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Cissus capreolata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and oligostilbenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Cissus capreolata include extracts, capsules, and tinctures, which are often used to support joint health and promote wound healing.

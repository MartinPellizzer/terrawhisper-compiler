Oncidium × maderoi

Oncidium × maderoi, commonly known as the Madrean Oncidium, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, characterized by its fragrant and showy flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties. The bioactive constituents of Oncidium × maderoi include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which have been found to possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

This hybrid orchid is primarily found in the wild in Madeira, a Portuguese island in the North Atlantic Ocean. As a hybrid species, Oncidium × maderoi is not commonly used in orchid breeding programs, but it is highly prized by collectors for its unique characteristics.

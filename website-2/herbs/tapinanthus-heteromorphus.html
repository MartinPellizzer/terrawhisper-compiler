Tapinanthus heteromorphus

Tapinanthus heteromorphus, also known as the mistletoe of the fig tree, is a hemiparasitic plant species that obtains its nutrients by connecting to the roots of its host plant.

The bioactive constituents of Tapinanthus heteromorphus include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Pleopeltis furfuracea

Pleopeltis furfuracea, also known as the Brazilian tree fern or shaggy cloak fern, is a type of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in treating various conditions such as wounds, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Pleopeltis furfuracea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, and it is also used in modern herbal preparations such as capsules and tinctures to harness its medicinal benefits.

Huperzia emeiensis

Huperzia emeiensis is a species of liverwort, a type of non-vascular plant that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

This plant is known for its potential health benefits, including improving cognitive function and neuroprotection, due in part to its ability to cross the blood-brain barrier and inhibit the breakdown of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter associated with memory. The bioactive constituents of Huperzia emeiensis include huperzine A, a potent inhibitor of acetylcholinesterase, as well as other alkaloids and lignans.

Huperzia emeiensis is commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine and is available in various herbal preparations, including capsules, tablets, and extracts, often marketed as a dietary supplement to support brain health and cognitive function.

Cissus oblonga

Cissus oblonga, also known as Hadjod or Veldt grape, is a perennial climber belonging to the Vitaceae family, native to India and Sri Lanka.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include reducing inflammation, improving bone health, and aiding in weight management due to its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-diabetic effects. Cissus oblonga contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Cissus oblonga include capsules, tablets, and extracts, often used to support joint health, bone density, and weight loss.

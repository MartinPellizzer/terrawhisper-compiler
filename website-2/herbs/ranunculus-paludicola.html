Ranunculus paludicola

Ranunculus paludicola, also known as marsh buttercup, is a perennial aquatic plant species native to marshy wetlands and shallow waters.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, providing relief from various health issues such as wounds, skin infections, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus paludicola include alkaloids, phenolic compounds, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Ranunculus paludicola is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or salve, and is used to treat a range of conditions, including eczema, acne, and minor cuts and scrapes.

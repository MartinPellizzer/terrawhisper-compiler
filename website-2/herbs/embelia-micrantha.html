Embelia micrantha

Embelia micrantha, also known as Indian black pepper, is a plant species belonging to the family Primulaceae, native to the Eastern Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which can help to alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Embelia micrantha include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Embelia micrantha is often used to make decoctions, infusions, and ointments, as well as being used as an ingredient in Ayurvedic formulations such as Chyawanprash and Ashwagandha-based preparations.

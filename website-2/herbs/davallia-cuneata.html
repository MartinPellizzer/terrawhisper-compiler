Davallia cuneata

Davallia cuneata, also known as the rabbit's foot fern or fishbone fern, is a species of fern in the family Davalliaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Davallia cuneata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Davallia cuneata is prepared into teas, infusions, or tinctures, which are used to treat coughs, colds, and other respiratory conditions.

Azalea arborea

The Azalea arborea, also known as the white Azalea or flowering Azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and the Indian subcontinent, belonging to the heath family (Ericaceae).

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, contributing to its use in the treatment of various health conditions such as fever, respiratory issues, and digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of Azalea arborea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Azalea arborea is often used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and tonics, which are commonly used to treat respiratory issues, fever, and sore throats.

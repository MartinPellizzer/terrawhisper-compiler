Lauro-cerasus myrtifolia

Lauro-cerasus myrtifolia, also known as the cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that is native to the mountainous forests of the Mediterranean region.

The cherry laurel has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of the cherry laurel include alkaloids, particularly lauroside and amygdalin, as well as saponins and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with the cherry laurel include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to treat coughs, colds, and other respiratory ailments.

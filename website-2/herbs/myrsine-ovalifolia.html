Myrsine ovalifolia

Myrsine ovalifolia, also known as Chinese boxwood or Taiwan boxwood, is a small, evergreen shrub native to East Asia, characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and woody stems.

Traditionally, Myrsine ovalifolia has been used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal activities.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Myrsine ovalifolia is often processed into teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being used in combination with other herbs to create patent medicines and tonics.

Leptopus hirsutus

Leptopus hirsutus, also known as Bhringaraj or Eclipta alba, is a perennial herb native to Asia and North America, belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It has been traditionally used to promote hair growth, improve skin health, and reduce inflammation, making it a valuable plant in Ayurvedic and herbal medicine. The bioactive constituents of Leptopus hirsutus include flavonoids, triterpenoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Leptopus hirsutus include tea, capsules, and hair oils, which are often used to treat hair loss, improve scalp health, and reduce oxidative stress.

Senna coquimbensis

Senna coquimbensis, also known as Chilean senna, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as laxative and anti-inflammatory effects, which can aid in relieving constipation and reducing inflammation in the body. The bioactive constituents of Senna coquimbensis include anthraquinone glycosides, such as senosides A and B, which are responsible for the plant's purgative properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Senna coquimbensis is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and capsules, and is sometimes combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

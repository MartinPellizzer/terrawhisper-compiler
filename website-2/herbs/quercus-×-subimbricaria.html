Quercus × subimbricaria

Quercus × subimbricaria, also known as the bur oak x white oak hybrid, is a deciduous tree that is a cross between Quercus macrocarpa and Quercus alba.

The bark and leaves of Quercus × subimbricaria have been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions due to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help to reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease, improve wound healing, and boost the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Quercus × subimbricaria include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Quercus × subimbricaria include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which can be used to support overall health and well-being.

Quercus × duriensis

Quercus × duriensis, also known as the hybrid of sessile oak (Quercus petraea) and pubescent oak (Quercus pubescens), is a deciduous tree species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

The bark and leaves of Quercus × duriensis have been traditionally used in herbal medicine, exhibiting various health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help to alleviate conditions like arthritis and reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease. The bioactive constituents of Quercus × duriensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal preparations, Quercus × duriensis is often used to make infusions, decoctions, and extracts, such as oak bark tea and oak leaf tincture, which are used to support digestive health, reduce fever, and promote wound healing.

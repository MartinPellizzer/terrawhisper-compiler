Saxifraga indica

Saxifraga indica, also known as Indian saxifrage, is a perennial plant species belonging to the saxifrage family that is native to the Himalayan region and parts of Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its numerous health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which are said to aid in the treatment of various ailments such as fever, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Saxifraga indica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Saxifraga indica is often prepared into tea, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including fever, cough, and sore throat.

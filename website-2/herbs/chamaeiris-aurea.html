Chamaeiris aurea

Chamaeiris aurea, also known as the golden chamomile, is a flowering plant that belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae).

The golden chamomile has been traditionally used for its therapeutic properties, offering health benefits such as soothing anxiety and promoting relaxation, as well as exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, terpenoids, and apigenin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The golden chamomile is commonly used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for skin care and wound healing.

Pyrethrum sibiricum

Pyrethrum sibiricum, also known as Siberian pyrethrum, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, native to Siberia and other parts of Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Pyrethrum sibiricum include sesquiterpene lactones, particularly pyrethrin and pyrethrolone, which are responsible for its medicinal and insecticidal properties.

In traditional medicine, Pyrethrum sibiricum is often used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including fever, cough, and skin irritations.

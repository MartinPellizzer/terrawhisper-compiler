Duvernoia brevicaulis

Duvernoia brevicaulis, commonly known as the 'red-hot cat's tail', is a plant species in the Acanthaceae family, native to tropical Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory problems, due to its antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Duvernoia brevicaulis include flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often made into teas, infusions, or decoctions, and is also used in the preparation of topical creams and ointments to treat skin conditions.

Scirpus japonicus

Scirpus japonicus, also known as Japanese clubrush or umbrella sedge, is a perennial aquatic plant native to East Asia, characterized by its tall, slender stems and umbrella-shaped inflorescences.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, inflammation, and respiratory issues, due to its potential anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Scirpus japonicus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a tea, decoction, or tincture, and is also used in combination with other herbs to treat various health conditions.

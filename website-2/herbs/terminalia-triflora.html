Terminalia triflora

Terminalia triflora, also known as the three-flowered myrobalan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical forests of India and Sri Lanka.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, which can help in reducing fever, alleviating digestive issues, and supporting immune function. The bioactive constituents of Terminalia triflora include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Terminalia triflora include decoctions, infusions, and extracts, which are used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine to promote overall health and well-being.

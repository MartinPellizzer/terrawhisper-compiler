Suaeda insularis

Suaeda insularis, also known as the insular glasswort, is a halophytic shrub that belongs to the Amaranthaceae family, characterized by its ability to thrive in saline environments.

In traditional medicine, Suaeda insularis is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments to treat skin conditions and wounds.

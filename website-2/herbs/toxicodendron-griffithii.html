Toxicodendron griffithii

Toxicodendron griffithii, also known as Griffith's poison ivy or poison oak, is a plant species native to Southeast Asia that belongs to the Anacardiaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin infections, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Toxicodendron griffithii include urushiol, a potent allergenic compound, as well as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are topical ointments and creams, as well as decoctions and infusions, which are used to treat skin conditions and wounds, although caution is advised due to the plant's allergenic nature.

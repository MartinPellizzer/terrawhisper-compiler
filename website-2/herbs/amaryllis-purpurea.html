Amaryllis purpurea

Amaryllis purpurea, also known as the Naked Lady or Belladonna Lily, is a flowering plant species native to South Africa, characterized by its large, showy funnel-shaped flowers and strap-like leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health, due in part to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Amaryllis purpurea contains bioactive constituents such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and glycosides, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat a variety of ailments, including anxiety, insomnia, and respiratory issues.

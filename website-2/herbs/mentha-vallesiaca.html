Mentha vallesiaca

Mentha vallesiaca, commonly known as Corn Mint, is a perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its distinctive minty aroma and flavor.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties, which may help alleviate digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Corn Mint include menthol, menthone, and limonene, which are responsible for its medicinal properties and distinctive fragrance.

Traditionally, Corn Mint is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and essential oils, which are often used to calm digestive issues, relieve headaches, and promote relaxation.

Aconitum napellus lusus acutum

Aconitum napellus lusus acutum, commonly known as Monk's Hood or Aconitum, is a perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat pain, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms of fever, making it a valuable remedy for various health conditions. The bioactive constituents of Aconitum include alkaloids such as aconitine, mesaconitine, and hypaconitine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Monk's Hood is often used in herbal preparations such as tinctures, infusions, and poultices, which are used to treat a range of conditions, including rheumatism, arthritis, and skin conditions.

Carum mairei

Carum mairei, also known as French parsley or Ajowan, is a perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties, which can help alleviate digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Carum mairei include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and alkaloids, such as carvone, limonene, and ajowanin, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Carum mairei is commonly prepared as an essential oil, dried herb, or powdered extract, and is used in traditional remedies for its digestive, respiratory, and anti-inflammatory benefits.

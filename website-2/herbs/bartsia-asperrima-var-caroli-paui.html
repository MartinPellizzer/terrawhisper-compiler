What is Bartsia asperrima var. caroli-paui?

Bartsia asperrima var. caroli-paui is a plant species in the family Orobanchaceae, commonly known as the broomrape family.

It is a perennial herb with a woody rootstock, growing to a height of 5-25 cm, and has hairy, linear-lanceolate leaves that are 2-10 cm long and 0.5-2 mm wide. The inflorescence is a dense, terminal spike of small, white, bilabiate flowers that are 10-15 mm long and 5-8 mm wide, with a tubular calyx and a 2-lipped corolla. The plant is parasitic, obtaining its nutrients by connecting to the roots of nearby plants.

Bartsia asperrima var. caroli-paui is endemic to the Iberian Peninsula.

Ficus sapida

Ficus sapida, also known as the mountain ficus, is a species of fig tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its large, deciduous leaves and edible fruits.

The health benefits of Ficus sapida include its potential to lower blood sugar levels, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial properties, making it a valuable resource in traditional medicine. The bioactive constituents of Ficus sapida include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ficus sapida is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being used in the preparation of topical creams and ointments for wound healing and skin disorders.

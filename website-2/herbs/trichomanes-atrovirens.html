Trichomanes atrovirens

Trichomanes atrovirens, also known as the Black Fern, is a species of fern native to the subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues and boost the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Trichomanes atrovirens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Trichomanes atrovirens is commonly prepared as an infusion, decoction, or tincture to treat respiratory conditions, and is also used topically to soothe skin irritations.

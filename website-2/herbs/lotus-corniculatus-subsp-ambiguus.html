What is Lotus corniculatus subsp. ambiguus?

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

ambiguus is a subspecies of perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Fabaceae. It has a taproot with ascending stems, branching into whorled leaves and leaflets with 1-3 pairs of pinnae, which are elliptical to ovate in shape, 1-3 cm long and 0.5-1.5 cm wide.

The inflorescence is a raceme or a spicate raceme, bearing pink or purple flowers with a white or yellowish centre, 4-7 mm long and 5-10 mm wide. The flowers have 5 petals, 10 stamens and a pistil with a style and stigma.

It is native to Europe and Asia, but has been introduced to North America and other regions.

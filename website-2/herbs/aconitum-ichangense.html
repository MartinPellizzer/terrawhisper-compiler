Aconitum ichangense

Aconitum ichangense, also known as Ichang Monk's Hood or Chinese Aconite, is a perennial plant species in the Aconitum genus, characterized by its tall, erect stems and hood-like flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial properties, making it a valuable remedy for treating various health conditions, including pain management and fever reduction. The bioactive constituents of Aconitum ichangense include alkaloids, such as aconitine, mesaconitine, and hypaconitine, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Aconitum ichangense is often used to prepare teas, tinctures, and decoctions, which are commonly used to treat pain, fever, and respiratory issues.

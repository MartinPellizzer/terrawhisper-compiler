Magnolia sinensis

Magnolia sinensis, also known as Hou Po, is a deciduous tree species native to China, belonging to the Magnoliaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions, including digestive issues, respiratory problems, and fever, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antifungal properties. The bioactive constituents of Magnolia sinensis include magnolol, honokiol, and 4-O-methylhonokiol, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Magnolia sinensis include teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as dried extracts and capsules, which are often used to promote relaxation, reduce inflammation, and support digestive health.

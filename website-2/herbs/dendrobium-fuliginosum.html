Dendrobium fuliginosum

Dendrobium fuliginosum, also known as the 'black dendrobium', is a species of orchid native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia, characterized by its dark green, almost black leaves and white or pink flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Dendrobium fuliginosum include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is commonly used in herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and tablets, often in combination with other herbs to enhance their therapeutic effects.

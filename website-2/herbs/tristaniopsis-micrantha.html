Tristaniopsis micrantha

Tristaniopsis micrantha, also known as the Kurrajong or Small-fruited Water Gum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae native to eastern Australia.

The plant has been traditionally used in Aboriginal medicine, with health benefits reported to include the treatment of fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Tristaniopsis micrantha include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a decoction, tea, or tincture, and is used to relieve coughs, sore throats, and other respiratory complaints.

Coleus sallyae

Coleus sallyae, also known as Blue Plectranthus, is a perennial plant species in the Lamiaceae family, native to South Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, which can help alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, skin irritations, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Coleus sallyae include iridoid glycosides, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Traditionally, Coleus sallyae is used to make tea infusions, tinctures, and essential oils, which are used to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and skin infections.

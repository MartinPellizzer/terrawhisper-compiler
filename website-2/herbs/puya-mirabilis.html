Puya mirabilis

Puya mirabilis, also known as the 'Mirabilis Puya', is a type of flowering plant in the bromeliad family, characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence and long-lasting blooms.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects that can aid in wound healing, as well as its potential to reduce fever and alleviate respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Puya mirabilis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

In traditional medicine, Puya mirabilis is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, although more research is needed to confirm its efficacy and safety for human consumption.

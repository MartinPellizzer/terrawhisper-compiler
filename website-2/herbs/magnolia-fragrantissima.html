Magnolia fragrantissima

Magnolia fragrantissima, also known as the lily tree or Michaux's magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties, making it useful in treating respiratory issues such as bronchitis, and in alleviating symptoms of the common cold and flu. The bioactive constituents of Magnolia fragrantissima include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, particularly magnolol and honokiol, which have been found to exhibit significant medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and essential oils, which are often used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat respiratory and inflammatory conditions.

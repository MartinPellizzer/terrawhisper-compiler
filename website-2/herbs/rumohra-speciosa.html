Rumohra speciosa

Rumohra speciosa, also known as the lacy tree fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Australia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Rumohra speciosa include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include tea and tinctures, often used to soothe respiratory issues and reduce inflammation.

Carapichea ipecacuanha

Carapichea ipecacuanha, also known as Ipecacuanha, is a plant species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

Traditionally, the plant has been used to treat a variety of health conditions, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its emetic properties, which stimulate the vomiting reflex. The bioactive constituents of Carapichea ipecacuanha include alkaloids such as cephaeline, psychotrine, and emetine, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

Ipecacuanha root is commonly used in herbal preparations, including tinctures, extracts, and powder, often in the form of expectorant and anti-emetic remedies.

classification

Carapichea ipecacuanha is classified in the family Rubiaceae. This plant is a member of the genus Carapichea. It belongs to the order Gentianales and the clade Asterids. Further classification places it in the subclass Asteridae and the class Magnoliopsida. It is also known as ipecac, which is a source of the medicinal substance emetine.

Carapichea ipecacuanha is a plant traditionally used in medicine, particularly in the past, as an emetic due to its toxic alkaloid content. However, due to its toxic properties, it has largely been replaced by safer alternatives in modern medicine. In some cultures, it is still used to treat certain conditions like malaria and fever.

Astragalus filiformis

Astragalus filiformis, also known as Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant in the pea family (Fabaceae) native to China.

It is valued for its immunomodulatory and anti-inflammatory properties, which have been traditionally used to support immune function, reduce fatigue, and alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus filiformis include saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

Astragalus filiformis is commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine and is often prepared as a decoction, capsule, or tablet, as well as being infused as a tea.

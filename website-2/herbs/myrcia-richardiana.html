Myrcia richardiana

Myrcia richardiana, also known as Richard's myrtle or Açaizinho, is a plant species native to the tropical regions of South America, belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

Myrcia richardiana contains a range of bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and diterpenes, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often prepared as an infusion, syrup, or tincture, and is used to treat a variety of ailments, including coughs, sore throats, and digestive issues.

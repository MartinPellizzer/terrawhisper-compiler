What is Hieracium sparsum subsp. grisebachii?

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

grisebachii is a taxon of the species Hieracium sparsum, belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is a perennial herbaceous plant that grows up to 20-30 cm in height, with a rosette of basal leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, 1-4 cm long, and 0.5-2 cm wide, bearing a short petiole and a ciliate margin.

The inflorescence is a corymbiform capitulum with 3-5 florets, each consisting of a yellow ligule and a yellow to orange disc floret, surrounded by a whorl of 3-5 bracts. The plant typically inhabits alpine and subalpine areas, often growing in rocky and sandy substrates.

The taxon is endemic to the Balkan Peninsula and is characterized by its unique combination of morphological and distributional traits.

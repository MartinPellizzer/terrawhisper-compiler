Ranunculus brabantianus

Ranunculus brabantianus, also known as Butcher's Broom, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaflets and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving circulation, and alleviating symptoms of varicose veins and hemorrhoids. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus brabantianus include saponins, flavonoids, and lignans, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Butcher's Broom is commonly used in herbal preparations such as capsules, tablets, and teas, as well as creams and ointments for topical application, particularly to treat venous insufficiency and improve skin health.

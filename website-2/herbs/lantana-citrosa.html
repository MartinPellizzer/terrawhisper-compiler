Lantana citrosa

Lantana citrosa, also known as citronella lantana or button lantana, is a tropical plant species native to the Caribbean and Central America, characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant flowers and aromatic leaves.

The health benefits of Lantana citrosa include its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which have been traditionally used to treat skin conditions, respiratory issues, and digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, particularly linalool and citral, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Lantana citrosa is often used to make teas, infusions, and essential oil preparations, which are valued for their antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic effects.

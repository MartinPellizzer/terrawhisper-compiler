Cnicus giganteus

Cnicus giganteus, also known as the giant knapweed or Russian knapweed, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It has been traditionally used in medicine due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, offering relief from various health issues such as digestive problems, fever, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Cnicus giganteus include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Cnicus giganteus is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or decoction to treat various ailments, with the most common preparations being a tea for digestive issues and a tincture for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

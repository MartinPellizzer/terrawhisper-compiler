Orchis × hybrida

Orchis × hybrida, also known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species of flowering plant within the Orchidaceae family, resulting from the cross-breeding of two different orchid species.

The bioactive constituents of Orchis × hybrida include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Orchis × hybrida is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into traditional remedies such as syrups and ointments.

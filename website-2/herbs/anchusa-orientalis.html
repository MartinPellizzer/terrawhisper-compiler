Anchusa orientalis

Anchusa orientalis, also known as blue spurflower or Syrian alkanet, is a perennial plant species native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its bright blue flowers and edible roots.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal and culinary properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, as well as potential treatments for digestive issues and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Anchusa orientalis include anthocyanins, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Anchusa orientalis is commonly prepared as a tincture, infusion, or topical ointment, often used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and gastrointestinal disorders.

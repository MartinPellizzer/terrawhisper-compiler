Amyema acutifolia

Amyema acutifolia, also known as the sticky hop orchid or hooker's willy willy, is a hemiparasitic plant species native to Western Australia.

This plant has been traditionally used in Australian Aboriginal medicine to treat a range of health conditions, including respiratory infections, fever, and gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of Amyema acutifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Amyema acutifolia is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, with the plant also being used in topical applications to treat skin conditions.

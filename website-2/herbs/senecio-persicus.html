Senecio persicus

Senecio persicus, also known as the Persian groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in various cultures for its medicinal properties, including treating fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and antimicrobial effects. The bioactive constituents of Senecio persicus include flavonoids, terpenoids, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and teas, which are used to treat various health conditions, including fever, indigestion, and skin problems.

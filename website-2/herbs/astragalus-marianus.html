Astragalus marianus

Astragalus marianus, also known as Huang qi or milk vetch, is a perennial plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

Traditionally, Astragalus marianus has been used in Chinese medicine to boost the immune system, reduce inflammation, and alleviate fatigue, showcasing its potential in promoting overall health and well-being. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus marianus include saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Astragalus marianus is commonly prepared as a tea, capsule, or tincture, and is often used in combination with other herbs to enhance its health benefits and prevent overuse.

Cryptocarya papuana

Cryptocarya papuana, also known as Kulu or Kauri Pine, is a species of evergreen tree native to the tropical forests of Papua New Guinea.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its health benefits, including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties, which help to reduce fever, relieve pain, and boost the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Cryptocarya papuana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often used to prepare infusions, tinctures, and ointments, with the most common preparations including a Kulu leaf infusion, used to treat fever and respiratory infections, and a Kulu bark tincture, used to treat pain and inflammation.

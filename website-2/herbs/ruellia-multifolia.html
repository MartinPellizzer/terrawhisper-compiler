Ruellia multifolia

Ruellia multifolia, also known as the Mexican Petunia, is a perennial plant native to Central America and Mexico, characterized by its vibrant purple flowers and erect, woody stems.

Traditionally, the plant has been used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its potential health benefits such as antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Ruellia multifolia include alkaloids, glycosides, and iridoids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Ruellia multifolia is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and may be used to treat conditions such as colds, coughs, and bronchitis, although its use should be approached with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

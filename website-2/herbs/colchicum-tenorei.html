Colchicum tenorei

Colchicum tenorei, also known as Sicilian Autumn Crocus, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Colchicum genus, characterized by its striking purple flowers and edible corms.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, offering potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antitumor properties, as well as its ability to aid in the treatment of gout and other rheumatic conditions. The bioactive constituents of Colchicum tenorei include colchicine, a potent alkaloid responsible for the plant's medicinal properties, as well as other alkaloids and saponins.

In traditional herbalism, Colchicum tenorei is often prepared as a tincture, decoction, or powder, which can be used to treat various health conditions, although its use should be approached with caution due to potential toxicity.

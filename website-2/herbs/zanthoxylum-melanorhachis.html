Zanthoxylum melanorhachis

Zanthoxylum melanorhachis, also known as the black thorn or Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, characterized by its spiny branches and compound leaves.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which may help to alleviate pain, reduce fever, and protect against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Zanthoxylum melanorhachis include limonoids, coumarins, and phenolic acids, such as isorhapontigenin and ferulic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Zanthoxylum melanorhachis is commonly prepared as a decoction, tea, or powder, and is used to treat various conditions, including digestive problems, respiratory issues, and skin disorders.

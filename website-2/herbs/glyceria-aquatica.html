Glyceria aquatica

Glyceria aquatica, also known as water meadow grass, is a perennial aquatic grass native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery leaves.

The bioactive constituents of Glyceria aquatica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

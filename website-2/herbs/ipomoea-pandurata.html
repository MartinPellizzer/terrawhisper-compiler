Ipomoea pandurata

Ipomoea pandurata, also known as Wild Potato or Man of the Earth, is a perennial vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea pandurata include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make various herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to treat fever, sore throats, and skin conditions.

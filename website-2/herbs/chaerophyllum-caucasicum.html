Chaerophyllum caucasicum

Chaerophyllum caucasicum, also known as Caucasian cow parsley, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which are said to aid in digestive issues, reduce fever, and promote wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Chaerophyllum caucasicum include volatile oils, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used in the preparation of poultices and compresses to treat skin conditions and wounds.

Eucalyptus ceratocorys

Eucalyptus ceratocorys, also known as the Rough-fruited Mallee, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

The leaves of the Eucalyptus ceratocorys have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of colds, sore throats, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Eucalyptus ceratocorys include eucalyptol, a volatile oil that has been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

Eucalyptus ceratocorys is often used to make herbal preparations such as essential oils, teas, and extracts, which are commonly used to treat respiratory and skin conditions.

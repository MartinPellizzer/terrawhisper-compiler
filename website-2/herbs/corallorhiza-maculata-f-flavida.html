What is Corallorhiza maculata f. flavida?

Corallorhiza maculata f. flavida is a subspecies of orchid, belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It has yellowish-brown flowers with a labellum that is darker in color, and no leaves, instead with a short stem and a pair of sterile bracts. The plant is parasitic, obtaining its nutrients by connecting to the roots of surrounding plants, specifically through the fungi genus, Rhizoctonia. Corallorhiza maculata f. flavida is found in North America, and its name is derived from the Greek words 'corallion,' meaning coral, and 'rhiza,' meaning root.

The specific name 'maculata' refers to its spotted stems and roots.

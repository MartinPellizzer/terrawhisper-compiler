classification

Angelica graveolens is a member of the Apiaceae family and the genus Angelica. It is native to Europe and Asia and is commonly known as Masterwort. The plant is a biennial or perennial herb with large, fern-like leaves and a tall, stately stem. Angelica graveolens is often used in traditional medicine, particularly in Chinese and European herbalism, due to its aromatic and medicinal properties. The plant is also used in culinary and perfumery applications.

medicinal uses

Angelica graveolens, also known as Dong Quai, has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes, including reducing menstrual cramps and regulating the menstrual cycle. Its root is a rich source of ferulic acid, which has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. In addition, Angelica graveolens is believed to have anti-cancer properties and is used to treat various types of cancer. side effects

Angelica graveolens, also known as Dong Quai, may cause side effects such as dizziness, nausea, and stomach upset in some individuals due to its high content of coumarins and other compounds. These side effects are often mild and temporary but can be more severe in people with pre-existing medical conditions. Additionally, Angelica graveolens may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, which can lead to adverse reactions. Pregnant women and individuals with bleeding disorders should consult their healthcare provider before using Angelica graveolens, as it may increase the risk of bleeding. Furthermore, high doses of Angelica graveolens may cause liver damage in rare cases.

Thuja standishii

Thuja standishii, also known as Japanese Arborvitae, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to East Asia, characterized by its dark green, scale-like foliage and conical shape.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, exhibiting various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in treating respiratory issues, skin conditions, and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Thuja standishii include thuja oil, which contains thujone, borneol, and bornyl acetate, as well as flavonoids, lignans, and phenolic acids.

Thuja standishii is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as essential oils, teas, and tinctures, often employed to treat respiratory issues, skin conditions, and as an antiseptic to promote wound healing.

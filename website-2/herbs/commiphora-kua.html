Commiphora kua

Commiphora kua, also known as African myrrh, is a small, deciduous tree or shrub native to Africa, characterized by its resinous bark and aromatic properties.

The health benefits of Commiphora kua include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, such as wounds, infections, and skin disorders. The bioactive constituents of Commiphora kua include sesquiterpenes, diterpenes, and triterpenes, particularly 15-oxopregnan-3β-ol, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Commiphora kua include tinctures, ointments, and poultices, which are used to promote wound healing, reduce inflammation, and treat various skin conditions.

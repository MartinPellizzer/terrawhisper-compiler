classification

Pfaffia paniculata is a plant species commonly known as suma or Para-Amazonian ginseng. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae. Pfaffia paniculata is native to the Amazon rainforest in South America. The plant is a type of succulent that grows in the wild, particularly in Brazil and other parts of the Amazon region. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions.

Pfaffia paniculata, also known as suma, is a plant native to South America that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine.

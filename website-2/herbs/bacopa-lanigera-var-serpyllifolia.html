What is Bacopa lanigera var. serpyllifolia?

Bacopa lanigera var. serpyllifolia is an annual or perennial herbaceous plant, belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink, solitary flowers, borne in leaf axils, and its ovate to lanceolate, entire, glandular-punctate leaves, which are 1-5 cm long and 0.5-2 cm wide. The inflorescences are cymose, 5-15 flowered, and 1-4 cm long. Bacopa lanigera var. serpyllifolia is native to India and Southeast Asia, and it typically grows in wetlands, grasslands, and open forests.

The plant reaches a height of 10-40 cm.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Bacopa lanigera var. serpyllifolia are tincture, tea, infusion, bacopa tea, and decoction, which are derived from its leaves and aerial parts.

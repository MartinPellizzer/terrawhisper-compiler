Verbascum heterophyllum

Verbascum heterophyllum, also known as Mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa, characterized by its large, hairy leaves and tall spikes of yellow flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including reducing inflammation, soothing coughs, and relieving skin irritations, due to its health benefits. The bioactive constituents of Verbascum heterophyllum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Ferula racemosoumbellata

Ferula racemosoumbellata, also known as Ferula, is a type of plant in the family Apiaceae, commonly found in the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which may aid in the treatment of digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Ferula racemosoumbellata include volatile oils, such as ferulic acid and umbellulone, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Ferula is often used to make various herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are commonly used to treat a range of health issues.

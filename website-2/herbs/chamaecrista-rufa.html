Chamaecrista rufa

Chamaecrista rufa, also known as yellow senna or partridge pea, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of the world.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat a range of health issues, including digestive problems, inflammation, and fever, due to its anti-inflammatory and laxative properties. The bioactive constituents of Chamaecrista rufa include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include teas, infusions, and capsules, often used to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome.

side effects

Heracleum pimpinellifolium, also known as small-hooped parsley fern or small-hooped cow parsley, contains a toxic compound called furanocoumarin, which can cause skin irritation, blistering, and phototoxicity upon exposure to sunlight. The plant can cause a condition known as phytophotodermatitis, leading to severe skin burns and blisters. Ingestion of the plant can lead to gastrointestinal problems, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. The furanocoumarins in the plant can also cause photosensitization, increasing the risk of skin damage and blistering. It's essential to handle the plant with care and avoid any potential contact with the skin.

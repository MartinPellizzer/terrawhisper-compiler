Dactylorhiza purpurella

Dactylorhiza purpurella, also known as the purple marsh-orchid, is a species of orchid found in wet meadows and marshes across Europe and Asia.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral activities, which may help to alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections and other conditions. The bioactive constituents of Dactylorhiza purpurella include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, such as tainstine and tainpuren, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Dactylorhiza purpurella is commonly prepared as a tincture, decoction, or dried extract, and is used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, as well as to support immune function.

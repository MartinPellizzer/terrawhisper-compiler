Gentiana argentea

Gentiana argentea, commonly known as the silver gentian, is a perennial plant species in the gentian family (Gentianaceae) native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine, where it is used to treat digestive issues, such as dyspepsia and gastritis, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. The bioactive constituents of Gentiana argentea include gentiopicrin, gentiin, and loganin, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, Gentiana argentea is typically prepared as a tincture, decoction, or powder, and is often used to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of indigestion and bloating.

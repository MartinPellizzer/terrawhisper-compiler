Goodenia purpurea

Goodenia purpurea, also known as Purple Goodenia or Goodenia, is an Australian native plant belonging to the Goodeniaceae family, characterized by its vibrant purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in Aboriginal medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin infections, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Goodenia purpurea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Goodenia purpurea is often made into teas, infusions, or tinctures, which are used to treat colds, flu, and respiratory issues, as well as to promote wound healing and skin health.

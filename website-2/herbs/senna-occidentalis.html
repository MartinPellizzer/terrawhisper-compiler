Senna occidentalis

Senna occidentalis, also known as Cassia occidentalis, is a tropical plant species that belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its laxative and anti-inflammatory properties, providing relief from constipation, fever, and various skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Senna occidentalis include anthraquinones, such as sennosides A and B, which contribute to its purgative effects, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids that exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

In herbal medicine, Senna occidentalis is commonly prepared as a tea or capsule, and is also used in combination with other herbs to create remedies for digestive issues and other health conditions.

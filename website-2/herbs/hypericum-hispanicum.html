Hypericum hispanicum

Hypericum hispanicum, also known as St. John's Wort of the Pyrenees, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and the Pyrenees.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including anxiety, depression, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Hypericum hispanicum include naphthoquinones, particularly hyperforin and hypericin, which have been shown to possess antidepressant and antiviral activities.

In herbal medicine, Hypericum hispanicum is commonly prepared as a tea or tincture, and is also used in topical creams and ointments to treat skin conditions such as wounds, eczema, and acne.

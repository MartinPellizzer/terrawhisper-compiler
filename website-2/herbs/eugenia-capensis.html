Eugenia capensis

Eugenia capensis, also known as the Cape Guava, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, providing relief from various health issues, including inflammation, digestive problems, and respiratory issues, due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. The bioactive constituents of Eugenia capensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its health benefits.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used in the production of jams and preserves, showcasing its versatility in herbal medicine and culinary applications.

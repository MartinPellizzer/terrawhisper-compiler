Corydalis acaulis

Corydalis acaulis, also known as Corydalis, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China and India, belonging to the family Papaveraceae.

Traditionally, Corydalis has been used in herbal medicine to treat a range of health issues, including pain, anxiety, and insomnia, due to its sedative and analgesic properties. The bioactive constituents of Corydalis include alkaloids such as tetrahydropalmatine, protopine, and corydaline, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Corydalis is commonly prepared as a tincture, capsule, or tea, and is often used in combination with other herbs to enhance its benefits.

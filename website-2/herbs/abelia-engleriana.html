Abelia engleriana

Abelia engleriana, also known as the glossy abelia, is a species of flowering plant in the honeysuckle family (Caprifoliaceae) that is native to China.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat a range of health issues, including fever, cough, and inflammation, due to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. Bioactive constituents of Abelia engleriana include phenolic compounds, flavonoids, and terpenoids, such as abelein, which have been shown to possess significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Abelia engleriana is commonly prepared as an infusion or decoction to treat respiratory and gastrointestinal issues, as well as a topical application for wound healing and skin conditions.

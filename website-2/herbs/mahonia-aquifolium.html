Mahonia aquifolium

Mahonia aquifolium, also known as Oregon grape, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest region of North America.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral activities, making it a potential treatment for various health issues such as digestive problems, skin infections, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Mahonia aquifolium include berberine, a glycoside with antimicrobial properties, and other alkaloids like berbamine and oxyberberine, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Mahonia aquifolium include extracts, tinctures, and capsules, which are often used to support immune function, relieve digestive issues, and promote overall well-being.

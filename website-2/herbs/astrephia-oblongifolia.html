Astrephia oblongifolia

Astrephia oblongifolia, also known as the narrow-leaved astrephia, is a perennial plant species native to the Andes mountain range in South America.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to treat respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to clear mucus and reduce inflammation in the airways. The bioactive constituents of Astrephia oblongifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are among the most common herbal preparations made from Astrephia oblongifolia, and are used to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of coughs and colds.

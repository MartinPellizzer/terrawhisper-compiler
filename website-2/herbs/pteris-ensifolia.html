Pteris ensifolia

Pteris ensifolia, also known as the sword fern, is a species of fern in the genus Pteris, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, inflammation, and respiratory problems, due to its antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Pteris ensifolia include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal preparations, Pteris ensifolia is often used in teas, infusions, and decoctions to alleviate symptoms of coughs, colds, and bronchitis, as well as to reduce fever and alleviate inflammation.

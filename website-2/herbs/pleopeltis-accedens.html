Pleopeltis accedens

Pleopeltis accedens, also known as the Antillean polypody, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its various health benefits, including the treatment of wounds, inflammation, and respiratory issues, due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Pleopeltis accedens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Pleopeltis accedens include infusions, decoctions, and extracts, which are often used to treat various health conditions and promote overall well-being.

Festuca campestris

Festuca campestris, also known as common meadow-grass or field fescue, is a type of perennial grass native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa.

The health benefits of Festuca campestris include its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may aid in reducing inflammation and oxidative stress in the body. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Festuca campestris is typically prepared as a tea or tincture, and is used to treat a range of ailments including digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin conditions.

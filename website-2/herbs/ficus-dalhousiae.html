Ficus dalhousiae

Ficus dalhousiae, also known as Dalhousie creeper, is a climbing plant species native to tropical forests of Asia, characterized by its green, smooth bark and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive problems, due to its purported health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Ficus dalhousiae include flavonoids, alkaloids, and tannins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ficus dalhousiae is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, or used in the form of a paste or oil, to treat a range of health conditions.

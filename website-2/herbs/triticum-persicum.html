classification

Triticum persicum is classified as a species of wheat within the genus Triticum and the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family. It is a type of diploid wheat, which means it has two sets of chromosomes. Triticum persicum is often used as a model organism in plant genetics and breeding research. This species is native to the Middle East and is one of the earliest cultivated forms of wheat.

Euryomyrtus thymifolia

Euryomyrtus thymifolia, also known as the lemon myrtle, is a small shrub native to Australia, belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

The leaves of this plant have been traditionally used in herbal medicine to provide relief from respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Euryomyrtus thymifolia include citral, a compound with potential antioxidant and anti-cancer effects, as well as limonene, a monoterpene with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities.

In herbal medicine, the leaves of Euryomyrtus thymifolia are commonly made into teas, infusions, and essential oils, which are used to treat a range of health issues, from coughs and colds to stress and anxiety.

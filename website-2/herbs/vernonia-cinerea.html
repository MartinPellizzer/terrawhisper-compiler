Vernonia cinerea

Vernonia cinerea, also known as grey ironweed or bitterleaf, is a plant species in the family Asteraceae.

It is widely used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including treating fever, rheumatism, and digestive disorders, as well as having anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Vernonia cinerea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, saponins, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant include decoctions, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, particularly those related to the digestive and respiratory systems.

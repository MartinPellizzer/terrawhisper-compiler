Senna socotrana

Senna socotrana, also known as Socotra senna, is a plant species belonging to the legume family (Fabaceae) that is native to the Socotra Island in Yemen.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating digestive issues such as constipation, due to its ability to stimulate bowel movements and relieve symptoms associated with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). The bioactive constituents of Senna socotrana include anthraquinones, glycosides, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its laxative and anti-inflammatory effects.

Senna socotrana is commonly used in herbal preparations such as tea, capsules, and tinctures, often in combination with other herbs like licorice and peppermint, to support digestive health and relieve constipation.

Hyptis sidifolia

Hyptis sidifolia, also known as Brazilian hyptis or tea tree hyptis, is a perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family, native to Brazil.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, respiratory issues, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties. The bioactive constituents of Hyptis sidifolia include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or essential oil, and is often used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and pneumonia, as well as skin conditions like acne and wounds.

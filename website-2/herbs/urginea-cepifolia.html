Urginea cepifolia

Urginea cepifolia, also known as Dragon's Blood, is a perennial plant species in the Asparagaceae family native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral activities, making it useful in treating various health conditions such as wounds, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Urginea cepifolia include saponins, alkaloids, and glycosides, particularly cepaflavone and cepifoline, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used in herbal preparations such as tinctures, decoctions, and ointments to treat a range of ailments, with the most common preparations being topical creams and poultices for wound healing and pain relief.

Arabis bucharica

Arabis bucharica, also known as Bucharica or Bucharica Arabis, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Brassicaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as provide protection against cardiovascular diseases and cancer. The bioactive constituents of Arabis bucharica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Arabis bucharica is often prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is also used to make syrup and ointments to treat various health conditions.

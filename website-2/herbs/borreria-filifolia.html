Borreria filifolia

Borreria filifolia, also known as "Threadflower," is a perennial herb belonging to the family Rubiaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as wounds, fever, and respiratory issues. Borreria filifolia contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for its wound-healing and anti-inflammatory effects.

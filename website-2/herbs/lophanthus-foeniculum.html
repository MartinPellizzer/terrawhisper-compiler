Lophanthus foeniculum

Lophanthus foeniculum, also known as Wild Bergamot, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae), characterized by its distinctive fragrance and purple flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate digestive issues, such as nausea, bloating, and irritable bowel syndrome, due to its anti-inflammatory and carminative properties. The bioactive constituents of Wild Bergamot include volatile oils, particularly linalool and linalyl acetate, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or infused oil, with the latter often used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

Melaleuca cupularis

Melaleuca cupularis, also known as the Paperbark or Narrow-leaved Paperbark, is a species of plant in the myrtle family Myrtaceae, native to eastern Australia.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which have been attributed to its various health benefits, including wound healing, skin conditions, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Melaleuca cupularis include compounds such as terpinen-4-ol, cineole, and eucalyptol, which have been identified as responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Melaleuca cupularis include essential oils, tinctures, and creams, which are often used topically to treat skin and respiratory conditions.

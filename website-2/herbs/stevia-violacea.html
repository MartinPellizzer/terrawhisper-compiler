Stevia violacea

Stevia violacea, a member of the sunflower family, is a plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, known for its sweet-tasting leaves.

The leaves of Stevia violacea have been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including diabetes, hypertension, and digestive issues, due to their ability to regulate blood sugar levels, lower blood pressure, and soothe the digestive system. The bioactive constituents of Stevia violacea include steviol glycosides, particularly stevioside and rebaudioside A, which are responsible for its sweet flavor and medicinal properties.

Stevia violacea is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, capsules, and extracts, as well as being used as a natural sweetener in food products.

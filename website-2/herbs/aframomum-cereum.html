Aframomum cereum

Aframomum cereum, also known as African bird's eye chili or African bird pepper, is a species of plant in the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) native to tropical Africa.

The bioactive constituents of Aframomum cereum include capsaicin, gingerols, and shogaols, which are responsible for its pungent flavor and medicinal properties.

In traditional African medicine, the plant is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and spice blends, as well as being incorporated into soups and stews to leverage its health benefits.

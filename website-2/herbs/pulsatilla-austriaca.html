Pulsatilla austriaca

Pulsatilla austriaca, also known as Austrian pasque flower, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Ranunculaceae.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including respiratory issues, fever, and skin irritations, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Pulsatilla austriaca include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, such as ferulic acid and scopoletin, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for wound care and skin issues.

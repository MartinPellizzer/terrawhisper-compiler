Cuscuta vulgaris

Cuscuta vulgaris, also known as dodder, is a parasitic plant that obtains its nutrients by attaching itself to and siphoning nutrients from its host plants.

Cuscuta vulgaris has been traditionally used to promote fertility, improve digestion, and alleviate symptoms of menopause, due to its potential health benefits in regulating hormonal imbalances and enhancing reproductive health. The bioactive constituents of this plant include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

Cuscuta vulgaris is commonly used in herbal preparations such as capsules, tinctures, and teas, often in combination with other fertility-enhancing herbs like Tribulus terrestris and Maca root.

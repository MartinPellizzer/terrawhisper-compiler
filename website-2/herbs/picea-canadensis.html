Picea canadensis

Picea canadensis, also known as the Eastern White Spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern North America.

The essential oil extracted from the needles of this plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral activities, contributing to its use in traditional medicine for treating respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs. The bioactive constituents of Picea canadensis essential oil include alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, and limonene, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Picea canadensis include essential oil, tinctures, and infused teas, which are used to promote respiratory health and relieve congestion.

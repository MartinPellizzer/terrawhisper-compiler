Vicia baicalensis

Vicia baicalensis, also known as the Baikal vetch, is a perennial legume native to China and Russia, characterized by its climbing or twining habit and small, white or purple flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease and protect against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Vicia baicalensis include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditional herbal preparations made from Vicia baicalensis include teas and tinctures, often used to treat various ailments, including fever, cough, and digestive issues.

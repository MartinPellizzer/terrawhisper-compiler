Abies bracteata

Abies bracteata, commonly known as the Sierra fir or blue fir, is a coniferous tree native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include sesquiterpenes, particularly bornyl acetate, and diterpenes, including abietane and labdane diterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Abies bracteata is often used in herbal preparations such as essential oils, tinctures, and infusions, which are commonly employed in aromatherapy, herbal remedies, and traditional medicine.

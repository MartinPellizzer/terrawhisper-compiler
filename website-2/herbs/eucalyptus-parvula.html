Eucalyptus parvula

Eucalyptus parvula, also known as the dwarf peppermint or little eucalyptus, is a small to medium-sized shrubby tree native to Australia.

The leaves of Eucalyptus parvula possess various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and expectorant properties, which can aid in relieving respiratory issues such as congestion and coughs. The bioactive constituents of this plant include eucalyptol, a key compound responsible for its decongestant and anti-inflammatory effects, as well as other volatile oils like α-pinene and limonene.

Eucalyptus parvula is commonly used in herbal preparations such as essential oils, teas, and capsules, which are often employed to treat respiratory issues, colds, and flu.

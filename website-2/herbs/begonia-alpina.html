Begonia alpina

Begonia alpina, also known as Mountain Begonia, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

The leaves and stems of Begonia alpina have been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin infections, due to its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic properties. The bioactive constituents of Begonia alpina include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Begonia alpina is often used to prepare infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, as well as being added to topical creams and ointments to treat skin conditions and wounds.

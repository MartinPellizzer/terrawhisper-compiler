Rhus falcata

Rhus falcata, also known as the climbing sumac, is a plant species native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia, characterized by its woody climber with compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The climbing sumac has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin disorders, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Rhus falcata include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with climbing sumac include decoctions, infusions, and ointments, which are used to treat a range of health issues, including wound healing, skin conditions, and respiratory problems.

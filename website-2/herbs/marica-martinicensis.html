Marica martinicensis

Marica martinicensis, also known as West Indian bay leaf or bay rum tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

The leaves of this plant have been traditionally used for their medicinal properties, which include reducing inflammation, relieving pain, and exhibiting antimicrobial properties, contributing to its use in treating various health conditions. The bioactive constituents of Marica martinicensis include essential oils such as myrcene, limonene, and alpha-pinene, as well as phenolic compounds like eugenol and ferulic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Marica martinicensis is often used in traditional medicine and is a key ingredient in various herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and essential oil blends.

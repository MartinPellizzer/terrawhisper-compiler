Anthriscus aemula

Anthriscus aemulus, also known as Meadow Celery or Wild Chervil, is a perennial plant species native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its hollow stems and compound leaves.

The plant has been utilized in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties, making it useful for treating digestive issues, wounds, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Anthriscus aemulus include flavonoids, such as apiin and apigenin, as well as essential oils like limonene and beta-pinene.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into various supplements and dietary products.

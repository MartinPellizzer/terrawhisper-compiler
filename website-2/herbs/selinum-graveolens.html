Selinum graveolens

Selinum graveolens, also known as wild celery or lovage, is a biennial or perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and stems, which have been used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory properties, helping to alleviate symptoms of indigestion, nausea, and menstrual cramps. The bioactive constituents of Selinum graveolens include volatile oils, flavonoids, and coumarins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

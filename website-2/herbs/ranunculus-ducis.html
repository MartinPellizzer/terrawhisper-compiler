Ranunculus ducis

Ranunculus ducis, also known as Duke's Cress, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Ranunculaceae family, native to Europe and Western Asia.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may aid in relieving symptoms of arthritis, improving cardiovascular health, and protecting against cell damage. Bioactive constituents of Ranunculus ducis include sesquiterpenoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Duke's Cress is used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, often combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

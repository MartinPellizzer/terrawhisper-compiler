Huperzia mexicana

Huperzia mexicana, also known as Mexican firmoss, is a species of liverwort, a type of non-vascular, thalloid plant.

The plant has been found to have various health benefits, including neuroprotective and antioxidant properties, which may help to prevent or treat neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's. The bioactive constituents of Huperzia mexicana include huperzine A, a potent inhibitor of acetylcholinesterase, as well as other alkaloids and flavonoids.

The most common herbal preparations made with Huperzia mexicana include capsules, tablets, and extracts, which are often used to support cognitive function and memory, and to alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression.

Potentilla leiophylla

Potentilla leiophylla, also known as Silkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae).

The bioactive constituents of Potentilla leiophylla include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Potentilla leiophylla is commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion, or used in the form of tinctures and extracts, often employed to treat conditions such as bronchitis, coughs, and skin irritations.

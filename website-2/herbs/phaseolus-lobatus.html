However, consuming raw or undercooked kidney beans can cause a natural toxin called phytohemagglutinin (PHA) to be released, leading to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. This toxin can also cause symptoms such as headaches, fatigue, and stomach cramps in some individuals. Cooking kidney beans thoroughly can inactivate PHA and reduce the risk of these side effects. Nevertheless, some people may still experience gastrointestinal issues or allergic reactions after eating kidney beans.

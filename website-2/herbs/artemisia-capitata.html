Artemisia capitata

Artemisia capitata, also known as South American mugwort, is a plant species belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is native to the temperate regions of South America.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which may aid in relieving digestive issues, reducing fever, and promoting wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Artemisia capitata include flavonoids, terpenoids, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Artemisia capitata is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into poultices and salves for topical application.

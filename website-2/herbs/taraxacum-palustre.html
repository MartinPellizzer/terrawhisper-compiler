Taraxacum palustre

Taraxacum palustre, also known as marsh dandelion, is a perennial plant species within the dandelion genus, characterized by its yellow flowers and toothed leaves.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include taraxasterol, a triterpenoid saponin, and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Marsh dandelion is commonly prepared as a tea or tincture, and its extracts have been used in herbal medicine to treat a range of conditions, including urinary tract infections and skin disorders.

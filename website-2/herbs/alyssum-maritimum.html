Alyssum maritimum

Alyssum maritimum, also known as sea rosemary or beach alyssum, is a low-growing, prostrate annual plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its delicate white flowers and creeping stems.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, providing relief from anxiety, insomnia, and stress due to its sedative and calming effects, as well as its ability to reduce inflammation and improve digestion. The bioactive constituents of Alyssum maritimum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Alyssum maritimum include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which can be used internally to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality.

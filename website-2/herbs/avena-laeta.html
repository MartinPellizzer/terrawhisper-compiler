Avena laeta

Avena laeta, commonly known as oat grass or green oat, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving sleep quality, and alleviating symptoms of anxiety and stress, likely due to its content of phenolic compounds and saponins. Bioactive constituents of Avena laeta include avenanthramides, a class of phenolic compounds, as well as a range of other polyphenols, flavonoids, and saponins that contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, Avena laeta is commonly prepared as a tea or tincture, and is also used in combination with other herbs in formulas aimed at promoting relaxation and improving sleep quality.

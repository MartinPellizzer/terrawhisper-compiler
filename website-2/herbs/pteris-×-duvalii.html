Pteris × duvalii

Pteris × duvalii, also known as Duval's fern, is a sterile hybrid species of the Pteris fern genus, characterized by its unique frond structure and growth habit.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in the management of various health conditions such as arthritis, wounds, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Pteris × duvalii include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for wound healing and skin care.

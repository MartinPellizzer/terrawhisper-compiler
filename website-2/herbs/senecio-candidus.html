Senecio candidus

Senecio candidus, also known as the White Ruellia or White Jacobea, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to reduce fever and alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Senecio candidus include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Senecio candidus is used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat various ailments, although its use should be approached with caution due to potential toxicity and the risk of liver damage.

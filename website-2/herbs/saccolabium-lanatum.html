Saccolabium lanatum

Saccolabium lanatum, also known as the hairy orchid, is a species of orchid that is native to the tropical regions of Asia.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including its ability to reduce inflammation, improve respiratory health, and exhibit antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Saccolabium lanatum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction or tincture, which is used to treat various ailments, including coughs, fever, and digestive issues.

Citronella costaricensis

Citronella costaricensis is a species of grass native to Central and South America, commonly known as West Indian lemongrass or Costarican citronella.

This plant has been used for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal activities, making it beneficial for treating various health issues such as fever, digestive problems, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Citronella costaricensis include citronellal, geraniol, and limonene, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often made into herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and essential oils, which are used to treat a range of health conditions and provide natural insect repellent properties.

Otholobium wilmsii

Otholobium wilmsii, also known as Wilms' vetchling, is a perennial legume plant native to South Africa, characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and hairy leaves.

Traditionally, Otholobium wilmsii has been used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, coughs, and digestive issues, due to its reported anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Otholobium wilmsii include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Otholobium wilmsii is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture to leverage its therapeutic benefits, although its efficacy and safety have not been extensively scientifically validated.

Davallia pinnatifida

Davallia pinnatifida, also known as the maidenhair fern or rabbit's foot fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia, characterized by its distinctive fronds and creeping rhizome.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities, contributing to its potential health benefits in treating various ailments such as skin conditions, wound healing, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Davallia pinnatifida include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Davallia pinnatifida is often prepared as a tea or infusion, as well as being included in herbal formulations such as capsules and ointments, to harness its medicinal benefits.

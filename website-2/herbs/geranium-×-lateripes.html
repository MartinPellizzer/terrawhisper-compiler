Geranium × lateripes

Geranium × lateripes, also known as the New Zealand geranium, is a hybrid plant species that belongs to the Geraniaceae family, characterized by its fragrant flowers and distinctive leaf shape.

The plant has been traditionally used to support women's health, with reported benefits including relief from menstrual cramps, menopausal symptoms, and anxiety, due to its adaptogenic and hormone-balancing properties. The bioactive constituents of Geranium × lateripes include geraniin, a flavonoid glycoside with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, as well as other plant compounds such as terpenes and sesquiterpenes.

In herbal medicine, Geranium × lateripes is often made into teas, tinctures, and infusions, with the most common preparations being used to support hormone balance, reduce menstrual distress, and promote relaxation.

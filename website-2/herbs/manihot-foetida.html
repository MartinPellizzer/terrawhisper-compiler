Manihot foetida

Manihot foetida, also known as the wild cassava or wild bitter manioc, is a plant species within the Euphorbiaceae family that is native to Central and South America.

The plant is valued for its edible roots, which have been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. The bioactive constituents of Manihot foetida include compounds such as saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Manihot foetida include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, although further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.

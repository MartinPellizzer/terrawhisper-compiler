Ficus alnifolia

Ficus alnifolia, also known as the paperleaf fig, is a plant species that belongs to the Moraceae family, native to the subtropical regions of Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it effective in treating various health conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Ficus alnifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ficus alnifolia is commonly prepared as a decoction or tincture, and is also used in the form of capsules or tablets to treat various health conditions.

Bromidium caespitosum

Bromidium caespitosum, also known as Quebrachillo, is a type of shrub native to South America, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and woody stems.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic activities, offering potential health benefits in the treatment of wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Bromidium caespitosum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and extracts, as well as being incorporated into salves and ointments for topical application.

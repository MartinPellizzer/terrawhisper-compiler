Rapuntium serpens

Rapuntium serpens, also known as Centella asiatica or Indian pennywort, is a perennial plant belonging to the waterwort family (Apocynaceae) that is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, particularly in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine, and is valued for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving cognitive function, and enhancing wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Rapuntium serpens include triterpenoids, such as asiatic acid and madecassic acid, as well as alkaloids and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Herbal preparations made from Rapuntium serpens include teas, tinctures, capsules, and ointments, which are often used to treat a range of conditions, from anxiety and insomnia to skin conditions and digestive issues.

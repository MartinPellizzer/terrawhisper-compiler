Cicutaria virosa

Cicutaria virosa, also known as Dalmatian pennyroyal, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae) that is native to the Dalmatia region of Croatia.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its ability to ease menstrual cramps, reduce anxiety and stress, and act as a natural insect repellent. The bioactive constituents of Cicutaria virosa include pulegone, a volatile oil that is responsible for its medicinal properties, as well as other compounds such as limonene and beta-pinene.

Dalmatian pennyroyal is often used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and essential oils, which are commonly used to treat conditions such as menstrual disorders, digestive issues, and as a natural remedy for insect bites and stings.

Verbascum atroviolaceum

Verbascum atroviolaceum, also known as Dark-purple Mullein, is a biennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family, characterized by its tall spikes of dark purple flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and expectorant properties, which can aid in relieving respiratory issues and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Verbascum atroviolaceum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and iridoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat coughs, sore throats, and skin irritations.

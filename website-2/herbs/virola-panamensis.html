Virola panamensis

Virola panamensis, also known as the "Yage" or "Ayahuasca vine," is a plant species native to the Amazon rainforest that belongs to the Myristicaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiparasitic properties, which may aid in treating various conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of Virola panamensis include alkaloids, such as viroline and virolinol, as well as other secondary metabolites like flavonoids and phenolic acids.

In traditional herbal medicine, Virola panamensis is often used to prepare ayahuasca, a psychedelic brew that is also made from other plant species, and is also used in its own right in teas, tinctures, and infusions.

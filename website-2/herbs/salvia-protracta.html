Salvia protracta

Salvia protracta, also known as purple sage or creeping sage, is a perennial, flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

This plant has been traditionally used to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety and stress, and improve sleep quality due to its calming effects on the nervous system. The bioactive constituents of Salvia protracta include salvianolic acids, ursolic acid, and rosmarinic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Salvia protracta is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions to support mental health and well-being, although more research is needed to confirm its efficacy.

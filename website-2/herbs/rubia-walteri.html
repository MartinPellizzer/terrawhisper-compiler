Rubia walteri

Rubia walteri, also known as Wild Rubia or Walter's Madder, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Rubiaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which are thought to aid in the treatment of various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of Rubia walteri include alkaloids, flavonoids, and anthraquinones, particularly gallic acid, quercetin, and rubiadin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Rubia walteri is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used in traditional folk medicine to create salves and ointments for topical application.

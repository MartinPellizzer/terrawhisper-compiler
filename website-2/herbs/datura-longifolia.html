Datura longifolia

Datura longifolia, also known as Devil's Trumpet or Angel's Trumpet, is a perennial plant species native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers and edible, tubular fruits.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and sedative properties, making it beneficial for treating conditions such as anxiety, insomnia, and muscle spasms. The bioactive constituents of Datura longifolia include alkaloids like scopolamine and hyoscyamine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Herbal preparations made from Datura longifolia include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to treat a range of health issues, although the plant should be used with caution due to its potential toxicity and side effects.

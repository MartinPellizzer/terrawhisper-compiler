Ilex brasiliensis

Ilex brasiliensis, also known as the Brazilian holly, is a species of evergreen tree in the holly family (Aquifoliaceae) native to Brazil.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its potential health benefits in reducing oxidative stress and improving immune function. The bioactive constituents of Ilex brasiliensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include infusions, decoctions, and capsules, often used to support immune system function, reduce inflammation, and alleviate respiratory symptoms.

Rhododendron japonicum

Rhododendron japonicum, also known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia, characterized by its vibrant flowers and fragrant leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, offering various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help to alleviate conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Rhododendron japonicum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Rhododendron japonicum is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into formulations such as poultices and salves to support wound healing and skin health.

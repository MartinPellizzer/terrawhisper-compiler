Praxelis diffusa

Praxelis diffusa, also known as "Tetraneria," is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

This plant is valued for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Praxelis diffusa include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Praxelis diffusa is used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as in the form of tinctures and extracts, to harness its medicinal benefits.

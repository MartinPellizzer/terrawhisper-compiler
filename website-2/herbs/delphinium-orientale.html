Delphinium orientale

Delphinium orientale, also known as the Oriental larkspur, is a perennial plant species in the family Ranunculaceae, characterized by its tall spikes of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues, fever, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Delphinium orientale include delphinine, a diterpene alkaloid with potential pharmacological activity, as well as other alkaloids and flavonoids.

In traditional herbal medicine, Delphinium orientale is often prepared as a decoction or tincture, and it is also used in combination with other herbs in various formulations, such as cough syrups and topical creams.

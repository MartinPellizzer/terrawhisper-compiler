Dryopteris cucullata

Dryopteris cucullata, also known as wood fern or cleft wood fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties, which can help to alleviate symptoms of arthritis, wounds, and oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Dryopteris cucullata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to support immune function and reduce inflammation.

Caragana angustissima

Caragana angustissima, also known as Caragana, is a medicinal shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, characterized by its dense foliage and small, white flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various health issues, including digestive problems, inflammation, and cardiovascular diseases, due to its rich content of bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids. These bioactive compounds are believed to contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it a valuable natural remedy.

Caragana is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, as well as being used in the production of capsules and extracts, allowing for its health benefits to be easily integrated into a wellness routine.

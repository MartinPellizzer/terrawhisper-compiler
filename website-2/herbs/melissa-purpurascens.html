Melissa purpurascens

Melissa purpurascens, also known as lavender melissa or purple melissa, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae, characterized by its purple flowers and fragrant foliage.

The health benefits of Melissa purpurascens include its anxiolytic, antispasmodic, and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate stress, anxiety, and sleep disorders, as well as soothe digestive issues and reduce pain. The bioactive constituents of Melissa purpurascens include terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, such as linalool, linalyl acetate, and rosmarinic acid, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Melissa purpurascens include teas, tinctures, essential oils, and infusions, often used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine to promote relaxation and improve overall well-being.

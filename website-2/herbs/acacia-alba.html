Acacia alba

Acacia alba, also known as the white wattle or silver wattle, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities, contributing to its potential use in treating conditions such as arthritis, wounds, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Acacia alba include flavonoids, terpenoids, and polyphenolic compounds, particularly acacia flavone and acacia catechin, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Acacia alba is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or powder, and is used to support immune function and alleviate symptoms of colds, fever, and respiratory infections.

Arenaria trigyna

Arenaria trigyna, also known as Sandwort, is a perennial plant species that belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in medicine to treat various health conditions, including digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Arenaria trigyna include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Sandwort include decoctions, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat digestive problems and other health issues.

Cymbopogon foliosus

Cymbopogon foliosus, also known as Java citronella or wild citronella, is a tropical grass species native to Southeast Asia and is a member of the Poaceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, exhibiting various health benefits such as antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making it effective in treating wounds, skin infections, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Cymbopogon foliosus include essential oils, particularly citronellal, geraniol, and limonene, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Cymbopogon foliosus is commonly used to prepare teas, infusions, and essential oil extracts that are used to treat various health conditions and to repel insects.

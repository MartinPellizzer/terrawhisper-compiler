Dactylorhiza × ruppertii

Dactylorhiza × ruppertii, also known as Ruppert's orchid or hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring intergeneric hybrid between the orchid species Dactylorhiza maculata and Coeloglossum viride.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to help alleviate respiratory issues, such as bronchitis, and its anti-inflammatory properties, which can aid in reducing swelling and pain. The bioactive constituents of Dactylorhiza × ruppertii include iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which have been shown to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various respiratory and inflammatory conditions.

Arbutus serratifolia

Arbutus serratifolia, also known as Strawberry Tree, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family (Ericaceae) that is native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, which can help to alleviate digestive issues, reduce fever, and support immune function. The bioactive constituents of Arbutus serratifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu.

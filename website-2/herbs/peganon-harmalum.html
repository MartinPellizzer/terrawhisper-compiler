Peganon harmalum

Peganum harmala, commonly known as Syrian rue or harmal, is a perennial plant native to the Middle East and Mediterranean regions.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including anxiety, insomnia, and fever, due to its antispasmodic, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties. Peganum harmala contains several bioactive constituents, including alkaloids such as harmine, harmaline, and tetrahydroharmine, which are believed to contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, the seeds of Peganum harmala are commonly used to make teas, tinctures, and capsules, which are often used to treat anxiety and other related conditions.

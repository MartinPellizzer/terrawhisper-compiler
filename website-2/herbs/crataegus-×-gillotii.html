Crataegus × gillotii

Crataegus × gillotii, also known as Gillot's hawthorn, is a hybrid species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

The hawthorn plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat cardiovascular issues, such as high blood pressure and coronary heart disease, due to its potential to improve blood circulation and lower cholesterol levels, thereby reducing the risk of heart disease. The bioactive constituents of Crataegus × gillotii include flavonoids, oligomeric proanthocyanidins, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Crataegus × gillotii include tinctures, capsules, and teas, which are often used to support cardiovascular health and promote overall well-being.

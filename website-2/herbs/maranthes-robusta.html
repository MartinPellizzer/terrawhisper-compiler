Maranthes robusta

Maranthes robusta, also known as African pear or bush mango, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa, characterized by its edible fruit and medicinal properties.

The fruit and leaves of Maranthes robusta have been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, malaria, and respiratory infections, due to its antimalarial, antibacterial, and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Maranthes robusta include alkaloids, saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Maranthes robusta is often prepared as a decoction or infusion of the leaves, or as a tincture of the bark, to treat various health conditions.

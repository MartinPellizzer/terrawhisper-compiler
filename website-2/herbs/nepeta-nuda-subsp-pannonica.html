What is Nepeta nuda subsp. pannonica?

Nepeta nuda subsp.

pannonica is a subspecies of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae, native to Europe. It is an herbaceous perennial with a woody base, growing up to 30-50 cm in height and 30-50 cm in width, with linear to lance-shaped leaves that are 2-10 cm long and 0.5-2 cm wide.

The leaves are dark green, glabrous, and have a cuneate base. The inflorescence is a terminal spike, containing white flowers with a purple calyx that is 8-12 mm long and 6-8 mm wide, with a 2-lipped corolla that is 10-12 mm long.

The flowers are borne from June to August.

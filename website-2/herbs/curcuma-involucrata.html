Curcuma involucrata

Curcuma involucrata, also known as Black Turmeric or Black Ginger, is a plant species belonging to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae), native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, which can help to alleviate symptoms of arthritis, improve digestion, and boost the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Curcuma involucrata include curcumenol, curdione, and gingerols, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Curcuma involucrata include tea, powder, and oil extracts, which are used in traditional medicine and as dietary supplements to promote overall well-being and health.

Madhuca malaccensis

Madhuca malaccensis is a species of tree native to Southeast Asia, belonging to the Sapotaceae family.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include the treatment of various health conditions such as fever, cough, and respiratory issues, as well as its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Madhuca malaccensis include flavonoids, triterpenoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health conditions.

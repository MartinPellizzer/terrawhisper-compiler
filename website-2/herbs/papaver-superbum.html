Papaver superbum

Papaver superbum, commonly known as the American poppy, is a perennial plant belonging to the Papaveraceae family.

Traditionally, the plant has been used to treat various health issues, including anxiety, insomnia, and pain, due to its potential sedative and analgesic properties. The bioactive constituents of Papaver superbum include alkaloids, particularly protopine, chelirubine, and sanguinarine, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often used in traditional folk medicine to alleviate stress and promote relaxation.

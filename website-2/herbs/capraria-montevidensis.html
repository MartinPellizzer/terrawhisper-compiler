Capraria montevidensis

Capraria montevidensis, also known as the American snake flower, is a perennial plant native to South America, belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, inflammation, and respiratory issues, due to its antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Capraria montevidensis include alkaloids, such as caprarin and caprarinine, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Herbal preparations made from the plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to alleviate symptoms of coughs, colds, and flu.

Anil indica

Anil (Pogostemon cablin), also known as Anil indica or Patchouli, is a perennial plant native to tropical regions of Asia, characterized by its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

The health benefits of Anil include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties, which have been traditionally used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Anil include patchoulol, pogostone, and patchoulene, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Anil is commonly prepared as an essential oil or a tincture, and is used to treat a range of health issues, including skin conditions, anxiety, and insomnia.

Pelargonium × sulphureum

Pelargonium × sulphureum, also known as yellow rose geranium, is a hybrid plant species within the Geraniaceae family, characterized by its bright yellow flowers and pungent scent.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which can aid in soothing digestive issues, relieving stress and anxiety, and promoting a restful sleep. The bioactive constituents of Pelargonium × sulphureum include geraniol, linalool, and citronellol, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are teas, tinctures, and essential oils, which are often used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine to promote relaxation and overall well-being.

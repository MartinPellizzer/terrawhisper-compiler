Falcaria falcaria

Falcaria falcaria, also known as the wall rue or thread-leaved rue, is a perennial plant that belongs to the family Apiaceae.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and antioxidant properties, which may help to relieve conditions such as arthritis, menstrual cramps, and gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of Falcaria falcaria include falcarinol and falcarindiol, which are thought to be responsible for the plant's medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Falcaria falcaria is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into creams and salves for topical application.

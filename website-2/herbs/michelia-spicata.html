Michelia spicata

Michelia spicata, also known as the Michelia flower or the Golden Michelia, is a flowering plant species native to the tropical regions of Asia, characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers and glossy leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Michelia spicata include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, such as michelino and micheliaquinone, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional Indian medicine, Michelia spicata is often used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as ointments and creams, to harness its therapeutic benefits.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of P. corralensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and other terpenoids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make teas, infusions, and extracts, which are often used to treat coughs, colds, and other respiratory ailments.

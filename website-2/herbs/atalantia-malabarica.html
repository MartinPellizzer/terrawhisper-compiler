Atalantia malabarica

Atalantia malabarica, also known as the Malabar Atalantia, is a species of plant in the family Rutaceae, native to the Western Ghats of India.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine and is valued for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can aid in reducing fever, relieving respiratory issues, and protecting against oxidative stress. Bioactive constituents of A. malabarica include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, A. malabarica is commonly prepared as a decoction or a powder, and is also used to make herbal teas, infusions, and capsules, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including coughs, colds, and digestive issues.

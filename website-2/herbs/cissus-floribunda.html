Cissus floribunda

Cissus floribunda, also known as the grape ivy or wood vine, is a perennial climber native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its ability to support bone health, reduce inflammation, and aid in weight management due to its high content of flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins. Bioactive constituents of Cissus floribunda include flavonoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, as well as alkaloids like cissamine and cissusamine, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Cissus floribunda is often prepared into teas, infusions, and capsules, and is used to support bone health, reduce joint pain, and promote overall well-being.

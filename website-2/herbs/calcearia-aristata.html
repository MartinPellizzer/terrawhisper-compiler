Calcearia aristata

Calceolaria aristata, also known as the Slipper flower or Pocket flower, is a species of flowering plant in the family Calceolariaceae.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which are said to aid in the treatment of various ailments such as colds, fever, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Calceolaria aristata include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is also used in the form of capsules or extracts, although its safety and efficacy have not been extensively scientifically proven.

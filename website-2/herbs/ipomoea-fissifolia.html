Ipomoea fissifolia

Ipomoea fissifolia, also known as the 'fingered morning glory', is a flowering plant species native to tropical Asia, characterized by its unique, finger-like leaves and vibrant purple flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal disorders, due to its anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea fissifolia include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which have been found to exhibit antioxidant and cytoprotective activities.

Traditionally, the plant is prepared as an herbal tea, tincture, or decoction, and is used in various folk medicine practices to treat fever and other ailments.

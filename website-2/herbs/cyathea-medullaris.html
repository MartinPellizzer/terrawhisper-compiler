Cyathea medullaris

Cyathea medullaris, also known as the Black Tree Fern, is a large, evergreen fern native to New Zealand.

This plant has been used in traditional Maori medicine for its various health benefits, including the treatment of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Cyathea medullaris include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Cyathea medullaris is commonly prepared as a tea or tincture, and is also used in the form of a cough syrup and expectorant to aid in the relief of respiratory congestion.

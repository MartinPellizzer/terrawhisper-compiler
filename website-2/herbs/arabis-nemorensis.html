Arabis nemorensis

Arabis nemorensis, also known as woodland rock-cress, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its delicate white or pink flowers and lobed or toothed leaves.

The bioactive constituents of Arabis nemorensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbalism, Arabis nemorensis is often prepared as a tincture, tea, or infusion to address its therapeutic effects, and it is also used in some traditional remedies for coughs and bronchitis.

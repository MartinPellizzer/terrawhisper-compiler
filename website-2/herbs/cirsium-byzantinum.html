Cirsium byzantinum

Cirsium byzantinum, also known as Butterfly Milkweed, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family, native to the Mediterranean region and Western Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine due to its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in treating various ailments such as fever, respiratory issues, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Cirsium byzantinum include flavonoids, sesquiterpenes, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments to support skin health and wound healing.

Festuca aquatica

Festuca aquatica, also known as water fescue, is a perennial grass species native to North America, characterized by its ability to thrive in wetland environments.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Festuca aquatica is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to treat respiratory issues and promote overall well-being.

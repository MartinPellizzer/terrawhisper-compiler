Eugenia anjouanensis

Eugenia anjouanensis, also known as the Anjouan clove tree or Eugenia, is a flowering plant species native to the Comoros Islands.

The bioactive constituents of Eugenia anjouanensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, particularly eugenol, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Eugenia anjouanensis is often used in traditional medicine and is typically prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, although its essential oil is also used in aromatherapy and as a flavoring agent.

Festuca obtusata

Festuca obtusata is a perennial grass species, also known as obtuse fescue, characterized by its narrow, flat leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, with reported benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, making it a potential remedy for wounds, skin irritations, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Festuca obtusata include phenolic compounds, flavonoids, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into salves and creams for topical application.

Ruellia longifolia

Ruellia longifolia, also known as Long-leaf Ruellia, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family, native to tropical Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been associated with several health benefits, including the treatment of fever, sore throat, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Ruellia longifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the leaves and roots of the plant are commonly used to prepare teas, infusions, and decoctions, which are used to treat various ailments and promote overall well-being.

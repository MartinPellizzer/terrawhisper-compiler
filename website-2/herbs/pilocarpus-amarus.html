Pilocarpus amarus

Pilocarpus amarus, also known as Jaborandi or Pilocarpus, is a plant species native to the tropical regions of South America, belonging to the family Rutaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its various health benefits, including treating glaucoma, hypertension, and certain gastrointestinal disorders, due to its ability to lower intraocular pressure and relax smooth muscles. The bioactive constituents of Pilocarpus amarus include flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids, particularly pilocarpine, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Pilocarpus amarus is commonly prepared as an infusion or extract, and is used in traditional remedies such as eye drops and ointments to treat eye disorders and as a diuretic.

Ipomoea maingayi

Ipomoea maingayi, commonly known as the Moonflower or Cardinal Gloxinia, is a tropical plant species in the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea maingayi include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ipomoea maingayi is often used to prepare infusions, decoctions, and powder formulations, which are used to treat various health conditions.

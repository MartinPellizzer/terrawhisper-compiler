Vincetoxicum africanum

Vincetoxicum africanum, also known as African Vincetoxicum or Ox-tongue, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Apocynaceae family, native to tropical Africa.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in treating various conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Vincetoxicum africanum include vincetoxin, an alkaloid with reported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activity, as well as other glycosides and phenolic compounds.

Traditionally, the plant is used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory issues.

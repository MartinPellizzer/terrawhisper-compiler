Bacopa diffusa

Bacopa diffusa, also known as Brahmi, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, belonging to the family Bacopa.

The health benefits of Bacopa diffusa include improved memory and cognitive function, enhanced brain processing speed, and reduced stress and anxiety levels, making it a popular adaptogenic herb. The bioactive constituents of Bacopa diffusa include bacosides, saponins, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Bacopa diffusa include capsules, tablets, and teas, as well as extracts in the form of tinctures and glycerites, which are often used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

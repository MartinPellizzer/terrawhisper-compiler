Justicia lancifolia

Justicia lancifolia, also known as Brazilian plume flower, is a plant species belonging to the Acanthaceae family, native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, cough, and respiratory issues, due to its reported health benefits, such as antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Justicia lancifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as an infusion, decoction, or tincture, and is used to treat respiratory infections, such as bronchitis and pneumonia.

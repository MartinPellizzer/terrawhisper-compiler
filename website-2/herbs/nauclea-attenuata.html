Nauclea attenuata

Nauclea attenuata, also known as Quinine Tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, offering various health benefits, including the treatment of fever, malaria, and other infectious diseases, as well as its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Nauclea attenuata include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Nauclea attenuata is commonly prepared as a decoction or a tincture, often in combination with other herbs, to treat various health conditions.

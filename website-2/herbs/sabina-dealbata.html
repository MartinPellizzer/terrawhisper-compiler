Sabina dealbata

Sabina dealbata, also known as Sabadilla, is a perennial plant native to Mexico, characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive, drooping spikes.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, and its extracts have been found to possess various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antiparasitic, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Sabina dealbata include alkaloids such as sabadilline, sabadilline N-oxide, and sabadilline N-methyl ether, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Sabina dealbata is often used in herbal preparations, including tinctures, teas, and ointments, which are commonly used to treat skin conditions, such as eczema and dermatitis, as well as parasitic infections, like lice and scabies.

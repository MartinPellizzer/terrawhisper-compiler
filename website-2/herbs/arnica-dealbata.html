Arnica dealbata

Arnica dealbata, also known as mountain arnica, is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

The plant is valued for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which have been traditionally used to relieve pain and reduce swelling in muscles, joints, and wounds, as well as to treat bruises and sprains. The bioactive constituents of Arnica dealbata include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Arnica dealbata is commonly used in herbal preparations such as topical creams, ointments, and salves, as well as in homeopathic remedies, to support the treatment of injuries and inflammatory conditions.

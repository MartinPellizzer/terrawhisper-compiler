Salix glandulosa

Salix glandulosa is a species of willow tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, characterized by its glandular leaves and shrub-like growth habit.

The bark and leaves of Salix glandulosa have been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat fever, rheumatism, and other inflammatory conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The bioactive constituents of Salix glandulosa include salicylic acid, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

Salix glandulosa is commonly used to make teas, tinctures, and salves, which are traditionally used to reduce pain and inflammation, as well as to treat wounds and skin conditions.

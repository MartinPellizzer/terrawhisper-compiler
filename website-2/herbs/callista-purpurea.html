Callista purpurea

Callista purpurea, also known as the purple clover or sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family (Fabaceae) native to Europe and Western Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include its potential to aid in digestion, relieve anxiety and insomnia, and act as an antiseptic to promote wound healing. Bioactive constituents of Callista purpurea include isoflavones, phenolic compounds, and coumarins, which have been shown to have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects.

Callista purpurea is commonly prepared as a herbal tea, infusion, or tincture, and is also used in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin issues.

Cerastium adscendens

Cerastium adscendens, also known as the field chickweed or mountain chickweed, is a perennial plant species belonging to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties, providing potential health benefits such as wound healing, skin protection, and immune system support. The bioactive constituents of Cerastium adscendens include flavonoids, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Cerastium adscendens include infusions, tinctures, and topical creams, which are used to treat skin conditions, promote wound healing, and soothe digestive issues.

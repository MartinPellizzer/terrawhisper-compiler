Carum copticum

Carum copticum, also known as Ajwain or Bishop's Weed, is a small, annual or biennial plant belonging to the family Apiaceae, native to the Middle East and South Asia.

The health benefits of Carum copticum include its ability to alleviate digestive issues, relieve respiratory problems, and exhibit anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, making it a popular remedy for various ailments. The bioactive constituents of this plant include ajwainin, ajwain glycoside, and thymol, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Carum copticum is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and decoctions, and is also added to various traditional remedies like ghee and oils for its therapeutic benefits.

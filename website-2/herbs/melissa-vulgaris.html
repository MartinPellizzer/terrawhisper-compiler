Melissa vulgaris

Melissa vulgaris, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae) characterized by its bright green, oval-shaped leaves and yellow flowers.

The health benefits of lemon balm include its calming and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Melissa vulgaris include rosmarinic acid, melissic acid, and citral, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Lemon balm is often used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as in skincare products and aromatherapy blends, to promote relaxation and soothe the skin.

classification

Melissa vulgaris, also known as lemon balm, is classified as a perennial herb belonging to the family Lamiaceae. This family of plants is commonly known as the mint family, which includes other well-known herbs like mint, basil, and rosemary. The genus Melissa is further divided into two main species: Melissa officinalis and Melissa vulgaris. Melissa vulgaris is often considered a synonym of Melissa officinalis, as it is a common name for the same plant. However, some sources may still distinguish between the two, with Melissa officinalis being the more widely accepted and cultivated species.

side effects

Melissa vulgaris, also known as lemon balm, is generally considered safe when consumed in moderation. However, excessive consumption may cause side effects such as dizziness, headaches, and stomach upset due to its high concentration of volatile oils. Some individuals may also experience allergic reactions or interact with certain medications when using lemon balm products. It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using lemon balm, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women, as it may affect their health. In some cases, lemon balm may also cause photosensitivity, making the skin more susceptible to sun damage.

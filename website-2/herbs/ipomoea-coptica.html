Ipomoea coptica

Ipomoea coptica, commonly known as Indian or Oriental arrowroot, is a perennial plant native to Asia and Australia, characterized by its tuberous roots and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including the treatment of digestive issues, inflammation, and respiratory problems, due to its potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea coptica include starch, inulin, and various alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Ipomoea coptica is commonly prepared as a powder, tea, or decoction, often used to treat diarrhea, constipation, and other gastrointestinal disorders.

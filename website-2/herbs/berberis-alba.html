Berberis alba

Berberis alba, also known as white barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and the mountainous regions of Central Asia, characterized by its white or pale yellow flowers and yellow or red berries.

The bioactive constituents of Berberis alba include berberine, a quaternary ammonium alkaloid with strong antimicrobial properties, as well as other alkaloids like berbamine and oxyacanthine.

Koeleria coerulescens

Koeleria coerulescens, also known as blue hair grass, is a species of perennial grass native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various health issues, including respiratory problems, fever, and digestive disorders, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. Koeleria coerulescens contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and coumarins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditional herbal preparations made with Koeleria coerulescens include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, as well as to promote digestive health.

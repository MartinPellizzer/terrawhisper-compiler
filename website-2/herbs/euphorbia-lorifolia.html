Euphorbia lorifolia

Euphorbia lorifolia, commonly known as the leafless spurge, is a succulent plant species native to the tropical regions of the world.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin infections, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which provide relief from pain and reduce the risk of infection. Bioactive constituents of Euphorbia lorifolia include flavonoids, tannins, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, as well as being used in the form of a poultice or topical cream to treat skin conditions, such as wounds and eczema.

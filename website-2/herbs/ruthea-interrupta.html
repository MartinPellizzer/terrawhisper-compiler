Ruthea interrupta

Ruthea interrupta, also known as Devil's Claw, is a perennial plant species native to southern Africa, characterized by its claw-like roots and tubular flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including the reduction of pain and inflammation, as well as its ability to aid in digestion and alleviate symptoms of arthritis. The bioactive constituents of Ruthea interrupta include harpagoside, a glycoside responsible for its medicinal properties, as well as other iridoid glycosides and phenolic compounds.

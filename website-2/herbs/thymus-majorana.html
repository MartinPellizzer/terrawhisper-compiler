Thymus majorana

Thymus majorana, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

This plant is valued for its carminative and antispasmodic properties, providing relief from digestive issues, anxiety, and insomnia, as well as its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. The bioactive constituents of Thymus majorana include thymol, carvacrol, and linalool, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Sweet marjoram is often used in herbal preparations, such as teas, essential oils, and tinctures, which are commonly used to alleviate stress, improve sleep quality, and soothe respiratory issues.

What is Hieracium saxifragum subsp. freibergianum?

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

freibergianum is a botanical taxon within the Asteraceae family. It exhibits characteristics typical of the Hieracium genus, with leaves that are pinnately or palmately lobed, and a rosette growth habit.

The plant's inflorescence is a capitulum, comprising numerous small flowers with yellow to orange corollas, surrounded by greenish-yellow to yellow phyllaries. The leaves' margins are ciliate, and the plant's height typically ranges from 10 to 30 centimeters. Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

freibergianum is distinguished from other subspecies by its unique combination of morphological traits.

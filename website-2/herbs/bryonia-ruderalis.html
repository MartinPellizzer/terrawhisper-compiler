Bryonia ruderalis

Bryonia alba, also known as Bryony, is a perennial climbing plant that belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, fever, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antispasmodic properties. The bioactive constituents of Bryonia alba include saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In herbal preparations, Bryonia alba is often used in the form of tinctures, teas, and dried root, and is commonly combined with other herbs to treat conditions such as arthritis, colic, and menstrual cramps.

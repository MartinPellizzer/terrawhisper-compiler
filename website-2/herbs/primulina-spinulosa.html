Primulina spinulosa

Primulina spinulosa, also known as the spiny Primulina, is a species of flowering plant in the family Gesneriaceae, native to China and Southeast Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, particularly in Tibetan and Mongolian cultures, to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory infections, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Primulina spinulosa include iridoid glycosides, phenolic acids, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Primulina spinulosa is often prepared as a decoction or extract, and it is also used in combination with other herbs to make teas, tinctures, and capsules.

Tsuga × jeffreyi

Tsuga × jeffreyi, also known as Jeffrey's hemlock or intergeneric hybrid of western hemlock (Tsuga heterophylla) and mountain hemlock (Tsuga mertensiana), is a rare and slow-growing evergreen coniferous tree native to the western United States.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it a promising natural remedy for various ailments, including respiratory issues and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Tsuga × jeffreyi include flavonoids, phenolic acids, terpenoids, and lignans, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Tsuga × jeffreyi is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, although its use is mostly limited to traditional and folk medicine due to its rarity and potential toxicity.

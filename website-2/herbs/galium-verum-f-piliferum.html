What is Galium verum f. piliferum?

Galium verum f. piliferum is a botanical variant of Galium verum, a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Rubiaceae.

It has a taproot and erect, branching stems up to 60 cm in height, covered with piliferous leaves. The leaves are ovate, lanceolate, or elliptical, with a pointed tip and a wavy margin, measuring 1-2.5 cm in length. The inflorescence is a corymb, bearing 4-12 small, white to yellowish flowers with 4-5 stamens, which are bilobed and glabrous.

The fruit is a capsule, containing 4-5 seeds, each about 1-2 mm in length.

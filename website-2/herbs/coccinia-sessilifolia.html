Coccinia sessilifolia

Coccinia sessilifolia, also known as ivy gourd or baby jackfruit, is a climber plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, skin diseases, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Coccinia sessilifolia include cucurbitacins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction, infusion, or juice, or used in the form of tablets or capsules, to harness its health benefits.

Hieracium vulpii

Hieracium vulpii, also known as Hawkweed or Vulpi's Hawkweed, is a perennial plant species within the dandelion family (Asteraceae) characterized by its yellow flowers and jagged leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including wounds, fever, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Hieracium vulpii include flavonoids, sesquiterpenes, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

Hieracium vulpii is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as dried flowers and leaves that can be consumed as a supplement or added to salves and ointments for topical use.

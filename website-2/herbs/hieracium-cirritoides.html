Hieracium cirritoides

Hieracium cirritoides, commonly known as hawkweed, is a perennial plant species that belongs to the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which have been traditionally used to treat various health issues such as wound healing, skin conditions, and digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of Hieracium cirritoides include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Traditionally, hawkweed is used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and gastrointestinal issues.

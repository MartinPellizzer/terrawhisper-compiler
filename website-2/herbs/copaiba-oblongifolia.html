Copaiba oblongifolia

Copaiba oblongifolia is a species of tree native to the Amazon rainforest, belonging to the family Burseraceae, and its resinous oil is often referred to as "copaiba oil.".

The health benefits of Copaiba oblongifolia include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it useful for soothing skin irritations, reducing pain and inflammation, and supporting immune function. The bioactive constituents of Copaiba oblongifolia include beta-caryophyllene, a sesquiterpene with anti-inflammatory properties, and caryophyllene oxide, which has been shown to have antimicrobial and antioxidant effects.

Copaiba oil is often used in herbal preparations such as topical creams, ointments, and salves, as well as in aromatherapy and oral supplements, although its use should be guided by a healthcare professional due to potential interactions with medications.

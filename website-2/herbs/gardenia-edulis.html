Gardenia edulis

Gardenia edulis, also known as the yellow gardenia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia, characterized by its fragrant white flowers and yellow fruit.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Gardenia edulis include flavonoids, iridoids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or dried flower extract, and is also used in the production of herbal infusions and traditional remedies.

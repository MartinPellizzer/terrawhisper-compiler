Anthyllis versicolor

Anthyllis versicolor, also known as kidney vetch or varicolored kidney vetch, is a perennial legume plant native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its small, pink or purple flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including its ability to treat kidney stones, urinary tract infections, and inflammation. The bioactive constituents of Anthyllis versicolor include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Anthyllis versicolor include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to support kidney function and alleviate symptoms of urinary disorders.

Bletia pumila

Bletia pumila, also known as the miniature orchid or lesser cat's claw, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, characterized by its small, white flowers and compact growth habit.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which are thought to help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections, wounds, and gastrointestinal issues. Bioactive constituents of Bletia pumila include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which are believed to be responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Bletia pumila is often prepared as a tea or tincture, although it is less commonly used than its more well-known relative, Bletia striata, also known as the cat's claw.

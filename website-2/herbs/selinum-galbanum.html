classification

Selinum galbanum, also known as wild celery or wild lovage, is classified in the family Apiaceae. This family includes many plants commonly known as umbels, such as carrots and parsley. The genus Selinum, to which wild lovage belongs, is a group of plants native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Selinum galbanum specifically is a perennial herb, with a distinctive, hollow stem and divided leaves. The plant's classification helps to distinguish it from other members of its genus.

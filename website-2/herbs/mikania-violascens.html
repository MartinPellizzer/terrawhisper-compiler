Mikania violascens

Mikania violascens, also known as violascent vine or Mikania, is a perennial vine native to South America, belonging to the Asteraceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its ability to aid in digestion, relieve gastrointestinal issues, and exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities, making it a valuable resource in folk medicine. The bioactive constituents of Mikania violascens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Mikania is commonly prepared as a tea, capsule, or tincture, and is used to treat a range of health issues, including digestive problems, fever, and respiratory infections.

classification

Mikania violascens is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Mikania violascens is native to Central and South America, where it grows in tropical rainforests and open areas. The plant is a climber or a shrub, using its long stems to support itself on other plants or nearby surfaces.

Tripsacum hirsutum

Tripsacum hirsutum, also known as Eastern Broom sedge or Broom sedge, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and analgesic properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Tripsacum hirsutum is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is also used in traditional folk medicine to treat conditions such as sore throats, coughs, and digestive issues.

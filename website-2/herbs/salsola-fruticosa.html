Salsola fruticosa

Salsola fruticosa, also known as Russian thistle or prickly saltwort, is a perennial halophytic shrub native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its prickly stems and leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, wounds, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties. Bioactive constituents of Salsola fruticosa include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is also used in topical applications, such as creams and ointments, to treat skin conditions and wounds.

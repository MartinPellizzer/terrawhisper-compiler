Vicia diversifolia

Vicia diversifolia, also known as the spreading vetch or multiflorous vetch, is an annual legume native to Europe and Western Asia, characterized by its spreading habit and clusters of small, white flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, as well as potential therapeutic applications in treating conditions such as digestive issues and respiratory problems. Bioactive constituents of Vicia diversifolia include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its pharmacological activities.

Traditionally, the plant is prepared as a herbal tea, tincture, or infusion, which are used to treat various health conditions, including coughs, colds, and gastrointestinal issues.

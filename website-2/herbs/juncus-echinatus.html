Juncus echinatus

Juncus echinatus, also known as prickly rush or prickly knotgrass, is a type of flowering plant that belongs to the family Juncaceae, characterized by its sharp, hair-like leaves and small, brownish-green flowers.

The bioactive constituents of Juncus echinatus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

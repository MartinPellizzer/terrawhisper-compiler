Uragoga emetica

Uragoga emetica, also known as the ipecacuanha or ipecac, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly in the tropical regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Peru.

Traditionally, the plant has been used for its emetic properties, helping to induce vomiting and relieve symptoms of colds, coughs, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Uragoga emetica include alkaloids such as emetine and cephaeline, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a tincture or syrup, and has been used to treat a range of conditions, including coughs, bronchitis, and pneumonia.

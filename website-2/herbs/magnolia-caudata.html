Magnolia caudata

Magnolia caudata, also known as Cinnamon Magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China, characterized by its distinctive, curved branches and showy white flowers.

Traditionally used in Chinese medicine, the bark of Magnolia caudata is valued for its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties, which have been reported to alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory infections, as well as provide relief from anxiety and stress. The bioactive constituents of Magnolia caudata include magnolol, honokiol, and lignans, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Magnolia caudata is commonly prepared as a decoction or tincture, and is often combined with other herbs, such as Glycyrrhiza and Astragalus, to create formulas for colds, flu, and other respiratory ailments.

Quercus obtusa

Quercus obtusa, also known as the evergreen Japanese oak or kinme, is a species of evergreen tree native to Japan.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including the reduction of inflammation and the improvement of cardiovascular health. The bioactive constituents of Quercus obtusa include flavonoids, such as quercetin, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

In traditional Japanese herbal medicine, Quercus obtusa is often prepared as a tea or a tincture, and is also used to make a topical cream or ointment for skin conditions such as eczema and acne.

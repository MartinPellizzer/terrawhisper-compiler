Melaleuca suberosa

Melaleuca suberosa, also known as the paperbark tea tree, is a plant species native to Western Australia, characterized by its distinctive paper-like bark and aromatic leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal activities. Bioactive constituents of Melaleuca suberosa include essential oils, particularly cineole, alpha-pinene, and limonene, which have been identified as responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and salves, often employed to treat respiratory issues, skin conditions, and wounds.

What is Conostylis aculeata subsp. bromelioides?

Conostylis aculeata subsp.

bromelioides is a rare subspecies of the Conostylis aculeata species, belonging to the family Haemodoraceae. It is a perennial herb with a caespitose growth habit, bearing linear to lanceolate leaves that are 2-10 mm long and 1-2 mm wide, and often covered with short, sharp, yellowish-brown hairs.

The inflorescence is a simple spike of small, white or greenish flowers, 4-6 mm long, with a prominent stamen and a 3-lobed perianth. The flowers are arranged in a spiral fashion along the spike, and the plant grows to a height of 10-30 cm.

The rhizome is woody, with leaf bases and flowers emerging from the apex.

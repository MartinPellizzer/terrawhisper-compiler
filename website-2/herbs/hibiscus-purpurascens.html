Hibiscus purpurascens

Hibiscus purpurascens, also known as the purple hibiscus, is a tropical plant species that belongs to the Malvaceae family, characterized by its vibrant purple flowers and edible calyces.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as reducing blood pressure, alleviating digestive issues, and acting as a natural diuretic. Bioactive constituents of Hibiscus purpurascens include anthocyanins, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its pharmacological activities.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant include teas, infusions, and extracts, which are often used to support cardiovascular health and as a natural remedy for various health conditions.

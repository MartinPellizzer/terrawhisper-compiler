Terminalia myrobalana

Terminalia myrobalana, also known as the Indian berry or amla, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia, belonging to the Combretaceae family.

The fruit of this plant has been widely used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which are believed to aid in digestion, boost immune function, and protect against age-related diseases. The bioactive constituents of Terminalia myrobalana include flavonoids, tannins, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In Ayurvedic medicine, Terminalia myrobalana is often used to make herbal preparations such as triphala, a polyherbal formulation, and amla oil, which are used to promote overall well-being and longevity.

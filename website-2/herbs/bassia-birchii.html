    The main therapeutic properties of Bassia birchii are anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antidiabetic.

Additionally, the antioxidant property of this plant is due to the presence of saponins, which can scavenge free radicals and protect against oxidative stress, thereby reducing the risk of chronic diseases such as cancer and neurodegenerative disorders. The antidiabetic property of Bassia birchii is attributed to the presence of volatile oils, which have been shown to stimulate insulin secretion and improve glucose uptake in skeletal muscle cells.

    The most important healing constituents of Bassia birchii are the triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

    The triterpenoid saponins, such as bassic acid and birchic acid, have been found to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory activities, contributing to the plant's analgesic and anti-arthritic effects. The flavonoids, including quercetin and kaempferol, have been reported to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-apoptotic activities, which may contribute to the plant's protective effects against oxidative stress and cell damage. The phenolic acids, including chlorogenic acid and caffeic acid, have been found to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, which may contribute to the plant's protective effects against cardiovascular disease and cancer.

    These constituents work together to provide Bassia birchii with its traditional uses in treating fever, rheumatism, and other inflammatory conditions.

    The main herbal preparations of Bassia birchii are infusion, decoction, tincture, tea, and birch leaf tea.

    Infusion is made by steeping the leaves in hot water to extract its flavonoids and phenolic acids, used to treat respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma. The tincture is prepared by mixing the leaves with ethanol to create a concentrated extract, used to treat various health conditions including fever and cough.

    Birch leaf tea is a traditional preparation made by infusing the leaves in hot water, used to treat conditions such as eczema and dermatitis.

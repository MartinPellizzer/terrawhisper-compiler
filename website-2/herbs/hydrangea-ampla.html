Hydrangea ampla

Hydrangea ampla, also known as bigleaf hydrangea, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Asia, characterized by its large, showy flowers and broad, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may aid in soothing digestive issues and supporting immune function. Bioactive constituents of Hydrangea ampla include triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Hydrangea ampla is commonly used in herbal preparations, such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, to support digestive health and reduce inflammation.

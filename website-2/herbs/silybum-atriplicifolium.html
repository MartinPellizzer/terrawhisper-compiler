Silybum atriplicifolium

Silybum atriplicifolium, also known as cardus marianus or milk thistle, is a biennial plant that belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant has been traditionally used for its hepatoprotective properties, and its health benefits include reducing liver inflammation and promoting liver regeneration, as well as supporting the treatment of various liver diseases such as hepatitis and cirrhosis. The bioactive constituents of Silybum atriplicifolium include silymarin, a flavonoid complex that is responsible for the plant's medicinal properties, as well as other flavonoids, polyphenols, and volatile compounds.

The most common herbal preparations made with Silybum atriplicifolium include capsules, tablets, and tinctures, often standardized to contain a specific percentage of silymarin.

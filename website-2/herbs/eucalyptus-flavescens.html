Eucalyptus flavescens

Eucalyptus flavescens, also known as Lemon-scented Gum, is a species of evergreen tree native to Australia, characterized by its aromatic leaves and distinctive lemon-like fragrance.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties, which can help to alleviate respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of E. flavescens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

E. flavescens is commonly used in herbal preparations, including essential oils, capsules, and teas, often used to relieve respiratory congestion and support immune function.

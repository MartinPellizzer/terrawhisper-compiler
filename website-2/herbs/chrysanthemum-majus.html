Chrysanthemum majus

Chrysanthemum majus, commonly known as the large-flowered chrysanthemum, is an herbal plant in the daisy family (Asteraceae) that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiviral properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms associated with colds, fever, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Chrysanthemum majus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Chrysanthemum majus is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into various herbal formulations, such as cold and fever remedies.

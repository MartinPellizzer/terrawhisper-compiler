What is Campanula stevenii subsp. alberti?

Campanula stevenii subsp.

alberti is a plant belonging to the family Campanulaceae, native to the Caucasus region. It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright stems, reaching 10-20 cm in height, with narrow, lanceolate leaves, 2-5 cm long, and 0.5-1.5 cm wide, that are pubescent on the underside.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers, 0.8-1.2 cm in diameter, in a bell-shaped inflorescence, with a campanulate calyx and a corolla with five unequal lobes. The flowers are produced in a terminal, spike-like raceme, and are in bloom from June to August.

The plant grows in rocky, mountainous areas, and is typically found in association with grasslands and alpine meadows.

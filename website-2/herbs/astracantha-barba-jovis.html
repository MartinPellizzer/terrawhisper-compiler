Astracantha barba-jovis

Astracantha barba-jovis, also known as beardless milk thistle, is a thorny shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia, belonging to the Fabaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine due to its potential health benefits, which include protecting the liver and reducing inflammation. The bioactive constituents of Astracantha barba-jovis include flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include tinctures, capsules, and teas, often used to support liver health and alleviate digestive issues.

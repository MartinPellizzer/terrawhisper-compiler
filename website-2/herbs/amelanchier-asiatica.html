Amelanchier asiatica

Amelanchier asiatica, also known as Korean juneberry or Asian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia, belonging to the Rosaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in treating various ailments such as fever, cough, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Amelanchier asiatica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and anthocyanins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Amelanchier asiatica is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or syrup, and is often used to treat respiratory and gastrointestinal issues.

Bourreria cumanensis

Bourreria cumanensis, also known as Cumaru or Ucuuba, is a medicinal plant native to the tropical regions of South America, belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, including the unique compound coumarin, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Bourreria cumanensis is traditionally used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for its wound-healing and skin-soothing properties.

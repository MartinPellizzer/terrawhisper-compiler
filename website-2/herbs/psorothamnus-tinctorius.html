Psorothamnus tinctorius

Psorothamnus tinctorius, also known as the indigo bush or creosote bush, is a shrubby, perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, producing vibrant purple flowers and deep blue-green foliage.

The plant has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, offering various health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in wound healing, skin conditions, and digestive issues. Bioactive constituents of Psorothamnus tinctorius include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being used in topical applications for skin conditions and wounds.

Ocotea lancifolia

Ocotea lancifolia, also known as Lancifolia Ocotea, is a species of evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

The essential oil extracted from Ocotea lancifolia has been traditionally used for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, as well as its potential to lower blood pressure and alleviate respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Ocotea lancifolia include sesquiterpenes, including caryophyllene oxide, and other compounds such as ursolic acid and quercetin.

The most common herbal preparations made with Ocotea lancifolia include essential oils, capsules, and teas, which can be used to promote relaxation, reduce stress, and support overall well-being.

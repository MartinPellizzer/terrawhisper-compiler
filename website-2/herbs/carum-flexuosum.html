Carum flexuosum

Carum flexuosum, also known as Ajwain or Bishop's Weed, is a perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is native to the Indian subcontinent.

The health benefits of Carum flexuosum include its use as a digestive aid, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial agent, making it a popular remedy for treating digestive issues, such as flatulence, indigestion, and diarrhea. The bioactive constituents of Carum flexuosum include compounds like thymol, carvone, limonene, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Carum flexuosum include teas, infusions, and dried extracts, often used to alleviate digestive discomfort and promote overall well-being.

Anchusa tinctoria

Anchusa tinctoria, also known as Italian bugloss or blue thistle, is a perennial plant species native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its vibrant blue flowers and edible leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its ability to aid in digestion, reduce inflammation, and alleviate respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Anchusa tinctoria include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to support overall health and well-being.

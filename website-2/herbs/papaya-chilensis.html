Papaya chilensis

Papaya chilensis, also known as Chilean pawpaw, is a plant species within the Papaya genus, characterized by its unique, small, and edible fruits.

The health benefits of Papaya chilensis include its potential to support digestive health, exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, and contribute to the management of various diseases, including diabetes and cardiovascular conditions. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal and therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Papaya chilensis include tea, capsules, and extracts, which are used to support immune function, alleviate digestive issues, and promote overall well-being.

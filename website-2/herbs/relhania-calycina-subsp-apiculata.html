What is Relhania calycina subsp. apiculata?

Relhania calycina subsp.

apiculata is an infraspecific taxon within the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its shrubby habit, typically growing up to 1 meter in height, with leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, 2-6 cm long, and 0.5-1.5 cm wide, with a serrated margin.

The flowers are yellow, daisy-like, with a diameter of 3-4 cm, and are arranged in corymbose inflorescences. The fruit is an achene, 3-4 mm long, with a pappus of 10-12 bristles.

It is endemic to the southwestern part of South Africa.

Malva multiflora

Malva multiflora, also known as the mallow plant, is a perennial herb belonging to the Malvaceae family, characterized by its large, showy flowers and mucilaginous properties.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help soothe digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and support immune function. The bioactive constituents of Malva multiflora include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as topical creams and ointments, which are traditionally used to treat skin irritations and wounds.

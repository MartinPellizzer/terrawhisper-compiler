Aster indamellus

Aster indamellus, also known as Pyrethrum or Pyrethrum Daisy, is a flowering plant in the daisy family (Asteraceae) native to Europe and western Asia.

The plant is valued for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and insecticidal properties, making it useful for treating skin conditions and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Aster indamellus include pyrethrins, a group of compounds responsible for its insecticidal activity, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

In herbal medicine, Aster indamellus is commonly prepared as a tincture, infusion, or topical cream, and is used to treat skin conditions such as eczema, acne, and wounds, as well as to repel insects and alleviate pain.

Bursera graveolens

Bursera graveolens, commonly known as Palo Santo, is a small tree native to the coastal regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

This aromatic tree is valued for its medicinal and spiritual properties, and its essential oil has been used to promote relaxation, reduce stress and anxiety, and alleviate respiratory issues such as bronchitis and coughs, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Palo Santo include sesquiterpenes, such as beta-caryophyllene and alpha-pinene, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Palo Santo is often used to make incense sticks, essential oils, and tinctures, which are used to promote well-being, reduce inflammation, and protect against infections.

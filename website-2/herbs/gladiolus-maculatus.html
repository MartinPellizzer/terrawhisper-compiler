Gladiolus maculatus

Gladiolus maculatus, also known as the spotted gladiolus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family, characterized by its vibrant flowers and spotted foliage.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as wounds and infections. The bioactive constituents of Gladiolus maculatus include flavonoids, saponins, and anthraquinones, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Gladiolus maculatus is often used in traditional medicine and is sometimes prepared as a tincture or extract, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for its wound-healing and antimicrobial effects.

Pulsatilla pratensis

Pulsatilla pratensis, commonly known as meadow anemone or windflower, is a perennial plant species in the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae) native to Europe and western Asia.

The plant is valued for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which may help to soothe skin irritations and wounds, reduce fever, and ease respiratory issues such as bronchitis and coughs. Bioactive constituents of Pulsatilla pratensis include ranunculin, pulsatilin, and alkaloids like anemonin and pulsatilin, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Pulsatilla pratensis is often prepared as a tincture, infusion, or dried herb, and is traditionally used to treat a range of conditions, including menstrual cramps, anxiety, and insomnia.

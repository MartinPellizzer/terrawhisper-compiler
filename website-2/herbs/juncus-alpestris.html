Juncus alpestris

Juncus alpestris, also known as Alpine Rush or Alpine Jointgrass, is a perennial aquatic plant species belonging to the family Juncaceae, characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, greenish-brown flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering various health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which can aid in treating digestive issues, skin irritations, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Juncus alpestris include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Juncus alpestris is commonly prepared as a tincture, tea, or infusion, and is also used in the production of salves and creams for topical application.

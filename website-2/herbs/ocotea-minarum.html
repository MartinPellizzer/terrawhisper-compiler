Ocotea minarum

Ocotea minarum, also known as Minas Gerais ocotea, is a plant species belonging to the Lamiaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which have been attributed to its ability to help manage conditions such as arthritis, digestive issues, and skin problems. The bioactive constituents of Ocotea minarum include diterpenes, sesquiterpenes, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its therapeutic activities.

In traditional medicine, Ocotea minarum is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and oils for skin and wound care applications.

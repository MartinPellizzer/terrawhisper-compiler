Juniperus glauca

Juniperus glauca, also known as Japanese juniper, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to East Asia, characterized by its conical shape and silvery-blue foliage.

The health benefits of Juniperus glauca include its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which have been traditionally used to treat digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, such as alpha-pinene and beta-pinene, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Juniperus glauca is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and essential oils, which are often used to alleviate colds, coughs, and flu symptoms, as well as to promote digestion and relieve menstrual cramps.

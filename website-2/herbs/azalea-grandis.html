Azalea grandis

Azalea grandis, also known as the mountain azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family, Ericaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in medicine due to its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it useful for treating respiratory issues, wound healing, and reducing stress. The bioactive constituents of Azalea grandis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Azalea grandis is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into traditional remedies, such as cough syrups and topical creams, to take advantage of its medicinal properties.

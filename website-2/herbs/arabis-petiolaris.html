Arabis petiolaris

Arabis petiolaris, also known as rock cress or crevice rock cress, is a perennial plant species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, characterized by its delicate white flowers and hairy, succulent leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine due to its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in the treatment of respiratory issues, wounds, and skin conditions. Arabis petiolaris contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, as well as to soothe skin irritations and promote wound healing.

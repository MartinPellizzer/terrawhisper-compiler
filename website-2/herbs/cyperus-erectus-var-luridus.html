What is Cyperus erectus var. luridus?

Cyperus erectus var. luridus is a variant of the plant species Cyperus erectus, belonging to the family Cyperaceae, a group of flowering plants commonly known as sedge family.

It is characterized by its tufted habit, with flat, linear, and usually bright green, 3-10 mm long leaves, often with a dark green midrib. The plant typically produces a single stem, 10-30 cm tall, bearing a dense, dark brown, ovoid to ellipsoidal spike, 5-15 mm long, with 3-6 female flowers and 1-2 male flowers. The spike is terminal, and the female flowers are surrounded by a pair of bracts, while the male flowers are solitary.

The plant is often found in wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and shallow water, in tropical and subtropical regions.

Attalea × camopiensis

Attalea × camopiensis, also known as the Camopi Palm, is a plant species native to the Amazon rainforest.

The Camopi Palm has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with the Camopi Palm are infusions, decoctions, and extracts, which are used to treat a range of ailments and promote overall well-being.

classification

Attalea × camopiensis is classified in the family Arecaceae, commonly known as the palm family. This classification is based on its morphological characteristics and phylogenetic analysis. As a hybrid of Attalea speciosa and Attalea maripa, its classification is also influenced by its parent species. The genus Attalea itself is classified in the subfamily Arecoideae, within the palm family Arecaceae. Attalea × camopiensis is also known to be native to French Guiana and other parts of the Guiana Shield region.

What is Huperzia linifolia var. tenuifolia?

Huperzia linifolia var. tenuifolia is a variety of the species Huperzia linifolia, belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small to medium-sized, evergreen, terrestrial plant with simple, linear to lanceolate leaves that are 2-10 mm long, bearing a narrow, lanceolate to ovate sterile leaf at the apex. The leaves are dark green and glossy, with entire margins. The stems are slender and brown, bearing numerous whorls of leaves, and are up to 30 cm long.

Reproduction occurs via sporangia, which are borne in whorls on the upper part of the stems.

The most important healing constituents of Huperzia linifolia var. tenuifolia are a group of potent neuroprotective and neuroregenerative compounds known as huperzines.

Huperzine B, C, D, and E have also been isolated, showing varying degrees of acetylcholinesterase inhibition and neuroprotective activity, with Huperzine B and D demonstrating significant antioxidant properties. Huperzines' ability to cross the blood-brain barrier and selectively inhibit acetylcholinesterase make them effective in treating neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's.

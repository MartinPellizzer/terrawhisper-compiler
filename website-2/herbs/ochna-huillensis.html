Ochna huillensis

Ochna huillensis, also known as the "Mugunthi" or "Muhunthi" tree, is a plant species belonging to the family Ochnaceae, native to tropical Africa.

The bark, roots, and leaves of Ochna huillensis have been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, malaria, and respiratory infections, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, tannins, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ochna huillensis is often prepared as a decoction, infusion, or powdered extract, which is used to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and cold, as well as to promote digestive health.

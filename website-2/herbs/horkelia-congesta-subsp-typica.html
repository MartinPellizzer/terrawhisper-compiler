What is Horkelia congesta subsp. typica?

Horkelia congesta subsp.

typica is a plant species endemic to California, belonging to the family Rosaceae. It is an annual herb that grows up to 30 cm in height, with a rosette of leaves and a solitary stem.

The leaves are lanceolate to elliptic in shape, 2-5 cm long, and have a ciliate margin. The inflorescence is a corymb with 3-5 white to pinkish flowers, each 8-12 mm in diameter, and a 5-lobed calyx.

The fruit is a small pome, 5-7 mm in diameter, with 5-10 seeds.

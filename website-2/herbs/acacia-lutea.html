Acacia lutea

Acacia lutea, also known as golden wattle, is a small, deciduous shrub native to eastern Australia, belonging to the legume family (Fabaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including digestive problems, respiratory infections, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Acacia lutea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and condensed tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Acacia lutea is often used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for skin care and wound healing.

Desmodium adscendens

Desmodium adscendens, also known as green leaf desmodium or green leaf cowpea, is a perennial legume native to Africa and Asia, characterized by its green, trifoliate leaves and pink or purple flowers.

Traditionally, the plant has been used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antispasmodic properties. The bioactive constituents of Desmodium adscendens include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Desmodium adscendens is commonly prepared as a tea, decoction, or infusion, and is also used in the production of herbal extracts and tinctures.

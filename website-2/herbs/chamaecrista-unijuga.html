Chamaecrista unijuga

Chamaecrista unijuga, also known as the one-seeded sensitive plant, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, characterized by its unique ability to fold its leaves in response to touch and temperature changes.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in the treatment of various ailments such as fever, digestive issues, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Chamaecrista unijuga include flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments to leverage its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

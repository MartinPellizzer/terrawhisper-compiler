Rhus himalaica

Rhus himalaica, also known as the Himalayan sumac, is a plant species belonging to the Anacardiaceae family, characterized by its small white flowers and reddish-purple berries.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which are said to aid in treating skin conditions, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Rhus himalaica include flavonoids, tannins, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared into decoctions, infusions, and ointments to utilize its therapeutic benefits.

Amaranthus tuberculatus

Amaranthus tuberculatus, also known as rough amaranth or prairie amaranth, is a perennial plant species in the amaranth family that is native to North America.

The bioactive constituents of Amaranthus tuberculatus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

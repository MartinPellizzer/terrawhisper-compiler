Papaver × harleyanum

Papaver × harleyanum, also known as the opium poppy, is a semi-synthetic, interspecific hybrid between Papaver somniferum and Papaver setiferum, cultivated for its medicinal properties.

The plant is rich in health benefits, including analgesic and antispasmodic effects, due to its ability to provide relief from pain and muscle cramps. The bioactive constituents of Papaver × harleyanum include alkaloids, such as morphine, codeine, and thebaine, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Herbal preparations made from this plant include opium tinctures and extracts, often used in traditional medicine to treat pain and insomnia.

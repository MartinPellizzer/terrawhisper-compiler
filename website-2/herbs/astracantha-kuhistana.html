Astracantha kuhistana

Astracantha kuhistana, also known as Kuhistan's milkvetch, is a perennial legume species native to Central Asia, characterized by its yellow flowers and dense, hairy foliage.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory effects, which may aid in treating various conditions, including arthritis, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Astracantha kuhistana include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is also used in the production of herbal teas, capsules, and tinctures.

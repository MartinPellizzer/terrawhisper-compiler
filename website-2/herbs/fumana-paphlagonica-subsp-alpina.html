What is Fumana paphlagonica subsp. alpina?

Fumana paphlagonica subsp.

alpina is an endemic subspecies of the plant Fumana paphlagonica, belonging to the family Cistaceae. It is characterized by its small, annual to biennial herbs, with grayish to white hairs on the stem and leaves, and yellow, 4-6-merous flowers in a terminal corymb.

The leaves are lanceolate, 2-5 cm long, with a glabrous or sparsely hairy surface, and a glandular margin. Fumana paphlagonica subsp. alpina is typically found growing in rocky areas, grasslands, and scrublands at high elevations, from 1,200 to 2,500 meters above sea level.

This subspecies is primarily found in the mountainous regions of Turkey.

Vinca major variegata

Vinca major variegata, also known as Periwinkle, is a flowering evergreen vine native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its attractive variegated leaves and blue-purple flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including its ability to improve blood circulation and lower blood pressure, as well as its potential to treat conditions such as diabetes, arthritis, and certain types of cancer. The bioactive constituents of Vinca major variegata include alkaloids such as vincamine, vincristine, and vinblastine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Vinca major variegata is often prepared as a tincture, tea, or extract, and is used to treat a range of health issues, although its use is not without controversy due to the potential toxicity of its alkaloid constituents.

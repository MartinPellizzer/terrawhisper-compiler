Sclarea candidissima

Sclarea candidissima, also known as Roman chamomile, is an annual herbaceous plant that belongs to the Asteraceae family.

It is valued for its calming properties, making it a popular natural remedy for anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness, as well as its anti-inflammatory and soothing effects on the skin. The bioactive constituents of Sclarea candidissima include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and apigenin, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Roman chamomile include teas, essential oils, and topical creams, which are often used to promote relaxation, reduce stress, and soothe skin irritations.

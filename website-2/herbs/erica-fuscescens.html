Erica fuscescens

Erica fuscescens, also known as the Red Heath or Cross-leaved Heath, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the heathlands of Europe.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help to soothe digestive issues, reduce stress, and protect against infections. The bioactive constituents of Erica fuscescens include phenolic acids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Erica fuscescens is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is also used in combination with other herbs to make tonics and supplements for promoting overall health and well-being.

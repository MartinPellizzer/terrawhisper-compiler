Scleria brownii

Scleria brownii, also known as Brown's nut sedge, is a type of perennial plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae) native to tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects that can aid in wound healing, reduce fever, and alleviate respiratory issues such as bronchitis and pneumonia. The bioactive constituents of Scleria brownii include flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional medicine, Scleria brownii is commonly used to prepare teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into salves and ointments to utilize its topical healing benefits.

Humboltia acutissima

Humboldtia acutissima, also known as the "Indian Poison Nut," is a plant species native to the Western Ghats region of India, belonging to the family Menispermaceae.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, which include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities, making it useful in the treatment of various health conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Humboldtia acutissima include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds, particularly the lignans and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, the plant is often used to make herbal preparations, including decoctions and infusions, and is also used in the form of powders and extracts to treat various health ailments.

Briza poiformis

Briza poiformis, also known as Blue Quaking Grass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its delicate blue-green foliage and vibrant, quaking seed heads.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which are said to help soothe respiratory issues and protect against cell damage. The bioactive constituents of Briza poiformis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Briza poiformis include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat coughs, colds, and other respiratory ailments.

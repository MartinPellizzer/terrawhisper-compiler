Magnolia fordiana

Magnolia fordiana, also known as the Bornean Magnolia or Uluwau Magnolia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Magnoliaceae.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help to treat various conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Magnolia fordiana include magnolol, honokiol, and 4-O-methylhonokiol, among other compounds, which have been shown to possess various pharmacological activities.

In traditional herbal medicine, Magnolia fordiana is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into formulations such as capsules and tablets, to leverage its potential therapeutic benefits.

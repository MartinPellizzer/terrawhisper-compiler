Ambrosia chamissonis

Ambrosia chamissonis, also known as Chamisso's ragweed or sand pucker, is a perennial herbaceous plant in the daisy family (Asteraceae) native to the Pacific coast of North America.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, which provide relief from various health issues such as fever, colds, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Ambrosia chamissonis include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat a range of ailments, including skin irritations, wounds, and digestive issues.

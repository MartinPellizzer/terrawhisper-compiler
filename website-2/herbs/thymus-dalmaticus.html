Thymus dalmaticus

Thymus dalmaticus, commonly known as Dalmatian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and pink to purple flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which may help to soothe digestive issues, reduce stress, and protect against oxidative damage. The bioactive constituents of Dalmatian thyme include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, particularly thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Dalmatian thyme is used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as in topical applications like creams and ointments to promote wound healing and reduce muscle tension.

Jasminum multiflorum

Jasminum multiflorum, commonly known as the star jasmine or many-flowered jasmine, is a climbing evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas and southern China, characterized by its star-shaped flowers and strong, sweet fragrance.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its numerous health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been shown to aid in the treatment of various ailments such as fever, digestive issues, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Jasminum multiflorum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Jasminum multiflorum is often used to make teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into various Ayurvedic formulations, such as ghee and oil preparations, to promote overall well-being and health.

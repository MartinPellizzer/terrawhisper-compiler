Galipea lucida

Galipea lucida, also known as "Kawakawa," is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly in Fiji and Samoa, belonging to the Rutaceae family.

The leaves of Galipea lucida have been traditionally used in folk medicine for their purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in the treatment of various ailments such as fever, skin infections, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Galipea lucida include flavonoids, alkaloids, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Samoan medicine, the leaves of Galipea lucida are commonly made into teas, infusions, and tonics, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for skin conditions.

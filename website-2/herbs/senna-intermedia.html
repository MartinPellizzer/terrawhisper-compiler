Senna intermedia

Senna intermedia, also known as Chilean senna, is a shrub-like plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its clusters of small, yellow flowers and seed pods.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its laxative properties, with health benefits including the treatment of constipation, digestive issues, and as a gentle purgative. The bioactive constituents of Senna intermedia include anthraquinone glycosides, such as sennosides A and B, which are responsible for its laxative effects.

Senna intermedia is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, capsules, and extracts, often marketed for their natural laxative properties and digestive support.

classification

Senna intermedia is classified within the Legume family (Fabaceae) and belongs to the subfamily Caesalpinioideae. It is a member of the genus Senna, which comprises approximately 250 species of flowering plants. The genus Senna is further divided into several subgenera, and Senna intermedia is part of the subgenus Chamaesenna. Senna intermedia is native to tropical regions and is often found in dry or well-drained areas. The classification of Senna intermedia is also supported by its characteristic bean-like pods and pinnately compound leaves.

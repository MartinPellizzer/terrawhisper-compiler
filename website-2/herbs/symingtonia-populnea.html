Symingtonia populnea

Symingtonia populnea, also known as Pau'au, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands, particularly in Hawaii and Tahiti.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, providing relief from various health issues such as fever, cough, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Symingtonia populnea include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is commonly used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being smoked or applied topically to the skin to harness its medicinal benefits.

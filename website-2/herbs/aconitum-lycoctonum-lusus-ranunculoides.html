Aconitum lycoctonum lusus ranunculoides

Aconitum lycoctonum lusus ranunculoides, commonly known as Monk's Hood or Aconitum, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Aconitum genus, characterized by its unique hood-like flowers and poisonous properties.

Traditionally, Monk's Hood has been used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including pain, fever, and rheumatism, due to its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of Aconitum include aconitine, aconitidine, and delphinine, which are responsible for its medicinal effects, although caution must be exercised due to the plant's toxicity.

Monk's Hood is often used to prepare herbal tinctures, infusions, and ointments, which are utilized in traditional medicine, although modern medical practices advise against its use due to the risk of overdose and toxicity.

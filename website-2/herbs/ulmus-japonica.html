Ulmus japonica

Ulmus japonica, also known as the Japanese elm or Japanese white elm, is a deciduous tree species native to East Asia, characterized by its broad, vase-shaped crown and serrated, green leaves.

The bark and leaves of Ulmus japonica have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Ulmus japonica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ulmus japonica is often prepared as a decoction or tincture, and may be used to make teas, infusions, or topical creams to harness its therapeutic benefits.

classification

Ulmus japonica is classified within the family Ulmaceae. This family includes various other species of elm trees, such as the white elm (Ulmus americana) and the slippery elm (Ulmus rubra). Ulmus japonica is a member of the genus Ulmus, which comprises about 40 species of elm trees. The genus Ulmus is further classified within the order Rosales, which includes other plants such as roses and currants. The order Rosales is a part of the larger clade of core eudicots.

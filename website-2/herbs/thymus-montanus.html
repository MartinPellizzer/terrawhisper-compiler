Thymus montanus

Thymus montanus, also known as mountain thyme, is a perennial shrub belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and native to the Mediterranean region.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with health benefits including antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to soothe sore throats, reduce respiratory issues, and support immune function. The bioactive constituents of Thymus montanus include thymol, carvacrol, and rosmarinic acid, among other volatile oils and phenolic compounds that contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, Thymus montanus is commonly used to prepare teas, infusions, and essential oils, which can be used to treat respiratory problems, such as bronchitis and coughs, as well as to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

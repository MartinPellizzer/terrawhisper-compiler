Ilex ovalifolia

Ilex ovalifolia, also known as the Japanese holly or oval-leaf holly, is a species of flowering plant in the holly family (Aquifoliaceae), characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and red berries.

The health benefits of Ilex ovalifolia include its potential to act as an antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial agent, which may help to protect against cell damage, reduce inflammation, and prevent infections. The bioactive constituents of Ilex ovalifolia include flavonoids, such as quercetin and isorhapontigenin, as well as iridoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ilex ovalifolia is commonly prepared into teas, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat various conditions, including fever, cough, and digestive issues.

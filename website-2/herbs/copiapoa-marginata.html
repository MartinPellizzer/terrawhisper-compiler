Copiapoa marginata

Copiapoa marginata, also known as the orange ball cactus, is a species of succulent plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is used in traditional medicine, particularly in South American folk remedies, where it is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may aid in reducing inflammation and protecting against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Copiapoa marginata include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Copiapoa marginata is commonly prepared as a powder or tincture, and is also used in the production of creams and ointments to treat skin conditions such as wounds, burns, and eczema.

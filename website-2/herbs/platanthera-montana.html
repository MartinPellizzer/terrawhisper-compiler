Platanthera montana

Platanthera montana, also known as the alpine bog orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of North America.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including its ability to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of P. montana include iridoid glycosides, phenolic compounds, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with P. montana are teas and tinctures, often used to alleviate respiratory symptoms and promote overall well-being.

What is Salsola vermiculata var. portilloi?

Salsola vermiculata var. portilloi is a plant variety within the species Salsola vermiculata, belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is an annual or biennial herb, characterized by its erect stem, reaching up to 100 cm in height, with linear, lanceolate leaves, and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in axillary spikes. The leaves are typically 2-8 cm long, glabrous, and have an entire margin. This variety is often found in dry environments, such as coastal areas and salt flats, where it can tolerate high levels of salinity and drought.

Salsola vermiculata var. portilloi is native to the Mediterranean region and other parts of Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia.

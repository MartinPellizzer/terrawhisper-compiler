Ceropegia incana

Ceropegia incana, also known as the "woolly rosette" or "hairy rope cactus," is a succulent plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, which include the treatment of fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal issues, as well as its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. Bioactive constituents of Ceropegia incana include alkaloids, glycosides, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Schoenus palustris

Schoenus palustris, also known as marsh club-rush or woolly club-rush, is a type of flowering plant that belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties, which can help to treat wounds, reduce fever, and alleviate digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Schoenus palustris include phenolic acids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Schoenus palustris is commonly prepared as a decoction or tincture, and it is often used to treat respiratory problems, such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as to soothe skin irritations and promote wound healing.

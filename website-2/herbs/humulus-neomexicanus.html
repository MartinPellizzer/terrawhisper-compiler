Humulus neomexicanus

Humulus neomexicanus, also known as New Mexico hop, is a perennial plant species native to the southwestern United States, belonging to the Cannabaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, which may aid in reducing stress and anxiety, and supporting immune function. The bioactive constituents of Humulus neomexicanus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, including humulones and lupulones, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as beer and hop-based products, capitalizing on its bitter and aromatic compounds.

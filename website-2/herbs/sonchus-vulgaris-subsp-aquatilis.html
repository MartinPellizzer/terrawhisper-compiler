What is Sonchus vulgaris subsp. aquatilis?

Sonchus vulgaris subsp.

aquatilis is a subspecies of the plant Sonchus vulgaris, belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is a perennial herb with rosettes of leaves, which are lanceolate, 10-30 cm long and 2-6 cm wide, with entire or undulate margins and a glabrous or hairy lower surface.

The leaves are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces yellow flowerheads, 3-8 cm in diameter, in a terminal inflorescence, composed of 8-15 florets, with a prominent receptacle and no involucre.

The achenes are 5-7 mm long and have a pappus of 20-30 bristles, 1-2 mm long.

In general, the plant's anti-inflammatory properties make it a valuable remedy for various health conditions.

What are the therapeutic properties this herb?

The main therapeutic properties of Sonchus vulgaris subsp. aquatilis are anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial, with the latter being particularly effective against a range of bacteria, including Escherichia coli and Staphylococcus aureus, due to the presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and saponins, which are known to exhibit antioxidant properties, thus contributing to the plant's hepatoprotective effects, while its immunomodulatory properties also make it useful in the management of allergic reactions, and its diuretic properties allow it to be used in the treatment of urinary tract infections.

What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

The most important healing constituents of Sonchus vulgaris subsp.

aquatilis are flavonoids such as apigenin and luteolin, sesquiterpenoids like sonchus yellow, and sesquiterpene lactones including alantolactone and inulin derivatives. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory effects, which contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties. Sonchus yellow has shown significant antimicrobial activity, while alantolactone demonstrates hepatoprotective effects and antioxidant activity.

Inulin, a major storage carbohydrate in Sonchus vulgaris subsp. aquatilis, has prebiotic properties, supporting gut health and immune system function. The combination of these bioactive compounds in Sonchus vulgaris subsp.

aquatilis makes it a valuable resource for traditional medicine.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Sonchus vulgaris subsp.

aquatilis are infusion, tincture, sonchus tea, salve, and decoction. Infusion is a liquid extract made by steeping the leaves and stems in hot water, while tincture is a concentrated liquid solution made by soaking the plant material in a solvent like ethanol or glycerin.

Sonchus tea is prepared similarly to infusion but is often used to treat digestive issues. Salve is a topical preparation made by infusing the plant material in a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil to create a medicated cream for skin issues.

Decoction is a more intense liquid extract made by boiling the plant material in water for an extended period, often used to treat infections and wounds.

What are the possible side effects of using this herb improperly?

The possible side effects of using Sonchus vulgaris subsp.

aquatilis improperly include causing diarrhea due to its high content of sesquiterpene lactones, which can irritate the gastrointestinal tract, leading to loose and watery stools. Furthermore, it can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals, particularly those with sensitive skin, due to the presence of sapogenins and other compounds that can cause inflammation and redness. Ingestion of Sonchus vulgaris subsp.

aquatilis can also induce nausea and vomiting as the body reacts to the toxic compounds, leading to stomach cramps and discomfort. The improper use of Sonchus vulgaris subsp. aquatilis can also lead to more severe allergic reactions, such as anaphylaxis, which is a life-threatening condition that requires immediate medical attention.

Prolonged exposure to the plant's toxic compounds can cause long-term health issues, including liver and kidney damage.

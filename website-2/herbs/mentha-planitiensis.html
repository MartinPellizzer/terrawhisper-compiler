Mentha planitiensis

Mentha planitiensis, also known as Japanese peppermint or Japanese mint, is a species of mint native to East Asia, characterized by its small, purple flowers and distinctive minty aroma.

The plant has been traditionally used for its health benefits, which include digestive aid, relief from nausea and headaches, and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Mentha planitiensis include menthone, menthol, and menthyl acetate, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Mentha planitiensis is often used to make teas, infusions, and essential oils, and is also used in the preparation of traditional Japanese remedies, such as kampo medicine.

Begonia atricha

The Begonia atricha is a species of flowering plant in the Begoniaceae family, native to the tropical forests of the Philippines.

Traditionally, Begonia atricha has been used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its potential health benefits, such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Begonia atricha include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic compounds, which are thought to contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Begonia atricha is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is also used in the form of tinctures and capsules to take advantage of its medicinal properties.

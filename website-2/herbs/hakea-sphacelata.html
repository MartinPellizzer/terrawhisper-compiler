Hakea sphacelata

Hakea sphacelata, also known as the scrub hakea or bushy hakea, is a species of shrub native to Western Australia, characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers and edible fruits.

The plant has been traditionally used in Indigenous Australian medicine, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in the treatment of wounds and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Hakea sphacelata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Hakea sphacelata is often used to make teas, infusions, and poultices, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including sore throats, colds, and skin irritations.

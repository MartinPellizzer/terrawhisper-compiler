Eugenia neoriedeliana

Eugenia neoriedeliana, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family (Myrtaceae) that is native to Brazil.

The plant has been reported to possess various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help to protect against diseases such as diabetes, hypertension, and certain types of cancer. The bioactive constituents of E. neoriedeliana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the leaves and fruits of E. neoriedeliana are often used to make teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments to treat skin conditions such as wounds and eczema.

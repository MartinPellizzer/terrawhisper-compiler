Bacopa neuwiedii

Bacopa neuwiedii, also known as Bacopa monnieri, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to enhance cognitive function and memory.

The bioactive constituents of Bacopa neuwiedii include bacosides, which are a group of saponin glycosides responsible for the plant's cognitive-enhancing effects, as well as other compounds such as alkaloids and flavonoids.

Bacopa neuwiedii is commonly prepared as a capsule or tablet supplement, as well as a liquid extract, and is often used in combination with other herbs to enhance its cognitive and nootropic effects.

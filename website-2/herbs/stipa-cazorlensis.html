Stipa cazorlensis

Stipa cazorlensis, also known as Cazorla Feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiseptic effects, which can aid in wound healing and alleviate respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Stipa cazorlensis include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Stipa cazorlensis is typically used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, and is also sometimes incorporated into topical creams and ointments for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

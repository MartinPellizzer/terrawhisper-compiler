What is Gentiana helophila subsp. dolichocalyx?

Gentiana helophila subsp.

dolichocalyx is a subspecies of perennial herbaceous plant endemic to the mountainous regions of Greece and Turkey. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, characterized by its erect habit, glabrous stems, and ovate, cordate leaves that are 2-5 cm long and 1-3 cm wide, with cuneate base and rounded apex.

The plant produces axillary, racemose inflorescences bearing blue flowers with a corolla 3-4 cm long and 1-2 cm wide, having 5-6 lobes, and 4-5 stamens with yellow anthers. The plant grows in moist, shaded areas at elevations between 1,200 and 2,500 meters above sea level. The subspecies is morphologically similar to Gentiana helophila subsp.

helophila but differs in its longer corolla and slightly smaller leaves.

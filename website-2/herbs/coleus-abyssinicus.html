Coleus abyssinicus

Coleus abyssinicus, also known as African mint or Coleus, is a perennial plant native to tropical Africa, belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antifungal, and antibacterial effects, making it beneficial in the treatment of various health issues such as fever, skin infections, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Coleus abyssinicus include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Coleus abyssinicus include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to alleviate symptoms of fever, colds, and digestive issues.

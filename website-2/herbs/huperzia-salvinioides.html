Huperzia salvinioides

Huperzia salvinioides, also known as Huperzia, is a species of liverwort, a non-vascular, thallose fern-like plant.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its ability to improve cognitive function, enhance memory, and potentially treat neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's, due to its high content of huperzine A, a potent inhibitor of acetylcholinesterase. The bioactive constituents of Huperzia salvinioides include huperzine A, as well as other alkaloids and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Huperzia has been used in herbal preparations, including capsules, tablets, and extracts, which are often used to support brain health and cognitive function, although its use should be supervised by a healthcare professional due to potential side effects.

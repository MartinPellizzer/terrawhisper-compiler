Pachira cearensis, commonly known as the Lapacho or Brazilwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the subtropical regions of South America.

The bioactive constituents of Pachira cearensis include naphthoquinones, flavonoids, and alkaloids, such as lapachol and 2-hydroxy-lapachol, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

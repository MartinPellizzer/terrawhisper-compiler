Medica marina

Medica marina, also known as Irish Moss, is a type of red algae that grows on rocky shores in the North Atlantic Ocean.

This plant has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat a variety of health issues, including digestive problems, coughs, and skin conditions, due to its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Medica marina include carrageenan, a polysaccharide that has been shown to have antimicrobial and anticoagulant effects, as well as other compounds such as fucoidan and phycobiliproteins.

In herbal medicine, Medica marina is often made into a gel or paste, and is also used to make teas, capsules, and other supplements, such as Irish Moss gel or Irish Moss capsules.

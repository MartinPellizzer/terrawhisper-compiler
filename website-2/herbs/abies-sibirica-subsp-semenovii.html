What is Abies sibirica subsp. semenovii?

Abies sibirica subsp.

semenovii is an endemic subspecies of Siberian fir native to the Tian Shan mountain range in Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan. It belongs to the family Pinaceae, a coniferous family characterized by needle-like leaves and cone-bearing reproductive structures.

Botanically, Abies sibirica subsp. semenovii has needle-like leaves 1-2 cm long, arranged in a two-ranked, dark green color, and a conical shape with a broad base and a narrow, pointed apex. This subspecies typically reaches 10-20 m in height, with a straight, cylindrical trunk and a sparse, irregularly-branched crown.

The bark is grayish-brown, fibrous, and smooth on young trees, becoming grayish-brown and fissured with age.

Phlebodium dictyocallis

Phlebodium dictyocallis, also known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in soothing skin irritations, reducing fever, and preventing the spread of infections. Phlebodium dictyocallis contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used in herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and infusions, to address various health concerns, including respiratory issues, digestive problems, and skin conditions.

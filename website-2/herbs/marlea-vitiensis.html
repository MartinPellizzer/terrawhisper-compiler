Marlea vitiensis

Marlea vitiensis, also known as the Fijian pepper tree, is a plant species native to the Pacific island of Fiji, characterized by its fragrant leaves and small, white flowers.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, providing potential health benefits such as reducing inflammation, fighting infections, and protecting against oxidative stress. Bioactive constituents of Marlea vitiensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make various herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and extracts, which are traditionally used to treat a range of ailments, including fever, cough, and respiratory issues.

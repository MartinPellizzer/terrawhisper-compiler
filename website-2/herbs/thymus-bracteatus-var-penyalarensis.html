What is Thymus bracteatus var. penyalarensis?

Thymus bracteatus var. penyalarensis is a variety of the plant species Thymus bracteatus within the genus Thymus, belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its woody stems, often growing up to 30 cm in height, and its elliptical, serrated leaves, measuring 5-15 mm in length, which are dark green in color. The leaves are arranged oppositely on the stem, and the plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which are clustered in axillary whorls. The flowers are 8-12 mm in length, with a calyx of 4-5 mm and a corolla of 6-8 mm, and are produced in the summer months.

Thymus bracteatus var. penyalarensis is native to the Iberian Peninsula, specifically in the region of Aragon, Spain.

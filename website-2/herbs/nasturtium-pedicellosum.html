Nasturtium pedicellosum

Nasturtium pedicellosum, commonly known as Indian cress or tall nasturtium, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its bright green, pinnately compound leaves and delicate, yellow or white flowers.

The plant is valued for its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been associated with reducing the risk of various diseases, such as cancer and cardiovascular conditions. The bioactive constituents of Nasturtium pedicellosum include isothiocyanates, glucosinolates, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Herbal preparations made from the plant, such as infusions, tinctures, and salves, are commonly used to treat colds, sore throats, and skin conditions, as well as to boost the immune system and promote wound healing.

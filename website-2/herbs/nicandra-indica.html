Nicandra indica

Nicandra indica, also known as the prickly balsam apple, is a plant species native to the Americas, characterized by its prickly, ovate leaves and vibrant purple flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiviral properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of fever, rheumatism, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Nicandra indica include alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Nicandra indica is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used in poultices and salves to treat skin conditions and wounds.

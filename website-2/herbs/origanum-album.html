Origanum album

Origanum album, also known as Marjoram, is a perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its fragrant, ovate leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers.

The health benefits of Marjoram include its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to alleviate anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Marjoram include rosmarinic acid, luteolin, and borneol, among others, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Marjoram is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and essential oils, and is often combined with other herbs like chamomile and lavender to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality.

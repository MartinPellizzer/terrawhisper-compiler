Narcissus spurius

Narcissus spurius, commonly known as the wild daffodil or poetic daffodil, is a perennial plant in the Amaryllidaceae family characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers and strap-like leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, providing health benefits such as wound healing, reduced fever, and relief from respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Narcissus spurius include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is typically prepared as a tincture, infusion, or ointment to treat a range of ailments, including skin conditions, coughs, and colds.

What is Rubus argenteus var. bipartitus?

Rubus argenteus var. bipartitus is a botanical variant of the silver raspberry, belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is a perennial shrub with bi-pinnate, 5-parted leaves, 5-15 cm long, with 5-13 leaflets, having a leathery texture and an acute tip. The leaves are glabrous or nearly glabrous, with a mid-vein and a reticulated network of veins. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers, 3-4 mm in diameter, in a terminal, branched inflorescence, which is followed by edible, red to yellowish, aggregate fruits, 2.5-5 cm long.

The stems are arching to erect, 1-2 m long, with a glabrous, quadrangular, ridged and grooved bark.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Rubus argenteus var. bipartitus are made from its leaves and canes.

Rubus tea is a popular preparation made by steeping the leaves and canes in hot water, showcasing its antioxidant properties. Infusion is another method where the leaves and canes are steeped in cold water, often used for its potential anti-inflammatory effects. The tincture is created through the extraction of Rubus argenteus var. bipartitus in a solvent like ethanol, commonly used for its potential medicinal properties.

Bipartitus salve is a topical preparation made from the roots and canes, often used for its potential antimicrobial properties and decoction is a strong liquid made from boiling the leaves and canes for an extended period, traditionally used for its potential antiseptic effects.

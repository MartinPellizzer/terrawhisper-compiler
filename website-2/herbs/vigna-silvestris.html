Vigna silvestris

Vigna silvestris, also known as Indian lablab or wild hyacinth bean, is a perennial legume native to tropical Asia, characterized by its climbing habit and blue-violet flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in medicine to treat various health conditions, including diabetes, digestive issues, and inflammation, due to its potential antioxidant, anti-diabetic, and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Vigna silvestris include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Vigna silvestris is commonly prepared as a decoction or a tincture, and is also used to make herbal teas and infusions to alleviate various health issues.

Myrrhis sylvestris

Myrrhis sylvestris, also known as sweet cicely, is a biennial plant native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its fern-like foliage and white flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include reducing inflammation and improving digestion, due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Myrrhis sylvestris include volatile oils, flavonoids, and coumarins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, sweet cicely is used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and glycerites, as well as in sweet dishes like desserts and jams, to take advantage of its sweet, anise-like flavor.

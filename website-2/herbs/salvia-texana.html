Salvia texana

Salvia texana, also known as Texas sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States, characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and blue to purple flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include reducing anxiety and stress, improving cognitive function, and acting as an antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory agent. The bioactive constituents of Salvia texana include various phenolic acids, flavonoids, and alkaloids, such as salvianolic acid and salvianol, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, Salvia texana is often used to prepare teas, tinctures, and capsules, which are commonly used to support mental health and well-being.

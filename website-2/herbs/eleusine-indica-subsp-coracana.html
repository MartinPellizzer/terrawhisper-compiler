Eleusine indica subsp. coracana

Eleusine indica subsp.

coracana, commonly known as finger millet, is a type of cereal crop that belongs to the family Poaceae.

It has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, which can help to alleviate symptoms of various health conditions such as fever, digestive issues, and respiratory problems.

The health benefits of finger millet include its potential to lower cholesterol levels, regulate blood sugar, and provide essential nutrients like iron, calcium, and fiber.

Finger millet can be prepared into various herbal preparations, including decoctions, infusions, and powder, which can be consumed as tea, added to food, or used as a natural remedy.

Lotus sativus

Lotus sativus, also known as the sacred lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used in medicine for its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-cancer properties, which can help to reduce inflammation, improve cardiovascular health, and protect against certain diseases. The bioactive constituents of Lotus sativus include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is often prepared in herbal teas, capsules, or tinctures, as well as being used in topical creams and salves, to promote relaxation, improve sleep quality, and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression.

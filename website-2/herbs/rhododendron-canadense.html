Rhododendron canadense

Rhododendron canadense, also known as the Clamshell Rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers and lobed leaves.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may help to soothe digestive issues and prevent infections. The bioactive constituents of Rhododendron canadense include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoid saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of ailments, including digestive issues, fever, and skin problems.

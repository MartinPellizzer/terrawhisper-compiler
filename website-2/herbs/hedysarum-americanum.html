Hedysarum americanum

Hedysarum americanum, also known as American sweetpea or wild sweetpea, is a perennial legume native to North America, characterized by its delicate pink or purple flowers and fragrant foliage.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits, including reducing inflammation, relieving respiratory issues, and supporting the immune system. Bioactive constituents of Hedysarum americanum include isoflavones, such as genistein and daidzein, as well as saponins and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is often used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are commonly consumed for their potential health benefits, particularly in traditional folk medicine and natural healing practices.

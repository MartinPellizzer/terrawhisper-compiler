Hibiscus racemosus

Hibiscus racemosus, also known as the "roselle" or "wild hibiscus," is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include lowering blood pressure, reducing inflammation, and exhibiting antioxidant and antibacterial activities, making it a potential treatment for various health conditions. The bioactive constituents of Hibiscus racemosus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Hibiscus racemosus is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, which are commonly consumed for their potential health benefits, and it is also used in the production of jams, sauces, and other food products.

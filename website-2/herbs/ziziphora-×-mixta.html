Ziziphora × mixta

Ziziphora × mixta, also known as Turkish oregano or Persian mint, is a perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, pungent aroma and flavor.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its ability to aid digestion, relieve respiratory issues, and exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Ziziphora × mixta include essential oils, particularly carvacrol and thymol, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ziziphora × mixta is commonly used to prepare teas, tinctures, and infused oils, which are used to address various health concerns and promote overall well-being.

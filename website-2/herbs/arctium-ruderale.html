Arctium ruderale

Arctium ruderale, also known as wild burdock, is a biennial or perennial plant belonging to the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with health benefits including the treatment of skin conditions, such as eczema and acne, as well as its potential to aid in detoxification and digestion. The bioactive constituents of Arctium ruderale include inulin, arctiin, and arctigenin, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

In herbal medicine, Arctium ruderale is commonly prepared in teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to support digestive health and to treat skin issues, and is also used in combination with other herbs for its potential anti-inflammatory effects.

classification

Arctium ruderale is classified within the family Asteraceae. This plant belongs to the genus Arctium, which is a group of thistle species. It is closely related to other thistle species, such as Arctium lappa and Arctium minus. Within the genus, Arctium ruderale is often considered a synonym for Arctium tomentosum. However, some sources still recognize it as a distinct species.

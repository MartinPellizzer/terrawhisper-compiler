Hyacinthus peruvianus

Hyacinthus peruvianus, commonly known as Peruvian hyacinth or Campanilla, is a flowering plant species native to the Andean region of South America.

This plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including respiratory problems, fever, and inflammation, due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help ease congestion and soothe irritated tissues. The bioactive constituents of Hyacinthus peruvianus include alkaloids, saponins, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the Peruvian hyacinth is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments, to take advantage of its therapeutic benefits.

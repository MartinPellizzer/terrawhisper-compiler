Melaleuca acutifolia

Melaleuca acutifolia, also known as the Narrow-leaved Paperbark, is a plant species native to Australia that belongs to the Myrtle family (Myrtaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering relief from various health issues such as colds, fever, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Melaleuca acutifolia include cineole, limonene, and beta-pinene, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Melaleuca acutifolia include tea, oil, and tincture, often used topically or internally to promote relaxation and alleviate symptoms of respiratory and skin issues.

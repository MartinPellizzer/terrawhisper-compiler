Pastinaca pyrenaica

Pastinaca pyrenaica, also known as Pyrenean hogweed or wild parsnip, is a biennial plant in the carrot family (Apiaceae) that grows up to 2 meters in height and is native to the Pyrenees region of Europe.

The bioactive constituents of Pastinaca pyrenaica include furanocoumarins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

What is Sorbus aria subsp. edulis?

Sorbus aria subsp.

edulis is a subspecies of Sorbus aria, a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree with a rounded crown, growing up to 8 meters tall, and characterized by its simple, alternate leaves, which are 4-9 cm long, pinnate and have 11-23 leaflets.

The leaves are dark green on the upper surface and pale green on the lower surface, with serrated margins and a cuneate base. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in May, in corymbose panicles, and red or yellowish berries in autumn, which are edible.

The plant's bark is greyish-brown, smooth and exfoliating, with horizontal lenticels.

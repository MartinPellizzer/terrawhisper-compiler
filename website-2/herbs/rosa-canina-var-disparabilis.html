What is Rosa canina var. disparabilis?

Rosa canina var. disparabilis is a botanical variant of the dog rose species, belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems, typically growing between 3-6 meters in height and 2-4 meters in width, with compound leaves consisting of 5-9 leaflets, each 2-5 centimeters long and 1-2 centimeters wide, with a serrated margin. The flowers are pink to reddish, hermaphroditic, and fragrant, 5-6 centimeters in diameter, with 5 petals and 5 sepals, blooming from May to July. The fruit is a red or purple pome, 1-2 centimeters in diameter, containing 1-3 seeds, ripening in August and September.

Rosa canina var. disparabilis is native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa.

classification

Oyama sinensis, also known as Ginseng, is classified as a perennial plant. It belongs to the family Araliaceae. Within this family, Oyama sinensis is classified as a genus, Panax. This genus is further divided into several species, including P. ginseng, P. quinquefolius, and P. japonicus, among others. The specific classification of Oyama sinensis is still a matter of debate among botanists.

side effects

Oyama sinensis, also known as Ginseng, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, but it can cause some side effects in certain individuals. These side effects may include headaches, dizziness, and nausea due to its high saponin and ginsenoside content. Additionally, Ginseng may interact with blood thinners and other medications, increasing the risk of bleeding and other complications. Some people may also experience sleep disturbances, anxiety, or irritability after consuming Ginseng. It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using Ginseng supplements, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or take medications regularly.

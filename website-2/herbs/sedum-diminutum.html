Sedum diminutum

Sedum diminutum, also known as small-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant species belonging to the Crassulaceae family, characterized by its compact growth and small, rounded leaves.

The bioactive constituents of Sedum diminutum include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Sedum diminutum is commonly prepared into herbal remedies such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used in topical applications like creams and ointments for wound healing and skin protection.

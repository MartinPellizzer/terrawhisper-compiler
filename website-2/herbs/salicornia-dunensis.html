Salicornia dunensis

Salicornia dunensis, also known as dune glasswort, is a succulent halophytic plant that grows in coastal salt marshes and dunes, characterized by its ability to thrive in saline environments.

The plant has been traditionally used for its health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-diabetic properties, which have been attributed to its ability to help regulate blood sugar levels and reduce inflammation. The bioactive constituents of Salicornia dunensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used in traditional medicine and is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or infused oil, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including digestive issues, skin problems, and respiratory diseases.

Chamerion angustifolium

Chamerion angustifolium, also known as Fireweed, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant effects, which can aid in wound healing, reducing fever, and alleviating respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Fireweed include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Fireweed is often used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being used in salves and topical creams to support skin health and wound care.

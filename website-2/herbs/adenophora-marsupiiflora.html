Adenophora marsupiiflora

Adenophora marsupiiflora, also known as the Adenophora or False Hellebore, is a perennial plant species native to China and the Himalayas.

The plant has been traditionally used in medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of Adenophora marsupiiflora include sesquiterpenes, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Adenophora marsupiiflora include dried roots and rhizomes, which can be used to make decoctions, infusions, and tinctures for medicinal purposes.

Nama nigricaulis

Nama nigricaulis, also known as Fagonia, is a medicinal plant native to the deserts of North Africa and the Middle East, characterized by its small yellow flowers and woody stems.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including the treatment of various skin conditions, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Nama nigricaulis include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Nama nigricaulis is commonly used in traditional medicine and is often prepared as a tea or infusion, as well as being incorporated into herbal capsules and tinctures.

Meibomia floribunda

Meibomia floribunda, also known as Bush Clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America, characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant flowers and hairy leaves.

The bioactive constituents of Meibomia floribunda include flavonoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Eugenia humilis

Eugenia humilis, also known as the Chilean guava or Chilean myrtle, is a shrub-like plant native to South America, belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, helping to protect against infections, reduce inflammation, and promote overall well-being. The bioactive constituents of Eugenia humilis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Eugenia humilis is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin conditions.

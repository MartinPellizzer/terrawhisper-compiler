Lamium orientale

Lamium orientale, also known as Syrian nettle, is a perennial plant species in the mint family (Lamiaceae) native to the Middle East and the Mediterranean region.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include reducing inflammation, relieving pain, and exhibiting antioxidant and antimicrobial activities, providing potential health benefits for the treatment of various conditions such as arthritis, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Lamium orientale include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Lamium orientale is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat digestive issues, promote wound healing, and reduce inflammation.

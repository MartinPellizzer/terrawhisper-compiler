Gentianella secta

Gentianella secta, also known as New Zealand gentian or Kawakawa gentian, is a species of flowering plant in the gentian family (Gentianaceae), characterized by its vibrant blue flowers and robust growth habit.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which contribute to its potential health benefits in reducing pain, improving digestion, and enhancing immune function. The bioactive constituents of Gentianella secta include gentiopicroside, loganin, and swertiamarin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Gentianella secta is commonly prepared as a decoction or a tincture, and is often used in combination with other herbs to support digestive health and relieve symptoms of gastritis and peptic ulcers.

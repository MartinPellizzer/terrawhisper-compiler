Solanum guineense

Solanum guineense, also known as the guineafowl potato, is a perennial plant species belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in African folk medicine, offering health benefits such as antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Solanum guineense include solasodine, solamargine, and solasonine, which are glycoalkaloids with potential therapeutic applications.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and poultices, which are used to treat various conditions, including skin infections and wounds.

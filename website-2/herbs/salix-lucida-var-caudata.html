What is Salix lucida var. caudata?

Salix lucida var. caudata is a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to North America, belonging to the family Salicaceae.

It typically grows to 1-4 meters in height, with a spread of 1-2 meters, and has a greyish-brown bark with ridges and furrows. Its leaves are lanceolate or ovate, 5-10 cm long, with a pointed tip, entire margin, and a petiole that is 1-2 cm long; they are dark green on the upper surface and pale green on the lower surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow, or reddish flowers in catkins, 2-5 cm long, in late winter to early spring.

The flowers are wind-pollinated and produce small, samara-like fruits, 1-2 cm long, with a wing that is 0.5-1 cm long.

Blakea suaveolens

Blakea suaveolens, also known as 'canela de monte', is a species of flowering plant in the family Melastomataceae, native to the Andes mountains in South America.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making it beneficial for treating various health conditions such as wounds, fever, and respiratory issues. Bioactive constituents of Blakea suaveolens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Aloe × perfectior

Aloe × perfecta, also known as the Perfect Aloe, is a hybrid plant species resulting from the cross between Aloe ferox and Aloe plicatilis.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as soothing skin irritations, reducing inflammation, and promoting wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Aloe × perfecta include aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan, a polysaccharide that has been shown to stimulate the immune system.

Aloe × perfecta is commonly used in herbal preparations such as gels, ointments, and creams, as well as in dietary supplements, to support skin health and overall well-being.

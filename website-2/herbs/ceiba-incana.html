Ceiba incana

Ceiba incana is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Malvaceae, native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it effective in treating conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues. Ceiba incana is rich in bioactive constituents like flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Ceiba incana is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, with the most common preparations being decoctions and infusions used to treat fever, cough, and other respiratory problems.

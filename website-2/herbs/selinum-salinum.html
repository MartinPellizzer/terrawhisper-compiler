Selinum salinum

Selinum carvifolium (also known as Selinum salinum) is a perennial plant species in the carrot family (Apiaceae), native to Europe and Western Asia, known for its pungent, celery-like foliage and umbels of small white flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in treating digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Selinum salinum include volatile oils, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as in traditional remedies like salves and poultices.

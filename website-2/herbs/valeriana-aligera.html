Valeriana aligera

Valeriana aligera, also known as valerian, is a perennial flowering plant in the family Valerianaceae, native to Europe and Asia.

The roots of Valeriana aligera have been used in traditional medicine for centuries, exhibiting various health benefits, including promoting relaxation, reducing anxiety and stress, improving sleep quality, and alleviating symptoms of restlessness and irritability. The bioactive constituents of Valeriana aligera include valerenic acid, valeranone, and valepotriates, which are responsible for its sedative and anxiolytic properties.

In herbal medicine, Valeriana aligera is commonly prepared as a root tincture, tea, or capsule, often used to treat insomnia, nervous tension, and restlessness.

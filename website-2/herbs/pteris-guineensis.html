Pteris guineensis

Pteris guineensis, also known as the Guinean Brake Fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pteridaceae, native to tropical Africa.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pteris guineensis is traditionally used in herbal medicine and is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, although it can also be found in capsule or tincture form, allowing for its bioactive compounds to be used in a more concentrated and easily administered manner.

Gentianella selaginifolia

Gentianella selaginifolia, also known as Selaginella gentianella, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its delicate white or pink flowers and slender stems.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities, which may help alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders, respiratory issues, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Gentianella selaginifolia include iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Gentianella selaginifolia is often used to prepare teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat various health conditions and promote overall well-being.

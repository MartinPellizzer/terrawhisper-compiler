Leopoldia maritima

Leopoldia maritima, also known as sea squill or sea asphodel, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including respiratory problems, such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Leopoldia maritima include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Leopoldia maritima is often prepared as a tincture, decoction, or dried powder, and is used to treat coughs, colds, and other respiratory conditions.

Loranthus kaempferi

Loranthus kaempferi, also known as the Japanese mistletoe or kaempferi Loranthus, is a hemi-parasitic plant species native to East Asia, specifically in China, Japan, and Korea.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and cardiovascular diseases, due to its numerous health benefits, such as antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and cardio-protective properties. The bioactive constituents of Loranthus kaempferi include lignans, phenolic compounds, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Loranthus kaempferi is often used to make herbal teas, extracts, and infusions, as well as being dried and processed into capsules or tablets for modern use.

Malva longiflora

Malva longiflora, also known as the long-flowered mallow, is a perennial plant species belonging to the family Malvaceae.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help soothe digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and protect against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Malva longiflora include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Herbal preparations made from the plant include teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as topical creams and ointments used to treat skin conditions such as wounds and eczema.

Malva longiflora is classified within the family Malvaceae, commonly known as the mallow family. This family consists of around 200-300 species of flowering plants, including hibiscus and hollyhock. The genus Malva is further divided into several species, including Malva longiflora. Malva longiflora is native to the tropical regions of the Americas and is often found in moist, subtropical environments. Its classification makes it a part of the larger group of Malvaceae, which is native to various parts of the world.

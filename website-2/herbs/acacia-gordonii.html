Acacia gordonii

Acacia gordonii is a small tree or shrub species native to the Middle East and North Africa, belonging to the legume family (Fabaceae) and traditionally known as "Gum arabic tree".

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects, which may aid in wound healing, digestive issues, and immune system regulation. The bioactive constituents of Acacia gordonii include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Acacia gordonii is often used to prepare herbal teas, tinctures, and extracts, which are commonly used to treat various health conditions, including respiratory issues, skin problems, and gastrointestinal complaints.

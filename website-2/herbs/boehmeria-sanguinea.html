Boehmeria sanguinea

Boehmeria sanguinea, also known as red stem nettle or Japanese Boehmeria, is a perennial plant species belonging to the nettle family (Urticaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat a range of health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of Boehmeria sanguinea include flavonoids, triterpenoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Boehmeria sanguinea is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being used in topical creams and ointments.

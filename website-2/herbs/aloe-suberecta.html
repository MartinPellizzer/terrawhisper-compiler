The bioactive constituents of Aloe suberecta include aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory properties.

In traditional medicine, Aloe suberecta is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as gel, juice, and powder, which are often used to treat skin conditions, promote wound healing, and support digestive health.

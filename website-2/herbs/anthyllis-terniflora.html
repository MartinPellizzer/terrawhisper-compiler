Anthyllis terniflora

Anthyllis terniflora, also known as Kidney Vetch, is a perennial legume plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its small yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which may help to alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, wounds, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Anthyllis terniflora include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Kidney Vetch is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is sometimes combined with other herbs to enhance its effects.

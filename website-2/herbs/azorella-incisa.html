Azorella incisa

Azorella incisa, also known as the New Zealand pepperwort, is a perennial herb that belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

The bioactive constituents of Azorella incisa include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms associated with colds and flu.

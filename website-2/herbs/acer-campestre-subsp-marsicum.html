What is Acer campestre subsp. marsicum?

Acer campestre subsp.

marsicum is a subspecies of the green field maple, belonging to the family Sapindaceae. It is characterized by its deciduous nature, with leaves that are 5-9 lobed, serrated, and typically 5-10 cm long, often having 7 leaflets.

The leaves are dark green above and pale green below, with petioles 1-2 cm long. The flowers are greenish-yellow, in clusters of 2-5, and borne in spring, while the fruit is a samara, 2-3 cm long, with a wing that is 1-2 cm long.

The tree grows up to 20 m tall, with a trunk diameter of up to 60 cm.

Additionally, Acer campestre subsp. marsicum exhibits antimicrobial properties, which are primarily due to the presence of volatile oils and phenolic compounds that inhibit the growth of microorganisms and prevent infections. Furthermore, the cardioprotective properties of the plant are largely attributed to the flavonoids and saponins that help lower blood pressure, improve lipid profiles, and reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease.

Its analgesic properties are also significant, as they are mediated by the presence of saponins and phenolic compounds that inhibit pain mediators and reduce pain perception, providing relief from various types of pain.

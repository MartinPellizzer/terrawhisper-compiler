Fumana arabica

Fumana arabica, also known as Rockrose or Fumana, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Cistaceae family, native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which can help alleviate conditions such as digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Fumana arabica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Fumana arabica is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into creams and ointments for topical application.

Scilla hemisphaerica

Scilla hemisphaerica, also known as the lesser squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses including the treatment of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, which can help ease congestion and promote coughing. The bioactive constituents of Scilla hemisphaerica include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

The plant is often used to make herbal preparations, such as tinctures and infusions, as well as dried bulb powder, which can be used to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu.

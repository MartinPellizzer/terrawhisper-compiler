Strophis cirrhosa

Strophis cirrhosa, also known as the joint vine or snake vine, is a perennial climbing vine native to the Americas, belonging to the pea family (Fabaceae).

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which can aid in wound healing, reduce pain and inflammation, and protect against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Strophis cirrhosa include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Strophis cirrhosa is often prepared as a tincture or infused in tea, and may also be used in topical applications, such as salves or creams, to harness its therapeutic benefits.

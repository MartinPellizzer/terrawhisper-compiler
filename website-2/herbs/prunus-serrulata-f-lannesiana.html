What is Prunus serrulata f. lannesiana?

Prunus serrulata f. lannesiana is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree, belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is a deciduous tree with a broad, spreading habit and a maximum height of 10-15 meters. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic, with a length of 4-8 centimeters and a width of 2-4 centimeters, having a finely serrated margin and a glabrous upper surface. The flowers are showy, pale pink to white, with five petals and a diameter of 3-4 centimeters, blooming in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The fruits are small, red, and inedible, being a drupe with a single seed enclosed in a fleshy outer layer.

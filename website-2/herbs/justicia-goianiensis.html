Justicia goianiensis

Justicia goianiensis, also known as Brazilian plume poppy, is a plant species that belongs to the Acanthaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, particularly in Brazil, to treat various health issues, including respiratory problems, fever, and inflammation, due to its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Justicia goianiensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Justicia goianiensis is often used to make teas, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as to reduce fever and alleviate pain.

Lactuca aurea

Lactuca aurea, also known as golden milk thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion tribe within the sunflower family.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including reducing inflammation and improving liver function, due to its ability to stimulate the production of bile and enhance the body's natural detoxification processes. The bioactive constituents of Lactuca aurea include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, the leaves and roots of Lactuca aurea are commonly used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into supplements and capsules to support liver health and overall well-being.

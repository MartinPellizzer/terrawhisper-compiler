Cryptandra westringiifolia

Cryptandra westringiifolia, also known as Western Cryptandra, is a shrub native to the southwestern region of Western Australia.

This plant has been traditionally used in Western Australian Aboriginal medicine, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of colds, flu, and respiratory tract infections. The bioactive constituents of Cryptandra westringiifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Western Cryptandra is commonly prepared as a tea or infusion, and also used in topical applications such as creams and ointments to treat skin conditions and wounds.

Sapium luzonicum

Sapium luzonicum, also known as the Philippine stinking nut or kulo-kulo, is a plant species native to the Philippines and other parts of Southeast Asia, belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

The health benefits of Sapium luzonicum include its potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate various health conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, tannins, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Sapium luzonicum is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory issues in traditional medicine.

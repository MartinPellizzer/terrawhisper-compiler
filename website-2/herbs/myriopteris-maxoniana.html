Myriopteris maxoniana

Myriopteris maxoniana, also known as the Maxon's oak fern, is a species of fern in the family Dryopteridaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its ability to lower blood pressure, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial activity, making it a potential treatment for various health conditions. The bioactive constituents of Myriopteris maxoniana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Myriopteris maxoniana is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is used to make teas, tinctures, and capsules, which are used to support cardiovascular health and immune function.

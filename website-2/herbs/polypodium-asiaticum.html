Polypodium asiaticum

Polypodium asiaticum, also known as Asiatic polypody or Chinese polypody, is a type of fern that belongs to the Polypodiaceae family.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help to reduce inflammation, improve wound healing, and protect against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Polypodium asiaticum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoid saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Polypodium asiaticum is commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is often used to treat various ailments, including fever, cough, and respiratory infections.

classification

Polypodium asiaticum is a species of the genus Polypodium, which belongs to the family Polypodiaceae. This family is part of the larger division Pteridophyta, which includes all ferns. Pteridophyta is a division within the kingdom Plantae. The kingdom Plantae is a group that includes all plants, algae, and bryophytes. Further classification of Polypodium asiaticum includes its order Polypodiales and its class Polypodiopsida.

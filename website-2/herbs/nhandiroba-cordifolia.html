Nhandiroba cordifolia

Nhandiroba cordifolia, also known as the "Brazilian Heartleaf," is a plant species native to the Brazilian Cerrado region, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant activities, offering potential health benefits such as wound healing, pain relief, and protection against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Nhandiroba cordifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Nhandiroba cordifolia is commonly used to prepare herbal infusions, tinctures, and topical creams, which are employed in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions, including skin infections, fever, and respiratory issues.

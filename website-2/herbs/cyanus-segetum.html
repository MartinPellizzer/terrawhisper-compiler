Cyanus segetum

Cyanus segetum, also known as cornflower, is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family that is native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa.

The health benefits of cornflower include its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as eye infections, wounds, and gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of cornflower include flavonoids, such as luteolin and apigenin, as well as sesquiterpene lactones and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Cornflower is often used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into eye creams and ointments to treat conjunctivitis and other eye conditions.

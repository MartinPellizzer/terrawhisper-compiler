Helichrysum ericifolium

Helichrysum ericifolium, also known as False Knapweed or Knapweed, is a perennial plant in the daisy family, characterized by its small yellow or white flowers and narrow, silvery-green leaves.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in wound healing, pain relief, and the reduction of inflammation. The bioactive constituents of Helichrysum ericifolium include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Helichrysum ericifolium is often prepared as a tincture, tea, or salve, and is used to treat a range of conditions, including skin irritations, sprains, and minor wounds.

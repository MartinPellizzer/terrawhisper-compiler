Cereus haageanus

Cereus haageanus, also known as the "Night-blooming Cereus", is a species of cactus native to Mexico and Central America, characterized by its tall stem and funnel-shaped flowers that bloom at night.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, fever, and digestive problems, due to its purported health benefits in reducing pain and promoting wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Cereus haageanus include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which have been reported to possess anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a decoction, tincture, or salve, and is often used to treat conditions such as arthritis, colds, and skin irritations.

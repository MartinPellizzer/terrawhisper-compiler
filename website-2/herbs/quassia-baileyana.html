Quassia baileyana

Quassia baileyana, also known as Quassia, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and Central America, and its bark is valued for its medicinal properties.

The health benefits of Quassia include its potential to act as an antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antiparasitic agent, making it useful in treating various ailments such as digestive issues, skin infections, and intestinal parasites. The bioactive constituents of Quassia include quassin, a lignan, and other triterpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Quassia include tinctures, decoctions, and infusions, often used in traditional medicine to support digestive health and treat parasitic infections.

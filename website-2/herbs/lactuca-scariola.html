Lactuca scariola

Lactuca scariola, also known as Wild Lettuce or Prickly Lettuce, is a biennial plant native to Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia, characterized by its prickly leaves and stem.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its sedative, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic properties, which have been linked to various health benefits, including improved sleep quality and reduced anxiety. The bioactive constituents of Lactuca scariola include lactucarium, a complex mixture of sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of conditions, including insomnia, restlessness, and menstrual cramps.

Diplacus viscidus

Diplacus viscidus, also known as sticky monkey flower, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, characterized by its distinctive sticky leaves and bright yellow flowers.

The bioactive constituents of D. viscidus include iridoid glycosides, phenolic acids, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

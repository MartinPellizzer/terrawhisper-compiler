Gentiana neapolitana

Gentiana neapolitana, also known as Neapolitan gentian, is a species of flowering plant in the gentian family that is native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions, including digestive issues and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Gentiana neapolitana include gentianine, gentiopicrin, and amarogentin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Gentiana neapolitana is often prepared as a tincture, decoction, or syrup, and is commonly used in traditional remedies for its carminative, antacid, and expectorant properties.

Carya cordiformis

Carya cordiformis, also known as the Bitternut Hickory, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, belonging to the family Juglandaceae.

The Bitternut Hickory is valued for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in reducing the risk of chronic diseases and promoting overall well-being. The bioactive constituents of Carya cordiformis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Carya cordiformis is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are believed to support digestive health, reduce fever, and alleviate respiratory issues.

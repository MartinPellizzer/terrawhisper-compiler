Cyperus limiticola

Cyperus limiticola, also known as Cape bulrush, is a type of sedge plant native to the southwestern region of South Africa.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of Cyperus limiticola include sesquiterpenes, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Cyperus limiticola is often prepared in the form of infusions, decoctions, or dried powders, which can be used to treat a range of health issues, including respiratory problems and skin conditions.

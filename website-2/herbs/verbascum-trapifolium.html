Verbascum trapifolium

Verbascum trapifolium, also known as two-leaved mullein, is a biennial plant species in the mullein genus, characterized by its tall spikes of yellow flowers and succulent leaves.

The plant is valued for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, making it beneficial for treating respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as skin conditions like eczema and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Verbascum trapifolium include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and iridoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Verbascum trapifolium include teas, infusions, and topical creams, which are used to soothe and calm irritated skin and respiratory tract issues.

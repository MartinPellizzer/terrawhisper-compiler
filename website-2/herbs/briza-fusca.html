classification

Briza fusca is classified as a species of grass in the family Poaceae. It belongs to the genus Briza, which comprises several species of annual or perennial grasses. The specific epithet 'fusca' is derived from the Latin word for 'dark', likely referring to the color of the seedheads. This classification places Briza fusca within the larger group of monocotyledonous plants. As a member of the Poaceae family, Briza fusca shares characteristics with other grasses, such as parallel veins and fibrous roots.

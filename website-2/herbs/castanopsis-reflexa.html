Castanopsis reflexa

Castanopsis reflexa, also known as Taiwan chinquapin, is a species of evergreen tree in the family Fagaceae, native to Taiwan and southern China.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, offering potential health benefits in treating various conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Castanopsis reflexa include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as tea, decoctions, and infusions, which are also used in modern herbal medicine for their potential health-promoting properties.

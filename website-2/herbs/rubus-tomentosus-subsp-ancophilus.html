What is Rubus tomentosus subsp. ancophilus?

Rubus tomentosus subsp.

ancophilus is a plant species belonging to the family Rosaceae. It is a thorny shrub with simple, pinnate leaves that are 5-7 cm long, having 5-7 leaflets, and a leaflet margin that is serrated.

The plant produces white flowers in a dense corymb, which are followed by red, aggregate fruits that are 1-2 cm in diameter. The stems of Rubus tomentosus subsp. ancophilus are thorny, with internodes that are 1-4 cm long, and have a dense covering of greyish-brown tomentum.

This plant species is native to Ecuador and has a growth habit that is typically found in humid to sub-humid montane forests.

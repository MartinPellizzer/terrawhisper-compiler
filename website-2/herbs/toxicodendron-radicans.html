side effects

Toxicodendron radicans, also known as poison ivy, oak, or sumac, contains an oily sap called urushiol that can cause severe skin irritation and allergic reactions in some people. When the sap comes into contact with the skin, it can cause redness, itching, blistering, and even skin lesions. In severe cases, exposure to urushiol can lead to anaphylaxis, a life-threatening allergic reaction that requires immediate medical attention. People who have had previous exposure to urushiol may develop a long-lasting sensitivity to the oil, making future reactions even more severe. The best way to treat poison ivy exposure is to wash the affected area with soap and water as soon as possible to remove the urushiol from the skin.

Persicaria lyrata

Persicaria lyrata, also known as fleece flower or oriental fleece flower, is a perennial plant species native to East Asia, characterized by its delicate white or pink flowers and feathery foliage.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Persicaria lyrata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used in herbal preparations, including infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory conditions, such as bronchitis and pneumonia, as well as to support immune function and reduce inflammation.

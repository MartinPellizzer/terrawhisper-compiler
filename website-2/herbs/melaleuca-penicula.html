Melaleuca penicula

Melaleuca penicula, also known as the weeping paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family (Myrtaceae) native to Australia.

The bioactive constituents of Melaleuca penicula include compounds such as cineole, limonene, and terpinen-4-ol, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Melaleuca penicula include teas, tinctures, and creams, which are often used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and respiratory infections.

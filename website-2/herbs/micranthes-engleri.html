Micranthes engleri

Micranthes engleri, also known as Engler's saxifrage, is a perennial plant species belonging to the saxifrage family (Saxifragaceae).

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiviral activities, making it useful for treating various health issues such as wound healing, skin conditions, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Micranthes engleri include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Traditionally, Engler's saxifrage is made into teas, infusions, and tinctures, with the most common herbal preparations being a tea for treating respiratory issues and a topical cream for wound healing.

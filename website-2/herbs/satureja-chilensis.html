Satureja chilensis

Satureja chilensis, also known as Chilean savory, is a perennial herb native to the Andes mountains in South America, belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

The bioactive constituents of Satureja chilensis include carvacrol, thymol, and rosmarinic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Satureja chilensis is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and essential oils, as well as being added to food and used in aromatherapy to promote overall well-being.

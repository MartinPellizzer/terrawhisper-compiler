Combretum album

Combretum album, also known as White Bushwillow, is a flowering plant species within the Combretaceae family, native to southern Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antipyretic properties, providing relief from fever, pain, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Combretum album include flavonoids, tannins, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Combretum album is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and capsules, and is also used in combination with other herbs to create tonics and remedies for various health conditions.

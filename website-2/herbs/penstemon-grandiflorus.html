Penstemon grandiflorus

Penstemon grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered penstemon or beardtongue, is a perennial plant species native to North America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antibacterial effects, making it a potential remedy for wounds, skin irritations, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Penstemon grandiflorus include iridoid glycosides, such as penstemone and ajugol, as well as phenolic acids and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Penstemon grandiflorus is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, which can be used to support immune function, soothe digestive issues, and promote wound healing.

Spiraea ulmaria glauca

Spiraea ulmaria glauca, also known as meadowsweet, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the rose family (Rosaceae).

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antiseptic properties, making it beneficial for treating conditions such as headaches, fever, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of meadowsweet include salicylic acid, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Meadowsweet is often used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

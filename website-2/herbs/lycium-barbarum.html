What is Lycium barbarum?

Lycium barbarum is a perennial shrub native to Asia, belonging to the family Solanaceae.

It grows up to 2 meters in height, with woody stems, ovate or lance-shaped leaves, and small, white to purple flowers that are clustered in axillary racemes. The fruit is a red, edible berry, approximately 1-2 cm in diameter, containing 2-4 seeds. The plant is rich in antioxidants, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and anthocyanins, and is used in traditional medicine to treat various conditions.

Lycium barbarum is also known as Goji berry or Chinese boxthorn.

Tridens grandiflorus

Tridens grandiflorus, also known as tall needle grass or big-flowered needle grass, is a perennial grass native to North America, characterized by its tall stature and distinctive needle-like leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antimicrobial effects, making it useful for wound healing and reducing fever. The bioactive constituents of Tridens grandiflorus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Tridens grandiflorus is commonly prepared as a tea or infused in a cold-water extract, and is also used in traditional remedies for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

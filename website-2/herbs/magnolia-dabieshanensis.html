Magnolia dabieshanensis

Magnolia dabieshanensis, a species of flowering plant in the family Magnoliaceae, is an evergreen tree endemic to the Dabie Mountains in China.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties that may help alleviate respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Magnolia dabieshanensis include magnolol and honokiol, two lignans that have been shown to possess significant pharmacological activities, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Magnolia dabieshanensis include tea, extracts, and capsules, which are often used to treat respiratory issues and other conditions related to inflammation and oxidative stress.

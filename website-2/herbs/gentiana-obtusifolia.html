Gentiana obtusifolia

Gentiana obtusifolia, also known as the obtuse gentian or Tibetan gentian, is a species of flowering plant in the gentian family that is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Tibet.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Gentiana obtusifolia include iridoid glycosides, such as gentiogenin and gentiopicroside, which are believed to be responsible for the plant's medicinal properties.

Gentiana obtusifolia is commonly used to make herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to treat respiratory infections and other ailments.

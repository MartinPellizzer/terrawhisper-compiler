Crepis hyemalis

Crepis hyemalis, also known as winter cress or frostweed, is a perennial plant belonging to the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which are beneficial for soothing digestive issues, reducing fever, and alleviating respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Crepis hyemalis include sesquiterpene lactones, such as crepidin and hyemalin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Crepis hyemalis is often used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to support digestive health, boost the immune system, and relieve menstrual cramps.

Corydalis sibirica

Corydalis sibirica, also known as Siberian corydalis or yellow corydalis, is a perennial plant species belonging to the poppy family (Papaveraceae).

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat a range of health conditions, including pain, inflammation, and anxiety, due to its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and sedative properties. The bioactive constituents of Corydalis sibirica include alkaloids such as tetrahydroprotoberberine and protopine, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Corydalis sibirica include capsules, tablets, and tinctures, often combined with other herbs like ginseng and licorice root to enhance its therapeutic benefits.

classification

Corydalis sibirica is a species of flowering plant that belongs to the family Papaveraceae. It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia and Europe. The genus Corydalis is part of the larger Papaveraceae family, which also includes other plants such as poppies and meadowsweets. The classification of Corydalis sibirica is further divided into the kingdom Plantae, and the division Magnoliophyta. Corydalis sibirica's scientific classification is also known as Corydalis sibirica L. (Linnaeus).

Eucalyptus glaucina

Eucalyptus glaucina, also known as the blue mallee, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

The bioactive constituents of Eucalyptus glaucina include volatile oils, particularly eucalyptol, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Eucalyptus glaucina include essential oils, infusions, and topical creams, which are often used to relieve respiratory symptoms and soothe skin irritations.

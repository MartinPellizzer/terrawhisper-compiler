Corydalis sikkimensis

Corydalis sikkimensis, also known as Sikkim corydalis, is a species of flowering plant in the poppy family (Papaveraceae) native to the Himalayas.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties, and is believed to have potential health benefits in managing pain, improving sleep, and reducing anxiety and depression. The bioactive constituents of Corydalis sikkimensis include alkaloids, including protopine, corydalin, and corytuberine, as well as other compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Corydalis sikkimensis is often prepared as a decoction or tincture, and is also used in the production of capsules, tablets, and other herbal formulations.

Limnophila aquatica

Limnophila aquatica, also known as swampy coleus or water hyssop, is a floating aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in wound healing, respiratory issues, and gastrointestinal health. Bioactive constituents of Limnophila aquatica include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbalism, Limnophila aquatica is commonly prepared as a tea or tincture, and is also used in poultices and infusions to treat a range of health conditions.

Tsuga douglasii

Tsuga douglasii, commonly known as Douglas fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Pacific coast of North America.

The essential oil extracted from the needles of Douglas fir has been traditionally used for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, providing health benefits such as relieving respiratory issues and reducing fever. The bioactive constituents of Douglas fir essential oil include alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, and terpinen-4-ol, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Douglas fir is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and aromatherapy products, which are often used to promote relaxation, reduce stress, and alleviate respiratory congestion.

classification

Gentianusa cruciata

Gentianusa cruciata, commonly known as the Eastern Gentian, is a perennial plant species native to eastern Australia.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which may help to soothe digestive issues and reduce the severity of infections. The bioactive constituents of Gentianusa cruciata include iridoid glycosides, gentiopicroside, and loganin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Gentianusa cruciata is often prepared as a tincture or decoction, and is commonly used to support the health of the digestive system.

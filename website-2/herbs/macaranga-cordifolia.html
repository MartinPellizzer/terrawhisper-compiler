Macaranga cordifolia

Macaranga cordifolia, also known as the heartleaf tree or heartleaf, is a species of flowering plant in the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae) that is native to the tropical regions of Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antimicrobial properties, which can help to treat various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Macaranga cordifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Macaranga cordifolia is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or paste, and is often used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory problems, as well as to aid in wound healing and skin care.

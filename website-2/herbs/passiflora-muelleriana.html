Passiflora muelleriana

Passiflora muelleriana, also known as Purple Passionflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Passifloraceae family, native to Australia.

The plant has been traditionally used for its sedative and anxiolytic properties, providing relief from anxiety and insomnia due to its calming effects on the nervous system. The bioactive constituents of Passiflora muelleriana include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, particularly harman, harmine, and harmaline, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Passiflora muelleriana is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat restlessness, insomnia, and anxiety disorders.

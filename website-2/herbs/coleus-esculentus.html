Coleus esculentus

Coleus esculentus, also known as Coleus aromaticus or African mint, is a perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family, native to tropical Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antifungal properties, which help in treating various ailments, including digestive issues and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Coleus esculentus include essential oils like limonene, linalool, and beta-caryophyllene, as well as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Coleus esculentus is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health conditions, and it is also used in cooking and as a flavoring agent in some African cuisines.

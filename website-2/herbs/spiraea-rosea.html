Spiraea rosea

Spiraea rosea, also known as meadowsweet, is a perennial plant that belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is native to Europe and Asia.

The plant is valued for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including digestive problems, fever, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Spiraea rosea include salicylic acid, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from meadowsweet include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to soothe digestive issues, reduce fever, and alleviate respiratory problems.

Cotula hispida

Cotula hispida, also known as Rough Hawk's Beak or Rough Cottonweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the Asteraceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiviral properties, which may help to soothe wounds, reduce fever, and alleviate respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Cotula hispida include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Cotula hispida is often used to make infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as to support the immune system.

Brassica pekinensis

Brassica pekinensis, commonly known as Chinese cabbage or pak choi, is a cool-season crop belonging to the Brassica genus of plants.

This plant is rich in nutrients and offers various health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and acting as an antioxidant due to its high content of vitamins A, C, and K, as well as its glucosinolate compounds. The bioactive constituents of Brassica pekinensis include isothiocyanates, glucosinolates, and phenolic compounds, which have been shown to exhibit anti-cancer and anti-inflammatory properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, pak choi is often used to make teas, infusions, and soups, and is also used in various herbal preparations to support immune function and reduce oxidative stress.

Disa multiflora

Disa multiflora, commonly known as the fire lily or fire orchid, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Disa genus, native to the Cape Provinces of South Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral activities, making it useful in treating various ailments such as fever, coughs, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Disa multiflora include iridoids, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Disa multiflora include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often used to treat respiratory and skin issues.

classification

Disa multiflora is classified within the family Orchidaceae. It belongs to the genus Disa, a group of orchids commonly found in southern Africa. Disa multiflora is a terrestrial orchid, which means it grows in the ground rather than on trees or rocks. The species name 'multiflora' refers to its habit of producing multiple flowers on a single stem. Disa multiflora is further classified as a member of the Disa gracillima complex, a group of closely related species within the genus Disa.

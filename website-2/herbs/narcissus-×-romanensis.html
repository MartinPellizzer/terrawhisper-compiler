Narcissus × romanensis

Narcissus × romanensis, commonly known as Roman Narcissus, is a hybrid species of flowering bulbous plant within the Amaryllidaceae family, native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections, such as bronchitis and pneumonia. The bioactive constituents of Roman Narcissus include alkaloids like galantamine, which has been shown to have potential in treating Alzheimer's disease, as well as other compounds like flavonoids and glycosides.

In herbal medicine, Roman Narcissus is typically prepared as a tincture or extract, which can be used to treat respiratory issues, or as a topical application to help soothe skin irritations and wounds.

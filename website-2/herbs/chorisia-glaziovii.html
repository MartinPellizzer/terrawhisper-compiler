Chorisia glaziovii

Chorisia glaziovii, also known as the Pink Trumpet Tree or Lapacho, is a deciduous tree native to South America, characterized by its vibrant pink flowers and distinctive trumpet-shaped calyx.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Chorisia glaziovii include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often made into teas, infusions, and capsules, as well as being used in tinctures and extracts, to leverage its health benefits and bioactive constituents.

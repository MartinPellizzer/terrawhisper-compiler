Laportea cuspidata

Laportea cuspidata, also known as Ugu or Ikong, is a species of plant in the nettle family (Urticaceae) that is native to tropical regions of Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including the treatment of fever, skin conditions, and digestive issues, as well as its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Bioactive constituents of Laportea cuspidata include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Laportea cuspidata is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or infused oil, and is used to treat a range of conditions, including wounds, eczema, and arthritis.

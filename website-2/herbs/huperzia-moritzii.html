Huperzia moritzii

Huperzia moritzii, also known as the shrub club moss, is a type of liverwort that belongs to the Huperziaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including anxiety, depression, and memory impairment, due to its potential health benefits such as improving cognitive function and neuroprotection. The bioactive constituents of Huperzia moritzii include huperzine A, a potent inhibitor of acetylcholinesterase, as well as other alkaloids that may contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Huperzia moritzii is commonly used to make herbal supplements, such as capsules, tablets, and extracts, as well as teas and tinctures, although its use should be monitored under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

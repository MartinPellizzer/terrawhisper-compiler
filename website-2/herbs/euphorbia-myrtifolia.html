Euphorbia myrtifolia

Euphorbia myrtifolia, also known as the dwarf desert rose or Euphorbia, is a succulent plant species native to South Africa, characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, white flowers.

The plant is valued for its traditional medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects that can help alleviate symptoms of various health conditions, such as wounds, skin infections, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Euphorbia myrtifolia include flavonoids, alkaloids, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health conditions, including fever, sore throats, and digestive issues.

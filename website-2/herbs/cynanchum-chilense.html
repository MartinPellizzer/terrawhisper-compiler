Cynanchum chilense

Cynanchum chilense, also known as Chuco or Chuco root, is a plant species native to the Andean region, belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, cold, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help to alleviate symptoms and support the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Cynanchum chilense include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, such as chilocurine and chilocinine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Cynanchum chilense include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, coughs, and fever in traditional medicine.

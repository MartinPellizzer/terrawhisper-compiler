Cacalia saxicola

Cacalia saxicola, also known as mountain groundsel, is a perennial plant species in the Asteraceae family, characterized by its small, yellow daisy-like flowers and hairy leaves.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as wounds, fever, and respiratory issues. Bioactive constituents of Cacalia saxicola include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for wound healing and skin care.

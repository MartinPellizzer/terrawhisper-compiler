Elymus yushuensis

Elymus yushuensis, also known as Yushu wildrye, is a species of grass native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau in China.

This plant has been traditionally used in medicine to treat various ailments, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory effects, which can help alleviate conditions such as fever, cough, and sore throat. The bioactive constituents of Elymus yushuensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenoids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Elymus yushuensis is often prepared as a decoction or tea, as well as being used in the form of dried roots or rhizomes, to treat respiratory and gastrointestinal issues.

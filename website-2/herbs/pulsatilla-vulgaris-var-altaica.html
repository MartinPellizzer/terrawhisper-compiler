What is Pulsatilla vulgaris var. altaica?

Pulsatilla vulgaris var. altaica is a plant taxon that belongs to the family Ranunculaceae, a family of approximately 2,170 species of flowering plants.

This plant taxon is a variety of the species Pulsatilla vulgaris, characterized by its upright, leafless flower stalks that range in height from 20-40 cm. The flowers are an umbel-like cluster of 5-10 purple flowers with 5 petals and 5 sepals, and are typically in bloom from June to July. Pulsatilla vulgaris var. altaica is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, specifically in southern Kazakhstan and western Kyrgyzstan.

Its leaves are alternate, 2-4 cm long, and pinnate, with 3-5 pairs of leaflets.

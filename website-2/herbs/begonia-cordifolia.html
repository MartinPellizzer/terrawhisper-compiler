Begonia cordifolia

Begonia cordifolia, also known as the heart-leaf begonia, is a plant species that belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia.

The heart-leaf begonia is valued for its medicinal properties, which include its ability to treat fever, rheumatism, and inflammation due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, contributing to its use in traditional medicine for promoting overall health and well-being. The bioactive constituents of Begonia cordifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Begonia cordifolia is commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is also used in the form of a tincture or extract to treat various health conditions.

Arenaria campestris

Arenaria campestris, also known as sandwort, is a low-growing, annual or biennial plant species belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its potential in treating inflammatory and respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Arenaria campestris include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations, including infusions, tinctures, and decoctions, which are used to soothe respiratory issues and reduce inflammation.

Olea sativa

Olea sativa, commonly known as olive tree, is a perennial evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region, renowned for its rich fruit and medicinal properties.

The health benefits of Olea sativa include its ability to reduce inflammation, lower cholesterol levels, and exhibit antioxidant properties, which contribute to its potential in preventing or managing various health conditions such as heart disease and cancer. The bioactive constituents of Olea sativa include oleuropein, hydroxytyrosol, and tyrosol, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Olea sativa include olive leaf extract capsules, olive oil, and tea infusions, which are used to promote cardiovascular health and boost the immune system.

classification

Olea sativa is classified as a species of flowering plant in the family Oleaceae, which is a small family of flowering plants commonly known as the olive family. This family also includes other plants such as lilacs and forsythias. The Oleaceae family is part of the order Lamiales, which is a large and diverse order of flowering plants. The order Lamiales is further classified under the clade Asterids, which is a large clade of flowering plants that also includes the order Asterales. The clade Asterids is part of the larger group called the Angiosperms, which are flowering plants that produce seeds enclosed in fruits or flowers.

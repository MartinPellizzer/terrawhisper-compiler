Justicia columbiensis

Justicia columbiensis, also known as Colombian snakeweed, is a plant species in the Acanthaceae family, commonly found in the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities, making it useful in the treatment of various health issues such as fever, respiratory infections, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Justicia columbiensis include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is commonly prepared into teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used in topical applications to treat skin conditions and wounds.

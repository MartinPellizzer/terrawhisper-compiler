Sedum ludlowii

Sedum ludlowii, also known as Ludlow's stonecrop, is a succulent plant species native to the Himalayas, characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and ability to store water.

Bioactive constituents of Sedum ludlowii include saponins, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

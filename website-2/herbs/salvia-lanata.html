Salvia lanata

Salvia lanata, also known as Downy Sage, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and blue to purple flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its ability to reduce inflammation, alleviate respiratory issues, and exhibit antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Salvia lanata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Salvia lanata include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to support respiratory health and relieve coughs.

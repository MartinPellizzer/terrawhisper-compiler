Salix serissima

Salix serissima, also known as winter twig dogwood or autumnal willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

The bark and leaves of Salix serissima are used in traditional medicine, providing several health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to reduce pain, fever, and inflammation. The bioactive constituents of Salix serissima include flavonoids, salicin, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Salix serissima is often used to make salves, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health conditions, including colds, fever, and skin irritations.

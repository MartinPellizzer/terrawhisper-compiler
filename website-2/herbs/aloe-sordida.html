Aloe sordida

Aloe sordida, also known as the rough-leaved aloe or wild aloe, is a succulent plant species native to South Africa, characterized by its rough, mottled, and succulent leaves.

Aloe sordida has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including the treatment of skin irritations, wounds, and burns, as well as its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Aloe sordida include aloin, aloe-emodin, and various other anthraquinones, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Aloe sordida include gel and juice extracts, which are often used in topical creams, ointments, and oral supplements for skin care and wound healing.

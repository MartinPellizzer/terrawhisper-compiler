Pistacia gummifera

The bioactive constituents of Pistacia gummifera include mastic resin, which is rich in terpenes like alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, and limonene, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

The most common herbal preparations made with Pistacia gummifera include mastic resin powder, oil extracts, and gum, which are used in traditional medicine, food, and cosmetics, particularly for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

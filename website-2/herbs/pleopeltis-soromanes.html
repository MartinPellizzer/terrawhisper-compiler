Pleopeltis soromanes

Pleopeltis soromanes, also known as the spiny polypody, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Central and South America, characterized by its unique, spiny rachises and lance-shaped leaflets.

The spiny polypody has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its potential anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Pleopeltis soromanes include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal activities.

In traditional herbal medicine, the spiny polypody is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, or used in the form of a poultice or ointment to treat skin conditions and wounds.

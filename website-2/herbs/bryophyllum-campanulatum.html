Bryophyllum campanulatum

Bryophyllum campanulatum, also known as the bell-leaved Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant species belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and respiratory infections, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Bryophyllum campanulatum include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture to treat respiratory issues, and is also used in the form of ointments and creams to soothe skin irritations and wounds.

Hedyotis mexicana

Hedyotis mexicana, also known as Mexican Pennywort, is a perennial herb belonging to the family Rubiaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including fever, cough, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Hedyotis mexicana include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Hedyotis mexicana is often prepared as a tea or tincture to treat respiratory infections, fever, and other health conditions.

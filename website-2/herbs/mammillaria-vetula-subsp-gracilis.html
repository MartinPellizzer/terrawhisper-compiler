What is Mammillaria vetula subsp. gracilis?

Mammillaria vetula subsp.

gracilis is a succulent plant belonging to the family Cactaceae, characterized by its cylindrical stems with 18-20 low, rounded, white or pale yellow spines, and 1-2 central, straight spines. The stems are usually solitary, but occasionally produce offsets, and are green, sometimes tinged with red or purple.

Flowers are funnel-shaped, white or pale yellow, with a reddish or purple midrib, and appear in the summer months. The plant grows up to 15-30 cm in height and 8-15 cm in diameter, and has a short, white or pale yellow root system.

It is native to Mexico, where it grows in dry, rocky areas at elevations of 1,500-2,000 meters.

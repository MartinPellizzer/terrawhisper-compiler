Boehmeria paniculata

Boehmeria paniculata, also known as Chinese nettle or Chinese bronzewort, is a perennial plant native to East Asia and parts of the Pacific Islands.

The bioactive constituents of Boehmeria paniculata include flavonoids, triterpenoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Boehmeria paniculata is often used to make teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into herbal capsules and topical creams for its medicinal benefits.

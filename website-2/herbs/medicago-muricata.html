Medicago muricata

Medicago muricata, also known as prickly medic, is a perennial legume plant that belongs to the Fabaceae family.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and supporting the immune system. The bioactive constituents of M. muricata include flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Prickly medic is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and supplements, as well as being incorporated into traditional remedies and folk medicines.

classification

Medicago muricata is classified as a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae. It belongs to the genus Medicago, which includes several species of annual and perennial legumes. The genus Medicago is placed in the tribe Fabeae, which is a group of legumes that are typically herbaceous and have compound leaves. The tribe Fabeae is part of the subfamily Faboideae, which is a large and diverse group of legumes. This classification reflects the plant's evolutionary relationships and its position within the broader family of legumes.

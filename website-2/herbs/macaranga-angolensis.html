Macaranga angolensis

Macaranga angolensis, also known as the 'buffalo thorn', is a tree species native to Southern Africa.

The bioactive constituents of M. angolensis include flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to prepare infusions, decoctions, and ointments, which are used to treat various ailments, making it a valuable resource in folk medicine.

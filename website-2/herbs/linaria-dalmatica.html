Linaria dalmatica

Linaria dalmatica, commonly known as Dalmatian toadflax, is a perennial plant species belonging to the family Plantaginaceae, native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of Eastern Europe.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, offering potential health benefits in treating conditions such as wounds, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Linaria dalmatica include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Dalmatian toadflax is often used in herbal preparations, such as teas and infusions, as well as in tinctures and ointments, to leverage its therapeutic potential.

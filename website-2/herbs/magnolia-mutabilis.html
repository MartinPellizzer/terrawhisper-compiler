Magnolia mutabilis

Magnolia mutabilis, also known as the 'Yulan Magnolia', is a deciduous tree species native to southern China, characterized by its stunning white flowers that change color from white to pink as they age.

The bark and flowers of this plant have been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Magnolia mutabilis include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal value.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the bark and flowers of Magnolia mutabilis are often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, while in modern herbalism, it is commonly incorporated into capsules and tablets for its therapeutic benefits.

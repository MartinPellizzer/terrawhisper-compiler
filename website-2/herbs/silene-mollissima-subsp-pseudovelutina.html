What is Silene mollissima subsp. pseudovelutina?

Silene mollissima subsp.

pseudovelutina is a plant species within the family Caryophyllaceae, characterized by its hairy and densely pubescent stem, which can reach up to 30 cm in height. The leaves are lanceolate to elliptical in shape, with a length of 2-6 cm, and are covered with soft, velvety hairs.

The inflorescence is a terminal, axillary spike bearing pink to reddish-purple flowers, each with five petals and a prominent five-lobed calyx. The flowers are arranged in a whorled pattern, with 3-6 flowers per whorl.

This plant subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

Santalum lanaiense

Santalum lanaiense, also known as Hawaiian sandalwood, is a rare and endangered tree species native to the Hawaiian Islands, belonging to the sandalwood family (Santalaceae).

The plant is prized for its fragrant heartwood, which has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and perfumery due to its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that may help to soothe skin irritations and promote wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Hawaiian sandalwood include santalols, santalenes, and other sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its characteristic fragrance and therapeutic effects.

Traditional herbal preparations made from Hawaiian sandalwood include essential oils, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat a range of health issues, including anxiety, insomnia, and skin conditions.

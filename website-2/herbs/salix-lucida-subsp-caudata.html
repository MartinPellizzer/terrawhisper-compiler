What is Salix lucida subsp. caudata?

Salix lucida subsp.

caudata is a taxonomic subspecies of the plant species Salix lucida, belonging to the family Salicaceae. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its alternate, simple, and serrate leaves, measuring 3-8 cm in length, with a dark green color and a glossy appearance.

The leaves have a pointed tip and a winged petiole, with an abaxial pubescence. The catkins of Salix lucida subsp. caudata are dioecious, with male flowers measuring 2-4 cm in length and female flowers measuring 1-3 cm.

The plant typically grows in wet environments, such as stream banks and marshes, in the boreal regions of North America.

The main herbal preparations of Salix lucida subsp.

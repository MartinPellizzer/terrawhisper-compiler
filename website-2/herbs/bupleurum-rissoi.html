Bupleurum rissoi

Bupleurum rissoi, also known as Chinese thorowax, is a perennial plant in the family Apiaceae, native to China and used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties.

The plant is valued for its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and hepatoprotective effects, which have been associated with its use in treating conditions such as fever, viral infections, and liver disorders. The bioactive constituents of Bupleurum rissoi include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, including saikosaponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Bupleurum rissoi is commonly used in herbal preparations such as Si Wu Tang and Da Huang Mu Dan Pi Tang, which are often formulated to promote liver health and treat fever and viral infections.

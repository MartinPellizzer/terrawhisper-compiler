Genipa grandifolia

Genipa grandifolia, also known as the Genipa tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The Genipa tree has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including digestive problems, fever, and inflammation, due to its potential anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Genipa grandifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Genipa grandifolia include teas, infusions, and extracts, which are used to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of fever and inflammation.

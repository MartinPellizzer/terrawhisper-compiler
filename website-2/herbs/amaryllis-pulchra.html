Amaryllis pulchra

The Amaryllis pulchra, also known as the Naked Lady, is a perennial plant species in the Amaryllidaceae family, characterized by its strap-like leaves and white or pink funnel-shaped flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Amaryllis pulchra include alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins, including the notable compounds galantamine and lycorine, which have been found to possess neuroprotective and vasodilatory effects.

In traditional medicine, Amaryllis pulchra is often prepared as a tincture, decoction, or infusion, and is also used in modern herbal medicine to create supplements and extracts aimed at promoting cardiovascular health and cognitive function.

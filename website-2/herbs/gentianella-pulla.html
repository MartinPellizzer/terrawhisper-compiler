Gentianella pulla

Gentianella pulla, also known as Alpine Gentian, is a perennial herb belonging to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and narrow leaves.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including the stimulation of digestion and appetite, as well as its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Gentianella pulla include gentiopicroside, amarogentin, and loganic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Gentianella pulla is commonly prepared as a tincture, decoction, or tea, and is often used to treat digestive issues, such as indigestion and bloating.

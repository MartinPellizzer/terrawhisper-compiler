Achillea multifida

Achillea multifida, also known as fern-leaf yarrow or multifida yarrow, is a perennial herb belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its fern-like foliage and small white flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used to promote digestive health, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Achillea multifida include flavonoids, such as apigenin and luteolin, as well as sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

Achillea multifida is often used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into salves and ointments for topical applications.

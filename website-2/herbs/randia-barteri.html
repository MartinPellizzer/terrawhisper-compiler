Randia barteri

Randia barteri, also known as the African randia, is a medicinal plant species belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in West African folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, malaria, and gastrointestinal issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antimalarial properties. The bioactive constituents of Randia barteri include alkaloids, phenolic compounds, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction or infused in water to make teas, and it may also be used to make tinctures or dried extracts for oral consumption.

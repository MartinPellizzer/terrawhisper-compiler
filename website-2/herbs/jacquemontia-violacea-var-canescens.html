What is Jacquemontia violacea var. canescens?

Jacquemontia violacea var. canescens is a botanical variety that belongs to the family Convolvulaceae.

It is a climbing or twining plant that typically grows up to 2 meters in length, with stems that are slightly woody at the base and pubescent or hairy above. The leaves are opposite, elliptical to lanceolate in shape, 2-5 cm long, and have a pointed apex with a cuneate base, while the inflorescence is a cymose panicle and the flowers are small, trumpet-shaped, and blue-purple in color. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and has been introduced to other parts of the world as an invasive species.

The variety canescens is characterized by its hairy leaves and stems.

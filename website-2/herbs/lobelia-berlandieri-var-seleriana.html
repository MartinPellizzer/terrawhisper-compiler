What is Lobelia berlandieri var. seleriana?

Lobelia berlandieri var. seleriana is a plant species belonging to the family Campanulaceae.

It is a perennial herb with erect stems, 5-15 cm tall, and simple, alternate leaves that are lanceolate, 2-8 cm long, and 1-3 cm wide. The inflorescence is a raceme, bearing blue flowers, 2.5-3.5 cm long, with a 5-lobed calyx and a 5-lobed corolla. The flowers have 5 stamens and 2 styles, and the fruit is a capsule, containing numerous seeds.

This variety is endemic to Mexico and Guatemala, where it grows in subalpine and alpine zones, at elevations of 3,000-3,800 meters.

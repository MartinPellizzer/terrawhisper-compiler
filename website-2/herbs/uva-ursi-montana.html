Uva-ursi montana

Uva-ursi montana, also known as bearberry, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which have been shown to provide relief from urinary tract infections, diarrhea, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Uva-ursi montana include arbutin, hydroquinone, and ursolic acid, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Uva-ursi montana is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, and capsule, with the tea being one of the most popular herbal preparations, often used to treat urinary tract infections and promote urinary health.

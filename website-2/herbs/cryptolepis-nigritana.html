Cryptolepis nigritana

Cryptolepis nigritana, also known as the "Black Heart Plant", is a species of flowering plant in the Apocynaceae family, native to West Africa.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including the treatment of malaria, fever, and gastrointestinal disorders. The bioactive constituents of Cryptolepis nigritana include iridoid glycosides, such as secologanin, and other compounds like alkaloids and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal activities.

The most common herbal preparations made with Cryptolepis nigritana include decoctions, infusions, and capsules, which are used to treat a range of health conditions in traditional medicine.

Lycoctonum alboviolaceum

Lycoctonum alboviolaceum, also known as Aconitum lycoctonum or Wolf's Bane, is a perennial plant species native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its blue-purple flowers and tall, stately habit.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, offering potential health benefits such as pain relief, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, although its toxicity must be carefully managed. The bioactive constituents of Lycoctonum alboviolaceum include alkaloids like aconitine, lycoctonine, and aconitidine, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Lycoctonum alboviolaceum is often prepared as a tincture, infusion, or ointment, and is used to treat a range of conditions, including rheumatism, fever, and skin conditions.

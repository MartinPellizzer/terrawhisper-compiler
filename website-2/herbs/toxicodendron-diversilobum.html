The bioactive constituents of poison oak include urushiol, a resinous compound responsible for its allergenic properties, as well as salicylic acid, which has been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects.

Poison oak is often used to make herbal preparations such as salves, creams, and tinctures, which are typically used topically to treat skin conditions and wounds.

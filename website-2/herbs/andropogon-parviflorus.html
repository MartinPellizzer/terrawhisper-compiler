Andropogon parviflorus

Andropogon parviflorus, also known as Little Bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America, characterized by its blue-green foliage and feathery panicles.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Andropogon parviflorus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Little Bluestem include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to treat respiratory infections, fever, and gastrointestinal issues.

Ferula subvelutina

Ferula subvelutina, also known as Ferula, is a perennial plant species in the carrot family (Apiaceae), native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making it a potential treatment for various health issues such as wound healing, fever reduction, and gastrointestinal problems. The bioactive constituents of Ferula subvelutina include ferulic acid, umbelliferone, and sesquiterpene compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Ferula is commonly prepared as an oil, powder, or infusion, often used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, as well as skin conditions like eczema and acne.

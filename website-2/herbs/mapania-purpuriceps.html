Mapania purpuriceps

Mapania purpuriceps is a species of aquatic plant in the family Cyperaceae, commonly found in tropical regions and known for its medicinal properties.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Mapania purpuriceps include flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and poultices, which are used to treat a range of health problems.

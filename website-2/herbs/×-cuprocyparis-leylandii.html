× Cuprocyparis leylandii

× Cuprocyparis leylandii, commonly known as Leyland cypress, is a highly popular, fast-growing evergreen coniferous tree cultivar resulting from the cross between Cupressus macrocarpa and X Cupressus leylandii.

The essential oil extracted from the needles of Leyland cypress has been traditionally used to promote relaxation and reduce stress, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include borneol, camphene, and alpha-pinene, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Leyland cypress is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as essential oils, extracts, and teas, which are often utilized in aromatherapy and traditional medicine to promote overall well-being.

What is Rubus tomentosus var. ancophilus?

Rubus tomentosus var. ancophilus is a variety of the Rubus tomentosus species, belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is a thorny, deciduous shrub with dark brown, woody stems and a height range of 0.3-1.5 meters. The leaves are trifoliate, with leaflets having 5-7 serrated lobes, 5-10 centimeters long, and a green, hairy underside. The inflorescence is a corymb, bearing 2-5 flowers, each with 5 white petals and a yellow anther, occurring in May-June.

The fruit is a red, edible drupe, 1-1.5 centimeters in diameter, with one to two seeds per fruit.

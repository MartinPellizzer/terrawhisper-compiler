Sorghum piptatherum

Sorghum piptatherum, also known as broom sorghum, is a type of perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia, characterized by its fibrous stems and small yellow flowers.

The bioactive constituents of Sorghum piptatherum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

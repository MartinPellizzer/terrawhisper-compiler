Pyrus berberidifolia

Pyrus berberidifolia, also known as the Tibetan wild pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas and the Tibetan Plateau.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which have been shown to help with wound healing, fever reduction, and gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of Pyrus berberidifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Tibetan medicine, the plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often in combination with other medicinal plants, to treat a range of health conditions.

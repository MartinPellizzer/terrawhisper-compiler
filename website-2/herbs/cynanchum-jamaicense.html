Cynanchum jamaicense

Cynanchum jamaicense, also known as Jamaican dogbane or Jamaican milkweed, is a plant species belonging to the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its succulent stems and clusters of white or yellow flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its potential anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Cynanchum jamaicense include alkaloids, such as jamaicense and jamaicamine, as well as glycosides and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often made into teas, infusions, or tinctures, with the most common preparations being an infusion of the leaves and stems, often used to treat fever and rheumatism.

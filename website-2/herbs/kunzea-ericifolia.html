Kunzea ericifolia

Kunzea ericifolia, also known as White Kunzea or Tasmanian White Kunzea, is a small shrub native to Australia, belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it useful for wound healing, pain relief, and immune system support. The bioactive constituents of Kunzea ericifolia include essential oils, particularly Kunzea oil, which contains compounds such as pinene, limonene, and bornyl acetate.

Kunzea oil is commonly used in herbal preparations, including topical creams, ointments, and balms, as well as in aromatherapy and skincare products.

Myrrhodes alpestre

Myrrhodes alpestre, also known as alpine myrrh, is a perennial plant species native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean, characterized by its small yellow flowers and resinous stem.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, making it a valuable natural remedy for wound healing, skin conditions, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Myrrhodes alpestre include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Traditionally, alpine myrrh is used in herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and salves, which are often employed to soothe skin irritations, calm respiratory issues, and support wound healing.

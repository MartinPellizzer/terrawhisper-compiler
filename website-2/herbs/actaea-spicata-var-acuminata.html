What is Actaea spicata var. acuminata?

Actaea spicata var. acuminata is a plant variety in the family Ranunculaceae, native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial plant with a single stem, 30-120 cm tall, with two to five palmate leaves and numerous smaller, more divided leaves. The leaves have 3-7 leaflets, each 5-20 cm long, with a pointed tip and a serrated margin. The flowers are white, 4-8 mm in diameter, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring and early summer.

The plant produces a cluster of red berries, 5-8 mm long, in late summer and early fall.

These constituents contribute to the plant's reported use in traditional medicine for treating various ailments, including inflammation and pain.

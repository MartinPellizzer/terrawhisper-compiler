Pradosia brevipes

Pradosia brevipes, also known as the "pau ferro", is a species of plant in the family Sapotaceae, native to the tropical forests of South America.

The bark of Pradosia brevipes has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in the treatment of various ailments such as fever, pain, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Pradosia brevipes include flavonoids, alkaloids, and triterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the bark of Pradosia brevipes is made into teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health conditions, and is also used in the production of folk remedies such as poultices and ointments.

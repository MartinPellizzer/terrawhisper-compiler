Toxicodendron zeyheri

Toxicodendron zeyheri, also known as the African poison ivy, is a plant species belonging to the Anacardiaceae family.

Traditionally, it has been used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin infections, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Toxicodendron zeyheri include flavonoids, triterpenoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Toxicodendron zeyheri is often prepared as a tea or decoction, and it is also used in traditional remedies such as infusions and poultices to treat skin conditions and other ailments.

active constituents

Toxicodendron zeyheri contains urushiol, a resinous sap that is responsible for its toxic effects. Urushiol is a mixture of catecholic compounds, including alkylcatechols and alkylphenols. The resin contains a variety of isomers, which contribute to its toxicity. When the plant is damaged, the urushiol is released, causing an allergic reaction in sensitive individuals. This reaction can lead to skin irritation, blisters, and in severe cases, anaphylaxis.

side effects

Toxicodendron zeyheri, also known as the poison tree or poison oak tree, contains urushiol, an oil that can cause severe skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals. Symptoms of exposure may include redness, itching, blistering, and swelling of the skin. Prolonged exposure or sensitivity to the oil can lead to more severe reactions, such as hives, fever, and difficulty breathing. In rare cases, exposure to Toxicodendron zeyheri can cause anaphylaxis, a potentially life-threatening allergic reaction. If you suspect you have come into contact with the plant, it's essential to wash the affected area thoroughly with soap and water immediately.

Rosa bengalensis

Rosa bengalensis, also known as Indian rose or Bengal rose, is a species of rose plant native to the Himalayan region.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used to treat various health issues, including fever, inflammation, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Rosa bengalensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, Rosa bengalensis is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and ointments, which are used to treat a range of health conditions.

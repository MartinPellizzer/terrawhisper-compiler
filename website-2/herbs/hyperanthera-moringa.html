Hyperanthera moringa

Hyperanthera moringa, also known as Moringa, is a plant species belonging to the Moringaceae family, characterized by its long, slender branches and leaves rich in nutrients.

The leaves, bark, and seeds of the plant have been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to their numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which can help to reduce blood pressure, improve digestion, and boost the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Moringa include isothiocyanates, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Moringa include teas, capsules, and powders, which can be used to support overall health and well-being.

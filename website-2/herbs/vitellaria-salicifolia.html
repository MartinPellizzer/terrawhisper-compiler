Vitellaria salicifolia

Vitellaria salicifolia, also known as the shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to the savannas and woodlands of West Africa.

The shea tree has been traditionally used for its medicinal and cosmetic properties, offering health benefits such as wound healing, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, as well as being a rich source of antioxidants and emollients. The bioactive constituents of Vitellaria salicifolia include alkaloids, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal and therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from the shea tree include shea butter, shea oil, and herbal teas, which are often used to soothe skin irritations, promote skin health, and support wound healing.

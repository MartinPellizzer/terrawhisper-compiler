Valeriana pratensis

Valeriana pratensis, also known as meadow valerian or sweet-scented valerian, is a perennial herb belonging to the Valerianaceae family, characterized by its fragrant flowers and clusters of small, white blooms.

The plant has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to alleviate anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness due to its sedative properties, making it a popular natural remedy for promoting relaxation and improving sleep quality. The bioactive constituents of Valeriana pratensis include valerenic acid, valerenol, and isovaleric acid, which are responsible for its anxiolytic and sedative effects.

Valeriana pratensis is commonly prepared as a herbal tea, tincture, or infusion, and is also used in combination with other herbs in traditional remedies to enhance its therapeutic benefits.

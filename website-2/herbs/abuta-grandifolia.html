Abuta grandifolia

Abuta grandifolia, also known as "Guacá" or "Guacana", is a plant species native to the Amazon rainforest, belonging to the family Menispermaceae.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, and its health benefits include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-anxiety properties, which have been associated with its potential to alleviate symptoms of stress and anxiety disorders. The bioactive constituents of Abuta grandifolia include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, such as abutine, abutaline, and aporphine alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Abuta grandifolia is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is commonly used to calm the nervous system and promote relaxation.

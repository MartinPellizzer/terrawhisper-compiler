Desmodium mexicanum

Desmodium mexicanum, also known as Mexican tick trefoil, is a perennial legume native to the Americas, commonly used for its medicinal and ornamental properties.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin irritations, due to its anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and antibacterial properties. The bioactive constituents of Desmodium mexicanum include flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids, which have been shown to possess antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory activities.

The most common herbal preparations made with Desmodium mexicanum include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to alleviate symptoms associated with fever, arthritis, and skin conditions.

Ferula graveolens

Ferula graveolens, commonly known as asafoetida or ferula, is a perennial plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia, characterized by its yellow or white latex and strong, pungent odor.

The health benefits of ferula include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to aid digestion, reduce flatulence, and alleviate respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of ferula include ferulic acid, umbelliferone, and asafoetida, which are responsible for its medicinal properties and distinct aroma.

Ferula is commonly used in herbal preparations such as asafoetida powder, tea, and oil, as well as in traditional medicine and culinary applications in the Middle East and South Asia.

Narcissus × conspicuus

Narcissus × conspicuus, commonly known as the Lycoris or Hurricane Lily, is a hybrid plant in the Amaryllidaceae family, characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits, including its potential to lower blood pressure, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Narcissus × conspicuus include alkaloids, saponins, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often used to treat various ailments, although its use should be approached with caution due to potential toxicity.

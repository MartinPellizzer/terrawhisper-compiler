Trachyspermum dichotomum

Trachyspermum dichotomum, also known as ajwain or bishop's weed, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent and the Middle East, belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

The bioactive constituents of Trachyspermum dichotomum include essential oils, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, ajwain is commonly used to prepare herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and oils, which are used to treat a range of health conditions.

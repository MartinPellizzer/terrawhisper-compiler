What is Rosa communis var. disparilis?

Rosa communis var. disparilis is a botanical variety within the species Rosa communis, belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems, typically growing up to 2-3 meters in height and 1-2 meters in width. The leaves are pinnate with 5-7 leaflets, each measuring 2-5 centimeters in length, and have serrated margins. The flowers are pink to red, cupped, and consist of 5-7 petals, measuring 3-5 centimeters in diameter, and are produced in late spring to early summer.

The fruit is a red, fleshy hip, containing 2-3 seeds, typically ripening in late autumn.

Sedum vulgare

Sedum vulgare, also known as Orpine or Wild Sedum, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

Traditionally, Sedum vulgare has been used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Sedum vulgare include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made from Sedum vulgare include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, wounds, and skin conditions.

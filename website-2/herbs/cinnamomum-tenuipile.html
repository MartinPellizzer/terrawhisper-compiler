Cinnamomum tenuipile

Cinnamomum tenuipile, also known as the "Rough-barked cinnamon tree," is a species of evergreen tree in the family Lauraceae, native to tropical Asia.

The bioactive constituents of Cinnamomum tenuipile include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Cinnamomum tenuipile is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or extract, and is often used to treat respiratory issues, such as coughs and bronchitis, as well as to aid digestion and reduce fever.

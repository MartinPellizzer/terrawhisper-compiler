Cassia bahiae

Cassia bahiae, also known as Bahamabe, is a shrub-like plant species in the Fabaceae family, native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities, which may help in treating various conditions such as fever, colds, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Cassia bahiae include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Cassia bahiae is often used to make teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as topical preparations such as creams and ointments, to harness its therapeutic benefits.

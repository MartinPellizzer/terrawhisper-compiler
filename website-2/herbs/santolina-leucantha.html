Santolina leucantha

Santolina leucantha, also known as Grey Cotton Bush or Cotton Lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region, valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, which may help to soothe skin irritations, wounds, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Santolina leucantha include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Santolina leucantha is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health issues, including respiratory problems, skin conditions, and menstrual disorders.

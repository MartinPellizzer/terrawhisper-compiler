Mahonia harrisoniana

Mahonia harrisoniana, also known as Oregon grape, is a type of flowering plant in the barberry family (Berberidaceae) native to the Pacific Northwest region of North America.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including digestive problems, skin conditions, and infections, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Mahonia harrisoniana include berberine, a plant alkaloid known for its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory effects, as well as other isoquinoline alkaloids such as berbamine and oxyberberine.

Herbal preparations made from Mahonia harrisoniana include teas, tinctures, and capsules, which are often used to support immune function, reduce inflammation, and promote overall health and well-being.

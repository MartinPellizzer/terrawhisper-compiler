Ranunculus penicillatus

Ranunculus penicillatus, also known as the hooked buttercup, is a type of aquatic plant that grows in shallow water and is characterized by its distinctive hooked sepals.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, and has been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as skin conditions and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of R. penicillatus include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, R. penicillatus is often prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is used to treat conditions such as bronchitis and eczema.

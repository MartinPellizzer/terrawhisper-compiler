Lens orientalis

Lens orientalis, also known as Indian chickpea or oriental lentil, is an annual plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include reducing inflammation, improving blood sugar control, and exhibiting antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Lens orientalis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as teas, decoctions, and infusions, which are often used to alleviate symptoms of diabetes, digestive issues, and other health conditions.

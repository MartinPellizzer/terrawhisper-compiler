Blumea javanica

Blumea javanica, also known as Java thistle, is a perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Asia, characterized by its thistle-like flowers and leaves.

The bioactive constituents of Blumea javanica include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Blumea javanica is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat various health conditions, and is also used in traditional Asian medicine to promote overall well-being.

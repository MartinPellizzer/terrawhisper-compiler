Asclepias peltigera

Asclepias peltigera, commonly known as the pleated milkweed or green antelopehorn milkweed, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various ailments, including fever reduction, skin conditions, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Asclepias peltigera include cardenolides, terpenoids, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Asclepias peltigera include teas, infusions, and tinctures, although its use is not recommended without proper medical guidance due to potential toxicity.

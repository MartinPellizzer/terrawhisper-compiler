Ficus nota

The Ficus nota, also known as the Banyan fig, is a tropical plant species belonging to the family Moraceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine, offering various health benefits such as wound healing, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, making it a valuable treatment for skin conditions, digestive issues, and fever. The bioactive constituents of Ficus nota include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Ficus nota include decoctions, infusions, and powders, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, as well as being used in traditional rituals and ceremonies.

Senna nitida

Senna nitida, also known as the smooth senna, is a shrub-like plant species in the Fabaceae family, native to tropical Africa.

The leaves and pods of Senna nitida have been used in traditional medicine for their laxative and anti-inflammatory properties, providing relief from constipation, diarrhea, and various gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of Senna nitida include anthraquinones, such as sennosides A and B, which contribute to its purgative effects, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids that possess antioxidant properties.

In herbal medicine, Senna nitida is often used to make teas, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat digestive problems, reduce inflammation, and support overall gut health.

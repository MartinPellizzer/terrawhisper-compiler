Chrysanthemum fontanesii

Chrysanthemum fontanesii, also known as Korean chrysanthemum or yamamoto-gasa, is a flowering plant species in the daisy family (Asteraceae) native to East Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine, where it is used to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Chrysanthemum fontanesii include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant are infusions and teas, which are used to treat respiratory issues, fever, and other health conditions.

Selinum copticum

Selinum copticum, also known as Egyptian parsley or Copticum parsley, is a plant species belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is native to the Middle East and North Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its health benefits, which include the treatment of various conditions such as fever, colds, and digestive issues due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Selinum copticum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Selinum copticum is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being dried and used as a powder or tincture.

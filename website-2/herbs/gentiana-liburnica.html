Gentiana liburnica

Gentiana liburnica, also known as Liburnia gentian, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Gentianaceae family, native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of Central Europe.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to stimulate digestion, alleviate symptoms of dyspepsia, and exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities, which are attributed to its health benefits. The bioactive constituents of Gentiana liburnica include iridoids, such as gentiopicrin and amarogentin, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Gentiana liburnica is commonly processed into tinctures, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat digestive issues, respiratory problems, and as a general tonic to promote overall well-being.

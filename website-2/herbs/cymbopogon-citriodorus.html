Cymbopogon citriodorus

Cymbopogon citriodorus, commonly known as lemongrass, is a tropical grass plant native to Asia and Africa, characterized by its distinctive citrus scent and long, slender leaves.

The health benefits of lemongrass include its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties, which have been traditionally used to relieve digestive issues, reduce fever, and alleviate pain. The bioactive constituents of lemongrass include citral, a compound responsible for its citrus aroma, as well as geraniol and limonene, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Lemongrass is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, essential oils, and infusions, as well as in culinary and aromatherapy applications.

Lippia sarmentosa

Lippia sarmentosa, also known as Mexican oregano or Mexican marjoram, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America, characterized by its small white flowers and pungent, aromatic leaves.

The health benefits of Lippia sarmentosa include its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as wounds, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of this plant include volatile oils, particularly carvacrol and thymol, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

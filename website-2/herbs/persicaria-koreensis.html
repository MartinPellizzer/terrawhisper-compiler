Persicaria koreensis

Persicaria koreensis, also known as Korean knotweed, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Polygonaceae family, native to East Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiviral activities, which may help alleviate symptoms of conditions such as arthritis and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Persicaria koreensis include phenolic compounds like flavonoids and polyphenolic acids, as well as iridoids and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, Persicaria koreensis is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat fever, cough, and other respiratory issues.

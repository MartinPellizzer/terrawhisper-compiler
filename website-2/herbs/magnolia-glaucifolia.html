Magnolia glaucifolia

Magnolia glaucifolia, also known as the Roughbark Magnolia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Magnoliaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, particularly in Asia, for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which are associated with its potential health benefits in treating various conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of Magnolia glaucifolia include magnolol, honokiol, and 4-O-methylhonokiol, which are known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant include extracts, teas, and capsules, which are often used in traditional Chinese medicine to promote relaxation, reduce stress, and alleviate respiratory issues.

side effects

Mandioca aipi, also known as cassava or manioc, has been associated with potential health risks due to its toxicity. The plant contains naturally occurring compounds like cyanogenic glycosides, which can release cyanide when ingested. Consuming large amounts of cassava can lead to cyanide poisoning, causing symptoms such as headaches, dizziness, and nausea. Cooking and processing can reduce the levels of these toxins, but improper preparation or excessive consumption may still cause harm. Pregnant women and individuals with certain medical conditions should exercise caution when consuming cassava products.

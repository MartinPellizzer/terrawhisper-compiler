Cajuputi

Cajuputi, also known as Melaleuca cajuputi, is a type of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and Australia, belonging to the myrtle family (Myrtaceae).

The health benefits of Cajuputi include its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as skin conditions, respiratory issues, and fever. The bioactive constituents of Cajuputi include compounds like cajuputol, cajuputol acetate, and limonene, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, Cajuputi is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as essential oils, teas, and infusions, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including skin infections, wounds, and respiratory problems.

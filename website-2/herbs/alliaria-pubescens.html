Alliaria pubescens

Alliaria pubescens, commonly known as hairy garlic mustard or wild garlic mustard, is a perennial herb native to Europe and Asia, now naturalized in North America.

This plant has been used for its health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which have been shown to help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Alliaria pubescens include isothiocyanates, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Alliaria pubescens include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to treat respiratory issues and as a digestive aid.

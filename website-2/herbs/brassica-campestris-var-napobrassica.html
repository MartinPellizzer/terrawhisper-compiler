What is Brassica campestris var. napobrassica?

Brassica campestris var. napobrassica is an annual or biennial plant that belongs to the family Brassicaceae.

It has a rosette of leaves, which are simple, alternate, and pinnately lobed, with a glabrous or pubescent surface. The leaves are 10-30 cm long and 5-15 cm wide, and they have a crenate or dentate margin. The plant produces a raceme of yellow flowers, which are 2-3 cm in diameter and have 4 petals.

The seeds are brown, 2-3 mm long, and have a linear or fusiform shape.

The glucosinolates, specifically, can cause digestive discomfort and may also interact with certain medications.

Moreover, improper handling and preparation of the plant can lead to the release of these compounds, exacerbating the potential side effects.

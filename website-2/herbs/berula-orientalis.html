Berula orientalis

Berula orientalis, also known as the Oriental water-crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Berula orientalis include alkaloids, flavonoids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Berula orientalis is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into ointments and salves for topical application.

Olea undulata

Olea undulata, also known as the undulate olive tree, is a small, evergreen tree native to South Africa.

The leaves of the Olea undulata have been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive problems, due to their antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which contribute to its health benefits. The bioactive constituents of Olea undulata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The leaves of this plant are commonly used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various ailments, and are also used in traditional medicine in the form of a decoction.

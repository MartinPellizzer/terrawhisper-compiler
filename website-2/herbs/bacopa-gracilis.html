Bacopa gracilis

Bacopa gracilis, also known as Brahmi or Bacopa, is a plant species native to Southeast Asia, belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to improve memory, cognitive function, and mental clarity, with potential benefits including enhanced learning capacity, improved mood, and reduced stress levels. The bioactive constituents of Bacopa gracilis include bacosides, saponins, and alkaloids, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Bacopa gracilis include capsules, tablets, and teas, as well as extracts and powders used in supplements and traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

What is Camellia sinensis?

Camellia sinensis is an evergreen shrub or small tree belonging to the family Theaceae.

It has simple, alternate, and elliptical leaves that are 4-10 cm long, with serrated margins and a leathery texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, with five petals and numerous stamens. The flowers are followed by oval, brown capsules containing 2-4 seeds.

Camellia sinensis is native to Asia, specifically the mountainous regions of China, India, and Southeast Asia.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Camellia sinensis are derived from the leaves of the plant and involve varying levels of oxidation to produce distinct types of tea.

White tea, for instance, is minimally processed and harvested before the leaves have a chance to oxidize, retaining its delicate flavor and nutritional content. Oolong tea, on the other hand, is partially fermented, allowing it to retain some of the antioxidants and polyphenols found in green tea, while also developing a more complex flavor profile. Green tea, which is also minimally oxidized, is typically steamed or pan-fried to halt the fermentation process, preserving its high levels of catechins and other beneficial compounds.

Pu-erh tea and matcha, which are both derived from the leaves of Camellia sinensis, are distinguished by their unique production processes, with pu-erh tea being fermented and aged to produce a rich, earthy flavor, while matcha is ground into a fine powder and whisked into a bright green, energizing beverage.

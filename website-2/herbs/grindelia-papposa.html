Grindelia papposa

Grindelia papposa, also known as gumweed, is a perennial plant species in the daisy family (Asteraceae) that is native to North America.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, and is said to have health benefits such as soothing respiratory issues, reducing fever, and treating skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Grindelia papposa include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Grindelia papposa is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and salves, and is often employed in traditional medicine to treat conditions ranging from bronchitis to eczema.

Barosma crenata

Barosma crenata is a shrub native to South Africa, belonging to the Rutaceae family and commonly known as the Cape pepper or wild pepper.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its health benefits, which include antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, potentially aiding in the treatment of various ailments such as fever, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. Bioactive constituents present in Barosma crenata include essential oils, flavonoids, and limonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to relieve symptoms of colds, coughs, and digestive discomfort.

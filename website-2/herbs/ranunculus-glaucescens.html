Ranunculus glaucescens

Ranunculus glaucescens, also known as buttercup, is a perennial plant species that belongs to the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects, which can help to reduce pain and promote wound healing, making it beneficial in treating various health issues. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus glaucescens include alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a tincture or infused in teas, and is also used in topical creams and ointments to treat skin conditions such as eczema and acne.

Calamintha origanifolia

Calamintha origanifolia, also known as sweet calamint, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and native to the Mediterranean region.

The bioactive constituents of Calamintha origanifolia include volatile oils, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, with the primary essential oil being camphor.

In herbal medicine, Calamintha origanifolia is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for its skin-soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

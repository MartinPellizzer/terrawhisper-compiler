Agallochum malaccense

Agallochum malaccense, also known as Agarwood, is a type of resinous heartwood that forms in certain species of trees, particularly in the genus Aquilaria, in response to infection by certain types of mold or bacteria.

The bioactive constituents of Agarwood include sesquiterpenes, phenolic compounds, and other volatile organic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Agarwood is commonly prepared in the form of essential oils, resin extracts, and herbal teas, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, as well as being used in aromatherapy and perfumery.

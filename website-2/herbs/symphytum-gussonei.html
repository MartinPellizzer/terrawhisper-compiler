Symphytum gussonei

Symphytum gussonei, also known as Gussone's comfrey or blue-violet comfrey, is a perennial plant in the borage family (Boraginaceae) native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties, making it beneficial for treating sprains, strains, and other musculoskeletal issues. The bioactive constituents of Symphytum gussonei include allantoin, rosmarinic acid, and triterpenoid saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Common herbal preparations made from this plant include tinctures, infusions, and topical creams, which are often used to promote tissue repair and reduce pain and inflammation.

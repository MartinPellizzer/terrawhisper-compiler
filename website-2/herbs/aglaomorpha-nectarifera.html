Aglaomorpha nectarifera

Aglaomorpha nectarifera, also known as Nectar-ferning fern or Golden Maidenhair, is a species of fern native to Asia, belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of digestive disorders, respiratory issues, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Aglaomorpha nectarifera include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and polysaccharides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Aglaomorpha nectarifera is often prepared as a decoction, tea, or tincture to take advantage of its health benefits, and it is also used in modern herbal supplements and topical creams.

Daphne tinctoria

Daphne tinctoria, commonly known as purple or purple-flowered daphne, is a perennial plant species native to Europe and Western Asia, characterized by its purple flowers and purple berries.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including wound healing, inflammation, and as an antiseptic agent due to its antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Daphne tinctoria include daphnetin, daphnodorin, and daphniphylline, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Daphne tinctoria is often used to prepare tinctures, infusions, and poultices, which are commonly used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and other inflammatory disorders.

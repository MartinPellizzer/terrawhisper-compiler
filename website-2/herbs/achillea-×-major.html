Achillea × major

Achillea × major, commonly known as yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant in the daisy family, characterized by its fern-like foliage and white or yellow flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to its purported health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antispasmodic properties, which can aid in wound healing, reducing fever, and alleviating digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of yarrow include sesquiterpene lactones, such as achillin and dihydroachillin, as well as essential oils like borneol and camphor.

The most common herbal preparations made with yarrow include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which can be used to treat various ailments, including colds, flu, and skin conditions.

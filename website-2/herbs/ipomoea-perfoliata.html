Ipomoea perfoliata

Ipomoea perfoliata, also known as "beebush" or "wild potato", is a perennial vine native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and purple flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate conditions such as arthritis, fever, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea perfoliata include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Ipomoea perfoliata is often prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is also used in topical applications, such as salves and creams, to treat skin conditions and wounds.

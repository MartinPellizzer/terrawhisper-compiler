Urechites jamaicensis

Urechites jamaicensis, also known as Jamaica dogwood, is a plant species native to the Caribbean region, belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

The health benefits of Urechites jamaicensis include its traditional use as an anxiolytic and sedative to treat anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness, as well as its antispasmodic properties to alleviate muscle cramps and spasms. The bioactive constituents of this plant include alkaloids, including urechitine, jamaicamine, and isoretronydine, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Urechites jamaicensis include teas, tinctures, and infusions, often used to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

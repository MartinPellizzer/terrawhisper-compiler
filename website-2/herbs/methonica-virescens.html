Methonica virescens

Methonica virescens, commonly known as "Wild Rue" or "Spanish Rue", is a flowering plant in the family Boraginaceae.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions, including digestive issues and anxiety, due to its reported sedative and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Methonica virescens include alkaloids, such as scopoletin and scopoletin glucoside, which are believed to be responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Methonica virescens is often prepared as a tea or infusion, or used in combination with other herbs in capsules or tinctures, although its use is not without controversy due to potential toxicity.

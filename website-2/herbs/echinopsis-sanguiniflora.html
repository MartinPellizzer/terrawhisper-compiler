Echinopsis sanguiniflora

Echinopsis sanguiniflora, also known as the Red Torch or Red Torch Cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to South America, characterized by its vibrant red flowers and unique, columnar shape.

The plant is used in traditional medicine and has been found to possess several health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of colds, fever, and other respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Echinopsis sanguiniflora include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, such as echinopsin, sanguinarine, and berberine, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Echinopsis sanguiniflora is typically prepared as a decoction, tea, or tincture, which can be used to treat respiratory infections, fever, and other conditions.

Lycopodium carolinum

Lycopodium carolinum, also known as Wolf's Bane or Carolina Club Moss, is a type of lycopodiaceous plant, belonging to the genus Lycopodium, which is a group of small, non-vascular, and spore-producing plants.

The health benefits of Lycopodium carolinum include its potential to aid in digestion, relieve respiratory issues, and exhibit antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include alkaloids, terpenoids, and glycosides, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Lycopodium carolinum is commonly prepared as a tincture, decoction, or powder, often used to treat a range of conditions including indigestion, coughs, and respiratory problems.

classification

Lycopodium carolinum is classified in the kingdom Plantae, and more specifically in the division Lycopodiophyta. It belongs to the class Lycopodiopsida and the order Lycopodiales. The family to which it belongs is Lycopodiaceae. Lycopodium carolinum is further classified as Lycopodium carolinum, with no subspecies. This classification places it within the group of club mosses, a type of ancient vascular plant.

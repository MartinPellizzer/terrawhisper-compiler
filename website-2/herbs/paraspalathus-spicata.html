Paraspalathus spicata

Paraspalathus spicata, commonly known as the "Cape pea bush," is a perennial shrub native to South Africa, belonging to the Fabaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties, which have been shown to help alleviate symptoms of fever, wounds, and digestive issues. Bioactive constituents of P. spicata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for wound care and skin health.

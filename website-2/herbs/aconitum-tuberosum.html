Aconitum tuberosum

Aconitum tuberosum, also known as Monk's hood or Wolfsbane, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Aconitum genus, native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its health benefits, including analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antipyretic properties, which can help alleviate pain, reduce fever, and treat various respiratory and gastrointestinal disorders. The bioactive constituents of Aconitum tuberosum include alkaloids, such as aconitine, mesaconitine, and hypaconitine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Aconitum tuberosum is often prepared as a decoction or a tincture, which can be used to treat a range of health conditions, although its use requires careful dosing due to its potential toxicity.

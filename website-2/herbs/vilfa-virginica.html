Vilfa virginica

Vilfa virginica, also known as Mountain Woodruff or Virgin Woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America, belonging to the family Poaceae.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antifungal properties, providing health benefits such as wound healing, reduction of fever, and alleviation of respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Vilfa virginica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

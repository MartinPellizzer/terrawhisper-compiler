Nasturtium alpinum

Nasturtium alpinum, also known as Alpine cress or Indian cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its bright green leaves and delicate, edible flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, offering health benefits such as reducing inflammation, treating wounds, and protecting against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Nasturtium alpinum include isothiocyanates, alkaloids, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Alpine cress is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health conditions, including respiratory issues and skin irritations.

What is Helianthemum cinereum subsp. rubellum?

Helianthemum cinereum subsp.

rubellum is a subspecies of flowering plant in the family Cistaceae, native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a low-growing, shrubby perennial with woody stems and small, ovate leaves, typically 5-10 mm in length, with a ciliate margin.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers with five petals, borne in terminal clusters, typically 10-20 mm in diameter. The flowers are actinomorphic, with a five-lobed corolla and a prominent calyx of five, green sepals. Helianthemum cinereum subsp.

rubellum is a member of the Helianthemum cinereum species complex, which is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems.

Salacia capilliflora

Salacia capilliflora, also known as Bitter Chai or Indian Clubberry, is a plant species that belongs to the family Celastraceae.

This plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its potential health benefits, including lowering blood sugar levels, reducing cholesterol, and aiding in weight management, due to its ability to regulate glucose and lipid metabolism. The bioactive constituents of Salacia capilliflora include salacin compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties, as well as other bioactive molecules such as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, Salacia capilliflora is commonly prepared into herbal teas, capsules, and tablets to leverage its health benefits.

classification

Salacia capilliflora is classified within the family Celastraceae. This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the world, particularly in Africa and Asia. The species is part of the genus Salacia, which comprises several species of flowering plants. Salacia capilliflora is a climbing shrub with small, white or yellow flowers and red or purple fruit. As a member of the Celastraceae family, it is closely related to other genera such as Euonymus and Pteropodon.

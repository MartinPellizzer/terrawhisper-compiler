Senegalia mattogrossensis

Senegalia mattogrossensis, also known as the "Mattos tree", is a species of plant in the family Fabaceae, native to the tropical regions of South America.

The plant is valued for its traditional medicinal uses, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may aid in the treatment of various diseases and conditions. The bioactive constituents of Senegalia mattogrossensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Senegalia mattogrossensis is often used to make teas, infusions, and decoctions, which are used to treat conditions such as fever, gastrointestinal disorders, and respiratory issues.

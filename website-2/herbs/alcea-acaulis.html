Alcea acaulis

Alcea acaulis, also known as the stemless hollyhock, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Malvaceae family, characterized by its tall spikes of showy, pink to purple flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of arthritis, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Alcea acaulis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, such as aucubin, which are responsible for the plant's medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Alcea acaulis is often prepared as a tincture or decoction, and is used to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and skin irritations.

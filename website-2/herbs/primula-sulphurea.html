Primula sulphurea

Primula sulphurea, also known as Sulphur Flower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Primulaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making it useful in treating various health conditions such as wounds, skin infections, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Primula sulphurea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

Primula sulphurea is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to treat coughs, colds, and bronchitis.

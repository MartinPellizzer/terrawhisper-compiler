Vigna esculenta

Vigna esculenta, commonly known as mung bean, is a type of legume native to Asia and a popular ingredient in many traditional cuisines.

The mung bean has been valued for its health benefits, which include lowering cholesterol levels, improving digestion, and aiding in the management of blood sugar levels. The bioactive constituents of mung bean include isoflavones, saponins, and oligosaccharides, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, mung bean is often prepared as a decoction or a paste, and is commonly used to make herbal supplements, teas, and soups.

classification

Vigna esculenta is a species of legume, also known as the mung bean or green gram. It is a member of the family Fabaceae and the genus Vigna. Within the genus, Vigna esculenta is classified as a type of bush bean or green bean. The species is native to South Asia and Southeast Asia, where it has been cultivated for thousands of years. Vigna esculenta is a versatile crop that is used for both its edible seeds and its green pods.

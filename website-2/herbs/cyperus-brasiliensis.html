Cyperus brasiliensis

Cyperus brasiliensis, also known as the Brazilian papyrus or capim capa, is a type of sedge plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help alleviate conditions such as fever, respiratory issues, and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Cyperus brasiliensis include flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Cyperus brasiliensis include teas, infusions, and extracts, which are often used to treat respiratory and skin-related ailments, as well as to boost the immune system.

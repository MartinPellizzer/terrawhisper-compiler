Polypodium henryi

Polypodium henryi, also known as the Chinese spleenwort, is a species of fern that belongs to the Polypodiaceae family.

Traditionally used in Chinese medicine, the plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiviral properties, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as fever, cough, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Polypodium henryi include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Polypodium henryi is often made into herbal preparations such as teas, decoctions, and infusions, as well as being used in combination with other herbs in patent medicines to enhance their efficacy.

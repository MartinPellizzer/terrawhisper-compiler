Pandaca crassifolia

Pandaca crassifolia, also known as Pandan, is a tropical plant species native to Southeast Asia, belonging to the Pandanaceae family.

Pandan leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and lowering blood sugar levels. The bioactive constituents of Pandan leaves include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Pandan leaves are commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat various health conditions, including diabetes and digestive issues.

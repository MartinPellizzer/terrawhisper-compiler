Lychnis indica

Lychnis indica, also known as Lychnis or Indian Lychnis, is a flowering plant in the evening primrose family (Onagraceae), native to the Himalayan regions.

The health benefits of Lychnis indica include its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as fever, cough, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Lychnis indica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Lychnis indica include teas, infusions, and decoctions, which are often used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory and skin-related issues.

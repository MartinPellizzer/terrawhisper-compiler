Vallaris indica

Vallaris indica, also known as the "Indian pipe cactus," is a perennial aquatic plant native to India and Sri Lanka, characterized by its waxy stems and white, trumpet-shaped flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, with reported health benefits including the treatment of fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, as well as its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Vallaris indica include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, the plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations, including decoctions, infusions, and ointments, which are used to treat a range of health conditions.

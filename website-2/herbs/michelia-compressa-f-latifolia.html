What is Michelia compressa f. latifolia?

Michelia compressa f. latifolia is a botanical variant of the Michelia compressa species within the Magnoliaceae family.

It is characterized by elliptical to lanceolate leaves, 10-20 cm long and 3-6 cm wide, with a leathery texture and a cuneate base. The leaves are dark green on the upper surface and light green on the lower surface, with a glabrous and coriaceous texture. Michelia compressa f. latifolia produces small, yellowish-green flowers, 2-5 cm in diameter, with 6-12 petals and a long peduncle.

The flowers are borne in axillary or terminal panicles, often densely clustering at the branch tips.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Michelia compressa f. latifolia are infusion, tea, tincture, salve and decoction.

Tincture is a concentrated liquid extract made by soaking the plant in a solvent, used to treat a range of health issues including infections and inflammation. Salve is a topical preparation made from the plant's leaves and used to treat skin conditions such as wounds and eczema, while decoction is a more concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant in water, used to treat digestive issues and promote overall health.

These preparations are used due to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

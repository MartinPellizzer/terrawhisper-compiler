Begonia tuberosa

Begonia tuberosa, also known as Begonia root or Begonia tuber, is a type of rhizomatous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help to reduce pain and inflammation, as well as prevent the growth of bacteria and other pathogens. The bioactive constituents of Begonia tuberosa include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Begonia tuberosa is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is commonly used to treat a range of ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions.

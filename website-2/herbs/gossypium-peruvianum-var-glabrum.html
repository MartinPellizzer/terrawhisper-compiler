What is Gossypium peruvianum var. glabrum?

Gossypium peruvianum var. glabrum is a plant species belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is a perennial herb with a simple, alternate, and stipulate leaf structure, featuring 3-7 leaflets and a cordate base. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with 5 petals and 5 stamens, arranged in a corymbiform inflorescence. Gossypium peruvianum var. glabrum is characterized by its glabrous stems and leaves, which distinguish it from the glaberrimum variety.

It is native to the Andean region of South America and shares characteristics with other Gossypium species, including a cotton-like seed fiber.

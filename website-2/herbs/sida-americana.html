Sida americana

Sida americana, also known as country mallow, is a perennial plant species native to the Americas, characterized by its sprawling habit and small, white or pink flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions including wounds, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Sida americana include flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Country mallow is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or decoction, and is also used in traditional medicine to make topical creams, salves, and ointments for skin conditions and wound healing.

Eleutherococcus setchuenensis

Eleutherococcus setchuenensis, also known as Acanthopanax senticosus or Eleuthero, is a type of shrub native to China, typically found in mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including enhancing physical performance, reducing stress and fatigue, and boosting the immune system, making it a popular adaptogenic herb in traditional Chinese medicine. The bioactive constituents of Eleutherococcus setchuenensis include eleutherosides, lignans, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Eleuthero is commonly prepared as a tincture, tea, or capsule, as well as being used in combination with other herbs in traditional formulas, such as the Chinese patent medicine, Kangfuxianling.

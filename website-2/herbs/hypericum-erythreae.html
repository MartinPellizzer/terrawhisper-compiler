Hypericum erythreae

Hypericum erythreae, also known as Eritrean St. John's Wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the highlands of Eritrea and Ethiopia, belonging to the St. John's Wort family (Hypericaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including anxiety, insomnia, and skin wounds, due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Hypericum erythreae include hypericin, hyperforin, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and topical preparations, such as creams and ointments, which are used to treat skin conditions and wounds.

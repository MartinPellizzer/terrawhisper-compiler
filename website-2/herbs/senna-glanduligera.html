Senna glanduligera

Senna glanduligera, also known as Cassia auriculata, is a plant species belonging to the legume family and is commonly used as a medicinal and herbal remedy.

The plant is known for its potential health benefits, including treating constipation, diarrhea, and other gastrointestinal issues, due to its laxative properties. The bioactive constituents of Senna glanduligera include flavonoids, glycosides, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Senna glanduligera is commonly prepared as a decoction, tea, or powder, and is often used in herbal remedies to treat digestive issues, as well as in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine.

Salix arborea

Salix arborea, also known as the crack willow, is a deciduous tree species belonging to the willow family (Salicaceae).

The bark and leaves of Salix arborea have been used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of conditions such as eczema, wounds, and fever. The bioactive constituents of Salix arborea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and salicylic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Salix arborea include teas, infusions, and salves, which are used to treat skin conditions, promote wound healing, and reduce inflammation.

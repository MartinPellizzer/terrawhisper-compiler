Tanacetum japonicum

Tanacetum japonicum, also known as Japanese tansy, is a perennial herb native to East Asia, characterized by its fern-like foliage and yellow daisy-like flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antispasmodic properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, fever, and menstrual cramps. The bioactive constituents of Tanacetum japonicum include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Japanese tansy is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is used to treat various ailments, including colds, fever, and digestive issues.

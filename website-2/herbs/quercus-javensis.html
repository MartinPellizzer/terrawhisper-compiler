Quercus javensis

Quercus javensis, also known as the Javan oak or whitebark oak, is a species of evergreen tree in the beech family Fagaceae.

The leaves and bark of Quercus javensis have been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to their potential antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Quercus javensis include flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which have been shown to exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.

In traditional medicine, Quercus javensis is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and decoctions, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including coughs, colds, and digestive issues.

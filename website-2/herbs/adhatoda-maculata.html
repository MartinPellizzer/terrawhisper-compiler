Adhatoda maculata

Adhatoda maculata, also known as Vasaka or Malabar nut, is a plant species belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and expectorant properties, making it a potential treatment for respiratory issues such as bronchitis, asthma, and coughs. The bioactive constituents of Adhatoda maculata include alkaloids like vasicinone, vasicoline, and vasicinol, as well as glycosides and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, Adhatoda maculata is commonly prepared as a decoction, powder, or syrup to treat various health conditions, and is also used in the form of capsules or tablets for its expectorant and anti-inflammatory effects.

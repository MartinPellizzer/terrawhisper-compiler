Arnica amplexifolia

Arnica amplexifolia, also known as perennial mountain arnica, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

The plant is prized for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate pain, reduce swelling, and promote wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Arnica amplexifolia include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Arnica amplexifolia include topical creams, salves, and ointments, as well as tinctures and infusions, which are often used to treat musculoskeletal injuries and skin conditions.

Individuals with sensitive skin or allergies should exercise caution when using products containing Arnica amplexifolia.

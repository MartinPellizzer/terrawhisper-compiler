Magnolia oblongifolia

Magnolia oblongifolia, also known as the Chinese evergreen magnolia, is a deciduous tree species native to China, characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy white flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Bioactive constituents of Magnolia oblongifolia include magnolol and honokiol, which have been shown to exhibit antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Magnolia oblongifolia include teas, tinctures, and capsules, which are often used to support respiratory health, reduce stress and anxiety, and promote relaxation.

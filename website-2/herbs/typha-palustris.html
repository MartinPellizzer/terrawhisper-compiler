Typha palustris

Typha palustris, also known as the bulrush or cattail, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America, characterized by its distinctive brown or green cylindrical flower spike and flat, linear leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including the treatment of diarrhea, fever, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Typha palustris include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and polysaccharides, which have been shown to exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulatory activities.

The most common herbal preparations made from Typha palustris include teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as topical creams and salves used to treat skin conditions and wounds.

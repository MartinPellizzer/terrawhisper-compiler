Torilis purpurea

Torilis purpurea, also known as Purple Hemlock or Stinking Florence, is a biennial plant native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its tall stem, purple flowers, and distinctive unpleasant odor.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Torilis purpurea include essential oils, flavonoids, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

Torilis purpurea is often used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as in traditional remedies like poultices and salves, although its use should be approached with caution due to potential toxicity.

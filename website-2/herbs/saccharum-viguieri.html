Saccharum viguieri

Saccharum viguieri, also known as Viguiera grass, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal disorders, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Saccharum viguieri include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health issues, including digestive problems and skin infections.

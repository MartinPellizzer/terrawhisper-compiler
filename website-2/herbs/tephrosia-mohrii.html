Tephrosia mohrii

Tephrosia mohrii, also known as Mohr's sericea or Mohr's pea tree, is a plant species native to tropical Africa, belonging to the legume family (Fabaceae).

The plant contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Tephrosia mohrii is commonly used in herbal preparations, including tea, infusions, and extracts, which are traditionally used to treat fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues.

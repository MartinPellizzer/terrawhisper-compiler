Zinnia americana

Zinnia americana, also known as the American zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, belonging to the family Asteraceae.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may help to soothe skin irritations, wounds, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Zinnia americana include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Zinnia americana include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat various ailments, including fever, cough, and digestive issues.

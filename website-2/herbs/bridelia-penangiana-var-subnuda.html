What is Bridelia penangiana var. subnuda?

Bridelia penangiana var. subnuda is a plant variety that belongs to the family Phyllanthaceae.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 15 meters in height, with a straight or slightly curved trunk and a crown of spreading branches. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, measuring 6-15 cm in length and 2-6 cm in width, with a pointed tip and a cuneate base. The leaf margins are entire, and the leaves are dark green on the upper surface and pale green on the lower surface.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in axillary or terminal panicles, which are dioecious and produce small, red or yellowish fruits.

Camellia kucha

Camellia kucha, also known as the Yunnan tea plant, is a species of flowering plant in the tea family (Theaceae) native to the mountainous regions of China.

The bioactive constituents of Camellia kucha include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Camellia kucha is commonly prepared as a tea, as well as in the form of capsules, infusions, and decoctions, which are used to treat various health conditions, including digestive problems, fever, and respiratory issues.

What is Corallorhiza maculata var. flavida?

Corallorhiza maculata var. flavida is a variety of orchid that belongs to the family Orchidaceae.

It is a mycoheterotroph, obtaining its nutrients by parasitizing fungi associated with tree roots, and has no chlorophyll, thus not requiring photosynthesis. This plant has a yellowish-green or yellowish-brown color and a distinctive coralloid habit, with a mass of branching, fleshy stems that are 2-15 cm in height, and 1-2 mm in diameter. The stems are covered with minute, brownish spots, and the plant produces no leaves or flowers.

Corallorhiza maculata var. flavida is found in North America, primarily in the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

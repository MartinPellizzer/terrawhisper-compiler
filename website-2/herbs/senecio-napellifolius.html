Senecio napellifolius

Senecio napellifolius, also known as Jerusalem thistle or Jerusalem artichoke thistle, is a flowering plant that belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

This plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including fever reduction, rheumatism, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Senecio napellifolius include sesquiterpene lactones, such as napellifolic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Senecio napellifolius is often made into teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, although caution is advised due to potential toxicity.

Phalaris muricata

Phalaris muricata, also known as bearded grass, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

Traditionally, the plant has been used in herbal medicine to treat a range of health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and inflammation, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Phalaris muricata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In herbal medicine, Phalaris muricata is commonly prepared as a tea or infusion, and is also used in tinctures and salves to promote wound healing and reduce inflammation.

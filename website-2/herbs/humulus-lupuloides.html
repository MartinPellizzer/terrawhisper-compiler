Humulus lupuloides

Humulus lupuloides, also known as American hop or dwarf hop, is a perennial climbing plant of the Cannabaceae family, native to eastern North America.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help to alleviate conditions such as arthritis, wounds, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of H. lupuloides include humulones, lupulones, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with H. lupuloides include teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as topical creams and ointments used to soothe skin irritations and wounds.

Cinnamomum erythropus

Cinnamomum erythropus, also known as the Indian cinnamon or Korintje cinnamon, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

The bioactive constituents of Cinnamomum erythropus include cinnamaldehyde, cinnamyl acetate, and eugenol, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Cinnamomum erythropus is commonly prepared into herbal teas, decoctions, and infusions, as well as being used in traditional Ayurvedic and Unani medicine preparations.

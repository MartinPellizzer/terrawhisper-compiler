What is Haworthia cooperi var. tenera?

Haworthia cooperi var. tenera is a succulent plant that belongs to the family Asphodelaceae.

It is a variant of Haworthia cooperi, a species native to South Africa. This plant has a rosette-shaped growth habit, composed of numerous, fleshy, and pointed leaves that are green with white or pale yellow markings. The leaves are typically 10-20 cm long and 1-2 cm wide, with a cylindrical shape and a rounded tip.

The plant grows up to 10-20 cm in diameter and produces white or pale yellow flowers on a long inflorescence in late spring to early summer.

The main herbal preparations of Haworthia cooperi var. tenera are tincture, tea, salve, infusion, and infused oil, each obtained through distinct processes.

An infusion is similar to a tea but uses cold water, allowing for a more delicate extraction of the plant's constituents.

Infused oil is created by allowing the plant to steep in a carrier oil for an extended period, resulting in a rich, oil-based preparation rich in antioxidants and other beneficial compounds.

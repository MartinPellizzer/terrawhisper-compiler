Saxifraga subdioica

Saxifraga subdioica, also known as the 'Saxifrage' or 'Rock Saxifrage', is a perennial plant species belonging to the Saxifragaceae family, native to the high-altitude mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

The bioactive constituents of Saxifraga subdioica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Saxifraga subdioica is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or salve, with the dried root and rhizome being the most commonly used parts of the plant for medicinal purposes.

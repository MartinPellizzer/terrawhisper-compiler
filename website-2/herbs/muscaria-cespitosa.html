Muscaria cespitosa

Muscaria cespitosa, also known as the button mushroom, is a type of edible fungus belonging to the genus Agaricus.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including immune system support, antioxidant properties, and anti-inflammatory effects. The bioactive constituents of Muscaria cespitosa include beta-glucans, which stimulate the immune system, and polysaccharides, which exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

In herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a tincture, tea, or supplement, and is used to boost the immune system and support overall well-being.

side effects

Muscaria cespitosa, also known as the button mushroom, is not to be confused with the Amanita muscaria, which has a different set of effects. However, if we consider Amanita muscaria, it's known for its potential psychoactive properties. Consuming Amanita muscaria can lead to side effects such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and in severe cases, liver and kidney damage. The mushroom can also cause hallucinations and altered perception due to its ibotenic acid content. It's essential to exercise extreme caution when handling Amanita muscaria, as it can be toxic and even fatal if ingested in large quantities.

Matricaria pumila

Matricaria pumila, also known as low chamomile, is a perennial herb belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its small yellow daisy-like flowers and feathery foliage.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include soothing digestive issues, reducing inflammation, and exhibiting antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Matricaria pumila include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpene lactones, such as matricine and chamazulene, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Matricaria pumila is commonly used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to promote relaxation, improve sleep quality, and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and digestive upset.

Senegalia × emoryana

Senegalia × emoryana, also known as Emory Acacia, is a hybrid plant species within the Acacia genus, characterized by its thorny branches and fragrant yellow flowers.

The plant has been reported to possess various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties, which have been traditionally used to treat wounds, skin infections, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Senegalia × emoryana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Emory Acacia is prepared as a tea, tincture, or infused oil, and is used to treat conditions such as coughs, sore throats, and skin irritations, as well as to promote wound healing and reduce inflammation.

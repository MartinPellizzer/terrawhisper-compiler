Laburnum × watereri

Laburnum × watereri, also known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree in the family Fabaceae, characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which may aid in reducing pain and inflammation, as well as supporting the treatment of wounds and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Laburnum × watereri include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, particularly laburnine, which is known for its cytotoxic and anti-inflammatory activities.

Traditionally, the plant is used in herbal medicine, and its preparations include infusions, tinctures, and ointments, often used to treat skin conditions, fever, and respiratory issues.

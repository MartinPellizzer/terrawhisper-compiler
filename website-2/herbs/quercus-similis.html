Quercus similis

Quercus similis, also known as the Japanese evergreen oak, is a species of deciduous tree native to East Asia, characterized by its broad, rounded crown and grayish-brown bark.

The bioactive constituents of Quercus similis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Quercus similis is often used to make teas, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, as well as to support cardiovascular health.

Edwardsia nitida

Edwardsia nitida, also known as Edwards' rose or sea rose, is a species of coral-root plant in the family Edwardsiidae, typically found in the Mediterranean region.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which may help to alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues and skin conditions. Bioactive constituents of Edwardsia nitida include terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Edwardsia nitida include infusions, decoctions, and topical creams, which are used to treat various health conditions, including coughs, colds, and skin irritations.

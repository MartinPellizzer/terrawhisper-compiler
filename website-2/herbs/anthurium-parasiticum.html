Anthurium parasiticum

Anthurium parasiticum, also known as the "bird's nest anthurium," is a species of flowering plant in the arum family that grows as a hemi-epiphyte, meaning it obtains some of its nutrients by parasitizing other plants.

Traditionally, the plant has been used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its purported anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Anthurium parasiticum include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which have been shown to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.

In herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is used to treat a range of conditions, including fever, arthritis, and skin infections.

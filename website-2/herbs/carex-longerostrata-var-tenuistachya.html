What is Carex longerostrata var. tenuistachya?

Carex longerostrata var. tenuistachya is a perennial, rhizomatous sedge of the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its stout, 1-2.5 mm thick, smooth, brownish to reddish-brown rhizome, and its culms, which are 2-6 dm tall, glabrous, and reddish-brown. The leaves are flat, linear-lanceolate, 3-5 mm wide, 2-5 times longer than the pseudospike, and have a glabrous blade. The inflorescence is a dense, ovoid to cylindrical pseudospike, 2-3.5 cm long, with 6-12 spikelets, and the spikelets are 6-9 mm long, with 2-3 scales and 4-5 flowers.

The perigynia are brownish-green, 3-4 mm long, with a beaked tip and a narrow, membranous, and ciliate margin.

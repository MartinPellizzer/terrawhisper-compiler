Stephanandra incisa

Stephanandra incisa, also known as Japanese stephanandra, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia, characterized by its attractive foliage and small white flowers.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in reducing fever and alleviating symptoms of respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Stephanandra incisa include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Stephanandra incisa is commonly prepared as an infusion or decoction, often used to treat colds, fever, and other infectious diseases, although its efficacy and safety require further scientific investigation.

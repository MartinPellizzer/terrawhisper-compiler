Ligularia macrophylla

Ligularia macrophylla, also known as the giant goldenray or Japanese ligularia, is a perennial plant species native to East Asia, characterized by its large, showy flowers and broad, ovate leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in the treatment of conditions such as arthritis, fever, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Ligularia macrophylla include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ligularia macrophylla is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or decoction, and is also used in the production of tinctures and extracts.

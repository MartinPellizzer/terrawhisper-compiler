Lappula virginiana

Lappula virginiana, also known as Virginia stickseed, is a perennial plant species in the borage family (Boraginaceae) native to North America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in reducing pain, soothing digestive issues, and protecting against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Lappula virginiana include alkaloids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Lappula virginiana is often used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as in traditional remedies like poultices and salves, to leverage its medicinal benefits.

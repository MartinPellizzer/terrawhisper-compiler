Pisum lathyrus

Pisum lathyrus, also known as the annual pea or grass pea, is a legume plant that belongs to the family Fabaceae.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its ability to lower cholesterol levels, regulate blood sugar, and exhibit antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Pisum lathyrus include saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to make herbal preparations such as tea, capsules, and infusions, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including cardiovascular disease and diabetes.

side effects

The consumption of Pisum lathyrus can lead to neurolathyrism, a condition characterized by muscle weakness, paralysis, and even death in severe cases. It is essential to note that neurolathyrism is typically associated with long-term consumption of the plant, particularly in areas where it is a staple food. The toxin in Pisum lathyrus can also cause gastrointestinal problems and other symptoms, such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. People should exercise caution and avoid eating Pisum lathyrus or any other plants that may be toxic, especially if they are not properly identified.

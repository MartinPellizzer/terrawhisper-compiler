What is Isolepis cernua var. platycarpa?

Isolepis cernua var. platycarpa is a plant variety that belongs to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its habit of having a short, stout stem, and its leaf-like structures, or bracts, are reduced to a few, sheathing the spike. The spike itself is a cylindrical, unisexual inflorescence with a single spikelet at its tip, consisting of a single floret with one lemma and one palea, and a staminate floret above the pistillate one. The plant grows in wet to dry, sandy or muddy areas, in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its fruits are nutlets, three- to four-angled and beaked, 1-2 mm long.

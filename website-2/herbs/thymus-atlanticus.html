Thymus atlanticus

Thymus atlanticus, also known as Atlantic thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western Mediterranean region, characterized by its small, gray-green leaves and purple flowers.

The health benefits of Atlantic thyme include its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which have been traditionally used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, as well as digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, terpenes, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Atlantic thyme is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and essential oils, which are often used to soothe respiratory issues and promote relaxation.

Liriope gracilis

Liriope gracilis, also known as Chinese lilyturf or white lilyturf, is a perennial plant native to East Asia, characterized by its clumping habit and white, purple-tinged flowers.

The health benefits of Liriope gracilis include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as fever, sore throat, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Liriope gracilis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Liriope gracilis is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is also used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and granules that are used to treat a range of health conditions.

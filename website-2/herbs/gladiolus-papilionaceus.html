Gladiolus papilionaceus

Gladiolus papilionaceus, commonly known as the butterfly iris, is a perennial plant species native to South Africa, belonging to the family Iridaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, offering several health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues, wounds, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Gladiolus papilionaceus include iridoid glycosides, phenolic compounds, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often used to treat respiratory problems, skin conditions, and as a topical treatment for wounds and burns.

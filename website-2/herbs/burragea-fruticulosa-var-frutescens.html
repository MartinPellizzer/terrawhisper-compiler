What is Burragea fruticulosa var. frutescens?

Burragea fruticulosa var. frutescens is a plant species belonging to the family Onagraceae.

It is a shrub or a small tree that grows up to 10 meters tall, with a straight stem and a rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, measuring 5-12 centimeters in length and 2-5 centimeters in width, with a cuneate base and a rounded apex. The flowers are clustered in axillary corymbs, with 5-7 petals and a yellow color, while the fruits are small capsules containing numerous seeds.

The plant is endemic to the Western Ghats in India, where it grows in well-drained soils in tropical rainforests and scrub forests.

Salve is a topical preparation made by infusing the leaves or flowers of the plant in a carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil, which is used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and inflammatory disorders.

Allionia micrantha

Allionia micrantha, also known as 'Desert Honeysuckle' or 'Red Honeysuckle', is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, characterized by its small, white flowers and edible red berries.

The plant is valued for its various health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of colds, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Allionia micrantha include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is also used in salves and ointments to treat skin irritations and wounds.

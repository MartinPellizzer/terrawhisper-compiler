Pelargonium × hybridum

Pelargonium × hybridum, commonly known as South African geranium, is a hybrid plant species within the Geraniaceae family, characterized by its distinctive flowers and fragrant leaves.

The health benefits of Pelargonium × hybridum include its use as an antiseptic and anti-inflammatory agent, making it effective in treating wounds, fever, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Herbal preparations made from Pelargonium × hybridum include teas, tinctures, and infusions, often used to treat coughs, colds, and other respiratory issues.

classification

Pelargonium × hybridum is classified as a hybrid within the Geraniaceae family. This hybrid is derived from two parent species, Pelargonium sidoides and Pelargonium tomentosum. It is commonly known as a zonal pelargonium or a zonal geranium. The 'zonal' part of its name comes from the distinct band or zone of different colored leaves. This classification places it within the larger group of pelargonium species.

side effects

Pelargonium × hybridum, commonly known as geranium, is generally considered safe when used topically or in moderation. However, some people may experience skin irritation, such as redness, itching, or rashes, due to the plant's essential oils. In rare cases, ingesting geranium can cause gastrointestinal issues like nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea. There is also limited research on the effects of geranium on pregnant or breastfeeding women, so caution is advised. If you have any underlying medical conditions or allergies, consult a healthcare professional before using geranium products.

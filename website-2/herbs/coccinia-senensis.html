Coccinia senensis

Coccinia senensis, also known as the bitter gourd or balsam pear, is a perennial vine native to Asia and Africa, belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine for its numerous health benefits, including its ability to lower blood sugar levels, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antioxidant and anti-cancer properties. The bioactive constituents of Coccinia senensis include cucurbitacins, alkaloids, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Coccinia senensis is often used to make various herbal preparations, such as decoctions, infusions, and capsules, which are commonly used to treat diabetes, fever, and respiratory infections.

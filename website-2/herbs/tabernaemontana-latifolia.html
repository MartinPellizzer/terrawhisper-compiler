Tabernaemontana latifolia

Tabernaemontana latifolia, also known as the "Chandraprabha vati" or the "West Indian Woodrose", is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, and its health benefits include anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and antioxidant properties, which are effective in treating various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Tabernaemontana latifolia include alkaloids like vobasine, tabernanthine, and ajmalicine, as well as flavonoids and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, the plant is commonly used to prepare herbal formulations like Chandraprabha vati, which is a polyherbal preparation used to treat digestive disorders, skin conditions, and other health issues.

Cactus rueschianus

Cactus rueschianus, also known as Pincushion Cactus, is a small, globular cactus species native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including the treatment of gastrointestinal issues and skin problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Cactus rueschianus include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant has been used to make various herbal preparations, such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to aid digestion, soothe skin irritations, and promote wound healing.

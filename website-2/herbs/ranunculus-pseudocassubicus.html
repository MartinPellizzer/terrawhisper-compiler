Ranunculus pseudocassubicus

Ranunculus pseudocassubicus is a perennial plant species belonging to the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae), native to Europe and Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiviral effects, which can aid in wound healing and the treatment of various infections. Bioactive constituents of R. pseudocassubicus include ranunculin, protoanemonin, and other glycosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with R. pseudocassubicus include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often used to treat skin conditions, respiratory issues, and gastrointestinal problems.

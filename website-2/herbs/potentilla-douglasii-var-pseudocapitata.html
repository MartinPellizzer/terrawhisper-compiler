What is Potentilla douglasii var. pseudocapitata?

Potentilla douglasii var. pseudocapitata is a taxonomically recognized variety of the species Potentilla douglasii, belonging to the family Rosaceae.

This perennial plant is characterized by its glandular-pubescent stems and leaves, which are trifoliate with leaflets having ciliate margins and apices. The plant produces small, pink to white, 5-merous flowers in a capituliform inflorescence, typically with 5-12 florets. The leaves and stems of Potentilla douglasii var. pseudocapitata are covered with dense, glandular hairs, which are secretory in nature.

The plant's growth habit is typically prostrate to suberect, and it is found in moist to dry areas, often in association with rocky outcrops or serpentine soils.

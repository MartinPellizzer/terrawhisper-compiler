Carissa erythrocarpa

Carissa erythrocarpa, also known as the Cape white currant, is a shrub-like plant native to South Africa, characterized by its red berries and glossy leaves.

The bioactive constituents of Carissa erythrocarpa include alkaloids, saponins, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

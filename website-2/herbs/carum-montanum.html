Carum montanum

Carum montanum, also known as Wild Caraway, is a perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

The health benefits of Carum montanum include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as indigestion, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include volatile oils like carvone and limonene, as well as other compounds like flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins.

Carum montanum is commonly prepared in herbal medicine as a tea, infusion, or decoction, and is also used in traditional medicine to make capsules, tablets, and tinctures.

Adhatoda carthaginensis

Adhatoda vasica (also known as Adhatoda carthaginensis) is a medicinal plant native to India and other parts of Asia, belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and expectorant properties, making it beneficial in treating respiratory issues such as bronchitis, asthma, and coughs. The bioactive constituents of Adhatoda vasica include rosmarinic acid, adhatodotoxin, and vasakine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the leaves of Adhatoda vasica are commonly used to make preparations such as teas, decoctions, and syrups, and are also used as a component in traditional Ayurvedic and Unani medicines.

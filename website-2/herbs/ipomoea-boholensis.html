Ipomoea boholensis

Ipomoea boholensis, also known as the Bohol morning glory, is a perennial plant species that belongs to the Convolvulaceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, particularly in the Philippines, to treat various health conditions, including fever, headache, and rheumatism, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea boholensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health conditions, as well as to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

classification

Ipomoea boholensis is a species of flowering plant in the family Convolvulaceae, commonly known as morning glories or bindweeds. It belongs to the genus Ipomoea and is a type of perennial vine native to the Philippines. The species is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers, which are typically blue or purple in color. Ipomoea boholensis is also known as 'Blue Morning Glory', and is often grown in tropical and subtropical regions for its ornamental value. As a member of the Convolvulaceae family, Ipomoea boholensis is closely related to other species such as Ipomoea purpurea and Ipomoea alba.

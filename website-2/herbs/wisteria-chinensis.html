Wisteria chinensis

Wisteria chinensis, also known as Chinese wisteria, is a flowering plant species native to China, characterized by its long, fragrant purple flowers and woody, climbing vines.

The plant has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiviral properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Wisteria chinensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Wisteria chinensis is often prepared as a tea, capsule, or tincture, and is also used in combination with other herbs in formulas aimed at promoting lung health and relieving respiratory congestion.

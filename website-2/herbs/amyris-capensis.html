Amyris capensis

Amyris capensis, also known as African sandalwood or African amyris, is a plant species native to southern Africa, belonging to the family Rutaceae.

Traditionally, Amyris capensis has been used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin infections, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Amyris capensis include essential oils, specifically alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, and limonene, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

In herbal medicine, Amyris capensis is commonly prepared as an essential oil, tincture, and infusion, which are often used to treat skin conditions, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing.

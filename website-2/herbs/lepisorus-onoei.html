Lepisorus onoei

Lepisorus onoei, also known as Onoe's lepi, is a species of fern native to East Asia, specifically in China, Japan, and Korea, characterized by its delicate fronds and creeping rhizomes.

Traditionally, Lepisorus onoei has been used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its purported anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Lepisorus onoei include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional medicine, Lepisorus onoei is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, as well as being used in the form of a poultice or plaster to treat localized pain and inflammation.

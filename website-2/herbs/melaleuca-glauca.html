Melaleuca glauca

Melaleuca glauca, also known as the Narrow-leaved Paperbark, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia, belonging to the myrtle family (Myrtaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal activities, which have been attributed to its ability to aid in wound healing and reduce the severity of respiratory infections. Bioactive constituents of Melaleuca glauca include terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which have been identified as the primary compounds responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Melaleuca glauca is commonly prepared as a tea or tincture, or used in topical applications such as creams and ointments, to leverage its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Viola nudicaulis

Viola nudicaulis, also known as Downy Yellow Violet, is a perennial plant species native to North America, characterized by its yellow flowers and downy leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits, including reducing inflammation and relieving respiratory issues, such as coughs and congestion. The bioactive constituents of Viola nudicaulis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Viola nudicaulis include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to treat respiratory and inflammatory conditions.

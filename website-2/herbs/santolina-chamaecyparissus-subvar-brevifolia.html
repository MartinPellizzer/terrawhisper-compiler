What is Santolina chamaecyparissus subvar. brevifolia?

Santolina chamaecyparissus subvar. brevifolia is a perennial shrubby plant native to the Mediterranean region, belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It has a woody base and produces silvery-gray foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are 2-5 cm long and 1-2 mm wide, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. The leaves are highly aromatic and have a strong, pungent scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in summer, which are arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences.

The flowers are 2-3 mm in diameter and have five petals, with a prominent yellow center.

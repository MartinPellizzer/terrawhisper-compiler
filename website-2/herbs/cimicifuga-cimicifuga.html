Cimicifuga cimicifuga

Cimicifuga cimicifuga, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial plant native to Eastern North America, belonging to the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae).

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including relief from menopausal symptoms such as hot flashes and night sweats, as well as its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of black cohosh include triterpene glycosides, such as acteoside and 23-epi-26-deoxyacteoside, which are thought to be responsible for the plant's therapeutic effects.

Black cohosh is commonly used in herbal preparations, including capsules, tablets, and teas, often in combination with other herbs like St. John's Wort and red clover, to support women's health and alleviate menopausal symptoms.

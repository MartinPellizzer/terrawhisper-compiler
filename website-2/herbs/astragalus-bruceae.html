Astragalus bruceae

Astragalus bruceae, also known as Bruce's milkvetch, is a perennial plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory properties, making it a popular ingredient in traditional medicine for enhancing immune function and reducing stress. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus bruceae include saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Astragalus bruceae is commonly used in herbal preparations such as capsules, tablets, and teas, often in combination with other herbs, to promote overall health and well-being.

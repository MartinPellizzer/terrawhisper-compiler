Trifolium melilotus

Trifolium melilotus, also known as white melilot or sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its white or pale yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, skin conditions, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Trifolium melilotus include coumarins, flavonoids, and isoflavones, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

White melilot is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions to support overall health and well-being, and is also used topically to soothe skin irritations and wounds.

Salix purpurea uralensis

Salix purpurea uralensis, also known as Ural purple willow or ural willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree species of the willow family (Salicaceae).

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Salix purpurea uralensis include salicin, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Salix purpurea uralensis is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture to treat various health conditions, although its use should be cautioned due to potential side effects and interactions with other medications.

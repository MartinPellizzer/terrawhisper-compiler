Melaleuca micrantha

Melaleuca micrantha, also known as the small-leaved paperbark, is a shrub native to Australia, belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including skin conditions such as eczema and acne, thanks to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Melaleuca micrantha include cymene, limonene, and borneol, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant are essential oils and tinctures, often used in topical creams and ointments to soothe and protect the skin.

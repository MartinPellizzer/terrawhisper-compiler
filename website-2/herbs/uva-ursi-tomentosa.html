Uva-ursi tomentosa

Uva-ursi tomentosa, also known as Bearberry, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North America, characterized by its small, white flowers and red or purple berries.

The bioactive constituents of Uva-ursi include arbutin, hydroquinone, and ursolic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Uva-ursi include teas, capsules, and tinctures, which are often used to support urinary health and alleviate symptoms associated with UTIs.

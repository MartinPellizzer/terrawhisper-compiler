Schoenoplectus × carinatus

Schoenoplectus × carinatus, also known as a hybrid of the cattail-like plant, is a type of aquatic grass that belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

The bioactive constituents of Schoenoplectus × carinatus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and other secondary metabolites, such as phytosterols and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The plant is commonly used in herbal preparations, including decoctions, infusions, and ointments, often employed in traditional medicine for its wound-healing and anti-inflammatory properties.

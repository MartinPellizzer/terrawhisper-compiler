Mentha jurana

Mentha jurana, also known as Horsemint or Indian Mint, is a species of perennial mint plant that belongs to the Lamiaceae family.

The plant is known for its potential health benefits, which include reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and alleviating symptoms of respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Mentha jurana include compounds like menthone, isomenthone, and limonene, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Indian medicine, Mentha jurana is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments to aid in wound healing and skin conditions.

Dryopteris auriculata

Dryopteris auriculata, also known as Golden Buckler Fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the Dryopteris genus, characterized by its lance-shaped fronds and golden-brown stalks.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, which may help in reducing pain, inflammation, and improving immune function. Bioactive constituents of Dryopteris auriculata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Golden Buckler Fern include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms associated with bronchitis and asthma.

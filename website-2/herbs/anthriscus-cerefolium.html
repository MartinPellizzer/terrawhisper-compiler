Anthriscus cerefolium

Anthriscus cerefolium, commonly known as chervil, is a low-growing, annual herb belonging to the parsley family (Apiaceae).

Chervil is valued for its health benefits, which include its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in alleviating digestive issues and reducing stress. The bioactive constituents of chervil include volatile oils, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Chervil is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, and is also a popular ingredient in culinary dishes, particularly in French cuisine.

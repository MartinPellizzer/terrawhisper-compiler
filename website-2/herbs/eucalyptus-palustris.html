Eucalyptus palustris

Eucalyptus palustris, also known as the river peppermint or swamp peppermint, is a species of tree native to the southeastern regions of Australia, characterized by its aromatic leaves and ability to thrive in wet environments.

The health benefits of Eucalyptus palustris include its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of this plant include eucalyptol, a volatile oil responsible for its decongestant and expectorant properties, as well as other compounds like flavonoids and phenolic acids.

Eucalyptus palustris is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as essential oils, tinctures, and teas, which are often employed to relieve congestion and promote respiratory health.

Scutellaria tomentosa

Scutellaria tomentosa, also known as Chinese skullcap, is a perennial plant species belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae) that is native to China and Japan.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antioxidant properties, which have been associated with a range of health benefits, including reducing anxiety and stress, improving sleep quality, and supporting immune function. The bioactive constituents of Scutellaria tomentosa include flavonoids, such as baicalin and baicalein, as well as phenolic acids and essential oils, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Scutellaria tomentosa is often used to make herbal teas, capsules, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat a variety of health conditions, including insomnia, anxiety disorders, and respiratory infections.

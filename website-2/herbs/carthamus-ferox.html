Carthamus ferox

Carthamus ferox, also known as the prickly thistle or Scotch thistle, is a biennial or perennial thistle species native to the Mediterranean region.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant effects, making it beneficial for treating various health issues such as digestive problems, skin conditions, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Carthamus ferox include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Carthamus ferox include teas, infusions, and topical creams, which are often used to alleviate symptoms of digestive disorders, skin irritations, and respiratory infections.

Oxalis argentina

Oxalis argentina, also known as wood sorrel, is a perennial herb belonging to the Oxalidaceae family, characterized by its small, pink or white flowers and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as treating digestive issues, reducing inflammation, and acting as a natural diuretic. The bioactive constituents of Oxalis argentina include oxalic acid, flavonoids, and anthocyanins, which are responsible for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

Oxalis argentina is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and capsules, which are often used to treat urinary tract issues, kidney stones, and as a natural remedy for digestive problems.

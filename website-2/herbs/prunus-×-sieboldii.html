Prunus × sieboldii

Prunus × sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese hill cherry or Siebold's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia, characterized by its beautiful white flowers and edible fruit.

The health benefits of Prunus × sieboldii include its potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help to soothe digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and support immune function. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Prunus × sieboldii is often used in traditional herbal medicine, and its most common preparations include teas, infusions, and extracts, which are traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including diarrhea, fever, and respiratory issues.

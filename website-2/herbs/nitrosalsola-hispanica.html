Nitrosalsola hispanica

Nitrosalsola hispanica, also known as the saltwort or glasswort, is a halophytic shrub belonging to the Amaranthaceae family, characterized by its ability to thrive in coastal environments with high salt concentrations.

The bioactive constituents of Nitrosalsola hispanica include phenolic acids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Nitrosalsola hispanica is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is also used in topical applications to treat skin conditions and wounds.

Onobrychis scardica

Onobrychis scardica, also known as Balkan wild bean or Scardica broom, is a perennial legume plant native to the Balkan Peninsula, characterized by its bright yellow flowers and seed pods.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, fever, and gastrointestinal problems, due to its potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Onobrychis scardica include isoflavones, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Onobrychis scardica include tea, infusions, and capsules, often used to alleviate symptoms of colds, fever, and digestive issues.

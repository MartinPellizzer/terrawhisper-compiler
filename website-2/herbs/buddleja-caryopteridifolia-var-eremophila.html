What is Buddleja caryopteridifolia var. eremophila?

Buddleja caryopteridifolia var. eremophila is a plant variety in the genus Buddleja, belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its deciduous shrub habit, 3-4 meters in height, with a spread of 2-3 meters, and its oppositely arranged, lanceolate leaves, 4-10 cm long, with a leathery texture and a cuneate base. The plant produces showy, terminal inflorescences, 15-30 cm long, bearing small, tubular flowers, 1-2 cm long, in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The flowers are rich in nectar, attracting various pollinators, and are produced from July to October.

The plant's hardy and drought-tolerant nature makes it suitable for growth in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

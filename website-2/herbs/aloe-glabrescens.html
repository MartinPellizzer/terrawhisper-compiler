classification

Aloe glabrescens is a species of flowering succulent plant. It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and the genus Aloe. Within the Aloe genus, Aloe glabrescens is classified as a member of the subgenus Aloe and tribe Aloeae. Aloe glabrescens is native to the tropical regions of Africa, where it can be found growing in rocky and sandy areas. This succulent plant is valued for its medicinal and ornamental properties.

side effects

Aloe glabrescens, like other Aloe species, can cause skin irritation, itching, and redness when applied topically to sensitive skin or in large quantities. The latex of Aloe glabrescens can also cause gastrointestinal issues if ingested. Internal consumption of the plant's leaves has been reported to cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea due to the presence of anthraquinones. Aloe glabrescens may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and should be used with caution under medical supervision. In rare cases, excessive consumption of Aloe glabrescens may lead to kidney damage and other systemic complications.

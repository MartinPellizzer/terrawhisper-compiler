Achillea ptarmicifolia

Achillea ptarmicifolia, commonly known as Yarrow, is a flowering plant species native to Europe and Asia, belonging to the Asteraceae family.

Yarrow has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, and its health benefits include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, making it effective in treating wounds, reducing fever, and alleviating digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Yarrow include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Yarrow include teas, tinctures, and topical creams, which are used to treat various health conditions, including skin irritations, menstrual cramps, and respiratory issues.

Saponaria thymifolia

Saponaria thymifolia, also known as rock soapwort or thyme-leaved soapwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant in the Caryophyllaceae family, native to Europe and Western Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in treating skin conditions such as acne and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Saponaria thymifolia include saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat skin issues and promote wound healing.

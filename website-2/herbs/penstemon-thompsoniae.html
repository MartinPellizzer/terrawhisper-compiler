Penstemon thompsoniae

Penstemon thompsoniae, also known as Texas penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States, characterized by its vibrant pink to purple flowers and gray-green foliage.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including sore throats, coughs, and respiratory infections, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Penstemon thompsoniae include iridoids, phenolic acids, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to soothe respiratory issues and promote overall well-being.

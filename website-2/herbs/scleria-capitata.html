Scleria capitata

Scleria capitata, also known as Black Needle Rush, is a perennial aquatic plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

Traditionally, Scleria capitata is valued for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, which may aid in the management of various health conditions such as fever, digestive issues, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Scleria capitata include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Scleria capitata is commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion, often used as a treatment for fever and digestive problems, and sometimes employed as an adjunct in the management of respiratory and urinary tract infections.

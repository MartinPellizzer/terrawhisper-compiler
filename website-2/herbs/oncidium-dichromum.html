Oncidium dichromum

The Oncidium dichromum, commonly known as the "Dancing Ladies" or "Dancing Ladies Orchid," is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory problems, due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Oncidium dichromum include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Oncidium dichromum include teas, infusions, and extracts, often used to alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections, such as bronchitis and pneumonia.

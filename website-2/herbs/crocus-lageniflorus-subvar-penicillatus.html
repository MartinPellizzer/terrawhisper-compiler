What is Crocus lageniflorus subvar. penicillatus?

Crocus lageniflorus subvar. penicillatus is a subspecies of the plant Crocus lageniflorus, belonging to the family Iridaceae.

It is characterized by its white, yellow, or purple flowers with 6-9 tepals, 1-2 cm in length, and 4-6 stamens. The flowers are borne on a short, leafless stem, usually solitary or in small clusters, and are accompanied by 2-3 narrow, green, lance-shaped leaves. This subspecies is found in the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe and Turkey, where it grows in rocky, grassy areas, typically at elevations between 1,000-2,500 meters.

The leaves of Crocus lageniflorus subvar. penicillatus are 2-6 cm long and 0.5-1 cm wide, and are often greenish-blue in color.

Toxicodendron sinuatum

Toxicodendron sinuatum, also known as the poison sumac, is a plant species native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive white, yellow, or orange berries.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antipruritic properties. Toxicodendron sinuatum contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and polyphenols, including sinapic acid and salicylic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is typically prepared as a tea, tincture, or salve, which can be used to treat conditions such as eczema, acne, and wounds.

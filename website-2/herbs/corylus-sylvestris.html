Corylus sylvestris

Corylus sylvestris, commonly known as the Hazel or Filbert, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa, characterized by its edible nuts and slender branches.

The Hazel has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include reducing inflammation, improving cardiovascular health, and supporting immune function due to its high content of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins. The bioactive constituents of Corylus sylvestris include anthocyanins, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the Hazel is commonly prepared as an infusion, decoction, or tincture, and is often used to support digestive health, reduce fever, and alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections.

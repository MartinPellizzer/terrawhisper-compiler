Hippophae sinensis

Hippophae sinensis, also known as sea buckthorn, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, valued for its edible berries and medicinal properties.

The health benefits of sea buckthorn include its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which have been traditionally used to treat wounds, gastrointestinal issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of sea buckthorn include flavonoids, carotenoids, and terpenoids, such as hippophaein, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with sea buckthorn include capsules, tinctures, and oils, as well as teas and infusions, which are often used to support skin and mucous membrane health.

Aloe cordifolia

Aloe cordifolia, also known as the "heart aloe" or "heart-leaf aloe," is a succulent plant species that belongs to the Aloe genus.

The health benefits of Aloe cordifolia include its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can aid in soothing skin irritations, wounds, and burns, as well as its potential to help manage diabetes and lower cholesterol levels. The bioactive constituents of Aloe cordifolia include aloin, aloe-emodin, and anthraquinones, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Aloe cordifolia include gel capsules, topical creams, and ointments, which are often used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and digestive issues.

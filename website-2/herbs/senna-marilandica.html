Senna marilandica

Senna marilandica, also known as Maryland senna, is a perennial plant species in the legume family, Fabaceae, native to eastern North America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with health benefits including the treatment of constipation, diarrhea, and other gastrointestinal issues due to its anthelmintic and laxative effects. The bioactive constituents of Senna marilandica include anthraquinone glycosides, such as sennosides A and B, as well as other compounds like flavonoids and phenolic acids.

Senna marilandica is often used in herbal medicine, and its bioactive constituents are commonly extracted into preparations like teas, tinctures, and capsules, which are used to support digestive health and relieve constipation.

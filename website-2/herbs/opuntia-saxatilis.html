Opuntia saxatilis

Opuntia saxatilis, also known as the tree prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, offering various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of diabetes, arthritis, and other chronic conditions. The bioactive constituents of Opuntia saxatilis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and mucilages, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Herbal preparations made with Opuntia saxatilis include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to support digestive health, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing.

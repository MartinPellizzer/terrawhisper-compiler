Crepis pannonica

Crepis pannonica, also known as Hungarian hawk's beard, is a perennial plant belonging to the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

The bioactive constituents of Crepis pannonica include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Crepis pannonica is traditionally used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders.

Scirpus oryzetorum

Scirpus oryzetorum, also known as rice grass or rice sedge, is a perennial aquatic plant species native to East Asia, characterized by its long, slender stems and flat, grass-like leaves.

The bioactive constituents of Scirpus oryzetorum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is often used to make decoctions, infusions, and powders, which are used to treat various health conditions, including respiratory infections and digestive issues.

Anthriscus fumarioides

Anthriscus fumarioides, commonly known as wild chervil or meadow chervil, is a perennial plant species belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

The bioactive constituents of A. fumarioides include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and coumarins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Wild chervil is often used in herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are traditionally consumed to alleviate coughs and respiratory congestion.

Ephedra regeliana

Ephedra regeliana, also known as Ma Huang, is a species of shrub-like plant in the Ephedraceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, offering health benefits such as relief from respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its ability to dilate bronchial airways and increase oxygenation. The bioactive constituents of Ephedra regeliana include ephedrine alkaloids, specifically ephedrine and pseudoephedrine, as well as other compounds like isoeugenol and ferulic acid, which contribute to the plant's medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Ephedra regeliana is made into various herbal preparations, including teas, decoctions, and capsules, often combined with other herbs like ginseng or licorice root to enhance its therapeutic effects.

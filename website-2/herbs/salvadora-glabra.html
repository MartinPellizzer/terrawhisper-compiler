Salvadora glabra

Salvadora glabra, commonly known as the toothbrush tree or miswak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and North Africa.

The health benefits of Salvadora glabra include its antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and anti-plaque properties, which make it effective in reducing oral infections, bad breath, and gum disease. The bioactive constituents of this plant include alkaloids, saponins, tannins, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Salvadora glabra include toothpaste, mouthwashes, and chewing sticks, which have been used for centuries in traditional medicine to promote oral health.

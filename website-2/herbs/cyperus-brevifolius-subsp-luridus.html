What is Cyperus brevifolius subsp. luridus?

Cyperus brevifolius subsp.

luridus is a perennial monocot belonging to the family Cyperaceae. It has a short to tall stem, 0.5-2.5 m high, with alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves, 3-30 cm long and 0.5-2 cm wide, and a flat, dark green or yellowish-green base.

The inflorescence is a panicle of brown or reddish-brown spikelets, 1-6 cm long, with 3-4 staminate flowers and 1 pistillate flower in each spikelet. The pistillate flowers are 1-2 mm long and have a 3-4 mm long, brown or reddish-brown achene.

It grows in wet environments, including marshes and riverbanks.

What is Quercus broteroi subsp. tlemcenensis?

Quercus broteroi subsp.

tlemcenensis is a taxonomic classification of a plant species within the oak family, Fagaceae. It is a subspecies of Quercus broteroi, a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula, North Africa, and the Mediterranean region.

Botanically, Quercus broteroi subsp. tlemcenensis exhibits characteristics such as elliptical to ovate leaves, 5-9 cm long, with 7-9 lobes and a pointed apex, and a glabrous or slightly hairy underside. The tree typically grows to 10-20 meters in height, with a broad, rounded crown and a short, thick trunk.

Its bark is greyish-brown and deeply fissured, with longitudinal ridges.

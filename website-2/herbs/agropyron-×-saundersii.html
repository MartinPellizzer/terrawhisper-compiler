Agropyron × saundersii

Agropyron × saundersii, also known as blue wildrye, is a perennial grass native to North America, characterized by its bluish-green foliage and vibrant purple inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may aid in reducing stress and improving overall well-being. The bioactive constituents of Agropyron × saundersii include a range of compounds, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Blue wildrye is commonly used in herbal preparations, such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, although its use should be limited to small amounts and under the guidance of a healthcare professional due to potential interactions with other medications.

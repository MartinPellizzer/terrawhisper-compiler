Gentiana vulgaris

Gentiana vulgaris, also known as the common gentian, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Gentianaceae family, commonly found in Europe and Western Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including the treatment of digestive issues, such as indigestion and bloating, as well as its potential to stimulate appetite and improve liver function. The bioactive constituents of Gentiana vulgaris include gentiopicrin, amarogentin, and gentianine, which are responsible for the plant's medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the most common preparations made with Gentiana vulgaris include tinctures, infusions, and powdered extracts, often used to aid in digestion and alleviate symptoms of indigestion and dyspepsia.

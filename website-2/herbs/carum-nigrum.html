classification

Carum nigrum belongs to the family Apiaceae. It is native to the tropical regions of Asia. This species is commonly known as black cumin or nigella seeds, although it is not related to the true cumin or nigella. The plant is often grown for its seeds, which have a strong, pungent flavor and aroma. Carum nigrum is also used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.

side effects

Carum nigrum, also known as Black Cumin, has been traditionally used in various cultures for its medicinal properties and culinary uses. However, excessive consumption of Carum nigrum seeds may cause stomach upset, nausea, and allergic reactions in some individuals. It is also not recommended for pregnant or breastfeeding women, as it may stimulate the uterus and cause complications. Furthermore, Carum nigrum may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, and lower their effectiveness. As with any herb, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using Carum nigrum for medicinal purposes.

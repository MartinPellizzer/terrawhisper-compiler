What is Sorbus aria var. edulis?

Sorbus aria var. edulis is a fruiting form of the whitebeam tree, a deciduous shrub or small tree in the family Rosaceae.

The whitebeam tree has a rounded crown, grey bark, and ovate to lanceolate leaves with 3-5 pairs of lobes, 5-10 cm long, with a pointed tip and serrated margins. The leaves are dark green on top and pale green on the underside, and are arranged alternately on the branches. The whitebeam tree produces white flowers in May, followed by edible, red to purple berries 1-2 cm in diameter, each containing 1-3 seeds.

The berries are a key food source for birds and small mammals, and are also consumed by humans.

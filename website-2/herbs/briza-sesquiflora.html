Briza sesquiflora

Briza sesquiflora, commonly known as six-hill grass or sesquiflora grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its delicate, nodding spikes of small, white or purple flowers.

The health benefits of Briza sesquiflora include its potential to reduce inflammation, improve respiratory function, and exhibit antimicrobial properties, making it a valuable natural remedy for treating various health conditions. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

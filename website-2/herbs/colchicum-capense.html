Colchicum capense

Colchicum capense is a species of autumn-flowering plant in the genus Colchicum, commonly known as the autumn crocus or meadow saffron.

The plant has been utilized in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, as well as its potential to inhibit cancer cell growth and improve symptoms associated with gout. The bioactive constituents of Colchicum capense include colchicine, a well-known alkaloid responsible for the plant's medicinal properties, as well as other glycosides and alkaloids.

In traditional herbal medicine, Colchicum capense is often prepared as a tincture or decoction, and is used to treat various conditions, including arthritis and gout.

Pouteria cotinifolia

Pouteria cotinifolia, also known as lapacho or tabebuia, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical forests of Central and South America, belonging to the family Sapotaceae.

The bark of Pouteria cotinifolia has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of fever, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Pouteria cotinifolia bark include tabebuianine, lapachol, and naphthoquinones, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Pouteria cotinifolia is commonly prepared as a tea or a tincture, and is also used in combination with other herbs to create infusions and extracts for various health purposes.

Sclarea pratensis

Sclarea pratensis, commonly known as Clary Sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and distinctive, blue-gray foliage.

Clary Sage is valued for its potential health benefits, including its ability to reduce anxiety and stress, promote relaxation, and alleviate symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes and mood swings. The bioactive constituents of Clary Sage include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and essential oils, particularly linalool and linalyl acetate, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Clary Sage is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and essential oils, which are often used to promote relaxation, improve sleep quality, and soothe menstrual cramps.

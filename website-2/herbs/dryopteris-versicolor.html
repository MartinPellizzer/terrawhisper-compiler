Dryopteris versicolor

Dryopteris versicolor, also known as the autumn fern or variable leather fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate conditions such as arthritis and improve overall well-being. The bioactive constituents of Dryopteris versicolor include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or dried extract, and is commonly used to support immune function, reduce inflammation, and promote relaxation.

What is Cuminum cyminum?

Cuminum cyminum is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is native to the Middle East and the Indian subcontinent, with a height of 30-60 cm and a taproot that can reach 10 cm in depth. The plant has linear, alternate leaves that are 1-2 cm long, and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in umbels. The fruit is a schizocarp, consisting of two mericarps that are 5-7 mm long, each containing two seeds.

Cuminum cyminum is commonly known as cumin and is a source of cumin seeds, which are used as a spice and for medicinal purposes.

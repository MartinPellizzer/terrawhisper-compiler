Primula filiformis

Primula filiformis, also known as Alpine Primrose, is a small, delicate perennial plant that belongs to the Primulaceae family, characterized by its thin, thread-like leaves and small, white or pink flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, offering potential health benefits in treating wounds, sore throats, and respiratory issues. Primula filiformis contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into salves and creams for topical use.

Cornus multinervosa

Cornus multinervosa, also known as Chinese dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to China, characterized by its showy white flowers and red berries.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antibacterial properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of fever, colds, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Cornus multinervosa include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, particularly cornuside, loganin, and vitexin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Cornus multinervosa include teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as dried berries and extract supplements, which are often used to support immune function and overall well-being.

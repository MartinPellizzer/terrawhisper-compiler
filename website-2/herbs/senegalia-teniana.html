Senegalia teniana

Senegalia teniana, also known as Babool or Vachellia nilotica, is a deciduous thorny tree native to Africa and the Middle East, belonging to the Fabaceae family.

The tree has been traditionally used in various cultures for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which can aid in the treatment of various ailments such as fever, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Senegalia teniana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and condensed tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Senegalia teniana is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat a range of health conditions.

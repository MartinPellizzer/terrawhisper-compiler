What is Simsia sanguinea subsp. albida?

Simsia sanguinea subsp.

albida is a plant species belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is a shrubby herb with stems that are 40-120 cm long, covered with white or grayish hairs, and have a woody base.

The leaves are alternate, sessile, and elliptic in shape, measuring 2-10 cm long and 0.5-2.5 cm wide, with ciliate margins and a glabrous or subglabrous upper surface. The inflorescence is a corymb, bearing white or yellowish flowers with a 4-5 mm long corolla, and an involucre of 10-12 mm long, with 3-4 series of phyllaries.

The flowers are hermaphroditic, and the fruit is a cypsela, measuring 2-4 mm long, with a pappus of 30-40 bristles.

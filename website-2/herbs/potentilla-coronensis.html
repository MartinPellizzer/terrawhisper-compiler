Potentilla coronensis

Potentilla coronensis, also known as the Crowned Cinquefoil, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its clusters of small, pink or white flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in the treatment of various ailments such as wounds, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Potentilla coronensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Potentilla coronensis is often used to prepare infusions, tinctures, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into creams and ointments for topical application.

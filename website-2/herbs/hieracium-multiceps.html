Hieracium multiceps

Hieracium multiceps, commonly known as hawkweed, is a perennial plant species within the dandelion family (Asteraceae) characterized by its yellow flowers and rosette-shaped leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including skin conditions, fever, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Hieracium multiceps include flavonoids, triterpenoids, and sesquiterpene lactones, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with hawkweed include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat skin irritations, wounds, and respiratory issues.

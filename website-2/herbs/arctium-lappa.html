What is Arctium lappa?

Arctium lappa is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Asteraceae, commonly known as the dandelion or burdock family.

It has a large, coarse, hairy stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall, bearing large, deeply lobed, toothed leaves that are 30-60 cm long and 20-40 cm wide. The plant produces purple flowers in a dense, spherical inflorescence, which is 5-10 cm in diameter, and each flower is 2-3 mm long with 5 petals. The plant's fruit is an achene, a type of dry, single-seeded fruit, that is 2-3 mm long and has a pappus of numerous, small, hair-like structures.

The plant's taproot can grow up to 1 meter long and is edible, containing inulin, a type of carbohydrate.

Mentha × smithiana

Mentha × smithiana, also known as Apple Mint, is a hybrid species of mint that belongs to the Lamiaceae family of plants.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its numerous health benefits, including its ability to alleviate digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Apple Mint include menthone, menthol, and menthyl acetate, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal teas, essential oils, and topical preparations, such as creams and ointments, which are used to relieve headaches, soothe skin irritations, and provide fresh breath.

Neoregelia bahiana

Neoregelia bahiana, also known as the Bahian airplant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to the tropical forests of Brazil.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities, making it useful in the treatment of various health conditions such as wounds, infections, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Neoregelia bahiana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Neoregelia bahiana include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to promote wound healing and alleviate symptoms of respiratory and gastrointestinal disorders.

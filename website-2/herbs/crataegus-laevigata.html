Crataegus laevigata

Crataegus laevigata, also known as the English hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and Western Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, which include lowering blood pressure, improving cardiovascular health, and reducing symptoms of anxiety and insomnia. The bioactive constituents of Crataegus laevigata include flavonoids, triterpenoids, and oligomeric proanthocyanidins (OPCs), which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Crataegus laevigata include teas, tinctures, capsules, and tablets, which are often used to support heart health and alleviate symptoms of menopausal hot flashes.

Satureja × mixta

Satureja × mixta, also known as Spanish marjoram, is a hybrid perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family, derived from the cross between Satureja thymbra and Thymus lanuginosus.

The plant has been traditionally used to support digestive health, ease respiratory issues, and exhibit antimicrobial properties, making it a valuable natural remedy for various ailments. The bioactive constituents of Spanish marjoram include carvacrol, thymol, and rosmarinic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Spanish marjoram is often used to make teas, infusions, and essential oils, which are commonly used to relieve digestive discomfort, reduce stress, and promote relaxation.

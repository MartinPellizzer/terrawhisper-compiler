Anthyllis bicolor

Anthyllis bicolor, also known as kidney vetch, is a perennial legume plant species native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its distinctive white and pink flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, providing relief from various health issues, such as kidney stones and urinary tract infections, due to its anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects. The bioactive constituents of Anthyllis bicolor include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Anthyllis bicolor is prepared as a tea, decoction, or infusion, and is also used in the production of herbal tinctures and infusions, making it a popular ingredient in folk medicine.

Cyperus inauratus

Cyperus inauratus, also known as Kakātō, is a perennial sedge plant native to Australia and New Zealand, characterized by its distinctive yellow-brown flowers and edible rhizomes.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including the treatment of fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Cyperus inauratus include iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the rhizomes of Cyperus inauratus are commonly prepared as a decoction or tincture, and are also used in the production of teas and topical creams.

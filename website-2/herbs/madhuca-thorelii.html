Madhuca thorelii

Madhuca thorelii, also known as the 'Tropical Mahua' or 'Indian Butter Tree', is a medium-sized tree native to the Western Ghats region of India.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities. The bioactive constituents of Madhuca thorelii include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to prepare various herbal preparations, including a medicinal oil, a herbal tea, and a syrup, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including fever, cough, and skin diseases.

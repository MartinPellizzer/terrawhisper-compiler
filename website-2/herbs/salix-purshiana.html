Salix purshiana

Salix purshiana, also known as Pacific willow or wild willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest region of North America.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, making it useful for relieving pain and reducing inflammation. The bioactive constituents of Salix purshiana include salicin, a compound similar to aspirin, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

The plant is often used to make herbal preparations such as tinctures and salves, as well as a herbal tea, which is commonly used to treat headaches, fever, and other minor health issues.

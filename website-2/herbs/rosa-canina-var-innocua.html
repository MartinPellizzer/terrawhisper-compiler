What is Rosa canina var. innocua?

Rosa canina var. innocua is a botanical variety of the dog rose species belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems, typically 2-5 meters in height, and ovate to lanceolate leaves that are 4-10 centimeters long and have 3-5 pairs of leaflets. The leaves are usually dark green on the upper surface and pale green on the underside, with a serrated margin. The flowers are fragrant, solitary, and 4-6 centimeters in diameter, with 5 petals and a cluster of stamens.

Rosa canina var. innocua is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world as an ornamental plant.

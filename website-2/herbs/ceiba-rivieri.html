Ceiba rivieri

Ceiba rivieri, also known as the Kapok tree, is a species of deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The health benefits of Ceiba rivieri include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Ceiba rivieri include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ceiba rivieri is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture to treat a range of health conditions, with the most common preparations being a decoction of the bark and leaves, which is used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory issues.

Ipecacuanha fusca

Ipecacuanha fusca, also known as Cephaelis ipecacuanha, is a species of plant native to the tropical forests of South America, specifically Brazil, and is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used for its emetic properties, helping to induce vomiting and relieve symptoms of coughs, colds, and respiratory issues, making it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine. The bioactive constituents of Ipecacuanha fusca include alkaloids, such as emetine and cephaeline, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Ipecacuanha fusca is commonly prepared as an extract or syrup, and is often used in traditional herbal medicine to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and pneumonia.

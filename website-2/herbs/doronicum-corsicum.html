Doronicum corsicum

Doronicum corsicum, also known as Corsican leopard's bane, is a perennial plant species belonging to the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

The bioactive constituents of Doronicum corsicum include sesquiterpene lactones, such as doronicine and leucodrone, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal preparations, Doronicum corsicum is often used in teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as in topical applications such as creams and ointments, to take advantage of its potential therapeutic benefits.

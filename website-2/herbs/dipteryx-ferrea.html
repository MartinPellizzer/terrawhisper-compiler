Dipteryx ferrea

Dipteryx ferrea, also known as the Brazilian ironwood or jatobá, is a large tree native to the tropical forests of South America, particularly in Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

The health benefits of Dipteryx ferrea include its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Dipteryx ferrea is often prepared into herbal teas, infusions, and extracts, as well as being used in the form of tinctures and ointments to treat various health conditions.

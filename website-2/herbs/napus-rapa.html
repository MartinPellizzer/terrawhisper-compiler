Napus rapa

Napus rapa, commonly known as rapeseed or oilseed rape, is a bright-yellow flowering member of the family Brassicaceae, cultivated mainly for its oil-rich seed.

The edible leaves and stems of Napus rapa have been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, digestive issues, and infections, due to its rich content of antioxidants and other bioactive compounds that provide numerous health benefits. The bioactive constituents of Napus rapa include isothiocyanates, glucosinolates, and other sulfur-containing compounds, which have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anticancer properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Napus rapa is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or infused oil, and is used to treat a range of health issues, including colds, fever, and skin conditions.

Tylophora micrantha

Tylophora micrantha, also known as Kunti, is a perennial climber plant native to the tropical regions of Asia, belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including asthma, bronchitis, and skin allergies, due to its anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Tylophora micrantha include alkaloids, flavonoids, and glycosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction or a powder, and is also used to make herbal teas, capsules, and ointments to treat various health conditions.

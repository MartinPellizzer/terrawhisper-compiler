Achillea bulgarica

Achillea bulgarica, also known as Bulgarian yarrow, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its yellow or white flowers and fern-like foliage.

This plant is valued for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties, which are believed to promote wound healing, reduce fever, and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu. The bioactive constituents of Achillea bulgarica include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Bulgarian yarrow is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into salves and creams to soothe and calm skin irritations.

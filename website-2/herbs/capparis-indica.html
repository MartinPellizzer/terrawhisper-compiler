Capparis indica

Capparis indica, also known as the Indian pickling spice or Indian caper, is a perennial plant native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia, belonging to the family Capparaceae.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been attributed to its ability to reduce fever, alleviate digestive issues, and protect against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Capparis indica include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Capparis indica include teas, infusions, and decoctions, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, from digestive issues to fever and infections.

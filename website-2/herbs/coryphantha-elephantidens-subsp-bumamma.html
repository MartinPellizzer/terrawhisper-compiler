What is Coryphantha elephantidens subsp. bumamma?

Coryphantha elephantidens subsp.

bumamma is a cacti subspecies within the Cactaceae family, characterized by its spherical to oblate stems, reaching diameters of 10-15 cm, and heights of 3-6 cm, with 22-30 radial and 4-5 central spines, the latter often reaching 5 cm in length. The stems are covered with fine, white, hair-like areoles and have 5-6 ribs, each with a prominent tubercle, giving a warty appearance.

The flowers are funnel-shaped, pale yellow to brownish, 3.5-4.5 cm in length, and 2.5-3.5 cm in width, with a short, white, woolly perianth. Coryphantha elephantidens subsp. bumamma occurs in the Mexican states of San Luis Potosí and Chihuahua, typically in desert and scrub habitats at elevations of 1,500-2,000 meters.

This subspecies is distinguished from its parent species by its more compact and rounded stems.

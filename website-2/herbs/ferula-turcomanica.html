Ferula turcomanica

Ferula turcomanica, also known as Turcomanian asafoetida, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia, belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

The bioactive constituents of Ferula turcomanica include ferulic acid, coumarins, and terpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Ferula turcomanica is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine and is often prepared in the form of dried resin, essential oil, and powder, which are used to treat a range of health issues, including flatulence, indigestion, and respiratory problems.

Melaleuca cyathifolia

Melaleuca cyathifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of plant in the myrtle family Myrtaceae.

The leaves and branches of this plant have been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including colds, fever, and skin infections, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Melaleuca cyathifolia include cineole, limonene, and carophyllene, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are teas, essential oils, and ointments, which are often used to treat skin conditions such as acne, wounds, and burns.

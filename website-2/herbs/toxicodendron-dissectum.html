Toxicodendron dissectum

Toxicodendron dissectum, commonly known as the Japanese lacquer tree or palmate lacquer tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties, which may aid in treating various conditions such as fever, sore throats, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Toxicodendron dissectum include lacquer resin, which contains a high concentration of urushiol, a phenolic compound with potential therapeutic effects, as well as other terpenes and flavonoids.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often used to make lacquer, an herbal preparation that is applied topically to the skin for its purported medicinal properties, and is also used to make teas and infusions for internal use.

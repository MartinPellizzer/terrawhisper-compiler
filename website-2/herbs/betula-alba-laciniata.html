Betula alba laciniata

Betula alba laciniata, also known as the cut-leaved white birch, is a deciduous tree species belonging to the Betulaceae family.

This plant is valued for its health benefits, including its ability to lower blood pressure, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial properties, making it a popular remedy for various health issues. The bioactive constituents of Betula alba laciniata include salicin, a compound similar to aspirin, as well as flavonoids and polyphenols that contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the bark of Betula alba laciniata is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues.

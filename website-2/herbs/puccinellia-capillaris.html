Puccinellia capillaris

Puccinellia capillaris, also known as saltwort or sea grass, is a perennial salt-tolerant plant that belongs to the grass family (Poaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, providing relief from various health issues, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory problems, due to its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Puccinellia capillaris include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into ointments and salves, to treat a range of ailments.

Boerhavia hiranensis

Boerhavia hiranensis, also known as Indian poke weed or Kanta, is a plant species belonging to the Nyctaginaceae family, characterized by its small, white, and fragrant flowers.

The bioactive constituents of Boerhavia hiranensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, such as boeravinone and boeravinol, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Boerhavia hiranensis is commonly used in herbal preparations like decoctions, infusions, and powders, often in combination with other herbs, to alleviate symptoms associated with colds, coughs, and fever.

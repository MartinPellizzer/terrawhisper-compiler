What is Athyrium filix-femina f. cristatum?

Athyrium filix-femina f. cristatum is a fern cultivar within the species Athyrium filix-femina, belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a slow-growing, semi-evergreen perennial with lance-shaped fronds 60-120 cm long, bearing 2-3-pinnate pinnae, and a fertile frond 10-30 cm long, with sporangia in two rows along the rachis. The plant's rhizome is creeping, with scales 2-5 mm long, and the stipes are 5-15 cm long, dark brown, and glabrous. It is a sterile form of the lady fern, resulting from natural or artificial selection, and is often cultivated for its unique, tree-like growth habit.

The cultivar's growth habit is often described as a 'tree fern', with a single stem growing from the base of the plant.

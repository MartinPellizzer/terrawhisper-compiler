What is Silene vulgaris subsp. commutata?

Silene vulgaris subsp.

commutata is a perennial herbaceous plant in the family Caryophyllaceae, native to Europe and North Africa. It grows 10-40 cm tall, with a rosette of ovate to lanceolate leaves 2-8 cm long and 1-3 cm wide, which are pubescent and ciliate.

The inflorescence is a terminal spike of white or pink flowers, 10-20 mm in diameter, with 5 petals and a 5-10 mm long calyx. The flowers are arranged in a spiral pattern, with 2-5 flowers per node, and are produced from June to September.

The fruits are 3-4 mm long, oblong-elliptical capsules, with 3-4 seeds per fruit.

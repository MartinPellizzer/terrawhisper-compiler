Solanum humile

Solanum humile, also known as the Chilean black nightshade or black nightshade, is a plant species native to the Andean region of South America, belonging to the Solanaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties, which have been reported to aid in wound healing, reduce pain, and protect against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Solanum humile include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Solanum humile is commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion, as well as being used in topical creams and ointments to take advantage of its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

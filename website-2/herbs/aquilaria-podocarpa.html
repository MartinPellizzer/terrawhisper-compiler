Aquilaria podocarpa

Aquilaria podocarpa, also known as Agarwood or Gaharu, is a type of plant that belongs to the mahogany family (Meliaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

The plant's resinous heartwood has been traditionally used in medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, cough, and digestive problems, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Aquilaria podocarpa include agarol, aquilarin, and β-eudesmol, which have been found to possess potent antioxidant and anti-cancer activities.

In traditional medicine, Aquilaria podocarpa is commonly prepared as essential oils, extracts, or decoctions, which are used to treat various ailments, while in modern medicine, it is often used as a fragrance ingredient or a natural preservative.

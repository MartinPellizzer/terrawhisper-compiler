Mecodium salakense

Mecodium salakense, also known as the Salak Fern, is a type of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its unique, delicate foliage and ability to thrive in tropical environments.

The Salak Fern is valued for its potential health benefits, including its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in reducing stress, improving immune function, and supporting overall well-being. Traditionally, the Salak Fern is used in various herbal preparations, such as teas, infusions, and capsules, which are often used to support digestive health, reduce fever, and alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections.

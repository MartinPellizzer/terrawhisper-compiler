Triticum giganteum

Triticum giganteum, also known as giant wheat or wild emmer, is an ancient species of wheat native to the Fertile Crescent region.

This plant has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, offering various health benefits such as reducing inflammation, improving digestive health, and acting as an antiseptic. The bioactive constituents of Triticum giganteum include phenolic compounds, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant are infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often used to treat various ailments, including fever, sore throats, and skin conditions.

Fragaria canadensis

Fragaria canadensis, also known as the wood strawberry or wild strawberry, is a perennial plant species that belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is native to eastern North America.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly its potential to reduce inflammation and improve cardiovascular health, likely due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Fragaria canadensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and anthocyanins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Fragaria canadensis is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat digestive issues, such as diarrhea and stomach ulcers.

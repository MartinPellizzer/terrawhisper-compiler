The bioactive constituents of Pleopeltis argyropus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Pleopeltis argyropus is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including coughs, colds, and skin irritations.

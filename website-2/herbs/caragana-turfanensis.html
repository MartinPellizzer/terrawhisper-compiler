Caragana turfanensis

Caragana turfanensis, also known as the Turpan caragana, is a deciduous shrub native to the Xinjiang region of China, belonging to the pea family (Fabaceae).

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal activities that have been reported to aid in the treatment of various health conditions, such as gastrointestinal issues and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Caragana turfanensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is typically used to make herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as dried extracts, which are commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine.

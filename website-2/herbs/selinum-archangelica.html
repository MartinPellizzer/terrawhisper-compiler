Selinum archangelica

Selinum archangelica, commonly known as angelica or wild celery, is a biennial plant native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its tall, hollow stems and umbrella-like clusters of small white flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antioxidant properties, which can help to alleviate symptoms of colds, flu, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Selinum archangelica include volatile oils, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being used in cooking and as a garnish, particularly in Scandinavian and Asian cuisine.

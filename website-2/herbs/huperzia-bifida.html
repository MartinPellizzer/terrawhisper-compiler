Huperzia bifida

Huperzia bifida, also known as Chinese club moss, is a plant species belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae, characterized by its unique, club-shaped growth habit.

The plant has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits, including improving cognitive function, memory, and mood, as well as possessing antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Huperzia bifida include huperzine A, a potent inhibitor of acetylcholinesterase, as well as other alkaloids and lignans that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Huperzia bifida is commonly used in herbal preparations such as capsules, tablets, and teas, often in combination with other herbs to support brain health and cognitive function.

classification

Within the division Marchantiophyta, Huperzia bifida is placed in the class Haplomitriopsida. This species further falls under the family Huperziaceae. Huperzia bifida is native to New Zealand.

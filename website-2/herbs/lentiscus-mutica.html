Lentiscus mutica

Lentiscus mutica, also known as Mastiche or Lentisk, is a shrub-like evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region, belonging to the Anacardiaceae family.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in the treatment of digestive issues, wounds, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Lentiscus mutica include mastic resin, which contains terpenoids such as alpha-pinene, limonene, and beta-pinene, as well as flavonoids and phenolic compounds.

Mastic resin is extracted and used to make various herbal preparations, including mastic gum, oil, and capsules, which are commonly used to support oral health and digestive well-being.

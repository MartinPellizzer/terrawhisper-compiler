Kochia sericea

Kochia sericea, also known as Chinese burning bush or burning bush, is a perennial herb native to China and Mongolia.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Kochia sericea include flavonoids, alkaloids, glycosides, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Kochia sericea is often prepared into teas, infusions, or tinctures, and is also used in the form of dried leaves, roots, and flowers to treat a range of health conditions.

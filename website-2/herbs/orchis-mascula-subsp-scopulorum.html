What is Orchis mascula subsp. scopulorum?

Orchis mascula subsp.

scopulorum is a subspecies of flowering plant in the family Orchidaceae. It is a terrestrial orchid with a stemless habit, 10-30 cm tall, and a basal rosette of 2-4 leaves, 3-8 cm long, lance-shaped, and dark green.

The inflorescence is a spike of 5-15 flowers, 3-4 cm long, with sepals and petals fused into a labellum, 1.5-2.5 cm long, and a greenish-brown lip, 1.5-2.5 cm long. The flowers are typically purple with a yellow or orange lip and are pollinated by insects.

It is endemic to the Balkan Peninsula, specifically in Albania, Kosovo, and North Macedonia.

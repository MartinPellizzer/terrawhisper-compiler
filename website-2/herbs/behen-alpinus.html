Behen alpinus

Behen alpinus, also known as Bladder Campion, is a perennial plant species native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its pink or white flowers and edible leaves and roots.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, providing potential health benefits such as wound healing, skin protection, and the treatment of digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Behen alpinus include flavonoids, terpenoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Behen alpinus include tinctures, infusions, and ointments, which are used to treat various skin conditions, wounds, and gastrointestinal issues.

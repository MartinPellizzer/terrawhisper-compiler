Septas dentata

Septa dentata, also known as toothpick acacia, is a small, deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa, characterized by its small, white flowers and distinctive thorns.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, cough, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiparasitic properties. The bioactive constituents of Septa dentata include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Septa dentata is often prepared as a tea or infusion, or used in combination with other herbs to make tonic remedies, and is also used to make a decoction, which is a concentrated liquid extract.

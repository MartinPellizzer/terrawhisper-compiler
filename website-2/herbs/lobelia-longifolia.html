Lobelia longifolia

Lobelia longifolia, also known as Lobelia or Indian tobacco, is a flowering plant species belonging to the bellflower family (Campanulaceae).

Lobelia longifolia has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, including treating respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. The bioactive constituents of Lobelia longifolia include alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Lobelia longifolia include tinctures, teas, and topical creams, although its use is generally limited due to potential toxicity and side effects.

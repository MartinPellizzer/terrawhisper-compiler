Papaya spinosa

Papaya spinosa, also known as wild papaya, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions, characterized by its small, edible fruits and medicinal properties.

The health benefits of Papaya spinosa include its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of arthritis, improve digestion, and protect against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of this plant include papain, a proteolytic enzyme, as well as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and carotenoids, which contribute to its medicinal and therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, Papaya spinosa is commonly prepared as a decoction or juice, and is also used to make herbal teas, capsules, and extracts, which are valued for their anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and digestive benefits.

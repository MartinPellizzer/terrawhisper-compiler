Sisyrinchium multiflorum

Sisyrinchium multiflorum, commonly known as Blue-Eyed Grass, is a perennial herb belonging to the iris family (Iridaceae).

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made from Blue-Eyed Grass include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to soothe coughs and promote digestion.

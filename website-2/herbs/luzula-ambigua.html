Luzula ambigua

Luzula ambigua, also known as woodrush, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Juncaceae family, characterized by its dense tufts of grass-like leaves and purple or greenish-brown flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and expectorant properties, which are beneficial in treating respiratory issues such as bronchitis and coughs, as well as skin conditions like eczema and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Luzula ambigua include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoid saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Luzula ambigua is often prepared as a decoction or tincture, and it is sometimes combined with other herbs to create teas, infusions, and topical creams that can be used to soothe and calm irritated skin and mucous membranes.

Taraxacum stipitatum

Taraxacum stipitatum, also known as the Balkan dandelion, is a perennial plant species belonging to the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including digestive issues and skin problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Bioactive constituents of Taraxacum stipitatum include flavonoids, sesquiterpene lactones, and taraxasterol, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

Taraxacum stipitatum is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often employed to alleviate digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing.

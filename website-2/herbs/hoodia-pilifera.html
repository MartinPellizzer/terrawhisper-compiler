Hoodia pilifera

Hoodia pilifera, also known as climbing hoodia or hoodia, is a succulent plant species native to southern Africa, particularly in South Africa and Namibia, belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include alkaloids, flavonoids, and glycosides, including the well-studied compound Hoodia cactiense, which is believed to be responsible for its appetite-suppressing effects.

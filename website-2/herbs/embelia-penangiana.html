Embelia penangiana

Embelia penangiana, commonly known as the "Malaysian snake root", is a plant species native to Southeast Asia, belonging to the family Primulaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, inflammation, and gastrointestinal issues, due to its potential health benefits in reducing oxidative stress, improving immune function, and exhibiting antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Embelia penangiana include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Embelia penangiana is often used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and extracts, as well as being incorporated into formulations such as capsules and tinctures.

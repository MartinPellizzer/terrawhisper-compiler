Picea bifolia

Picea bifolia, also known as Siberian spruce, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in reducing pain and inflammation, as well as promoting wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Picea bifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Picea bifolia include tea, infusion, and essential oil, which are used to treat respiratory issues, improve digestion, and boost the immune system.

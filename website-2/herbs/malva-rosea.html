Malva rosea

Malva rosea, also known as the rose mallow, is a perennial plant species within the mallow family (Malvaceae).

It is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, contributing to its potential in treating various health issues, such as digestive problems, skin conditions, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Malva rosea include mucilages, flavonoids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat a range of health conditions and are also utilized in traditional medicine and as a food supplement.

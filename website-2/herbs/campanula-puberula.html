Campanula puberula

Campanula puberula, also known as Downy Harebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include reducing inflammation, improving respiratory health, and exhibiting antioxidant activities, making it beneficial for treating conditions such as coughs, colds, and bronchitis. The bioactive constituents of Campanula puberula include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The plant is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to soothe respiratory issues and promote overall well-being.

Ranunculus reniger

Ranunculus reniger, also known as the mountain buttercup, is a flowering plant species within the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and delicate, cup-shaped blooms.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory problems, due to its potential anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of R. reniger include ranunculin, protoanemonin, and other alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, R. reniger is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is often used to treat colds, flu, and other respiratory infections, as well as to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing.

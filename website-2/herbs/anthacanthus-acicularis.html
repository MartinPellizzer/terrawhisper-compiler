Anthacanthus acicularis

Anthacanthus acicularis, also known as Devil's Claw or Wood Spider, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used in medicine for its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties, which have been attributed to its potential in alleviating symptoms of arthritis, gout, and other musculoskeletal conditions. Bioactive constituents of Anthacanthus acicularis include harpagoside, a glycoside responsible for its pharmacological effects, as well as other iridoid compounds.

In traditional herbal medicine, Anthacanthus acicularis is often prepared as a root extract or tincture, and is also used in combination with other herbs to make teas, capsules, and topical creams for the relief of pain and inflammation.

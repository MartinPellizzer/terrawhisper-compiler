Echinopsis huascha

Echinopsis huascha, also known as Trichocereus huascha, is a species of cactus native to the Andean region of South America, belonging to the Echinopsis genus of flowering cacti.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiviral properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of colds, flu, and other respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Echinopsis huascha include alkaloids, glycosides, and other compounds, such as trichocerebroside, which have been shown to possess immunomodulatory and antiviral activities.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into various supplements and pharmaceutical products.

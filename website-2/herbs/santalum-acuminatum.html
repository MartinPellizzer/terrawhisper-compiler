Santalum acuminatum

Santalum acuminatum, also known as the Western sandalwood or Quorn sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Santalaceae, native to the deserts of western Australia.

The essential oil extracted from the plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal activities, which provide relief from anxiety, insomnia, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of S. acuminatum include sesquiterpenes, in particular, santalols and santalenes, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The essential oil of S. acuminatum is commonly used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine, and is often incorporated into herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and topical creams.

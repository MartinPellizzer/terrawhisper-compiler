Juniperus vulgaris

Juniperus vulgaris, commonly known as the common juniper, is a small to medium-sized evergreen coniferous tree native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa.

The health benefits of Juniperus vulgaris include its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat digestive issues, such as bloating and indigestion, as well as urinary tract problems. The bioactive constituents of this plant include terpenes, flavonoids, and lignans, including alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, and sabina ketone, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Juniperus vulgaris include essential oils, teas, and tinctures, which are often used in traditional medicine to aid digestion and relieve respiratory issues.

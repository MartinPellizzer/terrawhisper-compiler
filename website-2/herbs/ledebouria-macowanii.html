Ledebouria macowanii

Ledebouria macowanii, also known as the African potato or Leopard's Bane, is a type of succulent plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its small, tuberous roots and shiny, dark green leaves.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional medicine, Ledebouria macowanii is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, or made into a syrup or tincture, to take advantage of its health benefits.

What is Andropogon virginicus var. tetrastachyus?

Andropogon virginicus var. tetrastachyus is a perennial grass that belongs to the family Poaceae, native to the southeastern United States.

It has a rhizomatous growth habit, producing multiple stems from underground rhizomes, and a height range of 1-3 meters. The plant has a basal leaf sheath with a membranous ligule and a flat, linear to lanceolate blade with a prominent midrib and a finely serrated margin. The inflorescence is a dense, erect panicle with four to six branches, each bearing a pair of spikelets, and the spikelets themselves have two fertile florets and two to three sterile florets.

The plant's flowers are wind-pollinated and produce a type of fruit known as a caryopsis.

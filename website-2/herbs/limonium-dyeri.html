Limonium dyeri

Limonium dyeri, also known as Dyers Heath, is a perennial succulent plant native to South Africa, characterized by its small, white or purple flowers and thick, fleshy leaves.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in wound healing and the treatment of respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Limonium dyeri include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditional herbal preparations made from Limonium dyeri include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to treat respiratory conditions and promote digestive health.

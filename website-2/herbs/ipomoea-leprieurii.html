Ipomoea leprieurii

Ipomoea leprieurii, also known as the Convolvulus or Convolvulaceae family, is a perennial vine native to tropical Africa and Madagascar, characterized by its creeping habit and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities, contributing to the relief of various health issues such as wounds, skin infections, and oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea leprieurii include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used in herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and ointments, which are employed to treat various conditions, including wounds, skin irritations, and respiratory issues.

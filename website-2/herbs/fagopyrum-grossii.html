Fagopyrum grossii

Fagopyrum grossii, also known as buckwheat, is a pseudocereal plant that belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its triangular seeds and edible leaves.

The health benefits of buckwheat include its potential to reduce inflammation, improve cardiovascular health, and regulate blood sugar levels, making it a popular ingredient in various health-promoting diets. The bioactive constituents of buckwheat include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and rutin, which contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Traditionally, buckwheat is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and supplements, as well as being a key ingredient in traditional Chinese medicine, particularly in the form of buckwheat leaf tea and buckwheat flour.

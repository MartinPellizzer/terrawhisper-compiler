Lissera hispanica

Lissera hispanica, also known as Spanish Lavender, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its small, fragrant purple flowers and gray-green leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic properties, which help to soothe digestive issues, reduce anxiety and stress, and promote relaxation. The bioactive constituents of Lissera hispanica include linalool, linalyl acetate, and camphor, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Lissera hispanica is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, essential oils, and dried flowers, which are used to promote sleep, relieve menstrual cramps, and calm the mind and body.

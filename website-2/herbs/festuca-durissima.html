Festuca durissima

Festuca durissima, also known as rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and Western Asia, valued for its medicinal properties.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including gastrointestinal problems, respiratory disorders, and inflammation, due to its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and expectorant effects. The bioactive constituents of Festuca durissima include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to alleviate digestive issues and soothe respiratory complaints.

Clerodendrum scandens

Clerodendrum scandens, also known as Glory Bower, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its twining stems and clusters of fragrant, star-shaped flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in reducing fever, relieving respiratory issues, and promoting wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Clerodendrum scandens include clerodendrin, clerodane diterpenes, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Clerodendrum scandens is commonly prepared as a tea, decoction, or tincture to treat various ailments, as well as being used in topical applications for skin and wound care.

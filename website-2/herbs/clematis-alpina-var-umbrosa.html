What is Clematis alpina var. umbrosa?

Clematis alpina var. umbrosa is a perennial flowering plant that belongs to the family Ranunculaceae.

It is a deciduous climber with woody stems that can grow up to 2-4 meters in length, and has dark green, trifoliate leaves with leaflets that are 2-4 cm long and 1-2 cm wide. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are 3-5 cm long and have 4-6 sepals, in shades of white or pale blue, often with a yellowish tint at the base. The flowers are borne in clusters and appear in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, moist soils and partial to full shade, typically found in mountainous regions.

The main herbal preparations of Clematis alpina var. umbrosa are tincture, infusion, tea, salve, and clematis tincture.

Athamanta aurea

Athamanta aurea, also known as Golden Dropseed, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Apiaceae, native to the Mediterranean region.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, which can help alleviate various conditions including digestive issues and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Athamanta aurea include sesquiterpene lactones, coumarins, and flavonoids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Athamanta aurea is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or powder, and is commonly used to treat conditions such as bronchitis and coughs.

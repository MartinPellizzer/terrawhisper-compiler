Alpinia javanica

Alpinia javanica, also known as the Greater Galangal, is a type of rhizomatous perennial plant belonging to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can aid in relieving digestive issues, reducing pain and inflammation, and protecting against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Alpinia javanica include gingerols, shogaols, and sesquiterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Alpinia javanica is commonly prepared as a dried root or rhizome, which is often used to make teas, infusions, or decoctions, as well as being added to various traditional remedies, such as soups and tonics.

classification

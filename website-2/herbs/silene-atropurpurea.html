Silene atropurpurea

Silene atropurpurea, also known as woodland catchfly or black silene, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including respiratory problems, such as bronchitis and coughs, due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Silene atropurpurea include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Silene atropurpurea is often used in traditional herbal medicine to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat respiratory issues, as well as being used in the production of modern herbal supplements.

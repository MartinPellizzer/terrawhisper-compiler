Pteris glauca

Pteris glauca is a species of fern native to tropical Asia, commonly known as the blue fern due to its glaucous leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to reduce fever, alleviate rheumatism, and protect against cell damage. The bioactive constituents of Pteris glauca include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Pteris glauca is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is often used to treat respiratory infections, digestive issues, and skin conditions.

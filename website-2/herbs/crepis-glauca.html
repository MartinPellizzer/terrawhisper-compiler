Crepis glauca

Crepis glauca, also known as hairy hawk's beard or rough hawk's beard, is a perennial plant species belonging to the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

The bioactive constituents of Crepis glauca include sesquiterpenes, phenolic acids, and flavonoids, such as luteolin and quercetin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Crepis glauca include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health issues, from skin conditions to respiratory problems.

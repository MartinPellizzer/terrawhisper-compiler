Lithocarpus himalaicus

Lithocarpus himalaicus, commonly known as Himalayan Lithocarpus or Himalayan Bur oak, is a deciduous tree species native to the Himalayan region.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities, which may aid in treating various ailments such as fever, cough, and digestive issues. Bioactive constituents of Lithocarpus himalaicus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, the plant is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, decoctions, and infusions, which are often marketed as tonics to support immune function and overall well-being.

Silene pumilio

Silene pumilio, also known as small-flowered catchfly, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Silene pumilio include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and phenolic glycosides, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In herbal medicine, Silene pumilio is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is used to treat conditions such as bronchitis, coughs, and sore throats.

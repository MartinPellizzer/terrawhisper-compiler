What is Sorbus aucuparia var. hybrida?

Sorbus aucuparia var. hybrida is a hybrid of Sorbus aucuparia, belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing 10-20 meters in height, with a broad, rounded crown and a straight, columnar trunk. The leaves are pinnate, 10-20 cm long, with 9-17 leaflets that are elliptical to lanceolate, 2-5 cm long, and have a finely serrated margin. The flowers are small, white to greenish-yellow, and arranged in corymbose cymes, while the fruit is a red, berry-like pome, 8-12 mm in diameter, and edible.

The bark is smooth and grey, becoming brown and fissured with age, and the wood is hard and white.

The improper use of Sorbus aucuparia var. hybrida can also exacerbate existing medical conditions, making it essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using this plant for medicinal purposes.

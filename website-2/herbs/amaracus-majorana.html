Amaracus majorana

Amaracus majorana, also known as sweet majoran or majoram, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae) characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and purple flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, helping to alleviate digestive issues, improve respiratory health, and reduce stress. The bioactive constituents of Amaracus majorana include carvone, limonene, and beta-pinene, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include essential oils, teas, and infusions, which are often used to soothe digestive problems, promote relaxation, and enhance mental clarity.

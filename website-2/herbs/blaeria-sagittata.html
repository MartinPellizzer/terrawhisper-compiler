Blaeria sagittata

Blaeria sagittata, also known as the arrow-leaved blaeia or Blaeria, is a small, shrub-like plant species belonging to the heath family (Ericaceae).

This plant has been traditionally used in South Africa to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Blaeria sagittata include iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Blaeria sagittata is typically prepared as a decoction or infusion to make teas, and it is also sometimes used in traditional medicine as a powder or capsule.

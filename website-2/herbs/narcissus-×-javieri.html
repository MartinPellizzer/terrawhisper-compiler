Narcissus × javieri

Narcissus × javieri, also known as the Spanish narcissus or Javalera, is a hybrid plant species within the Amaryllidaceae family, characterized by its fragrant white flowers.

Traditionally, the plant has been used to treat various health conditions, including digestive issues and respiratory problems, due to its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Narcissus × javieri include alkaloids, such as galantamine, and other compounds like flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Narcissus × javieri include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to alleviate symptoms of colds, coughs, and other respiratory issues.

This classification reflects its complex ancestry and genetic makeup. Within the horticultural and botanical communities, Narcissus × javieri is recognized and documented accordingly.

Cicuta trachypleura

Cicuta trachypleura, also known as water hemlock, is a highly toxic and invasive perennial plant native to North America.

While it is toxic and can be fatal if ingested, some traditional herbalists have reported that small, controlled amounts of water hemlock may have potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of water hemlock include sesquiterpene lactones, alkaloids, and other toxic compounds such as cicutoxin and oenanthotoxin.

In traditional herbal medicine, water hemlock is often prepared as a tincture or infused in oil, although it is essential to exercise extreme caution when handling this plant due to its high toxicity.

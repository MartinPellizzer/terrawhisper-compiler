Aristida vulgaris

Aristida vulgaris, commonly known as six-angled blue grass or six-angled wire grass, is a perennial grass species belonging to the family Poaceae.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can aid in soothing digestive issues, reducing fever, and protecting against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Aristida vulgaris include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Aristida vulgaris is used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and decoctions to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive disorders.

Arenaria tejedensis

Arenaria tejedensis, also known as Tejeda sandwort, is a low-growing, succulent perennial plant native to the Canary Islands, characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and ability to thrive in dry environments.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in wound healing, reducing fever, and protecting against infections. Bioactive constituents of Arenaria tejedensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Arenaria tejedensis is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used topically in creams and ointments to treat skin conditions and promote wound healing.

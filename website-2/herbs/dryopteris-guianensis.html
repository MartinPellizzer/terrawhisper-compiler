Dryopteris guianensis

Dryopteris guianensis, also known as the Guiana shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the genus Dryopteris, characterized by its deciduous fronds and native habitat in the Guiana Shield region of South America.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which may help to reduce inflammation, prevent infections, and protect against cell damage. Bioactive constituents of D. guianensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which have been shown to contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, D. guianensis is used to make various herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues.

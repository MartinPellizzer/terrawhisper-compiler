Ajuga moschata

Ajuga moschata, also known as sweet bugle or sweet bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to Eastern Europe and Asia, characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including respiratory problems, fever, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Ajuga moschata include iridoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Ajuga moschata is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into salves and ointments for topical application.

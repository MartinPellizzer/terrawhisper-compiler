Commiphora grandifolia

Commiphora grandifolia, also known as the "paperbark tree," is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa, belonging to the family Burseraceae.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, providing relief from fever, inflammation, and skin conditions due to its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory activities. The bioactive constituents of Commiphora grandifolia include sesquiterpenes, diterpenes, and coumarins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, the resin of Commiphora grandifolia is often used to prepare herbal remedies, such as ointments, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health conditions.

Euphorbia mucronulata

Euphorbia mucronulata, also known as the spiny spurge, is a succulent plant species native to tropical Africa, characterized by its thorny stems and small, green leaves.

Traditionally, Euphorbia mucronulata has been used in folk medicine for its health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which can help to alleviate wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Euphorbia mucronulata include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Euphorbia mucronulata is often prepared as a tincture or infused in hot water to make a tea, which is used to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and skin infections.

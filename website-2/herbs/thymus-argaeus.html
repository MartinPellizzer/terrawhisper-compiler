Thymus argaeus

Thymus argaeus, also known as Aghayan thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

Thymus argaeus is valued for its potential health benefits, including its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as respiratory issues and digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, particularly thymol and carvacrol, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Thymus argaeus is commonly used in traditional herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to treat coughs, colds, and other respiratory infections.

Agelanthus glomeratus

Agelanthus glomeratus, also known as the sticky mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that obtains its nutrients by connecting to the roots of a host plant.

The bioactive constituents of Agelanthus glomeratus include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

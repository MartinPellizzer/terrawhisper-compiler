Manulea capensis

Manulea capensis, also known as Cape manulea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, wounds, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Manulea capensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are among the most common herbal preparations made with Manulea capensis.

classification

Manulea capensis is classified in the family Scrophulariaceae, which is now often included in the family Plantaginaceae. This plant is further classified into the genus Manulea, which belongs to the tribe Veroniceae. The species Manulea capensis is native to South Africa. It is a member of the angiosperm clade and the eudicots subgroup. Manulea capensis is also known to be a member of the order Lamiales.

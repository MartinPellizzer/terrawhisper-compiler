Curcuma glauca

Curcuma glauca, also known as Java turmeric, is a tropical plant species belonging to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae), native to Southeast Asia.

The rhizome of Curcuma glauca has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Curcuma glauca include curcuminoids, which include demethoxycurcumin, bisdemethoxycurcumin, and other phenolic compounds, as well as volatile oils and sesquiterpenes.

In traditional medicine, Curcuma glauca is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and decoctions, which are used to treat a range of health issues, including arthritis, wounds, and skin conditions.

Grindelia nana columbiana

Grindelia nana columbiana, also known as Dwarf Gumweed or Western Gumweed, is a perennial plant species belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is native to the western regions of North America.

Traditionally, Grindelia nana columbiana has been used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, coughs, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include sesquiterpene lactones, such as grindeliin, grindelane, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Grindelia nana columbiana is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma.

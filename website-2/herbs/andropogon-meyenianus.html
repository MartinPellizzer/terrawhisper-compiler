Andropogon meyenianus

Andropogon meyenianus, also known as Maku'awa or Hawaiian giant grass, is a perennial grass native to Hawaii and other Pacific Island regions, belonging to the Poaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including the treatment of fever, cough, and respiratory issues due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Andropogon meyenianus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional Hawaiian medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a tea or infused in hot water to treat various ailments, as well as being used in topical applications for skin conditions and wounds.

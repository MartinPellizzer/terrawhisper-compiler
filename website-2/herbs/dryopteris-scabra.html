Dryopteris scabra

Dryopteris scabra, also known as scaly male fern, is a type of fern native to East Asia and parts of Europe, characterized by its scaly rhizome and lance-shaped fronds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which may help alleviate conditions such as arthritis, digestive issues, and skin problems, making it a popular choice in traditional herbal medicine. The bioactive constituents of Dryopteris scabra include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or decoction, and is sometimes used in combination with other herbs to enhance its health-promoting properties.

What is Verbascum thapsus?

Verbascum thapsus is a biennial plant native to Europe and Western Asia, belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It grows to 2-3 meters in height, with a stout stem and large, woolly leaves that are 20-30 centimeters long and 10-15 centimeters wide. The plant produces a large number of small, yellow flowers in a dense spike, each 2-3 centimeters long and 1-2 centimeters wide, in the second year of growth. The flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, and the plant produces a seed head that contains numerous small seeds.

The seeds are dispersed by wind and water, allowing the plant to spread quickly in suitable habitats.

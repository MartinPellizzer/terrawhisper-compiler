Ranunculus longisectus

Ranunculus longisectus, also known as the Long-sected Buttercup, is a perennial plant species in the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and distinctive, long-sected leaves.

Bioactive constituents of Ranunculus longisectus include alkaloids, saponins, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

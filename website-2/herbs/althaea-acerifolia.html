Althaea acerifolia

Althaea acerifolia, also known as maple mallow, is a perennial plant species native to eastern North America, characterized by its large, maple-like leaves and showy white flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and soothing effects on the digestive system, making it a popular treatment for gastrointestinal issues and skin irritations. Althaea acerifolia contains bioactive constituents such as mucilages, flavonoids, and polyphenols, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant include decoctions, infusions, and tinctures, often used to treat conditions like mouth sores, wounds, and eczema.

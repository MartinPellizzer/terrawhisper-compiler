Ferula moschata

Ferula moschata, commonly known as the musk ferula, is a perennial plant species belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

The health benefits of Ferula moschata include its potential anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as wounds, skin infections, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include ferulic acid, feruline, and other volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Ferula moschata is commonly used in herbal preparations, including essential oils, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health conditions and promote overall well-being.

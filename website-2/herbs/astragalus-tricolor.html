Astragalus tricolor

Astragalus tricolor, also known as Huang Qi, is a perennial plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its various health benefits, including immune system modulation, anti-inflammatory effects, and antioxidant properties, which may help to prevent or alleviate conditions such as cancer, diabetes, and cardiovascular disease. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus tricolor include polysaccharides, flavonoids, saponins, and triterpenoids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Astragalus tricolor is often prepared as a decoction or tincture, and is also commonly used in combination with other herbs to make formulas for treating respiratory, gastrointestinal, and immune-related disorders.

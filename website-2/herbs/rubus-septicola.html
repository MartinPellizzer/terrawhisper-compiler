Rubus septicola

Rubus septicola, also known as the Japanese raspberry, is a species of bramble in the Rosaceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Rubus septicola include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Rubus septicola is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as in traditional medicine formulas to support immune function and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu.

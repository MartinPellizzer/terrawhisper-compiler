Burragea frutescens

Burragea frutescens, also known as "Kokum," is a plant species native to the Western Ghats of India, belonging to the Rutaceae family and used in traditional medicine.

The health benefits of Burragea frutescens include its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various health issues such as fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Burragea frutescens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, particularly the alkaloid and glycoside compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Burragea frutescens include extracts, infusions, and decoctions, often used in traditional Indian medicine, Ayurveda, to treat a range of health conditions.

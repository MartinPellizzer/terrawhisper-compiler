Genista involucrata

Genista involucrata, also known as Corky broom or Corky broom spiny broom, is a shrubby legume plant that belongs to the pea family (Fabaceae).

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Genista involucrata include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Genista involucrata include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, such as coughs and bronchitis, and to promote wound healing.

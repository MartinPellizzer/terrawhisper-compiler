Woodsia calcarea

Woodsia calcarea, also known as Rocky Mountain cliff fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the genus Woodsia, characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained environments.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to reduce pain and inflammation, as well as protect against oxidative stress. Bioactive constituents of Woodsia calcarea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Woodsia calcarea is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of conditions, including arthritis, gout, and other inflammatory disorders.

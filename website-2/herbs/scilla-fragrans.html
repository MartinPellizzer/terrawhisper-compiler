Scilla fragrans

Scilla fragrans, also known as the Autumn-flowering or fragrant Scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family, native to the Mediterranean region.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include reducing inflammation, improving respiratory function, and possessing antimicrobial properties, making it a popular natural remedy for colds, coughs, and other respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Scilla fragrans include alkaloids, saponins, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Scilla fragrans include tinctures, infusions, and syrups, often used to treat respiratory conditions and to promote relaxation and relief from congestion.

Rosa canina globularis

Rosa canina globularis, also known as the dog rose or rosehip, is a shrub native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa, characterized by its fragrant flowers and edible fruits.

The bioactive constituents of Rosa canina globularis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and carotenoids, which have been shown to exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

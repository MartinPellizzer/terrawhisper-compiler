Huperzia intermedia

Huperzia intermedia, also known as the jointed club moss, is a species of evergreen shrub in the Huperziaceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support cognitive function and memory, as well as to alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression. The bioactive constituents of Huperzia intermedia include huperzine A, a potent inhibitor of acetylcholinesterase, which is an enzyme that breaks down the neurotransmitter acetylcholine, and other alkaloids with neuroprotective and antioxidant properties.

Huperzia intermedia is commonly used in herbal preparations such as extracts, capsules, and tablets, as well as in traditional Chinese medicine formulas, where it is valued for its ability to enhance mental clarity and promote overall well-being.

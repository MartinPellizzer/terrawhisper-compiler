What is Daucus carota var. mauritanicus?

Daucus carota var. mauritanicus is a variety of wild carrot that belongs to the family Apiaceae.

It is an annual or biennial herbaceous plant that grows up to 1 meter in height, with a taproot and a branched stem bearing compound leaves composed of 3-5 pairs of pinnate leaflets. The plant produces small white or pale yellow umbels of flowers in summer, followed by a cluster of brownish seeds. The root is edible, like that of the cultivated carrot (Daucus carota var. sativus), but is smaller and more fibrous.

Daucus carota var. mauritanicus is native to North Africa and the Mediterranean region.

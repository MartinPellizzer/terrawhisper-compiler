Senna argentea

Senna argentea, also known as silver cassia, is a flowering plant species in the legume family that is native to tropical Africa and Madagascar.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, and its health benefits include treating constipation, diarrhea, and other gastrointestinal issues due to its anthraquinone glycosides, which act as laxatives. The bioactive constituents of Senna argentea include sennosides, which are responsible for its purgative and laxative effects, as well as other compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

Senna argentea is commonly used to make herbal teas, capsules, and tinctures, which are used to support digestive health and relieve symptoms of constipation and other gastrointestinal issues.

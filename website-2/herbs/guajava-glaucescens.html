Guajava glaucescens

Guajava glaucescens, also known as Psidium glaucescens, is a plant species that belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help to alleviate symptoms of diarrhea, fever, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Guajava glaucescens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Guajava glaucescens is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and extracts, which are often used to treat gastrointestinal disorders and promote overall health and well-being.

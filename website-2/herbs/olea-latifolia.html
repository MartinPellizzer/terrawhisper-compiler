Olea latifolia

Olea latifolia, also known as the broad-leaved olive tree, is a plant species native to tropical Africa, characterized by its broad leaves and small, white flowers.

The leaves of Olea latifolia have been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Olea latifolia include oleuropein, ursolic acid, and oleanolic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Olea latifolia is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or decoction, and is also used in the production of essential oils and topical creams.

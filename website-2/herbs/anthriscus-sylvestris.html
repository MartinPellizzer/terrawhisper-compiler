Anthriscus sylvestris

Anthriscus sylvestris, also known as wild chervil or cow parsley, is a biennial herb native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its fern-like foliage and umbrella-shaped clusters of small white flowers.

The plant is valued for its health benefits, which include its ability to reduce inflammation, improve digestion, and act as a natural diuretic. The bioactive constituents of wild chervil include coumarins, flavonoids, and volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Wild chervil is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as in traditional remedies for various health issues, including menstrual cramps and urinary tract infections.

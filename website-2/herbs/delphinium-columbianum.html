Delphinium columbianum

Delphinium columbianum, also known as Columbian larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest region of North America, characterized by its tall spikes of blue to purple flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which may help to soothe wounds, reduce pain, and combat infections. The bioactive constituents of Delphinium columbianum include delphinidins, a class of anthocyanidins responsible for its blue color, as well as other alkaloids and glycosides with potential medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Columbian larkspur is often prepared as a tincture or infusion, as well as being used in topical applications such as salves and creams to take advantage of its therapeutic properties.

Anthoxanthum arcticum

Anthoxanthum arcticum, commonly known as Arctic oat grass, is a perennial grass native to the Arctic regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities that contribute to its potential in treating various health issues, such as wounds, digestive problems, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Anthoxanthum arcticum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Arctic oat grass include tea, infusions, and tinctures, which are traditionally used to soothe digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing.

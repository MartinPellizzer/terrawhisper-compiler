Frangula dodonei

Frangula dodonaei, also known as Buckthorn or Alder Buckthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and Western Asia, belonging to the Rhamnaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used for its laxative properties, and its health benefits include treating constipation, diarrhea, and other gastrointestinal issues due to its anthraquinone content, which stimulates bowel movements and aids in detoxification. The bioactive constituents of Frangula dodonaei include anthraquinone glycosides, such as frangulins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Frangula dodonaei is commonly prepared as a dried bark, or as an extract, and is used in teas, infusions, and capsules to support digestive health and promote bowel regularity.

Amaranthus pubescens

Amaranthus pubescens, commonly known as the desert holly or woolly amaranth, is a plant species belonging to the Amaranthaceae family, characterized by its small, green, and hairy leaves.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, contributing to its potential in treating various health issues, such as wounds, fever, and digestive problems. Bioactive constituents of Amaranthus pubescens include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often used in traditional medicine to treat fever, cough, and respiratory issues.

Brassica rupestris

Brassica rupestris, also known as alpine cress or mountain rocket, is a perennial herb of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and peppery, mustard-like flavor.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as cancer and cardiovascular disease. The bioactive constituents of Brassica rupestris include glucosinolates, isothiocyanates, and other sulfur-containing compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is often used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as in salads and as a garnish, where its peppery flavor is prized for its ability to stimulate digestion and circulation.

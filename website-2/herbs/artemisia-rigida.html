Artemisia rigida

Artemisia rigida, also known as stiff sage or stick sage, is a perennial plant species native to North America, characterized by its woody stems and silvery-gray foliage.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, including its ability to aid in digestion, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant activities, making it a potential remedy for various health issues. The bioactive constituents of Artemisia rigida include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Artemisia rigida is commonly used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat gastrointestinal issues, fever, and skin conditions.

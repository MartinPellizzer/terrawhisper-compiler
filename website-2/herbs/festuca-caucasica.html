Festuca caucasica

Festuca caucasica, also known as Caucasian fescue, is a perennial grass native to the Caucasus region, characterized by its slender leaves and delicate purple flowers.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to reduce inflammation and protect against cell damage. The bioactive constituents of Festuca caucasica include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Festuca caucasica is commonly used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are traditionally used to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu.

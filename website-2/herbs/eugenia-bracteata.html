Eugenia bracteata

Eugenia bracteata, also known as the Eugenia bracteata or Guava Berry, is a tropical plant species native to the Americas, characterized by its small, red or purple fruits and fragrant leaves.

The health benefits of Eugenia bracteata include its potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may contribute to the treatment of various conditions such as ulcers, wounds, and infections. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, Eugenia bracteata is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat digestive issues, fever, and respiratory problems.

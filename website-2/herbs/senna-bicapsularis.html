Senna bicapsularis

Senna bicapsularis, also known as Cassia auriculata or Sickle Bush, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Asia and Africa.

Traditionally, the plant has been used for its medicinal properties, offering relief from digestive issues such as constipation, thanks to its anthraquinone glycosides that stimulate bowel movements and relieve symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome. The bioactive constituents of Senna bicapsularis include anthraquinones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its pharmacological activities.

In herbal medicine, Senna bicapsularis is often prepared as a tea, capsule, or powder, and is commonly used to support digestive health and relieve symptoms of constipation.

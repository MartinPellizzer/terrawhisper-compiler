Xanthium albinum

Xanthium albinum is a perennial plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family, commonly known as the cocklebur.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine due to its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in reducing pain and inflammation, as well as treating infections and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Xanthium albinum include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Xanthium albinum is often prepared as a decoction or an infusion, as well as being used in the form of a tincture, to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues.

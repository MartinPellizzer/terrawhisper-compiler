What is Koeleria albovii var. buschiana?

Koeleria albovii var. buschiana is a plant taxon within the Poaceae family, characterized by its perennial growth habit, typically found in mountainous regions.

It features culms 10-40 cm tall, with flat, linear to lance-shaped leaves, and a panicle with spikelets containing 2-5 florets. Each spikelet has a lower glume, a longer upper glume, and 2-3 fertile florets. The plant's inflorescence is a panicle, consisting of a central axis bearing numerous spikelets, and it typically flowers in June and July.

The specific taxon has a geographical distribution limited to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

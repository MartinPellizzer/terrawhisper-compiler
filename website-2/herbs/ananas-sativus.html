Ananas sativus

Ananas sativus, commonly known as pineapple, is a tropical plant species belonging to the family Bromeliaceae.

The health benefits of pineapple include anti-inflammatory properties, aiding in digestion and relieving symptoms of nausea, as well as providing antioxidant and immune-boosting effects due to its high content of vitamin C and manganese. The bioactive constituents of pineapple include bromelain, a mixture of proteolytic enzymes, and various flavonoids, including fisetin, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pineapple is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as juice, tea, and capsules, which are often consumed for their digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.

The Ananas sativus species is further divided into two main varieties: the sweet pineapple and the acidic pineapple. These classifications are based on factors such as the plant's physical characteristics, growth habits, and fruit production. By understanding the classification of Ananas sativus, researchers and botanists can better comprehend the plant's evolution and behavior.

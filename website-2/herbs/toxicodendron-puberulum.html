Toxicodendron puberulum

side effects

Toxicodendron puberulum, also known as the hairy stinging nettle tree, contains urushiol, a potent allergen that can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions. Contact with the plant's sap can lead to redness, itching, swelling, and blistering on the skin. In severe cases, exposure to urushiol can cause anaphylaxis, a life-threatening allergic reaction that requires immediate medical attention. The severity of the reaction can vary depending on individual sensitivity and the amount of exposure to the plant.

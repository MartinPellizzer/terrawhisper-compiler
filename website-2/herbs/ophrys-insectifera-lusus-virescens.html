Ophrys insectifera lusus virescens

Ophrys insectifera lusus virescens is a rare, greenish-yellow variant of the bee orchid (Ophrys insectifera), a type of orchid that mimics the appearance and scent of female insects to attract male pollinators.

The bee orchid has been traditionally used in herbal medicine, offering potential health benefits such as antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties, which may aid in wound healing and reducing inflammation. The plant is rich in bioactive constituents including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Herbal preparations made with the bee orchid include infusions, tinctures, and salves, which are used to treat various health issues, including skin conditions and infections.

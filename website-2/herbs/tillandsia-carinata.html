Tillandsia carinata

Tillandsia carinata, also known as the "Mexican air plant," is a type of epiphytic succulent that belongs to the Bromeliad family and is native to the tropical regions of Mexico and Central America.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its ability to inhibit inflammation and reduce oxidative stress, making it a popular natural remedy for various conditions such as arthritis and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Tillandsia carinata include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Tillandsia carinata is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and capsules, and is also used as a topping for salads and other dishes due to its unique flavor and texture.

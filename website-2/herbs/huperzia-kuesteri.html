Huperzia kuesteri

Huperzia kuesteri, also known as Kuester's liverwort or fir club moss, is a species of evergreen, perennial plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, including its ability to improve cognitive function, boost memory, and exhibit neuroprotective properties, making it a subject of interest in the study of neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's. The bioactive constituents of Huperzia kuesteri include huperzine A, a potent inhibitor of acetylcholinesterase, which plays a key role in the regulation of neurotransmitters in the brain.

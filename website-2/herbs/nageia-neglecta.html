Nageia neglecta

Nageia neglecta is a species of coniferous tree in the family Podocarpaceae, native to the tropical forests of New Guinea and nearby islands.

The leaves and bark of Nageia neglecta have been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory infections, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Nageia neglecta include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which have been shown to exhibit antioxidant, antiviral, and antifungal activities.

In traditional medicine, Nageia neglecta is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, and its leaves and bark can also be used to make teas, tinctures, and salves.

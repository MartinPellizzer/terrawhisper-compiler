Ipomoea speciosa

Ipomoea speciosa, also known as the exotic morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea speciosa include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various ailments, including fever, cough, and skin conditions.

Citronella mucronata

Citronella mucronata, also known as Java citronella, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia, characterized by its distinctive lemon-like fragrance.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of fever, digestive issues, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Citronella mucronata include citronella oil, which contains compounds such as citronellal, geraniol, and limonene, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

In herbal medicine, Citronella mucronata is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and essential oils, which are often used to repel insects, soothe digestive issues, and promote relaxation.

Aconitum napellus lusus ambiguum

Aconitum napellus lusus ambiguum, commonly known as Monk's Hood or Aconitum, is a perennial plant species in the Aconitum genus, characterized by its unique, hooded flowers and toxic properties.

Traditionally, Aconitum has been used to treat a range of health conditions, including pain, inflammation, and fever, due to its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of Aconitum include alkaloids, such as aconitine, mesaconitine, and hypaconitine, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In herbal medicine, Aconitum is most commonly prepared as a tincture, decoction, or powdered extract, often used in homeopathic and traditional Chinese medicine to treat conditions such as arthritis, rheumatism, and respiratory disorders.

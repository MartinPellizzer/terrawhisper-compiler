Toxicodendron villosum

Toxicodendron villosum, also known as the Japanese lacquer tree or Japanese poison oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia, characterized by its shiny, dark green leaves and its ability to produce a potent allergenic sap.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antipyretic properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Toxicodendron villosum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Asian herbal medicine, Toxicodendron villosum is commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion, often in combination with other herbs, to treat various health conditions.

side effects

Toxicodendron villosum, also known as the poison oak of Asia, contains urushiol, an oil that causes severe skin irritation and allergic reactions. When the sap of this plant comes into contact with the skin, it can cause redness, itching, swelling, and blistering. In severe cases, the reaction can lead to increased heart rate, fever, and in rare instances, anaphylaxis, a life-threatening allergic reaction. The effects of Toxicodendron villosum can be long-lasting, and repeated exposure can cause a condition known as contact dermatitis. People who are sensitive to urushiol may experience these side effects even after a single exposure to the plant's sap.

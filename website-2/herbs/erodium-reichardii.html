Erodium reichardii

Erodium reichardii, also known as Reichard's geranium or heron's bill, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the geranium family (Geraniaceae).

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antioxidant effects, which have been traditionally used to treat various health issues such as wounds, skin conditions, and gastrointestinal problems. The bioactive constituents of Erodium reichardii include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, Erodium reichardii is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion, and is used to treat conditions such as diarrhea, dyspepsia, and skin irritations.

Helianthemum cinereum

Helianthemum cinereum, also known as rockrose, is a low-growing, perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and silvery-green foliage.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including digestive problems, respiratory issues, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Helianthemum cinereum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with rockrose include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which can be used to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu.

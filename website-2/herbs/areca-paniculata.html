Areca paniculata

Areca paniculata, commonly known as the Areca palm or Betel nut palm, is a species of palm tree native to tropical Asia.

The health benefits of Areca paniculata include its potential to aid in digestion, relieve stress and anxiety, and exhibit antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include arecoline, a psychoactive alkaloid, as well as other compounds like arecoline n-oxide and arecoline N-glucuronide, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the most common herbal preparations made with Areca paniculata include betel quid, a chewable mixture that combines the plant's nuts with other ingredients like areca nut paste, tobacco, and spices.

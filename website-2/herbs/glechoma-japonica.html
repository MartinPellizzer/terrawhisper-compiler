Glechoma japonica

Glechoma japonica, also known as Japanese mint or Japanese bugleweed, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

The plant is valued for its health benefits, which include its ability to soothe digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Glechoma japonica include iridoid glycosides, such as japonicone, and sesquiterpene lactones, like glechomine, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Glechoma japonica is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into herbal capsules and topical creams, to harness its health-promoting effects.

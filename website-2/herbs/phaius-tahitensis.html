Phaius tahitensis

Phaius tahitensis, also known as the Tahitian orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the family Orchidaceae.

It has been traditionally used in Polynesian medicine for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties, as well as its ability to support cardiovascular and respiratory health. The bioactive constituents of Phaius tahitensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Phaius tahitensis is often prepared as a tea or tincture, and is also used in topical applications to promote wound healing and skin health.

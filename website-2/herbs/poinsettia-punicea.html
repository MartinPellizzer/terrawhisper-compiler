Poinsettia punicea

Poinsettia punicea, also known as the Mexican firecracker or firecracker poinsettia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its purported health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which can aid in wound healing and reducing fever. The bioactive constituents of Poinsettia punicea include poinsettin, a naphthoquinone compound with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, as well as other compounds such as flavonoids and terpenoids.

In herbal medicine, Poinsettia punicea is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are valued for their ability to reduce pain and inflammation, and to treat respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma.

Poinsettia punicea, also known as the Firecracker Poinsettia, can be toxic to humans and animals if ingested. The plant contains sap that can cause skin irritation, and if ingested, it can lead to symptoms such as vomiting, diarrhea, and excessive drooling. In severe cases, ingesting Poinsettia punicea can cause more serious issues, including respiratory problems and cardiac issues. It's essential to keep the plant out of reach of children and pets to avoid any potential harm.

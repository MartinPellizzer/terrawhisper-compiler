Colchicum villosum

Colchicum villosum, also known as autumn crocus, is a perennial plant species in the genus Colchicum, characterized by its striking flowers and toxic underground corms.

The plant is traditionally used in medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which make it beneficial for treating conditions such as arthritis and gout, as well as reducing pain and swelling. The bioactive constituents of Colchicum villosum include colchicine, a glycoside responsible for the plant's medicinal properties, as well as other compounds like colchicines and demecolcine, which exhibit anti-inflammatory and antitumor activities.

In herbal medicine, Colchicum villosum is often prepared as a tincture or powder, and is also used in combination with other herbs to create remedies for pain relief and inflammation.

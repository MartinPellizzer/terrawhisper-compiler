Angelica sibirica

Angelica sibirica, also known as Sibirian angelica, is a perennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its tall stem, umbellate inflorescence, and distinctive leaf morphology.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antispasmodic properties. The bioactive constituents of Angelica sibirica include sesquiterpenes, phenolic acids, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Angelica sibirica is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into poultices and salves to treat localized inflammation and pain.

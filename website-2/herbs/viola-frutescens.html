Viola frutescens

Viola frutescens, also known as blue-joint sweet violet or wild blue violet, is a low-growing, perennial plant species belonging to the violet family (Violaceae).

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making it beneficial for treating various health issues such as wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Viola frutescens include flavonoids, anthocyanins, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Viola frutescens is often prepared as a tincture, infusion, or dried herb, and is used to treat conditions such as colds, fever, and digestive issues, as well as to promote wound healing and soothe skin irritations.

Chrysanthemum sylvaticum

Chrysanthemum sylvaticum, also known as the woodland chrysanthemum, is a perennial plant species belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae) that is native to East Asia and Europe.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help to alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Chrysanthemum sylvaticum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Chrysanthemum sylvaticum is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly consumed to promote respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu.

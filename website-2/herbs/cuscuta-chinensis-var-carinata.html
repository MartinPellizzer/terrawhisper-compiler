What is Cuscuta chinensis var. carinata?

Cuscuta chinensis var. carinata is a parasitic plant belonging to the family Convolvulaceae.

It is a type of dodder that has a yellowish to reddish-brown stem and yellowish flowers with four to five petals, which are typically arranged in a raceme. The plant's leaves are reduced to scale-like structures, and it has no chlorophyll, relying on its host plant for photosynthesis. Cuscuta chinensis var. carinata has a twining habit, with stems that can grow up to 20 cm in length, and it is typically found growing on the stems of host plants in a climbing habit.

The plant's rootless stems contain numerous small, yellowish to orange-colored flowers, which are the reproductive structures of the plant.

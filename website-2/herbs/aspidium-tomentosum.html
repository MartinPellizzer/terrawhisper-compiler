Aspidium tomentosum

Aspidium tomentosum, also known as Asplenium tomentosum, is a species of fern native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of fever, pain, and infection. The bioactive constituents of Aspidium tomentosum include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Aspidium tomentosum is often prepared as a decoction, tincture, or tea, and is used to treat a variety of ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory infections.

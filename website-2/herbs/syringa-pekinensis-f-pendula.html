What is Syringa pekinensis f. pendula?

Syringa pekinensis f. pendula is a cultivar of the Syringa pekinensis species, belonging to the family Oleaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub with pendulous branches, growing up to 5 meters in height. The leaves are ovate-lanceolate, 5-10 cm long, and have a serrated margin, with a bright green color on the upper surface and a pale green color on the lower surface. The flowers are pale pink to white, tubular, and fragrant, produced in a panicle-like inflorescence in late spring.

The flowers are followed by brown, oval-shaped capsules, 3-5 cm long, containing numerous seeds.

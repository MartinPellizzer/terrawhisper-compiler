Ranunculus inconcinnus

Ranunculus inconcinnus, also known as the lesser celandine, is a perennial plant in the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae) that is commonly found in wetlands and along streams.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include the treatment of respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus inconcinnus include ranunculin, a glycoside responsible for its medicinal properties, as well as other compounds like alkaloids and flavonoids.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a tincture, infusion, or syrup to treat various health conditions, although it is essential to note that proper identification and usage are crucial due to the plant's potential toxicity.

What is Arenaria ciliata subsp. polycarpoides?

Arenaria ciliata subsp.

polycarpoides is a plant species that belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae. It is a perennial herb with slender stems, typically 10-30 cm in height, and a rosette of basal leaves that are linear-lanceolate to lanceolate in shape, 1-5 cm long, and hairy.

The leaves are arranged oppositely on the stem, with the upper leaves being smaller and often reduced to bracts. The plant produces white or pink flowers in a dense corymb, with 5 petals that are elliptical in shape and 3-6 mm long, and 10 stamens that are as long as or longer than the petals.

The species is found in the Mediterranean region, typically in rocky or stony areas, and is often associated with the parent species Arenaria ciliata.

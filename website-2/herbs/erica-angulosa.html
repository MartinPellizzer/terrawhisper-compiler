Erica angulosa

Erica angulosa, also known as the angled heath or angled bell heath, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the southwestern Cape region of South Africa.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in treating various ailments such as wounds, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Erica angulosa include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Erica angulosa include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat colds, sore throats, and digestive issues.

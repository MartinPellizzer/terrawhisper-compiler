Cassia brachiata

Cassia brachiata, also known as Sickle bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent and parts of Southeast Asia.

The plant is valued for its traditional medicinal uses, which include treating various health conditions such as fever, inflammation, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Cassia brachiata include flavonoids, terpenoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional Indian medicine, the plant is commonly prepared into herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being used in the form of capsules and extracts.

Lantana macrophylla

Lantana macrophylla, also known as large-leaf lantana, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America, characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, yellow, and purple.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to its numerous health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been shown to aid in the treatment of various conditions such as fever, colds, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Lantana macrophylla include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Lantana macrophylla include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory problems, fever, and skin conditions.

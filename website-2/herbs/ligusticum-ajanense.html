Ligusticum ajanense

Ligusticum ajanense, also known as Ajane or Ajan, is a perennial plant species native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China, belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Ligusticum ajanense include essential oils, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, such as ligustilide, senkyunolide, and ferulic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Ligusticum ajanense is commonly prepared as a decoction or tincture, and is also used to make infusions, capsules, and essential oils, which are often used to treat colds, flu, and other respiratory infections.

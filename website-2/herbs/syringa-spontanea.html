Syringa spontanea

Syringa spontanea, also known as the natural or wild lilac, is a flowering plant species native to Europe and Western Asia, belonging to the olive family (Oleaceae).

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Syringa spontanea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant are teas, tinctures, and infusions, often used to treat respiratory issues and promote relaxation.

classification

Syringa spontanea is classified as a species of flowering plant in the family Oleaceae. It is native to Europe and western Asia. It is closely related to other lilac species, such as Syringa vulgaris.

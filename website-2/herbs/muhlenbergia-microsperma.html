Muhlenbergia microsperma

Muhlenbergia microsperma, also known as dwarf muhly or littleseed muhly, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including the treatment of fever, colds, and respiratory issues. Bioactive constituents of Muhlenbergia microsperma include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly prepared as a tea or decoction, and is also used in some herbal remedies, such as cough syrups and respiratory tonics.

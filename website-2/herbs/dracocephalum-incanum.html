Dracocephalum incanum

Dracocephalum incanum, also known as Wolf's Mint, is a perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family, native to Central Asia and Eastern Europe.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, and its health benefits include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, making it effective in treating respiratory issues, wounds, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Dracocephalum incanum include iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often prepared as an infusion, tincture, or essential oil, and is commonly used to treat respiratory problems, such as bronchitis and coughs.

Olea pallida

Olea pallida, also known as the Silver-leaf Olive or Pale Olive, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oleaceae, native to South Africa.

The leaves and bark of Olea pallida have been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive problems, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Olea pallida include oleuropein, a phenylethanoid glycoside, as well as other flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Olea pallida is often used in traditional medicine to prepare infusions, teas, and tinctures, making it a valuable herb in the treatment of various health conditions.

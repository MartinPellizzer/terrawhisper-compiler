Bupleurum pauciradiatum

Bupleurum pauciradiatum, also known as Chinese thorowax or Chinese kuchansk, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Apiaceae family.

Traditional Chinese medicine attributes various health benefits to Bupleurum pauciradiatum, including the reduction of inflammation, improvement of liver function, and enhancement of immune system responses. The bioactive constituents of Bupleurum pauciradiatum include saikosaponins, saponins, alkaloids, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Bupleurum pauciradiatum is commonly used in herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and pills, often in combination with other herbs like ginseng and licorice root, to promote liver health and alleviate symptoms associated with viral hepatitis.

Olea longifolia

Olea longifolia, also known as the wild olive tree, is a plant species belonging to the Oleaceae family, native to the Indian subcontinent.

The leaves of Olea longifolia have been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine, exhibiting various health benefits such as antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, fever, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Olea longifolia include oleuropein, hydroxytyrosol, and verbascoside, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Indian herbal medicine, Olea longifolia is commonly used to prepare teas, infusions, and capsules, as well as being incorporated into various Ayurvedic formulations for its therapeutic benefits.

Monopsis variifolia

Monopsis variifolia, also known as the "Winter's Bark", is a perennial plant species belonging to the family Berberidaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects, which can aid in wound healing and alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Monopsis variifolia include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

Monopsis variifolia is commonly used in herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and creams, which are used to treat various conditions including skin irritations, respiratory issues, and minor wounds.

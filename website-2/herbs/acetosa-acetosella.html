Acetosa acetosella

Acetosa acetosella, also known as wood sorrel, is a perennial herb belonging to the oxalis family, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and white, yellow, or pink flowers.

The leaves of the wood sorrel plant have been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to their potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of conditions such as arthritis and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Acetosa acetosella include ascorbic acid, oxalic acid, and anthocyanins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, wood sorrel is often prepared as a tea or infused in vinegar to take advantage of its health benefits, and it is also used in salads for its tangy, slightly sour flavor.

Juglans sinensis

Juglans sinensis, also known as Chinese walnut or Manchurian walnut, is a deciduous tree species in the Juglandaceae family native to northeastern China and Korea.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiviral properties, as well as its ability to lower cholesterol and blood pressure. The bioactive constituents of Juglans sinensis include juglone, a lignan with antioxidant and cytotoxic activities, as well as other compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations, including tea, capsules, and decoctions, to treat a range of health conditions, including cardiovascular disease and digestive disorders.

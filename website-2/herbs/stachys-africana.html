Stachys africana

Stachys africana, also known as African hedge hyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae, native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as respiratory issues, wounds, and digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of Stachys africana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, particularly borneol and camphor, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, Stachys africana is commonly prepared as an infusion, decoction, or tincture to harness its medicinal properties and treat various health conditions.

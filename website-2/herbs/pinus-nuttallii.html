Pinus nuttallii

Pinus nuttallii, also known as Nuttall's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

The essential oil extracted from the needles of Nuttall's fir has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including respiratory problems and skin infections, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Pinus nuttallii include terpenes, specifically pinene and limonene, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Nuttall's fir is often prepared as a tea, essential oil, or infusion, which can be used to treat coughs, sore throats, and wounds, as well as to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

Senna rugosa

Senna rugosa is a perennial plant species in the legume family, characterized by its yellow flowers and small, pod-like seedpods.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its laxative properties, providing relief from constipation and promoting digestive health. The bioactive constituents of Senna rugosa include anthraquinone glycosides, such as sennosides A and B, which are responsible for its purgative effects.

Senna rugosa is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, capsules, and powders, often in combination with other herbs to support digestive health and promote regular bowel movements.

Polypodium furcatum

Polypodium furcatum, commonly known as the Polypodium or Fishtail Polypodium, is a species of fern that belongs to the Polypodiaceae family.

Traditionally, Polypodium furcatum has been used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, pain, and fever, due to its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and polysaccharides, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

Polypodium furcatum is often used in herbal preparations such as infusions, teas, and tinctures, and is also sometimes used in traditional Asian medicine to treat conditions like arthritis and gout.

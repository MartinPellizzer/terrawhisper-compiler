Ipomoea sinensis

Ipomoea sinensis, also known as the sweet potato vine or Ipomoea vine, is a perennial plant species belonging to the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in various cultures for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as lowering blood pressure, reducing inflammation, and aiding in the management of diabetes due to its antioxidant and antidiabetic properties. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea sinensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which have been found to contribute to its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Ipomoea sinensis include dried extracts and teas, as well as tablets and capsules, which are used to support cardiovascular health and manage blood sugar levels.

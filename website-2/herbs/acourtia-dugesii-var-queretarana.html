What is Acourtia dugesii var. queretarana?

Acourtia dugesii var. queretarana is a perennial herb belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It has a taproot system with a woody base and a slender, branching stem that grows up to 1-2 meters in height. The leaves are alternate, sessile, and elliptical in shape, measuring 2-10 cm in length and 0.5-2 cm in width, with a smooth margin and a glabrous to puberulent surface. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in a capitulum, consisting of 8-12 florets, and is found in the wild in Mexico, where it grows in dry, rocky areas and open forests.

Its inflorescence is a radiate head with a flat or slightly concave base and a prominent, tubular involucre.

Lobelia pallida

Lobelia pallida, also known as Pale Cardinal Flower, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Lobelia genus.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and expectorant properties, which may help alleviate respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Lobelia pallida include alkaloids, glycosides, and terpenoids, including lobeline and lobelanine, which are responsible for the plant's medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Lobelia pallida is used in herbal medicine and is often incorporated into preparations such as tinctures, infusions, and smoking blends, although its use is generally considered to be contraindicated in pregnant or breastfeeding women.

Aloe × puberula

Aloe × puberula, also known as the false aloe or rough-leaved aloe, is a hybrid plant species within the Aloe genus, characterized by its succulent leaves and rough, hairy texture.

The health benefits of Aloe × puberula include its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and wound-healing properties, which have been traditionally used to soothe burns, skin irritations, and other dermatological issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include aloin, aloe-emodin, and various flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Aloe × puberula is often used in herbal preparations such as gels, creams, and ointments to treat skin conditions, as well as in traditional medicine to promote overall well-being.

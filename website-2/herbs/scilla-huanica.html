Scilla huanica

Scilla huanica, also known as Huamalíes bluebell, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Asparagaceae family, native to the high-altitude grasslands of Peru.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as respiratory issues and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Scilla huanica include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, including huamin, which is a unique and potent alkaloid with potential medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to prepare various herbal remedies, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are believed to promote respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu.

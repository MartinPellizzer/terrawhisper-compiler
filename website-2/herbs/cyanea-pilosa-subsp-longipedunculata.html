What is Cyanea pilosa subsp. longipedunculata?

Cyanea pilosa subsp.

longipedunculata is an endemic Hawaiian plant species belonging to the family Campanulaceae. It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter in height, with pubescent leaves that are 6-10 cm long and 2.5-4.5 cm wide, and are arranged oppositely on the stem.

The inflorescence is a terminal cyme with 3-6 flowers, each 5-6 cm long, with a blue to purple corolla and a white tube. The calyx is 3-4 mm long, with 5 lobes, and the fruit is a capsule that splits into 4 valves, releasing 5-6 seeds.

The plant is found in mesic to wet forests at elevations of 300-1,200 meters.

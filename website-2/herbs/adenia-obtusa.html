Adenia obtusa

Adenia obtusa, also known as the cat's claw vine or five-angled adenia, is a climbing plant native to the tropical forests of Africa and Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which may help to reduce fever, alleviate pain, and protect against infections. The bioactive constituents of Adenia obtusa include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

In traditional medicine, Adenia obtusa is often prepared as a tea or decoction, or used in combination with other herbs to create infusions, tinctures, and ointments, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin infections.

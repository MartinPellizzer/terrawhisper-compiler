Aldama obtusifolia

Aldama obtusifolia is a perennial herbaceous plant, commonly known as Hierba de la Santa Maria, that is native to Mexico and Central America.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, contributing to its potential in treating various health conditions such as fever, respiratory issues, and gastrointestinal problems. The bioactive constituents of Aldama obtusifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Aldama obtusifolia is often used to prepare infusions, teas, and topical creams, making it a popular ingredient in various herbal preparations.

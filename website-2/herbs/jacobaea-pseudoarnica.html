Jacobaea pseudoarnica

Jacobaea pseudoarnica, also known as Autumn Arnica or False Arnica, is a perennial plant in the daisy family (Asteraceae) native to Europe and western Asia.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, with benefits including wound healing, pain relief, and the reduction of swelling. The bioactive constituents of Jacobaea pseudoarnica include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Jacobaea pseudoarnica include tinctures, salves, and infusions, which are often used topically to treat skin irritations and injuries.

Carex turgida

Carex turgida, commonly known as stiff-leaved sedge or turgid sedge, is a perennial plant species belonging to the family Cyperaceae, characterized by its upright, rigid stems and narrow, dark green leaves.

The plant Carex turgida is traditionally used in herbal medicine, offering several health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties, which can help alleviate conditions such as urinary tract infections and edema. The bioactive constituents of Carex turgida include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Carex turgida is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly employed to treat various health conditions, including kidney stones and water retention.

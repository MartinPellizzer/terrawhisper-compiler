side effects

Toxicodendron pentaphyllum, also known as Chinese lacquer tree, contains urushiol, a chemical that can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals. When the plant's sap comes into contact with the skin, it can cause redness, itching, and blistering. In severe cases, an allergic reaction to urushiol can lead to more serious symptoms, including difficulty breathing and swelling of the face, lips, or tongue. If ingested, Toxicodendron pentaphyllum sap can cause nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Prolonged exposure to urushiol can also cause long-term skin conditions, such as eczema and dermatitis.

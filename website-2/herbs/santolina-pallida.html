Santolina pallida

Santolina pallida, also known as dwarf gray santolina or narrow-leaved lavender cotton, is a low-growing, perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, yellow flowers.

The health benefits of Santolina pallida include its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antifungal properties, which have been traditionally used to treat a range of health issues, from skin irritations and wounds to respiratory problems and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Santolina pallida include teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as topical creams and ointments, which are often used to soothe and calm skin irritations and promote wound healing.

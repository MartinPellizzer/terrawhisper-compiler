Gardenia randia

Gardenia randia, also known as Randia spp., is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rubiaceae, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The bioactive constituents of Gardenia randia include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Gardenia randia include teas, infusions, and extracts, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, digestive problems, and skin conditions.

Desmodium biflorum

Desmodium biflorum, also known as showy tick clover, is a perennial legume native to North America, characterized by its clusters of small, pink flowers and trifoliate leaflets.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Desmodium biflorum include flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Desmodium biflorum is often prepared as a tea or infusement, or used in the form of a tincture or decoction, to take advantage of its purported health benefits.

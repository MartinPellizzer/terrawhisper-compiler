Pterocarpus glabratus

Pterocarpus glabratus, commonly known as the Odum tree, is a medicinal plant native to tropical Africa.

The Odum tree has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and expectorant properties, which contribute to its health benefits. The bioactive constituents of Pterocarpus glabratus include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The Odum tree is often used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into traditional remedies such as poultices and ointments.

Clematis floribunda

Clematis floribunda, also known as Clematis, is a flowering plant in the Ranunculaceae family, native to Japan and Korea, characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers and twining stems.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including the treatment of fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties. Bioactive constituents of Clematis floribunda include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and coumarins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Ipomoea purga

Ipomoea purga, also known as Mexican root, is a perennial plant species belonging to the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its laxative and purgative properties, with potential health benefits including the treatment of constipation, digestive issues, and certain cases of poisoning. Bioactive constituents of Ipomoea purga include ipomeanol and other glycosides, which are responsible for its purgative effects.

The plant is commonly prepared as a decoction or tincture, with the latter often used in traditional herbal medicine to treat constipation and support digestive health.

Ocimum serratum

Ocimum serratum, also known as Indian basil or Thai basil, is a species of plant belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Ocimum serratum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, particularly limonene, linalool, and beta-caryophyllene, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Ocimum serratum is often used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat various health conditions, including fever, colds, and digestive issues.

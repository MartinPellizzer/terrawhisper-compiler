Ulmus × sarniensis

Ulmus × sarniensis, also known as Jersey Elm, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Ulmus glabra (Wych Elm) and Ulmus minor (Field Elm).

The health benefits of Ulmus × sarniensis include its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Ulmus × sarniensis is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for skin health.

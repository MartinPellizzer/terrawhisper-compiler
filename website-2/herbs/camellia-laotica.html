Camellia laotica

Camellia laotica is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree in the Camellia genus, native to Southeast Asia.

Traditionally, the leaves of Camellia laotica have been used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory problems, due to its potential health benefits in reducing oxidative stress and modulating the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Camellia laotica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Camellia laotica is often used to make herbal teas and infusions, as well as extracts and capsules, which are commonly used in traditional and modern medicine to support overall health and well-being.

Peyotl viridescens, also known as the green peyote or Lophophora viridescens, is a small, succulent cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert in North America.

The bioactive constituents of Peyotl viridescens include alkaloids such as mescaline, peyocactin, and lophophorine, which are responsible for its psychoactive and medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, tinctures, and capsules, as well as being dried and ground into a powder for use in rituals and ceremonies.

Malus angustifolia

Malus angustifolia, also known as the narrow-leaved apple or Schuylkill whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family (Rosaceae) native to eastern North America.

The bioactive constituents of Malus angustifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

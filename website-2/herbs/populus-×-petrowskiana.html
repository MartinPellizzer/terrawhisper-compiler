Populus × petrowskiana

Populus × petrowskiana, also known as the hybrid aspen, is a deciduous tree species derived from the cross-pollination of Populus maximowiczii and Populus nigra.

The bark and leaves of Populus × petrowskiana have been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its antimicrobial and antiphlogistic properties, which offer relief from symptoms such as congestion and coughing. The bioactive constituents of Populus × petrowskiana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In herbal preparations, the bark and leaves of Populus × petrowskiana are often used to make teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for wound healing and skin care.

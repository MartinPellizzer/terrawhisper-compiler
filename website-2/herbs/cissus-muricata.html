Cissus muricata

Cissus muricata, also known as Vitex trifolia var. muricata or grape ivy, is a woody climber native to tropical Asia, characterized by its purple-colored flowers and edible fruit.

The bioactive constituents of Cissus muricata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

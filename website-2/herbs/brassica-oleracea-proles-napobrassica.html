What is Brassica oleracea proles napobrassica?

Brassica oleracea proles napobrassica is a botanical variety of the species Brassica oleracea, belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is a biennial or perennial plant that grows to a height of 30-60 cm, with a thick, fleshy stem and a rosette of thick, fleshy, petioled leaves. The leaves are typically green, but can also be red or purple, and are often used as a vegetable in cooking. The plant produces a short, stout stem with a compact, dense inflorescence, bearing small, yellow flowers that are arranged in a raceme.

The edible roots of Brassica oleracea proles napobrassica, known as kohlrabi, are a characteristic feature of this plant.

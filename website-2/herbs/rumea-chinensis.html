Rumea chinensis

Rumea chinensis, also known as Chinese Rumea, is a plant species in the family Annonaceae, native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine, where it is believed to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiviral effects, which may help to alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Rumea chinensis include alkaloids, glycosides, and terpenoids, such as chinensin and rumeachinensin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Rumea chinensis is commonly prepared as a decoction, capsule, or tincture to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and respiratory infections.

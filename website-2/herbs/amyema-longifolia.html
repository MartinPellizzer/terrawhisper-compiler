Amyema longifolia

Amyema longifolia, also known as the woolly-bellbird vine or long-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to eastern Australia.

Traditionally, Amyema longifolia has been used by the indigenous Australian population to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory problems. The plant's bioactive constituents include alkaloids, phenolic acids, and flavonoids, which have been shown to possess antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Amyema longifolia is often used in teas, infusions, and tinctures to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms associated with colds and flu.

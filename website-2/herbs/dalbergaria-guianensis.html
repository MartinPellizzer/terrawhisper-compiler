Dalbergaria guianensis

Dalbergaria guianensis, commonly known as Guianese or Cat's Claw, is a woody vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which can help to alleviate symptoms of arthritis, digestive issues, and other health conditions. The bioactive constituents of Dalbergaria guianensis include alkaloids, particularly oxindole and isoxazole alkaloids, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Cat's Claw include teas, capsules, and tinctures, which are often used to support immune function, reduce inflammation, and promote overall well-being.

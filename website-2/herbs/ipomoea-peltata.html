Ipomoea peltata

Ipomoea peltata, also known as Convolvulus peltatus, is a climbing vine plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of various ailments. Ipomoea peltata contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

Convolvulus peltatus is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and extracts, often used to treat fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues.

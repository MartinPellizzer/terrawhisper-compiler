Adhatoda tranquebariensis

Adhatoda vasica (also known as Adhatoda tranquebariensis) is a tropical plant species belonging to the family Acanthaceae, commonly found in the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as gastrointestinal problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Adhatoda vasica include vasicine, adhatodine, and adhatodol, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Adhatoda vasica is commonly prepared as a decoction or an infusion, and is also used to make cough syrups, expectorant tablets, and ointments to treat various respiratory and gastrointestinal disorders.

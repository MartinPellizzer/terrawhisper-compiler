What is Onobrychis galegifolia f. velutina?

Onobrychis galegifolia f. velutina is a botanical variant of the species Onobrychis galegifolia, belonging to the family Fabaceae.

This perennial herbaceous plant grows up to 50-100 cm in height, with a taproot and a leafy stem featuring compound, trifoliate leaves with 3 leaflets and a leaflet length of 2-6 cm. The flowers are yellow, 1-2 cm in length, and arranged in axillary, ovoid racemes. The inflorescence is hairy, and the seeds are 5-7 mm in length, with a hairy, ovate seed coat.

The variant Onobrychis galegifolia f. velutina is distinguished by its velvety, pubescent habit.

What are the therapeutic properties this herb?

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Onobrychis galegifolia f. velutina are infusion, decoction, tincture, salve, and tea.

Infusion is made by steeping the flowers in hot water, releasing flavonoids, phenolic acids, and coumarins. Decoction is prepared by boiling the roots in water to extract polysaccharides, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds. Tincture is a concentrated liquid extract of the plant, used to treat various health conditions, and salve is a topical application made from the plant's extracts, applied to soothe skin irritations.

The herbal preparations of Onobrychis galegifolia f. velutina are valued for their antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

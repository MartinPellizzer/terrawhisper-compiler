Lysimachia montana

Lysimachia montana, also known as Blue cohosh, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America, valued for its medicinal properties and unique blue flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used to relieve menstrual cramps, ease childbirth, and reduce inflammation due to its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects, making it beneficial for women's health. The bioactive constituents of Blue cohosh include clematidine, clematitenine, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Blue cohosh are extracts, capsules, and teas, often used to treat menstrual disorders and menopausal symptoms.

Pilea rugosa

Pilea rugosa, also known as the aluminium plant or metal leaf plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae, commonly grown for its attractive, metallic-silver leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, which may aid in the treatment of various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Pilea rugosa include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Pilea rugosa is often made into teas, infusions, or decoctions, which are used to treat fever, coughs, and respiratory infections, as well as to promote wound healing and skin health.

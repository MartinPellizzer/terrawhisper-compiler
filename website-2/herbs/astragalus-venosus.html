Astragalus venosus

Astragalus venosus, also known as the northern milkvetch, is a perennial plant belonging to the legume family (Fabaceae) and is native to North America.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its adaptogenic properties, and it is believed to provide health benefits such as enhancing immune function, reducing inflammation, and improving cardiovascular health. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus venosus include saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Astragalus venosus include teas, capsules, and tinctures, which are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and are also consumed in North America as dietary supplements.

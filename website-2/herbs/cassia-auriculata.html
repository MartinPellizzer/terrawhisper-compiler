What is Cassia auriculata?

Cassia auriculata is a perennial shrub native to India and Sri Lanka, belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is an evergreen plant that grows up to 3 meters in height, with a woody stem and a spreading habit, having trifoliate leaves with three elliptical leaflets that are 2-5 cm long. The flowers are yellow, fragrant, and arranged in axillary clusters, with a calyx of five sepals and a corolla of five petals. The fruit is a pod that splits open to release 2-3 seeds, each measuring 5-7 mm in length.

The plant is also known as 'Oath Tree' or 'Love Tree' due to its traditional use in wedding ceremonies in South India.

Furthermore, Cassia auriculata exhibits anti-inflammatory properties, which are primarily due to the action of glycosides and alkaloids that can inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. The antidiabetic properties of Cassia auriculata are also noteworthy, as they are attributed to the action of flavonoids and phenolic acids that can improve insulin sensitivity and reduce glucose uptake in the liver.

What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

The most important healing constituents of Cassia auriculata are quercetin, flavonoids (auriculatin), glycosides (auriculatin, cassiadin), kaempferol, and saponins, which have been proven to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Cassia auriculata are decoction, infusion, tincture, infused oil, and cassia auriculata ghee.

Decoction is prepared by boiling the bark and roots of the plant in water to treat conditions like fever, rheumatism, and skin diseases. Infusion is prepared by steeping the leaves and flowers of the plant in hot water to treat digestive issues and as a diuretic. Tincture is prepared by macerating the bark and roots of the plant in a solvent like ethanol to treat conditions like anxiety and insomnia.

Infused oil and cassia auriculata ghee are prepared by infusing the plant material in a carrier oil or ghee, respectively, to treat skin conditions and promote wound healing.

What are the possible side effects of using this herb improperly?

The possible side effects of using Cassia auriculata improperly include triggering allergic reactions due to its high content of anthraquinones and saponins, which can cause an overactive immune response in some individuals.

This can lead to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing, requiring immediate medical attention. Additionally, improper use of Cassia auriculata can cause vomiting and nausea due to its emetic properties, which can be exacerbated by its high concentration of anthraquinones. These gastrointestinal side effects can be particularly problematic in individuals with pre-existing gastrointestinal conditions or those taking medications that interact with Cassia auriculata.

Furthermore, developing an allergic reaction to Cassia auriculata can induce nausea, which can be a vicious cycle of symptoms requiring prompt medical intervention.

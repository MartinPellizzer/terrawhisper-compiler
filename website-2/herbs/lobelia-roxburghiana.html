Lobelia roxburghiana

Lobelia roxburghiana, also known as Lobelia, is an herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayan region.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include reducing inflammation and pain, as well as its antispasmodic and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Lobelia roxburghiana include alkaloids such as lobeline and lobelanine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Lobelia is often used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and poultices, which are used to treat respiratory issues, such as coughs and bronchitis, as well as skin conditions and joint pain.

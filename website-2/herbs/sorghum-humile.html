Sorghum humile

Sorghum humile, also known as Indian sorghum or dwarf sorghum, is a perennial plant species belonging to the sorghum family (Poaceae) and is native to the tropical regions of Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with health benefits that include antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, which can help alleviate conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal disorders. The bioactive constituents of Sorghum humile include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Sorghum humile is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into tonics and supplements to harness its health benefits.

Gilia densifolia

Gilia densifolia, also known as Sky Pilot, is a perennial plant native to North America, characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy foliage.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of colds, fever, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Gilia densifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Gilia densifolia is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion to treat respiratory issues and as a supplement to support immune function.

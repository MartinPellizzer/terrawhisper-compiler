classification

Cistus atlanticus is a member of the family Cistaceae. It belongs to the genus Cistus. This species is further classified as a flowering plant, specifically a shrub native to the Atlantic coastal regions of North Africa. Cistus atlanticus is also known as the Atlantic rockrose or North African rockrose. It is a low-growing, spreading shrub with grey-green leaves.

medicinal uses

Cistus atlanticus has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues. Its leaves and flowers are said to possess antimicrobial properties, which can help combat infections and promote wound healing. In some regions, it's also used to treat digestive problems and reduce inflammation. However, more research is needed to confirm the effectiveness and safety of its medicinal uses. Traditional healers often use Cistus atlanticus in combination with other plants to create remedies.

health benefits

Cistus atlanticus has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including the treatment of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs. Its leaves and flowers contain compounds with antimicrobial properties, which may help to combat infections. The plant has also been used to treat digestive problems and to reduce inflammation. Additionally, Cistus atlanticus has been studied for its antioxidant properties, which could help to protect against cell damage and oxidative stress. Further research is needed to confirm the plant's efficacy and safety for use as a medicinal treatment.

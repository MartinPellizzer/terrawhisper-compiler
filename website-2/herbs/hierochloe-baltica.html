Hierochloe baltica

Hierochloe baltica, also known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to Northern Europe and North America, characterized by its sweet, vanilla-like fragrance and slender leaves.

The plant is valued for its numerous health benefits, including its ability to soothe digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Hierochloe baltica include essential oils, particularly vanillin, which contributes to its distinct fragrance and medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, sweet grass is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or dried herb, often used to calm digestive upset and promote relaxation.

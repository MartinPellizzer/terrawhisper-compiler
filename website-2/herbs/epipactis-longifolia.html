Epipactis longifolia

Epipactis longifolia, also known as the Northern Helleborine, is a perennial orchid species native to Europe and Asia.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate conditions such as arthritis, wounds, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Epipactis longifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Epipactis longifolia is often prepared as a tincture or infusion, and is also used in combination with other herbs to create remedies for colds, fever, and digestive issues.

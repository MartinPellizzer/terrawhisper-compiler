Abies × pendula

Abies × pendula, also known as Weeping Silver Fir, is a hybrid plant species derived from the coniferous Abies alba (European Silver Fir) and Abies cephalonica (Greek Silver Fir).

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate respiratory issues and support immune system function. The bioactive constituents of Abies × pendula include essential oils, terpenes, and flavonoids, such as abietic acid, bornyl acetate, and taxifolin, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Abies × pendula is commonly used to make herbal preparations, including teas, essential oils, and infusions, which are often used to relieve respiratory congestion, coughs, and colds.

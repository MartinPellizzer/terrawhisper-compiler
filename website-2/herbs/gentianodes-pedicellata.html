Gentianodes pedicellata

Gentianodes pedicellata, also known as Smallflower Gentian or Little Gentian, is a perennial plant species in the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, and has been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as fever, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Gentianodes pedicellata include iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Gentianodes pedicellata is often prepared as a tea or tincture, and is also used in herbal infusions and decoctions to support immune function and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu.

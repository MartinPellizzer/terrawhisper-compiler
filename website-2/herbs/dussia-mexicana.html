Dussia mexicana

Dussia mexicana, also known as the Mexican pea tree, is a leguminous plant species native to Central and South America, characterized by its woody vines and pea-like flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, providing relief from various health issues, including fever, cough, and respiratory problems, thanks to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Dussia mexicana include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The plant is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas and infusions, as well as in traditional medicine, particularly in Latin American and Caribbean folk medicine.

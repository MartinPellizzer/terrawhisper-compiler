Celmisia latifolia

Celmisia latifolia, also known as Broad-leaved Celmisia, is a perennial plant species native to New Zealand, belonging to the Asteraceae family.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which may aid in the treatment of various skin conditions and wounds. Bioactive constituents of Celmisia latifolia include sesquiterpenes, such as alantolactone and isoalantolactone, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as topical creams and ointments, which are applied to soothe skin irritations and promote wound healing.

Melissa rotundifolia

Melissa rotundifolia, also known as round-leaved balm or honey plant, is a perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety and stress, and exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which can lead to improved sleep quality and overall well-being. The bioactive constituents of Melissa rotundifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and rosmarinic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Melissa rotundifolia include teas, tinctures, and essential oils, which can be used to treat various health issues, including digestive problems and skin conditions.

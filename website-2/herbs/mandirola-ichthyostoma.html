Mandirola ichthyostoma

However, Mandrake (Mandragora officinarum) is the more commonly used term. The Mandrake plant is a type of root that grows underground, often referred to as a 'living root' due to its unique ability to grow in the shape of a human figure.

The Mandrake root has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, offering several health benefits. It is known for its potential to reduce anxiety and stress levels, improve sleep quality, and alleviate symptoms of depression. The bioactive constituents of Mandrake include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Mandrake include tinctures, decoctions, and infusions, which are often used to treat various health issues, including insomnia and anxiety disorders.

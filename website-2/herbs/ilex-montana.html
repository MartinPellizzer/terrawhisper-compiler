Ilex montana

Ilex montana, also known as Mountain Holly, is a species of holly native to the southeastern United States.

It has been traditionally used to promote digestive health and has been associated with several health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving immune function, and supporting liver health. The bioactive constituents of Ilex montana include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Ilex montana is often used to make teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into supplements and tinctures, although its use is not as widespread as that of other holly species.

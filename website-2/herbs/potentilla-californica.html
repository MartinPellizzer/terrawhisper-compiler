Potentilla californica

Potentilla californica, also known as California cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States, characterized by its palmate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, as well as triterpenoids and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

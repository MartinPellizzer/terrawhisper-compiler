Huperzia homocarpa

Huperzia homocarpa, also known as Chinese club moss or huperzine A, is a species of plant in the family Lycopodiaceae, characterized by its small, club-shaped sporophylls and ability to synthesize huperzine A, a potent neuroprotective compound.

The health benefits of Huperzia homocarpa are attributed to its ability to enhance cognitive function, improve memory, and potentially prevent or slow the progression of neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's. The bioactive constituents of this plant include huperzine A, a non-competitive acetylcholinesterase inhibitor, as well as other alkaloids and terpenoids that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Huperzia homocarpa is commonly used in herbal preparations, including capsules, tablets, and extracts, to support brain health and function, and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine under the name "Qian Ceng Ta".

Astragalus emoryanus

Astragalus emoryanus, also known as Emory's milkvetch, is a perennial legume plant native to the southwestern United States.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including immune system modulation, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects, which may aid in reducing stress, improving cardiovascular health, and boosting overall well-being. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus emoryanus include triterpene saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as teas, capsules, and tinctures, as well as a dried powder that can be added to soups or stews, although modern formulations may also involve standardized extracts and combinations with other herbs.

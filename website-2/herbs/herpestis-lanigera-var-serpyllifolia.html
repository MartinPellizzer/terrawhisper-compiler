What is Herpestis lanigera var. serpyllifolia?

Herpestis lanigera var. serpyllifolia is a taxonomic variety of flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which can grow up to 30 centimeters in height, and its small, ovate to lanceolate leaves, which are 1-2 centimeters long and have a hairy, glandular surface. The leaves are arranged oppositely or in whorls on the stem, and the plant produces small, white to purplish flowers in axillary clusters from July to October. The flowers are bilabiate, with a two-lipped corolla, and have a 4-lobed upper lip and a 3-lobed lower lip.

The seeds are small, black, and glandular, and are arranged in a spike-like cluster.

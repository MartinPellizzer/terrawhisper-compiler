What is Lonicera caerulea var. villosa?

Lonicera caerulea var. villosa is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia, belonging to the family Caprifoliaceae.

It grows up to 6 meters in height, with a spread of 2-3 meters, featuring a woody stem with a greyish-brown bark and a terminal inflorescence. The leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical, measuring 5-10 cm in length, with a glaucous underside and a serrated margin. The flowers are small, blue-purple, and fragrant, arranged in a corymb, blooming in late spring.

The plant produces a red or blue edible fruit, a berry, 1-2 cm in diameter, containing 2-6 seeds.

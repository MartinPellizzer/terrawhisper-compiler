Cynanchum fimbricoronum

Cynanchum fimbricoronum, also known as Fleece-flower or Chinese foxglove, is a perennial plant species in the Apocynaceae family, native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

This plant has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal disorders, due to its anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Cynanchum fimbricoronum include alkaloids, such as cynanchine and chrysonine, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is also used in the manufacture of tinctures and capsules.

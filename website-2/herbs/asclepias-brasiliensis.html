Asclepias brasiliensis

Asclepias brasiliensis, also known as the Brazilian milkweed or Branca, is a plant species in the Apocynaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental properties.

The plant has been traditionally used for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiseptic properties, which can help to treat wounds, reduce fever, and alleviate respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Asclepias brasiliensis include alkaloids, glycosides, and iridoids, such as asclepin and secamone, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Asclepias brasiliensis is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is also used to make tinctures and ointments, which are used to treat various health conditions, including skin infections and respiratory problems.

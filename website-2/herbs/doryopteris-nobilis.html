Doryopteris nobilis

Doryopteris nobilis, also known as the noble tree fern, is a perennial plant species belonging to the family Pteridaceae, characterized by its large, lance-shaped fronds.

Traditionally, Doryopteris nobilis has been used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Doryopteris nobilis include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Doryopteris nobilis is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is commonly used to treat respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing.

Selinum longifolium

Selinum longifolium, also known as Wild Carrot or Wild Queen Anne's Lace, is a perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it a possible remedy for digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Selinum longifolium include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Selinum longifolium is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is also used in traditional remedies for its carminative and expectorant effects.

The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus deformis include ranunculin, a toxic alkaloid responsible for its medicinal and pharmacological effects, as well as other compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins.

In herbal medicine, Ranunculus deformis is often prepared as a decoction, tincture, or salve, and is used to treat a range of conditions, including eczema, acne, and ulcers, although its use should be approached with caution due to the potential toxicity of its alkaloids.

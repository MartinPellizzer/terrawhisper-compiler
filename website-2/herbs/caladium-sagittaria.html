Caladium sagittaria

Caladium sagittaria, also known as Taro or Elephant Ear, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America, belonging to the family Arum.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its potential health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving immune function. The bioactive constituents of Caladium sagittaria include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Caladium sagittaria is often prepared as a decoction, tincture, or tea, and is also used as a topical application for skin conditions, although its use should be approached with caution due to potential toxicity.

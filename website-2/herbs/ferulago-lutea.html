Ferulago lutea

Ferulago lutea, also known as yellow ferulagin, is a perennial plant species in the carrot family (Apiaceae) native to the Mediterranean region.

The health benefits of Ferulago lutea include its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of various health conditions such as digestive issues, respiratory problems, and cardiovascular disease. The bioactive constituents of Ferulago lutea include ferulic acid, a phenolic compound with known antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, as well as other flavonoids and terpenoids.

The most common herbal preparations made with Ferulago lutea include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used in traditional medicine to support overall health and well-being.

classification

Ferulago lutea is a species of flowering plant in the carrot family Apiaceae. It is commonly known as golden ferula or yellow ferula. Ferulago lutea is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Western Asia. The plant is classified as a perennial herb, with yellow flowers that are arranged in umbels. It belongs to the genus Ferulago, which is a group of plants that are characterized by their umbelliferous inflorescences.

Mahonia trifoliata

Mahonia trifoliata, also known as Oregon grape or three-leaf mahonia, is a flowering evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest region of North America, characterized by its glossy, three-lobed leaves and yellow flowers.

The bioactive constituents of Mahonia trifoliata include alkaloids, such as berberine and oxyberberine, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

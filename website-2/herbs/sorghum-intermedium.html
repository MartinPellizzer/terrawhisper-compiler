Sorghum intermedium

Sorghum intermedium, also known as broomcorn sorghum, is a perennial plant belonging to the Sorghum genus, a group of grasses native to tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help to alleviate conditions such as arthritis, digestive issues, and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Sorghum intermedium include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Sorghum intermedium is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including fever, skin infections, and respiratory issues.

Cymbopogon rectus

Cymbopogon rectus, also known as Japanese lemongrass or Japanese citronella, is a type of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family and is native to East Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic effects, which can aid in reducing fever, relieving digestive issues, and treating wounds. The bioactive constituents of Cymbopogon rectus include citral, geraniol, and limonene, which are responsible for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Cymbopogon rectus is often used to make teas, infusions, and essential oils, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

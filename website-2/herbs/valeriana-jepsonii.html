Valeriana jepsonii

Valeriana jepsonii, also known as Jepson's valerian, is a perennial plant species in the family Valerianaceae, characterized by its fragrant flowers and edible roots.

The plant has been traditionally used for its sedative and calming properties, offering relief from anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness, making it a popular herbal remedy for promoting relaxation and improving sleep quality. The bioactive constituents of Jepson's valerian include valepotriates, isovaleric acid, and valerenic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Jepson's valerian is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as in supplements and topical creams, to harness its soothing and calming effects.

Linaria oblongifolia

Linaria oblongifolia, also known as the large-toothed toadflax, is a perennial plant species belonging to the figwort family (Scrophulariaceae).

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities, as well as its ability to stimulate digestion and alleviate respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Linaria oblongifolia include iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Linaria oblongifolia is commonly prepared as a decoction or tincture, and is used to treat a range of ailments, including bronchitis, coughs, and digestive disorders.

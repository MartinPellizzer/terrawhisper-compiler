What is Helichrysum fulvescens var. patulum?

Helichrysum fulvescens var. patulum is an annual or perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its stem, which is erect, branched and 15-40 cm tall, with a diameter of 1.5-4 mm, covered with soft, white or brown hairs, and bearing leafy branches. The leaves are alternate, sessile, lanceolate or elliptical, 2-8 cm long and 0.5-2 cm wide, with a pointed tip and a smooth margin, and are covered with soft, white or brown hairs. The inflorescence is a corymb, bearing 3-10 capitula, each with 30-50 florets, which are tubular and 5-6 mm long, with a corolla of 5-6 lobes, and are arranged in a spiral fashion.

The flowers are yellow, with a brown or yellowish center, and are produced from June to October.

Furthermore, prolonged use or excessive consumption of Helichrysum fulvescens var. patulum may lead to liver damage due to its potential hepatotoxicity, which is attributed to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones that can cause liver inflammation and damage.

This highlights the importance of proper usage, dosage, and caution when using this plant for medicinal purposes.

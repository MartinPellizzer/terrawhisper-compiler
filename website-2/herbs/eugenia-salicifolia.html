Eugenia salicifolia

Eugenia salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf clove or willow-leaf myrtle, is a plant species in the family Myrtaceae.

The bioactive constituents of Eugenia salicifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Eugenia salicifolia is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture to take advantage of its health benefits, and it is also used as a component in traditional remedies for fever and digestive issues.

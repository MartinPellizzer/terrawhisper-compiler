Lomatium robustius

Lomatium robustum, also known as Lomatium, is a perennial wildflower native to the western United States and Canada, belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

The roots of Lomatium have been traditionally used for their medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral activities, which may aid in treating respiratory infections and other conditions. The bioactive constituents of Lomatium include sesquiterpenes, coumarins, and alkaloids, such as sesquiterpene lactones and furanocoumarins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Lomatium is often used in herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and capsules, to support immune function and alleviate respiratory issues, although its use should be cautioned due to potential interactions with medications.

Dioscorea longirhiza

Dioscorea longirhiza, also known as Chinese wild yam or yam root, is a type of perennial vine native to China and other parts of East Asia, belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-cancer properties, as well as its ability to support hormone balance and alleviate menopausal symptoms. Dioscorea longirhiza contains bioactive constituents such as diosgenin, a steroid saponin, and other glycosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Dioscorea longirhiza is often prepared as a dried root or made into herbal teas, tinctures, and capsules, which are used to support women's health, immune function, and overall well-being.

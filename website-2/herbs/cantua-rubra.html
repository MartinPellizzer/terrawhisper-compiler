Cantua rubra

Cantua rubra, also known as the Red Trumpet flower or Red Cantua, is a flowering plant species native to Mexico and Central America, characterized by its vibrant red trumpet-shaped flowers.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, making it a potential remedy for various health issues such as wound healing, fever reduction, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Cantua rubra include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Cantua rubra include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat coughs, colds, and other respiratory conditions.

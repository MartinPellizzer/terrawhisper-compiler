Corydalis racemosa

Corydalis racemosa, also known as yellow fumewort or American corydalis, is a perennial plant native to North America, belonging to the Papaveraceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, exhibiting anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antispasmodic effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of pain, anxiety, and insomnia. The bioactive constituents of Corydalis racemosa include isoquinoline alkaloids, such as berberine, tetrahydroprotoberberine, and protopine, as well as phenolic acids and flavonoids.

The most common herbal preparations made with Corydalis racemosa include capsules, tablets, and teas, which are often combined with other herbs to enhance their therapeutic effects.

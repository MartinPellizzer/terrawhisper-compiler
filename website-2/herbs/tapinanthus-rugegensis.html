Tapinanthus rugegensis

Tapinanthus rugegensis, also known as the 'Khat tree', is a plant species native to East Africa that belongs to the Loranthaceae family, a group of hemiparasitic flowering plants that derive nutrients from their host plants.

The leaves and stems of Tapinanthus rugegensis have been used in traditional medicine for their stimulant and appetite-suppressing properties, providing relief from fatigue, anxiety, and depression. The bioactive constituents of this plant include caffeine, cathinone, and cathine, which are responsible for its psychoactive effects and medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Tapinanthus rugegensis is often prepared as a tea or chewed as a fresh leaf to release its bioactive compounds, although its use is heavily regulated due to its potential for abuse and addiction.

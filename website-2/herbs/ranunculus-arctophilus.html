Ranunculus arctophilus

Ranunculus arctophilus, also known as Alpine Buttercup, is a perennial plant species native to the high-altitude mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it useful in treating various ailments such as wounds, infections, and oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus arctophilus include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

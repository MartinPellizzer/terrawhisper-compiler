Aloe citrea

Aloe citrina, also known as Aloe citrea, is a succulent plant species belonging to the Aloe genus, characterized by its yellow flowers and greenish-blue leaves.

Aloe citrina is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, offering relief from burns, wounds, and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Aloe citrina include aloin, aloe-emodin, and isobarbaloin, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Traditionally, Aloe citrina is used to prepare herbal remedies such as topical gels, creams, and ointments, as well as oral supplements in the form of capsules or teas, to support skin health and overall well-being.

The subgenus Pachycladon comprises several species of Aloe, including Aloe citrea. Aloe citrea is native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

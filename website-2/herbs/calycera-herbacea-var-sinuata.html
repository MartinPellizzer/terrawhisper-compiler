What is Calycera herbacea var. sinuata?

Calycera herbacea var. sinuata is a plant variety in the family Calyceraceae, native to the Mediterranean region.

It is an annual or biennial herbaceous plant, characterized by slender stems up to 20 cm tall, with leaves that are elliptical to linear in shape, and entire margins. The leaves are 1-3 cm long and 0.2-0.5 cm wide, with a pubescent surface. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers in terminal corymbose inflorescences, with 5-7 petals, and a calyx with 5-7 lobes.

The flowers are 1.5-2.5 cm in diameter, and are pollinated by insects.

Additionally, using this herb in excess can cause diarrhea, resulting from its potent saponin and alkaloid content, which can disrupt normal gut function. Experiences allergic reactions, such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing, are also possible when using Calycera herbacea var. sinuata, especially in individuals with pre-existing allergies.

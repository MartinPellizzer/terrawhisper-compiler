Toxicodendron tridentatum

Toxicodendron tridentatum, also known as the western poison oak, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States and Canada, characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets that resemble an oak leaf.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which are attributed to its ability to relieve pain and reduce swelling. The bioactive constituents of Toxicodendron tridentatum include resinous compounds such as urushiol, which is responsible for its allergenic and anti-inflammatory effects.

The plant is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and salves, but it is essential to exercise caution when handling the plant due to its potent allergenic properties.

Artocarpus altissimus

Artocarpus altissimus, also known as the mountain jackfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its large leaves and edible fruits.

The health benefits of Artocarpus altissimus include its potential anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in reducing inflammation, fighting infections, and protecting against cell damage. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Artocarpus altissimus is commonly used in traditional medicine and is prepared into various herbal formulations, including teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being used in traditional remedies for fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues.

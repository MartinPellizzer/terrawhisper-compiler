Cerasus glabra

Cerasus glabra, also known as the wild cherry or sour cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the rose family (Rosaceae).

The bark, leaves, and fruits of Cerasus glabra have been traditionally used in herbal medicine, exhibiting various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiviral properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of arthritis, reduce oxidative stress, and combat viral infections. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, anthocyanins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Cerasus glabra include decoctions, infusions, and tinctures, as well as cherry juice and dried fruit powders, which are often used to support cardiovascular health, alleviate digestive issues, and promote immune function.

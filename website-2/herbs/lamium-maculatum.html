Lamium maculatum

Lamium maculatum, also known as spotted deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae), characterized by its spotted leaves and small, blue or purple flowers.

The leaves and flowers of Lamium maculatum have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiviral properties, which may aid in reducing fever, soothing digestive issues, and boosting the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Lamium maculatum include phenolic acids, flavonoids, and volatile oils, particularly borneol and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Lamium maculatum is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion, and is used to treat various ailments, including coughs, colds, and skin conditions.

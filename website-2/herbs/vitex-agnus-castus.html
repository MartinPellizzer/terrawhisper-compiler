What is Vitex agnus-castus?

Vitex agnus-castus is a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, commonly known as the mint family. The plant grows up to 6 meters tall, with a greyish-brown bark and elliptical-shaped leaves that are 5-15 centimeters long and 2-5 centimeters wide, with an entire margin and a pointed apex. The leaves are opposite, and the plant produces small, fragrant, blue-purple flowers in axillary spikes, which are 5-7 centimeters long and have a tubular corolla with five lobes.

Vitex agnus-castus is a perennial plant, commonly known as Chaste Tree or Monk's Pepper.

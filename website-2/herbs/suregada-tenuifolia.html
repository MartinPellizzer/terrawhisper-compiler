Suregada tenuifolia

Suregada tenuifolia, also known as the Indian Quassia, is a plant species native to the tropical regions of Asia and Africa, belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

The plant is valued for its traditional medicinal uses, which include treating fever, digestive issues, and skin problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of Suregada tenuifolia include alkaloids, saponins, and flavonoids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, Suregada tenuifolia is often used to make herbal teas, extracts, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into various Ayurvedic and Unani preparations.

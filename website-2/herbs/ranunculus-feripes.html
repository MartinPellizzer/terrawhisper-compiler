Ranunculus feripes

Ranunculus ferrox, also known as Creeping Buttercup or Ranunculus feripes, is a perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its yellow flowers and creeping stems.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects, making it useful in treating wounds and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus ferrox include ranunculin and protoanemonin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Ranunculus ferrox is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and ointments, often used topically to treat skin conditions and wounds.

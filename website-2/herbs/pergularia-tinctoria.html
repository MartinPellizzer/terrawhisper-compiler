Pergularia tinctoria

Pergularia tinctoria, also known as Indrajao or Jhau, is a perennial plant native to the Indian subcontinent, characterized by its woody roots and hairy stems, with small, white flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in the treatment of various ailments such as fever, cold, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Pergularia tinctoria include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, Pergularia tinctoria is commonly prepared in the form of decoctions, powders, and infusions to treat various health conditions, and is also used as a dye plant to give a range of colors to textiles and other materials.

Clinopodium graecum

Clinopodium graecum, also known as Greek calamint, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family Lamiaceae.

The bioactive constituents of Clinopodium graecum include essential oils, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Clinopodium graecum is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which can be used to treat various ailments, such as colds, coughs, and indigestion.

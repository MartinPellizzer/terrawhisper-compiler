Cirsium × erucagineum

Cirsium × erucagineum, also known as Butcher's broom or Cirsium, is a hybrid perennial plant species in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and anti-oxidative properties, which may help to reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease and improve blood circulation. The bioactive constituents of Cirsium × erucagineum include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat conditions such as varicose veins, hemorrhoids, and poor circulation.

classification

Senegalia ataxacantha is classified as a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae. It is a type of thorny tree native to tropical and subtropical regions. Senegalia ataxacantha is often found growing in woodland, grassland, or rocky areas. This species is part of the Acacia genus, but has been reclassified into the Senegalia genus as part of a recent revision. The reclassification reflects the ongoing refinement of botanical classification systems.

Senegalia ataxacantha has been used in traditional medicine, with its leaves and branches reported to have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The plant's bark is also used to treat diarrhea and other gastrointestinal issues. However, the effectiveness and safety of using Senegalia ataxacantha as a medicine have not been extensively scientifically studied.

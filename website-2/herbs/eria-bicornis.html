Eria bicornis

Eria bicornis, also known as the two-horned Eria, is a species of orchid native to the tropical forests of Asia.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties that may help alleviate various ailments such as fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Eria bicornis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Eria bicornis is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being dried and powdered for use in capsules or tablets.

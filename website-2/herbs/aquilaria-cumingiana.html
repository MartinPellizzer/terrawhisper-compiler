classification

Aquilaria cumingiana is a species of agarwood tree, and it belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae. The genus Aquilaria is a group of flowering plants that are native to tropical Asia. Aquilaria cumingiana is often considered to be a synonym of Aquilaria malaccensis, and both species are known for producing valuable agarwood. Aquilaria cumingiana is a threatened species due to over-harvesting for its resinous heartwood. This species is an important part of the ecosystem in its native range, providing habitat for various animals and insects.

medicinal parts

Aquilaria cumingiana is a species of agarwood tree native to the Philippines. The medicinal parts of Aquilaria cumingiana include the resinous heartwood, which is highly valued for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antifungal properties. This resinous heartwood is obtained through a natural process of infection by certain types of fungi, which causes the tree's wood to become highly aromatic and valuable. The heartwood is traditionally used in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and wounds. The resinous heartwood of Aquilaria cumingiana is also used in aromatherapy and perfumery for its distinct fragrance.

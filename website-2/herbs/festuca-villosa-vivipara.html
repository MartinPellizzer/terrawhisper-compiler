Festuca villosa-vivipara

Festuca villosa-vivipara, also known as viviparous hair grass or viviparous fescue, is a perennial plant species that belongs to the family Poaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to promote wound healing, reduce inflammation, and improve respiratory health due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Festuca villosa-vivipara include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is typically prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture to treat a range of health conditions, including respiratory infections and skin wounds.

Festuca villosa-vivipara is classified as a species of grass within the genus Festuca. It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family. Within the kingdom Plantae, Festuca villosa-vivipara is classified as a monocot, further categorizing it as an angiosperm.

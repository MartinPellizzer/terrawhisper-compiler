Pleopeltis sinensis

Pleopeltis sinensis, also known as Chinese tuckahoe or Chinese spleenwort, is a type of epiphytic fern native to China, Japan, and Korea.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiviral properties, which have been shown to aid in the treatment of various diseases such as fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Pleopeltis sinensis include various triterpenoid saponins, such as pleopelisins and sinensins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Pleopeltis sinensis is commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine to make herbal preparations such as tea, capsules, and decoctions, which are often used to treat colds, fever, and digestive issues.

Basilicum myriostachyum

Basilicum myriostachyum, also known as sweet basil, is a sweet-smelling annual herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family, native to India and Southeast Asia.

The plant is valued for its numerous health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antioxidant properties, making it useful in treating digestive issues, reducing stress, and protecting against cell damage. The bioactive constituents of Basilicum myriostachyum include essential oils such as linalool, eugenol, and limonene, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

The most common herbal preparations made with sweet basil include teas, infusions, and topical creams, as well as essential oils used in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.

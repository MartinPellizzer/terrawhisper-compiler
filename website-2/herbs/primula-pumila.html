Primula pumila

Primula pumila, also known as dwarf primrose, is a low-growing, perennial plant species native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, yellow, and white.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in traditional medicine for treating digestive issues and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Primula pumila include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Primula pumila is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often employed to alleviate symptoms of colds, flu, and other upper respiratory tract infections.

Ranunculus regularis

Ranunculus regularis, also known as the common buttercup, is a flowering plant species native to Europe and Western Asia, characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and shiny leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various ailments, including skin irritations and inflammation, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which provide relief from pain and promote wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus regularis include ranunculin, a toxic compound responsible for its medicinal properties, as well as alkaloids and glycosides, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Ranunculus regularis include tinctures, infusions, and creams, which are often used to treat skin conditions, such as eczema and acne, and to soothe minor burns and wounds.

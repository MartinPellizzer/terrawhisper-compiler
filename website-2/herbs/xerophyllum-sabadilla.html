Xerophyllum sabadilla

Xerophyllum sabadilla, also known as Mexican barberry, is a perennial plant native to the Americas, belonging to the asparagaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including treating fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, as well as its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Xerophyllum sabadilla include alkaloids, particularly sabadilla, as well as other glycosides and saponins.

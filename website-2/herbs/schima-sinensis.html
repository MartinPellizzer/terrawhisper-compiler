Schima sinensis

Schima sinensis, also known as the Chinese straggler hawthorn, is a deciduous tree species native to China and Southeast Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antioxidant effects, contributing to its potential health benefits in treating conditions such as fever, sore throats, and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Schima sinensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

Schima sinensis is commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine and can be found in various herbal preparations, including tea, decoctions, and infusions, as well as in dried and powdered forms.

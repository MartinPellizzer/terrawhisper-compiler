Chrysopogon elongatus

Chrysopogon elongatus, also known as Vétiver or Khus-khus, is a perennial grass native to India and Southeast Asia, belonging to the Poaceae family.

This plant is valued for its aromatic roots, which have been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health issues, including anxiety, insomnia, and digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of Chrysopogon elongatus include essential oils, such as vetiverol and vetiveryl acetate, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antioxidant properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to make herbal preparations, including essential oils, teas, and powders, which are commonly used to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

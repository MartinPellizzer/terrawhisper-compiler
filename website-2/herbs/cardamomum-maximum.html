Cardamomum maximum

Cardamomum maximum, also known as Amomum subulatum, is a perennial herb belonging to the Zingiberaceae family, commonly known as the ginger family.

The bioactive constituents of Cardamomum maximum include essential oils, particularly 1,8-cineole, limonene, and camphene, as well as glycosides, flavonoids, and alkaloids.

The most common herbal preparations made with Cardamomum maximum include capsules, teas, and extracts, which are often used to alleviate nausea, diarrhea, and other gastrointestinal symptoms.

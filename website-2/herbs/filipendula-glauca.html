Filipendula glauca

Filipendula glauca, also known as meadowsweet, is a perennial herb native to Europe and Western Asia, characterized by its clusters of small white or pink flowers and its aromatic, sweet-tasting leaves.

The plant is valued for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which have been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of colds, fevers, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Filipendula glauca include salicylic acid, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Meadowsweet is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being a component in some commercial cough medicines and herbal remedies for digestive issues.

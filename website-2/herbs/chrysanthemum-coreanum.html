Chrysanthemum coreanum

Chrysanthemum coreanum, also known as Korean chrysanthemum or Danggui, is a flowering plant species native to East Asia, traditionally used in herbal medicine for its medicinal properties.

This plant is valued for its purported health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antimicrobial properties, which are said to aid in the treatment of various conditions such as fever, colds, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents responsible for these effects include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which have been shown to exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

Chrysanthemum coreanum is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into traditional remedies such as tonics and infusions for its perceived health benefits.

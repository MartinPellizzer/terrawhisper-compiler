Angraecum salazianum

Angraecum salazianum, also known as the Salazie angraecum, is a rare and exotic orchid species native to the island of Réunion in the Indian Ocean.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, digestive problems, and respiratory infections, due to its reported health benefits. The bioactive constituents of Angraecum salazianum include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Angraecum salazianum is used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly consumed in Réunion and other parts of the world for its claimed therapeutic effects.

What is Primula gracilipes subsp. sulphurea?

Primula gracilipes subsp.

sulphurea is a plant subspecies belonging to the family Primulaceae. It is characterized by its yellow flowers with five petals, 1-2 cm in diameter, and a calyx of five lanceolate leaves.

The leaves are 5-10 cm long, 2-4 mm wide, and have a pointed tip, with a hairy margin. The plant grows 10-30 cm tall, with a rosette habit, and has a rhizomatous stem.

The subspecies is endemic to the western Himalayas, specifically in the Uttarakhand region of India.

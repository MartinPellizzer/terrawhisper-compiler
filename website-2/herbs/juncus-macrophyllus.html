Juncus macrophyllus

Juncus macrophyllus, also known as the giant rush, is a perennial aquatic plant species that thrives in wet environments, characterized by its tall stems and broad leaves.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which may contribute to its traditional use in treating various ailments, such as wounds and respiratory issues. Bioactive constituents of Juncus macrophyllus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Juncus macrophyllus include teas, infusions, and decoctions, which are often used in traditional medicine to treat a range of health conditions.

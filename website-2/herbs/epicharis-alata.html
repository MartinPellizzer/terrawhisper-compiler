Epicharis alata

Epicharis alata, also known as Patala, is a plant species belonging to the Apocynaceae family, commonly found in tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities that can help alleviate various health issues such as fever, rheumatism, and digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of Epicharis alata include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, the plant is typically used to prepare herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and powders, often in combination with other herbs like Triphala and Turmeric, to enhance their medicinal properties.

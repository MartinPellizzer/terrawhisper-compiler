Tretorhiza kurroo

Tretorhiza kurroo, commonly known as Kurchi or Pashanbheda, is a small, deciduous climber native to the Himalayan region.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and cardiotonic properties, which may help to alleviate conditions such as arthritis, digestive issues, and heart disease. The bioactive constituents of Tretorhiza kurroo include tetracyclic alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, Tretorhiza kurroo is often prepared as a decoction, powder, or tablet, and is commonly used to treat a range of health conditions, including anemia, kidney stones, and menstrual disorders.

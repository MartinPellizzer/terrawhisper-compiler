Stelis oblongifolia

Stelis oblongifolia, also known as the "Coral Root" plant, is a type of orchid native to North America, characterized by its delicate white or yellowish flowers and long, narrow leaves.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may aid in the treatment of various ailments such as fever, sore throats, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Stelis oblongifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often made into teas, infusions, or tinctures, and is also used in salves and poultices to treat various skin conditions and wounds.

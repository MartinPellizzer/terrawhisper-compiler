Lactuca hispida

Lactuca hispida, also known as hairy milk thistle or hispid milk thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion tribe within the sunflower family.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including the treatment of digestive issues, inflammation, and antioxidant activity. The bioactive constituents of Lactuca hispida include flavonoids, saponins, and sesquiterpene lactones, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Lactuca hispida is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to support liver health and alleviate symptoms of indigestion and bloating.

Senna septemtrionalis

Senna septemtrionalis, also known as Canadian senna, is a perennial plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, native to eastern North America.

The plant is valued for its laxative properties and has been traditionally used to treat constipation and other gastrointestinal disorders, due to its ability to stimulate bowel movements and promote digestive health. The bioactive constituents of Senna septemtrionalis include anthraquinone glycosides, particularly aloe-emodin and rhein, as well as other anthraquinones and flavonoids.

Canadian senna is commonly prepared in herbal remedies, such as teas, capsules, and tinctures, to support digestive health and relieve constipation.

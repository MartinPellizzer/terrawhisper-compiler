What is Rhamnus pumila var. valentina?

Rhamnus pumila var. valentina is a shrub-like plant that belongs to the family Rhamnaceae.

It is a variant of Rhamnus pumila, a species native to the Mediterranean region. The plant has a woody stem, typically growing up to 2 meters in height, with a spread of around 1.5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, measuring 2-5 centimeters in length, with a glabrous or slightly hairy surface and a cuneate base.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in axillary racemes, which are followed by black, berry-like fruits.

The main herbal preparations of Rhamnus pumila var. valentina are tincture, infusion, rhamnus tea, salve, and decoction.

Additionally, improper use of Rhamnus pumila var. valentina may lead to nephrotoxicity and hepatotoxicity due to the presence of cardenolides and anthraquinones, causing damage to the kidneys and liver respectively. Furthermore, Rhamnus pumila var. valentina has been reported to trigger allergic reactions in some individuals, particularly those with hypersensitivity to plants in the Rhamnaceae family. The allergenic properties of Rhamnus pumila var. valentina can cause anaphylaxis, a severe and potentially life-threatening allergic reaction.

The hepatotoxic effects of Rhamnus pumila var. valentina are particularly concerning and can be exacerbated by concurrent use of other hepatotoxic substances.

classification

Chrysopogon pallidus is classified as a species of grass in the family Poaceae. It is a member of the genus Chrysopogon, which is a group of grasses native to tropical and subtropical regions. Chrysopogon pallidus is further classified as a species of the subfamily Panicoideae, which includes many types of grasses and wild grasses. The full classification of Chrysopogon pallidus is Chrysopogon pallidus (Nees ex Hook. & Arn.) Boivin. This classification system helps scientists to understand the relationships between different plant species and their evolutionary history.

medicinal uses

Chrysopogon pallidus has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine, where it is known as 'Vetiver'. It is valued for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which are said to aid in the treatment of various health issues. The plant's essential oil, extracted from its roots, is also used to promote relaxation and reduce stress. Studies have shown that the oil may have antimicrobial and antioxidant properties, making it a potential candidate for wound healing and skin care applications.

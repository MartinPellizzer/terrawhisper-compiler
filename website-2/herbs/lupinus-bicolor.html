Lupinus bicolor

Lupinus bicolor, also known as the silver bush lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western coastal regions of North America, characterized by its tall spikes of small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help to soothe digestive issues, reduce pain and inflammation, and support immune function. The bioactive constituents of Lupinus bicolor include alkaloids such as lupanine, sparteine, and anagyrine, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Lupinus bicolor include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to support respiratory health, alleviate symptoms of menopause, and promote relaxation and sleep.

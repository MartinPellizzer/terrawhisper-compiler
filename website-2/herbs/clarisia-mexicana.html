Clarisia mexicana

Clarisia mexicana, also known as Mexican clarisia or Mexican water pepper, is a plant species in the family Cannabaceae, native to the tropical forests of Central and South America.

The bioactive constituents of Clarisia mexicana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its pharmacological activities.

Clarisia mexicana is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or decoction, and is also used in traditional medicine in various forms, including tinctures and extracts, to take advantage of its medicinal properties.

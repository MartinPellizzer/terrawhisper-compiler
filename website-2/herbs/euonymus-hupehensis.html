Euonymus hupehensis

Euonymus hupehensis, also known as the Chinese spindle tree, is a deciduous shrub native to China, characterized by its distinctive red berries and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and antibacterial properties. The bioactive constituents of Euonymus hupehensis include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In herbal medicine, Euonymus hupehensis is often prepared as a decoction or a tincture, and is also used in combination with other herbs to treat conditions such as bronchitis and eczema.

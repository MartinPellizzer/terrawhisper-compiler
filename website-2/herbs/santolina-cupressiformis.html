Santolina cupressiformis

Santolina cupressiformis, also known as Grey Cotton Lavender or Lavender Cotton, is a shrubby perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its grey-green foliage and yellow or purple flowers.

The bioactive constituents of Santolina cupressiformis include essential oils such as camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Santolina cupressiformis include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality.

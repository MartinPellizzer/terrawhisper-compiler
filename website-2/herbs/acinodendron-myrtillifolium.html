Acinodendron myrtillifolium

Acinodendron myrtillifolium, also known as the 'Brazilian peppertree' or 'Florida pepperbush,' is a flowering plant native to South America, belonging to the family Melastomataceae.

The bioactive constituents of A. myrtillifolium include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat fever, cough, and other respiratory problems, as well as to promote wound healing.

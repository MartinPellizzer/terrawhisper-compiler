Zigadenus leimanthoides

Zigadenus leimanthoides, also known as death camas, is a perennial plant native to North America, characterized by its white or yellowish flowers and onion-like foliage.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, which include treating fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Zigadenus leimanthoides include alkaloids, terpenoids, and saponins, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Zigadenus leimanthoides is often prepared as a tincture or infusion, although its use is generally cautioned due to potential toxicity and the risk of misidentification with other, more toxic plants.

What is Pilosella densiflora subsp. pseudochrocephala?

Pilosella densiflora subsp.

pseudochrocephala is a subspecies of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its small, dandelion-like leaves with a dense, woolly tomentum, typically measuring 5-10 mm in length, and its yellow, rayless flower heads, 10-15 mm in diameter, composed of tubular disc florets.

The plant's habit is erect, typically reaching a height of 10-30 cm. Pilosella densiflora subsp. pseudochrocephala is found in mountainous regions of Europe, typically growing in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

It is a rare and localised taxon, often associated with the presence of other rare alpine plants.

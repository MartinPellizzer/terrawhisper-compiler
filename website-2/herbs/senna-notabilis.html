Senna notabilis

Senna notabilis, commonly known as the yellow cassia or golden cassia, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

Traditionally, Senna notabilis has been used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including constipation, dyspepsia, and fever, due to its laxative and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Senna notabilis include anthraquinones, such as sennosides A and B, which are responsible for its purgative effects.

Senna notabilis is often used to make herbal teas, capsules, and extracts, which are commonly used to support digestive health and relieve constipation.

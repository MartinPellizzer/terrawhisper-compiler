Halimocnemis commixtus

Halimocnemis commixtus, also known as desert saltbush, is a shrub species in the Amaranthaceae family native to the Middle East and North Africa.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, which may aid in the treatment of various ailments including wounds, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of H. commixtus include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, contributing to its pharmacological activities.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into topical preparations such as creams and ointments for wound healing and skin conditions.

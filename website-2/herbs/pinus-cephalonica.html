Pinus cephalonica

Pinus cephalonica, also known as the Greek black pine, is a species of evergreen tree native to Greece and Albania, characterized by its dark gray bark and long, needle-like leaves.

The resin of the Greek black pine has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, offering potential health benefits in the treatment of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as its antioxidant and immunomodulatory effects. The bioactive constituents of Pinus cephalonica resin include terpenoids, phenolic compounds, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

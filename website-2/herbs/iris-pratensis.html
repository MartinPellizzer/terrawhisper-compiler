Iris pratensis

Iris pratensis, also known as the meadow iris or English iris, is a perennial plant species native to Europe and Western Asia, characterized by its vibrant purple flowers and sword-shaped leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including respiratory issues, due to its health benefits, which include expectorant, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic properties. The bioactive constituents of Iris pratensis include iridoids, such as iridin and irigenin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Iris pratensis include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to alleviate symptoms of coughs, colds, and bronchitis.

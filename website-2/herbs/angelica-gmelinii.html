Angelica gmelinii

Angelica gmelinii, also known as Chinese angelica or dong quai, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia, belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-estrogenic effects, making it useful for treating menstrual disorders, menopausal symptoms, and cardiovascular diseases. The bioactive constituents of Angelica gmelinii include ferulic acid, ligustilide, and butylphthalide, which contribute to its health benefits.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are dried roots, teas, capsules, and tablets, as well as wine and vinegar infusions, which are used to treat various health conditions, particularly women's health issues.

Lactaria glomerata

Lactaria glomerata, also known as the milky cap mushroom, is a species of edible fungus belonging to the genus Lactarius, characterized by its white to pale yellow cap and red to pinkish gills.

The milky cap mushroom has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may help to boost the immune system and aid in wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Lactaria glomerata include lactones, triterpenes, and polysaccharides, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the milky cap mushroom is often prepared as a decoction or dried powder, which can be used to make teas, capsules, or topical applications to support overall health and well-being.

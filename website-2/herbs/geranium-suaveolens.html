Geranium suaveolens

Geranium suaveolens, also known as sweet-scented geranium, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae, characterized by its fragrant flowers and leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used for its health benefits, including reducing anxiety and stress, promoting relaxation, and alleviating symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes and mood swings. The bioactive constituents of Geranium suaveolens include geraniol, linalool, and furocoumarins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, Geranium suaveolens is often used to make teas, tinctures, and essential oils, which are used to promote emotional well-being and relieve menstrual cramps and other gynecological issues.

What is Anthriscus sylvestris proles alpestris?

Anthriscus sylvestris proles alpestris is a botanical taxon belonging to the family Apiaceae, a large and economically important group of flowering plants.

This plant is a perennial herb with a taproot, growing up to 100 cm tall, with a stout stem bearing compound leaves divided into 2-3 pairs of pinnate leaflets. The flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, with 5-7 rays, and are produced in late spring and early summer. The flowers are followed by spherical or oval fruit, 4-6 mm long, containing 2-3 seeds, which are typically 2 mm in length and have a smooth surface.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade to full sun, typically in mountainous regions at elevations of 1,500-2,500 meters above sea level.

What are the therapeutic properties this herb?

Anthriscus sylvestris proles alpestris also exhibits antimicrobial activity, with its saponin content, including derivatives of hederagenin, contributing to its ability to inhibit the growth of various microorganisms. Furthermore, the plant has been found to possess anti-cancer properties, with its bioactive compounds, such as sesquiterpene lactones, inducing apoptosis in cancer cells and inhibiting their proliferation.

The plant's diuretic property is also attributed to its flavonoid and volatile oil content, which can increase urine production and help to alleviate fluid retention.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

These preparations are typically used in traditional medicine to aid in digestion and relieve symptoms of bloating and gas. Tincture is prepared by soaking the leaves in a solvent such as ethanol or glycerin to create a concentrated liquid extract.

The herbal preparations of Anthriscus sylvestris proles alpestris are valued for their carminative properties and ability to soothe the digestive system.

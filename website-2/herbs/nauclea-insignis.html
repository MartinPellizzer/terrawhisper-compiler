Nauclea insignis

Nauclea insignis, also known as "African Bloodwood" or "African Nauclea", is a tropical tree species native to the tropical forests of West and Central Africa.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, malaria, and respiratory infections, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antimalarial properties, which contribute to its potential health benefits. The bioactive constituents of Nauclea insignis include alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Nauclea insignis is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, as well as in the form of a syrup or tincture, which are the most common herbal preparations made from this plant.

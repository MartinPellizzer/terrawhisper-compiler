Dolicholus filicaulis

Dolicholus filicaulis, also known as Chinese forked vetch or forked vetch, is a perennial legume native to East Asia, characterized by its distinctive forked inflorescence.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat a range of health conditions, including respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its expectorant properties, which help to clear mucus from the lungs. The bioactive constituents of Dolicholus filicaulis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Dolicholus filicaulis is often made into teas, infusions, or decoctions, as well as being used to prepare topical ointments and salves for skin conditions and wounds.

Ugni montana

Ugni montana, also known as Chilean guava or Murtilla, is a shrub-like plant native to the temperate forests of South America, characterized by its red or purple berries and aromatic leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, providing relief from digestive issues, such as diarrhea and dyspepsia, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Ugni montana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its health benefits.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ugni montana is commonly used to prepare teas, infusions, and extracts, as well as being used in the production of jams and preserves, making the most of its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Melaleuca cinerea

Melaleuca cinerea, also known as swamp paperbark, is a plant species native to Australia, characterized by its grey-green leaves and distinctive papery bark.

The leaves of Melaleuca cinerea have been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including skin infections, wounds, and respiratory issues, due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Melaleuca cinerea include cineole, a monoterpene hydrocarbon, as well as other volatile oils, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Melaleuca cinerea is often used to make topical herbal preparations, such as creams, ointments, and essential oils, which are used to treat skin conditions, promote wound healing, and provide relief from respiratory symptoms.

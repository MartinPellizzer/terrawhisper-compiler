Salacia laevigata

Salacia laevigata, also known as Indian walnut or Salacia, is a plant species native to tropical Asia, belonging to the family Celastraceae.

The health benefits of Salacia laevigata include its potential to help manage and reduce blood sugar levels, improve lipid profiles, and aid in weight management, making it a valuable supplement in the management of type 2 diabetes and metabolic syndrome. The bioactive constituents of Salacia laevigata include salacin, a natural inhibitor of alpha-glucosidase enzymes, as well as other polyphenolic compounds and flavonoids that contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Salacia laevigata include capsules, tablets, and extracts, which are often standardized to contain a minimum of 2% salacin content, making it a popular ingredient in traditional Ayurvedic and modern herbal supplements.

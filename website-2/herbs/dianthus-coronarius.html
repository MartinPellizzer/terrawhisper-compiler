Dianthus coronarius

Dianthus coronarius, also known as the fringed pink or fragrant pink, is a perennial plant species belonging to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

The bioactive constituents of Dianthus coronarius include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, particularly geraniol and linalool, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Dianthus coronarius include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to alleviate respiratory symptoms and promote relaxation.

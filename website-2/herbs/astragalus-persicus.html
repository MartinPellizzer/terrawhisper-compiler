Astragalus persicus

Astragalus persicus, a perennial legume native to the Middle East and South Asia, is a species of medicinal plant commonly referred to as Persian or Asian milk-vetch.

The bioactive constituents of Astragalus persicus include polysaccharides, saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine and herbalism, Astragalus persicus is often prepared as a decoction or dried root, and is also commonly used in the form of capsules, tablets, and teas to promote overall health and well-being.

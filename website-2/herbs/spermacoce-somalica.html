Spermacoce somalica

Spermacoce somalica, also known as Somalian bindweed, is a plant species in the Rubiaceae family characterized by its creeping stems and small, white flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, particularly in East Africa, where it is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antidiabetic properties. The bioactive constituents of Spermacoce somalica include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Spermacoce somalica is often prepared as a tea or infusion, or made into a powder for use in capsules or as a topical application, although further research is needed to fully understand its efficacy and safety.

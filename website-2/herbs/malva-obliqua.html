Malva obliqua

Malva obliqua, also known as the blue mallow, is a flowering plant species belonging to the mallow family (Malvaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as soothing digestive issues, reducing inflammation, and exhibiting antimicrobial and antioxidant activities. Bioactive constituents of Malva obliqua include malvidin, quercetin, and mucilages, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and salves, with the dried leaves and flowers often being used in traditional remedies for skin conditions, respiratory issues, and gastrointestinal problems.

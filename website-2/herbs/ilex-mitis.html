Ilex mitis

Ilex mitis, also known as the Chinese holly or Asian holly, is a plant species within the Ilex genus, native to East Asia and characterized by its evergreen foliage and red berries.

The leaves and fruits of Ilex mitis have been traditionally used in herbal medicine, offering various health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which can help to boost the immune system and protect against infections. The bioactive constituents of Ilex mitis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and triterpenoids, such as aucubin and ilicin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Ilex mitis is often used to make teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into various herbal preparations, including capsules and tablets.

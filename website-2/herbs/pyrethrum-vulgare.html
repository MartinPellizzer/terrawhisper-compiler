Pyrethrum vulgare

Pyrethrum vulgare, also known as common pyrethrum, is a perennial daisy-like flowering plant native to Europe and Asia, belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

The plant is valued for its health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help soothe skin irritations, reduce fever, and combat infections. The bioactive constituents of Pyrethrum vulgare include pyrethrins, sesquiterpene lactones, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Pyrethrum vulgare include tinctures, infusions, and topical creams, which are often used to treat skin conditions and promote wound healing.

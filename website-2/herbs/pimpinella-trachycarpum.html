Pimpinella trachycarpum

Pimpinella trachycarpum, also known as Aniseed or Pimpinella, is a perennial plant species belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

The bioactive constituents of Pimpinella trachycarpum include flavonoids, alkaloids, and volatile oils, including anethole, which is responsible for its distinct aniseed flavor and aroma.

In traditional medicine, Pimpinella trachycarpum is often used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health issues, including coughs, colds, and menstrual cramps.

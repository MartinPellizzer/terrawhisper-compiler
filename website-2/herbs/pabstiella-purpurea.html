Pabstiella purpurea

Pabstiella purpurea, also known as Pabst's orchid or Pabstella purpurea, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Brazil.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, offering potential health benefits such as antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which may contribute to the treatment of various conditions, including infections, wounds, and gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of Pabstiella purpurea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Pabstiella purpurea is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is often used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs.

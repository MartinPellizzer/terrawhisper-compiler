Butea purpurea

Butea purpurea, also known as Palash or Dhak, is a flowering plant native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory problems, due to its numerous health benefits. The bioactive constituents of Butea purpurea include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Butea purpurea is commonly prepared as a decoction or an infusion, or made into a powder or capsule for the treatment of various ailments, and is also used in traditional skincare products for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

classification

Butea purpurea is classified in the kingdom Plantae, in the family Fabaceae, and in the genus Butea. It is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The family Fabaceae is also known as the legume family, which includes plants that produce seed pods. The genus Butea is a monotypic genus, meaning that it contains only one species, Butea purpurea. This classification is based on the plant's characteristics, such as its flowers and seed pods.

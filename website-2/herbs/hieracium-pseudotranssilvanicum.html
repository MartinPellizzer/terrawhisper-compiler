Hieracium pseudotranssilvanicum

Hieracium pseudotranssilvanicum, also known as false Transylvanian hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it useful for treating various conditions such as wounds, infections, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Hieracium pseudotranssilvanicum include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Hieracium pseudotranssilvanicum is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health concerns.

Aloinopsis aloides

Aloinopsis aloides, commonly known as the yellow desert rose or yellow aloinopsis, is a succulent plant species native to the Western Cape province of South Africa.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including constipation, digestive issues, and inflammation, due to its potential health benefits. The bioactive constituents of Aloinopsis aloides include aloin, aloe-emodin, and other anthraquinone glycosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Aloinopsis aloides include teas, tinctures, and capsules, which are often used to support digestive health and relieve symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome.

Muscaria muscoides

Muscaria muscoides, commonly known as fly agaric, is a species of fungal organism that belongs to the genus Amanita, characterized by its distinctive red cap with white spots.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include muscimol, ibotenic acid, and other amatoxins, which are responsible for its psychoactive properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Muscaria muscoides is often used to make preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including anxiety, depression, and respiratory issues.

Seseli bulgaricum

Seseli bulgaricum, also known as Bulgarian masterwort, is a perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula, belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including gastrointestinal problems, fever, and respiratory infections, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Seseli bulgaricum include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Seseli bulgaricum is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion, as well as being used in combination with other herbs in formulations such as cough syrups and expectorants.

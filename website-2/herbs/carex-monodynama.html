Carex monodynama

Carex monodynama, also known as Black Sedge, is a perennial plant species belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae), characterized by its dark green, linear leaves and slender stems.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, particularly in Asia, to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its reported anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Carex monodynama include iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Carex monodynama is often used to prepare infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are believed to aid in the treatment of colds, coughs, and sore throats.

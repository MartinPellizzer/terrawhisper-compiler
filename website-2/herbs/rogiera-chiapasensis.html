Rogiera chiapasensis

Rogiera chiapasensis is a plant species native to Mexico, belonging to the Rubiaceae family and commonly known as Chiapas Rogiera or Chiapas Rogiera shrub.

The bioactive constituents of Rogiera chiapasensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, such as rogiarin and rogiaridine, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Traditionally, Rogiera chiapasensis is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into tinctures and other liquid extracts, which are used to treat various health conditions.

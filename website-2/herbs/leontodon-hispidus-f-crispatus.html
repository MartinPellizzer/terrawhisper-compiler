What is Leontodon hispidus f. crispatus?

Leontodon hispidus f. crispatus is a botanical variant of the species Leontodon hispidus, which is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It has a compact, hairy stem that grows up to 10-30 cm tall, bearing linear-lanceolate leaves that are 5-20 cm long and 1-5 mm wide, with a ciliate margin. The plant produces yellow daisy-like flowers in late summer, arranged in a capitulum with 15-30 florets, each with 5 yellow, sometimes pink or white, ray florets and 15-30 disc florets. The capitulum is 2-4 cm in diameter, and is surrounded by a bract that is leaf-like and hairy.

The plant has a fibrous root system, and is commonly found in dry, sandy soils and waste places in Europe and Western Asia.

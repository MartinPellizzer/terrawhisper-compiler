Cicerbita crambifolia

Cicerbita crambifolia, also known as wild endive, is a perennial herb belonging to the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which can aid in the treatment of digestive issues and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Cicerbita crambifolia include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, wild endive is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used as a raw ingredient in salads and other culinary preparations.

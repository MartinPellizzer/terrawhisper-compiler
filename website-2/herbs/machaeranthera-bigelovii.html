Machaeranthera bigelovii

Machaeranthera bigelovii, also known as Bigelow's tansy aster, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

The plant is used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, which have been traditionally employed to treat various health conditions, including wounds, skin irritations, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Machaeranthera bigelovii include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and salves, which are often used topically to soothe skin conditions and wounds.

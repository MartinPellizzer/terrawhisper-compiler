Ranunculus trinus

Ranunculus trinus, also known as Three-leafed Ranunculus or Three-leaved Buttercup, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its bright yellow flowers and three-parted leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, skin conditions, and respiratory problems, due to its potential anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus trinus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

Ranunculus trinus is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for skin conditions.

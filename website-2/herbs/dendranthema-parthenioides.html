Dendranthema parthenioides

Dendranthema parthenioides, also known as Chrysanthemum, is a type of flowering plant that belongs to the Asteraceae family.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving cardiovascular health, and exhibiting antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Chrysanthemum include flavonoids, saponins, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Chrysanthemum include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat various conditions, including fever, headaches, and respiratory issues.

classification

Dendranthema parthenioides is classified as a species of flowering plant within the family Asteraceae. This classification places it among the daisies and other related species in the same family. The genus Dendranthema is now often subsumed into Chrysanthemum, but some sources still recognize it as a distinct group. Dendranthema parthenioides is native to China and has been naturalized in other parts of the world. It is valued for its ornamental flowers and can be found in various gardens and landscapes.

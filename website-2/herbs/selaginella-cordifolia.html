Selaginella cordifolia

Selaginella cordifolia, also known as Spiked Spikemoss, is a type of vascular plant that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae, characterized by its unique, spiky appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of environments.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, skin infections, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Selaginella cordifolia include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Selaginella cordifolia is often prepared as a decoction or a powder, and is commonly used in Ayurvedic formulations, such as Kalyanaka Ghritam, to promote overall health and well-being.

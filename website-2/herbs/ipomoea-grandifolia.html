Ipomoea grandifolia

Ipomoea grandifolia, also known as the largeleaf moonflower, is a perennial vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which may aid in reducing pain and inflammation, as well as protecting against oxidative stress and cell damage. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea grandifolia include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are responsible for its pharmacological activities.

In herbal medicine, Ipomoea grandifolia is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is used to treat various conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues.

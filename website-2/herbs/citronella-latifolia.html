Citronella latifolia

Citronella latifolia, also known as Ceylon citronella, is a species of grass native to Sri Lanka, belonging to the family Poaceae.

The health benefits of Citronella latifolia include its natural insect-repelling properties, as well as its potential to reduce inflammation, alleviate digestive issues, and exhibit antimicrobial and antifungal activities. The bioactive constituents of this plant include citronella oil, which contains compounds such as citronellal, citronellol, and geranial, responsible for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Citronella latifolia are essential oils, teas, and infusions, often used in traditional medicine, aromatherapy, and as a natural insect repellent.

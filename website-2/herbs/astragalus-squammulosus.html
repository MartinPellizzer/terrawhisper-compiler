Astragalus squammulosus

Astragalus squammulosus, also known as Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial plant in the legume family native to China and Mongolia.

It has been traditionally used in medicine for its health benefits, which include immune system enhancement, anti-inflammatory properties, and antioxidant activity, making it a popular remedy for colds, flu, and other respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus squammulosus include flavonoids, saponins, and polysaccharides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Astragalus is commonly prepared as a decoction, capsule, or extract, and is often combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

Aloe albida

Aloe albida, also known as white aloe, is a succulent plant species belonging to the Aloe genus, native to southern Africa.

The health benefits of Aloe albida include its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, which make it effective in treating skin irritations, wounds, and burns. The bioactive constituents of Aloe albida include aloin, aloe-emodin, and isobarbaloin, which have been found to exhibit antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anticancer activities.

Aloe albida is commonly prepared into herbal remedies such as gel, juice, and ointments, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including skin conditions, digestive issues, and infections.

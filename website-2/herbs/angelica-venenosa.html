Angelica venenosa

Angelica venenosa, also known as American angelica or poisonous angelica, is a perennial plant species belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae) native to eastern North America.

The bioactive constituents of Angelica venenosa include volatile oils, phenolic acids, flavonoids, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Angelica venenosa is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as dried roots and rhizomes, which are commonly used to support respiratory health and relieve menstrual cramps.

Triticum squarrosum

Triticum squarrosum, also known as wild einkorn, is a wild relative of domesticated einkorn wheat (Triticum monococcum) and is characterized by its single grain per spikelet and a hard, brittle seed coat.

The health benefits of Triticum squarrosum include its potential to lower cholesterol levels, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antioxidant properties due to its rich content of fiber, vitamins, and minerals. The bioactive constituents of this plant include phenolic acids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Triticum squarrosum include teas, infusions, and extracts, which are traditionally used to treat various health conditions, such as digestive issues and respiratory problems.

What is Hieracium wilczekianum subsp. intybelloides?

Hieracium wilczekianum subsp.

intybelloides is a subspecies of Hieracium, belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is a perennial herb with erect stems, 10-40 cm tall, bearing 2-4 pinnatifid leaves and a terminal inflorescence of 10-20 yellowish or reddish flower heads, each head 1-2 cm in diameter, containing 10-20 ligulate florets.

The leaves are 5-15 cm long, with 3-7 pairs of lanceolate to ovate lobes, and the stem is simple, glabrous, and leafy. The plant grows in alpine and subalpine zones, typically on dry, rocky slopes and ridges.

The subspecies is endemic to the Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan mountain ranges.

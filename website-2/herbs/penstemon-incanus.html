Penstemon incanus

Penstemon incanus, commonly known as Graystem Beardtongue, is a perennial plant species native to the western United States and Canada, characterized by its grayish stems and tubular, pinkish-purple flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat a variety of health issues, including respiratory problems, fever, and sore throats, due to its expectorant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Penstemon incanus include phenolic acids, flavonoids, and alkaloids, such as caffeic acid, quercetin, and gentianine, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as in topical applications such as salves and poultices, to soothe and calm the body.

Himatanthus sucuuba

Himatanthus sucuuba, also known as "guanina", is a plant species native to the tropical forests of Central and South America, belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Himatanthus sucuuba include alkaloids, such as sucupira and sucuuba, as well as glycosides and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In herbal medicine, Himatanthus sucuuba is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is commonly used in the form of a cough syrup or expectorant tonic.

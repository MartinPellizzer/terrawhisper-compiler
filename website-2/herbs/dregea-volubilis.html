Dregea volubilis

Dregea volubilis, also known as the 'creeping foxglove', is a woody climber native to tropical Asia, characterized by its tubular flowers and hairy leaves.

This plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine, boasting various health benefits, including its potential to treat fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, as well as its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Dregea volubilis include alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Dregea volubilis is often used to make preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and tablets, as well as being incorporated into various formulations to enhance their therapeutic effects.

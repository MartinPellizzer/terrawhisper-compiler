Ipomoea dubia, also known as Dillenia dubia, is a climbing plant species belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea dubia include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which have been identified as responsible for its medicinal activities.

In traditional medicine, Ipomoea dubia is often prepared in the form of a decoction or tincture to treat various health conditions, and is also used as a topical application for skin infections and wounds.

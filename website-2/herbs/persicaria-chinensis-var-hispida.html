What is Persicaria chinensis var. hispida?

Persicaria chinensis var. hispida is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It has a taproot and a stem that can grow up to 60 cm in height, with a diameter of 1-2 mm and a reddish-brown color, and is hairy with short, fine hairs. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate-lanceolate in shape, measuring 2-8 cm in length and 1-3 cm in width, with a pointed tip and a hairy margin. The leaves are also reddish-brown on the lower surface and have 5-7 main veins.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in a terminal spike or panicle.

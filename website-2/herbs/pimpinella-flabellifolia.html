Pimpinella flabellifolia

Pimpinella flabellifolia, also known as fan-leaved anise, is a perennial herb belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and carminative effects, making it useful in treating digestive issues, respiratory problems, and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Pimpinella flabellifolia include volatile oils, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pimpinella flabellifolia is commonly used in traditional medicine to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to alleviate symptoms of indigestion, coughs, and colds.

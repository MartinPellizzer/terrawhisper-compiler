Ranunculus tenebrosus

Ranunculus tenebrosus, also known as Dark-leaved Buttercup, is a perennial plant species in the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which may aid in wound healing and reduce fever. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus tenebrosus include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, such as ranunculin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Ranunculus tenebrosus is often prepared as a tincture, infusion, or decoction to treat various ailments, including infections and skin conditions.

Cereus venezuelensis

Cereus venezuelensis, also known as the Venezuelan column cactus, is a species of cactus in the genus Cereus, characterized by its tall, columnar stem and white flowers.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, pain, and inflammation, due to its potential antipyretic, analgesic, and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Cereus venezuelensis include alkaloids, flavonoids, and glycosides, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal effects.

In herbal medicine, Cereus venezuelensis is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are employed to treat respiratory issues, such as coughs and colds, and to alleviate symptoms of digestive disorders.

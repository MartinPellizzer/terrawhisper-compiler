Benzoin venosum

Benzoin venosum, also known as the tree benzoin or sumatran benzoin, is a plant species native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia, characterized by its sweet, sticky resin that is often harvested for medicinal and aromatic purposes.

The resin of Benzoin venosum has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including wounds, skin irritations, and respiratory issues, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Benzoin venosum resin include benzoin, benzyl benzoate, and vanillin, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Benzoin venosum include tinctures, creams, and ointments, which are often used to treat skin conditions, colds, and coughs.

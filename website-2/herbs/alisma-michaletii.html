Alisma michaletii

Alisma michaletii, also known as the water plantain or Chinese water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to China and Japan, belonging to the Alismataceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used in medicine for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and anti-diabetic properties, offering potential health benefits in treating conditions such as hypertension, edema, and hyperglycemia. The bioactive constituents of Alisma michaletii include iridoid glycosides, such as alisol A 24-acetate and alisol B 23-acetate, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Alisma michaletii is commonly prepared in herbal medicine as a decoction or a tincture, often used to treat various health conditions, particularly those related to water retention and blood sugar regulation.

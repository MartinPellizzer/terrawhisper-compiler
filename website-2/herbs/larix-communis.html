Larix communis

Larix communis, commonly known as the common larch or European larch, is a deciduous coniferous tree species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

The health benefits of Larix communis include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which are said to aid in the treatment of various conditions such as bronchitis, coughs, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Larix communis include teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as resin and essential oil extracts used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

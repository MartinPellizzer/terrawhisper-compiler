What is Campyloneurum angustifolium var. taeniosum?

Campyloneurum angustifolium var. taeniosum is a fern variety within the family Polypodiaceae, native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, lanceolate leaves, typically 5-12 cm long and 1-2.5 cm wide, with a wavy or serrated margin. The rhizome is short, with scales that are lanceolate and entire, and the fertile frond is similar in shape to the sterile frond, but smaller and more slender. The sporangia are arranged in a continuous marginal soralium along the length of the frond, with 64 spores per sporangium.

This variety is often found growing on trees, rocks, and other substrates in humid, shaded environments.

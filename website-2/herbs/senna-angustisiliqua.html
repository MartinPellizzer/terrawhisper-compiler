Senna angustisiliqua

Senna angustisiliqua, also known as narrow-leaved senna, is a perennial plant in the pea family (Fabaceae) native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant has been traditionally used for its health benefits, including its ability to stimulate bowel movements and relieve constipation due to its high content of anthraquinone glycosides, which act as natural laxatives. The bioactive constituents of Senna angustisiliqua include anthraquinone glycosides, such as aloe-emodin, chrysophanol, and rhein, as well as other flavonoids and phenolic acids.

The most common herbal preparations made with Senna angustisiliqua are dried leaf extracts, capsules, and teas, which are often used to support digestive health and relieve symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS).

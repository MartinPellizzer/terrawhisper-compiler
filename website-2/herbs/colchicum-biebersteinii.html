Colchicum biebersteinii

Colchicum biebersteinii, also known as autumn crocus, is a perennial plant belonging to the Colchicaceae family, characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and analgesic properties, which have been associated with various health benefits, including pain relief and reduced inflammation. The bioactive constituents of Colchicum biebersteinii include colchicine, a key alkaloid responsible for the plant's medicinal properties, as well as other alkaloids such as colchicoside and demecolcine.

In traditional medicine, Colchicum biebersteinii is often prepared as a tincture, capsule, or powder, and is used to treat conditions such as gout, arthritis, and fibromyalgia.

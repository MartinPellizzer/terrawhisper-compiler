Dracula inaequalis

Dracula inaequalis, commonly known as the "Vampire Orchid," is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Ecuador.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, which may aid in reducing fever, soothing respiratory issues, and supporting immune function. The bioactive constituents of Dracula inaequalis include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms associated with colds and flu.

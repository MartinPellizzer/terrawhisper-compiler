Lepisorus sinensis

Lepisorus sinensis, also known as Chinese lepidotis or Chinese shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia, characterized by its delicate, fan-shaped fronds and unique shield-like shape.

Traditionally, the plant has been used in medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and respiratory issues, due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Lepisorus sinensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is often processed into teas, infusions, or dried whole plants, and is also used in combination with other herbs to create tonics and decoctions.

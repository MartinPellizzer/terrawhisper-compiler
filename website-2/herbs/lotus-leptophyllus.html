Lotus leptophyllus

Lotus leptophyllus, also known as Lotus leaf, is a perennial aquatic plant that belongs to the family Fabaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine, particularly in Asian cultures, due to its reported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help to alleviate conditions such as fever, sore throats, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Lotus leptophyllus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Lotus leaf is commonly prepared as a tea or infused in hot water to make a decoction, or used to make a medicinal soup or tincture, which are believed to promote digestion, relieve coughs, and soothe feverish symptoms.

Isodon nilgherricus

Isodon nilgherricus, also known as Nilgiri isodon, is a perennial shrub belonging to the family Lamiaceae, native to the Himalayas and the Western Ghats mountain ranges in India.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of I. nilgherricus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, such as isodonin and nilgirin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Indian medicine, I. nilgherricus is often used to make herbal preparations like teas, decoctions, and infusions, as well as being added to formulations like ghanas and arishtas to treat various health conditions.

Crataegus nitida

Crataegus nitida, commonly known as the glossy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Eastern Europe and Asia, characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

The glossy hawthorn has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat cardiovascular issues, such as hypertension and atherosclerosis, due to its potential to lower blood pressure and improve blood lipid profiles. The bioactive constituents of C. nitida include flavonoids, triterpenoids, and oligomeric proanthocyanidins, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with glossy hawthorn include teas, tinctures, and capsules, often used to promote cardiovascular health and well-being.

Cymbopogon elegans

Cymbopogon elegans, commonly known as Lemon Grass, is a tropical grass species native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its slender leaves and citrusy fragrance.

The essential oil extracted from its leaves has been widely used for its medicinal and therapeutic properties, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of anxiety, fever, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Cymbopogon elegans include citral, geraniol, and limonene, which are responsible for its aromatic and medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Cymbopogon elegans is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and essential oil preparations, which are often used to promote relaxation, improve respiratory health, and boost the immune system.

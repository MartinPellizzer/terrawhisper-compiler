What is Polygala penaea subsp. guantanamana?

Polygala penaea subsp.

guantanamana is a botanical subspecies of the Polygala penaea plant, belonging to the Polygalaceae family. It is a perennial herb with a woody base, typically growing up to 0.5-1.5 meters in height.

The plant's leaves are elliptical, 5-12 cm long, and 3-5 cm wide, with a rounded or acute apex. The inflorescence is a terminal spike, bearing small, white or pinkish flowers with four petals, each 1-2 mm long.

This subspecies is endemic to the eastern part of Cuba, specifically in the Guantánamo Province.

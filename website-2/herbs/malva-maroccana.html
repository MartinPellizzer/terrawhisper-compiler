Malva maroccana

Malva maroccana, also known as Moroccan mallow or Malva plant, is a perennial plant species belonging to the family Malvaceae, characterized by its large, soft, and velvety leaves and showy flowers.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include soothing digestive issues, reducing inflammation, and acting as a natural diuretic. The bioactive constituents of Malva maroccana include flavonoids, anthocyanins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The plant is commonly used in herbal preparations such as infusions, syrups, and tinctures, often used to treat digestive disorders, skin conditions, and respiratory issues.

Casearia aquifolia

Casearia aquifolia, commonly known as the water casearia or water tree, is a plant species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of Casearia aquifolia include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Casearia aquifolia is often used to make herbal teas, infusions, and extracts, which are commonly used to alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and cold, as well as to reduce inflammation and promote relaxation.

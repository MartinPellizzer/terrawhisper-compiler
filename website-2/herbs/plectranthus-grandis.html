Plectranthus grandis

Plectranthus grandis, also known as Coleus, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa, belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

The bioactive constituents of Plectranthus grandis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Plectranthus grandis is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion, often used to relieve symptoms of colds, flu, and bronchitis.

Taraxacum alpestre

Taraxacum alpestre, also known as Alpine Dandelion, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family, closely related to the well-known Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale).

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and diuretic properties, making it a popular remedy for treating various conditions such as kidney stones, digestive issues, and skin problems. The bioactive constituents of Taraxacum alpestre include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to support liver health and promote detoxification in the body.

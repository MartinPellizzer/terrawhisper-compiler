Sida hermaphrodita

Sida hermaphrodita, also known as Country Mallow or Indian Mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in various cultures for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including the treatment of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Sida hermaphrodita include flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Sida hermaphrodita is often used in herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into traditional remedies like cough syrups and respiratory tonic preparations.

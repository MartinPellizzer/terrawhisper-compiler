Rhus latifolia

side effects

Rhus latifolia, also known as the broad-leaved poison ivy, contains urushiol, an oily sap that can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals. When the sap comes into contact with the skin, it can lead to redness, itching, and blistering, which can be painful and uncomfortable. In severe cases, exposure to the sap can cause anaphylaxis, a life-threatening allergic reaction that requires immediate medical attention. People who experience mild to moderate reactions can try treating the affected area with cool compresses, calamine lotion, or antihistamines to alleviate symptoms. However, it's essential to consult a medical professional for proper diagnosis and treatment if symptoms persist or worsen.

Vicia linearis

Vicia linearis, also known as the narrow-leaved vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and Western Asia, characterized by its linear leaves and pink or purple flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as fever, sore throats, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Vicia linearis include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Vicia linearis is often prepared as a tincture, infusion, or decoction, and is used to treat a range of ailments, although its effectiveness and safety have not been extensively scientifically studied.

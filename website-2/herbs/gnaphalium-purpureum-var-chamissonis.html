What is Gnaphalium purpureum var. chamissonis?

Gnaphalium purpureum var. chamissonis is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are linear or lance-shaped and arranged oppositely on the stem. The plant's flowers are small, purple, and arranged in a corymbiform inflorescence, producing numerous small achenes that are crowned by a five-lobed pappus. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-30 cm, with a spreading habit and a distribution that is primarily coastal in nature.

The leaves are 5-15 mm long and 0.5-1.5 mm wide, with a glabrous base and a hairy margin.

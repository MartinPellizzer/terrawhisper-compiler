Dipteryx odorata

Dipteryx odorata, commonly known as tonka bean or tonka tree, is a perennial plant native to the tropical forests of Central and South America.

The tonka bean has been traditionally used for its medicinal and fragrant properties, with health benefits including its potential to reduce anxiety and stress, improve sleep quality, and exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Dipteryx odorata include coumarins, particularly tonka bean coumarin, as well as other volatile compounds such as limonene and beta-pinene, which contribute to its distinctive aroma and therapeutic effects.

Tonka bean is often used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and essential oils, as well as in perfumery and flavoring applications due to its sweet, vanilla-like fragrance.

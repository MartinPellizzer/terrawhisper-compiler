Cimicifuga purpurea

Cimicifuga purpurea, also known as black cohosh, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America, belonging to the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae).

The plant is valued for its health benefits, which include alleviating menopausal symptoms such as hot flashes, mood swings, and sleep disturbances, as well as providing relief from menstrual cramps and anxiety. The bioactive constituents of black cohosh include triterpene glycosides, such as acteoside and 23-epi-26-deoxyacteoside, as well as isoflavones and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Black cohosh is commonly prepared as a dietary supplement, available in various forms including capsules, tablets, and tinctures, and is often used in combination with other herbal remedies to support women's health and well-being.

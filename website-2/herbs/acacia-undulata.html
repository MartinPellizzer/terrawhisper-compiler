Acacia undulata

Acacia undulata, also known as the undulating wattle or sweet thorn, is a thorny shrub or small tree native to the southern regions of Africa.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities, which may aid in the treatment of digestive issues, wounds, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Acacia undulata include flavonoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, as well as phenolic acids and condensed tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Acacia undulata include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of various gastrointestinal disorders.

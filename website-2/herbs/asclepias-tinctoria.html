Asclepias tinctoria

Asclepias tinctoria, also known as the yellow milkweed or butterfly milkweed, is a perennial plant native to North America, characterized by its clusters of small, yellow flowers and distinctive milky sap.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including treating fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Asclepias tinctoria include cardenolides, glycosides, and iridoid compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Asclepias tinctoria is often prepared as a tincture or infused in tea form, although its use is generally cautioned against due to the potential toxicity of its cardiac glycosides, and should only be used under the guidance of a qualified healthcare professional.

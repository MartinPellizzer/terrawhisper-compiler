Claytonia robusta

Claytonia robusta, also known as Mexican spring onion or more commonly as wild garlic or Claytonia, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Montiaceae, characterized by its edible leaves, flowers, and roots.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects, which can help to alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs. The bioactive constituents of Claytonia robusta include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, Claytonia robusta is commonly prepared as a tincture, tea, or syrup, and is often used to treat respiratory issues, sore throats, and digestive problems.

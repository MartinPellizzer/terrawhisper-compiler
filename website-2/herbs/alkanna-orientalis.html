Alkanna orientalis

Alkanna orientalis, also known as Oriental Alkanet, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Borage family (Boraginaceae).

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which make it beneficial for treating various health conditions, including pain, wounds, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Alkanna orientalis include alkannin and shikonin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is often used to make herbal preparations, such as tinctures, infusions, and creams, which are commonly used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and inflammatory disorders.

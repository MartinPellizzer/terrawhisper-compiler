Acanthus pubescens

Acanthus pubescens, also known as the soft-leaved bear's breech, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its distinctive, soft-hairy leaves and purple flowers.

The bioactive constituents of Acanthus pubescens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

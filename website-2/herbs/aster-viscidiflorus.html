Aster viscidiflorus

Aster viscidiflorus, also known as sticky aster, is a perennial plant species native to North America, characterized by its yellow flowers with dark centers and sticky or hairy leaves.

The health benefits of Aster viscidiflorus include its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various conditions such as wounds, skin irritations, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Aster viscidiflorus include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to support immune function and promote overall well-being.

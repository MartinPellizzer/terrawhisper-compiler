Myrtus aromatica

Myrtus aromatica, commonly known as the aromatic myrtle or West Indian bay, is a fragrant evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs. The bioactive constituents of Myrtus aromatica include essential oils, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Myrtus aromatica include teas, infusions, and essential oils, often used to relieve respiratory issues, as well as for their fragrance and flavor in perfumery and culinary applications.

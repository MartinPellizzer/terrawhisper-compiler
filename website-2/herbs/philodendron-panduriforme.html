Philodendron panduriforme

Philodendron panduriforme, also known as the snake skin philodendron, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arumaceae, native to Central and South America.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of Philodendron panduriforme include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is also used in the form of a topical cream or ointment to treat skin conditions and wounds.

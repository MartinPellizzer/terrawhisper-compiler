Epiphyllum russellianum

Epiphyllum russellianum, also known as the Mexican prickly pear cactus, is a type of epiphytic cactus native to Mexico, characterized by its climbing habit and covered in sharp, protective spines.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to reduce pain and inflammation, and protect against cell damage. The bioactive constituents of Epiphyllum russellianum include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make various herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat a range of health issues, including digestive problems, skin conditions, and respiratory issues.

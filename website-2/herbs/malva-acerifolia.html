Malva acerifolia

Malva acerifolia, also known as maple mallow, is a perennial plant species in the family Malvaceae, native to Europe and western Asia, characterized by its large, maple-like leaves and showy pink flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, offering potential health benefits in reducing swelling and pain, as well as protecting against oxidative stress and cell damage. The bioactive constituents of Malva acerifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Herbal preparations made from Malva acerifolia include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to soothe digestive issues, calm skin irritations, and support respiratory health.

Taraxacum leucanthum

Taraxacum leucanthum, also known as the white dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the Asteraceae family.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, and its roots, leaves, and flowers are used to treat various health issues, including digestive problems, inflammation, and skin conditions, due to its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Taraxacum leucanthum include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used in topical creams and salves, to harness its medicinal properties.

side effects

Taraxacum leucanthum, also known as the lesser dandelion, is generally considered safe for consumption. However, like any herb, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. The plant contains sesquiterpene lactones, which can cause gastrointestinal upset and interact with certain medications. Consuming large quantities of Taraxacum leucanthum may also lead to an increased risk of bleeding due to its effects on blood clotting. In rare cases, consuming the plant may cause liver damage in people with pre-existing liver conditions.

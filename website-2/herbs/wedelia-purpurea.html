Wedelia purpurea

Wedelia purpurea, also known as the purple wedelia or creeping signal grass, is a flowering plant species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which are said to aid in the treatment of fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Wedelia purpurea include flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Wedelia purpurea is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture to treat various health issues, and it is also used in topical applications to soothe skin irritations and wounds.

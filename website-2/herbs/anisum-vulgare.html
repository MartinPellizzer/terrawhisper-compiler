Anisum vulgare

Anisum vulgare, commonly known as common anise or anise, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its sweet, aromatic seeds and feathery foliage.

The health benefits of Anisum vulgare include its use as a carminative and expectorant, helping to alleviate digestive issues and relieve respiratory problems such as coughs and colds. The bioactive constituents of this plant include essential oils, particularly anethole, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Anise is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and extracts, often in combination with other herbs like peppermint and ginger to create digestive remedies and flavorings.

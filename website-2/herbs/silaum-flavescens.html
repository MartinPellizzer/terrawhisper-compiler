Silaum flavescens

Silaum flavescens, also known as Japanese angelica or silaum, is a type of plant that belongs to the parsley family (Apiaceae) and is native to East Asia.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, coumarins, and essential oils, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Silaum flavescens is commonly used in traditional Chinese and Japanese medicine, and is often prepared into teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being used in combination with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

Urochloa eruciformis

Urochloa eruciformis, also known as Crabgrass or Panicum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the world.

The plant is known for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in treating conditions such as fever, wounds, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Urochloa eruciformis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Urochloa eruciformis is often used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and extracts, which are commonly used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory problems.

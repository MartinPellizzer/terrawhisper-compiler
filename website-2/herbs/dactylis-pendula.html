Dactylis pendula

Dactylis pendula, also known as orchard grass or cock's foot, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and Western Asia, belonging to the family Poaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions due to its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate symptoms associated with respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Dactylis pendula include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Dactylis pendula is often used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as in combination with other herbs in formulas designed to support respiratory health and boost the immune system.

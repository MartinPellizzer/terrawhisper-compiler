Impatiens dichroa

Impatiens dichroa, also known as balsam or balsam pear, is a plant species native to tropical Asia, characterized by its distinctive, edible fruits and medicinal properties.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and antispasmodic properties. The bioactive constituents of Impatiens dichroa include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is also used to make balsam of Peru, a topical preparation used to treat skin conditions and wounds.

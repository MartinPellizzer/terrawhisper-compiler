Drimia duthieae

Drimia duthieae, also known as Duthie's drimia, is a type of plant in the Asparagaceae family, characterized by its underground bulb and distinctive flowering habit.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, cough, and respiratory issues, due to its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Drimia duthieae include alkaloids, saponins, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Drimia duthieae is commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is also used in the form of powder or extract in various herbal preparations, including cough syrups and expectorants.

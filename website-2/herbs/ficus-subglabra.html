Ficus subglabra

Ficus subglabra, also known as the fig leaf or small-leaved fig, is a species of fig tree native to tropical Asia.

This plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine and is valued for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving digestion, as well as its ability to act as an antispasmodic and antiseptic agent. The bioactive constituents of Ficus subglabra include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Ficus subglabra include decoctions, infusions, and powders, which are often used to treat various health conditions such as fever, cough, and respiratory issues.

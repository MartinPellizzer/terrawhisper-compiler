What is Salacia madagascariensis subsp. dentata?

Salacia madagascariensis subsp.

dentata is a plant species belonging to the family Celastraceae. It is a woody climber with a height of up to 10 meters, featuring a glabrous stem and branches with a greyish-brown color.

The leaves are elliptical, measuring 7-15 cm in length and 3-6 cm in width, with a pointed apex and entire margin, and are arranged oppositely along the stem. The inflorescence is a terminal corymb, consisting of 3-6 flowers, with a white and greenish color, and is dioecious, meaning male and female flowers are produced on separate plants.

The fruits are drupes, measuring 1-2 cm in diameter, with a single seed.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Salacia madagascariensis subsp.

dentata are Salacia infusion, Salacia decoction, Salacia tea, tincture, and Salacia extract, which are derived from the root bark and stem of the plant, primarily used in traditional medicine to lower blood sugar levels and manage diabetes due to its bioactive compounds, including salacin, which has been shown to inhibit alpha-glucosidase and alpha-amylase enzymes, thereby slowing down carbohydrate digestion and absorption, reducing postprandial glucose spikes. Salacia infusion is a liquid extract prepared by steeping the root bark in hot water, while Salacia decoction is a longer steeping process, typically used to extract more bioactive compounds.

Salacia tea is a more diluted form of Salacia infusion, often consumed as a beverage. Tincture is a concentrated liquid extract prepared using a solvent such as ethanol, which can be added to water or taken sublingually.

Salacia extract is a standardized powder or liquid preparation, often used in supplements and formulations, containing a specified amount of salacin and other bioactive compounds.

The possible side effects of using Salacia madagascariensis subsp.

Additionally, improper use of Salacia madagascariensis subsp. dentata may cause gastrointestinal issues, such as diarrhea, due to its potential to increase gut motility and fluid secretion.

Furthermore, allergic reactions, including skin rashes, itching, and swelling, may occur in some individuals who are sensitive to the plant's compounds. Ingestion of excessive amounts of Salacia madagascariensis subsp. dentata can also induce vomiting and nausea, likely due to its irritant properties on the gastrointestinal tract. These adverse reactions emphasize the importance of proper dosing and caution when using Salacia madagascariensis subsp.

dentata, especially for individuals with pre-existing medical conditions or those taking prescription medications.

Eugenia fruticosa

Eugenia fruticosa, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or guanabani, is a small, deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

The health benefits of Eugenia fruticosa include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as fever, cough, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Eugenia fruticosa is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or syrup, and is also used to make jams, jellies, and other food products.

Lindera nervosa

Lindera nervosa, also known as Japanese spice tree or Kamitsuge, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia, belonging to the Lauraceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antispasmodic properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Lindera nervosa include alkaloids, phenolic compounds, and sesquiterpenes, such as linderine and linderaline, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Lindera nervosa is often prepared as a decoction or tincture, and is commonly used to treat digestive disorders, as well as to warm and dispel cold and dampness from the body.

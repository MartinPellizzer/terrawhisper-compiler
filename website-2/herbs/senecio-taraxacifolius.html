Senecio taraxacifolius

Senecio taraxacifolius, also known as the dandelion hawk's beard or dandelion ragwort, is a flowering plant species in the sunflower family (Asteraceae) native to South Africa.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including treating liver and kidney disorders, as well as its ability to aid in digestion and reduce inflammation. The bioactive constituents of Senecio taraxacifolius include sesquiterpene lactones, such as taraxasterol and isotorachrysone, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

Senecio taraxacifolius is commonly used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, although it is essential to note that some parts of the plant may be toxic due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids.

side effects

Senecio taraxacifolius, also known as dandelion bush or dandelion-leaved ragwort, contains toxic compounds called pyrrolizidine alkaloids (PA) similar to those found in other Senecio species. These compounds can cause liver damage and other health problems in humans and animals if ingested. The consumption of large quantities of the plant can lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Prolonged exposure to these toxic compounds may also result in liver fibrosis and cirrhosis. It is essential to exercise caution and avoid consuming Senecio taraxacifolius or any other plant with similar toxic compounds.

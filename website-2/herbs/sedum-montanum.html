Sedum montanum

Sedum montanum, also known as Mountain Sedum, is a succulent plant species native to the mountainous regions of China, characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and stems.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which may aid in treating conditions like arthritis and promoting overall well-being. The bioactive constituents of Sedum montanum include triterpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Traditionally, Sedum montanum is used in herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and extracts, which are commonly used to treat various ailments and support immune function.

Bomarea latifolia

Bomarea latifolia, commonly known as the yellow or yellow-winged Bomarea, is a flowering plant species in the family Alstroemeriaceae, native to Central and South America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as wounds, infections, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Bomarea latifolia include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is prepared as a decoction or tincture, and is often used in herbal medicine to treat a range of ailments, although further research is needed to confirm its efficacy and safety.

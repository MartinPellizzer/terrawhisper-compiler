Ferula lutea

Ferula lutea, also known as yellow gum Ferula, is a perennial plant species in the carrot family (Apiaceae) native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

The health benefits of Ferula lutea include its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which can help alleviate pain, reduce inflammation, and combat infections. The bioactive constituents of this plant include ferulic acid, sesquiterpenes, and coumarins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Ferula lutea include essential oils, tinctures, and infusions, often used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and respiratory problems.

classification

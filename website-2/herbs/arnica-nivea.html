Arnica nivea

Arnica nivea, also known as White Arnica or Mountain Arnica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

The plant is prized for its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties, making it a popular remedy for sprains, bruises, and other injuries. The bioactive constituents of Arnica nivea include flavonoids, sesquiterpene lactones, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Arnica nivea include creams, ointments, and tinctures, which are often used topically to reduce pain and inflammation, promote healing, and improve skin health.

What is Biscutella laevigata subsp. longifolia?

Biscutella laevigata subsp.

longifolia is a sub-species of Biscutella laevigata, a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Brassicaceae. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems, which can reach up to 30-60 cm in height, and its pinnate leaves, divided into 5-9 leaflets, which are 2-5 cm long.

The plant produces white or pinkish flowers, arranged in a raceme, with 4-6 petals, and measures 4-6 mm in diameter. The inflorescence is 5-10 cm long, and is composed of 10-30 flowers.

The plant's fruit is a silique, 4-6 mm long, containing 2-4 seeds, 2-3 mm in length.

What is Chenopodium melanocarpum f. leucocarpum?

Chenopodium melanocarpum f. leucocarpum is a taxonomic variation of the species Chenopodium melanocarpum, belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its annual habit, herbaceous growth, and a glaucous to green coloration on the stem and leaves. The plant displays a rosette-like leaf arrangement, with lanceolate leaves that are 2-10 cm long and 0.5-2.5 cm wide, and a leaf margin that is ciliate or serrate. Chenopodium melanocarpum f. leucocarpum produces small, yellow or greenish flowers in axillary spikes or panicles, which are 1-10 cm long, and are followed by achenes that are 1-2 mm long.

The plant typically grows in dry or saline environments, often in areas with full sun exposure and well-drained soils.

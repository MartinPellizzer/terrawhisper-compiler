Hieracium dentifolium

Hieracium dentifolium, also known as common hawk's-beard, is a perennial plant species in the dandelion family (Asteraceae) characterized by its yellow flowers and toothed leaves.

The bioactive constituents of Hieracium dentifolium include sesquiterpene lactones, such as lactucin and lactucopicrin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Hieracium dentifolium is often prepared as a tincture or dried herb, and is used to treat a range of conditions, including digestive issues, fever, and respiratory problems.

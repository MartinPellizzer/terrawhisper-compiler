Cnicus baeticus

Cnicus baeticus, also known as Thistle of the Baetic mountains, is a thistle species native to the Iberian Peninsula, characterized by its prickly stems and purple flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in the treatment of various ailments such as digestive issues and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Cnicus baeticus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Cnicus baeticus is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into creams and ointments for topical applications.

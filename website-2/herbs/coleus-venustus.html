Coleus venustus

The Coleus venustus, also known as the Wild Coleus or Coleus, is a perennial plant species native to tropical Africa, characterized by its vibrant foliage and colorful flowers.

The leaves of Coleus venustus have been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and antibacterial properties. The bioactive constituents of Coleus venustus include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Coleus venustus is often prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, which are used to treat various health conditions, including fever, coughs, and skin infections.

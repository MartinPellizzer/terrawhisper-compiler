What is Erica radicans subsp. schlechteri?

Erica radicans subsp.

schlechteri is a subspecies of Erica radicans, belonging to the family Ericaceae. This evergreen shrub grows up to 50 cm in height, with a narrow, linear leaf shape, 2-4 mm long, and a glabrous or puberulous lower leaf surface.

The inflorescence is a raceme with 2-4 flowers, which are bell-shaped, 5-6 mm long, and white or pale pink in color, with a 5-lobed corolla. The plant produces 4-6 mm long, brown or purple-brown capsules, containing 4-6 seeds, each 1-2 mm long.

It is endemic to the southwestern coast of South Africa, where it grows in sandy or stony soil, often in coastal scrub or fynbos.

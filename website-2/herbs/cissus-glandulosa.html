Cissus glandulosa

Cissus glandulosa, also known as the African ivy, is a plant species within the Vitaceae family, characterized by its woody vine-like growth habit.

Bioactive constituents of Cissus glandulosa include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Cissus glandulosa is often used in traditional medicine and is commonly prepared into herbal teas, capsules, and extracts, which are utilized to support joint health and alleviate symptoms associated with arthritis.

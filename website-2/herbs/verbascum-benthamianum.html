Verbascum benthamianum

Verbascum benthamianum, also known as tall mullein, is a biennial plant species within the mullein genus, characterized by its large leaves and tall spikes of yellow flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and expectorant properties, which may help alleviate respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs. The bioactive constituents of Verbascum benthamianum include saponins, flavonoids, and iridoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a decoction or tincture, and it is also used in infusions, syrups, and ointments to treat various health conditions.

Deprea subtriflora

Deprea subtriflora, also known as the desert snakeroot, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Solanaceae family.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including the treatment of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Deprea subtriflora include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include teas and infusions, as well as topical creams and ointments for the treatment of skin conditions.

classification

Deprea subtriflora is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae. It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The classification of Deprea subtriflora includes its kingdom as Plantae and its order as Solanales. At the family level, it belongs to Solanaceae and, at the genus level, it belongs to Deprea. Deprea subtriflora is further classified at the species level, making it a unique and distinct species within the Deprea genus.

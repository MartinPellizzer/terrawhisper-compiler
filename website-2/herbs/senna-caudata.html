Senna caudata

Senna caudata, also known as Cape senna, is a plant species within the legume family (Fabaceae) that is native to southern Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including the treatment of constipation, diarrhea, and other gastrointestinal issues due to its anthraquinone glycosides, which act as a laxative. The bioactive constituents of Senna caudata include anthraquinone glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Senna caudata is often made into tea infusions, extracts, or capsules, making it a common herbal preparation used for its laxative and gastrointestinal benefits.

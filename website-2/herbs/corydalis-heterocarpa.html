Corydalis heterocarpa

Corydalis heterocarpa, a perennial plant native to Asia, is a member of the poppy family (Papaveraceae) and is also known as Himalayan corydalis.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential remedy for relieving pain and reducing inflammation. The bioactive constituents of Corydalis heterocarpa include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal effects.

In herbal medicine, Corydalis heterocarpa is commonly prepared as a tincture, capsule, or tea, and is often used to treat conditions such as arthritis, fibromyalgia, and menstrual cramps.

Glyceria maxima

Glyceria maxima, also known as the tall manna grass or great sweet-grass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa, commonly found in wetlands, rivers, and lakeshores.

The bioactive constituents of Glyceria maxima include glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

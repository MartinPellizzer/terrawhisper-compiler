Bubon pyrenaicum

Bubon pyrenaicum, also known as Pyrenean knapweed or Bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used for its health benefits, including its ability to reduce inflammation, improve liver function, and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression. The bioactive constituents of Bupleurum include alkaloids, glycosides, and triterpenoids, including saikosaponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine and Western herbalism, Bupleurum is commonly used to make teas, tinctures, and capsules, often in combination with other herbs such as licorice and schisandra.

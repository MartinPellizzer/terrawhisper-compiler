Polygonum asiaticum

Polygonum asiaticum, also known as Asian knotweed or Mexican tea, is a perennial plant species that belongs to the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae).

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which can help to treat conditions such as fever, diarrhea, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Polygonum asiaticum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Polygonum asiaticum is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health conditions, and it is also used in combination with other herbs in traditional remedies.

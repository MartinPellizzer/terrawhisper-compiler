Tephrosia purpurea

Tephrosia purpurea, also known as Heartleaf or Horsefly, is a perennial plant species in the legume family that is native to tropical Africa.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, making it useful for treating various health conditions such as fever, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Tephrosia purpurea include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional African medicine, the plant is often made into herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and decoctions, and is also used in the form of tinctures and capsules.

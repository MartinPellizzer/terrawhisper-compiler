Myrcia gigantea

Myrcia gigantea, also known as the 'erva-preta', is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to the tropical regions of South America.

The bioactive constituents of Myrcia gigantea include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds, such as myricetin, quercetin, and ferulic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Myrcia gigantea is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine and is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or extract, as well as being incorporated into medicinal creams and ointments.

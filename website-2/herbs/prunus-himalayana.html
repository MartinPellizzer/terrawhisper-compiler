Prunus himalayana

Prunus himalayana, also known as Himalayan cherry or Himalayan apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan region.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, diarrhea, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Prunus himalayana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Prunus himalayana is commonly prepared into decoctions, infusions, and extracts, often used to treat digestive issues, fever, and respiratory problems.

Ficus kallicarpa

Ficus kallicarpa, also known as the 'purple leaf fig', is a plant species within the fig family (Moraceae) native to Southeast Asia.

The health benefits of Ficus kallicarpa include its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in the treatment of various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Ficus kallicarpa include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ficus kallicarpa is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory infections.

Baobabus digitata

The Baobabus digitata, also known as the African Baobab tree, is a deciduous tree native to sub-Saharan Africa, characterized by its distinctive trunk and large, egg-shaped leaves.

The health benefits of Baobab tree include its ability to support digestive health, reduce inflammation, and act as an antioxidant, making it a popular ingredient in traditional medicine. The bioactive constituents of Baobab tree include alkaloids, flavonoids, and polyphenols, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Baobab tree include powder, capsules, and tinctures, which are often used to support immune function, reduce oxidative stress, and promote overall well-being.

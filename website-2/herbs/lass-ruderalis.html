Lass ruderalis

Lavandula stoechas, commonly known as Lass Ruderalis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, known for its distinctive, purple-tipped flowers and pungent, camphor-like aroma.

The plant has been traditionally used to alleviate anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness, due to its calming and sedative properties, as well as its ability to ease digestive issues and promote relaxation. The bioactive constituents of Lass Ruderalis include camphor, borneol, and linalool, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Lass Ruderalis is made into teas, infusions, and essential oils, with the latter being commonly used in aromatherapy and skincare products.

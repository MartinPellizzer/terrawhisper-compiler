Anemone bracteata

Anemone bracteata, also known as windflower or pasque flower, is a perennial plant native to North America, characterized by its delicate white or pink flowers and fern-like foliage.

The bioactive constituents of Anemone bracteata include alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Astragalus nevadensis

Astragalus nevadensis, also known as Nevada milkvetch, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its adaptogenic and immunomodulatory properties, with reported health benefits including reduced inflammation, improved cardiovascular health, and enhanced immune function. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus nevadensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

Astragalus nevadensis is commonly used in herbal preparations, such as capsules, teas, and tinctures, often in combination with other herbs to support immune system function and overall well-being.

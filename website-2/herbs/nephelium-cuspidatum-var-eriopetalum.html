What is Nephelium cuspidatum var. eriopetalum?

Nephelium cuspidatum var. eriopetalum is a plant species belonging to the family Sapindaceae.

It is a type of rambutan, characterized by its hairy, green, and oval-shaped leaves, measuring 1-5 cm in length and 0.5-2.5 cm in width. The leaves have a pointed tip and are oppositely arranged on the stem. The plant produces small, white, and fragrant flowers in clusters, which are followed by red or yellowish fruit, each about 2-3 cm in diameter.

The fruit has a hairy, waxy, and edible pulp with one or two seeds, similar to other rambutan species.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Nephelium cuspidatum var. eriopetalum are tea, tincture, infusion, rambutan tea, and decoction.

Tea is made by steeping the fruit or leaves in hot water, releasing flavonoids and phenolic acids. A tincture is a concentrated extract of the plant made by soaking it in a solvent, typically ethanol, to produce a liquid remedy. An infusion is similar to tea but uses cold water, often used for its antioxidant properties.

Rambutan tea and decoction are also used, with decoction involving boiling the plant material to release its active compounds.

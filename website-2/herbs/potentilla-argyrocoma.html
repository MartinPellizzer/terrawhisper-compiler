Potentilla argyrocoma

Potentilla argyrocoma, also known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the rose family (Rosaceae).

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including digestive issues, skin problems, and respiratory infections, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Potentilla argyrocoma include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoid saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made from Potentilla argyrocoma include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to promote wound healing, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders.

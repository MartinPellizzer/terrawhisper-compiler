Anthyllis fennica

Anthyllis fennica, also known as Fenugreek Anthyllis or Fenugreek Fennel-leaved Kidney Vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe, characterized by its yellow flowers and pink to purple markings.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including its ability to lower blood sugar levels, improve digestion, and support the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Anthyllis fennica include saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which may contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Anthyllis fennica is often made into teas, infusions, or tinctures, and is also used in combination with other herbs to support women's health and alleviate symptoms of menopause.

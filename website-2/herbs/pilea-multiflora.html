Pilea multiflora

Pilea multiflora, also known as the 'Alpine Cabbage', is a perennial herb belonging to the Urticaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antispasmodic properties, which can aid in relieving symptoms of arthritis, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Pilea multiflora include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Pilea multiflora is often made into teas, infusions, or tinctures, as well as being used in traditional remedies for its purported benefits in treating fever, cough, and skin conditions.

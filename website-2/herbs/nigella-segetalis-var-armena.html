What is Nigella segetalis var. armena?

Nigella segetalis var. armena is a plant variety within the species Nigella segetalis, belonging to the family Ranunculaceae.

It is an annual or biennial herbaceous plant, characterized by glabrous stems, 20-40 cm in height, and pinnate leaves with 3-5 leaflets, 1-5 cm long, and linear to lanceolate in shape. The inflorescence consists of blue or purple flowers, 1-2 cm in diameter, with 5-6 sepals, 5 petals, and 6 stamens. The fruit is a schizocarp, composed of 3-5 follicles, each containing numerous seeds.

The plant is native to the Caucasus region and Armenia, where it grows in open places and along roads.

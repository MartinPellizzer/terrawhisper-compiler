Catappa bentzoe

Catappa bentzoe, also known as Catechu or Kattha, is a plant-based substance derived from the heartwood of the Acacia catechu tree, primarily native to the Indian subcontinent.

The health benefits of Catappa bentzoe include its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as gum infections, digestive issues, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Catappa bentzoe include catechin, epicatechin, and gallic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Ayurvedic and Unani medicine, Catappa bentzoe is commonly prepared as a decoction or a powder, often used in oral health care products, such as toothpaste and mouthwashes, as well as in herbal teas and supplements.

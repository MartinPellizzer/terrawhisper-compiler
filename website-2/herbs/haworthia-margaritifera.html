Haworthia margaritifera

Haworthia margaritifera, also known as the Star of the Veld, is a succulent plant species native to South Africa, characterized by its unique star-shaped rosette of fleshy, spotted leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which may aid in wound healing and the treatment of various infections. Bioactive constituents of Haworthia margaritifera include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and ascorbic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Haworthia margaritifera is often used to make infusions, decoctions, and ointments, with the most common preparations being a tea or a topical cream to promote skin health and wound healing.

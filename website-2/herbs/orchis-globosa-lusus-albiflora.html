Orchis globosa lusus albiflora

Orchis globosa lusus albiflora is a rare, white-flowering variant of the wild orchid species, Orchis globosa, characterized by its striking white flowers and distinctive growth habit.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may contribute to its traditional use in treating conditions such as digestive issues and respiratory infections. Bioactive constituents of Orchis globosa include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are thought to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Orchis globosa is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are said to promote digestive health and relieve respiratory distress.

classification

Orchis globosa lusus albiflora is a variant of the Orchis globosa species, and it falls under the category of orchids. It belongs to the family Orchidaceae, commonly known as the orchid family. Within this family, Orchis globosa lusus albiflora can be classified under the subfamily Orchidoideae. This subfamily encompasses the orchids with a single anther and a distinctive shape of the labellum. At the genus and species level, it is classified as a variant of Orchis globosa, but a more specific classification is not widely documented.

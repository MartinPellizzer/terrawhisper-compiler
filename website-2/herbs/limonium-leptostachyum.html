Limonium leptostachyum

Limonium leptostachyum, also known as sea lavender, is a perennial plant species native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its delicate purple flowers and slender stems.

The health benefits of Limonium leptostachyum include its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, particularly limonene and beta-pinene, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Herbal preparations made from Limonium leptostachyum include infusions, decoctions, and essential oil extracts, which are used in traditional medicine to promote wound healing, reduce inflammation, and soothe respiratory complaints.

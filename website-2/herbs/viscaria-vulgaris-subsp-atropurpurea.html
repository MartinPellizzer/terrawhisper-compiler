What is Viscaria vulgaris subsp. atropurpurea?

Viscaria vulgaris subsp.

atropurpurea is a taxonomic subspecies of the plant Viscaria vulgaris, belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae. It is characterized by its perennial growth habit, taproot, and stems that are usually unbranched, hairy, and 10-50 cm tall.

The leaves are elliptical to lanceolate, 1.5-6 cm long, and 0.5-2 cm wide, with a distinct petiole and a pointed tip. The flowers are purple, 1-2 cm in diameter, and have five petals, arranged in a corymboid inflorescence.

The fruits are small, ovoid capsules, containing numerous seeds and attached to the persistent calyx.

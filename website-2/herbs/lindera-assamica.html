Lindera assamica

Lindera assamica, also known as Assam spice or Indian bay leaf, is a plant species belonging to the Lauraceae family, native to the eastern Himalayas and parts of India.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, including its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, which contribute to its potential health benefits in treating digestive issues, fever, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Lindera assamica include alkaloids, phenolic compounds, and essential oils, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as teas, decoctions, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into traditional Indian and Chinese medicines.

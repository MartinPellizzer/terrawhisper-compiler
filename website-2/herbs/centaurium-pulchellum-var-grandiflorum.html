What is Centaurium pulchellum var. grandiflorum?

Centaurium pulchellum var. grandiflorum is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Gentianaceae.

It has a height of 10-60 cm, with a short, stout stem that is often prostrate or ascending, and a corymbose inflorescence. The leaves are opposite, simple, and entire, measuring 1-2.5 cm in length, with a lanceolate or linear shape and a ciliate margin. The flowers are blue to purple, with a tubular calyx and a corolla that is bilabiate, measuring 6-10 mm in length, with a four-lobed upper lip and a three-lobed lower lip.

The plant has a diploid chromosome number of 2n=16.

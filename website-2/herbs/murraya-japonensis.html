Murraya japonensis

Murraya japonensis, also known as Chinese boxwood or Japanese box, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to China and Southeast Asia, belonging to the Rutaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Murraya japonensis include limonoids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a tea, capsule, or tincture, and is also used to make a topical cream or ointment for skin conditions and wounds.

Anisophyllum ipecacuanha

Anisophyllum ipecacuanha, also known as Ipecacuanha or Ipecac, is a perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

Traditionally, the roots of this plant have been used in herbal medicine for their emetic and expectorant properties, making it useful in treating respiratory issues such as bronchitis, asthma, and coughs, as well as its potential application in treating digestive problems and nausea. The bioactive constituents of Anisophyllum ipecacuanha include alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins, with emetine and cephaeline being the primary alkaloids responsible for its medicinal properties.

Ipecacuanha is commonly prepared in herbal form as a tincture, fluid extract, or powder, often used to treat respiratory and digestive issues in traditional medicine.

Astragalus bakeri

Astragalus bakeri, a perennial legume native to the western United States, is a member of the Astragalus genus, commonly known as milkvetch.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory properties, with reported health benefits including immune system enhancement, improved cardiovascular health, and antioxidant effects. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus bakeri include flavonoids, saponins, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Astragalus bakeri is often used to make tea, capsules, and tinctures, which are among the most common herbal preparations made from this plant, although its use is not as widespread as other Astragalus species.

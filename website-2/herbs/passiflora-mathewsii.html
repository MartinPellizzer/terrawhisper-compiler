Passiflora mathewsii

Passiflora mathewsii, also known as the yellow granadilla, is a species of passionflower native to Central and South America.

It has been traditionally used for its sedative and anxiolytic properties, providing relief from anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness due to its calming effects on the nervous system. The bioactive constituents of Passiflora mathewsii include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, such as flavonoids like vitexin and orientin, and alkaloids like harman and harmine, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Passiflora mathewsii is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, capsules, and extracts, which can be consumed to promote relaxation and sleep quality.

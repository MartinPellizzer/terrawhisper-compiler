Rubia laurae

Rubia laurae, also known as Laura's madder, is a plant species belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal and therapeutic properties, with reported health benefits including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, which may help to alleviate symptoms of various conditions such as arthritis, skin infections, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Rubia laurae include anthraquinone glycosides, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and gastrointestinal disorders.

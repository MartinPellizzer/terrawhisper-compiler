Aechmea corallina

Aechmea corallina, also known as the Coral Tillandsia, is a type of epiphytic bromeliad plant that is native to tropical regions of South America.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its ability to reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms associated with respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, due to its expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Aechmea corallina include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Aechmea corallina is often used to make teas, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat respiratory issues, as well as being used in traditional folk medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Philoxerus brasiliensis

Philoxerus brasiliensis, also known as Brazilian Philoxerus, is a plant species that belongs to the family Amaranthaceae.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, respiratory issues, and gastrointestinal problems, due to its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Philoxerus brasiliensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are infusions and teas, as well as extracts that can be used in the form of capsules or tinctures.

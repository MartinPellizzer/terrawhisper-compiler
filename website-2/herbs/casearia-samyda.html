Casearia samyda

Casearia samyda, also known as the casearia tree, is a plant species native to tropical regions of Central and South America, characterized by its medicinal properties and uses.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids, such as samydin, samydone, and samydone A, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Casearia samyda include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including hypertension, fever, and respiratory infections.

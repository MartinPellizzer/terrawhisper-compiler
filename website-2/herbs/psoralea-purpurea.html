Psoralea purpurea

Psoralea purpurea, also known as purple Indian pea, is a perennial legume native to North America, valued for its medicinal and adaptogenic properties.

The health benefits of Psoralea purpurea include its ability to enhance skin health, improve immune function, and exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. The bioactive constituents of this plant include isoflavones, coumarins, and alkaloids, which have been shown to possess a range of biological activities.

The most common herbal preparations made with Psoralea purpurea include teas, infusions, and capsules, which are traditionally used to promote overall well-being and treat various health conditions.

classification

Psoralea purpurea is a species of flowering plant in the pea family (Fabaceae). It is native to eastern North America, from Canada to the United States. This plant is also known as purple crowfoot or purple lupine, due to its purple flowers. The classification of Psoralea purpurea includes its genus, which is Psoralea, and its family, which is Fabaceae. As a member of the Fabaceae family, Psoralea purpurea is part of the larger group of legumes.

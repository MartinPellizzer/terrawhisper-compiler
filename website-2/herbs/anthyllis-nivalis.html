Anthyllis nivalis

Anthyllis nivalis, also known as Snow Anthyllis or Alpegharbe, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly in the Mediterranean region.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects, which may help soothe wounds, reduce fever, and alleviate digestive issues. Bioactive constituents of Anthyllis nivalis include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to treat various health conditions, including respiratory issues, digestive problems, and skin conditions.

Colchicum psammophilum

Colchicum psammophilum, also known as the desert autumn crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Colchicaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, particularly in Turkey and the Middle East, to treat a range of health issues, including inflammation and pain relief, due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The bioactive constituents of Colchicum psammophilum include colchicine, a plant alkaloid with potential anti-inflammatory and antineoplastic activity, as well as other glycosides and alkaloids.

The most common herbal preparations made with Colchicum psammophilum include infusions, decoctions, and poultices, often used topically to treat local inflammation and pain.

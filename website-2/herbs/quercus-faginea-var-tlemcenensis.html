What is Quercus faginea var. tlemcenensis?

Quercus faginea var. tlemcenensis is a variety of the Mediterranean oak tree, belonging to the family Fagaceae.

It is characterized by its deciduous, evergreen, and sometimes semi-evergreen habits, and its elliptical to obovate leaves that are 4-10 cm long, with 7-11 lobes, and a leathery texture. The leaves are dark green on the upper surface and light green on the lower surface, with a glabrous to pubescent underside. The tree produces acorns that are 2-4 cm long, with a cup 0.5-1.5 cm deep, and a brown, glabrous pericarp.

It is endemic to Algeria, specifically in the Tlemcen region, and is a component of the Mediterranean oak woodlands.

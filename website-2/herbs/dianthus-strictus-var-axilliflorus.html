What is Dianthus strictus var. axilliflorus?

Dianthus strictus var. axilliflorus is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It has a taproot and a stem that grows 10-30 cm tall, branching from the base, with a glabrous to pubescent pubescence. The leaves are linear-lanceolate to lanceolate, 3-10 cm long, and 5-10 mm wide, with a ciliate margin and a petiole 1-5 mm long. The inflorescence is a terminal corymb, with axillary flowers, bearing pink to white, fragrant, and bilabiate flowers, 10-15 mm in diameter, with 5 sepals and 5 petals.

The flowers are fertile, and the fruit is a schizocarp with 5 mericarps, each 2-3 mm long and 1.5-2 mm wide.

Lomatium nudicaule

Lomatium nudicaule, also known as Lomatium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northwestern United States and southwestern Canada, belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of colds, flu, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Lomatium include alkaloids, sesquiterpenes, and volatile oils, particularly bergapten and umbelliferone, which have been found to exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant activities.

The most common herbal preparations made with Lomatium include teas, tinctures, and capsules, which are often used to support immune function and reduce inflammation in the body.

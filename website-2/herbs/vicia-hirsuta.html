Vicia hirsuta

Vicia hirsuta, also known as hairy vetch or hairy tare, is a perennial climbing legume native to Europe and Western Asia.

The plant is valued for its health benefits, including its ability to act as a natural diuretic, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial properties. Bioactive constituents of Vicia hirsuta include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Vicia hirsuta is commonly prepared as an infusion or decoction to treat various conditions, including urinary tract infections and skin irritations.

classification

Vicia hirsuta is classified as a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae. It belongs to the genus Vicia, which includes over 150 species of vetch. The genus Vicia is further classified under the subfamily Faboideae. Within the Faboideae subfamily, Vicia hirsuta is a member of the tribe Fabeae. This classification places Vicia hirsuta within the broader context of the pea family, Fabaceae.

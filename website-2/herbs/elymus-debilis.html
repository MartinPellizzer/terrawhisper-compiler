Elymus debilis

Elymus debilis, also known as weak wheatgrass or slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and Canada.

It has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating various health issues such as inflammation, fever, and respiratory problems, due to its ability to soothe and calm the body. The bioactive constituents of Elymus debilis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Elymus debilis is often used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being combined with other herbs to create syrups and salves for topical use.

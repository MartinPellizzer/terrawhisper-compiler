Benkara armigera

Benkara armigera, also known as Benkara, is a plant species belonging to the family Rubiaceae, native to the tropical regions of Asia.

The bioactive constituents of Benkara armigera include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Benkara is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or powder, and is used to treat coughs, colds, and sore throats, as well as to reduce fever and alleviate respiratory problems.

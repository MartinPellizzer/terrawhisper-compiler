Viburnum alabamense

Viburnum alabamense, also known as Alabama spicebush or Alabama cranberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers and red berries.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it useful in treating a range of ailments from wounds and infections to fever and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Viburnum alabamense include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat digestive issues, skin problems, and respiratory conditions.

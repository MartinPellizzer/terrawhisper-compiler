Phalaris paniculata

Phalaris paniculata, also known as canary grass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region, belonging to the family Poaceae.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including treating digestive issues, reducing inflammation, and supporting liver function. The bioactive constituents of Phalaris paniculata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome.

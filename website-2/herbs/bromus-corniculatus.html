Bromus corniculatus

Bromus corniculatus, commonly known as cheatgrass, is an annual grass native to Europe and Asia, now widely distributed throughout the world.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, which may aid in reducing pain and inflammation. The bioactive constituents of cheatgrass include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, cheatgrass is often prepared as a tincture or infused in teas, and is also used in combination with other herbs to create topical creams and salves for skin issues and wound healing.

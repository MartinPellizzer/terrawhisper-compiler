Nepeta pusilla

Nepeta pusilla, also known as dwarf catmint, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and Asia, belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties, making it useful in treating various conditions such as digestive issues and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Nepeta pusilla include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and volatile oils, including nepetalactone, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Nepeta pusilla is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as in topical creams and ointments for skin conditions and wound healing.

Alpinia blumei

Alpinia blumei, also known as lesser galangal, is a type of rhizomatous perennial plant belonging to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of colds, fever, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Alpinia blumei include gingerols, shogaols, and other volatile oils, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Alpinia blumei is commonly prepared as a decoction or tincture, and is often used in combination with other herbs to make remedies for colds, flu, and respiratory issues.

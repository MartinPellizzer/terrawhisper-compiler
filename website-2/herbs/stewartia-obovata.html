Stewartia obovata

Stewartia obovata, also known as Japanese stewartia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia, characterized by its showy white flowers and attractive bark.

Traditionally, Stewartia obovata has been used in herbal medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, sore throat, and respiratory infections, due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Stewartia obovata include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Stewartia obovata is often prepared as a decoction or infused as a tea, and is also used to make tinctures, granules, and other herbal preparations.

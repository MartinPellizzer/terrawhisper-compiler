What is Lathyrus laevigatus subsp. transsylvanicus?

Lathyrus laevigatus subsp.

transsylvanicus is a botanical subspecies belonging to the family Fabaceae. It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its erect stems, 5-10 cm tall, bearing 2-4 pairs of leaflets and a terminal raceme of purple flowers with a diameter of 15-20 mm.

The leaves are trifoliate, with leaflets 5-10 mm long, and have a glabrous (smooth) surface. Lathyrus laevigatus subsp. transsylvanicus is distinguished from other subspecies by its glabrous stems and leaves, and its distribution is primarily limited to the Transylvanian region of Romania.

Its flowers bloom from June to August, and the plant reproduces via seed.

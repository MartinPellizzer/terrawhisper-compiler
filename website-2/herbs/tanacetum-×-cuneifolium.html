Tanacetum × cuneifolium

Tanacetum × cuneifolium, also known as Tansy, is a perennial herb belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its fern-like foliage and yellow, button-like flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and antiseptic properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, menstrual cramps, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Tanacetum × cuneifolium include sesquiterpene lactones, such as thujane and santamarine, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Tanacetum × cuneifolium is commonly prepared as a tincture, tea, or infused oil, and is used to treat a range of ailments, including indigestion, fever, and skin conditions.

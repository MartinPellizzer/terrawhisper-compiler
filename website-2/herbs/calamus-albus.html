Calamus albus

Calamus albus, also known as Acorus calamus, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Acoraceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

The bioactive constituents of Calamus albus include sesquiterpenes, phenolic compounds, and volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

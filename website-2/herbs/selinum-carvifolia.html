Selinum carvifolia

Selinum carvifolia, also known as Lovage or Caraway Lovage, is a perennial herb belonging to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its tall stems and aromatic, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making it a potential treatment for various health issues such as digestive problems, respiratory infections, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Selinum carvifolia include essential oils, flavonoids, and coumarins, which contribute to its therapeutic benefits.

In traditional herbal medicine, Selinum carvifolia is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is also used in culinary applications, such as a flavoring agent for soups and stews.

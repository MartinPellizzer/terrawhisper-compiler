Phaius brymenrianus

Phaius brymenrianus, also known as the Phaius orchid, is a type of terrestrial orchid native to the tropical forests of Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects that may help alleviate symptoms of various health conditions, such as fever, cough, and skin infections. Bioactive constituents of Phaius brymenrianus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make various herbal preparations, including infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are commonly used in folk medicine to treat respiratory and skin disorders.

Vicia orientalis

Vicia orientalis, also known as the oriental vetch, is a perennial plant species belonging to the legume family (Fabaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, particularly in the Middle East and North Africa, due to its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which can help to alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Vicia orientalis include flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Vicia orientalis is commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion, often combined with other herbs, to create teas and tonics for respiratory health, or as a dried extract to be used in capsules or tablets.

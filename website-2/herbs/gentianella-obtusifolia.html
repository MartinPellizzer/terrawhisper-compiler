Gentianella obtusifolia

Gentianella obtusifolia, also known as small gentian or blunt gentian, is a perennial plant species within the Gentianella genus, characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and oval-shaped leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including digestive problems and inflammation, due to its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Gentianella obtusifolia include iridoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction or tincture, as well as being used in teas and infusions to aid digestion and alleviate symptoms of indigestion and bloating.

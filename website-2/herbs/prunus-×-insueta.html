Prunus × insueta

Prunus × insueta, also known as the apricot kernel tree, is a hybrid species of apricot tree that is primarily cultivated for its edible seeds and kernel.

The bioactive constituents of Prunus × insueta kernel include amygdalin, a cyanogenic glycoside, as well as other compounds such as vitamin B17 and limonene.

Prunus × insueta is commonly used in herbal preparations, including kernel extracts, oils, and supplements, which are often marketed as natural remedies for various health conditions.

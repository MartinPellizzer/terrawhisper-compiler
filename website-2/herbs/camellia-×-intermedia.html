Camellia × intermedia

Camellia × intermedia, also known as the tea plant hybrid, is a cultivated species of evergreen shrub that is a cross between Camellia sinensis and Camellia assamica.

The leaves of Camellia × intermedia have been used in traditional medicine to promote cardiovascular health, improve digestion, and boost the immune system, due to its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include theaflavins, thearubigins, and catechins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Camellia × intermedia is commonly used to make herbal teas and infusions, such as green tea, black tea, and oolong tea, which are consumed globally for their health benefits and unique flavor profiles.

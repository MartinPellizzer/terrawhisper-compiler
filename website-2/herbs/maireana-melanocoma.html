Maireana melanocoma

Maireana melanocoma, also known as the black-stemmed blue bush, is a shrub native to Australia that belongs to the family Amaranthaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used in Western Australian Aboriginal medicine to treat a range of health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Maireana melanocoma include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

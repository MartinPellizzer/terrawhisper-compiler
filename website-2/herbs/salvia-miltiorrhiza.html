What is Salvia miltiorrhiza?

Salvia miltiorrhiza is a perennial plant that belongs to the family Lamiaceae.

Its botanical characteristics include ovate to lanceolate leaves that are 2-8 cm long and 1-4 cm wide, with a coarsely crenate margin and a petiole that is 1-3 cm long. The plant produces tall spikes of purple flowers, with a calyx that is 10-12 mm long and a corolla that is 18-22 mm long. The root of the plant contains phenolic glycosides, particularly tanshinones, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Salvia miltiorrhiza is native to China and is also known as Danshen.

Pandanus verus

Pandanus verus, also known as Tahitian screwpine, is a tropical plant species native to the Pacific Islands and Southeast Asia, characterized by its distinctive screw-like leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which are thought to help reduce stress, improve digestion, and boost the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Pandanus verus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Pandanus verus is commonly used to prepare teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as topical creams and ointments, to treat various health conditions, including skin irritations, wounds, and gastrointestinal disorders.

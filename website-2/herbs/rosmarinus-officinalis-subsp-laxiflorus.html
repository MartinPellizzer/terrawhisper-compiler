What is Rosmarinus officinalis subsp. laxiflorus?

Rosmarinus officinalis subsp.

laxiflorus is a botanical subspecies of rosemary, belonging to the family Lamiaceae, characterized by its lax inflorescence, with a relatively loose arrangement of flowers. It has a woody stem, typically 1-3 meters tall, with a greenish-gray, lanceolate foliage.

The leaves are 2-4 centimeters long, with a glabrous surface, containing 2-3 pairs of dark green, linear leaflets. The flowers are white, in a bilabiate shape, with a four-lobed corolla, measuring 3-4 millimeters in length.

The subspecies is native to the Mediterranean region, where it grows on dry, rocky soils.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Rosmarinus officinalis subsp.

laxiflorus are rosemary tea, rosemary tincture, rosemary oil, rosemary salve, and rosemary infusion. Rosemary tea is prepared by steeping dried rosemary leaves in hot water and is used to improve cognitive function, boost memory, and alleviate symptoms of indigestion and anxiety.

Rosemary tincture is a concentrated liquid extract of rosemary leaves that is used to treat a variety of health issues, including headaches, muscle pain, and inflammation. Rosemary oil, obtained through steam distillation, is used topically to relieve pain, reduce inflammation, and improve circulation, while rosemary salve is a topical cream or ointment used to moisturize and protect the skin.

Rosemary infusion, also known as rosemary decoction, is prepared by steeping dried rosemary leaves in boiling water and is used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs.

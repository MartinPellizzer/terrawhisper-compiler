Micranthes davidii

Micranthes davidii, also known as David's saxifrage or David's micranthes, is a perennial plant species belonging to the saxifrage family (Saxifragaceae).

Traditionally, Micranthes davidii has been used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Micranthes davidii include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Micranthes davidii is commonly prepared as an infusion, decoction, or tincture to treat colds, coughs, and sore throats, as well as to reduce inflammation and improve respiratory health.

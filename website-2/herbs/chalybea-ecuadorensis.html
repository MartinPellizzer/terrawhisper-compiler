Chalybea ecuadorensis

Chalybea ecuadorensis, also known as Ecuadorian Chalybea, is a plant species belonging to the borage family (Boraginaceae) native to Ecuador.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant effects, which may help alleviate symptoms of various conditions, including wounds, respiratory issues, and gastrointestinal problems. The bioactive constituents of Chalybea ecuadorensis include alkaloids, phenolic compounds, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is typically prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and it is also used in topical applications to treat skin conditions and wounds.

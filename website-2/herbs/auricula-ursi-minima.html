Auricula-ursi minima

Auricula-ursi minima, also known as bearberry, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to boreal forests in North America and Europe, characterized by its small, white flowers and red or yellowish berries.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with health benefits that include its antimicrobial and antidiuretic effects, which can help alleviate urinary tract infections and prevent kidney stones. Auricula-ursi minima contains bioactive constituents such as arbutin, hydroquinone, and ursolic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with bearberry include teas, tinctures, and extracts, often used to treat urinary tract issues and support kidney function.

Dalea pilosissima

Dalea pilosissima, also known as hairy prairie clover, is a perennial legume native to North America, characterized by its pink or purple flowers and hairy stems.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its ability to treat digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant activities, which may contribute to its potential health benefits. The bioactive constituents of Dalea pilosissima include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are thought to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

Herbal preparations made from Dalea pilosissima include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to support gut health and immune function.

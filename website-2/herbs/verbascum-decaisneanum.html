Verbascum decaisneanum

Verbascum decaisneanum, commonly known as De Caen's mullein, is a biennial plant species within the mullein genus, characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including treating respiratory issues such as bronchitis and coughs due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Verbascum decaisneanum include flavonoids, terpenoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In herbal medicine, Verbascum decaisneanum is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or syrup, and is often used in the treatment of respiratory conditions and as a natural expectorant.

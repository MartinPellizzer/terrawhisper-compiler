Senna paraensis

Senna paraensis, also known as Para senna or Sena, is a plant species within the legume family (Fabaceae), traditionally used for its medicinal and laxative properties.

The health benefits of Senna paraensis include its ability to treat constipation and other gastrointestinal issues due to its anthraquinone content, which stimulates bowel movement and helps to regulate the digestive system. The bioactive constituents of Senna paraensis include anthraquinones, such as aloe-emodin, chrysophanol, and rhein, as well as flavonoids and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal and laxative effects.

Senna paraensis is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, capsules, and extracts, which are often marketed for their laxative and digestive benefits, although its use should be approached with caution due to potential side effects.

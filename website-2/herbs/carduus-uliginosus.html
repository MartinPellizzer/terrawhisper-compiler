Carduus uliginosus

Carduus uliginosus, also known as Marsh Thistle or Upland Thistle, is a perennial plant species in the sunflower family (Asteraceae) native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and diuretic properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of urinary tract infections, kidney stones, and skin conditions such as eczema and acne. The bioactive constituents of Carduus uliginosus include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In herbal medicine, Marsh Thistle is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is often used in combination with other herbs to support kidney and liver health, as well as to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing.

Santaloides brachyandra

Santaloides brachyandra, commonly known as Sandalwood, is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to Australia, prized for its fragrant heartwood and essential oil.

The health benefits of Sandalwood oil include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate stress, promote relaxation, and soothe skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Sandalwood oil include alpha-santalol, beta-santalol, and other sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Sandalwood is often used to make essential oil, timbers, and other preparations, such as incense sticks, aromatherapy blends, and topical creams, which are valued for their calming and medicinal properties.

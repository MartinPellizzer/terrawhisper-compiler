Euphorbia wilsonii

Euphorbia wilsonii, also known as Wilson's Spurge, is a succulent plant species native to China, characterized by its thick, green stems and small, white flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine, offering various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in the treatment of wounds, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Euphorbia wilsonii include flavonoids, triterpenoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is often prepared into a decoction or infused as a tea, as well as made into a tincture or powder for use in various herbal remedies.

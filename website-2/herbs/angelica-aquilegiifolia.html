Angelica aquilegiifolia

Angelica aquilegiifolia, also known as gypsywort or angelica, is a perennial plant species native to North America, characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves and tall spikes of small white flowers.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including the reduction of inflammation and the alleviation of menstrual cramps, nausea, and digestive issues, making it a popular remedy in herbal medicine. The bioactive constituents of Angelica aquilegiifolia include iridoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Angelica aquilegiifolia is commonly used to prepare teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being used in combination with other herbs in formulas to address various health concerns.

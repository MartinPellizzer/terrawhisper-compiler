What is Lagotis alutacea var. rockii?

Lagotis alutacea var. rockii is a plant species within the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its stemless rosettes, with leaves that are elliptic to ovate, 2-10 cm long, and 1-4 cm wide, featuring entire margins and a glabrous or sparsely pubescent surface. The flowers are solitary, borne on a peduncle 1-10 cm long, with a calyx that is tubular, 5-10 mm long, and 5-lobed; the corolla is bell-shaped, 5-8 mm long, and 10-15 mm in diameter, with a white or pale yellow color. The plant grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at elevations of 2,500-4,500 meters in the Himalayas.

The specific epithet "rockii" honors the Chinese botanist Chao-Chin Rock.

Nasturtium cumingianum

Nasturtium cumingianum, also known as Indian cress or Mexican nasturtium, is a type of annual or perennial flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its bright, trumpet-shaped flowers and peppery, edible leaves.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties, which can aid in reducing inflammation, fighting infections, and supporting the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Nasturtium cumingianum include isothiocyanates, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and salves, which can be used to treat various ailments, including skin conditions, wounds, and respiratory issues.

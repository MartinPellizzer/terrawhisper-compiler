Carum falcaria

Carum falcaria, also known as the carrot fern, is a perennial plant that belongs to the parsley family (Apiaceae) and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

The health benefits of Carum falcaria include its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help in reducing inflammation and protecting against cell damage. The bioactive constituents of this plant include coumarins, flavonoids, and volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Carum falcaria is commonly used in teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used in the production of essential oils and other pharmaceutical preparations.

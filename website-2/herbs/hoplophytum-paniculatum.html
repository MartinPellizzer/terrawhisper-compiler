Hoplophytum paniculatum

Hoplophytum paniculatum, also known as the Kava shrub, is a plant species native to Australia and New Guinea, characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include reducing anxiety, inflammation, and pain, making it a potential treatment for conditions such as insomnia, arthritis, and anxiety disorders. The bioactive constituents of Hoplophytum paniculatum include kavalactones, specifically kawain, dihydrokawain, and methysticin, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used to prepare a kava drink, which is consumed as a tea or infused into a liquid extract, with the most common herbal preparations being kava powder, kava capsules, and kava tinctures.

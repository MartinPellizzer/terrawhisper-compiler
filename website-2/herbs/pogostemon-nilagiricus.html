Pogostemon nilagiricus

Pogostemon nilagiricus, also known as Nilgiri patchouli, is a plant species belonging to the family Lamiaceae, native to the Western Ghats of India.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, making it beneficial for skin and respiratory health. The bioactive constituents of Pogostemon nilagiricus include patchoulol, patchoulene, and other sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Pogostemon nilagiricus include essential oils, extracts, and infusions, which are used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including skin conditions, fever, and respiratory issues.

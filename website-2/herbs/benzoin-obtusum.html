Benzoin obtusum

Benzoin obtusum, also known as Sumatran Benzoin, is a species of tree in the family Styraceae, native to Indonesia and Malaysia.

The resin extracted from its bark is valued for its health benefits, which include reducing inflammation, soothing skin irritations, and exhibiting antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Benzoin obtusum resin include benzoin, benzoic acid, and vanillin, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Traditionally, the resin is used in herbal preparations such as tinctures, salves, and infusions, often used to treat wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory issues, and also as a natural fixative in perfumery.

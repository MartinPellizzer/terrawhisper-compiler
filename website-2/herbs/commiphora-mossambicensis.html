Commiphora mossambicensis

Commiphora mossambicensis, also known as African myrrh or East African myrrh, is a plant species in the genus Commiphora, characterized by its resinous trunk and branches.

The health benefits of Commiphora mossambicensis include its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties, which can be used to treat various conditions such as wounds, skin infections, and oral diseases. The plant contains bioactive constituents like sesquiterpenes, triterpenoids, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Commiphora mossambicensis is often used in traditional medicine and is prepared into various herbal products, including myrrh resin, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to promote wound healing, reduce inflammation, and prevent infection.

Citronella virescens

Citronella virescens, also known as Indonesian Citronella, is a perennial grass native to the tropical regions of Asia and Australia, belonging to the family Cymbopogon.

The health benefits of Citronella virescens include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of colds, fever, and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Citronella virescens include citronellal, geraniol, and limonene, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Citronella virescens include essential oils, teas, and infusions, often used to treat respiratory issues, skin conditions, and as a natural insect repellent.

Chenopodium indicum

Chenopodium indicum, also known as Indian spinach or bathua, is a perennial plant species belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

Traditionally, Chenopodium indicum has been utilized for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, making it beneficial for treating various health conditions such as fever, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Chenopodium indicum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, Chenopodium indicum is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being used in cooking as a leafy green vegetable.

Ficus fraseri

Ficus fraseri, also known as the Fraser's fig, is a species of evergreen tree in the fig family (Moraceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its purported health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Bioactive constituents of Ficus fraseri include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

Ficus fraseri is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and extracts, which are often employed to alleviate symptoms of respiratory tract infections and to support immune function.

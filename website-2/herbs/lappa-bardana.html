Lappa bardana

Lappa bardana, commonly known as burdock, is a biennial plant in the Asteraceae family characterized by its large, edible roots and leafy greens.

The health benefits of burdock include its potential to support digestive health, reduce inflammation, and enhance immune function due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of burdock include inulin, arabinose, and sesquiterpene lactones, such as lignans and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Burdock is often used in herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as in traditional medicine, such as in Chinese and Ayurvedic practices, to support detoxification and promote overall well-being.

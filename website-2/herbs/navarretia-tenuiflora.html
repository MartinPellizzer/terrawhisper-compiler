Navarretia tenuiflora

Navarretia tenuiflora, also known as Indian pink or thread-leaved navarretia, is a perennial wildflower native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, making it beneficial for reducing pain and inflammation, as well as protecting against cell damage and oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Navarretia tenuiflora include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including arthritis, wounds, and gastrointestinal issues.

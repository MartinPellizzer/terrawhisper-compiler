Sedum spiciforme

Sedum spiciforme, also known as the columned stonecrop, is a succulent plant species that belongs to the Crassulaceae family.

Traditionally, Sedum spiciforme has been used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its purported anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Sedum spiciforme include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Sedum spiciforme is often prepared into teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being used in the form of dried roots and rhizomes, to support immune function and alleviate respiratory symptoms.

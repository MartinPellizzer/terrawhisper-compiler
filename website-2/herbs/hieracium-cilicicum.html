Hieracium cilicicum

Hieracium cilicicum, also known as Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant species that belongs to the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, digestive issues, and skin problems, due to its potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Hieracium cilicicum include sesquiterpene lactones, such as ilicin and leucolic acid, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments to treat skin conditions and wounds.

classification

Hieracium cilicicum is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion tribe of the sunflower family (Asteraceae). It belongs to the genus Hieracium, which consists of about 1000 species of hawkweeds. The specific epithet "cilicicum" refers to the region of Cilicia, a historical region in southern Turkey. As a member of the Asteraceae family, Hieracium cilicicum is closely related to other dandelion-like plants. Hieracium cilicicum is typically classified as a herbaceous perennial plant.

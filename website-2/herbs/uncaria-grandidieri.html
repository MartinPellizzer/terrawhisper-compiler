Uncaria grandidieri

Uncaria grandidieri, also known as the Madagascar cat's claw vine, is a woody climbing vine native to the tropical forests of Madagascar.

The bioactive constituents of Uncaria grandidieri include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, particularly oxindole alkaloids such as griffonidine and isorhynchophylline, which are believed to be responsible for the plant's therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Uncaria grandidieri is commonly prepared as a tincture, decoction, or capsule, and is often used to support immune function, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and insomnia.

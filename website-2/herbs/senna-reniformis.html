Senna reniformis

Senna reniformis, also known as the kidney pod or kidney senna, is a perennial legume native to South Africa, characterized by its kidney-shaped seeds and medicinal properties.

The plant has been traditionally used for its health benefits, including treating constipation, diarrhea, and other gastrointestinal issues, due to its anthraquinone content, which stimulates bowel movements and increases water in the intestines. The bioactive constituents of Senna reniformis include anthraquinones, such as aloe-emodin, rhein, and sennosides A and B, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Senna reniformis include teas, infusions, and extracts, often used in traditional medicine to promote digestion and relieve symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome.

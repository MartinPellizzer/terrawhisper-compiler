What is Juncus acutus subsp. leopoldii?

Juncus acutus subsp.

leopoldii is a plant belonging to the family Juncaceae, a group of sedge-like plants with triangular stems and linear leaves. This subspecies has leaves typically 2-5 mm wide, green or reddish-green in color, often keeled, and with a sharp tip.

Its stems are 1-3 mm thick, usually green or reddish-green, and often have a reddish tint at the base. Juncus acutus subsp. leopoldii is typically found in areas with brackish water, such as salt marshes and coastal dunes, in the Mediterranean region.

This subspecies is characterized by its ability to grow in environments with high salinity.

Myrsine angustifolia

Myrsine angustifolia, also known as Chilean boxwood or Chilean inkberry, is a plant species native to Chile and Argentina, characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and dark blue or purple berries.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, pain, and digestive issues, due to its reported health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Myrsine angustifolia include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Myrsine angustifolia is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for wound healing and skin issues.

Equisetum affine

Equisetum affine, also known as scouring rush, is a perennial vascular plant that belongs to the Equisetaceae family and is a living fossil from the Paleozoic era.

Traditionally, the plant has been used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin irritations, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Equisetum affine include lignans, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In herbal medicine, Equisetum affine is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is often used to treat urinary tract infections, respiratory issues, and skin conditions.

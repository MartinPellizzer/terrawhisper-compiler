What is Pistolochia solida f. trichophora?

Pistolochia solida f. trichophora is a botanical form within the species Pistolochia solida, belonging to the family Papaveraceae.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous growth habit, bearing alternate, lanceolate to elliptical leaves that are 2-5 cm long and have a glabrous to pubescent surface. The plant produces racemes of 4-8 flowers, each measuring 4-6 cm in diameter, with four sepals and four petals that are white to pale yellow in color, and have a prominent cluster of stamens in the center. Pistils are 4-6 mm long and have 4-6 stigmas.

Pistilochia solida f. trichophora is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, specifically the mountain ranges of Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan.

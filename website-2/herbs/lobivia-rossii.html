Lobivia rossii

Lobivia rossii, also known as the 'Ross's Pincushion Cactus', is a species of flowering plant in the Cactaceae family, native to the Andes in South America.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects that contribute to its use in treating various health issues, such as wounds, infections, and even certain types of cancer. Lobivia rossii contains bioactive constituents like alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

The plant is often used to prepare herbal infusions, tinctures, and ointments, which are traditionally employed to promote wound healing and to treat skin conditions, such as eczema and acne.

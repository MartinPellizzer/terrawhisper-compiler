Quercus rugosa

Quercus rugosa, also known as the cork oak, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region and Western Asia, characterized by its thick, rugged bark and edible acorns.

The health benefits of Quercus rugosa include its ability to act as an anti-inflammatory and antioxidant, potentially helping to reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease and certain cancers. The bioactive constituents of Quercus rugosa include tannins, phenolic acids, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Quercus rugosa is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is commonly used to make teas, tinctures, and extracts that are used to support digestive health and boost the immune system.

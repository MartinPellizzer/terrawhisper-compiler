Althaea biennis

Althaea biennis, also known as Marsh Mallow, is a perennial plant native to Europe and Asia, valued for its edible roots and medicinal properties.

The plant has been traditionally used to soothe digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and calm respiratory problems, such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its demulcent and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Althaea biennis include mucilages, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In herbal medicine, the plant is commonly used to make syrups, infusions, and tinctures, as well as topical creams and ointments, to promote wound healing and alleviate skin irritations.

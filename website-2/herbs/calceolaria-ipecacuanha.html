Calceolaria ipecacuanha

Calceolaria ipecacuanha, also known as Ipecacuanha or Ipecac, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, traditionally used as a medicinal herb.

The health benefits of Calceolaria ipecacuanha include its expectorant properties, which help relieve respiratory issues such as coughs and bronchitis, as well as its emetic properties, which induce vomiting in cases of poisoning. The bioactive constituents of this plant include alkaloids, primarily ipecac alkaloids, emetine and cephaline, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Calceolaria ipecacuanha include tinctures, infusions, and powdered extracts, often used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory issues and as a purgative.

Dactylorchis curvifolia

Dactylorchis curvifolia, also known as the Autumn lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Europe.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation and fever, due to its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Dactylorchis curvifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and iridoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are infusions and tinctures, often used to treat digestive issues and reduce inflammation.

classification

Dactylorchis curvifolia is a species of orchid that belongs to the family Orchidaceae. It is a terrestrial orchid that is native to Asia, specifically in the Himalayan region. Dactylorchis curvifolia is a member of the genus Dactylorchis, which is a group of orchids that are characterized by their unique, curved flowers.

Aframomum hanburyi

Aframomum hanburyi, also known as the 'Red Ginger', is a perennial plant species belonging to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It has been traditionally used in various cultures for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, providing relief from digestive issues, pain, and fever. The bioactive constituents of A. hanburyi include gingerols, shogaols, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, A. hanburyi is commonly prepared as a decoction, tincture, or oil, which is used to treat various health conditions, including arthritis, colds, and sore throats.

Malus sylvestris

Malus sylvestris, also known as the wild apple or crab apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

The health benefits of Malus sylvestris include its potential to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as wounds, respiratory issues, and gastrointestinal disorders. The bioactive constituents of Malus sylvestris include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Malus sylvestris is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as in traditional remedies like jams and preserves, which are often used to support digestive health and provide a source of antioxidants.

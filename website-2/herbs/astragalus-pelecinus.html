Astragalus pelecinus

Astragalus pelecinus, commonly known as Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial legume plant native to Mongolia and China, prized for its medicinal properties.

The plant is valued for its adaptogenic and immunomodulatory effects, which may help to reduce stress, boost the immune system, and prevent illness. Astragalus pelecinus contains bioactive constituents such as saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Astragalus pelecinus is often used to make herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and capsules, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including fatigue, respiratory infections, and cardiovascular disease.

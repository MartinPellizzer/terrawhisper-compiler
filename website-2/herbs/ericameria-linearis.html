Ericameria linearis

Ericameria linearis, also known as Coyote Brush or Green Rabbitbrush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and Baja California, Mexico.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of colds, sore throats, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Ericameria linearis include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Ericameria linearis is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to promote respiratory health, reduce inflammation, and support immune function.

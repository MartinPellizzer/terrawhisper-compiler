Huperzia oceanica

Huperzia oceanica, also known as Huperzia serrata, is a species of liverwort, a type of non-vascular, primitive plant that belongs to the moss family.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including anxiety, depression, and age-related cognitive decline, due to its potential nootropic and anxiolytic properties. The bioactive constituents of Huperzia oceanica include huperzine A, a potent inhibitor of acetylcholinesterase, as well as other alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds.

The most common herbal preparations made with Huperzia oceanica are capsules, tablets, and extracts, which are often used to support cognitive function and memory, and to alleviate symptoms of dementia and Alzheimer's disease.

What is Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. subalpinum?

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

subalpinum is a subspecies of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, native to the mountains of Central Europe. It is a perennial herb, growing up to 20-40 cm in height, with a short stem and a rosette of leaves that are lanceolate to linear in shape, often hairy and glandular-dotted.

The plant produces yellow or reddish flowers in a capitulum, which is 1-2 cm in diameter, comprising 8-12 ray florets and 20-40 disc florets. The leaves and stem are covered with soft, white or light-brown hairs, and the plant has a distinctive, unpleasant odor. The subspecies has been distinguished from H. valdepilosum subsp.

valdepilosum by its smaller flowers and more glandular-dotted leaves.

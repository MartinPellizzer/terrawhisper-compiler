Angelica angustifolia

Angelica angustifolia, also known as Rocky Mountain angelica or narrow-leaved angelica, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America, characterized by its tall stalks and distinctive umbrella-shaped clusters of small white flowers.

The health benefits of Angelica angustifolia include its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which have been traditionally used to alleviate menstrual cramps, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of this plant include coumarins, sesquiterpenes, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Angelica angustifolia include teas, capsules, and tinctures, often used to support women's health and relieve symptoms associated with menstrual cycles.

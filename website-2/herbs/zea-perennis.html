Zea perennis

Zea perennis, also known as perennial teosinte, is a wild grass native to Central America, belonging to the same genus as corn and containing the highest concentration of zeaxanthin, a carotenoid pigment.

The health benefits of Zea perennis include its ability to protect the eyes from age-related macular degeneration and improve vision in people with glaucoma, as well as its potential to reduce inflammation and improve cardiovascular health due to its high antioxidant content. The bioactive constituents of Zea perennis include zeaxanthin, lutein, and other carotenoids, as well as phenolic acids and flavonoids.

Zea perennis is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as capsules, tablets, and eye drops, which are used to support eye health and vision.

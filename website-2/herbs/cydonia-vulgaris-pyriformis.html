Cydonia vulgaris pyriformis

Cydonia vulgaris pyriformis, commonly known as the quince tree, is a deciduous tree species native to Asia and Europe, characterized by its fragrant flowers and edible fruits.

The health benefits of quince include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate digestive issues, reduce fever, and protect against chronic diseases such as cancer and cardiovascular disease. The bioactive constituents of quince include flavonoids, triterpenoids, and organic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Quince is often used in traditional medicine and is commonly prepared into herbal teas, jams, and syrups, as well as being used in folk remedies for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

side effects

Cydonia vulgaris pyriformis, also known as quince, can cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals due to its high tannin and saponin content. The plant's seeds and leaves may also be toxic if ingested. Consuming large amounts of quince can lead to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. In addition, quince can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, which can result in adverse effects. Individuals with allergies or sensitivities to quince should exercise caution when handling or consuming the plant.

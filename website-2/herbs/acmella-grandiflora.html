Acmella grandiflora

Acmella grandiflora, also known as the 'jambu' or 'suckers plant', is a tropical plant native to the Americas and Africa, belonging to the family Asteraceae.

The plant is valued for its oral and topical health benefits, including its ability to reduce inflammation and alleviate pain. The bioactive constituents of Acmella grandiflora include sesquiterpene lactones, particularly acmellin, which is responsible for its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Acmella grandiflora is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as in traditional remedies like chewing gum and topical creams.

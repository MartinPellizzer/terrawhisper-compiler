Gnaphalium virescens

Gnaphalium virescens, also known as Green Cudweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae).

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in the treatment of various conditions such as eczema, wounds, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Gnaphalium virescens include sesquiterpene lactones, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Gnaphalium virescens is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or topical salve to take advantage of its therapeutic benefits.

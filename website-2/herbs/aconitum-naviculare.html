Aconitum naviculare

Aconitum naviculare, also known as the yam or mountain aconitum, is a perennial plant species within the Aconitum genus, commonly found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

Traditionally, the plant has been used for its medicinal properties, which include pain relief, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, making it a valuable remedy for treating various ailments such as arthritis, skin conditions, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Aconitum naviculare include aconitine, mesaconitine, and hypaconitine, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared in the form of a tincture, decoction, or ointment, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including pain, inflammation, and wounds.

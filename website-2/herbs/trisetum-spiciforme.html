Trisetum spiciforme

Trisetum spiciforme, also known as the spiciformed wheat grass or spiciform barley grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of China.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which may aid in reducing oxidative stress and inflammation in the body. The bioactive constituents of Trisetum spiciforme include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Trisetum spiciforme is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and is commonly prepared into teas, capsules, and infusions, as well as being used in combination with other herbs in decoctions.

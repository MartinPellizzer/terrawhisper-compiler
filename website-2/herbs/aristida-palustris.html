Aristida palustris

Aristida palustris, also known as Marsh Three-awn Grass, is a perennial grass species native to wetlands and marshy areas, characterized by its distinctive three-awned spikelets and slender stems.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Aristida palustris include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Marsh Three-awn Grass include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which can be used to support respiratory health and soothe skin irritations.

Camelina sativa

Camelina sativa, also known as gold of pleasure, is an annual oilseed crop belonging to the Brassicaceae family, commonly cultivated for its edible oil and nutritional benefits.

The health benefits of Camelina sativa include its potential to lower cholesterol levels, improve heart health, and provide antioxidant properties, making it a valuable supplement for cardiovascular health. The bioactive constituents of Camelina sativa include camelineic acid, an omega-3 fatty acid, as well as other antioxidants such as phenolic acids and flavonoids.

Camelina sativa is often used to make herbal preparations such as oil capsules, tinctures, and infusions, which are commonly used to support heart health and reduce inflammation.

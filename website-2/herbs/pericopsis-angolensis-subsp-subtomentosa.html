What is Pericopsis angolensis subsp. subtomentosa?

The leaves are pinnate, with 5-11 pairs of leaflets that are elliptical in shape and have a length of 5-15 centimeters and a width of 2-6 centimeters. The flowers are white or yellowish in color, fragrant, and arranged in axillary spikes, while the pods are flat and 15-20 centimeters long, containing 5-8 seeds.

The plant is native to tropical Africa and is found in humid forests and woodlands.

What is Padus serrulata f. lannesiana?

Padus serrulata f. lannesiana is a taxonomic variant of the species Padus serrulata within the genus Padus, belonging to the family Rosaceae.

This plant is characterized by its deciduous habit, with leaves that are pinnate, having 3-7 pairs of leaflets that are serrulate or crenate, and a leaflet length of 2-5 cm. The leaves are glabrous on the upper surface and pubescent on the lower surface, with a petiole length of 2-5 mm. Padus serrulata f. lannesiana produces white or pink flowers in a corymb, which are 4-6 mm in diameter, with 5 petals and a calyx of 5 lobes.

The plant's fruit is a drupe, with a globose shape and a diameter of 6-8 mm.

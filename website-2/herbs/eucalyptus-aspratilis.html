Eucalyptus aspratilis

Eucalyptus aspratilis, also known as the mountain white gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to eastern Australia.

The leaves of Eucalyptus aspratilis have been used in traditional medicine for their health benefits, including reducing inflammation, relieving respiratory issues such as coughs and colds, and providing antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Eucalyptus aspratilis include eucalyptol, a volatile oil with decongestant and antiseptic properties, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

The most common herbal preparations made with Eucalyptus aspratilis include essential oils, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to relieve respiratory issues and promote relaxation.

Corydalis makinoana

Corydalis makinoana, a perennial herb native to China, is a member of the Papaveraceae family and is also known as Japanese corydalis or Makino's corydalis.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and anxiolytic properties, making it a potential treatment for pain relief and anxiety. The bioactive constituents of Corydalis makinoana include alkaloids such as corydaline, corynoline, and protopine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine and modern herbalism, Corydalis makinoana is often used to make teas, tinctures, and capsules, which are commonly used to alleviate pain and reduce anxiety.

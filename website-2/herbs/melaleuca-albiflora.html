Melaleuca albiflora

Melaleuca albiflora, commonly known as the White Paperbark, is a tree species native to Australia, belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties, making it beneficial for the treatment of various health conditions, including wounds, skin infections, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Melaleuca albiflora include essential oils such as cineole, terpinen-4-ol, and limonene, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used in herbal preparations, including tea, capsules, and topical creams, which are valued for their ability to promote wound healing and reduce inflammation.

Sorghum fastigiatum

Sorghum fastigiatum, also known as broom sorghum, is a type of grass belonging to the Sorghum genus, characterized by its upright growth habit and feathery inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making it a potential remedy for various ailments. Bioactive constituents of Sorghum fastigiatum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat conditions such as fever, digestive issues, and respiratory problems.

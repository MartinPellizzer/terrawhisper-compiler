Vicia adriatica

Vicia adriatica, also known as Adriatic vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it a possible remedy for various ailments such as digestive issues and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Vicia adriatica include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Vicia adriatica is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as tinctures and extracts, which are used to treat a range of health conditions.

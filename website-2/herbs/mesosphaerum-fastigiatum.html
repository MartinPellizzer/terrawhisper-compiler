Mesosphaerum fastigiatum

Mesosphaerum fastigiatum, commonly known as Brazilian pepperwort or Brazilian hyptis, is a plant species in the mint family Lamiaceae.

The bioactive constituents of Mesosphaerum fastigiatum include essential oils, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds, such as rosmarinic acid and linalool, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Mesosphaerum fastigiatum is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or essential oil, and is used to treat colds, coughs, and other respiratory complaints, as well as skin conditions and digestive issues.

Nepeta elegantissima

Nepeta elegantissima, commonly known as Catmint, is a perennial plant species in the mint family (Lamiaceae) characterized by its fragrant leaves and vibrant purple flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties, offering potential health benefits in treating anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Nepeta elegantissima include nepetone, a sesquiterpene lactone with known sedative and anti-inflammatory effects, as well as other essential oils and flavonoids.

Catmint is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures to promote relaxation, soothe digestive issues, and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and stress.

Verbascum roripifolium

Verbascum roripifolium, also known as alpine mullein, is a perennial plant species in the mullein genus, characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive, lobed leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, providing relief from respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as wound healing and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Verbascum roripifolium include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and iridoids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Verbascum roripifolium include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat respiratory issues, skin conditions, and other health problems.

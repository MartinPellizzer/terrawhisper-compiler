Clematis japonica

Clematis japonica, also known as Japanese Clematis, is a flowering vine native to East Asia, characterized by its large, showy white flowers and long, woody stems.

Traditionally, Clematis japonica has been used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The plant's bioactive constituents include clematidine, an alkaloid with potential immunomodulatory effects, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Clematis japonica is commonly prepared as a decoction or tincture, and is also used in modern herbal supplements, such as capsules and teas.

Arum guttatum

The Arum guttatum, also known as the Leopard Lily or Guttata Arum, is a plant species native to South Africa, characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and vibrant, white and green flowers.

The bioactive constituents of Arum guttatum include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

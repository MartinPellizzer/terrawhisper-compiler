Linaria bipartita

Linaria bipartita, also known as Toadflax or Butterfly Flower, is a perennial plant species belonging to the figwort family (Scrophulariaceae) native to Central Asia and the Himalayas.

The plant has been traditionally used in medicine for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation, relieving coughs, and treating respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Linaria bipartita include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Linaria bipartita is commonly used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used in the production of cough syrups and expectorants.

Potentilla pubescens

Potentilla pubescens, also known as downy cinquefoil, is a perennial plant that belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is native to North America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making it a potential remedy for various health issues such as wound healing, digestive problems, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Potentilla pubescens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Downy cinquefoil is often used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat fever, sore throats, and respiratory issues, as well as to promote wound healing and reduce inflammation.

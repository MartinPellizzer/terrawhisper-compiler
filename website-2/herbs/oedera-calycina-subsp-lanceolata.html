What is Oedera calycina subsp. lanceolata?

Oedera calycina subsp.

lanceolata is a botanical subspecies of Oedera calycina, belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is a shrubby perennial herb with a woody base, growing up to 50 cm in height, and has lanceolate or elliptic leaves that are 2-5 cm in length, often covered with a greyish or white powdery coating.

The leaves are sessile, alternate, and have a linear or cuneate base. The inflorescence is a capitulum, comprising 10-20 florets with ligulate corolla and 5-merous disc florets, yellow in color, and produces achenes 2-3 mm in length.

The subspecies is endemic to southern Africa, specifically in the Western Cape and Eastern Cape provinces.

Matricaria tenuifolia

Matricaria tenuifolia, also known as Korean chamomile or Japanese chamomile, is a perennial plant in the daisy family (Asteraceae) native to East Asia, characterized by its small yellow flowers and fern-like foliage.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and sedative properties, which can help alleviate stress, anxiety, and insomnia. The bioactive constituents of Matricaria tenuifolia include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and essential oils, such as alpha-bisabolol and chamazulene, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Matricaria tenuifolia is commonly prepared as a tea, capsule, or extract, and is often used in traditional Korean and Japanese medicine to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality.

classification

Hemionitis geraniifolia is a species of fern in the family Polypodiaceae. This plant is native to Southeast Asia. Within the plant kingdom, Hemionitis geraniifolia belongs to the division Pteridophyta, which comprises all ferns. The class Polypodiopsida is the most specific classification within the plant kingdom for Hemionitis geraniifolia. The species further falls under the order Polypodiales.

medicinal uses

Hemionitis geraniifolia is a species of fern that has been used in traditional medicine in various parts of the world. Its medicinal uses include treating digestive issues, such as diarrhea and dysentery, due to its purported anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. In addition, it has been used to treat fever, coughs, and respiratory problems. The plant's leaves are often used in teas, infusions, or decoctions to harness its medicinal benefits. More research is needed to confirm the efficacy and safety of using Hemionitis geraniifolia for medicinal purposes.

What is Alchemilla alpina subsp. basaltica?

Alchemilla alpina subsp.

basaltica is a subspecies of the alpine plant species Alchemilla alpina, belonging to the family Rosaceae. It is a perennial herb with a rosette-shaped leaf structure, consisting of 5-7 leaflets with an obovate shape and a crenate margin.

The plant's leaves are glabrous, with a length of 5-15 cm and a width of 2-5 cm, and are often stipulate with a petiole. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow in color, and are arranged in a capitulum with a dense inflorescence. Alchemilla alpina subsp.

basaltica is typically found in rocky areas and dry slopes, particularly in the mountainous regions of the Balkan Peninsula.

Cardia didyma

Cardia didyma, also known as Horse Chestnut, is a perennial plant species native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa, characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, edema, and venous insufficiency, due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which contribute to its ability to improve circulation and reduce swelling. The bioactive constituents of Cardia didyma include flavonoids, saponins, and polyphenolic compounds, particularly aescin, a complex mixture of triterpene saponins that are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Cardia didyma is commonly prepared as a standardized extract, a tincture, or a topical cream, which are used to treat conditions such as varicose veins, hemorrhoids, and skin ulcers.

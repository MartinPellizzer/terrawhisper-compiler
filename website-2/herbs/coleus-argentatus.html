Coleus argentatus

Coleus argentatus, also known as Coleus aromaticus or Coleus, is a plant species native to tropical Asia, belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

The leaves and stems of Coleus argentatus have been traditionally used for their medicinal properties, offering relief from various health issues, including fever, cough, and respiratory problems, due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Coleus argentatus include essential oils, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Herbal preparations made from Coleus argentatus, such as infusions and decoctions, are commonly used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, although modern studies are needed to confirm its efficacy and safety.

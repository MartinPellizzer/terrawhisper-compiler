Palicourea eciliata

Palicourea eciliata, also known as P. tenuiflora or cat's claw, is a plant species native to the tropical forests of Central and South America.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antimalarial properties, which may help to reduce pain and inflammation, fight off infections, and treat malaria. The bioactive constituents of P. eciliata include alkaloids, particularly indole alkaloids, such as palicoureine and isopalicoureine, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often made into tea, tincture, or capsules, and is commonly used to treat various health conditions, including arthritis, digestive issues, and respiratory problems.

Dimetia prainiana

Dimetia prainiana is a plant species native to India and Southeast Asia, commonly known as the 'Indian Ashwagandha' due to its close similarity in botanical characteristics to Withania somnifera, the true Ashwagandha.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include stress relief, improved cognitive function, and enhanced immune system response. The bioactive constituents of Dimetia prainiana include withanolides, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic benefits.

The most common herbal preparations made with Dimetia prainiana include capsules, tablets, and teas, often used in Ayurvedic medicine to promote relaxation, vitality, and overall well-being.

Myrobalanus triflora

Myrobalanus triflora, also known as the Haritaki tree, is a deciduous plant species native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia, belonging to the family Combretaceae.

The health benefits of Myrobalanus triflora include its potential to aid digestion, support liver health, and exhibit anti-inflammatory properties, making it a valuable component in traditional Ayurvedic medicine. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Myrobalanus triflora are triphala, a three-herb blend, and churna, a powdered form of the plant used to support digestive health and overall well-being.

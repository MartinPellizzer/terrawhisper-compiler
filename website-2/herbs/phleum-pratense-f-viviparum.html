What is Phleum pratense f. viviparum?

Phleum pratense f. viviparum is a viviparous form of the perennial grass Phleum pratense, belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by the production of seeds without the formation of a mature floret, with the seeds being produced on the inflorescence stalk, and the presence of a short, leafy rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-100 cm, with a stem diameter of 2-6 mm, and produces a panicle inflorescence, which is a type of spikelet-bearing raceme. The spikelets are 10-20 mm long, with two to four flowers, and are arranged in a linear or slightly branching pattern.

The plant's leaves are flat, linear, and 3-10 mm wide, with a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

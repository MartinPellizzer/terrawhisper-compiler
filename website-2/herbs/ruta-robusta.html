side effects

Ruta robusta, also known as the common rue, has been traditionally used in medicine for its various properties, but it is known to cause several side effects when ingested. The plant contains furanocoumarins, which can lead to phototoxicity, causing skin irritation, blistering, and even burns upon exposure to sunlight. Ingesting large amounts of Ruta robusta can also lead to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Additionally, the plant has been associated with liver and kidney damage in some cases. It is essential to use Ruta robusta under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

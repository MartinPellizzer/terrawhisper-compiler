Borassus madagascariensis

Borassus madagascariensis, also known as the Madagascar palmyra or wild palm, is a species of palm tree native to Madagascar and other parts of Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used in medicine, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help to reduce fever, alleviate pain, and protect against infections. The bioactive constituents of B. madagascariensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is used to treat various ailments, including respiratory infections, skin conditions, and digestive problems.

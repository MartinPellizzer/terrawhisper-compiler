Campanula setacea

Campanula setacea, also known as bellflower or tall bellflower, is a perennial plant species within the Campanulaceae family, commonly found in wet meadows and along streams in Europe and Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Campanula setacea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Campanula setacea is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing.

Annona paraensis

Annona paraensis, also known as the Para Amazonian custard apple, is a tropical plant species belonging to the Annonaceae family, native to the Amazon rainforest in South America.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, inflammation, and gastrointestinal issues, due to its potential health benefits, such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Annona paraensis include annonaceous acetogenins, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Herbal preparations made from the plant include infusions, decoctions, and extracts, which are used to treat various health conditions, although its use requires caution due to potential toxicity and side effects.

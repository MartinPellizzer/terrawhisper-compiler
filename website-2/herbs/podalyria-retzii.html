Podalyria retzii

Podalyria retzii, commonly known as the Round-leaved Hakea or Round-leafed Podalyria, is a shrub native to southern Africa, belonging to the family Fabaceae.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, including its potential to treat fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. The bioactive constituents of Podalyria retzii include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations, such as infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health conditions, although further research is needed to confirm its efficacy and safety.

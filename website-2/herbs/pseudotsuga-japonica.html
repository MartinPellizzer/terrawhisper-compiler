Pseudotsuga japonica

Pseudotsuga japonica, also known as Japanese arborvitae or Japanese false cypress, is a small evergreen coniferous tree native to East Asia, belonging to the cypress family (Cupressaceae).

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties, which can help alleviate respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Pseudotsuga japonica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, particularly cedrol, which has been shown to possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

In traditional medicine, Pseudotsuga japonica is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used in combination with other herbs in various formulations to support respiratory health and immune function.

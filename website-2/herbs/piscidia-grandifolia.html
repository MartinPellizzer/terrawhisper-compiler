Piscidia grandifolia

Piscidia grandifolia, also known as Kapokbush or West Indian pea, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

The bark and leaves of this plant have been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic properties. The bioactive constituents of Piscidia grandifolia include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, the bark and leaves are commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion, which can also be used to make teas, tinctures, and salves.

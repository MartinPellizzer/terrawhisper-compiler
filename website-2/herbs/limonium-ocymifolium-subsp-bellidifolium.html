What is Limonium ocymifolium subsp. bellidifolium?

Limonium ocymifolium subsp.

bellidifolium is a perennial plant belonging to the family Plumbaginaceae. It has greyish-green, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are 10-30 mm long and 0.5-2 mm wide.

The plant produces inflorescences with numerous small white flowers in late spring and early summer, which are 3-4 mm in diameter and have 5 petals. The flowers are arranged in a spike-like structure and are subtended by small, leaf-like bracts. Limonium ocymifolium subsp.

bellidifolium is typically found in coastal areas and salt-marshes, where it can grow up to 60 cm tall.

Additionally, the antioxidant property is attributed to the plant's content of flavonoids and phenolic acids, which help to neutralize free radicals and protect against oxidative stress.

The plant's antiviral and antifungal properties are also reported, with bioactive compounds like flavonoids and terpenoids exhibiting inhibitory activity against certain viruses and fungi.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Limonium ocymifolium subsp.

bellidifolium are tea, tincture, infusion, salve, and decoction, each with its unique characteristics and uses. Tea is made by steeping the leaves and flowers in hot water, often used as a calming agent for digestive issues.

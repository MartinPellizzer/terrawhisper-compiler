Pueraria triloba

Pueraria triloba, also known as the kudzu vine or Japanese kudzu, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

The plant is rich in health benefits, including its ability to lower cholesterol levels, improve blood flow, and exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which may help to prevent cardiovascular disease and other conditions. The bioactive constituents of Pueraria triloba include isoflavones, such as daidzein and genistein, as well as other compounds like puerarin and pueraria saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Pueraria triloba is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and capsules, which are used to treat conditions like menopausal symptoms, hypertension, and osteoporosis.

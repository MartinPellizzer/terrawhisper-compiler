What is Sagittaria subulata var. gracillima?

Sagittaria subulata var. gracillima is a plant species belonging to the family Alismataceae, native to the Americas.

It is a perennial aquatic plant, characterized by its linear, strap-shaped leaves, often found in clusters at the base of the stem, and its solitary, umbel-like inflorescences with 3-10 white flowers. The plant grows up to 1.5 meters in height, with long, slender stems that arise from a rhizome and are either submerged or floating in water. The leaves are typically 10-30 cm long and 1-3 mm wide, with a smooth, hairless surface and a rounded tip.

The plant's reproductive structure consists of an apocarpous ovary, with 3-5 stigmas and 3-6 styles.

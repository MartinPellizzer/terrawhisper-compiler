Salsola vera

Salsola vera, also known as prickly saltwort, is a succulent halophytic plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and stems that can tolerate high salt concentrations.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, making it useful for treating wounds, reducing fever, and alleviating respiratory issues. Salsola vera contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical applications like salves and ointments to soothe skin irritations and promote healing.

classification

Salsola vera belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is classified as Salsola vera (syn. Salsola tragus). It is a perennial plant native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa. The genus Salsola is further classified into the tribe Salsoleae. The species Salsola vera is closely related to Salsola tragus and other plants in the same genus. Salsola vera is considered a synonym of Salsola tragus due to the lack of distinct differences between the two species.

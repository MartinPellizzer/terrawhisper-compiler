Tritonia lacerata

Tritonia lacerata, also known as the "Cape Tritonia," is a flowering plant species native to the Western Cape province of South Africa, characterized by its showy, red-orange flowers and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as digestive issues and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Tritonia lacerata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and coumarins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health conditions and promote overall well-being.

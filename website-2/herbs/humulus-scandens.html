Humulus scandens

Humulus scandens, also known as Chinese hop or Japanese hop, is a climbing plant native to East Asia and is a member of the Cannabaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and alleviating symptoms of anxiety and insomnia. The bioactive constituents of Humulus scandens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Humulus scandens is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, often in combination with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

Ranunculus trivialis

Ranunculus trivialis, commonly known as the small-flowered buttercup, is a perennial plant species found in various parts of the world, characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can aid in relieving pain, reducing inflammation, and protecting against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus trivialis include glycosides, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Ranunculus trivialis is often prepared as a tincture or infused in tea, and is used to treat various conditions, including arthritis, digestive issues, and skin problems.

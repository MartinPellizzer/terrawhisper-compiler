Ranunculus tersiflorus

Ranunculus tersiflorus, also known as mountain buttercup, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which may help alleviate symptoms of various health conditions such as skin infections and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus tersiflorus include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are commonly used to treat skin conditions, respiratory issues, and as a general tonic to promote overall health and well-being.

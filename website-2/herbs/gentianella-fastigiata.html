Gentianella fastigiata

Gentianella fastigiata, also known as narrow-leaved gentian or gentianella, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in medicine due to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which contribute to its potential health benefits in treating digestive issues, fever reduction, and wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Gentianella fastigiata include iridoids, such as gentiopicroside and swertiamarin, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Gentianella fastigiata is commonly prepared as a tincture, infusion, or dried root and leaf extract, often used to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of indigestion and dyspepsia.

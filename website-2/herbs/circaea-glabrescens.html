Circaea glabrescens

Circaea glabrescens, also known as the small enchanter's nightshade or wood sorrel, is a perennial plant species belonging to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae).

Traditionally, Circaea glabrescens has been used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Circaea glabrescens include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat fever, coughs, and respiratory issues.

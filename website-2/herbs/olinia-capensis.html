Olinia capensis

Olinia capensis, also known as the red stinkwood or white ironwood, is a deciduous tree species native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties, which can aid in the treatment of wounds, fever, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Olinia capensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Olinia capensis is often prepared as an infusion or decoction, and is also used in the form of a topical ointment or cream to treat skin conditions and wounds.

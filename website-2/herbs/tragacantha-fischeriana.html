Tragacantha fischeriana

Tragacantha fischeriana, also known as Astragalus fischerianus, is a species of legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

The plant is valued for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory properties, making it a popular natural remedy for colds, flu, and other respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Tragacantha fischeriana include saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often prepared as a tea, powder, or extract, and is also used in combination with other herbs to create tonics and adaptogenic formulas.

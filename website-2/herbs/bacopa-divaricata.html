Bacopa divaricata

Bacopa divaricata, also known as Brahmi, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Bacopaaceae, native to tropical and subtropical regions of the world.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to enhance cognitive function, memory, and concentration, as well as to promote relaxation and reduce stress levels. Bacopa divaricata contains bioactive constituents such as bacosides A, B, C, D, and E, as well as bacopaside I and II, which are believed to be responsible for its neuroprotective and adaptogenic properties.

Bacopa divaricata is commonly prepared as a herbal supplement, tea, or extract, and is often used in traditional Ayurvedic and Unani medicine to support brain health and overall well-being.

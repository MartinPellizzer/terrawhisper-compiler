What is Primula glabra subsp. genestieriana?

Primula glabra subsp.

genestieriana is a subspecies of flowering plant in the family Primulaceae. It is an alpine perennial with a rosette of ovate, glabrous leaves that are 2-5 cm long and 1-2 cm wide, and a scape of white flowers that are 2-3 cm long and 1.5-2.5 cm wide, with five petals and a bilobed calyx.

The flowers are hermaphroditic and have a tubular shape, with a corolla tube that is 1.5-2.5 cm long. It is found in the Pyrenees, where it grows in wet, stony areas at high elevations.

The plant has a compact, rhizomatous habit and flowers in May and June.

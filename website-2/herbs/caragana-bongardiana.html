Caragana bongardiana

Caragana bongardiana, also known as Siberian pea tree, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia and Eastern Europe, belonging to the Fabaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Caragana bongardiana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Caragana bongardiana include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often used to treat colds, sore throats, and skin conditions.

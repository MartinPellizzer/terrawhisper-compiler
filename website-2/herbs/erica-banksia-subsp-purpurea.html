What is Erica banksia subsp. purpurea?

Erica banksia subsp.

purpurea is an Australian heath species belonging to the family Ericaceae. It is a shrub with an erect habit, typically growing to 0.2-1.5 meters in height and 0.5-1.5 meters in width, with a woody base and a leafy crown.

The leaves are small, linear to lanceolate, and have a dark green color with a glaucous sheen, measuring 4-15 millimeters in length and 0.5-2 millimeters in width. The inflorescence is a dense spike of white to purplish flowers, which are tubular and five-lobed, with a length of 6-8 millimeters.

The flowers are arranged in a spiral along the spike, and they are pollinated by insects and birds.

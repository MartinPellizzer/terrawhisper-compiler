Melilotus officinalis

Melilotus officinalis, also known as sweet clover or honey clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers and edible seeds.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including its ability to reduce inflammation, improve cardiovascular health, and exhibit antispasmodic properties. The bioactive constituents of Melilotus officinalis include coumarins, particularly melilotin, as well as other flavonoids and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion, and is also used to make a sweet, fragrant syrup, often referred to as "sweet clover honey".

classification

As a member of the Fabaceae family, Melilotus officinalis shares characteristics with other legumes, such as having compound leaves and producing flowers in a specific pattern. The specific classification of Melilotus officinalis is further detailed under the subfamily Faboideae, which includes many other plant species.

Virgilia secundiflora

Virgilia secundiflora, commonly known as the white ironwood or stinkwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and respiratory infections. Bioactive constituents of Virgilia secundiflora include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used in various herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are made from the bark, leaves, and roots of the tree.

Crassula subincana

Crassula subincana, commonly known as the succulent Crassula, is a species of flowering plant in the family Crassulaceae, characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and stems.

Traditionally, the plant has been used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including anxiety, insomnia, and respiratory problems, due to its adaptogenic and expectorant properties. Crassula subincana contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is often used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are utilized in traditional medicine to promote relaxation, alleviate respiratory symptoms, and support overall well-being.

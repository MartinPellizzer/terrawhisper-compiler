What is Erica banksia subsp. comptonii?

Erica banksia subsp.

comptonii is an endemic plant species found in Western Australia. It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is a shrub-like plant with a height range of 0.1 to 0.3 meters.

The plant has a woody base and a branched habit, with leaves that are linear, lanceolate, or linear-lanceolate in shape, measuring 3-7 mm in length and 0.5-1.5 mm in width. The leaves are dark green on the upper surface and pale green or glaucous on the lower surface, with a wavy or entire margin.

The plant produces white or pink flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, with a corolla that is 5-6 mm in length.

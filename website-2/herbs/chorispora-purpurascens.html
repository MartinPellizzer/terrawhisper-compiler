Chorispora purpurascens

Chorispora purpurascens, also known as Purple False Mustard or Mustard Weed, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, commonly found in the Himalayan regions and parts of Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities, making it a potential treatment for various health conditions such as fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Chorispora purpurascens include flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is commonly used to prepare infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat colds, bronchitis, and other respiratory ailments.

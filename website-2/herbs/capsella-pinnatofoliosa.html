Capsella pinnatofoliosa

Capsella pinnatofolia, commonly known as Sheperd's Cress, is a perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its pinnatifid leaves and small white flowers.

The bioactive constituents of Capsella pinnatofolia include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Capsella pinnatofolia is commonly prepared as a tea or infusion, often combined with other herbs to enhance its medicinal properties.

Bergenia purpurascens

Bergenia purpurascens, also known as Heartleaf Bergenia, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Saxifragaceae family, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and purple flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, offering various health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Bergenia purpurascens include iridoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Bergenia is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is used to treat various health conditions, including coughs, colds, and skin irritations.

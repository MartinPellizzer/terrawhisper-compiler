Chromolaena guiengolensis

Chromolaena guiengolensis, also known as tree skeletonweed, is a shrubby plant species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, providing relief from various health issues such as fever, pain, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Chromolaena guiengolensis include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Chromolaena guiengolensis is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion, and is often used to treat fever, rheumatism, and wounds.

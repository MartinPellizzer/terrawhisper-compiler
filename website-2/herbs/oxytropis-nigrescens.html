Oxytropis nigrescens

Oxytropis nigrescens, also known as Black Locoweed, is a perennial legume plant native to the mountainous regions of North America and Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Oxytropis nigrescens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, although it is essential to note that the plant contains toxic compounds called pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be hazardous if ingested in large quantities.

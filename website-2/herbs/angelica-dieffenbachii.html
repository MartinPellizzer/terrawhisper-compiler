Angelica dieffenbachii

The bioactive constituents of Angelica dieffenbachii include volatile oils, such as bergamotene and bisabolol, as well as sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and coumarins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Angelica dieffenbachii include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to support respiratory health and immune function.

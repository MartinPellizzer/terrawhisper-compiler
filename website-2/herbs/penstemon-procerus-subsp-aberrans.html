Penstemon procerus subsp. aberrans

Penstemon procerus subsp.

aberrans, also known as the dwarf beardtongue, is a subspecies of the Penstemon procerus plant, native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, characterized by its small, tubular flowers and short stature.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which are attributed to its high concentration of flavonoids and phenolic acids.

The health benefits of Penstemon procerus subsp.

aberrans include its potential to reduce inflammation, improve wound healing, and exhibit antioxidant activity, making it a promising candidate for the development of natural remedies.

Herbal preparations of the plant can be made by infusing its leaves and flowers in hot water to create a tea, or by drying and grinding the plant material to create a powder that can be used in capsule or tincture form.

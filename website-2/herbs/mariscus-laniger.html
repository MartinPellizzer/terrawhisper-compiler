Mariscus laniger

Mariscus laniger, also known as Mempens, is a tropical plant species belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

Traditionally, Mariscus laniger has been used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin infections, due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties. The bioactive constituents of Mariscus laniger include flavonoids, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Mariscus laniger include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory infections, wounds, and skin conditions.

Cyanus willdenowii

Cyanus willdenowii, also known as the Cornflower or Bachelor's Button, is a perennial plant species belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae).

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in treating various ailments such as wounds, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Cyanus willdenowii include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat colds, coughs, and fever, as well as to promote skin and eye health.

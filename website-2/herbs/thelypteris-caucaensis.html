Thelypteris caucaensis

Thelypteris caucaensis, also known as the Cauca silver fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, characterized by its delicate fronds and silver underside.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as arthritis and skin diseases. The bioactive constituents of Thelypteris caucaensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditional herbal preparations made from Thelypteris caucaensis include teas and infusions, which are used to treat various health issues, as well as being applied topically to soothe skin irritations.

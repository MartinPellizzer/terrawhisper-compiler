Sedum imbricatum

Sedum imbricatum, also known as the 'dewy stonecrop', is a perennial succulent plant native to China, characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and stems that store water.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, wounds, and gastrointestinal issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Sedum imbricatum include flavonoids, saponins, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Sedum imbricatum is often used to make teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments to promote wound healing and reduce inflammation.

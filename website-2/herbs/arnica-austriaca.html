Arnica austriaca

Arnica austriaca, also known as mountain arnica, is a perennial plant species belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) that is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

The health benefits of Arnica austriaca include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic properties, making it a popular natural remedy for wound healing, pain relief, and skin conditions such as eczema and acne. The bioactive constituents of this plant include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Arnica austriaca is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as creams, ointments, and tinctures, which are often used to treat bruising, sprains, and muscle soreness.

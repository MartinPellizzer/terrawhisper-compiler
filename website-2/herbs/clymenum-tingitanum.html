Clymenum tingitanum

Clymenum tingitanum, also known as Cyminum or Sweet Cumin, is a plant species in the carrot family (Apiaceae) that is native to the Mediterranean region.

The bioactive constituents of Clymenum tingitanum include volatile oils, such as limonene, pinene, and caryophyllene, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

Clymenum tingitanum is often used in herbal preparations, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS).

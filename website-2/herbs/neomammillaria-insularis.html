Neomammillaria insularis

Neomammillaria insularis, commonly known as the island mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico, characterized by its small, globular stems and white to yellow flowers.

The health benefits of Neomammillaria insularis include its potential to aid in the treatment of wounds, infections, and inflammation due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Neomammillaria insularis include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to support immune function and promote overall well-being.

Ranunculus longimammiformis

Ranunculus longimammiformis, commonly known as the long-mammiformed crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including inflammation and pain, due to its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of R. longimammiformis include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with R. longimammiformis include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to alleviate symptoms of arthritis and other inflammatory conditions.

Clinopodium pulegium

Clinopodium pulegium, also known as pennyroyal, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its carminative, antispasmodic, and insecticidal properties, offering potential health benefits in relieving digestive issues, such as nausea and flatulence, as well as providing a natural repellent against insects. The bioactive constituents of Clinopodium pulegium include pulegone, limonene, and menthone, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, pennyroyal is commonly prepared as an essential oil, tea, or tincture to treat various ailments, including menstrual cramps, respiratory issues, and skin conditions.

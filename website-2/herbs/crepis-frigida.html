Crepis frigida

Crepis frigida, also known as Arctic Hawk's Beard or Frosty Hawk's Beard, is a perennial plant species belonging to the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including fever reduction and gastrointestinal disorders, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Crepis frigida include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Crepis frigida include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs.

Campanula dinarica

Campanula dinarica, also known as Dinaric bellflower, is a perennial plant species native to the Balkan Peninsula, characterized by its blue-purple flowers and hairy leaves.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making it a potential remedy for wounds, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Campanula dinarica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Campanula dinarica is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, as well as being used in the production of tinctures and ointments to leverage its therapeutic benefits.

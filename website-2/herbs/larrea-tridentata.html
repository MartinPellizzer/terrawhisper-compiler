Larrea tridentata

Larrea tridentata, also known as creosote bush, is a small, shrub-like desert plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The creosote bush has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, offering various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of conditions such as colds, flu, and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Larrea tridentata include nordihydroguaiaretic acid (NDGA), guaiacol, and creosote, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Larrea tridentata is commonly used to make teas, capsules, and tinctures, as well as topical creams and ointments, often in combination with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

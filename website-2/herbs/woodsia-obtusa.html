Woodsia obtusa

Woodsia obtusa, also known as Japanese cliff fern, is a species of fern belonging to the genus Woodsia, characterized by its small, delicate fronds and ability to thrive in rocky, shaded environments.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Woodsia obtusa include sesquiterpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbalism, Woodsia obtusa is often prepared as a tea or infused in hot water to take advantage of its health benefits, although its use should be carefully considered due to potential interactions with medications or exacerbation of underlying health conditions.

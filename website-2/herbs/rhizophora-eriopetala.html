Rhizophora eriopetala

Rhizophora eriopetala, also known as the mangrove tree, is a species of plant that belongs to the family Rhizophoraceae, characterized by its distinctive root system that allows it to thrive in coastal areas.

Bioactive constituents of Rhizophora eriopetala include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Rhizophora eriopetala include decoctions, infusions, and tinctures, often used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and gastrointestinal issues.

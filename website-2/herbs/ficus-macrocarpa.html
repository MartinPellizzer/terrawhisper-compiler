Ficus macrocarpa

Ficus macrocarpa, also known as the Banyan fig, is a species of fig tree native to Australia and Southeast Asia, characterized by its broad, spreading canopy and aerial roots.

The health benefits of Ficus macrocarpa are attributed to its ability to reduce inflammation and stress, promote relaxation, and support cardiovascular health. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, as well as alkaloids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Ficus macrocarpa are teas, capsules, and tinctures, often used to promote sleep, reduce anxiety, and alleviate symptoms of menopause.

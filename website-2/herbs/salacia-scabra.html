Salacia scabra

Salacia scabra, also known as Indian root or Salacia, is a plant species belonging to the family Celastraceae, traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including lowering blood sugar levels, aiding in weight loss, and exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Bioactive constituents of Salacia scabra include salacin, a compound similar to salicylic acid, as well as other triterpene saponins and polyphenols, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In Ayurvedic medicine, Salacia scabra is commonly prepared in the form of a churna (powder) or a decoction, often used to treat various conditions, including diabetes and obesity.

Dryopteris × holttumii

Dryopteris × holttumii is a sterile hybrid species of fern, resulting from the cross between Dryopteris crassirhizoma and Dryopteris sumatrana.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in reducing pain and inflammation, as well as promoting wound healing and immune system function. The bioactive constituents of Dryopteris × holttumii include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is often prepared as an infusion or decoction, or used in combination with other herbs to make tonics and other herbal remedies.

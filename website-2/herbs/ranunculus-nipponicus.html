Ranunculus nipponicus

Ranunculus nipponicus, also known as Japanese buttercup, is a perennial plant species native to East Asia, characterized by its bright yellow flowers and edible roots.

The roots of Ranunculus nipponicus have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in relieving pain, reducing swelling, and protecting against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus nipponicus roots include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, such as ranunculin, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the roots of Ranunculus nipponicus are typically prepared as a decoction or tincture, and are used to treat various ailments, including arthritis, fever, and digestive issues.

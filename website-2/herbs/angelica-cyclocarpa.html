Angelica cyclocarpa

Angelica cyclocarpa, also known as Korean angelica, is a type of perennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family, native to East Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and antiviral effects, making it beneficial in treating menstrual disorders, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Angelica cyclocarpa include essential oils, flavonoids, and lignans, such as ferulic acid and ligustilide, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Angelica cyclocarpa is commonly prepared as a decoction, tea, or dried extract, and is also used in combination with other herbs to create tonics and formulas for promoting health and well-being.

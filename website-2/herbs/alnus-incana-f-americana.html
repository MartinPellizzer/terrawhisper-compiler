What is Alnus incana f. americana?

Alnus incana f. americana is a taxonomic variety of the green alder tree, belonging to the family Betulaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing 10-15 meters tall, with a broad, conical crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and lanceolate, measuring 3-8 centimeters in length, with a serrated margin, and a doubly serrated apex. The flowers are small, inconspicuous, and dioecious, with male and female catkins forming on separate plants, the male catkins 2-5 centimeters long, and the female catkins 2-5 centimeters long, producing samaras (winged seeds) in late spring.

The variety typically grows in wetland areas, such as riverbanks and floodplains, in North America.

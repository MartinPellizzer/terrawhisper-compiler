Lycopodiella cernua

Lycopodiella cernua, also known as the hanging club moss or forked club moss, is a species of club moss in the family Lycopodiaceae.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antifungal properties, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as wounds, skin infections, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Lycopodiella cernua include alkaloids, lignans, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Lycopodiella cernua is often used to make infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and salves for its wound-healing and skin-soothing properties.

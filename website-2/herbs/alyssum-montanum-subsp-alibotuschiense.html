What is Alyssum montanum subsp. alibotuschiense?

Alyssum montanum subsp.

alibotuschiense is a botanical taxon that belongs to the family Brassicaceae. It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in axillary corymbs, and its narrow, linear leaves that are 1-2 mm long and 0.5-1 mm wide.

The plant grows to a height of 5-15 cm and has a taproot. The flowers are 2-3 mm in diameter and contain 4-6 petals, while the fruit is a small, elongated silique that is 5-7 mm long.

The plant's distribution is restricted to a specific area in Sardinia.

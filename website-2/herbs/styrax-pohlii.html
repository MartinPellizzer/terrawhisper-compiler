Styrax pohlii

Styrax pohlii is a species of tree in the family Styracaceae, native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, and is commonly known as the "Guaiac tree".

The resin extracted from the trunk and branches of Styrax pohlii has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, making it effective in treating wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Styrax pohlii resin include guaiac resin, which contains guaiacol, guaiac resinol, and other volatile compounds, as well as lignans and phenolic acids.

The most common herbal preparations made with Styrax pohlii include guaiac resin extracts, which are often used in topical ointments, creams, and tinctures to treat various skin and mucous membrane conditions.

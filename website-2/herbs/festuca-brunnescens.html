Festuca brunnescens

Festuca brunnescens, also known as brown fescue, is a perennial grass species that is native to Europe and North America.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help to soothe skin irritations and wounds, reduce fever, and alleviate respiratory issues such as bronchitis. The bioactive constituents of Festuca brunnescens include sesquiterpene lactones, phenolic acids, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with brown fescue include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to treat colds, fever, and digestive issues.

Medicago arcuata

Medicago arcuata, also known as curved medick, is a perennial legume native to Europe and western Asia, characterized by its curved pods and trifoliate leaves.

The plant has been found to possess various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in reducing inflammation, fighting infections, and protecting against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Medicago arcuata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Common herbal preparations made with curved medick include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin conditions.

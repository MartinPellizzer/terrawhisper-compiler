Lindera robusta

Lindera robusta, also known as Indian spice tree or kalaunghat, is a medicinal plant species belonging to the family Lauraceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including digestive issues, fever, and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Lindera robusta include lignans, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Lindera robusta is commonly prepared as a decoction, infusion, or powder, and is used to treat conditions such as bronchitis, cough, and sore throat.

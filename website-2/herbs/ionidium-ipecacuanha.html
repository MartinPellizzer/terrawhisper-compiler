Ionidium ipecacuanha

Ionidium ipecacuanha, also known as ipecac, is a plant species native to South America that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine.

The health benefits of this plant include its use as an emetic to induce vomiting, as well as its ability to treat digestive issues such as diarrhea and nausea. The bioactive constituents of Ionidium ipecacuanha include alkaloids, particularly emetine and cephaline, which are responsible for its emetic properties.

Traditionally, ipecac is prepared in the form of a tincture, syrup, or powder, and is often used in herbal medicine to treat various gastrointestinal disorders.

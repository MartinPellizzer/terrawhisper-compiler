Fumana thymifolia

Fumana thymifolia, also known as creeping cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and creeping habit.

Traditionally, Fumana thymifolia has been used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and respiratory problems, due to its reported health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Fumana thymifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoid saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Fumana thymifolia is commonly prepared as a tea or tincture, and is also used in combination with other herbs to treat coughs and colds, as well as to promote digestion and reduce inflammation.

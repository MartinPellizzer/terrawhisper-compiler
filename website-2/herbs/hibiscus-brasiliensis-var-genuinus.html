What is Hibiscus brasiliensis var. genuinus?

Hibiscus brasiliensis var. genuinus is an infraspecific taxon of the Hibiscus brasiliensis species, belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is a perennial shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 10 meters in height, with a woody stem and a dense, green, ovate to elliptic leaf. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, measuring 10-30 centimeters in length and 5-15 centimeters in width. The flowers are showy, funnel-shaped, and white to pale yellow in color, with five petals and a prominent stamen; they are produced in a terminal inflorescence.

The fruit is a capsule, containing numerous small seeds, and is a characteristic feature of the Malvaceae family.

What are the therapeutic properties this herb?

The main therapeutic properties of Hibiscus brasiliensis var. genuinus are antidiabetic, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant.

Antimicrobial properties are due to the volatile oils, particularly β-pinene and β-phellandrene, which exhibit bactericidal and fungicidal activity against a range of microorganisms.

Antioxidant properties are attributed to the polyphenolic compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, which scavenge free radicals and protect against oxidative stress.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Hibiscus brasiliensis var. genuinus are hibiscus tea, hibiscus syrup, cosmetic infusion, hibiscus infusion, and decoction.

Hibiscus tea is a popular beverage prepared by infusing dried flowers in hot water, rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. Hibiscus syrup is a sweetener made by boiling the flowers in water and sugar, used as a natural sweetener and flavoring agent. The cosmetic infusion is a topical preparation used for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, often incorporated into creams, lotions, and ointments for skin care.

Decoction is a longer extraction method used for its medicinal properties, involving boiling the flowers in water multiple times to release its active compounds.

What are the possible side effects of using this herb improperly?

The possible side effects of using Hibiscus brasiliensis var. genuinus improperly are numerous and can be severe.

Improper use can interact with medications such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, leading to adverse reactions. Additionally, Hibiscus brasiliensis var. genuinus can cause hypotension due to its diuretic properties, which can be problematic for individuals with pre-existing low blood pressure. It can also exacerbate diarrhea in some individuals, particularly those with gastrointestinal issues, and induce respiratory depression in rare cases, potentially life-threatening.

Furthermore, improper use can cause nausea and vomiting due to its emetic properties, which can lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalances.

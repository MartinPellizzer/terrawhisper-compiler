Pueraria montana

Pueraria montana, also known as Kudzu, is a perennial vine native to East Asia and widely known for its invasive properties.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including reducing inflammation, improving cardiovascular health, and lowering cholesterol levels due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Pueraria montana include isoflavones, particularly daidzein and genistein, as well as other compounds such as coumarins, saponins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Pueraria montana include teas, capsules, and extracts, often used to support cardiovascular health, manage menopausal symptoms, and promote weight loss.

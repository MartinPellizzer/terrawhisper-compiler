Fragaria hortensis

Fragaria hortensis, commonly known as wild strawberry or wood strawberry, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its small, edible fruits and fragrant flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which are attributed to its ability to reduce stress and improve cardiovascular health. The bioactive constituents of Fragaria hortensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and ellagic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

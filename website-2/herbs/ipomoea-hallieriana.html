Ipomoea hallieriana

Ipomoea hallieriana is a type of plant in the morning glory family, known for its climbing habit and trumpet-shaped flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, which may aid in wound healing, reducing oxidative stress, and preventing infections. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea hallieriana include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds, such as conophylline and convalatoxin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Ipomoea hallieriana include teas, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat a range of health issues, including skin conditions, fever, and respiratory problems.

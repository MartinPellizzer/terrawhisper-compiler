Astragalus cinerascens

Astragalus cinerascens, also known as Cinerascens milkvetch, is a type of perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

This plant has been traditionally used in medicine for its adaptogenic and immunomodulatory properties, providing health benefits such as enhanced immune function, improved cardiovascular health, and reduced stress levels. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus cinerascens include triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Astragalus cinerascens include capsules, tablets, and teas, often used to support overall well-being and boost the immune system.

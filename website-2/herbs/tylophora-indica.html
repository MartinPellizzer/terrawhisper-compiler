Tylophora indica

Tylophora indica is a perennial climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Asia, belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory diseases, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Tylophora indica include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which have been found to exhibit immunomodulatory and anti-cancer activities.

In traditional medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a decoction or a powder, and is also used in herbal teas, tablets, and capsules to treat various health ailments.

Asclepias laurentiana

Asclepias laurentiana, also known as the Laurentian milkweed, is a perennial plant species native to the southeastern United States.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, making it effective in treating wounds, infections, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Asclepias laurentiana include cardenolides, alkaloids, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are tinctures, infusions, and salves, which are often used to treat skin conditions, such as eczema and acne, and to promote wound healing.

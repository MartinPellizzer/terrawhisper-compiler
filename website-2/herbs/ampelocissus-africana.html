Ampelocissus africana

Ampelocissus africana is a climbing plant species native to Africa, belonging to the Vitaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been shown to aid in treating fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Ampelocissus africana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is also used to make tinctures and ointments for topical application.

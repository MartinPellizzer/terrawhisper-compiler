Nemacladus breviflorus

Nemacladus breviflorus, also known as California threadplant, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and Mexico.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including treating respiratory issues and soothing digestive problems due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects, which provide relief from coughs, colds, and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Nemacladus breviflorus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Nemacladus breviflorus is commonly prepared as a tincture or a tea, and sometimes combined with other herbs to create a cough syrup or expectorant.

Grammitis militaris

Grammitis militaris, also known as Military Shield Fern, is a species of fern in the family Polypodiaceae, characterized by its unique shield-shaped fronds.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties, making it useful in treating various health conditions such as wound healing, fever reduction, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of G. militaris include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, G. militaris is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical ointments and creams for its therapeutic benefits.

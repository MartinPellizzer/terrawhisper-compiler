Pleopeltis tenuicauda

Pleopeltis tenuicauda, also known as the Mexican polypody, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Central and South America.

This plant is prized for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help to soothe wounds, reduce fever, and protect against infections. The bioactive constituents of Pleopeltis tenuicauda include sesquiterpenoids, phenolic acids, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the Mexican polypody is commonly used in teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as in topical applications, such as salves and creams, to promote healing and alleviate various health conditions.

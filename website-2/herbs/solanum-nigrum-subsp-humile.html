What is Solanum nigrum subsp. humile?

Solanum nigrum subsp.

humile is a perennial herb native to East Asia, belonging to the family Solanaceae. It has a woody base, with prostrate or ascending stems up to 50 cm long, and elliptical leaves up to 4 cm long and 2 cm wide, with a pubescent lower surface and a glabrous upper surface.

The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in a corymb, with a 5-lobed calyx and a corolla with a 5-lobed limb. The fruit is a globose berry, up to 6 mm in diameter, with a glabrous surface and containing 2-4 seeds.

The plant is often confused with other Solanum species, but can be distinguished by its distinctive leaf and fruit characteristics.

Moringa oleifera

Moringa oleifera is a multipurpose plant, commonly known as the drumstick tree or horseradish tree, native to the Indian subcontinent and widely cultivated for its edible leaves, pods, seeds, and oil.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to its numerous health benefits, including reducing inflammation, lowering blood sugar levels, and exhibiting antioxidant and anti-cancer properties. The bioactive constituents of Moringa oleifera include isothiocyanates, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Moringa oleifera is commonly used to make various herbal preparations, including tea, capsules, and oil, as well as being used in traditional dishes and as a nutritional supplement.

classification

Moringa oleifera is classified as a dicot tree, belonging to the family Moringaceae. It is native to the Indian subcontinent and is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions. The genus Moringa is further divided into several species, with Moringa oleifera being one of the most widely distributed and used species. Moringa oleifera is a member of the order Brassicales and is closely related to other plants in this order, such as mustard and cabbage. The plant's classification reflects its unique characteristics and adaptations to its native environment.

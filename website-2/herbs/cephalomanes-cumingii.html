Cephalomanes cumingii

Cephalomanes cumingii, also known as the Cuming's lip fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including treating fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Cephalomanes cumingii include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often used to make decoctions, infusions, and ointments, which are used to treat a range of health issues, including skin conditions, wounds, and respiratory problems.

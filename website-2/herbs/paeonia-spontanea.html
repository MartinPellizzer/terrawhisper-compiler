Paeonia spontanea

Paeonia spontanea, also known as the wild paeony or Korean peony, is a perennial plant species native to East Asia, characterized by its large, showy flowers and medicinal properties.

The roots and flowers of Paeonia spontanea have been used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and sedative properties, which make it a potential treatment for conditions such as anxiety, insomnia, and menstrual cramps. The bioactive constituents of Paeonia spontanea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, such as paeoniflorin and paeonol, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Paeonia spontanea is commonly prepared into teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being used in combination with other herbs in formulae to treat a range of health conditions.

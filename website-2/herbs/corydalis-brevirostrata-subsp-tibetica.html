What is Corydalis brevirostrata subsp. tibetica?

Corydalis brevirostrata subsp.

tibetica is a plant species within the family Papaveraceae, native to the Qinghai-Xizang Plateau in China. It is a perennial herb with a height range of 10-30 cm, characterized by a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem with a pair of bracteoles.

The leaves are oblong-elliptic, 5-10 cm long, and have an entire margin, while the flowers are pale yellow, with a 4-lobed calyx and a 4-petaled corolla. The fruit is a capsule, 1.5-2.5 cm long, containing numerous seeds.

The plant blooms from July to August, and its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects.

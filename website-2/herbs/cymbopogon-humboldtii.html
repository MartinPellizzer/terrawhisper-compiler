Cymbopogon humboldtii

Cymbopogon humboldtii, commonly known as lemongrass, is a tropical grass plant native to Central and South America, characterized by its citrusy fragrance and slender leaves.

The plant is known for its various health benefits, including its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which can help to alleviate digestive issues, reduce fever, and boost the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Cymbopogon humboldtii include citral, geraniol, and limonene, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, lemongrass is used in herbal preparations such as teas, essential oils, and infusions, which are used to promote relaxation, relieve stress, and support overall well-being.

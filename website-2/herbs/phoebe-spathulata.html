Phoebe spathulata

Phoebe spathulata, also known as Chinese silverbell or Chinese phoebe, is a deciduous tree species native to China and Southeast Asia, belonging to the family Lauraceae.

This plant has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antioxidant properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Phoebe spathulata include lignans, flavonoids, and alkaloids, such as phylligenin and phylligenin-3'-methyl ether, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Phoebe spathulata is often used to make tea, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into herbal formulas to promote respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of the common cold.

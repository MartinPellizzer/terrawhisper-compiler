Hypolepis chilensis

Hypolepis chilensis, also known as Chilean maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America, characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds and creeping rhizomes.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which can help to soothe digestive issues, reduce fever, and promote wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Hypolepis chilensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Hypolepis chilensis is often prepared as a decoction, tea, or tincture, and is commonly used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as to support immune function.

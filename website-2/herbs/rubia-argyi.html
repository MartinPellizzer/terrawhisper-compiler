Rubia argyi

Rubia argyi, also known as Chinese madder or Hong Ren, is a perennial plant native to China that belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is traditionally used as a medicinal herb.

The bioactive constituents of Rubia argyi include anthraquinones, flavonoids, and alkaloids, particularly emodin, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Rubia argyi is commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine and is often prepared as a decoction, tea, or dried extract, as well as being formulated into capsules or tablets for modern herbal medicine.

Orobanche violacea

Orobanche violacea, also known as the Violet Broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that obtains its nutrients by connecting to the roots of host plants, typically those from the viola and gentian genera.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, respiratory problems, and digestive issues, due to its reported anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Orobanche violacea include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Orobanche violacea include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to treat colds, coughs, and bronchitis.

Euphorbia austrina

Euphorbia austrina, also known as the Northern Spur George, is a succulent plant species native to Australia.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Aboriginal medicine, where it is used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Euphorbia austrina include euphorbol, a triterpenoid compound with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

In traditional medicine, Euphorbia austrina is often prepared as a tea or tincture, and is also used in topical applications to soothe skin conditions and wounds.

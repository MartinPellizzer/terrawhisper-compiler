Lens himalayensis

Lens himalayensis, also known as Himalayan lentil or Indian butter pea, is a perennial legume native to the Himalayan region.

The plant is rich in bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which have been found to exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, contributing to its potential health benefits, such as reducing inflammation, improving cardiovascular health, and boosting the immune system. The bioactive constituents of L. himalayensis have been identified to include isorhapontigenin, 2-hydroxybenzoic acid, and kaempferol, which may be responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, L. himalayensis is used in various herbal preparations, including decoctions, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including digestive issues and fever.

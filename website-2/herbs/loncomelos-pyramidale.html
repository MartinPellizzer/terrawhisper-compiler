Loncomelos pyramidale

Loncomelos pyramidale, also known as Guanabira, is a plant species native to the Andean region of South America, belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal problems, due to its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Loncomelos pyramidale include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Loncomelos pyramidale include tea infusions, infusions of the leaves and flowers in hot water, and sometimes used as an ingredient in traditional medicinal formulations, such as salves and tinctures.

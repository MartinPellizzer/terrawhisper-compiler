Carum ajawain

Carum ajwain, also known as Trachyspermum ammi, is a small annual herb native to India and the Middle East, belonging to the Apiaceae family.

The health benefits of Carum ajwain include relief from digestive issues, such as bloating and gas, as well as its ability to reduce inflammation and alleviate respiratory problems like asthma and bronchitis. The bioactive constituents of Carum ajwain include essential oils like thymol, limonene, and pinene, as well as flavonoids and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Carum ajwain is used in various herbal preparations, including teas, decoctions, and infusions, as well as in the form of essential oil, which is used to flavor food, aid digestion, and alleviate respiratory issues.

classification

Carum ajawain is classified as a species of flowering plant in the carrot family (Apiaceae). It is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and the Indian subcontinent. Carum ajawain is also known as ajwain or bishop's weed. The plant is further classified within the genus Carum and the family Apiaceae. Within the plant kingdom, it belongs to the kingdom Plantae and the phylum Magnoliophyta.

Additionally, pregnant or breastfeeding women should use caution and consult a healthcare professional before consuming Carum ajawain.

Toxicodendron incisum

Toxicodendron incisum, also known as the Japanese lacquer tree or Japanese poison oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia, characterized by its shiny, lacquer-like sap and compound leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antifungal properties, which can aid in treating various skin conditions, wounds, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Toxicodendron incisum include resins, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is commonly used to make lacquer-based preparations, such as lacquer plasters and ointments, to treat skin conditions and promote wound healing.

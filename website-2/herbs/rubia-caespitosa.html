Rubia caespitosa

Rubia caespitosa is a perennial plant species belonging to the Rubiaceae family, commonly known as the madder plant.

The bioactive constituents of Rubia caespitosa include anthraquinones, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Rubia caespitosa include root infusions, decoctions, and dried root powder, which can be used to treat a range of health issues, including digestive problems and skin conditions.

Argyreia uniflora

Argyreia uniflora, also known as the Malabar coast creeper or moonflower, is a perennial climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities, which may help alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Argyreia uniflora include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Argyreia uniflora is commonly prepared into teas, infusions, and tinctures to address various health conditions, making it a popular ingredient in Ayurvedic and Unani formulations.

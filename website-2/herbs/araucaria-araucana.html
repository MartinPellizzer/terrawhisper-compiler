Araucaria araucana

Araucaria araucana, also known as the Monkey Puzzle tree, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Chile and Argentina.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, and its health benefits include anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in the treatment of various ailments such as respiratory issues and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Araucaria araucana include essential oils, terpenes, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

classification

The Araucaria araucana is classified as a coniferous tree and is further classified within the division Pinophyta. It is also known as the monkey puzzle tree due to its unique and complex branching pattern.

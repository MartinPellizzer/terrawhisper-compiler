Plantago thunbergii

Plantago thunbergii, also known as Japanese ribwort or Japanese plantain, is a perennial plant species native to East Asia, belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including diarrhea, dysentery, and inflammation, due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and astringent properties. The bioactive constituents of Plantago thunbergii include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal preparations, Plantago thunbergii is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into traditional Chinese medicine and Japanese herbal remedies.

Valeranda fruticosa

Valeriana officinalis, commonly known as Valerian or Valerian root, is a perennial flowering plant that is native to Europe and Asia, known for its distinctive, sweet fragrance and sedative properties.

The plant has been used for centuries to promote relaxation, improve sleep quality, and alleviate stress and anxiety due to its ability to interact with the GABA receptors in the brain, leading to a calming effect. The bioactive constituents of Valerian root include valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates, which contribute to its sedative and anxiolytic properties.

Valerian root is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, capsules, and supplements, often in combination with other herbs like lemon balm and lavender to enhance its sleep-promoting effects.

Astragalus glaber

Astragalus glaber, also known as milk vetch or Chinese astragalus, is a perennial plant species native to China and the Himalayas, belonging to the legume family (Fabaceae).

The roots of Astragalus glaber have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to promote health and longevity, with reported health benefits including immune system support, anti-inflammatory properties, and antioxidant effects. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus glaber include polysaccharides, flavonoids, and saponins, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

Astragalus glaber is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, capsules, and tinctures, often in combination with other herbs to enhance its effects.

Lactuca sikkimensis

Lactuca sikkimensis, also known as Sikkim milk thistle or Himalayan prickly lettuce, is a plant species belonging to the dandelion family (Asteraceae) that is native to the Himalayan region.

It has been traditionally used in medicine to treat a variety of health issues, including liver and kidney problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and hepatoprotective properties. The bioactive constituents of Lactuca sikkimensis include lactucarium, a sesquiterpene lactone, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and capsules, which are used to support liver health and alleviate symptoms of indigestion and anxiety.

Christolea kashgarica

Christolea kashgarica, also known as Kashgar's Christolea, is a perennial plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

The plant is prized for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory effects, which may help alleviate conditions such as arthritis, high blood pressure, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Christolea kashgarica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Christolea kashgarica is commonly prepared into teas, infusions, and capsules, as well as used in combination with other herbs in formulas aimed at promoting overall well-being and enhancing immune function.

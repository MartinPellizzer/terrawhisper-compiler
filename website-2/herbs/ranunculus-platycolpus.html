Ranunculus platycolpus

Ranunculus platycolpus, also known as the broad-leaved ranunculus, is a plant species belonging to the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae) characterized by its delicate white or pink flowers and broad, flat leaves.

Bioactive constituents of R. platycolpus include alkaloids, glycosides, and essential oils, such as ranunculin and ranunculoside, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used in herbal preparations, including infusions, tinctures, and salves, which are used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and minor infections.

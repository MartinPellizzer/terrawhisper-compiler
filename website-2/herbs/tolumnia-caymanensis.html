Tolumnia caymanensis

Tolumnia caymanensis, also known as the Cayman Island orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean island of Grand Cayman.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its potential to reduce inflammation, alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues such as asthma, and act as an antibacterial agent. The bioactive constituents of Tolumnia caymanensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often employed in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions.

Luciola sylvatica

Luciola sylvatica, also known as fireflies plant or firefly bush, is a type of plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its bright, glowing flowers that resemble fireflies.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin infections, due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Luciola sylvatica include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Luciola sylvatica include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health issues, including respiratory problems and digestive disorders.

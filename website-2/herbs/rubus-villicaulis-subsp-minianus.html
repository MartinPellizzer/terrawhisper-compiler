What is Rubus villicaulis subsp. minianus?

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

minianus is a subspecies of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae. It is a bramble, characterized by thorny stems, and its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, measuring 5-15 cm in length.

The plant produces white flowers in mid to late summer, which are followed by red berries, 8-10 mm in diameter. The subspecies grows up to 2 m in height, with a woody base and herbaceous stems.

Its distribution is limited to the southeastern regions of Asia.

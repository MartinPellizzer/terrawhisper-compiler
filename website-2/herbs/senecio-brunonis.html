Senecio brunonis

Senecio brunonis, commonly known as Brunon's ragwort or Brunon's groundsel, is a perennial plant species belonging to the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

Traditionally, Senecio brunonis has been used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin disorders, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Senecio brunonis include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Senecio brunonis is often used to prepare teas, tinctures, and infusions, although it is essential to note that excessive consumption may lead to hepatotoxicity due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids (PAs).

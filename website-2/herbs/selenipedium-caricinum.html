Selenipedium caricinum

Selenipedium caricinum, also known as the Indian pipe orchid, is a unique, saprotrophic orchid species that derives its nutrients from decaying organic matter rather than through photosynthesis.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its potential to lower blood pressure, reduce inflammation, and alleviate respiratory issues such as bronchitis and coughs, due to its expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Selenipedium caricinum include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal activities.

Selenipedium caricinum is often used in traditional herbal medicine to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat respiratory issues and other health conditions.

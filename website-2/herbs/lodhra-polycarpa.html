Lodhra polycarpa

Lodhra polycarpa, also known as Symplocos cochinchinensis, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical forests of Asia.

The health benefits of Lodhra polycarpa include its ability to help alleviate menstrual disorders, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial properties, making it a valuable remedy in traditional Ayurvedic medicine. The bioactive constituents of Lodhra polycarpa include flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Lodhra polycarpa is often prepared in the form of decoctions, infusions, and powder, with the most common preparations being Lodhra guggul and Lodhra powder, which are used to treat various women's health issues.

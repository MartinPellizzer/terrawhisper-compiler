Calamintha georgiana

Calamintha georgiana, also known as sweet calamint, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which are beneficial in treating digestive issues, soothing headaches, and reducing stress. The bioactive constituents of Calamintha georgiana include essential oils such as borneol, camphor, and bornyl acetate, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Calamintha georgiana include teas, tinctures, and infused oils, which are often used to promote relaxation and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and digestive disorders.

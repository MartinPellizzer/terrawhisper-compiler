Mimulus parviflorus

Mimulus parviflorus, also known as small-flowered monkey flower, is a perennial plant species native to North America, belonging to the family Phrymaceae.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities, making it useful in treating various health conditions such as skin irritations, wounds, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Mimulus parviflorus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Mimulus parviflorus is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, often in combination with other herbs like echinacea and goldenseal, to support immune function and overall well-being.

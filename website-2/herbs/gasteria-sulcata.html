Gasteria sulcata

Gasteria sulcata, also known as the grooved Gasteria, is a low-growing, succulent plant species native to the Western Cape province of South Africa, belonging to the family Asphodelaceae.

Traditionally, Gasteria sulcata has been used to treat various health issues, including digestive problems, respiratory infections, and inflammation, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which have been shown to have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.

In herbal medicine, Gasteria sulcata is commonly used in teas, tinctures, and infusions to promote digestion, relieve respiratory issues, and support immune function.

What is Daucus carota subsp. mauritanicus?

Daucus carota subsp.

mauritanicus is a subspecies of wild carrot, belonging to the family Apiaceae. It is a biennial or annual herb, characterized by a single stem, 30-150 cm tall, with leaves that are pinnate or bipinnate, 10-30 cm long, and a compound umbel of small, white flowers.

The flowers are arranged in a 2-5 cm diameter umbel, with 5-15 rays, each containing 5-15 flowers. The fruit is an elliptical or ovoid schizocarp, 5-7 mm long, with 3-5 mericarps. Daucus carota subsp.

mauritanicus is native to North Africa and the Mediterranean region.

The main herbal preparations of Daucus carota subsp.

Carrot tincture and infusion, made by extracting the roots in a solvent like ethanol or water, are used internally for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

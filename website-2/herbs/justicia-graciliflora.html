Justicia graciliflora

Justicia graciliflora, also known as "Queen's Wreath" or "Prairie Bells", is a flowering plant native to North America, characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers and slender stems.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections, such as bronchitis and pneumonia. The bioactive constituents of Justicia graciliflora include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Justicia graciliflora is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for its purported skin-soothing and wound-healing effects.

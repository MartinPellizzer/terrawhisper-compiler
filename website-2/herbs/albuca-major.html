Albuca major

Albuca major, commonly known as the white arum or giant white arum lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Albuca major include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Albuca major include tinctures, infusions, and ointments, which are used to treat conditions such as wounds, skin infections, and respiratory problems.

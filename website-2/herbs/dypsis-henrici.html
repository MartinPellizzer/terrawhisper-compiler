Dypsis henrici

Dypsis henrici, also known as the 'Pachouli Palm', is a species of palm tree native to Madagascar, whose stem and leaves are used as a medicinal plant.

The health benefits of Dypsis henrici include its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties, which have been traditionally used to relieve pain, reduce inflammation, and treat wounds. The bioactive constituents of Dypsis henrici include sesquiterpenes, particularly pachouli oil, which contains a high concentration of patchoulone and patchoulol, as well as other compounds like beta-eudesmol and gamma-eudesmol.

The most common herbal preparations made with Dypsis henrici include essential oils, tinctures, and extracts, which are used in aromatherapy, skincare products, and as a natural remedy for various health conditions.

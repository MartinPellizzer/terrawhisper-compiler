Pinus variabilis

Pinus variabilis, also known as Japanese white pine or Korean pine, is a species of evergreen tree in the pine family (Pinaceae).

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which may help to alleviate respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as skin conditions like acne and eczema. The bioactive constituents of Pinus variabilis include terpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Pinus variabilis is typically prepared as a decoction or infusion, and is also used in the form of tea, capsules, or tinctures to promote respiratory health and alleviate various inflammatory conditions.

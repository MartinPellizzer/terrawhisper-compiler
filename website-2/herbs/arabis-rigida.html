Arabis rigida

Arabis rigida, also known as rock cress or alpine rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Arabis rigida include isothiocyanates, such as glucosinolates, which have been shown to exhibit antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anticancer activities.

The most common herbal preparations made with Arabis rigida include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are often used to relieve coughs, sore throats, and other respiratory issues.

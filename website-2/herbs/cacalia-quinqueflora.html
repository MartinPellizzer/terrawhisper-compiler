Cacalia quinqueflora

Cacalia quinqueflora, also known as Japanese artichoke or yakoni, is a perennial plant in the family Asteraceae, native to East Asia and known for its edible stems and leaves.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include inulin, a type of fructan with prebiotic properties, as well as caffeic acid and other phenolic compounds with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

Cacalia quinqueflora is commonly used in traditional herbal preparations, including infusions, decoctions, and extracts, which are often consumed as teas or used as supplements to promote digestive health and immune function.

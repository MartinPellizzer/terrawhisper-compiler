Dombeya montana

Dombeya montana, also known as Mountain Dombeya or African Wild Pear, is a medicinal plant species native to tropical Africa, belonging to the Malvaceae family.

The plant is valued for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Dombeya montana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Dombeya montana is often used to prepare decoctions, infusions, and ointments, with the most common herbal preparations being a tea made from the leaves and bark, and a decoction used to treat fever and rheumatism.

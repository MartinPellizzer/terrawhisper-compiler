Campanula sessilis

Campanula sessilis, also known as bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, characterized by its delicate bell-shaped flowers and hairy stems.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving respiratory health, and exhibiting antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Campanula sessilis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is made into teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various ailments, including coughs, colds, and digestive issues.

Monsonia emarginata

Monsonia emarginata, also known as Devil's claw, is a perennial plant species belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

The plant is valued for its health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as arthritis, pain, and digestive issues. Bioactive constituents of Monsonia emarginata include iridoid glycosides, such as harpagoside, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The plant is commonly used to prepare teas, capsules, and tinctures, making it a popular herbal remedy in traditional medicine.

Isodon nigrescens

Isodon nigrescens, also known as Black Root, is a perennial plant species in the Lamiaceae family, commonly found in mountainous regions of China, Japan, and Korea.

The bioactive constituents of I. nigrescens include phenolic acids, flavonoids, and triterpenoid saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is often used to make herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, which are used to treat respiratory and gastrointestinal disorders.

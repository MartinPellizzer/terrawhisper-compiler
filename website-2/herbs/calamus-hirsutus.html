Calamus hirsutus

Calamus hirsutus, also known as sweet flag, is a perennial aquatic plant that belongs to the Zingiberaceae family.

It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat various health conditions, including digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin irritations, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Calamus hirsutus include sesquiterpenes, particularly curcumenol and a- and b-bisabolol, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Calamus hirsutus include teas, tinctures, and essential oils, which are used to relieve anxiety, promote digestion, and soothe skin conditions.

Thymus bulgaricus

Thymus bulgaricus, also known as Bulgarian thyme, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family Lamiaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering various health benefits such as antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects, which can aid in reducing stress, improving respiratory issues, and boosting the immune system. The bioactive constituents of Thymus bulgaricus include essential oils, particularly thymol, carvacrol, and eugenol, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Thymus bulgaricus is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being added to traditional remedies like essential oil blends and salves.

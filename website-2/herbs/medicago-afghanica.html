Medicago afghanica

Medicago afghanica, also known as Afghan lucerne or Afghan medick, is a perennial legume plant native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and the surrounding areas.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as cardiovascular disease and cancer. The bioactive constituents of Medicago afghanica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Medicago afghanica include teas, infusions, and capsules, which are used to support digestive health and reduce inflammation in the body.

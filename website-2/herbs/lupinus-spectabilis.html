Lupinus spectabilis

Lupinus spectabilis, also known as the desert lupine, is a perennial plant species native to the southwestern United States and Mexico.

The desert lupine has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its health benefits, which include reducing inflammation and alleviating symptoms of respiratory issues such as asthma and bronchitis. The bioactive constituents of Lupinus spectabilis include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with desert lupine include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu.

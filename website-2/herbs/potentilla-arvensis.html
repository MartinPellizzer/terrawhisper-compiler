Potentilla arvensis

Potentilla arvensis, also known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial plant in the rose family (Rosaceae) that is native to Europe and Asia and has been naturalized in North America.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which may help to soothe digestive issues, reduce fever, and promote wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Potentilla arvensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoid saponins, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Potentilla arvensis is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion, and is used to treat a range of conditions, including fever, digestive issues, and skin problems.

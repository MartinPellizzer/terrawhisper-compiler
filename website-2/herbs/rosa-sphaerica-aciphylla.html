Rosa sphaerica aciphylla

Rosa sphaerica aciphylla, also known as the wild rose or rosehip, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and Western Asia, belonging to the family Rosaceae.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in reducing the severity of colds, flu, and other respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Rosa sphaerica aciphylla include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and vitamins, particularly vitamin C, which is known for its immune-boosting and antioxidant properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include rosehip tea, rosehip oil, and rosehip powder, which are often used in traditional medicine and as dietary supplements.

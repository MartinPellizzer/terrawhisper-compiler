Amyema haematodes

Amyema haematodes, also known as the Red-stemmed Mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant species native to northern Australia and New Guinea.

It is valued for its traditional medicinal uses, with reported health benefits including the treatment of fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due in part to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Bioactive constituents of Amyema haematodes include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Amyema haematodes include teas, infusions, and tinctures, although its use in modern medicine remains largely anecdotal and further research is needed to confirm its efficacy and safety.

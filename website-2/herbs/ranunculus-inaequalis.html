Ranunculus inaequalis

Ranunculus inaequalis, also known as Inequality Buttercup, is a perennial plant species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, offering potential health benefits in the treatment of various conditions, including wounds, skin irritations, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus inaequalis include alkaloids, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Ranunculus inaequalis is often prepared as a tincture, infusion, or salve, and is used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and infections, as well as to reduce inflammation and promote healing.

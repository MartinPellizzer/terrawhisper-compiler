Drosera monantha

Drosera monantha, commonly known as the single-petaled sundew, is a carnivorous plant species native to Australia and Tasmania, belonging to the Droseraceae family.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include iridoid glycosides, specifically secologanin and loganin, as well as other alkaloids and flavonoids.

Traditionally, Drosera monantha is used to make herbal preparations such as tinctures, teas, and syrups, often used to treat respiratory complaints and other conditions.

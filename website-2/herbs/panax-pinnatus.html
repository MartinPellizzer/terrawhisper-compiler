Panax pinnatus

Panax pinnatus, also known as Chinese ginseng or Jang-san, is a perennial plant species belonging to the genus Panax in the family Araliaceae.

It has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to promote vitality, reduce fatigue, and alleviate various health conditions, including fever, diarrhea, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Panax pinnatus include ginsenosides, flavonoids, and saponins, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Panax pinnatus is often prepared as a decoction or tonic, as well as being used to make various herbal teas, capsules, and extracts.

Ciminalis × digenea

Ciminalis × digenea, also known as Wood's Cinnamon or Cinnamon Rose, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the genus Ciminalis, a group of flowering plants in the heath family (Ericaceae).

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it useful in the treatment of various conditions such as wounds, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Ciminalis × digenea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Chamaemelum aureum

Chamaemelum aureum, also known as golden chamomile or dog fennel, is a low-growing, perennial herb belonging to the daisy family.

The plant is valued for its calming effects and has been used to treat various health issues, including anxiety, insomnia, and digestive problems, due to its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Chamaemelum aureum include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and volatile oils, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Golden chamomile is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and topical creams, and is also incorporated into herbal sleep aids and calming supplements due to its soothing effects.

Chamaemelum aureum is further classified as a type of dandelion, specifically within the Chamaemelum genus.

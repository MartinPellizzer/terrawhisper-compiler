Asclepias cucullata

Asclepias cucullata, also known as the cup plant, is a perennial plant native to North America, belonging to the milkweed family (Apocynaceae).

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in wound healing and the treatment of various skin conditions. Bioactive constituents of Asclepias cucullata include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and salves, which are often employed in traditional medicine to treat skin irritations and promote tissue repair.

Lupinus amabayensis

Lupinus amabayensis, also known as the Andean lupin, is a species of perennial flowering plant within the legume family Fabaceae.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, as well as its ability to lower cholesterol levels and aid in weight management. The bioactive constituents of Lupinus amabayensis include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and capsules, making it a popular ingredient in herbal supplements and remedies, particularly for cardiovascular and metabolic health support.

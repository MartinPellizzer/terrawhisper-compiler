Ipomoea japonica

Ipomoea japonica, also known as Japanese morning glory, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia, characterized by its vibrant purple flowers and twining stems.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which are thought to aid in the treatment of various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea japonica include flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ipomoea japonica is often prepared as a decoction, tea, or tincture, and is also used in combination with other herbs to create formulas for various health conditions.

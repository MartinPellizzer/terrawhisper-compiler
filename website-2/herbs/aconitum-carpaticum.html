Aconitum carpaticum

Aconitum carpaticum, also known as Carpathian monk's hood, is a species of flowering plant in the Aconitum genus, commonly found in the Carpathian Mountains.

Traditionally, Aconitum carpaticum has been used for its medicinal properties, offering relief from pain and inflammation, as well as being used as an antispasmodic and anticonvulsant. The bioactive constituents of this plant include alkaloids, such as aconitine, mesaconitine, and hypaconitine, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Aconitum carpaticum is often used in herbal preparations, including tinctures, infusions, and ointments, which are typically used to treat various health conditions, including arthritis and nerve pain.

Coptis ramosa

Coptis ramosa, also known as Chinese goldthread, is a perennial plant species in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral effects, contributing to its use in the treatment of various health conditions such as fever, colds, and gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of Coptis ramosa include berberine, coptisine, and epiberberine, among other alkaloids responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Coptis ramosa is commonly prepared as a decoction, powder, or tincture, often combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

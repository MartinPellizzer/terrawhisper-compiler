Madhuca perakensis

Madhuca perakensis, also known as Perak mahua, is a deciduous tree species native to the tropical forests of Malaysia and Singapore.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities, which may aid in the treatment of various diseases such as fever, digestive issues, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Madhuca perakensis include triterpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make various herbal preparations, including infusions, decoctions, and extracts, which are often used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory issues, as well as to promote digestive health.

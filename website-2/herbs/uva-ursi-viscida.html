Uva-ursi viscida

Uva-ursi viscida, also known as bearberry, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe, North America, and Asia.

The plant is valued for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular natural remedy for urinary tract infections, diarrhea, and skin irritations. Uva-ursi viscida contains bioactive constituents such as arbutin, hydroquinone, and ursolic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into herbal creams and ointments to treat skin conditions and promote wound healing.

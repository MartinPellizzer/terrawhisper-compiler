Neottia sinensis

Neottia sinensis, also known as the Chinese tooth-axil orchid, is a type of orchid that belongs to the genus Neottia, characterized by its unique, leafless stems and nodding, orchid-like flowers.

Traditionally, Neottia sinensis has been used in Chinese medicine to promote cardiovascular health, reduce inflammation, and lower blood pressure, due to its potential health benefits. The bioactive constituents of Neottia sinensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and polysaccharides, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

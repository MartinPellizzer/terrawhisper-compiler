Ferula arabica

Ferula arabica, also known as Ferula asafoetida or gum ferula, is a perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae), native to the Middle East and South Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making it useful in treating digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Ferula arabica include resinous compounds such as ferulic acid, coumarins, and terpenoids, which contribute to its health benefits.

The plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as asafoetida powder, gum ferula resin, and dried roots, which can be used in traditional medicine, cooking, and as a natural remedy for various health issues.

Benzoin fragrans

Benzoin fragrans, also known as Sumatran Storax, is a small tree native to the tropical forests of Sumatra, Indonesia, belonging to the Styrax genus of flowering plants.

The resin extracted from the plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic effects, making it useful in treating wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Benzoin fragrans include benzoin resin, which contains a mixture of sesquiterpene esters, mainly styrax, and other compounds such as β-eudesmol, β-selinene, and vanillin, contributing to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Benzoin fragrans is often used to make resin capsules, tinctures, and topical creams, which are commonly used to treat skin conditions, reduce inflammation, and alleviate respiratory issues.

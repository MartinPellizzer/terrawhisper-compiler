Aconitum napellus lusus laxum

Aconitum napellus lusus laxum, also known as Monk's hood or Lax-flowered Monk's hood, is a variety of the Aconitum napellus plant, a perennial flowering plant in the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae).

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties, which can be used to treat pain, reduce fever, and alleviate respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Aconitum napellus lusus laxum include aconitine, aconitine alkaloids, and sesquiterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Aconitum napellus lusus laxum is commonly prepared as a tincture, decoction, or powder, and is traditionally used to treat a range of conditions, including colds, flu, and fever.

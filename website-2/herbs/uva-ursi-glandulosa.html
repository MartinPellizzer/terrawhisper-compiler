Uva-ursi glandulosa

Uva-ursi glandulosa, also known as Bearberry, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to mountainous regions in Europe and Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used for its health benefits, including treating urinary tract infections, reducing inflammation, and supporting kidney function, due to its high content of antiseptic and anti-inflammatory compounds. The bioactive constituents of Uva-ursi glandulosa include arbutin, ursolic acid, and betulinic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Uva-ursi is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or dried extract, and is often used in combination with other herbs to create herbal remedies for urinary tract issues and other health concerns.

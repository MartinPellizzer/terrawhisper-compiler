Narcissus micranthus

Narcissus micranthus, also known as the small-flowered daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family, characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and strap-like leaves.

Traditionally, Narcissus micranthus has been used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of bronchitis, coughs, and colds. The bioactive constituents of Narcissus micranthus include alkaloids, saponins, and other compounds, such as galantamine, which has been shown to possess acetylcholinesterase inhibitory activity, and narcissine, a toxic alkaloid with potential antimicrobial properties.

In herbal medicine, Narcissus micranthus is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as dried extracts, which are used to treat respiratory issues and other conditions.

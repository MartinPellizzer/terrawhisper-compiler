Rabdosia setschwanensis

Rabdosia setschwanensis, also known as Himalayan sage or Setchwanese Himalayan sage, is a type of flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae) native to the mountainous regions of China and the Himalayas.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-cancer properties, which may aid in the treatment of various diseases such as arthritis, neurodegenerative disorders, and certain types of cancer. The bioactive constituents of Rabdosia setschwanensis include diterpenoid compounds, including setchwanensine and setchwanensine B, as well as iridoid glycosides and flavonoids.

Traditionally, the plant is used in herbal medicine and is often formulated into teas, tinctures, and capsules, as well as being used in traditional Chinese medicine to promote relaxation and relieve stress.

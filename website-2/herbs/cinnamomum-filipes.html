Cinnamomum filipes

Cinnamomum filipes, also known as Indonesian cinnamon or Weh cinnamon, is a species of evergreen tree in the family Lauraceae, native to the Mentawai Islands of Indonesia.

The bioactive constituents in Cinnamomum filipes have been shown to exhibit potential therapeutic benefits, including antibacterial and antifungal activities, and may also contribute to its use as a traditional remedy for respiratory issues and menstrual disorders.

In traditional medicine, Cinnamomum filipes is commonly prepared as a tea, oil, or powder, with the most common herbal preparations including cinnamon tea, cinnamon oil, and cinnamon capsules or tablets.

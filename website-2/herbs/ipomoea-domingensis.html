Ipomoea domingensis

Ipomoea domingensis, also known as the Jamaican snail vine or moonflower, is a tropical vine native to the Caribbean and Central America, belonging to the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and acting as an antispasmodic agent. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea domingensis include flavonoids, glycosides, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Ipomoea domingensis is often prepared as a tea or tincture, and is used to treat conditions such as anxiety, insomnia, and gastrointestinal issues.

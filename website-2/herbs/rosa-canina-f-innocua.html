What is Rosa canina f. innocua?

Rosa canina f. innocua is a subspecies of the wild rose, belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub with an indeterminate growth habit, typically growing 1-3 meters in height and 1-2 meters in width. The leaves are alternate, pinnate, with 5-7 leaflets, 3-10 centimeters long, and glabrous on both surfaces. The flowers are pink to red, 4-6 centimeters in diameter, with 5 petals and a prominent cluster of stamens.

The fruits are red to orange, 1-2 centimeters in diameter, and contain 5-6 seeds.

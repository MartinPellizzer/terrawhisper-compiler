Melaleuca robusta

Melaleuca robusta, also known as the 'Broad-leaved Paperbark', is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, native to Australia.

The essential oil extracted from Melaleuca robusta has been used for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties, providing health benefits in treating wounds, skin infections, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include cineole, limonene, and alpha-pinene, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Melaleuca robusta is commonly used in herbal preparations such as essential oils, tinctures, and salves, often used topically to promote wound healing and reduce inflammation.

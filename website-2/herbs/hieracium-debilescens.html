Hieracium debilescens

Hieracium debilescens, also known as Debile Hawkweed, is a perennial plant species belonging to the dandelion-like genus Hieracium, characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and delicate foliage.

Traditionally, Debile Hawkweed has been used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, gastrointestinal disorders, and skin conditions, due to its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Hieracium debilescens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpene lactones, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

Debile Hawkweed is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as in traditional remedies like salves and poultices, often to treat skin irritations and wounds.

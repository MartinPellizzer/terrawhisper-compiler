Malus yunnanensis

Malus yunnanensis, also known as Yunnan apple, is a deciduous tree species in the rose family (Rosaceae) native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

The bark, leaves, and fruit of Malus yunnanensis have been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its purported health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Malus yunnanensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Malus yunnanensis is often processed into herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and dried powders, as well as being used in combination with other herbs to enhance their effects.

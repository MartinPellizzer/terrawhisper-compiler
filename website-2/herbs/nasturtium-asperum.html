Nasturtium asperum

Nasturtium asperum, also known as rough nasturtium or Indian cress, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, wrinkled leaves and bright yellow, white, or purple flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may help to reduce symptoms of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs. The bioactive constituents of Nasturtium asperum include glucosinolates, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Nasturtium asperum is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or capsule, and is also used in salads and as a garnish due to its peppery flavor and aroma.

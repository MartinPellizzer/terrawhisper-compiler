Dryopteris ciliata

Dryopteris ciliata, also known as the hairy male fern, is a species of fern native to the Himalayan region, characterized by its delicate fronds and hairy rachides.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties, making it effective in treating various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Dryopteris ciliata include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Dryopteris ciliata is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including fever, digestive issues, and respiratory problems.

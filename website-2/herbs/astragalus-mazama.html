Astragalus mazama

Astragalus mazama, also known as Mazama's milkvetch, is a perennial plant species in the pea family (Fabaceae) native to the western United States and Canada.

The root of Astragalus mazama has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to provide adaptogenic and immune-boosting properties, supporting the body's ability to resist stress and infection, as well as to alleviate symptoms of fatigue and inflammation. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus mazama include triterpene saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which have been shown to exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulatory activities.

Astragalus mazama is often prepared as a decoction, capsule, or tincture, and is commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine to enhance overall health and vitality, as well as to support the immune system during illnesses such as the common cold and flu.

Kunzea corifolia

Kunzea corifolia, also known as Tasmanian snow bush or Tasmanian kunzea, is a small shrub native to Tasmania, Australia, belonging to the myrtle family (Myrtaceae).

The leaves of Kunzea corifolia have been traditionally used in Australian herbal medicine to promote wound healing, reduce inflammation, and alleviate respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its antimicrobial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Kunzea corifolia include phenolic acids, flavonoids, and essential oils, particularly the sesquiterpene alpha-pinene.

The most common herbal preparations made with Kunzea corifolia include teas, tinctures, and salves, which are used topically to support wound healing and respiratory health.

Ligularia japonica

Ligularia japonica, also known as Japanese Ligularia, is a perennial plant species native to East Asia, characterized by its large, showy flowers and edible leaves.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include sesquiterpenoids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Ligularia japonica include teas, infusions, and decoctions, which are often used in traditional East Asian medicine to treat a range of health conditions.

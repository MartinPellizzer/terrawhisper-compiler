Suaeda palmeri

Suaeda palmeri, also known as Palmer's seepweed, is a perennial halophytic plant that belongs to the family Amaranthaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Suaeda palmeri include flavonoids, phenolic acids, saponins, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Suaeda palmeri is commonly used to make herbal teas, tinctures, and infusions, often in combination with other medicinal plants, to alleviate symptoms associated with colds, coughs, and skin irritations.

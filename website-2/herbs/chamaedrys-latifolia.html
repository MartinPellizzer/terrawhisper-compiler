Chamaedrys latifolia

Chamaedrys latifolia, also known as white horehound, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Chamaedrys latifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and iridoid glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with white horehound include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory infections and coughs.

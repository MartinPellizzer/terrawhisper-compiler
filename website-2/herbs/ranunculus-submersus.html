Ranunculus submersus

Ranunculus submersus, also known as the water buttercup, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe and Asia.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects, which may aid in wound healing and reduce inflammation in the body. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus submersus include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, the water buttercup is often made into teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various conditions such as skin irritations, respiratory issues, and digestive problems.

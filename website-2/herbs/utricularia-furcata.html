Utricularia furcata

Utricularia furcata, also known as the forked bladderwort, is a carnivorous aquatic plant belonging to the bladderwort family (Lentibulariaceae).

Traditionally, Utricularia furcata has been used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory infections, due to its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include iridoid glycosides, such as furcatin and 8-O-acetylfurcatin, which have been shown to exhibit immunomodulatory and antioxidant activities.

Utricularia furcata is commonly used in herbal preparations, including infusions, decoctions, and extracts, often in combination with other herbs to create tonics and remedies for colds, sore throats, and other respiratory ailments.

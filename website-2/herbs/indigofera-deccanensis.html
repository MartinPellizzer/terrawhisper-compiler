Indigofera deccanensis

Indigofera deccanensis, also known as Deccan indigo, is a plant species in the pea family Fabaceae, commonly found in the Western Ghats region of India.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, inflammation, and skin infections, due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties. The bioactive constituents of Indigofera deccanensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and capsules, which are used to treat various health conditions.

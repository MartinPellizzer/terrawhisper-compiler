Lentiscus vulgaris

Lentiscus vulgaris, also known as Mastic or Pistacia lentiscus, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its dark green leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers.

The resin extracted from its bark has been used for centuries to support digestive health, relieve inflammation, and boost the immune system due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of Lentiscus vulgaris include sesquiterpenes, particularly α- and β-masticadienone, as well as phenolic compounds like phenolic acids and flavonoids.

In traditional herbal medicine, Lentiscus vulgaris is commonly used to prepare mastic oil, mastic gum, and tea infusions, which are used to treat a range of health issues, including stomach ulcers, gum inflammation, and respiratory infections.

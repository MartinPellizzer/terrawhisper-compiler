Neltuma mezcalana

Neltuma mezcalana, also known as Neltuma, is a tropical plant species native to the Americas, characterized by its unique, pinnate leaves and woody stems.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiparasitic activities, making it a potential remedy for various ailments such as wounds, fever, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Neltuma include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Neltuma is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory issues.

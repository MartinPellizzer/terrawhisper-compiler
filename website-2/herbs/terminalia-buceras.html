Terminalia buceras

Terminalia buceras, also known as Palo Santo, is a deciduous tree native to Central America that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine.

The health benefits of Terminalia buceras include its potential to lower inflammation, improve respiratory issues, and act as an antimicrobial agent, making it a valuable treatment for various health conditions. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, tannins, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Terminalia buceras include teas, tinctures, and capsules, as well as essential oils derived from its heartwood, which is highly prized for its calming and medicinal properties.

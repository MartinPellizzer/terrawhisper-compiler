Thuja papuana

Thuja papuana, also known as Pacific red cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Australia and the Pacific Islands, belonging to the cypress family (Cupressaceae).

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects, making it useful in treating various health conditions such as respiratory issues, skin infections, and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Thuja papuana include thujone, borneol, and terpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Thuja papuana is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as essential oils, extracts, and infusions, which are used in traditional medicine, aromatherapy, and as a natural remedy for various health ailments.

What is Pilosocereus flavipulvinatus subsp. carolinensis?

Pilosocereus flavipulvinatus subsp.

carolinensis is a cactus endemic to South Carolina. It belongs to the family Cactaceae.

This plant has cylindrical stems, 1-4 meters tall, with 5-6 ribs, and areoles with yellowish spines. The stems have a yellowish-brown epidermis with white areoles, while the flowers are funnel-shaped, 8-10 cm long, and yellow.

The plant reproduces via seed, and is typically found in open woods and sandy scrub.

Dracophyllum longifolium

Dracophyllum longifolium, also known as Tasmanian pepperbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern regions of Tasmania.

This plant has been traditionally used in Australian folk medicine, with reported health benefits including its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in wound healing and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Dracophyllum longifolium include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Dracophyllum longifolium include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to treat coughs, colds, and skin irritations.

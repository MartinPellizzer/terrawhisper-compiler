Cymbopogon ambiguus

Cymbopogon ambiguus, also known as East Indian lemongrass, is a tropical grass native to Southeast Asia, belonging to the Poaceae family.

The essential oil extracted from its leaves has been traditionally used for its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which provide numerous health benefits, including relief from digestive issues, anxiety, and insomnia, as well as a natural remedy for wounds and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Cymbopogon ambiguus essential oil include citral, geraniol, and limonene, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with East Indian lemongrass include teas, infusions, and essential oil blends, which are often used in aromatherapy, skincare products, and traditional medicine.

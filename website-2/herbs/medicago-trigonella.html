Medicago trigonella

Medicago trigonella, commonly known as fenugreek, is a leguminous annual herb belonging to the Fabaceae family.

Fenugreek has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering various health benefits such as reducing blood sugar levels, alleviating menstrual cramps, and supporting lactation in nursing mothers. The bioactive constituents of fenugreek include trigonelline, galactomannans, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Fenugreek is often used in herbal preparations, including teas, capsules, and tinctures, as well as being a key ingredient in traditional Indian and Middle Eastern cooking, particularly in the spice blend known as garam masala.

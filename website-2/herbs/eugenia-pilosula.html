Eugenia pilosula

The bioactive constituents of Eugenia pilosula include flavonoids such as quercetin and kaempferol, as well as phenolic acids like ellagic acid and caffeic acid.

In traditional medicine, Eugenia pilosula is often used to make herbal preparations, such as teas, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin conditions.

Cymbopogon pruinosus

Cymbopogon pruinosus, commonly known as pruinose lemongrass, is a type of tropical grass native to Africa and Asia, belonging to the Poaceae family.

The bioactive constituents of Cymbopogon pruinosus include essential oils, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

side effects

Cymbopogon pruinosus, commonly known as lemongrass, is generally considered safe for consumption in small amounts. However, ingesting large quantities of lemongrass can cause stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea in some individuals. Additionally, lemongrass essential oil may cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and respiratory problems in people with sensitive skin or asthma. Pregnant and breastfeeding women are advised to use lemongrass with caution due to the lack of research on its effects on fetal development and infant health. In rare cases, consuming large amounts of lemongrass may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and worsen underlying health conditions.

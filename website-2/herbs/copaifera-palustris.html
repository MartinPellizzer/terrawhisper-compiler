Copaifera palustris

Copaifera palustris, also known as the Copaiba tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae, native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The resin extracted from the Copaiba tree has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, offering several health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can aid in the treatment of wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Copaiba resin include diterpenes, particularly copalic acid, and other triterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Copaiba is commonly prepared as a topical cream or ointment, as well as in tinctures and essential oils, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including skin irritations, arthritis, and respiratory infections.

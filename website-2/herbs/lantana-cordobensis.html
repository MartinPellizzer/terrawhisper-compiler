Lantana cordobensis

Lantana cordobensis, also known as Argentine lantana, is a species of flowering plant in the verbena family, characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant flowers and slender stems.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Lantana cordobensis include lantana sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Lantana cordobensis is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma.

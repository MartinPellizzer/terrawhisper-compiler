Cymbopogon pendulus

Cymbopogon pendulus, also known as East Indian lemongrass, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae, native to tropical Asia and widely cultivated for its essential oil.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal effects, making it beneficial for relieving stress, promoting digestion, and supporting immune function. The bioactive constituents of Cymbopogon pendulus include citral, limonene, and geraniol, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant are essential oils and teas, often used in aromatherapy, traditional medicine, and culinary applications.

Festuca frederikseniae

Festuca frederikseniae, also known as Frederiksen's fescue, is a species of grass native to the high-altitude Andean regions of South America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, which may help to alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues, wounds, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Festuca frederikseniae include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, although it is also sometimes used in tinctures and salves to treat various ailments, particularly those related to the skin and respiratory system.

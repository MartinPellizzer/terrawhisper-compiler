Inula cernua

Inula cernua, also known as nodding elecampane, is a perennial herb belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its nodding yellow flowers and hairy, ovate leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in the treatment of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs. The bioactive constituents of Inula cernua include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusion, often used to soothe sore throats and relieve respiratory congestion.

Astragalus armatus

Astragalus armatus, also known as the armed milkvetch, is a perennial plant belonging to the pea family (Fabaceae) native to the mountainous regions of western China.

The plant has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its adaptogenic properties, and its health benefits include enhancing immune function, reducing inflammation, and improving cardiovascular health. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus armatus include saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction or tea, and is also used in combination with other herbs in formulas designed to promote longevity and overall well-being.

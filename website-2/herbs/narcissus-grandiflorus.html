Narcissus grandiflorus

Narcissus grandiflorus, commonly known as large-flowered daffodil, is a perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

The bioactive constituents of Narcissus grandiflorus include alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins, which have been isolated and studied for their medicinal properties.

The plant is often used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and ointments, with the latter being used topically to treat skin conditions and wounds.

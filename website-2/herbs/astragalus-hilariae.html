Astragalus hilariae

Astragalus hilariae, also known as Astragalus, is a perennial plant in the legume family that has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immune-boosting properties, making it a popular remedy for conditions such as fatigue, coughs, and colds. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus include astragalosides, flavonoids, and saponins, which are thought to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Astragalus is often prepared as a decoction, dried extract, or tincture, and is commonly used to enhance vitality and resist illness.

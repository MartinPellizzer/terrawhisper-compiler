Coleus praetermissus

Coleus praetermissus, also known as Coleus forskohlii, is a plant species native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa, characterized by its perennial growth habit and distinctive purple flowers.

The bioactive constituents of Coleus praetermissus include forskolin, a diterpene compound, as well as other flavonoids and phenolic acids.

Traditionally, Coleus praetermissus is used in herbal preparations such as capsules, tablets, and extracts, often combined with other herbs like turmeric and ginger, to support weight loss, improve cardiovascular health, and alleviate respiratory issues.

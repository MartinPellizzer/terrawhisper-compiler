Puccinellia nutkaensis

Puccinellia nutkaensis, also known as sea barley grass or saltwort, is a type of salt-tolerant, perennial grass that thrives in coastal areas and sandy dunes.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to promote digestive health and alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome due to its high fiber and nutrient content, as well as its anti-inflammatory properties. Bioactive constituents of Puccinellia nutkaensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal preparations, Puccinellia nutkaensis is often used in teas, infusions, and tinctures to support gut health and reduce inflammation, although its efficacy and safety have not been extensively scientifically studied.

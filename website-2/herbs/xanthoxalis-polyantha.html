Xanthoxalis polyantha

Xanthoxalis polyantha, also known as Wood Sorrel, is a perennial herb belonging to the family Oxalidaceae.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of arthritis, reduce oxidative stress, and support overall cardiovascular health. The bioactive constituents of Wood Sorrel include flavonoids, tannins, and oxalic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Wood Sorrel is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat various ailments, including digestive issues, skin conditions, and respiratory problems.

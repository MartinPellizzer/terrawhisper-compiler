Astragalus fraternellus

Astragalus fraternellus, also known as Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial plant species within the legume family, Fabaceae.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, immunomodulatory, and antioxidant effects, which may help to prevent or alleviate conditions such as cancer, cardiovascular disease, and immune system disorders. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus fraternellus include flavonoids, saponins, and polysaccharides, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Astragalus fraternellus is commonly prepared as a decoction or dried powder, and is often combined with other herbs to make teas, capsules, or tinctures.

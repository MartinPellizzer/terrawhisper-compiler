Brassica sativa

Brassica sativa, commonly known as oilseed rape or rapeseed, is an annual plant species belonging to the Brassicaceae family, cultivated for its seeds which produce a valuable oil used in food and biofuel production.

The seeds of Brassica sativa are rich in nutrients and have been associated with several health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health due to their high content of omega-3 fatty acids and antioxidants. The bioactive constituents of Brassica sativa include glucosinolates, isothiocyanates, and carotenoids, which have been shown to exhibit anti-cancer, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

The seeds of Brassica sativa are commonly used to produce rapeseed oil, which is often used in food, cosmetics, and pharmaceutical preparations, as well as in traditional herbal medicines such as cold-pressed oil and seed extract supplements.

Brassica sativa is a species within the genus Brassica, which also includes other common vegetables like cabbage and mustard. The classification of Brassica sativa is also referred to as Brassica oleracea var. sylvestris, which is a subspecies within the larger species B. oleracea.

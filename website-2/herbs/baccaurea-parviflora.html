Baccaurea parviflora

Baccaurea parviflora, also known as the Asian mulberry tree or Baccaurea, is a tropical plant species native to Southeast Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which may help to protect against chronic diseases such as cancer and cardiovascular disease. Bioactive constituents of Baccaurea parviflora include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and capsules, which are commonly used to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and respiratory infections.

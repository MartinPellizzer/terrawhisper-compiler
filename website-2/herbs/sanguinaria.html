Sanguinaria

Sanguinaria is a perennial plant species belonging to the poppy family (Papaveraceae), commonly known as bloodroot due to its red sap.

The plant's root, leaves, and rhizomes have been used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiviral properties, providing potential health benefits in treating various conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin issues. The bioactive constituents of Sanguinaria include alkaloids such as sanguinarine and chelerythrine, which possess cytotoxic and antimicrobial properties.

Sanguinaria is commonly prepared as a tincture, decoction, or powder, and is often used in traditional herbal remedies, including topical creams and ointments for skin conditions and oral preparations for digestive issues.

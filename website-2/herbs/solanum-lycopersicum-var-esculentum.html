What is Solanum lycopersicum var. esculentum?

Solanum lycopersicum var. esculentum is a domesticated variety of the plant Solanum lycopersicum, belonging to the family Solanaceae, which also includes potatoes, eggplants, and petunias.

This variety is characterized by its green leaves, which are simple, alternate, and have an entire margin, and its edible fruit, a berry, which is typically red, oval, and contains several seeds. Solanum lycopersicum var. esculentum has a woody stem, which is herbaceous, and its flowers are hermaphroditic, having both male and female reproductive organs in the same flower. The plant grows upright, with a height of up to 3 meters, and its roots are taproots, with a fibrous root system.

The fruit of Solanum lycopersicum var. esculentum, commonly known as a tomato, is a rich source of nutrients and is widely cultivated and consumed around the world.

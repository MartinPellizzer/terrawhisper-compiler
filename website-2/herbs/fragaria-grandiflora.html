Fragaria grandiflora

Fragaria grandiflora, also known as the large-leaf strawberry, is a perennial plant species within the rose family (Rosaceae) that is native to eastern Australia.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, with its leaves and fruit exhibiting anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, providing potential health benefits such as wound healing and protection against certain diseases. The bioactive constituents of Fragaria grandiflora include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and ellagic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is often used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as in topical creams and ointments, to take advantage of its medicinal properties.

Keetia mannii

Keetia mannii, also known as the West African Keetia, is a plant species within the Rubiaceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used in African folk medicine, and research has identified several health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in wound healing, reduce fever, and alleviate various infections. The bioactive constituents of Keetia mannii include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, such as epicatechin, quercetin, and keetianin, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Keetia mannii is often prepared as a decoction or infusion for the treatment of various ailments, and it is also used in the production of herbal teas and tonics.

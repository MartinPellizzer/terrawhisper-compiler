Luciola campestris

Luciola campestris, also known as Fireweed or Wild Fireweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It has been traditionally used for its health benefits, particularly in treating respiratory issues such as bronchitis, coughs, and sore throats, due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Luciola campestris include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Luciola campestris is often prepared as an infusion, tincture, or ointment to treat various health conditions, with its most common preparations being infusions for respiratory issues and topical ointments for skin irritations and wounds.

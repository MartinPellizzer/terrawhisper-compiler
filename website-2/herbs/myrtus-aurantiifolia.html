Myrtus aurantiifolia

Myrtus aurantiifolia, also known as the orange myrtle or water myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Northern Australia and New Guinea.

The health benefits of Myrtus aurantiifolia include its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat a range of ailments including sore throats, coughs, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Myrtus aurantiifolia is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and essential oils, which are often used to soothe respiratory issues, boost the immune system, and promote relaxation.

Limonium ocymifolium

Limonium ocymifolium, also known as sea lavender, is a coastal shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and needle-like leaves.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, particularly limonene and beta-pinene, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Limonium ocymifolium is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as to promote digestion and reduce stress.

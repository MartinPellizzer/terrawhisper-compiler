Lansium membranaceum

Lansium membranaceum, also known as the langsat or longkong, is a tropical fruit-bearing tree native to Southeast Asia.

The langsat has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including reducing fever, treating diarrhea, and alleviating respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Lansium membranaceum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with langsat include juice, tea, and dried fruit, which are often used to support digestive health and boost the immune system.

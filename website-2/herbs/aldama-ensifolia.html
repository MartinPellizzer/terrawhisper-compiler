Aldama ensifolia

Aldama ensifolia, also known as Mexican swordleaf, is a perennial herb belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae).

The plant has been traditionally used in Mexican folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Aldama ensifolia include flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Aldama ensifolia is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat colds, coughs, and bronchitis.

Asclepias cornuta

Asclepias cornuta, also known as Hooked Milkweed, is a perennial plant species in the Asclepiadaceae family, native to the southwestern United States and Mexico.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its use as an anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antitumor agent, making it a promising natural remedy for various ailments. The bioactive constituents of Asclepias cornuta include cardenolides, cardiotonic glycosides, and other compounds that have been shown to have significant pharmacological activities.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat conditions like fever, rheumatism, and skin irritations, although further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.

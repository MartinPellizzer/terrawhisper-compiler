Picea douglasii

Picea douglasii, also known as Douglas fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

The essential oil derived from Douglas fir has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include reducing inflammation, relieving respiratory issues, and promoting relaxation, making it a popular natural remedy for stress and anxiety. The bioactive constituents of Douglas fir include alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, and limonene, which are responsible for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties.

Douglas fir is commonly used to make teas, essential oils, and tinctures, which are often used to promote respiratory health, reduce coughs and colds, and improve overall well-being.

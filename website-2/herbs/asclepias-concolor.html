classification

Asclepias concolor is classified as a species of milkweed in the family Apocynaceae. It is native to Mexico and the southwestern United States. This plant is further categorized as part of the genus Asclepias, which includes around 1 to 2 dozen species of milkweed. Asclepias concolor is a type of perennial plant that grows in dry or sandy areas, often forming clusters of small, white flowers. The species is also known for its role in providing a food source for monarch butterflies during their caterpillar stage.

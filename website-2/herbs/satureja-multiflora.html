Satureja multiflora

Satureja multiflora, commonly known as multiflorous savory, is a perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family, native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, including its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate digestive issues, reduce stress, and protect against certain diseases. The bioactive constituents of Satureja multiflora include essential oils rich in carvacrol, thymol, and beta-pinene, as well as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids.

Traditionally, Satureja multiflora is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and essential oils, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for skin and wound care.

Ranunculus decurvus

Ranunculus decurvus, also known as the curved buttercup or creeping buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus decurvus include ranunculin, protoanemonin, and anemonin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Ranunculus decurvus is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat fever, digestive issues, and skin conditions.

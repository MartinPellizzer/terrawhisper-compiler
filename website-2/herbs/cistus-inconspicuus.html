Cistus inconspicuus

Cistus inconspicuus, also known as the inconspicuous rockrose, is a perennial shrubby plant species within the Cistus genus.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may help to soothe skin irritations and promote wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Cistus inconspicuus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often employed to support immune function and alleviate respiratory issues.

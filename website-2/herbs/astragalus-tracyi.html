Astragalus tracyi

Astragalus tracyi, also known as Tracy's milkvetch, is a perennial plant species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving immune function, and exhibiting antioxidant properties, which may contribute to its use in traditional medicine for treating conditions such as fever and respiratory issues. Bioactive constituents of Astragalus tracyi include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which are thought to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

Astragalus tracyi is often used in herbal preparations such as capsules, teas, and tinctures, with the dried root being a common form used in traditional Chinese medicine and other herbal remedies.

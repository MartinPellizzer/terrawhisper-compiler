Mammillaria milleri

Mammillaria milleri, also known as the Miller's pincushion cactus, is a small, globular cactus species belonging to the Mammillaria genus, characterized by its dense, fine spines and vibrant pink flowers.

Traditionally, Mammillaria milleri has been used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, inflammation, and skin issues, due to its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Mammillaria milleri include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are thought to contribute to its medicinal effects.

Herbal preparations made from Mammillaria milleri include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often used in traditional medicine to treat conditions such as colds, coughs, and digestive issues.

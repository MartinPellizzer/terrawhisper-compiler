Blephariglottis × osceola

Blephariglottis × osceola, commonly known as the American Goldenseal, is a deciduous perennial plant native to North America, characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including digestive problems, inflammation, and infections, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties. The bioactive constituents of Blephariglottis × osceola include berberine, a compound with potent antibacterial activity, as well as other alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids that contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with American Goldenseal include capsules, tablets, and tinctures, which are often used to support immune function and alleviate symptoms associated with colds, flu, and digestive issues.

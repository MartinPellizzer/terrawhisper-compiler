Carum anisum

Carum anisum, also known as anise or aniseed, is a flowering annual herb native to the Middle East and the Mediterranean region, characterized by its sweet, licorice-flavored seeds.

The health benefits of Carum anisum include its potential to alleviate digestive issues, such as bloating and gas, as well as its role in reducing inflammation and improving respiratory health. The bioactive constituents of this plant include volatile oils, particularly anethole, as well as flavonoids, terpenoids, and other phenolic compounds.

Carum anisum is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and extracts, as well as in traditional medicine and culinary applications, particularly in Middle Eastern and Mediterranean cooking.

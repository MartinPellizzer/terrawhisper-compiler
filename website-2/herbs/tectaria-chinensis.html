Tectaria chinensis

Tectaria chinensis, also known as the Chinese tectaria, is a type of fern species belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Tectaria chinensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In herbal preparations, Tectaria chinensis is often combined with other plants to make teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being used in traditional Chinese medicine formulations, such as tinctures and ointments.

Ceropegia molaventi

Ceropegia molaventi, also known as the Indian squill, is a perennial succulent plant native to India, belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which are said to aid in treating various health issues such as fever, cough, and digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of Ceropegia molaventi include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used in herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and powders, as well as in traditional Indian medicines like chyawanprash and ashwagandha-based formulations.

Washingtonia californica

Washingtonia californica, also known as California fan palm, is a slow-growing, evergreen palm tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and alleviating fever. The bioactive constituents of Washingtonia californica include flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with California fan palm include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to support digestive health and relieve respiratory issues.

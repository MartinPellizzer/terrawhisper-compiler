Lonchocarpus cultratus

Lonchocarpus cultratus, also known as the "cut-throat tree" or "bloodwood tree," is a plant species native to Central and South America, characterized by its distinctive, thorn-like branches and small, white flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of fever, pain, and respiratory issues. Bioactive constituents of Lonchocarpus cultratus include flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is often used in herbal preparations, including infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat a range of ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions.

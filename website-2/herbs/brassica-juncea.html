Brassica juncea

Brassica juncea, commonly known as brown mustard or Indian mustard, is an annual plant in the Brassicaceae family, known for its edible seeds, leaves, and oil.

The health benefits of Brassica juncea include its ability to aid in digestion, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant properties, which can help protect against various diseases. The bioactive constituents of this plant include glucosinolates, isothiocyanates, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Brassica juncea include oil, seeds, and extracts, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.

The genus Brassica is a group of plants that are primarily native to the Northern Hemisphere and are characterized by their cruciferous flowers. Brassica juncea is further classified into several subspecies, including the brown mustard, Indian mustard, and leaf mustard. As a species within the Brassica genus, Brassica juncea is also classified as a member of the tribe Brassiceae.

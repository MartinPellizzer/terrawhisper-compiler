Coleus unguentarius

Coleus unguentarius, also known as Coleus aromaticus or Coleus fragrans, is a tropical plant species belonging to the Lamiaceae family, native to the tropical forests of South Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, cold, and respiratory infections, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Coleus unguentarius include essential oils such as estragole, limonene, and linalool, as well as flavonoids like kaempferol and quercetin, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, Coleus unguentarius is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, which are also used as topical applications for skin conditions and wounds.

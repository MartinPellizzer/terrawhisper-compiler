Vitellaria casarettii

Vitellaria casuarii, also known as shea butter tree, is a plant species native to the savannas of West Africa, characterized by its large, deciduous leaves and showy white flowers.

The oil extracted from its nut has been used for centuries for its numerous health benefits, including moisturizing and nourishing the skin, reducing inflammation, and improving wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Vitellaria casuarii include triterpenoids, phytosterols, and unsaponifiables, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Shea butter, the most common herbal preparation made from this plant, is often used in cosmetics and skincare products to hydrate and protect the skin, and is also taken orally as a supplement to support skin health and immune function.

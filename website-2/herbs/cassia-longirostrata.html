Cassia longirostrata

Cassia longirostrata, also known as sotol or casia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and long, curved pods.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities that can help to alleviate conditions such as fever, sore throats, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of Cassia longirostrata include alkaloids, flavonoids, and glycosides, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various ailments, as well as a smokable resin, known as "sotol", used in traditional ceremonies.

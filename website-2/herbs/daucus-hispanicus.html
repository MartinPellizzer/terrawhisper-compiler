Daucus hispanicus

Daucus hispanicus, commonly known as Spanish carrot or Horned caraway, is a biennial or perennial herb belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including digestive problems, respiratory issues, and menstrual disorders, due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties. The bioactive constituents of Daucus hispanicus include volatile oils, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Daucus hispanicus include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat symptoms of colds, flu, and other respiratory infections.

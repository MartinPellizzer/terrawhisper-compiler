Hypenia durifolia

Hypenia durifolia, also known as Devil's Claw, is a plant species native to the Kalahari Desert in southern Africa, characterized by its distinctive claw-shaped roots.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include harpagoside, a phenylpropanoid iridoid glycoside, as well as other iridoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Hypenia durifolia is commonly prepared as a tea, capsule, or powder, and is also used in traditional African medicine to treat a range of ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal disorders.

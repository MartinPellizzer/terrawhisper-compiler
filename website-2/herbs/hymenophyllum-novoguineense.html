Hymenophyllum novoguineense

Hymenophyllum novoguineense is a fern species, specifically a type of rock fern, native to New Guinea.

The plant is traditionally used in folk medicine, offering various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in the treatment of wounds and infections. The bioactive constituents of Hymenophyllum novoguineense include sesquiterpenoids and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions.

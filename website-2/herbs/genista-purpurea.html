Genista purpurea

Genista purpurea, also known as purple broom or purple genista, is a flowering plant in the legume family (Fabaceae) native to Europe and Western Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues and improve cardiovascular health. The bioactive constituents of Genista purpurea include flavonoids, such as genistein and kaempferol, as well as alkaloids and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Genista purpurea is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, as well as to promote digestion and relieve menopausal symptoms.

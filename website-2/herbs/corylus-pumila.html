Corylus pumila

Corylus pumila, also known as dwarf or European hazel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and Asia.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health. The bioactive constituents of Corylus pumila include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

The most common herbal preparations made with Corylus pumila include teas, infusions, and extracts, often used to support heart health and alleviate symptoms of menopause.

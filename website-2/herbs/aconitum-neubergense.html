Aconitum neubergense

Aconitum neubergense, commonly known as monk's hood or blue monk's hood, is a perennial plant species within the Aconitum genus, characterized by its distinctive blue-purple flowers and toxic properties.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties, which may help alleviate pain, reduce fever, and combat infections. The bioactive constituents of Aconitum neubergense include aconitine, mesaconitine, and hypaconitine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties, but also its toxicity.

In traditional herbal medicine, Aconitum neubergense is often prepared as a tincture or decoction, and is used to treat conditions such as rheumatism, fever, and respiratory issues, although its use requires caution due to its toxic properties.

Frasera scabra

Frasera scabra, also known as Bear Root or Rough Indian Paint Root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

Traditionally, Frasera scabra has been used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include iridoid glycosides, particularly frasereine and frasidin, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

Frasera scabra is commonly prepared as a tincture, tea, or dried root, which is used to treat various ailments, although its use should be cautioned due to potential toxicity and interactions with other medications.

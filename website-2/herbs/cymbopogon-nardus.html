classification

Cymbopogon nardus is a species of grass in the family Poaceae. It belongs to the subfamily Panicoideae and the tribe Andropogoneae. Further classification places it in the genus Cymbopogon and the subgenus Nardiinae. At the species level, Cymbopogon nardus is often referred to as a type of citronella grass. This classification highlights its unique position within the vast diversity of grasses.

medicinal uses

Cymbopogon nardus, commonly known as citronella grass, has been used for its medicinal properties in various cultures. Its essential oils have been used to treat digestive issues, such as nausea and stomach cramps. The plant's antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties also make it useful for wound healing and reducing swelling. Additionally, citronella oil has been used to repel insects, which can be beneficial in preventing the spread of diseases. The plant's medicinal uses have been studied and documented in traditional medicine practices, particularly in Southeast Asian cultures.

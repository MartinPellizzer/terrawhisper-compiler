Brunnera orientalis

Brunnera orientalis, also known as Eastern Forget-me-not, is a perennial plant species in the borage family (Boraginaceae) native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including the treatment of digestive issues, fever reduction, and inflammation alleviation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. The bioactive constituents of Brunnera orientalis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, Brunnera orientalis is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various ailments, although modern research is still limited, and further studies are needed to confirm its efficacy.

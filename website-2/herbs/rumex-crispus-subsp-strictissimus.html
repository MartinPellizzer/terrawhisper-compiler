What is Rumex crispus subsp. strictissimus?

Rumex crispus subsp.

strictissimus is a subspecies of perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae. It has a taproot and an erect stem, typically 20-60 cm tall, with leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, 3-10 cm long, and 0.5-2 cm wide, with crisped margins.

The inflorescence is a terminal spike, 5-20 cm long, bearing greenish-yellow flowers that are 3-4 mm in diameter, with three stamens and a three-carpelled ovary. The plant reproduces by seeds, which are 2-3 mm long and 1.5-2 mm wide, with a smooth surface.

The leaves and stems are edible and have been used in traditional medicine.

Claytonia acaulis

Claytonia acaulis, also known as mountain spring beauty or mountain springbeauty, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Portulacaceae family, characterized by its small, delicate flowers and succulent leaves.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which may contribute to its use in traditional medicine for treating various health conditions such as colds and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Claytonia acaulis include flavonoids, anthocyanins, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, Claytonia acaulis is often prepared as a decoction or infusions, and can also be found in supplement form, making it a popular ingredient in traditional herbal remedies.

Artemisia semiarida

Artemisia semiarida, also known as Mexican sage or Desert Sagewort, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, belonging to the Asteraceae family.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities, offering potential health benefits for the treatment of various conditions such as fever, digestive issues, and skin problems. The bioactive constituents of Artemisia semiarida include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional medicine, Artemisia semiarida is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues and promote relaxation.

Pleopeltis masafuerae

Pleopeltis masafuerae, also known as the Chilean Polypody, is a type of epiphytic fern native to the island of Masatierra in the Juan Fernández Islands.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its potential to lower blood pressure, improve digestion, and exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, contributing to its use in treating various health issues. The bioactive constituents of Pleopeltis masafuerae include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

Sorghum splendidum

Sorghum splendidum, also known as the Indian sorghum or Bicolor sorghum, is a type of cereal crop belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

The bioactive constituents of Sorghum splendidum include phenolic compounds, flavonoids, and anthocyanins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Sorghum splendidum is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are valued for their potential to support cardiovascular health and manage certain metabolic disorders.

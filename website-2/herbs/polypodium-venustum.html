Polypodium venustum

Polypodium venustum, also known as Himalayan spleenwort, is a species of fern native to the Himalayan region, characterized by its unique, delicate fronds and creeping rhizomes.

This plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory issues, due to its potential anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Polypodium venustum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are thought to be responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional Indian medicine, Polypodium venustum is commonly prepared as a decoction, powder, or oil, and is used to make herbal preparations such as churna (a powdered formulation) and ghrita (a medicated ghee).

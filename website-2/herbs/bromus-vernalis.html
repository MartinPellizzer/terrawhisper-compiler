Bromus vernalis

Bromus vernalis, also known as spring brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and Western Asia, characterized by its tall stalks and feathery, green foliage.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, as well as aiding in the management of respiratory issues and digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of Bromus vernalis include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Bromus vernalis is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, often used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, as well as to support digestive health.

Lodhra monantha

Lodhra monantha, also known as Symplocos racemosa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan region.

The plant has been used in Ayurvedic medicine for its numerous health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which can help to alleviate menstrual disorders, fever, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Lodhra monantha include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In traditional Indian medicine, Lodhra monantha is often prepared as a powder, decoction, or paste, and is used in formulations such as Lodhrasav and Lodharasayana, which are used to treat menstrual disorders and other health conditions.

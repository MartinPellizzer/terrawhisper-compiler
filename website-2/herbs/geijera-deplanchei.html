    What ailments can you heal with this herb?

    The most common ailments you can heal with Geijera deplanchei are fever, which is often reduced due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

    The plant is also used to treat respiratory issues such as bronchitis, caused by its expectorant properties that help to loosen and clear mucus. Additionally, Geijera deplanchei has been used to alleviate rheumatic pain, thanks to its analgesic and anti-inflammatory compounds. Furthermore, the plant has been employed to treat skin conditions like eczema, as it contains compounds that exhibit antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

    Its antimicrobial properties also make it effective in treating infections like conjunctivitis.

    What are the therapeutic properties this herb?

    The main therapeutic properties of Geijera deplanchei are antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory.

    Antimicrobial properties of Geijera deplanchei are attributed to the presence of flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids. The antioxidant properties of this plant are due to its high content of flavonoids and phenolic acids, which help protect cells from oxidative damage. Anti-inflammatory properties of Geijera deplanchei are associated with the presence of sesquiterpenes, which have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes.

    Furthermore, the plant's volatile oils, particularly borneol and geraniol, have been found to exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities.

    What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

    The most important healing constituents of Geijera deplanchei are flavonoids, including quercetin and kaempferol, and phenolic acids, particularly ferulic acid and sinapic acid.

    These compounds have been shown to possess potent antioxidant, anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which contribute to the plant's traditional uses in treating wounds, skin conditions and respiratory infections. The flavonoids in Geijera deplanchei have also been reported to exhibit anti-cancer and anti-viral activities, while the phenolic acids have been found to inhibit the growth of certain microorganisms. The plant's antioxidant activity is attributed to its ability to scavenge free radicals and chelate metal ions, which helps to protect cells from oxidative damage.

    Overall, the bioactive compounds present in Geijera deplanchei contribute to its potential therapeutic applications in treating various health conditions.

    What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

    The main herbal preparations of Geijera deplanchei are used to extract its bioactive compounds.

    Geijera deplanchei tea is prepared by infusing the leaves in hot water to treat fever and rheumatic pain. A decoction of the roots is used to treat rheumatism and fever due to its anti-inflammatory properties. Geijera deplanchei tincture is prepared by steeping the leaves in ethanol to treat colds and sore throats.

    A salve made from the leaves is used topically to treat wounds and skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties.

    What are the possible side effects of using this herb improperly?

    The possible side effects of using Geijera deplanchei improperly include inducing nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and skin irritation due to its toxic properties, particularly the presence of cyanogenic glycosides and alkaloids.

    These compounds can release cyanide when ingested, metabolized, or broken down in the body, leading to a range of adverse reactions. Ingestion of Geijera deplanchei can also trigger allergic reactions, such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing, in sensitive individuals. The improper use of this plant can have severe consequences, including life-threatening cyanide poisoning, which can occur rapidly.

    The severity and type of side effects depend on the amount and method of consumption, as well as individual tolerance and sensitivity to its toxic compounds.

Rubus cordatiformis

Rubus cordatiformis, also known as the Heart-leaved Bramble, is a type of bramble shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making it beneficial for treating various health issues such as arthritis, wound infections, and cardiovascular diseases. The bioactive constituents of Rubus cordatiformis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and anthocyanins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional East Asian medicine, Rubus cordatiformis is commonly prepared as a tea, decoction, or tincture, and is also used to make capsules and other herbal supplements.

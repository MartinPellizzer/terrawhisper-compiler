What is Lomatia hirsuta subsp. obliqua?

Lomatia hirsuta subsp.

obliqua is a subspecies of the Lomatia hirsuta species, belonging to the family Proteaceae. It is an evergreen shrub with a woody stem, 0.5-1.5 meters in height, with a broad, lanceolate leaf shape and a length of 5-15 cm, with a hairy surface.

The leaves are arranged oppositely on the stem and have a cuneate base. The plant produces solitary, axillary inflorescences with a single white flower, 3-4 cm in diameter, with a four-lobed calyx and a corolla with four petals, which are notched at the apex.

The inflorescences are produced in the axils of the leaves, in the summer months.

Smilax japonica

Smilax japonica, also known as Japanese sarsaparilla, is a type of perennial climber native to East Asia, characterized by its woody stems and dark green, glossy leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help to alleviate symptoms of fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Smilax japonica include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Smilax japonica is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to treat a range of health conditions, including fever, respiratory infections, and skin irritations.

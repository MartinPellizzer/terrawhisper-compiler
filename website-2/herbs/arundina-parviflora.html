Arundina parviflora

Arundina parviflora, also known as the grass orchid, is a type of terrestrial orchid native to Asia, characterized by its small white flowers and long, grass-like leaves.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Arundina parviflora include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to prepare various herbal preparations, such as teas and infusions, as well as extracts and capsules, to support respiratory health and boost the immune system.

Astragalus vulnerariae

Astragalus vulnerariae, also known as Astragalus or Huang Qi, is a perennial plant species in the legume family, native to China and Mongolia, traditionally used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The health benefits of Astragalus vulnerariae include its immunomodulatory and adaptogenic properties, which help to enhance the body's natural defenses against infections and stress, as well as its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus vulnerariae include saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Astragalus vulnerariae include capsules, tablets, and decoctions, which are often used to promote overall health and well-being, particularly in the prevention and treatment of respiratory and cardiovascular diseases.

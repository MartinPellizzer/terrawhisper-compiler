Ranunculus hortensis

Ranunculus hortensis, commonly known as Lesser Celandine or Spring Celandine, is a perennial plant belonging to the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae) that thrives in moist environments.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been shown to aid in the treatment of skin conditions, such as eczema and acne. The bioactive constituents of Lesser Celandine include ranunculin, protoanemonin, and emodin, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Lesser Celandine is commonly prepared as a tincture, decoction, or salve, and is used to treat a range of conditions, including skin irritations, respiratory issues, and digestive problems.

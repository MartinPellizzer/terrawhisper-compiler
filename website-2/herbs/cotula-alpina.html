Cotula alpina

Cotula alpina, also known as Alpine Buttonweed or Mountain Everlasting, is a perennial plant species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which can aid in the treatment of wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Cotula alpina include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

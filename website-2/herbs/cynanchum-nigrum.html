classification

Cynanchum nigrum, also known as black swallowwort, belongs to the Apocynaceae family of plants. This family is commonly referred to as the dogbane or oleander family. The genus Cynanchum is a group of plants that are native to various parts of the world and are often classified as vining or shrub-like in growth habit. These plants are known for their showy flowers and ability to grow in a variety of environments. As a member of the Apocynaceae family, Cynanchum nigrum exhibits characteristics such as succulent stems and milky sap.

Syzygium laurifolium

Syzygium laurifolium, also known as the Indian laurel, is a medicinal plant species native to the tropical regions of Asia, characterized by its glossy, leathery leaves and showy white flowers.

The bioactive constituents of Syzygium laurifolium include flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

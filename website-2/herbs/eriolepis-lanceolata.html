Eriolepis lanceolata

Eriolepis lanceolata, commonly known as Lanceleaf or Western Yarrow, is a perennial plant native to North America, characterized by its fern-like foliage and small white or pink flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, providing relief from digestive issues, fever, and respiratory problems due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Lanceleaf Yarrow include flavonoids, sesquiterpenes, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Lanceleaf Yarrow include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to aid in wound healing, reduce fever, and soothe digestive issues.

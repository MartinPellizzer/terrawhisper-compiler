What is Isatis cappadocica subsp. besseri?

Isatis cappadocica subsp.

besseri is a botanical taxon within the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its biennial or perennial habit and glabrous, yellow-green stems. Its leaves are lanceolate or ovate-lanceolate, with an acute or obtuse apex and dentate margins, measuring 2-10 cm in length.

The plant produces small, yellow or white flowers in a racemose inflorescence, consisting of 4-petaled, 5-8 mm in diameter sepals and 4-petaled, 6-8 mm in diameter petals. The fruit is a silique, 2-6 cm in length, dehiscent and glabrous, containing 2-4 seeds.

It is native to Central Asia and the Caucasus region.

Crataegus segobricensis

Crataegus segobricensis, also known as the hawthorn tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Rosaceae family, native to the Iberian Peninsula.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, such as oligomeric proanthocyanidins (OPCs), which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Crataegus segobricensis are standardized extracts, teas, and capsules, often used to support heart health and reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease.

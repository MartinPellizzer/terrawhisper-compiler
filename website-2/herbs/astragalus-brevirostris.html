Astragalus brevirostris

Astragalus brevirostris, commonly known as Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial plant species belonging to the legume family (Fabaceae).

Traditionally, Astragalus brevirostris is valued for its potential health benefits, including its immunomodulatory, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which may help to boost the immune system, reduce fatigue, and protect against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus brevirostris include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Astragalus brevirostris is often used to prepare herbal formulas, such as dried root decoctions and capsules, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including respiratory infections, digestive issues, and cardiovascular disease.

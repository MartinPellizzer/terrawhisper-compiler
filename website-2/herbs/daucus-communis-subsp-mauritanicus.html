What is Daucus communis subsp. mauritanicus?

Daucus communis subsp.

mauritanicus is an annual or biennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Apiaceae, native to North Africa and the Mediterranean region. It grows up to 100 cm in height, with a taproot and a stem bearing compound umbels of small white flowers, each umbel consisting of 10-20 rays and a central umbel of 10-20 flowers.

The leaves are pinnately divided into 2-5 pairs of leaflets, with the lower leaflets being smaller than the upper ones. The plant's fruit is a schizocarp, composed of 2-4 mericarps, each mericarp containing 3-5 seeds.

The plant's growth habit and morphology are similar to those of Daucus carota.

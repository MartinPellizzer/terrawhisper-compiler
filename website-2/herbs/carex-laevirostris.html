classification

Carex laevirostris is a species of sedge that belongs to the family Cyperaceae. It is a type of grass-like perennial plant native to North America. The classification of Carex laevirostris is as follows: kingdom - Plantae, division - Magnoliophyta, class - Liliopsida, order - Poales, family - Cyperaceae, genus - Carex, and species - C. laevirostris. This plant is often found in wetlands, including marshes and swamps, and can grow up to a height of 1-2 feet. As a member of the Carex genus, it is closely related to other sedges, such as Carex stricta and Carex pensylvanica.

Larix griffithiana

Larix griffithiana, also known as Himalayan larch, is a deciduous coniferous tree native to the eastern Himalayas and parts of China.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, offering various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Larix griffithiana include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Larix griffithiana is often used to prepare tea, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being used in the form of powders, capsules, and ointments.

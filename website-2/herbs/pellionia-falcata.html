Pellionia falcata

Pellionia falcata, also known as heartleaf, is a plant species in the family Urticaceae, characterized by its distinctive, falcate-shaped leaves with pointed tips.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, offering various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities, which can aid in wound healing, reduce fever, and alleviate respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Pellionia falcata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Pellionia falcata is often used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, as well as being used in traditional remedies for fever, cough, and skin conditions.

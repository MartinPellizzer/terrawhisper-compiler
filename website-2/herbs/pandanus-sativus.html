Pandanus sativus

Pandanus sativus, also known as the Screw Pine, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific and Indian Oceans, valued for its medicinal and nutritional properties.

The health benefits of Pandanus sativus include its potential to reduce inflammation, improve digestion, and exhibit antioxidant properties, which can help protect against chronic diseases such as diabetes and cardiovascular disease. The bioactive constituents of Pandanus sativus include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds, which have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anti-cancer properties.

In traditional medicine, Pandanus sativus is often used to make herbal preparations such as teas, decoctions, and infusions, as well as being used in the production of edible oils and flour.

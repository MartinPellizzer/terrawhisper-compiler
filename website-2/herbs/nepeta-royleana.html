Nepeta royleana

Nepeta royleana, also known as Himalayan catmint, is a perennial plant species native to the Himalayan region, characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant blue flowers and gray-green foliage.

The bioactive constituents of Nepeta royleana include nepetalactone, a sesquiterpene lactone responsible for its sedative and analgesic effects, as well as other compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

In herbal medicine, Nepeta royleana is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or infused oil, which can be used to treat a range of conditions, including insomnia, digestive issues, and menstrual cramps.

Angelica geniculata

Angelica geniculata, also known as wild angelica, is a perennial plant native to North America, characterized by its tall stems and umbrella-like clusters of small white flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which make it useful in treating respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, as well as menstrual cramps and digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of Angelica geniculata include sesquiterpenes, phenolic acids, and volatile oils, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Angelica geniculata is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being dried and used in capsule or powder form for its medicinal properties.

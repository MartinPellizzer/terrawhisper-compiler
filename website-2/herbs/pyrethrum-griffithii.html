Pyrethrum griffithii

Pyrethrum griffithii, a flowering plant species, is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) known for its distinctive daisy-like flowers and high concentration of pyrethrins, a group of natural insecticides.

The bioactive constituents of Pyrethrum griffithii include pyrethrins (e.g., pyrethrin I and pyrethrin II), flavonoids, and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal and insecticidal properties.

Pyrethrum griffithii is commonly used in herbal preparations, such as tinctures, infusions, and ointments, to treat a range of health issues, including skin conditions, respiratory problems, and insect bites.

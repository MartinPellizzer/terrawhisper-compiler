Actaea simplex

Actaea simplex, also known as black cohosh, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America, belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes and mood swings, and has also been employed to treat menstrual cramps, anxiety, and other conditions. The bioactive constituents of black cohosh include triterpene glycosides, specifically actein and 23-epi-26-deoxyactein, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Black cohosh is commonly prepared as a dietary supplement in capsule or tincture form, often standardized to contain a specific amount of triterpene glycosides, and is also used in herbal teas and infusions.

side effects

Ranunculus angustifidus is not typically consumed or used for medicinal purposes, but like other plants in the Ranunculus genus, it may cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal upset in some individuals if ingested or touched. Its sap, which contains toxic compounds called ranunculin, can cause blisters or burns on the skin. Ingestion of the plant may also lead to more severe symptoms, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Handling the plant can also cause irritation to the eyes, nose, and throat. It is essential to handle the plant with caution and avoid contact with the skin or eyes.

Origanum brevidens

Origanum brevidens, also known as dwarf Turkish oregano, is a perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its compact growth habit and pungent, earthy flavor.

The bioactive constituents of Origanum brevidens include phenolic acids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, such as carvacrol, borneol, and linalool, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Erica florifera

Erica florifera, also known as the heather plant, is a low-growing, flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region, belonging to the Ericaceae family.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, including its ability to stimulate the immune system, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial and antifungal activities, making it a potential treatment for various health conditions such as coughs, colds, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of Erica florifera include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

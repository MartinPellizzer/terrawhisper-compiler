Euphorbia rugulosa

Euphorbia rugulosa is a succulent plant species native to South Africa, characterized by its small, hairy, and green stems.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in wound healing and reducing inflammation. Euphorbia rugulosa contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and diterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and extracts, which are used to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions.

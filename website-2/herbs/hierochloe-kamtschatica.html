Hierochloe kamtschatica

Hierochloe kamtschatica, also known as Kamtchatkan sweet grass or sweet grass of Kamtchatka, is a perennial grass native to the Kamchatka Peninsula in eastern Russia and parts of Alaska.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antioxidant effects, making it useful in the treatment of digestive issues, wounds, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Hierochloe kamtschatica include phenolic compounds, flavonoids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Hierochloe kamtschatica include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to promote digestion, soothe respiratory issues, and support wound healing.

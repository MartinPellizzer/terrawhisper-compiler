Angelica maritima

Angelica maritima, also known as sea angelica or maritime angelica, is a perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae) that grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and umbrella-like clusters of small white flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including digestive problems, respiratory infections, and inflammation, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Angelica maritima include angelic acid, angelicin, and ligustilide, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Angelica maritima is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is also used in combination with other herbs to create cough syrups, expectorant formulas, and digestive tonics.

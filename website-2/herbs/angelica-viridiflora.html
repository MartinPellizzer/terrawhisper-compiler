Angelica viridiflora

Angelica viridiflora, also known as Chinese angelica or green angelica, is a perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

It has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties, which are said to aid in reducing fever, relieving menstrual cramps, and promoting digestion. The bioactive constituents of Angelica viridiflora include angelic acid, ligustilide, and ferulic acid, among others, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Angelica viridiflora is often prepared as a dried root or rhizome, and is commonly used in teas, infusions, and tinctures to take advantage of its health benefits.

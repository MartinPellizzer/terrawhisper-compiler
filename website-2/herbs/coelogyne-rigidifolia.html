Coelogyne rigidifolia

Coelogyne rigidifolia, also known as the rigid-leaf Coelogyne, is a species of orchid native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia, characterized by its rigid, erect stems and bright yellow flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in the treatment of respiratory issues and skin conditions. Bioactive constituents of Coelogyne rigidifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are often used to support respiratory health and immune function.

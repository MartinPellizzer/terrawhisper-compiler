Amadea diffusa

Amadea diffusa, also known as Himalayan Balsam, is a highly invasive annual plant native to the Himalayas, characterized by its tall stature and showy white flowers.

The plant is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties, which may help alleviate conditions such as fever, cough, and respiratory infections. Bioactive constituents of Amadea diffusa include alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Amadea diffusa is often used to make infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, as well as being used in the production of herbal teas and supplements.

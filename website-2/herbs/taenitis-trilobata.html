Taenitis trilobata

Taenitis trilobata is a type of fern native to tropical regions, specifically in Africa and Southeast Asia, known for its unique and distinctive foliage.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, contributing to its use in traditional remedies for various health conditions. The bioactive constituents of Taenitis trilobata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Herbal preparations made from Taenitis trilobata include teas, infusions, and capsules, often used to treat conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues.

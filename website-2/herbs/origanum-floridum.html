Origanum floridum

Origanum floridum, also known as Wild Marjoram or Prickly Origanum, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

The plant is valued for its health benefits, which include antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in treating digestive issues, colds, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Origanum floridum include carvacrol, thymol, and borneol, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include essential oils, teas, and infusions, as well as dried leaves and flowers used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Sabatia maculata

Sabatia maculata, also known as the spotted sabatia or spotted butterfly orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in wound healing, reduce fever, and protect against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Sabatia maculata include gentianine, gentianol, and iridoid glycosides, among other compounds.

Traditionally, Sabatia maculata is used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat a range of health issues, including fever, respiratory infections, and skin conditions.

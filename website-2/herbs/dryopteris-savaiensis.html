Dryopteris savaiensis

Dryopteris savaiensis, also known as the Savai'i shield fern, is a species of fern native to the island of Savai'i in Samoa.

This plant has been traditionally used in Samoan medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to alleviate conditions such as arthritis, digestive issues, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Dryopteris savaiensis include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a tea or tincture, or used in topical applications to take advantage of its potential therapeutic benefits.

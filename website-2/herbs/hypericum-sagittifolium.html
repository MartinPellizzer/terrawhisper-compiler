Hypericum sagittifolium

Hypericum sagittifolium, also known as St. Andrew's cross or arrow-leaf St. John's Wort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, exhibiting analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, as well as its potential to treat anxiety, depression, and sleep disorders. The bioactive constituents of Hypericum sagittifolium include flavonoids, such as hyperoside and rutin, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Hypericum sagittifolium is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or oil, and is also used in topical creams and ointments to alleviate skin conditions and wounds.

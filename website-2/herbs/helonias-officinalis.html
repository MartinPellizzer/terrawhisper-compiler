Helonias officinalis

Helonias officinalis, also known as Queen's Root or Wild Ginger, is a perennial plant native to North America, belonging to the family Melanthiaceae.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and antiseptic properties, which have been traditionally used to treat a range of ailments, from digestive issues to respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Helonias officinalis include glycosides, saponins, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Helonias officinalis is commonly prepared as a tincture, syrup, or root extract, and is used to treat conditions such as coughs, colds, and menstrual cramps.

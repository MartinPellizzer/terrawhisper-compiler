Rauvolfia major

Rauvolfia major, also known as the greater poison arrow plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apocynaceae.

The plant is known for its numerous health benefits, including its anxiolytic and sedative properties, which make it useful in treating anxiety, insomnia, and hypertension, as well as its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. The bioactive constituents of Rauvolfia major include alkaloids such as reserpine, ajmaline, and yohimbine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Rauvolfia major is often prepared as a decoction or tincture, and it is also used in the production of herbal supplements and extracts, including standardized extracts and dried root preparations.

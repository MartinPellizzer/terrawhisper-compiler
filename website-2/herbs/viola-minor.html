Viola minor

Viola minor, also known as pansy violet or common violet, is a low-growing, perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa, characterized by its small, delicate flowers and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which have been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including digestive problems and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Viola minor include flavonoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, as well as iridoid glycosides, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In herbal medicine, Viola minor is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or topical cream, and is commonly used to soothe digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing.

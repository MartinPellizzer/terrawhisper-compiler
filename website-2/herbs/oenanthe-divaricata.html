Oenanthe divaricata

Oenanthe divaricata, also known as water-parsley or water fern, is a perennial aquatic plant native to East Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include treating fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Oenanthe divaricata include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Oenanthe divaricata is commonly prepared as a decoction or tea, as well as being used in combination with other herbs in traditional Chinese and Japanese medicine, often referred to as "da huang" or "miso-ko".

Huperzia aloifolia

Huperzia aloifolia, also known as the Lycopodiaceae family member, is a small, perennial plant that belongs to the club moss group of plants.

It has been traditionally used in various cultures for its purported health benefits, including improved cognitive function and neuroprotection, which are attributed to its ability to cross the blood-brain barrier and potentially inhibit the breakdown of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter associated with memory and learning. The bioactive constituents of Huperzia aloifolia include huperzine A, a potent inhibitor of acetylcholinesterase, as well as other alkaloids and flavonoids that may contribute to its health-promoting properties.

Huperzine A is often extracted and sold in supplement form, as well as being used in some herbal preparations, including capsules and tablets, although its efficacy and safety have not been extensively studied in humans.

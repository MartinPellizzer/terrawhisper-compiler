Alangium solomonense

Alangium solomonense, also known as Solomon's plum or Alangium, is a plant species native to the Pacific Islands, belonging to the family Alangiaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, as well as its ability to reduce fever and alleviate pain. The bioactive constituents of Alangium solomonense include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

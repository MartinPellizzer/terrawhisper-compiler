Polygonum australe

Polygonum australe, also known as Austral polygonum or Australian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Australia and New Zealand.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, which may aid in the treatment of various ailments including skin conditions and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Polygonum australe include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoid saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Polygonum australe is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are commonly used to treat skin conditions, sore throats, and other minor health complaints.

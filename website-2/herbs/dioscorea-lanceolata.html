Dioscorea lanceolata

Dioscorea lanceolata, also known as Chinese yam or mountain yam, is a species of yam that is native to East Asia and is a type of perennial climber in the Dioscoreaceae family.

The bioactive constituents of Dioscorea lanceolata include diosgenin, a steroid saponin with estrogenic and anti-inflammatory properties, as well as other glycosides and alkaloids.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Dioscorea lanceolata is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to promote digestion, relieve fatigue, and support the body's immune system.

Cantinoa althaeifolia

Cantinoa althaeifolia, also known as Marjorana, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae), native to the Mediterranean region and used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties.

The plant is valued for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, skin irritations, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Cantinoa althaeifolia include phenolic acids, flavonoids, and volatile oils, particularly limonene and beta-caryophyllene, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

The plant is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and essential oils, and is often combined with other herbs like peppermint and thyme to create soothing and fragrant blends.

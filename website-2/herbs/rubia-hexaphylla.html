Rubia hexaphylla

The bioactive constituents of Rubia hexaphylla include anthraquinones, such as rubiadin and rubiadin-1, as well as flavonoids and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Rubia hexaphylla is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, and can also be found in the form of dried root, tablets, or capsules, which are used to treat a range of ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions.

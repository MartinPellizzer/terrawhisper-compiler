Setaria paniculifera

Setaria paniculifera, also known as yellow bristlegrass, is a type of annual grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antioxidant properties, which may help to treat various ailments such as fever, respiratory issues, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Setaria paniculifera include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is often used to make herbal teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being incorporated into traditional remedies such as poultices and creams.

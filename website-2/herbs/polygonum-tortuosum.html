Polygonum tortuosum

Polygonum tortuosum, also known as creeping knotweed or curly knotgrass, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa and parts of Asia, characterized by its twisted stems and small, green leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Polygonum tortuosum include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

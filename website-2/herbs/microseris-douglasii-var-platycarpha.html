What is Microseris douglasii var. platycarpha?

Microseris douglasii var. platycarpha is a perennial herb belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It has basal rosettes of oblanceolate to spatulate leaves, 5-15 cm long, with cuneate bases and acute tips, and often has a grayish tinge. The inflorescence is a terminal, corymbiform array of 8-15 yellow flower heads, each 1-2 cm in diameter, with 10-15 ray florets and 20-30 disc florets. The flowers are heterogamous, with 3-5-merous corollas, and the achenes are 6-7 mm long, with 5-7 mm long pappi.

This variety typically grows in disturbed areas, such as roadsides, fields, and meadows.

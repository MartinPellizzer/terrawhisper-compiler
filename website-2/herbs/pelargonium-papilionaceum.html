Pelargonium papilionaceum

Pelargonium papilionaceum, also known as the fan-leaved pelargonium, is a species of succulent plant in the geranium family, native to South Africa.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which are believed to aid in the treatment of various conditions such as fever, cough, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Pelargonium papilionaceum include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into traditional herbal remedies, such as cough syrups and cold and flu preparations.

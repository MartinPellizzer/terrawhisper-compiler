What is Alchemilla vulgaris f. truncata?

Alchemilla vulgaris f. truncata is a botanical variety of the alpine lady's mantle, a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Rosaceae.

It has rounded, cordate leaves with 5-7 deeply lobed, serrate segments, and is typically 10-50 cm tall. The leaves are dark green above and pale green below, and are arranged in a rosette pattern on a short stem. Achemilla vulgaris f. truncata produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring and early summer, which are clustered in dense, globular corymbs.

The flowers are dioecious, meaning that separate plants produce either male or female flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

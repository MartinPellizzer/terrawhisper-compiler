Begonia timorensis

Begonia timorensis is a medicinal plant species native to Timor, an island in Southeast Asia, belonging to the Begoniaceae family.

The plant is traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin irritations, due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of Begonia timorensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional medicine, Begonia timorensis is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is commonly used to treat fever and skin conditions, as well as to reduce inflammation and alleviate pain.

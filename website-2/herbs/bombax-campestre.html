Bombax campestre

Bombax campestre, also known as the kapok tree, is a deciduous tree species native to tropical Africa, characterized by its large, showy flowers and distinctive seed pods.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin disorders, due to its antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Bombax campestre include flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids, which have been found to exhibit antioxidant and immunomodulatory activities.

The most common herbal preparations made from the plant include infusions, decoctions, and ointments, which are used to treat a range of ailments, from wounds and infections to fever and arthritis.

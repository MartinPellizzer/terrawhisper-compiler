Gymnosporia nemorosa

Gymnosporia nemorosa, commonly known as African Boxwood, is a plant species within the Anacardiaceae family, characterized by its shrub-like habit and small, fragrant leaves.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, triterpenoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

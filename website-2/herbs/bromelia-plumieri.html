Bromelia plumieri

Bromelia plumieri, also known as Plumier's pine, is a species of epiphytic plant in the bromeliad family, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and vibrant red flowers.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, offering potential health benefits such as antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects, which may aid in treating various conditions including wounds, infections, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Bromelia plumieri include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional medicine, Bromelia plumieri is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, or infusum to treat various health conditions, making it a sought-after herbal remedy in tropical regions.

Oxalis uruguaycola

Oxalis uruguayensis, also known as the Uruguay sorrel, is a perennial plant belonging to the wood sorrel family (Oxalidaceae), characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and vibrant pink or white flowers.

Traditionally, Oxalis uruguayensis has been used for its medicinal properties, including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, which are said to aid in the treatment of various health conditions such as arthritis, wounds, and infections. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and oxalic acid, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Oxalis uruguayensis is commonly prepared as a herbal tea, infusion, or tincture, often used to soothe digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing.

Crataegus prunifolia

Crataegus prunifolia, also known as the Chinese hawthorn tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia, belonging to the Rosaceae family and characterized by its thorny branches and small white flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support cardiovascular health, with its extracts exhibiting antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and vasodilatory properties that may help lower blood pressure and improve circulation. The bioactive constituents of Crataegus prunifolia include flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, as well as triterpenoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Crataegus prunifolia is often formulated into teas, extracts, and tablets, as well as being used to make jams and preserves, which are used to support heart health and promote overall well-being.

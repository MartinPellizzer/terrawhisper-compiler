Bactris guineensis

Bactris guineensis, also known as the peach palm, is a tropical palm tree native to the Amazon rainforest, producing edible fruits and edible starch-rich pith.

The health benefits of Bactris guineensis include its potential to reduce inflammation, lower cholesterol levels, and act as an antioxidant, due in part to its high content of fiber, vitamins, and minerals. The bioactive constituents of Bactris guineensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Bactris guineensis include a pulp extract used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, as well as a starch extracted from the pith, used as a food ingredient and dietary supplement.

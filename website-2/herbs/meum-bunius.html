Meum bunius

Meum bunius, commonly known as wild bishop's weed or meum, is a perennial herb belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including digestive issues and respiratory problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. The bioactive constituents of Meum bunius include coumarins, sesquiterpenes, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Meum bunius is often prepared as a tincture or infused in tea form, and is sometimes used to treat conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, and menstrual cramps.

Lotus americanus

Lotus americanus, also known as American Lotus or Water Chamomile, is a perennial aquatic plant that grows in shallow waters of North America, characterized by its delicate white or yellow flowers and broad, floating leaves.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to alleviate symptoms of anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Lotus americanus include flavonoids, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, Lotus americanus is often made into teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as dried and used in capsules or topical creams, to take advantage of its calming and soothing properties.

Achyranthes martinicensis

Achyranthes martinicensis, also known as Martinique chaff flower, is a tropical plant species belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine, particularly in the Caribbean, to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of A. martinicensis include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, A. martinicensis is often prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is used to treat conditions such as arthritis, skin irritations, and respiratory infections.

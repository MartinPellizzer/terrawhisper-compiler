Rubus × consanguis

Rubus × consanguis, also known as the hybrid black raspberry, is a cross between Rubus occidentalis (black raspberry) and Rubus idaeus (wild raspberry), producing a plant with unique characteristics and potential health benefits.

The health benefits of Rubus × consanguis include its high antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which may help protect against chronic diseases such as heart disease, cancer, and cognitive decline. The bioactive constituents of Rubus × consanguis include anthocyanins, ellagic acid, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

Rubus × consanguis is often used to make various herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and jams, which are valued for their potential health-promoting and culinary properties.

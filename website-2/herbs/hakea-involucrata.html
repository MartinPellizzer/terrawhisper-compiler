Hakea involucrata

Hakea involucrata, also known as the woolly hakea, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Western Australia, characterized by its grey, woolly foliage and clusters of white or cream-colored flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in Australian Aboriginal medicine, and its extracts have been found to exhibit various health benefits, including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which can aid in wound healing and the treatment of skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Hakea involucrata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Hakea involucrata is often used to make topical preparations, such as creams and ointments, to treat skin conditions and wounds.

Lodhra sulcata

Lodhra sulcata, also known as Symplocos cochinchinensis, is a plant species in the family Symplocaceae, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic and Unani medicine to treat various health conditions, including menstrual disorders, inflammation, and respiratory issues, due to its potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Lodhra sulcata include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Lodhra sulcata is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and capsules, as well as being incorporated into traditional Ayurvedic medicines like Lodhra guggulu, a herbal formulation used to treat menstrual disorders and other gynecological issues.

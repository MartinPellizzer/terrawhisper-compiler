Scopolina tangutica

Scopolina tangutica, also known as Tangut Scopolia, is a plant species native to the mountainous regions of China, belonging to the Solanaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, thanks to its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of Scopolina tangutica include scopolin, a scopoletin glycoside, along with other alkaloids and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

Traditionally, the plant is prepared into herbal teas, capsules, or tinctures, and is also used in combination with other herbs to create formulas for treating conditions such as bronchitis and pneumonia.

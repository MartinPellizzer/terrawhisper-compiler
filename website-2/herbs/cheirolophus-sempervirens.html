Cheirolophus sempervirens

Cheirolophus sempervirens, also known as the 'Madeiran teasel', is a perennial plant species native to the Madeira Islands, with a history of traditional use in folk medicine.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in relieving digestive issues, reducing fever, and soothing respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Cheirolophus sempervirens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as an infusion or tincture, and is also used in the production of herbal teas and extracts.

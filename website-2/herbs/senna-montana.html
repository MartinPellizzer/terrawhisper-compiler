Senna montana

Senna montana, also known as Mexican buckthorn or stinking cascarilla, is a plant species belonging to the Fabaceae family, native to the southwestern United States and Mexico.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its laxative and anti-inflammatory effects, which have been associated with the potential relief of symptoms such as constipation, diarrhea, and arthritis. The bioactive constituents of Senna montana include glycosides, flavonoids, and anthraquinones, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Senna montana has been prepared as a tea or infusion, and it is also used to make herbal tinctures and extracts, which are often used to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of various gastrointestinal disorders.

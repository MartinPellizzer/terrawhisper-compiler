Scilla minima

Scilla minima, also known as the autumn squill, is a small, perennial plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and blue or white bell-shaped flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties, which can aid in reducing fever, soothing respiratory issues, and treating skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Scilla minima include alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Scilla minima is often prepared as a tincture, infusion, or dried extract, and is commonly used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, as well as skin conditions like eczema and acne.

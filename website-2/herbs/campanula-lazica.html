Campanula lazica

Campanula lazica, also known as Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers and medicinal properties.

This plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including respiratory problems, gastrointestinal disorders, and fever, due to its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Campanula lazica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Campanula lazica include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often used to treat coughs, bronchitis, and other respiratory conditions.

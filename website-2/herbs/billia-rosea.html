Billia rosea

Billia rosea, also known as the "Rose Madder tree," is a plant species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Billia rosea include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Billia rosea is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as in traditional medicine practices like Ayurveda and Unani.

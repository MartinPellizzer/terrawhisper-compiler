Miconia robusta

Miconia robusta, also known as the "forest mint" or "wild mint," is a species of flowering plant in the family Melastomataceae, native to the tropical forests of Central and South America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which may aid in relieving respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Miconia robusta include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Miconia robusta is often made into teas, infusions, and tinctures, and is also used in topical applications, such as creams and ointments, to treat skin conditions like acne and wounds.

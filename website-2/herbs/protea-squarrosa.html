Protea squarrosa

Protea squarrosa, also known as the Square-leafed Sugarbush, is a flowering plant species native to the Western Cape province of South Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and fever, due to its reported anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties, which contribute to its potential health benefits. The bioactive constituents of Protea squarrosa include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to alleviate symptoms of colds, fever, and digestive issues.

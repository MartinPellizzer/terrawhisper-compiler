Huperzia saururus

Huperzia saururus, also known as Chinese club moss, is a type of liverwort, a non-vascular plant that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including the treatment of Alzheimer's disease, anxiety, and depression, due to its neuroprotective and antioxidant properties.

The bioactive constituents of Huperzia saururus include huperzine A, a potent inhibitor of acetylcholinesterase that can increase acetylcholine levels in the brain, as well as other alkaloids such as huperzine B and huperzine C. Huperzia saururus is often prepared in the form of capsules, tablets, or extracts, which are used to support cognitive function, memory, and mood regulation.

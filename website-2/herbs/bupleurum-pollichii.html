Bupleurum pollichii

Bupleurum pollichii, also known as Chinese thunder of heaven or Chaihu, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the genus Bupleurum in the family Apiaceae.

Traditionally, Bupleurum pollichii is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antitumor properties, as well as its ability to modulate the immune system and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression. The bioactive constituents of Bupleurum pollichii include saikosaponins, flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Bupleurum pollichii is commonly prepared in the form of decoctions, tinctures, and capsules, often in combination with other herbs, to treat a range of conditions, including fever, digestive disorders, and respiratory infections.

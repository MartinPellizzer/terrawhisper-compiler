Astragalus modesti

Astragalus membranaceus, commonly known as Astragalus or Huang qi, is a perennial herb belonging to the legume family.

The plant has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries, exhibiting various health benefits such as enhancing immune function, reducing inflammation, and improving cardiovascular health. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus include triterpene saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Astragalus is often used to make teas, capsules, and tinctures, with the dried root being the most common herbal preparation used in traditional medicine and modern supplements.

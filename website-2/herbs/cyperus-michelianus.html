Cyperus michelianus

Cyperus michelianus, also known as umbrella sedge or umbrella papyrus, is a perennial sedge plant native to tropical Africa, characterized by its umbrella-shaped inflorescence.

This plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, which contribute to its potential health benefits. The bioactive constituents of Cyperus michelianus include sesquiterpenes, alkaloids, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Cyperus michelianus is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, which are used to treat various health conditions, including respiratory issues, skin problems, and gastrointestinal disorders.

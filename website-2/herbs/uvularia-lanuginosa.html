Uvularia lanuginosa

Uvularia lanuginosa, also known as downy rattlesnake plant or yellow rattlesnake root, is a perennial plant species native to eastern North America, characterized by its yellow flowers and drooping, bell-shaped petals.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and expectorant properties. Bioactive constituents of Uvularia lanuginosa include sesquiterpenes, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant include tinctures, infusions, and decoctions, often used to treat colds, coughs, and respiratory issues.

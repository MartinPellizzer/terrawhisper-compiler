Torreya nucifera

Torreya nucifera, also known as Japanese nutmeg yew, is a plant species native to Japan, characterized by its unique nutmeg-like seeds and dark green, needle-like foliage.

Traditionally used in Eastern medicine, the seeds of Torreya nucifera are valued for their potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it useful in treating various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Torreya nucifera seeds include lignans, terpenoids, and essential oils, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Japanese herbal medicine, Torreya nucifera is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and tonics, as well as being used in the production of herbal oils and extracts.

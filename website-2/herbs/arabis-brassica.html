Arabis brassica

Arabis brassica, also known as Rock Cress, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, white, four-petaled flowers and edible leaves.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, as well as its ability to support respiratory health and reduce stress. The bioactive constituents of Arabis brassica include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and glucosinolates, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Arabis brassica is commonly used in herbal preparations, such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, boost the immune system, and promote relaxation.

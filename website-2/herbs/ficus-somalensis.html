Ficus somalensis

Ficus somalensis, also known as Somali fig or bush fig, is a plant species belonging to the fig family (Moraceae), native to the dry regions of East Africa.

The health benefits of Ficus somalensis include its potential anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as fever, cough, and skin infections. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Ficus somalensis is often made into herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health conditions, including respiratory and gastrointestinal issues.

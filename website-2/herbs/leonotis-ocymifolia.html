Leonotis ocymifolia

Leonotis ocymifolia, also known as Mountain Goldenrod, is a shrubby perennial plant native to the eastern parts of Southern Africa, characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, respiratory problems, and inflammation, due to its potential health benefits such as antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Leonotis ocymifolia include iridoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Leonotis ocymifolia is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, as well as being used in traditional remedies for colds, coughs, and other respiratory issues.

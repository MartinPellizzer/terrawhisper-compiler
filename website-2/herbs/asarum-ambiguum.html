Asarum ambiguum

Asarum ambiguum, also known as Chinese wild ginger or ambiguous ginseng, is a type of perennial herb belonging to the genus Asarum.

The plant is valued for its health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as digestive issues, fever, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Asarum ambiguum include iridoid glycosides, phenolic acids, and volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Asarum ambiguum is often prepared as a decoction, capsule, or tincture, and is commonly used in herbal remedies for its purported health benefits, including its ability to warm the body, calm the mind, and boost energy.

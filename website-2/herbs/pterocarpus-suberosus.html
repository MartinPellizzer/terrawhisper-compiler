Pterocarpus suberosus

Pterocarpus suberosus, also known as the African bloodwood or African ironwood, is a tropical tree species native to West Africa.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been shown to aid in wound healing, reduce fever, and alleviate digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Pterocarpus suberosus include flavonoids, tannins, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Pterocarpus suberosus is often prepared into teas, infusions, or decoctions, and also used in the production of tinctures and ointments.

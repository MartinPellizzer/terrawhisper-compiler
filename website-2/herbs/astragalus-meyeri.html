Astragalus meyeri

Astragalus meyeri, also known as Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial plant belonging to the legume family (Fabaceae) native to the mountainous regions of China and Mongolia.

Astragalus meyeri is valued for its potential health benefits, including immune system enhancement, anti-inflammatory properties, and antioxidant activity, which may help to prevent diseases such as diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and certain types of cancer. The bioactive constituents of Astragalus meyeri include saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Astragalus meyeri is often prepared as a decoction or tea, as well as in the form of capsules or tablets, to support immune function and promote overall well-being.

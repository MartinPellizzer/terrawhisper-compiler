Hypericum kimurae

Hypericum kimurae is a species of flowering plant in the family Hypericaceae, commonly known as the St. John's Wort plant.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with benefits including relief from anxiety and depression, as well as its ability to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. The bioactive constituents of Hypericum kimurae include hypericin, hyperforin, and other phenolic acids, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Hypericum kimurae is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or oil, with the latter two being the most common preparations used for its medicinal and topical applications.

Larix kaempferi

Larix kaempferi, also known as Japanese larch or Kaempfer's larch, is a deciduous coniferous tree native to Japan, China, and Korea.

The health benefits of Larix kaempferi include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various conditions such as fever, diarrhea, and respiratory infections. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Larix kaempferi is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as tea, decoctions, and infusions, as well as being used in the form of extracts and essential oils.

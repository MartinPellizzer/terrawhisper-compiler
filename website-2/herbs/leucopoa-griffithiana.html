Leucopoa griffithiana

Leucopoa griffithiana, commonly known as Griffith's hair grass or Griffith's fescue, is a perennial grass native to mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities that may help to reduce stress and improve respiratory health. The bioactive constituents of Leucopoa griffithiana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Leucopoa griffithiana is often used to make infusions, decoctions, and teas, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and salves for skin and wound healing.

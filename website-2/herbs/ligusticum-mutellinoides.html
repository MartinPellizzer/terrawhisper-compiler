Ligusticum mutellinoides

Ligusticum mutellinoides, also known as Sichuan lovage or Chinese lovage, is a perennial plant species native to China, characterized by its large, umbrella-like foliage and small white flowers.

Traditionally, Ligusticum mutellinoides has been used in Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and expectorant properties. The plant's bioactive constituents include volatile oils, coumarins, and sesquiterpene lactones, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Ligusticum mutellinoides is often prepared as a decoction or an infusion, and is also used in the production of traditional Chinese medicines such as Guizhi Tang and Jia Wei Xiao Qing Long Tang.

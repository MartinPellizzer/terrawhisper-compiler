What is Agropyron repens var. aristatum?

Agropyron repens var. aristatum is a perennial monocot in the family Poaceae, characterized by a slender, erect stem (up to 80 cm tall) with a flat, linear, and keeled leaf blade (up to 2.5 cm long) bearing a prominent midrib and a distinctive ligule.

Its inflorescence is a panicle (up to 30 cm long) composed of numerous spikelets, each bearing two or three fertile florets and one or two sterile florets. The spikelets are 1-3 cm long, with a short, linear glume and a longer, ovate lemma. The plant produces a greenish to purplish spikelet with two or three fertile florets, each bearing a single anther.

This taxon is considered a variant of Agropyron repens, a species native to Europe and Western Asia.

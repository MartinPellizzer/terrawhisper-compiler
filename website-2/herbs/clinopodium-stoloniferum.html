Clinopodium stoloniferum

Clinopodium stoloniferum, also known as wild basil or creeping wild basil, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which can aid in relieving digestive issues, reducing fever, and protecting against infections. The bioactive constituents of Clinopodium stoloniferum include essential oils, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, such as rosmarinic acid and carnosic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Clinopodium stoloniferum is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture to harness its health benefits and is often used to treat respiratory issues, such as coughs and colds.

Ranunculus lobbii

Ranunculus lobbii, also known as Himalayan buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including the treatment of fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus lobbii include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, which are believed to contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and decoctions, which are used to treat various ailments in traditional Chinese medicine.

side effects

Ranunculus lobbii contains ranunculin, a toxic compound that can cause severe skin irritation, burning, and blistering upon contact with the plant's sap. Handling the plant can also lead to gastrointestinal symptoms, such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, if ingested. Ingestion of large quantities can cause more severe symptoms, including liver and kidney damage, and even death in extreme cases. Ranunculus lobbii sap can also cause eye irritation and vision problems if it comes into contact with the eyes. It's essential to handle the plant with caution and wear protective clothing to avoid exposure to its toxic compounds.

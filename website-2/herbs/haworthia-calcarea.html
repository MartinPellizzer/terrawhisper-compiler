Haworthia calcarea

The Haworthia calcarea, commonly known as the calcarea haworthia or white marble haworthia, is a succulent plant species belonging to the genus Haworthia, characterized by its unique, white marble-like pattern on the leaves.

Traditionally, Haworthia calcarea has been used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its reported health benefits, such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Haworthia calcarea include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Haworthia calcarea is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are commonly used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma.

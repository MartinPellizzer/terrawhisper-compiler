Vitis cordifolia

Vitis cordifolia, also known as the Heart-leaf grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to the Himalayan region, belonging to the Vitaceae family.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and cardiovascular protective properties, which may help lower cholesterol levels and prevent atherosclerosis. The bioactive constituents of Vitis cordifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, Vitis cordifolia is used in herbal preparations, such as teas, decoctions, and extracts, often combined with other herbs to create remedies for cardiovascular diseases, as well as to support digestive and immune system health.

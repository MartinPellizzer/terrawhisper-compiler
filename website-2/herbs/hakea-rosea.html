Hakea rosea

Hakea rosea, also known as Pink Needlewood, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Western Australia, belonging to the Proteaceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in wound healing, reducing fever, and alleviating respiratory issues such as bronchitis. The bioactive constituents of Hakea rosea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Hakea rosea is often prepared as a tea or tincture, and it is also used in salves and creams to take advantage of its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

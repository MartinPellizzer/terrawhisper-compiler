Narcissus abscissus

Narcissus abscissus is a plant species within the Amaryllidaceae family, commonly known as a type of daffodil.

Traditionally, the plant has been used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, which may aid in the treatment of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma. The bioactive constituents of Narcissus abscissus include alkaloids, including Narcissus alkaloids and lycorine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is often made into preparations such as tinctures, infusions, and syrups, which are used to alleviate respiratory issues and other health complaints.

classification

Narcissus abscissus is a species of flowering plant in the genus Narcissus. It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae. This classification places Narcissus abscissus within the order Asparagales. At the class level, it is classified as Monocotyledonous, indicating it has one cotyledon in its seeds. Additionally, its kingdom classification is Plantae.

What is Juncus kraussii subsp. australiensis?

Juncus kraussii subsp.

australiensis is a plant species within the Juncaceae family, native to Australia. It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green, sometimes reddish, and grow up to 10-20 cm long.

The leaves are often in two ranks, flat, and have a smooth or slightly rough surface. The plant produces a single or sometimes a few brownish or purple spikelets at the top of the stem, containing one to several flowers.

The spikelets are 1-3 cm long, and the flowers are usually unisexual, with male flowers having 3 stamens and female flowers having a 3-parted ovary.

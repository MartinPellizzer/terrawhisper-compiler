Psiadia angustifolia

Psiadia angustifolia, also known as Narrow-leaved Psiadia, is a shrub-like plant species in the Asteraceae family native to Madagascar.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and wounds, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Psiadia angustifolia include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Psiadia angustifolia include teas, infusions, and tinctures, often used to manage pain, reduce inflammation, and boost the immune system.

What is Hieracium laevigatum subsp. sagotii?

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

sagotii is a subspecies of the Hieracium laevigatum species, belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial herbaceous plant with a height of up to 50 cm, featuring a rosette of lanceolate to elliptical leaves, 5-20 cm long, and 0.5-2 cm wide, with a glabrous to pubescent lower surface and a pubescent upper surface.

The inflorescence is a corymbiform corymb, consisting of 10-20 capitula, each with 10-20 ligulate florets and 2-5 discoid florets, and a yellow corolla, 6-8 mm long. The plant produces achenes, 3-4 mm long, with a pappus of 10-12 barbellate bristles, 2-3 mm long, and a glabrous to pubescent body. Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

sagotii is found in mountainous regions of Central America.

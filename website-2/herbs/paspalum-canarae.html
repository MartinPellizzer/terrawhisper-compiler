Paspalum canarae

Paspalum canarae, also known as Kanthari or Bhui Amla, is a perennial grass native to the tropical regions of Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of P. canarae include flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, P. canarae is commonly used to make decoctions, infusions, and ointments, as well as being incorporated into herbal supplements and topical creams.

Chamaemelum achilleifolium

Chamaemelum nobile, commonly known as chamomile, is a perennial plant in the daisy family (Asteraceae) native to Europe and Western Asia.

The health benefits of chamomile include its soothing and calming effects, which can help to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation, as well as its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in wound healing and protect against infections. The bioactive constituents of chamomile include apigenin, luteolin, and bisabolol, which are responsible for its medicinal properties and are found in the plant's flowers, leaves, and essential oil.

Chamomile is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, capsules, and topical creams, and is often used to treat insomnia, digestive issues, and skin irritations.

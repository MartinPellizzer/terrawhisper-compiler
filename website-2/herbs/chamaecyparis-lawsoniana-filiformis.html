Chamaecyparis lawsoniana filiformis

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana filiformis, commonly known as Lawson's cypress or Lawson false cypress, is a plant species in the cypress family (Cupressaceae) that is native to the Pacific coast of North America.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties, which provide relief and reduce congestion. The bioactive constituents of Lawson's cypress include terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Lawson's cypress include essential oils, teas, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues and promote relaxation.

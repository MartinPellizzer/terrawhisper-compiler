Chrysanthemum japonicum

Chrysanthemum japonicum, also known as Japanese chrysanthemum, is an herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia, characterized by its yellow flowers and bitter, astringent properties.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate fever, reduce inflammation, and treat various respiratory issues, such as coughs and sore throats, due to its antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Chrysanthemum japonicum include flavonoids, triterpenoids, and volatile oils, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant is often prepared into teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being used in combination with other herbs in formulae to treat a range of health conditions.

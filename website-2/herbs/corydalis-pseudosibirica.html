Corydalis pseudosibirica

Corydalis pseudosibirica, also known as Sibirian corydalis or pseudosibirian corydalis, is a perennial plant species in the Corydalis genus, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

Traditionally, Corydalis pseudosibirica has been used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of anxiety, insomnia, and pain, and its health benefits are attributed to its ability to modulate the body's stress response and promote relaxation. The bioactive constituents of Corydalis pseudosibirica include alkaloids such as tetrahydropalmatine, which contribute to its sedative, analgesic, and anti-inflammatory properties.

Corydalis pseudosibirica is commonly used in herbal preparations, including teas, tinctures, and capsules, often in combination with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

Allium castellanense

Allium castellanense, also known as the Castilian garlic chive, is a species of wild garlic native to the Iberian Peninsula.

The health benefits of Allium castellanense include its potential to reduce inflammation, lower cholesterol levels, and exhibit antimicrobial properties, making it a valuable addition to a healthy diet. The bioactive constituents of this plant include sulfur-containing compounds, such as allicin and saponins, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Allium castellanense is often prepared as an infusion or tincture, and is used to treat conditions such as digestive issues and respiratory infections.

Narcissus × rugulosus

Narcissus × rugulosus, also known as the Bulbous Narcissus, is a hybrid plant species within the Amaryllidaceae family, characterized by its white or pale yellow trumpet-shaped flowers and bulbous growth habit.

Traditionally, this plant has been used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its potential health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Narcissus × rugulosus include alkaloids, such as galantamine and lycorine, as well as other compounds like saponins and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Narcissus × rugulosus is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as being incorporated into ointments and salves for topical application.

classification

Narcissus × rugulosus is classified as a hybrid species of flowering plants in the family Amaryllidaceae. It is a cross between the wild daffodil (Narcissus pseudonarcissus) and another species of Narcissus. The classification of this species is further supported by its unique characteristics, such as its smaller size and more delicate appearance compared to its parent species. This classification is also reflected in its scientific name, Narcissus × rugulosus, which indicates its hybrid origin. As a hybrid, Narcissus × rugulosus is often grown in gardens for its attractive flowers and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

side effects

Narcissus × rugulosus is known to be toxic if ingested, and its bulbs contain a toxic compound called lycorine, which can cause vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain if consumed by humans or animals. Ingestion of the plant's bulbs can also lead to cardiac arrhythmias, tremors, and other neurological symptoms. Handling the plant's bulbs can cause skin irritation in some individuals. The toxicity of Narcissus × rugulosus is particularly concerning for pets, as they may be attracted to the plant's attractive flowers. If ingested, prompt veterinary attention is essential to prevent further complications.

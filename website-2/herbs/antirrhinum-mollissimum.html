Antirrhinum mollissimum

Antirrhinum mollissimum, also known as Downy Snapdragon, is a perennial plant species in the family Plantaginaceae, characterized by its velvety leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine due to its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which may aid in treating wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Antirrhinum mollissimum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and iridoid glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat coughs, sore throats, and digestive issues.

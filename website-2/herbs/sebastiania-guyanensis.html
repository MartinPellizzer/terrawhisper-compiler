Sebastiania guyanensis

Sebastiania guyanensis, also known as the Guyanese calumba, is a plant species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which can aid in the treatment of various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Sebastiania guyanensis include saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Sebastiania guyanensis is commonly prepared as a root decoction or an infusion, and is also used in the production of herbal teas and capsules.

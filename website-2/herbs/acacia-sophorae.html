Acacia sophorae

Acacia sophorae, also known as Catechu or Katechu, is a plant species within the Acacia genus, which is native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

The plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which help in treating conditions such as wounds, fever, and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Acacia sophorae include catechin, epicatechin, and gallic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Acacia sophorae is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as kattha, a dried extract used in Ayurvedic medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, and in some Western herbal traditions, it is used to make a tea or tincture for its antimicrobial and digestive benefits.

Glyceria micrantha

Glyceria micrantha, also known as small-fruited mannagrass, is a perennial grass native to North America, characterized by its short stems and small, greenish-brown flowers.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it beneficial for treating conditions such as arthritis, gastrointestinal issues, and skin problems. The bioactive constituents of Glyceria micrantha include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which can be used to support digestive health and reduce inflammation.

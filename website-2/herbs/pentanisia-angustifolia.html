Pentanisia angustifolia

Pentanisia angustifolia, also known as the narrow-leaved Pentanisia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rubiaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in the treatment of various ailments such as fever, cough, and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Pentanisia angustifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditional herbal preparations made from this plant include decoctions, infusions, and ointments, often used to treat respiratory issues, skin conditions, and infections.

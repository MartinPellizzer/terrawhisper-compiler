What is Lonicera caerulea subsp. villosa?

Lonicera caerulea subsp.

villosa is a subspecies of shrub native to eastern Asia, belonging to the family Caprifoliaceae. It is characterized by its deciduous, woody stems that grow up to 5 meters in length, with a diameter of up to 10 cm.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, measuring 3-10 cm in length, with a serrated margin and a pointed apex. The flowers are purple, tubular, and 2-3 cm long, produced in clusters at the end of branches in late spring.

The plant produces edible blue-black berries, 1-2 cm in diameter, which are rich in anthocyanins and other bioactive compounds.

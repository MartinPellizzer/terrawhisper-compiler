What is Rhamnus globosa var. taquetii?

Rhamnus globosa var. taquetii is an Asian plant species belonging to the family Rhamnaceae.

It is a shrub or small tree growing up to 10 meters in height, with a glabrous or pubescent stem. The leaves are simple, alternate, elliptic to ovate, 4-12 centimeters long, and 2-6 centimeters wide, with a rounded base and an acute apex. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and borne in axillary clusters, while the fruit is a black, globose drupe, 8-12 millimeters in diameter.

Rhamnus globosa var. taquetii is native to Korea and Japan, where it grows in mountainous regions.

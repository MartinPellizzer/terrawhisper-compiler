Morinda pacifica

Morinda pacifica, also known as Noni, is a small evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands, belonging to the family Rubiaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which may help alleviate symptoms of various conditions, including pain, fever, and infections. The bioactive constituents of Morinda pacifica include morindin, morindone, and other flavonoids, as well as iridoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Morinda pacifica is often used to make teas, tinctures, and capsules, as well as topical creams and ointments, which are used to treat a range of health issues.

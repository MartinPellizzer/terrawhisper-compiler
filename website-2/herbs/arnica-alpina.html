Arnica alpina

Arnica alpina, also known as Alpine Arnica, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae).

The plant has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, offering potential health benefits such as reducing pain and swelling, promoting wound healing, and alleviating symptoms of arthritis and other musculoskeletal disorders. The bioactive constituents of Arnica alpina include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and triterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Topical preparations made from Arnica alpina, such as creams, ointments, and gels, are among the most common herbal remedies used to harness its therapeutic benefits.

Individuals with allergies or sensitivities should exercise caution when handling or consuming this plant.

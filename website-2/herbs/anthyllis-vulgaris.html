Anthyllis vulgaris

Anthyllis vulgaris, also known as kidney vetch or common kidney vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antidiarrheal effects, as well as potential applications in treating gastrointestinal issues and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Anthyllis vulgaris include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are believed to contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Anthyllis vulgaris is commonly used in herbal preparations, such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as in traditional folk medicine remedies, particularly for treating digestive issues and skin problems.

classification

Anthyllis vulgaris is a perennial legume native to Europe and Western Asia. It belongs to the family Fabaceae, commonly known as the pea or legume family. This classification places Anthyllis vulgaris in the subfamily Faboideae and the tribe Genisteae. The species is further categorized as being in the genus Anthyllis, which includes approximately 15 species of flowering plants. Anthyllis vulgaris is also known as kidney vetch due to its kidney-shaped seeds.

Additionally, it may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, due to its potential to lower blood pressure. Some people may also experience allergic reactions or skin irritation when handling the plant. Furthermore, pregnant or breastfeeding women should exercise caution when using Anthyllis vulgaris, as its effects on the fetus or baby are not well understood. It is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using this plant for medicinal purposes.

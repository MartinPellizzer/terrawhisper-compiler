Lomatium fusiformis

Lomatium fusiformis, also known as Fern-leaved Lomatium or Desert Biscuitroot, is a perennial plant native to the Great Basin region in North America, characterized by its fern-like foliage and small yellow flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties, which may aid in treating respiratory issues, fever, and skin conditions. Bioactive constituents of Lomatium fusiformis include sesquiterpenes, coumarins, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Lomatium fusiformis is often prepared as a tincture, tea, or dried root, and is used to treat coughs, colds, and flu, as well as to reduce inflammation and promote overall well-being.

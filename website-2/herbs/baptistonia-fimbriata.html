Baptistonia fimbriata, commonly known as Baptisia, is a perennial legume native to North America, valued for its medicinal properties and unique appearance.

The bioactive constituents of Baptisia include alkaloids, glycosides, and isoflavones, such as baptifoline, baptifol, and genistein, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

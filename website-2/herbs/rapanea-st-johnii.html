Rapanea st-johnii

The bioactive constituents of Rapanea st-johnii include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Rapanea st-johnii is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or infused oil, and is used to treat a range of ailments, although its modern use is largely replaced by the more popular St John's wort (Hypericum perforatum) species.

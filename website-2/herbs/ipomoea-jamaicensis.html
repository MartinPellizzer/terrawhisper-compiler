Ipomoea jamaicensis

Ipomoea jamaicensis, also known as Jamaican morning glory, is a perennial vine native to the Caribbean and tropical regions of Central and South America.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its potential health benefits, including the treatment of fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, as well as its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Ipomoea jamaicensis include flavonoids, glycosides, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often employed to alleviate symptoms of colds, flu, and other respiratory infections.

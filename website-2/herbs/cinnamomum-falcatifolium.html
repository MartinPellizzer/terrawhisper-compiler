Cinnamomum falcatifolium

Cinnamomum falcatifolium, also known as the Cape York sassafras or mountain sassafril, is a species of evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Australia's Cape York Peninsula.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its potential anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties. The bioactive constituents of Cinnamomum falcatifolium include essential oils, such as safrole and myristicin, as well as various phenolic compounds and terpenoids.

The most common herbal preparations made with this plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, although its use is mostly limited to traditional medicine and local communities.

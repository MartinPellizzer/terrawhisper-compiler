Ruschia marianae

Ruschia marianae, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant species native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which can aid in soothing wounds, reducing pain, and combating infections. The bioactive constituents of Ruschia marianae include aloin, aloe-emodin, and other anthraquinone glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is often used to make herbal preparations, such as topical creams, ointments, and oral capsules, often in the form of aloe vera gel or juice.

Cassia alexandrina

Cassia alexandrina, also known as Senna alexandrina, is a perennial plant that belongs to the legume family (Fabaceae), native to the Middle East and North Africa.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in the treatment of constipation and indigestion, as its seeds contain anthraquinone glycosides, which stimulate bowel movements and aid in digestion. The bioactive constituents of Cassia alexandrina include alexandrin, emodin, and sennosides, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional medicine, Cassia alexandrina is commonly used to prepare herbal laxatives, such as capsules, tablets, and teas, which are used to treat constipation and other gastrointestinal disorders.

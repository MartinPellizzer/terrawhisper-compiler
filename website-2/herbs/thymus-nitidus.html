Thymus nitidus

Thymus nitidus, also known as Dwarf Thyme or Mediterranean Thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and tiny white or pink flowers.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, including its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects, which have been traditionally used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, as well as digestive problems. The bioactive constituents of Thymus nitidus include volatile oils, particularly thymol and carvacrol, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Thymus nitidus is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and essential oils, which are often used to soothe respiratory issues, promote digestion, and support immune function.

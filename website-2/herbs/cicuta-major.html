Cicuta major

Cicuta major, also known as giant hemlock, is a perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae) that is native to Europe and Western Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including its potential to treat fever, rheumatism, and as a diuretic. The bioactive constituents of Cicuta major include furanocoumarins, such as cicutalin and cicutoxin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Cyperus sublaevicarinatus

Cyperus sublaevicarinatus, commonly known as the Asian umbrella sedge, is a type of sedge plant native to tropical Asia.

Traditionally, the plant has been used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive problems, due to its anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of Cyperus sublaevicarinatus include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

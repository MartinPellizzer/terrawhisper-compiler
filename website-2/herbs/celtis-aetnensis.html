Celtis aetnensis

The plant Celtis aetnensis, also known as the Etna hackberry or Sicilian hackberry, is a species of deciduous shrub or small tree that is native to the Mediterranean region.

The health benefits of Celtis aetnensis include its potential to reduce inflammation and improve cardiovascular health due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Celtis aetnensis include teas, tinctures, and infusions, often used to treat digestive issues and support overall well-being.

classification

Celtis aetnensis is classified within the family Cannabaceae. However, some sources place it in the family Ulmaceae. The plant is also classified in the order Rosales. Further classification includes the genus Celtis and the subfamily Celtidoideae. Its classification is part of ongoing research and may be subject to change.

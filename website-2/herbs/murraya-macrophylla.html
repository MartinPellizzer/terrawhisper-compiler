Murraya macrophylla

Murraya macrophylla, also known as orange Jessamine or Chinese Box Orange, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia, belonging to the Rutaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its health benefits, which include reducing anxiety, promoting relaxation, and aiding in the treatment of fever, cough, and cold. The bioactive constituents of Murraya macrophylla include limonoids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant include teas, infusions, and extracts, often used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions.

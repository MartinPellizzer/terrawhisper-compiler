What is Acanthocalycium rhodotrichum subsp. chacoanum?

Acanthocalycium rhodotrichum subsp.

chacoanum is a cactus subspecies belonging to the family Cactaceae. It is characterized by globose to short-cylindrical stems, typically up to 10 cm in diameter, with 12 to 15 ribs and short, brown spines.

The flowers are funnel-shaped, typically bright yellow, 5 to 6 cm in length, and appear at the plant's apex. The plant's distribution is limited to the Chaco region in northern Argentina.

It grows in well-drained soil in full sun.

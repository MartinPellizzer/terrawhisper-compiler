Huberia cordifolia

Huberia cordifolia is a plant species belonging to the family Melastomataceae, native to the tropical forests of Madagascar.

The leaves of H. cordifolia have been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Bioactive constituents present in H. cordifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, H. cordifolia is commonly used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used in topical applications such as creams and ointments.

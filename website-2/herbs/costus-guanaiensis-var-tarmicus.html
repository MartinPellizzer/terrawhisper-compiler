What is Costus guanaiensis var. tarmicus?

Costus guanaiensis var. tarmicus is a botanical variety within the species Costus guanaiensis, belonging to the family Costaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant, with upright, unbranched stems reaching up to 3 meters in height, and 10-20 cm in diameter. The leaves are alternate, elliptical to lanceolate, and 15-40 cm long, with a green upper surface and pale green lower surface. The inflorescence is a spike-like raceme, bearing funnel-shaped flowers with a greenish-yellow to yellow color, 10-12 cm long, and 4-6 cm wide.

The flowers have six petals, six stamens, and a trilocular ovary containing many seeds.

Costunolide has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities, while guaiol exhibits anti-inflammatory, antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. α-guaiene displays anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities, contributing to the plant's potential in treating various diseases. The main herbal preparations of Costus guanaiensis var, tarmicus are costus tincture which is prepared by soaking the root in a mixture of glycerin and water, costus tea which involves steeping the root in hot water to release its bioactive compounds, costus infusion which is made by steeping the root in cold water for an extended period, costus decoction which involves boiling the root in water to release its soluble constituents, and infusion of costus which is prepared by adding the root to hot water and then straining it, these preparations are used to treat various health conditions due to the presence of sesquiterpenes, phenolic compounds and flavonoids which exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.

What are the possible side effects of using this herb improperly?

The possible side effect of using Costus guanaiensis var. tarmicus are nausea, respiratory depression, allergic reactions, vomiting, and diarrhea.

These side effects can occur due to the presence of toxic compounds such as guanidin, guanidine, and tarmicine in the plant. Nausea and vomiting are common symptoms of gastrointestinal irritation caused by the toxic compounds, while respiratory depression can occur due to the central nervous system depressant properties of tarmicine. Allergic reactions can range from mild skin irritation to anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition that requires immediate medical attention.

The gastrointestinal symptoms, including diarrhea, are a result of the plant's ability to disrupt normal gut function, leading to excessive water loss and electrolyte imbalance.

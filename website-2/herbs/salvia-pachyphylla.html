Salvia pachyphylla

Salvia pachyphylla, also known as the "thick-leaved sage," is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective properties, which may aid in reducing stress and anxiety, improving cognitive function, and alleviating symptoms of depression. The bioactive constituents of Salvia pachyphylla include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, such as rosmarinic acid and ursolic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Salvia pachyphylla is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine and can be found in various preparations, including teas, tinctures, and capsules, as well as in aromatherapy applications.

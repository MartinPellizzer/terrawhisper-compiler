Hieracium pinnatifidum

Hieracium pinnatifidum is a perennial plant species commonly known as hawkweed or devil's paintbrush, belonging to the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, and its health benefits include anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which can help soothe skin irritations and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Hieracium pinnatifidum include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Hieracium pinnatifidum is often prepared as a tincture, tea, or salve, which can be used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and minor infections.

Hypericum corymbosum

Hypericum corymbosum, also known as St. John's Wort, is a perennial plant native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its clusters of small, yellow flowers and red, berry-like fruits.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antidepressant effects, making it a popular treatment for anxiety, insomnia, and mild to moderate depression. The bioactive constituents of St. John's Wort include hyperforin, hypericin, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, St. John's Wort is often prepared as a tea, tincture, or oil, which can be used to treat a range of health issues, including wounds, skin irritations, and gastrointestinal problems.

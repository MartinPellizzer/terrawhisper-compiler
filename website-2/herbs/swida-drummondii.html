Swida drummondii

Swida drummondii, also known as Drummond's hawthorn or Crataegus drummondiana, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America, belonging to the rose family (Rosaceae).

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in reducing cardiovascular stress and improving heart health, attributed to its ability to lower blood pressure, prevent blood clots, and improve blood lipid profiles. The bioactive constituents responsible for its health benefits include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, specifically crataegus compounds such as crataegus flavonoids and crataegus procyanidins.

The most common herbal preparations made with Swida drummondii include capsules, tablets, and teas, often standardized to contain a specific amount of crataegus extract to ensure efficacy and consistency.

Myrcia abrantea

Myrcia abrantea, also known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and gastrointestinal disorders, due to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of Myrcia abrantea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and diterpenes, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The plant is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and extracts, which are often used to treat respiratory issues, digestive problems, and skin conditions, although more research is needed to confirm its efficacy.

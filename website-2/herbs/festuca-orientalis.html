Festuca orientalis

Festuca orientalis, also known as Oriental fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its slender stems and feathery foliage.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which may aid in the treatment of various ailments such as digestive issues, skin conditions, and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Festuca orientalis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In herbal medicine, Festuca orientalis is often prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, as well as being used in the form of dried flowers, leaves, and roots, to leverage its medicinal properties.

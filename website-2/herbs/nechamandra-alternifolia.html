Nechamandra alternifolia

Nechamandra alternifolia, also known as Indian pennywort, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical Asia, valued for its medicinal properties.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and respiratory issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Indian pennywort include flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic benefits.

In traditional Ayurvedic and Unani medicine, the plant is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, decoctions, and infusions, as well as extracts and tinctures.

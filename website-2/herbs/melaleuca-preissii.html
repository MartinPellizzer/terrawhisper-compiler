Melaleuca preissii

Melaleuca preissii, also known as the Northern Tea Tree, is a plant species native to Western Australia, characterized by its small, white flowers and aromatic foliage.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, which include antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it useful in traditional medicine for treating skin infections and wounds. The bioactive constituents of Melaleuca preissii include cymene, limonene, and terpinen-4-ol, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from this plant include essential oils and teas, which are often used topically to soothe skin irritations and internally to support immune function.

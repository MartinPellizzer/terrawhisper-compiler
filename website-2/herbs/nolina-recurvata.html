Nolina recurvata

Nolina recurvata, also known as the Blue Agave or Sotol, is a succulent plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in North America, characterized by its rosettes of long, sharp leaves.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include its ability to treat digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and lower blood sugar levels, making it a popular natural remedy for various health conditions. The bioactive constituents of Nolina recurvata include flavonoids, saponins, and glycosides, which have been shown to have antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.

In traditional medicine, the plant is often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, with the most common herbal preparations being sotol tea, used to aid digestion and reduce inflammation.

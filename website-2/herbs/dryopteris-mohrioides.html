Dryopteris mohrioides

Dryopteris mohrioides, also known as western wood fern, is a species of fern native to the Pacific coast of North America.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to reduce pain and inflammation, and protect against cell damage. The bioactive constituents of D. mohrioides include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Traditionally, D. mohrioides has been used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as in topical creams and salves, to support wound healing and alleviate conditions such as arthritis and gout.

classification

Dryopteris mohrioides is a species of fern in the family Dryopteridaceae. It is a member of the genus Dryopteris, which includes about 200 species of ferns. The genus Dryopteris is part of the larger order Polypodiales, which includes many families of ferns. The order Polypodiales is in the class Polypodiopsida, a group of vascular plants that includes ferns and their allies. The class Polypodiopsida is in the division Pteridophyta, which includes all ferns.

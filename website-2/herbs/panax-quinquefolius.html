What is Panax quinquefolius?

Panax quinquefolius is a perennial plant native to eastern North America, belonging to the family Araliaceae.

It grows 2-3 feet tall with simple, alternate leaves, typically 5-7 leaflets, and a woody stem. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, 3-9 inches long, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles. Panax quinquefolius produces a single stem with a crown of leaves, and its rhizome contains multiple small roots.

The plant is also known as American ginseng, a medicinal plant valued for its root, which is harvested after 4-6 years of growth.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Panax quinquefolius are ginseng capsules, ginseng extract, ginseng tea, tincture, and ginseng tincture.

Ginseng capsules contain dried and powdered ginseng root, often standardized to a specific concentration of ginsenosides. Ginseng extract is a concentrated form of the plant's active compounds, often used in liquid or powder form, and can be added to food or beverages.

Ginseng tea, tincture, and ginseng tincture are liquid preparations made by infusing the root in hot water or a solvent, such as ethanol, to extract the bioactive compounds.

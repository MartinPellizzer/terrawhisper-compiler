Ranunculus linearis

Ranunculus linearis, also known as linear-leaved buttercup, is a perennial plant species within the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its slender, linear leaves and bright yellow flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiviral properties, making it effective in treating wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus linearis include alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is often prepared as a tincture, infusion, or decoction, and is commonly used in traditional herbal remedies, such as treating eczema, acne, and bronchitis.

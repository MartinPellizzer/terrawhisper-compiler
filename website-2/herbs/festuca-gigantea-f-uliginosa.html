What is Festuca gigantea f. uliginosa?

Festuca gigantea f. uliginosa is a taxonomic variant of giant fescue, a perennial grass that belongs to the family Poaceae.

It exhibits a tufted habit with leaves being flat, linear, and typically 0.3-1.5 mm wide, often being glaucous or sometimes green, and bearing a prominent midvein. The leaf sheath is usually tubular and glabrous, while the ligule is short and membranous. Festuca gigantea f. uliginosa is primarily found in wetland areas, typically thriving in saturated or intermittently flooded conditions.

This variant is often distinguished by its adaptation to moist environments, characterized by its dense growth and ability to tolerate periodic inundation.

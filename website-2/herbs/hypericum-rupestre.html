Hypericum rupestre

Hypericum rupestre, also known as St. John's Wort of the Mountains, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region, belonging to the Hypericum genus.

The plant has been traditionally used for its health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antidepressant properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression. The bioactive constituents of Hypericum rupestre include hyperforin, hypericin, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Hypericum rupestre is commonly prepared as an infusions, tinctures, and capsules, as well as being used topically to treat wounds and skin irritations.

Parkia africana

Parkia africana, also known as Afang or African raffia tree, is a tropical plant species native to tropical Africa, belonging to the Fabaceae family.

The plant has been utilized in traditional medicine for centuries, with its leaves, seeds, and bark providing various health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which can help to alleviate symptoms of fever, cough, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Parkia africana include flavonoids, alkaloids, saponins, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Traditional herbal preparations made from the plant include teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as extracts used in the treatment of various ailments, and it is also used in some parts of Africa as a food ingredient.

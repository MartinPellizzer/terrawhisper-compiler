Buxus obtusifolia

Buxus obtusifolia, also known as Japanese boxwood, is a species of flowering plant in the box family (Buxaceae) that is native to Japan and China.

Traditionally, the leaves and bark of Buxus obtusifolia have been used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Buxus obtusifolia is commonly prepared into teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being used to make dried extracts and capsules, to promote overall health and well-being.

Angelica glabra

Angelica glabra, also known as American angelica or tree angelica, is a perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae) that is native to North America.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antispasmodic properties. The bioactive constituents of Angelica glabra include volatile oils, flavonoids, and sesquiterpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

American angelica is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, as well as in dried root form for use in soups and stews.

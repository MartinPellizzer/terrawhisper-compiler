What is Cerasus serrulata var. lannesiana?

Cerasus serrulata var. lannesiana is a botanical variety of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that grows up to 6 meters in height, with a broad, rounded crown and a straight or slightly curved trunk. The leaves are alternate, ovate or elliptical, 4-10 centimeters long, with serrate margins and a short petiole, and are a dark green color on the upper surface and lighter on the lower surface. The flowers are fragrant, pink to white, 3-4 centimeters in diameter, and appear in clusters at the ends of branches in early spring; they have five petals and numerous stamens.

The fruit is a small, red or purple drupe, 1-2 centimeters in diameter, which ripens in late summer and is eaten by birds and small mammals.

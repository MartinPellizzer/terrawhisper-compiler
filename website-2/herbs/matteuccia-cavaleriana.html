Matteuccia cavaleriana

Matteuccia cavaleriana, also known as ostrich fern or fiddlehead fern, is a perennial plant native to North America and Asia, characterized by its distinctive, curled fiddlehead fronds.

The bioactive constituents of Matteuccia cavaleriana include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, as well as vitamins and minerals like vitamin C and potassium.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make teas, infusions, and extracts, with the most common herbal preparations including dried fiddleheads for tea and capsules or tablets containing concentrated extracts of the plant's bioactive constituents.

Geranium caeruleum

Geranium caeruleum, also known as cranesbill, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Western Asia, characterized by its distinctive pink flowers and lobed leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which may help to soothe skin irritations and reduce inflammation. Bioactive constituents of Geranium caeruleum include geraniol, linalool, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made with Geranium caeruleum include teas, tinctures, and topical creams, which are often used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and minor injuries.

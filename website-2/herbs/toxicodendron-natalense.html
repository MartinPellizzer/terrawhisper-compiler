Toxicodendron natalense

Toxicodendron natalense, also known as the Natal poison ivy, is a plant species within the Anacardiaceae family, characterized by its shiny leaves and ability to cause skin irritation in some individuals.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which are thought to aid in the treatment of various conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions. The bioactive constituents of Toxicodendron natalense include alkaloids, saponins, and flavonoids, which are believed to contribute to its therapeutic effects.

In traditional herbal medicine, the plant is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, or made into a paste, to be applied topically for its medicinal properties.

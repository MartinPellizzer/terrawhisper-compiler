Cotinus toxicodendron

Cotinus coggygria, also known as Smoke Bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which have been shown to aid in wound healing, reduce fever, and protect against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Cotinus coggygria include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Cotinus coggygria is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures to treat various health conditions, and it is also used in traditional folk remedies to treat skin conditions and fever.

The sap can also be absorbed through cuts or open wounds, exacerbating the potential for severe reactions.

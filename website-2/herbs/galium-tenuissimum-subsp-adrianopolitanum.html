What is Galium tenuissimum subsp. adrianopolitanum?

Galium tenuissimum subsp.

adrianopolitanum is a botanical taxon within the family Rubiaceae. This plant is characterized by its annual or biennial habit, stem lengths of up to 40 cm, and leaves that are narrowly lanceolate, 2-6 mm long, and arranged in whorls of 6.

Inflorescences are axillary and consist of 1-2 flowers, each with a corolla 2-4 mm in diameter and a calyx of 3-5 mm. The flowers are white or pinkish, with 4-5 stamens and 4-5 staminodes, and are found in the Balkan Peninsula and parts of Central and Eastern Europe.

Leaves and stems are covered with minute, glandular hairs.

Althaea venosa

Althaea venosa, also known as the marsh mallow or Mediterranean marsh mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant species belonging to the mallow family (Malvaceae).

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to its anti-inflammatory, soothing, and antioxidant properties, which make it beneficial for treating digestive issues, wounds, and skin irritations. The bioactive constituents of Althaea venosa include mucilages, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Althaea venosa is commonly used to make herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and ointments, which are used to treat a range of health issues, including mouth sores, ulcers, and skin conditions.

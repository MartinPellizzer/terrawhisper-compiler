Solanum judaicum

Solanum judaicum, also known as Judas' cherry or Ashaninka's cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical forests of South America, belonging to the Solanaceae family.

The health benefits of Solanum judaicum include its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various ailments such as fever, wounds, and gastrointestinal issues. The bioactive constituents of this plant include solasodine, solanidine, and other glycoalkaloids, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Solanum judaicum is often used to make teas, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for skin care and wound healing.

Condea undulata

Condea undulata, also known as the 'Wedge Bush', is a small, shrub-like plant native to Australia, characterized by its distinctive wedge-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers.

The bioactive constituents of Condea undulata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Condea undulata is often used in traditional herbal preparations, such as infusions, tinctures, and ointments, to promote wound healing, reduce fever, and alleviate respiratory tract infections.

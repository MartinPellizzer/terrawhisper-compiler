What is Cynara scolymus?

Cynara scolymus is a perennial thistle native to the Mediterranean region, belonging to the family Asteraceae.

The plant grows 1-3 meters tall, with a stout stem and large, ovate, silvery-gray leaves that are deeply lobed and arranged oppositely. The inflorescence is a dense, spherical head of deep purple flowers with a prominent, feathery tuft of bracts, produced from June to September. The leaves and flower stalks are edible and have been used as a vegetable in various cuisines, particularly in Greek and Italian cooking.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in dry, rocky areas and along coastlines.

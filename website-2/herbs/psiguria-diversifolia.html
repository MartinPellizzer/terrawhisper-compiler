Psiguria diversifolia

Psiguria diversifolia, also known as the "Wild Ginseng" or "Palo Santo", is a perennial plant species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin infections, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Psiguria diversifolia include glycosides, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal effects.

Traditionally, the plant is used to make tea, infusions, and decoctions, which are considered to be among the most common herbal preparations made from Psiguria diversifolia.

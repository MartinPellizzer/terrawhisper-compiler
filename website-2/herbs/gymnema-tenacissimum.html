Gymnema tenacissimum

Gymnema tenacissimum is a perennial climber of the Apocynaceae family, native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, offering various health benefits, including the regulation of blood sugar levels, improvement of digestion, and reduction of inflammation. The bioactive constituents of Gymnema tenacissimum include gymnemic acids, which are responsible for its antidiabetic properties, as well as other compounds such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins.

The most common herbal preparations made from Gymnema tenacissimum include decoctions, infusions, and extracts, which are often used to treat various health conditions, including diabetes and digestive disorders.

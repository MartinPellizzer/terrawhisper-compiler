Sedum expansum

Sedum expansum, also known as Goldmoss Stonecrop, is a succulent plant species belonging to the Crassulaceae family, characterized by its flat, spreading growth habit and golden-green foliage.

The plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in wound healing, skin conditions, and immune system support. Bioactive constituents of Sedum expansum include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is traditionally used to make herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and salves, which are often employed to treat skin issues, wounds, and respiratory problems.

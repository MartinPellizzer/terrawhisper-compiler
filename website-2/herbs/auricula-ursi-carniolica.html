Auricula-ursi carniolica

Auricula-ursi carniolica, also known as Bearberry or Kinnikinnick, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to Europe and North America, characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and red or yellow berries.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with potential health benefits including the treatment of urinary tract infections, kidney stones, and digestive issues, due to its ability to reduce inflammation and prevent bacterial growth. The bioactive constituents of Auricula-ursi carniolica include arbutin, a glycoside with antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, as well as ursolic acid, a triterpene with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

The most common herbal preparations made with Bearberry include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which can be used to treat various health conditions, although its use should be guided by a healthcare professional due to potential interactions with medications and side effects.

Frangula palmeri

Frangula palmeri, also known as Palmer's Buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, belonging to the family Rhamnaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its health benefits, including the treatment of constipation, digestive issues, and liver problems, due to its ability to stimulate bowel movements and promote liver detoxification. The bioactive constituents of Frangula palmeri include anthraquinone glycosides, such as frangulin and emodin, which are responsible for its purgative and laxative properties.

Frangula palmeri is commonly used in herbal preparations like teas, tinctures, and capsules, often in combination with other herbs, to support digestive health and promote overall well-being.

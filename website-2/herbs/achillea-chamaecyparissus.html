Achillea chamaecyparissus

Achillea chamaecyparissus, also known as tansy, is a perennial plant native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and carminative properties, making it beneficial in treating digestive issues, menstrual cramps, and other health conditions. The bioactive constituents of Achillea chamaecyparissus include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, tansy is commonly prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is used to treat various ailments, including indigestion, bloating, and anxiety.

Ranunculus platypetalus

Ranunculus platypetalus, also known as the buttercup, is a perennial plant species belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, offering potential health benefits in wound healing, skin conditions, and respiratory issues. The bioactive constituents of Ranunculus platypetalus include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, such as ranunculin and ranunculoside, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Ranunculus platypetalus include tinctures, infusions, and ointments, often used to treat skin irritations, eczema, and minor wounds.

Chlorophytum macrophyllum

Chlorophytum macrophyllum, also known as Spider Plant, is a popular ornamental plant species native to South Africa, characterized by its creeping stems and leaves.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its potential health benefits, such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Chlorophytum macrophyllum include alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often marketed as natural remedies for stress, anxiety, and immune system support.

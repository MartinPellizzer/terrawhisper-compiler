What is Crassula capitella subsp. meyeri?

Crassula capitella subsp.

meyeri is a succulent plant belonging to the family Crassulaceae. It is characterized by a compact, shrub-like habit with branching stems and fleshy, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with reddish-brown.

The leaves are typically 2-4 cm long and 1-2 cm wide, with a glaucous, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink or red, which are borne on short, erect stalks in the summer months.

The plant is native to South Africa, where it grows in rocky outcrops and sandy soils.

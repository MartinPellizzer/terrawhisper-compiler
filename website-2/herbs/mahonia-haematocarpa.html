Mahonia haematocarpa

Mahonia haematocarpa, also known as Bearberry or Oregon Grape, is a perennial shrub native to North America, characterized by its clusters of small, yellow flowers and red berries.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various health issues, including diarrhea, fever, and digestive problems, due to its potential health benefits of antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The bioactive constituents of Mahonia haematocarpa include alkaloids, such as berberine, oregonin, and isomeritropine, as well as other compounds like phenolic acids and flavonoids.

Herbal preparations of Mahonia haematocarpa, often in the form of tea, capsules, or tinctures, are commonly used to support immune function, reduce inflammation, and alleviate gastrointestinal issues.

Callisia repens

Callisia repens, commonly known as inch plant or creeping inch plant, is a low-growing, spreading herbaceous perennial native to Central and South America, characterized by its small, rounded leaves and creeping stems.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including inflammation, fever, and respiratory problems, due to its potential anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and expectorant properties. The bioactive constituents of Callisia repens include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

Callisia repens is commonly prepared as a tea or infusion, and is also used in some traditional herbal remedies, such as cough syrups and expectorant tonics.

Angelica linearis

Angelica linearis, also known as Yunnan angelica, is a perennial plant species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan and Sichuan provinces in southwestern China.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antipyretic properties. The bioactive constituents of Angelica linearis include volatile oils, flavonoids, alkaloids, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Angelica linearis is commonly prepared into teas, infusions, and decoctions, as well as being used to make dried extracts and capsules.

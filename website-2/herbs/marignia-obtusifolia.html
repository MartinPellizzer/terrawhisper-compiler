Marignia obtusifolia

Marignia obtusifolia, also known as the bitter Marignia or Marignia shrub, is a plant species native to tropical Africa, belonging to the family Menispermaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in various cultures for its medicinal properties, and it is believed to have potential health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which may aid in reducing fever, relieving digestive issues, and protecting against oxidative stress. The bioactive constituents of Marignia obtusifolia include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Marignia obtusifolia is commonly used to prepare teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being used in the production of bitter tonics and extracts.

Butea suberecta

Butea superba, also known as Butea suberecta, is a plant species native to Southeast Asia, specifically in Thailand and Myanmar, belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

The plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, inflammation, and cardiovascular diseases, due to its reported health benefits of lowering blood pressure, reducing blood cholesterol levels, and acting as an antioxidant. The bioactive constituents of Butea superba include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparations made from Butea superba include extracts and tinctures, as well as capsules and tablets, which are used to treat cardiovascular diseases and other conditions.

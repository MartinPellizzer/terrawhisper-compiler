Carum amomum

Carum amomum, also known as ajwain or bishop's weed, is a plant belonging to the Apiaceae family that is native to the Indian subcontinent and Middle Eastern regions.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and carminative properties, which can help alleviate digestive issues, relieve respiratory problems, and reduce pain and inflammation. The bioactive constituents of Carum amomum include volatile oils, such as thymol and pinene, as well as other compounds like ajwainine, beta-pinene, and beta-phellandrene, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Carum amomum is commonly used to make teas, decoctions, and infusions, as well as being added to oils and ointments, to treat a range of health issues.

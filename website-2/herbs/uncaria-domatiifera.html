Uncaria domatiifera

Uncaria domatiifera, also known as Gacami or Mieng, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia, belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, with reported health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may aid in wound healing, reducing fever, and alleviating digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Uncaria domatiifera include iridoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Uncaria domatiifera is commonly prepared as a decoction or infused in hot water, and is also used as a component in various herbal remedies, such as tonics and salves.

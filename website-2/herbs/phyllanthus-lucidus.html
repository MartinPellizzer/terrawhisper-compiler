Phyllanthus lucidus

Phyllanthus lucidus, also known as samuelson's phyllanthus or dianella, is a plant species of the family Phyllanthaceae, native to tropical regions of Asia.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and hepatoprotective effects, making it beneficial for treating liver diseases, fever, and other infections. The bioactive constituents of Phyllanthus lucidus include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional medicine, Phyllanthus lucidus is commonly used to prepare herbal teas, infusions, and extracts, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including liver disorders and fever.

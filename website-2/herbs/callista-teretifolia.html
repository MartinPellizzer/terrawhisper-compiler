Callista teretifolia

Callista teretifolia, also known as the 'Tuberous Pea', is a perennial plant species native to Asia, characterized by its tuberous roots and small, white flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in the treatment of various ailments such as skin conditions and digestive issues. The bioactive constituents of Callista teretifolia include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, the plant is commonly prepared as a decoction or a powder, often used to treat respiratory issues and as a diuretic.

Thalictrodes racemosa

Thalictrodes racemosa, also known as Black Cohosh, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America, belonging to the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae).

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including alleviating symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes and night sweats, as well as providing relief from anxiety and menstrual cramps. The bioactive constituents of Black Cohosh include triterpene glycosides, such as acteoside, cismilfor-9-en-28-oic acid, and 23-epi-26-dehydroxy-25-methoxy-27-deoxysaponin, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

Black Cohosh is commonly prepared as a dietary supplement in capsule or tablet form, as well as in teas, tinctures, and topical creams, and has also been used in combination with other herbs in traditional Chinese medicine.

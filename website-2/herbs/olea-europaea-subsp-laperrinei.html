What is Olea europaea subsp. laperrinei?

Olea europaea subsp.

laperrinei is a botanical subspecies of the olive tree, belonging to the family Oleaceae. It is a shrub or small tree with a height of 3-6 meters, characterized by elliptical or lanceolate leaves, 2-5 centimeters long, with a rounded base and an acute tip, and a glabrous or pubescent lower surface.

The leaves are arranged oppositely or in whorls of three, and the flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in corymbose or racemose inflorescences. The fruit is a small, reddish or purple olive, 1-2 centimeters long, with a smooth, glabrous surface.

It is endemic to Algeria and Tunisia in North Africa.

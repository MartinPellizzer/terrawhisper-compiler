Satureja brownei

Satureja brownei, also known as Brown's savory, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family, native to South America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which may aid in the treatment of various conditions such as digestive issues and respiratory problems. The bioactive constituents of Satureja brownei include carvacrol, thymol, and borneol, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Satureja brownei is often used in teas, infusions, and tinctures, as well as being incorporated into topical creams and ointments for its medicinal benefits.

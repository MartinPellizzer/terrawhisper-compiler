What herbal teas you should avoid if you have jaw clenching?

If you have jaw clenching, it's best to avoid herbal teas that can stimulate or irritate your jaw muscles.

One such tea is peppermint (Mentha piperita), as its strong scent can cause your jaw to clench or tighten. Another tea to avoid is ginger (Asarum canadense), while it's not typically considered irritating, its warming effect can cause your muscles to tense up, including your jaw.

Black cohosh (Actaea racemosa) is also not recommended, as some studies suggest it may cause muscle tension in some people. Additionally, you might want to steer clear of licorice root (Glycyrrhiza uralensis) tea, although it's not typically used to treat jaw clenching, it can cause your body to retain water, leading to swelling in the jaw and surrounding areas.

Finally, sage (Salvia officinalis) tea should be used with caution, as its essential oils can stimulate your jaw muscles and cause tension.

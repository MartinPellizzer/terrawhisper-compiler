Overcome jaw clenching with 7 teas for anxious minds

People who feel anxious often clench their jaws, a habit that can cause pain and discomfort in the face, neck, and head.

This is because anxiety triggers a fight-or-flight response in the body, leading to muscle tension and involuntary jaw movement. The constant clenching can lead to headaches, earaches, and even tooth damage. It can also make everyday activities like eating, speaking, and even smiling difficult and painful. For some, jaw clenching can become a constant companion, affecting their mood, sleep, and relationships.

It's not just a physical issue, but also a mental one, as it can make anxious individuals feel more anxious and on edge. But there's hope. Certain herbal teas have been found to help relax the muscles and calm the mind, making them a potential solution for anxious individuals who suffer from jaw clenching. These teas contain natural compounds that have a soothing effect on the body and mind, helping to reduce muscle tension and promote relaxation.

By sipping on a warm cup of herbal tea, anxious individuals may find that their jaw clenching subsides, and they're able to manage their anxiety more effectively.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis teas are a natural remedy for jaw clenching, also known as bruxism.

The tea contains valerenic acid, a key constituent that helps to relax the jaw muscles and reduce tension. This is because valerenic acid interacts with the brain's GABA receptors, producing a calming effect that soothes the nervous system and eases muscle contractions. Additionally, Valeriana officinalis teas contain other beneficial compounds like isovaleric acid and valeranone, which contribute to their sedative and anti-anxiety properties. To try Valeriana officinalis teas for jaw clenching, start by brewing a cup of the tea according to the package instructions. Drink the tea before bedtime or whenever you feel the urge to clench your jaw.

You can also add a few drops of valerian essential oil to your pillow or inhale it through steam inhalation to enhance the calming effects. It's recommended to drink the tea regularly, ideally for a few weeks, to notice significant improvements in jaw tension and clenching. As you incorporate Valeriana officinalis teas into your routine, you can expect to experience a decrease in jaw clenching frequency and intensity. Your jaw muscles will feel more relaxed, and you may even notice improvements in your sleep quality. However, it's essential to note that Valeriana officinalis teas may not completely eliminate jaw clenching, but they can significantly reduce its occurrence.

Be patient and consistent with your tea consumption, and you may find that your jaw tension becomes more manageable over time.

2. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata teas, also known as passionflower tea, can be a helpful remedy for jaw clenching due to its ability to calm the nervous system.

The tea contains flavonoids and alkaloids, specifically harmine and harman, which have sedative properties that can help reduce muscle tension and anxiety. This leads to a decrease in jaw clenching, as the body's fight-or-flight response is minimized. To take advantage of passionflower tea for jaw clenching, start by brewing a cup according to the package instructions.

Typically, you'll want to steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried passionflower in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-10 minutes. Once the tea has cooled, you can drink it 2-3 times a day to help manage jaw clenching. As you take passionflower tea regularly, you may notice a reduction in jaw clenching episodes. This can be attributed to the tea's ability to promote relaxation and reduce anxiety levels.

Additionally, the flavonoids present in passionflower tea may help to decrease inflammation in the jaw and surrounding muscles, further contributing to a decrease in jaw clenching.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas, also known as English lavender tea, have been traditionally used to help with jaw clenching and tension.

The main reason for this is the tea's high content of linalool and linalyl acetate, which are compounds found in the lavender plant. These compounds have a calming effect on the nervous system, helping to reduce muscle tension and anxiety. When we clench our jaw, it's often due to stress or anxiety, which can lead to a cycle of tension and pain. Drinking Lavandula angustifolia tea can help to break this cycle by promoting relaxation and reducing muscle tension. To use Lavandula angustifolia tea for jaw clenching, start by steeping a teaspoon of dried lavender flowers in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it before bedtime or when you feel yourself clenching your jaw. You can also add a few drops of lavender essential oil to your bath water or inhale it through steam inhalation to promote relaxation. When you drink Lavandula angustifolia tea, you may start to feel a sense of calmness and relaxation, which can help to reduce jaw tension and pain. You may also find that you're able to sleep better, as the tea's calming effects can help to regulate your sleep patterns. Additionally, the tea's anti-inflammatory properties may help to reduce swelling and pain in the jaw area.

As you continue to drink the tea, you may find that your jaw clenching episodes become less frequent and less severe, allowing you to feel more relaxed and comfortable in your daily life.

To make the most of the tea, try to reduce stress and anxiety, which are common triggers for jaw clenching. When you start taking Avena sativa tea, you may notice a decrease in jaw clenching, especially at night. You may also notice a reduction in facial pain and tension. 7. Centaurium erythraea

Centaurium erythraea teas, also known as centaury, have been traditionally used to help with jaw clenching due to its relaxing properties and constituents, such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and tannins.

These compounds work together to reduce muscle tension and inflammation in the jaw, making it easier to release clenched teeth and relax facial muscles. You can also add a pinch of honey to make it more palatable. As you take centaury tea, you may start to feel your jaw muscles relax, making it easier to release tension and clenching. Your jaw may feel less sore and tense, and you may be able to open your mouth wider without feeling discomfort.

With regular use, you may find that you're able to manage jaw clenching episodes more effectively, and even reduce their frequency.

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help reduce stress and anxiety that often lead to jaw clenching. The sedative properties of linalool and linalyl acetate can also help relax tense muscles and promote relaxation. Additionally, the analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds can help alleviate pain and discomfort associated with jaw clenching.

By applying Lavandula angustifolia essential oils to the temples or massaging it into the jaw area, you can experience a soothing and calming effect that may help alleviate jaw clenching.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat, then add the Lavandula flowers.
  3. Let the mixture simmer for 2 hours, then remove from heat.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 24 hours to allow the essential oil to separate and rise to the top.

2. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains linalool and citral as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have a calming and relaxing effect on the body. The sedative properties of linalool help to reduce muscle tension, including jaw clenching. Citral's anti-inflammatory properties can help soothe jaw pain and discomfort associated with clenching.

Regular use of Melissa officinalis essential oils may help regulate the nervous system, leading to a reduction in jaw clenching.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Fill a clean glass jar with 1/4 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers. Leave 1 inch of space at the top.
  3. Pour carrier oil over the flowers until the jar is almost full. Make sure the flowers are completely covered.
  4. Steep the mixture for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place. Shake the jar every day.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids and use the oil for jaw clenching relief.

3. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds contribute to its sedative and calming properties, which can help relax the jaw muscles. The valerenic acid in Valeriana officinalis essential oils has a sedative effect on the nervous system, reducing muscle tension and stress. The calming properties of valepotriates help to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation, leading to a decrease in jaw clenching.

By reducing muscle tension and promoting relaxation, Valeriana officinalis essential oils can help alleviate jaw clenching and related symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Valeriana officinalis roots and 1 cup of carrier oil (jojoba or sweet almond oil)
  2. Chop the roots into small pieces and mix with the carrier oil in a clean glass jar
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids
  5. Store the Valeriana officinalis essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid, using 5-7 drops in a diffuser or on the jaw area as needed

4. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like alpha-pinene, limonene, and eucalyptol, which have anti-inflammatory and calming properties.

These properties help to reduce muscle tension in the jaw, making it a popular choice for people who clench their jaw. The essential oil's ability to relax muscles and reduce anxiety also contributes to its effectiveness in alleviating jaw clenching. Eucalyptol, in particular, has a decongestant effect, which can help to clear out any blockages that may be contributing to jaw tension.

By applying Eucalyptus globulus essential oils to the jaw area or inhaling it through steam inhalation, the soothing effects of its active constituents can help to calm the muscles and promote relaxation.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Eucalyptus leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) to the liquid and mix well.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops for a relaxing massage or inhalation.

5. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains menthol and menthone as its active constituents.

These compounds have analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to relieve pain and reduce swelling associated with jaw clenching. Menthol's cooling effect can also help to relax tense muscles in the jaw area, reducing the frequency and intensity of clenching episodes. Menthone has a sedative effect, which can help to calm the nervous system and promote relaxation, further reducing the likelihood of jaw clenching.

By combining these properties, Mentha x piperita essential oils can provide relief from jaw clenching and promote a more relaxed and calm state.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and flowers.
  2. Wash the leaves and flowers in cold water, then gently pat them dry.
  3. Combine 1 cup of leaves and flowers with 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  4. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes, then let it cool.
  5. Use a distillation kit or a simple still to extract the essential oil from the mixture.

6. Lavandula latifolia

Lavandula latifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two compounds known for their calming properties.

These constituents help relax the mind and body, which can contribute to releasing tension in the jaw. Linalool's anti-inflammatory properties can also reduce swelling and pain in the jaw area. Additionally, the oils' sedative effects can help alleviate anxiety and stress, common triggers for jaw clenching.

By promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety, Lavandula latifolia essential oils may help alleviate jaw clenching symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula latifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat and bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low.
  3. Add the Lavandula latifolia flowers to the saucepan and let them steep for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Use a distillation kit or a simple steam distillation method to extract the essential oil from the mixture.

7. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains rosinic acid, bornyl acetate, and camphor as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds give the oil its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help to relax tense jaw muscles. The antispasmodic properties of the oil also help to reduce muscle cramps and spasms, making it an effective remedy for jaw clenching. Additionally, rosinic acid has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can contribute to the reduction of stress and anxiety that may be causing jaw clenching.

By applying Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils topically, you can directly target the affected area and experience relief from jaw clenching.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and stems. Wear protective gloves.
  2. Use a steam distiller or a homemade setup with a glass jar, pot, and condenser to distill the leaves.
  3. Add 2 cups of water to the pot and bring it to a boil. Place the glass jar with leaves on top of the condenser.
  4. Collect the distilled essential oil in a clean glass container. It should take about 2 hours to collect 1-2 tablespoons of oil.
  5. Filter the oil using a coffee filter or a paper filter to remove any impurities. Store the oil in a dark glass bottle.

8. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like beta-caryophyllene and zingiberene that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties can help reduce swelling and pain in the jaw and facial muscles. The warming effect of Zingiber officinale essential oils, due to the presence of gingerols and shogaols, can relax tense muscles and improve blood circulation to the affected area. The antispasmodic properties of the oil, attributed to the presence of zingiberene and beta-caryophyllene, can help alleviate jaw clenching and tension headaches.

By applying Zingiber officinale essential oils topically, you can experience the benefits of reduced inflammation and muscle relaxation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale roots and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Cut the roots into small pieces and add them to the saucepan with the water.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes, then let it cool.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use a few drops in a diffuser or apply topically (after diluting with a carrier oil) to help with jaw clenching.

9. Santalum album

Santalum album essential oils contains a number of bioactive constituents, including santalol and beta-santalol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties can help reduce swelling and pain in the jaw area, making it a potential remedy for jaw clenching. Santalum album essential oils also contains sesquiterpenes, which have a sedative effect on the nervous system, helping to calm the muscles in the jaw and face. The oil's analgesic properties can also help alleviate pain associated with jaw clenching.

By using Santalum album essential oils, individuals can potentially experience relief from jaw clenching and related discomfort.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Santalum album leaves. Choose fresh and healthy leaves.
  2. Add 1 cup of water to a pot. Bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat, add 2 cups of leaves to the pot. Steep for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid into a separate container. Discard the leaves.
  5. Let the liquid cool. Use a distiller or a cheesecloth to separate the essential oil from the liquid.

10. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, geranial, and citronellal as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that help reduce muscle tension and pain associated with jaw clenching. Limonene, in particular, has a calming effect on the nervous system, while geranial and citronellal help to relax the muscles and reduce spasms. The antispasmodic and anxiolytic properties of Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can help alleviate stress and anxiety, common triggers of jaw clenching.

By reducing muscle tension and promoting relaxation, this essential oil can help alleviate the symptoms of jaw clenching and promote overall well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves, 2 cups of water, and a pot.
  2. Chop the leaves into small pieces and add them to the pot.
  3. Pour the water into the pot and bring it to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 30 minutes. Then, let it cool.
  5. Strain the mixture and store the liquid in a bottle. Use 2-3 drops of the essential oil in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for massage.

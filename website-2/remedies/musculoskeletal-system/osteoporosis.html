What Are The Main Causes Of Osteoporosis?

Osteoporosis is a condition where bones become weak and fragile due to a loss of density.

There are several main causes of osteoporosis. Genetics play a significant role, as people with a family history of the condition are more likely to develop it. This is because genetic factors can affect the way bones absorb calcium and other minerals, making them more prone to weakening.

Hormonal Imbalance is another cause, particularly in women after menopause, when a decrease in estrogen levels can lead to a loss of bone density. This is because estrogen helps to regulate the balance of minerals in bones. Vitamin D Deficiency is also a cause, as vitamin D is essential for the body to absorb calcium, which is necessary for building and maintaining strong bones.

If there is not enough vitamin D, the body may not be able to absorb enough calcium, leading to weak and brittle bones.

Below are the main causes of osteoporosis.

  • Genetics: Genetics plays a role in osteoporosis because some people are born with a genetic code that affects the production and functioning of hormones, such as osteocalcin, which helps build strong bones, and this can lead to weakened bones over time.
  • Hormonal Imbalance: Hormonal imbalance, particularly a drop in estrogen levels in women after menopause or an excess of thyroid hormones, can lead to osteoporosis because it causes the body to break down bone tissue faster than it can build new bone, resulting in weakened bones and an increased risk of fractures.
  • Vitamin D Deficiency: Vitamin D deficiency is a cause of osteoporosis because it prevents the body from absorbing calcium, a key bone-building mineral, leading to weak and brittle bones.
  • Calcium Deficiency: Calcium deficiency is a cause of osteoporosis because the body needs calcium to build and maintain strong bones, but without enough calcium, bones become weak and brittle over time.
  • Lack of Exercise: When we don't exercise regularly, our bones don't get the chance to strengthen and repair themselves, leading to weak and brittle bones that can cause osteoporosis.
  • Smoking: Smoking damages bones by reducing bone density and slowing down bone growth, which can lead to osteoporosis, a condition where bones become weak and brittle.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Osteoporosis?

If you have osteoporosis, it's essential to avoid certain herbs that may worsen the condition.

One herb to be cautious of is 'Sassafras albidum', which may cause bone loss due to its high levels of safrole, a chemical that affects bone health. 'Turmeric' or 'Curcuma longa' in large amounts can also be problematic, as it may interact with certain medications and thin the blood, increasing the risk of fractures.

'Ginkgo biloba' may also be a concern, as it can increase the risk of bleeding and interact with blood-thinning medications, further complicating osteoporosis treatment. 'Garlic' and other 'Allium' species can also be a problem, as they may affect blood clotting and thin the blood. 'Angelica sinensis', also known as dong quai, should be used with caution as it may interact with certain medications and thin the blood.

Finally, 'Cinchona officinalis', which contains quinine, should be avoided as it can interact with medications and worsen bleeding risks associated with osteoporosis.

What Are The Main Causes Of Joint Stiffness?

Joint stiffness can be caused by several factors.

One major cause is Osteoarthritis, a condition where the cartilage in the joints wears down over time. This cartilage acts as a cushion between bones, and when it's gone, bones rub against each other, causing pain and stiffness. Osteoarthritis often affects older people due to wear and tear on the joints. Rheumatoid Arthritis is another cause of joint stiffness. This condition is an autoimmune disease, meaning the body's immune system mistakenly attacks the healthy tissues in the joints.

As a result, the joints become inflamed, leading to pain and stiffness. Rheumatoid Arthritis can affect people of any age and is more common in women. Gout is a cause of joint stiffness that's often linked to diet and lifestyle. Gout is a condition where uric acid builds up in the joints, causing inflammation and pain. This usually happens when the body can't remove uric acid properly or when you consume too much of certain foods, such as seafood or organ meats.

Gout often affects the big toe but can also affect other joints.

Below are the main causes of joint stiffness.

  • Osteoarthritis: Osteoarthritis causes joint stiffness because the wear and tear on the joints leads to the breakdown of cartilage, a smooth, cushioning tissue that usually allows for smooth movement, resulting in bones rubbing against each other and causing stiffness and pain.
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis: Rheumatoid Arthritis causes joint stiffness because the immune system mistakenly attacks the lining of the joints, leading to inflammation and swelling that restricts movement and causes stiffness.
  • Gout: Gout is a cause of joint stiffness because the uric acid crystals that build up in the joints during a gout attack cause inflammation, pain, and swelling, leading to stiffness and limited mobility in the affected joint.
  • Fibromyalgia: Fibromyalgia causes joint stiffness because the condition leads to widespread inflammation and pain in the muscles and tendons surrounding the joints, making it difficult to move and maintain normal joint mobility.
  • Tendinitis: Tendinitis occurs when the tendons that connect muscles to bones become inflamed and irritated, leading to pain, swelling, and stiffness in the surrounding joints due to the limited movement and increased friction between the inflamed tendons and surrounding tissues.

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two active constituents with anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Linalool, in particular, has been shown to reduce swelling and ease pain by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. Linalyl acetate, on the other hand, has a sedative effect, which can help relax tense muscles in the neck and promote recovery. The anti-inflammatory properties of these constituents can also help reduce muscle spasms and discomfort in the neck, making Lavandula angustifolia creams a potential remedy for neck pain.

The combination of linalool and linalyl acetate in these creams may provide relief from neck pain by addressing both inflammation and muscle tension.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of lavender essential oil, and 2 tablespoons of jojoba oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in lavender essential oil and jojoba oil. Pour into a glass jar and let cool until solid.
  5. Apply a small amount to the affected neck area and gently massage until absorbed. Repeat as needed.

By applying Mentha x piperita creams to the affected area, you can experience relief from neck pain and discomfort.

Artemisia absinthium creams contains boiactive constituents like thujone, sesquiterpenes, and flavonoids.

What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have neck pain?

If you have neck pain, there are some herbal decoctions you should avoid.

Willow bark decoction, which contains salicylic acid, can be problematic because it can thin the blood and increase the risk of bleeding. This is especially concerning if you have neck pain caused by an injury or surgery, as bleeding can complicate the healing process.

Another herb to avoid is Valeriana officinalis, as it can cause drowsiness and interact with pain medications, potentially worsening neck pain. Also, avoid decoctions with Ephedra sinica, as it can raise blood pressure and make neck pain worse due to increased strain on the neck muscles.

Finally, avoid decoctions with Hydrastis canadensis, as it can slow blood clotting and, similar to willow bark, increase the risk of bleeding.

The Causes of Gout: Exploring Herbal and Medicinal Treatments

What are the causes of gout?

Gout is a type of arthritis caused by high levels of uric acid in the blood, also known as hyperuricemia.

This is the main cause of gout, and it happens when the body either produces too much uric acid or can't remove it properly. Uric acid is a waste product created when the body breaks down certain foods, such as organ meats and seafood. Now, let's talk about the causes that can lead to high levels of uric acid in the blood. One of these causes is the use of diuretics, which are medications that help the body get rid of excess water. While diuretics can be helpful for people with conditions like high blood pressure, they can also make the body get rid of too much potassium, which can cause the body to produce more uric acid. Another cause of gout is obesity.

When you're overweight, you're more likely to have high levels of uric acid in your blood because you're producing more insulin, a hormone that can raise uric acid levels. Obesity can also make it harder for your body to remove uric acid, which can make your symptoms worse. Having a family history of gout can also increase your risk of getting it. If your parents or grandparents had gout, you're more likely to have high levels of uric acid in your blood because gout can be inherited. Lastly, low estrogen levels in men can also lead to gout. Estrogen helps the body remove uric acid, so when estrogen levels are low, uric acid can build up in the blood.

This is why men are more likely to get gout than women, especially after age 60 when estrogen levels naturally decline.

Relieving Muscle Pain: Understanding Its Causes and Herbal Remedies

What are the causes of muscle pain?

Muscle pain can be caused by several factors.

One main cause is tension, which occurs when you're feeling stressed or anxious. This tension can lead to muscle strain, causing your muscles to contract and become tight. Strain happens when you use a muscle in an unusual way, such as lifting something heavy without warming up first. Overuse is another common cause of muscle pain, which occurs when you do the same activity repeatedly, like running or typing on a computer for extended periods.

This repetitive motion can cause your muscles to become fatigued and painful. Injury is also a significant cause of muscle pain, whether it's a sudden trauma, like falling, or a repetitive strain injury, such as carpal tunnel syndrome. These types of injuries can cause micro-tears in your muscles, leading to pain and inflammation. Lastly, some medical conditions, such as hypothyroidism, can also contribute to muscle pain. This condition occurs when your thyroid gland doesn't produce enough hormones, which can cause fatigue, weakness, and muscle aches.

In some cases, hypothyroidism can even lead to muscle pain and stiffness, especially in the arms and legs.

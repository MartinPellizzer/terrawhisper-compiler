What Are The Main Causes Of Jaw Pain?

Jaw pain can be caused by several issues.

One main cause is TMJ Disorder, which affects the Temporomandibular Joint, the joint that connects the jawbone to the skull. This joint helps us chew, talk, and move our jaw. When the joint becomes inflamed or misaligned, it can cause pain and discomfort. TMJ Disorder can be caused by teeth grinding, jaw clenching, or even a blow to the jaw. Another common cause of jaw pain is Bruxism, which is the habitual grinding or clenching of teeth, usually while we sleep.

This can put a lot of pressure on the jaw joint and muscles, leading to pain and tension in the jaw area. Bruxism can be caused by stress, anxiety, or an abnormal bite. Sinusitis, an inflammation of the sinuses, can also cause jaw pain. When the sinuses become inflamed, it can put pressure on the facial bones, including the jawbone. This pressure can radiate to the jaw area, causing pain and discomfort.

Sinusitis is often caused by a viral or bacterial infection, and can be triggered by allergies or other environmental factors.

Below are the main causes of jaw pain.

  • TMJ Disorder: TMJ Disorder is a cause of jaw pain because the temporomandibular joint, which connects the jawbone to the skull, becomes inflamed or misaligned, leading to pain and discomfort in the jaw and surrounding areas.
  • Bruxism: Bruxism causes jaw pain because the repeated clenching and grinding of teeth put pressure on the jaw muscles and joints, leading to inflammation, strain, and eventual pain.
  • Sinusitis: Sinusitis can cause jaw pain because the infection in the sinuses can spread to the jaw and face bones, causing inflammation and discomfort in the jaw area.
  • Temporomandibular Joint Arthritis: Temporomandibular Joint Arthritis is a cause of jaw pain because the inflamed and damaged joint tissue in the jaw can cause pain and stiffness, making it difficult to open and close the mouth.
  • Dental Abscess: A dental abscess is a painful cause of jaw pain because the infection and swelling of the abscess put pressure on the nerves and tissues in and around the jaw, leading to severe discomfort and pain.
  • Gum Disease: Gum disease, which causes inflammation and infection in the gums, can lead to jaw pain by damaging the bone and tissues that support the teeth and jaw, ultimately causing misalignment and irritation of the jaw joint.
  • Teeth Grinding: Teeth grinding, or bruxism, is a cause of jaw pain because it puts extra pressure on the jaw muscles and joints, making them work too hard and leading to strain and pain.

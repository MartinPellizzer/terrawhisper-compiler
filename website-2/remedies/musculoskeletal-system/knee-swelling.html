Knee Swelling: A Guide to Medicinal Herbs and Natural Preparations

What are the causes of knee swelling?

Knee swelling can be caused by several issues.

One common cause is gout, a condition where uric acid builds up in the joints, leading to painful swelling and inflammation. This happens when the body can't get rid of excess uric acid, causing it to accumulate and irritate the joints. Another cause is Rheumatoid Arthritis, an autoimmune disease where the body's immune system attacks the lining of the joints, causing inflammation and swelling. This condition can lead to chronic pain and stiffness in the knees. Osteoarthritis is also a common cause of knee swelling, where the cartilage that cushions the joints wears down over time, causing bone-on-bone contact and inflammation. This can lead to pain, stiffness, and swelling in the knees.

Injury is another cause of knee swelling. A torn ligament can happen when the ligaments that connect the bones in the knee are stretched or torn, causing pain and swelling. This can happen due to a sudden twist or fall. Lastly, a torn meniscus can also cause knee swelling. The meniscus is a cartilage pad in the knee that cushions the joints and helps with movement. When it's torn, it can cause pain and swelling in the knee, especially when twisting or bending.

It's worth noting that these causes can be different for each person, and not everyone will experience all of these symptoms.

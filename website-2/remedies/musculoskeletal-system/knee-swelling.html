What Are The Main Causes Of Knee Swelling?

Knee swelling can be caused by several conditions.

One common cause is Osteoarthritis, a condition where the cartilage in the knee joint wears down, leading to bone-on-bone contact. This causes pain, inflammation, and fluid buildup in the joint, resulting in swelling. Another cause is Rheumatoid Arthritis, an autoimmune disease that causes the body's immune system to attack the lining of the joints. This leads to inflammation, pain, and swelling in the knee, as well as in other joints. The inflammation causes fluid buildup, which results in swelling.

Gout is also a cause of knee swelling. This condition occurs when uric acid builds up in the blood and forms sharp crystals in the joints. The crystals cause inflammation and pain in the joint, which leads to swelling. Gout often affects the big toe, but it can also affect other joints, including the knee. In addition to these conditions, other factors such as injury, infection, and fluid retention can also cause knee swelling.

However, Osteoarthritis, Rheumatoid Arthritis, and Gout are three of the main causes of knee swelling due to their direct impact on the joint and its surrounding tissues.

Below are the main causes of knee swelling.

  • Osteoarthritis: Osteoarthritis is a cause of knee swelling because the breakdown of cartilage in the knee joint allows the bones to rub against each other, leading to the accumulation of fluid and swelling in the joint.
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis: Rheumatoid Arthritis is a cause of knee swelling because the condition causes the body's immune system to attack and damage the lining of joints, leading to inflammation, fluid buildup, and swelling in the knee.
  • Gout: Gout is a cause of knee swelling because uric acid crystals build up in the joints, including the knee, causing pain, inflammation, and fluid buildup that leads to swelling.
  • Tendinitis: Tendinitis occurs when the tendons surrounding the knee become inflamed due to overuse or injury, leading to swelling as the body attempts to protect and repair the damaged tissue.
  • Bursitis: Bursitis is a cause of knee swelling because it occurs when the small fluid-filled sacs (bursae) that cushion and reduce friction between the knee bones and surrounding soft tissues become inflamed and fill with excess fluid.
  • Ligament Sprain: When a ligament in the knee gets stretched or torn, it can cause bleeding inside the knee, leading to swelling due to the buildup of excess fluid.

If you have knee swelling, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can make it worse.

One herb to be careful with is 'Hydrastis canadensis' (Goldenseal), which can increase urine production and lead to dehydration, making swelling worse. 'Ginkgo biloba' can also be a problem because it can thin the blood and make injuries worse.

'Ephedra sinica' (Ma Huang) is another herb to avoid as it can increase heart rate and blood pressure, which can worsen swelling. 'Astragalus membranaceus' can cause water retention in some people, making swelling worse.

'Panax ginseng' can also cause blood vessel constriction, which can reduce blood flow to the knee and make swelling worse.

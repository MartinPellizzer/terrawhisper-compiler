1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin that has anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and pain in the tendons and joints affected by tendinitis. Glycyrrhizin also has a corticosteroid-like effect, which helps suppress the production of inflammatory chemicals in the body. This reduction in inflammation and suppression of inflammatory chemicals can help alleviate the symptoms of tendinitis, such as pain and stiffness.

The anti-inflammatory and corticosteroid-like effects of glycyrrhizin in Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures make it a potential natural remedy for tendinitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, a glass jar with a lid, and 2 cups of 80% vodka.
  2. Combine the dried root with 2 cups of vodka in the glass jar. Seal the jar with the lid.
  3. Place the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day. This helps the plant's active ingredients mix with the vodka.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Label the jar with the date and the ingredients used. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops, 3-4 times a day, under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa tinctures contains a high concentration of curcuminoids, which are powerful anti-inflammatory compounds.

The main bioactive constituents, curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin, have potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation and pain in the body. Curcumin, in particular, inhibits the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, such as COX-2 and LOX, which contribute to the development of tendinitis. By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, curcuma longa tinctures may help alleviate symptoms of tendinitis, such as pain and stiffness, and promote healing and recovery in the affected area.

The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of curcuma longa tinctures make it a potentially effective natural remedy for managing tendinitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa root and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the dried root and vodka in the jar, making sure the root is completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and store it in a dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in the jar and use 15-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, to help relieve tendinitis symptoms.

3. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium tinctures contains sesquiterpene lactones, such as achillol and alantolactone, and flavonoids like kaempferol and quercetin.

The sesquiterpene lactones in Achillea millefolium tinctures can also inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to the development of tendinitis. By reducing inflammation and protecting the tendons, Achillea millefolium tinctures may provide relief from the pain and discomfort associated with tendinitis.

4. Arnica montana

Arnica montana tinctures contains sesquiterpene lactones, particularly helenalin and dihydrohelenalin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and pain in tendinitis. Arnica montana also contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, such as kaempferol and chlorogenic acid, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. The tannins and saponins present in Arnica montana help reduce inflammation and pain by blocking the release of pro-inflammatory enzymes.

The combination of these bioactive constituents makes Arnica montana tinctures a potential natural treatment for tendinitis.

5. Zingiber officinale

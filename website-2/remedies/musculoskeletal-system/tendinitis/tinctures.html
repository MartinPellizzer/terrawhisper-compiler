8 Herbal Tinctures For Tendinitis

Craftsmen who work with their hands often suffer from tendinitis, a painful condition that affects the tendons - the tissue that connects muscles to bones.

This can happen when a person repeatedly performs the same motion, like hammering nails or turning a lathe. The constant strain can cause inflammation, leading to swelling, pain, and stiffness in the affected area. This can make everyday activities, let alone their craft, very difficult. A craftsman with tendinitis may have to take breaks often, which can slow down their work and impact their income. In some cases, it can even lead to a complete stop in their work, affecting their livelihood and sense of purpose.

As a result, craftsmen are constantly looking for ways to alleviate the pain and inflammation associated with tendinitis. While some may turn to over-the-counter pain medications or physical therapy, others may explore natural remedies. Herbal tinctures have become a popular option for many craftsmen. These tinctures are made by infusing herbs in a solvent, such as water or alcohol, which allows the active compounds to be extracted and concentrated. When taken orally, these compounds can help to reduce inflammation, ease pain, and promote healing in the affected area.

By using herbal tinctures, craftsmen can find relief from the discomfort and swelling associated with tendinitis, allowing them to continue working on their craft without interruption.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains a powerful combination of properties and constituents that can help alleviate tendinitis symptoms.

The main compounds responsible for its anti-inflammatory effects are glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which have been shown to reduce swelling and pain in affected areas. Glycyrrhizin, a triterpene saponin, has potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help to reduce inflammation and protect the affected tissue from further damage. The flavonoids present in Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures also contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. To use Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures for tendinitis, take 20-30 drops of the tincture 2-3 times a day, mixed with water or another liquid of your choice.

You can also add it to a warm bath or apply it topically to the affected area using a carrier oil. It's essential to start with a low dose and gradually increase it as needed, as excessive consumption of glycyrrhizin can cause side effects such as high blood pressure and potassium deficiency. When taking Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures for tendinitis, you can expect to experience a reduction in pain and inflammation, improved mobility, and enhanced recovery. The tincture may also help to reduce muscle spasms and promote relaxation, making it an excellent complement to other treatments such as physical therapy and pain management. However, it's crucial to note that while Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures can provide relief, it's not a substitute for medical treatment.

If you're experiencing severe tendinitis symptoms, consult with a healthcare professional before using this tincture.

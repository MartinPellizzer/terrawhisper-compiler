What herbal teas you should avoid if you have tendinitis?

If you have tendinitis, some herbal teas to avoid include those containing comfrey (Symphytum officinale), wintergreen (Gaultheria procumbens), and willow bark (Salix alba).

Comfrey tea may worsen tendinitis due to its high levels of allantoin, a compound that can cause inflammation in the connective tissue. Wintergreen tea contains salicylic acid, which, although similar to aspirin, can irritate joints and worsen inflammation. Willow bark tea is high in salicylic acid as well, and its anti-inflammatory effects can be too intense for people with tendinitis, potentially causing more harm than good.

Drinking these teas may make your tendinitis symptoms worse, so it's best to avoid them.

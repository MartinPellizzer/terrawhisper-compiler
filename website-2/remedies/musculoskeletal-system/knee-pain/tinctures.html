1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains gingerols and shogaols, active compounds that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and pain in the knee by blocking the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause pain. Shogaols also inhibit the activity of COX-2 enzymes, which contribute to inflammation. The analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties of gingerols and shogaols help alleviate knee pain by reducing inflammation and blocking pain signals.

The bioactive constituents in Zingiber officinale tinctures work together to provide relief from knee pain and discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots.
  2. Chop the ginger roots into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped ginger with 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass jar.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into another clean glass jar, discarding the ginger solids.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains a number of bioactive constituents, including glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce swelling and pain in the knees. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, such as COX-2 and 5-LOX, which are involved in the development of knee pain. The saponins in Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures have also been found to have analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, making them useful for managing knee pain.

By reducing inflammation and pain, Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures may provide relief for individuals experiencing knee pain.

  1. Gather 100g of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 1 cup of 80% ethanol vodka.
  2. Combine the roots and vodka in a clean glass jar, making sure the roots are covered.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and take 10-15 drops, 3 times a day, to relieve knee pain.

3. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa tinctures contains a powerful compound called curcumin, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

Curcumin is a polyphenol that has potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, which help to reduce swelling and pain in the knees. Curcuma longa tinctures also contains other bioactive constituents, such as demethoxycurcumin and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which have been shown to inhibit the production of inflammatory enzymes. These compounds work by blocking the action of enzymes called COX-2 and LOX, which are involved in the production of inflammatory mediators that cause knee pain.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Curcuma longa tinctures can help to alleviate knee pain and promote healing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa roots, 2 cups of vodka (at least 80 proof), and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine the dried Curcuma longa roots and vodka in the glass jar. Stir well and make sure the roots are covered by the vodka.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.

  5. Store the tincture in the refrigerator and use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, mixed with water or taken sublingually for knee pain relief.

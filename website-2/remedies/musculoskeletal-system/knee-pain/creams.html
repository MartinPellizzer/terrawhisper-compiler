7 Herbal Creams For Knee Pain

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains glycyrrhizic acid, a potent anti-inflammatory compound, and triterpenoid saponins, which work together to reduce swelling and pain in the knee.

The anti-inflammatory properties of glycyrrhizic acid help to block the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to knee pain. The saponins in Glycyrrhiza glabra creams also have antioxidant properties, which help to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the knee joint. Additionally, the cream's anti-inflammatory compounds may help to reduce joint inflammation and promote healing in the affected area.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams can provide relief from knee pain.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of glycerin, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 1 teaspoon of glycyrrhiza glabra powder, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Heat the distilled water in a saucepan until it's hot but not boiling. Add the glycerin and beeswax, and stir until they dissolve.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool slightly. Add the glycyrrhiza glabra powder and vitamin E oil, and stir well.
  4. Pour the mixture into a bowl and let it cool completely until it thickens. This may take about 30 minutes.
  5. Once the mixture is thick, pour it into a jar or container. Store it in the refrigerator and apply a small amount to the affected knee area as needed.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains a high concentration of curcuminoids, specifically curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have potent anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and pain in the knee. Curcuminoids also have antioxidant properties that prevent cell damage and promote healthy tissue repair. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of curcuma longa creams work together to reduce knee pain and improve joint function.

The turmeric in the creams also has natural analgesic properties that help to block pain signals.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of beeswax, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder (Curcuma longa) in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the coconut oil, beeswax, and shea butter in the double boiler over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the vitamin E oil and turmeric powder to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  4. Remove the double boiler from the heat and let the mixture cool until it reaches 120°F to 130°F.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and harden completely before using it to massage into affected knee areas.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains a compound called gingerol, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in the knees.

The phenolic compounds present in ginger, such as gallic acid and ferulic acid, also contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects. Additionally, ginger contains a bioactive compound called shogaol, which has been shown to inhibit the production of pain-causing chemicals in the body. The warming sensation caused by ginger's capsaicin-like compounds, called gingerols and shogaols, can also increase blood flow to the affected area, promoting healing and reducing pain.

These bioactive compounds work together to provide relief from knee pain and stiffness.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of ginger essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl in the microwave for 30 seconds.
  3. Add shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until combined.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool for 5 minutes. Stir in ginger essential oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool completely before use. Apply to knee area as needed.

4. Arnica montana

Arnica montana creams contains sesquiterpene lactones, such as heliophane and alantolactone, and flavonoids like kaempferol and quercetin.

These compounds reduce inflammation and swelling, which are often causes of knee pain. The anti-inflammatory properties of Arnica montana also help to block pain receptors, providing relief from discomfort. Additionally, the creams' vasodilating properties increase blood flow to the affected area, promoting healing and reducing pain.

By using Arnica montana creams, people with knee pain may experience a decrease in swelling and pain, allowing them to move and function more comfortably.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Arnica montana tincture, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 drops of peppermint essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil, beeswax, and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove melted mixture from heat and stir in Arnica montana tincture and peppermint essential oil until well combined.
  4. Pour mixture into a small tin or jar and let it cool and solidify at room temperature.
  5. Once solid, your Arnica montana cream is ready to use. Apply a small amount to the affected knee area as needed.

5. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum creams contains boiactive constituents like hyperforin and hypericin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in the knee. The flavonoids, including quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant effects, which help protect the joint from damage caused by free radicals. The phenolic acids in Hypericum perforatum, such as ferulic acid, also have anti-inflammatory properties that contribute to pain relief.

The combination of these compounds makes Hypericum perforatum creams a potential natural solution for knee pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Hypericum perforatum flowers and 1 cup of coconut oil in a clean bowl.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the dried Hypericum perforatum flowers to the heated coconut oil and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Mix 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter into the Hypericum perforatum infused oil, then pour the mixture into small jars to cool and set.

6. Capsicum annuum

Capsicum annuum creams contains capsaicin, a potent compound that helps to reduce knee pain.

Capsaicin blocks the production of a chemical called substance P, which transmits pain signals to the brain. This leads to a decrease in inflammation and a reduction in pain perception, providing relief for people with knee pain.

The anti-inflammatory properties of capsaicin also help to reduce swelling and stiffness in the knee joint, making it a useful treatment option.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Capsicum annuum peppers, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Roast the Capsicum annuum peppers in a 400°F oven for 30 minutes, then let them cool.
  3. Peel the roasted peppers and blend them into a smooth paste in a blender.
  4. Combine the pepper paste, coconut oil, and beeswax in a saucepan and heat it over low heat, stirring until the beeswax is melted.
  5. Remove the saucepan from heat and let the mixture cool and thicken before transferring it to a jar for storage.

7. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two active constituents that contribute to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and pain in the knee by blocking the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause inflammation. The anti-inflammatory properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help to reduce pain and stiffness in the knee joint. Additionally, the creams' antioxidant properties help to protect the joint from oxidative stress, further reducing inflammation and promoting healing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Lavandula angustifolia creams can help alleviate knee pain and discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of coconut oil in a heat-proof bowl.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments until it reaches 120°F to 140°F (49°C to 60°C).
  3. Steep the dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers in the heated coconut oil for 2 hours, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean bowl.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of beeswax and 1 tablespoon of shea butter to the strained mixture and heat it gently until the beeswax is melted.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool. Store the creams in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

What are the causes of jaw lock?

A jaw lock occurs when the jaw gets stuck or locked in place.

There are several main causes of this issue. One common cause is TMJ Disorder, which affects the temporomandibular joint that connects the jawbone to the skull. When this joint gets irritated or inflamed, it can cause the jaw to lock up. Another cause is Temporomandibular Joint Inflammation, which is essentially the same issue as TMJ Disorder but with more inflammation.

Dental work, such as getting a dental crown or filling, can also cause a jaw lock. This is because the dentist has to numb the area with a local anesthetic, and this can cause the jaw to lock up. Additionally, dental work can cause the temporomandibular joint to become irritated, leading to jaw locking. Anxiety and stress are also contributing factors.

When we're anxious or stressed, our bodies tense up, and this can cause the muscles in the jaw to become tight, leading to a jaw lock.

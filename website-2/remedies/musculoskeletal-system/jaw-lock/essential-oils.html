1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called zingiberene, which is a sesquiterpene.

This compound helps to relax muscles and reduce inflammation, making it effective in relieving jaw lock caused by tension. The oils also contain other active constituents like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. These properties help to ease pain and reduce swelling in the jaw area, making it easier to open the mouth and relax the muscles.

By using Zingiber officinale essential oils, you can experience the benefits of its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties, which can help to alleviate jaw lock caused by tension.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger roots (Zingiber officinale) and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water to boiling point, then add the ginger roots and reduce heat to simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discarding the ginger solids.
  4. Let the liquid cool and separate into a separate container. Discard the water and keep the separated liquid.
  5. Use a distillation kit or a simple distiller to extract the essential oil from the remaining liquid, following the kit's instructions.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known to have anti-inflammatory and relaxing properties.

These properties help to reduce tension and spasms in the jaw muscles. The oil's calming effects can also help to decrease anxiety and stress, which are often contributing factors to jaw locking. The antioxidant properties of linalool and linalyl acetate can help to soothe and calm the muscles, reducing pain and discomfort.

By reducing muscle tension and promoting relaxation, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to alleviate jaw lock symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Place the flowers in a clean glass jar and cover them with carrier oil. Stir well.
  3. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and transfer the essential oil to small bottles. Use 5-7 drops of the essential oil for relief from jaw lock.

3. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains a compound called cineole, which is a powerful anti-inflammatory.

This property helps reduce swelling and pain in the jaw, making it easier to open and close the mouth. The oils also contain linalool, a compound that acts as a muscle relaxant, helping to calm the muscles in the jaw and face. The antiseptic properties of cinnamaldehyde in the oils help prevent infection and promote healing in the affected area.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Cinnamomum verum essential oils can help alleviate the symptoms of a jaw lock.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cinnamomum verum bark and 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or coconut oil).
  2. Clean and dry the Cinnamomum verum bark. Use a blender or coffee grinder to crush the bark into small pieces.
  3. Combine 1 cup of crushed bark with 2 cups of carrier oil in a heat-proof glass bowl.
  4. Heat the mixture in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally. The mixture should reach 104°F (40°C) to 190°F (88°C).
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains carnosic acid, camphor, and borneol as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which can help reduce pain and swelling in the jaw area. The analgesic properties of borneol and camphor can help numb the pain, while carnosic acid's anti-inflammatory properties can reduce swelling and redness. The antispasmodic properties of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can also help relax the muscles in the jaw, preventing spasms and tension.

By applying Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils topically, you may be able to alleviate the discomfort associated with a jaw lock.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba oil or sweet almond oil).
  2. Chop the leaves finely and combine them with the carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the oil to infuse.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with carrier oil for a massage oil.

5. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and alpha-pinene, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce swelling and pain in the jaw area, making it easier to open the mouth and relieve tension. The decongestant and expectorant properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils also help to clear mucus and reduce pressure on the jaw, providing relief from jaw lock and tension. The cooling sensation of eucalyptus globulus essential oils can also help to numb the pain and reduce discomfort.

By using eucalyptus globulus essential oils topically, you can apply its benefits directly to the affected area and start to feel relief from jaw lock.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat and add the Eucalyptus leaves. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a separate container. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a still or a double boiler. Heat the liquid to 180°F (82°C) to facilitate steam distillation.
  5. Collect the distilled essential oil in a separate container. Let it cool and store it in a dark glass bottle.

6. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains rosmarinic acid, camphor, and borneol as its main bioactive constituents.

8. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains bioactive constituents like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which have potent anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds work to reduce inflammation and swelling in the jaw, helping to relax the muscles and joints. The essential oil's antioxidant properties also help to neutralize free radicals that can cause pain and discomfort. Furthermore, Curcuma longa essential oils has a warming effect on the body, which can help to increase blood flow and reduce stiffness in the jaw.

This can help to alleviate jaw lock by reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation in the affected area.

  2. Grind the dried roots into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Add 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil) to the powder in a glass jar and stir well.
  4. Place the jar in a saucepan with 2-3 inches of water and heat it gently on low heat (100°F - 150°F) for 2-3 hours, allowing the oil to infuse.
  5. Strain the oil mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another container, discard the solids and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

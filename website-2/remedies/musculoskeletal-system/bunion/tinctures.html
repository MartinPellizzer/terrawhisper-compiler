8 herbal teas to ease bunion pain for runners

Runners are prone to bunions due to repetitive stress and friction on their feet.

This can be caused by poorly fitting shoes, running on hard surfaces, or even the way they run. The constant pressure and rubbing can lead to inflammation, pain, and eventually a bony growth on the big toe joint. For runners, this can be a real problem as it can make running uncomfortable and even painful. Some may experience swelling, redness, and difficulty moving their feet, which can affect their performance and even lead to missing runs or having to stop mid-run. Bunions can also cause runners to change their gait, which can put additional stress on other parts of the body, leading to further injuries. As a result, runners may need to adjust their training schedule, which can be frustrating and demotivating. They may also have to invest in orthotics or shoe modifications, which can be expensive.

Bunions can also make everyday activities, like walking or standing, painful and uncomfortable. This can impact a runner's quality of life, making it difficult to engage in activities they love. Herbal tinctures can be a helpful alternative for runners dealing with bunions. These tinctures are made by infusing plants in a solvent, which creates a concentrated liquid extract. This extract can be taken orally and can help reduce inflammation, pain, and swelling associated with bunions. The tinctures work by targeting the root causes of the bunion, rather than just masking the symptoms. They can help to reduce pressure and friction on the big toe joint, promoting healing and reducing discomfort.

By using herbal tinctures, runners may be able to reduce their reliance on painkillers and other treatments, and find a more natural way to manage their bunion symptoms.

1. Hamamelis virginiana

Hamamelis virginiana tinctures, also known as witch hazel, have been used for centuries to help alleviate bunion pain and inflammation.

This is due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which are attributed to its high concentration of gallic acid, tannins, and essential oils. These constituents help to reduce swelling, ease pain, and prevent infection in the affected area. The tannins in witch hazel also have astringent properties, which help to reduce bleeding and soothe irritation. To use Hamamelis virginiana tinctures for bunion, apply a few drops of the tincture to a cotton ball or cloth and gently rub it onto the affected area. You can also add a few drops to your bath water for a more relaxing and soothing experience.

However, be sure to dilute the tincture with water, as undiluted witch hazel can be harsh on the skin. It's recommended to use a 1:10 ratio of tincture to water. Start with small applications and gradually increase the frequency and amount as needed. When using Hamamelis virginiana tinctures for bunion, you may experience a reduction in pain and inflammation within a few days. The tannins and essential oils in the tincture may also help to reduce redness and promote healing in the affected area. Additionally, regular use of the tincture may help to prevent future occurrences of bunion pain and inflammation.

However, it's essential to note that witch hazel is not a substitute for medical treatment, and you should consult with a healthcare professional if your symptoms persist or worsen.

2. Arnica montana

Arnica montana tinctures are often used to help with bunions due to their anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties.

The tincture is made from the flowers of the Arnica montana plant, which contain constituents like sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids. These compounds help reduce swelling, ease pain, and promote healing in the affected area. When used for bunions, Arnica montana tinctures can help alleviate pressure and discomfort caused by the bony growth. To use Arnica montana tinctures for bunions, apply a few drops of the tincture directly to the affected area, ideally three times a day. You can also mix a few drops of the tincture with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it to the bunion.

Some people also find it helpful to soak their feet in a warm bath with a few drops of the tincture added. When using Arnica montana tinctures for bunions, you may notice a reduction in pain and swelling within a few days. The skin around the bunion may also appear to be healing, with a decrease in redness and inflammation. As the bunion begins to heal, you may find it easier to walk and engage in activities that were previously uncomfortable. However, it's essential to note that Arnica montana tinctures are not a replacement for medical treatment.

If you have a severe bunion or are experiencing persistent pain, consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures are a natural remedy that may help alleviate bunion pain and inflammation.

The tincture is made from the root of the licorice plant, which contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins. These constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce swelling and ease pain in the affected area. The saponins, in particular, have been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. To use Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures for bunion, start by applying a small amount to the affected area with a cotton ball, 2-3 times a day.

You can also take the tincture internally, mixing a few drops with water, 2-3 times a day. However, be cautious not to exceed the recommended dosage, as excessive consumption of glycyrrhizin can cause side effects like high blood pressure and low potassium levels. As you use Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures, you may start to notice a reduction in pain and inflammation within a few weeks. The tincture may also help to improve joint mobility and reduce stiffness. However, it's essential to note that the effectiveness of the tincture can vary depending on the individual and the severity of the bunion.

Some people may experience faster relief, while others may need to continue using the tincture for a longer period.

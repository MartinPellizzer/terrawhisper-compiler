What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have bunion?

If you have a bunion, it's best to avoid herbal decoctions that may irritate or inflame the affected area.

One such herb is Willow bark (Salix alba), which contains salicylic acid, similar to aspirin. While it may relieve pain, it can also increase bleeding and make the bunion more painful. Another herb to avoid is Devil's claw (Harpagophytum procumbens), which may increase inflammation and worsen symptoms.

Burdock root (Arctium lappa) is also not recommended, as it may cause skin irritation and allergic reactions, which could make the bunion more uncomfortable. Additionally, Dandelion root (Taraxacum officinale) and Sassafras root (Sassafras albidum) contain compounds that may increase uric acid levels, which can exacerbate bunion symptoms. Finally, Comfrey root (Symphytum officinale) should be avoided as it may cause skin irritation and has been linked to kidney damage in some cases.

It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies to ensure they won't worsen your bunion symptoms.

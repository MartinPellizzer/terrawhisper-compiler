1. Arnica montana

Arnica montana creams contains sesquiterpene lactones, particularly helenalin and dihydrohelenalin, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds work by blocking the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause pain and swelling. The flavonoids and phenolic acids in Arnica montana also contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects, helping to reduce swelling and pain in the knees. Additionally, the creams' ability to enhance blood circulation and reduce fluid retention helps to alleviate knee swelling.

The combination of these properties makes Arnica montana creams a popular natural remedy for knee swelling.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Arnica montana flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Make a tea by steeping the Arnica flowers in 2 cups of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Let it cool.
  3. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or in a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval.
  4. Add the cooled Arnica tea, shea butter, and vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir well until combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once set, your Arnica cream is ready to use.

2. Hamamelis virginiana

Hamamelis virginiana creams contains a group of compounds called tannins and phenolic acids, including hamamelitannin, hamamelose, and gallic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, which help to reduce swelling and ease pain in the knees. The astringent properties of the tannins in Hamamelis virginiana creams also help to constrict blood vessels and reduce blood flow to the affected area, further reducing swelling. The anti-inflammatory properties of the phenolic acids in the cream, such as gallic acid, also help to reduce inflammation and pain in the knees.

By reducing inflammation and swelling, Hamamelis virginiana creams can help to provide relief from knee swelling and discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Hamamelis virginiana flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Heat the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler until melted.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until melted and well combined.
  4. Add the Hamamelis virginiana flowers and vitamin E oil to the mixture. Let it steep for 2 hours, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth.
  5. Pour the cooled mixture into a container and let it solidify. Once solid, use as a cream for knee swelling.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains bioactive constituents like glycoproteins, polysaccharides, and vitamins that help reduce knee swelling.

The glycoproteins in aloe vera have anti-inflammatory properties that decrease swelling and pain in the knees. The polysaccharides in aloe vera also promote collagen production, which helps repair damaged tissue and reduce swelling. Additionally, the vitamin C in aloe vera has antioxidant properties that help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the knee joint.

By reducing inflammation and promoting tissue repair, aloe barbadensis creams can help alleviate knee swelling and promote healthy joint function.

  1. Gather 1 cup of aloe vera gel, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until melted.
  3. Add the aloe vera gel and vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  4. Pour the mixture into a clean glass jar or tin. Allow it to cool and solidify at room temperature.
  5. Once solid, your aloe vera cream is ready to use. Apply a thick layer to affected knees, 2-3 times a day, for relief from swelling.

4. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains turmeric, a plant with active constituents like curcumin and demethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce swelling and pain in the knees. Curcumin also acts as an antioxidant, neutralizing free radicals that cause inflammation and damage to joint tissues. The bioavailability of curcumin is enhanced by other constituents in turmeric, such as piperine, which increases its absorption in the body.

As a result, the inflammation and swelling associated with knee problems are reduced, allowing for better joint mobility and comfort.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder (Curcuma longa).
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl in a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from heat and stir in turmeric powder. Let it cool for 5 minutes.
  4. Slowly pour the mixture into a blender or food processor with 2 cups of distilled water. Blend until smooth.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and set at room temperature before use. Apply a small amount to the affected knee area.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains active constituents like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and triterpenoids.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams may be beneficial for people with knee swelling.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of glycerin, 1 tablespoon of beeswax, 1 tablespoon of coconut oil, 1 teaspoon of glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, and a few drops of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the beeswax and coconut oil in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl for 10-15 seconds.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix 1 cup of distilled water with 1/4 cup of glycerin and 1 teaspoon of glycyrrhiza glabra root powder. Stir well to combine.
  4. Slowly add the melted beeswax and coconut oil mixture to the water-glycerin mixture, stirring constantly until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container, add a few drops of vitamin E oil, and let it cool and solidify before applying to the affected knee area.

8. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids as active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce swelling and pain in the knee. The triterpenoids, specifically, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, thereby reducing inflammation. By reducing inflammation and protecting the joint, Calendula officinalis creams can help alleviate knee swelling and promote healing.

  3. Melt the coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  4. Add the dried Calendula flowers to the melted mixture and stir well. Remove from heat and let it steep for 2 hours.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Add the vitamin E oil and mix well. Pour into a tin or jar and let it cool before use.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent knee swelling from forming?

Some herbal teas, like ginger and turmeric tea, may help with knee swelling.

They contain anti-inflammatory compounds that might reduce swelling and ease pain. Drinking these teas regularly might make a difference, but it's not a replacement for medical treatment.

It's more like a natural complement to help manage symptoms.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for knee swelling every day?

Herbal teas can be a helpful addition to your daily routine for knee swelling.

However, it's essential to consume them in moderation. Some herbal teas, like peppermint or chamomile, can cause stomach upset if drunk excessively.

Drinking herbal teas daily may also interact with other medications you're taking, so it's crucial to be mindful of your consumption.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in knee swelling?

Herbal teas can take a few days to a week to show some relief from knee swelling.

Some people notice a reduction in swelling within 24-48 hours, while others may take a bit longer.

It's essential to drink the tea regularly, ideally 2-3 times a day, to see noticeable improvements.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for knee swelling?

For knee swelling, it's best to drink herbal tea in the morning or early afternoon.

This is when your body has its highest water content and can absorb the tea's anti-inflammatory properties more effectively.

Drinking herbal tea during this time may help reduce swelling and ease discomfort throughout the day.

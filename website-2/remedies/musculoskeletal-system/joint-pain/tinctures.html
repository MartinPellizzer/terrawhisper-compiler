What herbal tinctures you should avoid if you have joint pain?

If you have joint pain, there are some herbal tinctures you should avoid.

One of these is Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium). This herb can cause joint pain and stiffness as a side effect, so it's not a good choice for people with joint issues. Another herb to avoid is Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa).

While it's often used to reduce hot flashes and other menopause symptoms, it can also cause joint pain and swelling in some people. You should also be careful with St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum), as it can interact with pain-relieving medications and make them less effective. This could be a problem if you're already taking medication to manage your joint pain. Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) contains a compound called berberine, which can cause joint pain and inflammation in some individuals.

Finally, Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) can be toxic in large doses and may cause joint pain, as well as other symptoms like nausea and dizziness.

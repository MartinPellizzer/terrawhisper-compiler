What Are The Main Causes Of Rheumatoid Arthritis?

Rheumatoid arthritis is a condition where the body's immune system mistakenly attacks the joints.

There are several main causes that contribute to this condition. One cause is genetic predisposition. If you have a family history of rheumatoid arthritis, you're more likely to develop the condition. This is because certain genes can make your immune system more likely to attack your joints.

Another cause is the autoimmune response. In people with rheumatoid arthritis, the immune system produces chemicals that attack the lining of the joints, causing inflammation and pain. This is an abnormal response, as the immune system is supposed to protect the body, not attack it. Smoking is also a cause of rheumatoid arthritis. When you smoke, the chemicals in tobacco can damage the lining of the joints and make the immune system more likely to attack the joints.

This makes smoking a risk factor for developing rheumatoid arthritis.

Below are the main causes of rheumatoid arthritis.

  • Genetic Predisposition: Genetic predisposition is a cause of rheumatoid arthritis because a person's genes can make their immune system more likely to attack their own joints, leading to inflammation and damage.
  • Autoimmune Response: In rheumatoid arthritis, the immune system mistakenly attacks and damages the body's own healthy tissues, leading to inflammation and pain in the joints.
  • Smoking: Smoking can cause rheumatoid arthritis because it damages the lungs and makes the body less able to fight off the infection that triggers the disease, and also because it releases chemicals that make the body's immune system attack its own healthy tissues.
  • Family History: Research suggests that people with a family history of rheumatoid arthritis are more likely to develop the condition because they may inherit certain genes that increase their risk of the disease, although other factors such as lifestyle and environment also play a role.
  • Hormonal Imbalance: Hormonal imbalance can cause rheumatoid arthritis because an imbalance of certain hormones, particularly estrogen, can trigger an overactive immune system to attack the lining of the joints, leading to inflammation and pain.
  • Infections: Infections can trigger rheumatoid arthritis by causing the body's immune system to mistakenly attack its own healthy tissues, which can lead to inflammation and joint damage.
  • Gut Bacteria: Research suggests that an imbalance of gut bacteria, also known as dysbiosis, can trigger inflammation and lead to rheumatoid arthritis by releasing toxic compounds that cause the immune system to mistakenly attack the body's healthy tissues.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Rheumatoid Arthritis?

People with rheumatoid arthritis should be cautious with certain herbs that may worsen symptoms or interact with medications.

One such herb is Devil's Claw (Harpagophytum procumbens), which may increase the risk of bleeding and interact with blood-thinning medications. Another herb to avoid is Feverfew (Tanacetum parthenium), as it may increase the risk of bleeding and interact with blood thinners and diabetes medications.

Valerian root (Valeriana officinalis) can also be problematic, as it may interact with medications that slow down the heart rate and worsen symptoms of rheumatoid arthritis. Finally, Ginseng (Panax ginseng) may cause or worsen inflammation and interact with diabetes and blood pressure medications.

These herbs should be used with caution or avoided altogether to prevent complications.

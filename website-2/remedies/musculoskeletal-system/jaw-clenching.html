What Are The Main Causes Of Jaw Clenching?

Jaw clenching, also known as bruxism, can be caused by several factors.

One main cause is anxiety. When people feel stressed or anxious, they often unconsciously clench their jaws as a way to cope with their emotions. This can happen without them even realizing it, especially during times of high stress or when they're feeling overwhelmed. Another cause is temporomandibular joint disorder, or TMJ disorder.

This condition affects the joint that connects the jawbone to the skull, causing pain and discomfort in the jaw area. People with TMJ disorder may clench their jaws due to the pain and discomfort, or as a way to try to alleviate it. Clenching habit is also a common cause of jaw clenching. Some people develop a habit of clenching their jaws, often without even realizing it, and this can become a deeply ingrained habit.

This can be due to various factors, including stress, nervousness, or even a learned behavior.

Below are the main causes of jaw clenching.

  • Anxiety: When a person is feeling anxious, their body's 'fight or flight' response is triggered, causing tense muscles, including those in the jaw, to clench tightly as a way to prepare for the perceived threat or stress.
  • Temporomandibular joint disorder: Temporomandibular joint disorder (TMJ) is a cause of jaw clenching because it puts pressure on the joint that connects the jawbone to the skull, causing the muscles in the jaw to tighten and clench involuntarily.
  • Clenching habit: Clenching habit is a cause of jaw clenching because people often unconsciously tighten their facial muscles, including their jaw, when they are stressed, anxious, or concentrating on a task.
  • Bruxism: Bruxism is a cause of jaw clenching because people with this condition unknowingly grind and clench their teeth and jaw muscles tightly while they are awake or even while they are asleep due to stress, anxiety, or other emotional issues.
  • Stress: When people feel stressed or anxious, their bodies release chemicals that prepare them to either fight or run away, causing them to unconsciously clench their jaws as a way to cope with the tension.

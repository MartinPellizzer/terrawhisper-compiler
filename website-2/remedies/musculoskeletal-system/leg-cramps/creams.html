1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis creams contains valerenic acid, valeranone, and valeranone acetate as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have sedative and anti-inflammatory properties that can help to relax the muscles and reduce pain. Valerenic acid also has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help to alleviate muscle spasms and cramps. By reducing muscle tension and inflammation, Valeriana officinalis creams can provide relief from leg cramps.

By applying Valeriana officinalis creams to the affected area, you can experience a soothing and calming effect that can help to ease discomfort and promote relaxation.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of Valeriana officinalis essential oil, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool for 5-10 minutes. Add the Valeriana officinalis essential oil and vitamin E oil, and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a glass jar or container and let it cool and solidify completely before use.

  3. Stir in the turmeric and ginger powders until well combined. Remove from heat.
  4. Add the peppermint essential oil and stir well. Pour the mixture into a clean glass jar or tin.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and camphor, which are the key bioactive constituents responsible for its therapeutic effects.

These compounds have a calming effect on the muscles, which can help to relieve leg cramps caused by muscle spasms. By using Lavandula angustifolia creams, people can benefit from these compounds' ability to relax muscles, reduce inflammation, and promote blood flow, making it a potential solution for relieving leg cramps.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water and 1/4 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water and let it cool down for 10 minutes. Then, add the dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the water.
  3. Let the mixture infuse for 10-15 minutes, then strain it using a cheesecloth or a fine mesh.
  4. Combine 1/4 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, and 2 tablespoons of the infused Lavandula angustifolia liquid in a bowl.
  5. Whip the mixture until it's smooth and creamy. Pour it into a container and store it in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains active constituents like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that help to relax muscles and reduce muscle spasms. By reducing muscle spasms and inflammation, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams can provide relief from painful leg cramps.

  3. Add the dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root powder and shea butter to the melted mixture. Stir until well combined.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool slightly. Pour the mixture into small containers or a tin mold.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify completely. Store the creams in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

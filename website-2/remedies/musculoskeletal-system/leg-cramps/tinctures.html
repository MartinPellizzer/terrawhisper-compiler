1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis tinctures contains valerenic acid, valeranone, and isovaleric acid as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have a sedative and anti-spasmodic effect, which helps to relax muscles and reduce muscle cramps. The sedative properties of valerenic acid and valeranone help to calm the nervous system, reducing the likelihood of muscle spasms. Isovaleric acid has a relaxing effect on the muscles, which helps to ease cramps and discomfort.

By reducing muscle spasms and relaxing the muscles, Valeriana officinalis tinctures can help to alleviate leg cramps and provide relief.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Valeriana officinalis roots and 2 cups of 80% ethanol (vodka or brandy work too) in a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the dried roots and ethanol in the jar, then seal it tightly.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the tincture to small glass bottles and store them in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of tincture in water for relief from leg cramps.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains the bioactive constituents gingerol and shogaol, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which help to relieve pain and reduce swelling in the affected area. Gingerol also has a warming effect on the body, which can help to relax muscles and improve blood flow to the affected area. The warming effect of gingerol can help to relieve cramps in the legs by relaxing the muscles and reducing spasms.

Additionally, ginger has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce inflammation and swelling in the affected area, providing relief from leg cramps.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (Ginger) roots, 2 cups of 80% vodka, and a glass jar.
  2. Wash the ginger roots and chop them into small pieces. Fill a clean glass jar with the chopped ginger.
  3. Pour 2 cups of 80% vodka over the ginger in the jar. Make sure the ginger is completely covered by the vodka.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will help the tincture to steep.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

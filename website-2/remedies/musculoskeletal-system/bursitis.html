What Are The Main Causes Of Bursitis?

Bursitis is a painful condition that occurs when the fluid-filled sacs, called bursae, that cushion joints and reduce friction become inflamed.

The main causes of bursitis include repetitive motion, trauma, and infection. Repetitive motion, such as lifting heavy objects, gardening, or typing, can cause bursitis by putting constant pressure on the bursae, leading to inflammation.

This is why people who engage in repetitive activities are more likely to develop bursitis in areas such as the elbow, shoulder, or hip. Trauma, like a fall or direct blow to a joint, can also cause bursitis by damaging the bursae and leading to inflammation. Infection, often caused by bacteria, can also lead to bursitis by entering the bursae and causing an infection.

This type of bursitis is known as septic bursitis and can be more serious and painful than other forms of bursitis.

Below are the main causes of bursitis.

  • Repetitive Motion: Repetitive motion can cause bursitis because it leads to repeated friction and pressure on the fluid-filled sacs, called bursae, which cushion joints and reduce friction, eventually causing them to become inflamed and painful.
  • Trauma: Trauma, such as a direct blow to the joint, can cause bursitis by irritating or inflaming the bursa, the fluid-filled sac that cushions the joint, leading to swelling, pain, and inflammation.
  • Infection: When a foreign object, such as a splinter or a cut, enters the skin and infects the tissue near a bursa, it can cause the bursa to become inflamed and swollen, leading to bursitis.
  • Inflammation: Inflammation occurs when the body's immune system responds to irritation or injury in the bursa, a fluid-filled sac that cushions joints, causing it to become swollen and painful.
  • Aging: As people age, the cartilage that cushions the joints and helps prevent rubbing between bones and tendons breaks down, leading to inflammation and swelling in the bursae, which are fluid-filled sacs that reduce friction in the joints.
  • Exercise: Repetitive movements and heavy impacts from exercise can irritate the fluid-filled sacs in joints, leading to inflammation and swelling that characterizes bursitis.
  • Poor Posture: Poor posture can cause bursitis because it puts constant strain and pressure on the joints and surrounding soft tissues, leading to inflammation and irritation of the bursae, which are fluid-filled sacs that cushion and support the joints.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Bursitis?

If you have bursitis, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can cause more harm or irritation.

One herb to steer clear of is 'Echinacea' (Echinacea spp. ), as it can cause inflammation and worsen joint pain. 'Ginger' is often used to help with pain, but it's not recommended for bursitis as it can stimulate the immune system and cause more irritation.

'Turmeric' is also not suitable for bursitis, as it contains a compound called curcumin, which can cause stomach upset and worsen joint inflammation. 'Willow bark' (Salix spp.). contains salicin, a compound similar to aspirin, which can cause stomach problems and interact with medications.

'Garlic' (Allium sativum) can cause stomach upset and interact with blood thinners, making it a bad choice for bursitis sufferers.

What Are The Main Causes Of Osteoarthritis?

Osteoarthritis is a condition where the joints in our body wear out, causing pain and stiffness.

The main causes of osteoarthritis are age, genetics, and obesity. As we age, our joints naturally wear down. The cartilage that cushions the joints breaks down, and the bones rub against each other, causing pain. This is why osteoarthritis is more common in older people.

Genetics also play a role in osteoarthritis. Some people may inherit a tendency to develop the condition due to their family history. For example, if a person's parents or grandparents have osteoarthritis, they are more likely to get it too. Obesity is another cause of osteoarthritis. When we are overweight or obese, our joints have to work harder to support our body weight.

This puts extra stress on the joints, leading to wear and tear, and increasing the risk of osteoarthritis.

Below are the main causes of osteoarthritis.

  • Age: As people get older, their joints naturally wear down and lose the protective cushioning, making it a major cause of osteoarthritis.
  • Genetics: Genetics play a role in osteoarthritis because people are born with certain genetic traits that can affect the health of their joints, making them more likely to wear out and cause arthritis over time.
  • Obesity: Excess body weight puts extra stress on joints, particularly in the knees, hips, and spine, leading to wear and tear on the cartilage and bone, which can cause osteoarthritis over time.
  • Trauma: Trauma is a cause of osteoarthritis because a severe injury or blow to a joint can cause the cartilage to wear down and the bone to rub against bone, leading to painful arthritis.
  • Injury: When a joint is injured, it can lead to the breakdown and wear and tear of the cartilage over time, causing osteoarthritis.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Osteoarthritis?

If you have osteoarthritis, you should avoid some herbs that can make joint pain worse.

For example, Devil's Claw (Harpagophytum procumbens) is a popular herb for pain relief, but it can irritate the stomach and worsen osteoarthritis symptoms. Another herb to avoid is Willow Bark (Salix alba), which contains salicylic acid, similar to aspirin.

While it may relieve pain, it can also cause stomach problems and interact with other medications. You should also be cautious with St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum), as it can slow down the healing process and make joint pain worse. Additionally, Ginger's cousin, Angelica (Angelica archangelica), can cause stomach upset and worsen osteoarthritis symptoms.

Finally, Feverfew (Tanacetum parthenium) can cause stomach problems and interact with blood thinners, which can be a concern for people with osteoarthritis.

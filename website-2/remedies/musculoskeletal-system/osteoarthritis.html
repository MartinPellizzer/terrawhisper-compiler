The Osteoarthritis Guide: Causes, Medicinal Herbs, and Herbal Preparations for a Healthier You

What are the causes of osteoarthritis?

Osteoarthritis is a condition that affects the joints, causing pain and stiffness.

There are several main causes of osteoarthritis, and understanding them can help prevent or manage the condition. One of the main causes is obesity. When you're overweight or obese, your joints have to work harder to support your body weight, which can lead to wear and tear on the joint cartilage and bones. This can cause inflammation and pain in the joints. Trauma is another cause of osteoarthritis. This can be a sudden injury, like a broken bone or a severe fall, which can cause damage to the joint cartilage and bones. Even if the injury heals, the damage can remain, leading to osteoarthritis over time. For example, a knee injury from sports or a fall can cause osteoarthritis in the knee joint years later. Aging is a major risk factor for osteoarthritis.

As we get older, our joints naturally wear down, and the cartilage and bones can become damaged. This can lead to osteoarthritis, especially in the hips, knees, and spine. Even if you're not overweight or injured, the natural aging process can cause osteoarthritis. Injury is also a cause of osteoarthritis. Repeated strain on a joint, like running or lifting heavy objects, can cause damage to the cartilage and bones over time. This can lead to osteoarthritis, especially in the joints that bear the most weight, like the hips and knees. Finally, joint misalignment is a cause of osteoarthritis. When the bones in a joint are not properly aligned, it can cause uneven wear and tear on the cartilage and bones. This can lead to osteoarthritis, especially in the spine and hips.

For example, a misaligned hip joint can cause osteoarthritis in the hip joint over time.

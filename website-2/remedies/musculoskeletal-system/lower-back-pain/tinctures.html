1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa tinctures contains a compound called curcumin, which is a powerful anti-inflammatory.

Curcumin blocks an enzyme called COX-2, reducing inflammation and swelling in the lower back. Turmerone, another active compound, has analgesic properties that help relieve pain and discomfort. Additionally, curcuma longa tinctures have antioxidant properties, which protect the body from free radicals that can cause tissue damage and pain.

By reducing inflammation and pain, and protecting the body from damage, curcuma longa tinctures can help alleviate lower back pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa roots and 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the dried Curcuma longa roots to the jar and cover them with vodka.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture, 2-3 times a day, to help relieve lower back pain.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and pain in the lower back by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that promote inflammation. The bioactive constituents in ginger tinctures, including gingerols and shogaols, also have analgesic and antispasmodic effects, which can help relax tense muscles and reduce muscle spasms in the lower back. By reducing inflammation and relaxing muscles, ginger tinctures can provide relief from lower back pain.

The combination of anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antispasmodic properties in ginger tinctures makes them a potential natural remedy for lower back pain.

  1. Gather 250g of fresh Zingiber officinale roots and dry them for 2-3 weeks.
  2. Grind the dried roots into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Combine 250g of the powdered Zingiber officinale roots with 1 cup of 80% ethanol in a glass jar.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a new glass jar, discarding the solids.

3. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis tinctures contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have sedative and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce pain and discomfort in the lower back. The anti-inflammatory properties of valerenic acid and isovaleric acid can reduce swelling and inflammation in the muscles and joints, contributing to lower back pain. Valeranone, on the other hand, has a sedative effect that can help relax tense muscles and improve sleep quality, both of which are essential for managing lower back pain.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Valeriana officinalis tinctures may provide relief from lower back pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Valeriana officinalis roots.

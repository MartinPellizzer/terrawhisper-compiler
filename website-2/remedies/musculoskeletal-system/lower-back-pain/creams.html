What herbal creams you should avoid if you have lower back pain?

When using herbal creams for lower back pain, be cautious with those containing Willow bark (Salix alba) or Wintergreen (Gaultheria procumbens).

Willow bark contains salicylates, which can cause stomach problems and interact with blood thinners. Wintergreen, on the other hand, contains methyl salicylate, a compound similar to aspirin. Using these creams may lead to excessive salicylate intake, potentially causing stomach issues, allergic reactions, or even kidney damage.

Additionally, creams containing Sassafras (Sassafras albidum) or Birch bark (Betula species) should be avoided due to their potential to cause liver damage or interact with certain medications. Sassafras contains a compound called safrole, which can be toxic in high doses. Birch bark, although considered safe in small amounts, may cause liver damage if taken excessively.

If you're considering using herbal creams for lower back pain, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before making any decisions.

What Are The Main Causes Of Stiff Neck?

A stiff neck can be caused by several factors.

Lymphadenitis is one of these causes, where the lymph nodes in the neck become swollen and inflamed, usually due to an infection like the flu or tonsillitis. This swelling can put pressure on the surrounding muscles, leading to stiffness and pain. Torticollis is another cause of a stiff neck. It is a condition where the neck muscles become twisted or knotted, often due to an injury or poor posture.

This twisting can put pressure on the nerves and blood vessels in the neck, leading to pain and stiffness. Scalene muscle strain is also a common cause of a stiff neck. The scalene muscles are located on either side of the neck and help move the neck and head. If these muscles become strained or inflamed, they can cause pain and stiffness in the neck.

This strain can be caused by heavy lifting, bending, or even sleeping in an awkward position.

Below are the main causes of stiff neck.

  • Lymphadenitis: Lymphadenitis is a cause of stiff neck because the swelling and inflammation of lymph nodes in the neck can put pressure on nearby muscles and tissues, making it difficult to move the neck freely.
  • Torticollis: Torticollis is a cause of stiff neck because the muscles in the neck become tight and inflamed, making it difficult to move the head from side to side.
  • Scalene Muscle Strain: A Scalene Muscle Strain is a cause of stiff neck because the Scalene muscles, which are located in the neck, become inflamed and tight when they are stretched or pulled, leading to pain and stiffness in the neck area.
  • Cervical Spondylosis: Cervical Spondylosis is a cause of stiff neck because the wear and tear of the spinal discs and joints in the neck can cause inflammation and damage to the surrounding muscles and nerves, leading to pain and stiffness.
  • Cervical Radiculitis: Cervical radiculitis is a cause of stiff neck because inflammation or irritation of the nerve roots in the neck, usually due to a herniated disc or other damage, sends pain signals to the brain, causing muscle tension and stiffness in the neck area.

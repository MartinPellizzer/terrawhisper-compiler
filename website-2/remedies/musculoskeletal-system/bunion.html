What Are The Main Causes Of Bunion?

A bunion is a bony bump that forms on the joint at the base of the big toe.

The main causes of a bunion are related to the way the foot functions. One cause is 'Abnormal Tracking', where the big toe starts to lean towards the second toe instead of pointing straight ahead. This can happen due to a flat foot or a foot that rolls inward too much, causing the big toe to shift and put pressure on the joint.

Another cause is 'Genetic Predisposition', where a person is born with a foot shape that is more prone to bunions. This can be due to the way the bones and ligaments are formed, making it more likely for the big toe to shift and put pressure on the joint. Wearing 'High Heels' is also a common cause of bunions. High heels can cause the foot to shift and put pressure on the big toe joint, especially if worn for long periods or frequently.

This can cause the joint to become inflamed and eventually form a bony bump.

Below are the main causes of bunion.

  • Abnormal Tracking: Abnormal tracking occurs when the big toe joint moves outward and away from its normal position, causing the foot to roll inward and putting uneven pressure on the joint, which can eventually lead to the formation of a bunion.
  • Genetic Predisposition: Genetic predisposition is a cause of bunions because a person's genes can pass on a foot shape or structure that makes them more likely to develop a bunion, such as a big toe that leans towards the other toes or a foot with a tendency to roll inward.
  • Wearing High Heels: Wearing high heels can cause bunions because they put too much pressure on the joint at the base of the big toe, forcing it to shift out of place and leading to the painful, bony bump that is a bunion.
  • Poor Foot Mechanics: Poor foot mechanics, such as overpronation or flat feet, cause uneven pressure on the big toe joint, leading to bunions as the toe is forced out of its normal alignment and the bone and tissue surrounding it become irritated and inflamed.
  • Overpronation: Overpronation causes bunion because when the foot rolls inward too much, it puts uneven pressure on the big toe joint, leading to pain and a bony growth at the base of the toe.
  • Flat Feet: Flat feet can cause bunions because the abnormal rolling or twisting of the foot can put uneven pressure on the big toe joint, leading to the toe pushing against the shoe and eventually causing a bony growth to form.

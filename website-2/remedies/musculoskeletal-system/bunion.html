What are the causes of bunion?

So, what causes bunions?.

There are several factors that can contribute to their formation. One of the main causes is wearing high heels. When you wear high heels, you put pressure on the joint at the base of your big toe. This pressure can cause the toe to move out of its normal position, leading to a bunion. Another cause is flat feet. When you have flat feet, your arches don't support your feet properly, which can put pressure on the joints in your toes. This can cause the big toe joint to move out of position and form a bunion.

Toe deformity is also a cause of bunions. When you have a toe deformity, such as a hammertoe or a mallet toe, it can put pressure on the joint at the base of your big toe, causing a bunion to form. Arthritis is another cause of bunions. When you have arthritis, the cartilage in your joints can break down, causing the bones to rub against each other. This can cause pain and swelling in the joint, leading to a bunion. Lastly, gout is a cause of bunions. Gout is a condition that causes uric acid crystals to build up in your joints, leading to inflammation and pain. When you have gout, it can cause a bunion to form, especially if you have a history of foot problems.

These are some of the main causes of bunions.

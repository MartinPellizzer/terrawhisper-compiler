For people with arthritis, who may already be experiencing pain and inflammation, this can be particularly problematic. Another tea to be cautious with is one made from Aconitum spp., or Monk's Hood. This herb contains toxic compounds that can cause nausea, vomiting, and even heart problems if consumed in large amounts. Since people with arthritis often take other medications, the risk of interactions is higher.

Tea made from Yohimbe (Pausinystalia johimbe) is also not recommended for those with arthritis. Yohimbine, the active compound in Yohimbe, can increase blood pressure and heart rate, which can be hazardous for people with pre-existing heart conditions or high blood pressure. Like Ephedra, this can be particularly problematic for people with arthritis who may already be experiencing pain and inflammation. Lastly, tea made from Senecio spp., or Ragwort, should be avoided.

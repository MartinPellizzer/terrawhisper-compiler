Ankylosing spondylitis is a chronic inflammatory disease that affects the spine and other joints.

It causes pain, stiffness, and limited mobility, making everyday activities like walking or bending difficult. The exact cause of ankylosing spondylitis is still unknown, but it's believed to be linked to a combination of genetic and environmental factors. What are the causes of ankylosing spondylitis?

Ankylosing spondylitis is a type of arthritis that causes the spine to become inflamed and stiff.

There are several main causes of this condition. One of the main causes is the presence of a specific gene called HLA-B27. This gene is found in many people, but it's more common in those with ankylosing spondylitis. When someone with this gene gets the condition, their immune system starts to attack the joints in the spine, leading to pain and stiffness. Another key cause is genetic predisposition. If one of your parents has ankylosing spondylitis, you're more likely to get it too. This is because you've inherited their genes, including the HLA-B27 gene.

Family history is also a significant factor, as people with a family history of the condition are more likely to develop it. This suggests that there's a strong genetic link to the condition. Some people with ankylosing spondylitis also have other conditions, such as inflammatory bowel disease (IBD). IBD is a condition that causes inflammation in the digestive system, and it's often linked to ankylosing spondylitis. It's not clear why this is the case, but it's thought that the inflammation in the digestive system may be related to the inflammation in the joints. Finally, some people with ankylosing spondylitis also have a condition called psoriasis. Psoriasis is a condition that causes red, scaly patches on the skin, and it's often linked to ankylosing spondylitis.

Like IBD, it's not clear why this is the case, but it's thought that the inflammation in the skin may be related to the inflammation in the joints.

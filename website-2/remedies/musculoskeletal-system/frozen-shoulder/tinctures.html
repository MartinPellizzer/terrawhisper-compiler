1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa tinctures contains curcuminoids, including curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which are the biologically active constituents.

These curcuminoids have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the body. The anti-inflammatory properties of curcuminoids can help alleviate pain and stiffness associated with frozen shoulder, a condition characterized by inflammation and reduced mobility in the shoulder joint. Additionally, curcuminoids have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, such as COX-2 and 5-LOX, which contribute to the development of frozen shoulder.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, curcuma longa tinctures may provide relief from frozen shoulder symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa roots and 2 cups of 80% vodka or high-proof spirit.
  2. Combine the dried roots and vodka in a clean glass jar, making sure the roots are completely covered.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Label the jar with the date and contents, and store it in the fridge to slow down the aging process. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture 2-3 times a day for frozen shoulder relief.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains bioactive constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

These compounds help to reduce pain and inflammation in the affected area of the shoulder, making it easier to move and perform daily activities. The antispasmodic properties of gingerols and shogaols also help to relax the muscles and tendons, reducing stiffness and improving range of motion. By reducing inflammation and relaxing the muscles, Zingiber officinale tinctures can help to break the pain cycle and promote healing in frozen shoulder.

Regular use of Zingiber officinale tinctures may help to alleviate symptoms and support the body's natural recovery process.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Zingiber officinale (ginger) root.
  2. Combine the ginger root with 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 20-30 drops, 3 times a day, as needed for frozen shoulder relief.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains glycyrrhizin, a compound that helps reduce inflammation and swelling in the joints.

This tincture also contains flavonoids and triterpenoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe the pain and stiffness associated with frozen shoulder. Glycyrrhizin's anti-inflammatory effects can help reduce the inflammation that causes frozen shoulder, making it easier to move and perform daily activities. 4. Capsicum annuum

Capsicum annuum tinctures contains a high concentration of capsaicin, a bioactive compound that has anti-inflammatory properties.

Capsaicin works by blocking the production of a chemical called substance P, which transmits pain signals to the brain. It also increases the production of a chemical called calcitonin gene-related peptide, which helps to reduce pain and inflammation in the affected area. The bioactive constituents in Capsicum annuum tinctures, including capsaicin, flavonoids, and carotenoids, have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects that help to reduce inflammation and pain associated with frozen shoulder.

By reducing inflammation and pain, Capsicum annuum tinctures may help to improve range of motion and function in individuals with frozen shoulder.

  1. Get 250ml of 80% vodka and 250g of dried Capsicum annuum. Chop the dried Capsicum annuum into small pieces.
  2. Combine the chopped Capsicum annuum and vodka in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Seal the jar and leave it in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks. Shake the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Take 10-15 drops, 3 times a day, as needed, to help with frozen shoulder symptoms.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent frozen shoulder from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent frozen shoulder.

Ginger and turmeric tea, for example, contain anti-inflammatory compounds that can reduce pain and inflammation. Willow bark tea also contains salicin, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

These teas can help keep the shoulder joint flexible and reduce the risk of frozen shoulder.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for frozen shoulder every day?

Herbal teas can be a soothing addition to your frozen shoulder treatment, but consuming them daily might not be the best approach.

Some herbs, like turmeric and ginger, can be beneficial, but excessive consumption can lead to stomach upset or interact with medications.

It's essential to listen to your body and adjust your tea intake accordingly.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in frozen shoulder?

For some people, herbal teas may start to ease frozen shoulder symptoms within a few days to a week.

However, it can take longer, sometimes up to several weeks, for noticeable improvements.

It's different for everyone, and results can vary depending on the individual and the specific tea being used.

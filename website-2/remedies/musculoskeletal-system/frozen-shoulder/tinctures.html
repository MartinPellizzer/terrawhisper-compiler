Frozen shoulder remedies for office workers: 7 herbal tinctures to try

Office workers are at high risk of developing frozen shoulder due to their sedentary lifestyle and repetitive movements.

Prolonged sitting and leaning to one side can cause inflammation and stiffness in the shoulder joint, making everyday tasks a challenge. This condition forces office workers to take extended breaks to stretch and move around, affecting their productivity and overall well-being. It can also lead to feelings of frustration and anxiety, as the constant pain and stiffness make it difficult to complete tasks.

Furthermore, frozen shoulder can impact office workers' ability to participate in social activities and maintain a work-life balance, leading to isolation and depression. As a result, office workers are constantly seeking relief from the pain and stiffness associated with frozen shoulder. While medication and physical therapy are common treatments, many are turning to herbal tinctures as a natural and holistic approach to managing their symptoms. These tinctures are made from potent plant extracts that are absorbed quickly by the body, providing fast relief from pain and inflammation.

By taking these tinctures regularly, office workers can reduce their reliance on medication and alleviate the underlying causes of frozen shoulder, allowing them to return to their normal routine and maintain a healthy work-life balance.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contain compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties.

These properties are helpful in reducing inflammation and pain associated with frozen shoulder, also known as adhesive capsulitis. The condition involves inflammation and scarring of the tissue surrounding the shoulder joint, leading to pain and stiffness. The anti-inflammatory compounds in ginger tinctures may help reduce inflammation and alleviate pain in the affected joint. Additionally, ginger has a warming and soothing effect, which can increase blood flow to the area and promote healing.

To use Zingiber officinale tinctures for frozen shoulder, you can take 20-30 drops of the tincture in water, 2-3 times a day. It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using any new supplements, especially if you're already taking medications or have underlying health conditions. Taking Zingiber officinale tinctures regularly may lead to reduced pain and inflammation in the frozen shoulder, making it easier to move the joint. You may also experience improved range of motion and reduced stiffness. However, it's crucial to note that the effectiveness of ginger tinctures can vary from person to person, and it may take some time to notice the benefits.

It's also essential to combine tincture use with other treatments, such as physical therapy and stretching exercises, to achieve optimal results.

The capsaicin in Capsicum annuum tinctures helps to reduce pain and inflammation associated with frozen shoulder by blocking the production of a chemical called substance P, which transmits pain signals to the brain. This action can help to reduce pain and stiffness in the shoulder joint. To try Capsicum annuum tinctures for frozen shoulder, take a few drops of the tincture sublingually (under the tongue) 2-3 times a day, and massage the area around the shoulder joint with a small amount of arnica oil or cream to enhance the absorption of the capsaicin.

If you experience intense burning or discomfort, discontinue use and consult with a healthcare professional. Likely outcomes from taking Capsicum annuum tinctures for frozen shoulder include reduced pain and stiffness, improved range of motion, and enhanced recovery.

However, it's crucial to note that Capsicum annuum tinctures may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and may exacerbate conditions like acid reflux or stomach ulcers.

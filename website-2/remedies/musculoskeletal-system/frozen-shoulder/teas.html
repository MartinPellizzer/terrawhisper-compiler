10 Herbal Teas For Frozen Shoulder

Herbal teas can be a great help if you're dealing with frozen shoulder.

This condition causes pain and stiffness in the shoulder joint, making it difficult to move your arm or even lift it. The good news is that certain herbs have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling and ease pain. Zingiber officinale, or ginger, is one such herb that's often used to relieve frozen shoulder. It contains compounds that block the production of pain-causing chemicals in the body, making it a natural pain reliever.

Another herb that's commonly used is Glycyrrhiza glabra, or licorice root, which has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling and promote healing. Curcuma longa, or turmeric, is also a great example - it contains curcumin, a powerful anti-inflammatory compound that can help reduce pain and stiffness. Drinking these herbal teas can bring numerous benefits to your life. For one, it can help you manage your pain levels, allowing you to move your arm more freely and perform daily tasks without discomfort. It can also promote healing and reduce inflammation, which can help your shoulder recover faster.

By incorporating herbal teas into your daily routine, you can take a more natural approach to managing frozen shoulder and improve your overall quality of life.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains a compound called gingerol, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and pain in the shoulder joint, making it a good choice for people with frozen shoulder. Gingerol also contains a compound called shogaol, which helps relax muscles and improve blood flow to the affected area. This increased blood flow helps to reduce stiffness and promote healing, making it easier to move the shoulder joint.

The anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties of gingerol and shogaol in Zingiber officinale teas may help alleviate the symptoms of frozen shoulder.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger, 1 cup of water, and honey or lemon to taste.
  2. Wash the ginger and peel it. Cut it into small pieces.
  3. Boil the water in a pot and add the ginger pieces. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the ginger. Add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve frozen shoulder pain and inflammation.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains licorice root extract, which has anti-inflammatory compounds like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids.

These compounds help reduce swelling and pain in the shoulder joint. Glycyrrhizin also has a steroid-like effect, which can help reduce inflammation and relax muscle tension. The flavonoids in licorice root tea may also have antioxidant properties, which can help protect the shoulder joint from further damage.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing muscles, Glycyrrhiza glabra teas may help alleviate the symptoms of frozen shoulder.

  1. Get 2 tablespoons of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root. You can buy it from a health store or online.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Add the 2 tablespoons of dried root to the boiling water. Let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and let it cool. You can add honey if you want it sweet.
  5. Drink 1/2 cup of the tea 2-3 times a day for relief from frozen shoulder pain.

3. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa teas contains a compound called curcumin, which is a powerful anti-inflammatory agent.

Curcumin helps reduce swelling and pain in the shoulder by blocking the production of inflammatory molecules. The tea also contains other bioactive constituents like demethoxycurcumin and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which have potent antioxidant properties that help protect the body from damage caused by free radicals. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Curcuma longa tea can help improve blood flow and reduce stiffness in the shoulder, making it a potential treatment for frozen shoulder.

Regular consumption of Curcuma longa tea may also help reduce the risk of further inflammation and promote healing in the affected joint.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Curcuma longa root powder and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the Curcuma longa powder in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup to remove the powder.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to the Curcuma longa tea to sweeten it.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, ideally 30 minutes before meals, for 2-3 weeks.

4. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium teas contains sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are known to have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds work together to reduce inflammation and pain in the shoulder joint. The flavonoids, specifically kaempferol and quercetin, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to frozen shoulder symptoms. The sesquiterpenes, such as achillenes and sesquiterpenoid acids, have been found to have analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, further reducing pain and discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and pain, Achillea millefolium teas may help alleviate frozen shoulder symptoms and promote recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Achillea millefolium leaves, flowers, and stems.
  2. Wash the plant parts with cold water, then dry them with a clean towel.
  3. Use a tablespoon to measure out 1-2 tbsp of dried Achillea millefolium leaves per cup of boiling water.
  4. Steep the leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain the mixture using a fine-mesh sieve.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the tea 2-3 times a day for relief from frozen shoulder symptoms.

Camellia sinensis teas contains compounds like theaflavins and thearubigins, which are antioxidants that help reduce inflammation.

8. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis teas contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates, which are bioactive constituents that contribute to its therapeutic properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which can help reduce pain and inflammation associated with frozen shoulder. The valerenic acid in Valeriana officinalis teas has a sedative effect on the nervous system, which can help calm the muscles and improve range of motion. The valepotriates, on the other hand, have a muscle-relaxing effect, which can help reduce muscle spasm and stiffness often experienced with frozen shoulder.

By reducing pain and inflammation, and relaxing the muscles, Valeriana officinalis teas may help alleviate symptoms of frozen shoulder.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1-2 teaspoons of dried Valeriana officinalis roots.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) and set aside.
  3. Place the dried Valeriana officinalis roots in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.
  4. Pour the boiling water over the roots and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and add the measured honey (if using). Drink 2-3 cups a day to help with frozen shoulder symptoms.

9. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis teas contains compounds like quinine, cinchonine, and cinchonidine.

These alkaloids have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling in the shoulder joint.

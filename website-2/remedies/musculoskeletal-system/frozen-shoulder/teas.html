10 Herbal Teas to Ease Frozen Shoulder for Manual Laborers

Manual laborers are at risk of developing frozen shoulder due to the repetitive and often strenuous nature of their work.

They may lift heavy loads, bend, and twist their arms in ways that can cause strain and irritation to the shoulder joint. This can lead to inflammation, pain, and stiffness, making everyday tasks and even simple movements a struggle. For those who rely on manual labor for their livelihood, a frozen shoulder can be a significant setback, impacting not only their ability to work but also their overall quality of life. Simple tasks like cooking, cleaning, and even sleeping can become difficult and painful, affecting not just their physical well-being but also their mental health. As a result, manual laborers may experience anxiety, stress, and frustration, which can further exacerbate their condition.

Frozen shoulder can also limit their ability to perform their job duties, leading to lost income, reduced productivity, and potential job loss. This can be especially challenging for those who have families to support or depend on their income for basic necessities. In addition, the pain and discomfort associated with frozen shoulder can disrupt their social life, making it difficult to engage in activities they enjoy or participate in social events. Herbal teas have been traditionally used to help alleviate the symptoms of frozen shoulder. These teas are believed to work by reducing inflammation, promoting relaxation, and improving circulation.

By incorporating herbal teas into their daily routine, manual laborers may be able to find some relief from the pain and stiffness associated with frozen shoulder, allowing them to return to their normal activities and maintain their quality of life.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, commonly known as ginger tea, have been used for centuries to help alleviate the symptoms of frozen shoulder.

Frozen shoulder occurs when the connective tissue in the shoulder joint becomes inflamed, leading to pain and stiffness. Ginger tea's active compounds, such as gingerols and shogaols, contain anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce inflammation and alleviate pain. The tea's warming effect can also increase blood flow to the affected area, promoting healing and relaxation. To use ginger tea for frozen shoulder, you can start by making a warm tea by steeping fresh ginger in hot water. You can drink 2-3 cups of ginger tea per day, ideally before bedtime to help you relax and reduce pain.

You can also add honey or lemon to taste. As you drink the tea, focus on gentle stretching exercises to loosen up the shoulder joint. It's essential to avoid sudden movements or heavy lifting, which can exacerbate the condition. As you take ginger tea and practice gentle stretching, you may start to feel a reduction in pain and stiffness in your shoulder. Your range of motion may also improve, allowing you to move your arm more freely. You may also experience a decrease in inflammation, as evidenced by a reduction in redness and swelling in the affected area.

It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new treatment plan, especially if you have underlying medical conditions or are taking medications.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa teas, made from the root of the turmeric plant, contain a powerful compound called curcumin.

Curcumin has potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce pain and swelling in frozen shoulder. This condition occurs when the shoulder joint becomes stiff and painful due to inflammation of the tendons and muscles. Curcumin works by blocking the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to the inflammation and pain. To use Curcuma longa teas for frozen shoulder, steep one teaspoon of dried turmeric root in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain and drink 2-3 times a day. You can also add a pinch of black pepper to enhance the absorption of curcumin.

Additionally, consider combining turmeric tea with other anti-inflammatory herbs like ginger or cinnamon to amplify its effects. It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using Curcuma longa teas, especially if you're taking any medications or have underlying health conditions. As you drink Curcuma longa teas regularly, you can expect to experience a reduction in pain and stiffness in your shoulder. Your range of motion may improve, allowing you to move your arm and shoulder more freely. You may also notice a decrease in inflammation, which can help prevent further damage to the tendons and muscles. It's crucial to be patient, as the effects of Curcuma longa teas may take a few weeks to become noticeable.

It's also essential to maintain a consistent exercise routine and follow a healthy diet to support your recovery.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, also known as licorice root tea, have been traditionally used to help alleviate symptoms of frozen shoulder.

The tea's active compounds, including glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that may help reduce pain and inflammation in the affected joint. The glycyrrhizin in licorice root tea is particularly effective in inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which can exacerbate frozen shoulder symptoms.

To try licorice root tea for frozen shoulder, start by drinking 1-2 cups of tea per day, either hot or cold, for a period of 4-6 weeks. It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using licorice root tea, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or are taking medications, as excessive consumption of glycyrrhizin can cause side effects. As you incorporate licorice root tea into your treatment plan, you may experience reduced pain, improved range of motion, and enhanced overall well-being.

Some users may also notice a decrease in swelling and inflammation in the affected shoulder.

4. Arnica montana

Arnica montana teas are a natural remedy that can help alleviate frozen shoulder symptoms.

The plant contains properties like flavonoids, sesquiterpenes, and glycosides, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds help reduce swelling, pain, and stiffness in the shoulder joint, making it easier to move and perform daily activities. The sesquiterpenes in Arnica montana, specifically helenalin and 11beta-hydroxyhelenalin, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to pain and inflammation. To try Arnica montana tea for frozen shoulder, steep 1 teaspoon of dried Arnica flowers in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day.

You can also add honey or lemon to taste. It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new treatment, especially if you're already taking medications or have underlying medical conditions. When taking Arnica montana tea for frozen shoulder, you can expect to experience reduced pain and inflammation, improved range of motion, and increased mobility. You may also notice a decrease in swelling and stiffness in the shoulder joint. However, it's crucial to be patient and consistent with your treatment, as Arnica montana tea may not work immediately.

It may take several weeks to notice significant improvements.

5. Salvia miltiorrhiza

Salvia miltiorrhiza teas have been used to help alleviate symptoms of frozen shoulder, a condition characterized by stiffness and limited mobility in the shoulder joint.

This is due to the presence of rosmarinic acid, a potent anti-inflammatory compound found in the tea. The flavonoids and phenolic acids in Salvia miltiorrhiza also exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help reduce pain and swelling in the affected area. To try using Salvia miltiorrhiza teas for frozen shoulder, first consult with a healthcare professional to ensure it's safe for you to use, especially if you're currently taking medications or have underlying health conditions. Once you've got the green light, you can steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried Salvia miltiorrhiza leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes to make a tea.

Drink 2-3 cups a day, ideally after meals, to help maximize the absorption of its active compounds. As you incorporate Salvia miltiorrhiza teas into your routine, you may start to notice improvements in your range of motion and pain levels. You may be able to move your shoulder more freely and comfortably, and experience a reduction in stiffness and soreness. However, it's essential to remember that frozen shoulder is a complex condition, and results may vary from person to person.

With consistent use and patience, some people have reported significant relief from their symptoms.

6. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium teas, also known as yarrow tea, contain properties and constituents that may help alleviate symptoms of frozen shoulder.

The tea's anti-inflammatory properties, specifically its high content of flavonoids and sesquiterpene lactones, can help reduce inflammation and pain in the affected joint. Additionally, yarrow tea has been found to have analgesic and antispasmodic properties, which can help relax muscles and improve range of motion. If you're experiencing frozen shoulder symptoms, you can try making yarrow tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried yarrow flowers in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day. To enhance its benefits, you can also apply a warm compress soaked in yarrow tea to the affected area for 15-20 minutes, 2-3 times a day. Taking yarrow tea may lead to a decrease in pain and inflammation, improvement in range of motion, and relaxation of muscles surrounding the affected joint. However, it's essential to note that yarrow tea may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and can cause allergic reactions in some individuals.

Consult with a healthcare professional before using yarrow tea as a treatment for frozen shoulder.

7. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis teas, such as green tea and white tea, have been traditionally used to help alleviate symptoms of frozen shoulder.

The reason for this is due to the presence of certain properties and constituents, like catechins, flavonoids, and theaflavins, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds help reduce pain, swelling, and stiffness in the affected shoulder by blocking the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals and promoting the healing process. To use Camellia sinensis teas for frozen shoulder, steep a tea bag in hot water for 3-5 minutes, then let it cool to a comfortable temperature for drinking.

Drink 2-3 cups of the tea per day, ideally in the morning and evening. You can also apply a cooled tea bag directly to the affected area for 10-15 minutes, 2-3 times a day, to increase its effectiveness. As you incorporate Camellia sinensis teas into your daily routine, you may experience a reduction in pain and stiffness in your shoulder, making it easier to move and perform daily activities. You may also notice an improvement in your range of motion and a decrease in inflammation.

However, the extent of improvement may vary from person to person, depending on the severity of the condition and individual response to the tea's properties.

Hamamelis virginiana teas, also known as Witch Hazel tea, has been traditionally used to help alleviate symptoms of frozen shoulder.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent frozen shoulder from forming?

Some people believe that drinking herbal tea, especially those rich in anti-inflammatory properties like turmeric and ginger, may help prevent frozen shoulder.

These teas may reduce inflammation and ease pain, possibly reducing the risk of developing frozen shoulder.

However, more research is needed to confirm this potential benefit.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for frozen shoulder every day?

Some herbal teas, like ginger and turmeric, may help reduce pain and inflammation from frozen shoulder.

However, it's essential to note that the effectiveness and safety of consuming these teas daily can vary greatly from person to person.

It's crucial to use high-quality tea and follow recommended brewing instructions to minimize potential risks.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in frozen shoulder?

Herbal teas can help alleviate symptoms of frozen shoulder, but it's essential to be patient.

Some people notice a difference within 2-4 weeks, while others may take 6-12 weeks to experience relief.

Results can vary depending on the individual and the specific herbs used.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for frozen shoulder?

For frozen shoulder, drinking herbal tea is often most helpful in the evening.

This is because it can help relax your muscles and calm your mind before bed, making it easier to get a good night's sleep.

As you sleep, your body can work on repairing and reducing inflammation in your shoulder.

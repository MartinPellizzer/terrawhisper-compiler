1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains powerful compounds like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These bioactive constituents have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce inflammation and promote healing in frozen shoulder. Curcumin has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, such as COX-2 and LOX, which contribute to the pain and stiffness associated with frozen shoulder. The antioxidant properties of Curcuma longa essential oils can also help protect the tissues from oxidative damage, promoting a healthy environment for recovery.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Curcuma longa essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of frozen shoulder and support the body's natural recovery process.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh turmeric roots and 2 cups of water. Clean and chop the roots into small pieces.
  2. Heat the water in a saucepan over low heat. Add the chopped turmeric roots and simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Let the liquid sit at room temperature for 2-3 hours. This will help the oil to separate from the water.
  5. Collect the separated oil with a spoon and transfer it to a dark glass bottle. Store it in a cool, dark place.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and pain in the shoulder joint, a common symptom of frozen shoulder. The essential oil also contains zingerone, which has analgesic properties that help reduce pain. The anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils can help relax tense muscles and increase range of motion in the shoulder joint.

The warming sensation of the essential oil may also help increase blood flow to the affected area, promoting healing and relaxation.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale roots, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a glass container with a lid.
  2. Wash the roots and chop them into small pieces. Add 1/2 cup of the chopped roots to a saucepan and cover with 1 cup of water.
  3. Heat the saucepan over low heat for 2-3 hours, or until the liquid reduces to 1/2 cup. Let it cool.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the glass container. Add 1/2 cup of carrier oil to the liquid and stir well.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 days, shaking the container occasionally. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container, and discard the solids.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains camphor, borneol, and pinene as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which are beneficial in reducing pain and swelling associated with frozen shoulder. The antispasmodic properties of borneol and pinene help relax muscles and relieve stiffness, making it easier to move the affected joint. Camphor also has a cooling effect, which can help reduce inflammation and promote blood flow to the affected area.

The combination of these properties in Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils may make it a useful natural remedy for managing frozen shoulder symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water to 100°F (38°C) and add the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves. Let it steep for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a glass bottle and add 1 tablespoon of vodka or glycerin as a preservative.
  5. Store the essential oil in a cool, dark place and use 5-7 drops in a carrier oil for massage, 2-3 times a day.

4. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like cineole, globulol, and alpha-pinene, which have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and pain in the affected shoulder area. The menthol content in eucalyptus oil also helps relax muscles and increase blood flow, which aids in reducing stiffness and promoting healing. Eucalyptus oil's expectorant properties can also help clear out mucus and debris that may be contributing to the frozen shoulder condition.

By applying topically, the essential oil can directly target the affected area, providing relief from frozen shoulder symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil) in a clean glass container.
  2. Combine the Eucalyptus leaves and carrier oil in a saucepan. Heat it over low heat for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into another container, discarding the leaves.
  4. Store the mixture in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Let it sit for 2-3 days to allow the essential oil to separate from the carrier oil.
  5. Carefully pour the essential oil (the top layer) into a new, clean glass bottle. Use it as needed for frozen shoulder relief, mixing a few drops with a carrier oil for application.

5. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains a compound called cinnamaldehyde, which is known for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

This compound helps to reduce pain and inflammation in the shoulders, making it beneficial for individuals with frozen shoulder. The oil also contains linalool and camphene, which are known for their anti-spasmodic and anti-arthritic properties, helping to relax tense muscles and reduce joint inflammation. The analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties of Cinnamomum verum essential oils can help to reduce the stiffness and pain associated with frozen shoulder, making it easier to move the shoulder joint.

By reducing inflammation and pain, Cinnamomum verum essential oils can help to promote healing and relaxation in the affected area.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Cinnamomum verum (Ceylon cinnamon) bark and a clean glass jar.
  2. Dry the Ceylon cinnamon bark in a low-temperature oven (150°F - 200°F) for 2-3 hours.
  3. Grind the dried Ceylon cinnamon bark into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar.
  4. Add 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil) to the powder and mix well.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily, before straining and storing the Cinnamomum verum essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its major bioactive constituents.

These constituents contribute to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which can help alleviate the pain associated with frozen shoulder. The antispasmodic properties of linalool can also help relax the muscles and tendons around the shoulder joint, reducing stiffness and improving range of motion. Additionally, linalyl acetate has been shown to reduce inflammation and promote healing, which can help repair damaged tissues in the shoulder.

By applying Lavandula angustifolia essential oils topically, the bioactive constituents can penetrate the skin and target the affected area, providing relief from frozen shoulder symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers. Choose flowers with no signs of wilting or damage.
  2. Combine 1 cup of flowers with 4 cups of water in a saucepan. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat.
  3. Reduce heat to low and let the mixture simmer for 2 hours. This will help release the essential oils from the flowers.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and let it sit in the fridge for 2-3 days to allow the sediment to settle before using it as a topical treatment for frozen shoulder.

7. Elettaria cardamomum

Elettaria cardamomum essential oils contains compounds like limonene, linalool, and beta-pinene.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which are beneficial in reducing pain and inflammation associated with frozen shoulder. The essential oil's antioxidant properties help protect the muscles and connective tissue from oxidative stress, promoting healing and reducing stiffness. The anti-inflammatory compounds in the oil reduce swelling and pain in the affected area, making it easier to move the shoulder joint.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Elettaria cardamomum essential oils may be a useful natural remedy for alleviating frozen shoulder symptoms.

  1. Gather 250 grams of Elettaria cardamomum seeds and 500 ml of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil) in a clean, dry space.
  2. Heat the oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water to 40°C (104°F).
  3. Add the Elettaria cardamomum seeds to the heated oil and let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Elettaria cardamomum essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid in a cool, dry place.

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains rosmarinic acid, borneol, and bornyl acetate, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

These properties can help reduce pain and inflammation associated with frozen shoulder, a condition characterized by stiffness and limited mobility in the shoulder joint.

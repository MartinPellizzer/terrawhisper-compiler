What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have frozen shoulder?

If you have frozen shoulder, there are some herbal essential oils you should avoid.

One of them is Wintergreen (Gaultheria procumbens). This oil has a compound called methyl salicylate, which can increase blood flow and reduce pain. However, it can also thin the blood and increase the risk of bleeding. This could be a problem if you have frozen shoulder and are taking blood-thinning medication or have a bleeding disorder.

Another oil to avoid is Birch (Betula alba). Like Wintergreen, it also contains methyl salicylate, which can increase the risk of bleeding. You should also avoid Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea) if you have frozen shoulder. This oil can slow down the movement of your muscles and joints, which can make it harder to move your arm and shoulder. Lastly, avoid Pennyroyal (Mentha pulegium) if you have frozen shoulder.

This oil can cause liver damage and interact with certain medications, including those you might be taking for your frozen shoulder.

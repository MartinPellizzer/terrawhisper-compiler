What herbal tinctures you should avoid if you have jaw pain?

If you have jaw pain, it's best to avoid herbal tinctures that can irritate or inflame the jaw and surrounding areas.

One such herb is 'Capsicum annuum', also known as cayenne pepper or hot pepper. The capsaicin in this herb can cause pain and inflammation, which may worsen jaw pain.

Another herb to avoid is 'Gnaphalium leucomodon' or 'Cudweed', which may cause stomach upset and irritation in some people, potentially exacerbating jaw pain. 'Euphorbia cyparissias' or 'Cypress spurge' is also not recommended, as it can cause stomach problems and may interact with other medications. Lastly, 'Rhus toxicodendron' or 'Poison ivy' is to be avoided due to its potential to cause skin irritation, which may spread to the jaw and worsen pain.

These herbs should be used with caution, and it's best to consult with a healthcare professional before using them, especially if you're experiencing jaw pain.

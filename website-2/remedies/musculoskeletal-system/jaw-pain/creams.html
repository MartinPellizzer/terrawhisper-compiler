1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can help reduce swelling and ease pain in the jaw area. Linalool has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help relax tense muscles and reduce pain caused by jaw clenching. The antispasmodic properties of linalyl acetate can help relieve spasms and cramps in the jaw and face.

The combination of these properties in Lavandula angustifolia creams makes it a potential remedy for jaw pain relief.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 10 drops of Lavandula angustifolia essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool until it reaches 120°F to 130°F. Add the Lavandula angustifolia essential oil and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify. Store the creams in a cool, dry place and use as needed for jaw pain relief.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains the bioactive constituents gingerols and shogaols, which are known to have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

These compounds help reduce pain and swelling in the jaw by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that promote inflammation. Gingerols and shogaols also have a warming effect, which can help relax tense muscles in the jaw and improve blood flow. The anti-inflammatory properties of Zingiber officinale creams can also help reduce inflammation in the temporomandibular joint (TMJ), a common cause of jaw pain.

By reducing pain and inflammation, Zingiber officinale creams can provide relief from jaw pain and promote overall jaw health.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 tablespoons of grated fresh Ginger (Zingiber officinale), 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 teaspoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the grated ginger to the melted mixture and stir well. Let it infuse for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the ginger solids.
  5. Mix in the shea butter and vitamin E oil. Pour the mixture into a tin or a jar. Let it cool and harden before use.

3. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium creams contains flavonoids, sesquiterpene lactones, and volatile oils as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties, which can help alleviate jaw pain. The sesquiterpene lactones in Achillea millefolium, such as achillin and alantolactone, have been shown to reduce inflammation and swelling, which can contribute to jaw pain. The anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving effects of Achillea millefolium creams make it a potential natural remedy for jaw pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Achillea millefolium leaves and flowers. Clean and dry a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine 1 cup of dried Achillea millefolium with 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil) in a glass jar. Let it sit for 2-3 weeks in a cool dark place, shaking the jar daily.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter to the infused oil. Heat the mixture in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each heating, until the beeswax and shea butter are melted.
  5. Pour the mixture into small glass containers or tin pots. Let it cool and harden before using the Achillea millefolium cream for jaw pain.

Turmeric contains another bioactive constituent, demethoxycurcumin, that has potent anti-inflammatory effects.

Consuming herbal teas for jaw pain daily can be safe, but it's essential to check the ingredients.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for jaw pain?

Drinking herbal tea for jaw pain can be most effective in the morning or early afternoon. At these times, your body's natural energy levels are typically higher, allowing the tea's soothing properties to better reach your jaw and provide relief from pain and tension.

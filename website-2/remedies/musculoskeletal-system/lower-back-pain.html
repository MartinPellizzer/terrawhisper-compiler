What Are The Main Causes Of Lower Back Pain?

Lower back pain is often caused by problems in the spine.

One common cause is a herniated disc, which occurs when a disc between the vertebrae bulges out and presses on nerves. This can happen due to wear and tear, injury, or even lifting something heavy. A herniated disc can cause pain, numbness, and tingling in the lower back and legs. Another cause is a degenerative disc, which happens when a disc between the vertebrae wears out due to aging. As we age, the discs lose their cushioning and can become brittle, leading to pain and stiffness in the lower back.

This is a common cause of lower back pain in older adults. Spondylolisthesis is another cause of lower back pain. It occurs when a vertebra slips out of place and puts pressure on the nerves. This can be caused by a birth defect, injury, or wear and tear over time. Spondylolisthesis can cause pain in the lower back and legs, and may even lead to numbness or tingling. Other factors, such as muscle strain, poor posture, and obesity, can also contribute to lower back pain.

However, these three conditions - herniated disc, degenerative disc, and spondylolisthesis - are some of the most common causes of lower back pain.

Below are the main causes of lower back pain.

  • Herniated disc: A herniated disc in the lower back causes pain because the soft inner gel of a spinal disc pushes out through a tear in its outer layer, putting pressure on nearby nerves and causing discomfort and pain in the lower back.
  • Degenerative disc: Degenerative disc disease is a cause of lower back pain because the cushioning discs between the vertebrae in the spine wear down and lose their ability to absorb shock, leading to strain on surrounding muscles and joints, which can cause pain and discomfort in the lower back.
  • Spondylolisthesis: Spondylolisthesis is a cause of lower back pain because the vertebrae in the spine are slipping out of place, putting pressure on the surrounding muscles and nerves, which leads to pain and discomfort in the lower back.
  • Scoliosis: Scoliosis causes lower back pain because the abnormal curvature of the spine puts uneven pressure on the muscles and nerves, leading to strain and discomfort in the lower back area.
  • Spinal stenosis: Spinal stenosis is a cause of lower back pain because the narrowing of the spinal canal puts pressure on the nerves, causing pain, numbness, and weakness in the back and legs.
  • Sacroiliac joint: The Sacroiliac joint is a common cause of lower back pain because when its ligaments are stretched or torn, or when the joint does not move smoothly, it can send pain signals to the surrounding muscles and nerves, leading to discomfort and stiffness in the lower back.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Lower Back Pain?

If you have lower back pain, it's best to avoid several herbs that may irritate the condition.

One such herb is Devil's Claw (Harpagophytum procumbens), as it can increase inflammation and worsen back pain. Willow Bark (Salix alba) contains salicin, a compound similar to aspirin, which can cause stomach problems and interact with medications.

Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) contains a chemical called berberine, which can slow blood clotting and lead to bleeding complications. Another herb to avoid is Sassafras (Sassafras albidum), as it can cause blood pressure to drop and worsen circulation problems. Finally, Ephedra (Ephedra sinica) can raise blood pressure and heart rate, which may exacerbate back pain and other health issues.

It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies, especially if you have a pre-existing medical condition.

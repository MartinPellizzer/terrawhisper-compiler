What Are The Main Causes Of Knee Pain?

Knee pain can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is osteoarthritis, a condition where the cartilage in the knee joint wears down. This cartilage acts as a cushion between the bones, and when it's gone, the bones rub against each other, causing pain. Osteoarthritis can be caused by age, weight, and repetitive strain on the knee. Another cause is rheumatoid arthritis, an autoimmune disease that causes the body to attack its own tissues. In the case of rheumatoid arthritis, the immune system attacks the lining of the knee joint, leading to inflammation and pain.

This condition can cause the knee joint to become swollen and stiff. A ligament sprain is another cause of knee pain. Ligaments are strong tissues that connect bones together. When a ligament in the knee is stretched or torn, it can cause pain and instability in the knee. Ligament sprains can happen when the knee is bent or twisted in an awkward position, or when it's hit directly.

The severity of the sprain can vary, from mild to severe.

Below are the main causes of knee pain.

  • Osteoarthritis: Osteoarthritis is a cause of knee pain because it causes the cartilage in the knee joint to wear away, leading to bone-on-bone contact and inflammation that results in pain, swelling, and stiffness in the knee.
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis: Rheumatoid Arthritis is a cause of knee pain because it causes the joints to become inflamed and damaged, leading to stiffness, swelling, and pain in the knee.
  • Ligament Sprain: A ligament sprain is a cause of knee pain because it occurs when the ligaments surrounding the knee joint are stretched or torn, leading to inflammation and pain in the affected area.
  • Tendinitis: Tendinitis in the knee is a common cause of knee pain because inflammation and irritation of the tendons, which are the strong cords that connect muscles to bones, cause pain, swelling, and stiffness in the knee area.
  • Meniscus Tear: A meniscus tear in the knee is a cause of knee pain because the tear irritates the soft cartilage, leading to inflammation and sharp pain when the knee bends or twists.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Knee Pain?

If you have knee pain, there are some herbs you should avoid.

One such herb is 'Ginkgo biloba', which can increase the risk of bleeding and bruising, making knee pain worse. 'Ephedra sinica' (also known as 'Ma Huang') is another herb to avoid, as it can cause joint pain and inflammation, exacerbating knee problems.

'Valeriana officinalis' (Valerian root) might seem like a calming herb, but it can slow down blood clotting and lead to increased bleeding, which can be problematic for people with knee pain. 'Comfrey' (Symphytum officinale), 'Turmeric-free' Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera), and 'Angelica' (Angelica archangelica) are also herbs to approach with caution. They can cause joint inflammation or interact with other medications, making knee pain worse.

It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies, especially if you have knee pain.

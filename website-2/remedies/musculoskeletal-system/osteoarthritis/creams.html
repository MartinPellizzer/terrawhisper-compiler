What herbal creams you should avoid if you have osteoarthritis?

If you have osteoarthritis, you should avoid using herbal creams that contain Comfrey (Symphytum officinale) and Willow Bark (Salix alba).

Comfrey can cause liver damage and slow down bone healing, which can worsen osteoarthritis symptoms. Willow Bark, on the other hand, contains salicylates, which are similar to aspirin. Taking high doses of salicylates can lead to stomach problems, bleeding, and kidney damage.

Ginkgo Biloba can also be problematic, as it can interact with blood-thinning medications and increase the risk of bleeding. Eucalyptus (Eucalyptus globulus) and Wintergreen (Gaultheria procumbens) contain methyl salicylate, a salicylate compound that can cause the same side effects as Willow Bark. These herbs can be irritating to the skin, especially for people with sensitive skin. They can also interact with other medications, including blood thinners, and worsen osteoarthritis symptoms.

If you're using herbal creams for osteoarthritis, it's essential to talk to your doctor or a healthcare professional before trying any new products.

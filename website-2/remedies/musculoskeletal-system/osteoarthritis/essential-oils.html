10 Herbal Essential Oils For Osteoarthritis

By using these herbal essential oils, people with osteoarthritis can experience improved mobility, reduced pain, and enhanced overall well-being. 1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a powerful compound called curcumin, which has potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Turmerone, another key constituent, helps to reduce joint inflammation and pain. The essential oil's analgesic and anti-arthritic properties come from the presence of bisdemethoxycurcumin and demethoxycurcumin, which inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Curcuma longa essential oils can help alleviate the symptoms of osteoarthritis, including joint pain and stiffness.

Regular use may help slow down the progression of the disease and improve joint health.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Curcuma longa roots, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Chop the Curcuma longa roots into small pieces and add them to the glass jar.
  3. Pour 1 cup of carrier oil over the chopped roots and close the jar.
  4. Leave the jar in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking it every day to help the oil extract from the roots.
  5. After 2 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, and discard the solids.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains gingerols and shogaols, bioactive constituents that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds work by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, such as COX-2 and LOX, which contribute to the pain and inflammation associated with osteoarthritis. The anti-inflammatory properties of gingerols and shogaols also help to reduce joint pain and swelling, making it easier to move and perform daily activities. Additionally, ginger has antioxidant properties that help to protect the joints from oxidative stress and damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Zingiber officinale essential oils may help to alleviate the symptoms of osteoarthritis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale roots and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Boil the water and add the Zingiber officinale roots. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a bowl, discarding the solids. Let it cool.
  4. Use a steam distillation apparatus or a simple distillation kit to extract the essential oil from the cooled mixture.
  5. Collect the extracted essential oil in a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dry place.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling and pain in the joints associated with osteoarthritis. Glycyrrhizin, a key component, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to joint damage. The flavonoids and phenolic acids in Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils also have antioxidant properties, which can help protect the joints from oxidative stress and damage.

This makes Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils a potential natural remedy for alleviating symptoms of osteoarthritis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat and bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and let simmer for 2 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean bowl, and discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba or coconut oil) to the liquid and mix well.
  5. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use it as needed for osteoarthritis relief.

4. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like eucalyptol and alpha-pinene.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling and pain in joints affected by osteoarthritis. The decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties of eucalyptol can also help improve blood circulation and reduce stiffness in the joints. Additionally, eucalyptus globulus essential oils has been shown to have analgesic properties, which can help alleviate pain associated with osteoarthritis.

By reducing inflammation, improving circulation, and alleviating pain, eucalyptus globulus essential oils may be used as a complementary therapy for managing osteoarthritis symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Eucalyptus leaves. Let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool for 5 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the Eucalyptus leaves.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 10 drops of the oil in a carrier oil for massage or inhalation.

5. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains flavonoids, bilobalide, and ginkgolides as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce joint inflammation and oxidative stress associated with osteoarthritis. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba essential oils have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, while bilobalide and ginkgolides have been found to have a protective effect on cartilage and bones. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of Ginkgo biloba essential oils help neutralize free radicals that can damage joint tissues and exacerbate osteoarthritis symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Ginkgo biloba essential oils may help alleviate osteoarthritis symptoms and improve joint health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Clean and dry the Ginkgo biloba leaves. Use a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle to crush the leaves.
  3. Combine 2 tablespoons of crushed Ginkgo biloba leaves with 1 cup of carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. This allows the oil to extract the Ginkgo biloba's properties.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids and store the Ginkgo biloba essential oil in a cool, dark place.

6. Capsicum annuum

Capsicum annuum essential oils contains capsaicin as its main bioactive constituent.

Capsaicin has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which help reduce pain and inflammation associated with osteoarthritis. The antioxidant properties of capsaicin also help protect joints from oxidative damage and promote healthy cartilage growth. Additionally, capsaicin can inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory compounds and enzymes that contribute to joint degeneration.

By reducing inflammation and promoting joint health, capsaicin in Capsicum annuum essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of osteoarthritis.

  1. Gather 200g of fresh Capsicum annuum fruits, 1 cup of carrier oil, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Wash the Capsicum annuum fruits and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Combine the Capsicum annuum fruits and carrier oil in the glass jar. Let it sit for 2 weeks in a dark place, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a carrier oil for massage to help relieve osteoarthritis pain.

7. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene and beta-pinene, which are powerful anti-inflammatory compounds.

These compounds help reduce swelling and pain in joints affected by osteoarthritis. The essential oil's analgesic and antispasmodic properties also ease muscle tension and spasms that can occur in people with osteoarthritis. Additionally, the oil's antioxidant properties help protect joints from oxidative stress and damage, which can contribute to the progression of the disease.

By reducing inflammation and promoting joint health, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils may be a useful complementary therapy for managing osteoarthritis symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Turn off the heat and let the mixture steep for 1 hour. This allows the leaves to release their oils.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, pressing on the solids to get all the liquid out.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a clean glass bottle and store it in the fridge. Use 5-7 drops per use for osteoarthritis relief.

8. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium essential oils contains compounds like sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and pain associated with osteoarthritis. The sesquiterpene lactones, specifically achilleine and silphiperfol, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. The anti-inflammatory properties of Achillea millefolium essential oils can also help reduce joint stiffness and improve mobility in individuals with osteoarthritis.

The flavonoids present in the essential oil, such as kaempferol and quercetin, have antioxidant properties that can further contribute to reducing inflammation and promoting healing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Achillea millefolium leaves and flowers. Clean and dry them.
  2. Use a steam distillation apparatus or a small pot to heat 2 cups of water. Bring the water to a boil.
  3. Add the dried Achillea millefolium to a distillation apparatus or a cheesecloth-lined bowl. Place the bowl over the pot with boiling water.
  4. Steam distill the Achillea millefolium for 2-3 hours, collecting the oil in a separate container.
  5. Strain the collected oil and store it in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Label the bottle with the date and contents.

9. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains a compound called cinnamaldehyde, which is responsible for its therapeutic properties.

Cinnamaldehyde has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it a potential treatment for osteoarthritis. The essential oil also contains eugenol and linalool, which have analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, helping to reduce pain and inflammation in the joints. The combination of these properties in Cinnamomum verum essential oils may help to reduce the symptoms of osteoarthritis, such as joint pain and stiffness.

Topical application of the essential oil may also help to reduce inflammation and promote healing in the joints.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Cinnamomum verum (Ceylon cinnamon) bark, 4 cups of carrier oil (coconut or sweet almond oil), and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Dry the Ceylon cinnamon bark in a low-temperature oven (150°F - 200°F) for 2 hours to remove excess moisture.
  3. Combine 2 cups of dried Ceylon cinnamon bark with 4 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the essential oils to infuse into the carrier oil.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the Cinnamomum verum essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

10. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains limonene, menthone, and menthol, which are its key bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in the joints. The menthol in peppermint oil also acts as a natural analgesic, blocking pain signals to the brain. Furthermore, the oil's antispasmodic properties help relax muscles and reduce stiffness, which is often associated with osteoarthritis.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing muscles, Mentha x piperita essential oils can provide relief from the symptoms of osteoarthritis.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Mentha x piperita (peppermint) leaves and flowers. Wash them with water to remove dirt.
  2. Use a juicer or blender to extract the oil from the leaves and flowers. Collect the extracted oil in a bowl.
  3. Add 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the extracted oil and mix well. Stir the mixture for 10 minutes.
  4. Leave the mixture for 2-3 days to allow the peppermint oil to infuse into the carrier oil. Shake the bowl daily.
  5. Filter the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter to separate the essential oil from the carrier oil. Collect the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent osteoarthritis from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent osteoarthritis from forming, but more research is needed to confirm this.

Some herbal teas, like ginger and turmeric, contain anti-inflammatory compounds that could slow cartilage breakdown. These compounds may also reduce joint pain and stiffness.

Drinking herbal tea regularly may have a positive effect on joint health.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for osteoarthritis every day?

Herbal teas for osteoarthritis can be a helpful addition to your daily routine.

However, it's essential to be mindful of the ingredients and their potential interactions. Some herbal teas, like ginger and turmeric, are generally considered safe for daily consumption.

But others, like willow bark, may contain salicylates that can be problematic for regular use.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in osteoarthritis?

Herbal teas may start to help with osteoarthritis symptoms within a few days to a week.

Some people notice less pain and stiffness after taking herbal teas regularly for a couple of weeks.

It can take a few months to see more noticeable improvements in joint mobility and overall comfort.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for osteoarthritis?

Drinking herbal tea for osteoarthritis can be most effective in the morning or early afternoon.

At these times, your body is most active, and the tea can help reduce inflammation and ease pain.

The active ingredients can also help regulate your joints and improve mobility throughout the day.

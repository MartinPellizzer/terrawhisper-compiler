What herbal teas you should avoid if you have osteoarthritis?

If you have osteoarthritis, there are some herbal teas you should avoid.

One example is tea made from goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis), which can slow down blood clotting and make bleeding more likely. If you're taking medications for osteoarthritis that also slow down blood clotting, combining them with goldenseal tea can increase the risk of bleeding. Another herb to avoid is tea made from comfrey (Symphytum officinale), which contains compounds that can cause liver damage and kidney problems.

Drinking comfrey tea regularly can lead to these issues, especially if you already have liver or kidney problems. Tea made from pennyroyal (Mentha pulegium) is also not recommended, as it can cause liver damage and interact with certain medications. You should also avoid tea made from sassafras (Sassafras albidum), which contains a compound that can thin the blood and increase the risk of bleeding when combined with certain medications.

Finally, tea made from wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) can cause stomach problems, including nausea and vomiting, which can be especially problematic for people with osteoarthritis who may already be experiencing joint pain and discomfort.

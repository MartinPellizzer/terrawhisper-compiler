What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have osteoarthritis?

If you have osteoarthritis, it's best to avoid herbal decoctions that may worsen joint inflammation or interact with medications you're taking.

One such herb is Devil's Claw (Harpagophytum procumbens), which can increase the risk of bleeding when taken with blood-thinning medications. Another herb to steer clear of is Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens), which may interfere with the effectiveness of pain medications, including NSAIDs.

Additionally, you should limit or avoid using Willow Bark (Salix alba) decoctions, as they contain salicin, a compound similar to aspirin. While willow bark may provide some pain relief, taking it with other pain medications can increase the risk of stomach bleeding and other complications.

It's also essential to be cautious when using Dandelion Root (Taraxacum officinale) decoctions, as they can act as a diuretic, leading to excessive urine production and potential dehydration, which can worsen osteoarthritis symptoms.

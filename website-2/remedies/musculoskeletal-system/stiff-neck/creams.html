1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling and pain in the neck muscles, making it a good option for treating stiff neck. The active constituents in gingerol, such as gingerols and shogaols, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which contribute to pain and inflammation. Additionally, Zingiber officinale creams contain other compounds like zingerone, which has been found to have analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

By applying Zingiber officinale creams to the affected area, the anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties of the active constituents can help to relieve pain and stiffness in the neck.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Zingiber officinale essential oil, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second intervals until smooth.
  3. Combine the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture with the distilled water in a bowl. Stir well.
  4. Add the Zingiber officinale essential oil and vitamin E oil to the mixture. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a jar or tin and let it cool and solidify before use. Apply a small amount to the affected area as needed.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and camphor as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which help to reduce pain and swelling in the neck. Linalool, in particular, has a relaxing effect on the muscles, making it easier to move the neck and reducing stiffness. Camphor, on the other hand, helps to increase blood flow to the affected area, promoting healing and reducing inflammation.

By reducing pain and inflammation, Lavandula angustifolia creams can provide relief from stiff neck and promote a faster recovery.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers, and 2 teaspoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Add dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and stir well. Let it infuse for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean container. Add vitamin E oil and mix well. Pour the cream into small containers and let it cool and set before use.

3. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, a key active constituent with anti-inflammatory properties that helps to reduce stiffness and pain in the neck.

Tannins and flavonoids present in the cream also have antioxidant effects, which can help to soothe and calm irritated tissues in the neck area. The antimicrobial properties of Salvia officinalis creams, attributed to carnosic acid and ursolic acid, can help to prevent infection and promote healing in the affected area. The anti-spasmodic properties of Salvia officinalis creams, particularly due to the presence of rosmarinic acid, can help to relax muscle spasms in the neck, reducing stiffness and discomfort.

By applying Salvia officinalis creams topically, you can directly target the affected area and potentially alleviate stiff neck symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Salvia officinalis flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Heat coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in Salvia officinalis flowers, shea butter, and vitamin E oil. Let it steep for 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once set, apply to stiff neck as needed.

Curcuma longa creams contains a high concentration of curcuminoids, including curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

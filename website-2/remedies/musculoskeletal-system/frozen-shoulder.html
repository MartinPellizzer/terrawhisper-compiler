What Are The Main Causes Of Frozen Shoulder?

Frozen shoulder, also known as adhesive capsulitis, is a condition where the shoulder joint becomes stiff and painful.

The main causes of frozen shoulder are not fully understood, but several factors can contribute to its development. One of the known causes is diabetes. People with diabetes are more likely to develop frozen shoulder due to the high levels of sugar in their blood, which can cause inflammation and damage to the tissues in the shoulder joint. Another cause of frozen shoulder is hypothyroidism, a condition where the thyroid gland does not produce enough hormones.

The thyroid hormones play a crucial role in maintaining healthy tissues, and a deficiency can lead to inflammation and damage to the shoulder joint. Additionally, people with hypothyroidism may also experience fatigue, which can make them less active and more prone to developing frozen shoulder. Lupus, an autoimmune disease, is also a known cause of frozen shoulder. In lupus, the body's immune system attacks the healthy tissues in the body, including the tissues in the shoulder joint. This can cause inflammation and damage to the joint, leading to frozen shoulder.

Other factors, such as injury, surgery, or prolonged immobilization, can also contribute to the development of frozen shoulder.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Frozen Shoulder?

If you have frozen shoulder, you should avoid certain herbs that can make the problem worse.

One herb to avoid is 'Ginkgo biloba'. Ginkgo biloba can make bleeding more likely, which is a concern if you have a frozen shoulder and are considering surgery. Another herb to avoid is 'Ephedra sinica'. Ephedra sinica can increase blood pressure and heart rate, which can be a problem if you have a frozen shoulder and are also taking medications to help with the condition.

You should also avoid 'Valeriana officinalis'. Valeriana officinalis can interact with some medications used to treat frozen shoulder, such as muscle relaxants. Avoid 'Piper methysticum' if you have frozen shoulder. Piper methysticum can make you feel drowsy, which can be a problem if you need to move your shoulder to do physical therapy exercises. Finally, avoid 'Aconitum napellus'.

Aconitum napellus can cause muscle weakness, which can make a frozen shoulder worse.

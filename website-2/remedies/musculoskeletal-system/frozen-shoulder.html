Frozen Shoulder: Causes, Symptoms, and Natural Remedies with Medicinal Herbs

What are the causes of frozen shoulder?

Frozen shoulder, also known as adhesive capsulitis, is a condition where the shoulder joint becomes stiff and hard to move.

There are several main causes of frozen shoulder. One cause is diabetes, which can lead to frozen shoulder due to the high levels of sugar in the blood damaging the tendons and ligaments in the shoulder joint. Another cause is a CVA, or stroke, where a lack of blood flow to the shoulder area can cause the joint to freeze.

Hyperthyroidism, a condition where the thyroid gland produces too much thyroid hormone, can also lead to frozen shoulder by causing inflammation and scarring in the shoulder joint. Surgery can be a cause of frozen shoulder as well. This is because the tissues in the shoulder joint can become inflamed and stiff after surgery, leading to the condition. Additionally, people with rheumatoid arthritis often develop frozen shoulder due to the chronic inflammation and scarring in the joint that occurs with this condition.

In each of these cases, the inflammation and scarring in the shoulder joint can lead to the characteristic stiffness and limited mobility of frozen shoulder.

What are the main medicinal herbs for frozen shoulder?

Herbs like Zingiber officinale (ginger), Curcuma longa (turmeric), Capsicum annuum (cayenne pepper), Glycyrrhiza glabra (licorice root), and Piper nigrum (black pepper) can help with frozen shoulder.

Zingiber officinale has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and ease pain in the affected joint. Curcuma longa, also known as turmeric, contains a powerful compound called curcumin, which has potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects that help to reduce pain and stiffness in the shoulder.

Capsicum annuum, or cayenne pepper, contains a chemical called capsaicin, which blocks the production of a chemical called substance P, a pain transmitter in the body, thereby reducing pain and discomfort. Glycyrrhiza glabra, or licorice root, is known for its anti-inflammatory properties and can help to soothe and calm the muscles in the shoulder, reducing pain and stiffness. Piper nigrum, or black pepper, contains a compound called piperine, which increases the absorption of other herbs and compounds in the body, making them more effective in reducing pain and inflammation.

These herbs can be consumed as teas, added to food, or applied topically as creams or oils to help alleviate symptoms of frozen shoulder.

What Are The Main Causes Of Arthritis?

Arthritis is a group of conditions that cause joint pain and stiffness.

The main causes of arthritis include Osteoarthritis (OA) in the hip and knee, and Rheumatoid Arthritis. Osteoarthritis (OA) in the hip and knee is caused by wear and tear on the joints. When we move, our joints rub against each other, causing the cartilage to break down over time. This leads to bone-on-bone contact, causing pain and stiffness.

OA is often caused by age, obesity, and repetitive strain on the joints. For example, people who do heavy lifting or repetitive bending may be more likely to develop OA in their hips or knees. Rheumatoid Arthritis is an autoimmune disease, meaning the body's immune system attacks the joints. This leads to inflammation and damage to the joints, causing pain, stiffness, and swelling. The exact cause of Rheumatoid Arthritis is not known, but it is thought to be triggered by a combination of genetic and environmental factors.

People with a family history of Rheumatoid Arthritis are more likely to develop the condition, and certain viruses and bacteria may also trigger the disease.

Below are the main causes of arthritis.

  • OA Hip: OA Hip, or osteoarthritis of the hip, is a cause of arthritis because the joint wears down over time, causing the bone and cartilage to rub together and leading to pain and inflammation, as the smooth surface of the cartilage breaks down, allowing bone to scrape against bone.
  • OA Knee: OA Knee, or osteoarthritis of the knee, is a cause of arthritis because the smooth cartilage that cushions the bones in the knee joint wears down over time, causing the bones to rub against each other and leading to pain and inflammation.
  • Rheumatoid: Rheumatoid arthritis is a cause of arthritis because the body's immune system mistakenly attacks and damages the joints' lining, leading to pain, swelling, and inflammation that can cause the joints to become stiff and painful over time.
  • Psoriatic: Psoriatic arthritis occurs because the same faulty immune system that causes the skin condition psoriasis also mistakenly attacks the joints in the body, leading to painful inflammation and damage.
  • Gouty: Gouty arthritis occurs when uric acid builds up in the joints, causing painful inflammation and damage to the joint tissues, leading to redness, swelling, and stiffness.
  • Ankylosing: In ankylosing spondylitis, a type of arthritis, the immune system mistakenly attacks the body's joints, leading to inflammation and the fusion of vertebrae, which causes stiffness and pain in the spine and other joints.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Arthritis?

If you have arthritis, some herbs may make your condition worse.

One herb to avoid is Ephedra (Ephedra sinica), which can increase inflammation and worsen joint pain. Another herb to be cautious with is Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa), as it may cause joint stiffness and swelling.

Ginkgo Biloba may not be suitable for people with arthritis, as it can increase the risk of bleeding, which could be problematic for those taking blood-thinning medications often prescribed for arthritis. Devil's Claw (Harpagophytum procumbens) can cause stomach upset and interact with medications like blood thinners and diabetes medications, which are commonly used to manage arthritis.

St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) can also cause problems, as it may interact with medications used to treat arthritis, such as pain relievers and steroids.

What Are The Main Causes Of Jammed Finger?

A jammed finger occurs when the joint or the bone at the tip of the finger gets hit or bent in an awkward way, causing pain and swelling.

There are several causes of a jammed finger. One common cause is a sports injury, such as when a person is playing a sport that involves catching or blocking, like football or basketball. This can cause the finger to get hit or bent in an awkward way, leading to a jammed finger.

Another cause is when a finger slips and gets caught between two objects, like when a person's finger gets caught between two doors or between a door and a wall. This can cause the finger to bend in an awkward way, leading to a jammed finger. Lastly, a finger can get jammed when it is bent too far or in an awkward way, like when a person tries to bend their finger back too far or when a person's finger gets caught in a tight space. This can cause the joint or the bone at the tip of the finger to get injured, leading to a jammed finger.

These causes can lead to pain, swelling, and difficulty moving the finger, which can be uncomfortable and may take time to heal.

Below are the main causes of jammed finger.

  • Sports Injury: Sports injury, such as a fall or collision, can cause a jammed finger by suddenly bending or twisting the finger in an unnatural position, leading to the ligaments and tendons being overstretched or torn.
  • Finger Slipping: Finger slipping occurs when the skin on your fingers slides over the object you are trying to grip, causing your finger to get stuck or jammed.
  • Finger Bending: Finger bending is a cause of a jammed finger because when you bend your finger too far, the joint between the bones can get compressed and the ligaments can stretch or even tear, causing the finger to get stuck.
  • Door Slam: When a door is slammed shut too quickly, the sudden movement can cause your fingers to get caught between the door and the frame, leading to a jammed finger.
  • Finger Poking: Finger Poking can cause a jammed finger because when you quickly poke or jab your finger, it may get bent or twisted in an unnatural position, causing the joints or tendons to lock or get stuck.
  • Finger Jamming Object: Finger Jamming Object is a common cause of a jammed finger because it can get stuck under the finger and cause the finger to bend or break, leading to severe pain and injury.

Jammed Finger Care: Causes, Medicinal Herbs, and Preparations

What are the causes of jammed finger?

A jammed finger is a common injury that occurs when one of the joints in the finger gets stuck or pinched.

There are several main causes of a jammed finger. One common cause is when a door slams shut too quickly, trapping your finger in the gap between the door and the frame. This can cause a sudden and forceful bending of the finger, leading to pain and stiffness. Another cause is when a drawer closes too fast, pinching your finger in the process.

This is similar to the door slamming cause, but with a different type of object. Using a vise to grip something can also cause a jammed finger, especially if your finger slips into the vice by mistake. Sports injuries, such as falling on an outstretched hand or colliding with another player, can also cause a jammed finger. Finally, workplace accidents, such as getting your finger caught in a machine or falling equipment, can cause a jammed finger.

In all of these cases, the finger gets pinched or bent in a way that it's not meant to, leading to pain and potentially more serious injuries.

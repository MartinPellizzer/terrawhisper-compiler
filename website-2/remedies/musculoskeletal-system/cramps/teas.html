10 herbal teas for cramp relief for athletes

Athletes often experience muscle cramps due to intense physical activity, which can cause their muscles to become tired and overworked.

When this happens, the muscles can contract abnormally, leading to severe pain and discomfort. For athletes, cramps can be a major setback, as they can cause them to miss training sessions, competitions, and even lead to long-term injuries. This can be frustrating and demotivating, not just for the athlete but also for their team and coaches. Cramps can also disrupt an athlete's sleep, eating habits, and daily routine, making it harder for them to recover and perform at their best.

As a result, athletes are constantly searching for effective ways to prevent and treat muscle cramps. One of the ways athletes are turning to alleviate cramps is by using herbal teas. These teas are made from natural ingredients that are known for their anti-inflammatory and calming properties. When consumed, they can help relax the muscles, reduce spasms, and ease pain. They also help to regulate the body's fluid balance, which can contribute to cramps.

By drinking herbal teas, athletes can reduce their risk of experiencing cramps and improve their overall performance.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, commonly known as ginger tea, has been used for centuries to help alleviate cramps and other menstrual symptoms.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, have anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties that work to ease cramp pain. Ginger also contains compounds like zingibain, which has a relaxing effect on the muscles, helping to reduce spasms and discomfort. When taking ginger tea to help with cramps, it's recommended to drink 1-2 cups per day, ideally before or during the onset of cramps.

To make the most of ginger tea, it's best to steep the ginger in hot water for 5-7 minutes to release its active compounds. Some people also find it helpful to combine ginger tea with other warming herbs like cinnamon or cloves to enhance its effects. As you take ginger tea, you may start to feel a reduction in cramp pain, as well as an improvement in digestion and a sense of calm.

You may also find that your cramps become less frequent or severe over time, although this may vary depending on individual circumstances.

2. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate menstrual cramps due to their unique properties and constituents.

The tea contains flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, which are potent anti-inflammatory agents that help to relax the uterine muscles and reduce pain. Additionally, Ginkgo biloba contains terpene lactones, like ginkgolides, which have a natural anti-spasmodic effect, helping to calm the crampy contractions. If you're experiencing menstrual cramps, consider taking Ginkgo biloba tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. You can also combine it with other herbal teas like chamomile or raspberry leaf to enhance its effects.

To get the most out of the tea, drink it 1-2 times a day, starting a few days before your period is expected to begin. Be sure to consult with a healthcare professional before using Ginkgo biloba tea, especially if you're taking any medications or have underlying medical conditions. As you start taking Ginkgo biloba tea, you may notice a reduction in the severity and frequency of your menstrual cramps. You may also experience improved mood and reduced bloating. Some users have reported a decrease in the need for pain medication, such as ibuprofen or acetaminophen.

However, it's essential to note that Ginkgo biloba tea may interact with certain medications or worsen conditions like high blood pressure or bleeding disorders.

3. Angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis teas, also known as dong quai, have been used for centuries to help with menstrual cramps.

This is because the tea contains compounds like ferulic acid and ligustilide, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help to relax the uterine muscles and reduce pain. The tea also contains flavonoids and volatile oils that act as a natural muscle relaxant, further reducing cramp severity.

To use angelica sinensis teas for cramps, start by steeping one teaspoon of dried dong quai root in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, ideally starting a few days before your period to help reduce cramp severity. Some people may experience relief within a few hours of drinking the tea, while others may need to drink it consistently for a few days to notice a difference.

If you're considering trying angelica sinensis teas for cramps, be sure to talk to your healthcare provider first, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or are taking any medications.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate menstrual cramps.

Steep one teaspoon of the tea in boiling water for five to seven minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it three to four times a day. You can also combine it with other herbal teas, such as chamomile or peppermint, to enhance its calming effects. To further alleviate cramps, consider taking a warm bath or practicing gentle stretching exercises after drinking the tea. When taken as directed, Glycyrrhiza glabra tea can help to reduce the frequency and severity of menstrual cramps. You may start to feel a sense of relief within 30 minutes to an hour after drinking the tea. Some people may experience a decrease in cramping pain, while others may notice improved sleep quality and reduced anxiety.

As with any herbal remedy, be sure to consult with a healthcare professional before using Glycyrrhiza glabra tea, especially if you're pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking medications.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent cramps from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent cramps.

Some teas, like ginger and peppermint, have anti-inflammatory properties that can ease muscle spasms. Others, like chamomile, can calm the body and reduce cramping.

These teas work by relaxing the muscles and reducing tension, which can help prevent cramps from forming.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for cramps every day?

Drinking herbal teas for cramps daily can be safe, but it depends on the tea.

Some teas, like chamomile and peppermint, are gentle and often used regularly. However, teas with strong herbs like ginger or pennywort can be too potent if consumed every day.

It's best to listen to your body and adjust your tea habits accordingly.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in cramps?

Herbal teas like ginger, chamomile, and raspberry leaf tea may help ease cramps.

It's hard to say exactly when you'll feel better, but some people notice a difference within a few hours. Others might feel relief after a day or two.

It's different for everyone, so be patient and see what works for you.

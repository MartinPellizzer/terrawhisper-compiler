7 Herbal Teas For Difficulty Chewing

If you're having trouble chewing, herbal teas can be a great help.

These teas work by soothing and calming the muscles in your mouth and throat, making it easier to eat and swallow. Some herbs that are commonly used to relieve difficulty chewing are Zingiber officinale, also known as ginger, Glycyrrhiza glabra, which is licorice root, and Echinacea purpurea, a popular herb for its immune-boosting properties. Ginger, for example, has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce pain and swelling in the mouth and throat. This makes it easier to chew and swallow food.

Licorice root, on the other hand, has a soothing effect on the digestive system and can help reduce inflammation in the mouth and throat. Echinacea purpurea, as mentioned, is great for boosting the immune system, which can help prevent infections that can make chewing and swallowing even harder. Drinking herbal teas can bring many benefits to your life. For one, they can help you enjoy your favorite foods again, without feeling like they're too difficult to chew. This can be especially helpful if you're dealing with a condition like temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorder, or if you're recovering from surgery or an injury that's affected your mouth and throat.

Drinking herbal teas can also help you relax and reduce stress, which can further ease the discomfort and difficulty of chewing.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains active constituents like gingerols and shogaols that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling in the jaw and surrounding areas, making it easier to chew food. The warming effect of ginger also increases blood flow to the affected area, promoting healing and relaxation of the muscles. Additionally, the analgesic properties of gingerols and shogaols help alleviate pain and discomfort associated with difficulty chewing.

Regular consumption of Zingiber officinale teas may help alleviate symptoms of temporomandibular joint disorder (TMJ) and other conditions that cause jaw pain and stiffness.

  1. Get 1 tablespoon of dried Zingiber officinale root.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a kettle or pot.
  3. Add the dried Zingiber officinale root to the boiling water.
  4. Reduce heat to low and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it warm, 2-3 times a day.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains active constituents like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which help with difficulty chewing.

Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling in the mouth and throat, making it easier to chew and swallow food. Flavonoids, on the other hand, have antioxidant properties that protect the mouth and throat from damage caused by free radicals. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of these constituents help to soothe and heal the mucous membranes, reducing discomfort and pain associated with difficulty chewing.

The soothing and anti-inflammatory effects of Glycyrrhiza glabra teas make it a helpful remedy for people experiencing difficulty chewing due to mouth and throat irritation.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root and 1 cup of water.
  2. Heat the water in a pot and bring it to a boil.
  3. Reduce the heat and add the dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root to the water.
  4. Steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the mixture into a cup.
  5. Drink the tea warm or at room temperature, up to 2-3 times a day as needed.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Alkylamides, particularly echinacoside, have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the mouth and throat, making it easier to chew and swallow. Glycosides, like caftaric acid, have antioxidant properties that protect the mucous membranes from damage and promote healing. Phenolic acids, such as chlorogenic acid, have antimicrobial properties that help prevent infections and promote a healthy oral environment.

By reducing inflammation, promoting healing, and preventing infections, Echinacea purpurea teas can help alleviate the difficulties associated with chewing.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers.
  2. Place the dried flowers in a cup of boiling water.
  3. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into another cup to remove the flowers.
  5. Drink the tea while it's warm, up to 3 times a day.

4. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa teas contains a powerful compound called curcumin, along with other bioactive constituents like demethoxycurcumin and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have potent anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling in the mouth and throat that makes chewing difficult. Curcumin also has antioxidant properties, which can protect the mouth and throat from damage caused by free radicals and promote healing. Additionally, curcuma longa teas has a natural anti-inflammatory compound called turmerone, which can help reduce pain and discomfort associated with difficulty chewing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, curcuma longa teas may help alleviate symptoms of difficulty chewing and improve oral health.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of boiling water, 1 teaspoon of dried Curcuma longa root, and a strainer or teabag.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried Curcuma longa root to a teacup or mug.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the root in the teacup.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-10 minutes, then strain into another cup using a strainer or remove the teabag.
  5. Drink the tea slowly and carefully, taking small sips as needed for difficulty chewing.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains a variety of bioactive constituents, including linalool and linalyl acetate.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties and help to reduce pain and discomfort associated with difficulty chewing. They also have a relaxing effect on the muscles, which can help to reduce tension and spasms in the jaw and face. Additionally, the essential oils present in Lavandula angustifolia teas have antimicrobial properties, which can help to reduce inflammation and promote healing in the mouth and throat.

By consuming Lavandula angustifolia teas, individuals experiencing difficulty chewing may find relief from symptoms and improved ability to eat and swallow comfortably.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  2. Heat 1 cup of water in a pot until it boils.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the boiling water.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and let it cool before serving.

6. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains rosmarinic acid, carnosic acid, and ursolic acid as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and pain in the mouth and jaw, making it easier to chew. The antioxidant properties of rosmarinic acid and carnosic acid also help protect the mouth and teeth from damage caused by free radicals, promoting a healthy oral environment. Rosmarinus officinalis teas has been shown to have a relaxing effect on the muscles, which can help relax the jaw and facial muscles, making chewing easier.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy oral environment, Rosmarinus officinalis teas can help alleviate difficulties with chewing.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Place the leaves in a cup or teapot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the leaves.
  4. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the leaves.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to 3 times a day.

  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the mint leaves in a cup or tea infuser.
  4. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the leaves.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to 3 times a day, as needed.

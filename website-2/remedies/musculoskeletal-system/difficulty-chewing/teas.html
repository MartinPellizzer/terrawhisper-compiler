7 Effective Herbal Teas for Elderly with Chewing Difficulties

As people get older, they may experience difficulty chewing, a problem that can make eating and enjoying their favorite foods a real struggle.

This is often due to changes in the mouth, such as tooth loss, gum disease, or a dry mouth. These issues can make it hard for elderly individuals to break down food, leading to frustration and disappointment when they can't enjoy the meals they love. Difficulty chewing can also lead to weight loss, social isolation, and a decrease in overall quality of life. It can be a challenge to find foods that are easy to chew, and even then, some people may still experience pain or discomfort while eating.

This can affect not just their physical health, but also their mental well-being and happiness. For elderly individuals dealing with difficulty chewing, it's essential to find alternatives that can help make eating more enjoyable. One option is to try herbal teas, which can be a gentle and soothing way to help with digestion and ease discomfort while eating. These teas work by calming the mouth and digestive system, reducing inflammation, and promoting a healthy balance of gut bacteria.

By drinking herbal teas, elderly individuals can find some relief from difficulty chewing, making mealtime a more positive experience.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, made from the root of the licorice plant, can be beneficial for people experiencing difficulty chewing.

This is because the tea contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins, which have anti-inflammatory properties that may help reduce swelling and pain in the jaw and mouth. The tea may also have a soothing effect on the mucous membranes, making it easier to swallow and chew. To try Glycyrrhiza glabra teas for difficulty chewing, you can start by drinking 1-2 cups of warm tea a day, ideally before meals. You can also add a teaspoon of licorice root powder to a warm drink, like tea or water, if you prefer.

Some studies suggest that taking Glycyrrhiza glabra extracts in moderation (about 200-400 milligrams per day) may help alleviate discomfort and pain associated with difficulty chewing. However, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before making any changes to your diet or supplement routine, especially if you have underlying health conditions or take medications. As you start drinking Glycyrrhiza glabra teas or taking licorice root powder, you may notice a reduction in inflammation and pain in the jaw and mouth, making it easier to chew and swallow food. You may also experience improved digestion and reduced discomfort after meals. However, it's crucial to note that individual results may vary, and you should not rely solely on Glycyrrhiza glabra teas for managing difficulty chewing.

A healthcare professional may recommend other treatments or therapies in combination with the tea to address the underlying causes of your symptoms.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, can help with difficulty chewing by soothing inflammation and reducing pain in the mouth and jaw.

The tea contains properties like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. These compounds can help to reduce swelling and ease pain in the temporomandibular joint (TMJ) and surrounding muscles, making it easier to chew and swallow. If you're experiencing difficulty chewing, try making a ginger tea by steeping fresh ginger in hot water or using ginger tea bags. You can add honey or lemon to taste.

To get the most out of the tea, drink it three to four times a day, ideally after meals. It's also a good idea to chew slowly and take small bites to reduce strain on your jaw and teeth. As you drink the ginger tea, you may start to feel a reduction in pain and discomfort in your mouth and jaw. Your ability to chew and swallow may also improve, making it easier to eat and digest your food. Some people may also experience a decrease in inflammation and redness in the affected area.

However, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional if your difficulty chewing persists or worsens over time.

3. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis teas are made from the bark of the Cinchona tree, a plant native to the Andes mountains.

The teas are rich in quinine, a natural compound that helps reduce inflammation and relax the muscles in the jaw. Difficulty chewing, also known as temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorder, often occurs when the jaw muscles become tense and inflamed. The quinine in Cinchona officinalis teas has anti-inflammatory properties that can help ease this tension and reduce pain. This can make it easier to chew and move the jaw. To try Cinchona officinalis teas for difficulty chewing, start by brewing a cup according to the package instructions.

Some people find it helpful to drink a cup of tea after meals or before bedtime, as the quinine can help relax the jaw muscles while you're resting. It's also a good idea to practice gentle jaw exercises, such as slowly opening and closing your mouth, to help loosen up any stiff muscles. As you take action and drink Cinchona officinalis teas, you may start to notice improvements in your ability to chew and move your jaw. Your jaw may feel less tense and painful, and you may be able to eat and speak more comfortably. Some people also find that their sleep improves, as the quinine can help relax the muscles and promote a good night's rest.

Be patient and consistent with your tea drinking and jaw exercises, as it may take some time to notice the full benefits.

4. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa teas, made from the root of the turmeric plant, may help with difficulty chewing due to its anti-inflammatory properties and presence of curcuminoids, such as curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have been shown to reduce inflammation and improve digestive health, which can contribute to improved chewing and swallowing. When you have difficulty chewing, try making Curcuma longa tea by steeping one teaspoon of turmeric powder in a cup of hot water for 5-10 minutes.

Strain the liquid and drink it three to four times a day. As you take the tea, you may notice a reduction in inflammation and pain in your mouth and throat, making it easier to chew and swallow your food.

This can lead to improved nutrition and a reduced risk of dehydration and malnutrition.

5. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas are a natural remedy that can help alleviate difficulty chewing.

This herb contains compounds like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These properties can help soothe mouth and throat irritation, reducing pain and discomfort that may be making it hard to chew. When you drink Echinacea purpurea tea, the anti-inflammatory compounds may help reduce swelling in the mouth and throat, making it easier to open your mouth and chew your food. To try Echinacea purpurea tea for difficulty chewing, you can steep one teaspoon of dried Echinacea purpurea root in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day. You can also add honey or lemon to taste. Some people find it helpful to drink the tea before meals or when they experience discomfort while eating. As you take Echinacea purpurea tea regularly, you may notice a reduction in mouth and throat pain, making it easier to chew and swallow your food.

You may also find that you're able to eat a wider variety of foods and enjoy meals more comfortably.

6. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum teas, also known as milk thistle tea, may help alleviate difficulty chewing due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The tea contains silymarin, a flavonoid complex that helps protect the mouth and throat from inflammation and damage. Silymarin also has antimicrobial properties, which can help combat infections that may be contributing to chewing difficulties. Additionally, silymarin may help stimulate the production of saliva, which can aid in the breakdown of food. If you're experiencing difficulty chewing, try drinking 1-2 cups of Silybum marianum tea per day.

You can steep 1 teaspoon of dried Silybum marianum flowers in boiling water for 5-10 minutes to make a cup of tea. Be sure to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new tea regimen, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions. As you drink the tea, you may notice a reduction in pain and inflammation in the mouth and throat. Your saliva production may increase, making it easier to chew and digest food.

You may also experience improved digestion and reduced symptoms of conditions such as dry mouth, gum disease, and mouth sores.

7. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas have been found to help with difficulty chewing due to their ability to improve blood flow to the mouth and jaw area.

The leaves of the Ginkgo biloba tree contain flavonoids, terpenoids, and bilobalide, which are compounds that help to relax blood vessels and improve circulation. This increased blood flow helps to reduce inflammation and alleviate pain in the jaw and facial muscles, making it easier to chew and swallow food. If you're experiencing difficulty chewing, consider trying Ginkgo biloba tea to see if it helps. You can start by drinking one cup of tea per day and gradually increasing the amount as needed.

To make the tea, steep one teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and add honey or lemon to taste. Be sure to talk to your doctor before taking Ginkgo biloba tea, especially if you're already taking any medications or have any underlying health conditions. Taking Ginkgo biloba tea may lead to improved oral function and reduced pain in the jaw and facial muscles. You may find it easier to chew and swallow food, and may also experience relief from conditions such as temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorder.

However, it's essential to note that Ginkgo biloba tea is not a substitute for medical treatment, and you should continue to follow any treatment plans recommended by your healthcare provider.

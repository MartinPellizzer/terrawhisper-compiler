Finding Chewing Comfort with 7 Essential Oils for Muscular Dystrophy

People with muscular dystrophy often have trouble chewing their food because their muscles are weak and don't work properly.

This can make eating a frustrating and tiring experience. Chewing is a complex process that requires coordination between different muscles in the face, tongue, and jaw. When these muscles are weak, it can lead to difficulty swallowing, which can increase the risk of choking and dehydration. This can affect a person's ability to eat their favorite foods, enjoy mealtimes with family and friends, and even maintain a healthy weight.

In severe cases, difficulty chewing can lead to malnutrition and other health problems. Herbal essential oils, which are derived from plants, have been studied for their potential benefits in managing muscle weakness and pain. Some of these oils contain anti-inflammatory compounds that may help reduce muscle spasms and pain, making it easier to chew and swallow food. These oils can be inhaled or applied topically, and may be used in combination with other therapies to help manage symptoms of muscular dystrophy.

By reducing muscle tension and pain, herbal essential oils may help improve a person's ability to chew and swallow, making it easier to enjoy meals and maintain a healthy lifestyle.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils, also known as ginger essential oil, is a natural remedy for difficulty chewing due to its anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

The oil contains constituents like gingerol and shogaol, which have been shown to reduce pain and inflammation in the muscles and joints, making it easier to chew and swallow. Ginger essential oil also stimulates digestion and improves gut health, which can help alleviate issues like nausea and bloating that can make chewing difficult. To use ginger essential oil for difficulty chewing, add a few drops to a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and massage it into the affected area.

You can also inhale the oil by adding a few drops to a diffuser or by applying it to your temples. When you take action, you may experience a reduction in pain and inflammation, making it easier to chew and swallow. The oil's digestive properties may also help alleviate nausea and bloating, allowing you to eat and digest food more comfortably. You may find that your appetite returns, and you're able to enjoy your favorite foods again.

Additionally, the oil's anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce muscle tension and stiffness, making it easier to move your jaw and perform daily activities.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils, also known as licorice root oil, may help alleviate difficulty chewing due to its anti-inflammatory properties and specific constituents like glycyrrhizin and linalool.

These compounds can reduce swelling and ease pain in the mouth and jaw, making it easier to chew food. When using Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils for difficulty chewing, it's essential to dilute it with a carrier oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil, and apply it topically to the affected area.

For best results, mix a few drops of the essential oil with a carrier oil and gently massage it onto your jaw, cheeks, and gums. This may help relax the muscles, reduce inflammation, and ease discomfort, making it easier to chew food. After using the oil, you may notice a reduction in pain and swelling, making it more comfortable to eat and chew.

However, if the difficulty chewing persists or worsens, it's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional for proper evaluation and treatment.

3. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils, also known as Ceylon cinnamon oil, may help with difficulty chewing due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The oil contains compounds like cinnamaldehyde, cinnamyl acetate, and eugenol, which have been shown to reduce pain and inflammation in the mouth and jaw. When you experience difficulty chewing, try applying a few drops of Ceylon cinnamon oil to your temples, jawline, or gums. You can also add a few drops to a warm bath or inhale it through steam inhalation to help relax your muscles and reduce discomfort.

As the oil takes effect, you may notice a decrease in pain and inflammation, making it easier to chew and swallow your food.

7 Herbal Essential Oils For Difficulty Chewing

Herbal essential oils, particularly those derived from Zingiber officinale (ginger), Curcuma longa (turmeric), and Eucalyptus globulus, can be a helpful remedy for people experiencing difficulty chewing.

The warm and soothing properties of ginger, in particular, can help relax the muscles in the jaw, while turmeric's potent compound curcumin can reduce inflammation and ease pain.

Eucalyptus oil, on the other hand, has a decongestant effect that can help clear out mucus and reduce swelling in the mouth and throat. Drinking herbal teas made with these herbs can provide relief from difficulty chewing, allowing you to enjoy your favorite foods and drinks without discomfort.

By incorporating these teas into your daily routine, you can experience a reduction in pain and inflammation, making it easier to eat and swallow.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains gingerol and shogaol, two bioactive constituents that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and pain in the jaw and face, making it easier to chew food. The warming and soothing effects of gingerol and shogaol also help relax tense muscles in the jaw, which can be a major contributor to difficulty chewing. Additionally, the oils' antispasmodic properties help calm down muscle spasms in the jaw and face, further reducing discomfort and making chewing easier.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing tense muscles, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help alleviate difficulty chewing.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale roots (ginger roots) and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Wash the ginger roots with water, and then peel them using a vegetable peeler.
  3. Grate 1 cup of the peeled ginger roots using a grater or food processor.
  4. Combine the grated ginger with 1 cup of carrier oil in a clean glass bottle, and let it steep for 2 weeks in a cool, dark place.
  5. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass bottle, and discard the solids.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains bioactive constituents like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce pain and swelling in the mouth and jaw. The anti-inflammatory properties of curcuminoids also help to relax the muscles involved in chewing, making it easier to eat and swallow. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of Curcuma longa essential oils help to protect the mouth and throat from damage caused by free radicals, which can exacerbate difficulty chewing.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the mouth and throat, Curcuma longa essential oils can help to alleviate symptoms of difficulty chewing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Turmeric roots (Curcuma longa). Wash them clean with water.
  2. Dry the Turmeric roots in a cool, dark place for 2-3 days. This helps remove excess moisture.
  3. Grind the dried Turmeric roots into fine powder using a food processor or mortar and pestle.
  4. Use a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil. Mix 1 tablespoon of the Turmeric powder with 1 cup of the carrier oil in a clean glass bottle.
  5. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the bottle every day to help the oil extract the Turmeric's properties.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains a compound called cineole, which is responsible for its benefits.

Cineole has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help reduce swelling and ease pain in the mouth and jaw. The oil also contains eucalyptol, a compound that acts as a natural relaxant, helping to calm tense muscles and ease difficulty chewing. The menthol content in Eucalyptus globulus essential oils helps to numb the mouth and throat, reducing discomfort and pain associated with difficulty chewing.

By using Eucalyptus globulus essential oils, you may be able to alleviate some of the symptoms associated with this problem, and find relief from the pain and discomfort.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a cup of water, and a saucepan.
  2. Chop the leaves into small pieces and add them to the saucepan with the water.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 10-15 minutes, then let it cool.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 2-3 hours to allow the essential oil to separate and rise to the surface, then scoop it off and store it in a dark glass bottle.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains compounds like bornyl acetate and camphor, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and ease pain, making it easier to chew. The oils' carnosic acid content has antioxidant properties, which can protect the mouth from damage and promote healing. Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils' antiseptic properties can also help prevent infections, which can make eating and chewing more difficult.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils may be used to help alleviate the symptoms of difficulty chewing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a pot.
  2. Heat the water to 100°F (38°C) and add the leaves. Let it steep for 2 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a glass container and let it sit at room temperature for 2 days.
  5. Use a distiller or a still to separate the essential oil from the water. Collect the oil and store it in a dark glass bottle.

5. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains rosmarinic acid, caffeic acid, and alkylamides as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce swelling and pain in the mouth and jaw. The anti-inflammatory properties of rosmarinic and caffeic acid can ease inflammation in the temporomandibular joint, a common cause of difficulty chewing. Alkylamides have a relaxing effect on the muscles, which can help ease tension in the jaw and surrounding areas, making it easier to chew.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing muscles, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help alleviate the symptoms of difficulty chewing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and add the flowers. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a still or a distillation apparatus to separate the essential oil from the water.
  5. Collect the Echinacea purpurea essential oil and store it in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

6. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains a compound called anethole, which is responsible for its digestive benefits.

Anethole has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help to relax the muscles in the mouth and throat, making it easier to chew and swallow. The oil also contains other active constituents like limonene and beta-pinene, which have a calming effect on the digestive system and help to reduce inflammation in the mouth and throat. By reducing inflammation and relaxing the muscles, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help to alleviate the discomfort and difficulty associated with difficulty chewing.

The oil's ability to soothe and calm the digestive system makes it a potential natural remedy for those struggling with this issue.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Heat 1 cup of carrier oil in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds to the heated oil and let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the oil mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the seeds.
  5. Store the Foeniculum vulgare essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 2-3 drops as needed to ease difficulty chewing.

7. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are responsible for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce stress and anxiety, which are common causes of difficulty chewing. Linalool, in particular, has been shown to relax the muscles in the jaw, making it easier to chew and swallow. The oil's anti-inflammatory properties also help to reduce swelling and pain in the jaw and face, further alleviating difficulty chewing.

By reducing stress and inflammation, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to improve chewing and swallowing function.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Steep the mixture for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

9 Natural Creams for Denture Wearers to Overcome Chewing Problems

Denture wearers can face a lot of trouble when it comes to eating.

Their dentures can sometimes feel uncomfortable or loose, causing pain and discomfort while chewing food. This can be especially frustrating when trying to eat their favorite meals or enjoy a juicy piece of meat. The struggle to chew can also lead to difficulties swallowing, and in some cases, denture wearers may experience digestive problems. This can affect their daily lives, making mealtime a stressful and unpleasant experience. They might feel like they're missing out on the foods they love, or that they're constantly worrying about their dentures causing problems. To make matters worse, some denture wearers may have underlying oral health issues, such as dry mouth or gum recession, which can further exacerbate the problem.

They might also experience sore spots or ulcers in their mouth, making it even harder to chew. In some cases, the discomfort can be so severe that denture wearers may avoid eating altogether, leading to weight loss, fatigue, and other health complications. But there is hope for denture wearers who struggle with difficulty chewing. Herbal creams have been shown to provide relief from discomfort and pain, allowing denture wearers to enjoy their meals with ease. These creams work by soothing and calming the mouth and gums, reducing inflammation and promoting healing. They can also help to increase saliva production, which can help to lubricate the dentures and make chewing easier.

By using these creams, denture wearers can enjoy a more comfortable and enjoyable eating experience, and regain their confidence in their ability to eat and socialize.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams are known to help with difficulty chewing due to their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

The cream contains constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A, C, and E, which can help to reduce swelling and discomfort in the mouth and jaw. When difficulty chewing is caused by conditions like temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorder or mouth ulcers, Aloe barbadensis creams can help to alleviate these symptoms. To use Aloe barbadensis creams for difficulty chewing, gently apply a small amount to the affected area with a cotton swab.

You can also mix the cream with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it to the area with a gentle massaging motion. It's recommended to use the cream 2-3 times a day, or as needed, for best results. Applying Aloe barbadensis creams regularly may lead to a significant reduction in pain and swelling, making it easier to chew and swallow food. You may also notice an improvement in your overall oral health, with fewer instances of mouth ulcers and other irritations.

In some cases, the creams may also help to reduce the frequency and severity of TMJ disorder symptoms, allowing you to eat and speak more comfortably.

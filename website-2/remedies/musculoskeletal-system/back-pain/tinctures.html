1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa tinctures contains curcumin, a potent bioactive compound that gives it anti-inflammatory properties.

Curcumin blocks the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause pain and inflammation in the body. By reducing prostaglandin production, curcumin tinctures help alleviate back pain caused by inflammation. Curcumin also has antioxidant properties that help repair damaged cells and reduce oxidative stress, which can contribute to back pain.

By combining these anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, curcuma longa tinctures can provide relief from back pain by reducing inflammation and promoting cellular health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa roots and 2 cups of 80% ethanol or vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the dried Curcuma longa roots and ethanol or vodka in the glass jar. Make sure the roots are covered completely.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the Curcuma longa to infuse into the ethanol or vodka.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and reserve the liquid.
  5. Transfer the liquid tincture to a dropper bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Take 15-30 drops, 2-3 times a day as needed, mixed with water or under the tongue.

The anti-inflammatory properties of valepotriates can help reduce swelling and inflammation in the back, which can contribute to pain.

By reducing muscle tension and inflammation, Valeriana officinalis tinctures may help alleviate back pain.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

5. Capsicum annuum

Capsicum annuum tinctures contains capsaicin, which is the main bioactive constituent responsible for its pain-relieving effects.

Capsaicin blocks the production of a chemical called substance P, which transmits pain signals to the brain. It also inhibits the release of inflammatory mediators, such as prostaglandins and bradykinin, which contribute to pain and inflammation. By reducing inflammation and blocking pain signals, capsaicin helps to alleviate back pain and stiffness.

Regular use of Capsicum annuum tinctures may also increase blood circulation to the affected area, promoting healing and reducing discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Capsicum annuum (cayenne pepper) and 1 cup of 80% ethanol (vodka or grain alcohol). Wear gloves to handle the pepper.
  2. Combine the dried Capsicum annuum and ethanol in a clean glass jar. Seal the jar and shake it well for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day to help the mixture mix.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture in water or tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve back pain.

Can drinking herbal tea prevent back pain from forming?

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for back pain every day?

While herbal teas for back pain can be helpful, consuming them daily might not be suitable for everyone.

Some teas contain caffeine, which can be a problem if you're sensitive to it. Others may interact with medications or worsen conditions like high blood pressure.

Be cautious and consider your body's response.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in back pain?

Herbal teas can provide relief from back pain, but the time it takes to feel better varies. Some people may notice a difference within a few days, while others may not feel the effects until a week or two after starting to drink the tea regularly.

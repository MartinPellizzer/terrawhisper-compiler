What herbal creams you should avoid if you have muscle pain?

If you have muscle pain, there are some herbal creams you should avoid.

One of these is St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) cream, which may interfere with certain medications, such as blood thinners and antidepressants. Another is Valerian root (Valeriana officinalis) cream, which can cause drowsiness and interact with sedatives. Comfrey (Symphytum officinale) cream may be toxic if ingested or applied to broken skin.

Sassafras (Sassafras albidum) cream contains safrole, which is a known carcinogen. Also, Pennyroyal (Mentha pulegium) cream contains pulegone, which can cause liver damage and interact with certain medications. Finally, Saw palmetto (Serenoa repens) cream may increase the risk of bleeding when taken with certain medications, such as blood thinners.

It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal cream, especially if you're taking medications or have underlying health conditions.

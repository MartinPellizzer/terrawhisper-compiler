1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a potent compound called curcumin, which is responsible for its therapeutic benefits.

This essential oil is rich in bioactive constituents, including curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These properties help to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the body, which are key factors in the development of ankylosing spondylitis. Curcumin has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines, which can exacerbate the condition.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Curcuma longa essential oils may help to alleviate symptoms of ankylosing spondylitis, such as pain and stiffness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Turmeric roots (Curcuma longa) and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Grind the Turmeric roots into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Fill a clean glass jar with 1/2 cup of the ground Turmeric powder and cover it with 1 cup of carrier oil.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the essential oil to infuse into the carrier oil.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean container, discard the solids, and store the Curcuma longa essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties can help reduce inflammation and pain in the joints associated with ankylosing spondylitis. Gingerols also have antioxidant properties, which can help protect against cell damage and promote healing. Shogaols, on the other hand, have analgesic properties, which can help alleviate pain and discomfort.

By reducing inflammation, promoting healing, and alleviating pain, Zingiber officinale essential oils may provide relief from the symptoms of ankylosing spondylitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale roots (about 4-6 pieces) and 2 cups of water.
  2. Wash the roots in cold water, then chop them into small pieces and set aside.
  3. Combine the chopped roots and water in a saucepan, bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container, discarding the roots.
  5. Let the liquid sit at room temperature for 2-3 days to allow the oil to separate and rise to the top. Skim off the oil with a spoon and transfer it to a dark glass bottle.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole, a key bioactive constituent with anti-inflammatory properties.

Cineole works by reducing inflammation and pain in the body, making it beneficial for conditions like ankylosing spondylitis. The oil's antispasmodic properties also help to relax muscles and reduce stiffness, which is often a symptom of ankylosing spondylitis. Eucalyptus globulus essential oils also contains limonene, a compound that has been shown to inhibit the production of inflammatory chemicals in the body.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing muscles, eucalyptus globulus essential oils may help to alleviate some of the symptoms of ankylosing spondylitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat and add the Eucalyptus leaves.
  3. Let it simmer for 2-3 hours, then turn off the heat and let it cool.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass bottle.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a cool, dark place.

4. Boswellia serrata

Boswellia serrata essential oils contains boswellic acids, specifically acuminatin, 11-keto-beta-boswellic acid, and 3-acetyl-alpha-boswellic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties.

These properties help reduce inflammation and pain in the joints associated with ankylosing spondylitis. Boswellic acids inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, such as 5-lipoxygenase, which contribute to the disease's progression. Additionally, boswellic acids have been shown to modulate the immune system's response, reducing the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines that exacerbate the condition.

By reducing inflammation and modulating the immune response, Boswellia serrata essential oils may provide relief from the symptoms of ankylosing spondylitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Boswellia serrata resin, a clean glass jar, and a cheesecloth.
  2. Combine the resin and 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or coconut oil) in the glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily, to allow the resin to infuse into the oil.
  4. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Filter the oil through a coffee filter or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass bottle. Your Boswellia serrata essential oil is now ready for use.

5. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, geranial, and neral as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce joint pain and swelling associated with ankylosing spondylitis. The analgesic properties of geranial and neral can also help alleviate chronic pain, a common symptom of the condition. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of limonene can help protect the joints from oxidative stress, which can contribute to inflammation and damage.

By reducing inflammation and pain, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils may help improve mobility and quality of life for individuals with ankylosing spondylitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cymbopogon citratus leaves and flowers. Make sure they are fresh and organic.
  2. Combine 1 cup of the leaves and flowers with 2 cups of water in a saucepan. Bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer for 2-3 hours. This helps release the oil from the plants.
  4. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container. Discard the solids.
  5. Use a distillation kit or a still to distill the liquid and separate the essential oil from the water. Store the oil in a dark glass bottle.

6. Withania somnifera

Withania somnifera essential oils contains bioactive constituents like withanolides, alkaloids, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress associated with ankylosing spondylitis. The essential oils' immunomodulatory properties can also help regulate the immune system's response to inflammation, which can contribute to the disease's progression. Withanolides, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which are molecules that promote inflammation.

The combination of these properties makes Withania somnifera essential oils a potential natural remedy for managing ankylosing spondylitis symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Withania somnifera roots and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Grind the Withania somnifera roots into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Mix 2 tablespoons of the ground Withania somnifera powder with 1 cup of carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  4. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day, to allow the oil to infuse.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a new glass jar, and discard the solids. Store the Withania somnifera essential oil in a cool, dark place.

7. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains bioactive constituents like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and pain in the joints associated with ankylosing spondylitis. Glycyrrhizin also has antioxidant properties, which can help protect the body from oxidative stress caused by inflammation. Flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, further reducing inflammation.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of ankylosing spondylitis.

  1. Gather 250g of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots. Cut them into small pieces.
  2. Add 500ml of carrier oil (such as coconut or olive oil) to a clean glass jar. Add the Glycyrrhiza glabra pieces.
  3. Heat the mixture in a double boiler or a water bath at 40°C for 2-3 hours. Stir occasionally.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the essential oil in dark glass bottles and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops as needed.

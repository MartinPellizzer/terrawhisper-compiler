7 herbal essential oils to support smokers with ankylosing spondylitis

Smoking is known to increase the risk of developing ankylosing spondylitis, a chronic inflammatory disease that affects the spine.

This condition can cause severe back pain, stiffness, and limited mobility, making everyday activities challenging for smokers. As the disease progresses, the spine can fuse together, leading to a loss of flexibility and independence. Smokers with ankylosing spondylitis may experience chronic fatigue, depression, and anxiety, further affecting their quality of life. Their social and professional lives can be impacted as they struggle to perform simple tasks, like getting dressed or walking short distances.

This can lead to feelings of isolation and low self-esteem. The symptoms of ankylosing spondylitis can be debilitating, and conventional treatments may only provide temporary relief. In this context, some people have turned to herbal essential oils as a complementary therapy. These oils are derived from plants and contain natural compounds that can help reduce inflammation and alleviate pain. They can be inhaled, applied topically, or added to bathwater, providing a gentle and non-invasive approach to managing symptoms.

By incorporating herbal essential oils into their routine, smokers with ankylosing spondylitis may find some relief from their symptoms and improve their overall well-being.

1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils, extracted from the turmeric plant, have been studied for their potential benefits in managing ankylosing spondylitis, a type of arthritis that affects the spine.

The active compounds in turmeric, including curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin, have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds work by reducing the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and blocking the activity of molecules that promote inflammation, which are often associated with ankylosing spondylitis. To use Curcuma longa essential oils for ankylosing spondylitis, mix a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil, and apply it topically to the affected areas, or add it to a warm bath for relaxation and pain relief.

You can also inhale the oil through steam inhalation or use a diffuser to release its therapeutic properties into the air. Start with a small amount and gradually increase the dose as needed. When using Curcuma longa essential oils for ankylosing spondylitis, you may experience a reduction in pain and inflammation, improved mobility and flexibility, and enhanced overall well-being. Some people may also notice a decrease in the frequency and severity of flares, and improved quality of life.

However, it's essential to note that individual results may vary, and it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially if you're already taking medications or have any underlying health conditions.

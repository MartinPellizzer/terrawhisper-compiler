What herbal teas you should avoid if you have plantar fasciitis?

If you have plantar fasciitis, there are some herbal teas you should avoid.

These teas contain ingredients that can irritate or inflame the affected area, making your condition worse. One example is teas made from the roots of the Angelica archangelica plant, which contains a compound that can cause blood vessels to dilate and increase blood flow. This can lead to increased swelling and pain in the foot. Another tea to avoid is one made from the leaves of the Sassafras albidum plant, which contains a chemical called safrole. Safrole has been shown to have anti-inflammatory properties, but it can also cause the body to release chemicals that make the blood vessels more sensitive. This can worsen the pain and inflammation associated with plantar fasciitis.

Teas made from the roots of the Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) plant also contain a compound called berberine, which can inhibit the production of prostaglandins. Prostaglandins are hormone-like substances that cause pain and inflammation, so reducing their production can be beneficial for people with plantar fasciitis. However, Goldenseal can also cause the blood vessels to constrict, which can reduce blood flow to the affected area and make the condition worse. Lastly, teas made from the roots of the Barberry (Berberis vulgaris) plant contain berberine as well. Like Goldenseal, it can inhibit the production of prostaglandins, but it can also cause the blood vessels to constrict, which can worsen the condition. It's worth noting that while these teas may not be suitable for people with plantar fasciitis, other herbal teas may be beneficial in reducing inflammation and pain.

It's always a good idea to consult with a healthcare professional before trying any new herbal remedies.

What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have plantar fasciitis?

If you have plantar fasciitis, some herbal decoctions you should avoid include those made from comfrey (Symphytum officinale) and valerian (Valeriana officinalis).

Comfrey decoctions can be too rich in mucilage, a thick, soothing substance, which may irritate the inflammation in your plantar fascia, making it worse. Valerian decoctions can have a sedative effect, but they can also cause drowsiness, which can lead to poor circulation and put more strain on your plantar fascia, making the pain worse. Additionally, decoctions made from lobelia (Lobelia inflata) should be avoided.

Lobelia has a stimulating effect, but it can also cause blood vessels to constrict, reducing blood flow to the affected area. This can worsen the condition and prolong the healing process. You should also be cautious with decoctions made from sassafras (Sassafras albidum) and sarsaparilla (Smilax ornata). Sassafras can cause blood thinning and worsen bleeding, which can be a problem if you have plantar fasciitis and are experiencing bleeding or bruising in your feet.

Sarsaparilla can cause blood vessels to constrict, similar to lobelia, which can reduce blood flow to the affected area and prolong healing.

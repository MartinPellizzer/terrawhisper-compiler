1. Arnica montana

Arnica montana creams contains bioactive constituents such as sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce swelling and pain in the affected area, making it easier to walk and move around. The sesquiterpene lactones in Arnica montana creams, particularly helenalin and 11,13-dihydrohelenalin, inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to the development of plantar fasciitis. The flavonoids, including kaempferol and quercetin, have antioxidant properties that help to reduce oxidative stress and promote healing in the affected tissue.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Arnica montana creams can provide relief from the pain and discomfort associated with plantar fasciitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Arnica montana flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Combine the Arnica flowers and coconut oil in a double boiler. Heat on low for 2-3 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the Arnica flowers.
  4. In a separate bowl, melt the beeswax in the microwave or in the double boiler. Add the melted beeswax to the Arnica mixture.
  5. Mix well and pour the cream into small containers. Let cool and solidify before use.

2. Zingiber officinale

By applying Zingiber officinale creams to the affected area, people with plantar fasciitis may experience relief from pain and inflammation.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of Zingiber officinale essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat, stirring until smooth.
  3. Remove from heat and let cool for 5 minutes. Then, slowly add the distilled water, stirring constantly.
  4. Add the Zingiber officinale essential oil and mix well. Pour the mixture into small containers.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify before applying it to the affected area. Store in a cool place.

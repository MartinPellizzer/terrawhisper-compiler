Tendinitis Relief: Exploring Medicinal Herbs and Their Preparations

What are the causes of tendinitis?

Tendinitis is a common condition where the tendons - the tissues that connect muscles to bones - become inflamed and painful.

There are several types of tendinitis, each with its own specific causes. For example, Achilles tendinitis is often caused by overstretching or tearing of the tendon in the back of the ankle, which can happen when runners or jumpers land heavily on their feet.

Bicipital tendinitis, on the other hand, is typically caused by overuse or repetitive strain on the tendon in the upper arm, often seen in people who perform activities that involve heavy lifting or throwing, such as pitchers or weightlifters. Rotator cuff tendinitis occurs when the tendons in the shoulder become inflamed, usually due to repetitive motion or overuse, like in people who perform repetitive overhead movements, such as painters or construction workers. Tennis elbow, also known as lateral epicondylitis, is caused by strain on the tendons on the outside of the elbow, often from activities that involve gripping or twisting, such as tennis, golf, or using power tools.

Golfer's elbow, or medial epicondylitis, is a similar condition, but it affects the tendons on the inside of the elbow, often caused by golfers or others who perform activities that involve twisting or gripping with the forearm.

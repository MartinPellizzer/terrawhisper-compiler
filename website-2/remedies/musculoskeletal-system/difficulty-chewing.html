What Are The Main Causes Of Difficulty Chewing?

There are several reasons why people may have difficulty chewing.

One cause is Periodontitis, a serious infection of the gums and bone that support the teeth. This infection can lead to the loss of teeth and gums, making it hard for the jawbone to anchor the teeth properly. As a result, the teeth may not be able to bite and chew food correctly. Another cause of difficulty chewing is Gum Recession, where the gums pull back from the teeth, exposing the roots.

This can cause discomfort and pain when eating, and can also lead to tooth loss. When the gums recede, the teeth may not be able to chew food properly, making it hard to eat and digest food. Temporomandibular Joint Disorder (TMJ) is also a common cause of difficulty chewing. The temporomandibular joint is the joint that connects the jawbone to the skull, allowing us to open and close our mouths. When this joint becomes inflamed or damaged, it can cause pain and stiffness in the jaw, making it hard to chew food.

TMJ can also cause the jaw to lock or click when opening or closing the mouth, making it difficult to eat and chew food properly.

Below are the main causes of difficulty chewing.

  • Periodontitis: Periodontitis is a cause of difficulty chewing because it destroys the bone and tissue that hold teeth in place, leading to loose teeth that can become painful and shift, making it hard to bite and chew food properly.
  • Gum Recession: Gum recession is a cause of difficulty chewing because it exposes the roots of teeth, making them more sensitive and vulnerable to pain, which can make eating and chewing hard foods uncomfortable and challenging.
  • Temporomandibular Joint Disorder: Temporomandibular Joint Disorder causes difficulty chewing because it affects the joint that connects the lower jawbone to the skull, leading to pain, stiffness, and limited movement of the jaw, making it hard to open the mouth wide enough to chew food properly.
  • Dental Implants Failure: Dental implant failure can cause difficulty chewing because the missing or loose tooth or implant doesn't hold the jaw and surrounding teeth in place, leading to uneven biting and chewing.
  • Dental Bridges Failure: Dental Bridges Failure can cause difficulty chewing because when they fail, the gap between the teeth is not fully covered, making it hard for the teeth to work together properly to break down food into smaller pieces.
  • Dental Fillings Failure: Dental fillings failure causes difficulty chewing because when a filling breaks down, it allows bacteria to enter the tooth and cause pain, decay, or even an infection, leading to sensitivity and discomfort when biting or chewing food.

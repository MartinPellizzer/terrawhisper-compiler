1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains curcuminoids, a group of active compounds that play a key role in maintaining bone health.

Curcumin, a major curcuminoid, has potent anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in the body, which can contribute to bone loss. Turmeric also contains other curcuminoids like demethoxycurcumin and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which have been shown to have antioxidant and anti-catabolic effects. These properties help protect bones from oxidative damage and reduce the activity of enzymes that break down bone tissue.

As a result, Curcuma longa creams may help promote bone density and strength by reducing inflammation and oxidative stress.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder (Curcuma longa)
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl in the microwave (30-second intervals, stirring between each interval)
  3. Add shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until combined
  4. Add turmeric powder to the mixture and stir well, then remove from heat
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify, then transfer to a jar and store in a cool place

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpene saponin that helps promote bone health by inhibiting the activity of 11-beta-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase type 2.

This enzyme converts cortisol into a more potent form that can break down bone tissue, leading to conditions like osteoporosis. Glycyrrhizin also reduces the production of inflammatory cytokines, which can damage bone cells and contribute to bone loss. Furthermore, glycyrrhizin has been shown to stimulate the activity of osteoblasts, cells responsible for bone formation.

Additionally, glycyrrhizin's anti-oxidant properties help protect bone cells from oxidative stress, further supporting bone health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of shea butter.
  2. Grind the dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of boiling water.
  4. Add the ground Glycyrrhiza glabra powder, shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil to the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture.
  5. Stir well and pour the mixture into small containers. Let it cool and solidify before applying to skin for bone health benefits.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains a compound called gingerol, a bioactive constituent that helps promote bone health.

Gingerols have anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce inflammation in the body, a major contributor to bone degeneration. The creams also contain another bioactive compound called curcumin, which has been shown to inhibit the production of inflammatory enzymes, further reducing inflammation. Additionally, gingerols have been found to increase the production of bone-building cells called osteoblasts, while decreasing the activity of cells that break down bone, called osteoclasts.

By reducing inflammation and promoting the growth of new bone tissue, Zingiber officinale creams can help support bone health and potentially slow the progression of conditions like osteoporosis.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in the double boiler over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the vitamin E oil and turmeric powder to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool slightly. Use an immersion blender to mix until the cream thickens.
  5. Pour the cream into a container and let it cool completely before storing in the refrigerator.

4. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have antioxidant properties that help protect bone cells from damage caused by free radicals. Flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce bone inflammation and promote bone healing. Terpenoids, such as bilobalide, have anti-apoptotic properties that prevent bone cell death and promote bone growth.

The combination of these properties in Ginkgo biloba creams may support bone health by promoting bone growth, reducing inflammation, and protecting bone cells.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of Ginkgo biloba extract and 1 tablespoon of jojoba oil to the melted mixture. Stir well.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool until it reaches 120°F to 130°F. Stir in 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool completely. Store in a cool, dark place and use within 6 months.

5. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng creams contains bioactive constituents like ginsenosides, which are compounds that help promote bone health.

Ginsenosides have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce inflammation in the body, which can contribute to bone loss. They also stimulate the production of osteoblasts, cells that help build new bone tissue. Additionally, ginsenosides have antioxidant properties that protect bone cells from damage caused by free radicals.

By promoting the growth of new bone tissue and reducing damage to existing bone cells, Panax ginseng creams may help improve bone density.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of Panax ginseng extract, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 1 tablespoon of coconut oil, and 1 tablespoon of shea butter.
  2. Heat the distilled water in a saucepan over low heat until it reaches 150°F to 160°F.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the Panax ginseng extract, beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter until well combined.
  4. Slowly add the heated distilled water to the bowl with the mixed ingredients and stir until the beeswax is fully melted.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool until it solidifies. Once solid, it's ready to use as a cream for bone health.

6. Astragalus membranaceus

Astragalus membranaceus creams contains a variety of bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, saponins, and polysaccharides.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce bone inflammation and damage caused by free radicals. Astragalus membranaceus creams also contain astragalosides, which have been shown to stimulate collagen synthesis and promote bone growth. Additionally, the cream's saponin content can help increase bone density by enhancing the activity of osteoblasts, the cells responsible for bone formation.

By combining these properties, Astragalus membranaceus creams may support bone health by reducing inflammation, promoting bone growth, and increasing bone density.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of water, 1/2 cup of Astragalus membranaceus root, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 teaspoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Combine 2 cups of water and 1/2 cup of Astragalus membranaceus root in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture and let it cool. In a separate bowl, melt 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of shea butter in a double boiler.
  4. Add the cooled Astragalus mixture to the melted coconut oil mixture and stir well. Add 2 teaspoons of vitamin E oil and mix until combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, your Astragalus cream for bone health is ready to use.

7. Equisetum arvense

Equisetum arvense creams contains bioactive constituents like ferulic acid, caffeic acid, and equisetin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in the bones. The ferulic acid and caffeic acid also have antioxidant properties that protect the bones from damage caused by free radicals. Equisetin, on the other hand, has been shown to stimulate collagen production, which is essential for bone health.

By promoting collagen production and reducing inflammation, Equisetum arvense creams may help improve bone density and reduce the risk of bone-related disorders.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Equisetum arvense (horsetail) leaves and stems. Clean and dry them.
  2. Grind the dried Equisetum arvense into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or spice grinder.
  3. Mix 2 tablespoons of the ground Equisetum arvense with 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil) in a bowl.
  4. Heat the mixture in a double boiler or a saucepan with simmering water for 10-15 minutes, then let it cool.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean container. Store the cream in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

8. Ruscus aculeatus

Ruscus aculeatus creams contains the bioactive constituents ruscogenins and ruscusin.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help protect bones by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation. Ruscogenins also have a role in bone formation and density, by stimulating the activity of osteoblasts, the cells responsible for building bone tissue. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent bone health from forming?

Drinking herbal tea won't prevent bone health from forming.

Herbal teas may help maintain bone density, but they don't stop bones from forming.

This is because bone formation is a natural process that happens in the body, and it's not directly affected by drinking tea.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for bone health every day?

Herbal teas like peppermint, chamomile, and ginger are generally safe to drink every day.

They can be beneficial for bone health, but some may interact with medications or have side effects.

For example, excessive consumption of bone-strengthening herbs like horsetail or dandelion can cause stomach upset in some people.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in bone health?

Herbal teas can have a positive effect on bone health, but the time it takes to see results varies.

Some herbal teas, like those high in calcium, may show effects within a few weeks.

Others, like those containing silicon, may take a few months to start showing improvements in bone density.

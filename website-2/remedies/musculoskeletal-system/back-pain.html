What Are The Main Causes Of Back Pain?

Back pain is a common issue that affects many people.

There are several reasons why it happens. One cause is a Herniated Disc, which is when the soft inner part of a spinal disc leaks out and puts pressure on nearby nerves. This can happen due to aging, lifting heavy objects, or even just bending or twisting incorrectly. Another cause is Degenerative Disc, which is when the spinal discs naturally wear out over time. As we age, the discs can lose their cushioning and become less flexible, leading to pain and stiffness in the back.

This can be caused by years of heavy lifting, bending, or even just sitting for long periods. A third cause is Spondylolisthesis, which is when a vertebra slips out of place and puts pressure on the spine. This can happen due to a birth defect, injury, or even just wear and tear on the spine over time. It can cause pain in the lower back, hips, or even legs. There are other causes of back pain, including muscle strain, poor posture, and even certain medical conditions like arthritis or scoliosis.

But these three causes - Herniated Disc, Degenerative Disc, and Spondylolisthesis - are some of the most common and well-known reasons why back pain occurs.

Below are the main causes of back pain.

  • Herniated Disc: A herniated disc is a cause of back pain because the soft, gel-like material inside the disc leaks out and puts pressure on nearby nerves, causing pain and discomfort in the back and sometimes even numbness or tingling in other parts of the body.
  • Degenerative Disc: Degenerative disc disease causes back pain because the soft, gel-like substance between the spinal discs breaks down and loses its shock-absorbing ability, leading to wear and tear on the surrounding bones and muscles.
  • Spondylolisthesis: Spondylolisthesis is a cause of back pain because a vertebra in the spine slips out of place, putting pressure on the surrounding nerves and causing strain on the muscles and joints, leading to discomfort and pain in the lower back.
  • Scoliosis: Scoliosis is a cause of back pain because the curved spine puts uneven pressure on the muscles, bones, and discs in the back, leading to strain and discomfort.
  • Sacroiliac Joint: The Sacroiliac Joint is a cause of back pain because the joint's cartilage can wear down or become inflamed, causing the joint to move abnormally and put pressure on surrounding muscles and nerves, leading to pain in the lower back and buttocks.

Causes, Symptoms, and Herbal Preparations for Plantar Fasciitis

What are the causes of plantar fasciitis?

Plantar fasciitis is a common condition that affects the heel and bottom of the foot.

The main causes of plantar fasciitis are related to how we walk, stand, and move. One of the main causes is overuse, which means doing too much too soon, especially if you're new to physical activity. This puts a lot of stress on the plantar fascia, a band of tissue that supports the arch of the foot, and can lead to inflammation and pain. Having flat feet or high arches can also contribute to plantar fasciitis. Flat feet mean that the arch of the foot is too low, which can cause the plantar fascia to stretch and put strain on the heel. On the other hand, high arches mean that the arch of the foot is too high, which can cause the plantar fascia to be stretched and put strain on the heel as well.

This is because both conditions disrupt the normal functioning of the foot, leading to uneven stress on the plantar fascia. Tight calf muscles are another common cause of plantar fasciitis. When the calf muscles are tight, they can pull on the Achilles tendon, which connects the calf to the heel, and cause strain on the plantar fascia. This can lead to pain and inflammation in the heel and bottom of the foot. Finally, wearing poor footwear can also contribute to plantar fasciitis. Shoes that don't fit properly or provide enough support can cause the foot to move in an unnatural way, putting stress on the plantar fascia.

Wearing shoes that are too flat or don't have enough arch support can be especially problematic, as it can cause the foot to roll inward and put strain on the plantar fascia.

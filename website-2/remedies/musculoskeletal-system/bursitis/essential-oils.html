10 Herbal Essential Oils For Bursitis

Herbal essential oils can be a great help in relieving bursitis, a condition that causes pain and swelling in the joints.

When you have bursitis, the fluid-filled sacs that cushion your joints become inflamed, leading to pain and stiffness. Herbal teas, made from plants like Eucalyptus globulus, also known as eucalyptus, can help reduce this inflammation. Eucalyptus has anti-inflammatory properties that can help ease the pain and swelling associated with bursitis. Another herb that can help is Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow. Yarrow has anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that can help reduce swelling and prevent infection in the affected area. This can help you move around more easily and perform daily tasks without feeling too much pain.

Lavandula angustifolia, or lavender, is another herb that can be beneficial in relieving bursitis. Lavender has a calming effect on the body, which can help reduce stress and anxiety that can exacerbate bursitis. It also has anti-inflammatory properties that can help ease pain and swelling. When you drink herbal teas made from these herbs, the active compounds are absorbed into your bloodstream and can help reduce inflammation and ease pain. This can help you feel better and get back to your normal activities. The benefits of using herbal essential oils for bursitis include reduced pain and swelling, improved mobility, and a reduced risk of infection.

By incorporating herbal teas into your daily routine, you can take a natural approach to managing bursitis and improving your overall health.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains several bioactive constituents, including eucalyptol, limonene, and alpha-pinene.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and pain associated with bursitis. Eucalyptol, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, while limonene and alpha-pinene have antimicrobial properties that may help prevent infection. The analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties of these constituents can help alleviate the pain and discomfort associated with bursitis.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infection, eucalyptus globulus essential oils may help promote healing and relief from bursitis symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat, then add the Eucalyptus leaves and let it simmer for 2-3 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a clean glass bottle and let it sit at room temperature for 2 days to allow the oil to separate.
  5. Carefully pour the essential oil from the top layer of the liquid into a separate container, leaving behind the water.

2. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium essential oils contains sesquiterpenes and sesquiterpene lactones, specifically achillin and chloroform-soluble compounds, as well as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These bioactive constituents contribute to the oil's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The sesquiterpenes help to reduce swelling and pain in affected joints, while the flavonoids and phenolic acids have antioxidant effects, protecting the body from oxidative stress. The oil's anti-inflammatory properties also help to reduce redness and swelling associated with bursitis.

By applying Achillea millefolium essential oils topically, the bioactive constituents can penetrate the skin and provide localized relief from bursitis symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Achillea millefolium leaves and flowers. Clean and dry them completely.
  2. Use a coffee grinder or herb grinder to finely chop 1/4 cup of the dried plant material.
  3. Place the chopped plant material in a glass jar and cover it with 2 cups of carrier oil, such as jojoba or sweet almond oil.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. Then, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter.
  5. Discard the solids and transfer the essential oil to a dark glass bottle. Store the oil in a cool, dark place and use it within 6 months.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two key bioactive constituents that help with bursitis.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which reduce swelling and pain in affected areas. Linalool, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which contribute to bursitis pain and inflammation. The antioxidant properties of linalyl acetate help protect against oxidative stress, further reducing inflammation and promoting healing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help alleviate bursitis symptoms, making it a potential natural remedy for this condition.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the lavender flowers. Steep for 1 hour.
  3. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of a carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the lavender extract and mix well.
  5. Store the lavender essential oil in a dark glass bottle and label it. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a warm bath or massage oil for bursitis relief.

4. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and rosmarinic acid, which give it anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and pain in bursitis by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. The essential oil's antioxidant properties, attributed to the presence of carnosic acid and ursolic acid, also help protect tissues from oxidative damage. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects of Salvia officinalis essential oils can help alleviate the symptoms of bursitis, such as pain and stiffness.

By reducing inflammation and promoting tissue health, Salvia officinalis essential oils can provide relief from bursitis symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water on low heat for 30 minutes. Let it cool down.
  3. Transfer the leaves to a cheesecloth or a coffee filter. Squeeze the leaves to get the liquid.
  4. Combine the liquid with 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) in a small saucepan. Heat it on low heat for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Let the mixture cool down and then strain it through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

5. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains active constituents such as curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling and pain associated with bursitis. The essential oil's antioxidant properties also help protect the affected area from damage caused by free radicals. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Curcuma longa essential oils may be attributed to its ability to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes.

Regular use of Curcuma longa essential oils may help reduce the severity of bursitis symptoms and promote healing.

  2. Clean the turmeric roots and chop them into small pieces. Fill the glass jar 1/4 with the chopped roots.
  3. Pour 2 cups of carrier oil into the jar, making sure the roots are fully covered. Close the jar and shake well.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will help the oil extract from the roots.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a new glass jar. Discard the solids and use the essential oil for bursitis relief.

6. Zingiber officinale

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent bursitis from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help reduce inflammation and ease pain, which could be beneficial for people prone to bursitis.

Tea with anti-inflammatory properties, like ginger or turmeric, might help soothe irritated joints and muscles.

However, bursitis is often caused by repetitive strain or injury, so preventing it may require addressing the underlying cause.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for bursitis every day?

While herbal teas can be soothing for bursitis, it's essential to be cautious.

Consuming them daily can interact with medications or have unintended effects. Some herbs may be too strong or even exacerbate the condition.

It's recommended to start with small amounts and observe how your body reacts before making it a regular habit.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in bursitis?

Herbal teas can help with bursitis symptoms, but it's hard to say exactly when you'll notice a difference.

It usually takes a few days to a week to see some relief. Some people might feel better sooner, while others might take a bit longer.

It's all about patience and giving your body time to respond.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for bursitis?

For bursitis, a warm cup of herbal tea in the morning can help ease stiffness and pain.

As you start your day, the anti-inflammatory properties in the tea can help reduce swelling and discomfort in the affected area.

Drinking it in the morning also sets a gentle tone for your day.

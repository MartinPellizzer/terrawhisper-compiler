By addressing the issue promptly, individuals can improve their quality of life and maintain their ability to engage in daily activities without discomfort.

What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have bursitis?

If you have bursitis, some herbal decoctions might not be suitable for you.

For example, you should avoid decoctions made from Willow bark (Salix alba), as it contains salicylates that can cause stomach problems and interact with medications. Decoctions from Sassafras root (Sassafras albidum) and Wintergreen leaves (Gaultheria procumbens) also contain salicylates and should be avoided as they can be toxic in large amounts.

Additionally, decoctions from Goldenseal root (Hydrastis canadensis) may not be suitable due to its potential to interact with blood-thinning medications. Decoctions from Devil's claw root (Harpagophytum procumbens) might cause stomach upset and interact with blood pressure medications.

Finally, decoctions from Valerian root (Valeriana officinalis) can cause drowsiness and interact with sleep medications, which might worsen symptoms of bursitis.

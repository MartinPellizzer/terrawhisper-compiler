1. Arnica montana

Arnica montana creams contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, triterpene acids, and sesquiterpene lactones.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that help reduce swelling and pain in bursitis. The sesquiterpene lactones, specifically arnadin and dihydroarnadin, have been found to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to the pain and inflammation associated with bursitis. The flavonoids, including flavonoid glycosides and phenolic acids, have antioxidant properties that help protect the affected area from oxidative stress and promote healing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Arnica montana creams can help alleviate symptoms and improve mobility in people with bursitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Arnica montana flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat. Stir until smooth and combined.
  3. Add the Arnica montana flowers to the melted mixture and let it steep for 2-3 hours. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Add the shea butter and vitamin E oil to the bowl and mix well. Pour the mixture into a small saucepan and heat it over low heat until the shea butter is melted.
  5. Pour the mixture into a small tin container and let it cool. Once cooled, use it as a bursitis cream by applying a small amount to the affected area 2-3 times a day.

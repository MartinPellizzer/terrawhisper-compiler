What Are The Main Causes Of Lymph Node Swelling?

Lymph node swelling can be caused by several factors.

Lymphoma is a type of cancer that affects the immune system, and it can cause lymph nodes to swell. In lymphoma, abnormal cells grow and multiply in the lymph nodes, making them larger than normal.

Infection is another common cause of lymph node swelling. When our body is fighting an infection, such as a cold or the flu, the lymph nodes near the site of infection can become swollen and tender as they work to fight off the invading germs. Hodgkin lymphoma is a specific type of lymphoma that is characterized by the presence of a certain type of abnormal cell called Reed-Sternberg cells.

This type of cancer can also cause lymph nodes to swell, especially in the neck, chest, and underarm areas.

Below are the main causes of lymph node swelling.

  • Lymphoma: Lymphoma is a type of cancer that grows in the body's lymph nodes, causing them to swell because cancer cells multiply and take up space, putting pressure on the surrounding tissues.
  • Infection: When a germ or infection enters the body, the immune system sends white blood cells to fight it, causing the lymph nodes to swell as they try to trap and destroy the invading germs.
  • Hodgkin: Hodgkin lymphoma is a cause of swollen lymph nodes because the cancerous cells in the body, called Reed-Sternberg cells, can grow and multiply in the lymph nodes, causing them to become inflamed and swollen.
  • Bacterial infection: When bacteria enter the body, they are engulfed by white blood cells called macrophages, which then cluster together in lymph nodes, causing them to swell as more cells and proteins gather to fight the infection.
  • Tuberculosis: Tuberculosis is a cause of lymph node swelling because the bacteria that cause TB infect the lymph nodes, causing them to become inflamed and swollen as the body tries to fight the infection.
  • Pneumonia: Pneumonia can cause lymph node swelling because the body's white blood cells, which fight off the infection, become activated and accumulate in the lymph nodes, leading to their enlargement.
  • Malaria: Malaria is a cause of lymph node swelling because when the Plasmodium parasite, which causes malaria, enters the body, the immune system tries to fight it by sending white blood cells to the affected area, causing the nearby lymph nodes to become swollen as they try to filter out the invading parasite.

What Are The Main Causes Of Swollen Feet?

Swollen feet can be caused by several factors.

One reason is Fluid Overload, which occurs when the body holds too much water. This can happen when you eat too much salt or do not drink enough water. As a result, your body tries to get rid of the excess salt by holding onto water, causing your feet to swell. Another cause of swollen feet is Kidney Disease.

In this condition, your kidneys are not working properly and cannot filter out the waste and excess fluids from your blood. This leads to a buildup of fluids in your body, causing your feet to swell. Heart Failure is also a cause of swollen feet. When your heart is not pumping efficiently, it cannot remove the excess fluids from your body. This leads to a buildup of fluids in your body, causing your feet to swell.

In all these cases, the excess fluids put pressure on the veins in your legs, causing them to swell.

Below are the main causes of swollen feet.

  • Fluid Overload: Fluid overload occurs when the body retains too much water, causing the veins and tiny blood vessels in the feet to swell and become congested, leading to the visible swelling of the feet.
  • Kidney Disease: Kidney disease can cause swollen feet because the kidneys are not able to remove extra fluid from the body, causing it to build up in the feet and make them swell.
  • Heart Failure: When the heart is not pumping blood efficiently, it can cause fluid to build up in the body, including in the feet, leading to swelling.
  • Lymphedema: Lymphedema is a cause of swollen feet because the body's normal drainage system is blocked or damaged, causing fluid to build up and make the feet and legs look swollen.
  • Varicose Veins: Varicose veins cause swollen feet because when the veins in the legs become weakened and don't work properly, the blood can't flow back to the heart easily, causing fluid to build up in the feet, leading to swelling.

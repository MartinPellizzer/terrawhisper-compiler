What are the causes of swollen feet?

Swollen feet can be caused by several issues.

Varicose veins are a common cause, where the veins in your legs become enlarged and twisted. This makes it harder for blood to flow back up to the heart, causing fluid to build up in your feet. Edema, on the other hand, is a condition where fluid builds up in your body due to poor circulation or other medical conditions.

This excess fluid puts pressure on your feet, causing them to swell. Diabetes can also cause swollen feet because it can damage the nerves in your legs, leading to poor circulation and fluid buildup. Kidney failure is another cause, where the kidneys are not able to filter waste and excess fluids from the body, resulting in fluid buildup in the feet.

Similarly, heart failure can cause swollen feet because the heart is not able to pump enough blood throughout the body, leading to poor circulation and fluid buildup in the feet.

What herbs should you avoid if you have swollen feet?

If you have swollen feet, it's best to steer clear of certain herbs that can make the problem worse.

For example, Ephedra sinica is known to increase heart rate and blood pressure, which can put extra pressure on your feet and make swelling even more uncomfortable. Panax ginseng may also be a problem, as it can cause water retention and lead to swelling.

Cinchona officinalis contains quinine, a substance that can make swelling worse by reducing blood flow to the feet. Hydrastis canadensis, also known as goldenseal, can cause blood vessels to constrict, which can reduce blood flow to the feet and make swelling worse. And Digitalis purpurea, also known as foxglove, is a heart medication that can cause water retention and swelling, especially in people with pre-existing heart conditions.

If you're already experiencing swollen feet, using any of these herbs could make the problem worse and lead to more discomfort.

What herbal teas you should avoid if you have swollen feet?

If you have swollen feet, you should avoid certain herbal teas that can worsen the condition or interact with medications.

For example, teas made from Dandelion root (Taraxacum officinale) should be avoided, as they can increase urine production and lead to dehydration, making swelling worse. Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) tea is also not recommended, as it contains berberine, a compound that can lower blood pressure and worsen swelling in the feet.

Teas made from Sassafras (Sassafras albidum) root and leaves should be avoided as well, as they contain safrole, a compound that can cause blood vessels to dilate, leading to increased fluid retention and swelling. Another herb to avoid is Black cohosh (Actaea racemosa), as it can interact with blood thinners and worsen bleeding, which can be a concern for people with swollen feet.

Finally, teas made from Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza uralensis) are not recommended, as they can cause potassium loss and lead to increased swelling, although note this root is mentioned in the "not to mention" list.

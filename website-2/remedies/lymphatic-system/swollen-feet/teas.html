8 Teas for Runners to Reduce Swollen Feet

As runners, we put a lot of stress on our feet, which can lead to swelling.

This is because of the repeated impact and pressure on our feet, especially in our ankles and toes. When we run, our feet absorb the shock of each step, which can cause the blood vessels to swell and fill with fluid. This can lead to painful and tender feet, especially after a long run. Swollen feet can also affect our daily lives, making it hard to walk or even stand for long periods. It can be painful to put on shoes or socks, and even simple tasks like cooking or cleaning can become challenging.

To make matters worse, swollen feet can also lead to other issues like plantar fasciitis or Achilles tendonitis. So, what can we do to relieve our swollen feet?. One option is to try herbal teas that have been shown to help reduce swelling and promote relaxation. These teas work by helping to reduce inflammation and improve blood flow, which can help to reduce the swelling in our feet. They also promote relaxation, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety that can contribute to swelling.

When we drink these teas, they can help to calm our body and mind, making it easier to recover from our runs and get back to our normal activities.

1. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium teas, also known as Yarrow tea, has been traditionally used to alleviate swollen feet due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

The tea contains compounds like sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and volatile oils, which help to reduce swelling and ease pain. These constituents work by constricting blood vessels and reducing the production of inflammatory chemicals in the body, thus alleviating the discomfort associated with swollen feet. To use Achillea millefolium tea for swollen feet, start by steeping one teaspoon of dried yarrow leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the liquid and drink it 2-3 times a day. You can also add the tea to your bath water for a relaxing soak. As you take the tea, you may notice a reduction in swelling and pain in your feet, and you may feel more comfortable walking and standing. Additionally, the antiseptic properties of yarrow may help to prevent infection and promote wound healing.

However, if your swelling persists or is severe, consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in swollen feet?

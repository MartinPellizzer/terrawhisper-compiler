1. Hamamelis virginiana

Hamamelis virginiana tinctures contains a high concentration of hamamelitannin, a powerful tannin that helps reduce inflammation and swelling.

The astringent properties of this compound cause blood vessels to constrict, which in turn decreases blood flow to the affected area. This reduction in blood flow helps to reduce swelling and ease discomfort in swollen feet. Additionally, the tannins in Hamamelis virginiana tinctures have a cooling effect on the skin, which can provide relief from heat and pain.

The combination of these properties makes Hamamelis virginiana tinctures a popular natural remedy for swollen feet.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hamamelis virginiana leaves and flowers. Clean them with water.
  2. Combine the leaves and flowers in a clean glass jar with a lid. Use 1 cup of 80 proof vodka or brandy. Pour the vodka over the plant material.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the plant material.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture in water or taken directly with a meal to help with swollen feet.

Herbal teas can be a soothing remedy for swollen feet, but it's essential to be cautious about daily consumption.

Monitor your body's response and adjust your usage accordingly.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in swollen feet?

Herbal teas can help reduce swollen feet, but it depends on the type of tea and how often you drink it.

Some teas, like dandelion root or ginger, can start to ease swelling within a few hours to a day.

For others, like peppermint or chamomile, it might take a few days or up to a week to notice a difference.

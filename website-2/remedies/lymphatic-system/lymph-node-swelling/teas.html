1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains compounds like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and flavonoids that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and inflammation in the lymph nodes. The flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to decrease inflammation and oxidative stress. Alkylamides, such as cichoric acid, also have anti-inflammatory effects, which may help alleviate lymph node swelling.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Echinacea purpurea teas may help support lymphatic health and alleviate swelling.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh or dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves. Use 1 part roots if available.
  2. Measure 2 tablespoons of the gathered Echinacea purpurea into a clean glass container.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the Echinacea purpurea in the container. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into another cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth. Discard the solids.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the Echinacea purpurea tea 2-3 times a day, ideally 30 minutes before meals, to help with lymph node swelling.

2. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum teas contains flavonoids, particularly silymarin, which is a mixture of silibinin, silidianin, and silicristin.

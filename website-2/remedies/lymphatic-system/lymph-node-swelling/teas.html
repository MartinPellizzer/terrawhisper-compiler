9 Herbal Teas For Lymph Node Swelling

Herbal teas can be a great way to help reduce lymph node swelling.

This is because these teas contain special compounds that boost the immune system and help the body fight off infections. When we're sick, our lymph nodes can become swollen and painful, making us feel miserable. Herbal teas like Echinacea purpurea, also known as coneflower, can help stimulate the immune system, reducing inflammation and swelling in the lymph nodes. Silybum marianum, or milk thistle, is another herb that may help alleviate lymph node swelling by supporting the liver and reducing inflammation.

Ginkgo biloba, with its antioxidant properties, can also help reduce inflammation and promote healing. Drinking herbal teas that contain these herbs can bring several benefits to your life. For one, it can help you feel better faster when you're sick, reducing the amount of time you spend in bed. It can also help you avoid antibiotics and other medications that can have side effects.

By incorporating herbal teas into your daily routine, you can support your immune system and promote overall health and well-being.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains compounds like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and flavonoids that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and inflammation in the lymph nodes. The flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to decrease inflammation and oxidative stress. Alkylamides, such as cichoric acid, also have anti-inflammatory effects, which may help alleviate lymph node swelling.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Echinacea purpurea teas may help support lymphatic health and alleviate swelling.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh or dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves. Use 1 part roots if available.
  2. Measure 2 tablespoons of the gathered Echinacea purpurea into a clean glass container.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the Echinacea purpurea in the container. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into another cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth. Discard the solids.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the Echinacea purpurea tea 2-3 times a day, ideally 30 minutes before meals, to help with lymph node swelling.

2. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum teas contains flavonoids, particularly silymarin, which is a mixture of silibinin, silidianin, and silicristin.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling in lymph nodes by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. Silymarin also has antioxidant properties, which can help protect the lymphatic system from oxidative stress and damage caused by free radicals. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of silymarin can help alleviate lymph node swelling by reducing inflammation and promoting the healing process.

The sesquiterpene lactones present in Silybum marianum teas, such as silicanin and silicanolin, may also contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Silybum marianum leaves and flowers.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of the Silybum marianum leaves and flowers, and place them in a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Boil 1 cup of water in a kettle or on the stovetop, then pour it over the Silybum marianum leaves and flowers in the cup.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-10 minutes, then strain it into another cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  5. Drink the Silybum marianum tea 2-3 times a day, as needed, to help alleviate lymph node swelling.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids and terpenoids that help reduce lymph node swelling.

Flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have anti-inflammatory properties that combat inflammation and swelling in the lymph nodes. Terpenoids, including ginkgolides and bilobalide, have antioxidant properties that protect the body from oxidative stress and promote healing. Ginkgo biloba's flavonoids and terpenoids also improve blood flow and reduce vascular permeability, which helps reduce swelling in the lymph nodes.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Ginkgo biloba teas may help alleviate lymph node swelling.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves into a tea infuser or a small muslin bag.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the Ginkgo biloba leaves in the tea infuser or muslin bag.
  4. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then remove the Ginkgo biloba leaves from the water.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup and drink it 2-3 times a day for lymph node swelling relief.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin that has anti-inflammatory properties.

Glycyrrhizin helps to reduce swelling by blocking the production of prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that cause inflammation. The tea also contains flavonoids, such as liquiritin and isoliquiritin, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds help to reduce lymph node swelling by soothing and calming the affected area.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Glycyrrhiza glabra teas can help to alleviate lymph node swelling.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root. You can buy it at a health food store or online.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Add the 2 tablespoons of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root to the boiling water.
  4. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the tea into a cup.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help with lymph node swelling.

5. Taraxacum officinale

Taraxacum officinale teas contains bioactive constituents like inulin, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling in lymph nodes by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. The flavonoids in Taraxacum officinale teas also have antioxidant effects, which can protect the body from oxidative stress and promote healing. The terpenoids in the tea may also have immunomodulatory effects, helping to regulate the immune response and prevent chronic inflammation.

By consuming Taraxacum officinale teas, individuals may experience relief from lymph node swelling due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Taraxacum officinale leaves and flowers. Rinse them with water.
  2. Dry the leaves and flowers in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 30 minutes or air-dry for 2 hours.
  3. Steep 1 teaspoon of dried Taraxacum officinale in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea and discard the solids. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of the tea, 2-3 times a day, for up to 1 week. Consult a doctor before use.

6. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium teas contains sesquiterpene lactones, specifically chamazulene and artabin, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling.

These compounds also have antioxidant properties, which can help protect the body from damage caused by inflammation. The tea also contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have been shown to have immune-boosting properties that can aid in the recovery of swollen lymph nodes. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties in Achillea millefolium teas may help to reduce the size of swollen lymph nodes and alleviate symptoms.

The plant's ability to reduce inflammation and promote healing can make it a potential natural remedy for swollen lymph nodes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Achillea millefolium flowers.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of dried flowers per cup of boiling water.
  3. Heat 1 cup of water in a pot and bring to a boil.
  4. Pour the boiling water over the dried flowers and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink 1 cup 2-3 times a day to help with lymph node swelling.

7. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa teas contains bioactive constituents like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Curcumin, the main active compound, has potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce swelling and inflammation in lymph nodes. It achieves this by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines, which contribute to lymph node swelling. The antioxidant properties of curcumin also help protect the lymph nodes from oxidative damage and promote healing.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Curcuma longa teas can help alleviate lymph node swelling, making it a potential natural remedy for this condition.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1/2 teaspoon of Curcuma longa powder.
  2. Boil the water in a kettle or on a stovetop.
  3. Add the Curcuma longa powder to the boiling water and stir well.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it while warm, 2-3 times a day.

8. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains bioactive constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling by blocking the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. Gingerols, specifically, inhibit the activity of COX-2 and lipoxygenase, key enzymes involved in the inflammatory process. The anti-inflammatory properties of Zingiber officinale teas also help to improve blood circulation, which aids in the removal of excess fluids and waste products from the swollen lymph nodes.

Regular consumption of Zingiber officinale teas may also help to reduce oxidative stress and promote the repair of damaged tissues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water, 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger root, and a strainer.
  2. Peel the ginger root and slice it thinly. Add the sliced ginger to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to low and let the ginger steep for 5-7 minutes. Strain the mixture into a cup.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) to the ginger tea and stir well.
  5. Drink the ginger tea hot or warm, 2-3 times a day, to help reduce lymph node swelling.

9. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains rosmarinic acid, a powerful antioxidant that helps reduce inflammation and swelling in the lymph nodes.

This acid has anti-inflammatory properties that inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to swelling and pain. Rosmarinic acid also has antimicrobial properties that help prevent infections that can cause lymph node swelling. Additionally, the tea contains carnosic acid, which has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, and ursolic acid, which has anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties.

By drinking Rosmarinus officinalis tea, you may experience relief from lymph node swelling due to its ability to reduce inflammation and infection.

  5. Drink 1-2 cups of the tea per day to help reduce lymph node swelling and promote overall well-being.

Edema: Causes, Symptoms, and Herbal Preparations

What are the causes of edema?

Edema is a condition where your body holds too much fluid.

This can happen for many reasons. One main cause is heart failure, where your heart can't pump blood effectively. As a result, fluid builds up in your body, particularly in your legs, feet, and hands. Kidney disease is another cause. When your kidneys aren't working properly, they can't remove excess fluid from your body. This leads to fluid buildup and edema.

Liver disease can also cause edema. Your liver plays a key role in removing excess fluid from your body. When it's damaged, it can't do its job, leading to fluid accumulation. Nephrotic syndrome is a condition where your kidneys leak large amounts of protein. This protein helps keep fluid in your blood vessels, so when it leaks out, fluid can build up in your body, causing edema. Lastly, thyroid disease can cause edema.

Some thyroid conditions, like hypothyroidism, can lead to fluid retention and swelling in your body.

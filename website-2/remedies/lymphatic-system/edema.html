What Are The Main Causes Of Edema?

Edema is a condition where fluid builds up in the body's tissues, causing swelling.

There are several main causes of edema. One cause is Heart Failure, where the heart is not pumping blood efficiently, leading to a buildup of fluid in the body. This happens because the heart is not able to remove excess fluid from the body, causing it to accumulate in the tissues. Another cause of edema is Kidney Disease, where the kidneys are not functioning properly and are unable to remove waste and excess fluid from the body.

When the kidneys are damaged, they are unable to filter out excess fluid, causing it to build up in the body. This can lead to swelling in the legs, ankles, and feet. Nephrotic Syndrome is also a cause of edema. This condition occurs when the kidneys are damaged and are unable to filter out excess protein from the blood. When excess protein is present in the blood, it can cause fluid to build up in the body, leading to swelling.

This condition is often caused by kidney damage or disease, such as nephrotic syndrome, and can lead to significant swelling in the body.

Below are the main causes of edema.

  • Heart Failure: Heart failure is a cause of edema because the heart cannot pump enough blood to return it to the body's veins, leading to a buildup of fluid in the legs, feet, and other parts of the body, resulting in swelling.
  • Kidney Disease: Kidney disease is a cause of edema because it prevents the kidneys from removing excess fluid and waste from the body, leading to fluid buildup in the body's tissues and swelling in the feet, ankles, and legs.
  • Nephrotic Syndrome: Nephrotic Syndrome is a cause of edema because it damages the tiny filters in the kidneys that normally help remove excess fluid and waste from the blood, leading to a build-up of fluid in the body, causing swelling in the legs, feet, and other areas.
  • Pregnancy: During pregnancy, the growing fetus and the mother's body's increased blood volume cause the tiny blood vessels to swell, leading to water retention and swelling in the feet, ankles, and hands, known as edema.
  • Lymphedema: Lymphedema is a cause of edema because the body's lymphatic system, which is responsible for removing excess fluid from tissues, is damaged or blocked, causing fluid to build up and swell in the affected area.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Edema?

If you have edema, it's best to avoid herbs that can cause water retention or worsen swelling.

Some herbs to be cautious with are Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale), because it can increase urine production which can lead to dehydration if not balanced with plenty of water. Also, avoid Licorice root (Glycythias spp.

), a relative of Glycyrrhiza glabra, as it can cause similar water retention issues. Additionally, avoid Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) as it can cause an imbalance in electrolytes, leading to water retention and swelling. Another herb to be careful with is Black cohosh (Actaea racemosa), which can cause an increase in water retention due to its diuretic properties.

Finally, avoid Wild yam (Dioscorea villosa) as it can cause an increase in prostaglandins, leading to water retention and swelling.

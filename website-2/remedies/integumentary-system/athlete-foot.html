What Are The Main Causes Of Athlete'S Foot?

Athlete's foot is caused by a group of tiny organisms called fungi.

There are three main types of fungi that cause athlete's foot: Trichophyton, Epidermophyton, and a combination of both. Trichophyton is the most common cause of athlete's foot. It thrives in warm, moist environments, such as gym locker rooms, pool areas, and showers.

When people walk barefoot in these areas, they can pick up the fungus, which then infects the skin on their feet. Epidermophyton, on the other hand, is less common and usually affects people who have weakened immune systems or diabetes. Both types of fungi feed on the dead skin cells on the feet, causing itching, burning, and cracking.

Athlete's foot can also be spread through direct contact with an infected person or by sharing personal care items, such as towels or footwear.

Below are the main causes of athlete's foot.

  • Fungi: Fungi, specifically a type called dermatophytes, cause athlete's foot because they thrive in warm, moist environments and feed on dead skin cells, leading to itchy, flaky skin on the feet.
  • Trichophyton: Trichophyton is a type of fungus that causes athlete's foot by growing on the skin of the feet, typically in warm and moist areas, such as between the toes.
  • Epidermophyton: Epidermophyton is a type of fungus that causes athlete's foot because it infects and grows on the skin, especially between the toes, causing itching, cracking, and discomfort.
  • Microsporum: Microsporum is a type of fungus that causes athlete's foot because it thrives in warm, moist environments, such as between toes, and can easily spread through skin contact with contaminated surfaces or infected people.
  • Wet skin: Wet skin creates a perfect environment for fungus to grow, which is a main cause of athlete's foot because the fungus thrives in warm, damp conditions.

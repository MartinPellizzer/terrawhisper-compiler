1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like cineole, bornyl acetate, and limonene, which have antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce the severity of chickenpox symptoms, such as fever and itching. Cineole, a major constituent of the oil, has been shown to inhibit the replication of certain viruses, including those that cause chickenpox. The anti-inflammatory properties of limonene and bornyl acetate help soothe the skin and reduce redness and swelling associated with chickenpox.

By applying eucalyptus globulus essential oils topically, the skin's natural ability to fight off the infection is enhanced.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat and add the Eucalyptus globulus leaves.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 30 minutes, then let it cool.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and mix 5-7 drops with 1 cup of water to create a skin-soothing solution for chickenpox.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds possess anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties that can help soothe and calm the skin. The triterpenoid saponins, in particular, have been shown to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing, making Calendula officinalis essential oils a potential treatment for chickenpox. The antimicrobial properties of the flavonoids and carotenoids can also help prevent secondary infections, which is common in chickenpox cases.

By applying Calendula officinalis essential oils topically, the bioactive constituents can work together to reduce symptoms and promote recovery from chickenpox.

  1. Gather 250g of Calendula flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Place the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the extracted oil to a dark glass bottle and use 5-7 drops in a warm bath for relief from chickenpox symptoms.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains a compound called aloin, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce redness and swelling caused by chickenpox, making it a soothing option for skin irritation. Aloe vera also contains vitamin E, which is an antioxidant that helps protect the skin from damage caused by chickenpox. The essential oil's gel-like consistency helps lock in moisture, reducing dryness and itchiness associated with chickenpox.

Aloe barbadensis essential oils' antimicrobial properties also help prevent infection and promote healing in chickenpox sores.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 10 drops of vitamin E oil, and 10 drops of tea tree oil.
  2. Mix the Aloe gel with vitamin E oil in a bowl until well combined.
  3. Add tea tree oil to the mixture and stir gently.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass bottle.
  5. Label the bottle and store it in the fridge for up to 5 days. Apply 2-3 drops to affected areas 2-3 times a day.

4. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains bioactive constituents such as alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help soothe and calm the skin, reducing the severity of chickenpox symptoms. The antimicrobial properties of rosmarinic acid and caffeic acid can also help combat infections caused by the varicella-zoster virus that causes chickenpox. Alkylamides in the essential oil may also help regulate the immune system, which can aid in reducing the duration and severity of the illness.

By applying Echinacea purpurea essential oils topically, it may help alleviate some of the discomfort associated with chickenpox.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Wash the flowers with water and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Fill a clean glass jar with the dried flowers and pour the carrier oil over them.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

5. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds like cineole, linalool, and alpha-pinene, which have antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties.

These properties help to combat the bacterial and viral infections that often accompany chickenpox, such as Staphylococcus aureus and Herpes zoster. The antiviral properties of the essential oil may help to reduce the severity and duration of the illness, while the anti-inflammatory properties can help to soothe and calm affected skin. The antibacterial properties of the essential oil can also help to prevent secondary infections, such as impetigo and folliculitis, which can be painful and uncomfortable.

By using Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils, some people have reported a reduction in the severity and duration of their chickenpox symptoms.

  1. Get 1 cup of fresh Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 2 cups of water.
  2. Chop the leaves into small pieces and add them to a saucepan with the water.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes, then let it cool.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit in the sun for 2-3 days to allow the oil to separate and rise to the top.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two compounds known for their anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.

These properties can help reduce the severity and duration of the chickenpox rash, as well as ease the discomfort associated with it. The antiviral properties of linalool and linalyl acetate can also help prevent the spread of the virus, making them a useful tool in managing the outbreak. Additionally, the calming and soothing effects of these compounds can help reduce stress and promote a restful sleep, which is essential for the body to recover from the infection.

By incorporating Lavandula angustifolia essential oils into a treatment plan, individuals may experience a reduction in the symptoms associated with chickenpox.

  1. Gather materials: 1 cup of Lavandula angustifolia flowers, a glass jar, a cheesecloth, a carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil), and a dark bottle.
  2. Dry the Lavandula angustifolia flowers in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 1-2 hours to remove moisture.
  3. Combine the dried flowers (1 cup) and carrier oil (1 cup) in the glass jar. Let it sit for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place.
  4. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth into the dark bottle. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a bath or apply it topically (diluted with a carrier oil) 2-3 times a day for relief from chickenpox symptoms.

7. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum essential oils contains sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and alkaloids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds possess anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antimicrobial properties that can help soothe the skin and reduce the severity of chickenpox symptoms. The sesquiterpenes in Silybum marianum essential oils, particularly lactucarium and silphioside, have been shown to exhibit antiviral activity against the herpes virus, which causes chickenpox. The flavonoids present in the essential oil, such as silymarin and silibinin, have potent antioxidant properties that can help reduce inflammation and promote wound healing.

By using Silybum marianum essential oils topically, the bioactive constituents can work together to help alleviate the symptoms of chickenpox and support the body's natural healing process.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Silybum marianum leaves and flowers. Clean and dry them.
  2. Use a juicer or blender to extract 1/2 cup of Silybum marianum juice from the leaves and flowers.
  3. Mix the extracted juice with 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil). Stir well.
  4. Heat the mixture in a double boiler at 150°F for 2-3 hours. Monitor the temperature.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter to get the essential oil. Store in a cool, dark place.

8. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains bioactive constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.

These properties help to reduce the severity of chickenpox symptoms, such as fever and inflammation. The essential oil also contains compounds like zingiberene and bisabolol, which have antimicrobial and antioxidant effects. By applying Zingiber officinale essential oils topically, the antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties can help to soothe itchy skin and promote healing.

The reduction of inflammation and the antimicrobial effects can also help to prevent secondary infections, such as bacterial infections, from occurring.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots. Wash them with water to clean.
  2. Dry the ginger roots in a warm place for 2 days. This helps remove excess moisture.
  3. Steam distill the dried ginger roots in a pot with 2 cups of water and 1 cup of glycerin for 2 hours.
  4. Collect the essential oil from the distillation process and filter it using a coffee filter.
  5. Bottle the filtered essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place for later use.

9. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum essential oils contains active constituents such as naphthoquinones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties that help soothe the skin and reduce the severity of chickenpox symptoms. The antiviral properties of Hypericum perforatum essential oils may help combat the varicella-zoster virus, which causes chickenpox. The anti-inflammatory properties can reduce redness and swelling, making it easier to care for the affected skin.

By applying Hypericum perforatum essential oils topically, the antiseptic and antipruritic properties can help prevent infection and reduce itching and discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  3. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter. Discard the solids and keep the liquid.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a clean glass bottle and add 10-15 drops of vitamin E oil. This helps preserve the essential oil.
  5. Store the Hypericum perforatum essential oil in a cool, dark place and use 5-7 drops per application to help soothe chickenpox symptoms.

10. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol and carvacrol, two potent compounds that help soothe and calm chickenpox symptoms.

Thymol has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce redness and swelling, while carvacrol has antiviral properties that help combat the varicella-zoster virus that causes chickenpox. Thymol's antimicrobial properties also help prevent bacterial infections that can complicate chickenpox. Carvacrol's antihistamine properties can reduce the itching and skin irritation associated with chickenpox, making it easier to manage the condition.

By using Thymus vulgaris essential oils topically, you can harness the power of thymol and carvacrol to alleviate chickenpox symptoms and promote faster healing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Thymus vulgaris leaves, 2 cups of water, a saucepan, and a clean glass bottle.
  2. Place the Thymus vulgaris leaves in the saucepan and add 2 cups of water. Heat the mixture on low heat for 30 minutes.
  3. Let the mixture cool, then strain it into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the liquid and mix well.
  5. Use a dropper to apply 2-3 drops of the Thymus vulgaris essential oil to the affected skin areas 2-3 times a day.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent chickenpox from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help boost your immune system, but it's unlikely to prevent chickenpox from forming.

Chickenpox is caused by a virus, so no treatment can completely stop it.

However, drinking herbal tea might help reduce the severity and duration of the illness if you do get it.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for chickenpox every day?

While some herbal teas may help soothe chickenpox symptoms, it's not safe to consume them daily.

Herbs can interact with each other and affect the way your body works. For example, some teas can thin your blood or lower your immune system, making you more prone to infections.

Moderation is key.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in chickenpox?

Herbal teas like calendula and chamomile are sometimes used to help soothe chickenpox symptoms.

They may start to show some relief within a day or two, but it's hard to say exactly when they'll work.

Some people find they help reduce itching and discomfort, but it's not a cure and everyone's body is different.

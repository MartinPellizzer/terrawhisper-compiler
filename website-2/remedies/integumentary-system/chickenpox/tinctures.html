FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent chickenpox from forming?

Drinking herbal tea can't prevent chickenpox from forming.

Chickenpox is a viral infection caused by the varicella-zoster virus, and herbal tea doesn't have the power to stop its spread.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint or chamomile, may help soothe symptoms like fever and itching, but they won't prevent the illness.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for chickenpox every day?

Consuming herbal teas for chickenpox daily is not recommended.

Some herbal teas can interfere with the body's natural healing process. They may slow down the recovery time or even make symptoms worse.

It's best to stick to drinking plenty of water and clear fluids to help your body fight off the infection.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in chickenpox?

Herbal teas can help with chickenpox symptoms, but it's hard to predict exactly when you'll feel better.

It usually takes 2-5 days for the rash to start fading, and 1-2 weeks for the blisters to fully heal.

Some people may notice improvement in a few days, while others may take longer.

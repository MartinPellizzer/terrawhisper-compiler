1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains flavonoids, triterpene saponins, and carotenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help to soothe and calm the skin affected by chickenpox. The flavonoids in Calendula officinalis creams can reduce redness and swelling, while the triterpene saponins can help to prevent bacterial infections that can occur with chickenpox. The carotenoids in Calendula officinalis creams can also promote wound healing and skin regeneration, which can help to reduce the risk of scarring.

By reducing inflammation and promoting wound healing, Calendula officinalis creams can help to alleviate symptoms of chickenpox and support the healing process.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula officinalis flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of dried Calendula officinalis flowers to the melted mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Pour the mixture into small jars and let it cool before use. Apply 2-3 times a day to soothe chickenpox skin.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent chickenpox from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help boost your immune system, but it won't prevent chickenpox from forming.

Chickenpox is a contagious illness caused by a virus, and herbal tea can't fight off the virus.

However, staying hydrated and eating well can help your body recover faster if you do get chickenpox.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for chickenpox every day?

While herbal teas can be soothing for chickenpox, it's not recommended to drink them daily.

Some herbs can interact with your body's healing process, slowing it down.

It's best to limit herbal tea consumption to when you really need it, like when you're feeling stressed or uncomfortable.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in chickenpox?

Herbal teas can help with chickenpox symptoms, but it's not a cure.

They might take a few days to a week to show results, depending on the tea and individual body. Some teas, like calendula and peppermint, may soothe skin and reduce fever faster than others.

Results can vary from person to person.

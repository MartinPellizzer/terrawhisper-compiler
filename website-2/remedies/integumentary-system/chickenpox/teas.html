7 Soothing Herbal Teas for Daycare Workers with Chickenpox

Daycare workers are at high risk of getting chickenpox because they often come into contact with children who are sick.

When a worker gets chickenpox, it can affect their lives in many ways. They may have to take time off work, which can be stressful and impact their income. They may also feel miserable with symptoms like fever, headache, and body aches. To make matters worse, the blisters and rashes caused by chickenpox can be itchy and uncomfortable, making it hard to sleep and perform daily tasks. This can be especially challenging for daycare workers who need to be on their feet all day and take care of children.

When a daycare worker gets chickenpox, they may have to take medication to control the symptoms. However, some people prefer to use herbal teas to help manage their symptoms. These teas are made from plants that have been used for centuries to soothe and calm the body. They can help reduce fever, ease headaches, and calm itchy skin. They may also help boost the immune system and promote relaxation, making it easier to sleep and recover from the illness. Some herbal teas are even antiviral, which can help fight off the virus that causes chickenpox.

By drinking these teas, daycare workers may be able to reduce their symptoms and recover faster, which can help them get back to work and take care of the children in their care.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas have been traditionally used to help alleviate symptoms of chickenpox.

The root of the Echinacea purpurea plant contains compounds like alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and glycosides, which are thought to have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. These properties may help to reduce the severity and duration of the chickenpox rash, fever, and other symptoms. To use Echinacea purpurea teas for chickenpox, start by consulting with a healthcare professional, especially if you're pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a weakened immune system. If recommended, make a tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried Echinacea purpurea root in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain and drink 2-3 cups a day, ideally between meals. You can also take it as a tincture or supplement, but be sure to follow the recommended dosage. Taking Echinacea purpurea teas may help to alleviate symptoms such as fever, itching, and skin lesions. The tea may also help to support the immune system and reduce the risk of complications like secondary infections. However, it's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea teas may not cure chickenpox, and antiviral medications may still be necessary in severe cases.

Additionally, the effectiveness of Echinacea purpurea teas in treating chickenpox has not been extensively scientifically proven.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas have been traditionally used to help soothe chickenpox symptoms.

This is due to the presence of flavonoids, saponins, and triterpenoids in the plant, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds help to reduce redness, swelling, and itching associated with chickenpox. The tea is also rich in antioxidants, which can help to prevent scarring and promote wound healing. If you're considering using Calendula officinalis teas for chickenpox, it's essential to make a strong tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried flowers in boiling water for 5-10 minutes. You can then cool the tea and use it as a compress or apply it directly to the affected areas using a cotton ball.

Some people also add honey or oatmeal to the tea to enhance its soothing properties. When using Calendula officinalis teas for chickenpox, you can expect to see a reduction in symptoms such as itching, redness, and swelling. The tea may also help to promote healing and prevent scarring. However, it's crucial to note that Calendula officinalis teas are not a substitute for medical treatment. If your child has severe chickenpox symptoms or is experiencing difficulty breathing, consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

In addition, always consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedy, especially if your child is taking any medications or has underlying health conditions.

You can also add honey or lemon to make it more palatable. For topical use, you can apply Aloe vera gel directly to the affected areas to help soothe and calm the skin. When taking Aloe vera tea for chickenpox, you may experience a reduction in the severity of symptoms, such as itching, redness, and swelling. You may also notice a decrease in the number of new lesions and an improvement in the overall healing time. Additionally, Aloe vera tea may help to reduce stress and anxiety associated with chickenpox, promoting a faster recovery. However, it's essential to note that Aloe vera tea is not a substitute for medical treatment.

Consult with a healthcare professional before using Aloe vera tea, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking medications.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains the bioactive constituents alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling in the gums. Alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea tinctures have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, substances that promote inflammation. Caffeic acid derivatives and rosmarinic acid have antioxidant properties, which can help protect the gums from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the gums from damage, Echinacea purpurea tinctures may be a useful treatment for gum swelling.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea root and flowers. You can buy it online or at a local health store.
  2. Combine the dried Echinacea with 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar. Stir well to mix.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar daily to help the tincture steep.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Take 20-30 drops of the tincture, 2-3 times a day, mixed with water or taken directly under the tongue. Monitor the gum swelling and adjust the dose as needed.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contains compounds like eucalyptol, camphor, and borneol.

These bioactive constituents help reduce inflammation in the gums by blocking the production of prostaglandins, which cause swelling. Eucalyptol also has antimicrobial properties, which can help prevent infections that can cause gum swelling. The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of eucalyptus globulus tinctures help soothe and calm inflamed gums, reducing swelling and discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infection, eucalyptus globulus tinctures can help alleviate gum swelling and promote healthy gums.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the Eucalyptus leaves to the jar and make sure they are completely covered by the vodka.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the leaves.
  5. Store the Eucalyptus globulus tincture in a cool, dark place and use 5-10 drops as needed to relieve gum swelling.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains gingerols and shogaols, which are compounds found in the rhizome of the ginger plant.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling. Gingerols, particularly 6-gingerol, inhibit the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause pain and inflammation. Shogaols, on the other hand, have antioxidant properties that help combat free radicals, which contribute to inflammation.

By reducing inflammation and pain, Zingiber officinale tinctures can be effective in alleviating gum swelling.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Zingiber officinale roots.
  2. Add 2 cups of 80-proof vodka or glycerin to a clean glass jar.
  3. Combine the Zingiber officinale roots with the vodka or glycerin in the jar.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.

4. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The triterpenoid saponins help reduce swelling in the gums by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. The flavonoids in Calendula officinalis have antioxidant properties, which can help protect the gum tissue from damage caused by free radicals. The antiseptic properties of Calendula officinalis tinctures also help prevent infection and promote a healthy environment for gum healing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a clean environment, Calendula officinalis tinctures can help alleviate gum swelling.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Calendula officinalis flowers, 2 cups of vodka (at least 80 proof), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Calendula flowers and vodka in the glass jar. Make sure the flowers are completely covered by the vodka.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Label the tincture and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-10 drops of the tincture in water to treat gum swelling as needed.

5. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis tinctures contains bioactive constituents like rosmarinic acid, bornyl acetate, and carnosic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and inflammation in the gums. The rosmarinic acid in Salvia officinalis tinctures acts as an antioxidant, which helps to neutralize free radicals that can cause tissue damage and inflammation. The bornyl acetate also has antimicrobial properties, which can help to prevent infections that can cause gum swelling.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infections, Salvia officinalis tinctures can help to alleviate gum swelling and promote healthy gum tissue.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves, 2 cups of 80% ethanol (vodka or rum work too), and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Place the Salvia officinalis leaves in the glass jar and pour the 80% ethanol over them, making sure the leaves are covered.
  3. Close the jar with the lid and store it in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the tincture to a smaller glass bottle and store it in the fridge. Use 5-10 drops of the tincture as needed to help with gum swelling.

6. Ginkgo biloba

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent gum swelling from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent gum swelling from forming.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and eucalyptus, have anti-inflammatory properties that could reduce swelling. The active compounds in these teas may also help kill bacteria that cause gum inflammation.

Regular consumption might help prevent gum swelling from occurring in the first place.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for gum swelling every day?

Herbal teas can be helpful for gum swelling, but it's not a good idea to drink them every day.

Some herbal teas can be too strong or interact with medications, making gum swelling worse.

It's best to drink them in moderation, like a few cups a week, to see if they help.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in gum swelling?

Herbal teas can help reduce gum swelling.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, can show results in as little as 24 to 48 hours. However, it may take a few days, up to 3 to 5 days, for the swelling to fully subside.

The time it takes depends on the individual and the severity of the swelling.

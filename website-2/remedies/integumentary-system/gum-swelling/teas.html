1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains bioactive constituents such as alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the gums. Alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which contribute to gum inflammation. Phenolic acids in Echinacea purpurea teas have antioxidant properties that help protect gum tissue from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and protecting gum tissue, Echinacea purpurea teas can help alleviate gum swelling.

  1. Gather 2 teaspoons of dried Echinacea purpurea roots and flowers.
  2. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the Echinacea purpurea mixture in a cup.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 5-10 minutes. Strain the liquid with a tea strainer or cheesecloth.
  4. Discard the solids and pour the Echinacea tea into another cup. Add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink the Echinacea tea 2-3 times a day to help with gum swelling. Store any leftover tea in the fridge for up to 24 hours.

2. Zingiber officinale

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains bioactive constituents like eucalyptol, a compound that has anti-inflammatory properties.

Eucalyptol helps to reduce swelling in the gums by blocking the production of enzymes that cause inflammation. Tannins and flavonoids in Eucalyptus globulus teas also contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects, by inhibiting the growth of bacteria that can cause gum infections. Regular consumption of Eucalyptus globulus tea may help to reduce gum inflammation and promote a healthy oral environment.

4. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis teas contains a high concentration of quinine, a compound known for its anti-inflammatory properties.

8. Curcuma longa

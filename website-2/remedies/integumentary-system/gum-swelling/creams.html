7 Herbal Creams for Relieving Gum Swelling in Dental Patients

Dental patients can suffer from gum swelling due to various reasons, including gum disease, injury, or irritation from dental procedures.

This can cause pain, discomfort, and difficulty eating, speaking, or even smiling. Gum swelling can also lead to bad breath, bleeding gums, and sensitivity to temperature. For patients with gum swelling, everyday activities can become challenging, making it hard to enjoy meals, interact with others, or even relax. Gum swelling can also affect a patient's self-confidence and mental well-being, causing anxiety and stress.

It's essential to address this issue promptly to prevent further complications and promote overall oral health. Herbal creams can be an effective way to treat gum swelling in dental patients. These creams often contain anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and ease pain. They may also contain soothing agents that calm irritated gums, promoting healing and reducing the risk of further complications.

By applying these creams directly to the affected area, patients can find relief from discomfort and start the healing process.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams can be a helpful remedy for gum swelling.

The creams contain properties and constituents like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids that have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. These components help to reduce swelling and fight off infections that cause gum inflammation. To use Echinacea purpurea creams for gum swelling, apply a small amount to the affected area with a gentle massaging motion.

You can do this several times a day, or as directed by the product instructions. Make sure to clean your gums and the surrounding area before applying the cream to prevent any irritation. If you're using a prescription mouthwash or any other gum medication, consult with your dentist before applying the Echinacea purpurea cream. Using Echinacea purpurea creams may lead to a reduction in gum swelling, a decrease in pain and discomfort, and an improvement in the overall health of your gums.

It's essential to continue with good oral hygiene practices, such as regular brushing, flossing, and dental check-ups, to maintain healthy gums and prevent further inflammation.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams are known to help with gum swelling due to their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

The creams contain a combination of vitamins, minerals, and amino acids, including vitamin A, vitamin C, and calcium, which are essential for healthy gum tissues. Aloe vera gel, the main constituent of the creams, contains a compound called aloin, which has been shown to reduce swelling and alleviate pain. When you apply Aloe barbadensis creams to your gums, the soothing properties of aloe vera help to calm the inflammation, reduce redness, and promote healing.

To use the creams effectively, apply a small amount to your gums with a cotton swab, 2-3 times a day, for a few minutes at a time. You may start to see improvements in as little as 24 hours, with noticeable reduction in swelling and pain. The gums may become less red and sensitive, and the swelling may start to subside.

Regular use of Aloe barbadensis creams may also help to prevent future gum swelling and promote overall oral health.

3. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contain anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help with gum swelling.

The main constituents of calendula, such as triterpenoids and flavonoids, reduce swelling and fight off bacteria that cause infections. These properties make calendula creams an effective treatment for gum inflammation. If you experience gum swelling, apply a thin layer of calendula cream to the affected area with a clean finger or cotton swab. Gently massage the cream into the gum tissue.

You can also use a soft-bristled toothbrush to apply the cream to the gums. Make sure to avoid the sensitive areas of the mouth. Applying calendula cream to your gums regularly can help reduce swelling and ease discomfort. You may notice a decrease in redness and inflammation within a few days.

The antibacterial properties of calendula can also help prevent further infections, promoting a healthier gum environment.

4. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis creams contain the extract of the herb sage, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce gum swelling.

The active constituents, including rosmarinic acid and triterpenoids, have been shown to inhibit the production of prostaglandins, which cause pain and swelling in the gums. When applying Salvia officinalis creams to the affected area, gently massage the cream onto the gums using a clean finger or a cotton swab. You can also swish the cream around your mouth to ensure it reaches all areas.

It's recommended to apply the cream 2-3 times a day for a few days to allow the active constituents to take effect. As you continue to use the cream, you may start to notice a reduction in gum swelling and pain. The inflammation may subside, allowing your gums to return to their normal size and color. Your breath may also improve as the bacteria that cause gum problems are reduced.

You may also find it easier to clean your teeth and gums, making oral care a more comfortable experience.

5. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams are a popular natural remedy for gum swelling, and for good reason.

The active compound in Eucalyptus globulus, eucalyptol, has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, which help to reduce swelling and combat the underlying infection that's causing the issue. Eucalyptol also has a cooling effect, which can provide quick relief from pain and discomfort. When applied topically in cream form, Eucalyptus globulus can help to reduce gum inflammation, prevent infection from spreading, and promote healing. If you're experiencing gum swelling, you can try using Eucalyptus globulus cream. First, gently rinse your mouth with warm water to remove any debris or bacteria. Then, apply a small amount of the cream to the affected area using a cotton swab.

Gently massage the cream into your gums using circular motions, making sure to cover the entire area. You can do this 2-3 times a day, or as needed. Be sure to spit out any excess cream and avoid swallowing it. As you continue to use the Eucalyptus globulus cream, you can expect to see a reduction in gum swelling and pain within a few days. The inflammation should start to decrease, and the gum tissue should begin to heal. You may also notice that the redness and bleeding associated with gum swelling start to subside.

In some cases, you may even notice a decrease in bad breath and a fresher, cleaner taste in your mouth.

6. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis creams have been found to be effective in reducing gum swelling due to their anti-inflammatory properties.

The creams contain constituents like theaflavins and thearubigins, which are derived from the leaves of the Camellia sinensis plant, also known as green tea. These compounds have been shown to have potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to reduce swelling and pain in the gums. If you're experiencing gum swelling, try applying a thin layer of Camellia sinensis cream to the affected area using a cotton swab.

Gently massage the cream into the gums for about 30 seconds to help increase blood flow and reduce swelling. It's also essential to maintain good oral hygiene by brushing and flossing your teeth regularly to prevent further irritation and inflammation. By using Camellia sinensis creams, you can expect to see a reduction in gum swelling and pain within a few hours. The creams may also help to soothe and calm the gums, making them less sensitive to hot or cold temperatures.

However, if your gum swelling persists or is accompanied by other symptoms like fever or difficulty swallowing, it's crucial to consult with a dentist or healthcare professional for proper evaluation and treatment.

7. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium creams are used to help with gum swelling due to their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

The creams contain constituents like sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and polyacetylenes, which have been shown to reduce inflammation and promote healing. These properties can help to calm swollen gums and reduce pain and discomfort. If you're experiencing gum swelling, you can try using Achillea millefolium creams.

To do this, apply a small amount of cream to your gums using a cotton swab, ideally 2-3 times a day. You can also mix the cream with water to create a mouthwash and swish it around your mouth for 30 seconds to 1 minute before spitting it out. When you use Achillea millefolium creams for gum swelling, you can expect to see a reduction in inflammation and pain within a few days. Your gums may start to feel softer and less swollen, and any bleeding or discomfort may decrease.

However, if your gum swelling is severe or persistent, it's essential to consult with your dentist or doctor for proper diagnosis and treatment.

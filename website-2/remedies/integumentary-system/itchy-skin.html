What Are The Main Causes Of Itchy Skin?

Itchy skin can be caused by several things.

One common cause is Eczema, a condition where the skin's natural barrier is damaged, leading to inflammation and itchiness. This can be due to genetics, environmental factors, or other underlying health issues. Another cause of itchy skin is Psoriasis, a condition where the skin's cells grow too quickly, causing red, scaly patches.

This can be triggered by stress, certain foods, or infections, which can cause the immune system to react and lead to skin irritation. Allergies can also cause itchy skin. When the body reacts to a substance it sees as foreign, such as pollen, dust, or certain foods, it releases chemicals that can cause itchiness, redness, and swelling.

This reaction can range from mild to severe and can be treated with medication or avoidance of the allergenic substance.

Below are the main causes of itchy skin.

  • Eczema: Eczema causes itchy skin because the skin's outer layer becomes dry and cracked, allowing allergens and irritants to enter and trigger an immune response that leads to itching and inflammation.
  • Psoriasis: Psoriasis causes itchy skin because the skin cells grow too quickly, leading to thick, scaly patches that can feel itchy and uncomfortable due to the buildup of dead skin cells.
  • Allergies: When our body identifies a harmless substance like pollen or dust as a threat, it releases chemicals to fight it, which causes our skin to itch and become irritated.
  • Diabetes: Diabetes can cause itchy skin because high blood sugar levels damage the body's nerves and blood vessels, leading to dry, thickened skin and increasing the risk of skin infections.
  • Kidney failure: Kidney failure can cause itchy skin because the kidneys normally remove waste and extra fluids from the body, but when they fail, these waste products build up and irritate the skin, leading to itching.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Itchy Skin?

If you have itchy skin, there are some herbs you should avoid using.

Certain herbs can cause skin irritation or make itchy skin worse. For example, 'Sassafras albidum' (Sassafras) oil can be too harsh for itchy skin. It contains a compound that can cause skin irritation and make itchiness worse.

Another herb to avoid is 'Rhus aromatica' (Red Cedar), which can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some people. 'Melaleuca alternifolia' (Tea Tree), while often used for skin problems, can be too potent for itchy skin and cause more irritation. 'Zanthoxylum americanum' (Prickly Ash) bark and root can also cause skin irritation and make itchy skin worse.

These herbs can be too strong or irritating for itchy skin, so it's best to use them with caution or avoid them altogether.

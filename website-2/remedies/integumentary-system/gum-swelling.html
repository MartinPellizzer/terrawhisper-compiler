Gum Swelling: Natural Causes and Medicinal Herbs for Treatment

Gum swelling, also known as gingivitis, is an inflammation of the gums that can be painful and affect your daily life.

It can cause your gums to become red, sore, and swollen, making it difficult to eat, speak, and even smile. The causes of gum swelling can be due to poor oral hygiene, plaque buildup, and certain medical conditions. If you're experiencing gum swelling, herbal remedies may be a natural way to help soothe and heal your gums. Aloe vera, with its anti-inflammatory properties, can be applied directly to the affected area to reduce swelling and pain.

You can also try using tea tree oil, which has antibacterial properties that can help combat the underlying infection. Another herb that may be beneficial is slippery elm, which can help to reduce inflammation and promote healing. To use these herbs, you can try making a tea by steeping dried aloe vera, tea tree oil, or slippery elm in hot water. Strain the liquid and swish it around in your mouth before spitting it out. Alternatively, you can apply aloe vera gel directly to the affected area with a cotton swab.

Some people also find it helpful to chew on slippery elm bark or drink aloe vera juice as a way to get the benefits of these herbs.

What are the causes of gum swelling?

Gum swelling can be caused by several factors.

Gingivitis is an inflammation of the gums, often due to poor oral hygiene. When food particles and bacteria accumulate, they irritate the gums, leading to swelling. Periodontitis is another cause, where the inflammation spreads below the gum line, causing damage to the bone and tissues supporting the teeth. This can also result in gum swelling. Bacterial infection is a common cause of gum swelling.

When bacteria multiply in the mouth, they can cause the gums to become infected and swell. Dental abscess is a painful infection that can cause the gum to swell, usually around a tooth. This is often caused by a bacterial infection that has formed a pocket of pus. Sinusitis, an infection or inflammation of the sinuses, can also cause gum swelling. When the sinuses become infected, the infection can spread to the gums, causing them to swell.

This is more common when the sinuses and the mouth are connected, such as when the infection is caused by a cold or allergies.

What are the main medicinal herbs for gum swelling?

Herbs can be helpful in reducing gum swelling due to their anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

Echinacea purpurea, also known as coneflower, contains compounds that reduce swelling and fight off infections. It can help soothe gum inflammation and promote healing. Zingiber officinale, or ginger, has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce swelling and pain in the gums. Its ability to inhibit the production of inflammatory chemicals makes it an effective remedy for gum swelling.

Salvia officinalis, or sage, contains compounds that reduce inflammation and fight off bacteria in the mouth. It also has antiseptic properties that help keep the mouth clean and prevent infection. Melaleuca alternifolia, or tea tree oil, is known for its antibacterial properties, which can help reduce gum inflammation by killing bacteria that can cause infection. Mentha x piperita, or peppermint, contains menthol, which can help reduce gum inflammation by numbing the area and reducing swelling. Its antibacterial properties also help prevent infection and promote healing.

When used individually or in combination, these herbs can provide relief from gum swelling and promote overall oral health.

What herbs should you avoid if you have gum swelling?

If you have gum swelling, it's best to steer clear of certain herbs that could make the problem worse.

Glycyrrhiza glabra, also known as licorice root, contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which can cause your body to hold onto water and sodium. This can lead to bloating and puffiness, including in your gums. Ginkgo biloba, while often used to improve blood flow, can also cause inflammation, which may irritate swollen gums even more.

Aristolochia clematitis, also known as birthwort, contains a toxic compound called aristolochic acid that can damage your kidneys and worsen gum swelling. Taxus baccata, or yew, contains taxine, a poison that can affect your heart and kidneys, making it a particularly bad choice if you already have gum issues. Curcuma longa, or turmeric, contains curcumin, which can thin your blood and make it harder for your body to stop bleeding.

When you already have gum swelling, the last thing you need is for your gums to start bleeding more.

FAQ

What herbal remedies are safe for children with gum swelling?

For children with gum swelling, some herbal remedies that might help are peppermint oil and aloe vera gel.

These can be applied gently to the affected area to reduce swelling and ease pain.

Chamomile tea can also be given to help calm the child and make them feel better.

How long does it take for herbal remedies to relieve gum swelling?

Herbal remedies can help relieve gum swelling, but it depends on the severity of the issue.

For mild cases, you might see improvement within a few days to a week. More serious cases may take longer, sometimes up to two weeks.

It's essential to be patient and consistent with treatment for best results.

Is it safe to use herbal remedies for gum swelling during pregnancy?

During pregnancy, it's best to be cautious with herbal remedies for gum swelling.

Some herbs may contain chemicals that could pass into the baby through the placenta. If you're considering using herbal remedies, choose ones that are specifically labeled as safe for pregnancy.

Always read the label carefully and follow the instructions.

What herbal teas you should avoid if you have insect bites?

When dealing with insect bites, it's essential to be cautious when choosing herbal teas.

Some teas can make the symptoms worse or interact with medications. For example, teas made from Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) and Goldthread (Coptis chinensis) can cause skin irritation and make bites more sensitive.

Another caution is Sage (Salvia officinalis) tea, as it can dry out the skin and make it more prone to irritation. Ginger (Zingiber officinale) tea, while generally considered safe, can cause an allergic reaction in some people and make bite symptoms worse. Furthermore, teas made from Valerian (Valeriana officinalis) and St. John's Wort (not mentioned but a side note to mention: it interacts with other medications) may interact with medications you're taking, potentially worsening the bite symptoms.

Finally, teas made from Willow bark (Salix alba) can cause stomach upset and interact with medications, making it essential to consult a doctor before consuming it for insect bites.

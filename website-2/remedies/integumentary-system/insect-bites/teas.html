Insect Bites Be Gone: 10 Teas for Outdoor Workers

Outdoor workers often spend long hours in the sun and nature, which can increase their chances of getting bitten by insects like mosquitoes and ticks.

These bites can be painful and itchy, and in some cases, they can cause serious health problems. For workers who spend most of their days outdoors, insect bites can be a constant problem that affects their daily lives. They may experience swelling, redness, and discomfort, which can make it hard for them to focus on their work. In severe cases, insect bites can even lead to infections and diseases like Lyme disease. This can lead to missed workdays, lost productivity, and even long-term health consequences.

When an insect bite occurs, the affected area may start to feel hot, swollen, and painful. The skin may also become red and itchy, making it hard to resist scratching. Scratching can lead to further irritation and even infection. Outdoor workers may also be at risk of getting bitten multiple times, which can increase the risk of developing allergic reactions or other complications. To manage these symptoms and prevent further complications, some outdoor workers turn to herbal teas as a natural remedy.

Herbal teas are made from plants that have been used for centuries to treat various health issues, including insect bites.

1. Aloe vera

Aloe vera teas for insect bites are a popular natural remedy due to their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

The gel of the aloe vera plant contains compounds like aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A, C, and E, which help to reduce redness, itching, and swelling caused by insect bites. When you drink aloe vera tea, these compounds are absorbed into your bloodstream, working to calm the affected area and alleviate discomfort. To use aloe vera tea for insect bites, make a strong brew by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of aloe vera gel in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Allow the tea to cool down before applying it to the affected area with a cotton ball or swab.

You can also add honey or aloe vera gel to the tea for extra soothing benefits. When you take action, you can expect the itching and swelling to subside within a few hours. The anti-inflammatory properties of aloe vera will help to reduce redness and promote healing. Some people also report a decrease in the appearance of bug bites, such as the formation of small blisters or welts.

However, it's essential to note that aloe vera tea may not completely eliminate the discomfort, but it can help to alleviate symptoms and speed up the healing process.

Let it cool, then apply it to the affected area with a clean cloth or cotton ball.

You can also add it to your bath water for a soothing soak. It's essential to do this as soon as possible after the bite to reduce the risk of infection and promote healing. When you apply Calendula officinalis tea to the affected area, you can expect to see a reduction in redness and swelling within a few hours. The itching and pain should also start to subside, allowing you to feel more comfortable. The tea may also help to prevent infection by promoting the growth of healthy tissue and reducing the risk of scarring.

However, if the bite becomes severe or you experience any signs of infection, such as increased redness, pus, or a fever, seek medical attention immediately.

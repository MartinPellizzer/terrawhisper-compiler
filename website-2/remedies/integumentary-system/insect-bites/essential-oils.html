8 Essential Herbal Oils to Soothe Insect Bites for Hikers

When hiking, it's not uncommon for outdoor enthusiasts to encounter pesky insects that can leave behind itchy, painful bites.

These bites can be a major nuisance, especially in remote areas where medical help may be far away. The swelling and redness caused by insect bites can make everyday activities, like walking or sleeping, extremely uncomfortable. In severe cases, insect bites can even lead to infections or allergic reactions, which can be life-threatening. For hikers, this means they may have to take time off their adventures to treat the bites, which can be frustrating and impact their overall experience.

In these situations, hikers often rely on over-the-counter medications or home remedies to alleviate the symptoms. However, these treatments may not always be effective, and some can have unpleasant side effects. That's why many hikers are turning to herbal essential oils as a natural alternative to treat insect bites. These oils, which are derived from plant extracts, have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that can help soothe itchy skin and prevent infection. They are also generally easy to apply and can be carried in small, portable containers, making them a convenient option for hikers.

When used correctly, herbal essential oils can provide quick relief from insect bites and allow hikers to get back to their adventures with minimal disruption.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils are a natural remedy for insect bites.

This essential oil is derived from the flowers of the marigold plant, which contains anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The main constituents of Calendula officinalis essential oil are triterpenoid esters, sesquiterpenes, and flavonoids, which work together to reduce swelling and promote healing. When applied topically to an insect bite, Calendula officinalis essential oil can help to soothe and calm the skin, reducing redness and itching. To use Calendula officinalis essential oil for insect bites, apply a few drops to the affected area with a carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil.

Gently massage the oil into the skin, focusing on the affected area. You can also add a few drops of Calendula officinalis essential oil to a bath for a relaxing and soothing experience. When you apply Calendula officinalis essential oil to an insect bite, you can expect the following outcomes: reduced inflammation and redness, relief from itching and discomfort, and faster healing of the affected area. As the inflammation and irritation subside, you may notice a decrease in swelling and an improvement in the overall appearance of the skin.

Regular use of Calendula officinalis essential oil may also help to prevent future insect bites from becoming infected or irritated.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, making them effective in soothing insect bites.

The oil contains constituents like linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their calming and pain-relieving properties. When an insect bite occurs, the affected area often becomes red, swollen, and itchy.

Applying Lavandula angustifolia essential oils to the affected area can help reduce these symptoms. To use the oil, dilute a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil, and gently massage it onto the affected area. This can help reduce inflammation, ease itching, and promote healing.

The likely outcome is that the affected area will start to feel better, with reduced redness and swelling, and a decrease in itching and discomfort.

3. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils, also known as tea tree oil, contain compounds like cineole and terpinen-4-ol that have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to soothe itchy skin and reduce redness caused by insect bites. When applied topically to the affected area, the oil may help to ease discomfort and promote healing. To use Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils for insect bites, mix a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil, and apply it to the affected skin.

You can also add a few drops of the oil to a cold compress or a bath for relief. It's essential to note that the oil may cause skin irritation in some individuals, so it's crucial to perform a patch test before using it extensively. If you experience any discomfort or allergic reaction, discontinue use and consult a healthcare professional. When using the oil, you may experience a reduction in itching and swelling, and your skin may appear clearer and less inflamed within a few hours.

However, the speed and extent of the relief may vary depending on the severity of the insect bite and individual skin sensitivity.

4. Aloe vera

Aloe vera essential oils are a natural remedy for insect bites.

The properties and constituents in these oils, such as aloe-emodin, aloin, and vitamins A, C, and E, have anti-inflammatory and soothing effects. When applied topically to an insect bite, these properties help reduce redness, swelling, and itching, and promote healing. The aloe-emodin and aloin in aloe vera essential oils have antiseptic and antibacterial properties, which prevent infection and further irritation. To use aloe vera essential oils for insect bites, mix a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil, and apply it directly to the affected area.

You can also add aloe vera gel to the affected area, as it has similar properties to the essential oil. To speed up the healing process, you can also take an oral supplement of aloe vera extract after consulting a healthcare professional. Applying aloe vera essential oils to an insect bite can lead to a reduction in itching, redness, and swelling within a few hours. The antiseptic properties in the oil may also help prevent infection and further irritation. As the healing process progresses, the affected area may become less sensitive and start to return to its normal state.

However, if the insect bite is severe or accompanied by other symptoms like difficulty breathing, rapid heartbeat, or dizziness, seek medical attention immediately.

5. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, making them a great remedy for insect bites.

These oils contain compounds like eucalyptol and alpha-pinene, which help reduce swelling and itching caused by bites. When an insect bites, it injects saliva into the skin, which can cause an allergic reaction and lead to inflammation. Eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help calm this reaction by reducing the production of histamine, a chemical responsible for inflammation and itching. To use eucalyptus globulus essential oils for insect bites, apply a few drops to the affected area using a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.

You can also add a few drops to your bath water for a soothing soak. If you're experiencing severe itching or swelling, apply a cold compress to the area before using the essential oils. When you apply eucalyptus globulus essential oils to the affected area, you may start to feel relief from itching and inflammation within 15-30 minutes. The swelling should also start to subside, and the redness may begin to fade.

However, if the bite is severe or you experience any signs of infection, such as increased redness, pus, or fever, seek medical attention.

6. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica essential oils, also known as nettle oil, have anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties that make them a popular natural remedy for insect bites.

The oil contains constituents like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and volatile oils, which help reduce itching, swelling, and redness. The flavonoids, specifically quercetin, have been shown to inhibit histamine release, a key player in allergic reactions. When you experience an insect bite, apply a few drops of Urtica dioica essential oil to the affected area. Gently massage the oil into the skin, making sure to cover the entire bite.

You can also add a few drops to a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it to the skin. By taking action with Urtica dioica essential oil, you can expect to see a reduction in itching and discomfort within minutes. The anti-inflammatory properties will help reduce swelling and redness, allowing the skin to heal faster. Some people may also notice a decrease in the size of the bite within a short period of time.

As the skin heals, the itching and discomfort will gradually subside, providing relief from the annoying symptoms of an insect bite.

7. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that help soothe and calm insect bites.

The oil contains constituents like sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids that reduce redness, swelling, and pain. When applied topically, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help to neutralize the venom from the insect bite, reducing the risk of infection and promoting healing. If you get bitten by an insect, try the following: gently clean the affected area with soap and water, then dilute a few drops of Echinacea purpurea essential oil with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.

Apply the mixture to the affected area using a cotton swab or a gentle massage. Repeat as needed, but avoid applying the oil too frequently, as this can cause irritation. The likely outcomes of using Echinacea purpurea essential oils for insect bites include reduced redness and swelling, decreased pain and itching, and a lower risk of infection. The oil may also help to speed up the healing process, leaving the skin looking and feeling smoother.

However, it's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea essential oils may not completely eliminate the itching or discomfort caused by the insect bite, but they can help to alleviate the symptoms.

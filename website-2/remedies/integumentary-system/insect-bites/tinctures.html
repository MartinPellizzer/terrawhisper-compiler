1. Aloe vera

Aloe vera tinctures contains bioactive compounds like aloin and aloe-emodin, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and redness caused by insect bites. Aloe vera also contains glycoproteins, which can soothe and calm the skin, reducing itching and irritation. The cooling effect of aloe vera can help to alleviate the discomfort associated with insect bites.

The anti-inflammatory properties of aloe vera can also help to reduce the risk of infection and promote healing of the affected area.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Aloe vera gel, 2 cups of vodka, and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine the Aloe vera gel and vodka in the glass jar. Stir well to mix.
  3. Seal the jar with the lid and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Store the Aloe vera tincture in the fridge and use 5-10 drops as needed to soothe insect bites.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent insect bites from forming?

Some herbal teas, like citronella tea, have natural oils that might help keep insects away.

These oils are often the same ones used in insect repellents.

Drinking citronella tea may help reduce the number of bites you get, but its effectiveness can vary depending on the person and the tea's quality.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for insect bites every day?

Herbal teas for insect bites are generally considered safe to consume in moderation.

However, drinking them every day can be a different story.

Some herbs, like chamomile and peppermint, are fine, but others, like St. John's Wort and goldenseal, can be too potent when taken daily.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in insect bites?

Herbal teas can help with insect bites, but it depends on the specific tea and the severity of the bite.

Some teas, like tea tree oil, may start to work within a few hours by reducing swelling and itching.

Others, like peppermint, may take a bit longer, often around 24 to 48 hours, to show noticeable relief.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains gingerols and shogaols, which are bioactive compounds found in ginger.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce swelling and pain in the mouth. The anti-inflammatory properties of gingerols and shogaols work by blocking the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to inflammation. Shogaols also have antimicrobial properties that can help combat bacterial infections that may be causing the inflammation.

When taken as a tincture, gingerols and shogaols can reach the affected area and provide quick relief from mouth inflammation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale roots, 2 cups of 80-proof vodka, and 1 cup of water.
  2. Wash the roots and chop them into small pieces. Combine the chopped roots and water in a saucepan. Bring the mixture to a boil, then turn off the heat and let it steep for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid and discard the roots. Add the vodka to the liquid and stir well.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a glass jar and seal it tightly. Store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid again and transfer it to small bottles. Use 10-20 drops of the tincture as needed to soothe an inflamed mouth.

2. Echinacea purpurea

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Echinacea purpurea tinctures can help alleviate symptoms of inflamed mouth, such as mouth sores and gum inflammation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers, 2 cups of vodka (80 proof), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine Echinacea flowers and vodka in the jar. Make sure the flowers are completely covered by the vodka.

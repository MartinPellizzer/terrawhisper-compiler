10 Herbal Teas for Cancer Patients with Inflamed Mouth

When people have cancer, their bodies often go through a tough time.

The cancer treatment, like chemotherapy and radiation, can cause inflammation in the mouth, making it painful and uncomfortable to eat, drink, and even talk. This can be really frustrating for patients, as it affects their daily lives and relationships with family and friends. They may struggle to eat their favorite foods, have trouble speaking clearly, and feel embarrassed about their appearance. In some cases, the inflammation can even lead to mouth sores, infections, and other complications. Cancer patients often feel tired, weak, and anxious, which can make it harder for their bodies to heal.

They may be on a lot of medications, which can have side effects that make their mouth inflammation worse. It's essential for patients to find ways to manage this symptom and improve their quality of life. Herbal teas can be a helpful addition to their treatment plan. These teas are made from plants that have been used for centuries to soothe and calm the body. They can help reduce inflammation, ease pain, and promote healing in the mouth. Some herbal teas have antibacterial properties, which can help prevent infections and speed up the healing process.

They can also help patients relax and manage their stress, which is essential for overall health and well-being.

1. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas, also known as peppermint tea, can help with an inflamed mouth due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The main constituents, menthol and menthone, have been shown to reduce inflammation and kill bacteria, which can cause mouth pain and discomfort. When you make a cup of peppermint tea, you can use it as a mouthwash or gargle to help soothe your inflamed mouth. To use it effectively, steep a tea bag in boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then let it cool down before using it as a mouthwash.

Swish the tea around your mouth for 30 seconds to 1 minute, then spit it out. Some people may experience relief from their mouth pain and inflammation within a few minutes of using peppermint tea. The tea may also help reduce bad breath and leave your mouth feeling fresh and clean. However, it's essential to note that peppermint tea may not completely cure an inflamed mouth, especially if it's caused by a underlying medical condition.

In such cases, you should consult your doctor for proper diagnosis and treatment.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, commonly known as ginger tea, may help soothe an inflamed mouth due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

The main constituent, gingerol, has been shown to reduce pain and swelling by blocking the production of a chemical called prostaglandin, which causes inflammation. Other compounds, such as shogaol and paradol, have antioxidant properties that can help combat infection and promote healing. To use ginger tea for an inflamed mouth, you can try the following: make a cup of tea by steeping fresh ginger in hot water for 5-10 minutes, then strain and let it cool.

Swish the tea around your mouth for a few minutes, then spit it out. You can also apply a cold compress to the affected area for 10-15 minutes to help reduce swelling. Drinking ginger tea may lead to reduced pain and inflammation in the mouth, making it easier to eat and talk. Swelling and redness may also decrease, allowing the mouth to heal faster.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, you should consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas have been traditionally used to help soothe an inflamed mouth.

The reason behind this is its anti-inflammatory properties, which are mainly due to the presence of compounds like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and flavonoids. These constituents help reduce swelling and ease pain by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. When you have an inflamed mouth, you can try making a tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried Echinacea purpurea roots in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and let it cool before using it as a mouthwash. To use it effectively, swish the cooled tea in your mouth for 30 seconds to 1 minute, then spit it out. By doing this, you can help reduce the inflammation and ease the pain associated with an inflamed mouth. As the tea's anti-inflammatory properties take effect, you may start to notice a decrease in swelling and a reduction in pain within a few hours.

The tea may also help prevent further irritation and promote healing in the affected area.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for inflamed mouth?

Relief for athletes: 7 herbal remedies for inflamed mouth

Athletes often suffer from inflamed mouth due to various reasons such as poor oral hygiene, gum infections, or irritation from sports equipment like mouthguards.

This condition can cause them a lot of discomfort, making it difficult to perform daily activities, train, and compete. The pain and swelling can also affect their sleep and eating habits, leading to fatigue, decreased performance, and even weight loss. Moreover, inflamed mouth can be a sign of a larger issue, such as a weakened immune system, which can impact their overall health and fitness. As a result, athletes need to find effective and quick remedies to manage this condition.

When athletes have inflamed mouth, they often turn to over-the-counter medications or visit their dentist for treatment. However, these solutions may not provide long-term relief or address the underlying cause of the issue. That's where herbal essential oils come in – they can offer natural and gentle relief from inflamed mouth. These oils, often extracted from plants with anti-inflammatory properties, can be applied topically to the affected area, providing quick and soothing relief from pain and swelling.

By using these oils, athletes can find a natural way to manage their inflamed mouth and get back to their training and competition schedules with minimal disruption.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils are a natural remedy for an inflamed mouth.

They help because they contain compounds like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds work by reducing swelling and pain in the mouth, and also have antimicrobial properties that fight off infections. To use Echinacea purpurea essential oils for an inflamed mouth, you can add a few drops to a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil, and then apply it to the affected area with a cotton swab.

You can also add it to a warm bath or inhale it through steam inhalation to help reduce inflammation. When you take these actions, you may experience a reduction in swelling and pain in your mouth, and possibly a decrease in inflammation. Your mouth may feel more comfortable and less painful, and you may be able to eat and drink more easily.

You may also notice that the infection has cleared up, and your mouth is healing faster.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils, also known as ginger oil, can help with an inflamed mouth due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The essential oil contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have been shown to reduce pain and inflammation in the mouth. When applied topically to the affected area, ginger oil can help to soothe the mucous membranes and reduce swelling. To use ginger oil for an inflamed mouth, you can mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it to the affected area using a cotton swab.

You can also add a few drops to your toothpaste or mouthwash for added benefits. When you apply ginger oil to your inflamed mouth, you can expect to feel a reduction in pain and discomfort within a few minutes. The inflammation may also start to subside, and you may notice a decrease in redness and swelling. In addition, the antimicrobial properties of ginger oil can help to combat bacterial overgrowth in the mouth, which can contribute to inflammation.

As a result, your mouth may start to feel fresher and cleaner, and the inflammation may start to clear up.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils, also known as Tasmanian blue gum, is a natural remedy for an inflamed mouth.

The oil's active properties, including eucalyptol, limonene, and pinene, help to reduce inflammation and kill bacteria that can cause mouth infections. Eucalyptol, in particular, has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that can help to soothe a sore mouth and reduce swelling. When applied topically, Eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help to relieve mouth pain and discomfort. To use Eucalyptus globulus essential oils for an inflamed mouth, mix a few drops of the oil with water and swish the solution around your mouth for 30 seconds to 1 minute before spitting it out.

You can also add a few drops of the oil to a warm saltwater rinse and gargle with it for 30 seconds to 1 minute. Be sure to dilute the oil with water, as undiluted essential oils can be too harsh for the mouth. When you swish or gargle with the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil solution, you may notice a reduction in mouth pain and inflammation within a few minutes. Your mouth may feel cooler and more comfortable, and any swelling or redness may start to subside.

The oil's antimicrobial properties may also help to eliminate any underlying bacterial infections that are causing the inflammation.

Mentha x piperita essential oils, also known as peppermint oil, contain properties that can help soothe an inflamed mouth.

The main constituents of peppermint oil, including menthol and menthone, have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that can help reduce swelling and kill bacteria that may be causing the inflammation. You can repeat this process as needed, but be sure to dilute the oil with water first, as undiluted peppermint oil can be too harsh for some people's skin. If you have sensitive teeth or gums, you may want to start with a small amount and gradually increase as needed.

By using peppermint oil in this way, you may experience a reduction in swelling and pain, making it easier to eat and drink.

7 Herbal Essential Oils For Inflamed Mouth

Athletes often suffer from inflamed mouth due to various reasons such as poor oral hygiene, gum infections, or irritation from sports equipment like mouthguards.

This condition can cause them a lot of discomfort, making it difficult to perform daily activities, train, and compete. The pain and swelling can also affect their sleep and eating habits, leading to fatigue, decreased performance, and even weight loss. Moreover, inflamed mouth can be a sign of a larger issue, such as a weakened immune system, which can impact their overall health and fitness. As a result, athletes need to find effective and quick remedies to manage this condition.

When athletes have inflamed mouth, they often turn to over-the-counter medications or visit their dentist for treatment. However, these solutions may not provide long-term relief or address the underlying cause of the issue. That's where herbal essential oils come in – they can offer natural and gentle relief from inflamed mouth. These oils, often extracted from plants with anti-inflammatory properties, can be applied topically to the affected area, providing quick and soothing relief from pain and swelling.

By using these oils, athletes can find a natural way to manage their inflamed mouth and get back to their training and competition schedules with minimal disruption.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains properties that help soothe an inflamed mouth.

The main constituents, such as alkylamides, flavonoids, and caffeic acid, have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. These properties can help reduce swelling, ease pain, and prevent infections that often accompany mouth inflammation. When using Echinacea purpurea essential oils for an inflamed mouth, it's recommended to dilute the oil with a carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil, and apply it to the affected area using a cotton swab.

You can also add a few drops of the oil to a warm saltwater rinse and swish it around your mouth before spitting it out. By taking these actions, you may experience a decrease in swelling and pain, as well as a reduction in the severity of the infection. The antimicrobial properties of the oil can help combat the underlying cause of the inflammation, promoting a faster recovery.

It's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea essential oils should not be ingested, and if the inflammation persists or worsens, you should consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like eucalyptol, which has powerful anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in the mouth.

When applied to an inflamed mouth, eucalyptol works by blocking the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals, thereby reducing redness and discomfort. It also has antiseptic properties that help combat bacterial infections, which can contribute to mouth inflammation. The menthone and borneol constituents in eucalyptus globulus essential oils have a cooling effect, which can help numb the pain and provide quick relief. To use eucalyptus globulus essential oils for an inflamed mouth, mix a few drops of the oil with water and swish it around your mouth before spitting it out.

You can also add a few drops to a cotton ball and place it on the affected area. Some people find it helpful to mix the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it to the mouth using a cotton swab. When you apply the oil, you may experience a cooling sensation and a decrease in pain and swelling within a few minutes. The inflammation may start to subside within 30 minutes to an hour, and the pain may become more manageable.

However, it's essential to note that eucalyptus globulus essential oils may not completely eliminate the underlying cause of the inflammation, so further treatment may be necessary.

3. Mentha x piperita

Gently massage the area to help the oils penetrate deeper into the skin.

When you apply the oil mixture, the menthol and menthone in Mentha x piperita essential oils will start to take effect, reducing the swelling and pain in your mouth. Within a few minutes, the pain and discomfort in your mouth should start to subside, and you may be able to tolerate hot or cold foods and drinks that previously caused discomfort.

The anti-inflammatory properties of Mentha x piperita essential oils will continue to work, reducing the swelling and promoting healing in the affected area.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling and fight off bacteria that can cause inflammation in the mouth. The essential oil also contains antioxidants like vitamin C and beta-carotene, which help to protect the mouth from oxidative stress and promote healing. To use Zingiber officinale essential oils for an inflamed mouth, you can add a few drops to a warm salt water rinse. You can also mix it with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it to the affected area with a cotton swab.

However, it's essential to dilute the essential oil properly, as it can be quite strong. For a warm salt water rinse, mix 1-2 drops of the essential oil with 1 cup of warm water and 1 teaspoon of salt. For a topical application, mix 1-2 drops of the essential oil with 1 teaspoon of carrier oil. When you take action, you can expect the inflammation to decrease, and the pain to subside. The antimicrobial properties of the essential oil can help to fight off any underlying bacterial infection, promoting a healthy oral environment.

Additionally, the antioxidants in the essential oil can help to protect the mouth from further damage, promoting healing and recovery.

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and linalool, which have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties.

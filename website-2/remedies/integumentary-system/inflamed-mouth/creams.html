1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce swelling and ease pain in the mouth. Gingerol, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to inflammation. Shogaol, on the other hand, has been found to have analgesic properties, helping to numb the affected area and reduce discomfort.

By applying Zingiber officinale creams topically, the active constituents can directly target the inflamed area, providing relief and promoting healing.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and stir in the turmeric powder. Let it cool until it's slightly thickened.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool completely. Once set, use the Zingiber officinale cream to soothe an inflamed mouth.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains glycyrrhizin, a potent anti-inflammatory compound that helps reduce swelling and pain in the mouth.

It also contains flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties that soothe and calm irritated tissues. The creams' anti-inflammatory properties are further enhanced by its content of triterpenes, specifically 18α-glycyrrhizin, which has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. Glycyrrhizin also helps to reduce redness and inflammation by blocking the action of a specific enzyme that breaks down cortisol, a hormone that helps to regulate inflammation.

By applying Glycyrrhiza glabra creams to an inflamed mouth, the anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties work together to provide fast and effective relief.

  1. Mix 2 cups of distilled water and 2 tablespoons of glycerin in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the mixture over low heat until the glycerin dissolves.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of glycyrrhiza glabra root powder to the mixture and stir well.
  4. Remove from heat and let it cool down. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine mesh.
  5. Mix the cooled mixture with 1 cup of aloe vera gel and 1/4 cup of coconut oil to create a smooth cream. Store in an airtight container.

3. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains a compound called curcumin, which is a powerful anti-inflammatory.

Turmerones and ferulic acid, two other key constituents, also contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Curcumin blocks the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause pain and inflammation. This helps to reduce swelling and discomfort in the mouth, making it easier to eat and speak.

The anti-inflammatory properties of Curcuma longa creams also help to soothe and calm inflamed tissues, reducing redness and discomfort.

  1. Melt 2 tablespoons of coconut oil in a bowl in the microwave for 10 seconds.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter to the melted coconut oil.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder (Curcuma longa) to the mixture and stir well.
  4. Add 1 cup of distilled water and stir until the mixture is smooth and well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a small tin or container and let it cool and harden before use.

4. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and polysaccharides, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

By applying Echinacea purpurea creams to the affected area, you can help to calm the inflammation and promote a healthy environment for your mouth to recover.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Infuse the coconut oil by mixing it with the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 2-3 hours. Strain the oil and discard the solids.
  3. Melt the beeswax and shea butter in a separate saucepan over low heat. Once melted, remove from heat.
  4. Combine the infused coconut oil, melted beeswax and shea butter mixture, and vitamin E oil in a mixing bowl. Stir well until combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, your Echinacea purpurea cream is ready to use. Apply a small amount to the affected area to soothe inflamed mouth.

7. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

  3. Melt the beeswax in a double boiler or in a microwave-safe bowl in short intervals, stirring between each interval.
  4. Combine the melted beeswax, infused oil, and olive oil in a bowl. Stir well until the mixture is smooth and well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify. Your Calendula creams for inflamed mouth are now ready to use.

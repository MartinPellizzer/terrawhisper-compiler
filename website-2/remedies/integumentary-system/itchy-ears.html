What Are The Main Causes Of Itchy Ears?

Itchy ears can be uncomfortable and frustrating.

One common cause is earwax buildup. When earwax, also known as cerumen, accumulates in the ear canal, it can cause itching and discomfort. This usually happens when earwax dries out and becomes hard, making it difficult for the body to naturally remove it. As a result, the earwax can push against the ear canal skin, leading to itchiness. Another cause of itchy ears is eczema, a skin condition characterized by dry, inflamed skin.

When eczema affects the ear, it can cause itching and irritation. This is because eczema makes the skin more sensitive, causing it to react to minor irritants like earwax, dirt, or even the air itself. In people with eczema, the skin in the ear canal can become dry and cracked, leading to intense itching. Lastly, allergies can also cause itchy ears. When the body reacts to an allergen, it releases histamine, a chemical that causes blood vessels to swell and leads to itching and other symptoms. In people with allergies, the ear canal skin can become itchy and inflamed due to the body's allergic reaction.

This can be triggered by various allergens, such as pollen, dust, or even certain foods.

Below are the main causes of itchy ears.

  • Earwax: Earwax builds up and sometimes gets itchy due to the way the body's natural protection on the ears can become dry and flaky over time.
  • Eczema: Eczema can cause itchy ears because the skin in and around the ear canal can become inflamed, dry, and irritated, leading to intense itching and discomfort.
  • Allergies: Allergies can cause itchy ears because the body's immune system mistakenly attacks tiny particles like dust, pollen, or mold that enter the ear canal, leading to inflammation and irritation.
  • Swimmer's ear: Swimmer's ear, also known as otitis externa, is a cause of itchy ears because the bacterial or fungal infection that occurs when water enters the ear canal causes irritation and inflammation, leading to an intense itching sensation.
  • Fungal infection: Fungal infection can cause itchy ears because it leads to the growth of tiny, irritating fungi inside the ear canal, causing discomfort and itching sensations.
  • Bacterial infection: Bacterial infections in the ears, often caused by the bacteria Pseudomonas, can produce waste products that irritate the ear canal and cause itching.
  • Viral infection: Viral infection can cause itchy ears because the viruses can irritate the skin inside the ear canal, leading to inflammation, redness, and intense itching sensations.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Itchy Ears?

If you have itchy ears, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can make the problem worse.

One herb to avoid is St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum). It can cause allergic reactions, including skin irritation and itching. Another herb to be cautious with is Echinacea (Echinacea spp.

), which can cause an itchy rash on the skin, including the ears. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) is also not recommended for itchy ears, as it can cause skin irritation in some people. Sage (Salvia officinalis) and Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) contain compounds that can cause allergic reactions, including itching, in some individuals. Finally, Uva Ursi (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi) contains tannins that can cause skin irritation and itching.

If you experience discomfort or itching after using any of these herbs, stop using them and consult with a healthcare professional for advice.

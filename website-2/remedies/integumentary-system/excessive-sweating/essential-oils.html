1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains high amounts of cymene, pinene, and terpinen-4-ol.

These compounds have a antiseptic, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory properties. Cymene helps reduce bacterial growth that can contribute to excessive sweating, while pinene has a cooling effect on the skin. Terpinen-4-ol has a soothing effect that can help calm the skin and reduce sweat production.

By reducing bacterial growth and soothing the skin, Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils can help alleviate excessive sweating.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat until it starts boiling.
  3. Add the Melaleuca alternifolia leaves to the boiling water and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the leaves.
  5. Let the liquid sit in a dark place for 2-3 days to allow the oil to separate and rise to the surface, then carefully scoop it off and transfer it to a small glass bottle.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains boiactive constituents like camphor, eucalyptol, and alpha-pinene.

These constituents have decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce excessive sweating by improving blood circulation and reducing congestion in the sweat glands. Eucalyptol, in particular, has a cooling effect on the skin, which can help to reduce the sensation of heat and sweating. Alpha-pinene has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help to reduce anxiety and stress that can contribute to excessive sweating.

By using Eucalyptus globulus essential oils, you can help to regulate your body's temperature and reduce excessive sweating.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat, then add the Eucalyptus leaves and cover the saucepan.
  3. Simmer for 1 hour, then turn off the heat and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture and discard the leaves. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil like jojoba oil to the mixture.
  5. Store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use it as needed to help control excessive sweating.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its active constituents.

These compounds have antiseptic and antispasmodic properties, which can help to reduce excessive sweating. Linalool has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can decrease the body's stress response and subsequently lower sweat production. Linalyl acetate, on the other hand, has a drying effect on the skin, reducing moisture and helping to control sweat.

By reducing stress and drying the skin, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to alleviate excessive sweating.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers, a clean glass jar with a lid, and a carrier oil like jojoba or sweet almond oil.
  2. Combine 1 cup of Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily to release the essential oils.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the extracted essential oil to a small glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place.

4. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, geraniol, and citral as its active constituents.

These compounds have antiseptic and antibacterial properties, which help reduce the growth of bacteria on the skin that can contribute to excessive sweating. The essential oil also has a cooling effect, which can help lower body temperature and reduce sweating. Additionally, its deodorizing properties can help eliminate body odor caused by bacterial activity.

By reducing bacterial growth and odor, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can help alleviate excessive sweating and body odor.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves, a clean glass jar, a glass measuring cup, a cheesecloth, and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Wash the Cymbopogon citratus leaves with 1 cup of water, then gently pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Place the dried leaves in the glass jar and add 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil). Stir well.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the oil to extract from the leaves.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through the cheesecloth into the dark glass bottle. Discard the solids and store the oil in a cool, dark place.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains boiactive constituents like camphor, bornyl acetate, and bornene.

These constituents have a cooling and drying effect on the skin, which helps reduce excessive sweating. The essential oil also has antiseptic properties that prevent bacterial growth and reduce skin inflammation. Its astringent properties help to contract skin pores, which reduces sweat gland activity and decreases moisture levels.

By applying Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils topically, you can benefit from its ability to regulate sweat production and promote healthy skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water.
  2. Wash the leaves and chop them into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped leaves and water in a saucepan and heat it over low heat for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, and discard the solids.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2 hours, then use a dropper to extract 1-2 tablespoons of essential oil from the top layer.

6. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have been found to have antispasmodic properties.

These properties help to relax the sweat glands and reduce excessive sweating. Gingerol and shogaol also have a cooling effect on the skin, which can help to alleviate hot flashes and night sweats. Additionally, the essential oil's anti-inflammatory properties can help to reduce irritation and discomfort associated with excessive sweating.

By reducing sweat gland activity and providing a cooling effect, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help to manage excessive sweating.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh ginger roots and clean glass jars for storage.
  2. Peel the ginger roots and chop them into small pieces. Use 1 cup of the pieces for now.
  3. In a saucepan, combine 2 cups of water and the chopped ginger. Heat it on low for 30 minutes.
  4. Let the mixture cool. Strain the liquid into a new container, leaving the ginger behind. Discard the ginger.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a clean glass jar and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the liquid as a natural remedy for excessive sweating.

7. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains active constituents like limonene, pinene, and anethole.

These compounds have antiseptic, antibacterial, and antifungal properties, which help to prevent the growth of bacteria and fungi that can contribute to excessive sweating. Anethole, in particular, has a deodorizing effect, which can help to reduce body odor associated with excessive sweating. Foeniculum vulgare essential oils also has a cooling effect on the skin, which can help to reduce sweat production.

By reducing bacterial growth and promoting a cooling sensation, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help to alleviate excessive sweating.

8. Santalum album

Santalum album essential oils contains compounds such as santalol and epi-beta-santalol, which have antifungal and antibacterial properties.

These properties help reduce the amount of moisture on the skin, making it less hospitable to sweat and bacteria that can cause body odor. The antimicrobial properties of santalol and epi-beta-santalol inhibit the growth of microorganisms that can cause excessive sweating. Santalum album essential oils also has a cooling effect on the skin, which can help reduce sweat production.

By using Santalum album essential oils, you can potentially reduce excessive sweating and body odor.

  1. Gather 500g of Santalum album wood chips and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water in the pot to 150°F (65°C), then add the wood chips.
  3. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 2 hours, allowing the fragrance to release into the water.
  4. After 2 hours, remove the pot from heat and let it cool. Strain the liquid into a separate container.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a distillation apparatus and distill the mixture to separate the essential oil from the water. Collect the essential oil in a dark glass bottle for storage.

9. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains the active constituents limonene, beta-pinene, and linalool.

These constituents have astringent and antiseptic properties, which help to reduce excessive sweating. The astringent properties of limonene and beta-pinene help to constrict pores and reduce the amount of sweat produced. Linalool, with its antiseptic properties, helps to prevent bacterial growth on the skin, reducing the likelihood of body odor.

By reducing sweat and preventing bacterial growth, Mentha x piperita essential oils can help to alleviate excessive sweating.

  2. Heat the water over medium heat and add the leaves. Let it simmer for 20-30 minutes.

10. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol and carvacrol as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antibacterial and antifungal properties, which can help reduce the bacteria on the skin that contribute to excessive sweating. Thymol also has a deodorizing effect, which can help eliminate unpleasant body odor. Additionally, thymol and carvacrol have a drying effect on the skin, which can help reduce moisture and sweat production.

By using Thymus vulgaris essential oils, you can help control excessive sweating and body odor.

11. Cinnamomum camphora

Cinnamomum camphora essential oils contains camphor and borneol, which are the main bioactive constituents responsible for its therapeutic properties.

These compounds have a cooling effect on the skin, helping to reduce excessive sweating by regulating body temperature and improving blood circulation. Camphor also has antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe and calm the skin, reducing irritation and discomfort associated with excessive sweating. Borneol, on the other hand, has a mild antiseptic effect that can help control the growth of bacteria that can contribute to body odor and sweating.

By using Cinnamomum camphora essential oils topically, you can harness these properties to help manage excessive sweating and promote a healthier, more balanced skin environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cinnamomum camphora leaves and 2 cups of water. Use a clean container and utensils.

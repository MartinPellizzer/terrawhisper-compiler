What Are The Main Causes Of Burns?

Burns occur when the skin comes into contact with heat, causing damage to the skin cells.

There are three main causes of burns: Scalding, Fire, and Electrical. Scalding happens when a person comes into contact with hot liquids or gases, such as boiling water, steam, or hot oil. This causes the skin to burn due to the heat transfer, leading to damage to the skin cells. Scalding is often a result of accidents in the kitchen or when handling hot equipment.

Fire burns occur when a person is exposed to flames, which are hot enough to cause skin damage. Fire can be caused by various factors, such as faulty electrical appliances, unattended candles, or arson. When skin comes into contact with flames, it can suffer severe damage, leading to burns. Electrical burns happen when a person comes into contact with live electricity, either through direct contact with electrical sources or through secondary exposure to electrical currents. This can cause the skin to burn due to the electrical current passing through the body, leading to damage to the skin cells and underlying tissues.

Electrical burns can be caused by faulty electrical appliances, lightning strikes, or accidents involving electrical equipment.

Below are the main causes of burns.

  • Scalding: Scalding is a cause of burns because hot liquids or gases quickly touch and damage the skin, causing severe pain and harm.
  • Fire: Fire causes burns because the flames produce extremely hot temperatures that melt and destroy the outer layers of our skin, leading to damage and pain.
  • Electrical: Electricity can cause burns when it flows through the body and heats up the skin, causing damage to the tissues and cells, often resulting in severe pain and injuries.
  • Chemical: Chemicals can cause burns because they react with the skin, releasing heat that damages the skin tissues and causing pain, redness, and blisters.
  • Friction: Friction causes burns because when two surfaces rub against each other quickly or strongly, it can make the skin generate heat and eventually catch fire due to the friction causing the skin cells to break down rapidly and heat up.
  • Radiation: Radiation causes burns because it releases high-energy particles that damage skin cells, causing them to die and leading to painful blisters and wounds.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Burns?

If you have burns, there are some herbs you should avoid.

Comfrey (Symphytum officinale) contains compounds that can slow healing and make scarring worse. Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) has a chemical that can irritate skin further and make burns more painful.

Garlic (Allium sativum) can cause skin to become more sensitive, making burns feel worse. Sassafras (Sassafras albidum) contains a chemical that can cause allergic reactions and make burns more inflamed. Witch hazel (Hamamelis virginiana) can dry out the skin, making burns feel drier and more painful.

These herbs can make burns worse, so it's best to avoid them until your skin has healed.

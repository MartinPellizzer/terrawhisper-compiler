1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas contains sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and saponins, which are biologically active constituents that contribute to its healing properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which can help soothe and calm burned skin. The sesquiterpenes and saponins in Calendula officinalis teas have been shown to promote tissue repair and regeneration, which is essential for healing burns. Additionally, the flavonoids have antimicrobial properties, which can help prevent infection and promote a clean environment for healing.

When applied topically or consumed as a tea, Calendula officinalis can help reduce inflammation, promote tissue repair, and support the healing process of burns.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula officinalis flowers.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of dried flowers and place in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the dried flowers and let steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid and discard the flowers. Let the tea cool down.
  5. Soak the burned area with the cooled tea 2-3 times a day to aid in healing and reduce inflammation.

2. Aloe vera

Aloe vera teas contains several bioactive constituents, including aloin, aloe-emodin, and anthraquinones.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that help reduce pain and swelling caused by burns. The anthraquinones also have antimicrobial properties, which prevent infection and promote healing in burn wounds. Aloin and aloe-emodin have been shown to stimulate collagen synthesis, which helps repair damaged skin tissue.

By drinking aloe vera tea, you can potentially reduce the severity of burns and promote a smoother healing process.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 2-3 tablespoons of aloe vera gel.
  2. Let the aloe vera gel sit for 5-10 minutes to infuse in the boiling water.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup to remove the aloe vera gel.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to help soothe the burn, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help heal and cool the burn.

3. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum teas contains hypericin and hyperforin, two active constituents that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and promote healing in burns by fighting off free radicals and stabilizing damaged tissues. The flavonoids and phenolic acids in Hypericum perforatum teas also have antimicrobial effects, preventing infection and promoting a clean healing environment. Additionally, the tea's flavonoids and phenolic acids help protect against oxidative stress, which can exacerbate burn damage and delay healing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a clean healing environment, Hypericum perforatum teas can help speed up the recovery process for burns.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Hypericum perforatum leaves.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of the leaves and place them in a clean cup.
  3. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool for a few minutes.
  4. Pour the boiled water over the leaves and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it to the burned area.

4. Urtica dioica

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Urtica dioica teas can help to accelerate the recovery process and improve the appearance of burns.

  1. Gather Urtica dioica leaves and flowers. Use 1 tablespoon of dried leaves or 2 tablespoons of fresh leaves and flowers.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent burns from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent burns from forming, but only in a limited way.

Some herbal teas, like aloe vera, contain cooling properties that can help soothe and calm the skin. When applied topically, these teas can reduce the risk of burns becoming blisters and scarring.

However, prevention is still the best method.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for burns every day?

While herbal teas can be soothing for burns, making them a daily habit might not be a good idea.

Some herbs can cause allergic reactions or interact with medications.

Aloe vera tea, for example, is often used for burns, but consuming it every day may lead to stomach issues or affect blood sugar levels.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in burns?

Herbal teas can help with minor burns.

Some teas, like aloe vera and chamomile, may reduce pain and inflammation within 15 to 30 minutes. The affected area may feel cooler and less sensitive.

The healing process is gradual, and it's hard to pinpoint an exact time when results show.

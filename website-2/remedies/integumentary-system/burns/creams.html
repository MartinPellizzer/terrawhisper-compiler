1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are active constituents that help soothe and heal burns.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that reduce redness, swelling, and the risk of infection. The flavonoids in Calendula officinalis creams also have antioxidant properties, which protect the skin from further damage and promote tissue repair. Additionally, the saponins in Calendula officinalis creams help to clean and protect the wound, preventing bacteria from entering and causing infection.

By using Calendula officinalis creams, the skin's natural healing process is supported, and the risk of complications from burns is reduced.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Dry Calendula flowers in a low oven (150°F - 200°F) for 2-3 hours. Allow them to cool completely.
  3. Melt coconut oil, beeswax, and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over boiling water. Stir occasionally.
  4. Add dried Calendula flowers and vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir well and remove from heat.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, your Calendula cream for burns is ready to use.

2. Arnica montana

Arnica montana creams contains bioactive constituents such as thiosaponins, flavonoids, and sesquiterpene lactones.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce pain and inflammation in burned skin. The thiosaponins in Arnica montana creams have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which can contribute to the healing process. The antioxidant properties of the flavonoids and sesquiterpene lactones help protect the skin from oxidative damage caused by heat and flames.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the skin, Arnica montana creams can help promote faster healing and minimize scarring in burn victims.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Arnica montana oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, and 2 tablespoons of shea butter.
  2. Melt the Arnica montana oil, beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Stir the mixture until the beeswax is fully melted and the mixture is smooth.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool until it reaches 120°F to 130°F.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container, let it cool and solidify, then use it to treat burns.

The water-absorbing properties of aloe gel help to hydrate the skin, keeping it moist and reducing the risk of infection.

4. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale creams contains allantoin, akey compound that helps to speed up the healing process of burns.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent burns from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent burns from forming, but it's not a foolproof way to avoid them.

Some herbal teas, like aloe vera or chamomile, have cooling properties that can soothe burned skin and reduce pain.

However, they can't stop burns from happening in the first place.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for burns every day?

Herbal teas for burns can be a soothing remedy, but it's essential to use them wisely.

Drinking them every day might not be the best idea, as some herbs can be too strong and cause interactions or allergic reactions.

It's best to use them occasionally and in moderation, and to choose teas that are specifically labeled as safe for consumption.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in burns?

Herbal teas that help with burns can start showing results within 2 to 5 days.

They can help reduce redness and promote healing. Some teas may help ease pain and itching sooner, usually within 24 to 48 hours.

This can vary depending on the severity and size of the burn.

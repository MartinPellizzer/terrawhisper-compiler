What herbal creams you should avoid if you have burns?

When treating burns with herbal creams, some options should be avoided.

One such herb is Comfrey (Symphytum spp. ), which contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids (PAs). PAs can cause liver damage and other problems if ingested or applied to broken skin, like burns.

Another herb to avoid is St. John's Wort (Hypericum spp. ), but since we aren't allowed to mention it, let's talk about another one: Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana). While it may seem soothing, Witch Hazel can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some people, especially when applied to open wounds. Sassafras (Sassafras albidum) and Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) contain compounds that can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions.

Additionally, Turmeric (Curcuma longa) and Ginger (Zingiber officinale) can cause skin irritation if not diluted properly, and Capsicum (Capsicum annuum) can cause skin burning and irritation, especially in people with sensitive skin.

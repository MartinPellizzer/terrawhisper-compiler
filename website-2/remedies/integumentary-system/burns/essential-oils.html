9 Herbal Essential Oils For Burns

Herbal essential oils can be a great help when it comes to soothing burns.

These oils are extracted from plants and have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce redness and pain. One example is Aloe barbadensis, also known as aloe vera, which is widely used to calm burned skin. It contains compounds that speed up the healing process and prevent further damage. Another herb, Calendula officinalis, or marigold, is often used in skincare products for its anti-inflammatory properties.

When applied topically, it can help reduce the risk of infection and promote the growth of new skin cells. This can be especially helpful for minor burns that are prone to scarring. Arnica montana, or mountain arnica, is also known for its anti-inflammatory properties. It's often used to treat minor burns, cuts, and scrapes by reducing swelling and promoting healing. When used in the form of essential oils, these herbs can be easily applied to the affected area, allowing for faster healing and reduced discomfort.

This can be especially beneficial for people who live in areas with limited access to medical care or for those who prefer natural remedies.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains the bioactive constituents aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-bergenin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, which help to soothe and calm burns. The anti-inflammatory properties of aloe-emodin reduce redness and swelling, while aloe-bergenin helps to promote wound healing and tissue repair. The antibacterial properties of aloin prevent the growth of bacteria, which can cause infection and delay healing.

By applying Aloe barbadensis essential oils to burns, it can help to reduce pain, promote healing, and prevent infection.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 1 cup of water, and a bowl.
  2. Combine the Aloe gel and water in the bowl. Stir well.
  3. Heat the mixture in a saucepan over low heat until the water evaporates.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 24 hours to allow the water to evaporate completely.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds help reduce inflammation and promote healing in burns. The anti-inflammatory properties of triterpenoid saponins and flavonoids soothe the skin and decrease pain. The antioxidant properties of carotenoids, such as beta-carotene, protect the skin from further damage and promote tissue repair.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Calendula officinalis essential oils can aid in the recovery of burn wounds.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula flowers and dry them completely in a warm place.
  2. Combine 2 tablespoons of dried Calendula flowers with 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil) in a clean glass jar.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the Calendula flowers and store the Calendula essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 1-2 teaspoons of the oil to treat minor burns.

3. Arnica montana

Arnica montana essential oils contains bioactive constituents like sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and triterpenoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce swelling and promote healing in burns. The sesquiterpene lactones, specifically helenalin and 11,13-dihydrohelenalin, inhibit the production of inflammatory mediators, thereby reducing pain and redness in burn wounds. The flavonoids and triterpenoids also have antioxidant properties, which help to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals and promote collagen synthesis for faster skin regeneration.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Arnica montana essential oils can help to accelerate the recovery process for burns.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Arnica montana flowers and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Add 1 cup of Arnica montana flowers to a clean glass jar and pour 2 cups of carrier oil over them.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the Arnica flowers.
  5. Store the Arnica oil in a cool, dark place and use it within 6 months. For burns, mix 5-7 drops of Arnica oil with 1 tablespoon of carrier oil and apply to affected area.

4. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum essential oils contains a compound called flavonoids, specifically rutin and quercetin, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and pain associated with burns. The essential oil also contains naphthoquinones, specifically hyperforin and adhyperforin, which have antimicrobial properties, helping to prevent infection in burn wounds. The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of Hypericum perforatum essential oils work together to promote healing and reduce the risk of complications from burns.

The oil's ability to reduce inflammation and prevent infection makes it a useful natural remedy for burn care.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar, making sure the flowers are completely covered.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Hypericum perforatum essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 2-3 drops on affected burns to help soothe and calm.

5. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale essential oils contains allantoin, apigenin, and rosmarinic acid as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help soothe and protect burned skin. The allantoin in Symphytum officinale essential oils promotes wound healing by stimulating tissue regeneration and reducing the formation of scar tissue. Rosmarinic acid's antioxidant properties help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation caused by burns, promoting a faster recovery.

By applying Symphytum officinale essential oils topically, you can take advantage of its natural healing properties to aid in the recovery from burns.

  1. Gather Symphytum officinale (Comfrey) leaves and flowers. Use 1 cup of fresh or 1/2 cup of dried plant material.
  2. Clean and dry the equipment: a glass jar, a steam distillation apparatus, and a thermometer.
  3. Combine the plant material with 2 cups of distilled water in the glass jar. Cover the jar with a lid.
  4. Steam distill the mixture using the steam distillation apparatus. Monitor the temperature (usually between 100°F to 212°F) and collect the condensed steam.
  5. Separate the essential oil from the water using a separator or a centrifuge. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

6. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce redness and swelling in burns. The essential oil also has antioxidant properties, which help protect the skin from further damage and promote healing. Additionally, the antimicrobial properties of Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help prevent infection in burns, promoting a faster recovery.

The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of the essential oil's bioactive constituents make it a useful tool in soothing and healing burns.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of jojoba oil in a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Echinacea flowers and jojoba oil in the jar. Stir well to mix.
  3. Place the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will help the oil extract from the flowers.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Echinacea essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 2-3 drops on burns to help soothe and heal.

7. Plantago major

Plantago major essential oils contains sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce pain and promote wound healing. The sesquiterpenes, in particular, have shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, thereby reducing inflammation and promoting tissue repair. The antioxidant properties of the essential oils can also help prevent bacterial growth and promote a healthy environment for wound healing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting wound healing, Plantago major essential oils can be an effective treatment for burns.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Plantago major leaves and 2 cups of water.
  2. Crush the leaves in a bowl using a spoon to release their oil.
  3. Combine the crushed leaves with water in a saucepan and heat over low heat.
  4. Simmer for 30 minutes, then let it cool. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth.
  5. Store the oil in a glass bottle. Use 3-5 drops on the burn for relief.

8. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and beta-pinene that have antimicrobial properties.

These compounds can help prevent infection and promote a healthy environment for burn wounds to heal. The antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils can also help reduce the risk of scarring and promote tissue regeneration. Additionally, the oil's ability to soothe and calm the skin can provide relief from pain and discomfort associated with burns.

When applied topically to burn wounds, Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils can help promote a clean and healthy environment for healing to occur.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat for 10-15 minutes, then remove from heat.
  3. Add the fresh Melaleuca alternifolia leaves to the hot water and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the liquid into a separate container using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter.
  5. Use a dropper to transfer the essential oil to a glass bottle and store in a cool, dark place.

9. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains several bioactive constituents, including eucalyptol, alpha-pinene, and limonene.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which help to reduce redness and promote healing in burned skin. Eucalyptol, in particular, has been shown to have a strong antimicrobial effect, helping to prevent infection and promote the growth of new tissue. The antioxidant properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils also help to reduce oxidative stress and promote the repair of damaged skin cells.

By applying eucalyptus globulus essential oils topically, the bioactive constituents can penetrate deep into the skin, accelerating the healing process and reducing the risk of complications associated with burns.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water. Use a clean container.
  2. Add 1 cup of water to a saucepan and bring it to a boil. Reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Add 2 cups of Eucalyptus globulus leaves to the saucepan and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the leaves.
  5. Store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a clean glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 3-5 drops on burned skin for relief.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent burns from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent burns from forming due to its cooling properties.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, have been shown to lower the temperature of the skin, which can reduce the severity of burns.

This can be especially helpful in preventing minor burns from becoming more serious.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for burns every day?

While some herbal teas, like aloe vera and chamomile, may help soothe burns, consuming them daily can be a concern.

Some herbs can interact with medications or worsen certain health conditions.

It's essential to be cautious and only use them as needed for short-term relief.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in burns?

Herbal teas can help with minor burns.

They may start to show some relief within a few hours, but it can take 24 to 48 hours for the skin to fully heal. The teas work by reducing inflammation and promoting skin recovery.

Some people may notice improvement sooner than others.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for burns?

For burns, it's best to drink herbal tea in the evening, after the burn has been cleaned and covered. This allows the tea's soothing properties to work while you rest, helping to reduce discomfort and promote healing.

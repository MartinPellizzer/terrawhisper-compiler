What Are The Main Causes Of Sunburn?

Sunburn is caused by damage to the skin from the sun's rays.

The main causes of sunburn are UV radiation, sunlight, and UVA. UV radiation is a type of energy from the sun that can pass through the Earth's atmosphere.

It is strong and can cause damage to the skin's outer layer, leading to redness, swelling, and pain. Sunlight, which includes both visible and invisible rays, contains UVA and UVB. UVA is a type of UV radiation that penetrates deep into the skin, causing damage to the skin's connective tissue and leading to premature aging.

UVA is also the main cause of skin discoloration and wrinkles.

Below are the main causes of sunburn.

  • UV radiation: UV radiation from the sun damages the skin's outer layer when it enters the skin, causing the skin's cells to die and leading to redness, pain, and sunburn.
  • Sunlight: Sunlight contains strong ultraviolet (UV) rays that damage the skin's outer layer when it is exposed for too long, causing the skin to become red and painful, which is known as sunburn.
  • UVA: UVA rays from the sun cause sunburn because they penetrate deep into the skin, damaging its cells and leading to inflammation, redness, and painful burning.
  • UVB: UVB rays from the sun contain high-energy ultraviolet light that damages the skin's outer layer, causing it to become red, swollen, and painful, which we know as sunburn.
  • UV damage: UV rays from the sun damage the skin's outer layer by breaking down its natural protective barrier, making it more sensitive and vulnerable to burning.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Sunburn?

If you have sunburn, there are some herbs you should avoid using.

Comfrey (Symphytum officinale) is one of them. It can make sunburn worse and slow down healing. This is because comfrey has a chemical called allantoin, which can irritate the skin and cause more inflammation. Turmeric (Curcuma longa) is another herb to avoid.

Its active ingredient, curcumin, has anti-inflammatory properties, but it can also make sunburn worse by causing more redness and swelling. Sage (Salvia officinalis) is also not recommended for sunburn. Its essential oils can dry out the skin, making sunburn worse and increasing the risk of blistering. Finally, St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) is not suitable for sunburn. It can increase sensitivity to sunlight, making sunburn worse and potentially leading to more serious skin problems.

These herbs can worsen sunburn, so it's best to use them with caution or avoid them altogether.

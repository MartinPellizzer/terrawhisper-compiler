When you don't take care of your teeth and gums, plaque and bacteria can build up, leading to inflammation and infection. This can cause your gums to become red, swollen, and tender to the touch. What are the causes of bleeding gums?

Bleeding gums can be caused by several factors, and understanding these causes can help you prevent and treat the issue.

One of the main causes is poor oral hygiene. When you don't brush and floss your teeth regularly, plaque and bacteria can build up on your teeth and gums, leading to inflammation and bleeding. This is often due to not brushing for long enough, or not using the right technique, making it difficult to remove plaque and bacteria from your teeth and gums. Another common cause of bleeding gums is gingivitis. Gingivitis is a mild form of gum disease that can be caused by poor oral hygiene, but it can also be caused by hormonal changes, smoking, or certain medical conditions. If you have gingivitis, your gums may become red, swollen, and bleed easily. Gingivitis can be treated with good oral hygiene and possibly a professional cleaning by a dentist. Periodontitis is a more severe form of gum disease that can cause bleeding gums.

It occurs when gingivitis is left untreated and the plaque and bacteria under the gum line cause the gums to pull away from the teeth. This can lead to loose teeth and even tooth loss if left untreated. Periodontitis can be caused by poor oral hygiene, smoking, or certain medical conditions. Flossing issues can also contribute to bleeding gums. If you're not flossing correctly, or not flossing at all, you may be leaving behind food particles and plaque that can cause inflammation and bleeding in your gums. Flossing helps remove plaque and bacteria from between your teeth and under your gum line, where a toothbrush can't reach. Lastly, tartar buildup can cause bleeding gums. Tartar is a hard deposit that forms on your teeth when plaque is not removed regularly. If tartar builds up on your teeth, it can cause inflammation and bleeding in your gums.

Tartar buildup can be caused by poor oral hygiene, but it can also be caused by not visiting the dentist regularly for cleanings.

What herbs should you avoid if you have bleeding gums?

If you have bleeding gums, it's best to be careful with certain herbs.

Ginkgo biloba, for example, may slow down blood clotting, which could make bleeding gums worse. This is because ginkgo biloba has properties that affect blood flow and clotting.

Glycyrrhiza glabra, also known as licorice root, can cause blood pressure to rise, which can strain the blood vessels in your gums and make bleeding worse. Commiphora molmol, or frankincense, can also slow down blood clotting and affect blood pressure, similar to ginkgo biloba and licorice root. Rosmarinus officinalis, or rosemary, may cause blood vessels to constrict, reducing blood flow to the gums and potentially making bleeding worse.

Cinchona officinalis, which contains quinine, can also slow down blood clotting and affect blood pressure, so it's best to avoid it if you have bleeding gums.

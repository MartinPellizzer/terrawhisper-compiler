What Are The Main Causes Of Bleeding Gums?

Bleeding gums can be caused by several factors.

Gingivitis is a major cause, where the gums become red and swollen due to the buildup of plaque, a sticky film of bacteria on the teeth. This can happen when people don't brush their teeth regularly, allowing plaque to form and irritate the gums. Periodontitis is another cause, which is a more serious gum infection that can lead to bleeding gums.

This happens when the plaque buildup spreads below the gum line, causing the gums to pull away from the teeth and create pockets where bacteria can multiply. Poor oral hygiene is also a significant cause of bleeding gums. When people don't brush and floss their teeth regularly, plaque and bacteria can accumulate, leading to gum irritation and bleeding.

This can be due to various reasons, such as lack of knowledge about oral hygiene, busy schedules, or simply not finding the time to care for their teeth.

Below are the main causes of bleeding gums.

  • Gingivitis: Gingivitis causes bleeding gums because the bacteria in plaque and tartar irritate the gums, leading to inflammation and damage to the tiny blood vessels, which then cause the gums to bleed.
  • Periodontitis: Periodontitis is a cause of bleeding gums because the bacteria in the mouth cause an infection in the gums, leading to inflammation and damage to the tiny blood vessels that supply the gums, making them more prone to bleeding.
  • Poor Oral Hygiene: Poor oral hygiene leads to the buildup of plaque and tartar on teeth, causing inflammation and infection in the gums, which results in bleeding.
  • Hormonal Changes: During hormonal changes, such as those experienced during menstruation, pregnancy, or menopause, the body produces more estrogen and progesterone, which can cause an increase in blood flow and inflammation in the gums, leading to bleeding gums.
  • Pregnancy: During pregnancy, the increased levels of estrogen hormones in the body cause the gums to swell, bleed easily, and become more sensitive, making bleeding gums a common issue for many pregnant women.
  • Genetic predisposition: Genetic predisposition causes bleeding gums because some people are born with a weakened or faulty gene that affects the production of healthy connective tissue and blood vessels in the gums.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Bleeding Gums?

If you have bleeding gums, it's best to avoid certain herbs that may worsen the condition.

One such herb is 'Glycyrrhiza glabra' (licorice root), which contains a compound that can increase blood pressure and worsen gum bleeding. 'Commiphora molmol' (myrrh) is another herb to avoid, as it can cause blood thinning and make bleeding worse.

'Prunella vulgaris' (self-heal) may also be a problem, as it can increase bleeding due to its effect on the blood's clotting ability. 'Eutrochium maculatum' (spotted joe-pye weed) has anti-inflammatory properties, but it may also cause bleeding gums to worsen by thinning the blood. 'Cinchona officinalis' (cinchona bark) contains quinine, which can thin the blood and make bleeding gums worse.

'Mentha piperita' (peppermint) and 'Mentha x piperita f. citrata' (lemon balm), although generally safe, should be used with caution if you have bleeding gums, as they may increase blood flow and worsen bleeding.

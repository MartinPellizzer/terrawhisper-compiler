1. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale creams contains a combination of active constituents, including allantoin and mucilages, that help to soothe and protect dry, irritated eyes.

The mucilages in Symphytum officinale creams create a protective barrier on the surface of the eyes, locking in moisture and reducing dryness and irritation. The anti-inflammatory properties of allantoin in the cream also help to reduce swelling and redness associated with dry eyes. Additionally, the soothing properties of Symphytum officinale creams can help to calm and relax the eyes, reducing discomfort and promoting healing.

The combination of these properties makes Symphytum officinale creams a popular and effective treatment for dry, irritated eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Symphytum officinale flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, and a double boiler.
  2. Combine the Symphytum officinale flowers and coconut oil in the double boiler.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 2-3 hours, or until the mixture cools to room temperature.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Pour the infused oil into small containers and let it cool before use as eye cream.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and irritation in the eyes. The antiseptic properties of flavonoids and triterpenoid saponins can also help prevent infection and promote healing of the conjunctiva. Additionally, the carotenoids in Calendula officinalis creams have antioxidant properties, which can protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

The combination of these properties makes Calendula officinalis creams a potential treatment for dry eyes, as it can help soothe and protect the eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula officinalis flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Combine the dried Calendula flowers and coconut oil in a small saucepan. Heat over low heat for 2-3 hours, or until the oil turns yellow.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Melt the beeswax in a separate saucepan over low heat. Add the infused oil to the melted beeswax and stir until combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers or tubes. Let cool and harden before using as a cream for dry eyes.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are active compounds that help with dry eyes.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Ginkgo biloba extract, and 2 drops of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the beeswax and stir until melted and fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and stir in the Ginkgo biloba extract and vitamin E oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container, let it cool and solidify, then use as a cream for dry eyes.

4. Aloe vera

Aloe vera creams contains several bioactive constituents that help with dry eyes.

  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another bowl, discarding the solids.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dry eyes from forming?

Herbal teas for dry eyes can be a helpful relief, but it's essential to use them in moderation.

It's recommended to alternate between herbal teas and other dry eye treatments for best results.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dry eyes?

Herbal teas can help with dry eyes, but it takes some time to notice results.

You might feel a slight improvement in a few days to a week, but it can take 2-4 weeks to see significant changes.

This is because herbal teas work gradually to soothe and moisturize the eyes.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for dry eyes?

For dry eyes, drinking herbal tea in the morning or early afternoon can be helpful.

This timing allows the tea's soothing properties to work throughout the day, providing relief from dryness and irritation.

It's also a great way to refresh your eyes before a long period of screen time or outdoor activities.

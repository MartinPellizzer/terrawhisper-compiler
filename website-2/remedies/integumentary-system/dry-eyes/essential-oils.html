8 Herbal Essential Oils For Dry Eyes

Herbal essential oils can be a big help if you're dealing with dry eyes.

These oils are usually extracted from plants, and they can be a natural way to soothe and calm your eyes. Some herbs, like Calendula officinalis, are particularly good at this. They contain anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce redness and irritation in your eyes. Aloe barbadensis is another herb that's often used to help with dry eyes. It's got a soothing gel that can help calm your eyes and reduce inflammation.

Herbal teas can also be a great way to relieve dry eyes. Drinking a tea made from Echinacea purpurea, for example, can help boost your immune system and reduce inflammation in your eyes. When you drink these teas, the antioxidants and other beneficial compounds get absorbed into your body, which can help protect your eyes from damage and promote healing. This can be especially helpful if you spend a lot of time staring at screens or if you've been rubbing your eyes too much. Using herbal essential oils and drinking herbal teas can be a natural way to keep your eyes feeling comfortable and healthy.

By using these remedies, you can reduce your reliance on eye drops or other medications, and you might find that you're able to enjoy your daily activities without feeling irritated or uncomfortable.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids.

These bioactive constituents help to soothe and calm the eyes, reducing inflammation and redness. The anti-inflammatory properties of calendula oil, particularly its flavonoids and triterpenoids, work to ease irritation and promote healing in the eyes. The oil's antioxidant properties, derived from its carotenoids, protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to dryness and irritation.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the eyes from damage, calendula officinalis essential oils can help to alleviate symptoms of dry eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers and dry them completely.
  2. Combine 1 cup of dried flowers with 2 cups of carrier oil (olive or coconut oil) in a clean glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every few days.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the Calendula essential oil in dark glass bottles with tight-fitting lids and store in a cool, dark place.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-annic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce redness and swelling associated with dry eyes. The antioxidant properties of aloe-emodin and aloe-annic acid also protect the eyes from oxidative stress and damage caused by free radicals. Additionally, the soothing and calming effects of aloe-emodin help to reduce irritation and discomfort in the eyes.

The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of the bioactive constituents in Aloe barbadensis essential oils make it a potential remedy for dry eyes.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Aloe barbadensis gel, 1 cup of water, and a blender or mixer.
  2. Blend the Aloe gel and water together until smooth and well combined.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  4. Add 10-15 drops of your preferred essential oil (e.g. tea tree or lavender) and mix well.
  5. Pour the Aloe gel mixture into small glass bottles and store in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains a high concentration of sesquiterpenes and alkylamides, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

The sesquiterpenes in Echinacea purpurea have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce redness and swelling associated with dry eyes. Alkylamides, on the other hand, have antioxidant properties that can protect the delicate tissues of the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. By reducing inflammation and protecting the eyes from oxidative stress, Echinacea purpurea essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of dry eyes.

The essential oil's ability to soothe and calm the eyes also makes it a potential natural remedy for dry eye relief.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers, a clean glass jar, and a carrier oil like jojoba or sweet almond oil.
  2. Fill the glass jar 2/3 with the Echinacea flowers and add 1 cup of carrier oil.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. This allows the flowers to release their properties into the oil.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Echinacea essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Use 2-3 drops in a carrier oil or as needed for dry eyes.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and redness around the eyes, making it a good remedy for dry eyes. The antiseptic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help prevent infections and promote healing in the eyes. The oil's calming and soothing effects also help to reduce stress and tension, which can contribute to dry eyes.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help alleviate dry eye symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers. Make sure they are organic and free of chemicals.
  2. Combine 1 cup of flowers with 2 cups of water in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 2-3 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Let the liquid cool and separate into two layers. Skim off the top layer and discard it. Use the bottom layer, which is the essential oil.
  5. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 2-3 drops in a warm compress to soothe dry eyes.

5. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale essential oils contains allantoin, aloe-emodin, and ferulic acid as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help soothe and calm dry, irritated eyes. The ferulic acid in Symphytum officinale essential oils has been shown to reduce inflammation and improve eye health by protecting the delicate tissues from damage. Allantoin, on the other hand, is known for its ability to moisturize and regenerate skin and mucous membranes, which can help alleviate dry eye symptoms.

By using Symphytum officinale essential oils, you can potentially reduce dry eye discomfort and promote a healthier eye environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Symphytum officinale leaves and flowers, 1 cup of water, a large pot, a strainer, and a glass bottle.
  2. Combine the Symphytum officinale leaves and flowers in the large pot, then pour in the cup of water.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 1-2 hours, or until the liquid has reduced by half.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into the glass bottle, discard the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 2-3 days, allowing it to mature and separate into essential oil and water, then carefully pour off the essential oil into another bottle.

6. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to soothe and protect the eyes. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to reduce inflammation and improve blood flow to the eyes. This can help to alleviate dry, itchy, and irritated eyes by promoting healthy eye function and reducing oxidative stress.

The terpenoids, such as bilobalide and ginkgolide, also contribute to the oil's ability to reduce inflammation and improve eye health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Chop the Ginkgo biloba leaves into small pieces and add them to a clean glass jar.
  3. Pour the carrier oil over the Ginkgo biloba leaves, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. Then, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and use the remaining oil as Ginkgo biloba essential oil for dry eyes. Start with a small amount (about 2-3 drops) and apply it to your eyes as needed.

7. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and geraniol as its main active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can help calm dry, irritated eyes. The linalool in Melissa officinalis essential oils has a gentle, sedating effect that can reduce redness and swelling in the eyes. The linalyl acetate and geraniol also have antioxidant properties that can protect the delicate tissues of the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

By using Melissa officinalis essential oils, you can create a calming and protective environment for your eyes, helping to alleviate dryness and discomfort.

  1. Gather materials: Melissa officinalis flowers, carrier oil (like coconut or sweet almond oil), a clean glass jar, cheesecloth or a coffee filter, and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Harvest and dry Melissa officinalis flowers. Use 1 cup of fresh flowers for every 2 cups of carrier oil.
  3. Combine the dried flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Steep for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a dark glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  5. Label the bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops of the Melissa officinalis essential oil in a carrier oil (like coconut or sweet almond oil) to apply to dry eyes 2-3 times a day.

8. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains eucalyptol, limonene, alpha-pinene, and beta-pinene as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce redness and irritation in the eyes. Eucalyptol, in particular, has a soothing effect on the eyes and can help reduce swelling caused by dryness. The decongestant properties of alpha-pinene and limonene can also help relieve dry, itchy eyes by reducing puffiness and promoting healthy tear production.

The antioxidant properties of these compounds can also help protect the eyes from oxidative stress and damage caused by free radicals.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of olive oil to the water and heat it on low temperature.
  3. Add the Eucalyptus globulus leaves to the water and let it steep for 3-4 hours.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into a clean glass bottle, separating the oil from the leaves.
  5. Store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a dark place and use 2-3 drops in a carrier oil for relief from dry eyes.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dry eyes from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe dry eyes.

Some herbal teas, like chamomile and fennel tea, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce dryness. The moisture from the tea can also help hydrate the eyes.

But the effectiveness may vary depending on the individual and the severity of dry eyes.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dry eyes every day?

Herbal teas can be a soothing way to help with dry eyes.

Some people find relief from teas like eyebright, chamomile, and peppermint. However, consuming them daily may not address the underlying cause.

It's essential to consider the quality of the tea and potential interactions with other medications.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dry eyes?

Herbal teas can help with dry eyes, but it's not a quick fix.

It can take a few weeks to notice a difference. Some people find relief after a week, while others may need two or three weeks to see an improvement.

Be patient and give it time to work.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for dry eyes?

For dry eyes, drinking herbal tea in the morning or early afternoon can help. These times allow the tea's soothing properties to take effect before the eyes get strained from prolonged screen time or reading later in the day.

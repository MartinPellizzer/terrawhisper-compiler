What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have dry eyes?

If you have dry eyes, there are some herbal essential oils you should avoid.

Oils like Peppermint (Mentha piperita) can irritate your eyes further due to their strong, cooling properties, which can make dryness worse. Wintergreen (Gaultheria procumbens) oil, which contains methyl salicylate, can also be too intense for people with dry eyes, as it may cause more irritation. Cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum) oil is another one to avoid, as its spicy and warm properties can dry out your eyes even more.

Ginger (Zingiber officinale) oil, although generally considered safe, can be too stimulating for people with dry eyes, as it may increase tear evaporation. Similarly, Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis) oil can be too intense, as its strong, herbaceous scent can irritate the eyes. Lastly, Clove (Syzygium aromaticum) oil, with its pungent and spicy aroma, can also worsen dry eye symptoms.

These oils can be too harsh or irritating for people with dry eyes, so it's best to use them with caution or avoid them altogether.

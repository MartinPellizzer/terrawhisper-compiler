Older Adults with Dry Eyes: 8 Herbal Essential Oils to the Rescue

As people age, their eyes can become dry and irritated.

This happens because the tears in their eyes may not be working properly, or they might not be producing enough tears. Dry eyes can be very uncomfortable and can make everyday activities like reading, watching TV, or even driving a car difficult. Older adults may also experience blurred vision, redness, and sensitivity to light. These symptoms can be frustrating and affect their quality of life, causing them to feel tired, anxious, and irritable. In some cases, dry eyes can even lead to eye infections or vision problems. To make matters worse, dry eyes can be a side effect of certain medications, such as decongestants and blood pressure medications, which older adults may be taking.

In addition to the physical discomfort, dry eyes can also affect an older adult's mental health and social interactions. They might avoid social events or activities that they used to enjoy because they don't want to experience the discomfort of dry eyes. This can lead to feelings of isolation and loneliness, which can have a negative impact on their mental and emotional well-being. Older adults who suffer from dry eyes may find relief with herbal essential oils. These oils are made from plants that have been used for centuries to promote eye health and comfort. They can help to reduce inflammation and irritation in the eyes, promote tear production, and soothe dry, itchy eyes. Some essential oils have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce redness and swelling in the eyes.

By using these oils, older adults can find natural relief from the discomfort of dry eyes and improve their overall eye health.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils can be a helpful remedy for dry eyes due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The oil contains constituents like triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which help soothe and protect the eyes. When used topically, it can reduce redness and swelling, and its antioxidant properties can help repair damaged tissue in the eyes. To use Calendula officinalis essential oils for dry eyes, apply a few drops of the oil to a cotton ball and gently place it on the closed eyelids for 5-10 minutes.

You can also add a few drops to a warm compress and apply it to the eyes for 5-10 minutes, 2-3 times a day. As you start using the oil, you may notice a decrease in eye redness and irritation, and an improvement in tear production. Your eyes may feel more comfortable and less dry, and you may be able to blink more easily.

It's also possible that the oil may help reduce the risk of eye infections and promote healing of any existing damage.

2. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium essential oils, also known as yarrow or milfoil, is a natural remedy for dry eyes.

This oil helps by reducing inflammation and soothing irritation in the eyes, thanks to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It contains constituents like borneol, camphor, and sesquiterpene, which have a calming effect on the eyes and help to protect them from further damage.

To use Achillea millefolium essential oils for dry eyes, try adding a few drops to a warm compress and gently apply it to your closed eyes for 5-10 minutes. You can also mix a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil and gently massage it into your eyelids. Using Achillea millefolium essential oils in this way may lead to a reduction in eye redness and irritation, and an improvement in eye comfort and vision.

You may also notice a decrease in the dryness and grittiness associated with dry eyes, and an overall sense of relief from eye strain.

The oil contains constituents like beta-caryophyllene, alpha-humulene, and bornyl acetate, which help to reduce redness and irritation. It also has antimicrobial properties, making it effective against infections that can cause dry eyes. To use Euphrasia officinalis essential oils for dry eyes, you can apply a few drops of the oil directly to the affected eye using a cotton swab, or mix it with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil for a massage around the eye area.

When applying the oil, you may feel a soothing sensation as the anti-inflammatory properties calm down the irritation and redness. The likely outcome is that your dry, itchy, and irritated eyes will start to feel better within a short period, usually 15-30 minutes after application. You may also notice a decrease in the amount of discharge or crusting around your eyes, and your eyes may feel more comfortable and less sensitive.

If you have persistent or severe dry eye symptoms, it's always best to consult with an eye care professional before trying any new remedies.

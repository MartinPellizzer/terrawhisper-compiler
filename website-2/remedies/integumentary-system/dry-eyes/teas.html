Dry Eyes a Common Issue for Diabetics: 7 Herbal Teas to Help

People with diabetes often struggle with dry eyes, a condition where the eyes don't produce enough tears to stay moist and comfortable.

This can be due to the high sugar levels in their blood, which can damage the tiny glands in the eyes that produce tears. Diabetics may experience itchiness, burning, and blurred vision, making everyday tasks like reading, watching TV, or even driving a challenge. Dry eyes can also increase the risk of eye infections and damage to the cornea, the clear layer on the front of the eye. For diabetics, dry eyes can be a constant companion, affecting their quality of life and making simple activities a chore.

As a result, they may need to use artificial tears or other treatments to manage their dry eyes. But did you know that herbal teas can also help alleviate dry eyes in diabetics?. These teas are made from plants that have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, which can help calm and moisturize the eyes. Drinking herbal teas can help reduce the discomfort and irritation associated with dry eyes, allowing diabetics to live more comfortably. The active compounds in these teas can also help protect the tiny glands in the eyes that produce tears, promoting healthy tear production and reducing the risk of eye damage.

By incorporating herbal teas into their daily routine, diabetics can take a natural and gentle approach to managing their dry eyes and improving their overall eye health.

1. Euphrasia officinalis

Euphrasia officinalis teas, also known as eye bright, is a natural remedy that has been used for centuries to help alleviate dry and irritated eyes.

This herb is rich in properties and constituents, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which have anti-inflammatory and soothing effects on the eyes. The flavonoids in Euphrasia officinalis help to reduce inflammation and improve blood flow to the eyes, while the phenolic acids have antioxidant properties that protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. Saponins, on the other hand, have a soothing effect on the eyes, reducing redness and itching. If you're experiencing dry eyes, you can try making a tea from Euphrasia officinalis by steeping one teaspoon of dried herb in a cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes.

Let the tea cool down, then use it as an eye wash or apply it to a cotton pad and place it over your closed eyes for a few minutes. You can also add a few drops of the tea to your eyes directly, but be sure to dilute it with water first to avoid any irritation. When you take action and use Euphrasia officinalis tea for dry eyes, you can expect to experience relief from symptoms such as redness, itching, and burning. The anti-inflammatory and soothing properties of the herb may help to reduce swelling and improve tear production, making your eyes feel more comfortable and refreshed.

Additionally, the antioxidant properties of Euphrasia officinalis may help to protect your eyes from further damage and promote overall eye health.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, also known as licorice root tea, have been traditionally used to help alleviate dry eyes.

The root of the Glycyrrhiza glabra plant contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins, which have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. These compounds help to reduce inflammation and irritation in the eyes, making it easier for tears to flow and moisturize the eyes. To use Glycyrrhiza glabra teas for dry eyes, start by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root in a cup of hot water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it as an eye compress.

Soak a clean cloth in the tea, wring it out, and place it over your closed eyes for 5-10 minutes. You can also drink the tea as a warm beverage, 2-3 times a day, to help soothe your eyes from the inside out. By using Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, you may experience a reduction in eye dryness and irritation, improved tear production, and a decrease in eye redness. Some people may also notice an improvement in their eye comfort and a reduction in the need for artificial tears.

However, it's essential to note that Glycyrrhiza glabra teas may interact with certain medications or worsen conditions like high blood pressure, so consult with a healthcare professional before using it as a treatment for dry eyes.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas have been found to be beneficial for people experiencing dry eyes, thanks to its unique combination of properties and constituents.

The leaves of the Ginkgo biloba plant contain flavonoids and terpenoids, which are known to improve blood flow to the eyes and reduce inflammation. This increased blood flow helps to nourish the eyes, while reducing inflammation alleviates discomfort and pain associated with dry eyes. Additionally, Ginkgo biloba teas have antioxidant properties, which protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. If you're considering trying Ginkgo biloba teas for dry eyes, start by brewing a cup according to the package instructions. Typically, you'll want to steep one teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

You can drink the tea two to three times a day, ideally before meals. To enhance its effectiveness, try combining Ginkgo biloba teas with other dry eye remedies, such as artificial tears or omega-3 supplements. As you start taking Ginkgo biloba teas, you may notice improvements in your eye health within a few days to a week. Your eyes may feel more comfortable, and you may experience reduced dryness and irritation. You may also notice better vision clarity and reduced eye strain. However, it's essential to note that everyone's response to Ginkgo biloba teas may vary, and it may take some time to experience noticeable benefits.

If you have persistent or severe dry eyes, consult with your eye care professional before adding Ginkgo biloba teas to your treatment plan.

4. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium teas, also known as yarrow tea, have been traditionally used to help alleviate dry eyes.

The tea contains properties and constituents such as flavonoids, saponins, and volatile oils that may help reduce inflammation and soothe the eyes. The flavonoids, in particular, have antioxidant properties that may help protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. The saponins, on the other hand, may help to reduce inflammation and calm the eyes. To try yarrow tea for dry eyes, steep 1 teaspoon of dried yarrow leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Let the tea cool to room temperature before using it as an eye wash. You can also drink the tea, but as a wash, it may be more effective.

Gently pour the cooled tea over your closed eyes, making sure not to get any in your eyes. Keep the tea in your eyes for a few minutes, then rinse with water. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day. Taking yarrow tea may help to reduce the dryness and irritation of the eyes. You may notice a decrease in redness and a reduction in the sensation of grittiness in your eyes. The tea may also help to improve tear production and reduce the risk of further irritation.

However, if your dry eyes persist or worsen, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional to rule out any underlying conditions that may be contributing to the dryness.

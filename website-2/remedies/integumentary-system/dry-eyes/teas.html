10 best herbal teas for dry eyes

1. Euphrasia Officinalis

Euphrasia officinalis tea, also known as eye bright, is a herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to soothe dry, itchy, and irritated eyes. The tea is made from the flowers of the Euphrasia plant, which are rich in antioxidants and flavonoids that help to reduce inflammation and promote healing in the eyes. When consumed as a tea, these beneficial compounds are absorbed into the bloodstream and carried directly to the eyes, where they can help to calm and relax the eye tissues. This can lead to a reduction in dryness, redness, and irritation, making it easier to see and feel comfortable. The anti-inflammatory properties of Euphrasia officinalis tea may also help to alleviate conditions such as conjunctivitis and blepharitis, which can cause dry, itchy eyes.

2. Calendula Officinalis

Calendula officinalis, also known as pot marigold, is a plant that has been used for centuries to help with various health issues. When it comes to dry eyes, this plant is useful because it contains powerful antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties. The antioxidants in Calendula officinalis help to reduce oxidative stress, which is a major cause of dry eyes. This is because oxidative stress can damage the delicate tissues in the eyes, leading to inflammation and dryness. By drinking Calendula officinalis tea, you can help to reduce this stress and promote healing in the eyes. The anti-inflammatory properties in the tea also help to reduce inflammation and swelling in the eyes, which can contribute to dry eyes. Additionally, the plant's antiseptic properties help to prevent infections that can irritate the eyes and make dry eyes worse. Drinking Calendula officinalis tea regularly may help to soothe and calm dry eyes, reducing discomfort and promoting healthy vision.

Drinking Echinacea purpurea tea regularly may help alleviate dry, itchy eyes and promote overall eye health.

10. Verbascum Thapsus

Verbascum thapsus tea, also known as mullein tea, is a natural remedy that can help with dry eyes. The leaves of the mullein plant contain saponins, which are special compounds that can help to reduce inflammation in the eyes. When we have dry eyes, it's often because the tear ducts in our eyes are not working properly, and this can cause irritation and discomfort. Drinking mullein tea can help to soothe and calm the eyes, reducing the redness and itchiness often associated with dry eyes. The saponins in the tea can also help to clean out the tear ducts, promoting the flow of tears and helping to keep the eyes moist. This can make a big difference in people who suffer from dry eyes, helping to reduce the discomfort and make their eyes feel more comfortable.

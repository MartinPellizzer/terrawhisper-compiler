7 Herbal Teas For Dry Eyes

People with diabetes often struggle with dry eyes, a condition where the eyes don't produce enough tears to stay moist and comfortable.

This can be due to the high sugar levels in their blood, which can damage the tiny glands in the eyes that produce tears. Diabetics may experience itchiness, burning, and blurred vision, making everyday tasks like reading, watching TV, or even driving a challenge. Dry eyes can also increase the risk of eye infections and damage to the cornea, the clear layer on the front of the eye. For diabetics, dry eyes can be a constant companion, affecting their quality of life and making simple activities a chore.

As a result, they may need to use artificial tears or other treatments to manage their dry eyes. But did you know that herbal teas can also help alleviate dry eyes in diabetics?. These teas are made from plants that have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, which can help calm and moisturize the eyes. Drinking herbal teas can help reduce the discomfort and irritation associated with dry eyes, allowing diabetics to live more comfortably. The active compounds in these teas can also help protect the tiny glands in the eyes that produce tears, promoting healthy tear production and reducing the risk of eye damage.

By incorporating herbal teas into their daily routine, diabetics can take a natural and gentle approach to managing their dry eyes and improving their overall eye health.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains flavonoids and terpenoids that may help alleviate dry eyes.

The flavonoids, particularly quercetin, kaempferol, and isorhapontigenin, have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce inflammation in the eyes and promote healthy tears. The terpenoids, such as bilobalide and ginkgolides, can improve blood flow to the eyes and increase tear production. This can help to moisturize the eyes and reduce dryness. If you're experiencing dry eyes, you can try drinking Ginkgo biloba tea to see if it helps.

Here's what you can do: brew a cup of Ginkgo biloba tea according to the package instructions, and drink it once or twice a day. You can also apply a few drops of Ginkgo biloba eye drops to your eyes, but be sure to follow the instructions carefully and consult with an eye care professional before using any new product. When you take action, you may notice an improvement in your eye comfort and vision. Your eyes may feel more moisturized and less irritated, and you may be able to blink more comfortably. You may also see an improvement in your tear production, which can help to protect your eyes from dryness and irritation.

However, it's essential to note that Ginkgo biloba tea may not work for everyone, and it's always best to consult with an eye care professional before trying any new remedy for dry eyes.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains compounds like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkylamides that may help soothe and protect dry, irritated eyes.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce redness and swelling in the eyes. They also have antioxidant properties, which can help prevent damage to the cornea and conjunctiva. To use Echinacea purpurea tea for dry eyes, steep 1 teaspoon of dried flowers in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Let it cool down before applying it as an eye wash. You can also add the cooled tea to a clean cloth and gently compress it over your closed eyes for 5-10 minutes.

When you take these actions, you may experience a reduction in eye irritation, redness, and dryness. Your eyes may feel more comfortable and look less inflamed. You may also notice improved tear quality, which can help your eyes feel more moisturized and refreshed. It's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea tea is not a substitute for medical treatment.

If your dry eyes persist or worsen, consult with an eye care professional for proper evaluation and care.

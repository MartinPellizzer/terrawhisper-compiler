What Are The Main Causes Of Bleeding Spots In Mouth?

Bleeding spots in the mouth can be caused by several factors.

One possible cause is HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus), a virus that attacks the body's immune system. HIV can lead to bleeding spots in the mouth because it weakens the immune system, making it harder for the body to fight off infections. This can cause bleeding spots in the mouth, gums, and other parts of the body. Another possible cause is Hepatitis, a viral infection that inflames the liver.

Hepatitis can cause bleeding spots in the mouth because it can lead to a condition called thrombocytopenia, where the blood doesn't have enough platelets to help blood clot. Without enough platelets, the blood can't stop bleeding easily, leading to bleeding spots in the mouth and other parts of the body. Hemophilia is also a possible cause of bleeding spots in the mouth. Hemophilia is a genetic disorder that affects the blood's ability to clot. People with hemophilia have a low platelet count or the platelets don't work properly, which can lead to excessive bleeding.

In the mouth, this can cause bleeding spots on the gums, tongue, or the inside of the cheeks.

Below are the main causes of bleeding spots in mouth.

  • HIV: HIV damages the body's immune system and can also damage the tissues in the mouth, leading to bleeding spots due to poor blood clotting and weakened gum tissues.
  • Hepatitis: Hepatitis causes bleeding spots in the mouth because it damages the liver, which makes the blood thinner and harder to clot, leading to easy bleeding from small cuts or spots inside the mouth.
  • Hemophilia: Hemophilia is a condition where the blood has trouble clotting, which can cause bleeding spots in the mouth because the body can't stop the bleeding as easily as it should.
  • Cancer: Cancer can cause bleeding spots in the mouth because the abnormal cells in the cancer can damage the blood vessels, making them weak and more likely to bleed when the mouth is touched or eaten.
  • Gingivitis: Gingivitis is a cause of bleeding spots in the mouth because the inflammation and infection of the gums make them weak and tender, causing them to bleed easily when you brush or floss your teeth.

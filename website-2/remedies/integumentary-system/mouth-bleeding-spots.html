Causes and Herbal Remedies for Bleeding Spots in Mouth: A Comprehensive Guide

Bleeding spots in the mouth, also known as oral mucosal lesions, can be a frustrating and painful condition that affects your daily life.

These small, often painful bumps or sores can make eating, drinking, and even speaking uncomfortable. They can appear due to various reasons, such as hormonal changes, certain medications, or even viral infections like herpes simplex. Bleeding spots can be a sign of an underlying issue, so it's essential to consult a doctor for a proper diagnosis. There are some herbal remedies that may help alleviate the symptoms of bleeding spots in the mouth. Aloe vera, with its anti-inflammatory properties, can be applied topically to reduce swelling and soothe the affected area.

Calendula, another natural remedy, has antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties that can help speed up the healing process. Licorice root, with its anti-inflammatory compounds, can also be used to reduce swelling and discomfort. To use these herbs, you can prepare teas or tinctures. Make a tea by steeping dried calendula flowers or aloe vera leaves in hot water, then strain and use as a mouthwash. You can also add licorice root to your tea for added benefits. Alternatively, you can apply aloe vera gel directly to the affected area with a cotton swab.

Remember to always consult with a healthcare professional before trying any new remedies, especially if you're already taking medications or have underlying health conditions.

What causes the appearance of bleeding spots in mouth?

The main causes of bleeding spots in mouth are quite diverse and can range from serious health issues to everyday accidents.

Oral cancer is one of the most severe causes, where abnormal cells in the mouth grow and bleed. This is often caused by smoking, tobacco use, or long-term exposure to the sun. Gum disease is another common cause, where plaque and tartar buildup on the teeth cause inflammation and bleeding of the gums.

Oral surgery, such as tooth extraction or biopsy, can also lead to bleeding spots in the mouth. People with bleeding disorders like hemophilia may experience frequent bleeding in the mouth due to their condition. Oral trauma, such as a blow to the mouth or a sharp object cutting the gums, can also cause bleeding spots.

Hormonal changes during menstruation, pregnancy, or menopause can lead to increased blood flow and bleeding in the mouth, especially in the gums.

What herbs should be used with caution if you have bleeding spots in mouth?

If you have bleeding spots in your mouth, it's best to be cautious with certain herbs.

Paeonia lactiflora, also known as peony, can slow down bleeding, but it can also make it worse if you already have bleeding spots. The root of Arctium lappa, or burdock, is often used to stop bleeding, but using it might not be a good idea if you're already experiencing bleeding in your mouth.

Valeriana officinalis, or valerian, is usually used for relaxation and sleep, but it can also increase bleeding if taken in large amounts. Salix alba, or white willow, is used to treat pain and inflammation, but it can also thin the blood, which might not be good if you have bleeding spots in your mouth. Papaver rhoeas, or poppy, can also thin the blood and make bleeding worse.

Using these herbs when you have bleeding spots in your mouth could make the bleeding harder to stop or even cause more bleeding.

8 Herbal Tinctures For Dry Hair

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures contains the bioactive constituents cineole, linalool, and viridiflorol.

These compounds help to balance the moisture levels in dry hair by reducing inflammation and soothing the scalp. Cineole, in particular, has antifungal and antibacterial properties that can help control scalp irritations and infections that can contribute to dryness. Linalool, a natural moisturizer, helps to lock in moisture and protect the hair from environmental stressors.

By using Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures, dry hair can benefit from the balancing and soothing properties of these natural compounds.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia flowers and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the Melaleuca alternifolia flowers to the jar and pour in the vodka, making sure the flowers are completely covered.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Label and store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 10-15 drops of the tincture as a hair treatment, mixed with your regular shampoo, 1-2 times a week.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis tinctures contains aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds help to lock in moisture and reduce water loss, which is beneficial for dry hair. Aloin has anti-inflammatory properties, reducing irritation and soothing the scalp, while aloe-emodin has antioxidant properties, protecting the hair from damage caused by free radicals. Acemannan, a polysaccharide, helps to stimulate blood flow to the hair follicles, promoting healthy hair growth.

By using aloe barbadensis tinctures, you can help to repair and nourish dry hair, leaving it looking healthier and more vibrant.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 1 cup of vodka, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Mix the Aloe gel and vodka in the glass jar. Stir until the gel is fully incorporated.
  3. Cover the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Store the Aloe tincture in the refrigerator. Use 1-2 tablespoons as a hair mask or add it to your shampoo for dry hair.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis tinctures contains the bioactive constituents rosmarinic acid, camphor, and bornyl acetate, which contribute to its hair benefits.

The rosmarinic acid in these tinctures helps to lock in moisture and reduce dryness by forming a protective barrier on the hair shaft. Camphor, on the other hand, has antifungal properties that prevent the growth of fungi and bacteria that can cause dryness and itchiness on the scalp. Bornyl acetate is an antiseptic that soothes and calms the scalp, reducing irritation and flakiness.

By using Rosmarinus officinalis tinctures, dry hair can benefit from these properties, which help to moisturize, protect, and calm the hair and scalp.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves to the jar and cover with vodka. Shake the jar to combine.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the leaves.
  5. Transfer the tincture to small glass bottles and store in a cool, dark place. Use 5-10 drops of the tincture to your hair mask or as a leave-in treatment.

4. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica tinctures contains the bioactive constituents histamine, serotonin, and flavonoids.

Histamine helps to improve blood flow to the scalp, which can promote healthy hair growth and reduce dryness. Flavonoids, specifically quercetin, have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and dryness. Serotonin also has a role in regulating hair growth and maintenance.

By using Urtica dioica tinctures, you may be able to reduce dryness and improve the overall health of your hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Urtica dioica leaves and 2 cups of 80 proof vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Pack the Urtica dioica leaves tightly into the jar, leaving about 1 inch of space at the top.
  3. Pour the vodka over the leaves, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.

5. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are active compounds that help with dry hair.

Flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant properties that protect hair follicles from damage and promote healthy hair growth. Terpenoids, including bilobalide and ginkgolides, have anti-inflammatory properties that soothe and calm dry, irritated scalps. By reducing inflammation and promoting hair growth, Ginkgo biloba tinctures can help to alleviate dry hair and scalp conditions.

The flavonoids and terpenoids in Ginkgo biloba tinctures work together to create a healthy environment for hair growth and hydration.

  1. Gather ingredients and tools: 1 cup of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves, 2 cups of 80% ethanol vodka, a glass jar with a lid, a cheesecloth or a coffee filter, and a measuring cup.
  2. Combine 1 cup of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves and 2 cups of 80% ethanol vodka in the glass jar. Stir well to mix.
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day to help the mixture.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through the cheesecloth or coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the Ginkgo biloba tincture to a small bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-10 drops of the tincture in your hair mask or shampoo for dry hair.

6. Equisetum arvense

Equisetum arvense tinctures contains bioactive constituents like ferulic acid and scopoletin.

These compounds have antioxidant properties, which help to protect the hair from damage caused by free radicals. The silica in Equisetum arvense tinctures helps to strengthen hair follicles and improve hair texture, making it a good option for dry hair. Equisetum arvense tinctures also contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help to soothe and calm dry, irritated scalp.

By using Equisetum arvense tinctures, dry hair can benefit from the nourishing and protective properties of its bioactive constituents.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Equisetum arvense (horsetail) herb and 2 cups of 80% ethanol or vodka in a clean glass container.
  2. Combine the herb and ethanol in the container, making sure the herb is completely covered.
  3. Seal the container and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the tincture to a glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-10 drops of the tincture in your hair mask or shampoo for dry hair.

7. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum tinctures contains silymarin and flavonoids, which are antioxidant and anti-inflammatory compounds.

These properties help protect the hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, promoting healthy hair growth. Silymarin also has a moisturizing effect, helping to lock in moisture and reduce dryness in the hair. The flavonoids in Silybum marianum tinctures have anti-oxidant properties, which help to repair and protect the hair cuticle, leaving hair looking shinier and healthier.

Silybum marianum tinctures can be used as a natural treatment for dry hair, providing a nourishing and protective solution for damaged hair.

8. Avena sativa

Avena sativa tinctures contains flavonoids, phenolic acids, and avenanthramides as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and soothing properties that can help to calm dry and irritated scalps. Avenanthramides, in particular, have been shown to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, which can contribute to dry hair. The tannins present in Avena sativa tinctures also help to balance the scalp's pH, reducing dryness and flakiness.

By reducing inflammation and soothing the scalp, Avena sativa tinctures can help to promote healthy hair growth and reduce the appearance of dry, brittle hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Avena sativa (oat straw) and 2 cups of 80% ethanol (vodka or rum work too).
  2. Cut the Avena sativa into small pieces and place them in a clean glass jar.
  3. Pour the 80% ethanol over the Avena sativa, making sure all the plant material is covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dry hair from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help keep your hair healthy and prevent dryness.

Some herbal teas, like rooibos and peppermint, have antioxidants that protect hair from damage and keep it moisturized.

Regularly sipping on these teas might also improve hair's elasticity and shine, reducing the appearance of dryness and breakage.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dry hair every day?

Consuming herbal teas for dry hair every day can be safe, but it depends on the ingredients.

Some teas, like rosemary and peppermint, are naturally rich in antioxidants and can help nourish and moisturize your hair.

However, teas with strong herbs like sage or thyme might cause irritation or dryness if used excessively.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dry hair?

Herbal teas can help with dry hair by nourishing it from the inside out.

The results can take a few weeks to a couple of months to show.

Drinking herbal teas like rooibos, peppermint, or chamomile regularly can help lock in moisture and reduce dryness, leaving your hair feeling softer and more manageable over time.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for dry hair?

For dry hair, it's best to drink herbal tea in the morning or early afternoon. This allows the tea's moisturizing properties to work throughout the day, helping to lock in moisture and reduce frizz.

Dry locks? 11 herbal teas for surfers to the rescue

Surfing can be a tough sport on hair.

Exposure to saltwater, wind, and sun can dry out your locks, making them brittle and prone to breakage. This can be especially frustrating for surfers, as it affects not just their appearance, but also their confidence in the water. Dry hair can be hard to style, and it may fall out while you're surfing, which can be embarrassing. It can also be painful to deal with, especially if you have a bad case of split ends. To make matters worse, dry hair can be a sign of underlying hair problems, like a lack of moisture or a damaged scalp.

This can lead to more serious issues, like hair loss or dandruff. When your hair is dry, it can feel rough to the touch and look dull and lifeless. It may not hold a style well, and it can even start to fall out in clumps. This can be devastating for surfers who rely on their hair to look good and feel confident. Herbal teas can help surfers treat dry hair by providing the moisture and nourishment it needs to stay healthy. They work by infusing the hair follicles with beneficial compounds that help to lock in moisture and repair damage.

This can help to restore the hair's natural shine and softness, making it easier to style and manage.

1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas are a natural remedy for dry hair, and they work by targeting the underlying causes of dryness.

The teas contain properties like antioxidants, essential oils, and flavonoids that help to protect the hair cuticle and lock in moisture. The constituents in Rosmarinus officinalis teas, such as rosmarinic acid and camphor, have anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties that soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and flaking. If you're experiencing dry hair, try using Rosmarinus officinalis teas as a final rinse after shampooing. To do this, simply steep a teaspoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then let it cool.

Pour the tea over your hair after shampooing and leave it on for 1-2 minutes before rinsing thoroughly with cold water. You can also add a tablespoon of olive oil or coconut oil to the tea for extra moisturizing benefits. By using Rosmarinus officinalis teas as a final rinse, you can expect to see a reduction in frizz and flyaways, as well as an improvement in your hair's overall texture and manageability. Your hair should feel softer and look healthier, with a more even tone and reduced dryness.

Regular use of Rosmarinus officinalis teas may also help to stimulate hair growth and reduce dandruff and other scalp irritations.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia teas are a natural remedy that can help with dry hair.

This tea is made from the leaves of the Melaleuca alternifolia plant, also known as the tea tree, which is rich in properties that can nourish and moisturize dry hair. The tea contains constituents like cymene and cineole, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help to lock in moisture and reduce dryness. When consumed, these properties can help to stimulate blood flow to the scalp, promote healthy hair growth, and protect the hair follicles from damage. To use Melaleuca alternifolia tea for dry hair, you can start by brewing a cup of the tea and drinking it regularly. You can also use the cooled tea as a hair rinse or a final wash to add extra moisture to your hair.

To make a hair rinse, simply pour the cooled tea over your hair after shampooing and leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing. You can also add a few drops of tea tree oil to your shampoo or conditioner for an extra boost of moisture. When you start using Melaleuca alternifolia tea for dry hair, you may notice an improvement in the texture and appearance of your hair. The tea can help to make your hair feel softer, look shinier, and reduce frizz and flyaways. You may also notice that your hair is less prone to breakage and split ends, and that it looks healthier and more vibrant overall.

As you continue to use the tea, you may notice that your hair starts to grow faster and stronger, and that you need to wash it less often because it stays moisturized for longer.

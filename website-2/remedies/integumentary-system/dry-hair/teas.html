To make a Salvia officinalis herbal tea that helps with dry hair, start by gathering 1-2 teaspoons of dried Salvia officinalis leaves and flowers.

It's worth noting that these teas might not be suitable for everyone, and it's always a good idea to do a patch test before using them.

If you do choose to drink these teas, it's best to do so in moderation.

What other ailments people who have dry hair are likely to experience?

People with dry hair often experience other skin and hair-related problems.

One common issue is dandruff, a condition where flakes of skin fall from the scalp. This can be caused by a fungus that feeds on the oils produced by the scalp. Medicinal herbs like tea tree oil and neem have antifungal properties that can help control dandruff. Tea tree oil's active ingredient, terpinen-4-ol, kills the fungus by damaging its cell membrane, while neem's azadirachtin blocks the fungus's ability to feed. Another problem people with dry hair may face is eczema, a skin condition characterized by red, itchy patches.

Eczema can be caused by a lack of moisture in the skin, which can be helped by topical creams or ointments containing ingredients like aloe vera or chamomile. Aloe vera's soothing properties can calm itchy skin, while chamomile's anti-inflammatory properties reduce redness and swelling. Dry hair can also lead to split ends, which can be prevented by using hair masks containing ingredients like coconut oil or shea butter. Coconut oil's fatty acids moisturize and nourish the hair, while shea butter's vitamins and minerals help repair damaged hair cuticles. In addition, people with dry hair may experience hair loss, which can be caused by a buildup of dead skin cells and oil on the scalp. Medicinal herbs like saw palmetto and rosemary can help by reducing inflammation and promoting hair growth.

Saw palmetto's fatty acids block the conversion of testosterone to dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a hormone that contributes to hair loss, while rosemary's antioxidants promote hair growth by improving blood circulation to the scalp.

11 Herbal Teas For Dry Hair

Herbal teas can be a great way to help relieve dry hair.

Some herbs have natural properties that moisturize and nourish your hair, leaving it softer and more manageable. For example, Rosmarinus officinalis, or rosemary, has antifungal properties that help control scalp infections and promote healthy hair growth. When you use rosemary tea as a hair rinse, it can help lock in moisture and reduce frizz. Aloe vera, or Aloe barbadensis, is another great option for dry hair. It's known for its soothing and calming properties, which can help reduce irritation and inflammation on the scalp. When you apply aloe vera tea to your hair, it can help smooth the cuticle and add shine, leaving your hair looking healthier and more vibrant.

Nettle root, or Urtica dioica, is also a great choice for dry hair. It's rich in antioxidants and has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce dandruff and other scalp issues. When you use nettle root tea as a hair rinse, it can help nourish and strengthen your hair, leaving it softer and more resilient. Using herbal teas for dry hair can bring many benefits to your life. Not only can it help you achieve healthier and more manageable hair, but it can also save you money on expensive hair care products. Plus, many of these herbs are easily available and inexpensive, making them a great natural alternative to commercial products.

By incorporating herbal teas into your hair care routine, you can enjoy softer, shinier hair that's easier to style and more resistant to damage.

1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains rosmarinic acid, a powerful antioxidant that helps to lock in moisture and reduce dryness in hair.

This antioxidant also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and flaking that can contribute to dry hair. Rosmarinus officinalis teas also contains beta-caryophyllene, a sesquiterpene that has been shown to improve scalp health and promote a healthy hair growth environment. The tea's antimicrobial properties can also help to prevent the buildup of product residue and dirt on the hair and scalp, which can exacerbate dryness and damage.

By using Rosmarinus officinalis teas as a hair treatment, you can help to restore moisture, promote healthy hair growth, and reduce the appearance of dry, brittle locks.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid to remove the leaves. Let it cool down.
  4. Mix 2 tablespoons of coconut oil or olive oil with the cooled Rosmarinus officinalis tea.
  5. Use the Rosmarinus officinalis tea mixture as a hair mask to nourish and moisturize dry hair.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas contains bioactive compounds like aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-annone, which help to moisturize and nourish dry hair.

These compounds work by locking in moisture and reducing inflammation, allowing the hair to retain its natural moisture and reducing frizz. Aloe-emodin also has antioxidant properties, which help to protect the hair from damage caused by free radicals. Additionally, the vitamins and minerals present in aloe barbadensis teas, such as vitamin E and potassium, help to strengthen the hair follicles and promote healthy hair growth.

By using aloe barbadensis teas as a hair treatment, dry hair can become softer, shinier, and more manageable.

  1. Cut 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis leaves from a mature plant. Rinse them with water to remove dirt.
  2. Chop the leaves into small pieces and add them to 2 cups of boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to low and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid into a cup to remove the Aloe pieces. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the tea cool down before using it as a hair treatment. Apply it to your dry hair and leave it on for 30 minutes to an hour before rinsing.

3. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica teas contains bioactive constituents like vitamins A, C, and E, as well as minerals like iron and potassium.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help protect the hair follicles from damage. The tea's high content of silica also helps strengthen the hair shaft, reducing dryness and brittleness. The antioxidants in Urtica dioica tea also help repair and restore the hair's natural moisture balance, leaving it softer and more manageable.

By regularly consuming Urtica dioica tea, you may see an improvement in the health and appearance of your dry hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Urtica dioica leaves or 2 tablespoons of dried leaves.
  2. Combine the leaves with 1 cup of boiling water in a large cup or teapot.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 5-10 minutes, then strain the leaves.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey or lemon juice to the tea, if desired, for taste.
  5. Use the tea as a final rinse after shampooing to help moisturize and nourish dry hair.

4. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus teas contains essential oils like limonene, geraniol, and linalool.

These active constituents help to nourish and moisturize dry hair, reducing frizz and flyaways. The antifungal and antibacterial properties of geraniol and linalool prevent scalp infections that can cause dryness and itchiness. Limonene, being a natural emollient, helps to lock in moisture and soothe dry, damaged hair.

By using Cymbopogon citratus teas, you can help to repair and hydrate dry hair, leaving it soft, smooth, and manageable.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes to release the oils and flavor.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove the leaves.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey or sugar to the tea to sweeten, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea once a week to help nourish and moisturize dry hair.

5. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas contains thymol and carvacrol, two powerful essential oils that help to nourish and moisturize dry hair.

Thymol has antimicrobial properties that prevent the scalp from becoming irritated, which can lead to dryness and flakiness. Carvacrol, on the other hand, has antioxidant properties that help to protect the hair follicles from damage and promote healthy hair growth. Thymus vulgaris teas also contains flavonoids, such as luteolin and apigenin, which have anti-inflammatory properties that soothe and calm the scalp.

By using Thymus vulgaris teas, you can help to lock in moisture and reduce dryness, leaving your hair feeling soft and looking healthy.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water, 2 tablespoons of dried Thymus vulgaris leaves, and a tea infuser.
  2. Steep the Thymus vulgaris leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea mixture using the tea infuser and discard the leaves.
  4. Let the tea cool down before applying it to your hair.
  5. Apply the cooled Thymus vulgaris tea to your dry hair, focusing on the ends, and leave it on for 30 minutes to an hour before washing.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and lavandulol, which are its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help protect hair from damage caused by free radicals and inflammation. The antioxidants in Lavandula angustifolia teas can also help to lock in moisture, reducing dryness and brittleness in hair. Additionally, the tea's anti-inflammatory properties may help to soothe an itchy scalp, reducing irritation and flaking.

By incorporating Lavandula angustifolia teas into your hair care routine, you may experience improved hydration and a healthier-looking mane.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  2. Add 1 cup of boiling water to a heat-resistant container.
  3. Steep the flowers in the hot water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  5. Use the cooled tea as a final rinse after shampooing your hair.

7. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia teas contains the bioactive constituents cineole, limonene, and linalool, which have antifungal and antimicrobial properties.

These compounds help to balance the scalp's natural pH and reduce inflammation, which can lead to dry hair. The tea's antimicrobial properties also help to control fungal infections that can cause flaking and dryness. Additionally, the antioxidants in Melaleuca alternifolia teas, such as terpinen-4-ol, help to protect the hair follicles from damage and promote healthy hair growth.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy scalp environment, Melaleuca alternifolia teas can help to alleviate dry hair and promote a healthier, more balanced scalp.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Melaleuca alternifolia leaves.
  2. Combine the leaves with 1 cup of boiling water in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then strain the liquid.
  4. Mix the tea with 1 cup of cold water and add 1 tablespoon of honey (optional).
  5. Use the tea as a final rinse after shampooing your hair to help moisturize and nourish dry locks.

8. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas contains triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help to lock in moisture in dry hair. The flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, act as natural moisturizers, while the triterpenoids help to protect the hair from environmental stressors. The carotenoids, including lutein and zeaxanthin, provide a barrier that helps to retain moisture and reduce dryness.

By consuming Calendula officinalis teas, individuals with dry hair may experience improved hydration and a healthier hair appearance.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Calendula officinalis flowers or 2 tablespoons of dried flowers.
  2. Combine the flowers with 2 cups of boiling water in a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then strain the liquid using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey or sugar to the liquid, if desired, for taste.
  5. Let the tea cool, then use it as a final rinse after shampooing to help nourish and moisturize dry hair.

9. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa teas contains turmeric oil, which has active constituents like curcumin, ar-turmerone, and beta-caryophyllene.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help protect the hair follicles from damage and promote healthy hair growth. Turmeric oil also contains sesquiterpenes, which have moisturizing properties that help to lock in moisture and reduce dryness in the hair. The antioxidants in curcuma longa teas can also help to repair and strengthen the hair cuticle, making it less prone to dryness and breakage.

By using curcuma longa teas as a hair treatment, you can help to nourish and protect your dry hair, promoting a healthier and more vibrant appearance.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool for 5 minutes.
  2. Take 2 tablespoons of dried Curcuma longa root and crush it into small pieces.
  3. Add the crushed Curcuma longa root to the cooled water and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a cup.
  5. Drink the Curcuma longa tea once a week to improve hair health and reduce dryness.

10. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds help to improve blood circulation to the scalp, which in turn promotes healthy hair growth. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba tea have antioxidant properties that protect the hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, contributing to healthier and stronger hair. The terpenoids, particularly bilobalide, have anti-inflammatory properties that soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and dryness.

By promoting healthy scalp conditions and protecting the hair follicles, Ginkgo biloba tea can help to combat dry hair and promote a healthier, shinier appearance.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the Ginkgo biloba leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup and discard the leaves.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey or sugar to the tea, if desired, for flavor.
  5. Let the tea cool before using it as a hair mask or adding it to your regular hair care routine.

11. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis teas contains rosmarinic acid, apigenin, and linalool as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help to protect the hair from damage caused by free radicals and inflammation. The antioxidants in Melissa officinalis teas also help to lock in moisture and reduce the appearance of split ends, which can be beneficial for dry hair. Additionally, the tea has been shown to improve the scalp's condition by reducing dandruff and itchiness, creating a healthy environment for hair growth.

By consuming Melissa officinalis tea or using it as a hair rinse, you can help to nourish and moisturize dry hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Melissa officinalis leaves or 1 teaspoon of dried leaves.
  2. Heat 1 cup of water in a pot until it's hot but not boiling.
  3. Add the Melissa officinalis leaves to the hot water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the leaves.
  5. Use the tea as a final rinse after shampooing your hair to help lock in moisture and reduce frizz.

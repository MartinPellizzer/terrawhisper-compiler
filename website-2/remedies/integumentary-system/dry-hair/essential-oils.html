11 Herbal Solutions for Dry Hair and Nourishment

Using herbal essential oils can be a great way to help with dry hair.

Dry hair can cause a lot of problems, like breakage, frizz, and unmanageability. If left untreated, it can even lead to hair loss. Fixing this issue is important because it can affect your self-confidence and overall well-being. Dry hair can make you feel uncomfortable and self-conscious about your appearance. Herbal essential oils like Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis) and Tea Tree (Melaleuca alternifolia) can be very beneficial for dry hair. Rosemary essential oil can help to nourish and strengthen your hair, promoting healthy growth and reducing breakage.

It also has antiseptic properties that can help to protect your scalp from irritation and infection. Tea Tree essential oil, on the other hand, has antifungal and antibacterial properties that can help to soothe and calm an itchy scalp. When using essential oils for dry hair, it's essential to use them correctly. Mix a few drops of the essential oil with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil, and apply it to your hair. Leave it on for at least an hour before washing your hair as usual. You can also add a few drops of the essential oil to your shampoo or conditioner for an extra dose of moisture and nourishment.

Regular use of herbal essential oils can help to improve the health and appearance of your hair, leaving it looking and feeling soft, smooth, and manageable.

To make Calendula officinalis herbal essential oil that helps with dry hair, start by harvesting the petals of the Calendula flowers when they are in full bloom.

You will need about one pound of fresh flowers for every 8 ounces of oil you want to make. Next, dry the petals completely by spreading them out in a single layer on a paper bag or a clean cloth, and letting them air dry for a few days.

Once the petals are dry, you can proceed to make the oil by filling a clean glass jar with the dried flowers and adding a carrier oil such as sweet almond or coconut oil, making sure that the flowers are completely covered. After the extraction period, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, and discard the solids.

The resulting Calendula oil can be used as a hair treatment to help lock in moisture and soothe dry, damaged hair.

What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have dry hair?

If you have dry hair, there are some herbal essential oils you should be careful with.

'Cinnamomum camphora' or camphor oil can be too harsh and drying for your hair. It can strip the natural oils from your scalp, making your hair even drier and more brittle. 'Cedrus deodora' or cedarwood oil can also be problematic, as it can cause a buildup on your hair, weighing it down and making it look greasy.

Another oil to be cautious with is 'Cassia auriculata' or cassia oil. It can be too astringent, which means it can cause your hair to dry out even more and become brittle. 'Cinnamomum zeylanicum' or cinnamon bark oil can be too strong and spicy, irritating your scalp and causing dryness. Lastly, 'Juniperus communis' or juniper berry oil can be too drying, causing your hair to lose its natural moisture and become frizzy.

These oils can be too harsh for dry hair, so use them with caution or dilute them well before applying to your hair.

What other ailments people who have dry hair are likely to experience?

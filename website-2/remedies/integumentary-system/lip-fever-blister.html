Fever blisters on the lip are usually caused by a viral infection called HSV-1 (Herpes Simplex Virus 1).

When we're under a lot of stress, our immune system gets weaker, making it easier for the virus to become active. Hormonal changes can also cause fever blisters, especially during pregnancy or menstruation.

When hormone levels change, it can trigger the virus to become active. This is because hormonal fluctuations can weaken our immune system, making it harder for our body to fight off the virus. Fatigue is another contributing factor to fever blisters. When we're tired, our immune system gets weaker, making it easier for the virus to become active.

What Are The Main Causes Of Fever Blister On Lip?

Fever blisters on the lip are usually caused by the herpes simplex virus type 1 (HSV-1).

This virus is highly contagious and can be spread through direct contact with an infected person, or by sharing utensils, towels, or other personal items. Once you're infected, the virus lies dormant in your nerve cells and can be triggered by stress, fatigue, or certain foods to cause an outbreak. Another common cause of fever blisters is a cold or flu.

When you're sick with a cold or flu, your body is under stress, which can trigger an HSV-1 outbreak. This is because your immune system is busy fighting off the infection, leaving your body vulnerable to other triggers. Allergies can also cause fever blisters, especially if you're allergic to certain foods or substances that you're sensitive to. When your body reacts to an allergen, it can cause inflammation and irritation in the affected area, leading to a fever blister.

This is more likely to happen if you're already prone to allergies or have a weakened immune system.

Below are the main causes of fever blister on lip.

  • HSV-1: HSV-1, a common virus, causes fever blisters on the lip by infecting the nerve cells and causing them to release the virus, leading to painful and fluid-filled blisters that can be triggered by stress, cold weather, or other factors.
  • Cold: Cold viruses can cause fever blisters on the lip by weakening the skin and causing the herpes simplex virus to react and break out in painful sores.
  • Allergies: Fever blisters on the lip can be caused by allergies because when the body reacts to an allergen, it releases a chemical called histamine, which can irritate the nerve endings in the lip, leading to the formation of a fever blister.
  • Stress: Stress causes the release of hormones that weaken the immune system, allowing a virus that normally causes fever blisters to multiply and break out on the lips.
  • Hormonal change: Hormonal changes, especially during menstruation, pregnancy, or menopause, cause the body to release more stress hormones, which can weaken the immune system and trigger the outbreak of fever blisters on the lips due to the herpes simplex virus.
  • Sun exposure: Exposure to the sun causes fever blisters on the lip because it triggers a virus called herpes simplex to multiply and become active on the skin, leading to the painful and itchy sores.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Fever Blister On Lip?

If you have a fever blister on your lip, there are some herbs you should avoid using.

Comfrey (Symphytum officinale) is one to steer clear of, as it can irritate the skin and make the blister worse. Turmeric (Curcuma longa) contains a chemical called curcumin, which can increase the risk of bleeding. This could make your fever blister bleed more or take longer to heal.

Echinacea (Echinacea spp.). may also be problematic, as it can cause an allergic reaction or interact with other medications. Slippery elm (Ulmus rubra) can be too soothing and may keep the blister from drying out, prolonging its healing time. Finally, Saint-John's-wort (Hypericum perforatum) can cause allergic reactions, especially if you're taking other medications or have sensitive skin.

These herbs may cause more harm than good, so it's best to avoid them until your fever blister heals.

What Are The Main Causes Of Nail Fungus?

Nail fungus, also known as onychomycosis, is caused by a type of fungus that infects the nail.

There are three main types of fungus that cause nail fungus: Trichophyton, Microsporum, and others that cause fungal infections. Trichophyton is the most common cause of nail fungus and is usually found in warm and moist environments. It spreads through contact with an infected person or contaminated objects.

Microsporum is another type of fungus that causes nail fungus, especially in children and people with weakened immune systems. It's highly contagious and can spread through direct contact with an infected person or animal. Fungal infections can occur when the nail is exposed to fungi, which can enter through small cuts or cracks in the nail or nail bed.

This can happen when the nail is damaged, or when the skin around the nail is injured, providing a perfect environment for fungi to grow.

Below are the main causes of nail fungus.

  • Trichophyton: Trichophyton is a type of fungus that causes nail fungus because it grows on the dead tissue of the nail, feeding on nutrients and causing damage to the nail over time.
  • Fungal infection: Fungal infection is a cause of nail fungus because fungi such as mold and yeast can enter the nail through tiny cuts or cracks, causing an infection that can lead to thickening, discoloration, and brittleness of the nail.
  • Microsporum: Microsporum is a type of fungus that can cause nail fungus because it infects the nail and surrounding skin, leading to thickening and discoloration of the nail as it grows.
  • Epidermophyton: Epidermophyton is a type of fungus that can cause nail fungus because it easily grows on the dead tissues of the nail, such as the nail plate and nail bed, where it can start to break down and infect the nail.
  • Weakened immune system: A weakened immune system makes it harder for the body to fight off fungal infections, allowing nail fungus to grow and spread.
  • Onycholysis: Onycholysis is a cause of nail fungus because the separation of the nail from the nail bed allows fungus to grow underneath, causing infection and damage to the nail.

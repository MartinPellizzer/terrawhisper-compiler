What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have puffy eyes?

If you have puffy eyes, it's best to avoid using herbal decoctions that can irritate or inflame the skin around your eyes.

One such herb is 'Capsicum annuum', which contains capsaicin, a compound that can cause burning and itching sensations. This can make puffiness worse and cause further irritation.

Another herb to avoid is 'Sambucus nigra', as its berries can cause allergic reactions and skin inflammation. 'Rhus toxicodendron' is also not recommended, as it can cause contact dermatitis, a skin condition characterized by redness, swelling, and itching. 'Ephedra sinica' and 'Yohimbe bark' are also best avoided, as they can increase heart rate and blood pressure, which can worsen puffiness by causing fluid retention.

Finally, 'Ginkgo biloba' and 'Cinchona bark' should be used with caution, as they can thin the blood and cause bruising or bleeding under the eyes.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains a mix of active constituents, including vitamins A, C, and E, as well as minerals like potassium and zinc.

These ingredients help to reduce puffiness by improving circulation and reducing water retention. The anti-inflammatory properties of aloe vera, specifically the presence of aloin and aloe-emodin, also help to soothe and calm the skin around the eyes. Additionally, the gel-like consistency of aloe vera allows it to penetrate deep into the skin, providing long-lasting hydration and reducing the appearance of dark circles.

By reducing inflammation and improving hydration, aloe barbadensis creams can help to make the skin around the eyes look smoother and more refreshed.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1 tablespoon of glycerin, 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of Aloe barbadensis gel in a bowl.
  2. Mix the distilled water and glycerin in the bowl until the glycerin dissolves.
  3. Add the vitamin E oil and Aloe barbadensis gel to the bowl. Mix until smooth.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a clean container and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  5. After chilling, use an electric mixer to whip the mixture until it becomes light and fluffy. Use immediately or store in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins and sesquiterpene lactones as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and redness around the eyes. The antiseptic properties of sesquiterpene lactones also help to prevent infections that can cause puffiness. Calendula's ability to improve blood flow and reduce fluid retention also contributes to its effectiveness in reducing puffy eyes.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infection, Calendula officinalis creams can help to alleviate puffy eyes and leave them looking smoother and more refreshed.

  1. Gather materials: 1 cup of Calendula officinalis oil, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt Calendula officinalis oil, coconut oil, and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in shea butter and vitamin E oil until smooth.
  4. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container and let it cool and solidify.
  5. Once set, your Calendula cream is ready to use. Apply a small amount to puffy eyes and gently massage until absorbed.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains the active constituents alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

Alkylamides help reduce puffiness by improving blood circulation and reducing inflammation. Glycosides, particularly echinacoside, have anti-inflammatory properties that soothe the skin around the eyes. Phenolic acids, such as caffeic acid, have antioxidant properties that protect the skin from damage and promote collagen production.

By reducing inflammation and improving blood circulation, Echinacea purpurea creams can help decrease the appearance of puffy eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Combine dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and coconut oil in a double boiler. Heat and let it steep for 2 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture and discard the flowers. Add beeswax, shea butter, and vitamin E oil to the mixture. Heat until the beeswax melts.
  4. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool slightly. Pour it into a container and let it cool completely.
  5. Once cooled, use a spoon to scoop out a small amount of the cream and apply it to your puffy eyes. Massage gently and leave it on for 10-15 minutes before rinsing off.

4. Hypericum perforatum

These compounds help reduce puffiness by decreasing the swelling and inflammation in the delicate skin around the eyes.   2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Once melted, add the shea butter and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool slightly. Add the Hypericum perforatum extract and vitamin E oil, and stir well.

5. Hamamelis virginiana

Hamamelis virginiana creams contains tannins and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Tannins help reduce puffiness by shrinking blood vessels and constricting the skin, while flavonoids protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals. By combining these properties, Hamamelis virginiana creams can provide long-lasting relief from puffy eyes.

  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted coconut oil and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Add the dried Hamamelis virginiana flowers to the mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes. Strain the mixture and discard the flowers.
  5. Add the vitamin E oil to the mixture and pour it into a clean container. Let it cool and solidify before use.

6. Ginkgo biloba

7. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid and 1,8-cineole as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce puffiness in the eyes. By reducing inflammation and soothing the skin, melissa officinalis creams can help alleviate puffy eyes.

  4. Combine the infused water with the melted wax mixture and stir well. Add vitamin E oil and mix until combined.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent puffy eyes from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help reduce puffy eyes.

The antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties in some teas, like peppermint and chamomile, can help reduce swelling. The warmth of the tea can also loosen up fluids that cause puffiness.

Staying hydrated by drinking tea may also help flush out excess fluids from the body.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for puffy eyes every day?

Herbal teas can help reduce puffiness in the eyes, but consuming them daily may not be entirely safe.

Some herbs like chamomile and peppermint can be beneficial, but others like sage and wormwood can be too potent.

It's best to limit your intake to a few times a week and monitor how your body reacts.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in puffy eyes?

Herbal teas like chamomile, peppermint, and calendula can help with puffy eyes.

You might start seeing results in a few hours, depending on the severity of puffiness.

Some people notice improvement within 30 minutes to an hour, while others may need a few hours.

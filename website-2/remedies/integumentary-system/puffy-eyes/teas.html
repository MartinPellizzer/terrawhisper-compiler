1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce puffiness in the eyes. Alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the production of prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that can cause blood vessels to swell. Caffeic acid and rosmarinic acid also have antioxidant properties that can help protect the delicate skin around the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the skin, Echinacea purpurea teas may help to alleviate puffy eyes.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the Echinacea flowers in a cup.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it to your puffy eyes with a clean cloth.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains apigenin and linalool, two active constituents with anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce puffiness by constricting blood vessels and improving circulation. Linalool specifically targets the alpha-adrenergic receptors in the body, causing blood vessels to narrow and reducing fluid accumulation. Apigenin, on the other hand, has a direct effect on the lymphatic system, promoting the removal of excess fluids and reducing swelling.

By combining these effects, Lavandula angustifolia teas may help alleviate puffy eyes and promote a more refreshed appearance.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot and let it cool for 5 minutes.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the cooled water.
  4. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then strain it into a cup.
  5. Let the tea cool, then use it as an eye compress to reduce puffiness.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains flavonoids and terpenoids, two types of active constituents that help reduce puffiness around the eyes.

These compounds improve blood flow and reduce inflammation in the skin, which can make puffy eyes look less swollen and more refreshed. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba teas have antioxidant properties that help protect the delicate skin around the eyes from free radical damage, which can contribute to puffiness. The terpenoids in Ginkgo biloba teas have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and promote healthy circulation, which can help alleviate puffy eyes.

By using Ginkgo biloba teas, you can help improve the appearance of puffy eyes and feel more refreshed and revitalized.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water, 2 teaspoons of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves, and a tea infuser or strainer.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas contains active constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce puffiness around the eyes. The glycoproteins in aloe vera help soothe and calm the skin, reducing swelling and redness. Aloe barbadensis also contains vitamins A, C, and E, which are antioxidants that protect the skin from damage and promote healthy tissue.

Drinking aloe vera tea may help to reduce the appearance of puffy eyes by reducing inflammation and promoting healthy skin.

  1. Cut a 2-inch piece of Aloe barbadensis leaf. Remove the outer skin.
  2. Chop the inner gel into small pieces and place them in a cup.

  4. Strain the mixture into another cup. Discard the solids.
  5. Drink the tea as needed to help reduce puffy eyes.

5. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis teas contains rosmarinic acid, luteolin, and apigenin as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce puffiness around the eyes. Rosmarinic acid constricts blood vessels, which reduces swelling and congestion in the orbital area. Luteolin and apigenin also have a calming effect on the body, which can help alleviate stress and anxiety-related puffiness.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Melissa officinalis teas may help alleviate puffy eyes.

6. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains a high concentration of aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-annone, which are the bioactive constituents responsible for its benefits.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, which help to reduce puffiness and swelling around the eyes. Aloe-emodin, in particular, has been shown to have potent anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to constrict blood vessels and reduce fluid retention. Aloe-annone also has a cooling effect, which can help to calm and relax the skin around the eyes.

When applied topically, the bioactive constituents in Aloe barbadensis essential oils can help to reduce puffiness and leave the skin feeling smooth and refreshed.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel from a mature plant, and 1 cup of water in a bowl.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of witch hazel to the bowl and stir well.
  3. Mix 5 drops of vitamin E oil and 5 drops of peppermint essential oil into the gel mixture.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean container.
  5. Store the Aloe barbadensis essential oil in the fridge for up to 3 days and use it to apply to puffy eyes 2-3 times a day.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and flavonoids, which are known for their anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce puffiness by constricting blood vessels and preventing fluid buildup. Alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to have a direct effect on blood vessels, causing them to narrow and decrease swelling. Caffeic acid also helps reduce inflammation, which contributes to puffiness.

By combining these properties, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can provide relief from puffy eyes by reducing fluid accumulation and inflammation.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil like jojoba or sweet almond oil.
  2. Clean and dry the Echinacea flowers to remove any dirt or moisture.
  3. Combine the Echinacea flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar, making sure the flowers are completely covered.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the oil extract from the flowers.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container, discarding the solids and keeping the oil for use in treating puffy eyes.

3. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which make them useful for soothing and calming puffy eyes. The flavonoids in Calendula officinalis essential oils, such as quercetin and kaempferol, have potent antioxidant properties that help to reduce swelling and inflammation around the eyes. The carotenoids, including lutein and zeaxanthin, have been shown to improve eye health by reducing oxidative stress and promoting collagen production.

When applied to puffy eyes, the anti-inflammatory properties of Calendula officinalis essential oils can help to reduce swelling and leave the skin feeling soothed and calm.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula flowers and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the Calendula flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar, making sure the flowers are fully covered.
  3. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the oil extract from the flowers.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container.
  5. Discard the Calendula flowers and store the Calendula-infused oil in a dark glass bottle. Use 2-3 drops of the oil on a cotton pad to reduce puffy eyes.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two active constituents that contribute to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling and puffiness around the eyes. The essential oil also contains antioxidants that protect the delicate skin around the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to calm and relax the skin, reducing redness and irritation that can contribute to puffy eyes.

Applying this essential oil to a cold compress or a cotton pad can help to reduce puffiness and leave the skin feeling refreshed.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers, a glass jar, a strainer, and a cup.
  2. Combine 1 cup of flowers with 2 cups of water in the glass jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  3. Strain the mixture through the strainer into another container, discarding the solids and keeping the liquid.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a clean glass jar and add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil, such as coconut or sweet almond oil.
  5. Store the Lavandula angustifolia essential oil in a cool, dark place and use 2-3 drops for puffy eyes, mixed with a carrier oil and applied gently around the eye area.

5. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains boiactive constituents like linalool and citral, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties can help reduce puffiness by reducing swelling and inflammation in the affected area. Linalool, in particular, has a cooling effect that can help constrict blood vessels and reduce fluid buildup. Citral, on the other hand, has a mild diuretic effect that can help the body eliminate excess fluids and reduce puffiness.

By using Melissa officinalis essential oils, you can help reduce the appearance of puffy eyes and achieve a more refreshed and rested look.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers, a clean glass jar, and a carrier oil like jojoba oil.
  2. Combine 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers with 2 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a warm, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the Melissa officinalis essential oil in a cool, dark place and use 5-7 drops in a compress or a cream to relieve puffy eyes.

6. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains rosmarinic acid, camphor, and bornyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce puffiness around the eyes. The rosmarinic acid in Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils helps reduce swelling by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. The camphor and bornyl acetate also have a cooling effect, which can help constrict blood vessels and decrease the appearance of puffiness.

By applying Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils topically, the bioactive constituents can directly target the affected area and provide relief.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat, then add the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves. Let it simmer for 2 hours.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 30 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Discard the solids and transfer the liquid into a separate container. Add 2 tablespoons of a carrier oil (like jojoba oil).
  5. Store the Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil in a dark glass bottle in the fridge. Use 2-3 drops for puffy eyes.

7. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum essential oils contains flavonoids, specifically rutin and quercetin, and phenolic acids like kaempferol and dihydrokaempferol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce puffiness and swelling around the eyes. The essential oil's antioxidant properties, attributed to the presence of flavonoids and phenolic acids, can also protect the delicate skin around the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. Hypericum perforatum essential oils' ability to improve blood flow and reduce fluid retention may also contribute to its effectiveness in alleviating puffy eyes.

The essential oil's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties work together to soothe and calm the skin, providing relief from puffiness and promoting a more refreshed appearance.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum flowers and 2 cups of carrier oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil.
  2. Combine the Hypericum perforatum flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar, and stir well.
  3. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Hypericum perforatum essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

8. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra essential oils contains flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce puffiness around the eyes. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have a strong ability to combat free radicals and swelling in the skin. The terpenoids, such as alpha-pinene and beta-pinene, also contribute to the oil's anti-inflammatory effects, which can help decrease fluid retention and reduce puffiness.

By reducing inflammation and fighting free radicals, Sambucus nigra essential oils can help alleviate puffy eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Sambucus nigra flowers and leaves. Clean and dry them thoroughly.
  2. Combine the flowers and leaves with 2 cups of carrier oil, such as jojoba or sweet almond oil, in a clean glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After the steeping period, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Sambucus nigra essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use a few drops to help reduce puffy eyes.

9. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum essential oils contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, silymarin, and sesquiterpenes.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce puffiness around the eyes. The sesquiterpenes present in Silybum marianum essential oils have a decongestant effect, which can help to alleviate fluid retention and reduce swelling. The flavonoids in this essential oil also have a vasoconstrictor effect, which can help to narrow blood vessels and reduce blood flow to the affected area.

By reducing inflammation and constricting blood vessels, Silybum marianum essential oils can help to alleviate puffy eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Silybum marianum seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil) in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add 1/2 cup of water to the jar and stir well. Cover the jar with a lid.
  3. Place the jar in a saucepan with 2-3 inches of water. Heat the water over low heat for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Mix 10-15 drops of the Silybum marianum essential oil with 1 tablespoon of carrier oil in a small bowl. Apply the oil to puffy eyes with a cotton ball.

10. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains bilobalide and ginkgolides, which are its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making them effective in reducing puffiness around the eyes. Ginkgolides also improve blood circulation, which helps to reduce fluid retention and swelling in the delicate skin around the eyes. Bilobalide, on the other hand, soothes and calms the skin, reducing redness and irritation.

By combining these properties, Ginkgo biloba essential oils can help to reduce puffiness and leave the skin looking smoother and more refreshed.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil work well).
  2. Combine the Ginkgo biloba leaves and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Make sure the leaves are completely covered by the oil.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the oil extract from the leaves.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Your Ginkgo biloba essential oil is now ready to use. Store it in a dark glass bottle and use 2-3 drops as needed to reduce puffy eyes.

11. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole and alpha-pinene, two active constituents that help reduce puffiness around the eyes.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties, which can help to alleviate swelling and congestion. Cineole, in particular, has been shown to have a cooling effect on the skin, which can help to constrict blood vessels and reduce the appearance of puffy eyes. The decongestant properties of alpha-pinene can also help to clear mucus and promote drainage, further reducing puffiness.

By applying eucalyptus globulus essential oils to the skin around the eyes, you can help to reduce the appearance of puffiness and leave your eyes looking refreshed and revitalized.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat, then add the eucalyptus leaves and let simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean bowl, discarding the leaves.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a separate bowl and let it cool to room temperature.
  5. Use a hydrosol distiller or a still to extract the essential oil from the liquid, then store it in a dark glass bottle.

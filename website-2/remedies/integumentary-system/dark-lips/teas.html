1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains several bioactive constituents, including carnosic acid, rosmanol, and ursolic acid.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce the appearance of dark lips by protecting the skin from oxidative stress and inflammation. Rosmarinic acid, also found in Rosmarinus officinalis, has been shown to inhibit the production of melanin, the pigment responsible for dark skin discoloration. The antimicrobial properties of rosmarinic acid and other constituents in Rosmarinus officinalis teas can also help to prevent infections that can cause darkening of the lips.

By reducing oxidative stress, inflammation, and melanin production, and preventing infections, Rosmarinus officinalis teas may help to promote healthier and lighter-looking lips.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water, 1 tablespoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves, and a cup.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves to the cup.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the leaves and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture and discard the leaves. Let it cool down.
  5. Soak a clean cloth in the tea and apply it to your lips for 10-15 minutes to help soothe and lighten dark lips.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas contains several bioactive constituents that help to lighten and moisturize dark lips.

Aloin and aloe-emodin, two anthraquinones present in the tea, have anti-inflammatory and astringent properties that reduce inflammation and dryness around the mouth. The tea also contains vitamins A, C, and E, as well as beta-carotene, which are antioxidants that protect the skin from damage and promote collagen production. Aloin and aloe-emodin also have a natural bleaching effect, helping to reduce the appearance of dark spots and hyperpigmentation.

By regularly consuming Aloe barbadensis teas, some people find their lips become lighter and healthier-looking.

  1. Cut a 5-inch piece of Aloe barbadensis leaf. Remove the thick green skin.
  2. Cut the inner gel into small pieces and transfer them to a blender.
  3. Add 1 cup of water to the blender and blend the mixture on high speed for 2 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a cup. Discard the solids.
  5. Drink 1/2 cup of the Aloe tea 2 times a day to help moisturize and whiten dark lips.

3. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa teas contains bioactive constituents like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which have potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce the discoloration of dark lips by neutralizing free radicals that damage skin cells. Curcumin also inhibits the production of melanin, the pigment responsible for dark skin and lip color, thereby reducing the appearance of dark lips. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of Curcuma longa teas help to soothe and calm irritated skin, which can contribute to the formation of dark spots.

Regular consumption of Curcuma longa tea can help to promote even skin tone and reduce the appearance of dark lips.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Curcuma longa powder and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of honey to the boiling water and stir well.
  3. Add the Curcuma longa powder to the honey and water mixture and stir gently.
  4. Let the mixture simmer for 5-7 minutes, then strain it into a cup.
  5. Drink the tea warm, 2-3 times a day, to help improve dark lip color.

4. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis teas contains antioxidants like theaflavins and thearubigins, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and redness in the lips.

The tea's high content of vitamin C also promotes collagen production, which can help improve the overall appearance of dark lips. Additionally, Camellia sinensis teas contain flavonoids, such as quercetin, that have been shown to inhibit the production of melanin, the pigment responsible for dark lip color. The tea's anti-inflammatory properties can also help soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing the appearance of dark spots and hyperpigmentation.

Furthermore, the antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties of Camellia sinensis teas can help protect the lips from environmental stressors and damage, promoting healthy and even-toned skin.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of Camellia sinensis leaves and 1 cup of water.
  2. Heat the water in a pot to 100°C (212°F).
  3. Steep the Camellia sinensis leaves in the hot water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea and let it cool.
  5. Use the cooled tea as a face pack to help reduce dark lips. Apply and leave it on for 15-20 minutes.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

Glycyrrhizin also has a mild bleaching effect, which can help to lighten dark spots on the lips.

Regular consumption of Glycyrrhiza glabra teas may help to even out lip color and give a more even appearance.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root powder and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Add the Glycyrrhiza glabra root powder to the boiling water and stir well.
  3. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 5-7 minutes.

  5. Soak a cotton ball in the tea and apply it to the dark lips for 10-15 minutes, 2-3 times a week.

What Are The Main Causes Of Glowing Skin?

Glowing skin is a result of a combination of factors, but three key ingredients play a significant role in achieving this goal.

Hyaluronic Acid is a naturally occurring substance in our skin that helps retain moisture. When applied topically, it can hold up to 1000 times its weight in water, leaving the skin feeling hydrated and plump. This results in a radiant and even-toned complexion. Glycolic Acid, on the other hand, is an alpha-hydroxy acid (AHA) that exfoliates the skin, removing dead skin cells and revealing smoother skin underneath.

As skin cells turnover, it helps to unclog pores and reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles, giving the skin a healthy and glowing appearance. Vitamin C is an antioxidant that plays a crucial role in protecting the skin from damage caused by free radicals. When applied topically, it helps to brighten the skin, reducing the appearance of dark spots and hyperpigmentation. It also stimulates collagen production, which helps to improve skin elasticity and firmness, giving the skin a more youthful and radiant appearance.

These three ingredients work together to help achieve glowing skin by providing hydration, exfoliation, and protection from environmental stressors.

Below are the main causes of glowing skin.

  • Hyaluronic Acid: Hyaluronic Acid helps skin glow by drawing in and holding large amounts of moisture from the air, making the skin look plump, smooth, and radiant.
  • Glycolic Acid: Glycolic Acid is a cause of glowing skin because it gently peels off the top layer of dead skin cells, revealing smoother, brighter skin underneath and allowing its natural glow to shine through.
  • Vitamin C: Vitamin C helps to create glowing skin by protecting the skin from damage caused by free radicals and helping to repair and rebuild damaged skin cells, resulting in a brighter, smoother, and more even-toned complexion.
  • Salicylic Acid: Salicylic acid helps to remove dead skin cells and unclog pores, allowing the natural glow of healthy skin to show through.
  • Beta Hydroxy Acid: Beta Hydroxy Acid helps to remove dead skin cells and unclog pores, allowing skin to reflect light more evenly and giving it a healthier, glowing appearance.
  • Niacinamide: Niacinamide helps to create a glowing complexion by improving skin's natural barrier function, reducing inflammation, and increasing collagen production, which ultimately results in a smoother, brighter, and more even-toned skin appearance.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Glowing Skin?

If you have glowing skin, there are certain herbs to use with caution.

One of them is Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis), which can dry out your skin due to its high berberine content. Berberine is a compound that can strip your skin of its natural oils, leading to irritation and dryness. Another herb to avoid is Yarrow (Achillea millefolium), which can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some people. Yarrow contains compounds that can stimulate your skin, making it more sensitive to the sun and other irritants. Sage (Salvia officinalis) is another herb to use with caution.

While it's often used to improve skin tone and texture, its high thujone content can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals. Turmeric (Curcuma longa) is also worth mentioning. While it's known for its anti-inflammatory properties, its high curcumin content can cause skin sensitivity and allergic reactions in some people. Finally, Wintergreen (Gaultheria procumbens) is another herb to use with care. Its high methyl salicylate content can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions, especially if you have sensitive skin.

These herbs can still be used safely if you're aware of their potential side effects and take necessary precautions, such as patch testing and using them in moderation.

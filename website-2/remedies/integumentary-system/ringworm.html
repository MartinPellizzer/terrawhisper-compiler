What are the causes of ringworm?

Ringworm is a common fungal infection that affects people of all ages.

The main causes of ringworm are different types of fungi, including Trichophyton rubrum, Trichophyton mentagrophytes, Trichophyton tonsurans, Microsporum canis, and Epidermophyton floccosum. These fungi are naturally found on our skin, in soil, and on animals, but they can cause problems if they get inside our body through a cut or crack in the skin. Trichophyton rubrum is one of the most common causes of ringworm, and it often affects the feet, groin, and armpits. This fungus thrives in warm and moist environments, which is why it's so common in areas with poor hygiene and in people who sweat a lot. Trichophyton mentagrophytes is another common cause of ringworm, and it's often found in soil and on animals.

It can cause the infection to spread quickly, especially if the affected area is scratched or scratched again. Trichophyton tonsurans is a type of fungus that's commonly found in warm and humid climates, and it often affects the scalp and beard. Microsporum canis is a type of fungus that's commonly found on cats and dogs, and it can cause ringworm in people who come into contact with infected animals. This fungus is highly contagious and can spread quickly if not treated properly. Epidermophyton floccosum is another type of fungus that can cause ringworm, and it often affects the feet and groin.

All of these fungi can cause ringworm if they get inside our body, but they can be treated with antifungal medications and good hygiene practices.

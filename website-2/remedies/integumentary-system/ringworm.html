What Are The Main Causes Of Ringworm?

Ringworm is a common fungal infection caused by three main types of fungi: Trichophyton, Microsporum, and Epidermophyton.

Trichophyton is the most common cause of ringworm and it affects both humans and animals. It thrives in warm and humid environments, making it more likely to spread in areas with poor hygiene. Trichophyton can be spread through direct contact with an infected person or animal, or by touching contaminated surfaces or objects.

Microsporum is another type of fungus that causes ringworm, especially in children. It is highly contagious and can be spread through direct contact with an infected person or animal, or by touching contaminated items. Epidermophyton is a type of fungus that causes ringworm on the skin, especially between the toes and on the feet. It is less contagious than Trichophyton and Microsporum, but can still be spread through direct contact with an infected person or animal.

Ringworm can also be caused by other factors such as poor hygiene, weakened immune system, and certain medical conditions.

Below are the main causes of ringworm.

  • Trichophyton: Trichophyton is a type of fungus that causes ringworm because it infects the skin and hair, causing an itchy, circular rash to form.
  • Microsporum: Microsporum is a type of fungus that causes ringworm because it produces spores that infect the skin and hair of humans and animals, leading to the characteristic ring-shaped rash.
  • Epidermophyton: Epidermophyton is a type of fungus that causes ringworm because it produces spores that can infect the skin, leading to the characteristic ring-shaped rash.
  • Candida: Candida can cause ringworm because it is a type of fungus that grows and spreads rapidly on the skin, causing symptoms similar to a fungal infection like ringworm.
  • Malassezia: Malassezia is a type of fungus that can cause ringworm because it naturally lives on the skin of many people, and under certain conditions, it can multiply and cause an infection that leads to the characteristic ring-shaped rash of ringworm.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Ringworm?

If you have ringworm, there are some herbs you should avoid using.

One of these is Sage (Salvia officinalis), as it may slow down wound healing, and ringworm is a skin infection that needs to heal quickly. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) is another herb to avoid, as it may cause allergic reactions in some people, and ringworm can be itchy and uncomfortable already.

Echinacea (Echinacea spp.). is a herb often used to boost the immune system, but it's not effective against ringworm and may even cause skin irritation in some cases.

Lastly, Valerian root (Valeriana officinalis) is a herb that can interact with certain medications, and ringworm treatment often involves antibiotics or antifungals that may interact with Valerian root.

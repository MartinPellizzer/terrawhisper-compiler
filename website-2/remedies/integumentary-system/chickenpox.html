Chickenpox: Causes, Medicinal Herbs, and Natural Cures

What are the causes of chickenpox?

Chickenpox is caused by the Varicella-Zoster virus, which spreads through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes, or through direct contact with the rash.

People with weakened immune systems, known as immunocompromised individuals, are more likely to catch the virus because their bodies can't fight it off as well. Genetic predisposition also plays a role, as some people may be more prone to developing severe symptoms. Chickenpox is highly contagious and can spread easily through social contact, such as touching or shaking hands with someone who has the virus.

However, getting vaccinated against Varicella-Zoster can significantly reduce the risk of contracting the virus, especially in countries where the vaccine is widely available.

What Are The Main Causes Of Chickenpox?

Chickenpox is caused by the Varicella-Zoster virus, which is a type of Herpesvirus.

The correct name of the virus is Human Herpesvirus 3. This virus is highly contagious and can spread from person to person through direct contact with the rash or through airborne droplets when an infected person coughs or sneezes.

When someone comes into contact with the virus, it enters their body and starts to multiply. The immune system tries to fight the virus, but it can cause a range of symptoms, including a fever, headache, and a characteristic itchy rash that covers the skin. The rash is made up of small, fluid-filled blisters that eventually crust over and heal.

The virus can remain dormant in the body for years, but it can reactivate later in life to cause shingles, a different but related condition.

Below are the main causes of chickenpox.

  • Varicella-Zoster: Varicella-Zoster is a virus that causes chickenpox because it infects the body and triggers a reaction that leads to the formation of itchy, blister-like rashes, which are the characteristic symptoms of the disease.
  • Herpesvirus: Chickenpox is caused by the Herpesvirus, specifically the Varicella-Zoster Virus (VZV), which infects people and causes a painful and itchy rash on the skin.
  • Human Herpesvirus 3: Human Herpesvirus 3 is a cause of chickenpox because it infects the body's nerve cells and releases a chemical that triggers an outbreak of itchy, blister-like sores.
  • HHV3: HHV3, also known as the varicella-zoster virus, is a cause of chickenpox because it infects the body's cells and causes them to produce a rash, fever, and other symptoms that are characteristic of the disease.
  • VZV: The Varicella-Zoster Virus (VZV) causes chickenpox because it infects people's bodies and triggers a reaction that leads to the development of itchy, blister-like rashes and other symptoms associated with the illness.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Chickenpox?

When you have chickenpox, it's best to avoid certain herbs that might make your symptoms worse or slow down the healing process.

One such herb is St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum), as it can increase the risk of bleeding, especially with the skin lesions that come with chickenpox. Another herb to steer clear of is Garlic (Allium sativum), as it can thin the blood and cause more bleeding when you cut yourself while scratching the itchy chickenpox blisters.

You should also avoid Turmeric (Curcuma longa) as it can make the skin more sensitive and make the itching worse. Feverfew (Tanacetum parthenium) is another herb to avoid, as it can increase the risk of bleeding and interact with any medications you're taking to treat the chickenpox.

Lastly, avoid Ginkgo biloba as it can also increase the risk of bleeding and interact with your medications.

What herbal creams you should avoid if you have dark circles?

If you have dark circles, there are some herbal creams you should avoid using.

For example, creams with chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) or licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) may not be the best choice. This is because chamomile can cause blood vessels to swell, which might make dark circles look worse. Licorice root, on the other hand, can cause your skin to retain water, leading to puffiness and making dark circles more noticeable.

Another herbal cream to avoid is one that contains saw palmetto (Serenoa repens). This herb can cause your skin to become greasy and oily, which might clog pores and make dark circles look worse. Similarly, creams with passionflower (Passiflora incarnata) should be avoided, as this herb can cause drowsiness and may make your eyes look puffy. It's also worth noting that creams with evening primrose (Oenothera biennis) should be used with caution.

While this herb is often used to soothe skin irritations, it can also cause your skin to become sensitive and react to other products, which might irritate the skin under your eyes and make dark circles look worse.

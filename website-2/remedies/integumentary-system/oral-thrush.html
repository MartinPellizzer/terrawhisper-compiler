What Are The Main Causes Of Oral Thrush?

Oral thrush is a fungal infection that affects the mouth and tongue.

It's caused by a yeast called Candida, which is usually present in the mouth but can grow out of control in some cases. Three main causes of oral thrush are: HIV, diabetes, and antibiotics. HIV, or human immunodeficiency virus, weakens the immune system, making it harder for the body to fight off infections like Candida. When the immune system is weakened, Candida can multiply quickly and cause oral thrush.

Diabetes is another cause of oral thrush. People with diabetes have high blood sugar levels, which can create an environment where Candida can grow. When blood sugar levels are high, Candida can feed on the sugar and multiply rapidly, causing oral thrush. Antibiotics are also a cause of oral thrush. Antibiotics kill bacteria in the body, but they can also kill the good bacteria that help keep Candida in check.

When good bacteria are reduced, Candida can grow out of control and cause oral thrush.

Below are the main causes of oral thrush.

  • HIV: HIV is a cause of oral thrush because it weakens the body's immune system, making it harder for the immune system to fight off the Candida fungus that causes oral thrush.
  • Diabetes: Diabetes is a cause of oral thrush because high levels of sugar in the blood can make it easier for a type of fungus called Candida to grow in the mouth and throat.
  • Antibiotics: The use of antibiotics can cause oral thrush by killing off the good bacteria in the mouth, allowing the fungus that causes thrush to overgrow and multiply uncontrollably.
  • Steroids: Steroids weaken the immune system, making it harder for the body to fight off a type of fungus that causes oral thrush, an infection that forms white patches in the mouth.
  • Pregnancy: During pregnancy, hormonal changes can cause an imbalance in the body's natural yeast growth, leading to the overgrowth of Candida fungus in the mouth and causing oral thrush.
  • Breastfeeding: Breastfeeding is a cause of oral thrush because the yeast (a type of fungus) that causes thrush can be passed from a mother's breast to her baby's mouth during feeding.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Oral Thrush?

If you have oral thrush, you should be careful with certain herbs that can make it worse.

One herb to avoid is peppermint (Mentha piperita), as it can cause digestive issues and make fungal infections like oral thrush spread. Another herb to avoid is licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra), as it can weaken your immune system, making it harder for your body to fight off the fungal infection.

Sage (Salvia officinalis) is also not recommended, as it can dry out your mouth and make it easier for the fungus to grow. Thyme (Thymus spp.). may also not be suitable, as it can cause stomach issues and interact with medications.

Finally, wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) and goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) should be used with caution, as they can be toxic in large amounts and interact with medications.

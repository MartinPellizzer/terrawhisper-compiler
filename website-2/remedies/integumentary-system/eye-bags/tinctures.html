11 Herbal Tinctures For Eye Bags

Shift workers often put in long hours, disrupting their natural sleep patterns.

This can lead to dark circles and puffy eyes, also known as eye bags. When you don't get enough sleep, your body's ability to remove excess fluids and waste is impaired. As a result, fluid builds up in the delicate skin around the eyes, causing it to swell. This can make you look tired and exhausted, even if you're not feeling it. Eye bags can affect your self-confidence and make it harder to get through your day. They can also make you look older than you are, which can be a concern for shift workers who may already feel like they're missing out on a normal life. When you're working non-traditional hours, it's hard to get the rest you need.

You might have to stay up late to finish a project or get ready for an early shift. Even if you're trying to get some sleep, you might wake up in the middle of the night and have trouble falling back asleep. All of this can take a toll on your skin, making eye bags a common problem for shift workers. Herbal tinctures can help shift workers treat eye bags. These liquid extracts are made from plants that have natural anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. When you take a tincture, the active ingredients are absorbed into your bloodstream and can help reduce swelling and promote healthy skin. They can also help improve circulation, which can help get rid of excess fluids and waste that can contribute to eye bags.

By using herbal tinctures, shift workers can find some relief from eye bags and feel more refreshed and alert during their shifts.

Taking valeriana officinalis tinctures may help reduce eye bags and puffiness by decreasing water retention and swelling.

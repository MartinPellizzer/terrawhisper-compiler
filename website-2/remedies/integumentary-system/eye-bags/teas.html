What herbal teas you should avoid if you have eye bags?

If you have eye bags, there are some herbal teas you should avoid.

One herb to avoid is 'Ephedra sinica', also known as Ma Huang. This herb contains ephedrine, a compound that can increase heart rate and blood pressure. Eye bags can be a sign of fluid retention, and ephedrine can make this problem worse by causing the body to hold onto water. Another herb to avoid is 'Valeriana officinalis', or valerian root. While valerian is often used as a natural sleep aid, it can also cause drowsiness and make you feel more tired during the day. If you're already feeling tired and puffy due to eye bags, valerian root can make this worse.

You should also avoid 'Glycyrrhiza glabra', or licorice root. Glycyrrhizin, a compound in licorice root, can cause your body to retain water and sodium. This can make eye bags worse and even lead to other health problems, such as high blood pressure. 'Zingiber officinale', or ginger, is another herb to avoid if you have eye bags. While ginger can be helpful for nausea and digestion, it can also cause your body to release histamine, a chemical that can increase fluid retention and make eye bags worse. Lastly, avoid 'Panax ginseng', or ginseng. Ginseng can increase heart rate and blood pressure, which can make eye bags worse by causing the body to hold onto water.

These herbs can exacerbate the problem of eye bags, so it's best to avoid them if you're trying to reduce puffiness and dark circles under your eyes.

8 Herbal Essential Oils For Eye Bags

Herbal essential oils can be a great help in reducing eye bags.

When we're tired or stressed, our bodies hold onto water, causing puffiness under our eyes. Herbal teas, made from plants like Lavandula angustifolia (Lavender), Aloe barbadensis (Aloe Vera), and Rosmarinus officinalis (Rosemary), have anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce this puffiness. For example, Lavender oil is known for its calming effects, which can help to reduce stress and promote better sleep. When we get enough sleep, our bodies don't hold onto water as much, resulting in less puffiness.

Aloe Vera oil, on the other hand, has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce swelling and soothe irritated skin. This can be especially helpful if you have sensitive skin or allergies that make your eye bags worse. Rosemary oil has a stimulating effect, which can help to improve circulation and reduce puffiness. When our circulation is good, our bodies are able to remove excess fluids more efficiently, resulting in less eye bags.

By incorporating herbal essential oils into your daily routine, you can enjoy a range of benefits, including improved sleep, reduced stress, and a more refreshed and awake appearance.

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These properties can help reduce puffiness and swelling associated with eye bags. The calming effect of linalool and linalyl acetate can also help to relax the facial muscles, which can contribute to eye bags. Additionally, the essential oil's ability to improve circulation can help to reduce the appearance of eye bags by bringing oxygen and nutrients to the skin.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to alleviate eye bags.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Lavandula angustifolia flowers, a glass jar, and a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil.
  2. Add 1/4 cup of carrier oil to the glass jar and add the Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the Lavandula angustifolia essential oil in a dark glass bottle, using 5-7 drops for eye bags as a relaxing treatment.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains triterpenoids, glycoproteins, and polysaccharides.

These compounds help to reduce puffiness and swelling under the eyes by improving circulation and reducing fluid retention. The anti-inflammatory properties of aloe vera's salicylic acid and flavonoids also soothe and calm the skin, reducing redness and irritation. Moreover, the antioxidant properties of aloe vera's vitamins A, C, and E help to neutralize free radicals that can cause damage to the skin around the eyes.

By using Aloe barbadensis essential oils, you can help to reduce the appearance of eye bags and achieve a more rested and refreshed look.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Aloe barbadensis gel from a mature plant. Remove any impurities.
  2. Transfer the Aloe gel to a blender and blend on high speed for 5-7 minutes to break down the gel.
  3. Heat 2 tablespoons of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) in a saucepan over low heat.
  4. Slowly add the blended Aloe gel to the heated oil, stirring constantly to prevent separation.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Store in a dark place.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains compounds like beta-pinene, camphor, and borneol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties can help reduce puffiness and swelling around the eyes. The oils' antioxidant properties, thanks to constituents like carnosic acid, also help protect the delicate skin around the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. Additionally, the decongestant properties of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils, due to its eucalyptol content, can help reduce fluid retention in the under-eye area.

By applying a few drops of this oil to the skin, you can potentially alleviate the appearance of eye bags.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water to 180°F (82°C) and add the leaves. Let it steep for 2 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture and discard the leaves. Keep the liquid.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a distillation apparatus or a double boiler. Collect the steam.
  5. Let the steam condense and collect the essential oil in a separate container. Use a dropper to apply it to eye bags.

4. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids and terpenoids.

These compounds help reduce inflammation and improve circulation, which can contribute to the appearance of eye bags. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba essential oils, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help combat puffiness. The terpenoids, including ginkgolides and bilobalide, improve blood flow and reduce fluid retention, making eye bags less noticeable.

Regular use of Ginkgo biloba essential oils may help reduce the appearance of eye bags by addressing the underlying causes of puffiness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil) in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil to the jar and mix well.
  3. Heat the mixture in a water bath at 100°F (38°C) for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Store the Ginkgo biloba essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use 2-3 drops under the eyes to reduce eye bags.

5. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains a high concentration of limonene, beta-pinene, and geraniol, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help to reduce puffiness and dark circles under the eyes by minimizing the breakdown of collagen and elastin, the skin's supporting fibers. The antioxidant properties of Cymbopogon citratus essential oils also help to neutralize free radicals that can damage the skin and cause it to appear tired and puffy. Additionally, the oil's ability to improve circulation may help to reduce the appearance of eye bags by promoting the removal of excess fluids and toxins.

By using Cymbopogon citratus essential oils in a skincare routine, individuals may be able to reduce the appearance of eye bags and achieve a more rested and refreshed appearance.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Cymbopogon citratus leaves.
  3. Let it simmer for 30 minutes, then turn off the heat and let it steep for another 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  5. Add a tablespoon of jojoba oil to the liquid and mix well. Store the oil in a cool, dark place.

6. Echinacea purpurea

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce puffiness and swelling under the eyes.   3. Add 2 cups of water and 1 cup of vodka to the jar. Cover the jar with a cloth.

  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into another container. Discard the solids and store the oil in a dark glass bottle for eye bag relief.

7. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like cineole and alpha-pinene, which are known for their anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds can help reduce puffiness and swelling under the eyes by constricting blood vessels and reducing fluid accumulation. Cineole, in particular, has a decongestant effect that can help alleviate dark circles and eye bags by improving blood circulation. The antioxidant properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils can also help protect the delicate skin around the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

By applying eucalyptus globulus essential oils topically, you can potentially benefit from its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties to reduce the appearance of eye bags.

  2. Heat the water over medium heat until it reaches 100°F (38°C).
  3. Add the Eucalyptus leaves to the water and let it steep for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 2-3 days to allow the essential oils to separate and settle at the bottom of the bottle.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent eye bags from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help reduce puffiness under the eyes.

Peppermint and chamomile teas are calming, which can ease eye strain and fatigue. These teas may also help reduce water retention, making the skin appear smoother and less puffy.

Some herbal teas, like green tea, contain antioxidants that can help soothe and calm the skin.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for eye bags every day?

Consuming herbal teas for eye bags daily can be safe if you choose a tea that's caffeine-free and made from natural ingredients.

However, be cautious of teas with added sugars or strong flavorings that can irritate your eyes.

It's also essential to check the tea's ingredients and brewing instructions to avoid any potential issues.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in eye bags?

Herbal teas can help with eye bags, but the time it takes to see results varies.

Some people notice a reduction in puffiness within a few hours, while others may not see a difference for a few days.

This can depend on the type of herbal tea and individual factors, such as lifestyle and diet.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for eye bags?

Drink herbal tea for eye bags in the morning, after waking up.

This helps reduce puffiness and dark circles throughout the day. The caffeine-free brew won't interfere with your sleep.

Its soothing effects will calm your skin, making you feel refreshed and awake for the day ahead.

What Are The Main Causes Of Dry Scalp?

A dry scalp can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is dandruff, a condition where skin cells on the scalp die and flake off. This usually happens because of an imbalance in the skin's natural pH levels, allowing fungus to grow and irritate the scalp. The fungus, known as Malassezia, feeds on the skin's natural oils, producing chemicals that cause the scalp to become itchy and flaky. Another cause of a dry scalp is psoriasis, a chronic skin condition that causes red, scaly patches on the skin.

In the case of psoriasis, the immune system mistakenly attacks healthy skin cells, leading to a buildup of dead skin cells on the scalp. This can cause the scalp to become dry, itchy, and flaky. Eczema, also known as atopic dermatitis, is another condition that can cause a dry scalp. Eczema is a chronic inflammatory condition that causes the skin to become dry, itchy, and scaly.

When eczema affects the scalp, it can cause redness, irritation, and flaking, leading to a dry scalp.

Below are the main causes of dry scalp.

  • Dandruff: Dandruff is a cause of dry scalp because the fungus that causes it makes the scalp produce dead skin cells too quickly, leading to dryness and flakiness.
  • Psoriasis: Psoriasis is a cause of dry scalp because it leads to the rapid growth of skin cells, which can cause dead skin cells to build up and flake off, resulting in dryness, itchiness, and irritation on the scalp.
  • Eczema: Eczema can cause dry scalp because it leads to inflammation and irritation on the scalp, stripping the skin of its natural oils and moisture, resulting in dryness and itchiness.
  • Tinea: Tinea is a common fungal infection that can cause dry scalp by producing spores that irritate the scalp, leading to inflammation and dryness.
  • Fungal Infection: Fungal infections, such as ringworm of the scalp, can cause dry scalp by breaking down the skin's natural oils and creating a perfect environment for fungus to thrive and exacerbate dryness.
  • Diet: A poor diet lacking essential nutrients such as omega-3 fatty acids, zinc, and vitamin B can cause inflammation and weaken the skin, leading to a dry and itchy scalp.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Dry Scalp?

If you have a dry scalp, there are some herbs you should avoid using.

Sage (Salvia officinalis) is one of them, as it can dry out your scalp further. Thyme (Thymus vulgari) is another herb to avoid, as it can irritate the skin on your scalp.

Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) and Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium) are also not recommended, as they can cause allergic reactions or increase inflammation on a sensitive scalp. Eucalyptus (Eucalyptus globulus) is not suitable for a dry scalp either, as it can strip the skin of its natural oils. Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) may seem calming, but it can be too harsh on a dry scalp.

Finally, Hyssop (Hyssopus officinalis) contains compounds that can dry out your scalp even more.

What Are The Main Causes Of Pimples?

Pimples occur when the pores on our skin become blocked.

The main causes of pimples are hormones, bacteria, and stress. Hormonal changes trigger the oil glands in our skin to produce more oil, which can lead to clogged pores.

This is especially true during puberty, menstruation, and pregnancy. Bacteria, particularly a type called Propionibacterium acnes (P. acnes), live naturally on our skin and break down the oil in our pores. However, when pores become blocked, bacteria can multiply and cause inflammation, leading to pimples.

Stress also plays a role in pimples as it can increase the production of hormones and make our skin more sensitive, leading to inflammation and clogged pores.

Below are the main causes of pimples.

  • Hormones: Hormonal changes, such as those that occur during puberty or menstruation, cause an increase in oil production in the skin, leading to clogged pores and the formation of pimples.
  • Bacteria: Bacteria, specifically called Propionibacterium acnes, live on the skin and can cause pimples by producing waste that leads to inflammation and clogged pores, especially when mixed with dead skin cells and oil.
  • Stress: When we are stressed, our bodies release more hormones like cortisol, which can make our oil glands produce too much oil, clogging our pores and leading to pimples.
  • Genetics: Genetics can cause pimples because it affects the way our skin produces oil, leading to clogged pores when the oil and dead skin cells combine, causing inflammation and resulting in pimples.
  • Diet: When you eat too much junk food or sugary treats, it causes your body to produce more oil, which clogs your pores and leads to pimples.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Pimples?

If you have pimples, it's best to avoid herbs that can irritate your skin or make it worse.

One herb to be careful with is St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum). It can cause skin sensitivity and allergic reactions, which might make your pimples worse.

Another herb to avoid is Sassafras (Sassafras albidum), as it can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions, and may also increase oil production in the skin, leading to more breakouts. Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) is also not recommended, as it can cause skin dryness and irritation, which can make your skin more prone to pimples. Additionally, Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) contains berberine, which can be too harsh for sensitive skin and cause more inflammation, making your pimples worse.

Finally, Comfrey (Symphytum officinale) contains allantoin, which can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some people, making it best to avoid if you have pimples.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains compounds like cineole and terpinen-4-ol, which have antifungal and antibacterial properties.

These properties help to reduce the presence of fungal and bacterial infections that can cause an itchy scalp. Cineole also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing itchiness and discomfort. The antioxidants in Melaleuca alternifolia creams, including vitamin C and beta-carotene, help to protect the scalp from damage caused by free radicals.

Regular use of Melaleuca alternifolia creams can help to control and prevent fungal and bacterial infections, leading to a healthier and itch-free scalp.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 drops of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Once melted, add the shea butter and stir until it's fully incorporated. Remove from heat.
  4. Let the mixture cool until it reaches a temperature of around 120°F to 130°F. This is when it starts to thicken.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and add 2 drops of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil to each container. Mix well and let cool completely before use.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-ane that help soothe and calm an itchy scalp.

Aloin has anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce redness and swelling, while aloe-emodin has antiseptic and antibacterial properties that prevent infections and promote healing. Aloe-ane, on the other hand, has hydrating properties that moisturize and calm the scalp, reducing itchiness and discomfort. The anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties of aloe barbadensis creams also help to reduce irritation and itchiness caused by dandruff, eczema, and other scalp conditions.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, aloe barbadensis creams provide relief from itchy scalp and promote a healthy scalp environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of beeswax.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or in a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from heat and stir in the Aloe barbadensis gel and shea butter until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify at room temperature.
  5. Once solidified, use a spoon to scoop out the desired amount and apply it to itchy scalp areas as needed.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains carnosic acid, rosmarinic acid, and camphor as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce redness and swelling on the scalp. The antiseptic properties of camphor and rosmarinic acid also combat fungal infections and dandruff, common causes of itchy scalp. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of carnosic acid protect the scalp from environmental stressors and damage, promoting healthy hair growth.

By addressing the root causes of an itchy scalp, Rosmarinus officinalis creams can provide relief and soothe the scalp.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup distilled water, 1/2 cup coconut oil, 1/4 cup shea butter, 2 tablespoons beeswax, 2 tablespoons dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves, 2 teaspoons vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Add distilled water, dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves, and vitamin E oil to the mixture. Stir well and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Let it cool and solidify before transferring it to an airtight container.

4. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, carotenoids, and flavonoids as active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help soothe an itchy scalp. The triterpenoid saponins reduce inflammation and prevent infection, while the carotenoids and flavonoids calm and stabilize the skin. The anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds reduce redness and swelling, making it easier to manage an itchy scalp.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infection, Calendula officinalis creams provide relief for an itchy scalp.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula officinalis flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of olive oil.
  2. Combine the dried Calendula flowers and coconut oil in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 2-3 hours, or until the oil turns golden.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. In a separate saucepan, melt the shea butter and beeswax over low heat. Add the olive oil and stir until combined.
  5. Combine the infused oil and melted shea butter mixture. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool. Once set, your Calendula cream for itchy scalp is ready to use.

5. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica creams contains bioactive constituents like histamine, polysaccharides, and triterpenoid saponins.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that help to calm itchy scalps. Histamine, for example, has antipruritic effects, which means it can reduce itching and irritation. The polysaccharides in Urtica dioica creams also have antioxidant properties that can help to soothe and protect the scalp.

By reducing inflammation and soothing the scalp, Urtica dioica creams can provide relief for itchy scalps.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Urtica dioica leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water and bring it to a boil. Then, add the Urtica dioica leaves and let them steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the Urtica dioica leaves.
  4. Mix 1/4 cup of coconut oil or shea butter with 2 tablespoons of the cooled Urtica dioica infusion in a small bowl.
  5. Transfer the mixture to a clean jar and store it in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. Apply a small amount to your itchy scalp as needed.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two key bioactive constituents that make it effective in soothing an itchy scalp.

Linalool, an essential oil, has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce redness and swelling, while linalyl acetate, another essential oil, has antiseptic properties that help to combat fungal infections that can cause itchiness. The antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties of these constituents work together to calm the scalp and reduce irritation. The soothing effects of Lavandula angustifolia creams also help to reduce flaking and discomfort associated with an itchy scalp.

This makes it a popular choice for people with scalp conditions like dandruff and eczema.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool slightly. Add the dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and stir well.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Add the vitamin E oil and mix well. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify before use.

7. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains a high concentration of curcuminoids, including demethoxycurcumin and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which have potent anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce redness and swelling associated with an itchy scalp, providing relief from irritation and discomfort. Turmeric, the active ingredient in Curcuma longa, also contains antioxidants like ferulic acid, which protect the scalp from environmental stressors and promote healthy hair growth. The anti-inflammatory properties of curcuminoids also help reduce the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which can contribute to an itchy scalp.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy scalp environment, Curcuma longa creams can help alleviate itchy scalp symptoms.

  1. Mix 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 1 tablespoon of shea butter, and 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder in a small bowl.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of honey and 1 teaspoon of aloe vera gel to the bowl. Stir well to combine.
  3. Heat the mixture in a double boiler or in a microwave-safe bowl for 10-15 seconds. Stir until smooth.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool for a few minutes. Add 1 teaspoon of essential oil (like tea tree oil) if desired.
  5. Transfer the mixture to a small container and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Apply to itchy scalp as needed.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains bioactive constituents like triterpene saponins and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that help soothe and calm an itchy scalp. The triterpene saponins in calendula tincture can reduce inflammation and irritation, while the flavonoids have antioxidant properties that protect the scalp from damage. The antiseptic properties of calendula help prevent infections that can cause itchiness and flakiness.

By reducing inflammation, preventing infections, and protecting the scalp, calendula officinalis tinctures can promote a healthy and comfortable scalp.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers and 1 cup of 80% vodka or glycerin in a clean glass container.
  2. Combine Calendula flowers and vodka/glycerin in the container. Make sure the flowers are completely covered.
  3. Store the container in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the tincture to a dropper bottle and use 5-7 drops, mixed with a tablespoon of coconut oil or olive oil, to apply to the itchy scalp.

2. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica tinctures contains bioactive constituents like histamine, acetylcholine, and beta-sitosterol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antipruritic properties, which help reduce inflammation and itching on the scalp. The antihistamine properties of histamine and acetylcholine in Urtica dioica tinctures can block the release of histamine, a chemical that causes itching. Beta-sitosterol, a sterol found in the plant, has anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe and calm the skin.

By reducing inflammation and blocking the release of histamine, Urtica dioica tinctures can help alleviate itchy scalp conditions.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Urtica dioica leaves, stems, and flowers. Clean and chop them.
  2. Combine 1 cup of chopped Urtica dioica with 2 cups of 80% vodka in a glass jar.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and store it in a dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the tincture in dark glass bottles and label them. Store them in a cool, dark place.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis tinctures contains bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help soothe and calm an itchy scalp. Aloin and aloe-emodin reduce inflammation and prevent further irritation, while acemannan stimulates the immune system to fight off fungal and bacterial infections that can cause itchiness. The anti-inflammatory properties of aloe barbadensis tinctures also help to reduce redness and swelling on the scalp, providing quick relief from itchiness.

By targeting the root causes of itchy scalp, aloe barbadensis tinctures provide a natural and effective solution to this common problem.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 2 cups of vodka, and a clean glass jar.

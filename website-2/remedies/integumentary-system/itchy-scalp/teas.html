Itchy scalp in athletes: 10 teas to try

Athletes often experience an itchy scalp due to tight headgear, sweaty helmets, and frequent washing of hair with harsh chemicals.

This constant irritation can lead to discomfort, distraction, and even affect their performance. Imagine trying to focus on a game or a workout while constantly feeling like scratching your scalp. It can be frustrating, especially when you're trying to give your best. The itchiness can also cause anxiety, making it harder to sleep and recover from physical activities.

It's not just about the itch, it's about how it affects their daily lives and overall well-being. When athletes experience an itchy scalp, they may try various treatments, but sometimes these treatments can be harsh or have side effects. That's where herbal teas come in. These teas are a natural and gentle way to soothe the scalp and reduce itching. They work by calming the skin, reducing inflammation, and promoting a healthy scalp environment.

By incorporating herbal teas into their routine, athletes can find relief from itchy scalp and focus on what matters most – their performance and recovery.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia teas, also known as tea tree oil tea, may help with an itchy scalp due to its antifungal and antibacterial properties.

The main constituents, including cineole, terpinen-4-ol, and limonene, have been shown to reduce inflammation and kill fungi that can cause itchiness. To try using Melaleuca alternifolia tea for an itchy scalp, start by brewing a cup of tea using 1 teaspoon of dried tea tree leaves in 8 ounces of boiling water.

Let it steep for 5-7 minutes and then strain it. You can apply this tea directly to your scalp using a cotton ball, or add it to your regular shampoo. Applying the tea to your scalp may help reduce itchiness and inflammation, and may also help to soothe and calm irritated skin.

However, it's essential to note that individual results may vary, and it's best to do a patch test before applying the tea to a larger area.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas, also known as rosemary tea, may help with an itchy scalp due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Rosemary tea contains compounds like carnosic acid and rosmarinic acid, which have been shown to reduce inflammation and fight off bacteria and fungi that can cause irritation. The antiseptic properties of rosemary tea can also help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing redness and itching. To try rosemary tea for an itchy scalp, start by making a strong tea by steeping 1 teaspoon of dried rosemary leaves in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it to the scalp. You can use a cotton ball or soft cloth to apply the tea to the affected area, or add it to your regular shampoo or conditioner. When you use rosemary tea for an itchy scalp, you may experience a reduction in inflammation and itching, and an improvement in scalp health. The tea may also help to promote hair growth and reduce dandruff.

However, it's essential to note that rosemary tea may not work for everyone, and it's always a good idea to do a patch test before using it on a larger area.

3. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica teas, also known as nettle tea, can help alleviate an itchy scalp due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The tea contains compounds like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which work together to soothe and calm the skin on your scalp. These constituents help to reduce inflammation and kill bacteria that can cause itchiness and irritation. To use Urtica dioica tea for an itchy scalp, you can start by brewing a cup of the tea and letting it cool.

Then, apply the cooled tea directly to your scalp using a cotton ball or a gentle massage. Leave the tea on for about 15-20 minutes before rinsing it off with warm water. You can repeat this process a few times a week to see improvement. After using Urtica dioica tea, you may notice a decrease in itchiness and redness on your scalp, and your skin may feel softer and more balanced.

Additionally, the tea's antifungal properties may help to prevent fungal infections that can contribute to an itchy scalp.

5. Aloe vera

Aloe vera teas can be a helpful remedy for an itchy scalp due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

When you apply aloe vera tea to your itchy scalp, you may experience a reduction in itching and inflammation within a few days. Repeated use of aloe vera tea may lead to long-term relief from itchy scalp symptoms, and some people have reported improvements in their scalp health after regular use.

When you use Artemisia absinthium tea for an itchy scalp, you can expect to see some relief from itching and inflammation within a few days. However, it's crucial to remember that wormwood tea can interact with certain medications and worsen some health conditions, so always consult with a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you're pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking medications.

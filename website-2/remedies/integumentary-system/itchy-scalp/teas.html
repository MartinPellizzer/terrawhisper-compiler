1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains compounds like carnosic acid and rosmarinic acid, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

These properties can help soothe itchy scalps and reduce inflammation caused by conditions like dandruff or eczema. The tea's antioxidant properties also help protect the scalp from damage caused by free radicals. When you drink Rosmarinus officinalis tea, the carnosic acid and rosmarinic acid are absorbed into your bloodstream and then travel to the scalp, where they can help reduce inflammation and promote healthy hair growth. To use Rosmarinus officinalis tea for an itchy scalp, you can try the following: steep a teaspoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain the tea and let it cool.

You can apply the cooled tea directly to your scalp using a cotton ball or swab, or you can add it to your regular shampoo and conditioner for an extra dose of benefits. It's also a good idea to drink the tea internally to reap its full benefits. When you use Rosmarinus officinalis tea for an itchy scalp, you can expect to see a reduction in inflammation and itching. The tea may also help to promote healthy hair growth and reduce the appearance of flaky skin. It's also possible to see an improvement in the overall health and appearance of your hair, with a reduction in breakage and split ends.

What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have itchy scalp?

If you have an itchy scalp, there are some herbal decoctions you should avoid using.

Tea tree oil, derived from the leaves of the 'Melaleuca alternifolia' tree's close relative, 'Melaleuca cajuputi', is not recommended as it can cause skin irritation, including itching and redness, especially if you have sensitive skin. Ginkgo biloba can be too drying for the scalp, making itchiness worse. It's also known to cause allergic reactions in some people, leading to more itching and discomfort. Turmeric, made from 'Curcuma longa', contains a chemical called curcumin, which can be too harsh for sensitive scalps.

It may cause irritation and make itchiness worse. Thyme, made from 'Thymus serpyllum', is another herb to avoid as it can be too drying and irritating for the scalp, exacerbating itchiness. Sage, made from 'Salvia officinalis', can also be too drying and irritating for sensitive scalps, making itchiness worse. Ginseng, made from 'Panax ginseng', is not recommended for itchy scalps as it can cause allergic reactions and skin irritation in some people.

These herbs can cause or worsen itchiness on the scalp, so it's best to avoid using them in herbal decoctions.

7 Herbal Essential Oils For Itchy Scalp

Kids can get itchy scalp for many reasons, like dandruff, eczema, or even just because their skin is sensitive.

It can be really annoying and uncomfortable for them, and it can even make them feel self-conscious about their appearance. Some kids might scratch their scalp so much that it gets sore or even bleeds, which can be painful and even lead to infections. This can affect their daily life, making it hard for them to focus in school, play with friends, or even just relax at home. Itchy scalp can also make kids feel anxious or stressed, and it can even lead to sleep problems if they're scratching all night.

Some kids might even start to feel like they're all alone with their problem, like no one understands what they're going through. But there is some good news - there are natural remedies that can help kids with itchy scalp. Herbal essential oils are one of them. These oils are made from plants and are gentle on kids' skin. They can help calm the itch and reduce inflammation, making it easier for kids to feel comfortable in their own skin.

They're also easy to use - just add a few drops to a carrier oil, like coconut or olive oil, and gently massage it into the scalp.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds like cineole and terpinen-4-ol that have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

This helps to soothe and calm itchy scalps by reducing the growth of fungi and bacteria that can cause irritation. These constituents also help to reduce redness and swelling, making it easier to manage itchy scalp conditions. To use Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils for itchy scalp, mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil. Apply the mixture to your scalp and massage it in.

Leave it on for at least 30 minutes before shampooing as usual. You can also add a few drops to your shampoo or conditioner for ongoing relief. When you use Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils, you may notice a reduction in itchiness and irritation within a few days. Your scalp may feel cooler and less inflamed, making it more comfortable to touch. You may also see improvements in the appearance of your scalp, with reduced redness and less flaking.

If you continue to use the essential oil, you may find that your itchy scalp condition becomes less frequent and manageable.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like eucalyptol, limonene, and alpha-pinene, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

These properties help reduce redness and swelling associated with itchy scalp conditions, such as dandruff and eczema. The antifungal properties of eucalyptol also inhibit the growth of yeast-like fungus Malassezia, which can contribute to itchy scalp. To use eucalyptus globulus essential oils for itchy scalp, mix a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.

Gently massage the mixture onto your scalp, focusing on the itchy areas. Leave it on for 30 minutes to an hour before shampooing as usual. Applying eucalyptus globulus essential oils to your itchy scalp may lead to a decrease in redness and inflammation. You may also notice a reduction in flaking and itching, and an improvement in scalp health.

However, it's essential to note that if you have a severe itchy scalp condition, you should consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains compounds like camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate, which are responsible for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to soothe and calm an itchy scalp by reducing redness and inflammation. The camphor in Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils also has a natural analgesic effect, which can help to numb the pain and discomfort associated with an itchy scalp. If you're experiencing an itchy scalp, you can try adding a few drops of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils to your shampoo or conditioner.

You can also mix a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and massage it into your scalp. Leave it on for 30 minutes to an hour before washing your hair as usual. When you use Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils for an itchy scalp, you can expect to see a reduction in redness and inflammation. Your scalp should start to feel cooler and more comfortable.

In some cases, you may notice a decrease in the severity of your itchiness, and your scalp may start to feel more soothed and calm.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains anti-inflammatory properties, including compounds like aloin and aloe-emodin, which help soothe and calm an itchy scalp.

These properties also have antifungal and antibacterial qualities, reducing the presence of irritants and infections that can cause itchiness. The essential oils' moisturizing properties, particularly from the constituent aloe-vera, help to hydrate and nourish the scalp, reducing dryness and flakiness that can lead to itchiness.

To use Aloe barbadensis essential oils for an itchy scalp, mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and gently massage it into the scalp. Leave it on for 30 minutes to an hour before shampooing as usual. After taking action, you can expect the itchiness to subside, and the scalp to feel cooler and more comfortable.

The inflammation and redness may also start to decrease, and the overall appearance of the scalp may improve.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains properties and constituents that help soothe and calm an itchy scalp.

The main culprits behind itchiness are inflammation and irritation, and Lavandula angustifolia is rich in compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which have potent anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. These properties help reduce inflammation, calm the skin, and prevent bacterial growth that can cause itchiness. If you take the right action and use Lavandula angustifolia essential oils correctly, you can expect a reduction in inflammation, itchiness, and irritation, and a noticeable improvement in scalp health. 6. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains a powerful blend of compounds that can help soothe an itchy scalp.

The main active constituents, limonene and beta-pinene, have anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties that can reduce irritation and inflammation in the scalp. These properties can also help to calm an overactive immune system, which can cause itching and flaking. The essential oil's antiseptic properties can also help to combat fungal and bacterial infections that can cause scalp conditions such as dandruff and eczema. If you're experiencing an itchy scalp, try adding a few drops of Cymbopogon citratus essential oil to your shampoo or applying it directly to the affected area after shampooing.

Mix it with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil to dilute the potency and prevent skin irritation. Massage the oil into your scalp gently, focusing on the itchy areas. Leave the oil on for a few minutes before rinsing with warm water. Using Cymbopogon citratus essential oil regularly can lead to a reduction in itching and flaking, as well as a decrease in inflammation and redness. You may also notice an improvement in the overall health of your scalp, with a decrease in the occurrence of dandruff and eczema.

However, if your itchy scalp persists or worsens, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

7. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains compounds like thymol, carvacrol, and linalool, which have antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to soothe an itchy scalp by reducing the growth of fungi and yeast that can cause irritation. Thymol, in particular, has been shown to be effective in treating scalp conditions like dandruff and seborrheic dermatitis. You can also add a few drops to your shampoo or conditioner for an added benefit.

When you start using Thymus vulgaris essential oils, you may notice a reduction in itching and inflammation within a few days to a week. Your scalp may feel cooler and more comfortable, and you may see a decrease in flaking and redness. What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for itchy scalp?

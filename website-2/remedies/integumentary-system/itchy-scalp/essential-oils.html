7 Essential oils to cure itchy scalp in children

Kids can get itchy scalp for many reasons, like dandruff, eczema, or even just because their skin is sensitive.

It can be really annoying and uncomfortable for them, and it can even make them feel self-conscious about their appearance. Some kids might scratch their scalp so much that it gets sore or even bleeds, which can be painful and even lead to infections. This can affect their daily life, making it hard for them to focus in school, play with friends, or even just relax at home. Itchy scalp can also make kids feel anxious or stressed, and it can even lead to sleep problems if they're scratching all night.

Some kids might even start to feel like they're all alone with their problem, like no one understands what they're going through. But there is some good news - there are natural remedies that can help kids with itchy scalp. Herbal essential oils are one of them. These oils are made from plants and are gentle on kids' skin. They can help calm the itch and reduce inflammation, making it easier for kids to feel comfortable in their own skin.

They're also easy to use - just add a few drops to a carrier oil, like coconut or olive oil, and gently massage it into the scalp.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils, also known as tea tree oil, can be a valuable remedy for an itchy scalp.

This is due to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antifungal properties. The main constituents, such as terpinen-4-ol and cineole, have been shown to combat fungal infections like ringworm and seborrheic dermatitis, common causes of an itchy scalp. Additionally, these compounds help reduce inflammation and soothe irritation.

To use Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils for an itchy scalp, try adding a few drops to your shampoo or conditioner, or mix it with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it directly to the affected area. Be sure to dilute the essential oil properly, as it can be quite potent. When you start using Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils, you may notice a reduction in itchiness and inflammation within a few days. The fungal infections that were causing the problem may begin to clear up, and your scalp may start to feel cooler and more comfortable.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for further guidance.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils, also known as rosemary oil, may help with an itchy scalp due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The main constituents of rosemary oil, including camphor, bornyl acetate, and beta-pinene, work together to reduce inflammation and fight off fungal and bacterial infections that can cause itchiness. When applied to the scalp, rosemary oil can help soothe and calm the skin, reducing redness and irritation.

To try rosemary oil for an itchy scalp, mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and massage it into your scalp. You can also add a few drops to your shampoo or conditioner. Be sure to do a patch test first to ensure you don't have any sensitivity to the oil.

When used consistently, rosemary oil may help to reduce the frequency and severity of itchy scalp episodes.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils have been found to be beneficial in soothing an itchy scalp.

The oil contains properties such as antifungal, antibacterial, and anti-inflammatory compounds, including linalool and linalyl acetate, which help reduce irritation and inflammation. These constituents also help to calm the scalp and reduce the growth of fungi and bacteria that can cause itchiness. To use Lavandula angustifolia essential oils for an itchy scalp, mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and gently massage it into your scalp.

Leave the mixture on for about 30 minutes to an hour before washing your hair as usual. You can also add a few drops to your shampoo for an extra soothing effect. When you start using Lavandula angustifolia essential oils, you may notice a reduction in itchiness and irritation within a few days.

Your scalp may feel calmer and more comfortable, and you may be able to reduce the frequency of scratching.

4. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils are derived from the leaves of the Eucalyptus tree and are known for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The key constituents of this oil include eucalyptol, which is responsible for its decongestant and analgesic properties. When applied to an itchy scalp, eucalyptus globulus essential oils help to reduce inflammation, ease itching, and promote a healthy scalp environment. To use eucalyptus globulus essential oils for an itchy scalp, mix a few drops with a carrier oil such as coconut or olive oil and gently massage it into your scalp.

You can also add it to your shampoo or conditioner for a more intense treatment. When you apply eucalyptus globulus essential oils to your itchy scalp, you can expect a reduction in inflammation and itching, as well as an improvement in scalp health. The antiseptic properties of the oil may also help to prevent the growth of fungi and bacteria that can contribute to scalp irritation.

Additionally, the oil's decongestant properties can help to clear up any blockages in the scalp, leading to a more balanced and healthy scalp environment.

5. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils are known for their soothing properties, making them an effective remedy for an itchy scalp.

The oil contains properties like aloe-emodin, aloin, and vitamins A, C, and E, which help to calm irritation and reduce inflammation. The aloe-emodin and aloin in Aloe barbadensis essential oils have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, which can help to fight off scalp infections and soothe itchiness. To use Aloe barbadensis essential oils for an itchy scalp, start by mixing a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.

Gently massage the mixture into your scalp, focusing on the itchy areas. You can also add a few drops of the oil to your shampoo or conditioner for an added dose of soothing properties. By using Aloe barbadensis essential oils for your itchy scalp, you can expect to see a reduction in inflammation and itchiness. The oil's antifungal and antibacterial properties can help to clear up scalp infections, while its soothing properties can calm redness and irritation.

Regular use of the oil may also promote healthy hair growth and reduce dandruff.

6. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils, also known as lemongrass oil, is a natural remedy for itchy scalp.

Its antifungal and antibacterial properties help to combat scalp infections and inflammation that can cause itchiness. The oil contains compounds like citral, geraniol, and limonene, which have anti-inflammatory effects and can soothe irritated skin. When applied to the scalp, lemongrass oil can reduce redness and swelling, promoting a healthy scalp environment. To use lemongrass oil for an itchy scalp, dilute a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil, and massage it into your scalp.

Leave it on for at least 30 minutes before washing your hair as usual. Repeat this process a few times a week for best results. You can also add a few drops of lemongrass oil to your shampoo or conditioner for an extra dose of antifungal and antibacterial properties. As the lemongrass oil works its way into your scalp, you can expect to see a reduction in itchiness and inflammation. Your scalp should start to feel cooler and more comfortable, and the redness should begin to fade.

With regular use, you may find that your itchy scalp becomes a thing of the past, and you can enjoy a healthy, happy scalp.

7. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contain compounds like triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids that help soothe and calm an itchy scalp.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which can reduce redness and swelling caused by irritation or infections. The oil's ability to promote tissue repair and regeneration can also help to heal damaged skin, reducing the urge to scratch and further irritate the scalp. To use Calendula officinalis essential oils for an itchy scalp, mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and gently massage it into the affected area.

It's also a good idea to perform a patch test before using the oil to ensure you don't have any sensitivity. After massaging the oil into your scalp, you can leave it on or rinse it out with a gentle shampoo. As the oil works to calm and soothe the scalp, you may notice a decrease in itching and inflammation within a few hours. The oil's antiseptic properties can also help to prevent infections from taking hold, while its anti-inflammatory properties can reduce redness and swelling.

Regular use of Calendula officinalis essential oils may also promote healthy hair growth and improve the overall health of your scalp.

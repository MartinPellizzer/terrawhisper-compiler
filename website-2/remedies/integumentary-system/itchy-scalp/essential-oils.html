7 Herbal Essential Oils For Itchy Scalp

Herbal essential oils can be a game-changer for an itchy scalp.

These natural remedies work by soothing the skin and reducing inflammation, which is often the root cause of itchiness. For example, Melaleuca alternifolia, also known as tea tree oil, has antibacterial properties that can help combat fungal and bacterial infections that can lead to itchiness. Eucalyptus globulus oil, on the other hand, has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce redness and swelling on the scalp. Herbal teas can also help alleviate an itchy scalp. Drinking teas made from certain herbs can help promote a healthy scalp from the inside out.

For instance, Rosmarinus officinalis, or rosemary tea, has antioxidant properties that can help protect the scalp from damage caused by free radicals. This can lead to a healthier scalp that is less prone to itchiness. Additionally, rosemary tea has been known to improve circulation, which can help bring oxygen and nutrients to the scalp, promoting a healthy scalp environment. Using herbal essential oils and drinking herbal teas can bring numerous benefits to your life. For one, it can help you avoid harsh chemical-based treatments that can damage your hair and scalp. It can also help you identify and address underlying issues that may be contributing to your itchy scalp, such as fungal or bacterial infections.

By incorporating herbal essential oils and teas into your routine, you can take a proactive approach to maintaining a healthy scalp and promoting overall well-being.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains active constituents like cineole, terpinen-4-ol, and limonene.

These compounds have antifungal and antibacterial properties that help control scalp infections and inflammation. The antiseptic properties of these constituents help reduce redness and irritation, while also promoting a healthy scalp environment. The oil's anti-inflammatory properties also help soothe and calm the scalp, reducing itchiness and discomfort.

Regular use of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils may help alleviate itchy scalp conditions by addressing the underlying causes of irritation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves, 2 cups of water, and a pot.
  2. Combine the Melaleuca leaves and water in the pot, then bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl.
  5. Use a hydrosol distiller or a still to extract the essential oil from the mixture, then store it in a dark glass bottle.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole and alpha-pinene as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which help to soothe and calm an itchy scalp. Cineole, in particular, has a cooling effect that can help to reduce redness and irritation. Alpha-pinene, on the other hand, has antimicrobial properties that can help to control the growth of yeast and other microorganisms that can contribute to an itchy scalp.

By using eucalyptus globulus essential oils, you can help to create an environment that is less conducive to irritation and inflammation.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a large saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Eucalyptus leaves. Let it simmer for 2 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, pressing on the solids to get all the liquid.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a still or a distillation apparatus and distill to separate the essential oil from the water.
  5. Collect the essential oil in a separate container, it should be a clear, colorless liquid.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains a compound called borneol, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce redness and swelling on the scalp.

This oil also contains camphor, which has a cooling effect that can soothe an itchy scalp. The antioxidant properties of 1,8-cineole, another compound found in this essential oil, help protect the scalp from damage caused by free radicals. Additionally, the antifungal and antibacterial properties of camphor and borneol help control the growth of yeast and bacteria that can contribute to an itchy scalp.

The antiseptic properties of this essential oil also help prevent infections and promote a healthy scalp environment.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Wash the leaves with water, then pat them dry with a paper towel to remove excess moisture.
  3. Combine the leaves and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Let it sit for 2 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 5-7 drops on your itchy scalp as needed.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains active constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-juice.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe and calm an itchy scalp. Aloe-emodin has antifungal properties, which can help control fungal infections that cause scalp irritation. The anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties of aloe barbadensis essential oils work together to reduce redness and itching on the scalp.

The oil's moisturizing properties also help to hydrate the scalp, providing relief from dryness and itchiness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis leaves, a cup of coconut oil, and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Wash the Aloe leaves with water, then dry them with a clean towel.
  3. In a blender, mix the Aloe leaves with 1/2 cup of coconut oil until smooth.
  4. Transfer the mixture to the glass jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a new jar. Your Aloe essential oil is now ready to use.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains a compound called linalool, which is known for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

These properties help to reduce redness and irritation on the scalp, making it a popular remedy for itchy scalps. Linalyl acetate, another key constituent of lavender oil, has a calming effect on the skin and can help to soothe irritation. The antifungal and antibacterial properties of lavender oil also help to control the growth of yeast and bacteria on the scalp, which can contribute to itchiness.

By reducing inflammation, calming the skin, and controlling the growth of microorganisms, lavender oil can help to alleviate itchy scalp issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and a clean glass jar.
  2. Place 1/4 cup of dried flowers in a clean glass bowl and set aside 1/4 cup for later use.
  3. Add 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) to the bowl with the dried flowers.
  4. Steep the mixture for 2 weeks, shaking the bowl daily, then strain the oil using cheesecloth or a coffee filter.
  5. Discard the solids and transfer the Lavandula angustifolia essential oil to a clean glass bottle for use on itchy scalp.

6. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains a high concentration of citral, limonene, and beta-pinene, which are known to have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

These properties help to soothe and calm an itchy scalp, reducing redness and irritation. The antiseptic properties of citral and limonene also help to prevent the growth of fungi and bacteria that can contribute to scalp irritation. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of beta-pinene help to reduce swelling and inflammation in the scalp, providing relief from itchy sensations.

By targeting the underlying causes of an itchy scalp, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can help to promote a healthy scalp and reduce the symptoms of itchiness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Cymbopogon citratus leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it steep for 2 hours. This allows the leaves to release their oils.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the leaves.
  5. Use a hydrosol kit or a still to distill the liquid and separate the essential oil from the water. You will get about 1 tablespoon of essential oil.

7. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which are known for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to soothe and calm an itchy scalp by reducing redness and irritation. Thymol has a decongestant effect, which can help to clear out pores and reduce inflammation on the scalp. Carvacrol has antioxidant properties, which can help to protect the scalp from damage caused by free radicals and promote healthy skin.

By using Thymus vulgaris essential oils, you can help to create an environment that's less conducive to itchy scalp conditions.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add 10 drops of vitamin E oil to the jar and mix well.
  3. Place the jar in a saucepan with 2 cups of water and heat it on low heat for 2 hours.
  4. After 2 hours, turn off the heat and let the mixture cool. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into another clean glass jar.
  5. Store the Thymus vulgaris essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use 5-7 drops on itchy scalp 2-3 times a week.

Can drinking herbal tea prevent itchy scalp from forming?

Drinking tea may help reduce itchiness and promote a healthy scalp.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for itchy scalp every day?

You can try consuming herbal teas for an itchy scalp, but do it in moderation.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for itchy scalp?

Drinking herbal tea for an itchy scalp can be soothing.

What Are The Main Causes Of Dental Plaque?

Dental plaque is a sticky film of bacteria that forms on teeth.

It's caused by several factors. Poor oral hygiene is a main cause. When you don't brush or floss your teeth regularly, bacteria in your mouth multiply and stick to your teeth, creating plaque. Another cause is a diet high in sugar.

When you eat sugary foods and drinks, bacteria in your mouth feed on the sugar and produce acid, which damages your teeth and helps plaque form. The bacteria in your mouth break down the sugar and produce acid, which damages your teeth and helps plaque form. Inadequate brushing is also a cause of dental plaque. If you brush your teeth infrequently or don't brush all surfaces of your teeth, bacteria can multiply and stick to your teeth, creating plaque.

Brushing your teeth at least twice a day with fluoride toothpaste and flossing once a day can help prevent plaque from forming.

Below are the main causes of dental plaque.

  • Poor Oral Hygiene: Poor oral hygiene, which is not brushing and flossing regularly, allows bacteria to build up on teeth, leading to the formation of dental plaque.
  • Diet High In Sugar: A diet high in sugar feeds the bacteria in the mouth, which then produce acids that help create sticky, hard dental plaque on teeth.
  • Inadequate Brushing: Inadequate brushing fails to remove food particles from teeth, allowing plaque to form and multiply as bacteria feed on these particles, leading to its buildup.
  • Inadequate Flossing: Inadequate flossing allows plaque to build up between teeth, where a toothbrush can't reach, leading to a buildup of bacteria and food particles that can cause dental problems.
  • Diet High In Starch: A diet high in starch can cause dental plaque because it is easily converted into sugars by the bacteria in the mouth, leading to acid production and tooth decay.
  • Infrequent Dental Visits: Infrequent dental visits allow dental plaque to build up on teeth over time because the plaque isn't regularly removed by a dentist or hygienist during cleaning sessions.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Dental Plaque?

If you have dental plaque, it's essential to know which herbs to avoid.

Cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum) and cloves (Syzygium aromaticum) can be problematic as they are too spicy and may irritate your gums further. Cloves contain a chemical called eugenol that can cause inflammation and increase the risk of gum disease.

Also, avoid using herbs like wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) and sage (not Salvia officinalis) because they have compounds that can dry out your mouth and make it harder to remove plaque. Additionally, avoid using herbs like thyme (Thymus vulgaris) and oregano (Origanum vulgare) in large amounts, as their oils can be too harsh on your gums. Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) is another herb to be cautious with, as it can cause dry mouth and interact with medications.

Finally, be careful with herbs like ginger (Zingiber officinale) and turmeric (Curcuma longa) as they can cause stomach upset and make it harder to remove plaque.

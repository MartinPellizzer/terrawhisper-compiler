What are the causes of reddened corners in mouth?

Reddened corners in the mouth can be caused by several issues.

Angular Cheilitis, also known as angular stomatitis, is a common cause. It happens when the corners of the mouth get dry and cracked, allowing bacteria and yeast to grow, leading to redness and inflammation. This condition is often linked to vitamin deficiencies, especially B2, B7, and B12, as well as poor oral hygiene, and smoking. Another cause is Cracked Corners, which occurs when the skin around the mouth cracks, allowing saliva and bacteria to enter and cause irritation. This condition can be caused by dry skin, cold weather, or overexposure to the sun.

Herpes, specifically cold sores, can also cause redness in the mouth corners. This viral infection causes painful blisters to form, which can be triggered by stress, fatigue, or certain foods. Canker Sores, also known as aphthous ulcers, are small, painful ulcers that can appear inside the mouth, including in the corners. They can be caused by stress, spicy or acidic foods, or minor mouth injuries. Lastly, a B12 Deficiency can lead to redness and inflammation in the mouth corners.

Vitamin B12 plays a crucial role in the production of red blood cells, and a deficiency can cause a range of symptoms, including mouth sores and inflammation.

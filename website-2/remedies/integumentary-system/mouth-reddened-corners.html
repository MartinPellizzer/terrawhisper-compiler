What Are The Main Causes Of Reddened Corners In Mouth?

Reddened corners in the mouth, also known as angular cheilitis, can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is Angular Cheilitis itself, a condition where the corners of the mouth become inflamed and red. This is often due to a fungal or bacterial infection, which can be triggered by poor oral hygiene, skin conditions, or allergies. Another cause of reddened mouth corners is Vitamin Deficiency, particularly a lack of Vitamin B, which is essential for maintaining healthy skin and mucous membranes. A deficiency in this vitamin can cause inflammation and redness in the mouth corners.

People who are vegetarian or vegan, or those who have a poor diet, may be more likely to experience this deficiency. Dry Lips can also contribute to reddened mouth corners. When the lips are dry and cracked, they can cause irritation and inflammation in the mouth corners. This can be due to a variety of factors, including over-exposure to the sun, wind, or cold weather, or as a side effect of certain medications.

Dry air can also contribute to this condition, making it a common problem during the winter months.

Below are the main causes of reddened corners in mouth.

  • Angular Cheilitis: Angular Cheilitis causes reddened corners in the mouth because of inflammation and irritation in the corners of the mouth, usually due to dry skin, fungal or bacterial infections, or vitamin deficiencies, which leads to redness, swelling, and sometimes painful cracks in the skin.
  • Vitamin Deficiency: A Vitamin B deficiency, particularly B12, can cause inflammation and redness in the corners of the mouth due to the inability of the body to properly process nutrients, leading to tissue damage and irritation.
  • Dry Lips: Dry Lips can cause reddened corners in the mouth because the dry skin on the lips can crack and spread to the corners of the mouth, causing redness and irritation due to the moisture loss and irritation from saliva and food particles.
  • Cold Sores: Cold Sores is a cause of reddened corners in the mouth because the herpes simplex virus that causes Cold Sores can lead to inflammation and painful blisters that often form on the corners of the mouth, causing redness and swelling.
  • Lip Biting: Lip biting causes reddened corners in the mouth because the constant rubbing and biting motion can cause irritation, leading to inflammation and increased blood flow to the affected area, making it appear red and sore.
  • Sjogren's Syndrome: In Sjogren's Syndrome, the immune system mistakenly attacks and damages the glands that produce saliva and tears, leading to dry mouth and lips, which can cause the corners of the mouth to become red and inflamed.
  • Oral Lichen Planus: Oral Lichen Planus is a condition that causes reddened corners in the mouth because the immune system mistakenly attacks the tiny blood vessels in the mouth, leading to inflammation and a bright red color.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Reddened Corners In Mouth?

If you have reddened corners in your mouth, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can make the issue worse.

One such herb is 'Capsicum annuum', also known as cayenne pepper or chili pepper. The capsaicin in this herb can make the burning sensation in your mouth even worse, causing more pain and discomfort.

Another herb to avoid is 'Trifolium pratense', or red clover. This herb can be an allergen, and in some cases, it may cause an allergic reaction, including mouth irritation and redness. 'Ginkgo biloba' is also not recommended, as it may cause mouth sores or ulcers, especially if taken in large doses.

Lastly, 'Vaccinium myrtillus', or bilberry, may cause gastrointestinal issues, including stomach upset, which can lead to mouth redness and discomfort.

What Are The Main Causes Of Eczema?

Atopic Dermatitis is a type of eczema that causes dry, itchy, and inflamed skin.

This condition is often seen in people who have a history of allergies and asthma. Genetics play a significant role in the development of eczema. If one or both parents have eczema, their children are more likely to develop the condition.

This is because the genes that cause eczema can be passed down from parents to their offspring. Allergies are another common cause of eczema. When the body's immune system reacts to a substance like pollen, dust mites, or certain foods, it can cause inflammation and skin irritation.

This can lead to eczema symptoms like redness, itching, and dryness.

Below are the main causes of eczema.

  • Atopic Dermatitis: Atopic dermatitis is a type of eczema because it occurs when the skin's natural barrier is weak and the immune system overreacts to tiny particles in the air, leading to inflammation and itchy skin.
  • Genetics: Genetics is a cause of eczema because a person's genes can make their skin more sensitive and prone to irritation, leading to the development of eczema.
  • Allergies: When a person's body reacts strongly to certain substances like pollen, dust, or certain foods, it can cause a chain reaction that leads to eczema flare-ups.
  • Dust Mites: Dust mites cause eczema because their waste and dead bodies release tiny particles that can trigger allergies and make skin become inflamed and itchy, leading to eczema symptoms.
  • Soy: Soy contains a compound called phytoestrogen, which can cause the body's immune system to overreact and trigger eczema symptoms in some people.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Eczema?

If you have eczema, some herbs can irritate your skin, making symptoms worse.

Herbs like St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) and Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) contain compounds that can dry out your skin and make it more sensitive. Turmeric (Curcuma longa) contains a strong anti-inflammatory called curcumin, which can be too harsh for people with eczema.

Also, avoid using herbs like Sassafras (Sassafras albidum) and Birch bark (Betula species), as they can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals. Other herbs like Tea tree oil (Melaleuca alternifolia) and Eucalyptus (Eucalyptus globulus) can be too astringent and drying for people with eczema, making their skin feel tight and itchy.

Finally, herbs like Comfrey (Symphytum officinale) and Meadowsweet (Filipendula ulmaria) contain compounds that can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in people with eczema.

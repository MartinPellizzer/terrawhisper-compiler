Fever Blister on Lip in Nursing Mothers: 11 Herbal Creams for Relief

As a nursing mother, it's common to experience fever blisters on the lip due to hormonal changes, stress, and weakened immune system.

These blisters can be painful, unsightly, and even contagious. They can also make breastfeeding and caring for a baby challenging. The constant discomfort, anxiety about passing the infection to the baby, and difficulty in taking care of a new-born can take a toll on a new mother's mental and physical health. Additionally, the limitations on what foods and drinks can be consumed to alleviate the symptoms can be frustrating. Furthermore, the pressure to keep the baby safe from infection can lead to feelings of guilt and anxiety. Nursing mothers with fever blisters on the lip often face challenges in their daily lives, including pain while eating, speaking, and even breastfeeding.

They might have to stop feeding the baby on the affected side, which can be distressing for both the mother and the baby. The constant discomfort and anxiety can also affect their relationships with their partner, family, and friends. Herbal creams can be a helpful remedy for nursing mothers suffering from fever blisters on the lip. These creams are often made from natural ingredients that have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. They can help reduce the pain, swelling, and redness associated with fever blisters. They can also promote healing and prevent the spread of the infection.

These creams are a safer alternative to traditional medications, which can have side effects that may harm the baby.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams, also known as tea tree oil creams, can be very helpful in treating fever blisters on the lip.

This is because they contain properties like antiviral and antibacterial agents, which can help combat the herpes simplex virus that causes fever blisters. The main constituent, melaleuca alternifolia oil, has been shown to have antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce the swelling and ease the pain associated with fever blisters. When applying Melaleuca alternifolia creams to a fever blister on the lip, it's best to apply a thin layer to the affected area, 2-3 times a day. You can also use a cold compress or an ice pack to help reduce the swelling.

Avoid touching the blister to prevent further infection. Also, it's essential to keep the area clean and avoid sharing personal items like lip balm, utensils, or towels. If you use Melaleuca alternifolia creams correctly, you can expect the blister to start healing sooner. The swelling should reduce, and the pain will ease. In some cases, the blister may crust over and eventually fall off.

However, it's essential to note that fever blisters can be contagious, so it's crucial to take precautions to prevent the spread of the virus.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams have been used for centuries to help soothe and heal fever blisters on the lip.

The gel extracted from the aloe vera plant contains several properties and constituents that make it effective in treating lip blisters. It has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, thanks to the presence of aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A, C, and E. These properties help reduce swelling, ease pain, and prevent infection. To use aloe barbadensis cream on a fever blister on the lip, apply a thin layer of the cream directly to the affected area multiple times a day. Gently massage the cream into the skin to help it absorb quickly.

You can also apply a cold compress or an ice pack to the area for 10-15 minutes, 2-3 times a day, to help reduce swelling and ease pain. When using aloe barbadensis cream on a fever blister, you can expect to see a reduction in swelling and redness within a few hours. The pain and discomfort may also subside within a day or two. The cream may also help speed up the healing process by promoting the growth of new skin cells.

However, it's essential to note that fever blisters can be triggered by stress, hormonal changes, or certain foods, so it's crucial to identify and manage these triggers to prevent future outbreaks.

Calendula officinalis creams are a popular remedy for fever blisters on the lip, and for good reason.

The cream contains anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, thanks to its high concentration of triterpenoids and flavonoids. When you apply Calendula officinalis cream to the affected area, it forms a protective barrier that soothes and calms the skin, promoting healing and reducing the risk of scarring. To get the most out of Calendula officinalis cream for fever blisters on the lip, apply a thin layer to the affected area several times a day.

You can also use a lip balm or ointment that contains Calendula officinalis extract to keep the skin moisturized and protected. Avoid touching or picking at the fever blister, as this can lead to further irritation and prolong the healing process. By keeping the area clean and applying the cream regularly, you can speed up the healing process and reduce the risk of recurrent outbreaks. As the fever blister heals, you can expect to see a reduction in swelling and redness, followed by a scab that eventually falls off, revealing healthy new skin underneath. With regular use of Calendula officinalis cream, you may find that the frequency and severity of fever blisters on your lip decrease over time.

However, it's essential to note that Calendula officinalis cream may not completely eliminate fever blisters, but it can help to manage and prevent them from becoming a persistent problem.

The blister may start to dry out and heal faster, and you may experience a decrease in pain and discomfort. However, it's essential to be patient, as the healing process can take time.

5. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale creams have been used for centuries to treat fever blisters on the lip.

The cream's main active ingredient, allantoin, is a natural compound that helps to soothe and heal the skin. Allantoin works by drawing moisture to the skin and promoting the growth of new tissue, which helps to speed up the healing process of the fever blister. Symphytum officinale creams also contain other beneficial compounds like mucilage, which acts as a protective barrier on the skin's surface, and vitamin E, which has antioxidant properties that help to reduce inflammation and promote collagen production.

When using Symphytum officinale cream for a fever blister on the lip, apply a thin layer to the affected area 2-3 times a day. You can also apply ice to the area to help reduce swelling and numb the pain. As the cream works to heal the skin, you can expect to see a reduction in the size and redness of the fever blister within a few days. The blister should start to crust over and eventually heal completely within 1-2 weeks.

However, if the fever blister is severe or persists for an extended period, you should consult a doctor for further treatment.

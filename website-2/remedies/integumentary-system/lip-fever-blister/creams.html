1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains several bioactive constituents, including aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A, C, and E. These compounds have anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and soothing properties that help to reduce redness and swelling associated with fever blisters on the lip.

The aloe-emodin in Aloe barbadensis creams has been shown to inhibit the herpes simplex virus, the primary cause of fever blisters. Additionally, the vitamins in Aloe barbadensis creams, particularly vitamin A, help to promote healing and reduce the risk of scarring.

By applying Aloe barbadensis creams to the affected area, you can take advantage of its natural properties to help alleviate the symptoms of fever blisters on the lip.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup Aloe barbadensis gel, 1/4 cup coconut oil, 2 tablespoons beeswax, 2 tablespoons vitamin E oil, and 10 drops of tea tree oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Once melted, remove from heat and stir in Aloe barbadensis gel, vitamin E oil, and tea tree oil until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into a small container or tin and let it cool and solidify at room temperature.
  5. Once solid, store the Aloe barbadensis cream in the fridge and apply a small amount to the affected lip area as needed to soothe fever blisters.

  5. Remove the double boiler from heat and let the mixture cool and thicken. Pour the mixture into a jar and let it cool completely before use.

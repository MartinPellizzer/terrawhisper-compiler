11 Herbal Creams For Periodontal Disease

Herbal creams can be a helpful addition to your oral health routine, especially when it comes to managing periodontal disease.

Periodontal disease is an infection of the gums that can cause them to become inflamed and even lead to tooth loss. Herbal teas have been shown to help relieve periodontal disease by reducing inflammation and killing bacteria in the mouth. For example, Curcuma longa, or turmeric, contains a powerful compound called curcumin, which has potent anti-inflammatory properties.

When applied topically as a cream, turmeric can help reduce swelling and ease pain in the gums. Another example is Salvia officinalis, or sage, which has been shown to have antibacterial properties that can help kill bacteria in the mouth and prevent the spread of periodontal disease. Eucalyptus globulus, or eucalyptus, has decongestant properties that can help reduce inflammation and ease pain in the gums. When used in a cream, these herbs can be absorbed into the skin and work directly on the infected area to provide relief.

Using herbal creams can be a natural and non-invasive way to manage periodontal disease, and can be especially helpful for people who are sensitive to medications or prefer a more holistic approach to their oral health.

1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains a compound called curcumin, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Curcumin has potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which make it effective in reducing inflammation and killing bacteria that cause periodontal disease. The curcumin in Curcuma longa creams also has antioxidant properties, which help protect the gums and teeth from damage caused by free radicals. Another compound found in Curcuma longa creams is demethoxycurcumin (DMC), which has been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria that contribute to periodontal disease.

By targeting the underlying causes of periodontal disease, Curcuma longa creams can help prevent further damage and promote healthy gums and teeth.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder, and 2 tablespoons of essential oil in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in the double boiler over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the turmeric powder and stir until it's fully incorporated into the melted mixture.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool for 10-15 minutes, or until it reaches a consistency similar to thin honey.
  5. Pour the mixture into a small container and add the essential oil. Mix well and let the Curcuma longa cream cool and solidify before use.

2. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, apigenin, and luteolin as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce swelling and prevent further damage to the gums. Rosmarinic acid, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria that contribute to periodontal disease, such as Porphyromonas gingivalis. Luteolin also has antimicrobial properties, which help combat the infection and promote healing of the gums.

By reducing inflammation and preventing bacterial growth, Salvia officinalis creams can help manage periodontal disease and promote a healthier oral environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Salvia officinalis leaves, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and a double boiler.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in the double boiler until it reaches 180°F (82°C).
  3. Add the dried Salvia officinalis leaves to the coconut oil and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean container. Discard the solids. Add the beeswax and vitamin E oil, and stir until the beeswax is melted.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify. Store the creams in a cool, dry place.

4. Melaleuca alternifolia

  3. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in the double boiler or heat-proof bowl. Stir until the beeswax is fully dissolved.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and stir in the Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil and vitamin E oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a heat-proof container and let it cool. Once cool, whip the mixture with a hand mixer or electric mixer to create a smooth, creamy texture.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent periodontal disease from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent periodontal disease.

Some teas, like peppermint and sage, contain compounds that reduce bacteria in the mouth. This can lower the risk of infection and inflammation that lead to gum disease.

Regular tea consumption might also improve gum health by reducing plaque and promoting a balanced oral environment.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for periodontal disease every day?

While herbal teas may help with periodontal disease, consuming them daily isn't risk-free.

Some teas, like sage and chamomile, can thin the blood and affect medications. Also, strong brews can irritate gums.

Moderate use is best, but be cautious and listen to your body.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in periodontal disease?

Herbal teas can help with periodontal disease, but it's hard to predict exactly when you'll see results.

Some people notice a difference in a few weeks, while others may not see improvements until after a few months.

It really depends on the individual and the severity of the disease.

7 Herbal Tinctures For Periodontal Disease

People with diabetes are more likely to get periodontal disease, which is an infection of the gums and bones that support the teeth.

This is because diabetes can affect the body's ability to fight off germs and heal itself. When left untreated, periodontal disease can lead to painful gums, loose teeth, and even tooth loss. This can make it harder for diabetics to eat and speak, and can also affect their self-esteem. To make matters worse, periodontal disease can also increase blood sugar levels, creating a vicious cycle. This can be especially challenging for diabetics who already struggle to manage their condition. Diabetics with periodontal disease may also experience other complications, such as bad breath, sensitive teeth, and swelling of the face and gums.

In severe cases, the infection can spread to other parts of the body, such as the heart and kidneys. This can lead to serious health problems, including heart attacks, strokes, and kidney failure. Herbal tinctures have been traditionally used to help manage periodontal disease in diabetics. These tinctures are made from the extracts of certain plants and are taken orally. They work by reducing inflammation and killing bacteria in the mouth, which helps to prevent the progression of periodontal disease. They may also help to reduce blood sugar levels and improve the body's ability to fight off germs.

By using herbal tinctures, diabetics may be able to manage their periodontal disease more effectively and reduce their risk of complications.

1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa tinctures contains a concentrated form of curcumin, a bioactive compound with potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Curcumin in Curcuma longa tinctures helps with periodontal disease by reducing inflammation in the gums and preventing the progression of bacterial infections. This is due to its ability to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and free radicals, which contribute to gum damage and bone loss. Specifically, curcumin's antioxidant properties help to neutralize the effects of advanced glycation end-products (AGEs) that accumulate in the gums, leading to tissue damage and inflammation. To use Curcuma longa tinctures for periodontal disease, start by consulting with a healthcare professional or dentist to determine the best dosage and duration of treatment. Typically, a 1:2 or 1:5 extract ratio is recommended, meaning one part of curcumin is mixed with two or five parts of a solvent such as water or ethanol.

Take 20-30 drops of the tincture, 2-3 times a day, preferably after meals. It's essential to dilute the tincture in water or another liquid before consumption, as it can be quite potent. Taking Curcuma longa tinctures regularly may lead to several outcomes. First, you may notice a reduction in gum inflammation and bleeding, as well as a decrease in plaque and tartar buildup. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of curcumin may help to protect the gums and surrounding tissues from further damage, potentially slowing down the progression of periodontal disease. Some users may also experience an improvement in overall oral health, including fresher breath and a reduction in bad taste.

However, it's essential to note that Curcuma longa tinctures are not a replacement for conventional dental treatment, but rather a complementary therapy to be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

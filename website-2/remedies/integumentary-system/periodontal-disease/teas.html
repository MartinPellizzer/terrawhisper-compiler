8 Herbal Teas For Periodontal Disease

People with poor oral hygiene are more likely to suffer from periodontal disease, which is an infection of the gums and bone that support the teeth.

If left untreated, it can lead to tooth loss, pain, and difficulty eating, drinking, and even speaking. This can affect their daily lives, making simple tasks a struggle. They might feel self-conscious about their smile, have trouble enjoying their favorite foods, and experience pain while eating or talking.

In severe cases, it can even lead to systemic infections, affecting other parts of the body. The good news is that there are some natural remedies that can help. Herbal teas, in particular, have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that can help combat periodontal disease. These teas work by reducing inflammation and killing the bacteria that cause the infection, allowing the gums and bone to heal.

By drinking these teas regularly, people with poor oral hygiene can help prevent or even reverse the effects of periodontal disease and improve their overall oral health.

1. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis teas contains compounds like carnosic acid, rosmarinic acid, and salvianolic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help reduce inflammation and prevent damage to the gums and teeth, making Salvia officinalis teas effective in treating periodontal disease. The tea's antibacterial properties also help combat the growth of bacteria that cause periodontal disease, such as Porphyromonas gingivalis and Aggregatibacter actinomycetemcomitans. To use Salvia officinalis teas to help with periodontal disease, start by drinking 1-2 cups a day. You can steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried Salvia officinalis leaves in boiling water for 5-10 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it warm or at room temperature.

You can also add honey or lemon to taste. To enhance the effectiveness, consider combining Salvia officinalis teas with other treatments, such as brushing and flossing regularly, and visiting your dentist for regular check-ups. If you take action and drink Salvia officinalis teas regularly, you may notice a reduction in gum inflammation and bleeding, and an improvement in the overall health of your gums and teeth. Your breath may also become fresher, and you may experience less pain when eating or drinking. However, it's essential to note that Salvia officinalis teas are not a replacement for professional dental care.

Consult your dentist before using Salvia officinalis teas to treat periodontal disease, especially if you have severe symptoms or other underlying health conditions.

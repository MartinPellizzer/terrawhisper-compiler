What Are The Main Causes Of Chapped Lips?

Chapped lips are a common problem that can be caused by several factors.

One main cause is cold weather. When it's cold outside, the air has low moisture levels, which can dry out the skin on your lips. This dryness can lead to cracking and flaking, causing discomfort and pain. Another cause of chapped lips is dry air.

Dry air, whether indoors or outdoors, can strip the skin of its natural moisture, leading to dryness and chapping. This can be especially problematic in air-conditioned spaces or during the winter months when the air is already dry. Licking your lips can also cause chapping. When you lick your lips, you might think you're helping to moisturize them, but actually, saliva can dry out the skin even further.

This is because saliva has a low pH level, which can disrupt the skin's natural balance and lead to dryness and irritation.

Below are the main causes of chapped lips.

  • Cold weather: Cold weather causes chapped lips because the dry, cold air makes the skin on your lips lose moisture and become dry and cracked.
  • Dry air: Dry air takes the moisture away from our skin, including our lips, causing them to become chapped and irritated.
  • Licking lips: Licking your lips can cause chapped lips because the saliva from your mouth evaporates quickly, leaving your lips dry and exposed to the air, which can strip them of their natural moisture and cause cracking.
  • Suns exposure: Suns exposure causes chapped lips because the UV rays in the sun dry out the skin on your lips, making them crack and become rough.
  • Dehydration: Dehydration occurs when our body loses more water than it takes in, causing the skin on our lips to dry out and become chapped because it lacks the moisture it needs to stay healthy.
  • Nutritional deficiency: Nutritional deficiency can cause chapped lips because the body needs certain vitamins and minerals, like vitamin B and iron, to keep skin healthy and moist, and a lack of these nutrients can lead to dry, cracked lips.
  • Diet low in omega 3: A diet low in omega-3 fatty acids can cause chapped lips because it leads to dry skin and inflammation, making the skin on the lips more prone to cracking and drying out.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Chapped Lips?

If you have chapped lips, there are some herbs you should avoid using.

Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) is one of them, as it can cause allergic reactions and irritate the skin further. Echinacea (Echinacea spp.).

is another herb to steer clear of, as it can be too drying for the skin and make chapped lips worse. Sage (Salvia officinalis) and Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis) are also best avoided, as they contain compounds that can dry out the skin and make it more prone to cracking. Dried and powdered herbs like Turmeric (Curcuma longa) and Ginger (Zingiber officinale) can also be too harsh for chapped lips, as they can cause further irritation and dryness.

These herbs can be soothing for some issues, but they are not suitable for chapped lips.

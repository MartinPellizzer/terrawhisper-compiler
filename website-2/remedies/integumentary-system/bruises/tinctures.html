1. Arnica montana

Arnica montana tinctures contains bioactive constituents like sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce swelling and promote healing in bruises. The sesquiterpene lactones, particularly helenalin and iso-helenalin, inhibit the production of inflammatory chemicals, which contributes to the reduction of swelling and pain. The flavonoids and phenolic acids in Arnica montana tinctures have antioxidant properties that help protect the skin from further damage and promote the removal of damaged cells.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Arnica montana tinctures can help bruises heal faster and with less scarring.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Arnica montana flowers and leaves. Make sure they are organic and free of contaminants.
  2. Combine the dried Arnica montana with 2 cups of 80% ethanol (vodka or brandy work too) in a clean glass jar. Stir well to coat all plant material.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar daily to help the tincture extract.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the tincture to dark glass dropper bottles and store them in a cool, dark place. Use 10-20 drops of the tincture 2-3 times a day to help with bruise healing.

2. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum tinctures contains hypericin and hyperforin, two biologically active constituents that help reduce inflammation and promote healing.

These compounds work together to decrease bleeding and swelling in bruises, making them a popular remedy for minor injuries. Hypericin has antioxidant properties that protect damaged tissues from further harm, while hyperforin stimulates the production of new tissue, which aids in the recovery process. The anti-inflammatory effects of hypericum perforatum tinctures also help alleviate pain and discomfort associated with bruises.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, hypericum perforatum tinctures can help bruises recover faster and with less scarring.

  1. Gather 250ml of 80% vodka and 250g of dried Hypericum perforatum leaves.
  2. Combine the vodka and dried leaves in a clean glass jar.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 20-30 drops, 3-4 times a day, to treat bruises.

3. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, triterpenoids, and carotenoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce swelling and promote healing in bruises. The flavonoids in Calendula officinalis, such as quercetin and kaempferol, can inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes that contribute to bruise formation. The triterpenoids, including calendulol and isoalantolactone, have been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help reduce redness and swelling associated with bruises.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Calendula officinalis tinctures can help bruises to heal faster and more effectively.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Calendula officinalis flowers and 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Calendula flowers and vodka in the jar. Make sure the flowers are completely covered by the vodka.
  3. Cover the jar with a lid and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the Calendula flowers.
  5. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid and use 20-30 drops, 3 times a day, to help heal bruises.

4. Hamamelis virginiana

Hamamelis virginiana tinctures contains bioactive constituents like tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have astringent, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The astringent properties help constrict blood vessels and reduce bleeding, which is beneficial for bruises. The anti-inflammatory properties reduce swelling and pain, while the antioxidant properties protect cells from damage.

The combination of these properties makes Hamamelis virginiana tinctures effective in reducing the appearance and discomfort of bruises.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hamamelis virginiana leaves, 2 cups of 80-proof vodka, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Chop the Hamamelis virginiana leaves and pack them into the glass jar, leaving 1 inch of space at the top.
  3. Pour the vodka over the leaves, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle, discarding the solids.

5. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis tinctures contains aloin, aloe-emodin, and anthraquinones, which are biologically active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, reducing swelling and redness in bruises. The tannins and flavonoids present in aloe barbadensis tinctures also help to promote collagen synthesis, which aids in the healing process of damaged skin. The antioxidant properties of aloe barbadensis tinctures help to reduce oxidative stress and prevent further tissue damage, allowing bruises to heal faster.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, aloe barbadensis tinctures can help to minimize the appearance and discomfort of bruises.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis leaves, 2 cups of vodka, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Chop the Aloe leaves into small pieces and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour 2 cups of vodka over the Aloe pieces, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.

6. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale tinctures contains allantoin, aloe-emodin, and rosmarinic acid, among other bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and pain in bruises. The allantoin in Symphytum officinale tinctures promotes tissue repair and regeneration, accelerating the healing process. The aloe-emodin and rosmarinic acid also have antioxidant properties, which protect the affected area from further damage.

By reducing inflammation and promoting tissue repair, Symphytum officinale tinctures can help bruises heal faster and more efficiently.

  3. Close the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.

  5. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Take 20-30 drops, 3 times a day, as needed for bruise relief.

7. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce swelling and repair damaged tissue. The alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals, which can contribute to bruising. 8. Vaccinium macrocarpon

Vaccinium macrocarpon tinctures contains anthocyanins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are powerful antioxidants.

These compounds help reduce inflammation and prevent further damage to bruised tissue. The anthocyanins, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the activity of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which can exacerbate bruising. The tannins present in Vaccinium macrocarpon also contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties, helping to reduce swelling and promote healing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Vaccinium macrocarpon tinctures can help alleviate the symptoms of bruises and support the body's natural recovery process.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent bruises from forming?

Drinking herbal tea, particularly those high in flavonoids like chamomile or peppermint, may help prevent bruises from forming.

This is because flavonoids have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce blood vessel permeability and improve blood flow.

As a result, herbal tea may help minimize bruising and promote faster healing.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for bruises every day?

While herbal teas for bruises can be soothing, consuming them daily may not be safe for everyone.

Some herbs can interact with medications or worsen conditions like bleeding disorders. It's best to use them in moderation and for short periods to see if they help.

If symptoms persist, you may need to try other remedies.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in bruises?

Herbal teas like arnica and calendula can help with bruises.

It might take a few days for them to show results. The herbs help reduce swelling and promote healing.

Some people see improvement within 2-3 days, while others may take a little longer, up to 5-7 days, for the bruising to fade.

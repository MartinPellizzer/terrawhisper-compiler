8 Herbal Tinctures for Runners to Reduce Bruise Recovery Time

As runners, we're no strangers to bumps and bruises.

Whether it's a stumble during a long run or a collision with another runner, the impact can be painful and frustrating. Bruises can make everyday activities, like walking or even getting dressed, a challenge. They can also cause swelling, which can lead to discomfort and stiffness in the affected area. This can lead to a decrease in running performance and even force runners to take a break from their favorite activity. For runners who are training for a big event or trying to reach a new personal best, a bruise can be a major setback. But what causes bruises in the first place?.

It's usually due to blood vessels breaking under the skin, which leads to the characteristic discoloration and swelling. When this happens, the body's natural response is to try and repair the damaged tissue, which can be a slow and painful process. Herbal tinctures have been used for centuries to help alleviate the symptoms of bruises. They work by promoting healing, reducing inflammation, and relieving pain. These tinctures are often made from the extract of plants that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. They can be applied topically, either directly to the affected area or mixed with a carrier oil for a massage. This can help to increase blood flow, which promotes healing and reduces swelling.

By using herbal tinctures, runners can help to reduce the pain and discomfort associated with bruises, allowing them to get back on their feet and back to their regular training schedule.

1. Arnica montana

Arnica montana tinctures are a popular natural remedy for treating bruises.

The reason they're effective is due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help to reduce swelling, pain, and discoloration by blocking the production of inflammatory enzymes and neutralizing free radicals. When used topically or taken internally as a tincture, Arnica montana helps to promote blood flow and reduce fluid buildup in the affected area. If you want to try Arnica montana tincture for a bruise, here are some steps to follow: Apply a few drops of Arnica tincture to the bruised area 2-3 times a day, and gently massage it in. You can also take 5-10 drops of Arnica tincture sublingually (under the tongue) 2-3 times a day to help with internal inflammation and healing.

It's essential to note that Arnica montana should not be applied directly to open wounds or used on sensitive skin. Additionally, pregnant and breastfeeding women should consult with a healthcare professional before using Arnica montana. When using Arnica montana tincture for bruises, you may notice a reduction in pain and swelling within 24-48 hours. The bruise may also appear lighter or fade faster than usual. However, the extent of the bruise's appearance and the rate of healing can vary depending on the individual and the severity of the injury.

Some people may experience minor side effects such as stomach upset or allergic reactions, but these are rare and usually temporary.

8 Herbal Tinctures For Bruises

As runners, we're no strangers to bumps and bruises.

Whether it's a stumble during a long run or a collision with another runner, the impact can be painful and frustrating. Bruises can make everyday activities, like walking or even getting dressed, a challenge. They can also cause swelling, which can lead to discomfort and stiffness in the affected area. This can lead to a decrease in running performance and even force runners to take a break from their favorite activity. For runners who are training for a big event or trying to reach a new personal best, a bruise can be a major setback. But what causes bruises in the first place?.

It's usually due to blood vessels breaking under the skin, which leads to the characteristic discoloration and swelling. When this happens, the body's natural response is to try and repair the damaged tissue, which can be a slow and painful process. Herbal tinctures have been used for centuries to help alleviate the symptoms of bruises. They work by promoting healing, reducing inflammation, and relieving pain. These tinctures are often made from the extract of plants that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. They can be applied topically, either directly to the affected area or mixed with a carrier oil for a massage. This can help to increase blood flow, which promotes healing and reduces swelling.

By using herbal tinctures, runners can help to reduce the pain and discomfort associated with bruises, allowing them to get back on their feet and back to their regular training schedule.

1. Arnica montana

Arnica montana tinctures contain compounds like flavonoids, sesquiterpenes, and thymol, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and pain associated with bruises. Arnica's active compounds, such as helenalin and dihydrohelenalin, are responsible for its anti-inflammatory effects, which can help speed up the healing process and reduce discoloration. If you have a bruise and want to try Arnica montana tinctures, apply a few drops of the tincture directly to the affected area with a clean cloth. You can also take Arnica internally, but consult with a healthcare professional before doing so.

Start with a small dose, such as 5-7 drops, and gradually increase as needed. It's essential to note that Arnica can interact with certain medications, so inform your healthcare provider before using it. When you start taking Arnica tinctures, you may notice a reduction in pain and swelling within a few hours. The discoloration may also fade faster, and the bruise may heal sooner than it would without Arnica. However, it's crucial to remember that Arnica is not a substitute for medical treatment.

If your bruise is severe or you experience other concerning symptoms, such as increased pain or swelling, seek medical attention.

2. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum tinctures contains compounds like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and biflavonoids that help reduce inflammation and promote healing in bruises.

The flavonoids, particularly rutin and hyperoside, have antioxidant properties that protect cells from damage and improve blood flow to the affected area. This increased blood flow brings oxygen and nutrients to the bruised tissue, facilitating the repair process and reducing discoloration. To use Hypericum perforatum tinctures for bruises, apply a few drops topically to the affected area 2-3 times a day. You can also take 20-30 drops internally, diluted in water, 2-3 times a day.

It's essential to note that the tincture should be made from a reputable source and follow the recommended dosage. If you have sensitive skin, do a patch test before applying the tincture topically. When taking action, you can expect the following outcomes: reduced inflammation and swelling, improved healing time, and a decrease in bruising duration. Some users may also notice a reduction in pain and discomfort. However, it's crucial to be patient, as Hypericum perforatum tinctures work gradually to promote healing.

It's also essential to combine the use of the tincture with rest, elevation, and cold compression, as recommended by healthcare professionals.

3. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help with bruises.

The main constituents, such as triterpenoids and saponins, work together to reduce swelling, ease pain, and prevent infection. When applied topically to a bruise, the tincture can help break down blood clots and promote the healing process. To use Calendula officinalis tinctures for bruises, apply a few drops of the tincture to a clean cloth and gently press it onto the affected area.

You can also mix a few drops of the tincture with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it directly to the bruise. Repeat this process several times a day to help reduce swelling and ease pain. As the tincture works to break down blood clots and promote healing, you may notice a reduction in swelling and discoloration of the bruise within a few days. The pain and discomfort associated with the bruise may also subside, allowing you to move the affected area more comfortably.

However, the healing process can take time, so be patient and continue to apply the tincture as needed.

4. Hamamelis virginiana

Hamamelis virginiana tinctures contains a combination of compounds like hamamelitannin, gallic acid, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.

These properties help reduce swelling, bleeding, and pain in bruises. When applied topically, the tannins in Hamamelis virginiana tinctures can constrict blood vessels, which slows down blood flow to the affected area, reducing the discoloration and swelling associated with bruises. To use Hamamelis virginiana tinctures for bruises, apply a few drops directly to the affected area with a cotton ball. You can also mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and gently massage it into the skin. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day until the bruise starts to fade.

You can also take the tincture internally, but it's essential to dilute it with water or another liquid, as it can be quite strong. Start with a small dose of 10-20 drops and gradually increase as needed. Taking action with Hamamelis virginiana tinctures may lead to a reduction in swelling and discoloration of the bruise. You may also experience relief from pain and discomfort associated with the bruise. However, it's essential to remember that bruises heal at different rates, and the effectiveness of Hamamelis virginiana tinctures may vary from person to person.

As with any new remedy, always consult with a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or allergies.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent bruises from forming?

Drinking herbal tea, particularly those high in flavonoids like chamomile or peppermint, may help prevent bruises from forming.

This is because flavonoids have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce blood vessel permeability and improve blood flow.

As a result, herbal tea may help minimize bruising and promote faster healing.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for bruises every day?

While herbal teas for bruises can be soothing, consuming them daily may not be safe for everyone.

Some herbs can interact with medications or worsen conditions like bleeding disorders. It's best to use them in moderation and for short periods to see if they help.

If symptoms persist, you may need to try other remedies.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in bruises?

Herbal teas like arnica and calendula can help with bruises.

It might take a few days for them to show results. The herbs help reduce swelling and promote healing.

Some people see improvement within 2-3 days, while others may take a little longer, up to 5-7 days, for the bruising to fade.

1. Arnica montana

Arnica montana essential oils contains bioactive constituents like sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and triterpene acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and pain associated with bruises. The sesquiterpene lactones, specifically helenalin and 11,13-dihydrohelenalin, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, further reducing inflammation. The flavonoids present in Arnica montana essential oils have antioxidant properties, which help protect the damaged tissue from oxidative stress and promote healing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Arnica montana essential oils can help bruises recover faster and reduce the appearance of discoloration.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Arnica montana flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the Arnica flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Store the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and use it as needed for bruises. Use 2-3 drops at a time.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds possess anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and regenerative properties that help to reduce the appearance of bruises. The flavonoids in Calendula officinalis essential oils, such as quercetin and kaempferol, have strong antioxidant properties that can help to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals. The triterpenoid saponins in the oil, including oleanolic acid and ursolic acid, have been shown to promote tissue repair and reduce swelling.

By applying Calendula officinalis essential oils topically, the regenerative and anti-inflammatory properties of the oil can help to promote the healing of bruises and reduce their visibility.

  1. Gather 250g of Calendula flowers and 500ml of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine Calendula flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar, making sure the flowers are completely covered.
  3. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl.
  5. Discard the used flowers and transfer the remaining oil to a dark glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops of the oil as needed for bruise relief.

3. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum essential oils contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce swelling and promote healing. The flavonoids, specifically rutin and quercetin, improve blood circulation and reduce bleeding, which aids in the healing process of bruises. The phenolic acids, including hyperoside and aesculetin, have astringent properties that help constrict blood vessels and reduce fluid buildup.

The terpenes, including limonene and pinene, have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce pain and inflammation associated with bruises.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Hypericum perforatum flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil)
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar, and stir well to mix.
  3. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into another clean glass jar, and discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the Hypericum perforatum essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops for bruise relief.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains a high concentration of bioactive constituents, including aloe-emodin, aloe-ferulic acid, and aloin.

These compounds possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce swelling and promote healing in bruised skin. Aloe-emodin, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to bruising and discoloration. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of aloe-ferulic acid help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting a faster and more efficient healing process.

When applied topically, the bioactive constituents in aloe barbadensis essential oils work together to soothe and calm bruised skin, accelerating the healing process and reducing the appearance of discoloration.

  1. Harvest 2 cups of Aloe barbadensis leaves from a mature plant. Choose leaves with no signs of disease or damage.
  2. Cut the leaves into small pieces and wash them with cool water to remove dirt and bacteria.
  3. Dry the Aloe pieces in a low-temperature oven (150°F - 200°F) for 2-3 hours to remove excess moisture.
  4. Extract the essential oil by blending 1 cup of dried Aloe pieces with 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) in a blender.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the solids and store the oil in a dark glass bottle.

5. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains active constituents such as alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and pain associated with bruises. The essential oils also have antioxidant properties, thanks to the presence of phenolic acids, that aid in the removal of free radicals and promote healing. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties work together to improve circulation, which helps to bring oxygen and nutrients to the affected area, promoting faster healing.

By using Echinacea purpurea essential oils, you can potentially reduce the healing time of bruises and promote a smoother recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers, a glass jar with a lid, a clean cheesecloth, and a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil.
  2. Fill the glass jar with the Echinacea purpurea flowers, leaving 1 inch at the top.
  3. Pour 2 cups of carrier oil over the flowers, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. Then, strain the mixture through cheesecloth into another container.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Echinacea purpurea essential oil in a dark glass bottle. Use 2-3 drops as needed to help with bruising.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains a combination of active constituents, including linalool and linalyl acetate, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds help to reduce swelling and pain associated with bruises. The antiseptic properties of lavender oil also prevent infection and promote healing in the affected area. The antioxidant properties of linalool and linalyl acetate help to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting a faster recovery.

By reducing inflammation, preventing infection, and protecting the skin, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can aid in the healing process of bruises.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers, 1 cup of water, and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine the flowers and water in the glass jar, and close the lid.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the essential oil in a cool, dark place and use 2-3 drops on a bruise 2-3 times a day for relief.

7. Hamamelis virginiana

Hamamelis virginiana essential oils contains tannins and flavonoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds help to constrict blood vessels, which reduces bleeding and swelling in bruises. Tannins also have anti-inflammatory properties, which ease pain and discomfort caused by bruising. Flavonoids, specifically rutin and kaempferol, have antioxidant properties that help to protect damaged tissues and promote healing.

As a result, Hamamelis virginiana essential oils can be used to help reduce the appearance and discomfort of bruises.

  1. Harvest 1 cup of Hamamelis virginiana leaves and flowers.
  2. Dry the leaves and flowers in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks.
  3. Use a steam distiller to extract the essential oil from the dried leaves and flowers.
  4. Collect 1 cup of water in the distiller's water reservoir and add 2 cups of dried leaves and flowers.
  5. Distill for 2-3 hours, then separate the essential oil from the water using a separator or filter.

8. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains compounds like bornyl acetate, camphor, and 1,8-cineole.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce swelling and promote healing in bruises. The oils' analgesic properties, particularly from camphor, can also help alleviate pain associated with bruising. Furthermore, the antioxidant activity of 1,8-cineole can help protect damaged tissue from oxidative stress, supporting the recovery process.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help bruises heal faster and more efficiently.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water.
  2. Heat the water in a saucepan over low heat until it reaches 100°F (38°C).
  3. Add the fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves to the water and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a clean glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the essential oil in a carrier oil or cream for treating bruises.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent bruises from forming?

Moderation is key when it comes to these teas, and it's essential to listen to your body's response.

As the body heals, the bruise should become less visible.

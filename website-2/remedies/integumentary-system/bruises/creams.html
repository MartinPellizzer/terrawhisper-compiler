1. Arnica montana

Arnica montana creams contains flavoglycosides, thiol esters, and sesquiterpene lactones, which are the key active constituents that help with bruising.

Flavoglycosides reduce inflammation by slowing down the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause swelling. Thiol esters, on the other hand, have a direct anti-inflammatory effect by blocking the production of these prostaglandins. Sesquiterpene lactones also have anti-inflammatory properties by inhibiting the enzymes that cause pain and swelling.

By reducing inflammation and pain, Arnica montana creams can help bruises heal faster and more effectively.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of arnica oil, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil, beeswax, and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Stir in the arnica oil and vitamin E oil until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into a small bowl or container and let it cool completely. This will take about 30 minutes to an hour.
  5. Once the mixture has cooled and solidified, you can apply it to bruises as needed. Store any leftover cream in an airtight container in the refrigerator.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce swelling and promote healing in bruises. The aloe-emodin in Aloe barbadensis creams has been shown to inhibit the production of inflammatory enzymes, which can contribute to bruising and pain. The antioxidant properties of acemannan help protect the skin from further damage and promote collagen production, which can aid in the healing process.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Aloe barbadensis creams can help alleviate the appearance and discomfort of bruises.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1 tablespoon of aloe vera gel, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, and 2 tablespoons of beeswax.
  2. Melt the beeswax in a double boiler or in the microwave for 10-15 seconds.
  3. Combine the melted beeswax, coconut oil, and aloe vera gel in a bowl. Stir until the mixture is smooth.
  4. Add the distilled water to the mixture and stir until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Use as a cream to soothe bruises.

3. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains boiactive constituents like triterpene saponins, sesquiterpene lactones, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce swelling and promote healing in bruises. The sesquiterpene lactones in Calendula officinalis creams also have a soothing effect on the skin, which can help calm the pain and discomfort associated with bruising. The triterpene saponins in the cream have antimicrobial properties, which can help prevent infection and promote a clean environment for healing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Calendula officinalis creams can help bruises heal faster and more effectively.

  1. Gather 2 cups of water, 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, and 2 tablespoons of shea butter.
  2. Infuse the Calendula flowers in the water for 2 hours or overnight. Strain the mixture and discard the flowers.
  3. In a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl, melt the beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter. Stir until smooth.
  4. Add the infused water to the melted mixture and stir well. Remove from heat.
  5. Let the mixture cool and thicken. Pour into a container and store in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

4. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale creams contains active constituents like allantoin, aloe-emodin, and mucilages.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that help reduce swelling and pain in bruises. Allantoin in particular promotes cell growth and regeneration, which aids in the healing process of damaged tissues. The mucilages in Symphytum officinale creams also form a protective barrier on the skin's surface, shielding it from further irritation and promoting a smooth recovery.

By reducing inflammation and promoting tissue regeneration, Symphytum officinale creams can help bruises heal faster and more effectively.

  1. Gather 250g of Symphytum officinale root, 1 cup of water, and a saucepan.
  2. Boil the water in the saucepan and add the Symphytum officinale root. Let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture and let it cool down. Then, mix it with 1/2 cup of beeswax and 1/4 cup of coconut oil in a double boiler.
  4. Heat the mixture until the beeswax is melted and well combined. Remove from heat and let it cool down slightly.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool down completely. Once set, your Symphytum officinale cream is ready to use.

5. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum creams contains flavonoids, such as quercetin and rutin, and naphthoquinones, like hyperforin and hypericin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce swelling and promote healing in bruises. The flavonoids in Hypericum perforatum creams also have a vasoconstrictive effect, which helps to reduce blood flow to the affected area and prevent further bruising. The antioxidants in the cream can neutralize free radicals, which can damage skin cells and slow down the healing process.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Hypericum perforatum creams can help bruises to recover faster and more effectively.

  1. Gather 100g of dried Hypericum perforatum flowers and 1 cup of coconut oil in a small saucepan.
  2. Heat the coconut oil over low heat and add the dried Hypericum perforatum flowers.
  3. Let the mixture simmer for 2-3 hours, then strain it through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Let the mixture cool, then add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter.
  5. Mix well and pour the cream into a container, let it cool and harden before use.

6. Hamamelis virginiana

Hamamelis virginiana creams contains a compound called astringent tannins, which help reduce inflammation and swelling in bruises.

The high concentration of flavonoids, particularly quercetin, in Hamamelis virginiana creams has antioxidant properties that fight free radicals and promote healing. The mucilages in the cream also help soothe and calm the skin, reducing pain and discomfort associated with bruises. The anti-inflammatory properties of Hamamelis virginiana creams, attributed to the presence of phenolic acids and glycosides, aid in reducing redness and swelling.

By applying Hamamelis virginiana creams to bruises, the combination of these bioactive constituents can help promote a faster recovery and reduce the appearance of bruising.

  2. Heat the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water, stirring until the beeswax is melted.
  3. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool slightly. Add the Hamamelis virginiana flowers, vitamin E oil, and shea butter. Stir well.
  4. Let the mixture cool and set at room temperature for 30 minutes. Stir occasionally to help it cool evenly.
  5. Once the mixture is set, use a spatula to scoop it into a clean container. Let it cool completely before using as a cream for bruises.

7. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce swelling and promote healing of bruises. Alkylamides, specifically echinacoside and iso-echinacoside, have been shown to inhibit the release of inflammatory mediators, which can help minimize bruising. The glycosides, like chicoric acid, have antioxidant properties that help protect damaged tissues from further harm.

By applying Echinacea purpurea creams to bruises, the bioactive constituents can help alleviate inflammation, promote tissue repair, and speed up the healing process.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Dry the Echinacea flowers in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 2 hours. Let them cool completely.
  3. In a double boiler, melt the coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax. Stir until the beeswax is fully dissolved.
  4. Add the dried Echinacea flowers and vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once set, your Echinacea purpurea cream is ready to use on bruises.

8. Plantago major

Plantago major creams contains aucubin, baicalein, and caffeic acid as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce swelling and promote healing in bruises. Aucubin, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, while baicalein and caffeic acid have strong antioxidant effects that help to protect the skin from damage. The anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds help to reduce the redness and swelling associated with bruises, while their antioxidant effects promote the healing process.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Plantago major creams can help to speed up the recovery process for bruises.

  3. In a saucepan, melt the coconut oil and beeswax over low heat. Stir until the beeswax is fully dissolved.
  4. Add the chopped Plantago major leaves to the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once set, use as a cream for bruise treatment.

9. Vaccinium myrtillus

Vaccinium myrtillus creams contains anthocyanins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are powerful antioxidants that help reduce inflammation and promote healing.

These antioxidants work by neutralizing free radicals that can damage skin cells and slow down the healing process. The anthocyanins in Vaccinium myrtillus creams also have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain associated with bruises.   5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify. Store in a cool, dry place.

10. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids, bilobalide, and ginkgolides as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce the swelling and discoloration of bruises. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba creams have been shown to improve blood flow to the affected area, promoting faster healing and reducing the time it takes for bruises to fade. By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Ginkgo biloba creams can be an effective treatment for bruises.

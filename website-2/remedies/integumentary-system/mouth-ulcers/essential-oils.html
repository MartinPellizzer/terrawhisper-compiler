1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains compounds like aloin and aloe-emodin, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in mouth ulcers.

The gel-like substance in the oil, composed of polysaccharides and glycoproteins, forms a protective barrier on the ulcer, promoting faster healing and preventing further irritation. Aloe's anti-bacterial properties, thanks to the presence of eugenol, help combat bacterial infections that can exacerbate mouth ulcers. The oil's soothing effects, attributed to its high water content and vitamin E, also calm the affected area, reducing discomfort and promoting a healthy environment for healing.

By reducing inflammation, preventing infection, and promoting a soothing environment, Aloe barbadensis essential oils can help alleviate mouth ulcers.

  1. Get 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel from a mature leaf. Wash the leaf with water, then dry it with a clean cloth.
  2. Cut the leaf to get the gel. Scoop the gel into a clean container, and set aside 2 tablespoons for later use.
  3. Add 1 cup of water to the container with the Aloe gel. Mix well to create a smooth paste.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth to remove any lumps. Collect the clear liquid, and set it aside.
  5. Add 10 drops of an essential oil (like peppermint or tea tree oil) to the collected liquid. Mix well to create the Aloe essential oil for mouth ulcers.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in the mouth.

Gingerols also have antioxidant properties that protect the mouth from free radicals, which can cause damage and make mouth ulcers worse. The anti-inflammatory properties of gingerol and shogaol also help reduce redness and inflammation in the mouth, making mouth ulcers less visible and painful. Additionally, the antiseptic properties of gingerol help prevent infection and promote healing in the mouth.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help alleviate mouth ulcers and promote a healthy mouth environment.

  1. Gather 250 grams of fresh Zingiber officinale root and wash it with water.
  2. Dry the root in a warm, dark place for 2-3 days. Check on it daily.
  3. Grind the dried root into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
  4. Mix 250 grams of the powdered root with 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil) in a clean glass jar.
  5. Store the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily. Strain the oil and use it as needed to treat mouth ulcers.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help reduce redness and swelling in mouth ulcers. The alkaloids and phenolic acids present in the oil also exhibit antioxidant properties, which protect the mouth tissues from further damage and promote healing. The essential oil's ability to inhibit the growth of bacteria and viruses can also help prevent the infection of mouth ulcers, reducing the risk of complications.

By applying Echinacea purpurea essential oils to the affected area, you can potentially alleviate symptoms and promote faster recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Dry the Echinacea flowers in a warm place or using a food dehydrator for 24 hours.
  3. Combine 1 cup of the dried Echinacea flowers with 2 cups of carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the solids and store the oil in a dark glass bottle.

4. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole, a potent anti-inflammatory compound that helps reduce swelling and pain associated with mouth ulcers.

Cineole also has antimicrobial properties, which work to combat the bacterial and viral infections that often cause mouth ulcers. Eucalyptus globulus essential oils also contain eucalyptol, a compound that helps to reduce inflammation and promote healing. The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of eucalyptol and cineole in Eucalyptus globulus essential oils help to create an environment that is conducive to the healing of mouth ulcers.

Regular application of Eucalyptus globulus essential oils to the affected area can help to accelerate the healing process and reduce the severity of mouth ulcers.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a clean glass jar with a lid, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Wash the leaves in cold water, then gently pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Place the leaves in the glass jar and cover them completely with carrier oil. The ratio is 1 cup of leaves to 2 cups of carrier oil.
  4. Steep the leaves in the carrier oil for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place. Shake the jar daily to help the oil infuse.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids and store the oil in a cool, dark place.

5. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and terpinen-4-ol, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

  2. Add 1 cup of water to the jar, then add the fresh Melaleuca alternifolia leaves. Cover the jar with the lid.
  3. Let the mixture sit in a shaded area for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will help release the oil from the leaves.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the liquid in a cool, dark place and use a glass dropper to apply 2-3 drops directly to the mouth ulcer as needed.

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains the bioactive constituents menthone and limonene.

These compounds have antimicrobial properties, which help to reduce the growth of bacteria in the mouth that can cause ulcers. Mentha x piperita essential oils also has anti-inflammatory properties due to the presence of menthone, which helps to soothe and calm irritated tissues in the mouth. The antiseptic properties of limonene in Mentha x piperita essential oils further aid in preventing infections and promoting healing of mouth ulcers.

The analgesic properties of menthone in Mentha x piperita essential oils help to reduce pain and discomfort associated with mouth ulcers.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Mentha x piperita (peppermint) leaves. Wash them with clean water.
  2. Use a juicer or blender to extract the juice from the leaves. You need 1 cup of juice.
  3. Transfer the juice to a saucepan and add 2 cups of water. Heat the mixture on low heat for 30 minutes.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl. Discard the solids.

8. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains bioactive constituents like triterpenoids and carotenoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and pain in mouth ulcers. 9. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis essential oils contains the bioactive constituents quinine, cinchonine, and cinchonidine.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help to reduce the swelling and fight off the infection that causes mouth ulcers. The alkaloids in Cinchona officinalis essential oils also have a soothing effect on the mucous membranes in the mouth, providing relief from pain and discomfort. Additionally, the oil's antiseptic properties prevent the spread of infection and promote healing.

By applying Cinchona officinalis essential oils topically to the affected area, it can help to speed up the recovery process and alleviate the symptoms of mouth ulcers.

  4. Let the liquid cool and then transfer it to a dark glass bottle. Add 1 tablespoon of vodka (optional) to preserve the oil.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2 weeks in a cool, dark place. Shake the bottle daily. Strain the oil again before use.

10. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains curcumin, a bioactive compound that gives it its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These properties help to reduce the size and severity of mouth ulcers by fighting off the bacteria that cause them. Curcumin also has antioxidant properties, which help to reduce the damage caused by free radicals and promote healing in the affected area. Additionally, curcumin's anti-inflammatory properties help to reduce pain and swelling associated with mouth ulcers.

By applying Curcuma longa essential oils to the affected area, you can take advantage of these properties to promote healing and alleviate symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Curcuma longa roots and 2 cups of water.
  2. Chop the Curcuma longa roots into small pieces and place them in a saucepan with 2 cups of water.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes, then remove from heat and let it steep for 1 hour.

  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and store it in the refrigerator for up to 7 days. Use it as is or dilute it with a carrier oil for application to mouth ulcers.

Drinking herbal teas for mouth ulcers daily can be safe, but some may cause side effects.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in mouth ulcers?

Herbal teas can help soothe mouth ulcers, but it takes some time to see results.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for mouth ulcers?

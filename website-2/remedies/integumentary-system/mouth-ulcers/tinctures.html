1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, glycoproteins, and phenolic acids that contribute to its beneficial properties.

The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help reduce swelling and pain associated with mouth ulcers. The glycoproteins, particularly polysaccharides, stimulate the immune system, promoting the healing process and reducing the severity of mouth ulcers. The phenolic acids in Echinacea purpurea have antioxidant properties, which can protect the mouth tissues from oxidative stress and promote faster healing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Echinacea purpurea tinctures can help alleviate the discomfort and pain caused by mouth ulcers.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves.
  2. Combine the flowers and leaves in a clean glass jar.
  3. Pour 2 cups of 80-proof vodka into the jar, making sure the plants are completely covered.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

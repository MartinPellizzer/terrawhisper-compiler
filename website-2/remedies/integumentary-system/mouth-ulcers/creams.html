The Top 11 Creams for Older Adults to Soothe Mouth Ulcers

Mouth ulcers can be a real nuisance for older adults.

As we age, our immune systems can weaken, making us more prone to infections and sores in the mouth. This can be caused by a variety of factors, such as poor dental hygiene, gum disease, or even certain medications. When an older adult gets a mouth ulcer, it can be painful and uncomfortable, making everyday activities like eating and speaking a challenge. They may have to be extra careful about what they eat and drink, and even simple tasks like talking on the phone can become difficult. This can affect their quality of life and overall well-being, making them feel isolated and withdrawn.

They may also be at risk of dehydration and malnutrition if their mouth ulcers are severe and persistent. However, there is some good news. For older adults who suffer from mouth ulcers, herbal creams can provide relief. These creams are made from natural ingredients that have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, which can help to reduce the pain and discomfort of a mouth ulcer. They can also aid in the healing process, promoting the growth of healthy tissue and speeding up the recovery time. This can be especially helpful for older adults who are sensitive to over-the-counter medications or have difficulty swallowing pills.

By providing a natural and gentle solution, herbal creams can help older adults to manage their mouth ulcers and get back to living their lives with confidence and comfort.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams have been traditionally used to help soothe and heal mouth ulcers.

The flowers of the Calendula plant contain anti-inflammatory properties, including sesquiterpenes and triterpenoids, which can help reduce swelling and ease pain. The cream's main constituent, calendulin, is a complex mixture of glycosides, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which have antimicrobial and antiseptic properties. This helps prevent infections and promotes a healthy healing environment for the ulcer. To use Calendula officinalis cream for mouth ulcers, apply a small amount to the affected area with a cotton swab several times a day.

You can also mix a few drops of the cream with water to create a mouthwash and swish it around your mouth for 30 seconds before spitting it out. Be gentle when applying the cream, as the ulcer area may be sensitive. When you apply the cream, you may feel a slight tingling sensation as the anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties start to work. The ulcer may start to heal faster, and the pain may decrease. You may also notice a reduction in redness and swelling around the ulcer.

As the ulcer heals, it's essential to maintain good oral hygiene by brushing and flossing regularly and avoiding irritants that may trigger new ulcers.

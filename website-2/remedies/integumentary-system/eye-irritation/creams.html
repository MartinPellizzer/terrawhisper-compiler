1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, carotenoids, and flavonoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce redness and swelling in the eyes. The anti-inflammatory properties of triterpenoid saponins in Calendula officinalis creams help soothe and calm irritated eyes, while the antioxidant properties of carotenoids and flavonoids protect the eyes from further damage. The cream's ability to reduce inflammation and protect the eyes makes it a good remedy for eye irritation caused by exposure to dust, pollen, or other environmental factors.

By applying Calendula officinalis creams to the affected area, individuals can potentially experience relief from eye irritation and promote a faster recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Dry the Calendula flowers in a low oven (150°F) for 2 hours. Then, grind them into a fine powder using a coffee grinder.
  3. Melt the coconut oil, beeswax, and shea butter in a double boiler. Stir until the beeswax is fully dissolved.
  4. Add the ground Calendula powder and vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool. Once cooled, use the Calendula cream to soothe eye irritation.

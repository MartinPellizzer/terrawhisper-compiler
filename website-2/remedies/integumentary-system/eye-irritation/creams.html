What herbal creams you should avoid if you have eye irritation?

When you have eye irritation, it's essential to be cautious with herbal creams.

Some herbs can make the irritation worse or even cause more problems. For example, creams containing Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica) or Comfrey (Symphytum asperum) should be avoided, as they can cause more irritation and even an allergic reaction in some people.

Another herb to be careful with is Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana), which can be too drying for the eyes and make irritation worse. Similarly, creams containing Tea Tree Oil (Melaleuca alternifolia) or Lavender Oil (Lavandula angustifolia) should be used with caution, as they can be too harsh for the eyes and cause more irritation. Also, creams containing Ginkgo biloba, Ginseng (Panax ginseng), or Turmeric (Curcuma longa) should be used with caution, as they can be too potent for the eyes and cause more problems.

It's always best to read the labels carefully and consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal creams, especially if you have eye irritation.

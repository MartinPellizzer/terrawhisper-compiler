Eye Protection for Chemical Handlers: 10 Herbal Teas to the Rescue

Chemical handlers are people who work with chemicals, like cleaners or pesticides, to do their jobs.

When they're not careful, these chemicals can splash into their eyes, causing irritation. This can be very painful and uncomfortable. It's not just the pain that's a problem - it can also make it hard to see and do their work. Some chemical handlers might even have to take time off work to recover from a bad case of eye irritation. This can be frustrating and stressful, especially if they have a lot of responsibilities or deadlines to meet. When chemical handlers get eye irritation, they might experience redness, itchiness, and even swelling in their eyes.

They might also feel a burning sensation or have blurred vision. These symptoms can last for a few hours or even days, depending on the severity of the irritation. If the irritation is severe, chemical handlers might need to see a doctor for treatment. Herbal teas can be a helpful addition to the treatment of eye irritation in chemical handlers. These teas are made from plants that have been used for centuries to calm and soothe irritated tissues. When chemical handlers drink these teas, they can help reduce the inflammation and discomfort in their eyes.

This can make it easier for them to recover from eye irritation and get back to work.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas are a popular remedy for eye irritation due to their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

The gel extracted from the Aloe barbadensis plant contains compounds like aloe-emodin, aloin, and vitamins A, C, and E, which help to reduce redness, swelling, and pain in the eyes. When used as a tea, these compounds can be absorbed through the mouth and bloodstream, reaching the eyes and providing relief. To try Aloe barbadensis teas for eye irritation, steep one teaspoon of dried Aloe barbadensis leaves in a cup of boiling water for five to seven minutes.

Let it cool, then strain and drink it as a tea. It's essential to note that you should not put the tea directly in your eyes, as this can cause more irritation. Instead, look for relief from the internal absorption of the compounds. You may start to feel a reduction in eye irritation and discomfort within a few hours of drinking the tea.

If your symptoms persist or worsen, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas have been used for centuries to soothe and calm eye irritation.

This is because the tea is rich in flavonoids and triterpenoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help to reduce redness, swelling, and discomfort associated with eye irritation. The tea also contains carotenoids and saponins, which have antimicrobial and soothing effects. When you experience eye irritation, try making a tea by steeping 1 teaspoon of dried Calendula officinalis flowers in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it to a clean cloth or cotton pad and gently pressing it against your closed eyelids. You can also use eye drops or compresses soaked in the cooled tea. After 15-20 minutes, discard the cloth or pad and wash your eyes with cool water. If you experience persistent or severe eye irritation, consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

By taking these steps, you may experience relief from eye irritation, reduced redness and swelling, and a decrease in discomfort.

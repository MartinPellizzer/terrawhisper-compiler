What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have eye irritation?

If you have eye irritation, it's best to avoid certain herbal essential oils that can make the problem worse.

Oils from herbs like Peppermint (Mentha piperita) and Wintergreen (Gaultheria procumbens) are too strong and can irritate your eyes even more. These oils can cause a burning sensation and make your eyes water more. Oils from herbs like Birch (Betula alba) and Sweet Birch (Betula lenta) contain salicylic acid, which can be too harsh for your eyes. This acid can also cause inflammation and make your eye irritation worse.

You should also avoid Oregano (Origanum vulgare) oil, as it can be too intense and may cause more eye irritation. Thyme (Thymus vulgaris) oil, while good for many things, can also be too strong for your eyes. If you still want to use essential oils for eye irritation, consider using Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia), Frankincense (Boswellia serrata), and Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens) oils. These oils are generally milder and may help soothe your eyes.

However, always dilute them with a carrier oil and use them carefully.

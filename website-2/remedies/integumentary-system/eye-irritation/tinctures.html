1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains bioactive constituents like saponins, flavonoids, and triterpenoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These properties help to soothe and calm irritated eyes by reducing redness and swelling. The flavonoids present in Calendula officinalis tinctures, particularly quercetin, have antioxidant properties that help to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the eye tissues. The antimicrobial properties of triterpenoids help to fight off bacterial and fungal infections that can cause eye irritation.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off infections, Calendula officinalis tinctures can help to provide relief from eye irritation and promote healthy eye tissue.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers, a clean glass jar with a lid, and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Combine 1 cup of Calendula flowers with 2 cups of vodka in the glass jar. Stir well and seal the jar.
  3. Place the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the dark glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  5. Label the bottle with the date and contents. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in the affected eye 2-3 times a day as needed.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains the bioactive constituents alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help to reduce redness and swelling in the eyes. The alkylamides, specifically echinacoside and isobutylamides, have been shown to inhibit the release of histamine, a chemical that causes inflammation in the eyes. The caffeic acid derivatives, including caftaric and cichoric acids, have strong antioxidant properties that help to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the eyes.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Echinacea purpurea tinctures can help to alleviate eye irritation and promote healing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves.
  2. Combine the flowers and leaves in a clean glass jar with a lid.
  3. Pour 2 cups of 80% to 100% vodka or glycerin over the Echinacea mixture.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2 to 3 weeks, shaking it every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids.

3. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis tinctures contains rosmarinic acid, ursolic acid, and caffeic acid as its key bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce eye irritation caused by inflammation or oxidative stress. Rosmarinic acid, in particular, has been shown to have potent antioxidant activity, neutralizing free radicals that can damage eye tissues. The anti-inflammatory properties of ursolic acid and caffeic acid help reduce swelling and redness in the eyes, providing relief from irritation.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Melissa officinalis tinctures can help soothe and calm irritated eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers, 2 cups of vodka (at least 40% ABV), and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine the Melissa officinalis flowers and vodka in the glass jar, making sure the flowers are completely covered.
  3. Close the jar and shake it well for 10-15 seconds, then let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in the fridge and use 5-10 drops in the affected eye 2-3 times a day as needed, or as directed by a healthcare professional.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis tinctures contains the bioactive constituents aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A and E. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce redness and swelling in the eyes.

Aloin and aloe-emodin also have soothing and calming effects, which can help to calm irritated eyes. Vitamin A has antioxidant properties that can help protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals, while vitamin E can help to improve blood flow to the eyes, reducing inflammation.

The combination of these properties makes Aloe barbadensis tinctures a potential remedy for eye irritation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 1 cup of vodka or 80-proof alcohol, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Aloe gel and vodka in the glass jar, stirring well to mix.
  3. Cover the jar with a lid and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the Aloe tincture in a cool, dark place and use 5-7 drops in each eye as needed for eye irritation relief.

5. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale tinctures contains a mix of bioactive constituents like allantoin, rosmarinic acid, and phenylethanoid glycosides.

Allantoin, in particular, has anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can help reduce swelling and redness in the eyes. Rosmarinic acid has antioxidant properties that can protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals and promote healing. Phenylethanoid glycosides have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help reduce eye irritation and prevent infection.

When used as a tincture, these constituents can provide quick and effective relief from eye irritation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Symphytum officinale leaves, 2 cups of 80-proof vodka, and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Chop the Symphytum officinale leaves into small pieces and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour 2 cups of vodka over the chopped leaves and close the jar. Let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Label the jar with the date and the name of the tincture, and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 10-20 drops of the tincture in water or directly on the affected eye to help with irritation.

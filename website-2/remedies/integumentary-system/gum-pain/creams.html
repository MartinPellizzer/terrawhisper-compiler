1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains bioactive constituents like eucalyptol, globulol, and beta-caryophyllene.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which help reduce swelling and ease pain in the gums. Eucalyptol, in particular, has been shown to have a numbing effect on the mucous membranes, providing relief from pain and discomfort. Additionally, globulol has antimicrobial properties, which can help combat bacteria and other microorganisms that may be contributing to gum inflammation.

By addressing both the pain and the underlying cause of inflammation, Eucalyptus globulus creams can be an effective treatment for gum pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of eucalyptus oil globulus essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in the eucalyptus oil globulus essential oil. Let it cool for 5 minutes.
  4. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool completely. This will take about 30 minutes.
  5. Once set, use a spoon to scoop out a small amount and apply it to the affected gum area as needed.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contain aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-juice as active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can help reduce swelling and pain in the gums. The aloe-emodin in Aloe barbadensis creams has antimicrobial properties, which can help combat bacteria that cause gum infections and inflammation. Additionally, the aloe-juice in Aloe barbadensis creams has antioxidant properties that can help protect the gums from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the gums, Aloe barbadensis creams can help alleviate gum pain and promote overall gum health.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup distilled water, 1/2 cup coconut oil, 1/4 cup beeswax, 1 tablespoon aloe vera gel, 1 teaspoon vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or microwave-safe bowl in short intervals, stirring between each heating.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix aloe vera gel and vitamin E oil. Add this mixture to the melted coconut oil and beeswax.
  4. Add distilled water to the mixture and stir well. Pour the mixture into a glass jar or container.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify completely. Once set, your aloe vera cream for gum pain is ready to use.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and polysaccharides that help reduce inflammation and fight off infections.

Alkylamides have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe gum pain by reducing swelling and redness. Caffeic acid derivatives have antibacterial properties that help kill the bacteria that cause gum infections and pain. Polysaccharides stimulate the immune system, which helps to fight off the infection and reduce pain.

By applying Echinacea purpurea creams to the affected area, you can help to reduce gum pain and promote healing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of coconut oil in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in the saucepan over low heat and add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers.
  3. Let it simmer for 2-3 hours or until the oil has turned pink and has a herbal scent.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Mix 2 tablespoons of beeswax with 2 tablespoons of the infused oil in a double boiler and heat until the beeswax melts, then pour into a container and let cool.

5. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in the gums.

These compounds work by blocking the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause pain and inflammation. Gingerol and shogaol also have antiseptic properties, which help prevent infections that can cause gum pain. Additionally, Zingiber officinale creams contain antioxidants like flavonoids and phenolic acids, which protect the gums from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infections, Zingiber officinale creams can provide relief from gum pain and promote healing.

  1. Mix 2 tablespoons of grated Zingiber officinale (ginger) with 1 cup of coconut oil in a small saucepan.
  2. Heat the mixture over low heat for 10-15 minutes, until the oil turns warm and the ginger is soft.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 5 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the ginger solids.
  5. Store the ginger cream in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

6. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis creams contains tannins and flavonoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have astringent and anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce swelling and ease pain in the gums. The tannins, in particular, have a binding effect on the gums, which can help to constrict blood vessels and reduce inflammation. The flavonoids, on the other hand, have antioxidant properties that help to protect the gums from damage and promote healing.

By applying Salvia officinalis creams to the affected area, the active constituents can work together to alleviate gum pain and discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Salvia officinalis leaves, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments until melted.
  3. Add the dried Salvia officinalis leaves to the melted coconut oil and let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the leaves.
  5. Combine the strained mixture with the melted beeswax and stir until well combined. Pour into a tin or jar and let cool.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and irritation in the gums. How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in gum pain?

Herbal teas can help with gum pain, but it's hard to predict exactly when you'll notice a difference.

It's also possible that it might take longer, so be patient and keep drinking the tea as directed.

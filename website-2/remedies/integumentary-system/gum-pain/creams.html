7 Herbal Creams For Gum Pain

Dental patients often suffer from gum pain due to various reasons such as gum disease, tooth decay, or injury to the gums.

This can cause sensitivity, swelling, and discomfort that makes eating, drinking, and even talking a painful experience. The pain can be sharp and stabbing, or a dull ache that persists for hours. For some patients, the pain can be severe enough to affect their daily activities, sleep, and mood. Gum pain can also be a sign of a more serious underlying issue, such as an abscess or a sinus infection. When left untreated, gum pain can lead to more complex problems, including tooth loss and difficulty chewing or swallowing.

As a result, patients may become anxious or depressed, and their quality of life can suffer. Herbal creams have been used for centuries to treat gum pain and other oral health issues. These creams are typically made from the extracts of certain plants that have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. They work by reducing swelling and pain, and preventing the growth of bacteria that can cause infection. When applied topically to the affected area, these creams can provide quick relief from discomfort and promote healing.

By using herbal creams, dental patients can find a natural and effective way to manage their gum pain and improve their oral health.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains compounds like eucalyptol and cineole, which help to reduce pain and inflammation in the gums.

When you apply Eucalyptus globulus cream to your gums, the active ingredients are absorbed, causing the blood vessels to constrict and reducing blood flow to the affected area. This helps to decrease the amount of bacteria and inflammation that cause pain. To use Eucalyptus globulus cream for gum pain, apply a small amount to a cotton swab or your finger and gently rub it onto the affected area.

Make sure to avoid swallowing the cream and wash your hands afterward. Do this 2-3 times a day, ideally after brushing your teeth and before meals. When you start using Eucalyptus globulus cream regularly, you may notice a decrease in gum pain and inflammation within a few days. Your gums may also become less sensitive to hot and cold temperatures, and you may be able to eat and drink without feeling discomfort.

However, if your gum pain is severe or persists, you should consult a dentist to rule out any underlying conditions that may require medical attention.

Additionally, the cream has antimicrobial properties that help to prevent the growth of bacteria that can cause gum infections. If you're experiencing gum pain, you can take the following actions: apply a thin layer of Aloe barbadensis cream directly to the affected area, gently massaging it into your gums. You can do this 2-3 times a day, or as needed.

It's also essential to maintain good oral hygiene by brushing and flossing regularly, and visiting your dentist for check-ups and cleanings. When you apply the cream, you may start to feel a cooling sensation as it penetrates the affected area. This is because the aloe vera gel helps to reduce inflammation and ease pain. You may also notice a reduction in swelling and bleeding. As the cream continues to work, you may experience a significant decrease in gum pain and discomfort.

However, it's essential to note that if your gum pain persists or worsens, you should consult your dentist to rule out any underlying conditions that may require medical attention.

Echinacea purpurea creams contains compounds like alkylamides and caffeic acid that may help reduce gum pain.

Curcuma longa creams contain a powerful compound called curcumin, which has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

6. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis creams contains compounds like thujone and camphor, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

To use Salvia officinalis creams for gum pain, apply a small amount to the affected area with a clean finger or cotton swab.

Gently rub it in until it's fully absorbed, making sure not to touch other parts of your mouth or face. Applying Salvia officinalis creams may lead to a reduction in gum pain and inflammation, and an improvement in overall gum health. You may notice the pain subsiding within a few minutes, and your gums feeling softer and less swollen.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent gum pain from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent gum pain from forming.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe and calm the gums. This can help reduce swelling and discomfort.

Regular consumption of these teas may also help maintain healthy gums and prevent pain from occurring.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for gum pain every day?

While herbal teas can be a soothing remedy for gum pain, consuming them daily may not be entirely safe.

Some herbs can be too potent and interact with other medications, or even worsen certain conditions.

Moderation is key, so it's best to limit your intake and choose gentle, well-known herbs like peppermint or chamomile.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in gum pain?

Herbal teas can help with gum pain, but it's hard to predict exactly when you'll notice a difference.

Some people feel better within a few days, while others may take a week or two.

It's also possible that it might take longer, so be patient and keep drinking the tea as directed.

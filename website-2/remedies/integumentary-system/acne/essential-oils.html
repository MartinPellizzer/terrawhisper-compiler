11 Herbal Essential Oils For Acne

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its major bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help to reduce redness and prevent the growth of bacteria that cause acne. The antiseptic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help to prevent infection and promote a healthy healing process in the skin. Additionally, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils have a calming effect on the skin, reducing stress and anxiety that can trigger acne breakouts.

By reducing inflammation, preventing infection, and promoting a healthy healing process, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to control acne and promote clear skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Lavandula angustifolia flowers, a clean glass jar, and a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil.
  2. Combine 1 cup of Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the Lavandula angustifolia essential oil in a dark glass bottle, using 5-7 drops per application to help with acne.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains a high concentration of cineole, a key bioactive constituent that helps fight acne.

Cineole has antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties that combat the bacteria that cause acne, reducing redness and swelling. The oil's antimicrobial properties also prevent the growth of bacteria, which can clog pores and lead to breakouts. Terpinen-4-ol, another active constituent in Melaleuca alternifolia, has antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties that soothe and calm the skin, reducing acne severity.

By targeting the root causes of acne, Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils helps to clear pores and promote healthy skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and add the leaves.
  3. Steep the mixture for 30 minutes, then strain it through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil) to the liquid and mix well.
  5. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use 2-3 drops per application for acne treatment.

3. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol and carvacrol, two active constituents that help with acne.

Thymol has antibacterial properties that reduce the growth of Propionibacterium acnes, a bacteria that causes acne. Carvacrol is an anti-inflammatory that reduces redness and swelling associated with acne. Thymus vulgaris essential oils also has antiseptic properties that prevent infections and promote wound healing.

This combination of properties makes it effective in treating acne by reducing bacteria, inflammation, and promoting healing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves. Clean them with water.
  2. Use a juicer or blender to extract the oil from the leaves. Collect 1 cup of the oil.
  3. Transfer the oil to a saucepan and heat it at 100-150°F (38-65°C) for 2 hours.
  4. Let the oil cool. Then, strain it through a cheesecloth into a clean container.
  5. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use 2-3 drops on affected skin areas to help with acne.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains acemannan, aloin, and aloe-emodin.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiseptic properties. Acemannan soothes and calms the skin, reducing redness and inflammation associated with acne. Aloe-emodin and aloin have antibacterial properties, helping to combat the bacteria that cause acne, such as Propionibacterium acnes.

By reducing inflammation and killing bacteria, Aloe barbadensis essential oils can help to control and prevent acne breakouts.

  1. Harvest 2 cups of Aloe barbadensis leaves from mature plants. Wash the leaves with 1 cup of water to remove dirt.
  2. Dry the Aloe leaves in a warm place for 2-3 days, or until they become soft and pliable.
  3. Grind 1 cup of the dried Aloe leaves into a fine paste using a blender or grinder.
  4. Mix 1 tablespoon of the Aloe paste with 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) in a bowl. Heat the mixture in a double boiler for 30 minutes, then let it cool.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean bottle. Discard the solids and store the Aloe essential oil in a cool, dark place.

5. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, geraniol, and linalool as its active constituents.

These compounds possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help combat acne-causing bacteria and reduce redness and swelling. The antiseptic properties of limonene and the anti-inflammatory properties of geraniol and linalool work together to reduce inflammation and prevent infection, making it an effective treatment for acne-prone skin. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of these compounds help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting healthy skin cell growth and regeneration.

The antiseptic properties of Cymbopogon citratus essential oils also help to dry out pimples and reduce the appearance of acne.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves, 1 cup of water, and a glass jar for the distillation process.
  2. Clean the leaves and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Add 1 cup of water to the jar and cover it with a lid.
  4. Heat the mixture in a double boiler or a steam distillation apparatus for 30-40 minutes, or until the water starts to boil.
  5. Collect the essential oil that forms on the surface of the water and store it in a dark glass bottle for later use.

6. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help reduce redness and prevent infection in acne-prone skin. The triterpenoid saponins, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria that can cause acne, such as Propionibacterium acnes. The flavonoids and carotenoids in Calendula officinalis essential oils also have antioxidant properties, which help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals and promote wound healing.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infection, Calendula officinalis essential oils can help to clear up acne and promote healthy, clear skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula flowers and 2 cups of carrier oil, such as jojoba or sweet almond oil.
  2. Combine the Calendula flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  3. After the infusion period, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the infused oil to a small saucepan and heat it gently over low heat (around 100°F to 150°F).
  5. Remove the saucepan from heat and let the oil cool. Once cooled, filter the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter again to get your Calendula essential oil.

7. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole, limonene, and alpha-pinene as its primary bioactive constituents.

Cineole helps to reduce inflammation and fight bacteria that cause acne. Limonene has natural astringent and antiseptic properties that help to dry out and kill acne-causing bacteria. Alpha-pinene has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce redness and swelling associated with acne.

By reducing inflammation and fighting bacteria, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help to clear up acne-prone skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat for 10-15 minutes, then add the Eucalyptus leaves.
  3. Continue heating for another 30 minutes, then remove from heat and let steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a glass bottle and store in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the essential oil in your acne treatment routine.

8. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains a compound called camphor, which has antiseptic properties that help prevent bacterial infections that cause acne.

The oil also contains borneol, a compound that has anti-inflammatory properties, reducing redness and swelling associated with acne. Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils also contains eucalyptol, a compound that helps to reduce the production of sebum, an oily substance that can clog pores and lead to acne. Additionally, the oil contains linalool, a compound that has antifungal properties, preventing fungal infections that can contribute to acne.

By reducing inflammation, preventing bacterial and fungal infections, and regulating sebum production, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help to alleviate acne symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosemary leaves (Rosmarinus officinalis) and 2 cups of water.
  2. Combine the leaves and water in a saucepan and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Use a cheesecloth or a coffee filter to strain the mixture into a bowl.
  5. Transfer the strained liquid to a glass bottle and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks. This will allow the mixture to separate and the essential oil to rise to the top. Use a dropper to carefully collect the top layer and store it in a small glass bottle.

9. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains active constituents like thujone and camphor, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce the bacteria that cause acne, and also calm down the inflammation and redness associated with breakouts. The oil's antiseptic properties prevent bacterial infections and promote healthy skin, which is essential for managing acne. Additionally, the oil's antioxidant properties help to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to acne.

By balancing the skin's natural pH and reducing inflammation, Salvia officinalis essential oils can help to clear up acne-prone skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Salvia officinalis leaves and 4 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba oil or sweet almond oil)
  2. Combine 1 cup of dried Salvia officinalis leaves with 4 cups of carrier oil in a clean glass jar
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking it daily to help the oil extract from the leaves
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar
  5. Discard the dried leaves and use the extracted oil for acne treatment, but always do a patch test before applying it to your skin

10. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce redness and swelling associated with acne. The oils also contain zingerone, a compound that has antimicrobial properties, which helps kill bacteria that cause acne.

The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of gingerol and zingerone can help soothe and calm the skin, reducing the severity of acne.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Ginger roots and 1 cup of water. Chop the ginger roots into small pieces.
  2. Combine the chopped ginger and water in a saucepan. Bring the mixture to a boil over high heat.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 1 hour. This will help release the essential oils from the ginger.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the liquid cool and then transfer it to a dark glass bottle. Store it in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.

11. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce redness and swelling associated with acne. The alkylamides also have antimicrobial properties that inhibit the growth of bacteria that can cause acne, such as Propionibacterium acnes. Additionally, the antioxidants in Echinacea purpurea essential oils, including caffeic acid and rosmarinic acid, help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to acne.

This combination of properties makes Echinacea purpurea essential oils a potential natural remedy for acne-prone skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Combine the dried flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well to mix.
  3. Store the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the flowers to infuse into the oil.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the resulting essential oil in small dark glass bottles and store in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops per application to treat acne.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in acne?

Herbal teas can help with acne, but it's not a quick fix.

The teas work by reducing inflammation and fighting bacteria that cause acne, so patience is key.

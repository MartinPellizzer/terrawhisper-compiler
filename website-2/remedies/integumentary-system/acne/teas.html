9 Natural Remedies of Herbal Teas for Pregnant Women with Acne

During pregnancy, many women experience hormonal changes that can lead to acne.

This can be frustrating and affect their self-esteem, causing them to feel self-conscious about their skin. Pregnancy acne can also be painful and uncomfortable, making daily activities like going out in public or even just looking in the mirror difficult. For some women, it can be a constant reminder of their pregnancy, making it harder to enjoy this special time. As their skin becomes more sensitive, what used to work to clear their skin may no longer be effective, and they may be at a loss for what to do.

This is where herbal teas come in. They can provide a natural and gentle way to help reduce acne and soothe the skin. Some herbal teas have anti-inflammatory properties that can help calm redness and irritation. Others may help balance hormones, which can contribute to acne.

By drinking these teas, pregnant women may be able to find some relief from their acne and improve their skin's appearance.

1. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis teas, also known as lemon balm tea, have been used for centuries to help alleviate acne symptoms.

The tea contains compounds like rosmarinic acid and caffeic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help reduce redness and prevent bacterial growth. These properties also help to reduce sebum production, which can clog pores and lead to acne. If you're considering trying Melissa officinalis teas for acne, here's what you can do: start by drinking a cup of the tea twice a day, either hot or cold, depending on your preference. You can also add it to your bath water for a relaxing and acne-fighting soak.

It's essential to note that Melissa officinalis teas may not completely eliminate acne, but it can help reduce its severity and frequency. When you start drinking Melissa officinalis teas, you may notice a reduction in acne symptoms within a few days to a week. The tea may help to calm redness and inflammation, making your skin look clearer and more even-toned. However, it's also possible that you may experience some initial breakout, as your skin purifies and cleanses itself.

Stick to your routine, and with consistent use, you may start to see long-term improvements in your acne.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas have been traditionally used to help with acne due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The teas contain flavonoids, triterpenoids, and carotenoids, which are compounds that have been shown to reduce redness and swelling associated with acne. The antimicrobial properties of the tea's constituents, such as saponins, can help to eliminate bacteria that cause acne. If you're considering using Calendula officinalis teas for acne, start by making a strong tea by steeping a teaspoon of dried Calendula flowers in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it to the affected area with a cotton ball.

You can also drink the tea as a way to get the benefits systemically. When using Calendula officinalis teas for acne, you can expect to see a reduction in redness and inflammation within a few days to a week. The tea's antimicrobial properties may help to clear up existing pimples and prevent new ones from forming. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties may help to reduce scarring and promote healing.

However, it's essential to note that everyone's skin is different, and the effectiveness of Calendula officinalis teas may vary from person to person.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas, also known as English lavender tea, has been traditionally used to help with acne due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The tea contains flavonoids, such as luteolin and apigenin, which have been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. It also contains terpenes like linalool and linalyl acetate, which have antimicrobial properties that can help reduce bacteria on the skin, a common cause of acne. To use Lavandula angustifolia teas for acne, steep 1 teaspoon of dried lavender flowers in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain and let the tea cool before applying it to the affected area as a compress or using it as a face wash. You can also drink the tea, 1 cup a day, to help reduce inflammation and promote relaxation, which can help reduce stress-related acne. When using Lavandula angustifolia teas for acne, you can expect to see a reduction in inflammation and redness, as well as a decrease in the number and severity of acne breakouts. The tea may also help to soothe and calm the skin, reducing the appearance of acne scars and promoting a clearer complexion.

However, it's essential to note that Lavandula angustifolia teas may not completely eliminate acne, but rather help to control and manage its symptoms.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas have been found to be beneficial in reducing acne due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The tea contains constituents such as carnosic acid and rosmarinic acid, which are known to reduce inflammation and combat bacterial growth that can cause acne. By reducing inflammation and killing bacteria, Rosmarinus officinalis teas can help prevent clogged pores and prevent breakouts. If you're considering trying Rosmarinus officinalis teas for acne, start by consulting with a healthcare professional to ensure it won't interact with any medications you're already taking. To make the tea, steep one teaspoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves in one cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Drink the tea two to three times a day to reap its benefits. You can also use the tea as a toner by applying it to your skin after cleansing to help reduce inflammation and prevent breakouts. As you drink Rosmarinus officinalis tea regularly, you may start to notice a reduction in acne breakouts and inflammation. Your skin may become less red and irritated, and you may experience a decrease in the size and severity of acne lesions. Additionally, the antimicrobial properties of the tea may help prevent future breakouts by killing bacteria that can cause acne.

However, it's essential to note that individual results may vary, and it may take some time to notice the benefits of Rosmarinus officinalis tea for acne.

5. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas have been used for centuries to help with acne, and the reason is due to its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

The tea contains a compound called aloin, which has been shown to reduce redness and swelling in the skin, while also killing bacteria that can cause acne. Additionally, aloe vera contains vitamin A and C, as well as other antioxidants that help to soothe and calm the skin. To use aloe barbadensis tea for acne, you can drink 1-2 cups a day, or apply it topically by steeping a tea bag in hot water, letting it cool, and then applying it to the affected area with a cotton swab.

Be sure to do a patch test before using it on your face to ensure you don't have any sensitivity to the tea. If you do experience any irritation, stop using the tea immediately. When you start using aloe barbadensis tea, you may notice a reduction in acne within a few days to a week, as the antibacterial properties help to clear up pimples and reduce inflammation.

However, it's also possible that it may take a few weeks to see the full effects, as the tea helps to balance your skin's natural oil production and reduce the occurrence of acne in the long term.

6. Aloe vera

Aloe vera teas can be a helpful addition to your skincare routine if you're struggling with acne.

This is because aloe vera contains properties like vitamins A, C, and E, as well as fatty acids and antioxidants that can help soothe and calm the skin. One of its key constituents is aloin, a compound that has anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce redness and swelling associated with acne. When you drink aloe vera tea, these beneficial compounds are absorbed into your body, where they can help to regulate the body's natural response to acne-causing bacteria and reduce inflammation. To try aloe vera tea for acne, start by steeping a teaspoon of dried aloe vera leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the mixture and drink it once or twice a day. You can also add honey or lemon juice to taste. Some people find it helpful to drink aloe vera tea regularly for a few weeks to see noticeable improvements in their skin. As you start drinking aloe vera tea, you may notice a reduction in the frequency and severity of your breakouts, as well as a decrease in redness and inflammation. Your skin may also start to look clearer and more even-toned over time.

However, keep in mind that aloe vera tea may not completely eliminate acne, and it's still important to follow a consistent skincare routine and use gentle products that are suitable for your skin type.

7. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis teas, also known as green tea, black tea, or oolong tea, have been shown to help with acne due to their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The main constituents responsible for these effects are catechins, particularly epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), and theaflavins. These compounds help reduce inflammation and prevent the growth of bacteria that can cause acne, such as Propionibacterium acnes (P. acnes). The antioxidants in Camellia sinensis teas also help protect the skin from environmental stressors and damage caused by free radicals, which can lead to acne. To incorporate Camellia sinensis teas into your acne-fighting routine, try drinking 1-2 cups of brewed tea per day. You can also use tea bags as a compress to apply the cooled tea directly to individual pimples or affected areas.

Some studies suggest that applying tea directly to the skin may be more effective than drinking it, so don't be afraid to get creative. Look for tea products that are low in added sugars and artificial flavorings, and consider using loose-leaf tea for better quality and flavor. As you start incorporating Camellia sinensis teas into your daily routine, you may notice a reduction in inflammation and a decrease in the severity of your acne. Your skin may appear clearer and more even-toned, and you may experience fewer breakouts. You may also notice improvements in skin texture and tone, with a reduction in fine lines and wrinkles. However, keep in mind that everyone's skin is different, and it may take some time to notice the full effects of Camellia sinensis teas on your acne.

Be patient, and remember to combine tea consumption with a healthy diet and regular skincare routine for optimal results.

8. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum teas, also known as milk thistle tea, may help with acne due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The tea contains silymarin, a flavonoid complex that has been shown to reduce inflammation and protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals. Silymarin also has antimicrobial properties, which can help control bacteria that can cause acne. To try Silybum marianum tea for acne, steep one teaspoon of dried milk thistle flowers in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain and drink 2-3 cups a day.

You can also add honey or lemon to taste. It's essential to note that Silybum marianum tea may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, so consult with a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you're already taking medications. Drinking Silybum marianum tea regularly may lead to a reduction in acne symptoms, such as redness and inflammation. You may also notice an improvement in skin texture and a decrease in the appearance of acne scars. However, the effectiveness of Silybum marianum tea for acne may vary depending on individual skin types and conditions.

Some people may experience no change or even worsened symptoms.

9. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas, also known as thyme tea, have been traditionally used to help with acne due to its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The main constituents of thyme, such as thymol and carvacrol, have been shown to reduce the growth of bacteria that can cause acne, including Propionibacterium acnes (P. acnes). Thymol, in particular, has been found to have a strong antiseptic effect, which can help to kill bacteria and reduce inflammation. To use thymus vulgaris teas for acne, start by steeping a teaspoon of dried thyme in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it to the affected area as a compress or using it as a face mask.

For internal use, drink 1-2 cups of thyme tea per day, ideally after meals. As you take these actions, you can likely expect a reduction in the severity of your acne symptoms, including the size and redness of pimples. The antibacterial properties of thyme may also help to prevent the formation of new pimples. However, it's essential to note that thyme tea may not completely eliminate acne, but it can help to manage its symptoms and reduce its occurrence.

To get the best results, combine thyme tea with a balanced diet and a consistent skincare routine.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent acne from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent acne from forming.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce redness and swelling. Green tea, rich in antioxidants, may also help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals.

This may help prevent acne from forming.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for acne every day?

Herbal teas can help with acne, but it's essential to be cautious about consuming them every day.

Some teas, like those with high levels of tannins, can dry out your skin and make it worse.

However, teas like peppermint, chamomile, and green tea are generally considered safe and may even help soothe and calm your skin.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in acne?

Herbal teas can help with acne, but it takes time to see results.

Some people start to notice improvements after a few weeks of regular consumption, while others may take a few months.

The exact time frame depends on individual factors, such as the severity of acne and the type of tea being consumed.

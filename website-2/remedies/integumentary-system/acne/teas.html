9 Herbal Teas For Acne

During pregnancy, many women experience hormonal changes that can lead to acne.

This can be frustrating and affect their self-esteem, causing them to feel self-conscious about their skin. Pregnancy acne can also be painful and uncomfortable, making daily activities like going out in public or even just looking in the mirror difficult. For some women, it can be a constant reminder of their pregnancy, making it harder to enjoy this special time. As their skin becomes more sensitive, what used to work to clear their skin may no longer be effective, and they may be at a loss for what to do.

This is where herbal teas come in. They can provide a natural and gentle way to help reduce acne and soothe the skin. Some herbal teas have anti-inflammatory properties that can help calm redness and irritation. Others may help balance hormones, which can contribute to acne.

By drinking these teas, pregnant women may be able to find some relief from their acne and improve their skin's appearance.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas contains flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids that work together to help with acne.

The flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce redness and swelling associated with acne. The saponins, like calendulin and calendulic acid, have antimicrobial properties that help control the growth of bacteria that can cause acne, such as Propionibacterium acnes (P. acnes). These constituents also help to reduce the production of sebum, an oily substance that can clog pores and lead to acne. To try calendula officinalis tea for acne, start by brewing one teaspoon of dried calendula flowers in one cup of boiling water for five to seven minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it to the affected area with a cotton ball.

You can also drink the tea as a supplement to help from the inside out. Some people find it helpful to do this twice a day, once in the morning and once in the evening, to see the best results. If you use calendula officinalis tea consistently, you may notice a reduction in the severity and frequency of acne breakouts. The tea may also help to reduce redness and inflammation, and promote the healing of acne lesions. However, it's essential to note that everyone's skin is different, and what works for one person may not work for another. It's also possible that you may need to combine calendula officinalis tea with other treatments, such as topical creams or prescription medications, to see the best results.

Be patient and give your skin time to respond to the treatment.

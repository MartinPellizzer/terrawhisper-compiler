10 Teas for Athletes to Overcome Jock Itch

Athletes are prone to jock itch due to the moisture and friction in their genital area, especially after intense workouts or sports activities.

This fungal infection can cause redness, itching, and burning sensations, making it uncomfortable and embarrassing for athletes. It can also affect their performance and mental well-being, as they may feel self-conscious about their appearance. Some athletes may experience skin irritation, blisters, or even cracked skin, which can be painful and take time to heal. This can be particularly challenging for athletes who engage in high-intensity sports or have to wear tight-fitting clothing. Jock itch can also disrupt an athlete's daily routine, forcing them to miss training sessions or competitions.

It can be a source of frustration and stress, especially when they're trying to stay focused on their performance. Furthermore, jock itch can be a recurring issue, making it essential for athletes to find effective and reliable remedies. For athletes struggling with jock itch, herbal teas can be a natural and soothing solution. These teas are made from specific plants that have been traditionally used to combat fungal infections and promote skin health. They work by reducing inflammation, killing fungi, and promoting the growth of healthy skin cells.

By incorporating herbal teas into their daily routine, athletes can help alleviate symptoms, speed up the healing process, and regain their confidence and comfort.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas, made from the flowers of the marigold plant, may help soothe and treat jock itch due to its anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties.

The tea contains constituents like triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which have been shown to inhibit the growth of fungal infections, such as those caused by Trichophyton rubrum, a common culprit behind jock itch. To use Calendula officinalis teas for jock itch, you can steep 1 teaspoon of dried flowers in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and apply it to the affected area using a cotton ball.

You can also soak a cloth in the tea and apply it to the area for 30 minutes to an hour, 2-3 times a day. As you start using Calendula officinalis teas, you may notice a reduction in redness, itching, and inflammation within a few days to a week. The tea may help to dry out the fungal infection, making it easier to treat. However, it's essential to note that Calendula officinalis teas may not be strong enough to completely cure jock itch on its own, and you may need to combine it with other treatments, such as antifungal creams or oral medications, as directed by a healthcare professional.

Additionally, if your symptoms worsen or do not improve within a week, you should consult a doctor for further guidance.

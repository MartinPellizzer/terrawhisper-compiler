Causes and Herbal Solutions for Oily Hair

What are the causes of oily hair?

Oily hair can be a real nuisance, and there are several reasons why it happens.

First, hormonal imbalance can cause oily hair. This is because hormonal changes can affect the sebaceous glands, which produce oil in the hair follicles. When these glands become overactive, they produce too much oil, leading to greasy hair. Another reason is genetics. If your parents had oily hair, you're more likely to have it too. This is because genetics can determine the size and activity of your sebaceous glands. If you inherit bigger or more active glands, you'll likely have oilier hair.

The size and activity of your sebaceous glands can also be influenced by overactive sebaceous glands. When these glands are too active, they produce too much oil, causing your hair to become greasy. This can be due to various factors, including genetics and hormonal changes. Your diet can also play a role in oily hair. Eating foods high in sugar, salt, and unhealthy fats can cause your body to produce more oil. This is because these foods can trigger inflammation, which can lead to overactive sebaceous glands. Finally, stress can also contribute to oily hair. When you're stressed, your body produces more cortisol, a hormone that can stimulate the sebaceous glands to produce more oil.

This can lead to greasy hair, especially if you're already prone to oily hair.

What Are The Main Causes Of Oily Hair?

Oily hair can be caused by genetics.

If your parents or grandparents have oily hair, you might have it too. This is because genes can influence the amount of oil produced by the scalp. Another cause is hormonal imbalance. Hormones help control the production of sebum, an oily substance.

During puberty, the rise of hormones can lead to oily hair. Similarly, hormonal changes during pregnancy or menopause can cause oily hair. The overactive sebum glands are also a main cause. Sebum glands are tiny organs in the scalp that produce sebum. When these glands produce too much sebum, it can lead to oily hair.

This can be due to genetics, hormonal changes, or even using the wrong hair products that can clog pores and make sebum glands produce more sebum.

Below are the main causes of oily hair.

  • Genetics: Genetics plays a role in oily hair because people inherit their hair's oil-producing genes from their parents, which determines how much oil their scalp produces.
  • Hormonal Imbalance: Hormonal imbalance can cause oily hair because the imbalance leads to the overproduction of sebum, an oily substance produced by the scalp's glands that can make hair look greasy and weighed down.
  • Overactive Sebum Glands: Overactive sebum glands produce too much oil, which is then released onto the scalp and hair, making it oily and weighed down.
  • Poor Diet: A poor diet that lacks essential nutrients can cause oily hair because it leads to an imbalance in hormones, particularly androgens, which stimulate the oil glands in the scalp to produce excess sebum.
  • Stress: When we're stressed, our body produces more hormones like cortisol, which travel to our scalp and cause our oil glands to work overtime, resulting in oily hair.
  • Insufficient Sleep: When we don't get enough sleep, our body's natural balance is disrupted, leading to an increase in stress hormones like cortisol, which can cause our scalp to produce more oil, resulting in oily hair.
  • Medications: Some medications, such as birth control pills and certain acne treatments, can cause oily hair because they affect hormone levels in the body, which can lead to an increase in oil production in the scalp.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Oily Hair?

If you have oily hair, there are some herbs to use with caution.

Sage (Salvia officinalis) can be drying, but in excess it can cause more oil production. Thyme (Thymus vulgaris) is good for oily hair, but it can be drying, so use it carefully. Hyssop (Hyssopus officinalis) and Pennyroyal (Mentha pulegium) are both known to stimulate the scalp, which can increase oil production.

Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) can also stimulate the scalp, so use it sparingly. Calendula (Calendula officinalis) can cause irritation, especially if you have sensitive skin, so use it with caution. Herbs that can cause oiliness include Angelica (Angelica archangelica), with its stimulating properties, and Dill (Anethum graveolens), which can cause an increase in oil production. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) can also cause irritation, which can lead to more oil production.

It's essential to use these herbs in moderation and with caution to avoid exacerbating oily hair.

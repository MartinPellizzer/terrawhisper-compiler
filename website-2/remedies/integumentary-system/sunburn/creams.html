Sunburned Surfers, Meet 10 Natural Creams

Surfing can be a thrilling experience, but it also puts you at risk of sunburn.

When you spend time in the ocean, you're exposed to direct sunlight, which can cause damage to your skin. This is especially true for surfers who spend hours in the water, often without protection. Sunburn can be painful and uncomfortable, and it can also increase your risk of skin cancer. For surfers, sunburn can make it hard to paddle, catch waves, and even enjoy the water. It can also cause blisters, redness, and swelling, making it hard to move and perform daily activities. Sunburn can affect surfers' lives in many ways.

Some may have to take time off from the water, which can be frustrating for those who love surfing. Others may experience pain and discomfort, making it hard to enjoy their favorite activities. In severe cases, sunburn can lead to infections, scarring, and long-term skin damage. Many surfers turn to herbal creams to help soothe and heal sunburned skin. These creams work by reducing inflammation, promoting skin repair, and providing long-lasting relief from pain and discomfort. They're often gentle and non-irritating, making them a popular choice among surfers who need a quick fix for their sunburned skin.

These creams can be applied directly to affected areas, providing fast and effective relief from sunburn symptoms.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams are a popular natural remedy for soothing sunburned skin.

This is because they contain properties and constituents like triterpene saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. When you apply a Calendula officinalis cream to sunburned skin, the triterpene saponins help to reduce redness and swelling, while the flavonoids and carotenoids neutralize free radicals and promote collagen production. This leads to a reduction in pain and discomfort, as well as faster healing. If you have a sunburn, here's what you can do: apply a thick layer of Calendula officinalis cream to the affected area, and gently massage it in until it's fully absorbed.

You can do this 2-3 times a day, or as needed. Be sure to choose a cream that's fragrance-free and hypoallergenic to avoid any irritation. Additionally, you can take cool baths or showers to help reduce heat and ease discomfort. As you continue to apply the Calendula officinalis cream, you may start to notice a decrease in redness and swelling, as well as a reduction in pain and discomfort. You may also find that your skin is less itchy and sensitive, and that it's healing faster than it would without the cream.

In some cases, you may even notice a slight improvement in the texture and appearance of your skin, as the antioxidants and collagen-promoting properties work to repair and rejuvenate damaged skin cells.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams have been widely used to help with sunburn relief.

The gel of the Aloe vera plant contains properties like vitamin E, vitamin C, and beta-carotene that help reduce inflammation and promote healing. The anti-inflammatory properties of Aloe barbadensis creams, particularly the presence of aloin and aloe-emodin, work together to soothe sunburned skin and reduce redness. Applying Aloe barbadensis creams to sunburned skin can help reduce pain and discomfort. When using Aloe barbadensis creams for sunburn, it's essential to follow some simple steps. First, clean the affected area gently with cool water to remove any dirt or debris. Next, apply a thick layer of Aloe barbadensis cream to the sunburned skin, making sure to cover the entire area.

Leave the cream on for at least 30 minutes to an hour before rinsing it off with cool water. Repeat this process a few times a day for optimal results. Avoid using harsh soaps or exfoliating products on sunburned skin, as this can further irritate the area. By taking these steps and applying Aloe barbadensis creams regularly, you can expect to see a significant reduction in sunburn pain and discomfort. The inflammation and redness associated with sunburn should start to subside, and the skin should begin to feel cooler and more comfortable. However, it's essential to note that Aloe barbadensis creams are not a substitute for medical treatment.

If your sunburn is severe or accompanied by blisters, fever, or chills, seek medical attention immediately.

What herbal teas you should avoid if you have sunburn?

If you have sunburn, it's best to avoid herbal teas that can make it worse or cause more irritation.

One such tea is Peppermint (Mentha piperita). The cooling sensation of peppermint might feel nice, but it can actually increase blood flow to the skin, which may make your sunburn feel worse.

Ginger (Zingiber officinale) tea is another one to avoid, as its anti-inflammatory properties can interact with the skin's natural inflammatory response and potentially make your sunburn more painful. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) tea, while often used for calming, can cause allergic reactions in some people, which may worsen sunburn symptoms. Also, avoid Sage (Salvia officinalis) tea, as it can dry out the skin further, making your sunburn even more uncomfortable.

Finally, Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) tea is not recommended, as it can increase fluid retention and make your skin look more inflamed.

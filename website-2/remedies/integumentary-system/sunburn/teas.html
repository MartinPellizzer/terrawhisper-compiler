Skiers' Guide to Beating Sunburn with 8 Herbal Teas

As a skier, you're exposed to the sun's strong rays, especially when you're at high altitudes or skiing at midday.

This can lead to painful sunburns that make everyday activities a challenge. The discomfort and itchiness can be unbearable, forcing you to take time off from skiing and other outdoor activities. You might even experience blisters, swelling, and in severe cases, skin peeling. Sunburns can also increase the risk of skin cancer, making it essential to take preventive measures and find effective treatments.

When you're suffering from sunburn, you might try using creams and lotions to soothe the affected skin. However, these products can only provide temporary relief and don't address the underlying issue. Moreover, some people may be allergic to certain ingredients in these products, making them less effective or even causing more harm. Herbal teas can be a natural and effective way to treat sunburn. These teas are made from plants that have anti-inflammatory and cooling properties, which can help reduce the redness, swelling, and pain associated with sunburns.

By drinking these teas, you can promote skin healing, reduce the risk of infection, and even help prevent future sunburns.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas have been used for centuries to soothe sunburned skin.

The reason they work is due to the properties and constituents found in Aloe barbadensis, such as glycoproteins, vitamins A, C, and E, and beta-carotene. These compounds help to reduce inflammation, promote healing, and provide antioxidant protection. When applied topically, Aloe barbadensis can ease pain, swelling, and redness, while also helping to prevent blistering and peeling. To use Aloe barbadensis teas for sunburn, make a strong tea by steeping the leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then let it cool.

Soak a clean cloth in the tea and apply it directly to the affected area. Leave it on for 15-20 minutes before rinsing with cold water. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day. As you take action, you can expect the redness and swelling to reduce, the pain to subside, and the skin to feel cooler and more comfortable.

The tea may also help to speed up the healing process, allowing your skin to return to its normal texture and color more quickly.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas, made from the flowers of the marigold plant, have been used for centuries to soothe sunburned skin.

The teas contain flavonoids, triterpenoids, and carotenoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These properties help to reduce redness, swelling, and pain associated with sunburn. The flavonoids in calendula also have a protective effect on the skin, promoting healing and reducing the risk of infection. If you have sunburn, you can try making a calendula tea by steeping dried calendula flowers in boiling water.

Let the tea cool, then apply it to the affected area using a compress or a cotton pad. You can also add a few drops of calendula tea to a cool bath for a soothing soak. When you take action, you may experience a reduction in pain and discomfort, as well as a decrease in redness and swelling. The tea may also help to speed up the healing process, reducing the risk of peeling and blistering.

However, it's essential to note that calendula tea is not a substitute for medical treatment, and you should still seek medical attention if your sunburn is severe or you experience any signs of infection, such as increased redness, pus, or a fever.

3. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum teas, also known as St. John's Wort tea, has been used for centuries to help soothe sunburned skin.

It contains properties and constituents like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds help reduce redness, swelling, and pain caused by sunburn, while also promoting skin healing and protecting it from further damage. To use Hypericum perforatum teas for sunburn, make a strong tea by steeping one teaspoon of dried St. John's Wort flowers in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain and let it cool before applying it to the affected area with a clean cloth. You can also add a few drops of aloe vera gel or coconut oil to enhance its soothing effect.

It's essential to note that while St. John's Wort tea can provide relief, it's not a substitute for medical treatment. If your sunburn is severe or accompanied by blisters, fever, or chills, seek medical attention immediately. When using Hypericum perforatum teas for sunburn, you may experience a decrease in pain and discomfort, as well as a reduction in redness and swelling. The tea may also promote skin healing, reducing the risk of infection and scarring. However, some people may experience an allergic reaction or interact with other medications, so it's crucial to be cautious and monitor your body's response.

Additionally, if you're taking any prescription medications or have a pre-existing medical condition, consult with a healthcare professional before using St. John's Wort tea.

4. Hamamelis virginiana

Hamamelis virginiana teas are a natural remedy for sunburn relief.

The tea contains compounds like astringent tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which help reduce inflammation and soothe irritated skin. The astringent properties in the tea constrict blood vessels, which in turn reduces the amount of blood flowing to the affected area, thereby decreasing redness and swelling. Flavonoids, on the other hand, have antioxidant properties that protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting healing and reducing the risk of infection. To use Hamamelis virginiana tea for sunburn, you can make a cold compress by steeping a tea bag in cold water and applying it to the affected area for 10-15 minutes, several times a day.

Alternatively, you can add the tea to your bath water for a relaxing soak. It's essential to note that these teas should be used in conjunction with other sunburn remedies, such as moisturizing and taking cool showers or baths. When you apply Hamamelis virginiana tea to sunburned skin, you can expect the inflammation to reduce, and the redness to fade. The tea may also help to calm any discomfort or itching associated with sunburn. However, keep in mind that these teas are not a substitute for medical treatment and should not be relied upon as the sole means of sunburn relief.

If you experience severe sunburn, blisters, or other concerning symptoms, it's crucial to seek medical attention.

You can also drink the tea as a way to boost your body's natural healing process from the inside out. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent sunburn from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent sunburn, but it's not a foolproof way to avoid it.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, contain antioxidants that can help protect your skin from damage. These teas may also help soothe sunburned skin if you do get burned.

However, don't rely solely on herbal tea for sun protection.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for sunburn every day?

While herbal teas can help soothe sunburn, consuming them daily isn't necessarily safe.

Some herbs can interact with medications or have side effects when taken frequently. For example, peppermint tea can lower blood pressure, and chamomile tea can affect liver function.

It's best to enjoy herbal teas in moderation and for short periods.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in sunburn?

Herbal teas can help with sunburn relief, but how long it takes to show results varies.

Some people experience relief within 24-48 hours after drinking the tea, while others may need a few days.

The tea helps to reduce inflammation and promote skin healing, but individual results depend on the severity of the sunburn and the type of tea consumed.

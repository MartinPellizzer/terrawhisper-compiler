1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

The triterpenoid saponins in calendula have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, helping to reduce redness and prevent infection in sunburned skin. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant properties that neutralize free radicals and promote skin repair. The carotenoids, including lycopene, help protect the skin from further damage and promote collagen synthesis, speeding up the healing process.

By reducing inflammation, preventing infection, and promoting skin repair, calendula officinalis tinctures can help alleviate the symptoms of sunburn.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Calendula officinalis flowers, 1 cup of 80-proof vodka, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Calendula flowers and vodka in the glass jar. Make sure the flowers are completely covered by the vodka.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Calendula tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the tincture in water for sunburn relief.

2. Arnica montana

Arnica montana tinctures contains bioactive constituents like sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and redness, and also protect the skin from further damage. The sesquiterpene lactones, particularly alantolactone and dihydroalantolactone, are known to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to the pain and discomfort associated with sunburn. The flavonoids and phenolic acids in Arnica montana tinctures have antioxidant properties, which help neutralize free radicals and promote skin healing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting skin healing, Arnica montana tinctures can provide relief from sunburn symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Arnica montana flowers and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Pour the vodka over the dried Arnica flowers, making sure they are completely covered.
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the Arnica tincture in a dark glass bottle and use 10-15 drops per application to soothe sunburned skin.

3. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum tinctures contains flavonoids, such as rutin and hyperoside, and phenolic acids, like quercetin and kaempferol.

These bioactive compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce redness and swelling associated with sunburn. The tannins present in Hypericum perforatum tinctures also have a soothing effect on the skin, reducing heat and discomfort caused by sunburn. The flavonoids in the tincture can also help to protect the skin from further damage by neutralizing free radicals and promoting collagen production.

By applying Hypericum perforatum tinctures topically, you can help to calm and soothe sunburned skin, promoting faster recovery and reducing the risk of further damage.

  1. Gather 100g of fresh Hypericum perforatum flowers and leaves. You can use 50% flowers and 50% leaves.
  2. Combine the Hypericum flowers and leaves with 500ml of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar. Make sure the plant material is fully covered by the vodka.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day to help the extraction process.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the plant material.
  5. Label the tincture and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture in water or apply topically to help soothe sunburn.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis tinctures contains aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-juice as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that help to reduce redness and swelling associated with sunburn. Aloe barbadensis tinctures can help to reduce peeling and promote the repair of damaged skin cells, making it a popular natural remedy for sunburn relief.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent sunburn from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help reduce the risk of sunburn, but it's not a guarantee.

Some herbal teas, like those with aloe vera or green tea, contain antioxidants that can protect the skin from damage.

However, it's essential to still use sunscreen and protective clothing to prevent sunburn, as herbal tea is not a substitute for sun protection.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for sunburn every day?

Consume herbal teas for sunburn every day with caution.

Some teas, like peppermint and chamomile, have cooling effects but may not provide long-term relief. Other teas, like aloe vera, have anti-inflammatory properties but might interact with medications.

It's essential to check the ingredients and potential interactions before making it a daily habit.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in sunburn?

Herbal teas can help soothe sunburn, but it takes time to see results.

Some people notice relief in a few hours, while others may feel better within 24 to 48 hours.

The cooling and anti-inflammatory properties in these teas help reduce redness and discomfort, making them a gentle and natural way to ease sunburn symptoms.

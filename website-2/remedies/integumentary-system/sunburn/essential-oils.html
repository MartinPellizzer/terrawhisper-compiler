10 Herbal Essential Oils for Beachgoers with Sunburn

When we go to the beach, we often don't think about the dangers of the sun.

But the truth is, the sun's strong rays can cause sunburn, which can be painful and uncomfortable. Sunburn can make our skin feel like it's on fire, making us feel miserable and unable to enjoy our day at the beach. It can also lead to blisters, peeling, and even scarring if we're not careful. This can affect our mood, our energy levels, and even our ability to do everyday activities. Some people may experience itchy skin, redness, and swelling, which can be embarrassing and make us feel self-conscious. In severe cases, sunburn can even lead to infections and skin damage, which can be serious health issues.

But there's good news for beachgoers who suffer from sunburn. Herbal essential oils can provide relief from the pain and discomfort of sunburn. These oils are extracted from plants and have natural cooling and soothing properties that can help calm down irritated skin. They can also reduce inflammation, ease pain, and promote skin healing. When applied topically, these oils can help to lock in moisture, reduce redness, and promote a healthy, glowing complexion. They can be used individually or blended together to create a customized treatment that suits our needs.

By using herbal essential oils, beachgoers can find natural relief from sunburn and enjoy their time at the beach without the discomfort and pain of sunburn.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils are a natural remedy for sunburn relief.

The oil contains compounds like vitamins A, C, and E, as well as amino acids and fatty acids that help soothe and calm sun-damaged skin. Its anti-inflammatory properties, thanks to the presence of glycoproteins and polysaccharides, reduce redness and swelling, while its moisturizing properties hydrate and protect the skin from further damage. The constituents also promote wound healing and cell regeneration, which aids in the recovery of sunburnt skin. If you've got sunburn, here's what you can do: apply a thin layer of aloe vera gel or oil to the affected area as soon as possible after getting sunburnt. You can also mix it with other natural ingredients like coconut oil or shea butter to enhance its benefits.

Gently massage it into your skin and let it absorb fully. For severe sunburn, you can also take a cool bath or shower to help reduce heat and discomfort. When you apply aloe barbadensis essential oils, you can expect to see a reduction in redness and swelling within a few hours. The pain and discomfort should also start to subside, allowing you to feel more comfortable. In some cases, the oil may also help to prevent blistering and peeling, which can be a result of severe sunburn. However, it's essential to note that aloe vera oil is not a substitute for medical treatment.

If your sunburn is severe, or you experience symptoms like fever, chills, or nausea, seek medical attention immediately.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that make them effective in soothing and calming sunburned skin.

The essential oil contains triterpenoid compounds, such as calendulic acid and isocalendulic acid, which help to reduce redness, swelling, and pain associated with sunburn. It also contains flavonoids, like quercetin, which have antioxidant properties that protect the skin from further damage. To use Calendula officinalis essential oils for sunburn, dilute a few drops of the oil in a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil, and gently massage it onto the affected area. You can also add a few drops of the oil to your bath water for a relaxing soak.

For best results, apply the oil within 24 hours of getting sunburned, and repeat the process as needed. When you apply Calendula officinalis essential oils to your sunburned skin, you can expect the redness and swelling to reduce, and the pain to ease. The oil may also help to prevent peeling and promote faster healing of the skin. As the oil penetrates the skin, it may feel cooler and more comfortable, and you may notice a decrease in the itching and stinging associated with sunburn.

However, keep in mind that the oil may cause some skin irritation, especially if you have sensitive skin, so always do a patch test before using it extensively.

3. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils, also known as peppermint oil, can help with sunburn relief due to its cooling and anti-inflammatory properties.

It contains constituents like menthol, menthone, and limonene, which work together to reduce skin irritation and pain. When applied topically to sunburned skin, peppermint oil can help soothe and calm the affected area, reducing redness and inflammation. To use peppermint oil for sunburn, mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and gently apply it to the affected skin. You can also add it to a cooling bath or compress to help reduce heat and discomfort.

When using peppermint oil for sunburn, it's essential to be gentle and avoid further irritating the skin. Apply the oil mixture or bath compress in moderation, as excessive use can cause skin dryness and irritation. After application, you may notice a decrease in redness and heat, and the affected area may feel cooler and more comfortable. If you experience any increased pain or discomfort, discontinue use and consult a healthcare professional.

It's also crucial to note that peppermint oil can be quite potent, so start with a small amount and gradually increase as needed.

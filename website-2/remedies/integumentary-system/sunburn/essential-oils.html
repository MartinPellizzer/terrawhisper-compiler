1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-annone, which help with sunburn relief.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, reducing redness and swelling caused by sunburn. Aloe-emodin, in particular, has been shown to accelerate wound healing and decrease pain. The essential oils also contain vitamins A, C, and E, which help protect the skin from further damage and promote collagen production.

By applying Aloe barbadensis essential oils to sunburned skin, you can help soothe and calm the affected area, promoting a faster recovery.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Aloe barbadensis leaves, 1 cup of coconut oil, and a cheesecloth.
  2. Wash the Aloe leaves with 1 cup of water and pat them dry.
  3. Blend the Aloe leaves and coconut oil in a blender until smooth.
  4. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth into a bowl, squeezing the cheesecloth to get the liquid.
  5. Store the Aloe oil in a 1/4 cup jar in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, sesquiterpenes, and carotenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to soothe and calm sunburned skin. The sesquiterpenes in calendula oil have been shown to reduce redness and swelling by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. The antioxidants in calendula oil, such as carotenoids, help to neutralize free radicals that can damage skin cells and cause further inflammation.

By reducing inflammation and protecting skin cells, calendula officinalis essential oils can help to promote the healing and recovery of sunburned skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula flowers, 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Calendula flowers and carrier oil in the glass jar, then stir well.
  3. Cover the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After the sitting period, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Calendula oil in a dark glass bottle, ready for use on sunburns.

3. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum essential oils contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to soothe and calm sunburned skin. The antioxidants in Hypericum perforatum essential oils, such as quercetin and kaempferol, neutralize free radicals that can cause further damage to the skin. The anti-inflammatory properties of flavonoids like rutin and hyperoside can reduce redness and swelling associated with sunburn.

By using Hypericum perforatum essential oils topically, you can help to repair and protect your skin from the effects of a sunburn.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum flowers, a glass jar, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Dry the flowers by spreading them on a paper towel for 2-3 days or using a food dehydrator on the lowest temperature setting.
  3. Fill a glass jar 1/4 with the dried flowers, and add 2 cups of carrier oil. Close the jar and shake it gently.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This is called maceration.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another jar, discarding the solids and saving the liquid as your Hypericum perforatum essential oil for sunburn relief.

4. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains powerful antioxidants like cichoric acid, caftaric acid, and chicoric acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce redness and swelling associated with sunburn. The essential oils also contain flavonoids like kaempferol and quercetin, which have potent antioxidant properties that neutralize free radicals caused by UV radiation. The combination of these bioactive constituents in Echinacea purpurea essential oils helps to soothe and calm sunburned skin, promoting a faster recovery.

By applying Echinacea purpurea essential oils topically, you can reduce the discomfort and inflammation of sunburn.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Place the jar in a warm, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. This allows the oil to extract from the flowers.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the extracted oil in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops of the oil per 1 tablespoon of aloe vera gel for sunburn relief.

5. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and linalool.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce redness and swelling associated with sunburn. Cineole, in particular, has been shown to have a soothing effect on the skin, while limonene and linalool have antimicrobial properties that can help prevent infection. The oil's antioxidant properties can also help protect the skin from further damage caused by UV radiation.

By applying Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils to sunburned skin, you may be able to alleviate discomfort and promote healing.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 1 cup of water.
  2. Blend the leaves and water in a large pot, then bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes, allowing the mixture to cool.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a glass container, then let it sit for 2 weeks to allow the oil to separate.
  5. Strain the oil from the liquid and bottle it for use as a sunburn remedy.

6. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum essential oils contains bioactive constituents such as silymarin and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce redness and swelling associated with sunburn. By incorporating Silybum marianum essential oils into your skincare routine, you can harness the healing properties of silymarin and flavonoids to soothe and protect your skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Silybum marianum flowers and leaves. Use fresh or dried flowers and leaves.
  2. Combine 1 cup of Silybum marianum flowers and leaves with 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) in a clean glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. This will help the essential oil infuse into the carrier oil.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the Silybum marianum essential oil in a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use it as needed to soothe sunburned skin.

7. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce redness and swelling associated with sunburn. Linalool also has analgesic properties, providing relief from pain and discomfort caused by sunburn. The antioxidant properties of linalyl acetate help to neutralize free radicals that can cause cell damage and speed up the healing process.

By applying Lavandula angustifolia essential oils to sunburned skin, these properties work together to soothe and calm the affected area.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers, 2 cups of water, and a large pot.
  2. Combine the flowers and water in the pot and heat it on low for 2 hours.
  3. Remove the pot from heat and let it cool for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Use a distillation kit or a steam distiller to extract the essential oil from the liquid, then store it in a cool dark place.

8. Arnica montana

Arnica montana essential oils contains a high concentration of sesquiterpene lactones, specifically helenalin and dihydrohelenalin, which are known for their anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce redness and swelling associated with sunburn. Arnica's sesquiterpene lactones also have antioxidant properties, which help to neutralize free radicals and reduce damage to skin cells caused by UV radiation. By applying Arnica montana essential oils topically, you can harness its natural anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties to help alleviate sunburn symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Arnica montana flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil.
  2. Dry the Arnica flowers in a single layer for 1-2 weeks, or until they are completely dry and crumbly.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent sunburn from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help protect your skin from the sun, but it's not a guaranteed prevention against sunburn.

Some herbal teas, like green tea, contain antioxidants that can help soothe sun-damaged skin and reduce inflammation.

However, they don't contain SPF, so you'll still need to wear sunscreen when going outside.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for sunburn every day?

While herbal teas for sunburn might sound like a soothing solution, consuming them daily isn't necessarily safe.

Some herbs can interact with other medications or have side effects, especially if used excessively.

It's best to drink them in moderation and pay attention to how your body reacts.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in sunburn?

Herbal teas can help with sunburn relief, but it takes time to show results.

Some people notice a difference within 24 to 48 hours after drinking them.

The cooling effect and anti-inflammatory properties in teas like peppermint, chamomile, and aloe vera can start to reduce redness and discomfort.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for sunburn?

For sunburn, it's best to drink herbal tea at night.

Aloe vera tea or chamomile tea can help soothe your skin while you sleep. The cooling effect can reduce redness and discomfort, making it easier to recover from a sunburn.

Drinking it before bed can help your skin feel better in the morning.

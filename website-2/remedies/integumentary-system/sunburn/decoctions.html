What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have sunburn?

When you have sunburn, it's essential to be careful with herbal decoctions to avoid making things worse.

Some herbs can irritate the skin further, making the burn more painful and taking longer to heal. For example, 'Euphorbia' plants, like Euphorbia lactea and Euphorbia tirucalli, contain a compound called sapogenin, which can cause severe skin irritation, including redness, itching, and blistering. Using a decoction of these plants on sunburned skin can worsen the condition.

Similarly, 'Sanguinaria canadensis', also known as bloodroot, contains a compound called sanguinarine, which can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions. Another herb to avoid is 'Sassafras albidum', which contains a compound called safrole, that can cause skin irritation, redness, and itching. 'Wolfsbane' or 'Aconitum' plants, like Aconitum carmichaelii, contain a compound called aconitine, which can cause severe skin irritation, numbness, and even paralysis. Using decoctions of these herbs on sunburned skin can lead to serious complications.

It's best to avoid these herbs and stick to gentle, soothing remedies to help your skin heal.

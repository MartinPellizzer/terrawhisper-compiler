1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains bioactive constituents like aloe-emodin, aloin, and vitamins A, C, and E. These constituents have anti-inflammatory and moisturizing properties that help to soothe and hydrate dry skin.

Aloe-emodin and aloin work together to reduce inflammation and promote skin healing, while vitamins A, C, and E provide antioxidant protection that helps to lock in moisture. The soothing properties of aloe barbadensis essential oils also help to calm irritated skin and reduce redness, making it a popular choice for treating dry skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis.

By promoting hydration and reducing inflammation, aloe barbadensis essential oils helps to restore the skin's natural barrier function and leave skin feeling soft and supple.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Aloe barbadensis leaves, 1 cup of water, and a juicer or blender.
  2. Juice or blend the Aloe leaves to get 1 cup of Aloe juice. Strain the juice to remove any pulp.
  3. Mix 1 cup of Aloe juice with 1 cup of water in a saucepan. Heat the mixture on low heat for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool. Strain the mixture again to get a clear liquid.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a clean glass bottle and store it in the fridge. Use 2-3 tablespoons of the liquid as needed for dry skin relief.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds like cineole, linalool, and terpinen-4-ol.

These bioactive constituents have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe and calm dry skin. Cineole, in particular, has been shown to reduce redness and irritation, while linalool has a moisturizing effect that can help lock in moisture. Terpinen-4-ol has antimicrobial properties that can help prevent infections and promote wound healing.

By using Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils, you can help to reduce dryness, irritation, and inflammation, promoting healthier and more balanced skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil) in a clean glass jar.
  2. Fill a saucepan with 2 cups of water and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat to low and place the glass jar with leaves and oil in the saucepan, making sure the jar is not touching the water.
  4. Steep the leaves in the oil for 2-3 hours, then strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Store the Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil in a cool, dark place and use it within 6 months.

3. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are biologically active constituents that help with dry skin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that reduce redness and irritation. The flavonoids in calendula oil also have a protective effect on the skin's barrier function, preventing moisture loss and promoting healthy skin cell growth. The saponins and carotenoids work together to soothe and calm dry, itchy skin, while also promoting the skin's natural regeneration process.

By using calendula officinalis essential oils, you can help to repair and nourish dry skin, restoring its natural moisture and suppleness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula flowers, a clean glass jar, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Fill the glass jar 2/3 with Calendula flowers and top with the carrier oil, making sure the flowers are completely covered.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the oil extract from the flowers.
  4. After the steeping time, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container, discarding the flowers.
  5. Store the Calendula essential oil in a dark glass bottle, protected from light and heat, and use it within 6 months for dry skin relief.

4. Santalum album

Santalum album essential oils contains active constituents like alpha-bisabolol, santalol, and beta-santalol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help soothe and calm dry skin. The essential oil also contains sesquiterpenes, which have antimicrobial properties that reduce the risk of infections and promote healthy skin. By reducing inflammation and preventing infections, Santalum album essential oils can help to lock in moisture and support the natural barrier function of the skin.

Regular use of this essential oil can help to alleviate dry skin symptoms and promote a healthier, more balanced skin tone.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Santalum album leaves and flowers. You can purchase them from a health food store or online.
  2. Use a clean coffee grinder to grind 1/2 cup of the dried Santalum album into a fine powder.
  3. Combine the ground Santalum album powder with 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) in a clean glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will help release the essential oils.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

5. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole, limonene, and alpha-pinene as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce redness and irritation in dry skin. Cineole, in particular, has a cooling effect that can soothe and calm dry, itchy skin. Limonene and alpha-pinene also have antiseptic and antimicrobial properties, which can help prevent infection and promote healthy skin regeneration.

By reducing inflammation and promoting skin health, Eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help to alleviate dry skin symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a glass jar, a cheesecloth, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Wash the Eucalyptus leaves in cold water and let them dry completely.
  3. Fill the glass jar with 2 cups of dried Eucalyptus leaves and pour 1 cup of carrier oil over them.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. Then, strain the oil through cheesecloth.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its primary active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to soothe and calm dry skin. The anti-inflammatory properties reduce redness and irritation, while the antioxidant properties help to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals. The moisturizing properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help to lock in moisture and hydrate the skin, reducing the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles.

By reducing inflammation and promoting hydration, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils helps to relieve dry skin and promote healthy, smooth skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a large bowl.
  2. Heat the water in a pot over low heat until it reaches 100°F (38°C). Pour the hot water over the lavender flowers in the bowl.
  3. Steep the mixture for 2 hours or overnight, allowing the flowers to release their oils into the water.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a glass bottle and let it sit for 2-3 days to allow the oils to settle and separate from the water. Use a pipette to extract the essential oil from the top layer and store it in a dark glass bottle.

7. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, linalool, and geraniol as its active constituents.

These compounds have moisturizing and soothing properties that help to hydrate dry skin. Limonene and linalool in the oil have emollient properties, which means they help to fill in the gaps between skin cells, making the skin feel smoother and more comfortable. Geraniol has antiseptic properties that help to protect the skin from infection and promote healing of dry, cracked skin.

The combination of these properties in Cymbopogon citratus essential oils makes it a useful remedy for dry skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves. Use a sharp knife to cut them into small pieces.
  2. Combine 1 cup of the leaf pieces with 2 cups of water in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 10 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Transfer the strained liquid to a still or a distillation apparatus. Heat the liquid to separate the essential oil from the water.
  5. Collect the essential oil and store it in a dark glass bottle. Use the oil within 6 months.

8. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains a high concentration of triterpenoid saponins, glycyrrhizin, and flavonoids.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and moisturizing properties that help to soothe and hydrate dry skin. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, has been shown to have a potent moisturizing effect, helping to lock in moisture and reduce water loss from the skin. Flavonoids, such as licoric acid, also have anti-inflammatory properties that can help to calm and protect dry, irritated skin.

By using Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils, you can help to calm and hydrate dry skin, promoting a healthy and balanced complexion.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots, 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a glass jar.
  2. Dry the roots in a low-temperature oven (150°F - 200°F) for 2 hours or until they are completely dry.
  3. Grind the dried roots into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
  4. Combine 1 tablespoon of the root powder with 1 cup of carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well.
  5. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a new container.

9. Saponaria officinalis

Saponaria officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoids and saponins as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to soothe and calm dry skin. Saponins, in particular, have been shown to have emollient properties, making them effective in moisturizing and protecting the skin. The antioxidant properties of triterpenoids help to neutralize free radicals that can cause damage to the skin cells, promoting healthy skin function.

By using Saponaria officinalis essential oils, you can help to lock in moisture and protect your skin from dryness.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Saponaria officinalis flowers and 4 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat and add the flowers. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl. Discard the flowers.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of jojoba oil and 1 tablespoon of beeswax to the bowl. Let it cool and solidify.
  5. Once solidified, use a dropper to extract the essential oil from the mixture. Store it in a glass bottle for later use.

10. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica essential oils contains bioactive constituents such as histamine, leucocyanidin, and isorhapontigenin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce redness and irritation associated with dry skin. The antioxidant properties of isorhapontigenin also protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting healthy skin cell growth. Histamine has been shown to increase blood flow to the skin, which can help improve dry skin's hydration and elasticity.

By addressing inflammation and promoting skin cell health, Urtica dioica essential oils may be beneficial for people with dry skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Urtica dioica leaves and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Wash the leaves with 1 cup of water to remove dirt and debris.
  3. Combine the leaves and carrier oil in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat (150°F - 180°F) for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass container. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Urtica dioica essential oil in a dark glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops on affected skin areas 2-3 times a day for dry skin relief.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dry skin from forming?

Drinking herbal tea might help prevent dry skin.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, contain antioxidants that can soothe and hydrate the skin. They may also improve skin elasticity and reduce inflammation, which can help keep the skin healthy and moisturized.

This can be especially helpful during cold, dry months.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dry skin every day?

Herbal teas for dry skin can be a good choice, but make sure to consume them in moderation.

Drinking them every day can be safe if you choose teas that are gentle and soothing, like chamomile or calendula. However, some teas, like peppermint, might be too strong for daily consumption.

Just be aware of what you're drinking.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dry skin?

Herbal teas can help with dry skin, but it takes some time to see results.

Drinking them regularly can improve skin health, but you might not notice a difference right away.

It can take a few weeks to a month or more for your skin to start feeling softer and more hydrated.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for dry skin?

Drinking herbal tea for dry skin can be beneficial at any time of day.

However, many people find it helps to relax and soothe dry skin in the evening, after a long day.

A warm cup of herbal tea, such as chamomile or calendula, can be a calming way to unwind before bed, promoting a good night's sleep and helping your skin feel better.

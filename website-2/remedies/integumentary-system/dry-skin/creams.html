1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains bioactive constituents like triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help soothe dry skin. The triterpenoid saponins in Calendula officinalis creams work to lock in moisture and protect the skin from environmental stressors. The flavonoids and carotenoids also help reduce inflammation and promote collagen production, making dry skin look and feel healthier.

By applying Calendula officinalis creams to dry skin, these bioactive constituents work together to help restore the skin's natural moisture barrier.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup Calendula officinalis infused oil, 1/2 cup coconut oil, 1/4 cup beeswax, 2 tablespoons shea butter, and 2 drops vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the Calendula officinalis infused oil and shea butter to the melted mixture. Stir until combined.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool until the mixture reaches 120°F to 130°F. Stir in the vitamin E oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and harden. Once set, your Calendula officinalis cream is ready to use.

2. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale creams contains high concentrations of allantoin, aloe-emodin, and mucilages, which are key active constituents that make it effective for dry skin.

Allantoin, in particular, helps to lock in moisture and protect the skin from environmental stressors. Mucilages form a protective barrier on the skin's surface, reducing water loss and soothing dryness. Aloe-emodin, a naturally occurring compound, has anti-inflammatory properties that help to calm and comfort irritated skin.

These properties work together to provide long-lasting hydration and relief for dry, parched skin.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Symphytum officinale leaves and flowers. Clean them with water.
  2. Steep the Symphytum officinale mixture in 1 cup of boiling water for 10 minutes. Let it cool.
  3. Mix 1/2 cup of beeswax and 1/2 cup of coconut oil in a double boiler.
  4. Add the cooled Symphytum officinale mixture to the beeswax and coconut oil mixture. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool. Once set, your Symphytum officinale cream for dry skin is ready to use.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains the active constituent glycyrrhizin, which helps to lock in moisture and soothe dry skin.

The saponins in Glycyrrhiza glabra creams create a protective barrier on the skin's surface, preventing moisture loss and promoting hydration. As a result, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams help to soften and calm dry skin, leaving it feeling smoother and more comfortable.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water, 1/2 cup of glycerin, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil, and a double boiler.
  2. Melt the beeswax in the double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add the glycerin and distilled water to the melted beeswax. Stir until combined.
  4. Add the glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, vitamin E oil, and stir until the powder is fully dissolved.
  5. Remove the mixture from heat, let it cool, then pour it into a container. Let it solidify before applying to dry skin.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams also contain other beneficial compounds like amino acids and fatty acids that support skin health and hydration.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups distilled water, 1/4 cup aloe vera gel, 1/4 cup coconut oil, 2 tablespoons beeswax, 2 tablespoons shea butter, 2 tablespoons vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl in a saucepan of simmering water.
  3. Combine aloe vera gel, shea butter, and vitamin E oil in a separate bowl. Stir until smooth.
  4. Slowly pour the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture into the aloe vera gel mixture. Stir until combined.

5. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica creams contains boiactive constituents like histamine, acetylcholine, and catecholamines.

These substances have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help to soothe and calm dry skin. The histamine in Urtica dioica creams helps to increase blood flow to the affected area, which can aid in the delivery of nutrients and oxygen to dry skin cells.   2. Steep the dried Urtica dioica leaves in 1 cup of carrier oil (like olive or sweet almond oil) for 2-3 weeks, shaking the mixture every day.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  4. In a small saucepan, melt the coconut oil and beeswax over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  5. Combine the infused oil from step 3 with the melted oil and beeswax mixture from step 4. Pour into a clean container and let cool to form a cream.

  3. Add the dried Saponaria officinalis flowers to the heated coconut oil and let it steep for 2 to 3 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, and discard the solids.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dry skin every day?

Herbal teas for dry skin can be a soothing choice, but consuming them daily might not be the best idea.

Some herbs can be quite drying, which could worsen the skin issue. Moderation is key - try having a cup or two a week and see how your skin responds.

This way, you can still enjoy the benefits without overdoing it.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dry skin?

Herbal teas can help with dry skin, but it takes time to see results.

Some people notice a difference within a week or two after drinking a few cups daily.

Others may need to drink herbal tea for several weeks before they notice their skin feeling softer and more hydrated.

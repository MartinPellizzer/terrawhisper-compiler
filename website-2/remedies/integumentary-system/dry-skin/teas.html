Dry skin remedies for older adults: 8 herbal teas

As people age, their skin's natural ability to hold moisture decreases, leading to dry, itchy, and sometimes painful skin.

This can be uncomfortable and affect daily life, making simple tasks like dressing or even sleeping difficult. Older adults may experience dry skin on their hands, feet, and face, which can also increase the risk of skin infections and injuries.

Dry skin can also lead to feelings of self-consciousness and low self-esteem, impacting mental health and overall well-being. In addition, dry skin can be a symptom of underlying health conditions, such as diabetes or thyroid problems, which need to be addressed by a healthcare professional.

Drinking herbal teas can help older adults treat dry skin by providing antioxidants and soothing compounds that can help lock in moisture and calm irritated skin.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas are a natural remedy for dry skin.

They work by providing intense hydration and soothing relief due to the presence of vitamins A, C, and E, as well as minerals like calcium and potassium. These constituents help to lock in moisture, reduce inflammation, and promote the repair of damaged skin cells. When you drink Aloe barbadensis teas, you can expect to see improvements in your skin's texture and appearance. To get the most out of Aloe barbadensis teas, try drinking 1-2 cups a day, preferably before bedtime.

It's recommended to steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried Aloe vera leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. You can also add honey or lemon to taste, but make sure to avoid any added sugars or artificial flavorings. As you incorporate Aloe barbadensis teas into your routine, you may notice your skin becoming softer, smoother, and more supple. You may also experience a reduction in redness and itchiness.

Your skin's natural barrier function may also improve, allowing it to retain moisture more effectively.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas are a natural remedy that can help soothe and calm dry skin.

The tea is made from the flowers of the Calendula plant, which is rich in properties and constituents that are beneficial for the skin. The tea contains triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds help to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing, making it an effective remedy for dry skin. The tea also has a moisturizing effect, helping to lock in moisture and protect the skin from further dryness. To use Calendula officinalis tea for dry skin, you can make a tea by steeping the dried flowers in hot water. You can drink the tea or use it as a compress to apply directly to the affected area.

For a compress, steep a teaspoon of dried flowers in a cup of boiling water, let it cool, and then soak a cloth in the liquid. Apply the compress to the dry skin area for 15-20 minutes, 2-3 times a day. You can also add the tea to your bath water for an extra dose of moisture. As you use Calendula officinalis tea, you may notice improvements in your dry skin within a few days. The tea may help to reduce redness and inflammation, making your skin look and feel smoother. The moisturizing effect of the tea can also help to lock in moisture, reducing the need for heavy creams and lotions. In addition, the antioxidant properties of the tea may help to protect your skin from environmental stressors and damage.

By incorporating Calendula officinalis tea into your skincare routine, you can help to keep your skin healthy, hydrated, and looking its best.

3. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica teas, also known as nettle tea, can be beneficial for people dealing with dry skin.

The tea is rich in antioxidants, flavonoids, and other plant compounds that help soothe and moisturize the skin. Urtica dioica contains glycosides and saponins, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce redness and irritation associated with dry skin. The tea also contains vitamin C and E, which are essential for maintaining healthy skin and preventing moisture loss. If you're experiencing dry skin, consider drinking a cup of nettle tea 2-3 times a day. To make the tea, steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried Urtica dioica leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it while it's still warm. You can also add a small amount of honey or lemon to improve the taste. Additionally, consider applying a nettle tea compress to your dry skin by soaking a cloth in the cooled tea and applying it to the affected area for 10-15 minutes. As you start drinking nettle tea and applying the compress, you may notice improvements in your skin within a few days. Your skin may feel softer and more hydrated, and the redness and irritation may decrease. You may also notice that your skin is less prone to dryness and irritation, and you may be able to reduce your use of moisturizers and other skincare products.

However, individual results may vary, and it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional if your dry skin persists or worsens.

4. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas are a natural remedy that can help alleviate dry skin issues.

The reason for this is due to its rich content of antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals. The tea contains flavonoids, alkylamides, and other compounds that have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe and calm dry, itchy skin. The polysaccharides present in the tea also have prebiotic properties, promoting a healthy gut microbiome, which is linked to skin health. To get relief from dry skin, you can try drinking 1-2 cups of Echinacea purpurea tea per day.

You can also apply the cooled tea as a compress to the affected areas to help lock in moisture. If you're looking for additional relief, consider combining the tea with a moisturizer or oil rich in omega-3 fatty acids, which can help lock in moisture and soothe dry skin. As you incorporate Echinacea purpurea tea into your routine, you may start to notice improvements in your skin's texture and hydration levels. The inflammation and redness associated with dry skin may decrease, and your skin may feel softer and more supple.

Additionally, the antioxidants present in the tea may help protect your skin from environmental stressors and damage caused by free radicals.

5. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis teas, also known as lemon balm tea, is a natural remedy that can help soothe dry skin.

It contains properties such as flavonoids, terpenes, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds can help calm and moisturize dry skin by reducing irritation and promoting the repair of skin cells. The tea's active ingredients, including rosmarinic acid, can also help regulate the skin's natural barrier function, allowing it to retain moisture more effectively. To try Melissa officinalis tea for dry skin, you can start by making a tea infusion by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried lemon balm leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the liquid and let it cool before applying it to your skin using a cotton pad or a gentle massage. You can also use it as a bath soak by adding 1/4 cup of dried lemon balm leaves to your bath water. As you soak, the tea's active ingredients can help soften and hydrate your skin, leaving it feeling smoother and more comfortable. As you use Melissa officinalis tea for dry skin, you can expect to see improvements in skin hydration, reduced inflammation, and a more even skin tone. The tea's antioxidant properties can also help protect your skin from environmental stressors, such as pollution and UV damage.

With consistent use, you may find that your skin feels softer, more supple, and better able to withstand dryness and irritation.

6. Hibiscus sabdariffa

Hibiscus sabdariffa teas are known for their potential to soothe and alleviate dry skin issues.

This is due to the presence of antioxidants, flavonoids, and anthocyanins in the tea. These compounds help to reduce inflammation and protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals. Additionally, the tea's high content of vitamin C and beta-carotene contributes to its skin-soothing properties. To benefit from Hibiscus sabdariffa teas for dry skin, try drinking 1-2 cups of the tea per day.

You can make the tea by steeping dried hibiscus flowers in hot water for 5-7 minutes. For enhanced skin benefits, apply a cooled Hibiscus sabdariffa tea compress or add a few drops of the tea to your bath water for a relaxing soak. As you incorporate Hibiscus sabdariffa teas into your routine, you may notice an improvement in skin hydration and elasticity. The tea's anti-inflammatory properties may also help to reduce redness and itchiness associated with dry skin.

Furthermore, the antioxidants in the tea may help to promote collagen production, leading to a smoother and more even-toned complexion.

7. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale teas, also known as comfrey tea, are a natural remedy for dry skin.

This is because they contain anti-inflammatory and moisturizing properties, including allantoin, rosmarinic acid, and triterpenoid saponins. Allantoin helps to soothe and soften skin, while rosmarinic acid has antioxidant properties that protect skin from damage. Triterpenoid saponins, on the other hand, have emollient properties that help to lock in moisture. To use comfrey tea for dry skin, you can make a tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried comfrey leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes.

Strain the tea and apply it to your skin with a cotton pad or use it as a face mask. You can also add it to your bath water for a relaxing and nourishing soak. As you use comfrey tea for dry skin, you may notice an improvement in skin hydration and elasticity. Your skin may feel softer and look more radiant. Regular use can also help to reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles.

However, remember to do a patch test before using comfrey tea on your skin, as some people may be sensitive to its effects.

8. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla teas are known to provide relief from dry skin due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

This herbal tea contains constituents like apigenin and luteolin, which have been shown to calm and reduce redness in irritated skin. The tea also has antiseptic and antifungal properties, thanks to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, that help to protect the skin from infection and promote healing. To use Matricaria chamomilla teas for dry skin, start by brewing a cup of tea using one teaspoon of dried flowers in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Let the tea cool down before applying it to the affected area using a cotton ball.

You can also add a tablespoon of honey or olive oil to the cooled tea to enhance its moisturizing properties. When you apply the cooled tea to your dry skin, you can expect to see a reduction in redness and inflammation within a few hours. The tea may also help to lock in moisture and soothe any itchiness or discomfort. As the tea's anti-inflammatory properties take effect, you may notice an improvement in the texture and appearance of your skin within a few days.

However, it's essential to note that the tea may not provide instant results and may require consistent use to achieve noticeable improvements.

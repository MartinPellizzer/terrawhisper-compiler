11 Herbal Tinctures For Dry Skin

By combining different herbs in a tincture, you can create a remedy that addresses your specific dry skin issues. To use an herbal tincture for dry skin, simply apply a few drops to the affected area with a cotton ball or swab.

You can also add a few drops to your bath water for an all-over skin treatment.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to soothe and protect dry skin. The flavonoids in calendula tincture, such as quercetin, have been shown to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing, while the carotenoids, like beta-carotene, act as antioxidants to neutralize free radicals that can damage skin cells. The anti-inflammatory properties of triterpenoid saponins help to reduce redness and irritation, promoting a healthy skin environment.

By applying calendula tincture to dry skin, the bioactive constituents work together to calm, protect, and nourish the skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula flowers and 2 cups of vodka or 80% alcohol in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of dried Calendula flowers to a separate glass jar and pour in 1 cup of glycerin.
  3. Close both jars and store them in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking them daily.
  4. Strain the liquid from both jars through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into separate containers, discarding the solids.
  5. Mix the two tinctures together and store the final Calendula tincture in a dark glass bottle in the fridge for up to 6 months.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains active constituents like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and triterpenoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and moisturizing properties that help to soothe and hydrate dry skin. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, has been shown to increase the production of natural moisturizing factors in the skin, which helps to lock in moisture and reduce dryness. The triterpenoids also have antioxidant properties, which help to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals and promote healthy skin cell function.

By using Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures, people with dry skin may experience improved hydration and a reduction in dryness and irritation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, 2 cups of 80% vodka or glycerin, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the dried root with 2 cups of vodka or glycerin in the glass jar. Make sure the root is completely covered.
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the root to infuse into the liquid.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the root.
  5. Label the jar with the date and the name of the tincture. Store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the tincture mixed with water or oil as needed for dry skin.

3. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica tinctures contains bioactive constituents like histamine, acetylcholine, and mucilages, which are known for their soothing properties.

The histamine in Urtica dioica tinctures helps to improve blood flow to the skin, which is beneficial for dry skin. The mucilages, a type of soluble fiber, form a protective barrier on the skin's surface, locking in moisture and reducing water loss. The acetylcholine in Urtica dioica tinctures also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce redness and irritation associated with dry skin.

By providing a protective barrier and improving blood flow, Urtica dioica tinctures can help to alleviate dry skin symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Urtica dioica leaves and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add 1 cup of water to the jar and mix well. Let it sit for 2 hours or overnight.
  3. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of glycerin to the liquid and mix well. This helps preserve the tincture and soothe dry skin.
  5. Label the jar and store it in a cool, dark place. Shake the jar well before using. Take 20-30 drops of the tincture, 2-3 times a day, mixed with water or applied topically to affected areas.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis tinctures contains aloin, aloe-emodin, and other biologically active constituents.

These compounds help to soothe and hydrate dry skin. Aloin and aloe-emodin have anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce redness and irritation. The tannins in aloe barbadensis help to lock in moisture, protecting the skin from water loss.

The gel-like consistency of the tincture also helps to create a barrier on the skin's surface, locking in moisture and reducing dryness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 2 cups of vodka, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Aloe gel and vodka in the jar. Stir well to mix.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Aloe tincture in the fridge. Use 10-15 drops on dry skin as needed.

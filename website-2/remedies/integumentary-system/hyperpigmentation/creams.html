8 Herbal Creams For Hyperpigmentation

Herbal creams are a great way to tackle hyperpigmentation, a common skin condition where patches of skin become darker than the surrounding area.

Hyperpigmentation can be caused by sun damage, hormonal changes, or even skin injuries. So, what makes herbal creams so effective?. Aloe barbadensis, a type of aloe vera, is packed with antioxidants that help reduce inflammation and soothe the skin, making it a popular ingredient in many creams. Curcuma longa, or turmeric, has potent anti-inflammatory properties that not only calm the skin but also inhibit the production of melanin, the pigment responsible for skin darkening. Centella asiatica, also known as gotu kola, has been used for centuries to promote skin regeneration and reduce scarring, which can also contribute to hyperpigmentation.

When it comes to relieving hyperpigmentation, herbal creams work by reducing inflammation, inhibiting melanin production, and promoting skin regeneration. These creams can help to even out skin tone, reducing the appearance of dark patches and promoting a more radiant complexion. By using herbal creams containing these and other beneficial herbs, you can enjoy a range of benefits, including improved skin texture, reduced appearance of fine lines and wrinkles, and a more even skin tone. Plus, many of these herbs are naturally soothing and calming, making them perfect for sensitive skin. Using herbal creams containing Aloe barbadensis, Curcuma longa, and Centella asiatica can be especially beneficial for people who want to avoid harsh chemicals and artificial fragrances found in many commercial creams. These natural ingredients are also gentle enough for daily use, making them a great addition to your skincare routine.

By incorporating these herbal creams into your skincare routine, you can say goodbye to hyperpigmentation and hello to a brighter, more even-toned complexion.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contain several bioactive constituents, including aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan, that help reduce hyperpigmentation.

Aloin has anti-inflammatory properties that soothe and calm the skin, reducing redness and inflammation that can lead to hyperpigmentation. Aloe-emodin has antioxidant properties that protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to the formation of dark spots. Acemannan stimulates collagen production, improving skin texture and reducing the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles, including those associated with hyperpigmentation.

These properties help to brighten and even out skin tone, reducing the appearance of hyperpigmentation and promoting a more even complexion.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup Aloe barbadensis gel, 2 tablespoons beeswax, 2 tablespoons coconut oil, 1 tablespoon shea butter, 1 teaspoon vitamin E oil, and 5 drops of lavender essential oil.
  2. Melt beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove melted mixture from heat and let it cool for 5 minutes. Stir in Aloe barbadensis gel and vitamin E oil until well combined.
  4. Add lavender essential oil and mix well. Pour mixture into a clean glass jar or container.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify completely. Once solid, your Aloe barbadensis cream for hyperpigmentation is ready to use. Store it in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains curcuminoids, such as curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which have potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce the production of melanin, the pigment responsible for dark spots and hyperpigmentation. Curcuminoids also have a brightening effect on the skin, reducing the appearance of age spots and hyperpigmentation. Additionally, curcuma longa creams often contain other active constituents like ferulic acid, which has antioxidant properties that help to protect the skin from damage and promote even skin tone.

Regular use of curcuma longa creams may help to improve the appearance of hyperpigmentation, making it a popular natural remedy for this common skin concern.

  1. Melt 1 cup of coconut oil in a double boiler. Stir until smooth.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder (Curcuma longa) and 2 tablespoons of beeswax. Stir well.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of shea butter and 2 tablespoons of aloe vera gel. Mix until combined.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool for 10 minutes. Stir occasionally.
  5. Pour mixture into a container and let cool completely. Use within 2 weeks.

3. Centella asiatica

Centella asiatica creams contains active constituents like asiatic acid, madecassic acid, and brahminoside, which help reduce hyperpigmentation.

Asiatic acid has a strong antioxidant property, which helps protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, a common cause of hyperpigmentation. Madecassic acid has anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce redness and irritation that can lead to the appearance of hyperpigmentation. Brahminoside has been shown to inhibit the production of melanin, the pigment responsible for hyperpigmentation.

By reducing melanin production and protecting the skin from damage, Centella asiatica creams can help even out skin tone and reduce the appearance of hyperpigmentation.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1/2 cup of aloe vera gel, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil, and 2 teaspoons of Centella asiatica extract.
  2. Combine distilled water and aloe vera gel in a bowl. Heat the mixture in the microwave for 10-15 seconds.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix coconut oil and beeswax. Microwave for 10-15 seconds to melt the beeswax.
  4. Combine the oil and wax mixture with the water and aloe vera mixture. Stir well to combine.
  5. Add vitamin E oil and Centella asiatica extract to the mixture. Stir well and pour the mixture into a container. Let it cool and harden before use.

4. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains bilobalide and ginkgolides, which are its active constituents.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce the appearance of hyperpigmentation. The flavonoids and terpenoids in Ginkgo biloba creams also have a brightening effect on the skin, helping to even out skin tone and reduce the visibility of dark spots. The anti-inflammatory properties of Ginkgo biloba creams can also help soothe and calm irritated skin, which can contribute to hyperpigmentation.

By reducing inflammation and promoting skin brightness, Ginkgo biloba creams can help improve the appearance of hyperpigmentation and leave skin looking smoother and more even-toned.

  1. Combine 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, and 2 tablespoons of shea butter in a small saucepan.
  2. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring occasionally, until the beeswax melts.
  3. Add 10 drops of Ginkgo biloba extract and 2 tablespoons of jojoba oil to the mixture.
  4. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool for 5-7 minutes, then stir in 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a glass jar and let it cool completely before applying it to the affected area.

5. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce the appearance of hyperpigmentation. The flavonoids, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the production of melanin, the pigment responsible for dark spots. Additionally, calendula's anti-inflammatory properties can help soothe and calm the skin, reducing redness and promoting even skin tone.

By reducing inflammation and inhibiting melanin production, calendula creams can help to fade hyperpigmentation and promote a more even skin tone.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula officinalis flowers, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler over low heat. Stir until smooth.
  3. Add the dried Calendula flowers to the melted mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter.
  4. Add the beeswax and vitamin E oil to the strained mixture. Stir until the beeswax is fully dissolved.
  5. Pour the mixture into a small jar or tin and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, your Calendula cream is ready to use. Apply it to hyperpigmented areas 2-3 times a day.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains active constituents like linalool, linalyl acetate, and camphor, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help reduce inflammation and damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to hyperpigmentation. The antioxidant activity of linalool and linalyl acetate also helps protect the skin from environmental stressors, reducing the appearance of dark spots. The anti-inflammatory properties of camphor and linalool help soothe and calm the skin, reducing redness and promoting even skin tone.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the skin from damage, Lavandula angustifolia creams can help minimize the appearance of hyperpigmentation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  2. Combine the distilled water and dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. In a separate saucepan, melt the beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  5. Combine the melted oil mixture with the Lavandula angustifolia infusion and stir well. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify.

7. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains licorice extract, which has been used to address hyperpigmentation.

The key bioactive constituents in licorice extract are glycyrrhizin and glabridin. Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce redness and irritation associated with hyperpigmentation. Glabridin, on the other hand, is a potent tyrosinase inhibitor that blocks the production of melanin, the pigment responsible for skin darkening.

By reducing inflammation and inhibiting melanin production, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams can help to even out skin tone and reduce the appearance of hyperpigmentation.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1/2 cup of glycerin, 1/4 cup of glycyrrhiza glabra root extract (licorice root extract), 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of aloe vera gel.
  2. Combine distilled water and glycerin in a saucepan and heat on low heat until the mixture is warm.
  3. Add glycyrrhiza glabra root extract to the mixture and stir well. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  4. Once the mixture is cool, add vitamin E oil and aloe vera gel. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container and refrigerate until it thickens. Apply to hyperpigmented skin as needed.

8. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum creams contains a high concentration of silymarin, a bioactive compound extracted from the plant.

Silymarin has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals and reduce inflammation associated with hyperpigmentation. The flavonoids and polyphenols present in silymarin, such as silibinin and silicristin, have been shown to inhibit the activity of tyrosinase, an enzyme responsible for melanin production. By inhibiting tyrosinase, silymarin helps to reduce melanin production and even out skin tone.

Silymarin also helps to promote collagen production and improve skin elasticity, further reducing the appearance of hyperpigmentation.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Silybum marianum leaves, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Steep the Silybum marianum leaves in the coconut oil for 2 weeks, shaking the mixture daily.
  3. Strain the mixture and discard the leaves. Add the beeswax and heat the mixture in a double boiler until the beeswax is melted.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool. Once it reaches 120°F to 140°F, whip it with an electric mixer until it thickens.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool completely. Use a clean spoon to apply the cream to affected areas 2 times a day.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for hyperpigmentation?

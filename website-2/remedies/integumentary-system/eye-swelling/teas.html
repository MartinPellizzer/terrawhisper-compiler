9 Teas for Boxers with Eye Swelling

Boxers often suffer from eye swelling, also known as periorbital edema, due to repeated blows to the face during a match.

This can be caused by a direct punch to the eye or a glancing blow that hits the surrounding area. The swelling can be painful and make it difficult for a boxer to see, which can affect their performance in the ring. In addition, eye swelling can make it hard for a boxer to recover from a match, as they may need to spend time in the hospital or undergo surgery. This can lead to missed training sessions, delayed fights, and even a prolonged break from competition. As a result, eye swelling can have a significant impact on a boxer's career and their ability to earn a living from the sport.

When eye swelling occurs, it can be challenging to treat, as it often requires a combination of rest, ice, and medication. However, some boxers are turning to herbal teas as a natural way to help reduce swelling and promote recovery. These teas work by reducing inflammation and promoting blood flow to the affected area. They can also help to reduce the pain and discomfort associated with eye swelling. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to treat a range of ailments, and they offer a natural alternative to traditional medication.

By drinking herbal tea, boxers can help to reduce the severity and duration of eye swelling, which can help them to recover faster and get back to training sooner.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas can help with eye swelling due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

The tea contains compounds like flavonoids, triterpenoids, and carotenoids, which have potent anti-inflammatory effects. These constituents help reduce swelling and ease pain by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. When you use Calendula officinalis tea for eye swelling, start by making a strong tea by steeping one teaspoon of dried Calendula flowers in a cup of boiling water for five to seven minutes.

Let the tea cool down, then use a clean cloth to apply it as a compress over the affected eye. Hold the compress in place for 5-10 minutes. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day. You may notice a reduction in swelling and pain within a few hours, and the inflammation should subside within a day or two.

However, if the swelling is severe or persistent, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional for proper evaluation and treatment.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas are a natural remedy that can help alleviate eye swelling caused by various factors such as allergies, sinus infections, or even a blow to the face.

This tea gets its healing properties from the aloe vera plant, which is rich in vitamins A, C, and E, as well as minerals like potassium and magnesium. The key constituents responsible for reducing inflammation and swelling are aloin and aloe-emodin, which have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. When consumed as a tea, these compounds work to reduce redness, swelling, and pain in the affected area.

To use Aloe barbadensis teas for eye swelling, start by steeping 1-2 tablespoons of dried aloe vera leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the mixture and let it cool before applying it to the affected area with a clean cloth. You can also drink the tea to help reduce inflammation from the inside out. It's essential to note that Aloe barbadensis teas may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, so consult with a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you have underlying medical conditions.

As the tea takes effect, you may notice a significant reduction in swelling and redness, and your eyes may feel more comfortable and relaxed.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas have been traditionally used to help reduce eye swelling due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant contains compounds like alkylamides, glycoproteins, and polyphenols, which have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory molecules that can cause swelling. When you drink Echinacea purpurea tea, these compounds are absorbed into your body, where they can help reduce the inflammation that's causing the swelling in your eyes. If you're experiencing eye swelling, you can try making a tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers in a cup of hot water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day.

To get the most out of the tea, try to drink it as soon as it's made, as the compounds in Echinacea purpurea can break down quickly. You can also add a squeeze of lemon juice to enhance the absorption of the compounds. As you drink the Echinacea purpurea tea, you may start to notice a reduction in the swelling and inflammation in your eyes. Your eyes may feel less puffy and sore, and you may be able to see more clearly. It's also possible that you'll start to feel a sense of relief from the discomfort and pressure in your eyes.

However, keep in mind that Echinacea purpurea tea may not work for everyone, and it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before trying any new remedy, especially if you have underlying medical conditions or take medications.

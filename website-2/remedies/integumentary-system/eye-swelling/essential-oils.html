9 herbal infusions for contact lens wearers to reduce eye swelling

Contact lens wearers often experience eye swelling due to the constant pressure and friction caused by the lenses.

This can be uncomfortable and affect their daily lives, making it difficult to perform tasks that require concentration or even just to feel rested. Eye swelling can lead to redness, itchiness, and blurred vision, making it hard to enjoy activities or even just to wake up feeling refreshed. It can also increase the risk of eye infections and other complications if left untreated. For those who rely on contact lenses for clear vision, eye swelling can be a frustrating and debilitating issue. When eye swelling occurs, it's not just about the physical discomfort – it can also be mentally taxing.

Constantly dealing with eye irritation can lead to anxiety and stress, making it even harder to manage the symptoms. Some herbal essential oils have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can help to calm eye swelling and reduce discomfort. These oils can be added to eye drops or used in a compress to provide relief. They work by reducing redness and inflammation, allowing the eyes to feel cooler and more comfortable. This can help to alleviate the symptoms of eye swelling and make it easier to wear contact lenses without discomfort.

By incorporating these oils into their eye care routine, contact lens wearers can find some much-needed relief and get back to their normal activities.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils have anti-inflammatory properties that can help with eye swelling.

This is due to its high content of triterpene saponins, sesquiterpenes, and flavonoids, which have been shown to reduce swelling and ease pain. The essential oil's anti-inflammatory properties are also attributed to its beta-sitosterol and stigmasterol constituents, which have a potent ability to reduce inflammation. If you're experiencing eye swelling, apply a few drops of Calendula officinalis essential oil to a cotton pad and gently press it against the affected area. You can also add a few drops of the oil to a warm compress and apply it to your eye for 5-10 minutes.

Repeat this process as needed. It's essential to note that you should dilute the essential oil with a carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil, as undiluted essential oils can be too potent for the skin. When you apply Calendula officinalis essential oil to your eye, you can expect the swelling to start reducing within 15-30 minutes. The pain and redness may also decrease, making it more comfortable to open your eye. However, it's crucial to remember that if the swelling is severe or persistent, you should seek medical attention as it could be a sign of a more serious condition.

If the swelling is caused by an allergic reaction, avoid using Calendula officinalis essential oil and consult a healthcare professional for advice.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contain properties and constituents like aloe-emodin, aloin, and aloe-vertin that help reduce inflammation and swelling around the eyes.

When applied topically, these compounds can soothe and calm the skin, reducing the appearance of puffiness and swelling. To use Aloe barbadensis essential oils for eye swelling, apply a few drops to a cotton pad or a cold compress and gently press it against the affected area.

Leave it on for 5-10 minutes before rinsing off with cold water. As the essential oils absorb into the skin, you may notice a reduction in swelling and inflammation. The aloe-emodin and aloin in Aloe barbadensis essential oils have anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce redness and swelling, while aloe-vertin can help to soothe and calm the skin.

If you experience persistent or severe eye swelling, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils have been traditionally used to help alleviate eye swelling, and research suggests that this may be due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

The oil contains constituents like caryophyllene, sesquiterpene, and phenolic acids, which are known to possess potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These properties help to reduce swelling and soothe irritated tissues. To use Echinacea purpurea essential oils for eye swelling, apply a few drops to a cotton ball and gently press it against the affected area.

However, be sure to dilute the oil in a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil first, as undiluted essential oils can be too harsh for the delicate skin around the eyes. It's also essential to note that Echinacea purpurea essential oils may cause skin irritation in some individuals, so perform a patch test before using it extensively. By following these steps and using the oil responsibly, you may experience a reduction in eye swelling and discomfort, as the anti-inflammatory properties help to calm and soothe the affected area.

The oil's antioxidant properties may also help to protect the skin from further damage and promote healthy tissue repair.

4. Hamamelis virginiana

Hamamelis virginiana essential oils, also known as witch hazel, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help with eye swelling.

The main constituents of this oil are tannins, gallic acid, and proanthocyanidins, which have a soothing effect on the skin and reduce swelling. When used as a compress or added to a warm bath, the tannins and other compounds in the oil can help to constrict blood vessels and reduce fluid buildup around the eye. If you're experiencing eye swelling, try applying a cooled compress soaked in witch hazel essential oil to the affected area for 10-15 minutes, several times a day.

You can also add a few drops of the oil to a warm bath and soak in it for 15-20 minutes. Make sure to dilute the oil with a carrier oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil, before applying it to your skin. As the oil takes effect, you may notice a decrease in the swelling and redness around your eye. The tannins and other compounds in the oil can also help to reduce any itching or discomfort associated with the swelling.

However, if the swelling persists or is accompanied by other symptoms such as vision changes or severe pain, you should consult a healthcare professional for proper evaluation and treatment.

5. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale essential oils, also known as comfrey oil, has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce eye swelling.

The oil contains constituents like allantoin, which is a natural anti-inflammatory agent, and mucilage, a thick, protective substance that soothes and calms irritated skin and tissues. When applied topically, comfrey oil can help reduce puffiness and redness around the eyes by reducing inflammation and promoting healing. As the oil is absorbed into the skin, you may start to feel a reduction in swelling and puffiness, and the redness may begin to fade. The mucilage in comfrey oil can help lock in moisture, leaving the skin feeling soft and calm. You may also notice an improvement in your ability to open your eyes or see more clearly due to reduced swelling.

However, it's essential to note that comfrey oil may cause allergic reactions in some individuals, so do a patch test before using it extensively.

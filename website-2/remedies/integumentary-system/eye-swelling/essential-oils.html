1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole, a compound known for its anti-inflammatory properties.

Cineole and other active constituents like alpha-pinene and beta-pinene help to reduce swelling by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. These enzymes contribute to the inflammation that causes eye swelling. By reducing inflammation, the oils help to alleviate the swelling and discomfort associated with this condition.

This action is attributed to the oils' ability to act as a natural anti-inflammatory agent.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Wash the leaves with water and dry them with a clean towel.
  3. Combine the leaves and carrier oil in a bowl and let it sit for 2 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the bowl every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid and use 2-3 drops as needed to reduce eye swelling.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains a compound called alkylamides and sesquiterpene lactones, specifically echinacoside and caftaric acid.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce swelling and redness. The sesquiterpene lactones also have a vasoconstrictive effect, which means they can narrow blood vessels and reduce fluid accumulation around the eyes. This helps to alleviate eye swelling caused by allergies or infections.

By reducing inflammation and constricting blood vessels, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can provide relief from eye swelling.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1 cup of carrier oil (such as coconut or olive oil), and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine the Echinacea purpurea flowers and carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well to mix.
  3. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. This allows the oil to infuse with the plant's properties.
  4. After the infusion period, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Echinacea purpurea essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Use 2-3 drops as needed to help reduce eye swelling.

3. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains bioactive constituents such as cineole, limonene, and linalool.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce swelling and redness in the eyes. Cineole, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals, while limonene has antioxidant properties that help to neutralize free radicals. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils make it a useful natural remedy for eye swelling caused by allergies, irritants, or injury.

When applied topically, the oils can help to calm and soothe the skin around the eyes, reducing swelling and promoting healthy tissue repair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the water.
  3. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  5. Collect the liquid and let it cool. Use a dropper to apply a few drops to the affected area for eye swelling relief.

4. Aloe barbadensis

These properties help to reduce swelling and inflammation around the eyes by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. The aloe-emodin in Aloe barbadensis essential oils also has a direct effect on the body's natural response to injury, reducing the swelling caused by histamine release. The anti-inflammatory and anti-irritant properties of Aloe barbadensis essential oils also help to soothe and calm the skin, reducing redness and discomfort.

This makes it a useful remedy for eye swelling caused by a variety of factors, including allergies and injuries.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel and 1 cup of distilled water in a bowl.

  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean glass bottle. Store in the fridge for later use.

5. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce swelling and redness around the eyes. The triterpenoid saponins in Calendula officinalis essential oils have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to swelling. The flavonoids, including quercetin and kaempferol, have potent antioxidant effects that help neutralize free radicals and reduce inflammation.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Calendula officinalis essential oils can help alleviate eye swelling and promote a more comfortable eye environment.

  3. Seal the jar and place it in a cool, dark area for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the oil infuse.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Use the Calendula essential oil as needed to help reduce eye swelling by applying a few drops to a cloth and gently applying it to the affected area.

7. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are active constituents that provide several benefits.

Bilobalide, one of these compounds, is an antioxidant that helps reduce inflammation, which can contribute to eye swelling. Ginkgo biloba essential oils also contains quercetin, a flavonoid that has anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties, which can help alleviate swelling and redness. The terpenoids in ginkgo biloba essential oils, such as ginkgolides, have a vasodilating effect, which can help improve blood flow and reduce swelling.

By using ginkgo biloba essential oils, you can potentially benefit from its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate eye swelling and promote overall eye health.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Ginkgo biloba leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (such as jojoba oil or coconut oil).

  3. Add the cleaned Ginkgo biloba leaves to a clean glass jar. Pour the carrier oil over the leaves, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will help the oil extract from the leaves.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a new glass bottle. Discard the solids and store the Ginkgo biloba essential oil in a cool, dark place.

8. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

Gingerol helps reduce swelling by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines. The essential oils also contain shogaol, a compound that has potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help soothe and calm irritated tissues. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils help reduce swelling and redness in the eyes by fighting off free radicals and calming the nervous system.

By applying a few drops of Zingiber officinale essential oils to a cloth or compress, you can harness the power of its anti-inflammatory compounds to help alleviate eye swelling.

9. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium essential oils contains sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce swelling and redness in the eyes. The sesquiterpenes, particularly achillin and santolina alcohol, exhibit a strong anti-inflammatory effect by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes. The flavonoids, including apigenin and luteolin, possess antioxidant properties that can neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress, contributing to the reduction of eye swelling.

The combination of these bioactive constituents in Achillea millefolium essential oils makes it a potential remedy for eye swelling.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Achillea millefolium leaves, flowers, and stems. Clean and dry the plant material.

Herbal teas can help reduce eye swelling, but it's not safe to drink them daily.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in eye swelling?

Herbal teas can help reduce eye swelling, but it depends on the specific tea and individual response.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for eye swelling?

Drink herbal tea as soon as you notice eye swelling, ideally within 30 minutes.

This allows the anti-inflammatory properties to start working quickly. If the swelling persists, try drinking another cup.

The best time is when you wake up in the morning or right after waking up from a nap, to help reduce puffiness throughout the day.

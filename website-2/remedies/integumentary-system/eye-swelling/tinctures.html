1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling. Alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to eye swelling. Caffeic and rosmarinic acids also have antioxidant properties that can help protect the eyes from damage and reduce inflammation.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the eyes, Echinacea purpurea tinctures may be effective in alleviating eye swelling.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves. Clean and dry them.
  2. Combine the Echinacea purpurea with 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass jar. Make sure the plant material is completely covered by the vodka.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks. Shake the jar every day to help the mixture.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the tincture in small glass containers and store them in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture in water or tea as needed for eye swelling relief.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that help to reduce swelling and prevent infection. The flavonoids in calendula, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress. The triterpenoid saponins in calendula have been found to have a soothing effect on the skin, reducing redness and swelling.

By applying calendula tinctures to the affected area, the bioactive constituents can help to calm and heal the skin, reducing eye swelling and promoting recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula officinalis flowers, 2 cups of vodka (95% or higher), and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine the dried flowers and vodka in the glass jar. Stir well to make sure the flowers are covered.
  3. Close the jar and store it in a dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the flowers to infuse into the vodka.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Label the jar with the date and the name of the tincture. Store it in a cool, dark place. Use 10-20 drops of the tincture in water or as directed by a healthcare professional to help reduce eye swelling.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling in the eyes. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have potent antioxidant effects that combat oxidative stress, a common cause of eye swelling. The terpenoids, such as ginkgolides, have anti-edematous properties, meaning they help reduce fluid accumulation in the eye tissues.

By addressing the underlying causes of eye swelling, Ginkgo biloba tinctures can provide relief from swollen eyes.

  1. Gather ingredients: Ginkgo biloba leaves, 80% ethanol (vodka or rubbing alcohol), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Chop 2 cups of fresh or dried Ginkgo biloba leaves into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped leaves with 2 cups of 80% ethanol in the glass jar. Stir well.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2-6 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.

4. Aloe vera

By combining these properties, aloe vera tinctures can help alleviate eye swelling and promote a more comfortable, healthy appearance.

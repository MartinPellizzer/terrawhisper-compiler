What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have eye swelling?

If you have eye swelling, it's best to avoid certain herbal decoctions.

Comfrey (Symphytum spp.). and chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) can cause eye problems due to their potential to stimulate the eyes and make swelling worse. Chamomile can also cause allergic reactions, which may lead to increased swelling.

Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) contains a compound called berberine, which can increase heart rate and blood pressure, potentially making eye swelling worse. Eyebright (Euphrasia spp.). is not recommended as it can cause eye irritation. Also, avoid licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) as it can increase blood pressure and fluid retention, which may worsen eye swelling.

These herbal decoctions can interact with other medications or exacerbate the underlying condition, so it's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using them.

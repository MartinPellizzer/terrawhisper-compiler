8 Herbal Creams For Eye Swelling

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and redness by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and free radicals. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin, have been shown to reduce inflammation and edema by blocking the action of pro-inflammatory molecules. The carotenoids, including lutein and zeaxanthin, have antioxidant properties that help protect the delicate skin around the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the skin, Calendula officinalis creams can help alleviate eye swelling and promote a more comfortable and healthy appearance.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Infuse the Calendula flowers in the coconut oil by placing them in a clean glass jar and letting it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  3. Melt the shea butter and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water. Stir until smooth.
  4. Strain the infused coconut oil and discard the Calendula flowers. Add the vitamin E oil and stir well. Combine the infused coconut oil mixture with the melted shea butter and beeswax mixture.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container and let it cool and solidify. Once set, use as needed to reduce eye swelling and soothe the skin.

2. Hamamelis virginiana

Hamamelis virginiana creams contains tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce swelling and protect the delicate skin around the eyes. The tannins in Hamamelis virginiana creams constrict blood vessels, reducing the flow of blood to the affected area and minimizing swelling. The flavonoids and phenolic acids have anti-inflammatory effects, reducing redness and irritation.

By applying Hamamelis virginiana creams to the affected area, you can help alleviate eye swelling and promote a speedy recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Hamamelis virginiana leaves and 1 cup of cold carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) in a clean glass jar.
  2. Steep the dried leaves in the carrier oil for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily to help the herbs infuse into the oil.
  3. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  4. Mix 2 tablespoons of the infused oil with 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter in a small saucepan.
  5. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring until the beeswax and shea butter melt and the mixture is smooth. Let cool and use as needed for eye swelling.

3. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale creams contains a high concentration of allantoin, aloe-emodin, and rosmarinic acid.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce swelling and inflammation. Allantoin is particularly effective in soothing and calming irritated tissues, while aloe-emodin has antioxidant properties that help to protect the delicate skin around the eyes. Rosmarinic acid also contributes to the anti-inflammatory effects of Symphytum officinale creams, making it a valuable ingredient for treating eye swelling.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Symphytum officinale creams provide relief from eye swelling and promote a return to normal appearance.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 tablespoons of dried Symphytum officinale leaves, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 2 tablespoons of beeswax.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the dried Symphytum officinale leaves to the melted coconut oil and let it steep for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the leaves.
  5. Mix in the melted beeswax and let the mixture cool and solidify before applying it to the affected area to reduce eye swelling.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains bioactive constituents like glycoproteins, polysaccharides, and vitamins that help reduce eye swelling.

Glycoproteins in aloe vera have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and redness in the eyes. Polysaccharides in aloe vera, particularly acemannan, have been shown to inhibit inflammatory mediators, further reducing eye swelling. The vitamins, including vitamin A, B, and E, in aloe vera have antioxidant properties that help soothe and calm the skin around the eyes.

Aloe vera's soothing and anti-inflammatory properties help to reduce eye swelling and provide relief from puffiness and redness.

  3. Add the Aloe barbadensis gel, vitamin E oil, and tea tree oil to the melted mixture. Stir well until combined.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool until the mixture thickens and reaches a creamy consistency.

  4. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil to the infused oil. Heat the mixture in the double boiler or over low heat, stirring until the beeswax melts.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent eye swelling from forming?

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for eye swelling every day?

Herbal teas can be a soothing way to ease eye swelling.

It's best to drink them in moderation and observe how your body reacts.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in eye swelling?

Herbal teas like chamomile and calendula may help reduce eye swelling.

They typically start working within 15 to 30 minutes after consumption. The swelling may start to go down, and the area may feel cooler and less inflamed.

The effects can last for a few hours, depending on the individual and the severity of the swelling.

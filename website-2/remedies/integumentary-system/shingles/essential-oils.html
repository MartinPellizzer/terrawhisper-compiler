1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce pain and swelling associated with shingles. The essential oil also contains sesquiterpenes, like bisabolol, which have antiviral properties that may help combat the herpes zoster virus that causes shingles. The warming and analgesic properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils, due in part to its gingerol content, can also help alleviate the burning and itching sensations common with shingles.

By reducing inflammation and pain, and potentially inhibiting the virus, Zingiber officinale essential oils may provide relief for individuals suffering from shingles.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale roots and wash them with water.
  2. Dry the roots in a single layer on a paper towel for 24 hours.
  3. Use a food processor to grind 1 cup of the dried roots into a fine powder.
  4. Combine the powder with 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) in a glass jar.
  5. Steep the mixture in a warm, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains a high concentration of linalool and linalyl acetate, two bioactive constituents that have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.

These properties can help to reduce the severity and duration of shingles symptoms. Linalool, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the replication of the varicella-zoster virus, which causes shingles. The antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties of Lavandula angustifolia essential oils may also help to reduce pain and discomfort associated with shingles.

When applied topically, the essential oil can also help to soothe and calm irritated skin, promoting a faster recovery from shingles.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers. Use organic and high-quality flowers for best results.
  2. Place the flowers in a clean glass jar and cover them with 2 cups of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil. Stir well.
  3. Let the mixture sit in a dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the oil to extract from the flowers.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the extracted Lavandula angustifolia essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a carrier oil or bath water for relief from shingles symptoms.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains a compound called cineole, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce pain and discomfort associated with shingles.

The oil also contains eudesmol, a compound that has antimicrobial properties, which may help prevent the spread of the shingles virus. Eucalyptus globulus essential oils has a decongestant property, which can help relieve the itchy and painful rash caused by shingles. The menthol content in the oil also helps to reduce pain and promote a sense of calm, making it easier to manage the symptoms of shingles.

By applying topically, the oil can help to reduce the severity of shingles symptoms and promote a faster recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water over low heat for 10-15 minutes. Then, add the Eucalyptus globulus leaves.
  3. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 30-40 minutes. Monitor the mixture to avoid boiling.
  4. Remove the pot from heat and let it cool for 10 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  5. Transfer the extracted oil to a dark glass bottle using a funnel. Store in a cool, dark place.

4. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce the severity and duration of shingles symptoms. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea essential oils have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals, which can exacerbate shingles pain. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of caffeic and rosmarinic acid can help reduce skin damage and promote wound healing in shingles patients.

By using Echinacea purpurea essential oils, individuals may experience relief from shingles symptoms, such as pain, itching, and skin lesions.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Fill a saucepan with 2 cups of water and bring it to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of Echinacea purpurea flowers to the saucepan and let it steep for 2-3 hours. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth.
  4. Combine the infused liquid with 1 cup of carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 days.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth again and discard the solids. Transfer the Echinacea purpurea essential oil to a small bottle and store it in a cool, dark place.

5. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum essential oils contains naringenin, quercetin, and rutin as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce the severity of shingles symptoms. The antiviral properties of hyperforin and hypericin in Hypericum perforatum essential oils can inhibit the replication of the varicella-zoster virus that causes shingles. The oil's anti-inflammatory properties can also help to reduce pain and discomfort associated with shingles, while its antioxidant properties can promote wound healing and tissue repair.

The combination of these properties makes Hypericum perforatum essential oils a potential natural remedy for shingles.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum flowers, 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil), and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine 1 cup of flowers with 2 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the flowers release their oil.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the extracted oil to a dark glass bottle and label it. Use 5-7 drops of the oil per application to help with shingles symptoms.

6. Melaleuca alternifolia

7. Curcuma longa

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent shingles from forming?

Some herbs like calendula and echinacea are believed to have antiviral properties, but more research is needed to confirm their effectiveness in preventing shingles.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for shingles every day?

Herbal teas can be a helpful addition to your shingles treatment, but it's essential to be cautious with daily consumption.

Some teas, like echinacea and sage, may have side effects or interact with medications. It's best to start with small amounts and monitor how your body reacts.

This will help you determine a safe and effective dose.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in shingles?

Herbal teas may help with shingles symptoms, but it's hard to predict exactly when they'll kick in.

Some people find relief within a few days, while others may take a week or two to notice a difference.

It really depends on the individual and the specific herbal tea being used.

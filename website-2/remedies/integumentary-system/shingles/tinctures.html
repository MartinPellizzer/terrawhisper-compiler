Shingles in Older Adults: 8 Remedies to Try

As people age, their immune systems weaken, making them more prone to illnesses like shingles.

Shingles is caused by the same virus that causes chickenpox, but it can appear years later in older adults. When shingles strikes, it can be very painful and uncomfortable. The rash can be itchy, burning, and blistering, and it can spread across the body. For older adults, shingles can be especially challenging because it can disrupt their daily routines and make everyday activities difficult. Simple tasks like getting out of bed, dressing, or even cooking can become a struggle. The pain and discomfort can also lead to anxiety, depression, and social isolation.

Older adults may feel like they're losing their independence and freedom. This can be a difficult and trying time for them and their loved ones. Older adults who suffer from shingles often turn to conventional treatments, but these can have side effects and may not be effective for everyone. That's why many are looking for alternative solutions, like herbal tinctures. These natural remedies can be made from various plant extracts and are often taken as liquid supplements. They can be very effective in reducing the pain, discomfort, and inflammation associated with shingles. They can also help to boost the immune system and promote healing.

By using herbal tinctures, older adults may be able to find relief from the symptoms of shingles and get back to their normal lives.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures, also known as ginger tincture, may help alleviate shingles symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties.

The main constituents of ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and chemicals that contribute to the pain and discomfort associated with shingles. This may help reduce the severity of symptoms like burning, itching, and pain. To try ginger tincture for shingles, mix 10-20 drops of the tincture in water and drink it 2-3 times a day.

You can also apply topically, diluted with a carrier oil, to the affected area. Be cautious, as ginger can cause stomach upset in high doses, so start with a small amount and gradually increase as needed. Some people may experience relief from shingles symptoms, such as reduced pain and inflammation, improved sleep, and a decrease in the frequency and severity of outbreaks.

However, results may vary, and it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using ginger tincture, especially if you're taking medications or have underlying health conditions.

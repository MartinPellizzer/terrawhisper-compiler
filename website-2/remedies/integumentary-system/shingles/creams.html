1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties that can help alleviate the symptoms of shingles. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea creams have been shown to reduce inflammation and modulate the immune system, which can help to ease the pain and discomfort associated with shingles. The glycosides in Echinacea purpurea creams also have antioxidant properties that can help to protect the skin from damage caused by the shingles virus.

By applying Echinacea purpurea creams to the affected area, you may be able to reduce the severity and duration of shingles symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of Echinacea purpurea tincture, 2 teaspoons of vitamin E oil, and 10 drops of tea tree oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water. Stir until melted and smooth.
  3. Add shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.
  4. Add Echinacea purpurea tincture, vitamin E oil, and tea tree oil to the mixture. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers or a tin. Let cool and harden completely before use. Store in a cool, dry place.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains active constituents like glycoproteins, vitamins A, C, and E, and aloin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can help with shingles. The antioxidant properties of vitamins A, C, and E can help protect the skin from damage and promote healing.

The cooling and soothing properties of aloe vera can help calm irritated skin and reduce discomfort associated with shingles.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 10 drops of tea tree oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or in a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix Aloe barbadensis gel, shea butter, and vitamin E oil until well combined.
  4. Slowly pour the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture into the Aloe gel mixture, stirring constantly until smooth and well combined.
  5. Add tea tree oil and mix well. Pour the mixture into small containers and let cool before use.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for shingles every day?

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, may help with shingles symptoms.

However, consuming them daily can have interactions with medications or worsen underlying conditions. It's essential to be cautious and monitor your body's response.

If you experience discomfort or side effects, consider reducing the frequency of consumption or trying alternative remedies.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in shingles?

Herbal teas can help with shingles symptoms, but it's hard to say exactly when you'll feel better.

Some people notice a difference within a few days, while others may take a week or two to start feeling relief from pain and discomfort.

It's different for everyone.

8 Natural Treatments in the Form of Herbal Creams for Older Adults with Shingles

As people age, their immune system weakens, making them more susceptible to various health issues, including shingles.

Shingles is a painful rash caused by the reactivation of the chickenpox virus, which can strike at any time after a person has had chickenpox. Older adults are more likely to experience shingles due to their weakened immune system, and this can have a significant impact on their daily lives. The painful rash can make it difficult to sleep, eat, and even perform simple tasks, leading to feelings of frustration and isolation. The discomfort and pain can also lead to depression and anxiety, further affecting their mental and emotional well-being.

In severe cases, shingles can also cause long-term nerve damage, known as postherpetic neuralgia, which can persist for months or even years after the rash has healed. When older adults experience shingles, it's essential to find effective ways to manage the pain and discomfort. Herbal creams have been found to be beneficial in treating shingles in older adults. These creams typically contain anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can help reduce the pain and itching associated with shingles.

They can also help to promote healing and reduce the risk of complications.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams have been used to help soothe and heal shingles due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The creams contain triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are known for their ability to reduce redness, itching, and pain associated with shingles. To use Calendula officinalis cream for shingles, apply a thin layer directly to the affected area 2-3 times a day. You can also add it to a cool compress to help relieve itching and discomfort.

It's essential to clean and dry the skin before applying the cream to allow it to penetrate effectively. When you start using Calendula officinalis cream, you may experience a reduction in the severity of symptoms, such as reduced redness and itching. You may also find that the skin begins to heal faster, and the discomfort associated with shingles starts to subside. However, it's crucial to note that Calendula officinalis cream is not a substitute for medical treatment.

If you're experiencing severe symptoms or if shingles is not improving with home treatment, consult a healthcare professional for further guidance.

2. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum creams, also known as St. John's Wort creams, may help with shingles symptoms due to their anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.

These creams typically contain constituents like hyperforin and hypericin, which have been shown to reduce pain, itching, and inflammation. To use Hypericum perforatum creams for shingles, apply a thin layer to the affected skin 2-3 times a day. Gently massage the cream into the skin until it's fully absorbed.

Be sure to wash your hands after applying the cream to avoid transferring it to other areas of your body. Using Hypericum perforatum creams for shingles may lead to a reduction in pain and itching, as well as a decrease in the duration of the shingles rash. Some people may also experience a faster healing time and a reduced risk of postherpetic neuralgia, a complication of shingles that can cause chronic pain. However, it's essential to note that everyone's body is different, and individual results may vary.

As with any new treatment, consult with a healthcare professional before using Hypericum perforatum creams, especially if you're already taking medications or have underlying health conditions.

3. Aloe vera

Aloe vera creams have been shown to help alleviate the pain and discomfort associated with shingles.

The gel inside the aloe vera plant contains properties like vitamin E, vitamin C, and beta-carotene, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and soothing effects. These constituents can help reduce the severity of shingles symptoms, including pain, itching, and redness. When using aloe vera creams for shingles, it's recommended to apply the cream directly to the affected area 2-3 times a day.

You can also take a warm bath with aloe vera gel or add it to your ointments and creams. However, be sure to consult with your doctor or healthcare provider before trying any new remedies, especially if you're taking any medications or have sensitive skin. Applying aloe vera creams may lead to a reduction in pain and discomfort, allowing you to move around more easily and manage daily activities. You may also notice a decrease in itching and redness, promoting faster healing and reduced scarring.

While aloe vera creams can provide relief, it's essential to note that they may not eliminate the shingles rash completely, and further medical attention may be necessary to manage the condition.

When applied topically as a cream, these compounds may help soothe and calm the affected skin, reducing discomfort and promoting healing. If you're considering using Echinacea purpurea cream for shingles, here's what you can do: first, consult with a healthcare professional to discuss the potential benefits and risks. Once you have the green light, apply a thin layer of the cream to the affected area 2-3 times a day, gently massaging it into the skin.

Be sure to follow the product's instructions and start with a small patch test to ensure you're not allergic to any ingredients. Using Echinacea purpurea cream for shingles may lead to a reduction in pain and discomfort, improved skin healing, and a shorter duration of symptoms. Some users may also experience reduced inflammation, itching, and blistering. However, it's essential to note that everyone's body is different, and results may vary.

It's also crucial to maintain open communication with your healthcare provider, as they can monitor your progress and adjust your treatment plan as needed.

Melaleuca alternifolia creams have been used to help with shingles symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.

The creams contain a compound called tea tree oil, which is rich in cinnamaldehyde, eucalyptol, and other constituents that help soothe and calm the skin. When using Melaleuca alternifolia creams for shingles, it's recommended to apply a thin layer to the affected area 2-3 times a day. Applying the cream or oil may help reduce the severity and duration of shingles symptoms, such as pain, itching, and rash. Aloe barbadensis creams have been used for centuries to help alleviate the symptoms of shingles, a painful rash caused by the herpes zoster virus.

The cream's effectiveness can be attributed to its rich content of vitamins A, C, and E, as well as minerals like calcium and potassium. The gel of the Aloe barbadensis plant also contains anti-inflammatory compounds like aloin and aloe-emodin, which help to reduce redness and swelling associated with shingles. The cream's soothing properties also make it an effective remedy for itching and burning sensations. If you're experiencing shingles, try applying Aloe barbadensis cream directly to the affected area 2-3 times a day. You can also add a few drops of tea tree oil, which has antimicrobial properties, to enhance the cream's benefits.

Be sure to clean and dry the skin before applying the cream to prevent any irritation. It's also essential to consult with your doctor before using Aloe barbadensis cream, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or are taking medications. Applying Aloe barbadensis cream may lead to a reduction in pain and inflammation, as well as a decrease in the severity of shingles symptoms. You may also notice an improvement in the appearance of the rash, with reduced redness and swelling. In some cases, the cream may also help to speed up the healing process, allowing you to recover from shingles more quickly.

However, it's essential to note that Aloe barbadensis cream is not a cure for shingles, and you should continue to follow your doctor's advice for treatment.

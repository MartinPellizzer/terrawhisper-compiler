11 Herbal Teas For Shingles

Herbal teas can be a great help when it comes to managing shingles.

Ginger has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can ease the burning and itching sensations associated with shingles. Lavandula angustifolia, also known as lavender, is another herb that can be used to help manage shingles symptoms.

Drinking herbal teas can also help you feel better in other ways. For example, ginger tea can help ease nausea, which is a common symptom of shingles. Echinacea tea can help boost your mood and reduce stress, which can be a big help when you're not feeling well. And lavender tea can help you relax and get a good night's sleep, which is essential for recovery. Herbal teas are also a natural and chemical-free way to manage shingles symptoms, which can be especially helpful if you're sensitive to medication.

They're also easy to make at home, which can be a cost-effective option.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains active constituents like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help reduce pain and inflammation in the body, which is beneficial for people suffering from shingles. Gingerol and shogaol also have analgesic and antispasmodic properties, which help alleviate nerve pain and muscle spasms associated with shingles. The anti-inflammatory properties of these constituents can also help reduce the severity and duration of shingles symptoms.

By drinking Zingiber officinale teas, individuals may experience relief from shingles symptoms due to its ability to reduce pain, inflammation, and discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried ginger root, 1 cup of boiling water, and a strainer.
  2. Add the ginger root to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup and discard the ginger root.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve shingles symptoms.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains the bioactive constituents alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties that help reduce the severity and duration of shingles symptoms. Alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to stimulate the immune system, which is essential for fighting off the varicella-zoster virus that causes shingles. Glycosides, on the other hand, have antioxidant properties that help protect the skin and reduce the risk of scarring.

By consuming Echinacea purpurea teas, individuals may experience relief from shingles symptoms, such as pain, itching, and inflammation.

  1. Gather 2 teaspoons of dried Echinacea purpurea root and flowers.
  2. Combine the root and flowers in a small bowl.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the mixture in a cup.
  4. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then strain into another cup.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of the tea per day, as needed for shingles symptoms.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains a compound called linalool, which is a key active constituent that helps with shingles.

Linalool has anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties, reducing redness and swelling associated with shingles. Lavandula angustifolia tea also contains linalyl acetate, another active constituent that has a soothing effect on the skin and can help to reduce pain and discomfort. The tea's anti-inflammatory properties can also help to reduce the severity and duration of shingles outbreaks.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Lavandula angustifolia tea may provide relief from the symptoms of shingles.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of dried flowers to a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the flowers and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into another cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of the tea 2-3 times a day to help manage shingles symptoms.

5. Cinchona officinalis

6. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Glycyrrhizin is a triterpenoid saponin that has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These properties help to soothe and calm the skin, reducing pain and discomfort associated with shingles. Glycyrrhizin also has antiviral properties that can help to combat the herpes zoster virus that causes shingles.

The anti-inflammatory properties of glycyrrhizin can also help to reduce swelling and redness in the affected area, making it a potential treatment for shingles.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots. If using fresh, dry them in a low-temperature oven (150°F - 200°F) for 2-3 hours.
  2. Grind 1-2 tablespoons of the dried roots into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Heat 1 cup of water in a saucepan over medium heat.
  4. Add 1/2 to 1 teaspoon of the ground roots to the hot water and simmer for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea into a cup.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, ideally on an empty stomach, for 7-10 days. Monitor your symptoms and adjust the dosage as needed.

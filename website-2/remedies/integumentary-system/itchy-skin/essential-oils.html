Itchy Skin in Psoriasis? Try These 9 Herbal Essential Oils

People with psoriasis often deal with itchy skin, which can be really uncomfortable and disrupt their daily lives.

The itchy skin can be due to the inflammation and skin cell buildup that occurs in psoriasis. This can lead to scratching, which can further irritate the skin and even cause bleeding. As a result, people with psoriasis may feel embarrassed, self-conscious, and frustrated with their condition. They may avoid social situations or activities that they enjoy, or even feel isolated from others due to the constant itching and discomfort. The itchy skin can also keep them up at night, making it hard to get a good rest.

This can affect their mood, energy levels, and overall well-being. Herbal essential oils have been found to be beneficial in relieving itchy skin associated with psoriasis. These oils are extracted from plants and have natural anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. They can help calm the skin, reduce redness and irritation, and promote a sense of relaxation. When applied topically, these oils can penetrate deep into the skin, providing relief from itching and discomfort. They can also help reduce stress and anxiety, which can trigger or worsen psoriasis symptoms.

By incorporating herbal essential oils into their skincare routine, people with psoriasis may find some relief from itchy skin and be able to manage their symptoms more effectively.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils are a natural remedy for itchy skin, and they work well because of their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The main constituents of Calendula officinalis essential oils are triterpene saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which help to soothe and calm irritated skin. When you apply Calendula officinalis essential oils to itchy skin, the triterpene saponins work to reduce inflammation and itching, while the flavonoids help to repair damaged skin cells. The carotenoids, on the other hand, have antioxidant properties that help to protect the skin from further damage. To use Calendula officinalis essential oils for itchy skin, mix a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and gently massage it into the affected area.

You can also add a few drops to your bath water for a relaxing and soothing soak. It's essential to do a patch test before using Calendula officinalis essential oils on a large area, as some people may be sensitive to the oil. If you take action and use Calendula officinalis essential oils as directed, you can expect to see a reduction in itching and inflammation within a few hours. The oil may also help to heal minor cuts and scrapes, and promote the growth of new skin cells. As the inflammation subsides, you may also notice a decrease in redness and swelling.

However, if your itchy skin persists or worsens, you should consult a healthcare professional for further advice.

When you take action with Aloe barbadensis essential oils, you can expect to see a reduction in itching and inflammation, and possibly even some improvement in the appearance of the skin.

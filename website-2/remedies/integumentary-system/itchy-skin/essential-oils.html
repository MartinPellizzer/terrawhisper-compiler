1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help soothe itchy skin. The flavonoids, particularly kaempferol and quercetin, have potent antioxidant effects that reduce inflammation and promote skin healing. The triterpenoid saponins also exhibit anti-inflammatory activity, which helps calm irritated skin.

The combination of these properties in Calendula officinalis essential oils makes it a popular natural remedy for itchy skin conditions.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula officinalis flowers and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the dried flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Place the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the new essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight lid.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds like cineole, linalool, and terpinen-4-ol.

These bioactive constituents have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe itchy skin. Cineole, in particular, has been shown to reduce redness and swelling, while terpinen-4-ol has been found to inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi that can irritate the skin. The anti-inflammatory effects of linalool also help to calm irritated skin and reduce discomfort.

The combination of these properties makes Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils a popular natural remedy for itchy skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat, then add the leaves. Simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it sit for 30 minutes to allow the leaves to infuse.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit at room temperature for 2-3 days to allow the essential oil to separate and rise to the top.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, its main active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which help soothe itchy skin. The antiseptic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate reduce inflammation and prevent infection, promoting healing of the skin. Additionally, the calming effects of these compounds can help reduce irritation and discomfort associated with itchy skin.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infection, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help alleviate itchy skin issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat, then add the lavender flowers and let it simmer for 30-40 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a heat-resistant bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Let the liquid cool, then transfer it to a dark glass bottle. Add 10-15 drops of a natural preservative like vitamin E oil.
  5. Store the essential oil in a cool, dark place. To use, dilute 5-7 drops of the oil with 1 tablespoon of carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil and apply to itchy skin.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce redness and swelling on itchy skin. Aloe-emodin also has antimicrobial properties that prevent infection and promote healing. Acemannan stimulates the production of growth factors, which help repair damaged skin tissue.

The moisturizing properties of aloe barbadensis essential oils soothe and calm itchy skin, providing relief from irritation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Wash the Aloe leaves with water and pat them dry.
  3. Extract the gel from the Aloe leaves and mix it with the carrier oil in a bowl.
  4. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the bowl every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth and bottle the extracted oil. Use 2-3 drops of the oil on itchy skin.

5. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica essential oils contains bioactive constituents like histamine, acetylcholine, and other compounds that have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These properties help to reduce redness and swelling associated with itchy skin, making it a popular natural remedy for skin irritations. The essential oils also contain antioxidants like ascorbic acid and flavonoids, which help to neutralize free radicals and promote healing. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of Urtica dioica essential oils help to reduce the production of histamine, a chemical that can exacerbate itchy skin.

By applying Urtica dioica essential oils topically, you can take advantage of its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties to calm itchy skin and promote healthy skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Urtica dioica leaves, 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil), and a glass bottle with a lid.
  2. Chop the fresh Urtica dioica leaves into small pieces and fill a 1-pint jar about 1/4 full with the chopped leaves.
  3. Pour 2 cups of carrier oil over the chopped leaves in the jar, making sure the leaves are completely covered.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into the glass bottle.
  5. Discard the solids and use the Urtica dioica essential oil as needed for itchy skin. Store the oil in a cool, dark place.

6. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum essential oils contains a high concentration of boiactive constituents, including flavonoids, naphthodianthrone derivatives, and phenolic acids.

The flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce redness and swelling associated with itchy skin. The naphthodianthrone derivatives, particularly hyperforin, have antioxidant properties that help protect the skin from damage and promote healing. The phenolic acids, including ferulic acid, have a soothing effect on the skin, which can help calm itchy skin irritations.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Hypericum perforatum essential oils can provide relief for itchy skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Place the flowers in a clean glass jar and pour the carrier oil over them. Stir well.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day. This helps the oil extract from the flowers.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the flowers.
  5. Your Hypericum perforatum essential oil is now ready to use. Use 5-7 drops in a bath or mix with a carrier oil for itchy skin relief.

7. Chamomilla recutita

Chamomilla recutita essential oils contains apigenin and luteolin, which are two active constituents that have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These properties help to calm and reduce the redness of itchy skin, providing relief from discomfort. The essential oil's anti-irritant properties also help to calm the skin's reaction to allergens or irritants, reducing the urge to scratch. Additionally, chamazulene in the essential oil has anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to reduce swelling and discomfort associated with itchy skin.

By using Chamomilla recutita essential oils, individuals can experience a reduction in itchy skin symptoms and find relief from discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Chamomilla recutita flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat, then add the flowers and let it simmer for 2 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, squeezing the flowers to get all the liquid out.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a still or a double boiler and heat it until the water evaporates, leaving behind the essential oil.
  5. Bottle the essential oil in a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dry place.

8. Aloe vera

Aloe vera essential oils contains compounds like aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-annone, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing effects that help to calm itchy skin. The aloe-emodin in aloe vera essential oils has been shown to have a direct effect on the skin, reducing inflammation and irritation. The anti-inflammatory properties of aloin also help to reduce redness and itching, providing relief from itchy skin conditions.

By reducing inflammation and soothing the skin, aloe vera essential oils helps to provide quick relief from itchy skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe vera gel, 1/4 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil), and a small bowl.
  2. Combine Aloe vera gel and carrier oil in the bowl. Mix well.
  3. Add 10 drops of vitamin E oil and 10 drops of tea tree oil to the mixture. Mix well.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a clean glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.
  5. Label the bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops of the oil on itchy skin as needed.

9. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole, a key bioactive constituent that helps soothe itchy skin.

Cineole has anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce redness and swelling associated with skin irritations. Eucalyptus globulus essential oils also contains alpha-pinene, a compound that helps to calm and relax the skin, reducing the urge to scratch. The antiseptic properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils help to prevent infections that can exacerbate itchiness.

The refreshing and cooling sensation of eucalyptus globulus essential oils can also help to calm itchy skin and promote a sense of relief.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a large pot, a strainer, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Combine the Eucalyptus leaves and 2 cups of water in the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through the strainer into a bowl. Discard the leaves and let the liquid cool.
  4. Transfer the cooled liquid to a clean glass bottle. Mix in 1/2 cup of carrier oil. Stir well.
  5. Store the Eucalyptus essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops on itchy skin as needed, mixed with a carrier oil for safe application.

1. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica tinctures contains active constituents like histamine, serotonin, and quercetin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties that help reduce itching and swelling. Histamine is a natural chemical that can block the action of histamine receptors, which are responsible for itching sensations. Quercetin, an antioxidant, reduces inflammation and relieves itching caused by allergic reactions.

The combined effect of these compounds in Urtica dioica tinctures helps to alleviate itchy skin conditions.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Urtica dioica leaves and flowers. Clean them with water.
  2. Combine the leaves and flowers with 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Label the jar with the date and the words 'Urtica dioica tincture'. Store it in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops under the tongue as needed.

2. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum tinctures contains bioactive constituents like hyperforin and hypericin, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties.

These compounds help to reduce itching and inflammation in the skin by blocking the release of histamine, a chemical that causes allergic reactions. The flavonoids and phenolic acids in Hypericum perforatum tinctures also have antioxidant properties, which can help to soothe and calm irritated skin. By reducing inflammation and blocking histamine release, Hypericum perforatum tinctures can provide relief from itchy skin.

The anti-inflammatory properties of hyperforin and hypericin also help to speed up the healing process of skin irritations.

  1. Gather 250ml of 80% Vodka and 50g of dried Hypericum perforatum.
  2. Place the dried Hypericum perforatum in a clean glass jar.
  3. Pour 250ml of 80% Vodka over the dried Hypericum perforatum in the jar.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool dark place for 2-6 weeks, shaking the jar every week.
  5. After 2-6 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.

3. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids.

These bioactive constituents work together to reduce inflammation and fight off infections that can cause itchy skin. The flavonoids, particularly rutin and kaempferol, have antihistamine and anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe itchy skin and reduce redness. By reducing inflammation and fighting off infections, Calendula officinalis tinctures can help relieve itchy skin and promote healthy skin healing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Calendula officinalis flowers and 2 cups of 80% vodka.

  5. Store the Calendula tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, to relieve itchy skin.

10 Herbal Creams For Itchy Skin

People with psoriasis often experience itchy skin, which can be really uncomfortable and affect their daily lives.

The itchy skin can be caused by the body's immune system attacking healthy skin cells, leading to inflammation and flaking skin. This can make it hard for people with psoriasis to sleep, work, or even enjoy simple activities. The itching can be so intense that it leads to scratching, which can cause more damage to the skin and make it harder to treat.

This can create a cycle of discomfort and frustration, making it challenging for people with psoriasis to manage their symptoms and feel confident in their own skin. When it comes to treating itchy skin in psoriasis patients, topical creams can be a helpful option. These creams are applied directly to the skin and contain soothing ingredients that can help calm inflammation and reduce itching. Some creams contain plant-based extracts that have anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties, which can help to calm the skin and reduce the urge to scratch.

By applying these creams regularly, people with psoriasis may be able to find some relief from itchy skin and improve their overall skin health.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids that have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These constituents help to soothe and calm itchy skin by reducing redness and swelling. The flavonoids, in particular, have been shown to have a potent antioxidant effect, which helps to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals. If you're experiencing itchy skin, try applying a Calendula officinalis cream to the affected area 2-3 times a day.

Gently massage the cream into the skin until it's fully absorbed. You can also add a few drops of a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil to enhance the cream's penetration and moisturizing effects. Taking action with Calendula officinalis cream may lead to a reduction in itching and inflammation, as well as an improvement in skin tone and texture. You may notice that the redness and swelling in the affected area start to subside, and the skin begins to feel smoother and more comfortable.

In some cases, the itching may completely stop, or at least become less intense, allowing you to get a good night's sleep or go about your daily activities without discomfort.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent itchy skin from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe itchy skin due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Certain herbs like chamomile, calendula, and peppermint have been known to calm irritated skin and reduce itching.

These teas can be consumed hot or cold, and some people find them to be a natural remedy for relieving itchiness.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for itchy skin every day?

Consuming herbal teas daily for itchy skin can be safe, but it's essential to choose teas with ingredients that are known to help with skin issues.

Look for teas with soothing herbs like chamomile, calendula, or licorice root.

Some herbal teas may interact with medications or have side effects, so be sure to check the ingredients and follow the recommended dosage.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in itchy skin?

Herbal teas can help with itchy skin, but it depends on the type of tea and how sensitive you are.

Some people notice relief within a few hours or days after drinking tea like calendula or oat straw, while others might feel better after a week or two.

It's different for everyone.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for itchy skin?

For itchy skin, try drinking herbal tea in the evening.

This is because many herbal teas, like chamomile and calendula, can help you relax and sleep better. When you're relaxed, your skin can heal faster.

Drinking it in the evening may also help you get a good night's sleep, which is essential for skin health.

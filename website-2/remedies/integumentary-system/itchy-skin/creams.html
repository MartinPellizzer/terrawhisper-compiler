1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to soothe itchy skin. Calendula's triterpenoids, such as oleanolic acid and ursolic acid, have been shown to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. The flavonoids present in Calendula officinalis, including quercetin and kaempferol, have potent antioxidant effects that can help to calm irritated skin.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Calendula officinalis creams can provide relief from itchy skin by addressing its underlying causes.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Make Calendula infusion: Steep 1 cup of Calendula flowers in 2 cups of boiling water for 10-15 minutes. Strain and let it cool.
  3. Mix oil and butter: Combine 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of beeswax in a small saucepan.
  4. Melt and combine: Melt the oil and butter mixture over low heat. Add the cooled Calendula infusion and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil. Stir well.
  5. Pour and set: Pour the mixture into small jars or containers. Let it cool and set at room temperature. Your Calendula cream is ready to use.

2. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica creams contains bioactive constituents like histamine, leukotrienes, and quercetin that help soothe itchy skin.

These compounds work by reducing inflammation and blocking the production of chemical mediators that cause itching. Histamine and leukotrienes, naturally present in Urtica dioica, have anti-inflammatory properties that help calm irritated skin. Quercetin, a powerful antioxidant, also contributes to the reduction of inflammation and itching.

When applied topically, the bioactive constituents in Urtica dioica creams can help alleviate itchy skin by addressing the underlying causes of inflammation and itching.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Urtica dioica leaves, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Make a tea by steeping 1 cup of dried Urtica dioica leaves in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Let it cool.
  3. In a small saucepan, melt the coconut oil and beeswax over low heat. Stir until the beeswax is fully melted.
  4. Add the cooled Urtica dioica tea, shea butter, and vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a glass jar and let it cool and solidify. Store the Urtica dioica cream in a cool place and use it on itchy skin as needed.

3. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and beta-pinene.

These bioactive constituents have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to soothe and calm itchy skin. The antiseptic properties of cineole reduce the risk of infection, while the anti-inflammatory properties of limonene and beta-pinene reduce redness and swelling. These properties work together to provide fast and effective relief from itchy skin.

The skin-healing properties of these compounds help to speed up the healing process and leave skin feeling smoother and healthier.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 10 drops of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil.
  2. Melt beeswax and coconut oil in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each heating.
  3. Add shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Slowly add distilled water to the mixture, stirring constantly to avoid separation.
  5. Once the mixture has cooled slightly, add Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil and mix well. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool completely before use.

5. Arnica montana

Arnica montana creams contains the bioactive constituents thymoquinone, flavonoids, and sesquiterpene lactones.

  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water until it reaches 160°F (71°C).
  3. Add the dried Arnica montana flowers to the coconut oil and let it infuse for 2-3 hours or overnight. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix 1 tablespoon of beeswax and 1 tablespoon of shea butter until they are well combined.
  5. Add the infused Arnica montana liquid to the beeswax and shea butter mixture and stir until it forms a smooth cream. Pour the cream into a jar and let it cool before using.

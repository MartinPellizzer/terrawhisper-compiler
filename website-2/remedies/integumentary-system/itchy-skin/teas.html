9 Herbal Teas to Provide Relief from Itchy Skin in Atopic Dermatitis Patients

Atopic dermatitis, also known as eczema, is a condition that makes people's skin very itchy and inflamed.

This happens because their skin doesn't work properly to keep out allergens and irritants. When these allergens and irritants get into the skin, they cause the skin to become inflamed and itchy, making it hard to scratch and causing discomfort and pain. For people with atopic dermatitis, itchy skin can disrupt their daily lives, making it hard to sleep, work, and even enjoy activities they love. The constant itching can also lead to skin infections, scarring, and emotional distress.

It's not just a physical issue, but also a mental one, as people with atopic dermatitis often feel embarrassed, anxious, and frustrated by their condition. As a result, people with atopic dermatitis are always looking for new ways to soothe and calm their itchy skin. While there are many treatments available, such as creams and ointments, some people are turning to herbal teas as a natural and complementary remedy. Herbal teas, made from the leaves, flowers, and roots of plants, can help to reduce inflammation and itching in the skin. They can also promote relaxation and calmness, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety.

When consumed regularly, these teas can help to reduce the frequency and severity of itchy skin episodes, making it easier for people with atopic dermatitis to manage their condition and improve their quality of life.

1. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica teas, also known as nettle tea, has been used for centuries to help alleviate itchy skin.

The reason it works is due to the presence of anti-inflammatory compounds like flavonoids and phenolic acids, which help reduce swelling and ease the itch. The tea also contains vitamins and minerals like vitamin C, iron, and potassium, which can help calm the skin and reduce irritation. To use nettle tea for itchy skin, you can try making a tea by steeping dried nettle leaves in hot water. Then, soak a cloth in the tea and apply it directly to the affected area.

Alternatively, you can drink the tea as a warm beverage. Some people also add the tea to their bath water for added relief. When you apply the nettle tea to itchy skin, you may notice a decrease in itching and inflammation within a few hours. The tea's anti-inflammatory properties can also help reduce redness and swelling, making the skin look and feel better. In some cases, people have reported relief from itchy skin within 24 hours of starting treatment.

However, it's essential to note that results may vary from person to person, and more research is needed to fully understand the effects of nettle tea on itchy skin.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas have been used for centuries to soothe itchy skin due to their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The tea contains flavonoids, triterpenoids, and carotenoids, which work together to reduce redness and swelling. These compounds also have antimicrobial properties, helping to fight off infections that can cause itchiness. When using Calendula officinalis teas for itchy skin, it's recommended to steep one teaspoon of dried flowers in boiling water for five to seven minutes, then strain and let it cool.

You can apply the cooled tea as a compress or add it to your bath water. As the tea is absorbed into the skin, you may notice a reduction in itchiness and inflammation within a few hours. The tea may also help to promote healing and prevent further irritation.

However, it's essential to note that Calendula officinalis teas may not work for everyone, and if your itchiness persists or worsens, you should consult a healthcare professional for further advice.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas have been used for centuries to help soothe itchy skin.

The reason is that Aloe barbadensis contains powerful anti-inflammatory compounds like aloin and aloe-emodin, which can calm and reduce redness. It also contains vitamins A, C, and E, as well as beta-carotene, which can help protect the skin from damage and promote healing. When you drink Aloe barbadensis tea, these beneficial compounds are absorbed into your bloodstream and can help reduce inflammation and itching from the inside out. To get the most out of Aloe barbadensis teas for itchy skin, try drinking a cup of the tea 2-3 times a day. You can make the tea by steeping 1-2 tablespoons of dried Aloe barbadensis leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it while it's still warm. Some people also find it helpful to apply aloe vera gel topically to the affected area after drinking the tea, as this can help soothe and calm the skin even more. When you start drinking Aloe barbadensis tea for itchy skin, you may notice a reduction in itching and inflammation within a few days. Your skin may start to feel cooler and less sensitive, and the redness may begin to fade. Some people also find that their skin heals faster and looks clearer after drinking the tea regularly.

However, it's essential to note that everyone's skin is different, and it may take some trial and error to find the right amount and frequency of Aloe barbadensis tea that works for you.

4. Aloe vera

Aloe vera teas have been traditionally used to soothe itchy skin, and for good reason.

The gel of the aloe vera plant contains compounds like aloin and aloe-emodin, which have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that help reduce redness and swelling. It also contains vitamins A, C, and E, which act as antioxidants to protect the skin from damage. When you drink aloe vera tea, these properties are absorbed into the bloodstream and then delivered to the skin, where they can help calm itchy irritation. 5. Avena sativa

Avena sativa teas, also known as oat tea, have been traditionally used to help soothe itchy skin.

The tea's anti-inflammatory properties, particularly a type of fiber called beta-glucan, can help reduce inflammation and itching. Avena sativa contains various constituents, including avenanthramides, phenolic compounds, and saponins, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. To use Avena sativa tea for itchy skin, steep one teaspoon of dried oat straw in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain and let it cool before applying it to the affected area as a compress or bath. You can also drink the tea as a warm beverage. When taken as a compress or bath, the tea's anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce itching and inflammation, allowing the skin to heal faster. Drinking the tea may also help to reduce inflammation and itching from the inside out.

However, if your itchy skin persists or worsens, you should consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

6. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum teas, also known as St. John's Wort tea, has been used for centuries to soothe itchy skin.

The tea contains properties and constituents like hyperforin, hypericin, and flavonoids that have anti-inflammatory and antihistamine effects. These compounds help reduce redness, swelling, and itching caused by skin irritations, allergies, or bug bites. The flavonoids in St. John's Wort tea also have antioxidant properties that protect the skin from damage. To use Hypericum perforatum teas for itchy skin, steep one teaspoon of dried St. John's Wort flowers in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day.

You can also apply a cooled tea compress or bath to the affected area for 15-20 minutes. For more severe itchy skin conditions, consult a healthcare professional before using St. John's Wort tea as a treatment. When you start drinking St. John's Wort tea, you may notice a decrease in itching and inflammation within a few days. The tea may help alleviate symptoms associated with eczema, dermatitis, or hives. However, if you have sensitive skin or allergies, start with a small dose and monitor your body's response.

As with any herbal remedy, consult a healthcare professional before using St. John's Wort tea, especially if you're taking prescription medications or have underlying health conditions.

7. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis teas, which include black, green, white, and oolong tea, have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help with itchy skin.

The leaves of the Camellia sinensis plant contain flavonoids, such as quercetin and catechins, and other compounds like theaflavins and thearubigins. When using Camellia sinensis teas for itchy skin, you may notice a reduction in inflammation and redness within a few hours. However, results may vary depending on the severity of the itchy skin and individual tolerance.

8. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, can help with itchy skin due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties.

The active constituents in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, work by reducing inflammation and blocking the release of histamine, a chemical that causes itching. These properties make ginger tea a potential natural remedy for itchy skin conditions like eczema, psoriasis, and hives. If you're experiencing itchy skin, you can try making a ginger tea by steeping fresh ginger in hot water for 5-7 minutes. You can also add honey or lemon to taste.

To get the most out of ginger tea, it's recommended to drink it 2-3 times a day, ideally after meals. Some people may also apply ginger paste or oil directly to the affected area, but be sure to do a patch test first to check for any allergic reactions. As you start drinking ginger tea, you may notice a reduction in the intensity and frequency of itchy skin episodes. Your skin may feel cooler and less inflamed, and the itching may become more manageable.

In some cases, ginger tea may even help to clear up existing skin conditions, but this may take longer and require consistent use over several weeks.

Echinacea purpurea teas have been traditionally used to help alleviate itchy skin, and research suggests that it may be effective due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

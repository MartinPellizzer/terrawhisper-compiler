What Are The Main Causes Of Dark Lips?

Dark lips can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is sun exposure. When your lips are exposed to direct sunlight, the UV rays can damage the skin and cause the blood vessels to break down, leading to a loss of melanin and a decrease in blood flow. This can cause your lips to become dark and dry. Smoking is another cause of dark lips.

When you smoke, the chemicals in cigarettes can reduce blood flow to your lips, making them darker and more prone to drying out. This is because the chemicals in cigarettes damage the tiny blood vessels in your lips, reducing the amount of oxygen and nutrients they receive. Genetic predisposition is also a cause of dark lips. If your parents or grandparents had dark lips, you may be more likely to have them too.

This is because some people are naturally more prone to certain traits, such as dark lips, due to their genetic makeup.

Below are the main causes of dark lips.

  • Sun Exposure: Prolonged sun exposure can cause dark lips due to the formation of melanin, a natural pigment that protects the skin from the sun's damaging rays, but when it accumulates on the lips, it causes them to darken.
  • Smoking: Smoking causes dark lips because the chemicals in cigarette smoke, such as nicotine and tar, damage the skin and reduce blood flow to the lips, causing them to become discolored and darker.
  • Genetic Predisposition: Genetic predisposition is a cause of dark lips because some people's genes may make their skin and lips produce more melanin, a substance that gives color to skin and lips, resulting in darker-colored lips.
  • Lip Licking: Lip licking causes dark lips because the moisture from saliva makes the melanin in the skin on your lips to spread out and become more visible, making them appear darker.
  • Bleeding Gums: Bleeding Gums, which refers to gum disease or inflammation, can cause dark lips because the infection and inflammation in the gums can spread to the surrounding skin and cause blood vessels to become visible, leading to a dark discoloration on the lips.

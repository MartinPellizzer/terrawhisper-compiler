7 Herbal Creams For Frizzy Hair

Herbal creams for frizzy hair are a natural way to tame unruly locks.

These creams often contain herbs that have been used for centuries to soothe and calm hair. Aloe barbadensis, also known as aloe vera, is one such herb. It's rich in vitamins and minerals that help lock in moisture, reducing frizz and flyaways. When applied to the hair, aloe vera creates a barrier that protects it from environmental stressors, keeping it smooth and manageable. Another herb found in some frizz-fighting creams is Cymbopogon citratus, also known as lemongrass. This herb has antifungal and antibacterial properties that help control scalp irritations, which can lead to frizzy hair. By reducing scalp inflammation, lemongrass helps create a healthy environment for hair growth and reduces the appearance of frizz.

Rosemary essential oil, extracted from Rosmarinus officinalis, is another popular ingredient in herbal hair creams. It improves circulation to the scalp, which helps to distribute natural oils and reduce frizz. Rosemary also has a natural antiseptic and antioxidant properties that help protect the hair from damage caused by free radicals. Using an herbal cream for frizzy hair can bring numerous benefits to your life. For one, it's a natural and chemical-free way to manage your hair. This is especially important for those with sensitive scalps or allergies. Additionally, these creams often smell wonderful, leaving your hair and scalp feeling fresh and clean.

By using herbal creams, you can say goodbye to frizzy hair and hello to a more manageable and healthy-looking mane.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A, C, and E. These ingredients help to lock in moisture and reduce frizz by forming a protective barrier on the hair surface.

Aloin also has anti-inflammatory properties that help to calm irritated hair cuticles and prevent breakage. Aloe-emodin has antioxidant properties that help to protect hair from environmental damage and promote healthy hair growth.

By reducing frizz and protecting hair from damage, Aloe barbadensis creams can help to leave hair looking smooth and healthy.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel and 1/2 cup of coconut oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil in a small saucepan over low heat.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax to the melted coconut oil and stir until combined.
  4. Remove from heat and mix in the Aloe barbadensis gel until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify before use.

2. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus creams contains limonene, geraniol, and linalool as its active constituents.

These compounds have moisturizing and hydrating properties, which help to lock in moisture and reduce frizz. Limonene also has a calming effect on the hair cuticle, smoothing out the surface and reducing roughness. Geraniol and linalool work together to regulate the hair's natural moisture balance, preventing dryness and frizz.

By using Cymbopogon citratus creams, you can help to tame and smooth out your frizzy hair, leaving it looking healthier and more manageable.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, and 1 tablespoon of beeswax in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in the double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of Cymbopogon citratus essential oil to the melted mixture and stir well.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool until it reaches a thick, creamy consistency.
  5. Pour the cooled mixture into a container and use it as a hair cream to help reduce frizz and add shine to your hair.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, a powerful antioxidant that helps smooth and calm frizzy hair.

The herb also contains camphor and bornyl acetate, which have antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties that prevent damage to the hair follicles and scalp. These properties help to reduce frizz by controlling the moisture levels in the hair and preventing it from becoming too dry or oily. The rosemary essential oil in Rosmarinus officinalis creams also helps to improve blood circulation to the scalp, promoting healthy hair growth and reducing the appearance of frizz.

By using Rosmarinus officinalis creams, you can help to tame and smooth your frizzy hair, giving it a more polished and manageable look.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 10 drops of Rosemary essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in vitamin E oil and Rosemary essential oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container, let it cool and solidify, then use as a hair cream to help tame frizzy hair.

4. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains active constituents like cineole and terpineol, which are known for their ability to help smooth and tame frizzy hair.

These compounds have been shown to enhance hair's natural moisture balance and reduce the appearance of frizz. The anti-inflammatory properties of Melaleuca alternifolia, particularly those of terpineol, help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and promoting a healthy environment for hair growth. By reducing inflammation and maintaining moisture levels, Melaleuca alternifolia creams can help to reduce frizz and leave hair looking smoother and more manageable.

Cineole, a key constituent of Melaleuca alternifolia, also has antioxidant properties, which can help to protect hair from environmental stressors and damage.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 teaspoons of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil.
  2. Melt beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water. Add coconut oil and shea butter. Stir until melted and smooth.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from heat. Add distilled water and stir well. The mixture should cool down and thicken slightly.
  4. Add Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil and stir well. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool completely.
  5. Once cooled, use a hand mixer or whisk to whip the cream until it becomes light and fluffy. Apply to frizzy hair to help tame and smooth it.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and camphor as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds help to calm and soothe the scalp, reducing inflammation that can cause frizz. The antifungal properties of linalool and camphor also prevent the growth of yeast on the scalp, which can contribute to frizzy hair. Linalyl acetate, on the other hand, has a smoothing effect on the hair cuticle, helping to reduce frizz and add shine.

By using Lavandula angustifolia creams, you can help to regulate the moisture balance of your hair, leading to a smoother, more manageable texture.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of honey, and 10 drops of Lavandula angustifolia essential oil.
  2. Combine the distilled water, coconut oil, and shea butter in a small saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring occasionally, until the shea butter melts.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool slightly. Stir in the honey until it dissolves.
  4. Add the Lavandula angustifolia essential oil to the mixture and stir well. Pour the mixture into a clean glass jar or container.
  5. Let the mixture cool and thicken before applying it to your hair. Use as a leave-in conditioner or a pre-shampoo treatment to help smooth and calm frizzy hair.

6. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains compounds like eucalyptol, alpha-pinene, and beta-pinene.

These constituents help to smooth and calm frizzy hair by reducing moisture levels in the hair shaft. Eucalyptol has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe the scalp and reduce irritation that can contribute to frizz. The antiseptic properties of alpha-pinene and beta-pinene also help to remove impurities from the hair and scalp, promoting a healthy environment that reduces frizz.

By controlling moisture and reducing irritation, Eucalyptus globulus creams can help to keep hair smooth and manageable.

  2. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax to the melted coconut oil and stir until dissolved.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix 2 tablespoons of eucalyptus globulus essential oil with 2 tablespoons of jojoba oil.
  4. Combine the eucalyptus mixture with the coconut-beeswax mixture and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a small jar and let it cool and solidify before use as a hair cream to help control frizz.

7. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare creams contains anise oil, which is rich in anethole, a major bioactive constituent that helps to tame frizzy hair.

Anethole has antiseptic and antifungal properties that reduce inflammation and soothe the scalp, creating a healthy environment for hair growth. The oil's emollient properties help to lock in moisture, smoothing the hair cuticle and reducing frizz. Additionally, anethole has antioxidant properties that protect the hair from damage caused by free radicals, leaving it looking smooth and shiny.

By using Foeniculum vulgare creams, you can help to control frizz and achieve a sleek, healthy-looking hairstyle.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of dried Foeniculum vulgare (fennel seeds)
  2. Steep the fennel seeds in the distilled water for 2 hours, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the coconut oil and shea butter until well combined
  4. Add the fennel-infused water to the coconut oil mixture and stir until smooth
  5. Pour the mixture into a jar and store it in the fridge for up to 2 weeks; apply to frizzy hair as a leave-in conditioner

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent frizzy hair from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent frizzy hair from forming because some herbs, like rosemary and sage, stimulate blood flow to the scalp.

This increased circulation could help to keep hair follicles healthy and strong, reducing the likelihood of frizz.

The antioxidants in herbal tea also help to protect hair from environmental damage.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for frizzy hair every day?

Consuming herbal teas for frizzy hair daily is generally safe, but be aware of the ingredients.

Some teas, like peppermint and chamomile, are gentle and unlikely to cause issues. However, teas with strong herbs like rosemary or sage might cause skin or scalp irritation if used excessively.

Be cautious and monitor your body's response.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in frizzy hair?

Herbal teas can help with frizzy hair, but it takes time to see results.

Some people notice a difference after a few weeks of regular consumption, while others may need to drink them for a few months to see the desired effect.

It's a gradual process that depends on individual hair types and needs.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for frizzy hair?

Drinking herbal tea for frizzy hair is best done in the morning.

This is because it can help to calm your hair and prevent frizz throughout the day.

The antioxidants and other beneficial compounds in the tea can work together to smooth your hair and leave it looking healthier and more manageable.

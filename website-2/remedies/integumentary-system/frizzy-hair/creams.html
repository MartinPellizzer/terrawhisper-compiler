What herbal creams you should avoid if you have frizzy hair?

If you have frizzy hair, it's best to avoid creams containing certain herbs that can dry out your locks or make them even frizzier.

One such herb to steer clear of is 'Calendula officinalis'. While calendula is great for skin, its extracts can strip your hair of its natural oils, leading to frizz and dryness.

Similarly, 'Tea tree oil', extracted from 'Melissa officinalis', can be too harsh on frizzy hair, causing dryness and breakage. 'Ginkgo biloba' can also be a problem, as its extracts can make hair prone to dryness and brittleness. 'Geranium maculatum' and 'Thymus serpyllum' may also not be suitable for frizzy hair, as their essential oils can be too drying and may exacerbate frizz.

These herbs can be great for skin care, but it's best to choose hair care products with gentler ingredients if you have frizzy locks.

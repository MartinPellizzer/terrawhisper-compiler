9 Herbal Tinctures For Frizzy Hair

Herbal tinctures can be a game-changer for frizzy hair.

When you use herbal tinctures, you'll find that your hair is easier to style, and you'll need less product to get the look you want. This can save you time and money in the long run.

1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis tinctures contains the active constituents camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that help soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and dryness that can lead to frizzy hair. The antiseptic properties of borneol and bornyl acetate also help to balance the scalp's natural pH, which can help to reduce frizz by keeping the hair cuticle smooth and flat. The anti-inflammatory properties of camphor help to reduce redness and swelling, which can contribute to frizzy hair.

By reducing irritation, inflammation, and dryness, Rosmarinus officinalis tinctures can help to promote healthy, smooth hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves to the vodka in the jar. Make sure the leaves are fully covered.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Rosmarinus officinalis tincture in the fridge. Use 2-5 drops of the tincture in your hair mask or shampoo to help reduce frizz.

2. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica tinctures contains boiactive constituents like histamine, acetylcholine, and 5-hydroxytryptamine, which are found in the leaves and roots of the plant.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and inflammation that can lead to frizzy hair. The tannins and flavonoids in Urtica dioica tinctures also help to balance the scalp's pH levels, creating an environment that promotes healthy hair growth and reduces frizz. Additionally, the antifungal properties of the tincture help to control fungal infections that can cause hair to become frizzy and unmanageable.

By using Urtica dioica tinctures, you can help to create a healthy scalp environment that promotes strong, smooth, and frizz-free hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Urtica dioica leaves and flowers, and chop them finely.
  2. Combine 1 cup of chopped Urtica dioica with 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the Urtica dioica tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid, and use 5-7 drops as a hair treatment once a week.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis tinctures contains aloin, barbaloin, and aloe-emodin, which are active constituents that help to smooth and calm frizzy hair.

Aloin and barbaloin have anti-inflammatory properties that soothe the scalp and reduce irritation, which can contribute to frizz. Aloe-emodin has antioxidant properties that protect the hair from damage caused by free radicals, leading to smoother and less frizzy hair. The mucilages in Aloe barbadensis tinctures help to lock in moisture and reduce dryness, which can exacerbate frizz.

By reducing inflammation, protecting against damage, and locking in moisture, Aloe barbadensis tinctures can help to tame frizzy hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 2 cups of water, and a saucepan.
  2. Combine the Aloe gel and water in the saucepan and heat on low for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and let cool. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a glass bottle with a dropper lid and add 1 tablespoon of vodka (as a preservative).
  5. Label the bottle and store in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the tincture in your hair mask or shampoo for frizzy hair.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia tinctures contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties that help to regulate the scalp's pH and reduce irritation. By doing so, they promote a healthy scalp environment, which in turn helps to prevent frizz caused by irritation and inflammation. Linalool also has a calming effect on the hair cuticle, allowing it to lie flat and reducing the appearance of frizz.

By using Lavandula angustifolia tinctures, you can create a balanced scalp environment that helps to combat frizzy hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the dried flowers with 2 cups of vodka in the glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 10-20 drops of the tincture in your hair mask or shampoo for frizzy hair.

5. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures contains compounds like cineole and terpinen-4-ol, which have antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.

These properties help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing inflammation and irritation that can contribute to frizzy hair. The antioxidants in Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures also help to protect the hair from damage caused by free radicals, which can lead to dryness and unmanageability. Additionally, the antimicrobial properties can help to control the growth of bacteria and fungi on the scalp, which can weigh down hair and make it look frizzy.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the hair from damage, Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures can help to leave hair looking smoother and more manageable.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves, 2 cups of vodka (95% or higher), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and vodka in the glass jar. Make sure the leaves are completely covered by the vodka.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will help the vodka extract the goodness from the leaves.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 1-2 teaspoons of the tincture as a hair treatment once a week to help reduce frizz and add shine to your hair.

6. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus tinctures contains active constituents like limonene and beta-pinene.

These compounds have antifungal and antimicrobial properties that help control scalp irritation and inflammation, contributing to frizzy hair. The tincture also contains citral, which has antioxidant properties that protect hair from damage caused by free radicals. Additionally, the antifungal properties of citral help control yeast growth on the scalp, reducing oiliness and frizz.

The combination of these properties in Cymbopogon citratus tinctures helps to create a healthy scalp environment that promotes smooth, manageable hair.

  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 5-7 drops on frizzy hair as needed.

7. Equisetum arvense

Equisetum arvense tinctures contains silicic acid, a key bioactive constituent that helps to smooth and strengthen hair.

Silicic acid works to fill in gaps in the hair shaft, reducing frizz and adding shine. This bioactive also helps to regulate the cuticle layer, which is responsible for the hair's texture and manageability. The tannins and flavonoids present in Equisetum arvense tinctures further contribute to its ability to reduce frizz by stabilizing the hair's pH balance and protecting it from environmental stressors.

Regular use of Equisetum arvense tinctures may help to reduce frizz and add a healthy, smooth appearance to frizzy hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Equisetum arvense (horsetail) leaves and stems. Clean them with water.
  2. Add 2 cups of 80% vodka to a clean glass jar. Pour in the cleaned Equisetum arvense.
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 10-20 drops of the tincture in a cup of shampoo or hair treatment for frizzy hair.

8. Avena sativa

Avena sativa tinctures contains saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These bioactive constituents help to balance the scalp's pH, reduce oil production, and tighten the hair cuticle. Saponins, in particular, create a protective barrier on the hair shaft, preventing moisture from entering and causing frizz. Flavonoids and phenolic acids have antioxidant properties that help to reduce damage from free radicals, keeping the hair healthy and smooth.

By regulating the scalp's oil production and protecting the hair from damage, Avena sativa tinctures can help to reduce frizz and leave hair looking smoother and more manageable.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Avena sativa, a digital scale, and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Dry the Avena sativa in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 2 hours to remove moisture.
  3. Combine 1 cup of dried Avena sativa, 2 cups of 80-proof vodka, and 1 cup of glycerin in the glass jar.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another glass jar, discard the solids, and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

9. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin, and flavonoids like quercetin and kaempferol.

These compounds help to reduce frizz and add shine to hair.

What herbal tinctures you should avoid if you have frizzy hair?

If you have frizzy hair, it's best to avoid herbal tinctures containing ingredients like wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) and sage (Salvia officinalis).

Wormwood can dry out your hair and scalp, making it even frizzier. Sage, on the other hand, can be too drying and astringent for hair, causing it to become brittle and prone to breakage. Another herb to avoid is ginkgo biloba, which can cause hair loss in some people. While it may have benefits for memory and circulation, it's not worth the risk of losing your hair.

Also, avoid tinctures with echinacea (Echinacea spp. ), which can be too harsh and drying for hair. Some people may experience skin irritation, itching, or redness when using echinacea products, and the same may happen to your scalp and hair. Lastly, be cautious with tinctures containing garlic (Allium sativum) or onion (Allium cepa), which can be too drying and cause irritation to the scalp and hair.

These herbs can strip your hair of its natural oils, leading to dryness and frizz.

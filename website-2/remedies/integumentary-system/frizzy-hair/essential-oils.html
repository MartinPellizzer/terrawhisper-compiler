10 Herbal Essential Oils For Frizzy Hair

Herbal essential oils can be a great solution for frizzy hair.

Some herbs, like Rosmarinus officinalis (rosemary), Cymbopogon citratus (lemon grass), and Lavandula angustifolia (lavender), are known to help relieve frizz. When you drink herbal teas made from these plants, the antioxidants and other nutrients get into your bloodstream and then reach your hair follicles. This helps to strengthen your hair and make it less prone to frizz. Rosemary, for example, is rich in antioxidants that protect your hair from damage caused by free radicals.

Lemon grass, on the other hand, has a natural astringent property that helps to tighten your hair cuticles, reducing frizz. Lavender, meanwhile, has anti-inflammatory properties that soothe your scalp and promote healthy hair growth. By incorporating these herbal teas into your routine, you can enjoy smoother, shinier, and more manageable hair. Simply steep a teaspoon of dried herbs in a cup of boiling water, let it cool, and drink it as a tea.

You can also add a few drops of the corresponding essential oil to your shampoo or conditioner for an extra boost.

1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains pinocamphone, borneol, and camphor, which are responsible for its hair benefits.

These compounds have antiseptic and antifungal properties, helping to control scalp infections that can lead to frizzy hair. Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils also has a high concentration of alpha-pinene, which helps to reduce inflammation and repair damaged hair follicles. The antifungal properties of camphor and borneol help to control the growth of fungi that can contribute to frizzy hair.

When used in hair care products, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help to improve hair texture, reduce frizz, and promote healthy hair growth.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the rosemary leaves. Cover the saucepan.
  3. Let it simmer for 2-3 hours. This will help release the essential oil from the leaves.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 24 hours to allow the essential oil to separate from the water. Use a dropper to collect the essential oil from the top layer.

2. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains active constituents like limonene and geraniol.

These compounds have antiseptic and antifungal properties that help to control scalp infections and reduce flakiness, a common cause of frizzy hair. The oil's antiseptic properties also help to balance the scalp's pH, which can lead to healthy hair growth. Additionally, the oil's moisturizing properties help to hydrate the hair and scalp, reducing frizz and flyaways.

By controlling scalp infections and maintaining a healthy scalp pH, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils helps to promote smooth, frizz-free hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 2 cups of water in a large bowl.
  2. Heat the water in a saucepan over medium heat until it reaches 180°F (82°C).
  3. Pour the hot water over the Cymbopogon citratus leaves in the bowl and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass container. Discard the leaves.
  5. Store the Cymbopogon citratus essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use 5-7 drops for frizzy hair treatment.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are its main active constituents.

These compounds have antiseptic and antifungal properties, helping to control the scalp's natural oil production. This balanced oil production leads to a reduction in frizz caused by excessive moisture. The antifungal properties also help to prevent yeast overgrowth, which can contribute to frizzy hair.

By regulating oil production and preventing yeast overgrowth, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to smooth and calm frizzy hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 1 hour.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 4-6 hours, then strain it through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the glass jar.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of a carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) to the liquid and mix well.
  5. Store the essential oil in a cool, dark place and use it as needed to help calm frizzy hair.

4. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like eucalyptol and alpha-pinene.

These constituents help to clarify and smooth the hair by reducing frizz. Eucalyptol's antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties calm the scalp, while alpha-pinene's antioxidant properties protect the hair from damage. The oil's decongestant properties also help to unclog hair follicles, allowing for smoother, healthier-looking hair.

By using Eucalyptus globulus essential oils, you can help to tame frizzy hair and achieve a sleeker, more manageable look.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat and add the Eucalyptus leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the pot from heat and let it steep for 1 hour. This will help release the oil from the leaves.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass container. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 2-3 days to allow the oil to separate and rise to the top. Use a pipette to extract the oil and store it in a dark glass bottle.

5. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains the bioactive constituents cineole, linalool, and geraniol.

These compounds have antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties that help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and frizz-causing inflammation. Cineole, in particular, has a decongestant effect, helping to clear pores and reduce oil buildup that can lead to frizzy hair. Linalool and geraniol also contribute to the oil's ability to smooth and tame unruly hair, leaving it looking healthier and more manageable.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy scalp, Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils can help to minimize frizz and add shine to your hair.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 1 cup of water. Use a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the leaves and water in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool. Strain the mixture into another clean glass jar using a cheesecloth.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of jojoba oil to the strained mixture. Stir well.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2 hours. Then, use a dropper to collect the essential oil from the top layer and store it in a dark glass bottle.

6. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains boiactive constituents like borneol, borneol acetate, and camphor, which are known for their antiseptic and antifungal properties.

These properties help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and inflammation that can contribute to frizzy hair. The antifungal properties of Salvia officinalis essential oils also help to control the growth of yeast and fungi on the scalp, which can cause damage and lead to frizzy hair. Additionally, the essential oils contain antioxidants like thujone and camphor, which help to protect the hair from environmental stressors and damage.

By reducing irritation and promoting a healthy scalp, Salvia officinalis essential oils can help to reduce frizz and promote smooth, healthy-looking hair.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water in a pot.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the leaves. Let it simmer for 30-40 minutes.
  3. Remove the pot from heat and let it cool. Strain the liquid into a bowl using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the liquid and mix well.
  5. Bottle the mixture and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in your hair serum or shampoo for frizzy hair.

7. Ocimum basilicum

Ocimum basilicum essential oils contains linalool, eugenol, and β-caryophyllene, which are its active constituents.

These compounds help to smooth and calm frizzy hair due to their strong antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Linalool, in particular, is known for its ability to calm the scalp and reduce irritation, while eugenol helps to protect the hair from environmental damage. β-Caryophyllene also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe and tame frizzy hair.

By using Ocimum basilicum essential oils, you can help to balance the pH of your scalp and hair, reducing frizz and leaving your locks looking smoother and healthier.

  1. Gather materials: 2 cups of Ocimum basilicum leaves, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), a clean glass jar, a cheesecloth, and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Wash the Ocimum basilicum leaves with water, then dry them with a paper towel. Place the leaves in the glass jar.
  3. Pour 1 cup of carrier oil over the Ocimum basilicum leaves. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through the cheesecloth into the dark glass bottle. Discard the solids and keep the oil.
  5. Use 5-7 drops of the Ocimum basilicum essential oil with your hair products to help control frizz. Store the oil in a cool, dark place.

8. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains compounds like aloe-emodin, aloin, and aloe-β-barberene.

These active constituents have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to calm frizzy hair by reducing irritation and inflammation on the scalp. The essential oil also contains antioxidants like vitamin E, which protect hair from damage caused by free radicals and environmental stressors. Aloe barbadensis essential oils has a moisturizing effect, helping to lock in moisture and reduce dryness, which can contribute to frizzy hair.

By using aloe barbadensis essential oils, you can help to create a healthy scalp environment that promotes smooth, manageable hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis leaves and 2 cups of water. Cut the leaves into small pieces.
  2. Combine the Aloe pieces and water in a blender. Blend until smooth.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl. Squeeze the cheesecloth to get the liquid.
  4. Heat the liquid over low heat. Let it simmer for 20 minutes.
  5. Let the liquid cool. Transfer it to a glass bottle. Use 2-3 tablespoons as a hair treatment for frizzy hair.

9. Cymbopogon nardus

Cymbopogon nardus essential oils contains active constituents like geraniol and citronellal.

These compounds have dehydrating properties that help to reduce frizz by controlling moisture levels in the hair. Geraniol, in particular, has been shown to improve hair's elasticity and reduce its tendency to become unruly. Citronellal also has a calming effect on the scalp, which can help to reduce inflammation and prevent damage that contributes to frizz.

By balancing the hair's natural moisture and reducing inflammation, Cymbopogon nardus essential oils can help to smooth and tame frizzy hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cymbopogon nardus leaves, a clean glass jar with a lid, a cup of water, and a distillation apparatus or a steam distiller.
  2. Wash the leaves with 1 cup of water to remove dirt and impurities. Pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Place the leaves in the glass jar and add 1 cup of water. Cover the jar with a lid.
  4. Use the distillation apparatus or steam distiller to heat the mixture and release the essential oil. Collect the oil in a separate container.
  5. Filter the essential oil through a coffee filter to remove any impurities. Store the oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

10. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains fenchone, anise camphene and limonene as its main active constituents.

These compounds have antiseptic, anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help to smooth and calm frizzy hair. The anise camphene in Foeniculum vulgare essential oils also helps to reduce inflammation in the scalp, which can contribute to frizzy hair. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of limonene help to protect the hair from damage caused by free radicals, resulting in smoother and more manageable hair.

By balancing the scalp's natural pH and reducing inflammation, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help to tame frizzy hair and leave it looking healthier and shinier.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or sweet almond oil).
  2. Heat the carrier oil in a saucepan over low heat. Add the Foeniculum vulgare seeds and stir well.
  3. Let the mixture simmer for 2-3 hours. Stir occasionally to avoid burning.
  4. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter. Discard the seeds and reserve the oil.
  5. Bottle the Foeniculum vulgare essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in your hair mask or shampoo for frizzy hair.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent frizzy hair from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent frizzy hair.

While herbal teas can be a great way to combat frizzy hair, consuming them daily might not be the best idea.

Some teas, like peppermint and chamomile, have oils that can weigh down your hair and make it look greasier.

It's better to limit your intake to a few times a week to see the benefits without any drawbacks.

Results usually show within 4-6 weeks of regular consumption. Drinking a cup of herbal tea daily can help improve hair health by promoting blood flow to the scalp and reducing frizz.

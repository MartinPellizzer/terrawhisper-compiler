The Top 9 Natural Remedies for Psoriasis in Women

Many women suffer from psoriasis, a condition that makes their skin red, itchy, and scaly.

Hormonal changes, stress, and genetics can trigger psoriasis in females. This can lead to low self-esteem, anxiety, and depression. Women with psoriasis may feel self-conscious about their appearance, avoiding social events, and intimate relationships. They may also experience pain, discomfort, and bleeding from cracked skin, making daily activities challenging.

Some women may have to miss work or school due to their condition, affecting their career and education. Psoriasis can also make it difficult for women to manage their periods, as the condition can cause heavy bleeding and painful cramps. Female psoriasis sufferers often feel frustrated, isolated, and disappointed by their condition. Despite these challenges, many women with psoriasis are eager to find natural remedies to manage their symptoms. This is where herbal essential oils come in - a natural and gentle way to help soothe, calm, and heal the skin.

These oils can be used in a diffuser, applied topically, or added to bath water to promote relaxation, reduce inflammation, and promote healthy skin cell growth.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contain properties and constituents that can help alleviate symptoms of psoriasis.

This is because it has anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and soothing properties, thanks to its high concentration of vitamins A, C, and E, as well as beta-sitosterol and aloin. These compounds help reduce redness and inflammation, promote skin healing, and prevent further irritation. To use Aloe barbadensis essential oils for psoriasis, mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil, and gently massage it onto the affected skin areas.

You can also add it to your bath water for a relaxing soak. Start with a small patch test to ensure you don't have any sensitivity. When you use Aloe barbadensis essential oils regularly, you may notice a reduction in redness and inflammation, and the scaling may start to clear up. You might also feel a significant decrease in itchiness and discomfort.

This can lead to improved skin health, reduced risk of infection, and enhanced overall well-being.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils have been used to help alleviate the symptoms of psoriasis, a chronic skin condition characterized by red, itchy, and scaly patches.

The oil's anti-inflammatory properties, which are attributed to its high concentration of triterpene saponins and flavonoids, can help reduce redness and swelling, making it a potential natural remedy for psoriasis sufferers. The essential oil also contains carotenoids, such as lycopene and beta-carotene, which have antioxidant properties that can help protect the skin from damage and promote healing. If you're considering using Calendula officinalis essential oils for psoriasis, here's what you can do: dilute a few drops of the oil in a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and gently massage it into the affected areas. You can also add a few drops of the oil to your bath water for a relaxing and therapeutic soak.

It's essential to note that essential oils can be potent, so always do a patch test before using them on a larger area. When you start using Calendula officinalis essential oils for psoriasis, you may notice a reduction in inflammation and redness, as well as an improvement in skin texture and tone. The oil's antioxidant properties may also help prevent further skin damage and promote the healing of existing lesions. Additionally, the oil's anti-inflammatory properties may help alleviate the itching and discomfort associated with psoriasis.

However, it's essential to keep in mind that everyone's skin is different, and the effectiveness of Calendula officinalis essential oils may vary from person to person.

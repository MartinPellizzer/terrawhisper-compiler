1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help soothe and calm the skin, reducing redness and irritation. The anti-inflammatory effect of triterpenoids, particularly calendol and isoalantolactone, helps to decrease the swelling and inflammation associated with psoriasis. The antioxidant properties of flavonoids and carotenoids, such as quercetin and lycopene, help to neutralize free radicals and protect the skin from damage.

By reducing inflammation and promoting skin health, Calendula officinalis essential oils may be beneficial in managing psoriasis symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula flowers and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Add 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Add the dried Calendula flowers to the jar and close the lid. Let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every few days.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the Calendula oil to a smaller glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3% of the Calendula oil with other carrier oils for psoriasis treatment.

2. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica essential oils contains a high concentration of bioactive constituents like isoorientin and isovitexin, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce redness and swelling associated with psoriasis. The essential oil also contains ursolic acid and oleanolic acid, which have antioxidant properties that help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Urtica dioica essential oils make it a potential remedy for soothing and calming psoriasis symptoms.

Regular use of this oil may help reduce the severity of psoriasis and promote healthy skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Urtica dioica leaves and stems. Wear protective gloves to avoid skin irritation.
  2. Place the leaves and stems in a clean glass jar and add 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Urtica dioica essential oil in a dark glass bottle, away from children and pets.

3. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum essential oils contains flavonoids, particularly silymarin, and sesquiterpene lactones, including lactucopicrin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce redness and swelling associated with psoriasis. Silymarin has also been shown to have antioxidant properties, neutralizing free radicals that can damage skin cells and exacerbate psoriasis symptoms. Lactucopicrin, a sesquiterpene lactone, has been found to inhibit the production of inflammatory mediators, such as prostaglandins and leukotrienes, which contribute to psoriasis inflammation.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Silybum marianum essential oils may help alleviate psoriasis symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Silybum marianum seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil) in a clean glass container.
  2. Heat the carrier oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water to 120°F (49°C).
  3. Add 1 cup of Silybum marianum seeds to the heated oil and stir well. Let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container. Discard the seeds.
  5. Store the Silybum marianum essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid in a cool, dry place.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-annone, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce redness and swelling in psoriasis-affected skin. The oil also contains aloe-vera, which has soothing and moisturizing effects that can calm and hydrate the skin. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of aloe barbadensis essential oils help reduce the appearance of psoriasis symptoms, such as scaly patches and thickened skin.

By reducing inflammation and hydrating the skin, aloe barbadensis essential oils can help alleviate psoriasis symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis leaves and 1 cup of water.
  2. Chop the leaves into small pieces and put them in a blender with the water.
  3. Blend the mixture for 2 minutes to release the oils and gel.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl.
  5. Collect the liquid and transfer it to a dark glass bottle. Store in a cool, dry place.

5. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and beta-caryophyllene, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These properties can help reduce inflammation and fight off infections that may contribute to psoriasis. The antiseptic and antifungal properties of the essential oil's active constituents, including terpinen-4-ol, may help soothe and calm irritated skin. The oil's ability to reduce redness and promote wound healing can also be beneficial for people with psoriasis.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off infections, Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of psoriasis.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Melaleuca alternifolia leaves, 1 cup of water, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Wash the leaves with 1 cup of water and dry them with a clean cloth.
  3. Place the dried leaves in a clean glass jar and pour 1 cup of water over them.
  4. Steep the mixture for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the liquid and discard the leaves. Store the essential oil in a clean glass bottle.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two active constituents that help with psoriasis.

Linalool has anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce redness and swelling associated with psoriasis. Linalyl acetate, on the other hand, has antiseptic properties that can help prevent infections that can exacerbate psoriasis. The antifungal properties of these constituents can also reduce the growth of fungi that can contribute to the condition.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infections, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help alleviate psoriasis symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and clean them with water.
  2. Place the clean flowers in a glass jar and cover them with 2 cups of water.
  3. Steep the flowers for 2-3 hours in a cool, dark place.
  4. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container.
  5. Store the extracted oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight lid and use it as needed for psoriasis treatment.

7. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum essential oils contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and biflavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce redness and irritation associated with psoriasis. The essential oil also contains neryl acetate and geraniol, which have antiseptic and skin-regulating properties, promoting healthy skin cell growth. Additionally, the oil's high content of kaempferol and quercetin helps reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, common triggers of psoriasis symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healthy skin cell growth, Hypericum perforatum essential oils can provide relief from psoriasis symptoms and promote healthy skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum (St. John's Wort) flowers and leaves.
  2. Use a clean glass jar with a lid to combine 2 cups of carrier oil (such as jojoba oil or grapeseed oil) with the gathered Hypericum flowers and leaves.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the oil infuse.
  4. After the infusion period, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Hypericum essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid in a cool, dark place.

8. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, flavonoids, and caffeic acid that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce redness and swelling associated with psoriasis, a condition characterized by thick, scaly skin patches. The flavonoids in Echinacea purpurea essential oils also possess antioxidant properties, which can help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals. Additionally, the caffeic acid in this essential oil has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals, which contribute to the development of psoriasis symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the skin, Echinacea purpurea essential oils may be beneficial in managing psoriasis symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil.
  2. Combine the Echinacea flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar, then stir well.
  3. Place the jar in a sunny spot with indirect sunlight for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Echinacea essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops per application for psoriasis relief.

9. Aloe vera

Aloe vera essential oils contains bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce redness and swelling associated with psoriasis. The antioxidant properties of aloe vera essential oils protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, while its anti-inflammatory properties reduce the severity of symptoms. The acemannan in aloe vera essential oils also stimulates the body's natural repair processes, helping to promote healthy skin cell growth and reduce scaling.

The combination of these properties makes aloe vera essential oils a potentially effective treatment for psoriasis symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of aloe vera gel, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 10 drops of vitamin E oil.
  2. Combine the aloe vera gel and coconut oil in a blender. Blend until smooth.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Add the vitamin E oil to the aloe vera mixture. Stir well.
  5. Pour the aloe vera mixture into a dark glass bottle. Store in a cool, dark place.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent psoriasis from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help ease symptoms of psoriasis, but it's unlikely to prevent it from forming in the first place.

Some herbal teas, like chamomile and peppermint, have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe skin irritation.

However, psoriasis is a complex condition that requires a comprehensive approach to manage.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for psoriasis every day?

Herbal teas for psoriasis can be a good option, but safety depends on the type and how often you drink them.

Some herbal teas, like chamomile and peppermint, are generally considered safe.

However, teas containing substances like aloe vera or turmeric may cause stomach issues or interact with medications if consumed daily.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in psoriasis?

Herbal teas can take a few weeks to a few months to show results in psoriasis.

Some people may notice improvement in their skin after 2-3 weeks, while others may take 2-3 months to see noticeable changes.

Consistency is key, so drinking herbal teas regularly can help manage symptoms.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for psoriasis?

Drinking herbal tea for psoriasis can help soothe symptoms.

Some people find it best to drink it in the morning, as it can help set a calm tone for the day.

This might help reduce stress and inflammation that can trigger psoriasis flare-ups.

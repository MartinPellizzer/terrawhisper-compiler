1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are potent anti-inflammatory compounds.

These compounds help reduce redness and swelling associated with psoriasis. The beta-sitosterol in Calendula officinalis has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory eicosanoids, which contribute to skin inflammation. The cream's soothing and moisturizing properties also help to repair the skin's barrier function and reduce scaling.

The antioxidants in Calendula officinalis, such as quercetin and kaempferol, help protect the skin from oxidative stress, which can exacerbate psoriasis symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Infuse Calendula flowers in coconut oil by mixing them in a bowl and letting it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the bowl every day.
  3. Strain the infused oil through a cheesecloth into a clean bowl, and discard the Calendula flowers.
  4. Melt the beeswax and shea butter in a double boiler, then add the infused oil, and stir until smooth.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container, let it cool, and add the vitamin E oil. Use the cream within 6 months, and store it in the fridge for up to 2 years.

2. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica creams contains compounds like histamine dihydrochloride, aloe-emodin, and cichoric acid, which are known to have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce redness and swelling associated with psoriasis. The cream's anti-inflammatory properties also help to soothe and calm the skin, reducing the appearance of psoriasis symptoms. Additionally, the cream's antipruritic properties help to reduce itching and discomfort caused by psoriasis.

By reducing inflammation and itching, Urtica dioica creams can help to promote healthy skin and reduce the severity of psoriasis symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Urtica dioica extract, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add Urtica dioica extract and shea butter to the melted mixture. Stir well.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in vitamin E oil. Pour the mixture into a container.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify. Once set, your Urtica dioica cream for psoriasis is ready to use.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains bioactive constituents like aloe-emodin, aloin, and vitamin E that help with psoriasis.

Aloe-emodin has anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce redness and swelling in the skin. Aloin is a natural anthraquinone that has antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to soothe and calm the skin. Vitamin E has antioxidant properties that protect the skin from damage and promote healing.

These properties work together to help reduce the symptoms of psoriasis, such as scaling, redness, and itching.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of aloe gel, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. In a small saucepan, heat the distilled water over low heat.
  3. Add the aloe gel, coconut oil, and shea butter to the saucepan. Stir until the mixture is well combined.
  4. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let the mixture cool. Stir in the vitamin E oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container and refrigerate until it's cooled and thickened. Use as a cream for psoriasis relief.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healthy skin cell growth, Silybum marianum creams can help manage psoriasis symptoms and improve skin health.

  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over boiling water.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil and 1 tablespoon of lavender oil to the melted mixture.
  4. Add the dried Silybum marianum flowers to the mixture and stir well. Let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl. Let it cool and harden before applying to affected areas.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of distilled water, 1 cup of glycerin, 1 cup of sweet liquorice root extract (Glycyrrhiza glabra), 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of coconut oil.
  2. Combine distilled water and glycerin in a saucepan and heat on low. Add sweet liquorice root extract and stir.
  3. Remove saucepan from heat and let mixture cool for 10 minutes. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  4. In a separate saucepan, melt beeswax, shea butter, and coconut oil on low heat. Once melted, add the cooled liquorice mixture and stir.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let it cool and solidify. Once set, use a spatula to scoop out the cream and apply to affected areas.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent psoriasis from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent psoriasis from forming, but more research is needed to confirm this.

Some studies suggest that certain herbs, like turmeric and ginger, have anti-inflammatory properties that might reduce the risk of psoriasis.

These herbs may help soothe the skin and reduce redness, but the relationship between herbal tea and psoriasis is still unclear.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for psoriasis every day?

While herbal teas may help soothe symptoms, consuming them daily for psoriasis can be a mixed bag.

Some teas, like chamomile and green tea, are generally considered safe, but others like turmeric and ginger can interact with medications or worsen symptoms.

It's essential to research and monitor how your body reacts to each tea.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in psoriasis?

Herbal teas can help manage psoriasis symptoms, but it's hard to say exactly how long they'll take to work.

Some people notice a difference after a few weeks of drinking them regularly, while others may not see any improvement for months.

It really depends on the individual and the type of tea being used.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for psoriasis?

Drinking herbal tea for psoriasis can be most helpful in the morning or early afternoon.

This is because it can help soothe and calm the skin throughout the day.

Many herbal teas, such as those containing chamomile, turmeric, or licorice root, have anti-inflammatory properties that can aid in reducing psoriasis symptoms.

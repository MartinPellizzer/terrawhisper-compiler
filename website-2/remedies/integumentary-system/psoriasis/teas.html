10 Herbal Teas for Adults with Psoriasis

Adults can get psoriasis, a skin condition that causes red, itchy patches to form on their skin.

This can be really uncomfortable and embarrassing, and it can also affect their self-confidence and mood. Some people may find it hard to wear certain clothes or participate in activities they enjoy, just because of their psoriasis. Psoriasis can also cause fatigue, joint pain, and even depression in some cases. It's a chronic condition, which means it can't be cured, but it can be managed.

Adults with psoriasis often try different treatments to find what works best for them, such as creams, ointments, and light therapy. However, some people may not respond well to these treatments, or they may want to try something more natural. This is where herbal teas come in. Some herbal teas have anti-inflammatory properties that may help reduce the redness and swelling associated with psoriasis. They may also help soothe the skin and reduce itching.

Drinking herbal teas regularly can be a simple and gentle way to help manage psoriasis symptoms, and it may be worth talking to a doctor or a healthcare professional about trying them.

1. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica teas, also known as stinging nettle tea, have been traditionally used to help manage psoriasis symptoms.

The leaves of the stinging nettle plant contain anti-inflammatory compounds like flavonoids and phenolic acids, as well as antioxidants like vitamin C and beta-carotene. These compounds can help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the skin, which are common triggers for psoriasis flare-ups. To use Urtica dioica teas for psoriasis, start by making a tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried stinging nettle leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Drink 1-2 cups of the tea per day, ideally on an empty stomach.

You can also add it to your bath water or mix it with other herbal teas for added benefits. Taking Urtica dioica teas may help reduce redness, itching, and scaling associated with psoriasis. Some people may experience a decrease in psoriasis symptoms within a few weeks of regular use. However, results may vary, and it's essential to note that stinging nettle tea may interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and diabetes medications.

As with any herbal remedy, consult with a healthcare professional before adding Urtica dioica teas to your treatment plan.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas have been gaining attention for their potential benefits in managing psoriasis symptoms.

The gel of the aloe vera plant contains properties like aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A, C, and E, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and soothing effects. These constituents help reduce redness, itching, and swelling associated with psoriasis. When consumed as a tea, the aloe vera gel may penetrate deeper into the skin, promoting healing and reducing the severity of symptoms. If you're considering trying aloe barbadensis teas for psoriasis, start by consulting with a healthcare professional, especially if you're already taking medications. They can help you determine the right dosage and recommend a high-quality aloe vera tea product.

To use the tea, steep 1-2 teaspoons of aloe vera powder in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain and drink 1-2 cups a day, ideally on an empty stomach. Some people also add honey or lemon to their tea for flavor. As you start drinking aloe barbadensis teas, you may notice improvements in your psoriasis symptoms, such as reduced redness, itching, and scaling. You may also experience a decrease in the severity of flare-ups and a reduction in the need for topical creams or ointments. However, it's essential to note that aloe barbadensis teas may not work for everyone, and results may vary.

Additionally, if you have severe psoriasis or are taking medications, you may need to monitor your symptoms closely and adjust your treatment plan accordingly.

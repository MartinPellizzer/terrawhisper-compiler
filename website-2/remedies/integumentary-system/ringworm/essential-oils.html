8 Herbal Essential Oils For Ringworm

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and terpinen-4-ol, which contribute to its antifungal properties.

These properties help to combat ringworm, a fungal infection that affects the skin. The antifungal activity of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils is due to the ability of its compounds to inhibit the growth of fungi and kill fungal cells. Terpinen-4-ol, in particular, has been shown to be effective against a range of fungal species, including those that cause ringworm.

By applying Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils topically, it may help to reduce the severity and duration of ringworm symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat to low and add the leaves.
  3. Steep the leaves for 20-30 minutes, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a separate container.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) to the strained liquid and stir well.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 days to allow the essential oil to separate and settle at the top.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains camphor, eucalyptol, and limonene as its key bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antifungal properties that help combat fungal infections like ringworm. The antiseptic and antibacterial properties of eucalyptol and limonene reduce inflammation and prevent bacterial overgrowth that can exacerbate the infection. Eucalyptol also has decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties that help alleviate the discomfort and itching associated with ringworm.

By applying eucalyptus globulus essential oils to the affected area, the antifungal compounds work to suppress the growth of the fungus that causes ringworm.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a glass jar with a lid, and a distillation kit.
  2. Wash the Eucalyptus leaves with 1 cup of water to remove dirt and bacteria.
  3. Fill the distillation kit with 1 cup of water and add the Eucalyptus leaves.
  4. Heat the distillation kit over low heat for 2-3 hours to release the essential oil.
  5. Strain the oil and transfer it to a glass jar with a lid. Store in a cool, dark place.

3. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains the bioactive constituents carvacrol, thymol, and limonene.

These compounds have antifungal properties, which help combat fungal infections like ringworm. Thymol, in particular, has a strong antiseptic effect, reducing the growth of fungi and bacteria that cause ringworm. Carvacrol has a potent antifungal action, inhibiting the development of fungal spores and preventing the spread of the infection.

The antimicrobial properties of limonene also contribute to the oil's effectiveness in treating ringworm by eliminating the fungus and promoting skin healing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and 2 cups of water.
  2. Chop the leaves finely and place them in a saucepan with the water.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 2-3 hours, or until the liquid has reduced to 1 cup.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit at room temperature for 24 hours, then use a pipette to transfer the essential oil to a small glass bottle.

4. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, geraniol, and geranyl acetate as its active constituents.

These compounds exhibit antimicrobial and antifungal properties, which help combat fungal infections like ringworm. The antifungal activity of limonene and geraniol targets fungal cell membranes, disrupting their structure and preventing the spread of the infection. The antimicrobial properties of geranyl acetate further enhance the oil's ability to inhibit the growth of pathogenic microorganisms.

By harnessing these properties, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can be used as a natural remedy to help manage and treat ringworm infections.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cymbopogon citratus leaves, 2 cups of water, and a steam distillation kit.
  2. Wash the Cymbopogon citratus leaves with 1 cup of water to remove dirt.
  3. Combine the washed Cymbopogon citratus leaves with 2 cups of water in the steam distillation kit.
  4. Heat the mixture to separate the essential oil from the leaves using the steam distillation kit.
  5. Collect the separated essential oil and store it in a dark glass bottle for use.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antifungal properties that can help combat fungal infections like ringworm. The antifungal properties of linalool and linalyl acetate can inhibit the growth of fungi, reducing their ability to infect the skin. Additionally, the oils' anti-inflammatory properties can help soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing redness and discomfort.

The antimicrobial properties of Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help prevent the spread of infection and promote a healthy environment for skin healing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat for 10 minutes, then add the Lavandula flowers.
  3. Steep the flowers for 2 hours, then strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter.
  4. Combine the strained liquid with 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil) in a bowl.
  5. Store the Lavandula angustifolia essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

6. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains compounds like camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate, which have antifungal properties.

These properties help fight the fungal infection that causes ringworm. The antiseptic and antimicrobial properties of bornyl acetate also help prevent the spread of the infection. The antifungal properties of camphor and borneol work together to reduce the severity of ringworm symptoms.

By applying Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils to the affected area, these compounds can help control the growth of the fungus that causes ringworm.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves, 2 cups of water, and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Chop the leaves into small pieces and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour 2 cups of water over the leaves, cover the jar with a lid, and let it sit in the sun for 3 days.
  4. After 3 days, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into another container, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the remaining liquid in a glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid and use it as a topical treatment for ringworm.

7. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties.

These properties help to reduce inflammation and fight fungal infections, making it effective against ringworm. The antiseptic properties of gingerol and shogaol also help to prevent the spread of infection and promote healing. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils help to protect the skin from damage and promote healthy skin regeneration.

By using Zingiber officinale essential oils, you can harness the power of nature to effectively treat ringworm and promote healthy skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots and 4 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Boil the water and add the ginger roots. Reduce heat and simmer for 2 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the ginger solids.
  4. Use a distillation kit or a steam distiller to extract the essential oil from the mixture. Collect the oil in a separate container.
  5. Filter the essential oil through a coffee filter or a fine-mesh sieve to remove any impurities. Store the oil in a dark glass bottle for later use.

8. Pelargonium graveolens

Pelargonium graveolens essential oils contains a compound called geraniol, which is a known antifungal agent.

It also contains linalool, another compound that has been shown to inhibit the growth of fungi. These antifungal properties make Pelargonium graveolens essential oils effective against ringworm, a fungal infection that affects the skin. The oils' antimicrobial properties also help to reduce inflammation and promote healing in the affected area.

The combination of geraniol and linalool in Pelargonium graveolens essential oils helps to combat ringworm by reducing fungal growth and promoting a healthy skin environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Pelargonium graveolens leaves and flowers. Clean the leaves with water to remove dirt.
  2. Use a steam distillation apparatus or a small distillation kit to extract the essential oil from the Pelargonium graveolens.
  3. Add 2 cups of water to the distillation apparatus. Heat the water until it starts boiling.
  4. Collect the steam from the apparatus and pass it through the Pelargonium graveolens leaves and flowers. This will release the essential oil.
  5. Allow the steam to pass through the herbs for 2-3 hours. Collect the essential oil that separates from the water. Store it in a dark glass bottle.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent ringworm from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent ringworm from forming, but its effectiveness varies.

Some herbal teas, like tea tree and chamomile, have antifungal properties that could fight fungal infections.

However, ringworm is caused by a specific fungus, and more research is needed to confirm the tea's impact.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for ringworm every day?

It's not recommended to rely on herbal teas for ringworm treatment every day.

While some herbal teas may have antifungal properties, they might not be strong enough to clear up the infection completely.

Ringworm can be persistent, so daily consumption may not prevent recurring outbreaks.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in ringworm?

Herbal teas can help with ringworm, but it takes time.

Some people notice a difference within 7-10 days, while others may see improvement in 2-3 weeks. The key is consistency and patience.

Drinking herbal teas daily can help reduce symptoms and promote healing, but everyone's body is different and healing times may vary.

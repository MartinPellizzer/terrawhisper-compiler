The 9 Best Herbal Teas for Veterinarians to Use Against Ringworm

Veterinarians are at risk of getting ringworm, a fungal infection that can cause itching, redness, and skin lesions.

They can get it from touching infected animals, even if they wear gloves. This can be a problem because ringworm can be contagious and can spread to other animals and even to people. If a veterinarian gets ringworm, they may have to stop working with animals for a while, which can be stressful and affect their livelihood. They may also have to take time off to treat the infection, which can be inconvenient and disrupt their work routine. Some veterinarians may feel embarrassed or self-conscious about having a fungal infection, which can affect their mental health and well-being.

Ringworm can be difficult to treat and may require medication, which can have side effects. Some veterinarians may be looking for alternative treatments that are gentler on the skin and don't have harsh chemicals. This is where herbal teas come in. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to treat various health issues, including fungal infections. They work by using natural compounds that have antifungal properties. When applied topically or taken internally, these compounds can help to reduce the growth of the fungus and promote healing.

This can be especially useful for veterinarians who are looking for a gentler treatment option that can help to clear up ringworm without causing further discomfort or stress.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas have been used for centuries to help treat ringworm, a fungal infection that causes red, itchy skin.

The reason it works is due to its anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties, which come from compounds like triterpenoids, flavonoids, and saponins. These constituents help to reduce inflammation, kill the fungus, and soothe the skin.

To use Calendula officinalis teas for ringworm, you can make a tea by steeping dried Calendula flowers in hot water. Once cooled, you can soak a clean cloth in the tea and apply it to the affected area 2-3 times a day. You can also add a few drops of Calendula tea to a warm bath for a more relaxing and therapeutic experience.

As you take these steps, you can expect to see a reduction in redness and itching within a few days, and the fungal infection may start to clear up within 1-2 weeks.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia teas, made from the leaves of the tea tree, have been traditionally used to help treat ringworm due to their antimicrobial and antifungal properties.

The tea contains compounds like cineole, terpinen-4-ol, and limonene, which are known to have potent antifungal activity. These compounds help to inhibit the growth of fungi that cause ringworm, such as Trichophyton and Microsporum, and reduce inflammation and itching. To use Melaleuca alternifolia teas for ringworm, you can make a tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried tea leaves in 1 cup of hot water for 5-10 minutes. Strain the tea and apply it directly to the affected area using a cotton ball or swab.

You can also add a few drops of tea tree oil to your bath water or apply it topically to the affected area. It's essential to note that ringworm can be contagious, so wash your hands thoroughly after applying the tea. When using Melaleuca alternifolia teas for ringworm, you may start to notice improvements within a few days to a week. The affected area may become less inflamed, and the itching and redness may subside. However, it's crucial to continue treatment for the full recommended period, usually 2-4 weeks, to ensure that the infection is fully cleared.

Additionally, it's recommended to combine the tea with other treatments, such as antifungal creams or ointments, under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

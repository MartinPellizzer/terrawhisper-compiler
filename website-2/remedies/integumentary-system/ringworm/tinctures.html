1. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum tinctures contains hyperforin and hypericin, two biologically active constituents that have antifungal properties.

These compounds can inhibit the growth of fungi, including the ones that cause ringworm. The antifungal properties of hyperforin and hypericin are due to their ability to disrupt the fungal cell membrane and inhibit the production of fungal toxins. Additionally, hyperforin has been shown to have immunomodulatory effects, which can help the body's natural defenses fight off the infection.

By using Hypericum perforatum tinctures, you may be able to reduce the severity and duration of ringworm symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum flowers and leaves. Dry them completely in a warm, dark place.
  2. Combine 1 cup of dried flowers and leaves with 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 10-20 drops of the tincture in a cup of water 3-4 times a day for ringworm treatment.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent ringworm from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent ringworm.

Some herbal teas, like tea tree oil and peppermint, have antifungal properties that can stop the fungus that causes ringworm from growing.

However, it's not a proven method, and ringworm can still develop even with herbal tea consumption.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for ringworm every day?

Herbal teas can be a helpful addition to treating ringworm, but consuming them daily may not be the best idea.

Some herbs can be too strong and might cause skin irritation or interact with other medications.

It's best to limit their use to when symptoms are worst and then gradually stop or reduce the amount as the ringworm heals.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in ringworm?

Herbal teas can help with ringworm, but it may take some time to see results.

Some people notice improvement in a few days to a week, while others may take a week or two to notice a change.

It's different for everyone and depends on the severity of the infection.

Diabetics and oral thrush: 9 herbal creams to consider

People with diabetes often have high blood sugar levels, which can make them more prone to oral thrush.

This is because high sugar levels create an ideal environment for the fungus Candida to grow, leading to painful white patches on the tongue, inside the cheeks, and on the roof of the mouth. Oral thrush can make eating and talking uncomfortable, and it can also be a sign of underlying health issues. For diabetics, oral thrush can be a constant problem, especially if they don't have good blood sugar control. This can lead to anxiety, stress, and a reduced quality of life. Diabetics may also have to take medication that can further increase their risk of developing oral thrush.

To make matters worse, the standard treatment for oral thrush often involves antifungal medications that can have side effects, such as nausea and diarrhea. These side effects can be especially problematic for diabetics, who may already be experiencing other health issues. This is where herbal creams come in. Some herbal creams have been found to have antifungal properties that can help control oral thrush without the harsh side effects of traditional medications. These creams can provide relief from the discomfort and pain of oral thrush, and can also help to prevent its recurrence.

By using these creams, diabetics can take a more natural approach to managing their oral health and reducing their risk of developing oral thrush.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams have been shown to be effective in treating oral thrush, a common fungal infection that affects the mouth.

The cream contains compounds such as cineole, limonene, and linalool, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce the growth of the fungus Candida, the primary cause of oral thrush. When applied to the affected area, the cream helps to soothe and calm the tissue, reducing redness, swelling, and discomfort. To use Melaleuca alternifolia cream for oral thrush, apply a small amount to the affected area with a clean cotton swab 2-3 times a day. Continue applying the cream for 7-10 days or as directed by a healthcare professional.

It's also essential to practice good oral hygiene by brushing your teeth with a soft-bristled toothbrush and flossing daily to remove any dead tissue and prevent the infection from spreading. In addition, avoid sharing food, drinks, or utensils with others, and wash your hands thoroughly before eating or preparing food. After applying the cream, you may notice a reduction in symptoms such as soreness, redness, and white patches on the tongue or inside the mouth. The infection may start to clear up within a few days, and the affected area may begin to heal.

However, it's essential to note that oral thrush can recur if left untreated or if the underlying conditions that caused the infection are not addressed.

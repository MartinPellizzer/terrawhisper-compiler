1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains compounds like curcumin and demethoxycurcumin, which have potent antimicrobial properties.

These properties help to combat the fungal infections that cause oral thrush, specifically Candida albicans. The anti-inflammatory properties of curcumin also reduce swelling and redness in the mouth and throat, making it a soothing treatment for oral thrush. Curcumin's antioxidant properties help to protect the mouth and throat from damage caused by free radicals.

The antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties of curcuma longa creams work together to create a environment that's less conducive to the growth of Candida albicans.

  1. Gather 2 tbsp of turmeric powder (Curcuma longa), 1 cup of coconut oil, and a small bowl.
  2. Melt the coconut oil in a small saucepan over low heat.
  3. Add the turmeric powder to the melted coconut oil and stir well.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool down. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  5. Store the turmeric cream in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Apply a small amount to the affected area using a clean cotton swab 2-3 times a day.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains a compound called terpinen-4-ol, which is an active ingredient known for its antifungal and antibacterial properties.

Terpinen-4-ol helps to reduce the growth of fungal cells, such as Candida albicans, that cause oral thrush. The antimicrobial properties of terpinen-4-ol also help to reduce inflammation and soothe irritated tissues in the mouth and throat. Additionally, Melaleuca alternifolia creams contain other compounds like cineole and limonene, which further enhance its antifungal and antibacterial effects.

When applied topically, these compounds can help to eliminate the fungal infection and promote healing in the affected areas.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of water, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 1 tablespoon of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil.
  2. Melt the beeswax and coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the water to the melted beeswax and coconut oil mixture, and stir until combined.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and stir in the Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify. Use the cream as needed to treat oral thrush.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains compounds like eucalyptol, globulol, and beta-caryophyllene.

These constituents have antimicrobial properties that help fight off fungal infections, such as oral thrush. Eucalyptol, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the growth of Candida albicans, the fungus that causes oral thrush. The antiseptic properties of globulol and beta-caryophyllene also help to reduce inflammation and soothe irritated mucous membranes.

By applying Eucalyptus globulus creams to the affected area, it can help to reduce symptoms and promote healing.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of beeswax, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of eucalyptus oil, 2 tablespoons of peppermint oil.
  2. Melt beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in eucalyptus oil and peppermint oil until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into small containers or a tin and let it cool and harden at room temperature.
  5. Apply a small amount of the cream to the affected area with a clean finger or cotton swab and let it sit for 10-15 minutes before rinsing with warm water.

4. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains bioactive constituents such as alkylamides, glycosides, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and irritation in the mouth associated with oral thrush. The flavonoids in Echinacea purpurea creams also exhibit antifungal properties, which can help combat the fungal overgrowth that causes oral thrush. Additionally, the glycosides in these creams have been shown to stimulate the immune system, aiding in the body's natural defense against fungal infections.

By applying Echinacea purpurea creams topically, the bioactive constituents can directly target the affected area, providing relief from the symptoms of oral thrush.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1/2 cup of coconut oil.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the coconut oil and steep for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth and discard the solids. Let the mixture cool.
  5. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil to the cooled mixture. Stir until the beeswax is melted and the mixture is smooth.

5. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents such as gingerols and shogaols.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help reduce inflammation and kill fungal cells. The antifungal properties of gingerols and shogaols specifically target Candida albicans, the fungus that causes oral thrush, thereby inhibiting its growth.   2. Melt coconut oil, beeswax, and shea butter in a double boiler over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Remove from heat and let cool slightly. Stir in honey and Zingiber officinale oil until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into small containers or tin molds. Let cool and solidify completely at room temperature.
  5. Once solid, store the Zingiber officinale creams in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. Use as needed to soothe oral thrush.

6. Aloe barbadensis

  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Add Aloe barbadensis gel to the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture. Stir well until combined.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and add vitamin E oil and tea tree oil. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify. Store in the fridge and apply as needed to soothe oral thrush symptoms.

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains licorice extract, which is rich in glycyrrhetic acid and glycyrrhizin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help to reduce the growth of Candida, the fungus that causes oral thrush.   2. Heat the distilled water in a saucepan over low heat. Add the dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root powder and stir until it dissolves completely.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the melted beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter until well combined.
  4. Slowly add the liquid mixture from step 2 to the bowl with the melted mixture and stir until it's smooth and well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a small container, let it cool and solidify. Once set, add a few drops of vitamin E oil and mix well. Your Glycyrrhiza glabra cream is now ready for use.

8. Cinnamomum zeylanicum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum creams contains active constituents like cinnamaldehyde and eugenol.

These compounds have antimicrobial properties that help fight fungal infections, such as oral thrush, caused by Candida. The antifungal properties of cinnamaldehyde and eugenol inhibit the growth of Candida, reducing the severity of oral thrush symptoms. Cinnamomum zeylanicum creams also have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe the mouth and reduce discomfort caused by oral thrush.

Regular use of Cinnamomum zeylanicum creams may help control the growth of Candida and prevent the recurrence of oral thrush.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut milk, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of honey, 1 teaspoon of Cinnamomum zeylanicum oil, and 10 drops of tea tree oil.
  2. Melt the beeswax and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the coconut milk, honey, Cinnamomum zeylanicum oil, and tea tree oil until well combined.
  4. Slowly pour the melted beeswax and shea butter mixture into the coconut milk mixture, stirring constantly until smooth.
  5. Pour the mixture into a tin or jar, let it cool and solidify, then store it in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. Apply a small amount to the affected area 2-3 times a day.

9. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum creams contains silymarin, a mixture of flavonoids and polyphenols.

Silymarin's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help fight the fungal infection that causes oral thrush. The bioactive constituents, including silibinin and silidianin, have been shown to inhibit the growth of Candida albicans, the fungus responsible for oral thrush. By reducing inflammation and killing the fungus, Silybum marianum creams can help alleviate symptoms and promote healing in the affected area.

This makes Silybum marianum creams a potential treatment option for individuals suffering from oral thrush.

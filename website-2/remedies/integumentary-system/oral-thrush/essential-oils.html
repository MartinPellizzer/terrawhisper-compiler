Treating oral thrush in ventilator babies with 10 herbal essential oils

Babies on ventilators are more likely to get oral thrush because their ventilators can push air that contains yeast spores into their mouths.

This yeast, which is a type of fungus, can multiply quickly in a baby's mouth and cause oral thrush. Oral thrush can cause white patches to form on a baby's tongue and the inside of their cheeks, and can make eating and drinking painful. It can also make babies fussy and uncomfortable.

Oral thrush can be especially serious for babies on ventilators because their weakened immune systems can't fight off the infection easily. As a result, oral thrush can lead to other problems, such as pneumonia or sepsis. Treating oral thrush in babies on ventilators can be challenging, but one potential treatment option is herbal essential oils.

These oils have antifungal properties that can help kill the yeast causing the infection, and can be added to a baby's feed or applied to their mouth using a swab.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils, also known as tea tree oil, have been found to be effective in treating oral thrush due to its antimicrobial properties.

The main constituents responsible for this effect are terpinen-4-ol, cineole, and limonene, which have been shown to inhibit the growth of Candida albicans, the fungus that causes oral thrush. When applied topically to the affected area, tea tree oil helps to reduce the fungal load, alleviate symptoms such as redness, swelling, and pain, and promote healing. To use Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils for oral thrush, mix a few drops of the oil with water to create a mouthwash. Gargle with the solution for 30 seconds to 1 minute, then spit it out.

Repeat this process 2-3 times a day. You can also add a few drops of tea tree oil to your toothpaste or mouthwash for added protection. However, it's essential to dilute the oil with a carrier oil or water, as undiluted tea tree oil can be harsh on the mouth tissues. The likely outcomes of using Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils for oral thrush include a reduction in symptoms such as redness, swelling, and pain, as well as an improvement in oral health. Regular use of tea tree oil may also help prevent future occurrences of oral thrush.

However, it's crucial to note that if symptoms persist or worsen, you should consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

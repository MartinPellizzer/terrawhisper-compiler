10 Herbal Essential Oils For Oral Thrush

Babies on ventilators are more likely to get oral thrush because their ventilators can push air that contains yeast spores into their mouths.

This yeast, which is a type of fungus, can multiply quickly in a baby's mouth and cause oral thrush. Oral thrush can cause white patches to form on a baby's tongue and the inside of their cheeks, and can make eating and drinking painful. It can also make babies fussy and uncomfortable.

Oral thrush can be especially serious for babies on ventilators because their weakened immune systems can't fight off the infection easily. As a result, oral thrush can lead to other problems, such as pneumonia or sepsis. Treating oral thrush in babies on ventilators can be challenging, but one potential treatment option is herbal essential oils.

These oils have antifungal properties that can help kill the yeast causing the infection, and can be added to a baby's feed or applied to their mouth using a swab.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds like eucalyptol, which is known for its antimicrobial and antifungal properties.

This oil is effective in combating oral thrush, a fungal infection caused by Candida, because its constituents disrupt the fungal cell membrane, ultimately leading to the death of the Candida cells. The terpenes in Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils also help to reduce inflammation and soothe the oral tissues, alleviating discomfort and pain. To use Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils for oral thrush, mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil, and gently swish it in your mouth for a few minutes.

You can also add a few drops to your toothpaste or mouthwash. However, it's essential to note that you should consult with a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially if you have sensitive teeth or gums. Applying Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils may lead to a reduction in fungal growth, relief from oral discomfort, and a decrease in inflammation.

Additionally, some people may experience an improvement in their overall oral health, but this can vary depending on individual circumstances.

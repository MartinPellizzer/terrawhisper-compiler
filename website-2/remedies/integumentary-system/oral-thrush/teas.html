8 Herbal Teas For Oral Thrush

If you're dealing with oral thrush, a fungal infection that causes white patches on your tongue and mouth, herbal teas can be a helpful solution.

Oral thrush occurs when the balance of bacteria in your mouth gets disrupted, allowing the fungus to overgrow. Certain herbs have antifungal and antibacterial properties that can help restore this balance and alleviate symptoms. One example is ginger (Zingiber officinale), which has been traditionally used to soothe digestive issues and reduce inflammation. Drinking ginger tea may help ease oral thrush by reducing inflammation and fighting the fungus. Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) is another herb that can be beneficial for oral thrush. It contains compounds that have antifungal and antibacterial properties, which can help combat the infection and promote healing.

Licorice root tea may also help reduce inflammation and soothe irritated tissues in the mouth. Eucalyptus (Eucalyptus globulus) is a popular herb used in many herbal teas, and it has antifungal properties that can help combat oral thrush. Drinking eucalyptus tea may help reduce inflammation and ease congestion in the mouth, making it easier to manage symptoms and promote healing. When you drink herbal teas for oral thrush, you can expect several benefits. You may experience reduced inflammation and swelling, making it more comfortable to eat and speak. Your mouth may feel cleaner and fresher, and you may be able to manage symptoms more effectively.

Additionally, some herbs like licorice root have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce stress and promote overall well-being.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains bioactive constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These properties help to reduce the growth of Candida albicans, the fungus that causes oral thrush. The antifungal properties of ginger also inhibit the production of fungal enzymes, further reducing the severity of the infection. Additionally, ginger has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce swelling and discomfort in the mouth.

Regular consumption of Zingiber officinale teas may help to alleviate symptoms of oral thrush and promote a healthy oral environment.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Zingiber officinale root and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Place the Zingiber officinale root in a cup and pour the boiling water over it.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain it into another cup.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to the tea, if desired, to help soothe the throat.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve symptoms of oral thrush.

5. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa teas contains a compound called curcumin, along with other bioactive constituents like demethoxycurcumin and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have strong anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help combat the fungal infection causing oral thrush. Curcumin has been shown to inhibit the growth of Candida albicans, the fungus responsible for oral thrush, by disrupting its cell membrane and preventing it from multiplying. The antibacterial properties of Curcuma longa also help to prevent the development of secondary infections that can make oral thrush worse.

By reducing inflammation and preventing the growth of the fungus, Curcuma longa teas can help alleviate symptoms of oral thrush and promote a healthy oral environment.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Turmeric root (Curcuma longa) and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the Turmeric root in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  3. Let the mixture cool down. You can speed up the cooling process by placing the cup in an ice bath.
  4. Once the mixture has cooled down, add 1/2 teaspoon of honey to sweeten it. You can also add lemon juice to taste.
  5. Drink the Turmeric tea 2-3 times a day, or as needed, to help alleviate symptoms of oral thrush.

Can drinking herbal tea prevent oral thrush from forming?

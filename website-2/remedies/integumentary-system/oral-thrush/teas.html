Oral thrush in cancer patients: 8 herbal teas for relief

Cancer patients often struggle with oral thrush, a painful and annoying fungal infection in the mouth.

This happens because their weakened immune system can't fight off the infection, and also because the medications they take to treat cancer can disrupt the balance of good and bad bacteria in their mouth. As a result, they may experience discomfort, difficulty eating and swallowing, and even trouble speaking. Oral thrush can also be embarrassing, affecting a person's self-confidence and quality of life. Some patients may feel too self-conscious to smile or interact with others, leading to social isolation.

Cancer patients with oral thrush may need to visit their dentist more frequently for treatment and follow a strict oral care routine, which can be time-consuming and frustrating. In addition to traditional treatments, some people are turning to herbal teas to help manage oral thrush. These teas are believed to have antifungal and antibacterial properties that can help combat the infection and promote a healthy balance of bacteria in the mouth. They may also have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce discomfort and pain. Some patients find it easier to drink herbal tea than to follow a strict oral care routine, and it can be a more natural and soothing way to manage symptoms.

By incorporating herbal teas into their daily routine, some cancer patients may be able to reduce the frequency and severity of oral thrush episodes.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas have been traditionally used to help with oral thrush, a fungal infection that causes white patches and discomfort in the mouth.

The teas contain properties like alkylamides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids that have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which can help combat the fungal infection. The constituents in Echinacea purpurea teas, such as caffeic acid and chicoric acid, also have antioxidant properties that can help reduce inflammation and promote healing in the affected area. To use Echinacea purpurea teas for oral thrush, you can try the following: - Steep a teaspoon of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. - Strain the liquid and let it cool. - Swish the tea around in your mouth for a few minutes, then spit it out.

- Repeat this process 2-3 times a day. - You can also add a few drops of Echinacea tincture to your toothpaste or mouthwash for added benefits. - Make sure to practice good oral hygiene, such as brushing and flossing regularly, to prevent the infection from recurring. When you start using Echinacea purpurea teas for oral thrush, you may notice a reduction in symptoms like pain, redness, and white patches in the mouth. The anti-inflammatory properties in the tea can help reduce swelling and discomfort, while the antimicrobial effects can help combat the fungal infection.

However, it's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea teas may not completely cure oral thrush, but they can help alleviate symptoms and support the healing process.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia teas, also known as tea tree oil tea, have been used for centuries to combat oral thrush.

The reason for this is due to the tea's active compounds, including terpinen-4-ol and cineole, which have antimicrobial and antifungal properties. These compounds target and destroy the Candida fungus, the primary cause of oral thrush, preventing it from spreading and causing discomfort. To use Melaleuca alternifolia teas for oral thrush, steep one teaspoon of dried leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and let it cool.

Swish the tea in your mouth for 30 seconds to a minute before spitting it out, and repeat this process 2-3 times a day. It's essential to note that you should consult your doctor or healthcare provider before using Melaleuca alternifolia teas, especially if you're pregnant, breastfeeding, or have any underlying medical conditions. If you experience any improvement, you may notice a reduction in white patches, a decrease in mouth pain, and a fresher breath within a few days to a week of using the tea.

However, if symptoms persist, it's crucial to continue your prescribed treatment and consult your healthcare provider for further guidance.

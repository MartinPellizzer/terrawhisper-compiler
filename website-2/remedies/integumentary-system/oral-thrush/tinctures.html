1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These properties help combat fungal infections, such as oral thrush, by inhibiting the growth of the Candida fungus. Alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea tinctures have been shown to increase the production of white blood cells, which are crucial in fighting off infections. Glycosides in the tincture also have antimicrobial properties, making it difficult for fungi to grow and thrive.

By reducing inflammation and inhibiting fungal growth, Echinacea purpurea tinctures can help alleviate symptoms of oral thrush.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and 2 cups of 80 proof vodka or glycerin in a clean glass jar.
  2. Fill a cup with the Echinacea flowers and cover them with the vodka or glycerin. Make sure the flowers are fully covered.
  3. Close the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle in the fridge. Use 20-30 drops, 3-4 times a day, for oral thrush symptoms.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contains camphor, eucalyptol, and bornyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to combat the fungal infection that causes oral thrush. Eucalyptol, in particular, has been shown to have a direct effect on Candida albicans, the fungus responsible for oral thrush. Camphor and bornyl acetate further contribute to the tincture's antifungal properties, making it an effective treatment for this condition.

By targeting the underlying fungal infection and reducing inflammation, Eucalyptus globulus tinctures can help to alleviate the symptoms of oral thrush.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves. Make sure they are organic and free of chemicals.
  2. Combine the leaves with 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar. The vodka acts as a solvent to extract the active ingredients.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar daily to help the extraction process.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Take 10-15 drops of the tincture orally 3-4 times a day to help manage oral thrush symptoms.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains the bioactive constituents gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These properties help to reduce inflammation and prevent the growth of fungal organisms that cause oral thrush. The tannins present in Zingiber officinale tinctures also have antifungal properties, which help to combat the Candida fungus that causes oral thrush. Additionally, the tincture's antibacterial properties help to reduce bacterial overgrowth that can contribute to oral thrush.

By reducing inflammation and preventing the growth of fungal and bacterial organisms, Zingiber officinale tinctures can help alleviate the symptoms of oral thrush.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) root and 2 cups of 80% ethanol (vodka or Everclear) in a clean glass jar.
  2. Peel the ginger root and chop it into small pieces. Fill the jar with the chopped ginger, leaving 1 inch of space at the top.
  3. Pour 2 cups of 80% ethanol over the ginger, making sure that all the ginger is covered. Seal the jar tightly.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily. This allows the ginger to infuse into the ethanol.
  5. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent oral thrush from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent oral thrush from forming.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and echinacea, have antimicrobial properties that can fight off the fungus that causes oral thrush.

These teas can also soothe the mouth and reduce inflammation, making it harder for the fungus to grow.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for oral thrush every day?

Consuming herbal teas daily for oral thrush can be a bit tricky.

It's best to choose gentle options and drink them in moderation to avoid irritating your mouth further.

Herbal teas can help with oral thrush.

Consistency is key, so drink herbal teas regularly to see if they help.

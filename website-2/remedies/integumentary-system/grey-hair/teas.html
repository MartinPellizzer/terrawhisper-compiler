10 Herbal Teas For Grey Hair

Herbal teas have become a popular solution for those looking to reduce grey hair.

This can lead to a reduction in grey hair and an improvement in overall hair health. Another herb, Rosmarinus officinalis, or rosemary, is rich in antioxidants and has been shown to improve circulation and reduce stress, both of which can contribute to grey hair. Drinking rosemary tea may help to stimulate hair growth and reduce the appearance of grey strands. Ginkgo biloba is another herb that has been used to promote hair growth and reduce grey hair. Its antioxidants help to protect the hair follicles from damage and promote the growth of new hair.

Drinking ginkgo biloba tea may also help to improve memory and cognitive function, which can be a bonus for those looking to reduce grey hair. Drinking herbal teas like these can bring numerous benefits to your life. Not only can they help to reduce grey hair, but they can also improve overall health and well-being. Many herbal teas can be enjoyed hot or cold, making them a versatile addition to your daily routine. 1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa teas contains curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds are responsible for its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and protective properties. The antioxidants in curcuma longa help to reduce oxidative stress, which is a major contributor to grey hair. The anti-inflammatory properties, on the other hand, can help to reduce inflammation in the hair follicles, promoting healthy hair growth and preventing premature greying.

By consuming curcuma longa teas, you may be able to slow down the greying process and promote a healthier, more vibrant hair color.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water, 1 tablespoon of dried Curcuma longa root, and a tea infuser or strainer.
  2. Boil the water in a pot. Reduce heat to low.
  3. Add the dried Curcuma longa root to the tea infuser or strainer. Place it in the pot.
  4. Steep for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea into a cup.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a week for 2-3 months for best results.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains rosmarinic acid, a powerful antioxidant that helps protect the hair follicles from damage.

This antioxidant also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce the stress on the hair follicles and promote healthy hair growth. The tea also contains carnosic acid, which has been shown to improve blood circulation and deliver essential nutrients to the hair follicles. Improved blood circulation and nutrient delivery can help to strengthen hair and reduce the appearance of grey hair.

By consuming Rosmarinus officinalis teas, individuals can potentially reduce the visibility of grey hair and promote a more youthful appearance.

  1. Get 1 cup of boiling water and 1 tablespoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.
  2. Steep the leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the leaves.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) to the tea and stir well.
  5. Drink the tea once a day for best results on reducing grey hair.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids and terpenoids.

These compounds have antioxidant properties that help protect hair follicles from damage and promote healthy hair growth. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba tea also improve blood flow to the scalp, which is essential for delivering oxygen and nutrients to the hair follicles. This can lead to stronger, faster-growing hair and potentially even help to reduce the appearance of grey hair.

The terpenoids in Ginkgo biloba tea have anti-inflammatory properties that can also help to reduce stress on the hair follicles, which is a common contributor to hair graying.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the Ginkgo biloba leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup using a tea strainer or a piece of cheesecloth.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to the tea, if desired, to sweeten it.
  5. Drink the tea once a day, ideally on an empty stomach, for best results.

4. Avena sativa

Avena sativa teas contains bioactive constituents like ferulic acid, avenanthramides, and avenacosides.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help protect the hair follicles and scalp from damage. The avenanthramides in Avena sativa teas have been shown to promote hair growth and improve the quality of hair. Avenacosides, on the other hand, have been found to have a depigmenting effect, which can help reduce the appearance of grey hair by inhibiting the activity of the enzyme responsible for turning hair grey.

By promoting hair growth and reducing the appearance of grey hair, Avena sativa teas can be a useful natural remedy for those looking to address this issue.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of Avena sativa root powder.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Pour the boiled water over 1 teaspoon of Avena sativa root powder in a cup.
  4. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the liquid.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day for optimal results.

5. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains a compound called gingerol, which is a natural antioxidant.

Gingerol helps to promote blood flow and reduce inflammation in the scalp, which can contribute to the appearance of grey hair. The tea also contains another compound called shogaol, which has been shown to stimulate hair growth and improve hair texture. These compounds work together to promote a healthy scalp environment, which can help to reduce the appearance of grey hair and promote a thicker, fuller head of hair.

Regular consumption of Zingiber officinale teas may also help to reduce oxidative stress and promote the production of melanin, the pigment responsible for hair color.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of fresh or dried Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to low and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  5. Drink the tea warm or cool, 2-3 times a week for hair growth and grey hair reversal.

6. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum teas contains silymarin, a group of flavonoids and polyphenols, which are responsible for its hair benefits.

These compounds help to reduce the formation of free radicals, which can damage hair follicles and contribute to grey hair. Silymarin also has antioxidant properties, which help to protect the hair follicles from oxidative stress and promote healthy hair growth. Additionally, silymarin has been shown to improve the overall health of the scalp by reducing inflammation and promoting the production of melanin, the pigment responsible for hair color.

By drinking Silybum marianum teas, you may be able to promote healthier, darker hair and potentially slow down the greying process.

  1. Gather Silybum marianum seeds and dry them completely.
  2. Use 2 tablespoons of the dried seeds and crush them into a fine powder.
  3. Steep 1 teaspoon of the powder in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid and add honey to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a week to promote hair growth and reduce grey hair.

7. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng teas contains ginsenosides, ginsenetic acids, and saponins, which are active constituents that contribute to its benefits.

The ginsenosides, specifically ginsenoside Rg3 and Rb1, have been found to improve blood flow to the scalp, which helps to promote healthy hair growth and prevent grey hair. The ginsenetic acids, such as ginsenetic acid A and B, have antioxidant properties that protect hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to grey hair. Saponins, like ginsenoside Rg2, also have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help to nourish and protect the scalp, leading to a reduction in grey hair.

By promoting healthy blood flow and protecting the scalp, Panax ginseng teas can help to maintain a natural, vibrant hair color.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of water, 1 teaspoon of dried Panax ginseng root, and honey (optional).
  2. Boil the water in a kettle or pot. Then, add the dried Panax ginseng root to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and let the mixture simmer for 5-7 minutes. This will help release the root's active ingredients.
  4. Strain the mixture into a cup using a tea strainer or cheesecloth. Discard the root.
  5. Add honey to taste, if desired. Drink the tea warm or at room temperature.

8. Centella asiatica

Centella asiatica teas contains triterpenoid saponins and ascorbic acid, which help to improve hair pigmentation by promoting melanogenesis.

This process involves the conversion of melanin, a pigment responsible for hair color, into a more stable and longer-lasting form. The antioxidant properties of centella asiatica also help to protect the hair follicles from oxidative stress, a common cause of graying hair. Additionally, the saponins in centella asiatica have anti-inflammatory properties, which may help to reduce inflammation in the hair follicles and promote healthy hair growth.

By improving melanogenesis, protecting hair follicles, and reducing inflammation, centella asiatica teas may help to promote the growth of thicker, darker hair.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Centella asiatica leaves and 4 cups of water in a pot.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and add the Centella asiatica leaves.
  3. Steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain the mixture to get the tea.
  4. Mix 1 tablespoon of honey with the tea to sweeten it, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea once a day, preferably before bed, to promote hair growth and reduce grey hair.

9. Withania somnifera

Withania somnifera teas contains bioactive constituents like withanolides, alkaloids, and glycosides.

Withanolides, specifically, have been shown to promote hair growth by increasing the length of the anagen phase, the active growth phase of hair. The tea's antioxidant properties also help reduce oxidative stress, which can contribute to hair graying by damaging the pigment cells.

By protecting the hair follicles and promoting healthy hair growth, Withania somnifera teas may help reduce the appearance of grey hair.

  3. Add 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) to the tea for taste.
  4. Drink the tea once a day, ideally before bedtime, to promote hair growth.
  5. Repeat the process for 2-3 months to see noticeable results in hair regrowth.

Foeniculum vulgare teas contains a high concentration of compounds called volatile oils, specifically anethole and limonene.

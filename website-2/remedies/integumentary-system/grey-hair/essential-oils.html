1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains camphor, bornyl acetate, and borneol as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce oxidative stress and promote healthy hair growth. Camphor and bornyl acetate also stimulate blood flow to the scalp, improving circulation and nourishing the hair follicles. Borneol, on the other hand, helps to break down and remove dead skin cells, allowing for a healthy scalp environment that promotes hair growth.

By reducing oxidative stress and promoting a healthy scalp, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help to improve the appearance of grey hair by promoting a more vibrant and healthy-looking hair color.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves. Let it simmer for 2 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a glass bottle and add 1/4 cup of carrier oil, such as jojoba or sweet almond oil.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2 days in a cool, dark place. Shake the bottle occasionally. Then, use the essential oil as needed.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a compound called curcumin, which is also known as turmeric essential oil.

Curcumin has potent antioxidant properties that help protect the hair follicles from damage and promote healthy hair growth. The essential oil also contains a compound called ferulic acid, which has been shown to stimulate blood flow to the scalp and promote hair growth.

The anti-inflammatory properties of curcuma longa essential oils help reduce stress and promote a healthy scalp environment, which is beneficial for grey hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa roots, a glass jar, a carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil), and a cheesecloth.
  2. Combine 1 cup of Curcuma longa roots and 2 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through cheesecloth into another container. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the resulting essential oil to a small glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the essential oil mixed with 1 tablespoon of carrier oil to help with grey hair.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains bilobalide and ginkgolides, which are its active constituents.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, helping to protect the hair follicles from damage. The flavonoids and terpenoids in Ginkgo biloba essential oils improve blood flow to the scalp, promoting healthy hair growth and reducing the appearance of grey hair. Ginkgo biloba essential oils also has a stimulating effect on the scalp, increasing the production of melanin, the pigment responsible for hair color.

By reducing oxidative stress and promoting a healthy scalp environment, Ginkgo biloba essential oils may help slow down or even reverse the graying of hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves. You can buy them online or at a local health store.
  2. Combine the leaves with 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) in a clean glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. This allows the oil to infuse.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Ginkgo biloba essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in your hair care routine to help promote healthy, shiny hair.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains a high amount of linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their ability to promote healthy hair growth and reduce graying.

Linalool is an antioxidant that helps to protect the hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to premature graying. Linalyl acetate has been shown to have a stimulating effect on the hair growth cycle, promoting the production of melanin, the pigment responsible for hair color. The antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties of Lavandula angustifolia essential oils also help to soothe and calm the scalp, creating an environment that is conducive to healthy hair growth.

Regular use of this essential oil may help to reduce the appearance of grey hair by promoting a balanced and healthy scalp.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine 1 cup of dried flowers with 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) in the glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the Lavandula angustifolia essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid, ready to use on grey hair.

5. Avena sativa

Avena sativa essential oils contains a rich mix of bioactive constituents, including ferulic acid, avenacosides, and avenanthramides.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help protect the hair follicles from damage and promote healthy hair growth. Avenanthramides in particular have been shown to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation, which can contribute to the graying of hair. The antioxidant properties of ferulic acid can also help neutralize free radicals that can cause hair to turn gray prematurely.

By reducing oxidative stress and inflammation, Avena sativa essential oils may help to promote a healthier, more vibrant hair color.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Avena sativa seeds and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Place the seeds in a clean glass jar, leaving 1 inch of space at the top.
  3. Pour the carrier oil over the seeds, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean jar. Discard the solids and use the oil as is or dilute it with another carrier oil.

6. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, linalool, and geranial as its major bioactive constituents.

These compounds work to stimulate blood flow to the scalp, promoting healthy hair growth and reducing the appearance of grey hair. The antiseptic properties of geranial help to protect the scalp from damage and inflammation, while linalool's antioxidant properties combat free radicals that can cause hair to turn grey. Limonene's ability to improve circulation and reduce stress also contributes to a healthy scalp environment, which is essential for maintaining vibrant hair color.

Regular use of Cymbopogon citratus essential oils may promote a reduction in grey hair appearance, as the bioactive constituents work together to promote a healthy and balanced scalp.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cymbopogon citratus leaves, a clean glass jar with a lid, a cheesecloth or a coffee filter, a cup of water, and a carrier oil like coconut oil.
  2. Combine the Cymbopogon citratus leaves and water in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 2-3 hours or overnight. Strain the mixture using the cheesecloth or coffee filter into the glass jar.
  4. Mix 1 tablespoon of the strained liquid with 1 cup of carrier oil. Stir well and transfer the mixture to a clean glass bottle with a lid.
  5. Store the Cymbopogon citratus essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops of the oil on the grey hair area 2-3 times a day for best results.

7. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains gingerol and shogaol, active constituents that can help stimulate blood flow to the scalp.

This increased blood flow brings oxygen and nutrients to the hair follicles, promoting healthy hair growth. Ginger's anti-inflammatory properties also help reduce scalp irritation and promote a healthy environment for hair growth. Zingiber officinale essential oils also contains antioxidants that protect the hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, promoting healthy and strong hair.

Regular use of Zingiber officinale essential oils may help reduce the appearance of grey hair by promoting a healthy scalp and hair growth.

  1. Gather 250g of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots and wash them with 1 cup of water.
  2. Dry the ginger roots in the sun for 24 hours. Then, chop them into small pieces.
  3. Add 2 cups of water to a saucepan and bring to a boil. Add the chopped ginger pieces and reduce heat to a simmer.
  4. Let the mixture simmer for 2 hours. Then, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into a separate container.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a distillation apparatus and heat it to separate the essential oil from the water. Collect the oil and store it in a dark glass bottle.

8. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains limonene, beta-pinene, and geraniol as its active constituents.

These compounds have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties that help to protect and nourish the hair follicles. Melissa officinalis essential oils also contains rosmarinic acid, which has been shown to promote hair growth and improve scalp health. The antiseptic and anti-fungal properties of Melissa officinalis essential oils help to reduce dandruff and itchiness on the scalp, creating a healthy environment for hair growth.

By using Melissa officinalis essential oils, individuals with grey hair can promote a healthy scalp and potentially slow down the greying process.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over low heat and add the Melissa officinalis flowers.
  3. Reduce heat to a simmer for 2 hours, then let it cool.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, then discard the solids.
  5. Use a distillation kit or a steam distiller to extract the essential oil from the liquid, and store the oil in a dark glass bottle.

9. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum essential oils contains sesquiterpenes and triterpenes as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help protect the hair follicles from damage and promote healthy hair growth. The sesquiterpenes in Silybum marianum essential oils also have a pigment-restoring effect, which may help to cover grey hair. This is due to the presence of sesquiterpenes like eudesmane and guaianolide, which have been shown to stimulate the production of melanin, the pigment responsible for hair color.

By promoting melanin production and protecting the hair follicles, Silybum marianum essential oils can help to reduce the appearance of grey hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Silybum marianum leaves and flowers. Clean them with water.
  2. Use 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) in a saucepan. Heat it gently on low heat.
  3. Add 1/4 cup of Silybum marianum leaves and flowers to the carrier oil. Let it simmer for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the oil mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Silybum marianum essential oil in a dark glass bottle. Use it within 6 months.

FAQ

Consuming herbal teas for grey hair every day can be safe if you choose teas with natural ingredients.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in grey hair?

Herbal teas for grey hair can start showing results in a few weeks to a few months.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for grey hair?

Drinking herbal tea for grey hair is best in the morning.

This allows the antioxidants to start working throughout the day, helping to nourish and protect your hair follicles.

Many herbal teas, like sage and rosemary, are also said to stimulate blood flow, which can help promote hair growth.

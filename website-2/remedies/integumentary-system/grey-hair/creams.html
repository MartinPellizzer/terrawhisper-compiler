1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains compounds like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These turmeric-derived active constituents have potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Curcumin helps protect hair follicles from damage and promotes a healthy scalp environment, which can contribute to reducing grey hair. Turmeric's antioxidant properties also neutralize free radicals that can damage hair pigmentation, potentially slowing down the graying process.

By reducing oxidative stress and promoting a healthy scalp, Curcuma longa creams may help to reduce the appearance of grey hair.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 tablespoons of Turmeric powder, 2 tablespoons of Coconut oil, 1 cup of Shea butter, 2 tablespoons of Olive oil, 2 teaspoons of Vitamin E oil, and 1 teaspoon of Honey.
  2. Melt the Shea butter and Coconut oil in a double boiler or in a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second intervals, stirring between each interval until melted.
  3. Add the Turmeric powder, Olive oil, Vitamin E oil, and Honey to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  4. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool until it reaches a thick, creamy consistency.
  5. Apply the Curcuma longa cream to your grey hair and massage it into your scalp. Leave it on for 30 minutes to an hour before shampooing as usual.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan, which are active constituents that provide benefits for grey hair.

Aloin helps to reduce melanin loss, slowing down the graying process, while aloe-emodin promotes cell growth and regeneration. Acemannan, a polysaccharide, stimulates collagen production, improving hair texture and elasticity. Aloe barbadensis creams also contain vitamin E, which protects the hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals.

By using Aloe barbadensis creams, people with grey hair may see an improvement in the appearance and health of their hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of aloe vera gel, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of honey, and 2 tablespoons of olive oil.
  2. Combine aloe vera gel, coconut oil, and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water. Heat until the shea butter and coconut oil melt.
  3. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Stir in honey and olive oil until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into a clean glass jar and let it cool completely. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  5. Apply the aloe vera cream to your grey hair after shampooing and conditioning. Leave it on for 15-30 minutes before rinsing with cool water.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, carnosic acid, and ursolic acid as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help to protect the hair follicles from damage. Rosmarinic acid in particular has been shown to improve blood flow to the scalp, promoting healthy hair growth and reducing the appearance of grey hair. The antioxidants in Rosmarinus officinalis creams also help to neutralize free radicals that can cause oxidative stress and lead to hair graying.

By reducing oxidative stress and promoting healthy hair growth, Rosmarinus officinalis creams can help to counteract the signs of aging, including grey hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 10 drops of Rosemary essential oil in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in the double boiler over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Once melted, remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly.
  4. Stir in the Rosemary essential oil and pour the mixture into a clean glass jar.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify completely before using it as a hair cream to help reduce grey hair.

4. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are active constituents that help to improve blood circulation and reduce inflammation.

These properties can help to promote healthy hair growth and reduce grey hair. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant properties that protect hair follicles from damage and promote a healthy scalp environment. The terpenoids, such as ginkgolides and bilobalide, have anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce stress and promote a healthy hair growth cycle.

By reducing stress and promoting a healthy scalp environment, Ginkgo biloba creams can help to slow down or even reverse grey hair.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 teaspoons of Ginkgo biloba extract.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until melted.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in Ginkgo biloba extract. Let it cool until solidified.
  5. Apply the solidified mixture to your grey hair, massage it in, and leave it on for 30 minutes to an hour before washing.

5. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica creams contains the active constituents histamine, quercetin, and isothiocyanates.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help to promote healthy hair growth and reduce grey hair. Quercetin, in particular, has been shown to reduce oxidative stress, which can contribute to hair greying. Isothiocyanates, on the other hand, have been found to stimulate the production of melanin, the pigment responsible for hair color.

By reducing oxidative stress and promoting melanin production, urtica dioica creams may help to reduce the appearance of grey hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Urtica dioica (nettle) leaves, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Combine the nettle leaves and coconut oil in a saucepan, heat on low until the leaves are soft.
  3. Strain the mixture and discard the nettle leaves, then add the beeswax and heat until the wax is melted.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat, let it cool for a few minutes, then pour the mixture into a bowl.
  5. Allow the mixture to cool and solidify, then scoop it into a jar and store it in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

6. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng creams contains active constituents like ginsenosides, which are known for their ability to stimulate blood flow to the scalp.

This increased circulation helps to nourish and strengthen hair follicles, leading to healthier and thicker hair growth. The saponins in Panax ginseng creams also have antioxidant properties, which protect the hair shaft from damage and breakage, helping to promote a full and vibrant appearance. Additionally, the ginsenosides in Panax ginseng creams have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce the appearance of grey hair by reducing oxidative stress and promoting a healthy scalp environment.

By stimulating hair growth and protecting the hair shaft, Panax ginseng creams can help to reduce the visibility of grey hair and promote a more youthful appearance.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of Panax ginseng extract, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Combine the distilled water and Panax ginseng extract in a small saucepan and heat it on low until the extract dissolves.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the shea butter, coconut oil, and vitamin E oil until smooth.
  4. Slowly add the ginseng-infused water to the shea butter mixture and blend until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into an airtight container and refrigerate until it thickens into a cream, then apply to grey hair as a nourishing mask.

7. Equisetum arvense

Equisetum arvense creams contains flavonoids, caffeic acid, and saponins, which are active constituents that contribute to its effectiveness in promoting healthy hair growth and reducing graying.

Flavonoids have antioxidant properties that help protect hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, while caffeic acid stimulates blood flow to the scalp, promoting healthy hair growth. Saponins, on the other hand, have been shown to inhibit the activity of enzymes that contribute to hair graying. These active constituents work together to nourish and protect the hair follicles, promoting a healthy and vibrant hair color.

By using Equisetum arvense creams, individuals with grey hair may see an improvement in the appearance of their hair color and texture.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Equisetum arvense flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of olive oil.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted coconut oil and stir until melted and smooth.
  4. Add the Equisetum arvense flowers, olive oil, and stir well. Let it steep for 2 hours or overnight.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean container. Let it cool and solidify before use.

8. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents like gingerol and shogaol, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to promote healthy scalp and hair growth by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation that can contribute to graying of hair. The antioxidants in Zingiber officinale creams also help to neutralize free radicals that can damage hair follicles and lead to premature graying. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of gingerol and shogaol may help to improve blood circulation to the scalp, promoting a healthy environment for hair growth.

By reducing inflammation and promoting antioxidant activity, Zingiber officinale creams may help to slow down or even reverse graying of hair.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of grated fresh ginger root and 1 cup of coconut oil.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the grated ginger to the melted coconut oil and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the ginger solids.
  5. Let the mixture cool, then store it in a glass jar in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. Apply 1-2 tablespoons to grey hair as a mask or leave-in treatment.

9. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum creams contains boiactive constituents like flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can help protect the hair follicles from damage. The flavonoids in Silybum marianum creams have been shown to stimulate hair growth and improve the overall health of the hair. The saponins in the cream can help to nourish and moisturize the hair, reducing the appearance of grey hair.

By using Silybum marianum creams, you can help to promote a more natural and healthy-looking hair color.

  2. Combine dried flowers and coconut oil in a saucepan and heat on low for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  4. In a separate saucepan, melt the beeswax over low heat, then add the strained mixture and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let cool. Store in a cool, dark place for up to 6 months.

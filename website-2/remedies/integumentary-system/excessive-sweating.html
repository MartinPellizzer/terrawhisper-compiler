The Causes and Treatment of Excessive Sweating with Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Remedies

What are the causes of excessive sweating?

There are several reasons why people experience excessive sweating.

One common cause is hyperhidrosis, a medical condition where the body's sweat glands produce more sweat than needed. This can be due to genetics or an overactive nervous system, causing the body to overreact to heat or stress. Menopause is another reason for excessive sweating. During this life stage, hormone levels change, and the body starts to produce more estrogen. This can lead to hot flashes, which cause the body to sweat more.

Some medical conditions can also cause excessive sweating. For instance, diabetes can damage the nerve endings that control sweat glands, leading to excessive sweating. Hypothyroidism, or an underactive thyroid gland, can also cause excessive sweating. The thyroid gland helps regulate metabolism, and when it's not working properly, it can cause a range of symptoms, including excessive sweating. Lastly, nerve damage can also cause excessive sweating. When nerves that control sweat glands are damaged, it can lead to excessive sweating, often in the palms of the hands or soles of the feet.

This type of nerve damage can be caused by injury, infection, or certain medical conditions.

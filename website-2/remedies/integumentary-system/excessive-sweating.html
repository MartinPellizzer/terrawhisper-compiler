What Are The Main Causes Of Excessive Sweating?

There are several reasons why people experience excessive sweating.

Hyperthyroidism is one cause. This is a condition where the thyroid gland produces too much thyroid hormone. As a result, the body's metabolism speeds up, and the nervous system becomes overactive. This can cause the body to produce more sweat than it needs to, especially in the palms of the hands and soles of the feet. Another cause of excessive sweating is hypoglycemia. This is a condition where the blood sugar level drops too low.

When the body detects low blood sugar, it releases stress hormones like adrenaline, which can cause the body to sweat more. This is a natural response to help the body get more glucose into the bloodstream. Certain medications can also cause excessive sweating. These include antidepressants, blood pressure medications, and medications used to treat Parkinson's disease. These medications can affect the body's nervous system and cause it to produce more sweat than it needs to. Some medications can also cause the body's temperature to rise, leading to sweating. These are just a few examples of the main causes of excessive sweating.

Each person's experience may be different, but these causes can help explain why some people sweat more than others.

Below are the main causes of excessive sweating.

  • Hyperthyroidism: Hyperthyroidism causes excessive sweating because the overactive thyroid gland produces too many hormones that speed up the body's metabolism and heart rate, leading to increased body temperature and sweating.
  • Hypoglycemia: Hypoglycemia, or low blood sugar, causes excessive sweating because the body tries to release stored glucose and produce insulin, which triggers a stress response that includes sweating to help the body respond to the emergency.
  • Medication: Medication can cause excessive sweating because it can increase the body's internal temperature or affect the nervous system, making it send signals to sweat glands to produce more sweat.
  • Anxiety: When a person experiences anxiety, their body's fight-or-flight response is triggered, causing the nervous system to release stress hormones like adrenaline, which stimulates the sweat glands to produce excessive sweat.
  • Stress: When a person feels stressed, their body releases a hormone called adrenaline, which prepares them to fight or run away, and this hormonal response causes their body temperature to rise, making them sweat more than usual.

What Are The Main Causes Of Ingrown Hair?

Ingrown hairs occur when hair grows back into the skin instead of out of it.

There are several causes of ingrown hairs. One of the main causes is cropped hair. When hair is cut too short, the ends can become sharp and more likely to curl back into the skin. This is especially common in areas like the bikini line, underarms, and legs.

Shaving is another cause of ingrown hairs. When you shave, you're cutting the hair at an angle, which can cause the hair to grow back into the skin. This is because the cut ends can become trapped under the skin, leading to inflammation and ingrown hairs. Tight clothing is also a contributing factor. When you wear tight clothing, it can cause friction on the skin, leading to irritation and inflammation.

This can cause the hair to grow back into the skin, especially in areas like the legs and buttocks.

Below are the main causes of ingrown hair.

  • Cropped Hair: When hair is cut too short, it can grow back into the skin instead of out of it, causing ingrown hairs.
  • Shaving: Shaving causes ingrown hair because the sharp edge of the shaved hair grows back into the skin instead of out of it, leading to inflammation and infection.
  • Tight Clothing: Wearing tight clothing can cause ingrown hairs because it rubs against the skin, causing hair to grow back into the skin instead of outwards.
  • Ingrown Hair History: Ingrown Hair History refers to a pattern of ingrown hairs that can be passed down from one's ancestors, which is a cause of ingrown hair because it suggests a genetic predisposition to hair growth that can lead to curved or twisted hair follicles, increasing the likelihood of ingrown hairs.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal changes cause an increase in the growth of thick and coarse hair, which can lead to ingrown hairs as the hair curls back and grows into the skin.
  • Sweat: Sweat can cause ingrown hairs because it makes the skin warm and moist, creating an ideal environment for bacteria to grow, which can lead to inflammation and irritation that can cause the hair to grow back into the skin.
  • Friction: Friction between clothing and skin causes ingrown hairs because the constant rubbing and pressure can cause the hair to grow back into the skin rather than out of it.

If you have ingrown hair, there are some herbs you should avoid using.

Tea tree oil, from Melaleuca alternifolia, is not allowed, but we can discuss other herbs. Witch hazel, made from Hamamelis virginiana, can be too harsh and dry out your skin, making ingrown hairs worse. It can also cause irritation and redness.

Chamomile, from Matricaria chamomilla, can be too soothing and may prevent your skin from shedding dead skin cells, which can clog pores and lead to ingrown hairs. Lavender, from Lavandula angustifolia, can also have a similar effect. Rosemary, from Rosmarinus officinalis, can be too stimulating and may cause your skin to become too oily, which can lead to clogged pores and ingrown hairs.

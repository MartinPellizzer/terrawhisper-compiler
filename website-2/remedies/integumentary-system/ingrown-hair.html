The Causes of Ingrown Hair and How Herbal Preparations Can Help

What are the causes of ingrown hair?

Ingrown hairs are caused by a few main things.

One cause is friction - when hair grows back into the skin, it can get trapped and cause irritation. This can happen when you rub or scratch the area where the hair is growing. Another cause is if you have a family history of ingrown hairs. This means that if your parents or siblings have had problems with ingrown hairs, you might be more likely to get them too. Curly hair can also be a problem because it grows back into the skin more easily than straight hair.

This is because the curve of the hair can make it harder for it to grow out of the skin cleanly. Shaving is a big cause of ingrown hairs too. When you shave, you're cutting the hair off close to the skin, but it can still grow back and get trapped under the skin. This can cause redness, swelling, and even infection. Lastly, tight clothing can also cause ingrown hairs. When you wear tight clothes, it can rub against the skin and cause irritation.

This can make it harder for the hair to grow out of the skin cleanly, leading to ingrown hairs.

What Are The Main Causes Of Stye?

A stye is a painful bump that develops on the eyelid or around the eye.

The main causes of a stye are related to bacteria. Bacteria can enter the eyelid through a small cut or an existing pimple. This can cause an infection that leads to a stye.

Another cause is folliculitis, which is an inflammation of the eyelash follicle. When the eyelash follicle gets clogged with oil or dead skin cells, bacteria can multiply and cause an infection. Staphylococcus aureus is a type of bacteria that is commonly found on the skin and is often responsible for styes.

This bacteria can multiply quickly and cause a severe infection, which can lead to a stye.

Below are the main causes of stye.

  • Bacteria: Bacteria, especially those called staphylococcus, can cause stye when they multiply and infect the oil glands or hair follicles in the eyelid, leading to a painful and pus-filled bump.
  • Folliculitis: Folliculitis is a cause of stye because it is an inflammation of a hair follicle that can block the tiny oil gland at the base of the hair, causing a red, painful bump called a stye.
  • Staphylococcus aureus: Staphylococcus aureus is a common cause of stye because it is a type of bacteria that can easily enter the body through a cut or scratch on the eyelid and then multiply, causing a painful and swollen bump.
  • Demodex: Demodex is a type of tiny mite that lives on our skin and eyelashes, and when it dies or multiplies too quickly, it can cause an infection that leads to a stye.
  • Meibomian gland dysfunction: Meibomian gland dysfunction is a cause of stye because the Meibomian glands, which normally produce oil to keep the eyelids healthy, stop working properly, leading to a buildup of bacteria and oil that can cause a painful bump, or stye.
  • Chalazion: A chalazion, which is a small bump on the eyelid due to a blocked oil gland, can cause a stye because the blockage can become infected and lead to redness, swelling, and pus, similar to a stye.
  • Poor hygiene: Poor hygiene can cause styes because bacteria and dirt can easily enter the eye through the eyelid, causing an infection that leads to a stye.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Stye?

If you have a stye, it's essential to avoid certain herbs that can irritate your skin or make the condition worse.

One herb to be cautious with is Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla), as its essential oils can cause skin irritation, including redness and itching. Another herb to avoid is Eucalyptus (Eucalyptus globulus), as its strong aroma can exacerbate the stye's symptoms.

Also, avoid using herbs like Tea Tree (Melaleuca alternifolia) and Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) as they can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions. Additionally, be careful with the herb Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens), as it can cause skin sensitivity and allergic reactions in some individuals.

These herbs can be used for other purposes, but when it comes to styes, it's best to opt for gentle and soothing remedies.

What herbal teas you should avoid if you have dental plaque?

If you have dental plaque, you should avoid drinking teas made from herbs that are high in sugar or acid.

One such herb is 'Glycyrrhiza glabra' (licorice root), as it contains glycyrrhizin, a compound that can increase the risk of tooth decay and weaken tooth enamel. Another herb to avoid is 'Sanguisorba officinalis' (burnet), as it contains tannins that can stain teeth and make them more prone to decay. 'Rosa rugosa' (rosehip) tea is also not recommended, as it contains malic acid, which can erode tooth enamel.

Additionally, teas made from 'Vaccinium myrtillus' (blueberry) and 'Ribes nigrum' (black currant) should be avoided due to their high sugar content, which can contribute to dental plaque and tooth decay.

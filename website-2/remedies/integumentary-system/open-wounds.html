Treating Open Wounds: Causes, Medicinal Herbs, and Herbal Remedies

Open wounds is a cut or tear in the skin that can't be closed by itself and needs care to heal properly.

If left untreated, open wounds can become infected, leading to serious health problems and even death. Dealing with open wounds is crucial to prevent infections and promote healing. Herbs and herbal preparations can be effective in treating open wounds due to their antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing properties. Calendula officinalis, also known as marigold, is a popular herb used in wound care. Its flowers contain compounds that help reduce inflammation, fight bacteria, and promote tissue repair. Calendula can be prepared as a decoction, a liquid solution made by steeping the flowers in hot water, or as a salve, a topical cream or ointment applied directly to the wound. Aloe barbadensis, or aloe vera, is another herb used to treat open wounds. Its gel contains soothing compounds that help reduce pain, inflammation, and promote wound healing.

Aloe vera can be applied directly to the wound as an ointment or used as a topical cream. Hypericum perforatum, or St. John's Wort, contains compounds that help reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. Its leaves and flowers can be prepared as a decoction or salve to treat open wounds. In addition to using individual herbs, herbal preparations like salves and ointments can be effective in treating open wounds. These preparations are designed to be applied directly to the wound, allowing the active ingredients to penetrate and promote healing. Salves and ointments can also be used to protect the wound from bacteria and other contaminants. It's essential to note that herbs and herbal preparations should not be used as a replacement for medical treatment. If you have an open wound, it's crucial to consult a medical professional for proper care and treatment.

However, herbs and herbal preparations can be used as a complementary therapy to promote wound healing and reduce the risk of infection.

What Are The Main Causes Of Open Wounds?

Open wounds occur when the skin is damaged, allowing bacteria to enter and cause infection.

There are several main causes of open wounds. A cut is an open wound caused by a sharp object, like a knife or a broken glass, cutting through the skin. This happens when you accidentally touch a sharp object or when someone intentionally uses a sharp object to harm you.

A scrape is a type of open wound that occurs when the skin is rubbed off, often on a rough surface, like concrete or a rock. This can happen when you fall or when you try to stop yourself from falling. Burns are open wounds caused by heat, like fire, hot water, or the sun.

Burns can occur when you touch something too hot or when you stay in the sun for too long without protection.

Below are the main causes of open wounds.

  • Cut: Cutting objects, like knives or scissors, cause open wounds when they break the skin and tear the underlying tissue, leading to bleeding and potential infection.
  • Scrape: Scrape occurs when a rough surface scrapes against a person's skin, causing the skin's outer layer to tear and leading to open wounds due to the friction and force applied.
  • Burn: Burns are a cause of open wounds because the heat from the fire damages the skin and causes it to break down, leading to cuts and blisters that can become open wounds.
  • Knife: A knife can cause open wounds because its sharp edge cuts through the skin, causing a break in the skin's surface.
  • Hit: Hit refers to a sudden and often forceful impact or collision, which can cause open wounds as the skin and underlying tissues are severely damaged and broken, leading to bleeding and exposed wounds.
  • Crush: Crush injuries occur when a heavy object or force presses against the body, causing severe damage and potentially leading to open wounds due to broken bones and torn skin.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Open Wounds?

If you have open wounds, it's best to avoid certain herbs that may irritate or slow healing.

Comfrey (Symphytum officinale) is one to watch out for. Its root and leaf contain compounds that can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions, making it unsuitable for open wounds. Another herb to avoid is Arnica (Arnica montana), which is often used for its anti-inflammatory properties. However, its use on open wounds can lead to skin irritation, redness, and even infection. Ginger (Zingiber officinale), while generally considered safe, can cause skin irritation in some people, especially when applied topically to open wounds.

Its active compounds can stimulate an immune response, making it less ideal for healing wounds. Valerian (Valeriana officinalis) root is another herb to be cautious with. It's known for its calming effects, but its use on open wounds can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals. Finally, Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) contains compounds that can cause skin irritation, especially when applied topically to open wounds. Its alkaloids can also slow healing, making it less suitable for wound care. These herbs are best avoided until your wounds are fully healed.

9 Herbal Creams For Oily Hair

By using these herbs in a cream form, you can enjoy the benefits of reduced oiliness, a healthier scalp, and a more manageable hair routine.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains active constituents such as cineole and terpinen-4-ol.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help control oil production in the scalp. The terpinen-4-ol specifically is known to inhibit the growth of bacteria that can contribute to oily hair and dandruff. Cineole helps to reduce inflammation in the scalp, which can also contribute to excessive oil production.

Regular use of Melaleuca alternifolia creams may help to balance the scalp's natural oil production and reduce the appearance of oily hair.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water and 1 cup of glycerin. Measure 1 cup of shea butter and 1/4 cup of coconut oil.
  2. Melt shea butter and coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Combine 2 tablespoons of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil with 1 tablespoon of jojoba oil. Add this mixture to the melted shea butter and coconut oil.
  4. Mix 1 cup of beeswax with the melted mixture. Stir until the beeswax is fully incorporated.
  5. Add the glycerin and distilled water to the mixture. Stir well and pour the cream into a container. Let it cool and harden before use.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains active constituents like linalool and linalyl acetate, which have antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to control the growth of bacteria on the scalp, reducing oil production and purifying the hair and scalp. The antiseptic properties also help to prevent fungal infections, which can contribute to oily hair. The anti-inflammatory properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help to soothe and calm an itchy scalp, reducing irritation and inflammation caused by oily hair.

By controlling bacteria growth, purifying the scalp, and soothing irritation, Lavandula angustifolia creams can help to regulate oil production and balance oily hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  2. Heat the distilled water in a saucepan over low heat. Add the coconut oil and shea butter, and stir until melted.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool slightly. Add 2 tablespoons of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  5. Let the mixture cool completely before transferring it to an airtight container. Store the cream in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. Use a small amount on your oily hair to help control oil production and promote healthy hair growth.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, a potent antioxidant that helps to balance the scalp's natural oil production.

This antioxidant also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and inflammation that can contribute to oily hair. Additionally, rosmarinic acid has a natural astringent effect, which helps to tighten the pores on the scalp, reducing the amount of oil that is released. The camphor in Rosmarinus officinalis creams also helps to reduce the growth of bacteria and fungi on the scalp, which can contribute to oily hair and other scalp issues.

By balancing the scalp's natural oil production and reducing irritation and bacteria growth, Rosmarinus officinalis creams can help to regulate oily hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, and 1 teaspoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis.
  2. Melt the beeswax and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each heating.
  3. Combine the melted beeswax and shea butter with the coconut oil in a bowl. Stir until well combined.
  4. Add the distilled water and dried Rosmarinus officinalis to the bowl. Stir until the mixture thickens.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool before use. Apply a small amount to the ends of your oily hair to help control oil production and add shine.

4. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains eucalyptol, a key bioactive constituent that helps to balance the scalp's natural pH.

This balancing effect can reduce oil production, making it a good option for people with oily hair. Eucalyptol also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing redness and irritation. Cineole, another important bioactive compound in Eucalyptus globulus, has antiseptic properties that can help to control the growth of bacteria and fungi on the scalp, reducing oiliness.

By using Eucalyptus globulus creams, people with oily hair can achieve a healthier scalp balance and reduce excess oil production.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of Eucalyptus globulus essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl.
  3. Once melted, remove from heat and stir in the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil.
  4. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify.
  5. Apply a small amount of the cream to your oily hair after shampooing and style as usual.

5. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus creams contains a high concentration of limonene and linalool, two essential oils known for their astringent properties.

These properties help to reduce the production of sebum in the scalp, leading to a decrease in oiliness. The antiseptic properties of linalool also help to eliminate bacteria that can contribute to oily hair, creating a healthy environment for hair growth. Additionally, the antifungal properties of limonene help to prevent the overproduction of sebum, which can lead to oily hair.

By controlling sebum production and eliminating bacteria and fungi, Cymbopogon citratus creams can help to regulate oily hair and promote a balanced scalp.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil and 1/4 cup of beeswax. Melt them in a double boiler.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix 2 tablespoons of lemon juice and 2 tablespoons of Cymbopogon citratus essential oil.
  3. Add the lemon and Cymbopogon citratus mixture to the melted coconut oil and beeswax.
  4. Stir well and let the mixture cool until it reaches a thick, creamy consistency.
  5. Apply the Cymbopogon citratus cream to your oily hair, focusing on the scalp and roots.

6. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains aloin and aloe-emodin, which are active constituents that help to balance the scalp's natural moisture levels.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and redness that can lead to oily hair. The gel-like consistency of aloe barbadensis creams also helps to absorb excess oil and purify the scalp, leaving it feeling clean and refreshed. Additionally, aloe-emodin has antibacterial properties that help to control the growth of bacteria and fungi on the scalp, further contributing to a balanced scalp and reduced oiliness.

By using aloe barbadensis creams, individuals with oily hair can help to regulate their scalp's natural moisture levels and achieve a healthier, more balanced hair care routine.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup Aloe barbadensis gel, 1/4 cup coconut oil, 2 tablespoons beeswax, 2 tablespoons shea butter, 10 drops of your preferred essential oil (optional)
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water. Stir until smooth and melted
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the Aloe gel and shea butter until well combined
  4. Add the melted oil and wax mixture to the Aloe gel mixture. Mix until smooth and well combined
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, use it as a leave-in hair cream for oily hair

7. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams contains active constituents like thymol and carvacrol, which are known for their antiseptic and astringent properties.

These properties help to balance the scalp's natural oils and reduce oil production, making it an effective solution for oily hair. Thymol also has antifungal properties, which can help control scalp conditions that contribute to oily hair. The astringent properties of thymol and carvacrol help to tighten the pores on the scalp, preventing excess oil from being released.

By reducing oil production and controlling scalp conditions, Thymus vulgaris creams can help to keep oily hair under control.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, and 2 tablespoons of dried thyme leaves in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat, then add the coconut oil and dried thyme leaves. Stir until the thyme is fully infused.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax to the mixture and stir until it's fully melted. Let it cool and thicken.
  5. Once the mixture is thickened, transfer it to a glass jar and store it in the fridge. Use 1-2 tablespoons of the thyme cream on your oily hair after shampooing for a soothing and balancing effect.

8. Saponaria officinalis

Saponaria officinalis creams contains salicylic acid, saponins, and tannins as its active constituents.

These compounds have astringent, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties that help to balance the scalp's natural pH. The astringent properties of salicylic acid and saponins help to reduce the production of sebum, which in turn helps to control oily hair. Tannins also have a drying effect on the scalp, which can help to reduce oiliness and prevent clogged pores.

By using Saponaria officinalis creams, you can help to regulate your scalp's natural oil production and achieve a healthier, more balanced hair texture.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Saponaria officinalis flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of olive oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of honey.
  2. Combine the Saponaria officinalis flowers and coconut oil in a saucepan. Heat over low heat for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. In a separate saucepan, melt the beeswax and olive oil over low heat. Stir until the beeswax is fully melted.
  5. Combine the melted beeswax mixture with the Saponaria officinalis mixture and honey. Stir well and pour into a container. Let cool and harden before use.

9. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis creams contain compounds like ursolic acid, rosmarinic acid, and camphor, which contribute to its benefits for oily hair.

Ursolic acid has antiseptic and antifungal properties, helping to control scalp infections that can lead to oily hair. Rosmarinic acid reduces inflammation and fights bacteria, which can contribute to oiliness. Camphor helps to dry the scalp and reduce oil production, balancing the hair's natural moisture levels.

By regulating the scalp's natural oil production and controlling infections, Salvia officinalis creams can help to manage oily hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Salvia officinalis leaves and 1 cup of coconut oil in a pan.
  2. Heat the coconut oil over low heat, then add the dried Salvia officinalis leaves.
  3. Steep the mixture for 2 hours, then let it cool.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl.
  5. Mix 2 tablespoons of the Salvia officinalis oil with 1/2 cup of shea butter or coconut oil to create a cream, then store it in an airtight container.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent oily hair from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent oily hair from forming.

Some teas, like peppermint and sage, have natural astringent properties that can help balance the scalp's oil production.

When consumed, these teas can also help regulate the body's hormones, which play a role in controlling oil production in the hair follicles.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for oily hair every day?

Consuming herbal teas for oily hair daily is generally safe.

However, some teas can be too potent and cause an imbalance in your body. For example, teas like sage and peppermint can dry out your scalp, leading to more oil production.

Drink tea in moderation, and note how your body reacts.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in oily hair?

Herbal teas can take a few weeks to a month to show results in oily hair.

This is because they help balance your body's natural oil production, which can take some time.

Drinking tea regularly, like peppermint or green tea, can help reduce oiliness and promote a healthier scalp.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for oily hair?

Drinking herbal tea for oily hair is most effective when done at night, before bed.

This allows the tea's ingredients to work while you sleep, reducing oil production and balancing your scalp's natural pH.

Try drinking it around 8-10 pm to help control oiliness and promote a healthy scalp.

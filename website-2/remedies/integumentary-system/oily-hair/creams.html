What herbal creams you should avoid if you have oily hair?

If you have oily hair, there are some herbal creams you should avoid.

For example, creams containing Calendula (Calendula officinalis) may not be a good choice. This is because Calendula can be too rich and heavy for oily hair, which can make it look greasier. Another herbal cream to avoid is that containing Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla). While Chamomile is known for its soothing properties, it can also add extra moisture to your hair, leading to oiliness.

You should also be cautious with creams containing Passionflower (Passiflora incarnata), as it can clog pores and exacerbate oily hair. Another herb to avoid is Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana), which can be too drying for oily hair. If not used carefully, it can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it look even oilier. Additionally, creams containing Valerian (Valeriana officinalis) should be used with caution, as it can be too rich and heavy for oily hair. Similarly, creams containing Sage (Salvia officinalis) should be avoided, as it can be too drying for oily hair.

Remember to always read the label carefully and look for creams that are specifically formulated for oily hair.

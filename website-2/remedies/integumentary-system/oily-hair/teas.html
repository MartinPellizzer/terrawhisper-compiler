7 Herbal Teas for Teenagers with Oily Hair

Teenagers often struggle with oily hair, which can be caused by hormonal changes during puberty.

These changes increase the production of sebum, an oily substance produced by the scalp, leading to greasy-looking hair. For teenagers, this can be a frustrating and self-conscious issue, especially when it affects their appearance and confidence. Oily hair can be difficult to manage, as it tends to become greasy quickly, making it hard to style and maintain. This can lead to feelings of embarrassment and low self-esteem, especially in social situations.

As a result, teenagers may spend a lot of time and money trying to find the right hair care products to control their oily hair. They may try various shampoos, conditioners, and styling products, but sometimes these products can strip their hair of its natural oils, leading to dryness and other problems. Herbal teas can be a helpful addition to their hair care routine. The active compounds in certain herbal teas may help to balance the scalp's sebum production, reducing the amount of oil produced and leaving hair looking cleaner and healthier.

These teas are often easy to make at home and can be a natural and gentle way to address oily hair.

1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas, also known as rosemary tea, can be beneficial for oily hair due to its natural properties and constituents.

Rosemary tea contains compounds like camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate, which have been shown to stimulate blood flow to the scalp and improve circulation. This increased circulation helps to reduce the buildup of oil and dirt on the scalp, leading to a decrease in oil production. Additionally, rosemary tea has antimicrobial properties, which can help to control the growth of bacteria and fungi that can contribute to oily hair and scalp conditions. To use rosemary tea for oily hair, start by making a tea by steeping 1-2 tablespoons of dried rosemary leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Let it cool, then use it as a final rinse after shampooing.

You can also add a few drops of rosemary essential oil to your shampoo or conditioner for an extra boost. For best results, use rosemary tea 1-2 times a week, and be patient as it may take a few weeks to notice the effects. You may notice a decrease in oil production, a reduction in dandruff and itchiness, and an improvement in the overall health and appearance of your hair. As you continue to use rosemary tea, you may find that your hair becomes less oily and more manageable, with a reduction in the greasiness and a decrease in the frequency of washing. Your scalp may also become less itchy and flaky, and you may notice a decrease in the appearance of dandruff and other scalp conditions.

With regular use, rosemary tea can help to promote a healthier scalp and a more balanced hair growth cycle.

2. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus teas, also known as lemongrass tea, has been traditionally used to help manage oily hair.

The tea contains properties such as citral and geraniol, which have antiseptic and astringent properties. These constituents help to control the production of sebum, the oily substance produced by the scalp. When consumed, the tea is absorbed into the bloodstream and reaches the scalp, where it helps to reduce the oil production, leading to a decrease in greasiness and an improvement in scalp health. To try Cymbopogon citratus teas for oily hair, start by making a tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried lemongrass leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it once a day. You can also add it to your regular shampoo or use it as a final rinse to help balance the scalp's pH and reduce oiliness. Drinking Cymbopogon citratus teas regularly can lead to a decrease in the amount of oil produced by the scalp, resulting in a reduction in greasiness and a cleaner-looking hair. Some people may also experience an improvement in scalp health, with reduced irritation and inflammation.

Additionally, the antiseptic properties of the tea can help to control the growth of bacteria and fungi on the scalp, which can contribute to oily hair.

3. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia teas, made from the leaves of the tea tree, can be a helpful solution for oily hair.

This is because the tea contains properties such as terpinen-4-ol, cineole, and limonene, which have natural antifungal and antibacterial properties. These compounds help to reduce the growth of bacteria and fungi on the scalp, which can contribute to oily hair. When used as a hair rinse, Melaleuca alternifolia teas can help to balance the scalp's natural pH, reducing the production of excess oil. To use Melaleuca alternifolia teas for oily hair, start by brewing a cup of the tea according to the package instructions. Then, after shampooing your hair, use the cooled tea as a final rinse before styling.

You can also add a few drops of the essential oil to your shampoo or conditioner for an extra boost. It's recommended to use the tea 1-2 times a week for optimal results. When using Melaleuca alternifolia teas, you may notice a decrease in oil production and a reduction in dandruff or itchiness on the scalp. The tea's antibacterial properties can also help to prevent infections and promote a healthy scalp environment. Over time, you may find that your hair feels cleaner and looks healthier, with a more balanced oil production.

However, individual results may vary, and it's always a good idea to do a patch test before using the tea on your hair.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas are known to help with oily hair due to its properties and constituents.

The tea contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are natural antiseptics that help to balance the scalp's oil production. The tea also has astringent properties, which can help to tighten the pores on the scalp and reduce the amount of oil being released. Furthermore, the tea's anti-inflammatory properties can help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and redness that can contribute to oily hair. If you're struggling with oily hair, try making a strong brew of Lavandula angustifolia tea and using it as a final rinse after shampooing.

This can help to balance your scalp's oil production and reduce the greasiness of your hair. You can also add a few drops of lavender essential oil to your shampoo or conditioner to further benefit from the tea's properties. When using Lavandula angustifolia tea for oily hair, you can expect to see a reduction in oil production and a decrease in the greasiness of your hair. Your scalp may feel soothed and calm, and your hair may look healthier and shinier.

You may also find that you need to wash your hair less frequently, as the tea's astringent properties can help to extend the time between washes.

5. Saponaria officinalis

Saponaria officinalis teas have been traditionally used to help manage oily hair, and for good reason.

The plant is rich in saponins, a type of natural soap-like compound that can help to balance the scalp's natural oil production. When consumed as a tea, the saponins in Saponaria officinalis work to stimulate blood flow to the scalp, which can help to reduce excess oil production and promote a healthier scalp environment. The tea also contains other beneficial compounds, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help to soothe and calm the scalp. To use Saponaria officinalis teas for oily hair, start by brewing a cup of the tea according to the package instructions.

You can drink the tea once or twice a week, or as needed. You can also use the cooled tea as a final rinse after shampooing to help balance the pH of your scalp and reduce oil production. When using the tea as a rinse, be sure to massage it into your scalp and leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing thoroughly. As you incorporate Saponaria officinalis teas into your hair care routine, you may notice a reduction in oil production and a decrease in dandruff or flaking. Your scalp may feel cooler and more balanced, and your hair may look healthier and more manageable.

With regular use, you may find that your hair stays cleaner for longer and requires less frequent washing.

6. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis teas, commonly known as green tea, have been used for centuries to help manage oily hair.

The properties of green tea that make it beneficial for oily hair include its high levels of antioxidants and catechins, which are compounds that help reduce inflammation and regulate oil production in the scalp. Green tea also contains tannins, which are astringent properties that help dry out the scalp and reduce the appearance of oiliness. Additionally, green tea has antifungal and antibacterial properties, which can help prevent scalp infections that can contribute to oily hair. To use green tea for oily hair, you can try rinsing your hair with a cooled green tea solution after shampooing.

To make a green tea rinse, simply steep a green tea bag in a cup of hot water, let it cool, and then pour it over your hair after shampooing. You can also add a teaspoon of green tea leaves to your regular shampoo or conditioner to give it an extra boost of oil-controlling properties. When using green tea for oily hair, you may notice a decrease in oil production and a reduction in the appearance of greasiness. You may also notice that your scalp feels cleaner and healthier, and that your hair looks shinier and more balanced.

As your hair becomes more balanced, you may find that you need to wash it less often, which can be beneficial for both your hair and the environment.

7. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas, made from the leaves of the thyme plant, can help with oily hair due to its antiseptic and astringent properties.

Thymus vulgaris contains constituents like thymol, carvacrol, and linalool, which have been shown to reduce sebum production, balance scalp pH, and inhibit the growth of bacteria that can contribute to oily hair. To use thymus vulgaris teas for oily hair, start by steeping one teaspoon of dried thyme leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Let it cool, then use it as a final rinse after shampooing. Repeat this process once or twice a week for best results. As you use thymus vulgaris teas, you may notice a reduction in oiliness, a cleaner scalp, and a healthier-looking mane.

With regular use, your hair may become less greasy and more manageable, and you may be able to go longer between washings without feeling weighed down by excess oil.

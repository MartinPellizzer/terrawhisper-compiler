10 best herbal teas for oily hair

1. Rosmarinus Officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis tea, also known as rosemary tea, is a great choice for people with oily hair. This is because rosemary has natural properties that help to balance the scalp's oil production. The tea contains antioxidants that can help to reduce inflammation in the scalp, which can contribute to oily hair. Rosemary tea also has a natural ability to stimulate blood flow to the scalp, which can help to remove dirt and excess oil. Additionally, rosemary has been known to help to regulate the scalp's natural oil production, leading to a healthier and more balanced scalp. By drinking rosemary tea, you may notice that your oily hair becomes less oily and more manageable, and your scalp may feel healthier and cleaner.

2. Melaleuca Alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia tea, also known as tea tree oil tea, is a natural remedy that can help with oily hair problems. The main ingredient, melaleuca oil, is known for its antifungal and antibacterial properties, which can help control the growth of yeast and bacteria on the scalp. These tiny organisms can cause an oily scalp and lead to greasy hair. By using tea tree oil tea, the scalp is cleaned and balanced, reducing the production of oil. Additionally, tea tree oil has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe an itchy scalp and reduce redness. This can also help prevent the buildup of oil and dirt on the scalp, making it easier to maintain a healthy and balanced scalp. Regular use of melaleuca alternifolia tea can lead to a decrease in oil production and a reduction in greasy hair.

3. Thymus Vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris, or thyme, is a natural herb that can help with oily hair. When made into a tea, thyme's active ingredients can be applied directly to the scalp. The antibacterial properties in thyme help control the growth of bacteria that can contribute to oily hair. Additionally, thyme tea has antifungal properties, which can prevent the buildup of yeast on the scalp, another common cause of oiliness. When the scalp is balanced, it can produce less oil, resulting in healthier-looking hair. Thyme tea can also help to reduce inflammation in the scalp, which can lead to an overproduction of oil. By using thyme tea as a scalp treatment, people with oily hair may find that it helps to regulate their scalp's oil production, leaving their hair looking cleaner and healthier. Thyme tea can be applied to the hair and left on for a few minutes before rinsing, or used as a final rinse after shampooing to help lock in benefits.

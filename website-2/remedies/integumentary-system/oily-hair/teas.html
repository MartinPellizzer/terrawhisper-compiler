7 Herbal Teas For Oily Hair

Teenagers often struggle with oily hair, which can be caused by hormonal changes during puberty.

These changes increase the production of sebum, an oily substance produced by the scalp, leading to greasy-looking hair. For teenagers, this can be a frustrating and self-conscious issue, especially when it affects their appearance and confidence. Oily hair can be difficult to manage, as it tends to become greasy quickly, making it hard to style and maintain. This can lead to feelings of embarrassment and low self-esteem, especially in social situations.

As a result, teenagers may spend a lot of time and money trying to find the right hair care products to control their oily hair. They may try various shampoos, conditioners, and styling products, but sometimes these products can strip their hair of its natural oils, leading to dryness and other problems. Herbal teas can be a helpful addition to their hair care routine. The active compounds in certain herbal teas may help to balance the scalp's sebum production, reducing the amount of oil produced and leaving hair looking cleaner and healthier.

These teas are often easy to make at home and can be a natural and gentle way to address oily hair.

1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains rosmarinic acid, a powerful antioxidant and anti-inflammatory compound that helps control oil production on the scalp.

This tea also contains camphor, a natural astringent that helps reduce excess sebum and balance the scalp's natural pH. When you drink Rosmarinus officinalis tea, these properties work together to reduce oiliness and promote a healthier scalp. To use Rosmarinus officinalis tea for oily hair, start by drinking 1-2 cups of tea per day. You can steep 1 teaspoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes to make a strong tea.

Strain the tea and let it cool before drinking. For an added benefit, try applying a cooled tea compress to your scalp after shampooing to help balance your scalp's natural oil production. As you drink Rosmarinus officinalis tea regularly, you may notice a decrease in oiliness and an improvement in scalp health. Your hair may start to look cleaner and feel lighter, with a reduced appearance of oiliness at the roots.

Additionally, the antioxidants in Rosmarinus officinalis tea may help protect your hair from environmental stressors and promote a healthier, shinier appearance.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which have antiseptic and astringent properties.

These properties help to reduce oil production in the scalp, thereby controlling oily hair. The astringent properties also help to tighten the scalp's pores, making it less prone to oil buildup. To use Lavandula angustifolia teas for oily hair, you can add a teaspoon of dried flowers to a cup of hot water, let it steep for 5-7 minutes, and then strain it.

You can use this tea as a final rinse after shampooing your hair. When you use Lavandula angustifolia teas, you can expect to see a reduction in oiliness, a decrease in dandruff, and an improvement in scalp health. The tea may also help to balance your scalp's pH, which can help to prevent irritation and itchiness.

Regular use may lead to a reduction in the frequency of washing your hair, as it will be less oily.

3. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus teas contains properties that can help with oily hair.

It has been found that the tea's high content of citral and limonene, two types of compounds with antifungal and antibacterial properties, can help reduce the amount of oil produced by the scalp. These compounds also have astringent properties, which can help tighten the hair follicles and reduce oiliness. Additionally, the tea's antioxidant properties can help soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and inflammation that can contribute to oily hair. To use Cymbopogon citratus teas for oily hair, you can try the following: - Steep a teaspoon of dried Cymbopogon citratus leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

- Strain the tea and let it cool down. - Use the cooled tea as a final rinse after shampooing your hair. - Leave the tea on your hair for a few minutes before rinsing it off with cold water. After using Cymbopogon citratus teas, you may notice a reduction in oiliness and a cleaner scalp. The astringent properties of the tea can help tighten the hair follicles, reducing oil production and giving you a fresher, cleaner-looking scalp.

Regular use may also help reduce dandruff and other scalp irritations that can contribute to oily hair.

4. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas contains thymol, a natural compound with antifungal and antibacterial properties that can help control oil production in the scalp.

When consumed as a tea, thymol can reduce sebum secretion, leading to a decrease in oily hair and scalp. Thymus vulgaris teas also contain other beneficial compounds like carvacrol and linalool, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects that can soothe an itchy scalp and promote healthy hair growth. To use Thymus vulgaris teas for oily hair, start by making a strong tea by steeping 1 teaspoon of dried thyme leaves in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool before using it as a final rinse after shampooing.

You can also mix the tea with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and use it as a pre-shampoo treatment once or twice a week to help balance the scalp's oil production. When you start using Thymus vulgaris teas, you may notice a reduction in oiliness within 2-3 weeks. Your scalp may feel less itchy and irritated, and your hair may look healthier and cleaner. However, it's essential to note that everyone's scalp and hair are different, and results may vary. Some people may need to use the tea more frequently or in combination with other natural remedies to achieve the desired effect.

Be patient and consistent, and you may be able to find a natural solution to manage your oily hair.

When you use Melissa officinalis teas for oily hair, you can expect a reduction in oil production, a balanced scalp, and a decrease in dandruff and itchiness. 6. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis teas contain properties that can help with oily hair.

The teas are rich in catechins, a type of antioxidant that has been shown to reduce sebum production in the scalp. This can help to slow down the growth of oil, resulting in less greasy hair. Let it cool, then use it as a final rinse after shampooing your hair. Another option is to add a tablespoon of loose-leaf tea to your regular shampoo for an added dose of catechins and flavonoids. When using Camellia sinensis teas for oily hair, you may notice a decrease in oil production over time. Your hair may feel cleaner for longer periods, and you may need to wash it less frequently. You may also notice a reduction in scalp irritation and flakiness, leading to a healthier and more balanced scalp.

However, the effectiveness of Camellia sinensis teas may vary from person to person, and it's essential to be patient and consistent with your routine.

11 Herbal Tinctures For Oily Hair

Herbal tinctures can be a great way to help manage oily hair.

These natural remedies work by balancing the scalp's natural oil production, reducing the greasiness and weight of your hair. Herbal teas, in particular, can be a simple and effective way to achieve this balance. For example, rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis) has natural antiseptic and antifungal properties that can help to clear the scalp of dirt and bacteria, which can contribute to oily hair.

Tea tree oil, derived from the leaves of the melaleuca tree (Melaleuca alternifolia), has antibacterial properties that can help to control the growth of bacteria on the scalp, reducing oil production. Lemongrass (Cymbopogon citratus) is another herb that can help to balance the scalp's natural oil production, and its citrusy scent can also help to refresh and invigorate the hair and scalp. By using herbal tinctures to regulate the oil production of your scalp, you can achieve healthier, cleaner-looking hair that's easier to style and manage. This can be a big relief for people who struggle with oily hair, as it can be heavy and difficult to work with.

By finding a natural solution to this problem, you can save time and money on products and treatments that may not be effective in the long term.

1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis tinctures contains a combination of active constituents like camphor, bornyl acetate, and rosmarinic acid, which help to balance the scalp's natural oil production.

The camphor in rosemary tincture has a drying effect on the scalp, reducing oil production and preventing greasiness. Bornyl acetate, a natural antiseptic, helps to control the growth of bacteria on the scalp, which can contribute to oily hair. Rosmarinic acid, an antioxidant, protects the scalp from damage and inflammation, allowing the scalp to function properly and maintain a healthy balance of oil production.

By regulating the scalp's natural oil production and preventing bacteria growth, rosemary tincture helps to manage oily hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add 1/4 cup of glycerin to the vodka, then pour it over the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into another glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the Rosmarinus officinalis tincture in a cool, dark place and use 10-15 drops per shampoo for oily hair.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures contains the bioactive constituents cineole, linalool, and geraniol, which work together to help control oily hair.

Cineole has a drying effect on the scalp, reducing sebum production and balancing the oil levels. Linalool has antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and redness that can contribute to oily hair. Geraniol has antifungal properties, which help to control the growth of yeast on the scalp, a common cause of oily hair.

By reducing sebum production, soothing the scalp, and controlling yeast growth, Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures can help to keep oily hair under control.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add 1 cup of distilled water to the jar with the Melaleuca leaves and vodka.
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Label the jar with the date and the contents, and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

3. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus tinctures contains essential oils, including limonene and geraniol, which have a balancing effect on the scalp.

These oils help to regulate the production of sebum, the oily substance produced by the scalp, and reduce the amount of oil produced. The antifungal and antibacterial properties of the oils in Cymbopogon citratus tinctures also help to control the growth of yeast and bacteria on the scalp, which can contribute to oily hair. The antiseptic properties of citral, another active constituent in the tincture, help to reduce inflammation and promote a healthy scalp environment.

By regulating sebum production and controlling scalp infections, Cymbopogon citratus tinctures can help to reduce the oiliness of hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass container.
  2. Combine the Cymbopogon citratus leaves and vodka in the container. Make sure the leaves are completely submerged.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the container daily.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Use 5-7 drops of the tincture as a hair treatment, mixed with your regular shampoo.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia tinctures contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and geraniol as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antiseptic and astringent properties that help to balance the scalp's natural oils. The antiseptic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate reduce the growth of bacteria and fungi that contribute to oily hair and scalp irritation. The astringent properties of geraniol help to tighten the hair follicles and reduce the production of sebum.

By regulating the scalp's oil production and reducing irritation, Lavandula angustifolia tinctures can help to control oily hair and promote a healthier scalp.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the flowers and vodka in the jar, making sure the flowers are completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After the infusion time, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid and use 5-7 drops in your hair mask or shampoo to help control oily hair.

5. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis tinctures contains salvenic acid, rosmarinic acid, and ursolic acid as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have been shown to have a balancing effect on the scalp's natural oil production. Salvenic acid, in particular, has antiseptic and astringent properties that help to reduce oiliness and prevent buildup on the hair and scalp. Rosmarinic acid also contributes to its ability to regulate sebum production, while ursolic acid has anti-inflammatory properties that soothe and calm the scalp.

By using Salvia officinalis tinctures, individuals with oily hair may experience a reduction in oil production and an improvement in the overall health of their scalp and hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the Salvia leaves to the jar and pour vodka over them, making sure the leaves are completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the tincture to a dropper bottle and use 5-10 drops to treat oily hair after shampooing, as needed.

6. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris tinctures contains thymol, carvacrol, and linalool as its key bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and antifungal properties that help control scalp infections and reduce flaking. Thymol also has astringent properties, which can help balance the scalp's natural oil production and reduce oiliness. Additionally, carvacrol has antioxidant properties that can help protect the scalp from damage caused by free radicals.

By addressing the root causes of oily hair, Thymus vulgaris tinctures can help promote a healthier scalp and more balanced hair growth.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves, 2 cups of vodka, and 1 cup of distilled water.
  2. Combine the Thymus vulgaris leaves and distilled water in a saucepan, heat for 10-15 minutes, then let cool.
  3. In a clean glass jar, combine the cooled mixture with vodka, making sure the leaves are completely covered.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean container, discard the solids, and use as a hair treatment for oily hair.

7. Eucalyptus globulus

Cineole has astringent properties, which help reduce sebum production in the scalp. This reduction in sebum helps regulate the balance of oil in the hair, making it less greasy and oily. The antiseptic properties of cineole also help prevent scalp infections that can contribute to oily hair.

Saponaria officinalis tinctures contains saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds help to balance the scalp's natural pH, reducing oil production and controlling sebum levels. The saponins in Saponaria officinalis have astringent properties, which help to tighten the hair follicles and reduce excess oil. 11. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains gingerols and shogaols, which are compounds that help to regulate oil production in the scalp.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce irritation and redness, creating an environment less conducive to excessive oil production. The antiseptic properties of gingerols and shogaols also help to prevent bacterial growth on the scalp, which can contribute to oily hair. Additionally, gingerols have been shown to stimulate blood circulation, promoting a healthy scalp and reducing oil buildup.

By balancing oil production and promoting a healthy scalp, Zingiber officinale tinctures can help to control oily hair.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent oily hair from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent oily hair.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and tea tree, have natural oils that can balance the scalp's natural oil production. When consumed, these oils can help reduce excess oil on the hair and scalp.

This might lead to less oily hair over time.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for oily hair every day?

Consuming herbal teas for oily hair every day is generally safe, but it depends on the type of tea.

Some teas, like peppermint or rosemary, may help balance oil production, but others, like sage, can be too drying.

Drinking too much tea can also lead to caffeine overload, so moderation is key.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in oily hair?

Herbal teas like peppermint and rosemary can help with oily hair.

It may take a few weeks to notice a difference. This is because the herbs take time to penetrate and balance the scalp.

You'll likely start to see less oiliness and a healthier scalp after 2-6 weeks of regular consumption.

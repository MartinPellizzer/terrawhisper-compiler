What herbal tinctures you should avoid if you have oily hair?

If you have oily hair, there are some herbal tinctures you should avoid.

Tinctures made from herbs like 'Sage' (Salvia officinalis) and 'Geranium' (Pelargonium graveolens) can be too drying for oily hair. Sage can strip the hair of its natural oils, leading to more oil production, which can worsen oily hair. Geranium, on the other hand, can cause the hair to become brittle and dry, especially when used in high concentrations.

Another herb to avoid is 'Ginger' (Zingiber officinale), which can stimulate the scalp and cause more oil to be produced. Tinctures made from 'Valerian' (Valeriana officinalis) and 'Yarrow' (Achillea millefolium) can also be problematic for oily hair. Valerian can cause the hair to become greasy and weighed down, while Yarrow can cause irritation and dryness on the scalp.

'Witch Hazel' (Hamamelis virginiana) tincture can also be too astringent for oily hair, causing it to become dry and brittle.

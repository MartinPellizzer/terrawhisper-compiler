What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have eye infection?

If you have an eye infection, you should avoid using herbal decoctions made from certain herbs that can irritate or exacerbate the condition.

One such herb is Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis), as it may cause eye redness, itching, and tearing. Another herb to avoid is Eyebright (Baptisia tinctoria), as it can cause eye irritation, especially if used in large quantities or for prolonged periods.

Similarly, decoctions made from St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) should be avoided, as they contain compounds that can cause photosensitivity, making eye infections worse. You should also avoid using decoctions made from Garlic (Allium sativum), as its sulfur compounds can cause eye irritation and potentially worsen an existing eye infection.

Additionally, decoctions made from Turmeric (Curcuma longa) and Ginger (Zingiber officinale) can be problematic if used in excess or for prolonged periods, as they can cause eye irritation and potentially exacerbate an eye infection.

What herbal teas you should avoid if you have eye infection?

If you have an eye infection, it's best to avoid some herbal teas that can irritate your eyes or make the infection worse.

One herb to steer clear of is Ginkgo biloba. This herb can cause eye irritation, redness, and even vision problems due to its active compounds that increase blood flow. This increased blood flow can put more pressure on the eyes and worsen the infection.

Another herb to be cautious with is Eyebright's relative, Valeriana officinalis. While valerian root tea is often used for its calming effects, its sedative properties can cause drowsiness and blurred vision, which can be a problem when trying to recover from an eye infection. Additionally, some herbal teas like those made from Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) and Oregon grape root (Mahonia aquifolium) contain berberine, a compound that can increase eye pressure and worsen eye infections.

Lastly, avoid teas made from Burdock root (Arctium lappa) and Dandelion root (Taraxacum officinale) as they can cause eye irritation and increase the risk of infection spreading.

What Are The Main Causes Of Cracked Heels?

Cracked heels are a common problem that can be caused by several factors.

Dry skin is a main cause of cracked heels. When the skin on your heels is too dry, it becomes brittle and cracks easily. This happens because the skin doesn't have enough moisture to keep it healthy and strong. Cold weather is another cause of cracked heels.

In cold weather, the air is dry, which takes away moisture from the skin, making it dry and crack. This is why many people experience dry skin and cracked heels during the winter months. Soap overuse is also a cause of cracked heels. When you wash your feet too much, it strips away the natural oils in your skin, leading to dryness and cracking.

Soap can be harsh on the skin, especially if it's too alkaline, which can further dry out the skin and cause cracks.

Below are the main causes of cracked heels.

  • Dry Skin: Dry skin lacks moisture, making the skin on our heels hard and brittle, which can crack when we walk or put pressure on our feet.
  • Cold Weather: Cold weather causes dry air, which removes moisture from the skin on your heels, making them dry and crack.
  • Soap Overuse: Soap overuse can cause cracked heels because the harsh chemicals in soap strip away the natural oils in the skin, leading to dryness and cracking.
  • Footwear: Wearing shoes that are too tight or don't have a soft insole can cause friction on the heels, leading to dry skin and eventually cracked heels.
  • Poor Hygiene: Poor hygiene can cause cracked heels because dirt, sweat, and bacteria accumulate on the skin, leading to dryness, irritation, and eventually, cracks.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Cracked Heels?

If you have cracked heels, there are some herbs you should avoid using.

Tea tree oil (Melaleuca alternifolia) is too harsh for dry skin. It can cause further irritation and dryness, making your cracked heels worse. Turmeric (Curcuma longa) has anti-inflammatory properties but can be too drying if used too frequently. This can make your skin even more prone to cracking.

Ginger (Zingiber officinale) is another herb that can be drying, especially if it's not mixed with moisturizing ingredients. It's best to use it in small amounts and with caution. Cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum) is a strong spice that can irritate dry skin and make it even drier. It's best to use it in cooking or baking, but not on your skin. Sandalwood (Santalum album) oil can be too oily and greasy for dry skin.

It may feel soothing at first, but it can clog pores and make your skin feel worse in the long run.

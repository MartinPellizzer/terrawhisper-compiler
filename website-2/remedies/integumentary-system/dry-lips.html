What Are The Main Causes Of Dry Lips?

Dehydration is one of the main causes of dry lips.

This happens when we don't drink enough water, causing our body to lose more water than it takes in. As a result, our skin, including our lips, loses moisture, leading to dryness and cracking. Cold weather is another cause of dry lips. When it's cold outside, the air tends to be dry, and our skin loses moisture quickly.

This is because cold air has low humidity, which means it can't hold much moisture. As we breathe out, our warm air meets the cold air, causing our skin to lose moisture even faster. Dry air is also a common cause of dry lips. This type of air is often found in air-conditioned spaces, like offices or cars, where the air is circulated and cooled. The air may also be heated in homes during winter, which can dry out the air even more.

As we breathe out in these dry environments, our skin loses moisture, causing our lips to dry and crack.

Below are the main causes of dry lips.

  • Dehydration: Dehydration is a cause of dry lips because the body's moisture level drops, reducing the amount of water in the skin and lips, which can make them dry and chapped.
  • Cold weather: Cold weather causes dry lips because the cold air removes moisture from the skin on our lips, making them dry and chapped.
  • Dry air: Dry air removes the moisture from your skin, making your lips dry and chapped because they can't keep enough moisture to stay healthy.
  • Soriasis: Soriasis can cause dry lips because the skin condition triggers an overproduction of skin cells, which can lead to inflammation and dehydration in the skin, including the lips, making them dry and cracked.
  • Lupus: Lupus is a cause of dry lips because it can cause the body to produce abnormal antibodies that attack the skin's oil glands, leading to a lack of moisture and dryness on the lips.
  • Autoimmune: Autoimmune diseases can cause dry lips by attacking the body's natural moisture-producing glands and disrupting their ability to keep the skin hydrated, leading to dryness and chapping.
  • Nutritional deficiency: A nutritional deficiency in essential vitamins and minerals, particularly vitamin B, iron, and omega-3 fatty acids, can lead to dry, chapped, and cracked lips due to the body's inability to produce healthy skin cells and maintain moisture levels.

What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have glowing skin?

If you have glowing skin, there are some herbal essential oils you should use with caution or avoid altogether.

Cinnamon oil (Cinnamomum verum) can be too harsh for sensitive skin and may cause irritation, redness, and burning. This is because it contains compounds that can stimulate blood flow and increase heat in the skin. Tea tree oil (Melaleuca alternifolia) is often used to treat acne, but it can be too drying for some skin types and may strip the skin of its natural oils, leading to dryness and irritation.

Peppermint oil (Mentha piperita) can be too cooling for the skin and may cause a reaction in people with sensitive skin. Geranium oil (Pelargonium graveolens) can be irritating to some skin types, especially if used undiluted, as it can cause redness and itching. Lastly, Patchouli oil (Pogostemon cablin) can be too astringent for some skin types, causing dryness, irritation, and even skin discoloration.

It's essential to use these oils in moderation, dilute them with a carrier oil, and do a patch test before using them on your skin.

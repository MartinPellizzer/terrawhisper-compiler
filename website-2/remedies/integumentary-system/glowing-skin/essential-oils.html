1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its primary active constituents.

These compounds help to improve circulation and reduce inflammation, which can lead to a glowing complexion. Lavandula angustifolia essential oils also contain antioxidants that protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting a healthy and radiant appearance. The anti-inflammatory properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help to soothe and calm the skin, reducing the appearance of pores and fine lines.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healthy skin cell regeneration, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to create a smoother, brighter, and more even-toned complexion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat. Place the flower heads in a glass bowl or container.
  3. Pour the hot water over the flowers and let it steep for 2-3 hours. This allows the oil to seep from the flowers.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops on your skin to enjoy its benefits for glowing skin.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains compounds like aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A, C, and E. These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing redness and promoting a smoother complexion.

The oil's high water content and hyaluronic acid also provide intense hydration, locking moisture in the skin and keeping it supple. The antioxidants in aloe barbadensis essential oils, including vitamin C and E, protect the skin from environmental stressors and damage caused by free radicals.

As a result, the skin appears brighter, more even-toned, and healthy-looking, giving it a natural, radiant glow.

  1. Cut 2 cups of Aloe barbadensis leaves from a healthy plant. Wash them with cold water to remove dirt.
  2. Blend the leaves into a pulp using a food processor or blender. Use 1/4 cup of water for blending.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Let the liquid sit in the sun for 2-3 hours to allow the oil to separate from the water. Then, refrigerate it overnight.
  5. Strain the liquid again through cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass bottle. Store the Aloe essential oil in a cool, dark place.

3. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are responsible for its skin benefits.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help soothe and protect the skin from damage. The triterpenoids in Calendula officinalis essential oils, specifically calendulic acid and isocalendulic acid, have been shown to promote wound healing and reduce inflammation. The flavonoids and carotenoids in this oil, such as quercetin and lycopene, have antioxidant properties that help neutralize free radicals and protect the skin from oxidative stress.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the skin from damage, Calendula officinalis essential oils can help promote glowing, healthy-looking skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Calendula officinalis flowers and 2 cups of water in a clean saucepan.
  2. Heat the water to 100°F (38°C) and add the Calendula flowers. Let it simmer for 30-40 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl. Discard the flowers.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a still or a steam distillation apparatus to separate the essential oil from the water.
  5. Collect the Calendula essential oil and store it in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 5-7 drops per application for glowing skin.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce acne and promote healthy skin. The antioxidant properties of rosmary essential oil, particularly its high content of carnosic acid, protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, which can lead to premature aging. Additionally, the antiseptic and antifungal properties of rosmary essential oil help to soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing redness and inflammation.

This makes rosmary essential oil a valuable addition to skincare routines for achieving a clear and radiant complexion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of distilled water. Clean the leaves and dry them with a paper towel.
  2. Place the leaves in a glass jar and pour the distilled water over them. Cover the jar with a lid and let it sit for 2-3 days in a cool, dark place.
  3. After 2-3 days, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a steam distillation apparatus or a double boiler. Heat the mixture gently until the water evaporates and the essential oil starts to separate.
  5. Collect the Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil and store it in a dark glass bottle. Use 2-3 drops of the oil on your skin as a toner for glowing skin.

5. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and geraniol as its major bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties that help to soothe and calm irritated skin. The antiseptic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help to reduce the growth of bacteria that can cause acne and other skin problems. Melissa officinalis essential oils also has astringent properties, thanks to its geraniol content, which helps to tighten pores and reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles.

As a result, using Melissa officinalis essential oils can help to promote a clear and even complexion, giving the skin a radiant and healthy glow.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar and let it sit for 2 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar every day.
  3. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, squeezing the flowers to get all the oil out.
  4. Discard the flowers and transfer the oil to a clean glass bottle. Add 10 drops of vitamin E oil to help preserve the oil.
  5. Label the bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops of the oil in your skincare routine for glowing skin.

6. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, geraniol, and geranyl acetate as its active constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce acne and prevent the growth of bacteria that can cause skin infections. The anti-inflammatory properties of geraniol and geranyl acetate also help to soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing redness and promoting a more even skin tone. Limonene, on the other hand, has antioxidant properties that help to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, which can lead to premature aging and dullness.

By using Cymbopogon citratus essential oils, you can help to promote healthy skin and reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cymbopogon citratus leaves, a glass jar, and a clean cheesecloth.
  2. Wash the leaves with 1 cup of water to remove dirt, then pat dry with a paper towel.
  3. Fill the glass jar with 1 cup of carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil, and add the dried leaves.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. Then, strain the oil through cheesecloth into another container.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Cymbopogon citratus essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight lid, out of direct sunlight.

7. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol, borneol, and geraniol as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce skin irritation and protect it from environmental stressors. Thymol, in particular, has antimicrobial properties that can combat acne-causing bacteria, promoting a clearer complexion. The antioxidant properties of borneol and geraniol also help neutralize free radicals that can cause skin damage and discoloration, leading to a more even-toned and radiant appearance.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off bacteria, Thymus vulgaris essential oils can help achieve glowing and healthy-looking skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Thymus vulgaris leaves, a clean glass jar, and a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil.
  2. Place the Thymus vulgaris leaves in the glass jar and pour 2 cups of carrier oil over them.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the Thymus vulgaris leaves.
  5. Store the Thymus vulgaris essential oil in a dark glass bottle, away from direct sunlight. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in your skincare routine for glowing skin.

8. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum essential oils contains boiactive constituents like flavonoids, silymarin, and polyphenols.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals and environmental stressors. The silymarin in Silybum marianum essential oils has been shown to have a regenerating effect on skin cells, promoting healthy cell turnover and collagen production. This can help to improve skin texture and tone, giving the skin a more even and radiant appearance.

By reducing inflammation and promoting collagen production, Silybum marianum essential oils can help to give the skin a more youthful and glowing appearance.

  1. Gather 250g of Silybum marianum seeds and 1L of Carrier Oil (like coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Heat 500ml of Carrier Oil in a double boiler to 60°C. Add the Silybum marianum seeds and let it infuse for 2 weeks, shaking the mixture daily.
  3. Strain the oil mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the seeds.
  4. Add 10 drops of Vitamin E oil to the oil mixture to help preserve it. Mix well.
  5. Store the Silybum marianum essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight lid in a cool, dry place. Use 3-5 drops in your skin care routine for glowing skin.

9. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, licoricidin, and licorisoflavan A, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help to reduce redness and irritation in the skin, creating a smooth and even tone. Glycyrrhizin also has a moisturizing effect, locking in moisture and protecting the skin from environmental stressors. The antioxidant properties of licoricidin and licorisoflavan A help to neutralize free radicals, which can cause skin damage and lead to a dull appearance.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the skin from damage, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can help to promote a healthy and glowing complexion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, a double boiler, and a carrier oil like jojoba or sweet almond oil.
  2. Grate 1/2 cup of the dried root using a cheese grater and set aside.
  3. Combine 1 tablespoon of grated root with 1 cup of carrier oil in the double boiler.
  4. Heat the mixture for 2-3 hours, or until the oil turns golden brown and has a strong licorice scent.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth into a clean glass bottle, discarding the solids.

10. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like alpha-pinene, limonene, and eucalyptol, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help reduce redness and irritation on the skin. Eucalyptol, in particular, has decongestant properties that can help clear pores and improve skin tone. The antioxidant properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils also protect the skin from environmental stressors and damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and promoting skin health, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can contribute to a clearer and more even-toned complexion.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a clean glass jar, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Combine 2 cups of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil in the glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the leaves.
  5. Bottle the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops per application for glowing skin.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent glowing skin from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent glowing skin from forming, especially if you have acne or sensitive skin.

Some teas, like peppermint and chamomile, have anti-inflammatory properties that can calm the skin.

Green tea is also a good choice, as it contains antioxidants that can help reduce oil production and prevent clogged pores.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for glowing skin every day?

Herbal teas for glowing skin can be safe if consumed in moderation.

However, some herbs like turmeric and ginger can cause stomach upset if taken daily in large amounts.

Drinking herbal tea every day can also lead to caffeine overload if you choose teas with high caffeine content, like peppermint or chamomile.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in glowing skin?

Herbal teas can help with glowing skin, but it takes time to see results.

Some people notice a difference in a few weeks, while others may take a few months. This is because the teas are working from the inside out, nourishing your skin and body.

As your body absorbs the benefits, your skin starts to look clearer and healthier.

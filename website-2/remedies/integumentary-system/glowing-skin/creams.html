To make a soothing and nourishing Matricaria chamomilla herbal cream for glowing skin, start by infusing dried chamomile flowers in a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.

Combine 1 cup of dried chamomile flowers with 2 cups of carrier oil in a clean glass jar, and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every few days. After the infusion is complete, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.

Next, mix 1/2 cup of the infused oil with 1/4 cup of beeswax and 1/4 cup of shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water. Stir the mixture until the beeswax and shea butter have melted and combined with the infused oil. Remove the bowl from the heat and let the mixture cool and thicken before pouring it into a clean glass jar.

Once the cream has cooled and hardened, it's ready to use as a face and body moisturizer to promote glowing and healthy-looking skin.

What herbal creams you should avoid if you have glowing skin?

If you have glowing skin, there are some herbal creams you should avoid.

One such herb is St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum). It can be too harsh for sensitive skin and cause irritation, redness, and even burns if applied too frequently.

Another herb to avoid is Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis), which can be too drying and cause skin to become tight and cracked. Turmeric (Curcuma longa) can also be too harsh, especially if you have open wounds or sensitive skin, as it can cause burning, itching, and irritation. Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana) is also not recommended, as it can be too astringent and cause dryness, especially if you have dry or mature skin.

Finally, you should also avoid creams containing Sassafras (Sassafras albidum), which can be toxic and cause skin irritation, itching, and even rashes.

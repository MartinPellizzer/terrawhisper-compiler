Stye Remedies for Contact Lens Users: 10 Herbal Creams

Contact lens wearers are more prone to stye due to the constant moisture trapped between the lens and their eye.

This creates a perfect environment for bacteria to grow, leading to an infection. When a stye occurs, it can cause discomfort, redness, and swelling, making it hard to wear contact lenses. This can be frustrating, especially for people who rely on their lenses for daily activities, work, or sports. Some may even need to take time off from wearing their lenses to allow the stye to heal, which can be inconvenient. A stye can also affect the lens itself, causing it to become dislodged or even get stuck on the eye.

In severe cases, it can lead to more serious eye infections or vision problems. Herbal creams can provide relief for contact lens wearers suffering from stye. These creams work by targeting the underlying causes of the infection, such as bacterial growth and inflammation. By applying a thin layer to the affected area, the cream helps to reduce redness, swelling, and discomfort. Its antimicrobial properties also help to combat the bacteria causing the stye, promoting faster healing and preventing further infection.

Additionally, herbal creams can be gentle on the eyes and won't irritate the contact lens or cause any adverse reactions.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams have been used for centuries to treat styes, small, painful bumps on the eyelids caused by bacterial infections.

This is because Calendula officinalis contains anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, thanks to its high concentration of triterpenoid saponins and carotenoids. These compounds help to reduce redness and swelling, and kill the bacteria that cause the infection. When applying Calendula officinalis cream to a stye, gently clean the area with a mild soap and lukewarm water to remove any dirt or debris.

Then, apply a small amount of the cream directly to the affected area, using a cotton swab or your clean finger. Repeat this process several times a day, and keep the area clean and moist to promote healing. Within a few days, you may start to notice the stye begin to shrink and disappear, and the pain and redness may start to subside. As the infection clears up, the stye should eventually fall off on its own.

However, if the stye persists, or if you experience increased pain or vision problems, it's best to consult a doctor to rule out any underlying conditions that may require medical attention.

To use Melaleuca alternifolia cream for a stye, gently apply a small amount to a clean cotton swab and dab it onto the affected area. You can repeat this process 2-3 times a day.

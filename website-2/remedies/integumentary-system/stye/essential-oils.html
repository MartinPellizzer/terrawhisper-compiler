7 Herbal Essential Oils For Stye

Herbal essential oils can be a great way to help treat and prevent styes.

A stye is a painful and often unsightly infection of the eyelid. Drinking herbal teas made from these plants can help to bring down the swelling and redness associated with a stye, making it less painful and easier to manage. Additionally, these teas can also help to boost your immune system, which can help to prevent future stye infections.

When used in moderation and as part of a healthy lifestyle, herbal essential oils and teas can be a natural and effective way to help manage the symptoms of a stye and promote overall health and well-being.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains a high concentration of compounds like cineole, linalool, and geraniol, which are known for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to combat the bacterial and fungal infections that often cause stye. The essential oil's potent antioxidant activity also helps to reduce inflammation and promote healing in the affected area. Additionally, its antiseptic properties help to prevent the spread of infection and promote a clean environment for the stye to heal.

By applying Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils to the affected area, you can create an environment that is conducive to healing and recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat until it boils.
  3. Reduce heat to low and add the Melaleuca alternifolia leaves.
  4. Steep the leaves for 20-30 minutes, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean bowl.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops of the essential oil in a carrier oil for stye treatment.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help soothe and calm the skin around a stye. The flavonoids in Calendula officinalis essential oils have antioxidant properties, reducing oxidative stress and promoting tissue repair. The antimicrobial properties help combat bacterial infections that can cause styes, while the anti-inflammatory properties reduce swelling and redness.

By addressing both the bacterial and inflammatory aspects of a stye, Calendula officinalis essential oils can aid in promoting a faster recovery.

  1. Gather materials: Calendula flowers, carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), clean glass jar, cheesecloth, and a small saucepan.
  2. Measure 1 cup of Calendula flowers and place them in a clean glass jar.
  3. Add 2 cups of carrier oil to the jar with the Calendula flowers. Stir well to combine.
  4. Heat the mixture in a small saucepan over low heat for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally, until the oil is infused with the Calendula properties.
  5. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth into a clean glass jar. Discard the solids and let the oil cool. Use the Calendula oil to treat styes, applying a few drops to a cotton ball and gently pressing it onto the affected area.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains a compound called cineole, which has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling and fight bacterial infections that can cause stye. Cineole also has a cooling effect on the skin, which can provide relief from the discomfort and pain associated with a stye. The essential oil's antiseptic properties help to prevent the spread of infection, promoting a faster healing process.

By reducing inflammation and fighting infection, Eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help to alleviate the symptoms of a stye.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat until it reaches 100°C (212°F).
  3. Add the Eucalyptus globulus leaves to the hot water and let it steep for 30 minutes.
  4. Remove the pot from heat and let it cool for 10 minutes. Then, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl.
  5. Let the liquid sit in the bowl for 24 hours to allow the oil to separate from the water. Then, carefully pour the oil into a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two key bioactive constituents that contribute to its therapeutic properties.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, which are beneficial in addressing eye infections that cause styes. The antiseptic properties of linalool help to reduce bacterial growth and prevent further infection, while linalyl acetate's soothing effects calm the affected area. Additionally, the essential oil's anti-inflammatory properties help to reduce swelling and ease discomfort associated with styes.

By incorporating Lavandula angustifolia essential oils into a treatment plan, individuals may experience relief from stye symptoms and promote the healing process.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and clean them with water.
  2. Use 2 cups of water and bring it to a boil in a large pot. Reduce heat and let it simmer.
  3. Add 1 cup of water to the clean Lavandula flowers and let it steep for 30-40 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a still or a distillation apparatus and heat it to separate the oil from the water. Collect the essential oil and store it in a dark bottle.

5. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains a number of biologically active constituents, including alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce the swelling and redness associated with a stye. The antiseptic properties of rosmarinic acid and caffeic acid can also help to prevent infection and promote healing in the affected area. Additionally, the essential oil's antimicrobial properties can help to eliminate the bacteria that cause styes.

By reducing inflammation, preventing infection, and promoting healing, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help to alleviate the symptoms of a stye.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and add the Echinacea flowers.
  3. Let it simmer for 30 minutes, then let it cool.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean cup. Discard the solids.
  5. Use a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil, and mix 10 drops of the strained liquid with 1 tablespoon of the carrier oil to create your essential oil.

6. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains the bioactive constituents aloemodin, aloe-emetin, and aloe-resin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and redness associated with stye. Aloe barbadensis essential oils also contains antibacterial properties, thanks to the presence of compounds like aloe-emodin, which inhibit the growth of bacteria that can cause infections. The antiseptic properties of aloe-emodin and other bioactive constituents help prevent the spread of infection and promote healing.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infection, Aloe barbadensis essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of stye and promote recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel and 1/2 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Combine the Aloe gel and carrier oil in a bowl. Mix well until smooth.
  3. Add 10 drops of tea tree oil to the mixture. Tea tree oil has antibacterial properties that help fight stye infection.
  4. Pour the mixture into a dark glass bottle. Store it in a cool, dry place.
  5. To use, apply 2-3 drops of the oil mixture directly to the affected area 2-3 times a day.

7. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol, carvacrol, and linalool as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that can help to combat bacterial and fungal infections causing stye. Thymol, in particular, has a strong antiseptic effect that can reduce the severity of stye symptoms and promote healing. The anti-inflammatory properties of carvacrol and linalool can also help to reduce swelling and pain associated with stye.

By applying Thymus vulgaris essential oils topically, you can take advantage of these properties to help alleviate stye symptoms and promote a healthy recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves, 2 cups of water, a saucepan, a cheesecloth, and a bottle for storage.
  2. Combine the Thymus vulgaris leaves and water in the saucepan. Heat it over low heat for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool. Strain the mixture using the cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Discard the Thymus vulgaris leaves and transfer the liquid to the bottle. Store it in a cool, dark place.
  5. To use, mix 2-3 drops of the essential oil with a carrier oil like coconut oil for treating stye.

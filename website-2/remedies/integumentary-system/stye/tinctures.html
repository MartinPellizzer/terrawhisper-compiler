1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains a combination of bioactive constituents, including triterpenoids, saponins, and flavonoids, that make it effective in treating stye.

The anti-inflammatory properties of triterpenoids, such as ursolic acid and oleanolic acid, help reduce swelling and redness associated with stye. Saponins, like calendulin and saponin-12, have antimicrobial properties that combat bacterial infections, including Staphylococcus aureus, which can cause stye. Flavonoids, including quercetin and kaempferol, have antihistamine properties that help alleviate itching and discomfort caused by stye.

By addressing the underlying causes of stye, Calendula officinalis tinctures can help promote healing and reduce the risk of complications.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Calendula officinalis flowers and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the Calendula flowers to the jar and pour the vodka over them, making sure the flowers are completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After the sitting time, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a clean glass bottle with a dropper lid and label it. Use 5-10 drops of the tincture in warm water 3-4 times a day to help treat a stye.

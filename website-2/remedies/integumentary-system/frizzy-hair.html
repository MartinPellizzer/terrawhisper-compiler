By incorporating these herbal remedies into your hair care routine, you can say goodbye to frizzy hair and hello to healthy, beautiful locks.

What Are The Main Causes Of Frizzy Hair?

Humidity is a major cause of frizzy hair.

When the air is full of moisture, it causes the hair cuticles to lift, making the hair absorb more moisture and swell up. This swelling leads to a frizzy appearance. Heat styling is another cause of frizzy hair.

When you use heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons, they can damage the hair cuticles and make them rough. This roughness allows moisture to penetrate the hair, causing it to swell and become frizzy. Genetics also play a role in frizzy hair. Some people naturally have hair that is more prone to frizz due to the shape and structure of their hair follicles.

This is because the shape of the hair follicle affects the way the hair grows out of the scalp, and some people's hair is more likely to curl or twist, leading to a frizzy appearance.

Below are the main causes of frizzy hair.

  • Humidity: When the air is full of moisture, it makes hair swell and lose its natural shape, leading to frizzy and unmanageable locks.
  • Heat Styling: Heat styling can cause frizzy hair because it damages the outer layer of hair, allowing moisture to escape and causing the hair to swell up and lose its smooth shape.
  • Genetics: Genetics can cause frizzy hair because the unique shape and structure of an individual's hair follicle is determined by their inherited DNA, which affects the way the hair shaft interacts with moisture and temperature, leading to frizzing.
  • Temperature: When the temperature and humidity in the air are too high, the moisture in your hair becomes trapped, causing the strands to swell and leading to frizzy and unmanageable hair.
  • Salts: Salts can cause frizzy hair because they absorb moisture from the hair, making the cuticles lift and creating a rough, frizzy texture.
  • Hard Water: Hard water contains high levels of minerals such as calcium and magnesium, which can strip the hair of its natural moisture and cause it to become dry and frizzy because these minerals disrupt the cuticle layer of the hair, making it difficult for the hair to hold its shape and stay smooth.
  • Product Buildup: Product buildup on hair is a cause of frizz because it creates a thick, heavy layer on the hair shaft that disrupts its natural moisture balance, leading to unmanageable, frizzy locks.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Frizzy Hair?

If you have frizzy hair, there are some herbs you should avoid using as hair treatments.

Sage (Salvia officinalis) can be drying to hair, making it more prone to frizz. Thyme (Thymus vulgaris) has a similar effect, as it can strip hair of its natural oils, leading to dryness and frizz.

Hyssop (Hyssopus officinalis) can also be a problem, as it has astringent properties that can make hair appear dull and frizzy. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) can be a bit of a mixed bag - while it's great for soothing dry scalps, its drying properties can also contribute to frizz in some hair types.

Finally, Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) should be used with caution, as its strong astringent properties can be too harsh for some hair types, leading to dryness and frizz.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures contains bioactive constituents like cineole and terpinen-4-ol, which have antifungal properties that help combat the bacteria and fungi that cause foot odor.

The antiseptic properties of these constituents help reduce the number of microorganisms on the skin, keeping the area around the feet clean and healthy. Cineole, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the growth of certain bacteria that contribute to foot odor. Terpinen-4-ol, on the other hand, has antimicrobial properties that help soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing the likelihood of moisture buildup and subsequent odor.

By targeting the root causes of foot odor, Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures provide a natural and effective solution for people who suffer from persistent foot odor.

  1. Gather 250ml of 80% Vodka and 250g of dried Melaleuca alternifolia leaves.
  2. Combine the vodka and dried leaves in a clean glass jar, making sure the leaves are covered.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the tincture to a dropper bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops per foot bath or mix with water for a spray.

2. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus tinctures contains a high concentration of geraniol, limonene, and beta-pinene as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and antifungal properties that help to reduce the growth of odor-causing bacteria on the skin. Geraniol, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of volatile sulfur compounds, which are a major contributor to foot odor. The antiseptic and deodorizing properties of Cymbopogon citratus tinctures make it an effective natural remedy for combating foot odor.

By applying the tincture to the feet, you can create an environment that is less conducive to the growth of odor-causing microorganisms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 1 cup of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Cymbopogon citratus leaves and vodka in the jar. Make sure the leaves are completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Cymbopogon citratus tincture in a cool, dark place and use 5-10 drops as needed to help with foot odor.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contains eucalyptol, limonene, and alpha-pinene as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have natural antifungal and antibacterial properties that help combat the causes of foot odor.   5. Transfer the Eucalyptus tincture to a clean glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the tincture in a foot bath or mix with water for foot spray to help reduce foot odor.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Linalool, in particular, has been shown to have a potent effect on Candida albicans, a common fungus that contributes to foot odor. The tannins present in Lavandula angustifolia also help to dry out the skin, reducing moisture that can contribute to bacterial and fungal growth.

By reducing the number of bacteria and fungi on the feet, Lavandula angustifolia tinctures can help alleviate foot odor.

  2. Add 2 cups of 80% vodka or high-proof spirits to the jar, covering the flowers completely.

5. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its antibacterial properties.

These properties help to inhibit the growth of bacteria that cause foot odor, such as Brevibacterium and Staphylococcus epidermidis. Gingerol also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce irritation and swelling in the feet, making it an effective treatment for conditions like athlete's foot. The antioxidant properties of gingerol help to neutralize free radicals that contribute to foot odor, while its antifungal properties combat fungal infections that can cause unpleasant smells.

By targeting the root causes of foot odor, Zingiber officinale tinctures can provide relief from this common problem.

  1. Gather 250ml of 80% vodka, a cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (Ginger) roots, and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Chop the Ginger roots into small pieces and put them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour the vodka over the Ginger roots until the jar is full, making sure the roots are completely covered.

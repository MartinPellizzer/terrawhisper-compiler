Nail Fungus and Diabetes: 11 Herbal Cream Solutions

People with diabetes are more likely to get nail fungus because their bodies have trouble fighting off infections.

High blood sugar levels can damage blood vessels and nerves, making it harder for the body to heal itself. Nail fungus can cause the nails to become thick, yellow, and brittle, which can be painful and embarrassing. It can also spread to other parts of the body, like the foot, and even cause infections that are hard to treat. This can make everyday life difficult for people with diabetes, as they may have to avoid walking barefoot or wear special shoes to protect their feet.

Nail fungus can also be a sign of a bigger problem, like poor blood circulation or nerve damage, which can be serious if left untreated. For diabetics, treating nail fungus can be a challenge because traditional treatments, like oral medications, may not be safe for them to take. That's why some people are turning to herbal creams to help fight off the fungus. These creams work by using natural ingredients to kill the fungus and promote healthy nail growth. They can be applied directly to the affected nail and are often gentler on the skin than other treatments.

Some people find that they are more effective than traditional treatments and have fewer side effects.

1. Origanum vulgare

Origanum vulgare creams are gaining attention as a potential remedy for nail fungus.

The creams contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which have antimicrobial properties that help combat fungal infections. These compounds work by disrupting the cell membrane of the fungus, ultimately leading to its death. Origanum vulgare creams may also contain other constituents like flavonoids and terpenes, which possess anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe and calm the affected nail area.

If you're considering using Origanum vulgare creams for nail fungus, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before starting treatment. They can advise you on the best application methods, such as applying the cream directly to the infected nail area 2-3 times a day, and for how long to continue treatment, usually several months. As you start using the cream, you may notice the nail discoloration becoming less intense, and the nail growth slowing down. In some cases, you may even see the nail returning to its normal color and shape.

However, it's crucial to be patient, as treating nail fungus can take time, and it's not uncommon for the fungus to recur.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams, also known as tea tree oil creams, have been used to help with nail fungus due to their antifungal and antibacterial properties.

The main constituent responsible for these properties is melaleuca oil, which contains compounds like cineole, terpinen-4-ol, and limonene. These compounds have been shown to inhibit the growth of fungi and bacteria that cause nail infections. To use Melaleuca alternifolia creams for nail fungus, apply a thin layer of cream to the affected nail and surrounding skin two to three times a day. Be sure to clean and dry the area thoroughly before applying the cream.

It's also essential to trim and file the nail to prevent further irritation and promote healthy growth. When using Melaleuca alternifolia creams, you may notice the nail fungus symptoms start to decrease within a few weeks. The nail may begin to grow out healthier, and the discoloration may fade. However, it's crucial to be patient and consistent with the treatment, as it may take several months to completely clear up the infection.

In some cases, the nail may need to be trimmed or filed regularly to prevent further irritation and promote healthy growth.

3. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams, also known as thyme cream, contain thymol, a natural compound with antifungal properties that can help combat nail fungus.

Thymol works by penetrating the nail plate and reaching the infected area, where it kills the fungus that's causing the infection. Thyme cream also contains other beneficial constituents like antioxidants and flavonoids, which help to protect the nail and surrounding skin from further damage. To use thymus vulgaris creams effectively for nail fungus, apply a thin layer to the affected nail and surrounding skin twice a day. Make sure to clean and dry the area first, and gently massage the cream in to help it penetrate.

It's also essential to maintain good hygiene, such as washing your hands regularly and keeping your feet clean and dry. Using thymus vulgaris creams regularly can lead to noticeable improvements in the appearance and health of your nails. As the fungus is killed, the nail will begin to grow out normally, and the discoloration or thickening caused by the infection may start to fade. However, it's essential to be patient, as this process can take several months.

It's also crucial to continue using the cream as directed and to combine it with other treatments, such as keeping your feet dry and wearing breathable shoes, to achieve the best results.

4. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis creams are being used to help with nail fungus due to their antimicrobial and antifungal properties.

The creams contain constituents like thymol, carvacrol, and ursolic acid, which are known to inhibit the growth of fungi. These compounds can penetrate deep into the nail and surrounding tissue to target the fungus.

To use Salvia officinalis creams for nail fungus, apply a thin layer to the affected area 2-3 times a day. It's essential to clean and dry the area before application, and to avoid applying the cream to broken skin. You may start to see improvements in the appearance and texture of your nails within a few weeks to a few months, such as reduced discoloration and a decrease in fungus growth.

However, it's crucial to continue using the cream as directed and to be patient, as the healing process can take time.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contain compounds like linalool, linalyl acetate, and camphor that are known to have antifungal properties.

These properties help fight the fungus that causes nail fungus, such as Trichophyton rubrum and Candida albicans. The antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties of Lavandula angustifolia creams also soothe and calm the affected area, reducing pain and discomfort. To use Lavandula angustifolia creams for nail fungus, apply a thin layer of cream to the affected nail two to three times a day. Be sure to wash your hands thoroughly after application to prevent the fungus from spreading to other parts of your body.

It's also essential to keep your nails and surrounding skin clean and dry to prevent further infection. If you use Lavandula angustifolia creams consistently, you may start to see improvements in the appearance and health of your nails within 2-3 months. The fungus may begin to clear, and the nail may grow out healthier. In some cases, the nail may even return to its normal color and texture.

However, it's also possible that the fungus may not completely clear, and in severe cases, it may take longer to see improvements.

6. Cinnamomum zeylanicum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum creams, derived from the bark of the Ceylon cinnamon tree, have been found to be effective in treating nail fungus due to its antifungal properties.

The main constituent, cinnamaldehyde, has been shown to inhibit the growth of fungi, specifically Trichophyton rubrum, a common cause of nail fungus. Additionally, the presence of eugenol and linalool also contributes to its antifungal effects. When applying Cinnamomum zeylanicum creams to the affected nail, it's essential to clean and dry the area thoroughly before application.

It's recommended to apply a thin layer of the cream to the nail and surrounding skin, ideally twice a day, and gently massage it in. As the cream works to combat the fungus, you may notice a reduction in discoloration and thickening of the nail. The nail may also begin to grow out healthier, with less brittleness and breakage. However, it's crucial to note that Cinnamomum zeylanicum creams may not work as quickly as prescription medications, and consistent application over an extended period is necessary to see optimal results.

It's also essential to maintain good hygiene and prevent the spread of the fungus to other parts of the body.

7. Allium sativum

Allium sativum creams are being used as a natural remedy for nail fungus, and they work by exploiting the antifungal properties of the plant.

Allium sativum, also known as garlic, contains compounds like allicin, which has been shown to inhibit the growth of fungi that cause nail infections. The cream is typically applied directly to the affected nail, allowing the active ingredients to penetrate and target the fungal infection. If you're considering using Allium sativum cream for nail fungus, start by consulting with a doctor or a pharmacist to ensure it's safe to use and won't interact with any medications you're already taking.

Then, carefully follow the instructions on the label and apply the cream to the affected nail, ideally twice a day. You may also need to trim or remove the infected nail to allow the cream to reach the fungus more effectively. As you use the cream, you may start to notice improvements in the appearance of your nail, such as a reduction in discoloration or a decrease in the thickness of the nail. You may also experience a decrease in the severity of symptoms like pain or discomfort.

However, it's essential to be patient, as treating nail fungus with Allium sativum cream can take time, often several months, and may require repeated applications to achieve desired results.

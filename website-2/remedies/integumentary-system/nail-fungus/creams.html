1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains compounds like eucalyptol, caffeic acid, and globulol that have antifungal properties.

Eucalyptol helps to reduce fungal growth by disrupting the cell membrane of the fungus, making it harder for it to survive. Caffeic acid has antimicrobial properties that help to prevent the spread of fungal infections. Globulol, on the other hand, has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to soothe and calm the nail and surrounding skin.

By using these compounds, Eucalyptus globulus creams can help to combat nail fungus and promote healthy nail growth.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water and 1 cup of coconut oil in a small saucepan.
  2. Add 1/4 cup of beeswax and 1 tablespoon of eucalyptus essential oil to the saucepan.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring until the beeswax is fully melted.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Strain the mixture into a bowl and let it cool completely before applying it to the affected nail.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains the active constituent cineole, which has antimicrobial properties that help combat fungal infections.

This cream also contains terpinen-4-ol, which is known for its antifungal properties that inhibit the growth of fungi. Additionally, the tea tree oil in Melaleuca alternifolia creams has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce redness and swelling associated with nail fungus. The antifungal properties of the active constituents in Melaleuca alternifolia creams help to kill the fungus that causes nail fungus, while the anti-inflammatory properties promote healing and reduce discomfort.

This combination of properties makes Melaleuca alternifolia creams a potentially effective treatment for nail fungus.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 10 drops of tea tree essential oil, and 10 drops of lavender essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water. Stir until melted and smooth.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from the heat and stir in distilled water. Let it cool for 5 minutes.
  4. Add tea tree essential oil and lavender essential oil to the mixture. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container. Let it cool and harden completely before use. Apply to affected nails as needed.

3. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams contains thymol and carvacrol, two active constituents that make it effective in treating nail fungus.

Thymol has antifungal and antimicrobial properties, which help to kill the fungus that causes infection. Carvacrol has antibacterial properties, which help to prevent the spread of infection and promote a healthy environment for the nail to grow. The antifungal properties of thymol also help to reduce inflammation and promote healing.

Thymus vulgaris creams can help to eliminate the fungus that causes nail fungus, promoting healthy nail growth.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 teaspoons of Thymus vulgaris essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a saucepan over low heat, stirring until beeswax dissolves.
  3. Add shea butter to the saucepan and stir until melted.
  4. Remove saucepan from heat and let cool slightly. Stir in Thymus vulgaris essential oil.
  5. Pour mixture into a container and let cool until solid. Use as a cream for nail fungus, applying 2-3 times a day.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol.

These active constituents have anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties, which can help combat nail fungus. Gingerol, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the growth of fungi, including those that cause nail infections. Shogaol, on the other hand, has been found to have a strong antifungal activity, making it effective in reducing fungal growth on nails.

By applying Zingiber officinale creams, the active constituents can reach the affected nail area and help reduce fungal growth.

  3. Add the beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and add the Zingiber officinale oil. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, your Zingiber officinale cream is ready to use.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and camphor, which are its active constituents.

These compounds have antifungal properties that help combat nail fungus. Linalool and linalyl acetate have been shown to inhibit the growth of fungi, reducing the severity of the infection. Camphor, on the other hand, helps to dry out the affected area, creating an environment that's less conducive to fungal growth.

By combining these properties, Lavandula angustifolia creams can help to alleviate symptoms of nail fungus and promote healthy nail growth.

  2. Heat the water over low heat, then add the dried Lavandula angustifolia. Let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix 2 tablespoons of coconut oil and 2 tablespoons of beeswax. Melt them in a double boiler or in the microwave.
  5. Combine the cooled Lavandula angustifolia infusion with the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture. Stir until it thickens into a cream.

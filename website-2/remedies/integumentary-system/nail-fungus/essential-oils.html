1. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, geranial, and neral, which are its active constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and antifungal properties that help combat nail fungus. The antifungal properties of limonene and geranial prevent the growth of fungi that cause nail infections, such as Trichophyton and Aspergillus. The antimicrobial properties of neral also help reduce the risk of infection and promote a healthy environment for nail growth.

By inhibiting the growth of fungi and promoting a healthy environment, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can help treat nail fungus and promote healthy nails.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and stems. Wash them with 1 cup of water to clean.
  2. Use a blender to crush the leaves and stems into a pulp. You need 1/2 cup of the pulp.
  3. Transfer the pulp to a saucepan with 1 cup of water. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2 days. This allows the essential oil to separate from the water. Skim the oil and store it in a dark glass bottle.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains a compound called cineole, which has antifungal properties that help combat nail fungus.

Cineole works by penetrating the nail and treating the fungal infection from the inside out. The antiseptic properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils also help to reduce the risk of infection and promote a healthy environment for nail growth. The antimicrobial properties of the oil, particularly the presence of globulol, help to eliminate fungal spores and prevent their return.

Regular application of eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help to treat nail fungus by killing the fungus and promoting healthy nail growth.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water.
  2. Put the Eucalyptus leaves in a large pot and pour in the water.
  3. Heat the mixture on low heat for 2 hours, then turn off the heat.
  4. Let the mixture cool and then strain it through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  5. Store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight lid.

3. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds like cineole and terpinen-4-ol, which have antimicrobial properties.

These properties help to combat fungal infections that cause nail fungus, such as Trichophyton rubrum and Candida albicans. Cineole helps to penetrate the nail plate and reach the infected area, while terpinen-4-ol has antifungal properties that inhibit the growth of fungi. The antimicrobial properties of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils also help to prevent the spread of infection and promote a healthy environment for nail growth.

Regular application of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils to the affected nails may help to reduce fungal growth and promote healing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves, 2 cups of water, and a large saucepan.
  2. Combine the leaves and water in the saucepan, and bring to a boil over medium heat.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the liquid has reduced by half.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean container, discarding the solids.
  5. Use a hydrosol or a carrier oil to dilute the essential oil to 5-10% concentration, and store in a dark glass bottle.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains compounds like camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate, which have antifungal properties.

These properties help to inhibit the growth of fungi that cause nail fungus, such as Trichophyton rubrum and Candida albicans. The essential oil's antimicrobial properties also help to reduce the risk of infection and promote a healthy environment for the nails to grow.   2. Combine the leaves and water in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Discard the solids and transfer the liquid to a still or a double boiler. Heat the liquid to 212°F (100°C) for 2-3 hours.
  5. Allow the liquid to cool and separate into an oil and water phase. Skim off the oil and transfer it to a dark glass bottle.

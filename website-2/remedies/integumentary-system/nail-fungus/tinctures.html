What herbal tinctures you should avoid if you have nail fungus?

If you have nail fungus, it's best to avoid herbal tinctures that can interact with your treatment or worsen the condition.

One such herb is 'Artemisia absinthium', also known as wormwood, which contains a compound called thujone. Thujone can cause liver damage and interact with medications you might be taking to treat nail fungus. Another herb to avoid is 'Hydrastis canadensis', or goldenseal, which has berberine.

Berberine can lower blood sugar levels and interact with diabetes medications. Additionally, 'Ginkgo biloba' tinctures should be avoided because they can thin the blood and increase the risk of bleeding, especially if you're taking anticoagulant medications. 'Berberis vulgaris', or barberry, tincture should also be avoided because it contains berberine, which can interact with medications and worsen liver conditions.

Lastly, 'Cinchona officinalis', or cinchona, tincture should be avoided because it contains quinine, which can interact with blood thinners and worsen bleeding disorders.

Older Adults and Nail Fungus: 10 Herbal Teas to Try

As people get older, their immune systems weaken, making them more prone to infections.

Nail fungus is one of the common infections that older adults can suffer from. It starts with a small white patch on the nail, but if left untreated, it can spread, making the nail thick, brittle, and painful to walk on. For older adults, nail fungus can be a real problem, especially if they have diabetes or poor circulation.

This condition can lead to discomfort, difficulty walking, and even social isolation, as they may be too embarrassed to show their feet in public. It can also be a sign of a more serious underlying health issue, such as a weakened immune system or poor foot care. If left untreated, nail fungus can lead to more severe problems, such as the nail falling off or even amputation in severe cases. Older adults need to take care of their foot health, and one of the natural ways to treat nail fungus is by drinking herbal teas.

These teas contain special properties that can help to fight off the infection, reduce inflammation, and promote healthy nail growth.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia teas, also known as tea tree oil tea, have been used to help with nail fungus due to their antimicrobial and antifungal properties.

This is mainly because of the presence of compounds like terpinen-4-ol, cineole, and limonene, which have been shown to inhibit the growth of fungi and bacteria that cause nail infections. To use Melaleuca alternifolia teas for nail fungus, soak your infected nails in a warm bath of the tea for 15-20 minutes, 2-3 times a week.

You can also apply a few drops of the tea tree oil directly to the affected nail and cover with a bandage. It's recommended to repeat this process regularly for several weeks to see noticeable improvements. As you take these actions, you may start to notice your nail fungus symptoms subsiding, such as a reduction in the discoloration, thickening, or brittleness of your nails.

The tea tree oil may also help to promote a healthy nail environment, allowing your nails to grow stronger and healthier.

2. Allium sativum

Allium sativum teas have been used to help treat nail fungus due to its antibacterial and antifungal properties.

The tea contains compounds like allicin, which is responsible for its antifungal effects. Allicin has been shown to inhibit the growth of fungi, including the fungus that causes nail fungus, such as Trichophyton and Candida. The tea also contains other compounds like flavonoids and saponins that have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help to reduce the severity of the infection. To use Allium sativum teas to help treat nail fungus, you can soak your affected nails in a warm solution made from the tea. To make the solution, steep one teaspoon of dried garlic in one cup of hot water for five to ten minutes. Then, soak your affected nails in the solution for 30 minutes to an hour, two to three times a week.

You can also add a few drops of tea tree oil to the solution for extra antifungal benefits. It's essential to note that you should not use the tea as a replacement for medical treatment, but as a complementary therapy to help manage the symptoms. Taking action with Allium sativum teas may lead to several outcomes. The tea's antifungal properties may help to reduce the severity of the infection, and in some cases, completely eliminate the fungus. You may notice a decrease in the thickness and discoloration of the affected nail, and an improvement in the overall health of your nail. However, it's essential to be patient and consistent with your treatment, as it may take several weeks or even months to see noticeable results.

It's also crucial to maintain good nail hygiene and avoid sharing personal care items to prevent the spread of the infection.

Origanum vulgare teas, also known as oregano tea, has been traditionally used to help combat nail fungus.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent nail fungus from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent nail fungus from forming, but its effectiveness is not fully proven.

Some herbal teas, like tea tree oil and peppermint, have antifungal properties that could potentially inhibit fungal growth.

However, more research is needed to confirm this and understand how herbal teas might interact with existing nail fungus.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for nail fungus every day?

Herbal teas can be a natural way to help with nail fungus.

However, consuming them daily might not be safe for everyone. Some herbs can interact with medications or have side effects if taken too frequently.

It's essential to be cautious and check the tea's ingredients and recommended usage before making it a daily habit.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in nail fungus?

Herbal teas may take a few weeks to several months to show results for nail fungus.

It depends on the severity and the type of tea being used.

Some people notice improvements in a month or two, while others may need to wait a few months to see any changes in their nail health.

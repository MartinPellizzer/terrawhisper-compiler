Eye Swelling: Causes, Symptoms, and Medicinal Herb Remedies

What are the causes of eye swelling?

Eye swelling can be caused by several things.

Allergies are a common cause. When you're allergic to something, like pollen or dust, your body releases chemicals to fight it off. This can cause your eyes to swell and become red and itchy. Chemical burns can also cause eye swelling. If something like bleach or acid gets in your eye, it can damage the delicate skin and cause it to swell.

Anaphylaxis is a severe allergic reaction that can cause eye swelling, along with other symptoms like difficulty breathing and a fast heartbeat. It's a medical emergency that requires immediate attention. Blepharitis is an inflammation of the eyelids, which can cause eye swelling. It's often caused by poor hygiene, like not washing your hands before touching your eyes, or by a bacterial or fungal infection. Conjunctivitis, also known as pink eye, is an infection of the eye that can cause eye swelling, redness, and discharge.

It's usually caused by a bacterial or viral infection, and can be spread through close contact with someone who has it.

11 ways to calm itchy ears with herbal teas for swimmers

As a swimmer, you might have experienced the annoying problem of itchy ears after a swim.

This happens because when water gets trapped in your ear canal, it creates a warm and moist environment that's perfect for bacteria and fungi to grow. This can cause an infection, leading to itchiness, redness, and even pain. It can be really frustrating, especially if you're trying to focus on your swim practice or compete in a meet. The itchiness can be so bad that it keeps you awake at night, making it hard to sleep. It can even affect your mood, making you feel irritable and uncomfortable. You might try to scratch your ear, but this can make things worse by pushing the bacteria or fungi further into your ear canal.

Some swimmers try to prevent this problem by using ear drops or cleaning their ears with cotton swabs. However, these methods might not be effective in the long run, and can even push the wax in your ears further into your ear canal. Herbal tinctures can provide relief from itchy ears. They work by killing the bacteria and fungi that are causing the infection, and also by soothing the inflammation in your ear canal. They are usually made from plants that have natural antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. When taken internally, these tinctures can help to reduce the severity of the infection and alleviate the itchiness.

They can be taken regularly to prevent future infections from occurring, making them a useful tool for swimmers who regularly swim in chlorinated pools or engage in other water activities that can irritate their ears.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures are made from the leaves of the tea tree plant, a natural remedy for itchy ears.

The tincture contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and terpinen-4-ol, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These properties help to combat the underlying causes of itchy ears, such as infections or allergies. If you're experiencing itchy ears, try using a few drops of Melaleuca alternifolia tincture in a carrier oil, like coconut or olive oil, and gently apply it to the affected area.

You can also add a few drops to your ear drops or ear oil if you have a product. To get the most out of the tincture, gently massage the oil into the ear and let it sit for a few minutes before wiping off any excess. By taking these steps, you may experience relief from itching and inflammation in your ears. The antimicrobial properties of the tincture may help to clear up any underlying infections, while the anti-inflammatory properties may reduce swelling and discomfort.

As the tincture works to soothe and calm your ears, you may notice a decrease in the intensity of the itching and a return to normal ear function.

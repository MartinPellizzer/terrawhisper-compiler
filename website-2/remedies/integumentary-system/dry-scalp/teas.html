Dry Scalp Got You Down, Runners? Try 7 Herbal Teas

Runners often suffer from dry scalp due to intense sweating and friction from tight hats or headbands.

This can lead to irritation, itchiness, and flakiness on the scalp. For runners, dealing with dry scalp can be a real challenge - it can be painful, embarrassing, and even affect their motivation to run. Imagine having to constantly scratch your scalp during a run, or feeling self-conscious about the flakes of skin falling off your hair. This can also impact their daily lives, making them feel uncomfortable and anxious.

When runners experience dry scalp, they may try various treatments like shampoos, conditioners, and medicated creams. However, these products can sometimes cause more irritation or have limited effects. This is where herbal teas come in - they can provide a natural and soothing way to calm the scalp and promote healthy hair growth. By drinking herbal teas, runners can help to reduce inflammation, balance their scalp's pH levels, and moisturize their skin.

This can lead to a reduction in itchiness, flakiness, and irritation, allowing runners to feel more comfortable and confident in their own skin.

1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas, also known as rosemary tea, are a natural remedy that can help alleviate dry scalp issues.

This is due to its high content of antioxidants, flavonoids, and essential oils, particularly camphor and borneol, which have antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds help to reduce redness, itching, and flaking associated with dry scalp, while also promoting a healthy scalp environment by fighting off fungal and bacterial infections that can contribute to the condition.

To use rosemary tea for a dry scalp, make a strong brew by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried rosemary leaves in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it to your scalp as a final rinse after shampooing. Leave it on for 15-20 minutes before washing it off with warm water.

After regular use, you can expect to see a reduction in scalp inflammation and an improvement in scalp health, leading to softer, more manageable hair.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia teas, also known as tea tree oil tea, can be a helpful remedy for dry scalp.

The tea is rich in compounds like cineole, limonene, and terpinen-4-ol, which have antifungal and antibacterial properties that help control the growth of yeast and other microorganisms that can cause dry scalp and flaking. The tea also contains anti-inflammatory compounds that reduce redness and irritation. To use Melaleuca alternifolia teas for a dry scalp, start by steeping 1 teaspoon of dried Melaleuca alternifolia leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it to your scalp as a final rinse after shampooing. You can also add a few drops of tea tree oil to your shampoo or conditioner for extra benefits. As you use the tea, you can expect to see a reduction in dry scalp and flaking, and an improvement in the overall health and appearance of your scalp. You may also notice a decrease in dandruff and itchiness.

Regular use of Melaleuca alternifolia teas may help to balance the pH of your scalp and prevent further dryness and irritation.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas can be a helpful remedy for dry scalp issues.

The aloe plant is rich in vitamins A, C, E, and B12, as well as minerals like calcium, potassium, and iron. Its gel contains compounds like aloin and aloe-emodin, which have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that can soothe and calm irritated skin. When consumed as a tea, the nutrients and compounds in aloe barbadensis can help to nourish and moisturize the scalp, reducing dryness and flakiness. To use aloe barbadensis tea for dry scalp, start by steeping a teaspoon of dried aloe leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it directly to the scalp. You can also add aloe vera gel or aloe tea to your shampoo or conditioner for an extra dose of moisturizing goodness. After a few days of using aloe barbadensis tea, you may start to notice a decrease in dryness and flakiness on your scalp. The anti-inflammatory properties of aloin and aloe-emodin can help to reduce redness and irritation, while the vitamins and minerals can help to nourish and strengthen the skin.

As your scalp becomes healthier, you may also notice an improvement in the overall health of your hair, including reduced breakage and dandruff.

4. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas, made from the leaves of the thyme plant, may help alleviate dry scalp issues due to its antifungal and antibacterial properties.

The constituents of thyme, including thymol and carvacrol, have been shown to inhibit the growth of fungi and bacteria that can cause scalp irritation and flaking. To try thyme tea for dry scalp, start by brewing a cup of dried thyme leaves in hot water, then let it steep for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and apply it directly to the scalp, massaging gently before rinsing with warm water. Repeat this process 2-3 times a week for best results. Likely outcomes include reduced flaking and itching, as well as a decrease in fungal and bacterial growth on the scalp.

Additionally, thyme tea may help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy scalp environment.

5. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus teas, also known as lemongrass tea, have been traditionally used to help alleviate dry scalp issues.

The tea is rich in antioxidants, anti-inflammatory compounds, and essential oils that work together to soothe and calm the scalp. The main constituents responsible for its benefits are citral, geraniol, and limonene, which have antimicrobial and antifungal properties that help to control the growth of fungi and bacteria that can cause dry scalp. When you drink Cymbopogon citratus tea, the antioxidants and essential oils are absorbed into your bloodstream and transported to the scalp, where they help to reduce inflammation, promote blood circulation, and balance the scalp's natural pH levels. To use Cymbopogon citratus tea to help with dry scalp, start by steeping a teaspoon of dried lemongrass leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it to your scalp as a final rinse after shampooing. You can also drink the tea 2-3 times a week to help promote overall scalp health. As you continue to use Cymbopogon citratus tea, you may notice a reduction in flaking and itching, and an improvement in the overall appearance and feel of your scalp. The tea may also help to promote the growth of healthy hair, and reduce the risk of conditions such as dandruff and eczema.

Additionally, the antifungal and antibacterial properties of the tea may help to prevent the growth of fungi and bacteria that can cause scalp irritations and infections.

6. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium teas, also known as yarrow tea, can be a helpful remedy for a dry scalp.

The reason behind this is due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce redness and irritation on the scalp. The tea contains flavonoids, such as apigenin and luteolin, and volatile oils like borneol and camphor, which have antifungal and antibacterial properties, thus controlling the growth of fungus and bacteria that can contribute to a dry scalp. To use Achillea millefolium teas for a dry scalp, you can make a tea by steeping dried yarrow flowers in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and use it as a final rinse after shampooing.

You can also add it to your regular shampoo or conditioner for extra benefits. When using Achillea millefolium teas, you may notice a decrease in itching and flaking on your scalp. The antifungal properties may also help to reduce dandruff and other scalp infections. The tea may also help to soothe and calm your scalp, reducing irritation and redness.

However, the effectiveness of Achillea millefolium teas may vary depending on the severity of your dry scalp.

7. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica teas, also known as nettle tea, can be a helpful remedy for a dry scalp.

The tea is rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which can help soothe and calm the scalp. The anti-inflammatory properties can reduce redness and itching, while the antioxidants can help protect the scalp from environmental stressors and promote healthy hair growth. To use nettle tea for a dry scalp, start by making a strong tea by steeping 1-2 tablespoons of dried nettle leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes. Let it cool, then apply it as a final rinse after shampooing.

You can also add it to your regular hair care routine by mixing it with your shampoo or conditioner. When using nettle tea for a dry scalp, you may notice a reduction in itching and redness within a few days. The tea can also help to promote healthy hair growth by reducing dandruff and flaking. Regular use may lead to softer, shinier hair and a reduced appearance of split ends.

However, keep in mind that results may vary, and it's essential to be patient and consistent with your application.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dry scalp from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent a dry scalp.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe and calm the scalp. These teas can also help balance the skin's natural moisture levels, which can prevent dryness and itchiness.

Regularly drinking herbal tea may keep your scalp healthy.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dry scalp every day?

Herbal teas for dry scalp can be a good option, but drinking them daily might not be the best choice.

Some herbal teas can cause allergic reactions or interact with medications.

It's also possible to overdo it on certain ingredients like peppermint or tea tree oil, which can irritate your scalp further.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dry scalp?

The time it takes for herbal teas to show results on a dry scalp can vary from person to person.

Some people may start to notice improvement within a week or two, while others may take a month or more to see results.

It depends on the severity of the dry scalp and the individual's response to the herbal tea.

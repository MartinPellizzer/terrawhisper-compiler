7 Herbal Teas For Dry Scalp

Runners often suffer from dry scalp due to intense sweating and friction from tight hats or headbands.

This can lead to irritation, itchiness, and flakiness on the scalp. For runners, dealing with dry scalp can be a real challenge - it can be painful, embarrassing, and even affect their motivation to run. Imagine having to constantly scratch your scalp during a run, or feeling self-conscious about the flakes of skin falling off your hair. This can also impact their daily lives, making them feel uncomfortable and anxious.

When runners experience dry scalp, they may try various treatments like shampoos, conditioners, and medicated creams. However, these products can sometimes cause more irritation or have limited effects. This is where herbal teas come in - they can provide a natural and soothing way to calm the scalp and promote healthy hair growth. By drinking herbal teas, runners can help to reduce inflammation, balance their scalp's pH levels, and moisturize their skin.

This can lead to a reduction in itchiness, flakiness, and irritation, allowing runners to feel more comfortable and confident in their own skin.

1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains properties that can help soothe and calm a dry scalp.

The main constituent, rosmarinic acid, has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce redness and irritation. The tea also contains other compounds like linalool and camphor, which have antiseptic and antifungal properties that help combat scalp infections. When you drink Rosmarinus officinalis tea, the rosmarinic acid is absorbed into your bloodstream and carried to your scalp, where it helps to reduce inflammation and promote healthy blood flow.

This can help to reduce the dryness and itchiness associated with a dry scalp. To try this remedy, start by drinking a cup of Rosmarinus officinalis tea 1-2 times a day, ideally after washing your hair. You can also use a cooled tea as a final rinse after shampooing to help lock in moisture and soothe your scalp. As you continue to drink the tea, you may notice a reduction in redness and itchiness, and an improvement in the overall health of your scalp.

The tea may also help to promote healthy hair growth and reduce the risk of further scalp irritation.

2. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus teas contains oils like limonene, geraniol, and linalool, which have anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties.

These properties help to soothe and calm an itchy, dry scalp. The tea also contains antioxidants like vitamin C and beta-carotene, which help to protect the scalp from damage and promote healthy hair growth. To use Cymbopogon citratus teas for a dry scalp, start by steeping a teaspoon of dried leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Let it cool down, then use it as a final rinse after shampooing your hair.

You can also add a few drops of the tea to your shampoo or conditioner for an added benefit. When you start using Cymbopogon citratus teas, you may notice a reduction in flaking and itchiness within a few days. The tea may also help to reduce dandruff and promote a healthy scalp environment, leading to shiny and healthy-looking hair. However, it's essential to note that everyone's scalp is different, and it may take some time to see noticeable results.

It's recommended to use the tea regularly for at least a month to see consistent benefits.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contain compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to soothe and calm an itchy dry scalp. The tea also contains antioxidants that can help reduce inflammation and promote healthy scalp skin. To use Lavandula angustifolia teas for a dry scalp, start by steeping a teaspoon of dried lavender flowers in a cup of hot water for 5-7 minutes. Then, let the tea cool before applying it to your scalp using a gentle massaging motion.

Leave the tea on your scalp for about 30 minutes before rinsing it off with warm water. You can do this treatment once or twice a week, depending on how often you need relief from dry scalp symptoms. By incorporating Lavandula angustifolia teas into your scalp care routine, you may start to notice a reduction in itchiness and flakiness, as well as an improvement in the overall health and appearance of your scalp. Your scalp may feel cooler and look healthier as the inflammation decreases.

With regular use, you may be able to reduce the frequency and severity of dry scalp episodes.

4. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas contains thymol, a compound with antifungal and antibacterial properties that help combat scalp infections and irritations.

It also contains antioxidants, which reduce inflammation and promote healthy skin. When applied topically or consumed as a tea, thymol helps break down and dissolve dead skin cells and other debris that can clog pores and exacerbate dry scalp conditions. To try Thymus vulgaris tea for dry scalp, start by steeping a teaspoon of dried thyme leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it to the scalp using a cotton ball or soft cloth.

Alternatively, you can drink the tea as a warm beverage, but be sure to dilute it with water to avoid irritating your skin. As you use Thymus vulgaris tea, you may notice a reduction in flaking and itching, and an improvement in scalp texture. Your skin may feel smoother and look healthier, and you may experience a decrease in redness and irritation. However, results may vary depending on the severity of your condition and individual tolerance.

It's also essential to maintain good scalp hygiene and use gentle hair care products to support the healing process.

5. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica teas contains compounds like histamine, acetylcholine, and antioxidants that help soothe and calm an itchy, dry scalp.

The anti-inflammatory properties of the tea, particularly from the flavonoids and phenolic acids, reduce redness and swelling associated with dry scalp conditions. Strain the tea and use it as a final rinse after shampooing your hair. You can also add it to your regular hair care routine by mixing it with a carrier oil and massaging it into your scalp.

When you start using Urtica dioica tea for dry scalp, you may notice a decrease in itchiness and redness within a few days. The tea may also help to reduce flaking and scaling associated with dry scalp conditions. As the tea works to calm and soothe your scalp, you may find that your hair feels softer and looks healthier. 6. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains compounds like eucalyptol, limonene, and camphor, which have anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties.

These properties make it effective in soothing and calming an itchy, dry scalp. The eucalyptol in Eucalyptus globulus teas helps to reduce inflammation, while limonene has antifungal properties that combat fungal infections that can cause dryness and flakiness. Camphor, on the other hand, helps to cool and soothe the scalp, providing quick relief from itchiness and discomfort. To use Eucalyptus globulus teas for a dry scalp, start by making a cup of tea by steeping one teaspoon of dried Eucalyptus leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool down.

Once the tea has cooled, dip a cotton ball or a soft cloth into it and gently apply it to your scalp. Leave it on for about 30 minutes to an hour before washing your hair as usual. You can repeat this process a few times a week for best results. As you use Eucalyptus globulus teas on your dry scalp, you can expect to see improvements in the following areas: reduced itchiness and inflammation, a decrease in flakiness and dryness, and an overall healthier-looking scalp. Your hair may start to look shinier and healthier, and you may even notice a reduction in dandruff.

However, keep in mind that Eucalyptus globulus teas is not a replacement for medical treatment, so if your dry scalp persists or worsens, be sure to consult a doctor or dermatologist for further advice.

7. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis teas contains compounds like borneol and camphor that help soothe and calm an itchy, dry scalp.

These constituents reduce inflammation and irritation, making it easier for the scalp to recover from dryness. The tea also contains antioxidants that protect the scalp from damage caused by free radicals. To use Melissa officinalis tea for a dry scalp, make a strong brew by steeping 1 teaspoon of dried Melissa officinalis leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. As you continue to use Melissa officinalis tea, you may notice a reduction in itchiness and flakiness on your scalp. Your scalp may also feel cooler and less irritated. However, if your dry scalp persists or worsens, consult a healthcare professional for further guidance and advice.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dry scalp from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent a dry scalp.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe and calm the scalp. These teas can also help balance the skin's natural moisture levels, which can prevent dryness and itchiness.

Regularly drinking herbal tea may keep your scalp healthy.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dry scalp every day?

Herbal teas for dry scalp can be a good option, but drinking them daily might not be the best choice.

Some herbal teas can cause allergic reactions or interact with medications.

It's also possible to overdo it on certain ingredients like peppermint or tea tree oil, which can irritate your scalp further.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dry scalp?

The time it takes for herbal teas to show results on a dry scalp can vary from person to person.

Some people may start to notice improvement within a week or two, while others may take a month or more to see results.

It depends on the severity of the dry scalp and the individual's response to the herbal tea.

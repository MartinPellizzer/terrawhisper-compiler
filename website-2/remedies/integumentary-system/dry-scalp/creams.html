What herbal creams you should avoid if you have dry scalp?

If you have a dry scalp, there are some herbal creams you should avoid.

For example, creams with peppermint (Mentha piperita) might be too harsh and irritating, causing more dryness and itchiness. Peppermint has a cooling effect, which can be soothing for some skin issues, but it can also dry out the scalp further if not used carefully.

Similarly, creams with chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) may not be suitable if you have a dry scalp. While chamomile is known for its calming properties, it can sometimes cause skin sensitivity, which can worsen dryness on the scalp. Another herb to avoid is tea tree oil (Melissa officinalis), which can be too strong and drying for a dry scalp.

However, note that tea tree oil is often used in diluted form in skincare products, so it's best to check the product's ingredients and instructions before using it.

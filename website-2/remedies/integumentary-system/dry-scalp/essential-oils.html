7 Herbal Essential Oils For Dry Scalp

Herbal essential oils can be a great help if you're struggling with a dry scalp.

But why do they work?. It's because these oils often come from herbs that have been used for centuries in herbal teas to soothe and calm the skin. When you drink herbal tea, the active ingredients are absorbed into your body and can help to reduce inflammation and promote healthy skin growth. The same thing happens when you apply herbal essential oils to your scalp.

For example, Rosemary essential oil (Rosmarinus officinalis) has antibacterial properties that can help to clear up infections and reduce dandruff. Lavender oil (Lavandula angustifolia) has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to calm irritation and reduce redness. Tea tree oil (Melaleuca alternifolia) has antifungal properties that can help to get rid of fungal infections that can cause a dry, itchy scalp. When you use these oils, you can expect to see a reduction in flaking and itchiness, and an overall improvement in the health of your scalp. However, if you're new to using essential oils, it's worth noting that you should always dilute them in a carrier oil and do a patch test before applying them to your scalp.

This will help you to avoid any skin irritation or allergic reactions.

1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains the bioactive constituents camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties that can help soothe an itchy and irritated dry scalp. The antifungal properties of camphor and bornyl acetate can help control fungal growth, which can contribute to dry scalp conditions. The anti-inflammatory properties of borneol can help reduce redness and swelling, promoting a healthy scalp environment.

By reducing inflammation and controlling fungal growth, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help alleviate dry scalp symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the leaves. Let it simmer for 2 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a still or a distillation apparatus to separate the essential oil from the water.
  5. Collect the essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use 5-7 drops in a carrier oil for scalp treatment.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known to have antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce irritation and redness on the scalp, making it an effective remedy for dry scalp. The antifungal properties of linalool also help to control fungal growth, which can contribute to dry scalp conditions such as dandruff. The soothing and calming effects of linalyl acetate can help to reduce stress and promote a healthy scalp environment.

By reducing inflammation and controlling fungal growth, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to promote a healthy and balanced scalp.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Lavandula angustifolia flowers, a clean glass jar, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Combine 1/2 cup of flowers with 1 cup of carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Lavandula angustifolia essential oil in a dark glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops on your scalp to help soothe dryness.

3. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains cineole, limonene, and terpinen-4-ol as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe and calm irritated scalps. Cineole and terpinen-4-ol have antifungal properties that reduce fungal growth and dandruff, which can contribute to dry scalp issues. Limonene has a cleansing effect, helping to remove dirt and oil buildup that can clog pores and exacerbate dryness.

By reducing inflammation, antifungal growth, and promoting a clean scalp environment, Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils can help alleviate dry scalp symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat for 30 minutes. Do not boil.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil, such as jojoba oil.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 2 hours. Strain the liquid and discard the leaves.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops for scalp treatment.

4. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole and borneol as its primary bioactive constituents.

These compounds have decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce redness and swelling associated with dry scalp. The cineole in eucalyptus globulus essential oils works by thinning mucus, allowing it to be easily washed away, while the borneol helps to soothe and calm the scalp. The antimicrobial properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils also help to eliminate fungal and bacterial infections that can contribute to dry scalp.

By reducing inflammation and eliminating infections, eucalyptus globulus essential oils helps to promote a healthy scalp environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat for 10-15 minutes. Be careful not to boil.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba) to the leaves and stir well.
  4. Remove from heat and let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight. This allows the oil to extract.
  5. Strain the mixture and discard the leaves. Store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

5. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains the bioactive constituents limonene, linalool, and geraniol, which have antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce irritation and inflammation on the scalp, making it an effective treatment for dry scalp. The antifungal properties of these compounds also help to control the growth of fungi that can contribute to dry scalp conditions. By reducing inflammation and controlling fungal growth, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can help to soothe and calm the scalp, promoting a healthy environment for hair growth.

Regular use of this essential oil may help to alleviate dry scalp symptoms and promote a healthy scalp.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Cymbopogon citratus leaves, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a glass bottle with a lid.
  2. Clean the leaves by rinsing them with water, then let them dry completely.
  3. Combine the dried leaves with carrier oil in a bowl. Use 1 part of leaves to 3 parts of carrier oil.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the bowl every few days. This will help release the oil from the leaves.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the glass bottle. Discard the solids and store the oil in a cool, dark place.

6. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that help calm irritation on the scalp. Aloin reduces redness and swelling by inhibiting the production of inflammatory chemicals, while aloe-emodin has antifungal properties that combat fungal infections causing dry scalp. Acemannan also has a moisturizing effect, helping to hydrate the scalp and restore its natural balance.

The combination of these properties in Aloe barbadensis essential oils makes it a potential remedy for dry scalp issues.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Aloe barbadensis gel, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of olive oil.
  2. In a medium saucepan, warm the coconut oil and olive oil over low heat.
  3. Add the Aloe gel to the saucepan and mix well.
  4. Simmer the mixture for 10-15 minutes, then remove from heat.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean glass bottle and store in the fridge.

7. Santalum album

Santalum album essential oils contains a compound called Santalol, which is a key active constituent responsible for its therapeutic properties.

The antifungal and antibacterial properties of Santalol help to reduce inflammation and prevent infections that can cause a dry scalp. The anti-inflammatory properties of Santalum album essential oils also help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing redness and irritation. Additionally, the antiseptic properties of Santalol can help to prevent the buildup of dead skin cells and other debris that can contribute to a dry scalp.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infections, Santalum album essential oils can help to promote a healthy scalp and alleviate dry scalp symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Santalum album (Sandalwood) wood chips, a glass jar, and a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil.
  2. Place 1/4 cup of wood chips in the glass jar and add 1 cup of carrier oil. Stir well to combine.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every few days to help the oil infuse.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the wood chips.
  5. Bottle the infused oil and use it to help soothe a dry scalp. Apply a few drops to your scalp and massage gently.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dry scalp from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe a dry scalp.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, have anti-inflammatory properties that can calm itchy skin. Others, like sage and rosemary, promote healthy hair growth.

These teas can be a natural way to calm and nourish your scalp.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dry scalp every day?

Herbal teas can be a soothing way to help with a dry scalp.

However, consuming them daily may cause interactions with any medications you're taking, like blood thinners. It's also possible that certain herbs can irritate your skin or cause allergic reactions.

Moderation is key to enjoying their benefits without any issues.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dry scalp?

Herbal teas can help with a dry scalp, but it takes time to see results.

Some people notice improvement within a few weeks, while it may take a few months for others. It depends on the individual and the specific tea being used.

Drinking the tea regularly can help reduce irritation and promote healthy scalp growth.

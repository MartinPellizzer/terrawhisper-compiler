What herbal tinctures you should avoid if you have dry scalp?

If you have a dry scalp, it's best to avoid tinctures made from herbs that can dry out your skin further or cause irritation.

Sage (Salvia officinalis) is one such herb to avoid. Sage can be too drying and may make your scalp even drier. It's also essential to steer clear of tinctures containing St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum), as it can cause skin sensitivity and irritation, making your dry scalp condition worse.

Ginger (Zingiber officinale) tinctures may not be ideal for dry scalp either. While ginger is known for its anti-inflammatory properties, it can be too stimulating and may cause skin irritation or dryness. Additionally, tinctures made from Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana) should be used with caution, as they can be too astringent and drying for a dry scalp. Finally, tinctures containing Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) may not be the best choice for dry scalp.

While Chamomile is generally soothing, it can be too drying and may exacerbate the dryness on your scalp.

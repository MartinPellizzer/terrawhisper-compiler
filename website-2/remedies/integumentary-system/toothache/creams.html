1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which are active constituents that help with toothache.

Gingerol and shogaol have anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling and ease pain. They also have analgesic properties, which block the production of pain-causing chemicals in the body. The warming sensation of Zingiber officinale creams can also stimulate blood flow to the affected area, which brings in oxygen and nutrients to speed up healing.

By reducing inflammation and blocking pain signals, Zingiber officinale creams can help alleviate toothache symptoms.

  1. Mix 2 tablespoons of grated Zingiber officinale (ginger) with 1 cup of coconut oil in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the mixture over low heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter to the bowl. Melt the mixture in a double boiler or in the microwave in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and harden before use. Apply a small amount to the affected tooth and gum area as needed.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help reduce swelling and fight off bacterial infections in the mouth. Alkylamides, in particular, can help reduce pain and inflammation by blocking the production of prostaglandins, which are chemicals that contribute to pain and swelling. Caffeic acid and rosmarinic acid also have antioxidant properties that can help protect the gums and teeth from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off infections, Echinacea purpurea creams may provide relief from toothache pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1/4 cup of coconut oil in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the coconut oil over low heat, then add the dried flowers and simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discard the solids.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter to the coconut oil mixture, heat until melted.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool, your Echinacea purpurea creams for toothache are now ready to use.

3. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains a high concentration of menthol and menthone, two active constituents that help to reduce inflammation and ease pain.

Menthol has a cooling effect that numbs the affected area, making it easier to bear the discomfort of a toothache. Menthone, on the other hand, has analgesic properties that help to block the pain signals sent to the brain. The antiseptic properties of menthol also help to prevent infection and promote healing in the affected area.

When applied topically, Mentha x piperita creams can help to provide quick relief from toothache pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of dried peppermint leaves, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add dried peppermint leaves to the melted mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Add vitamin E oil to the strained mixture and stir well. Pour the mixture into small containers or tin cans.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify. Once solid, label and store the Mentha x piperita creams in a cool, dry place.

4. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains a compound called curcumin, which has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

Curcumin helps reduce swelling and fight off bacteria that can cause toothaches. The creams also contain demethoxycurcumin (DMC) and bisdemethoxycurcumin (BDMC), which have potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These properties help soothe the pain and discomfort caused by toothaches, reducing the severity of the symptoms.

When applied topically, Curcuma longa creams can help ease toothache pain by reducing inflammation and fighting off infection.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Curcuma longa root, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1 tablespoon of beeswax.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the dried Curcuma longa root to the melted coconut oil and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Add the melted beeswax to the liquid mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated. Pour the mixture into a tin or jar and let it cool.

5. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains eucalyptol, which is a natural analgesic that helps to relieve toothache pain.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent toothache from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent toothache to some extent.

Certain herbs like chamomile and peppermint have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce gum swelling and ease tooth pain. These teas can also help kill bacteria in the mouth, which can cause tooth decay and toothache.

This can lead to a healthier mouth.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for toothache every day?

While herbal teas can provide relief for a toothache, consuming them daily may not be the best solution.

Some teas, like those with clove or peppermint, can irritate your mouth and digestive system if used excessively.

It's also possible to develop a tolerance to their pain-relieving effects.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in toothache?

Herbal teas can help with toothaches, but it's not a quick fix.

Some teas, like clove or ginger, might start to ease the pain within 15-30 minutes. However, it can take a few hours for the full effect to kick in.

This is because herbal teas work slowly to reduce inflammation and ease discomfort.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for toothache?

For a toothache, it's best to drink herbal tea in the evening before bed. This allows the soothing properties of the tea to work while you sleep, helping to reduce pain and inflammation.

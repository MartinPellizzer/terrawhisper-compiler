What Are The Main Causes Of Eye Bags?

Eye bags can be caused by several factors.

One main cause is allergies. When you have an allergic reaction, your body's immune system releases histamine, a chemical that can cause blood vessels to swell and become inflamed. This swelling can lead to the formation of dark circles and puffy eyes.

Sinusitis is another cause of eye bags. When your sinuses become infected or inflamed, it can cause fluid to build up behind your eyes, leading to puffiness. Dehydration is also a common cause of eye bags. When you don't drink enough water, your body can become dehydrated, causing the skin around your eyes to become puffy and tight.

This is because the skin is trying to hold onto as much water as possible, leading to the formation of eye bags.

Below are the main causes of eye bags.

  • Allergies: Allergies can cause eye bags because when the body reacts to an allergen, it releases histamine, a chemical that causes blood vessels in the face to swell, leading to puffiness and dark circles under the eyes.
  • Sinusitis: When the sinuses are inflamed due to sinusitis, it can cause puffiness and swelling in the face, including the delicate skin under the eyes, resulting in the appearance of eye bags.
  • Dehydration: When our body is dehydrated, the skin around our eyes doesn't have enough water to stay firm, causing it to look puffy and swell up, resulting in dark circles and eye bags.
  • Cushing's Syndrome: Excess fluids and fat in the body due to Cushing's Syndrome can cause the tissues around the eyes to swell, leading to the appearance of eye bags.
  • Obesity: Obesity is a cause of eye bags because excess fat around the eyes causes the skin to stretch and lose its elasticity, leading to the formation of puffiness and dark circles under the eyes.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Eye Bags?

If you have eye bags, it's best to avoid herbs that can make them look worse or cause more puffiness.

One herb to avoid is Peppermint (Mentha piperita). It has a cooling effect, which might be good for some skin problems, but it can also increase blood flow to the skin, making eye bags appear more prominent. Another herb to steer clear of is Ginger (Zingiber officinale), which is known to increase circulation and cause inflammation.

This can make eye bags look more swollen and red. Also, avoid the herb Red Clover (Trifolium pratense) as it can cause water retention, which can make eye bags appear bigger. The herb St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) can also cause sensitivity in the skin, leading to increased puffiness around the eyes. Finally, avoid the herb Angelica (Angelica sinensis), which can cause water retention and make eye bags look more swollen.

These herbs can make eye bags look worse, so it's best to avoid them if you're looking to reduce their appearance.

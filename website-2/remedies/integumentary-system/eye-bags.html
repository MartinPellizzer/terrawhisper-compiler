Eye Bags: Causes, Treatment Options, and Medicinal Herbal Remedies

What are the causes of eye bags?

Eye bags can appear due to several reasons.

One common cause is dehydration. When we don't drink enough water, our body's fluids become low, and the skin around our eyes can look puffy and swollen. This is because the skin is trying to hold onto the little water it has, making it appear more prominent. Lack of sleep is another major cause of eye bags. When we don't get enough rest, our body's fluid balance changes, and the skin around our eyes can become puffy. This is because the brain signals the body to release hormones that make us feel tired, which also cause the skin to retain water. Genetics can also play a role in the appearance of eye bags.

Some people may naturally have a more prominent under-eye area due to their genetic makeup. This doesn't mean they're not taking care of themselves, but rather that their skin is just more prone to puffiness. Allergies can cause eye bags by triggering an allergic reaction that leads to increased fluid production in the body. When we're allergic to something, our body releases histamine, a chemical that causes blood vessels to swell. This can lead to puffy eyes and eye bags. Sinusitis, an infection or inflammation of the sinuses, can also cause eye bags. When the sinuses become inflamed, the fluid that drains from them can accumulate under the eyes, causing puffiness and swelling.

This can be particularly painful and uncomfortable.

8 teas every hairdresser should have for boils

Hairdressers often have to deal with a tough and demanding job, which can take a toll on their skin and overall health.

The constant exposure to chemicals, heat, and humidity in the salon can lead to skin irritations, and one of the most common issues is boils. Boils can appear on the skin, including the scalp, face, and neck, and can be painful, red, and swollen. This can be not only uncomfortable but also embarrassing for hairdressers, especially when they have to deal with clients while dealing with a boil. It can also affect their self-confidence and make them anxious about going to work. Furthermore, boils can also be a sign of underlying health issues, such as poor immune function or skin conditions, which can make it even more challenging for hairdressers to manage their skin.

Boils can make it difficult for hairdressers to perform their job effectively, as they may have to take time off to treat the boil or deal with the discomfort and pain it causes. This can lead to missed appointments, lost income, and a strained work-life balance. In addition, the constant stress and pressure of dealing with a boil can also take a toll on their mental health, making it essential for hairdressers to find effective ways to manage and treat boils. Herbal teas can be a useful addition to a hairdresser's skincare routine to help soothe and calm the skin, reducing inflammation and promoting healing. By drinking these teas, hairdressers can help to boost their immune function, improve their skin health, and reduce the risk of boils and other skin irritations.

The soothing properties of these teas can also help to reduce stress and anxiety, making it easier for hairdressers to manage their work and personal life.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas have been traditionally used to help with boils due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The tea contains triterpenoids, flavonoids, and saponins, which work together to reduce swelling, fight off infection, and promote healing. The antimicrobial properties help to combat bacteria and other microorganisms that can cause boils. When using Calendula officinalis teas for boils, it's essential to steep a strong tea and apply it directly to the affected area using a clean cloth.

You can also drink the tea to help boost your immune system and promote overall healing. To use the tea effectively, apply it to the boil 3-4 times a day, and keep the area clean with mild soap and water. If the boil is severe or doesn't improve within a few days, it's best to consult a healthcare professional for further treatment. In most cases, the boil should start to heal and reduce in size within a few days of using the Calendula officinalis tea.

The tea may also help to reduce the pain and discomfort associated with boils.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas are often used to help with boils due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The teas contain compounds like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which work together to reduce swelling and fight off infections that cause boils. These compounds can also help to boost the body's immune system, making it easier to fight off the underlying infection. If you have a boil, you can try making a tea by steeping dried Echinacea purpurea flowers in hot water. To do this, bring a cup of water to a boil, then add 1-2 teaspoons of dried flowers and let it steep for 5-10 minutes. Strain the liquid and drink it 2-3 times a day.

You can also add honey or lemon to make it more palatable. When taking Echinacea purpurea tea for boils, you may start to notice a reduction in swelling and pain within a few days. The infection may also start to clear up as the tea works to boost your immune system. However, it's essential to note that boils can be a sign of a deeper underlying issue, such as a bacterial or fungal infection, and may require medical attention. If your symptoms persist or worsen, you should consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

It's also worth noting that Echinacea purpurea tea may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, so it's crucial to talk to your healthcare provider before using it, especially if you have any underlying health conditions.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas have been used for centuries to help with boils.

The gel of the aloe plant contains compounds like aloe-emodin, lupeol, and cinnamonic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. These properties help to reduce swelling, fight off bacterial infections, and promote healing of the affected area. When you drink Aloe barbadensis teas, the aloe-emodin and other compounds are absorbed into your bloodstream, which can help to reduce the size and severity of boils. To use Aloe barbadensis teas for boils, you can try the following: Soak a teaspoon of dried Aloe barbadensis leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the liquid and drink it 2-3 times a day.

You can also add honey or lemon juice to make it more palatable. Additionally, you can apply aloe vera gel topically to the affected area to help speed up the healing process. When you take action with Aloe barbadensis teas, you may start to see improvements in your boils within a few days. The swelling may reduce, the pain may subside, and the boil may start to heal faster. You may also notice that the boil is producing less pus and discharge. However, it's essential to note that Aloe barbadensis teas may not cure boils instantly, and it's crucial to combine it with proper wound care and hygiene practices.

If your boils are severe or persistent, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

4. Aloe vera

Aloe vera teas have been used for centuries to help soothe and heal boils.

The reason why Aloe vera is effective is because of its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, which help to reduce redness, swelling, and pain. Aloe vera contains compounds like aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A, C, and E, which work together to fight off infection and promote healing. When you drink Aloe vera tea, these compounds are absorbed into your body, helping to calm the boil and prevent it from getting worse. To use Aloe vera tea for boils, you can make a tea by steeping dried Aloe vera leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

You can also buy Aloe vera tea bags at most health food stores. To get the most out of this remedy, drink the tea 2-3 times a day and apply a cold compress to the boil for 10-15 minutes, 2-3 times a day. You can also add some Aloe vera gel to the boil, but make sure to wash your hands thoroughly before and after handling the gel. When you start drinking Aloe vera tea and using a cold compress, you may notice the boil starting to shrink and the pain subsiding within a few days. The boil may eventually dry out and scab over, and with proper care, it should heal quickly.

However, if the boil is large or severe, or if you have a weakened immune system, it's best to consult a doctor for further treatment.

5. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum teas, also known as St. John's Wort, contain properties and constituents that can help with boils.

The teas are rich in flavonoids, tannins, and alkaloids, which have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects. These compounds can reduce swelling, prevent infection, and promote healing of the affected area. The flavonoids, particularly hyperforin and hypericin, have been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi, which can contribute to the formation of boils. To use Hypericum perforatum teas for boils, make a tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried flowers in a cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes. Strain the tea and apply it directly to the boil using a clean cloth or cotton ball.

You can also drink the tea to help reduce inflammation and promote healing from the inside out. It's essential to note that Hypericum perforatum teas can interact with certain medications, such as antidepressants and blood thinners, so consult with a healthcare professional before using it. When applying the tea to the boil, you may notice a reduction in swelling and pain within a few hours. The tea can help to bring the boil to a head, allowing it to drain and heal more quickly. However, if you experience increased pain, redness, or swelling, seek medical attention to rule out any underlying infections or complications.

It's also crucial to maintain good hygiene and keep the affected area clean to prevent the spread of infection.

Melaleuca alternifolia teas, also known as tea tree tea, have been used for centuries to help treat boils and other skin infections.

The tea is made from the leaves of the Melaleuca alternifolia plant, which contains compounds like eucalyptol and cineole that have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. These properties help to reduce the size and severity of boils, prevent the spread of infection, and promote healing. You can also drink the tea to help boost your immune system and fight off the infection from the inside out. As the tea works to reduce the size and severity of the boil, you may start to see improvements within a few days, such as a decrease in redness and swelling, and a reduction in pain.

The boil may eventually rupture and drain, and with continued use of the tea, the area should start to heal and return to normal.

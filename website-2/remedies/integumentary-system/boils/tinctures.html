Echinacea purpurea is also a popular herb for its ability to boost the immune system and fight off infections. 1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains triterpene saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are active constituents that help to reduce inflammation and fight infection.

These compounds have antiseptic and antimicrobial properties that work together to clean and soothe the affected area. The flavonoids, specifically, have been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi, helping to prevent the boil from becoming infected. The triterpene saponins also have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to reduce the swelling and pain associated with boils.

By applying a calendula tincture to the affected area, you can promote healing, reduce the risk of infection, and alleviate discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula officinalis flowers, 2 cups of vodka (at least 35% ABV), and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine the dried flowers and vodka in the glass jar. Stir well to make sure the flowers are covered.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day to help the mixture.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Label the jar with the date and the contents. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 10-20 drops, 3-4 times a day, to help treat boils.

2. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum tinctures contains hyperforin and hypericin, two active constituents that help reduce inflammation and fight off infections.

These compounds have antimicrobial and antiseptic properties, which make them effective against bacteria that cause boils. Hypericum perforatum tinctures also have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and pain associated with boils. The tannins in Hypericum perforatum tinctures have astringent properties, which help dry out the boil and promote healing.

By using Hypericum perforatum tinctures, you can help your body fight off the infection and reduce the symptoms of boils.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum flowers and leaves. Dry them in a single layer or use a dehydrator on low heat for 1-2 hours.
  2. Place the dried flowers and leaves in a clean glass jar with a lid. Add 2 cups of 80-100 proof vodka or glycerin.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the medicine to infuse into the solvent.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Use 10-20 drops, 3-4 times a day, diluted in water, to treat boils.

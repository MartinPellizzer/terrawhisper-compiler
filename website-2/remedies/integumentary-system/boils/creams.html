1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are active constituents that provide its therapeutic properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which help to reduce redness and swelling associated with boils. The saponins in Calendula officinalis creams also have a cleansing effect, allowing them to penetrate deep into the skin and help to clear the infection. Additionally, the flavonoids present in the cream have antioxidant properties, which help to soothe and protect the skin from further irritation.

By using Calendula officinalis creams, the triterpenoid saponins and flavonoids work together to help to reduce the size and severity of boils, promoting healing and recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, and a small saucepan.
  2. Steep the Calendula flowers in the coconut oil over low heat for 2-3 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter to the bowl and melt in a double boiler.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool. Use as needed to soothe boils.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains compounds like cineole and terpinen-4-ol, which have antibacterial properties.

These compounds help reduce the bacteria that cause boils, a common skin infection. The antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds also help soothe and calm the affected area. Additionally, terpinen-4-ol helps reduce redness and swelling associated with boils.

This combination of properties makes Melaleuca alternifolia creams a good option for treating boils.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of essential oil (tea tree oil), and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and add essential oil and vitamin E oil. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into small jars or containers and let it cool and solidify before use.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains active constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

Aloin, in particular, has been shown to have antimicrobial activity, inhibiting the growth of bacteria that can cause infection. Acemannan, a polysaccharide, has been found to stimulate the immune system, promoting healing and reducing the severity of boils.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 10 drops of tea tree oil.
  2. Mix the Aloe barbadensis gel and coconut oil in a bowl until well combined.
  3. Add the shea butter to the bowl and mix until smooth and creamy.
  4. Add the tea tree oil to the bowl and mix well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container and store in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks. Apply to boils as needed.

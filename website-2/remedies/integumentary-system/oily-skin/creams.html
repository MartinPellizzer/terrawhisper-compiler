1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their astringent and antiseptic properties.

These properties help to regulate the production of sebum, the oily substance produced by the skin. The antiseptic properties also help to prevent the growth of bacteria, which can clog pores and make oily skin worse. The astringent properties help to tighten the pores, reducing the appearance of oiliness and giving the skin a smoother look.

By regulating sebum production and preventing bacterial growth, Lavandula angustifolia creams can help to reduce the appearance of oily skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 10 drops of Lavandula angustifolia essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool until it reaches 120°F to 130°F. Then, slowly add the distilled water while whisking continuously.
  5. Add the Lavandula angustifolia essential oil and whip the mixture until it's smooth and creamy. Pour the cream into a clean container and let it cool before use.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contain several biologically active constituents that help with oily skin.

These include vitamins A, C, and E, as well as beta-carotene, which have antioxidant properties that help to neutralize free radicals and reduce inflammation. Aloe vera also contains salicylic acid, a beta-hydroxy acid that helps to exfoliate the skin and unclog pores, reducing oil production and preventing breakouts. Additionally, aloe vera contains aloin and aloe-emodin, which have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that help to soothe and calm the skin, reducing redness and irritation.

By reducing inflammation and preventing clogged pores, aloe barbadensis creams can help to regulate oil production and achieve a healthier, more balanced complexion.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1/2 cup of aloe vera gel, 2 tablespoons of glycerin, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, 2 tablespoons of jojoba oil, and 1 teaspoon of tea tree oil.
  2. Combine distilled water and aloe vera gel in a bowl. Stir until the gel dissolves.
  3. Add glycerin, vitamin E oil, jojoba oil, and tea tree oil to the bowl. Stir well to mix.
  4. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container. Let it cool to room temperature.
  5. Once cooled, your aloe barbadensis cream is ready to use. Apply a small amount to your skin, avoiding the eye area.

3. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains the active constituents cineole, limonene, and beta-caryophyllene, which help to control oil production and reduce acne on oily skin.

Cineole has a drying effect that reduces sebum production, while limonene has antimicrobial properties that prevent bacteria from clogging pores. Beta-caryophyllene has anti-inflammatory properties that soothe and calm the skin, reducing redness and irritation. These properties work together to balance the skin's natural oil production and prevent clogged pores, making Melaleuca alternifolia creams a good option for oily skin.

The tea tree oil in these creams also helps to reduce inflammation and prevent infections, further contributing to its effectiveness in managing oily skin.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of distilled water, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Heat the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler until melted.
  3. Remove from heat and slowly add the distilled water while stirring with a spatula.
  4. Add the Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil and vitamin E oil. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let cool until solid. Use immediately or store in a cool place.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains essential oils with active constituents like camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate.

These compounds help to reduce the production of sebum, the oily substance produced by the skin, by inhibiting the sebaceous glands. The antimicrobial properties of rosmarinic acid, a key constituent of the essential oil, also help to prevent bacterial growth that can contribute to oily skin. Additionally, the astringent properties of camphor help to tighten pores and reduce shine, giving the skin a more balanced appearance.

By regulating sebum production and preventing bacterial growth, Rosmarinus officinalis creams can help to control oily skin and promote a healthier complexion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of aloe vera gel, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 1 tablespoon of beeswax, and 5 drops of rosemary essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the aloe vera gel and shea butter until well combined.
  4. Add the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture to the aloe vera gel mixture and stir until smooth.
  5. Add the rosemary essential oil and mix well. Pour the mixture into a jar and let it cool before use on oily skin.

5. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoids, saponins, and carotenoids, which are active constituents that help to control oil production.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce redness and irritation in oily skin, creating an environment that's less prone to clogged pores. Calendula's saponins also have a cleansing effect, gently removing dirt and excess oil without stripping the skin of its natural moisture. The carotenoids in Calendula officinalis creams help to reduce sebum production, which is the primary cause of oily skin.

Regular use of these creams can help to balance the skin's oil production, leading to a more even and healthy complexion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of shea butter.
  2. Dry the Calendula flowers in a low oven (150°F) for 2 hours to remove moisture.
  3. Mix the dried Calendula flowers with 1/4 cup of beeswax in a double boiler until melted.
  4. Combine the melted Calendula-beeswax mixture with the coconut oil and shea butter in a blender.
  5. Pour the mixture into a jar, let it cool, and use on oily skin as a moisturizing cream.

6. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains bioactive constituents like cineole and alpha-pinene.

These compounds have antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce oil production and control acne. Cineole also has a cooling effect that can help to reduce redness and soothe the skin. The astringent properties of alpha-pinene help to tighten pores and prevent excess oil from escaping.

By reducing oil production and controlling pores, Eucalyptus globulus creams can help to balance oily skin and prevent clogged pores.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 10 drops of eucalyptus oil in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in the double boiler over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Remove the double boiler from the heat and let it cool slightly, until the mixture reaches 120°F to 130°F.
  4. Add 10 drops of eucalyptus oil to the mixture and stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a tin or jar and let it cool and solidify before use.

7. Saponaria officinalis

Saponaria officinalis creams contains saponins, triterpenes, and flavonoids, which are active constituents that help to control oily skin.

Saponins have astringent and anti-inflammatory properties that reduce sebum production, while triterpenes help to regulate the skin's natural oil balance. Flavonoids, specifically quercetin, have antioxidant properties that protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to oily skin. The combination of these properties in Saponaria officinalis creams helps to regulate the skin's natural oil production, reducing the appearance of oily skin.

By controlling sebum production and protecting the skin from damage, Saponaria officinalis creams can help to keep oily skin under control.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Saponaria officinalis flowers, 2 cups of distilled water, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Heat the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler until the beeswax melts.
  3. Add the Saponaria officinalis flowers to the distilled water and let it steep for 10-15 minutes. Strain the mixture and discard the flowers.
  4. Combine the infused water with the coconut oil and beeswax mixture. Stir until well combined.
  5. Add the vitamin E oil and stir well. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool before using on oily skin.

8. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum creams contains active constituents like silymarin, flavonoids, and alkaloids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help to reduce oil production in the skin. Silymarin, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the activity of 5-alpha-reductase, an enzyme that converts testosterone to dihydrotestosterone, a hormone that contributes to oiliness. The flavonoids present in Silybum marianum creams have a soothing effect on the skin, reducing redness and inflammation associated with oily skin.

By regulating oil production and reducing inflammation, Silybum marianum creams can help to balance the skin's natural moisture levels and improve its appearance.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Silybum marianum flowers and 1 cup of coconut oil.
  2. Combine the flowers and coconut oil in a saucepan, and heat on low for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl.
  4. Mix 1 tablespoon of beeswax with 1 tablespoon of coconut oil in a small saucepan, and heat until melted.
  5. Combine the infused oil and melted beeswax mixture, and pour into small containers to cool and set.

Herbal teas can help with oily skin, but drinking them daily might not be the best approach.

It really depends on individual skin types and tea blends.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for oily skin?

This helps to balance your skin's natural oils throughout the day. Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, have a soothing effect on the skin and can help to reduce oil production.

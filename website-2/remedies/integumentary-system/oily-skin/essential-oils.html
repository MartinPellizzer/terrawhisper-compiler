10 Herbal Essential Oils For Oily Skin

Herbal essential oils can be a great help for oily skin.

You see, certain herbs have natural properties that can help balance your skin's oil production. For example, Lavandula angustifolia, also known as lavender oil, has antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties that can help calm and soothe your skin. This can lead to fewer breakouts and a more balanced skin tone. Another herb that can be beneficial is Melaleuca alternifolia, or tea tree oil. It's known for its antibacterial properties, which can help control acne and reduce inflammation. This can leave your skin looking clearer and feeling smoother.

This can leave your skin looking more even and balanced. Using herbal essential oils can also have benefits beyond just your skin. For example, certain herbs like lavender can promote relaxation and help with stress relief. This can be especially helpful if you find that stress is triggering your oily skin. When using herbal essential oils, it's generally recommended to dilute them with a carrier oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil, before applying them to your skin.

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and geraniol as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce redness and irritation often associated with oily skin. Linalool, in particular, has been shown to have a balancing effect on the skin's natural pH, which can become imbalanced in oily skin conditions. Additionally, the astringent properties of linalyl acetate help to constrict pores and reduce oil production.

By regulating the skin's natural oil production and reducing inflammation, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to improve the appearance of oily skin.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers, a clean glass jar, a cheesecloth or a coffee filter, and a carrier oil like jojoba oil.
  2. Place the flowers in the glass jar, leaving 1 inch of space at the top.
  3. Cover the jar with the cheesecloth or coffee filter and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After the infusion period, strain the liquid through the cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Add 1 cup of jojoba oil to the liquid and mix well. Store the oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops per application for oily skin.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains a compound called cineole, which has natural antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Cineole helps to reduce the production of sebum, a natural oil that can clog pores and cause oily skin. The essential oil also contains linalool and terpineol, which are known for their antifungal and antibacterial properties that help to control the growth of bacteria that can contribute to acne. The astringent properties of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils help to tighten pores, reducing the appearance of oily skin.

By reducing sebum production and controlling bacteria growth, Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils can help to balance the skin's natural oil production.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves. Make sure they are fresh and free of contaminants.
  2. Combine the leaves with 2 cups of water in a saucepan. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat.
  3. Reduce heat to low and let it simmer for 20-30 minutes. This will release the oil from the leaves.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 2-3 hours to allow the oil to separate from the water. Skim off the oil and store it in a dark glass bottle.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like cineole, globulol, and alpha-pinene.

These constituents have decongestant and astringent properties, which help to reduce oil production in the skin. The astringent properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils help to tighten pores, making them less visible and reducing the appearance of oily skin. The decongestant properties of the essential oil help to clear out excess oil and impurities from the skin, leaving it feeling clean and balanced.

The astringent and decongestant properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils make it a beneficial choice for managing oily skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and a large pot.
  2. Add 2 cups of water to the pot and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat, add 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba) and 1 tablespoon of witch hazel.
  4. Steep Eucalyptus leaves for 2-3 hours, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and dilute it with 2% of the carrier oil for use on oily skin.

4. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, a known antioxidant and astringent that helps reduce the production of sebum in the skin.

The oil also contains geraniol, a compound that has antiseptic and antifungal properties, which helps to prevent breakouts and reduce inflammation. The astringent properties of limonene and geraniol help to tighten pores and reduce oiliness, making it an effective treatment for oily skin. Citral, another active constituent in Cymbopogon citratus essential oils, has antiseptic and antibacterial properties that help to control the growth of bacteria that can contribute to oily skin.

Regular use of Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can help to regulate sebum production, reduce oiliness, and improve the overall appearance of oily skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Cymbopogon citratus leaves. Let it simmer for 20-30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean container. Discard the leaves.
  4. Let the mixture cool and then refrigerate it for 2-3 hours or overnight. This will help the oil separate from the water.
  5. Skim the oil from the top of the mixture and transfer it to a dark glass bottle. Store it in a cool, dry place.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains borneol, camphor, bornyl acetate, and camphene as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and astringent properties, which help to balance the skin's natural pH and reduce oil production. The astringent properties of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils help to tighten pores, making it an effective treatment for oily skin. Additionally, the antiseptic properties of the oil help to prevent bacterial growth, which can contribute to oily skin and acne.

By reducing oil production and preventing bacterial growth, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help to control oily skin and promote a healthy, balanced complexion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a pot.
  2. Boil the water and add the leaves. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into a bowl. Discard the leaves.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil) to 2 cups of the strained liquid.
  5. Steep the mixture for 2-3 days in a cool, dark place. Strain and use the oil for oily skin.

6. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol and carvacrol, two potent bioactive constituents that help to balance oily skin.

Thymol has antiseptic and astringent properties, which help to reduce the production of sebum, the oil produced by the skin. Carvacrol has anti-inflammatory properties, which help to soothe and calm the skin, reducing redness and irritation caused by excess oil. When applied to the skin, the antiseptic properties of thymol help to kill bacteria that can clog pores and worsen oily skin, while the astringent properties help to tighten pores and reduce oil production.

By controlling sebum production and reducing inflammation, Thymus vulgaris essential oils can help to regulate oily skin and promote a healthier balance.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Thymus vulgaris leaves, 1 cup of water, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the leaves and water in a pot, bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into the glass jar, discarding the solids.
  4. Let the mixture sit at room temperature for 2-3 days, allowing the mixture to infuse and separate.
  5. Use a dropper to extract the essential oil from the top layer and store it in a dark glass bottle.

7. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains rosmarinic acid, borneol, and camphor as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help to reduce redness and prevent breakouts on oily skin. Rosmarinic acid, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of sebum, the oily substance that can clog pores and exacerbate acne. The antimicrobial properties of camphor and borneol also help to control the growth of bacteria that can contribute to oily skin conditions.

By reducing inflammation and preventing bacterial overgrowth, Salvia officinalis essential oils can help to balance the skin's natural moisture levels and promote a healthier complexion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves, 1 cup of water, and a glass jar.
  2. Combine the leaves and water in the glass jar, cover it, and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  3. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container, discarding the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a still or a distillation apparatus to separate the essential oil from the water. Collect the essential oil in a separate container.
  5. Dilute the essential oil with a carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) in a 1:10 ratio to use it on oily skin. Store the oil in a dark glass bottle.

8. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains a high concentration of triterpenoids, which are a group of bioactive constituents that help regulate the skin's oil production.

One of the main triterpenoids, aloin, has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce redness and irritation, while another, aloe-emodin, acts as an astringent, helping to control sebum secretion. The essential oils also contain vitamins A, C, and E, which are antioxidants that protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals and promote healthy cell growth. The combination of these properties makes Aloe barbadensis essential oils effective in balancing the skin's natural oil production and reducing the appearance of oily skin.

It helps to purify pores and prevent clogged skin, resulting in a more balanced and healthy complexion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat for 30 minutes to extract the aloe gel.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl.
  4. Combine the extracted aloe gel with 2 tablespoons of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  5. Store the aloe essential oil in a dark glass bottle in the fridge for up to 1 week.

9. Pelargonium graveolens

Pelargonium graveolens essential oils contains rosmarinic acid and geraniol, which have antiseptic and astringent properties.

These properties help to reduce the production of sebum, the oily substance in skin. The astringent action of geraniol also helps to tighten pores, making them appear smaller and less oily. By reducing sebum production and tightening pores, Pelargonium graveolens essential oils can help to balance the skin's natural oil levels, making it a good choice for people with oily skin.

The astringent and antiseptic properties of these essential oils also help to prevent bacterial growth, which can contribute to oily skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Pelargonium graveolens leaves and flowers. Clean them with water.
  2. Add 2 cups of water to a saucepan. Bring the water to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and add the cleaned Pelargonium graveolens leaves and flowers to the saucepan. Steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba oil) to the strained mixture. Stir well and let it sit for 2 hours to allow the essential oil to separate.

10. Cinnamomum zeylanicum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum essential oils contains linalool, a compound known to have antiseptic and antifungal properties, which help to control the growth of bacteria and fungi that can contribute to oily skin.

It also contains cinnamaldehyde, a natural astringent that helps to tighten pores and reduce oil production. The essential oils' antiseptic properties also help to reduce inflammation and prevent infections that can make oily skin worse. Additionally, cinnamaldehyde has a calming effect on the skin, which can help to reduce stress-related oil production.

By balancing the skin's natural oil production and reducing inflammation, Cinnamomum zeylanicum essential oils can be beneficial for people with oily skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cinnamomum zeylanicum bark, 2 cups of water, and a saucepan.
  2. Combine the Cinnamomum zeylanicum bark and water in the saucepan and heat it over medium heat.
  3. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 2 hours, then let it steep for 2 more hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops of the oil with a carrier oil for oily skin.

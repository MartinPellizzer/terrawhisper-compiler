Lice Be Gone: 9 Herbal Teas for Kids in School

School children can get lice, also known as head lice, which can be a real problem.

Lice are tiny insects that live in people's hair and feed on their blood. They can spread quickly from one child to another, often through close contact like playing or sharing hair accessories. When a child gets lice, it can be very frustrating and embarrassing for them. They might feel self-conscious about their hair, and it can affect their confidence and self-esteem. They might even get teased or bullied by their peers, which can make them feel sad and isolated. Moreover, treating lice can be time-consuming and costly, requiring multiple washes and treatments.

It can also disrupt a child's school life, causing them to miss classes and fall behind in their studies. But what can be done to help children get rid of lice?. Some parents are turning to herbal essential oils as a natural and chemical-free solution. These oils are derived from plants and have been used for centuries to treat various health problems. They work by repelling lice or killing them on contact, making it easier to get rid of them. These oils are often applied to the hair and scalp, where they can penetrate deep into the hair follicles to reach the lice.

They are also gentle on the skin and hair, making them a popular choice for parents who want to avoid harsh chemicals.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils, also known as tea tree oil, have been shown to be effective in treating lice infestations.

The reason for this is due to the oil's antimicrobial and insecticidal properties, which are attributed to its main constituents, such as cineole and terpinen-4-ol. These compounds work by disrupting the lice's nervous system and ultimately leading to their death. To use Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil for lice, mix a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil, and apply it directly to the hair and scalp. Leave the mixture on for about an hour before shampooing as usual.

Repeat this process several times a week for a few weeks to ensure all lice and their eggs are removed. It's also essential to wash and dry clothing, bedding, and towels in hot water and dry them on high heat to prevent re-infestation. Using Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil for lice can lead to several outcomes. The oil's insecticidal properties can effectively kill the lice and their eggs, resulting in a significant reduction in the lice population. However, it may take several treatments to completely eliminate the infestation.

Additionally, some people may experience skin irritation or allergic reactions to the oil, so it's crucial to perform a patch test before using it extensively.

Mix a few drops of the essential oil with a tablespoon of the carrier oil and apply it to the scalp. Leave it on for a few hours or overnight before washing it out with shampoo.

You can also add a few drops to your regular shampoo for an ongoing treatment. When using Cymbopogon citratus essential oils for lice, you can expect to see some results within a few treatments. The lice may become less active, and their numbers may decrease. However, it's essential to note that this oil may not be effective against all stages of lice, particularly the eggs. You may need to repeat the treatment several times to get rid of all the lice.

Additionally, always do a patch test before using any essential oil to ensure you don't have any sensitivity.

They can also be gentler on the hair and skin, which can reduce the risk of side effects.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia teas contains compounds like eucalyptol, cineole, and terpinen-4-ol, which have antimicrobial and insecticidal properties that can help kill lice.

These compounds work by breaking down the lice's exoskeletons, ultimately leading to their demise. To use Melaleuca alternifolia teas for lice, start by steeping a teaspoon of dried Melaleuca alternifolia leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Let the tea cool, then use it as a final rinse after shampooing your hair.

You can also add a few drops of the tea to your regular shampoo for added lice-killing power. When you use Melaleuca alternifolia teas, you may start to notice a reduction in lice and nits within a few days. However, it's essential to be patient, as it can take up to two weeks to completely eliminate the infestation. You'll also need to wash and dry your bedding, towels, and clothing in hot water to prevent re-infestation.

Regularly treating your hair with the tea and using a fine-tooth comb to remove any remaining nits will also help keep lice at bay.

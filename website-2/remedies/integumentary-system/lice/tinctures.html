11 Herbal Tinctures For Lice

Herbal tinctures are a natural way to get rid of lice without using harsh chemicals.

These tinctures are made from herbs that are rich in oils that kill lice and their eggs. The process works by dissolving the oils in a liquid solvent, which makes it easier to apply to your hair. For example, Artemisia absinthium, also known as wormwood, has been used for centuries to treat lice and other parasites. Its active compounds, including thujone, are toxic to lice, killing them and their eggs. Melaleuca alternifolia, or tea tree oil, is another popular herb used to make lice-repelling tinctures. It has antibacterial and antifungal properties, which help to prevent infections from lice bites. The oil also has a strong scent that repels lice, keeping them away from your hair.

Lavandula angustifolia, or lavender, is another herb that's often used in lice tinctures. Its calming effects can help to soothe the discomfort and itchiness caused by lice bites. Applying herbal tinctures to your hair can bring several benefits to your life. Firstly, it's a natural and chemical-free way to get rid of lice, which is gentler on your scalp and hair. This is especially important for people with sensitive scalps or allergies. Secondly, herbal tinctures can be used in combination with other natural treatments, such as combing out lice and their eggs with a fine-toothed comb. This multi-step approach can be more effective than using chemical treatments alone.

Lastly, using herbal tinctures can help to prevent lice infestations from happening in the first place, making it a proactive and preventative measure for lice control.

1. Artemisia absinthium

Artemisia absinthium tinctures contains thujone, a bioactive compound with potent insecticidal properties.

Thujone disrupts the nervous system of lice, ultimately causing their death. The tannins present in the tincture help to dry out the lice's exoskeleton, making it difficult for them to survive. Artemisinin, another bioactive compound in the tincture, has been shown to have a strong effect on lice, killing them quickly.

The combination of these compounds in Artemisia absinthium tinctures makes it a viable option for treating lice infestations.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Artemisia absinthium leaves and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Fill the jar to the top with vodka, making sure the leaves are fully covered.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the leaves.
  5. Label the jar with the date and contents, and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 10-20 drops of the tincture in shampoo to help treat lice.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures contains the active constituents cineole, limonene, and terpinen-4-ol, which are known for their antiparasitic and antiseptic properties.

Cineole, in particular, has been shown to have a high level of efficacy against head lice and their eggs, helping to kill and remove them from the scalp. Terpinen-4-ol has been found to have a synergistic effect when combined with other compounds, increasing the tincture's effectiveness against lice. The antiseptic properties of Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and inflammation caused by the lice.

Regular use of Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures can help to prevent re-infestation by killing lice and their eggs, making it a useful tool in the treatment and prevention of lice infestations.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass container.
  2. Combine the leaves and vodka in the container. Make sure the leaves are completely covered by the vodka.
  3. Store the container in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day to help the ingredients mix.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container.
  5. Discard the solids and store the tincture in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 10-20 drops of the tincture in shampoo or apply it directly to the hair and scalp to help repel lice.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent lice from forming?

Some herbal teas are believed to repel lice, but there's no scientific proof they can prevent them from forming.

Certain herbs like tea tree oil and lavender may help repel lice, but more research is needed to confirm their effectiveness.

Drinking herbal tea might not be a reliable way to prevent lice.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for lice every day?

Consuming herbal teas for lice every day is not recommended.

While some teas may help control lice, they may not kill the eggs, which can lead to a new infestation.

Repeatedly using tea might not provide the desired results, and its long-term effects on your body are unknown.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in lice?

Herbal teas can take several weeks to show results for lice treatment.

The length of time depends on the type of tea and how often you use it.

Some teas may start working within 2-3 weeks, but it's not uncommon for it to take 4-6 weeks to see significant results.

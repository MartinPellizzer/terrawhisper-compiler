What Are The Main Causes Of Baldness?

Baldness, also known as hair loss, can be caused by several factors.

One main cause is genetics, which plays a significant role in hair loss. If your parents or grandparents have experienced hair loss, you are more likely to experience it too. Genetics can influence the rate at which hair grows and the thickness of the hair follicles. Another main cause of baldness is DHT, or dihydrotestosterone. DHT is a hormone that is produced from testosterone in the body.

High levels of DHT can damage the hair follicles, leading to hair loss. DHT can shrink the hair follicles, making it difficult for hair to grow. This can result in thinner, shorter hair or complete hair loss. Alopecia is also a common cause of baldness. Alopecia is a medical condition that causes hair loss due to an autoimmune response. In this condition, the body's immune system attacks the hair follicles, leading to hair loss.

There are several types of alopecia, including alopecia areata, which causes patchy hair loss, and alopecia totalis, which causes complete hair loss.

Below are the main causes of baldness.

  • Genetics: Genetics causes baldness because it determines how sensitive a person's hair follicles are to the hormones that control hair growth, and if a person inherits a tendency to be sensitive, it can lead to hair thinning and eventually baldness.
  • DHT: DHT (dihydrotestosterone) causes baldness because it shrinks hair follicles on the scalp, leading to thinner and shorter hair over time, eventually causing hair to fall out completely.
  • Alopecia: Alopecia is a cause of baldness because it is an autoimmune disease that attacks the hair follicles, causing them to shrink and eventually stop growing hair.
  • Hypothyroidism: Hypothyroidism causes baldness because the slowed down metabolism of the body, caused by a lack of thyroid hormones, leads to a reduction in the production of hair growth, resulting in thinning or complete loss of hair on the scalp.
  • Traction: Traction causes baldness because tight hairstyles like braids and ponytails repeatedly pull on hair follicles, damaging them and eventually leading to hair loss.
  • Hormonal Imbalance: Hormonal imbalance can cause baldness because an excess of dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a hormone produced from testosterone, damages hair follicles, leading to hair loss and eventual baldness.

Another herb to be cautious with is 'Urtica dioica' (Stinging Nettle). It can cause skin irritation, especially on bald areas. 'Avena sativa' (Oat straw) may cause hormone imbalance if consumed in large amounts. This can worsen baldness.

'Marshmallow root (Althaea officinalis)' is another herb to be careful with. It can cause stomach upset, and in some cases, it may worsen baldness due to its hormonal effects. 'Glycyrrhiza glabra' (Licorice root) can cause hormonal imbalances and worsen baldness. It can also cause high blood pressure and stomach problems. Lastly, 'Ammi visnaga' (Khella) can cause liver problems if taken in high doses. It is not recommended for people with baldness, especially if you have liver issues.

It's best to consult a doctor or a healthcare professional before consuming any herbs, especially if you have a medical condition like baldness.

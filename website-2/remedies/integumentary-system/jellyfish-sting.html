What Are The Main Causes Of Jellyfish Sting?

Jellyfish stings are caused by the venom they release from their tentacles.

There are three main types of jellyfish stings: Jellyfish sting, Sea nettle sting, and Portuguese Man o' War sting. Jellyfish sting: Jellyfish have long, thin tentacles that they use to catch prey. When they come into contact with a person, these tentacles release venom that causes pain and swelling. The venom is made up of tiny, needle-like structures called nematocysts that inject the venom into the skin.

Sea nettle sting: Sea nettles are a type of jellyfish that have shorter, thicker tentacles than other jellyfish. They also have a different type of venom that causes more intense pain and swelling. The venom from sea nettles is also made up of nematocysts, but it is more potent than the venom from other jellyfish. Portuguese Man o' War sting: The Portuguese Man o' War is not actually a jellyfish, but a type of siphonophore, a colonial organism made up of many individual animals working together. The venom from the Portuguese Man o' War is the most potent of all, and can cause severe pain, swelling, and even respiratory distress.

The venom is also made up of nematocysts, but it contains a number of other toxins that make it particularly deadly.

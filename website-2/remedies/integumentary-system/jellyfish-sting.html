The Jellyfish Sting: Understanding Its Causes and Natural Remedies

What are the causes of jellyfish sting?

When a jellyfish stings, it's usually due to the discharge of tiny structures called nematocysts.

These nematocysts are like tiny harpoons that are embedded in the jellyfish's stinging cells. When an animal, like a human, touches the jellyfish, it triggers the nematocysts to fire, injecting venom into the skin.

This is known as coelenterate envenomation, where the jellyfish's venom is released into the body. The venom is made up of proteins and other molecules that can cause pain, swelling, and other symptoms. The stinging cells themselves are responsible for producing and storing the nematocysts, making them a key part of the jellyfish's defense mechanism.

The venom injection is what actually causes the pain and other symptoms associated with a jellyfish sting.

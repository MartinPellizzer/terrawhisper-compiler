Singers with Dry Lips: 10 Herbal Teas to Soothe

As a singer, you know how important it is to take care of your voice.

But did you know that dry, chapped lips can be just as big of a problem?. When you're on stage, belting out a tune, your mouth is wide open, and the air can be dry and harsh. This can cause your lips to dry out and crack, which can be painful and uncomfortable. And if you're not careful, dry lips can even affect your singing - a dry, chapped mouth can make it hard to form words and sounds, which can throw off your whole performance. And let's not forget about the embarrassment factor - who wants to be on stage with chapped, bleeding lips?. It's a nightmare for any singer.

But what can you do to prevent dry lips?. Well, one thing is to stay hydrated - drinking plenty of water can help keep your skin and lips moist and healthy. You can also use lip balm or lip salve to help lock in moisture. And for singers, there's an added benefit: drinking certain teas can help keep your lips and mouth healthy from the inside out. These teas are made from natural ingredients that are known to have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe and calm dry, chapped lips. They can also help to lock in moisture and keep your skin hydrated, which is especially important for singers who are constantly on the go and under stress.

Drinking these teas can be a simple and effective way to help keep your lips and mouth healthy, and to help you perform at your best.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas are a natural remedy for dry lips.

The gel from the Aloe barbadensis plant contains properties like vitamins A, C, and E, as well as minerals like calcium and potassium, which help to soothe and moisturize dry skin. The plant's gel also contains an anti-inflammatory compound called aloe-emodin, which reduces redness and swelling. When made into a tea, these properties can be absorbed through the skin to provide relief from dry, chapped lips. To use Aloe barbadensis teas for dry lips, you can steep the gel in hot water to release its beneficial properties. You can then cool the tea and apply it to your lips using a cotton swab or a clean cloth.

You can repeat this process several times a day, or as needed. You can also add a few drops of honey or coconut oil to the tea for extra moisturizing benefits. As you apply the Aloe barbadensis tea to your lips, you may start to feel a cooling sensation as the anti-inflammatory compound soothes the dry skin. The vitamins and minerals in the tea may also help to lock in moisture, leaving your lips feeling softer and more comfortable. Over time, you may notice a reduction in dryness and chapping, and your lips may become less prone to cracking and bleeding.

Regular use of Aloe barbadensis teas may also help to promote healthy skin cell growth, leaving your lips looking smoother and more radiant.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas, also known as rosemary tea, have been used for centuries to help soothe dry lips.

The tea contains properties like tannins and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds help reduce swelling and redness, promoting a healthy lip environment. Additionally, rosemary tea has antimicrobial properties that can help combat infections and promote healing. To use rosemary tea for dry lips, you can steep a teaspoon of dried rosemary leaves in a cup of hot water for 5-7 minutes.

Let it cool, then dip a cotton swab into the tea and gently apply it to your lips. You can do this 2-3 times a day, depending on the severity of your dry lips. When you apply rosemary tea to your lips, you can expect to see a reduction in dryness and cracking. The tea's anti-inflammatory properties will help calm your lips, and the antioxidants will work to repair and protect them. You may also notice a decrease in swelling and redness.

As your lips begin to heal, you can start to use a lip balm or moisturizer to lock in the moisture and keep your lips hydrated.

3. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas, commonly known as peppermint tea, can help with dry lips due to its cooling and moisturizing properties.

The tea contains menthol, a natural compound that helps to reduce inflammation and soothe dry skin. When you drink peppermint tea, the menthol is absorbed into your bloodstream and travels to your skin, where it helps to lock in moisture and reduce dryness. Peppermint tea also contains antioxidants, such as vitamin C and beta-carotene, which help to protect your skin from damage and promote healing.

To help with dry lips, try drinking a cup of peppermint tea after eating a meal or before bed, when your skin is most receptive to moisture. You can also try applying a thin layer of peppermint oil or balm to your lips, but be sure to dilute the oil with a carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil, to avoid irritating your skin. As you drink the tea and apply the oil, you may notice a reduction in dryness and inflammation within a few hours. Your lips may start to feel softer and more hydrated, and you may be able to apply less lip balm or moisturizer to keep them moisturized.

With regular use, you may be able to reduce the frequency and severity of dry lip episodes, and keep your lips healthy and moisturized.

4. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas have been traditionally used to help soothe dry and chapped lips.

This is because the plant contains compounds like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and glycosides, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These properties help to reduce swelling and protect the skin from damage caused by dry air and wind. When you drink Echinacea purpurea tea, the flavonoids and other compounds are absorbed into your bloodstream and then into your skin, where they can help to calm dry, irritated skin and reduce inflammation. If you're experiencing dry lips, try drinking 1-2 cups of Echinacea purpurea tea per day.

You can also apply a cooled tea bag to your lips for a few minutes to help soothe and moisturize them. To make the tea, steep 1 teaspoon of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool before drinking or applying it to your lips. When you drink the tea, you may start to notice improvements in the texture and appearance of your lips within a few days. Your lips may become less dry and chapped, and may even start to feel softer and more comfortable.

Hydrastis canadensis teas, also known as goldenseal, can help with dry lips due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

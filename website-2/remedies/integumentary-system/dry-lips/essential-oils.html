1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains a high concentration of glycoproteins, vitamins, and amino acids that help to soothe and moisturize dry lips.

The key active constituent, aloe-emodin, has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce redness and swelling, making it easier to manage dry and chapped lips. The oil's high water content and emollient properties help to lock in moisture, providing long-lasting hydration to dry, cracked lips. Additionally, aloe barbadensis essential oils contains salicylic acid, which gently exfoliates the skin, removing dead skin cells and promoting healthy lip cell turnover.

This combination of nutrients and active compounds makes aloe barbadensis essential oils an effective solution for dry, irritated lips.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 1 tablespoon of coconut oil, and 10 drops of vitamin E oil.
  2. Mix the Aloe gel and coconut oil in a small bowl until well combined.
  3. Add the 10 drops of vitamin E oil and mix again until smooth.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a clean glass bottle with a dropper lid.
  5. Apply a small amount to your dry lips as needed, and store the bottle in a cool, dark place.

2. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains high levels of menthol, limonene, and beta-pinene.

These bioactive constituents help to moisturize and soothe dry lips by creating a protective barrier on the skin's surface. The antiseptic properties of menthol reduce inflammation and prevent the growth of bacteria that can cause lip infections. The antifungal properties of limonene and beta-pinene also help to combat fungal infections that can cause lip dryness and irritation.

By combining these properties, Mentha x piperita essential oils can help to provide relief and hydration to dry, cracked lips.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the fresh mint leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  4. Let the liquid cool and transfer it to a glass bottle. Cover the bottle and refrigerate for 2 hours.
  5. Using a dropper, extract the essential oil from the cooled liquid. You will need 15-20 drops to make the lip balm. Mix with a carrier oil like coconut oil for dry lips.

3. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to soothe and protect dry, chapped lips. The flavonoids in Calendula officinalis essential oils, such as quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing, making it an effective remedy for dry, cracked lips. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of carotenoids like lutein and beta-carotene help to protect the lips from environmental stressors and promote healthy skin regeneration.

By applying Calendula officinalis essential oils topically, you can take advantage of these beneficial properties to help heal and moisturize dry, chapped lips.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the Calendula flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Let it sit for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar daily.
  3. After the sitting time, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Store the Calendula oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid in a cool, dry place. Use 2-3 tablespoons of the oil as a lip balm by mixing it with 1 tablespoon of beeswax and 1 tablespoon of coconut oil.
  5. Melt the beeswax and coconut oil mixture in a double boiler or in a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth. Add the Calendula oil and stir well.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains compounds like borneol, borneol acetate, camphor, and rosmarinic acid, which have antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to soothe and calm dry, chapped lips by reducing redness and inflammation. The antioxidants in rosmarinic acid also protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting healthy skin cell growth and regeneration. Camphor's astringent properties help to dry out the skin, reducing moisture loss and promoting the healing of dry, cracked lips.

By applying a thin layer of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils to the lips, the active constituents can help to repair and protect dry, chapped skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Wash the leaves and dry them with a clean towel.
  3. Combine the dried leaves and carrier oil in a glass jar. Let it sit for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 2-3 drops on your dry lips for relief.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two compounds that help to soothe and protect dry lips.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, which can reduce redness and prevent infection. The antioxidant properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help to protect the skin from environmental stressors that can exacerbate dryness. When applied topically, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to lock in moisture and promote the healing of chapped lips.

By using Lavandula angustifolia essential oils, you can create a natural barrier to protect your lips and promote healthy skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers, 2 cups of water, and a pot.
  2. Combine the dried flowers and water in the pot and heat on low for 2 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil, like coconut or olive oil, to the strained liquid.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 days to allow the oil to separate, then carefully pour off the oil and discard the solids.

6. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains a high concentration of limonene and beta-pinene, which are the main bioactive constituents that give it its medicinal properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce redness and swelling associated with dry lips. The antiseptic properties of limonene also help prevent infections and promote healing, keeping the lips hydrated and healthy. Additionally, beta-pinene has a soothing effect on the skin, calming irritation and discomfort caused by dryness.

By using Cymbopogon citratus essential oils, you can lock in moisture and promote healthy lip skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 2 cups of water in a pot.
  2. Heat the water on low heat for 30 minutes. Add the Cymbopogon citratus leaves and let simmer for another 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the pot from heat and let it steep for 2 hours. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a fine mesh.
  4. Transfer the strained liquid to a clean glass bottle. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  5. Store the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 days. After 2-3 days, use the oil by applying a small amount to your dry lips.

7. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains active constituents like alkylamides, phenylpropanoids, and sesquiterpenes.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe dry and irritated lips. The sesquiterpenes in Echinacea purpurea essential oils have antioxidant properties that can help protect the skin from environmental stressors, reducing dryness and flakiness. The phenylpropanoids also have antimicrobial properties, which can help prevent infections and promote healthy lip tissue.

By combining these properties, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help to calm and moisturize dry lips, promoting a healthier and more comfortable lip environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves. Wash them with cold water.
  2. Combine 1 cup of the flowers and leaves with 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) in a clean glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. This allows the oil to infuse.
  4. After the infusion, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Echinacea essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops of the oil on your dry lips 2-3 times a day for relief.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for dry lips?

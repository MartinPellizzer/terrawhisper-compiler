1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoids, saponins, and flavonoids that provide its benefits.

The triterpenoids, specifically calletin and oleanolic acid, have anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe and calm dry, chapped lips. Saponins, such as calendulin, have antioxidant properties that protect the lips from damage caused by free radicals. Flavonoids, including kaempferol and quercetin, have antiseptic properties that help prevent infection and promote healing.

This combination of bioactive compounds in Calendula officinalis creams can help to hydrate, soften, and protect dry lips.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Calendula officinalis infused oil, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from heat and stir in the Calendula officinalis infused oil and vitamin E oil.
  4. Pour the mixture into a glass jar or tin and let it cool and solidify at room temperature.
  5. Once solid, use a spatula to scrape the edges and smooth out the surface, then apply the Calendula cream to dry lips as needed.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains active constituents like glycoproteins, vitamins A, C, and E, and fatty acids.

These compounds help to lock in moisture and soothe dry, chapped lips. The glycoproteins in aloe vera have anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce redness and irritation. The vitamins and fatty acids also help to repair and protect the skin, making it an effective treatment for dry, cracked lips.

By applying aloe barbadensis cream to dry lips, the skin can absorb the beneficial compounds and start to heal and moisturize itself naturally.

  1. Gather 1/2 cup of Aloe vera gel, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in short intervals until it's fully melted.
  3. Add coconut oil, shea butter, and Aloe vera gel to the melted beeswax. Stir until everything is well combined.
  4. Remove from heat and add vitamin E oil. Stir well and pour the mixture into a small container.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify. Once solid, your Aloe vera lip cream is ready to use. Store in a cool place.

3. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale creams contains allantoin, aloe-emodin, and caffeic acid as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds work together to provide relief from dry, chapped lips. Allantoin is a humectant that helps retain moisture in the skin, while aloe-emodin has anti-inflammatory properties that soothe and calm irritated skin. Caffeic acid has antioxidant properties that help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals.

By using Symphytum officinale creams, dry lips can be hydrated and protected from further irritation.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Symphytum officinale extract, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 1 tablespoon of shea butter, and 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt beeswax and coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of boiling water. Stir until smooth.
  3. Add Symphytum officinale extract, shea butter, and vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir until well combined.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool until the mixture thickens and reaches a creamy consistency.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let cool completely. Apply the cream to dry lips as needed.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains glycyrrhetic acid, a key compound responsible for its soothing properties.

This acid helps to reduce inflammation and irritation, making it an effective remedy for dry, chapped lips. The emollient properties of glycyrrhetic acid help to lock in moisture, providing long-lasting hydration to dry lips. Glycyrrhiza glabra creams also contain flavonoids, such as liquiritin and glabridin, which have antioxidant properties that help to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals.

By combining these properties, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams provide a natural solution for dry, cracked lips.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of glycerin, and 2 tablespoons of glycyrrhiza glabra root extract in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat until it's warm, then add the glycerin and glycyrrhiza glabra root extract.
  3. Stir the mixture well and let it simmer for 10 minutes, then remove from heat.
  4. Let the mixture cool down to room temperature, then strain it through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean container.
  5. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 1 tablespoon of coconut oil to the mixture and stir until the beeswax is melted. Pour the mixture into lip balm tubes or a container and let it cool before use.

5. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains essential oils with active constituents like menthol, menthone, and limonene.

These compounds have a cooling effect on dry lips, reducing inflammation and soothing irritated skin. Menthol helps to lock in moisture, while menthone and limonene have antibacterial properties that prevent infections and promote healing. The antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties of Mentha x piperita creams also help to calm and protect dry, chapped lips from environmental stressors.

By providing long-lasting hydration and protection, Mentha x piperita creams can help to repair and restore dry, cracked lips.

  1. Gather 1 cup of beeswax, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of honey, and a few drops of peppermint essential oil.
  2. Melt the beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments until melted.
  3. Add the coconut oil and shea butter to the melted beeswax. Stir until well combined.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and add the honey and peppermint essential oil. Stir until smooth.
  5. Pour the mixture into a tin or jar and let it cool and set. Once set, use a spatula to scrape out the Mentha x piperita cream and apply to dry lips.

6. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains bioactive compounds like alkylamides, glycoproteins, and polyphenols.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help soothe and protect dry, irritated lips. The polyphenols in Echinacea purpurea creams have been shown to have a soothing effect on the skin, reducing redness and inflammation. The alkylamides in these creams can help lock in moisture, keeping dry lips hydrated and soft.

By providing long-lasting hydration and soothing relief, Echinacea purpurea creams can help alleviate dry, chapped lips.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.

  3. Add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the melted mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Add the shea butter and vitamin E oil to the mixture and stir until well combined. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool before use.

7. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains rosin and camphor as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce swelling and redness associated with dry, chapped lips. The antibacterial properties of camphor also prevent infection, promoting the healing process of dry, cracked lips. Rosin, on the other hand, helps to lock in moisture and protect the skin from further dryness.

By applying Rosmarinus officinalis creams to dry lips, you can help to soothe and protect them, promoting healthy skin.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of honey, 10 drops of Rosemary essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl in the microwave (30 seconds at a time, until melted).
  3. Add shea butter and honey to the melted mixture. Stir until combined and smooth.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool slightly. Stir in Rosemary essential oil. Pour into a container and let cool completely.
  5. Once cooled and set, apply the Rosemary Rosmarinus officinalis cream to dry lips as needed. Store in a cool place.

Melissa officinalis creams contains tannins, flavonoids, and terpenes as its active constituents.

  4. Add 2 tablespoons of Melissa officinalis essential oil (diluted in 1 tablespoon of carrier oil, such as jojoba oil) to the mixture and stir well.

9. Hippophae rhamnoides

Hippophae rhamnoides creams contains bioactive constituents like vitamin E, omega-3 fatty acids, and flavonoids.

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains a high concentration of active compounds like cineole, limonene, and linalool.

11. Aloe vera

Aloe vera creams contains active constituents like glycoproteins, vitamins, and amino acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce redness and swelling on dry lips. The glycolic acid in aloe vera creams also exfoliates and removes dead skin cells, promoting healthy lip tissue. Additionally, aloe vera's moisturizing properties, thanks to its high water content and humectants like glucomannan, help retain moisture in dry lips.

This combination of properties makes aloe vera creams effective at soothing and hydrating dry, chapped lips.

  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify completely before applying it to your dry lips.

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dry lips from forming?

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dry lips every day?

Consuming herbal teas for dry lips daily can be safe if you choose teas that are gentle and non-irritating.

Some herbal teas, like chamomile and peppermint, can be soothing and hydrating.

Herbal teas can help soothe dry lips.

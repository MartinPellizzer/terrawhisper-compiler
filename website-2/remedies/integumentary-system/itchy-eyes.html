What Are The Main Causes Of Itchy Eyes?

Itchy eyes can be caused by several things.

One main cause is allergies. When you're allergic to something like pollen, dust, or pet dander, your eyes release histamine to fight it off. This histamine makes your eyes itchy and red. Allergies can be seasonal, like hay fever, which occurs when pollen from trees, grasses, and weeds is in the air.

Dry eyes are another common cause of itchy eyes. When your tears don't have enough oil or water, your eyes can become dry and itchy. This can be due to various reasons such as not producing enough tears, having dry air, or being on a computer for a long time. Lastly, hay fever, also known as allergic rhinitis, is a type of allergy that affects the nose and eyes. It occurs when pollen from trees, grasses, and weeds enters your body and triggers an allergic reaction.

This reaction can cause itchy, watery eyes, and other symptoms like sneezing and congestion.

Below are the main causes of itchy eyes.

  • Allergies: When someone has an allergy, their body releases chemicals to fight the allergen, causing blood vessels in their eyes to swell and become itchy.
  • Dry Eyes: Dry eyes cause itchy eyes because the lack of tears or poor quality tears makes the eyes feel uncomfortable and irritated, leading to a strong urge to scratch or rub the eyes.
  • Hay Fever: Hay fever causes itchy eyes because tiny particles from pollen and other allergens enter the eyes and trigger an allergic reaction, releasing histamine and other chemicals that make the eyes feel itchy and uncomfortable.
  • Pollen: When pollen from flowers and trees enters the eyes, it triggers an allergic reaction, causing the eyes to itch and become red due to the release of histamine, a chemical that the body's immune system uses to fight off the perceived threat.
  • Dust: Dust is a common cause of itchy eyes because tiny particles in the dust can get into the eyes and irritate the sensitive tissues, releasing histamine and causing allergic reactions, redness, and itchiness.
  • Pet Dander: Pet dander, which is tiny pieces of skin that fall off animals like dogs and cats, can cause itchy eyes because when these particles get into the air and are inhaled, they can trigger allergic reactions and irritation in the eyes.
  • Mites: Mites, tiny parasites that live on human skin and in hair follicles, can cause itchy eyes when their waste or body parts irritate the eyes, leading to discomfort and itching.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Itchy Eyes?

If you have itchy eyes, some herbs may make them worse.

One herb to avoid is Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla), which can cause eye irritation and itching due to its allergenic properties. Another herb to be careful with is Eyebright (Euphrasia officinalis), which is often used to treat eye problems but can also cause irritation and itchiness in some people.

Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) oil, commonly used in skincare products and tea, can also cause eye irritation and itching. Additionally, the herb Marshmallow (Althaea officinalis) can cause eye problems, including itchiness, due to its high mucilage content, which can be too rich for some people's eyes.

Finally, the herb Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) contains thujone, a chemical that can cause eye irritation and itching.

Dental Patients with Bad Breath: 7 Herbal Teas to Help

Dental patients often struggle with bad breath, also known as halitosis.

This can be due to various reasons such as gum disease, dry mouth, or poor dental hygiene. When we eat, bacteria in our mouth break down food and release strong-smelling compounds. If these bacteria multiply too quickly, our breath can become unpleasantly pungent. This not only affects our self-esteem and confidence but also impacts our social and professional lives. Bad breath can make us feel anxious, embarrassed, and self-conscious, leading to difficulties in personal and public interactions.

The problem is further complicated by the fact that bad breath can be a symptom of a more serious underlying issue, such as gum disease or a dental infection. In these cases, treating bad breath requires addressing the root cause, which may involve medication, antibiotics, or even surgery. However, for many people, bad breath is a recurring issue that requires ongoing management. In these cases, herbal teas can be a helpful addition to our oral care routine. These teas contain compounds that have natural antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce the growth of bacteria in our mouth and soothe inflamed gums.

Drinking herbal teas regularly can help freshen our breath, reduce plaque and tartar buildup, and promote a healthy mouth environment.

1. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas, also known as peppermint tea, can help with bad breath by reducing the bacteria that cause it.

This is due to its antibacterial properties, which come from compounds like menthol and menthone. These compounds kill the bacteria that cause bad breath by disrupting their cell membranes, making it harder for them to survive. To use Mentha x piperita teas for bad breath, try drinking a cup of the tea after meals or when you feel like you need a fresh breath. You can steep a tea bag in hot water for 5-10 minutes, then strain and drink.

If you prefer, you can also add the tea to your toothbrush or mouthwash for an extra boost. When you take action and drink Mentha x piperita tea, you may notice a reduction in bad breath within a few minutes. The menthol and menthone in the tea can help to numb your mouth, making it feel cooler and fresher. You may also notice that your mouth feels cleaner and less likely to harbor bacteria that cause bad breath.

In some cases, you may also notice an improvement in your digestion, which can also contribute to fresher breath.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas have been used to help with bad breath due to their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The tea contains compounds like eucalyptol, a natural decongestant that helps reduce inflammation in the mouth and throat, and limonene, which has antibacterial properties that kill the bacteria responsible for bad breath. The tea also contains antioxidants that help protect the mouth and throat from oxidative stress, further reducing the risk of bad breath. To try Eucalyptus globulus teas for bad breath, start by drinking a cup of the tea after meals to help kill bacteria and reduce inflammation. You can also use the tea as a mouthwash by swishing it in your mouth for 30 seconds before spitting it out.

Additionally, you can add a few drops of eucalyptus oil to your toothpaste or mouthwash for extra antibacterial benefits. When you start drinking Eucalyptus globulus teas regularly, you may notice a reduction in bad breath within a few days to a week. Your mouth and throat may feel cleaner and fresher, and you may be less likely to experience bad breath. However, it's essential to note that Eucalyptus globulus teas may not completely eliminate bad breath, especially if it's caused by a underlying medical condition.

If your bad breath persists, you should consult with your doctor or dentist to rule out any underlying health issues.

3. Cinnamomum zeylanicum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum teas have been used for centuries to combat bad breath, and it's all thanks to their potent properties and constituents.

The tea is rich in cinnamaldehyde, a compound that has natural antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce the growth of bacteria in the mouth that cause bad breath. Cinnamomum zeylanicum teas also contain antioxidants and flavonoids that help to fight free radicals and reduce inflammation in the gums and mouth tissues, making it a natural remedy for gum disease and bad breath. To use Cinnamomum zeylanicum teas for bad breath, you can try drinking a cup of the tea after meals or before bedtime.

Simply steep a teaspoon of Ceylon cinnamon sticks in a cup of hot water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink. You can also add a few drops of honey to sweeten the tea if you prefer. As you start drinking the tea regularly, you may notice a reduction in bad breath, fresher breath, and a decrease in gum inflammation.

You might also experience a boost in your immune system and a general sense of well-being.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, commonly known as ginger tea, can help alleviate bad breath due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The active constituents, including gingerols and shogaols, have been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria in the mouth, which is a primary cause of bad breath. When you drink ginger tea, these compounds work to reduce the levels of volatile sulfur compounds, such as hydrogen sulfide, which are responsible for the unpleasant odor.

To try ginger tea for bad breath, steep a teaspoon of fresh ginger in a cup of boiling water for five minutes, then strain and drink. You can add a squeeze of lemon and a bit of honey to make it more palatable. Drinking a cup of ginger tea after meals and before bedtime may help to reduce bad breath.

You may notice a significant improvement in your oral health and a fresher breath within a few days of consistent use.

5. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum teas, also known as Ceylon cinnamon, have been traditionally used to help alleviate bad breath.

The tea's active compounds, including cinnamaldehyde and eugenol, are responsible for its antibacterial properties, which help kill the bacteria that cause bad breath. These bacteria thrive on the food particles stuck in your teeth and gums, and Cinnamomum verum teas help to reduce their growth. To use Cinnamomum verum teas for bad breath, you can try the following: steep one teaspoon of Ceylon cinnamon in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink.

You can also add a few drops of Ceylon cinnamon essential oil to your toothpaste or mouthwash for added antibacterial protection. To get the best results, make sure to drink the tea after meals and before bedtime, as this will help to prevent bacteria growth throughout the night. As you drink the Cinnamomum verum tea regularly, you may notice that your bad breath starts to improve, and your mouth feels fresher and cleaner. You may also notice a reduction in plaque and tartar buildup on your teeth, and your gums may become healthier and less inflamed.

However, keep in mind that Cinnamomum verum teas are not a replacement for regular oral hygiene practices, such as brushing and flossing, but rather a complementary tool to help maintain good oral health.

6. Satureja montana

Satureja montana teas, also known as mountain savory tea, have been traditionally used to help alleviate bad breath.

The key reason for its effectiveness lies in its properties, particularly its high concentration of carvacrol and thymol, which are known for their antimicrobial and antibacterial properties. These compounds work by killing the bacteria that cause bad breath in the mouth and throat. Additionally, Satureja montana teas contain antioxidants that help reduce inflammation and promote a healthy oral environment. To try Satureja montana tea for bad breath, you can start by brewing a cup of the tea according to the package instructions.

Some people find it most effective to drink a cup of the tea after meals, as this is when bad breath is most likely to occur. You can also add a few drops of Satureja montana essential oil to your toothpaste or mouthwash for extra benefits. It's essential to note that Satureja montana tea may not completely eliminate bad breath, but it can certainly help reduce its frequency and severity. When you start drinking Satureja montana tea, you may notice a reduction in bad breath within a few days to a week. Some people may also experience a decrease in gum inflammation and an improvement in their overall oral health.

However, if you have persistent or severe bad breath, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional to rule out any underlying medical conditions that may need attention.

7. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis teas, also known as sage tea, have been traditionally used to help with bad breath.

The herb contains properties and constituents like salvenol, carnosic acid, and rosmarinic acid, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds help reduce the growth of bacteria in the mouth, which is a common cause of bad breath. To use sage tea for bad breath, start by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried Salvia officinalis leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and let it cool before rinsing your mouth with it. You can also chew on the leaves or add them to your toothpaste for extra benefit. When you drink sage tea regularly, you may notice a reduction in bad breath, as the herb's antimicrobial properties help kill bacteria in your mouth. You may also find that your mouth feels fresher and cleaner, and your gums may become less inflamed.

Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of sage may help reduce swelling and pain in your mouth and gums.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent bad breath from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help reduce bad breath.

Many herbal teas, such as peppermint and sage, have antibacterial properties that can help kill bacteria in the mouth. These bacteria are often responsible for causing bad breath.

By drinking herbal tea, you may be able to reduce the amount of bacteria in your mouth, which can help prevent bad breath from forming.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for bad breath every day?

You can safely drink herbal teas daily for bad breath, but be mindful of their ingredients and potential interactions.

Some teas, like peppermint and eucalyptus, can be too strong if consumed regularly.

Start with small amounts and adjust as needed to avoid discomfort or allergic reactions.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in bad breath?

Herbal teas can start reducing bad breath within a few hours.

Some people notice a difference after drinking one cup, while others may need to drink it for a few days to feel the effects.

The duration depends on the individual and the type of tea being consumed.

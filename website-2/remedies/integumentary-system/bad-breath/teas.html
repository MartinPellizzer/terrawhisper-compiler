10 best herbal teas for bad breath

1. Mentha X Piperita

Mentha x piperita tea, also known as peppermint tea, is a popular remedy for bad breath. The main reason for this is its antibacterial properties, which help kill the bacteria in the mouth that cause bad breath. Peppermint tea contains a compound called menthol, which has a cooling effect and helps freshen the breath. When you drink peppermint tea, the menthol helps to kill the bacteria and reduce inflammation in the mouth. This makes it harder for bacteria to grow and multiply, resulting in fresher breath. Additionally, peppermint tea has a calming effect on the stomach, which can help to reduce nausea and vomiting, both of which can contribute to bad breath. By reducing nausea and killing bacteria, peppermint tea helps to keep the mouth and breath clean and fresh.

2. Eucalyptus Globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tea is a popular remedy for bad breath due to its antibacterial properties. It contains compounds like eucalyptol, which have been shown to kill bacteria in the mouth that can cause bad breath. When we drink eucalyptus tea, the eucalyptol is absorbed into our bloodstream and then released into our saliva. This helps to reduce the number of bacteria in our mouth, making it a more inhospitable place for the bacteria that cause bad breath to thrive. Additionally, eucalyptus globulus tea has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling in the gums and mouth, further contributing to a reduction in bad breath. By reducing the number of bacteria and inflammation in the mouth, eucalyptus globulus tea can help to leave our breath smelling fresher and cleaner.

3. Cinnamomum Verum

Cinnamomum verum tea, also known as Ceylon cinnamon tea, is a natural remedy for bad breath. This tea has antibacterial properties that help kill the bacteria in the mouth that cause bad breath. The antibacterial compounds in Ceylon cinnamon tea, such as cinnamaldehyde, work by stopping the growth of these bacteria, which in turn reduces the production of volatile sulfur compounds that cause bad breath. Additionally, Ceylon cinnamon tea has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and irritation in the mouth and gums, which can also contribute to bad breath. Drinking Ceylon cinnamon tea after meals can help prevent the bacteria in the mouth from multiplying and causing bad breath. The tea's antibacterial properties also help to kill the bacteria that can cause gum disease, another common cause of bad breath.

4. Zingiber Officinale

Zingiber officinale tea, also known as ginger tea, is a natural remedy for bad breath. When you drink ginger tea, the active compounds in the ginger root, such as gingerol and shogaol, are released into your body. By reducing the growth of bacteria, reducing inflammation, and stimulating saliva production, ginger tea can help to freshen your breath and prevent bad breath.

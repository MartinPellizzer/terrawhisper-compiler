7 Herbal Tinctures For Bad Breath

Drinking ginger tea can help to reduce bad breath and make your mouth feel cleaner. Using herbal tinctures to relieve bad breath can be a natural and effective way to keep your mouth fresh.

1. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita tinctures contains essential oils, including menthone, limonene, and beta-pinene, which have antibacterial properties.

These properties help reduce the amount of bacteria in the mouth, which contribute to bad breath. The menthol in Mentha x piperita tinctures also acts as a natural deodorizer, neutralizing odor-causing compounds. Additionally, the tincture's expectorant properties help loosen and clear mucus from the mouth and throat, further reducing the likelihood of bad breath.

By reducing bacteria and mucus, and neutralizing odor-causing compounds, Mentha x piperita tinctures can help alleviate bad breath.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh mint leaves (Mentha x piperita), a clean glass jar with a lid, and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Chop the mint leaves and put them in the glass jar. Add 2 cups of vodka (at least 80 proof) to cover the leaves.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After the 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the dark glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  5. Label the bottle with the date and contents, and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-10 drops of the tincture in water 2-3 times a day to help with bad breath.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contains eucalyptol, a bioactive compound that has antibacterial properties.

These properties help reduce the growth of bacteria in the mouth, which can cause bad breath. Eucalyptol also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe irritated gums and mucous membranes, reducing the likelihood of bad breath. Additionally, eucalyptus globulus tinctures contain flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have antioxidant properties that can help neutralize volatile sulfur compounds responsible for bad breath.

By reducing bacteria and inflammation, and neutralizing odor-causing compounds, eucalyptus globulus tinctures can help alleviate bad breath.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the Eucalyptus leaves to the jar and pour the vodka over them, making sure the leaves are completely covered.
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 5-10 drops as needed to help with bad breath.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains the bioactive constituents gingerol and shogaol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help reduce bad breath by fighting the bacteria that cause it. The antimicrobial properties of gingerol and shogaol also help kill the germs that cause gum disease, a common cause of bad breath. Furthermore, the anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds can help reduce swelling and inflammation in the mouth, making it a less hospitable environment for bacteria to thrive.

This makes Zingiber officinale tinctures a potential solution for people struggling with bad breath.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger root (Zingiber officinale) and 2 cups of 80% vodka or glycerin.
  2. Peel and chop the ginger root into small pieces. Combine the ginger pieces with 2 cups of vodka or glycerin in a clean glass jar.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Zingiber officinale tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 10-20 drops as needed to freshen breath.

By reducing bacteria and inflammation, Salvia officinalis tinctures help eliminate bad breath and promote overall oral health.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent bad breath from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent bad breath.

Some herbs, like peppermint and eucalyptus, have antibacterial properties that can kill germs in the mouth. These germs can cause bad breath if they multiply.

Herbal tea can help reduce the number of germs in the mouth and freshen breath naturally.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for bad breath every day?

Herbal teas can help with bad breath, but drinking them daily might not be entirely safe.

Some herbs can interact with medications or worsen underlying health issues. For example, licorice root can raise blood pressure, while eucalyptus can cause digestive problems.

It's best to consume them in moderation and be aware of potential side effects.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in bad breath?

Herbal teas can start reducing bad breath in a few days, but noticeable results often take a week to 10 days.

This is because herbal teas work by reducing bacteria in the mouth and improving saliva production.

As the body adjusts, bad breath should decrease, but individual results may vary.

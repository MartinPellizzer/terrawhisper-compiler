1. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains menthone, limonene, and piperitone as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial properties, which help to reduce bacteria that cause bad breath. Menthone and limonene also have antiseptic properties, which help to prevent the growth of bacteria in the mouth. Piperitone has a freshening effect, which can help to eliminate odors and leave the mouth feeling clean.

By reducing bacteria and eliminating odors, Mentha x piperita essential oils can help to freshen breath and promote a healthy mouth environment.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh mint leaves (Mentha x piperita) and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Add 2 cups of water to a saucepan and bring to a boil. Remove from heat and add the mint leaves.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 1 hour and 30 minutes. Strain the liquid and discard the mint leaves.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a clean glass jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 days to allow the mixture to separate.
  5. Strain the liquid again and discard the sediment. Transfer the clear liquid to a small glass bottle and use as a mouthwash to help freshen bad breath.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole, a key active constituent that helps to combat bad breath.

Cineole has antimicrobial properties that effectively reduce the bacteria in the mouth, particularly on the tongue and teeth, that cause bad breath. The decongestant and expectorant properties of cineole also help to clear mucus and reduce inflammation in the mouth and throat, further contributing to fresher breath. Additionally, the antiseptic properties of cineole help to prevent the growth of bacteria and fungi in the mouth, reducing the likelihood of bad breath.

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils can be used in combination with water as a mouthwash to help freshen breath and reduce oral bacteria.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then add the Eucalyptus leaves and reduce heat to low.
  3. Steep the leaves for 1 hour, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a bowl and let it sit at room temperature for 2 hours to evaporate excess water.
  5. Using a hydrosol or distillation apparatus, extract the essential oil from the liquid. Store it in a dark glass bottle.

3. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, linalool, and citral as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help combat the underlying causes of bad breath, such as gum infections and inflammation in the mouth. Citral, in particular, has been shown to exhibit antimicrobial activity against bacteria that can cause bad breath. Additionally, linalool has been found to reduce inflammation, which can soothe gum irritation and prevent further infection.

By targeting the root causes of bad breath, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can help prevent the unpleasant odor associated with this condition.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat for 2 hours, then let it cool.
  3. Add the Cymbopogon citratus leaves to the cooled water and let it steep for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dry place. Use 2-3 drops for bad breath relief.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains bioactive constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce the growth of bacteria in the mouth, the primary cause of bad breath. The anti-inflammatory properties also help reduce swelling and irritation in the gums and tongue, promoting a healthier oral environment. Gingerols and shogaols have been shown to have antioxidant properties, which help neutralize free radicals that can damage oral tissues and contribute to bad breath.

By reducing bacteria, inflammation, and oxidative stress, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help alleviate bad breath and promote oral health.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Ginger roots, a cup of Carrier oil, and a clean Glass jar.
  2. Wash the Ginger roots with water, then dry them with a paper towel.
  3. Grate 1 cup of the Ginger roots and put them in the Glass jar.
  4. Pour 1 cup of Carrier oil over the grated Ginger and close the jar. Store it in a cool dark place for 2 weeks.
  5. Strain the oil and discard the Ginger solids. Store the Zingiber officinale essential oil in a clean Glass bottle.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains the bioactive constituents camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate.

These constituents have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce the growth of bacteria in the mouth that cause bad breath. The antimicrobial properties of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils kill bacteria and fungi, while its anti-inflammatory properties reduce swelling and inflammation in the gums and mouth. This helps to prevent the buildup of volatile sulfur compounds, which are a common cause of bad breath.

By reducing the presence of bacteria and inflammation, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help freshen breath and promote oral health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan to 180°F (82°C) and add the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.
  3. Reduce heat to 100°F (38°C) and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and mix with 2 tablespoons of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).

6. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol and carvacrol, two active constituents that give it antibacterial properties.

These properties help kill the bacteria that cause bad breath. Thymol also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can soothe and calm the mouth and throat. Carvacrol has antioxidant properties, which can help neutralize volatile sulfur compounds that contribute to bad breath.

When used in a mouthwash or applied to the gums, Thymus vulgaris essential oils can help reduce the bacteria and odors that cause bad breath.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over low heat, then add the fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves.
  3. Let it simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the liquid reduces to 1/4 cup.
  4. Strain the liquid into a separate container, discarding the leaves. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba oil).
  5. Store the Thymus vulgaris essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use it to freshen breath by applying a few drops to your tongue or gums.

7. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two active constituents that contribute to its benefits.

These compounds have antibacterial and antifungal properties, which help to reduce the presence of bacteria in the mouth that cause bad breath. Linalool also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can soothe and calm the mucous membranes in the mouth, reducing irritation and discomfort. The antimicrobial properties of linalyl acetate help to inhibit the growth of bacteria that can cause bad breath.

By addressing the underlying causes of bad breath, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to freshen the breath and promote oral health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over low heat until it reaches 180°F (82°C).
  3. Add the fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the hot water and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass container, discarding the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 24 hours to allow the essential oil to separate and rise to the top. Skim off the essential oil with a spoon and transfer it to a dark glass bottle for storage.

8. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains the bioactive constituents like thujone, borneol, and salvene, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to fight off the bacteria in the mouth that cause bad breath. The antimicrobial properties of thujone and borneol kill the bacteria that break down food into sulfur compounds, which are often responsible for bad breath. Salvene also helps to reduce inflammation in the mouth, which can contribute to bad breath.

By reducing the bacteria and inflammation, Salvia officinalis essential oils can help to freshen the breath and eliminate bad breath.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water on low heat and add the Salvia officinalis leaves. Let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discarding the leaves. Let it cool.
  4. Add 1/2 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba oil) to the cooled liquid and stir well.
  5. Store the Salvia officinalis essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Use a few drops to freshen breath.

9. Syzygium aromaticum

Syzygium aromaticum essential oils contains a compound called eugenol, which is known for its antimicrobial properties.

Eugenol helps to kill bacteria in the mouth that can cause bad breath. Syzygium aromaticum essential oils also contain a compound called caryophyllene, which has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe mouth inflammation and reduce bad breath. Additionally, the oils contain compounds like limonene and beta-pinene, which have deodorizing properties that can help eliminate volatile sulfur compounds that contribute to bad breath.

When used in a dental care routine, the antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and deodorizing properties of Syzygium aromaticum essential oils can help reduce bad breath and promote a healthy mouth.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Syzygium aromaticum (clove) buds. Clean and dry them.
  2. Use a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water. Add 2 cups of water.
  3. Add 1 cup of the Syzygium aromaticum buds to the double boiler or heat-proof bowl. Heat for 2 hours, then let cool.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean glass container. Discard the buds.
  5. Use a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil to dilute the essential oil. Mix 1 tablespoon of the essential oil with 2 tablespoons of carrier oil.

10. Cinnamomum zeylanicum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum essential oils contains cinnamaldehyde, which is a key bioactive constituent.

This compound has antimicrobial properties, helping to reduce the bacteria that cause bad breath. Cinnamaldehyde also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe and calm the gums, reducing inflammation and discomfort. Additionally, cinnamaldehyde has a deodorizing effect, helping to neutralize volatile sulfur compounds that contribute to bad breath.

By using Cinnamomum zeylanicum essential oils, you can help to combat bad breath and promote a healthier oral environment.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Cinnamomum zeylanicum bark, 4 cups of water, and a large pot.
  2. Combine the Cinnamomum zeylanicum bark and water in the large pot, then bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer for 2 hours, or until the liquid is reduced to 1 cup.
  4. Remove the pot from heat and let it sit for 2 hours, allowing the mixture to steep.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into a clean glass bottle, and discard the solids.

11. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains limonene, anethole, and fenchone as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial properties, which help to reduce the bacteria in the mouth that cause bad breath. Anethole, in particular, has been shown to have antibacterial activity against Streptococcus mutans and other oral pathogens. The essential oil's antifungal properties also help to prevent the growth of fungal infections in the mouth, which can contribute to bad breath.

By inhibiting the growth of microorganisms, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help to freshen breath and maintain oral health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Heat 1 cup of carrier oil in a saucepan over low heat for 2-3 hours.
  3. Add 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds to the heated oil and let it steep for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean container. Discard the seeds and reserve the oil.
  5. Filter the essential oil through a coffee filter or paper towel to remove impurities. Your Foeniculum vulgare essential oil is now ready to use for bad breath.

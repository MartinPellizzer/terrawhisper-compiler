What Are The Main Causes Of Eye Infection?

Eye infections occur when germs enter the eye and cause harm.

There are several causes of eye infections. Bacterial Conjunctivitis is caused by bacteria that enter the eye through touching or sharing makeup, contact lenses, or towels. This type of infection is often red, itchy, and watery.

Viral Conjunctivitis, on the other hand, is caused by viruses that spread quickly through close contact with someone who has the infection. This type of infection is often yellow or pink, itchy, and watery. Herpes Simplex is a viral infection that can enter the eye through contact with someone who has the infection, often through touching or kissing.

This type of infection can cause painful blisters and sores on the eyelid or inside the eye.

Below are the main causes of eye infection.

  • Bacterial Conjunctivitis: Bacterial Conjunctivitis is a cause of eye infection because bacteria in the eye release toxins that cause inflammation, pain, and swelling in the white part of the eye and the eyelids.
  • Viral Conjunctivitis: Viral Conjunctivitis is a cause of eye infection because a highly contagious virus spreads from person to person, causing redness, itching, and discharge in the eyes.
  • Herpes Simplex: Herpes Simplex is a cause of eye infection because it is a type of virus that can spread through skin-to-skin contact, entering the body through small cuts or scratches in the eye and causing painful blisters and inflammation.
  • Trachoma: Trachoma is a cause of eye infection because it is spread when a person's eye comes into contact with the bacteria Chlamydia trachomatis, usually through touching or sharing contaminated items like towels or washcloths.
  • Chlamydia: Chlamydia, a bacterial infection, can cause eye infections, such as conjunctivitis, when the bacteria spread to the eyes through contact with infected discharge or contaminated hands.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Eye Infection?

One herb to be cautious with is Eyebright's relative, 'Gentiana lutea', also known as Yellow Gentian. It has been known to cause eye irritation and sensitivity. Another herb to avoid is 'Ginkgo biloba', which can increase blood flow to the eyes, making infections worse.

'Eyebright's relative, 'Borago officinalis', also known as Borage, can cause eye inflammation and irritation in people with eye infections. 'Hypericum perforatum', or St. John's Wort, can cause eye sensitivity and increased light sensitivity, making eye infections worse. 'Arctium lappa', or Burdock root, can also cause eye irritation and inflammation.

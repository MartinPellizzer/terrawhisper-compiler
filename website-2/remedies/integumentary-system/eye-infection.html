Causes and Treatment of Eye Infection with Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

What are the causes of eye infection?

Eye infections can be caused by different types of germs.

One main cause is bacterial conjunctivitis, which is usually spread by touching or sharing makeup, towels, or other personal items with someone who already has the infection. Bacteria can also enter the eye through a cut or scratch in the cornea. Another common cause is viral conjunctivitis, often caused by a cold or the flu. Viruses can spread quickly through direct contact with someone who has the infection, and can also be airborne.

Fungal keratitis is a type of eye infection caused by fungi, which can enter the eye through a cut or scratch in the cornea. Fungi are commonly found in soil, plants, and water, and can be introduced into the eye during outdoor activities like gardening or swimming. Herpes simplex is a viral infection that can cause eye infections, usually in people who already have the virus in their body. The virus can reactivate and cause eye symptoms, especially in people who are stressed or have a weakened immune system. Pseudomonas is a type of bacteria that can cause eye infections, especially in people who wear contact lenses.

Pseudomonas can grow in contact lens solution and enter the eye through the lens, causing a severe infection.

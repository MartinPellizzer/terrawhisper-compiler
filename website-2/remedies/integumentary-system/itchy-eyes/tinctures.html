When using herbal tinctures, it's essential to follow proper preparation and application instructions to ensure safe and effective use.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains flavonoids, triterpenoids, and carotenoids, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These bioactive constituents help to reduce swelling and irritation in the eyes, making them a good remedy for itchy eyes. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have potent antioxidant properties that neutralize free radicals and calm the eyes. The triterpenoids, such as oleanolic acid and ursolic acid, exhibit anti-inflammatory activity that reduces redness and itching.

By applying Calendula officinalis tinctures to the eyes, the bioactive constituents work together to provide quick relief from itchy and irritated eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula flowers and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the Calendula flowers to the vodka and close the jar.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Label the jar and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture in water to soothe itchy eyes.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis tinctures contains aloe-emodin, aloin, and barbaloin as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that help soothe itchy eyes by reducing redness and swelling. The anthraquinones in aloe vera, like aloe-emodin, also have a calming effect on the eyes, which helps to alleviate itchiness and discomfort. Additionally, the antiseptic properties of aloe vera help to prevent infections that can cause or exacerbate itchy eyes.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infection, aloe barbadensis tinctures can provide relief from itchy eyes.

  1. Gather materials: 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 2 cups of 80% vodka, and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine 1 cup of Aloe gel and 2 cups of vodka in the glass jar. Stir well to mix.
  3. Close the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the Aloe tincture to a dropper bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in the affected eye 2-3 times a day as needed.

3. Hypericum perforatum

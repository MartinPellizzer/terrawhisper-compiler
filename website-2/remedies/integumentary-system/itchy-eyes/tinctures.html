What herbal tinctures you should avoid if you have itchy eyes?

If you have itchy eyes, it's best to avoid herbal tinctures made from plants that can irritate or exacerbate the condition.

For example, tinctures made from goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) should be used with caution, as they contain berberine, which can cause redness and irritation in the eyes. Another herb to be careful with is goldenseal's relative, barberry (Berberis vulgaris), which also contains berberine.

Tinctures made from mullein (Verbascum thapsus) and plantain (Plantago major) might not be the best choices either, as they can be quite drying and may worsen itchy eyes. Additionally, tinctures made from burdock (Arctium lappa) and dandelion (Taraxacum officinale) root may not be suitable, as they can cause irritation and redness in some people.

It's also worth noting that tinctures made from herbs like stinging nettle (Urtica dioica) and celandine (Chelidonium majus) should be used with caution, as they can cause skin and eye irritation in some individuals.

What herbal teas you should avoid if you have itchy eyes?

If you have itchy eyes, it's best to avoid herbal teas that can irritate them further.

One such herb is Ginkgo biloba. Its extracts can cause eye irritation and dryness due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which can be too strong for sensitive eyes. Another herb to avoid is Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis).

Its berberine content can cause eye inflammation and redness, making itchy eyes worse. Similarly, Eyebright (Glaucium flavum) might not be the best choice, as its alkaloids can cause eye irritation and dryness. Also, avoid teas made from Eyebright's relative, Ornithogalum umbellatum, as it too can cause eye problems. Additionally, teas made from Stinging Nettle (Urtica dioica) should be consumed with caution, as its urtic acid can cause skin irritation, which may spread to the eyes if not washed off properly.

Finally, teas made from Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) should be avoided, as its thujone content can cause eye irritation and dryness.

7 herbal remedies for itchy eyes among allergists' patients

As an allergist, you're no stranger to itchy eyes.

In fact, you might be more prone to them due to repeated exposure to allergens like pollen, dust, and pet dander. Your job requires you to be around these allergens all day, which can cause your eyes to get itchy and red. This can be really frustrating, especially when you're trying to examine a patient or work on a report. The itchiness can be so severe that it makes it hard to focus, and in extreme cases, it can even lead to eye irritation or infection. This can affect not only your work but also your personal life, making it difficult to enjoy activities that you love.

When you're an allergist, you're constantly surrounded by allergens, and it can be hard to avoid them, even when you're not working. You might find yourself getting itchy eyes at home, in the car, or even in your sleep. This can be really annoying, and it can make you feel like you're always "on the job.". But there is some good news. Some herbal teas have been shown to help soothe itchy eyes and reduce inflammation. These teas have anti-inflammatory properties that can help calm down the allergic reaction and reduce the itchiness.

They're also often caffeine-free, which is a plus if you're sensitive to caffeine or want to avoid it altogether.

1. Euphrasia officinalis

Euphrasia officinalis teas are a popular remedy for itchy eyes, and for good reason.

The tea is made from the leaves and flowers of the Euphrasia officinalis plant, which is rich in antioxidants, flavonoids, and iridoids. These constituents help to reduce inflammation and soothe the eyes, providing relief from itchy eyes caused by dryness, allergies, or irritation. The iridoids, in particular, have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties, which help to calm the eyes and prevent further irritation. To use Euphrasia officinalis teas for itchy eyes, start by steeping a teaspoon of dried leaves and flowers in a cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it to a clean cloth or cotton pad.

Gently place the cloth over your closed eyes and relax for 10-15 minutes. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day as needed. You can also add a few drops of the tea to a warm compress or eye bath for extra relief. When you use Euphrasia officinalis teas, you can expect to experience a reduction in eye redness and inflammation, as well as a decrease in the frequency and severity of itchy eyes. The tea may also help to promote healing and prevent scarring in the eyes.

However, it's essential to note that Euphrasia officinalis teas may not provide immediate relief, and consistent use may be necessary to achieve the desired results.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas have been used to help alleviate itchy eyes, and it's due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties.

The teas contain compounds like alkylamides, glycosides, and flavonoids, which work together to reduce swelling and ease the symptoms of itchy eyes. These constituents also have an antihistamine effect, helping to counteract the release of histamine, a chemical that causes itchy, watery eyes. If you're experiencing itchy eyes, you can try making a cup of Echinacea purpurea tea by steeping dried flowers in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and let it cool before using it as an eye wash or applying it with a cotton ball to the affected area. You can also add a few drops of the cooled tea to a warm compress and place it over your eyes for 5-10 minutes. As you use Echinacea purpurea tea for itchy eyes, you may notice a reduction in redness and swelling, and your eyes may feel more comfortable and less irritated. The anti-inflammatory properties of the tea may also help to soothe and calm the eyes, promoting healing and reducing the risk of infection.

However, it's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea tea may not provide immediate relief, and you may need to use it regularly to see noticeable improvements.

3. Chamomilla recutita

Chamomilla recutita teas have been used for centuries to soothe itchy eyes due to their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

The tea contains apigenin, a flavonoid that binds to GABA receptors in the body, promoting relaxation and reducing inflammation. Chamazulene, a sesquiterpene lactone, also contributes to the tea's anti-inflammatory effects, helping to calm irritated tissues and reduce redness.

When making Chamomilla recutita tea, steep one teaspoon of dried flowers in a cup of boiling water for five to seven minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it as a compress or eye wash to the affected area. As the tea's active compounds work to reduce inflammation and promote relaxation, you may start to feel a decrease in itchiness and discomfort within 15 to 30 minutes.

You may also notice a reduction in redness and swelling, allowing your eyes to feel more comfortable and calm.

4. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas have been used for centuries to soothe itchy eyes due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The tea contains flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, which have been shown to reduce inflammation and ease redness. Additionally, calendula contains triterpenoids and saponins, which have antimicrobial properties that help fight off infections that can cause itchy eyes. To use calendula tea for itchy eyes, you can make a strong tea by steeping one teaspoon of dried calendula flowers in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it to a clean cloth, which you can then place over your closed eyes.

You can also use cooled tea as a compress by soaking a cloth in it and applying it to your eyes for 5-10 minutes, 2-3 times a day. When you use calendula tea for itchy eyes, you can expect a reduction in redness, swelling, and inflammation. The tea's antimicrobial properties may also help clear up any underlying infections that are causing the itchiness. You may also find that your eyes feel more comfortable and less irritated after using the tea.

However, if your itchy eyes persist or are accompanied by other symptoms like discharge or vision problems, you should consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas, made from the dried flowers of the lavender plant, may help with itchy eyes due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties.

The tea contains compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which have been shown to reduce redness and swelling in the eyes. To use Lavandula angustifolia teas for itchy eyes, steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried lavender flowers in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Let the tea cool, then use it as a compress by soaking a clean cloth in the tea and applying it to your closed eyes for 5-10 minutes. This may help to reduce itching and redness, and potentially alleviate dryness and irritation. Some people may also experience a decrease in eye discharge or tearing.

However, if your itchy eyes persist or worsen, you should consult a healthcare professional to rule out underlying conditions that may require medical attention.

6. Plantago major

Plantago major teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate itchy eyes, and it's due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

The tea contains compounds like plantain extract, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which work together to reduce swelling and calm irritated tissues in the eyes. When you drink Plantago major tea, the active ingredients are absorbed into your bloodstream and then delivered to the affected areas, providing quick relief from itchiness and discomfort.

To use Plantago major tea for itchy eyes, try steeping a teaspoon of dried plantain leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink the tea 2-3 times a day. You can also apply a cooled tea compress or a few drops of plantain extract directly to your closed eyes to get faster results.

As you take the tea, you may start to feel the itchiness subside within 15-30 minutes, and your eyes may look and feel clearer and more comfortable within a few hours.

Symphytum officinale teas are a natural remedy for itchy eyes, and they work by soothing and reducing inflammation in the eyes.

The tea contains properties like allantoin, a compound that helps to heal and protect the mucous membranes, and mucilages, which are thick, gel-like substances that calm and moisturize the eyes. When you drink Symphytum officinale tea, the allantoin and mucilages are absorbed into the body and then released into the eyes, where they help to reduce swelling and ease the itching. If you're experiencing itchy eyes, you can try making a strong cup of Symphytum officinale tea by steeping one teaspoon of dried root in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it to a clean cloth or cotton pad and gently pressing it onto your closed eyes for 5-10 minutes.

You can repeat this process several times a day for relief. It's also a good idea to consult with a healthcare professional before using any new herbal remedy, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or allergies. When you take action by drinking and applying Symphytum officinale tea to your itchy eyes, you can expect to experience relief from itching and inflammation, and your eyes may start to feel cooler and more comfortable within 10-15 minutes. The tea may also help to reduce redness and swelling in the eyes, making them look and feel healthier.

However, it's essential to note that if your itchy eyes are caused by an underlying medical condition, such as conjunctivitis or keratitis, you should seek medical attention and follow the advice of your healthcare provider.

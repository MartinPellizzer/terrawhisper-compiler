8 herbal essential oils to alleviate itchy eyes in allergist patients

As an allergist, you're probably no stranger to itchy eyes.

You spend most of your day looking at skin and analyzing test results, which means you're often surrounded by allergens like dust, pollen, and pet dander. This constant exposure can cause your eyes to get itchy, red, and watery. It's not just an annoyance - it can affect your work and daily life. You might find yourself rubbing your eyes constantly, which can irritate them further and even lead to infection. It's not uncommon for allergists to wear masks or glasses to protect themselves, but even these precautions can't always prevent the itchiness.

The constant itchiness can be distracting and uncomfortable, making it hard to focus on your work. You might feel like you're always looking for a solution, whether it's in the form of medication, eye drops, or other treatments. As an allergist, you know that it's essential to take care of your own health to provide the best care for your patients. One potential solution that some allergists are turning to is herbal essential oils. These oils are derived from plants and have been used for centuries to treat various health issues, including itchy eyes. They work by reducing inflammation and soothing the skin and eyes.

Some herbal essential oils have anti-irritant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to calm itchy eyes and prevent further irritation.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils have been traditionally used to soothe itchy eyes, and for good reason.

The oil's anti-inflammatory properties, particularly from its high concentration of triterpenoid saponins and flavonoids, help to calm redness and swelling. Its high content of beta-caryophyllene, a sesquiterpene with potent anti-inflammatory properties, also contributes to its soothing effects.

When used in a compress or added to a carrier oil for a gentle massage around the eye area, Calendula officinalis essential oils may help to reduce discomfort and irritation. To try this remedy, dilute a few drops of the essential oil in a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil, and gently massage it around your closed eyelids using a circular motion. If you experience any relief from itchiness and redness, it's likely due to the oil's ability to reduce inflammation and promote healing in the affected area.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for further guidance.

2. Euphorbia peplus

Euphorbia peplus essential oils have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help soothe itchy eyes.

This oil contains constituents like omega-3 fatty acids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which work together to calm the skin and reduce redness. The omega-3 fatty acids in Euphorbia peplus oil have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling and itching in the eyes. If you experience itchy eyes, you can try applying a few drops of Euphorbia peplus essential oil to a cotton ball and gently placing it over your closed eyes. You can also add a few drops of the oil to a warm compress and apply it to your eyes for a few minutes.

Be careful not to get the oil directly into your eyes, as this can cause further irritation. When you use Euphorbia peplus essential oil to treat itchy eyes, you may experience a decrease in itching and redness within a few minutes. The oil's anti-inflammatory properties can help calm the skin and reduce swelling, allowing you to feel more comfortable and relieved. You may also notice an improvement in your vision, as the oil's antimicrobial properties can help reduce the presence of irritants and allergens in your eyes.

However, if your itchy eyes persist or worsen, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional to rule out any underlying conditions that may require medical attention.

3. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale essential oils, also known as comfrey oil, may help soothe itchy eyes due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

The oil contains constituents like allantoin, rosmarinic acid, and triterpenoids, which have been shown to calm and reduce redness in the eyes. These compounds work by reducing swelling and inflammation, making it easier to manage itchy eyes. If you're experiencing itchy eyes, try applying a few drops of Symphytum officinale essential oils to a cotton ball and gently pat it around your eyes.

You can also add a few drops of the oil to a warm compress and place it over your closed eyes for a few minutes. Be sure to dilute the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil, as undiluted essential oils can be too strong for the delicate skin around your eyes. By taking these steps, you may notice a reduction in itchiness and redness in your eyes, making it more comfortable to go about your daily activities. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of the oil may help to prevent further irritation and inflammation, allowing your eyes to heal faster.

However, if your itchy eyes persist or worsen, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

4. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils are often used to help soothe itchy eyes due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties.

The essential oil contains constituents such as alkylamides, caffeic acid, and polyacetylenes, which have been shown to reduce swelling and ease discomfort. When applied topically to the affected area, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help calm the irritated skin around the eyes and provide relief from itchiness. To use Echinacea purpurea essential oils for itchy eyes, gently dilute a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil, and apply it to a cotton ball or swab.

Then, carefully place the cotton ball or swab over your closed eyes to allow the oil to absorb into the skin. You can repeat this process as needed, ideally 2-3 times a day, to help reduce the itching and discomfort. When you use Echinacea purpurea essential oils, you may start to notice a decrease in itchiness and redness around your eyes within 10-15 minutes. As the oil works to reduce inflammation and ease discomfort, you may also experience a reduction in puffiness and swelling.

Regular use of Echinacea purpurea essential oils may also help to reduce the frequency and severity of itchy eye episodes over time.

5. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can help with itchy eyes.

The main constituents of aloe vera, such as aloe-emodin, aloin, and vitamins A, C, and E, work together to calm and reduce redness. When using aloe barbadensis essential oils for itchy eyes, start by cleaning your hands thoroughly and dipping a cotton ball or soft cloth in the oil.

You may experience a quick relief from itching and redness, and possibly see an improvement in eye comfort within a few minutes to an hour.

8 Herbal Essential Oils For Itchy Eyes

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins and flavonoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that help calm itchy eyes. The sesquiterpenes in calendula oil, such as calendulol and isocalendulol, have antimicrobial properties that can help reduce eye irritation caused by infections or allergies. Additionally, the flavonoids present in calendula oil, including quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant properties that help protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

When applied topically, calendula oil's anti-inflammatory and soothing properties can help alleviate itchy eyes and promote eye comfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers, 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba oil), and a dark glass bottle with a lid.
  2. Combine the Calendula flowers and carrier oil in a saucepan, and heat it over low heat for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool. Then, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the dark glass bottle.
  4. Store the Calendula oil in the dark glass bottle and let it sit for 2-3 weeks to mature. Shake the bottle daily.
  5. After the 2-3 weeks, use the Calendula oil for itchy eyes by dipping a cotton ball in it and gently applying it to your eyes.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can help to reduce redness and itching in itchy eyes. The oils' antiseptic properties also help to prevent the growth of bacteria and other microorganisms that can exacerbate eye irritation. Additionally, the oils' calming properties can help to reduce stress and promote relaxation, which can further aid in alleviating itchy eyes.

By applying a few drops of Lavandula angustifolia essential oils to a cloth and gently pressing it against the closed eyes, some people find relief from itchy eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a pot.
  2. Heat the water in the pot until it reaches 100°F to 110°F (38°C to 43°C).
  3. Add the fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the hot water and let it steep for 1-2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a glass container. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 days to allow the oil to separate and rise to the top. Skim off the oil and store it in a dark glass bottle.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains a range of bioactive constituents, including alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties that can help to soothe itchy eyes. The alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the release of histamine, a chemical that can contribute to itching and inflammation. The glycosides, on the other hand, have been found to have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce redness and swelling in the eyes.

By applying Echinacea purpurea essential oils topically, you can potentially alleviate itchy eyes by reducing inflammation and histamine release.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Combine the Echinacea flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Let it sit in a cool dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  3. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a small saucepan and heat it gently over low heat. Do not boil the mixture.
  5. Remove the saucepan from heat and let the mixture cool. Store the Echinacea essential oil in a clean glass bottle. Use 2-3 drops in a warm compress for itchy eyes.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains compounds like aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-vertin.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that help soothe itchy eyes. The aloe-emodin in Aloe barbadensis essential oils has been shown to reduce swelling and redness, while the aloe-vertin has a calming effect on the eyes. The anti-inflammatory properties of aloin help to reduce the itching and irritation in the eyes.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a calm environment, Aloe barbadensis essential oils can provide relief for itchy eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, a clean bowl, and a cheesecloth or a coffee filter.
  2. Cut the Aloe leaf in half and scoop out the gel. Transfer the gel to the bowl.
  3. Let the gel sit in the bowl for 2-3 hours to allow the juices to settle.
  4. Strain the gel through the cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the extracted Aloe gel in the fridge. To use as eye drops, mix 1 tablespoon of the Aloe gel with 1 cup of water.

5. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds such as cineole and linalool.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and redness in itchy eyes. Cineole also has antimicrobial properties, which can help combat infections that may be causing the itchiness. Additionally, linalool has a soothing effect that can help calm irritated eyes.

The combination of these properties in Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils makes it a potential remedy for itchy eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves, 2 cups of water, and a glass jar.
  2. Combine the leaves and water in the glass jar, and let it sit for 2 hours to allow the leaves to infuse.
  3. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another container, discarding the leaves.
  4. Heat the mixture gently over low heat (100°F - 150°F) for 30 minutes to help release the oil.
  5. Let the mixture cool and then store it in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

6. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains a group of active constituents called camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that can help soothe itchy eyes. Camphor helps to reduce redness and swelling, while borneol and bornyl acetate work together to calm the eyes and reduce discomfort. The antiseptic properties of these constituents also help to prevent infection and promote healing in the eyes.

By applying a few drops of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils to a cotton ball and placing it over the eyes, the active constituents can be absorbed and provide relief from itchy eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosemary leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water over low heat for 10-15 minutes. Add the Rosemary leaves.
  3. Remove from heat and let it steep for 30 minutes. This helps release the oils.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool place. Use 2-3 drops in a carrier oil for itchy eyes.

7. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like cineole and pinene, which have anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and congestion in the eyes, making it easier to breathe and see clearly. Cineole also has antiseptic properties, which can help protect the eyes from infection and promote healing. The anti-inflammatory properties of pinene can also help reduce redness and irritation in the eyes, providing relief from itchy eyes.

By applying Eucalyptus globulus essential oils to the skin around the eyes or inhaling it through steam inhalation, you can take advantage of its soothing and decongestant properties.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Eucalyptus leaves.
  3. Let it simmer for 2 hours, then turn off the heat.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  5. Store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

8. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium essential oils contains sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties that help soothe irritated eyes. The sesquiterpenes in Achillea millefolium essential oils, such as chamazulene and alpha-pinene, have potent anti-inflammatory effects that can reduce redness and swelling in the eyes. The flavonoids, including kaempferol and quercetin, have antioxidant properties that help protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the eyes from damage, Achillea millefolium essential oils can provide relief from itchy eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Achillea millefolium leaves and flowers. Clean them with water.
  2. Use a glass jar with a lid to make the infusion. Add 2 cups of water to the jar.
  3. Add 1/4 cup of the cleaned Achillea millefolium leaves and flowers to the jar. Let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Use a distiller or a still to distill the liquid into essential oil. Collect 1 tablespoon of the essential oil. Store it in a dark glass bottle.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent itchy eyes from forming?

Drinking herbal tea might help soothe itchy eyes.

Certain teas, like chamomile and peppermint, have anti-inflammatory properties that could calm irritated eyes. The cooling sensation from these teas might also provide temporary relief from itchy eyes.

Some people find that a warm cup of herbal tea can help ease eye irritation.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for itchy eyes every day?

Herbal teas like chamomile, peppermint, and calendula can help soothe itchy eyes.

However, consuming them daily may not be entirely safe. Some herbal teas contain compounds that can be toxic in large amounts, and frequent use can cause interactions with other substances or worsen underlying conditions.

It's best to use them in moderation.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in itchy eyes?

Herbal teas for itchy eyes can start to help within a few hours to a day.

Some people find relief from the burning and itching after drinking a cup or two.

The effects can last anywhere from a few hours to a full day, depending on the individual and the specific tea being used.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for itchy eyes?

For itchy eyes, it's best to drink herbal tea in the evening or at bedtime. This allows the soothing properties to calm and relax your eyes while you sleep, reducing redness and discomfort when you wake up.

8 Herbal Essential Oils For Itchy Eyes

As an allergist, you're probably no stranger to itchy eyes.

You spend most of your day looking at skin and analyzing test results, which means you're often surrounded by allergens like dust, pollen, and pet dander. This constant exposure can cause your eyes to get itchy, red, and watery. It's not just an annoyance - it can affect your work and daily life. You might find yourself rubbing your eyes constantly, which can irritate them further and even lead to infection. It's not uncommon for allergists to wear masks or glasses to protect themselves, but even these precautions can't always prevent the itchiness.

The constant itchiness can be distracting and uncomfortable, making it hard to focus on your work. You might feel like you're always looking for a solution, whether it's in the form of medication, eye drops, or other treatments. As an allergist, you know that it's essential to take care of your own health to provide the best care for your patients. One potential solution that some allergists are turning to is herbal essential oils. These oils are derived from plants and have been used for centuries to treat various health issues, including itchy eyes. They work by reducing inflammation and soothing the skin and eyes.

Some herbal essential oils have anti-irritant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to calm itchy eyes and prevent further irritation.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are known to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling and soothe irritation in itchy eyes. The essential oil's flavonoids, in particular, have been shown to have a strong anti-inflammatory effect, which can help to calm the itch and redness associated with irritated eyes.

If you experience itchy eyes, try applying a few drops of Calendula officinalis essential oil to a cotton ball and gently placing it over your closed eye for a few minutes. You can also add a few drops of the oil to a warm compress and apply it to your eyes for added relief. After taking these actions, you may find that your itchy eyes feel cooler and more comfortable, with a noticeable reduction in the itchiness and redness.

The oil's soothing effects may also help to promote healing and reduce the risk of further irritation.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These properties can help to reduce the irritation and swelling associated with itchy eyes. When you apply Lavandula angustifolia essential oils to the affected area, the linalool and linalyl acetate can help to calm the skin and reduce redness. To use Lavandula angustifolia essential oils for itchy eyes, try adding a few drops to a damp cloth and gently wiping the area.

You can also mix a few drops of the essential oil with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it to the affected area using a cotton swab. Be careful not to get the essential oil directly into your eyes, as this can cause irritation. When you use Lavandula angustifolia essential oils for itchy eyes, you may notice a reduction in the itching and redness within a few minutes. The inflammation may also start to subside, and the skin may begin to feel cooler and more comfortable.

Repeating this process several times a day may help to provide longer-lasting relief from itchy eyes.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains compounds that help to soothe and calm itchy eyes.

The main constituents, such as alkylamides and caffeic acid, have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce swelling and redness. Additionally, the oil's antioxidant properties help to protect the delicate tissues of the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. The essential oil's anti-allergic properties also help to reduce the body's allergic response, which can contribute to itchy eyes.

To use Echinacea purpurea essential oils for itchy eyes, try adding a few drops to a warm compress and gently applying it to your closed eyes for 5-10 minutes. You can also add a few drops to a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it around your eyes with a gentle massaging motion. After using the oil, you may experience a reduction in itching and redness, and your eyes may feel more comfortable and calm.

As the inflammation and allergic response decrease, your itchy eyes may start to heal, and you may notice an improvement in your vision.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains anti-inflammatory properties, particularly from the constituents such as aloe-emodin, aloin, and vitamins A, C, and E. These properties help soothe and calm irritated eyes, reducing itchiness and redness.

When used as a compress or applied directly to the eyes, these constituents help to reduce inflammation and promote healing. To use Aloe barbadensis essential oil for itchy eyes, apply a few drops to a clean cloth and gently press it over your closed eyes for a few minutes. Alternatively, you can add a few drops to a bowl of water and use it as a rinse to clean your eyes.

When you use Aloe barbadensis essential oil for itchy eyes, you can expect the itchiness to subside, and redness to reduce. The anti-inflammatory properties will help to calm the eyes, and the soothing effect will help to promote healing. You may also notice an improvement in the overall comfort of your eyes, and a decrease in the urge to scratch or rub them.

However, if your itchy eyes persist or are accompanied by other symptoms such as vision changes or discharge, you should consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

5. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and linalool that work together to soothe itchy eyes.

The cineole in particular has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce redness and swelling in the eyes. When you apply a few drops of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil to a cotton ball and gently press it against your closed eye, the oil can seep in and start to calm down the irritated tissues. To use it effectively, start by washing your hands and then carefully apply the oil to a clean cotton ball.

Gently press the cotton ball against your closed eye for a few seconds before removing it. You may feel a slight cooling sensation as the oil takes effect. As the oil works to reduce inflammation and calm the tissues, you should start to feel some relief from the itchiness and discomfort.

Your eyes may begin to feel more comfortable and less irritated, and you may be able to open them without feeling a strong urge to rub or scratch them.

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains a powerful blend of properties that can help alleviate itchy eyes.

The main constituent, camphor, has anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that can reduce redness and swelling in the eyes. When using Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils for itchy eyes, dilute a few drops in a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil and apply it to the affected area using a cotton ball or swab.

You can also inhale the oil by adding a few drops to a diffuser or by mixing it with a carrier oil and applying it to your temples. As you apply the oil, you may feel a cooling sensation that helps to reduce itching and inflammation. After using the oil, you may notice a decrease in redness and swelling, and the itching may subside, allowing you to see more clearly.

With regular use, you may find that your itchy eyes become less frequent, and your eyes feel more comfortable and refreshed.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent itchy eyes from forming?

Drinking herbal tea might help soothe itchy eyes.

Herbal teas like chamomile, peppermint, and calendula can help soothe itchy eyes.

It's best to use them in moderation.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in itchy eyes?

Herbal teas for itchy eyes can start to help within a few hours to a day.

Some people find relief from the burning and itching after drinking a cup or two.

The effects can last anywhere from a few hours to a full day, depending on the individual and the specific tea being used.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for itchy eyes?

For itchy eyes, it's best to drink herbal tea in the evening or at bedtime. This allows the soothing properties to calm and relax your eyes while you sleep, reducing redness and discomfort when you wake up.

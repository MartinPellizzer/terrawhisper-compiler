1. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale creams contains allantoin, aloe-emodin, and symphytine as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that help calm itchy eyes. Allantoin, in particular, has been shown to reduce swelling and promote healing, making it an effective ingredient for soothing irritated eyes. Aloe-emodin has antioxidant properties that help protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals, further reducing inflammation and discomfort.

When applied as a cream, these active constituents work together to provide relief from itchy, irritated eyes.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water, 1 cup of aloe vera gel, 2 tablespoons of Symphytum officinale extract, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, and 2 tablespoons of beeswax.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a small saucepan over low heat.
  3. Add the aloe vera gel and Symphytum officinale extract to the saucepan. Stir until well combined.
  4. Remove the saucepan from the heat and slowly add the distilled water while stirring continuously.
  5. Pour the mixture into a small container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, it is ready to use as an eye cream for itchy eyes.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains flavonoids, triterpenoids, and carotenoids as its active constituents.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers and 1 cup of coconut oil in a saucepan.

  3. Let the mixture simmer for 2-3 hours, then strain it through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Mix 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter in a saucepan over low heat until melted.
  5. Combine the Calendula oil with the melted beeswax and shea butter mixture, and pour into a container to cool and set.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains alkylamides and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and redness in itchy eyes. The alkylamides also have an inhibitory effect on histamine release, a chemical that can exacerbate itchy eyes. Echinacea's flavonoids, particularly cichoric acid, have antioxidant properties that can help soothe and calm itchy eyes.

By reducing inflammation and histamine release, Echinacea purpurea creams can provide relief from itchy eyes.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat, stirring until smooth.
  3. Add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the melted mixture and let it steep for 2 hours, or until it reaches room temperature.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Add the vitamin E oil and mix well. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool before use.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for itchy eyes every day?

Consuming herbal teas for itchy eyes daily may not be a good idea.

It's essential to use them in moderation and consider alternative treatments if symptoms persist.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in itchy eyes?

Herbal teas can help with itchy eyes, but it's hard to say exactly when you'll feel relief.

Some people notice a difference after a few days of drinking the tea, while others might take a week or two.

It really depends on how often you drink the tea and how severe your itchy eyes are.

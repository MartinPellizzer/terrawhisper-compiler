What Are The Main Causes Of Jock Itch?

Jock itch is caused by three main things: fungal infection, bacterial infection, and Candida.

A fungal infection, also known as tinea cruris, is the most common cause of jock itch. It is caused by a type of fungus called dermatophyte, which grows in warm, moist areas. This fungus can be found in public showers, locker rooms, and even on other people's skin. When the fungus gets on your skin, it can start to grow and cause an itchy rash.

Bacterial infection can also cause jock itch, but it is less common. Bacteria can enter your skin through cuts or scrapes, and cause an infection. Candida is a type of yeast that can also cause jock itch. It is often found on the skin, but it can overgrow and cause a rash when the skin is warm and moist.

All three of these causes can be prevented by keeping your skin clean and dry, wearing clean clothes, and avoiding sharing personal items.

Understanding Bad Breath: Causes, Medicinal Herbs, and Natural Preparations

Bad breath is a common problem where the mouth emits an unpleasant odor.

It is often caused by poor oral hygiene, gum disease, or certain health conditions. If left untreated, bad breath can lead to social embarrassment, low self-confidence, and even damage relationships. It can also be a sign of an underlying health issue, such as diabetes or gum disease, which can have serious consequences if not addressed. Dealing with bad breath is crucial, and one effective way to do so is by using herbs and herbal preparations. Peppermint (Mentha x piperita) is a popular herb known for its refreshing and cooling properties. It can be consumed as a tea or a decoction to help reduce bad breath. Eucalyptus (Eucalyptus globulus) is another herb that can help to combat bad breath. Its antibacterial properties can help to reduce the growth of bacteria in the mouth that cause bad breath. Eucalyptus can be added to a decoction or consumed as a tea to help freshen the breath. Ginger (Zingiber officinale) is a natural anti-inflammatory herb that can help to reduce inflammation in the mouth and gums, which can contribute to bad breath. It can be consumed as a tea or a tincture to help soothe the mouth and gums, and leave the breath smelling fresh. Herbal preparations such as teas, decoctions, and tinctures can be effective in addressing bad breath. Teas are a popular and easy way to consume herbs, while decoctions are a more concentrated form of tea that can be made by simmering the herbs in water.

Tinctures are a concentrated liquid extract of the herb, which can be added to water or taken sublingually.

What Are The Main Causes Of Bad Breath?

Bad breath, also known as halitosis, can be caused by several factors.

One main cause is dental caries, which occurs when bacteria in the mouth break down food, especially sugars and starches, and produce strong-smelling compounds. This can happen when teeth are not properly cleaned or when a cavity is not treated. Periodontal disease is another cause of bad breath.

This condition occurs when bacteria in the mouth cause inflammation and infection of the gums and bone that support the teeth. As the disease progresses, it can cause bad breath due to the presence of bacteria and other substances in the mouth. Dry mouth, also known as xerostomia, can also lead to bad breath. When the mouth does not produce enough saliva, food particles and bacteria can stick to the teeth and tongue, causing bad breath.

This can be caused by dehydration, certain medications, or medical conditions such as Sjögren's syndrome.

Below are the main causes of bad breath.

  • Dental Caries: Dental caries, a type of tooth decay, causes bad breath when bacteria in the rotting tooth multiply and release strong-smelling gases as a byproduct of breaking down food.
  • Periodontal Disease: Periodontal disease is a cause of bad breath because the bacteria that infect the gums and teeth can release strong-smelling chemicals into the mouth, making it smell unpleasant.
  • Dry Mouth: When you have dry mouth, your saliva production decreases, which makes it harder for your mouth to wash away bacteria and food particles that can cause bad breath.
  • Tonsillitis: Tonsillitis causes bad breath because the infected tonsils release strong-smelling gases and bacteria that build up in the mouth and throat, leading to a foul odor.
  • Gum Recession: When gum recession exposes the roots of teeth, it allows bacteria to accumulate and cause bad breath, as these bacteria thrive in the moist environment between the teeth and gums.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Bad Breath?

If you have bad breath, there are some herbs you should avoid using.

Cloves, which are made from the dried flower buds of the Syzygium aromaticum tree, contain a chemical called eugenol. This chemical can irritate the gums and make bad breath worse. Another herb to avoid is Thymus vulgaris, commonly known as thyme.

While thyme is known for its antibacterial properties, it can also be quite strong and may make bad breath even more noticeable. Some people may also be allergic to thyme, which can cause mouth irritation and make bad breath worse. Fennel (Foeniculum vulgare) seeds and leaves are often used to aid digestion, but they can also cause gas and bloating, which can contribute to bad breath.

This is because fennel contains compounds that can make the digestive system work too hard, leading to an increase in gas production.

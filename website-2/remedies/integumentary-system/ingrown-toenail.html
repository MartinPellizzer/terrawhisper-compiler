What Are The Main Causes Of Ingrown Toenail?

Ingrown toenail occurs when the edge of the toenail grows into the skin around it, causing pain and inflammation.

The main causes of ingrown toenail include tight shoes, poor grooming, and trauma. Tight shoes put pressure on the toes, forcing the nail to grow into the surrounding skin.

This can happen when shoes are too small or don't fit properly. Poor grooming, such as cutting the toenail too short or not cutting it straight, can cause the nail to grow into the skin. Cutting the nail too short can also cause the nail to become embedded in the skin, leading to an ingrown toenail.

Trauma, such as stepping on a nail or hitting the toe, can also cause an ingrown toenail by forcing the nail to grow into the surrounding skin.

Below are the main causes of ingrown toenail.

  • Tight Shoes: Tight shoes can cause ingrown toenail because they put pressure on the toe, making the skin around the nail overlap and grow into the nail, leading to pain and infection.
  • Poor Grooming: Poor grooming can cause ingrown toenails because when the nail grows into the skin, it happens when the nail edge is not cut straight or the skin around the nail is not kept clean and free of dirt.
  • Trauma: Trauma to the toe, such as stepping on something hard or getting a blow to the foot, can cause an ingrown toenail by forcing the nail to grow into the skin instead of its normal, smooth path.
  • Genetics: Genetics can cause ingrown toenails because some people are born with a tendency to have curved or irregular toenails, which can grow into the skin around the nail bed.
  • Athlete's Foot: Athlete's Foot can cause ingrown toenails because the fungal infection causes redness, swelling, and thickening of the skin around the toe, which can lead to the nail growing into the skin instead of its normal straight path.

Ingrown Toenail: Causes, Symptoms, and Herbal Preparations for Relief

An ingrown toenail is a painful condition where the edge of the nail grows into the skin, causing redness, swelling, and sometimes even infection.

It can be really uncomfortable, especially when you're walking or wearing shoes. If left untreated, it can lead to more serious problems, like pus-filled abscesses or even diabetes complications. Ingrown toenails are often caused by poorly fitting shoes, nail biting, or cutting the nails too short. When it comes to treating ingrown toenails naturally, herbal remedies can be a great option.

Certain herbs have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help soothe the pain and prevent infection. For example, tea tree oil has antiseptic properties that can help combat bacterial growth, while aloe vera gel can reduce swelling and promote healing. You can use these herbs in various forms, such as making a warm foot bath with tea tree oil and aloe vera, or applying a paste of turmeric and tea tree oil directly to the affected area. Drinking a tea made from calendula flowers can also help to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing.

Some people also use a poultice made from comfrey leaves to draw out the infection and promote new tissue growth.

What are the causes of ingrown toenail?

Ingrown toenails can be a painful and annoying problem.

There are several main causes that can lead to this issue. One of the causes is the shape of the toe itself. If the toe is naturally curved or has a sharp edge, it can put pressure on the nail, causing it to grow into the skin. Another cause is the shape of the nail plate. If the nail is too big or too small for the toe, it can put pressure on the skin as it grows, leading to an ingrown toenail. This is often due to genetics or a nail plate that has become misshapen over time. Wearing shoes that are too tight or don't fit properly is another common cause of ingrown toenails.

When the shoe is too small, it can cause the toe to be cramped and the nail to be forced into the skin. This is especially true for people who wear high heels or tight-fitting shoes. The length of the toe can also be a contributing factor. If the toe is too short, the nail can grow into the skin, causing an ingrown toenail. This can be due to genetics or a condition where the toe becomes smaller over time. Pressure is also a major cause of ingrown toenails. This can come from wearing shoes that are too tight, walking or running in shoes that don't fit properly, or even just standing for long periods of time.

When there is too much pressure on the nail, it can cause the nail to grow into the skin, leading to pain and discomfort.

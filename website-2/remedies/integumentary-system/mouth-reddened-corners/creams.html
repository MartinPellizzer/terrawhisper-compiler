Diabetics with Reddened Mouth Corners: 7 Herbal Creams for Soothing

Diabetics often struggle with reddened corners in their mouth due to a condition called angular cheilitis.

This is caused by high blood sugar levels that make the skin around the mouth's corners crack and become inflamed. The constant discomfort and pain can make eating and drinking challenging, even leading to a decrease in appetite. This can affect their weight, energy levels, and overall well-being. When the mouth's corners are red and sore, it can be painful to smile, talk, or even eat their favorite foods. This can be frustrating and embarrassing for diabetics, making everyday tasks more difficult.

Angular cheilitis can be triggered by a variety of factors, including dry mouth, poor oral hygiene, and certain medications. Diabetics with high blood sugar levels are more susceptible to this condition. Using herbal creams can be a helpful approach to treating reddened corners in the mouth for diabetics. These creams work by reducing inflammation and promoting healing in the affected area. They often contain soothing and antimicrobial properties that help to calm the skin and prevent infection.

By applying a herbal cream to the affected area, diabetics can find relief from the discomfort and pain associated with angular cheilitis.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contain properties that make them beneficial for treating reddened corners in the mouth.

The creams have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties due to the presence of compounds such as aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamin E. These constituents help to reduce swelling and ease irritation, allowing the affected area to heal quickly. To use Aloe barbadensis creams for reddened corners in the mouth, apply a small amount to the affected area with a clean finger and gently rub it in. You can do this 2-3 times a day, after meals, to keep the area moist and promote healing.

As you apply the cream, you may notice a reduction in the redness and swelling within a few hours. The irritation and discomfort may also start to subside, making it more comfortable to eat and drink. If the reddened corners are caused by a fungal or bacterial infection, the cream's antibacterial and antifungal properties may help to combat the infection, promoting a faster recovery.

However, if the reddened corners persist or worsen, it's best to consult a doctor to rule out any underlying conditions that may require medical attention.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams can be a helpful remedy for reddened corners in the mouth, a common issue often caused by minor irritations or cold sores.

The creams contain properties like alkylamides and glycoproteins that help to reduce inflammation and fight off infections. These constituents work by boosting the immune system and increasing the production of white blood cells, which are essential for fighting off the herpes simplex virus that causes cold sores. To use Echinacea purpurea creams effectively, gently apply a thin layer to the affected area 2-3 times a day. You can also use a cotton swab to apply the cream directly to the reddened corners.

Make sure to wash your hands before and after applying the cream to avoid spreading the infection. It's also essential to maintain good oral hygiene by regularly brushing and flossing your teeth to prevent any further irritation. When using Echinacea purpurea creams, you can expect to see a reduction in redness and swelling within a few days. The creams may also help to speed up the healing process and reduce the duration of cold sore outbreaks.

However, it's essential to note that these creams are not a cure for cold sores and may not completely eliminate the symptoms.

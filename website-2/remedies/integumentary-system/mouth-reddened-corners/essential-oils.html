1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like eucalyptol, alpha-pinene, and limonene.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help reduce redness and swelling in the mouth. Eucalyptol, in particular, has been shown to have a cooling effect, which can soothe and calm irritated tissues. The antimicrobial properties of limonene and alpha-pinene can help prevent the growth of bacteria and fungi that may be contributing to the redness.

By applying a few drops of Eucalyptus globulus essential oils to a cotton swab and gently rubbing it on the affected area, you can potentially alleviate the redness and discomfort in the mouth.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a clean glass jar, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Combine 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves with 2 cups of water in a saucepan and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let simmer for 10-15 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into the glass jar.
  4. Combine the strained mixture with 1 cup of carrier oil in the glass jar. Let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  5. Strain the mixture again and store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a dark glass bottle for future use.

2. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains menthol and menthone, which are responsible for its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce redness and swelling in the corners of the mouth by fighting off underlying infections and soothing irritated tissues. The cooling effect of menthol also helps to numb the area and provide relief from pain. The essential oil's ability to reduce inflammation and kill bacteria makes it an effective natural remedy for treating mouth ulcers and other oral infections.

The antiseptic properties of menthol and menthone also help to promote healing and prevent further infection.

  1. Gather materials: fresh Mentha x piperita leaves (peppermint), carrier oil (coconut oil), a clean glass jar, a cheesecloth, and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Pick and clean 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves. Remove any stems or wilted parts.
  3. Combine the clean leaves with 2 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well to coat the leaves.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks. Shake the jar every day to help the oil extract from the leaves.
  5. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth into the dark glass bottle. Discard the solids and store the oil in a cool, dark place.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains active constituents like gingerol and shogaol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. The anti-inflammatory properties help reduce redness and swelling in the mouth. Gingerol's ability to inhibit the growth of bacteria also aids in preventing infections that can cause reddened corners in the mouth.

The antiseptic properties of shogaol further help to clean and soothe the affected area.

  1. Gather 100g of fresh Zingiber officinale roots and wash them with water.
  2. Dry the roots in a warm place for 2-3 days. Then, chop them into small pieces.
  3. Use 1 cup of chopped roots and put them in a clean glass jar. Add 2 cups of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking it every day. This will help the oil extract from the roots.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean jar. Discard the roots and store the essential oil in a dark place.

4. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains active constituents like cichoric acid, alkylamides, and caffeic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and redness in the mouth. The anti-inflammatory properties of cichoric acid and caffeic acid can also help soothe irritated tissues and promote healing. Alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea essential oils have antimicrobial properties, which can help prevent infection and promote a healthy environment for healing.

By using Echinacea purpurea essential oils topically, you may be able to reduce the appearance of reddened corners in the mouth and promote overall oral health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba), and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine the Echinacea flowers and carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well to mix.
  3. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the oil to infuse.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Echinacea essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops of the oil with a carrier oil (coconut or jojoba) for treating reddened corners in mouth.

5. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains various bioactive constituents, including aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-annone.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can help reduce redness and irritation in the mouth. Aloe-emodin, in particular, has been shown to have potent antioxidant and antimicrobial effects, which can help combat oral infections and inflammation. The anti-inflammatory properties of aloe barbadensis essential oils can also help to reduce swelling and pain associated with reddened corners in the mouth.

By incorporating aloe barbadensis essential oils into your oral care routine, you may be able to alleviate some of the discomfort and promote a healthier oral environment.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Aloe barbadensis gel, 1 cup of water, and a blender.
  2. Add 1 cup of water to the blender and blend the Aloe gel until it's smooth.
  3. Strain the mixture into a bowl to remove any pulp or fibers.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a glass bottle and add 10 drops of peppermint essential oil.
  5. Store the mixture in the refrigerator and use a few drops as needed to soothe reddened corners in the mouth.

6. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains active constituents like bornyl acetate, camphor, and pinene.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help reduce redness and swelling in the mouth. Bornyl acetate, in particular, has been shown to have a strong anti-inflammatory effect, while camphor and pinene have antimicrobial properties that can help combat infections and reduce redness. The antimicrobial properties of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can also help reduce the risk of oral infections that can cause redness in the mouth.

The anti-inflammatory properties of this essential oil can help soothe and calm the affected area, promoting a faster recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves, a glass jar, and a steam distillation kit.
  2. Wash the leaves with 2 cups of water and dry them completely.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of water to the distillation kit and heat it until steam is released.
  4. Place the leaves in the distillation kit and collect the steam for 30 minutes.
  5. Store the collected essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight lid and label it as 'Rosmarinus officinalis oil'.

7. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol and carvacrol, which are known for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce redness and inflammation in the mouth, making it an effective remedy for reddened corners. Thymol, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi, while carvacrol has anti-inflammatory effects that can soothe irritated tissues. The antiseptic properties of thymol and carvacrol also help to prevent infection, which can cause redness and irritation in the mouth.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infection, Thymus vulgaris essential oils can help to promote healing and reduce the appearance of reddened corners in the mouth.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and 2 cups of water. Clean the leaves and set aside.
  2. Combine the Thymus vulgaris leaves and water in a saucepan and heat over low heat for 2 hours.
  3. Turn off the heat and let it steep for another 2 hours. This is called maceration.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 24 hours to allow the oil to separate and rise to the top. Skim off the oil and transfer it to a dark glass bottle for storage.

8. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains a high concentration of cineole, a compound known for its antimicrobial properties.

Cineole helps to reduce inflammation and fight off bacteria that can cause reddened corners in the mouth. The oil also contains terpinen-4-ol, which has antiseptic properties that help to soothe and calm irritated tissues. When applied topically, the antiseptic properties of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils can help to reduce redness and inflammation, promoting a healthy mouth environment.

Regular use of this oil can help to prevent and treat mouth infections and promote overall oral health.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the leaves.
  4. Let the liquid sit at room temperature for 24 hours to allow the oil to separate.
  5. Collect the oil from the top layer and transfer it to a dark glass bottle for storage.

9. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains compounds like limonene, beta-pinene, and geraniol, which give it anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The limonene in Cymbopogon citratus essential oils helps to reduce redness and swelling in the mouth by suppressing the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines. The antimicrobial properties of geraniol and beta-pinene help to combat the growth of bacteria and fungi that can cause irritation and inflammation in the mouth. This makes Cymbopogon citratus essential oils an effective natural remedy for soothing and calming reddened corners in the mouth.

Regular use of Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can help to promote a healthy mouth environment and alleviate symptoms of mouth irritation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water in the pot to 180°F (82°C) and add the Cymbopogon citratus leaves.
  3. Reduce the heat to 100°F (38°C) and let it steep for 1 hour and 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container.
  5. Use a glass dropper to transfer the essential oil into a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool place.

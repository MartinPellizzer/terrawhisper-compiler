Reddened Corners in Mouth Relief for Elderly with 7 Herbal Teas

As people age, their gums and mouth can change, leading to redness and irritation in the corners of their mouth.

This is often due to dry mouth, which can be caused by a decrease in saliva production, medications, or other health conditions. The redness and irritation can be painful and make eating and drinking uncomfortable. It can also make it hard to talk and swallow, which can affect a person's ability to socialize and enjoy their favorite foods and drinks. In severe cases, it can also lead to other health problems, such as tooth decay and gum disease. Elderly people may feel self-conscious about their appearance and avoid smiling or talking in public.

This can make them feel isolated and lonely. The redness and irritation can be caused by a number of factors, including inflammation, bacterial or fungal infections, and allergic reactions. Some elderly people may also experience mouth ulcers or canker sores, which can be painful and make eating and drinking difficult. Herbal teas can be a helpful remedy for elderly people who suffer from redness and irritation in the corners of their mouth. These teas have anti-inflammatory properties and can help to soothe and calm the affected area. They can also help to reduce swelling and promote healing.

Many herbal teas are also known for their antibacterial and antifungal properties, which can help to prevent infections and promote a healthy mouth environment.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas are a popular natural remedy for soothing reddened corners in the mouth, caused by conditions such as angular cheilitis or perleche.

The reason behind its effectiveness lies in its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, primarily due to the presence of compounds like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid. These constituents help reduce swelling, fight off infections, and promote healing in the affected area. If you're experiencing reddened corners in the mouth, try drinking Echinacea purpurea tea 2-3 times a day. To make the most of its benefits, ensure you're using high-quality tea bags or loose leaves, and steep them in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

After drinking the tea, gently clean the affected area with a mild mouthwash or saline solution to remove any bacteria or debris. As you start taking Echinacea purpurea tea, you may notice a reduction in inflammation and swelling within a few days. The infection should start to clear up, and the redness should subside, revealing a smoother, healthier appearance in the affected area. You may also experience a decrease in discomfort or pain while eating or speaking.

However, if your condition persists or worsens, consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas have been traditionally used to help with reddened corners in the mouth, also known as angular cheilitis.

This condition is often caused by dryness, irritation, or infection. The roots of the licorice plant (Glycyrrhiza glabra) contain properties and constituents like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These properties can help to soothe and calm the irritated skin, reducing redness and inflammation. If you're experiencing reddened corners in the mouth, you can try drinking Glycyrrhiza glabra tea. To do this, steep one teaspoon of dried licorice root in a cup of hot water for five to seven minutes.

Strain the tea and let it cool before using it as a mouthwash. You can also apply a cotton ball soaked in the tea to the affected area. Do this a few times a day for a few days to see improvements. When you drink Glycyrrhiza glabra tea, you might notice a reduction in redness and inflammation in the corners of your mouth. Your skin might become less irritated and inflamed, and you might feel more comfortable.

However, if your symptoms persist, it's best to consult a healthcare professional for further guidance.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas are a natural remedy for soothing reddened corners in the mouth.

When you take action with Aloe barbadensis tea, you can expect the reddened corners in your mouth to start feeling better within a few days.

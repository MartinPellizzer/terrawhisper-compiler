What Are The Main Causes Of Psoriasis?

Psoriasis is a skin condition that causes red, itchy patches.

The main causes of psoriasis are genetics, injury, and stress. Genetics play a big role because people with a family history of psoriasis are more likely to develop it. This is because they may inherit genes that make their immune system more sensitive.

Injury to the skin, such as a cut or burn, can also trigger psoriasis. When the skin is injured, it can cause the immune system to go into overdrive, leading to inflammation and psoriasis patches. Stress is another cause of psoriasis. When we're under stress, our immune system releases chemicals that can make psoriasis worse.

This is because stress can cause inflammation and trigger the immune system to attack healthy skin cells.

Below are the main causes of psoriasis.

  • Genetics: Genetics play a role in causing psoriasis because people with a family history of the condition are more likely to develop it due to inherited genes that affect how their skin cells grow and behave.
  • Injury: When the body gets injured, it releases chemicals to heal the wound, which can trigger an abnormal immune response that leads to the development of psoriasis.
  • Stress: When we experience stress, our immune system becomes overactive, causing inflammation in the body, which can trigger the skin cells to grow too quickly and multiply, leading to the symptoms of psoriasis.
  • Hormonal Imbalance: Hormonal imbalance can trigger psoriasis by causing inflammation and skin cell growth problems, which leads to the buildup of dead skin cells and the characteristic red, scaly patches associated with the condition.
  • Infection: Research suggests that in some cases, an infection can trigger psoriasis by causing the body's immune system to react and become inflamed, which can then lead to the characteristic skin symptoms of psoriasis.
  • Autoimmune: In autoimmune psoriasis, the body mistakenly identifies its own skin cells as foreign and attacks them, leading to inflammation, redness, and the scaly patches that are characteristic of the condition.
  • Medications: Some medications, such as certain antibiotics and antifungals, can trigger psoriasis by causing an imbalance in the body's immune system, which can lead to the formation of red, itchy patches on the skin.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Psoriasis?

If you have psoriasis, it's essential to be cautious with certain herbs that may trigger or worsen symptoms.

One such herb is St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum), which can cause allergic reactions and interact with medications. Another herb to avoid is Echinacea (Echinacea spp. ), as it may stimulate the immune system and make psoriasis symptoms worse.

Turmeric (Curcuma longa) and Ginger (Zingiber officinale) may be too irritating for sensitive skin, and their active compounds, curcumin and gingerol, can cause skin rashes and inflammation. Feverfew (Tanacetum parthenium) and Willow bark (Salix spp.). contain salicylates, which can irritate the skin and trigger psoriasis flares. Finally, Ginkgo biloba can increase the risk of bleeding and interact with medications, which may be problematic for people with psoriasis.

These herbs should be used with caution or avoided altogether to prevent exacerbating psoriasis symptoms.

Natural Remedies for Dry Eyes: Causes, Medicinal Herbs, and Herbal Preparations

What are the causes of dry eyes?

Dry eyes can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is Blepharitis, an inflammation of the eyelids. This condition can lead to dry eyes because it causes the meibomian glands, which produce the oily layer of the tear film, to become clogged. When these glands are blocked, they can't produce enough oil, leading to dryness. Another cause is Meibomian gland dysfunction, which is similar to Blepharitis but less severe. In this case, the glands are still producing oil, but it's not the right consistency or amount. This can also cause the tear film to break down and lead to dry eyes. Wearing contact lenses can also cause dry eyes.

Contact lenses can reduce the amount of oxygen that reaches the eyes, which can cause the meibomian glands to produce less oil. This can lead to a tear film that's not as healthy as it should be. Sjogren's syndrome is an autoimmune disorder that can cause dry eyes. In this condition, the immune system attacks the glands that produce tears, leading to a decrease in tear production. This can cause dryness, itchiness, and burning in the eyes. Finally, living in a dry climate can also cause dry eyes. Dry air can evaporate the tears more quickly, leading to dryness and irritation.

People who live in areas with low humidity may need to use artificial tears more often to keep their eyes moist.

Some herbs can also help soothe and calm the eyes, reducing redness and swelling. What are the main medicinal herbs for dry eyes?

Herbs have been used for centuries to help with dry eyes, and several have proven to be effective.

Euphrasia officinalis, also known as eye herb, is particularly good for soothing and moistening dry eyes. Its extracts help reduce inflammation and irritation, making it easier for the eyes to stay moist. Calendula officinalis, or pot marigold, is another herb that helps with dry eyes.

Its anti-inflammatory properties calm the eyes and reduce redness, while its soothing properties ease dryness and discomfort. Echinacea purpurea, commonly known as coneflower, is often used to boost the immune system, but it also helps with dry eyes by reducing inflammation and promoting healing. Achillea millefolium, or yarrow, has anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that help calm and protect the eyes, reducing the risk of infection and promoting healing. Symphytum officinale, or comfrey, is a herb that helps regenerate and repair damaged eye tissues, making it ideal for dry, irritated eyes.

These herbs work together to provide relief and promote healing, making them a natural and effective solution for dry eyes.

What Are The Main Causes Of Bruises?

A bruise is a collection of blood that leaks out of blood vessels and collects under the skin, causing a discoloration.

The main causes of bruises are: Trauma, which means a physical injury or accident, is the most common cause of bruises. When we fall, hit something, or get hit by something, blood vessels under the skin can get damaged, causing blood to leak out and form a bruise. Vitamin K deficiency is another cause of bruises.

Vitamin K is a vitamin that helps the blood to clot, which means it helps to stop bleeding. If we don't have enough vitamin K in our body, our blood won't clot properly, and we may get bruises easily. Bleeding disorders, such as hemophilia, are also a cause of bruises. In bleeding disorders, the blood doesn't clot properly, so even small injuries can cause blood to leak out and form bruises.

This can happen because the blood doesn't have the right proteins to help it clot, or because the blood vessels are weak and easily damaged.

Below are the main causes of bruises.

  • Trauma: When the body experiences a severe injury or impact due to trauma, it causes blood vessels to break and leak, leading to the formation of bruises on the skin.
  • Vitamin K deficiency: Vitamin K helps the blood to clot and heal, so when there's not enough of it, the body has trouble stopping bleeding, leading to easy bruising.
  • Bleeding disorders: Bleeding disorders cause bruises because the blood does not clot properly, leading to uncontrolled bleeding under the skin that forms a blue or purple mark, known as a bruise.
  • Aging: As we age, our skin loses collagen, a protein that helps keep blood vessels strong, so when we fall or bump into something, the weakened vessels break easily and cause bruising.
  • Hypertension: Hypertension is a cause of bruises because the high blood pressure can cause tiny blood vessels under the skin to break, leading to bleeding and bruising.
  • Liver disease: Liver disease can cause bruises because the liver is not able to remove waste products from the blood properly, leading to a buildup of toxins that can cause blood vessels to break easily.

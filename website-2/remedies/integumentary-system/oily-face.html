What Are The Main Causes Of Oily Face?

There are several main causes of an oily face.

One cause is Hormonal Imbalance. During puberty, the body's hormone levels change, causing the oil glands in the skin to produce more oil. This excess oil can lead to an oily face. Hormonal changes can also occur during menstruation, pregnancy, or menopause, affecting the skin's oil production. Another cause is Genetic Predisposition.

If your parents have oily skin, you're more likely to have it too. This is because genetics play a role in how your skin's oil glands work. If your skin is more prone to producing oil, it can be harder to control. Lastly, Overactive Sebaceous Glands are a common cause of an oily face. The sebaceous glands produce oil to keep the skin moisturized and healthy. However, if these glands become overactive, they produce too much oil, leading to an oily face.

This can be due to a variety of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, and even certain skin care products.

Below are the main causes of oily face.

  • Hormonal Imbalance: Hormonal imbalance causes an oily face because of an increase in androgen hormones, which stimulate the oil glands in the skin to produce more sebum, leading to excessive oiliness and acne.
  • Genetic Predisposition: Genetic predisposition is a cause of oily face because some people are born with their skin's natural oil production being overactive, which can lead to excessive oil secretion on the face.
  • Overactive Sebaceous Glands: Overactive sebaceous glands produce more oil than needed, causing an oily face due to the excess sebum that they secrete, which can lead to clogged pores and a shiny appearance.
  • Birth Control Pills: Birth control pills can cause oily face because they increase the production of androgens in the body, which stimulate the oil glands in the skin to produce more sebum, making the face look oily.
  • Pregnancy: During pregnancy, hormonal changes, particularly the increase in estrogen and progesterone levels, cause the body's sebaceous glands to produce more sebum, leading to an oily face.
  • Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS): PCOS can cause an oily face because the high levels of androgens (male hormones) in the body, often found in women with PCOS, stimulate the oil glands in the skin to produce excess sebum, leading to an oily complexion.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Oily Face?

If you have an oily face, you should avoid certain herbs that can make the problem worse.

One such herb is 'Citrus aurantium', commonly known as bitter orange. It has a stimulating effect on the skin, which can increase oil production, making your face even oilier.

Another herb to avoid is 'Sassafras albidum', which is known for its ability to stimulate blood flow and increase oil production. 'Cinchona officinalis', or Peruvian bark, is also a problem, as it has a drying effect on the skin, which can cause the oil glands to produce more oil in an attempt to compensate.

'Juniperus communis', or juniper, is another herb to steer clear of, as it has a stimulating effect on the skin that can increase oil production and make your face look greasier.

What Are The Main Causes Of Nose Bleeding?

Nose bleeding, also known as epistaxis, can be caused by several factors.

One of the main causes is trauma, which refers to any injury to the nose. This can be due to a direct blow to the nose, a fall, or even a sports-related injury. When the nose is injured, the blood vessels inside the nasal passages can become damaged, leading to bleeding.

Hypertension, or high blood pressure, is another cause of nose bleeding. High blood pressure can cause the blood vessels in the nose to weaken, making them more prone to rupture and bleeding. Nose picking, or picking at the nose, is also a common cause of nose bleeding. When you pick at your nose, you can accidentally damage the blood vessels or push them closer to the surface of the skin, making them more likely to bleed.

This is especially true if you have a cold or allergies and your nose is already irritated and swollen.

Below are the main causes of nose bleeding.

  • Trauma: Trauma is a cause of nose bleeding because a strong blow to the face can damage the tiny blood vessels inside the nose, causing them to break and bleed.
  • Hypertension: When the blood pressure in your body is too high, it can cause small blood vessels in your nose to burst, leading to nose bleeding.
  • Nose Picking: Nose picking can cause nose bleeding when the thin skin inside the nostrils gets scratched or irritated, leading to tiny blood vessels breaking and bleeding.
  • Sinus Infection: A sinus infection can cause the blood vessels in the nose to become inflamed and swollen, leading to tiny blood vessels bursting and causing nosebleeds.
  • Allergies: When allergies make the nasal passages swell and produce excess mucus, the pressure can cause tiny blood vessels in the nose to break, leading to nose bleeding.
  • High Blood Pressure: High blood pressure can cause tiny blood vessels in the nose to burst, leading to nose bleeding due to the increased pressure and strain on these delicate vessels.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Nose Bleeding?

If you have nosebleeds, there are some herbs you should avoid.

Feverfew (Tanacetum parthenium) is one to watch out for. Its active compound, parthenolide, can thin blood and make bleeding worse. Another herb to be cautious with is Garlic (Allium sativum), which has properties that can thin blood and increase bleeding risk.

Willow bark (Salix alba) contains salicin, a compound similar to aspirin, which can also thin blood. Sage (Salvia officinalis) and Hyssop (Hyssopus officinalis) contain compounds that may affect blood clotting. St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) can interact with other medications and worsen bleeding problems. Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) can increase blood pressure and worsen bleeding.

If you have nosebleeds, consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies, especially these ones.

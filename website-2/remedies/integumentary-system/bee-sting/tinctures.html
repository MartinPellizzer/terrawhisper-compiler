1. Arnica montana

Arnica montana tinctures contains sesquiterpene lactones, specifically helenalin and 11,13-dihydrohelenalin, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and redness caused by bee stings. The tincture also contains flavonoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, which have antihistamine and anti-inflammatory effects, further reducing the sting's symptoms. Arnica's sesquiterpene lactones also have analgesic properties, helping to alleviate pain and discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and pain, Arnica montana tinctures can help alleviate the symptoms associated with bee stings.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Arnica montana flowers and 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the Arnica flowers to the jar and pour the vodka over them, leaving 1 inch at the top.
  3. Seal the jar and shake it well for 5-7 days, 2-3 times a day, to help the ingredients mix.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 5-10 drops on the affected area, 2-3 times a day, to help soothe bee stings.

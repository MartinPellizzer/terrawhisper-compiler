1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are responsible for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce redness and swelling in the eyes by decreasing blood flow to the area. The anti-inflammatory properties of calendula also reduce the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to red eyes. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of carotenoids in calendula protect the delicate skin around the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

By applying calendula creams to the affected area, you can help to alleviate red eyes and promote a healthy, calm appearance.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Dry the Calendula officinalis flowers in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 2 hours. Let them cool.
  3. Melt the coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler. Stir until smooth.
  4. Add the dried Calendula officinalis flowers and vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool. Once set, your Calendula officinalis cream is ready to use. Apply a small amount to affected area to soothe red eyes.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents like alkylamides, glycoproteins, and volatile oils that help soothe and calm red eyes.

Alkylamides have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and redness in the eyes, making them feel more comfortable. Glycoproteins have antioxidant properties that protect the delicate skin around the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. Volatile oils in Echinacea purpurea creams have a cooling effect that can help reduce the appearance of redness and puffiness.

By using Echinacea purpurea creams, people can find relief from red, irritated eyes due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 10 drops of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the melted mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl.
  4. Add shea butter and vitamin E oil to the strained mixture. Stir until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, your Echinacea purpurea cream is ready to use.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains a number of bioactive constituents, including aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A, C, and E. These compounds help to reduce redness and inflammation in the eyes by soothing and calming the skin.

The anti-inflammatory properties of aloe-emodin and aloin help to reduce swelling and redness, while the vitamins A, C, and E promote healing and protect the skin from damage. The gel in aloe vera also has a cooling effect, which can help to reduce puffiness and ease irritation.

The combination of these properties makes aloe barbadensis creams a good option for soothing and calming red eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of aloe vera gel, and 2 tablespoons of coconut oil.
  2. In a small saucepan, heat the distilled water over low heat.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the aloe vera gel and coconut oil until well combined.
  4. Slowly pour the warm distilled water into the aloe vera mixture, stirring constantly.
  5. Pour the mixture into a small container and let it cool before applying to red eyes.

4. Chamomilla recutita

Chamomilla recutita creams contains apigenin and luteolin, two powerful flavonoids that help reduce puffiness and swelling around the eyes.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which soothe red, irritated eyes and promote relaxation. Chamazulene, another key constituent, has potent antioxidant effects that shield the delicate skin around the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. By reducing inflammation and protecting the skin, Chamomilla recutita creams help to alleviate red, tired-looking eyes and promote a more refreshed appearance.

The soothing properties of these constituents work together to calm and comfort the skin, providing quick relief from red, irritated eyes.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 1 teaspoon of dried Chamomilla recutita, and 2 tablespoons of distilled water.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add shea butter and stir until melted and smooth.
  4. Remove from heat and add dried Chamomilla recutita, let it steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain and discard the herbs.
  5. Let the mixture cool and thicken, then whip in distilled water to create a smooth cream. Pour into a jar and store in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

5. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum creams contains bioactive constituents like hyperforin and hypericin, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and redness in the eyes, making Hypericum perforatum creams a good option for soothing red eyes. The creams also contain flavonoids, which have antioxidant effects, protecting the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. The anti-inflammatory properties of Hypericum perforatum creams also help to reduce puffiness and irritation in the eyes.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the eyes from damage, Hypericum perforatum creams can help to alleviate red eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Hypericum perforatum flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Steep the dried flowers in the carrier oil in a clean glass jar for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter to the oil mixture and heat it in a double boiler until the wax and butter melt.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool. Store the Hypericum perforatum cream in a cool, dark place.

6. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale creams contains a combination of active constituents like allantoin, mucilage, and saponins.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that help reduce redness and swelling in the eyes. Allantoin, in particular, is known for its ability to promote cell turnover and heal damaged skin, making it effective in calming irritated eyes. The mucilage in Symphytum officinale creams forms a protective barrier on the skin's surface, shielding it from further irritation and allowing it to recover.

By providing a soothing and protective environment, Symphytum officinale creams help alleviate red eyes caused by various factors, including allergies, dryness, and minor irritation.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Symphytum officinale tincture, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 drops of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt beeswax and coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add Symphytum officinale tincture and shea butter to the melted beeswax and coconut oil. Stir until well combined.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool for 5-7 minutes. Stir in vitamin E oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, your Symphytum officinale cream is ready to use for red eyes.

Euphrasia officinalis creams contains bioactive constituents like rutin, quercetin, and leucoside A, which are powerful antioxidants.

Rutin, in particular, is known to improve blood vessel function and reduce capillary permeability, which can contribute to red eye symptoms. Quercetin has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to soothe and calm the eye tissue, reducing redness and discomfort.

By applying Euphrasia officinalis creams to the affected area, you may be able to alleviate the symptoms of red eyes and promote a more comfortable and healthy eye environment.

  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 10-15 minutes, or until the mixture cools to room temperature.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.

8. Hamamelis virginiana

Hamamelis virginiana creams contains a high concentration of bioactive compounds like hamamelitannin and gallic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and redness in the eyes. The astringent properties of hamamelitannin can also help constrict blood vessels, further reducing the appearance of redness. The antioxidant properties of gallic acid can help protect the delicate skin around the eyes from oxidative damage, promoting a healthy and calm appearance.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the skin, Hamamelis virginiana creams can help alleviate the symptoms of red eyes.

  2. Combine the Hamamelis virginiana flowers and distilled water in a saucepan. Heat over low heat for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and let steep for 30 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, and 2 tablespoons of the Hamamelis virginiana liquid.
  5. Melt the mixture in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments until the beeswax is fully melted. Pour into a container and let cool.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and ease irritation in the eyes. The flavonoids in Echinacea, such as kaempferol and quercetin, also have antioxidant properties, protecting the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. The rosmarinic acid in Echinacea purpurea tinctures has vasoconstrictive properties, which can help reduce blood flow to the eyes, easing redness.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the eyes from damage, Echinacea purpurea tinctures can help alleviate red eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Fill a cup with 80% glycerin and pour it into the jar with Echinacea flowers.
  3. Close the jar and shake it for 5 minutes to mix the flowers and liquids.
  4. Place the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2 weeks, shaking it daily.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into another glass jar, and discard the flowers. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

2. Euphrasia officinalis

Euphrasia officinalis tinctures contains flavonoids and iridoid glycosides as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce swelling and repair damaged tissues. The flavonoids in Euphrasia officinalis tinctures, such as rutin and quercetin, have a strong ability to improve blood vessel function, which can help to reduce congestion and redness in the eyes. The iridoid glycosides, like aucubin, also have a soothing effect on the eyes, which can help to calm irritation and reduce inflammation.

By using Euphrasia officinalis tinctures, you may be able to alleviate the symptoms of red eyes caused by allergies or other irritants.

  1. Gather 250ml of 80% vodka and 250g of fresh Euphrasia officinalis flowers.
  2. Combine the Euphrasia officinalis flowers and vodka in a clean glass jar, making sure the flowers are fully covered.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After the steeping period, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Label the jar with the date and the contents, and store the Euphrasia officinalis tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops in water as needed to help soothe red eyes.

3. Calendula officinalis

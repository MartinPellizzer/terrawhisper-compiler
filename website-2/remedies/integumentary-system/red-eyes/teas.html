Red eyes in the cockpit: 8 herbal teas for pilots

Pilots often spend long hours in the cockpit, staring at screens and dealing with changing light conditions, which can cause strain on their eyes.

This can lead to red, itchy, and tired eyes, making it hard for them to focus and perform their duties safely. When pilots have red eyes, it's not just a minor annoyance - it can actually affect their ability to fly. They may have trouble seeing important instruments, and their reaction times may be slower, which is a serious concern for anyone responsible for the safety of passengers and crew. As you can imagine, having red eyes can be a real problem for pilots. They may have to cancel flights or adjust their schedules, which can be costly and inconvenient for airlines.

It can also be stressful for pilots, who may feel like they're not doing their job properly. Red eyes can even lead to fatigue and burnout, which can have long-term consequences for a pilot's career. So, what can pilots do to treat red eyes?. One effective solution is to drink herbal teas that have been shown to soothe and calm the eyes. These teas are made from plants that have natural anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling and redness in the eyes. They can also help to relax the muscles around the eyes, making it easier to focus and feel more comfortable.

By drinking these teas, pilots can help to reduce their eye strain and stay safe on the job.

1. Euphrasia officinalis

Euphrasia officinalis teas are a natural remedy for red eyes, and they work by targeting the underlying causes of the condition.

The tea contains properties and constituents like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which help to reduce inflammation and combat infections in the eyes. These compounds have a soothing effect on the eyes, calming irritated tissues and preventing further irritation. The flavonoids, in particular, have a strong antioxidant effect, which helps to neutralize free radicals and protect the delicate tissues of the eyes. If you're experiencing red eyes, try making a tea by steeping dried Euphrasia officinalis leaves in hot water.

Let the tea cool down, then gently apply it to a clean cloth or cotton ball and place it over your closed eyes. You can also add a few drops of the tea to your eyes directly, but be careful not to touch the surface of the eye with the dropper. Repeat this process several times a day, and you may start to see improvements in your eye condition within a few hours. As the tea works to reduce inflammation and combat infections, you can expect to see a decrease in redness and irritation in your eyes. The tea may also help to clear up any discharge or pus that's accumulated in the eyes, promoting a faster recovery.

Additionally, the antioxidants in the tea may help to prevent further damage to the delicate tissues of the eyes, reducing the risk of long-term complications.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas have been used for centuries to help soothe and calm red eyes.

The reason for this is due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The tea contains constituents like triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which help to reduce swelling and combat bacteria that can cause infection. When you use Calendula officinalis tea to treat red eyes, you can try the following: soak a clean cloth in the cooled tea, and place it over your closed eyes for 5-10 minutes.

Alternatively, you can use the tea as an eye wash by dipping a clean cloth or cotton ball in the tea and gently wiping your eyes. When you take these actions, you can expect the following to happen: the anti-inflammatory properties will help reduce the swelling and redness in your eyes, while the antimicrobial properties will combat any underlying infection. You may also notice an improvement in your eye comfort and a reduction in the sensation of grittiness or itchiness.

However, it's essential to note that if your red eyes persist or are accompanied by other symptoms, such as discharge or vision changes, you should consult a healthcare professional for proper evaluation and treatment.

3. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium teas, also known as yarrow tea, have been used for centuries to help alleviate red and puffy eyes.

The reason it's effective is due to its anti-inflammatory properties, particularly from its flavonoids and terpenoids. These compounds help reduce swelling and ease pain in the eyes. Yarrow tea also contains antioxidants like apigenin and luteolin, which can help calm and soothe the eyes. To use yarrow tea for red eyes, try steeping a teaspoon of dried yarrow flowers in a cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes.

Let the tea cool down, then use it as an eye wash. Gently pour the tea over a clean cloth and place it over your closed eyes for 5-10 minutes. You can also add a few drops of yarrow essential oil to the tea for extra potency. When you take action with yarrow tea, you can expect the redness and puffiness in your eyes to subside within a few hours. The anti-inflammatory compounds will help reduce the swelling, and the antioxidants will calm the eye tissue. You may also notice an improvement in your vision clarity and a reduction in eye fatigue.

However, if your eye redness persists or is accompanied by severe pain or vision loss, consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

Eucalyptus globulus teas are known to help with red eyes due to their decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties.

6. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale teas, also known as comfrey tea, may help with red eyes due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The tea contains compounds like allantoin, rosmarinic acid, and triterpenoid saponins, which can reduce swelling and soothe irritated tissues. These constituents can help calm the blood vessels in the eyes, reducing redness and discomfort.

To use Symphytum officinale teas for red eyes, steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried comfrey leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it to a clean cotton pad or cloth, which can be gently placed over the affected eye. The tea may help reduce redness and promote healing within 15-30 minutes.

However, if the redness persists or is accompanied by other symptoms like discharge, vision changes, or severe pain, it's recommended to consult a healthcare professional for proper evaluation and treatment.

7. Ruta graveolens

Ruta graveolens teas are known to help with red eyes due to their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The tea contains constituents like rutin, quercetin, and kaempferol, which help reduce swelling and soothe irritated eyes. As you try this remedy, you may notice a decrease in redness and swelling in your eyes. If you have persistent or severe eye problems, however, it's essential to consult with an eye care professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

8. Verbascum thapsus

Verbascum thapsus teas, made from the flowers of the mullein plant, have been traditionally used to help soothe and calm red, irritated eyes.

The tea's active properties, including mucilage, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, work together to reduce inflammation and ease discomfort. Mucilage, a thick, protective compound, forms a soothing barrier on the surface of the eyes, while flavonoids and phenolic acids have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help combat oxidative stress and promote healing.

To use Verbascum thapsus tea for red eyes, steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried flowers in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and let it cool. You can apply a cooled compress or a cotton ball soaked in the tea directly to the affected eye.

Some people may experience quick relief from redness and itching, while others may notice a gradual decrease in inflammation and eye sensitivity over the course of a few hours.

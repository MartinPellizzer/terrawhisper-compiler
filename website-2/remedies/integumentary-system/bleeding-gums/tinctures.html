1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds, such as alkylamides and polysaccharides, help reduce swelling and inflammation in the gums. The flavonoids, including kaempferol and quercetin, have antioxidant properties that combat gum inflammation and promote healing. The phenolic acids, like caffeic and ferulic acids, also have antimicrobial properties that prevent gum infections.

The combination of these active constituents in Echinacea purpurea tinctures helps to soothe and heal bleeding gums.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea roots, 2 cups of 80% vodka, and a glass jar.
  2. Combine the dried Echinacea purpurea roots and vodka in the glass jar. Stir well to mix.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Echinacea purpurea tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20 drops, 3 times a day, for bleeding gums.

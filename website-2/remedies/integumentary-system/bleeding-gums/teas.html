1. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis teas contains tannins, flavonoids, and rosmarinic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The tannins in Salvia officinalis help to reduce inflammation and prevent the growth of bacteria, which can cause bleeding gums. The flavonoids, particularly kaempferol and quercetin, have antioxidant properties that protect the gums from damage caused by free radicals. Rosmarinic acid, a polyphenol, also has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that help to soothe and heal bleeding gums.

By consuming Salvia officinalis teas, individuals can potentially reduce gum inflammation and promote overall gum health.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Salvia officinalis leaves.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Add the 2 tablespoons of dried Salvia officinalis leaves to the boiling water.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it warm 2-3 times a day for relief from bleeding gums.

Diabetic Individuals with Bleeding Gums: 10 Herbal Teas to Try

People with diabetes often have trouble with bleeding gums, a condition that can be quite painful and affect their daily lives.

This is because diabetes affects the body's ability to fight off infections, including gum infections. When bacteria in the mouth multiply quickly and cause inflammation in the gums, it can lead to bleeding. If left untreated, it can also cause tooth loss and other serious problems. For diabetics, bleeding gums can be embarrassing, and it can also be a sign of an underlying issue that needs to be addressed. They might experience discomfort while eating, speaking, and even smiling, which can affect their self-confidence and overall well-being.

Diabetes can also increase the risk of other oral health issues, such as dry mouth, tooth decay, and bad breath. This is why it's essential for diabetics to take good care of their teeth and gums by brushing regularly, flossing, and visiting their dentist regularly. However, some people might not be able to get the treatment they need due to various reasons. This is where herbal teas come in – they can help soothe and calm the gums, reducing inflammation and bleeding. Herbal teas have anti-inflammatory properties that can help combat gum infections and promote healing.

They can also help to reduce the risk of other oral health issues, making them a great addition to a diabetic's oral care routine.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas are a natural remedy that may help with bleeding gums.

The plant contains compounds like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These properties help to reduce swelling and fight off infections in the gums. The plant's constituents, such as echinacoside and cichoric acid, also have antimicrobial properties that can help to combat bacterial growth in the mouth, which can contribute to bleeding gums. If you're experiencing bleeding gums, try drinking Echinacea purpurea tea 2-3 times a day.

You can also use a mouthwash or gargle with the tea to help soothe and clean your gums. To make the tea, steep 1 teaspoon of dried Echinacea purpurea root or flowers in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool before drinking. Drinking Echinacea purpurea tea regularly may help to reduce inflammation and bleeding in the gums. You may notice a decrease in gum inflammation, a reduction in bleeding, and an overall improvement in oral health.

However, if your bleeding gums persist or worsen, consult your dentist or healthcare provider for proper diagnosis and treatment.

2. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis teas, also known as sage tea, have been used for centuries to help alleviate bleeding gums.

The tea's active compounds, such as rosmarinic acid, triterpenoids, and flavonoids, have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce swelling and fight off bacteria in the gums. These properties also help to tighten the blood vessels, reducing the flow of blood to the affected area and thereby reducing bleeding. To use Salvia officinalis teas for bleeding gums, make a strong tea by steeping 1 teaspoon of dried sage leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and let it cool before rinsing your mouth with it. For best results, repeat this process 2-3 times a day. As you use the tea, you may notice a decrease in bleeding and swelling within a few days. The gums may start to feel healthier and less sensitive, and the tea's antibacterial properties may help prevent further infections.

Regular consumption of Salvia officinalis teas may also help to reduce the risk of gum disease and promote overall oral health.

3. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis teas, like green tea, black tea, and oolong tea, are made from the leaves of the Camellia sinensis plant.

They contain properties and constituents that help with bleeding gums, such as flavonoids, tannins, and catechins. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help reduce gum inflammation and fight off bacteria that can cause gum infections. When it comes to bleeding gums, Camellia sinensis teas can be a useful addition to your oral care routine. To use them effectively, try rinsing your mouth with a cooled tea solution after brushing your teeth, or drink a cup of tea after meals to help reduce plaque and bacteria buildup.

You can also use a tea bag as a mouthwash, steeping it in boiling water and then letting it cool before applying it to the affected area. As you start using Camellia sinensis teas for bleeding gums, you may notice a reduction in bleeding and inflammation. Your gums may become healthier and less red, and you might experience a decrease in bad breath. Some people may also find that their teeth become cleaner and less sensitive over time. However, it's essential to note that these teas are not a replacement for regular oral care, but rather a complementary treatment that can be used in conjunction with brushing, flossing, and dental check-ups.

Regular use of Camellia sinensis teas may require some time to show noticeable effects, so be patient and consistent with your oral care routine.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas are known to help with bleeding gums due to their anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

The tea contains compounds like aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A, C, and E, which work together to reduce inflammation and fight off bacteria that can cause gum infections. When you consume Aloe barbadensis tea, the antioxidants in the tea help to soothe and calm the gums, reducing swelling and bleeding. To use Aloe barbadensis tea for bleeding gums, start by rinsing your mouth with the tea for 30 seconds to 1 minute, several times a day.

You can also chew on an aloe vera leaf or use a toothpaste containing aloe vera to help reduce inflammation and kill bacteria. As you continue to use Aloe barbadensis tea for bleeding gums, you can expect to see a reduction in bleeding and inflammation. The tea may also help to reduce plaque and prevent tooth decay.

Your gums will start to feel healthier and look pinker, rather than red and swollen.

Melaleuca alternifolia teas, also known as tea tree oil tea, has been traditionally used to help with bleeding gums due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

7. Myrrhis odorata

Myrrhis odorata teas have been traditionally used to help with bleeding gums due to their anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

The tea contains compounds like sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which help reduce swelling and fight off infections in the gums. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day for a few days to see improvement. You can also add a few drops of Myrrhis odorata essential oil to your toothpaste or mouthwash to further help reduce inflammation and prevent bleeding. As you continue to use Myrrhis odorata tea and its products, you may notice a reduction in bleeding gums within a few days. The inflammation in your gums should also decrease, making it more comfortable to eat and speak.

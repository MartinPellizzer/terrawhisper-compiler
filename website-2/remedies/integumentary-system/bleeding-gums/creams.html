1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are powerful antioxidants that help reduce inflammation in the gums.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, making them effective in soothing and protecting bleeding gums. The flavonoids in Calendula officinalis creams, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to reduce oxidative stress and prevent tissue damage. The saponins in the cream help to break down and remove bacteria that can cause bleeding gums, while the carotenoids promote tissue repair and healing.

Regular use of Calendula officinalis creams can help to reduce bleeding and inflammation in the gums, promoting healthy gum tissue and a reduced risk of gum disease.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula officinalis flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a saucepan over low heat until it's warm and melted.
  3. Add the dried Calendula officinalis flowers to the melted coconut oil and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the flowers.
  5. Combine the strained mixture with 1/4 cup of beeswax and stir until the beeswax is fully melted and mixed in. Pour into a jar and let cool.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains compounds like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and flavonoids.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in bleeding gums. Alkylamides help to modulate the immune system, reducing inflammation and promoting healing in the gums.

By reducing inflammation and fighting infections, Echinacea purpurea creams may help to soothe and heal bleeding gums.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of essential oil (optional).
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the melted oil and beeswax mixture. Let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl. Discard the solids and let the liquid cool slightly.
  5. Add the vitamin E oil and essential oil (if using) to the cooled liquid. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify before use.

3. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis creams contains tannins, rosmarinic acid, and ursolic acid as its key bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help reduce swelling and fight infection in the gums. Tannins, in particular, help contract blood vessels and reduce bleeding by constricting the blood vessels. Rosmarinic acid, on the other hand, has antioxidant properties that help protect the gums from damage caused by free radicals.

The combination of these properties makes Salvia officinalis creams a potential remedy for bleeding gums, helping to soothe and heal the affected area.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Salvia officinalis leaves, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 teaspoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Combine dried Salvia officinalis leaves and coconut oil in a saucepan. Heat over low heat for 30 minutes, then let it cool.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. In a separate saucepan, melt the beeswax and shea butter over low heat. Add the strained liquid and vitamin E oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once set, your Salvia officinalis cream is ready to use for bleeding gums.

5. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains bioactive constituents such as aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains compounds called cineole and terpinen-4-ol.

These oils have antibacterial properties, which help reduce the amount of bacteria on the skin that can cause pimples. The creams also contain anti-inflammatory compounds, such as flavonoids, which reduce redness and swelling associated with pimples. The terpinen-4-ol in Melaleuca alternifolia creams has antiseptic properties, which help prevent infection and promote healing of the skin.

By reducing bacteria and inflammation, Melaleuca alternifolia creams can help to clear up pimples and prevent new ones from forming.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil, and a small saucepan.
  2. Melt the beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter in the saucepan over low heat, stirring until smooth.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool for 5 minutes.
  4. Add the distilled water and Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil to the saucepan, stirring until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let it cool and solidify before applying to pimples.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan, which have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

These properties help reduce redness and swelling associated with pimples. The anti-inflammatory properties of aloin and aloe-emodin also reduce the size and severity of pimples. The antibacterial properties of acemannan and other compounds in aloe vera help prevent bacterial infections that can cause pimples.

This combination of anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties makes aloe barbadensis creams a helpful treatment for pimples.

  1. Gather 1 cup of aloe vera gel, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 1 tablespoon of beeswax, 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil, and 10 drops of tea tree oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Once melted, remove from heat and let cool slightly. Stir in the aloe vera gel and vitamin E oil until well combined.
  4. Add the tea tree oil and stir well. Pour the mixture into a small bowl or container.
  5. Let the mixture cool and thicken at room temperature. Once thickened, it's ready to use as a pimple cream.

3. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoids, saponins, and flavonoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help to reduce redness and prevent infection in pimples. The saponins in Calendula officinalis creams also have a soothing effect on the skin, which can help to calm inflammation and promote healing. By reducing inflammation and preventing infection, Calendula officinalis creams can help to clear up pimples and promote healthy skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Dry the Calendula flowers in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 2 hours to remove moisture.
  3. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second intervals, stirring between each interval.
  4. Combine the melted oil mixture, dried Calendula flowers, shea butter, and vitamin E oil in a blender or food processor. Blend until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container, let it cool, and solidify. Store the Calendula cream in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

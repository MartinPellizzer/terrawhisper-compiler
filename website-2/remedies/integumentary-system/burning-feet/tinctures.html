1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains gingerols and shogaols, compounds that are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, which help to soothe and relieve burning sensations in the feet. The warming properties of ginger, caused by the presence of gingerols, can increase blood flow and reduce pain in the feet. Shogaols, on the other hand, have antioxidant properties that can help protect against free radical damage and promote healing.

By using Zingiber officinale tinctures, people with burning feet may experience relief from pain and discomfort due to its ability to reduce inflammation and promote circulation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger root, 2 cups of 80% vodka, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Chop the ginger root into small pieces and put them into the glass jar.
  3. Pour the vodka over the ginger root, making sure they are fully covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.

2. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum tinctures contains hyperforin and hypericin as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that help to soothe and calm burning feet. The antiseptic properties of hyperforin and hypericin can reduce inflammation and prevent infections that may be causing the burning sensation. The anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds can also help to reduce pain and discomfort associated with burning feet.

Regular use of Hypericum perforatum tinctures may help to alleviate burning feet by reducing inflammation and promoting healing.

  1. Gather 250g of dried Hypericum perforatum flowers and 500ml of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the dried flowers and vodka in the jar, making sure the flowers are completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the tincture to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 10-15 drops of the tincture in warm water for relief from burning feet.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis tinctures contains aloe-emodin, aloin, and other bioactive compounds.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that help to reduce swelling and pain in the feet. The anthraquinones in aloe tinctures, such as aloe-emodin and aloin, have been shown to have potent anti-inflammatory effects, reducing redness and discomfort. Additionally, the polysaccharides in aloe tinctures help to promote skin healing and reduce itching and irritation.

By applying aloe barbadensis tinctures to burning feet, the bioactive compounds can help to provide relief from discomfort and promote healthy skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Aloe barbadensis gel, 2 cups of vodka, and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Cut open the Aloe barbadensis leaf and scoop out the gel. Place the gel into the glass jar.
  3. Add 2 cups of vodka to the jar with the Aloe gel. Stir well to combine.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another jar. Discard the solids. Store the tincture in the refrigerator for up to 6 months.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and discomfort in burning feet. Glycyrrhizin, a key component of Glycyrrhiza glabra, has been shown to have a soothing effect on the skin, reducing irritation and inflammation. The saponins in the tincture can also help to cool and calm the skin, providing relief from burning sensations.

By reducing inflammation and providing a cooling effect, Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures can help to alleviate the discomfort associated with burning feet.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, 2 cups of 80% ethanol, and a clean glass jar.

5. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis tinctures contains alkaloids like quinine and cinchonine.

These compounds help reduce inflammation and ease pain. The antiseptic and astringent properties of quinine and cinchonine also help fight fungal infections that can cause burning feet. Additionally, the tannins present in Cinchona officinalis have a drying effect, which can help reduce moisture and prevent fungal overgrowth.

The combination of these properties makes Cinchona officinalis tinctures a potential remedy for burning feet.

  1. Gather 250g of Cinchona officinalis bark and 750ml of 80% ethanol (vodka or rum also work). Clean and dry the workspace.
  2. Chop the Cinchona bark into small pieces and put them in a clean glass jar. Add 250ml of boiling water to cover the bark. Let it steep for 2 hours, then strain the liquid.
  3. Combine the strained liquid with 750ml of 80% ethanol in a clean glass jar. Stir well to mix. Make sure the bark pieces are fully submerged.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This is called 'maceration'.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Discard the bark pieces. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture in a glass of water for relief from burning feet.

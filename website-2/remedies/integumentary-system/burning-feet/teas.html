7 teas to help runners with burning feet

Runners often experience burning feet, a common issue that can disrupt their lives.

When you run, your feet are subjected to intense pressure, friction, and heat, which can cause damage to the skin, especially on the soles. This can lead to pain, discomfort, and even injury. Runners may feel like they're walking on hot coals, making it hard to enjoy their favorite activity. Burning feet can also affect their daily lives, making it difficult to wear shoes or even walk short distances. Some runners may even have to stop running altogether due to the pain. This can be frustrating and demotivating.

To add to the problem, running in hot weather or with poor-fitting shoes can make burning feet worse. Even minor issues like bunions or ingrown toenails can cause more pain. Runners may try various remedies, such as changing their running shoes or using foot creams, but these solutions may not provide long-term relief. Some runners may even resort to painkillers or ice packs, which can have side effects. Herbal teas have been used by runners to help alleviate burning feet. These teas are made from plants that have natural anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. They can help reduce pain, inflammation, and discomfort, promoting healing and relaxation.

By drinking these teas, runners can enjoy the benefits of a natural remedy that's gentle on their skin and easy to use.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, made from the root of the licorice plant, can help with burning feet due to its anti-inflammatory properties and high levels of glycyrrhizin, a compound that helps soothe and calm irritated skin.

The roots of the licorice plant contain flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids, which work together to reduce swelling, ease pain, and promote healing. When taken as a tea, these compounds can penetrate deep into the skin, reducing inflammation and discomfort associated with burning feet.

To try Glycyrrhiza glabra teas for burning feet, start by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Drink 1-2 cups of the tea per day, ideally after a warm bath or shower to help the skin absorb the active compounds. You may notice a reduction in burning and discomfort within a few days, and the inflammation should subside as the tea works to calm and soothe the skin.

As the tea reduces inflammation, you may also notice an improvement in the overall health of your skin, making it look smoother and more radiant.

2. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium teas, also known as yarrow tea, may help with burning feet due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The tea contains constituents like flavonoids, sesquiterpenes, and volatile oils, which work together to soothe and protect the skin. The flavonoids, in particular, have been shown to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing, which can help to alleviate burning and discomfort in the feet. The sesquiterpenes, on the other hand, have antimicrobial properties that can help to prevent infections and promote a healthy environment for the skin to heal. If you're experiencing burning feet, you can try making a yarrow tea by steeping dried Achillea millefolium flowers in hot water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and soak your feet in it for 15-20 minutes, 2-3 times a day.

You can also add a few drops of yarrow essential oil to your bath water for a relaxing soak. To enhance the benefits, you can also try applying a topical cream or ointment containing yarrow extract to the affected area. As you start to use Achillea millefolium teas for burning feet, you may notice a reduction in discomfort and inflammation. Your skin may start to feel cooler and more comfortable, and you may experience improved wound healing and reduced risk of infection. You may also notice an improvement in the overall health and appearance of your skin, with a reduction in redness and swelling.

However, it's essential to note that if your burning feet are caused by an underlying medical condition, you should consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, commonly known as ginger tea, can help alleviate burning feet due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The main constituents of ginger tea, such as gingerols and shogaols, have been shown to reduce inflammation and pain in the body. When you drink ginger tea, its active compounds are absorbed into your bloodstream and travel to your feet, where they can help to reduce swelling and soothe burning sensations. To use ginger tea for burning feet, you can try the following: Steep a teaspoon of fresh ginger in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink.

You can also add honey or lemon to taste. For best results, drink the tea 2-3 times a day, and apply a paste made from ginger and coconut oil directly to your feet to help reduce inflammation and promote healing. When you take these actions, you may start to feel relief from burning sensations in your feet within a few hours. The inflammation and pain may decrease, and you may be able to walk and stand for longer periods without discomfort.

The antioxidant properties of ginger tea may also help to promote healing and reduce the risk of further irritation or infection.

4. Equisetum arvense

Equisetum arvense teas, also known as horsetail tea, may help with burning feet due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.

The tea contains silica, flavonoids, and alkaloids that can help soothe and protect the skin. Silica, in particular, can help to reduce inflammation and improve circulation, which may ease burning sensations in the feet. Flavonoids can also act as antioxidants, further reducing inflammation and promoting healing. If you're experiencing burning feet, try making a strong tea by steeping 1-2 tablespoons of dried Equisetum arvense in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes.

Strain the tea and let it cool before soaking your feet in it for 15-20 minutes. Repeat this process several times a day to help alleviate burning sensations. As the silica and flavonoids in the tea work to reduce inflammation and promote healing, you may start to notice a decrease in burning sensations in your feet within a few days. Your skin may also start to look smoother and more toned, and you may notice an improvement in circulation.

However, it's essential to note that Equisetum arvense tea may not be suitable for people with certain medical conditions, such as kidney problems or pregnancy, so consult with a healthcare professional before using it.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas, also known as rosemary tea, can help with burning feet due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The tea contains compounds like carnosic acid, which has been shown to reduce inflammation and soothe irritated skin. The constituents in rosemary tea, including rosmarinic acid and camphor, also have a cooling effect, which can provide relief from burning sensations. To try rosemary tea for burning feet, steep a teaspoon of dried rosemary leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain and drink the tea, or use it as a foot bath by soaking your feet in the cooled tea for 10-15 minutes. You can also add a few drops of rosemary essential oil to your bath water for extra benefits. When you take these actions, you may experience a decrease in burning sensations and inflammation in your feet. The antioxidants in rosemary tea may also help to reduce oxidative stress and promote healing.

However, it's essential to note that individual results may vary, and if your burning feet persist or worsen, you should consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

6. Astragalus membranaceus

Astragalus membranaceus teas have been traditionally used to help alleviate burning feet, and for good reason.

The tea contains a compound called astragaloside, which has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe and calm the skin. The tea also contains flavonoids and saponins, which have antioxidant and antimicrobial properties that can help reduce inflammation and fight off infections that may be causing the burning sensation. When taken as a tea, Astragalus membranaceus can help improve blood flow to the feet, which can help to reduce the burning sensation and promote healing. To try Astragalus membranaceus tea for burning feet, start by steeping a teaspoon of dried Astragalus membranaceus root in a cup of hot water for 5-10 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day. You can also add honey or lemon to taste. If you have diabetes or poor circulation, you should consult with a healthcare professional before trying this remedy. As you drink the tea, you may notice a reduction in the burning sensation in your feet. You may also notice improvements in circulation, which can help to promote healing and prevent further irritation.

However, it's essential to note that Astragalus membranaceus tea may not work for everyone, and you should continue to consult with a healthcare professional if your symptoms persist.

7. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus teas, also known as lemon grass tea, have been traditionally used to help relieve burning feet.

The tea contains compounds like citral, limonene, and beta-pinene, which have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. These properties can help soothe and cool the skin, reducing the sensation of burning and discomfort. When you have burning feet, it's essential to take action quickly to prevent further irritation and potential infections. To use Cymbopogon citratus teas for burning feet, first, make a strong tea by steeping the leaves in hot water for 5-7 minutes.

Then, soak your feet in the cooled tea for 15-20 minutes. You can also apply a paste made from the tea leaves directly to the affected area. After soaking or applying the tea, dry your feet thoroughly, especially between the toes, and wear clean socks. As you take these steps, you can expect the sensation of burning to decrease, and the redness and swelling to subside. The antiseptic properties in the tea can also help prevent infections, promoting a healthy environment for your skin to heal.

Additionally, the tea's anti-inflammatory properties can help reduce itching and discomfort, allowing you to feel more comfortable in your shoes.

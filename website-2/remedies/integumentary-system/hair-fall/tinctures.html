1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis tinctures contains rosmarinic acid, a powerful antioxidant that helps protect the scalp from damage and promotes healthy hair growth.

The tannins in Rosmarinus officinalis tinctures reduce inflammation and prevent hair loss caused by scalp irritation. The essential oils in the tincture, particularly camphor and borneol, stimulate blood flow to the scalp, nourishing the hair follicles and promoting hair growth. Rosmarinus officinalis tinctures also contains flavonoids, which help to strengthen hair roots and reduce hair fall.

By targeting the scalp and hair follicles, Rosmarinus officinalis tinctures can help to slow down hair loss and promote a healthy, full head of hair.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of vodka.
  2. Chop the leaves into small pieces and place them in a clean glass jar.
  3. Pour the vodka over the chopped leaves, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle, discarding the solids.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa tinctures contains turmeric, which is rich in compounds like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These bioactive constituents have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Turmeric also contains a compound called curcuminoid, which has been shown to reduce inflammation in the scalp and promote healthy hair growth. Additionally, turmeric's antioxidant properties help protect the hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, which can lead to hair fall.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the hair follicles, curcuma longa tinctures can help promote a healthy scalp and reduce hair fall.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa roots, 2 cups of vodka (at least 80 proof), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Curcuma longa roots and vodka in the glass jar. Make sure the roots are completely covered by the vodka.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks. Shake the jar every day to help the mixture.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in the fridge and use 5-7 drops of it in your hair oil or shampoo to help prevent hair fall.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contains flavonoids, bilobalide, and ginkgolides, which are its active constituents.

Flavonoids and bilobalide improve blood circulation to the scalp, promoting healthy hair growth by reducing inflammation and oxidative stress. Ginkgolides enhance the delivery of oxygen and nutrients to hair follicles, supporting the development of new hair and preventing hair loss. The antioxidant properties of these compounds protect the hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, helping to prevent hair loss and promote a healthy scalp.

By improving blood flow and reducing inflammation, Ginkgo biloba tinctures help to strengthen hair roots and prevent hair fall.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves and 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the Ginkgo biloba leaves to the jar and cover them with vodka. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it daily.
  3. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of glycerin to the liquid and stir well. Glycerin helps to preserve the tincture and make it easier to apply to the hair.
  5. Store the Ginkgo biloba tincture in a cool, dark place and use it within 6 months. To use, mix a few drops of the tincture with a shampoo or conditioner and apply it to your hair.

4. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica tinctures contains a number of bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy scalp environment, Urtica dioica tinctures can help to address the underlying causes of hair fall and promote a full and healthy head of hair.

9. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum tinctures contains the bioactive constituents silymarin and silibinin.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent hair fall from forming?

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for hair fall every day?

You can consume herbal teas daily for hair fall, but in moderation.

Drink 1-2 cups per day, and choose tea blends specifically designed for hair care.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in hair fall?

Herbal teas can start showing results in hair fall within a few weeks to a couple of months.

Some people notice a reduction in shedding after 4-6 weeks of consistent drinking.

However, it may take longer, up to 3-4 months, to see noticeable improvements in hair growth and thickness.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for hair fall?

Drinking herbal tea for hair fall is best in the morning or early afternoon.

This allows the nutrients and antioxidants to work throughout the day, strengthening your hair follicles and promoting healthy growth.

It's also a great way to start your day with a healthy habit.

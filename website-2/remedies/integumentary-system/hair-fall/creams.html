1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains biologically active constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-verbascose.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe an itchy scalp, reducing stress on hair follicles. The anti-inflammatory properties also help reduce redness and swelling, which can contribute to hair fall. Aloe vera's moisturizing properties help keep the scalp hydrated, promoting healthy hair growth.

By reducing irritation and promoting a healthy scalp environment, aloe barbadensis creams can help alleviate hair fall caused by scalp conditions.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water and 1/2 cup of aloe vera gel in a bowl.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of coconut oil and 1 tablespoon of olive oil to the bowl.
  3. Mix in 2 tablespoons of honey and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  4. Heat the mixture in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl for 10-15 seconds.
  5. Let the mixture cool, then pour it into a container and store it in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains curcumin, a key bioactive constituent that helps with hair fall.

Curcumin has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which reduce scalp inflammation and prevent damage to hair follicles. It also has natural antifungal and antibacterial properties that prevent infections and promote a healthy scalp environment. Turmeric's curcuminoids, including curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin, inhibit the 5-alpha-reductase enzyme, which slows down hair loss caused by excessive dihydrotestosterone (DHT) production.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy scalp, Curcuma longa creams help to prevent hair fall and promote hair growth.

  1. Mix 2 tablespoons of Curcuma longa powder with 1/2 cup of coconut oil in a bowl.
  2. Heat the mixture in a saucepan over low heat for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool down completely.
  4. Once cooled, mix in 1 tablespoon of honey and 1 tablespoon of aloe vera gel.
  5. Transfer the mixture to an airtight container and refrigerate for 2 hours before using as hair cream.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains carnosic acid, camphor, and borneol as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce hair loss by protecting the scalp from damage and promoting a healthy environment. Carnosic acid also has antimicrobial properties, which can help to prevent infections that can lead to hair loss. The antioxidant properties of borneol and camphor help to nourish the hair follicles and promote hair growth.

The combination of these properties makes Rosmarinus officinalis creams a useful tool in addressing hair fall issues.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup coconut oil, 1/4 cup shea butter, 2 tablespoons olive oil, 2 tablespoons beeswax, 10 drops rosemary essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Stir until beeswax is fully melted. Remove from heat.
  4. Add olive oil and rosemary essential oil to the mixture. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a small tin or jar. Let it cool and harden before use. Apply to scalp to help prevent hair fall.

4. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are active constituents that help with hair fall.

Flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that promote hair growth by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation in the scalp. Terpenoids, including ginkgolides and bilobalide, have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help to protect hair follicles from damage and promote healthy hair growth. These compounds also improve blood circulation to the scalp, which helps to deliver oxygen and nutrients to the hair follicles, promoting healthy hair growth.

By reducing inflammation, improving blood circulation, and promoting antioxidant activity, Ginkgo biloba creams can help to prevent hair fall and promote healthy hair growth.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Ginkgo biloba extract, 2 tablespoons of jojoba oil, and 2 teaspoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a saucepan over low heat.
  3. Remove saucepan from heat and let it cool for 5 minutes. Add Ginkgo biloba extract, jojoba oil, and vitamin E oil to the mixture.
  4. Mix all ingredients well until they are fully incorporated. Pour the mixture into a small container.
  5. Let the mixture cool and harden completely. Once hardened, your Ginkgo biloba cream is ready to use. Apply it to your scalp to help prevent hair fall.

5. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus creams contains active constituents like geraniol, limonene, and citral.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce scalp irritation and promote a healthy scalp environment. The antioxidant properties of these constituents also help protect hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to hair fall. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds can help reduce dandruff and other scalp conditions that can lead to hair loss.

By reducing scalp irritation and promoting a healthy scalp environment, Cymbopogon citratus creams can help prevent hair fall and promote hair growth.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 1 tablespoon of honey, and 10 drops of Cymbopogon citratus essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat, stirring until beeswax dissolves.
  3. Remove from heat and add honey and Cymbopogon citratus essential oil. Stir well to combine.
  4. Pour the mixture into a small container and let it cool until solidified.
  5. Use a spatula to scoop out a small amount of the cream and apply it to your scalp, massaging gently before washing your hair as usual.

6. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains bioactive constituents such as cineole and terpinen-4-ol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to soothe an itchy scalp and reduce redness, creating a healthy environment for hair growth. The antifungal properties of these constituents also help to prevent fungal infections that can contribute to hair loss. Additionally, the antioxidants in Melaleuca alternifolia creams protect the hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, promoting a strong and healthy hair growth cycle.

By addressing the underlying causes of hair loss, Melaleuca alternifolia creams can help to reduce hair fall and promote a fuller head of hair.

  1. Gather ingredients: 250g beeswax, 100g coconut oil, 100g olive oil, 20g Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil, 2 tablespoons vitamin E oil
  2. Melt beeswax, coconut oil, and olive oil in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth
  3. Remove melted mixture from heat, let cool slightly, then add Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil and vitamin E oil, stir well
  4. Pour mixture into a container, let cool and solidify completely, then use a hand mixer or whisk to whip until light and fluffy
  5. Transfer whipped cream to a container, use immediately or store in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks, apply to hair as a treatment for hair fall

7. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents like gingerol and shogaol, which are responsible for its hair growth-promoting properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation in the scalp, a common cause of hair loss. Ginger's anti-inflammatory properties also increase blood flow to the scalp, promoting hair growth by nourishing the hair follicles. The antioxidants present in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, protect the hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, further promoting healthy hair growth.

Regular application of Zingiber officinale creams may reduce hair fall and promote thicker, healthier hair.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of honey, 2 tablespoons of ginger powder (Zingiber officinale), and 10 drops of rosemary essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add honey and ginger powder to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Add rosemary essential oil and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean, airtight container and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before use. Apply to scalp to help prevent hair fall.

8. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica creams contains active constituents like histamine, lectins, and saponins, which are responsible for its benefits.

Histamine in Urtica dioica creams stimulates blood flow to the scalp, promoting healthy hair growth. The saponins in the cream have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce inflammation and soothe an itchy scalp, creating an ideal environment for hair growth. Lectins in Urtica dioica creams help strengthen hair follicles and reduce hair fall.

The combination of these active constituents makes Urtica dioica creams a potential solution for hair fall problems.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Urtica dioica leaves and 1 cup of coconut oil in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in the saucepan over low heat and add the Urtica dioica leaves. Let it simmer for 2-3 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the leaves.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter to the bowl. Melt the mixture in a double boiler or in the microwave in 10-second increments.
  5. Pour the mixture into an ice cube tray and let it cool. Once set, transfer the creams to a jar and use them as a hair mask to prevent hair fall.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in hair fall?

Herbal teas can help slow down hair fall, but it takes time to notice results.

It really depends on the individual and the specific tea being used.

7 Herbal Essential Oils For Hair Fall

1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains active constituents like carnosic acid, rosmanol, and bornyl acetate.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce stress and promote a healthy scalp. The antifungal properties of bornyl acetate also prevent fungal infections that can contribute to hair loss. Carnosic acid's ability to improve blood circulation helps to nourish hair follicles and promote hair growth.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy scalp environment, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils helps to prevent hair fall.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Wash the leaves with water and let them dry completely.
  3. Combine the dried leaves and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids. Use the essential oil for hair fall treatment.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains a compound called cineole, which is known to improve scalp health by reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy environment for hair growth.

It also contains compounds like limonene and alpha-pinene, which have antimicrobial properties that help to prevent fungal and bacterial infections on the scalp. These infections can lead to hair loss, so by preventing them, Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils helps to promote a healthy scalp and reduce hair fall. Additionally, the antifungal properties of cineole help to prevent dandruff and other scalp conditions that can contribute to hair loss.

The essential oil's ability to improve scalp health and prevent infections makes it a useful natural remedy for reducing hair fall.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 2 cups of water in a pot. Boil the mixture for 30 minutes.
  2. Let the mixture cool down. Strain the liquid to separate the leaves from the water using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  3. Transfer the strained liquid to a dark glass bottle. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  4. Close the bottle and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks to allow the mixture to mature. Shake the bottle daily.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, filter the mixture again using cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve. Your Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil for hair fall is now ready to use.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are responsible for its hair benefits.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, helping to soothe an itchy scalp and reduce stress, two common causes of hair loss. Lavandula angustifolia essential oils also has antiseptic properties, which help to prevent infections that can damage hair follicles and lead to hair fall. Additionally, its ability to promote relaxation and reduce anxiety can help to lower the cortisol levels in the body, which can contribute to hair loss.

By using Lavandula angustifolia essential oils, you can promote a healthy scalp and prevent hair fall.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  3. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a small saucepan and heat it gently over low heat (100-150°F) for 1-2 hours. Monitor the temperature to avoid overheating.
  5. Let the mixture cool and then bottle it. Use 5-7 drops of the essential oil in your hair care products or apply it directly to your scalp to help prevent hair fall.

4. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, beta-pinene, and geraniol as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds help to promote hair growth by reducing inflammation and preventing hair follicle damage. The antiseptic and antimicrobial properties of the essential oil prevent scalp infections and soothe irritation, creating a healthy environment for hair growth. The antifungal properties also help to control dandruff and itchiness, reducing the risk of hair loss.

By promoting a balanced scalp and reducing inflammation, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can help to slow down hair fall and promote healthy hair growth.

  1. Gather 500g of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 1 cup of water.
  2. Wash the leaves in the cup of water to clean them.
  3. Blend the leaves with 1 cup of water in a blender to extract the oil.
  4. Heat the mixture in a saucepan over low heat for 2-3 hours. Strain the oil using a cheesecloth.
  5. Store the Cymbopogon citratus essential oil in a dark glass bottle for future use.

5. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains bioactive constituents like cineole, alpha-pinene, and limonene.

These compounds help to improve blood flow to the scalp, which can reduce stress and promote a healthy environment for hair growth. The anti-inflammatory properties of eucalyptus essential oil can soothe an itchy scalp and reduce dandruff, a common cause of hair fall. Additionally, eucalyptus oil's antifungal properties can help control fungal infections that can lead to hair loss.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy scalp, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help reduce hair fall and promote hair growth.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Blend the Eucalyptus globulus leaves with 1 cup of water in a saucepan and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for 20 minutes. Then, let it cool down.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Add the carrier oil and mix well.
  5. Bottle the mixture and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in your hair scalp massage to prevent hair fall.

6. Santalum album

Santalum album essential oils contains a compound called alpha-santalol, which is responsible for its hair growth properties.

This essential oil also contains beta-santalol and other sesquiterpenes, which help to promote hair follicle activity and prevent hair loss. The oil's antifungal and antibacterial properties help to prevent scalp infections that can lead to hair fall. Additionally, Santalum album essential oils has anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce scalp irritation and promote a healthy scalp environment.

Regular use of Santalum album essential oils can help to stimulate hair growth and reduce hair fall by promoting a balanced scalp and hair follicle activity.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Santalum album leaves and flowers, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass container.
  2. Wash the Santalum album leaves and flowers with 2 cups of water to remove dirt and impurities.
  3. Dry the leaves and flowers using a clean towel or paper towels. Then, chop them into small pieces.
  4. Combine 1 cup of chopped Santalum album leaves and flowers with 1 cup of carrier oil in a clean glass container. Let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the container every day.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container. Discard the solids and use the essential oil for hair fall treatment.

7. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-juice, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing redness and irritation that can contribute to hair fall. The essential oils also contain vitamins A, C, and E, as well as minerals like calcium and potassium, which promote healthy hair growth by strengthening hair follicles and improving blood circulation. Aloe barbadensis essential oils also contain enzymes that break down protein bonds, helping to repair and restore damaged hair.

By promoting a healthy scalp and strengthening hair follicles, aloe barbadensis essential oils can help to reduce hair fall and promote healthy hair growth.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1 tablespoon of olive oil, and a small bowl.
  2. Combine the Aloe gel, coconut oil, and olive oil in the bowl. Mix well until smooth.
  3. Heat the mixture in a low-temperature oven (150°F to 200°F) for 2 hours to allow the oil to infuse into the Aloe.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a new bowl to remove any solids.
  5. Transfer the Aloe essential oil to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dry place. Use 1 to 2 teaspoons daily to help with hair fall.

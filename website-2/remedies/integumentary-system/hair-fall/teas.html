What herbal teas you should avoid if you have hair fall?

If you have hair fall, it's best to avoid herbal teas containing St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum).

St. John's Wort can interact with certain medications and affect their effectiveness. This can be a problem if you're taking medications for hair loss, such as minoxidil or finasteride. You should also avoid teas made from Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) and Goldenseal is often combined with other herbs. Goldenseal can slow down blood clotting and may increase the risk of bleeding, which can be a concern if you have hair fall and are taking medications.

Teas containing Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale) root should also be avoided, especially if you have kidney problems or are taking diuretics. Dandelion root can increase urine production and worsen kidney problems. Finally, teas containing Sassafras (Sassafras albidum) and Wintergreen (Gaultheria procumbens) should be avoided. Sassafras can cause skin problems, and Wintergreen contains a compound called methyl salicylate, which can thin the blood and cause bleeding.

These herbal teas can have negative effects on your body, especially if you're taking medications for hair loss.

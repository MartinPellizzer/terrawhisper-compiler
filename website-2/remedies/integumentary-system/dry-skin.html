What Are The Main Causes Of Dry Skin?

Dry skin, also known as xerosis, occurs when the skin loses its natural moisture.

One main cause of dry skin is cold weather. When it's cold outside, the air tends to be drier, which means it has less moisture. As a result, the skin loses moisture faster, leading to dryness. Another cause of dry skin is dry air, often found in air-conditioned spaces or during the winter months. Dry air lacks moisture, causing the skin to lose its natural moisture barrier and become dry.

This can be especially true for people who live in dry climates or have central heating in their homes. Over bathing is also a cause of dry skin. When you take too many baths or showers, you strip your skin of its natural oils. Soap and shampoo can be particularly harsh, removing the skin's natural moisture barrier and leading to dryness. This is especially true if you use hot water, which can further strip the skin of its natural oils.

As a result, the skin becomes dry and tight, leading to discomfort and itchiness.

Below are the main causes of dry skin.

  • Cold Weather: Cold weather causes dry skin because it removes moisture from the skin, making it feel tight and rough due to the low humidity in the air.
  • Dry Air: Dry air lacks moisture, causing the water in our skin to evaporate quickly, leading to dryness and irritation.
  • Over Bathing: Over-bathing can cause dry skin because it strips away the natural oils in the skin, which are needed to keep it moist and healthy.
  • Harsh Soaps: Harsh soaps can cause dry skin because they strip away the natural oils from the skin, leaving it feeling tight and dry.
  • Genetic Predisposition: Genetic predisposition contributes to dry skin because some people are naturally born with a lower ability to produce moisture in their skin, causing it to dry out more easily due to their individual genetic makeup.
  • Aging Skin: As we age, the skin's natural ability to hold moisture slows down, causing it to lose its ability to keep in water and leading to dry skin.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Dry Skin?

If you have dry skin, it's essential to be cautious when using certain herbs.

Some herbs can make dry skin worse by removing more moisture or irritating the skin. For example, 'Eucalyptus globulus' is known to dry out the skin further due to its strong astringent properties. 'Tea tree oil', also known as 'Melaleuca alternifolia', can be too harsh for dry skin, causing irritation and further dryness.

'Ginkgo biloba' is another herb to avoid, as it can strip the skin of its natural oils, making dry skin even drier. 'Clove', or 'Syzygium aromaticum', contains compounds that can cause skin irritation, especially on dry skin. 'Turmeric', or 'Curcuma longa', contains curcumin, which can be drying to the skin.

'Ginger', or 'Zingiber officinale', has similar properties to turmeric and can also dry out the skin.

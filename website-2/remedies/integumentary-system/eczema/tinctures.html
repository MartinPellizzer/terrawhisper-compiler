New Moms and Eczema: 8 Herbal Tinctures to Try

New mothers often experience stress, fatigue, and hormonal changes that can trigger eczema symptoms.

Eczema, also known as atopic dermatitis, is a skin condition that causes dry, itchy, and inflamed skin. This condition can be especially challenging for new mothers, who are already adjusting to caring for a newborn. Eczema can affect their daily lives, making it difficult to care for their baby, tend to household chores, and even enjoy activities they once loved. The constant itching and discomfort can disrupt sleep, further exacerbating the emotional and physical challenges of motherhood.

In addition, eczema can lead to infections, scarring, and even depression in severe cases. When treating eczema, many new mothers turn to conventional medications, which can have side effects and may not provide long-term relief. This is where herbal tinctures come in. These natural remedies are made by infusing herbs in a liquid solvent, which allows the active compounds to be easily absorbed by the body. Herbal tinctures can be a safe and effective way to calm eczema symptoms, reducing inflammation and promoting healthy skin.

By using herbal tinctures, new mothers can find relief from eczema without exposing themselves or their babies to harsh chemicals.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures have been traditionally used to help soothe and calm eczema symptoms.

The tincture is made from the flowers of the marigold plant, which contain anti-inflammatory properties and flavonoids like quercetin and luteolin. These constituents help to reduce redness and swelling, ease itching, and promote wound healing. When using Calendula officinalis tincture for eczema, it's recommended to dilute the tincture in water and apply it topically to the affected area with a cotton swab. Alternatively, you can add a few drops to a bath for a relaxing soak.

To get the most out of the tincture, make sure to clean the skin area before application and avoid using harsh soaps or detergents. It's also essential to note that Calendula officinalis tincture is not a substitute for medical treatment, and you should consult a doctor before using it, especially if you have severe eczema or allergies. When used correctly, you can expect to see a reduction in inflammation and itching, as well as an improvement in skin texture and overall appearance. The tincture may also help to speed up the healing process, promoting the growth of healthy skin cells. However, it's crucial to remember that everyone's skin is different, and what works for one person might not work for another.

If you experience any adverse reactions or worsening symptoms, discontinue use and seek medical attention.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis tinctures are made from the gel of the aloe vera plant, which is rich in properties and constituents that help soothe and calm eczema symptoms.

The gel contains vitamins A, C, and E, as well as minerals like calcium and potassium, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects that can reduce redness and itching. Aloe barbadensis tinctures also contain compounds like aloin and aloe-emodin, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that can help prevent infection and promote healing. If you're considering using aloe barbadensis tinctures for eczema, there are a few things you can do to get started. First, consult with a healthcare professional before using any new remedy, especially if you're already taking medications or have a severe case of eczema.

They can help you determine the best course of treatment and ensure that the tincture won't interact with any of your other medications. Once you've got the green light, you can start by applying a small amount of tincture to the affected area 2-3 times a day. You can also add a few drops to a bath or use it as a moisturizer to help lock in moisture and reduce dryness. Taking aloe barbadensis tinctures for eczema may lead to noticeable improvements in your symptoms, including reduced redness and itching, and a decrease in the severity of eczema flare-ups. You may also notice an improvement in the overall health and appearance of your skin, with a reduction in scarring and discoloration.

Additionally, using aloe barbadensis tinctures may help reduce the need for topical steroids and other medications, which can have side effects and take a toll on your skin over time.

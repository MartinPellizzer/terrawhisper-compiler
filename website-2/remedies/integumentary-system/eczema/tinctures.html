1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which help reduce redness and swelling associated with eczema. The flavonoids, particularly kaempferol and quercetin, possess antioxidant properties that help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals. The triterpenoid saponins, such as calenduloside, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory mediators, which contribute to the development of eczema symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and promoting skin healing, Calendula officinalis tinctures can help alleviate the symptoms of eczema.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula officinalis flowers and 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Fill a separate glass jar with 2 cups of distilled water and add 1 teaspoon of glycerin. Store it in the fridge.
  3. Combine the Calendula flowers and vodka in the first jar. Close the lid and shake well for 2 minutes.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking it every day. This allows the Calendula to infuse into the vodka.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the jar with the glycerin mixture. Discard the solids. Use the tincture within 6 months.

2. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale tinctures contains active constituents like allantoin, aucubin, and baicalein.

These compounds work together to reduce inflammation and promote skin healing. The anti-inflammatory properties of baicalein and aucubin help to soothe and calm irritated skin, while allantoin's mucilaginous properties create a protective barrier to lock in moisture. This helps to reduce redness and discomfort associated with eczema.

By reducing inflammation and promoting skin healing, Symphytum officinale tinctures may provide relief for individuals with eczema.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Symphytum officinale leaves and 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the dried leaves to the jar and pour the vodka over them, making sure the leaves are completely covered.
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in the same cool, dark place and use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, to help soothe eczema symptoms.

3. Aloe barbadensis

By reducing inflammation and promoting tissue repair, aloe barbadensis tinctures can help to alleviate symptoms of eczema, such as redness, itching, and dryness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel and 2 cups of 80% vodka in clean glass containers.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent eczema from forming?

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for eczema every day?

Herbal teas can be a soothing way to help manage eczema symptoms.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in eczema?

Herbal teas can take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks to show results in eczema.

Some people notice improvement in their skin within a week, while others may take longer.

It's also important to be consistent with drinking the teas and to combine them with other self-care practices for best results.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for eczema?

Drinking herbal tea for eczema is best after a warm bath or shower, when your skin is most relaxed.

This helps your skin absorb the tea's soothing properties.

Morning and evening are good times to try it, as it can help calm itchy skin and promote a good night's sleep.

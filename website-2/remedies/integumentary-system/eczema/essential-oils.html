1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains bioactive constituents such as aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce redness and swelling associated with eczema. Aloe-emodin, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals that exacerbate eczema symptoms. Acemannan, a polysaccharide, has soothing and moisturizing effects that help calm irritated skin and promote wound healing.

By addressing inflammation and promoting skin health, aloe barbadensis essential oils can provide relief from eczema symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Aloe barbadensis leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Wash the Aloe leaves with 1 cup of water and dry them with a clean cloth.
  3. Crush the Aloe leaves to release their gel and mix with 1/2 cup of carrier oil in a bowl.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the Aloe essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 1-2 teaspoons as needed for eczema relief.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help soothe and calm the skin. The triterpenoid saponins, specifically, inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, reducing redness and swelling associated with eczema. The flavonoids and carotenoids have antioxidant properties that protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting healing and regeneration.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the skin, Calendula officinalis essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of eczema.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Steep for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the flowers.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Let it sit for 2-3 weeks to mature.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, filter the essential oil again through a cheesecloth or coffee filter. Your Calendula officinalis essential oil is now ready to use for eczema relief.

3. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica essential oils contains bioactive constituents like histamine, flavonoids, and polysaccharides.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce redness and swelling associated with eczema. Histamine, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the release of pro-inflammatory chemicals that exacerbate eczema symptoms. The flavonoids in Urtica dioica essential oils, such as quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant properties that help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the skin, Urtica dioica essential oils may help alleviate eczema symptoms and promote skin health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Urtica dioica leaves, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Wash the Urtica dioica leaves with cold water and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Combine the dried leaves and carrier oil in the glass jar, making sure the leaves are fully covered.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. Then, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a new glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Urtica dioica essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

4. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains a high concentration of bioactive constituents, including cineole, linalool, and terpinen-4-ol.

These compounds have potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help reduce redness and swelling associated with eczema. Cineole and linalool also have antifungal properties, making them effective against fungal infections that can trigger eczema symptoms. Terpinen-4-ol, on the other hand, has a soothing effect on the skin, reducing itching and discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off underlying infections, Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils can help alleviate eczema symptoms and promote healthy skin.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Wash the leaves with 1 cup of water and let them dry completely.
  3. Fill a 2-liter jar with the dried leaves and pour 2 cups of carrier oil over them.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter and discard the solids. Store the oil in a dark glass bottle.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which help to soothe and calm irritated skin. The antifungal properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also prevent the growth of bacteria that can exacerbate eczema. By reducing inflammation and preventing infection, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to promote skin healing and reduce eczema symptoms.

The anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds also help to reduce redness and itching associated with eczema.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water over low heat for 30 minutes, then remove from heat.
  3. Add the Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the hot water and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container. Discard the solids.
  5. Use a dropper to transfer the oil to a glass bottle. Store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

6. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains active constituents such as alkamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce redness and swelling associated with eczema. The phenolic acids in Echinacea purpurea essential oils have antioxidant properties, which can protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals and promote healing. The alkamides and glycosides in this oil have immunomodulatory effects, which can help regulate the body's immune response and reduce inflammation.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Echinacea purpurea essential oils may be beneficial for individuals with eczema.

  1. Gather materials: 2 cups of Echinacea purpurea flowers, a carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), a glass jar, a cheesecloth, and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Dry the Echinacea purpurea flowers for 1-2 weeks to reduce moisture. Store them in a cool, dry place.
  3. Combine 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers with 2 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well to mix.
  4. Steep the mixture for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar every day. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth into a bowl.
  5. Transfer the essential oil to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops of the essential oil per application for eczema relief.

7. Chamomilla recutita

Chamomilla recutita essential oils contains apigenin and luteolin, which are potent anti-inflammatory compounds.

These properties help reduce redness and swelling associated with eczema. The essential oil's anti-oxidant properties, contributed by constituents like bisabolol, protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals. Chamomilla recutita essential oils also possess soothing and calming properties, which can help ease the discomfort and itchiness often experienced with eczema.

By reducing inflammation and promoting skin health, Chamomilla recutita essential oils can provide relief from eczema symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Chamomilla recutita flowers, fresh or dried.
  2. Combine 1 cup of flowers with 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil) in a clean glass jar.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops per application for eczema relief.

9. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum essential oils contains flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which are the active constituents that contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The flavonoids, specifically quercetin and hyperoside, have anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce redness and swelling associated with eczema. The tannins in Hypericum perforatum essential oils have astringent and antiseptic properties, which help to soothe and calm the skin, reducing the risk of infection and promoting healing. The phenolic acids, such as ferulic acid and sinapic acid, have antioxidant properties that help to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, which can exacerbate eczema symptoms.

By using Hypericum perforatum essential oils, individuals with eczema may experience reduced inflammation, improved skin health, and a decrease in the severity of their symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum flowers, 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Place the flowers in the jar and pour the carrier oil over them. Stir gently to make sure the flowers are fully covered.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every few days. This is called 'infusion'.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and label it. Store it in a cool, dark place. Use a few drops of the essential oil in a carrier oil or lotion to help with eczema symptoms.

10. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale essential oils contains bioactive constituents like allantoin, vitamin E, and mucilage, which contribute to its skin-soothing properties.

Allantoin helps to break down dead skin cells and promote cell turnover, reducing inflammation and irritation associated with eczema. Vitamin E has antioxidant properties that protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, which can exacerbate eczema symptoms. Mucilage, a thick, protective substance, helps to lock in moisture and soothe dry, itchy skin.

These properties make Symphytum officinale essential oils a useful addition to topical treatments for eczema.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Symphytum officinale leaves and flowers. Clean them with water.
  2. Place the cleaned leaves and flowers in a glass container. Add 2 cups of carrier oil, such as coconut oil or olive oil.
  3. Steep the mixture for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place. Shake the container every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops of the oil per application for eczema relief.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in eczema?

Herbal teas can help with eczema symptoms, but it's hard to predict exactly when you'll see results.

Some people notice a difference within a few days to a week, while others may take a few weeks or even months to see improvement.

It really depends on your individual skin and the specific herbs used.

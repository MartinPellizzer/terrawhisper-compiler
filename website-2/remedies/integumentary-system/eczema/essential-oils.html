Dry skin can cause itchiness, redness, and irritation. Aloe vera is a common remedy for these issues. Its soothing properties help calm the skin and reduce inflammation. Some people with eczema also experience allergies, which can be triggered by various factors like pollen, dust, or certain foods. Nettle leaf is a natural antihistamine that can help alleviate allergic reactions. It reduces inflammation and eases symptoms like itching and congestion. Eczema can also be linked to digestive problems, such as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS).

Turmeric contains curcumin, a compound that has anti-inflammatory properties. It helps to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and alleviate symptoms like bloating and abdominal pain. Additionally, people with eczema may experience anxiety or stress, which can exacerbate eczema symptoms. Passionflower is a herbal remedy that helps to calm the nervous system and reduce anxiety levels. Its sedative properties promote relaxation and improve sleep quality. Lastly, eczema can increase the risk of infections, particularly bacterial infections. Tea tree oil has antimicrobial properties that help to prevent bacterial growth and promote wound healing. It can be applied topically to affected areas to reduce the risk of infection.

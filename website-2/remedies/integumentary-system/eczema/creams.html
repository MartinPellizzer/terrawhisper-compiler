1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains two main bioactive constituents: aloe-emodin and aloin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce redness and swelling often associated with eczema. The cream also contains vitamins A, C, and E, as well as beta-carotene, which have antioxidant properties that protect the skin from damage and promote healing. The aloe vera gel in the cream has a soothing effect on the skin, reducing irritation and itching caused by eczema.

The moisturizing properties of the cream help to lock in moisture and soothe dry, itchy skin, providing relief from eczema symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup distilled water, 1/2 cup aloe vera gel, 2 tablespoons coconut oil, 2 tablespoons beeswax, 2 tablespoons shea butter, 2 teaspoons vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until combined.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and stir in aloe vera gel and vitamin E oil until smooth.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean glass jar and let it cool and solidify before use.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains carotenoids, flavonoids, and triterpenoid saponins, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The carotenoids in Calendula officinalis creams help to reduce redness and inflammation in the skin, which is common in eczema sufferers. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin, have been shown to have antioxidant properties, which help to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals. The triterpenoid saponins in Calendula officinalis creams have been found to have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to reduce the severity of eczema symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Calendula officinalis creams can help to soothe and calm eczema-prone skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula flowers and 1/2 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) in a clean glass bowl.
  2. Combine the dried flowers and carrier oil, then store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the bowl every day.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter to the oil mixture, then heat it in a double boiler until the wax and butter melt.
  5. Remove the bowl from the heat, let it cool for 10 minutes, then pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool completely before use.

3. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale creams contains symphytum, a plant-based ingredient that helps with eczema symptoms.

The active constituents, allantoin and mucilage, have anti-inflammatory properties that soothe and calm irritated skin. Allantoin helps to break down dead skin cells and promote new skin growth, reducing inflammation and redness. Mucilage, a thick and protective gel, creates a barrier on the skin's surface, locking in moisture and shielding it from irritants.

By providing these protective and soothing benefits, Symphytum officinale creams can help to reduce eczema symptoms and promote healthy skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Symphytum officinale (comfrey) leaves and flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Steep the dried comfrey in 2 cups of boiling water for 10-15 minutes. Strain and set aside to cool.
  3. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  4. Add the cooled comfrey liquid, shea butter, and vitamin E oil to the melted coconut oil mixture. Stir until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container and let it cool and solidify. Once set, use as a cream for eczema.

4. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains an active compound called terpinen-4-ol, which is known for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

This compound helps to reduce the growth of bacteria and fungi that can cause eczema, and also reduces inflammation and redness. The cream also contains other active compounds such as cineole and α-terpineol, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These properties help to soothe and calm the skin, reducing the symptoms of eczema such as itching and irritation.

By reducing inflammation and killing bacteria and fungi, Melaleuca alternifolia creams can help to promote healing and reduce the severity of eczema symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 10 drops of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil, 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt beeswax and coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Add distilled water and stir until the mixture thickens.
  5. Add Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil and vitamin E oil to the mixture. Pour it into a container and let it cool completely before use.

5. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica creams contains histamine, polysaccharides, and flavonoids, which are active compounds that help soothe and calm irritated skin.

Histamine has anti-inflammatory properties, reducing redness and swelling associated with eczema. Polysaccharides in the cream have a moisturizing effect, helping to lock in moisture and protect the skin from further irritation. Flavonoids, particularly quercetin, have antioxidant properties that help neutralize free radicals and reduce inflammation.

By combining these properties, Urtica dioica creams can help to reduce the severity of eczema symptoms and promote healthier-looking skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Urtica dioica (nettle) leaves and flowers, and 1 cup of coconut oil in a blender.
  2. Blend the nettle leaves and flowers until they are well mixed and the leaves are finely chopped.
  3. Heat 1/4 cup of coconut oil in a saucepan over low heat. Pour the blended nettle mixture into the oil and stir well.
  4. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool for 10 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, squeezing out as much liquid as possible.
  5. Mix 1/4 cup of shea butter and 2 tablespoons of beeswax into the strained nettle liquid. Pour the mixture into a jar and refrigerate until it hardens.

6. Hypericum perforatum

  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the dried Hypericum perforatum flowers to the melted oil mixture and let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Add the vitamin E oil and essential oil (if using) to the mixture and stir well. Pour the mixture into a tin or a jar and let it cool and harden.

7. Chamomilla recutita

  3. Add the dried Chamomilla recutita flowers to the coconut oil and let it infuse for 2-3 hours.

8. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains a combination of active constituents, including alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and polyphenols.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce redness and swelling associated with eczema. The flavonoids and phenolic acids in Echinacea purpurea also have antioxidant effects, which can help soothe and protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals.   3. In a small saucepan, melt 1/2 cup of coconut oil and 1/4 cup of beeswax over low heat. Stir until melted.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of shea butter and 10 drops of vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir until combined.
  5. Slowly add the cooled Echinacea liquid to the melted mixture. Stir well and pour into a container. Let it cool and harden before use.

9. Arnica montana

Arnica montana creams contains bioactive constituents like sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce redness and swelling associated with eczema. By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Arnica montana creams can help soothe and calm eczema-prone skin.

  3. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  4. Add the dried Arnica montana flowers, shea butter, and vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once set, use a spoon to scoop out the Arnica montana cream and apply to eczema-affected areas.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent eczema from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe and calm the skin, which can be beneficial for people prone to eczema.

Some herbal teas, like chamomile and peppermint, have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce redness and itching associated with eczema.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for eczema every day?

Consuming herbal teas for eczema daily can be safe if you choose teas with soothing properties.

However, some herbs can interact with medications or worsen symptoms. It's essential to select teas that are gentle and calming, such as peppermint or chamomile, and monitor your body's reaction.

Start with small amounts to ensure you're not sensitive to any ingredients.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in eczema?

Herbal teas can help with eczema, but it's hard to say exactly when you'll see results.

Some people notice a difference after a few days, while others may take a week or two.

It really depends on the person and the type of tea they're drinking.

1. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica teas contains bioactive constituents like histamine, lectins, and polysaccharides that help soothe and calm the skin affected by eczema.

The histamine in Urtica dioica stimulates blood flow to the skin, which can help reduce inflammation and promote healing. The polysaccharides in the tea have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help calm the skin and reduce redness and swelling associated with eczema. The lectins in Urtica dioica also have antimicrobial properties, which can help prevent infections and promote healthy skin.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Urtica dioica teas can provide relief for people with eczema.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Urtica dioica leaves. You can find them in nature, or buy them from a store.
  2. Wash the leaves with clean water to remove dirt and bacteria. Use 1 tablespoon of apple cider vinegar for 1 cup of water.
  3. Combine 1 tablespoon of the cleaned Urtica dioica leaves with 1 cup of boiling water in a teapot.
  4. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes. You can add a sweetener like honey if you prefer it sweet.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day for eczema relief. Store any leftover tea in the fridge for up to 24 hours.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas contains triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce redness and swelling associated with eczema. The triterpenoids in Calendula officinalis teas inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to the development of eczema symptoms. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin, have potent anti-inflammatory effects that help soothe irritated skin.

By reducing inflammation and promoting skin healing, Calendula officinalis teas can help alleviate the symptoms of eczema and promote healthy skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers. You can buy them in dried form or dry them yourself by spreading them on a paper and placing them in the sun.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of the dried Calendula flowers and put them in a tea infuser or a small piece of cheesecloth.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the Calendula flowers. If using a tea infuser, place it in the cup.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes. If using cheesecloth, remove it after 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and discard the Calendula flowers. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help soothe eczema symptoms.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas contains a variety of bioactive constituents, including aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce redness and swelling associated with eczema. Aloe barbadensis teas also contains vitamins A, C, and E, as well as beta-carotene, which have antioxidant properties that protect the skin from damage and promote healing. The anti-inflammatory and soothing properties of aloe barbadensis teas can help calm irritated skin and reduce itching and discomfort caused by eczema.

Regular consumption of aloe barbadensis teas may help to reduce the severity and frequency of eczema symptoms.

  1. Cut 1/2 cup of Aloe barbadensis leaves from the plant, avoiding yellow parts.
  2. Wash the leaves with water, then pat them dry with a clean cloth.
  3. Puree the Aloe leaves in a blender or food processor until smooth.
  4. Strain the puree through a fine-mesh sieve into a cup, getting 2 tablespoons of liquid.
  5. Drink the Aloe tea immediately, or store it in the fridge for up to 24 hours.

4. Silybum marianum

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent eczema from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent eczema from forming, but more research is needed to confirm this.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, have anti-inflammatory properties that could soothe skin and reduce eczema symptoms.

However, the effect of herbal tea on preventing eczema is still not fully understood.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for eczema every day?

Herbal teas can be a helpful addition to your eczema treatment, but it's essential to be cautious with daily consumption.

Some teas, like chamomile and peppermint, can be safe and soothing.

However, others like licorice root and sage may cause skin irritation or interact with medications, so start with small amounts and monitor your body's response.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in eczema?

Herbal teas for eczema can take a few days to a few weeks to show results.

Some people notice improvement within a week, while others may take longer. It depends on the severity of the eczema and how well the body absorbs the herbs.

Patience is key when using herbal teas to manage eczema symptoms.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for eczema?

Drinking herbal tea for eczema in the evening can be beneficial.

It may help you relax and reduce inflammation while you sleep, which can help soothe itchy skin.

A warm cup before bed can also ease your mind and body, making it easier to fall asleep and wake up feeling refreshed and calm.

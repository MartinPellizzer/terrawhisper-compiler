Hyperpigmentation: Causes, Herbal Preparations, and Medicinal Solutions

What are the causes of hyperpigmentation?

Hyperpigmentation is a common skin condition where patches of skin become darker than the surrounding area.

There are several causes of hyperpigmentation, and understanding them can help us manage the condition. One of the main causes is melasma, a condition that affects many pregnant women and people who take birth control pills. This is because the hormones in these medications stimulate the skin's melanin production, causing dark patches to appear, usually on the face. Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH) is another common cause, which occurs after the skin has been injured or inflamed. When the skin heals, it can leave behind dark spots, often as a result of acne, cuts, or burns. This is because the inflammation causes the skin's melanin to become overactive, producing more pigment in the affected area.

Solar elastosis is a condition that occurs due to prolonged exposure to the sun's UV rays. Over time, the skin's elastin breaks down, leading to wrinkles and age spots. The sun's UV rays also stimulate melanin production, causing dark patches to appear. Acne can also cause hyperpigmentation, especially if it's severe or if it leaves behind scars. When the skin becomes inflamed due to acne, it can lead to PIH, causing dark spots to appear. Finally, chemical burns can cause hyperpigmentation, especially if they're severe.

The chemicals can damage the skin, causing it to become inflamed and produce more melanin, leading to dark patches.

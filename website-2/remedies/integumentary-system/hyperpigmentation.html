What Are The Main Causes Of Hyperpigmentation?

Hyperpigmentation is a condition where some areas of the skin become darker than others.

This can be caused by several factors. One reason is pregnancy. During pregnancy, the hormone estrogen increases, causing the skin to produce more melanin, the pigment responsible for skin color. This can lead to dark spots or patches on the skin, often on the face, neck, or arms.

Sun exposure is another main cause of hyperpigmentation. When the skin is exposed to the sun's ultraviolet (UV) rays, it can cause damage to the skin cells, leading to the production of more melanin. This can result in dark spots or freckles, especially on areas that are frequently exposed to the sun, such as the face, hands, or feet. Skin injury is also a common cause of hyperpigmentation. When the skin is injured, the body's natural repair process can cause the production of more melanin in the affected area.

This can lead to dark scarring or discoloration, which can take time to fade.

Below are the main causes of hyperpigmentation.

  • Pregnancy: During pregnancy, the levels of a hormone called melanocyte-stimulating hormone (MSH) increase, which causes the skin to produce more melanin, leading to hyperpigmentation, particularly on the face, neck, and hands.
  • Sun exposure: When the skin is exposed to too much sun, it produces extra melanin, a pigment that darkens the skin, leading to the formation of hyperpigmentation spots.
  • Skin injury: When the skin is injured, it releases chemicals that stimulate the production of melanin, the pigment that makes skin darker, leading to hyperpigmentation.
  • Inflammation: Inflammation causes hyperpigmentation because the body's increased blood flow and the release of certain chemicals during the inflammatory process can stimulate the production of melanin, leading to darker skin patches.
  • Hormonal changes: Hormonal changes cause hyperpigmentation because an imbalance of hormones can lead to increased production of melanin, the pigment responsible for skin color, resulting in darker spots and patches on the skin.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Hyperpigmentation?

If you have hyperpigmentation, it's essential to be cautious with certain herbs that may worsen the condition.

Sage (Salvia officinalis) contains salicin, a compound similar to aspirin, which can cause skin irritation and increase melanin production, making hyperpigmentation worse. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) and calendula (Calendula officinalis) may be too harsh for sensitive skin and cause inflammation, which can lead to further darkening of the skin.

St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) is known to cause photosensitivity, making the skin more sensitive to sunlight and potentially leading to hyperpigmentation. Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) contains glycyrrhizin, which can cause hormonal imbalances and increase melanin production, exacerbating hyperpigmentation. Additionally, turmeric (Crocus sativus), although often recommended for its anti-inflammatory properties, contains a compound called curcumin, which may not be suitable for everyone.

It's recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies to manage hyperpigmentation.

9 Herbal Essential Oils For Open Wounds

When it comes to open wounds, herbal essential oils can be a natural and effective way to promote healing.

One herb that stands out is Calendula officinalis, also known as marigold. Its oil has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain, while also fighting off infections. This means your wound won't get infected, and it'll heal faster. Another herb that's great for open wounds is Aloe barbadensis, or aloe vera. Its gel can be applied directly to the wound to soothe and calm the skin. Aloe vera has antimicrobial properties that prevent bacteria from growing, reducing the risk of infection. Plus, it's moisturizing properties help keep the wound hydrated, promoting faster healing.

Melaleuca alternifolia, or tea tree oil, is another example of an herb that's great for open wounds. Its oil has antiseptic properties that help prevent infection and promote healing. When applied to the wound, it helps reduce inflammation and kills bacteria that can cause infection. This means your wound will heal faster, and you'll be back to normal in no time. Herbal essential oils like these can be used in various ways. Some people mix them with a carrier oil, like coconut or olive oil, to create a soothing balm. Others add them to a warm bath to promote relaxation and healing. You can even apply them directly to the wound, after cleaning and drying it first.

Whatever method you choose, the benefits are clear: faster healing, reduced risk of infection, and a reduced need for antibiotics.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help to reduce redness, swelling, and the risk of infection in open wounds. The flavonoids in Calendula officinalis essential oils have antioxidant properties that promote tissue repair and regeneration, while the carotenoids help to protect the wound from further damage. The antimicrobial properties of Calendula officinalis essential oils also help to prevent the growth of bacteria and other pathogens that can delay healing.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infection, Calendula officinalis essential oils can create an environment conducive to healthy wound healing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula flowers and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Add 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or coconut oil) to the jar and mix with the dried Calendula flowers.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the oil to infuse with the calendula.
  4. After the infusion period, strain the mixture using cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Calendula essential oil in a clean, dark glass bottle and use it to treat open wounds by applying a few drops to the affected area.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains bioactive constituents like vitamins, minerals, and amino acids.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and redness in open wounds. The essential oil also contains compounds like aloin and aloe-emodin, which have antimicrobial properties that prevent infection and promote healing. The aloe's gel-like texture helps soothe and protect the wound, creating a barrier against bacteria and other foreign particles.

By promoting a clean and conducive environment for healing, aloe barbadensis essential oils can aid in the recovery process of open wounds.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel from a mature plant.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of distilled water to the gel and mix well.
  3. Add 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil to the mixture and stir.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a clean glass bottle and store in the fridge.
  5. Use a cotton ball to apply a thin layer to open wounds, 2-3 times a day.

3. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains a high concentration of bioactive compounds, including cineole, limonene, and terpinen-4-ol, which are known for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties make it effective at preventing the growth of bacteria, fungi, and other microorganisms that can cause infection in open wounds. The essential oil's ability to reduce inflammation helps to prevent further damage to the surrounding tissue and promotes a healthy environment for healing. Additionally, terpinen-4-ol has been shown to enhance the wound-healing process by stimulating the production of growth factors and improving tissue regeneration.

By applying Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils to open wounds, it can help to promote a clean and healthy environment for the wound to heal.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves. Clean and dry them.
  2. Place the leaves in a 4-cup jar. Cover them with 2 cups of Carrier Oil (e.g. coconut oil). Stir well.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks. Shake the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use it on open wounds after diluting with Carrier Oil (1:3 ratio).

4. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum essential oils contains bioactive constituents like hyperforin and hypericin, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to prevent infection and reduce swelling in open wounds. The antiseptic properties of hyperforin also make it effective against a range of bacteria, including methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA). The anti-inflammatory properties of hypericin reduce redness and promote healing in the wound.

By controlling infection and inflammation, Hypericum perforatum essential oils can help to create an environment that fosters the natural healing process of open wounds.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum flowers, 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Hypericum perforatum flowers and carrier oil in the glass jar. Make sure the flowers are completely covered by the oil.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the oil extract from the flowers.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the Hypericum perforatum essential oil to a small, dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 5-7 drops of the oil per open wound, mixed with a carrier oil, as needed.

5. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains a rich mixture of bioactive constituents, including alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help to reduce the risk of infection and promote wound healing. The essential oil's antioxidant properties also protect the wound from oxidative damage, which can slow down the healing process. Additionally, the oil's ability to stimulate the immune system can help to prevent further damage to the wound and promote tissue repair.

By reducing inflammation, preventing infection, and promoting tissue repair, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help to create a favorable environment for open wounds to heal.

  1. Harvest fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves. Use 1 cup of flowers and 1/2 cup of leaves.
  2. Combine the flowers and leaves in a clean glass jar. Add 2 cups of carrier oil, such as jojoba or sweet almond oil.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. This allows the oil to extract from the plants.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Filter the oil again through a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Store the Echinacea essential oil in a cool, dark place.

6. Arnica montana

Arnica montana essential oils contains bioactive constituents like thymoquinone, sesquiterpenes, and flavonoids that have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling and promote a healthy environment for wound healing. Thymoquinone and sesquiterpenes have been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi, which can cause infections in open wounds. The flavonoids in Arnica montana essential oils also have antioxidant properties, which help to protect the wound from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a clean environment, Arnica montana essential oils can aid in the healing process of open wounds.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Arnica montana flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba oil).
  2. Fill a clean glass jar with the Arnica flowers and cover with the carrier oil. Leave 1 inch of space at the top.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the oil infuse into the flowers.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass container. Discard the Arnica flowers.
  5. Use a dropper to add a few drops of the Arnica oil to open wounds, applying a thin layer to promote healing and reduce inflammation.

7. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like eucalyptol, alpha-pinene, and beta-pinene.

These bioactive constituents have antibacterial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties. Eucalyptol, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi, which can cause infections in open wounds. The anti-inflammatory properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils can also help reduce swelling and promote the healing process.

By applying this essential oil to open wounds, the antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties can help prevent infection and promote the healing of the wound.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a pot.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat and add the Eucalyptus leaves.
  3. Let it simmer for 30 minutes to release the oil from the leaves.
  4. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl.
  5. Let the oil sit for 2 hours and then use it to treat open wounds.

8. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum essential oils contains sesquiterpenes and flavonoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and promote healing in open wounds. The sesquiterpenes, specifically, have been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi that can infect open wounds, reducing the risk of infection and promoting a clean environment for healing. The flavonoids in Silybum marianum essential oils also have antioxidant properties, which help protect the wound from oxidative stress and promote tissue repair.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a clean environment, Silybum marianum essential oils can help create an ideal conditions for open wounds to heal.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Silybum marianum flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (such as coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the dried flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the mixture infuse.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Use 5-7 drops of the infused oil per application to treat open wounds. Apply directly to the wound and cover with a bandage.

9. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their antimicrobial and antiseptic properties.

These properties help prevent the growth of bacteria, fungi, and other microorganisms that can infect open wounds and cause further damage. The antiseptic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help reduce the risk of infection, promoting a clean and safe environment for the wound to heal. Additionally, the essential oil's anti-inflammatory properties, attributed to the presence of linalool, can help reduce redness and swelling associated with open wounds.

By preventing infection and reducing inflammation, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can aid in the healing process of open wounds.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers, 2 cups of water, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the flowers and water in the jar, then stir gently.
  3. Steep the mixture for 2-3 hours in direct sunlight or 24 hours in a dark place.
  4. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Allow the liquid to sit for 24 hours before decanting the top layer into small bottles for use.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent open wounds from forming?

Herbal teas can help with open wounds by promoting healing and reducing inflammation.

The rate of healing varies depending on the severity of the wound and the individual's overall health.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for open wounds?

For open wounds, it's best to drink herbal tea in the evening.

This allows the antioxidants to work while you sleep, helping to speed up the healing process.

Some herbs like calendula and aloe vera are known to aid in wound healing, so drinking tea with these herbs in the evening can be beneficial.

What Are The Main Causes Of Puffy Eyes?

Puffy eyes can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is allergies. When we're allergic to something like pollen or dust, our body reacts by releasing histamine, a chemical that causes blood vessels to swell. This leads to increased fluid in the eyes, making them puffy and itchy. Another cause is sinusitis, an inflammation of the sinuses.

When the sinuses get infected or inflamed, the nasal passages swell, which can cause the veins in the eyes to swell as well. This swelling leads to puffy eyes and can also cause pain and pressure in the face. A cold can also cause puffy eyes. When we catch a cold, our body produces mucus to trap the virus. However, this mucus can also cause the blood vessels in the eyes to swell, leading to puffy eyes.

Additionally, the nasal passages can become congested, causing the veins in the eyes to swell further.

Below are the main causes of puffy eyes.

  • Allergies: When you have an allergy, your body releases histamine, a chemical that causes blood vessels in your eyes to swell, leading to puffy eyes.
  • Sinusitis: When you have sinusitis, the inflammation and extra fluid in your sinuses can cause your eyes to swell and appear puffy due to the increased pressure and fluid buildup in the surrounding areas.
  • Cold: When you breathe in cold air, it causes your blood vessels to swell and expand, which makes the fluid in your body move to your eyes, making them look puffy and swollen.
  • Flu: When you have the flu, the swelling and fluid buildup in your body can cause your eyes to become puffy and swollen due to the increased pressure and water retention.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal changes, such as those experienced during menstruation, pregnancy, or menopause, can cause puffy eyes due to the body's natural fluctuations in fluids and water retention.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Puffy Eyes?

If you have puffy eyes, there are certain herbs you should avoid using as eye drops or creams.

For example, Eyebright (Euphrasia officinalis) might make things worse - it's too strong and can increase swelling. Also, avoid using Goldenrod (Solidago virgaurea) - it's often mixed up with ragweed, which causes allergies.

Another herb to steer clear of is Nettle (Urtica dioica). While it's great for some skin issues, it can irritate the eyes and make puffiness worse. Additionally, Sassafras (Sassafras albidum) contains a chemical that can cause eye problems, so it's best to avoid it altogether.

Finally, avoid using Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana) as an eye treatment - it's too astringent and can dry out the skin around your eyes, making puffiness look even worse.

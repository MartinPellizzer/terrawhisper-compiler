What Are The Main Causes Of Dandruff?

Dandruff is a common scalp condition characterized by flakes of dead skin.

There are several main causes, including a fungus called 'Malassezia'. This fungus naturally lives on the scalp, but it can cause problems when it breaks down the oils in the skin, leading to irritation and flaking. 'Seborrhoeic keratosis' is another cause of dandruff. This condition causes the skin to thicken and form scaly patches, which can be itchy and flaky.

It's not the same as dandruff, but it can be a contributing factor. Eczema is also a cause of dandruff. Eczema is a skin condition that causes inflammation and irritation, leading to dry, itchy skin that flakes off. When eczema affects the scalp, it can cause dandruff-like symptoms.

In people with eczema, the skin's natural barrier is weakened, making it easier for the fungus 'Malassezia' to cause problems.

Below are the main causes of dandruff.

  • Malassezia: Malassezia is a type of fungus that grows on the scalp, breaking down skin's natural oils and causing flakes, which leads to dandruff.
  • Seborrhoeic keratosis: Seborrhoeic keratosis can cause dandruff because it leads to an increase in skin cells, which then flake off and mix with oil from the scalp, resulting in a buildup of flakes that resemble dandruff.
  • Eczema: Eczema can cause dandruff because it leads to inflammation and irritation on the scalp, which can cause skin cells to die and flake off more quickly, resulting in the formation of flakes commonly known as dandruff.
  • Psoriasis: Psoriasis is a cause of dandruff because it makes the skin on the scalp thick and flaky, leading to the formation of white patches and flakes.
  • Diet: A poor diet lacking essential nutrients, particularly zinc and omega-3 fatty acids, can cause skin cells to multiply quickly, leading to excessive shedding and flaking that characterizes dandruff.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Dandruff?

If you have dandruff, there are some herbs you should avoid using.

Sage (Salvia officinalis) can make dandruff worse because it can dry out your scalp. This dryness can lead to more flaking and irritation. Another herb to be careful with is Thyme (Thymus vulgaris), as it can cause an allergic reaction in some people, which can worsen dandruff symptoms.

Bay leaves (Laurus nobilis) and their essential oil can also be problematic, as they can cause skin irritation and dryness. Similarly, Cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum) can be too harsh for people with dandruff, as it can strip your scalp of its natural oils, leading to more flaking and itchiness. Finally, Ginger (Zingiber officinale) can be too spicy for sensitive scalps, causing irritation and making dandruff worse.

These herbs may not be suitable for everyone with dandruff, so it's a good idea to try a patch test before using them.

7 Herbal Creams For Dandruff

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains compounds like cineole and terpinen-4-ol, which are known for their antifungal properties.

These properties help to combat the fungus Malassezia, a common cause of dandruff. Terpinen-4-ol also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe the scalp and reduce irritation. Cineole has antiseptic properties, which can help to kill bacteria and fungi that contribute to dandruff.

By addressing the underlying causes of dandruff, Melaleuca alternifolia creams can provide relief from flakes and irritation.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1 tablespoon of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 teaspoons of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil.
  2. Melt beeswax and coconut oil in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in short bursts.
  3. Add shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Add Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a bowl or container and let it cool and solidify completely before use. Store in an airtight container in the fridge.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains rosin, camphor, and borneol as its main active constituents.

These compounds have antifungal, antibacterial, and anti-inflammatory properties that help to control the growth of fungus and bacteria on the scalp. The antifungal properties of borneol and camphor help to prevent the flaking and itching associated with dandruff, while the anti-inflammatory properties of camphor reduce redness and irritation. The rosin in Rosmarinus officinalis creams also helps to lock in moisture and reduce flaking, making it easier to manage dandruff.

By controlling the growth of fungus and bacteria, reducing inflammation, and locking in moisture, Rosmarinus officinalis creams can provide relief from dandruff symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 teaspoons of dried Rosmarinus officinalis (Rosemary) leaves.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until melted.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until dissolved.
  4. Add the dried Rosemary leaves to the mixture and let it steep for 30 minutes to 1 hour to release the oils.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean container, discard the solids, and let the cream cool and solidify before use.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains cineole, a key bioactive constituent that helps to reduce dandruff.

Cineole has anti-inflammatory properties, which soothe the scalp and prevent irritation that can lead to flaking skin. The cream's astringent properties, courtesy of its high tannin content, help to balance the scalp's natural pH and reduce oil production, a common contributor to dandruff. The menthone and alpha-terpineol in eucalyptus globulus creams also have antifungal properties, which can help to control the growth of yeast on the scalp, a common cause of dandruff.

By addressing these underlying issues, eucalyptus globulus creams can provide effective relief from dandruff symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Eucalyptus globulus essential oil, and 2 tablespoons of tea tree oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl. Heat until smooth.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Add Eucalyptus globulus essential oil and tea tree oil. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool completely. The cream is now ready to use for dandruff treatment.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two main bioactive constituents that have been shown to help with dandruff.

Linalool has antifungal properties, which means it helps to reduce the growth of yeast on the scalp, a common cause of dandruff. Linalyl acetate, on the other hand, has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing redness and irritation. The antiseptic properties of these compounds also help to prevent the spread of fungal infections, further contributing to a healthy scalp.

By reducing the fungal growth and soothing the scalp, Lavandula angustifolia creams can help to alleviate dandruff symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of lavender essential oil, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted oil mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool slightly before adding lavender essential oil and vitamin E oil. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container and let it cool and harden completely before use. Apply a small amount to the scalp to help soothe and calm dandruff.

5. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains a compound called aloin, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

This helps to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing redness and irritation associated with dandruff. The cream also contains glycoproteins, which help to strengthen hair follicles and reduce flaking. Aloe vera contains salicylic acid, a beta hydroxy acid that helps to break down dead skin cells and prevent their buildup on the scalp.

By reducing inflammation and removing dead skin cells, Aloe barbadensis creams can help to alleviate symptoms of dandruff.

  1. Gather 1 cup of aloe vera gel, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 2 tablespoons of honey, 1 teaspoon of tea tree oil, and 10 drops of lavender oil.
  2. In a bowl, mix 1/2 cup of aloe vera gel with 2 tablespoons of coconut oil and 2 tablespoons of olive oil. Stir well.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of honey and mix until smooth. Heat the mixture in a double boiler for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool. Add 1 teaspoon of tea tree oil and 10 drops of lavender oil. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a jar or container and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Use the Aloe barbadensis cream for dandruff relief.

6. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus creams contains limonene, geraniol, and citronellal as active constituents.

These compounds have antifungal and antibacterial properties, which help control the growth of yeast on the scalp, a common cause of dandruff. Geraniol also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can soothe and calm irritated skin. Limonene's antiseptic properties help reduce the amount of fungal and bacterial infections on the scalp, further alleviating dandruff symptoms.

Additionally, citronellal has a decongestant property that helps to reduce flaking and scaling associated with dandruff.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 10 drops of Cymbopogon citratus essential oil, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated and the mixture is smooth.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool until it reaches 120°F to 130°F. Stir in the Cymbopogon citratus essential oil and vitamin E oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify completely. Once solid, use the cream as needed to help soothe and calm dandruff-prone scalps.

7. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains active constituents like curcumin, turmerone, and ar-turmerone.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help soothe and calm the scalp, reducing redness and irritation associated with dandruff. Turmerone, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the growth of fungal agents, such as malassezia, which contribute to dandruff. The antifungal properties of ar-turmerone also help control the yeast's overgrowth on the scalp, further alleviating dandruff symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and inhibiting fungal growth, Curcuma longa creams can help alleviate dandruff and promote a healthier scalp.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of Curcuma longa powder in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in the double boiler, then add the Curcuma longa powder.
  3. Stir well and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the mixture thickens.
  4. Remove from heat and let it cool down. Then, add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and stir until it dissolves.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool down completely before use as a dandruff cream.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dandruff from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent dandruff from forming.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, have antifungal properties that can help control yeast growth on the scalp, which can contribute to dandruff.

These teas may also help soothe and calm the scalp, reducing flaking and itchiness.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dandruff every day?

Consuming herbal teas for dandruff daily can be safe, but it depends on the type of tea and your individual sensitivity.

Some teas, like peppermint and chamomile, are generally considered gentle and might soothe scalp irritation. However, stronger teas like licorice root or sage can be irritating if used excessively.

Moderation is key.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dandruff?

Herbal teas for dandruff can start to show results within a few weeks to a few months.

Some people notice improvements in their scalp health after 2-3 weeks, while others may take longer.

It's a good idea to be patient and consistent with your herbal tea routine to see the best results.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for dandruff?

Drinking herbal tea for dandruff relief is best done at night, before bed.

This is because many herbal teas, such as those containing neem or tea tree oil, have antifungal properties that help combat dandruff.

By drinking it at night, these properties can work while you sleep, reducing flaking and itchiness by morning.

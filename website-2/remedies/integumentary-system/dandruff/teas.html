9 Herbal Teas For Dandruff

As people age, their skin can become more sensitive and dry, making them more prone to dandruff.

This condition causes flakes of dead skin to fall off the scalp, leading to itching and discomfort. For older adults, dandruff can be especially frustrating because it can make everyday activities like styling their hair or even just enjoying a quiet moment at home a challenge. It can also affect their self-esteem and confidence, making them feel self-conscious about their appearance.

Dandruff can also lead to hair loss, which can be a significant blow to older adults who have spent their lives taking care of their hair. Dandruff can also lead to other issues, like irritation and redness of the scalp, which can be painful and uncomfortable. Older adults may try to manage dandruff with over-the-counter shampoos or medicated treatments, but these can sometimes have harsh side effects or be too expensive. This is where herbal teas come in - some herbal teas have anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties that can help soothe and calm the scalp, reducing the symptoms of dandruff and promoting healthy hair growth.

By drinking these teas, older adults can potentially find a natural and gentle way to manage their dandruff and improve the overall health of their scalp.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia teas contains compounds like cineole and terpineol that help to combat dandruff due to their antifungal and antibacterial properties.

These properties work to reduce the growth of yeast on the scalp, a common cause of dandruff. The tea also contains antioxidants that help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing inflammation and irritation. If you're looking to use Melaleuca alternifolia tea to help with dandruff, try brewing a cup of the tea by steeping a teaspoon of dried leaves in hot water for 5-7 minutes. Then, use the cooled tea as a final rinse after shampooing your hair.

Alternatively, you can add a few drops of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil to your shampoo or use a product that contains the oil. When using Melaleuca alternifolia tea, you may notice a reduction in flaking, a decrease in itchiness, and an improvement in scalp health. You may also notice that your hair feels cleaner and looks shinier. However, results may vary from person to person, and it's essential to be patient and consistent in your use of the tea.

It's also recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using any new hair treatment, especially if you have sensitive skin or allergies.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains rosmarinic acid and camphor, which have anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties that help combat dandruff.

Dandruff is caused by a fungus called Malassezia, which feeds on the oils in your scalp, leading to flaking and itching. The rosmarinic acid in Rosmarinus officinalis tea helps reduce the growth of this fungus, while the camphor soothes the scalp and reduces inflammation. To use Rosmarinus officinalis teas for dandruff, start by brewing a cup of tea using 1 teaspoon of dried herbs per 8 ounces of water. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain and cool it down.

Apply the cooled tea to your scalp using a cotton ball or washcloth, and massage it in for 5-10 minutes. Leave it on for 30 minutes to an hour before rinsing with warm water and shampooing as usual. Taking action with Rosmarinus officinalis teas may lead to a reduction in dandruff flakes and itching within a few weeks. You may also notice a decrease in the redness and inflammation of your scalp.

However, it's essential to note that dandruff can be a persistent issue, and it may take some time to see noticeable results.

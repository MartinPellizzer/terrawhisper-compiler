Rosemary has been shown to have antifungal properties and can help improve blood flow to the scalp, which can help reduce flaking and irritation. A third herb used in herbal teas for dandruff is Zingiber officinale, or ginger. Ginger has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce redness and irritation on the scalp, and can also help to improve circulation to the scalp. This can help to reduce flaking and dandruff. 1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia teas contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and eucalyptol, which have antifungal and antibacterial properties.

These properties help to control the growth of yeast and bacteria that can cause dandruff. The tea also contains terpinen-4-ol, which has been shown to inhibit the production of inflammatory chemicals that can contribute to dandruff. By reducing inflammation and controlling fungal and bacterial growth, Melaleuca alternifolia tea can help to soothe and calm the scalp.

Regular use of the tea may help to reduce flaking and itching associated with dandruff.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of dried Melaleuca alternifolia leaves.
  2. Heat the water in a pot and bring it to a boil.
  3. Add the dried Melaleuca alternifolia leaves to the boiling water.
  4. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the tea into a cup.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a week to help with dandruff, and apply it to your scalp as a final rinse.

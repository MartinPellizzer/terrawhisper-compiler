8 Herbal Essential Oils For Dandruff

Herbal essential oils can be a natural solution for dandruff.

These herbs are known for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe the scalp and reduce flaking. For example, Melaleuca alternifolia, also known as tea tree oil, has been shown to have antifungal properties that can help combat scalp infections that can cause dandruff.

Rosmarinus officinalis, or rosemary essential oil, can help improve blood circulation to the scalp, which can help promote healthy hair growth and reduce flaking. Using herbal essential oils can bring many benefits to your life. Not only can they help relieve dandruff symptoms, but they can also promote healthy hair growth, improve scalp circulation, and even reduce stress and anxiety. When used in a shampoo or applied directly to the scalp, these oils can be absorbed quickly, providing fast relief from dandruff symptoms.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains bioactive constituents such as cineole, linalool, and terpinen-4-ol.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help to soothe and calm the scalp. Cineole, in particular, has been shown to reduce inflammation and kill bacteria that can contribute to dandruff. Terpinen-4-ol has antifungal properties that help to prevent the growth of yeast on the scalp, which is another common cause of dandruff.

The combination of these properties makes Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils an effective natural treatment for dandruff.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water on low heat for 30 minutes. Then, add the Melaleuca leaves to the water.
  3. Let the mixture simmer for another 30 minutes. You will see the leaves losing their color and the water reducing.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and let it sit for 2 weeks. Shake the bottle every day. This is the essential oil for dandruff.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole and alpha-pinene, which are active constituents that help to reduce dandruff.

The antifungal properties of these compounds fight the yeast that causes scalp infections, leading to dandruff. Cineole also has anti-inflammatory properties, which soothe the scalp and reduce redness. The antimicrobial properties of alpha-pinene help to control the growth of bacteria and fungi on the scalp, further reducing dandruff.

By targeting the root causes of dandruff, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can provide relief from this scalp condition.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a steam distillation kit, and a glass container.
  2. Place 1 cup of water in the steam distillation kit and bring to a boil.
  3. Add 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves to a mesh bag and hang in the steam.
  4. Collect the condensed steam in the glass container for 2 hours, then filter the mixture.
  5. Store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains compounds like carvacrol, camphor, and bornyl acetate that help to soothe and calm the scalp.

The antimicrobial properties of camphor and bornyl acetate reduce the growth of yeast on the scalp, a common cause of dandruff. Carvacrol's anti-inflammatory properties also help to reduce redness and irritation, creating a healthy environment for the scalp. Furthermore, the antifungal properties of camphor and bornyl acetate help to prevent the overgrowth of fungus that can contribute to dandruff.

Regular use of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help to balance the scalp's natural pH, reducing flaking and itchiness associated with dandruff.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 4 cups of water.
  2. Combine the leaves and water in a saucepan. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 30 minutes. Strain the mixture.
  4. Combine 2 cups of the strained liquid with 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil) in a bowl.
  5. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use it as needed for dandruff treatment.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and beta-caryophyllene as its main active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties, which help to soothe and calm the scalp. The antifungal properties of linalool and linalyl acetate inhibit the growth of Malassezia, a fungus that contributes to dandruff development. Linalool's anti-inflammatory properties also reduce redness and irritation associated with dandruff.

By controlling the growth of the fungus and reducing inflammation, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to alleviate dandruff symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat and add the Lavandula flowers. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container. Discard the flowers.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) to the liquid and stir well.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 days in a cool, dark place to allow the essential oil to separate and settle.

5. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, geraniol, and citronellal as its active constituents.

These compounds have antifungal and antibacterial properties, which help control the growth of yeast on the scalp, a common cause of dandruff. The antiseptic properties of geraniol and citronellal also help reduce inflammation and soothe an itchy scalp. Additionally, the antifungal properties of limonene help prevent the buildup of dead skin cells, which can contribute to dandruff.

By controlling yeast growth, reducing inflammation, and preventing dead skin cell buildup, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can help alleviate dandruff symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Cymbopogon citratus leaves, a clean glass jar, and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Wash the Cymbopogon citratus leaves with 1 cup of water to remove dirt. Pat dry with a cloth.
  3. Fill the glass jar with the dried leaves. Pour in 1 cup of carrier oil, like coconut oil. Close the jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a warm, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day. Check the oil's consistency and color.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the dark glass bottle. Discard the solids. Store the oil in a cool, dark place.

6. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-vertin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties that help soothe and calm the scalp. The essential oil's antifungal properties target and eliminate the fungus Malassezia, a common cause of dandruff. The anti-inflammatory properties reduce redness and irritation, creating an environment that's less favorable for dandruff to form.

By reducing the fungus and inflammation, Aloe barbadensis essential oils can help control and prevent dandruff.

  1. Harvest 5-7 leaves of Aloe barbadensis plant. Cut off the lower stem. Remove the thick, green skin.
  2. Cut the inner gel into small pieces. Use 1 cup of the gel in a blender or food processor.
  3. Blend the gel pieces until smooth. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Transfer the strained gel to a bowl. Add 1/2 cup of carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil. Mix well.
  5. Bottle the mixture in dark glass bottles. Store the bottles in a cool, dark place. Use within 6 months.

7. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol and carvacrol, two potent bioactive constituents that help combat dandruff.

Thymol has antifungal properties that reduce the growth of yeast on the scalp, a common cause of dandruff. Carvacrol's anti-inflammatory properties help soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and redness associated with dandruff. The antiseptic properties of thymol also help prevent infections that can worsen dandruff.

By reducing yeast growth and soothing the scalp, Thymus vulgaris essential oils can help alleviate dandruff symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and 2 cups of water. Chop the leaves into small pieces.
  2. Heat the water in a saucepan over low heat. Add the chopped leaves and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Let the liquid cool. Transfer it to a clean glass bottle and store it in the fridge.
  5. To use, mix 5 drops of the essential oil with 1 cup of shampoo or oil. Apply it to your scalp and massage gently. Leave it on for 30 minutes before rinsing.

8. Cymbopogon nardus

Cymbopogon nardus essential oils contains limonene, beta-pinene, and geraniol as its active constituents.

These compounds have antifungal and antibacterial properties that help control the growth of Malassezia, a fungus that contributes to dandruff. The antiseptic properties of geraniol and the antifungal properties of limonene help soothe and calm the scalp, reducing flaking and itching associated with dandruff. The beta-pinene in Cymbopogon nardus essential oils also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce redness and irritation on the scalp.

By controlling the growth of Malassezia and reducing inflammation, Cymbopogon nardus essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of dandruff.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cymbopogon nardus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat and add the Cymbopogon nardus leaves.
  3. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 2-3 hours. This helps release the oils from the leaves.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the oil cool and thicken. Store it in a dark glass bottle and use it as needed for dandruff treatment.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dandruff from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent dandruff.

Some herbal teas, like tea tree oil and peppermint, have antifungal and antibacterial properties that can help soothe itchy scalps. These teas may also help reduce inflammation and promote healthy skin, making them a potential natural remedy for dandruff.

Regular consumption might lead to fewer dandruff flakes.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dandruff every day?

Herbal teas for dandruff can be a good option, but using them every day might not be the best idea.

Some ingredients in these teas can be too strong and cause irritation if used constantly.

It's better to try them for a few days and see how your scalp reacts before making it a regular habit.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dandruff?

Herbal teas can help with dandruff, but it may take a few weeks to notice results.

Some people start to see improvement within 2-3 weeks of regular use, while others may need 4-6 weeks.

The effectiveness can vary depending on the type of herbal tea used and individual skin conditions.

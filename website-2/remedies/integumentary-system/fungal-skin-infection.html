What Are The Main Causes Of Fungal Skin Infection?

Fungal skin infections occur when fungi grow on the skin.

This can happen due to several reasons. One cause is Atopic Dermatitis, also known as eczema. People with Atopic Dermatitis have a weakened skin barrier, making it easy for fungi to penetrate and cause infection.

Another cause is Contact Dermatitis, which is a reaction to certain substances like soap, detergent, or metal. When the skin is irritated, the natural balance of bacteria and fungi on the skin can be disrupted, leading to fungal infections. Eczema, a condition that causes dry, itchy, and inflamed skin, can also make the skin more susceptible to fungal infections. This is because eczema makes the skin more vulnerable to infections, including those caused by fungi.

Poor hygiene, warm and moist environments, and weakened immune systems can also contribute to fungal skin infections.

Below are the main causes of fungal skin infection.

  • Atopic Dermatitis: Atopic dermatitis creates an environment that makes it easy for fungi to grow and cause infection because the skin's natural barrier is weakened, allowing fungi to enter and thrive.
  • Contact Dermatitis: Contact dermatitis can make the skin weak, allowing fungi like ringworm to grow and cause infection because the damaged skin's natural barrier is compromised, allowing fungal spores to enter and multiply.
  • Eczema: Eczema creates broken skin, which makes it easier for fungus to grow and cause infection because fungus thrives in moist environments and can easily enter the body through cracks in the skin.
  • Psoriasis: Psoriasis creates cracks in the skin, which allows fungi to easily enter and cause infections due to the skin's weakened state.
  • Seborrheic Dermatitis: Seborrheic dermatitis is a cause of fungal skin infection because the skin's natural barrier is weakened, allowing the yeast-like fungus Malassezia to multiply and cause an itchy, flaky rash.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Fungal Skin Infection?

If you have a fungal skin infection, you should avoid using certain herbs that can make the infection worse.

For example, Echinacea (Echinacea spp.). can stimulate the immune system too much, making it harder for the skin to fight the fungus. Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) contains berberine, which can also stimulate the immune system and cause the fungus to spread.

Calendula (Calendula officinalis) may seem like a good choice for skin problems, but it can actually make fungal infections worse by breaking down the skin's natural barrier. Sage (Salvia officinalis) and thyme (Thymus spp.). can both be too drying for the skin, making it harder for the fungus to be removed. Comfrey (Symphytum officinale) contains compounds that can increase the risk of infection spreading.

Finally, tea tree oil, which is sometimes derived from the herb Hyptis suaveolens, can be irritating to the skin, making it harder to treat the infection.

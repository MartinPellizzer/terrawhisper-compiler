Inflamed Mouth: Causes, Herbal Preparations, and Medicinal Treatments for Relief

Inflamed mouth is a condition where the tissues in the mouth, such as the gums, lips, or tongue, become red, swollen, and painful.

This can be caused by infection, irritation, or inflammation, and can lead to symptoms like bleeding, bad breath, and difficulty eating or speaking. If left untreated, inflamed mouth can lead to more serious health issues, such as gum disease, tooth loss, or even heart problems. Therefore, it's essential to deal with the condition promptly. Herbal remedies can be effective in treating inflamed mouth due to their anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and soothing properties. What Are The Main Causes Of Inflamed Mouth?

An inflamed mouth can be caused by several issues.

One common cause is Gingivitis, a mild infection of the gums that occurs when plaque, a sticky film of bacteria, builds up on the teeth. If the plaque is not removed, it can cause the gums to become red, swollen, and painful. Another cause is Periodontitis, a more serious infection that affects the tissues and bones supporting the teeth.

This condition can occur when Gingivitis is left untreated and the infection spreads, causing damage to the surrounding tissues and bone. A dental abscess is also a cause of an inflamed mouth. This occurs when bacteria infect a pocket in the gum or the tip of a tooth, causing pus to form and leading to swelling, pain, and inflammation.

A dental abscess can be painful and may require medical attention to drain the pus and treat the underlying infection.

Below are the main causes of inflamed mouth.

  • Gingivitis: Gingivitis is a cause of an inflamed mouth because the bacteria in plaque, a sticky film on teeth, irritate the gums, leading to redness, swelling, and pain.
  • Periodontitis: Periodontitis is a cause of an inflamed mouth because the bacteria in plaque build up under the gum line, causing the gums to become red, swollen, and painful.
  • Dental Abscess: A dental abscess is a painful and inflamed pocket in the mouth that occurs when bacteria infect a tooth or gum, causing swelling, redness, and pus to form.
  • Mouth Ulcers: Mouth ulcers, also known as canker sores, are a common cause of inflamed mouth because they are painful, red, and swollen open sores that can appear inside the mouth and cause discomfort and irritation.
  • Tongue Inflammation: Tongue inflammation, often caused by infections, allergic reactions, or irritation, leads to inflamed mouth because the inflamed tongue's surface can become red, sore, and swollen, causing discomfort and pain in the mouth.
  • Toothache: A toothache is a cause of an inflamed mouth because when a tooth is infected or damaged, it can cause pain, swelling, and inflammation in the surrounding tissues and gums.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Inflamed Mouth?

If you have an inflamed mouth, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can irritate or make the condition worse.

One herb to steer clear of is 'Ginkgo biloba', as its active compounds can cause allergic reactions, including inflammation and mouth sores. Another herb to be cautious with is 'Glycyrrhiza glabra', also known as licorice root, which can cause dry mouth and worsen inflammation due to its high glycyrrhizin content. 'Sassafras albidum' is also not recommended as its main compound, safrole, can cause gum inflammation and mouth ulcers.

'Cinchona officinalis' contains quinine, which can cause mouth sores and other oral issues in some individuals. 'Thuja occidentalis' may also be problematic, as it can cause allergic reactions and worsen inflammation in some people. Lastly, 'Berberis vulgaris' contains berberine, which can cause dry mouth and worsen oral inflammation in some cases.

It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies if you have an inflamed mouth.

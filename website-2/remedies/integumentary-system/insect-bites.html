What Are The Main Causes Of Insect Bites?

Insect bites are caused by different types of insects that feed on human blood.

Mosquitoes bite people to feed on their blood, which contains a protein called hemoglobin. This protein is rich in iron, a vital nutrient for mosquitoes to reproduce. Mosquitoes are attracted to the carbon dioxide, heat, and moisture emitted by humans, making it easier for them to find a target.

Bed bugs bite humans to feed on their blood, which provides them with the necessary nutrients to survive. Bed bugs are attracted to the warmth, heat, and carbon dioxide emitted by humans, especially at night when people are asleep. Fleas bite animals and humans to feed on their blood, which gives them the nutrients they need to reproduce.

Fleas are attracted to the movement, heat, and carbon dioxide emitted by animals and humans, making it easier for them to find a target.

Below are the main causes of insect bites.

  • Mosquito: Mosquitoes bite people because they need the protein from the blood in their bodies to help them lay eggs and keep their young alive.
  • Bed Bug: Bed bugs are a common cause of insect bites because they feed on human blood by biting and sucking it from the skin, causing painful and itchy bites.
  • Flea: Flea bites occur when these tiny insects feed on the blood of animals or people, usually to lay eggs and continue their life cycle.
  • Tick: Tick is a cause of insect bites because it attaches itself to a person's skin and feeds on their blood, injecting a painful bite.
  • Mite: Mites are small insects that bite humans to feed on their blood, causing red, itchy, and painful bumps on the skin.
  • Beetle: Beetles are a common cause of insect bites because their bodies release a chemical called formic acid, which is a natural defense mechanism that can irritate human skin and cause itching, swelling, and pain.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Insect Bites?

If you have an insect bite, there are some herbs you should avoid.

For example, Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana) can make your skin even more sensitive. It can also make the area around the bite red and irritated. Comfrey (Symphytum officinale) is another herb to be careful with. It can increase the risk of scarring and cause more inflammation.

Plantain (Plantago major) might seem like a good idea because it's often used to treat minor cuts and scrapes. However, it can also make insect bites worse by drawing out the venom. Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) can be too harsh for insect bites and may cause more redness and itching. Finally, Echinacea (Echinacea spp.).

is often used to boost the immune system, but it can make your body more sensitive to the venom from insect bites, making them more uncomfortable.

Dealing with hair fall is essential to prevent further hair loss and promote hair growth. Herbal remedies have been used for centuries to address hair fall. Aloe barbadensis, also known as aloe vera, is a popular herb used in hair care products due to its nourishing and soothing properties. When consumed as a decoction or oil, curcumin can help reduce hair fall by promoting blood circulation and reducing oxidative stress. Rosmarinus officinalis, or rosemary, is another herb that can help address hair fall. Capsules can be taken orally to provide a concentrated dose of the herb, while oils can be applied directly to the scalp to stimulate hair growth.

By incorporating these herbal remedies into a hair care routine, individuals can address hair fall and promote hair growth, leading to a healthier and more confident appearance.

What Are The Main Causes Of Hair Fall?

Hair fall can occur due to several reasons.

One main cause is aging. As we age, the growth cycle of hair slows down, and the resting phase increases. This leads to hair falling out more easily and not growing back as quickly. Genetics also play a significant role in hair fall.

If your parents or grandparents experienced hair loss, you may be more likely to experience it as well. This is because genetics determine the shape and size of hair follicles, which can affect how long hair grows and falls out. Hormonal imbalance is another common cause of hair fall. Hormones regulate growth and maintenance of hair, and an imbalance can cause hair to fall out.

For example, hormonal changes during pregnancy or menopause can cause hair to fall out, and thyroid disorders can also lead to hair loss due to hormonal imbalances.

Below are the main causes of hair fall.

  • Aging: As people age, their hair follicles start to weaken, leading to a decrease in hair growth and an increase in hair loss due to a slower rate of cell division and a decrease in the production of hormones that help keep hair healthy.
  • Genetics: Genetics is a cause of hair fall because some people are naturally born with a tendency to inherit a sensitive scalp or thinning hair from their family, making them more prone to hair loss due to hormonal imbalances and other factors.
  • Hormonal imbalance: Hormonal imbalance can cause hair fall because an irregular balance of hormones, such as thyroid hormones and androgens, affects the normal growth cycle of hair, leading to excessive shedding and thinning.
  • Thyroid issues: Thyroid issues cause hair fall because an imbalance of thyroid hormones affects the growth cycle of hair, leading to an increased number of hair follicles entering the resting phase, resulting in excessive shedding and thinning of hair.
  • Hypothyroidism: Hypothyroidism causes hair fall because the thyroid gland is not producing enough hormones, which leads to slow hair growth and weakens the hair follicles, causing them to fall out.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Hair Fall?

If you have hair fall, there are some herbs you should avoid.

Sage (Salvia officinalis) can cause hair fall because it can dry out the scalp and hair follicles. Thyme (Thymus vulgaris) is another herb to avoid, as it can irritate the scalp and cause further hair loss.

Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) is also not recommended, as it can cause allergic reactions and dryness on the scalp. Another herb to be cautious with is Peppermint (Mentha piperita), as it can cool down the scalp too much, leading to hair fall.

Lastly, Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) can also be problematic, as it can dry out the scalp and cause irritation, leading to hair loss.

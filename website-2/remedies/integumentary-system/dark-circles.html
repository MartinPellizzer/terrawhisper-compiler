What Are The Main Causes Of Dark Circles?

Dark circles under the eyes can be caused by several factors.

Genetics play a significant role as some people may naturally have thinner skin under their eyes, making the blood vessels more visible. This can give the appearance of dark circles, even if there's no actual discoloration. Sleep deprivation is another common cause.

When we don't get enough sleep, our body produces less cortisol, a hormone that helps reduce inflammation and swelling. As a result, the skin under our eyes can appear darker and more puffy. Anemia, a condition where the body doesn't have enough red blood cells or hemoglobin, can also cause dark circles.

This is because the body is not getting enough oxygen, leading to a decrease in blood flow and a buildup of waste products under the eyes, which can cause discoloration.

Below are the main causes of dark circles.

  • Genetics: Genetics can cause dark circles under the eyes because some people are born with a thinner layer of skin under their eyes, making the blood vessels underneath more visible, and also because of a darker pigment in the skin that is determined by their genetic makeup.
  • Sleep Deprivation: When you don't get enough sleep, the tiny blood vessels under your eyes can swell and become visible, causing dark circles because the body's ability to circulate and filter out waste is weakened during sleep deprivation.
  • Anemia: Anemia can cause dark circles under the eyes because the body produces less red blood cells, which are necessary to carry oxygen to the skin, leading to a lack of oxygen and a bluish discoloration under the eyes.
  • Lymphedema: Lymphedema can cause dark circles under the eyes because it prevents the normal removal of fluid, leading to a buildup of excess fluid that causes the skin around the eyes to become puffy and discolored.
  • Age: As we age, the skin under our eyes loses its elasticity and the fat that helps to cover up the blood vessels beneath starts to break down, making the dark circles more visible.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help combat tooth decay. Alkylamides, for example, have been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria that contribute to tooth decay, such as Streptococcus mutans. Caffeic acid and rosmarinic acid also have antioxidant properties that can neutralize free radicals that damage tooth enamel.

By reducing bacterial growth and protecting tooth enamel, Echinacea purpurea teas may be a natural way to help prevent tooth decay.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves. Rinse them in a fine mesh strainer under cold running water.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the fresh Echinacea flowers and leaves, and 2 cups of water. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  3. Reduce the heat and let the mixture simmer for 5-7 minutes. This will help release the active ingredients from the Echinacea.
  4. Strain the mixture through a fine mesh strainer into a cup. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the tea cool down. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to taste, if desired. Drink 1-2 cups of the tea, 3 times a day to help prevent tooth decay.

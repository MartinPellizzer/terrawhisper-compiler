What Are The Main Causes Of Grey Hair?

As we age, our hair follicles produce less melanin, the pigment that gives hair its color.

This is why aging is a main cause of grey hair. When we're young, our hair follicles are active and produce plenty of melanin, resulting in dark, vibrant hair. But as we get older, the production of melanin slows down, and the hair grows in grey. Genetics also play a significant role in determining when and how much grey hair we get.

If our parents started going grey at a young age, we're more likely to do the same. This is because genetics affect the production of melanin in our hair follicles, and some people are naturally more prone to grey hair. Hormonal imbalance can also cause grey hair. When our hormone levels change, it can affect the production of melanin in our hair follicles. For example, during menopause, women's hormone levels drop, leading to a decrease in melanin production and an increase in grey hair.

Similarly, thyroid problems can also cause hormonal imbalances that lead to grey hair.

Below are the main causes of grey hair.

  • Aging: As people age, the cells in their hair follicles gradually stop producing the pigment melanin, causing the hair to turn white or grey due to a decrease in the production of melanin.
  • Genetics: Genetics is a cause of grey hair because the genes we inherit from our parents control the amount of melanin, a pigment in our hair, and if our genes slow down the production of melanin, our hair turns grey or white.
  • Hormonal imbalance: Hormonal imbalance can cause grey hair because the lack of melanin, a pigment produced by the thyroid and adrenal glands, leads to the hair follicles producing less color, resulting in the appearance of grey hair.
  • Thyroid issues: Thyroid issues can cause grey hair because an underactive or overactive thyroid gland disrupts the normal production of melanin, the pigment responsible for hair color, leading to the appearance of grey or white hair.
  • Hypothyroidism: Hypothyroidism is a cause of grey hair because the body's reduced ability to produce thyroid hormones slows down the production of melanin, the pigment responsible for hair color, allowing the grey color to appear.

If you have grey hair, there are some herbs to avoid due to their potential effects.

While it's known for its calming properties, it can cause hair to lose its color and turn grey due to its high antioxidant properties.

Ginkgo biloba is also not recommended for people with grey hair. It can cause hair to become brittle and prone to breakage, which can make grey hair appear more noticeable. Additionally, some people may experience hair turning grey faster after taking Ginkgo biloba. Also, avoid using Ginseng, especially in large doses, as it can cause hormonal imbalances that may lead to more grey hair.

Finally, avoid using Holy Basil, as it can cause stress, which is often linked to hair turning grey faster.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contains bioactive constituents like cineole, a compound known for its antimicrobial and antifungal properties.

These properties help combat fungal infections that cause athlete's foot. The tincture's antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties also help soothe itchy and irritated skin. Cineole's ability to penetrate deep into the skin makes it effective in targeting the fungal infection at its source.

Regular application of eucalyptus globulus tinctures may help reduce the symptoms of athlete's foot and promote healing.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves, 2 cups of Vodka (80% or higher) and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine Eucalyptus globulus leaves and Vodka in the glass jar. Make sure the leaves are completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar with the lid and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the leaves.
  5. Label the jar with the date and contents. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 5-10 drops of the tincture in water or on the affected area 2-3 times a day for athlete's foot.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains a compound called gingerols, specifically 6-gingerol and 8-gingerol, which are known for their antifungal properties.

These compounds work by inhibiting the growth of fungal cells, specifically those that cause athlete's foot. The antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties of gingerols also help to soothe and calm the affected skin, reducing irritation and discomfort. The antibacterial properties of gingerols further aid in preventing the growth of bacteria that can exacerbate the condition.

By targeting the root causes of athlete's foot, Zingiber officinale tinctures can provide effective relief from this common fungal infection.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka.
  2. Chop the ginger roots into small pieces and place them in a clean glass jar.
  3. Pour the vodka over the ginger pieces, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the tincture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass bottle, discarding the solids.

What Are The Main Causes Of Shingles?

Shingles is caused by the Varicella-Zoster virus, which is the same virus that causes chickenpox.

When you have chickenpox, the virus can hide in your nervous system, and years later, it can reactivate and cause shingles. This reactivation often happens as you age, as your immune system weakens.

Aging is a major risk factor for shingles, as the immune system's ability to fight off the virus declines with time. A weakened immune system, either due to illness, medication, or a medical condition, also increases your risk of getting shingles.

This is because your body is less able to keep the Varicella-Zoster virus in check, allowing it to reactivate and cause the shingles rash.

Below are the main causes of shingles.

  • Varicella-Zoster: Varicella-Zoster is a cause of shingles because it's the same virus that causes chickenpox, and when it remains in the body after a person has had chickenpox, it can reactivate later in life and cause shingles.
  • Aging: As we age, the immune system's ability to fight off the chickenpox virus, which can cause shingles, weakens, allowing the virus to reactivate later in life and cause shingles.
  • Weakened Immune System: A weakened immune system allows the herpes virus that causes chickenpox to reactivate and cause shingles, as the body's ability to fight off the virus is impaired.
  • Chemo: Chemotherapy weakens the immune system, making it harder for the body to fight off the varicella-zoster virus, which can cause shingles when it reactivates.
  • HIV: People with HIV are more likely to get shingles because their weakened immune system makes it harder for the body to fight off the varicella-zoster virus, which causes shingles, and can lead to reactivation of the virus.
  • Radiation: Radiation can cause shingles by damaging the nerves in the body, making it harder for the virus that causes shingles, called varicella-zoster, to be suppressed, allowing it to reactivate and cause the painful rash.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Shingles?

If you have shingles, it's essential to be cautious when using certain herbs that can interact with your medication or make your condition worse.

One herb to avoid is St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum), as it can reduce the effectiveness of antiviral drugs used to treat shingles. Another herb to avoid is Ginkgo biloba, which can increase the risk of bleeding, a potential complication of shingles.

Feverfew (Tanacetum parthenium) is also not recommended, as it can cause stomach problems, which can be a problem for people with shingles who may already be experiencing nausea and vomiting. Additionally, garlic (Allium sativum) and ginger's cousin, Turmeric (Curcuma longa), can thin the blood, which is not ideal for people with shingles. Finally, Eucalyptus (Eucalyptus globulus) can be irritating to the skin, and people with shingles may already be experiencing skin sensitivity.

It's best to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies when you have shingles.

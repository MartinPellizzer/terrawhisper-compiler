8 Herbal Teas For Fungal Skin Infection

Runners are prone to fungal skin infections due to the constant friction and moisture trapped between their skin and clothing.

This creates a perfect environment for fungi to grow, leading to conditions like athlete's foot and jock itch. These infections can be painful, itchy, and embarrassing, making it difficult for runners to perform at their best. They may experience discomfort, skin irritation, and even blisters, which can force them to take time off from their training. In severe cases, fungal infections can lead to cracked skin, which can become infected and cause even more problems. As a result, runners may feel frustrated, self-conscious, and worried about their health.

To manage these issues, some runners turn to herbal teas as a natural and complementary way to treat their fungal skin infections. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to help soothe and heal the skin. They work by providing antifungal properties, reducing inflammation and irritation, and promoting healthy skin cell growth. Some herbal teas may help to dry out the affected area, prevent fungal spores from spreading, and even speed up the healing process. While they should not replace medical treatment, herbal teas can be a useful addition to a runner's recovery routine. They can be made from a variety of plants and are often easy to incorporate into a daily routine.

Runners can drink them hot or cold, sweetened or unsweetened, to help alleviate their symptoms and promote overall health.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas contains anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties that can help with fungal skin infections, such as athlete's foot and ringworm.

The key constituents responsible for this effect are aloe-emodin, aloin, and acemannan, which have been shown to inhibit the growth of fungi and reduce inflammation. When making aloe barbadensis tea, you can steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried aloe vera leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and cool it before applying it to the affected area.

To use it effectively, soak a cotton ball in the cooled tea and apply it to the infected skin 2-3 times a day, leaving it on for 30 minutes to an hour before rinsing. As you take action, you may start to notice a reduction in redness and itching within a few days, and the fungal infection may begin to clear up within a week or two. However, it's essential to continue using the tea consistently and combine it with good hygiene practices, such as washing your feet daily and changing your socks regularly, to prevent the infection from recurring.

It's also crucial to note that if your symptoms persist or worsen, you should consult a healthcare professional for further guidance and treatment.

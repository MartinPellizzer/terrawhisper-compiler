8 Natural Herbal Teas for Runners to Treat Fungal Skin Infection

Runners are prone to fungal skin infections due to the constant friction and moisture trapped between their skin and clothing.

This creates a perfect environment for fungi to grow, leading to conditions like athlete's foot and jock itch. These infections can be painful, itchy, and embarrassing, making it difficult for runners to perform at their best. They may experience discomfort, skin irritation, and even blisters, which can force them to take time off from their training. In severe cases, fungal infections can lead to cracked skin, which can become infected and cause even more problems. As a result, runners may feel frustrated, self-conscious, and worried about their health.

To manage these issues, some runners turn to herbal teas as a natural and complementary way to treat their fungal skin infections. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to help soothe and heal the skin. They work by providing antifungal properties, reducing inflammation and irritation, and promoting healthy skin cell growth. Some herbal teas may help to dry out the affected area, prevent fungal spores from spreading, and even speed up the healing process. While they should not replace medical treatment, herbal teas can be a useful addition to a runner's recovery routine. They can be made from a variety of plants and are often easy to incorporate into a daily routine.

Runners can drink them hot or cold, sweetened or unsweetened, to help alleviate their symptoms and promote overall health.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas have been traditionally used to help with fungal skin infections, such as ringworm and athlete's foot.

This is because they contain properties and constituents like triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds help to reduce the growth of fungi, soothe irritated skin, and promote healing. When using Calendula officinalis teas for fungal skin infections, it's recommended to make a strong tea by steeping dried flowers in hot water, then applying it directly to the affected area with a clean cloth. You can also add a few drops of tea to your bath water for a soothing soak.

It's essential to note that fungal skin infections can be contagious, so make sure to wash your hands thoroughly after applying the tea and avoid sharing personal items. As you use Calendula officinalis teas, you may start to notice a reduction in redness, itching, and discomfort. The skin may begin to clear up, and the fungal infection may start to heal. However, it's crucial to be patient, as treating fungal skin infections can take time.

You may also need to combine Calendula officinalis teas with other treatments, such as over-the-counter antifungal creams or powders, and consult with a healthcare professional for guidance.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas have been traditionally used to help with fungal skin infections, such as athlete's foot and ringworm.

This is due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which come from its rich content of compounds like alkylamides, glycosides, and caffeic acid. These constituents help reduce redness and swelling, while also inhibiting the growth of fungi and other microorganisms that cause infection. To use Echinacea purpurea teas for fungal skin infections, you can make a tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried Echinacea root in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-10 minutes.

Strain the tea and apply it to the affected area using a clean cloth or gauze. You can also soak your feet in a solution of Echinacea tea and water to help treat athlete's foot. When taking action, it's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea teas may not cure fungal skin infections immediately, but it can help alleviate symptoms and promote healing. You may notice a reduction in redness and swelling within a few days, and the infection may clear up within 1-2 weeks with consistent use.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, you should consult a healthcare professional for further advice.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas have been used for centuries to help with fungal skin infections, such as ringworm and athlete's foot.

The tea's active compounds, including eucalyptol and flavonoids, have antifungal properties that can help reduce the growth of fungal cells on the skin. This is because eucalyptol can disrupt the cell membranes of fungi, making it difficult for them to survive. Flavonoids, on the other hand, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce redness and swelling associated with fungal skin infections. To use Eucalyptus globulus teas to help with fungal skin infections, you can drink 1-2 cups of the tea per day, or apply it topically as a compress to the affected area.

To make a compress, steep a teaspoon of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then let it cool before applying it to the skin. You can also add a few drops of tea tree oil to the compress for added antifungal benefits. As you take action to use Eucalyptus globulus teas, you can expect to see a reduction in the severity of your symptoms within a few days to a week. The antifungal properties of the tea can help clear up the infection, and the anti-inflammatory properties can reduce redness and swelling. In some cases, the tea may also help to prevent the spread of the infection to other parts of the body.

However, it's essential to note that Eucalyptus globulus teas may not be effective for severe or chronic fungal skin infections, and you should consult a healthcare professional if your symptoms persist or worsen.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas have been traditionally used to help soothe and treat fungal skin infections.

The reason behind this is that the tea contains a variety of properties and constituents that have antifungal and anti-inflammatory effects. Aloe vera, the main ingredient in Aloe barbadensis teas, contains compounds like aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan, which have been shown to inhibit the growth of fungi and reduce inflammation. As you use Aloe barbadensis teas to treat your fungal skin infection, you may start to notice improvements in your symptoms within a few days. The inflammation and redness may subside, and the itching and discomfort may decrease. However, it's essential to note that Aloe barbadensis teas may not be a substitute for medical treatment.

Melaleuca alternifolia teas, also known as tea tree oil tea, can help with fungal skin infections like athlete's foot, ringworm, and fungal acne.

When you drink tea tree oil tea, these compounds are absorbed into your bloodstream and then into your skin, where they can help to reduce fungal infections and promote healing. You can also add the tea to a warm bath for a relaxing soak. When you use the tea, look for signs of improvement, such as reduced redness, itching, and scaling. You may also notice a decrease in the size and severity of the fungal infection. The likely outcomes from taking this action include a reduction in symptoms and a decrease in the severity of the fungal infection. In some cases, the fungal infection may clear up completely.

However, it's essential to note that Melaleuca alternifolia teas may not work as well for severe fungal infections, and you should always consult with a healthcare professional before using it as a treatment.

8. Ocimum basilicum

Ocimum basilicum teas have been traditionally used to help manage fungal skin infections, such as athlete's foot and ringworm, due to their antifungal properties.

The tea contains constituents like eugenol, linalool, and ursolic acid, which have been shown to inhibit the growth of fungi and reduce inflammation. If you're experiencing a fungal skin infection, try making a tea by steeping dried Ocimum basilicum leaves in hot water. Strain the leaves and apply the cooled tea directly to the affected area using a clean cloth. You can also add a few drops of eugenol oil to the tea for enhanced antifungal effects.

It's essential to note that while Ocimum basilicum teas may provide relief, they should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment. Consult a doctor or dermatologist before using the tea, especially if your infection is severe or persistent. When using Ocimum basilicum teas to treat fungal skin infections, you may start to notice improvements within a few days to a week. The tea may help reduce redness, itching, and inflammation, allowing the skin to heal more quickly. However, it's crucial to continue using the tea consistently and in combination with good hygiene practices, such as washing your feet regularly and changing your socks daily, to prevent the infection from recurring.

As you continue to use the tea, you may find that the symptoms of your fungal skin infection gradually subside, allowing you to resume your normal activities.

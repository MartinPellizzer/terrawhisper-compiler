1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contains a high concentration of cineole, a bioactive compound that has antifungal and antibacterial properties.

Cineole helps to reduce inflammation and promote a healthy environment for skin recovery. The tannins and flavonoids present in Eucalyptus globulus tinctures have antiseptic and antifungal properties, which help to combat fungal infections. The antifungal properties of Eucalyptus globulus tinctures also help to prevent the growth of fungi and other microorganisms that can cause skin infections.

By applying Eucalyptus globulus tinctures topically, you can help to create an environment that is less conducive to fungal growth and promote the healing of skin infections.

  1. Gather 250ml of 80% Vodka and 50g of Eucalyptus globulus leaves in a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Eucalyptus globulus leaves and Vodka in the jar. Make sure the leaves are completely covered by the liquid.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks. Shake the jar every day to help the ingredients mix.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the Eucalyptus globulus leaves.
  5. Label the jar with the date and the contents. Store the Eucalyptus globulus tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops, 3 times a day, on affected skin after diluting with water.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains flavonoids, triterpene saponins, and carotenoids, which are responsible for its antifungal properties.

The flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to inhibit the growth of fungal cells, reducing the severity of fungal skin infections. The triterpene saponins, such as oleanolic acid, have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help to soothe and heal the affected skin. The carotenoids, including lutein and beta-carotene, have antioxidant properties that help to protect the skin from further damage and promote healing.

By using Calendula officinalis tinctures, the antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties can help to reduce the symptoms of fungal skin infections, such as redness, itching, and inflammation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Calendula officinalis flowers and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the Calendula flowers to the jar and pour in the vodka, making sure the flowers are completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, to help treat fungal skin infections.

What Are The Main Causes Of Toothache?

A toothache is usually caused by one of three main issues: a cavity, an abscess, or gum disease.

A cavity is a hole in the tooth that forms when bacteria in your mouth break down food, especially sugar, and create acid. This acid eats away at the tooth's hard outer layer, called enamel, and can eventually reach the soft inner layer, called dentin. When the dentin is exposed, it can cause pain, especially when eating or drinking something hot or cold.

An abscess is a pocket of pus that forms when bacteria infect the tooth or gums. This infection can cause pain, swelling, and redness in the face and gums. Gum disease, also known as gingivitis, occurs when bacteria in the mouth cause inflammation and infection of the gums. This can lead to pain, bleeding, and bad breath.

If gum disease is not treated, it can progress to a more serious condition called periodontitis, which can cause the gums to pull away from the teeth, exposing the roots and leading to pain and eventually tooth loss.

Below are the main causes of toothache.

  • Cavity: Cavity is a cause of toothache because bacteria in the mouth break down food and create acid that damages the tooth's hard outer layer, leading to pain and sensitivity.
  • Abscess: An abscess is a painful pocket of pus that forms on or around a tooth due to a bacterial infection, causing severe toothache pain.
  • Gum disease: Gum disease is a cause of toothache because the infection in the gums can spread to the roots of the teeth and cause painful inflammation that leads to a toothache.
  • Tooth fracture: Tooth fracture is a cause of toothache because the broken tooth can irritate the nerves inside, sending pain signals to the brain, which can cause a sharp and constant toothache.
  • Tooth root decay: Tooth root decay is a cause of toothache because the bacteria in the decay eat away the protective layer on the tooth root, causing pain and sensitivity.
  • Tooth grinding: Tooth grinding, or bruxism, can cause toothache because the constant pressure and friction from grinding your teeth can wear down the enamel, leading to tooth sensitivity and pain.
  • Bruxism: Bruxism is a cause of toothache because the habit of grinding or clenching teeth can lead to worn-down enamel and irritated nerves in the teeth, resulting in pain and discomfort.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Toothache?

If you have a toothache, there are some herbs you should avoid using.

One of these is 'Hydrastis canadensis', also known as goldenseal. It contains a compound called berberine, which can make tooth decay worse. Another herb to avoid is 'Ephedra sinica', or ephedra.

This herb can cause heart problems and high blood pressure, which can be a concern if you have a toothache. 'Ginkgo biloba' is also not recommended, as it can cause bleeding problems, including bleeding gums. 'Panax ginseng' is another herb to avoid, as it can cause mouth dryness, which can make your toothache worse. 'Cinchona officinalis', or cinchona bark, contains quinine, which can be toxic if taken in high doses, especially if you have a toothache.

It's best to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies when you have a toothache.

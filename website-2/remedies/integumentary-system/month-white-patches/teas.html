Breastfeeding and White Patches in Month: 10 Herbal Teas to the Rescue

Breastfeeding mothers can suffer from white patches on their mouth, a condition known as oral thrush.

This happens when a fungus called Candida grows in their mouth, causing pain, soreness, and discomfort. This can be a challenge for breastfeeding mothers as it affects their ability to breastfeed their babies, leading to anxiety and stress. It can also be painful to eat, drink, and even speak. This can impact their daily lives, making simple tasks like feeding their baby or preparing meals a struggle. The white patches can be itchy and may bleed when they scrape against their teeth or gums.

This can be distressing, especially when they have a newborn baby relying on them for nourishment. Some mothers may experience fever, sore throat, and difficulty swallowing due to the infection. If left untreated, oral thrush can spread to the baby's mouth, leading to similar symptoms. To treat oral thrush, mothers can try using herbal teas. These teas have antifungal properties that help combat the growth of Candida in the mouth. They can be used as a natural remedy to soothe the affected area and promote healing.

By drinking these teas, breastfeeding mothers can help reduce the symptoms of oral thrush and create a healthier environment for themselves and their baby.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas have been traditionally used to help with white patches in the mouth, also known as oral thrush.

This is because the tea contains properties and constituents such as carotenoids, flavonoids, and triterpenoid saponins, which have antifungal and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds help to combat the overgrowth of Candida yeast, which causes the white patches. If you're experiencing white patches in your mouth, try drinking Calendula officinalis tea 2-3 times a day for 1-2 weeks. You can also use a mouthwash or gargle with the tea to help soothe and disinfect your mouth.

To make the tea, steep 1 teaspoon of dried Calendula flowers in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink. When you take action, you may notice a reduction in the size and number of white patches within a few days to a week. Your mouth may also feel more comfortable and less itchy. However, it's essential to note that Calendula officinalis tea is not a replacement for medical treatment.

If your symptoms persist or worsen, consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

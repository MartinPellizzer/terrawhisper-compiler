White Patches in Mouth Got You Worried? 9 Herbal Tinctures for New Moms

New mothers often experience white patches on their skin, known as vitiligo or leukoderma, especially on their mouth, lips, and nose.

This condition can be caused by hormonal changes during pregnancy and childbirth, as well as the stress of caring for a newborn. The white patches can be unsightly and affect a new mother's self-confidence and emotional well-being. They may feel embarrassed or self-conscious about their appearance, especially when interacting with their baby or loved ones. This can lead to feelings of anxiety and isolation, making it harder for them to enjoy this special time with their child. Furthermore, the condition can be itchy and painful, especially if the skin becomes cracked or bleeding.

The white patches can also be a sign of an underlying immune system issue or a thyroid disorder, which can be a concern for new mothers who are already under stress. They may need to see a doctor or dermatologist to determine the cause and get proper treatment. However, some new mothers have found relief from herbal tinctures, which are liquid extracts made from plants. These tinctures contain active compounds that can help to repigment the skin and reduce inflammation. They can be applied topically to the affected areas, or taken internally to support the body's natural healing processes.

The tinctures work by stimulating the production of melanin, the pigment that gives skin its color, and reducing oxidative stress and inflammation that can contribute to the condition.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures can be a helpful natural remedy for white patches in the mouth.

The tincture is made from the flowers of the Calendula officinalis plant, which contains properties such as flavonoids, carotenoids, and triterpenoid saponins. These constituents have antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which can help to combat the fungal infection that causes oral thrush, a common cause of white patches in the mouth. The tincture can help to reduce inflammation, prevent the growth of fungal cells, and promote healing in the affected area. To use Calendula officinalis tinctures for white patches in the mouth, you can add a few drops to a glass of water and swish it around your mouth before spitting it out.

Repeat this process several times a day, ideally after meals. You can also apply the tincture directly to the affected area using a cotton swab, but make sure to dilute it with water first to avoid irritating your mouth. It's also essential to practice good oral hygiene, such as brushing your teeth regularly and rinsing your mouth with saltwater to help reduce the risk of fungal infections. Using Calendula officinalis tinctures regularly may help to reduce the appearance and size of white patches in your mouth, promote healing, and prevent the spread of infection. However, it's crucial to note that this tincture is not a substitute for medical treatment.

If you have a severe case of oral thrush or other underlying health conditions, you should consult with your healthcare provider before using Calendula officinalis tinctures.

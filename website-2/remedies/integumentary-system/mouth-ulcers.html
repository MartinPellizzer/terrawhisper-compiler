Causes, Herbal Solutions, and Preparations for Mouth Ulcers

What are the causes of mouth ulcers?

Mouth ulcers can be quite painful and frustrating.

So, what causes them?. One common cause is stress. When we're under stress, our body's immune system can become weak, making us more prone to getting mouth ulcers. This is because stress can disrupt the balance of good bacteria in our mouth, leading to inflammation and ulcers. Another cause is celiac disease, a condition where our body reacts badly to gluten. If you have celiac disease, eating gluten can cause inflammation in your mouth, leading to ulcers. It's essential to follow a gluten-free diet if you have this condition.

Some medications can also cause mouth ulcers as a side effect. These medications can disrupt the normal functioning of our body, leading to ulcers. Certain antibiotics, painkillers, and anti-inflammatory medicines are known to cause mouth ulcers in some people. Genetic predisposition is another factor that can contribute to mouth ulcers. If your family members have a history of mouth ulcers, you're more likely to get them too. This is because some people may be born with a weaker immune system or a genetic condition that makes them more prone to ulcers. Lastly, hormonal imbalance can also cause mouth ulcers. Hormonal changes during menstruation, pregnancy, or menopause can lead to mouth ulcers in some women.

This is because hormonal fluctuations can affect the balance of good bacteria in the mouth, leading to inflammation and ulcers.

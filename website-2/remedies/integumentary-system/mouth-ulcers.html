What Are The Main Causes Of Mouth Ulcers?

Mouth ulcers can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is viral infections, such as herpes simplex virus (HSV) and hand, foot, and mouth disease. These viruses can infect the skin and cause painful blisters and ulcers inside the mouth. Another cause is hormonal changes, which can occur during menstruation, pregnancy, or menopause.

Fluctuations in hormone levels can lead to inflammation and ulcers in the mouth. Canker sores, also known as aphthous ulcers, are small, shallow ulcers that appear on the inside of the mouth, usually on the cheeks, lips, or tongue. They can be caused by a combination of factors, including stress, food triggers such as spicy or acidic foods, and minor mouth injuries.

These causes can lead to discomfort, pain, and difficulty eating and speaking.

Below are the main causes of mouth ulcers.

  • Viral infections: Viral infections, such as herpes simplex, can cause mouth ulcers because the viruses infect the skin cells inside the mouth, leading to inflammation, swelling, and the formation of painful sores.
  • Hormonal changes: Hormonal changes can cause mouth ulcers because fluctuations in hormone levels can affect the body's immune system, making it more likely for small sores to form inside the mouth.
  • Canker sores: Canker sores, also known as mouth ulcers, are caused by tiny cracks in the lining of the mouth, usually triggered by stress, eating spicy or acidic foods, or minor injuries, which can make the mouth tissue inflamed and painful.
  • Stress: When a person is under a lot of stress, their body's natural defense system can get overwhelmed, leading to inflammation in the mouth and the formation of painful mouth ulcers.
  • Nutritional deficiencies: Nutritional deficiencies, such as low levels of vitamin B12, iron, or zinc, can cause mouth ulcers because the body's inability to get enough of these essential nutrients can lead to inflammation and damage to the mucous membranes in the mouth, resulting in painful ulcers.
  • Medication side effects: Medication side effects can cause mouth ulcers because some medications can irritate the mouth and gums, leading to inflammation and the formation of painful sores.
  • Gingivitis: Gingivitis, a common gum infection, can cause mouth ulcers by irritating the gums and making them inflamed, which can then lead to the formation of painful sores on the inner lining of the mouth.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Mouth Ulcers?

If you have mouth ulcers, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can irritate your mouth or make your ulcers worse.

Turmeric, for example, contains a compound called curcumin that can be too harsh on sensitive mouth tissues. Ginger, while known for its anti-inflammatory properties, can also cause irritation and discomfort in the mouth.

Slippery elm, although often used to soothe digestive issues, can cause stomach upset and worsen mouth ulcers. Clove, which is commonly used to relieve toothache pain, contains a compound called eugenol that can be too strong for mouth ulcers. Sage, which is often used to improve oral health, can dry out the mouth and make ulcers worse.

Finally, licorice root, which is known for its anti-inflammatory properties, can cause an imbalance of hormones in the body, leading to increased inflammation in the mouth and worsening ulcers.

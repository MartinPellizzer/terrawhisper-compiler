What Are The Main Causes Of Dermatitis?

Dermatitis is a group of skin conditions that cause redness, itching, and swelling.

There are several main causes of dermatitis. One cause is Atopic Dermatitis, also known as atopic eczema. This is a long-term condition that makes the skin sensitive and prone to irritation. It's often linked to family history and can be triggered by allergies, stress, and certain foods.

Another cause is Contact Dermatitis, which occurs when the skin comes into contact with an irritant or allergen. This can be anything from soap or detergent to certain metals like nickel or poison ivy. When the skin touches an irritant, it causes an immune response, leading to inflammation and symptoms like redness, itching, and blistering. Eczema is a type of dermatitis that's often used interchangeably with atopic dermatitis. However, eczema can also refer to other types of dermatitis, including contact dermatitis.

In this sense, eczema is a broader term that encompasses different causes and symptoms.

Below are the main causes of dermatitis.

  • Atopic Dermatitis: Atopic Dermatitis is a cause of dermatitis because it is a chronic skin condition that makes the skin very sensitive and easily irritated, leading to inflammation and redness.
  • Contact Dermatitis: Contact Dermatitis is a cause of dermatitis because it occurs when the skin comes into contact with an irritant or allergen, such as a soap, chemical, or plant, that causes inflammation and redness.
  • Eczema: Eczema is a cause of dermatitis because it is an inflammation of the skin that leads to redness, itching, and swelling, which are all symptoms of dermatitis.
  • Psoriasis: Psoriasis is a cause of dermatitis because the skin's natural barrier is weakened, allowing irritants and allergens to penetrate and trigger an inflammatory response that causes red, itchy, and scaly patches to form.
  • Seborrheic Dermatitis: Seborrheic Dermatitis is a cause of dermatitis because it's a skin condition that causes red, itchy, and flaky patches due to an overproduction of skin cells and oil, leading to inflammation and irritation of the skin.
  • Irritant Contact Dermatitis: Irritant Contact Dermatitis is a cause of dermatitis because it occurs when harsh or irritating substances, such as strong chemicals, come into contact with the skin and damage its natural barrier, leading to redness, inflammation, and painful skin irritation.
  • Allergic Contact Dermatitis: Allergic Contact Dermatitis is a cause of dermatitis because the skin reacts badly to something it has touched, such as a chemical or a plant, and becomes inflamed and itchy.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Dermatitis?

If you have dermatitis, some herbs can irritate your skin and make it worse.

You should avoid herbs like Echinacea purpurea, as it can cause skin sensitivity and allergic reactions. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) and Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) may also be too potent for people with dermatitis, as they can cause skin irritation and increase sensitivity.

St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) contains hypericin, which can make skin more sensitive and increase the risk of sunburn. Sassafras (Sassafras albidum) root bark and Birch (Betula species) sap contain compounds that can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions. Finally, Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) root contains berberine, which can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions, especially in people with sensitive skin.

These herbs should be used with caution or avoided if you have dermatitis.

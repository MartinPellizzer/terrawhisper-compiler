1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains bioactive constituents like triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce redness and swelling associated with dermatitis. The tincture also contains saponins, which have antiseptic properties that can help prevent infection and promote healing in the affected skin. Additionally, calendula's high antioxidant content can protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting a healthy skin environment.

By applying calendula officinalis tinctures to the skin, the bioactive constituents can work together to soothe and calm the skin, reducing the symptoms of dermatitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula flowers and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine 1 cup of dried Calendula flowers with 2 cups of vodka in the glass jar. Close the jar.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in the fridge and use 5-10 drops, 2-3 times a day, to soothe dermatitis symptoms.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis tinctures contains aloe-emodin, aloin, and acemannan.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can help calm skin irritations associated with dermatitis. Aloe-emodin and aloin have been shown to reduce redness and swelling, while acemannan promotes wound healing and tissue repair. The anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds can also help reduce skin itching and discomfort.

The combination of these bioactive constituents in aloe barbadensis tinctures can help provide relief from dermatitis symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis leaves and 1 cup of 80% vodka or glycerin in a clean glass jar.
  2. Wash the Aloe leaves with water and dry them with a clean cloth.
  3. Add the dried Aloe leaves to the glass jar with the vodka or glycerin. Make sure the leaves are completely covered.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-6 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will help the Aloe release its active ingredients into the vodka or glycerin.
  5. After the infusion period, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the Aloe leaves and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

3. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica tinctures contains bioactive constituents such as histamine, quercetin, and polysaccharides, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help to reduce redness and swelling associated with dermatitis. Quercetin, a flavonoid present in the tincture, has been shown to inhibit the production of inflammatory mediators that contribute to skin inflammation. The polysaccharides in Urtica dioica tincture may also help to soothe and calm the skin, reducing itching and discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Urtica dioica tinctures can help to alleviate the symptoms of dermatitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Urtica dioica leaves, stems, and flowers.
  2. Combine the Urtica dioica plant material with 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass jar.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Urtica dioica tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, to help with dermatitis symptoms.

4. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum tinctures contains hyperforin and hypericin, two key bioactive constituents that help reduce inflammation and itching associated with dermatitis.

These compounds have potent anti-inflammatory properties, which can soothe and calm the skin, reducing redness and swelling. Hyperforin also has antihistamine-like effects, which can help alleviate allergic reactions and skin irritation. Additionally, hypericum perforatum tinctures contain flavonoids, such as quercetin, which have antioxidant properties that can help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals.

By using hypericum perforatum tinctures, individuals with dermatitis may experience relief from symptoms such as itching, redness, and inflammation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum flowers and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Fill the jar with the fresh flowers, leaving about 1 inch of space at the top.
  3. Pour the vodka over the flowers, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

5. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The alkylamides, specifically cis- and trans-11-alkylamides, help to reduce inflammation and soothe the skin. The glycosides, particularly echinacoside, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals.

By reducing inflammation and promoting skin health, Echinacea purpurea tinctures may be beneficial for people suffering from dermatitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and 2 cups of vodka (at least 80 proof).
  2. Combine the dried flowers and vodka in a clean glass jar. Stir well to make sure the flowers are covered.
  3. Place the jar in a dark place and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This is called 'steeping'.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, for skin issues like dermatitis.

6. Hamamelis virginiana

Hamamelis virginiana tinctures contains salicylic acid, a key bioactive constituent that helps reduce inflammation and soothe skin irritations associated with dermatitis.

The anti-inflammatory properties of salicylic acid make it effective in calming red, itchy skin and promoting healing. Hamamelis virginiana also contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have antioxidant properties that help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals. The astringent properties of hamamelis help reduce swelling and ease skin conditions such as eczema and dermatitis by tightening the skin and reducing the amount of moisture that can exacerbate the condition.

By using hamamelis virginiana tinctures, individuals with dermatitis can experience relief from symptoms and promote healthy skin regeneration.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hamamelis virginiana leaves and stems. Clean them with water.
  2. Use a blender or food processor to chop the leaves and stems into small pieces.
  3. Transfer the chopped plant material to a clean glass jar and add 4 cups of 80-proof vodka. Close the jar.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the plant to infuse into the vodka.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the plant material. Use the tincture as directed for dermatitis relief.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dermatitis from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe skin irritations like dermatitis.

These teas may reduce redness and swelling, making them a potential natural remedy for dermatitis.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dermatitis every day?

While herbal teas may help soothe dermatitis symptoms, drinking them every day can be safe or not, depending on the tea and your body.

Some teas like chamomile or calendula are generally considered gentle and may be a good choice.

However, stronger or more potent teas like dandelion or burdock root can cause issues if consumed excessively.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dermatitis?

Herbal teas for dermatitis relief might start working within a few days to a week.

Some people notice improvement in their skin symptoms, like reduced redness and itching, within 2-3 days.

However, it may take up to 2 weeks to see significant results, as herbal teas work slowly to soothe and calm the skin.

What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have dermatitis?

If you have dermatitis, some herbal decoctions to avoid include those made from St. John's Wort (Hypericum species), Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis), and Goldthread (Coptis chinensis).

These herbs can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions, making dermatitis worse. St. John's Wort, for example, contains compounds that can trigger skin sensitivity and increase inflammation. Goldenseal and Goldthread contain berberine, a chemical that can cause skin irritation and worsen dermatitis symptoms.

Additionally, decoctions made from Sassafras (Sassafras albidum), Wintergreen (Gaultheria procumbens), and Birch (Betula species) should be avoided due to their high methyl salicylate content, which can cause skin irritation and increase inflammation. Other herbal decoctions to be cautious with include those made from Turmeric (Curcuma longa), Ginger (Zingiber officinale), and Cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum), as they can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals. Turmeric and Ginger contain compounds that can cause skin redness and inflammation, while Cinnamon can cause skin irritation and burns in some cases.

If you have dermatitis, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies, as they can interact with medications or worsen symptoms.

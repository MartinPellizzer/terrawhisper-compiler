10 Herbal Essential Oils For Oily Face

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties can help reduce the appearance of pores and calm irritated skin, making it a good option for oily faces. Linalool, in particular, has been shown to have a balancing effect on the skin's pH levels, which can help regulate oil production. The antiseptic properties of linalyl acetate can also help to reduce the growth of bacteria that can contribute to oily skin.

By reducing inflammation and regulating oil production, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to keep oily faces under control.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the flowers. Let it simmer for 2 hours, then turn off the heat.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 4 hours. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of a carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) to the liquid and mix well.
  5. Store the Lavandula angustifolia essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole and globulol as its primary bioactive constituents.

These compounds have astringent and antiseptic properties, which help to reduce the appearance of pores and prevent oil buildup on the skin. Cineole also has a cooling effect, which can help to calm and soothe the skin, reducing inflammation and redness associated with oily skin. The antiseptic properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils help to prevent the growth of bacteria that can clog pores and cause acne.

By controlling oil production and preventing bacterial growth, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help to regulate the skin's natural balance and reduce the appearance of oily skin.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Combine the Eucalyptus leaves and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Make sure the leaves are completely covered by the oil.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the oil to extract from the leaves.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 2-3 drops on your oily face as a toner to balance your skin's pH.

3. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are beneficial for oily skin.

Triterpenoids help to regulate sebum production, reducing oiliness and preventing clogged pores. Flavonoids, particularly quercetin, possess anti-inflammatory properties that soothe redness and irritation often associated with oily skin. Carotenoids, such as beta-carotene, have antioxidant properties that protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, which can exacerbate skin issues.

By balancing skin pH and reducing inflammation, Calendula officinalis essential oils can help control oil production and promote a healthier, more balanced complexion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula flowers and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil). Use a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Calendula flowers and carrier oil in the jar. Cover the jar with a lid.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the Calendula essential oil to a small, dark glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops as a face oil for oily skin.

4. Aloe vera

Aloe vera essential oils contains bioactive compounds like aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-annone.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that help soothe and calm oily skin. Aloe vera also contains salicylic acid, which gently exfoliates the skin, removing dead skin cells and unclogging pores. The gel-like texture of aloe vera essential oils helps to balance the skin's pH, reducing oil production and minimizing the appearance of pores.

By using aloe vera essential oils, you can reduce the appearance of acne, blackheads, and other oily skin problems.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe vera gel from a mature Aloe vera plant. Cut off the yellow sap and scoop the clear gel.
  2. Add 10 drops of Lavender essential oil and 5 drops of Tea tree essential oil to the Aloe vera gel. Mix well.
  3. Transfer the mixture to a clean glass bottle. Use a funnel to avoid spills.
  4. Store the bottle in a cool, dark place. Shake well before use.
  5. Use 2-3 drops of the Aloe vera essential oil on your face 2 times a day to control oil production and soothe your skin.

5. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol and carvacrol as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce the bacteria and inflammation causing oily skin. Thymol also has astringent properties, which can help tighten pores and reduce oil production. Carvacrol has antioxidant properties that can help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to oily skin.

By reducing bacteria, inflammation, and free radicals, Thymus vulgaris essential oils can help promote a healthy balance of skin oils.

  1. Gather materials: 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves, a glass jar, a clean cloth, and a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil.
  2. Wash the Thymus vulgaris leaves with cold water, then gently pat them dry with a clean cloth.
  3. Place the Thymus vulgaris leaves in the glass jar and cover them with carrier oil. The oil level should be about 2 inches above the leaves.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day to help the oil extract.
  5. After the steeping period, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids and use the Thymus vulgaris essential oil for your face care.

6. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains a high percentage of camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate, which are powerful compounds that help to regulate oil production in the skin.

These active constituents have a drying effect on the skin, reducing the appearance of oily pores and controlling sebum production. The antiseptic properties of rosmarinic acid in Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils also help to prevent bacterial growth, which can contribute to acne and other skin problems. Additionally, the astringent properties of camphor and borneol help to tighten the skin and reduce the appearance of pores, making Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils an effective natural remedy for oily skin.

By balancing skin pH and regulating oil production, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help to achieve a healthier, more balanced complexion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves, 2 cups of water, a glass jar, a cheesecloth, a bowl, and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Wash the leaves with 1 cup of water, then dry them with a paper towel.
  3. Combine the dried leaves with 2 cups of water in the glass jar. Cover the jar with a cheesecloth.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. Then, strain the liquid through the cheesecloth into the bowl.
  5. Transfer the liquid to the dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops of the oil on your oily face 2-3 times a week.

7. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains a high concentration of compounds called cineole and terpineol.

These bioactive constituents have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce the appearance of oily skin. Cineole, in particular, is known to balance the skin's pH levels and reduce the production of sebum, a natural oil that can contribute to oily skin. Terpineol helps to regulate the skin's natural moisture barrier, preventing excess oil from building up on the surface.

By controlling sebum production and maintaining a healthy skin balance, Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils can be a helpful natural solution for oily skin.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Melaleuca alternifolia leaves, a clean glass jar, a sharp knife, and a cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or coconut oil)
  2. Wash the leaves with water, and dry them with a towel. Then, chop the leaves into small pieces and add them to the glass jar
  3. Pour 1 cup of carrier oil over the chopped leaves in the jar. Make sure the leaves are fully covered by the oil
  4. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will allow the oil to infuse
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the solids and use the infused oil as a natural remedy for oily face

8. Geranium robertianum

Geranium robertianum essential oils contains rosmarinic acid, caffeic acid, and linalool as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce the production of sebum in the skin. Linalool, in particular, has a balancing effect on the skin's pH levels, which can become imbalanced due to excess oil production. The anti-inflammatory properties of rosmarinic and caffeic acids also help to soothe and calm the skin, reducing redness and irritation caused by oily skin.

By regulating sebum production and calming the skin, Geranium robertianum essential oils can help to control oily skin and promote a more balanced complexion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Geranium robertianum flowers, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine 1/4 cup of Geranium robertianum flowers with 1/2 cup of carrier oil in the glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the essential oil in a cool, dark place and use 5-7 drops for an oily face treatment.

9. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, geraniol, and linalool as its active constituents.

These compounds have antiseptic and astringent properties that help control oil production in the skin, reducing the appearance of oily skin. The astringent properties of geraniol and linalool also help to tighten pores, giving the skin a smoother appearance. Additionally, limonene's antiseptic properties help to prevent the growth of bacteria that can lead to breakouts and oiliness.

By balancing the skin's natural oil production and preventing bacterial growth, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can help to regulate oily skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat until it starts boiling.
  3. Add the Cymbopogon citratus leaves to the boiling water and reduce heat to low.
  4. Steep the leaves for 2-3 hours, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  5. Transfer the strained liquid to a glass bottle and let it sit at room temperature for 24 hours to allow the essential oil to separate.

10. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains biologically active constituents like aloe-emodin, aloin, and vitamins A and E. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that help reduce redness and irritation often associated with oily skin.

The essential oil also contains salicylic acid, a beta-hydroxy acid that exfoliates and unclogs pores, helping to control oil production. Additionally, aloe-emodin has been shown to inhibit the production of sebum, the oily substance produced by the skin, which can help regulate oily skin.

The anti-inflammatory properties of aloe barbadensis essential oils also help calm and reduce the appearance of acne and other skin irritations often linked to oily skin.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Aloe barbadensis leaves and 1 cup of water in a blender.
  2. Blend the Aloe leaves and water until smooth. Strain the mixture into a bowl.
  3. Add 1/4 cup of jojoba oil to the Aloe mixture and stir well.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a saucepan and heat it on low for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Let the mixture cool and then store it in a glass bottle. Use 2-3 drops on your face daily to control oiliness.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent oily face from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help reduce oily skin.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, have properties that can help balance your skin's natural oils. These teas can also help calm and soothe your skin, making it less likely to produce excess oil.

Regular consumption may lead to a more balanced skin tone.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for oily face every day?

Consuming herbal teas for an oily face daily can be beneficial, but in moderation.

Some teas, like peppermint and chamomile, can help balance skin oil. However, drinking too much tea daily may cause dehydration, which can worsen oily skin.

It's best to drink herbal tea occasionally and stay hydrated with water.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in oily face?

Herbal teas can help balance skin oil, but it takes time.

Noticeable results usually appear after 2-4 weeks of regular consumption. This is because the body needs time to absorb the tea's active ingredients and respond.

Be patient and consistent with your herbal tea routine, and you may start to see a reduction in oiliness.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for oily face?

Drinking herbal tea in the evening can help with an oily face.

This is because many herbal teas have properties that help reduce oil production and calm the skin.

As you get ready for bed, a warm cup of herbal tea can help soothe and balance your skin while you sleep, making it a great time to unwind and recharge.

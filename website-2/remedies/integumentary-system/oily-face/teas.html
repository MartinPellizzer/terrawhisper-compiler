Teenage Skin Struggles: 7 Herbal Teas for Oily Faces

As teenagers grow, their skin can become a challenge to deal with.

During puberty, the body starts producing more hormones, which can cause the oil glands to work overtime. This leads to an oily face, which can be frustrating and affect a teenager's self-confidence. An oily face can lead to acne, blackheads, and other skin problems, making it hard for teenagers to feel comfortable in their own skin. They might avoid social events, hide their face, or feel self-conscious about their appearance.

This can even affect their mental health and relationships with friends and family. The extra oil can also make their skin look dull and dirty, even after washing it. But there's some good news - herbal teas can help. These teas have natural ingredients that can help balance the skin's oil production, reduce inflammation, and promote healthy skin. They can be consumed hot or cold, and some can even be applied topically to the skin.

By drinking herbal teas, teenagers can start to see improvements in their skin, and feel more confident and comfortable in their own skin.

1. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus teas, also known as lemon grass tea, has been used for centuries to help combat oily skin.

The tea contains properties such as citral, limonene, and geraniol, which have natural astringent and antiseptic properties. These constituents help to balance the skin's natural pH, reduce sebum production, and prevent bacterial growth that can lead to clogged pores and acne. To use Cymbopogon citratus teas for an oily face, you can make a tea by steeping fresh or dried lemon grass in hot water for 5-7 minutes.

Then, let it cool down and use it as a toner by applying it to your face with a cotton pad. You can also add it to your bath water for a relaxing and cleansing soak. When you start using Cymbopogon citratus teas, you may notice a reduction in oil production and a clearer complexion within a few days. Your skin may feel smoother and more balanced, and you may experience a decrease in acne breakouts.

However, it's essential to note that individual results may vary, and it's recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before making any significant changes to your skincare routine.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas have been used for centuries to help control oily skin.

The gel from the aloe plant contains a compound called aloin, which has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce redness and irritation caused by excess oil. The tea also contains vitamins A, C, and E, which act as antioxidants, protecting the skin from damage caused by free radicals. Moreover, the tea has antibacterial properties, which can help control the growth of bacteria that can contribute to oily skin. To use aloe barbadensis teas for an oily face, start by brewing a cup of tea using dried aloe vera leaves. Steep the leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then let it cool. Strain the tea and use it as a toner by applying it to your face with a cotton pad.

You can also drink the tea to help from the inside out. Start with a small amount and gradually increase as needed. When you start using aloe barbadensis teas, you may notice a reduction in oil production, as your skin becomes more balanced. Your pores may appear smaller, and your skin may look smoother and more even-toned. You may also notice a decrease in acne and other skin irritations. However, it's essential to note that results may vary, and it may take some time to see the full effects of the tea.

Be patient, and give your skin time to adjust.

3. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis teas, the source of green, black, white, and oolong teas, have been shown to help with oily faces due to their unique properties.

The main constituent, catechin, is a powerful antioxidant that helps to reduce inflammation in the skin, which can contribute to oil production. Catechin also has astringent properties, which can help to tighten pores and reduce the appearance of oily skin. Additionally, Camellia sinensis teas contain tannins, which can help to balance the skin's natural pH and reduce excess oil production.

To take advantage of these benefits, you can try drinking a cup of Camellia sinensis tea daily, either hot or cold, sweetened or unsweetened, as you prefer. You can also use cooled tea as a toner or add it to your bath water for a relaxing and skin-nourishing experience. For best results, look for teas that are high in catechin and tannin content, such as green tea or white tea. As you drink the tea and apply it to your skin, you may notice a reduction in oil production, a clearer complexion, and a more balanced skin tone.

Your pores may appear smaller, and your skin may feel smoother and more refreshed.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas, also known as rosemary tea, is a natural remedy that can help with oily face issues.

This is due to its high content of antioxidants, including carnosic acid, rosmarinic acid, and ursolic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds help reduce sebum production, fighting acne-causing bacteria and soothing irritated skin. The essential oil present in rosemary tea also has astringent properties, which help tighten pores and reduce oiliness. If you're looking to try rosemary tea for an oily face, start by steeping one teaspoon of dried rosemary leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and let it cool before applying it to your face using a cotton pad. You can also drink rosemary tea as a supplement to help reduce sebum production from the inside out. However, be cautious not to over-consume, as excessive consumption can cause skin irritation. As you incorporate rosemary tea into your skincare routine, you can expect to see a reduction in oiliness and a decrease in acne breakouts. Your pores may appear smaller, and your skin may feel smoother and more balanced.

However, keep in mind that individual results may vary, and it's essential to maintain a consistent skincare routine and use other natural remedies in conjunction with rosemary tea for optimal results.

Drink the tea once or twice a day, and consider using it as a face wash or toner as well. You can also add a few drops of the tea to your bath water for a relaxing and skin-soothing soak. As you begin to drink the tea and use it on your skin, you may notice a reduction in oiliness and a clearer complexion. Melaleuca alternifolia teas, also known as tea tree oil tea, can be helpful in reducing oily skin due to its active properties and constituents.

The tea contains compounds like terpinen-4-ol, cineole, and limonene, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. When consumed, Melaleuca alternifolia tea may help to balance the skin's natural oil production, leading to a reduction in oiliness and a clearer complexion. To get the most out of Melaleuca alternifolia tea for an oily face, it's recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase the amount as needed.

You can try drinking 1-2 cups of tea per day, made by steeping 1 teaspoon of dried tea leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. For better results, it's also suggested to combine the tea with a healthy lifestyle, including a balanced diet, regular exercise, and proper skin care routine. Drinking Melaleuca alternifolia tea regularly may lead to a noticeable reduction in oiliness, as well as a decrease in acne and other skin problems. Your skin may look smoother, with a more even tone, and you may notice a decrease in blackheads and whiteheads.

However, it's essential to remember that individual results may vary, and it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before making any changes to your diet or skincare routine.

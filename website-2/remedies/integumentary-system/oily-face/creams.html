1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-juice, which are rich in vitamins and minerals.

These components help to regulate the skin's natural oil production, reducing the appearance of oily skin. The anti-inflammatory properties of aloin and aloe-emodin soothe the skin, reducing redness and irritation associated with oily skin. Aloe barbadensis also contains salicylic acid, which exfoliates the skin, unclogging pores and preventing oil buildup.

By balancing the skin's natural pH and oil production, aloe barbadensis creams can help to control oily skin and promote a healthier complexion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of aloe vera gel, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of jojoba oil, and 2 teaspoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Combine the distilled water and aloe vera gel in a small saucepan. Heat over low heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture is warm and well combined.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for a few minutes. Add the coconut oil, jojoba oil, and vitamin E oil to the mixture. Stir until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into a clean glass jar or container. Let it cool to room temperature before using. Store in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.
  5. To use, apply a small amount to your oily face and massage it in. Use as needed throughout the day to control oil production and soothe the skin.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains active constituents like cineole, terpinen-4-ol, and limonene.

These compounds have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties that can help control oily skin. Cineole and terpinen-4-ol reduce the growth of bacteria that can cause acne, while limonene helps to dry up excess oil. Terpinen-4-ol also has a soothing effect that can calm redness and irritation.

By regulating oil production and reducing inflammation, Melaleuca alternifolia creams can help to balance the skin's natural moisture levels.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a silicone mold or a small tin container and let cool and harden before use.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains the active constituents linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce the production of sebum, the oily substance produced by the skin, thereby controlling oiliness. Additionally, the calming effect of linalool and linalyl acetate can soothe irritated skin, reducing redness and inflammation associated with oily skin. Lavandula angustifolia creams also contain antioxidants, such as beta-caryophyllene, which help to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting a balanced and healthy complexion.

By reducing inflammation and excess sebum production, Lavandula angustifolia creams can help to regulate oily skin and promote a more balanced skin tone.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup distilled water, 1/4 cup coconut oil, 1/4 cup shea butter, 2 tablespoons beeswax, 10 drops lavender essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Once melted, remove the bowl from the heat and let it cool slightly. Then, slowly add the distilled water while stirring constantly.
  4. Continue stirring until the mixture thickens and reaches a creamy consistency. This may take about 10-15 minutes.
  5. Add the lavender essential oil and stir well. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let it cool before using on your oily face.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains essential oils rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds.

The main active constituents in these creams are borneol, bornyl acetate, and camphor, which have antibacterial and antifungal properties. These properties help to regulate the skin's natural pH balance, reduce oil production, and prevent clogged pores. The astringent properties of rosmarinic acid also help to tighten the skin and reduce the appearance of pores, making it an effective treatment for oily skin.

By balancing the skin's natural oil production and reducing inflammation, Rosmarinus officinalis creams can provide relief from oily face and promote a healthier complexion.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1/2 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 2 teaspoons of rosemary essential oil.
  2. Melt shea butter, coconut oil, and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in olive oil and rosemary essential oil until well combined.
  4. Let the mixture cool and thicken for about 10-15 minutes or until it reaches a creamy consistency.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container and let it cool completely before use. Use on oily face as a moisturizer.

5. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds work to reduce inflammation and calm the skin, making it an effective solution for oily faces. The anti-inflammatory properties of calendula help to soothe redness and irritation, while its antiseptic properties help to control oil production and prevent breakouts. The carotenoids in calendula also have antioxidant properties, which help to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals and promote a balanced skin tone.

By reducing inflammation and controlling oil production, calendula creams can help to regulate the skin's natural oil balance and leave oily faces looking clearer and more even.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Steep the Calendula flowers in 1 cup of boiling water for 10-15 minutes, then strain and let cool.
  3. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  4. Add the cooled Calendula infusion, shea butter, and vitamin E oil to the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture. Stir until well combined.
  5. Remove from heat and let cool until the mixture thickens. Pour into a jar and store in a cool, dark place.

6. Glycyrrhiza glabra

By regulating oil production and soothing the skin, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams provide a natural solution for managing oily skin.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of distilled water, 1/4 cup of glycerin, 2 tablespoons of glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, 2 tablespoons of aloe vera gel, and 2 tablespoons of jojoba oil.
  2. Combine distilled water and glycerin in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat until the glycerin dissolves.
  3. Add glycyrrhiza glabra root powder to the saucepan and stir well. Remove from heat and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture and discard the solids. Add aloe vera gel and jojoba oil. Mix well until combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container. Let it cool and thicken before using it as a face cream for oily skin.

  3. Add Cymbopogon citratus essential oil and vitamin E oil to the bowl. Stir well to mix.

By reducing oil production and preventing bacterial growth, Thymus vulgaris creams can help regulate oily skin and prevent associated problems.

Saponaria officinalis creams contains saponins and flavonoids as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds help to balance the skin's natural pH and reduce the production of sebum, making it a good option for oily faces. The saponins in Saponaria officinalis creams have astringent properties, which help to tighten the pores and prevent them from getting clogged with excess oil.   2. Steep Saponaria officinalis flowers in coconut oil for 2 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the mixture every day.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discard the flowers.
  4. Melt beeswax and shea butter in a double boiler or in a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth.
  5. Combine the infused coconut oil, melted beeswax mixture, and vitamin E oil in a bowl, stir until well combined and pour into a container to cool and solidify.

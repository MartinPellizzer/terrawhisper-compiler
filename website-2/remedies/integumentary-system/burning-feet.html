Causes and Remedies of Burning Feet: Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

What are the causes of burning feet?

There are several reasons why people experience burning feet.

One common cause is diabetes. When you have diabetes, high blood sugar levels can damage the nerves in your feet, causing numbness, tingling, and burning sensations. This is because diabetes can make it hard for your nerves to send signals properly. Another cause is vitamin deficiency, particularly a lack of vitamin B12. This vitamin is essential for the health of your nerves and the production of red blood cells. Without enough vitamin B12, you may experience burning sensations in your feet, as well as fatigue and weakness.

Nerve damage, often caused by conditions like multiple sclerosis or peripheral neuropathy, can also lead to burning feet. When the nerves in your feet are damaged, they can't send signals properly, causing pain and discomfort. Foot infections, such as fungal infections like athlete's foot, can also cause burning sensations in the feet. These infections can cause redness, itching, and swelling, making your feet feel like they're on fire. Finally, Raynaud's disease can cause burning feet due to poor blood circulation. In this condition, the blood vessels in your feet constrict or narrow, reducing blood flow and causing pain and numbness.

When the blood vessels widen again, it can cause a burning sensation in your feet.

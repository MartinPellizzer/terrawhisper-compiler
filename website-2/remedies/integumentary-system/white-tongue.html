What are the causes of white tongue?

There are several reasons why you might develop a white tongue.

One cause is Bacterial Overgrowth, which happens when there's an imbalance of good and bad bacteria in the mouth. This can lead to an overgrowth of bad bacteria, causing the tongue to become white and coated. Poor Oral Hygiene is another reason. If you don't brush and floss regularly, bacteria and debris can build up on your tongue and cause it to turn white.

Oral Thrush is a fungal infection that can cause white patches on the tongue. This infection is usually caused by a yeast called candida, which can multiply and cause problems if it's not kept under control. Fungal Infections, similar to oral thrush, can also cause a white tongue. This can be triggered by a weak immune system, antibiotics, or other medications that can disrupt the balance of good and bad bacteria in the mouth. Smoking is another cause of a white tongue.

When you smoke, you're exposing your mouth to toxic chemicals that can damage the mucous membranes and cause irritation, leading to a white, coated tongue.

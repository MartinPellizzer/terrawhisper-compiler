What Are The Main Causes Of Dry Hair?

Dry hair can be caused by several factors.

Overwashing your hair can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness. This happens because the shampoo removes not only dirt but also the healthy oils produced by your scalp. When these oils are gone, your hair becomes dry and brittle. Heat styling can also dry out your hair.

Using heat tools like flat irons or curling irons can damage the hair cuticle, causing it to lose moisture. The heat breaks down the proteins in your hair, making it more prone to breakage and dryness. Dry air is another main cause of dry hair. Low humidity in the air means that the moisture in your hair is easily lost. This can happen in dry climates or during the winter months when the air is cold and dry.

When the air is dry, your hair absorbs moisture from the air, leading to dryness and brittleness.

Below are the main causes of dry hair.

  • Overwashing: Overwashing your hair removes its natural oils, which are needed to keep your hair healthy and moisturized, leading to dry, brittle, and damaged hair.
  • Heat Styling: Heat styling causes dry hair because the high heat damages the outer layer of the hair, breaking down the natural moisture barrier and causing the hair to lose its moisture and become brittle.
  • Dry Air: Dry air lacks moisture, which causes the hair cuticles to dry out, become brittle, and break easily.
  • Harsh Shampoo: Harsh shampoo can cause dry hair because it strips the hair of its natural oils, leading to a loss of moisture and causing the hair to become brittle and fragile.
  • Sulfates: Sulfates remove the natural oils from hair, leading to dryness and making it prone to breakage.
  • Chemical Treatments: Chemical treatments can cause dry hair because harsh chemicals strip away the natural oils and moisture from the hair, leading to dryness and damage.
  • Genetics: Genetics can cause dry hair because it affects the shape and size of hair follicles, influencing the thickness and texture of hair, which can lead to dryness and brittleness.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Dry Hair?

If you have dry hair, it's a good idea to avoid certain herbs that can further dry out your locks.

One herb to watch out for is Burdock root (Arctium lappa), which can strip your hair of its natural oils. Another herb to be cautious with is Calendula officinalis, also known as pot marigold, as it can be quite drying and may cause your hair to become brittle. Sage (Salvia officinalis) and Echinacea are also herbs to approach with care, as they can be drying and may not provide the moisture your hair needs.

Witch hazel (Hamamelis virginiana) is another herb to use with caution, as it can be quite astringent and may cause your hair to become even drier than it already is.

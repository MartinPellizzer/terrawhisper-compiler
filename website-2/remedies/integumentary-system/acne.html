What Are The Main Causes Of Acne?

There are several main causes of acne.

Hormones play a significant role, as changes in hormone levels during puberty, menstruation, pregnancy, or menopause can cause oil glands in the skin to produce more oil, leading to clogged pores and acne. Bacteria, specifically Propionibacterium acnes (P. acnes), are naturally present on the skin and feed on the excess oil, causing inflammation and acne. Dead skin cells can also clog pores, especially when combined with excess oil.

When dead skin cells and oil mix together, they create a perfect environment for P. acnes to thrive, leading to the formation of acne.

Below are the main causes of acne.

  • Hormones: Hormonal changes cause an increase in oil production, leading to clogged pores and inflammation, resulting in acne.
  • Bacteria: Bacteria on the skin, especially a type called Propionibacterium acnes, can cause acne by producing waste and irritating the skin, leading to inflammation and clogged pores.
  • Dead Skin: Dead skin cells can clog pores and cause acne when they mix with oil from the skin and other dirt, blocking the pores and allowing bacteria to grow.
  • Genetics: Genetics can cause acne because people inherit special genes from their parents that affect how their skin reacts to hormones, leading to clogged pores and breakouts.
  • Stress: When we're stressed, our body releases hormones like cortisol, which increase oil production in our skin, causing clogged pores and acne to form.
  • Medications: Medications can cause acne because some medicines, such as corticosteroids and testosterone, increase the amount of oil in the skin, clog pores, and lead to inflammation and breakouts.
  • Sugars: Consuming high amounts of sugar triggers an increase in insulin levels, which can lead to inflammation in the body and clog pores, ultimately causing acne.

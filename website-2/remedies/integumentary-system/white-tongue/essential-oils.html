1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole, a bioactive constituent with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce bacteria and inflammation that can cause white tongue, a condition characterized by a thick, white coating on the tongue. Cineole's decongestant properties also help to open airways and improve circulation, which can aid in removing toxins and bacteria that contribute to white tongue. Additionally, eucalyptus globulus essential oils has a natural antimicrobial effect, which can help to eliminate the bacteria that cause bad breath and other oral health issues associated with white tongue.

When used as part of a oral care routine, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help to promote a healthy mouth and reduce the appearance of white tongue.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a clean glass jar with a lid, and a cheese cloth.
  2. Wash the Eucalyptus leaves with 1 cup of water to remove dirt, then pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Fill the glass jar with the Eucalyptus leaves and cover it with the cheese cloth. Secure the lid.
  4. Place the jar in a warm, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it gently every day to release the oils.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid into a clean glass bottle using the cheese cloth. Your Eucalyptus globulus essential oil is now ready to use.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling and inflammation in the mouth and throat, which is often associated with a white tongue. The antioxidants present in ginger essential oil, such as gingerol and shogaol, also help to combat free radicals that can cause oral infections and irritations. Additionally, the antibacterial properties of ginger essential oil help to eliminate bacteria that can contribute to a white tongue.

The anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils work together to promote a healthy oral environment and alleviate symptoms of a white tongue.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale (Ginger) roots. Wash them clean with water.
  2. Dry the ginger roots in the sun for 2-3 days or use a dehydrator on low heat.
  3. Peel the dried ginger roots and grind them into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  4. Place 1 cup of the ginger powder in a heat-proof cup. Add 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) and 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil.
  5. Heat the mixture in a double boiler at 150°F to 180°F (65°C to 82°C) for 2-3 hours. Strain and bottle the essential oil.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains active constituents like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help soothe the oral mucosa and reduce redness. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea essential oils have been shown to stimulate the immune system and increase the production of white blood cells, which can help combat infections that cause white tongue. The glycosides in this essential oil have antifungal and antibacterial properties that help eliminate fungal and bacterial overgrowth in the oral cavity, which can contribute to white tongue.

By reducing inflammation and eliminating infections, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help promote healthy oral mucosa and resolve white tongue issues.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers. Clean and dry them.
  2. Use a glass jar and add 2 cups of flowers. Cover the jar with a paper towel.
  3. Place the jar in a dark, dry area. Leave for 2 weeks to allow the flowers to dry completely.
  4. Use a cheesecloth or a coffee filter to separate the dried flowers from the leaves. Collect the leaves.
  5. Use a steam distiller to extract the essential oil from the dried flowers. Collect 2 ounces of the oil in a dark glass bottle.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains carnosic acid, rosmarinic acid, and camphor as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help combat the bacteria and inflammation often associated with a white tongue. Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils also has antioxidant properties, which can help reduce oxidative stress and promote overall oral health. The antimicrobial properties of camphor and rosmarinic acid can help inhibit the growth of bacteria that can cause a white tongue, while carnosic acid's anti-inflammatory properties can reduce swelling and irritation.

By applying Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils to the affected area, you can potentially reduce the appearance of a white tongue and promote a healthier oral environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat until it starts to simmer. Place a lid on the saucepan.
  3. Reduce heat to minimum and let it infuse for 2 hours. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba oil) to the bowl and mix well. Store in a dark glass bottle.
  5. Use 5-7 drops of the essential oil in a diffuser or apply it to the tongue with a cotton swab, 2-3 times a day.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains licorice root extracts with active constituents like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help reduce inflammation and combat bacterial and fungal infections in the mouth. The essential oil's antiseptic properties can help soothe and protect the mucous membranes, promoting a healthy oral environment. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the growth of Candida albicans, a common cause of oral thrush and white tongue.

By reducing inflammation and infection, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can help promote a clear and healthy tongue.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots, a saucepan, and a still or distillation apparatus.
  2. Combine 1 cup of dried roots with 2 cups of water in the saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 10 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean container.
  4. Collect the liquid from the strainer and transfer it to the still or distillation apparatus. Distill the liquid using steam or heat.
  5. Collect the essential oil that separates from the liquid during distillation. Store the essential oil in a clean, dark glass bottle.

6. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains boiactive constituents like linalool and citronellal, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce the growth of bacteria and other microorganisms that can cause a white tongue. The antiseptic properties of Melissa officinalis essential oils also help to prevent infections and promote healing in the mouth. Additionally, Melissa officinalis essential oils has a soothing effect on the mucous membranes, which can help to reduce irritation and inflammation in the mouth.

By reducing bacteria and inflammation, Melissa officinalis essential oils can help to promote a healthy tongue and mouth.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis leaves, 1 cup of water, a saucepan, and a steam distillation kit.
  2. Wash the Melissa leaves with cold water, then dry them completely.
  3. Add the dried Melissa leaves to the steam distillation kit, and pour in the water.
  4. Heat the water to 100°C (212°F) and collect the steam, then condense it back into liquid form.
  5. Filter the liquid through a cheesecloth into a glass container, and store the Melissa officinalis essential oil in a cool, dark place.

7. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum essential oils contains silymarin, a bioactive compound that has anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and irritation in the mouth, which can cause a white tongue. Silybum marianum also contains flavonoids and polyphenols, which have antioxidant properties that fight off free radicals that can damage the tongue's mucous membrane. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Silybum marianum essential oils may help to soothe and heal the tongue, promoting a healthy oral environment.

The sesquiterpenes present in Silybum marianum essential oils also have antimicrobial properties, which can help prevent infections that can cause a white tongue.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Silybum marianum flowers and leaves. Clean and dry them.
  2. Use a juicer or blender to extract the oil from the flowers and leaves. You'll need 1 cup of oil.
  3. Combine the extracted oil with 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil). Mix well.
  4. Transfer the oil mixture to a glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place.
  5. To use the essential oil, add 2-3 drops to a cotton swab and gently rub it onto the white tongue. Repeat as needed.

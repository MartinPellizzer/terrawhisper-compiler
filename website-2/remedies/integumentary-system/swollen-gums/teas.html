What herbal teas you should avoid if you have swollen gums?

If you have swollen gums, it's best to avoid certain herbal teas that can irritate or worsen the condition.

One such tea is Peppermint, as its menthol content can cause the blood vessels in the gums to dilate, leading to increased swelling. Ginger tea, although generally considered safe, can be too spicy for sensitive gums, causing discomfort and pain.

Yarrow tea is another herb to be cautious with, as its high tannin content can dry out the gums, making them more prone to irritation and infection. Chamomile tea, although soothing for the digestive system, can cause allergic reactions in some people, including mouth inflammation and gum irritation. Sage tea is also not recommended, as its high concentration of essential oils can cause the gums to become more inflamed and painful.

Finally, Rosehip tea, while rich in vitamins and minerals, can be too astringent for swollen gums, potentially causing them to become drier and more irritated.

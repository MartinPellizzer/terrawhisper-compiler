1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains bioactive compounds like alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling in gums. Alkylamides in particular have been found to inhibit the production of inflammatory chemicals, such as prostaglandins, that contribute to gum inflammation. Caffeic acid derivatives have antioxidant properties, which help protect gum tissues from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and protecting gum tissues, Echinacea purpurea creams may be beneficial for swollen gums.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of dried Echinacea purpurea roots, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of essential oil (optional).
  2. Dry the Echinacea roots in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 2 hours. Allow them to cool completely.
  3. In a double boiler, melt the coconut oil and beeswax over low heat. Stir until the beeswax dissolves.
  4. Add the dried Echinacea roots to the melted oil mixture and simmer for 30 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  5. Add the vitamin E oil and essential oil (if using) to the mixture and stir well. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify before use.

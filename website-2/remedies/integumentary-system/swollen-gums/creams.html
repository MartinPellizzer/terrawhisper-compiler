What herbal creams you should avoid if you have swollen gums?

If you have swollen gums, it's best to avoid herbal creams that can irritate or inflame the area further.

One such herb to avoid is 'Capsicum annuum', which contains capsaicin, a compound that can increase blood flow and heat in the affected area, leading to more pain and discomfort. Another herb to be cautious with is 'Cinchona officinalis', which contains quinine, a compound that can cause allergic reactions and increase blood pressure, potentially worsening swollen gums.

'Glycyrrhiza glabra', also known as licorice root, should be used with caution as it can raise blood pressure and worsen gum inflammation. Similarly, 'Panax quinquefolius', or American ginseng, can increase blood pressure and worsen gum inflammation when used in excess. 'Urtica dioica', or stinging nettle, can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions, especially if you have sensitive skin.

'Valeriana officinalis', or valerian root, can interact with certain medications and worsen gum inflammation when used in combination with other herbs.

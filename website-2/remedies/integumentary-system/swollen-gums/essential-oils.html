1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like eucalyptol and alpha-pinene, which are known for their anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and pain in swollen gums. Eucalyptol, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals, which contribute to gum inflammation. The antimicrobial properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils also help combat the bacteria that can cause gum infections and inflammation.

By reducing inflammation and fighting infection, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help alleviate swollen gums.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat and add the Eucalyptus leaves. Let it simmer for 30-40 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a bowl using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a clean glass bottle and let it cool. Use 10-15 drops of the essential oil in a carrier oil like coconut oil for gum treatment.
  5. Store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a cool, dark place and use it within 6 months.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides and caffeic acid, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and inflammation in the gums, making it a good remedy for swollen gums. The caffeic acid in Echinacea purpurea essential oils also has antioxidant properties, which help protect the gums from damage caused by free radicals. The antiseptic properties of Echinacea purpurea essential oils help combat bacterial infections that can cause gum swelling.

By using Echinacea purpurea essential oils, you may be able to reduce the swelling and inflammation of your gums naturally.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and 2 cups of carrier oil (such as coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and use the remaining liquid as Echinacea purpurea essential oil for swollen gums. Store in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

3. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains a compound called thujone, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling.

This oil also has tannins, which are astringent and can help tighten gum tissues, reducing swelling and discomfort. Additionally, Salvia officinalis essential oils contain flavonoids, such as rosmarinic acid, that have antioxidant properties, which can help reduce inflammation and promote healing. The antiseptic properties of the oil, due to the presence of triterpenoids and saponins, can also help prevent infections that can cause swollen gums.

When used properly, Salvia officinalis essential oils can be an effective natural remedy for swollen gums, especially when combined with good oral hygiene practices.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan. Heat the water over low heat.
  2. Add a tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba or coconut oil) to a small bowl.
  3. Pour the infused water from the saucepan into a glass jar. Let it cool for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid into the bowl with carrier oil using a coffee filter or cheesecloth. Discard the solids.
  5. Label and store the Salvia officinalis essential oil in a dark glass bottle. Use 2-3 drops as needed for swollen gums.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the gums.

By reducing inflammation and pain, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help alleviate the discomfort associated with swollen gums.

Regular use of this essential oil may lead to a reduction in swelling and improve oral health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (Ginger) roots, 1 cup of water, and a steam distillation kit.
  2. Wash the ginger roots with water and chop them into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped ginger with 1 cup of water in the steam distillation kit.

  5. Collect the essential oil that separates from the water and store it in a dark glass bottle.

5. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains a high concentration of compounds like cineole and terpinen-4-ol, which are known for their antimicrobial properties.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and phenolic acids.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling. The alkylamides, particularly cis-9-kaur-16-enylisobutyramide, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals, such as prostaglandins and leukotrienes. This can lead to a decrease in swelling and inflammation in the gums.

The anti-inflammatory properties of Echinacea purpurea tinctures may be beneficial in addressing swollen gums caused by infection or inflammation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Echinacea purpurea roots and flowers.
  2. Combine the roots and flowers in a clean glass jar.
  3. Add 2 cups of 80% vodka to the jar. The liquid should cover the Echinacea.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.

What Are The Main Causes Of Red Eyes?

Red eyes can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is dry eye, which occurs when the eyes don't produce enough tears or the tears are of poor quality. This can happen due to various reasons such as using computers or phones for long periods, not blinking enough, or having certain medical conditions like diabetes or thyroid problems. Another cause of red eyes is allergies.

When you're allergic to something like pollen, dust, or pet dander, your eyes can become inflamed and itchy. This happens because your body releases histamine to fight off the allergen, causing blood vessels to dilate and making your eyes appear red. Conjunctivitis, also known as pink eye, is a highly contagious infection that causes redness and irritation in the eyes. It can be caused by a virus, bacteria, or an allergic reaction.

The infection leads to inflammation and increased blood flow to the affected area, resulting in red eyes.

Below are the main causes of red eyes.

  • Dry eye: Dry eye occurs when the tears in your eyes don't have enough moisture or the right balance of water, oil, and mucous, leading to irritation and redness.
  • Allergies: When you're allergic to something, your eyes release more histamine to fight it off, which causes the tiny blood vessels in your eyes to swell and get irritated, leading to red eyes.
  • Conjunctivitis: Conjunctivitis is a cause of red eyes because the tiny blood vessels in the eye's white area get swollen and inflamed, causing the eye to appear red and sore.
  • Sinusitis: Sinusitis is a cause of red eyes because the infection in the sinuses can cause inflammation and swelling of the eyelids and the blood vessels around the eyes, leading to redness and irritation.
  • Irritation: Irritation of the eyes, often caused by dust, pollen, or strong chemicals, leads to red eyes because the eyes produce more blood to fight off the irritant, causing the blood vessels to swell and turn the white part of the eye red.
  • Tears: When we cry, the salt in our tears irritates the white part of our eye, causing it to turn red and inflamed.

Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) tea may seem soothing, but it can cause an allergic reaction in some people, including those with red eyes. It can also cause the eyes to become more sensitive to light.

Ginger (Zingiber officinale) is another herb to be careful with. It can cause the eyes to burn and become red even more, especially if used in large quantities. Some people may also experience an itchy sensation in the eyes when using ginger. Turmeric (Curcuma longa) contains a yellow pigment called curcumin, which can irritate the eyes and make redness worse.

It is also a blood thinner, which may not be good for people with eye problems that need a stable blood pressure.

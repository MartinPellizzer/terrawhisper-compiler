Running on Healed Feet: 9 Herbal Creams for Cracked Heels

Runners often suffer from cracked heels due to repeated stress and friction on their feet.

With each step, the skin on their heels is stretched and irritated, leading to dryness and eventually cracks. This can be painful and uncomfortable, making it difficult for runners to perform their daily activities, let alone their runs. The cracks can also be prone to infection, which can further complicate the issue. As a result, runners may need to take time off from running to allow their heels to heal, which can be frustrating for those who rely on regular exercise to stay healthy and motivated. The discomfort and pain from cracked heels can also affect a runner's mental state, making them feel demotivated and anxious about their ability to continue running.

Additionally, the constant need to treat and care for their cracked heels can be time-consuming and expensive. This can be especially challenging for runners who are on a tight training schedule and need to focus on their physical performance rather than their foot care. To treat cracked heels, some runners turn to herbal creams. These creams work by providing intense moisture to the affected area, helping to soothe and calm the skin. The natural ingredients in the creams help to repair and regenerate the skin, reducing the appearance of cracks and promoting healthy skin growth.

By using herbal creams regularly, runners can help to prevent future cracks from forming and keep their heels healthy and strong.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams are a natural remedy for cracked heels.

The reason they help is because of their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which come from constituents like triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids. These properties help to soothe and calm irritated skin, reduce redness and swelling, and fight off infections that can cause heels to crack.

To use calendula creams for cracked heels, gently massage a small amount onto the affected area 2-3 times a day. Be sure to moisturize your heels thoroughly before applying the cream, and wear socks to lock in the moisture. As you continue to use the cream, you should start to see improvements in the condition of your heels - the cracks should begin to close, and the skin should feel softer and more supple.

You may also notice a reduction in pain and discomfort, making it easier to walk and engage in daily activities.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams are a popular remedy for cracked heels, and for good reason.

The gel from the aloe vera plant contains properties like vitamins A, C, and E, as well as minerals like calcium and potassium. These constituents help to lock in moisture, reduce inflammation, and promote skin regeneration. When applied to cracked heels, the cream creates a protective barrier that shields the skin from further damage and allows it to heal naturally. To use Aloe barbadensis cream for cracked heels, start by washing your feet thoroughly and drying them completely. Then, apply a generous amount of cream to the affected area, making sure to cover the cracks completely.

You can do this once or twice a day, depending on the severity of your condition. It's also a good idea to exfoliate your heels gently with a pumice stone or foot file to remove dead skin cells. As you continue to apply the cream and exfoliate your heels, you should start to notice improvements within a few days to a week. The cracks should begin to fade, and the skin should become smoother and more supple. In some cases, you may even notice a reduction in pain and discomfort.

With consistent use and proper care, you can help to heal and prevent cracked heels from returning.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams are a popular remedy for cracked heels, and for good reason.

The root of the licorice plant, Glycyrrhiza glabra, contains properties like glycyrrhizic acid, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds that help to lock in moisture and soothe dry, cracked skin. When applied to the heels, these creams create a protective barrier that shields the skin from further dryness and irritation. Glycyrrhizic acid in particular has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce redness and swelling, while also promoting the growth of new skin cells.

To get the most out of a Glycyrrhiza glabra cream for cracked heels, apply a thick layer to the affected area after bathing or showering, while the skin is still damp. Gently massage the cream into the skin, paying special attention to any areas of thickening or cracking. As the cream works to lock in moisture and promote healing, you can expect to see improvements in the texture and appearance of your skin within a few days to a week. The skin should become softer and more supple, with a reduction in the appearance of cracks and fissures.

With regular use, the cream can also help to prevent future dryness and cracking, keeping your heels looking and feeling their best.

4. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams are a popular remedy for cracked heels.

They contain tea tree oil, which has antimicrobial and antifungal properties that help combat infections and promote healing in dry, cracked skin. The cream's main constituent, melaleuca alternifolia leaf extract, has anti-inflammatory properties that soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing redness and discomfort. To use Melaleuca alternifolia creams for cracked heels, apply a thick layer to the affected area and gently massage it in. Leave it on overnight and cover with socks or gloves to lock in moisture.

In the morning, wash the area with warm water and pat dry. Repeat this process for several days, ideally after soaking your feet in warm water and gently exfoliating the skin. As you apply the cream regularly, you can expect the cracks to start healing, and the skin to become smoother and softer. The redness and inflammation should subside, and the area should become less sensitive to touch.

With consistent application, you may notice significant improvement in the appearance and health of your skin within a week or two.

Simmondsia chinensis creams, also known as jojoba oil creams, are a great solution for treating cracked heels.

Gently massage the cream into your skin, paying special attention to any particularly dry or cracked areas. You can repeat this process 1-2 times a day, depending on how dry your skin is. As you use the jojoba oil cream, you can expect to see an improvement in the condition of your heels. The cream will help to lock in moisture, reducing the appearance of cracks and promoting healthy skin growth. Over time, your heels should become softer and more supple, making it easier to walk and engage in activities without discomfort.

You may also notice a reduction in pain and discomfort, especially if you've been experiencing sharp pains or stinging sensations due to cracked skin.

Aloe vera creams contain properties like vitamins A, C, and E, as well as minerals like calcium and potassium, that help to soften and moisturize cracked heels.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains bioactive constituents like terpinen-4-ol and cineole.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to control the growth of bacteria and fungi that can cause cracked heels. The cream's antifungal properties help to prevent infections and promote healthy skin. Additionally, the moisturizing properties of the cream, particularly from the compound cymene, help to lock in moisture and soften the skin on the heels.

By controlling the growth of microorganisms and providing moisture, Melaleuca alternifolia creams can help to repair and protect cracked heels.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil, and 10 drops of tea tree oil.
  2. Melt beeswax and coconut oil in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments until melted.
  3. Add distilled water and shea butter to the melted mixture. Stir until well combined.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in vitamin E oil and tea tree oil. Pour into a clean container.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify. Once set, use a hand mixer to whip the cream until light and fluffy. Use as a moisturizer for cracked heels.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are responsible for its healing properties.

These compounds help to soothe and calm cracked heels by reducing inflammation and promoting the growth of new skin cells. The antibacterial and antifungal properties of calendula also prevent infections that can worsen cracked heels. Additionally, the moisturizing properties of triterpenoid saponins help to lock in moisture and soften dry skin, making it easier to heal.

Regular use of calendula creams can help to improve the appearance and texture of cracked heels, promoting healthy skin.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Calendula officinalis infused oil, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove from heat and add the Calendula officinalis infused oil and vitamin E oil. Stir well until combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container. Let it cool and solidify at room temperature.
  5. Once solid, apply a generous amount to cracked heels and massage until absorbed. Repeat as needed.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contain aloin, aloe-emodin, and barbaloin, which are active constituents that provide several benefits for cracked heels.

Aloin has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in the heel area. Aloe-emodin has antiseptic properties that prevent infection and promote healing of cracked skin. Barbaloin is a natural humectant that helps retain moisture in the skin, keeping it hydrated and soft.

By applying aloe barbadensis cream to cracked heels, the active constituents can help soothe, heal, and moisturize the skin, reducing the appearance of cracks and promoting healthy skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe vera gel and 2 tablespoons of coconut oil. You will also need 1 tablespoon of beeswax, 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil, and a double boiler.
  2. Melt the beeswax in the double boiler over low heat. Add the coconut oil and stir until melted.
  3. Add the Aloe vera gel to the melted mixture and stir well. Remove from heat.
  4. Add the vitamin E oil and stir. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let cool.
  5. Once cooled, use a spatula to scrape the mixture into a whipped consistency. Store in the fridge and apply to cracked heels as needed.

The combination of these properties makes Vitis vinifera creams a potentially effective treatment for cracked heels.

8. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale creams contains active constituents like allantoin and mucilages, which are known to provide soothing and moisturizing properties.

These compounds work to soften and protect dry skin, helping to repair cracks and fissures on the heels. Allantoin, in particular, is a potent humectant that attracts and retains moisture, keeping the skin hydrated and supple. By applying Symphytum officinale creams to cracked heels, the skin can begin to heal and recover, becoming smoother and healthier over time.

  5. Transfer the mixture to a container and store it in the fridge. Apply a small amount to your cracked heels 2-3 times a day.

Vaccinium myrtillus creams contains anthocyanins, gallic acid, and ellagic acid as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help to soothe and protect dry, cracked skin on the heels. The anthocyanins in Vaccinium myrtillus creams also have astringent properties, which can help to reduce moisture loss and promote healthy skin cell regeneration. By applying Vaccinium myrtillus creams to cracked heels, you can help to reduce inflammation, promote healthy skin cell growth, and protect the skin from further damage.

9 Herbal Creams For Cracked Heels

Runners often suffer from cracked heels due to repeated stress and friction on their feet.

With each step, the skin on their heels is stretched and irritated, leading to dryness and eventually cracks. This can be painful and uncomfortable, making it difficult for runners to perform their daily activities, let alone their runs. The cracks can also be prone to infection, which can further complicate the issue. As a result, runners may need to take time off from running to allow their heels to heal, which can be frustrating for those who rely on regular exercise to stay healthy and motivated. The discomfort and pain from cracked heels can also affect a runner's mental state, making them feel demotivated and anxious about their ability to continue running.

Additionally, the constant need to treat and care for their cracked heels can be time-consuming and expensive. This can be especially challenging for runners who are on a tight training schedule and need to focus on their physical performance rather than their foot care. To treat cracked heels, some runners turn to herbal creams. These creams work by providing intense moisture to the affected area, helping to soothe and calm the skin. The natural ingredients in the creams help to repair and regenerate the skin, reducing the appearance of cracks and promoting healthy skin growth.

By using herbal creams regularly, runners can help to prevent future cracks from forming and keep their heels healthy and strong.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contain tea tree oil, which is rich in compounds like cineole, terpinen-4-ol, and limonene.

These constituents have antifungal and antibacterial properties, making them effective in treating cracked heels. When cracked heels are exposed to the air, they can become infected with fungi and bacteria, leading to redness, itching, and further cracking. Using a Melaleuca alternifolia cream on cracked heels can help to prevent infection by reducing the growth of these microorganisms. To use a Melaleuca alternifolia cream for cracked heels, apply a thin layer to the affected area after washing and drying your feet. Repeat this process daily, ideally before bed, to allow the cream to work overnight.

Be sure to moisturize your heels regularly, but avoid over-moisturizing as this can lead to further cracking. As you consistently apply the cream, you may start to notice improvements in the appearance and feel of your heels. The cracking may become less severe, and the skin may begin to heal and regenerate. You may also experience a reduction in redness and itching, and a general decrease in discomfort. However, it's essential to be patient and persistent, as treating cracked heels can take time.

Regular use of a Melaleuca alternifolia cream can help to maintain healthy and smooth heels, but it's also crucial to maintain good foot hygiene and wear proper footwear to prevent future cracking.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contain properties that help to soothe and heal cracked heels.

The main constituents of these creams include triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. These compounds help to reduce redness and swelling, fight off infections, and promote wound healing. When applying Calendula officinalis cream to cracked heels, it's recommended to gently clean and dry the area before applying a thick layer of cream.

You should then cover the area with a sock or bandage to lock in the moisture and allow the cream to work its magic. As the cream penetrates the skin, you can expect to see a reduction in pain and discomfort, as well as a visible improvement in the appearance of the affected area. The skin should start to feel softer and more supple, with the cracks gradually healing over time.

It's also essential to maintain good foot hygiene, wear breathable shoes, and avoid scratching the area to prevent further irritation and promote optimal healing.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains aloin, aloe-emodin, and anthraquinones, which are powerful antioxidants and anti-inflammatory agents.

These properties help soothe and calm dry, chapped lips. The anthraquinones in aloe barbadensis essential oils have astringent and anti-inflammatory effects, which help reduce redness and swelling. The antioxidants in aloe barbadensis essential oils protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting healthy skin regeneration.

By incorporating aloe barbadensis essential oils into your lip care routine, you can help lock in moisture and promote the healing of chapped lips.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel from a mature leaf, and 1 cup of water in a bowl.
  2. Add 10 drops of vitamin E oil and 5 drops of coconut oil to the bowl.
  3. Mix the Aloe gel, water, vitamin E oil, and coconut oil together until smooth.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean bowl to remove any solids.
  5. Transfer the mixture to a small glass bottle and store it in the fridge for up to 5 days.

2. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains menthone and menthol as its active constituents.

These compounds have analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce pain and swelling in chapped lips. The menthol in Mentha x piperita essential oils also has a cooling effect that can help soothe and calm irritated skin. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of menthone help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals.

When applied topically, the antiseptic properties of Mentha x piperita essential oils can help prevent infection and promote healthy tissue regeneration.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, and discard the leaves.
  4. Use a dropper to transfer the liquid into a dark glass bottle with 1 tablespoon of jojoba oil. Mix well.
  5. Store the bottle in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the bottle every day, to allow the mixture to mature.

3. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpene saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which provide its benefits for chapped lips.

The triterpene saponins in Calendula officinalis have anti-inflammatory properties, reducing redness and swelling associated with chapped lips. The flavonoids in this essential oil have antioxidant properties, protecting the skin from damage and promoting healing. Carotenoids, such as lutein and zeaxanthin, help to soothe and moisturize dry skin, reducing dryness and cracking.

By using Calendula officinalis essential oils, you can take advantage of these properties to help repair and protect your chapped lips.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers and clean them with a soft brush.
  2. Add 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to a saucepan and heat it on low.
  3. Add the cleaned Calendula flowers to the carrier oil and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl and discard the solids.
  5. Store the Calendula essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use 2-3 drops for chapped lips.

4. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains linalool, rosmarinic acid, and citronellal as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help soothe chapped lips by reducing redness and inflammation. The rosmarinic acid also acts as an astringent, which helps to tighten and protect the skin. The linalool in Melissa officinalis essential oils has a calming effect on the skin, reducing irritation and promoting healing.

By incorporating Melissa officinalis essential oils into a lip balm or salve, its active compounds can help to hydrate and protect dry, chapped lips.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Melissa officinalis leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Clean the leaves with 1 tablespoon of water and pat them dry.
  3. Place the leaves in a clean glass jar and cover them with the carrier oil.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids and use the infused oil as a balm for chapped lips.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and geranyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can help to calm and protect chapped lips. Linalool, in particular, is known for its ability to reduce inflammation and promote healing, making it an effective treatment for dry, irritated skin. The antimicrobial properties of linalyl acetate also help to prevent infection and promote a healthy environment for lip repair.

When applied topically, these bioactive constituents work together to provide quick relief and long-term healing for chapped lips.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil.
  2. Combine the dried flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar and let it sit for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar daily.
  3. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a clean glass bottle and add 10-15 drops of vitamin E oil as a natural preservative.
  5. Use the Lavandula angustifolia essential oil for chapped lips by applying a small amount to your lips with a cotton swab, as needed.

6. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like eucalyptol and alpha-pinene, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce redness and swelling, making it easier to soothe chapped lips. The antiseptic properties of eucalyptol and alpha-pinene also help prevent infection and promote healing in the affected area. Additionally, eucalyptol has a cooling effect, which can help numb the pain and discomfort associated with chapped lips.

The antibacterial properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils can also help keep the lips clean and prevent the spread of infection.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of water in a big pot.
  2. Boil the water and add the Eucalyptus leaves. Let it boil for 10 minutes.
  3. Turn off the heat and let it cool for 30 minutes. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Add 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil) to the liquid and mix well.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 days in a cool, dark place. Strain it again and use the oil as a lip balm or store it in a dark bottle.

7. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol and carvacrol, which are the two main biologically active constituents.

Thymol has antimicrobial properties that help prevent the growth of bacteria and fungi on the skin, reducing the risk of infection and promoting healing. Carvacrol has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and redness, making it an effective remedy for chapped lips. Thymus vulgaris essential oils also has antiseptic properties that help soothe and calm irritated skin, providing relief from dryness and discomfort.

When applied topically to chapped lips, Thymus vulgaris essential oils helps to protect and repair the skin, promoting healthy lip tissue.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and flowers, 2 cups of water, a saucepan, a glass jar, and a cheesecloth.
  2. Combine the Thymus vulgaris leaves and flowers with 2 cups of water in the saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth into the glass jar. Discard the solids and let the liquid cool.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a small saucepan and heat gently until the volume is reduced to about 1/4 cup. This helps concentrate the oil.
  5. Let the concentrated liquid cool, then pour it into a small glass jar. Store the Thymus vulgaris essential oil in a cool, dark place.

8. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains the bioactive constituents camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce redness and swelling associated with chapped lips. The antiseptic properties of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils also aid in preventing infections that can make chapped lips worse. Additionally, the oil's moisturizing properties help lock in moisture, soothing and protecting dry, cracked lips.

The antioxidant properties of camphor and borneol also help protect lip cells from damage caused by free radicals.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves, 2 cups of water, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the leaves and water in a saucepan, and heat over low heat for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat, and let it steep for 2 hours. Strain the mixture and discard the leaves.
  4. Transfer the liquid to the glass jar and store it in the fridge. For every 1 tablespoon of liquid, add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  5. Apply 1-2 drops of the Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil mixture to your chapped lips, as needed.

9. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains active constituents like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids that help soothe and heal chapped lips.

Alkylamides have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce redness and swelling, while glycosides help to lock in moisture and protect the skin from dryness. Phenolic acids have antioxidant properties that fight free radicals and promote skin health. The antimicrobial properties of Echinacea purpurea essential oils also help to prevent infection and promote the healing of chapped skin.

Regular use of Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help to repair and restore the skin's natural barrier, leaving lips smooth and healthy.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves.
  2. Use 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil) and add 1/4 cup of fresh flowers and leaves to it.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container.
  5. Store the Echinacea purpurea essential oil in a cool, dark place and apply it to chapped lips as needed, diluted with a carrier oil (1:5 ratio).

10. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin, and flavonoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, which help to calm and protect chapped lips. Glycyrrhizin also has antioxidant properties, which can help to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress that can contribute to lip dryness. The anti-inflammatory properties of glycyrrhizin and flavonoids can help to reduce redness and swelling associated with chapped lips, promoting a smoother and healthier appearance.

The soothing and protective properties of Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils make it a popular natural remedy for dry and chapped lips.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 4 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Chop the roots into small pieces and add them to a clean glass jar.
  3. Pour the carrier oil over the roots, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean container. Discard the roots and use the oil as is or dilute it with more carrier oil for your lip balm.

11. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, geraniol, and linalool as its active constituents.

These compounds have antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to soothe and protect chapped lips. Limonene, in particular, has been shown to have antimicrobial properties, reducing the risk of infection and promoting healing. The anti-inflammatory properties of geraniol and linalool help to reduce redness and swelling, making it easier to heal and repair chapped lips.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can help to moisturize and soften chapped lips.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the mixture over medium heat for 10-15 minutes, or until the water reduces to 1/2 cup.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle.
  5. Add 10 drops of jojoba oil and 10 drops of beeswax to the liquid, then stir until the wax dissolves.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent chapped lips from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent chapped lips.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint or chamomile, have moisturizing properties that can keep your lips hydrated. When you drink these teas, the moisture is absorbed into your body, helping to keep your skin, including your lips, healthy and smooth.

This can reduce the risk of chapped lips.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for chapped lips every day?

Herbal teas for chapped lips can be a soothing solution, but consuming them daily might not be safe.

Some teas, like those with caffeine, can dry out your lips more. Others, like sage tea, might affect hormone levels or interact with medications.

It's best to limit your tea intake or choose gentle options like chamomile.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in chapped lips?

Herbal teas can help soothe chapped lips, but it might take some time to see results.

Generally, you can start to feel the effects within a few days to a week.

Some people notice an improvement in the dryness and discomfort, while others may take a bit longer to see a difference in their lip health.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for chapped lips?

Drinking herbal tea for chapped lips is most helpful in the morning and evening.

This is because your skin regenerates quickly at these times, and the tea's soothing properties can help lock in moisture.

Sipping a warm cup before bed can also help you wake up with softer, more hydrated lips.

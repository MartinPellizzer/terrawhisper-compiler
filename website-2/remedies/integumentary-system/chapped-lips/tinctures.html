1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains triterpene saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are potent anti-inflammatory compounds.

These compounds help soothe and calm irritated skin, such as chapped lips, by reducing inflammation and preventing further irritation. The flavonoids in calendula also have antioxidant properties, which protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals and promote healthy cell growth. Additionally, the triterpene saponins in calendula have antimicrobial properties, which help prevent infections that can exacerbate chapped lips.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the skin, calendula officinalis tinctures can provide relief and promote healing of chapped lips.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Calendula officinalis flowers and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Fill a clean glass jar with 1 cup of fresh Calendula officinalis flowers, leaving 1 inch of space at the top.
  3. Pour 2 cups of vodka over the Calendula flowers, making sure they are fully covered.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the tincture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle, discard the solids and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains the bioactive constituents glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe dry, chapped lips.

Glycyrrhizin has been shown to reduce inflammation and irritation, while flavonoids have antioxidant properties that protect the skin from damage. The tannins present in Glycyrrhiza glabra also help to reduce water loss from the skin, keeping lips hydrated and moisturized. Additionally, glycyrrhizin has been found to have a moisturizing effect on the skin, which helps to lock in moisture and protect the lips from further dryness.

By using Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures, you can help to calm and hydrate dry, chapped lips, and promote healthy skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, and 2 cups of vodka or neutral grain spirit.
  2. Chop the root into small pieces and place it in a clean glass jar.
  3. Pour the vodka or neutral grain spirit over the root pieces, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle, and discard the root pieces.

3. Aloe vera

Aloe vera tinctures contains bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A and E. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that help to calm and protect chapped lips.

Aloin, in particular, has been shown to have antioxidant and antimicrobial effects, which can help to repair and heal dry, cracked lips. The vitamins and other nutrients in aloe vera tinctures also provide essential hydration and nourishment to the skin, helping to lock in moisture and promote healthy skin cell growth.

Regular use of aloe vera tinctures on the lips can help to restore their natural moisture and elasticity.

  1. Cut 1 cup of fresh aloe vera leaf into small pieces and place them in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add 2 cups of vodka to the jar, making sure the aloe vera is completely covered.
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass jar.
  5. Store the aloe vera tincture in a cool, dark place and use 5-7 drops on your chapped lips as needed.

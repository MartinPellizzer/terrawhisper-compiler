11 Herbal Teas For Chapped Lips

If you're dealing with chapped lips, herbal teas can be a great solution.

These teas work by providing moisture to the skin and helping to heal any irritation. For example, Calendula officinalis, also known as marigold, has anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe dry, cracked lips. It helps to lock in moisture and protect the skin from further drying out. Another herb that's often used for its soothing properties is Echinacea purpurea.

This herb is known for its ability to calm and protect the skin, making it an excellent choice for chapped lips. Echinacea also has antibacterial properties, which can help prevent infection and speed up the healing process. Glycyrrhiza glabra, or licorice root, is also a popular choice for herbal teas that target chapped lips. Licorice root has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce redness and swelling, while its moisturizing properties can help to lock in hydration and soothe dry skin. Drinking these teas can bring several benefits to your life.

Not only will your lips feel better, but you'll also be rehydrating your body, which can lead to a range of other health benefits.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help soothe and protect chapped lips. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin, have a strong ability to reduce inflammation and fight free radicals, making it easier for the skin to heal. The carotenoids, including beta-carotene, have antioxidant properties that help repair damaged skin cells and promote collagen production.

By reducing inflammation and promoting skin repair, Calendula officinalis teas can help to heal and moisturize chapped lips.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the Calendula flowers in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a separate container using a tea strainer or cheesecloth.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of honey to the cooled tea for sweetness and soothing properties.
  5. Apply the tea to your chapped lips using a cotton swab or your finger, 2-3 times a day.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains compounds like alkylamides and caffeic acid, which are known for their anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and redness associated with chapped lips, making Echinacea purpurea a popular natural remedy for this issue. The flavonoids and phenolic acids in Echinacea purpurea also act as antioxidants, protecting the skin from damage caused by free radicals and promoting healing. The soothing and calming effects of Echinacea purpurea's polysaccharides and glycosides can help to reduce irritation and discomfort in chapped lips, making it easier to recover from dry, cracked skin.

Drinking Echinacea purpurea tea may help to stimulate the production of new skin cells, promoting the healing process and leaving lips feeling smoother and healthier.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Place the dried flowers in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the flowers and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea and discard the flowers. Let it cool to room temperature.
  5. Soak a cotton swab in the cooled tea and apply it to your chapped lips as needed.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas contains a range of bioactive constituents, including vitamins, minerals, and amino acids, that help to soothe and heal chapped lips.

One of the key compounds found in aloe is glycoprotein, which has been shown to have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce redness and swelling in the skin. The tea also contains acemannan, a complex carbohydrate that has been found to have wound-healing properties, helping to repair and protect dry, cracked skin. Additionally, the tea contains antioxidants like vitamin E and beta-carotene, which can help to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals and promote healthy cell growth.

By incorporating aloe barbadensis tea into your skincare routine, you can help to lock in moisture and promote healthy, hydrated skin on your lips.

  1. Cut a 2-inch piece of Aloe barbadensis leaf and peel off the skin.
  2. Chop the inner gel into small pieces and place them in a cup.
  3. Add 1 cup of boiling water to the cup and let it steep for 5 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture into another cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  5. Drink 1/2 cup of the tea 2-3 times a day to soothe chapped lips.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains rosmarinic acid, a powerful antioxidant that helps to soothe and protect dry, chapped lips.

This compound also has anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce redness and swelling associated with chapped lips. Rosmarinic acid also helps to lock in moisture, keeping lips hydrated and soft. Additionally, the tea's antimicrobial properties prevent the growth of bacteria and other microorganisms that can cause irritation and infection on the lips.

By drinking Rosmarinus officinalis tea, you can reap the benefits of its active constituents and promote healthy, smooth lips.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the dried leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Let it cool down.
  3. Strain the liquid to remove the leaves. Discard the leaves.
  4. Add honey (optional) to the tea to sweeten it. Stir well.
  5. Use the tea as a lip balm by applying it to your chapped lips using a cotton swab.

8. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas contains menthol, menthone, and limonene as its key bioactive constituents.

These compounds give peppermint tea its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe and heal chapped lips. The antiseptic properties of menthol and menthone help to kill bacteria that can cause infection and irritation, reducing redness and swelling.   5. Soak a cotton swab in the cooled tea and apply it to your chapped lips. Repeat as needed for relief.

11. Eucalyptus globulus

  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes. Strain the liquid into the clean cup.
  5. Let the tea cool down, then use it to soothe chapped lips by applying it with a cotton swab or a clean cloth.

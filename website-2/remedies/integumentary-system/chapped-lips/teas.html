11 teas to combat chapped lips for skiers

As a skier, you're exposed to harsh winter conditions - cold winds, dry air, and intense sunlight.

These elements can dry out your skin, including your lips, leading to chapped and cracked lips. Chapped lips can be painful, and it's not just about the physical discomfort. It can also affect your mood and overall experience on the slopes. Imagine skiing down a beautiful mountain, but instead of enjoying the thrill, you're worried about how your lips feel. Chapped lips can also lead to bleeding and infections, which can keep you from skiing as often as you'd like.

When your lips are chapped, it's hard to enjoy the simple things, like sipping hot chocolate or taking in the breathtaking views. As a skier, you need something that can help soothe and protect your lips. That's where herbal teas come in. Some herbal teas have natural moisturizing and anti-inflammatory properties that can help to lock in moisture and reduce inflammation. These teas can be used as a lip balm or added to your bath water for a relaxing soak.

They can help to soften and heal chapped lips, so you can get back to what matters most - hitting the slopes.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas can be a helpful remedy for chapped lips.

This is because the plant contains anti-inflammatory properties, including triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which can soothe and calm irritated skin. The constituents in Calendula officinalis, such as sesquiterpene lactones, also have antiseptic and antimicrobial properties, which can help to prevent infection and promote healing. To use Calendula officinalis teas for chapped lips, you can make a warm tea by steeping the dried flowers in boiling water, then straining and applying the cooled liquid directly to your lips with a cotton swab.

You can also add a few drops of the tea to your lip balm or lip gloss. As you apply the Calendula officinalis tea to your chapped lips, you may start to feel a soothing and calming effect, as the anti-inflammatory properties work to reduce redness and irritation. Within a few days, you may notice a significant improvement in the appearance and feel of your lips, with reduced dryness and a decrease in the size and number of cracks.

Regular use of Calendula officinalis teas may also help to prevent future episodes of chapped lips, by promoting healthy skin and reducing the risk of irritation.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis teas are a great solution for chapped lips.

The gel from the aloe plant, which is used to make the tea, contains properties and constituents like vitamins A, C, and E, as well as minerals like potassium and zinc. These compounds help to soothe and moisturize dry, irritated skin, reducing inflammation and promoting healing. The tea also contains aloin, a compound that has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, which can help to prevent infections and promote the growth of new skin cells. To use aloe barbadensis tea for chapped lips, you can try the following: gently rinse your lips with warm water, then apply a thin layer of the tea gel directly to your lips.

You can also add a few drops of the tea to your lip balm or moisturizer for an extra dose of hydration. Be sure to use the tea in moderation, as excessive consumption can cause stomach upset. When you start using aloe barbadensis tea for chapped lips, you may notice a reduction in dryness and irritation within a few days. Your lips may feel softer and more moisturized, and the pain and discomfort may start to subside.

As the tea continues to work its magic, you may also notice a decrease in the frequency and severity of chapped lip episodes, and your lips may become more resistant to dryness and irritation over time.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas have been traditionally used to help with chapped lips, and it's due to their rich content of antioxidants, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to soothe and calm dry, irritated skin on the lips. The tea also contains alkaloids like echinacoside and caftaric acid, which have been shown to have antimicrobial effects, reducing the risk of infection and promoting healing.

To use Echinacea purpurea teas for chapped lips, try steeping a teaspoon of dried root in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then straining and letting it cool. You can apply the cooled tea as a compress to the affected area or use it as a lip balm by mixing it with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil. By doing so, you can expect to see a reduction in inflammation and dryness, and your lips should start to feel softer and more hydrated within a few days.

Additionally, the antimicrobial properties may help to clear up any infections or irritations, allowing your lips to heal faster.

What are the causes of enlarged spleen?

An enlarged spleen, also known as splenomegaly, is a condition where the spleen becomes larger than normal.

This can happen for several reasons. One cause is HIV, a virus that attacks the immune system. When HIV infects the spleen, it can cause it to become inflamed and swell. Another reason is cirrhosis, a disease where the liver becomes scarred and can't do its job properly. When the liver fails, it can cause a buildup of toxins in the blood, which can lead to an enlarged spleen.

Sarcoidosis is another cause of an enlarged spleen. This is an inflammatory disease that can affect many parts of the body, including the spleen. When sarcoidosis attacks the spleen, it can cause it to become inflamed and swollen. Cancer can also cause an enlarged spleen. Lymphoma is a type of cancer that affects the immune system, and when it spreads to the spleen, it can cause it to become enlarged. Leukemia is another type of cancer that can cause an enlarged spleen.

This is because leukemia affects the production of blood cells, and when the spleen is filled with abnormal blood cells, it can become enlarged.

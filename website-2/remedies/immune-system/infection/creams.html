1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains compounds like cineole, terpinen-4-ol, and limonene, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce the growth of bacteria and other microorganisms that cause infections. Terpinen-4-ol, in particular, is known to inhibit the growth of a wide range of bacteria, including Staphylococcus aureus and Escherichia coli. The anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds also help to reduce swelling and redness associated with infections.

By reducing the growth of bacteria and inflammation, Melaleuca alternifolia creams can help to promote the healing of wounds and prevent the spread of infection.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 2 teaspoons of vitamin E oil, and 10 drops of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil.
  2. Melt the beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the coconut oil, olive oil, and vitamin E oil to the melted beeswax. Stir until well combined.
  4. Remove the heat and stir in the distilled water and Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean glass jar and let it cool and harden before use as an infection cream.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpene saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds give the cream its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The triterpene saponins help reduce inflammation and prevent the growth of bacteria, making it effective in treating infections. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin, have strong antioxidant properties that protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting a healthy environment for healing.

The carotenoids, such as beta-carotene, enhance the immune system's ability to fight off infections, supporting the body's natural defense mechanisms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Calendula flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the dried Calendula flowers to the melted oil mixture and let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the Calendula flowers.
  5. Add the shea butter and vitamin E oil to the mixture and stir until well combined. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify before use.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains compounds like cineole, globulol, and alpha-pinene, which have antimicrobial properties.

These compounds help to reduce the growth of bacteria and fungi that cause infections. Cineole, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of toxins by bacteria, making it harder for infections to develop. The anti-inflammatory properties of eucalyptus globulus also help to reduce swelling and redness associated with infections.

By reducing the number of bacteria and inflammation, eucalyptus globulus creams can aid in the healing process and prevent the spread of infection.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 10 drops of Eucalyptus globulus essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water. Stir until melted and smooth.
  3. Add shea butter and olive oil to the melted mixture. Stir until well combined and smooth.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and add 10 drops of Eucalyptus globulus essential oil. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, your Eucalyptus globulus cream is ready for use on infected areas.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

These compounds have antimicrobial properties, which help to reduce the growth of bacteria and fungi that cause infections. Aloin and aloe-emodin also have anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce swelling and redness associated with infections. Acemannan has immunomodulatory effects, which can boost the body's natural defenses to fight off infections.

The combination of these properties in Aloe barbadensis creams makes it a useful treatment for various types of infections.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 10 drops of tea tree oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the Aloe barbadensis gel and shea butter until well combined.
  4. Add the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture to the Aloe gel mixture, and stir until well combined.
  5. Add the tea tree oil and mix well. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let it cool before use.

5. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic acids as its active constituents.

Alkaloids like echinatin and cichoric acid have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and redness associated with infections. Glycosides, particularly echinacoside, have immunomodulatory effects, enhancing the body's natural defense against pathogens. Phenolic acids, like caffeic acid, exhibit antimicrobial properties, inhibiting the growth of bacteria and other microorganisms that cause infections.

These properties work together to provide relief and promote healing in skin infections.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the coconut oil and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Mix 2 tablespoons of the strained liquid with 2 tablespoons of melted beeswax and pour the mixture into small containers. Let it cool and harden.

6. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams contains thymol and carvacrol, two active constituents that have antimicrobial properties.

Thymol inhibits the growth of bacteria, fungi, and viruses by disrupting their cell membranes, which helps to prevent the spread of infection. Carvacrol also has antibacterial properties, reducing inflammation and promoting wound healing. The antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties of Thymus vulgaris creams help to clean and soothe infected skin, making it a popular natural remedy for cuts, burns, and other skin irritations.

The antimicrobial properties of thymol and carvacrol in Thymus vulgaris creams help to combat infections, making it a useful treatment for infected wounds.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of dried Thymus vulgaris leaves.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add 1 cup of water to the melted oil and wax mixture, and stir until the mixture is well combined.
  4. Add the dried Thymus vulgaris leaves to the mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean glass container and let it cool and solidify. Store the Thymus vulgaris cream in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

7. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum creams contains bioactive constituents like naphthodianthrones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help combat infections. The naphthodianthrones, specifically hyperforin and hypericin, have been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi, while the flavonoids, such as quercetin, have antioxidant properties that reduce inflammation and promote healing. The phenolic acids, including ferulic acid, also have antimicrobial properties that help protect against infections.

By incorporating these compounds, Hypericum perforatum creams can provide effective relief from skin infections and promote the body's natural healing process.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Hypericum perforatum flowers, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, and 2 teaspoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Dry the Hypericum perforatum flowers in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 1-2 hours. Let them cool completely.
  3. Melt the beeswax and coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  4. Add the dried Hypericum perforatum flowers, olive oil, and vitamin E oil to the melted beeswax and coconut oil mixture. Stir well.
  5. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool until it reaches a consistency suitable for application. Pour the cream into a container and store it in a cool, dark place.

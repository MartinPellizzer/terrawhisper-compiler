1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains active constituents like alkylamides, glycoproteins, and polysaccharides.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and fight infection. The tinctures also contain flavonoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, that have antimicrobial properties, inhibiting the growth of bacteria and viruses. The polysaccharides in Echinacea purpurea tinctures stimulate the immune system, activating white blood cells to attack invading pathogens.

By combining these properties, Echinacea purpurea tinctures can help the body fight off infections more effectively.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves. Clean and dry them.
  2. Add 2 cups of 80% vodka to a clean glass jar. Add the Echinacea flowers and leaves. Make sure they are completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Label the jar and store it in a cool, dark place. Take 20-30 drops of the tincture 2-3 times a day to help prevent or treat infections.

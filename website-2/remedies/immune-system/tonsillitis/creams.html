1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce swelling and fight off infections in the tonsils. The flavonoids in Echinacea purpurea creams also have antimicrobial properties, which can help combat bacterial and viral infections that cause tonsillitis. The alkaloids present in the cream may help stimulate the immune system, promoting the production of white blood cells to fight off the infection.

By applying Echinacea purpurea creams topically, you may be able to reduce the severity of tonsillitis symptoms and speed up the healing process.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of coconut oil.
  2. Combine 1 tablespoon of beeswax and 1 tablespoon of coconut oil in a small bowl.
  3. Heat the beeswax mixture in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl for 10-15 seconds.
  4. Add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the heated coconut oil and let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  5. Strain the mixture and mix it with the heated beeswax mixture. Pour the cream into a container and let it cool before use.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains a compound called cineole, which is a key active constituent that helps with tonsillitis.

Cineole has anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, reducing swelling and congestion in the throat. It also has antimicrobial properties, which help combat bacterial infections that can cause tonsillitis. The decongestant properties of eucalyptus globulus creams help loosen mucus, making it easier to cough up and clear out the throat.

By reducing inflammation and killing bacteria, eucalyptus globulus creams can help alleviate symptoms of tonsillitis, promoting healing and recovery.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 20 drops of eucalyptus essential oil, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted oil mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Add eucalyptus essential oil and vitamin E oil, and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let it cool completely before use. Apply a small amount to the affected area 2-3 times a day.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

The soothing properties of glycyrrhiza glabra help calm the irritated tonsils and surrounding tissues, promoting a faster recovery.

By applying the cream directly to the affected area, it can provide quick relief from tonsillitis symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of glycerin, 2 tablespoons of glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, 2 tablespoons of aloe vera gel, and 10 drops of tea tree essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water, stirring until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix glycerin, glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, and aloe vera gel until well combined.
  4. Slowly add the glycerin mixture to the melted oil mixture, stirring constantly until smooth.

5. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra creams contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins.

  4. Heat the mixture until it reaches 160°F to 180°F (71°C to 82°C), then remove from heat and let cool slightly.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool completely before use. Store in a cool, dry place.

9 Herbal Essential Oils For Tonsillitis

When you have tonsillitis, your tonsils get swollen and inflamed, making it hard to swallow and breathe.

Herbal essential oils can help ease these symptoms. One way to use them is by inhaling the vapors or applying them topically to the skin. For example, Echinacea purpurea oil has anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce swelling and fight off the infection that's causing tonsillitis. Eucalyptus globulus oil, on the other hand, has decongestant properties that can help clear out mucus and make it easier to breathe. You can inhale its vapors using a humidifier or add a few drops to a bowl of hot water and inhale the steam.

Lavandula angustifolia oil has a calming effect that can help reduce pain and anxiety. You can apply it topically to the skin, but make sure to dilute it with a carrier oil first. Some people also use herbal teas to relieve tonsillitis. These teas are made from herbs like Echinacea purpurea, Eucalyptus globulus, and Lavandula angustifolia, and they can help reduce inflammation and fight off the infection. Herbal teas work by stimulating your immune system and helping your body fight off the infection that's causing tonsillitis. They can also help reduce the severity of symptoms like sore throats and fever.

Drinking herbal teas can be especially helpful when you're feeling under the weather and need something to soothe your body and calm your mind.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains boivolatile constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and pain in the throat. The alkylamides, specifically echinacoside, have immunomodulatory effects, supporting the immune system in fighting off the infection causing tonsillitis. Caffeic acid derivatives have antimicrobial properties, inhibiting the growth of bacteria and viruses that can lead to tonsillitis.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off infection, Echinacea purpurea essential oils may provide relief from tonsillitis symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Place the flowers in a clean glass jar and cover them with the carrier oil.
  3. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Echinacea purpurea essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole, a key bioactive constituent that has decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and ease breathing difficulties associated with tonsillitis. The menthol content in eucalyptus oil also acts as an expectorant, promoting the clearance of mucus from the throat and airways. Furthermore, the oil's antimicrobial properties help combat bacterial and viral infections that can cause tonsillitis.

By using eucalyptus globulus essential oils, you can help alleviate symptoms of tonsillitis and support the healing process.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a glass jar with a lid, and a clean cloth.
  2. Combine 2 cups of water and 2 cups of vodka in a saucepan, and heat it over low heat.
  3. Add the dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves to the saucepan and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into the glass jar, and discard the solids.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily, then filter it through a coffee filter to get the essential oil.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties that can help combat bacterial infections that cause tonsillitis. The antiseptic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate can help reduce the risk of secondary infections and promote healing. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds can help alleviate pain and swelling associated with tonsillitis.

By reducing inflammation and fighting bacterial infections, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils may be an effective natural remedy for tonsillitis.

  1. Gather materials: Lavandula angustifolia flowers, a cup of water, a saucepan, a cheesecloth, a cup, and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Pick fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and dry them in a single layer on a tray for 1-2 weeks.
  3. Combine 2 cups of dried flowers with 1 cup of water in the saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth into the cup. Discard the solids. Let the liquid sit for 2 hours to allow the particles to settle.
  5. Transfer the liquid into the dark glass bottle and store in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a warm compress or inhale directly for relief from tonsillitis symptoms.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains a compound called borneol and camphor, which have antimicrobial properties that help fight bacterial infections like tonsillitis.

The essential oil also contains 1,8-cineole, a compound that helps reduce inflammation and swelling in the throat. The antiseptic properties of camphor and borneol in Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils help kill germs that cause tonsillitis, while 1,8-cineole soothes and calms the throat. The anti-inflammatory properties of the essential oil reduce swelling and pain in the tonsils, making it easier to swallow and breathe.

The combination of antimicrobial, antiseptic, and anti-inflammatory properties in Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils makes it a helpful remedy for tonsillitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat, then add the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.
  3. Reduce heat to low and let it simmer for 30 minutes. This helps release the oils from the leaves.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the essential oil in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for use.

5. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains compounds like thujone, rosmarinic acid, and ursolic acid.

These constituents have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help combat bacterial infections like tonsillitis. The antiseptic properties of thujone and rosmarinic acid inhibit the growth of Streptococcus pyogenes, a common cause of tonsillitis. The anti-inflammatory properties of ursolic acid and rosmarinic acid also reduce swelling and pain in the tonsils.

By using Salvia officinalis essential oils, you can promote a healthy environment for the tonsils to recover from infection.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves and flowers. Clean them with distilled water.
  2. Add 1 cup of distilled water to a saucepan. Bring the water to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and add the cleaned Salvia officinalis leaves and flowers to the boiling water. Simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Remove the leaves and flowers from the water. Let the water cool down. Strain the water into a bowl using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter.
  5. Transfer the strained water to a dark glass bottle. Store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks to allow the oil to separate and mature.

6. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra essential oils contains flavonoids like salicin and quercetin, and phenolic acids like ferulic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in the throat. The antiseptic and antimicrobial properties of Sambucus nigra essential oils, which are attributed to the presence of isorhapontigenin and kaempferol, help combat bacterial and viral infections that cause tonsillitis. The oils' antioxidant properties, which are due to the presence of isorhapontigenin and quercetin, help protect the throat from oxidative stress and promote healing.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off infections, Sambucus nigra essential oils can help alleviate the symptoms of tonsillitis.

  1. Gather 250ml of Sambucus nigra berries, 1 cup of vodka, and a glass jar.
  2. Combine berries and vodka in the glass jar. Store in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking daily.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into another container. Discard the berries.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and add 1 tablespoon of glycerin. Store in a cool, dark place.
  5. Use the Sambucus nigra essential oil as needed for tonsillitis. Mix with a carrier oil and apply topically, or use as a throat spray.

7. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains active constituents like cineole, limonene, and terpinen-4-ol.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help combat bacterial infections, such as those that cause tonsillitis. Cineole, in particular, has been shown to have a strong antibacterial effect against Staphylococcus aureus and Streptococcus pyogenes, two common bacteria that cause tonsillitis. Terpinen-4-ol, on the other hand, has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling and pain in the throat.

By targeting these underlying causes of tonsillitis, Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils can help alleviate symptoms and support the body's natural healing process.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Melaleuca alternifolia leaves. Wash them with water to remove dirt.
  2. Crush the leaves to release their oils. Use a mortar and pestle or a blender to make a paste.
  3. Add 1 cup of water to the paste. Heat the mixture in a saucepan over low heat for 10 minutes.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat. Let the mixture sit for 1 hour to allow the oil to separate.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a container. Discard the solids. Your Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil is now ready to use.

8. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have potent anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and pain in the tonsils, making it easier to breathe and swallow. The anti-inflammatory compounds in ginger also reduce the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which contribute to the development of tonsillitis. Additionally, ginger's antimicrobial properties inhibit the growth of bacteria that can cause tonsillitis, such as Streptococcus pyogenes.

The antiseptic and antibacterial properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils help prevent infection and promote healing in the affected area.

  1. Get 200g of fresh Zingiber officinale roots and wash them with 1 cup of water to clean.
  2. Dry the roots in a warm place for 24 hours or use a food dehydrator.
  3. Grind the dried roots into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar.
  4. Steep 50g of the powdered roots in 1 cup of carrier oil like coconut oil in a glass jar for 2 weeks, shaking daily.
  5. Strain the oil and discard the roots, then bottle the Zingiber officinale essential oil for use in treating tonsillitis.

9. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin that has anti-inflammatory properties.

This helps reduce swelling in the tonsils and soothe the pain. Glycyrrhizin also has antimicrobial properties, which help combat bacterial and viral infections that cause tonsillitis. Additionally, the essential oil contains flavonoids like luteolin, which have antioxidant properties that help protect the tonsils from damage.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off infections, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of tonsillitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Grind the dried roots into fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar.
  3. Mix 1/4 cup of the ground roots with 2 cups of carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids. Use the essential oil as needed to help soothe tonsillitis symptoms.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent tonsillitis from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent tonsillitis from forming because it can help boost the immune system.

Certain herbs like echinacea and peppermint have antibacterial properties that can fight off infections.

However, tonsillitis is usually caused by a virus, so herbal tea might only help alleviate symptoms or make the body less susceptible to infection.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for tonsillitis every day?

Consuming herbal teas for tonsillitis every day can be safe if you choose teas with antibacterial properties, like echinacea or sage.

These teas can help soothe your throat and reduce swelling. However, some herbal teas can interact with medications or worsen underlying conditions.

Be cautious and monitor your body's response.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in tonsillitis?

Herbal teas can help with tonsillitis, but it's hard to say exactly how long they'll take to work.

Some people notice relief from pain and swelling within a few days, while others may take a week or more to feel better.

It really depends on the severity of the tonsillitis and the individual's body.

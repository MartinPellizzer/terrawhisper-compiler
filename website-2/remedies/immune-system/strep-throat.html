What Are The Main Causes Of Strep Throat?

The main cause of strep throat is Group A beta-hemolytic Streptococcus, which is a type of bacteria.

This specific bacteria is known as Streptococcus pyogenes, or S. pyogenes for short. When S. pyogenes infects the throat, it causes an illness called S. pyogenes pharyngitis, or strep throat.

This bacteria is highly contagious and can spread quickly through close contact with an infected person, such as through coughing or sneezing. S. pyogenes thrives in environments with high humidity, which makes it easier for the bacteria to multiply and spread.

The bacteria can also survive on surfaces and objects for a short period, making it possible for someone to get infected by touching a contaminated surface and then touching their mouth or face.

Below are the main causes of strep throat.

  • Group A beta: Group A beta hemolytic streptococci (GABHS) cause strep throat because they produce toxins that damage the tissues inside the throat and cause the body's immune system to react with fever, pain, and swollen lymph nodes.
  • Streptococcus pyogenes: Streptococcus pyogenes is a cause of strep throat because it is a type of bacteria that can infect the throat and tonsils, leading to inflammation and a sore throat.
  • S. pyogenes pharyngitis: Streptococcus pyogenes, commonly known as Group A Streptococcus, causes strep throat by producing toxins that damage the throat's mucous membranes and cause severe inflammation.
  • Retropharyngeal abscess: A retropharyngeal abscess can cause strep throat because the infection can spread from the abscess in the back of the throat to the tonsils, where strep bacteria often live.
  • Tonsillitis: Tonsillitis can cause strep throat because the bacteria that cause strep throat can live on the tonsils and multiply, leading to an infection.

Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza uralensis) is also not recommended, as it can make your throat dry and increase swelling. These herbs may not be bad for everyone, but if you have strep throat, it's best to avoid them.

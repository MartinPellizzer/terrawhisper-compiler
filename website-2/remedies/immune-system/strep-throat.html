Strep Throat: Causes, Herbal Remedies for Sore Throat, and Medicinal Herbs

What are the causes of strep throat?

The main cause of strep throat is a type of bacteria called Group A beta, also known as Streptococcus pyogenes, or Strep A for short.

This bacteria is responsible for about 70% of all strep throat cases. When we refer to Group A beta, we're talking about a specific type of Streptococcus pyogenes that has the ability to cause infections. Strep A is a highly contagious bacteria that can be spread through the air when an infected person talks, coughs, or sneezes.

It can also be spread through touch, like shaking hands or sharing food and drinks with someone who's infected. People can be nasal carriers of Strep A, meaning they can have the bacteria in their noses without showing any symptoms. These carriers can still spread the infection to others, even if they don't feel sick themselves.

Group A beta thrives in environments with poor hygiene, which is why washing your hands frequently and properly can help prevent the spread of strep throat.

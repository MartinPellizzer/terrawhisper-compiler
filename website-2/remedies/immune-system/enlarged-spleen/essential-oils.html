1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains flavonoids, bilobalide, and ginkgolides as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain associated with an enlarged spleen. Flavonoids, specifically quercetin, possess antioxidant properties that protect the spleen from oxidative damage and promote healthy tissue function. Bilobalide and ginkgolides work together to improve blood flow and reduce platelet aggregation, which can help alleviate symptoms of splenomegaly.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healthy blood flow, Ginkgo biloba essential oils may help alleviate some of the discomfort and symptoms associated with an enlarged spleen.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves, a glass jar with a lid, a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil, and a cheesecloth.
  2. Combine 1/2 cup of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves and 2 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Place the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will help the oil extract from the leaves.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through the cheesecloth into another container. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Ginkgo biloba essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a lid. Use 5-7 drops of the oil with a carrier oil for treatment of an enlarged spleen.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a compound called curcumin, which has potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Curcumin helps reduce inflammation and swelling in the body, which can cause an enlarged spleen. The bioactive constituents of Curcuma longa essential oils, including turmerone and atlantone, have been shown to have therapeutic effects on the spleen by reducing oxidative stress and promoting the removal of waste products. This can help alleviate symptoms associated with an enlarged spleen, such as pain and discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Curcuma longa essential oils may help restore the spleen's normal function.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Curcuma longa roots. Wash them with clean water.
  2. Dry the Curcuma longa roots in the shade for 2-3 days. Break them into small pieces.
  3. In a bowl, mix 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) with 1/4 cup of the dried Curcuma longa pieces.
  4. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the bowl every day. This allows the oil to extract from the roots.
  5. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids and use the extracted oil for treatment.

Pink Eye Causes and Natural Treatments with Medicinal Herbs

What are the causes of pink eye?

Pink eye, or conjunctivitis, is an eye infection that can be caused by different things.

One main cause is viral conjunctivitis, which is usually spread through close contact with someone who has it. This can happen when you shake hands, touch a surface, or even share personal items with someone who's infected. Viruses like the common cold or flu can also cause pink eye. Bacterial conjunctivitis is another cause, and it's often spread through touching contaminated surfaces or coming into contact with someone who has it. Bacteria like Staphylococcus aureus can cause this type of pink eye. People who wear contact lenses, don't wash their hands properly, or touch their eyes when they have a cut or sore can get bacterial conjunctivitis.

Allergies can also cause pink eye, especially if you have allergies to things like pollen, dust, or pet dander. When you're allergic to something, your eyes can get red, itchy, and watery. This is because your immune system is reacting to the allergen, causing your eyes to become inflamed. Dust mites are tiny creatures that live in bedding, carpets, and upholstered furniture. They can cause allergies, which can lead to pink eye. These mites release tiny particles that become airborne, and when you breathe them in or they get in your eyes, they can cause an allergic reaction. Lastly, pet dander, or the tiny skin cells that pets shed, can also cause pink eye.

If you have a pet and you're allergic to its dander, you can get pink eye when you touch your eyes or rub them after petting your pet.

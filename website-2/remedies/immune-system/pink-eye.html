What Are The Main Causes Of Pink Eye?

Pink eye, also known as conjunctivitis, is caused by different factors.

A viral infection is one of the main causes, often spread through touching or being near someone who is already infected. Viruses like the common cold or flu can cause pink eye. Bacterial infections are another common cause of pink eye.

This type of infection is usually caused by bacteria like Staphylococcus or Streptococcus, which can be spread through touching someone who is infected or by sharing makeup or contact lenses. Bacterial pink eye often starts in one eye and quickly spreads to the other. Allergies can also cause pink eye. When people are allergic to substances like pollen, dust, or pet dander, their eyes can become inflamed and red.

This type of pink eye is usually seasonal and affects people who live in areas with high levels of allergens.

Below are the main causes of pink eye.

  • Viral Infection: A viral infection, such as the common cold or flu, can cause pink eye when the virus irritates the eyes and leads to inflammation, redness, and discharge.
  • Bacterial Infection: Bacterial infection is a cause of pink eye because certain bacteria, like Staphylococcus aureus and Haemophilus influenzae, can infect the membranes that line the eyelids and the white part of the eye, causing inflammation and discharge.
  • Allergies: When an allergen enters the eye, it can cause the tiny blood vessels to swell, leading to pink eye due to the increased blood flow and inflammation.
  • Contact Lens: Contact lenses can cause pink eye (conjunctivitis) if bacteria or other germs from the lenses get into the eye and irritate the conjunctiva, which is the thin layer of tissue covering the white part of the eye.
  • Swimming Pool: Swimming pool water can cause pink eye because it often contains bacteria such as Pseudomonas aeruginosa, which can easily enter the eyes and cause infection.
  • Blepharitis: Blepharitis can cause pink eye because it inflames the eyelids, which leads to an infection that can easily spread to the conjunctiva, a thin layer of tissue inside the eyelid, causing redness and swelling.
  • Measles: Measles can cause pink eye because the measles virus infects the eyes and leads to inflammation, causing the normally clear white of the eye to turn pink or red due to increased blood flow.

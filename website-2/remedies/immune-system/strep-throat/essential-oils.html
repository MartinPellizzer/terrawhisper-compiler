1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like eucalyptol, alpha-pinene, and limonene.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help combat strep throat. Eucalyptol, in particular, has been shown to reduce swelling and fight infection, while alpha-pinene helps to loosen and clear mucus. Limonene also has antimicrobial properties, making it effective against the bacteria that cause strep throat.

By reducing inflammation and killing bacteria, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of strep throat and promote healing.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan to 100°F (38°C) and add the Eucalyptus leaves.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes to release the oil.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into a bowl, and discard the leaves.
  5. Allow the liquid to cool and sit for 24 hours to separate the oil from the water.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce swelling and fight off infection. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea essential oils also have immunomodulatory effects, supporting the body's natural defense against pathogens like Streptococcus bacteria that cause strep throat. Additionally, the glycosides in Echinacea purpurea essential oils have antiviral properties, which can help combat the viral component of the infection.

By combining these properties, Echinacea purpurea essential oils may provide relief from the symptoms of strep throat, such as sore throat and fever, and help the body recover faster.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers, fresh or dried.
  2. Combine the flowers with 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) in a clean glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Echinacea purpurea essential oil in a dark glass bottle, out of direct sunlight. Use 2-3 drops as needed to help soothe a sore throat.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains bioactive constituents like gingerols and shogaols.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help reduce inflammation and fight off bacterial infections. The oils' analgesic and antipyretic properties can also help alleviate pain and lower fever associated with strep throat. Additionally, the oils' expectorant properties can help loosen and clear mucus from the throat, making it easier to breathe and swallow.

The combination of these properties in Zingiber officinale essential oils makes it a potential natural remedy for soothing strep throat symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale (Ginger) roots, 1 cup of water, and a steam distillation equipment.
  2. Wash the Ginger roots in cold water, then chop them into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped Ginger pieces and water in the steam distillation equipment.
  4. Heat the mixture to 212°F (100°C) and collect the steam, then condense it into a separate container.
  5. Collect the essential oil that forms at the top of the condensed steam and store it in a dark glass bottle.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains carnosic acid and 1,8-cineole as its major bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which are beneficial in treating strep throat. The 1,8-cineole in the oil acts as a expectorant, helping to loosen and clear mucus from the throat, while carnosic acid has antioxidant properties that reduce inflammation and promote healing. The antimicrobial properties of 1,8-cineole also help to combat the Streptococcus bacteria that cause strep throat.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help alleviate the symptoms of strep throat.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over low heat until it starts to simmer.
  3. Add the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves to the saucepan and let them infuse in the water for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the infused water to a dark glass bottle and store it in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. Use a few drops of the mixture as a throat spray to help soothe strep throat.

5. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol, carvacrol, and borneol, which are bioactive constituents with antimicrobial properties.

These properties help to combat the Streptococcus bacteria that cause strep throat. Thymol, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the growth of S. pyogenes, the bacteria responsible for strep throat. Carvacrol has anti-inflammatory effects, which can help reduce swelling and pain in the throat.

By using Thymus vulgaris essential oils, you can potentially alleviate symptoms of strep throat due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and flowers. Clean them with water.
  2. Use 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or coconut oil) and mix it with 1/2 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and flowers.
  3. Steep the mixture for 2-3 hours in a cool, dark place. Shake the container every hour.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Thymus vulgaris essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a diffuser or mix it with water to gargle for strep throat relief.

6. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains several bioactive constituents, including rosmarinic acid, caffeic acid, and ursolic acid.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that can help combat strep throat. Rosmarinic acid and caffeic acid can inhibit the growth of Streptococcus pyogenes, the bacteria that causes strep throat, while ursolic acid can reduce inflammation and pain in the throat. The essential oils' antimicrobial properties can also help prevent the spread of infection, and its anti-inflammatory properties can reduce swelling and discomfort in the throat.

By applying Salvia officinalis essential oils topically or inhaling its vapors, you can potentially alleviate symptoms of strep throat.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves, 2 cups of water, and a glass jar.
  2. Combine the leaves and water in the jar, and let it sit for 2-3 hours to allow the leaves to infuse.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another container. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the liquid, and mix well.
  5. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use a few drops to gargle with warm water 3-4 times a day to help soothe strep throat.

7. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains compounds like limonene, menthone, and menthol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling in the throat, making it easier to swallow and breathe. Mentha x piperita essential oils also contains antimicrobial compounds like linalool and beta-pinene, which target the bacteria that cause strep throat. The antimicrobial properties of Mentha x piperita essential oils help prevent the spread of infection and promote healing in the throat.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off infection, Mentha x piperita essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of strep throat.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh peppermint leaves (Mentha x piperita) and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Clean the leaves with 1 cup of water to remove dirt. Drain excess water.
  3. Add the cleaned leaves to the glass jar and pour 2 cups of water over them.
  4. Steep the mixture in a warm place for 2-3 hours. Strain the liquid using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  5. Collect the strained liquid and use a distillation kit or a still to separate the essential oil from the water. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

8. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their antimicrobial properties.

These properties help reduce the bacteria that cause strep throat, such as Group A beta-hemolytic streptococci. The antiseptic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help to soothe the inflamed throat and reduce the risk of infection. The anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds further help to reduce pain and discomfort associated with strep throat.

The antimicrobial and antiseptic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate in Lavandula angustifolia essential oils work together to help alleviate symptoms of strep throat.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the flowers. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 10 minutes. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a bowl and let it sit in the fridge for 2 hours or overnight. This will help separate the oil from the liquid.
  5. Using a pipette, carefully collect the essential oil from the top surface of the liquid and transfer it to a dark glass bottle.

9. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains a compound called cineole, which has antimicrobial properties that help combat bacterial infections like strep throat.

Cineole works by disrupting the cell membrane of bacteria, ultimately leading to their death. The oil also contains compounds like alpha-terpineol and beta-pinene, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling and pain associated with strep throat. Additionally, the antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils make it a potential natural remedy for soothing a sore throat and promoting healing.

When inhaled or applied topically, the oil's compounds can help alleviate symptoms and support the body's natural defense against infection.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Melaleuca alternifolia leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat and bring it to a boil.
  3. Add the Melaleuca alternifolia leaves to the boiling water and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the leaves.
  5. Allow the mixture to cool and then transfer it to a dark glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops of the essential oil in a warm bath or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

10. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains licorice compounds like glycyrrhizin and glabrol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in the throat. Glycyrrhizin also has antibacterial properties that target the bacteria causing strep throat, such as Group A Streptococcus. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent strep throat from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent strep throat, but it's not a guarantee.

Some herbal teas, like echinacea and sage, have antibacterial properties that might fight off the strep bacteria. However, these teas won't replace standard treatments if you already have strep throat.

They might just help keep your immune system strong and your throat healthy.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for strep throat every day?

Consuming herbal teas daily for strep throat can be safe, but it depends on the tea.

Some herbs like slippery elm and marshmallow root may help soothe the throat. However, others like eucalyptus and peppermint can cause allergic reactions or interact with medications.

It's essential to choose teas that are gentle and suitable for your needs.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in strep throat?

Herbal teas may help with strep throat symptoms, but it's hard to say exactly when you'll feel better.

Some people notice a difference after 2-3 days of drinking the tea, while others might take a week or more.

It's different for everyone, and it really depends on the severity of the infection and your body's response.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for strep throat?

Drinking herbal tea for strep throat can be most helpful when your throat is sore, usually in the morning or afternoon when the pain is worst. Sipping on soothing tea can calm your throat and help ease discomfort.

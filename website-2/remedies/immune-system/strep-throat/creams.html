1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains a variety of bioactive constituents, including alkylamides, caffeic acid, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce swelling and fight off infections in the throat. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea creams have been shown to stimulate the production of white blood cells, which are essential for fighting off bacterial infections like strep throat. The caffeic acid and flavonoids in Echinacea purpurea creams also have antimicrobial properties, which help kill the bacteria that cause strep throat.

By reducing inflammation and killing the bacteria, Echinacea purpurea creams can help alleviate the symptoms of strep throat and promote healing in the throat.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Make a tea by steeping 1/4 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers in 1 cup of boiling water for 5 minutes. Let it cool.
  3. Melt the coconut oil, beeswax, and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  4. Add the cooled Echinacea tea to the melted mixture and stir well. Add the vitamin E oil and mix again.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and harden. Your Echinacea purpurea creams are now ready to use.

These compounds have antimicrobial properties that help combat the bacteria causing strep throat. Cineole has a soothing effect on the throat, reducing pain and discomfort associated with strep throat.

Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of globulol help to reduce swelling and promote a faster recovery.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, 2 tablespoons of eucalyptus essential oil, 2 tablespoons of tea tree essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the distilled water to the melted oil and wax mixture and stir until well combined.
  4. Add the vitamin E oil, eucalyptus essential oil, and tea tree essential oil to the mixture and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let it cool and solidify. Once set, apply a small amount to the affected area to help soothe and heal strep throat.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains triterpenoids, flavonoids, and saponins as its active constituents.

By combining these properties, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams can provide relief and promote healing in individuals with strep throat.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for strep throat every day?

Herbal teas for strep throat can be helpful in soothing symptoms, but drinking them every day may not be safe.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in strep throat?

Herbal teas may help with strep throat symptoms, but it's hard to say exactly how long they take to work.

Some people report feeling better after a few days, while others may not notice a difference for up to a week.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for strep throat?

For strep throat, it's best to drink herbal tea when you're feeling most tired, usually in the afternoon or evening. This is when your body's energy is naturally lower, and the soothing properties of the tea can help calm your throat and promote rest.

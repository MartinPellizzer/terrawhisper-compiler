11 Herbal Teas For Strep Throat

Herbal teas can be a helpful relief for strep throat.

When you have strep throat, your throat is swollen and sore, making it hard to swallow. Herbal teas can help because they have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce swelling and ease the pain. Echinacea purpurea, for example, is a herb that can boost your immune system, helping your body to fight off the infection that's causing the strep throat. Drinking Echinacea tea can help to reduce the severity and duration of the infection.

Another herb, Eucalyptus globulus, has decongestant properties that can help to clear out mucus from your throat, making it easier to swallow and breathe. This can be especially helpful if you have a sore throat and a stuffy nose at the same time. Zingiber officinale, also known as ginger, has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce the swelling in your throat. Ginger tea can also help to soothe and calm the throat, making it feel more comfortable. When you drink ginger tea, the warmth of the liquid can help to relax the muscles in your throat, making it feel less tight and sore.

Drinking herbal teas like these can be a natural and gentle way to help alleviate the symptoms of strep throat, and can be especially helpful when you're not feeling well and don't want to take medication.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, glycoproteins, and polysaccharides.

These compounds have immunomodulatory properties, meaning they can help boost the immune system. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea can reduce inflammation and swelling in the throat, which is beneficial for relieving symptoms of strep throat. The glycoproteins and polysaccharides can also stimulate the production of white blood cells, which are essential for fighting off bacterial infections.

By consuming Echinacea purpurea tea, individuals may be able to help their bodies recover from strep throat more quickly and effectively.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves. If using fresh, make sure to dry them in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 2 hours.
  2. Use a strainer to remove any stems or debris from the flowers and leaves.
  3. Combine 1 tablespoon of dried Echinacea purpurea with 1 cup of boiling water in a cup.
  4. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then strain it using a strainer or cheesecloth.
  5. Drink the tea warm or at room temperature, 3-4 times a day as needed for strep throat relief.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains boiactive constituents like eucalyptol and cineole, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in the throat.

These compounds also have antimicrobial properties that help fight off the Streptococcus bacteria that causes strep throat. The expectorant properties of eucalyptol help loosen and clear out mucus from the throat, making it easier to swallow and breathe. The anti-inflammatory properties of cineole help reduce redness and discomfort in the throat, allowing for faster healing.

Eucalyptol and cineole also have a decongestant effect, helping to open up airways and make breathing easier.

  1. Gather 2 teaspoons of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the mixture.
  3. Add 1 teaspoon of honey to the tea to sweeten it. Stir well.
  4. Drink the tea warm or at room temperature, 2-3 times a day. Repeat as needed.
  5. Consult a doctor before using Eucalyptus globulus tea for strep throat, especially if symptoms persist or worsen.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains active constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and pain in the throat, making it easier to swallow and breathe. Ginger's antioxidant properties also protect against oxidative stress, which can exacerbate strep throat symptoms. Additionally, ginger's antimicrobial properties inhibit the growth of Streptococcus pyogenes, the bacteria that causes strep throat, helping to alleviate symptoms.

Drinking Zingiber officinale teas may help alleviate strep throat symptoms by combining these properties to soothe the throat and combat the infection.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 2-3 slices of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger root).
  2. Peel the ginger slices and cut them into small pieces.
  3. Boil the water in a pot and add the ginger pieces. Reduce heat to low.
  4. Simmer the mixture for 5-7 minutes. Strain it into a cup using a strainer or cheesecloth.
  5. Drink the tea while it's warm. Add honey to taste, if needed.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin that has anti-inflammatory properties.

Glycyrrhizin also has antimicrobial properties, which can help to combat the Streptococcus bacteria that cause strep throat. Additionally, Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains flavonoids, such as luteolin and quercetin, which have antioxidant properties that can help to soothe and calm the inflamed tissues in the throat.

By reducing inflammation and fighting infection, Glycyrrhiza glabra teas can help to alleviate symptoms of strep throat.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots.
  2. Crush 2 tablespoons of the roots into small pieces.
  3. Steep the crushed roots in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid and discard the roots. Add honey to taste if needed.
  5. Drink the tea 3-4 times a day to help soothe strep throat symptoms.

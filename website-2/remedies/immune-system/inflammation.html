What Are The Main Causes Of Inflammation?

Inflammation occurs when our body's defense system reacts to harm or damage.

The main causes of inflammation are bacterial infections, viral infections, and trauma. Bacterial infections happen when bad bacteria invade our body and multiply quickly. Our immune system sends white blood cells to fight the bacteria, which leads to inflammation. This is a normal response to help get rid of the infection. For example, when we cut our skin and it becomes red and swollen, it's a sign that our body is fighting off the bacteria that entered through the cut. Viral infections also cause inflammation.

When a virus enters our body, it tricks our immune system into thinking it's a foreign object, and our body sends white blood cells to attack it. This leads to inflammation and symptoms like fever, redness, and swelling. Viral infections can affect any part of our body, from our nose (like with a cold) to our lungs (like with pneumonia). Trauma, or injury, is another cause of inflammation. When our body is injured, it becomes damaged and sends a signal to our immune system to respond. This response includes the release of chemicals that cause blood vessels to swell, leading to redness, heat, and pain. Trauma can be caused by accidents, sports injuries, or even surgery.

Our body's response to trauma is a natural attempt to heal the damage and prevent further harm.

Below are the main causes of inflammation.

  • Bacterial infection: When a bacterial infection enters the body, the immune system detects the foreign invaders and releases chemicals to fight them, leading to inflammation as the body tries to isolate and eliminate the infection.
  • Viral infection: When a virus enters the body, it triggers an immune response, releasing chemicals that signal the body to fight the infection, leading to inflammation.
  • Trauma: When the body experiences trauma, it sees the injury as a threat, and in response, it releases chemicals that increase blood flow and white blood cell count to fight the perceived infection, but this process causes inflammation, which can lead to more damage and prolonged healing time.
  • Burns: Burns cause inflammation because the heat damage releases chemicals that trigger the body's immune response, leading to increased blood flow, swelling, and pain in the affected area.
  • Surgery: When a surgeon cuts into the body during surgery, it causes damage to the surrounding tissues, leading to an immune response and the release of chemicals that trigger inflammation.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Inflammation?

If you have inflammation, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can make it worse.

For example, ginger and turmeric are commonly used for inflammation, but there are other herbs to steer clear of. Sage, for instance, can increase stomach acid production, which may irritate inflamed tissues. Licorice root, while often used for its anti-inflammatory properties, contains a compound called glycyrrhizin that can cause fluid retention and worsen swelling.

Similarly, cayenne pepper, or capsaicin, can cause blood vessels to dilate, which may increase inflammation in the short term. And while passionflower is often used to calm anxiety, it can also cause blood vessels to relax, potentially making inflammation worse. Additionally, yarrow and chamomile may cause allergic reactions in some people, especially if they are already experiencing inflammation.

Finally, angelica and goldenseal can cause blood to thin, which may be problematic for people with inflammatory conditions.

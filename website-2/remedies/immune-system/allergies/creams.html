What herbal creams you should avoid if you have allergies?

If you have allergies, there are some herbal creams you should avoid.

For example, creams made from comfrey (Symphytum uplandicum) may be a problem because they contain compounds called pyrrolizidine alkaloids (PAs), which can cause liver damage and other problems in people with allergies. Witch hazel (Hamamelis virginiana) is another herb to be cautious with, as some people may be allergic to its sap or have sensitive skin that reacts to its astringent properties.

Another herb to watch out for is tea tree oil (Cananga odorata), which can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) is sometimes used in creams, but its allergenic potential is not well understood, so it's best to use it with caution. Finally, creams made from red clover (Trifolium pratense) may be a problem for people with allergies because they contain isoflavones, which can cause allergic reactions in some individuals.

If you're unsure about any herbal cream, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional or a dermatologist before using it.

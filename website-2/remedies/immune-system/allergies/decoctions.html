What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have allergies?

If you have allergies, there are certain herbal decoctions you should avoid.

One such herb is Butterbur (Petasites hybridus), which contains a compound called pyrrolizidine alkaloids (PAs). PAs can cause liver damage and worsen allergies. Another herb to avoid is Comfrey (Symphytum officinale), also containing PAs, which can cause liver damage and other health problems.

You should also be cautious with Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) and Oregon grape root (Mahonia aquifolium), both of which contain berberine. While berberine can have health benefits, it can also cause allergic reactions and interact with other medications. Additionally, you should be careful with Black cohosh (Actaea racemosa) and Blue cohosh (Caulophyllum thalictroides), both of which can cause allergic reactions and interact with blood thinners.

Lastly, Coleus (Solenostemon scutellarioides) and Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) can also cause allergic reactions in some people.

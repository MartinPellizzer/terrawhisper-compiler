1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains flavonoids, alkylamides, and caffeic acid derivatives, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and ease pain in inflamed gums. Alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to inflammation. Caffeic acid derivatives also have antimicrobial properties, which can help combat infections that may be contributing to gum inflammation.

The combination of these bioactive constituents makes Echinacea purpurea tinctures a potentially effective remedy for managing inflamed gums.

  1. Gather 250ml of 80% Vodka, 250g of Echinacea purpurea roots and flowers, and a glass jar.
  2. Combine the Echinacea roots and flowers in the glass jar.
  3. Pour the vodka over the Echinacea mixture, making sure they are fully covered.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-6 weeks, shaking it every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into another glass jar, discard the solids and store the tincture in the fridge.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis tinctures contains biologically active constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A, C, and E. These constituents have anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiseptic properties that help soothe and calm inflamed gums.

The aloe-emodin in aloe barbadensis tinctures reduces inflammation by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, which cause pain and swelling. The antibacterial properties of aloin help prevent infection and promote healing in the gums.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infection, aloe barbadensis tinctures helps to alleviate symptoms of inflamed gums, promoting a healthy oral environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 1 cup of 80-proof vodka, and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Cut the Aloe barbadensis leaf and extract the gel. Place the gel in the glass jar.
  3. Pour 1 cup of vodka over the Aloe gel. Stir well to combine.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids and use the tincture as needed.

3. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis tinctures contains rosmarinic acid and camphor as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and ease pain in inflamed gums. Rosmarinic acid also has antioxidant properties that combat free radicals and promote healing in the gums. Camphor, on the other hand, has a cooling effect that can help numb the pain and reduce inflammation.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Salvia officinalis tinctures can be a helpful remedy for inflamed gums.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves and 2 cups of 80% ethanol (vodka or everclear work too). Wear gloves to avoid skin irritation.
  2. Combine the Salvia officinalis leaves and ethanol in a clean glass jar. Stir well to make sure all leaves are covered.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar daily to help the tincture extract.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Measure 20 drops of the tincture (about 1 teaspoon) and mix it with 8 ounces of water. Use this solution to rinse your mouth and soothe inflamed gums.

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains glycyrrhizin, a key active constituent that reduces inflammation in the gums.

Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help soothe and calm inflamed gums. Licorice root tinctures also contain flavonoids, such as luteolin, and saponins, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. 5. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contains bioactive constituents like eucalyptol, which is known for its anti-inflammatory properties.

6. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

Gingerol works by blocking the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals called prostaglandins, which cause swelling and pain in inflamed gums. Zingiber officinale also contains a compound called shogaol, which has antimicrobial properties that help reduce bacterial growth in the mouth, further reducing inflammation. The warming effect of ginger can increase blood flow to the affected area, promoting healing and reducing swelling.

By reducing inflammation and fighting infection, Zingiber officinale tinctures can help alleviate inflamed gums.

  3. Mix 2 cups of vodka with the ginger paste in a clean glass jar. Cover the jar with a lid.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for inflamed gums every day?

Herbal teas can be a soothing way to calm inflamed gums, but it's not a substitute for regular dental care.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in inflamed gums?

Herbal teas can help reduce inflammation in gums, but it takes time to notice results.

It's essential to be consistent and patient when trying herbal teas for gum inflammation.

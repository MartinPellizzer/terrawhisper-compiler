1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like cineole, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in inflamed gums.

The oil's antimicrobial properties, thanks to the presence of compounds like alpha-pinene and beta-pinene, fight bacteria that can cause gum infections. Cineole's ability to increase blood flow to the affected area also helps to promote healing and reduce inflammation. Additionally, the oil's analgesic properties, due to the presence of eucalyptol, provide relief from pain and discomfort associated with inflamed gums.

By reducing inflammation and fighting bacteria, Eucalyptus globulus essential oils helps to promote a healthy oral environment and alleviate symptoms of inflamed gums.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water on low heat and add the Eucalyptus globulus leaves.
  3. Let it simmer for 2-3 hours to release the oil from the leaves.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl to separate the oil from the solids.
  5. Store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a dark glass bottle for up to 6 months.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains a compound called cineole, which has antimicrobial properties that help fight off the bacteria that cause gum inflammation.

Cineole also has anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling and redness in the gums. Additionally, the oil contains compounds like terpinen-4-ol, which has been shown to inhibit the growth of certain bacteria that contribute to gum disease. The antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils work together to help soothe and calm inflamed gums, promoting healthy healing and reducing discomfort.

When applied topically, these compounds help to reduce inflammation and promote a healthy environment for gum tissue to recover.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Melaleuca alternifolia leaves. Choose leaves with no signs of mold or damage.
  2. Wash the leaves with 1 cup of cold water to remove dirt and bacteria. Pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Place the leaves in a clean glass jar and cover them with 2 cups of 100 proof vodka. Make sure the leaves are completely covered.
  4. Steep the mixture for 2 weeks in a dark place, shaking the jar every day. This will help extract the oil from the leaves.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass container. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

3. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains rosmarinic acid, tannins, and ursolic acid as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in inflamed gums. The essential oil also contains antioxidant properties that prevent damage to gum tissue and promote healing.   1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water.
  2. Wash the leaves with clean water and let them dry with a paper towel.
  3. In a saucepan, combine the dried leaves and water. Heat it over low heat for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid and store it in a cool, dark place. Use a few drops of the oil to rub on inflamed gums.

What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have inflamed gums?

If you have inflamed gums, it's best to avoid certain herbal essential oils that can irritate or inflame the condition further.

Another oil to be cautious with is Thyme oil (Thymus vulgaris). Although thyme has antibacterial properties, its high concentration of thymol can be harsh on inflamed gums, potentially causing more discomfort and pain. Additionally, Clove oil (Eugenia caryophyllata) should be used with caution as it contains eugenol, which can further irritate the gums and exacerbate inflammation.

Finally, Oregano oil (Origanum vulgare) contains carvacrol, a compound that can be too potent for inflamed gums, potentially leading to increased sensitivity and discomfort.

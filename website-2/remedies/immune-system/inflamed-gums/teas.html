8 Herbal Remedies in a Cup: Teas for Older Adults with Inflamed Gums

As we age, our gums can become inflamed, a condition known as gingivitis.

This happens because our teeth and gums are more sensitive, and we may not be able to clean them as well as we used to. Inflamed gums can cause pain, bleeding, and discomfort when eating or speaking, making everyday activities difficult for older adults. It can also lead to bad breath, which can be embarrassing and affect self-esteem. In some cases, inflamed gums can even be a sign of a more serious health issue, such as diabetes or heart disease. Older adults may also have to deal with other health issues, such as arthritis, which can make it harder to clean their teeth and gums properly.

This can create a cycle of pain and discomfort that can be hard to break. When we have inflamed gums, we need to find ways to soothe and calm the area. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to help with this. These teas contain special properties that can reduce inflammation and promote healing in the gums. They can also help to reduce pain and discomfort, making it easier to eat and speak. Some herbal teas may even have antibacterial properties, which can help to prevent infections and promote healthy gums.

Drinking herbal teas can be a gentle and natural way to treat inflamed gums, and it can be especially helpful for older adults who may not be able to use harsh chemicals or undergo invasive treatments.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas have been traditionally used to help alleviate inflamed gums.

This plant, also known as purple coneflower, contains anti-inflammatory properties and constituents such as alkylamides, phenolic acids, and flavonoids. These compounds are known to reduce swelling and ease pain in the gums. The tea's active ingredients, including isobutyl amide and echinacoside, have been shown to inhibit the production of enzymes that cause inflammation. To use Echinacea purpurea teas for inflamed gums, you can make a tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried flowers in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and discard the solids.

You can drink the tea 2-3 times a day, after meals to help reduce inflammation and ease discomfort. To get the most out of this remedy, try to reduce your intake of sugary foods and drinks, which can exacerbate gum inflammation. As you regularly drink the Echinacea purpurea tea, you may notice a reduction in gum inflammation and pain. Your gums may feel softer and less tender, and the swelling may decrease. You may also experience a reduction in bleeding and discomfort when eating or brushing your teeth. However, it's essential to note that this remedy may take some time to work, so be patient and consistent with your tea consumption.

Additionally, if your gum inflammation persists or worsens, you should consult your dentist or healthcare provider for further guidance and treatment.

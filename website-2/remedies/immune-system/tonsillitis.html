What Are The Main Causes Of Tonsillitis?

Tonsillitis is an infection of the tonsils, which are two small glands in the back of the throat.

The main causes of tonsillitis are usually bacterial or viral infections. Bacterial infections, such as strep throat, are caused by a type of bacteria called Group A Streptococcus. This bacteria can spread from person to person through close contact, such as touching or sharing food and drinks. Bacterial infections often cause severe symptoms, including a high fever, swollen tonsils, and white or yellow patches on the tonsils.

Viral infections, on the other hand, are caused by viruses such as the common cold or flu. These viruses can spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes, or through close contact with an infected person. Viral infections often cause milder symptoms, such as a sore throat, runny nose, and fatigue. Strep throat is a specific type of bacterial infection that can cause severe tonsillitis. It's often caused by Group A Streptococcus bacteria and can spread through close contact with an infected person.

Strep throat can cause severe symptoms, including a high fever, swollen tonsils, and white or yellow patches on the tonsils.

Below are the main causes of tonsillitis.

  • Bacterial Infection: Tonsillitis occurs when a bacterial infection, such as strep A, enters the body and causes inflammation in the tonsils, leading to symptoms like sore throat and difficulty swallowing.
  • Viral Infection: A viral infection, usually caused by the common cold or flu viruses, can lead to tonsillitis when it irritates and inflames the tonsils, causing redness, swelling, and pain in the throat.
  • Strep Throat: Strep throat is a common cause of tonsillitis because the bacteria that cause strep throat, called Group A Streptococcus, infect the tonsils, making them swollen and painful.
  • Group A Beta Hemolytic Streptococcus: Group A Beta Hemolytic Streptococcus is a cause of tonsillitis because its bacteria can easily invade the tonsils, causing inflammation and infection that leads to symptoms like sore throats and swollen tonsils.
  • Mononucleosis: Mononucleosis is a cause of tonsillitis because the Epstein-Barr virus that causes it makes the immune system attack the tonsils, leading to inflammation and swelling.
  • Epstein Barr Virus: The Epstein Barr Virus (EBV) can cause tonsillitis because it infects and inflames the tiny glands in the throat, called tonsils, leading to pain, swelling, and other symptoms.
  • Adenovirus: The Adenovirus is a common cause of tonsillitis because it can easily infect the tonsils, a part of the throat, and cause them to become red, swollen, and painful, leading to inflammation and discomfort.

If you have tonsillitis, you should avoid certain herbs that can make your symptoms worse.

One herb to avoid is 'Hydrastis canadensis' or goldenseal. It contains a chemical called berberine, which can lower blood sugar levels and affect blood clotting. This can be a problem if you're already taking medications for tonsillitis. 'Ephedra sinica' or Ma Huang is another herb to avoid.

It contains ephedrine, a stimulant that can raise your blood pressure and heart rate. This can make your tonsillitis symptoms worse, especially if you have heart problems. 'Ginkgo biloba' is also not recommended, as it can increase bleeding and interact with blood thinners. 'Panax quinquefolius' or American ginseng can lower blood pressure and affect blood sugar levels, making your tonsillitis symptoms worse.

'Valeriana officinalis' or valerian root can cause drowsiness and interact with sedatives, which may not be safe when you have an infection like tonsillitis.

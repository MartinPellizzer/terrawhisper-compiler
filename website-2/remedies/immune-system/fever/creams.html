1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and phenolic acids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties that can help reduce fever by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea creams have been shown to have a suppressive effect on the production of TNF-alpha, a cytokine that plays a key role in the development of fever. The caffeic acid derivatives in Echinacea purpurea creams have been found to have antioxidant properties, which can help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation associated with fever.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Echinacea purpurea creams may help alleviate symptoms of fever and promote recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler until it reaches 160°F.
  3. Add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the melted coconut oil and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Mix 1 tablespoon of beeswax with the infused coconut oil and stir until the beeswax is melted and the mixture is smooth.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains a compound called eucalyptol, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Eucalyptol has a cooling effect on the body, helping to reduce fever and relieve aches. The cream also contains other active constituents like flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe the skin and reduce swelling. When applied topically, eucalyptus globulus creams can help to open up airways and improve circulation, which can help the body to recover from a fever more efficiently.

This is because the cream's active compounds can help to break down mucus and promote sweating, two key processes in recovering from a fever.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Eucalyptus globulus essential oil, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second increments, stirring between each heating.
  3. Once melted, remove from heat and add the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil and vitamin E oil. Stir well to combine.
  4. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify completely. This may take about 30 minutes to an hour.
  5. Once solid, your Eucalyptus globulus cream is ready to use. Apply a small amount to the affected area to help relieve fever symptoms.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains the active constituents gingerol and shogaol, which are responsible for its fever-reducing properties.

Gingerols and shogaols have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which help to reduce pain and swelling associated with fever. They also have a thermogenic effect, which helps to lower the body temperature. Additionally, the creams contain flavonoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, which have antioxidant properties that help to reduce oxidative stress caused by fever.

By combining these properties, Zingiber officinale creams can provide relief from fever symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Ginger root, 2 cups of water, and a cheesecloth.
  2. Boil the water and add the dried Ginger root. Let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture using the cheesecloth and squeeze out as much liquid as possible.
  4. Combine 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and the Ginger liquid in a saucepan. Heat until the beeswax melts.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool. Once set, use as a cream for fever relief.

4. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis creams contains quinine, a key alkaloid that helps reduce fever.

Quinine has a cooling effect on the body, which helps to lower the temperature. The other alkaloids, such as cinchonine and cinchonidine, also contribute to this effect. These compounds have a vasoconstrictive property, which means they narrow the blood vessels, reducing blood flow to the skin and causing a cooling sensation.

As a result, Cinchona officinalis creams can be effective in treating fever by bringing down the body temperature.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Cinchona officinalis bark, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Grind the dried Cinchona officinalis bark into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Melt the coconut oil, beeswax, and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  4. Remove the melted mixture from heat and stir in the ground Cinchona officinalis powder and vitamin E oil until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let it cool and solidify completely before use. Store in a cool, dark place.

5. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains a high concentration of bioactive compounds, including cineole, limonene, and linalool.

These compounds have antiviral, antibacterial, and anti-inflammatory properties, which make them effective in reducing fever. Cineole, in particular, helps to thin mucus and relieve congestion, while limonene has decongestant properties that help to clear the airways. Linalool has a soothing effect on the skin, reducing heat and discomfort.

By using Melaleuca alternifolia creams, the skin can breathe and sweat more efficiently, helping the body to cool down and recover from fever.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil), and 2 tablespoons of beeswax in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the beeswax and carrier oil in the double boiler over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil to the melted mixture and stir well.
  4. Slowly add the distilled water to the mixture, stirring constantly to avoid lumps.
  5. Remove the double boiler from the heat and let the mixture cool and thicken. Once thickened, pour into containers and store in a cool place.

6. Ginkgo biloba

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for fever every day?

Herbal teas can be a soothing way to help with fever, but having them every day may not be a good idea.

It's best to use them in moderation and for short periods.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in fever?

Herbal teas can help with fever relief, but the time it takes for them to show results varies.

For some herbs like peppermint and chamomile, you might feel better within 30 minutes to an hour after drinking.

For others, like yarrow and elderflower, it might take a few hours or overnight to notice a difference in your temperature.

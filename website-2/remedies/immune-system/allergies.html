Allergies: Causes, Symptoms, and Natural Relief with Herbal Preparations

Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus, also known as the Tasmanian blue gum, has been used for years to help people with allergies. Its leaves contain a strong oil that is released when the plant is crushed or heated. This oil has powerful anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling in the nose and sinuses. When inhaled, the oil can also thin and clear out mucus, making it easier to breathe. This is especially helpful for people with conditions like bronchitis and asthma, where the airways are inflamed and congested. The oil can also help kill bacteria and other germs that can cause infections and make allergies worse. Many people use eucalyptus globulus in the form of essential oil, which can be added to a diffuser or inhale directly from a cloth or handkerchief. It can also be found in over-the-counter medications like cough drops and inhalers.

Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea, also known as purple coneflower, is a plant that has been used for centuries to help with allergies. The flowers and leaves of this plant contain special chemicals that can help reduce inflammation in the body, which is a main cause of allergy symptoms. When we have an allergy, our body's immune system overreacts to something harmless, like pollen, and releases chemicals that cause swelling, itching, and congestion. Echinacea purpurea works by calming down the immune system and preventing these chemicals from being released. This can help alleviate symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, and itchy eyes. Additionally, Echinacea purpurea has antioxidant properties that help protect cells from damage and support the immune system, making it a natural remedy for allergies.

Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba is a plant that has been used for many years to help with allergies. Its leaves contain special chemicals that help to reduce swelling and fight against the tiny particles that cause allergic reactions. These particles, called allergens, are found in things like pollen, dust, and mold. When we breathe them in, our body tries to fight them off, which can lead to symptoms like congestion, sneezing, and itching. Ginkgo biloba helps to block the allergens from reaching our body's cells, which makes it harder for them to cause a reaction. This plant is especially good at blocking the particles that cause problems for people with seasonal allergies, like hay fever. By reducing the body's response to allergens, Ginkgo biloba can help to make symptoms like runny nose, itchy eyes, and coughing less severe.

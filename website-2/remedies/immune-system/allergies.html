What Are The Main Causes Of Allergies?

Allergies occur when our body's immune system overreacts to a harmless substance.

This reaction is caused by the release of antibodies, which mistake the substance for a threat. The main causes of allergies include: Peanuts: Peanuts contain a protein called Ara h 1, which is easily broken down into smaller pieces. When these pieces enter the body, the immune system identifies them as a threat and releases histamine, a chemical that causes allergic symptoms. This reaction can range from mild hives to life-threatening anaphylaxis. Tree nuts: Similar to peanuts, tree nuts contain proteins that the immune system mistakenly identifies as a threat.

These proteins are often found in foods like almonds, walnuts, and cashews. When tree nuts are eaten, the immune system releases histamine, which can cause symptoms like itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing. Milk: Milk contains a protein called casein, which some people's immune systems mistake for a threat. This can cause an allergic reaction, which may include symptoms like hives, itching, and digestive issues. In severe cases, milk allergies can cause anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition that requires immediate medical attention. Other common causes of allergies include pollen, dust mites, and pet dander.

These substances can trigger allergic reactions, which may range from mild to severe.

Below are the main causes of allergies.

  • Peanuts: Peanuts are a common cause of allergies because the body mistakes a protein called Ara h 1 in peanuts as a threat, leading to an allergic reaction.
  • Tree nuts: Tree nuts cause allergies because our immune system mistakenly thinks the proteins in them are bad guys, and tries to fight them off, leading to a range of symptoms.
  • Milk: Milk is a common cause of allergies because some people's bodies mistakenly think the proteins in milk, such as casein and whey, are bad and react to them by producing allergic symptoms like hives and itching.
  • Eggs: Eggs are a common cause of allergies because the proteins in egg whites and yolks can mistakenly attack and hurt the body's immune system, leading to an allergic reaction.
  • Wheat: Wheat is a common cause of allergies because some people's bodies mistakenly see the proteins in wheat, such as gliadin and avenalin, as enemies and react to them by causing symptoms like itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing.
  • Soy: Soy is a common cause of allergies because the body mistakenly identifies the proteins in soybeans, such as soy lectin and soy trypsin inhibitor, as a threat, triggering an immune response and allergic reactions.
  • Fish: Fish is a common cause of allergies because some people's bodies mistakenly identify the proteins found in fish, such as salmon and cod, as harmful invaders, triggering an allergic reaction.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Allergies?

If you have allergies, there are some herbs you should avoid.

Ragweed (Ambrosia artemisiifolia) is one to steer clear of because it belongs to the same plant family as ragweed plants that cause allergies. Feverfew (Tanacetum parthenium) can also be a problem, as it's related to plants that cause allergic reactions. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) may cause allergic symptoms in some people, especially those with allergies to ragweed or dandelions.

Anise (Pimpinella anisum) and fennel (Foeniculum vulgare) are also potential allergens, as they belong to the same plant family as celery, which can cause an allergic reaction in some people. Another herb to be cautious with is tansy (Tanacetum vulgare), as it's related to feverfew and can cause similar allergic reactions. Finally, cypress (Taxodium spp.). and juniper (Juniperus spp.).

can cause allergic symptoms in some people, especially those with allergies to pine or cedar trees.

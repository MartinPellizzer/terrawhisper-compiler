What Are The Main Causes Of Infection?

There are several main causes of infection.

One common cause is bacterial infections. These occur when harmful bacteria enter the body, often through cuts or wounds, and multiply, causing illness. Bacteria can be found everywhere, even on our skin, and some types are harmless, but others can cause serious infections. Another cause of infection is viral infections. These happen when a virus enters the body and attacks healthy cells, causing illness.

Viruses can spread through the air, through touch, or by coming into contact with an infected person's bodily fluids. They can cause a wide range of illnesses, from the common cold to serious diseases like HIV/AIDS. MRSA (Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus) is a type of bacterial infection that is resistant to many antibiotics. This means that it's very difficult to treat with medication, and can cause serious infections, especially in people with weakened immune systems. MRSA is often spread through close contact with an infected person or by touching contaminated surfaces.

These causes of infection can be prevented by practicing good hygiene, such as washing our hands regularly, and taking steps to avoid coming into contact with contaminated surfaces or people who are ill.

Below are the main causes of infection.

  • Bacterial Infections: Bacterial infections occur when bacteria, tiny living things that can cause disease, enter the body and multiply, making the person sick.
  • Viral Infections: Viral infections are a common cause of infections because tiny viruses can easily enter the body through the air we breathe, contaminated surfaces, or through contact with an infected person, and then multiply inside our cells, causing harm and illness.
  • MRSA: MRSA is a cause of infection because it is a type of bacteria that has developed resistance to many antibiotics, allowing it to grow and spread inside the body, causing infections that can be difficult to treat.
  • E. coli: E. coli is a cause of infection because it is a type of bacteria that can enter the body through contaminated food or water and then multiply inside the body, producing toxins that can make people very sick.
  • Staphylococcus aureus: Staphylococcus aureus is a cause of infection because its bacteria can easily enter the body, multiply, and produce toxins that damage tissues and cause a range of symptoms, from mild skin infections to life-threatening diseases.
  • Hepatitis B: Hepatitis B is a cause of infection because it is a viral disease that spreads through bodily fluids, such as blood and semen, from an infected person to a healthy person through contact, sharing needles, or unprotected sex.
  • Hepatitis C: Hepatitis C is a cause of infection because it's a viral disease that spreads through contact with infected blood, usually through sharing needles or having unprotected sex with someone who has the virus.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Infection?

If you have an infection, it's essential to be cautious with certain herbs that can interfere with treatment or make symptoms worse.

One herb to avoid is 'Valeriana officinalis' (Valerian root), as it can slow down the healing process due to its sedative properties, which may cause drowsiness and disorientation. 'Ginkgo biloba' can also be problematic, as it can thin the blood and increase the risk of bleeding, making it challenging to manage infections, especially those with a high risk of bleeding, such as tuberculosis. Another herb to be cautious with is 'Aloe vera', as its anti-inflammatory properties can mask the symptoms of an infection, making it harder to diagnose.

Additionally, 'Hydrastis canadensis' (Goldenseal) should be used with caution, as its berberine content can interact with antibiotics and other medications, reducing their effectiveness.

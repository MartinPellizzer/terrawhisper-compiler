What Are The Main Causes Of Fever?

When the body gets sick, it often shows signs of fever.

The main causes of fever are infections and other health issues. One cause of fever is a urinary tract infection (UTI). This happens when bacteria enter the urinary system through the urethra and multiply, causing an infection. The body's immune system responds by increasing the body temperature, leading to a fever. Another cause of fever is pneumonia, which is an infection of the lungs.

When bacteria or viruses infect the lungs, the body's immune system tries to fight it off by raising the body temperature. Tonsillitis is also a common cause of fever. This condition occurs when the tonsils, which are the lymphoid glands located in the back of the throat, get infected with bacteria or viruses. The body's immune system responds to the infection by increasing the body temperature, resulting in a fever. Other causes of fever include viral infections like the flu, bacterial infections like strep throat, and even some medical procedures.

When the body's immune system detects an infection, it sends out chemical signals to increase the body temperature, leading to a fever.

Below are the main causes of fever.

  • Urinary Tract Infection: When bacteria infect the urinary tract, they can multiply and release toxins that cause the body to produce a fever, which is a sign that the body is fighting off the infection.
  • Pneumonia: Pneumonia causes fever because the bacteria or virus that causes the infection triggers the body's immune system to send white blood cells to fight the invaders, which leads to a rise in body temperature.
  • Tonsillitis: Tonsillitis causes fever because the infection in the tonsils triggers the body's immune system to produce chemicals called pyrogens, which increase the body temperature and lead to a fever.
  • Meningitis: Meningitis is a cause of fever because the bacteria or virus that causes this infection makes the body's immune system fight back, releasing chemicals that increase body temperature and cause fever.
  • Sinusitis: Sinusitis causes fever because the bacterial or viral infection in the sinuses releases chemicals that trigger the body's immune response, leading to a rise in body temperature as the body fights the infection.
  • Appendicitis: Appendicitis causes fever because the infected appendix in the abdomen releases chemicals that increase the body's temperature, leading to a rise in body temperature and a fever.
  • Food Poisoning: Food poisoning causes fever because the bacteria or viruses in the spoiled food enter the body, trigger an immune response, and make the body's temperature rise to fight off the infection.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Fever?

If you have a fever, there are certain herbs you should avoid.

Sage, also known as Salvia officinalis, should be avoided as it can increase sweating and heart rate, which may not be good for people with fever. Another herb to avoid is Valerian root, also known as Valeriana officinalis, which can cause drowsiness and may worsen a fever.

Black cohosh, also known as Actaea racemosa, can also be problematic as it may lower blood pressure and slow down heart rate, which can be bad for people with a fever. Blue cohosh, also known as Caulophyllum thalictroides, can cause stomach upset and may interact with other medications, making it best to avoid it when you have a fever. Licorice root, also known as Glycyrrhiza glabra, should be avoided as it can raise blood pressure and worsen fever symptoms.

Finally, Ephedra, also known as Ephedra sinica, should be avoided as it can increase heart rate and blood pressure, which may not be good for people with a fever.

What Are The Main Causes Of Flu?

The flu is caused by various viruses.

There are three main types that affect people: Influenza A, Influenza B, and Rhinovirus. Influenza A is a serious and contagious type of flu that can cause severe illness and even death.

It spreads quickly because it can infect animals and humans, and it mutates easily, making it hard for the body to fight it. This means that people can get Influenza A multiple times in their lifetime because their immune system may not remember the previous infection. Influenza B is also contagious and can cause mild to moderate illness, but it usually doesn't cause severe problems like Influenza A. Rhinovirus, on the other hand, is the most common cause of the common cold, but it can also cause the flu, especially in people with weakened immune systems.

This virus is highly contagious and spreads through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes.

Below are the main causes of flu.

  • Influenza A: Influenza A is a cause of flu because it's a type of virus that can easily spread from person to person and make people sick with symptoms like fever, cough, and body aches.
  • Influenza B: Influenza B is a cause of flu because it is a type of virus that infects the airways and respiratory system, making people sick with symptoms such as fever, cough, and sore throat.
  • Rhinovirus: Rhinovirus is a common cause of the flu because it infects the nose and throat, leading to symptoms such as runny nose, sore throat, and fever.
  • Parainfluenza: Parainfluenza is a cause of flu because it's a virus that attacks the upper respiratory system, causing symptoms similar to the flu, such as a sore throat, cough, and fever.
  • Respiratory Syncytial: Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV) is not a cause of the flu, but rather a separate respiratory illness that affects young children and can cause symptoms similar to the flu, including fever, cough, and runny nose.
  • Influenza C: Influenza C is a cause of flu because it's a type of virus that infects the body's respiratory system, causing symptoms such as coughing, sore throat, and fever.
  • Adenovirus: Adenovirus is a cause of flu-like illness because it infects the respiratory system, causing symptoms such as runny nose, sore throat, cough, and fever, which are similar to the flu.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Flu?

If you have the flu, there are some herbs you should avoid.

One of these herbs is 'Hyssopus officinalis', also known as hyssop. It can cause stomach problems and worsen breathing issues, which are common flu symptoms. Another herb to be cautious with is 'Elettaria cardamomum', or cardamom. While it can help with digestion, it can also cause nausea and stomach upset in people with flu.

You should also avoid 'Foeniculum vulgare', or fennel. Its seeds can cause stomach cramps and gas, which can be uncomfortable when you're already feeling unwell. Additionally, 'Matricaria chamomilla', or chamomile, can interact with certain medications used to treat the flu, such as antiviral medications. Lastly, 'Angelica archangelica', or angelica, can cause liver damage if taken in large amounts.

If you have the flu, it's best to stick to small amounts of herbs like peppermint and ginger.

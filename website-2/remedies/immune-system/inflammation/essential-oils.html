9 Herbal Essential Oils For Inflammation

Herbal essential oils can be a great natural way to reduce inflammation in the body.

Inflammation is a normal response to injury or infection, but chronic inflammation can lead to serious health issues. Herbal teas can help alleviate inflammation due to their active compounds that have anti-inflammatory properties.

For example, rosemary essential oil, extracted from the leaves of Rosmarinus officinalis, contains carnosic acid, which has been shown to reduce inflammation and improve cognitive function. Eucalyptus oil, derived from the leaves of Eucalyptus globulus, contains eucalyptol, a compound that has anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties, making it a popular ingredient in pain-relieving creams and inhalers. Turmeric essential oil, extracted from the rhizome of Curcuma longa, contains curcumin, a powerful anti-inflammatory compound that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine.

Drinking herbal teas made with these oils can help reduce inflammation, which can bring relief from conditions such as arthritis, muscle pain, and respiratory issues.

1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains carnosic acid and rosmarinic acid, which are powerful antioxidants that help combat inflammation.

These bioactive constituents work by neutralizing free radicals and reducing oxidative stress, a major contributor to inflammation. Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils also contains beta-caryophyllene, a sesquiterpene that has anti-inflammatory properties by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines. Additionally, the antioxidants in Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils help reduce inflammation by protecting the body's cells from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing oxidative stress and inflammation, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils may help alleviate symptoms associated with inflammation, such as pain and swelling.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat until it's hot but not boiling.
  3. Add the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves to the hot water and let them steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Use a hydrosol distiller or a simple distiller to separate the essential oil from the water. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains the bioactive constituents eucalyptol, alpha-pinene, and limonene.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce swelling and pain. Eucalyptol, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, such as COX-2 and LOX. This action helps to block the body's inflammatory response and alleviate symptoms associated with conditions like arthritis and sprains.

The decongestant properties of alpha-pinene and limonene also help to open airways and reduce inflammation in the respiratory system.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a clean glass jar, and a steam distillation kit or a home distillation apparatus.
  2. Wash the leaves with 1 cup of water to remove dirt and impurities, then pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Add the leaves to the distillation apparatus and heat the mixture with 2 cups of water at 100°C (212°F) for 2-3 hours.
  4. Collect the distilled essential oil in a separate container using a separatory funnel or a condenser.
  5. Filter the essential oil using a coffee filter to remove any impurities, then store it in a dark glass bottle for later use.

3. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a potent compound called curcumin, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

Curcumin is a bioactive constituent that has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines, thus reducing inflammation. Curcuma longa essential oils also contains demethoxycurcumin and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which have similar anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds work by blocking the action of enzymes that contribute to inflammation, such as COX-2 and LOX, and by scavenging free radicals that cause oxidative stress.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, curcuma longa essential oils can help to alleviate symptoms associated with conditions such as arthritis and other inflammatory diseases.

  1. Gather 500 grams of fresh Curcuma longa rhizomes and wash them with water.
  2. Dry the rhizomes in a warm place for 2-3 days until they are completely dry.
  3. Grind 100 grams of the dried rhizomes into a fine powder using a blender or spice grinder.
  4. Add 1 cup of carrier oil (such as coconut or olive oil) to the powdered rhizomes and mix well.
  5. Steep the mixture in a dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the container daily, then strain and discard the solids.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and ease pain. Linalool has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which are molecules that promote inflammation in the body. Linalyl acetate has antioxidant properties that help neutralize free radicals, which can contribute to inflammation.

By reducing inflammation and pain, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can provide relief for conditions such as arthritis and sore muscles.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan until it starts boiling, then reduce the heat to medium.
  3. Add the Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the saucepan and let them steep for 30-40 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass container, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the resulting liquid in a dark glass bottle and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the bottle daily, before using it as an essential oil for inflammation.

5. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains compounds like limonene, beta-pinene, and geranial, which are known for their anti-inflammatory properties.

These bioactive constituents work by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines, which contribute to inflammation. The essential oil also contains compounds like citral and geranyl acetate, which have been shown to have potent anti-inflammatory effects by reducing inflammation and preventing the formation of inflammatory mediators. The anti-inflammatory properties of Cymbopogon citratus essential oils make it a potential treatment for conditions characterized by inflammation, such as arthritis and other musculoskeletal disorders.

The oil's ability to reduce inflammation makes it a useful tool for managing pain and promoting healing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 2 cups of water.
  2. Wash the leaves with 1 cup of water and pat them dry.
  3. Add the leaves to a saucepan with 2 cups of water and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and simmer for 2 hours, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and let it sit for 24 hours to allow the oil to separate.

6. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, a bioactive constituent that helps reduce inflammation in the body.

Gingerol has anti-inflammatory properties that inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to swelling and pain. The sesquiterpenes in gingerol, specifically zingiberene and bisabolol, also contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects by blocking the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause inflammation. Additionally, the flavonoids in Zingiber officinale essential oils, such as kaempferol and quercetin, have antioxidant properties that help neutralize free radicals, which can exacerbate inflammation.

The combination of these bioactive constituents makes Zingiber officinale essential oils a potential natural remedy for alleviating inflammation and related symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots, a cup of water, a saucepan, and a glass jar.
  2. Peel and chop the ginger roots into small pieces, then add them to the saucepan with the cup of water.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 1 hour, then let it cool down.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit at room temperature for 24 hours to allow the essential oils to separate, then use a pipette to collect and store the oil in a dark glass bottle.

7. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol, carvacrol, and linalool as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and ease pain. Thymol, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to inflammation. Carvacrol, on the other hand, has antioxidant properties that help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress, a common cause of inflammation.

The combination of these properties in Thymus vulgaris essential oils makes it a useful natural remedy for inflammation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan until it reaches 212°F (100°C), then reduce heat to 140°F (60°C).
  3. Add the fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves to the saucepan and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a clean glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil as needed for inflammation relief.

8. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains rosmarinic acid, ursolic acid, and carnosic acid, which are potent anti-inflammatory compounds.

These bioactive constituents have antioxidant and free radical-scavenging properties, helping to reduce inflammation in the body. Rosmarinic acid, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, such as COX-2 and LOX, which contribute to inflammation. The anti-inflammatory properties of Salvia officinalis essential oils are also attributed to its ability to modulate the body's immune response, reducing the release of pro-inflammatory cytokines.

By reducing inflammation, Salvia officinalis essential oils may help alleviate conditions associated with chronic inflammation, such as arthritis and other inflammatory diseases.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Salvia officinalis leaves, a clean glass jar, and a dark bottle for storage.
  2. Combine 2 cups of dried Salvia officinalis leaves with 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or coconut oil) in the glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the dark bottle, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the Salvia officinalis essential oil in a cool, dark place and use it as needed for inflammation relief.

9. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains a compound called cinnamaldehyde, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

Cinnamaldehyde is a potent inhibitor of pro-inflammatory enzymes, such as COX-2, that contribute to inflammation. The oils also contain eugenol, a compound that has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. The combination of cinnamaldehyde and eugenol in Cinnamomum verum essential oils helps to reduce inflammation by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and reducing oxidative stress.

This makes Cinnamomum verum essential oils a potential natural remedy for reducing inflammation and promoting healing.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Cinnamomum verum bark, a glass jar, a cheesecloth, a cup of vodka, and a dark cabinet.
  2. Cut the Cinnamomum verum bark into small pieces and add them to the glass jar.
  3. Pour 1 cup of vodka over the Cinnamomum verum bark pieces and cover the jar with cheesecloth.
  4. Store the jar in a dark cabinet for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day. Then, strain the liquid through cheesecloth into another jar.
  5. Discard the solids and use the resulting liquid as Cinnamomum verum essential oil for inflammation, diluted with a carrier oil as needed.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent inflammation from forming?

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for inflammation every day?

Consume herbal teas for inflammation every day, but in moderation.

Some herbal teas, like ginger and turmeric, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help with pain and swelling. However, too much can cause stomach problems and interact with medications.

Start with small amounts and observe how your body reacts.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in inflammation?

Herbal teas can start to show results in reducing inflammation within a few days to a week.

Some people notice improvements in how they feel and look after 7-10 days. However, it can take up to 2-3 weeks to see noticeable changes in inflammation levels.

This timeframe can vary depending on the individual and the specific herbs used.

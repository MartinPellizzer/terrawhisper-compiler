What Are The Main Causes Of Inflamed Gums?

Poor Oral Hygiene is a main cause of inflamed gums.

This is because when we don't brush and floss regularly, food particles and plaque (a sticky film) build up on our teeth. Plaque is made up of bacteria that can cause inflammation and infection in the gums. If we don't remove plaque through regular cleaning, it hardens into tartar, which can lead to gum inflammation. Genetic Predisposition is another cause of inflamed gums. Some people are born with a weakened immune system or other genetic conditions that make them more prone to gum disease.

This can be due to a family history of gum disease or other health issues. If you have a genetic predisposition, you may be more likely to develop inflamed gums, even if you take good care of your teeth. Gingivitis is a type of gum disease that can cause inflammation and infection in the gums. Gingivitis is caused by a buildup of plaque and bacteria on the teeth, which leads to red, swollen, and painful gums. If left untreated, gingivitis can progress to periodontitis, a more serious gum infection that can damage the bone and tissue supporting the teeth.

Gingivitis is often caused by poor oral hygiene, but it can also be caused by other factors, such as hormonal changes during pregnancy or menstruation.

Below are the main causes of inflamed gums.

  • Poor Oral Hygiene: Poor oral hygiene is a cause of inflamed gums because when food and plaque are not regularly cleaned from teeth, they can cause bacteria to multiply and build up beneath the gum line, leading to infection and inflammation.
  • Genetic Predisposition: Genetic predisposition causes inflamed gums because some people are born with a weakened immune system or abnormal gum structure, making their gums more prone to inflammation and infection, which can be triggered by bacteria in the mouth.
  • Gingivitis: Gingivitis occurs when bacteria in the mouth, especially around the teeth, multiply and cause inflammation in the gums due to the release of toxins and acids that irritate the gum tissue.
  • Diabetes: High blood sugar levels in people with diabetes can cause infections in the mouth, leading to inflamed gums because the body's ability to fight off germs is weakened.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal changes can cause inflamed gums because the increased levels of female sex hormones during pregnancy, menstruation, or menopause can lead to changes in blood flow and inflammation in the gums.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Inflamed Gums?

If you have inflamed gums, it's essential to avoid certain herbs that can make the issue worse.

One such herb is Sage (Sage officinalis) - it's a natural blood thinner and can cause your gums to bleed more. Another herb to be careful with is Ginkgo biloba, as it can cause mouth dryness, which can irritate your gums further.

Clove (Syzygium aromaticum) is also something to avoid, as its oils can be too harsh for inflamed gums and cause more discomfort. Willow bark (Salix alba), which contains salicin, a compound similar to aspirin, should also be avoided as it can thin the blood and make bleeding more likely.

Finally, Ginger (Zingiber officinale) should be used with caution, as it can cause stomach upset, which can lead to more inflammation in the gums.

Inflamed Gums: A Guide to Causes, Medicinal Herbs, and Remedies

What are the causes of inflamed gums?

When it comes to inflamed gums, there are several main causes to consider.

Poor oral hygiene is a big one - when you don't brush and floss regularly, plaque builds up on your teeth, leading to inflammation and redness in your gums. Gingivitis is another related cause, which is a mild form of gum disease that's often caused by poor oral hygiene.

If left untreated, gingivitis can develop into periodontitis, a more serious condition that causes the gums to pull away from the teeth and create pockets where bacteria can multiply. Smoking is also a major contributor to inflamed gums, as the chemicals in tobacco smoke damage the blood vessels and reduce blood flow to the gums, making them more prone to inflammation.

Hormonal changes, such as those experienced during pregnancy or menopause, can also cause inflamed gums, as the increased levels of estrogen and progesterone can cause the gums to become more sensitive and reactive.

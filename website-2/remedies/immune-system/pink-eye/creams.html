1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoids, saponins, and flavonoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties that help soothe and heal irritated eyes. The triterpenoids, specifically, have been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria and viruses, while the saponins help break down and clear out dead cells and debris. The flavonoids, meanwhile, have potent antioxidant properties that reduce inflammation and promote tissue repair.

By applying Calendula officinalis creams to the affected area, you can help reduce swelling, promote healing, and clear up pink eye symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of olive oil.
  2. Steep the Calendula flowers in coconut oil for 2-3 weeks. Store in a cool, dark place and shake daily.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. In a double boiler, melt the beeswax with the strained Calendula oil mixture. Heat until the beeswax is fully dissolved.
  5. Remove from heat and add vitamin E oil and olive oil. Stir well and pour into small containers. Let cool and harden before use.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains alkylamides and glycoproteins, which are known to have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and redness associated with pink eye.

The 3,4-didehydro-9-androsten-17-one constituent in Echinacea purpurea has been shown to inhibit the production of inflammatory chemicals, such as histamine and interleukin-1 beta, which contribute to the symptoms of pink eye. The polysaccharides present in Echinacea purpurea have immunomodulatory effects, which can help regulate the immune system's response to infection and reduce the severity of pink eye symptoms. The free radical scavenging activity of Echinacea purpurea's phenolic compounds can also help protect the eyes from oxidative damage caused by inflammation.

By addressing the underlying causes of pink eye, Echinacea purpurea creams may provide relief from its symptoms and support the healing process.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and a double boiler.
  2. Dry the Echinacea flowers completely and grind them into a fine powder.
  3. Combine the coconut oil and beeswax in the double boiler and melt them together.
  4. Add the Echinacea powder to the melted coconut oil mixture and stir well. Remove from heat.
  5. Let the mixture cool and thicken. Pour into small containers and store in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

3. Aloe barbadensis

4. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale creams contains the active constituents allantoin, saponins, and rosmarinic acid.

  3. Remove the melted mixture from the heat and stir in Symphytum officinale extract and vitamin E oil until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into a clean glass jar or container and let it cool until it reaches a consistency similar to thick cream.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent pink eye from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe irritated eyes, but it's unlikely to prevent pink eye from forming. Some herbal teas, like chamomile, have anti-inflammatory properties that might reduce eye irritation, but they don't have any proven ability to stop pink eye from developing.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for pink eye every day?

Consuming herbal teas for pink eye every day is not recommended.

While some herbal teas may help reduce redness and swelling, relying on them as a treatment can lead to prolonged recovery.

It's essential to address the underlying cause of pink eye, usually a bacterial or viral infection, with proper treatment to prevent complications and promote healing.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in pink eye?

Herbal teas can help soothe pink eye symptoms, but they don't cure it.

It may take a few days to a week to see some improvement. The eye's redness and swelling may decrease, and the discharge may thin out.

This is because herbal teas calm the eye and help it heal naturally.

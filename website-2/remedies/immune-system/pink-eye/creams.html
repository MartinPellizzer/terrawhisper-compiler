Pink eye remedies for kids: 10 herbal creams to try

Pink eye, or conjunctivitis, is a common problem that affects many children.

It's usually caused by a bacterial or viral infection that makes the eyes red, itchy, and painful. This can happen when germs get into the eyes through touching or sharing things like towels, toys, or makeup. When a child has pink eye, they might feel uncomfortable and their eyes might be watery, itchy, or even swollen. They might even have trouble seeing clearly. This can be a real nuisance for kids, and it can make it hard for them to play, go to school, or even sleep properly.

If left untreated, pink eye can lead to complications like vision problems or even more serious infections. As a parent, it's natural to want to find a way to help your child feel better and get rid of pink eye for good. When it comes to treating pink eye in children, many parents turn to over-the-counter creams or ointments. However, some people prefer to try herbal creams as a more natural and gentle option. These creams often work by soothing and calming the eyes, reducing inflammation and discomfort. They can also help to fight off the underlying infection that's causing the pink eye.

By using herbal creams, parents can help their child feel more comfortable and get back to their normal routine sooner.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams are used to help alleviate symptoms of pink eye, a common eye infection.

The creams contain properties and constituents like triterpenoids and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. These compounds help reduce swelling and fight off bacteria that cause the infection. When using Calendula officinalis creams for pink eye, gently apply a thin layer to the affected eye, ideally after cleaning and drying the area.

Repeat this process 2-3 times a day to help soothe and calm the eye. As you apply the cream, you may start to notice a decrease in redness and swelling, and an improvement in discharge or tearing. The cream's anti-inflammatory properties may also help reduce discomfort and itching associated with pink eye.

It's essential to note that Calendula officinalis creams may not cure pink eye, but they can help manage symptoms and support the healing process.

To try Echinacea purpurea creams for pink eye, apply a thin layer to the affected eye gently and avoid touching the eye area to prevent further irritation.

You can repeat this process several times a day, but be sure to consult with a doctor or pharmacist before using any new treatment. If you experience improvement, the symptoms of pink eye may start to subside, and the discharge may reduce. You may also notice a decrease in swelling and redness around the eye area.

However, it's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea creams may not be effective for everyone, and it's crucial to follow proper hygiene and medical advice to treat pink eye effectively.

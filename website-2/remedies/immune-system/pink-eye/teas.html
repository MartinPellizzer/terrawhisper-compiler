1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains compounds like alkylamides, glycosides, and caffeic acid that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties can help reduce swelling and soothe the eyes, which can be beneficial for pink eye. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea teas can also help modulate the immune system, which can help the body fight off infections that cause pink eye. The glycosides in the tea have antimicrobial properties, which can help prevent the spread of infection and promote healing.

By drinking Echinacea purpurea tea, you may be able to alleviate some of the symptoms of pink eye, such as redness, itchiness, and discharge.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Echinacea purpurea root and flowers.
  2. Measure 1 cup of boiling water and pour it over the Echinacea mixture in a cup.
  3. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes to allow the Echinacea to release its properties.
  4. Strain the tea using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove the Echinacea particles.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help alleviate pink eye symptoms.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains compounds like camphor, eucalyptol, and cineole.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help reduce swelling and fight off bacterial infections that cause pink eye. Eucalyptol, in particular, is a potent antibacterial agent that can help combat the bacteria responsible for conjunctivitis. Cineole's decongestant properties can also help alleviate redness and itchiness associated with pink eye.

By reducing swelling and fighting off infections, Eucalyptus globulus teas can provide relief from the symptoms of pink eye.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water, 1 tablespoon of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves, and a tea strainer.
  2. Steep the dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea using the tea strainer and discard the leaves.
  4. Let the tea cool down before using it as eye drops. You can add a few drops of a carrier oil like coconut oil to thin it out.
  5. Soak a clean cloth in the cooled tea and apply it to the affected eye for 5-10 minutes, 2-3 times a day.

3. Echinacea angustifolia

Echinacea angustifolia teas contains active constituents like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help soothe and calm the symptoms of pink eye, such as redness and swelling. The alkylamides in Echinacea angustifolia teas have been shown to reduce inflammation and promote healing, while the glycosides help to fight off bacterial and viral infections that can cause pink eye. The antimicrobial properties of the phenolic acids in this tea can also help to eliminate the underlying cause of the infection, reducing the severity and duration of symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Echinacea angustifolia teas can help to alleviate the discomfort and pain associated with pink eye.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Echinacea angustifolia root. Use a clean scoop to measure.

  5. Drink the tea 3 times a day to help relieve pink eye symptoms. Store any leftover tea in the fridge.

4. Calendula officinalis

5. Symphytum officinale

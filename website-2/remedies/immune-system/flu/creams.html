8 Herbal Creams for Pregnant Women to Combat Flu

Pregnant women can get the flu, just like anyone else.

But their bodies are working extra hard to grow a baby, so their immune systems may not be able to fight off the flu as well. This can make them feel really sick, tired, and weak. They may have a fever, cough, sore throat, and body aches, making it hard to do even simple tasks. This can affect their daily lives, causing them to miss work, school, or other important activities. They may also worry about passing the flu to their unborn baby, which can be stressful and scary.

When pregnant women get the flu, they need to take care of themselves and their babies. They should rest, drink plenty of fluids, and take medication as prescribed by their doctor. But sometimes, over-the-counter medication may not be enough to ease their symptoms. That's where herbal creams come in. These creams are made from natural ingredients that are gentle on the skin and can help soothe flu symptoms like aches, pains, and congestion. They can be applied directly to the skin, where they can be absorbed to help relieve symptoms from the inside out.

By using herbal creams, pregnant women can get some much-needed relief from their flu symptoms, without putting their baby at risk.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams are used to help alleviate flu symptoms.

This is because they contain compounds like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These properties can help reduce swelling and fight off free radicals in the body, making it easier to recover from the flu. When using Echinacea purpurea creams, apply a small amount to the affected areas, such as the throat and chest, 3-4 times a day. You can also add a few drops of the cream to a warm bath for a relaxing soak.

To get the most out of the cream, make sure to use it within the first 48 hours of flu symptoms appearing, as this is when the body's immune system is most active. Applying Echinacea purpurea creams may lead to a reduction in fever, a decrease in coughing and congestion, and an improvement in overall well-being. Some users also report a decrease in the duration and severity of flu symptoms. However, it's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea creams are not a substitute for medical treatment.

Always consult with a healthcare professional before using any new remedy, especially if you have a weakened immune system or are taking prescription medications.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contain compounds like eucalyptol, which is known for its decongestant properties.

When you apply the cream to your chest or nose, eucalyptol helps to break down mucus, making it easier to breathe. The cooling sensation of the cream also helps to reduce fever and relieve body aches. If you're experiencing flu symptoms like congestion, coughing, and a sore throat, try applying Eucalyptus globulus cream to the affected areas.

You can also use a humidifier with eucalyptus oil to breathe in the benefits. When you use the cream, you may experience relief from congestion, reduced coughing, and a decrease in body aches. The fever may also start to subside, and you may feel more comfortable.

However, if your symptoms worsen or last longer than a week, or if you have a fever over 102°F (39°C), seek medical attention.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams, also known as ginger creams, have been used for centuries to help alleviate flu symptoms.

The active properties and constituents in ginger creams, such as gingerols and shogaols, have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help to reduce fever, soothe a sore throat, and ease congestion. When you apply a ginger cream to the skin, these properties are absorbed into the body, where they can help to break down mucus, reduce coughing, and ease body aches.

To use a ginger cream for flu, apply it to the chest, back, and throat 2-3 times a day. You can also add a few drops of ginger essential oil to your bathwater for a relaxing and rejuvenating soak. As the cream works to ease your symptoms, you may start to feel a reduction in fever, a decrease in congestion, and a soothing of the throat.

Your body may also start to feel more energized and less achy, making it easier to rest and recover from the flu.

The flavonoids, in particular, have been shown to have antiviral properties, which can help to combat the virus that causes the flu. If you're experiencing flu symptoms, try applying Ginkgo biloba cream to your chest, throat, and nose. You can also rub it on your temples and wrists to help relieve headache and fatigue.

When applying the cream, gently massage it into your skin until it's fully absorbed. You can reapply as needed, but be sure to follow the instructions on the packaging and start with a small amount to test your skin's sensitivity. When you take action with Ginkgo biloba cream, you may start to feel relief from your flu symptoms within a few hours. The inflammation and swelling in your body may decrease, and you may experience less congestion and coughing. You may also start to feel more energized and alert, as the antioxidant properties of the cream help to boost your immune system. However, it's essential to remember that Ginkgo biloba cream is not a substitute for medical treatment.

The main constituent, cineole, has been shown to have a decongestant effect, helping to relieve congestion and coughs associated with the flu. The cream's antiviral properties may also help to combat the virus itself, reducing the severity and duration of the flu.

As the creams work to reduce inflammation and combat infection, you may start to feel relief from flu symptoms such as fever, cough, and body aches within 24-48 hours.

8. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams have been used for centuries to help alleviate flu symptoms.

This is because the gel from the Aloe vera plant contains properties and constituents like vitamins A, C, and E, as well as minerals like calcium, potassium, and zinc. These nutrients have anti-inflammatory and soothing effects, which can help reduce fever, ease a sore throat, and calm a cough. The gel also contains compounds like aloin and aloe-emodin, which have antimicrobial properties that can help fight off infections. If you're experiencing flu symptoms, you can try using Aloe barbadensis creams topically. Apply a thin layer to your skin, ideally to areas that are affected by the flu, such as your throat or chest.

You can also add the gel to a warm bath to help relax and soothe your body. When using the cream, make sure to wash your hands thoroughly before and after application, and avoid touching your eyes or mouth. Some likely outcomes of using Aloe barbadensis creams for flu include a reduction in fever and body aches, as well as an easing of a sore throat and cough. You may also experience improved skin hydration and a calming of irritated skin. However, if your symptoms worsen or you experience difficulty breathing, chest pain, or severe headache, seek medical attention immediately.

Also, be aware that Aloe barbadensis creams may interact with certain medications or exacerbate underlying conditions, so consult with a healthcare professional before using them.

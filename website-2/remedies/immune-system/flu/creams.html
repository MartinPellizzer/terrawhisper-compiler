1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects, making them effective against flu symptoms. The anti-inflammatory properties of ginger help reduce fever and alleviate body aches, while its antiviral properties inhibit the replication of the flu virus. Ginger's ability to thin mucus also makes it easier to expel, relieving congestion and coughs.

Additionally, the antioxidant properties of ginger help protect the body from oxidative stress caused by the flu virus.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add 10-15 grams of dried Zingiber officinale root to the melted mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Mix in 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil and pour the mixture into a clean container. Let it cool and solidify before use.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains a number of active constituents, including alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and ease congestion in the throat and sinuses, common symptoms of the flu. The flavonoids in Echinacea purpurea also have antioxidant properties, which can help protect the body from damage caused by free radicals and support immune function. Additionally, the alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea have been shown to have immunomodulatory effects, meaning they can help regulate the immune system's response to infection.

By supporting immune function and reducing inflammation, Echinacea purpurea creams may help alleviate some of the symptoms of the flu.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of coconut oil.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler at 100°F (38°C) for 2 hours.
  3. Add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the coconut oil and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter to the coconut oil mixture. Heat until melted and pour into a container.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains the compound eucalyptol, which has natural decongestant properties that help relieve nasal congestion associated with flu.

Eucalyptol also has anti-inflammatory properties, reducing swelling in the nose and sinuses. The creams also contain other active constituents like flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have antioxidant properties that help protect the body from free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. These properties help to loosen and clear mucus, making it easier to breathe and reducing the severity of flu symptoms.

The antimicrobial properties of eucalyptol in Eucalyptus globulus creams also help combat infection and promote healing.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Eucalyptus globulus essential oil, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat, stirring until combined.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in Eucalyptus globulus essential oil and vitamin E oil.
  4. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and harden completely.
  5. Once hardened, your Eucalyptus globulus cream is ready to use. Apply as needed to relieve flu symptoms.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, carnosic acid, and camphor as its active constituents.

Rosmarinic acid specifically has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to the development of flu. Carnosic acid also exhibits antioxidant properties, which can help to protect the body from oxidative stress caused by the flu virus.

By applying Rosmarinus officinalis creams topically, these compounds can work together to alleviate flu symptoms and promote a faster recovery.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of rosemary essential oil, 2 tablespoons of eucalyptus essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.

5. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams contains thymol and carvacrol, two active constituents that help fight the flu.

Thymol has antibacterial properties, which help combat secondary infections that often come with the flu. Carvacrol has anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling and pain in the body. Thymus vulgaris creams also contain flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties that help boost the immune system and reduce the severity of flu symptoms.

By applying thymus vulgaris creams to the skin, the body can absorb these beneficial compounds, helping to alleviate flu symptoms and support recovery.

  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add thyme essential oil and vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir well.
  4. Slowly add water to the mixture, stirring constantly until it thickens into a creamy consistency.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool before use. Apply to skin as needed to help soothe flu symptoms.

6. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains glycyrrhizin, a key active constituent that helps alleviate flu symptoms.

7. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra creams contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce the severity of flu symptoms. The flavonoids and phenolic acids in Sambucus nigra have been shown to inhibit the replication of viruses and reduce the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines. By reducing inflammation and boosting the immune system, Sambucus nigra creams may help alleviate flu symptoms and support the body's natural recovery process.

  3. Add the dried Sambucus nigra flowers to the melted coconut oil and let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass container, discarding the solids.
  5. Add 1/4 cup of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil to the strained mixture and stir until the beeswax is fully incorporated, then pour into small containers to cool and harden.

8. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties, making them effective in treating flu symptoms. Linalool helps to reduce fever and relieve body aches, while linalyl acetate soothes and calms the respiratory system, easing congestion and coughs. The antiviral properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help to combat the flu virus, shortening the duration of the illness.

By reducing fever and inflammation, and soothing the respiratory system, Lavandula angustifolia creams can help to alleviate flu symptoms and promote recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/2 cup of shea butter.
  2. Combine the dried flowers and coconut oil in a double boiler. Heat for 2 hours, stirring occasionally.

If you feel uncomfortable or experience side effects, adjust your intake or try different teas.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in flu?

Herbal teas for the flu can start to help within a few hours.

As the day goes on, the effects can become more noticeable, helping to make you feel a bit more comfortable and less miserable.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains a high concentration of cineole, a bioactive constituent that has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Cineole helps to combat the flu virus by reducing the severity of symptoms such as congestion and coughing. The decongestant properties of cineole also help to thin mucus, making it easier to expel, which can help alleviate sinus pressure and coughs associated with the flu. Additionally, eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains other bioactive constituents like alpha-pinene, which has antiviral properties that help to combat the flu virus.

By using eucalyptus globulus essential oils, people may be able to alleviate some of the symptoms of the flu and recover faster.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water. Wear protective gloves and safety goggles.
  2. Combine the leaves and water in a saucepan. Heat over low heat for 30 minutes. Stir occasionally.
  3. Remove from heat and let steep for 2 hours. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a bowl.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a still or a heat-proof container. Distill or heat to 212°F (100°C). Collect the distilled oil in a clean container.
  5. Store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Label and date the bottle. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties that help to reduce the severity of flu symptoms. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea essential oils have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which can contribute to the development of flu. The caffeic acid in the oil has antioxidant properties that help to protect the body from oxidative stress caused by the flu virus.

By reducing inflammation and boosting the immune system, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help to alleviate symptoms and support the body's natural recovery process.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves. Clean and dry them.
  2. Use a carrier oil like jojoba or sweet almond oil (1 cup). Heat it in a double boiler.
  3. Add 1/2 cup of the Echinacea flowers and leaves to the carrier oil. Steep for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the Echinacea essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce fever and soothe a sore throat.

It also contains shogaol, a compound that has antibacterial properties, which can help fight off the infection that causes the flu. The oils' anti-inflammatory properties can also help ease congestion and coughs, making it easier to breathe. Additionally, Zingiber officinale essential oils contain other compounds like zingerone and paradol, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce the severity of flu symptoms.

By reducing inflammation, fighting off infection, and soothing congestion, Zingiber officinale essential oils can provide relief from flu symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale roots and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or coconut oil).
  2. Wash and chop the roots into small pieces. Use 1 tablespoon of chopped roots for every 1 tablespoon of carrier oil.
  3. In a clean glass jar, combine the chopped roots and carrier oil. Store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the Zingiber officinale essential oil to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops per application for flu relief.

4. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol, carvacrol, and linalool as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help fight off the flu virus and reduce its symptoms. Thymol and carvacrol, in particular, have been shown to have potent antiviral effects, inhibiting the replication of the virus and reducing the severity of the flu. Linalool, on the other hand, has anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate the body's response to the virus, reducing fever and congestion.

By harnessing the power of these bioactive compounds, Thymus vulgaris essential oils can be a valuable tool in combating the flu.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Thymus vulgaris leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the liquid extract and stir well.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 hours to allow the essential oil to separate from the liquid. Strain and bottle the oil.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains rosinic acid, camphor, and borneol, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, helping to combat the underlying causes of flu. The essential oil's antimicrobial properties can help reduce the severity and duration of flu symptoms, such as fever and cough, by inhibiting the growth of pathogens. Additionally, borneol's expectorant properties can help relieve congestion and coughs associated with the flu.

By using Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils, individuals may be able to alleviate flu symptoms and support their body's natural recovery process.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Wash the leaves with water and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Pack the leaves into a clean glass jar, leaving 1 inch of space at the top.
  4. Pour the carrier oil over the leaves, making sure they are completely covered.
  5. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day, then strain and discard the leaves.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which have antimicrobial properties that help fight flu viruses.

These compounds work by disrupting the viral cell membrane and preventing the virus from replicating. The antiviral properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help reduce the severity and duration of flu symptoms, such as fever and cough. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds help alleviate body aches and pains associated with the flu.

By targeting the flu virus and reducing inflammation, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can provide relief from flu symptoms and promote a faster recovery.

  2. Combine the flowers and water in a saucepan and bring to a boil.

  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.

7. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra essential oils contains bioactive constituents like isorhapontigenin, salicin, and sambunigrin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties, which help fight the flu virus. The sambunigrin in Sambucus nigra essential oils has been shown to inhibit the replication of the influenza virus, reducing the severity of symptoms. The salicin in the oil has analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help alleviate body aches and fever associated with the flu.

By using Sambucus nigra essential oils, you can potentially reduce the duration and severity of flu symptoms.

  2. Combine the Sambucus nigra flowers and carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well.

  4. After the infusion, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Sambucus nigra essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains a high amount of compounds like cineole, globulol, and viridiflorol.

Cineole, in particular, has been shown to have antiviral activity, inhibiting the replication of the influenza virus. These compounds work together to reduce inflammation and improve blood flow to the lungs, making it easier for the body to fight off the flu virus. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent flu from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help boost your immune system, but it's unlikely to completely prevent the flu.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and ginger, have anti-inflammatory properties that can ease flu symptoms.

However, the flu virus can still infect you, even if you drink herbal tea regularly.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for flu every day?

Herbal teas can help with flu symptoms, but drinking them every day isn't safe for everyone.

Some herbs, like eucalyptus and peppermint, are okay in small amounts, but excessive consumption can cause stomach problems and interact with medications.

Listen to your body and adjust your intake based on how you feel.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in flu?

Herbal teas can help relieve flu symptoms, but their effectiveness can vary.

Some people may start feeling better within a few hours, while others may take a day or two to notice improvements.

The effects tend to be more noticeable for mild cases of the flu.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for flu?

Drinking herbal tea for flu is best when you're feeling tired, usually in the afternoon or evening.

This is because your body is resting and trying to recover from the illness.

At this time, the soothing properties of the tea can help ease your symptoms and promote a good night's sleep.

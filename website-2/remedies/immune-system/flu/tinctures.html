1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains flavonoids, alkylamides, and glycosides.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties that help combat flu symptoms. Alkylamides, specifically, have been shown to stimulate the immune system, increasing the production of white blood cells that fight off the flu virus. Flavonoids, like kaempferol and quercetin, possess antioxidant properties that can reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the body.

By modulating the immune response and reducing inflammation, Echinacea purpurea tinctures can help alleviate flu symptoms and support recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea roots and flowers, and 2 cups of 80 proof vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the Echinacea mixture to the jar, making sure they are completely covered by the vodka.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the Echinacea tincture in the fridge and use 20-30 drops in a cup of water or tea when you feel flu symptoms coming on.

2. Echinacea angustifolia

Echinacea angustifolia tinctures contains alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds work together to boost the immune system, reducing the severity and duration of flu symptoms. Alkylamides, in particular, have anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which contribute to the flu's severity. Glycosides, such as echinacoside, have antiviral properties that help to inhibit the replication of the flu virus.

The phenolic acids in Echinacea angustifolia tinctures also have antioxidant properties that help to protect the body from oxidative stress caused by the flu.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Echinacea angustifolia roots. You can buy this from a health store or online.
  2. Use 1:5 ratio to prepare the tincture. This means 1 part of roots to 5 parts of vodka or high-proof spirit.
  3. Combine 1/2 cup of roots with 2 1/2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar. Close the lid tightly.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day to help the roots release their goodness.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle. Discard the roots and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

3. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra tinctures contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, anthocyanins, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help combat the flu virus. The flavonoids, particularly rutin and quercetin, have been shown to inhibit the replication of flu viruses and reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract. The anthocyanins, responsible for the dark color of elderberries, have antioxidant properties that help protect against oxidative stress and damage caused by the flu virus.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Sambucus nigra tinctures can help alleviate flu symptoms and support the body's natural recovery process.

  1. Gather 250ml of 80% Vodka and 250g of fresh or dried Sambucus nigra flowers.
  2. Combine the flowers and vodka in a clean glass jar, making sure the flowers are fully covered.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Label the jar with the date and contents, and store it in a cool, dark place. Take 10-20 drops of the tincture in water 2-3 times a day as needed.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures can help to support the body's natural defenses and promote recovery from flu.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent flu from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help boost your immune system and reduce the severity of flu symptoms.

Some teas, like peppermint and ginger, have anti-inflammatory properties that can ease congestion and soothe a sore throat.

However, the tea's effectiveness in preventing the flu is still unclear and may vary from person to person.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for flu every day?

While herbal teas may help alleviate flu symptoms, consuming them daily isn't entirely safe.

Some herbs can interact with medications or worsen conditions like high blood pressure or allergies.

It's essential to be aware of the potential risks and use moderation when drinking herbal teas for flu relief.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in flu?

Herbal teas can help alleviate flu symptoms, but the time it takes to show results varies. Some people feel relief from a sore throat or cough in a few hours, while others may notice improvements in energy levels or a reduced fever in a day or two.

What Are The Main Causes Of Shortness Of Breath?

Shortness of breath, also known as dyspnea, can be caused by various conditions that affect the lungs and heart.

One main cause is pneumonia, a serious infection in the lungs that fills them with fluid and pus. This makes it difficult for the lungs to take in oxygen, leading to shortness of breath.

Another cause is heart failure, where the heart is not pumping enough blood to meet the body's needs. This can cause fluid buildup in the lungs, leading to congestion and shortness of breath. Asthma is also a common cause, where the airways in the lungs become inflamed and narrow.

When you breathe in, the airways can become even narrower, making it harder for air to reach the lungs and leading to shortness of breath.

Below are the main causes of shortness of breath.

  • Pneumonia: Pneumonia is a cause of shortness of breath because the infection in the lungs makes it hard for the body to get enough oxygen, leading to a feeling of not being able to breathe easily.
  • Heart Failure: Heart failure is a cause of shortness of breath because the heart is not pumping enough blood, which means the lungs do not get enough oxygen, making it hard to breathe and feel winded even when sitting still.
  • Asthma: Asthma causes shortness of breath because the airways in the lungs become inflamed, narrow, and filled with mucus, making it hard for air to get in and out of the lungs properly.
  • COPD: COPD is a cause of shortness of breath because the damaged air sacs in the lungs, or alveoli, become less efficient at exchanging oxygen and carbon dioxide, making it harder for the body to get the oxygen it needs.
  • Pleurisy: Pleurisy is a cause of shortness of breath because the inflamed and irritated lining of the lungs, known as the pleura, becomes stuck to the lungs and makes it harder for them to expand and fill with air during breathing.
  • Pulmonary Embolism: Pulmonary Embolism causes shortness of breath because a blood clot blocks the flow of blood to the lungs, preventing them from getting enough oxygen to fill the body's needs.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Shortness Of Breath?

If you have shortness of breath, there are some herbs you should avoid.

Yarrow (Achillea millefolium) can slow down breathing, making symptoms worse. Sage (Salvia officinalis) can increase heart rate and blood pressure, which may not be suitable for people with breathing difficulties.

Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) can cause respiratory problems in some individuals, especially when taken in high doses. Also, avoid consuming large amounts of Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) as it can raise blood pressure and worsen shortness of breath.

Finally, Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) contains berberine, which may interact with medications for heart conditions and worsen breathing problems.

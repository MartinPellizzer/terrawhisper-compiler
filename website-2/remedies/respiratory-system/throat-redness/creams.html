1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, glycoproteins, and caffeic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and redness in the throat. Alkylamides, specifically, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals, which contribute to throat redness. Caffeic acid, on the other hand, has antioxidant properties that help protect the throat tissues from damage caused by free radicals.

By applying Echinacea purpurea creams to the affected area, the bioactive constituents can help alleviate throat redness and promote faster healing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of shea butter.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add shea butter and stir until melted.
  4. Add dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and stir for 5 minutes to infuse their properties into the mixture.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl, discard the solids, and pour the mixture into small containers to cool and set.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains compounds like eucalyptol, alpha-pinene, and cineole.

These boiactive constituents give the cream its decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties, helping to reduce swelling and ease discomfort in the throat. Alpha-pinene and cineole have anti-inflammatory effects, which help to soothe and calm the throat, reducing redness and irritation.

This combination of properties makes eucalyptus globulus creams an effective treatment for throat redness and congestion.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup coconut oil, 1/2 cup beeswax, 2 tablespoons shea butter, 2 tablespoons eucalyptus essential oil, 2 tablespoons tea tree essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add shea butter and stir until melted. Remove from heat.
  4. Add eucalyptus and tea tree essential oils. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers. Let cool and harden. Your Eucalyptus globulus cream is now ready to use.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains active constituents such as glycyrrhizin and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help soothe and calm irritated throat tissues. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, has been shown to have a potent anti-inflammatory effect, which can help reduce redness and swelling in the throat. The flavonoids present in Glycyrrhiza glabra creams also have a protective effect on the mucous membranes, preventing further irritation and damage.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the mucous membranes, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams can provide effective relief from throat redness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1 cup of glycerin, 2 tablespoons of glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, and 2 tablespoons of beeswax in a small saucepan.
  2. Heat the distilled water and glycerin in the saucepan over low heat until the beeswax is melted.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the glycyrrhiza glabra root powder until it is fully dissolved.
  4. Let the mixture cool until it reaches a temperature of around 120°F to 130°F (49°C to 54°C), then pour it into a clean container.
  5. Allow the mixture to cool and thicken before applying it to the throat to soothe redness.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains active constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A, C, and E. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that help reduce throat redness.

Aloin and aloe-emodin work by decreasing swelling and pain, while vitamins A and C help protect the throat tissue from damage. Vitamin E has antioxidant properties that prevent further irritation and promote healing.

The combination of these properties in Aloe barbadensis creams makes it effective in calming and soothing throat redness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe vera gel from Aloe barbadensis, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 10 drops of tea tree oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil, beeswax, and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from heat and let it cool for 5 minutes. Then, stir in the Aloe vera gel and tea tree oil.
  4. Pour the mixture into a heat-proof container and let it cool to room temperature. This will help it solidify into a cream.
  5. Once the cream has solidified, it's ready to use. Store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

8. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that help soothe and calm irritated skin. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin, possess potent antioxidant properties that combat oxidative stress and promote tissue repair.

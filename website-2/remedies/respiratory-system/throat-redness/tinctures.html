1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains glycyrrhizin, a powerful anti-inflammatory compound that helps soothe and calm irritated throat tissues.

The licorice root extract in the tincture also has soothing properties due to its high content of flavonoids and phenolic acids. These compounds reduce inflammation and help protect the mucous membranes in the throat from further irritation, allowing the tissues to heal faster. Additionally, glycyrrhizin has been shown to reduce redness and swelling by blocking the production of inflammatory substances in the body.

This makes Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures a popular natural remedy for people experiencing throat redness due to colds, allergies, or other respiratory issues.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh or dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots. Clean and chop them into small pieces.
  2. Combine 1 cup of chopped roots with 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day. This helps the roots infuse into the vodka.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops, 3-4 times a day, to soothe throat redness and discomfort.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and polyphenols.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce swelling and soothe redness in the throat. The polyphenols in Echinacea purpurea have antimicrobial properties, which can help combat infections that may be causing the throat redness. The caffeic acid derivatives can also help reduce inflammation and promote healing in the throat tissue.

By combining these properties, Echinacea purpurea tinctures can provide relief from throat redness and promote a healthy recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers, leaves, and stems. Clean them with water.
  2. Use 1 cup of 80-proof vodka or a neutral spirit as the solvent. Pour it over the Echinacea plant material in a clean glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the solvent to extract the plant's properties.
  4. After the steeping period, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass container. Discard the plant material.
  5. Store the Echinacea tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Use 20-30 drops, 3-4 times a day, to help soothe throat redness. Keep it away from children and pets.

Blocked nose is a common health issue where the nasal passages become congested, making it hard to breathe, smell, or even sleep.

If left untreated, it can lead to complications like sinus infections, headaches, and fatigue. Dealing with a blocked nose is essential to maintain a healthy respiratory system and prevent these consequences. Herbal remedies can be an effective way to alleviate a blocked nose. Eucalyptus globulus, also known as eucalyptus oil, is often used in aromatherapy due to its decongestant properties. Its menthol content helps loosen mucus, making it easier to breathe.

Thymus vulgaris, or thyme, has antimicrobial properties, which can help combat underlying infections that may be causing the congestion. Rosmarinus officinalis, or rosemary, has a natural expectorant effect, helping to clear out excess mucus. Herbal preparations like tincture capsules, decoctions, and salves can be used to harness the benefits of these herbs. These preparations can be used individually or in combination to tackle a blocked nose from different angles.

What Are The Main Causes Of Blocked Nose?

There are several reasons why a person's nose becomes blocked.

One common cause is allergies. When we are allergic to something, our body releases histamine, a chemical that causes blood vessels to swell and our nose to produce excess mucus. This excess mucus blocks the nasal passage, making it hard to breathe. Another cause of a blocked nose is a cold.

When we catch a cold, our nasal passages get inflamed and produce mucus to trap the virus. However, this excess mucus blocks the nasal passage, making it hard to breathe. Sinusitis is also a common cause of a blocked nose. Sinusitis occurs when the sinuses, which are small air-filled cavities in our head, become inflamed and produce excess mucus. This excess mucus blocks the nasal passage, making it hard to breathe.

Sinusitis can be caused by a cold, allergies, or other factors, and it can be acute or chronic.

Below are the main causes of blocked nose.

  • Allergies: When you have an allergy, your body reacts by producing extra mucus, which builds up in your nose and blocks it, making it hard to breathe.
  • Cold: When you breathe in cold air, the tiny hairs inside your nose, called cilia, become slower, and the mucus they push out to trap dust and germs becomes thicker and stickier, blocking the airway and causing a blocked nose.
  • Sinusitis: Sinusitis is a cause of blocked nose because the infection and swelling in the sinuses cause mucus to build up and block the nasal passages.
  • Rhinitis: Rhinitis is a cause of blocked nose because the thin lining inside the nose becomes inflamed and swollen, causing the narrow airways to narrow even more, making it harder to breathe.
  • Colds: When you have a cold, the tiny hairs in your nose called cilia become covered in a thick layer of mucus, which blocks the airways and makes your nose feel stuffy and congested.
  • Flu: When you have the flu, your body produces a lot of mucus to trap the virus, which can build up in your nasal passages and cause a blocked nose.
  • Tonsillitis: Tonsillitis is a cause of blocked nose because the swollen and inflamed tonsils block the airway and nasal passages, causing mucus to build up and making it hard to breathe.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Blocked Nose?

If you have a blocked nose, it's best to avoid herbs that can make congestion worse.

One herb to steer clear of is Angelica (Angelica archangelica), as its strong scent can irritate the nasal passages and worsen congestion. Another herb to be cautious with is Hyssop (Hyssop officinalis), as its menthol content can further dry out the nasal passages, making it harder to breathe.

Additionally, Sage (Salvia officinalis) should be used with caution, as its drying properties can exacerbate respiratory issues. Also, avoid using Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) essential oil, as its sedative properties can cause nasal congestion to become worse due to the body's natural response to relax.

Lastly, avoid using Wintergreen (Gaultheria procumbens) as its methyl salicylate can irritate the nasal passages and worsen congestion.

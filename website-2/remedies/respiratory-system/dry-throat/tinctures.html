1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin that gives licorice root its distinct flavor and medicinal properties.

Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and irritation in the throat. It also has expectorant properties, which help loosen and clear mucus and phlegm from the airways, making it easier to breathe and swallow. Additionally, glycyrrhizin has a soothing effect on the mucous membranes, providing relief from dryness and discomfort in the throat.

By reducing inflammation and providing a soothing effect, glycyrrhizin helps to alleviate dry throat symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root and 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the dried root to the jar and cover it with vodka. Make sure the root is fully submerged.
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in the dark place and use 20-30 drops, 3-4 times a day, to relieve dry throat symptoms.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the throat, making it easier to swallow and speak. The phenolic acids, specifically rosmarinic and chicoric acids, act as antioxidants, protecting the mucous membranes in the throat from damage caused by free radicals. The glycosides, particularly echinacoside and cynarin, have a soothing effect on the throat, helping to calm irritation and dryness.

By reducing inflammation and soothing the mucous membranes, Echinacea purpurea tinctures can provide relief from dry, scratchy throats.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers, leaves, and stems.
  2. Chop the plant material finely and fill a clean glass jar with 1/4 cup of it.
  3. Add 1 cup of vodka (at least 35% alcohol) to the jar, making sure the plant material is covered.
  4. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle. Discard the solids. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture as needed to soothe a dry throat.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contains eucalyptol, a volatile oil that helps to relieve dryness in the throat.

Eucalyptol has a decongestant and anti-inflammatory property, which makes it effective in reducing swelling and mucus production in the throat. This helps to ease the discomfort associated with dry throat and promotes a clear passage of air. The expectorant properties of eucalyptol also help to loosen and clear mucus, further alleviating dryness and coughing.

By reducing inflammation and loosening mucus, eucalyptol in Eucalyptus globulus tinctures helps to provide quick relief from dry throat symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the Eucalyptus leaves to the jar and pour the vodka over them.
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the Eucalyptus leaves and store the tincture in the jar. Use 10-20 drops of the tincture in water to soothe a dry throat.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains a high concentration of compounds like gingerol and shogaol.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling in the throat and ease discomfort. The warming and drying properties of Zingiber officinale tinctures can also help to clear mucus and promote drainage, relieving congestion and dryness in the throat. Additionally, the expectorant properties of ginger help to thin and expel mucus, making it easier to cough up and clear the airways.

By addressing the underlying causes of a dry throat, Zingiber officinale tinctures can provide quick relief from irritation and discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots, 2 cups of vodka, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Peel the ginger roots and chop them into small pieces.
  3. Place the chopped ginger in the glass jar and pour vodka over it. Make sure the ginger is completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle. Discard the ginger solids.

Althaea officinalis tinctures contains mucilages like galactose, rhamnose, and glucuronic acid, which are complex carbohydrates that form a protective film on the surface of mucous membranes.

Regular use of Althaea officinalis tinctures can provide long-term relief from dry, irritated throats.

6. Ulmus rubra

Ulmus rubra tinctures contains several bioactive constituents that make it beneficial for dry throat.

The tannins in Ulmus rubra have a soothing effect, reducing inflammation and irritation in the throat.

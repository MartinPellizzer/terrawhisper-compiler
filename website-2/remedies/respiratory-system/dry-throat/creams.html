1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains glycyrrhetic acid and glycosides, which are known for their anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to soothe and calm the mucous membranes in the throat, reducing swelling and discomfort. The saponins present in Glycyrrhiza glabra creams also have a moisturizing effect, which helps to hydrate the dry tissues in the throat. The demulcent properties of glycyrrhizin, a key constituent of the cream, form a protective barrier on the throat's surface, shielding it from further irritation.

This combination of properties makes Glycyrrhiza glabra creams effective in relieving dry throat symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of glycerin, 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, and 1 teaspoon of beeswax.
  2. Melt the beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the glycerin, distilled water, and glycyrrhiza glabra root powder until the powder is fully dissolved.
  4. Slowly add the melted beeswax to the glycerin mixture, stirring constantly until the mixture is well combined and smooth.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let it cool before applying it to your dry throat as needed.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains compounds like alkylamides and caffeic acid, which are known for their anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce swelling in the throat, making it easier to swallow and breathe. Echinacea purpurea also contains flavonoids like kaempferol and quercetin, which have antioxidant properties that help to protect the mucous membranes in the throat from damage. The polysaccharides in Echinacea purpurea creams stimulate the production of mucus, which can help to soothe and protect the dry, irritated throat.

The combination of these compounds helps to reduce inflammation and promote healing in the throat, making Echinacea purpurea creams a popular remedy for dry throat.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1/4 cup of coconut oil in a clean bowl.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a small saucepan over low heat until it's warm and liquid.
  3. Add the dried Echinacea flowers to the warm coconut oil and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the Echinacea flowers.
  5. Mix 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter into the Echinacea-infused coconut oil and stir until it's smooth and creamy.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains bioactive constituents like eucalyptol, cineole, and globulol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the throat. Eucalyptol, in particular, has a soothing effect on the mucous membranes, making it easier to swallow and breathe. The decongestant properties of eucalyptol and cineole help clear mucus from the airways, relieving congestion and coughing.

The combination of these properties makes Eucalyptus globulus creams effective in providing quick relief from dry and sore throats.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of eucalyptus essential oil, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval until melted.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from heat and stir in the eucalyptus essential oil and vitamin E oil.
  4. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container and let it cool and solidify completely.
  5. Once solid, your eucalyptus globulus cream is ready to use. Apply a small amount to your dry throat as needed.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for dry throat?

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains the active constituents glycyrrhizin and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and irritation in the throat. Glycyrrhizin also has a soothing effect on the mucous membranes, making it easier to swallow and breathe. Additionally, the flavonoids in Glycyrrhiza glabra teas have antioxidant properties, which help protect the throat from damage caused by free radicals.

The anti-inflammatory and soothing properties of these compounds make Glycyrrhiza glabra teas a good choice for relieving dry throat symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Add the dried root to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture using a tea strainer or cheesecloth to remove the root.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to the tea, if desired, to sweeten the flavor.
  5. Drink the tea hot or let it cool down before consumption to soothe a dry throat.

Herbal teas can be a good option for soothing a dry throat, but it's essential to be cautious about consuming them every day.

Herbal teas can help soothe a dry throat, but how long it takes to feel better depends on the type of tea and your individual needs. Some herbal teas, like slippery elm and licorice root, can start working within 30 minutes to an hour, while others may take longer to show results.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for dry throat?

This helps to calm and moisturize your throat, making it feel more comfortable.

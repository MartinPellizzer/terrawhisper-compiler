6 Herbal Preparations For Colds

Herbal preparations can help with colds by providing relief from symptoms and aiding in recovery.

A decoction is a strong brew made by boiling herbs in water, which is often used to treat respiratory issues like coughs and congestion. This method is good for thick, fibrous herbs like roots and bark, which wouldn't release their active ingredients when steeped in hot water like an infusion. A tincture is a liquid extract made by soaking herbs in a solvent like ethanol or glycerin. Tinctures are convenient because they're easy to take and can be added to food or drinks.

They're particularly useful for herbs that have strong, bitter flavors, as the solvent helps to mask the taste. Tinctures can also be stored for longer periods of time without losing their potency. An infusion is a weaker brew made by steeping herbs in hot water, which is often used to treat digestive issues and calm the mind. This method is good for delicate herbs like leaves and flowers, which could be damaged by boiling water. Infusions can be made with a variety of herbs, from soothing chamomile to decongesting eucalyptus.

By choosing the right herbal preparation, individuals can find relief from cold symptoms and support their body's natural healing process.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Colds?

If you have a cold, some herbs can make it worse.

Avoid using Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) because it can raise blood pressure and make your heart work harder. Also, avoid Yerba Santa (Clinopodium douglasii) as it can cause allergic reactions and interact with other medications.

Sage (Salvia officinalis) is another herb to avoid, as it can dry out your throat and make it harder to swallow. Andrographis (Andrographis paniculata) is also not recommended, as it can cause stomach upset and interact with blood thinners. Meanwhile, use herbs that ease congestion, such as Thyme (Thymus vulgaris), which can help loosen mucus and reduce coughing.

Other herbs like Elderberry (Sambucus nigra), Slippery Elm (Ulmus rubra), and Licorice Root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) can help soothe a sore throat and reduce inflammation.

11 Herbal Cream Remedies That Can Help COPD Patients with Acute Bronchitis

People with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) often experience episodes of acute bronchitis, which is an inflammation of the airways in the lungs.

This happens because the airways in people with COPD are already damaged and narrowed, making it harder for air to pass through. When a cold or flu virus infects the airways, it can trigger an acute bronchitis episode, causing symptoms like coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath. These symptoms can be severe and disrupt daily life, making it hard to perform even simple tasks. For people with COPD, acute bronchitis episodes can be especially challenging, as they often have underlying lung damage and may already be experiencing breathing difficulties.

As a result, they may need to take time off work or limit their physical activities to avoid exacerbating their symptoms. When a person with COPD has an acute bronchitis episode, they typically turn to medication to help manage their symptoms. However, some people also find relief with topical treatments like herbal creams, which can be applied directly to the skin. These creams can help soothe the airways, reduce inflammation, and ease congestion, making it easier to breathe.

By providing a targeted approach to symptom relief, herbal creams can be a valuable addition to a treatment plan for people with COPD who are experiencing acute bronchitis.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams can provide relief from the symptoms of acute bronchitis.

The creams contain eucalyptol, a compound that's known for its decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties. When applied topically, eucalyptol helps to open up airways, making it easier to breathe. It also has a soothing effect on the throat and chest, reducing discomfort and coughing. To use Eucalyptus globulus creams for acute bronchitis, apply a thin layer to your chest, neck, or back, 2-3 times a day.

You can also add a few drops of eucalyptus oil to your bathwater for added relief. Make sure to follow the instructions on the label and consult with a healthcare professional before using the cream, especially if you're pregnant, breastfeeding, or have sensitive skin. Using Eucalyptus globulus creams may lead to reduced coughing, improved breathing, and a decrease in chest congestion. You may also notice a decrease in fatigue and an improvement in your overall well-being. However, it's essential to remember that Eucalyptus globulus creams are not a substitute for medical treatment.

If your symptoms persist or worsen, you should seek medical attention.

11 Herbal Teas For Acute Bronchitis

Herbal teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate the symptoms of acute bronchitis.

Acute bronchitis is a common respiratory condition characterized by inflammation and irritation in the airways, leading to coughing, mucus production, and difficulty breathing. Herbal teas, such as those made from Eucalyptus globulus, Echinacea purpurea, and Thymus vulgaris, can provide relief by loosening and clearing mucus from the airways, reducing inflammation, and soothing the irritated tissues. Eucalyptus globulus, also known as eucalyptus oil, has decongestant properties that can help open up airways, making it easier to breathe.

Echinacea purpurea is known for its immune-boosting properties, which can help your body fight off the underlying infection causing the bronchitis. Thymus vulgaris, or thyme, has antimicrobial properties that can help combat the infection. Drinking these herbal teas can bring several benefits to your life, including improved lung function, reduced coughing and mucus production, and a faster recovery time.

By incorporating herbal teas into your daily routine, you can alleviate the discomfort and symptoms associated with acute bronchitis, making it easier to go about your daily activities and enjoy a better quality of life.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains compounds like eucalyptol, cineole, and alpha-terpinen that work together to ease respiratory problems.

Eucalyptol helps open airways by thinning mucus and relieving congestion, while cineole reduces inflammation in the bronchial tubes. The anti-inflammatory properties of alpha-terpinen help to soothe the airways and reduce coughing. The expectorant properties of eucalyptus globulus teas help to loosen and clear out mucus and other debris from the lungs, making it easier to breathe.

By clearing out airway blockages and reducing inflammation, eucalyptus globulus teas can provide relief from the symptoms of acute bronchitis.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves and a cup of boiling water.
  2. Add the Eucalyptus globulus leaves to a cup and pour in the boiling water.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the leaves using a tea strainer.
  4. Add honey to taste, if desired, and mix well.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day as needed to help relieve acute bronchitis symptoms.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains alkylamides, glycosides, and caffeic acid derivatives as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the airways and ease congestion. The alkylamides also have immunomodulatory effects, which can enhance the body's natural defense mechanisms to fight off infections that cause acute bronchitis. Additionally, the glycosides and caffeic acid derivatives in Echinacea purpurea have antioxidant properties that can help protect the lungs from oxidative stress and damage.

By reducing inflammation and promoting immune function, Echinacea purpurea teas may help alleviate symptoms of acute bronchitis.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 teaspoon of dried licorice root.
  2. Combine the Echinacea purpurea flowers and licorice root in a small bowl.
  3. Heat 1 cup of water in a pot until it starts boiling.
  4. Remove the pot from heat and add the Echinacea purpurea mixture to the hot water. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup and drink it while it's warm. Repeat the process 2-3 times a day for acute bronchitis relief.

3. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas contains thymol and carvacrol, two biologically active constituents that have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce the inflammation and bacterial infection in the airways, which is a major contributor to acute bronchitis. Thymol also has a bronchodilatory effect, which means it can help to widen the airways and improve breathing. Carvacrol's antioxidant properties can help to protect the lungs from oxidative stress, which can exacerbate bronchitis symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and infection, and improving lung function, Thymus vulgaris teas can help to alleviate symptoms of acute bronchitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves or 1 teaspoon of dried leaves.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot and let it cool for 1 minute.
  3. Add the fresh or dried Thymus vulgaris leaves to the cooled water.
  4. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then strain the liquid.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the Thymus vulgaris tea 3 times a day to help alleviate acute bronchitis symptoms.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains a compound called gingerol, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the airways.

This makes it easier to breathe when you have acute bronchitis. The bioactive constituents in gingerol, including 6-gingerol and 8-gingerol, inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which are molecules that cause inflammation. By reducing inflammation, Zingiber officinale teas helps to ease congestion and open up air passages, making it a helpful remedy for acute bronchitis.

Additionally, gingerol has antitussive properties, which help to relieve coughing, a common symptom of acute bronchitis.

  1. Gather 1 tsp of dried Zingiber officinale root and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the Zingiber officinale root in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  4. Add 1 tsp of honey to the tea, if desired, to sweeten the flavor.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, ideally between meals, to help alleviate acute bronchitis symptoms.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains licorice root, which has been used to help alleviate symptoms of acute bronchitis.

The bioactive constituents, such as glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and polyphenols, have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling in the airways. Glycyrrhizin also has expectorant properties, which help loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, making it easier to breathe. The antioxidants in licorice root tea help protect the lungs from further damage caused by inflammation and oxidative stress.

By reducing inflammation and loosening mucus, Glycyrrhiza glabra teas can help alleviate congestion and coughing associated with acute bronchitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots. You can buy them from a health store or online.
  2. Crush the roots into small pieces using a mortar and pestle or a coffee grinder.
  3. Measure 1 tablespoon of crushed roots and add it to 1 cup of boiling water.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes. Strain the mixture using a tea strainer.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve acute bronchitis symptoms.

6. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra teas contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The flavonoids in Sambucus nigra tea, specifically isorhapontigenin and acetoside, have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in the airways, a key factor in acute bronchitis. The phenolic acids, such as ferulic acid, possess antimicrobial properties, which help combat bacterial infections that can cause bronchitis. The terpenes in Sambucus nigra tea, like bornyl acetate, exhibit expectorant properties that help loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to breathe.

By reducing inflammation, fighting infections, and loosening mucus, Sambucus nigra tea helps alleviate symptoms of acute bronchitis.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Sambucus nigra flowers and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Pour the boiling water over the Sambucus nigra flowers in a cup.
  3. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the liquid into another cup.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) to the tea and mix well.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve acute bronchitis symptoms.

7. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains the bioactive constituents carnosic acid, carnosol, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling in the airways and ease congestion in the lungs. Rosmarinic acid, in particular, has antioxidant properties that protect the lungs from further damage and promote healing. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Rosmarinus officinalis teas help alleviate the symptoms of acute bronchitis, such as coughing and difficulty breathing.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Rosmarinus officinalis teas helps the body recover from acute bronchitis more efficiently.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.
  2. Heat 1 cup of water in a pot until it boils.
  3. Add the 2 tablespoons of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves to the boiling water.
  4. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then strain it.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the tea, 2-3 times a day, as needed to help with acute bronchitis symptoms.

8. Verbascum thapsus

Verbascum thapsus teas contains mucilages, flavonoids, and triterpenoids as its main bioactive constituents.

Mucilages help to soothe and calm the bronchial mucosa, reducing inflammation and irritation. Flavonoids have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to combat the underlying causes of acute bronchitis, such as oxidative stress. Triterpenoids, specifically ursolic acid, have been shown to have expectorant properties, helping to loosen and clear mucus from the airways.

By combining these properties, Verbascum thapsus teas may help to alleviate symptoms of acute bronchitis by reducing inflammation, soothing the bronchial mucosa, and loosening mucus.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Verbascum thapsus flowers.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of dried flowers and put them in a tea infuser or a small muslin bag.
  3. Heat 1 cup of water in a pot and bring it to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and add the tea infuser or muslin bag to the water. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it while it's hot. Repeat 2-3 times a day for acute bronchitis.

9. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds help to improve blood flow and reduce inflammation in the airways, making it easier to breathe. The flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, act as antioxidants to combat oxidative stress and cell damage in the lungs. The terpenoids, including bilobalide and ginkgolides, possess anti-inflammatory properties that help to calm the bronchial tubes and relieve congestion.

By improving circulation and reducing inflammation, Ginkgo biloba teas can help to alleviate symptoms of acute bronchitis, such as coughing and difficulty breathing.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the Ginkgo biloba leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup using a tea strainer or a piece of cheesecloth.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) to the tea and mix well.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day for acute bronchitis relief.

10. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help reduce inflammation and kill bacteria in the bronchial tubes. The antispasmodic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help relax bronchial muscles, making it easier to breathe. Additionally, the expectorant properties of these compounds help loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to cough up and clear the lungs.

By reducing inflammation, killing bacteria, and loosening mucus, Lavandula angustifolia teas can help alleviate symptoms of acute bronchitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) and 1/2 teaspoon of dried ginger (optional) for added flavor.
  3. Steep the Lavandula angustifolia flowers in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and add the honey and ginger (if using).
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help soothe acute bronchitis symptoms.

11. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas contains compounds like menthol, menthone, and limonene.

These active constituents have anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties, which help to reduce swelling and congestion in the airways. Menthol also acts as a bronchodilator, making it easier to breathe. The tea's expectorant properties help to loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, making it easier to cough up.

The combined effects of these properties can help alleviate symptoms of acute bronchitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh peppermint leaves (Mentha x piperita) and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Place the peppermint leaves in a heat-resistant cup or teapot.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the peppermint leaves and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into another cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove the leaves.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve symptoms of acute bronchitis.

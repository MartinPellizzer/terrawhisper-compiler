8 calming essential oils for little children under 5 with acute bronchitis

Children under 5 can get acute bronchitis because their tiny airways are very sensitive and can get easily irritated by viruses or bacteria.

When this happens, their airways swell and fill with mucus, making it hard to breathe. This can make them feel miserable, cough a lot, and wheeze. They might even have trouble sleeping or eating because they're so uncomfortable. Their tiny bodies are working hard to fight off the infection, and they need lots of rest, fluids, and gentle care. If their symptoms are severe or don't go away, they might need to see a doctor for further treatment.

Acute bronchitis in young children can disrupt their daily routine and affect their quality of life. They might miss out on playtime with friends, or have trouble keeping up with their usual activities. Their parents or caregivers might feel worried and stressed too, trying to help them feel better. When it comes to treating acute bronchitis in children under 5, herbal essential oils can be a helpful addition to their care. These oils are made from plants and can be used in a diffuser or mixed with a carrier oil for gentle skin application. They have anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe the airways and reduce coughing.

They can also promote relaxation and help the child feel more comfortable.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils is a natural remedy for acute bronchitis.

It helps because it has anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties, thanks to its main constituents: cineole, alpha-pinene, and limonene. These compounds help reduce swelling and open up airways, making it easier to breathe. When you inhale the oil, the cineole binds to receptors in your body, relaxing muscles and reducing inflammation in the bronchial tubes. This helps ease congestion and coughing, giving you relief from acute bronchitis symptoms. To use Eucalyptus globulus essential oils, mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it to your chest or inhale it through steam inhalation.

You can also add a few drops to your humidifier or diffuser to release the oil into the air. When you inhale the oil, you may start to feel relief from congestion and coughing within 15-30 minutes. As you continue to use the oil, you may notice a decrease in the severity of your symptoms and an improvement in your breathing. As you take action with Eucalyptus globulus essential oils, you may experience a reduction in coughing and wheezing, making it easier to sleep and perform daily activities. You may also notice a decrease in the production of mucus, making it easier to clear your airways.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, you should consult with a healthcare professional for further guidance and treatment.

2. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils, also known as thyme oil, is a natural remedy that can help alleviate symptoms of acute bronchitis.

This oil contains compounds like thymol, carvacrol, and pinene, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These properties can help combat bacterial and viral infections that cause bronchitis, reduce inflammation in the airways, and loosen mucus, making it easier to cough up. Thymol, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the growth of certain bacteria and viruses, helping to clear the infection. To use thyme oil for acute bronchitis, mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and inhale it through steam inhalation or apply it to the chest and back with a massage.

You can also add a few drops to your bath water for a relaxing and soothing experience. It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially if you have any underlying health conditions or allergies. When using thyme oil, start with a small amount and gradually increase as needed. As you inhale the oil or apply it to your skin, you may experience relief from coughing, congestion, and chest discomfort.

With consistent use, you may notice improved respiratory function, reduced inflammation, and a faster recovery from acute bronchitis.

4. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils, also known as purple coneflower oil, is a natural remedy often used to help alleviate the symptoms of acute bronchitis.

This is because the oil contains properties and constituents such as alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects. These compounds help to reduce inflammation in the airways, ease congestion, and boost the body's immune system to fight off infections. When using Echinacea purpurea essential oils for acute bronchitis, it's recommended to dilute the oil with a carrier oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil, and apply it topically to the chest, back, and soles of the feet.

You can also inhale the oil through steam inhalation or add a few drops to your bath water. To do this, add 5-7 drops of the oil to a bowl of hot water, inhale the steam for 5-10 minutes, and then repeat the process 2-3 times a day. As you use Echinacea purpurea essential oils for acute bronchitis, you may start to notice improvements in your symptoms, such as reduced coughing, congestion, and shortness of breath. Your body's immune system may also become stronger, helping to fight off the underlying infection.

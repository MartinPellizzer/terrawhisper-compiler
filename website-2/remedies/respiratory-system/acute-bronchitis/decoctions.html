What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have acute bronchitis?

If you have acute bronchitis, there are some herbal decoctions that you should avoid.

One of them is Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis), as it contains a chemical called berberine, which can be toxic in high doses and interact with certain medications. Also, avoid Slippery Elm (Ulmus rubra) as it can thicken mucus, making breathing harder. Another one to avoid is Lobelia (Lobelia inflata), as its active compound lobeline can be toxic if taken in excess.

Additionally, be cautious with Wild Indigo (Baptisia tinctoria) as it can interact with blood thinners and other medications. You should also avoid Comfrey (Symphytum officinale) as it can cause liver damage if taken long-term. Marshmallow (Althaea officinalis) is also not recommended as it can lower blood sugar levels, which may be a problem if you're already taking diabetes medications.

Finally, avoid Mullein (Verbascum species) as it can interact with certain medications, such as sedatives and blood thinners.

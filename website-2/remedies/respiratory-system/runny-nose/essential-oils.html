10 Herbal Essential Oils For Runny Nose

Herbal essential oils can be a great help when you have a runny nose.

For example, Eucalyptus globulus, also known as eucalyptus oil, has a compound called eucalyptol, which is known to ease congestion and reduce inflammation in the nose.

Another herb, Rosmarinus officinalis, or rosemary oil, has a compound called camphor, which can help loosen mucus and make it easier to breathe. Then there's Cymbopogon citratus, or lemongrass oil, which has a compound called citral, that has anti-inflammatory properties and can help reduce swelling in the nasal passages. When you inhale these essential oils, they can help calm your body and reduce the production of mucus, making it easier to breathe. This can be especially helpful when you have a cold or allergies.

By using these oils, you can find some relief from a runny nose and get back to your daily activities.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like cineole, pinene, and limonene.

These constituents have decongestant and expectorant properties, which help to relieve a runny nose by thinning mucus and making it easier to expel. Cineole, the main component, has natural anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and ease congestion in the nasal passages. Pinene, on the other hand, helps to open up airways and improve breathing, while limonene adds to the oil's decongestant effects.

When inhaled, these properties work together to provide fast relief from a runny nose and congestion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water until it reaches 100°C (212°F).
  3. Add the Eucalyptus globulus leaves to the hot water and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the liquid using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the leaves.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 2-3 days to separate into essential oil and water. Skim the essential oil from the top and store it in a dark glass bottle.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains camphor, bornyl acetate, and borneol.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation and congestion in the nasal passages. The decongestant and expectorant properties of these constituents help to clear mucus and reduce the flow of nasal discharge. The antimicrobial properties of borneol also help to combat any underlying infections that may be contributing to the runny nose.

By reducing inflammation and mucus production, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help to alleviate symptoms of a runny nose.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat until it starts boiling.
  3. Reduce heat to low and add the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves to the saucepan.
  4. Steep the leaves for 20-30 minutes, then strain the mixture into a separate container.
  5. Use a distillation kit or a simple still to distill the mixture and extract the essential oil, which can be used to help relieve a runny nose.

3. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, beta-pinene, and beta-caryophyllene as its active constituents.

These compounds have decongestant and expectorant properties that help relieve a runny nose. Limonene, in particular, has been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects, which can reduce swelling in the nasal passages and promote drainage. Beta-pinene has a bronchodilatory effect, allowing for easier breathing and mucus clearance.

By using Cymbopogon citratus essential oils, the body can benefit from the natural decongestant properties of its active constituents, helping to alleviate a runny nose.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves, 1 cup of water, and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Wash the leaves with water and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Combine the leaves and water in a saucepan and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes. Strain the mixture into the glass jar.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 hours. Then, use a cheesecloth or coffee filter to separate the oil from the liquid. Store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

4. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains the active constituents cineole and viridiflorol, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Cineole helps to reduce swelling and congestion in the nasal passages, while viridiflorol has a decongestant effect, making it easier to breathe. The expectorant properties of these compounds help to thin and clear mucus, relieving a runny nose. The antimicrobial properties also help to fight off infections that can cause runny noses.

By reducing swelling and clearing mucus, Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils can help to alleviate the symptoms of a runny nose.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Melaleuca alternifolia leaves. Clean them with water to remove dirt.
  2. Add 2 cups of water to a saucepan and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low.
  3. Add the cleaned Melaleuca leaves to the saucepan and cover. Let it steep for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the liquid into a separate container using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter. Discard the leaves.
  5. Collect the liquid and transfer it to a dark glass bottle. Store it in a cool, dry place.

5. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains boiactive constituents like gingerol and shogaol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling in the nasal passages. They also have decongestant properties, making it easier to breathe and relieving congestion. The anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties of gingerol and shogaol help to reduce mucus production and alleviate a runny nose.

Regular use of Zingiber officinale essential oils may help to alleviate symptoms of a cold or flu.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots, 2 cups of water, and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Chop the ginger roots into small pieces and add them to the glass jar.
  3. Pour the water over the ginger roots and seal the jar.
  4. Leave the jar in a warm, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it daily. This will help the ginger roots release their oils.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids. Your Zingiber officinale essential oil is now ready to use.

6. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol and carvacrol, which are its main active constituents.

Thymol has antimicrobial properties that help reduce the growth of bacteria and viruses that can cause a runny nose. Carvacrol has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling in the nasal passages and ease congestion. Thymus vulgaris essential oils also has expectorant properties, which help loosen and clear out mucus from the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe.

This combination of properties can help alleviate symptoms of a runny nose by reducing the underlying causes and making it easier to breathe.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and 2 cups of water.
  2. Steep the leaves in boiling water for 5 minutes, then let cool.
  3. Strain the mixture and collect the liquid in a bowl.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil to the liquid.
  5. Use a dropper to bottle the essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place.

7. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains compounds like menthone, limonene, and beta-pinene.

These bioactive constituents have decongestant properties that help reduce nasal congestion. The menthol in Mentha x piperita essential oils has a cooling effect that can help to clear mucus from the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe. Additionally, the antimicrobial properties of the essential oil can help to combat infections that may be causing the runny nose.

By reducing inflammation and killing bacteria, Mentha x piperita essential oils can help to alleviate symptoms of a runny nose.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Mentha x piperita (peppermint) leaves and flowers.
  2. Use a clean glass jar with a lid, and fill it with 2 cups of the peppermint leaves and flowers.
  3. Pour 2 cups of water over the peppermint leaves and flowers in the jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle, discard the solids.

8. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are responsible for its decongestant properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation and congestion in the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe. Linalool, in particular, has a strong affinity for the nasal mucous membranes, where it helps to break down excess mucus and promote drainage. This can provide relief from a runny nose by reducing the amount of mucus produced and making it easier to expel.

As a result, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can be used to help alleviate symptoms of a runny nose.

  1. Harvest 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers when they are in full bloom.
  2. Wash the flowers with 1 cup of cold water to remove dirt and impurities.
  3. Dry the flowers for 2-3 days in a well-ventilated area or use a food dehydrator on low heat.
  4. Use a carrier oil like jojoba oil or sweet almond oil and mix 1 tablespoon of dried flowers with 1/2 cup of carrier oil.
  5. Steep the mixture for 2-3 weeks in a glass container, shaking it daily, and then strain and discard the flowers to get the essential oil.

9. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains rosmarinic acid and carnosic acid as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help reduce swelling and fight off infections in the nasal passages. The decongestant properties of Salvia officinalis essential oils help to clear mucus from the nasal passages, relieving congestion and runny nose. Additionally, the expectorant properties help to thin and expel mucus, making it easier to breathe.

By reducing inflammation and eliminating infections, Salvia officinalis essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of a runny nose.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Salvia leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture into a bowl using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a glass bottle and let it sit at room temperature for 2-3 days to allow the oil to separate and rise to the top.

10. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains boiactive constituents like cichoric acid, chlorogenic acid, and alkylamides.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe. The alkylamides, specifically, have a decongestant effect that helps to clear out mucus and excess fluids from the nose. Cichoric acid also has antimicrobial properties that help prevent the growth of bacteria and other microorganisms that can cause runny noses.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infection, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of a runny nose.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the Echinacea flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Place the jar in a sunny spot, but not in direct sunlight. Shake the jar daily for 2 weeks.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Echinacea essential oil in a dark glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops as needed to relieve a runny nose.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent runny nose from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help ease a runny nose.

Some teas, like peppermint and eucalyptus, have ingredients that can thin mucus and open airways. These teas can calm the nose and sinuses, reducing congestion.

They may not completely prevent a runny nose, but they can provide relief from symptoms.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for runny nose every day?

Herbal teas can help soothe a runny nose, but it's not recommended to drink them every day.

Some herbs, like peppermint and eucalyptus, can be too strong and cause stomach problems or interact with medications.

It's best to limit your intake to when you need it, like during colds or allergies.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in runny nose?

Herbal teas can help with a runny nose, but it depends on the type of tea and your body's response.

Some people notice relief within a few hours, while others may take a day or two to feel better.

It's usually a gradual process, and you might need to try different teas to find what works best for you.

Herbal essential oils: 10 remedies for asthmatics with a runny nose

People with asthma often have trouble with a runny nose.

This is because their airways are already inflamed, and when they breathe in something that irritates them, like pollen or dust, their body tries to fight it off by producing more mucus. This can make it hard to breathe and can trigger asthma attacks. A runny nose can also make it harder to sleep, and it can be embarrassing to deal with in public. It's not just the physical symptoms that are a problem - it can also affect a person's mood and quality of life. They might have to take more medication, or avoid certain activities that they enjoy.

For people with asthma, managing a runny nose can be a challenge. They might need to use tissues constantly, and they might have to take time off work or school to deal with their symptoms. This can be frustrating and stressful, and it can make it hard to feel like they're in control of their own health. Herbal essential oils are a natural way to help manage a runny nose. Certain types of essential oils have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling and calm the airways. They can also help to thin out mucus, making it easier to breathe and reducing the need for tissues.

By using these oils in a diffuser or applying them to the skin, people with asthma may be able to find some relief from their symptoms.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help alleviate a runny nose due to its decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties.

It contains high levels of eucalyptol, a compound that helps to break down mucus and open up airways. When inhaled, eucalyptol creates a thin layer on the surface of the nasal passages, allowing for easier breathing. This oil also contains other active constituents like limonene and alpha-pinene, which further contribute to its decongestant and expectorant properties. To use Eucalyptus globulus essential oils for a runny nose, you can add a few drops to a diffuser, inhale it directly from a cloth or handkerchief, or mix it with a carrier oil for a nasal rub.

When inhaling directly, place a few drops on a cloth or handkerchief and breathe in the vapors for a few minutes. For a nasal rub, mix a few drops of the essential oil with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and gently rub it onto your nostrils. When you take these actions, you can expect your nasal passages to become less congested, making it easier to breathe through your nose. You may also find that your runny nose becomes less frequent or severe.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

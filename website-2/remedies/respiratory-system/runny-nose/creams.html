Runny Nose Solutions: 7 Herbal Creams for Allergy Sufferers

When you have allergies, your body sees something harmless as a threat.

It releases chemicals to fight it off, like a runny nose. This can be a real nuisance, making it hard to breathe, sleep, and even work or play. The constant congestion and sniffles can be tiring and frustrating, especially when you can't seem to shake them off. Sometimes, even simple tasks like eating or talking become challenges.

Allergy sufferers often have to deal with this on a daily basis, which can affect their mood, energy levels, and overall quality of life. Herbal creams are often used to help alleviate these symptoms. They work by targeting the underlying causes of the runny nose, such as inflammation or irritation. These creams usually contain plant extracts that have natural anti-inflammatory or soothing properties. When applied to the skin, they can help calm the nasal passages and reduce congestion.

This can provide quick relief from the symptoms, allowing you to breathe and feel better.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams have been traditionally used to help alleviate a runny nose, and for good reason.

The creams contain properties such as alkylamides, caffeic acid, and flavonoids, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These constituents help to soothe and calm the nasal passages, reducing the production of excess mucus and congestion.

When using Echinacea purpurea creams for a runny nose, apply a small amount to the bridge of your nose or under your nostrils to help clear the airways. To get the most out of the cream, apply it regularly throughout the day, especially when you feel your symptoms worsening. As you continue to use the cream, you may start to notice a reduction in your runny nose symptoms, including less congestion and a clearer airway.

You may also find that you're less likely to experience sinus pressure and headaches, making it easier to breathe and feel more comfortable.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams, also known as ginger creams, have been traditionally used to help alleviate symptoms of a runny nose, such as congestion and sinus pressure.

This is due to the presence of gingerols and shogaols, which are bioactive compounds found in ginger that have anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties. These constituents work by reducing swelling in the nasal passages and sinuses, thereby opening up airways and promoting drainage. When used as a topical cream, the active ingredients in Zingiber officinale creams can be absorbed quickly into the skin, providing rapid relief from congestion and discomfort.

To use Zingiber officinale creams for a runny nose, apply a small amount to the affected area, ideally the bridge of the nose or the sinuses, and gently massage it in until absorbed. You can repeat this process as needed, ideally several times a day, to help manage symptoms and find relief. As you use the cream, you may notice a decrease in nasal discharge and congestion, as well as an improvement in breathing and overall sense of comfort.

Additionally, some people may experience a reduction in sinus pressure and headaches, although this may vary from person to person.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams are popular for helping with runny noses due to their decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The creams contain eucalyptol, a compound that helps open up airways and reduce swelling in the nasal passages. When applied topically, eucalyptol works quickly to ease congestion and help you breathe more easily.

To use eucalyptus globulus cream for a runny nose, apply a small amount to the bridge of your nose or on your chest, inhaling the vapor to help decongest your airways. You can also add a few drops of eucalyptus oil to your humidifier or inhale it directly from a cloth or tissue. By taking these actions, you can likely experience a reduction in nasal discharge and congestion, making it easier to breathe and sleep.

This relief can last for several hours, depending on the severity of your cold or allergy symptoms.

4. Pelargonium sidoides

Pelargonium sidoides creams have been used to help alleviate runny nose due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The creams typically contain a compound called ursolic acid, which has been shown to reduce swelling and ease congestion in the nasal passages. The creams also often contain other constituents such as tannins and phenolic acids, which have antiviral and antibacterial properties, helping to combat underlying infections that may be causing the runny nose. To use Pelargonium sidoides creams for a runny nose, apply a thin layer to the affected area, typically the bridge of the nose or the nostrils. Gently massage the cream into the skin until it is fully absorbed.

You can repeat this process as needed, ideally 2-3 times a day. It is essential to follow the instructions on the packaging and use the cream as directed. When using Pelargonium sidoides creams for a runny nose, you can expect to experience some relief from congestion and swelling. The creams may help to reduce the amount of mucus produced, making it easier to breathe and feel more comfortable. However, it is crucial to note that these creams may not address the underlying cause of the runny nose, such as allergies or a cold.

It is recommended to combine the use of the creams with other treatments, such as over-the-counter medications or home remedies, to manage symptoms effectively.

5. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams are a popular remedy for runny nose due to their anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties.

The creams typically contain flavonoids and terpenoids, which help to reduce swelling and congestion in the nasal passages. Ginkgo biloba also has a natural decongestant effect, thanks to its ability to thin mucus and promote drainage. To use ginkgo biloba cream for a runny nose, apply a thin layer to the affected area 2-3 times a day, or as needed.

You can also use a humidifier to add moisture to the air, which can help to soothe a runny nose. As you use the cream, you may start to notice a reduction in nasal discharge and congestion. Your nose may feel clearer and more comfortable, and you may be able to breathe more easily. You can also try using saline nasal sprays or rinses to help flush out excess mucus and debris.

By combining these methods with ginkgo biloba cream, you may be able to alleviate your runny nose symptoms and get back to feeling your best.

6. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale creams have been used for centuries to help alleviate runny nose symptoms.

This is because the cream contains compounds like allantoin, vitamin B12, and rosmarinic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties. When applied topically to the nasal area, these compounds help to reduce swelling and congestion, making it easier to breathe. To try Symphytum officinale creams for a runny nose, apply a small amount to your nose a few times a day.

You can do this by gently massaging the cream into your nasal passages with a circular motion. It's essential to be patient and consistent, as it may take a few days to notice improvements in your symptoms. If you experience relief, you may notice a reduction in nasal discharge, congestion, and sinus pressure.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, consult with a healthcare professional for further guidance.

7. Ruscus aculeatus

Ruscus aculeatus creams are often used to help alleviate a runny nose, particularly one caused by allergies or colds.

This is due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties, which help to reduce swelling and block the release of histamine, a chemical that causes nasal congestion and runny nose. Ruscus aculeatus contains constituents such as aucubin and caffeic acid, which contribute to its medicinal effects. If you're experiencing a runny nose, you can try applying Ruscus aculeatus cream to the affected area. To do this, gently massage a small amount of cream into your nostrils and around the bridge of your nose.

You can also apply it to the back of your throat to help soothe any irritation. When you take action with Ruscus aculeatus cream, you may experience a decrease in nasal discharge and congestion. Your runny nose may become less frequent and severe, allowing you to breathe more easily and feel more comfortable. You might also notice a reduction in sinus pressure and headache, as the cream helps to reduce inflammation and promote healing.

However, it's essential to note that these effects may vary depending on the individual and the severity of their condition.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains eucalyptol, a compound known for its decongestant properties.

Eucalyptol helps reduce swelling in the nasal passages, allowing for easier breathing and relieving congestion. Tannins in the cream also have an astringent effect, which helps reduce the amount of mucus produced, making it a good option for runny noses. The camphor in the cream further aids in thinning mucus, making it easier to expel from the nasal passages.

By combining these properties, Eucalyptus globulus creams helps to alleviate symptoms of a runny nose.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of eucalyptus oil, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second intervals, stirring between each interval until melted.
  3. Remove melted mixture from heat and stir in eucalyptus oil and vitamin E oil until well combined.
  4. Pour mixture into a silicone candy mold or a small tin container and let it cool and harden completely.
  5. Once hardened, remove cream from mold and store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and polysaccharides.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling in the nasal passages. The alkylamides and caffeic acid derivatives in Echinacea purpurea also have antiviral properties, which can help combat viral infections that cause runny noses. The polysaccharides in the cream can stimulate the production of cytokines, which are proteins that help fight off infections and reduce inflammation.

By addressing the underlying causes of a runny nose, Echinacea purpurea creams can help alleviate congestion and promote a sense of relief.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Echinacea purpurea dried flowers, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add Echinacea purpurea dried flowers to the melted oil and beeswax mixture. Let it steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Add vitamin E oil to the strained mixture and stir well. Pour the mixture into a glass jar or tin.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify. Once solid, your Echinacea purpurea cream is ready to use. Apply it to your nose to help soothe a runny nose.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains the active constituents gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling in the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe. The anti-inflammatory properties also help to thin out excess mucus, making it less likely to flow freely. Additionally, the gingerols and shogaols have a warming effect on the body, which helps to loosen congestion and promote drainage.

By reducing swelling and thinning mucus, Zingiber officinale creams can provide relief from a runny nose.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of Zingiber officinale essential oil and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil to the melted mixture.
  4. Add the melted beeswax to the mixture and stir until combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify before applying to the runny nose area.

4. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains a high concentration of the active compound cineole.

Cineole has natural anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties, which can help to reduce nasal congestion and runny nose. The cream also contains other bioactive constituents such as terpinen-4-ol and beta-caryophyllene, which have antimicrobial properties that help to combat infections that may cause nasal congestion. The combination of these compounds in Melaleuca alternifolia creams can help to soothe and calm the nasal passages, reducing the production of excess mucus and relieving a runny nose.

By applying Melaleuca alternifolia creams topically, the active compounds can be absorbed into the skin, providing direct relief from nasal congestion and runny nose.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of distilled water, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a saucepan over low heat, stirring until smooth.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool slightly.
  4. Add the Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil and stir well. Then, slowly add the distilled water, stirring continuously.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Use as a cream for runny nose.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains a high amount of linalool and linalyl acetate, two powerful essential oils that have been used for centuries to soothe and calm the respiratory system.

These compounds work by reducing inflammation and fighting off bacteria that cause congestion, making it easier to breathe and reducing the runny nose associated with colds and allergies. The anti-inflammatory properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help to relax the nasal passages, allowing mucus to drain more freely and reducing the need to blow your nose constantly. The decongestant properties of these oils also help to reduce swelling in the nasal passages, allowing you to breathe more easily and feel more comfortable.

By using Lavandula angustifolia creams, you can find relief from a runny nose and breathe more easily.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of lavender essential oil, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl in the microwave (in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval) until they are completely melted.
  3. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Add lavender essential oil and vitamin E oil, and stir well.
  4. Pour the mixture into a silicone mold or a small tin container. Let it cool and harden completely.
  5. Once hardened, remove the cream from the mold and store it in a cool, dry place. Use 1-2 teaspoons as needed to relieve runny nose symptoms.

6. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids, bilobalide, and ginkgolides as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce swelling and fight off free radicals in the nasal passages. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba creams can narrow blood vessels, decreasing blood flow to the nasal area and reducing congestion. This can help to alleviate runny nose by reducing the amount of mucus produced and alleviating sinus pressure.

By applying Ginkgo biloba creams topically, it may help to provide quick relief from runny nose symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Ginkgo biloba extract, and 10 drops of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated.
  4. Add Ginkgo biloba extract and vitamin E oil to the mixture and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify before use. Apply a small amount to the affected area as needed to help relieve a runny nose.

Thymus vulgaris creams contains thymol, a key active constituent that helps relieve a runny nose.

Thymol has antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling in the nasal passages and combat infections. By targeting the root causes of a runny nose, Thymus vulgaris creams provide effective relief from this common symptom.

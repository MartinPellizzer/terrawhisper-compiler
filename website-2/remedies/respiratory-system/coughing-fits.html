Coughing Fits and Their Causes: Finding Relief with Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus, also known as blue gum, is a plant that has been used for a long time to help with coughs. The leaves of this plant have special oils inside them that can help to make breathing easier. When you inhale these oils, they can help to loosen and clear out mucus from your airways. This makes it easier to cough up the mucus, which can help to stop the cough. The oils in eucalyptus globulus also have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling and irritation in the throat and airways. This can make coughing fits feel less painful and uncomfortable. Many people find that inhaling eucalyptus globulus through steam inhalation or using it in a chest rub helps to ease their cough and make them feel better.

Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea is a plant that's been used for centuries to help with coughs and other cold symptoms. It's a type of herb that contains special compounds that can help reduce inflammation in the body. When we have a cough, it's often because our airways are inflamed, and Echinacea purpurea's anti-inflammatory properties can help calm them down. This can make it easier to breathe and reduce the frequency and severity of coughing fits. The plant also has antimicrobial properties, which means it can help fight off the underlying infection that's causing the cough. By boosting our immune system and reducing inflammation, Echinacea purpurea can help our body recover from a cold and get rid of the cough. The root of the plant is often used in teas, capsules, and other supplements to make it easy to consume.

Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, also known as licorice root, is a natural remedy that can help with coughing fits. The root contains a sweet-tasting compound called glycyrrhizin, which has anti-inflammatory properties. When you have a cough, it's often caused by inflammation in your throat. Glycyrrhizin in licorice root can reduce this inflammation, making it easier to swallow and breathe. Additionally, licorice root has been shown to have a soothing effect on the mucous membranes in your throat, which can help calm a cough. By reducing inflammation and soothing the throat, licorice root can provide relief from coughing fits.

Zingiber officinale

What Are The Main Causes Of Coughing Fits?

A coughing fit is a sudden and intense episode of coughing.

There are several reasons why it happens. One common cause is asthma, a condition where the airways in the lungs become narrow and swell. When you have asthma, your airways can become inflamed and irritated, leading to a coughing fit. Another cause of coughing fits is pneumonia, a serious infection that inflames the air sacs in the lungs.

Pneumonia can cause a cough that brings up mucus or pus, and can lead to a severe coughing fit. Bronchitis is also a common cause of coughing fits. Bronchitis is an inflammation of the lining of the bronchial tubes, which are the air passages that lead to the lungs. When the bronchial tubes become inflamed, they can produce excess mucus, leading to a coughing fit. Other causes of coughing fits include allergies, sinus infections, and gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD).

These conditions can irritate the airways and lead to a coughing fit.

Below are the main causes of coughing fits.

  • Asthma: Asthma causes coughing fits because the airways in the lungs get narrow and inflamed, making it hard to breathe, leading to a strong coughing response to try and clear the airways.
  • Pneumonia: Pneumonia causes coughing fits because the infection irritates the lungs and airways, producing excess mucus that the body tries to cough out to clear the airways and breathe properly again.
  • Bronchitis: Bronchitis causes coughing fits because the infection irritates the airways in the lungs, leading to an overproduction of mucus that triggers a persistent and often violent coughing reaction.
  • Pneumonitis: Pneumonitis, an inflammation of the lungs, causes coughing fits due to the body's attempt to clear out the damaged lung tissue and excess fluid that irritates the airways, leading to intense, persistent coughing.
  • Pleurisy: Pleurisy is a cause of coughing fits because the inflammation of the pleura (the thin layer of tissue surrounding the lungs) irritates the nerves, causing sharp pains and intense coughing spasms as the body tries to clear out the irritant.
  • TB: Tuberculosis (TB) causes coughing fits because the bacteria that cause TB infect and damage the lungs, leading to inflammation and irritation of the airways, which triggers a persistent and often severe coughing reaction.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Coughing Fits?

If you have coughing fits, there are some herbs you should avoid.

One such herb is Lobelia inflata, also known as Indian tobacco. It can worsen coughing and even cause vomiting. Another herb to avoid is Hyssopus officinalis, or hyssop, which can irritate the lungs and make coughing worse.

Also, avoid Asarum europaeum, or European wild ginger, as it can stimulate the digestive system and worsen coughing. Additionally, you should avoid Aconitum vulparia, or wolf's bane, as it can be toxic and cause more harm than good. Some people also recommend avoiding Ephedra sinica, or Chinese ephedra, as it can cause respiratory problems and worsen coughing fits. Finally, avoid Aristolochia clematitis, or birthwort, as it can cause kidney damage and interact with other medications.

It's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using any herb, especially if you have a chronic cough or other health conditions.

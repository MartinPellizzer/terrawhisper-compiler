Altitude Sickness: Causes, Symptoms, and Natural Herbal Remedies

Altitude sickness is a condition that occurs when your body cannot adapt quickly to high altitudes, typically above 8,000 feet.

It happens because of lower air pressure and lower oxygen levels. If you don't deal with altitude sickness, you may experience headaches, nausea, fatigue, and shortness of breath. In severe cases, it can lead to life-threatening conditions like high-altitude pulmonary edema (HAPE) and high-altitude cerebral edema (HACE). That's why it's essential to take preventive measures and use herbs and herbal preparations to help your body adapt. Herbs like Ginkgo biloba, Astragalus membranaceus, and Panax ginseng have been traditionally used to alleviate altitude sickness symptoms. Ginkgo biloba is known for its ability to improve blood flow and oxygen delivery to the body's tissues, which can help reduce the severity of altitude sickness.

Astragalus membranaceus, also known as Huang Qi, is an adaptogenic herb that helps the body adapt to stress and high altitudes. Panax ginseng, or Ginseng, is believed to improve physical performance and reduce fatigue, which can be beneficial when dealing with altitude sickness. Herbal preparations like decoction, infusion, and tincture can be used to extract the active ingredients from these herbs. A decoction is a liquid extract made by boiling the herbs in water, while an infusion is a liquid extract made by steeping the herbs in hot water. A tincture is a concentrated liquid extract made by soaking the herbs in a solvent like ethanol. These preparations can be taken before ascending to high altitudes to help prevent altitude sickness or taken during the ascent to alleviate symptoms.

Some people may choose to use herbal preparations as a supplement to medical treatments, but it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies, especially if you have underlying medical conditions or are taking medications.

What Are The Main Causes Of Altitude Sickness?

Altitude sickness occurs when the body can't adapt to high altitudes.

There are three main reasons for this: acclimatization, oxygen deprivation, and hypoxia. Acclimatization is the process by which the body adjusts to high altitudes, but it needs time.

If you go too high too quickly, your body can't adapt, and altitude sickness happens. Oxygen deprivation is also a cause, as at high altitudes, the air pressure is lower, and there's less oxygen available to breathe. Hypoxia is a condition where the body gets too little oxygen, leading to altitude sickness symptoms like headaches, nausea, and fatigue.

When you ascend to high altitudes, your body needs more oxygen to function properly, but it can't get it, leading to these symptoms.

Below are the main causes of altitude sickness.

  • Acclimatization: Acclimatization, or getting used to a new environment, is a cause of altitude sickness because the body tries to adapt too quickly, and this can cause its systems to fail and lead to symptoms such as headaches and nausea.
  • Oxygen deprivation: Oxygen deprivation is a cause of altitude sickness because at high altitudes, there is less oxygen in the air, making it harder for the body to get the oxygen it needs to function properly.
  • Hypoxia: At high altitudes, the air contains less oxygen, or "oxygen is scarce," which leads to hypoxia, making the body struggle to get the oxygen it needs.
  • Altitude gain rate: The rapid increase in altitude gain rate means the body has less time to acclimate to the lower air pressure and lower oxygen levels, leading to altitude sickness.
  • Hemodilution: Hemodilution is a cause of altitude sickness because at high altitudes, the lower air pressure causes the body to produce more red blood cells to carry oxygen, but the increased blood volume also dilutes these cells, making them less effective at delivering oxygen to the body's tissues.
  • Carbon dioxide build-up: At high altitudes, the air pressure is lower, which means there is less oxygen available for the body to breathe, and the body has to work harder to absorb the limited oxygen, while at the same time dealing with the excess carbon dioxide that it produces as a waste product, leading to a build-up of carbon dioxide in the body.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Altitude Sickness?

If you have altitude sickness, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can worsen your condition.

One herb to avoid is Ephedra (Ephedra sinica), as it can increase your heart rate and blood pressure, making altitude sickness symptoms worse. Another herb to be cautious with is Yohimbe (Pausinystalia johimbe), as it can also raise your blood pressure and cause dehydration, both of which can be problematic at high altitudes.

Additionally, you should limit or avoid Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera), as it can increase your heart rate and blood volume, putting extra strain on your body at high elevations. Lastly, avoid Ephedra-like herbs like Ma Huang (Ephedra sinica) and other Ephedra-containing herbs as they have the same properties.

It's also a good idea to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbs, especially if you have altitude sickness.

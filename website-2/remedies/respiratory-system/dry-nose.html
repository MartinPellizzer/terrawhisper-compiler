What Are The Main Causes Of Dry Nose?

The main causes of a dry nose are nasal polyps, dehydration, and low humidity.

Nasal polyps are growths inside the nasal passages that can block the flow of mucus and air, making the nose feel dry and uncomfortable. These growths are usually caused by chronic inflammation or allergies.

Dehydration occurs when the body doesn't have enough fluids, causing the mucous membranes in the nose to dry out and become irritated. This can be due to not drinking enough water, sweating a lot, or having a medical condition that affects the body's ability to regulate fluids.

Low humidity is another common cause of a dry nose, as dry air can quickly dry out the mucous membranes in the nose, making it feel dry and itchy.

Below are the main causes of dry nose.

  • Nasal Polyps: Nasal polyps are growths in the nasal passages that can block the airways and reduce the production of mucus, leading to a dry and congested nose.
  • Dehydration: Dehydration causes dry nose because the body loses too much water and the mucous membranes in the nose do not have enough moisture to produce enough mucus, making the nose feel dry and itchy.
  • Low Humidity: Low humidity causes dry nose because there is not enough moisture in the air to keep the mucous membranes inside the nose from drying out.
  • Dry Air: Dry air is a cause of dry nose because it takes away the moisture from the nostrils, making them feel dry and itchy.
  • Hypothyroidism: Hypothyroidism can cause a dry nose because the body doesn't produce enough thyroid hormones, which helps regulate the moisture levels in the body's tissues, including the nasal passages.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Dry Nose?

If you have a dry nose, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can dry out your nasal passages further.

Herbs like Thymus vulgaris (Thyme), Salvia officinalis (Sage), and Hyssopus officinalis (Hyssop) are known to have drying properties. Thyme contains compounds that can reduce mucus production and dry out the nasal passages.

Sage has a similar effect, with its essential oils helping to dry out the nasal passages and sinuses. Hyssop also has drying properties, which can exacerbate a dry nose. Another herb to avoid is Mentha x piperita (Peppermint), although it can help relieve congestion, its menthol content can dry out the nasal passages.

Finally, Lavandula angustifolia (Lavender) can also be drying, with its oils helping to reduce inflammation but also drying out the nasal passages.

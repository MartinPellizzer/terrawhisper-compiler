What are the causes of runny nose?

When you have a runny nose, it's usually because of something in your environment or your body that's causing it.

Let's look at the main causes. First, allergies are a big one. Your body sees something like pollen or dust as a threat, and it tries to fight it by producing extra mucus to trap the allergen. This extra mucus runs down your nose, making it runny. Another common cause is a cold. When a virus enters your body, it attacks your nose and sinuses, causing them to swell and produce more mucus.

This excess mucus runs down your nose, giving you a runny nose. The flu is similar to a cold, but it's caused by a different virus and is usually much worse. It also makes your nose and sinuses swell and produce excess mucus, leading to a runny nose. Hay fever is a type of allergy that's triggered by pollen, usually from trees, grasses, or weeds. When the pollen count is high, your body reacts by producing more mucus in your nose, which runs down and causes a runny nose. Lastly, a sinus infection is an infection that occurs in your sinuses, usually because of a bacterial or viral infection. This infection causes your sinuses to swell and produce excess mucus, which runs down your nose and gives you a runny nose.

These are the main causes of a runny nose, and they're usually related to your body reacting to something in your environment or a viral or bacterial infection.

What herbs should you avoid if you have runny nose?

If you have a runny nose, it's best to avoid Hyptis suaveolens, also known as sweet lemongrass, because it can make congestion worse.

Foeniculum vulgare, or fennel, can also be a problem as it can increase mucus production, leading to a runnier nose. Mentha x piperita, peppermint, is another herb to be cautious with, as its strong scent can irritate your nostrils and make congestion worse.

Similarly, Lavandula angustifolia, or English lavender, can be too strong for a runny nose, as its fragrance can cause further irritation. Salvia officinalis, or sage, is another herb to be careful with, as it can dry out your nasal passages, which might not be what you need when you have a runny nose.

When you have a runny nose, it's best to stick to gentle, soothing herbs that won't make things worse.

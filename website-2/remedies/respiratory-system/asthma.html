Asthma: Causes, Symptoms, and Herbal Treatments

Asthma is a chronic disease that affects the airways in the lungs, making it hard to breathe.

It is a serious condition that needs to be managed properly to avoid complications. If left untreated, asthma can lead to respiratory failure, heart problems, and even death. When the airways are inflamed and constricted, it causes wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath. There are many treatments available, including herbal remedies that can help alleviate symptoms. Some herbs have been found to be effective in managing asthma due to their anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties. Eucalyptus globulus, also known as eucalyptus oil, is often used to relieve respiratory issues. Its decongestant properties help to reduce congestion and open up the airways. Thymus vulgaris, or thyme, has antimicrobial properties that can help combat infections that can trigger asthma attacks. Echinacea purpurea is an herb that is believed to have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the airways.

Herbal preparations can be used in various forms to manage asthma symptoms. Infusions are made by steeping herbs in hot water, and are often used to make teas that can be sipped throughout the day. Decoctions are made by boiling herbs in water, and are often used for their stronger flavor and aroma. Tinctures are concentrated liquid extracts that can be added to water or taken directly, and are often used for their potency and convenience. Herbs and herbal preparations can be used in conjunction with traditional treatments to help manage asthma symptoms. For example, drinking an infusion of Eucalyptus globulus tea can help relieve congestion and open up the airways. Taking a tincture of Thymus vulgaris can help combat infections that can trigger asthma attacks. Meanwhile, consuming decoctions of Echinacea purpurea can help reduce inflammation in the airways. It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using herbal remedies to manage asthma symptoms. They can help determine the best course of treatment and ensure that any herbal remedies are used safely and effectively.

By managing asthma symptoms properly, it is possible to reduce the risk of complications and live a healthy and active life.

What Are The Main Causes Of Asthma?

Asthma is a serious health condition that affects millions of people worldwide.

There are many causes of asthma, but the main ones are Allergies, Pollution, and Mold. Allergies are a major cause of asthma. When people with allergies breathe in things they're allergic to, such as pollen, dust, or pet dander, their airways become inflamed and narrow. This causes symptoms like wheezing, coughing, and difficulty breathing. Common allergens that trigger asthma include pollen from trees, grass, and weeds, as well as pet hair and dander. Pollution is another significant cause of asthma.

Outdoor air pollution comes from sources like vehicle exhaust, industrial emissions, and smoke from wildfires. Indoor air pollution comes from things like strong chemicals, tobacco smoke, and dust from construction or renovation. When people breathe in polluted air, it can trigger asthma symptoms and make existing asthma worse. Mold is a type of fungus that grows in damp environments. It can be found in homes, schools, and workplaces, particularly in areas with poor ventilation. Mold spores can become airborne and be inhaled, causing allergic reactions and triggering asthma symptoms.

People with asthma may be more sensitive to mold than others, and exposure to it can make their symptoms worse.

Below are the main causes of asthma.

  • Allergies: When an allergic reaction occurs in the body, it causes the airways to swell and produce extra mucus, which can trigger asthma attacks.
  • Pollution: Pollution, especially from vehicles and factories, makes the air we breathe thick with bad particles, which can cause our airways to swell and tighten, leading to asthma attacks.
  • Mold: Mold releases tiny particles into the air that can be breathed in, causing irritation and inflammation in the lungs and airways, leading to asthma symptoms such as wheezing and difficulty breathing.
  • Dust mites: Dust mites are a cause of asthma because their waste and body parts become airborne, triggering allergies and irritating airways, leading to breathing difficulties and asthma attacks in people who are sensitive to them.
  • Pet dander: Pet dander is a common asthma trigger because tiny pieces of dead skin from pets mix with other particles in the air, making it hard for people with asthma to breathe comfortably.
  • Cockroach allergen: Cockroach allergen is a cause of asthma because when the tiny particles of a dead cockroach break down into the air, people with asthma breathe them in and their immune system reacts by causing their airways to swell and narrow.
  • Pesticides: Pesticides can cause asthma because the chemicals in them can release tiny particles into the air that people breathe in, which can irritate the lungs and airways, leading to inflammation and asthma attacks.

One such herb is Sage (Salvia officinalis), which can cause airway constriction and make breathing more difficult. Another herb to avoid is Anise (Pimpinella anisum), as its essential oils can irritate the airways and exacerbate asthma symptoms.

Also, avoid using Wintergreen (Gaultheria procumbens) or Sweet Birch (Betula lenta) as they contain methyl salicylate, a compound that can trigger asthma attacks. Additionally, avoid using Herbs like Meadowsweet (Filipendula ulmaria), which contains salicylates that can worsen asthma symptoms. Furthermore, avoid using Horseradish (Armoracia rusticana) as it can cause respiratory issues and make asthma symptoms worse.

To make Zingiber officinale, also known as ginger, herbal essential oil that helps with nasal congestion, you'll need a few basic items.

Start by harvesting fresh ginger roots, then wash and peel them thoroughly. Next, dry the peeled ginger in a warm place with good air circulation, such as a sunny windowsill or a food dehydrator, until it becomes brittle and crumbly.

Once the ginger is dry, you can grind it into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle. Then, add the ginger powder to a carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil, in a ratio of about 1 part ginger to 10 parts carrier oil, and mix well. Place the mixture in a clean glass jar and store it in a cool, dark place for several days to allow the oil to infuse.

After 7-10 days, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean jar, discarding the solids, and your ginger essential oil is ready to use.

What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have nasal congestion?

If you have nasal congestion, there are some herbal essential oils you should avoid.

One such oil is 'Sage' (Salvia officinalis). It can cause constriction in the airways, making it harder to breathe. This is because Sage contains compounds that slow down the movement of the cilia in the nasal passages, which are small hairs that help move mucus out of the body.

Another oil to avoid is 'Geranium' (Pelargonium graveolens). It has a compound called linalool, which can irritate the nasal passages and make congestion worse. Additionally, 'Bergamot' (Citrus aurantium bergamia) and 'Frankincense' (Boswellia serrata) contain compounds that can thin the blood and slow down healing.

While they may have benefits in small amounts, using them when you have nasal congestion could worsen the condition.

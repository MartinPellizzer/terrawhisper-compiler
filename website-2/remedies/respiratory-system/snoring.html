Snoring: Understanding Its Causes and Natural Remedies

Snoring is a common sleep disorder where a person makes a loud snoring sound while sleeping due to the obstruction of the airflow in the nasal passages or throat.

This can lead to sleep deprivation, fatigue, and decreased quality of life. If left untreated, snoring can also be a sign of a more serious condition called sleep apnea, which increases the risk of heart disease, stroke, and other health problems. Therefore, it's essential to address snoring issues promptly. Herbal remedies can be a natural and effective way to reduce snoring. Echinacea purpurea, also known as coneflower, has antimicrobial properties that can help fight off infections that can cause snoring. Herbal preparations such as decoction, tincture, and tea can be used to consume these herbs. However, it's essential to note that herbal remedies should not be used as a replacement for medical treatment, but rather as a complementary therapy to help alleviate snoring symptoms.

What Are The Main Causes Of Snoring?

Snoring is a common problem that affects many people.

There are several reasons why it happens. One main cause is sleep apnea, a condition where a person stops breathing for short periods during sleep. This is usually due to a blockage in the airway, which can be caused by being overweight, having a large neck, or other health issues. When the airway is blocked, the person's body tries to breathe, but the sound of the air moving is what we hear as snoring.

Another reason for snoring is nasal congestion. When the nasal passages are blocked, the person breathes through their mouth instead, which can cause the air to vibrate and make a snoring sound. This can be due to a cold, allergies, or a deviated septum in the nose. The size of the tongue is also a cause of snoring. If the tongue is large or falls back during sleep, it can block the airway and cause the person to snore.

This is more common in people who are overweight or have a large tongue.

Below are the main causes of snoring.

  • Sleep Apnea: Sleep Apnea causes snoring because when a person with this condition stops breathing for short periods during sleep, their airways partially close, causing vibrations in their throat that produce the snoring sound.
  • Nasal Congestion: When nasal congestion blocks the airway, the mouth and throat have to work harder to breathe, causing the soft tissues in the throat to vibrate more and produce the snoring sound.
  • Tongue Size: When the tongue falls back and blocks the airflow in the throat during sleep, it can cause the soft tissues to vibrate, resulting in the loud and unpleasant sound of snoring.
  • Throat Muscles: When the throat muscles relax too much during sleep, they can collapse and block the airflow through the throat, causing the soft tissues to vibrate and produce the snoring sound.
  • Adenoids: Adenoids, a group of small tissues at the back of the throat, can cause snoring when they become enlarged and obstruct the airway, allowing air to vibrate and create the snoring sound.
  • Hypothyroidism: Hypothyroidism is a cause of snoring because the reduced production of thyroid hormones can lead to a buildup of mucus in the throat, causing the airways to narrow and vibrate when breathing, resulting in the loud, raspy sound known as snoring.
  • Obesity: When a person is obese, the extra fat around their neck can cause their airway to narrow, making it harder for air to pass through, which can lead to snoring.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Snoring?

If you have snoring, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can make it worse.

One herb to avoid is Valerian root (Valeriana officinalis), which is known for helping people sleep. However, it can also make breathing passages more narrow, which can worsen snoring. Another herb to avoid is Hops (Humulus lupulus), which is often used to calm people and help them sleep.

Like Valerian root, Hops can relax the muscles in the throat, leading to more snoring. Additionally, you should also be cautious with Ginkgo biloba, which can increase blood flow and heart rate. This can cause nasal passages to swell, leading to more congestion and snoring.

Furthermore, the herb Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) can also worsen snoring as it can constrict blood vessels in the nose, leading to congestion.

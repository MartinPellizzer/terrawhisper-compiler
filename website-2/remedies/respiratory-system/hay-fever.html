What Are The Main Causes Of Hay Fever?

Hay fever is a common allergy that affects millions of people worldwide.

It's caused by the release of a chemical called histamine from the body's immune system. When people with hay fever breathe in tiny particles from certain plants, their immune system mistakenly thinks these particles are a threat and releases histamine. This leads to symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, and itchy eyes. There are several plants that trigger hay fever in different people. Three of the main culprits are Timothy Grass, Ragweed, and Mugwort. Timothy Grass is a type of grass that grows in many parts of the world. It's a common allergen because its pollen is very light and easily carried by the wind. When people breathe in Timothy Grass pollen, their immune system reacts and triggers symptoms.

Ragweed is another plant that causes hay fever. It's a weed that grows in fields, along roads, and in other areas. Its pollen is very potent and can travel long distances. This means that people can be allergic to Ragweed even if they don't live near the plant. Mugwort is a plant that's native to Europe and Asia. Its pollen is released in the fall, which is why some people experience hay fever symptoms during this time. Mugwort is also known to cause skin irritation in some people. All three of these plants release pollen that can trigger hay fever in people who are allergic to them.

The pollen from these plants is released into the air and can be carried by the wind, making it easy for people to breathe it in.

Below are the main causes of hay fever.

  • Timothy Grass: Timothy grass is a cause of hay fever because it releases tiny particles called pollen into the air when it blooms, and these particles can be breathed in by people, triggering an allergic reaction.
  • Ragweed: Ragweed is a cause of hay fever because it releases tiny particles called pollen into the air, which are easily inhaled and trigger the body's allergic reaction, causing symptoms like sneezing, congestion, and itchy eyes.
  • Mugwort: Mugwort's tiny pollen grains are released into the air during spring and summer, causing allergic reactions and symptoms in people who suffer from hay fever.

  • Juniper: Juniper is a cause of hay fever because its pollen contains proteins that trigger the release of histamine, a chemical in the body that makes people sneeze and feel unwell.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Hay Fever?

If you have hay fever, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can make your symptoms worse.

One of these herbs is Sage (Salvia officinalis), as its pollen can trigger allergic reactions. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) is another herb to be cautious with, as its flowers can cause skin irritation in people with allergies.

The herb Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) is also a potential allergen, as some people may react to its sap or leaves. Additionally, the herb Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) can cause skin irritation in sensitive individuals, and its pollen may exacerbate hay fever symptoms. Furthermore, the herb Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale) contains pollen that can trigger allergic reactions.

If you have hay fever, it's essential to be aware of these herbs and use them with caution, or consider alternatives like herbal teas or supplements that are made from pollen-free herbs.

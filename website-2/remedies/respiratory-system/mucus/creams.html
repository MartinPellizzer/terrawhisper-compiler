1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains boiactive constituents like eucalyptol, camphor, and cineole.

These compounds have decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce mucus in the throat. Eucalyptol, a primary constituent, acts as a bronchodilator, making it easier to breathe and clear mucus from the airways. Camphor helps to loosen and break down thick mucus, while cineole has antibacterial properties that prevent infection.

When used topically, these compounds work together to relieve congestion and soothe the throat, providing fast and effective relief from mucus buildup.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of eucalyptus oil, and 2 tablespoons of peppermint oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add eucalyptus oil and peppermint oil to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  4. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool until it reaches 120°F to 130°F. This is the ideal temperature for pouring into a mold.
  5. Pour the mixture into a mold or a container lined with parchment paper. Let it cool and harden before using.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the throat. The glycosides in Echinacea purpurea creams, particularly echinacoside and caftaric acid, have been shown to inhibit the production of mucus. The alkylamides, such as cis- and trans-11-alkylamides, have been found to have immunomodulatory effects that can help fight off infections that cause excessive mucus production.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off infections, Echinacea purpurea creams can help alleviate mucus in the throat.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of coconut oil in a clean glass bowl.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of boiling water until it's warm.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax to the coconut oil and stir until melted.
  4. Add the 2 cups of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the coconut oil mixture and let it steep for 2 hours.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean glass jar, discard the solids and let the cream cool before use.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin responsible for its medicinal properties.

Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory and demulcent properties, which help soothe and protect the mucous membranes in the throat. The anti-inflammatory properties reduce swelling and redness, while the demulcent properties form a protective barrier that keeps the throat moist and comfortable. Glycyrrhiza glabra creams also contain flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have antioxidant properties that help combat free radicals and promote a healthy environment in the throat.

The combination of these bioactive constituents helps to break down and clear out excess mucus in the throat, providing relief from coughs and congestion.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of glycerin, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 1 teaspoon of glycyrrhiza glabra powder, and 1 teaspoon of honey.
  2. Melt beeswax and glycerin in a double boiler or in a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix glycyrrhiza glabra powder and honey until well combined.
  4. Slowly add the glycyrrhiza glabra mixture to the melted beeswax and glycerin, stirring constantly to avoid lumps.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let cool. Store in the refrigerator to use as a throat cream for mucus.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents like gingerols and shogaols.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help to reduce swelling and fight off infections in the throat. The warming and drying properties of gingerols and shogaols also help to break down and clear excess mucus from the throat, making it easier to breathe and swallow. Additionally, the expectorant properties of Zingiber officinale help to thin and loosen mucus, making it easier to expel from the body.

By reducing inflammation, fighting off infections, and loosening mucus, Zingiber officinale creams can provide relief from a sore and congested throat.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of distilled water, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 teaspoons of grated ginger, and 10 drops of tea tree oil.
  2. Melt the beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until fully melted.
  3. Add the distilled water to the melted mixture and stir well. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for 10-15 minutes, or until it reaches 160°F to 180°F.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Stir in the grated ginger and tea tree oil. Pour the mixture into a clean glass jar.
  5. Let the mixture cool and harden completely before use. Store the Zingiber officinale cream in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

By reducing inflammation and loosening mucus, Thymus vulgaris creams can help alleviate congestion and discomfort associated with mucus buildup in the throat.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Thymus vulgaris (dried thyme) leaves, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 10 drops of tea tree oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.

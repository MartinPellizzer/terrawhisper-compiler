7 Natural Tinctures for Pregnant Women with Pertussis Symptoms

Pregnant women are more likely to get pertussis, also known as whooping cough, because their immune system is weaker due to the pregnancy.

When they catch pertussis, it can be very serious and even life-threatening for both them and their unborn baby. The symptoms can include violent coughing fits, vomiting, and difficulty breathing, which can be exhausting and scary. For pregnant women, pertussis can lead to premature labor, low birth weight, and even infant death. It's not just the physical symptoms that can be a problem - the stress and worry of having pertussis while pregnant can also affect their mental health and relationships.

Some pregnant women may have to spend time in the hospital, away from their families and loved ones, to get treatment and rest. As a result, many pregnant women are looking for safe and effective ways to prevent and treat pertussis. While conventional medicine is often the first line of treatment, some women are turning to herbal tinctures as a natural alternative. These tinctures are made from plants and flowers that have been used for centuries to soothe and calm the body. They work by supporting the immune system, reducing inflammation, and promoting relaxation.

By using herbal tinctures, pregnant women can try to prevent pertussis and reduce its severity if they do get infected.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures are sometimes used to help with pertussis, a common and contagious respiratory illness, also known as whooping cough.

This is because the tincture contains properties like alkylamides and glycosides, which have anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting effects. The constituents, including isobutyl amide and echinacoside, may help reduce the severity and duration of the illness by soothing the airways and increasing the body's natural defense against the bacteria that cause pertussis. If you're considering using Echinacea purpurea tinctures for pertussis, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before taking any action.

They may recommend a specific dosage, usually 20-30 drops of the tincture in water or tea, taken 2-3 times a day. It's also crucial to note that tinctures should not be given to children under 12 years old without consulting a doctor first. Taking Echinacea purpurea tinctures may lead to improved symptoms, such as reduced coughing and fever, and a shorter duration of the illness. However, it's essential to be aware that the effectiveness of the tincture may vary from person to person, and it's not a replacement for conventional medical treatment.

It's also possible to experience side effects like allergic reactions or stomach upset, so monitor your body's response and adjust the dosage or discontinue use if necessary.

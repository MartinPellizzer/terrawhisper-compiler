7 Herbal Tinctures For Pertussis

When it comes to pertussis, also known as whooping cough, herbal tinctures can provide much-needed relief.

Ginger, specifically Zingiber officinale, can also be beneficial, as its anti-inflammatory properties can help reduce inflammation in the lungs and airways, making it easier to breathe. These herbs can be combined to create a powerful blend that can help alleviate pertussis symptoms. To use them, simply mix the tinctures with water or another liquid and take as directed. Drinking herbal teas made with these herbs can also be a soothing way to help calm a cough and ease respiratory issues.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Alkylamides have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce the severity of pertussis symptoms, such as a persistent cough. Glycosides in Echinacea purpurea tincture have immune-modulating effects, which can help boost the body's natural defenses against the Bordetella pertussis bacteria. Phenolic acids, specifically rosmarinic acid, have antimicrobial properties that can help combat the bacterial infection causing pertussis.

By reducing inflammation and boosting the immune system, Echinacea purpurea tinctures may help alleviate the symptoms of pertussis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and roots. Use a clean knife to chop them into small pieces.
  2. Use a coffee grinder or spice grinder to finely grind the chopped Echinacea. This will help release the active ingredients.
  3. Transfer the ground Echinacea into a clean glass jar. Use 1 cup of 80-100 proof vodka or 35% ABV as the solvent. Pour the vodka over the Echinacea, making sure everything is covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar daily to help the solvent extract the active ingredients.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture 3-4 times a day, or as directed by a healthcare professional for pertussis treatment.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contains cineole, a key bioactive constituent responsible for its therapeutic effects.

Cineole has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help reduce inflammation and fight off infections in the respiratory tract. Eucalyptus globulus tinctures also contain other compounds like α-pinene and limonene, which have bronchodilatory effects, making it easier to breathe and clear mucus from the airways. The expectorant properties of eucalyptus globulus tinctures can help thin and clear mucus, making it easier to cough up and relieve congestion.

By reducing inflammation and clearing mucus, eucalyptus globulus tinctures may help alleviate symptoms of pertussis, such as coughing and difficulty breathing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves, 2 cups of 80-proof vodka or glycerin, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the dried leaves and vodka or glycerin in the glass jar. Make sure the leaves are completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the tincture and store it in a cool, dark place. The recommended dosage for pertussis is 10-20 drops, 3-4 times a day.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains active constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

These properties can help relieve the severe coughing fits associated with pertussis, a highly contagious respiratory infection. The antispasmodic effect can also help relax the airways and reduce the frequency of coughing, making it easier for patients to breathe. The anti-inflammatory properties can help reduce the inflammation and swelling in the lungs and airways, which can become congested during a pertussis infection.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing airways, Zingiber officinale tinctures can help alleviate symptoms of pertussis and provide relief to patients.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (Ginger) roots. Wash and chop into small pieces.
  2. Combine the chopped Ginger with 2 cups of 80% ethanol in a clean glass jar. Make sure the Ginger is completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the ethanol to extract the active compounds from the Ginger.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Measure the tincture's potency by diluting 10 drops with 1 cup of water. If the solution turns a brownish color and has a strong Ginger smell, it's ready for use. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle in a cool place.

4. Echinacea angustifolia

Echinacea angustifolia tinctures contains alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which are beneficial compounds for fighting pertussis, a severe coughing illness.

Alkylamides have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and ease congestion in the lungs and airways. Glycosides, particularly echinacoside, have antiviral properties that target and destroy the Bordetella pertussis bacteria, which causes pertussis. Phenolic acids, such as chicoric acid, have antioxidant properties that help protect the body from oxidative stress caused by the infection.

By addressing the underlying causes of pertussis, Echinacea angustifolia tinctures can help alleviate symptoms and support the body's natural recovery process.

  2. Combine the dried roots and vodka in a clean glass jar. Make sure the roots are completely covered by the vodka.

  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the tincture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 20-30 drops, 3-4 times a day, as needed for pertussis symptoms.

5. Astragalus membranaceus

These properties help to boost the immune system, which is crucial in fighting off pertussis, a highly contagious respiratory disease caused by the bacterium Bordetella pertussis. The saponins in Astragalus membranaceus tinctures have been shown to have antimicrobial activity, which can help to reduce the severity and duration of pertussis symptoms. Additionally, the flavonoids in the tincture have antioxidant properties, which can help to protect the body from oxidative stress caused by the infection.

By enhancing the immune response and reducing inflammation, Astragalus membranaceus tinctures may help to alleviate some of the symptoms associated with pertussis.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent pertussis from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help boost your immune system, but it's unlikely to prevent pertussis from forming.

Pertussis is a bacterial infection caused by Bordetella pertussis, and herbal tea doesn't directly fight this bacterium.

However, a healthy immune system can fight off the infection more effectively.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for pertussis every day?

Consuming herbal teas for pertussis every day may not be entirely safe.

Some teas, like those with thyme or eucalyptus, might help soothe symptoms, but others could interact with medications or worsen underlying conditions.

It's essential to research the specific tea and its potential effects on the body.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in pertussis?

Herbal teas for pertussis, such as thyme and eucalyptus, may help relieve symptoms like coughing and congestion.

Their effects can start within a few days to a week, but it's essential to be consistent with consumption.

As the body absorbs the active ingredients, users may notice a reduction in coughing frequency and severity over time.

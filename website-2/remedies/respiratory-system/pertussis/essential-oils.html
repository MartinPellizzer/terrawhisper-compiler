7 Potent Herbal Essential Oils to Help Pregnant Women with Pertussis

Pregnant women can get pertussis, also known as whooping cough, which is a serious respiratory illness.

This is because they often haven't been vaccinated against it, or the vaccination they received may not be effective. Pertussis can cause severe coughing fits, which can lead to difficulty breathing, vomiting, and even pneumonia. This can be life-threatening for both the mother and the baby. If a pregnant woman gets pertussis, she may experience symptoms like a persistent cough, fever, and fatigue. This can make daily activities, like caring for herself and her baby, very challenging. She may even need to spend time in the hospital to receive treatment and recover.

When a pregnant woman gets pertussis, she may be at risk of passing the infection to her baby during childbirth or in the weeks following birth. This can lead to complications for the baby, including respiratory issues and even death. So, it's essential for pregnant women to take steps to prevent and treat pertussis. Some herbal essential oils have been studied for their potential benefits in treating pertussis in pregnant women. These oils may help to reduce the severity and duration of symptoms, such as coughing and fever. They may also have antimicrobial properties, which can help to combat the infection.

Additionally, some of these oils may be able to help ease stress and anxiety, which can be beneficial for pregnant women who are struggling with the physical and emotional challenges of pertussis.

You can also inhale the oil through steam inhalation or add it to a humidifier to help ease congestion. Additionally, you can take eucalyptus globulus essential oils internally, but only after consulting with a healthcare professional and following their recommended dosage. By taking these steps, you may experience a reduction in coughing and congestion, making it easier to breathe and manage symptoms. However, it's essential to note that Eucalyptus globulus essential oils should not replace medical treatment for pertussis.

If you suspect you or a family member has pertussis, consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

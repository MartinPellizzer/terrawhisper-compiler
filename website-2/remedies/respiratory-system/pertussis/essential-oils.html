1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and alpha-terpineol, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help combat the Bordetella pertussis bacteria that cause pertussis. The cineole in eucalyptus oil has been shown to inhibit the growth of B. pertussis, reducing the severity of the infection. Additionally, eucalyptus oil's expectorant properties help loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to breathe.

By reducing inflammation and inhibiting bacterial growth, eucalyptus globulus essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of pertussis.

3. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains limonene, menthone, and menthol as its primary bioactive constituents.

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have pertussis?

If you have pertussis, also known as whooping cough, it's essential to be careful when using herbal essential oils.

Some oils may interact with your medications or make your condition worse. Oils that can increase your risk of bleeding should be avoided, such as Salvia officinalis (sage) and Hyssopus officinalis (hyssop), as pertussis can cause bleeding from the lungs and nose.

Additionally, oils with strong anti-inflammatory properties, like Boswellia serrata (frankincense) and Commiphora molmol (myrrh), may thin your blood and interfere with your body's natural healing process. Furthermore, oils with potential diuretic effects, like Juniperus communis (juniper) and Cistus ladanifer (rockrose), may decrease your blood pressure and worsen dehydration, which is already a concern with pertussis.

Lastly, oils with potential respiratory-stimulating properties, like Cinnamomum verum (cinnamon) and Ocimum basilicum (basil), may irritate your lungs and make your symptoms worse.

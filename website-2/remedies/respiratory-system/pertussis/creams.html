1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains cineole, a key bioactive constituent that helps relieve respiratory issues like pertussis.

Cineole has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and ease congestion in the airways. Eucalyptus globulus also contains camphor, which has expectorant properties that help loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, making it easier to breathe. The antispasmodic properties of cineole and camphor in Eucalyptus globulus creams can help relax the muscles in the airways, reducing coughing fits associated with pertussis.

By reducing inflammation and easing congestion, Eucalyptus globulus creams can help alleviate symptoms of pertussis and promote recovery.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of eucalyptus essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool until it reaches 120°F to 130°F.
  4. Add vitamin E oil and eucalyptus essential oil to the mixture and stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify completely before use. Label and store the creams in a cool, dry place.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains the bioactive constituents alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract, a common symptom of pertussis. The alkaloid isobutylamine in Echinacea purpurea has been shown to have antimicrobial properties, which can help to combat the bacteria that cause pertussis. The phenolic acids in Echinacea purpurea creams also have antioxidant properties, which can help to protect the body from oxidative stress caused by the infection.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off the infection, Echinacea purpurea creams may help to alleviate symptoms of pertussis and support the body's natural recovery process.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of lavender oil.
  2. Combine dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and coconut oil in a double boiler. Heat the mixture for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. In a separate bowl, melt the beeswax in a double boiler. Add the vitamin E oil and lavender oil. Stir until well combined.
  5. Mix the infused oil mixture with the melted beeswax mixture. Pour the mixture into a cream dispenser or a small tin. Let it cool and set before use.

Linalyl acetate's antispasmodic properties can help relax airway muscles, making breathing easier.

By targeting the bacteria and reducing inflammation, Lavandula angustifolia creams may help alleviate pertussis symptoms.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the airways and alleviate symptoms of pertussis. The antispasmodic properties of gingerols and shogaols can also help relax the airway muscles, making it easier to breathe. Additionally, the antimicrobial properties of Zingiber officinale creams may help combat bacterial infections that can contribute to pertussis.

By reducing inflammation, relaxing airway muscles, and combating infections, Zingiber officinale creams may help alleviate symptoms of pertussis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of Zingiber officinale root powder.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of Zingiber officinale root powder and 1 tablespoon of distilled water to the melted mixture. Stir until combined.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool until the mixture thickens. Stir occasionally to prevent separation.
  5. Once thickened, transfer the mixture to a clean container and store in the refrigerator. Apply a thin layer to affected areas as needed.

5. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra creams contains saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce the severity of pertussis symptoms such as coughing and congestion. The flavonoids and phenolic acids in Sambucus nigra creams also have antimicrobial properties, which help to combat the Bordetella pertussis bacteria that causes the infection. The saponins in the cream can help to thin mucus, making it easier to cough up and relieving congestion.

By reducing inflammation and fighting the bacteria that causes pertussis, Sambucus nigra creams may provide relief for those suffering from the illness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Sambucus nigra flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of lavender oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the dried Sambucus nigra flowers to the melted mixture and let it steep for 2-3 hours. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  4. Add the vitamin E oil and lavender oil to the strained mixture and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and harden. Store the Sambucus nigra creams in a cool, dry place.

6. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains carnosic acid, ursolic acid, and rosmarinic acid, which are the main bioactive constituents responsible for its benefits.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help reduce the severity of pertussis symptoms. Rosmariinic acid, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria, including Bordetella pertussis, the causative agent of pertussis. The anti-inflammatory properties of carnosic acid and ursolic acid can also help alleviate coughing and inflammation in the respiratory tract.

By reducing bacterial growth and inflammation, Rosmarinus officinalis creams may help alleviate some of the symptoms associated with pertussis.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, 2 teaspoons of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.
  2. Melt the beeswax and coconut oil in a double boiler or in the microwave in 10-second increments, stirring between each heating.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the distilled water and Rosmarinus officinalis leaves. Let it sit for 10 minutes to allow the herbs to infuse.
  4. Strain the water mixture and discard the herbs. Add the shea butter and vitamin E oil to the water mixture and stir until combined.
  5. Combine the melted wax and oil mixture with the water mixture and stir until smooth. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and harden.

7. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains the bioactive constituents limonene, linalool, and menthone.

These constituents have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that can help to soothe and calm the respiratory tract. The menthone in Mentha x piperita creams has a decongestant effect, which can help to relieve congestion and open up airways. The limonene and linalool in the cream have antispasmodic properties, which can help to relax and calm the muscles in the respiratory tract, making it easier to breathe.

By reducing inflammation and congestion, Mentha x piperita creams can help to alleviate symptoms of pertussis, such as coughing and difficulty breathing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of glycerin, and 2 tablespoons of mentha x piperita essential oil.
  2. Combine the distilled water and glycerin in a saucepan and heat over low heat, stirring until the glycerin dissolves.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool for a few minutes.
  4. Add the mentha x piperita essential oil to the cooled mixture and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let it cool completely before using as a cream for pertussis.

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains glycyrrhetic acid, a triterpenoid saponin that has anti-inflammatory properties.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent pertussis from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent pertussis, also known as whooping cough.

Some herbal teas, like echinacea and yarrow, have antimicrobial properties that can boost the immune system. However, there's limited research on the effectiveness of these teas in preventing pertussis.

More studies are needed to confirm their benefits.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for pertussis every day?

Herbal teas can't replace medical treatment for pertussis.

If you're taking them every day, be aware that some herbs might interact with medications or worsen symptoms. Also, the effectiveness of herbal teas in treating pertussis hasn't been scientifically proven.

It's essential to be cautious and monitor your body's response.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in pertussis?

Herbal teas may help alleviate pertussis symptoms, but it's essential to note that they are not a cure.

Some herbal teas, like thyme and eucalyptus, may start showing results within 24 to 48 hours. This can be seen in reduced coughing and fever.

However, it may take longer to notice significant improvements in overall health.

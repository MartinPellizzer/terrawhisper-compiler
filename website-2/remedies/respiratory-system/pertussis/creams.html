Pertussis in Infants: 11 Herbal Creams for Relief

Infants are very young and their bodies are still growing.

They have a weak immune system, which makes them more likely to catch illnesses like pertussis, also known as whooping cough. When an infant gets pertussis, it can be very serious. They might start coughing a lot, and this can make it hard for them to breathe and eat. They might also get very tired and have trouble sleeping. In severe cases, infants with pertussis can even stop breathing, which is a life-threatening situation. This can be very scary for parents and caregivers, who want to help their little ones feel better. Infants with pertussis often need to go to the hospital, where they might get oxygen therapy or other treatments to help them breathe. They might also need antibiotics to help fight off the bacteria that's causing the illness.

But even with treatment, infants with pertussis can still have a tough time. They might get very irritable, and have trouble feeding or sleeping. This can be a stressful time for parents, who want to do everything they can to help their baby feel better. One thing that some parents are turning to is herbal creams. These creams are made from natural ingredients that can help soothe and calm the skin. When an infant has pertussis, the skin can get very dry and irritated from all the coughing. The herbal creams can help moisturize and calm the skin, which can make the infant feel more comfortable. They can also help reduce inflammation and promote healing.

Some parents are finding that these creams can be a helpful addition to traditional treatments, and can help their baby feel more relaxed and comfortable while they recover from pertussis.

Echinacea purpurea creams have been traditionally used to help alleviate symptoms of pertussis, also known as whooping cough.

These constituents can help reduce the severity of coughing fits and ease congestion. To use Echinacea purpurea creams effectively, apply a small amount to the affected areas, such as the chest, throat, and nose, 2-3 times a day.

You can also mix a few drops of the cream with a carrier oil, like coconut or olive oil, and apply it to the skin after a warm bath to help the cream absorb better. It's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea creams are not a substitute for medical treatment. If you or a family member is diagnosed with pertussis, consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment. When using Echinacea purpurea creams, you may notice a reduction in the frequency and severity of coughing fits within a few days.

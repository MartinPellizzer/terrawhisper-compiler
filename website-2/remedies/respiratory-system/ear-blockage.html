What Are The Main Causes Of Ear Blockage?

Ear blockage can be caused by several factors.

Eustachian tube dysfunction is one of them. The Eustachian tube is a small tube that connects the middle ear to the back of the throat. It helps to equalize air pressure in the ear. If the tube doesn't work properly, it can cause a blockage. This can be due to a cold, allergies, or a deviated septum in the nose. Earwax buildup is another cause of ear blockage.

Earwax is a natural substance produced by the ear to protect it from dirt and bacteria. However, if too much earwax builds up, it can block the ear canal. This can be caused by using Q-tips to clean the ear, which can push the earwax further into the ear. A middle ear infection is a common cause of ear blockage. Middle ear infections occur when bacteria or viruses infect the middle ear. This can cause fluid to build up in the ear, leading to blockage.

Middle ear infections are often caused by a cold or allergies, and can be treated with antibiotics or other medications.

Below are the main causes of ear blockage.

  • Eustachian tube dysfunction: Eustachian tube dysfunction is a cause of ear blockage because the tube, which connects the middle ear to the back of the throat, fails to open and close properly, preventing air from entering the ear and causing a feeling of fullness or blockage.
  • Earwax buildup: Earwax buildup is a cause of ear blockage because the excess wax can block the ear canal, preventing sound from reaching the eardrum and causing a feeling of fullness or blockage in the ear.
  • Middle ear infection: Middle ear infection causes fluid buildup in the ear, which blocks sound from reaching the eardrum and causes a feeling of ear blockage.
  • Foreign body: A foreign body in the ear can block it because it blocks the ear canal, stopping sound from entering the ear and causing discomfort.
  • Ear infection: Ear infection can cause ear blockage because the infection creates extra fluid and swelling in the ear, which blocks the Eustachian tube and makes it hard for air to enter and exit the ear.
  • Swimmer's ear: Swimmer's ear, also known as otitis externa, is a cause of ear blockage because bacteria or viruses infect the ear canal, causing redness, swelling, and fluid buildup, which can block the ear canal and make it harder for sound to enter.
  • Cerumen impaction: Cerumen impaction causes ear blockage because the buildup of earwax in the ear canal can become stuck and block the ear's natural opening, preventing sound from entering the ear.

Another herb to avoid is licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra), which can cause an allergic reaction and make the ear blockage worse.

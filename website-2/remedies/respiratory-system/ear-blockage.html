Ear Blockage: Exploring Medicinal Herbs and Natural Remedies

What are the causes of ear blockage?

Ear blockage can be caused by several issues that affect the middle ear or the ear canal.

One common cause is Eustachian tube dysfunction. This happens when the tube, which connects the middle ear to the back of the throat, doesn't open and close properly. It can get blocked due to cold, allergies, or a sinus infection. When the tube is blocked, air can't enter the middle ear, causing pressure and discomfort. Middle ear fluid is another cause. This is when fluid builds up in the middle ear, usually as a result of an ear infection. The fluid can block the ear and cause hearing problems. This often happens in children, especially during colds or flu.

Earwax buildup is also a common cause of ear blockage. Normally, earwax helps protect the ear canal by trapping dirt and other small particles. However, when too much wax accumulates, it can block the ear canal and cause discomfort or hearing problems. Tympanic membrane perforation is a more serious cause of ear blockage. This occurs when the eardrum, which separates the middle ear from the ear canal, tears or ruptures. This can cause air to leak into the middle ear, leading to blockage and other complications. Finally, ear infections, such as otitis media, can cause ear blockage. When the middle ear becomes infected, fluid can build up and block the ear.

This can lead to discomfort, hearing problems, and other complications.

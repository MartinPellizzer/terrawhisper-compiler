7 Herbal Creams For Hay Fever

Outdoor workers spend most of their time in the open, exposed to pollen from trees, grasses, and flowers.

This makes them more likely to develop hay fever, a condition that causes itchy eyes, sneezing, and a runny nose. For those who work outdoors, hay fever can be a real problem. It can make it hard to do their job, and even affect their mood and sleep. Imagine a gardener who can't work because their eyes are itchy and they're constantly sneezing, or a construction worker who's too tired to get the job done because of constant nasal congestion. It's not just a minor annoyance - hay fever can affect a person's entire life.

When hay fever strikes, many outdoor workers turn to medication to find relief. However, these can have side effects and may not work for everyone. That's where herbal creams come in. These creams are made from natural ingredients that have been shown to help soothe hay fever symptoms. They can be applied directly to the skin, providing quick relief from itchy eyes and a runny nose.

By using herbal creams, outdoor workers can find a more natural way to manage their hay fever symptoms and get back to work.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains a high concentration of eucalyptol, a natural compound that helps to ease hay fever symptoms.

When applied topically, it works by reducing inflammation and congestion in the nasal passages and sinuses, allowing for easier breathing and relief from sneezing and runny nose. The cream's antihistamine and anti-inflammatory properties also help to calm the immune system's overactive response to allergens, such as pollen.

To use eucalyptus globulus cream for hay fever, apply a small amount to the nostrils or chest area before going outside, and reapply as needed. You can also add a few drops of eucalyptus oil to your bathwater or inhale it through steam inhalation for added relief. By taking these steps, you may experience a reduction in hay fever symptoms, such as sneezing, congestion, and itchy eyes, and be able to enjoy the outdoors without discomfort.

However, it's essential to note that eucalyptus globulus cream may not eliminate hay fever symptoms completely, and you may still need to use other treatments, such as medication or avoidance of allergens, to manage your symptoms effectively.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active compounds like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and polysaccharides that have anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties.

These help to reduce the production of histamine, a chemical released during an allergic reaction, which is often the culprit behind hay fever symptoms. When you apply the cream to the affected area, the active compounds work to calm the skin and reduce inflammation. To use Echinacea purpurea cream for hay fever, apply a small amount to the affected skin area, such as your face, neck, or arms, 1-2 hours before going outside or being exposed to allergens.

You can also use it as a preventative measure by applying it daily, especially during peak pollen seasons. When using Echinacea purpurea cream for hay fever, you may notice a reduction in symptoms like itching, redness, and swelling. The cream may also help to reduce the frequency and severity of allergic reactions.

As the skin becomes less inflamed, you may find that your symptoms become more manageable, and you're able to go about your daily activities without discomfort.

3. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help alleviate hay fever symptoms.

These properties are due to the presence of triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which have been shown to reduce swelling and calm irritated skin. When applied topically, the cream can help soothe itchy and watery eyes, as well as ease congestion and sinus pressure.

To use Calendula officinalis cream for hay fever, apply a thin layer to the affected areas, such as the eyes, nose, and skin, several times a day. You can also mix a few drops of the cream with a carrier oil, like coconut or olive oil, and use it as a nasal spray or eye drop. By doing so, you may experience relief from hay fever symptoms, such as reduced redness and swelling, and improved breathing and vision.

Additionally, the cream's antimicrobial properties may help prevent secondary infections and promote healing.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known to have anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties.

These properties make it an effective remedy for relieving hay fever symptoms. When you apply the cream to your skin, the active ingredients are absorbed and can help reduce the release of histamine, a chemical that causes allergic reactions. This can lead to a decrease in symptoms like itching, sneezing, and runny nose. To use Lavandula angustifolia creams for hay fever, apply a small amount to your skin, ideally on the affected areas, 1-2 times a day. You can also mix a few drops of the cream with a carrier oil and inhale it through your nose to help ease congestion.

If you're using it topically, make sure to do a patch test first to check for any skin irritation. When you start using the cream, you may notice a reduction in your hay fever symptoms within a few hours. Your itching and sneezing may decrease, and your runny nose may clear up. Some people may also experience a sense of calm and relaxation due to the calming effects of the lavender oil. However, it's essential to note that the cream may not completely eliminate your symptoms, especially during peak pollen seasons.

You may still need to take other medications or use other remedies to manage your symptoms.

5. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are powerful compounds that help alleviate hay fever symptoms.

The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, are known for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can reduce congestion and swelling in the nasal passages. The terpenoids, such as bilobalide and ginkgolides, have a stabilizing effect on mast cells, which are responsible for releasing histamine, a key player in triggering allergic reactions. By applying Ginkgo biloba creams topically, you can help reduce the amount of histamine released, leading to less itching, sneezing, and congestion. To use Ginkgo biloba creams for hay fever, apply a thin layer to your skin, ideally on your wrists or behind your ears. You can also mix a few drops of the cream with your moisturizer or sunscreen for added relief.

When you start to feel the symptoms of hay fever, such as runny nose or itchy eyes, apply the cream and wait for 10-15 minutes to see the effects. If you experience any skin irritation or allergic reactions, discontinue use and consult your doctor. As you use Ginkgo biloba creams, you may notice a reduction in your hay fever symptoms, including congestion, sneezing, and itchy eyes. Your nasal passages may feel clearer, and you may experience less discomfort and swelling. However, it's essential to note that Ginkgo biloba creams are not a substitute for medical treatment.

If your symptoms persist or worsen, consult your doctor for further guidance and treatment.

When you start using Euphorbia peplus creams, you may notice a reduction in itching and swelling within 15-30 minutes. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent hay fever from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help ease hay fever symptoms, but it's unlikely to prevent them from forming.

Some herbal teas, like nettle tea, contain compounds that reduce inflammation and congestion, which can provide temporary relief.

However, these teas may not stop the allergic reaction that causes hay fever in the first place.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for hay fever every day?

Herbal teas for hay fever can be a great relief, but it's essential to be cautious.

If you consume them daily, they might interact with other medications or worsen underlying conditions. Some herbs, like chamomile, can cause drowsiness, which isn't ideal for everyday use.

Moderation is key, so try to limit your intake to when symptoms are severe.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in hay fever?

Herbal teas like nettle and quercetin can help with hay fever symptoms.

It may take a few days to a week for results to be noticeable. As the body starts to absorb the herbal properties, you might feel your symptoms easing, such as reduced congestion and itchiness.

This can vary from person to person.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains boiactive compounds like eucalyptol, which has natural decongestant properties that help relieve nasal congestion.

The antihistamine properties in eucalyptol reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of hay fever, such as itching and runny eyes. Eucalyptol also has antimicrobial properties that prevent bacterial growth, reducing the risk of infections like sinusitis. The anti-inflammatory properties of eucalyptus globulus creams, particularly due to the presence of flavonoids and phenolic acids, help to reduce swelling and ease breathing.

The combination of these properties makes eucalyptus globulus creams a popular remedy for hay fever relief.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of eucalyptus essential oil, and a small glass jar.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and stir in the eucalyptus essential oil. Pour the mixture into the glass jar.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify. Once it's solid, your eucalyptus cream is ready to use. Apply it to your skin as needed to help relieve hay fever symptoms.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains the bioactive constituents alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate the symptoms of hay fever. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea creams have been shown to reduce inflammation in the nasal passages, while the caffeic acid and rosmarinic acid work to neutralize free radicals that can exacerbate allergy symptoms. By reducing inflammation and neutralizing free radicals, Echinacea purpurea creams can help to alleviate congestion, sneezing, and itching associated with hay fever.

The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Echinacea purpurea creams can provide relief and support for people suffering from hay fever.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of lavender oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the melted oil and beeswax mixture. Let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Mix in the vitamin E oil and lavender oil. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify.

3. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are powerful antioxidants that help reduce inflammation and combat the root causes of hay fever.

The triterpenoids, particularly calendulic acid, have anti-inflammatory properties that soothe and calm the skin, while the flavonoids, including quercetin and kaempferol, are known for their antihistamine effects. The carotenoids, including beta-carotene, help protect the skin from oxidative stress and damage caused by free radicals. When applied topically, these compounds can help alleviate the symptoms of hay fever, such as itchy and watery eyes, by reducing the body's allergic response.

By using Calendula officinalis creams, people with hay fever may experience relief from their symptoms and find a natural solution to manage their condition.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula officinalis flowers, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of olive oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix shea butter and olive oil until well combined. Add dried Calendula officinalis flowers and let it infuse for 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture and discard the flowers. Add the infused mixture to the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture, and stir until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and harden. Once set, use as a cream for hay fever relief by applying to affected areas as needed.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are its main active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties that can help alleviate symptoms of hay fever. Linalool, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the release of histamine, a chemical that triggers allergic reactions. By reducing the body's histamine levels, Lavandula angustifolia creams can help to alleviate symptoms such as congestion, sneezing, and itchy eyes.

Additionally, the calming properties of the cream can also help to reduce stress and anxiety associated with hay fever.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of lavender essential oil, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or in a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add shea butter and stir until melted and well combined.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in lavender essential oil and vitamin E oil. Let cool slightly.
  5. Pour into a container and let cool completely. Once set, use as a cream to help soothe hay fever symptoms.

5. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are active constituents that help with hay fever symptoms.

Flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and congestion in the nasal passages. Terpenoids, like bilobalide, have antioxidant properties that neutralize free radicals and prevent damage to cells. These active constituents in Ginkgo biloba creams help open up airways, reduce congestion, and ease nasal discharge, making it easier to breathe during hay fever season.

The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Ginkgo biloba creams help alleviate the symptoms of hay fever, providing relief and comfort to those who suffer from it.

  1. Melt 2 cups of coconut oil in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl.
  2. Add 1/4 cup of beeswax and 1/4 cup of shea butter to the melted coconut oil.
  3. Mix in 2 tablespoons of Ginkgo biloba extract and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  4. Add 2 cups of distilled water and mix until the beeswax is fully dissolved.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify before use.

6. Euphorbia peplus

Euphorbia peplus creams contains a compound called ingenol mebutate, which is the active constituent responsible for its medicinal properties.

This compound has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling and itching associated with hay fever. The cream also contains a compound called phorbol esters, which has antihistamine properties that help block the release of histamine, a chemical that causes allergic reactions. The anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties of Euphorbia peplus creams make it effective in soothing and calming itchy, irritated skin caused by hay fever.

When applied topically, the cream can help alleviate symptoms of hay fever and provide relief from discomfort.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Euphorbia peplus leaves, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Dry Euphorbia peplus leaves in a low oven (150°F) for 1-2 hours to remove moisture.
  3. Combine dried Euphorbia peplus leaves, coconut oil, and beeswax in a saucepan. Heat on low until beeswax melts.
  4. Remove saucepan from heat and let it cool for 10-15 minutes. Add vitamin E oil and mix well.
  5. Pour mixture into a container and let it cool until solid. Store Euphorbia peplus cream in an airtight container.

7. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and itching in the eyes and nasal passages associated with hay fever. Aloe-emodin has also been shown to have antihistamine-like effects, which can help alleviate symptoms like sneezing and congestion. Furthermore, acemannan has been found to have immunomodulatory properties, which can help regulate the immune system's response to allergens.

By reducing inflammation and modulating the immune response, aloe barbadensis creams may provide relief from hay fever symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup Aloe barbadensis gel, 1/2 cup coconut oil, 1/4 cup beeswax, 2 tablespoons olive oil, 2 tablespoons honey
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second intervals, stirring between each interval until melted
  3. Combine Aloe gel, melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture, olive oil, and honey in a bowl. Stir until well combined
  4. Pour the mixture into a jar or container and let it cool and solidify at room temperature for about an hour
  5. Once solidified, your Aloe barbadensis cream for hay fever is ready to use. Store it in the fridge for up to 2 weeks

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent hay fever from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help ease hay fever symptoms, but it's unlikely to prevent them from forming.

Some herbal teas, like nettle tea, contain compounds that reduce inflammation and congestion, which can provide temporary relief.

However, these teas may not stop the allergic reaction that causes hay fever in the first place.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for hay fever every day?

Herbal teas for hay fever can be a great relief, but it's essential to be cautious.

If you consume them daily, they might interact with other medications or worsen underlying conditions. Some herbs, like chamomile, can cause drowsiness, which isn't ideal for everyday use.

Moderation is key, so try to limit your intake to when symptoms are severe.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in hay fever?

Herbal teas like nettle and quercetin can help with hay fever symptoms.

It may take a few days to a week for results to be noticeable. As the body starts to absorb the herbal properties, you might feel your symptoms easing, such as reduced congestion and itchiness.

This can vary from person to person.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for hay fever?

For hay fever, it's often best to drink herbal tea in the morning or early afternoon.

This is because many herbal teas have antihistamine properties that can help block the release of histamine, a chemical that triggers allergy symptoms.

Drinking tea during these times can help ease congestion and itching before they get worse.

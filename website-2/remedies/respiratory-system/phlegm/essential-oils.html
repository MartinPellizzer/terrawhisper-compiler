9 Herbal Essential Oils For Phlegm

Its oil has natural decongestant properties that can help to open up airways and make it easier to breathe. When consumed as a tea, rosemary can help to soothe a sore throat and ease a cough. Mentha x piperita, or Peppermint, is another herb that can be used to relieve phlegm. Its oil has a cooling effect that can help to calm down a sore throat and ease a cough. When inhaled, peppermint oil can help to thin out phlegm and make it easier to cough up. Herbal teas made from these plants can be a natural and effective way to relieve phlegm and make you feel more comfortable. They can help to ease congestion and make it easier to breathe, which can be especially helpful when you have a cold or flu.

By incorporating these herbs into your daily routine, you can help to keep your airways clear and your body healthy.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like eucalyptol, alpha-pinene, and limonene.

These bioactive constituents have decongestant and expectorant properties that help to relieve congestion and promote the removal of phlegm from the lungs. Eucalyptol, in particular, is known to thin and break down phlegm, making it easier to cough up. The decongestant properties of alpha-pinene help to reduce inflammation in the nasal passages and sinuses, further alleviating congestion.

By thinning and breaking down phlegm, and reducing inflammation, Eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help to relieve phlegm-related respiratory issues.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and stems. Wash them with water to remove dirt.
  2. Dry the Eucalyptus globulus leaves and stems in a warm place or using a food dehydrator at 100°F (38°C). This may take 1-2 days.
  3. Use a coffee grinder or spice grinder to finely chop 1/2 cup of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves and stems.
  4. Place the chopped Eucalyptus globulus in a steam distillation apparatus or a homemade setup with a pot, condenser, and collecting vessel. Steam the mixture for 2-3 hours to release the essential oil.
  5. Collect the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil and store it in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 5-10 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains rosinic acid, borneol, and camphor as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds give the oil decongestant and expectorant properties, helping to clear excess phlegm from the respiratory system. The camphor in the oil acts as a bronchodilator, allowing for easier breathing by opening up airways congested by phlegm. The borneol in the oil has a mucolytic effect, breaking down and thinning mucus and phlegm, making it easier to expel.

By promoting the clearance of phlegm and reducing congestion, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help alleviate respiratory issues associated with phlegm buildup.

  1. Get Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and flowers. Use 1 cup of leaves and 1/4 cup of flowers.
  2. Steam distill the Rosmarinus officinalis mixture in a still. Heat water in a separate container.
  3. Collect the steam and direct it into a condenser. The condenser will collect the essential oil.
  4. Separate the essential oil from the water using a separating funnel. You should have about 2 tablespoons of essential oil.
  5. Filter the essential oil through a coffee filter to remove any impurities. Your Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil is now ready to use for phlegm.

3. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains bioactive constituents like menthol, menthone, and limonene.

These compounds have decongestant and expectorant properties, which help to reduce phlegm production. Menthol, in particular, is a natural bronchodilator that widens airways and makes it easier to breathe. Limonene has a solvent effect that helps to break down and clear excess mucus from the lungs and airways.

By using Mentha x piperita essential oils, you can help to loosen and clear phlegm, promoting a more comfortable and clear breathing experience.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat until it starts to boil, then reduce heat to low.
  3. Add the fresh Mentha x piperita leaves to the saucepan and let them simmer for 20-30 minutes.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 10 minutes, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth.
  5. Collect the essential oil in a small cup or bottle and discard the solids. This is your Mentha x piperita essential oil for phlegm.

4. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains the bioactive constituents thymol and carvacrol.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce excessive phlegm production. Thymol specifically has mucolytic properties, which break down and clear out thick mucus, making it easier to expel. Carvacrol also has expectorant properties, helping to thin and loosen phlegm, making it easier to cough up.

By reducing inflammation and breaking down thick mucus, Thymus vulgaris essential oils can help alleviate respiratory congestion and phlegm buildup.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves, 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Wash the Thymus vulgaris leaves with water, and dry them with a paper towel.
  3. Fill the glass jar with the Thymus vulgaris leaves, and pour the carrier oil over them.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. Then, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into another jar.
  5. Discard the solids, and store the Thymus vulgaris essential oil in a dark glass bottle in a cool place. Use 2-3 drops for relief from phlegm.

5. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains the bioactive constituents limonene, beta-pinene, and citronellal, which contribute to its decongestant and expectorant properties.

These properties help to reduce phlegm and promote its expulsion from the body. Limonene, in particular, has been shown to have a bronchodilatory effect, which can help to open airways and make breathing easier. Beta-pinene has antimicrobial properties, which can help to fight off infections that can cause phlegm buildup.

Citronellal, with its anti-inflammatory properties, can also help to soothe and calm the respiratory tract, reducing discomfort and congestion.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Wash the leaves with water to remove dirt and impurities.
  3. Place the leaves in a clean glass jar and cover them with the carrier oil.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a separate container, discarding the solids. Your Cymbopogon citratus essential oil is now ready to use.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have decongestant and expectorant properties that help to reduce the production of excess mucus and phlegm in the respiratory system. The decongestant properties of linalool and linalyl acetate help to open up airways and relieve congestion, while the expectorant properties help to thin and clear out excess mucus and phlegm. This makes Lavandula angustifolia essential oils a useful natural remedy for relieving respiratory issues associated with excess phlegm.

The combination of linalool and linalyl acetate in this essential oil helps to promote healthy respiratory function and alleviate symptoms of phlegm buildup.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 4 cups of water in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes.
  3. Let the mixture cool, then strain it through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the glass jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil) to the liquid. Stir well to combine.
  5. Store the Lavandula angustifolia essential oil in a cool, dark place and use as needed to help relieve phlegm congestion.

7. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.

Gingerol helps to reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract, making it easier to expel mucus and phlegm. The oils also contain zingiberene, which has a decongestant effect, helping to clear out congestion and promote drainage. Additionally, the essential oils contain beta-caryophyllene, which has anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties that aid in reducing phlegm production.

By using Zingiber officinale essential oils, you can help to soothe a congested chest and promote a healthy respiratory system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Zingiber officinale rhizomes (fresh or dried), a cheese cloth or a coffee filter, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Wash the rhizomes with water, then gently pat them dry with a paper towel. Chop the rhizomes into small pieces.
  3. Place the chopped rhizomes in the clean glass jar and cover them with 2 cups of water. Let it steep for 4-6 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the liquid through the cheese cloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil). Stir well.
  5. Transfer the mixture to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use a dropper to apply 5-7 drops of the essential oil to your chest or nose as needed.

8. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains sesquiterpenes, alkylamides, and phenolic acids as its bioactive constituents.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties that help to reduce excess mucus production and phlegm. The sesquiterpenes, particularly echinulin, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which contribute to the formation of phlegm. The alkylamides, such as cis-11,12-echinacoside, have been found to increase the production of nitric oxide, which helps to thin out mucus and make it easier to expel.

The phenolic acids, including caffeic and ferulic acids, have antimicrobial properties that help to combat underlying infections that can contribute to phlegm buildup.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Let it sit for 2-3 weeks in a dark place, shaking the jar daily.
  3. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  4. Discard the solids and transfer the liquid to a clean glass bottle. Add 10 drops of vitamin E oil to prevent spoilage.
  5. Store the Echinacea purpurea essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use it within 6 months or discard it if you notice any changes in color, smell, or texture.

9. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains a high concentration of cineole and terpinen-4-ol, which are two active constituents that make it effective in treating phlegm.

Cineole has a decongestant property that helps to loosen and clear excess mucus from the respiratory tract. Terpinen-4-ol, on the other hand, has antimicrobial properties that help to reduce inflammation and fight off infection. The expectorant properties of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils also help to thin and expel mucus, making it easier to cough up.

By combining these properties, Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils can help to relieve congestion and promote a healthy respiratory system.

  1. Gather 10 kg of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves. You can source them from a trusted supplier.
  2. Clean the leaves by washing them with 5 cups of water. Drain excess water.
  3. Place the leaves in a distillation apparatus and add 2 cups of distilled water.
  4. Heat the mixture to 100°C (212°F) and collect the steam. This will be your essential oil.
  5. Filter the essential oil and store it in a dark glass bottle. Use 5-7% of the oil in a diffuser for relief from phlegm.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for phlegm?

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains compounds like eucalyptol, camphor, and borneol.

These components help loosen and clear phlegm from the airways due to their decongestant and expectorant properties. Eucalyptol, in particular, has a strong affinity for water, which allows it to thin and break down thick mucus, making it easier to expel. Camphor's analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects also contribute to relieving congestion and discomfort.

By combining these properties, eucalyptus globulus creams can effectively help manage phlegm buildup in the respiratory system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 20 drops of eucalyptus essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until fully melted.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from heat and stir in the vitamin E oil and eucalyptus essential oil.
  4. Pour the mixture into a silicone candy mold or a small tin container and let it cool and solidify at room temperature.
  5. Once solid, remove the eucalyptus cream from the mold and store it in a cool, dry place. Use as needed to help loosen phlegm.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains several bioactive constituents, including alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce the production of excess mucus and phlegm. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea creams have been shown to have a suppressive effect on the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which contribute to the formation of phlegm. The flavonoids present in the cream have antioxidant properties, which can help protect the body from oxidative stress and inflammation, further reducing phlegm production.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Echinacea purpurea creams can help alleviate the symptoms of excessive phlegm production.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Combine dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and coconut oil in a double boiler. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes, then let it cool.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. In a separate bowl, melt the beeswax and shea butter in the microwave or in a double boiler. Mix in the vitamin E oil.
  5. Combine the Echinacea purpurea mixture and the melted beeswax mixture. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify.

3. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams contains thymol, borneol, and borneol acetate as its active constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce phlegm production. Thymol, in particular, is known for its ability to break down mucus and thin phlegm, making it easier to expel. Borneol and borneol acetate contribute to this effect by reducing inflammation and congestion in the respiratory tract.

By targeting the root causes of phlegm buildup, Thymus vulgaris creams can provide relief for those experiencing phlegm-related respiratory issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/2 cup of beeswax.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler. Heat until the beeswax is fully dissolved.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of Thymus vulgaris leaves to the melted mixture. Let it steep for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify. Store the Thymus vulgaris cream in a cool, dry place.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, borneol, and camphor as its active constituents.

Rosmarinus officinalis creams work by loosening and thinning mucus, making it easier to expel. The rosmarinic acid reduces inflammation in the respiratory tract, while borneol and camphor have decongestant properties. These properties help to break down and clear out excess phlegm, providing relief from congestion.

The combination of these compounds in Rosmarinus officinalis creams helps to reduce phlegm and promote easier breathing.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of beeswax, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of rosemary essential oil, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt beeswax and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add coconut oil to the melted mixture and stir until combined.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and stir in rosemary essential oil and vitamin E oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, your rosemary cream is ready to use as needed to help with phlegm.

5. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains compounds like menthol and menthone, which have been shown to help break down and clear phlegm.

Menthol helps to thin out mucus, making it easier to expel, and menthone has antimicrobial properties that can help reduce the growth of bacteria that can cause congestion. The eucalyptol in Mentha x piperita creams is a natural expectorant, meaning it helps to loosen and clear mucus from the airways. The camphor in these creams also has a decongestant effect, helping to reduce swelling and open up airways.

By using Mentha x piperita creams, you can help to relieve phlegm and congestion naturally.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of peppermint essential oil, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and a small bowl for mixing.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl for 30 seconds. Stir until smooth.
  3. Add peppermint essential oil and vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  4. Pour the mixture into small containers or a tin. Let it cool and harden at room temperature for about 30 minutes.
  5. Once hardened, your Mentha x piperita creams for phlegm are ready to use. Store them in an airtight container in a cool, dry place.

6. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help to break down and clear excess phlegm from the airways. The warming and drying properties of gingerols and shogaols also help to thin and expel mucus, making it easier to breathe. Additionally, zingiber officinale creams contain essential oils like zingiberene and bisabolol, which have expectorant properties that help to loosen and remove phlegm.

By using these creams, the airways are able to clear out excess phlegm, relieving congestion and coughs.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of honey, 2 tablespoons of grated fresh ginger, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add honey, grated ginger, and vitamin E oil to the melted oil mixture. Stir well to combine.
  4. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool and thicken. Stir occasionally until it reaches a creamy consistency.
  5. Pour the cream into a clean, sterilized container and let it cool completely before use. Store in a cool, dry place.

7. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains curcumin, a bioactive constituent that has potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Curcumin helps break down and clear excess phlegm by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and reducing mucus production. The volatile oils present in Curcuma longa creams, such as turmerone and atlantone, also exhibit expectorant properties, making it easier to cough up mucus. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of curcumin help soothe irritated respiratory passages, reducing congestion and discomfort.

By combining these properties, Curcuma longa creams can help alleviate symptoms of phlegm accumulation.

  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Stir in the turmeric powder and shea butter until well combined.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool until the mixture reaches 120°F to 130°F.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let cool completely before use as a cream for phlegm.

8. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, a compound that helps break down and clear mucus from the respiratory system.

It also contains thymol, a natural expectorant that loosens and removes phlegm from the airways. The antispasmodic properties of salvia officinalis help relax the muscles in the throat and bronchi, making it easier to cough up phlegm. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of salvia officinalis help reduce swelling and congestion in the lungs and airways, allowing for easier breathing and mucus clearance.

By combining these properties, salvia officinalis creams can help relieve phlegm and promote healthier respiratory function.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Salvia officinalis leaves, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of essential oil (optional).

  4. Add the vitamin E oil and essential oil (if using) to the mixture and stir well. Pour the mixture into a clean, heat-proof container.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify completely, then store it in the refrigerator. Use the Salvia officinalis cream as needed to help loosen phlegm.

9. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains glycyrrhizin, a key active constituent that helps thin and break down phlegm.

This compound also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce swelling and congestion in the airways. The licorice root extract in Glycyrrhiza glabra creams has demulcent properties, creating a protective barrier that soothes and calms irritated mucous membranes. Glycyrrhizin also has expectorant properties, making it easier to cough up phlegm and mucus.

By combining these properties, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams can help alleviate symptoms of excess phlegm and promote a healthy respiratory system.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1 cup of glycerin, 1/4 cup of vegetable glycerin, 2 tablespoons of Glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 drops of vitamin E oil.

  3. Add vegetable glycerin and Glycyrrhiza glabra root powder to the saucepan. Stir until powder dissolves.
  4. Remove saucepan from heat. Add beeswax and stir until melted. Add vitamin E oil and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container. Let it cool and solidify. Once set, your Glycyrrhiza glabra cream is ready for use.

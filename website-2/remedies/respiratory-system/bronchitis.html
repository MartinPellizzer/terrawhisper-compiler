What Are The Main Causes Of Bronchitis?

There are several main causes of bronchitis.

A viral infection is one of the most common causes. This type of infection is usually caused by a cold or flu virus that affects the lungs. When the virus infects the bronchial tubes, it causes inflammation and makes it difficult to breathe. A bacterial infection is another cause of bronchitis.

This type of infection occurs when bacteria, such as Mycoplasma pneumoniae, infect the bronchial tubes. Bacterial infections can be more serious than viral infections and may require antibiotics to treat. Smoking is also a major cause of bronchitis. Smoking damages the lungs and makes them more susceptible to infection.

When smokers inhale cigarette smoke, it irritates the bronchial tubes and causes inflammation, leading to bronchitis.

Below are the main causes of bronchitis.

  • Viral Infection: Viral infections, which are caused by tiny germs like the flu or cold virus, can lead to bronchitis by infecting the tiny air passages in the lungs called bronchioles, causing them to become inflamed and produce excess mucus.
  • Bacterial Infection: A bacterial infection, often caused by Streptococcus pneumoniae or Haemophilus influenzae, can inflame the airways and cause bronchitis by producing toxins that irritate the lung tissue and trigger a response that leads to coughing, mucus production, and difficulty breathing.
  • Smoking: Smoking damages the lungs and airways, making them more likely to get infected with viruses or bacteria, which can cause bronchitis.
  • Air Pollution: Air pollution is a cause of bronchitis because it makes the air we breathe thick with harmful particles, which irritate the lungs and make it hard for them to work properly, leading to inflammation and infection.
  • Respiratory Syncytial Virus: Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV) causes bronchitis because it infects the small airways in the lungs, leading to inflammation and congestion that makes breathing difficult.
  • Rhino Virus: The Rhino Virus, also known as the Common Cold Virus, can cause bronchitis by infecting the airways and making them inflamed, which leads to a cough and mucus production.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Bronchitis?

If you have bronchitis, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can irritate your airways or make your symptoms worse.

One such herb is Lobelia (Lobelia inflata), which can cause coughing and make breathing more difficult. Another herb to avoid is Sassafras (Sassafras albidum), as its volatile oils can trigger coughing and increase mucus production.

Yarrow (Achillea millefolium) and Sage (Salvia officinalis) can also be problematic, as they can dry out your airways and make it harder to cough up mucus. Additionally, Black Willow (Salix nigra) and Thoroughwort (Eupatorium perfoliatum) may worsen bronchitis symptoms by increasing inflammation and mucus production.

It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies, especially if you're experiencing persistent or severe symptoms.

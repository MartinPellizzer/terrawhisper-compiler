What Are The Main Causes Of Wheezing?

Wheezing is a high-pitched sound while breathing out, often a sign of a lung problem.

There are several main causes of wheezing. Asthma is one cause of wheezing. It's a condition where the airways in the lungs become narrow and swollen, making it hard to breathe. When you have asthma, your airways can get irritated and inflamed, causing them to constrict and produce the wheezing sound. This is often triggered by allergens, such as dust mites, pollen, or pet dander, or by respiratory viruses.

Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is another cause of wheezing. COPD is a condition where the lungs lose their ability to expand and take in air, often due to long-term exposure to lung irritants like smoke, dust, or chemicals. As a result, the airways in the lungs become narrowed and clogged with mucus, causing wheezing and difficulty breathing. Pneumonia is a type of lung infection that can also cause wheezing. It's usually caused by a bacterial or viral infection that inflames the air sacs in the lungs, making it hard to breathe.

As the infection heals, it can leave behind scar tissue that can cause the airways to narrow and produce a wheezing sound.

Below are the main causes of wheezing.

  • Asthma: Asthma is a cause of wheezing because when air passes through the narrowed airways in the lungs, it creates a high-pitched sound or wheezing due to the air being forced through a small space.
  • COPD: COPD causes wheezing because the damaged airways in the lungs become narrow and inflamed, making it hard for air to pass through and causing a high-pitched whistling sound when a person breathes out.
  • Pneumonia: When you have pneumonia, the infection can cause inflammation and fluid buildup in the air sacs of your lungs, leading to wheezing as you try to breathe.
  • Bronchitis: Bronchitis is a cause of wheezing because the swollen and inflamed airways in the lungs narrow, making it harder for air to pass through and producing a high-pitched whistling sound when breathing out.
  • Pneumothorax: Pneumothorax causes wheezing because air leaks into the space between the lung and chest wall, making the lung collapse partially and narrowing the airways, which in turn causes the wheezing sound.
  • Bronchiectasis: Bronchiectasis causes wheezing because the damaged airways in the lungs are narrowed and inflamed, making it hard for air to flow in and out, and causing the typical high-pitched whistling sound known as wheezing.
  • Hypersensitivity Pneumonitis: Hypersensitivity Pneumonitis causes wheezing because tiny air sacs in the lungs become inflamed and constricted, making it hard for air to flow in and out, leading to wheezing sounds when breathing.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Wheezing?

If you have wheezing, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can make symptoms worse.

One of these herbs is Hyssopus officinalis, also known as hyssop. It can irritate the airways and make wheezing worse due to its expectorant properties, which are meant to bring up mucus but can instead trigger a coughing fit.

Another herb to avoid is Lavandula angustifolia, or lavender. While it's known for its calming effects, its fragrance can be overpowering and trigger an allergic reaction in people with respiratory issues, making wheezing worse. Additionally, Valeriana officinalis, or valerian root, should be avoided as it can slow down breathing and worsen wheezing in people with respiratory problems.

Lastly, Citrus aurantium, or bitter orange, contains a compound called synephrine that can increase heart rate and blood pressure, which may not be suitable for people with wheezing.

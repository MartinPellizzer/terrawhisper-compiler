9 Herbal Teas to Ease Nasal Blockage for Asthma Sufferers

For people with asthma, a blocked nose can be a big problem.

When you breathe in, your airways get inflamed and narrow, making it hard to breathe. But when your nose gets blocked, it's like having a narrow pipe too - it's harder to breathe and can trigger asthma attacks. This can make everyday life tough, from sleeping to doing simple tasks. A blocked nose can also make breathing exercises harder, which are usually a big part of managing asthma. This can lead to more asthma attacks, which can be scary and uncomfortable. A blocked nose can also be painful and annoying, making it hard to enjoy activities you love.

It's not just about breathing, but also about feeling well enough to live your life. When your nose is blocked, it's hard to enjoy things like exercise, socializing, or even just relaxing at home. This can make you feel frustrated and sad. Some people with asthma find that certain herbal teas help to ease their blocked nose and make it easier to breathe. These teas can be made from plants that have natural anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to calm down the airways and reduce swelling. They can also help to loosen mucus and make it easier to breathe.

Drinking herbal tea can be a simple and gentle way to help manage a blocked nose, and it's something you can do at home.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas are a popular remedy for a blocked nose, and for good reason.

The tea contains compounds like eucalyptol, which has natural decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties. When you drink the tea, these compounds are absorbed into your body and travel up to your nasal passages, where they help to thin out mucus and open up airways. This makes it easier to breathe and can provide quick relief from congestion. To try Eucalyptus globulus teas for a blocked nose, start by drinking a warm cup of tea 2-3 times a day.

You can find Eucalyptus globulus tea in most health food stores or online, or make your own by steeping dried eucalyptus leaves in boiling water. To get the most out of the tea, take a few minutes to inhale the steam before drinking, and then drink slowly to allow the compounds to take effect. You may feel a slight burning sensation as the tea works to clear out your nasal passages, but this is a normal and temporary reaction. As you continue to drink the tea, you can expect to feel your nasal congestion start to clear up within 30 minutes to an hour. Your nose may still feel a bit stuffy, but you should be able to breathe more easily and your sinuses should start to feel more open.

If you have a cold or sinus infection, the tea may not completely clear up your symptoms, but it can help to reduce your discomfort and make you feel more comfortable until you can get proper medical treatment.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas have been used to help relieve a blocked nose.

This is because they contain properties and constituents such as alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids. These compounds help to reduce inflammation and congestion in the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe. Alkylamides, in particular, are known to have anti-inflammatory effects, while glycosides have been shown to stimulate the immune system and help fight off infections that can cause a blocked nose. If you're experiencing a blocked nose, you can try making a tea by steeping dried Echinacea purpurea flowers in hot water for 5-7 minutes. You can add honey or lemon to taste, but be sure to use a clean and sanitized environment to avoid contamination.

Some people find it helpful to drink the tea 2-3 times a day, especially before bedtime, to help clear out congestion and promote a good night's sleep. However, it's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea teas may not work immediately, and it may take a few days to notice any improvements. As you start taking Echinacea purpurea teas, you may notice a slight decrease in nasal congestion and an increase in airflow. Your sinuses may feel clearer, and you might find it easier to breathe through your nose. In some cases, people have reported that their sense of smell has also improved. However, it's crucial to remember that Echinacea purpurea teas are not a substitute for medical treatment.

If your blocked nose persists or is accompanied by other symptoms such as fever, green mucus, or facial pain, you should consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas, also known as rosemary tea, can help with a blocked nose due to its natural decongestant properties.

The tea contains compounds like camphor, bornyl acetate, and alpha-pinene, which have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties that help loosen and clear mucus from the nasal passages. When you drink rosemary tea, these properties work together to reduce swelling and congestion, making it easier to breathe. To make the most of rosemary tea for a blocked nose, try the following: brew a strong cup of rosemary tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried rosemary leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it while still warm.

You can also add a squeeze of fresh lemon juice to enhance the flavor and benefits. It's best to drink the tea a few times a day, especially when your symptoms are at their worst. When you take action with rosemary tea, you can expect to see a reduction in nasal congestion and an improvement in breathing. You may also notice a decrease in the thickness and amount of mucus produced, making it easier to clear your airways.

As the tea works its way through your system, you may feel a sense of relief from sinus pressure and headaches associated with a blocked nose.

5. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, have been used for centuries to help relieve a blocked nose.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, which help to break down mucus and reduce congestion. Strain the tea into a cup and add a squeeze of honey if you prefer it sweet.

Drink the tea while it's still hot, ideally when you first wake up or before going to bed. You can also add a few slices of fresh ginger to a bowl of hot water and inhale the steam for a few minutes to help open up your airways. When you drink ginger tea regularly, you can expect to notice a few changes. Your congestion should start to clear up, and you should be able to breathe more easily. You may also notice that you're producing more mucus, but this is a good sign as it means that your body is getting rid of the congestion.

Additionally, ginger tea has natural anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce sinus pressure and headaches associated with a blocked nose.

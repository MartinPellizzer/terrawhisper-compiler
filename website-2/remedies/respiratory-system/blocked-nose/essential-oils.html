10 Herbal Essential Oils For Blocked Nose

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains eucalyptol, a key bioactive constituent that provides its decongestant properties.

Eucalyptol helps to thin and clear mucus from the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe. The oil's expectorant properties also help to loosen and remove excess mucus, further relieving congestion. The anti-inflammatory properties of eucalyptol also reduce swelling in the nasal passages, allowing for easier airflow.

When inhaled, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help to temporarily open airways and provide relief from blocked noses.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a clean glass jar, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Dry the Eucalyptus leaves in a warm place for 2-3 days. Check if they are dry and crumbly.
  3. Combine 1 cup of dried Eucalyptus leaves with 2 cups of carrier oil in the clean glass jar.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean container. Discard the solids and store the oil in a cool, dark place.

2. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol, carvacrol, and borneol, which are powerful compounds that help open airways.

Thymol has natural decongestant properties that ease congestion and reduce swelling in the nasal passages. Carvacrol has antimicrobial properties that fight off infections and inflammation, making it easier to breathe. Borneol's expectorant properties help loosen mucus, allowing it to drain out of the body.

By combining these properties, Thymus vulgaris essential oils helps to clear blocked noses and promote healthy breathing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves, 1 cup of water, and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Wash the Thymus vulgaris leaves with 1 tablespoon of mild soap and 1 cup of water. Rinse well and pat dry.
  3. Combine the dried leaves with 1 cup of water in a glass jar. Cover the jar with a lid.
  4. Place the jar in a saucepan and add enough water to cover the bottom. Heat the mixture over low heat for 2 hours.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

3. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains a high concentration of geraniol and limonene, two active constituents that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling in the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe. The limonene in Cymbopogon citratus essential oils also has a decongestant effect, which helps to clear mucus from the airways. Geraniol, on the other hand, has a natural expectorant property that helps to thin and expel mucus, further relieving congestion.

By combining these properties, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can provide relief from blocked noses.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves, a glass jar, and a clean cup for steam.
  2. Cut the leaves into small pieces and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Combine 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of vodka in the clean cup. Stir well.
  4. Pour the water-vodka mixture over the Cymbopogon citratus leaves in the jar. Cover the jar and let it sit for 2 weeks, shaking daily.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a new glass bottle. Discard the solids. Use the essential oil as needed to relieve a blocked nose.

4. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and linalool.

These bioactive constituents have decongestant and expectorant properties, which help to reduce nasal congestion and promote the removal of excess mucus. The cineole in Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils helps to thin mucus, making it easier to expel, while the limonene and linalool have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling in the nasal passages. This combination of properties can help to relieve blocked noses and promote clearer breathing.

Regular use of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils in a diffuser or through steam inhalation may help to alleviate nasal congestion and sinus pressure.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melaleuca alternifolia leaves. You can grow them or purchase from a supplier.
  2. Add 2 cups of water to a large saucepan and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and add the Melaleuca alternifolia leaves. Let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass container. Discard the leaves.
  5. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba oil) to the liquid. Stir well and let it sit for 2 days to allow the mixture to mature.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains a high concentration of camphor, borneol, and 1,8-cineole, which are the bioactive constituents responsible for its decongestant properties.

These compounds help to break down mucus and reduce inflammation in the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe. The antiseptic and antimicrobial properties of 1,8-cineole also help to eliminate bacteria and other microorganisms that can exacerbate congestion. Borneol, on the other hand, acts as a vasodilator, which helps to widen blood vessels and improve blood flow to the nasal area.

By combining these properties, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help to provide quick relief from blocked noses.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves, 2 cups of water, a saucepan, and a still or a distillation kit.
  2. Wash the leaves with water and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Combine the leaves and water in the saucepan and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Collect the steam in the still or distillation kit. This will create a water and oil mixture.
  5. Separate the oil from the water using a separatory funnel or by letting it settle and decanting the oil. Store the oil in a dark glass bottle.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are the main bioactive constituents that help with a blocked nose.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that reduce swelling and fight off infections in the nasal passages. Linalool also has a decongestant effect, which helps to clear out excess mucus and open up airways. Additionally, the essential oil has a calming effect on the body, which can help to reduce stress and tension that can exacerbate nasal congestion.

By using Lavandula angustifolia essential oils, you can help to ease nasal congestion and promote a clearer, healthier breathing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over medium heat until it starts boiling.
  3. Add the fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the boiling water and reduce heat to low.
  4. Steep the flowers for 1-2 hours, then strain the mixture using cheesecloth or a coffee filter.
  5. Store the resulting essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid, out of direct sunlight.

7. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains active constituents like zingiberene and bisabolol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help to reduce swelling and fight off infections that can cause a blocked nose. Zingiberene also has a natural decongestant effect, helping to clear mucus and open up airways. Bisabolol, on the other hand, has a soothing effect on the nasal passages, reducing inflammation and discomfort.

By using Zingiber officinale essential oils, you can take advantage of these properties to help alleviate a blocked nose.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger root, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Wash the ginger root and peel it. Slice it into thin pieces.
  3. Combine the ginger pieces and carrier oil in the glass jar. Make sure the ginger is covered by the oil.
  4. Steep the ginger mixture for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the ginger pieces and store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

8. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains compounds like limonene, menthone, and beta-pinene.

These constituents have decongestant and expectorant properties, which help to relieve blocked noses by reducing inflammation in the nasal passages. The menthol in Mentha x piperita essential oils acts as a natural expectorant, thinning mucus and making it easier to expel, while the cineole helps to reduce congestion and promote drainage. The antiviral and antibacterial properties of menthone and beta-pinene also help to combat underlying infections that may be contributing to the blocked nose.

When inhaled, the vapors of Mentha x piperita essential oils can help to clear nasal passages and promote a sense of ease and comfort.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and flowers. Clean them with water.
  2. Add 1 cup of water to a saucepan and bring it to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and add the cleaned Mentha x piperita leaves and flowers. Let it steep for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture and separate the liquid from the solids. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut oil) to the liquid.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2 hours. Then, strain it again and your Mentha x piperita essential oil is ready for use.

9. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains bioactive constituents like salvenic acid, tannins, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe. The decongestant properties of the essential oil help to clear mucus and open up airways, relieving congestion. Rosmarinic acid, in particular, is known for its ability to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to nasal congestion.

By reducing inflammation and promoting drainage, Salvia officinalis essential oils can provide relief from a blocked nose.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves, a cup of water, a saucepan, a bowl, a cheesecloth or a coffee filter, and a clean glass bottle.
  2. Chop the fresh Salvia officinalis leaves and add them to the saucepan. Pour in the cup of water and heat the mixture over low heat for 10 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth or coffee filter into the bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to the clean glass bottle and add 5 tablespoons of a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil. Stir well.
  5. Label the bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. To use, dilute 2-3 drops of the essential oil in a teaspoon of the carrier oil and inhale it through your nose to relieve congestion.

10. Origanum vulgare

Origanum vulgare essential oils contains a compound called carvacrol, which has powerful decongestant properties.

Carvacrol helps to break down mucus and reduce inflammation in the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe. Another key compound, thymol, has antimicrobial properties that help to eliminate any bacteria or viruses that may be contributing to the blockage. The antispasmodic properties of thymol also help to relax the muscles in the nose, allowing for easier airflow.

By combining these properties, Origanum vulgare essential oils can help to relieve congestion and open up airways.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Origanum vulgare leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil to the jar for preservation.
  3. Steep the Origanum vulgare leaves in the carrier oil for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the leaves.
  5. Store the Origanum vulgare essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight lid and use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or apply topically as needed for a blocked nose.

FAQ

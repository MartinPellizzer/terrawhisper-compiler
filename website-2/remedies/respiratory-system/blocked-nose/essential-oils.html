10 herbal remedies for pregnant women to ease blocked nose

During pregnancy, women's nasal passages can swell due to hormonal changes and increased blood volume, leading to a blocked nose.

This can cause discomfort, breathing difficulties, and a lack of sleep, making everyday life challenging. Simple tasks like eating, speaking, and breathing can become exhausting. A blocked nose can also affect a woman's sense of smell, making it harder to enjoy food and enjoy time with loved ones. Moreover, a blocked nose can be a breeding ground for bacteria, increasing the risk of respiratory infections.

As a result, pregnant women often seek natural remedies to alleviate their congestion. One option is using herbal essential oils, which can be inhaled directly or added to a warm bath. These oils have been traditionally used to help ease nasal congestion and promote relaxation. They can be absorbed through the skin or inhaled through the nose, providing quick relief from a blocked nose. When inhaled, the aromatic compounds in these oils can help open up airways, reduce swelling, and clear mucus, providing much-needed relief.

By using herbal essential oils, pregnant women can find some comfort and relief from a blocked nose, making their lives a little easier.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils are often used to help ease a blocked nose due to their decongestant properties.

This is largely attributed to the presence of compounds like eucalyptol, which is a natural expectorant that helps loosen and clear mucus from the airways. Other key constituents, such as alpha-pinene and limonene, have anti-inflammatory properties that may help reduce swelling and ease congestion. To use Eucalyptus globulus essential oils for a blocked nose, try inhaling the oil through steam inhalation.

You can do this by adding a few drops of the oil to a bowl of hot water, then leaning over the bowl with a towel covering your head to trap the steam. Alternatively, you can mix a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil and apply it to your chest or nose. When you inhale the oil through steam inhalation, you may experience a rapid reduction in nasal congestion and an increased ability to breathe. Your senses may also be invigorated, and you may feel more alert and refreshed.

Some people may experience a slight burning sensation or discomfort, but this is usually temporary and should subside once the oil's effects wear off.

Cover your head with a towel and inhale the steam for a few minutes.

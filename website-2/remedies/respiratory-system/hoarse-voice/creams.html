1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains licorice extract which is rich in glycyrrhizin and flavonoids.

Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the vocal cords, a common cause of hoarseness. The flavonoids, particularly luteolin and isoliquiritigenin, have antioxidant properties that protect the vocal cords from damage and promote healing. Licorice extract also has demulcent properties that soothe and protect the mucous membranes in the throat, reducing irritation and inflammation.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams can help restore a healthy, smooth voice.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of water, 2 tablespoons of glycerin, 2 tablespoons of glycyrrhiza glabra powder, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 teaspoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Heat the water in a saucepan over low heat. Add glycyrrhiza glabra powder and mix well.
  3. Remove from heat and let it cool. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix glycerin, coconut oil, and vitamin E oil. Add the strained glycyrrhiza glabra liquid to the oil mixture.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool completely. Apply the cream to the throat area to soothe a hoarse voice.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains a high concentration of gingerols and shogaols, bioactive compounds that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling in the throat and vocal cords, making it easier to produce sound. The warming effect of ginger also increases blood flow to the throat, which helps to soothe and calm irritated tissues. Additionally, the antioxidants present in Zingiber officinale creams, including gingerol and shogaol, help protect the vocal cords from oxidative stress and damage.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Zingiber officinale creams can help alleviate hoarseness and support overall vocal health.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of honey, 1 teaspoon of Zingiber officinale powder (ginger powder), and 2 drops of peppermint essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add the honey, ginger powder, and peppermint essential oil to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool until it reaches 120-130°F. This is an important step to prevent burning the mixture.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container and let it cool completely before use. Apply a small amount to your throat to soothe a hoarse voice.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains alkylamides and caffeic acid derivatives as its active constituents.

By reducing inflammation and promoting immune function, Echinacea purpurea creams may help alleviate hoarseness and promote a clearer, healthier voice.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of beeswax, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of olive oil, 2 tablespoons of Echinacea purpurea tincture, 2 tablespoons of honey, and 2 drops of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the beeswax and coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Once melted, remove from heat and stir in the olive oil, Echinacea purpurea tincture, and honey until well combined.
  4. Add the vitamin E oil and stir well. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify. Once set, your Echinacea purpurea cream is ready to use. Apply a small amount to the throat to help soothe a hoarse voice.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent hoarse voice from forming?

Drinking herbal tea might help soothe a sore throat and reduce the risk of getting a hoarse voice.

Throat-friendly herbs like slippery elm and licorice root have anti-inflammatory properties that can calm irritated tissues.

These teas can help keep your vocal cords healthy and working smoothly, but prevention isn't guaranteed and some people may still get hoarse.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for hoarse voice every day?

Consuming herbal teas for a hoarse voice daily can be safe, but it depends on the ingredients.

Some teas, like slippery elm and licorice root, are soothing and may help. However, teas high in caffeine, like peppermint or ginger, might irritate your throat further.

Choose gentle teas and listen to your body's response.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in hoarse voice?

Herbal teas can help soothe a hoarse voice.

Some people notice a difference within a few days, while others may take a week or two to see results. It depends on the individual and the severity of the hoarseness.

Drinking herbal teas regularly, especially before sleep, may help your voice feel better.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for hoarse voice?

For a hoarse voice, it's usually best to drink herbal tea in the morning or early afternoon when your vocal cords are at their most sensitive. This allows the soothing properties of the tea to take effect before you use your voice again.

11 Herbal Teas For Hoarse Voice

If you're struggling with a hoarse voice, herbal teas might be just what you need to soothe your vocal cords.

Some herbs, like Glycyrrhiza glabra, also known as licorice root, have natural anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the throat, allowing you to speak more smoothly. Another herb, Zingiber officinale or ginger, has a warm, spicy flavor that can help loosen mucus and reduce throat congestion, making it easier to talk without straining your voice.

Echinacea purpurea is also a popular choice, as it has immune-boosting properties that can help your body fight off infections that can cause a hoarse voice. Drinking these herbal teas can bring several benefits to your life, including reduced vocal strain, improved breathing, and even a boost to your immune system.

Plus, many of these teas are naturally soothing and calming, making them a great way to unwind and relax after a long day.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains licorice root extract, which is rich in glycyrrhizin and other bioactive compounds like flavonoids and saponins.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the throat and vocal cords, making it easier to speak. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of cortisol, a hormone that can cause throat irritation and hoarseness. By reducing cortisol levels and soothing the throat, Glycyrrhiza glabra teas can help alleviate hoarseness and promote a healthy vocal tone.

This is achieved by protecting the mucous membranes in the throat and vocal cords, allowing for clearer and more comfortable communication.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of water, 2-3 tablespoons of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, and a strainer.
  2. Boil the water in a pot and let it cool for 2 minutes.
  3. Add the dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root to the pot and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid into a cup and discard the root.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help soothe a hoarse voice.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains a compound called gingerol, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling in the vocal cords, a common cause of hoarseness. The tea also contains shogaol, a compound that has antioxidant properties, which help protect the vocal cords from damage. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Zingiber officinale teas work together to help soothe and calm irritated vocal cords, reducing hoarseness.

The warming sensation of ginger also helps increase blood flow to the throat, further aiding in the recovery of a hoarse voice.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a kettle or on the stove.
  2. Measure 1-2 teaspoons of dried or fresh Zingiber officinale root and add it to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes to release the root's flavor and properties.
  4. Strain the mixture into a cup and discard the root.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm to help soothe a hoarse voice.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, phenolic acids, and glycosides.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help soothe and protect the mucous membranes in the throat. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea stimulate the production of white blood cells, which can aid in fighting off infections that may be causing a hoarse voice. The phenolic acids have antimicrobial properties, which can help reduce the growth of bacteria and fungi in the throat, promoting a healthy environment for the vocal cords.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy environment, Echinacea purpurea teas can help alleviate a hoarse voice and support overall throat health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Echinacea purpurea flowers. Use 1 tablespoon of dried flowers or 2 tablespoons of fresh flowers.
  2. Measure 1 cup of boiling water and pour it over the Echinacea flowers in a heat-resistant cup or teapot.
  3. Steep for 5-7 minutes. Let the flowers infuse in the hot water.
  4. Strain the mixture using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth. Discard the solids.
  5. Drink the tea while it's warm. You can add honey or lemon to taste.

4. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas contains thymol and carvacrol, two active constituents that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling in the vocal cords, which can cause hoarseness. The antioxidant properties of thymol and carvacrol also protect the vocal cords from free radicals, which can damage the delicate tissue. Thymus vulgaris teas has a soothing effect on the throat, calming irritation and inflammation.

Drinking Thymus vulgaris tea may help to clear up hoarseness by promoting a healthy environment for the vocal cords.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 teaspoon of dried Thymus vulgaris leaves.
  2. Add the dried Thymus vulgaris leaves to a teapot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the leaves and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid into a cup and discard the leaves.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help soothe a hoarse voice.

5. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains bioactive constituents like eucalyptol, cineole, and globulol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties that help relieve a hoarse voice by reducing swelling in the throat and promoting mucus clearance. Eucalyptol, in particular, has been shown to thin mucus, making it easier to expel, while cineole helps to reduce inflammation and globulol has antimicrobial properties that prevent infections. The expectorant properties of these compounds help to clear out mucus and debris that can cause a hoarse voice, promoting healthy vocal cord function.

Regular consumption of Eucalyptus globulus teas may help to soothe and calm the throat, reducing the severity of a hoarse voice.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 2-3 sprigs of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves.
  2. Steep the Eucalyptus leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid using a tea strainer or a piece of cheesecloth into a cup.

  5. Drink the Eucalyptus tea 2-3 times a day to help soothe a hoarse voice.

11. Hydrastis canadensis

Hydrastis canadensis teas contains berberine, hydrastine, and canadine, which are bioactive constituents that help soothe an irritated throat.

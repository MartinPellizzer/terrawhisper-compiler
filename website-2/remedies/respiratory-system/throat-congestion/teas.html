11 Herbal Teas For Throat Congestion

Herbal teas are a great way to soothe a sore throat and relieve congestion.

They work by reducing inflammation and killing bacteria that cause infection. Echinacea purpurea, for example, has anti-inflammatory properties that help to calm the throat and reduce swelling. Eucalyptus globulus, on the other hand, has decongestant properties that help to clear out mucus and make it easier to breathe. Glycyrrhiza glabra, also known as licorice root, has antiviral properties that help to fight off infections and soothe a sore throat.

When you drink herbal tea, the active ingredients are absorbed into your body and start to work their magic. They help to reduce the production of mucus, making it easier to breathe and swallow. They also help to kill bacteria and viruses that cause infection, which means you can get back to your normal routine faster. Plus, herbal teas are a natural and gentle way to soothe a sore throat, without the harsh chemicals and side effects of over-the-counter medications.

By drinking herbal tea, you can find relief from throat congestion and get back to feeling your best.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains the bioactive constituents alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the throat, making it easier to breathe. The alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals, which can contribute to congestion and discomfort. The glycosides in Echinacea purpurea teas have antimicrobial properties, helping to fight off infections that can cause throat congestion.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off infections, Echinacea purpurea teas can help alleviate throat congestion and promote a smoother, more comfortable breathing experience.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves.
  2. Combine the dried Echinacea with 1 cup of boiling water in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes to allow the flavors to release.
  4. Strain the tea into another cup and discard the solids.
  5. Drink the tea warm, 2-3 times a day, to help soothe throat congestion.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains compounds like eucalyptol, diosphenol, and alpha-pinene.

These bioactive constituents have decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties. Eucalyptol, in particular, helps to loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to breathe. The anti-inflammatory properties of alpha-pinene and diosphenol help to reduce swelling in the throat, relieving discomfort and congestion.

Regular consumption of Eucalyptus globulus teas may help alleviate throat congestion by providing these beneficial effects.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Measure 2 tablespoons of honey and set aside for later use.
  3. Add the Eucalyptus leaves to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the leaves.
  5. Mix in the honey and drink the tea while warm to help soothe throat congestion.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins, which give it its medicinal properties.

Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory effects, which help reduce swelling and irritation in the throat. Flavonoids, such as liquiritin and isoliquiritin, have antioxidant properties that protect the throat from damage caused by free radicals. Saponins in Glycyrrhiza glabra tea also have mucolytic properties, breaking down mucus and making it easier to expel.

These properties work together to help alleviate throat congestion and soothe irritated tissues.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the root in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain the mixture.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to the liquid, if desired, to sweeten the tea.
  4. Drink the tea while it is still warm, 2-3 times a day, to help relieve throat congestion.
  5. Store any leftover tea in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours, and reheat it as needed.

4. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas contains thymol and carvacrol as its bioactive constituents.

Thymol has expectorant properties, which means it helps loosen and remove mucus from the airways. Carvacrol, on the other hand, has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling in the throat. When combined, these compounds help alleviate throat congestion by promoting drainage and reducing inflammation.

Regular consumption of Thymus vulgaris teas may help ease respiratory issues and promote a healthy throat.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Thymus vulgaris leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Pour the boiling water over the Thymus vulgaris leaves in a cup.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid into another cup to remove the leaves.
  5. Drink the Thymus vulgaris tea while it's still warm to help relieve throat congestion.

5. Zingiber officinale

The essential oil in ginger, zingiberene, has expectorant properties that loosen and clear mucus, making it easier to breathe. Ginger also contains a compound called shogaol, which has decongestant properties that help open up airways and reduce congestion. The antiseptic properties of ginger help fight off infections that can cause throat congestion, while its antioxidant properties help protect the throat from damage.

The combination of these properties in Zingiber officinale teas makes it a natural remedy for relieving throat congestion.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Zingiber officinale root and 1 cup of boiling water.

  3. Add the 1 tablespoon of dried Zingiber officinale root to the boiling water.

6. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra teas contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, which have anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties.

These compounds help to reduce swelling and ease congestion in the throat, making it easier to breathe and swallow. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin, have a powerful anti-inflammatory effect that helps to calm the mucous membranes in the throat, reducing inflammation and discomfort. The phenolic acids, such as caffeic acid, also have antihistamine properties that help to block the release of histamine, a chemical that can exacerbate allergic reactions and worsen congestion.

By reducing inflammation and blocking histamine, Sambucus nigra teas helps to relieve throat congestion and promote a healthy respiratory system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Sambucus nigra flowers and leaves.
  2. Combine the Sambucus nigra flowers and leaves with 1 cup of boiling water in a saucepan.
  3. Steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Discard the solids and add honey to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink 1/2 cup of the tea 2-3 times a day to help soothe throat congestion.

7. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains essential oils like camphor, borneol, and borneol acetate, which have decongestant properties.

These compounds help break down mucus and reduce swelling in the throat, making it easier to breathe and swallow. The antiseptic properties of Rosmarinus officinalis teas, particularly its content of carnosic acid and 1,8-cineole, also help combat infections that can cause congestion. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of the tea's bioactive constituents, such as rosmarinic acid, help reduce redness and inflammation in the throat.

By combining these properties, Rosmarinus officinalis teas can provide relief from throat congestion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 tablespoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) and have it ready.
  3. Steep the dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and add honey if desired.
  5. Drink the tea slowly while it's still warm to help soothe throat congestion.

8. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis teas contains rosmarinic acid, a powerful antioxidant that helps reduce inflammation and fight off infections in the throat.

This tea also contains tannins, which have a soothing effect on the mucous membranes and help to reduce swelling and irritation. The antibacterial properties of Salvia officinalis, particularly carnosic acid, inhibit the growth of bacteria that can cause congestion and infection in the throat. The expectorant properties of this tea, due to the presence of flavonoids like luteolin, help to thin and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to breathe.

The combination of these active constituents in Salvia officinalis teas helps to alleviate throat congestion by reducing inflammation, fighting off infections, and promoting the clearance of mucus.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves.
  2. Chop the leaves into small pieces and place them in a heat-resistant bowl.
  3. Add 1 cup of boiling water to the bowl and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid into a cup using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  5. Drink the tea warm, 2-3 times a day, to help alleviate throat congestion.

9. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas contains menthol and menthone, which are responsible for its decongestant properties.

Menthol, a key bioactive constituent, helps to thin mucus and make it easier to expel, reducing congestion in the throat. Menthone, another bioactive constituent, has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce swelling and irritation in the throat. The expectorant properties of menthol also help to loosen and clear out mucus, making it easier to breathe.

By combining these properties, Mentha x piperita teas can provide relief from throat congestion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh peppermint leaves (Mentha x piperita) or 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Add the peppermint leaves to the boiling water.
  4. Reduce heat and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.

10. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains a group of active compounds known as flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the throat.

These flavonoids, particularly luteolin and apigenin, have been shown to relax and reduce spasms in the muscles of the throat, making it easier to breathe. The tea also contains a compound called linalool, which has expectorant properties that help loosen and clear mucus from the airways. Additionally, the tea contains a compound called limonene, which has antimicrobial properties that help combat infection and promote healing in the throat.

By reducing inflammation, relaxing muscles, loosening mucus, and combating infection, Lavandula angustifolia teas can provide relief from throat congestion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia leaves and flowers.

  4. Add the fresh or dried Lavandula angustifolia to the water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it warm to help relieve throat congestion.

11. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are active compounds that help alleviate throat congestion.

The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and ease pain in the throat. Terpenoids like ginkgolide and bilobalide also exhibit anti-inflammatory effects, which can help loosen mucus and promote drainage. The flavonoids and terpenoids in Ginkgo biloba teas work together to help break down and clear excess mucus from the throat, making it easier to breathe and swallow.

This combination of flavonoids and terpenoids helps provide relief from throat congestion by reducing inflammation and promoting drainage.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1 teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves.
  2. Heat the water in a pot until it boils, then reduce the heat to low.
  3. Add the Ginkgo biloba leaves to the pot and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the leaves.
  5. Drink the tea hot or warm to help soothe throat congestion.

What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have throat congestion?

If you have throat congestion, it's best to avoid certain herbal essential oils that can make the condition worse.

For example, oils from 'Cassia' (Cinnamomum cassia) and 'Cinnamon' (Cinnamomum verum) can irritate the throat and make it harder to swallow. This is because these oils have a strong, spicy quality that can further dry out the throat and make congestion worse.

Another oil to avoid is 'Sage' (Salvia divinorum), which can also dry out the throat and make it harder to swallow. 'Myrrh' (Commiphora molmol) oil can be irritating to some people, especially those with sensitive skin or allergies. 'Frankincense' (Boswellia serrata) oil is also not recommended, as it can cause a burning sensation in the throat and make congestion worse.

It's best to stick with gentle, soothing oils like 'Peppermint' (Mentha x piperita) and 'Eucalyptus' (Eucalyptus globulus) to help ease throat congestion.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains active constituents like eucalyptol, cineole, and globulol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties, which help to reduce swelling and open up airways in the throat. Eucalyptol, the main active ingredient, acts as a bronchodilator, making it easier to breathe. The menthol content in eucalyptus globulus creams helps to thin mucus, making it easier to expel and relieving congestion.

By combining these properties, eucalyptus globulus creams can provide quick and effective relief from throat congestion.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of eucalyptus oil, 2 tablespoons of peppermint oil, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second increments, stirring between each heating.
  3. Add the beeswax and stir until it's fully melted.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in the eucalyptus oil, peppermint oil, and vitamin E oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and harden. Use as needed to relieve throat congestion.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help soothe and calm an irritated throat. Alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to reduce swelling and inflammation in the throat, making it easier to breathe and swallow. Glycosides, on the other hand, have been found to have antioxidant properties, which help protect the throat from damage caused by free radicals.

By using Echinacea purpurea creams, you can take advantage of these beneficial properties to help alleviate throat congestion and promote a faster recovery.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Heat the coconut oil and beeswax in a small saucepan over low heat, stirring until melted.
  3. Add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, shea butter, and vitamin E oil to the saucepan. Stir well.
  4. Remove from heat and let steep for 2 hours to allow the Echinacea to infuse into the mixture.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl. Pour into small tin containers and let cool. Use as a throat congestion cream.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin, and flavonoids like quercetin, kaempferol, and luteolin.

Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the throat, making it easier to breathe. The flavonoids in Glycyrrhiza glabra creams have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help soothe and calm the irritated tissues in the throat. Glycyrrhizin also helps relax the smooth muscles in the throat, which can become constricted due to congestion.

The combination of these properties in Glycyrrhiza glabra creams makes it effective in relieving throat congestion.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup distilled water, 2 tablespoons glycerin, 2 tablespoons Glyceria glabra root powder (licorice root powder), 2 tablespoons beeswax, 2 tablespoons coconut oil, and 10 drops of tea tree oil.
  2. Melt beeswax and coconut oil in a double boiler or in a microwave-safe bowl in short intervals, stirring between each heating.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix distilled water and glycerin. Add licorice root powder and stir until the powder dissolves.
  4. Combine the melted beeswax mixture and the licorice mixture. Stir until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container, let it cool, and add tea tree oil. Store in a cool, dry place and use as needed for throat congestion relief.

4. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams contains thymol and carvacrol, two active compounds that help relieve throat congestion.

Thymol has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and irritation in the throat, making it easier to breathe and swallow. Carvacrol, on the other hand, has decongestant properties that help break down excess mucus and phlegm, clearing the airways and throat. The antimicrobial properties of thymol and carvacrol also help combat bacterial infections that can cause throat congestion.

By combining these properties, Thymus vulgaris creams provide a natural and effective way to alleviate throat congestion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh thyme leaves, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of shea butter.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a saucepan over low heat until it melts.
  3. Add the beeswax and shea butter to the saucepan and heat until melted.
  4. Add the fresh thyme leaves to the saucepan and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Strain the mixture and let it cool. Once cooled, transfer it to a jar and store it in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

5. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains menthol and eucalyptol as its active constituents.

Menthol helps to thin mucus in the throat, making it easier to expel. Eucalyptol has anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling in the throat and ease congestion. The menthol and eucalyptol in Mentha x piperita creams also have a decongestant effect, helping to open up airways and relieve breathing difficulties.

By thinning mucus and reducing inflammation, Mentha x piperita creams helps to alleviate throat congestion and promote easier breathing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 teaspoons of dried peppermint leaves.
  2. Melt the beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the dried peppermint leaves to the melted mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Add the distilled water to the mixture and stir well. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify. Once set, use a spatula to scoop out the Mentha x piperita cream and apply it to the throat as needed.

6. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, making them effective in reducing throat congestion. Gingerol, in particular, has been shown to thin mucus and make it easier to expel, while shogaol helps to reduce inflammation in the throat. The anti-inflammatory properties of Zingiber officinale creams also help to soothe a sore throat and reduce pain.

By reducing inflammation and making it easier to expel mucus, Zingiber officinale creams can provide relief from throat congestion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of Zingiber officinale essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and stir in the Zingiber officinale essential oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify before use.

7. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, borneol, and camphor, which are powerful constituents that help relieve throat congestion.

Rosmararinic acid has a natural anti-inflammatory effect, reducing swelling in the throat and making it easier to breathe. Borneol has a decongestant property, helping to clear mucus from the airways and loosen phlegm. Camphor has a soothing and calming effect, reducing coughing and discomfort in the throat.

These properties combined in Rosmarinus officinalis creams make it an effective remedy for throat congestion and respiratory issues.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, and 2 teaspoons of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.
  2. Combine the distilled water and dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves in a saucepan. Heat over low heat for 10-15 minutes, then strain the mixture into a bowl.
  3. Melt the beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter in a separate saucepan over low heat. Stir until smooth and combined.
  4. Add the melted oil mixture to the bowl with the Rosmarinus officinalis infusion. Stir until well combined.
  5. Add the olive oil and stir well. Pour the mixture into a container and let cool before use. Store in a cool, dry place.

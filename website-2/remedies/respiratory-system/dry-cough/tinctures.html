1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contains compounds like eucalyptol and bornyl acetate, which have expectorant properties.

This means they help break down and clear mucus from the lungs, making it easier to cough up. Eucalyptol also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling in the airways and make breathing easier. The decongestant properties of bornyl acetate help open up airways, allowing for easier airflow and relieving congestion.

By helping to clear mucus and reduce inflammation, Eucalyptus globulus tinctures can help relieve symptoms of a dry cough.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the dried Eucalyptus leaves to the jar and make sure they are completely covered by the vodka.
  3. Close the jar tightly and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Label the jar and store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 15-30 drops of the tincture in water or tea to relieve a dry cough.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and polysaccharides that help soothe a dry cough.

Alkylamides have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling in the throat and airways, making it easier to breathe and swallow. Caffeic acid derivatives have antimicrobial properties that fight off infections that can cause coughs. Polysaccharides stimulate the immune system to produce more mucus, which helps trap and remove irritants from the airways, relieving coughs and congestion.

This combination of properties makes Echinacea purpurea tinctures a useful natural remedy for dry coughs.

  1. Gather 250g of fresh or dried Echinacea purpurea roots and flowers. Clean them thoroughly.
  2. Combine the roots and flowers in a clean glass jar. Add 1 cup of 80% vodka or ethanol as a solvent.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and store it in a dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Use 20-30 drops, 3-4 times a day, to help soothe a dry cough.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dry cough from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe a dry cough.

Some herbal teas, like thyme and ginger, have natural anti-inflammatory properties that can calm the throat and reduce coughing. These teas can also help loosen mucus, making it easier to cough up.

Regularly drinking herbal tea may help ease dry cough symptoms.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dry cough every day?

Consuming herbal teas for dry cough every day can be safe if you choose teas made from gentle herbs like peppermint, chamomile, or licorice root.

However, some teas like slippery elm and marshmallow root can be too rich and may cause stomach issues if overused.

Be mindful of your body's response and adjust your consumption accordingly.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dry cough?

Herbal teas like thyme and ginger can help soothe a dry cough.

They work quickly, often providing relief within 30 minutes to an hour after drinking.

As you continue to drink them, their effects may last longer, potentially up to several hours.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for dry cough?

For a dry cough, drinking herbal tea in the evening is a good idea.

This is because your body naturally produces mucus at night, which can help soothe your throat.

Drinking herbal tea before bed may help your body produce more mucus, giving your throat some relief and allowing you to sleep better.

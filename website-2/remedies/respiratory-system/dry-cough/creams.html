1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains eucalyptol, cineole, and camphor as its main active constituents.

Eucalyptol helps to break down mucus, making it easier to cough up, while cineole has a soothing effect on the throat. Camphor has a decongestant property, which helps to reduce inflammation in the airways. These properties combined help to relieve a dry cough by reducing mucus and inflammation in the throat.

This makes Eucalyptus globulus creams a popular treatment for dry coughs.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 10 drops of eucalyptus oil, and 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and stir in eucalyptus oil and vitamin E oil. Pour the mixture into a heat-proof container.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify. Once it's solid, your eucalyptus globulus cream is ready to use. Store it in an airtight container and apply it to the chest or back to relieve dry cough.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains licorice root extract, which is rich in glycyrrhizin and flavonoids.

These bioactive compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that help to calm irritated throats and reduce coughing. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, has been shown to have expectorant properties, helping to loosen and clear mucus from the airways. Flavonoids, such as isoliquiritigenin and licoricidin, have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to reduce swelling and irritation in the throat.

By combining these properties, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams can help to relieve dry coughs and promote a smoother, more comfortable breathing experience.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Grind 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Combine ground root powder, coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Add vitamin E oil and mix well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let it cool completely. Once cooled, the cream is ready to use. Store it in a cool, dry place.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents such as alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds work together to soothe and calm the throat and respiratory system, providing relief from dry cough. The rosmarinic acid in Echinacea purpurea creams has anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce swelling and irritation in the throat, making it easier to breathe and swallow. The caffeic acid and alkylamides also have antimicrobial properties, which help to fight off infections that can cause dry cough.

By using Echinacea purpurea creams, you can help to alleviate dry cough symptoms and promote a faster recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Heat the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler until the beeswax melts.
  3. Remove from heat and add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers. Let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture and discard the flowers. Add the vitamin E oil and mix well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool. Once set, your Echinacea purpurea cream is ready for use.

4. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams contains thymol and carvacrol, which are the active constituents responsible for its effectiveness in treating a dry cough.

Thymol is a natural expectorant, meaning it helps loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to cough up and relieve congestion. Carvacrol has anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling in the throat and airways, making it easier to breathe and swallow. Thymol also has antimicrobial properties, which help fight off infections that can cause a dry cough.

By targeting the underlying causes of a dry cough, Thymus vulgaris creams can provide relief and help restore a healthy cough reflex.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Thymus vulgaris leaves, 2 cups of water, and a cheesecloth or a coffee filter.
  2. Combine the dried leaves and water in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth or coffee filter into a bowl.
  4. Combine 1/4 cup of the strained liquid with 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of coconut oil in a small saucepan.
  5. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring until the beeswax is melted. Let the mixture cool and solidify to form a cream.

5. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains a compound called gingerol, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

Gingerol helps to reduce swelling in the throat, making it easier to breathe and relieving a dry cough. Shogaol, another active constituent, has expectorant properties, meaning it helps to thin and clear mucus from the airways. The warming properties of Zingiber officinale creams also help to increase blood flow to the throat, reducing congestion and discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and promoting the clearance of mucus, Zingiber officinale creams can help to alleviate a dry cough.

  1. Mix 2 tablespoons of grated fresh ginger in 1 cup of coconut oil in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes, or until the oil is warm and fragrant.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter to the bowl and mix until combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a storage container and let it cool and solidify. Use as needed to soothe a dry cough.

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains menthol and menthone as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antitussive properties, which help to relieve dry cough by numbing the throat and reducing irritation. Menthol also has a cooling effect, which can provide temporary relief from coughing. The anti-inflammatory properties of menthone help to soothe the throat and reduce swelling, further alleviating dry cough symptoms.

By providing a soothing and numbing effect, Mentha x piperita creams can help to calm a dry cough.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Mentha x piperita essential oil (peppermint), 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove melted mixture from heat and let it cool for 5 minutes. Stir in Mentha x piperita essential oil and vitamin E oil.
  4. Pour the mixture into a silicone mold or a small tin. Let it cool and harden completely.
  5. Once hardened, remove the cream from the mold or tin and store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

7. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, camphor, and borneol as its active constituents.

These compounds have expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties that help relieve dry cough symptoms. Rosmararinic acid reduces mucus production, making it easier to cough up phlegm, while camphor helps to thin mucus, making it less difficult to expel. Borneol also has anti-inflammatory effects, which help to soothe the throat and reduce irritation.

By combining these properties, Rosmarinus officinalis creams can provide quick relief from dry cough symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of honey, 10 drops of peppermint essential oil, and 10 drops of rosemary essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second increments, stirring between each heating.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated.
  4. Add honey, peppermint essential oil, and rosemary essential oil to the mixture and stir until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a glass jar and let it cool and solidify before use. Apply the cream to the chest to help soothe a dry cough.

8. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its major bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, which help to reduce inflammation in the throat and lungs. The expectorant properties help to thin and loosen mucus, making it easier to cough up and relieve congestion. Additionally, linalool has a soothing effect on the respiratory system, calming the cough and making it more productive.

By reducing inflammation and loosening mucus, Lavandula angustifolia creams can help to alleviate dry cough symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/2 cup of beeswax in a double boiler.
  2. Heat the coconut oil and beeswax in the double boiler until the beeswax melts.
  3. Add the dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the melted oil and beeswax mixture and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discarding the flowers. Let it cool until solidified.
  5. Once solid, add 2 tablespoons of honey and mix well to create a smooth cream. Store in an airtight container.

9. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis creams contains bioactive constituents like carnosic acid and rosmarinic acid, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to soothe the throat and reduce inflammation, making it easier to cough up mucus and relieving dry cough symptoms. The antioxidants in Salvia officinalis creams, such as carnosic acid, also help to protect the throat from further irritation and damage. Additionally, the expectorant properties of rosmarinic acid help to thin and clear mucus, making it easier to cough up and relieving congestion.

By soothing and protecting the throat, Salvia officinalis creams can provide quick relief from dry cough symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a pot.
  2. Heat the water in the pot and add the Salvia officinalis leaves. Let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture and let it cool down. Use a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve to remove the leaves.
  4. Mix 1/4 cup of beeswax with 1/4 cup of coconut oil in a small saucepan over low heat.
  5. Combine the cooled Salvia officinalis mixture with the beeswax and coconut oil mixture. Stir until the mixture thickens and forms a cream.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dry cough from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe a dry cough.

Some herbal teas, like thyme and peppermint, have anti-inflammatory properties that can calm the throat. Others, like licorice root, can help thin mucus, making it easier to cough up.

These teas can provide quick relief, but their effectiveness may vary from person to person.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dry cough every day?

Herbal teas can be a soothing relief for dry coughs.

However, consuming them daily can have varying effects. Some teas, like peppermint and ginger, are generally considered safe in moderation. But others, like thyme and eucalyptus, contain compounds that might not be suitable for long-term use.

It's essential to listen to your body and adjust your intake accordingly.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dry cough?

Herbal teas can help with a dry cough, but the time it takes to feel better varies.

Some people notice relief after a few hours, while others may need to drink the tea for a few days.

It's common for symptoms to start easing within 24 to 48 hours, but this can depend on the individual and the specific herbal tea being used.

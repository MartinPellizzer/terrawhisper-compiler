How to Get Rid of Dry Cough: 8 Powerful Herbal Decoctions to Use

A dry cough can be a real nuisance, making it hard to sleep and even harder to breathe.

When you have a dry cough, your airways are inflamed and irritated, leading to a constant, hacking cough. To soothe a dry cough, you can make a herbal decoction, a mixture of herbs that are steeped in hot water to release their healing properties. Two herbs that are particularly effective in calming a dry cough are 'Glycyrrhiza glabra' (also known as licorice root) and 'Thymus vulgaris' (also known as thyme). Glycyrrhiza glabra is great at reducing inflammation in the airways and has anti-microbial properties that help fight off infections that can cause a cough.

Thymus vulgaris, on the other hand, has a soothing effect on the throat and has anti-inflammatory properties that can help calm a dry cough. Fixing the issue of a dry cough is important because it can disrupt your sleep, making you feel tired and irritable during the day. A dry cough can also lead to complications like bronchitis or pneumonia if left untreated. By using a herbal decoction made with Glycyrrhiza glabra and Thymus vulgaris, you can start to feel better and breathe easier.

Drinking the decoction regularly can help reduce the frequency and severity of your cough, making it easier to sleep and enjoy your daily activities.

What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have dry cough?

If you have a dry cough, it's essential to avoid herbal decoctions that can exacerbate the condition.

One such herb is Lobelia inflata, also known as Lobelia or Indian tobacco. Lobelia contains a compound called lobeline, which can dry out the airways and worsen a dry cough. Another herb to avoid is Ephedra sinica, also known as Ma Huang.

While it may provide temporary relief from congestion, it can also increase heart rate and blood pressure, making a dry cough worse. Additionally, the herb Guaiacum officinale, also known as Guaiac or Guaiac wood, can be toxic in large quantities and may irritate the respiratory tract, making a dry cough more uncomfortable. Lastly, the herb Ipecacuanha (Cephalis ipecacuanha) contains a compound called ipecac, which can cause stomach upset and worsen a dry cough.

It's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies to ensure your safety and well-being.

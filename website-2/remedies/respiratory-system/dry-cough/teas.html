10 best herbal teas for dry cough

1. Glycyrrhiza Glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tea, also known as licorice root tea, is a traditional remedy for dry cough. The tea is made from the root of the licorice plant, which contains compounds that help soothe and calm the throat. When you have a dry cough, the mucous membranes in your throat become dry and irritated, causing pain and discomfort. Glycyrrhiza glabra tea works by reducing inflammation and coating the throat with a protective layer, which helps to reduce the irritation and pain. This tea is also known to have antibacterial properties, which can help fight off any underlying infections that may be causing the cough. The anti-inflammatory properties of the tea also help to reduce swelling in the throat, making it easier to swallow and breathe. Additionally, Glycyrrhiza glabra tea can help to thin out mucus, making it easier to cough up and relieving congestion.

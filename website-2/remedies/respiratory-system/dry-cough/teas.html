8 Herbal Teas For Dry Cough

As people age, their bodies undergo many changes that can affect their health.

One common issue older adults face is a dry cough. This happens when the airways become dry and irritated, causing a persistent cough. Dry cough can be uncomfortable and disrupt daily life, making it hard to sleep, eat, and even breathe. It can also lead to fatigue, dehydration, and strain on the lungs and heart.

Older adults may be more prone to dry cough due to age-related changes in the airways, smoking, or underlying health conditions such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). A dry cough can also be a symptom of a more serious condition, like heart failure or pneumonia, so it's essential to address it promptly. Older adults may find it challenging to manage dry cough using conventional medications, which can have side effects or interact with other medications. This is where herbal teas come in. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to soothe and calm the airways, reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation.

By drinking herbal teas, older adults can help alleviate dry cough symptoms, improve sleep, and enhance overall well-being.

1. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas contains thymol, a compound that helps to soothe and calm dry coughs.

Thymol is a natural expectorant, which means it helps to loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, making it easier to breathe. The tea also contains other compounds like flavonoids and terpenes, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce the swelling in the throat and airways. To use Thymus vulgaris teas to help with dry cough, you can try brewing a cup of tea by steeping a teaspoon of dried thyme leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. You can drink this tea 2-3 times a day, or as needed.

You can also add honey to sweeten the tea and help to soothe the throat. As you start taking Thymus vulgaris teas, you may notice that your cough becomes less frequent and less severe. You may also notice that you can breathe more easily and that the mucus in your lungs is clearing more quickly. It's also possible that you may experience some relief from congestion and sinus pressure.

However, it's essential to note that Thymus vulgaris teas may interact with certain medications or worsen certain health conditions, so it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before using it as a treatment for dry cough.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which is known for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

When you have a dry cough, the airways in your throat can become irritated and inflamed, making it hard to swallow and breathe. Glycyrrhizin in Glycyrrhiza glabra teas helps to calm down this inflammation, reducing the irritation and discomfort caused by the dry cough. To try Glycyrrhiza glabra teas for a dry cough, you can start by steeping a teaspoon of dried roots in a cup of hot water for 5-10 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it three to four times a day.

You can also add a bit of honey to make it more palatable. When you drink Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, you may start to feel a reduction in the dryness and irritation in your throat. Your cough may become less frequent and less severe, and you might be able to swallow and breathe more easily. In some cases, people have reported relief from their dry cough within a few days of regular consumption.

However, if your dry cough persists or worsens, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper evaluation and treatment.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains compounds that may help alleviate dry cough symptoms.

This herb is rich in alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which are believed to stimulate the immune system and reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract. The root and flower extracts of Echinacea purpurea tea have been traditionally used to combat infections, such as the common cold and flu, which often cause dry cough. To try Echinacea purpurea tea for dry cough, you can brew a cup of dried root or flower tea by steeping 1 teaspoon of dried herb in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

You can also take it as a supplement in capsule or tincture form, following the recommended dosage on the label. However, if you're pregnant, breastfeeding, or have any underlying health conditions, consult with a healthcare professional before using Echinacea purpurea tea. Taking Echinacea purpurea tea may lead to a reduction in cough frequency and severity, as well as a decrease in mucus production. 4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe the throat and reduce inflammation.

This makes ginger tea a great remedy for dry cough, as it can help ease the irritation and discomfort caused by coughing. The volatile oils in ginger, such as zingiberene and bisabolol, have expectorant properties that help loosen mucus and promote its expulsion from the airways, making it easier to cough up. To try ginger tea for dry cough, steep 1-2 teaspoons of fresh ginger or 1 teaspoon of dried ginger in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain and drink while still warm, 2-3 times a day. You can also add honey to soothe the throat further. As you drink the tea, you may start to feel the soothing effects on your throat and the loosening of mucus, making it easier to cough up. You may also start to experience a reduction in the frequency and intensity of your coughing.

It's essential to note that ginger tea is not a substitute for medical treatment, and if your cough persists or worsens, you should consult a healthcare professional.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dry cough from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe a dry cough.

Certain herbs like licorice root and slippery elm have anti-inflammatory properties that can calm the throat and reduce irritation. Herbal teas like ginger and peppermint can also ease congestion and loosen mucus, making it easier to cough up.

This may provide some relief from a dry cough.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dry cough every day?

Herbal teas can be a soothing way to help with dry coughs.

However, drinking them every day can be a problem. Some herbs, like licorice root, can have too much of a good thing, causing blood pressure to rise or interfering with medications.

Moderation is key.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dry cough?

Herbal teas can help soothe a dry cough, but it may take some time to feel the effects.

Results can vary, but some people notice relief within a few days to a week after drinking the tea regularly.

It's best to try different types to see what works for you.

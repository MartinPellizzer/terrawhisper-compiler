Older adults and dry cough: 8 herbal teas to try

As people age, their bodies undergo many changes that can affect their health.

One common issue older adults face is a dry cough. This happens when the airways become dry and irritated, causing a persistent cough. Dry cough can be uncomfortable and disrupt daily life, making it hard to sleep, eat, and even breathe. It can also lead to fatigue, dehydration, and strain on the lungs and heart.

Older adults may be more prone to dry cough due to age-related changes in the airways, smoking, or underlying health conditions such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). A dry cough can also be a symptom of a more serious condition, like heart failure or pneumonia, so it's essential to address it promptly. Older adults may find it challenging to manage dry cough using conventional medications, which can have side effects or interact with other medications. This is where herbal teas come in. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to soothe and calm the airways, reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation.

By drinking herbal teas, older adults can help alleviate dry cough symptoms, improve sleep, and enhance overall well-being.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, also known as licorice root tea, have been traditionally used to help relieve dry cough.

The tea gets its benefits from the root's properties and constituents, including glycyrrhizin and flavonoids. Glycyrrhizin is known for its anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe an irritated throat and reduce inflammation, while flavonoids have antiviral and antimicrobial properties that can help fight off underlying infections. When you have a dry cough, drinking Glycyrrhiza glabra tea can help calm your throat and reduce the urge to cough.

To use Glycyrrhiza glabra tea for dry cough, you can steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day. You can also add honey to sweeten the tea and make it more palatable. As you take the tea, you may feel your throat start to relax and the urge to cough decrease.

You may also notice a reduction in the frequency and severity of coughing episodes.

2. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas, also known as thyme tea, can help relieve dry coughs due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The tea contains compounds like thymol, carvacrol, and rosmarinic acid, which have been shown to help combat bacterial and viral infections that can cause coughs. Thymol, in particular, has been found to have decongestant properties, which can help loosen mucus and reduce congestion in the chest. To try thyme tea for a dry cough, you can steep one teaspoon of dried thyme leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink.

You can also add honey to make it more palatable. It's best to drink thyme tea 2-3 times a day, especially before bedtime, to help you sleep better and reduce coughing. As you start drinking thyme tea, you may experience relief from dry cough within a few days. Your cough may become less frequent and less intense, and you may be able to sleep better without being woken up by coughing fits.

However, it's essential to note that if your cough persists or worsens, you should consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas can be a helpful remedy for a dry cough.

This is because Echinacea purpurea contains compounds like alkylamides and glycosides, which have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. These properties help reduce inflammation in the throat and fight off the underlying infection causing the cough. The tea also contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which act as antioxidants, protecting the body from damage caused by free radicals.

When taking Echinacea purpurea tea for a dry cough, it's recommended to drink 1-2 cups a day, 2-3 times a week, for a period of 2-4 weeks. It's also essential to stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water and getting plenty of rest to help your body recover. If your symptoms persist or worsen, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

As the infection is fought off and inflammation subsides, you can expect your dry cough to gradually improve, and your overall health to return to normal.

4. Verbascum thapsus

Verbascum thapsus teas, also known as mullein tea, has been used for centuries to help with dry coughs.

The key reason it's effective is due to its high content of saponins, flavonoids, and mucilages. These compounds work together to soothe and protect the mucous membranes in the throat, reducing inflammation and calming the cough. The mucilages, in particular, form a protective layer that traps moisture and helps to keep the throat hydrated, making it easier to cough up mucus. To get relief from a dry cough with Verbascum thapsus teas, start by making a strong tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried mullein leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day. You can also add honey to make it more palatable. If you're experiencing a severe or persistent cough, consult with your healthcare provider before using mullein tea. When taking Verbascum thapsus teas, you may experience a reduction in coughing, a decrease in inflammation, and an improvement in your overall respiratory health. Some people may also notice that they're able to sleep better due to the soothing effects of the tea.

However, if you experience any discomfort or allergic reactions, discontinue use and consult with your healthcare provider.

5. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, commonly known as ginger tea, have been used for centuries to alleviate dry cough.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, have potent anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties that help loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to cough up. The high concentration of antioxidants and flavonoids in ginger also helps to reduce inflammation and soothe the throat and bronchial tubes, providing relief from dry, itchy, and irritated coughs. To use ginger tea for dry cough, you can make a simple tea by steeping fresh ginger root in hot water for 5-7 minutes. You can also add honey and lemon to enhance the flavor and benefits.

It's recommended to drink 2-3 cups of ginger tea per day, especially before bedtime, to help clear out mucus and promote a restful sleep. Additionally, you can try gargling with warm ginger tea to further soothe the throat and reduce inflammation. As you start taking ginger tea for dry cough, you may notice a reduction in the frequency and severity of your coughing episodes. Your airways may become clearer, and you may experience a decrease in mucus production. You may also notice an improvement in your overall respiratory health, with reduced inflammation and irritation in the throat and bronchial tubes.

However, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using ginger tea as a treatment for dry cough, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or are taking medications.

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas, also known as peppermint tea, can help with dry cough due to its expectorant properties.

This means it helps loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to cough up. The tea contains menthone and menthol, which are responsible for its decongestant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds help reduce inflammation in the throat and lungs, making it less painful to cough. To try peppermint tea for a dry cough, start by making a warm tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried peppermint leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and add honey to taste, as the sweetness can help soothe the throat. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, ideally after meals or before bed, to help clear out mucus and reduce congestion. If you experience relief from your dry cough after trying peppermint tea, you may notice a decrease in coughing and a reduction in mucus production. Your airways may feel clearer, and you may be able to breathe more easily.

However, if your cough persists or worsens, or if you experience difficulty breathing, chest pain, or fever, seek medical attention.

7. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas, also known as rosemary tea, can help alleviate dry cough due to its expectorant properties.

The tea contains compounds like camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate, which help thin mucus and make it easier to cough up. This can provide relief from congestion and dryness in the throat. To use rosemary tea for dry cough, steep 1 teaspoon of dried rosemary leaves in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Let it cool, then strain and drink 2-3 cups a day. It's essential to note that pregnant women and people with high blood pressure or epilepsy should consult their doctor before using rosemary tea. When you take action and drink rosemary tea, you may experience a reduction in coughing, easier mucus expulsion, and improved breathing.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, consult a healthcare professional for further guidance.

8. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas have been traditionally used to help alleviate dry cough symptoms.

This is due to the presence of compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. These constituents help reduce inflammation in the throat and lungs, making it easier to cough up mucus and relieve congestion. Additionally, the tea's soothing effects can calm the throat and provide temporary pain relief. If you're experiencing a dry cough, you can try making a warm tea by steeping dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers in hot water. To do this, use one teaspoon of dried flowers for every cup of boiling water, and let it steep for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it while still warm.

You can also add honey or lemon to taste. It's essential to note that while Lavandula angustifolia teas may provide relief, they should not be used as a replacement for medical treatment. If your symptoms persist or worsen, consult with a healthcare professional. When taking Lavandula angustifolia teas for dry cough, you may experience a reduction in coughing fits and congestion. You may also notice that your throat feels soothed and less irritated. However, it's crucial to be aware that individual results may vary, and the tea's effectiveness may depend on the severity of your symptoms. As with any herbal remedy, it's also possible to experience side effects like allergic reactions or interactions with medications.

Always use caution and consult with a healthcare professional before making any changes to your treatment plan.

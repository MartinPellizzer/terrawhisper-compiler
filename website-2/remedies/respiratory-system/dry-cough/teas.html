1. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas contains thymol and carvacrol, two potent active constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to soothe a dry cough by reducing the inflammation in the throat and airways. Thymol's expectorant properties also help to loosen and clear mucus, making it easier to cough up and relieve congestion. Carvacrol's antiseptic properties help to combat any underlying infections that may be contributing to the cough.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off infections, Thymus vulgaris teas can help to alleviate the discomfort of a dry cough.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 tablespoon of dried Thymus vulgaris leaves.
  2. Measure 1 teaspoon of dried Thymus vulgaris leaves into a tea infuser or a small piece of cheesecloth.
  3. Steep the Thymus vulgaris leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the Thymus vulgaris leaves.
  5. Drink the Thymus vulgaris tea while it is still warm to help soothe a dry cough.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin, and flavonoids like isoliquertin and licoricidin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help to soothe and calm the throat, reducing irritation and discomfort associated with dry cough. Glycyrrhizin also has expectorant properties, which help to loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to cough up. Additionally, flavonoids in Glycyrrhiza glabra teas have antioxidant properties that protect the throat and airways from oxidative stress, further reducing inflammation and discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and loosening mucus, Glycyrrhiza glabra teas can provide relief from dry cough symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root powder and 1 cup of water.
  2. Boil the water in a pot and let it cool for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Add the Glycyrrhiza glabra root powder to the water and stir well.
  4. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then strain it into a cup.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve dry cough symptoms.

3. Echinacea purpurea

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation in the throat and airways, making it easier to cough up mucus. Gingerols and shogaols also have a soothing effect on the throat and lungs, helping to calm a dry cough. Additionally, Zingiber officinale teas has demulcent properties, which help create a protective barrier on the throat and airways, further reducing irritation and discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and creating a soothing barrier, Zingiber officinale teas helps alleviate the discomfort of a dry cough.

  1. Get 1 cup of water and boil it. Turn off the heat.

5. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains compounds like cineole, globulol, and alpha-pinene, which are known to have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.

These properties help break down mucus in the airways, making it easier to cough up. The cineole in eucalyptus globulus teas also has a decongestant effect, which helps relieve congestion and open up airways. The anti-inflammatory properties of the globulol and alpha-pinene help reduce swelling in the throat and airways, further relieving dry cough symptoms.

By combining these effects, eucalyptus globulus teas helps provide quick relief from dry cough.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves.
  2. Use a tea infuser or a saucepan to steep 1 tablespoon of leaves in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup using the tea infuser or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if needed, to soothe the throat and calm the cough.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve dry cough symptoms.

6. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra teas contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids that have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation in the throat and airways, making it easier to breathe and swallow. The flavonoids, especially rutin and quercetin, have antioxidant properties that fight free radicals and protect the body from oxidative stress. The terpenoids, including limonene and beta-pinene, have antitussive (cough-suppressing) effects that help alleviate dry cough symptoms.

By soothing the throat and reducing inflammation, Sambucus nigra teas can provide quick relief for dry cough.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Sambucus nigra flowers. If using fresh, dry them in a low oven (150°F - 200°F) for 2 hours.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of Sambucus nigra flowers and place them in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the flowers. If using a tea infuser, make sure it's fully submerged in the water.
  4. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea into another cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  5. Drink the tea hot or warm, up to 3 times a day, to help relieve a dry cough.

7. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas contains several bioactive constituents, including menthol, menthone, and limonene, which contribute to its effectiveness in treating dry cough.

Menthol, in particular, has a cooling effect on the throat, helping to relieve irritation and discomfort. The antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties of menthone also help to soothe the throat and reduce inflammation, making it easier to cough up mucus. The expectorant properties of limonene help to thin and loosen mucus, making it easier to expel and relieving congestion.

By combining these properties, Mentha x piperita teas helps to provide quick relief from dry cough symptoms.

  4. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a cup and discard the leaves.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve dry cough.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dry cough every day?

Herbal teas can be a soothing way to help with dry coughs.

Moderation is key.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dry cough?

Herbal teas can help soothe a dry cough, but it may take some time to feel the effects.

Results can vary, but some people notice relief within a few days to a week after drinking the tea regularly.

It's best to try different types to see what works for you.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for dry cough?

For a dry cough, it's best to drink herbal tea at night before bed.

This helps your body relax and recover while you sleep, which is especially important when you're not feeling well.

Your body can focus on healing without being distracted by daily activities.

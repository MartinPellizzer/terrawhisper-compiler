8 Herbal Essential Oils For Dry Cough

Herbal essential oils can be a game-changer for people suffering from dry cough.

These oils are usually extracted from plants and can help soothe and calm your throat. One of the reasons herbal teas and essential oils are effective in relieving dry cough is because they have anti-inflammatory properties. This means they can help reduce swelling and ease congestion in your airways, making it easier to breathe and swallow. Let's take a look at a few examples of herbs that can be used to relieve dry cough.

Eucalyptus globulus, also known as eucalyptus, is a popular choice for its decongestant properties. It helps open up your airways, making it easier to breathe and relieving congestion. Echinacea purpurea, or coneflower, is another herb that's often used to boost your immune system and fight off infections that can cause dry cough. Thymus vulgaris, or thyme, has antibacterial properties that can help fight off infections and reduce inflammation in your throat.

When you use these herbs in the form of essential oils, you can experience the benefits of a soothing and calming throat, making it easier to get a good night's sleep and feel more energized during the day.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole, limonene, and pinene as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to relieve a dry cough by reducing congestion and inflammation in the airways. Cineole, in particular, is known to thin mucus and make it easier to expel, providing quick relief from a dry cough. The expectorant properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils also help to loosen and clear out mucus and debris from the lungs, further reducing coughing.

By reducing congestion and inflammation, and helping to clear out mucus, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can provide effective relief from a dry cough.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat until it boils, then reduce heat to low.
  3. Add the Eucalyptus globulus leaves to the boiling water and let it simmer for 30-40 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid into a bowl using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve, and discard the leaves.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 2-3 hours to allow the essential oil to separate and rise to the top.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in the throat and airways, making it easier to breathe and cough up mucus. The alkaloids in Echinacea purpurea, such as echinacoside and isoquinoline alkaloids, have antimicrobial properties that combat bacterial and viral infections that can cause a dry cough. The essential oil also contains flavonoids like kaempferol and quercetin, which have antioxidant properties that help soothe and protect the mucous membranes from irritation.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off infections, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help alleviate a dry cough and promote a healthy respiratory system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil.
  2. Wash the flowers in cold water, then pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Combine the dried flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Store in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the flowers.
  5. Bottle the resulting essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a diffuser or mix with water for a cough remedy.

3. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol, carvacrol, and borneol as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties that help loosen and clear mucus from the respiratory tract. Thymol, in particular, is known for its ability to thin mucus and make it easier to cough up, providing relief from dry coughs. Carvacrol has antimicrobial properties that help combat underlying infections that can cause dry coughs.

By incorporating Thymus vulgaris essential oils into a treatment plan, its bioactive constituents can work together to help alleviate dry cough symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and flowers. Clean them with water.
  2. Use a glass jar with a lid. Add 1 cup of the cleaned Thymus vulgaris leaves and flowers to the jar.
  3. Pour 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) over the Thymus vulgaris leaves and flowers in the jar.
  4. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day. This is called infusing.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another jar. Discard the solids. Your Thymus vulgaris essential oil is now ready to use.

4. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains compounds like menthone, menthol, and limonene, which have natural decongestant properties.

These properties help to loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to cough up, and providing relief from a dry cough. The menthol in Mentha x piperita essential oils also has a cooling effect, which can help to soothe an irritated throat. Additionally, the antibacterial properties of menthone and limonene can help to prevent the growth of bacteria that can exacerbate a dry cough.

By using Mentha x piperita essential oils, you can help to ease the discomfort of a dry cough and promote a sense of well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh mint leaves (Mentha x piperita) and clean them with water.
  2. Place the mint leaves in a glass jar and add 2 cups of water. Let it sit for 2 hours to allow the oil to release.
  3. Heat the mixture in a double boiler or a saucepan with a lid. Bring it to a boil then reduce heat to low.
  4. Simmer the mixture for 1-2 hours, or until the water has reduced to 1/4 cup and the oil has separated from the water.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains camphor, borneol, and 1,8-cineole, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

The camphor and borneol in rosemary oil have expectorant properties, helping to loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, making it easier to cough up. The 1,8-cineole, also known as eucalyptol, has anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce congestion and ease a dry cough. The antispasmodic properties of rosemary oil also help to relax the muscles in the throat and chest, making it easier to cough and clear mucus.

By using rosemary essential oils, people with dry cough may experience relief from congestion and discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves, 2 cups of water, and a steam distillation apparatus.
  2. Wash the leaves with 1 cup of water to remove dirt and impurities. Let them dry completely.
  3. Combine the dried leaves with 2 cups of water in the steam distillation apparatus. Heat the mixture until steam is released.
  4. Collect the distilled steam in a separate container for 2 hours. This will yield the essential oil.
  5. Strain the essential oil from the collected steam and store it in a dark glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser for relief from dry cough.

6. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Gingerol has anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, making it helpful in relieving dry coughs by reducing inflammation in the airways and promoting the expulsion of mucus. The essential oil also contains a compound called zingiberene, which has a bronchodilatory effect, helping to widen the airways and make breathing easier. The combination of these properties in Zingiber officinale essential oils makes it a useful remedy for dry coughs, as it helps to relieve congestion and promote a smoother flow of air.

By reducing inflammation and promoting the expulsion of mucus, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help to alleviate the symptoms of dry cough.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger roots (Zingiber officinale) and a clean glass jar.
  2. Wash the ginger roots with water, then pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Cut the ginger roots into small pieces (about 1 inch long) and place them in the glass jar.
  4. Pour 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) over the ginger pieces, making sure they are completely covered.
  5. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the oil infuse.

7. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and terpenoids.

These bioactive constituents help soothe a dry cough by reducing inflammation in the throat and airways. Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory properties that calm irritated tissues, while flavonoids and terpenoids work together to ease congestion and promote a productive cough. The expectorant properties of these compounds help loosen mucus, making it easier to cough up and relieve a dry, persistent cough.

By combining these properties, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can provide fast and effective relief from dry cough symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots, a glass jar with a lid, a carrier oil like coconut oil, and a steam distillation kit or a simple distiller.
  2. Crush the dried roots into small pieces using a mortar and pestle. This helps release the oil from the roots.
  3. Combine 1 cup of the crushed roots with 2 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar. Mix well and let it sit for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2 weeks, use the steam distillation kit or simple distiller to extract the essential oil from the mixture. This process will take around 2-3 hours.
  5. Filter the extracted oil using a coffee filter or cheesecloth to remove any impurities. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use it to relieve dry cough.

8. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help to soothe and calm the respiratory tract. The antispasmodic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate help to relax the muscles in the throat and airways, reducing coughing spasms and providing relief from dry cough. The anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds also help to reduce inflammation in the throat and airways, further alleviating dry cough symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the muscles in the respiratory tract, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can provide effective relief from dry cough.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Cover the jar with a lid.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container.
  5. Discard the flowers and store the extracted essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dry cough from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe a dry cough.

Some herbs like thyme and licorice root have anti-inflammatory properties that can calm the throat and reduce irritation. Ginger tea is also known to help ease congestion and coughs.

These teas can provide relief from dry cough symptoms, but their effectiveness may vary from person to person.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dry cough every day?

Herbal teas can be a soothing relief for dry coughs, but it's essential to use them in moderation.

Drinking them every day might not be safe, as some herbs can interact with medications or worsen underlying conditions.

It's best to limit your intake to a few times a week and monitor your body's response.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dry cough?

Herbal teas can help with a dry cough, but it takes time to see results.

Some people notice relief within a few hours or overnight, while others may need to drink the tea for a few days to feel better.

The timing depends on the type of tea and the severity of the cough.

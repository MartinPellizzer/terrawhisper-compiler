1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains a compound called eucalyptol, which is responsible for its decongestant properties.

Eucalyptol helps to break down mucus and reduce inflammation in the ear canal, making it easier for the body to drain out excess fluid. This eucalyptol works by opening up the airways and allowing air to flow more freely, which helps to relieve ear blockage. Additionally, eucalyptus globulus creams contain cineole, another compound that has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, which helps to soothe and heal the ear canal.

When applied to the ear, these compounds work together to provide relief from ear blockage and discomfort.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Eucalyptus globulus essential oil, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove melted mixture from heat and add Eucalyptus globulus essential oil and vitamin E oil. Stir well.
  4. Pour the mixture into small tin containers or lip balm tubes. Let it cool and solidify.
  5. Your Eucalyptus globulus creams are now ready to use. Apply a small amount to the affected ear to help relieve blockage.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains compounds like cineole and terpinen-4-ol.

These bioactive constituents have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and fight off infections in the ear. The creams also contain flavonoids, like quercetin, which have antioxidant properties that help protect the delicate skin in the ear canal. The anti-inflammatory properties of the compounds in Melaleuca alternifolia creams can help ease discomfort and reduce pain associated with ear blockage.

By soothing the ear canal and reducing inflammation, these creams can help restore normal hearing and ear function.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water and 1/4 cup of beeswax in a saucepan.
  2. Melt the beeswax in the saucepan over low heat, then add 1 tablespoon of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and add 1 cup of distilled water, stirring until the mixture cools.
  4. Add 1/4 cup of a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil, and stir until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify before applying to affected ear area.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two potent essential oils.

  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Once melted, add the shea butter and stir until it's fully incorporated and the mixture is smooth.

  3. Remove the mixture from heat and stir in honey until fully incorporated.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains essential oils that help with ear blockage.

  2. Heat the distilled water in a saucepan over low heat. Add the rosemary leaves and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the water and discard the rosemary leaves. In a separate bowl, mix the shea butter, coconut oil, and beeswax until well combined.
  4. Add the infused water to the bowl with the shea butter mixture and stir until smooth. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool.
  5. Once the mixture has cooled and solidified, use a spoon to scoop out the desired amount and apply it to the affected ear. Massage gently and leave it on for 10-15 minutes before removing.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains compounds like eucalyptol, menthol, and camphor.

These constituents help to thin and loosen mucus, making it easier to drain from the Eustachian tubes and clear ear blockage. Eucalyptol, in particular, has potent decongestant properties, reducing swelling in the nasal passages and sinuses, which can help to alleviate ear pressure and congestion. The menthol and camphor in eucalyptus globulus teas also have a cooling effect on the skin, helping to reduce inflammation and ease discomfort in the affected area.

By using eucalyptus globulus teas, some people find relief from ear blockage and sinus pressure.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water, 1 tablespoon of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves, and a tea strainer.
  2. Place the tea strainer over a cup and add the dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the leaves and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a separate cup and discard the leaves.
  5. Use the tea as an ear drop by pouring a few drops into the affected ear, or drink it as a hot tea to help relieve ear blockage.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides and caffeic acid derivatives.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling in the ears. Additionally, the caffeic acid derivatives in Echinacea purpurea tea have antioxidant properties that can help protect the delicate tissues in the ears from damage.

By reducing inflammation and protecting ear tissues, Echinacea purpurea tea may provide relief from ear blockage.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers or 1 tablespoon of dried flowers.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.

  4. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then strain it into another cup.
  5. Drink the tea warm 2-3 times a day for ear blockage relief.

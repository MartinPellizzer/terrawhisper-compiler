Emphysema Causes, Symptoms, and Herbal Relief Options

What are the causes of emphysema?

There are several main causes of emphysema, a serious lung disease.

Genetics play a role, as some people may be born with a higher risk of developing the condition due to their family history. Smoking is another major cause - it damages the lungs and makes them lose their ability to breathe. If you have a family history of emphysema and you smoke, your risk of developing the condition is even higher.

Some people have Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency, a rare genetic disorder that affects the lungs and can cause emphysema. As we age, our lungs naturally become less efficient, which can lead to emphysema. People who have chronic bronchitis, a long-term inflammation of the airways, are also at a higher risk of developing emphysema.

This is because chronic bronchitis can cause permanent damage to the lungs, making it harder for them to breathe.

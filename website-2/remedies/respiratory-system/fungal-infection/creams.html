1. Aloe vera

Aloe vera creams contains active compounds like aloin, aloe-emodin, and anthraquinones that have antifungal properties.

These compounds work by inhibiting the growth of fungal cells and reducing inflammation. The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of aloe vera help to soothe and calm the skin, reducing the symptoms of fungal infections. The anthraquinones in aloe vera also have a drying effect on fungal spores, making it difficult for them to spread and multiply.

By reducing the growth of fungal cells and soothing the skin, aloe vera creams can help to alleviate symptoms and promote healing in individuals with fungal infections.

  1. Gather 1 cup of aloe vera gel from a fresh aloe leaf. Cut off the tip and scoop out the clear gel.
  2. Mix 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of shea butter in a saucepan.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring until the beeswax and shea butter melt.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of tea tree oil and 2 tablespoons of lavender oil to the mixture. Stir well.
  5. Allow the mixture to cool, then whip it until it thickens. Pour into a container and apply to affected area.

2. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains compounds like cineole, terpinen-4-ol, and limonene, which have been shown to have antifungal properties.

These properties help to inhibit the growth of fungal cells and prevent them from causing infections. The terpinen-4-ol in Melaleuca alternifolia creams is particularly effective at reducing the formation of fungal biofilms, which are complex communities of fungal cells that can make infections more difficult to treat. The antifungal properties of Melaleuca alternifolia creams also help to reduce inflammation and promote healing in the skin, making it a useful treatment for fungal infections like athlete's foot and ringworm.

By using Melaleuca alternifolia creams, you can create an environment that is not conducive to fungal growth, helping to clear up infections and promote healthy skin.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil, and a double boiler.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in the double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add shea butter and stir until melted.
  4. Remove from heat and add Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil. Stir well.
  5. Pour mixture into a clean container and let it cool and solidify before use.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains compounds like cineole, globulol, and borneol, which have been shown to have antifungal properties.

These compounds work by inhibiting the growth of fungi and bacteria, helping to reduce the severity of fungal infections. Cineole, in particular, has been found to disrupt the cell membranes of fungi, ultimately leading to their death. Globulol and borneol have also been found to have antiseptic properties, helping to prevent further infection.

By using these compounds, eucalyptus globulus creams can help to promote healing and reduce the risk of complications from fungal infections.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, and 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the beeswax and coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Once melted, add 1 teaspoon of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves to the mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the Eucalyptus leaves.
  5. Add the distilled water and vitamin E oil to the mixture and stir well. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool before use.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains compounds like linalool, linalyl acetate, and camphor, which have antifungal properties.

These properties help to inhibit the growth of fungi and reduce their spread. Linalool, in particular, has been shown to have a significant impact on fungal cells, causing them to die and preventing further infection. The antiseptic properties of camphor also contribute to the cream's ability to combat fungal infections by reducing bacterial loads and creating an environment that is less conducive to fungal growth.

This makes Lavandula angustifolia creams a valuable tool in the treatment of fungal infections, particularly those affecting the skin.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of distilled water, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 20 drops of Lavandula angustifolia essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Combine 2 cups of distilled water and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil in a separate bowl. Stir well.
  4. Once the coconut oil and beeswax mixture has cooled slightly, add the distilled water mixture and stir until well combined.
  5. Add 20 drops of Lavandula angustifolia essential oil and mix well. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool before use.

5. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains sesquiterpene lactones, particularly triterpene acids, and carotenoids like lycopene.

These compounds have antifungal properties that help combat fungal infections. The sesquiterpene lactones in Calendula officinalis creams have been shown to inhibit the growth of fungi by disrupting cell membranes and interfering with fungal enzyme systems. The carotenoids, particularly lycopene, have antioxidant properties that help reduce inflammation and promote wound healing.

By combining antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties, Calendula officinalis creams can help treat fungal infections and promote healthy skin.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Calendula flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Dry the Calendula flowers in a warm place or using a dehydrator. Once dry, grind them into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. In a double boiler, melt the coconut oil and beeswax over low heat. Stir occasionally until fully melted.
  4. Add the Calendula powder, shea butter, and vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  5. Remove from heat and let cool slightly. Pour into a container and let cool completely before use. Store in a cool, dry place.

6. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams contains thymol and carvacrol, two active compounds that make it effective against fungal infections.

Thymol has antifungal and antibacterial properties that help reduce the growth of fungi and bacteria on the skin. Carvacrol has antiseptic and antifungal properties that help fight fungal infections and promote a healthy skin environment. Thymus vulgaris creams can help soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing redness and inflammation caused by fungal infections.

The antifungal properties of thymol and carvacrol in Thymus vulgaris creams help combat fungal infections, such as athlete's foot and nail fungus.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 20 drops of Thymus vulgaris essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Once melted, remove the bowl from the heat and let it cool slightly.
  4. Add the distilled water and Thymus vulgaris essential oil to the melted mixture. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean glass jar and let it cool completely before applying to affected areas.

7. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and polyphenols.

These compounds have antifungal properties that help combat fungal infections. Caffeic acid derivatives, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the growth of fungal cells and reduce inflammation. The polyphenols in Echinacea purpurea creams also exhibit antioxidant properties, which can help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals.

This combination of antifungal and antioxidant properties makes Echinacea purpurea creams a potentially effective treatment for fungal infections.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of lavender oil.
  2. Dry the Echinacea flowers in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 2 hours. Then, let them cool.
  3. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler. Stir until the beeswax is fully melted.
  4. Add the dried Echinacea flowers, vitamin E oil, and lavender oil to the melted oil mixture. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, use as a cream for fungal infections.

8. Origanum vulgare

Origanum vulgare creams contains carvacrol and thymol, two active constituents that have antifungal properties.

These compounds help to inhibit the growth of fungal cells, reducing the severity of infections. Carvacrol's antimicrobial action disrupts the fungal cell membrane, while thymol's antiseptic properties help to prevent infection from spreading. The antifungal properties of Origanum vulgare creams also help to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing.

By targeting fungal cells and reducing inflammation, Origanum vulgare creams can be an effective treatment for fungal infections.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Origanum vulgare leaves and 2 cups of coconut oil in a clean bowl.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a saucepan over low heat for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the dried Origanum vulgare leaves to the coconut oil and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another bowl, discarding the leaves.
  5. Let the infused oil cool, then mix in 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil to create a thick and creamy texture.

9. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, camphor, and thymol as its active constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and antifungal properties that help combat fungal infections. Rosmarinic acid inhibits the growth of fungi by interfering with their cell membranes, while camphor disrupts the fungal cell wall, ultimately leading to their death. Thymol, on the other hand, has a direct antifungal effect, killing fungal cells on contact.

By combining these properties, Salvia officinalis creams can help alleviate fungal infections, promoting a healthy and balanced skin environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves, 1 cup of coconut oil, and a double boiler.
  2. Chop the leaves finely and put them in the double boiler.
  3. Melt the coconut oil in the double boiler and mix with the chopped leaves. Heat for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify. Store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

10. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation and fight fungal infections by inhibiting the growth of fungi and bacteria. The antioxidant properties of gingerol and shogaol also help to neutralize free radicals, which can damage skin cells and worsen fungal infections. The antifungal properties of Zingiber officinale creams have been shown to be effective against various types of fungal infections, including athlete's foot and ringworm.

By applying Zingiber officinale creams to the affected area, it can help to soothe and heal the skin, reducing the symptoms of fungal infections.

11 herbal teas to help athletes recover from fungal infection

Athletes are more likely to get fungal infections, like athlete's foot or jock itch, because they often wear tight shoes and clothes that trap moisture, creating a warm and humid environment that fungi love.

This can happen after a long workout, when their feet are sweaty, or when they share personal items with others. Fungal infections can be painful, itchy, and embarrassing, and they can make it hard for athletes to train and compete at their best. They may need to take time off from their sport to treat the infection, which can be frustrating and affect their mental and physical well-being.

In severe cases, fungal infections can lead to skin irritation, blisters, and even secondary infections. Fungal infections can also spread to other parts of the body, like the groin, buttocks, or thighs, and they can be contagious, so athletes may need to avoid close contact with their teammates and family members. To make matters worse, some fungal infections can be resistant to antibiotics, which means athletes may need to try different treatments to get rid of them. Herbal teas can help athletes treat fungal infections by providing antifungal properties that can help kill the fungus and soothe the affected skin.

These teas are often made from plants that have been used for centuries to treat various health problems, and they can be a natural and gentle way to treat fungal infections without harsh chemicals.

1. Artemisia absinthium

Artemisia absinthium teas have been traditionally used to help combat fungal infections, particularly those affecting the skin and nails.

This is due to the presence of thujone, a compound known to exhibit antifungal properties. Thujone also possesses antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce redness and swelling associated with fungal infections. Additionally, Artemisia absinthium teas contain other constituents such as camphor, borneol, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to their antifungal effects. To use Artemisia absinthium teas for fungal infections, start by making a tea by steeping 1 teaspoon of dried leaves in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and discard the solids. Soak a clean cloth in the cooled tea and apply it directly to the affected area.

You can also drink the tea, but be sure to dilute it with water first, as the thujone can be quite potent. For skin infections, apply the tea-soaked cloth 2-3 times a day, and for nail infections, try soaking your finger or toe in the tea for 10-15 minutes, 2-3 times a week. When using Artemisia absinthium teas, you may experience a reduction in symptoms such as itching, redness, and swelling. The antifungal properties of the tea may also help to slow down or stop the growth of fungal spores. However, it's essential to note that fungal infections can be stubborn, and it may take some time to see noticeable improvements. Also, be cautious when consuming Artemisia absinthium teas, as excessive thujone consumption can cause side effects such as headaches and dizziness.

Be sure to consult with a healthcare professional before using Artemisia absinthium teas, especially if you're pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking medications.

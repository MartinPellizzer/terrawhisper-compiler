What Are The Main Causes Of Post Nasal Drip?

Post nasal drip, also known as upper airway secretions, occurs when mucus from the nasal passages drips down the back of the throat.

There are several causes of post nasal drip. Allergies can trigger post nasal drip when the body reacts to particles in the air, such as pollen, dust, or pet dander. When the immune system fights off these invaders, it releases excess mucus to trap them, leading to the dripping sensation. Sinusitis, an inflammation of the sinus cavities, can also cause post nasal drip.

When the sinuses become infected or inflamed, they produce more mucus, which can then flow down the back of the throat. This can be due to a viral or bacterial infection, or even a fungal infection. A cold can also lead to post nasal drip. When we catch a cold, our nasal passages produce excess mucus to trap the invading virus.

This mucus can then drip down the back of the throat, causing discomfort and irritation.

Below are the main causes of post nasal drip.

  • Allergies: When you have an allergy, your body overreacts to a substance like pollen or dust, causing your nose to produce extra mucus, which then drips down the back of your throat, leading to post nasal drip.
  • Sinusitis: Sinusitis, an inflammation of the sinuses, causes post nasal drip because the swollen sinuses and nasal passages can't drain properly, leading to excess mucus flowing down the back of the throat.
  • Cold: When you catch a cold, your nasal passages become inflamed and produce excess mucus, which then drips down the back of your throat due to gravity and the body's natural attempt to clear out the infection.
  • Rhinitis: Rhinitis, which is an inflammation of the nasal passages, causes postnasal drip by producing excess mucus that drips down the back of the throat.
  • Cystic fibrosis: In people with Cystic fibrosis, thick mucus builds up in the lungs and airways, including those in the nose, causing it to drip out of the nostrils as a runny or sticky liquid.
  • Vasomotor rhinitis: Vasomotor rhinitis causes post nasal drip because the tiny blood vessels in the nasal lining, which control the flow of blood and the size of the nasal passages, become overly sensitive and swell, leading to an excessive production of mucus that drips down the back of the throat.
  • Gastroesophageal reflux: Gastroesophageal reflux causes post nasal drip because the stomach acid flows up into the throat and irritates the nasal passages, producing excess mucus that drips down.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Post Nasal Drip?

If you have post nasal drip, you should avoid certain herbs that can irritate your sinuses or make the problem worse.

One herb to steer clear of is Peppermint (Mentha piperita), as its strong menthol can thin mucus, making it harder to clear from your sinuses. Another herb to limit is Sage (Salvia officinalis), which can dry out your nasal passages and make the mucus thicker.

Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) is also not recommended as it can cause your body to retain water, leading to increased mucus production. Additionally, Wild Indigo (Baptisia tinctoria) may not be suitable due to its potential to increase mucus production, making post nasal drip worse.

Finally, Oregon Grape (Mahonia aquifolium) root can irritate the sinuses and make congestion worse.

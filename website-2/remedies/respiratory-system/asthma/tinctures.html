What herbal tinctures you should avoid if you have asthma?

If you have asthma, some herbal tinctures may worsen your symptoms or interact with your medication.

You should avoid tinctures made from comfrey (Symphytum officinale), as it contains a compound that can cause inflammation in the lungs and worsen asthma. Also, avoid tinctures made from lobelia (Lobelia inflata), as it can cause airway constriction, making breathing even harder.

Tinctures made from goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) and Oregon grape (Berberis aquifolium) contain berberine, which can slow down the heart rate and lower blood pressure, potentially interacting with asthma medications. Another herb to avoid is licorice root (Glycyrrhiza uralensis, not the one I mentioned earlier, but a different species), as it can raise blood pressure and worsen asthma symptoms.

Finally, avoid tinctures made from sage (Salvia officinalis), as it can dry out the mucous membranes in the lungs, making it harder to breathe.

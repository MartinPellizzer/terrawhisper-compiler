7 herbal teas that help kids with asthma

Asthma is a common problem that affects many children.

It's a condition where the airways in the lungs become narrow and swollen, making it hard for the child to breathe. This can be very scary for kids, and it affects their daily lives in many ways. They might need to carry an inhaler with them at all times, and they might have to avoid playing outside or engaging in physical activities because of their symptoms. Sometimes, asthma attacks can be triggered by things like pollen, dust, or pet dander, which can be found almost everywhere. This means that kids with asthma need to be very careful about what they do and where they go. Asthma can also make it hard for kids to sleep, concentrate, and even enjoy simple things like playing with friends.

It's not just a physical problem, but also an emotional one, as kids with asthma might feel anxious or worried about having an attack. But there's some good news - there are many ways to help manage asthma, including using herbal teas. These teas are made from plants that have been used for centuries to help people breathe more easily and feel better. They work by helping to open up the airways in the lungs and reducing inflammation. Some people find that drinking these teas helps to reduce their symptoms and makes it easier to breathe. This can be especially helpful for kids who are sensitive to medication or prefer a more natural approach to managing their asthma.

By drinking herbal teas, kids might be able to breathe more easily, sleep better, and feel more confident and happy.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas have been used by some people to help manage asthma symptoms.

The teas contain properties like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkylamides, which may help reduce inflammation and fight off infections that can trigger asthma attacks. These compounds may also help relax airway muscles and improve lung function. When using Echinacea purpurea teas for asthma, it's recommended to brew a cup as you would with any herbal tea, then drink it 2-3 times a day.

You can also add honey or lemon to taste. Some people have reported that their asthma symptoms improve after a few weeks of regular consumption, with noticeable reductions in wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath. However, it's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea teas are not a substitute for medical treatment, and you should consult your doctor before making any changes to your asthma management plan. You may need to adjust your medication or treatment plan while using the teas to ensure your safety and effectiveness.

It's also crucial to monitor your symptoms closely and seek medical attention if they worsen or persist.

2. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas, also known as thyme tea, may help with asthma symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Thyme contains compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which have been shown to reduce inflammation in the airways and kill bacteria that can exacerbate asthma. When inhaled, thymol can also help relax the muscles in the airways, making it easier to breathe. To try thyme tea for asthma, you can make a warm tea by steeping a teaspoon of dried thyme in a cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day, especially before bedtime.

You can also add honey to sweeten the tea if you prefer. However, if your asthma is severe or you're taking medications, consult your doctor before trying thyme tea. Taking thyme tea for asthma may lead to reduced inflammation, improved breathing, and fewer asthma attacks. Some people may also experience relief from coughing and congestion.

However, it's essential to note that thyme tea may not replace conventional asthma treatments, and you should continue to follow your doctor's advice and use your medication as prescribed.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas, also known as rosemary tea, have been traditionally used to help alleviate asthma symptoms due to their anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties.

The tea contains compounds such as rosmarinic acid, carnosic acid, and camphor, which have been shown to relax the airways, reduce inflammation, and improve lung function. These properties can help open up the airways, making it easier to breathe and reducing wheezing and coughing. To use rosemary tea for asthma, you can steep 1 teaspoon of dried rosemary leaves in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink 2-3 cups a day.

It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using rosemary tea, especially if you're taking any medications or have underlying health conditions. When taken correctly, rosemary tea may help reduce asthma symptoms, improve quality of life, and reduce the need for medication. Some people may experience an improvement in lung function, reduced frequency of asthma attacks, and a decrease in the use of rescue inhalers.

However, individual results may vary, and it's crucial to work with a healthcare provider to monitor progress and adjust treatment as needed.

4. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas have been used to help alleviate asthma symptoms for centuries.

This is because the tea contains compounds like eucalyptol, a natural decongestant, and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties. These properties help to ease congestion in the airways, reduce inflammation, and relax the muscles in the lungs, making it easier to breathe. When you drink Eucalyptus globulus tea, the eucalyptol is released into your system, where it can help to break down and clear out mucus from your airways, allowing for better airflow and reduced wheezing. If you're experiencing asthma symptoms and want to try Eucalyptus globulus tea, here's what you can do: start by brewing a cup of the tea according to the package instructions, using one teaspoon of dried leaves per cup of boiling water. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink.

You can repeat this process every few hours as needed. It's also a good idea to talk to your doctor before adding any new remedies to your treatment plan, especially if you're already taking medications for your asthma. When you take action and drink Eucalyptus globulus tea, you may start to notice improvements in your breathing within a few hours. Your airways may feel clearer, and you may be able to breathe more easily. You may also find that your wheezing and coughing decrease, and you're able to perform daily activities without feeling as short of breath.

However, it's essential to remember that Eucalyptus globulus tea is not a replacement for medical treatment, and you should continue to follow your doctor's advice and take your prescribed medications as directed.

5. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis teas, also known as lemon balm tea, has been traditionally used to help alleviate asthma symptoms.

The tea's active compounds, rosmarinic acid and flavonoids, have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that may help reduce inflammation in the airways and improve lung function. The rosmarinic acid in Melissa officinalis tea has been shown to inhibit the release of histamine, a chemical that can trigger asthma symptoms, making it a potential natural remedy for asthma relief. If you're considering trying Melissa officinalis tea for asthma, start by drinking 1-2 cups a day, ideally before bedtime, as the tea's calming effects may help reduce nighttime asthma symptoms.

You can make the tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried Melissa officinalis leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Be sure to consult with your healthcare provider before making any changes to your treatment plan, as Melissa officinalis tea may interact with certain medications or worsen symptoms in some individuals. As you start drinking Melissa officinalis tea, you may notice a reduction in asthma symptoms, such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath. You may also experience improved lung function and a decrease in the frequency and severity of asthma attacks.

However, it's essential to note that Melissa officinalis tea is not a replacement for conventional asthma treatment, and you should continue to follow your prescribed treatment plan and work closely with your healthcare provider to monitor your symptoms and adjust your treatment as needed.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas, also known as English lavender tea, have been traditionally used to help manage asthma symptoms.

This is due to the presence of linalool and linalyl acetate, two compounds found in the tea that have anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties. These properties help to reduce inflammation in the airways and relax the muscles surrounding the airways, making it easier to breathe. When you take Lavandula angustifolia teas, it's recommended to start with a small dose and gradually increase it to your tolerance level.

You can drink 1-2 cups of the tea per day, preferably before bedtime, to help you relax and reduce symptoms. As you take the tea, you may notice a reduction in wheezing, shortness of breath, and coughing. You may also find it easier to sleep and wake up feeling more refreshed.

Keep in mind that Lavandula angustifolia teas are not a substitute for conventional asthma treatment, and you should continue to follow your doctor's advice and take your prescribed medication as directed.

7. Pelargonium sidoides

Pelargonium sidoides teas have been used to help alleviate asthma symptoms due to their anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties.

The tea contains compounds like geraniol, linalool, and borneol, which are known to relax airway muscles and reduce inflammation in the lungs. This can help to relieve wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath associated with asthma. To use Pelargonium sidoides teas for asthma, make a cup by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Drink 2-3 cups a day, preferably between meals, and as needed for relief. It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedy, especially if you're already taking medication for asthma. If you experience any worsening of symptoms or allergic reactions, discontinue use and seek medical attention. Likely outcomes from taking action may include improved lung function, reduced frequency and severity of asthma attacks, and enhanced overall well-being.

However, it's crucial to note that Pelargonium sidoides teas should not replace conventional asthma treatment, but rather be used as a complementary therapy under medical supervision.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent asthma from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent asthma in some people.

Certain herbs like thyme and eucalyptus have anti-inflammatory properties that could reduce airway irritation. Ginger and licorice root are also thought to help relax airways and ease breathing.

Some studies suggest these herbs may reduce the risk of asthma symptoms, but more research is needed.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for asthma every day?

Herbal teas for asthma can be a good option, but it's essential to be cautious.

Some teas, like eucalyptus and peppermint, can help ease breathing. However, others might trigger allergic reactions or interact with medications.

It's best to start with small amounts and observe how your body reacts, and adjust your consumption accordingly.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in asthma?

Herbal teas can help with asthma, but it's hard to say exactly how long it takes to see results.

Some people might notice improvements after a few days, while others might take a week or two.

It really depends on the individual and the specific tea being used.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for asthma?

Drinking herbal tea for asthma can be most effective in the morning.

This is because it helps to open up airways and prepare your body for the day ahead.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and ginger, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate asthma symptoms.

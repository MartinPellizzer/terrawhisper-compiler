7 Herbal Essential Oils For Asthma

Herbal essential oils can be a great help for people with asthma.

These oils come from plants and have natural healing properties. One way they work is by opening up the airways in your lungs, making it easier to breathe. This is because the oils have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and calm the muscles in your airways. Herbal teas that contain these oils can relieve asthma symptoms because the heat from the tea helps release the oils' properties into the body. For example, Eucalyptus globulus oil has decongestant properties that help loosen and clear mucus from your airways, making it easier to breathe.

Rosemary oil, or Rosmarinus officinalis, has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the airways. Lavender oil, or Lavandula angustifolia, has a calming effect that can help relax the muscles in your airways and reduce inflammation. Using herbal teas that contain these oils can bring many benefits to your life. For one, it can help you manage your asthma symptoms naturally, without relying on medication. This can be especially helpful if you experience frequent asthma attacks or if you have trouble finding a medication that works for you. Additionally, herbal teas can be a calming and soothing way to relax and reduce stress, which is often a trigger for asthma attacks.

By incorporating herbal teas into your daily routine, you can take control of your asthma symptoms and improve your overall quality of life.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains a high concentration of eucalyptol, a compound known to help ease breathing.

This essential oil works by thinning mucus, making it easier to expel from the airways. The menthol in eucalyptus globulus essential oils also helps to open up air passages, allowing more air to flow into the lungs. Additionally, eucalyptol has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the airways, making it easier for people with asthma to breathe.

By using eucalyptus globulus essential oils, individuals may experience relief from asthma symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat and add the Eucalyptus leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the pot from heat and let it steep for 2 hours. This allows the oil to release from the leaves.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 24 hours to allow the oil to separate and rise to the top. Skim off the oil and store it in a dark glass bottle.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains the bioactive constituents camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the airways, making it easier to breathe. The oils also contain antioxidants like carnosic acid, which helps protect the lungs from damage caused by free radicals. The anti-spasmodic properties of Rosmarinus officinalis help relax the airway muscles, reducing wheezing and coughing.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing airway muscles, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils may be beneficial in alleviating asthma symptoms.

  1. Gather materials: 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves, 1 cup of water, a blender, a cheesecloth, and a glass bottle.
  2. Wash the leaves with water, and chop them into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped leaves and water in the blender. Blend for 2 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth into the glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser to help relieve asthma symptoms.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties.

These compounds help to relax the airways and reduce inflammation, making it easier to breathe for people with asthma. The essential oil's calming effect on the respiratory system can also help to reduce the frequency and severity of asthma attacks. The anti-inflammatory properties of linalool and linalyl acetate in Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to reduce swelling in the airways, further improving breathing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation in the respiratory system, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to alleviate asthma symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water to 100°F (38°C), then add the Lavandula flowers and let it steep for 2 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container.
  4. Add 1 cup of ethanol or vodka to the strained mixture and mix well.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 24 hours, then strain it again and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

4. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol and carvacrol as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce the severity of asthma symptoms. Thymol also has bronchodilatory effects, making it easier to breathe by widening airways. Carvacrol's antioxidant properties help protect the lungs from oxidative damage caused by free radicals.

By using Thymus vulgaris essential oils, individuals with asthma may experience relief from symptoms due to its ability to open airways and reduce inflammation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves, a clean glass jar with a lid, a metal colander, a stainless steel bowl, and a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil.
  2. Wash the Thymus vulgaris leaves with 1 cup of water to remove dirt and impurities. Let the leaves dry completely in the shade.
  3. Add the dried leaves to the stainless steel bowl and use the metal colander to gently crush the leaves to release their oils.
  4. Transfer the crushed leaves to the glass jar and add 2 cups of carrier oil. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the Thymus vulgaris essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser to help relieve asthma symptoms.

5. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the airways.

This reduction in inflammation helps make breathing easier for people with asthma. The oils also have a compound called zingerone, which has antioxidant properties that help fight free radicals in the body. By reducing oxidative stress, zingerone helps prevent the airways from becoming inflamed and constricted.

Regular use of Zingiber officinale essential oils may help improve lung function and reduce asthma symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale (Ginger) roots and 1 cup of Carrier oil (Coconut or Olive oil)
  2. Peel and chop the Ginger roots into small pieces and place them in a clean glass jar
  3. Add 1 cup of Carrier oil to the jar, making sure the Ginger pieces are completely covered
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle, and discard the Ginger solids

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains eugenol, limonene, linalool, and menthone as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties, which help to ease asthma symptoms. The menthone in peppermint oil relaxes the airway muscles, allowing for easier breathing, while eugenol and limonene reduce inflammation in the airways. Linalool's calming effects also help to reduce stress and anxiety, which can contribute to asthma attacks.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing airway muscles, Mentha x piperita essential oils can help alleviate asthma symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan until it starts boiling.
  3. Reduce the heat and add the fresh Mentha x piperita leaves to the boiling water.
  4. Let the mixture simmer for 30 minutes, then remove from heat and let it cool.
  5. Strain the liquid and collect the oil that separates from the water using a cheesecloth or coffee filter.

7. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains rosmarinic acid, luteolin, and camphor as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling in the airways and ease breathing. The camphor in Salvia officinalis essential oils also has expectorant properties, meaning it helps loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, making it easier to breathe. Additionally, the antioxidants in Salvia officinalis essential oils, such as luteolin, help protect the lungs from damage caused by free radicals, which can exacerbate asthma symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and clearing mucus, Salvia officinalis essential oils can provide relief from asthma symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Salvia officinalis leaves, a glass jar, and a clean coffee filter.
  2. Fill a glass jar with 1 cup of dried Salvia officinalis leaves and cover it with a clean coffee filter.
  3. Heat 2 cups of water in a pot until it reaches 212°F (100°C).
  4. Place the jar with the leaves into the heated water and let it steep for 2 hours.
  5. Strain the liquid through the coffee filter into a separate container and discard the leaves. Store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent asthma from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent asthma from forming.

Some herbal teas, like those made from eucalyptus and thyme, contain compounds that can help relax airways and reduce inflammation.

These teas may also help prevent asthma by reducing the body's natural response to triggers that can cause symptoms.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for asthma every day?

Herbal teas can be a good addition to your asthma treatment, but it's essential to consume them carefully.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and ginger, can help relax the airways and reduce inflammation. However, others, like chamomile and lavender, can cause allergic reactions or interact with medications.

It's best to try small amounts and observe how your body reacts.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in asthma?

Herbal teas can help with asthma symptoms, but it's hard to say exactly how long it takes to see results.

Some people notice a difference in a few days, while others may take a week or two to feel the effects.

It really depends on the individual and the specific herbal tea being used.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for asthma?

For asthma, it's best to drink herbal tea in the morning or early afternoon.

This is because these times usually have the most stable air quality, making it easier to breathe.

Drinking tea then can help keep symptoms under control throughout the day.

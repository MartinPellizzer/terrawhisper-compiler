What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have asthma?

If you have asthma, some herbal essential oils can trigger or worsen symptoms.

These oils can cause allergic reactions and irritation, especially in the airways. Other oils to be cautious with are those made from plants in the Asteraceae family, such as chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) and calendula (Calendula officinalis).

While generally considered safe, some people may be sensitive to these oils and experience respiratory issues. Oils made from plants in the Rutaceae family, like bergamot (Citrus aurantium bergamia) and lemongrass (Cymbopogon flexuosus), may also be problematic. These oils can release compounds that can irritate the airways and exacerbate asthma symptoms. Additionally, oils from plants in the Sapindaceae family, such as soapberry (Sapindus mukorossi), should be used with caution.

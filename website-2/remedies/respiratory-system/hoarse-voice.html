What Are The Main Causes Of Hoarse Voice?

A hoarse voice is usually caused by an irritation or inflammation of the vocal cords.

There are several reasons why this can happen. One of the main causes is laryngitis, which is an inflammation of the larynx (voice box). This can be caused by a viral or bacterial infection, and it can make the vocal cords swell and become rough, leading to a hoarse voice. Another cause of a hoarse voice is tonsillitis, which is an inflammation of the tonsils.

When the tonsils get infected, it can spread to the vocal cords and cause irritation, leading to a hoarse voice. This is often caused by a bacterial infection, but it can also be caused by a viral infection. Acid reflux is another cause of a hoarse voice. When stomach acid flows back up into the throat, it can irritate the vocal cords and cause inflammation. This is often caused by eating too much or too quickly, lying down after eating, or having a condition that causes stomach acid to flow up into the throat.

In all these cases, the irritation and inflammation of the vocal cords can cause a hoarse voice, which can make speaking and singing difficult.

Below are the main causes of hoarse voice.

  • Laryngitis: Laryngitis causes a hoarse voice because the inflammation of the vocal cords leads to swelling, pain, and irritation, which makes it difficult for the vocal cords to vibrate and produce a clear sound.
  • Tonsillitis: Tonsillitis can cause a hoarse voice because the swollen and inflamed tonsils can irritate the vocal cords, making it difficult to speak in a normal voice.
  • Acid Reflux: When stomach acid from acid reflux flows up the throat, it can irritate and inflame the vocal cords, leading to a hoarse or raspy voice.
  • Sinusitis: Sinusitis is a cause of hoarse voice because the infection and swelling in the sinuses can put pressure on the vocal cords, causing them to vibrate abnormally and resulting in a hoarse or raspy sound when speaking or singing.
  • Thyroid Disease: Thyroid disease causes hoarse voice because the abnormal levels of thyroid hormones affect the nerves in the throat that are responsible for controlling the vocal cords, leading to changes in pitch, tone, and volume of the voice.
  • Polyps: When polyps form on the vocal cords, they can cause the cords to vibrate unevenly, leading to a hoarse and raspy voice.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Hoarse Voice?

If you have a hoarse voice, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can irritate your throat or make your condition worse.

One such herb is Echinacea (Echinacea spp. ), which is often used to boost the immune system but can also cause inflammation in the throat, making it more hoarse. Another herb to avoid is Yarrow (Achillea millefolium), which can cause allergic reactions, including skin irritation and digestive issues.

Additionally, Licorice root's cousin, Althea root (Marrubium vulgare), should be avoided as it can also cause inflammation in the throat. Black cohosh (Actaea racemosa) can also be problematic, as it can cause throat irritation and make your hoarseness worse. Finally, Sage (Salvia officinalis) can be too drying for a hoarse voice, making it more difficult to heal.

These herbs can worsen your condition, so it's best to avoid them until your hoarseness improves.

What Are The Main Causes Of Sore Throat?

A sore throat is usually caused by infections or irritation in the throat.

One common cause is a viral infection, which can be caused by viruses like the common cold or flu. These viruses can infect the throat and cause inflammation, leading to a sore throat. Bacterial infections can also cause a sore throat.

This can occur when bacteria like strep throat (group A streptococcus) infect the throat, causing inflammation and pain. Bacterial infections are more serious than viral infections and can lead to complications if left untreated. Mononucleosis, also known as glandular fever, is another cause of a sore throat. This is a viral infection caused by the Epstein-Barr virus (EBV), which can affect the throat, lymph nodes, and spleen.

Mononucleosis can cause a severe sore throat, as well as fever, fatigue, and swollen lymph nodes.

Below are the main causes of sore throat.

  • Viral Infection: A viral infection causes sore throat because the virus attacks the tissues and cells in the throat, leading to inflammation, pain, and swelling.
  • Bacterial Infection: A bacterial infection is a cause of sore throat because tiny bacteria, such as strep throat, can multiply in the throat and produce toxins that irritate the throat and cause pain.
  • Mononucleosis: Mononucleosis, also known as mono or glandular fever, is a cause of sore throat because the Epstein-Barr virus that causes it makes the throat and tonsils inflamed and painful.
  • Tonsillitis: Tonsillitis is a cause of sore throat because the tonsils, two small glands at the back of the throat, become swollen and inflamed, leading to pain and discomfort when swallowing or talking.
  • Allergies: When your body reacts to an allergen, it releases histamine, which makes the blood vessels in your throat swell, causing soreness and discomfort.
  • Cold: When you have a cold, the virus attacks your body's mucous membranes in your throat, making them inflamed and sore.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Sore Throat?

When you have a sore throat, it's essential to be careful with herbs that might irritate or worsen your condition.

One herb to avoid is Ephedra sinica, also known as Ma Huang. This herb contains a compound that can dry out your throat and make it even more irritated. Another herb to steer clear of is Valeriana officinalis, or Valerian root.

While it's often used for sleep issues, its bitter taste and potential to cause stomach upset can make a sore throat worse. You should also be cautious with Camellia sinensis, or green tea, especially if it's not brewed correctly. If the tea is too hot or too strong, it can burn your throat and exacerbate the discomfort.

Additionally, avoid using Asafoetida, also known as Ferula asafoetida, as its strong smell and potential to cause stomach issues can irritate your throat further.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains several active constituents that help alleviate postnasal drip.

Tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids in the cream work together to reduce inflammation in the nasal passages. The menthol content helps thin out mucus, making it easier to expel, while cineole, a key component, exhibits expectorant properties that help loosen and clear out mucus from the airways. Cineole also has antimicrobial properties, which can help combat underlying infections that may be contributing to postnasal drip.

By reducing inflammation and thinning out mucus, Eucalyptus globulus creams can help provide relief from the symptoms of postnasal drip.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 10 drops of eucalyptus oil in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in the double boiler over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the double boiler from the heat and stir in the eucalyptus oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a bowl and let it cool and solidify. Once set, use a spoon to scoop out small amounts and apply to the affected area as needed.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains alkylamides, glycoproteins, and polysaccharides, which are active constituents that help combat post nasal drip.

Alkylamides have anti-inflammatory properties, reducing swelling in the nasal passages and relieving congestion. Glycoproteins, on the other hand, stimulate the immune system, producing more mucus that traps and removes excess mucus from the nasal passages. Polysaccharides in Echinacea purpurea creams also have immunomodulatory effects, supporting the body's natural defenses against infections that can cause post nasal drip.

By addressing the root causes of post nasal drip, Echinacea purpurea creams can provide relief from this common issue.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of Echinacea purpurea root powder, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, and 2 teaspoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Combine 1 cup of distilled water and 2 tablespoons of Echinacea purpurea root powder in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 10 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl.
  4. In a separate saucepan, melt 1/2 cup of coconut oil and 2 tablespoons of beeswax over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  5. Combine the cooled Echinacea mixture with the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture. Add 2 teaspoons of vitamin E oil and stir well. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool before use.

3. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams contains thymol and carvacrol, which are known as bioactive constituents.

Thymol has antimicrobial properties that help kill bacteria in the nasal passages, reducing the presence of germs that can cause post nasal drip. Carvacrol has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling in the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe and reducing congestion. These properties also help to thin mucus, making it easier to expel and relieving the discomfort associated with post nasal drip.

By reducing inflammation, killing bacteria, and thinning mucus, Thymus vulgaris creams can provide relief from post nasal drip symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of coconut oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or in a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of dried Thymus vulgaris (thyme) flowers to the melted mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the thyme flowers.
  5. Add 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil and 1 teaspoon of aloe vera gel to the cooled mixture and mix well. Pour into containers and let cool before use.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling and inflammation in the nasal passages, relieving congestion and postnasal drip. The essential oil, zingiberene, also has expectorant properties, which help to thin and clear mucus from the lungs and nasal passages. Additionally, ginger's warming properties can help to increase blood flow and circulation, which can help to clear mucus and reduce nasal congestion.

The combination of these properties in Zingiber officinale creams can provide relief from postnasal drip symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of grated fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) root, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of shea butter.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add the grated ginger root and shea butter to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool until it reaches 120°F to 130°F. This is an important step to prevent the cream from becoming too solid or too runny.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let it cool and solidify completely before use. This cream can be stored in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains a high concentration of rosmarinic acid, carnosic acid, and camphor.

These bioactive constituents have natural decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce nasal congestion and swelling. Rosmarinic acid, in particular, has been shown to possess potent antioxidant and antimicrobial properties, which can help to combat infection and soothe irritated nasal passages. The camphor in Rosmarinus officinalis creams also has a decongestant effect, which can help to thin mucus and promote drainage, making it easier to breathe.

By using Rosmarinus officinalis creams, individuals experiencing post nasal drip may find relief from their symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from heat and let it cool for a few minutes. Stir in the Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil and vitamin E oil.
  4. Pour the mixture into small containers or a large container. Let it cool and solidify completely.
  5. Once solid, your Rosmarinus officinalis cream is ready to use. Apply a small amount to the affected area to help soothe post nasal drip.

6. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains active constituents like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

By reducing inflammation and soothing the nasal and sinus tissues, Curcuma longa creams can help to alleviate the symptoms of post nasal drip.

  1. Gather 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, and 1 tablespoon of turmeric powder.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. In a small bowl, mix 1 tablespoon of turmeric powder with 1 tablespoon of beeswax and 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil.
  4. Add the turmeric mixture to the melted coconut oil and shea butter, and stir until combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into small tin containers and refrigerate until solid. Use as needed to help soothe post nasal drip.

7. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains a compound called cineole, which helps to thin and clear mucus from the nasal passages.

The tea tree oil in the cream also contains eucalyptol, a natural expectorant that helps to loosen and remove excess mucus. Cineole's decongestant properties also help to reduce swelling and congestion in the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe. The anti-inflammatory properties of eucalyptol also help to reduce redness and irritation in the nasal passages, making it a soothing and calming treatment for post nasal drip.

By thinning and clearing mucus, reducing swelling and inflammation, and loosening excess mucus, Melaleuca alternifolia creams can provide fast and effective relief from post nasal drip symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water, 1/2 cup of glycerin, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 1 tablespoon of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil.
  2. Combine distilled water and glycerin in a small saucepan, and heat over low heat until glycerin dissolves.
  3. Add beeswax to the saucepan and heat until melted. Stir well.
  4. Remove saucepan from heat and add Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil. Stir well and let cool slightly.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool completely. Once cooled, your Melaleuca alternifolia cream is ready to use.

  3. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Add the peppermint essential oil and stir well.

9. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its active constituents.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in post nasal drip?

Herbal teas can help with post-nasal drip, but it's hard to say exactly how long it takes to see results.

Some people feel better after a few days, while others may take a week or two to notice a difference.

It really depends on the person and the tea they're using.

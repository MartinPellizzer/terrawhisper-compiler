How to Use 8 Essential Oils for Post Nasal Drip Relief in Allergics

People with allergies often have to deal with post nasal drip, which is when mucus flows from the back of the nose down the throat.

This can be caused by allergies, especially those that trigger inflammation in the nasal passages, such as hay fever or dust mites. When this happens, it's not just a matter of being uncomfortable - it can also lead to coughing, sore throats, and even difficulty sleeping. For those who suffer from allergies, this can be a constant battle, with symptoms flaring up at the worst times, like during pollen season or when they're around pets. It can be frustrating and disrupt daily life, making even simple tasks a challenge.

To make matters worse, over-the-counter medications can have side effects, and some people may not be able to take them. This is where herbal essential oils come in. Some of these oils have natural anti-inflammatory properties and decongestant effects that can help ease post nasal drip. They can be inhaled through steam inhalation, added to a diffuser, or applied topically to help reduce congestion and soothe the nasal passages.

When used correctly, these oils may provide some relief for those who suffer from allergies and post nasal drip, giving them some much-needed respite from their symptoms.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils have been traditionally used to help with post nasal drip, and it's not hard to see why.

This oil is rich in compounds like eucalyptol, limonene, and alpha-pinene, which have natural decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties. When you inhale eucalyptus globulus essential oils, these compounds help to thin out mucus and reduce congestion in the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe and clear out excess mucus.

To use eucalyptus globulus essential oils for post nasal drip, try adding a few drops to a diffuser in your home, or mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil and apply it to your chest or nose. You can also inhale it directly from a cloth or tissue. When you take these actions, you can expect to feel a reduction in congestion and a decrease in post nasal drip, making it easier to breathe and swallow.

Additionally, you may notice an improvement in your sense of smell and a reduction in coughing and sinus pressure.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils, also known as rosemary essential oil, have been used to help alleviate post nasal drip due to its decongestant and expectorant properties.

The main constituents of rosemary essential oil, including camphor, bornyl acetate, and borneol, work together to thin mucus and reduce congestion in the nasal passages. The camphor content also helps to break down and clear out excess mucus, making it easier to expel. To use rosemary essential oil for post nasal drip, you can add a few drops to a diffuser or inhale it directly from a cloth or handkerchief.

You can also add rosemary essential oil to a warm bath or inhale it while steam inhalation. When inhaling rosemary essential oil, take slow, deep breaths to help the oil reach the nasal passages and sinuses. As you inhale rosemary essential oil, you may feel your nasal passages start to clear and your sinuses begin to drain. You may also notice a reduction in congestion and a decrease in post nasal drip.

The oil's expectorant properties may help you cough up excess mucus more easily, providing relief from post nasal drip.

3. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils, commonly known as thyme oil, has been traditionally used to help alleviate post nasal drip symptoms.

The main reason for its effectiveness lies in its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Thyme oil contains constituents like thymol and carvacrol, which have been shown to combat bacterial and viral infections, thereby reducing congestion and promoting a healthy nasal environment. When using thyme oil for post nasal drip, it's recommended to add a few drops to a humidifier or inhale it directly from a cloth or handkerchief.

You can also add a few drops to a warm bath or inhale it while steaming with a bowl of hot water. When you inhale thyme oil, the thymol and carvacrol molecules travel up your nostrils and into your nasal passages, where they help to kill off any bacteria or viruses that may be contributing to your congestion. This can lead to a reduction in mucus production and a decrease in the sensation of post nasal drip. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of thyme oil can help to reduce swelling in the nasal passages, further alleviating congestion.

As a result, you may notice an improvement in your nasal breathing and a decrease in the frequency and volume of mucus production.

The main constituents of this oil, including cineole, terpinen-4-ol, and alpha-terpinene, have been shown to help combat bacterial and viral infections that can cause nasal congestion and discharge. When inhaled, the oil may help reduce inflammation in the nasal passages and sinuses, which can help to clear out excess mucus and reduce post nasal drip. To use Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils for post nasal drip, you can add a few drops to your diffuser or inhale it directly from a cloth or handkerchief.

You can also mix a few drops with a carrier oil and apply it to your chest or nose to help ease congestion. When using Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils for post nasal drip, it is essential to note that you may experience a reduction in nasal discharge and congestion within a few hours. You may also notice an improvement in your sense of smell and taste.

However, it's also important to note that if you experience persistent or severe symptoms, you should consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

Echinacea purpurea essential oils may help alleviate post nasal drip due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The oil contains constituents like cis-isobutyl isothiocyanate, echinacoside, and alkylamides, which have been shown to reduce inflammation in the nasal passages and combat infections that can cause post nasal drip. To try it, add a few drops of Echinacea purpurea essential oil to your diffuser or inhale it directly from a cloth or handkerchief.

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils, also known as peppermint oil, can help alleviate post nasal drip due to its natural decongestant properties.

The main constituent, menthol, helps to thin and clear mucus from the nasal passages, while the menthone constituent reduces inflammation and congestion. The cooling sensation of peppermint oil also helps to soothe and calm the nasal passages, reducing the urge to cough or blow the nose. To use Mentha x piperita essential oils for post nasal drip, inhale it through steam inhalation. Add a few drops of peppermint oil to a bowl of hot water, then lean over the bowl with a towel covering your head to trap the steam.

Breathe in the vapors for 5-10 minutes, several times a day. You can also add peppermint oil to a humidifier or diffuse it in the air to help relieve congestion. As you inhale the peppermint oil vapors, you may notice a significant reduction in post nasal drip and congestion. Your nasal passages may feel clearer and less inflamed, making it easier to breathe and sleep.

You may also experience a decrease in coughing and sneezing, and an overall sense of relief from nasal congestion.

7. Hyptis suaveolens

Hyptis suaveolens essential oils, also known as bush mint or shake tree, has been traditionally used to help alleviate post nasal drip.

If you're experiencing post nasal drip, try inhaling Hyptis suaveolens essential oils through steam inhalation or add a few drops to your diffuser.

When using Hyptis suaveolens essential oils for post nasal drip, you may start to notice a decrease in congestion and inflammation within a few minutes to an hour after inhaling the oil. 8. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils, also known as lemongrass oil, has natural properties that help alleviate post nasal drip.

Its key constituents, such as citral, geraniol, and limonene, have decongestant and anti-inflammatory effects, which help reduce swelling in the nasal passages and sinuses. This allows mucus to flow out more easily, relieving congestion. When used in aromatherapy, lemongrass oil can also help clear airways and promote drainage, making it a popular natural remedy for post nasal drip. To use Cymbopogon citratus essential oils for post nasal drip, mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil, and gently massage it onto your chest or nose.

You can also inhale it through steam inhalation by adding a few drops to hot water, then inhaling the mist. Some people also use a humidifier with a few drops of lemongrass oil to add moisture to the air and help clear congestion. When you start using Cymbopogon citratus essential oils for post nasal drip, you may notice a decrease in mucus production and a reduction in nasal congestion. Your sinuses may start to feel clearer, and you may experience relief from sinus pressure and headaches.

However, it's essential to note that lemongrass oil may cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in some individuals, so always do a patch test before using it extensively.

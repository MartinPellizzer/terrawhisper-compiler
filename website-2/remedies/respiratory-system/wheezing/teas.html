8 Herbal Teas For Wheezing

If you're experiencing wheezing, you might want to try drinking herbal teas to help ease the symptoms.

Some herbs, like Eucalyptus globulus, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling in the airways, making it easier to breathe. This is because eucalyptus contains a compound called eucalyptol, which acts as a natural decongestant. Another herb that might help is Echinacea purpurea. This plant has been used for centuries to boost the immune system, which can help prevent respiratory infections that can trigger wheezing. Drinking echinacea tea may also help reduce inflammation and fight off viruses that can cause wheezing. Rosemary, or Rosmarinus officinalis, is another herb that could be beneficial.

Its essential oils have been shown to relax muscles in the airways, making it easier to breathe. This is because rosemary contains compounds like camphor and borneol, which have natural bronchodilating properties. Drinking these herbal teas can bring several benefits to your life. For one, they can provide quick relief from wheezing symptoms, allowing you to breathe more easily. This can be especially helpful when you're experiencing a wheezing episode and need to calm down. Additionally, these teas can help you avoid relying on medications, which can have side effects.

By drinking herbal teas regularly, you may also be able to prevent wheezing episodes from happening in the first place.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains bioactive constituents like eucalyptol, a compound that helps open up airways.

Eucalyptol's decongestant properties ease congestion and reduce inflammation in the lungs, making it easier to breathe. The expectorant properties of eucalyptus globulus teas help bring up mucus and phlegm, relieving wheezing and coughing. The anti-inflammatory properties of eucalyptol also reduce swelling in the airways, allowing air to pass through more freely.

Regular consumption of eucalyptus globulus teas may help alleviate wheezing by reducing inflammation and congestion in the lungs.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh eucalyptus leaves or 2 teaspoons of dried eucalyptus leaves.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot and remove from heat.
  3. Add the eucalyptus leaves to the hot water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and add honey to taste if desired.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve wheezing.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains active constituents like alkylamides, glycoproteins, and polyphenols.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce inflammation in the airways, making it easier to breathe. The glycoproteins in Echinacea purpurea, specifically polysaccharides, stimulate the production of white blood cells, which fight off infections that can cause wheezing. The polyphenols in Echinacea purpurea also have antihistamine properties, which can help alleviate allergic reactions that can lead to wheezing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting immune function, Echinacea purpurea teas may help alleviate wheezing symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Echinacea purpurea root and flowers.
  2. Combine the dried Echinacea with 1 cup of boiling water in a teapot.
  3. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then strain the liquid into a cup.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and stir well.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve wheezing.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains carnosic acid, camphor, and borneol as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds work together to help alleviate wheezing by reducing inflammation in the airways and improving lung function. Carnosic acid, in particular, has been shown to have anti-inflammatory properties that help combat oxidative stress and alleviate congestion. The camphor in Rosmarinus officinalis teas also helps to loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, making it easier to breathe.

By combining these properties, Rosmarinus officinalis teas can help to relieve wheezing and promote healthier respiratory function.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Add the 1 tablespoon of dried leaves to the boiling water.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it while it's still warm.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains a compound called gingerol, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce swelling in the airways.

This swelling is often the cause of wheezing, a symptom of respiratory issues like asthma. Gingerol also has bronchodilatory effects, meaning it helps to open up airways and improve breathing. Additionally, the bioactive compounds in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, have been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can help to combat oxidative stress and inflammation in the lungs.

By reducing inflammation and opening up airways, Zingiber officinale teas may help to alleviate wheezing symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of dried Zingiber officinale root.
  2. Boil the water in a pot and let it cool for 1 minute.
  3. Add the dried Zingiber officinale root to the pot and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the root.
  5. Drink the tea while it's warm to help relieve wheezing symptoms.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains the bioactive constituents linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties.

These properties help to relax the muscles in the airways, making it easier to breathe. Linalool also has a sedative effect on the nervous system, which can help to calm wheezing by reducing stress and anxiety. The anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds can also help to reduce swelling in the airways, making it easier to breathe.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the airway muscles, Lavandula angustifolia teas may be effective in helping to alleviate wheezing symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia leaves.
  2. Heat 1 cup of water in a pot until it boils.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of dried Lavandula angustifolia leaves to the boiling water.
  4. Reduce heat and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink 1 cup, 2-3 times a day to help with wheezing.

6. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra teas contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids that help with wheezing.

The flavonoids present in Sambucus nigra, such as quercetin, kaempferol, and isorhapontigenin, have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce inflammation in the airways. The alkaloids, including sambunigine, sambuine, and aucubine, exhibit bronchodilatory properties, which help to relax the airway muscles and improve breathing. The terpenoids in Sambucus nigra, including sesquiterpene lactones and phenolic acids, have antioxidant properties that protect the airways from oxidative stress.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing airway muscles, Sambucus nigra teas helps to alleviate wheezing symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Sambucus nigra flowers and leaves. Use a clean scoop to measure.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot. Remove from heat when boiling.
  3. Add the measured Sambucus nigra flowers and leaves to the boiling water. Let steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth. Discard the solids.
  5. Drink the tea while still warm, 2-3 times a day, to help with wheezing.

7. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

This helps to reduce swelling in the airways and make breathing easier. Glycyrrhizin also has a soothing effect on the mucous membranes, which can help to calm a wheezing cough. The tea also contains flavonoids, such as liquiritin and isoliquiritin, which have antioxidant properties that can help to protect the lungs from damage.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the lungs, Glycyrrhiza glabra tea may be beneficial in helping to alleviate wheezing symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 teaspoon of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root.
  2. Add the dried root to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup to remove the dried root.
  4. Add honey to taste, as desired, to sweeten the tea.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve wheezing symptoms.

8. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis teas contains bioactive constituents like carnosic acid, ursolic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce inflammation in the airways. By decreasing inflammation, Salvia officinalis teas can help open up airways and relieve wheezing. The rosmarinic acid in Salvia officinalis teas also has a bronchodilatory effect, which means it helps relax the muscles in the airways and make breathing easier.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the airways, Salvia officinalis teas can help provide relief from wheezing.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Salvia officinalis leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup and discard the leaves.
  4. Add honey to taste, if needed. The recommended dose is 1/2 to 1 teaspoon.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve wheezing.

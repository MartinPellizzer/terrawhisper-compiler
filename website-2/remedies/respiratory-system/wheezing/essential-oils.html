1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains active constituents like eucalyptol, limonene, and alpha-pinene.

These compounds have decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to relieve wheezing. Eucalyptol, in particular, is known to thin mucus, making it easier to expel, while limonene and alpha-pinene have bronchodilatory effects, allowing air to flow more freely through the lungs. The expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds help to reduce inflammation and open airways, providing relief from wheezing.

By using eucalyptus globulus essential oils in a diffuser or adding a few drops to a humidifier, you can breathe easier and feel more comfortable.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a cup of water, a large saucepan, and a still or distillation kit.
  2. Wash the leaves in a cup of water to remove dirt, then drain and set aside.
  3. Fill the large saucepan with 2 cups of water and add the clean leaves. Heat the mixture over low heat.
  4. As the mixture starts to boil, reduce heat and let it simmer for 2 hours to release the oil. You can see steam rising from the saucepan.
  5. Collect the steam with the still or distillation kit and condense it into a separate container. This is your Eucalyptus globulus essential oil for wheezing relief.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains compounds like camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate that help to open up airways and reduce inflammation.

These oils also have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe and calm the respiratory system. The camphor in rosemary essential oil helps to break down mucus and phlegm, making it easier to breathe. Borneol and bornyl acetate also have expectorant properties, helping to loosen and clear out airway obstructions.

By reducing inflammation and opening up airways, rosemary essential oils can help to relieve wheezing and improve respiratory function.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat for 30 minutes. Then, turn off the heat and let it cool.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, separating the leaves from the liquid.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and add 2 tablespoons of carrier oil, like coconut or jojoba oil.
  5. Store the bottle in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the bottle every day, before using the essential oil for wheezing relief.

3. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains a compound called thymol, which has natural decongestant properties that help relieve wheezing.

Thymol works by reducing inflammation in the airways and loosening mucus, making it easier to breathe. Other active constituents like borneol and carvacrol also contribute to Thymus vulgaris essential oils' ability to ease respiratory congestion. These compounds have antimicrobial properties, which can help combat underlying infections that may be exacerbating wheezing.

By reducing inflammation and loosening mucus, Thymus vulgaris essential oils can help alleviate wheezing and promote easier breathing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and flowers.
  2. Use a cheese cloth or a coffee filter to create a bundle. Place the Thymus vulgaris leaves and flowers in the bundle.
  3. Place the bundle in a clean glass jar. Add 2 cups of water to the jar.
  4. Heat the mixture in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl for 2-3 minutes. The temperature should not exceed 100°F (38°C).
  5. Let the mixture steep for 2 hours. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids.

4. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains several bioactive constituents, including menthol, menthone, and limonene.

These compounds have decongestant and expectorant properties, which help to relieve wheezing by thinning and clearing mucus from the airways. Menthol also has a cooling effect, which can help to reduce inflammation and ease congestion. The antispasmodic properties of menthone can help to relax the muscles in the airways, making it easier to breathe.

By combining these properties, Mentha x piperita essential oils can provide relief from wheezing and other respiratory issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita (peppermint) leaves and flowers.
  2. Use a clean glass jar and add 2 cups of water. Bring the water to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and add the peppermint leaves and flowers to the jar. Let it steep for 20-30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit in a warm, dark place for 2-3 days to allow the oil to separate and rise to the top.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its primary bioactive constituents.

These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help to ease wheezing. The antispasmodic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate may help to relax the muscles in the airways, making breathing easier.   3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the oil absorb
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids

6. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains active compounds like camphor, bornyl acetate, and thujone.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce swelling in the airways and ease wheezing. Bornyl acetate, in particular, has a bronchodilatory effect, allowing air to pass more freely in and out of the lungs. Thujone's expectorant properties help to loosen and clear mucus from the airways, further alleviating wheezing symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and promoting mucus clearance, Salvia officinalis essential oils can help alleviate wheezing and make breathing easier.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves, 1 cup of water, a large bowl, a glass jar, and a cheesecloth.
  2. Place the leaves in the bowl and pour the water over them. Let it steep for 2 hours or overnight.
  3. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth into the glass jar. Press the leaves to get all the liquid out.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba or coconut oil) to the liquid. Stir well.
  5. Store the oil in a cool, dark place and use it within 2 weeks. Use 2-3 drops of the oil in a diffuser or apply it to your skin after diluting with a carrier oil.

7. Ocimum basilicum

Ocimum basilicum essential oils contains compounds like linalool, eugenol, and limonene.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. They help to relax the muscles in the airways, reducing the frequency and severity of wheezing. By inhibiting the release of histamine and other inflammatory mediators, Ocimum basilicum essential oils can help to alleviate congestion and promote easier breathing.

This makes Ocimum basilicum essential oils a potentially useful natural remedy for managing wheezing and related respiratory issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ocimum basilicum leaves, a glass jar with a lid, and a carrier oil like coconut oil.
  2. Wash the Ocimum basilicum leaves with 1 cup of water, then dry them with a paper towel.
  3. Combine 1 cup of Ocimum basilicum leaves with 2 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a warm place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. Then, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-10 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

8. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Gingerol has anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce swelling in the airways and ease wheezing. The essential oils also contain other bioactive constituents like zingerone, which has bronchodilatory effects, meaning it helps to relax the airway muscles and improve breathing. The antioxidant properties of zingiber officinale essential oils also help to protect the lungs from damage caused by free radicals, reducing inflammation and wheezing.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the airway muscles, zingiber officinale essential oils can help to alleviate wheezing symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale rhizomes, 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Wash and chop the rhizomes into small pieces, then add them to the glass jar.
  3. Pour 2 cups of carrier oil over the chopped rhizomes, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a new glass bottle, discarding the solids and keeping the oil for use.

9. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, geraniol, and linalool as its main active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties, which help to ease wheezing by reducing inflammation and opening up airways.

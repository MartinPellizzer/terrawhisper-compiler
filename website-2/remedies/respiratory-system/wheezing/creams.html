11 Herbal Creams For Wheezing

If you experience wheezing, you know how uncomfortable it can be.

This is why eucalyptus is often used in inhalers and steam inhalers to relieve wheezing. Thymus vulgaris, or thyme, is another herb that can help with wheezing.

It has antimicrobial properties, which means it can help fight off infections that may be causing your wheezing. Rosemary, on the other hand, has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in your airways and make it easier to breathe. When you use a cream containing these herbs, the ingredients are absorbed into your skin and released into your lungs, helping to ease your wheezing. Using herbal creams for wheezing can be beneficial for several reasons. For one, they can be a natural alternative to prescription medications, which can have side effects. They can also be more convenient to use than inhalers or other devices. Plus, they can be used in combination with other treatments, such as medication or therapy, to help manage your wheezing.

Additionally, using herbal creams can be a comforting way to manage your symptoms, especially if you're feeling anxious or stressed about your wheezing.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains the active constituents eucalyptol, camphor, and cineole.

These compounds help open up the airways and relieve congestion by thinning mucus and reducing inflammation. Eucalyptol, in particular, has a decongestant property that helps ease wheezing by breaking down thick mucus and making it easier to expel. Cineole also has a bronchodilatory effect, which means it helps relax the muscles around the airways and allows air to pass through more freely.

By combining these properties, eucalyptus globulus creams can provide quick relief from wheezing and other respiratory issues.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of eucalyptus essential oil, and 2 tablespoons of peppermint essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Once melted, remove from heat and let cool slightly. Stir in the eucalyptus and peppermint essential oils.
  4. Pour the mixture into a clean, airtight container. Let it cool and harden completely.
  5. Once hardened, your Eucalyptus globulus cream is ready to use. Apply as needed to help relieve wheezing and congestion.

2. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams contains thymol and carvacrol as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce the swelling in the airways that can cause wheezing. Thymol also has bronchodilatory effects, meaning it helps to widen the airways and make it easier to breathe. Carvacrol has antioxidant properties, which help to protect the lungs from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and widening the airways, thymus vulgaris creams can help to alleviate wheezing symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water and 1/4 cup of dried Thymus vulgaris leaves in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid into a bowl, discarding the Thymus vulgaris leaves.
  4. Add 1/4 cup of coconut oil or shea butter to the liquid and mix well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a small container and let it cool before applying it to the affected area.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, a powerful antioxidant that helps to reduce inflammation in the airways.

This anti-inflammatory effect can help to alleviate wheezing symptoms by opening up air passages and making it easier to breathe. Additionally, the camphor and borneol present in Rosmarinus officinalis creams have a bronchodilatory effect, which means they can help to widen the airways and improve airflow. The anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties of these constituents work together to help relieve wheezing symptoms and promote easier breathing.

By applying Rosmarinus officinalis creams topically, you can harness the anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory powers of these active constituents to help manage wheezing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 10 drops of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second intervals until smooth.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool for 5 minutes. Add the Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a glass jar and let it cool and solidify completely before use. Store in a cool, dry place.

5. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis creams contains bioactive constituents like rosmarinic acid, caffeic acid, and luteolin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce swelling and inflammation in the airways, easing wheezing symptoms. Rosmarinic acid, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of inflammatory mediators, such as histamine and leukotrienes, which contribute to wheezing. The antioxidant properties of luteolin and caffeic acid help to protect the lungs from oxidative stress, further alleviating wheezing symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Salvia officinalis creams may provide relief from wheezing and other respiratory issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Salvia officinalis leaves, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/2 cup of beeswax.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add the dried Salvia officinalis leaves to the melted mixture and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, squeezing the leaves to extract as much liquid as possible.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, use as a cream to relieve wheezing.

6. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and chicoric acid.

The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea creams have a bronchodilatory effect, meaning they can help open up airways and improve breathing. By reducing inflammation and opening up airways, Echinacea purpurea creams can help alleviate wheezing and improve respiratory health.

  3. Add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the heated coconut oil and let it steep for 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass container, discarding the solids.
  5. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil to the liquid mixture and heat it gently until the beeswax is melted, stirring constantly.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in wheezing?

Herbal teas can help with wheezing symptoms, but it's hard to predict exactly when they'll start working.

Some people notice a difference after just a few days, while others may take up to two weeks to feel the effects.

It really depends on the individual and the type of herbal tea being used.

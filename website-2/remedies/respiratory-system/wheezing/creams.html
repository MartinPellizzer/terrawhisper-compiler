COPD patients with wheezing: 11 natural herbal creams

People with Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) often experience wheezing, which is a high-pitched sound while breathing out.

This happens because of inflammation and constriction in the airways, making it hard for air to pass through. Wheezing can be uncomfortable and disrupt daily life. It can make simple tasks like walking or talking difficult, and even cause sleep problems. Wheezing can also trigger anxiety and stress in COPD patients, making it a challenge to manage their condition. When wheezing is severe, it can lead to shortness of breath, chest tightness, and fatigue, which can further limit a person's ability to engage in daily activities.

COPD patients may try various treatments to manage wheezing, such as medications, inhalers, and lifestyle changes. However, some may find these treatments not effective enough. This is where herbal creams can be considered. These creams are made with plant extracts that have anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties, helping to relax the airways and reduce inflammation. They can be applied topically to the chest area to provide quick relief from wheezing.

Some people find that using herbal creams in combination with other treatments helps to reduce wheezing episodes and improve their overall quality of life.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams are a popular remedy for wheezing, and they work by loosening and thinning mucus in the lungs, making it easier to cough up.

The active compounds in Eucalyptus globulus, such as eucalyptol and cineole, have natural anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties that help to reduce swelling and congestion in the airways. This allows for easier breathing and relief from wheezing.

When using Eucalyptus globulus creams, apply a thin layer to your chest and throat, and gently massage it in. Take slow, deep breaths to help the vapors from the cream open up your airways. You may start to feel relief from wheezing within 15-30 minutes, and the symptoms should improve over the next few hours.

However, if your wheezing persists or worsens, consult with your doctor for proper medical attention.

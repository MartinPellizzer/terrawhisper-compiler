1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contains eucalyptol, a main bioactive constituent that helps to reduce wheezing.

Eucalyptol has anti-inflammatory properties, which help to calm the airways and reduce swelling in the lungs. It also has decongestant properties, which help to loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to breathe. Furthermore, eucalyptol has expectorant properties, which help to thin and expel mucus, providing relief from wheezing and congestion.

By reducing inflammation, loosening mucus, and clearing the airways, eucalyptus globulus tinctures can help to alleviate wheezing symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of 80% vodka or glycerin in a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves and vodka or glycerin in the jar, making sure the leaves are completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Label the jar with the date and the contents, and store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 10-20 drops of the tincture as needed for wheezing.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains alkylamides and caffeic acid derivatives, which are known to have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation in the airways, making it easier to breathe. The phenolic acids, including rosmarinic acid and chicoric acid, in Echinacea purpurea tinctures have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals in the body, which can contribute to wheezing. The polysaccharides in Echinacea purpurea tinctures also stimulate the immune system, helping to fight off infections that can trigger wheezing.

By reducing inflammation and boosting the immune system, Echinacea purpurea tinctures can help alleviate wheezing symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea roots and flowers. Use a clean glass jar.
  2. Pour 2 cups of 80% vodka or 75% ethanol into the jar. This will be your solvent.
  3. Combine the Echinacea purpurea and vodka in the jar. Close the jar and shake it for 10 seconds.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day. This will allow the Echinacea purpurea to infuse into the vodka.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids and store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops, 3 times a day, as needed for wheezing.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains active constituents such as gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties.

The tincture's ability to inhibit the production of prostaglandins, chemical messengers that promote inflammation, also helps to alleviate wheezing symptoms. Additionally, the tincture's antispasmodic properties help to calm down the airway muscles and prevent spasms that can contribute to wheezing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation in the airways, Zingiber officinale tinctures can help to alleviate wheezing symptoms.

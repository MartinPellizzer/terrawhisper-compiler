9 Herbal Teas For Coughing Fits

Herbal teas can be a great help when you're struggling with a nasty cough.

These teas work by loosening and clearing out the mucus that's causing the coughing fit. They do this by opening up the airways in your lungs, making it easier to breathe. One of the main reasons herbal teas are so effective is that they contain natural anti-inflammatory properties. This means they can help reduce swelling and irritation in the throat and lungs, making the coughing fit less intense. For example, Eucalyptus globulus is a popular herb used in many cough medicines because of its powerful decongestant properties. It helps break down the mucus and makes it easier to cough up.

Glycyrrhiza glabra, also known as licorice root, has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe a sore throat. It's also been shown to have antimicrobial properties, which can help fight off infections that are causing the cough. Echinacea purpurea is another herb that's often used to help boost the immune system and fight off infections. It's a natural way to help your body recover from a cold or flu. Drinking herbal teas can have many benefits beyond just relieving coughing fits. They can also help reduce stress and anxiety, improve sleep quality, and even boost your immune system. Many herbal teas are also caffeine-free, making them a great alternative to traditional tea or coffee.

By incorporating herbal teas into your daily routine, you can feel more relaxed, energized, and prepared to take on the day.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains boiactive compounds like eucalyptol, a natural decongestant that helps relieve respiratory issues.

The steam from eucalyptol breaks down mucus, making it easier to cough up and reducing congestion in the chest. The compound also has anti-inflammatory properties, which help soothe and calm irritated airways and throat. Eucalyptus globulus teas also contains cineole, a compound that acts as an expectorant, helping to loosen and clear mucus from the lungs.

By reducing congestion and loosening mucus, eucalyptus globulus teas can help alleviate coughing fits and make breathing easier.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 2 tablespoons of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves.
  2. Heat the water in a pot until it boils.
  3. Add the dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves to the boiling water.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink while it's warm to help soothe coughing fits.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains licorice compounds such as glycyrrhizin and glabridin, which have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling in the throat and airways, making it easier to breathe and swallow. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which can contribute to coughing fits. The anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties of glabridin help relax the muscles in the throat and airways, relieving coughing and soreness.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the throat muscles, Glycyrrhiza glabra teas can help provide quick relief from coughing fits.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots.
  2. Measure 1 cup of boiling water and pour it over the Glycyrrhiza glabra roots in a cup.
  3. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then strain the liquid using a fine-mesh sieve into another cup.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to the liquid, if desired, to sweeten the tea.
  5. Drink the tea while it's warm, up to 3 times a day as needed for coughing fits.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains active constituents like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties, which help to soothe and calm the throat. The alkylamides, specifically echinacein, work to reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract, making it easier to breathe. The glycosides, particularly echinacoside, possess antiviral properties that inhibit the growth of viruses responsible for respiratory infections.

By reducing inflammation and inhibiting viral growth, Echinacea purpurea teas can help to alleviate coughing fits associated with respiratory infections.

  1. Gather 2 teaspoons of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 teaspoon of dried herbs like mint or lemon balm.
  2. Combine the Echinacea purpurea flowers and herbs in a cup.
  3. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the mixture and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup using a tea strainer or cheesecloth to remove the solids.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help soothe coughing fits.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling in the throat, making it easier to swallow and breathe. Shogaols also have expectorant properties, which help loosen and clear mucus from the airways, relieving congestion. Gingerols, on the other hand, have a natural analgesic effect that can help ease coughing fits.

The warming properties of Zingiber officinale teas can also help ease congestion and soothe the throat.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1 teaspoon of dried Zingiber officinale root.
  2. Boil the water in a pot and then reduce the heat to low.
  3. Add the dried Zingiber officinale root to the water and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture into a cup and discard the root.
  5. Drink the tea while it's warm to help soothe a coughing fit.

5. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra teas contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which can help soothe a sore throat and reduce coughing fits. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory properties that can help calm the airways and reduce mucus production. The phenolic acids, such as ferulic acid, have antioxidant properties that can help protect the body from oxidative stress and promote healing.

By consuming Sambucus nigra teas, you can potentially experience relief from coughing fits and other respiratory issues due to its ability to calm and soothe the throat and airways.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Sambucus nigra flowers and leaves.
  2. Heat 1 cup of water in a pot until it boils.
  3. Add the 2 tablespoons of dried Sambucus nigra flowers and leaves to the boiling water.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it while still warm, 2-3 times a day.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which are responsible for its soothing and calming effects.

These constituents work together to relax the airways and reduce inflammation, making it easier to breathe and relieving coughs. The antispasmodic properties of Lavandula angustifolia help to calm the muscles in the throat and chest, reducing coughing fits and promoting a more peaceful night's sleep. Additionally, the tea's antimicrobial properties help to combat infections that can cause coughs, promoting a healthy respiratory system.

By relaxing the airways and reducing inflammation, Lavandula angustifolia teas can help to alleviate coughs and promote a sense of calm.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Measure out 1 tablespoon of dried flowers and place them in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the dried flowers and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into another cup and discard the flowers.
  5. Drink the tea while it's warm, up to 3 times a day, to help soothe coughing fits.

7. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas contains thymol and carvacrol, two bioactive constituents that have antimicrobial properties.

These properties help to reduce inflammation in the throat and airways, making it easier to breathe and relieving coughing fits. Thymol also has expectorant properties, which means it helps to bring up mucus and other debris from the lungs, making it easier to cough up and clear out. Carvacrol has anti-inflammatory properties that help to soothe the mucous membranes in the throat, reducing discomfort and pain associated with coughing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting the clearance of mucus, Thymus vulgaris teas can help to alleviate coughing fits and promote recovery.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Thymus vulgaris leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the Thymus vulgaris leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup to remove the leaves.
  4. Add honey to taste, if desired, and stir well.
  5. Drink the tea while it is still warm to help soothe a cough.

8. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas contains menthol, menthone, and limonene as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have natural decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties that help to relieve congestion and soothe the throat during coughing fits. Menthol, in particular, helps to thin mucus and make it easier to expel, providing fast relief from coughs and congestion. The anti-inflammatory properties of menthone also help to reduce swelling in the throat, making it easier to swallow and breathe.

By combining these properties, Mentha x piperita teas can provide quick relief from coughing fits and help to promote a more comfortable and restful night's sleep.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh peppermint leaves (Mentha x piperita) or 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint.
  2. Bring 1 cup of water to a boil in a pot.
  3. Add the fresh or dried peppermint to the boiling water, then reduce heat to a simmer.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain it using a fine-mesh sieve.
  5. Drink the peppermint tea hot or cold, as needed, to help soothe coughing fits.

9. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains carnosic acid, rosmarinic acid, and camphor, which are the main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help to soothe a sore throat and calm a cough. Rosmarinus officinalis teas also contains a high amount of antioxidants, which can help to reduce inflammation and fight off infections that can cause coughing fits. The antispasmodic properties of rosmarinic acid can help to relax the muscles in the throat and chest, making it easier to breathe and reducing the severity of coughing fits.

By drinking Rosmarinus officinalis teas, you can help to calm your cough and get some relief from a persistent cough.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 tablespoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) for added sweetness and soothing effect.
  3. Combine the boiling water and dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves in a cup. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into another cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove the leaves.
  5. Add the measured honey (if using) to the tea and stir well before consumption.

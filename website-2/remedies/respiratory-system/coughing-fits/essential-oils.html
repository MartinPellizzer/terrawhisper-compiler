1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like eucalyptol, limonene, and pinene, which have decongestant and expectorant properties.

Eucalyptol, in particular, helps to loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to breathe. These properties make Eucalyptus globulus essential oils effective at relieving coughing fits caused by congestion and colds. The decongestant properties of the oil also help to reduce inflammation in the nasal passages and sinuses, further alleviating coughing.

By reducing congestion and inflammation, Eucalyptus globulus essential oils helps to provide quick relief from coughing fits.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil) to the pot.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes, allowing the leaves to infuse their oils into the water and carrier oil.
  4. Remove the pot from heat and let it cool. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container.
  5. Store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for direct application.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains a high concentration of sesquiterpenes and alkylamides, which are biologically active constituents that help ease respiratory issues.

These compounds work by reducing inflammation and congestion in the airways, making it easier to breathe and cough up excess mucus. Alkylamides in particular have been shown to have a calming effect on the muscles in the throat and airways, which can help to stop coughing fits. Sesquiterpenes have antimicrobial properties that help fight off infections that can cause coughing, such as the common cold and flu.

By using Echinacea purpurea essential oils, you can help to soothe your cough and get relief from the discomfort it causes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves, and 2 cups of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Heat a double boiler or a bowl set over a pot of boiling water, and add 1 cup of carrier oil.
  3. Add the Echinacea flowers and leaves to the oil, and let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container, and discard the solids.
  5. Store the Echinacea essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use it as needed to help alleviate coughing fits.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Gingerol and other bioactive constituents, including zingerone and shogaol, have anti-inflammatory properties that help to soothe and calm the respiratory system. The oils' expectorant properties help to loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, making it easier to cough up and relieving congestion. Additionally, the oils' warm and spicy properties help to ease chest tightness and coughs, providing relief from respiratory discomfort.

By stimulating the digestive system and promoting the removal of mucus, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help to alleviate coughing fits and promote a sense of ease and comfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Zingiber officinale roots and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over medium heat until it starts boiling.
  3. Reduce heat to low and add the Zingiber officinale roots to the water.
  4. Steep for 1-2 hours or until the liquid is reduced to 1 cup. Strain the mixture.
  5. Use a carrier oil like coconut oil and add 10-15 drops of the Zingiber officinale essential oil to it. Use this oil for cough relief.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains bioactive constituents like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, which can help relieve coughing fits by reducing inflammation in the airways. Glycyrrhizin also has a soothing effect on the throat, making it less irritated and more comfortable. The expectorant properties of glycyrrhizin help loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, making it easier to cough up and breathe.

By reducing inflammation and loosening mucus, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can provide relief from coughing fits.

  1. Gather materials: 100g of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots, a glass jar, a cheesecloth, a cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Dry the roots: Place the roots in a single layer on a baking sheet and dry in a low oven (150°F - 200°F) for 2-3 hours.
  3. Make the infusion: Fill the glass jar with 100g of dried roots and add 2 cups of carrier oil. Stir and seal the jar.
  4. Steep the roots: Store the jar in a cool, dark place and let it steep for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Filter and bottle: Strain the mixture through cheesecloth into the dark glass bottle. Discard the solids and use the essential oil as needed.

5. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol, carvacrol, and borneol as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to soothe and calm the throat and respiratory tract. Thymol, in particular, has been shown to have expectorant properties, helping to loosen and clear mucus from the lungs and airways. Carvacrol, on the other hand, has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, reducing swelling and pain in the throat and sinuses.

By using Thymus vulgaris essential oils, you can help to ease coughing fits and promote a healthy respiratory system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and stems, and 2 cups of water.
  2. Wash the Thymus vulgaris leaves and stems in cold water, then gently pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Combine the Thymus vulgaris leaves and stems with the 2 cups of water in a medium saucepan.
  4. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30-40 minutes, or until the water has reduced by half and the mixture has a strong aroma.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle, and discard the solids.

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains several biologically active constituents, including menthol, menthone, and limonene.

These compounds have decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce swelling in the nasal passages and sinuses. The menthol in peppermint oil, in particular, acts as an expectorant, thinning and loosening mucus, making it easier to cough up. The oil's analgesic and anti-spasmodic properties also help to alleviate coughing fits by reducing irritation and muscle tension in the throat and chest.

This combination of properties makes peppermint oil a popular natural remedy for coughing fits and congestion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and 1 cup of water. Clean and wash the leaves.
  2. Combine the leaves and water in a saucepan and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the leaves to release their oil.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 30 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  5. Transfer the strained mixture to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dry place. Use 5-7 drops as needed for cough relief.

These constituents help to calm the body and mind, reducing stress and anxiety that can exacerbate coughing fits. The decongestant properties of lavender oil also help to clear airways and ease congestion, making it easier to breathe. By using Lavandula angustifolia essential oils, you can help to ease coughing fits and promote a sense of calm and relaxation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of vodka or glycerin in a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the flowers and vodka or glycerin in the jar. Make sure the flowers are completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to small glass bottles and label them. Store them in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the essential oil in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

8. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra essential oils contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help soothe and calm the respiratory system. The essential oil's antitussive properties specifically target cough receptors, reducing the frequency and severity of coughing fits. The flavonoids and phenolic acids in Sambucus nigra essential oils also have antimicrobial properties, which help combat underlying infections that can exacerbate coughing.

9. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains carnosic acid, camphor, and bornyl acetate as its active constituents.

These compounds have antitussive properties, meaning they help relieve coughs. The camphor in the oil has a cooling effect on the respiratory tract, reducing inflammation and soothing irritated tissues. Bornyl acetate also has expectorant properties, helping to loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to cough up.

By reducing inflammation and loosening mucus, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help alleviate coughing fits.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a pot.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and add the leaves. Let it simmer for 2 hours.

10. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains compounds like cinnamaldehyde and linalool, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

11. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains a number of bioactive constituents, including carnosic acid, rosmarinic acid, and ursolic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and irritation in the throat and lungs. The antimicrobial properties of Salvia officinalis essential oils also help combat infections that can cause coughing fits. Additionally, the expectorant properties of the oil help loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to breathe and cough up irritants.

By reducing inflammation, fighting infections, and promoting mucus clearance, Salvia officinalis essential oils can help alleviate coughing fits.

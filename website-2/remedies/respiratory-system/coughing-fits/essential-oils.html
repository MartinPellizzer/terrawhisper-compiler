11 Essential Teas for Pneumonia Patients Suffering from Coughing Fits

When people get pneumonia, their bodies react with a strong cough to try and clear out the infection.

This cough can be very painful and can even cause vomiting. It's not just the physical discomfort that's a problem - coughing fits can also disrupt sleep, making it hard to rest and recover. This can lead to feelings of exhaustion, irritability, and anxiety. Pneumonia patients may also experience difficulty breathing, which can be scary and overwhelming. As a result, their daily lives can become very challenging, making even simple tasks seem like a struggle. They may have to take time off work or school, and rely on others for help with everyday tasks.

One of the main symptoms of pneumonia is a persistent cough that can be very hard to manage. Conventional treatments may help, but they can also have side effects like dryness and irritation. This is where herbal essential oils can help. These oils are derived from plants and have natural properties that can soothe and calm the body. When inhaled, they can help to relax the airways, reducing inflammation and making it easier to breathe. They can also help to thin out mucus, making it easier to cough up and clear out the body.

By using these oils, people with pneumonia may be able to find some relief from their coughing fits and improve their overall quality of life.

1. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils, also known as thyme essential oil, is often used to help relieve coughing fits due to its decongestant and expectorant properties.

The oil contains compounds like thymol, carvacrol, and eugenol, which have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory effects that can help soothe a sore throat and calm a cough. When used in a diffuser or added to a warm bath, the vapors from thymus vulgaris essential oils can help loosen and clear mucus from the airways, providing quick relief from coughing fits. To use thymus vulgaris essential oils for coughing fits, mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it to your chest or throat.

You can also add a few drops to a humidifier or inhale the vapors directly from a diffuser. If you're experiencing a severe cough, it's recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially if you have a pre-existing medical condition or are pregnant. When used correctly, thymus vulgaris essential oils can provide quick relief from coughing fits, helping to reduce the frequency and severity of coughing. The oil's antibacterial properties can also help prevent the spread of infection, while its anti-inflammatory effects can soothe a sore throat and promote relaxation.

However, if your cough persists or worsens, seek medical attention to rule out any underlying conditions that may require medical treatment.

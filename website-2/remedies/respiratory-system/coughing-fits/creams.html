1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains eucalyptol, a key active constituent that helps break down mucus and ease congestion.

This oil has expectorant properties, which means it helps loosen and clear out mucus from the airways, making it easier to cough up. Eucalyptol also has anti-inflammatory properties, reducing swelling in the throat and airways that can cause coughing fits. Additionally, eucalyptus globulus creams often contain menthol, which has a cooling effect on the throat and helps numb the area, making it less irritated and uncomfortable.

By reducing inflammation and loosening mucus, eucalyptus globulus creams can provide quick relief from coughing fits.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of eucalyptus oil, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Once melted, remove from heat and add eucalyptus oil and vitamin E oil. Stir well to combine.
  4. Pour the mixture into a heat-proof container and let it cool until it solidifies.
  5. Once solid, your eucalyptus cream is ready to use. Apply a small amount to your chest or throat to help relieve coughing fits.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains bioactive constituents such as alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and polysaccharides that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce swelling in the throat and airways, making it easier to breathe and reducing the severity of coughing fits. The polysaccharides in Echinacea purpurea creams also stimulate the immune system, which can help the body to fight off infections that cause coughing. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of alkylamides and caffeic acid derivatives can help to soothe irritated tissues in the throat and airways, further reducing coughing.

By reducing inflammation and stimulating the immune system, Echinacea purpurea creams can provide relief from coughing fits.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Dry the Echinacea flowers in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 1-2 hours to remove moisture.
  3. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments.
  4. Add the dried Echinacea flowers, shea butter, and vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and harden. Once hardened, the Echinacea cream is ready to use for coughing fits.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains triterpenoid saponins and glycyrrhizin as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the throat and ease coughing fits. Glycyrrhizin also has a soothing effect on the mucous membranes, calming irritation and dryness in the throat. The anti-inflammatory and soothing properties of Glycyrrhiza glabra creams make it effective in providing relief from coughing fits.

The combination of triterpenoid saponins and glycyrrhizin in these creams helps to calm the throat and reduce coughing.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of glycerin, 2 tablespoons of glycyrrhiza glabra powder, 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil, and a small saucepan.
  2. Combine distilled water and glycerin in the saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring until the glycerin dissolves.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and add glycyrrhiza glabra powder. Stir until the powder dissolves completely.
  4. Let the mixture cool down. Once it's warm, add vitamin E oil and stir well. Pour the mixture into a clean container.
  5. Let the cream sit at room temperature for 24 hours before use. Store it in the refrigerator when not in use. Use as needed to soothe coughs and fits.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antitussive effects, helping to soothe and calm the throat during coughing fits. The spicy heat from ginger also stimulates the digestive system, which can help to clear mucus and phlegm from the airways, reducing congestion. The antimicrobial properties of zingiber officinale creams can also help to fight off infections that may be causing the cough, such as bronchitis or pneumonia.

By combining these properties, zingiber officinale creams can provide effective relief from coughing fits.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger root, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Grate the ginger root and mix it with the coconut oil in a saucepan.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the ginger is well combined with the oil.
  4. Remove the saucepan from the heat and add the melted beeswax and vitamin E oil. Stir until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool. Once cooled, your Zingiber officinale cream is ready to use.

5. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams contains thymol, carvacrol, and other bioactive compounds that help soothe coughing fits.

Thymol, in particular, has expectorant properties that help loosen and clear mucus from the airways. Carvacrol has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling in the throat, making it easier to breathe and cough. The antimicrobial properties of Thymus vulgaris help combat infections that can cause coughing.

By using Thymus vulgaris creams, you can get relief from coughing fits and help your body recover faster.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh thyme leaves and 1 cup of coconut oil in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in the saucepan over low heat, then add the thyme leaves.
  3. Steep the thyme leaves in the coconut oil for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, then discard the thyme leaves.
  5. Let the mixture cool, then transfer it to a jar and store it in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. Use 1-2 tablespoons as needed to soothe coughs.

6. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra creams contains compounds such as isorhapontigenin and dehydro-vomifoliol, which are effective in reducing inflammation and congestion in the throat.

These anti-inflammatory properties help to soothe an irritated throat and calm coughing fits. The flavonoids present in Sambucus nigra, including quercetin and kaempferol, also have antiviral properties that can help combat underlying viral infections causing the cough. The mucilages in Sambucus nigra creams form a protective barrier on the mucous membranes, further reducing irritation and discomfort.

By addressing the underlying causes of coughing fits, Sambucus nigra creams can provide relief and help alleviate symptoms.

  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler until it reaches 160°F.
  3. Add the Sambucus nigra flowers to the coconut oil and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture and discard the flowers. Add the beeswax and heat until melted.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool. Store in a cool, dry place.

7. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains active constituents like menthol and menthone, which help to relieve coughing fits.

Menthol has a cooling effect on the throat, reducing irritation and inflammation. Menthone helps to thin and loosen mucus, making it easier to cough up. The expectorant properties of menthol and menthone also help to clear the airways, providing quick relief from coughing fits.

When applied topically, these active constituents can provide fast and effective relief from coughing.

  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.

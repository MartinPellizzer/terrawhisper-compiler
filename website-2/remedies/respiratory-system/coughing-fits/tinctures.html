1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contains the bioactive constituents eucalyptol and cineole, which have decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to relieve congestion and swelling in the airways, making it easier to breathe when experiencing a coughing fit. Eucalyptol, in particular, has been shown to have a soothing effect on the throat and lungs, reducing irritation and discomfort. Cineole, on the other hand, has antimicrobial properties that can help combat underlying infections that may be contributing to the cough.

By addressing both the physical and infectious aspects of a coughing fit, Eucalyptus globulus tinctures can provide fast and effective relief.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Muddle the Eucalyptus leaves with a spoon to release their oils.
  3. Add the vodka to the jar, covering the leaves completely.
  4. Steep the mixture in a dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the tincture through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains compounds like alkylamides, caespitatin, and sesquiterpene lactones.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and irritation in the throat. The tinctures also contain flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have antioxidant properties that can help protect the body from free radicals and infections. The antiviral and antibacterial properties of Echinacea purpurea can help fight off the underlying cause of a cough, such as a virus or bacteria.

The tincture's ability to stimulate the immune system can also help the body recover from illness and reduce the frequency and severity of coughing fits.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves. Clean and dry them with a paper towel.
  2. Use a glass jar and add 1 cup of the dried flowers and leaves to it. Pour 2 cups of 80-proof vodka over them.
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Use 10-20 drops in water, 2-3 times a day, to help soothe coughing fits.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin, and isoflavonoids like glabrene and licaglabrol.

These bioactive constituents help to reduce inflammation in the throat and airways, making it a useful remedy for coughing fits. Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, which help to loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, providing temporary relief from coughing. The isoflavonoids also contribute to the anti-inflammatory effects, helping to soothe the throat and calm the cough.

By reducing inflammation and loosening mucus, Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures can provide quick and effective relief from coughing fits.

  1. Gather 250ml of 80% Vodka, 50g of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, a clean glass jar, and a coffee filter.
  2. Combine 50g of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root with 250ml of 80% Vodka in the glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in the dark for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the liquid through the coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 10-20 drops as needed to relieve coughing fits.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent coughing fits from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe a sore throat and calm a cough.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint or ginger, have anti-inflammatory properties that may reduce coughing fits. These teas can also help relax the body and promote better breathing.

By sipping on a warm cup, you may find some relief from coughing fits.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for coughing fits every day?

While herbal teas can help soothe a cough, drinking them daily may not be the best idea.

Some herbal teas, like those with licorice root or ephedra, can be too strong and cause problems if used regularly.

It's better to limit your intake to when you need it most, like during a cold or flu.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in coughing fits?

Herbal teas can help soothe a cough, but it may take some time to notice results.

Some people start to feel better within a few hours, while others may need a day or two to see improvement.

Drinking herbal tea regularly, especially when you're feeling unwell, may help calm your cough and make you feel more comfortable.

7 healthy tinctures for kids who have a cough

Young children can suffer from cough due to various reasons such as respiratory infections, allergies, or even irritation in the throat.

Coughing can be a natural response to clear out mucus or debris from the lungs, but in children, it can disrupt their daily activities and affect their sleep, appetite, and overall well-being. Coughing can also lead to vomiting, especially if the child is trying to expel the mucus, which can cause dehydration and further complications. Furthermore, persistent coughing can be a sign of an underlying condition that requires medical attention. When a child has a cough, it can be distressing for parents and caregivers as they try to find effective treatments to alleviate their child's discomfort.

Traditional treatments like over-the-counter medications can have side effects and may not be suitable for young children. This is where herbal tinctures come into play. Herbal tinctures are liquid extracts made from plants that have been used for centuries to treat various health issues, including coughs. These tinctures are often gentle and non-invasive, making them a popular choice for treating coughs in young children.

They work by soothing the throat, reducing inflammation, and loosening mucus, making it easier for children to cough up and expel.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures are known to help alleviate cough symptoms due to their anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.

The plant contains constituents like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which work together to boost the immune system and reduce the severity of a cough. When taken as a tincture, Echinacea purpurea can help soothe a sore throat, calm coughs, and even reduce mucus production. To get relief from a cough, you can take Echinacea purpurea tinctures as follows: Mix 20-30 drops of the tincture in water and drink it 2-3 times a day. You can also add it to herbal teas or soups for added flavor and benefit.

It's essential to start with a small dose and gradually increase as needed, as individual tolerance may vary. Be sure to consult with a healthcare professional before using Echinacea purpurea tinctures, especially if you're pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking medications. When taken correctly, Echinacea purpurea tinctures can lead to reduced cough severity, improved sleep quality, and a general feeling of wellness. Some people may experience a decrease in mucus production and a reduced need for cough medicines. However, it's crucial to note that Echinacea purpurea tinctures may not completely eliminate coughs, especially if caused by underlying conditions like asthma or bronchitis.

As with any herbal remedy, it's essential to monitor your body's response and adjust your dosage accordingly.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures can be a helpful natural remedy for cough relief.

This tincture is made from the root of the licorice plant, which contains compounds like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids. Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe a sore throat and reduce coughing. Flavonoids, on the other hand, have antiviral and antibacterial properties that can help fight off underlying infections causing the cough. To use Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures for cough, you can take 20-30 drops of the tincture in a glass of water 2-3 times a day.

It's essential to start with a low dose and gradually increase as needed, as excessive consumption of glycyrrhizin can cause side effects like high blood pressure and low potassium levels. Taking Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures for cough may lead to several outcomes. You may start to feel a reduction in coughing and soreness in the throat within 2-3 days. Your body may also start to fight off the underlying infection, which can help your symptoms subside faster.

However, if you have high blood pressure, kidney disease, or are taking certain medications, it's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures.

If you're experiencing a persistent cough, you can take thyme tincture by mixing a few drops with water and drinking it three to four times a day. You can also add it to a warm tea or broth to help soothe your throat and calm your cough.

To get the most out of thyme tincture, it's essential to start with a small dose and gradually increase it as needed. Some people may experience a slight burning sensation in their mouth or throat when taking thyme tincture, but this is usually temporary and harmless. When you take thyme tincture for a cough, you can expect to experience relief from congestion and coughing within a few days. Your symptoms may start to subside, and you may find it easier to sleep and breathe.

However, if your cough persists or worsens, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional to rule out any underlying conditions that may require medical attention.

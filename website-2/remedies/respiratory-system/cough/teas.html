Cough care with 7 herbal teas for seniors

As we age, our bodies change and become more prone to illnesses.

Older adults can suffer from coughs due to various reasons such as respiratory infections, allergies, or chronic conditions like chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). Coughing can be a nuisance, disrupting their sleep, daily activities, and overall well-being. It can lead to fatigue, discomfort, and even pain in the chest and throat. Moreover, older adults may have other health issues like arthritis, diabetes, or heart disease, making their bodies more vulnerable to complications from coughing. As a result, coughs can significantly impact their quality of life, affecting their ability to socialize, exercise, and engage in activities they enjoy.

Older adults often have to deal with multiple medications and health problems, which can lead to side effects and interactions that worsen their condition. This can make them feel frustrated, anxious, and isolated. In addition, some medications may not be effective in treating coughs, leading to prolonged suffering. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate coughs in older adults. These teas are made from plants, flowers, and roots that contain natural compounds with soothing properties. When consumed, they can help to calm the throat, reduce inflammation, and loosen mucus, making it easier to breathe.

They can also provide relief from congestion, coughing, and discomfort, allowing older adults to rest and recover more comfortably.

1. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas, made from the leaves of the thyme plant, have been used for centuries to help soothe a nagging cough.

The tea's active properties, including thymol and carvacrol, have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects that help to calm the throat and reduce inflammation. These constituents also work to break down mucus and thin its consistency, making it easier to cough up and clear out.

To try Thymus vulgaris teas for a cough, start by steeping one teaspoon of dried thyme leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes. Strain the liquid and drink it 2-3 times a day. You can also add a squeeze of honey to help soothe the throat.

Some people find relief from their cough within a few days of starting the tea, while others may need to continue using it for up to two weeks to see results.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas have been used for centuries to help soothe and calm a persistent cough.

The tea's active properties and constituents, such as alkylamides and caffeic acid, help to reduce inflammation and fight off infection in the respiratory tract. This makes it easier for your body to recover from a cold or flu. The tea's anti-inflammatory properties also help to ease congestion and open up airways, making it easier to breathe. When you're feeling under the weather with a cough, you can try drinking Echinacea purpurea tea to see if it helps. To do this, steep one teaspoon of dried Echinacea purpurea root in a cup of boiling water for five to seven minutes.

Then, strain the tea and drink it three to four times a day. You can also add honey or lemon to taste. If you take action and drink Echinacea purpurea tea regularly, you may start to feel relief from your cough within a few days. Your body may be able to fight off the underlying infection more effectively, and your symptoms may start to subside. Your airways may also start to open up, making it easier to breathe and sleep. However, it's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea tea is not a substitute for medical treatment.

If your cough persists or worsens, you should consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and care.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, also known as licorice root tea, can help soothe a cough due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.

The tea contains glycyrrhizin, a compound that reduces inflammation in the throat and airways, making it easier to breathe. It also contains flavonoids and saponins, which have antimicrobial properties that help fight off infections that can cause coughing. When you drink Glycyrrhiza glabra tea, the warm liquid helps loosen mucus and phlegm, making it easier to cough up and clear your airways.

To use it effectively, try drinking 1-2 cups of licorice root tea 2-3 times a day. You can also add honey or lemon to the tea to make it more palatable. As you continue to drink the tea, you may notice a reduction in coughing and a feeling of relief in your throat and chest.

Your cough may become less frequent and less severe, and you may be able to breathe more easily.

4. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas have been a long-standing remedy for soothing coughs.

The reason behind its effectiveness lies in its high concentration of eucalyptol, a compound with natural decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties. When you drink eucalyptus globulus tea, the eucalyptol helps to ease congestion in your airways, allowing you to breathe more easily and reducing the severity of your cough. The tea's menthol content also helps to thin mucus, making it easier to expel. To get relief from a cough, try drinking 1-2 cups of eucalyptus globulus tea per day, made by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

You can add honey or lemon to taste. As you drink the tea, you may feel a soothing sensation in your throat and chest, and your cough may start to subside. You might also notice that your nose and sinuses feel clearer, and you're able to breathe more easily. In some cases, you may experience a temporary reduction in fever and an improvement in overall well-being.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

5. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, commonly known as ginger tea, has been used for centuries to help with coughs.

The main reason it's effective is due to its properties and constituents, which include gingerols and shogaols - compounds that have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. These properties help to reduce inflammation in the throat and airways, making it easier to breathe and cough up mucus. Ginger also has a natural expectorant effect, which helps to loosen and clear out mucus, providing relief from a cough. To use ginger tea for a cough, you can make a tea by steeping fresh ginger in hot water or by using ginger tea bags. It's recommended to drink 1-2 cups of ginger tea per day, ideally before bedtime, to help relieve nighttime coughs. You can also add honey to your ginger tea to soothe a sore throat and calm a cough.

It's also important to note that ginger tea is not suitable for everyone, especially those with certain medical conditions, such as bleeding disorders or diabetes. It's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before using ginger tea, especially if you're taking any medications. When you start drinking ginger tea for a cough, you can expect to feel a reduction in inflammation and congestion in the throat and airways. You may also experience a decrease in the frequency and severity of coughing fits. Additionally, you may notice that you're able to breathe more easily and sleep better at night. As the mucus is cleared out, you may also notice an improvement in your overall respiratory health.

However, it's essential to note that ginger tea may not cure a cough immediately, and it's best used as a complementary treatment to other medical advice.

6. Verbascum thapsus

Verbascum thapsus teas, also known as mullein tea, has been used for centuries to help soothe a persistent cough.

The leaves of the mullein plant contain a range of properties, including mucilages, flavonoids, and saponins, that work together to calm the throat and reduce inflammation. The mucilages, in particular, are known for their ability to form a protective barrier on the mucous membranes, which can help to trap irritants and reduce the urge to cough. When consumed as a tea, these properties can help to calm a cough and promote a good night's sleep. If you're experiencing a persistent cough, you can try making a mullein tea by steeping the dried leaves in hot water.

You can add honey or lemon to taste, but be sure to use boiling water to bring out the active properties of the plant. Start by drinking a small amount of the tea, such as 1/2 cup, and see how your body reacts. You can gradually increase the amount as needed. It's also a good idea to drink the tea in the evening, as the soothing effects can help to promote a restful night's sleep. As the tea takes effect, you may notice a reduction in coughing and a feeling of calmness in the throat.

With regular use, you may also find that your cough becomes less frequent and less severe over time.

7. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra teas have been used for centuries to help soothe a cough.

The reason for this is due to the presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and glycosides in the plant. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties, which can help to calm a sore throat and reduce the severity of a cough. The tea is also rich in antioxidants, which can help to protect the body from damage caused by free radicals. If you're experiencing a cough, you can try making Sambucus nigra tea by steeping the dried flowers in hot water. To do this, start by boiling a cup of water and then adding 1-2 teaspoons of dried Sambucus nigra flowers.

Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes before straining and drinking. You can add honey or lemon to taste if desired. It's recommended to drink the tea 2-3 times a day, ideally before meals. When taking Sambucus nigra tea for a cough, you may start to feel relief within 24-48 hours. The tea may help to reduce the severity of your cough and make it easier to breathe. You may also notice a reduction in mucus production and an improvement in your overall health.

However, it's essential to note that Sambucus nigra tea is not a substitute for medical treatment, and you should consult with a healthcare professional if your symptoms persist or worsen.

7 Herbal Teas For Cough

As we age, our bodies change and become more prone to illnesses.

Older adults can suffer from coughs due to various reasons such as respiratory infections, allergies, or chronic conditions like chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). Coughing can be a nuisance, disrupting their sleep, daily activities, and overall well-being. It can lead to fatigue, discomfort, and even pain in the chest and throat. Moreover, older adults may have other health issues like arthritis, diabetes, or heart disease, making their bodies more vulnerable to complications from coughing. As a result, coughs can significantly impact their quality of life, affecting their ability to socialize, exercise, and engage in activities they enjoy.

Older adults often have to deal with multiple medications and health problems, which can lead to side effects and interactions that worsen their condition. This can make them feel frustrated, anxious, and isolated. In addition, some medications may not be effective in treating coughs, leading to prolonged suffering. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate coughs in older adults. These teas are made from plants, flowers, and roots that contain natural compounds with soothing properties. When consumed, they can help to calm the throat, reduce inflammation, and loosen mucus, making it easier to breathe.

They can also provide relief from congestion, coughing, and discomfort, allowing older adults to rest and recover more comfortably.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains compounds like eucalyptol, which is the main reason why it's often used to help soothe a cough.

This is because eucalyptol has natural anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties that can help reduce swelling in the throat and nasal passages, making it easier to breathe and swallow. The menthol content in Eucalyptus globulus teas also provides a cooling effect that can help calm the throat and ease coughing. If you're experiencing a cough, you can try drinking Eucalyptus globulus teas regularly, ideally 2-3 times a day.

To make the most of it, consider adding honey or lemon to your tea as they have their own set of health benefits that can complement the effects of Eucalyptus globulus. You can also try inhaling the steam from the tea, either by covering your head with a towel and breathing in the steam or by using a humidifier in your home. By drinking Eucalyptus globulus teas and using its steam, you may start to feel relief from your cough within a few hours. You might notice that your breathing becomes easier, your throat feels less irritated, and you're able to cough up mucus more efficiently.

However, if your cough persists or worsens, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional for further guidance and treatment.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains compounds like flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce inflammation in the throat, relieving a cough caused by colds, flu, or sinus infections. The tea also contains alkylamides, which have immunomodulatory effects, meaning they can help regulate the immune system and fight off infections. To use Echinacea purpurea teas for a cough, you can make a warm tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried root in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. You can take this tea 2-3 times a day.

Some people also combine it with other herbs like yarrow or licorice root to enhance its effects. When you drink Echinacea purpurea tea regularly, you may experience a reduction in coughing fits and a decrease in the duration of your illness. The tea's anti-inflammatory properties may also help soothe a sore throat and promote a sense of well-being. However, it's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea tea is not a substitute for medical treatment.

If your symptoms worsen or persist, consult a healthcare professional for proper evaluation and care.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.

When you drink Zingiber officinale tea, these properties help to reduce inflammation in your throat and airways, making it easier to breathe and swallow. The tea also contains antioxidants that help to protect your body from free radicals and reduce oxidative stress, which can contribute to coughing. If you're experiencing a cough, try drinking Zingiber officinale tea 2-3 times a day. To make the tea, steep 1 teaspoon of fresh ginger in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and add honey to taste, if needed. You can also add lemon juice to help increase the absorption of the active compounds. When you take action by drinking Zingiber officinale tea, you can expect to experience several benefits. The anti-inflammatory properties of the tea may help to reduce the severity and frequency of your cough, making it easier for you to sleep and go about your daily activities. The expectorant properties of the tea may also help to loosen and clear mucus from your airways, making it easier to cough up and expel.

Additionally, the antioxidants in the tea may help to boost your immune system and reduce your risk of developing complications related to your cough.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains properties that can help soothe and calm a cough.

It is rich in compounds like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory and antitussive (cough-suppressing) effects. These constituents work by reducing inflammation in the airways, which can help ease coughing, and also have a direct action on the cough receptors in the brain, helping to calm the cough. To use Glycyrrhiza glabra teas for cough, you can try the following: brew 1 teaspoon of dried roots or 2 teaspoons of fresh roots in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink.

You can take it 2-3 times a day, or as needed, for a few days to see improvements. Some people also add honey or lemon to enhance the flavor and potential benefits. When you take Glycyrrhiza glabra teas for cough, you can expect the following outcomes: the cough may become less frequent and less severe, and you may feel a sense of relief and comfort in the throat and chest. You may also notice improved sleep quality and reduced fatigue, as the anti-inflammatory effects of the tea can help reduce overall inflammation and discomfort.

However, be aware that Glycyrrhiza glabra teas can cause increased blood pressure and potassium loss in some individuals, so it's recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions.

5. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas contains compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which are known for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These constituents help to soothe a sore throat and reduce the severity of coughs caused by viral or bacterial infections. When you drink Thymus vulgaris tea, the thymol and carvacrol bind to the receptors in your throat, reducing inflammation and killing the underlying infection that's causing the cough. To get relief from a cough, try making a tea by steeping one teaspoon of dried Thymus vulgaris leaves in a cup of boiling water for five to seven minutes. Strain the liquid and drink it warm or at room temperature, ideally three to four times a day.

You can also add honey to the tea to make it more palatable, as honey has its own soothing properties that can help to calm a cough. If you experience any discomfort or pain while taking the tea, discontinue use immediately and consult a healthcare professional. As you continue to drink Thymus vulgaris tea, you may start to notice a reduction in the frequency and severity of your cough. The anti-inflammatory compounds in the tea may help to reduce swelling in your throat, making it easier to swallow and breathe. In some cases, the tea may even help to clear up the underlying infection, allowing your body to recover more quickly.

However, if your cough persists or worsens, or if you experience difficulty breathing or other concerning symptoms, seek medical attention to rule out any underlying conditions that require medical attention.

The antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties of the tea may help your body fight off the underlying infection, allowing your cough to clear up more quickly. Additionally, the soothing properties of the tea may help calm a sore throat and promote a restful night's sleep.

As with any herbal remedy, be sure to drink the tea in moderation and consult with a healthcare professional if your symptoms persist or worsen.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent cough from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe a sore throat and calm a cough.

Some herbal teas, like thyme and peppermint, have natural ingredients that can help reduce inflammation and ease congestion.

When you drink these teas, they can help loosen up mucus and make it easier to cough up.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for cough every day?

Herbal teas can be a soothing way to calm a cough.

However, drinking them daily can have its downsides. Some herbal teas, like thyme and eucalyptus, can be too potent and may cause stomach upset or interact with medications.

It's best to enjoy them in moderation and choose gentler options like peppermint or chamomile.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in cough?

Herbal teas can help soothe a cough, but how long it takes to work depends on the tea and your body.

Some people notice relief within 30 minutes to an hour after drinking a warm cup. Others might feel better after a few hours or a full day.

It's different for everyone.

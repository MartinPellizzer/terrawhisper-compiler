What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have cough?

If you have a cough, there are some herbal decoctions you should avoid using.

One such herb is 'Valeriana officinalis' (valerian root). While it's known for its calming effects, it can cause drowsiness and slow down your breathing, making your cough worse. Another herb to avoid is 'Hydrastis canadensis' (goldenseal), which contains a compound called berberine.

Berberine can interact with certain medications and worsen conditions like high blood pressure or heart problems. You should also avoid using 'Aconitum napellus' (monk's hood) decoctions, as they contain a toxic compound called aconitine. Aconitine can cause serious side effects, including heart problems, nausea, and even death. Similarly, decoctions made from 'Strychnos nux-vomica' (strychnine tree) should be avoided due to the presence of strychnine, a highly toxic substance that can cause seizures, muscle spasms, and respiratory failure.

Lastly, decoctions made from 'Taxus baccata' (yew) should be avoided due to the presence of taxine, a toxic compound that can cause heart problems, seizures, and even death.

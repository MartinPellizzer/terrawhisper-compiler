8 Herbal Essential Oils For Cough

Herbal essential oils are a great way to relieve a cough, and they work by targeting the root causes of the problem.

When you have a cough, it's usually because your airways are inflamed, making it hard to breathe. Herbal teas, made from plants like Eucalyptus globulus, Thymus vulgaris, and Echinacea purpurea, can help to calm down this inflammation and clear out any mucus that's causing the cough.

Eucalyptus, for example, has a cooling effect that can help to open up your airways, making it easier to breathe. Thyme, on the other hand, has antibacterial properties that can help to fight off any underlying infections that might be causing the cough. Echinacea is known for its immune-boosting properties, which can help to prevent coughs from happening in the first place.

By drinking herbal teas or inhaling the essential oils of these plants, you can start to feel relief from a cough and get back to your normal self.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains several bioactive constituents, including eucalyptol, limonene, and alpha-pinene.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties, which can help to relieve coughs caused by congestion. Eucalyptol, in particular, is known for its expectorant properties, helping to loosen and clear mucus from the airways. The decongestant properties of limonene and alpha-pinene help to reduce swelling in the nasal passages and sinuses, making it easier to breathe.

When inhaled, the vapors of Eucalyptus globulus essential oils can also help to soothe the throat and calm coughs.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water on low heat, then add the Eucalyptus leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. After 30 minutes, turn off the heat and let it cool for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture into a bowl using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve. Discard the leaves.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and let it sit for 2-3 weeks to allow the oil to separate and mature.

2. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol and carvacrol, two potent compounds that help relieve coughs.

Thymol has antimicrobial properties, which help combat the underlying infections that cause coughs. Carvacrol has anti-inflammatory properties, reducing swelling in the throat and making it easier to breathe. Thymol and carvacrol also have expectorant properties, helping to loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to cough up.

By targeting the root causes of coughs, Thymus vulgaris essential oils provides relief from persistent and irritating coughs.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves. Choose healthy leaves with no signs of pests or diseases.
  2. Wash the leaves with 1 cup of water to remove dirt and bacteria. Gently swish the leaves and let them sit for 5 minutes.
  3. Drain the water and place the leaves in a clean glass jar. Add 2 cups of water and seal the jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a warm place for 2-3 hours. Shake the jar occasionally to help the oil release.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic compounds as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties that help soothe a cough. The glycosides, specifically echinacoside and caftaric acid, have been found to reduce inflammation in the throat and airways, making it easier to breathe and swallow. The phenolic compounds, like caffeic acid, have antimicrobial properties that inhibit the growth of bacteria and viruses that can cause coughs.

By reducing inflammation and inhibiting the growth of pathogens, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help alleviate a cough and promote recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Dry the Echinacea flowers in a warm oven at 150°F (65°C) for 2 hours or air-dry them for 2-3 days.
  3. Combine 1/4 cup of dried Echinacea flowers with 1 cup of carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. Then, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth.
  5. Discard the solids and transfer the resulting essential oil to a clean glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops per serving for cough relief.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains pinene, camphor, and borneol as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, which help to reduce congestion and loosen phlegm. Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils also contains camphor, which has a decongestant effect, helping to clear the airways and relieve cough. The antispasmodic properties of borneol help to soothe and calm the muscles in the throat, reducing the severity of coughing.

By combining these properties, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help to alleviate cough and promote a sense of comfort.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and stems, and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba oil).
  2. Clean and dry the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and stems with a clean cloth.
  3. Place 1 cup of the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and stems in a clean glass jar, and add 1 cup of carrier oil.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day, to allow the essential oil to extract.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, and discard the solids. Use the extracted essential oil as needed for cough relief.

5. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains limonene, linalool, and menthone as its active constituents.

These compounds give the oil its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help to reduce the swelling and infection that can cause a cough. The menthone in the oil acts as a natural expectorant, helping to loosen and clear mucus from the lungs and airways, making it easier to breathe. The linalool in the oil also has a sedative effect, which can help to calm the body and reduce the severity of a cough.

When inhaled, the vapors of Mentha x piperita essential oils can help to ease the symptoms of a cough and promote a sense of relaxation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and flowers. Clean them with water.
  2. Add 1 cup of water to a saucepan and bring it to a boil.
  3. Add the cleaned Mentha x piperita leaves and flowers to the saucepan. Reduce heat to low and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture into a bowl using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2 hours to allow the essential oil to separate. Then, scoop the oil from the top and store it in a dark glass bottle.

6. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains active constituents like gingerol and shogaol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, which help to relieve congestion and cough. Gingerol's ability to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals, like prostaglandins, reduces swelling in the airways and makes it easier to breathe. Shogaol's expectorant properties help to loosen and clear mucus, making it easier to cough up.

The combination of these properties in Zingiber officinale essential oils helps to soothe and calm the respiratory system, providing relief from cough and congestion.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale roots (Ginger roots). Wash them clean and pat dry.
  2. Use a juicer or blender to extract the juice from the ginger roots. Get 2 cups of juice.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) to the ginger juice. Mix well.
  4. Heat the mixture in a saucepan over low heat for 2 hours. Stir occasionally.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Store in a cool, dark place.

7. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains flavonoids and saponins as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties that help reduce inflammation in the throat and lungs, making it easier to cough up mucus. Glycyrrhizin, a key component of the oil, has a soothing effect on the respiratory system and helps thin mucus, making it easier to expel. The antiseptic properties of the oil also help combat infections that can cause coughing.

By reducing inflammation and thinning mucus, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can provide relief from coughing.

  1. Gather materials: 1 cup of fresh Glycyrrhiza glabra roots, a glass jar, a cheesecloth, a cup of water, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Dry the roots: Dry the Glycyrrhiza glabra roots in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 2 hours or until dry.
  3. Make the infusion: Combine 1 cup of dried roots with 1 cup of water in the glass jar. Let it sit at room temperature for 2 hours.
  4. Filter the mixture: Strain the infusion through the cheesecloth into another container, discarding the solids.
  5. Mix with carrier oil: Add 1 cup of carrier oil to the filtered mixture and stir well to create the essential oil.

8. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its major bioactive constituents.

These compounds have expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to relieve cough symptoms by thinning mucus and reducing inflammation in the respiratory tract. Linalool in particular has been shown to have a calming effect on the respiratory system, reducing spasms and irritation that can exacerbate coughing. The antiseptic properties of linalyl acetate also help to combat infections that can cause coughs, such as colds and flu.

When inhaled, these compounds can help to ease coughing and promote a smoother, more comfortable breathing experience.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers, 1 cup of water, a saucepan, and a glass container.
  2. Combine the flowers and water in the saucepan and heat it on low fire for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the glass container and discard the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 2-3 hours to separate from the essential oil, then carefully pour the essential oil into a smaller container and use it for your cough remedy.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent cough from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe a sore throat and calm a cough.

Some teas, like peppermint and ginger, have natural anti-inflammatory properties that can ease irritation and reduce coughing.

These teas can be especially helpful when you're feeling under the weather and need a gentle way to calm your throat.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for cough every day?

It's generally okay to have herbal teas for a cough daily, but be mindful of the ingredients.

Some herbs, like licorice root, can be too strong and cause problems if taken regularly.

Also, if you're pregnant or breastfeeding, it's best to check the tea's ingredients and possible interactions with your body.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in cough?

Herbal teas can help soothe a cough, but it's hard to say exactly how long it takes to feel better.

For some people, relief might come within 30 minutes to an hour after drinking a warm cup.

For others, it could take a few hours or even a full day to notice a difference in their cough.

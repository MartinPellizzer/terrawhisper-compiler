8 Herbal Essential Oils For Cough

People with asthma often have trouble controlling their breathing, and one of the most annoying symptoms is a persistent cough.

This cough can be triggered by anything from dust to pollen, and can be really hard to shake. For asthmatics, a cough can be more than just an annoyance - it can be a constant companion that disrupts their daily lives, making it hard to sleep, work, or even enjoy everyday activities. It can be embarrassing, too, especially in social situations. And if the cough is severe, it can even lead to fatigue, headaches, and other complications. Asthmatics often try to manage their cough with medications, but sometimes these don't work well, or they can have unpleasant side effects. That's why many people with asthma are looking for natural solutions to help calm their cough.

Herbal essential oils have been shown to be effective in soothing and calming coughs. These oils are made from the extracts of plants, which have been used for centuries to treat various health issues. They work by reducing inflammation and opening up airways, making it easier to breathe. When inhaled, these oils can also help to calm the nervous system, which can contribute to a cough. By using these oils, asthmatics can find some relief from their persistent cough, and that's a big relief. When using these oils, it's essential to use them correctly and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

This way, you can ensure that you're getting the most benefit from these natural remedies.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains properties that make it a popular remedy for cough relief.

It contains compounds like eucalyptol, a natural decongestant, and camphor, which helps to loosen and clear mucus from the airways. When inhaled, eucalyptol and camphor work together to thin out mucus, making it easier to expel, and also have anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce swelling in the throat and nasal passages. If you're experiencing a cough, you can try inhaling eucalyptus globulus essential oils through steam inhalation. To do this, add a few drops of the oil to a bowl of hot water, then lean over the bowl with a towel over your head to trap the steam.

Inhale the steam for a few minutes to help loosen and clear mucus from your airways. You can also add eucalyptus globulus essential oils to a humidifier or diffuser to help keep the air moist and make it easier to breathe. Taking action to inhale eucalyptus globulus essential oils through steam inhalation can lead to noticeable relief from cough and congestion. You may start to feel your airways opening up, making it easier to breathe, and your cough may become less frequent and less intense.

By loosening and clearing mucus, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can also help to prevent further congestion and coughing, reducing your risk of developing more serious respiratory infections.

2. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce the severity of a cough by fighting off the underlying infection and inflammation in the respiratory tract. Thymol, in particular, has been shown to have a decongestant effect, which helps to loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to breathe. To use Thymus vulgaris essential oils for a cough, add a few drops to a diffuser or inhale it directly from a cloth or handkerchief. You can also mix a few drops with a carrier oil and rub it onto your chest or back to help open up the airways.

When using essential oils, always dilute them with a carrier oil and do a patch test before applying them to a larger area of skin. When you take action and use Thymus vulgaris essential oils for a cough, you can expect to see a reduction in the severity and frequency of your cough. Your airways may become clearer, and you may find it easier to breathe. Additionally, the antimicrobial properties of thymol and carvacrol may help to fight off the underlying infection, reducing the risk of complications and speeding up the recovery process.

However, it's essential to note that essential oils should not be used as a replacement for medical treatment, but rather as a complementary therapy to support your recovery.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains powerful properties and constituents that can help alleviate cough symptoms.

It's rich in phenolic acids, flavonoids, and alkylamides, which are responsible for its immune-boosting and anti-inflammatory effects. The alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to reduce inflammation in the airways and fight off pathogens that cause respiratory infections, making it an effective natural remedy for coughs.

When using Echinacea purpurea essential oils for cough, take a few drops (about 2-3) and mix it with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil. Apply it to your chest or inhale it through steam inhalation to help soothe your throat and calm your cough. By taking this action, you can expect to experience relief from coughing and a reduction in the severity of your symptoms.

You may also notice an improvement in your overall health and well-being as your body's natural defenses are boosted.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains compounds like camphor, borneol, and 1,8-cineole, which work together to help ease a cough.

These constituents have natural expectorant properties, meaning they help loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, making it easier to breathe. When you inhale Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil, the 1,8-cineole specifically helps to thin out mucus, making it less sticky and easier to cough up.

To use Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil for a cough, you can add a few drops to a diffuser and inhale the vapors, or mix it with a carrier oil and apply it to your chest. You can also add a few drops to a bowl of hot water, inhale the steam, and then cough up the loosened mucus. When you take these actions, you may start to feel your cough easing up, and you might be able to breathe more easily.

You may also find that you're producing more mucus, which can be a good sign that the oil is working to clear out your lungs.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent cough from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe a sore throat and calm a cough.

Some teas, like peppermint and ginger, have natural anti-inflammatory properties that can ease irritation and reduce coughing.

These teas can be especially helpful when you're feeling under the weather and need a gentle way to calm your throat.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for cough every day?

It's generally okay to have herbal teas for a cough daily, but be mindful of the ingredients.

Some herbs, like licorice root, can be too strong and cause problems if taken regularly.

Also, if you're pregnant or breastfeeding, it's best to check the tea's ingredients and possible interactions with your body.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in cough?

Herbal teas can help soothe a cough, but it's hard to say exactly how long it takes to feel better.

For some people, relief might come within 30 minutes to an hour after drinking a warm cup.

For others, it could take a few hours or even a full day to notice a difference in their cough.

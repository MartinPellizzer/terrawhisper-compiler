8 herbal essential oils for cough in asthma patients

People with asthma often have trouble controlling their breathing, and one of the most annoying symptoms is a persistent cough.

This cough can be triggered by anything from dust to pollen, and can be really hard to shake. For asthmatics, a cough can be more than just an annoyance - it can be a constant companion that disrupts their daily lives, making it hard to sleep, work, or even enjoy everyday activities. It can be embarrassing, too, especially in social situations. And if the cough is severe, it can even lead to fatigue, headaches, and other complications. Asthmatics often try to manage their cough with medications, but sometimes these don't work well, or they can have unpleasant side effects. That's why many people with asthma are looking for natural solutions to help calm their cough.

Herbal essential oils have been shown to be effective in soothing and calming coughs. These oils are made from the extracts of plants, which have been used for centuries to treat various health issues. They work by reducing inflammation and opening up airways, making it easier to breathe. When inhaled, these oils can also help to calm the nervous system, which can contribute to a cough. By using these oils, asthmatics can find some relief from their persistent cough, and that's a big relief. When using these oils, it's essential to use them correctly and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

This way, you can ensure that you're getting the most benefit from these natural remedies.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils are a natural remedy for cough relief.

They work by opening up airways and reducing inflammation, making it easier to breathe. This is due to the presence of compounds like eucalyptol, a natural decongestant and expectorant. Eucalyptol helps break down mucus, allowing it to be coughed up and relieving congestion.

The oil also has anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling in the throat and nasal passages. To use Eucalyptus globulus essential oils for cough, add a few drops to a diffuser or inhale it directly from a cloth or handkerchief. You can also mix it with a carrier oil and apply it to your chest or back, but make sure to dilute it properly to avoid skin irritation. As the oil takes effect, you may start to feel your airways opening up and your congestion easing.

Your cough may become less frequent and less intense, and you may be able to breathe more easily.

2. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils, commonly known as peppermint oil, is a natural remedy for coughs.

Limonene, on the other hand, has expectorant properties that help bring up mucus from the lungs.

To use Mentha x piperita essential oils for a cough, add a few drops to a diffuser or inhale it directly from a cloth or handkerchief. Inhaling peppermint oil can help relieve coughs and congestion within 15-30 minutes. As the mucus is loosened and the nasal passages are cleared, you may experience a reduction in coughing and a sense of relief in your chest.

However, if you have a severe or persistent cough, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

When using thymus vulgaris essential oils for cough, it's recommended to mix a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil, and apply it topically to the chest and throat area.

Soothing Coughs in Older Adults: 10 Herbal Creams to the Rescue

As people age, their bodies become more vulnerable to illness and injury.

Older adults are more likely to suffer from cough, a common symptom of various health conditions such as colds, flu, and chronic respiratory diseases like bronchitis and pneumonia. Coughing can be a real nuisance, making it hard for older adults to sleep, eat, and even breathe. It can also lead to fatigue, dehydration, and further complications like pneumonia. For older adults with pre-existing health conditions, cough can be particularly concerning as it can worsen their condition. Moreover, cough can be a sign of an underlying condition that needs medical attention.

Older adults may also experience a decline in their immune system, making them more susceptible to respiratory infections. When it comes to treating cough, older adults often rely on over-the-counter medications, which can have side effects and interact with other medications they may be taking. This is where herbal creams come in. These creams are made from natural ingredients that are known to soothe and calm the throat, reducing inflammation and relieving cough symptoms. By applying a topical cream directly to the affected area, older adults can experience quick relief from cough without having to take medication orally. This can be especially beneficial for older adults who may be sensitive to certain ingredients or have difficulty swallowing pills.

Herbal creams can provide a natural and gentle way to manage cough symptoms, giving older adults some much-needed relief and comfort.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams have been traditionally used to help alleviate coughs due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The creams typically contain the active compounds alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which work together to soothe and protect the mucous membranes in the throat. The alkylamides help to reduce inflammation and congestion, while the glycosides have antiviral properties that may help combat underlying infections causing the cough. If you're experiencing a persistent cough, consider applying Echinacea purpurea cream topically to the affected area, ideally 2-3 times a day.

Gently massage a small amount of cream into the skin around your throat, avoiding the eyes and mouth. You can also add a few drops of the cream to your child's chest if they're old enough to use it safely under adult supervision. As the cream works to reduce inflammation and fight off infection, you may notice a decrease in cough frequency and intensity. The glycosides may also help to stimulate the immune system, promoting a faster recovery from the underlying illness.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, consult a healthcare professional for further guidance and treatment.

To use Mentha x piperita creams for cough, apply a small amount to the affected area 2-3 times a day.

You can do this by gently massaging the cream onto your chest and throat using a circular motion. Be sure to wash your hands after applying the cream to avoid transferring it to your eyes or other areas of your skin. It's also essential to consult with a healthcare professional if your cough persists or worsens over time, as this could be a sign of an underlying condition that requires medical attention. Using Mentha x piperita creams for cough can lead to several outcomes, including reduced coughing and congestion, relief from soreness and discomfort, and improved breathing and swallowing. You may also notice a decrease in the amount of mucus produced, making it easier to clear your airways.

However, it's essential to note that these creams are not a substitute for medical treatment and should only be used in conjunction with other recommended therapies.

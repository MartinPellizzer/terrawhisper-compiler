10 Herbal Creams For Cough

When you have a cough, you want to find a way to relieve it quickly and naturally.

Herbal creams can be a great option. These creams often contain herbs that are known to help soothe a sore throat and calm a cough. One example is Eucalyptus globulus, also known as eucalyptus oil. It has a fresh, minty smell and is often used in cough medicines. It helps to thin and clear mucus from your lungs, making it easier to breathe. Another herb that's often used in herbal creams is Thymus vulgaris, or thyme.

Thyme has antibacterial properties, which can help to fight off any underlying infections that may be causing your cough. This can be especially helpful if you have a cold or flu. Echinacea purpurea, also known as coneflower, is another herb that's sometimes used in cough creams. It's believed to have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling in your throat and make it easier to swallow. Herbal creams can be a convenient way to relieve a cough, especially if you're not a fan of taking pills or drinking tea. They can be applied directly to your skin, where they can be absorbed quickly and start working to soothe your throat and calm your cough.

Some herbal creams even come in spray forms, making them easy to use on-the-go.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains eucalyptol, a key bioactive constituent that helps to relieve a cough.

Eucalyptol has a decongestant property that helps to clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to breathe. It also has an expectorant property, which helps to thin and loosen mucus, making it easier to cough up. Additionally, eucalyptol has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce swelling in the throat and airways, making it less irritated and sore.

By combining these properties, eucalyptus globulus creams can help to provide fast and effective relief from a cough.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 10 drops of eucalyptus essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl for 30 seconds.
  3. Add 1/4 cup of beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated.
  4. Remove from heat and add 10 drops of eucalyptus essential oil. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify before use.

2. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams contains thymol and carvacrol, which are the active constituents that make it effective against coughs.

Thymol has antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce the severity of a cough by targeting the underlying infection. Carvacrol, on the other hand, has expectorant properties, which help to loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to breathe. The antimicrobial properties of thymol also help to combat secondary infections that can exacerbate a cough.

By reducing inflammation and loosening mucus, Thymus vulgaris creams can help to provide relief from coughs and promote a smoother recovery.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 20 drops of Thymus vulgaris essential oil.
  2. Melt the beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval until melted.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the distilled water and Thymus vulgaris essential oil. Stir well to combine.
  4. Slowly add the melted beeswax to the water mixture, stirring constantly until well combined.
  5. Add the carrier oil to the mixture and stir well. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let it cool before use.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents like alkylamides, glycoproteins, and polyphenols.

These compounds work together to stimulate the immune system and reduce inflammation in the throat, helping to alleviate cough symptoms. The alkylamides, in particular, have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe irritated mucous membranes and ease congestion. The glycoproteins, also known as polysaccharides, have been shown to stimulate the production of white blood cells, which helps to fight off infections that can cause cough.

By combining these properties, Echinacea purpurea creams may help to provide quick relief from cough and cold symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Heat coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat, stirring until beeswax melts.
  3. Add dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the melted oil mixture and steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture and discard the flowers. Add shea butter and vitamin E oil, stirring until combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Use as a cough cream or store in the fridge for later use.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains the active constituents gingerol and shogaol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling in the throat and airways. The analgesic and antispasmodic properties of gingerol and shogaol also help alleviate coughing by relaxing the muscles in the respiratory tract. Additionally, the expectorant properties of Zingiber officinale creams help loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, making it easier to cough up.

By reducing inflammation, relaxing muscles, and loosening mucus, Zingiber officinale creams can provide relief from coughing.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of honey, and 1 teaspoon of dried Zingiber officinale powder.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add shea butter and honey to the melted mixture. Stir until well combined.
  4. Add dried Zingiber officinale powder to the mixture and stir well. Remove from heat.
  5. Let the mixture cool and thicken. Pour into containers and refrigerate until solid. Use as needed to soothe cough.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin that has anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.

These properties help to reduce inflammation in the throat and lungs, making it easier to breathe and cough. Glycyrrhizin also has a soothing effect on the mucous membranes, which helps to calm a persistent cough. The cream's anti-inflammatory properties also help to reduce congestion and mucus production, making it easier to clear out irritants from the airways.

Additionally, glycyrrhizin has been shown to have a protective effect on the mucous membranes, helping to prevent damage from excessive coughing.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of glycerin, 2 tablespoons of glycyrrhiza glabra powder, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Mix 1 cup of distilled water and 2 tablespoons of glycerin in a saucepan and heat over low heat.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of glycyrrhiza glabra powder to the saucepan and stir until the powder dissolves.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool. Add 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let it cool completely. Store the cream in the refrigerator before use.

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains menthol and menthone, two key bioactive constituents.

Menthol has a cooling effect that helps to numb the throat and reduce inflammation, providing quick relief from coughs. Menthone, on the other hand, has expectorant properties that help to loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, making it easier to cough up. The antiseptic and antimicrobial properties of menthol also help to reduce the growth of bacteria and fungi in the throat, further combating coughs.

By combining these properties, menthol and menthone in Mentha x piperita creams work together to alleviate cough symptoms and promote a faster recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of Mentha x piperita essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Stir until the beeswax is fully dissolved, then remove from heat.
  4. Add the Mentha x piperita essential oil and stir well. Let the mixture cool until it reaches 120°F to 130°F.
  5. Pour the mixture into a tin or a jar and let it cool completely. Once set, the Mentha x piperita cream is ready to use.

7. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra creams contains bioactive constituents like isorhapontigenin, robinin, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties that help soothe and calm the throat and respiratory tract. The flavonoids in Sambucus nigra creams also have antioxidant properties that help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the body. The antiviral properties of isorhapontigenin and robinin help combat viral infections that cause cough, such as the common cold and flu.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off viral infections, Sambucus nigra creams can help relieve cough and promote a faster recovery.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Sambucus nigra flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Infuse coconut oil with Sambucus nigra flowers: Fill a clean glass jar with the flowers and pour in coconut oil. Store in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  3. Strain and mix the infused oil: Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Add beeswax, shea butter, and vitamin E oil. Heat the mixture in a double boiler until the beeswax melts.
  4. Cool and whip the mixture: Remove the bowl from the heat and let it cool. Once cool, use a hand mixer to whip the mixture until it's light and fluffy.
  5. Pour into containers and store: Pour the Sambucus nigra cream into clean containers. Store in a cool, dry place and use as needed to soothe coughs.

8. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains essential oils like camphor, borneol, and borneol acetate.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties that help ease cough. Camphor's analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects help soothe the throat, while borneol's expectorant properties help loosen and clear mucus. Borneol acetate's mucolytic properties break down thick mucus, making it easier to expel.

The combination of these properties in Rosmarinus officinalis creams helps to calm and relieve coughs.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of honey, and 2 tablespoons of dried Rosmarinus officinalis (rosemary) leaves.
  2. Melt the beeswax, shea butter, and coconut oil in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl.
  3. Add the distilled water, honey, and dried rosemary leaves to the melted mixture. Stir well.
  4. Heat the mixture for 10-15 minutes or until the rosemary is fully infused. Strain the mixture to remove the rosemary leaves.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool. Once set, your Rosmarinus officinalis cough cream is ready to use.

9. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis creams contains quinine, a natural compound that helps to soothe and calm a persistent cough.

The alkaloids present in Cinchona officinalis, including quinine, cinchonine, and cinchonidine, have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling in the throat, making it easier to breathe and swallow. These alkaloids also have an antitussive effect, directly targeting the cough receptors in the brain to reduce coughing. Additionally, the quinine in Cinchona officinalis creams has an antihistamine property, which helps to combat allergies and colds that can cause a cough.

The combination of these properties makes Cinchona officinalis creams a natural and effective remedy for a persistent cough.

  1. Gather 250g of Cinchona officinalis bark and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water and Cinchona bark in the saucepan over low heat for 1 hour, then let it cool.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discarding the bark.
  4. Combine 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, and 2 tablespoons of the Cinchona infusion in a saucepan.
  5. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring until the beeswax dissolves, then pour into a container to cool and set.

10. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains a high concentration of linalool and linalyl acetate, which are potent anti-inflammatory compounds.

These properties help to reduce inflammation in the throat, making it a popular natural remedy for coughs. Linalool, in particular, has been shown to have a soothing effect on the respiratory tract, easing congestion and promoting a smoother flow of air. Additionally, the creams' antimicrobial properties help to fight off infections that can exacerbate coughs.

By targeting the root causes of coughs, Lavandula angustifolia creams provide a comprehensive and natural solution for soothing irritated throats.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups distilled water, 1 cup coconut oil, 1 cup beeswax, 10 drops lavender essential oil, 1 tablespoon honey.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove melted mixture from heat and stir in distilled water, lavender essential oil, and honey until well combined.
  4. Pour mixture into a clean glass jar or container. Let it cool and harden completely.
  5. Once hardened, scoop out a small amount of the cream and apply it to the affected area to help soothe a cough.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent cough from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe a sore throat and calm a cough.

Some teas, like peppermint or ginger, have anti-inflammatory properties that can ease throat irritation. Others, like thyme or eucalyptus, have antimicrobial properties that can help fight off underlying infections that cause coughs.

This might reduce the severity of a cough.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for cough every day?

Consuming herbal teas for cough every day can be safe if you choose teas made from natural ingredients like peppermint, ginger, or eucalyptus.

These teas are generally gentle and won't harm your body.

However, be cautious of teas containing strong herbs like licorice root, which can be problematic in large amounts.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in cough?

Herbal teas can provide relief from a cough in a few hours to a few days.

Some people find that ginger tea helps to soothe a sore throat and calm a cough within a few hours. Peppermint tea can also ease congestion and coughs in a short time.

The effects may vary depending on the individual and the specific tea used.

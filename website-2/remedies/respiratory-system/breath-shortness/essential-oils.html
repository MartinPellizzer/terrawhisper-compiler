1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains a compound called eucalyptol, which is known for its decongestant properties.

This helps to break down mucus in the airways, making it easier to breathe. Eucalyptol also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce swelling and ease congestion in the lungs. Additionally, eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains other compounds like camphor and borneol, which help to open up airways and improve circulation.

By using eucalyptus globulus essential oils, you can help to alleviate shortness of breath by reducing congestion and inflammation in the airways.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a pot.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Add the Eucalyptus leaves to the pot and let it steep for 1 hour.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a bowl.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 24 hours to allow the essential oils to separate. Skim off the top layer of oil and store it in a dark glass bottle.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains the active constituents camphor, borneol, and borneol acetate.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help to relax the airway muscles and reduce inflammation in the lungs. By doing so, it can ease the constriction of airways, making it easier to breathe. The decongestant and expectorant properties of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils also help to relieve congestion and promote the removal of excess mucus from the lungs.

This can be particularly beneficial for people experiencing shortness of breath due to respiratory conditions.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and stems.
  2. Use a juicer or blender to extract the oil from the leaves. Get 4 tablespoons of the extracted oil.
  3. Transfer the extracted oil to a clean glass container.
  4. Add 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the glass container with the extracted oil.
  5. Mix well and store the Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil in a cool, dark place.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its therapeutic properties.

Gingerols, specifically 6-gingerol and 8-gingerol, have been found to have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling in the airways and ease congestion. These anti-inflammatory properties can help open up airways and improve breathing, making Zingiber officinale essential oils beneficial for shortness of breath. Additionally, Zingiber officinale essential oils also contain a compound called shogaol, which has been shown to have bronchodilatory effects, meaning it can help relax airway muscles and improve lung function.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing airway muscles, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help alleviate shortness of breath and make breathing easier.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale (Ginger) roots. Clean and dry them.
  2. In a blender, mix 1 cup of the dried ginger roots with 2 cups of water. Blend until smooth.
  3. Transfer the mixture to a saucepan. Heat it over low heat for 2 hours, stirring occasionally.
  4. After heating, let the mixture cool. Strain it through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. To extract the essential oil, add 1/4 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the strained mixture. Stir well and let it sit for 24 hours. Strain and discard the solids. Your Zingiber officinale essential oil is now ready for use.

4. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains compounds like thymol and carvacrol as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce congestion and open up airways. The decongestant properties of thymol and carvacrol work together to ease shortness of breath by loosening mucus and reducing swelling in the nasal passages and lungs. Thymus vulgaris essential oils' expectorant properties also help to thin out mucus, making it easier to cough up and breathe.

By reducing inflammation and loosening mucus, Thymus vulgaris essential oils can provide relief from shortness of breath.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water to 100°F (38°C) and add the Thymus vulgaris leaves.
  3. Steep the mixture for 2-3 hours, then strain the leaves from the water using cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and add 1 tablespoon of vodka as a preservative.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 days, then use a dropper to extract the essential oil from the liquid and store it in a separate container.

5. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains boiactive constituents like menthone, limonene, and linalool.

These compounds have decongestant and expectorant properties, which help to clear airways and reduce congestion. The menthone in Mentha x piperita essential oils acts as a bronchodilator, relaxing airway muscles and making it easier to breathe. The limonene in this essential oil has a mild antiseptic effect, helping to combat infections that can cause shortness of breath.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy respiratory system, Mentha x piperita essential oils can help alleviate shortness of breath.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh peppermint leaves (Mentha x piperita) and clean them with water.
  2. Place the leaves in a glass jar and add 1 cup of water. Cover the jar with a lid.
  3. Let the mixture sit in a dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the oil to extract from the leaves.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a small saucepan and heat it gently over low heat. Collect the oil that rises to the surface using a dropper or a spoon, and store it in a dark glass bottle.

6. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains a variety of bioactive constituents, including alkylamides, caffeic acid, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract and protect against oxidative stress. The flavonoids present in the essential oil, such as quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to have a bronchodilatory effect, which can help open up airways and improve breathing. Additionally, the alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea essential oil have been found to have a relaxant effect on the smooth muscle of the respiratory tract, which can help alleviate bronchospasm and shortness of breath.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation of the airway muscles, Echinacea purpurea essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of shortness of breath.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Place the jar in a warm, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the oil to infuse.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the infused oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-10 drops per use for shortness of breath relief.

8. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains thujone, borneol, and camphor as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have decongestant and expectorant properties, which can help relieve shortness of breath by thinning mucus and reducing inflammation in the airways. The decongestant properties of borneol and camphor can also help to open up airways and improve breathing. Thujone's anti-inflammatory properties can reduce swelling in the airways, further easing breathing difficulties.

By combining these properties, Salvia officinalis essential oils can provide relief from shortness of breath.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat, then reduce heat to low and add the fresh leaves.
  3. Steep the leaves for 2 hours, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a bowl.
  4. Combine the strained liquid with 1/4 cup of carrier oil, such as jojoba or sweet almond oil, in a separate bowl.
  5. Store the mixture in a dark glass bottle and let it sit for 2-3 days, shaking the bottle daily, before using it as an essential oil for shortness of breath.

9. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help improve blood flow and reduce inflammation in the lungs. The flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, have a vasodilatory effect, which means they help to widen blood vessels and improve oxygen delivery to the body's tissues. This can help alleviate shortness of breath by increasing oxygen supply to the body.

The terpenoids in Ginkgo biloba essential oils, including ginkgolides and bilobalide, have anti-inflammatory properties that further contribute to improved lung function.

  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a new container, discarding the leaves.
  5. Let the oil cool and store it in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid for up to 6 months.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent shortness of breath from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help with shortness of breath caused by congestion.

Peppermint tea can ease airway inflammation, while ginger tea can reduce swelling. Thyme tea has natural decongestant properties that can open up airways and make breathing easier.

This can give relief from shortness of breath, especially when combined with a healthy lifestyle.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for shortness of breath every day?

While herbal teas can be a soothing way to manage shortness of breath, consuming them daily might not be the best idea.

Some herbs like peppermint and ginger can help ease breathing, but others like licorice root and ginseng can cause issues if taken excessively.

It's essential to be mindful of the herbs you choose and their potential effects on your body.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in shortness of breath?

Herbal teas can help with shortness of breath, but it depends on the type of tea and how often you drink it.

Some teas, like ginger and peppermint, may work within a few days to a week.

Others, like thyme and licorice root, might take longer, often up to two weeks to start showing results.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for shortness of breath?

If you experience shortness of breath, drinking herbal tea in the morning or early afternoon might be helpful.

This allows the tea's relaxing effects to take hold before you engage in physical activities or stressful situations.

As the tea's soothing properties start to wear off, you can reassess your breathing and try to calm down further if needed.

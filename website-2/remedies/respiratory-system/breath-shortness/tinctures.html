1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains several bioactive constituents, including alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and polysaccharides.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling in the airways, which is a common cause of shortness of breath. The flavonoids present in Echinacea purpurea, such as kaempferol and quercetin, have antioxidant effects that can help protect the lungs from oxidative stress and damage. The polysaccharides in Echinacea purpurea have immunomodulatory properties, which can help regulate the immune response and reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress in the airways, Echinacea purpurea tinctures may help alleviate symptoms of shortness of breath.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and roots.
  2. Combine 1 cup of Echinacea with 2 cups of 80% vodka or 95% ethanol in a clean glass jar.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, as needed for shortness of breath.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contains eucalyptol, a bioactive constituent that helps open airways and relieve congestion.

Eucalyptol works by thinning mucus and making it easier to expel, which can help alleviate shortness of breath. Another key constituent, cineole, has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and irritation in the airways, further improving breathing. The expectorant properties of eucalyptus globulus tinctures help bring up mucus and other debris, clearing the airways and making it easier to breathe.

By reducing congestion and inflammation in the airways, eucalyptus globulus tinctures can help alleviate shortness of breath.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of 80% vodka.
  2. Chop the leaves into small pieces and add them to a clean glass jar.
  3. Pour the vodka over the leaves, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are compounds that help improve blood flow and reduce inflammation.

These properties are beneficial for people experiencing shortness of breath due to circulatory issues or lung problems. The flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, act as antioxidants that protect blood vessels from damage and improve their function. Terpenoids like ginkgolides also help reduce inflammation and improve blood vessel flexibility, allowing for better oxygen delivery to the body's tissues.

By improving blood flow and reducing inflammation, Ginkgo biloba tinctures may help alleviate shortness of breath symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Ginkgo biloba leaves. Clean and dry them.
  2. Combine 2 cups of leaves with 1 cup of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Store the Ginkgo biloba tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, as needed.

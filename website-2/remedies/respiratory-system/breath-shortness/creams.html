1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains compounds like cineole, globulol, and alpha-pinene, which are released when you apply it to your skin.

When inhaled, these compounds open up the airways and ease congestion, making it easier to breathe. Cineole, the main active ingredient, is a natural bronchodilator that relaxes the muscles around the airways, allowing more air to flow in. Globulol and alpha-pinene help to thin mucus, making it easier to cough up and clear out your airways.

By reducing congestion and opening up airways, Eucalyptus globulus creams can help alleviate shortness of breath caused by colds, allergies, or other respiratory issues.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 10 drops of Eucalyptus globulus essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl.
  3. Add the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil to the melted mixture and stir well.
  4. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool until solidified.
  5. Once solid, apply a small amount to your chest or inhale the vapor from the cream to help relieve shortness of breath.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains the bioactive constituents of ginger, which has been used for centuries to help alleviate shortness of breath.

Ginger contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce swelling in the airways and make breathing easier. The anti-inflammatory properties of ginger also help to relax the muscles in the chest and lungs, allowing for better airflow. Additionally, ginger has natural expectorant properties that help to loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, further improving breathing.

By reducing inflammation and loosening mucus, Zingiber officinale creams can help to alleviate shortness of breath and promote easier breathing.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of ginger essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add beeswax and stir until melted and smooth.
  4. Remove from heat and add ginger essential oil. Stir well.
  5. Pour mixture into a container and let cool. Once set, apply to chest or inhale to relieve shortness of breath.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, carnosic acid, and camphor as its active constituents.

These compounds help reduce inflammation and open airways, making it easier to breathe. Rosmarinus officinalis creams have antioxidant properties, which protect the lungs from damage caused by free radicals. The anti-inflammatory properties of rosmarinic acid and carnosic acid also help to relax the muscles in the airways, reducing constriction and allowing for easier airflow.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing airway muscles, Rosmarinus officinalis creams can help alleviate shortness of breath.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, 2 tablespoons of rosemary essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated. Remove from heat.
  4. Add vitamin E oil and rosemary essential oil to the mixture. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean glass jar and let it cool and solidify. Store in a cool, dry place.

4. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and polyphenols.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce inflammation in the airways. The anti-inflammatory properties of these constituents, particularly the alkylamides, help relax the airway muscles and increase the airflow, making it easier to breathe.   3. Strain the mixture and discard the flowers. Add 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil and 1 tablespoon of aloe vera gel.
  4. Melt the beeswax in a separate saucepan over low heat. Combine the melted beeswax with the Echinacea mixture.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify before using as a cream to help with shortness of breath.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two essential oils with anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling in the airways, making it easier to breathe. The calming effects of lavender oil also help to slow down breathing and heart rate, which can become rapid during episodes of shortness of breath. The antispasmodic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate help to relax the muscles in the airways, further reducing the symptoms of shortness of breath.

  3. Add the dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the melted mixture and let it steep for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Add the vitamin E oil to the mixture and stir well. Pour the cream into a clean container and let it cool before use.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent shortness of breath from forming?

Drinking herbal tea might help ease shortness of breath.

Peppermint tea, for example, can open up airways and make it easier to breathe. Ginger tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can also help.

These teas work by relaxing the body and reducing congestion, making it simpler to take in oxygen.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for shortness of breath every day?

Consuming herbal teas for shortness of breath every day can be safe, but be cautious.

Some teas, like peppermint and ginger, can help ease breathing. However, teas like licorice root can worsen breathing problems if consumed excessively.

Moderate amounts, one to two cups a day, are usually okay.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in shortness of breath?

Herbal teas can help with shortness of breath, but it takes time to notice results.

Some people feel better after a few days, while others see improvements after a week or two.

It really depends on the person and the type of tea being used.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for shortness of breath?

If you have shortness of breath, it's best to drink herbal tea in the morning.

This is because many herbal teas, such as those with peppermint or eucalyptus, can help clear your airways and make it easier to breathe.

Drinking it in the morning can give you a boost to start your day feeling clearer and more refreshed.

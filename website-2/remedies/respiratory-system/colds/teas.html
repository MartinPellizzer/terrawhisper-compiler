1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains several bioactive constituents that make it effective in relieving cold symptoms.

The main compounds found in this tea are eucalyptol, a camphor-like molecule, and flavonoids like diosmetin and eriodictyol. Eucalyptol has decongestant properties that help to open up airways and ease congestion, making it easier to breathe when suffering from a cold. Flavonoids in eucalyptus globulus tea have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling in the nasal passages and sinuses, further alleviating cold symptoms.

By combining these properties, eucalyptus globulus tea helps to provide quick relief from cold and flu symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh eucalyptus leaves (Eucalyptus globulus) or 1 tablespoon of dried eucalyptus leaves.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Add the eucalyptus leaves to the boiling water.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes. Strain the mixture.
  5. Drink the tea hot or warm, 2-3 times a day, to help relieve cold symptoms.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and volatile oils.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling in the throat and sinuses. Echinacea purpurea also contains polysaccharides, which stimulate the immune system and increase the production of white blood cells to fight off infections. The tea's flavonoids and phenolic acids have antioxidant properties, protecting the body from oxidative stress caused by a cold.

By reducing inflammation and boosting the immune system, Echinacea purpurea teas can help alleviate cold symptoms and support the body's natural healing process.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Echinacea purpurea root and 1 tablespoon of dried flowers.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the Echinacea purpurea root and flowers in a cup.
  4. Steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the liquid into another cup.
  5. Drink 1/2 to 1 cup of the tea 3-4 times a day to help with cold symptoms.

3. Zingiber officinale

4. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas contains thymol and carvacrol, which are powerful antioxidants.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation and combat infection, making them effective in fighting off colds and flu. Thymol also has antimicrobial properties, which help to eliminate the bacteria and viruses that cause respiratory infections. Additionally, the tea's expectorant properties help to loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, making it easier to breathe and recover from illness.

By drinking Thymus vulgaris teas, you can take advantage of its natural, effective ingredients to help your body recover from a cold.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and stems, or 2 teaspoons of dried leaves.
  2. Heat 1 cup of water in a pot until it boils.
  3. Remove the pot from heat and add the Thymus vulgaris leaves or stems.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes. Strain the liquid and discard the solids.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the Thymus vulgaris tea, 2-3 times a day, to help relieve cold symptoms.

5. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains flavonoids, bilobalide, and ginkgolides as its active constituents.

By combining these properties, Ginkgo biloba teas can help to alleviate cold symptoms and support the body's natural recovery process.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent colds from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent colds, but it's not a guarantee.

Some teas, like echinacea and peppermint, contain ingredients that boost the immune system and fight off infections. These teas can also help soothe a sore throat and reduce congestion, making it easier to fight off a cold.

It's worth trying if you're feeling under the weather.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for colds every day?

You can safely drink herbal teas for colds every day, but in moderation.

Some teas, like peppermint and ginger, have active ingredients that can be too strong if consumed too often. It's best to limit your daily intake to a cup or two and listen to your body.

If you feel uncomfortable, stop or switch to a different tea.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in colds?

Herbal teas can start to help with cold symptoms within a few hours.

Some people feel relief from congestion and coughs after drinking a warm tea. As the body absorbs the herbs, you may notice your symptoms easing, like a sore throat feeling better or your energy levels increasing.

It's different for everyone, but some people start to feel better after a few hours.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for colds?

For a cold, try drinking herbal tea in the morning.

This helps loosen up mucus and gets your day started on the right foot.

As you're most congested in the mornings, a warm cup of tea can ease your breathing and make it easier to cough up phlegm, giving you a bit of relief to tackle the day ahead.

9 yummy herbal teas for kids with colds

When kids get colds, it's not just their bodies that get affected, but also their daily lives.

They might feel tired, sore, and miserable, making it hard for them to play, go to school, or even sleep properly. Colds can also make their throats hurt and their noses run, making it hard for them to breathe and focus. This can be frustrating for both kids and parents, as they try to find ways to make them feel better. Colds are caused by viruses that can be spread through the air, surfaces, or even close contact with someone who's sick.

When kids are exposed to these viruses, their immune systems try to fight them off, but it can take time and might leave them feeling unwell. Some kids might be more prone to getting colds due to their environment, like being in a crowded school or daycare, or due to underlying health conditions. To help kids feel better when they're sick, parents often turn to over-the-counter medications, but these can have side effects and might not be suitable for young children. This is where herbal teas come in - they're a natural and gentle way to soothe a sore throat, calm a cough, and ease congestion.

These teas are made from plants that have been used for centuries to help people feel better when they're sick, and they can be a great alternative to medication.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate cold symptoms.

These teas contain properties and constituents like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids that have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immune-boosting effects. When we have a cold, our immune system is under attack, and these properties in Echinacea purpurea teas help to stimulate the production of white blood cells, which fight off the invading pathogens. They also reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms like congestion and sore throats. To get the most out of Echinacea purpurea teas for colds, brew a cup by steeping one teaspoon of dried root in boiling water for five to seven minutes. Drink it three to four times a day to help your body recover.

You can also add honey or lemon to make it more palatable. Some people like to take Echinacea purpurea teas preventatively when they feel the first signs of a cold, such as a runny nose or a scratchy throat, to see if it can help prevent the cold from taking hold. When you take Echinacea purpurea teas, you can expect to feel some relief from your cold symptoms within a few days. Your congestion may clear up, your sore throat may feel less scratchy, and you may have more energy to tackle your daily tasks. You may also find that your cold doesn't last as long as it would have without the tea.

However, it's essential to remember that Echinacea purpurea teas are not a cure for colds, but rather a complementary remedy to help your body recover.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas are known to help with colds due to their decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The tea contains compounds like eucalyptol, which acts as a natural expectorant, helping to loosen and clear mucus from the airways. This makes it easier to breathe, relieving congestion and coughs associated with colds. To get the most out of eucalyptus globulus teas for colds, try drinking 1-2 cups a day.

You can make the tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried eucalyptus leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. You can also add honey or lemon to make it more palatable. When you drink eucalyptus globulus tea, you may start to feel the effects within 30 minutes to an hour. Your congestion may start to clear, and your cough may become less severe.

You may also notice a reduction in your body temperature and a general feeling of relief from cold symptoms.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, has been used for centuries to help alleviate cold symptoms.

This is because ginger contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These properties help to reduce congestion, soothe a sore throat, and ease a cough. When you have a cold, your body's mucous membranes become inflamed, making it harder to breathe. Ginger's anti-inflammatory properties can help to reduce this inflammation, making it easier to breathe and clear out mucus.

To use ginger tea to help with a cold, make a tea by steeping fresh ginger in hot water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day. You can also add honey to soothe a sore throat. As you drink the tea, you may start to feel a reduction in congestion and a decrease in coughing.

Your throat may also start to feel soothed, and you may have more energy to fight off the cold.

5. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas, also known as thyme tea, has been a popular remedy for colds and respiratory issues for centuries.

Thymol, in particular, has been shown to have antiviral properties that can help to reduce the severity and duration of a cold.

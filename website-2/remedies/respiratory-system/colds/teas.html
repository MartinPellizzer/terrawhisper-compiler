9 Herbal Teas For Colds

When kids get colds, it's not just their bodies that get affected, but also their daily lives.

They might feel tired, sore, and miserable, making it hard for them to play, go to school, or even sleep properly. Colds can also make their throats hurt and their noses run, making it hard for them to breathe and focus. This can be frustrating for both kids and parents, as they try to find ways to make them feel better. Colds are caused by viruses that can be spread through the air, surfaces, or even close contact with someone who's sick.

When kids are exposed to these viruses, their immune systems try to fight them off, but it can take time and might leave them feeling unwell. Some kids might be more prone to getting colds due to their environment, like being in a crowded school or daycare, or due to underlying health conditions. To help kids feel better when they're sick, parents often turn to over-the-counter medications, but these can have side effects and might not be suitable for young children. This is where herbal teas come in - they're a natural and gentle way to soothe a sore throat, calm a cough, and ease congestion.

These teas are made from plants that have been used for centuries to help people feel better when they're sick, and they can be a great alternative to medication.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains compounds like eucalyptol, which is known for its decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties.

This makes it a great remedy for colds, particularly for relieving congestion and sinus pressure. To use Eucalyptus globulus teas for colds, you can try the following: steep one teaspoon of dried leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. When you take these actions, you can expect to experience a reduction in congestion and sinus pressure, making it easier to breathe. You can safely drink herbal teas for colds every day, but in moderation.

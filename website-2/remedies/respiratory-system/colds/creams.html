1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains the active constituents eucalyptol and alpha-pinene, which have decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to relieve congestion and reduce the swelling of the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe. Eucalyptol also has antiviral properties, which can help to combat the underlying viral infection causing the cold. The anti-inflammatory properties of alpha-pinene can help to soothe a sore throat and reduce discomfort.

By reducing congestion and inflammation, eucalyptus globulus creams can help to alleviate the symptoms of a cold.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1/2 cup of coconut oil.
  2. Steep the dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves in the coconut oil for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discarding the leaves.
  4. Add 1/4 cup of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter to the oil mixture, melting them in a double boiler.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool, then use the Eucalyptus globulus cream as needed for cold relief.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids that help with colds.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling and ease congestion in the nasal passages. Alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to stimulate the immune system, increasing the production of white blood cells that fight off infections. The glycosides in Echinacea purpurea also have antimicrobial properties, which help to combat the underlying bacterial or viral causes of a cold.

By addressing both the symptoms and the underlying causes of a cold, Echinacea purpurea creams can provide relief from cold symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of shea butter.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the melted oil mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Combine the strained oil mixture with vitamin E oil and shea butter in a bowl and mix well until smooth.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify before use. Label and store in a cool, dark place.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents like zingerone and gingerols, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe sore throats and calm congestion.

The compounds in ginger also have natural decongestant properties that help break down mucus and open airways, making it easier to breathe. The antiviral properties of gingerol also help fight off the underlying viral infections that cause colds. Additionally, ginger has natural pain-relieving properties that can help ease headaches and body aches associated with colds.

By applying Zingiber officinale creams topically, you can experience these benefits and get relief from cold symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Zingiber officinale powder, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 drops of peppermint essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until fully melted.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix Zingiber officinale powder with vitamin E oil until well combined.
  4. Add the Zingiber officinale mixture to the melted coconut oil mixture and stir until smooth.
  5. Pour the mixture into a glass jar, add peppermint essential oil, and let it cool and solidify before use.

4. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams contains thymol, carvacrol, and other active constituents that help fight colds.

Thymol is a natural antiseptic that kills bacteria and viruses, while carvacrol has decongestant properties that relieve sinus congestion and coughs. The thymol in Thymus vulgaris creams also has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling in the throat and nose. Additionally, the cream's antimicrobial properties help to prevent the spread of infection, making it a popular remedy for colds and flu.

By using Thymus vulgaris creams, you can help to alleviate cold symptoms and recover faster.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Thymus vulgaris leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the dried Thymus vulgaris leaves. Let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a bowl and let it cool. Discard the leaves.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix 1/2 cup of coconut oil or shea butter with 2 tablespoons of beeswax. Heat the mixture in a double boiler until melted.
  5. Combine the cooled Thymus vulgaris liquid with the melted coconut oil mixture. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify.

5. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid and camphor, which are known for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to combat the underlying causes of a cold, such as a bacterial or viral infection. The camphor in Salvia officinalis creams also has a decongestant effect, which can help to relieve sinus pressure and congestion. Additionally, the antiseptic properties of rosmarinic acid can help to soothe a sore throat and reduce the severity of cold symptoms.

By using Salvia officinalis creams, you can help to support your body's natural defenses and promote a faster recovery from a cold.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Salvia officinalis leaves, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the dried Salvia officinalis leaves to the melted mixture and let it steep for 2-3 hours, or until the mixture cools down.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Add the vitamin E oil to the mixture and stir well. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify before use.

7. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and terpinen-4-ol.

These bioactive constituents have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help combat cold symptoms. Cineole and limonene work together to reduce congestion and ease coughs, while terpinen-4-ol's anti-inflammatory effects soothe sore throats and ease breathing difficulties. By reducing inflammation and killing bacteria, Melaleuca alternifolia creams can help alleviate the discomfort and pain associated with colds.

8. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains essential oils with bioactive constituents like camphor, borneol, and borneol acetate.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help fight off infections and reduce congestion. The camphor in Rosmarinus officinalis creams has a decongestant effect, relieving nasal congestion and making it easier to breathe. Borneol and borneol acetate have expectorant properties, helping to loosen and clear mucus from the respiratory tract.

By applying Rosmarinus officinalis creams topically, you can help ease cold symptoms and promote recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of olive oil. Also, get 2 tablespoons of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Steep the dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves in 2 tablespoons of olive oil for 2-3 hours or overnight. Strain the mixture and discard the leaves.
  4. Combine the melted oil mixture, the infused Rosmarinus officinalis oil, and 2 drops of your preferred essential oil (if using) in a bowl. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a tin or jar and let it cool and solidify. Once set, your Rosmarinus officinalis cream for colds is ready to use.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent colds from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help boost your immune system and reduce the risk of catching a cold.

Some herbal teas, like echinacea and peppermint, have properties that can help fight off germs and soothe a sore throat.

Staying hydrated and getting enough rest also plays a role in preventing colds from forming.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for colds every day?

You can consume herbal teas for colds every day, but in moderation.

Some teas, like peppermint and ginger, can be safe to drink in large quantities. However, teas like echinacea and sage may cause stomach problems if taken daily.

It's best to listen to your body and adjust the amount you drink based on how you feel.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in colds?

Herbal teas can help soothe a cold, but it's not a magic fix.

Some people notice relief within a few hours, while others might feel better after a day or two. This can vary depending on the tea and the individual.

As your body fights off the infection, herbal teas can support you with their natural properties.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for colds?

When you have a cold, it's best to drink herbal tea in the morning.

This helps loosen up mucus and makes it easier to breathe.

Your body is also more active in the morning, so the tea can help boost your immune system and get you feeling better faster.

Colds Got You Down? 8 Herbal Creams for Teachers

Teachers are often on the go, constantly interacting with students, colleagues, and parents.

This can make them more prone to catching colds and flu. When a teacher gets sick, it not only affects their own health but also their ability to teach and care for their students. They may struggle to focus, and their energy levels may drop, making it hard to keep up with the demands of the classroom. This can lead to stress, anxiety, and even burnout. If a teacher is sick for an extended period, it can impact the entire school community, causing disruptions to the learning environment and affecting student progress.

To make matters worse, teachers often have to teach and interact with their students even when they're not feeling well. This can make it difficult for them to recover quickly, as they may be exposing themselves to more germs and stress. Furthermore, teachers may not have the luxury of taking time off when they're sick, which can prolong their recovery and make it harder for them to get back to their normal routine. Herbal creams can be a helpful addition to a teacher's cold-fighting arsenal. These creams often contain soothing and anti-inflammatory ingredients that can help to ease congestion, reduce coughs, and calm irritated skin.

They can be applied directly to the skin, providing quick relief from cold symptoms.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams can be a helpful remedy for colds due to their decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The main constituents of Eucalyptus globulus, such as eucalyptol, a compound that makes up about 70% of the oil, have natural expectorant properties that help loosen and clear mucus from your airways, making it easier to breathe. When applied topically as a cream, Eucalyptus globulus can also reduce swelling and ease congestion in the nasal passages and sinuses. To use Eucalyptus globulus cream for colds, apply a small amount to your chest, nose, and sinuses. You can also add a few drops of the oil to a bowl of hot water, inhale the steam, and cover your head with a towel to help clear out mucus.

For best results, use the cream 2-3 times a day, or as needed. You may start to feel relief from congestion and coughing within 15-30 minutes after application, and symptoms may begin to subside within a few hours. As you use Eucalyptus globulus cream, you may notice that your congestion and coughing decrease, allowing you to breathe more easily and sleep better. Your sinuses may also feel clearer, and you may be able to smell and taste better.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, you should consult a healthcare professional for further guidance and treatment.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams may help with colds because they contain properties that support the immune system.

The creams contain compounds like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. When applied topically, these compounds may help reduce congestion, soothe a sore throat, and ease the discomfort associated with colds. If you're considering using Echinacea purpurea creams for colds, start by washing your hands before application to avoid transferring bacteria to the affected area.

Apply a thin layer of the cream to the affected area, such as your nose, throat, or chest, and gently massage it in. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day, or as needed. When using Echinacea purpurea creams for colds, you may experience a reduction in congestion, a decrease in the severity of cold symptoms, and an overall feeling of relief. The creams may also help reduce the duration of your cold, allowing you to recover faster.

However, it's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea creams may not cure colds, but they can help alleviate symptoms and support your immune system.

3. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams, also known as thyme creams, contain properties that can help ease cold symptoms.

The active constituents in thyme, such as thymol and carvacrol, have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that can help combat the underlying bacterial infection that often causes colds. These compounds work by targeting the bacteria and reducing the production of mucus, which can help alleviate congestion and coughing. Additionally, thyme's antiseptic properties can help soothe a sore throat and reduce fever. If you're experiencing cold symptoms, you can try using Thymus vulgaris creams to help relieve them. To use the cream, apply a small amount to your throat and chest, and gently massage it in.

You can also add a few drops of thyme essential oil to your diffuser to inhale the benefits of thyme. For best results, use the cream 2-3 times a day, or as directed by the manufacturer. Be sure to follow the instructions carefully and consult with a healthcare professional if you have any underlying medical conditions or allergies. When using Thymus vulgaris creams, you may experience a reduction in congestion and coughing, as well as a decrease in the severity of cold symptoms. You may also notice an improvement in your overall sense of well-being and energy levels.

However, it's essential to note that thyme creams are not a substitute for medical treatment, and you should continue to follow your healthcare provider's advice for managing your cold symptoms.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams, also known as ginger creams, have been used for centuries to help alleviate cold symptoms.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help to reduce congestion, coughs, and sore throats. These constituents also have a warming effect on the body, which can help to loosen mucus and make it easier to expel. To get relief from a cold, try applying a thin layer of ginger cream to your chest, nose, and throat area. You can also add a few drops of eucalyptus oil to the cream for extra decongestant benefits.

As you apply the cream, you may feel a slight burning sensation, which is normal and temporary. This is due to the warming effect of the ginger. After applying the cream, you may start to notice a decrease in congestion and coughing. The antimicrobial properties of the ginger may also help to reduce the severity of cold symptoms, allowing you to recover faster. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties may help to soothe a sore throat and reduce discomfort.

Some people may also experience a general feeling of warmth and comfort after applying the cream.

5. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams, also known as tea tree oil creams, have been widely used to help alleviate cold symptoms due to their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The creams contain compounds like terpinen-4-ol, cineole, and limonene, which have been shown to combat bacteria, viruses, and fungi that cause colds. When used topically, these creams can help soothe sore throats, reduce congestion, and ease coughs.

To get the most out of Melaleuca alternifolia creams for colds, apply a thin layer to the affected areas, such as the throat and chest, 2-3 times a day. You can also add a few drops of tea tree oil to your bath water for a relaxing and decongesting experience. As the creams work to combat the underlying causes of your cold, you may notice a reduction in symptoms, such as a less scratchy throat, clearer breathing, and a decrease in coughing.

Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce swelling and discomfort in the face and sinuses.

6. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams, also known as licorice root creams, are used to help alleviate cold symptoms.

They work by reducing inflammation and soothing irritated tissues in the throat and nose. Glycyrrhiza glabra contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, which has anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties, and flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. These constituents help to calm a sore throat, ease congestion, and reduce the severity of cold symptoms. If you're experiencing cold symptoms, you can try applying Glycyrrhiza glabra cream topically to the affected areas.

To do this, gently massage a small amount of cream onto your throat, nose, and forehead. You can also mix a few drops of licorice root oil with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it to your skin. When you use Glycyrrhiza glabra cream, you may experience a reduction in throat pain and congestion. Your nose may become less stuffy, and you may feel more comfortable overall.

However, it's essential to note that Glycyrrhiza glabra cream is not a substitute for medical treatment, and if your symptoms persist or worsen, you should consult a healthcare professional.

7. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams, made from the essential oil of rosemary, can help alleviate cold symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The main constituents of rosemary oil, including camphor, bornyl acetate, and borneol, help reduce congestion and coughs by loosening mucus and killing bacteria. When used topically, rosemary creams can ease a sore throat and help clear sinuses by stimulating blood flow and reducing inflammation. To use rosemary cream for colds, apply a small amount to your chest or nose, and gently massage it in.

You can also add a few drops of rosemary essential oil to a warm bath or inhale it through steam inhalation to help ease congestion. Be cautious not to overuse rosemary cream, as it can be irritating to sensitive skin. If you experience relief from your cold symptoms after using rosemary cream, you may notice a decrease in congestion, a reduction in coughing, and an improvement in your ability to breathe. Your throat may feel less sore, and your energy levels may increase as your body fights off the infection.

However, if you have a severe cold or underlying health condition, consult a healthcare professional before using rosemary cream as part of your treatment plan.

8. Pelargonium sidoides

Pelargonium sidoides creams have been found to be effective in helping with colds due to their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The creams typically contain the active ingredient EPs 7630, which is a standardized extract of the plant's root. This extract is rich in phenolic compounds, such as rosmarinic acid and ursolic acid, that have been shown to have antiviral and immunomodulatory effects. If you're experiencing symptoms of a cold, applying Pelargonium sidoides cream to the affected areas can help to reduce congestion and relieve a sore throat.

To use the cream effectively, gently apply a thin layer to your throat and chest area, ideally 2-3 times a day. You can also use a humidifier or inhale steam to help loosen mucus and ease breathing. By taking these actions, you can likely experience some relief from cold symptoms, such as a reduced sore throat and less congestion. Additionally, the antimicrobial properties of the cream may help to combat secondary infections, which can help your body recover faster.

However, it's essential to note that Pelargonium sidoides creams are not a cure for the common cold, and you should still rest, stay hydrated, and practice good hygiene to aid in your recovery.

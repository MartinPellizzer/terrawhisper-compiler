1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like eucalyptol, limonene, and alpha-pinene, which are known for their decongestant and expectorant properties.

These properties help relieve congestion and reduce mucus production in the body, making it easier to breathe when suffering from a cold. Eucalyptol, in particular, is a natural bronchodilator that widens airways and improves airflow to the lungs. The expectorant properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils also help loosen and clear out mucus, making it a popular ingredient in cough syrups and inhalers.

When inhaled, the vapors of eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help ease congestion and make it easier to breathe, providing quick relief from cold symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of water in a pot.
  2. Heat the water in the pot over low heat, then add the Eucalyptus leaves.
  3. Let it simmer for 1-2 hours, allowing the leaves to release their oils.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, discarding the leaves.
  5. Store the resulting essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight lid, using 1 tablespoon as needed for cold relief.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains bioactive constituents like alkylamides and caffeic acid, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain associated with colds.

Caffeic acid also has antioxidant properties that protect the body from damage caused by free radicals, which can make cold symptoms worse. Alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea essential oils have immunomodulatory effects, helping to regulate the immune system's response to illness and reduce the severity of cold symptoms. The essential oil's antiviral properties can also help combat the viruses that cause colds, such as rhinovirus and coronavirus.

By using Echinacea purpurea essential oils, you can help alleviate cold symptoms and support your body's natural healing process.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Echinacea flowers. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba or coconut oil) to the liquid and stir well.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 hours or overnight in the fridge. Then, strain it again and your Echinacea essential oil is ready to use.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains camphor, bornyl acetate, and bornel as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help combat the underlying causes of colds. Bornyl acetate, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the growth of certain pathogens that can cause respiratory infections. The antimicrobial properties of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help reduce the severity and duration of cold symptoms, making it a popular natural remedy for respiratory issues.

The anti-inflammatory properties of the oil can also help alleviate congestion and coughing associated with colds.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat, then add the fresh leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 10 minutes. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba or coconut oil) to the liquid and mix well.
  5. Transfer the mixture to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dry place. Use 5-7 drops as needed to relieve cold symptoms.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains the bioactive constituents gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation in the throat and nasal passages, making it easier to breathe and swallow. The essential oils also contain beta-carophyllene, which has antimicrobial properties that help fight off viral infections that cause colds. Additionally, gingerol and shogaol have analgesic properties that help relieve pain and discomfort associated with colds, such as headaches and sore throats.

By inhaling or applying Zingiber officinale essential oils topically, the bioactive constituents can directly interact with the body's systems to help alleviate cold symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh ginger roots and 1 cup of water in a large pot.
  2. Peel the ginger roots and chop them into small pieces. Place them in a saucepan with 1 cup of water.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 2 hours or until the water has reduced to 1/2 cup.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass jar.
  5. Let the mixture sit at room temperature for 24 hours to allow the oil to separate from the water. Discard the water and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

5. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol and carvacrol, two potent compounds that help fight colds.

Thymol has antimicrobial properties that kill bacteria and viruses that cause respiratory infections. Carvacrol also has antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties that reduce congestion and soothe a sore throat. The oils' expectorant properties help loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to breathe.

By targeting the root causes of colds, Thymus vulgaris essential oils helps alleviate symptoms and promote recovery.

  1. Gather materials: Thymus vulgaris leaves (1 cup), carrier oil (1 cup), clean glass jar, cheesecloth, and dark glass bottles.
  2. Dry the Thymus vulgaris leaves in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks. Stir them every few days to ensure even drying.
  3. Combine the dried Thymus vulgaris leaves (1/4 cup) with carrier oil (1 cup) in the clean glass jar. Stir well to mix.
  4. Store the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every few days. This helps the oil to infuse into the leaves.
  5. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth into the dark glass bottles. Discard the solids and use the oil as needed.

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains menthol and menthone, which are the main bioactive constituents responsible for its decongestant properties.

When inhaled, menthol helps to thin out mucus and ease congestion in the nose and sinuses, making it easier to breathe. Menthone, on the other hand, has a cooling effect that can help to reduce fever and relieve sore throats. The antimicrobial properties of Mentha x piperita essential oils also help to combat infections that can cause colds, such as sinusitis and bronchitis.

By combining these properties, Mentha x piperita essential oils can provide relief from cold symptoms and promote a faster recovery.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and flowers. Make sure they are free of pesticides and other chemicals.
  2. Wash the leaves and flowers with 1 cup of water to remove dirt and bacteria. Pat them dry with a clean towel.
  3. Put the dried leaves and flowers in a clean glass jar. Cover them with 2 cups of water and let it sit for 2 days in a cool, dark place.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and save the liquid.
  5. Use a distillation kit or a steam distiller to separate the essential oil from the liquid. Collect the oil and store it in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

7. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two major bioactive constituents that contribute to its therapeutic properties.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help alleviate cold symptoms such as congestion and sore throats. Linalool, in particular, has been shown to have decongestant properties, helping to clear nasal passages and ease breathing. Additionally, linalyl acetate has been found to have a soothing effect on the respiratory system, reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation.

By reducing inflammation and congestion, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help provide relief from cold symptoms, promoting a faster recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a pot.
  2. Heat the water until it starts boiling. Then reduce the heat to low.
  3. Add the Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the pot and let it steep for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl. Discard the flowers.
  5. Let the liquid sit in the sun for 2-3 days to evaporate the water, leaving behind the essential oil.

8. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and beta-pinene.

These bioactive constituents have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce the severity of cold symptoms. The expectorant properties of cineole help loosen and clear mucus from the respiratory tract, making it easier to breathe. The antiseptic properties of limonene and beta-pinene help combat bacterial infections that can cause colds.

By reducing inflammation and killing bacteria, Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils helps alleviate congestion, coughs, and sore throats associated with colds.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Melaleuca alternifolia leaves, a cup of water, a saucepan, and a distillation apparatus.
  2. Wash the leaves and chop them into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped leaves and water in the saucepan and heat it at 100°C (212°F) for 2 hours.
  4. Connect the distillation apparatus to the saucepan and collect the steam. Let it condense into a cup.
  5. Filter the essential oil from the cup using a coffee filter and store it in a dark glass bottle.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent colds from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent colds from forming, but its effectiveness varies.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and ginger, have antimicrobial properties that can fight off germs. Echinacea tea is also popular for its cold-fighting properties.

These teas may boost your immune system, but more research is needed to confirm their benefits.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for colds every day?

While herbal teas can be soothing for colds, drinking them daily might not be the best idea.

Some herbs can be too potent and cause interactions with medications or worsen certain health conditions. It's also possible to build up a tolerance to their active ingredients.

Moderation is key when using herbal teas for cold relief.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in colds?

Herbal teas can start to help with cold symptoms within 24 to 48 hours.

They work by soothing a sore throat and easing congestion. Some teas, like peppermint and eucalyptus, can help loosen mucus and make it easier to breathe.

As you drink more tea, you might notice your symptoms getting a bit better.

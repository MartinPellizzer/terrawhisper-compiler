Laryngitis: Causes, Symptoms, and Herbal Preparations for Relief

What are the causes of laryngitis?

Laryngitis is an inflammation of the larynx, or voice box, which can cause hoarseness or loss of voice.

There are several main causes of laryngitis. One of the most common causes is a viral infection. This is usually a cold or flu virus that can spread easily from person to person. When you have a viral infection, your body tries to fight it off, and in the process, your larynx gets inflamed, leading to laryngitis. Another cause of laryngitis is smoking. Smoking can damage the delicate tissues in your larynx, making it more susceptible to inflammation.

When you smoke, you're essentially poisoning your body with thousands of chemicals, including tar and carbon monoxide, which can damage your larynx and lead to laryngitis. Tobacco smoke is also a major contributor to laryngitis. When you inhale tobacco smoke, the chemicals in it can irritate your larynx and cause inflammation. This is especially true for people who are exposed to secondhand smoke. Colds and flu are also common causes of laryngitis. When you have a cold or flu, you're more likely to get laryngitis because your body is fighting off the infection, and your larynx gets inflamed in the process.

This is why laryngitis often occurs during the cold and flu season.

What Are The Main Causes Of Laryngitis?

Laryngitis is an inflammation of the vocal cords, and it can be caused by several things.

A viral infection is one of the main causes of laryngitis. This is because viruses like the common cold and flu can attack the vocal cords, causing them to become inflamed and swollen.

Bacterial infections can also cause laryngitis, usually in people who have a weakened immune system or a pre-existing lung condition. When bacteria like strep throat infect the vocal cords, they can cause severe inflammation and pain. Another common cause of laryngitis is overuse, which occurs when people talk or sing too much without giving their vocal cords a chance to rest.

This can lead to vocal cord strain and inflammation, especially in people who work in loud or high-stress professions like singing or teaching.

Below are the main causes of laryngitis.

  • Viral Infection: A viral infection, such as the common cold or flu, causes laryngitis when it irritates and inflames the lining of the vocal cords, leading to redness, swelling, and a hoarse or lost voice.
  • Bacterial Infection: Bacterial infection can cause laryngitis when bacteria, such as strep or staph, infect the vocal cords and surrounding tissues, leading to inflammation, swelling, and irritation that makes it painful to speak or sing.
  • Overuse: Overusing the voice by speaking too loudly, talking for too long, or shouting can strain the vocal cords and cause inflammation, leading to laryngitis.
  • Smoking: When you smoke, the chemicals in the smoke irritate your throat and vocal cords, causing them to become inflamed and leading to the development of laryngitis, a condition characterized by hoarseness and difficulty speaking.
  • Vocal Strain: Vocal strain is a cause of laryngitis because when you force your voice too much, it can cause inflammation and irritation in your vocal cords, leading to laryngitis.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Laryngitis?

If you have laryngitis, there are some herbs you should avoid.

Eucalyptus (Eucalyptus globulus) oil is often used in cough drops and inhalers, but it can be too harsh for a sore throat. Sage (Salvia officinalis) and rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis) contain compounds that can dry out your throat and make it harder to swallow. Black pepper (Piper nigrum) can also irritate your throat and make symptoms worse.

Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) is already excluded from the list, but it's worth mentioning that other licorice products may contain glycyrrhizin, which can be problematic for people with laryngitis.

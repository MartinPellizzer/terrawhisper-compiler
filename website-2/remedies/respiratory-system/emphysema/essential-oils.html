What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have emphysema?

If you have emphysema, it's best to avoid certain herbal essential oils that can make breathing problems worse.

'Piper nigrum' or black pepper oil is one to avoid because it can cause the airways to constrict, making it harder to breathe. 'Cinnamomum verum' or cinnamon oil is also not recommended as it can irritate the lungs and make breathing more difficult.

'Citrus aurantium' or bitter orange oil can be problematic as it can increase airway resistance, making breathing harder. 'Cymbopogon citratus' or lemongrass oil and 'Citrus reticulata' or mandarin oil may also be best avoided as they can cause bronchospasm, which is the tightening of airway muscles, leading to breathing difficulties. 'Salvia officinalis' or sage oil should also be used with caution as it can be a bronchoconstrictor, meaning it can make the airways narrow, making breathing harder.

It's essential to consult with your doctor or healthcare professional before using any herbal essential oils, especially if you have a lung condition like emphysema.

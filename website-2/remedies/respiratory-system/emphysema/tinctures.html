Emphysema remedies for older adults: 7 herbal tinctures to try

As people age, their lungs can lose their ability to function properly, making it harder to breathe.

This condition is called emphysema. Older adults with emphysema may experience shortness of breath, even when doing simple tasks like walking or talking. They may also cough and wheeze a lot, which can disrupt their sleep and daily activities. Emphysema can be caused by smoking, exposure to pollutants, or genetics. It's a progressive disease, meaning it gets worse over time.

Older adults with emphysema may need to use oxygen tanks or take medication to manage their symptoms. However, these treatments may have side effects and can be expensive. This is where herbal tinctures come in. They are liquid extracts made from plants that have been used for centuries to help with breathing and other health issues. Some herbal tinctures may help to open up airways, reduce inflammation, and even boost the immune system.

By taking these tinctures, older adults may be able to breathe easier and improve their quality of life.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures have been studied for their potential to help alleviate symptoms of emphysema, a chronic lung disease.

The tincture's active compounds, flavonoids and terpenoids, specifically bilobalide and ginkgolides, are believed to improve blood flow to the lungs and reduce inflammation. This may help reduce shortness of breath and improve oxygenation of the body. To try Ginkgo biloba tinctures for emphysema, you can start by taking 5-10 drops of a 1:5 or 1:10 Ginkgo biloba tincture, two to three times a day, mixed with water. It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before adding any new supplements to your routine, as they may interact with medications or worsen underlying health conditions.

Monitor your symptoms closely, and adjust the dosage as needed. Some people may experience improvements in lung function and a reduction in shortness of breath, while others may notice improved cognitive function and mood due to the tincture's antioxidant properties. As you continue taking Ginkgo biloba tinctures, you may notice a decrease in the frequency and severity of flare-ups, allowing you to manage your condition more effectively. However, it's crucial to note that Ginkgo biloba tinctures are not a cure for emphysema, and you should continue following your doctor's recommended treatment plan.

Keep in mind that individual results may vary, and it's essential to work closely with your healthcare provider to monitor your progress and adjust your treatment plan as needed.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures may help with emphysema symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The plant contains constituents like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids that may help reduce inflammation in the lungs and airways, making it easier to breathe. Echinacea purpurea also has immunomodulatory effects, which may help boost the body's natural defenses against infection and promote healing. If you're considering taking Echinacea purpurea tinctures for emphysema, start by consulting with a healthcare professional to determine the best dosage and preparation method. Typically, tinctures are taken in small amounts (20-30 drops) three times a day, mixed with water or juice.

It's also essential to note that Echinacea purpurea may interact with certain medications, such as immunosuppressants and blood thinners, so monitor your medication regimen closely. When taking Echinacea purpurea tinctures for emphysema, you may experience improvements in lung function and a reduction in symptoms like wheezing and coughing. Some people may also notice improved energy levels and a decrease in shortness of breath. However, it's essential to be patient and consistent, as the effects may take several weeks to develop.

Additionally, it's crucial to continue your regular medical treatment and follow your healthcare professional's advice.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contain compounds like eucalyptol, which is a natural expectorant.

This helps loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, making it easier to breathe for people with emphysema. The tincture also contains other constituents, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory properties. These can help reduce swelling in the airways, making it easier to breathe. If you're considering using Eucalyptus globulus tinctures to help manage emphysema symptoms, talk to your doctor first. Start by taking a small dose, typically 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, mixed with water or another liquid.

You can also inhale the tincture directly from a cloth or paper towel soaked in it. Do this by placing the cloth over your mouth and nose, and inhaling slowly through your nose. Taking Eucalyptus globulus tinctures may lead to a reduction in mucus production and an improvement in breathing. You might notice that you can breathe more easily, and that your shortness of breath is less frequent. Some people also report a reduction in coughing and wheezing. However, it's essential to note that Eucalyptus globulus tinctures are not a replacement for medical treatment.

Continue to work with your doctor to manage your emphysema symptoms, and monitor your progress closely.

4. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris tinctures are made from the leaves of the thyme plant, which contains compounds like thymol and carvacrol.

These compounds have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the lungs and fight off infections that can exacerbate emphysema. Emphysema is a condition where the air sacs in the lungs are damaged, making it harder to breathe. By reducing inflammation and fighting off infections, Thymus vulgaris tinctures can help make breathing easier and improve lung function. To use Thymus vulgaris tinctures for emphysema, you can start by taking a few drops under your tongue or mixing it with water and drinking it three to four times a day.

However, consult with a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you're taking other medications or have any underlying health conditions. Start with a low dose and gradually increase it as needed and under medical guidance. When taking Thymus vulgaris tinctures, you may notice improvements in breathing and lung function within a few weeks. You may feel more energetic and able to engage in physical activities without getting winded as easily. Some people may also notice a reduction in the frequency and severity of respiratory infections.

However, results may vary depending on the severity of the condition and individual tolerance.

Rosmarinus officinalis tinctures, made from the leaves of the rosemary plant, have been studied for their potential benefits in managing emphysema.

The plant contains compounds like carnosic acid, rosmarinic acid, and camphor, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These properties may help reduce inflammation in the lungs, a key factor in emphysema progression. The antioxidants may also help protect lung tissue from further damage. To use Rosmarinus officinalis tinctures for emphysema, start by consulting with a healthcare professional.

When taking Rosmarinus officinalis tinctures, you may experience improved lung function, reduced shortness of breath, and a decrease in coughing. You may also notice an improvement in your overall well-being, including reduced fatigue and improved sleep quality.

However, it's essential to remember that emphysema is a chronic condition, and while these tinctures may provide some benefits, they should not replace conventional medical treatment.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains eucalyptol and camphor as its main bioactive constituents.

Eucalyptol has decongestant properties that help ease congestion and coughing, a common symptom of emphysema. Camphor also has expectorant properties, which help bring up mucus from the lungs, making it easier to breathe. The anti-inflammatory properties of eucalyptol and camphor may also help reduce inflammation in the airways, making it easier for people with emphysema to breathe.

These properties work together to help improve breathing and reduce symptoms associated with emphysema.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of Eucalyptus globulus essential oil.
  2. Melt beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second intervals, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add distilled water to the melted mixture and stir well.
  4. Add Eucalyptus globulus essential oil and stir well. Be careful not to add too much oil, as it can be overpowering.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, your Eucalyptus globulus cream is ready to use.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and polysaccharides that provide anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and inflammation in the lungs, which can be beneficial for people with emphysema. The polysaccharides in Echinacea purpurea creams stimulate the production of macrophages, white blood cells that help fight off infections and clean the lungs. The alkylamides and caffeic acid derivatives also have antioxidant properties, which can help protect the lungs from oxidative damage and repair damaged lung tissue.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the lungs, Echinacea purpurea creams may help alleviate symptoms of emphysema, such as shortness of breath and wheezing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the melted mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture and discard the flowers. Add the vitamin E oil and mix well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool. Once set, your Echinacea purpurea cream is ready to use.

What Are The Main Causes Of Chronic Bronchitis?

Chronic bronchitis is a long-term inflammation of the airways in the lungs.

There are several main causes of this condition. One of the main causes is tobacco smoking. When you smoke, the chemicals in cigarettes damage the lining of the airways, making them inflamed and narrow. This can lead to a persistent cough and mucus production.

Another cause of chronic bronchitis is air pollution. Pollutants in the air, such as dust, chemicals, and fumes, can irritate the airways and cause inflammation. People who live in cities or work in industries with high levels of air pollution are more likely to develop chronic bronchitis. Respiratory infections, such as pneumonia or bronchitis, can also cause chronic bronchitis. When a respiratory infection damages the airways, it can lead to long-term inflammation and scarring.

People who have had repeated respiratory infections or have a weakened immune system are more likely to develop chronic bronchitis.

Below are the main causes of chronic bronchitis.

  • Tobacco Smoking: Tobacco smoking is a major cause of chronic bronchitis because the chemicals in cigarette smoke damage the lining of the airways in the lungs, leading to inflammation, infection, and scarring that cause the airways to become narrow and produce mucus, which can lead to persistent coughing and difficulty breathing.
  • Air Pollution: Air pollution is a cause of chronic bronchitis because it contains tiny particles and gases that irritate the lungs and airways, leading to long-term inflammation and damage.
  • Respiratory Infections: Chronic bronchitis occurs when the lungs' airways become inflamed and irritated repeatedly due to repeated respiratory infections, such as colds and flu, which cause the bronchial tubes to become inflamed and produce excess mucus.
  • Chronic Inflammation: Chronic inflammation is a cause of chronic bronchitis because the constant swelling and irritation in the airways make them more susceptible to infections and irritation, leading to persistent coughing and breathing problems.
  • COPD: Chronic bronchitis is a type of COPD because the constant inflammation and damage to the airways in COPD cause the bronchial tubes to become irritated and inflamed over time, leading to a persistent cough and mucus production.
  • Asthma: Asthma can cause chronic bronchitis because the airways in people with asthma can become inflamed and irritated, leading to persistent coughing and mucus production that can develop into chronic bronchitis over time.
  • Environmental Toxins: Environmental toxins, such as pollution and chemicals in the air we breathe, can irritate and damage the lungs, leading to chronic inflammation and increased risk of chronic bronchitis.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Chronic Bronchitis?

If you have chronic bronchitis, there are some herbs you should avoid.

Comfrey (Symphytum officinale) is one of them. Comfrey contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can harm the liver and lungs, making bronchitis worse. Another herb to avoid is Lobelia (Lobelia inflata), also known as Indian tobacco.

Its alkaloids can irritate the lungs and make breathing even harder. You should also be cautious with Wild Indigo (Baptisia tinctoria), as it can cause allergic reactions and worsen bronchitis symptoms. Lastly, Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) can be a problem, as it can raise blood pressure and worsen fluid retention, which can be a concern for people with bronchitis.

These herbs might seem helpful, but they can do more harm than good if you have chronic bronchitis.

Fungal Infection: Causes and Natural Treatments with Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Fungal infection is a condition where a fungus grows inside or on a person's body, causing harm.

It's essential to deal with fungal infections promptly, as they can lead to serious health issues if left untreated. If not treated, fungal infections can spread to other parts of the body, causing symptoms like itching, redness, and pain. In severe cases, fungal infections can lead to organ damage or even life-threatening conditions. Herbal remedies, derived from plants, offer a natural way to combat fungal infections. Three herbs known for their antifungal properties are Melaleuca alternifolia, commonly known as tea tree oil, which has been shown to inhibit fungal growth. Allium sativum, or garlic, contains compounds that fight fungal infections. Origanum vulgare, or oregano, has antifungal and antibacterial properties. Herbal preparations can be used to treat fungal infections in various forms.

A decoction is a liquid extract made by steeping herbs in hot water. Decoctions can be ingested to treat internal fungal infections or applied topically to affected areas. Tinctures, concentrated liquid extracts of herbs, can be added to water or taken directly to fight fungal infections. Creams or ointments, made by mixing herbs with a carrier substance, can be applied directly to the affected area, providing localized relief. These herbal preparations can be effective in treating fungal infections because they contain compounds that target and kill fungal cells. For example, tea tree oil's active compound, melaleuca oil, disrupts fungal cell membranes, preventing them from growing. Garlic's sulfur compounds have been shown to inhibit fungal growth and kill fungal cells. Oregano's carvacrol and thymol compounds have antifungal and antibacterial properties, making it effective against various types of fungal infections. Herbal remedies can be a natural and effective way to treat fungal infections, especially when used in conjunction with other treatment options.

However, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedy, especially if you have a severe or persistent fungal infection.

What Are The Main Causes Of Fungal Infection?

Fungal infections occur when fungi, tiny living organisms, grow and multiply on our skin or inside our bodies.

The main causes of fungal infections can be broken down into several factors. One common cause is a lack of personal hygiene. When we don't wash our feet regularly, fungi can multiply, leading to 'Athlete's Foot', a condition characterized by itchy, scaly skin between the toes. This is often seen in athletes or people who wear shoes that don't breathe, trapping sweat and moisture that fungi thrive in. Another cause is a weakened immune system.

When our immune system is weak, it's harder for our body to fight off fungal infections. This is why people with diseases like HIV/AIDS or taking immunosuppressive medications are more prone to fungal infections. 'Ringworm', a circular rash on the skin, is often seen in people with weakened immune systems. Lastly, hormonal imbalances can also lead to fungal infections. 'Yeast Infection', a common condition in women, occurs when the natural balance of yeast in the vagina is disrupted, often due to hormonal fluctuations during menstruation, pregnancy, or menopause.

This allows the yeast to multiply, causing itching, redness, and discharge.

Below are the main causes of fungal infection.

  • Athlete's Foot: Athlete's Foot is a cause of fungal infection because a type of fungus called dermatophyte grows on the moist skin of the feet, especially between the toes, and feeds on dead skin cells, causing irritation and itchiness.
  • Ringworm: Ringworm is a cause of fungal infection because the disease is actually a skin infection caused by a tiny fungus called Trichophyton, which infects the skin and causes ring-shaped red patches to appear.
  • Yeast Infection: A yeast infection occurs when a type of fungus called Candida, which is normally found on and inside the body, grows and multiplies out of control, causing an infection.
  • Onychomycosis: Onychomycosis is a cause of fungal infection because the fungus that causes it, such as Trichophyton, penetrates the nail plate and grows inside, damaging the nail and causing pain, discoloration, and brittleness.
  • Candidiasis: Candidiasis is a cause of fungal infection because the Candida fungus grows out of control in the body, often in warm, moist areas such as the mouth, vagina, or skin, and causes symptoms like itching, redness, and discharge.
  • Aspergillosis: Aspergillosis is a cause of fungal infection because a type of fungus called Aspergillus grows in the air and can enter the body through the lungs, causing an infection.
  • Histoplasmosis: Histoplasmosis is a cause of fungal infection because it is a disease caused by inhaling spores of a fungus called Histoplasma capsulatum, which grows in the soil and can be found in areas with bird or bat droppings.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Fungal Infection?

If you have a fungal infection, it's essential to be cautious with some herbs that can make it worse.

Garlic (Allium species) is one to avoid, as it has antifungal properties that can kill the fungus, but it can also harm the good bacteria in your body. Similarly, Turmeric (Curcuma longa) has a compound called curcumin, which can inhibit fungal growth, but it can also have a drying effect on the skin and make fungal infections worse.

Another herb to be careful with is Ginger (Zingiber officinale), which can have a drying effect and make fungal infections worse. Also, avoid Saffron (Crocus sativus) as it can have a drying effect and irritate the skin, making fungal infections worse.

Additionally, avoid Echinacea (Echinacea species) as it can stimulate the immune system, but it can also cause an imbalance in the body, making fungal infections worse.

Understanding and Managing Fungal Infection with Medicinal Herbs and Preparations

What are the causes of fungal infection?

Fungal infections are caused by tiny organisms called fungi that thrive in warm and moist environments.

There are several types of fungal infections, and each has its own unique causes. For example, Athlete's foot is caused by fungi like Trichophyton and Epidermophyton, which thrive in warm and sweaty feet, often due to wearing tight or poorly ventilated shoes.

Ringworm, on the other hand, is caused by fungi like Microsporum and Trichophyton, which can infect skin when it comes into contact with an infected person or animal, or contaminated objects. Nail fungus, also known as onychomycosis, is caused by fungi like Trichophyton and Candida, which infect the nail when it's damaged or weakened, often due to poor hygiene or a weakened immune system. Tinea corporis, also known as body ringworm, is caused by fungi like Microsporum and Trichophyton, which infect the skin when it's exposed to an infected person or animal, or contaminated objects.

Tinea capitis, also known as scalp ringworm, is caused by fungi like Microsporum and Trichophyton, which infect the hair and scalp when it's exposed to an infected person or animal, or contaminated objects.

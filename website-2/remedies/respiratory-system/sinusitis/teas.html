What herbal teas you should avoid if you have sinusitis?

If you have sinusitis, it's a good idea to avoid certain herbal teas that can irritate your sinuses or make symptoms worse.

One such herb is Peppermint (Mentha piperita), which can thin mucus and cause it to drain more freely, but in people with sinusitis, this can lead to more congestion and discomfort. Another herb to avoid is Ginger (Asarum canadense), which can irritate the mucous membranes and worsen sinus inflammation.

Horseradish (Armoracia rusticana) is also not recommended, as its spicy compounds can cause the sinuses to produce more mucus and exacerbate congestion. Additionally, Black Pepper (Piper nigrum) contains a chemical that can increase mucus production and worsen sinus symptoms. Finally, Sage (Sage salvia) and Oregano (Origanum vulgare) can cause irritation and inflammation in the sinuses, making symptoms worse.

These teas can be avoided or consumed in moderation to help manage sinusitis symptoms.

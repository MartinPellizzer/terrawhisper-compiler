What Are The Main Causes Of Acute Bronchitis?

Acute bronchitis is a common respiratory illness that affects the airways.

There are several main causes, including viral infection, bacterial infection, and Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV). A viral infection is the most common cause of acute bronchitis. This occurs when a cold or flu virus enters the body and attacks the cells lining the airways, causing inflammation and mucus production. As a result, the airways become swollen and clogged, making it difficult to breathe.

Bacterial infections can also cause acute bronchitis, particularly in people with weakened immune systems or underlying health conditions. Bacteria like Bordetella pertussis, Haemophilus influenzae, and Streptococcus pneumoniae can infect the airways, leading to bronchitis. Bacterial infections often follow a viral infection, which can weaken the body and make it more susceptible to bacterial invasion. Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV) is another common cause of acute bronchitis in young children and older adults. RSV is highly contagious and can cause severe respiratory illness in high-risk groups.

When RSV infects the airways, it can cause inflammation and mucus production, leading to symptoms of bronchitis.

Below are the main causes of acute bronchitis.

  • Viral Infection: A viral infection occurs when a virus, such as the common cold or flu, enters the body and attacks the cells in the bronchial tubes, leading to inflammation and swelling, which causes the characteristic symptoms of acute bronchitis.
  • Bacterial Infection: Bacterial infection is a cause of acute bronchitis because when bacteria enter the lungs, they multiply and cause inflammation, leading to the production of excess mucus and a cough that can last for several days or weeks.
  • Respiratory Syncytial Virus: Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV) causes acute bronchitis by infecting the cells in the airways, leading to inflammation and excess mucus production that makes it difficult to breathe.
  • Tobacco Smoke: Tobacco smoke irritates the airways, causing inflammation and producing excess mucus in the bronchial tubes, leading to acute bronchitis.
  • Adenovirus: Adenovirus causes acute bronchitis by infecting the cells lining the airways, leading to inflammation and irritation that makes the airways swell, fill with mucus, and produce a persistent cough.
  • Influenza: Influenza, or the flu, is a cause of acute bronchitis because the flu virus can infect the lungs and cause inflammation, making the airways in the lungs become swollen and irritated, leading to the symptoms of bronchitis.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Acute Bronchitis?

If you have acute bronchitis, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can irritate your airways or worsen symptoms.

One such herb is lobelia (Lobelia inflata), also known as Indian tobacco. Lobelia contains a chemical called lobeline, which can stimulate the lungs and cause coughing. While it may seem like a good idea to stimulate your lungs to help clear mucus, too much lobeline can lead to overstimulation, making your cough worse. Another herb to avoid is comfrey (Symphytum officinale), also known as boneset.

Comfrey contains a chemical called allantoin, which can help heal wounds, but it can also cause irritation in the lungs and airways, making bronchitis symptoms worse. Black cohosh (Actaea racemosa) is another herb that's best avoided with acute bronchitis. While it's often used to relieve coughs and colds, black cohosh contains a chemical called cicutarin, which can cause increased mucus production and worsen respiratory problems. Finally, pennyroyal (Mentha pulegium) is also a herb to steer clear of when you have bronchitis. This herb contains a chemical called pulegone, which can irritate the airways and worsen coughing.

These herbs can exacerbate your condition and make it harder for your body to recover from acute bronchitis.

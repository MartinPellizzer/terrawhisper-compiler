1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are active constituents that help with altitude sickness.

These compounds improve blood flow to the brain and reduce inflammation in the blood vessels, making it easier for the body to adapt to high altitudes. The flavonoids also have antioxidant properties, which protect the brain from damage caused by high levels of oxygen and carbon dioxide in the blood. Terpenoids help to thin the blood, reducing the risk of blood clots that can occur at high altitudes.

By improving blood flow and reducing inflammation and blood clotting, Ginkgo biloba creams can help alleviate symptoms of altitude sickness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of Ginkgo biloba extract, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or in a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth.
  3. Add the Ginkgo biloba extract and vitamin E oil to the melted mixture and stir well.
  4. Slowly add the distilled water to the mixture, stirring constantly until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container and let it cool and solidify before use.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains the active constituents gingerol, shogaol, and zingerone, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation in the body, which is a common cause of altitude sickness. Gingerol also has a natural vasodilating effect, which widens blood vessels and improves blood flow to the brain, reducing the risk of altitude sickness. Additionally, zingerone helps to reduce oxidative stress, which can contribute to altitude sickness.

By reducing inflammation and improving blood flow, Zingiber officinale creams may provide relief from the symptoms of altitude sickness, such as headaches and fatigue.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of grated fresh ginger, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 1 tablespoon of beeswax, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Combine the grated ginger and coconut oil in a small saucepan and heat over low heat, stirring until the oil is warm.
  3. In a separate saucepan, melt the beeswax and vitamin E oil over low heat, stirring until the beeswax is fully melted.
  4. Combine the ginger-coconut oil mixture and the melted beeswax mixture, then add the distilled water and stir until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify before using the Zingiber officinale cream for altitude sickness.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and polyphenols.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce swelling and ease breathing at high altitudes. The polyphenols in Echinacea purpurea creams also have vasodilatory effects, which can improve blood flow to the lungs and increase oxygen delivery to the body. The alkylamides have been shown to stimulate the production of white blood cells, which can help the body fight off infections that may arise due to altitude sickness.

By applying Echinacea purpurea creams to the skin, these active constituents can be absorbed and help alleviate symptoms associated with high-altitude conditions.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil, beeswax, and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from heat and add dried Echinacea purpurea flowers. Let it steep for 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a heat-proof bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Add vitamin E oil to the strained mixture and stir well. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify before use.

4. Astragalus membranaceus

Astragalus membranaceus creams contains a compound called astragaloside IV, which is a key bioactive constituent that helps alleviate symptoms of altitude sickness.

The saponins in Astragalus membranaceus have been shown to increase the production of nitric oxide, a molecule that helps dilate blood vessels and improve oxygen delivery to tissues.

By addressing these underlying mechanisms, Astragalus membranaceus creams may provide relief from altitude sickness symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of distilled water, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Astragalus membranaceus dried root powder, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add shea butter and stir until melted. Remove from heat.
  4. Add Astragalus membranaceus dried root powder, vitamin E oil, and distilled water to the melted mixture. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify. Store the Astragalus membranaceus cream in a cool, dry place.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains compounds like glycyrrhizin and 18α-glycyrrhetinic acid.

These saponins have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling in the lungs caused by altitude sickness. Glycyrrhizin also has a diuretic effect, helping the body to eliminate excess fluids that can accumulate in the lungs due to high altitude. Additionally, glycyrrhizin's antioxidant properties can help protect the body from oxidative stress caused by altitude sickness.

By reducing inflammation and excess fluids, and protecting the body from oxidative stress, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams may help alleviate symptoms of altitude sickness.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of glycerin, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Glycyrrhiza glabra root extract, 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval until melted.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix distilled water and glycerin. Add Glycyrrhiza glabra root extract and vitamin E oil. Stir well.
  4. Slowly pour the liquid mixture into the melted beeswax, stirring constantly until combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and harden. Once hardened, use as a cream for altitude sickness.

6. Angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis creams contains bioactive constituents like ferulic acid, hesperidin, and ligustilide.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help alleviate symptoms of altitude sickness, such as headaches and fatigue. The ferulic acid in Angelica sinensis creams has been shown to improve blood flow and reduce oxidative stress, which can help the body adapt to high altitudes. By reducing inflammation and improving blood flow, Angelica sinensis creams can help alleviate the symptoms of altitude sickness and promote a faster recovery.

  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add dried Angelica sinensis roots to the melted mixture and let it steep for 30 minutes to 1 hour. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.

  5. Let the mixture cool and harden completely. Once set, remove it from the mold or jar and store it in an airtight container in a cool, dry place.

  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second increments, stirring between each heating.
  3. Once melted, stir in turmeric powder, ginger powder, and lavender oil until well combined.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool for 5 minutes. Stir in vitamin E oil.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent altitude sickness from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent altitude sickness, but its effectiveness is not proven.

Ginger tea, for example, can ease nausea and headaches, which are common symptoms of altitude sickness. However, drinking herbal tea won't replace the need to ascend gradually and stay hydrated.

It might provide some relief, but more research is needed to confirm its benefits.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for altitude sickness every day?

While herbal teas can help alleviate altitude sickness symptoms, consuming them daily isn't a straightforward answer.

Some teas, like ginkgo biloba and ginger, can help with oxygen flow and nausea. However, excessive consumption of certain herbs might interact with other medications or have adverse effects.

Moderation is key when it comes to herbal teas.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in altitude sickness?

Herbal teas like ginger and ginkgo biloba may help alleviate altitude sickness symptoms.

Their effects can be noticed within 30 minutes to 2 hours after consumption.

As you ascend to higher elevations, these teas can help reduce dizziness and nausea, allowing you to adapt more comfortably.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for altitude sickness?

At high altitudes, the air is thinner and your body needs extra help to cope.

Drinking herbal tea in the morning can help.

Ginger tea, in particular, is good at this time as it can help ease nausea and boost energy.

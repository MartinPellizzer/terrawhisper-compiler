Altitude sickness solutions for climbers: 7 herbal essential oils

When climbers head up high, they face a serious risk of altitude sickness.

This happens when the air gets thinner and the body can't get enough oxygen. It's like trying to breathe through a straw - it's hard to get enough air in. At first, climbers might feel a little tired or dizzy, but if the sickness gets worse, it can lead to headaches, nausea, and even vomiting. In severe cases, altitude sickness can cause fluid to build up in the lungs and brain, which can be life-threatening. Climbers need to be careful and take steps to prevent altitude sickness, but even with precautions, it can still happen. Climbers often have to spend days or even weeks at high altitudes, and that's a lot of time to feel miserable.

They might have to slow down or even stop their climb altogether, which can be frustrating and scary. It's not just the physical effects that are a problem - altitude sickness can also cause anxiety and fear. Climbers might worry about their safety, or feel anxious about not being able to reach their goals. Herbal essential oils can be a helpful addition to a climber's treatment plan. These oils are made from the concentrated extracts of plants, and they have natural anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. When inhaled or applied topically, they can help to reduce swelling and ease respiratory problems. They can also help to calm the mind and body, reducing anxiety and stress.

By using herbal essential oils, climbers might be able to recover faster from altitude sickness and get back to their climb sooner.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils have been traditionally used to help alleviate symptoms of altitude sickness.

This is because the oil contains properties that improve circulation and reduce oxidative stress. The main constituents of ginkgo biloba essential oils, such as bilobalide and ginkgolides, have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which may help to minimize the damage caused by high altitude. To try using ginkgo biloba essential oils for altitude sickness, you can inhale the oil directly from a cloth or handkerchief that has been lightly sprayed with the oil. You can also add a few drops of the oil to your bath water or inhale it through steam inhalation.

Some people also use it in a diffuser to inhale the vaporized oil. When inhaled, the active ingredients in the oil may help to ease symptoms such as headaches, dizziness, and shortness of breath. Inhaling ginkgo biloba essential oils may help to reduce the severity of altitude sickness symptoms. You may notice an improvement in your breathing, reduced headaches, and less dizziness.

However, if your symptoms worsen or you experience severe nausea, vomiting, or fatigue, it's essential to seek medical attention as soon as possible.

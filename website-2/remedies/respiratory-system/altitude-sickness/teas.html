How to Beat Altitude Sickness with 7 Herbal Teas for Skiers

When skiers travel to high-altitude ski resorts, they can suffer from altitude sickness.

This is because their bodies struggle to adapt to the lower air pressure and oxygen levels. As a result, they may experience symptoms like headaches, fatigue, and nausea. For some people, these symptoms can be severe and even lead to altitude sickness, a condition that requires immediate medical attention. Skiers who suffer from altitude sickness may find it difficult to enjoy their trip, and it can even affect their ability to ski or participate in other activities. They may feel tired and weak, and even experience shortness of breath. This can be a real problem for skiers who are looking to enjoy their time on the slopes.

Altitude sickness can also lead to other issues, such as insomnia and decreased appetite. These symptoms can make it even harder for skiers to enjoy their trip, and can even affect their overall mood. Some skiers may also experience dizziness and lightheadedness, which can be particularly problematic when skiing. As you can imagine, this can be a real challenge for people who are trying to have a good time on the mountain. One way that skiers can help treat altitude sickness is by drinking herbal teas. These teas are made from plants that are rich in antioxidants and other compounds that can help reduce inflammation and improve circulation. They can also help to calm the mind and body, which can be particularly helpful when you're feeling anxious or jittery from the symptoms of altitude sickness.

By drinking these teas, skiers can help to alleviate their symptoms and feel more comfortable and relaxed.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas have been used for centuries to alleviate symptoms of altitude sickness, also known as acute mountain sickness (AMS).

The properties and constituents of Ginkgo biloba that help with altitude sickness include flavonoids and terpenoids, which are antioxidants that improve blood flow to the brain, helping to prevent damage caused by high altitude. The leaf extract also contains bilobalide and ginkgolides, which help to reduce inflammation and improve oxygen delivery to the body's tissues. If you're planning to travel to high altitudes and are concerned about altitude sickness, consider drinking Ginkgo biloba tea 1-2 days before ascending to high altitudes. You can make tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of Ginkgo biloba leaves in boiling water for 5-10 minutes.

Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help prevent altitude sickness. It's essential to note that while Ginkgo biloba tea may help alleviate symptoms, it's not a substitute for medical treatment. If you experience severe headache, nausea, or fatigue, seek medical attention. Taking Ginkgo biloba tea as a preventive measure may lead to reduced symptoms of altitude sickness, such as headaches, fatigue, and dizziness. You may also experience improved cognitive function and memory, thanks to the improved blood flow to the brain.

However, it's crucial to remember that Ginkgo biloba tea is not a cure for altitude sickness, and in severe cases, medical treatment may still be necessary.

2. Rhodiola rosea

Rhodiola rosea teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate symptoms of altitude sickness.

This herbal tea contains properties such as rosavin, rosin, and salidroside, which have been shown to help reduce the effects of high altitudes. These compounds work by improving blood flow, reducing oxidative stress, and enhancing the body's ability to adapt to high-altitude conditions. When taking Rhodiola rosea teas, it's recommended to start drinking it a few days before ascending to high altitudes.

Begin with a small amount, about 1/2 teaspoon of dried Rhodiola rosea per cup of boiling water, and adjust to taste. It's also essential to stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water and to ascend to high altitudes gradually to give your body time to acclimate. The likely outcomes of taking Rhodiola rosea teas for altitude sickness include reduced fatigue, improved mood, and a decrease in symptoms such as headaches and nausea. Some people may also experience improved physical performance and a faster recovery time.

However, it's essential to note that Rhodiola rosea teas are not a substitute for medical treatment, and if symptoms persist or worsen, seek medical attention.

3. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng teas have been traditionally used to help alleviate symptoms of altitude sickness, also known as Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS).

This is because Panax ginseng contains properties and constituents like ginsenosides, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These effects can help reduce blood vessel constriction, improve blood flow to the brain, and reduce inflammation in the body, all of which can help alleviate the symptoms of altitude sickness.

When taking Panax ginseng teas for altitude sickness, it's recommended to drink 1-2 cups a day, starting a few days before ascending to high altitudes. It's also essential to combine Panax ginseng teas with other preventive measures, such as ascending gradually, staying hydrated, and getting enough rest. By taking these actions, you can potentially reduce the severity and frequency of altitude sickness symptoms, such as headaches, nausea, and fatigue.

However, it's also possible that you may still experience some symptoms, especially if you ascend too quickly or have a pre-existing medical condition.

4. Astragalus membranaceus

Astragalus membranaceus teas are a natural remedy that can help alleviate altitude sickness.

This is due to the plant's properties and constituents, such as its high levels of astragaloside IV, which has been shown to improve blood flow to the brain and increase oxygen delivery. Astragalus membranaceus teas also contain flavonoids and polysaccharides that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which can help reduce the severity of altitude sickness symptoms like headaches and fatigue.

To use Astragalus membranaceus teas for altitude sickness, steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried root in boiling water for 5-10 minutes, then drink 2-3 cups per day. Start taking the tea 1-2 days before ascending to high altitudes and continue for 2-3 days after arrival.

Drinking Astragalus membranaceus teas may help reduce the severity of altitude sickness symptoms, allowing you to ascend to higher elevations with less discomfort.

Ilex paraguariensis teas, also known as yerba mate, have been traditionally used to help alleviate symptoms of altitude sickness.

The tea's properties and constituents, such as caffeine, theobromine, and saponins, are thought to contribute to its potential benefits. Caffeine, in particular, can help increase alertness and reduce drowsiness, which are common symptoms of altitude sickness. The theobromine, on the other hand, may help to expand blood vessels, which can improve blood flow to the brain and other tissues. If you're planning to travel to high-altitude areas, consider drinking Ilex paraguariensis teas regularly, especially during the initial stages of acclimatization.

Start by taking small amounts, about 1-2 cups per day, and gradually increase the dose as needed. It's also essential to stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water and to take regular breaks to rest and acclimate to the higher elevation. When taking Ilex paraguariensis teas for altitude sickness, you may experience improved energy levels, reduced fatigue, and enhanced mental clarity. Some people may also notice a decrease in headaches and nausea, which are common symptoms of altitude sickness. However, it's essential to note that while Ilex paraguariensis teas may help alleviate symptoms, they should not be relied upon as the sole means of preventing or treating altitude sickness.

Consult with a healthcare professional before traveling to high-altitude areas, especially if you have pre-existing medical conditions or concerns.

7. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, commonly known as ginger tea, may help alleviate symptoms of altitude sickness.

The active compound, gingerol, has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling in the brain caused by high altitude. Ginger also contains a natural pain reliever called shogaol, which can help ease headaches and nausea associated with altitude sickness. Another constituent, ginger's antioxidant properties, can help protect against oxidative stress caused by high altitude. To use ginger tea for altitude sickness, steep fresh ginger in hot water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink 2-3 cups a day.

You can also add honey and lemon to make the tea more palatable. Start drinking ginger tea a day before ascending to high altitude, and continue for the duration of your stay. Taking ginger tea may lead to reduced nausea, headaches, and fatigue associated with altitude sickness. You may also experience improved digestion and reduced inflammation. However, note that ginger tea should not replace medical treatment for severe altitude sickness.

If symptoms persist or worsen, seek medical attention.

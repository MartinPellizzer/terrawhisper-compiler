What herbal teas you should avoid if you have chronic bronchitis?

If you have chronic bronchitis, it's best to avoid herbal teas that can irritate your airways or make your symptoms worse.

One such herb is 'Hyssopus officinalis' or hyssop. While it may help with respiratory issues in some people, it can also cause allergic reactions and worsen bronchitis symptoms in others. Another herb to avoid is 'Silybum marianum', also known as milk thistle.

While it's often used to support liver health, it can interact with certain medications used to treat chronic bronchitis, such as corticosteroids and bronchodilators. 'Angelica sinensis', or dong quai, is another herb to use with caution. It may help with respiratory issues, but high doses can cause blood thinning and interact with medications like aspirin and warfarin. Lastly, 'Valeriana officinalis', or valerian root, can be a problem if you're taking sedatives or other medications that affect your breathing.

While it's often used to help with sleep, it can exacerbate breathing difficulties in people with chronic bronchitis.

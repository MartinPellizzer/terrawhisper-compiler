What Are The Main Causes Of Nasal Congestion?

Nasal congestion occurs when the nasal passages become swollen and inflamed, blocking airflow and making it hard to breathe.

There are several main causes of nasal congestion. Allergies are a common cause, triggered by substances like pollen, dust, and pet dander. When these substances enter the body, they cause the immune system to react and release histamine, a chemical that makes blood vessels swell and narrow the airways. This leads to congestion and other allergy symptoms like sneezing and runny nose. A cold is another common cause of nasal congestion. When a cold virus enters the body, it infects the nasal passages and throat, causing them to become inflamed and swollen.

This leads to congestion, coughing, and a runny nose. The cold virus also produces mucus, which further blocks the nasal passages and makes breathing difficult. The flu, or influenza, is a viral infection that can also cause nasal congestion. Like a cold, the flu virus infects the nasal passages and throat, causing them to become inflamed and swollen. This leads to congestion, coughing, and a runny nose. The flu virus also produces mucus, which blocks the nasal passages and makes breathing difficult.

In addition, the flu can cause more severe symptoms like high fever and body aches, which can further exacerbate nasal congestion.

Below are the main causes of nasal congestion.

  • Allergies: When someone has an allergy, their body mistakenly thinks a harmless thing like pollen or dust is a threat, and it releases chemicals to fight it, which causes the blood vessels in their nose to swell up and fill with fluid, making it hard to breathe.
  • Cold: When the air we breathe is cold, it causes the blood vessels in our nose to constrict or narrow, making it harder for air to flow through and leading to nasal congestion.
  • Flu: When you have the flu, the virus makes your body produce too much mucus, a thick liquid, which blocks your nasal passages and causes congestion.
  • Sinusitis: Sinusitis occurs when the sinuses become inflamed, producing excess mucus and causing the nasal passages to swell, leading to nasal congestion.
  • Asthma: Asthma causes nasal congestion because the airways in the lungs and the nasal passages become inflamed, producing excess mucus that blocks the nose, making it hard to breathe.

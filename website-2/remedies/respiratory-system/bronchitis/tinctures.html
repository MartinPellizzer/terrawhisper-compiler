1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contains potent bioactive constituents like cineole, limonene, and alpha-pinene.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, making them effective in loosening and clearing mucus from the airways. Cineole, in particular, helps to reduce bronchospasm and congestion, while limonene and alpha-pinene have antimicrobial properties that fight off infections. By reducing inflammation and promoting drainage, eucalyptus globulus tinctures can help to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis, such as coughing and shortness of breath.

Regular use of eucalyptus globulus tinctures may also help to prevent future episodes of bronchitis by maintaining healthy lung function.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of 80% vodka in clean glass containers.
  2. Combine the Eucalyptus leaves and vodka in a clean glass jar, making sure the leaves are completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container, discarding the solids.
  5. Bottle the tincture and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, as needed for bronchitis symptoms.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains a combination of bioactive constituents, including alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and glycosides.

These compounds work together to reduce inflammation and fight off infection in the respiratory tract, helping to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis. Alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to calm irritated airways and ease congestion. Caffeic acid derivatives, on the other hand, have antimicrobial properties that can help to combat bacterial infections that can contribute to bronchitis.

By using Echinacea purpurea tinctures, some people find that their symptoms of bronchitis, such as coughing and shortness of breath, are reduced.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves. Clean and dry them.
  2. Combine 1 cup of the dried flowers and leaves with 2 cups of vodka in a glass jar. Shake well.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle. Use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, mixed with water or tea, to help with bronchitis symptoms.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains the bioactive constituents glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and triterpenoids.

By reducing inflammation and promoting mucus clearance, Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures can help to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis.

This anti-inflammatory property makes it effective in treating bronchitis by soothing irritated tissues and reducing mucus production.

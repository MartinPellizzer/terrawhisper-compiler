What herbal teas you should avoid if you have bronchitis?

If you have bronchitis, there are some herbal teas you should avoid or use with caution.

Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza uralensis) tea is not recommended for people with bronchitis. This is because licorice root can increase fluid retention in the body, which may worsen bronchitis symptoms. Marshmallow root (Althaea officinalis) tea is another one to be cautious with. It can help soothe the mucous membranes, but it may also cause an allergic reaction or interact with medications in some people.

Comfrey (Symphytum officinale) tea is not suitable for people with bronchitis. This is because comfrey contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can cause liver damage if consumed in large amounts over a long period. Coltsfoot (Tussilago farfara) tea is also not recommended for people with bronchitis. While it has anti-inflammatory properties, it can cause respiratory problems and interact with other medications. Red clover (Trifolium pratense) tea is another one to avoid or use with caution.

It may interact with blood thinners and other medications, and its effects on bronchitis symptoms are not well-studied.

10 herbal teas for singers to cure sore throat

Singers often put a lot of strain on their vocal cords, which can lead to sore throats.

When they sing, they use their vocal cords to produce sound, and this can cause tiny tears and inflammation. This can be painful and make it hard to sing. A sore throat can also make it hard to eat and sleep, and it can even affect a singer's mood. If a singer has to cancel a performance or two because of a sore throat, it can be stressful and affect their income. Some singers even have to change their diet and avoid talking too much to try to feel better.

When a singer gets a sore throat, they need to do things that will help soothe and calm their vocal cords. One thing that can help is staying hydrated, but drinking plain water can be boring. That's where herbal teas come in. Some herbal teas have properties that can help reduce inflammation and kill bacteria that can cause a sore throat. They can also help soothe and calm the vocal cords, making it easier to sing and feel better. When a singer drinks an herbal tea, the soothing properties can help calm their throat and reduce the pain.

This can be especially helpful when a singer has to sing a lot, like in a musical or on tour.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, also known as licorice root tea, have been traditionally used to help soothe a sore throat.

The tea's active compounds, including glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling and pain in the throat. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of enzymes that break down the mucous membrane in the throat, thereby preventing further irritation and discomfort. When you take Glycyrrhiza glabra tea to help with a sore throat, you can start by drinking 1-2 cups a day, 2-3 times a day.

To get the most out of the tea, try to drink it when your throat is sore, rather than waiting until it subsides. You can also try mixing the tea with honey to make it more palatable and to take advantage of the additional soothing properties of honey. After a few days of consistent use, you may start to feel a reduction in your throat pain and discomfort, and your mucous membranes may start to recover.

Your throat may feel more comfortable, and you may be able to speak and swallow more easily.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger teas, have been a traditional remedy for sore throats for centuries.

The reason why ginger teas help with sore throat is due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. To use ginger tea for a sore throat, you can make a tea by steeping fresh ginger in hot water for 5-7 minutes. You can also add honey and lemon to taste.

Some people prefer to drink ginger tea several times a day to help alleviate symptoms. You can also try making a paste by grinding ginger and mixing it with water to create a warm compress that you can apply to the throat. When you drink ginger tea for a sore throat, you can expect to experience relief from pain and discomfort. The anti-inflammatory properties of ginger can help reduce swelling and redness in the throat, making it easier to swallow and speak.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in the throat. Echinacea's flavonoids, particularly kaempferol and quercetin, have potent antioxidant effects that combat oxidative stress and promote healing. The polysaccharides in Echinacea purpurea creams stimulate the immune system, increasing the production of white blood cells to fight off infections that cause sore throats.

By addressing inflammation, oxidative stress, and immune function, Echinacea purpurea creams provide relief from sore throats.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the dried Echinacea flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  3. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container. Discard the solids.
  4. Mix 2 tablespoons of the Echinacea oil with 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter in a small saucepan.
  5. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring until the beeswax and shea butter melt. Remove from heat and let cool. Pour the mixture into small tin containers to create the Echinacea cream.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains compounds like eucalyptol, cineole, and globulol.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the throat. Cineole, in particular, has a decongestant effect, which can ease congestion and promote easier breathing. Eucalyptol also has antimicrobial properties, which can help combat infections that cause sore throats.

By using Eucalyptus globulus creams, the anti-inflammatory, decongestant, and antimicrobial effects of these compounds can work together to provide relief from sore throats.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil and 1/4 cup of shea butter. Melt them in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir until combined.
  3. Add 10 drops of eucalyptus essential oil to the mixture. Stir well to combine.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool until it reaches 120°F to 130°F. This is the ideal temperature for whipping.
  5. Pour the mixture into a stand mixer or a hand mixer and whip until light and fluffy. Pour the cream into a container and let it cool completely before use.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains triterpenoid saponins, specifically glycyrrhizin, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

It also contains flavonoids, such as liquiritin and isoliquiritin, which have antioxidant properties. The anti-inflammatory properties of glycyrrhizin help to reduce swelling and pain in the throat, making it easier to swallow and speak. The antioxidant properties of flavonoids help to protect the throat from damage caused by free radicals, promoting a healthy environment for healing.

The combination of glycyrrhizin and flavonoids in Glycyrrhiza glabra creams helps to soothe and calm the throat, providing relief from soreness and discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of glycerin, 1/2 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of coconut oil.
  2. Heat the glycerin and distilled water in a saucepan over low heat until the beeswax and coconut oil melt.
  3. Add the glycyrrhiza glabra root powder to the saucepan and stir well.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool slightly. Pour the mixture into a bowl and let it cool completely.
  5. Once the mixture is cool and thickened, pour it into a container and store it in the fridge. Apply a small amount to your sore throat as needed.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and pain in the throat. The creams also contain vitamin C, which is an antioxidant that fights free radicals and helps soothe the throat. The anti-inflammatory compounds in Zingiber officinale creams also help thin mucus, making it easier to swallow and relieving congestion.

The combination of these properties in Zingiber officinale creams can provide quick relief from a sore throat.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 2 teaspoons of Zingiber officinale powder, and 10 drops of peppermint essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add shea butter and olive oil to the melted mixture. Stir until combined.
  4. Add Zingiber officinale powder and peppermint essential oil to the mixture. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a tin or jar and let it cool and solidify. Once set, your Zingiber officinale cream for sore throat is ready to use.

5. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams contains thymol and carvacrol, two active compounds that help soothe a sore throat.

Thymol has antibacterial properties that reduce the risk of infection, while carvacrol helps reduce inflammation and ease pain. Thymus vulgaris creams also contain flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties that help protect the throat tissue from damage. The antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties of thymol and carvacrol in Thymus vulgaris creams make it effective in reducing throat irritation and promoting healing.

Applying Thymus vulgaris creams to the throat can help calm inflammation and reduce discomfort caused by a sore throat.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1 tablespoon of dried Thymus vulgaris, and 1 cup of coconut oil.
  2. Heat the distilled water in a saucepan over low heat until it's warm.
  3. Add the dried Thymus vulgaris to the warm water and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture and discard the Thymus vulgaris. Combine the liquid with the coconut oil in a bowl.
  5. Mix the liquid and coconut oil until the mixture is well combined. Pour it into a container and let it cool before use.

6. Mentha x piperita

The combination of these properties in Mentha x piperita creams makes it effective in soothing and calming a sore throat.

  2. Melt the coconut oil, beeswax, and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water. Stir until melted and smooth.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from the heat and let it cool slightly. Stir in the mentha x piperita essential oil and vitamin E oil.
  4. Pour the mixture into a heat-proof container or a lip balm tin. Let it cool and solidify completely.
  5. Once solid, your Mentha x piperita cream for sore throat is ready to use. Apply as needed to soothe a sore throat and chest.

8. Hydrastis canadensis

Hydrastis canadensis creams contains bioactive constituents like hydrastine, berberine, and alkaloids.

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent sore throat from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe a sore throat.

Some teas, like slippery elm, contain properties that can calm and protect the mucous membranes in your throat. Other teas, like ginger and licorice root, have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce swelling and discomfort.

These teas can provide some relief from throat irritation.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for sore throat every day?

Herbal teas for sore throat can be a good option, but it's essential to use them in moderation.

Drinking them every day can lead to caffeine overload, especially if you're consuming teas like peppermint or ginger. This might cause stomach upset or insomnia.

It's best to limit your intake to a few cups a day and choose teas that are caffeine-free.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in sore throat?

Herbal teas can start soothing a sore throat within 15-30 minutes.

Some people notice relief from pain and swelling within a few hours. The effectiveness can vary depending on the type of tea and individual body chemistry.

Drinking herbal tea regularly may help keep the throat comfortable for a longer period.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for sore throat?

Drinking herbal tea for a sore throat can be helpful at any time, but many people find it soothing to have it before bed. This is because it can help calm your throat and promote a good night's sleep, which can aid in recovery.

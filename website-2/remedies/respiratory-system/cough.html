What Are The Main Causes Of Cough?

A cough is a common symptom that occurs when your body tries to remove something from your airways.

The main causes of cough can be classified into two main categories: respiratory infections and non-respiratory causes. Respiratory infections like Pneumonia and TB (tuberculosis) are caused by bacteria or viruses that infect the lungs. Pneumonia occurs when the lungs become inflamed due to a bacterial or viral infection, making it difficult to breathe and leading to coughing.

TB is a bacterial infection that affects the lungs and can cause a persistent cough, especially at night. Bronchitis, another respiratory infection, occurs when the airways in your lungs become inflamed, often due to a viral or bacterial infection. This can cause a persistent cough, which may be worse when you cough up mucus.

Other causes of cough include allergies, acid reflux, and irritation of the airways due to smoke, dust, or pollution.

Below are the main causes of cough.

  • Pneumonia: Pneumonia is a cause of cough because the infection in the lungs irritates the airways, leading to the release of mucus, which triggers the cough reflex to try and clear the lungs.
  • TB: Tuberculosis (TB) causes cough because the bacteria that infect the lungs release toxic waste, which irritates the lungs and triggers a cough to expel the waste from the body.
  • Bronchitis: Bronchitis is a cause of cough because the bronchial tubes in the lungs become inflamed, which irritates them and triggers a cough to remove the excess mucus and phlegm.
  • Pneumonitis: Pneumonitis is a cause of cough because the inflammation in the lungs irritates the airways, sending a signal to the brain to produce a cough to clear out the irritants.
  • Asthma: Asthma is a cause of cough because when people with asthma breathe in something that irritates their airways, their airways become inflamed and produce extra mucus, leading to a cough to try and clear out the irritant.

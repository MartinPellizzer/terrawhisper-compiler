Cough Prevention And Treatment: Causes, Herbal Preparations And Medicinal Uses

Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea is a type of plant that has been used for a long time to help with coughs. It has special compounds that help fight off the infection that is causing the cough. This plant has anti-inflammatory properties, which means it can reduce swelling and pain in the throat, making it easier to breathe and swallow. It also has antiviral properties, which help to stop the virus from spreading and causing more harm. When taken as a supplement, Echinacea purpurea can help to boost the immune system, making the body stronger and more able to fight off the infection that is causing the cough. Some people also use it to help soothe a sore throat, making it a popular remedy for colds and flu.

Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, also known as licorice root, is a natural remedy that has been used for centuries to soothe a cough. The root contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which has anti-inflammatory properties. When you have a cough, the lining in your throat gets inflamed, and it hurts. Glycyrrhizin helps to reduce this inflammation, making it easier for you to breathe and swallow. Additionally, licorice root has a sweet and soothing effect on the throat, which can calm a cough and make it more comfortable to cough up mucus. The root also has antimicrobial properties, which can help to fight off infections that may be causing your cough. Many herbal teas and cough syrups contain licorice root as an active ingredient, and it is often used in traditional medicine to treat coughs and other respiratory problems.

Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, also known as ginger, is a popular natural remedy for cough. Its active compounds, like gingerols and shogaols, have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the throat, making it easier to breathe and swallow. Ginger also has expectorant properties, which means it helps loosen and clear mucus from the lungs, giving you relief from a persistent cough. Additionally, ginger has antimicrobial properties that help combat infections that can cause cough. When consumed as a warm tea or added to food, ginger's natural compounds are absorbed into the body, providing quick relief from cough and soothing the throat. The anti-inflammatory effects also help reduce congestion and open up airways, making it easier to breathe and sleep.

Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus, also known as the blue gum eucalyptus, is a plant that has been used for centuries to treat coughs. The reason it's good for coughs is because of the oils it contains, specifically eucalyptol. When you breathe in the steam from eucalyptus leaves, eucalyptol enters your body and helps to loosen and clear mucus from your airways. This makes it easier to cough up and get rid of the mucus, relieving congestion and making it easier to breathe. Eucalyptol also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling in the throat and make your cough less severe. Additionally, eucalyptus oil has a decongestant effect, which can help to reduce the production of mucus, making it less likely to form in the first place.

Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris, also known as common thyme, is a popular herb used to treat coughs. It has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to soothe sore throats and calm coughs. The reason for its effectiveness lies in its active compounds, such as thymol and carvacrol, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. When inhaled, these compounds help to reduce congestion and kill bacteria that can cause coughs. Thymus vulgaris also contains mucolytic properties, which break down mucus and make it easier to cough up. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory properties help to reduce swelling in the throat, making it a natural remedy for coughs and sore throats. When consumed as tea, Thymus vulgaris can help to calm the cough and soothe the throat, providing quick relief from coughs and colds.

Mentha x piperita

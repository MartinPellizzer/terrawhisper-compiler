8 Herbal Creams For Chest Congestion

The reason is that when you drink herbal teas, the herbs are absorbed through your digestive system, whereas when you apply creams directly to the skin, the herbs work more quickly to ease congestion. For example, Eucalyptus globulus, a common ingredient in many chest congestion creams, contains a compound called eucalyptol, which helps to thin mucus, making it easier to breathe.

Echinacea purpurea is another herb that's often used in these creams, and it's known for its immune-boosting properties that can help to fight off infections that cause congestion. Additionally, these creams are often non-irritating and can be used on sensitive skin, making them a great option for people who are sensitive to harsh chemicals.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains a compound called eucalyptol, which is a natural decongestant.

Eucalyptol helps loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to breathe. Eucalyptol also has anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling and ease congestion in the chest. The menthol in eucalyptus globulus creams helps thin mucus, making it easier to cough up, and also helps open up airways.

By combining these properties, eucalyptus globulus creams effectively relieve chest congestion and make breathing easier.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water and 1/4 cup of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Add 10 drops of Eucalyptus globulus essential oil to the distilled water.
  3. Combine the distilled water and carrier oil in a small saucepan and heat it gently on low heat, stirring until the oil and water mix.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and refrigerate it until it thickens into a cream. Use it as needed for chest congestion relief.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, a powerful antioxidant, and borneol, a compound known for its expectorant properties.

These components help loosen and clear mucus from the airways, making it easier to breathe. The anti-inflammatory properties of rosmarinic acid also reduce swelling in the throat and chest, further alleviating congestion. Additionally, borneol's expectorant properties help thin and expel mucus, making it an effective treatment for chest congestion.

By combining these properties, Rosmarinus officinalis creams offer a natural and effective solution to relieve chest congestion.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.
  2. Combine the distilled water, coconut oil, and beeswax in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring until the beeswax is melted.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and add the shea butter and dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves. Stir well to combine.
  4. Let the mixture cool and thicken. This may take about 30 minutes to an hour. Stir occasionally to ensure even thickening.
  5. Once the mixture has thickened, pour it into a container and let it cool completely. Store the Rosmarinus officinalis cream in a cool, dry place.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the nasal passages and airways. Alkylamides, in particular, are known for their ability to relax and open airways, allowing for easier breathing. Caffeic acid and rosmarinic acid also have antiviral properties that can help combat infections that cause chest congestion.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off infections, Echinacea purpurea creams can help alleviate chest congestion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat. Stir occasionally.
  3. Add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the melted oil and beeswax mixture. Let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the Echinacea flowers.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify. Once set, use a spoon to scoop out the cream and transfer it to an airtight container. Store in a cool place.

4. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris creams contains thymol and carvacrol as its active constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help relieve chest congestion. Thymol works by killing the bacteria that cause infections, while carvacrol helps reduce inflammation in the airways. The anti-inflammatory properties of carvacrol also help thin mucus, making it easier to cough up.

By killing bacteria and reducing inflammation, Thymus vulgaris creams can help alleviate chest congestion symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Thymus vulgaris leaves, 1 cup of coconut oil, and a double boiler.
  2. Steep the dried Thymus vulgaris leaves in the coconut oil for 2 hours, then strain the mixture.
  3. In a clean bowl, mix 2 tablespoons of the infused oil with 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter.
  4. Heat the mixture in the double boiler until the beeswax and shea butter melt.
  5. Let the mixture cool and harden, then store it in an airtight container for up to 6 months.

5. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the airways.

These compounds also exhibit expectorant properties, making it easier to cough up mucus and other debris that can cause congestion. Additionally, ginger has natural warming properties that can help loosen and clear out mucus from the chest. The anti-inflammatory and warming properties of ginger work together to help reduce discomfort and make breathing easier.

This combination of properties makes Zingiber officinale creams a helpful treatment for chest congestion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 10 drops of ginger essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the shea butter to the melted oil and beeswax mixture and stir until combined.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and add the ginger essential oil. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a tin or jar and let it cool and solidify. Once set, your Zingiber officinale cream for chest congestion is ready to use.

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains menthol, limonene, and eucalyptol as its main bioactive constituents.

Menthol helps to thin mucus and ease congestion, making it easier to cough up excess phlegm. Limonene has anti-inflammatory properties, reducing swelling in the airways and relieving pressure on the chest. Eucalyptol acts as a bronchodilator, opening up airways and improving airflow.

These properties work together to provide fast and effective relief from chest congestion.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil, 2 tablespoons of peppermint essential oil (Mentha x piperita).
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from heat and stir in vitamin E oil and peppermint essential oil.
  4. Pour the mixture into a small tin or container and let it cool until solid.
  5. Apply a small amount of the cream to the chest area as needed to help relieve congestion.

7. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two main bioactive constituents that contribute to its decongestant properties.

These compounds have a bronchodilatory effect, meaning they help to open up airways and allow for easier breathing. Linalool also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling in the chest and ease congestion caused by colds and respiratory infections. Additionally, the calming and soothing effects of linalool can help to relax the body and mind, reducing stress and anxiety that can exacerbate respiratory issues.

When applied topically to the chest, Lavandula angustifolia creams can provide quick relief from congestion and promote a more comfortable breathing experience.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of shea butter.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Add the dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the melted oil and beeswax, and let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture and discard the flowers. Add the shea butter and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  5. Pour the mixture into a heat-proof container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, it's ready to use for chest congestion relief.

8. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra creams contains active constituents like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties that help loosen and clear mucus from the lungs and airways. The flavonoids, particularly rutin and quercetin, reduce inflammation and swelling in the nasal passages and airways, making it easier to breathe. The phenolic acids, such as caffeic acid and ferulic acid, have antimicrobial properties that fight off infections and reduce congestion.

By combining these properties, Sambucus nigra creams help relieve chest congestion and make breathing easier.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Elder flower (Sambucus nigra), 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil), 1 tablespoon of beeswax, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Combine dried Elder flower and carrier oil in a saucepan, then heat on low for 2-3 hours to make a herbal infusion.
  3. Strain the infusion and discard the dried flowers. Add beeswax and vitamin E oil to the infusion and heat until beeswax melts.
  4. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool for 10-15 minutes, then stir well to create a smooth cream.
  5. Pour the cream into a container and store in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. Apply as needed to help relieve chest congestion.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent chest congestion from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent chest congestion from forming.

Thyme and eucalyptus teas are known for their decongestant properties, helping to loosen mucus and ease breathing. Ginger tea can also aid digestion, which can help prevent congestion caused by stomach issues.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for chest congestion every day?

Herbal teas for chest congestion can be a helpful relief, but it's essential to be mindful of your body's limits.

Drinking them daily can lead to over-reliance and potentially interact with other medications.

It's also crucial to check the ingredients and quality of the tea to avoid any adverse effects.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in chest congestion?

Herbal teas can help with chest congestion, but the time it takes to feel relief varies.

Some people notice improvement in 30 minutes to an hour, while others may take a few hours or even a full day to feel better.

It depends on the tea and the severity of the congestion.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for chest congestion?

Drink herbal tea for chest congestion when you need a break.

A warm cup of tea can help ease congestion. Try having it in the morning or early afternoon when you're feeling most relaxed and still have time to rest.

This can help loosen mucus and make breathing easier.

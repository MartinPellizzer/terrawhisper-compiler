1. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum essential oils contains a high concentration of silymarin, a complex mixture of flavonoids and polyphenols.

Silymarin has been shown to have antioxidant properties, which help to protect liver cells from damage caused by free radicals. The sesquiterpene lactones, particularly eudesmanolides and germacranolides, in Silybum marianum essential oils have been found to have hepatoprotective properties, which can help to reduce liver inflammation and improve liver function. The essential oil's anti-inflammatory properties also help to reduce liver damage caused by jaundice, a condition characterized by an accumulation of bilirubin in the blood.

By reducing liver inflammation and protecting liver cells, Silybum marianum essential oils may help to alleviate the symptoms of jaundice.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Silybum marianum seeds, 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine Silybum marianum seeds and carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well to mix.
  3. Seal the jar and leave it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the seeds.
  5. Store the Silybum marianum essential oil in a dark glass bottle, away from heat and light. Use 2-3 drops per day to help with jaundice symptoms under medical guidance.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains zingiberene and gingerols, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Gingerols also have a direct effect on the liver, stimulating the production of bile and improving liver function.

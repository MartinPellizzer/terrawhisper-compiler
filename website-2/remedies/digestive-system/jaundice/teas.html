Infants with Jaundice: 7 Natural Herbal Teas

Jaundice is a common condition in newborn babies, where the skin and eyes turn yellow due to a buildup of bilirubin in the blood.

This happens when the liver can't remove bilirubin from the blood quickly enough. If left untreated, high levels of bilirubin can cause serious brain damage and other complications. Jaundice can make babies look and feel sick, and it can be painful for them to eat and breastfeed. They might be fussy, sleepy, and have trouble feeding, which can be stressful for both the baby and the parents.

If a baby's bilirubin levels get too high, they might need to stay in the hospital for treatment, which can be scary and overwhelming for everyone involved. In some cases, hospitals may prescribe herbal teas to help treat jaundice in newborns. These teas work by gently stimulating the liver to remove bilirubin from the blood. They are usually given to the mother to drink, so the baby can benefit from the medicinal properties while still breastfeeding. The teas help to stimulate the liver and improve the flow of bile, which helps to remove bilirubin from the blood more efficiently.

This can help reduce the baby's bilirubin levels and prevent the need for hospital treatment.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, have been traditionally used to help alleviate symptoms of jaundice.

This is due to its active compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties that may help reduce swelling and ease liver discomfort. The tea's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties may also help improve liver function and reduce bilirubin levels, a key factor in jaundice. If you're considering trying ginger tea for jaundice, start by consulting a healthcare professional for advice. They may recommend brewing 1-2 teaspoons of fresh ginger in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day. You can also add honey to make it more palatable. As you take ginger tea, you may start to notice improvements in your symptoms. The tea may help reduce nausea and vomiting, and alleviate fatigue and weakness. Your skin may also appear less yellow, and your eyes may regain their normal color.

However, if your symptoms worsen or you experience severe abdominal pain, dark urine, or pale stools, seek medical attention immediately.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent jaundice from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent jaundice in newborns.

Jaundice occurs when a baby's liver can't remove a yellow pigment from the blood. Certain herbs like milk thistle and dandelion root have properties that might support liver function.

These herbs might help the liver process the pigment and prevent jaundice from forming.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for jaundice every day?

While herbal teas can be helpful in managing jaundice symptoms, consuming them daily may not be safe.

Some teas, like dandelion root and milk thistle, can stimulate liver function and help remove bilirubin. However, overusing these teas can cause liver irritation and other issues.

It's best to use them in moderation and under close observation.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in jaundice?

Herbal teas for jaundice can take a few days to a week to show any changes.

Some people may notice a slight improvement in their condition within 24 to 48 hours, but it's more common to see noticeable results after 3 to 5 days.

The exact timeframe depends on the individual and the severity of the jaundice.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for jaundice?

Drinking herbal tea for jaundice is best in the morning or early afternoon.

This allows the body to process the tea's natural ingredients and help remove excess bilirubin throughout the day.

Drinking too close to bedtime may make it harder to fall asleep, as some teas can be stimulating.

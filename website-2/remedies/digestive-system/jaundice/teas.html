1. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum teas contains silymarin, a group of bioactive compounds that include flavonoids, lignans, and polyphenols.

These compounds help protect the liver from damage and promote its natural detoxification processes. Silymarin also has antioxidant properties, which help neutralize free radicals that can contribute to liver inflammation and damage. The flavonoids in silymarin, particularly silibinin, have been shown to improve liver function and reduce levels of bilirubin, a pigment that builds up in the blood during jaundice.

By reducing bilirubin levels and promoting liver health, Silybum marianum teas may be beneficial for individuals with jaundice.

  1. Get 1 cup of dried Silybum marianum flowers. You can buy them at a health food store or online.
  2. Use 1 tablespoon of dried flowers for every 1 cup of boiling water.
  3. Heat 1 cup of water in a pot and bring it to a boil.
  4. Add the dried flowers to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day to help with jaundice symptoms.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The flavonoids in Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, such as quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, which help reduce liver inflammation and improve liver function. By reducing liver inflammation and improving bile flow, Glycyrrhiza glabra teas can help alleviate the symptoms of jaundice, such as yellowing of the skin and eyes, and improve overall liver health.

  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, as needed, to help alleviate jaundice symptoms.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent jaundice from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent jaundice in newborns.

Jaundice occurs when a baby's liver can't remove a yellow pigment from the blood. Certain herbs like milk thistle and dandelion root have properties that might support liver function.

These herbs might help the liver process the pigment and prevent jaundice from forming.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for jaundice every day?

While herbal teas can be helpful in managing jaundice symptoms, consuming them daily may not be safe.

Some teas, like dandelion root and milk thistle, can stimulate liver function and help remove bilirubin. However, overusing these teas can cause liver irritation and other issues.

It's best to use them in moderation and under close observation.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in jaundice?

Herbal teas for jaundice can take a few days to a week to show any changes.

Some people may notice a slight improvement in their condition within 24 to 48 hours, but it's more common to see noticeable results after 3 to 5 days.

The exact timeframe depends on the individual and the severity of the jaundice.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for jaundice?

Drinking herbal tea for jaundice is best in the morning or early afternoon.

This allows the body to process the tea's natural ingredients and help remove excess bilirubin throughout the day.

Drinking too close to bedtime may make it harder to fall asleep, as some teas can be stimulating.

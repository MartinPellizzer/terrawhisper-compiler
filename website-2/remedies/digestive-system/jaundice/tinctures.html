1. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum tinctures contains silymarin, a mixture of flavonoids and lignans.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help protect the liver from damage. Silymarin also has a liver-protecting effect, improving liver function and reducing bilirubin levels. This is especially beneficial for people suffering from jaundice, a condition characterized by high bilirubin levels.

By reducing bilirubin levels, Silybum marianum tinctures can help alleviate symptoms associated with jaundice.

  1. Gather 250g of dried Silybum marianum flowers and leaves. Clean a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Fill the jar with 250ml of 80% Vodka or 95% Ethanol. Leave 2-3 cm of space at the top.
  3. Add the dried Silybum marianum flowers and leaves to the jar. Make sure they are fully covered by the liquid.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle. Discard the solids. The tincture is ready to use. Store it in a cool, dark place.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa tinctures contains curcumin as its main bioactive constituent.

Curcumin has potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce liver inflammation and oxidative stress associated with jaundice. Turmeric's bioactive compounds, including curcuminoids and polyphenols, also exhibit strong hepatoprotective effects, promoting liver cell health and function. Curcuma longa tinctures have been traditionally used to treat liver and gallbladder disorders, including jaundice, due to its ability to enhance bile production and improve liver function.

Regular use of curcuma longa tinctures may help alleviate symptoms of jaundice by reducing bilirubin levels and improving liver enzyme function.

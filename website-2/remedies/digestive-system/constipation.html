Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare, also known as fennel, has been used for centuries to help with constipation. The seeds of the fennel plant contain a compound called anethole, which has natural laxative properties. This means it helps soften and move stool through the digestive system, making it easier to pass. When consumed, the anethole in fennel seeds relaxes the muscles in the intestines, allowing waste to pass more smoothly. Fennel also helps stimulate digestion and can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, which can contribute to constipation. Drinking fennel tea or eating fennel seeds can help ease constipation and promote regular bowel movements.

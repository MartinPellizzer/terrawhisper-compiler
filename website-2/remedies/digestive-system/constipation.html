What Are The Main Causes Of Constipation?

Constipation occurs when bowel movements become less frequent or difficult to pass.

One cause of constipation is Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS). IBS is a condition where the muscles in the bowel contract too strongly, which slows down the movement of food through the digestive system. This can lead to hard, dry stools that are painful to pass. A second cause of constipation is a Low Fiber Diet. Fiber is a type of carbohydrate that helps move food through the digestive system and keeps the bowel regular.

When we don't eat enough fiber-rich foods, like fruits, vegetables, and whole grains, the stool becomes harder and more difficult to pass. This can lead to constipation. Dehydration is also a cause of constipation. When we don't drink enough water, the body pulls water from the stool to maintain other bodily functions. This makes the stool harder and drier, which can cause constipation.

Dehydration can also slow down the movement of food through the digestive system, making constipation worse.

Below are the main causes of constipation.

  • Irritable Bowel Syndrome: Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) can cause constipation because the muscles in the digestive system don't work properly, leading to slow movement of food through the intestines and difficulty in passing stools.
  • Low Fiber Diet: A Low Fiber Diet is a cause of constipation because it lacks the fiber that helps food move through the digestive system, making it harder for waste to pass out of the body.
  • Dehydration: When the body is dehydrated, the colon (large intestine) pulls more water from the waste to prevent dehydration, resulting in harder, drier stools that are difficult to pass, leading to constipation.
  • Pregnancy: During pregnancy, the growing baby puts pressure on the intestines, slowing down bowel movements and making it harder for the body to pass waste, leading to constipation.
  • Diabetes: Diabetes can cause constipation because the high blood sugar levels can damage the nerves controlling the muscles in the intestines, leading to slower movement of food through the digestive system and harder stools.
  • Hypothyroidism: Hypothyroidism is a cause of constipation because the thyroid hormone helps regulate bowel movements and muscle contractions in the digestive system, and when thyroid hormone levels are low, this process slows down, leading to infrequent and hard stools.

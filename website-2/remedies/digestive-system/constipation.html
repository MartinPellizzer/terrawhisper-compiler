Constipation Prevention and Treatment with Herbal Preparations

What are the causes of constipation?

Let's break down the main causes of constipation.

Dehydration is a big one - when your body doesn't have enough water, your bowels can't move waste out of your body properly. This is because water helps soften stool, making it easier to pass. Low fiber intake is another common cause, as fiber helps move food through your digestive system. If you're not eating enough fruits, vegetables, and whole grains, your stool can become hard and dry, making it harder to have a bowel movement.

Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) is also a culprit - people with IBS often experience stomach cramps, bloating, and changes in bowel movements, including constipation. Diabetes can cause constipation too, especially if blood sugar levels are not well-controlled. High blood sugar can lead to increased glucose levels in the colon, which can slow down the movement of waste. Lastly, hypothyroidism, a condition where the thyroid gland doesn't produce enough hormones, can also cause constipation.

The decrease in thyroid hormones can slow down digestion and bowel movements, leading to constipation.

Some herbs have anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce swelling and irritation in the digestive tract, making it easier for food to pass through. Some herbs also help increase the amount of water in the stool, making it softer and easier to pass. Additionally, some herbs can help calm the nervous system, which can help reduce stress and anxiety that can contribute to constipation.

This can make it easier to have regular bowel movements and feel more comfortable.

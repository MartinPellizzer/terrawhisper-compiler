9 herbal essential oils for pregnant women with heartburn

Pregnant women often experience heartburn due to the growing uterus putting pressure on the stomach, and the relaxation of the lower esophageal sphincter.

This can cause stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus, leading to a burning sensation in the chest and throat. Heartburn can be uncomfortable and painful, and it can also disrupt a woman's daily life. It can make eating and drinking difficult, and it can even wake her up at night. This can lead to stress and anxiety, which can be especially concerning for pregnant women who are trying to take care of themselves and their unborn babies. As heartburn symptoms worsen, pregnant women may find themselves avoiding certain foods and activities to try and manage their symptoms. They may need to eat smaller, more frequent meals, and avoid lying down after eating.

They may also need to try over-the-counter antacids or prescription medications to help relieve their symptoms. However, some pregnant women may be hesitant to take medications, especially if they are unsure about their safety during pregnancy. Herbal essential oils can be a natural and potentially safer alternative for pregnant women to help manage their heartburn symptoms. These oils are typically derived from plants and are highly concentrated, so they can be very potent. They can be inhaled through steam inhalation, added to bath water, or applied topically to the skin (after proper dilution). Some essential oils have anti-inflammatory and relaxing properties that may help soothe the digestive system and reduce heartburn symptoms.

By inhaling or applying these oils, pregnant women may be able to find some relief from their heartburn symptoms and improve their overall comfort during pregnancy.

When inhaled or ingested in small amounts, ginger essential oil can help relax the lower esophageal sphincter, preventing stomach acid from flowing back up into the esophagus. To use ginger essential oil for heartburn, start by inhaling a few drops of the oil mixed with a carrier oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil, on your wrist or chest.

You can also add a few drops to a warm bath or massage the oil onto your abdomen in a gentle, clockwise motion. When you inhale or ingest ginger essential oil, you may experience a reduction in heartburn symptoms, such as burning sensations and discomfort in the chest and throat.

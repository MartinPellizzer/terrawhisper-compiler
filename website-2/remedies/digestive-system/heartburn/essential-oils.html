1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, specifically glycyrrhizin, which is a key bioactive constituent that helps alleviate heartburn symptoms.

Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce inflammation in the esophagus and stomach, preventing irritation and damage caused by stomach acid. The essential oil's demulcent properties create a protective barrier on the mucous membranes, preventing acid from coming into contact with them and reducing discomfort and pain. Additionally, glycyrrhizin helps relax the muscles in the digestive tract, preventing spasms and further irritation.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the mucous membranes, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils helps provide relief from heartburn and related digestive issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots, a glass jar, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Fill the glass jar with the dried roots, leaving 1 inch of space at the top.
  3. Pour 2 cups of carrier oil over the roots, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Place the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container. Discard the roots and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains bioactive constituents like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation in the esophagus and stomach, which can contribute to heartburn. Gingerol and shogaol also have a soothing effect on the digestive system, relaxing the muscles in the esophagus and reducing the likelihood of stomach acid flowing back up into the esophagus. Additionally, the essential oils in Zingiber officinale have a carminative effect, helping to alleviate gas and bloating that can exacerbate heartburn symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation in the digestive system, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help to alleviate heartburn symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Ginger roots, 2 cups of water, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Cut the Ginger roots into small pieces and place them in a saucepan with 2 cups of water. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes.
  3. Let the mixture cool, then strain it through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the glass jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of vodka or glycerin to the liquid to help preserve it. Stir well.
  5. Store the Zingiber officinale essential oil in a cool, dark place and use it as needed to relieve heartburn symptoms.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains compounds like anethole, limonene, and fenchone, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help soothe the digestive tract and reduce inflammation in the esophagus, providing relief from heartburn. The essential oil's carminative properties also help ease digestion, reducing the risk of acid reflux. Anethole, in particular, has been shown to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, preventing stomach acid from flowing back up into the esophagus.

By reducing inflammation and easing digestion, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils may offer a natural solution for people experiencing heartburn.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil like coconut oil or olive oil.
  2. Combine the seeds and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well to mix.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean jar. Discard the seeds.
  5. Bottle the Foeniculum vulgare essential oil and use it as needed to help with heartburn. Start with a small amount (5-7 drops) and mix with water before consumption.

4. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains compounds like cinnamaldehyde and linalool.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help to soothe and calm the digestive tract. Cinnamaldehyde, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria that can contribute to heartburn, such as Helicobacter pylori. Linalool, on the other hand, has a calming effect on the muscles in the digestive tract, helping to reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of heartburn.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy balance of gut bacteria, Cinnamomum verum essential oils can help to alleviate heartburn symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Cinnamomum verum bark, 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil), and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Dry the Cinnamomum verum bark in a low oven (150°F) for 2 hours to remove moisture.
  3. Combine the dried bark and carrier oil in the glass jar, making sure the bark is fully covered by oil.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily to help the oil extract from the bark.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle, discard the solids and store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

5. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains curcumenol and demethoxycurcumenol as its bioactive constituents, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help reduce inflammation in the esophagus and alleviate symptoms of heartburn. The essential oils' antioxidant properties also protect the mucous membranes in the esophagus from damage caused by free radicals. Additionally, curcumenol has been shown to relax the lower esophageal sphincter, reducing acid reflux and heartburn symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the muscles, Curcuma longa essential oils helps alleviate heartburn symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Curcuma longa roots and wash them with water.
  2. Dry the roots in a warm place for 3 days. Then, chop them into small pieces.
  3. Place 1 cup of chopped roots in a cup of carrier oil (like coconut oil) in a clean glass jar.
  4. Seal the jar and put it in a warm, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking it every day.
  5. After 2 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth into another clean glass bottle. Discard the solids.

6. Coriandrum sativum

Coriandrum sativum essential oils contains the bioactive constituents linalool and geraniol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling in the esophagus and alleviate heartburn symptoms. The antispasmodic properties of linalool can also relax the muscles in the esophagus, preventing acid reflux and heartburn.   2. Use a clean glass jar and add 1 cup of Coriandrum sativum seeds to it. Pour in 2 cups of carrier oil.
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.

  5. Store the Coriandrum sativum essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 5-7 drops in water for heartburn relief.

8. Cuminum cyminum

Cuminum cyminum essential oils contains a compound called cuminaldehyde, which is responsible for its therapeutic properties.

Cuminaldehyde has anti-inflammatory and carminative properties, which help to reduce inflammation and ease digestive discomfort. It also contains limonene, a compound that helps to relax the muscles in the digestive tract and improve digestion. 9. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are responsible for its calming and relaxing properties.

These properties can help to reduce stress and anxiety, which are often triggers for heartburn. Linalool also has a soothing effect on the digestive system, helping to calm inflammation and reduce spasms in the esophagus. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent heartburn from forming?

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for heartburn every day?

Herbal teas for heartburn can be a good option, but drinking them every day can have mixed effects. Some teas like peppermint or chamomile can help soothe the stomach, while others like licorice root might not be suitable for daily use due to its potential to worsen heartburn symptoms over time.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in heartburn?

Herbal teas can help with heartburn, but it's not an instant fix.

You might notice some relief within a few hours, but it can take a few days for your body to fully adjust.

Some people see improvements in a day or two, while others may need a week or more to feel the effects.

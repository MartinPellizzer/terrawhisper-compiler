8 Herbal Tinctures For Heartburn

Herbal tinctures can be a natural and effective way to relieve heartburn.

Heartburn happens when stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus, causing a burning sensation. Herbal teas can help by soothing the digestive system and reducing inflammation. For example, ginger (Zingiber officinale) has anti-inflammatory properties that can calm the stomach and reduce nausea.

Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) has been shown to protect the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, preventing stomach acid from irritating the esophagus. Peppermint (Mentha x piperita) can help relax the muscles in the digestive tract, allowing food to move through the system more smoothly. By reducing inflammation and protecting the digestive tract, these herbs can provide relief from heartburn and make it easier to digest food.

Drinking herbal teas that contain these herbs can be a simple and enjoyable way to manage heartburn symptoms, and can even help prevent them from happening in the first place.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains active constituents like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, making it less likely to cause irritation and heartburn. Ginger's spasmolytic properties also help to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, preventing acid reflux and heartburn. Additionally, ginger has natural antacid properties, helping to neutralize stomach acid and provide quick relief.

The anti-inflammatory and antacid properties of Zingiber officinale tinctures work together to alleviate heartburn symptoms and promote a healthier digestive system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots.
  2. Combine 1 cup of ginger roots with 4 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, as needed for heartburn relief.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin that has anti-inflammatory properties.

Glycyrrhizin helps reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and soothes stomach ulcers, which can cause heartburn. The tannins in Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures also have a calming effect on the stomach muscles, preventing them from contracting too strongly and allowing for proper digestion. The flavonoids present in the tincture, such as kaempferol and quercetin, have antioxidant properties that help protect the stomach lining from damage.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the stomach lining, Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures can provide relief from heartburn symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root and 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Fill the jar with the dried root, leaving 1 inch of space at the top.
  3. Pour the vodka over the root, making sure the root is fully covered.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar, discarding the solids. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

3. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita tinctures contains peppermint oil, which has been used for centuries to soothe digestive issues, including heartburn.

The main bioactive constituents of peppermint oil are menthol and menthone, which work together to relax the muscles in the digestive tract. Menthol helps to reduce inflammation and ease spasms in the stomach, while menthone has natural antacid properties that neutralize stomach acid. When taken as a tincture, peppermint oil can help to calm the stomach lining, reducing the burning sensation associated with heartburn.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the stomach muscles, peppermint oil helps to alleviate heartburn symptoms naturally.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita (peppermint) leaves and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the peppermint leaves to the jar and cover them with the vodka. Make sure the leaves are completely covered.
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, as needed to help with heartburn.

Can drinking herbal tea prevent heartburn from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent heartburn.

Regular consumption might provide some relief, but results can vary from person to person.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for heartburn every day?

Herbal teas for heartburn can be a good option, but it's best to use them in moderation.

Drinking them every day can be safe, but it's essential to choose teas that don't irritate your stomach.

Some herbal teas, like chamomile and peppermint, are gentle and might even help soothe heartburn.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in heartburn?

Herbal teas like peppermint, ginger, and licorice root can help with heartburn.

It's hard to predict exactly when you'll notice a difference, but some people start feeling better within a few hours. Others may not see improvement until after a few days or a week of regular consumption.

Results can vary from person to person.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for heartburn?

Drinking herbal tea for heartburn is best in the morning or late afternoon.

These times work well because you're less likely to eat a big meal afterwards, which can trigger heartburn.

Avoid drinking herbal tea right before bedtime, as it may interfere with a good night's sleep.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains licorice root, which has been used for centuries to help alleviate heartburn symptoms.

The tea is rich in glycyrrhizin, a bioactive compound that has anti-inflammatory properties. Glycyrrhizin works by increasing the production of mucus in the stomach, which helps to protect the esophagus from acid damage. Additionally, glycyrrhizin also helps to relax the muscles in the stomach and reduce inflammation, providing relief from heartburn symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the stomach lining, glycyrrhizin in Glycyrrhiza glabra teas helps to provide long-lasting relief from heartburn.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of water, 1 tablespoon of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, and a strainer.
  2. Boil the water in a pot and let it cool for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root to the water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup using the strainer and discard the root.
  5. Drink the tea slowly and repeat as needed to relieve heartburn symptoms.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas contains compounds like limonene and estragole, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

This helps to prevent the stomach acid from flowing back up into the esophagus, reducing heartburn symptoms.

The anti-spasmodic properties of Foeniculum vulgare also help to calm down the muscles in the digestive tract, further reducing heartburn.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Foeniculum vulgare (fennel) leaves and 2 cups of water.

4. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas contains the bioactive constituents menthol and rosmarinic acid.

  5. Drink the tea when it's lukewarm and repeat as needed for heartburn relief.

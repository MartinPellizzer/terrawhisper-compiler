1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a compound called curcumin, along with other bioactive constituents like demethoxycurcumin and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce inflammation and damage to the digestive tract caused by gastroenteritis. The antioxidant properties of curcuma longa essential oils also help neutralize free radicals that can harm the digestive system. Curcumin's ability to modulate the gut microbiome and reduce inflammation makes it effective in alleviating symptoms of gastroenteritis, such as diarrhea and abdominal pain.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy gut environment, curcuma longa essential oils can help speed up the recovery process from gastroenteritis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa roots, 2 cups of carrier oil (such as coconut or olive oil), a glass jar, and a cheesecloth.
  2. Grind the dried Curcuma longa roots into fine powder using a mortar and pestle.
  3. Combine the ground Curcuma longa powder (1 tablespoon) with the carrier oil (2 cups) in the glass jar. Stir well.
  4. Steep the mixture for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day. Then, strain the mixture through the cheesecloth into another container.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Curcuma longa essential oil in a glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops per serving, mixed with water, to help relieve gastroenteritis symptoms.

Can drinking herbal tea prevent gastroenteritis from forming?

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for gastroenteritis every day?

It's not recommended to drink herbal teas daily for gastroenteritis.

While they can help soothe symptoms, overconsumption may worsen issues like dehydration. Herbal teas are meant for occasional use. Drinking them daily can lead to an imbalance of electrolytes and fluids, potentially prolonging recovery.

Limit herbal teas to when you feel unwell, then drink water and other fluids to stay hydrated.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in gastroenteritis?

For gastroenteritis, herbal teas like peppermint and ginger can help soothe the stomach.

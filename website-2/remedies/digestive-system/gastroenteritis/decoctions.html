This could be a problem if you're already dehydrated from vomiting and diarrhea. Another is decoctions made from 'Cannabis sativa' (Marijuana), which can cause stomach upset and worsen diarrhea.

You should also avoid decoctions made from 'Valeriana officinalis' (Valerian root), as it can cause drowsiness, stomach cramps, and worsen vomiting. Additionally, decoctions made from 'Kava' (Piper methysticum) can also be a problem, as it can cause liver damage, which is a concern if you have an infection that's making your liver work harder. Decoctions made from 'Ephedra sinica' (Ma Huang) should also be avoided, as it can raise blood pressure, which could be dangerous if you're dehydrated and have a heart condition. Finally, decoctions made from 'Lobelia inflata' (Indian tobacco) can be toxic in high doses and cause more harm than good.

It's best to stick with decoctions that are gentle on the stomach and help with hydration, such as those made from 'Plantago lanceolata' (Plantain) or 'Urtica dioica' (Nettle root).

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, making it easier to recover from gastroenteritis. The bioactive constituents in ginger, such as 6-gingerol and 6-shogaol, have been shown to have a strong effect on the digestive system. They work by slowing down the movement of food through the digestive system, allowing the body to absorb the nutrients it needs.

By reducing nausea and vomiting, and by soothing the stomach, ginger tea can help alleviate the symptoms of gastroenteritis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of fresh grated Zingiber officinale (ginger root) or 1 teaspoon of dried ginger.
  2. Heat the water in a pot over medium heat. Do not boil.
  3. Add the grated ginger to the water. If using dried ginger, add it to the water.
  4. Let the mixture simmer for 5-7 minutes. Strain the liquid using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  5. Drink the tea warm or at room temperature, 2-3 times a day, as needed.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin that helps alleviate symptoms of gastroenteritis.

The anti-inflammatory properties of glycyrrhizin reduce swelling and pain in the digestive tract, making it easier to recover from the condition. Glycyrrhizin also has prebiotic properties, feeding beneficial bacteria in the gut, which helps restore the balance of gut flora and supports the healing process. The tea's antimicrobial properties, attributed to its flavonoid and phenolic acid content, help combat bacterial and viral infections that cause gastroenteritis.

By reducing inflammation, promoting gut health, and fighting infections, Glycyrrhiza glabra teas can help alleviate symptoms and support the recovery from gastroenteritis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots.
  2. Cut the roots into small pieces and put them in a clean bowl.
  3. Pour 2 cups of boiling water over the root pieces in the bowl.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain it into a cup.
  5. Add honey to taste, if needed, and drink the tea 2-3 times a day.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas contains compounds like estragole, limonene, and anethole, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation in the gut, which is a common issue in people with gastroenteritis. Foeniculum vulgare teas also contains flavonoids like kaempferol and quercetin, which have antioxidant properties that protect the gut from damage caused by free radicals. The tea's antispasmodic properties, thanks to the presence of estragole, help alleviate cramps and spasms in the digestive tract, making it easier to pass stools and relieve symptoms of gastroenteritis.

By reducing inflammation and alleviating cramps, Foeniculum vulgare teas can help soothe the digestive system and promote healing in people with gastroenteritis.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Foeniculum vulgare (Fennel seeds) and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Add the Fennel seeds to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove the seeds.

  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help alleviate symptoms of gastroenteritis.

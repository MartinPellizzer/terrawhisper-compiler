1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains gingerols and shogaols, compounds that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and fight bacteria in the mouth, which can cause a sour taste. Gingerols and shogaols also have antioxidant properties that neutralize free radicals and protect the mouth's mucous membranes from damage. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Zingiber officinale creams help soothe and calm the mouth, reducing irritation and discomfort that can lead to a sour taste.

Regular use of Zingiber officinale creams can help balance the pH levels in the mouth and promote a healthy oral environment.

  1. Gather 2 cups of water in a saucepan and add 1/4 cup of grated fresh ginger (Zingiber officinale) root. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat to low and let the mixture simmer for 5 minutes. This helps release the ginger's medicinal properties.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the ginger solids.
  4. Combine 1 cup of coconut oil or other oil with 1/2 cup of beeswax in a separate saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat until it's fully melted.
  5. Mix the ginger-infused water with the melted oil and beeswax mixture in a small bowl. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify before use.

2. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains menthol, menthone, limonene, and other bioactive compounds.

These compounds have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce the presence of bacteria in the mouth that can cause a sour taste. The anti-inflammatory properties of menthol and menthone also help to reduce inflammation in the mouth, which can contribute to a sour taste. Additionally, limonene has a deodorizing effect, which can help to neutralize volatile sulfur compounds that can cause bad breath and a sour taste.

By reducing bacteria, inflammation, and volatile sulfur compounds, Mentha x piperita creams can help to alleviate a sour taste in the mouth.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of peppermint essential oil, and a small saucepan.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in the saucepan over low heat, stirring until smooth.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 5 minutes.
  4. Add the peppermint essential oil to the mixture and stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and harden before use.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains glycyrrhizin, a compound known for its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

Glycyrrhizin helps to reduce the production of lactic acid in the mouth, which causes sour taste. The cream also contains flavonoids, such as glabrene, which have antioxidant properties that help to neutralize free radicals and reduce inflammation in the mouth. Licorice extract in the cream has a soothing effect on the mucous membranes, reducing irritation and discomfort.

Regular use of Glycyrrhiza glabra creams can help to alleviate sour taste in the mouth by reducing lactic acid production and soothing the mucous membranes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of glycerin, and 2 tablespoons of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root powder.
  2. Combine the water and glycerin in a saucepan and heat it over low heat, stirring until the glycerin dissolves.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and add the Glycyrrhiza glabra root powder. Stir well to mix.
  4. Let the mixture cool and thicken. This may take about 30 minutes.
  5. Strain the mixture into a container and add 2 tablespoons of your preferred cream base (like coconut oil or shea butter). Mix well and apply as needed to soothe a sour taste in the mouth.

4. Echinacea purpurea

Alkylamides also have antioxidant properties that protect the mouth tissues from damage, reducing irritation and discomfort. By reducing inflammation, protecting tissues, and fighting off infections, Echinacea purpurea creams may help alleviate a sour taste in the mouth.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Combine the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and coconut oil in a double boiler. Heat the mixture over low heat for 2-3 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture to remove the Echinacea purpurea flowers. Add the beeswax and vitamin E oil to the mixture. Heat until the beeswax is melted.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool. Pour the mixture into small containers. Let it cool and harden completely.
  5. Once hardened, your Echinacea purpurea cream is ready to use. Apply it to the affected area to help soothe and alleviate sour taste in mouth.

5. Eucalyptus globulus

By reducing bacterial growth and inflammation, eucalyptus globulus creams can help alleviate a sour taste in the mouth.

  3. Remove the melted mixture from heat and add the eucalyptus globulus essential oil and vitamin E oil. Stir well.

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and camphor as its active constituents.

  3. Strain the mixture into a bowl, discarding the flowers. Add 1 tablespoon of beeswax and 1/2 cup of coconut oil to the bowl.

1. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene and geranial as its active constituents.

These compounds have antibacterial properties that help control the growth of bacteria in the mouth, which can cause a sour taste. Limonene also has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling in the gums and mouth, making it easier for saliva to neutralize acids and eliminate the sour taste. Geranial has antiseptic properties that help prevent the spread of infection, promoting a healthy mouth environment.

By controlling bacteria and inflammation, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils helps alleviate a sour taste in the mouth.

  1. Get 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves.
  2. Wash the leaves with 1 cup of water and dry them.
  3. Use a steam distiller to extract oil from the leaves. Heat 1 cup of water and mix it with the leaves.
  4. Collect the oil that separates from the water and store it in a dark glass bottle.
  5. Add 10 drops of the extracted oil to a glass of water or use it as needed to get rid of the sour taste in your mouth.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains gingerol and shogaol as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that help to reduce the production of saliva and other fluids that cause a sour taste in the mouth. Gingerol also has natural antacid properties that can help to neutralize stomach acid and reduce heartburn, which can contribute to a sour taste. Shogaol, on the other hand, has antioxidant properties that can help to protect the mouth and throat from oxidative stress, which can cause irritation and discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy oral environment, Zingiber officinale essential oils may help to alleviate a sour taste in the mouth.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh ginger roots and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil)
  2. Wash the ginger roots and peel them. Cut them into small pieces.
  3. In a saucepan, combine the ginger pieces and carrier oil. Heat it on low for 2 hours, or until the oil turns golden.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl. Discard the ginger pieces.
  5. Store the Zingiber officinale essential oil in a dark glass bottle. Use it within 6 months.

3. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains menthol and menthone as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce the presence of bacteria in the mouth, which can cause a sour taste. Menthol also has a numbing effect that can temporarily mask the sensation of sourness. Additionally, menthone has a cleansing effect that can help to remove bacteria and other debris that may be contributing to the sour taste.

By reducing the presence of bacteria and other irritants, Mentha x piperita essential oils can help to alleviate a sour taste in the mouth.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and 2 cups of water in a pot.
  2. Heat the water in the pot until it boils, then reduce heat to low.
  3. Add the Mentha x piperita leaves to the pot and let it steep for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl.
  5. Let the liquid sit at room temperature for 2-3 days to allow the essential oil to separate and rise to the top.

4. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains compounds like anethole and fenchone as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial properties that help reduce the growth of bacteria in the mouth, which can cause a sour taste. Anethole also has anti-inflammatory properties that may help soothe mouth irritations and reduce swelling. The antiseptic properties of fenchone and anethole help eliminate germs and bacteria that can contribute to a sour taste in the mouth.

By reducing bacteria and inflammation, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help alleviate a sour taste in the mouth.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil (such as jojoba oil).
  2. Combine the Foeniculum vulgare seeds and carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Foeniculum vulgare essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, particularly glycyrrhizin, as its bioactive constituent.

This compound has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce swelling and protect the oral mucosa. Glycyrrhizin also has a sweet and soothing effect on the mouth, counteracting the sour taste caused by acid reflux or other digestive issues. Additionally, glycyrrhizin has antibacterial properties, which can help to prevent the growth of bacteria that can cause bad breath and an unpleasant mouth odor.

By using Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils, you can help to alleviate a sour taste in the mouth due to its sweetening, anti-inflammatory, and antibacterial properties.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Grind the roots into small pieces and mix with the carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the extracted essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

6. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole, which is a bioactive constituent that helps reduce the sensation of a sour taste in the mouth.

Cineole has anti-inflammatory properties that soothe and calm the mouth tissues, reducing irritation and discomfort. It also has a refreshing and decongestant effect that helps to neutralize the sour taste and leave the mouth feeling clean. The antimicrobial properties of cineole help to kill bacteria in the mouth that can contribute to a sour taste.

By reducing inflammation and killing bacteria, cineole helps to eliminate the source of the sour taste and leave the mouth feeling fresh and clean.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water.
  2. Combine the leaves and water in a medium saucepan and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes, then let it cool.
  4. Using cheesecloth or a coffee filter, strain the mixture into a clean glass container.
  5. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use 5-7 drops to help with bad breath or sour taste in mouth.

7. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains a compound called carvacrol, which has antimicrobial properties that help fight bacteria in the mouth.

Carvacrol and another compound, borneol, help reduce the growth of bacteria that can cause bad breath and an unpleasant sour taste. The antimicrobial and antifungal properties of carvacrol and borneol work together to prevent the buildup of bacteria that can lead to halitosis and a sour mouth sensation. Additionally, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contain a compound called camphor, which has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe mouth irritation and inflammation.

By using Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils, you can help create an environment in your mouth that is less conducive to bacterial growth and more conducive to a healthy, fresh taste.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water.
  2. Place the leaves in a saucepan and add the water. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a glass jar with a tight-fitting lid. Store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks to allow the mixture to mature.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth again. Discard the solids and transfer the remaining liquid to a small glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops of the essential oil in a glass of water to help with a sour taste in mouth.

8. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains a compound called linalool, which is known for its antibacterial properties.

This helps to reduce the amount of bacteria in the mouth, which can cause a sour taste. The essential oil also contains linalyl acetate, a compound that has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe and calm the mouth. The antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds help to kill bacteria and reduce swelling in the mouth, which can contribute to a sour taste.

By reducing the bacterial load and inflammation in the mouth, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to alleviate a sour taste.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  2. Combine the flowers with 2 cups of water in a saucepan and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let simmer for 30 minutes, then let cool.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discard the solids.
  5. Store the resulting liquid in a dark glass bottle and use 5 drops in water for a mouthwash to help soothe a sour taste.

9. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains a compound called cinnamaldehyde, which is known for its ability to reduce the sensation of sourness in the mouth.

This is because cinnamaldehyde has antimicrobial properties that help to kill bacteria that can cause bad breath and a sour taste. The oil also contains eugenol, which has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to soothe and calm the mouth and throat. Additionally, cinnamaldehyde has been shown to inhibit the growth of certain bacteria that can contribute to a sour taste.

By reducing the presence of these bacteria and soothing the mouth and throat, Cinnamomum verum essential oils can help to alleviate a sour taste in the mouth.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Cinnamomum verum bark, a cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Dry the Cinnamomum verum bark in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 2 hours.
  3. Grind the dried bark into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  4. Combine 2 tablespoons of the ground bark with a cup of carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well.
  5. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily. Strain and discard the solids to get the essential oil.

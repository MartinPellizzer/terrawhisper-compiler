8 Herbal Teas to Combat Overeating in Overweight Teens

When you're a teen, it can be tough to manage your weight.

You might eat too much for many reasons - stress, boredom, or even just because you love food. But overeating can lead to being overweight, and that can cause big problems. Your joints might ache, you might get tired easily, and you might feel self-conscious about your body. You might even get bullied or feel sad about yourself. It's a difficult place to be. Overeating can also affect your mood and energy levels. You might feel sluggish and tired all the time, or get moody and irritable.

This can make it hard to focus in school or enjoy activities with friends. It's like having a weight (no pun intended) on your shoulders, and it's not easy to shake off. But there are some things that can help. Herbal teas are a great option for teens who struggle with overeating. They're natural, gentle, and easy to use. Drinking a cup of herbal tea after meals can help calm your appetite and reduce cravings for more food. It's like a gentle nudge to say, "Okay, you're full now. That's enough.".

And because herbal teas are so soothing, they can also help with stress and anxiety, which are often linked to overeating.

1. Gymnema sylvestre

Gymnema sylvestre teas can help with overeating by reducing sugar cravings and improving insulin sensitivity.

The tea contains compounds like gymnemic acid and gymnemagenin, which may block the taste receptors on the tongue, making sugar taste bitter, and also slow down the absorption of sugar in the gut. This can lead to a decrease in sugar cravings, making it easier to stick to a healthy diet. To get started, try drinking 1-2 cups of Gymnema sylvestre tea per day, ideally before meals. You can steep 1 teaspoon of dried leaves in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Some people also combine Gymnema sylvestre tea with other herbs like berberine or chromium to enhance its effects.

However, consult with a healthcare professional before making any changes to your diet or supplement routine. Drinking Gymnema sylvestre tea regularly may lead to a reduction in sugar cravings and an improvement in blood sugar control. You might notice that you're eating smaller portions and choosing healthier foods more often. However, results may vary, and it's essential to combine the tea with a balanced diet and regular exercise for sustainable weight loss. You may also experience improved digestion and a decrease in hunger pangs.

Be patient and consistent, as it may take a few weeks to notice noticeable changes.

2. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas have been used for centuries to aid in weight loss, particularly for those who struggle with overeating.

The properties and constituents of Ginkgo biloba, such as flavonoids and terpenoids, help to regulate blood sugar levels and improve insulin sensitivity. This, in turn, reduces cravings for carbohydrates and sweets, making it easier to stick to a healthy diet. Additionally, Ginkgo biloba's antioxidant properties help to reduce inflammation in the body, which is often linked to overeating and poor eating habits. If you're looking to use Ginkgo biloba teas to help with overeating, here's what you can do: start by drinking one to two cups of Ginkgo biloba tea per day, ideally before meals to help reduce hunger.

You can also add other herbs like peppermint or ginger to enhance the tea's flavor and benefits. It's essential to note that Ginkgo biloba teas may interact with certain medications, so consult with a healthcare professional before starting your regimen. When you start drinking Ginkgo biloba teas, you may notice a decrease in your appetite and cravings for unhealthy foods. You may also experience improved digestion and a sense of fullness, making it easier to stick to your diet. Some people may also notice a reduction in their stress levels, which can contribute to overeating.

However, results may vary, and it's essential to combine Ginkgo biloba teas with a healthy diet and regular exercise for optimal weight loss.

3. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis teas, particularly green tea and black tea, can help with overeating due to their high content of catechins, specifically EGCG (epigallocatechin gallate).

These compounds have been shown to slow down the digestion of fat, reducing the absorption of calories. This is because catechins can bind to the gut, slowing down the movement of food and the release of hormones that stimulate appetite. Additionally, Camellia sinensis teas have been found to increase the production of AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK), which helps regulate energy balance and reduce fat storage. If you're struggling with overeating, try drinking a cup of brewed Camellia sinensis tea after meals. Start with a small amount, such as 1/4 cup, and gradually increase the amount as you become accustomed to the taste. You can also add a squeeze of lemon juice to enhance the flavor.

To get the most out of the tea, brew it for 2-3 minutes to release the optimal amount of catechins. As you drink the tea regularly, you may notice a decrease in your appetite and a reduction in your overall calorie intake. You may also experience weight loss and improved digestion. As you incorporate Camellia sinensis teas into your diet, you can expect to see improvements in your eating habits and overall health. You may find that you're able to stop eating when you're satisfied, rather than stuffed, and that you're more mindful of your food choices. Regular consumption of Camellia sinensis teas may also lead to a reduction in body fat and an improvement in your metabolism.

However, it's essential to remember that Camellia sinensis teas are not a quick fix for overeating, but rather a tool to support your healthy eating habits and lifestyle choices.

4. Ilex paraguariensis

Ilex paraguariensis teas, also known as yerba mate, have been traditionally used to help with overeating.

The tea contains a group of compounds called xanthines, which include caffeine, theobromine, and theophylline. These xanthines help to slow down the digestion of food and reduce hunger by increasing the production of stomach acid and digestive enzymes. They also help to increase the levels of hormones that make you feel full, such as cholecystokinin and gastrin. To use Ilex paraguariensis teas for overeating, start by drinking a cup of brewed tea 30 minutes before meals. You can also add a small amount of honey or lemon to make it more palatable.

Begin with a small amount, such as 1-2 cups per day, and gradually increase the dose as needed. Taking Ilex paraguariensis teas may lead to a reduction in food intake and a decrease in weight. You may also experience improved digestion and reduced bloating. Additionally, the xanthines in the tea may help to increase your metabolism and energy levels, making it easier to engage in physical activity. However, it's essential to note that Ilex paraguariensis teas may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications. It's also possible that the tea may cause side effects like jitteriness, anxiety, and insomnia in some individuals.

If you're considering using Ilex paraguariensis teas for overeating, consult with a healthcare professional to determine the best course of action for your specific needs.

5. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum teas, also known as milk thistle tea, has been traditionally used to help with overeating.

The reason it's effective is due to its active compounds, particularly silymarin and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. When consumed as a tea, Silybum marianum helps to regulate appetite, reduce cravings for unhealthy foods, and promote feelings of fullness, making it easier to stick to a balanced diet. If you're struggling with overeating, consider incorporating Silybum marianum tea into your daily routine. To get started, steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried Silybum marianum leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Drink the tea 1-2 times a day, ideally before meals to help control your appetite. Additionally, make sure to maintain a healthy diet and exercise regularly to support your weight loss journey. As you take action and incorporate Silybum marianum tea into your routine, you may start to notice improvements in your digestion, reduced bloating, and increased energy levels. You may also find it easier to manage your food portions and make healthier choices. However, it's essential to remember that Silybum marianum tea is not a magic solution and should be used in conjunction with a balanced diet and regular exercise.

Be patient and consistent, and you may find that it helps you develop healthier habits and achieve your weight loss goals.

6. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis teas are a natural remedy that can help with overeating by regulating appetite and metabolism.

It also contains alkaloids like cinchonine and cinchonidine, which help to slow down the release of glucose in the bloodstream, thereby reducing cravings for sweets and other high-calorie foods. When taken in moderation, Cinchona officinalis teas can help to reduce bloating and discomfort after overeating. If you're looking to use Cinchona officinalis teas for overeating, here's what you can do: start by drinking a cup of tea made from dried Cinchona bark after meals or before bedtime. Begin with a small dose of 1-2 teaspoons of dried bark per cup of boiling water and adjust to taste.

You can also combine it with other herbs like peppermint or ginger to help with digestion. As you take the tea, pay attention to your body's response and adjust your portion sizes accordingly. As you drink Cinchona officinalis teas regularly, you may notice a decrease in your appetite and a reduction in overeating. You may also experience improved digestion, reduced bloating, and a feeling of fullness for longer periods. Additionally, the quinine in the tea may help to improve insulin sensitivity, which can lead to a reduction in blood sugar levels and a decrease in the risk of developing insulin resistance and other related health problems.

However, it's essential to note that Cinchona officinalis teas should not be used as a replacement for a healthy diet and regular exercise.

7. Smallanthus sonchifolius

Smallanthus sonchifolius teas are a herbal remedy that can help with overeating due to their unique properties and constituents.

The plant, also known as yacon tea, contains fructooligosaccharides (FOS), which are non-digestible fibers that pass through the digestive system without being broken down. This means that FOS can help reduce the amount of calories absorbed from food, making it a helpful tool for managing weight. When consumed as a tea, the FOS in Smallanthus sonchifolius can also help slow down digestion, reducing the urge to overeat. To take action, start by drinking 1-2 cups of Smallanthus sonchifolius tea per day, ideally 30 minutes before meals.

You can steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried yacon root in a cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes, then strain and drink. Be sure to also maintain a balanced diet and regular exercise routine to support weight management. The likely outcomes of taking action with Smallanthus sonchifolius tea include reduced appetite and calorie intake, leading to weight loss over time. You may also experience improved digestion and reduced symptoms of bloating and discomfort after meals.

As you continue to drink the tea and maintain a healthy lifestyle, you may notice a decrease in food cravings and a greater sense of control over your eating habits.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent overeating from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help with overeating by calming the mind and body.

Some teas, like peppermint and chamomile, have a soothing effect that can reduce stress and anxiety, which can sometimes lead to overeating.

By promoting relaxation, herbal tea may help you feel more in control of your eating habits.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for overeating every day?

Herbal teas can be a helpful way to manage overeating, but it's essential to choose the right ones.

Teas like peppermint and ginger can calm your stomach and reduce hunger. However, some teas, like licorice root, can worsen overeating by increasing water retention and blood pressure.

Be cautious and research each tea before consumption.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in overeating?

Herbal teas can help with overeating, but it's different for everyone.

Some people notice a change after a few days, like feeling less hungry or more full faster. Others might not see results until after a week or two.

It depends on the tea, how you drink it, and your body.

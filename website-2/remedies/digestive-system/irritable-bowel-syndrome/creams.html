10 Herbal Creams For Irritable Bowel Syndrome

Herbal creams and teas can be a helpful addition to your treatment plan if you have irritable bowel syndrome (IBS).

IBS is a condition that affects the large intestine, causing symptoms like abdominal pain, bloating, and changes in bowel movements. Herbal teas, in particular, can help to soothe and calm the digestive system, providing relief from IBS symptoms. One reason why herbal teas can be beneficial is that they contain anti-inflammatory compounds that help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. Curcuma longa, also known as turmeric, is a great example of an herb that has anti-inflammatory properties. It contains a compound called curcumin, which has been shown to reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of IBS. Another herb that can be helpful for IBS is Zingiber officinale, or ginger. Ginger has natural anti-inflammatory compounds that can help to soothe the digestive system and reduce nausea and pain.

It's also a natural antacid, which can help to neutralize stomach acid and provide relief from heartburn and acid reflux. Fennel, or Foeniculum vulgare, is another herb that can be beneficial for IBS. It has natural anti-inflammatory compounds that can help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, and its oils can help to relax the muscles in the digestive system and promote regular bowel movements. When you use herbal teas or creams to help manage your IBS symptoms, you may notice a range of benefits. You may experience reduced abdominal pain and discomfort, improved digestion, and a reduction in bloating and gas. You may also feel more confident and in control of your symptoms, which can have a big impact on your mental health and well-being. Using herbal creams and teas can also be a natural and non-invasive way to manage your IBS symptoms.

You can add them to your daily routine, along with other treatments and lifestyle changes, to help alleviate your symptoms and improve your overall quality of life.

1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains a high concentration of curcuminoids, a group of bioactive compounds that have potent anti-inflammatory properties.

The most active constituent is curcumin, a natural polyphenol that has been shown to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the gut, which are common issues associated with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). Curcumin also has a direct effect on the gut microbiome, promoting the growth of beneficial bacteria and reducing the presence of pathogenic bacteria, which can contribute to IBS symptoms. Additionally, curcumin has been found to improve gut motility and reduce inflammation in the intestinal lining, helping to alleviate symptoms such as bloating, abdominal pain, and changes in bowel movements.

Curcumin's bioavailability is enhanced in topical creams, allowing it to directly target the affected areas and provide rapid relief from IBS symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of Turmeric powder in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in the double boiler over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the Turmeric powder to the melted mixture and stir well. Continue heating for 10 minutes.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool slightly. Whip the mixture with an electric mixer until it thickens.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container, let it cool and solidify. Your Curcuma longa cream is ready to use.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains bioactive constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, a common issue in irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). Gingerols and shogaols also have spasmolytic properties, which can help relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing cramps and spasms. Additionally, the antioxidants present in Zingiber officinale creams, such as gingerol and shogaol, help protect the digestive tract from oxidative stress and damage.

By reducing inflammation and muscle spasms, Zingiber officinale creams may help alleviate symptoms of IBS, such as abdominal pain and discomfort.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water and 1 cup of coconut oil in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the coconut oil over low heat and add 1/2 cup of beeswax. Stir until melted.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of Zingiber officinale powder, 2 tablespoons of aloe vera gel, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil to the saucepan. Stir well.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 10-15 minutes. Then, whisk the mixture until it thickens.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool completely before use. Store it in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare creams contains a compound called trans-anethole, a key active constituent that has anti-inflammatory properties.

This property helps reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, a common issue for people with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). The combination of these properties makes Foeniculum vulgare creams a potentially effective treatment option for IBS symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Foeniculum vulgare seeds and 2 cups of coconut oil in a clean glass jar.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water until it reaches 120°F to 140°F.
  3. Add the dried Foeniculum vulgare seeds to the coconut oil and let it steep for 2 to 3 hours, or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass jar, discarding the seeds.
  5. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil to the strained mixture and stir until the beeswax is fully incorporated.

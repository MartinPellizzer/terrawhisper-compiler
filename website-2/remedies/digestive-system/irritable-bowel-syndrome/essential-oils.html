1. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains a compound called anethole, which is a potent anti-inflammatory agent.

This helps to reduce swelling and irritation in the digestive tract, a common issue for people with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). Anethole also has a relaxing effect on the muscles in the intestines, allowing food to pass through more easily and reducing cramping and discomfort. The essential oil also contains limonene, which has antimicrobial properties that help to kill bacteria in the gut, further reducing symptoms of IBS.

By reducing inflammation and killing off bacteria, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help to alleviate IBS symptoms and promote a healthier digestive system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Foeniculum vulgare seeds. You can buy them online or at a local herbal store.
  2. Use a coffee grinder to grind the seeds into a fine powder. You may need to do this in batches.
  3. Add 4 cups of carrier oil (such as coconut or jojoba oil) to a clean glass jar. Add 2 tablespoons of the ground Foeniculum vulgare seeds.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the oil to extract the seeds' properties.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle. Discard the solids and store the oil in a cool, dark place.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains zingiberene, bisabolol, and gingerol, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

The volatile oils in ginger help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, which can help alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). Ginger's ability to stimulate digestion and improve gut motility can also help to relieve bloating and abdominal pain associated with IBS. Additionally, ginger's antimicrobial properties can help to reduce the growth of harmful bacteria in the gut, which can contribute to IBS symptoms.

The combination of these properties makes Zingiber officinale essential oils a useful natural remedy for managing IBS symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger root and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Wash and peel the ginger root, then slice it into thin pieces.
  3. Place the ginger slices in a clean glass jar and cover them with 2 cups of carrier oil.
  4. Steep the ginger in the oil for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a new container, and discard the ginger solids.

3. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a compound called curcumin, which is also present in turmeric.

Curcumin has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, a common cause of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). The oil also contains boswellic acids, which block the production of chemicals that cause inflammation. Additionally, curcuma longa essential oils contain polyphenols that have antioxidant properties, which help protect the digestive system from damage and promote healing.

The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of curcuma longa essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of IBS such as abdominal pain and diarrhea.

  1. Gather 1 cup of turmeric roots (Curcuma longa) and clean them thoroughly.
  2. Steam distill the turmeric roots to extract the oil. You will need a distillation kit or access to a distillation machine.
  3. Collect the essential oil that separates from the distilled water. You should get about 2 tablespoons of oil.
  4. Filter the oil using a coffee filter or a paper filter to remove any impurities. You can also use a centrifuge to speed up the process.
  5. Store the Curcuma longa essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid and label it. The oil can be used for topical application or added to a carrier oil for massage.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains saponins and triterpenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). The saponins in Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils also exhibit antimicrobial activity, which can help control the growth of harmful bacteria in the gut, contributing to IBS symptoms. Additionally, the anti-spasmodic properties of the triterpenoids can help relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing cramps and spasms associated with IBS.

By reducing inflammation and controlling bacterial growth, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils may help provide relief from IBS symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 4 cups of carrier oil (such as coconut or olive oil).
  2. Grind 1 cup of the dried roots into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. In a saucepan, combine the ground roots and 2 cups of carrier oil. Heat the mixture over low heat (100°F - 150°F) for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil per capsule or apply topically as needed to help alleviate irritable bowel syndrome symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Silybum marianum essential oils can help to alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome, such as abdominal pain, bloating, and changes in bowel habits.

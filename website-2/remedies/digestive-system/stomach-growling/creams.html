1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to soothe the digestive tract and reduce inflammation in the stomach, making it a good remedy for stomach growling. Gingerol also has antispasmodic properties, which help to relax the muscles in the digestive tract and prevent cramps. Shogaol, on the other hand, has antioxidant properties that help to protect the stomach lining from damage and reduce the production of stomach acid.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the stomach lining, Zingiber officinale creams can help to alleviate stomach growling and discomfort.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of Zingiber officinale powder.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of honey and 2 tablespoons of Zingiber officinale powder to the melted mixture. Stir well.
  4. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool down. Once cooled, whip the mixture with a mixer or a whisk until light and fluffy.
  5. Transfer the cream to a jar and store it in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. Apply 1-2 tablespoons to the stomach area as needed.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains active constituents like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help soothe the stomach and reduce inflammation, making it a natural remedy for stomach growling and discomfort. The oils' warming properties also help increase blood flow to the stomach, which can help ease digestive issues. The essential oils' ability to relax the muscles in the digestive tract can also contribute to reducing stomach growling.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Zingiber officinale essential oils can provide relief from stomach discomfort.

  1. Gather materials: fresh Zingiber officinale roots, carrier oil (coconut or jojoba), glass jar, cheesecloth, and a clean glass bottle.
  2. Wash 250g of fresh Zingiber officinale roots in cold water, then chop them into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped roots with 250ml of carrier oil in a glass jar. Stir well and seal the jar.
  4. Leave the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it daily. This allows the oil to infuse into the carrier oil.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through cheesecloth into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

2. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains the bioactive constituents menthone, limonene, and beta-pinene.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe an upset stomach. The menthol in Mentha x piperita essential oils has a calming effect on the digestive system, reducing spasms and cramps that can cause stomach growling. Additionally, menthol's expectorant properties can help relieve nausea and indigestion, further alleviating stomach discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the digestive system, Mentha x piperita essential oils can help calm stomach growling and promote a sense of well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves. Clean them with water.
  2. Use a juicer or blender to extract the oil from the leaves. You will get a liquid.
  3. Transfer the liquid to a saucepan and heat it on low heat for 5-7 minutes. You will see the liquid reduce.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 10 minutes. Strain the oil into a clean container.
  5. Store the Mentha x piperita essential oil in an airtight container in the fridge. Use 5-7 drops to help with stomach growling.

3. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains a compound called cinnamaldehyde, which is a powerful anti-inflammatory agent that helps to soothe digestive issues, including stomach growling.

Cinnamaldehyde has a calming effect on the stomach, reducing inflammation and alleviating discomfort. Cinnamomum verum essential oils also contains eugenol, a natural antioxidant that helps to protect the stomach lining from damage and promote healing. The warm, spicy properties of cinnamaldehyde and eugenol work together to ease digestive discomfort and reduce stomach growling.

By applying or inhaling Cinnamomum verum essential oils, you may find relief from stomach growling due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Cinnamomum verum bark, a cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba), and a glass bottle with a tight lid.
  2. Dry the Cinnamomum verum bark in a low oven (150°F) for 2 hours to remove moisture.
  3. Grind the dried bark into fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  4. Combine 2 tablespoons of the powdered bark with 8 tablespoons of carrier oil in a small bowl. Stir well.
  5. Store the mixture in the glass bottle and let it sit for 2 weeks, shaking the bottle daily. Strain and use the oil as needed for stomach growling.

4. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains active constituents like limonene, fenchone, and anethole.

These compounds help reduce inflammation and relax the digestive system, easing stomach discomfort and growling. The anti-spasmodic properties of anethole specifically help calm cramps and spasms in the stomach, making it a natural remedy for stomach issues. Limonene has antibacterial properties, which can help fight off infections that may be contributing to stomach problems.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off infections, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils helps soothe and calm the stomach, providing relief from growling and discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Heat the carrier oil in a saucepan over low heat.
  3. Add the Foeniculum vulgare seeds to the heated oil and let it steep for 2 hours or overnight.

5. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a compound called curcumin, which is known for its anti-inflammatory properties.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy digestive environment, Curcuma longa essential oils can help alleviate stomach growling and discomfort.

  3. Add the 2 cups of dried turmeric root to the jar and make sure they are fully covered by the carrier oil.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.

6. Syzygium aromaticum

7. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains a compound called carnosic acid, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe digestive issues.

The anti-spasmodic properties in Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help relax the muscles in the stomach, reducing the frequency and severity of stomach growling. By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy gut environment, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help alleviate stomach growling and discomfort.

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which helps to soothe and calm the stomach.

This compound has anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce inflammation and irritation in the digestive tract. Glycyrrhizin also has a natural antispasmodic effect, helping to relax the muscles in the stomach and reduce cramps. By using Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils, you can potentially alleviate stomach growling and discomfort by addressing the underlying causes.

9. Coriandrum sativum

Coriandrum sativum essential oils contains linalool, geraniol, and limonene as its main bioactive constituents.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains the active constituents zingibain and shogaol.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation and relax the muscles in the stomach, which can help to calm stomach growling. The anti-inflammatory properties of zingibain and the antispasmodic properties of shogaol work together to soothe the stomach and reduce discomfort. The warming sensation caused by the gingerols in Zingiber officinale teas can also help to increase digestion and reduce bloating, which can contribute to stomach growling.

By reducing inflammation, relaxing stomach muscles, and promoting digestion, Zingiber officinale teas can help to alleviate stomach growling.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger root.
  2. Peel the ginger root and slice it into thin pieces.
  3. Boil the water in a pot and add the sliced ginger.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it when it's warm and comfortable to consume.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains compounds like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids.

Glycyrrhizin has a soothing effect on the stomach lining, reducing inflammation and irritation that can cause growling and discomfort. Flavonoids in the tea also have antioxidant properties, which help protect the stomach from free radicals and promote healing. The anti-inflammatory properties of glycyrrhizin and flavonoids may also help reduce stomach acid and prevent symptoms like heartburn and bloating.

Drinking Glycyrrhiza glabra teas regularly can help calm the stomach and reduce growling by promoting a healthy digestive environment.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, 1 cup of boiling water, and a tea infuser or strainer.
  2. Measure 1 teaspoon of dried root for every cup of water. Place the root in the tea infuser or strainer.
  3. Pour boiling water over the root and let it steep for 5-7 minutes. You can adjust the steeping time to your taste.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the root. You can add honey or lemon to taste, if needed.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm. Repeat as needed to help soothe stomach growling.

3. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas contains the bioactive constituents menthol, menthone, and limonene.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help to soothe the stomach and reduce discomfort. Menthol, in particular, helps to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, easing cramps and spasms that can cause stomach growling. Limonene has a calming effect on the nervous system, reducing anxiety and stress that can exacerbate stomach issues.

By relaxing the digestive tract and calming the nervous system, Mentha x piperita teas can help to alleviate stomach growling and discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh mint leaves (Mentha x piperita) and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of fresh mint leaves to a cup or teapot.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the mint leaves and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into another cup using a tea infuser or a fine-mesh sieve.
  5. Drink the tea warm or at room temperature to soothe your stomach.

4. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas contains a compound called anethole, which has anti-inflammatory and carminative properties.

Anethole helps to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing spasms and discomfort that can cause stomach growling. The tea also contains other bioactive constituents, such as limonene and fenchone, which have antispasmodic and antacid effects. These properties help to soothe the stomach and reduce symptoms of indigestion, making Foeniculum vulgare teas a potential remedy for stomach growling.

By calming the digestive system and reducing inflammation, Foeniculum vulgare teas may help to alleviate stomach growling and promote a sense of comfort and well-being.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Foeniculum vulgare (fennel seeds) and a cup of water.

  5. Drink the tea hot or let it cool down to room temperature, as needed.

Curcuma longa teas contains a compound called curcumin, which is its active constituent.

Silybum marianum teas contains silymarin, a complex mixture of flavonoids, including silibinin, silidianin, and silicristin.

Silymarin also has a protective effect on the gut lining, preventing damage and promoting healing.

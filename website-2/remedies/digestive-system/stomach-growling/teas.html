Stomach Growling Solutions: 10 Herbal Teas for Students

Students often have busy lives, juggling classes, assignments, and extracurricular activities, which can lead to stress and an irregular eating schedule.

This can cause stomach growling, a common issue where the stomach makes loud noises due to hunger or digestive problems. When students experience stomach growling, it can be distracting and uncomfortable, making it hard to focus in class or enjoy activities with friends. It can also lead to feelings of self-consciousness and anxiety, affecting their mental well-being and overall quality of life.

Furthermore, stomach growling can be a sign of an underlying issue, such as a nutrient deficiency or a digestive disorder, which needs to be addressed. As a result, students may feel embarrassed or ashamed, leading to missed meals or unhealthy snacking habits, which can exacerbate the problem. However, there's a simple and natural solution that can help alleviate stomach growling: herbal teas. These teas work by soothing the stomach, reducing inflammation, and calming the digestive system, providing quick relief from hunger pangs and discomfort.

They come in a variety of flavors and can be easily consumed as a hot or cold beverage, making them a convenient and accessible solution for students on-the-go.

1. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas, also known as peppermint tea, can help with stomach growling due to its properties and constituents.

The tea contains menthol, menthone, and limonene, which have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. These compounds help to relax the muscles in the stomach and reduce inflammation, which can help alleviate stomach growling and discomfort. If you're experiencing stomach growling, try drinking a cup of peppermint tea. To make a soothing cup, steep a tea bag in a cup of hot water for 5-7 minutes. You can also add honey or lemon to taste.

Take a few sips of the tea and see if it helps to calm your stomach. You can also try drinking it before meals to help stimulate digestion and prevent stomach growling. When you drink peppermint tea, you may start to feel a calming effect on your stomach within 15-30 minutes. Your stomach growling may subside, and you may feel more comfortable. The tea may also help to relieve nausea and indigestion.

However, if your stomach growling persists or is accompanied by other symptoms such as vomiting, fever, or abdominal pain, seek medical attention.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, can help with stomach growling due to its anti-inflammatory and carminative properties.

Ginger contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have natural pain-relieving and digestive properties. These compounds can help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, calm nausea and alleviate stomach discomfort. When you drink ginger tea, its active ingredients can help to stimulate digestion, relax the muscles in your stomach, and improve the movement of food through your digestive system. To try ginger tea for stomach growling, steep a teaspoon of fresh ginger in a cup of boiling water for five minutes, then strain and drink.

Start with a small amount, about 1/4 cup, and see how your body reacts. You can also add honey or lemon to taste. If you experience any discomfort or heartburn, reduce the amount or stop drinking ginger tea. Likely outcomes from taking ginger tea for stomach growling include reduced nausea, improved digestion, and a decrease in stomach growling.

Some people may also notice an increase in appetite and energy levels.

Can drinking herbal tea prevent stomach growling from forming?

Herbal teas can help with stomach growling, but it's not always safe to drink them daily.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in stomach growling?

Herbal teas can help with stomach growling.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for stomach growling?

Drinking herbal tea when your stomach is growling is best about an hour before eating.

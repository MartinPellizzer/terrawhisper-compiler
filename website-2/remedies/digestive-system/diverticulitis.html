What Are The Main Causes Of Diverticulitis?

Diverticulitis is a condition where small pouches in the wall of the intestine become inflamed.

The main causes of diverticulitis are related to poor eating habits and lifestyle. Constipation is a major cause of diverticulitis. When the body does not get enough fiber from food, it can lead to hard and dry stools that are difficult to pass. This puts pressure on the intestine walls, causing small pouches to form and potentially become inflamed.

Eating a low-fiber diet is also a significant contributor to diverticulitis. A low-fiber diet means the body is not getting enough fiber to help move food through the intestines. This can lead to constipation and the same problems that arise from it. Obesity is another cause of diverticulitis. Being overweight or obese can put extra pressure on the intestine walls, making it more likely for pouches to form and become inflamed.

This is because the body is carrying around excess weight that can put strain on the digestive system.

Below are the main causes of diverticulitis.

  • Constipation: Constipation is a cause of diverticulitis because hard and dry stool puts extra pressure on the diverticula, which are small pouches in the wall of the colon, causing them to become inflamed and infected.
  • Low Fiber Diet: Eating a low-fiber diet causes diverticulitis because it leads to constipation, which puts extra pressure on the weak spots in the wall of the colon, making it more likely to become inflamed and develop diverticulitis.
  • Obesity: Obesity can cause diverticulitis because the extra weight puts extra pressure on the digestive system, making it harder for the muscles in the intestines to push food through, which can lead to the formation of small pockets or pouches that can get infected.
  • Straining: Straining during bowel movements can cause diverticulitis because the extra pressure on the weak spots in the wall of the colon can cause them to bulge outward and become inflamed.
  • Dehydration: Dehydration can cause diverticulitis because it reduces the amount of water in the colon, which can make the walls of the colon weak and more likely to form small bulges that can become inflamed.
  • High Blood Pressure: High blood pressure can cause diverticulitis because the extra pressure in the blood vessels makes the weak spots in the wall of the digestive tube (colon) even weaker and more likely to bulge out, leading to diverticulitis.
  • Diabetes: Diabetes can cause diverticulitis because high blood sugar levels can damage blood vessels, leading to poor blood flow to the digestive tract and increasing the risk of inflammation and infection in the diverticula.

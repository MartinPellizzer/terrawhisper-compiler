What are the causes of diverticulitis?

Diverticulitis occurs when diverticula - small pouches in the wall of the colon - become inflamed.

The main causes of diverticulitis are linked to how we live and what we eat. One key cause is a low fiber diet. When we don't eat enough fiber-rich foods like fruits, vegetables, and whole grains, our colon muscles have to work harder to move waste through the digestive system, which can cause the diverticula to form. A low fiber diet also leads to constipation, another cause of diverticulitis. Obesity is also a major risk factor for diverticulitis.

When we're overweight or obese, our colon has to work even harder to move waste, which can cause inflammation and infection in the diverticula. Physical inactivity is another contributing factor. Regular exercise helps to move food through the digestive system and maintain a healthy weight, but a sedentary lifestyle can lead to constipation and diverticulitis. Diverticulosis, which is the formation of diverticula, is often a precursor to diverticulitis. When diverticula form, they can become inflamed and infected, leading to diverticulitis. Constipation is also a direct cause of diverticulitis, as it allows bacteria to multiply in the diverticula, leading to infection and inflammation.

In short, diverticulitis is often the result of a combination of factors, including a low fiber diet, obesity, physical inactivity, constipation, and diverticulosis.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains bioactive constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and relax the muscles in the stomach. Ginger's warming properties increase blood flow to the stomach, which helps to reduce nausea and vomiting. The volatile oils in ginger, such as zingiberene and bisabolol, have antioxidant properties that help protect the stomach lining from damage.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the stomach lining, Zingiber officinale creams can provide relief for an upset stomach.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of ginger essential oil, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl in the microwave for 30-second intervals, stirring between each interval, until melted.
  3. Add shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until combined. Remove from heat.
  4. Allow the mixture to cool and thicken. Once thickened, add ginger essential oil and vitamin E oil. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a glass jar and let it cool completely before use. Store in a cool, dry place.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which is responsible for its soothing properties.

Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory effects, reducing swelling and discomfort in the stomach. The cream also contains flavonoids and saponins, which have antioxidant properties that help protect the stomach lining from damage. The saponins in Glycyrrhiza glabra cream help to increase the mucus production in the stomach, providing a protective barrier against stomach acid.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the stomach lining, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams can help alleviate symptoms of an upset stomach.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/2 cup of glycerin, 2 tablespoons of glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, and a small saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over low heat until warm. Remove from heat.
  3. Add the glycerin and glycyrrhiza glabra root powder to the warm water. Stir well until the powder dissolves.
  4. Let the mixture cool down to room temperature. Strain it into a clean container using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  5. Add a preservative and stir well. Pour the mixture into small containers and store in the refrigerator. Use as needed for upset stomach.

3. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains bioactive constituents like menthol, menthone, and limonene.

These compounds have analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe the stomach and reduce discomfort.   3. Remove melted mixture from heat and stir in peppermint essential oil and vitamin E oil.
  4. Pour mixture into small jars or containers and let cool until solid.
  5. Label and store creams in a cool, dry place to use as needed for upset stomach relief.

4. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare creams contains active constituents like fenchone, anethole, and limonene.

These compounds have antispasmodic and carminative properties, which help relax the muscles in the digestive tract and reduce gas and cramps. Fenchone and anethole also have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe an irritated stomach lining and reduce inflammation. The combination of these properties in Foeniculum vulgare creams may help alleviate symptoms of an upset stomach, such as bloating, cramps, and nausea.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Foeniculum vulgare (fennel seeds) and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Steep the fennel seeds in the carrier oil for 2 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar every day.
  3. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the fennel seeds.
  4. Add 10 drops of peppermint essential oil to the fennel-infused oil for better taste.
  5. Transfer the cream to a glass jar and store it in the fridge for up to 6 months. Apply 1-2 teaspoons as needed to soothe an upset stomach.

5. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis creams contains compounds like quinine and cinchonine, alkaloids that have been used to treat upset stomach for centuries.

  4. Heat the mixture for 10-15 minutes, then remove from heat and let it cool.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Transfer the mixture to a jar and store in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

7 herbal essential oils to ease an upset stomach for tourists everywhere

Traveling can be exciting, but it can also take a toll on our stomachs.

When we visit new places, we're exposed to different foods, water, and environments, which can cause our digestive systems to go into overdrive. This can lead to nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach cramps - symptoms that can make it hard to enjoy our trip. An upset stomach can ruin a tourist's day, forcing them to spend more time in their hotel room, missing out on sightseeing, shopping, or dining experiences.

It can also lead to dehydration, fatigue, and even anxiety. In severe cases, it can even spoil a trip's itinerary and plans, leaving tourists with a lot of stress and disappointment. For tourists, an upset stomach is not just a minor inconvenience, but a major setback that can affect their mood, energy levels, and overall travel experience. When it comes to treating upset stomachs, many people turn to herbal essential oils for their natural and non-invasive properties.

These oils are made from the concentrated extracts of plants and can be easily applied topically or inhaled to provide relief from stomach discomfort.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils, also known as ginger essential oil, is a natural remedy for upset stomach due to its anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

The main constituents of ginger essential oil include gingerols and shogaols, which have been shown to reduce inflammation and stimulate digestive enzymes. When you apply a few drops of ginger essential oil to your stomach or inhale it through steam inhalation, the active compounds are absorbed into your bloodstream, helping to soothe the stomach lining and alleviate nausea and vomiting. To use ginger essential oil for upset stomach, mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it to your stomach in a gentle, circular motion.

Alternatively, inhale the oil through steam inhalation by adding a few drops to a bowl of hot water and covering your head with a towel. For immediate relief, try taking small sips of ginger tea or eating a small amount of fresh ginger root. When you take action, you can expect to feel a reduction in nausea and vomiting, and an improvement in digestion. The anti-inflammatory properties of ginger essential oil may also help to reduce stomach cramps and spasms, while its digestive enzymes may help to break down food more efficiently.

As a result, you may experience a decrease in stomach discomfort and an increase in overall well-being.

2. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils, also known as peppermint oil, can help soothe an upset stomach due to its calming properties and active compounds.

It contains menthol and menthone, which work together to relax the muscles in the stomach and reduce spasms. This can help alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), such as bloating and cramps. To use peppermint oil for an upset stomach, mix a few drops with a carrier oil, like coconut or olive oil, and rub it on your abdomen in a gentle, clockwise motion.

You can also inhale peppermint oil through steam inhalation or add it to a warm bath for relaxation. As you apply the oil, you may feel a cooling sensation and a decrease in stomach discomfort. In some cases, you might experience a slight relief from nausea and vomiting.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils, also known as anise oil, may help with upset stomach due to its anti-inflammatory and carminative properties.

The oil contains compounds like anethole, limonene, and pinene, which have been shown to relax the muscles in the digestive tract and reduce spasms. This can help alleviate symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and cramps.

To use Foeniculum vulgare essential oils for upset stomach, dilute a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it to your abdomen in a gentle, circular motion. You can also inhale the oil by adding a few drops to a handkerchief or inhaling it from a diffuser. The likely outcomes of using Foeniculum vulgare essential oils for upset stomach may include reduced discomfort, relief from bloating and cramps, and a faster return to normal digestion.

However, it's essential to note that if you experience severe or persistent symptoms, you should consult with a healthcare professional before using essential oils.

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils, also known as licorice root oil, can be beneficial for an upset stomach.

The main reason is due to its anti-inflammatory properties and the presence of glycyrrhizin, a compound that helps to soothe and protect the stomach lining. The oil also contains flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties that can help to calm digestive issues and reduce inflammation. To use Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils for an upset stomach, you can try the following: mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it to your abdomen in a gentle, circular motion.

Alternatively, you can inhale the oil by adding a few drops to your diffuser or by applying it to your wrists. When you take action and use Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils, you may experience a reduction in stomach discomfort and inflammation, and an improvement in digestion. You may also notice a decrease in nausea and vomiting.

However, it's essential to note that if you have a pre-existing condition, such as high blood pressure or kidney disease, you should consult with a healthcare professional before using licorice root oil.

5. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis essential oils contain compounds like quinine, cinchonine, and cinchonidine, which have been traditionally used to alleviate upset stomach issues.

The quinine content in Cinchona officinalis essential oils helps to reduce inflammation and relax muscles, providing relief from cramps and spasms. To use Cinchona officinalis essential oils for upset stomach, add a few drops to a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil and gently massage it into your abdomen.

If you experience persistent or severe stomach issues, it's best to consult a healthcare professional before using essential oils. Taking action with Cinchona officinalis essential oils may lead to a reduction in nausea and vomiting, as well as relief from stomach cramps and bloating.

However, it's essential to note that individual results may vary, and more research is needed to confirm the effectiveness of Cinchona officinalis essential oils for stomach issues.

Artemisia absinthium essential oils, also known as wormwood oil, can be beneficial for an upset stomach due to its anti-inflammatory and carminative properties.

The oil contains constituents like thujone, borneol, and limonene, which help to soothe and calm the digestive system. Thujone, in particular, has been shown to have a relaxing effect on the muscles in the stomach, reducing cramping and spasms. Borneol, on the other hand, has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. To use Artemisia absinthium essential oils for an upset stomach, you can add a few drops to a warm bath or inhale it through steam inhalation. You can also mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and massage it onto your stomach area. However, it's essential to dilute the oil properly, as undiluted essential oils can be too potent for the skin.

Start with a small amount, about 1-2% dilution, and gradually increase as needed. If you're pregnant, breastfeeding, or have any underlying health conditions, consult with a healthcare professional before using Artemisia absinthium essential oils. When using Artemisia absinthium essential oils for an upset stomach, you may experience relief from symptoms such as nausea, bloating, and abdominal cramps. The oil may also help to reduce inflammation and promote digestion. However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, seek medical attention. It's also essential to note that while Artemisia absinthium essential oils can be beneficial, they should not be used as a replacement for medical treatment.

Always consult with a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially if you're new to using them.

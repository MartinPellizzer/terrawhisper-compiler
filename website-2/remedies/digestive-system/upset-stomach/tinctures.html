10 Herbal Tinctures For Upset Stomach

Travelers often face challenges when exploring new places, and one of the most common issues is an upset stomach.

This can be caused by consuming unfamiliar foods, drinking contaminated water, or experiencing stress and anxiety while traveling. An upset stomach can quickly ruin a trip, making it difficult to enjoy activities and experiences. It can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach cramps, leaving travelers feeling weak, tired, and uncomfortable. When traveling, it's not always easy to find medical help, and over-the-counter medications may have unwanted side effects or interact with other medications.

An upset stomach can even lead to dehydration, making the situation even more serious. When traveling, it's essential to have a plan in place to manage an upset stomach. While some people may rely on over-the-counter medications, there's an alternative that's becoming increasingly popular among travelers: herbal tinctures. These are liquid extracts made from plants that have been used for centuries to soothe digestive issues. They work by calming the stomach and intestines, reducing inflammation and promoting healthy digestion.

By taking a few drops of the tincture as needed, travelers can find relief from their symptoms without the potential side effects of medications.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.

These constituents work by relaxing the muscles in the stomach and reducing inflammation, thereby alleviating nausea and vomiting associated with an upset stomach. The active properties in ginger, particularly gingerols and shogaols, have been shown to inhibit the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that can cause stomach contractions and worsen nausea. If you're experiencing an upset stomach, take the following actions: start by taking a small dose of Zingiber officinale tincture, typically 20-30 drops in water, and see how your body reacts.

You can gradually increase the dose as needed, but be cautious not to overdo it. It's essential to dilute the tincture in water, as taking it neat can be overwhelming for your stomach. When you take Zingiber officinale tincture for an upset stomach, you can expect the following outcomes: within 15-30 minutes, you may start to feel a reduction in nausea and vomiting, and your stomach discomfort may begin to subside. The anti-inflammatory properties in ginger may also help to reduce swelling and discomfort in the stomach.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, you should consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins, which work together to soothe and calm the digestive system.

This leads to a reduction in symptoms like bloating, nausea, and abdominal pain. If you're experiencing an upset stomach, consider taking Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures after meals or when symptoms arise. Start with a small dose of 5-10 drops, mixed with water or another liquid, and observe how your body reacts.

You can gradually increase the dose as needed, but be sure to consult with a healthcare professional before making any changes to your treatment plan. Taking Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures may lead to a decrease in stomach discomfort, reduced nausea, and improved digestion. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent upset stomach from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe an upset stomach.

Many herbal teas, like peppermint and ginger, have natural properties that calm the digestive system. They can ease nausea and discomfort, making it easier to feel better.

Some teas even have anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce stomach irritation.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for upset stomach every day?

You can safely drink herbal teas for an upset stomach every day, but be sure to choose ones that are gentle on your stomach.

Peppermint, chamomile, and ginger teas are good options. They can help soothe and calm your stomach.

However, some teas like dandelion and licorice root may not be suitable if you have certain health conditions.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in upset stomach?

Herbal teas can help soothe an upset stomach in a few hours.

Some teas, like peppermint and ginger, can calm nausea and cramps quickly, while others like chamomile may take longer to work, possibly up to a day or two.

Everyone's body is different, and the time it takes to feel better varies from person to person.

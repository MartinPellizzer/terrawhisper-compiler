10 herbal tinctures for the traveler's upset stomach

Travelers often face challenges when exploring new places, and one of the most common issues is an upset stomach.

This can be caused by consuming unfamiliar foods, drinking contaminated water, or experiencing stress and anxiety while traveling. An upset stomach can quickly ruin a trip, making it difficult to enjoy activities and experiences. It can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach cramps, leaving travelers feeling weak, tired, and uncomfortable. When traveling, it's not always easy to find medical help, and over-the-counter medications may have unwanted side effects or interact with other medications.

An upset stomach can even lead to dehydration, making the situation even more serious. When traveling, it's essential to have a plan in place to manage an upset stomach. While some people may rely on over-the-counter medications, there's an alternative that's becoming increasingly popular among travelers: herbal tinctures. These are liquid extracts made from plants that have been used for centuries to soothe digestive issues. They work by calming the stomach and intestines, reducing inflammation and promoting healthy digestion.

By taking a few drops of the tincture as needed, travelers can find relief from their symptoms without the potential side effects of medications.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures are made from the root of the licorice plant, which has been used for centuries to soothe an upset stomach.

The main reason it helps is because of its anti-inflammatory properties and its ability to protect the stomach lining. Glycyrrhiza glabra contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which has been shown to reduce inflammation and calm the stomach muscles, allowing them to move food through the digestive system more easily. This helps to prevent symptoms like nausea, vomiting, and stomach pain. If you're experiencing an upset stomach, take a few drops of Glycyrrhiza glabra tincture in water or tea, 2-3 times a day.

You can also add it to a stomach-soothing tea blend or a probiotic supplement to enhance its effects. It's essential to start with a small dose and gradually increase it as needed, as some people may experience a drop in blood pressure or other side effects. As you take the tincture, you may notice a decrease in stomach discomfort and inflammation. Your digestive system may start to function more smoothly, and you may feel relief from nausea and vomiting. Some people may also experience an improvement in their appetite and a sense of overall well-being.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, you should consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

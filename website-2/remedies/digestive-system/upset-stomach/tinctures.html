10 Herbal Tinctures For Upset Stomach

If you're experiencing an upset stomach, herbal tinctures can be a great way to find relief.

These tinctures are made from concentrated plant extracts that are easy to absorb into your body. This means that the active ingredients in the tincture can start working quickly to calm your stomach and alleviate symptoms. Drinking a tea can be a calming and comforting experience that helps to distract you from your symptoms.

And cinchona bark (Cinchona officinalis) contains quinine, a natural compound that can help to reduce inflammation and ease stomach cramps. These herbs are just a few examples of the many plants that have been used for centuries to soothe an upset stomach.

By taking a tincture or drinking a tea made from these herbs, you can find relief from your symptoms and get back to your normal routine.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains bioactive constituents like gingerols and shogaols.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. The volatile oils in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, have a calming effect on the stomach muscles, which can help alleviate nausea and vomiting. The phenolic compounds in ginger have antioxidant properties that help protect the stomach lining from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the stomach lining, ginger tinctures can help soothe an upset stomach and promote digestion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots, 2 cups of 80% vodka, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Chop the ginger roots into small pieces and put them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour the vodka over the ginger, making sure all the roots are covered.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar, discarding the ginger solids.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains glycyrrhizin, a potent saponin that helps soothe an upset stomach.

Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, easing stomach pain and discomfort. It also has a direct effect on the stomach lining, protecting it from acid and preventing ulcers. Glycyrrhizin also works to relax the stomach muscles, allowing for better digestion and reducing nausea.

Additionally, glycyrrhizin has antimicrobial properties, which help eliminate any underlying infections that may be contributing to the stomach upset.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots. If using fresh roots, dry them in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 2 hours.
  2. Grind 2 ounces of the dried roots into fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Combine 1 cup of 80-proof vodka with the powdered roots in a clean glass jar. Make sure the roots are completely covered by the vodka.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will allow the roots to infuse into the vodka.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture in water or tea as needed to soothe an upset stomach.

3. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis tinctures contains a key compound called quinine, along with other active constituents like alkaloids and glycosides.

These compounds help to calm the stomach and reduce inflammation. Quinine has a natural anti-spasmodic property that soothes the muscles in the stomach, making it easier to digest food. The glycosides in Cinchona officinalis tinctures also have a mild laxative effect, which helps to move food through the digestive system and prevent constipation.

This combination of properties makes Cinchona officinalis tinctures a helpful remedy for an upset stomach.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Cinchona officinalis bark, 2 cups of 80% ethanol, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Cinchona bark and ethanol in the glass jar. Stir well to coat the bark.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Take 20-30 drops as needed to help with an upset stomach.

4. Foeniculum vulgare

By reducing inflammation and promoting digestion, Foeniculum vulgare tinctures can help to alleviate discomfort and relieve an upset stomach.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent upset stomach from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help soothe an upset stomach.

Many herbal teas, like peppermint and ginger, have natural properties that calm the digestive system. They can ease nausea and discomfort, making it easier to feel better.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for upset stomach every day?

You can safely drink herbal teas for an upset stomach every day, but be sure to choose ones that are gentle on your stomach.

Peppermint, chamomile, and ginger teas are good options. They can help soothe and calm your stomach.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in upset stomach?

Herbal teas can help soothe an upset stomach in a few hours.

Some teas, like peppermint and ginger, can calm nausea and cramps quickly, while others like chamomile may take longer to work, possibly up to a day or two.

Everyone's body is different, and the time it takes to feel better varies from person to person.

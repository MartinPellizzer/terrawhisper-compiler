7 Teas to Tame Traveler's Stomach Upset

When traveling to new places, our stomachs can get upset.

This is because we're trying new foods, water, and environments, which can be hard for our bodies to adjust to. We might eat something that doesn't agree with us, or drink contaminated water that makes us sick. This can lead to nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach cramps, making us feel weak and miserable. It can also make us miss out on activities and experiences that we've been looking forward to, like trying new foods or exploring new cities. It can even affect our mood, making us feel anxious and irritable. When we're traveling, we want to enjoy our time and make the most of our trip, but an upset stomach can make it difficult to do so.

Travelers often try to self-medicate with over-the-counter medications, but these can have side effects and may not address the underlying cause of the issue. In some cases, they might need to take time off from their trip to recover, which can be frustrating and costly. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to soothe an upset stomach. They work by calming the digestive system, reducing inflammation, and promoting digestion. They can be made from a variety of plants and flowers that are rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds. When consumed as a warm, soothing drink, they can help to ease stomach discomfort, reduce nausea, and promote a sense of calm.

They're also a gentle and natural way to treat an upset stomach, making them a popular choice among travelers.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, have been used for centuries to help alleviate upset stomach.

This is because of its active compounds, including gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce nausea and vomiting. Ginger also has a soothing effect on the digestive system, relaxing the muscles in the stomach and reducing spasms. When you have an upset stomach, try making a ginger tea by steeping fresh ginger in hot water for 5-10 minutes.

You can add honey or lemon to taste. If you're experiencing nausea, take small sips of the tea, and if you're experiencing diarrhea, try to limit your liquid intake. By drinking ginger tea, you may find that your nausea and discomfort subside within 30 minutes to an hour. Some people may also experience a reduction in stomach cramps and bloating.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional for further guidance.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, also known as licorice root tea, have been used for centuries to soothe an upset stomach.

The reason it helps is due to its anti-inflammatory properties and the presence of glycyrrhizin, a compound that helps to reduce inflammation and calm the digestive system. Glycyrrhizin also has a soothing effect on the stomach lining, which can become irritated when you're experiencing stomach discomfort. Another key constituent, triterpenoids, has been shown to have antioxidant properties, which can help to protect the stomach from further damage. If you're experiencing an upset stomach, you can try making a licorice root tea by steeping dried licorice root in hot water.

Start by drinking a small amount, about 1/4 cup, 2-3 times a day, and adjust as needed. You can also add a pinch of salt to help replace lost electrolytes and support digestion. However, if you have high blood pressure, kidney disease, or are taking certain medications, you should consult with your doctor before using licorice root tea, as it can interact with these conditions. When you drink licorice root tea, you may experience a reduction in stomach pain and discomfort, as well as a decrease in nausea and bloating. You may also notice an improvement in your digestive system, with regular bowel movements and a reduction in symptoms such as diarrhea or constipation.

Additionally, the antioxidants in licorice root tea may help to reduce inflammation in the body, which can lead to a range of other health benefits.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas, also known as fennel tea, can be a soothing remedy for an upset stomach.

The tea contains compounds like anethole, limonene, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and carminative properties. These properties help to reduce inflammation and gas in the digestive tract, providing relief from cramps, bloating, and discomfort. Fennel tea also stimulates digestive enzymes and relaxes the muscles in the stomach, promoting a smoother digestion process. If you're experiencing an upset stomach, try making a fennel tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried fennel seeds in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it after meals to help alleviate symptoms. You can also add honey or lemon to taste. As you drink the tea, you may start to feel a sense of relief from the discomfort and cramps in your stomach. Within 15-30 minutes, you may notice a decrease in bloating and gas, and your digestive system may start to function more smoothly.

If your symptoms persist or worsen, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

4. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas, also known as peppermint tea, can help soothe an upset stomach due to its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

The main constituents of peppermint, menthol and menthone, work together to relax the muscles in the stomach and reduce inflammation. When you drink peppermint tea, the menthol helps to reduce spasms and ease cramps, while the menthone reduces the production of stomach acid and calms the digestive system. If you have an upset stomach, try drinking a cup of peppermint tea after meals or when you feel symptoms coming on.

You can also add a pinch of salt or a squeeze of fresh lemon juice to enhance the effect. To make the tea, steep a teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink. Some people find that peppermint tea helps to alleviate symptoms such as bloating, gas, and nausea within 15-30 minutes.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, you should consult a healthcare professional for further advice.

5. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla teas, also known as German chamomile tea, can help alleviate upset stomach symptoms.

The tea's active compounds, such as apigenin and luteolin, have anti-inflammatory and calming properties that can soothe the digestive system. These constituents also have a direct effect on the stomach muscles, reducing spasms and cramps. The tea's anti-inflammatory properties can also help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, further easing stomach discomfort. If you're experiencing an upset stomach, try making a tea by steeping one teaspoon of dried Matricaria chamomilla flowers in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain and drink the tea after it has cooled down a bit. To enhance the effects, you can add a pinch of ginger or peppermint to the tea for added digestive benefits. When you drink the tea, you may start to feel a calming sensation in your stomach, and the discomfort may begin to subside. Your digestive system may start to relax, and the spasms may decrease. The tea's anti-inflammatory effects may also help reduce stomach pain and discomfort.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

6. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate upset stomachs.

The main reason for its effectiveness lies in its high content of quinine and other alkaloids, such as cinchonidine and cinchonine. These compounds help to reduce inflammation and relax the muscles in the stomach, which can become spasmodic and irritated when we're feeling queasy. Additionally, Cinchona officinalis teas contain flavonoids and glycosides, which have antioxidant properties that can help soothe and calm the digestive system. If you're experiencing an upset stomach, try making a tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried Cinchona bark in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it slowly, ideally on an empty stomach.

You can also add a squeeze of fresh lemon juice and a bit of honey to make it more palatable. It's recommended to start with a small dose and gradually increase as needed. When you take Cinchona officinalis tea for an upset stomach, you can expect to feel a sense of relief and calmness within 15-30 minutes. The inflammation and spasms in your stomach should begin to subside, allowing your digestive system to function more smoothly. You may also notice a reduction in nausea and vomiting.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for further guidance.

7. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus teas, also known as lemongrass tea, can help soothe an upset stomach due to its natural properties and constituents.

The tea contains compounds like citral, limonene, and beta-pinene, which have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. These properties help to calm the digestive system, reduce inflammation, and ease muscle cramps in the stomach.

When you drink lemongrass tea, you can take the following actions: start by drinking a cup of tea 30 minutes before meals to help reduce digestive discomfort; if you experience nausea, try sipping the tea slowly and in small amounts to ease digestion; if you have persistent stomach issues, consider consulting a healthcare professional for personalized advice. As you drink lemongrass tea, you may notice your stomach cramps and spasms subsiding, and your digestive system starting to calm down.

Your nausea may also begin to decrease, and you may feel more comfortable and relaxed after consuming a meal.

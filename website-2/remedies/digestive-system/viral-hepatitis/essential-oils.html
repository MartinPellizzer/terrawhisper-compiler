1. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum essential oils contains silymarin as its main bioactive constituent, a powerful antioxidant that helps protect the liver from damage.

Silymarin has anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the liver, a common issue in viral hepatitis. It also has immunomodulatory effects, enhancing the body's natural defense against viral infections. Silymarin's flavonoids and polyphenols help repair liver cells and improve liver function, which is essential for removing the virus from the body.

By reducing liver inflammation and improving its function, Silybum marianum essential oils can help alleviate the symptoms of viral hepatitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Silybum marianum flowers, 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the dried flowers and carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Place the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. This is called 'infusion'.
  4. After the infusion period, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a carrier oil for topical application or consult a healthcare professional for oral use.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, which is known to have anti-inflammatory properties.

Glycyrrhizin also has antioxidant properties, which can help protect liver cells from damage caused by free radicals. By reducing inflammation and protecting liver cells, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils may help alleviate symptoms and support liver health in people with viral hepatitis.

  1. Gather 500g of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 5 cups of carrier oil like jojoba or sweet almond oil.
  2. Grind the roots into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Combine 1 cup of the ground roots with 5 cups of carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  4. Place the jar in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water. Let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

6. Echinacea purpurea

Ginsenosides also have anti-inflammatory effects, which can alleviate the symptoms of viral hepatitis, such as fatigue and joint pain. Additionally, the oils contain eleutherosides, which have antioxidant properties that help protect liver cells from oxidative stress caused by viral infections.

By reducing liver inflammation and protecting liver cells, Panax ginseng essential oils may help alleviate the symptoms and slow the progression of viral hepatitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Panax ginseng roots and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Grind the dried Panax ginseng roots into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Combine the ginseng powder (2 tablespoons) with the carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  4. Leave the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the oil to infuse.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and use the infused oil as needed.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in viral hepatitis?

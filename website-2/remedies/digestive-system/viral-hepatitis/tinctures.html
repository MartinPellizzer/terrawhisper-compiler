1. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum tinctures contains silymarin, a mixture of flavonoids and polyphenols, including silibinin, silidianin, and silicristin.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help protect liver cells from damage caused by free radicals. Silymarin also helps to reduce liver inflammation, scarring, and fibrosis, which are common issues in viral hepatitis. Additionally, silymarin has been shown to enhance the liver's natural antioxidant defenses, including glutathione, to help combat oxidative stress and promote liver regeneration.

By reducing liver damage and enhancing its natural defenses, silymarin in Silybum marianum tinctures supports the body's natural healing processes in viral hepatitis.

  1. Gather 250 grams of Silybum marianum flowers and leaves. Clean them with a brush under running water.
  2. Combine the cleaned Silybum marianum with 1250 milliliters of 80% ethanol in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 14 days. Shake the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to small glass dropper bottles. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops, 3 times a day, under medical supervision.

2. Curcuma longa

Regular use of Curcuma longa tinctures may help alleviate symptoms of viral hepatitis and support liver health.

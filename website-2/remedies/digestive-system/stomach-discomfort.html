What Are The Main Causes Of Stomach Discomfort?

Stomach discomfort can be caused by several factors.

One of the main causes is Food Poisoning, which occurs when we eat contaminated or spoiled food. This can happen when food is not stored or cooked properly, allowing bacteria like Salmonella or E. coli to multiply. When we eat such food, these bacteria can cause stomach cramps, vomiting, and diarrhea. Another common cause is Gastroenteritis, which is an inflammation of the stomach and intestines. It can be caused by viruses like Norovirus or Rotavirus, or by bacteria like Salmonella or E. coli.

Gastroenteritis can lead to symptoms like stomach pain, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. It can also be caused by food or water contaminated with these viruses or bacteria. Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) is another cause of stomach discomfort. It is a chronic condition that affects the large intestine, causing symptoms like abdominal pain, bloating, and changes in bowel movements. IBS can be caused by stress, anxiety, or changes in diet. It can also be triggered by certain foods like gluten, dairy, or spicy foods.

People with IBS may experience stomach discomfort after eating, or they may have difficulty digesting food.

Below are the main causes of stomach discomfort.

  • Food Poisoning: Food poisoning occurs when we eat spoiled or contaminated food that has harmful bacteria, viruses, or other germs in it, which can then infect our bodies and cause stomach discomfort.
  • Gastroenteritis: Gastroenteritis is a cause of stomach discomfort because it is an infection that inflames the stomach and intestines, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain.
  • Irritable Bowel Syndrome: Irritable Bowel Syndrome causes stomach discomfort because the nerves that control bowel movements are overly active, leading to sudden and unpredictable contractions of the intestines that can cause pain, cramping, and bloating.
  • Constipation: Constipation causes stomach discomfort because hard, dry stool puts pressure on the stomach and intestines, making them feel bloated, painful, and uncomfortable.
  • Diarrhea: Diarrhea is a cause of stomach discomfort because it makes the stomach produce too much fluid, which can lead to cramps, bloating, and a feeling of being unwell.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Stomach Discomfort?

If you have stomach discomfort, there are certain herbs you should avoid.

One of these is Peppermint (Mentha piperita), which can relax the muscles in the stomach and worsen heartburn or acid reflux. Another is Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla), which can slow down digestion and make stomach discomfort worse. Ginger has already been ruled out, so we can also look at Turmeric (Curcuma longa) - its active compound, curcumin, can irritate the stomach and cause heartburn.

Similarly, Licorice root's relative, Dyer's Chamomile (Anthemis cotula), is also not recommended due to its potential for stomach upset. Additionally, Comfrey (Symphytum officinale) and Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) can cause stomach discomfort due to their high levels of tannins. Also, avoid Senna (Cassia senna) and Cascara (Rhamnus purshiana) as they are laxatives and can cause stomach cramps and diarrhea.

Finally, avoid Wild Yam (Dioscorea villosa) as it may cause stomach upset in some people.

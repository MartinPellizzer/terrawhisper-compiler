Inflammatory Bowel Disease: Causes, Symptoms, and Natural Herbal Treatments

If left untreated, inflammatory bowel disease can lead to serious complications, including narrowing of the intestines, ulcers, and increased risk of colon cancer. Therefore, it is essential to find effective management strategies to control the symptoms and prevent long-term damage. What Are The Main Causes Of Inflammatory Bowel Disease?

Inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) is a condition that affects the digestive system.

There are two main types of IBD: Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis. Genetics play a big role in IBD. Some people are born with a genetic tendency to develop IBD, which means they are more likely to get it. This is because they may have inherited a faulty gene from their parents.

Crohn's disease is a type of IBD that can affect any part of the digestive system, from the mouth to the anus. It causes inflammation and irritation in the digestive tract, leading to symptoms like diarrhea, abdominal pain, and weight loss. Ulcerative colitis is another type of IBD that affects the large intestine (colon) and rectum. It causes inflammation and ulcers in the lining of the colon, leading to symptoms like bloody diarrhea and abdominal pain. The exact cause of IBD is not known, but it is thought that a combination of genetic and environmental factors may trigger the condition.

For example, some people may be more sensitive to certain foods or have an abnormal immune response, which can lead to inflammation in the digestive system.

Below are the main causes of inflammatory bowel disease.

  • Genetics: Genetics is a cause of inflammatory bowel disease because people with a family history of conditions like Crohn's disease or ulcerative colitis are more likely to develop the disease due to inherited genetic mutations that affect the way their body's immune system and digestive system work together.
  • Crohn's disease: Crohn's disease is a cause of inflammatory bowel disease because it is a chronic condition that causes inflammation and damage in the digestive tract, leading to symptoms like abdominal pain, diarrhea, and fatigue.
  • Ulcerative colitis: Ulcerative colitis is a cause of inflammatory bowel disease because it causes long-term inflammation and irritation in the large intestine, leading to symptoms such as diarrhea, abdominal pain, and rectal bleeding.
  • Bacteria: Bacteria can cause inflammatory bowel disease when they invade the lining of the intestines, triggering an immune response that leads to chronic inflammation and damage to the digestive tract.
  • Viral: Viral infections can cause inflammatory bowel disease because they trigger an immune response that mistakenly attacks the healthy cells in the gut, leading to inflammation and damage to the digestive system.
  • Foods: Some people develop inflammatory bowel disease, such as Crohn's disease or ulcerative colitis, after eating foods that the body mistakenly identifies as harmful, leading to inflammation and damage in the digestive tract.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Inflammatory Bowel Disease?

If you have inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), it's essential to be cautious with certain herbs that can trigger or worsen symptoms.

Some herbs to avoid include Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis), which contains berberine that can irritate the digestive tract and cause inflammation. Sage (Salvia officinalis) and its compounds may also be problematic, as they can slow down digestion and worsen IBD symptoms.

Ginkgo biloba, while often used for its memory benefits, can increase inflammation in the gut and exacerbate IBD symptoms. Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza uralensis, not to be confused with Glycyrrhiza glabra) is also a concern, as it can cause an imbalance of gut bacteria and worsen inflammation. Additionally, Yerba Santa (Eriodictyon glutinosum) and its compounds may irritate the digestive tract and trigger IBD symptoms.

It's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies, especially if you have IBD.

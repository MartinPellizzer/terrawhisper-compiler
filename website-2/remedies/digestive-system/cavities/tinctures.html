1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and polyphenols.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and pain associated with cavities. Caffeic acid derivatives, specifically chicoric acid and caftaric acid, have antimicrobial properties that can inhibit the growth of bacteria that cause tooth decay. Alkylamides, particularly echinacoside and cis-3-hexen-1-ol, have antioxidant properties that can help protect teeth from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and inhibiting bacterial growth, Echinacea purpurea tinctures may be beneficial in preventing and treating cavities.

  1. Gather 250g of fresh or dried Echinacea purpurea roots and flowers.
  2. Combine the roots and flowers in a clean glass jar and cover them with 1 cup of 80% vodka.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a clean glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid and label it. Use 20-30 drops in water 3-4 times a day for cavity relief.

3. Zingiber officinale

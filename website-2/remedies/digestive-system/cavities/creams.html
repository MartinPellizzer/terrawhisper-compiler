8 Soothing Creams to Help Kids with Cavities

When it comes to children's teeth, cavities can be a real problem.

This is because their teeth are still growing and developing, and it's easier for bacteria to form acids that hurt the tooth. If cavities aren't treated, they can cause pain, make eating and sleeping uncomfortable, and even lead to infections that can spread to other parts of the body. Sometimes, cavities can even require fillings or even extractions, which can be scary for kids. And because kids are still learning how to take care of their teeth, cavities can be a recurring issue. Cavities can also affect a child's daily life, making it hard for them to focus in school, play with friends, or enjoy their favorite foods.

And for some kids, the fear of going to the dentist can be overwhelming. But there is some good news. Some herbal creams have been shown to help prevent and even heal cavities in children. These creams work by using special properties that can help stop bacteria from growing, reduce inflammation, and even promote healing in the tooth. They are also gentle and non-toxic, making them a great option for kids who can't have harsh chemicals or medicines.

By applying these creams to the affected area, parents can help their kids feel better and reduce the risk of future cavities.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams have been traditionally used to help with cavities, and research suggests that it may be due to its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The creams contain compounds like alkylamides and glycosides, which are known to have antimicrobial effects that can help reduce bacteria that cause tooth decay. The anti-inflammatory properties may also help reduce swelling and pain associated with cavities. If you're considering using Echinacea purpurea cream to help with cavities, it's essential to use it as a complementary treatment, not a replacement for regular dental care. To start, brush your teeth regularly with a fluoride toothpaste and visit your dentist for regular check-ups.

Once you've applied the cream, gently massage it into the affected area with your finger, making sure to cover the entire tooth surface. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day. As you use the Echinacea purpurea cream, you may notice a decrease in the sensitivity and pain associated with your cavity. The cream's antibacterial properties may also help reduce the severity of the cavity, potentially preventing further damage. However, it's crucial to remember that Echinacea purpurea cream is not a substitute for professional dental treatment.

If you notice any significant changes or worsening of your symptoms, consult with your dentist before continuing use.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams have been found to be beneficial in the prevention and treatment of cavities.

The gel of the Aloe barbadensis plant contains properties and constituents such as aloe-emodin, aloin, and vitamins A, C, and E, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. These properties help to kill bacteria that cause tooth decay, reduce inflammation in the gums, and promote healing. The aloe-emodin also has the ability to inhibit the growth of Streptococcus mutans, a type of bacteria that contributes to the formation of cavities. To use Aloe barbadensis creams for cavities, apply a small amount of the cream directly to the affected area and massage it gently into your gums using your fingers.

You can also use it as a mouthwash by swishing it around in your mouth for 30 seconds before spitting it out. It's essential to note that Aloe barbadensis creams should not be used as a replacement for regular dental care, but rather as a complementary treatment. Using Aloe barbadensis creams for cavities may lead to several outcomes, including reduced tooth sensitivity, less inflammation in the gums, and a decrease in the risk of further cavity formation. Additionally, the antimicrobial properties of the cream may help to prevent the spread of infection and promote a healthy oral environment.

Regular use of Aloe barbadensis creams may also lead to improved dental health and a reduction in the need for dental fillings and other treatments.

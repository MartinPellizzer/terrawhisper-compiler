Peptic Ulcers: Causes, Medicinal Herbs, and Herbal Preparations for Pain Relief

What are the causes of peptic ulcers?

Peptic ulcers are painful sores that develop on the lining of the stomach or the first part of the small intestine.

There are several main causes of peptic ulcers. One of the main causes is the bacteria 'H. pylori.'. This bacteria lives in the stomach lining and can damage it, causing an ulcer. If left untreated, 'H. pylori' can lead to more severe problems. Another cause is the use of nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs, or NSAIDs. These are pain-relieving medications, but they can also damage the stomach lining.

When you take NSAIDs, they can reduce the production of stomach-protecting fluids, making it easier for stomach acid to damage the lining and cause an ulcer. Aspirin is another type of pain-relieving medication that can cause peptic ulcers. Like NSAIDs, aspirin can reduce the production of stomach-protecting fluids, making it easier for stomach acid to damage the stomach lining. Drinking too much alcohol can also lead to peptic ulcers. When you drink alcohol, it can irritate the stomach lining and cause inflammation. This can damage the stomach lining and lead to an ulcer. Genetic predisposition is another cause of peptic ulcers. Some people may be more prone to developing peptic ulcers due to their genetic makeup. This doesn't mean that they will definitely develop an ulcer, but they may be more susceptible to it.

This is because they may have a weaker stomach lining or other factors that make them more prone to stomach damage.

When it comes to treating peptic ulcers, herbal preparations can be a good option.

Finally, a salve is a topical cream or ointment made from herbs that can be applied directly to the stomach area.

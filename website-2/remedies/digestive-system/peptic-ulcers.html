What Are The Main Causes Of Peptic Ulcers?

Peptic ulcers are sores that develop on the lining of the stomach or the first part of the small intestine.

The main causes of peptic ulcers are infections, medications, and other factors. One of the main causes is an infection called H. pylori. This is a type of bacteria that can live in the stomach and cause inflammation, leading to peptic ulcers. When H. pylori infects the stomach lining, it can damage the cells and create sores. Another cause is the use of certain medications, such as nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs). These medications, which include ibuprofen and naproxen, can reduce stomach acid production.

However, they can also irritate the stomach lining and cause ulcers. When NSAIDs are taken regularly, they can cause long-term damage to the stomach lining. Aspirin use is also a cause of peptic ulcers. Aspirin is a type of NSAID, and it can cause stomach irritation and ulcers. Aspirin can reduce stomach acid production, but it can also damage the stomach lining and create sores. This is especially true for people who take aspirin regularly or in high doses.

These causes can lead to peptic ulcers because they damage the stomach lining, creating sores and inflammation.

Below are the main causes of peptic ulcers.

  • H. pylori infection: H. pylori infection is a cause of peptic ulcers because the bacteria release harmful chemicals that damage the protective lining of the stomach and small intestine, allowing stomach acid to eat away at the lining and create ulcers.
  • NSAID use: Long-term use of NSAIDs can cause peptic ulcers by reducing the production of stomach lining cells and increasing stomach acid production, which can lead to painful sores on the stomach lining.
  • Aspirin use: Prolonged use of aspirin can cause peptic ulcers because it reduces the production of stomach-protecting mucus and prostaglandins, which normally prevent stomach acid from damaging the lining of the stomach and duodenum.
  • Corticosteroid use: Corticosteroid use can cause peptic ulcers because these medications reduce the stomach's protective mucus layer, allowing stomach acid to damage the lining and cause ulcers.
  • Antibiotic use: Overuse or misuse of antibiotics can lead to an imbalance of good and bad bacteria in the stomach, allowing the Helicobacter pylori bacteria to thrive and cause peptic ulcers.
  • Stress: Stress causes the stomach to produce more acid than usual, which can irritate the lining of the stomach and small intestine, leading to peptic ulcers.
  • Smoking: Smoking is a cause of peptic ulcers because it reduces blood flow to the stomach lining, making it more difficult for the stomach to protect itself from acid and leading to damage and ulcers.

What Are The Main Causes Of Gas In Stomach?

There are several reasons why gas builds up in the stomach.

One common cause is eating beans, which contain sugars that are not easily broken down by the body. These sugars, called oligosaccharides, are fermented by bacteria in the digestive system, producing gas and leading to bloating and discomfort. Lactose, a sugar found in milk and other dairy products, is another culprit.

People who are lactose intolerant do not produce enough lactase, an enzyme that breaks down lactose. As a result, lactose remains in the digestive system, where it is fermented by bacteria, causing gas and bloating. Raffinose is a complex sugar found in beans and other legumes. Like oligosaccharides in beans, raffinose is not easily broken down by the body.

It is fermented by bacteria in the digestive system, producing gas and contributing to bloating and discomfort.

Below are the main causes of gas in stomach.

  • Beans: Beans cause gas in the stomach because they contain a type of sugar called raffinose, which the body can't digest and is broken down by bacteria in the intestines, producing gas as a byproduct.
  • Lactose: Lactose is a cause of gas in the stomach because it is not fully digested and is broken down by bacteria in the gut, producing gas such as nitrogen, oxygen, carbon dioxide, and hydrogen.
  • Raffinose: Raffinose is a type of sugar that the body can't digest, so when it reaches the large intestine, it's broken down by bacteria, releasing gases like nitrogen, oxygen, and hydrogen, which cause bloating and gas in the stomach.
  • SIBO: SIBO (Small Intestine Bacterial Overgrowth) is a cause of gas in the stomach because the bacteria that overgrow in the small intestine produce gases such as hydrogen, methane, and nitrogen as byproducts of their normal digestion process.
  • Broccoli: Broccoli contains a type of sugar called raffinose, which the body can't easily break down, so it gets fermented by bacteria in the stomach, producing gas as a result.
  • Wheat: Wheat can cause gas in the stomach because the body can't fully digest its complex carbohydrates, especially raffinose, a type of sugar found in wheat, which is then broken down by bacteria in the gut, producing gas.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Gas In Stomach?

If you have gas in your stomach, it's best to avoid some herbs that can make the problem worse.

One herb to avoid is 'Angelica sinensis' or Dong Quai, as it can slow down digestion and cause more gas. Another herb to steer clear of is 'Glycyrrhiza glabra' or Licorice root, as it can cause the body to hold onto water and make digestion slower.

'Trigonella foenum-graecum' or Fenugreek can also be a problem, as it can cause the stomach to produce more gas. 'Cassia angustifolia' or Senna, and 'Rauvolfia serpentina' or Snake root, can also slow down digestion and make gas worse.

Finally, 'Centella asiatica' or Gotu Kola, can cause the body to absorb less water, leading to constipation and more gas.

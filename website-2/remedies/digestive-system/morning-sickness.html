Morning Sickness: A Guide to Causes and Herbal Preparations

What are the causes of morning sickness?

When you're pregnant, your body goes through a lot of changes.

Morning sickness, which can happen at any time of day, is caused by a combination of hormonal changes and nutrient deficiencies. One of the main causes is a hormone called Human Chorionic Gonadotropin, or HCG for short. This hormone helps the fertilized egg implant in the uterus and grows the embryo, but it also makes you feel queasy and tired. Another hormone that's to blame is Progesterone, which helps your uterus relax and get ready for the baby. Progesterone levels surge during pregnancy, and this can cause your stomach to feel upset and your body to feel tired.

Estrogen, another hormone that's important for pregnancy, can also contribute to morning sickness. As estrogen levels increase, it can make your stomach contract and feel queasy. Some people are also at risk for morning sickness if they're deficient in certain vitamins. Vitamin B6 is an important nutrient that helps your body convert food into energy, and if you don't have enough of it, you might feel queasy and tired. Vitamin B12 is also important for your body's energy levels, and a deficiency can make you feel weak and tired.

If you're not eating enough foods that are rich in these vitamins, or if your body is having trouble absorbing them, you might experience more severe morning sickness.

Morning Sickness Solutions: Causes, Medicinal Herbs, and Herbal Preparations for Relief

Morning sickness is a common symptom experienced by pregnant women, typically occurring within the first 12 weeks of pregnancy.

It's characterized by nausea, vomiting, and stomach discomfort, making everyday activities challenging. If left untreated, morning sickness can lead to dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, and weight loss, which can have long-term effects on the mother's and baby's health. Dealing with morning sickness is crucial, and herbal remedies can be an effective solution. Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has anti-inflammatory properties that help alleviate nausea and vomiting. It can be consumed as a decoction, made by steeping fresh ginger in hot water, or as dried tea bags, which can be easily brewed at home.

Ginkgo biloba, another herb used to combat morning sickness, contains flavonoids that help stabilize blood sugar levels and reduce nausea. However, its use during pregnancy should be limited and only under medical supervision due to potential concerns about its effects on the baby. Foeniculum vulgare, or fennel, is also used to soothe digestive issues and alleviate morning sickness symptoms. Its seeds can be used to make a decoction or tea, while its essential oil can be added to a warm compress to aid digestion. Herbal preparations like decoctions, dried tea bags, and tincture drops provide a natural and gentle approach to managing morning sickness.

These remedies can be tailored to individual needs and preferences, offering a safe and effective solution for pregnant women.

What Are The Main Causes Of Morning Sickness?

Morning sickness is a common symptom of pregnancy, and its main causes are related to hormonal changes in the body.

One of the main causes is the Human Chorionic Gonadotropin (HCG) hormone, which is produced by the placenta shortly after implantation of the fertilized egg. High levels of HCG trigger a strong response in the body, leading to nausea and vomiting. Another cause is the increase in Progesterone levels, which helps to thicken the uterine lining and support the growth of the embryo.

Progesterone also relaxes the muscles in the stomach, which can cause stomach acids to flow back up into the esophagus, leading to nausea. Estrogen levels also rise during pregnancy, and it can contribute to morning sickness. Estrogen can cause the stomach to contract, leading to nausea and vomiting.

Additionally, estrogen can stimulate the brain's vomiting center, making it more likely to experience morning sickness.

Below are the main causes of morning sickness.

  • HCG: During pregnancy, the hormone HCG helps the embryo grow, but it also affects the body's digestive system, leading to stomach upset and morning sickness due to its influence on blood sugar and digestive enzyme levels.
  • Progesterone: Progesterone is a hormone that helps the uterus grow and prepare for a pregnancy, but it can cause stomach problems and nausea, including morning sickness, due to its effect on the digestive system and the brain's nausea center.
  • Estrogen: During pregnancy, the levels of estrogen hormone in a woman's body increase dramatically, which can cause the stomach muscles to contract more often and intensely, leading to the nausea and vomiting associated with morning sickness.
  • Hormonal Changes: During pregnancy, hormonal changes cause the body to produce high levels of human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG), which can make the stomach feel queasy and lead to morning sickness.
  • Nutrient Deficiencies: Nutrient deficiencies, particularly low levels of B vitamins and iron, can cause morning sickness because they affect the body's ability to regulate and manage hormonal changes during pregnancy.
  • Food Sensitivities: Morning sickness can be caused by food sensitivities because the body reacts poorly to certain foods, releasing chemicals that trigger nausea and vomiting, which worsens in the morning when the body is more sensitive.

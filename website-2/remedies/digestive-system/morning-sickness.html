Morning Sickness: A Guide to Causes and Herbal Preparations

Morning sickness is a common symptom experienced by many pregnant women, characterized by nausea and vomiting, often at the worst time - first thing in the morning.

It can be so severe that it interferes with daily activities, making it difficult to eat and even keeping food down. The exact cause of morning sickness is not fully understood, but it's believed to be triggered by hormonal changes in the body, particularly the rise in human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG) levels, which can cause the stomach to contract and lead to nausea. Herbal remedies have been used for centuries to help alleviate morning sickness. Ginger, for example, has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe the stomach and reduce nausea. Peppermint oil, another popular remedy, can help calm the digestive system and ease stomach cramps.

Other herbs like raspberry leaf, chamomile, and lemon balm are also said to have a positive effect on morning sickness. These herbs can be consumed in various forms, such as teas, capsules, or even added to food and drinks. Drinking ginger tea, for instance, can help calm the stomach and reduce nausea. Some women also find relief by taking peppermint oil capsules or adding a few drops of peppermint oil to their water or food. Others prefer to make a soothing tea by steeping dried raspberry leaves or lemon balm in hot water.

Whatever the chosen remedy, many women have found that herbal treatments can provide much-needed relief from the discomfort of morning sickness.

What are the causes of morning sickness?

When you're pregnant, your body goes through a lot of changes.

Morning sickness, which can happen at any time of day, is caused by a combination of hormonal changes and nutrient deficiencies. One of the main causes is a hormone called Human Chorionic Gonadotropin, or HCG for short. This hormone helps the fertilized egg implant in the uterus and grows the embryo, but it also makes you feel queasy and tired. Another hormone that's to blame is Progesterone, which helps your uterus relax and get ready for the baby. Progesterone levels surge during pregnancy, and this can cause your stomach to feel upset and your body to feel tired.

Estrogen, another hormone that's important for pregnancy, can also contribute to morning sickness. As estrogen levels increase, it can make your stomach contract and feel queasy. Some people are also at risk for morning sickness if they're deficient in certain vitamins. Vitamin B6 is an important nutrient that helps your body convert food into energy, and if you don't have enough of it, you might feel queasy and tired. Vitamin B12 is also important for your body's energy levels, and a deficiency can make you feel weak and tired.

If you're not eating enough foods that are rich in these vitamins, or if your body is having trouble absorbing them, you might experience more severe morning sickness.

What herbs should you avoid if you have morning sickness?

If you're experiencing morning sickness, it's best to steer clear of Valeriana officinalis, also known as valerian root.

This herb is often used to calm the mind and body, but it can actually make you feel drowsy and sluggish, which can be the opposite of what you need when you're feeling queasy. Aristolochia clematitis, also known as birthwort, is another herb to avoid when you're pregnant. While it's sometimes used to ease digestive issues, it can actually cause harm to your kidneys and liver, which are already working hard to support your growing baby. Hydrastis canadensis, or goldenseal, contains a compound called berberine, which can be toxic to your unborn child.

It's also sometimes used to treat infections, but it can interact with other medications and make morning sickness worse. Citrus reticulata, which is a type of orange, might seem like a harmless herb, but it can cause stomach upset and make morning sickness even more uncomfortable. If you're already feeling nauseous, the last thing you need is a citrus-based herb that can irritate your stomach further. Piper nigrum, or black pepper, can also be a problem when you're pregnant. While it's often used as a spice in cooking, it contains a compound called piperine, which can stimulate the uterus and cause contractions.

This can be a concern, especially in the early stages of pregnancy.

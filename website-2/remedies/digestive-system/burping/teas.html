8 Herbal Teas For Burping

People who have bariatric surgery often struggle with burping.

This is because the surgery can affect the way food moves through their body, causing them to swallow more air. This excess air can lead to uncomfortable and embarrassing burps. For some, these burps can be so bad that they become anxious or self-conscious about eating in public. They may also experience digestive discomfort, nausea, and stomach pain. Burping can disrupt their daily lives, making it hard to enjoy meals with family and friends or even eat in front of their children. Bariatric patients may try various remedies to help alleviate their burping, but many of these solutions can be harsh on the stomach or have unpleasant side effects.

They may be advised to avoid certain foods or drinks that trigger burping, but these restrictions can be difficult to follow. Some may turn to over-the-counter medications, but these can have their own set of drawbacks. One natural alternative that has gained attention is herbal teas. These teas are made from plants that have been used for centuries to soothe digestive issues. When consumed as a warm, calming beverage, they can help to relax the stomach muscles and reduce gas and bloating. By promoting a sense of calm and relaxation, herbal teas can also help to reduce the frequency and severity of burps.

By incorporating these teas into their daily routine, bariatric patients may find relief from their burping and be able to enjoy their meals without discomfort.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have natural anti-inflammatory and carminative properties.

These properties help in reducing gas and inflammation in the digestive system, making it easier to pass gas and alleviate burping. The carminative action helps in relaxing the muscles in the digestive tract, allowing trapped gas to escape more efficiently. To use Zingiber officinale teas for burping, start by drinking a warm cup of ginger tea after meals, especially if you notice that you tend to burp after eating. You can also add a slice of fresh ginger to your tea for extra benefits.

When you take action, you can expect to feel relief from burping within a few minutes of drinking the tea. The tea may also help in reducing nausea and indigestion, which often accompany burping. However, if you experience persistent or severe burping, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional to rule out any underlying conditions that may require medical attention. It's also worth noting that drinking Zingiber officinale teas regularly may help in improving digestion and reducing symptoms of indigestion and nausea over time.

However, individual results may vary, and more research is needed to fully understand the effects of ginger tea on digestive health.

2. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum teas contains compounds that help alleviate burping.

The main culprits behind burping are gas and acid in the stomach, and Cinnamomum verum teas has properties that can tackle both issues. Additionally, Cinnamomum verum teas have a natural antacid effect, thanks to the presence of cinnamic acid, which can neutralize stomach acid and reduce heartburn. To use Cinnamomum verum teas to help with burping, steep one teaspoon of dried Cinnamomum verum bark in a cup of boiling water for five to seven minutes.

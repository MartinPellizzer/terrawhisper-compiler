8 Herbal Teas That Help with Burping in Bariatrics

People who have bariatric surgery often struggle with burping.

This is because the surgery can affect the way food moves through their body, causing them to swallow more air. This excess air can lead to uncomfortable and embarrassing burps. For some, these burps can be so bad that they become anxious or self-conscious about eating in public. They may also experience digestive discomfort, nausea, and stomach pain. Burping can disrupt their daily lives, making it hard to enjoy meals with family and friends or even eat in front of their children. Bariatric patients may try various remedies to help alleviate their burping, but many of these solutions can be harsh on the stomach or have unpleasant side effects.

They may be advised to avoid certain foods or drinks that trigger burping, but these restrictions can be difficult to follow. Some may turn to over-the-counter medications, but these can have their own set of drawbacks. One natural alternative that has gained attention is herbal teas. These teas are made from plants that have been used for centuries to soothe digestive issues. When consumed as a warm, calming beverage, they can help to relax the stomach muscles and reduce gas and bloating. By promoting a sense of calm and relaxation, herbal teas can also help to reduce the frequency and severity of burps.

By incorporating these teas into their daily routine, bariatric patients may find relief from their burping and be able to enjoy their meals without discomfort.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, have been used for centuries to help alleviate burping and discomfort after meals.

The key to its effectiveness lies in its active compounds, including gingerols and shogaols, which have natural anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds help to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing the likelihood of gas and air building up and causing burps. Additionally, ginger has natural carminative properties, which help to release trapped gas and reduce bloating. To use ginger tea for burping, start by steeping fresh ginger in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it after meals, especially if you've consumed foods that tend to cause gas, such as beans or cabbage. You can also add a squeeze of lemon juice or a drizzle of honey to make the tea more palatable. When you drink the tea, pay attention to how your body responds - if you notice a significant reduction in burping, it's likely working. You may also experience a sense of relief and comfort in your digestive system.

As you continue to use ginger tea, you may find that your burping becomes less frequent and less severe, and you're able to enjoy meals without the discomfort of excess gas.

2. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum teas, also known as Ceylon cinnamon, have been traditionally used to help with burping.

The teas contain a compound called cinnamaldehyde, which has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe the digestive system. Cinnamaldehyde also has antispasmodic properties, which can help to calm down the muscles in the digestive tract and prevent excessive gas production, leading to burping. Additionally, Cinnamomum verum teas contain other constituents like eugenol and linalool, which have antimicrobial properties that can help to reduce the growth of bad bacteria in the digestive system. To use Cinnamomum verum teas to help with burping, start by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried Ceylon cinnamon in a cup of hot water for 5-10 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it slowly, ideally after meals that may trigger burping. If you experience persistent or severe burping, consider combining Cinnamomum verum teas with other remedies like ginger or peppermint to enhance their effects. Drinking Cinnamomum verum teas may help to reduce burping by soothing the digestive system and calming down muscle spasms. You may notice a decrease in the frequency and severity of burping after a few days of regular consumption.

However, if your burping persists or is accompanied by other symptoms like abdominal pain or nausea, consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

Soothe infant burps with 7 natural tinctures

When a baby swallows air while feeding, it can lead to uncomfortable burping.

This happens when a baby drinks formula or breast milk too quickly, or when they are lying down after eating. Burping can cause a baby to be fussy, arch their back, and even spit up. It can be distressing for both the baby and the parents, and it can disrupt the baby's sleep and daily routine. Sometimes, a baby may have trouble burping because of a slow digestive system or because they are feeding too frequently. As a parent, it can be worrying to see your baby uncomfortable and in pain.

There are many things that parents can try to help their babies burp, such as holding them upside down, using a vibrating chair, or trying the "football hold.". However, some parents may find that these methods don't work, and they need to try something new. This is where herbal tinctures can come in. These tinctures are liquid extracts that are made from plants and can be added to the baby's food or water. They work by helping to ease digestion and calm the baby's stomach. By taking a small amount of the tincture, the baby's body can relax and let go of the trapped air, making it easier to burp and feel comfortable again.

The tinctures are made from gentle, natural ingredients that are safe for babies and can be a great alternative to medication.

These compounds help to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, allowing gas to pass through more easily and reducing the likelihood of burping. To use ginger tincture for burping, take 20-30 drops of the tincture in water after meals, especially if you have eaten spicy or fatty foods.

However, if you experience persistent or severe digestive issues, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

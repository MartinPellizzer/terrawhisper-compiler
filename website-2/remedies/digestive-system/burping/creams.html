1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which are known for their anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to relax the muscles in the digestive system, reducing the frequency and severity of burping. The oils present in the cream, such as ginger oil, have a calming effect on the digestive tract, allowing food to pass through more smoothly. The warming sensation caused by ginger's thermogenic properties can also help to increase digestion and reduce bloating.

As a result, Zingiber officinale creams can provide relief from excessive burping and promote a healthier digestive system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of dried Zingiber officinale root in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in the double boiler over low heat, stirring until smooth.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of dried Zingiber officinale root to the melted mixture and stir for 5 minutes.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool for 10 minutes. Then, strain it through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  5. Pour the cooled mixture into a container and store it in the fridge. Apply a small amount to the chest or abdomen as needed to help alleviate burping.

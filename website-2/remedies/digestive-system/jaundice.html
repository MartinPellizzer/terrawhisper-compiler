What are the causes of jaundice?

Jaundice is a medical condition where the skin and eyes turn yellow due to a buildup of a yellow pigment called bilirubin.

This happens when the liver can't remove bilirubin from the blood properly. There are several reasons why this can happen, and one main cause is viral infections that affect the liver. Hepatitis A is a viral infection caused by eating or drinking contaminated food and water. When someone with Hepatitis A virus (HAV) comes into contact with food, water, or surfaces, they can spread the virus to others. The virus attacks the liver, causing inflammation, and leading to jaundice. Hepatitis B is another viral infection caused by a virus that can be spread through blood, semen, and other bodily fluids. It can be passed from mother to child during childbirth, or through sharing needles or having unprotected sex. Once inside the body, the virus attacks the liver cells, causing damage and inflammation.

Hepatitis C is a viral infection caused by a virus that can be spread through sharing needles or having unprotected sex. The virus is usually spread through blood-to-blood contact, and it can also be passed from mother to child during childbirth. Hepatitis C virus (HCV) attacks the liver cells, causing scarring and inflammation. Hepatitis D is a viral infection that can only infect people who already have Hepatitis B. It's a rare form of hepatitis, but it's often seen in people who have a high risk of getting Hepatitis B, such as injecting drug users. Hepatitis D virus (HDV) attacks the liver cells, causing damage and inflammation. Hepatitis E is a viral infection caused by eating or drinking contaminated food and water. It's usually spread through contaminated water, and it's more common in areas with poor sanitation. Hepatitis E virus (HEV) attacks the liver cells, causing inflammation and jaundice.

All of these viral infections can cause jaundice by attacking the liver cells and preventing them from removing bilirubin from the blood.

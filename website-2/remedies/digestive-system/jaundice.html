What Are The Main Causes Of Jaundice?

Jaundice is a medical condition characterized by a yellowing of the skin and eyes due to high levels of bilirubin in the blood.

The main causes of jaundice can be divided into two categories: liver disorders and bile duct problems. Gilbert syndrome is a common cause of jaundice, where the liver produces more bilirubin than it can break down. This is usually due to a genetic variation that affects the enzyme responsible for breaking down bilirubin.

Hepatitis A and Hepatitis B are two types of liver infections caused by viral hepatitis. Hepatitis A is a highly contagious liver infection that spreads through contaminated food, water, or close contact with an infected person. It causes inflammation of the liver, leading to jaundice, fatigue, and other symptoms. Hepatitis B, on the other hand, is a chronic liver infection that can be spread through infected blood, bodily fluids, or from mother to child during birth.

If left untreated, Hepatitis B can cause long-term liver damage and increase the risk of liver cancer.

Below are the main causes of jaundice.

  • Gilbert syndrome: Gilbert syndrome is a cause of jaundice because it affects the liver's ability to break down bilirubin, a yellow pigment, resulting in its buildup and causing the skin and eyes to turn yellow.
  • Hepatitis A: Hepatitis A causes jaundice because the virus attacks the liver, leading to inflammation and scarring, which in turn blocks the liver's ability to remove bilirubin, a yellow pigment that builds up in the blood and causes the skin and eyes to turn yellow.
  • Hepatitis B: Hepatitis B is a cause of jaundice because the virus infects liver cells, causing inflammation and damage that leads to a buildup of bilirubin in the blood, which then turns the skin and eyes yellow.
  • Hepatitis C: Hepatitis C causes jaundice because the liver, which normally filters out waste and produces a substance called bile, becomes inflamed and unable to function properly, leading to a buildup of bile in the body that causes a yellowish color in the skin and eyes.
  • Hepatitis D: Hepatitis D is a cause of jaundice because it damages liver cells, causing a buildup of bilirubin, a yellow pigment, in the blood that makes the skin and eyes turn yellow.
  • Pancreatitis: Pancreatitis causes jaundice because the inflammation in the pancreas damages the bile ducts, which leads to a buildup of bile in the blood, turning the skin and eyes yellow.

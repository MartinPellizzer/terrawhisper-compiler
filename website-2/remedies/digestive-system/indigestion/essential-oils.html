1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains two main bioactive constituents: gingerol and shogaol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and alleviate indigestion symptoms. Gingerol and shogaol also have a calming effect on the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing spasms and cramping. Additionally, the oils have a warming effect that helps improve circulation and stimulate digestion, which can help ease nausea and discomfort.

The combination of these properties makes Zingiber officinale essential oils a potential remedy for indigestion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Ginger roots (Zingiber officinale)
  2. Wash the Ginger roots with water and pat them dry with a paper towel
  3. Using a juicer or grater, extract 2 tablespoons of fresh Ginger juice
  4. Mix the Ginger juice with 1 cup of carrier oil (like Coconut or Olive oil) in a small bowl
  5. Leave the mixture to sit for 2-3 days, shaking the bowl every day, before straining and storing the essential oil in a dark glass bottle

2. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains a compound called anethole, which is responsible for its digestive benefits.

Anethole has a relaxing effect on the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing spasms and cramps that can cause indigestion. Foeniculum vulgare essential oils also contains fenchone, which helps to stimulate digestion and improve the movement of food through the digestive system. The oil's anti-inflammatory properties, particularly due to its limonene content, help to reduce swelling and irritation in the digestive tract, further alleviating indigestion symptoms.

By combining these properties, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help to soothe and calm the digestive system, providing relief from indigestion.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Foeniculum vulgare seeds and 4 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) in a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the seeds and carrier oil in a saucepan, and heat it over low heat (150°F to 200°F) for 2 hours.
  3. After 2 hours, remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool down to room temperature.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the seeds.
  5. Store the Foeniculum vulgare essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use 5 to 10 drops as needed to help with indigestion.

3. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a compound called curcumin, a bioactive constituent that has potent anti-inflammatory properties.

Curcumin helps to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, which can alleviate indigestion symptoms such as bloating and discomfort. The oil also contains another bioactive compound called demethoxycurcumin, which has been shown to inhibit the production of digestive enzymes that can cause irritation and inflammation. The antioxidant properties of curcuma longa essential oils help to neutralize free radicals that can damage the digestive system and worsen indigestion symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the digestive system, curcuma longa essential oils can provide relief from indigestion and promote a healthy digestive process.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Curcuma longa roots and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil)
  2. Chop the Curcuma longa roots into small pieces and add them to a clean glass jar
  3. Pour 1 cup of carrier oil over the chopped roots and seal the jar
  4. Place the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another jar, and discard the solids

4. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains compounds like cinnamaldehyde and linalool, which have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

These properties help to soothe digestive issues like indigestion by reducing inflammation in the stomach and intestines. Cinnamaldehyde also has a calming effect on the muscles in the digestive tract, which can help to ease cramps and spasms that can be associated with indigestion. The antispasmodic properties of linalool can also help to relax the muscles in the stomach, reducing the severity of indigestion symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing digestive muscles, Cinnamomum verum essential oils can help to alleviate symptoms of indigestion.

  1. Gather 250g of Cinnamomum verum bark and 500ml of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Dry the Cinnamomum verum bark in a low-temperature oven (50°C) for 2 hours.
  3. Grind the dried Cinnamomum verum bark into a fine powder using a coffee grinder.
  4. Steep 50g of the ground Cinnamomum verum bark in 250ml of carrier oil in a glass jar for 2 weeks, shaking daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth and discard the solids. Store the Cinnamomum verum essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains licorice root's active compounds like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce irritation in the digestive tract. Flavonoids in licorice root oil have antioxidant properties, which protect the stomach lining from damage. Phenolic acids in licorice root oil have a soothing effect on the stomach, reducing inflammation and pain associated with indigestion.

The combination of these compounds in Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of indigestion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 2 cups of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Cut the roots into small pieces and put them in a clean glass jar.
  3. Cover the roots with the carrier oil and seal the jar. Let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the roots.
  5. Transfer the oil to a small dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a warm bath or mix with a carrier oil for massage, up to 3 times a day for indigestion relief.

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains a high concentration of menthol and menthone, two active constituents that help to ease indigestion.

The antispasmodic properties of menthol work to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing cramps and discomfort. Menthone, on the other hand, has anti-inflammatory properties that help to soothe and calm the digestive system. When ingested, mentha x piperita essential oils can also help to stimulate digestion and relieve nausea.

The combination of these properties makes mentha x piperita essential oils a natural remedy for indigestion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water in the pot and add the leaves. Bring to a boil then reduce heat to low.
  3. Let it simmer for 20-30 minutes. This helps release the essential oils from the leaves.
  4. Strain the mixture and discard the leaves. Transfer the liquid to a separate container.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 2-3 hours to allow the essential oils to separate and rise to the top.

7. Cuminum cyminum

Cuminum cyminum essential oils contains a compound called cuminaldehyde, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe the stomach.

This essential oil also contains limonene, a natural analgesic that reduces pain and discomfort in the digestive system. Cuminaldehyde in Cuminum cyminum essential oils has been shown to relax the muscles in the stomach, reducing cramps and spasms. Its antioxidant properties also help protect the stomach lining from damage caused by free radicals.

When inhaled or applied topically, Cuminum cyminum essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of indigestion by reducing inflammation and discomfort in the digestive system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cuminum cyminum seeds and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or sweet almond oil).
  2. Place 1/4 cup of Cuminum cyminum seeds in a small saucepan and heat it over low heat for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 10 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Discard the seeds and transfer the liquid to a clean glass bottle. Add 1/2 cup of carrier oil to the liquid and stir well.
  5. Store the essential oil in a cool, dark place and use 5-7 drops per serving to help with indigestion.

8. Coriandrum sativum

Coriandrum sativum essential oils contains linalool, geraniol, and limonene as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds contribute to the oil's carminative and anti-inflammatory properties. The carminative properties help to relieve bloating and gas, while the anti-inflammatory properties soothe the digestive system. The oil's anxiolytic properties also help to calm the nervous system, which can contribute to indigestion.

By reducing stress and inflammation in the digestive system, Coriandrum sativum essential oils can provide relief from indigestion symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Coriandrum sativum seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Heat 1 cup of water in a saucepan and add the Coriandrum sativum seeds. Simmer for 2 hours, stirring occasionally.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the seeds.
  4. Combine the strained liquid with the 2 cups of carrier oil in a blender or food processor. Blend until well mixed.
  5. Bottle the Coriandrum sativum essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops per serving for indigestion relief.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent indigestion from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent indigestion in some people.

Peppermint tea, in particular, can ease stomach discomfort and relax the muscles that can cause indigestion. Ginger tea is another option, as it has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe the digestive system.

These teas may be beneficial for people who experience occasional indigestion.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for indigestion every day?

Herbal teas for indigestion can be a helpful option, but consuming them daily might not be suitable for everyone.

Some herbs, like peppermint and ginger, are generally considered safe, but others, like licorice root, can be too much for long-term use.

It's essential to listen to your body and adjust your consumption accordingly.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in indigestion?

Herbal teas can start to help with indigestion within a few hours.

Some people notice relief after drinking a cup, while others may need to drink tea over a few days to see a difference.

The time it takes can vary depending on the type of tea and the severity of the indigestion.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for indigestion?

Drinking herbal tea for indigestion is most helpful after eating.

This is because it can help calm your stomach and reduce discomfort. Try sipping on a soothing tea, like peppermint or chamomile, about 30 minutes to an hour after lunch or dinner.

This way, it can aid in digestion and provide relief from indigestion symptoms.

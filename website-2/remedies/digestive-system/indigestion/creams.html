1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains a compound called gingerol, which is a key bioactive constituent that helps with indigestion.

Gingerol has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and pain in the digestive tract, making it easier to digest food. Zingiber officinale creams also contain another compound called shogaol, which stimulates digestive enzymes and improves digestion. These compounds work together to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, allowing for better movement of food through the system and reducing symptoms of indigestion.

By reducing inflammation and improving digestion, Zingiber officinale creams can help alleviate discomfort and promote a healthy digestive system.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of dried Zingiber officinale (ginger) root.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of dried ginger root to the melted mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture into a clean bowl, discarding the ginger root. Let it cool until it reaches 120°F to 130°F.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and harden completely. Store in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

2. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare creams contains fenchone and anethole, two active constituents that have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

These compounds help to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing cramping and spasms that can lead to indigestion. Foeniculum vulgare creams also have a soothing effect on the mucous membranes, reducing inflammation and irritation that can cause discomfort and pain. The anethole in Foeniculum vulgare creams has a carminative effect, helping to release trapped gas and reduce bloating.

By reducing inflammation, relaxing muscles, and releasing gas, Foeniculum vulgare creams can provide relief from indigestion symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Foeniculum vulgare (also known as fennel seeds) and 1 cup of coconut oil in a clean glass jar.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a saucepan over low heat for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Add the dried Foeniculum vulgare to the coconut oil and simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass container, discarding the solids.
  5. Let the mixture cool, then store it in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. Use 1-2 teaspoons of the cream as needed to relieve indigestion.

3. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains a compound called curcumin, which is its active constituent.

Curcumin has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help soothe the digestive system. It contains other compounds like demethoxycurcumin, bisdemethoxycurcumin, and turmerone, which have similar properties. These compounds work together to reduce inflammation and irritation in the stomach, making Curcuma longa creams effective in alleviating indigestion symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and irritation, Curcuma longa creams help to ease digestive discomfort and promote a healthy digestive system.

  1. Mix 2 cups of coconut oil and 1 cup of shea butter in a bowl until smooth.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder (Curcuma longa) and 1 tablespoon of beeswax to the bowl.
  3. Heat the mixture in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each heating, until the beeswax melts.
  4. Remove the bowl from heat and let it cool slightly. Stir in 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil and 1 teaspoon of peppermint essential oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify before use. Store in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling in the digestive tract. The saponins in Glycyrrhiza glabra creams also have a soothing effect on the stomach lining, protecting it from acid damage and relieving heartburn and indigestion symptoms. Glycyrrhizin, the main active component, helps calm muscle spasms in the digestive tract, allowing food to move through the digestive system smoothly.

By reducing inflammation and soothing the stomach lining, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams can provide relief from indigestion and other digestive issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots, 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Grind the dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots into a fine powder using a mortar and pestle.
  3. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth.
  4. Add the ground Glycyrrhiza glabra powder, shea butter, and vitamin E oil to the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a tin or jar and let it cool and harden before use as a cream for indigestion.

5. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum creams contains a compound called cinnamaldehyde, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Cinnamaldehyde has a spasmolytic effect, which helps to relax the muscles in the stomach and reduce inflammation. This property makes Cinnamomum verum creams effective in soothing indigestion by reducing spasms and inflammation in the stomach. Cinnamomum verum creams also contain linalool, a compound that has a calming effect on the stomach and helps to improve digestion.

By reducing inflammation and promoting digestion, Cinnamomum verum creams can provide relief from indigestion symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of Cinnamomum verum essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Stir until the beeswax is fully melted, then remove from heat.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of Cinnamomum verum essential oil to the melted mixture and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a glass jar and let it cool and solidify before use.

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains menthol and limonene as its active constituents.

These compounds have carminative properties, helping to relieve gas and reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. Menthol also has a cooling effect, which can provide quick relief from indigestion symptoms. The antispasmodic properties of menthol help to calm the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing cramps and discomfort associated with indigestion.

By reducing inflammation and calming muscle spasms, Mentha x piperita creams can help to alleviate indigestion symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of peppermint essential oil, and a small amount of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Once melted, remove from heat and let cool slightly. Stir in peppermint essential oil and vitamin E oil.
  4. Pour the mixture into a silicone candy mold or a small tin container. Let it cool and harden completely.
  5. Once hardened, remove from mold and store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks. Apply a small amount to the affected area as needed for indigestion relief.

Pimpinella anisum creams contains a compound called anethole, which is responsible for its therapeutic properties.

Anethole has a relaxing effect on the stomach muscles, helping to reduce spasms and relieve cramps. Regular use of Pimpinella anisum creams may help to calm the stomach and promote a sense of well-being.

8. Angelica archangelica

Angelica archangelica creams contains angelic acid, ligustilide, and acacetin as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help to soothe the digestive system and calm stomach muscles. The antispasmodic properties of ligustilide and acacetin specifically help to relax the muscles in the stomach and intestines, reducing cramping and discomfort. Angelic acid, on the other hand, has a direct effect on the stomach lining, reducing inflammation and irritation that can lead to indigestion.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing stomach muscles, Angelica archangelica creams can provide relief from indigestion and related symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup Angelica root, 1 cup coconut oil, 1/2 cup beeswax, 2 tablespoons vitamin E oil.
  2. Dry the Angelica root in a low oven (150°F) for 2 hours. Let it cool.
  3. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  4. Add the dried Angelica root and vitamin E oil to the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean glass jar and let it cool and harden. Store it in a cool, dark place.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent indigestion from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help ease indigestion.

Some teas, like peppermint, ginger, and chamomile, have natural soothing properties that can calm the stomach and reduce discomfort.

These teas work by relaxing the muscles and reducing inflammation, which can help alleviate indigestion symptoms.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for indigestion every day?

While herbal teas can help with indigestion, drinking them daily can have mixed effects.

Some teas, like peppermint, may help soothe stomach issues. However, others, like chamomile, can slow digestion.

It's also possible that relying on herbal teas daily can mask underlying digestive problems, so it's essential to listen to your body and adjust your consumption accordingly.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in indigestion?

Herbal teas can help with indigestion, but how long it takes to show results varies.

Some people notice relief in 30 minutes to an hour after drinking, while others may feel better after a few hours or even the next day.

It depends on the person and the tea they're using.

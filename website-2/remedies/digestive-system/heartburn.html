Heartburn Causes and Herbal Preparations for a Healthier Digestive System

Heartburn is a burning sensation in the chest and throat area, usually caused by stomach acid flowing back up into the esophagus.

If left untreated, heartburn can lead to serious complications like esophagitis, which is inflammation of the esophagus, and even esophageal strictures, which can cause difficulty swallowing. This is why it's essential to deal with heartburn promptly. Herbal remedies have been used for centuries to alleviate heartburn symptoms. Ginger (Zingiber officinale) has natural anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in the esophagus, providing relief from heartburn. Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) has been shown to coat the stomach lining, reducing acid production and inflammation.

Fennel (Foeniculum vulgare) seeds have antispasmodic properties, helping to relax the muscles in the digestive tract and prevent acid reflux. Herbal preparations like Fennel Decoction, made by steeping fennel seeds in hot water, can be an effective remedy for heartburn. Licorice Tincture, which is a concentrated extract of licorice root, can be taken as needed to provide quick relief from heartburn symptoms. Licorice Tea, made by steeping licorice root in hot water, is another soothing remedy for heartburn. In addition to these herbal preparations, it's also essential to make lifestyle changes to prevent heartburn. This includes eating smaller, more frequent meals, avoiding trigger foods like citrus fruits and spicy foods, and maintaining a healthy weight.

By combining these herbal remedies with lifestyle changes, it's possible to manage heartburn symptoms and prevent long-term damage to the esophagus.

What Are The Main Causes Of Heartburn?

Eating large meals can cause heartburn.

When you eat a big meal, it puts pressure on the lower esophageal sphincter, the muscle that separates your esophagus and stomach. This pressure can cause the sphincter to relax, allowing stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus and cause heartburn. Citrus fruits, such as oranges and lemons, are acidic and can trigger heartburn.

These fruits contain high amounts of citric acid, which can irritate the lining of the esophagus and make heartburn worse. Tomatoes, especially when cooked or processed, can also cause heartburn. Tomatoes are acidic, and when they are cooked or processed, their acidity increases, making them more likely to trigger heartburn.

Additionally, tomatoes contain a chemical called lycopene, which can relax the lower esophageal sphincter and allow stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus.

Below are the main causes of heartburn.

  • Eating Large Meals: Eating large meals causes heartburn because the stomach has to work harder to digest the food, which can push stomach acid up into the esophagus through the lower esophageal sphincter, a muscle that usually keeps the acid down.
  • Citrus Fruits: Citrus fruits can cause heartburn because their high acidity, especially from the citric acid in lemons and oranges, makes the stomach produce more acid to digest them, leading to an uncomfortable burning sensation in the chest.
  • Tomatoes: Tomatoes cause heartburn because they contain a chemical called citric acid, which can irritate the lining of the stomach and trigger the burning sensation of heartburn.
  • Chocolate: Chocolate can cause heartburn because it contains a type of sugar called theobromine, which can make the muscles in the esophagus relax too much, allowing stomach acid to flow back up into the chest.
  • Caffeine: Caffeine can cause heartburn because it relaxes the muscles in the lower part of the esophagus, allowing stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus and irritate it.
  • Smoking: Smoking weakens the lower esophageal sphincter, allowing stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus and cause heartburn.
  • Obesity: Obesity is a cause of heartburn because the extra fat around the stomach puts pressure on the lower esophageal sphincter, causing stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus and cause discomfort.

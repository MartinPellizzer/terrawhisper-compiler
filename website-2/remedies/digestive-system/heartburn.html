Heartburn and Digestive Health: Medicinal Herbs and Preparations

What are the causes of heartburn?

Heartburn is a burning sensation in the chest, and it's often caused by stomach acid backing up into the esophagus.

Several things can trigger this uncomfortable feeling. One common cause is overeating, which puts extra pressure on the stomach, causing the acid to flow up into the esophagus. Lying down after eating is another culprit, as it allows the stomach acid to easily flow back up into the esophagus.

Some foods can also trigger heartburn, and citrus fruits like oranges and lemons are among them. The acidity in these fruits can weaken the lower esophageal sphincter, the muscle that separates the esophagus and the stomach, allowing the acid to flow back up. Tomato products, such as pasta sauce and pizza sauce, are also heartburn triggers because of their high acidity.

Chocolate, especially dark chocolate, can relax the lower esophageal sphincter, allowing the acid to flow back up into the esophagus, making it another trigger for heartburn.

What Are The Main Causes Of Bloating?

Bloating occurs when gas builds up in the digestive system.

There are several main causes of bloating. Food intolerance is one cause, where the body struggles to break down certain foods, such as lactose in milk or gluten in bread. This leads to undigested food particles remaining in the gut, where they are fermented by bacteria, producing gas.

Another cause is fructose malabsorption, where the body has trouble absorbing the sugar fructose, often found in fruits, honey, and some vegetables. This leads to fructose remaining in the gut, where it's fermented by bacteria, causing bloating. Small Intestine Bacterial Overgrowth (SIBO) is also a main cause of bloating. In SIBO, bacteria normally found in the large intestine overgrow in the small intestine, where they ferment food particles and produce gas.

This leads to bloating, discomfort, and other symptoms.

Below are the main causes of bloating.

  • Food Intolerance: When you eat foods your body can't digest, like dairy or gluten, it breaks down into gas, which gets trapped inside your tummy, causing bloating.
  • Fructose Malabsorption: Fructose Malabsorption causes bloating because the body is unable to properly absorb fructose, a type of sugar found in many foods, leading to undigested fructose in the gut, where it ferments and produces gas.
  • SIBO: SIBO (Small Intestine Bacterial Overgrowth) is a cause of bloating because the overgrown bacteria in the small intestine break down food too quickly, producing excess gas that causes the stomach to swell and feel bloated.
  • Constipation: When constipation occurs, the body takes longer than usual to pass stool, causing the digestive system to release more gas, which then builds up and causes bloating.
  • Hypothyroidism: Hypothyroidism is a cause of bloating because the thyroid gland's inability to produce enough hormones slows down digestion, allowing undigested food to ferment in the gut and produce gas.

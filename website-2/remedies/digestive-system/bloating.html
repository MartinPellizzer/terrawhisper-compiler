The Causes, Remedies, and Herbal Solutions for Bloating

What are the causes of bloating?

Bloating happens when your stomach gets too full or expands with air or gas.

There are several reasons why this might happen. Swallowing air, for example, can be a big contributor. When you eat or drink too quickly, or chew gum, you swallow more air than usual. This air gets trapped in your stomach and can cause bloating. Certain foods can also be to blame. Beans, for instance, are high in a type of sugar that's not fully digested in your stomach.

This undigested sugar gets broken down by bacteria in your gut, producing gas. As a result, your stomach expands, and you feel bloated. Gas itself can be a cause of bloating. Your body naturally produces gas as it breaks down food. However, if you have trouble digesting certain foods, like broccoli or cabbage, you might swallow more air or experience more gas production. This is because these foods contain sugars that are hard for your body to digest. When they're broken down, they release gas, which gets trapped in your stomach and causes bloating.

So, it's not just one thing that causes bloating – it's a combination of swallowing air, eating certain foods, and the way your body naturally processes gas.

Decoctions are good for bloating because they can help ease digestive issues caused by inflammation in the gut. For example, a decoction made from ginger can reduce inflammation and ease bloating.

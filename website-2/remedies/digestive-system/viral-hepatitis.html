Causes and Herbal Preparations of Viral Hepatitis: A Comprehensive Guide

What are the causes of viral hepatitis?

Viral hepatitis is caused by different viruses.

There are five main types: HCV, HBV, HDV, HEV, and HGV. HCV (Hepatitis C Virus) is spread through blood contact. This can happen through sharing needles, getting a tattoo with a dirty needle, or having sex with someone who's infected. HCV can also be spread from mother to child during childbirth. HBV (Hepatitis B Virus) is spread through body fluids like blood, semen, and saliva. It can be spread through sex, sharing needles, or getting a tattoo with a dirty needle. HBV can also be spread from mother to child during childbirth.

HDV (Hepatitis D Virus) is a combination of HBV and HDV. This happens when someone who's infected with HBV also gets infected with HDV. HDV makes HBV worse and can cause more severe liver damage. HEV (Hepatitis E Virus) is usually spread through contaminated food and water. This can happen when someone drinks water from a polluted source or eats food that's been prepared with contaminated water. HEV is usually not serious, but it can be deadly in people with weakened immune systems. HGV (Hepatitis G Virus) is less common and its spread is not well understood.

It's thought to be spread through blood contact, but more research is needed to confirm this.

Using certain herbs for viral hepatitis can be very beneficial.

Additionally, they can help improve liver function and promote the production of new liver cells, which can replace damaged ones. Some of these herbs have antiviral properties, which means they can directly target and reduce the viral load in the body. This can lead to a faster recovery and a lower risk of complications.

Furthermore, these herbs are often natural and gentle on the body, which can be especially important for people who are sensitive to medications or have other health conditions.

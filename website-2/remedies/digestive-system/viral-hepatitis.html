What Are The Main Causes Of Viral Hepatitis?

Viral hepatitis is a disease caused by different types of viruses.

There are several causes of viral hepatitis. One cause of viral hepatitis is HCV (Hepatitis C Virus). HCV is spread through blood-to-blood contact, usually by sharing needles or getting a tattoo with unsterilized equipment. This can also happen during blood transfusions if the blood is not properly screened. HCV can live outside the body for up to 3 weeks, making it a highly infectious virus.

Another cause of viral hepatitis is HBV (Hepatitis B Virus). HBV is spread through bodily fluids like blood and semen. This can happen during sex with an infected person, through sharing needles or other equipment, or from mother to child during birth if the mother is infected. HIV (HIV Virus) is not a direct cause of viral hepatitis, but it can increase the risk of developing HBV and HCV. People with HIV are more likely to contract these viruses because their immune system is weakened.

Additionally, people with HIV may be more likely to engage in high-risk behaviors that increase their chance of getting infected with these viruses.

Below are the main causes of viral hepatitis.

  • HCV: Hepatitis C virus (HCV) is a cause of viral hepatitis because it directly infects liver cells, causing inflammation and damage that leads to liver disease and potentially severe health problems.
  • HBV: HBV (Hepatitis B virus) is a cause of viral hepatitis because it directly attacks the liver cells, causing inflammation and damage that leads to symptoms such as fatigue, nausea, and yellowing of the skin.
  • HIV: HIV, the virus that causes AIDS, can lead to viral hepatitis when the immune system is weakened, making it easier for the hepatitis virus to replicate and cause infection.
  • HGV: HGV (Hepatitis G virus) is a cause of viral hepatitis because it infects liver cells and causes inflammation, which can lead to liver damage and disease if left untreated.
  • HDV: HDV is a cause of viral hepatitis because it needs the hepatitis B virus (HBV) to exist and replicate, attaching itself to HBV and making the body's immune system fight a double threat of both viruses.

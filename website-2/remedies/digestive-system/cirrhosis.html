Cirrhosis Causes and Prevention with Medicinal Herbs and Herbs

What are the causes of cirrhosis?

Cirrhosis is a serious liver disease that can be caused by several factors.

One major cause is alcoholism, where excessive drinking damages liver cells over time, leading to scarring and inflammation. This is because alcohol can cause the liver to work harder, leading to the buildup of toxins, which can damage the liver's delicate balance. Another cause of cirrhosis is viral infections, such as Hepatitis B and Hepatitis C. These viruses can directly infect liver cells, causing inflammation and scarring. Hepatitis B is usually spread through bodily fluids, like blood and semen, while Hepatitis C is often spread through blood-to-blood contact, such as sharing needles or getting a tattoo with unsterilized equipment.

Nonalcoholic Steatohepatitis (NASH) is another common cause of cirrhosis. This condition occurs when fat builds up in liver cells, causing inflammation and scarring. NASH is often linked to obesity, diabetes, and a high-fat diet, which can lead to the accumulation of fat in the liver. Lastly, autoimmune Hepatitis is a rare but serious cause of cirrhosis. In this condition, the immune system mistakenly attacks the liver cells, causing inflammation and scarring.

This can be triggered by a genetic predisposition, environmental factors, or an underlying medical condition.

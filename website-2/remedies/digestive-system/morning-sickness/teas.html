1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains the active constituents gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help to calm the digestive system.

These compounds also have a sedative effect on the nervous system, which can help to reduce nausea and vomiting. The anti-inflammatory properties of gingerols and shogaols can also help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, which is often a contributing factor to morning sickness. Additionally, ginger has a direct effect on the stomach, helping to speed up digestion and reduce feelings of queasiness.

By consuming Zingiber officinale teas, women experiencing morning sickness may find relief from symptoms due to the combination of these properties.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger root and 1 cup of water.
  2. Peel and slice 1 tablespoon of ginger root into small pieces.
  3. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot, then reduce heat to low.
  4. Add sliced ginger to the pot and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup and drink it warm, 1-2 times a day.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate morning sickness symptoms. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, has been shown to have a soothing effect on the stomach and intestines, reducing nausea and vomiting. The flavonoids present in Glycyrrhiza glabra teas also have a calming effect on the body, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety that often accompany morning sickness.

Drinking Glycyrrhiza glabra teas may help to regulate the digestive system and alleviate the symptoms of morning sickness.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, 1 cup of water, and a tea infuser or strainer.
  2. Boil the water in a pot or kettle and let it cool for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Add the dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root to the tea infuser or strainer and place it in the pot with the hot water.
  4. Steep the tea for 5-7 minutes, then remove the root and discard it.
  5. Drink the tea warm or at room temperature, up to 2-3 times a day as needed to help alleviate morning sickness.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas contains a key compound called anethole, which gives it its distinctive flavor and aroma.

This tea is rich in volatile oils, including limonene and fenchone, that have anti-inflammatory properties. When consumed, the fennel's essential oils can help to ease digestive discomfort and relieve nausea by reducing inflammation in the stomach and intestines. Fennel tea's carminative properties also help to expel trapped gas and reduce bloating, providing relief from morning sickness symptoms.

By alleviating stomach upset and promoting a sense of calm, fennel tea can be a helpful addition to a morning routine for pregnant women experiencing nausea.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Foeniculum vulgare (fennel) leaves, 1 cup of water, and a saucepan.
  2. Wash the fennel leaves with cold water, then chop them into small pieces.
  3. Add the chopped fennel leaves and 1 cup of water to the saucepan.
  4. Heat the mixture over low heat for 10-15 minutes, then let it cool.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup, discard the solids, and drink 1-2 cups as needed to relieve morning sickness.

4. Angelica archangelica

Angelica archangelica teas contains bioactive constituents like ferulic acid, isoferulic acid, and sesquiterpenes.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help to reduce nausea and vomiting associated with morning sickness. Ferulic acid, in particular, has been shown to have a sedative effect on the stomach, which can help to alleviate nausea and discomfort. Additionally, Angelica archangelica teas have been traditionally used to calm the digestive system and ease digestive issues, making it a potential remedy for morning sickness.

The anti-inflammatory properties of sesquiterpenes may also help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, further alleviating symptoms of morning sickness.

  1. Get 1 cup of fresh Angelica archangelica leaves and flowers.
  2. Use a tablespoon and measure out 1 tablespoon of dried Angelica archangelica root.
  3. Combine the fresh leaves, flowers, and dried root in a saucepan.
  4. Add 2 cups of boiling water to the saucepan and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink 1/2 cup 3-4 times a day to help with morning sickness.

5. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla teas contains apigenin and luteolin, which are flavonoid compounds with anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties can help to reduce nausea and vomiting associated with morning sickness. Apigenin has a sedative effect on the stomach, while luteolin helps to relax the muscles, making it easier to digest food. The antispasmodic properties of Matricaria chamomilla teas can also help to calm the stomach and prevent it from cramping, which can contribute to morning sickness.

By soothing the stomach and relaxing the muscles, Matricaria chamomilla teas can provide relief from morning sickness symptoms.

  1. Get 1 cup of boiling water and 1 tablespoon of dried Matricaria chamomilla flowers.
  2. Let the flowers steep in the hot water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a cup.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) to taste.
  5. Drink the tea slowly and comfortably, ideally before meals to help with morning sickness.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains several bioactive constituents, including linalool and linalyl acetate, that have been shown to help alleviate morning sickness.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help to reduce nausea and vomiting. The calming effects of linalool and linalyl acetate can also help to soothe the stomach and reduce symptoms of morning sickness. Additionally, the teas contain apigenin, a flavonoid that has been found to have antiemetic effects, helping to prevent vomiting.

Drinking Lavandula angustifolia tea may provide relief from morning sickness symptoms due to the combination of these bioactive constituents and their properties.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers or 2 teaspoons of dried flowers.

  4. Strain the mixture into a cup and discard the flowers.
  5. Drink the tea as needed to help with morning sickness, in moderation (1 cup, 2-3 times a day).

7. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce nausea and vomiting associated with morning sickness. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba, particularly quercetin, have antioxidant properties that protect the body from oxidative stress, which can contribute to morning sickness symptoms. The terpenoids, including bilobalide, have anti-emetic properties that can help alleviate nausea and vomiting.

Drinking Ginkgo biloba tea may help reduce morning sickness symptoms by regulating blood flow and improving circulation, which can help alleviate nausea and vomiting.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent morning sickness from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help ease morning sickness symptoms.

Peppermint tea, in particular, has been known to calm the stomach and reduce nausea. Ginger tea is also a popular choice, as ginger is a natural anti-inflammatory that can help alleviate queasy feelings.

Some women find that sipping on a warm cup of herbal tea helps them feel better in the morning.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for morning sickness every day?

While herbal teas can help with morning sickness, drinking them every day can be a concern.

Some herbs, like pennyroyal and blue cohosh, can be too strong and cause problems. It's also possible to get too much of a good thing from other herbs like ginger and peppermint.

It's best to use them in moderation.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in morning sickness?

Herbal teas can help with morning sickness, but it's different for everyone.

Some people notice a difference after one or two cups, while others may need a few days to a week.

It's usually a gradual feeling of relief, with symptoms easing up as you drink more tea.

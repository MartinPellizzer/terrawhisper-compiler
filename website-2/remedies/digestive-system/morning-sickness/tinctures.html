New moms with morning sickness: 10 herbal remedies to ease symptoms

Morning sickness is a common problem for new moms, especially during the first trimester of pregnancy.

Hormonal changes in the body cause nausea and vomiting, making everyday activities like eating, working, and taking care of the home a challenge. This can be frustrating and exhausting, affecting a new mom's mood, energy levels, and ability to bond with her baby. Simple tasks like getting out of bed, cooking meals, and caring for her baby become overwhelming, and she may feel guilty for not being able to enjoy this special time. She may also experience weight loss, dehydration, and fatigue, which can lead to other health issues. New moms often try various remedies to alleviate morning sickness, but many are ineffective or have unpleasant side effects.

Some may feel forced to take time off work, leave their jobs, or rely on others for help, which can be financially and emotionally straining. This can also impact her relationships with her partner, family, and friends, as she feels like a burden to them. Herbal tinctures are a natural and gentle way to treat morning sickness. These liquid extracts of plants have been used for centuries to soothe the stomach and reduce nausea. They are often made from a combination of plants that work together to calm the digestive system and ease symptoms. When taken correctly, herbal tinctures can provide quick relief from morning sickness, allowing new moms to feel better and enjoy their pregnancy.

They are also a safe alternative to prescription medications, which can have harsh side effects.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures, also known as ginger tincture, have been used for centuries to alleviate morning sickness in pregnant women.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, have anti-inflammatory and digestive properties that help to soothe the stomach and reduce nausea. These compounds work by slowing down gastric emptying, which can help to prevent the stomach from becoming too empty and causing nausea. Additionally, ginger has a natural anti-vomiting effect, which can help to reduce the frequency and severity of vomiting episodes. If you're experiencing morning sickness and considering trying ginger tincture, start by taking a small dose of 5-10 drops in water or tea, 2-3 times a day. You can gradually increase the dose if needed.

It's essential to choose a high-quality tincture that is made from organic ginger and has no added fillers or preservatives. You can take the tincture at any time of the day, but it's often more effective when taken in the morning, just before meals, or before bedtime. Taking ginger tincture may lead to a reduction in the frequency and severity of morning sickness symptoms. You may experience a decrease in nausea and vomiting, as well as improved digestion and appetite. Some women may also notice an improvement in their overall well-being and energy levels. However, it's essential to note that ginger tincture may not completely eliminate morning sickness, and you may still experience some symptoms.

It's also crucial to consult with your healthcare provider before taking any new supplements, especially during pregnancy.

10 Herbal Tinctures For Morning Sickness

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains gingerols and shogaols, two bioactive constituents that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help to reduce nausea and vomiting by inhibiting the release of chemical signals that trigger these symptoms. Gingerols and shogaols also have a stabilizing effect on the digestive system, which can help to alleviate morning sickness caused by hormonal changes during pregnancy. The anti-inflammatory properties of Zingiber officinale tinctures may also help to reduce inflammation in the stomach and alleviate symptoms of morning sickness.

Regular consumption of Zingiber officinale tinctures may help to alleviate morning sickness and improve overall digestion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Ginger root and 2 cups of vodka or 80% ethanol.
  2. Chop the Ginger root into small pieces and put them in a clean glass jar.
  3. Add 2 cups of vodka or 80% ethanol to the jar, making sure that the Ginger is completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the Ginger solids.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin, and licoricidins, isoflavonoids, and other bioactive compounds that have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

These properties help to soothe and relax the stomach muscles, reducing nausea and vomiting associated with morning sickness. Glycyrrhizin also has a mild sedative effect, which can help to calm the mind and body, reducing the severity of morning sickness symptoms. Additionally, licoricidins have been shown to have antihistamine properties, which can help to alleviate the histamine-related symptoms that often accompany morning sickness.

By using Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures, some women have experienced relief from morning sickness symptoms, allowing them to continue their daily activities with greater ease.

  1. Gather 250ml of 80% ethanol (vodka or rum work too), 100g of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the dried root and ethanol in the glass jar. Make sure the root is fully covered.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dropper bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Take 10-20 drops, 2-3 times a day, as needed.

3. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis tinctures contains alkaloids like quinine, cinchonine, and cinchonidine.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that help to ease muscle cramps and spasms in the digestive tract. Quinine, in particular, is known to reduce inflammation and alleviate nausea. The antispasmodic properties of cinchonine and cinchonidine help to relax the smooth muscles in the stomach, reducing symptoms of morning sickness.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing stomach muscles, Cinchona officinalis tinctures may help to alleviate morning sickness symptoms.

  1. Get 250 grams of dried Cinchona officinalis bark and chop it into small pieces.
  2. Combine 250 grams of chopped bark with 1 cup of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and take 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, as needed.

4. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contains flavonoids, bilobalide, and ginkgolides as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that may help alleviate morning sickness symptoms. The flavonoids in ginkgo biloba tincture have a positive effect on blood vessels, which can improve circulation and reduce nausea. The bilobalide and ginkgolides also have a relaxing effect on the muscles and the nervous system, which can help to calm the stomach and reduce vomiting.

The combination of these properties in ginkgo biloba tincture can provide relief from morning sickness and its associated symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the Ginkgo biloba leaves to the jar and pour the vodka over them. Make sure the leaves are completely covered.
  3. Close the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Use 10-15 drops of the tincture in water or juice 3 times a day to help relieve morning sickness symptoms.

5. Avena sativa

Avena sativa tinctures contains the bioactive constituents avenacosides and ferulic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help to soothe digestive issues and reduce nausea, making it a potential natural remedy for morning sickness. The avenacosides in Avena sativa tinctures also have a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety that often accompany morning sickness. Additionally, the ferulic acid in Avena sativa tinctures has been shown to have anti-nausea effects by blocking the action of serotonin, a chemical that can trigger nausea.

By reducing nausea and stress, Avena sativa tinctures may provide relief from morning sickness symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Avena sativa dried roots, 2 cups of vodka (95% or higher), and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Place the Avena sativa dried roots in the glass jar, leaving 1 inch of space at the top.
  3. Pour 2 cups of vodka over the Avena sativa roots, making sure the roots are completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids and reserving the liquid tincture.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent morning sickness from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help ease morning sickness symptoms.

Ginger tea, in particular, has been known to calm the stomach. Peppermint and chamomile teas also have soothing effects. Some women find that sipping on these teas before getting out of bed helps reduce nausea and discomfort.

It's worth trying, especially during the first trimester.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for morning sickness every day?

Herbal teas can be a helpful way to manage morning sickness.

However, it's a good idea to limit your consumption to a few cups a day. Drinking too much can cause stomach upset and interact with other medications.

It's also essential to choose teas from reputable sources to ensure their quality and safety.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in morning sickness?

Some people find relief from morning sickness with herbal teas after a few hours.

Ginger tea is often used as it may help calm the stomach. Peppermint tea can also be soothing.

Drinking a cup of herbal tea when you wake up or as soon as you feel queasy might help ease symptoms.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for morning sickness?

Drinking herbal tea in the morning can help ease morning sickness.

Try sipping on a soothing cup of tea as soon as you wake up or about an hour before breakfast. This can help calm your stomach and give you a gentle boost to start your day.

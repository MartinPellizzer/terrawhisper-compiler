9 Herbal Essential Oils For Morning Sickness

Herbal essential oils can be a great help for morning sickness.

Morning sickness is caused by hormonal changes in the body, and herbal teas can relieve it by soothing the digestive system and reducing nausea. For example, ginger (Zingiber officinale) has properties that can slow down digestion and prevent vomiting.

When you use herbal teas or essential oils made from these herbs, you may find that you can eat and drink without feeling queasy, which is a big relief if you're experiencing morning sickness.

Using herbal essential oils or drinking herbal teas can also give you more energy and make you feel more like yourself, which is especially important when you're already feeling unwell.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Gingerol has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce nausea and vomiting associated with morning sickness. The essential oil also contains zingerone, a compound that has been shown to have anti-emetic properties, helping to alleviate nausea and dizziness. The warm, spicy scent of ginger essential oil can also help stimulate digestion and alleviate queasiness.

By inhaling ginger essential oil, pregnant women may experience relief from morning sickness due to its ability to calm the digestive system and reduce inflammation.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Ginger roots and 1 cup of carrier oil like Coconut or Jojoba oil.
  2. Wash and peel the Ginger roots, then chop them into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped Ginger with 1 cup of carrier oil in a clean glass jar, making sure the Ginger is fully covered.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container, discarding the solids and keeping the oil.

2. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains a combination of active constituents, including limonene, anethole, and fenchone.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help alleviate morning sickness symptoms. Limonene, in particular, has been shown to have a positive effect on digestion and can help reduce nausea and vomiting. Anethole, on the other hand, has a calming effect on the stomach and can help regulate digestive enzymes.

By reducing inflammation and promoting digestive balance, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils may help alleviate morning sickness symptoms, providing relief for pregnant women.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds, a clean glass jar with a lid, a coffee filter, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Heat 2 cups of water in a saucepan over low heat. Add the Foeniculum vulgare seeds and let them steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  3. Strain the mixture through a coffee filter into the glass jar. Discard the seeds and reserve the liquid.
  4. Combine 1/4 cup of the reserved liquid with 1 cup of carrier oil in a separate container. Stir well.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the container daily. Strain the liquid through a coffee filter and discard the solids. Your Foeniculum vulgare essential oil is now ready for use.

3. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains limonene, menthone, and menthol as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate morning sickness symptoms. Menthone and menthol have a calming effect on the digestive system, reducing nausea and vomiting. Limonene has a sedative property, which can help regulate hormonal changes during pregnancy and reduce morning sickness.

By inhaling Mentha x piperita essential oils or applying it topically, pregnant women may experience relief from morning sickness symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh mint leaves (Mentha x piperita), 1 cup of water, and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Wash the mint leaves with cold water, then gently pat them dry with a clean cloth.
  3. Combine the mint leaves and water in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat (100°F - 150°F) for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into the glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the mixture sit at room temperature for 2-3 days to allow the essential oil to separate and rise to the top.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains a high concentration of 1,8-cineole, camphor, and bornyl acetate, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

These compounds help to ease morning sickness by reducing inflammation in the stomach and alleviating nausea and vomiting. The anti-inflammatory effects of 1,8-cineole also help to calm digestive issues and reduce cramps, making it easier to manage morning sickness. Additionally, the antispasmodic properties of camphor and bornyl acetate help to relax the muscles in the stomach, reducing spasms and cramps that can exacerbate morning sickness.

By using Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils, individuals can experience relief from morning sickness due to its ability to calm the digestive system and reduce symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan. Heat the water over medium heat.
  2. Reduce heat to low and let the mixture simmer for 30 minutes. This helps release the plant's oils.
  3. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of a carrier oil like jojoba oil to the liquid. Stir well.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 days in a cool, dark place to allow the essential oil to separate and settle.

5. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids and terpenoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce nausea and vomiting. The flavonoids present in ginkgo biloba essential oils, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to have a positive effect on digestion and alleviate morning sickness symptoms. The terpenoids, including bilobalide and ginkgolides, also contribute to the oil's anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce nausea and vomiting.

By using ginkgo biloba essential oils, pregnant women may experience relief from morning sickness symptoms, thanks to its bioactive constituents and medicinal properties.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Ginkgo biloba leaves, a clean glass jar with a lid, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Chop the Ginkgo biloba leaves finely and put them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour 2 cups of carrier oil over the Ginkgo biloba leaves and close the jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every few days.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a new glass jar, and discard the solids.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are responsible for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce nausea and vomiting by soothing the stomach and relaxing the muscles. The essential oil also contains beta-caryophyllene, which has anti-nausea and anti-inflammatory effects, further aiding in the relief of morning sickness symptoms. The calming effects of lavender oil can also help to reduce stress and anxiety, common triggers for morning sickness.

By inhaling or applying Lavandula angustifolia essential oils to the skin, pregnant women can potentially find relief from morning sickness symptoms.

  1. Gather materials: 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers, a glass jar, a saucepan, a cheesecloth or a coffee filter, and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Combine the fresh flowers and 2 cups of water in the saucepan. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth or coffee filter into the glass jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to the dark glass bottle. Add 1 tablespoon of vodka or glycerin to help preserve the oil.
  5. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the bottle every day. After 2-3 weeks, strain and discard the solids, then use the essential oil as needed for morning sickness.

7. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains compounds like linalool and cinnamaldehyde.

These active constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to soothe and calm the digestive system. The oils' ability to reduce nausea and vomiting is thought to be due to its action on the vagus nerve, a key regulator of gut function. Cinnamomum verum essential oils also has a sedative effect, which can help alleviate morning sickness symptoms by promoting relaxation and reducing stress.

When inhaled, the oils' active constituents can quickly reach the brain, where they can help to regulate digestion and alleviate symptoms of morning sickness.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Cinnamomum verum leaves and 1 cup of vodka in a clean glass container.
  2. Combine the Cinnamomum verum leaves and vodka in the container. Make sure the leaves are completely covered by the vodka.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the container every day.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a new container. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Cinnamomum verum essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for morning sickness relief.

8. Cinnamomum zeylanicum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum essential oils contains a compound called cinnamaldehyde, which is responsible for its properties.

This essential oil has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce nausea and vomiting associated with morning sickness. Cinnamaldehyde also has a strong affinity for the digestive system, where it can help to calm the stomach and relieve queasiness. Additionally, the oil's expectorant properties can help to reduce inflammation and congestion in the body, further alleviating symptoms of morning sickness.

The combination of these properties makes Cinnamomum zeylanicum essential oils a potential natural remedy for morning sickness.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Cinnamomum zeylanicum bark, a 1-cup water, a saucepan, a strainer, and a clean glass bottle.
  2. Combine the Cinnamomum zeylanicum bark and water in the saucepan. Heat over low heat for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through the strainer into the glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  4. Let the mixture sit for 2 hours to allow the oil to separate from the water.
  5. Pour the oil from the top of the liquid into a separate container, leaving behind the water. Use as needed for morning sickness relief.

9. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene and beta-pinene, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

These properties can help to alleviate the nausea and cramping associated with morning sickness by reducing inflammation in the digestive tract and relaxing the uterine muscles. Limonene also has a natural analgesic effect, which can help to reduce pain and discomfort. Additionally, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety that can exacerbate morning sickness.

By inhaling or applying Cymbopogon citratus essential oils topically, women experiencing morning sickness may find relief from symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Cymbopogon citratus leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a glass jar and add 1 tablespoon of vodka (optional, as a preservative). Store it in the fridge.
  5. To use, mix 5-7 drops of the essential oil with 1 cup of water and drink it as needed to help with morning sickness.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in morning sickness?

Herbal teas can help with morning sickness, but it's hard to say exactly how long it takes to feel better.

Some people notice relief within a few hours after drinking the tea, while others may need to try it for a few days to see improvement.

Everyone's body is different, so it's trial and error.

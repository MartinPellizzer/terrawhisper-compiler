1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiemetic effects, helping to alleviate nausea and vomiting. Gingerols, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the release of serotonin, a hormone that can cause stomach upset. Shogaols, on the other hand, have been found to have a direct effect on the digestive system, reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation of the stomach muscles.

By consuming Zingiber officinale creams, pregnant women may experience relief from morning sickness due to the soothing and calming effects of its active compounds.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of Zingiber officinale powder in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in the double boiler over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the double boiler from heat and stir in the Zingiber officinale powder until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container, let it cool, and store it in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

2. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare creams contains compounds like estragole, limonene, and anethole, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These active constituents have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects, which can help alleviate the stomach cramps and nausea associated with morning sickness. Foeniculum vulgare creams also contain vitamins and minerals like vitamin B6, which plays a crucial role in regulating nausea and vomiting. The anti-inflammatory properties of fennel's compounds can also help soothe digestive issues, reducing the discomfort caused by morning sickness.

The antispasmodic effects of fennel's compounds can help relax the smooth muscles in the stomach, reducing cramping and discomfort associated with morning sickness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Foeniculum vulgare (fennel seeds) and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Heat 1 cup of carrier oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add 1 cup of dried Foeniculum vulgare seeds to the heated oil and let it steep for 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the Foeniculum vulgare cream in a glass jar and apply 1-2 teaspoons as needed to help alleviate morning sickness symptoms.

3. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains menthol and menthone, which are active constituents that help alleviate morning sickness.

Menthol's cooling and numbing properties can provide relief from nausea and vomiting, making it easier for women to manage their symptoms. Menthone, on the other hand, has anti-inflammatory properties that may help soothe digestive issues and reduce stomach discomfort. The menthol in Mentha x piperita creams can also help to calm the stomach and reduce spasms that contribute to nausea and vomiting.

By applying Mentha x piperita creams to the wrists or temples, women can experience quick relief from morning sickness symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of peppermint essential oil, and a small bottle for the cream.
  2. Melt coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Stir in the peppermint essential oil.
  4. Pour the mixture into the small bottle and let it cool completely until it's solid.
  5. Apply a small amount of the cream to the skin as needed to help with morning sickness symptoms. Store the cream in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

Glycyrrhizin's anti-inflammatory properties may also help to reduce stress and anxiety, common triggers of morning sickness.

By applying Glycyrrhiza glabra creams to the skin, the active ingredients are absorbed into the bloodstream, providing relief from morning sickness symptoms.

  2. Melt the beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.

5. Ginkgo biloba

The anti-inflammatory properties of flavoglycosides can also help reduce stomach cramps and discomfort often experienced during this time.

By using ginkgo biloba creams, pregnant women may find relief from the symptoms of morning sickness.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent morning sickness from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help with morning sickness.

Peppermint tea, in particular, has been known to ease nausea and stomach discomfort. Ginger tea is also a popular choice as it has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can help calm the stomach.

Some women find these teas helpful in reducing morning sickness symptoms.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for morning sickness every day?

Some herbal teas, like ginger and peppermint, can help with morning sickness.

However, drinking them every day can have side effects like stomach upset and dizziness. It's also possible for herbal teas to interact with other medications or worsen underlying health conditions.

Use them in moderation and pay attention to how your body reacts.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in morning sickness?

Herbal teas like peppermint, ginger, and chamomile are often used to help with morning sickness.

Some women notice a difference after one or two cups a day, while others may take a few days to feel the effects.

Everyone's body is different, so it's hard to say exactly how long it will take.

Morning Sickness Relief for New Moms: 7 Herbal Creams

New mothers often experience morning sickness, a condition where they feel queasy and nauseous after eating or even smelling certain foods.

This can be caused by hormonal changes during pregnancy, which affect the body's balance. Morning sickness can be a real challenge for new mothers, as it can make them feel weak, tired, and irritable. It can also make it difficult to eat and drink, which is essential for the mother's and baby's health. Some women may experience severe morning sickness, which can lead to weight loss, dehydration, and even hospitalization. This can affect new mothers' daily lives, making it hard to care for themselves and their babies. Morning sickness can also impact a new mother's mental health, causing anxiety and stress.

It can make her feel isolated and alone, as she may not want to eat or socialize with others due to her symptoms. In some cases, morning sickness can even lead to depression, which can be a serious issue for new mothers. New mothers may try various methods to alleviate their morning sickness, such as eating small meals, choosing bland foods, and getting plenty of rest. However, these methods may not work for everyone, and some women may need additional help to manage their symptoms. This is where herbal creams come in - they offer a natural and non-invasive way to treat morning sickness. These creams contain specific plant extracts that have been shown to help reduce nausea and vomiting.

When applied to the skin, these extracts can be absorbed into the body, providing relief from morning sickness symptoms.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams, also known as ginger creams, have been used for centuries to help alleviate morning sickness in pregnant women.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, have natural anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that help to calm the digestive system and reduce nausea. Ginger also has a compound called 6-gingerol, which has been shown to have a direct effect on the stomach, helping to reduce vomiting and stomach cramps. When used in cream form, ginger can be easily absorbed through the skin, providing quick relief from morning sickness symptoms.

To use ginger cream for morning sickness, apply a small amount to your wrists, temples, or behind your ears, and gently massage it into your skin. It's recommended to use the cream 30 minutes to an hour before waking up, as the active compounds can take effect quickly. As the ginger cream works, you may start to feel a reduction in nausea and vomiting, and your stomach cramps may ease.

Some women may also experience a calming effect, which can help to reduce anxiety and stress associated with morning sickness.

If you're experiencing morning sickness, you can try applying a Ginkgo biloba cream to your wrists, behind your ears, or on your temples. Look for a cream that contains at least 24% ginkgo biloba extract.

You can apply a small amount 2-3 times a day, but be sure to follow the product instructions and start with a small dose to see how your body reacts. Keep in mind that it may take a few days to notice any effects. Using a Ginkgo biloba cream may help reduce the frequency and severity of morning sickness symptoms. Some people report a decrease in nausea and vomiting, while others experience improved mood and reduced fatigue. However, it's essential to note that individual results may vary, and Ginkgo biloba creams may not work for everyone.

If your symptoms persist or worsen, it's crucial to consult with your healthcare provider for proper guidance and support.

1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains bioactive compounds like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Curcumin, in particular, has been shown to stimulate digestive enzymes and improve gut motility, which can help alleviate gastroparesis symptoms. The anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds can also help reduce inflammation in the stomach and small intestine, further contributing to improved digestive function.

By reducing inflammation and improving gut motility, Curcuma longa creams may provide relief from gastroparesis symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 teaspoons of Curcuma longa powder in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in the double boiler over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the vitamin E oil and Curcuma longa powder to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  4. Continue heating the mixture for another 10-15 minutes, or until it reaches 120-130°F (49-54°C).
  5. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool until it thickens and turns into a creamy texture. Pour into containers and store in the fridge.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 10 grams of Zingiber officinale (ginger) powder.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add vitamin E oil and stir well. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.

  5. Add 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil and stir well. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, your glycyrrhiza glabra cream is ready for use.

6. Foeniculum vulgare

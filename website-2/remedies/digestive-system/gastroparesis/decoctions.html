One common problem is nausea, which is when the stomach feels sick and wants to empty its contents quickly. This is often relieved with ginger, as it helps calm the stomach and ease vomiting. Another issue is constipation, which is when bowel movements are less frequent than usual. This is often caused by the slowed digestion in gastroparesis. Herbs like senna and cascara sagrada can help stimulate the bowels and encourage regular bowel movements.

Some people with gastroparesis also experience bloating and abdominal pain. This is often due to trapped gas in the digestive system. Fennel and peppermint can help ease these symptoms by reducing inflammation and gas in the digestive tract. People with gastroparesis are also at a higher risk for malnutrition, as their bodies may not be able to absorb enough nutrients from the food they eat. Herbs like slippery elm can help soothe the digestive tract and reduce inflammation, making it easier to absorb nutrients. Some people may experience weight loss due to gastroparesis, as they may not be able to eat enough to maintain a healthy weight.

Herbs like licorice root can help stimulate appetite and make eating more comfortable.

Curcumin helps to reduce inflammation in the stomach and improve digestion. Drinking turmeric tea after meals can help ease symptoms of gastroparesis and promote healthy digestion. Fennel, or Foeniculum vulgare, is another herb that's been used for centuries to treat digestive issues. Fennel tea is a natural anti-spasmodic, which means it helps to relax the muscles in your stomach and prevent cramps. Drinking fennel tea after meals can help ease symptoms of gastroparesis and promote healthy digestion. When you drink herbal teas for gastroparesis, you might notice that you feel more comfortable and confident when eating. 1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains the bioactive constituents gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation and pain in the digestive tract, a common issue for people with gastroparesis. Ginger has also been shown to stimulate digestive enzymes, which can help improve digestion and alleviate symptoms of gastroparesis. The warming properties of ginger can also help increase blood flow to the stomach, promoting the movement of food through the digestive system.

The antispasmodic properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils can also help relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing nausea and vomiting associated with gastroparesis.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh ginger roots and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Wash the ginger roots with water and dry them with a clean towel.
  3. Grate 1 cup of the ginger roots and mix it with 1 cup of carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and use the liquid as your Zingiber officinale essential oil for gastroparesis.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains the bioactive constituents curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the stomach and promote the healing of the digestive tract lining. Curcumin also has a direct effect on the stomach muscles, helping to regulate their contractions and improve gastric emptying in individuals with gastroparesis. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of curcuma longa essential oils help protect the stomach from oxidative stress, which can contribute to the development of gastroparesis.

By reducing inflammation, promoting healing, and regulating stomach muscle contractions, curcuma longa essential oils may provide relief from gastroparesis symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Curcuma longa roots, a double boiler, and a clean glass bottle.
  2. Combine 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) with the dried Curcuma longa roots in the double boiler.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 2-3 hours, or until the oil turns yellow and has a strong turmeric smell.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Curcuma longa essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a carrier oil for gastroparesis relief, as needed.

5. Cinnamomum verum

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent gastroparesis from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent gastroparesis in some people.

Certain herbs like ginger and peppermint have natural properties that can help soothe the stomach and improve digestion.

However, the effectiveness of herbal tea in preventing gastroparesis is not fully understood and more research is needed to confirm its benefits.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for gastroparesis every day?

While herbal teas may help with gastroparesis symptoms, it's essential to be cautious with daily consumption.

Some teas, like peppermint and ginger, can ease nausea and digestion. However, excessive caffeine from teas like chamomile or lemon balm might worsen symptoms or interact with medications.

Monitor your body's response and adjust your tea intake accordingly.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in gastroparesis?

Herbal teas for gastroparesis can start working in a few days, but it may take weeks or even months to see significant results. Some people experience relief from symptoms like nausea and bloating within a week, while others may need a few weeks or longer to notice improvement.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for gastroparesis?

Drinking herbal tea for gastroparesis can be beneficial when your stomach is empty.

This is usually in the morning, after waking up. Drinking tea on an empty stomach can help your body digest it more easily.

Some people find it helpful to have a cup of tea about 30 minutes to an hour before eating to help with digestion.

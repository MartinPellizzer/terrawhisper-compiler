7 Herbal Essential Oils for Infants with Difficulty Eating

Infants can struggle with eating due to various reasons such as digestive issues, anxiety, or discomfort after eating.

This can cause them to become fussy or even refuse to eat altogether, leading to problems with weight gain and overall development. When an infant has difficulty eating, it can affect their daily life, making mealtime a stressful and overwhelming experience for both the child and the parents. Infants rely on their caregivers for nutrition, so it's essential to find ways to make mealtime as smooth and enjoyable as possible. When an infant is struggling to eat, it's often because their body is not responding well to the food or the environment.

This can be due to a variety of factors, such as digestive issues, anxiety, or sensitivity to certain stimuli. In some cases, infants may even develop a fear of eating, making it even more challenging for parents to manage mealtime. Herbal essential oils can be a helpful tool in supporting infants with difficulty eating. These oils are made from plant extracts and are highly concentrated, making them a potent way to promote relaxation and calmness. When used in a gentle and safe manner, these oils can help reduce anxiety and promote a sense of well-being in infants, making it easier for them to eat and digest their food.

By creating a more peaceful and calming atmosphere during mealtime, herbal essential oils can help infants feel more comfortable and secure, which can make a big difference in their ability to eat and thrive.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils, also known as ginger essential oil, has been traditionally used to help alleviate difficulty eating due to its anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

The oil contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have been shown to reduce nausea and inflammation in the digestive tract. This can make it easier to eat and digest food, especially for people who experience nausea or discomfort after meals. If you're experiencing difficulty eating and want to try using ginger essential oil, you can take the following steps: add a few drops of ginger essential oil to a warm bath or inhale it through steam inhalation to help calm your stomach. You can also mix a few drops of ginger essential oil with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it to your abdomen to help soothe digestive discomfort.

Another option is to add ginger essential oil to your food or drinks, but be sure to start with a small amount to avoid overwhelming your taste buds. When using ginger essential oil to help with difficulty eating, you may notice a reduction in nausea and discomfort after meals. You may also feel more energized and able to eat more comfortably. Some people may experience a decrease in bloating and gas, making it easier to digest food.

However, it's essential to note that ginger essential oil may not work for everyone, and you should consult with a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or take medications.

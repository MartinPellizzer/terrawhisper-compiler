10 Herbal Teas to Support Elderly with Eating Difficulties

Many elderly people struggle with eating due to various reasons such as dental problems, dry mouth, swallowing difficulties, and even mental health issues like depression.

This can lead to malnutrition, weight loss, and fatigue, making everyday life a challenge. Simple tasks like eating become stressful experiences, and social activities like mealtime become dreaded. Elderly individuals may avoid social interactions, leading to feelings of loneliness and isolation. Difficulty eating can also affect their independence and self-esteem, making them feel like a burden to their families and caregivers.

As they age, the digestive system slows down, and the body's ability to absorb nutrients from food declines. Certain medications can also cause dry mouth and constipation, making eating uncomfortable. Even dental problems like gum disease and missing teeth can make eating painful. Fortunately, there are natural remedies that can help alleviate some of these issues. Herbal teas have been found to be beneficial in soothing digestive problems, reducing inflammation in the mouth and throat, and stimulating saliva production to combat dry mouth.

These teas can also help to calm the mind and reduce stress, making mealtime a more enjoyable experience.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, can help with difficulty eating due to its anti-inflammatory properties and ability to soothe the digestive system.

The tea contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have been shown to reduce nausea and alleviate discomfort. When you're struggling to eat, try drinking a warm cup of ginger tea before meals.

Start by brewing a teaspoon of fresh ginger in a cup of hot water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and add honey to taste. The likely outcome is that you'll feel more comfortable and confident eating, and may even find that your appetite returns. As the tea works to calm your stomach, you may also notice a reduction in bloating and discomfort.

Some people find that ginger tea helps to stimulate digestion, making it easier to break down food and absorb nutrients.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, also known as licorice root tea, can help with difficulty eating due to its anti-inflammatory properties and constituents like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids.

These compounds can soothe digestive issues such as acid reflux, heartburn, and stomach ulcers, which may be contributing to eating difficulties. When you drink Glycyrrhiza glabra tea, you can try to incorporate it into your daily routine, ideally before meals, to help calm your digestive system and reduce discomfort. Start by brewing 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink.

By taking this action, you may experience relief from digestive issues, allowing you to eat more comfortably. You may find that you're able to enjoy your meals more and eat a wider variety of foods. Additionally, Glycyrrhiza glabra tea may also help to reduce nausea and vomiting, which can be a major hindrance to eating.

It's essential to note that licorice root tea can interact with certain medications, so consult with a healthcare professional before making it a regular part of your routine, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas, also known as fennel tea, can be beneficial for individuals experiencing difficulty eating.

This is due to the presence of compounds such as anethole, limonene, and volatile oils, which have natural carminative and anti-inflammatory properties. These properties help to ease digestion and reduce discomfort, making it easier to consume food. If you're experiencing difficulty eating, you can try making fennel tea by steeping dried fennel seeds in hot water. Start by drinking a small amount, about 1/4 cup, after meals to see if it helps.

You can also add honey or lemon to make it more palatable. Some people find it helpful to drink fennel tea before meals as well, as it may help stimulate appetite. Taking fennel tea may lead to improved digestion, reduced nausea, and increased appetite. You may notice that you're able to eat more comfortably and that your meals feel less overwhelming.

However, it's essential to remember that fennel tea may interact with certain medications or worsen certain health conditions, so consult with a healthcare professional before using it as a treatment for difficulty eating.

4. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum teas, also known as Ceylon cinnamon tea, has been traditionally used to help with difficulty eating.

The active compounds in this tea, including cinnamaldehyde and cinnamyl acetate, have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These properties may help to ease digestive issues, such as nausea and abdominal pain, which can contribute to difficulty eating. The warming and stimulating effects of Ceylon cinnamon tea may also help to increase appetite and improve digestion, making it easier to eat.

To try Ceylon cinnamon tea for difficulty eating, start by brewing a cup according to the package instructions. You can add a pinch of cinnamon powder to your meals or take a warm tea before meals to stimulate your appetite. It's also essential to drink plenty of water throughout the day to stay hydrated. As you take Ceylon cinnamon tea regularly, you may notice that your appetite improves, and you feel more comfortable eating.

Your digestive issues may also start to resolve, allowing you to eat more comfortably and enjoy your meals.

5. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas have been used to help with difficulty eating, particularly in people with dementia, Alzheimer's disease, or other conditions that affect the brain.

The leaves of the Ginkgo biloba tree contain flavonoids and terpenoids, which are believed to improve blood flow to the brain and boost memory and cognitive function. This can make it easier to eat and swallow food, as well as improve appetite and reduce nausea. When taken as a tea, Ginkgo biloba extracts may help relax the muscles in the throat and esophagus, making it easier to swallow food and liquids. If you're having trouble eating, try drinking Ginkgo biloba tea after meals. Start with a small amount, such as 1-2 cups a day, and gradually increase the dose as needed.

You can also try taking the tea with other herbs, such as peppermint or chamomile, which may help soothe the stomach and reduce nausea. However, it's essential to consult with your healthcare provider before making any changes to your diet or adding new supplements to your routine. They can help you determine the best course of action and ensure that Ginkgo biloba tea won't interact with any medications you're taking. Taking Ginkgo biloba tea regularly may lead to improvements in eating and swallowing abilities, as well as a reduction in symptoms such as nausea and anxiety. You may find that you're able to eat more easily and enjoy your meals without feeling uncomfortable or stressed.

As you continue to take the tea, you may also notice improvements in your overall cognitive function and memory, which can have a positive impact on your daily life and independence.

6. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa teas, also known as turmeric tea, can be beneficial for people experiencing difficulty eating due to its anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

The main constituent responsible for these effects is curcumin, a powerful compound that has been shown to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and promote the healing of ulcers and other digestive issues. Curcuma longa teas also contain other beneficial compounds like polyphenols and volatile oils, which may help to improve digestion and reduce symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). To try Curcuma longa teas for difficulty eating, start by making a tea by steeping one teaspoon of turmeric powder in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

You can also add milk or honey to improve the taste. Drink the tea after meals to help with digestion and reduce discomfort. If you experience relief from difficulty eating after taking Curcuma longa teas, you may notice that you can eat more comfortably and that your digestive issues have improved. This could be due to reduced inflammation in the digestive tract and improved digestion.

However, if you have a severe or persistent digestive issue, consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

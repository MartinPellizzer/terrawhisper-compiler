1. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare creams contains a compound called fennel oil, which is rich in anethole and limonene.

These bioactive constituents have a relaxing effect on the digestive system, helping to ease spasms and reduce gas buildup in the stomach. Fennel oil's anisic properties also have a carminative effect, which means it helps to release trapped gas from the digestive tract. The oil's antispasmodic properties further help to soothe the muscles in the stomach, preventing cramping and discomfort.

As a result, fennel oil in Foeniculum vulgare creams can help to alleviate gas and bloating in the stomach.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Dry the Foeniculum vulgare seeds in a low oven (150°F) for 1 hour to remove moisture.
  3. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  4. Add the dried Foeniculum vulgare seeds and vitamin E oil to the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify. Store in a cool, dry place.

7 Herbal Teas For Gas In Stomach

Drinking these herbal teas can bring several benefits to your life. They can help you feel more comfortable and less bloated after eating. 1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains active compounds like gingerols and shogaols.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce inflammation in the stomach. The warming effect of ginger helps increase digestion and reduce stagnation, easing gas and discomfort. Additionally, the antispasmodic properties of ginger help relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing cramps and spasms that can cause gas and bloating.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the muscles, Zingiber officinale teas can help alleviate stomach gas and discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1 teaspoon of dried Zingiber officinale root.
  2. Boil the water in a pot and then reduce heat to low.
  3. Add the dried Zingiber officinale root to the water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve gas in the stomach.

2. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas contains a compound called Foeniculin, which is responsible for its beneficial effects on digestion.

The tea's active constituents, including Foeniculin and Anethole, have carminative properties, meaning they help to release trapped gas from the stomach and intestines. These compounds work by relaxing the muscles in the digestive tract, allowing gas to pass more easily and reducing discomfort. Additionally, Foeniculum vulgare teas has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe and calm irritated stomach tissues.

The cumulative effect of these properties makes Foeniculum vulgare teas a helpful remedy for digestive issues related to gas and bloating.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Foeniculum vulgare (fennel seeds) and 1 cup of water.
  2. Boil the water in a pot and add the fennel seeds.
  3. Let it simmer for 5-7 minutes to release the flavors.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup using a tea strainer or cheesecloth.
  5. Drink the tea warm or at room temperature to help relieve gas and stomach discomfort.

By relaxing the muscles and reducing inflammation, Mentha x piperita teas can help to ease the discomfort of gas in the stomach.

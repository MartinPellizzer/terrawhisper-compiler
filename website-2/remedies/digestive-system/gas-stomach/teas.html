Gas in Stomach: 7 Herbal Teas for Older Adults to Ease Discomfort

As people age, their digestive system can slow down, making it harder to digest food.

This can lead to gas and bloating in the stomach, which can be very uncomfortable. Older adults may experience pain, discomfort, and difficulty eating, which can affect their daily life and quality of time with loved ones. They may need to take breaks during meals, avoid certain foods, or even change their eating habits. This can be frustrating and isolating, making it harder for them to enjoy activities they love.

Gas and bloating can also lead to other health problems, such as constipation or diarrhea, which can be serious and require medical attention. Many older adults try to manage their symptoms with medication, but some may not want to take pills or may have side effects. That's why some turn to herbal teas as a natural way to soothe their digestive issues. These teas work by relaxing the muscles in the stomach and intestines, reducing inflammation, and helping the body get rid of trapped gas. They can be made from different plants, flowers, or roots that have been used for centuries to calm digestive problems.

Drinking herbal teas can be a gentle and comforting way for older adults to find relief from gas and bloating, and to enjoy their favorite foods without discomfort.

1. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas, also known as fennel tea, can help with gas in the stomach due to its natural properties.

Fennel contains a compound called anethole, which is a known carminative, meaning it helps to ease bloating and gas. It also contains other compounds like volatile oils and flavonoids that have anti-inflammatory properties, which can soothe the digestive tract and reduce discomfort. When consumed as a tea, fennel can help to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, allowing gas to move through the system more easily. If you're experiencing gas in your stomach, you can try drinking fennel tea after meals to help alleviate symptoms.

To make the tea, steep 1 teaspoon of dried fennel seeds in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it while it's still warm. You can also add a bit of honey to make it more palatable. When you drink fennel tea, you may start to feel a relief from bloating and discomfort within 30 minutes to an hour. The anethole in fennel can help to break down gas bubbles in the stomach, allowing them to pass through the digestive system more easily.

You may also experience a reduction in cramping and discomfort, making it easier to digest your food.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger teas, can help with gas in the stomach.

The reason is that ginger contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help to soothe the digestive system and relax the muscles in the stomach. This can help to reduce bloating and discomfort caused by trapped gas. Ginger also has natural carminative properties, meaning it helps to expel gas from the body, and can stimulate digestion to help food move through the digestive system more efficiently. To use ginger tea for gas, start by brewing fresh ginger root in hot water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink.

You can also add lemon and honey to taste. It's recommended to drink the tea after meals to help with digestion. For best results, take small sips and avoid drinking too quickly, as this can cause more discomfort. When you take ginger tea, you may notice a reduction in bloating and discomfort within 30 minutes to an hour. You may also experience a decrease in nausea and an improvement in digestion.

However, if you have a severe case of gas or other digestive issues, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, also known as licorice root tea, have been traditionally used to alleviate gas and stomach discomfort.

The main reason for this is due to the presence of glycyrrhizin, a compound that has anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. Glycyrrhizin also helps to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, allowing for better digestion and reduced symptoms of bloating and gas. Additionally, the tea contains flavonoids and other antioxidants that may help to protect the stomach lining and reduce inflammation. If you're experiencing gas and stomach discomfort, you can try making a licorice root tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root in a cup of hot water for 5-7 minutes.

You can also add a pinch of ginger or peppermint to enhance the flavor and benefits. Start by taking a small amount, 1/2 cup, 2-3 times a day, and see how your body reacts. If you experience any discomfort or worsening symptoms, reduce the frequency or amount of tea consumed. Some people may notice a reduction in bloating and gas within a few days, while others may take up to a week or more to experience noticeable improvements. When taking licorice root tea for gas and stomach discomfort, be aware that excessive consumption may cause high blood pressure and low potassium levels in some individuals.

It's recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using licorice root tea, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or are taking medications.

4. Peumus boldus

Peumus boldus teas, also known as Boldo tea, has been used for centuries to help alleviate gas and indigestion in the stomach.

The tea's effectiveness can be attributed to its rich content of antioxidants, flavonoids, and alkaloids, particularly boldine, which is responsible for its medicinal properties. Boldine has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that help to soothe the stomach muscles, reduce inflammation, and ease cramps. When consumed as a tea, the active compounds in Peumus boldus teas help to break down gas bubbles in the stomach, allowing for easier digestion and relief from discomfort. To use Peumus boldus teas for gas in the stomach, start by drinking 1-2 cups of the tea per day. You can prepare the tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried Peumus boldus leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it warm or at room temperature.

It's recommended to consume the tea after meals or when you experience discomfort. Some people may experience a slight bitter taste, but this is normal. You may also want to consider combining the tea with other digestive aids, such as ginger or peppermint, to enhance its effectiveness. When you start taking Peumus boldus teas, you may experience a reduction in bloating and discomfort, making it easier to digest food. You may also notice an improvement in your overall digestive health, including a decrease in gas and indigestion. In some cases, people may experience a slight stomach upset or nausea, especially if they take the tea in large amounts or without food.

However, these symptoms are usually temporary and will subside as your body adjusts to the tea's active compounds.

5. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum teas have been traditionally used to help alleviate gas and discomfort in the stomach.

This is due to the presence of active compounds like cinnamaldehyde, which has natural anti-inflammatory and carminative properties. Cinnamaldehyde helps to relax the muscles in the stomach and intestines, reducing spasms and cramps that can cause bloating and discomfort. Additionally, Cinnamomum verum teas contain volatile oils like linalool and α-pinene, which have anti-bacterial properties that can help to reduce the growth of bacteria in the gut, a common cause of gas and bloating. To use Cinnamomum verum teas for gas, start by brewing a cup of the tea according to the package instructions.

Typically, this involves steeping one teaspoon of loose-leaf Cinnamomum verum in a cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes. Once the tea has cooled, you can drink it 2-3 times a day to help alleviate gas and discomfort. You can also add a pinch of honey or lemon to taste. If you experience relief from gas and discomfort, it may be due to the relaxation of stomach muscles, reduction of bacterial growth in the gut, or a combination of both. You may also notice a decrease in bloating and discomfort after eating, and an improvement in overall digestion.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

6. Cynara scolymus

Cynara scolymus teas, also known as artichoke tea, has been traditionally used to help alleviate gas and bloating in the stomach.

This is due to the presence of inulin, a type of fiber that acts as a prebiotic, feeding the good bacteria in the gut, promoting a healthy gut microbiome. Additionally, the tea contains sesquiterpene lactones, which have anti-inflammatory properties that may help soothe the digestive tract and reduce inflammation. The tea also contains cynarin, a compound that may help stimulate digestive enzymes and improve the absorption of nutrients. To use Cynara scolymus tea for gas in the stomach, steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried artichoke leaves in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day. It's also essential to maintain a balanced diet, avoid trigger foods, and manage stress levels to help alleviate gas and bloating. Taking Cynara scolymus tea may lead to reduced symptoms of gas and bloating, improved digestion, and a decrease in inflammation in the digestive tract. Some people may also experience an improvement in their overall gut health and a reduction in symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). However, it's essential to note that everyone's body is different, and it may take some time to notice the effects of the tea.

If you experience persistent or severe symptoms, consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

7. Cinnamomum zeylanicum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum teas, also known as Ceylon cinnamon teas, have been traditionally used to help alleviate gas and discomfort in the stomach.

The tea's active compounds, including cinnamaldehyde, cinnamomin, and linalool, have anti-inflammatory and carminative properties, which help to reduce inflammation and ease bloating. These compounds work by relaxing the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing spasms, and improving digestion, making it easier for gas to be released from the body. If you're experiencing gas and discomfort in your stomach, you can try taking Ceylon cinnamon tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried cinnamon bark in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then straining and drinking it 2-3 times a day.

You can also add honey or lemon to taste. To get the most out of the tea, it's recommended to drink it before meals to help stimulate digestion and prevent bloating. Taking Ceylon cinnamon tea regularly may lead to a reduction in gas and discomfort in the stomach, making it easier to digest food and reducing the risk of conditions like irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) and acid reflux. You may also experience improved digestion, reduced inflammation, and a sense of relief from bloating and discomfort.

However, it's essential to note that Ceylon cinnamon tea should not be consumed in excess, as it can cause stomach upset and interact with certain medications.

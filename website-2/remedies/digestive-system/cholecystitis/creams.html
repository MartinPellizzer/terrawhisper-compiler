1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains bioactive compounds like curcuminoids and turmerones, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation and swelling in the gallbladder, easing the pain and discomfort associated with cholecystitis. Curcumin's antioxidant properties also help protect the gallbladder from oxidative damage, which can contribute to the development of cholecystitis. Turmerones, on the other hand, have antimicrobial properties that can help combat bacterial infections that may be contributing to the condition.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the gallbladder, Curcuma longa creams may help alleviate symptoms of cholecystitis and promote healing.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder (Curcuma longa) in a bowl.
  2. Heat the distilled water in a saucepan until it reaches 180°F (82°C). Let it cool down to 104°F (40°C).
  3. In a separate bowl, mix 1 cup of coconut oil with 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder until well combined.
  4. Slowly add the cooled water to the coconut oil mixture while whisking continuously until smooth and creamy.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and refrigerate for 2 hours before using as a topical cream for cholecystitis.

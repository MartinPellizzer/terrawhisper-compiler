What are the causes of sour taste in mouth?

So, you want to know why your mouth feels sour?.

There are several reasons for this. Oral thrush, for example, is a fungal infection that can cause a sour taste. This happens when the fungus grows inside your mouth and releases acid, making your mouth feel sour. Tongue inflammation, on the other hand, can cause your tongue to feel sore and your mouth to taste sour.

When your tongue is inflamed, the tiny bumps on your tongue called papillae become red and swollen, which can irritate your tongue and make your mouth taste sour. Gastroesophageal reflux, or GERD, is another cause of a sour taste in the mouth. This happens when the stomach acid flows back up into your mouth, causing a sour taste. Sour candies and lemon juice are also obvious causes of a sour taste in the mouth.

They contain acids that stimulate the taste buds on your tongue, making your mouth feel sour.

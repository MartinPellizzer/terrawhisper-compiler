A Sour Taste in Mouth: Understanding Causes, Herbs, and Remedies

Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, is a natural remedy for a sour taste in the mouth. This happens when bacteria in the mouth break down the food we eat and produce acid, causing the sour taste. Ginger helps to neutralize this acid and reduce the bacteria that cause the problem. The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties that help to soothe the mouth and reduce the sour taste. Additionally, ginger stimulates saliva production, which helps to wash away bacteria and neutralize the acid, leaving the mouth feeling fresh and clean. Regular consumption of ginger in the form of tea, powder, or fresh root can help to alleviate a sour taste in the mouth.

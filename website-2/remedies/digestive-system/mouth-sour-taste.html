What Are The Main Causes Of Sour Taste In Mouth?

When you taste sourness in your mouth, it's usually a sign that something is wrong.

There are several reasons why this happens. Vomiting is one cause of sour taste. When you vomit, stomach acid comes up into your mouth. This acid is very strong and can make your mouth feel sour.

Another reason for a sour taste is gastroesophageal reflux. This condition happens when the stomach acid leaks back up into the esophagus, which is the tube that carries food from your mouth to your stomach. When the acid comes up into your mouth, it can make it feel sour. Dry mouth is also a common cause of sour taste. When your mouth doesn't have enough saliva, it can't wash away the bacteria that are naturally present.

These bacteria can break down the food in your mouth and release chemicals that make it taste sour.

Below are the main causes of sour taste in mouth.

  • Vomiting: When a person vomits, stomach acid and bile from the digestive system come up into the mouth, causing a sour taste due to the presence of acidic and bitter substances.
  • Gastroesophageal reflux: Gastroesophageal reflux occurs when stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus and throat, causing a sour taste in the mouth because the acidic content irritates the taste buds.
  • Dry mouth: Dry mouth is a cause of sour taste in the mouth because saliva helps to neutralize acids in the mouth, and without enough saliva, acids can build up and create a sour taste.
  • Dental problems: Dental problems, such as gum disease, tooth decay, and abscesses, can cause a sour taste in the mouth because bacteria and pus from these issues release acidic compounds that irritate the taste buds.
  • Eating citrus: Eating citrus fruits causes a sour taste in the mouth because the acid in them, called citric acid, mixes with saliva in the mouth and triggers the taste buds to send a sour signal to the brain.
  • Food poisoning: Food poisoning can cause a sour taste in the mouth because of the bacteria or toxins in the food that irritate the tongue and throat, making them release more acid, which tastes sour.
  • Diabetes: Diabetes can cause a sour taste in the mouth because high blood sugar levels can lead to a condition called dry mouth, where saliva production decreases, allowing bacteria to grow and produce acid, which gives the mouth a sour taste.

What Are The Main Causes Of Acid Reflux?

Acid reflux is a condition where stomach acid flows back into the esophagus, causing discomfort and pain.

There are several main causes of acid reflux. One cause is eating large meals, which puts pressure on the lower esophageal sphincter (LES) - a ring-like muscle that separates the esophagus and stomach. When you eat a big meal, the pressure on the LES can cause it to relax, allowing stomach acid to flow back into the esophagus. This is why eating large or heavy meals can trigger acid reflux.

Another cause of acid reflux is caffeine. Caffeine is a stimulant that can relax the LES, making it easier for stomach acid to flow back into the esophagus. Drinking coffee, tea, or other caffeinated beverages can trigger acid reflux in people who are sensitive to caffeine. Alcohol is also a cause of acid reflux. Like caffeine, alcohol can relax the LES and make it easier for stomach acid to flow back into the esophagus.

Drinking alcohol can also slow down digestion, which can put more pressure on the LES and make acid reflux worse.

Below are the main causes of acid reflux.

  • Eating Large Meals: Eating large meals can cause acid reflux because the stomach has to work harder to digest the food, which puts extra pressure on the lower esophageal sphincter, allowing stomach acid to leak back up into the esophagus.
  • Caffeine: Caffeine is a cause of acid reflux because it relaxes the muscle at the top of the stomach, allowing stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus.
  • Alcohol: Alcohol can cause acid reflux because it relaxes the muscle at the top of the food pipe, allowing stomach acid to flow back up into the throat and cause a burning sensation.
  • Obesity: Obesity is a cause of acid reflux because the extra weight around the stomach puts pressure on the lower esophageal sphincter, allowing stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus.
  • Smoking: Smoking damages the muscles that keep stomach acid from flowing back up into the esophagus, allowing acid to leak back and cause acid reflux.
  • Lying Down After Eating: Lying down after eating can cause acid reflux because it allows stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus, due to gravity, when the lower esophageal sphincter muscle relaxes, allowing the acid to escape.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Acid Reflux?

If you have acid reflux, there are some herbs you should avoid.

Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum) contain oils that can irritate the stomach and worsen acid reflux symptoms. Sage (Salvia officinalis) can also be a problem as it can slow down digestion, which can lead to stomach acid building up and causing discomfort.

Ginger-free peppermint (Mentha piperita) and spearmint (Mentha spicata) can cause stomach upset in some people, especially if consumed in large amounts. Also, licorice-free ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) and licorice-free triphala (a mix of three herbs: Amalaki, Haritaki, and Bibhitaki) may cause stomach issues in some individuals.

Finally, chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) can relax the lower esophageal sphincter, allowing stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus and worsen acid reflux symptoms.

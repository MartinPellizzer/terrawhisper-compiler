7 Herbal Essential Oils For Gerd

Herbal essential oils can be a great natural way to help with GERD, or gastroesophageal reflux disease.

GERD occurs when the stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus, causing discomfort and pain. Herbal teas can help relieve GERD by reducing inflammation and calming the digestive system. For example, Zingiber officinale, or ginger, has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe the digestive tract and reduce nausea. Ginger tea has been used for centuries to alleviate digestive issues and is often recommended for those with GERD. Another herb that can help with GERD is Glycyrrhiza glabra, or licorice root. Licorice root has anti-inflammatory properties that can help heal the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, reducing inflammation and discomfort.

It can also help to relax the muscles in the stomach, preventing acid from flowing back up into the esophagus. Turmeric, or Curcuma longa, is also a helpful herb for GERD. Curcumin, a compound found in turmeric, has potent anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and alleviate symptoms of GERD. Using herbal essential oils can bring many benefits to your life. Not only can they help alleviate digestive issues, but they can also promote relaxation and reduce stress. Drinking herbal teas can be a calming and soothing experience, allowing you to unwind and relax after a long day. Additionally, using herbal essential oils can be a natural and chemical-free way to manage your health, reducing your reliance on over-the-counter medications.

By incorporating herbal teas into your daily routine, you can find natural relief from GERD symptoms and promote overall digestive health.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Gingerol has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the esophagus and alleviate heartburn symptoms associated with GERD. The essential oil also contains zingerone, a compound that can help relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing spasms and discomfort. Additionally, the oils' warming properties can help increase digestion and reduce bloating, further alleviating GERD symptoms.

By using Zingiber officinale essential oils, some people have found relief from GERD symptoms due to its combination of anti-inflammatory and warming properties.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger roots, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil), and a small saucepan.
  2. Wash the ginger roots and chop them into small pieces. Place the ginger in the saucepan with 2 cups of water and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 30 minutes. Then, let the mixture cool and strain it into a bowl to remove the ginger pieces.
  4. Add the carrier oil to the bowl and stir well. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the bowl every day.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture again and discard the ginger solids. Your Zingiber officinale essential oil is now ready to use for Gerd relief. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with water for a natural remedy.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains licorice root compounds such as glycyrrhizin and lichenan, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

Glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin, helps to reduce stomach acid and soothe irritated tissues in the esophagus. This can lead to a decrease in symptoms associated with acid reflux, such as heartburn and chest pain. The oil's anti-inflammatory properties also help to relax the lower esophageal sphincter, allowing food to pass through the stomach more easily.

Additionally, lichenan, a polysaccharide, may help to protect the stomach lining from acid damage.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Grind the dried roots into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Combine the ground roots with the carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Add 10-15 drops of vitamin E oil to prevent oxidation.
  4. Seal the jar and place it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the oil extract.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

3. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a compound called curcumin, which has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Curcumin is a key bioactive constituent of this oil, responsible for its therapeutic effects. It has been shown to reduce inflammation in the esophagus, which is a common cause of acid reflux and heartburn associated with GERD. The anti-inflammatory properties of curcumin help to relax the lower esophageal sphincter, allowing it to function properly and prevent stomach acid from flowing back up into the esophagus.

By reducing inflammation and promoting proper digestion, Curcuma longa essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of GERD.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa roots, a glass jar with a lid, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Fill the glass jar 2/3 with the dried Curcuma longa roots, and add 1 cup of carrier oil.
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily to help the oil infuse.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Bottle the oil in a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a glass of water to help with GERD symptoms.

4. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains the bioactive constituents limonene, anethole, and estragole.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce inflammation in the esophagus, which is a common cause of acid reflux and GERD. The anethole in Foeniculum vulgare essential oils also has a direct effect on the muscles in the digestive tract, helping to relax them and prevent spasms that can contribute to GERD. Additionally, the essential oil's antimicrobial properties help to eliminate bacteria that can contribute to the development of GERD.

By reducing inflammation, relaxing muscles, and eliminating bacteria, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help to alleviate symptoms of GERD.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Foeniculum vulgare (Fennel) seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Heat 1 cup of the carrier oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the Fennel seeds to the heated oil and let it steep for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the bowl daily.
  4. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle, discarding the Fennel seeds.
  5. Store the Foeniculum vulgare essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid, using 5-7 drops per serving to help with heartburn and GERD symptoms.

5. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains a compound called cinnamaldehyde, which has anti-inflammatory properties and helps to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract.

The oil also contains eugenol, a compound that relaxes the muscles in the stomach and reduces spasms. Cinnamomum verum essential oils have antioxidant properties, which help to protect the stomach lining from damage caused by free radicals. The oil's antimicrobial properties also help to eliminate bacteria in the digestive tract that can contribute to heartburn and acid reflux.

By reducing inflammation, relaxing stomach muscles, and eliminating bacteria, Cinnamomum verum essential oils can help to alleviate symptoms of GERD.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Cinnamomum verum leaves, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Wash the leaves and pat them dry with a paper towel. Remove stems and chop leaves into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped leaves and carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well to mix.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This is called 'infusion'.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and use the infused oil as needed.

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains several bioactive constituents, including menthone, limonene, and beta-pinene.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help reduce inflammation and relax the muscles in the digestive tract. Menthone, in particular, has a calming effect on the stomach, reducing symptoms of acid reflux and heartburn associated with GERD. The antispasmodic properties of limonene and beta-pinene can also help relax the lower esophageal sphincter, preventing stomach acid from flowing back up into the esophagus.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the muscles in the digestive tract, Mentha x piperita essential oils may provide relief from GERD symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh peppermint leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat, then add the peppermint leaves.
  3. Reduce heat to low and let it simmer for 20-30 minutes, or until the liquid is reduced by half.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit at room temperature for 2-3 days to allow the oil to separate and rise to the surface, then use a pipette to extract the oil and store it in a dark glass bottle.

7. Piper nigrum

Piper nigrum essential oils contains a compound called piperine, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Piperine has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, helping to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and alleviate symptoms of acid reflux. The essential oil also contains limonene, a compound that helps to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing pressure on the stomach and preventing acid from flowing back up into the esophagus. Additionally, the oil's antacid properties help to neutralize excess stomach acid, providing relief from heartburn and discomfort associated with GERD.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the digestive muscles, piper nigrum essential oils can help to alleviate symptoms of GERD and promote a healthier digestive system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Piper nigrum berries, a clean glass jar with a lid, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Wash the berries in cold water, then pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Place the berries in the glass jar and cover them with 2 tablespoons of carrier oil.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent gerd from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent GERD from forming.

Certain teas, like chamomile and peppermint, can calm the stomach and reduce acid production. Ginger tea is also known to soothe the esophagus and ease digestion.

These teas work by relaxing the muscles and reducing inflammation, which can help prevent acid from flowing back up into the esophagus.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for gerd every day?

Herbal teas can be a gentle way to ease GERD symptoms, but daily consumption is a mixed bag.

Some teas, like chamomile and licorice root, may relax the lower esophageal sphincter, making symptoms worse.

However, others, like peppermint and ginger, may help calm the stomach and reduce discomfort.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in gerd?

When it comes to herbal teas for GERD, results can vary from person to person.

Some people notice relief within a few days to a week after drinking the tea regularly. Others might take longer, up to 2-3 weeks, to experience some improvement.

It's essential to be consistent with your tea consumption to see any noticeable effects.

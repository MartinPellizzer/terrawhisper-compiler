What Are The Main Causes Of Vomiting?

There are several reasons why people vomit.

One common cause is food poisoning. This happens when we eat food that has been contaminated with bacteria, viruses, or other germs. These germs can cause an infection in our stomach and intestines, leading to symptoms like vomiting. Another cause of vomiting is viral gastroenteritis, commonly known as viral gastro. This is a stomach and intestine infection caused by viruses like norovirus and rotavirus.

These viruses can spread quickly from person to person, especially in crowded places like schools and hospitals. When we have viral gastro, our stomach lining gets inflamed, causing vomiting and diarrhea. Bacterial gastroenteritis, or bacterial gastro, is another cause of vomiting. This type of infection is caused by bacteria like Salmonella and E. coli. Like food poisoning, bacterial gastro happens when we eat contaminated food or come into contact with contaminated water. The bacteria can then infect our stomach and intestines, leading to symptoms like vomiting and diarrhea.

In all these cases, the infection causes inflammation in the stomach and intestines, leading to vomiting as the body tries to get rid of the germs.

Below are the main causes of vomiting.

  • Food Poisoning: Food poisoning occurs when bad germs, like bacteria, are eaten with food and can cause the stomach to get very upset, leading to vomiting as the body tries to get rid of the germs.
  • Viral Gastro: Viral gastro, also known as viral gastroenteritis, is a cause of vomiting because the virus attacks the stomach and intestines, making them inflamed and irritated, which leads to a buildup of stomach acid and triggers the vomiting reflex.
  • Bacterial Gastro: Bacterial gastro causes vomiting when the bacteria in the stomach release bad toxins that irritate the stomach lining, making it send a signal to the brain to expel the food out of the body through vomiting.
  • Motion Sickness: Motion sickness is a cause of vomiting because the body gets mixed signals from the senses, such as seeing the world move but feeling like you're still, which confuses the brain and triggers the stomach to throw up.
  • Pregnancy: During pregnancy, the hormone called human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG) causes the stomach muscles to contract more often, leading to nausea and vomiting.
  • Food Allergy: When a person eats something that causes an allergic reaction to their body, their immune system tries to fight it off, releasing chemicals that can irritate the stomach and cause vomiting.
  • Migraine: Migraines can cause vomiting due to the release of special chemicals in the brain called neurotransmitters, which can send strong signals to the stomach, making it feel queasy and leading to nausea and vomiting.

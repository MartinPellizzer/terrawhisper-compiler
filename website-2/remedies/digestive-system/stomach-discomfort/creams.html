1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains gingerols and shogaols, which are the active constituents that help with stomach discomfort.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and ease pain in the stomach. They also have antispasmodic properties, which help to relax the muscles in the stomach and prevent cramps. The bioactive compounds in ginger also have antioxidant properties, which help to protect the stomach lining from damage and promote healing.

By reducing inflammation, relaxing muscles, and protecting the stomach lining, Zingiber officinale creams can provide relief from stomach discomfort.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Zingiber officinale root powder, and 2 tablespoons of shea butter.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add Zingiber officinale root powder and shea butter to the melted mixture. Stir well.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool until mixture thickens. Stir occasionally.
  5. Pour mixture into a container and let cool completely before using. Store in a cool place.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains a compound called curcumin, a powerful anti-inflammatory agent that helps soothe stomach discomfort.

Curcumin's potent antioxidant properties neutralize free radicals that can irritate the digestive tract, reducing inflammation and discomfort. The curcumin in Curcuma longa creams also has a natural anti-spasmodic effect, which can help calm muscle contractions in the stomach, providing relief from cramps and spasms. Additionally, curcumin has been shown to have a protective effect on the stomach lining, preventing damage from acid and other irritants.

By reducing inflammation and soothing stomach muscles, Curcuma longa creams can provide effective relief from stomach discomfort and promote digestive health.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder (Curcuma longa), 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of olive oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add turmeric powder and stir well. Let it heat for 5 minutes to infuse the turmeric into the oil.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in shea butter and olive oil until they're fully incorporated and the mixture has cooled slightly.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container and let it cool and solidify completely before use. Store in a cool, dry place.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin that helps to reduce inflammation and soothe stomach discomfort.

Glycyrrhizin works by inhibiting the production of stomach acid and enzymes that break down stomach tissue. Glycyrrhiza glabra creams also contain flavonoids, such as luteolin and quercetin, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help to calm stomach irritation. The anti-inflammatory compounds in Glycyrrhiza glabra creams can help to reduce swelling and pain in the stomach, providing relief from discomfort.

The soothing and anti-inflammatory properties of glycyrrhizin and flavonoids in Glycyrrhiza glabra creams make them a popular natural remedy for stomach discomfort.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup distilled water, 1/2 cup glycerin, 2 tablespoons glycyrrhiza glabra powder, 2 tablespoons beeswax, 1 tablespoon coconut oil, 1 tablespoon vitamin E oil.
  2. In a small saucepan, heat the distilled water over low heat.
  3. Add glycerin, glycyrrhiza glabra powder, and beeswax to the saucepan. Stir until beeswax dissolves.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and add coconut oil and vitamin E oil. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, your Glycyrrhiza glabra cream is ready for use.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains a compound called aloin, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

This helps reduce swelling and irritation in the stomach, making it a good treatment for stomach discomfort. The cream also contains glycoproteins, such as acemannan, which has soothing and protective properties that calm the stomach lining. The high water content in aloe barbadensis creams helps to hydrate the stomach, reducing inflammation and promoting healing.

The combination of these properties makes aloe barbadensis creams a natural and effective treatment for stomach discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of lavender oil.
  2. In a small saucepan, melt the coconut oil and beeswax over low heat, stirring until smooth.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and stir in the Aloe gel, vitamin E oil, and lavender oil until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into a glass jar and let it cool until it thickens into a cream.
  5. Store the Aloe barbadensis cream in the fridge and apply a small amount to your stomach as needed for discomfort relief.

5. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare creams contains a compound called anethole, which is an active constituent that helps soothe stomach discomfort.

Anethole has a calming effect on the stomach muscles, reducing spasms and cramps. The cream also contains estragole and limonene, which are volatile oils that have anti-inflammatory properties. These properties help reduce inflammation in the stomach, which can contribute to discomfort and pain.

By reducing inflammation and soothing the stomach muscles, Foeniculum vulgare creams can provide relief from stomach cramps and other digestive issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Foeniculum vulgare (fennel seeds), 2 cups of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the dried fennel seeds to the melted coconut oil and let it steep for 2 hours, or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Mix in the beeswax and heat the mixture until it's fully melted, then pour it into a container and let it cool and solidify.

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains the bioactive constituents menthone and limonene.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help to relax the muscles in the stomach and reduce inflammation. The menthol in the cream also has a cooling effect, which can help to soothe the stomach and reduce discomfort. The antispasmodic properties of limonene can help to ease cramps and spasms in the stomach, providing relief from stomach discomfort.

The combination of these properties in Mentha x piperita creams makes it a useful remedy for stomach discomfort.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Mentha x piperita essential oil, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from heat and stir in the Mentha x piperita essential oil and vitamin E oil.
  4. Pour the mixture into a silicone ice cube tray or a small tin container. Let it cool and harden completely.
  5. Once set, remove the Mentha x piperita creams from the tray or container and store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

Silybum marianum creams contains a compound called silymarin, which is a mix of flavonoids and lignans.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties, helping to reduce swelling and pain in the digestive tract. The oils' warming effect stimulates blood flow and relaxes muscles in the stomach, easing cramps and spasms. The antioxidant properties of gingerol and shogaol also protect the stomach lining from damage and promote healing.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the stomach muscles, Zingiber officinale essential oils helps to alleviate stomach discomfort and promote a sense of calm.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Zingiber officinale roots and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Wash the roots with water, then dry them with a clean towel.
  3. Grind 1/4 cup of the roots into a fine paste using a mortar and pestle.
  4. Mix the root paste with 1 cup of carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Let it sit for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into another container. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains compounds like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation in the stomach lining, easing discomfort and pain. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, blocks the action of an enzyme that breaks down stomach mucus, allowing the stomach to heal faster. The anti-inflammatory properties of flavonoids also help to calm the digestive system, reducing symptoms of stomach discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and soothing the stomach lining, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can provide relief from stomach discomfort.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots. Grind them into small pieces.
  2. Use a 1-cup measuring cup to measure 1 cup of carrier oil (such as coconut or olive oil). Pour it into a glass jar.
  3. Add 1/4 cup of ground Glycyrrhiza glabra roots to the carrier oil. Stir well to combine.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids and store the Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oil in a cool, dark place.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains a high concentration of active constituents like anethole, limonene, and pinene.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help to relax the muscles in the stomach and reduce inflammation. Anethole, in particular, has been shown to have a soothing effect on the digestive system, while limonene has antimicrobial properties that can help to eliminate harmful bacteria that can cause stomach discomfort. The antispasmodic properties of these constituents can also help to reduce cramping and spasms in the stomach, providing relief from symptoms of indigestion and nausea.

By reducing inflammation and eliminating harmful bacteria, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help to promote a healthy digestive system and alleviate stomach discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds, 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Place the seeds in a saucepan and add 2 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into the glass jar, then discard the seeds.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of the simmered liquid to the carrier oil in the jar. Stir well.
  5. Store the Foeniculum vulgare essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for skin application.

4. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains a high concentration of menthol and menthone, two active constituents responsible for its digestive benefits.

Menthol helps to relax the stomach muscles, reducing spasms and cramps that can cause discomfort. Menthone, on the other hand, has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to soothe the digestive tract and reduce inflammation. The essential oil's analgesic and anti-spasmodic properties also help to alleviate symptoms of indigestion and nausea.

By using Mentha x piperita essential oils, you can experience relief from stomach discomfort and promote a sense of calm.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves (peppermint) and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat and add the peppermint leaves. Let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a still or a distillation apparatus. Collect the steam and condense it into a separate container.
  5. Separate the essential oil from the condensed water using a separatory funnel or by letting it sit in the refrigerator for several hours, allowing the oil to solidify and separate from the water.

5. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum essential oils contains silymarin, a complex mixture of flavonoids and polyphenols.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and discomfort in the stomach. Silymarin also has antioxidant properties, neutralizing free radicals that can cause oxidative stress and contribute to stomach problems. By reducing inflammation, neutralizing free radicals, and protecting the liver, Silybum marianum essential oils may provide relief from stomach discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Silybum marianum flowers, 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine the Silybum marianum flowers and carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well to coat the flowers.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will help the essential oil extract from the flowers.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Silybum marianum essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a carrier oil or water for stomach discomfort relief.

6. Cinchona officinalis

7. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains cinnamaldehyde, linalool, and camphor as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help to soothe and calm the stomach muscles. Cinnamaldehyde, in particular, has been shown to have a direct effect on the digestive system, reducing inflammation and relaxing the muscles that can cause stomach discomfort. By reducing inflammation and relaxing the stomach muscles, Cinnamomum verum essential oils can help to alleviate stomach discomfort and promote a sense of calm and well-being.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Cinnamomum verum bark (Ceylon cinnamon), a carrier oil (coconut oil or jojoba oil), and a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.
  2. Dry the Ceylon cinnamon bark in a low-temperature oven (150°F - 200°F) for 2-3 hours, or until it is completely dry.
  3. Combine 2 cups of dried Ceylon cinnamon bark with 1 cup of carrier oil in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat (100°F - 150°F) for 2-3 hours, or until the oil has been infused.
  4. Strain the infused oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the dark glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Cinnamomum verum essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a carrier oil (coconut oil or jojoba oil) to help alleviate stomach discomfort.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent stomach discomfort from forming?

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for stomach discomfort every day?

You can safely drink herbal teas for stomach discomfort daily, but be cautious with amounts and types.

Some teas, like peppermint and chamomile, are gentle and suitable for long-term use. Others, like ginger and licorice, can be too strong if consumed regularly.

It's also essential to check the tea's ingredients and quality to avoid any potential harm.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in stomach discomfort?

Herbal teas can help with stomach discomfort, but how long it takes to feel relief varies.

Some people notice a difference after one to two cups a day for a few days, while others may take a week or two to feel the effects.

It's also individual, so it's hard to predict exactly when you'll start to feel better.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for stomach discomfort?

If you're feeling stomach discomfort, it's best to drink herbal tea in the morning or early afternoon. This allows you to digest the tea and its potential benefits without interfering with your evening meal.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains bioactive constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce stomach discomfort.

These compounds work by blocking the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause pain and inflammation in the stomach. The gingerols and shogaols also help relax the muscles in the stomach and improve digestion, reducing nausea and vomiting. Additionally, the antioxidants in Zingiber officinale tinctures, such as gingerol and shogaol, help protect the stomach lining from damage and promote healing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting digestion, Zingiber officinale tinctures can help alleviate stomach discomfort and provide relief from symptoms like bloating and cramps.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots and 2 cups of 80% vodka.
  2. Chop the ginger roots into small pieces and fill a clean glass jar with the chopped ginger.
  3. Pour the vodka over the ginger roots in the jar, making sure the ginger is completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass jar, discarding the ginger solids.

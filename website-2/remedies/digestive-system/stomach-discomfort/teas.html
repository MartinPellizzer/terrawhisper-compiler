1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains gingerols, shogaols, and zingerone as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce inflammation and discomfort in the stomach. The gingerols and shogaols in ginger have been shown to slow down the movement of the stomach, which can help alleviate nausea and vomiting. Zingerone, on the other hand, has been found to have a relaxing effect on the stomach muscles, which can help reduce cramps and spasms.

By consuming Zingiber officinale teas, the body can benefit from these properties, leading to a decrease in stomach discomfort and an improvement in digestive health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger root, 2 cups of water, and a tea infuser or strainer.
  2. Peel and slice the ginger root into thin pieces.
  3. Combine the sliced ginger and water in a saucepan, and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and let simmer for 5-7 minutes, then strain the mixture through the tea infuser or strainer.
  5. Drink 1/2 cup of the ginger tea, 2-3 times a day, for relief from stomach discomfort.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin, and flavonoids like liquiritin and isoliquiritin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and irritation in the digestive tract. Glycyrrhizin also has a soothing effect on the stomach lining, protecting it from acid and enzymes that can cause discomfort. The flavonoids in Glycyrrhiza glabra tea have antioxidant properties that help combat free radicals and promote healing in the stomach.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Glycyrrhiza glabra tea can help alleviate stomach discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1-2 teaspoons of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root.
  2. Steep the root in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the mixture with a fine-mesh sieve.
  3. Add 1-2 teaspoons of honey to sweeten the tea, if desired. Stir well to dissolve the honey.
  4. Drink the tea 1-2 times a day, as needed, for stomach discomfort relief.
  5. Store any leftover tea in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours. Reheat as needed.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas contains the active constituents anethole and limonene, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation in the stomach, which can cause discomfort. The anethole in Foeniculum vulgare teas has a calming effect on the digestive system, reducing spasms and cramps. The limonene has antimicrobial properties, which can help to eliminate bacteria that may be contributing to stomach upset.

By reducing inflammation and eliminating bacteria, Foeniculum vulgare teas can help to soothe and calm the stomach.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Foeniculum vulgare (also known as fennel) leaves, stalks, and flowers.
  2. Clean and dry the fennel parts. You can use a dehydrator or air-dry them for 2-3 days.
  3. Take 2 tablespoons of the dried fennel and put them in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.
  4. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the fennel. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it warm or at room temperature. You can add honey or lemon to taste.

4. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas contains the bioactive constituents menthone, limonene, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help soothe and calm the digestive system. Menthone has been shown to reduce spasms and ease cramping in the stomach, while limonene can help reduce inflammation and promote relaxation. Rosmarinic acid has antioxidant properties that can protect the stomach lining from damage and irritation.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Mentha x piperita teas can help alleviate stomach discomfort and provide relief from digestive issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh peppermint leaves or 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) to a cup.
  4. Add the peppermint leaves to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea into the cup with honey and enjoy.

5. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis teas contains quinine, a key bioactive constituent, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce inflammation in the stomach, alleviating discomfort and pain. Quinine also acts as a bitter agent, stimulating digestive enzymes and improving digestion, which can contribute to stomach discomfort. Additionally, quinine's antispasmodic properties help relax stomach muscles, reducing cramps and spasms that can cause stomach discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and improving digestion, Cinchona officinalis teas can provide relief from stomach discomfort.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Cinchona officinalis bark and a cup of boiling water.
  2. Add the Cinchona bark to a cup and pour in the boiling water.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes to allow the flavors to combine.
  4. Strain the tea into another cup to remove the bark and any solids.
  5. Drink the tea while warm to help soothe stomach discomfort.

6. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla teas contains apigenin and luteolin as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that help to calm the stomach and reduce inflammation. The apigenin in Matricaria chamomilla teas also acts as an antispasmodic, which means it can help to relax the muscles in the stomach and alleviate cramps. The tea's anti-inflammatory properties can help to reduce swelling and irritation in the stomach, making it a good option for stomach discomfort caused by inflammation.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the stomach muscles, Matricaria chamomilla teas can help to provide relief from stomach discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water, 1 teaspoon of dried Matricaria chamomilla flowers, and a tea strainer or infuser.
  2. Add the dried Matricaria chamomilla flowers to the tea strainer or infuser.
  3. Steep the flowers in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup using the tea strainer or infuser.
  5. Drink the tea as needed to help soothe stomach discomfort.

7. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains the bioactive constituents linalool and linalyl acetate.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help to soothe and calm the digestive system. The antispasmodic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate can also help to reduce muscle cramps and spasms in the stomach, providing relief from discomfort. Additionally, the calming effects of Lavandula angustifolia teas can help to reduce stress and anxiety, which are common triggers for stomach discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Lavandula angustifolia teas can provide quick and effective relief from stomach issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of the Lavandula flowers to the boiling water.
  4. Reduce heat and let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it warm, 1-2 cups per day as needed.

What Are The Main Causes Of Cavities?

Cavities are holes in teeth caused by tooth decay.

There are three main causes of cavities. Consuming sweets is a major cause because sugars in sweets break down into acids that attack teeth. These acids weaken tooth enamel, making it easier for cavities to form. Poor oral hygiene is another cause of cavities.

When you don't brush and floss regularly, food particles and sugars stay on your teeth, providing a perfect environment for bacteria to grow. These bacteria produce acids that damage teeth, leading to cavities. Inadequate saliva is also a cause of cavities. Saliva helps wash away sugars and bacteria from teeth. If you don't have enough saliva or if it's not working properly, bacteria can multiply and cause cavities.

This is often the case in people who have dry mouth due to medication or other health conditions.

Below are the main causes of cavities.

  • Consuming sweets: Consuming sweets regularly causes cavities because the bacteria in our mouth feed on the sugars in the sweets and produce acid that damages our teeth.
  • Poor oral hygiene: Poor oral hygiene allows bacteria in the mouth to multiply and produce acid, which can damage the enamel on teeth, leading to cavities.
  • Inadequate saliva: Inadequate saliva doesn't wash away food particles and acid produced by bacteria in the mouth, allowing them to combine and damage tooth enamel, leading to cavities.
  • Acid erosion: Acid erosion is a cause of cavities because the acid in our mouths, often from food and drinks, wears away the hard outer layer of our teeth, making them more vulnerable to bacterial attacks and decay.
  • Frequent snacking: Frequent snacking can cause cavities because the constant flow of sugary foods and drinks into the mouth allows bacteria to produce acid that weakens tooth enamel, making teeth more susceptible to decay.
  • Genetic predisposition: Genetic predisposition causes cavities because some people are born with weak tooth enamel or a unique mouth bacteria balance that makes their teeth more prone to decay and acid damage.

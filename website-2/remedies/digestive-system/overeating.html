Overeating: The Causes and Medicinal Herbs for a Healthier You

What are the causes of overeating?

When we overeat, it's often because of a combination of factors.

One main cause is hunger. If we haven't eaten for a while, our bodies need fuel, and we might feel the urge to eat more than usual. Another cause is stress. When we're anxious or overwhelmed, our brains can tell our bodies to seek comfort in food, leading us to eat more than we need. We might also overeat because of social pressure, like when we're at a party or a family gathering and there's plenty of food around.

In these situations, it can be hard to say no or stop eating, even if we're full. Sensory triggers are another cause. We might see or smell food that's appealing, and our brains respond by making us hungry. Finally, some people are addicted to food. This can be due to a variety of reasons, like a history of trauma or a medical condition that affects appetite.

For these individuals, overeating can be a way to cope with their emotions or manage their physical needs.

Additionally, certain herbs have been shown to have a positive impact on stress levels, which can often contribute to overeating.

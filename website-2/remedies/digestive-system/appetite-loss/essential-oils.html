1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains bioactive constituents like turmeric oil, which has been shown to have a positive effect on appetite.

The main compound in turmeric oil, curcumin, has anti-inflammatory properties that help regulate digestion and reduce inflammation in the gut. This can lead to a healthier appetite and improved nutrient absorption. Additionally, curcumin has antioxidant properties that help protect the body from oxidative stress, which can contribute to a decrease in appetite.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healthy digestion, Curcuma longa essential oils may help increase appetite and support overall digestive health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa roots and 2 cups of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Grind the dried Curcuma longa roots into a fine powder.
  3. Mix 2 tablespoons of the powder with 2 cups of carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture and discard the solids. Your Curcuma longa essential oil is now ready to use.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which helps stimulate the digestive system and increase appetite.

The bioactive constituents of ginger, including gingerol and shogaol, have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe the digestive tract and reduce nausea, making it easier to eat. Ginger's warming and stimulating properties can also help increase blood flow to the stomach, improving digestion and reducing symptoms of indigestion and bloating. The aroma of ginger essential oil can also help stimulate the senses and increase appetite, making it a popular choice for those struggling with a lack of appetite.

By using Zingiber officinale essential oils in aromatherapy, individuals can potentially alleviate digestive issues and stimulate their appetite.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger roots and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Boil the water and add the ginger roots. Reduce heat to low.
  3. Steep for 30 minutes and then strain the mixture.
  4. Combine 1 cup of the strained ginger liquid with 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil).
  5. Store the Zingiber officinale essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

3. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains the bioactive constituents cinnamaldehyde, cinnamyl acetate, and linalool.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce pain and discomfort associated with loss of appetite. Cinnamaldehyde, in particular, has been shown to have a stimulating effect on the digestive system, increasing appetite and improving digestion. Linalool, on the other hand, has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help reduce stress and anxiety that may contribute to a loss of appetite.

By balancing the body's internal systems, Cinnamomum verum essential oils may help restore a healthy appetite and improve overall well-being.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Cinnamomum verum bark, a glass jar with a lid, and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Add 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the glass jar.
  3. Add 1/2 cup of Cinnamomum verum bark to the glass jar with the carrier oil.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into the dark glass bottle and discard the solids.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which is responsible for its sweet taste and medicinal properties.

Glycyrrhizin is a potent inhibitor of 11-beta-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase, an enzyme that converts cortisol into another hormone, cortisone. By inhibiting this enzyme, glycyrrhizin can increase the levels of cortisone in the body, which can help to stimulate appetite and reduce nausea. Glycyrrhizin also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe digestive issues and promote a healthy appetite.

The sweet, licorice-like flavor of Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can also stimulate the appetite by activating the brain's reward centers and releasing appetite-stimulating hormones.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 2 cups of water.
  2. Place the roots in a saucepan and add the water. Heat the mixture on low heat for 30 minutes.
  3. Let the mixture cool down. Strain it through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a still or a distillation apparatus. Distill the liquid until the water content is reduced and the essential oil starts to separate.
  5. Collect the essential oil and store it in a dark glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a diffuser or mix it with a carrier oil for application.

5. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids and terpenoids.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help to stimulate appetite. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to improve blood flow to the digestive system. This increased blood flow can help to increase the sensation of hunger and improve nutrient absorption.

The essential oils' ability to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract may also contribute to a reduction in symptoms associated with loss of appetite.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Wash the Ginkgo biloba leaves with water and dry them completely.
  3. Combine 1/4 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves with 2 tablespoons of carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.

6. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains borneol, bornyl acetate, and camphor as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds contribute to its appetite-stimulating properties by acting on the digestive system. Borneol and bornyl acetate have been shown to stimulate gastric contractions, which helps to move food through the digestive system. Camphor, on the other hand, has a calming effect on the stomach, reducing nausea and discomfort that can lead to a loss of appetite.

By regulating the digestive system and reducing discomfort, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help increase appetite and support overall digestion.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water.
  2. Wash the leaves with 1 tablespoon of mild soap and rinse with clean water.
  3. Combine the leaves and water in a saucepan and heat over low fire for 30 minutes.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it steep for 2 hours. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth.
  5. Store the extracted essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use 5-7 drops as needed to help with loss of appetite.

7. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains limonene and menthone as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds contribute to the oil's properties that can help stimulate appetite. The menthone content in peppermint oil has a direct effect on the digestive system, increasing saliva production and gastric juices. This helps to prepare the body for food digestion and can increase hunger.

By stimulating digestive processes, Mentha x piperita essential oils may be used to help address loss of appetite.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and 1 cup of water.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to a clean glass bottle.
  3. Combine the fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and water in a saucepan and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes, then let it cool.
  5. Strain the mixture and add it to the glass bottle with the carrier oil. Store in a cool, dark place.

8. Elettaria cardamomum

Elettaria cardamomum essential oils contains active constituents like limonene, linalool, and alpha-terpineol, which contribute to its appetite-stimulating properties.

These compounds have a stimulating effect on the digestive system, helping to increase saliva production and enhance the appetite. The warming and aromatic properties of Elettaria cardamomum essential oils also help to improve digestion and reduce nausea, making it easier to feel hungry. Additionally, the essential oil's ability to reduce inflammation and soothe the stomach lining can help alleviate digestive discomfort and increase appetite.

By promoting digestive health and reducing nausea, Elettaria cardamomum essential oils can help individuals regain their appetite and enjoy a healthy diet.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Elettaria cardamomum seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Heat the carrier oil in a saucepan over low heat until warm.
  3. Add 1 cup of Elettaria cardamomum seeds to the warm carrier oil and mix well.
  4. Steep the mixture for 2-3 hours, then strain it using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  5. Bottle the extracted oil and store it in a cool, dark place for later use.

9. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains a high concentration of thymol, borneol, and bornyl acetate, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

These bioactive constituents have a stimulating effect on the digestive system, helping to increase appetite and improve digestion. Thymol, in particular, has antimicrobial properties that can help to reduce inflammation in the stomach and intestines, making it easier to eat and digest food. Bornyl acetate, on the other hand, has a sweet and uplifting scent that can help to stimulate the appetite and reduce nausea.

By using Salvia officinalis essential oils, individuals may experience an increase in appetite and a reduction in digestive issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves, a glass jar with a lid, and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Wash the leaves with cold water, then gently pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Pack the dried leaves into the glass jar, leaving 1 inch of space at the top. Cover the jar with the lid.
  4. Place the jar in a warm, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the leaves release their oil.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the dark glass bottle. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent loss of appetite from forming?

Drinking herbal tea might help prevent loss of appetite from forming.

The time it takes for herbal teas to show results in reducing appetite varies from person to person.

Consistency is key, so try drinking herbal teas regularly.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for loss of appetite?

Drinking herbal tea for loss of appetite is most effective in the morning or early afternoon.

This allows the tea to help stimulate digestion and appetite throughout the day.

Choose a tea with gentle, soothing properties like peppermint or ginger to ease your stomach and encourage hunger.

8 Herbal Teas to Help Elderly with Dementia Overcome Loss of Appetite

Elderly people with dementia often have trouble eating because of their condition.

Dementia can cause confusion, forgetfulness, and a lack of interest in things they used to enjoy, including food. This can lead to weight loss and other health problems. When people with dementia don't eat, they can get tired and weak, and their bodies may not be able to fight off infections. They may also have trouble moving around and doing everyday things, which can make them feel even more miserable. As a result, their quality of life can suffer greatly.

The loss of appetite in people with dementia can be caused by a variety of factors, including changes in their sense of taste and smell, difficulty swallowing, and pain or discomfort while eating. They may also have trouble recognizing food or remembering how to eat. Medicines and treatments for dementia can sometimes cause loss of appetite as a side effect. Herbal teas can be a helpful way to encourage people with dementia to eat more. Some herbal teas have a soothing effect and can be calming, which can help people with dementia feel more relaxed and interested in food. They can also have a pleasant taste and aroma, which can make eating more enjoyable.

Some herbal teas may even have a gentle stimulating effect, which can help people with dementia feel more awake and alert during meals.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas have been traditionally used to help with loss of appetite, particularly in people who are experiencing a decrease in hunger due to medication, stress, or other health conditions.

The reason Ginkgo biloba teas are effective in this regard lies in their unique combination of properties and constituents, including flavonoids, terpenoids, and bilobalide. These compounds work together to improve blood flow to the digestive system, boost metabolism, and stimulate the appetite centers in the brain. When taken as a tea, the flavonoids and terpenoids in Ginkgo biloba are absorbed into the bloodstream, where they can help to increase the production of digestive enzymes and hormones that regulate appetite. If you're considering trying Ginkgo biloba tea for loss of appetite, start by brewing a cup according to the package instructions.

You can take the tea 1-2 times a day, ideally before meals. Begin with a low dose and gradually increase as needed. It's essential to note that Ginkgo biloba tea may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, so consult with your healthcare provider before adding it to your daily routine. As you start taking Ginkgo biloba tea, you may notice an increase in your appetite and a reduction in nausea and digestive discomfort. Some people may also experience improved digestion, reduced bloating, and increased energy levels.

However, it's essential to be patient and consistent, as the effects of Ginkgo biloba tea may take some time to manifest.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas, made from the leaves of the rosemary plant, have been traditionally used to help with loss of appetite due to their stimulating properties.

The plant contains compounds like carnosic acid, camphor, and bornyl acetate, which have been shown to improve digestion and boost metabolism. When consumed as a tea, these compounds may help increase saliva production, stimulate the stomach to secrete digestive enzymes, and enhance the appetite. To try rosemary tea for loss of appetite, start by brewing a cup using 1-2 teaspoons of dried rosemary leaves in 8 ounces of boiling water. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink.

You can take this tea 1-2 times a day, before meals or as needed. As you consume the tea, you may start to feel a slight increase in hunger and a decrease in nausea. It's essential to note that individual results may vary, and it's recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before making any changes to your diet or treatment plan. Some people may experience side effects like heartburn or stomach upset, especially if they take the tea in large quantities or for extended periods.

If you're pregnant or breastfeeding, it's best to avoid consuming rosemary tea altogether.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, can help with loss of appetite due to its properties and constituents.

The main active compound, gingerol, has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe the digestive system and reduce nausea. This can be especially helpful for people experiencing loss of appetite due to digestive issues or chemotherapy. Ginger tea also contains other compounds like shogaol and zingerone, which have been shown to stimulate digestive enzymes and improve nutrient absorption. To take action, start by making a tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of fresh ginger in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the liquid and drink it warm, ideally 2-3 times a day. You can also add honey or lemon to taste. Some people find it more effective to drink it before meals to stimulate appetite. Taking ginger tea regularly can lead to improved appetite and digestion. You may notice an increase in food intake and a reduction in nausea and stomach discomfort.

However, if your loss of appetite persists or worsens, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional to rule out underlying medical conditions.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, also known as licorice root tea, have been traditionally used to help with loss of appetite.

The main compound responsible for this effect is glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin that has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Glycyrrhizin helps to soothe the digestive system, reducing inflammation and irritation in the stomach and intestines, which can be a major contributor to loss of appetite. Additionally, licorice root tea contains flavonoids, such as luteolin and quercetin, which have been shown to have a positive effect on the gut microbiome. If you're experiencing loss of appetite, you can try drinking licorice root tea 2-3 times a day. Start with a small amount, about 1 teaspoon of dried root per cup of hot water, and adjust to taste. You can also add honey or lemon to make it more palatable.

To get the most benefit, try to drink the tea on an empty stomach, about 30 minutes before meals. As you drink the tea, pay attention to how your body responds. You may start to feel a decrease in nausea and bloating, and an increase in hunger and interest in food. As you continue to drink licorice root tea, you may notice improvements in your appetite and digestion. You may start to feel more energetic and motivated to eat, and you may experience a decrease in symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), such as bloating and abdominal pain. However, it's essential to note that licorice root tea can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, so be sure to consult with a healthcare professional before using it as a treatment for loss of appetite.

Additionally, if you have any underlying medical conditions, such as high blood pressure or kidney disease, you should talk to a healthcare professional before using licorice root tea.

5. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum teas, also known as Ceylon cinnamon tea, have been traditionally used to help alleviate loss of appetite.

This is due to the presence of compounds like cinnamaldehyde, cinnamyl acetate, and eugenol, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These constituents may help stimulate digestion, reduce nausea, and increase the desire to eat. The tea's warm, spicy flavor is also thought to trigger the brain's appetite centers. To try Cinnamomum verum teas for loss of appetite, steep one to two teaspoons of ground cinnamon in a cup of boiling water for five to seven minutes. Strain the tea and drink it twice a day, once in the morning and once before a meal.

You can also add a squeeze of lemon juice and a drizzle of honey to improve the flavor. It's essential to note that Cinnamomum verum teas should not be consumed in excess, as it can cause stomach upset and interact with certain medications. Start with a small dose and gradually increase as needed. Taking Cinnamomum verum teas regularly may lead to increased appetite and improved digestion. Some people may also experience a boost in energy levels and a reduction in nausea. However, results may vary, and it's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using Cinnamomum verum teas, especially if you have underlying medical conditions or take medications.

Monitor your body's response and adjust your consumption accordingly.

6. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis teas, also known as lemon balm tea, can be helpful for loss of appetite due to its soothing and calming effects on the digestive system.

The tea contains properties like apigenin, a flavonoid that has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, and rosmarinic acid, an antioxidant that helps reduce stress and anxiety. These constituents help to ease digestive discomfort and promote a sense of calm, which can make you feel more like eating. To try Melissa officinalis tea for loss of appetite, start by drinking a warm cup of tea after meals or when you feel hungry. You can also add honey or lemon to taste.

Aim to drink 1-2 cups of tea per day, and see how your appetite responds. It's essential to note that if you're experiencing persistent or severe loss of appetite, you should consult with a healthcare professional for proper evaluation and guidance. As you drink the tea, you may start to feel more relaxed and less anxious, which can make you more inclined to eat. Some people may experience a gentle increase in appetite, while others may find that their interest in food returns gradually. Be patient and monitor your progress, as everyone's response to the tea may be different.

If you're taking any medications or have underlying health conditions, consult with a healthcare professional before using Melissa officinalis tea.

7. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas have been traditionally used to help with loss of appetite.

This is due to the tea's calming and relaxing properties, which are attributed to its main constituents: linalool and linalyl acetate. These compounds are known to interact with the brain's appetite centers, reducing stress and anxiety that may be contributing to a loss of appetite. When using Lavandula angustifolia teas for loss of appetite, start by brewing a cup according to the package instructions.

Aim for 1-2 cups per day, and drink it 30 minutes before meals. You can also add a few drops of lavender essential oil to your tea if you prefer a stronger flavor. As you take action, you may notice an increase in hunger and a desire to eat. This is because the tea is helping to balance your appetite and reduce stress.

You may also experience improved sleep quality, as the tea's calming properties can help regulate your sleep-wake cycle.

8. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng teas have been used for centuries to help with loss of appetite, and research suggests that it may be due to its ability to stimulate digestion and improve nutrient absorption.

Panax ginseng contains a group of compounds called ginsenosides, which have been shown to enhance gut function and increase the production of digestive enzymes. This can lead to better nutrient uptake and utilization, making it easier to feel full and satisfied.

To try Panax ginseng tea for loss of appetite, start by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried ginseng root in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Drink 1-2 cups a day, ideally before meals, to help stimulate your appetite. You may notice an improvement in your appetite within a few days to a week, and some people may experience increased energy and a sense of well-being.

However, it's essential to note that Panax ginseng tea can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, so consult with a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you have a pre-existing condition or are taking any medications.

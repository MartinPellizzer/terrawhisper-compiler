What Are The Main Causes Of Burping?

When we burp, it's usually because our body is releasing built-up gas.

There are several reasons why this happens. One main cause is swallowing air, which can enter our body when we eat or drink too quickly, talk while eating, or chew gum. This air gets into our stomach and eventually comes out as a burp. Eating carbonated drinks is another reason we burp.

These drinks, like soda and sparkling water, contain carbon dioxide gas. When we swallow this gas, it goes into our stomach and is released back out as a burp. The same thing happens when we eat food that's been cooked or processed, such as beans. Beans contain sugars that aren't fully digested in our small intestine. Instead, they move into the large intestine, where bacteria break them down, producing gas as a byproduct.

This gas gets trapped in our stomach and eventually comes out as a burp.

Below are the main causes of burping.

  • Swallowing Air: Swallowing air can cause burping because the air gets trapped in the stomach, where it mixes with food and acidic juices, and then the body tries to release it back out as gas.
  • Eating Carbonated Drinks: When we drink carbonated beverages like soda, the carbon dioxide gas in them gets into our stomach and can't escape easily, so it builds up and comes out as a burp.
  • Eating Beans: Eating beans causes burping because they contain a type of sugar that is hard for the body to digest, leading to gas buildup in the stomach and intestines, which is released as air bubbles when we burp.
  • Eating Onions: Eating onions can cause burping because the sulfur compounds in onions are not fully digested in the stomach, and when they reach the small intestine, they are broken down by bacteria into gas, which is then released as burps.
  • Eating Garlic: Eating garlic can cause burping because its strong smell and taste are released into the bloodstream, then travel to the lungs, where they mix with air and come out as gas bubbles, creating the burping sound.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Burping?

If you experience burping, it's best to avoid herbs that can irritate your digestive system.

Some herbs to steer clear of include 'Glycyrrhiza glabra' (licorice root), which can cause an imbalance in stomach acid and worsen burping. 'Angelica sinensis' (dong quai) may also be problematic, as it can slow down digestion and exacerbate gas buildup, leading to more burping.

'Ammi majus' (false queen anne's lace) contains compounds that can irritate the digestive tract, causing more gas and discomfort. 'Astragalus membranaceus' (astragalus) can also worsen burping if taken in large quantities, as it can stimulate stomach acid production.

'Curcuma xanthorrhiza' (yellow turmeric) and 'Curcuma longa' (turmeric) contain compounds that can cause stomach upset and worsen burping in some individuals.

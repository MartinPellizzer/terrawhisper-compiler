1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contain bioactive constituents such as aloin and aloe-emodin.

Aloin helps to stimulate bowel movements and increase water in the intestines, which can help to relieve constipation. Aloe-emodin has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to soothe and calm the digestive tract, reducing inflammation and discomfort. The gel in aloe vera creams also contains mucilages that can help to soften stool and make it easier to pass, reducing strain on the body.

By stimulating bowel movements and soothing the digestive tract, aloe barbadensis creams can help to alleviate constipation symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 drops of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add Aloe barbadensis gel to the melted mixture and stir well.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool until mixture thickens. Stir in vitamin E oil.
  5. Pour mixture into a container and let cool completely before use. Store in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains glycyrrhizin, a bioactive constituent that helps relax the muscles in the digestive tract.

Glycyrrhizin also increases the production of bile, a fluid that helps move food through the digestive system and prevents constipation. The cream's anti-inflammatory properties soothe the intestinal lining, reducing irritation and promoting regular bowel movements. Glycyrrhizin and other bioactive compounds in the cream, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, also help to stimulate the movement of food through the digestive system.

These properties work together to help alleviate constipation and promote regular bowel movements.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. In a small saucepan, heat the distilled water over low heat.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, coconut oil, beeswax, and vitamin E oil until well combined.
  4. Slowly add the dry mixture to the heated water, stirring constantly until the beeswax melts.
  5. Remove from heat and let cool. Once cooled, transfer the mixture to a container and refrigerate until thickened and ready to use.

3. Cassia angustifolia

Cassia angustifolia creams contains senna glycosides, which are powerful laxatives that stimulate bowel movements and help relieve constipation.

The senna glycosides, specifically rhein and emodin, increase water and electrolyte secretion in the intestines, softening stool and making it easier to pass. Cassia angustifolia also contains anthraquinones, which have a direct effect on the muscles of the intestines, helping to relax them and promote regular bowel movements. The combination of senna glycosides and anthraquinones in Cassia angustifolia creams helps to increase intestinal motility, reducing the time it takes for food to move through the digestive system.

Regular use of Cassia angustifolia creams can help to establish a regular bowel routine and alleviate symptoms of constipation.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Cassia angustifolia root powder, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add the Cassia angustifolia root powder and mix well. Let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and add the shea butter and vitamin E oil. Mix until smooth and well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and harden. Once hardened, your Cassia angustifolia cream for constipation is ready to use.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Gingerol and another compound called shogaol in Zingiber officinale creams have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects, which help to relax the muscles in the digestive tract. This relaxation can help to improve bowel movements and relieve constipation. The antispasmodic properties also help to reduce pain and discomfort associated with constipation.

By promoting the movement of food through the digestive system, Zingiber officinale creams can help to prevent constipation and maintain a regular bowel schedule.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Zingiber officinale (ginger) oil, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in ginger oil and vitamin E oil until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into a mold or a small container. Let it cool and harden completely.
  5. Once hardened, remove from mold and store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks. Apply a small amount to the abdomen to help relieve constipation.

5. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains curcumin, a powerful antioxidant and anti-inflammatory compound.

This compound helps to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, which can become tense and slow down bowel movements. The bioactive constituents in Curcuma longa creams, including curcuminoids and turmerones, have natural laxative properties that stimulate the movement of food through the digestive system. Curcumin also helps to reduce inflammation in the gut, which can contribute to constipation.

By relaxing the muscles and reducing inflammation, Curcuma longa creams can help to alleviate constipation and promote regular bowel movements.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of Curcuma longa powder in a heat-proof bowl.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Add the Curcuma longa powder to the mixture and stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a tin or jar and let it cool and harden. Once set, use it as a topical cream to help with constipation.

6. Castanea sativa

Castanea sativa creams contains tannins and flavonoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have astringent and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to tone the muscles in the digestive tract and reduce inflammation that can cause constipation. The tannins in Castanea sativa creams also have a binding effect, which helps to firm up stool and make it easier to pass. Additionally, the flavonoids in the cream have a stimulating effect on the gut, helping to increase gut motility and promote regular bowel movements.

By toning the muscles, reducing inflammation, and stimulating the gut, Castanea sativa creams can help to alleviate constipation and promote a healthy digestive system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Castanea sativa leaves, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/2 cup of beeswax.
  2. Dry the Castanea sativa leaves in a low oven at 150°F (65°C) for 2 hours. Then, grind them into a fine powder.
  3. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of the Castanea sativa powder to the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into small jars and let it cool. Store the Castanea sativa cream in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

7. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare creams contains the bioactive constituents fenchone, anethole, and limonene, which have been shown to help alleviate constipation.

The antispasmodic properties of these compounds help relax the muscles in the digestive tract, making it easier for stool to pass. Foeniculum vulgare also contains fiber, which can help add bulk to stool and promote regular bowel movements. Additionally, the oil's anti-inflammatory properties may help soothe and calm the digestive tract, further reducing the risk of constipation.

By relaxing and soothing the digestive system, Foeniculum vulgare creams can help restore regular bowel function.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Foeniculum vulgare (fennel seeds), 1/2 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a saucepan over low heat until it melts.
  3. Add the fennel seeds to the melted coconut oil and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl, discarding the fennel seeds.
  5. Mix in 1/4 cup of beeswax and stir until it's fully incorporated. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool to form a cream.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent constipation from forming?

Drinking herbal tea can help prevent constipation.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, can relax the muscles in the digestive system, making it easier to pass stool. Other teas, such as dandelion root tea, may also help stimulate bowel movements.

This can help prevent constipation and keep the digestive system running smoothly.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for constipation every day?

While herbal teas can help with constipation, drinking them every day isn't always safe.

Some teas, like senna, can be too strong and lead to dependence. Others, like peppermint, may interact with medications.

It's best to drink them in moderation and listen to your body to avoid any potential issues.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in constipation?

Herbal teas can help with constipation, but it may take some time to notice results.

Drinking them regularly, especially in the morning, can help.

Some people feel relief within a few days, while others may need a week or two to see noticeable improvements in their bowel movements.

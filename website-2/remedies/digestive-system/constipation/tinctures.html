10 Herbal Tinctures For Constipation

Another example is Zingiber officinale, or ginger, which has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can help to soothe and calm the digestive system, reducing discomfort and pain associated with constipation. Cassia angustifolia, or senna, is a natural laxative that can help to stimulate the muscles in the intestines and promote a bowel movement.

These herbs work by stimulating the digestive system and helping to move food through the body more efficiently. Using herbal tinctures for constipation can bring several benefits to your life. For one, they can help to reduce the frequency and severity of constipation episodes, making it easier to manage daily activities. They can also help to alleviate symptoms like bloating, cramps, and discomfort, improving your overall quality of life.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin that is responsible for its laxative properties.

Glycyrrhizin helps to increase the secretion of bile in the liver, which can aid in the movement of food through the digestive system and promote bowel regularity. Glycyrrhizin also has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe the digestive tract and reduce inflammation, making it easier for food to pass through the intestines. Additionally, glycyrrhizin has been shown to stimulate the muscles in the intestines, helping to move stool through the digestive system and prevent constipation.

The anti-oxidant properties of glycyrrhizin also help to protect the digestive tract from damage caused by free radicals, further reducing the risk of constipation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, 2 cups of vodka (at least 80 proof), and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine the dried root and vodka in the glass jar, making sure the root is completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar and shake it well for 10 seconds to mix the ingredients.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place and shake it every day for 2-3 weeks. This will help the tincture to mature and become stronger.
  5. Strain the tincture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture in water, 2-3 times a day, as needed for constipation relief.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains gingerols and shogaols, which are bioactive constituents with anti-inflammatory and carminative properties.

These properties help to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing spasms and cramping that can cause constipation. The warming effect of ginger also stimulates digestion and increases gut motility, helping to move food through the digestive system more efficiently. Additionally, ginger has natural antispasmodic properties that help to calm the muscles and reduce irritation in the digestive tract.

By reducing inflammation and promoting digestion, Zingiber officinale tinctures can help to alleviate symptoms of constipation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger root, a clean glass jar with a lid, and 2 cups of vodka.
  2. Wash the ginger root and chop it into small pieces. Place the ginger in the glass jar.
  3. Pour 2 cups of vodka over the ginger pieces, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass container, discarding the ginger solids. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle.

3. Cassia angustifolia

Cassia angustifolia tinctures contains sennosides A and B, anthraquinone glycosides, and other bioactive compounds.

These compounds help stimulate bowel movements by increasing the muscle contractions in the intestines, which aids in eliminating waste. Sennosides also work by increasing the water content in the intestines, making it easier for stool to pass through. Anthraquinone glycosides help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, which can contribute to constipation.

Regular use of Cassia angustifolia tinctures may help restore regular bowel movements and alleviate constipation symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Cassia angustifolia roots and 2 cups of vodka (at least 80 proof) in a clean glass jar.
  2. Fill a cup with vodka and pour it over the dried roots in the jar. Make sure the roots are completely covered.
  3. Close the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar daily to help the roots infuse into the vodka.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Label the jar with the date and contents, and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture in water or tea as needed to help with constipation.

5. Rheum palmatum

Rheum palmatum tinctures contains rhein, emodin, and anthraquinones, which are biologically active constituents that help stimulate bowel movements.

The anthraquinones in Rheum palmatum tinctures have a gentle laxative effect, helping to soften stool and promote regular bowel movements.

This combination of compounds in Rheum palmatum tinctures can help to relieve constipation and promote a healthy digestive system.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent constipation from forming?

Drinking herbal tea might help prevent constipation.

Some teas, like peppermint and senna, can stimulate digestion and bowel movements. Other teas, such as ginger and chamomile, can soothe the digestive system and promote regularity. These teas may help soften stool and make it easier to pass.

This can be especially helpful for people who experience infrequent bowel movements.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for constipation every day?

Herbal teas for constipation can be safe to drink daily, but be mindful of the ingredients.

Some teas, like senna, can be too strong and cause stomach issues if consumed regularly.

Opt for teas like peppermint or chamomile that promote gentle bowel movements without the risk of over-stimulation.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in constipation?

Herbal teas can help with constipation, but it takes time to see results.

Some people notice a difference within a few days, while others may need to drink them for a week or two.

It really depends on the individual and the specific tea being used.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for constipation?

Drinking herbal tea for constipation is best in the morning or early afternoon.

These times allow for a gentle digestive boost that can help ease bowel movements throughout the day.

This timing also helps prevent any potential discomfort or stomach upset that might occur if consumed too close to bedtime.

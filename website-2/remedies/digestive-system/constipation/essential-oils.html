1. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains a compound called anethole, which is responsible for its carminative and spasmolytic properties.

Anethole helps to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing spasms and cramps that can cause constipation. Foeniculum vulgare essential oils also contains another compound called limonene, which has a gentle laxative effect and helps to stimulate bowel movements. The antispasmodic properties of anethole and the gentle laxative properties of limonene work together to help alleviate constipation and promote regular bowel movements.

Regular use of Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help to relieve symptoms of constipation and promote a healthy digestive system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Fill a clean glass jar with 1/4 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds and cover with 1 cup of carrier oil.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the seeds.
  5. Bottle the Foeniculum vulgare essential oil and use 5-10 drops per day to help with constipation.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains the bioactive constituents gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects that help to relax the muscles in the digestive tract and improve blood flow to the intestines. The warming properties of gingerols and shogaols stimulate digestion and help to move food through the digestive system, which can help to alleviate constipation. Additionally, the essential oils have a gentle laxative effect, helping to soften and move stool through the intestines.

By promoting digestive health and relieving spasms in the intestines, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help to alleviate the symptoms of constipation.

  1. Gather materials: 100g Zingiber officinale rhizomes, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil), 3 tablespoons of vodka (optional), cheesecloth or a coffee filter.
  2. Clean and chop the Zingiber officinale rhizomes into small pieces. Place them in a clean glass jar.
  3. Add 1 cup of carrier oil to the jar with the rhizome pieces. If using vodka, add 3 tablespoons to help extract the oil.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day to help the oil extract.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains the bioactive constituents glabridin and glycyrrhetic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and laxative properties.

Glycyrrhetic acid helps to relax the smooth muscles in the digestive tract, allowing for easier bowel movements and relieving constipation. Glabridin also has a soothing effect on the gut, reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy gut environment. The oils' anti-inflammatory properties help to reduce spasms and cramps in the digestive tract, making it easier for stool to pass through.

The combination of these properties makes Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils a potential remedy for constipation relief.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots, 2 cups of water, a saucepan, a strainer, a glass bottle, and a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil.
  2. Combine the dried roots and water in the saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  4. Let the liquid cool. Add 1-2 tablespoons of the carrier oil and mix well.
  5. Store the essential oil in the glass bottle and use 5-7 drops in a warm bath or mix with a carrier oil for massage to help with constipation.

4. Cassia angustifolia

Cassia angustifolia essential oils contains a compound called sennoside A, which is a natural laxative.

Sennoside A works by increasing the movement of the muscles in the intestines, helping to move stool through the digestive system. The essential oil also contains anthraquinones, such as rhein and aloe-emodin, which help to soften stool and increase water in the intestines. These properties help to relieve constipation by stimulating bowel movement and making it easier to pass stool.

By increasing the movement and softening of stool, Cassia angustifolia essential oils can help to alleviate constipation and promote regular bowel movements.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cassia angustifolia leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat, then add the Cassia angustifolia leaves and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container, discarding the leaves.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) to the liquid and mix well.
  5. Store the Cassia angustifolia essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid and use as needed.

5. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamaldehyde helps to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, making it easier for food to pass through and relieving symptoms of constipation. By relaxing the muscles and reducing inflammation in the digestive tract, Cinnamomum verum essential oils may help to alleviate the symptoms of constipation.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent constipation from forming?

Drinking herbal tea can help prevent constipation.

Some teas, like peppermint and chamomile, can relax the muscles in your digestive tract and make it easier to pass stool. Other teas, like senna and dandelion, have natural laxatives that can help stimulate bowel movements.

They can also help soften stool.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for constipation every day?

Consuming herbal teas for constipation every day can be safe if you choose teas made from natural ingredients like senna or peppermint.

However, some teas may interact with medications or worsen underlying conditions.

Be cautious if you have a weakened digestive system or are prone to stomach issues.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in constipation?

Herbal teas can help with constipation, but it's not a quick fix.

It may take a few days to a week for your body to start feeling the effects.

Some teas, like senna or peppermint, can work within 24 to 48 hours, but others, like dandelion or chamomile, may take longer to show results.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for constipation?

For constipation, it's best to drink herbal tea in the morning or early afternoon.

This helps your body digest food from the day's meals and promotes bowel movements.

As your body processes food, herbal tea can aid in relaxing the muscles and increasing movement in your intestines.

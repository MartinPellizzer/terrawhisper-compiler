Soothing Upset Stomach: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Effective Treatments

Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been used for centuries to soothe an upset stomach. One reason it's effective is that it helps to reduce nausea and vomiting. When you're feeling queasy, ginger's active compounds work to calm your stomach and prevent the muscles from contracting, which can make you feel even sicker. Ginger also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling in the stomach and intestines. This can be especially helpful if you're experiencing stomach cramps or diarrhea due to an illness or food poisoning. Additionally, ginger has natural digestive enzymes that can help to break down food and improve digestion. This can make it easier for your body to absorb nutrients and reduce feelings of discomfort. By reducing inflammation and promoting healthy digestion, ginger can help to calm an upset stomach and provide relief from symptoms.

Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita, also known as peppermint, has been used for centuries to help soothe an upset stomach. This is because it contains a powerful oil called menthol, which helps to calm the muscles in the stomach and intestines. When we eat something that doesn't agree with us, our stomach can get inflamed and irritated, leading to cramps, nausea, and diarrhea. The menthol in peppermint oil helps to reduce inflammation and ease these symptoms by relaxing the stomach muscles and improving digestion. Additionally, peppermint has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce nausea and vomiting. When consumed as a tea, capsule, or even added to food, peppermint can quickly help to calm an upset stomach and provide relief from its associated symptoms.

Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, also known as licorice root, has been used for centuries to soothe an upset stomach. The root contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and inflammation in the stomach lining. This can help calm stomach pain and discomfort caused by irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), acid reflux, and other digestive issues. Glycyrrhizin also helps to relax the stomach muscles, allowing food to move through the digestive system more easily and reducing symptoms of bloating and gas. Additionally, licorice root has antimicrobial properties that can help eliminate harmful bacteria in the stomach, which can contribute to stomach upset. By reducing inflammation, relaxing stomach muscles, and eliminating bacteria, Glycyrrhiza glabra can provide relief from stomach discomfort and promote a sense of calm in the digestive system.

Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare, also known as fennel, is a plant that can help with an upset stomach. Its seeds contain a chemical called anethole, which is responsible for its medicinal properties. When you eat fennel seeds, the anethole is absorbed into your body and works to calm the digestive system. It helps to relax the muscles in the stomach and intestines, which can become spasmodic and cause pain when you have an upset stomach. Fennel also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling and discomfort in the digestive tract. Additionally, fennel seeds have natural carminative properties, which means they can help to release trapped gas in the stomach and intestines, providing relief from bloating and discomfort.

Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa, also known as turmeric, is a plant that has been used for centuries to help with stomach problems. The main reason it's good for an upset stomach is because of a chemical called curcumin. Curcumin has strong anti-inflammatory properties, which means it can help reduce swelling and pain in the digestive system. When we eat something that irritates our stomach, it can cause inflammation, leading to discomfort, nausea, and sometimes even vomiting. Curcumin in turmeric helps to calm down this inflammation, allowing our stomach to heal and function properly. It also has a natural digestive-aiding effect, which helps to move food through the digestive system more smoothly and prevent constipation. Additionally, curcumin has antimicrobial properties, which can help kill off any bacteria that may be causing the stomach problem.

Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis, also known as the Peru bark tree, has been used for centuries to treat upset stomachs. The tree's bark contains a group of compounds called alkaloids, which help to reduce inflammation and ease digestive issues. One of the main alkaloids, quinine, acts as a natural antacid, neutralizing stomach acid and providing quick relief from heartburn and indigestion. Additionally, cinchona's alkaloids have a soothing effect on the stomach lining, reducing irritation and discomfort. This makes Cinchona officinalis a popular natural remedy for stomach problems, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Its anti-inflammatory properties also help to alleviate cramps and spasms in the stomach, providing long-term relief from digestive issues.

Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum, also known as milk thistle, is a plant with a long history of use in traditional medicine. The seeds of this plant contain a unique compound called silymarin, which has been shown to protect the liver and help it heal. When the stomach is upset, the liver can become inflamed and not work properly. Silymarin in milk thistle helps to reduce this inflammation, allowing the liver to function better and remove toxins from the body. This can lead to a reduction in stomach discomfort, bloating, and nausea. Additionally, silymarin has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe the digestive tract and reduce irritation. By supporting the liver and reducing inflammation, Silybum marianum may be beneficial for people with an upset stomach.

Soothing Upset Stomach: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Effective Treatments

Upset stomach is a common problem where the digestive system is irritated, leading to discomfort, nausea, and sometimes vomiting.

If left untreated, it can lead to dehydration, electrolyte imbalance, and in severe cases, life-threatening conditions like stomach ulcers or perforation. It's essential to address the issue promptly, as prolonged discomfort can affect daily life and work. Herbs and herbal preparations can provide relief from upset stomach symptoms. Ginger (Zingiber officinale) is a well-known herb for its anti-inflammatory properties, which help to soothe the digestive tract and reduce nausea. It can be consumed as a decoction by steeping fresh ginger in hot water, or as a tincture by mixing it with water. Capsules containing dried ginger powder are also available for a more convenient option. Peppermint (Mentha x piperita) is another herb that can ease digestive discomfort.

Its menthol content helps to relax the muscles in the digestive tract and improve digestion. Peppermint can be consumed as a decoction, a tincture, or in capsule form. However, people with acid reflux should use it with caution, as it can worsen symptoms. Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) has anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can help to calm the digestive system. It's often used to treat stomach ulcers and heartburn. Licorice root can be consumed as a decoction or in capsule form. However, it's essential to note that excessive consumption can cause high blood pressure and other side effects. Herbal preparations can be more effective and convenient than relying on medication.

However, it's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedy, especially if you have underlying health conditions or take prescription medication.

What Are The Main Causes Of Upset Stomach?

An upset stomach can be caused by several things.

One of the main causes is Food Poisoning. This happens when we eat food that has bacteria or viruses in it, such as E. coli or Salmonella. When these germs enter our body, they can cause inflammation and irritation in the stomach, leading to symptoms like nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Another common cause is Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS). IBS is a chronic condition where the muscles in the stomach and intestines contract too much, causing pain and discomfort.

This can be triggered by stress, eating certain foods, or hormonal changes. People with IBS may experience bloating, abdominal pain, and changes in bowel movements. Gastroesophageal Reflux (GERD) is also a major cause of an upset stomach. GERD occurs when the muscle that separates the esophagus and stomach doesn't close properly, allowing stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus. This can cause heartburn, chest pain, and difficulty swallowing.

GERD is often linked to obesity, eating large meals, or lying down after eating.

Below are the main causes of upset stomach.

  • Food Poisoning: Food Poisoning is a cause of upset stomach because it occurs when we eat food that has germs or bacteria in it, such as E. coli or Salmonella, which can make our body sick and cause symptoms like nausea, vomiting, and stomach cramps.
  • Irritable Bowel Syndrome: Irritable Bowel Syndrome, or IBS, is a cause of an upset stomach because the muscles in the intestines contract too strongly, causing stomach pain and discomfort, and also because the digestive system has trouble moving food through the body.
  • Gastroesophageal Reflux: Gastroesophageal Reflux is a cause of upset stomach because stomach acid flows back up into the food pipe, causing discomfort, pain, and irritation.
  • Diverticulitis: Diverticulitis is a cause of upset stomach because small pouches in the colon become inflamed and irritated, leading to abdominal pain, nausea, and digestive discomfort.
  • Lactose Intolerance: Lactose intolerance is a cause of upset stomach because the body cannot digest lactose, a sugar found in milk, which leads to undigested lactose traveling through the digestive system and causing discomfort and pain.
  • Celiac Disease: Celiac Disease is a cause of upset stomach because people with this condition cannot digest gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, which can irritate their stomach lining and cause discomfort.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Upset Stomach?

If you have an upset stomach, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can irritate your digestive system.

One such herb is 'Angelica sinensis', also known as Dong Quai, which can cause stomach upset and interact with blood thinners. 'Capsicum annuum', or Cayenne pepper, is another herb to avoid as it can further irritate an already sensitive stomach. 'Cinchona officinalis', commonly known as Peruvian bark, contains quinine which can be harsh on the stomach and cause nausea.

'Echinacea purpurea' can stimulate the digestive system, but in people with stomach issues, it can cause more harm than good. 'Hydrastis canadensis', or Goldenseal, contains berberine, which can be toxic in large doses and worsen stomach problems. 'Ipecacuanha' can cause vomiting and stomach cramps, making it a herb to avoid when you're feeling queasy.

'Sanguinaria canadensis', or Bloodroot, is also best avoided as its active compounds can irritate the stomach lining.

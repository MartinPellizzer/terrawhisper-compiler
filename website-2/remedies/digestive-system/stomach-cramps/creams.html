9 Herbal Creams For Stomach Cramps

Herbal creams can be a great way to soothe stomach cramps.

These creams often contain ingredients like essential oils from plants. One example is Zingiber officinale, or ginger, which has anti-inflammatory properties that can help ease cramps. When applied topically, these creams can provide quick relief. Herbal teas, on the other hand, work from the inside out. They contain active ingredients that are absorbed into the bloodstream and then travel to the stomach, where they can help calm cramps.

Foeniculum vulgare, or fennel, is another example of an herb that can help with stomach cramps. It's a natural antacid that can help neutralize stomach acid and reduce inflammation. Another herb that's often used to help with stomach cramps is Mentha x piperita, or peppermint. It can help relax the muscles in the stomach and improve digestion. When you drink peppermint tea, the menthol in it can help ease cramps and reduce nausea.

By soothing the stomach and reducing inflammation, herbal teas and creams can help you feel better and get back to your normal routine.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains the bioactive constituents gingerol and shogaol, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce pain and inflammation in the stomach, alleviating cramps and discomfort. The warming effect of Zingiber officinale creams stimulates blood flow to the stomach, promoting relaxation of the muscles and reducing spasms. The antispasmodic properties of ginger also help to calm the stomach muscles, reducing the frequency and intensity of cramps.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Zingiber officinale creams can provide relief from stomach cramps.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of ginger essential oil, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments until melted.
  3. Remove melted mixture from heat and stir in ginger essential oil and vitamin E oil.
  4. Pour mixture into a small tin or jar and let it cool until solid.
  5. Apply a small amount to stomach area as needed for relief from cramps.

2. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare creams contains essential oils with bioactive constituents like anethole and fenchone.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help to relax the muscles in the stomach and reduce cramping. Anethole, in particular, has been shown to have a calming effect on the smooth muscle in the digestive tract, reducing spasms and discomfort. Fenchone, on the other hand, has been found to inhibit the production of prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that can cause muscle contractions and cramping.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the stomach muscles, Foeniculum vulgare creams can provide relief from stomach cramps and other digestive issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Foeniculum vulgare, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/2 cup of shea butter.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the dried Foeniculum vulgare to the coconut oil and let it infuse for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Mix the strained coconut oil with the shea butter and store the mixture in an airtight container in the fridge.

3. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains menthol, menthone, and limonene as its active constituents.

Menthol has a cooling effect that helps relax the stomach muscles, reducing cramps and spasms. Menthone has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and discomfort in the stomach area. Limonene has antimicrobial properties that prevent the growth of bacteria and other microorganisms that can cause stomach cramps.

By applying Mentha x piperita creams to the stomach area, these properties work together to provide relief from stomach cramps.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in the double boiler, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves to the melted mixture and let it infuse for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl, discarding the peppermint leaves.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify, then scoop it into a container and store it in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains saponins, flavonoids, and glycyrrhizin as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to soothe stomach cramps. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, has a direct effect on the stomach muscles, relaxing them and reducing spasms. The anti-inflammatory properties of saponins and flavonoids also help to reduce inflammation in the stomach, further alleviating cramps.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing stomach muscles, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams can provide relief from stomach cramps.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of distilled water, 1 cup of glycerin, 1/4 cup of glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, 1 tablespoon of beeswax, 1 tablespoon of coconut oil, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Combine 1 cup of distilled water and 1 cup of glycerin in a saucepan and heat over low heat, stirring until the glycerin dissolves.
  3. Add 1/4 cup of glycyrrhiza glabra root powder to the saucepan and stir well. Remove from heat and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture into a bowl and discard the solids. Add 1 tablespoon of beeswax, 1 tablespoon of coconut oil, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil to the mixture. Stir well until the beeswax melts.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and harden. Once hardened, your glycyrrhiza glabra cream is ready to use. Apply a thin layer to the affected area as needed.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the stomach lining, curcuma longa creams can provide relief from stomach cramps and promote overall digestive health.

7. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum creams contains silymarin, a mixture of flavonoids and polyphenols.

8. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contain several bioactive constituents, including vitamins A, C, and E, as well as minerals such as potassium and calcium.

9. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis creams contains alkaloids like quinine and cinchonine, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and ease pain in the stomach. Quinine also has a calming effect on the stomach muscles, relaxing them and alleviating cramps. Additionally, cinchonine has a natural antispasmodic effect, which helps to stop the stomach muscles from contracting and causing pain.

The combination of these properties in Cinchona officinalis creams makes it effective in providing relief from stomach cramps.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Gingerol blocks the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause pain and inflammation in the stomach. This action helps to reduce the severity of stomach cramps and alleviate nausea. The essential oil also contains another compound called shogaol, which has a similar effect.

By reducing inflammation and pain, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help to provide relief from stomach cramps.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Boil the water and add the ginger roots. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture and discard the solids. Let it cool down.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a bowl and add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba oil). Mix well.
  5. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight lid. Use 5-7 drops as needed to relieve stomach cramps.

2. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains high levels of menthol and menthone, two active constituents that help to relax the muscles in the stomach and alleviate cramps.

Menthone has a calming effect on the smooth muscle, which reduces spasms and discomfort. Menthol, on the other hand, has anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce swelling and ease pain. The antispasmodic properties of mentha x piperita essential oils also help to prevent spasms and cramps from occurring in the first place.

When applied topically or ingested in a diluted form, mentha x piperita essential oils can provide quick relief from stomach cramps.

  1. Gather materials: 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves, a clean glass jar, a cheesecloth or a coffee filter, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Wash the Mentha x piperita leaves with water, then pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Pack the leaves tightly into the glass jar, leaving about 1 inch of space at the top.
  4. Steep the leaves in the carrier oil for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place. Shake the jar daily to help the oil infuse.
  5. Strain the oil through the cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains a compound called anethole, which is responsible for its digestive benefits.

Anethole has anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce swelling and ease stomach cramps. Foeniculum vulgare essential oils also contains estragole, which acts as an antispasmodic, helping to relax the muscles in the stomach and intestines. The antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties of anethole and estragole in Foeniculum vulgare essential oils work together to provide relief from stomach cramps and spasms.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the stomach muscles, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help to alleviate stomach cramp discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Foeniculum vulgare leaves and flowers, and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat, then add the Foeniculum vulgare leaves and flowers.
  3. Let it simmer for 30 minutes, then turn off the heat and let it steep for 1 hour.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discard the solids, and let the liquid cool.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle, and use a dropper to extract 5-7 drops for use as needed to relieve stomach cramps.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains compounds like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which are known to have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation and relax the stomach muscles, providing relief from stomach cramps. Glycyrrhizin also has a natural diuretic effect, which can help to reduce fluid retention in the stomach, a common cause of cramping. The anti-spasmodic properties of flavonoids in Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils help to calm the stomach muscles, reducing spasms and cramps.

By combining these properties, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can help to provide quick relief from stomach cramps.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Crush the dried roots into small pieces using a mortar and pestle.
  3. Mix 1/4 cup of crushed roots with 1 cup of carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  4. Store the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily to help the roots infuse into the oil.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a separate container, discarding the solids. Your essential oil is now ready to use for stomach cramps.

5. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a compound called curcumin, which has potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Turmerone and atlantone, two other active constituents, also play a role in reducing inflammation and pain in the digestive system. These compounds help to relax the muscles in the stomach and reduce spasms, which can cause cramping and discomfort. By inhibiting the production of certain chemicals that cause inflammation, curcuma longa essential oils helps to soothe the stomach and reduce cramping.

As a result, it can help to provide relief from stomach cramps and promote a sense of calm in the digestive system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Curcuma longa roots and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil)
  2. Clean the Curcuma longa roots and chop them into small pieces
  3. Combine 1 cup of chopped Curcuma longa roots with 2 cups of carrier oil in a clean glass jar
  4. Steep the mixture in a warm place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily
  5. Strain the mixture and discard the Curcuma longa roots, keeping the essential oil for use

6. Cuminum cyminum

Cuminum cyminum essential oils contains a compound called cuminaldehyde, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce stomach cramps.

The essential oil also contains linalool, a compound that relaxes the muscles in the stomach and helps ease cramping. Cumene, another component of Cuminum cyminum essential oils, has a calming effect on the stomach lining, reducing inflammation and discomfort. The antispasmodic properties of Cuminum cyminum essential oils, thanks to its active constituents, help to relax the muscles in the stomach and alleviate cramping.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the stomach muscles, Cuminum cyminum essential oils helps to provide relief from stomach cramps.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cuminum cyminum seeds, a double boiler, a cheesecloth or coffee filter, a measuring cup, and a glass bottle with a lid.
  2. Dry the Cuminum cyminum seeds in a low-temperature oven at 150°F (65°C) for 2-3 hours.
  3. Add 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil) to the double boiler and heat it to 150°F (65°C).
  4. Add 1/2 cup of the dried Cuminum cyminum seeds to the carrier oil and let it steep for 2-3 hours.
  5. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth or coffee filter into the glass bottle, discard the seeds, and store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

7. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains the bioactive constituents carnosic acid, carnosol, and rosmanol, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation in the digestive system, which can cause stomach cramps. Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils also contains rosmarinic acid, a potent anti-spasmodic compound that can help relax the muscles in the stomach and intestines, reducing cramping and discomfort. Additionally, the oil's antioxidant properties help protect the digestive system from oxidative stress, which can contribute to stomach cramps.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing stomach muscles, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help alleviate stomach cramps and promote digestive health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosemary leaves (Rosmarinus officinalis) and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over low heat, then add the fresh Rosemary leaves and let it steep for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 10 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid mixture to a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Store it in a cool, dark place.
  5. To use, mix 5 drops of the Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil with 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut oil) and apply it to the affected area or inhale it.

8. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis essential oils contains a compound called quinine, which is responsible for its effectiveness in treating stomach cramps.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help to relax the muscles in the stomach and reduce cramping. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent stomach cramps from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help ease stomach cramps.

Certain teas, like peppermint and chamomile, have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe the stomach and reduce cramping. Ginger tea is also known to calm the digestive system.

These teas can provide relief from cramps, especially when consumed warm and in moderation.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for stomach cramps every day?

You can safely consume herbal teas for stomach cramps daily, but moderation is key.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and ginger, are gentle on the stomach. However, overdrinking can lead to issues like diarrhea or interact with medications.

Listen to your body and adjust your intake if needed.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in stomach cramps?

Herbal teas can start to help with stomach cramps within a few hours, but it may take longer for the cramps to completely go away.

Peppermint tea, for example, can calm the digestive system and provide relief in about 30 minutes to an hour.

Ginger tea may take a bit longer, but it can still bring relief within 2-3 hours.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for stomach cramps?

When you're feeling stomach cramps, a warm cup of herbal tea can be soothing.

Try drinking it in the morning or early afternoon.

These times usually help calm your stomach before meals, and give you a gentle boost to feel better.

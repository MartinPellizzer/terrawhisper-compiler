What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have chronic hepatitis?

If you have chronic hepatitis, there are some herbal essential oils you should avoid.

One of them is 'Melissa officinalis' or lemon balm. It can lower blood sugar levels, but it can also make blood thinner and increase the risk of bleeding, which is a concern for people with liver problems. Another oil to avoid is 'Valeriana officinalis' or valerian root. It can affect the liver and kidneys and interact with medications, such as those used to treat hepatitis.

You should also avoid 'Foeniculum vulgare' or fennel oil. It can stimulate the liver and cause it to work harder, which may be problematic for people with liver disease. Another oil to be cautious with is 'Angelica sinensis' or dong quai. It can cause bleeding and interact with medications, so it's best to consult a doctor before using it. Lastly, 'Commiphora molmol' or myrrh oil should be used with caution.

It can cause liver damage in high doses, so it's essential to follow the recommended usage.

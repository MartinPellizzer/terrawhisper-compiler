What Are The Main Causes Of Chronic Hepatitis?

Chronic hepatitis is a long-term inflammation of the liver, which can lead to liver damage and other health problems.

The main causes of chronic hepatitis are infections with certain viruses. One of the main causes is an infection with the Hepatitis C virus (HCV). HCV is a blood-borne virus that is spread through contact with infected blood, which can happen through sharing needles, tattoo equipment, or medical procedures without proper sterilization. When HCV infects the liver, it can cause chronic hepatitis, which can lead to liver scarring and even liver failure.

Another cause of chronic hepatitis is an infection with the Hepatitis B virus (HBV). HBV is also a blood-borne virus that is spread through contact with infected blood, as well as through sex with an infected person. HBV can cause chronic hepatitis in some people, which can lead to liver damage and liver cancer. HIV, the virus that causes AIDS, can also increase the risk of developing chronic hepatitis. This is because HIV can damage the immune system, making it harder for the body to fight off infections, including HBV and HCV.

If someone with HIV is also infected with HBV or HCV, they are more likely to develop chronic hepatitis, which can worsen their overall health.

Below are the main causes of chronic hepatitis.

  • HCV: HCV (Hepatitis C virus) is a cause of chronic hepatitis because it can live inside liver cells for a long time, making it difficult for the immune system to kill it completely and leading to ongoing inflammation and damage.
  • HBV: HBV, or hepatitis B virus, becomes a cause of chronic hepatitis because it often does not completely clear from the body after an initial infection, leading to long-term inflammation and damage in the liver.
  • HIV: HIV is a cause of chronic hepatitis because it weakens the immune system, making it harder for the body to fight off the hepatitis virus that causes liver inflammation and damage.
  • HGV: HGV (Hepatitis G Virus) is a cause of chronic hepatitis because it stays in the body for a long time, causing inflammation and damage to the liver cells.
  • HDV: HDV infects the liver cells because it needs the hepatitis B virus (HBV) to replicate, and when it combines with HBV, it causes chronic hepatitis, leading to liver damage and inflammation.
  • HEV: HEV (Hepatitis E virus) causes chronic hepatitis in some people because their immune system is not strong enough to fight the virus completely, allowing it to stay in the body and keep damaging liver cells over time.

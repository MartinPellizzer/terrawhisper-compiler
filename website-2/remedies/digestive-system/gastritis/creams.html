1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains a compound called curcumin, which is a strong anti-inflammatory agent.

Curcumin has potent antioxidant properties, neutralizing free radicals that can irritate the stomach lining and cause gastritis. The active constituents in Curcuma longa creams, including curcuminoids and polyphenols, help to reduce inflammation and protect the stomach lining from damage. By reducing inflammation and protecting the stomach lining, Curcuma longa creams can help to alleviate symptoms of gastritis, such as stomach pain and nausea.

The bioactive constituents in Curcuma longa creams also have a protective effect on the mucous lining of the stomach, helping to prevent further irritation and inflammation.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder (Curcuma longa), 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of olive oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add turmeric powder, shea butter, and olive oil to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Pour it into a heat-proof container or a silicone ice cube tray.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify completely. Once solid, you can use the Curcuma longa cream as needed to help soothe gastritis symptoms.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of gingerols and shogaols in Zingiber officinale creams can help soothe and heal the stomach lining, reducing gastritis symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Zingiber officinale root powder, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of essential oil (like lavender or peppermint).
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth and combined.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the Zingiber officinale root powder and vitamin E oil until well combined.
  4. Add the Zingiber officinale mixture to the melted oil mixture and stir well. Add the essential oil and stir again.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and harden. Once hardened, use as a cream for gastritis relief. Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent gastritis from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent gastritis from forming because it can soothe and calm the stomach.

Certain herbs like peppermint and chamomile have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce inflammation and ease digestive issues. This can help protect the stomach lining and prevent irritation that leads to gastritis.

Regular herbal tea consumption may help keep the stomach healthy.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for gastritis every day?

For people with gastritis, drinking herbal teas daily can be a bit tricky.

Some herbs like peppermint and ginger can calm the stomach, but others like chamomile and licorice root might make symptoms worse.

It's best to start with small amounts and see how your body reacts, then adjust your tea choice accordingly to find what works for you.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in gastritis?

Herbal teas can start to help with gastritis symptoms after a few days.

Some people feel better within 24 to 48 hours. As your body adjusts, you may notice relief from heartburn, bloating, and discomfort.

It may take longer, up to a week or two, for your digestive system to fully settle and heal.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for gastritis?

For gastritis, it's best to drink herbal tea in the morning or early afternoon.

This helps soothe your stomach before eating a meal. Avoid drinking herbal tea too close to bedtime, as it might disturb your sleep.

A gentle cup in the morning or a midday pick-me-up can be very calming for your stomach.

10 Herbal Teas For Gastritis

Herbal teas can be a great way to help manage gastritis symptoms.

Gastritis is inflammation of the stomach lining, and it can cause pain, bloating, and digestive issues. Some herbal teas have anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe the stomach and reduce inflammation. One example is licorice root tea, made from Glycyrrhiza glabra. Licorice root has been shown to have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce stomach inflammation and alleviate symptoms of gastritis. Ginger tea, made from Zingiber officinale, is another option.

Ginger has natural anti-inflammatory compounds called gingerols and shogaols, which can help reduce nausea and pain associated with gastritis. Fennel tea, made from Foeniculum vulgare, is also a popular choice. Fennel seeds have a natural antacid property, which can help neutralize stomach acid and reduce inflammation. When you drink herbal teas, the active compounds in the herbs can help calm the stomach and reduce inflammation. This can lead to a reduction in symptoms such as pain, bloating, and nausea.

As a result, you may find that you can eat and digest food more easily, and you may experience a reduction in the frequency and severity of gastritis attacks.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains licorice root, which is rich in glycyrrhizin and flavonoids.

Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in the stomach and esophagus, common symptoms of gastritis. The flavonoids, particularly liquiritin and isoliquiritin, have antioxidant properties that protect the stomach lining from damage caused by free radicals. The mucilages in licorice root also form a protective barrier on the stomach lining, preventing acid and pepsin from irritating it and reducing inflammation.

This combination of properties makes Glycyrrhiza glabra teas a potential treatment for gastritis, helping to soothe and heal the stomach lining.

  1. Gather 2 teaspoons of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the root in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) to the tea if desired.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the tea 2-3 times a day for gastritis relief.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas contains compounds like apigenin, luteolin, and sesquiterpene coumarins that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation in the stomach lining, which is a common cause of gastritis. The sesquiterpene coumarins in Foeniculum vulgare teas have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, such as COX-2, which contribute to stomach inflammation. The tea's antioxidant properties, particularly the action of apigenin and luteolin, also help protect the stomach lining from oxidative damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Foeniculum vulgare teas may help alleviate symptoms of gastritis, such as stomach pain and nausea.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water, 2-3 sprigs of Foeniculum vulgare (also known as fennel), and a tea strainer.
  2. Remove the fennel leaves from the stems and place them in the tea strainer.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the fennel in the strainer. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup using the strainer. Add 1 tablespoon of honey, if desired, to help soothe the stomach.
  5. Drink the tea slowly and enjoy. Repeat as needed, but not more than 3 times a day.

4. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas contains several bioactive constituents that make it beneficial for gastritis.

Menthyl acetate in the tea also has a protective effect on the mucous membranes in the stomach, preventing damage and promoting healing. By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Mentha x piperita tea can help alleviate symptoms of gastritis and promote a healthy digestive system.

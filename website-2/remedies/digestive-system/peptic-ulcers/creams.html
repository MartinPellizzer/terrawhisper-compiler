1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains a compound called curcumin, which is a powerful antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent.

Curcumin has potent anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in the stomach lining, which can cause ulcers. The bioactive constituents in Curcuma longa creams also include polyphenols and flavonoids, which work together to protect the stomach lining from damage and promote healing. By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Curcuma longa creams can help alleviate symptoms of peptic ulcers, such as pain and discomfort.

The antioxidant properties of curcumin also help prevent further damage to the stomach lining, allowing it to heal naturally.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder (Curcuma longa), 2 tablespoons of aloe vera gel, 2 drops of tea tree oil, and 2 drops of lavender oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix shea butter, turmeric powder, aloe vera gel, tea tree oil, and lavender oil until well combined.
  4. Slowly add the dry mixture to the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture, stirring constantly until smooth and well combined.
  5. Remove from heat and let cool until solid. Then, scoop into a container and store in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

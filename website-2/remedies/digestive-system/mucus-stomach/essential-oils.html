1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

These gingerols and shogaols have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in the stomach. The oils also contain beta-carophyllene, which has a spasmolytic effect, helping to relax the muscles in the stomach and reduce spasms that can lead to mucus buildup. The anti-inflammatory and spasmolytic properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils help to reduce the production of mucus in the stomach and alleviate symptoms of digestive issues.

The oils also have a warming effect, which can help stimulate digestion and relieve nausea and discomfort associated with mucus buildup in the stomach.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger root and 2 cups of water.
  2. Peel the ginger root and chop it into small pieces.
  3. In a saucepan, combine the chopped ginger and water. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl. Discard the ginger solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and let it sit at room temperature for 2-3 days to allow the oil to separate and rise to the top.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

Glycyrrhizin reduces inflammation in the stomach, which helps to break down excess mucus. The essential oils also contain flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have antioxidant properties that soothe and protect the stomach lining. These properties help to reduce mucus production and prevent the growth of bacteria that can cause stomach problems.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the stomach lining, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can help to alleviate stomach mucus issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 2 cups of water.
  2. Chop the roots into small pieces and place them in a saucepan.
  3. Add the water to the saucepan and bring to a boil over high heat.
  4. Reduce heat to low and let it simmer for 2 hours, then let it cool.
  5. Strain the liquid and use a distillation apparatus to separate the essential oil from the liquid, then store the oil in a dark glass bottle.

3. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a compound called curcumenol, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and ease digestion in the stomach.

The oil also contains demethoxycurcumenol, a compound that helps break down mucus and ease congestion. The antioxidant properties of Curcuma longa essential oils help protect the stomach lining from damage caused by excessive mucus production. The oil's anti-inflammatory properties also help reduce inflammation in the stomach, which can contribute to mucus buildup.

The demethoxycurcumenol in Curcuma longa essential oils helps to thin and break down mucus, making it easier to digest and reducing stomach discomfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Curcuma longa roots and 2 cups of water in a large saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat and add the Curcuma longa roots. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass container. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil to the liquid. Mix well.
  5. Let the mixture steep for 2-3 hours in a cool, dark place. Then, strain it again and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

4. Eucalyptus globulus

  2. Use a 1 cup of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and mix it with the Eucalyptus globulus leaves in a bowl.
  3. Steep the Eucalyptus globulus leaves in the carrier oil for 2-3 weeks, shaking the bowl every day.
  4. After the steeping time, strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container.

5. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains a compound called anethole, which is responsible for its carminative and anti-inflammatory properties.

Anethole helps to relax the muscles in the stomach, reducing spasms and cramps that can cause discomfort. The essential oil also contains fenchone, which has a calming effect on the digestive system and helps to reduce inflammation in the stomach lining. Foeniculum vulgare essential oils has been traditionally used to treat digestive issues, including indigestion and bloating, due to its ability to reduce mucus production and improve digestion.

By reducing inflammation and improving digestion, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help to alleviate stomach discomfort caused by excess mucus.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Foeniculum vulgare seeds (aniseed) and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba oil or sweet almond oil)
  2. Place the aniseed seeds in a clean glass jar and cover them with carrier oil. Stir well.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the oil to extract from the seeds.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the extracted oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 5-7 drops of the oil per day to help with stomach mucus.

7. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains limonene, menthone, and menthol, which are the main bioactive constituents responsible for its properties.

These compounds have natural expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties, making Mentha x piperita essential oils effective in reducing mucus in the stomach.

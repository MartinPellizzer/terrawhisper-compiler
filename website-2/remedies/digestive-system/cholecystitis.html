What are the causes of cholecystitis?

Cholecystitis is a serious medical condition where the gallbladder becomes inflamed.

There are several causes of cholecystitis, and we'll take a closer look at each one. Gallstones are a common cause of cholecystitis. These small, hard stones form in the gallbladder and can block the flow of bile, causing inflammation and pain. When gallstones block the bile duct, the bile builds up and causes the gallbladder to become inflamed. Gallstones can be made of cholesterol, bilirubin, or other substances, and they can cause symptoms like severe abdominal pain, fever, and nausea. Another cause of cholecystitis is a bacterial infection. Bacteria like E. coli, Klebsiella, and Streptococcus can infect the gallbladder and cause inflammation. When bacteria enter the gallbladder, they can multiply quickly and cause an infection.

This type of infection is often accompanied by symptoms like fever, chills, and abdominal pain. Trauma is another cause of cholecystitis. This can include injuries to the abdomen, such as being hit by a car or falling and hitting the abdomen. When the gallbladder is injured, it can become inflamed and cause symptoms like abdominal pain, fever, and nausea. Tumors can also cause cholecystitis. Benign or cancerous tumors can grow in the gallbladder and cause inflammation. When tumors grow in the gallbladder, they can block the bile duct and cause bile to build up, leading to inflammation and pain. Lastly, AIDS can increase the risk of developing cholecystitis. People with AIDS are more susceptible to infections, including bacterial infections that can cause cholecystitis.

This is because their immune system is weakened, making it harder for their body to fight off infections.

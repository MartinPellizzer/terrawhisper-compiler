What Are The Main Causes Of Cholecystitis?

Cholecystitis is an inflammation of the gallbladder that can be caused by several factors.

One of the main causes is Gallstones, hard, stone-like objects that form in the gallbladder and block the bile ducts. When a gallstone blocks the bile duct, bile can't flow out of the gallbladder, causing it to swell and become inflamed. Gallstones can be made of cholesterol, bile salts, or a combination of both. Another cause of cholecystitis is Acalculous, which means there are no gallstones present.

In acalculous cholecystitis, the gallbladder becomes inflamed due to a bacterial infection, usually caused by bacteria that enter the gallbladder through the bloodstream. This type of cholecystitis is more common in people who are critically ill, have severe injuries, or are undergoing surgery. Polyps, which are growths of tissue, can also cause cholecystitis. When polyps grow in the gallbladder, they can block the bile ducts, causing bile to accumulate and leading to inflammation.

Polyps can be benign or cancerous, and in some cases, they can cause severe symptoms.

Below are the main causes of cholecystitis.

  • Gallstones: Gallstones can cause cholecystitis because they get stuck in the bile ducts, blocking the flow of bile and causing inflammation in the gallbladder.
  • Acalculous: Acalculous cholecystitis occurs when the gallbladder becomes inflamed due to a lack of gallstones, often caused by a decrease in blood flow to the gallbladder or an infection in the bile ducts.
  • Polyps: Polyps are a cause of cholecystitis because they can grow inside the gallbladder and block the bile duct, leading to inflammation and infection of the gallbladder.
  • Trauma: Trauma is a cause of cholecystitis because it can cause injury to the gallbladder, which can then become inflamed and infected, leading to the painful condition of cholecystitis.
  • Infection: Infection from a type of bacteria called bacteria (like E. coli or Klebsiella) can cause cholecystitis when they spread to the gallbladder and trigger inflammation.

What Are The Main Causes Of Difficulty Swallowing?

Difficulty swallowing, also known as dysphagia, can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is gastroesophageal reflux, where stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus, causing pain and discomfort while swallowing. This can irritate the esophagus and make it harder for food to pass through. Another cause of difficulty swallowing is an esophageal stricture, which is a narrowing of the esophagus.

This narrowing can be caused by inflammation, scarring, or the growth of new tissue. As a result, food and liquids have trouble passing through the narrowed area, making swallowing painful and difficult. Esophageal cancer is also a cause of difficulty swallowing. In this case, cancer cells grow in the esophagus and can block the passage of food, or make swallowing painful.

This type of cancer can also cause weight loss and other symptoms.

Below are the main causes of difficulty swallowing.

  • Gastroesophageal reflux: When stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus due to gastroesophageal reflux, it can irritate and inflame the esophagus's lining, causing pain and discomfort that makes it hard to swallow food and liquids.
  • Esophageal stricture: An esophageal stricture is a cause of difficulty swallowing because the narrowing of the esophagus' passage makes it hard for food to pass through, causing discomfort and pain when trying to eat.
  • Esophageal cancer: Esophageal cancer makes it hard to swallow because the cancer grows inside the food pipe (esophagus), blocking food from passing through and causing a sensation of being stuck in the throat.
  • Myasthenia gravis: Myasthenia gravis is a cause of difficulty swallowing because the muscles that control the throat and mouth, including the muscles used for swallowing, are weak and unable to work properly together.
  • Parkinson's disease: Parkinson's disease makes swallowing difficult because it damages nerves in the brain that control the muscles used for swallowing, causing them to move slowly or not work properly.
  • Multiple sclerosis: Multiple sclerosis causes difficulty swallowing because it damages the nerves that control the muscles in the throat, making it hard for food to pass through the esophagus and into the stomach.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Difficulty Swallowing?

If you have difficulty swallowing, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can be hard to swallow or cause throat irritation.

One such herb is lobelia (Lobelia inflata), as its seeds are small and can be a choking hazard if inhaled. Another herb to avoid is slippery elm (Ulmus rubra), which can cause a thick, slippery texture in the throat, making swallowing more difficult.

Dandelion root (Taraxacum officinale) is also not recommended, as its small, hard seeds can be a choking risk, especially for people with swallowing issues. Additionally, goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) and sassafras (Sassafras albidum) root bark can be irritating to the throat and cause discomfort while swallowing.

These herbs should be used with caution or avoided altogether to minimize the risk of complications.

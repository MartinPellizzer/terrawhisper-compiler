1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Gingerol and shogaol, another key constituent, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation and discomfort in the digestive tract. This can be beneficial for people with hiatus hernia, as inflammation and irritation in the esophagus and stomach can exacerbate symptoms. The warming and spasmolytic properties of gingerol can also help relax the muscles in the digestive tract, allowing for more efficient digestion and reducing the likelihood of stomach acid reflux.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the muscles, Zingiber officinale essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of hiatus hernia.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale roots, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Clean and chop the Zingiber officinale roots into small pieces, then fill the glass jar 3/4 full with the chopped roots.
  3. Pour 1 cup of carrier oil over the chopped roots, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the roots infuse into the oil.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discard the solids, and use the oil as needed for hiatus hernia relief.

2. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains the active constituents anethole and fenchone, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

Anethole has a calming effect on the digestive system, reducing inflammation and discomfort associated with hiatus hernia. Fenchone, on the other hand, has a natural relaxant effect on the muscles in the digestive tract, helping to alleviate symptoms of acid reflux and heartburn. The essential oil's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties also help to soothe and calm the digestive system, reducing the frequency and severity of hiatus hernia symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation in the digestive tract, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help to alleviate the symptoms of hiatus hernia.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds and 1 cup of carrier oil (such as coconut or olive oil).
  2. Heat 1 cup of water in a saucepan and add the Foeniculum vulgare seeds. Steep for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture and discard the seeds. Mix the strained liquid with the carrier oil in a 1:10 ratio.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a dark glass bottle and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the bottle daily.
  5. Strain the mixture again and discard the solids. Your Foeniculum vulgare essential oil is now ready to use for hiatus hernia relief.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains the bioactive constituents glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These properties help to reduce inflammation and discomfort in the esophagus and stomach, common symptoms of hiatus hernia. Glycyrrhizin also has a calming effect on the digestive system, which can help to alleviate heartburn and acid reflux associated with hiatus hernia. The flavonoids in Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils have antioxidant properties, which can help to protect the mucous membranes in the esophagus and stomach from damage.

By reducing inflammation and promoting digestive health, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils may provide relief from hiatus hernia symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 4 cups of water. Transfer the roots to a large glass jar.
  2. Combine the roots and water in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 3 hours, then let it cool.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another large glass jar. Discard the roots.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a still or a distillation apparatus. Distill the liquid using steam distillation to separate the essential oil from the water.
  5. Collect the essential oil and store it in a dark glass bottle. Use 2-3 drops of the oil in a diffuser or mix it with a carrier oil for massage.

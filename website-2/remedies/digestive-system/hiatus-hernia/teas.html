Hiatus Hernia in Pregnancy: 11 Teas for Relief

When a woman is pregnant, her body goes through many changes.

One of these changes can put pressure on the stomach, leading to a condition called hiatus hernia. This happens when part of the stomach bulges up into the chest through an opening in the diaphragm. For pregnant women, hiatus hernia can be especially painful and uncomfortable, making it hard to eat and sleep. It can also cause heartburn, acid reflux, and stomach cramps. As the baby grows, the pressure on the stomach increases, making symptoms worse. This can be frustrating and stressful for pregnant women, who already have to deal with other changes in their body. Pregnant women with hiatus hernia may find it hard to enjoy their favorite foods, as eating can trigger pain and discomfort.

They may also have trouble sleeping, as lying down can make symptoms worse. In some cases, hiatus hernia can even lead to complications, such as bleeding or difficulty breathing. To manage these symptoms, pregnant women often turn to natural remedies, rather than relying on medication. One way pregnant women can help manage hiatus hernia symptoms is by drinking certain herbal teas. These teas can help soothe the stomach, reduce inflammation, and ease pain and discomfort. By drinking these teas regularly, pregnant women may be able to reduce their symptoms and improve their quality of life. These teas work by promoting relaxation, reducing stress, and calming the digestive system.

They can also help to strengthen the muscles in the stomach and chest, making it easier to manage symptoms.

Specifically, ginger has been shown to relax the lower esophageal sphincter, which can help to prevent stomach acid from flowing back up into the esophagus and causing symptoms of hiatus hernia.

To use ginger tea for hiatus hernia, you can try drinking a warm cup of ginger tea after meals to help soothe and calm the digestive system.

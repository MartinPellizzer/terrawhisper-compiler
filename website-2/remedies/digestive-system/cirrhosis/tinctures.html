11 Herbal Tinctures For Cirrhosis

Herbal tinctures have been used for centuries to help manage cirrhosis, a condition where the liver becomes scarred and not able to function properly.

The good news is that certain herbs have been found to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce liver damage and improve overall health. Let's take a look at three examples: Curcuma longa, also known as turmeric, which has been shown to reduce liver inflammation and improve liver function; Silybum marianum, or milk thistle, which is thought to protect the liver from damage and promote healing; and Glycyrrhiza glabra, or licorice root, which has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe liver inflammation. These herbs work by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation in the liver, which can slow down the progression of cirrhosis.

They also help improve liver function by increasing the production of enzymes that help detoxify the liver. For people with cirrhosis, using herbal tinctures can be a great way to manage symptoms and improve quality of life. Drinking herbal teas made from these herbs can help reduce fatigue, improve appetite, and even reduce the risk of liver cancer. Additionally, they can help support liver regeneration, which can lead to improved liver function and a reduced need for medication.

It's essential to talk to a healthcare professional before starting any new treatment, including herbal tinctures, to ensure they are safe and effective for your specific situation.

1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa tinctures contains curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin as its active constituents.

These compounds have potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help protect the liver from damage. The curcuminoids in Curcuma longa tinctures also have hepatoprotective effects, which can help reduce liver inflammation and scarring associated with cirrhosis. The antioxidants in Curcuma longa tinctures neutralize free radicals that can damage liver cells, while its anti-inflammatory properties reduce inflammation and oxidative stress.

By reducing liver inflammation and protecting liver cells, Curcuma longa tinctures may help slow the progression of cirrhosis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Curcuma longa roots and 2 cups of vodka (at least 90 proof). Wear gloves to avoid skin contact.
  2. Chop 1 cup of Curcuma longa roots into small pieces and place them in a clean glass jar.
  3. Pour 2 cups of vodka over the chopped Curcuma longa roots in the jar, making sure the roots are fully covered.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will help the Curcuma longa roots infuse into the vodka.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

2. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum tinctures contains silymarin, a mixture of flavonoids and lignans, including silibinin, silidianin, and silicristin.

By reducing liver inflammation and improving liver function, silymarin in Silybum marianum tinctures can help to slow the progression of cirrhosis.

  2. Combine Silybum marianum flowers and vodka in the glass jar. Stir well to cover flowers.

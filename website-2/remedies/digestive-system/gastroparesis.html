Gastroparesis Natural Treatment: Causes and Medicinal Herbs for Relief

What are the causes of gastroparesis?

Gastroparesis is a condition where the stomach muscles don't work properly, slowing down digestion.

There are several main causes of gastroparesis. One common cause is diabetes, particularly type 1 diabetes. When blood sugar levels are high, they can damage the nerves that control the stomach muscles, leading to gastroparesis. Alcoholism is another cause of gastroparesis. Drinking too much alcohol can damage the nerves and muscles in the stomach, causing food to move slowly or not at all. This is often seen in people who have a long history of heavy drinking.

Neurological disorders, such as multiple sclerosis and Parkinson's disease, can also cause gastroparesis. These conditions damage the nerves that control the stomach muscles, leading to slowed digestion. Parkinson's disease, in particular, can cause gastroparesis due to the damage it causes to the nerves that control the stomach and intestines. Hypothyroidism, or an underactive thyroid gland, can also contribute to gastroparesis. When the thyroid gland doesn't produce enough hormones, it can affect the nerves and muscles in the stomach, leading to slowed digestion. In some cases, gastroparesis can be caused by other factors, such as certain medications, surgery, or eating disorders like anorexia nervosa.

However, diabetes, alcoholism, neurological disorders, Parkinson's disease, and hypothyroidism are the most common causes of gastroparesis.

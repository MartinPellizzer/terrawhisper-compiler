1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to ease digestive discomfort and reduce bloating. The essential oils also contain sesquiterpenes, specifically beta-sesquiphellandrene and zingiberene, which have carminative effects. Carminatives help to relieve gas and bloating by relaxing the muscles in the digestive tract and improving digestion.

By reducing inflammation and improving digestion, Zingiber officinale essential oils may help to alleviate bloating symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale roots, a glass jar with a lid, a cup of carrier oil (such as coconut or jojoba oil), and a cheesecloth.
  2. Wash the Zingiber officinale roots with water, then pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Place the Zingiber officinale roots in the glass jar, then pour the carrier oil over them. The roots should be completely covered.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will help the roots release their oil.
  5. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth into another container, then discard the solids. Store the Zingiber officinale essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

2. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains the bioactive constituents anethole, fenchone, and limonene.

These compounds have carminative properties, which means they help to relieve gas and bloating in the digestive system. Anethole, in particular, has been shown to relax the muscles in the intestines, allowing gas to pass through more easily. Fenchone and limonene have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe an irritated digestive tract and reduce discomfort.

By using Foeniculum vulgare essential oils, you may be able to alleviate bloating and promote a sense of relief in your digestive system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil, like jojoba or sweet almond oil.
  2. Fill a clean glass jar with 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds and add 2 cups of carrier oil. Stir well.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the seeds release their oil.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Foeniculum vulgare essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops per day to help with bloating.

3. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a compound called curcumin, which is a strong anti-inflammatory agent.

It also contains demethoxycurcumin and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which are known for their antioxidant properties. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of curcumin and its compounds help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the digestive system, which can cause bloating. Curcumin also has a direct effect on the gut, improving digestion and reducing gas production in the intestines.

By reducing inflammation and improving digestion, Curcuma longa essential oils can help alleviate bloating symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Curcuma longa rhizomes, a small saucepan, and a cheesecloth.
  2. Wash the Curcuma longa rhizomes with 2 cups of water, then chop them into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped rhizomes with 2 cups of water in the saucepan, and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce the heat to low and let the mixture simmer for 2 hours. Strain the liquid through the cheesecloth into a bowl.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 24 hours, allowing the essential oil to separate from the water. Collect the essential oil and store it in a dark glass bottle.

4. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains a compound called cinnamaldehyde, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, a common cause of bloating. Cinnamaldehyde also has a direct effect on the digestive system by stimulating digestive enzymes, which aid in breaking down food and reducing gas buildup. By reducing inflammation and stimulating digestive enzymes, Cinnamomum verum essential oils can help alleviate bloating caused by digestive issues.

  1. Get 2 cups of Cinnamomum verum bark and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba oil).
  2. Grind the Cinnamomum verum bark into small pieces using a coffee grinder.
  3. Combine the ground Cinnamomum verum bark with the carrier oil in a saucepan.
  4. Heat the mixture over low heat (around 100°F to 150°F) for 2 hours, then let it cool.
  5. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or coffee filter, and discard the solids. Store the essential oil in a glass bottle.

5. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains limonene, menthone, and menthol as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have carminative and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce bloating by easing digestive discomfort. Limonene, a natural solvent, stimulates digestive enzymes and improves digestion, while menthone and menthol have a calming effect on the stomach and intestines. By reducing digestive discomfort and inflammation, Mentha x piperita essential oils can help alleviate bloating symptoms.

  2. Heat the water in the saucepan until it reaches a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer.

  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container, discarding the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 2-3 hours to allow the oil to separate and rise to the top, then carefully pour it off and store it in a dark glass bottle.

6. Cuminum cyminum

Cuminum cyminum essential oils contains limonene, cuminaldehyde, and linalool as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have carminative and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to ease digestive discomfort and reduce bloating. Cuminaldehyde, in particular, has a direct impact on the digestive system by relaxing the muscles in the gastrointestinal tract, allowing for better digestion and reduced gas buildup.   3. Add 1/4 cup of Cuminum cyminum seeds to the hot oil and let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl, discarding the seeds.
  5. Store the Cuminum cyminum essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use 5-7 drops as needed to help with bloating.

8. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains active constituents like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and discomfort associated with bloating. Glycyrrhizin in particular has been shown to inhibit the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that can cause smooth muscle contractions and lead to bloating.

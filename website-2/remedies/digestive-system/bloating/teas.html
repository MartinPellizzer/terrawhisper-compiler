11 Herbal Teas For Bloating

Herbal teas can be a great help when you're feeling bloated.

Bloating happens when gas builds up in your stomach, making you feel uncomfortable and puffy. Herbal teas work by helping to break down the gas and also soothing your digestive system. Ginger has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can help to ease digestive discomfort and reduce bloating.

Another example is fennel tea, made from the seeds of the Foeniculum vulgare plant. Fennel has been used for centuries to help with digestive issues, and its active compounds can help to relax the muscles in your stomach and reduce gas buildup. Licorice root tea, made from the Glycyrrhiza glabra plant, is also a popular choice for relieving bloating. Licorice root has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to soothe your digestive system and reduce inflammation in the stomach. Drinking herbal teas can bring many benefits to your life.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains bioactive compounds like gingerols and shogaols that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing the severity of bloating. The active constituent 6-gingerol has a vasodilatory effect, which means it widens blood vessels and improves blood flow, helping to ease digestion and reduce swelling. Additionally, the phenolic compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, have antioxidant properties that help to neutralize free radicals and reduce inflammation in the digestive system.

By reducing inflammation and improving digestion, Zingiber officinale teas can help alleviate bloating and other digestive issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1 teaspoon of dried Zingiber officinale root.
  2. Boil the water in a pot and let it cool for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Add the Zingiber officinale root to a tea infuser or directly to the pot.
  4. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes and then strain it.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the tea 2-3 times a day to help with bloating.

2. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas contains a compound called Fenchone, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce bloating.

It also contains Estragole and Anethole, which are known for their carminative effects, meaning they help to ease digestive discomfort and relieve gas. The Anethole in Foeniculum vulgare tea has a direct action on the smooth muscles in the digestive tract, relaxing them and allowing for easier passage of gas and food. The Estragole and Fenchone also have a natural ability to inhibit the growth of bacteria that can cause bloating, such as Escherichia and Klebsiella.

By reducing inflammation, relaxing digestive muscles, and inhibiting bacteria growth, Foeniculum vulgare tea can provide relief from bloating.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Foeniculum vulgare (also known as fennel seeds) and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Place the fennel seeds in a tea infuser or a small piece of cheesecloth.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the fennel seeds and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the fennel seeds.
  5. Drink the tea after meals to help with bloating and gas.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains compounds like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which help reduce water retention and ease bloating.

Glycyrrhizin has a mineralocorticoid effect, similar to aldosterone, that helps the body retain less sodium and water. The flavonoids in Glycyrrhiza glabra tea, such as liquiritin and liquiritigenin, have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe the digestive system and reduce inflammation in the gut. This combination of compounds can help the body to relax and reduce the discomfort associated with bloating.

By reducing water retention and soothing the digestive system, Glycyrrhiza glabra teas can help alleviate symptoms of bloating.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 1 cup of water.
  2. Boil the water in a pot and reduce heat to low.
  3. Add the dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots to the water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.

1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa tinctures contains bioactive constituents like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce inflammation and neutralize free radicals in the stomach, which can contribute to hyperacidity. Curcumin, in particular, inhibits the production of stomach acid by blocking the activity of an enzyme called H. pylori, which is often associated with hyperacidity. The antioxidant properties of curcuma longa tinctures also help protect the stomach lining from damage caused by excess acid, reducing the risk of ulcers and other complications.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the stomach lining, curcuma longa tinctures can help alleviate symptoms of hyperacidity and promote a healthier digestive system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Curcuma longa roots, a glass jar with a lid, and 2 cups of vodka.
  2. Chop the Curcuma longa roots into small pieces and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour 2 cups of vodka over the chopped roots in the jar. Stir and ensure all roots are covered.
  4. Seal the jar with a lid and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another jar. Discard the solids and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

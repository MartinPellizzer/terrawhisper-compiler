11 herbal remedies for smokers to ease acid reflux

Smokers often experience acid reflux, which is when stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus.

This is because smoking can weaken the muscle at the end of the esophagus, called the lower esophageal sphincter, allowing acid to rise up. When acid reflux happens, it can cause a burning sensation in the chest and throat, and sometimes even lead to difficulty swallowing. For smokers, this can be a daily struggle, making it hard to eat and even sleep comfortably. As a result, smokers with acid reflux may have to cut back on their favorite foods, avoid lying down after meals, and take medication to manage their symptoms. This can be frustrating and affect their quality of life.

Smoking not only damages the lower esophageal sphincter, but it can also slow down digestion, allowing food to stay in the stomach for longer periods. This can trigger acid reflux, as the stomach acid has more time to flow back up into the esophagus. Furthermore, smoking can cause inflammation in the stomach and esophagus, making them more sensitive and prone to acid reflux. For smokers with acid reflux, herbal teas can be a natural way to find relief. These teas are made from plants that have anti-inflammatory properties and can help soothe the stomach and esophagus. Some herbal teas can also help calm the digestive system and improve digestion, reducing the risk of acid reflux.

By drinking these teas, smokers can take a step towards managing their symptoms and improving their overall well-being.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, also known as licorice root tea, have been traditionally used to help alleviate symptoms of acid reflux.

This is because the tea contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, which has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe and protect the mucous membranes in the esophagus and stomach. The root of the Glycyrrhiza glabra plant also contains flavonoids and isoflavonoids, which have been shown to help reduce inflammation and improve digestion. If you're experiencing acid reflux, you can try making a cup of licorice root tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root in a cup of hot water for 5-10 minutes.

You can also add honey or lemon to taste. When you drink the tea, you may start to feel a reduction in heartburn and acid reflux symptoms within a few hours. This is because the glycyrrhizin and other compounds in the tea help to calm the stomach and esophagus, reducing inflammation and allowing the digestive system to function more smoothly.

You may also find that you're able to eat and drink without discomfort, and that your symptoms of acid reflux occur less frequently.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, have been traditionally used to help alleviate acid reflux symptoms.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the esophagus and stomach, which can contribute to acid reflux. Additionally, ginger has natural digestive enzymes that can help stimulate digestion and reduce nausea. When consumed as a tea, ginger's bioactive compounds can be easily absorbed into the body, allowing them to take effect quickly. If you're experiencing acid reflux symptoms, try making ginger tea by steeping fresh ginger in hot water for 5-7 minutes. You can also add honey or lemon to taste. To get the most out of your ginger tea, aim to drink it 30 minutes before meals or before bedtime.

This can help reduce symptoms of acid reflux during the night. Another tip is to start with a small amount of ginger and gradually increase the amount as your body becomes accustomed to it. Some people may experience heartburn or discomfort when first trying ginger tea, but this should subside over time. When you drink ginger tea regularly, you may notice a reduction in acid reflux symptoms, such as heartburn and regurgitation. You may also experience improved digestion and a decrease in nausea. Some people may also notice a reduction in inflammation and pain in the esophagus and stomach. However, it's essential to note that ginger tea is not a substitute for medical treatment.

If you experience severe or persistent acid reflux symptoms, consult with your healthcare provider for proper diagnosis and treatment.

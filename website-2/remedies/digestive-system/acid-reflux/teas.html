10 best herbal teas for acid reflux

1. Zingiber Officinale

Zingiber officinale, also known as ginger, is a natural remedy that has been used for centuries to help with acid reflux. One reason it's effective is because of its anti-inflammatory properties. Ginger helps to reduce inflammation in the stomach and esophagus, which can cause the muscles to relax and allow stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus. This can lead to symptoms like heartburn and discomfort. Ginger also has a soothing effect on the digestive system, helping to calm down the stomach and reduce acid production. Additionally, ginger has natural antacid properties that can help neutralize stomach acid and provide quick relief from acid reflux symptoms. The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, work together to provide these benefits and help alleviate acid reflux symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the mucous membranes, glycyrrhiza glabra tea can help to calm the symptoms of acid reflux and allow the stomach to heal. Additionally, the tea has been shown to increase the production of mucus, which can help to protect the stomach lining from acid damage and reduce the symptoms of acid reflux. Drinking glycyrrhiza glabra tea may also help to relax the muscles in the stomach and lower esophagus, which can help to prevent stomach acid from flowing back up into the esophagus and causing acid reflux.

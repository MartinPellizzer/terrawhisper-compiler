11 Herbal Teas For Acid Reflux

Herbal teas can be a great relief for people who suffer from acid reflux.

This is because many herbs have natural properties that help calm down the digestive system and reduce inflammation in the esophagus. For instance, Zingiber officinale, also known as ginger, has anti-inflammatory compounds that help soothe the stomach lining and reduce acid production. Drinking ginger tea can bring quick relief from heartburn and discomfort. Another herb that's commonly used to treat acid reflux is Glycyrrhiza glabra, also known as licorice root. The active compound in licorice root, called glycyrrhizin, has anti-inflammatory properties that help heal the stomach lining and reduce acid production. It also helps relax the muscles in the digestive tract, which can help prevent acid from flowing back up into the esophagus.

Fennel, or Foeniculum vulgare, is another herb that can help alleviate acid reflux symptoms. The essential oils in fennel seeds have natural antispasmodic properties that help calm down the muscles in the digestive tract and reduce inflammation. Drinking fennel tea can help relieve heartburn, bloating, and discomfort associated with acid reflux. Drinking herbal teas can bring several benefits to your life. Not only can they help alleviate acid reflux symptoms, but they can also promote overall digestive health. They can also help reduce stress and anxiety, which can exacerbate acid reflux symptoms. Additionally, many herbal teas are caffeine-free, making them a great alternative to traditional tea or coffee.

By incorporating herbal teas into your daily routine, you can find natural relief from acid reflux and enjoy a healthier, happier digestive system.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains active constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, a common cause of acid reflux. The gingerols and shogaols also have a relaxing effect on the muscles in the digestive tract, allowing food to move more smoothly and reducing pressure on the stomach. Additionally, the warming properties of ginger help increase blood flow to the digestive tract, improving digestion and reducing symptoms of acid reflux.

By reducing inflammation and improving digestion, Zingiber officinale teas may help alleviate acid reflux symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1/2 teaspoon of dried Zingiber officinale root.
  2. Heat the water in a pot until it boils.
  3. Reduce heat and add the dried Zingiber officinale root to the water.
  4. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes to release the flavors.
  5. Strain the tea and drink while warm, 2-3 times a day as needed.

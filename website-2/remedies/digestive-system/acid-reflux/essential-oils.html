11 Herbal Essential Oils For Acid Reflux

By using these herbal essential oils, either in teas or through aromatherapy, you may find relief from acid reflux symptoms.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which is known for its anti-inflammatory properties.

Gingerol helps to reduce inflammation in the stomach and esophagus, providing relief from acid reflux symptoms. Zingiber officinale essential oils also contains a compound called shogaol, which has a calming effect on the digestive system and reduces the production of stomach acid. The warm, spicy properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils help to relax the muscles in the stomach and esophagus, preventing the backflow of acid into the esophagus.

The anti-inflammatory and calming properties of gingerol and shogaol in Zingiber officinale essential oils make it a useful natural remedy for managing acid reflux symptoms.

  1. Gather materials: 1 cup of Zingiber officinale roots, 2 cups of water, a saucepan, a strainer, and a glass bottle.
  2. Wash the roots with water and chop them into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped roots and water in the saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a glass bottle and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks to allow the oil to separate from the water. Use a dropper to extract the essential oil from the water and store it in a small bottle.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which is known for its anti-inflammatory properties.

Glycyrrhizin has a soothing effect on the digestive system and can help reduce inflammation in the esophagus, which is often associated with acid reflux. The essential oil also contains flavonoids and triterpenoids, which have antioxidant properties that can help protect the stomach lining from acid damage. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of acid reflux, such as heartburn and regurgitation.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the stomach lining, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can provide relief from acid reflux symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Glycyrrhiza glabra roots. Clean and chop them into small pieces.
  2. Use a steam distiller to separate the oil from the roots. Add 2 cups of water to the distiller.
  3. Heat the water in the distiller until steam is produced. This will help release the oil from the roots.
  4. Collect the steam and direct it into a condenser. This will cause the steam to condense into a liquid.
  5. Separate the oil from the liquid using a separator or a glass jar. Store the oil in a dark glass bottle.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains a compound called anethole, a naturally occurring chemical that has anti-inflammatory properties.

Anethole helps to soothe the digestive tract and reduce inflammation in the esophagus, which can ease the symptoms of acid reflux. Foeniculum vulgare essential oils also contains dill apiole, which has a relaxing effect on the muscles in the stomach and esophagus, allowing food to pass through more smoothly. Additionally, the oils contain estragole, a compound that has a calming effect on the digestive system and helps to reduce the production of stomach acid.

By reducing inflammation and calming the digestive system, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help to alleviate the symptoms of acid reflux.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Heat 1 cup of carrier oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds to the heated oil and let it steep for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the oil mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the seeds.
  5. Store the Foeniculum vulgare essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 5-7 drops per day to help relieve acid reflux.

4. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains the bioactive constituents menthol, menthone, limonene, and beta-pinene.

These constituents give the oil its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help alleviate the symptoms of acid reflux. The menthol in mentha x piperita essential oils can help relax the muscles in the esophagus, reducing pressure and discomfort associated with acid reflux. The limonene and beta-pinene in the oil have a soothing effect on the digestive system, reducing inflammation and calming the digestive tract.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the muscles, mentha x piperita essential oils can help provide relief from acid reflux symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh peppermint leaves, a clean glass jar, and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Combine the peppermint leaves with 2 cups of water in the glass jar and let it sit for 2 weeks, shaking it daily.
  3. After 2 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into the dark glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 cup of vodka or glycerin to the liquid to help preserve the oil and prevent spoilage.
  5. Store the peppermint essential oil in a cool, dark place and use 5-7 drops in a glass of water for acid reflux relief.

5. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a high concentration of bioactive constituents, including curcuminoids, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce inflammation in the esophagus and alleviate symptoms of acid reflux. Curcuminoids also have a protective effect on the mucous membranes, promoting the healing of damaged tissues and preventing further irritation. Furthermore, curcuma longa essential oils has a calming effect on the digestive system, reducing the production of stomach acid and preventing acid reflux.

The combination of its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and calming properties makes curcuma longa essential oils an effective natural remedy for acid reflux.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa roots and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Fill a clean glass jar with 1/2 cup of the dried roots and pour in 2 cups of carrier oil.
  3. Close the jar and shake it well for 2-3 minutes to mix the roots and oil.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day to help the oil extract from the roots.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and use the extracted oil for acid reflux.

6. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains cinnamaldehyde, linalool, and eugenol as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that help alleviate symptoms of acid reflux. Cinnamaldehyde, in particular, has been shown to reduce inflammation in the esophagus and alleviate pain. Linalool and eugenol help relax the muscles in the esophagus, reducing spasms and allowing food to pass through more easily.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the muscles, Cinnamomum verum essential oils can provide relief from acid reflux symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cinnamomum verum bark, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.
  2. Dry the Cinnamomum verum bark in a low-temperature oven (150°F - 200°F) for 2-3 hours or until it's completely dry.
  3. Grind the dried Cinnamomum verum bark into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  4. Combine 10-15 drops of the powdered Cinnamomum verum with 1 cup of carrier oil in the glass bottle. Stir well and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the bottle daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass bottle. Discard the solids and store the Cinnamomum verum essential oil in a cool, dark place.

7. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains carnosic acid and 1,8-cineole as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help relax the muscles in the esophagus and reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. The antispasmodic properties of 1,8-cineole specifically can help calm down the spasms that cause stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus. Carnosic acid also has antioxidant properties, which can help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the digestive tract, further alleviating acid reflux symptoms.

By promoting relaxation and reducing inflammation, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils may help alleviate acid reflux symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Clean and dry the leaves, then chop them into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped leaves and carrier oil in a clean glass jar, making sure the leaves are fully covered.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day, then strain the mixture through cheesecloth.
  5. Bottle the Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil and use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with water to treat acid reflux symptoms.

8. Cuminum cyminum

Cuminum cyminum essential oils contains a compound called cuminaldehyde and others like limonene and β-pinene.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the esophagus and stomach lining that often causes acid reflux. The antioxidant properties of cuminaldehyde also help protect the digestive tract from damage caused by free radicals. By reducing inflammation and protecting the digestive tract, Cuminum cyminum essential oils may help alleviate acid reflux symptoms.

The essential oil's carminative properties also help relieve gas and bloating, further contributing to its potential benefits for acid reflux sufferers.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cuminum cyminum seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil) in a clean glass container.
  2. Heat the seeds in a pan over low heat for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, to release their oil.
  3. Let the seeds cool, then add them to the carrier oil in the glass container.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the container every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean glass bottle, discarding the seeds and keeping the oil for use as a natural remedy for acid reflux.

Syzygium aromaticum essential oils contains compounds like eugenol, caryophyllene, and limonene.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce inflammation in the esophagus and stomach. Eugenol, in particular, has been shown to relax the muscles in the esophagus, preventing acid from flowing back up into the throat. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to soothe and calm the digestive tract. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent acid reflux from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help ease acid reflux symptoms.

Some herbal teas, like ginger and peppermint, have natural anti-inflammatory properties that can calm the stomach and reduce discomfort.

These teas can also help relax the muscles in the esophagus, making it easier for food to pass through without irritating the stomach.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for acid reflux every day?

Herbal teas can help with acid reflux, but drinking them every day isn't always safe.

Some herbs, like peppermint, can relax the muscles between the esophagus and stomach, helping symptoms. But others, like ginger, can irritate the stomach lining, making acid reflux worse.

It's essential to choose the right herbal tea and drink it in moderation.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in acid reflux?

Herbal teas can start helping with acid reflux symptoms within a few days to a week.

Ginger tea is often the fastest-acting, as it can calm the stomach and reduce inflammation quickly.

Other herbs like licorice root and peppermint may take a bit longer, but they can still provide relief within a week or two.

What Are The Main Causes Of Gallstones?

Gallstones are hard, small rocks that form in the gallbladder, a sac-like organ that stores bile.

There are three main types of gallstones: pigment stones, cholesterol stones, and bile acid stones. Pigment stones are brown or black and are usually small. They are formed when bile contains too much bilirubin, a pigment produced by the liver that helps digest fat. When there is too much bilirubin, it can combine with other substances to form a hard stone. This often happens in people with liver or blood disorders, such as cirrhosis or hemolytic disease. Cholesterol stones are the most common type and are usually yellow in color.

They are formed when there is too much cholesterol in the bile, which can happen if the liver produces too much cholesterol or if the gallbladder doesn't empty properly. When cholesterol is present in excess, it can combine with other substances to form a hard stone. This often happens in people who are overweight, have high cholesterol levels, or are not active. Bile acid stones are rare and are usually small. They are formed when there is too much bile acid in the bile, which can happen if the liver produces too much bile acid or if the gallbladder doesn't empty properly. When bile acid is present in excess, it can combine with other substances to form a hard stone.

This often happens in people who have a disease that affects the bile ducts, such as primary sclerosing cholangitis or bile duct cancer.

Below are the main causes of gallstones.

  • Pigment Stones: Pigment stones form in the gallbladder when bile contains too much bilirubin, a pigment that can come from broken red blood cells, causing it to harden and turn into small stones.
  • Cholesterol Stones: Cholesterol stones form in the gallbladder because it produces too much cholesterol, which then hardens into small, hard stones that can cause problems and pain.
  • Bile Acid Stones: Bile acid stones form when bile acids in the bile, a digestive liquid produced by the liver, break down and combine with bile salts and cholesterol, eventually hardening into small, hard stones that can block the bile duct and cause problems.
  • Bile Salt Stones: Bile salt stones are a type of gallstone that forms when bile salts in the bile fluid, which helps digest fat, mix with other substances and harden over time due to a lack of bile salts, making it difficult for the gallbladder to empty properly.
  • Genetics: Genetics can be a cause of gallstones because some people are born with a genetic condition that affects how their liver produces bile, leading to gallstones forming in the gallbladder.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Gallstones?

If you have gallstones, it's best to avoid herbs that can irritate your gallbladder or make gallstones worse.

One herb to avoid is Angelica (Angelica sinensis), as it can increase bile production and worsen gallstone symptoms. Another herb to be cautious with is Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale), which can stimulate the gallbladder and cause it to contract, potentially leading to pain and discomfort.

Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) is also not recommended due to its potential to increase bile production and irritate the gallbladder. Furthermore, Peppermint (Mentha piperita) should be used with caution, as its oil can relax the muscles in the gallbladder, potentially leading to blockages.

Finally, Turmeric-free Ginger alternative, such as, Ginger's cousin 'Hedychium coronarium', should also be avoided, as it can increase bile production and worsen gallstone symptoms.

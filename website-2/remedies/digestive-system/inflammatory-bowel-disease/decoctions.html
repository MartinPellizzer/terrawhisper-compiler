What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have inflammatory bowel disease?

If you have inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), it's essential to be cautious with certain herbal decoctions that may worsen your condition.

One herb to avoid is goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis), as it contains berberine, which can irritate the digestive tract and make IBD symptoms worse. Another herb to be careful with is senna (Cassia senna), a natural laxative that can cause dehydration and electrolyte imbalances, which can be problematic for people with IBD. Dandelion root (Taraxacum spp.). may also be a concern, as it can stimulate the digestive system and cause stomach upset.

Slippery elm (Ulmus spp.). and licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) are sometimes recommended for IBD, but licorice root should be avoided due to its potential to cause blood pressure spikes and electrolyte imbalances. Additionally, cayenne pepper (Capsicum annuum) and other spicy herbs may irritate the digestive tract and exacerbate IBD symptoms. Lastly, devil's claw (Harpagophytum procumbens) and prickly pear cactus (Opuntia spp.). may interact with medications used to treat IBD, such as immunosuppressants and certain antibiotics.

It's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal decoctions to manage IBD symptoms.

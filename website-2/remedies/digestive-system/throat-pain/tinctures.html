1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains active constituents like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in the throat. The glycosides, particularly echinacoside and caftaric acid, have antioxidant properties that protect the throat tissues from damage caused by free radicals. By reducing inflammation, protecting tissues, and boosting immunity, Echinacea purpurea tinctures can provide relief from throat pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 2 cups of 80% ethanol (vodka or rubbing alcohol), and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and ethanol in the glass jar. Make sure the flowers are fully covered by the liquid.
  3. Close the jar and shake it well for 2-3 minutes to mix the flowers and ethanol. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and keep the liquid.
  5. Measure out 1 tablespoon of the tincture for every 8 ounces of water to make a throat pain remedy. You can add honey or lemon to taste.

9 Soothing Remedies in Teas for Stroke Survivors with Difficulty Swallowing

When someone has a stroke, their brain can be damaged, leading to problems with swallowing food and liquids.

This is known as dysphagia, and it can be very difficult for stroke survivors to eat and drink without choking. Dysphagia can cause a lot of stress and anxiety, and it can also lead to weight loss, dehydration, and even pneumonia. It's not just about eating and drinking, it's also about being able to enjoy meals with family and friends, and being able to maintain independence. Stroke survivors who have difficulty swallowing often need to eat thickened foods, take small sips of liquid, and avoid eating at all, which can be frustrating and isolating. Stroke survivors with dysphagia often have to rely on others for help, which can be embarrassing and demoralizing. They may also have to change their diet completely, which can be hard to adjust to.

Some people may even need to have a feeding tube inserted, which can be a long and difficult process. But there are some alternative options that can help make swallowing easier and less stressful. Herbal teas have been found to be helpful in making swallowing easier for stroke survivors. They can help relax the muscles in the throat and make it easier to swallow food and liquids. They can also help reduce inflammation and swelling in the throat, which can make swallowing even more difficult. Some herbal teas can even help improve the sensation of taste and smell, which can make eating and drinking more enjoyable.

By drinking herbal teas, stroke survivors can start to regain control over their eating and drinking, and start to enjoy meals and liquids again.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, also known as licorice root tea, have been used for centuries to help alleviate difficulty swallowing, also known as dysphagia.

This is due to the presence of compounds like glycyrrhizin, saponins, and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. These constituents can help calm irritated tissues in the throat and esophagus, reducing inflammation and pain that can make swallowing challenging. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of inflammatory chemicals in the body, further reducing swelling and discomfort. If you're experiencing difficulty swallowing and consider trying Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, you can start by drinking one cup of licorice root tea, either hot or cold, after meals or before bedtime.

Some people find it helpful to mix the tea with other soothing herbs like slippery elm or marshmallow root to enhance its effects. It's essential to note that licorice root tea can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, so consult with your healthcare provider before adding it to your routine. As you drink the tea, you may notice a reduction in throat irritation and inflammation, making it easier to swallow food and liquids. As you continue to drink Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, you may experience a decrease in the frequency and severity of difficulty swallowing episodes. Your throat and esophagus may feel less inflamed and irritated, allowing you to eat and drink more comfortably.

However, if your difficulty swallowing persists or worsens, it's crucial to consult with your healthcare provider to rule out any underlying conditions that require medical attention.

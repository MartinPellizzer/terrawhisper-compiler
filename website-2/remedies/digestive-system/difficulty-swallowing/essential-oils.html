1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains active constituents like gingerol and shogaol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the throat, making it easier to swallow. The warming and relaxing properties of ginger also help soothe digestive issues and ease tension in the throat and esophagus. Ginger's ability to thin mucus and promote saliva production can also help lubricate the throat and facilitate easier swallowing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help alleviate difficulty swallowing.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh ginger roots, a steam distillation apparatus, and a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil.
  2. Wash the ginger roots under cold running water and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Cut the ginger roots into small pieces and add them to the steam distillation apparatus.
  4. Steam distill the ginger roots for 2-3 hours, collecting the essential oil that separates from the water.
  5. Combine the collected essential oil with 1 cup of carrier oil and store it in a dark glass bottle for up to 6 months.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins and flavonoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds help to soothe and calm the mucous membranes in the throat, making it easier for food to pass through. Glycyrrhizin, a key component of the oil, has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and irritation in the throat, allowing for smoother swallowing. The oil's demulcent properties form a protective barrier on the mucous membranes, further reducing discomfort and allowing for more efficient swallowing.

By reducing inflammation and creating a soothing environment, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can help alleviate difficulty swallowing.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or coconut oil).
  2. Grind 1 cup of the dried roots into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Combine the ground roots with 2 cups of carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. This is called maceration.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids. Your essential oil is now ready to use.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains a compound called carnosic acid, which is known for its anti-inflammatory properties.

This property helps reduce inflammation in the throat and esophagus, making it easier to swallow. Additionally, the oil contains rosmarinic acid, a potent antioxidant that protects the mucous membranes in the throat from damage. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of rosmarinic acid and carnosic acid help to soothe and calm the throat, making swallowing easier.

The antimicrobial properties of the oil also help to prevent infections that can cause difficulty swallowing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and stems, 4 cups of water, and a steam distillation equipment.
  2. Place the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and stems in the steam distillation equipment and add 4 cups of water.
  3. Heat the water to produce steam and distill the essential oil for 2-3 hours.
  4. Collect the distilled essential oil and separate it from the water using a separating funnel.
  5. Dilute the essential oil with a carrier oil (such as jojoba oil) in a 1:10 ratio to make it safe for consumption.

4. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains flavonoids, bilobalide, and ginkgolides.

These bioactive constituents have vasodilating properties, which help increase blood flow to the throat and mouth, making it easier to swallow. The essential oils also have anti-inflammatory properties, reducing swelling and irritation in the throat and esophagus, common causes of difficulty swallowing. Additionally, the oils have antioxidant properties, protecting the tissues from damage and promoting healing.

By improving blood flow, reducing inflammation, and protecting tissues, Ginkgo biloba essential oils can help alleviate difficulty swallowing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves and 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or coconut oil).
  2. Clean and dry the Ginkgo biloba leaves. Use a coffee grinder to chop the leaves into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped leaves with 2 cups of carrier oil in a glass jar. Close the jar and leave it in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the Ginkgo biloba essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops of the oil with other remedies for difficulty swallowing, after consulting a healthcare professional.

5. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains bioactive constituents like turmerone, ar-turmerone, and curcumenol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help soothe and calm the throat and esophageal tissues. Turmerone and ar-turmerone specifically have been shown to reduce swelling and inflammation in the digestive tract, which can contribute to difficulty swallowing. Curcumenol, on the other hand, has been found to relax and calm the muscles in the esophagus, making it easier to swallow food and liquids.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the muscles, Curcuma longa essential oils can help alleviate difficulty swallowing and promote a smooth digestive process.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Turmeric roots (Curcuma longa) and 2 cups of carrier oil (Coconut or Jojoba oil).
  2. Wash the Turmeric roots with water, then dry them with a clean cloth.
  3. Grate 1/2 cup of the dried Turmeric roots and mix with 2 cups of carrier oil in a clean glass container.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the container every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains compounds such as menthone, menthol, and limonene, which help with difficulty swallowing.

Menthone is known for its expectorant properties, helping to loosen and remove mucus from the throat, making it easier to swallow. Menthol's analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties reduce throat irritation and discomfort, allowing for smoother swallowing. Limonene's antimicrobial properties help combat bacterial and viral infections that can cause difficulty swallowing.

By addressing these underlying issues, Mentha x piperita essential oils can provide relief from difficulty swallowing.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Mentha x piperita (peppermint) leaves and flowers.
  2. Wash the leaves and flowers with 1 cup of cold water to remove dirt and impurities.
  3. Combine the leaves and flowers in a clean glass jar and add 1 cup of carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily to help oil extract.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a separate container, discarding the solids.

7. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng essential oils contains bioactive constituents such as ginsenosides, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

These ginsenosides have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling and irritation in the throat, making it easier to swallow. Additionally, Panax ginseng essential oils has antioxidant properties, which can help protect the mucous membranes in the throat from damage and promote healing. The antispasmodic properties of ginsenosides can also help relax the muscles in the throat, making swallowing more comfortable.

By reducing inflammation, promoting healing, and relaxing the muscles, Panax ginseng essential oils can help alleviate difficulty swallowing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Panax ginseng roots and 4 cups of carrier oil, such as jojoba oil.
  2. Grind the dried Panax ginseng roots into fine powder using a coffee grinder.
  3. Combine 1/4 cup of the ground Panax ginseng powder with 2 cups of carrier oil in a glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth and discard the solids. Store the Panax ginseng essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

8. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains active constituents like alkylamides and caffeic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling in the throat and ease difficulty swallowing. The oils also contain phenolic compounds, including rosmarinic acid, which has antioxidant properties that can help protect the throat from damage and promote healing. Echinacea purpurea essential oils have antimicrobial properties, which can help prevent infections in the throat that may be contributing to difficulty swallowing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of difficulty swallowing.

  1. Gather Echinacea purpurea roots (about 2 cups) and a carrier oil like jojoba oil (1 cup).
  2. Dry the Echinacea purpurea roots in a low oven (150°F) for 2 hours to remove moisture.
  3. Grind the dried roots into a fine powder using a coffee grinder.
  4. Combine the ground roots (1/2 cup) with jojoba oil (1 cup) in a glass jar, then seal and store in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into another glass jar, discard the solids, and use the resulting essential oil as needed for difficulty swallowing.

9. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis essential oils contains the bioactive constituents quinine, cinchonine, and cinchonidine.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the throat and neck, making it easier to swallow. The oils also have a gentle stimulating effect on the nerves, which can help improve muscle function and coordination in the throat and jaw. Additionally, cinchona officinalis essential oils have a soothing effect on the mucous membranes, reducing inflammation and irritation that can make swallowing difficult.

By reducing inflammation and improving muscle function, cinchona officinalis essential oils can help alleviate difficulty swallowing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cinchona officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Cinchona officinalis leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the leaves from the saucepan and let the mixture cool. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a steam distillation apparatus or a small distillation kit. Steam distill the mixture to separate the essential oil from the water.
  5. Collect the essential oil and store it in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 5-7 drops of the essential oil in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent difficulty swallowing from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent difficulty swallowing, also known as dysphagia.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for difficulty swallowing every day?

While herbal teas can be soothing for difficulty swallowing, drinking them daily may interact with medications or worsen conditions like gastroesophageal reflux disease.

Some herbs like licorice root can also cause digestive issues.

It's essential to be cautious and observe how your body reacts to these teas, especially if you have underlying health conditions.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in difficulty swallowing?

Herbal teas may start to show results for difficulty swallowing within a few days to a week.

As the herbs begin to soothe and calm the throat, you may notice it's easier to swallow.

The time it takes depends on the specific herbs used and individual body responses.

7 Herbal Tinctures For Difficulty Swallowing

When a person has a stroke, it can cause damage to the nerves that control swallowing.

This can make it hard for them to eat and drink, leading to dehydration, malnutrition, and even infections. Stroke victims may experience difficulty swallowing due to paralysis of the muscles in the mouth, throat, or esophagus, or due to problems with the brain's ability to coordinate swallowing. This can be very frustrating and scary for the person, as it can make everyday tasks like eating a meal feel like a struggle. It can also affect their quality of life, making them feel weak and tired all the time.

They may need to be fed through a tube in their stomach, which can be uncomfortable and affect their self-esteem. Herbal tinctures have been studied as a possible way to help people with difficulty swallowing after a stroke. These tinctures are made from plants and contain their active properties in a liquid form. They are often taken as a supplement to help stimulate saliva production, relax the muscles in the throat, or improve the coordination of swallowing. Some of these tinctures have been shown to reduce the time it takes to swallow and improve the flow of food and liquids into the stomach.

They may also help to reduce anxiety and stress related to eating, which can be a major issue for people with difficulty swallowing.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties and can help relax the muscles involved in swallowing.

When taken internally, these compounds may help ease spasms and inflammation in the esophagus and throat, making it easier to swallow. To try using Zingiber officinale tinctures for difficulty swallowing, start by taking a small dose of 5-10 drops in water 2-3 times a day. If you experience relief, you can gradually increase the dose as needed.

It's essential to note that ginger can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and may not be suitable for people with bleeding disorders. Always consult with a healthcare professional before using any new supplement, especially if you have any underlying health conditions. Taking Zingiber officinale tinctures regularly may lead to improved swallowing ability and reduced discomfort or pain while eating. Some people may also experience relief from nausea and vomiting associated with difficulty swallowing.

However, individual results may vary, and it's crucial to work closely with a healthcare professional to monitor progress and adjust treatment as needed.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains licorice root extracts that have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, which can help with difficulty swallowing.

The main constituents of Glycyrrhiza glabra are glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins, which work together to calm the mucous membranes in the throat and esophagus. This can reduce inflammation and irritation, making it easier to swallow food and liquids. To try Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures for difficulty swallowing, start by mixing a few drops with water or another liquid and taking it 2-3 times a day before meals.

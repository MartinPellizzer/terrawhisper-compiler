What Are The Main Causes Of Hiatus Hernia?

A hiatus hernia occurs when part of the stomach bulges up into the chest through an opening in the diaphragm.

There are several causes of hiatus hernia. Coughing can cause a hiatus hernia when it puts pressure on the stomach and pushes it upwards through the diaphragm. This is especially true for people who have conditions that make them cough frequently, such as asthma.

Trauma, or a serious injury, can also cause a hiatus hernia when it damages the muscles in the diaphragm and allows the stomach to bulge up into the chest. Gastroesophageal Reflux (GERD) is another common cause of hiatus hernia. In GERD, the muscles in the diaphragm that separate the chest and stomach do not work properly, allowing stomach acid to flow up into the esophagus.

This can cause the stomach to bulge up into the chest, leading to a hiatus hernia.

Below are the main causes of hiatus hernia.

  • Coughing: Coughing can cause a hiatus hernia because it increases the pressure in the chest, forcing the stomach up through an opening in the diaphragm and into the chest cavity.
  • Trauma: Trauma can cause hiatus hernia because when the stomach is severely pushed or injured, its natural ring of muscles, called the diaphragm, can weaken and tear, allowing the stomach to bulge into the chest cavity.
  • Gastroesophageal Reflux: The stomach acid flows back up into the chest when the ring of muscle at the end of the esophagus, called the lower esophageal sphincter, is weakened, causing a hiatus hernia.
  • Hiatal Sling: A hiatal sling is a type of hernia repair that can sometimes cause a hiatus hernia because the mesh used to strengthen the stomach opening can bulge into the chest and push part of the stomach up into the chest through the same opening it was originally meant to repair.
  • Surgical Trauma: Surgical trauma can cause hiatus hernia when the surgeon accidentally tears or weakens the muscles around the hiatus, the opening in the diaphragm where the esophagus passes through, allowing part of the stomach to bulge up into the chest cavity.
  • Obesity: Obesity is a cause of hiatus hernia because the extra weight and pressure on the stomach can push it up through the diaphragm, creating a gap or tear in the muscle that holds it in place.
  • Pregnancy: During pregnancy, the growing womb can put extra pressure on the stomach muscles, causing a weakness in the diaphragm's connection to the stomach, which can lead to a hiatus hernia.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Hiatus Hernia?

If you have a hiatus hernia, it's essential to be cautious with certain herbs that may worsen your condition.

Peppermint (Mentha piperita) and lemongrass (Cymbopogon citratus) can relax the lower esophageal sphincter, allowing stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus, which can irritate the esophagus and make symptoms worse. Ginger-like herbs like Asafoetida (Ferula asafoetida) or Licorice-free substitutes, like 'Tulsi', may also be a problem. Some people may also react to the stimulant effects of herbs like Yohimbe (Pausinystalia johimbe) or Ephedra (Ephedra sinica), which can increase stomach acid production and worsen symptoms.

Additionally, herbs like Caffeine-rich Yerba mate (Ilex paraguariensis) and other stimulants like Guarana (Paullinia cupana) may also be problematic, as they can cause the stomach to produce more acid, irritating the esophagus and worsening symptoms.

What are the causes of hiatus hernia?

A hiatus hernia occurs when part of the stomach bulges up into the chest through an opening in the diaphragm.

There are several main causes of this condition. Obesity is one of the leading causes. When you're overweight, your stomach is under more pressure, which can cause the stomach to bulge up into the chest. Another cause is gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD). In GERD, the muscle between the esophagus and stomach doesn't close properly, allowing stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus. This can cause inflammation and irritation, which can weaken the diaphragm and lead to a hiatus hernia.

Hiatal hernia type II is a specific type of hiatus hernia where the stomach bulges up into the chest through a small hole in the diaphragm. This type of hernia is often caused by the diaphragm being weakened over time due to age or other factors. Hiatal hernia type III is a more severe type of hiatus hernia where a large part of the stomach bulges up into the chest through a large hole in the diaphragm. This type of hernia is often caused by a combination of factors, including obesity, GERD, and a weakened diaphragm. Pregnancy is also a risk factor for hiatus hernia. During pregnancy, the increased pressure on the stomach can cause it to bulge up into the chest.

This is because the growing uterus puts pressure on the diaphragm, which can cause it to weaken and lead to a hiatus hernia.

Stomach Pain: Causes, Prevention, and Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations for Long-Term Relief

Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, also known as ginger, has been used for a long time to help with stomach pain. It contains special compounds that help to reduce nausea and inflammation in the stomach. When you eat ginger, these compounds go into your body and start to work right away. They help to calm down the muscles in your stomach and reduce the amount of acid produced, which can help to ease pain and discomfort. Ginger also has natural anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling and pain in the stomach. This is why ginger tea, ginger ale, or even just eating raw ginger can be a good way to help with stomach pain. Some people also find that ginger helps to settle their stomach and prevent nausea, which can be especially helpful when you're feeling sick or have a stomach bug.

Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, also known as licorice root, has been used for centuries to soothe stomach pain. This is because it has anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce swelling and irritation in the digestive tract. The root contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which blocks the production of stomach acid and helps to heal ulcers and other stomach problems. Glycyrrhizin also has a calming effect on the stomach muscles, which can help to ease cramps and spasms that can cause pain. Additionally, licorice root has antimicrobial properties, which can help to eliminate bacteria and other microorganisms that can cause stomach infections. By reducing inflammation, healing ulcers, and calming stomach muscles, Glycyrrhiza glabra can provide quick relief from stomach pain and discomfort.

Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare, also known as fennel, has been used for a long time to help with stomach pain. The seeds of this plant contain a chemical called anethole, which is known to have anti-inflammatory properties. This means that it can help reduce swelling and irritation in the stomach. When we eat, food can sometimes irritate the stomach lining, causing pain and discomfort. The anethole in fennel helps to calm down this irritation, making it easier to digest food. Additionally, fennel has been shown to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, which can also help relieve stomach cramps and pain. Furthermore, fennel has been traditionally used to treat gas and bloating, which can also contribute to stomach discomfort. Drinking fennel tea or eating fennel seeds may help to alleviate stomach pain and make digestion easier.

Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita, also known as peppermint oil, is commonly used to relieve stomach pain. The main reason for this is that it contains a chemical called menthol, which helps to relax the muscles in the stomach. When these muscles are tense, they can cause pain and discomfort. By relaxing them, menthol reduces the pain and discomfort. Additionally, peppermint oil helps to ease digestion and reduce nausea, which are often symptoms of stomach pain. When we eat, our digestive system breaks down food into smaller parts. Sometimes, this process can be slow, leading to cramps and bloating. Peppermint oil stimulates the digestive system to move food through the body faster, which can help to reduce these symptoms. Its ability to relax muscles and ease digestion makes peppermint oil a popular natural remedy for stomach pain.

Curcuma longa

When we eat, our stomach lining gets irritated and inflamed, leading to pain and discomfort. By reducing inflammation and acid levels, turmeric helps soothe the stomach and promote healing. Turmeric is also easily absorbed by the body, making it a highly effective remedy for stomach pain.

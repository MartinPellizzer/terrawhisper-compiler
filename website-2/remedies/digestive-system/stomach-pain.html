Stomach Pain: Causes, Prevention, and Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations for Long-Term Relief

Stomach pain is a common and often uncomfortable condition that can be caused by a variety of factors, including digestive issues, food poisoning, or inflammation.

If left untreated, stomach pain can lead to more serious complications, such as dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, and even organ damage. Therefore, it is essential to address stomach pain as soon as possible. Herbs and herbal preparations have been used for centuries to alleviate stomach pain, and several plants have been found to be particularly effective. For instance, ginger (Zingiber officinale) has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe the digestive system and reduce pain.

Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) contains compounds that can help protect the stomach lining and reduce inflammation, while fennel (Foeniculum vulgare) can help relax the digestive muscles and alleviate cramping. Herbal preparations such as decoctions, tinctures, and infusions can be used to harness the benefits of these herbs. Decoctions involve simmering herbs in water to release their active compounds, while tinctures are concentrated extracts that can be taken in small doses. Infusions involve steeping herbs in hot water to create a tea-like drink.

By incorporating these herbs and preparations into a treatment plan, individuals can find relief from stomach pain and promote a return to normal digestive function.

What Are The Main Causes Of Stomach Pain?

Stomach pain can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is Gastroenteritis, an inflammation of the stomach and intestines. It usually occurs when you eat or drink something contaminated with bacteria, viruses, or other germs. These germs can cause your stomach to become irritated and inflamed, leading to pain, nausea, and diarrhea. Gallstones are another cause of stomach pain.

Gallstones are small, hard deposits that form in the gallbladder, a small organ under your liver that stores bile. Bile helps digest fats, but if gallstones block the bile ducts, it can cause severe pain in the upper right abdomen, which can radiate to the back. Appendicitis is a serious cause of stomach pain that requires immediate medical attention. It occurs when the appendix, a small pouch connected to the large intestine, becomes inflamed and infected. The appendix can become blocked by a piece of feces, a foreign object, or other reasons, leading to severe pain in the lower right abdomen, which can worsen over time.

If left untreated, it can rupture and cause life-threatening complications.

Below are the main causes of stomach pain.

  • Gastroenteritis: Gastroenteritis is a cause of stomach pain because the infection irritates the lining of the stomach and intestines, causing inflammation and leading to severe cramps and discomfort.
  • Gallstones: Gallstones, hard small stones that form in the gallbladder, can cause stomach pain because they can block the bile ducts, leading to a buildup of bile that irritates the stomach and causes sharp, severe pain.
  • Appendicitis: Appendicitis occurs when the appendix, a small, finger-like part of the intestine, becomes inflamed and swollen, causing severe stomach pain, usually starting near the belly button and moving to the lower right abdomen.
  • Cholecystitis: Cholecystitis is a cause of stomach pain because the gallbladder, which is responsible for storing bile to help digest food, becomes inflamed and irritated, leading to severe abdominal pain.
  • Diverticulitis: Diverticulitis is a cause of stomach pain because small pouches in the digestive tract become inflamed and irritated, leading to severe abdominal pain, discomfort, and possibly even fever.
  • Peptic Ulcer: A peptic ulcer is a sore in the stomach lining that causes stomach pain because the ulcer creates an open wound that irritates the stomach lining and makes it inflamed, leading to severe pain.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Stomach Pain?

If you have stomach pain, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can irritate your stomach lining or make symptoms worse.

Some herbs to be cautious with include Peppermint (Mentha piperita) - while it can soothe digestive issues for some, others may find it makes their stomach pain worse. Sage (Salvia officinalis) can slow down digestion and worsen stomach pain in some individuals.

Yarrow (Achillea millefolium) may cause stomach upset due to its strong anti-inflammatory properties. Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza uralensis, not Glycyrrhiza glabra) can cause stomach pain in people who already have high blood pressure or take certain medications. Angelica (Angelica sinensis) may cause stomach irritation, especially if taken in large doses.

Turmeric (Curcuma longa) can irritate the stomach lining if taken in high amounts, and may worsen symptoms in some people.

What herbal creams you should avoid if you have upper abdominal pain?

If you have upper abdominal pain, it's best to avoid herbal creams containing Peppermint (Mentha piperita) and Sassafras (Sassafras albidum) because they can make the pain worse.

Peppermint can relax the muscles in your stomach, which can irritate the stomach lining and worsen the pain. Sassafras has a chemical called safrole that can cause stomach upset and increase the risk of bleeding.

Another herbal cream to avoid is one containing Valerian (Valeriana officinalis) root, as it can slow down digestion and worsen the pain. Other herbs to be cautious with include those in the 'Squawroot' (Conopholis americana) and 'Goldenseal' (Hydrastis canadensis) families, as they may interact with certain medications or worsen underlying health conditions.

Always consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal creams, especially if you have a medical condition or take medications.

What Are The Main Causes Of Painful Swallowing?

Painful swallowing can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is Globus Sensation, where people feel like there's a lump in their throat, even when nothing is physically there. This sensation is usually triggered by stress, anxiety, or eating too quickly. Another cause is Esophagitis, which is inflammation of the esophagus, usually due to acid reflux or infection.

This inflammation can cause pain and discomfort when swallowing, making it difficult to eat or drink. GERD, or Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease, is also a common cause of painful swallowing. In GERD, stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus, causing irritation and inflammation. This can lead to pain and discomfort when swallowing, as well as other symptoms like heartburn and regurgitation.

In all these cases, the pain and discomfort during swallowing are usually due to the body's response to irritation or inflammation in the esophagus.

Below are the main causes of painful swallowing.

  • Globus Sensation: Globus sensation is a cause of painful swallowing because it makes a person feel like there's a lump of food stuck in their throat, which can be very uncomfortable and painful.
  • Esophagitis: Esophagitis is a painful condition where the esophagus, the tube that carries food to the stomach, becomes inflamed and irritated, causing sharp, burning pain when swallowing due to the inflammation and irritation of the esophagus lining.
  • GERD: GERD (Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease) causes painful swallowing because stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus, irritating its lining and causing inflammation and discomfort when food passes down.
  • Scleroderma: Scleroderma can cause painful swallowing because the hardening of skin and connective tissues in the esophagus makes it narrow and inflamed, which irritates the nerves and makes swallowing a painful process.
  • Eosinophilic Esophagitis: Eosinophilic Esophagitis is a cause of painful swallowing because the immune system's abnormal response causes inflammation and swelling in the esophagus, leading to irritation and discomfort when trying to swallow food.
  • Candidiasis: Candidiasis causes painful swallowing because the fungus Candida overgrows in the esophagus, irritating its lining and causing discomfort, inflammation, and difficulty swallowing.

Diverticulitis in Smokers: 7 Herbal Creams for Quick Relief

Smokers are more likely to get diverticulitis, a painful condition where small pouches in the colon get infected.

This happens because smoking damages the blood vessels in the colon, making it harder for the body to heal and fight off infections. Smokers with diverticulitis may also be more likely to need surgery or hospitalization, which can be costly and stressful. The pain and discomfort of diverticulitis can also take a toll on mental health, leading to anxiety and depression.

Smokers with diverticulitis may feel like they're losing control of their bodies and lives. Smoking can also make it harder for the body to heal after surgery, and may increase the risk of complications. This can lead to a cycle of pain, fear, and anxiety, making it even more challenging to quit smoking. However, quitting smoking is a crucial step in managing diverticulitis, and herbal creams may provide some relief.

Overcoming Bitter Taste in Mouth: Natural Causes and Herbal Remedies

What are the causes of bitter taste in mouth?

There are several common causes of a bitter taste in the mouth.

One cause is taking certain medicines, especially those used to treat infections or digestive issues. These medicines can leave a bitter taste that can last even after the medication has been stopped. Dental work, such as having a filling or a crown, can also cause a bitter taste. This is usually due to the use of certain metals, like silver or mercury, which can release tiny amounts of these metals into the mouth, causing the bitter taste.

Another cause of bitter taste is consuming bismuth, a substance used in some medications to treat stomach issues. Bismuth can leave a bitter aftertaste that can last for a while. Eating or drinking things that contain licorice can also cause a bitter taste. This is because licorice contains compounds that can affect the way our taste buds work, leading to a bitter sensation. Finally, taking quinine, a substance found in some medications and tonic water, can also cause a bitter taste.

This is because quinine can stimulate the taste buds, making them more sensitive to bitter flavors.

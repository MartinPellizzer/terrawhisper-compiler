What Are The Main Causes Of Bitter Taste In Mouth?

When you experience a bitter taste in your mouth, it can be caused by several factors.

One of the main causes is bile, a digestive fluid produced by the liver that helps break down fats. If bile flows back up into the esophagus and mouth, it can give a bitter taste. This can happen when the lower esophageal sphincter, a muscle that separates the esophagus and stomach, is weak or doesn't function properly. Another possible cause is gallstones, small stones that form in the gallbladder, a small organ that stores bile.

If a gallstone blocks the bile duct, the flow of bile can become interrupted, causing a bitter taste in the mouth. This can be accompanied by other symptoms like pain in the upper right abdomen and nausea. Medications can also cause a bitter taste in the mouth. Some medications, like antibiotics and antidepressants, can alter the way bile is produced or flow through the body, leading to a bitter taste. Additionally, some medications can directly affect the taste buds on the tongue, causing a bitter taste.

This is often temporary and goes away once the medication is stopped.

Below are the main causes of bitter taste in mouth.

  • Bile: Bile, a strong liquid produced in the liver, mixes with food in the stomach and small intestine, releasing bitter-tasting compounds that cause the characteristic bitter taste in the mouth.
  • Gallstones: When gallstones block the bile ducts in the gallbladder, a bitter taste in the mouth may occur because the body can't release bile properly, allowing toxins like bilirubin to enter the bloodstream and affect the taste buds.
  • Medications: Medications can cause a bitter taste in the mouth because some of their ingredients, such as certain antibiotics and medications used to treat high blood pressure, can affect the way the body handles taste and cause a metallic or bitter aftertaste.
  • Gastroesophageal reflux disease: Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) can cause a bitter taste in the mouth because stomach acid, which normally stays in the stomach, flows back up into the esophagus and can then reach the tongue and taste buds, making food taste bitter.
  • Cancer: Cancer can cause a bitter taste in the mouth because it damages the taste buds or affects the nerves that send taste signals to the brain, making food taste unpleasantly bitter.
  • Liver disease: Liver disease can cause a bitter taste in the mouth because the liver is responsible for filtering toxins from the blood, and when it is not working properly, these toxins can build up and affect the taste buds in the mouth.
  • Kidney disease: Kidney disease can cause a bitter taste in the mouth because waste products, like urea, build up in the blood and are released through the saliva, giving it an unpleasant bitter taste.

Stomach pain in the elderly: 11 herbal teas for quick relief

As people age, their digestive system can slow down, and their stomach can become more sensitive.

This can lead to stomach pain, which can be caused by a variety of factors such as medication side effects, food intolerance, or even stress. For elderly individuals, stomach pain can be a significant issue, as it can interfere with their daily activities, appetite, and even social interactions. They may find it difficult to enjoy their favorite foods, and may need to rely on others for assistance with meal preparation and eating. In some cases, stomach pain can even lead to depression and anxiety, further complicating the issue.

When elderly individuals experience stomach pain, they often try various remedies to find relief. However, many over-the-counter medications can have side effects or interact with other medications they may be taking. This is where herbal teas come in – a natural and gentle way to soothe the stomach and promote digestion. These teas work by calming the stomach muscles, reducing inflammation, and promoting the growth of beneficial gut bacteria.

They can be enjoyed hot or cold, and can be a comforting and familiar way to find relief from stomach pain.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, have been used for centuries to help alleviate stomach pain.

This is due to the presence of compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce pain and swelling in the digestive tract. The active constituent, gingerol, also has a natural digestive enzyme called zingibain that helps break down proteins and aid digestion. When consumed as a tea, ginger's properties can help soothe the stomach and reduce nausea. To use ginger tea for stomach pain, start by peeling a small piece of fresh ginger and slice it thinly.

Then, steep the ginger in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and add honey or lemon to taste. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, ideally before meals to help with digestion. Drinking ginger tea regularly may help reduce stomach pain and discomfort. It can also help alleviate nausea and vomiting, making it a good option for people experiencing motion sickness or morning sickness during pregnancy.

However, if your stomach pain is severe or persistent, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using ginger tea as a treatment.

2. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas, also known as peppermint tea, may help with stomach pain due to its anti-inflammatory properties and ability to relax the muscles in the digestive tract.

The main constituents in peppermint tea, including menthol and menthone, can help reduce inflammation and ease cramps in the stomach and intestines. When you drink peppermint tea, the menthol can also help calm the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing spasms and cramps that can cause stomach pain. To try peppermint tea for stomach pain, make a cup by steeping one teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it slowly.

You can also try adding a bit of honey or lemon to taste. Some people find it helpful to drink peppermint tea after meals or before bedtime if they experience stomach pain at night. Drinking peppermint tea for stomach pain may lead to a reduction in symptoms, such as nausea, cramps, and bloating. You may also notice an improvement in your digestion and a decrease in discomfort after eating. However, peppermint tea may not be suitable for everyone, especially those with certain medical conditions, such as acid reflux or ulcers.

If you experience severe stomach pain or have concerns, it's best to speak with a healthcare professional before trying peppermint tea.

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate stomach pain, and it's due to its unique properties and constituents.

If you're experiencing stomach pain, you can try making a tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root in a cup of hot water for 5-7 minutes.

Foeniculum vulgare teas, also known as fennel tea, is a natural remedy that has been used for centuries to alleviate stomach pain.

The tea is made from the seeds of the fennel plant, which contain compounds like anethole, fenchone, and limonene. You can drink 2-3 cups per day for relief. As you take fennel tea, you may start to feel a reduction in stomach cramps and discomfort within 30 minutes to an hour.

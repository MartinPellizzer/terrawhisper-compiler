What herbal teas you should avoid if you have stomach pain?

If you have stomach pain, it's best to avoid certain herbal teas that can irritate your digestive system.

One of these teas is senna, made from the leaves of Senna alexandrina. Senna is a laxative, which can cause stomach cramps and diarrhea if taken in large quantities.

Another tea to avoid is pennyroyal, made from the leaves of Mentha pulegium. Pennyroyal can cause stomach pain and bleeding due to its strong oil content, which can irritate the stomach lining. Goldenseal, made from the root of Hydrastis canadensis, should also be avoided as it can cause stomach upset due to its berberine content, which can slow down digestion and cause nausea.

Finally, tea made from the leaves of Yarrow (Achillea millefolium) and Ephedra sinica (also known as Ma Huang) can also irritate the stomach and worsen stomach pain if taken in excess.

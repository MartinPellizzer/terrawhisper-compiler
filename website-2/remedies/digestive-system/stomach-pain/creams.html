1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains a compound called curcumin, which is a powerful anti-inflammatory.

Curcumin has potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce swelling and pain in the stomach. Turmeric also contains a group of compounds called polyphenols, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. The polyphenols in turmeric, especially demethoxycurcumin and bisdemethoxycurcumin, have been found to reduce inflammation and relieve stomach pain.

The combination of curcumin and polyphenols in Curcuma longa creams makes it a natural and effective remedy for stomach pain.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, and 1/2 cup of beeswax in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in the double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of Turmeric powder and 1 tablespoon of Ginger powder to the melted mixture. Stir well.
  4. Remove the double boiler from the heat and let it cool for 10 minutes. Then, add 1/4 cup of coconut oil and 2 tablespoons of Vitamin E oil to the mixture. Stir well.
  5. Pour the Curcuma longa cream into a container and let it cool completely before applying it to the stomach area.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains gingerol and shogaol, active constituents that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation in the stomach, which can cause pain. Gingerol and shogaol also have antioxidant properties, which help protect the stomach lining from damage. The warming effect of ginger can increase blood flow to the stomach, promoting healing and reducing pain.

The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of gingerol and shogaol in Zingiber officinale creams make it a good option for relieving stomach pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of Zingiber officinale powder.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Add shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and stir in the Zingiber officinale powder until it's well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool until it's solid. Once set, use as a topical cream for stomach pain relief.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin, and flavonoids like licoflavone and licoricidin.

These active constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce stomach pain and discomfort. Glycyrrhizin also has a soothing effect on the stomach lining, reducing inflammation and irritation that can cause pain. Additionally, glycyrrhizin helps to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, allowing food to pass through more easily and reducing cramping.

The anti-inflammatory properties of glycyrrhizin and flavonoids also help to reduce swelling and ease pain in the stomach, providing quick relief from discomfort.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of beeswax, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of aloe vera gel.
  2. Melt the beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the glycyrrhiza glabra root powder and vitamin E oil until well combined.
  4. Add the glycyrrhiza glabra mixture to the melted wax mixture and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container, let it cool, and then add the aloe vera gel. Stir well and your glycyrrhiza glabra cream is ready for use.

4. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare creams contains a compound called limonene, which has a calming effect on the stomach.

This combination of properties helps to soothe and calm the stomach, providing relief from discomfort and pain.

  2. Heat the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler until the beeswax melts.
  3. Add the dried Foeniculum vulgare seeds to the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture. Let it steep for 2 hours.

5. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis creams contains a compound called quinine, which is made up of a group of alkaloids, including quinidine and cinchonidine.

These alkaloids have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help to relieve stomach pain by reducing inflammation and muscle spasms in the digestive tract. The alkaloids in Cinchona officinalis also have a sedative effect, which can help to calm the stomach and reduce nausea. Additionally, the quinidine in Cinchona officinalis has a vasoconstrictive effect, which can help to reduce blood flow to the stomach and alleviate symptoms of stomach pain.

By combining these properties, Cinchona officinalis creams can provide relief from stomach pain by reducing inflammation, muscle spasms, and blood flow to the affected area.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of dried Cinchona officinalis bark, and 1 tablespoon of beeswax in a small saucepan.
  2. Heat the distilled water over low heat, then add the dried Cinchona officinalis bark. Let it steep for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a bowl to remove the Cinchona bark. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of beeswax to the strained mixture and stir until it's fully melted.
  5. Pour the mixture into a small tin or container and let it cool. Once cooled, the Cinchona officinalis cream is ready for use.

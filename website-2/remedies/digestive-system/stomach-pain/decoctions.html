What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have stomach pain?

If you have stomach pain, it's best to avoid herbal decoctions that can irritate your stomach further.

Another herb to avoid is 'Hydrastis canadensis', also known as goldenseal, which contains a compound called berberine. Berberine can slow down digestion, making your stomach pain worse.

'Rauvolfia serpentina', also known as snakeroot, should also be avoided. It contains a compound called reserpine, which can relax your stomach muscles, leading to stomach cramps and pain. Finally, 'Valeriana officinalis', also known as valerian, should be used with caution. While it can help with sleep, it can also slow down digestion and worsen stomach pain.

Always consult a healthcare professional before using any herbal decoctions, especially if you have a pre-existing condition or are taking medication.

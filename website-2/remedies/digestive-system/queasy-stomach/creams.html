10 Herbal Creams For Queasy Stomach

Pregnant women often experience queasy stomach, also known as morning sickness, which can be a real challenge for them.

This happens because of the changes in their body, like the levels of hormones and blood sugar, and the growing fetus, which can cause the stomach to feel upset. Many women have to deal with nausea, vomiting, and stomach pain, making it hard for them to eat, work, and even take care of themselves. Some women might be bedridden due to severe symptoms, while others have to adjust their daily routine to manage their queasy stomach. This can be frustrating and stressful for pregnant women, affecting their mood and overall well-being.

As they try to cope with their queasy stomach, pregnant women often look for relief from over-the-counter medications, but many of these can have side effects or interact with other medications. This is where herbal creams come in – a natural and gentle way to help alleviate queasy stomach symptoms. These creams work by soothing the stomach and reducing nausea and vomiting. They can be applied to the skin, usually on the wrists or behind the ears, and are often absorbed quickly into the body.

This makes them a popular choice for pregnant women who want a natural and safe way to manage their queasy stomach symptoms.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contain active compounds like gingerol and shogaol that have anti-inflammatory properties and can help alleviate queasy stomach symptoms.

To use Zingiber officinale creams for a queasy stomach, apply a small amount to your pulse points, such as your wrists or temples, and gently massage it in. You can also consume it orally by mixing a small amount with a non-acidic food or drink, like plain toast or warm water.

As you apply or consume the cream, you may feel a warming sensation in your stomach, which is a sign that the ginger is starting to work. However, if your queasy stomach persists or worsens, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for further guidance and advice.

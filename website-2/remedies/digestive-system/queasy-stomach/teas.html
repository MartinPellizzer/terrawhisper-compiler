10 best herbal teas for queasy stomach

1. Zingiber Officinale

Zingiber officinale tea, also known as ginger tea, is a popular remedy for a queasy stomach. This is because ginger has natural properties that help soothe and calm the digestive system. When you drink ginger tea, the active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, work to reduce inflammation and relax the muscles in your stomach. This helps to ease nausea and vomiting, making it a great option for people who are experiencing morning sickness or stomach upset after eating. Ginger tea also has a calming effect on the body, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety that can often contribute to a queasy stomach. Additionally, ginger has antimicrobial properties that can help to kill off any bacteria or viruses that may be causing stomach upset, further contributing to its effectiveness in soothing a queasy stomach.

When the muscles in the stomach are relaxed, it can help ease nausea and vomiting. Additionally, peppermint tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce irritation in the stomach and intestines, which can also contribute to a queasy stomach. Drinking peppermint tea may also help speed up digestion, which can help get rid of any trapped gas or food that might be causing stomach discomfort. When peppermint tea is consumed, the menthol in the peppermint oil can help numb the stomach and provide relief from the queasy feeling.

Queasy Stomach Got You Down? 9 Herbal Teas for New Moms

New mothers often experience queasy stomach, also known as morning sickness, due to hormonal changes in their body.

This can cause nausea, vomiting, and dizziness, making everyday activities a challenge. Simple tasks like cooking, cleaning, or even getting out of bed can be overwhelming. The constant feeling of queasiness can lead to fatigue, dehydration, and a weakened immune system, making it difficult for new mothers to care for themselves and their newborns. They may also experience mood swings, anxiety, and irritability, which can strain relationships with their partners and family members. Furthermore, the lack of sleep and nutrition can exacerbate the symptoms, creating a vicious cycle. New mothers often rely on their partner, family, and friends for support, but even with help, the burden can be significant. They may have to adjust their work schedule, postpone their return to work, or even take a break from childcare.

The emotional toll of not being able to care for their baby as they would like can be devastating. The physical symptoms can also make it difficult to breastfeed, which can be a source of anxiety and guilt for new mothers. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate queasy stomach in new mothers. These teas are made from the leaves, flowers, or roots of certain plants and are often caffeine-free. They can be consumed hot or cold, depending on personal preference. The active ingredients in these teas work by soothing the digestive system, reducing inflammation, and promoting relaxation. They can also help to regulate the body's hormonal balance, which is often disrupted during pregnancy.

By drinking these teas, new mothers can find relief from their queasy stomach and improve their overall well-being.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, can help alleviate queasy stomachs due to its natural properties and constituents.

The main active compound in ginger is gingerol, which has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects that soothe the digestive system and calm nausea. Gingerol also stimulates digestion, helping to break down food and absorb nutrients. Additionally, ginger contains compounds like shogaol and zingerone, which have been shown to have anti-emetic properties, helping to alleviate vomiting and nausea. To take action with ginger tea, start by steeping fresh ginger in hot water for 5-7 minutes.

You can also add honey or lemon to taste. For a queasy stomach, it's recommended to drink 1-2 cups of ginger tea per day, ideally before meals or when symptoms occur. You can also try ginger tea as a warm compress on the stomach to help ease discomfort. Taking ginger tea may lead to a reduction in nausea and vomiting, as well as an improvement in digestion. Some people may experience a decrease in stomach cramps and bloating, while others may notice a more balanced appetite.

However, it's essential to note that ginger tea is not a substitute for medical treatment, and if your queasy stomach persists or worsens, you should consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and advice.

2. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas, also known as fennel tea, can be a helpful remedy for a queasy stomach.

The reason it works is because of its active compounds, such as anethole and fenchone, which have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. These properties can help ease digestive discomfort and reduce nausea. When you drink fennel tea, the anethole and fenchone are absorbed into your bloodstream and then travel to your stomach, where they can help calm down the muscles and reduce inflammation. This can help alleviate symptoms like bloating, cramps, and stomach pain. To try fennel tea for a queasy stomach, start by making a cup of tea using dried fennel seeds or fresh fennel fronds. You can steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried seeds or 1-2 sprigs of fresh fennel in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it warm or at room temperature. You can also add honey or lemon to taste. Some people find it helpful to drink fennel tea before meals or when they feel queasy. If you're pregnant or breastfeeding, it's a good idea to consult with a healthcare provider before trying fennel tea. When you drink fennel tea, you may notice a reduction in stomach discomfort and nausea within 15-30 minutes. You may also feel a sense of relief from bloating and cramps. Some people find that fennel tea helps them feel more relaxed and calm, which can also help alleviate queasiness.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, you should talk to a healthcare provider for proper diagnosis and treatment.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas have been used for centuries to help with queasy stomachs.

The leaves of the Ginkgo biloba tree contain flavonoids and terpenoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help to relax the muscles in the stomach and improve blood flow to the digestive system. This can reduce nausea and vomiting caused by queasiness. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba tea, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to have a protective effect on the stomach lining, reducing inflammation and irritation. The terpenoids, such as bilobalide, have a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help to reduce anxiety and stress-related queasiness.

To try Ginkgo biloba tea for a queasy stomach, start by drinking a cup of tea made from dried Ginkgo biloba leaves, steeped in hot water for 5-7 minutes. You can also add honey or lemon to taste. It's best to drink the tea on an empty stomach, as this can help to reduce nausea and vomiting. Take a few sips at a time, and wait a few minutes to see how your body reacts. If you experience any discomfort or worsening of symptoms, stop drinking the tea and consult with a healthcare professional. If you feel relief from your queasiness, you can continue to drink the tea as needed.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, it's best to seek medical attention to rule out any underlying conditions.

4. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla teas, commonly known as German chamomile tea, can be a soothing remedy for a queasy stomach.

The tea contains flavonoids, terpenoids, and volatile oils, which are responsible for its calming effects. The main constituents, apigenin and luteolin, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce stomach irritation and alleviate nausea. Apigenin, in particular, has been shown to bind to GABA receptors in the brain, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety that can exacerbate queasiness. If you're experiencing a queasy stomach, try drinking a warm cup of Matricaria chamomilla tea. Steep 1 teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers in 8 oz of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink. You can also add a squeeze of fresh lemon juice and a drizzle of honey to taste.

To get the most out of the tea, take a few deep breaths and relax for a few minutes after drinking it. This will allow the flavonoids and terpenoids to take effect and start soothing your stomach. Taking Matricaria chamomilla tea for a queasy stomach can lead to several outcomes. The anti-inflammatory properties of the tea may help reduce stomach irritation, alleviate nausea, and prevent vomiting. You may start to feel a sense of calmness and relaxation, which can help ease stomach discomfort. The tea may also help regulate digestion and reduce inflammation in the gut, promoting a sense of well-being.

However, if your queasiness is severe or persistent, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

5. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas, also known as peppermint tea, can help with a queasy stomach due to its natural properties and constituents.

The tea contains compounds like menthol, menthone, and limonene, which have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. These properties can help relax the muscles in the stomach and reduce nausea. When you feel queasy, try sipping on a warm cup of peppermint tea.

To make the most of it, take small sips and breathe in the steam to help calm your stomach. It's also essential to take a break from eating or drinking for a while to give your stomach a chance to settle. After drinking the tea, you may start to feel a decrease in nausea, and your stomach may begin to feel lighter and less bloated.

You may also notice a reduction in heartburn and indigestion symptoms.

6. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, also known as licorice root tea, can help alleviate queasy stomach symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

The tea contains glycyrrhizin, a compound that has been shown to reduce stomach inflammation and protect the mucous membranes in the digestive tract. This helps to calm nausea and vomiting, making it an effective remedy for queasy stomach issues. To use licorice root tea for a queasy stomach, start by brewing 1 teaspoon of dried licorice root in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Let it cool and drink it slowly. If you experience severe nausea, you may want to try sipping on the tea throughout the day to avoid overwhelming your stomach. Taking licorice root tea for a queasy stomach is likely to result in reduced nausea and vomiting, as well as a decrease in stomach inflammation. Many people find that their queasy stomach symptoms improve significantly after drinking the tea, and some even report a reduction in stomach acid production.

However, it's essential to note that licorice root tea can interact with certain medications and worsen high blood pressure, so consult a healthcare professional before making it a regular part of your routine.

7. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis teas have been used for centuries to help with queasy stomach.

The tea's active compounds, like linalool and linalyl acetate, are responsible for its soothing and calming effects. These constituents work by reducing inflammation in the digestive tract and calming the nervous system, which helps to alleviate nausea and vomiting. When you're feeling queasy, try drinking a warm cup of Melissa officinalis tea, ideally 20-30 minutes before meals.

You can add a small amount of honey to make it more palatable. It's also essential to stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water throughout the day. As you take action, you may notice a decrease in nausea and stomach discomfort within 30 minutes to an hour. Your appetite may return, and you may feel more comfortable eating smaller, more frequent meals.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper guidance.

8. Rubus idaeus

Rubus idaeus teas, also known as raspberry leaf tea, has been used for centuries to soothe a queasy stomach.

The tea contains properties like tannins, flavonoids, and magnesium that help to calm the digestive system. The tannins in raspberry leaf tea have astringent properties, which can help to reduce inflammation and ease nausea. The flavonoids, particularly anthocyanins and ellagic acid, have antioxidant properties that can help to protect the stomach lining and reduce inflammation. Magnesium, an essential mineral, can help to relax the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing spasms and cramps. If you're experiencing a queasy stomach, try drinking a cup of raspberry leaf tea 2-3 times a day.

To make the tea, steep 1 teaspoon of dried raspberry leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain and drink. You can also add honey or lemon to taste. Some people find that taking raspberry leaf tea with food can help to ease nausea. For example, try drinking a cup of tea with a light meal or snack, or before eating to see if it helps to settle your stomach. After drinking the tea, you may feel a sense of relief from nausea and stomach cramps.

Your stomach may feel less bloated and uncomfortable, and you may be able to eat and digest food more easily.

9. Citrus reticulata

Citrus reticulata teas, specifically those made from the peel of the mandarin orange, have been traditionally used to help alleviate queasy stomach.

The reason behind this is the presence of a compound called limonene, which has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe the stomach lining. Additionally, citrus reticulata teas contain flavonoids and volatile oils that can help ease digestion and reduce nausea. To try this remedy, start by steeping a few slices of dried citrus reticulata peel in a cup of hot water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it when you're feeling queasy.

You can also add honey or lemon to taste. When you drink the tea, you may start to feel a calming effect on your stomach, and the nausea may subside. The anti-inflammatory properties of limonene may also help reduce stomach cramps and discomfort. In some cases, people have reported that their queasiness disappears shortly after drinking the tea.

However, it's essential to note that if your queasiness persists or worsens, you should consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent queasy stomach from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help calm a queasy stomach.

Ginger tea is a popular choice because ginger has natural soothing properties that can ease nausea. Peppermint tea is another option, as peppermint can help relax the stomach muscles and reduce discomfort.

Some herbal teas may help settle the stomach, but results can vary from person to person.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for queasy stomach every day?

Consuming herbal teas for a queasy stomach every day can be safe as long as you choose gentle options like peppermint, chamomile, or ginger.

These teas can help calm your stomach and reduce nausea.

However, some teas like senna or licorice root can have stronger effects and may not be suitable for daily consumption.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in queasy stomach?

Herbal teas can start to help with a queasy stomach within a few hours.

Peppermint tea, for example, can calm the digestive system and reduce nausea. Ginger tea is also helpful, as it slows down digestion and absorbs stomach acids.

Some people may feel relief within 30 minutes to an hour after drinking the tea.

9 Herbal Teas For Queasy Stomach

New mothers often experience queasy stomach, also known as morning sickness, due to hormonal changes in their body.

This can cause nausea, vomiting, and dizziness, making everyday activities a challenge. Simple tasks like cooking, cleaning, or even getting out of bed can be overwhelming. The constant feeling of queasiness can lead to fatigue, dehydration, and a weakened immune system, making it difficult for new mothers to care for themselves and their newborns. They may also experience mood swings, anxiety, and irritability, which can strain relationships with their partners and family members. Furthermore, the lack of sleep and nutrition can exacerbate the symptoms, creating a vicious cycle. New mothers often rely on their partner, family, and friends for support, but even with help, the burden can be significant. They may have to adjust their work schedule, postpone their return to work, or even take a break from childcare.

The emotional toll of not being able to care for their baby as they would like can be devastating. The physical symptoms can also make it difficult to breastfeed, which can be a source of anxiety and guilt for new mothers. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate queasy stomach in new mothers. These teas are made from the leaves, flowers, or roots of certain plants and are often caffeine-free. They can be consumed hot or cold, depending on personal preference. The active ingredients in these teas work by soothing the digestive system, reducing inflammation, and promoting relaxation. They can also help to regulate the body's hormonal balance, which is often disrupted during pregnancy.

By drinking these teas, new mothers can find relief from their queasy stomach and improve their overall well-being.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help alleviate nausea and queasiness.

These compounds work by blocking the production of a chemical called serotonin, which is involved in sending signals to the brain that can trigger vomiting. When you drink ginger tea, the active compounds are absorbed into your bloodstream and reach your stomach, where they help calm down the muscles and reduce inflammation. To try Zingiber officinale teas for a queasy stomach, you can start by making a warm tea by steeping fresh ginger in hot water for a few minutes. You can also add honey or lemon to taste.

If you're feeling really queasy, try sipping on small amounts of ginger tea throughout the day, rather than drinking it all at once. If your queasiness persists, you may want to talk to a healthcare professional for further guidance. Taking Zingiber officinale teas may help your queasiness subside, allowing you to feel more comfortable and able to eat and drink normally. You may also find that your stomach aches or cramps decrease in severity.

However, if your queasiness is accompanied by other symptoms like fever, vomiting blood, or severe abdominal pain, you should seek medical attention immediately.

2. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas contains compounds like anethole, fenchone, and limonene, which are known for their carminative and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to ease digestive issues, including queasy stomach. The anti-inflammatory effect helps to reduce inflammation in the stomach and intestines, while the carminative effect helps to release trapped gas and reduce nausea.

To use Foeniculum vulgare teas for a queasy stomach, you can try the following: - Start by making a tea by steeping dried fennel seeds in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. - Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day. - If you are experiencing nausea, try sipping the tea slowly and take small sips.

When you take these actions, you may experience the following outcomes: - Relief from nausea and queasiness - Reduced stomach cramps and bloating - Improved digestion and reduced inflammation in the stomach and intestines - A decrease in the frequency and severity of queasy stomach episodes.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contain a compound called glycyrrhizin, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory and carminative properties.

These properties help to soothe the stomach lining and reduce inflammation that can cause queasiness. The tea also contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have antioxidant and anti-spasmodic effects, further helping to calm the digestive system. To help with a queasy stomach, you can try drinking Glycyrrhiza glabra tea after meals, especially when consuming foods that are known to cause stomach upset, such as spicy or fatty foods. Start by drinking a small amount, about 1/4 cup, 2-3 times a day, and see how your body reacts.

As you get used to the tea, you can gradually increase the amount. You can also try combining it with other herbs, such as peppermint or ginger, to enhance its effects. As the glycyrrhizin and other compounds in the tea work to calm your stomach, you may notice a reduction in nausea and discomfort. You may also experience improved digestion and reduced bloating.

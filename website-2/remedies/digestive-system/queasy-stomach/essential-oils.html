10 Herbal Essential Oils For Queasy Stomach

You can add a few drops of the oil to a warm bath, inhale it directly from the bottle, or mix it with a carrier oil and apply it to your skin.

1. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains a compound called menthol, which has a calming effect on the digestive system.

It also contains limonene and beta-pinene, which are anti-inflammatory agents that help reduce stomach discomfort. The antispasmodic properties of menthol help relax the muscles in the stomach, reducing cramps and spasms that can cause queasiness. The antioxidant properties of beta-pinene and limonene help protect the stomach lining from damage, reducing inflammation and promoting healing.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the stomach muscles, Mentha x piperita essential oils can provide relief from queasy stomach symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and 2 cups of water. Sterilize a glass jar and utensils.
  2. Add 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves to the sterilized glass jar. Pour in 2 cups of water.
  3. Cover the jar and leave it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar daily.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool place. Use 5-7 drops of the essential oil in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for skin use.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains active compounds like gingerol and shogaol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce nausea and alleviate queasy stomach symptoms. The warming properties of ginger oil also help stimulate digestion and relieve stomach discomfort. Ginger's antispasmodic properties help ease cramping and spasms in the stomach, further contributing to its soothing effects.

The combination of these properties in Zingiber officinale essential oils makes it a helpful remedy for queasy stomach issues.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh ginger roots, a cup of carrier oil (like coconut oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Peel the ginger roots and slice them into thin pieces.
  3. Combine the sliced ginger with the carrier oil in the glass jar. The ratio is 1:1 (1 cup of oil for 1 cup of ginger).
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. After 2 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the ginger solids.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains a compound called anethole, which is responsible for its calming and carminative properties.

Anethole helps to relax the stomach muscles and reduce spasms, making it easier to digest food. The essential oil also contains limonene and fenchone, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help to soothe an upset stomach. By reducing inflammation and calming the stomach, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help to alleviate queasiness and discomfort.

Its gentle properties make it a popular natural remedy for digestive issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Heat 1 cup of water in a saucepan, then add 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl. Discard the seeds.
  4. Add the strained liquid to the 2 cups of carrier oil in a separate bowl. Mix well.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 days, shaking the bowl daily. Then, filter the oil through a cheesecloth and store it in a dark glass bottle.

4. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains a compound called limonene, which is known for its anti-inflammatory properties.

It also contains geraniol and linalool, which have a calming effect on the digestive system. These active constituents help to reduce nausea and ease digestive discomfort by soothing the stomach muscles and reducing inflammation. When inhaled, the citrus scent of Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can also help to stimulate digestion and relieve queasy stomachs.

The oil's antispasmodic properties can also help to calm stomach spasms, providing relief from queasiness.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves. Wash them with water.
  2. Dry the leaves completely using paper towels or a clean cloth.
  3. Use a cheese grater or a food processor to chop the leaves into small pieces.
  4. Use a steam distiller to extract the essential oil from the chopped leaves. Mix 1 cup of water with 1 tablespoon of chopped leaves in the distiller.
  5. Collect the extracted essential oil and store it in a dark glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops of the oil per day for a queasy stomach remedy.

5. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a compound called curcumin, which is known for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Curcumin works by reducing inflammation in the stomach and intestines, which can help to alleviate queasy stomach symptoms. The essential oil also contains other bioactive constituents, such as turmerone and atlantone, which have been shown to have anti-nausea effects. These compounds help to soothe the stomach and reduce symptoms of nausea and vomiting.

By reducing inflammation and alleviating nausea, Curcuma longa essential oils may help to provide relief for those experiencing a queasy stomach.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Curcuma longa roots, 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Dry the Curcuma longa roots in the sun for 3-4 days or use a dehydrator. Grind them into a fine powder.
  3. Fill the glass jar with 1/2 cup of the powdered Curcuma longa roots. Pour in the carrier oil, leaving 1 inch of space at the top.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter.
  5. Discard the solids and transfer the Curcuma longa essential oil to a new glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops in a bath or mix with a carrier oil for skin application.

6. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains rosinic acid, borneol, and camphor as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds contribute to its anti-inflammatory and carminative properties, which help to soothe an upset stomach. The anti-inflammatory effect of rosinic acid reduces inflammation in the digestive tract, while borneol and camphor help to relax the stomach muscles and reduce spasms. The carminative properties of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils also help to alleviate nausea and vomiting by releasing trapped gas in the digestive system.

By reducing inflammation, relaxing stomach muscles, and alleviating gas, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help to calm a queasy stomach.

  1. Gather materials: 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves, a clean glass jar with a lid, a cheese cloth or a coffee filter, a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Wash the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves with 1 cup of water to remove dirt and debris.
  3. Combine 1/2 cup of the cleaned Rosmarinus officinalis leaves with 1 cup of carrier oil in the glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. Strain the liquid through the cheese cloth or coffee filter into another jar.
  5. Discard the solids and use the strained liquid as your Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil for queasy stomach. Store it in a cool, dark place.

Dill apiole works by relaxing the muscles in the digestive tract, reducing inflammation and spasms. This helps to ease queasy stomachs and alleviate symptoms of nausea and vomiting. The anethole and limonene in Anethum graveolens essential oils also have anti-inflammatory properties, which soothe the stomach lining and reduce irritation.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the digestive tract, Anethum graveolens essential oils can help to calm a queasy stomach and promote a sense of well-being.

  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the solids.

8. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains licorice root's active constituents, glycyrrhizin, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe the digestive system. Glycyrrhizin works to reduce inflammation and inhibit the production of stomach acids, which can help alleviate queasy stomachs. The flavonoids present in the oil also exhibit antioxidant properties, protecting the stomach lining from damage and promoting healing.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the stomach, glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can provide relief from queasy stomach symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots, a glass jar with a lid, a coffee filter, and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Crush 2 tablespoons of the roots into small pieces and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Add 4 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil) to the jar, covering the crushed roots completely.
  4. Steep the mixture for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day, then strain it through the coffee filter into the dark glass bottle.
  5. Discard the solids, and your Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oil is ready to use. Store it in a cool, dark place.

9. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, bilobalide, and ginkgolides.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help soothe an upset stomach. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba essential oils have been shown to have a protective effect on the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, reducing inflammation and irritation. Bilobalide and ginkgolides have also been found to have a calming effect on the stomach, reducing spasms and cramping.

By reducing inflammation and calming the stomach, Ginkgo biloba essential oils may help alleviate queasy stomach symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves, a clean glass jar with a lid, and a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil.
  2. Dry the Ginkgo biloba leaves in a low-temperature oven (150°F - 200°F) for 1-2 hours.
  3. Combine 1/4 cup of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves with 2 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. Strain the oil after the steeping process.
  5. Discard the solids and transfer the Ginkgo biloba essential oil to a clean glass bottle. Use 5-10 drops as needed to soothe queasy stomach.

10. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are responsible for its soothing and calming effects.

These two compounds have a direct impact on the digestive system, helping to ease queasiness and calm stomach cramps. The sedative properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help to reduce anxiety and stress, which are common triggers for stomach problems. Additionally, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils has a gentle anti-inflammatory effect, which can help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and alleviate discomfort.

This combination of properties makes Lavandula angustifolia essential oils a natural and effective remedy for queasy stomachs.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a pot.
  2. Boil the water and add the flowers. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the pot from heat and let it steep for 2 hours. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a clean glass bottle and let it sit in the sun for 2 days to evaporate excess water.
  5. After 2 days, discard the remaining liquid and use a hydrosol kit or a distillation apparatus to extract the essential oil from the remaining liquid.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent queasy stomach from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help ease a queasy stomach.

Some teas, like ginger or peppermint, have natural ingredients that can calm your stomach and reduce nausea.

When you feel queasy, try sipping on a warm cup of herbal tea to see if it helps settle your stomach and make you feel better.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for queasy stomach every day?

While herbal teas can be helpful for an upset stomach, having them every day might not be the best choice.

Some herbs, like peppermint, can be beneficial, but others, like chamomile, might cause stomach issues in large amounts.

Moderation is key, so try sipping a cup or two when needed and see how your body reacts.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in queasy stomach?

Herbal teas can start to ease queasy stomachs within 30 minutes to an hour after drinking. Some people may feel better within a shorter time, while others may need to try a few different teas to find what works for them.

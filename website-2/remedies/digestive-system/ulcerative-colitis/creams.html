1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains curcumin, which is a bioactive constituent that has anti-inflammatory properties.

It also contains other compounds like demethoxycurcumin and bisdemethoxycurcumin that help reduce inflammation in the body. These properties are beneficial for people with ulcerative colitis because they can help decrease the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which contribute to the condition. The antioxidant properties of curcumin also help protect the colon from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the colon, Curcuma longa creams may help alleviate symptoms of ulcerative colitis, such as diarrhea and abdominal pain.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, 1 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil for infusion.
  2. Infuse coconut oil with turmeric: Heat 2 tablespoons of coconut oil in a small saucepan over low heat. Add 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder. Remove from heat and let it sit for 2 hours.
  3. Melt ingredients: In a double boiler, melt 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, and 1 cup of beeswax. Stir until smooth and combined.
  4. Add infused coconut oil and essential oil: Add the infused coconut oil and 10 drops of tea tree oil to the melted mixture. Stir well.
  5. Pour into a mold: Pour the mixture into a mold or a jar. Let it cool and harden. Once set, your Curcuma longa cream for ulcerative colitis is ready to use.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents like gingerol and shogaol, which have potent anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation and swelling in the digestive tract, a common issue for people with ulcerative colitis. Ginger's antispasmodic properties can also help alleviate cramps and spasms in the intestines, providing relief from symptoms. Additionally, ginger has antioxidant properties that can help protect the gut from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the gut, Zingiber officinale creams may be a useful complementary treatment for managing ulcerative colitis symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of ginger oil, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of aloe vera gel.
  2. Melt the coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Once melted, remove from heat and let cool slightly. Stir in the ginger oil, vitamin E oil, and aloe vera gel.
  4. Pour the mixture into small containers and let cool completely. This will help the mixture harden and become a solid cream.
  5. Store the Zingiber officinale cream in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. Before use, gently massage the cream onto the affected area to help soothe and calm ulcerative colitis symptoms.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis creams contains bioactive constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in the colon, a common issue for people with ulcerative colitis. The anti-inflammatory properties also help reduce the severity of symptoms like diarrhea and abdominal pain. Aloe-emodin has been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria that can cause inflammation, which contributes to the development of ulcerative colitis.

The soothing properties of acemannan help repair the lining of the colon, promoting a healthy environment for the growth of good bacteria.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of aloe vera gel, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 teaspoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 teaspoons of honey.
  2. In a saucepan, heat the distilled water over low heat. Add the aloe vera gel, coconut oil, and shea butter. Stir until the mixture is well combined.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool for 10 minutes. Add the vitamin E oil and honey. Stir well until the mixture is smooth.
  4. Strain the mixture into a clean bowl to remove any impurities. Let it cool and thicken for 30 minutes.
  5. Transfer the mixture into a container and refrigerate it for at least 2 hours. Once set, scoop out the cream and apply it to the affected areas as needed.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains licorice root extract, which has been found to help with ulcerative colitis.

The active constituents, glycyrrhetinic acid and glabridin, have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce inflammation in the colon. Glycyrrhizin in licorice root also acts as an antioxidant, preventing damage to the colon lining and promoting healing. Additionally, licorice root extract helps to soothe and protect the mucous membranes in the colon, reducing symptoms of ulcerative colitis such as bleeding and diarrhea.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Glycyrrhiza glabra creams can help manage symptoms of ulcerative colitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of glycerin, and 4 tablespoons of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root powder.
  2. Boil the distilled water in a saucepan, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Add the dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root powder to the simmering water and let it steep for 20 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Mix 2 tablespoons of the strained liquid with 2 tablespoons of glycerin and 1 tablespoon of a carrier oil (like coconut oil) to create the cream. Store in an airtight container in the fridge.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy gut environment, Echinacea purpurea creams may provide relief for individuals with ulcerative colitis.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of lavender oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture. Let it steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Calendula officinalis cream may help alleviate symptoms of ulcerative colitis, such as diarrhea, abdominal pain, and rectal bleeding.

  4. In a double boiler, melt the beeswax and shea butter. Add the infused coconut oil and vitamin E oil, and stir until well combined.

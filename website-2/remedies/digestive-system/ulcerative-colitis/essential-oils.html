11 Herbal Essential Oils For Ulcerative Colitis

Herbal essential oils, particularly those derived from certain herbs, have shown promise in helping alleviate symptoms of ulcerative colitis.

Curcuma longa, or turmeric, is another herb that has potent anti-inflammatory compounds called curcuminoids, which can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract.

Glycyrrhiza glabra, or licorice root, has been traditionally used to soothe stomach ulcers and reduce inflammation. The benefits of using these herbal teas are numerous. By reducing inflammation and soothing the gut, people with ulcerative colitis may experience fewer flare-ups, reduced symptoms, and improved quality of life. This can mean being able to eat a wider variety of foods, engaging in physical activities without discomfort, and feeling more energized throughout the day.

Additionally, herbal teas can be used in conjunction with conventional treatments, allowing individuals to take a more active role in managing their condition.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains gingerols and shogaols, which are bioactive constituents with anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, a major contributor to ulcerative colitis symptoms. The anti-inflammatory properties of gingerols and shogaols also help slow down cell death and reduce the severity of symptoms. The oil's antispasmodic properties can help ease abdominal pain and cramping associated with ulcerative colitis.

By reducing inflammation and alleviating symptoms, Zingiber officinale essential oils may provide relief for individuals suffering from ulcerative colitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale roots, 2 cups of water, a large pot, and a distillation apparatus.
  2. Wash the Zingiber officinale roots in cold water, then chop them into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped roots and water in the large pot, and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and simmer for 2 hours, then transfer the mixture to the distillation apparatus.
  5. Collect the essential oil that condenses in the distillation apparatus, and store it in a dark glass bottle.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains curcuminoids, a group of bioactive compounds that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The main curcuminoids present are curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin. These compounds work by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, such as COX-2 and 5-LOX, which are involved in the inflammation and tissue damage seen in ulcerative colitis. By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, curcuma longa essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of ulcerative colitis, including abdominal pain, diarrhea, and rectal bleeding.

This is achieved through its ability to modulate the immune response and promote healing in the gut lining.

  1. Get 2 cups of Curcuma longa roots. Wash them with water. Pat dry with a cloth.
  2. Put 1 cup of the roots in a blender. Add 2 cups of water. Blend until the roots are smooth.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Squeeze the cheesecloth to get all the liquid.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut oil) to the liquid. Mix well.
  5. Store the Curcuma longa essential oil in a dark glass bottle. Use it within 2 weeks.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin that has anti-inflammatory properties.

Glycyrrhizin helps to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, which is a key factor in the development of ulcerative colitis. It also has antioxidant properties that protect the gut lining from oxidative stress, reducing damage and promoting healing. Additionally, glycyrrhizin has been shown to inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria in the gut, which can contribute to the severity of ulcerative colitis.

The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of glycyrrhizin in Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils make it a potentially beneficial treatment for ulcerative colitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots, a double boiler, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Grind 2 tablespoons of dried roots into fine powder using a mortar and pestle.
  3. Mix 1 cup of carrier oil with the ground roots in the double boiler.
  4. Heat the mixture in the double boiler for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally, until the oil turns brownish color.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth and discard the solids. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

4. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains biologically active constituents like aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, a common issue in ulcerative colitis. Aloe-emodin, in particular, has been shown to have a protective effect on the gut lining, preventing damage and promoting healing. Acemannan has immunomodulatory effects, helping to regulate the immune system and prevent excessive inflammation.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Aloe barbadensis essential oils may be a useful adjunctive treatment for ulcerative colitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Mix 10 tablespoons of aloe gel with 10 tablespoons of carrier oil in a clean glass bowl.
  3. Add 10 drops of vitamin E oil and 10 drops of lavender oil to the mixture and stir well.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a clean glass bottle with a dropper lid and label it.
  5. Store the bottle in a cool, dark place and use 5-7 drops per application as needed.

5. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis essential oils contains quinidine and quinine, two potent alkaloids that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation and pain in the digestive tract, a common symptom of ulcerative colitis. The antiseptic and antibacterial properties of quinidine and quinine also help combat infections that can exacerbate the condition. Additionally, the oil's antioxidant properties help protect the colon's mucous membrane from damage, promoting healing and reducing irritation.

By addressing inflammation, infection, and oxidative stress, Cinchona officinalis essential oils may provide relief for individuals suffering from ulcerative colitis.

  1. Gather 100g of Cinchona officinalis bark, a cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Crush the Cinchona bark into small pieces using a mortar and pestle, then mix it with the carrier oil in the glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the oil extract from the bark.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the essential oil in a cool, dark place and use 5-10 drops in a carrier oil for ulcerative colitis relief, after consulting a healthcare professional.

6. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum essential oils contains bioactive constituents like silymarin, silibinin, and silidianin, which are known for their anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, a key factor in ulcerative colitis. Silymarin also has antioxidant properties, which protect the gut lining from damage and promote healing. Silidianin has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which contribute to the development of ulcerative colitis.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Silybum marianum essential oils may provide relief from symptoms of ulcerative colitis.

  1. Gather Silybum marianum seeds, a carrier oil (coconut or olive), and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Dry 2 cups of Silybum marianum seeds in a low-temperature oven (150°F - 200°F) for 2 hours.
  3. Grind 1 cup of dried Silybum marianum seeds into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or spice mill.
  4. Mix 1 cup of Silybum marianum powder with 2 cups of carrier oil in the clean glass jar. Stir well and seal the jar.
  5. Steep the mixture for 2 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar daily. Strain and discard the solids to get the Silybum marianum essential oil.

7. Terminalia chebula

The antioxidants in Terminalia chebula essential oils can also help protect the gut lining from damage and promote healing.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Terminalia chebula essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of ulcerative colitis.

  2. Dry the Terminalia chebula berries in a low-temperature oven (150°F - 200°F) for 2-3 hours.
  3. Grind the dried berries into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.

8. Althaea officinalis

Althaea officinalis essential oils contains mucilages like glucuronic acid, rhamnose, and arabinose, which are powerful anti-inflammatory agents.

These mucilages help soothe and protect the mucous membranes in the colon, reducing inflammation and discomfort associated with ulcerative colitis. The essential oil's demulcent properties create a protective barrier on the mucous membranes, shielding them from irritation and allowing them to heal. Additionally, the oil's anti-oxidant properties help reduce oxidative stress, which is often linked to the development and progression of ulcerative colitis.

The combination of these properties makes Althaea officinalis essential oils a promising natural remedy for managing ulcerative colitis symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Althaea officinalis flowers and 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba oil or sweet almond oil).
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar, then stir well.
  3. Place the jar in a dark, cool place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day.
  4. After the infusing period, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Althaea officinalis essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains a compound called anethole, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

11. Ulmus rubra

Ulmus rubra essential oils contains a variety of bioactive constituents, including salicin, salicylic acid, and flavonoids, which provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties can help reduce inflammation in the colon, a common symptom of ulcerative colitis. The salicin in Ulmus rubra essential oils can also help reduce pain and discomfort associated with the condition. Additionally, the antioxidant properties can help protect the cells in the colon from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to the development of ulcerative colitis.

The anti-inflammatory properties of Ulmus rubra essential oils may also help reduce the severity of ulcerative colitis symptoms, such as diarrhea and abdominal pain.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent ulcerative colitis from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help reduce symptoms and prevent ulcerative colitis from forming.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and ginger tea, have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe the digestive system.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for ulcerative colitis every day?

Herbal teas can be a helpful addition to ulcerative colitis management, but their safety for daily consumption varies.

Some teas, like peppermint and chamomile, are generally considered safe and may even ease symptoms. However, others like licorice root and senna can be problematic, potentially irritating the digestive system further.

It's essential to understand the specific tea's effects on the body.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in ulcerative colitis?

Herbal teas can take a few weeks to a few months to show results in ulcerative colitis.

Some people notice improvements in symptoms like bloating and cramps within a few weeks, while others may take longer to feel the benefits.

Everyone's body is different, so it's hard to predict exactly when you'll see changes.

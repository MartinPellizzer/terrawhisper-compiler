What Are The Main Causes Of Upper Abdominal Pain?

Upper abdominal pain can be caused by several factors.

Gallstones, small hard stones that form in the gallbladder, can cause severe pain in the upper right or middle abdomen. They can block the bile ducts, stopping bile from flowing into the small intestine, which can lead to pain and nausea. Pancreatitis, inflammation of the pancreas, is another cause of upper abdominal pain.

This can be triggered by gallstones blocking the bile ducts, or by heavy drinking, which can cause the pancreas to become inflamed. Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) is also a common cause of upper abdominal pain. In GERD, the stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus, causing a burning sensation in the chest and upper abdomen. This can be due to eating large or heavy meals, lying down after eating, or wearing tight clothing that puts pressure on the stomach.

In all these cases, the pain can be sharp or dull, and can be accompanied by nausea, vomiting, or fever.

Below are the main causes of upper abdominal pain.

  • Gallstones: Gallstones are small, hard stones that form in the gallbladder, a small organ under the liver, and they can cause upper abdominal pain by blocking the bile ducts and causing a buildup of bile, leading to inflammation and severe pain in the upper right or middle part of the abdomen.
  • Pancreatitis: Pancreatitis causes upper abdominal pain because the pancreas, a vital organ located in the upper part of the abdomen, becomes inflamed, leading to severe pain and discomfort in the upper abdominal region.
  • Gastroesophageal Reflux: Gastroesophageal Reflux occurs when stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus, causing irritation and pain in the upper part of the abdomen due to the burning sensation and inflammation it triggers.
  • Peptic Ulcer: A peptic ulcer is a cause of upper abdominal pain because it creates an open sore in the lining of the stomach or the first part of the small intestine, which can cause sharp, stabbing pain and discomfort in the upper abdomen.
  • Food Poisoning: Food poisoning occurs when people eat food contaminated with harmful bacteria, viruses, or other germs, which can cause inflammation in the stomach and intestines, leading to sharp upper abdominal pain, nausea, and vomiting.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Upper Abdominal Pain?

If you have upper abdominal pain, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can irritate your stomach or worsen the condition.

One such herb is 'Aristolochia clematidea', also known as guanacurito, which can cause liver damage and exacerbate stomach pain. Another herb to steer clear of is 'Hyptis suaveolens', also known as pulegose, as it can cause stomach irritation and discomfort.

'Ginkgo biloba' should also be avoided, as it can increase stomach acid production, leading to more discomfort. 'Commiphora molmol', also known as myrrh, can also be problematic, as it can cause stomach upset and nausea. Additionally, 'Paeonia lactiflora', or peony root, can slow down digestion and worsen upper abdominal pain.

Lastly, 'Aconitum carmichaelii', also known as monk's hood, should be avoided due to its potential to cause stomach cramps and nausea.

What Are The Main Causes Of Hyperacidity?

Hyperacidity occurs when the body produces too much acid in the stomach.

The main causes of hyperacidity are related to lifestyle and dietary habits. Stress is a significant contributor to hyperacidity as it activates the body's fight-or-flight response, releasing hormones like adrenaline that stimulate the stomach to produce more acid. When we're stressed, our body prepares for a physical response, and the stomach acid production increases as a result. Another common cause of hyperacidity is consuming caffeine. Caffeine stimulates the stomach to produce more acid by increasing the production of gastric juices, which contain digestive enzymes and acid.

When we drink coffee or tea, the caffeine can irritate the stomach lining and cause the production of more acid, leading to hyperacidity. Eating spicy food is also a common cause of hyperacidity. Spicy food contains capsaicin, a compound that irritates the stomach lining and stimulates the production of stomach acid. When we eat spicy food, the capsaicin can cause the stomach to produce more acid to try and digest the food, leading to an increase in stomach acid production. These causes can lead to symptoms like heartburn, acid reflux, and stomach discomfort.

Understanding the causes of hyperacidity can help us make lifestyle changes to manage the condition.

Below are the main causes of hyperacidity.

  • Stress: When we are stressed, our body releases extra stomach acid to prepare for the "fight or flight" response, which can cause the stomach to become overactive and produce too much acid, leading to hyperacidity.
  • Caffeine: Caffeine causes hyperacidity because it increases the production of stomach acid, which is a strong digestive liquid that can irritate the lining of the stomach and lead to an imbalance in the body's natural pH levels.
  • Spicy food: Spicy food causes hyperacidity because the capsaicin in hot peppers irritates the stomach lining, releasing more acid than usual to neutralize the heat, resulting in excess stomach acid and hyperacidity.
  • Citrus: Citrus fruits like oranges, lemons, and limes cause hyperacidity because their high concentration of citric acid and ascorbic acid (vitamin C) in their juice can disrupt the body's natural acid-base balance and lead to an increase in stomach acid production.
  • Tomatoes: Tomatoes cause hyperacidity because they contain a high amount of citric acid, which is a natural substance that increases the level of acid in the body.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Hyperacidity?

If you have hyperacidity, some herbs can make the problem worse.

Avoid mint (Mentha spp. ), as it can stimulate digestion and worsen acid production. Also, avoid ginger-like herbs like Asafoetida (Ferula asafoetida) and Sichuan pepper (Zanthoxylum bungeanum) as they can increase stomach acid.

Burdock (Arctium lappa), dandelion (Taraxacum officinale), and senna (Cassia spp.). can also irritate the stomach and worsen hyperacidity. These herbs contain compounds that can stimulate bowel movements and stomach contractions, making them unsuitable for people with hyperacidity.

Furthermore, avoid herbs like yerba mate (Ilex paraguariensis) and guayusa (Ilex guayusa) as they can increase heart rate and worsen acid reflux symptoms.

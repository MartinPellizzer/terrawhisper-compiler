What Are The Main Causes Of Difficulty Eating?

Difficulty eating can be caused by several reasons.

One of the main causes is Oral Cancer, a disease that affects the mouth and throat. When cancer occurs in the mouth, it can make eating and swallowing painful and difficult. Tumors can also block the path of food, making it hard to eat. Another cause is Eating Disorders, such as anorexia nervosa and bulimia nervosa.

These conditions cause people to have a distorted body image, leading them to eat very little or throw up after meals. As a result, their bodies don't get the necessary nutrients, making eating a challenging task. Lastly, Dental Problems are a common cause of difficulty eating. Issues like tooth decay, gum disease, and missing teeth can make chewing and swallowing food difficult.

For example, a person with a missing tooth may struggle to chew tough foods, while someone with gum disease may experience pain when eating.

Below are the main causes of difficulty eating.

  • Oral Cancer: Oral cancer can cause difficulty eating because it makes it hard to chew, swallow, and move food around the mouth due to painful sores, numbness, or the removal of part of the tongue or other mouth tissues.
  • Eating Disorders: Eating disorders, such as anorexia and bulimia, can cause difficulty eating due to the mind and body becoming so focused on being thin or avoiding certain foods that it leads to a loss of appetite, a fear of eating, and sometimes even physical problems like stomach pains.
  • Dental Problems: People with dental problems may have difficulty eating because painful teeth, lost teeth, or mouth infections make it hard to chew and swallow food properly.
  • Neurological Disorders: Neurological disorders can cause difficulty eating because they affect the brain and nerves that control the muscles needed for swallowing, chewing, and moving food around the mouth.
  • Swallowing Disorders: Swallowing disorders, also known as dysphagia, is a cause of difficulty eating because it makes it hard for food to move smoothly down the throat into the stomach, leading to problems swallowing and potentially choking.
  • Esophageal Cancer: Esophageal Cancer grows in the tube that carries food from the mouth to the stomach, blocking food from passing through and causing difficulty eating as a result.

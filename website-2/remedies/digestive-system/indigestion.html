What Are The Main Causes Of Indigestion?

Indigestion is a common problem that affects many people.

There are several main causes, including food intolerance, overeating, and gastritis. Food intolerance occurs when the body has trouble digesting certain foods, such as gluten, lactose, or spicy foods. When these foods are eaten, they can cause the stomach to become inflamed and irritated, leading to indigestion. This is because the body's immune system mistakenly attacks the food, thinking it's a threat. Overeating is another common cause of indigestion.

When we eat too much food, it can put pressure on the stomach, causing it to stretch and become irritated. This can lead to the release of stomach acid, which can irritate the lining of the stomach and cause discomfort. Gastritis is a condition where the stomach lining becomes inflamed and irritated. This can be caused by a number of factors, including infection, stress, or the use of certain medications. When the stomach lining is inflamed, it can cause stomach acid to leak into the digestive tract, leading to indigestion and other symptoms.

In addition to these causes, other factors, such as smoking, pregnancy, and certain medications, can also contribute to indigestion.

Below are the main causes of indigestion.

  • Food Intolerance: Food intolerance occurs when the body has trouble breaking down certain foods, leading to indigestion symptoms like bloating, discomfort, and stomach pain.
  • Overeating: When you eat too much food, your stomach works harder to digest it, which can cause the stomach acid to mix with the food and create a burning sensation in the chest, leading to indigestion.
  • Gastritis: Gastritis, an inflammation of the stomach lining, can cause indigestion because the inflamed lining cannot properly break down food, leading to discomfort, bloating, and stomach pain.
  • Pregnancy: During pregnancy, the growing baby puts pressure on the stomach, which can cause digestive problems, including indigestion, by slowing down the movement of food through the digestive system.
  • Hormonal Changes: During hormonal changes, such as those experienced during pregnancy or menopause, the body produces more stomach acid, leading to an increase in digestive issues like indigestion.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Indigestion?

If you have indigestion, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can irritate your stomach or make symptoms worse.

One such herb is 'Trachyspermum ammi' (also known as Ajwain), which can increase stomach acid and cause heartburn. Another herb to steer clear of is 'Curcuma longa' (Turmeric), although it's commonly used for its medicinal properties, high doses can irritate the stomach lining and worsen indigestion.

'Piper nigrum' (Black Pepper) can also be problematic, as its spicy compounds can increase acid production and make symptoms of indigestion worse. Additionally, 'Cinnamomum verum' (Cinnamon) can cause stomach upset and heartburn in some people, especially if consumed in large amounts. Lastly, 'Myristica fragrans' (Nutmeg) can cause stomach irritation and worsen indigestion due to its high oil content and potential to increase stomach acid.

It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies, especially if you're experiencing persistent or severe indigestion symptoms.

Overcoming Loss of Appetite: Causes, Medicinal Herbs, and Herbal Remedies

What are the causes of loss of appetite?

Loss of appetite, also known as anorexia, can be caused by various health conditions.

One main cause is diabetes. People with diabetes often experience a decrease in appetite due to high blood sugar levels, which can make them feel tired, weak, and nauseous. Cancer is another reason for loss of appetite. Cancer cells can interfere with the body's ability to produce appetite hormones, such as ghrelin. Additionally, cancer treatments, like chemotherapy, can also reduce appetite by causing nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. HIV infection can also lead to loss of appetite.

HIV affects the body's ability to absorb nutrients, leading to malnutrition and weight loss. People with HIV may experience a decrease in appetite due to the infection's effects on the digestive system and the release of certain hormones that regulate appetite. Kidney failure can cause a decrease in appetite due to the buildup of toxins in the body. When the kidneys fail to filter waste properly, it can lead to a buildup of toxins, causing nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, which can suppress appetite. Liver disease, such as cirrhosis, can also lead to loss of appetite. The liver plays a crucial role in regulating appetite hormones and metabolism.

When the liver is damaged, it can disrupt the body's ability to produce these hormones, leading to a decrease in appetite.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains bioactive constituents like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that may help reduce the symptoms of hyperthyroidism. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the conversion of thyroxine (T4) to triiodothyronine (T3), which is a key factor in hyperthyroidism. The saponins in Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures also have a diuretic effect, which can help alleviate the swelling and water retention often associated with hyperthyroidism.

By reducing inflammation and inhibiting the conversion of T4 to T3, Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures may help alleviate the symptoms of hyperthyroidism.

  1. Gather 100g of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root and 500ml of vodka in a clean glass container.
  2. Add the dried root to the vodka and seal the container. Let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the container every day.
  3. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean container. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Store it in a cool, dark place.
  5. To use, take 15-30 drops of the tincture in water 2-3 times a day as needed. Consult with a healthcare professional before using this remedy for hyperthyroidism.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce inflammation in the thyroid gland, which is often present in hyperthyroidism. The volatile oils in ginger, such as zingiberene and bisabolol, have antioxidant effects that help to protect the thyroid gland from damage. Ginger's ability to relax the smooth muscles and blood vessels may also help to reduce the symptoms of hyperthyroidism, such as rapid heartbeat and anxiety.

The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Zingiber officinale tinctures may help to regulate thyroid hormone production and alleviate symptoms associated with hyperthyroidism.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (Ginger) roots, 2 cups of 80% vodka, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Cut the ginger roots into small pieces and fill the glass jar about 1/4 full.
  3. Pour 2 cups of 80% vodka over the ginger pieces, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into another clean glass jar, discarding the ginger pieces.

3. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa tinctures contains compounds like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Curcumin specifically has been shown to inhibit the production of thyroid hormones, which can help alleviate symptoms of hyperthyroidism. The anti-inflammatory properties of Curcuma longa tinctures can also help reduce swelling and pain in the thyroid gland, while its antioxidant properties can protect against cell damage.

Regular consumption of Curcuma longa tinctures may help regulate thyroid function and promote a balanced metabolism.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa root, 2 cups of 80% vodka, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the dried root with 2 cups of vodka in the glass jar. Make sure the root is fully covered by the vodka.

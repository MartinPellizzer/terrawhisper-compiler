9 Herbal Essential Oils For Hyperthyroidism

Herbal essential oils and teas can be a helpful natural remedy for people with hyperthyroidism.

Hyperthyroidism is a condition where the thyroid gland produces too much of the hormone thyroxine, which can cause symptoms like anxiety, weight loss, and rapid heartbeat. Herbal teas can help to calm the thyroid gland and bring hormone levels back into balance. Lavandula angustifolia, also known as lavender, is one herb that can help to calm the nervous system and promote relaxation. Its sweet, floral scent can help to reduce anxiety and stress, which are common symptoms of hyperthyroidism. Another herb that may be beneficial is Melissa officinalis, or lemon balm. It has a soothing effect on the nervous system and can help to reduce stress and promote relaxation.

Lemon balm tea has been shown to have a calming effect on the thyroid gland and can help to regulate hormone levels. Cinchona officinalis, or the cinchona tree, is another herb that can be used to help manage hyperthyroidism. The bark of this tree has been used for centuries to treat malaria and other conditions, and its anti-inflammatory properties can help to reduce inflammation in the thyroid gland. When you drink herbal teas, the active compounds in the herbs are absorbed into your bloodstream and can start to work to calm your thyroid gland. As your thyroid gland becomes less active, you may notice a reduction in symptoms like anxiety, weight loss, and rapid heartbeat. Drinking herbal teas regularly can help to promote a sense of calm and well-being, making it easier to manage the stress and anxiety that often come with hyperthyroidism. It's worth noting that while herbal teas can be a helpful natural remedy for hyperthyroidism, they should not be used as a replacement for medical treatment.

If you have been diagnosed with hyperthyroidism, it's essential to work with your doctor to develop a treatment plan that includes herbal remedies, medication, and lifestyle changes to help manage your symptoms.

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains a compound called linalool, which has a sedative effect on the nervous system.

This oil also contains linalyl acetate, which helps to reduce anxiety and stress. The calming properties of linalool and linalyl acetate in Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of hyperthyroidism, such as anxiety and insomnia. The sedative effect of this oil can also help to regulate sleep patterns, which is often disrupted in people with hyperthyroidism.

The reduction of anxiety and stress caused by the linalool and linalyl acetate in Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to manage symptoms of hyperthyroidism.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over low heat for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Add the fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the hot water and let it steep for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit in a dark place for 2-3 days to allow the essential oil to separate and rise to the top.

2. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains boiactive constituents like rosmarinic acid, luteolin, and apigenin.

These compounds have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties. The antioxidant properties of Melissa officinalis essential oils help neutralize free radicals that can damage thyroid tissue, while its anti-inflammatory properties reduce swelling and inflammation in the thyroid gland. The antiseptic properties of Melissa officinalis essential oils also help prevent infections and promote healing in the thyroid area.

By reducing inflammation, preventing infections, and promoting healing, Melissa officinalis essential oils may be beneficial in managing hyperthyroidism symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Melissa officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over low heat until it starts to simmer.
  3. Add the Melissa officinalis leaves to the saucepan and let it steep for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a glass container. Discard the solids.
  5. Use a still or a distillation equipment to separate the essential oil from the water. Collect the essential oil in a small glass container.

3. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis essential oils contains a compound called quinidine, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce thyroid gland inflammation associated with hyperthyroidism.

Quinidine also has a diuretic effect, which can help remove excess fluids and reduce swelling in the thyroid gland. The essential oil also contains alkaloids like quinine and cinchonine, which have antioxidant properties that help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress on the thyroid gland. Additionally, quinidine has a sedative effect, which can help regulate thyroid function and alleviate symptoms of hyperthyroidism such as anxiety and insomnia.

The combination of these properties in Cinchona officinalis essential oils makes it a potential natural remedy for hyperthyroidism.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cinchona officinalis bark. You can purchase it from a herbal store or online.
  2. Dry the Cinchona officinalis bark in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 2 hours.
  3. Grind 2 tablespoons of the dried Cinchona officinalis bark into a fine powder using a spice grinder.
  4. Combine the ground powder with 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil) in a clean glass jar.
  5. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day. Strain and bottle the essential oil for use.

4. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum essential oils contains silymarin, a complex mixture of bioactive compounds including flavonoids, lignans, and polyphenols.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the body. The essential oils also contain sesquiterpenes, which have been shown to have a regulatory effect on the thyroid gland, potentially helping to alleviate symptoms of hyperthyroidism. The antioxidant properties of silymarin may also help protect the thyroid gland from damage caused by free radicals, supporting its proper functioning.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, and regulating thyroid function, Silybum marianum essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of hyperthyroidism.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Silybum marianum seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil (such as jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. In a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl, combine the Silybum marianum seeds and carrier oil. Heat the mixture at 150-180°F (65-82°C) for 4-6 hours.
  3. After 4-6 hours, remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle.
  4. Discard the solids and reserve the liquid. Store the liquid in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks to allow the mixture to mature.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid again and discard the solids. Your Silybum marianum essential oil is now ready for use. Use 5-10 drops per application, diluted with a carrier oil if needed.

5. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, helping to reduce thyroid inflammation and oxidative stress. The essential oils also contain zingerone, which has been shown to inhibit the production of thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH), a key factor in hyperthyroidism. The warming and stimulating properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils may help to improve circulation and reduce swelling in the thyroid gland.

By reducing inflammation and regulating thyroid function, Zingiber officinale essential oils may provide relief from hyperthyroidism symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots. Clean and dry them.
  2. Place the ginger roots in a 2-liter glass jar. Add 1 cup of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  3. Seal the jar and leave it in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks. Shake the jar daily.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth into a separate container. Discard the ginger roots.
  5. Filter the oil again using a coffee filter. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

6. Avena sativa

Avena sativa essential oils contains a compound called avenanthramides, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties can help reduce the inflammation that can contribute to hyperthyroidism. Avena sativa essential oils also contains a compound called ferulic acid, which has been shown to have a regulating effect on the thyroid gland. The antioxidant properties in Avena sativa essential oils can help protect the thyroid gland from damage caused by free radicals, which can exacerbate hyperthyroidism.

Additionally, Avena sativa essential oils has been found to have a balancing effect on the body's stress response, which can help alleviate the symptoms of hyperthyroidism.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Avena sativa seeds and 4 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Heat the carrier oil in a double boiler to 150°F (65°C). Add 1 tablespoon of Avena sativa seeds to the oil.
  3. Steep the seeds in the oil for 2-3 weeks, shaking the mixture every day. This allows the oil to absorb the seed's properties.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the seeds.
  5. Store the Avena sativa essential oil in a dark glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops per day, mixed with a carrier oil, to help manage hyperthyroidism symptoms.

7. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valepotriates, isovaleramide, and valerenic acid as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have a sedative and anti-anxiety effect, which can help reduce stress and anxiety associated with hyperthyroidism. The antispasmodic properties of valerenic acid can also help alleviate muscle tension and cramps often experienced by people with hyperthyroidism. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of valeriana officinalis essential oils can help protect the body from oxidative stress caused by excessive thyroid hormone production.

By reducing anxiety and stress, and protecting the body from oxidative stress, valeriana officinalis essential oils can potentially help alleviate symptoms of hyperthyroidism.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Valeriana officinalis roots and 1 cup of water. Chop the roots into small pieces.
  2. Combine the chopped roots and water in a saucepan. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and let the mixture simmer for 2 hours. Strain the liquid using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter.
  4. Combine the strained liquid with 1 tablespoon of carrier oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil, in a dark glass bottle.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 days to allow the essential oil to separate from the liquid. Strain the mixture again and discard the liquid. Your Valeriana officinalis essential oil is now ready to use.

8. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata essential oils contains flavonoids and alkaloids as its active constituents.

These compounds, particularly apigenin and kaempferol, have anti-inflammatory properties that may help alleviate symptoms associated with hyperthyroidism. The flavonoids also exhibit antioxidant properties, which can help reduce oxidative stress and protect against thyroid damage. Furthermore, the alkaloids in Passiflora incarnata essential oils have a sedative effect, which can help regulate sleep patterns and reduce anxiety, common issues faced by individuals with hyperthyroidism.

The combination of these properties in Passiflora incarnata essential oils may contribute to its potential benefits in managing hyperthyroidism symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Passiflora incarnata flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat, then add the flowers and let it simmer for 2-3 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass container.
  4. Discard the solids and add 1 tablespoon of a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil to the liquid.
  5. Store the Passiflora incarnata essential oil in a dark glass bottle in a cool, dry place.

9. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum essential oils contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and xanthones.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immune-boosting properties that may help alleviate hyperthyroidism symptoms. Flavonoids, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the production of thyroid hormones, which can help regulate an overactive thyroid gland. The antioxidant properties of Hypericum perforatum essential oils can also help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation associated with hyperthyroidism.

The plant's active compounds may also help stabilize the thyroid gland and promote a healthy balance of thyroid hormones.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum leaves and flowers. Clean and dry them thoroughly.
  2. Place 1 cup of the dried plant material in a clean glass jar. Add 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or grapeseed oil).
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Hypericum perforatum essential oil in a dark glass bottle. Use 5-10 drops of the oil in a carrier oil or lotion for external use, or as directed by a healthcare professional for hyperthyroidism.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent hyperthyroidism from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help reduce the risk of developing hyperthyroidism, but it's not a guaranteed prevention.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, have been shown to have a positive effect on thyroid health by reducing inflammation and balancing hormone levels.

However, more research is needed to confirm these findings.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for hyperthyroidism every day?

While herbal teas may seem like a natural way to manage hyperthyroidism, consuming them daily can interact with medication or worsen symptoms.

Some teas, like licorice root, can even mimic the effects of the thyroid hormone, causing more problems.

It's essential to be cautious and consider individual tolerance when using herbal teas for this condition.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in hyperthyroidism?

Herbal teas can help manage hyperthyroidism symptoms, but results vary.

Some people notice a decrease in anxiety and heart rate within a few days to a week after starting to drink teas like ashwagandha and licorice root.

However, it may take a few weeks or even months to see noticeable improvements in thyroid function and overall well-being.

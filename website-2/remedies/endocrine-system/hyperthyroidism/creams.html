1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains bioactive constituents like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulatory properties that help regulate thyroid function. Curcumin, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) and reduce the conversion of T4 to T3, which is often elevated in hyperthyroidism. The anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds may also help reduce inflammation in the thyroid gland, which can contribute to hyperthyroidism symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and regulating thyroid hormone production, Curcuma longa creams may help alleviate symptoms of hyperthyroidism.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of Curcuma longa powder.
  2. Melt the coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool slightly. Add the Curcuma longa powder and mix well.
  4. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool completely. Once cooled, use a hand mixer to whip the mixture until it becomes light and fluffy.
  5. Apply the Curcuma longa cream to the affected area 2-3 times a day as needed, or as directed by a healthcare professional.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents like gingerol, shogaol, and zingerone, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, which can be associated with hyperthyroidism symptoms. Gingerol and shogaol also have a regulatory effect on thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) levels, which can become imbalanced in hyperthyroidism. Additionally, zingerone has a potent antioxidant effect that can protect the thyroid gland from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and regulating TSH levels, Zingiber officinale creams may help alleviate some symptoms of hyperthyroidism.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of ginger root powder (Zingiber officinale), and 2 tablespoons of shea butter.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add the ginger root powder and shea butter to the melted mixture. Stir well until combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into a silicone mold or a small tin container. Let it cool and harden at room temperature for 30 minutes.
  5. Once hardened, remove the cream from the mold and store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. Use as needed to help manage hyperthyroidism symptoms.

3. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum creams contains silymarin, a compound made of silibinin, silidianin, and silicristin.

Silymarin has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help protect the liver and thyroid gland. It also has a regulatory effect on thyroid hormone production, which can help to decrease excessive thyroid hormone levels in hyperthyroidism. Silicristin, a key component of silymarin, has been shown to inhibit the conversion of T4 to T3, a process that can contribute to hyperthyroidism.

By reducing T3 production, silymarin can help to alleviate symptoms of hyperthyroidism such as weight loss, rapid heartbeat, and anxiety.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Silybum marianum flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the dried flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Let it steep for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  3. After the steeping process, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of beeswax to the oil mixture and heat it in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each heating, until the beeswax is fully melted.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool. Store the cream in a cool, dark place. Use as needed for hyperthyroidism relief.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhizin's ability to reduce cortisol levels can help alleviate these symptoms and promote a more balanced thyroid function.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of glycerin, 2 tablespoons of glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, 1 tablespoon of beeswax, and 1 tablespoon of coconut oil.

  4. Combine the glycyrrhiza glabra paste and the melted mixture in a blender. Blend until smooth.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool. Store the creams in a cool, dry place.

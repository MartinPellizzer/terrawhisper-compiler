Understanding Hypothyroidism: Causes, Remedies, and Herbal Treatments

What are the causes of hypothyroidism?

There are several main causes of hypothyroidism, a condition where the thyroid gland doesn't produce enough thyroid hormones.

One of the most common causes is autoimmune disease, specifically Hashimoto's thyroiditis. This is when the immune system mistakenly attacks the thyroid gland, leading to inflammation and damage. Iodine deficiency is another major cause of hypothyroidism. Iodine is a crucial element for the production of thyroid hormones. If there's not enough iodine in the body, the thyroid gland can't produce the hormones it needs.

Thyroid surgery, or removal of part or all of the thyroid gland, can also lead to hypothyroidism. This is because the thyroid gland is responsible for producing thyroid hormones, and if it's damaged or removed, hormone production is severely impaired. Radiation therapy to the neck can also damage the thyroid gland and lead to hypothyroidism. This is because radiation can damage the cells in the thyroid gland, making it harder for the gland to produce hormones. Lastly, thyroiditis, or inflammation of the thyroid gland, can cause hypothyroidism.

This is often caused by an autoimmune response, but it can also be caused by other factors, such as a viral infection.

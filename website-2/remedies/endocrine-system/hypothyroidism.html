What Are The Main Causes Of Hypothyroidism?

Hypothyroidism is a condition where the thyroid gland does not produce enough thyroid hormones.

There are several causes of hypothyroidism. One of the main causes is Hashimoto's disease, an autoimmune disorder where the immune system mistakenly attacks and damages the thyroid gland. This makes it harder for the gland to produce thyroid hormones. Autoimmune hypothyroidism is the most common cause of hypothyroidism and can be triggered by genetic factors or other underlying health conditions.

Another main cause of hypothyroidism is iodine deficiency. Iodine is an essential nutrient that the thyroid gland needs to produce thyroid hormones. If the diet is lacking in iodine, the thyroid gland cannot produce enough thyroid hormones, leading to hypothyroidism. Iodine deficiency is common in areas where the soil lacks iodine, and it can also be caused by a diet that is not well-balanced. In some cases, hypothyroidism can be caused by other factors such as thyroid surgery, radiation therapy, or certain medications.

However, Hashimoto's disease and iodine deficiency are the two main causes of hypothyroidism.

Below are the main causes of hypothyroidism.

  • Hashimoto: Hashimoto's disease is a cause of hypothyroidism because the body's immune system mistakenly attacks and damages the thyroid gland, which then fails to produce enough thyroid hormones.
  • Autoimmune: Autoimmune is a cause of hypothyroidism because the body's immune system mistakenly attacks and destroys the thyroid gland, leading to a decrease in hormone production and resulting in a condition where the thyroid gland does not produce enough hormones.
  • Iodine Deficiency: Iodine deficiency is a cause of hypothyroidism because the thyroid gland needs iodine to produce the hormone thyroxine, and without enough iodine, the gland cannot make enough thyroxine, leading to a lack of thyroid hormones.
  • Radiation: Radiation can cause hypothyroidism by damaging the thyroid gland's ability to produce hormones, as high levels of radiation can kill or destroy thyroid cells, leading to a decrease in hormone production and a condition known as hypothyroidism.
  • Thyroid Surgery: Thyroid surgery can cause hypothyroidism because the surgeon may accidentally damage or remove the thyroid gland, which is the main gland responsible for producing thyroid hormones that regulate metabolism and other bodily functions.
  • Pituitary Problem: A pituitary problem is a cause of hypothyroidism because the pituitary gland, which controls the thyroid gland, fails to produce enough thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH), leading to reduced thyroid hormone production.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Hypothyroidism?

If you have hypothyroidism, it's essential to be cautious with certain herbs that might worsen your condition.

One of the herbs to avoid is Kava (Piper methysticum), which can slow down thyroid function and lead to decreased thyroid hormone production. Another herb to steer clear of is Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza uralensis, not Glycyrrhiza glabra, which is already excluded), which can cause a condition called Cushing's syndrome, leading to high blood pressure and thyroid problems.

You should also be careful with Dong Quai (Angelica sinensis), which can affect thyroid function and worsen hypothyroidism symptoms. Additionally, some people with hypothyroidism may need to avoid Blue Cohosh (Caulophyllum thalictroides) and Wild Yam (Dioscorea villosa), as they can contain compounds that may interact with thyroid medications or worsen the condition. Finally, Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa) may also be problematic for people with hypothyroidism, as it can affect thyroid hormone production and worsen symptoms.

It's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies, especially if you have a pre-existing medical condition like hypothyroidism.

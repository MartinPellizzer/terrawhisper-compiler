7 Herbal Teas For Adrenal Fatigue

Herbal teas can be a helpful way to manage adrenal fatigue, a condition where your adrenal glands, responsible for producing stress hormones, become exhausted.

This can leave you feeling tired, anxious, and struggling to cope with daily tasks. Herbal teas can offer relief by providing adaptogenic properties, which help your body adapt to stress and promote balance. For example, Withania somnifera, also known as ashwagandha, is an herb that has been shown to reduce cortisol levels and improve sleep quality, while also reducing anxiety and stress.

Panax ginseng is another herb that can help regulate cortisol levels and improve energy levels, making it easier to tackle daily tasks. Rhodiola rosea has also been found to reduce stress and anxiety, while also improving mental clarity and focus. These herbs work by stimulating the production of certain neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and dopamine, which can help improve your mood and reduce feelings of fatigue.

Drinking herbal teas made from these herbs can be a soothing way to unwind and recharge, providing a natural alternative to prescription medications or other treatments.

1. Withania somnifera

Withania somnifera teas contains bioactive constituents like withanolides, alkaloids, and glycosides.

These compounds have adaptogenic properties, helping the body respond to stress and promoting balance in the endocrine system. The withanolides in Withania somnifera teas have been shown to reduce cortisol levels and support the adrenal glands, which are often overworked in cases of adrenal fatigue. The antioxidant properties of glycosides in the tea help protect the adrenal glands from oxidative stress, further supporting their function.

By reducing cortisol levels and promoting adrenal gland health, Withania somnifera teas can help alleviate symptoms of adrenal fatigue, such as fatigue, brain fog, and insomnia.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of Withania somnifera root powder and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Add the Withania somnifera root powder to the boiling water and stir well.
  3. Reduce heat to low and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and add honey or lemon to taste, if needed.
  5. Drink the tea 1-2 times a day, ideally in the morning and afternoon.

2. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng teas contains bioactive constituents like ginsenosides, which are responsible for its health benefits.

These compounds help to reduce stress and fatigue by increasing the production of neurotransmitters like dopamine and serotonin, which regulate mood and energy levels. Ginsenosides also have adaptogenic properties, allowing them to help the body adapt to physical and mental stress, thereby reducing the negative effects of adrenal fatigue. Additionally, Panax ginseng teas have antioxidant properties, which help to protect the adrenal glands from oxidative damage and promote their function.

By promoting adrenal health and reducing oxidative stress, Panax ginseng teas can help alleviate symptoms of adrenal fatigue.

  1. Gather 2 cups of water and 1 teaspoon of dried Panax ginseng root.
  2. Bring the water to a boil in a pot, then reduce heat to low.
  3. Add the dried Panax ginseng root to the pot and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the root. Add honey to taste, if needed.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of the tea per day, ideally before meals or as a bedtime tea.

3. Rhodiola rosea

Rhodiola rosea teas contains bioactive constituents like rosavin, roseroot extract, and salidroside that help alleviate adrenal fatigue.

These compounds have adaptogenic properties, allowing the body to adapt to stress and balance the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis. Rosavin and roseroot extract help to normalize cortisol levels, while salidroside supports the health of adrenal glands. By regulating cortisol production and supporting adrenal function, Rhodiola rosea teas can help to reduce fatigue, improve mental clarity, and increase energy levels.

The adaptogenic properties of Rhodiola rosea teas also help to improve sleep quality and reduce stress, further contributing to its benefits for adrenal fatigue.

  1. Get 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried Rhodiola rosea root to a tea infuser.
  3. Steep the infuser in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the infuser.
  5. Drink the tea and repeat as needed, ideally 2-3 times a day.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains licorice root extract, which is rich in glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin that helps alleviate adrenal fatigue.

Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce cortisol levels, allowing the adrenal glands to recover and function properly. The root extract also contains flavonoids, such as liquiritin and isoliquiritin, which have antioxidant properties that protect the adrenal glands from oxidative stress. Additionally, glycyrrhizin helps regulate the body's electrolyte balance, particularly potassium, which is often imbalanced in individuals with adrenal fatigue.

By reducing cortisol levels and promoting electrolyte balance, Glycyrrhiza glabra teas helps support the adrenal glands and aid in recovery from fatigue.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root, a cup of boiling water, and a tea infuser or strainer.
  2. Steep the dried root in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid using a tea infuser or strainer into a cup.
  4. Add honey to taste, if desired, and stir well.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the tea 2-3 times a day to support adrenal function and relieve fatigue.

5. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis teas contains a rich source of valerenic acid, valepotriates, and isovaleric acid, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

These bioactive constituents have a sedative effect on the nervous system, helping to reduce stress and anxiety that often accompany adrenal fatigue. The valepotriates in valerian root tea have a calming effect on the body, promoting relaxation and reducing cortisol levels, which are often elevated in individuals with adrenal fatigue. The isovaleric acid in valerian root tea has a sedative effect on the nervous system, promoting a restful sleep and reducing fatigue.

Regular consumption of valerian root tea can help alleviate symptoms of adrenal fatigue by promoting relaxation, reducing stress, and regulating cortisol levels.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 teaspoon of dried Valeriana officinalis root.
  2. Steep the root in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey or sweetener (optional) to taste.
  5. Drink the tea 1-2 times a day, ideally before bedtime, to help with adrenal fatigue.

6. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains a group of bioactive constituents called flavonoids and terpenoids.

These compounds, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce oxidative stress in the body, a common issue associated with adrenal fatigue. The leaves of the Ginkgo biloba plant also contain bilobalide, which has been shown to improve blood flow and increase oxygen delivery to cells, supporting the adrenal glands' function. Additionally, Ginkgo biloba's antioxidant properties can help protect the adrenal glands from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing oxidative stress and improving blood flow, Ginkgo biloba teas may help alleviate symptoms of adrenal fatigue.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Place the Ginkgo biloba leaves in a tea infuser or a small muslin bag.
  3. Steep the leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the leaves.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, ideally before meals.

7. Schisandra chinensis

Schisandra chinensis teas contains bioactive constituents like schisandrins, schisantherin, and lignans that help alleviate adrenal fatigue.

These compounds have adaptogenic properties, which enable the body to cope with stress and regulate cortisol levels. Schisandrins, in particular, have been shown to stimulate the adrenal glands, promoting the production of cortisol and other vital hormones. The antioxidants and lignans present in the tea also help protect the adrenal glands from oxidative damage and inflammation, supporting their optimal functioning.

By supporting adrenal health, Schisandra chinensis teas can help alleviate symptoms associated with adrenal fatigue, such as fatigue, insomnia, and weight changes.

  1. Get 1 tablespoon of dried Schisandra chinensis root. You can buy it from a health store or online.
  2. Measure 1 cup of boiling water. You can use a kettle to boil the water.
  3. Place the 1 tablespoon of dried Schisandra chinensis root in a cup or teapot.
  4. Pour the boiling water over the Schisandra chinensis root. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it hot or cold. You can add honey or lemon to taste.

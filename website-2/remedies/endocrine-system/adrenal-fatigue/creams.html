When taken as a tea, these herbs can be absorbed into the bloodstream and provide the body with the nutrients it needs to recover from adrenal fatigue.

1. Withania somnifera

Withania somnifera creams contains bioactive constituents such as withanolides, alkaloids, and glycosides.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce cortisol levels and alleviate symptoms of adrenal fatigue. The withanolides in Withania somnifera creams have been shown to inhibit the enzyme 11-beta-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase, which converts cortisol into its more potent form. By inhibiting this enzyme, Withania somnifera creams may help reduce cortisol levels and promote a sense of calm and well-being.

The antioxidants in Withania somnifera creams can also help protect the adrenal glands from oxidative stress, which can contribute to adrenal fatigue.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Withania somnifera root powder, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of essential oil (optional).
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add Withania somnifera root powder and stir well. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes to allow the powder to infuse into the oil.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the solids. Add vitamin E oil and essential oil (if using) and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a tin or a jar and let it cool and solidify. Once set, your Withania somnifera cream is ready to use.

2. Schisandra chinensis

Schisandra chinensis creams contains schisandrins, schisandrin B, and schisantherin A, which have been found to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the body, which can contribute to adrenal fatigue. Schisandra chinensis also contains schisandrins, which have been shown to have adaptogenic properties, helping the body adapt to stress and promoting balance in the adrenal system. The antioxidants in Schisandra chinensis creams can also help reduce cortisol levels and promote the production of other essential hormones.

By reducing oxidative stress and promoting balance in the body, Schisandra chinensis creams may help alleviate symptoms of adrenal fatigue.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Schisandra chinensis berries, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil, 1 teaspoon of essential oil (optional).
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water. Stir until the beeswax is fully melted.
  3. Add the shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Add the dried Schisandra chinensis berries to the mixture and steep for 10-15 minutes, allowing the berries to infuse their properties into the cream.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the solids. Add the vitamin E oil and essential oil (if using) and stir to combine. Pour the mixture into a glass jar and let it cool before use.

3. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng creams contains bioactive constituents like ginsenosides, particularly ginsenoside Rg1, ginsenoside Rb1, and ginsenoside Re.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce oxidative stress, a common issue in adrenal fatigue. Ginsenosides also stimulate the production of ATP, the energy currency of cells, which is essential for adrenal function. Additionally, Panax ginseng creams contain eleutherosides, which have been shown to increase the production of cortisol and adrenaline, helping to alleviate symptoms of adrenal fatigue.

By addressing oxidative stress and stimulating energy production, Panax ginseng creams may provide relief for individuals experiencing adrenal fatigue.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Panax ginseng root extract, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, 2 tablespoons of aloe vera gel.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove melted mixture from heat and let cool slightly. Add Panax ginseng root extract, vitamin E oil, and aloe vera gel. Stir well.
  4. Pour mixture into a small tin or container. Let it cool and harden at room temperature.
  5. Once hardened, your Panax ginseng cream is ready to use. Apply a small amount to affected areas as needed.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent adrenal fatigue from forming?

Drinking herbal tea can help support adrenal function, which may help prevent adrenal fatigue from forming.

Herbal teas like ashwagandha, ginseng, and licorice root contain adaptogenic properties that can help regulate stress hormones.

These teas can promote relaxation and balance the body's response to stress, potentially reducing the risk of adrenal fatigue.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for adrenal fatigue every day?

Consuming herbal teas daily for adrenal fatigue can be safe if done in moderation.

Some herbal teas like ashwagandha, rhodiola, and ginseng may help reduce stress and fatigue. However, overconsumption can cause interactions with other medications or worsen conditions like high blood pressure.

Moderation is key when using herbal teas as a supplement.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in adrenal fatigue?

Herbal teas can help with adrenal fatigue, but it's hard to say exactly how long they'll take to work.

Some people notice a difference within a few weeks, while others may need to drink them for a few months to feel the effects.

It's different for everyone, and it depends on the teas and the person's body.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for adrenal fatigue?

For adrenal fatigue, it's best to drink herbal tea in the morning or early afternoon when your energy is usually lower.

This helps boost your energy levels and calm your nervous system.

Drinking tea too close to bedtime can interfere with your sleep, so it's better to have it a few hours before bed.

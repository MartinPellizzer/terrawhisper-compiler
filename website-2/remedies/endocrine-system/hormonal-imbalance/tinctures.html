1. Vitex agnus-castus

Vitex agnus-castus tinctures contains flavonoids, iridoids, and terpenoids, including aucubin, acacetin, and ursolic acid.

These compounds have a regulating effect on hormones, particularly estrogen, which can become imbalanced during menopause or PMS. The flavonoids and iridoids in Vitex agnus-castus tinctures help to stabilize hormone production and reduce symptoms of hormonal imbalance, such as mood swings and irregular periods. The terpenoids, like ursolic acid, have anti-inflammatory properties that can also contribute to hormone regulation.

By addressing hormonal imbalance, Vitex agnus-castus tinctures can help to alleviate symptoms and promote a more stable hormonal environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Vitex agnus-castus flowers and 2 cups of 80% ethanol (vodka or brandy work too).
  2. Combine the dried flowers and ethanol in a clean glass jar, making sure the flowers are covered.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After the steeping time, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the tincture to smaller glass bottles, using 1-2 tablespoons as a typical dose. Store in a cool, dark place.

2. Cimicifuga racemosa

Cimicifuga racemona tinctures contains a compound called actein, which is a saponin.

Actein helps to regulate hormonal balance by inhibiting the production of estrogen, thereby reducing its negative effects on the body. It also contains sesquiterpenes like cimicifugoside and para-caffeic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, helping to soothe the body's hormonal responses. The tannins in Cimicifuga racemosa also help to reduce inflammation and calm the nervous system, leading to a more balanced hormonal state.

By reducing inflammation and regulating hormonal production, Cimicifuga racemosa tinctures can help alleviate symptoms associated with hormonal imbalance.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cimicifuga racemosa roots, dried and crushed.
  2. Add 2 cups of 80% ethanol to a clean glass jar containing the crushed roots. The roots should be covered by the ethanol.
  3. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid tincture to a dark glass bottle and label it. Store it in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, as needed.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia tinctures contains bioactive constituents like linalool and linalyl acetate, which have a calming effect on the body and help regulate hormonal balance.

These constituents have a sedative and anti-anxiety effect, which can help reduce stress and promote relaxation, leading to a decrease in cortisol levels. The antispasmodic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate can also help alleviate menstrual cramps and other symptoms associated with hormonal imbalances. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of these constituents can help protect the body from free radical damage and promote hormonal balance.

By reducing stress and promoting relaxation, Lavandula angustifolia tinctures can help regulate the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, leading to a more balanced hormonal system.

  1. Gather 250ml of 80% ethanol and 500g of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  2. Combine the dried flowers and ethanol in a clean glass jar, making sure the flowers are completely covered.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the tincture to dark glass bottles with dropper lids, labeling and storing in a cool, dark place.

4. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum tinctures contains flavonoids, specifically hyperoside and rutin, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help to regulate hormonal balance by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation in the body. Hypericum perforatum also contains phloroglucinol derivatives, such as hyperforin and adhyperforin, which have a modulating effect on the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, a key regulator of the body's hormonal response. By influencing the HPA axis, these compounds can help to stabilize cortisol levels and improve hormonal balance.

The flavonoids and phloroglucinol derivatives in Hypericum perforatum tinctures work together to create a balancing effect on the body's hormonal system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Hypericum perforatum flowers, 2 cups of vodka, and a clean glass jar.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains bioactive constituents like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Glycyrrhizin helps to regulate the balance of hormones in the body by inhibiting the enzyme 11-beta-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase, which converts cortisol into its active form. The flavonoids present in Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures also have a regulatory effect on the body's hormonal response, helping to balance the production and utilization of hormones.

By regulating cortisol levels and influencing hormone production, Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures can help to alleviate symptoms of hormonal imbalance.

  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 20-30 drops under the tongue 2-3 times a day as needed.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, including glycyrrhetinic acid, which is known to mimic the effects of cortisol in the body.

Cortisol is a hormone that helps regulate metabolism, including the metabolism of thyroid hormones. Glycyrrhetinic acid also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the thyroid gland that can contribute to hypothyroidism. Additionally, glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains flavonoids, such as luteolin and quercetin, which have antioxidant properties that can help protect the thyroid gland from oxidative stress.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils may help support thyroid function and alleviate symptoms of hypothyroidism.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots. Clean and chop them into small pieces.
  2. Place 2 cups of water in a saucepan and add the chopped roots. Bring to a boil then reduce heat.
  3. Simmer for 2 hours. Strain the mixture into a bowl using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Combine the strained liquid with 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) in a separate bowl.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 24 hours. Strain it again and discard the roots. Your essential oil is now ready for use.

2. Cassia auriculata

Cassia auriculata essential oils contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the thyroid gland. The flavonoids in Cassia auriculata, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to increase the production of thyroid hormones by stimulating the thyroid gland's function. The phenolic acids, such as ferulic acid, can also help protect the thyroid gland from oxidative stress and damage.

By reducing inflammation and promoting thyroid hormone production, Cassia auriculata essential oils may be beneficial in supporting the treatment of hypothyroidism.

  1. Gather 250 grams of Cassia auriculata leaves and flowers. Clean and dry them.
  2. Use a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil (1 cup) and mix it with the Cassia auriculata leaves and flowers in a glass container.
  3. Steep the mixture in a warm place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the container daily. This allows the oil to extract from the plant.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the extracted Cassia auriculata essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight lid. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a carrier oil for massage or topical application for hypothyroidism relief.

3. Withania somnifera

Withania somnifera essential oils contains a range of bioactive constituents, including withanolides, alkaloids, and saponins.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation associated with hypothyroidism. The withanolides in Withania somnifera essential oils have been shown to increase thyroid hormone production and improve thyroid function in animal studies. Additionally, the alkaloids present in this essential oil have been found to have a regulating effect on the thyroid gland, helping to normalize thyroid hormone levels.

The antioxidant properties of Withania somnifera essential oils may also help to protect the thyroid gland from damage caused by free radicals.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Withania somnifera roots and 1 cup of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Grind the dried Withania somnifera roots into fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Mix 2 tablespoons of the ground roots with 8 ounces of carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily to help the oil extract.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.

  3. Place the dried Panax ginseng root in a cheesecloth or a coffee filter.

  5. Strain the oil and discard the root. Store the Panax ginseng essential oil in a cool, dark place.

5. Astragalus membranaceus

6. Rosmarinus officinalis

8. Curcuma longa

By reducing inflammation and improving thyroid hormone production, curcuma longa essential oils may be beneficial for individuals with hypothyroidism.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent hypothyroidism from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent hypothyroidism in some cases.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for hypothyroidism every day?

Consuming herbal teas for hypothyroidism daily can be safe, but it's essential to check the ingredients.

Moderate amounts are key, as excessive consumption can lead to side effects.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in hypothyroidism?

Herbal teas can help with hypothyroidism symptoms, but it takes time to see results.

Some people notice improvements in a few weeks, while others may not see a difference for 2-3 months.

It's essential to drink herbal teas consistently and combine them with a balanced diet to support thyroid health.

1. Astragalus membranaceus

Astragalus membranaceus tinctures contains saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which are active compounds that help with hypothyroidism.

Saponins stimulate the immune system, while flavonoids have antioxidant properties that protect the thyroid gland from damage. The polysaccharides in Astragalus membranaceus tinctures have anti-inflammatory effects, which can reduce inflammation in the thyroid gland. This can help improve thyroid function and increase the production of thyroid hormones.

Regular use of Astragalus membranaceus tinctures may also help boost energy levels and reduce fatigue associated with hypothyroidism.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Astragalus membranaceus roots and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Fill a saucepan with 2 cups of water, and bring it to a boil. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Add the Astragalus membranaceus roots to the saucepan and let it simmer for another 10-15 minutes. Turn off the heat and let it steep for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the glass jar with the vodka. Discard the solids.
  5. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. After 2-3 weeks, strain and bottle the tincture.

2. Schisandra chinensis

Schisandra chinensis tinctures contains a combination of bioactive constituents, including schisandrins, lignans, and polysaccharides, that work together to support thyroid health.

Schisandrins have been shown to stimulate the production of thyroid hormones, while lignans help to regulate the conversion of T4 to T3, the active form of thyroid hormone. The polysaccharides in Schisandra chinensis have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce inflammation in the thyroid gland and promote healing. By regulating thyroid hormone production and reducing inflammation, Schisandra chinensis tinctures can help to alleviate symptoms of hypothyroidism, such as fatigue and weight gain.

This herb has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to support thyroid function and overall health.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Schisandra chinensis berries and 1 cup of 80% vodka.
  2. Combine the dried berries and vodka in a clean glass jar, making sure the berries are completely covered.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place and shake it every day for 2-3 weeks.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the tincture to small dark glass bottles and store them in the fridge, taking 15-30 drops 2-3 times a day as needed.

3. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng tinctures contains ginsenosides, a group of bioactive constituents that have been shown to have a positive effect on the thyroid gland.

These compounds, particularly ginsenoside Rg1 and Rb1, have been found to increase the production of thyroid hormones by stimulating the thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) and promoting the conversion of T4 to T3. The adaptogenic properties of ginsenosides also help to reduce stress, which can exacerbate hypothyroidism. Additionally, Panax ginseng has been shown to improve blood flow to the thyroid gland, allowing it to function more efficiently.

By combining these properties, Panax ginseng tinctures may help to alleviate symptoms of hypothyroidism and support thyroid health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Panax ginseng root, 2 cups of 80% vodka or grain alcohol, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the dried Panax ginseng root with 2 cups of vodka in the glass jar, making sure the root is fully covered.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-6 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After the maceration period, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass container, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the tincture to smaller bottles and store them in the fridge. Use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

4. Withania somnifera

Withania somnifera tinctures contains bioactive constituents like withanolides, withaferin A, and somniferin.

These compounds have adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce stress and inflammation, which can contribute to hypothyroidism. Withanolides also have antioxidant properties, which help protect the thyroid gland from damage caused by free radicals. The adaptogenic properties of Withania somnifera tinctures help regulate the body's response to stress, which is often linked to thyroid function.

By reducing stress and inflammation, and protecting the thyroid gland, Withania somnifera tinctures may help alleviate symptoms of hypothyroidism.

  1. Gather 250 grams of dried Withania somnifera root, 1 cup of 80-proof vodka or glycerin, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the dried root and vodka or glycerin in the glass jar. Stir well to coat the root evenly.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day to help the tincture extract.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the tincture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Label the jar with the date, ingredients, and dosage instructions (start with 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day). Store the tincture in the fridge to prolong its shelf life.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin that has anti-inflammatory properties.

Glycyrrhizin inhibits the enzyme 11-beta-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase, which helps to increase cortisol levels. Increased cortisol levels help to increase thyroid hormone production by stimulating the production of thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH). The saponins in Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures also have antioxidant properties, which help to protect the thyroid gland from oxidative damage.

Glycyrrhizin and other bioactive constituents in Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures may help to support thyroid function and alleviate hypothyroidism symptoms by regulating cortisol and thyroid hormone levels.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh or dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and clean them.
  2. Crush the roots into small pieces using a mortar and pestle.
  3. Combine the crushed roots with 1 cup of 80% ethanol in a clean glass jar.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

6. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains gingerols and shogaols, which are active constituents that help increase blood circulation and reduce inflammation.

These properties can improve the delivery of thyroid hormones to the body's cells, which is essential for managing hypothyroidism. The anti-inflammatory compounds in ginger tinctures can also reduce swelling and pain in the thyroid gland, making it easier for the gland to produce hormones. Additionally, the warming properties of ginger can stimulate digestion and improve the absorption of nutrients, including those necessary for thyroid function.

By enhancing thyroid hormone production and improving hormone delivery, Zingiber officinale tinctures may be a useful complementary therapy for managing hypothyroidism symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka.
  2. Chop the ginger roots into small pieces and place them in a clean glass jar.
  3. Pour the vodka over the ginger, making sure the roots are completely covered. Use a clean utensil to gently press the ginger pieces.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every 2-3 days. This will help the tincture to extract the active compounds from the ginger.
  5. After the extraction period, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

7. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa tinctures contains bioactive compounds like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the thyroid gland. Curcumin also has the ability to modulate the immune system and improve the function of thyroid hormones, promoting a healthier thyroid gland. The tincture's antioxidant properties also help to protect the thyroid gland from damage caused by free radicals, supporting its proper functioning.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Curcuma longa tinctures may help to alleviate symptoms of hypothyroidism and promote a more balanced thyroid function.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Curcuma longa roots, and 2 cups of vodka (at least 80 proof).
  2. Cut the Curcuma longa roots into small pieces and place them in a clean glass jar.
  3. Pour the vodka over the Curcuma longa roots, making sure they are completely covered.

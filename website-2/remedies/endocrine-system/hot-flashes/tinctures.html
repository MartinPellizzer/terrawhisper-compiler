1. Paeonia lactiflora

Paeonia lactiflora tinctures contains isoflavones, which are plant-based compounds that mimic the effects of estrogen in the body.

Isoflavones in Paeonia lactiflora tinctures, such as peonidin, kaempferol, and quercetin, have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that may help alleviate hot flashes. These properties can help regulate hormonal changes and reduce symptoms associated with menopause. The flavonoids in Paeonia lactiflora tinctures, including kaempferol and quercetin, have been shown to have a positive effect on the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis, which can help stabilize hormone levels.

By regulating hormonal changes and reducing inflammation, Paeonia lactiflora tinctures may provide relief from hot flashes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Paeonia lactiflora roots and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass container.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of glycerin to the container with the vodka.
  3. Pack the Paeonia lactiflora roots into a clean glass jar, leaving 1 inch of space at the top.
  4. Pour the vodka-glycerin mixture over the roots, making sure they are completely covered.
  5. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day, then strain and discard the roots.

2. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis tinctures contains valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, which are the main biologically active constituents.

These compounds have a sedative effect on the nervous system, helping to calm hot flashes caused by hormonal imbalances. Valerenic acid also blocks the activity of the neurotransmitter GABA, which can contribute to hot flashes. The antispasmodic properties of valerenic acid and isovaleric acid can also help to relieve the physical symptoms of hot flashes, such as sweating and palpitations.

By reducing stress and anxiety, and alleviating physical symptoms, Valeriana officinalis tinctures can provide relief from hot flashes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Valeriana officinalis roots and flowers.
  2. Combine the roots and flowers in a clean glass jar and cover them with 2 cups of 80% vodka or 60% glycerin.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Label the tincture and store it in a cool, dark place. Take 10-20 drops, 2-3 times a day as needed for hot flashes.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Cimicifuga racemosa tinctures can provide relief from hot flashes.

Hormonal Imbalance: Understanding Causes, Benefits of Herbal Preparations, and Medicinal Herbs

Hormonal imbalance is a condition where the levels of hormones in the body become too high or too low, affecting the way the body functions.

It's essential to deal with hormonal imbalance as it can lead to various health issues, such as mood swings, weight gain, insomnia, and fertility problems. If left untreated, hormonal imbalance can increase the risk of developing serious health conditions like osteoporosis, thyroid disorders, and even certain types of cancer. Herbal preparations are a natural way to restore hormonal balance. For instance, 'Angelica sinensis', also known as dong quai, is used to treat menstrual cramps and irregular periods by stimulating the production of estrogen and progesterone. This herb can be consumed in various forms, such as capsules or decoctions. A decoction, which is a liquid extract made by boiling herbs in water, can be a more effective way to absorb the active compounds of angelica sinensis, as it allows the body to absorb the nutrients more easily.

'Cimicifuga racemosa', or black cohosh, is another herb used to treat hot flashes and night sweats in menopausal women. It works by reducing the production of estrogen and progesterone, thereby alleviating symptoms of hormonal imbalance. Cimicifuga racemosa is often used in the form of tincture, which is a concentrated liquid extract of the herb that can be taken sublingually or added to water or tea. 'Vitex agnus-castus', or chasteberry, is used to regulate menstrual cycles and alleviate symptoms of PMS. It works by stimulating the production of progesterone, which helps to balance the levels of estrogen in the body. Vitex agnus-castus can be consumed in various forms, such as capsules or tincture, and is often used in combination with other herbs to achieve optimal results. Herbal preparations can be an effective way to manage hormonal imbalance, as they are often less likely to cause side effects compared to synthetic hormone replacement therapy.

However, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before taking any herbal remedy, as they can interact with other medications or worsen certain health conditions.

What Are The Main Causes Of Hormonal Imbalance?

Hormonal imbalance occurs when the body produces too much or too little of certain hormones.

This can happen due to various reasons. One of the main causes is Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS), a condition where the ovaries have small cysts on them, affecting hormone production. This leads to an imbalance of estrogen and progesterone, causing irregular periods, weight gain, and acne. Another cause is Hypothyroidism, a condition where the thyroid gland doesn't produce enough thyroid hormones. These hormones regulate metabolism, and their deficiency can lead to fatigue, weight gain, and dry skin.

When the thyroid gland produces too little thyroid hormones, the body's metabolism slows down, causing hormonal imbalance. On the other hand, Hyperthyroidism is a condition where the thyroid gland produces too many thyroid hormones. This leads to an increased metabolism, causing weight loss, anxiety, and an irregular heartbeat. When the thyroid gland produces too many hormones, it can disrupt the body's hormonal balance, affecting other hormone-producing glands like the ovaries and adrenal glands. Additionally, hormonal imbalance can be caused by genetics, stress, certain medications, and an unhealthy diet.

These factors can affect hormone production, leading to an imbalance in the body's hormonal system.

Below are the main causes of hormonal imbalance.

  • Polycystic Ovary: Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a cause of hormonal imbalance because it affects the ovaries, leading to an imbalance of estrogen and progesterone hormones, which can disrupt the body's normal menstrual cycle and lead to a variety of symptoms.
  • Hypothyroidism: Hypothyroidism is a cause of hormonal imbalance because the thyroid gland, which produces hormones that regulate metabolism and growth, is not working properly, leading to a decrease in the production of these essential hormones.
  • Hyperthyroidism: Hyperthyroidism occurs when the thyroid gland produces too much thyroid hormone, causing an imbalance in the body's hormonal levels and disrupting the normal functioning of various bodily systems.
  • Pituitary Tumors: A pituitary tumor is a cause of hormonal imbalance because it can produce too much or too little of certain hormones, disrupting the body's normal balance of hormones and affecting other hormone-producing glands, such as the thyroid, adrenal, and reproductive glands.
  • Adrenal Insufficiency: Adrenal Insufficiency occurs when the adrenal glands do not produce enough hormones, such as cortisol, leading to a hormonal imbalance that affects the body's ability to regulate various functions and respond to stress.
  • Adrenal Hyperplasia: Adrenal Hyperplasia is a cause of hormonal imbalance because the adrenal glands, which produce important hormones, become enlarged and unable to function properly, leading to an imbalance of hormones such as cortisol and aldosterone in the body.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Hormonal Imbalance?

If you have hormonal imbalance, it's best to avoid some herbs that can affect hormone levels or interact with medications.

One such herb is Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra), which can raise blood pressure and worsen hormonal imbalances. Another herb to avoid is Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens), as it can mimic estrogen in the body and worsen hormonal imbalances, especially in women with estrogen dominance.

St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) is also not recommended, as it can interact with birth control pills and hormone replacement therapy, and affect hormone levels. Additionally, Wild Yam (Dioscorea villosa) may be contraindicated, as its estrogenic properties can worsen hormonal imbalances, especially in women with estrogen dominance or polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS).

These herbs may interact with medications or worsen hormonal imbalances, so it's best to consult with a healthcare professional before using them.

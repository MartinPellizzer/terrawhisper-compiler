What Are The Main Causes Of High Cholesterol?

There are several main causes of high cholesterol.

One cause is a genetic disorder, where a person is born with a condition that affects their body's ability to remove excess cholesterol. This condition is rare and is usually diagnosed at birth or in early childhood. Another cause is obesity, which is when a person has too much body fat.

This extra fat can cause the liver to produce more cholesterol, leading to high levels in the blood. A diet high in saturated fats is also a major cause of high cholesterol. Saturated fats are found in foods like butter, lard, and some types of meat and dairy products.

When we eat too much of these foods, our liver has to work harder to remove the excess cholesterol, leading to high levels in the blood.

Below are the main causes of high cholesterol.

  • Genetic Disorder: Genetic disorders can cause high cholesterol because some people are born with a faulty gene that affects how their body breaks down and removes excess cholesterol from the blood.
  • Obesity: When a person is obese, their body stores excess fat, which can lead to an increase in triglycerides (a type of fat) and low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol in the blood, contributing to high cholesterol levels.
  • Diet High Saturated Fats: Consuming high amounts of saturated fats in a diet can raise blood cholesterol levels because these fats are broken down into cholesterol particles in the body, which then accumulate in the bloodstream, potentially leading to high cholesterol.
  • Diet High Cholesterol Foods: Eating foods with high levels of saturated and trans fats, such as butter, lard, and processed snacks, can cause high cholesterol by blocking the body's ability to remove low-density lipoprotein (LDL) or "bad" cholesterol from the bloodstream.
  • Smoking: Smoking causes high cholesterol because the chemicals in cigarette smoke damage the blood vessels and make them less efficient at removing excess cholesterol, leading to its buildup in the bloodstream.
  • Physical Inactivity: When we are physically inactive, our bodies don't get enough exercise to move the bad fats from our bloodstream, which can then build up in our arteries and cause high cholesterol levels.
  • Age: As people get older, their bodies naturally produce less of a good cholesterol called HDL and more of a bad cholesterol called LDL, which can lead to high cholesterol levels.

The Holistic Approach to Hyperthyroidism: Causes, Herbal Remedies, and Natural Treatments

What are the causes of hyperthyroidism?

Hyperthyroidism occurs when your thyroid gland produces too much thyroid hormone.

This can happen due to various reasons. One of the most common causes is Graves' disease, an autoimmune disorder where your immune system mistakenly attacks your thyroid gland, telling it to produce more hormone. Another cause is a thyroid nodule, a growth on your thyroid gland that can produce excess hormone. Sometimes, these nodules are harmless, but in some cases, they can be cancerous.

Thyroiditis is inflammation of your thyroid gland, which can also lead to an overproduction of thyroid hormone. This inflammation can be caused by a viral infection, an autoimmune disorder, or a radiation exposure. A pituitary adenoma is a rare cause of hyperthyroidism, where a non-cancerous growth on your pituitary gland tells your thyroid gland to produce more hormone. Lastly, thyroid cancer can also cause hyperthyroidism, especially if the cancerous cells are producing thyroid hormone.

However, this is a rare occurrence.

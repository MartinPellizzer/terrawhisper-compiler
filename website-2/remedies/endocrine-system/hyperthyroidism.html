What Are The Main Causes Of Hyperthyroidism?

Hyperthyroidism occurs when the thyroid gland produces too much thyroid hormone.

There are several main causes of this condition. One of the main causes is Graves' disease, an autoimmune disorder where the immune system mistakenly attacks the thyroid gland, causing it to produce excess thyroid hormone. This happens because the immune system mistakenly identifies the thyroid gland as a foreign body and produces antibodies that stimulate the gland to produce more hormone. Another cause of hyperthyroidism is toxic nodules, which are abnormal growths on the thyroid gland that produce excess thyroid hormone.

These nodules can become overactive and start producing too much hormone, leading to hyperthyroidism. This can occur when the thyroid gland is exposed to high levels of iodine, which can cause the growth of these nodules. TSH receptor antibodies are another cause of hyperthyroidism. These antibodies can mimic the action of thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH), which is a hormone produced by the pituitary gland that stimulates the thyroid gland to produce thyroid hormone. When TSH receptor antibodies are present, they can stimulate the thyroid gland to produce excess hormone, leading to hyperthyroidism.

This can occur in people with conditions such as Hashimoto's thyroiditis, an autoimmune disorder that can cause the production of TSH receptor antibodies.

Below are the main causes of hyperthyroidism.

  • Graves: Graves' disease is a cause of hyperthyroidism because the body's immune system mistakenly attacks the thyroid gland, causing it to produce too much thyroid hormone.
  • Toxic nodules: Toxic nodules cause hyperthyroidism because they are abnormal growths on the thyroid gland that produce too much thyroid hormone, leading to an overactive thyroid.
  • TSH receptor antibodies: TSH receptor antibodies cause hyperthyroidism by stimulating the thyroid gland to produce excess thyroid hormones, even when the thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) is low, leading to an overproduction of thyroid hormones and subsequent hyperthyroidism symptoms.
  • Subacute de Quervain thyroiditis: Subacute de Quervain thyroiditis is a cause of hyperthyroidism because the inflammation in the thyroid gland causes the release of stored thyroid hormones into the bloodstream, leading to higher-than-normal levels of these hormones.
  • Silent thyroiditis: Silent thyroiditis is a cause of hyperthyroidism because the body's immune system mistakenly attacks the thyroid gland, releasing stored thyroid hormones into the blood, which can cause an overactive thyroid condition.
  • Pituitary hyperplasia: Pituitary hyperplasia is a cause of hyperthyroidism because when the pituitary gland grows too much, it produces too much thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH), which in turn stimulates the thyroid gland to produce too much thyroid hormone.
  • Thyroiditis: Thyroiditis is a cause of hyperthyroidism because when the thyroid gland becomes inflamed, it can release too much thyroid hormone into the bloodstream, leading to an overactive thyroid.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Hyperthyroidism?

If you have hyperthyroidism, it's essential to avoid certain herbs that can worsen your condition.

Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) is not listed, but we can say that it is to be avoided, and so is licorice root's close relative, sweet root. Licorice root can increase aldosterone levels, which can worsen hyperthyroidism. Other herbs to avoid include: Fenugreek (Trigonella foenum-graecum) because it can lower blood sugar levels and worsen hypoglycemia in people with hyperthyroidism.

Ginkgo biloba because it can increase thyroid hormone levels and worsen hyperthyroidism. Mugwort (Artemisia vulgaris) and dandelion root (Taraxacum officinale) because they can stimulate the thyroid gland and worsen hyperthyroidism. Also avoid goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) because it can interact with medications used to treat hyperthyroidism.

And avoid passionflower (Passiflora incarnata) because it can cause drowsiness, which can worsen hyperthyroidism symptoms like anxiety and tremors.

The Holistic Approach to Hyperthyroidism: Causes, Herbal Remedies, and Natural Treatments

Hyperthyroidism is a condition where your thyroid gland produces too much thyroid hormone.

This can cause your metabolism to speed up, leading to weight loss, anxiety, and a racing heart. You may feel jittery, have trouble sleeping, and experience changes in your menstrual cycle or libido. If left untreated, hyperthyroidism can lead to serious complications like heart problems and osteoporosis. The causes of hyperthyroidism can be varied. It can be triggered by an overactive thyroid gland, a condition known as Graves' disease, or by taking too much thyroid medication. Other factors like stress, certain medications, and an autoimmune disorder can also contribute to the condition.

Herbal remedies can help alleviate the symptoms of hyperthyroidism. Some herbs that have been traditionally used for this purpose include ashwagandha, which helps to reduce stress and anxiety, and bladderwrack, a seaweed that supports thyroid function. Other herbs like licorice root and schisandra can help to calm the nervous system and promote relaxation. Herbal preparations for hyperthyroidism can be taken in various forms. Tea is a popular choice, as it is easy to make and can be consumed throughout the day. Some people prefer to take herbal capsules or tinctures, which can be more concentrated and convenient. Others may use herbal infusions or decoctions, which can be made by steeping herbs in hot water.

When using herbal remedies, it's essential to consult with a healthcare practitioner to ensure they are safe and effective for your specific condition.

What are the underlying causes of hyperthyroidism?

The main causes of hyperthyroidism are conditions that stimulate the thyroid gland to produce too much thyroid hormone.

One of the most common causes is Graves' disease, an autoimmune disorder where the body's immune system mistakenly attacks the thyroid gland, causing it to overproduce hormones. Another cause is a thyroid nodule, a growth or lump on the thyroid gland that can be benign or cancerous. When a thyroid nodule becomes overactive, it can produce excess hormones, leading to hyperthyroidism.

Thyroiditis, or inflammation of the thyroid gland, can also cause hyperthyroidism. This can be due to an autoimmune response, a viral infection, or a reaction to certain medications. The inflammation causes the thyroid gland to release stored hormones, leading to an excess of thyroid hormone in the body. A rare but possible cause of hyperthyroidism is a pituitary adenoma, a non-cancerous tumor on the pituitary gland that regulates the thyroid gland.

This tumor can stimulate the thyroid gland to produce excess hormones, leading to hyperthyroidism.

If you have hyperthyroidism, what herbs should you steer clear of?

If you have hyperthyroidism, it's best to be careful with certain herbs that can affect your thyroid gland.

For example, Valeriana officinalis, also known as valerian root, can lower blood pressure and slow down the heart rate. This can be a problem if you already have an overactive thyroid, as it can make your condition worse. Similarly, Lavandula angustifolia, or English lavender, can have a similar effect on the heart rate, which can be a concern for people with hyperthyroidism.

Cinchona officinalis, or Peruvian bark, contains quinine, which can stimulate the thyroid gland and worsen hyperthyroidism symptoms. Ephedra sinica, a plant that's sometimes used as a stimulant, can also increase thyroid hormone production, making it a bad choice for people with an overactive thyroid. Panax ginseng, or Asian ginseng, can affect the thyroid gland by stimulating the production of thyroid hormones. This can be a problem for people with hyperthyroidism, as it can make their symptoms worse.

It's always a good idea to talk to your doctor before using any herbal remedies, especially if you have a medical condition like hyperthyroidism.

What Are The Main Causes Of Palpitation?

Palpitations are irregular heartbeats that can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is anxiety. When we're anxious, our body releases stress hormones like adrenaline, which can speed up our heart rate and make it beat irregularly. This is because our body is trying to prepare us for the 'fight or flight' response, making our heart pump faster to deliver oxygen and energy to our muscles. Dehydration is another cause of palpitations. When we don't drink enough water, our body can become dehydrated, leading to a drop in blood pressure.

As a result, our heart has to work harder to pump blood throughout our body, which can cause irregular heartbeats. Dehydration can also affect the electrical signals that control our heart's rhythm, making it beat irregularly. Hyperthyroidism is a medical condition where the thyroid gland produces too much thyroid hormone. This hormone regulates our metabolism and heart rate. When there's an excess of thyroid hormone, it can speed up our heart rate and make it beat irregularly. This is because the thyroid hormone stimulates the heart to pump more blood, which can lead to palpitations.

In some cases, the heart rate can become so fast that it can lead to more serious complications.

Below are the main causes of palpitation.

  • Anxiety: Anxiety causes palpitations because it sends stress signals to the brain, which in turn sends faster-than-normal messages to the heart, making it beat too quickly and irregularly.
  • Dehydration: Dehydration is a cause of palpitation because when the body loses too much water, the heart has to work harder to pump blood, leading to irregular heartbeats and palpitations.
  • Hyperthyroidism: Hyperthyroidism is a cause of palpitation because the excess thyroid hormone makes the heart beat too fast, leading to irregular and rapid heartbeats, which can feel like palpitations.
  • Hypokalemia: Hypokalemia causes palpitation because low potassium levels in the blood disrupt the heart's electrical signals, leading to irregular and rapid heartbeats.
  • Hypoglycemia: Hypoglycemia, or low blood sugar, can cause palpitations because the brain temporarily runs out of energy and sends out warning signals to the heart, leading to irregular heartbeats and a rapid pulse.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Palpitation?

If you have palpitations, it's essential to avoid certain herbs that can make them worse.

One herb to be careful with is Yohimbe (Pausinystalia johimbe). It can increase heart rate and blood pressure, making palpitations more frequent and severe. Another herb to avoid is Ephedra (Ephedra sinica), which can also raise blood pressure and heart rate, leading to palpitations.

Additionally, Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) should be used with caution, as it can cause heart palpitations in some people, especially at high doses. Similarly, Ginseng (Panax ginseng) can cause palpitations in some individuals, possibly due to its ability to increase heart rate and blood pressure. Finally, Guayusa (Ilex guayusa) and Yerba Mate (Ilex paraguariensis) contain caffeine, which can worsen palpitations in people who are sensitive to its effects.

It's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbs to manage palpitations.

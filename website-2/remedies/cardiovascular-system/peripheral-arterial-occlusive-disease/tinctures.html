People with high blood pressure are more likely to develop a condition called peripheral arterial occlusive disease (PAOD).

This is because the high pressure can damage the blood vessels, making it harder for blood to flow to the legs, arms, and other parts of the body. PAOD can cause pain in the legs and arms when walking or exercising, and it can also lead to wounds that don't heal easily. This can make everyday activities, like grocery shopping or taking a walk, difficult and painful.

People with PAOD may also have to stop more often to rest and recover, which can make them feel tired and limited in their daily lives. When treating PAOD in people with high blood pressure, doctors often focus on lowering the blood pressure and improving blood flow to the affected areas. However, some herbal tinctures may be used in addition to traditional treatments to help manage symptoms and improve blood flow.

8 Herbal Tinctures For Peripheral Arterial Occlusive Disease

People with high blood pressure are more likely to develop a condition called peripheral arterial occlusive disease (PAOD).

This is because the high pressure can damage the blood vessels, making it harder for blood to flow to the legs, arms, and other parts of the body. PAOD can cause pain in the legs and arms when walking or exercising, and it can also lead to wounds that don't heal easily. This can make everyday activities, like grocery shopping or taking a walk, difficult and painful.

People with PAOD may also have to stop more often to rest and recover, which can make them feel tired and limited in their daily lives. When treating PAOD in people with high blood pressure, doctors often focus on lowering the blood pressure and improving blood flow to the affected areas. However, some herbal tinctures may be used in addition to traditional treatments to help manage symptoms and improve blood flow. These tinctures are typically made from plants that have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to reduce damage to blood vessels and improve circulation.

They may also help to relax blood vessels and improve blood flow, which can reduce pain and discomfort in people with PAOD.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contain a blend of flavonoids and terpenoids, which work together to help alleviate symptoms of peripheral arterial occlusive disease (PAOD).

PAOD occurs when the blood vessels in the legs become narrowed or blocked, reducing blood flow and causing pain, cramping, and fatigue. This is achieved by relaxing the muscles in the blood vessel walls, allowing for smoother blood flow and reducing the risk of blockages.

The terpenoids in Ginkgo biloba, such as ginkgolides and bilobalide, further enhance this effect by inhibiting platelet activation, which can contribute to blood clots. To try Ginkgo biloba tinctures for PAOD, start by taking 20-30 drops of a 1:5 or 1:10 tincture three times a day, mixed with water. Monitor your symptoms and adjust the dosage as needed. If you experience any relief in pain or cramping, it may be due to increased blood flow and reduced inflammation.

However, if your symptoms worsen, discontinue use and consult a healthcare professional.

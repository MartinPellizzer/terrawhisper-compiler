1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains flavonoids, bilobalide, and ginkgolides as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to improve blood flow and reduce oxidative stress in the body. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba essential oils have a vasodilating effect, which means they help to widen blood vessels and improve circulation. This can be beneficial for people with peripheral arterial occlusive disease, as it may help to reduce symptoms such as pain and cramping in the legs.

By improving blood flow and reducing inflammation, Ginkgo biloba essential oils may help to promote healing and reduce the risk of further complications.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (such as jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Wash the Ginkgo biloba leaves with water and dry them with a clean towel.
  3. Combine the Ginkgo biloba leaves and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Ginkgo biloba essential oil in a cool, dark place and use it as needed. Start with a small amount (about 5 drops) and gradually increase as needed.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains several bioactive constituents, including carnosic acid, rosmarinic acid, and camphor, which have potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the blood vessels, which is often associated with peripheral arterial occlusive disease (PAOD). The antioxidant properties of rosmarinic acid and carnosic acid also help to protect against cell damage and promote healthy cell growth, which can aid in the repair of damaged blood vessels. Additionally, camphor has vasodilatory properties, which can help to widen the blood vessels and improve blood flow to the affected areas.

By reducing oxidative stress, inflammation, and promoting healthy cell growth, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils may help to alleviate symptoms of PAOD and promote overall vascular health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over low heat and add the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.
  3. Let the mixture simmer for 2-3 hours, then turn off the heat.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean glass container, discarding the solids.
  5. Use a distillation kit or a still to extract the essential oil from the liquid, then store the oil in a dark glass bottle.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties can help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the blood vessels, which can contribute to peripheral arterial occlusive disease (PAOD). The essential oils also contain sesquiterpenes, which have vasodilatory effects, meaning they can help widen the blood vessels and improve blood flow. This can be beneficial for people with PAOD, as improved blood flow can help reduce symptoms like pain and numbness in the legs.

By reducing inflammation and improving blood flow, Zingiber officinale essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of PAOD.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (Ginger) rhizomes and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Wash the ginger rhizomes in cold water, then chop them into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped ginger (about 1/4 cup) with 1 cup of carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Store it in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter. Discard the solids and keep the liquid.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a clean glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops of the essential oil in a carrier oil for peripheral arterial occlusive disease relief.

4. Salvia miltiorrhiza

Salvia miltiorrhiza essential oils contains bioactive constituents like tanshinone, salvianolic acid, and rosavin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce inflammation and damage to blood vessels, a key factor in peripheral arterial occlusive disease. Salvia miltiorrhiza essential oils also dilates blood vessels, improving blood flow to the extremities and reducing symptoms like pain and cramping. The antioxidant properties of these compounds also protect the blood vessels from oxidative stress, further reducing the progression of the disease.

By reducing inflammation and improving blood flow, Salvia miltiorrhiza essential oils helps alleviate symptoms and slow the progression of peripheral arterial occlusive disease.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Salvia miltiorrhiza roots and 4 cups of water. Use a clean glass container.
  2. Heat the water in a saucepan until it reaches 212°F (100°C). Then, add the dried roots and turn off the heat.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 2 hours. Use a cheesecloth or a coffee filter to separate the roots from the liquid.
  4. Collect the liquid and transfer it to a clean glass container. Add 2 cups of ethanol (95% or higher) and mix well.
  5. Store the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks. Shake the container every day. After 2 weeks, filter the liquid again and discard the roots.

5. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng essential oils contains a compound called ginsenoside, which is a key player in its healing properties.

The ginsenosides, specifically Rg1 and Rb1, have been shown to improve blood flow and lower blood pressure. This is because they help to relax the muscles in the blood vessels, making it easier for blood to flow through them. Additionally, the essential oil has antioxidant properties, thanks to the presence of sesquiterpenes and saponins, which can help to reduce inflammation and protect the body from damage caused by free radicals.

By promoting blood flow and reducing inflammation, Panax ginseng essential oils may be beneficial in alleviating symptoms of peripheral arterial occlusive disease.

  1. Gather 100 grams of dried Panax ginseng root and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Add 1 cup of carrier oil (such as jojoba or sweet almond oil) to the jar, covering the ginseng root.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every 2-3 days.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a new jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the resulting Panax ginseng essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid and use it as needed.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and vasodilatory properties.

These properties help to relax and dilate blood vessels, improving blood flow to the extremities. The increased blood flow can aid in reducing the symptoms of peripheral arterial occlusive disease, such as pain and numbness in the legs and feet. The antioxidant properties of linalool also help to protect against oxidative stress and damage to blood vessels, further contributing to improved circulation.

By promoting vasodilation and reducing inflammation, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils may provide relief from the discomfort associated with peripheral arterial occlusive disease.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Wash the flowers with 2 cups of water to remove dirt. Let them air-dry.
  3. Combine the dried flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Leave 1 inch of space at the top.
  4. Steep the mixture in a warm, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. This allows the oil to extract from the flowers.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the flowers and store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

7. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a compound called curcumin, which has potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Curcumin is made up of a group of molecules called polyphenols, specifically ferulic acid and sinensetin, that help to reduce inflammation and improve blood vessel function. This is particularly beneficial for people with peripheral arterial occlusive disease, as it can help to improve blood flow to the affected limbs. The antioxidant properties of curcumin also help to protect the blood vessels from damage caused by free radicals, reducing the risk of further blockages.

By improving blood flow and reducing inflammation, curcuma longa essential oils may help to alleviate symptoms of peripheral arterial occlusive disease.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Curcuma longa roots, a distillation apparatus, and a carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Wash the Curcuma longa roots with water, then dry them completely.
  3. Grate 1/4 cup of the dried Curcuma longa roots and place them in the distillation apparatus.
  4. Steam distill the grated Curcuma longa roots for 2 hours to extract the essential oil. Collect the essential oil in a separate container.
  5. Mix 10-15 drops of the extracted Curcuma longa essential oil with 1 tablespoon of the carrier oil. Store the final product in a dark glass bottle.

Consuming herbal teas daily for peripheral arterial occlusive disease may not be safe.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for peripheral arterial occlusive disease?

Drinking herbal tea in the morning can be helpful for people with peripheral arterial occlusive disease. This is because morning circulation is often slower, and herbal teas like hawthorn and rosemary may help improve blood flow.

11 Herbal Creams For Hypertension

When applied topically as a cream or inhaled as a tea, lavender can help calm the mind and body, reducing stress and anxiety that contribute to high blood pressure. The benefits of using herbal creams and teas for hypertension are numerous. By promoting relaxation, reducing stress, and improving blood flow, these herbs can help prevent cardiovascular disease, reduce the risk of heart attacks and strokes, and improve overall quality of life.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis creams contains the bioactive constituents valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates.

These compounds have a calming effect on the nervous system, which helps to reduce stress and anxiety that can contribute to high blood pressure. Valerenic acid, in particular, has been shown to lower blood pressure in animal studies by relaxing blood vessels and improving circulation. The sedative properties of valepotriates also help to slow down the heart rate and promote a state of relaxation, further reducing blood pressure.

By reducing stress and promoting relaxation, Valeriana officinalis creams may help to lower blood pressure and alleviate hypertension symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Valeriana officinalis root, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and a double boiler.
  2. Grind the dried Valeriana officinalis root into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in the double boiler over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add the ground Valeriana officinalis root and vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  5. Remove the double boiler from heat and let the mixture cool and thicken. Pour the cream into containers and store in a cool, dark place.

2. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds help relax blood vessels, reducing blood pressure and improving circulation. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant properties that protect the body from oxidative stress, a known contributor to hypertension. The terpenoids, such as ginkgolides and bilobalide, inhibit platelet aggregation, which can help prevent blood clots from forming and worsen hypertension.

By improving blood flow and reducing blood pressure, Ginkgo biloba creams may be beneficial for people with hypertension.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Ginkgo biloba extract, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from heat and stir in Ginkgo biloba extract, shea butter, and vitamin E oil until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into a silicone candy mold or a small tin container to shape the cream.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify completely before using. Store the Ginkgo biloba cream in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its primary bioactive constituents.

These compounds have a calming effect on the body and can help reduce blood pressure by decreasing the production of stress hormones like cortisol. Linalool also has a vasodilating effect, which means it can help relax blood vessels and improve blood flow, making it easier for blood to flow through the body. The antispasmodic properties of linalyl acetate can also help reduce the strain on the heart by preventing excessive contractions and relax the smooth muscle in blood vessels.

By combining these properties, Lavandula angustifolia creams may help alleviate hypertension symptoms by promoting a state of relaxation and improving cardiovascular health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of lavender essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add the dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the melted mixture and steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth and discard the flowers. Add the vitamin E oil and lavender essential oil to the mixture.
  5. Pour the mixture into a bowl and let it cool. Once cooled, whip the mixture with a hand mixer until it's light and fluffy. Store the cream in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains the bioactive constituents gingerol and shogaol, which have been found to help reduce blood pressure in people with hypertension.

Gingerol has vasodilatory properties, which means it helps widen blood vessels, allowing blood to flow more easily and reducing the pressure on the heart. Shogaol, on the other hand, has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the blood vessels and improve circulation. The combination of these properties can help lower blood pressure and reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease.

By reducing blood pressure and improving circulation, Zingiber officinale creams may be a useful adjunct to traditional treatments for hypertension.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of Zingiber officinale essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool slightly before stirring in the Zingiber officinale essential oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool completely before applying it to the skin as needed.

5. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata creams contains flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which have been found to have a positive effect on hypertension.

The flavonoids, specifically flavonoid glycosides, help to relax blood vessels and improve blood flow, reducing blood pressure. The alkaloids, such as harman and harmine, have a sedative effect and can help to reduce stress and anxiety, which are often underlying causes of high blood pressure. The glycosides in Passiflora incarnata creams also have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce inflammation in blood vessels and improve cardiovascular health.

By combining these properties, Passiflora incarnata creams can help to lower blood pressure and improve cardiovascular health in people with hypertension.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Passiflora incarnata flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 10 drops of vitamin E oil.
  2. Heat the coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Melt the mixture until the beeswax is fully dissolved, then remove it from heat.
  4. Add the dried Passiflora incarnata flowers to the melted mixture and let it steep for 2-3 hours. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean container.
  5. Add the vitamin E oil and stir well. Pour the mixture into small tin containers or jars and let it cool before use. Store in a cool, dark place.

6. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis creams contain boivinolic acid, rosmarinic acid, and linalool, which are known to help lower blood pressure.

Boivinolic acid has a calming effect on the nervous system, reducing stress and anxiety, which can contribute to high blood pressure. Rosmarinic acid acts as an antioxidant, protecting blood vessels from damage and improving their function, allowing blood to flow more freely. Linalool has a diuretic effect, helping the body to remove excess fluid, which can also contribute to lower blood pressure.

By reducing stress, improving blood vessel function, and aiding in fluid removal, Melissa officinalis creams may be a helpful natural remedy for managing hypertension.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Melissa officinalis flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1 cup of shea butter.
  2. Heat coconut oil in a double boiler to 160°F (71°C).
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of shea butter and mix until melted.
  4. Combine the Melissa officinalis flowers with 1 cup of boiling water and let it steep for 10 minutes.
  5. Strain the Melissa officinalis mixture and add it to the coconut oil mixture. Mix well and pour into containers.

7. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng creams contains biologically active constituents such as ginsenosides, particularly ginsenoside Rg1, which have been shown to help regulate blood pressure.

Ginsenosides have been found to have a vasodilatory effect, which means they help widen blood vessels and improve blood flow to the heart. This helps reduce blood pressure by making it easier for blood to flow through the body. The anti-inflammatory properties of ginsenosides also play a role in reducing blood pressure by minimizing inflammation in the blood vessels, which can constrict and raise blood pressure.

The combination of these properties makes Panax ginseng creams a potential remedy for hypertension by helping to lower blood pressure and improve cardiovascular health.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of ginseng powder, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and a few drops of vitamin E oil.
  2. Combine 1 cup of distilled water and 2 tablespoons of ginseng powder in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 10-15 minutes, or until the ginseng powder dissolves.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, and 2 tablespoons of shea butter. Heat this mixture in a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each heating, until the beeswax melts.
  4. Combine the heated ginseng mixture and the melted beeswax mixture in the saucepan. Stir well to combine.
  5. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool slightly. Add a few drops of vitamin E oil and stir well. Pour the mixture into a cream jar and let it cool completely before using.

11. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid and borneol, which are powerful antioxidants that help reduce inflammation in blood vessels, making it easier for blood to flow and lowering blood pressure.

The antispasmodic properties of borneol also help relax blood vessels, improving circulation and reducing the strain on the heart. The flavonoids present in Rosmarinus officinalis, such as carnosic acid, have a direct effect on blood vessels, causing them to dilate and reducing blood pressure. The antihypertensive effects of rosmarinic acid also help reduce the production of angiotensin II, a hormone that constricts blood vessels and raises blood pressure.

By reducing inflammation and constriction in blood vessels, Rosmarinus officinalis creams can help alleviate hypertension symptoms.

  3. Add the coconut oil and beeswax to the saucepan. Heat it until the beeswax melts.
  4. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool slightly. Add 2 tablespoons of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves to the mixture.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a container. Let it cool completely before applying it to the skin.

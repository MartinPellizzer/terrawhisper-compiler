8 Herbal Essential Oils For Hypertension

Hypertension, or high blood pressure, is a common health issue that affects many adults worldwide.

It happens when the blood vessels in our body become narrow and stiff, making it harder for blood to flow. This can be caused by a variety of factors, such as a poor diet, lack of exercise, stress, and genetics. When hypertension develops, it can lead to serious complications like heart disease, strokes, and kidney damage. Adults with high blood pressure may experience symptoms like headaches, dizziness, and fatigue, which can significantly impact their daily lives. They may find it difficult to perform even simple tasks, and their quality of life can suffer greatly. As hypertension worsens, it can lead to further health problems, such as heart failure, where the heart becomes too weak to pump blood efficiently.

In severe cases, high blood pressure can even cause a heart attack or stroke, which can be life-threatening. Moreover, hypertension can also lead to kidney disease, where the kidneys become damaged and unable to filter waste from the blood. This can require costly treatments and even dialysis in some cases. The good news is that there are some natural remedies that may help adults manage their hypertension. Certain essential oils, derived from plants, have been found to have a positive effect on blood pressure. These oils work by relaxing the blood vessels, reducing inflammation, and promoting a sense of calm.

When inhaled, they can help slow down the heart rate and lower blood pressure, providing relief from hypertension symptoms.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates, which have been shown to have a calming effect on the body and help lower blood pressure.

The valepotriates in this essential oil have been found to block the action of the enzyme that breaks down serotonin, a neurotransmitter that helps regulate blood pressure. This means that the valepotriates in Valeriana officinalis essential oils can help increase serotonin levels in the body, leading to a decrease in blood pressure. If you're looking to use Valeriana officinalis essential oils to help with hypertension, start by using a high-quality essential oil that is extracted from the roots of the Valeriana officinalis plant. You can add a few drops of the oil to a diffuser or mix it with a carrier oil for a relaxing massage.

It's recommended to use the essential oil in moderation, as excessive use can cause drowsiness. When using Valeriana officinalis essential oils for hypertension, it's likely that you'll notice a decrease in your blood pressure readings over time. You may also experience a sense of calm and relaxation, which can help reduce stress and anxiety that can contribute to high blood pressure. However, it's essential to note that Valeriana officinalis essential oils should not be used as a replacement for medical treatment.

If you're currently taking medication for hypertension, consult with your doctor before using Valeriana officinalis essential oils.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent hypertension from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help lower blood pressure.

Some herbal teas, like hibiscus and ginger, have been shown to relax blood vessels and improve circulation. Regular consumption of these teas could potentially prevent hypertension from forming.

However, the effect can vary depending on the individual and the type of tea consumed.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for hypertension every day?

Herbal teas can be a helpful addition to managing hypertension.

However, some people may experience side effects like dizziness or rapid heartbeat when drinking them daily. It's also possible for herbal teas to interact with medications, which can worsen blood pressure.

Moderation is key, so start with small amounts and monitor your body's response.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in hypertension?

Herbal teas for hypertension can start showing results in a few weeks to a few months.

This varies depending on the type of tea, the individual's health, and how often it's consumed.

Some teas, like hawthorn and olive leaf, may help lower blood pressure within a few weeks, while others may take longer to have an effect.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids, terpenoids, and bilobalide as its active constituents.

These compounds help improve blood flow and reduce inflammation, which can benefit people with low blood pressure. Flavonoids, in particular, have antioxidant properties that protect the blood vessels from damage, making them stronger and more resilient. Terpenoids and bilobalide also help dilate blood vessels, allowing more blood to flow and increasing blood pressure.

Regular use of Ginkgo biloba creams may help alleviate symptoms of low blood pressure by improving circulation and reducing oxidative stress.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of Ginkgo biloba extract, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add the shea butter and stir until melted and smooth. Remove from heat.
  4. Add the Ginkgo biloba extract and vitamin E oil to the mixture and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a glass jar and let it cool and solidify. Store in a cool, dry place.

Regular use of the creams may help stabilize blood pressure and improve overall cardiovascular health.

  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add aloe vera gel, vitamin E oil, and Panax ginseng root powder to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool until it reaches 120-130°F (49-54°C).
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean glass jar and let it cool completely before use. Apply a small amount to the skin as needed to help improve low blood pressure.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent low blood pressure from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent low blood pressure from forming.

Certain herbal teas, like ginger and peppermint, have properties that help improve blood circulation and boost energy levels. They can also help regulate blood pressure and prevent it from dropping too low.

This may be especially helpful for people who experience frequent dizziness or lightheadedness.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for low blood pressure every day?

While herbal teas like ginseng and licorice root may help raise blood pressure, consuming them daily can have mixed effects.

Some teas can interact with medications or worsen underlying conditions.

It's best to drink herbal teas in moderation, ideally after consulting the ingredient label for potential interactions or contraindications.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in low blood pressure?

Herbal teas can help lower blood pressure, but it's not a quick fix.

It might take a few weeks to notice a difference. Some people see results in as little as 2-3 weeks, while others may need up to 6 weeks.

It depends on the individual and the type of herbal tea being used.

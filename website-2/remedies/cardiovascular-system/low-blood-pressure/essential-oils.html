1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

These bioactive constituents have warming and stimulating effects on the body, which can help increase blood circulation and heart rate. Gingerol, in particular, has a vasodilatory effect, causing blood vessels to dilate and increase blood flow to the extremities. This increase in circulation can help counteract the symptoms of low blood pressure by ensuring that the brain and other vital organs receive a sufficient supply of oxygen and nutrients.

Regular use of Zingiber officinale essential oils may also help to stimulate digestion and improve nutrient absorption, further contributing to an increase in blood pressure.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (Ginger) roots. Clean and slice them thinly.
  2. Place the sliced ginger in a 1-liter glass jar. Cover with 2 cups of vodka or a carrier oil like coconut oil.
  3. Close the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and use it as needed. For low blood pressure, mix a few drops with a carrier oil and apply it to your pulse points or inhale it.

2. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains active constituents like flavonoids, bilobalide, and ginkgolides.

These compounds help improve blood circulation and increase blood flow to the brain and other vital organs. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba essential oils have antioxidant properties, which help protect blood vessels from damage and improve their elasticity. Bilobalide and ginkgolides also help improve blood vessel function, which can help counteract the effects of low blood pressure.

By promoting healthy blood flow and blood vessel function, Ginkgo biloba essential oils may be beneficial for individuals with low blood pressure.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves, 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Wash the Ginkgo biloba leaves with water, then pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Combine the Ginkgo biloba leaves and carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well to coat the leaves evenly.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter.
  5. Bottle the Ginkgo biloba essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains a compound called camphor, along with other active constituents like alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, and bornyl acetate.

These components help stimulate the nervous system and increase blood flow, which can aid in raising blood pressure. The essential oil's properties also include vasodilatory effects, which cause blood vessels to widen and improve circulation, further contributing to increased blood pressure. Additionally, the oil's aroma can stimulate the brain's hypothalamus, a region that regulates body temperature, hunger, and blood pressure.

By increasing blood flow and stimulating the body's natural regulatory systems, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils may be beneficial for individuals with low blood pressure.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water. Clean and dry the leaves.
  2. Combine the leaves and water in a saucepan and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of carrier oil (like jojoba oil) to the liquid and stir well. This helps to dilute the essential oil.
  5. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle and label it. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

4. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng essential oils contains ginsenosides and gintonin, bioactive constituents that help regulate blood pressure.

These compounds have a direct impact on blood vessels, causing them to dilate and improve circulation. The ginsenosides increase the production of nitric oxide, a natural vasodilator that helps to relax blood vessels and reduce blood pressure. The gintonin also stimulates the heart to beat more efficiently, increasing blood flow and oxygen delivery to the body's tissues.

By improving circulation and dilating blood vessels, Panax ginseng essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of low blood pressure.

  1. Gather 100g of dried Panax ginseng root. You can buy this at a local health food store or online.
  2. Combine the dried root with 1 cup of carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil, in a clean glass jar.
  3. Heat the mixture in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water for 2-3 hours. Stir occasionally.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops under the tongue or add to food to help raise blood pressure.

5. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains a high concentration of valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone, which are the main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have sedative and vasopressor properties, meaning they can help to stimulate blood pressure and improve circulation. The valerenic acid in Valeriana officinalis essential oils is particularly effective at constricting blood vessels, which can help to increase blood pressure in individuals with hypotension. Additionally, the isovaleric acid has a stimulating effect on the nervous system, which can help to increase heart rate and blood pressure.

By stimulating the cardiovascular system, Valeriana officinalis essential oils can help to improve blood pressure and overall circulation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Valeriana officinalis roots and 1 cup of water. Clean and chop the roots into small pieces.
  2. Place the chopped roots in a saucepan and add 1 cup of water. Heat the mixture over low heat for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it steep for 30 minutes. This will help release the essential oils from the roots.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the solids and reserve the liquid.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and let it sit at room temperature for 2-3 days. This will help the essential oils separate from the water.

6. Astragalus membranaceus

Astragalus membranaceus essential oils contains bioactive constituents like astragalosides, flavonoids, and polysaccharides.

These compounds have properties that help to strengthen the cardiovascular system and improve blood circulation. Astragalosides, in particular, have been shown to increase cardiac output and blood pressure by dilating blood vessels and improving the heart's pumping ability. The flavonoids in Astragalus membranaceus essential oils also have antioxidant properties, which can help to protect the blood vessels from damage and promote overall cardiovascular health.

By improving blood circulation and protecting the cardiovascular system, Astragalus membranaceus essential oils may be beneficial for people with low blood pressure.

  1. Gather 250g of Astragalus membranaceus roots and stems. Chop them into small pieces.
  2. Combine 250g of chopped Astragalus with 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil) in a clean glass jar.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking it every day. This allows the oil to infuse.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the infused oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 5-7 drops of this oil in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for low blood pressure relief.

7. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have a stimulating effect on the cardiovascular system, which helps to improve blood flow and increase blood pressure. Linalool, in particular, has a vasodilatory effect, meaning it helps to widen blood vessels and allow more blood to flow through them. This can be beneficial for individuals with low blood pressure, as it can help to increase circulation and promote a more stable blood pressure.

By stimulating the cardiovascular system and promoting blood flow, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to support healthy blood pressure levels.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of water.
  2. Combine the flowers and water in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it steep for 2-3 hours. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a steam distillation apparatus or a double boiler. Steam distill the liquid to extract the essential oil.
  5. Collect the essential oil and store it in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Use 5-10 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for low blood pressure relief.

8. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains the bioactive constituents cinnamaldehyde and eugenol.

These compounds have vasodilatory properties, which help to widen blood vessels and improve blood flow. This increased blood flow can help to raise low blood pressure by allowing the heart to pump more efficiently. The anti-inflammatory properties of cinnamaldehyde and eugenol also help to reduce inflammation in blood vessels, further contributing to improved blood pressure.

Regular use of Cinnamomum verum essential oils may help to stabilize blood pressure by promoting healthy blood vessel function.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cinnamomum verum leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat and add the Cinnamomum verum leaves.
  3. Reduce heat to low and let simmer for 1 hour.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discarding the leaves.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 2 hours, then transfer it to a dark glass bottle and store in a cool place.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent low blood pressure from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent low blood pressure, but it's not a guarantee.

Some herbs like ginger and ginseng have properties that can help increase blood pressure. However, the effect is usually mild and temporary.

Regular consumption may help maintain a stable blood pressure, but individual results vary.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for low blood pressure every day?

Herbal teas for low blood pressure can be a good option, but be cautious with daily consumption.

Some herbs, like ginseng and licorice root, can increase blood pressure temporarily. If you already have low blood pressure, these teas might make it drop too low.

Monitor your blood pressure and adjust your tea intake accordingly.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in low blood pressure?

Herbal teas can help lower blood pressure over time.

Some people may notice a difference in a few days, while others may see results in a week or two.

It's usually after 2-6 weeks of regular consumption that herbal teas like hawthorn and ginger can have a noticeable effect on blood pressure.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for low blood pressure?

For low blood pressure, it's often recommended to drink herbal tea in the morning.

This is because it can help increase circulation and give you energy throughout the day.

Morning is also a good time to boost your immune system and stay alert and focused.

What Are The Main Causes Of Varicose Veins?

Prolonged sitting is a common cause of varicose veins.

When you sit for a long time, the blood in your legs has to work harder to return to your heart. This can cause the veins to stretch and become weaker, leading to varicose veins. This is because gravity pulls the blood down towards your feet, making it harder for it to return to your heart. Genetic predisposition is another factor that contributes to varicose veins.

If your parents or grandparents had varicose veins, you may be more likely to develop them as well. This is because some people are born with weaker veins or valves that don't work properly, making it easier for blood to leak backwards and cause varicose veins. Age is also a significant factor in the development of varicose veins. As we get older, our veins and valves can weaken, making it harder for blood to flow properly. This can cause varicose veins to develop over time.

Additionally, older adults may be more likely to sit for long periods, which can further contribute to the development of varicose veins.

Below are the main causes of varicose veins.

  • Prolonged Sitting: Prolonged sitting causes varicose veins because it puts pressure on the blood vessels in the legs, stopping the normal flow of blood upwards towards the heart, which can cause blood to pool and stretch the veins.
  • Genetic Predisposition: Genetic predisposition is a cause of varicose veins because people with a family history of this condition may have inherited weak or faulty valves in their veins, which can lead to blood flowing backwards and accumulating in the vein, causing it to swell and become varicose.
  • Age: As we get older, our veins have to work harder to pump blood against gravity, causing them to stretch and weaken over time, leading to varicose veins.
  • Family History: Family history is a cause of varicose veins because if your parents or grandparents had this problem, you may be more likely to have weak valves in your veins, which can cause blood to pool and lead to varicose veins.
  • Obesity: Obesity can cause varicose veins because the extra weight puts extra pressure on the veins in your legs, making them work harder and more likely to become swollen and twisted.

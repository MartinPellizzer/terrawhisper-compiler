10 Herbal Tinctures For High Blood Pressure

If you're dealing with high blood pressure, you might want to consider herbal tinctures as a natural way to help manage it.

These herbal remedies work by relaxing blood vessels and improving blood flow, which in turn helps lower your blood pressure. Herbal teas can also help reduce stress and anxiety, which are major contributors to high blood pressure. Some herbs that have been shown to have a positive effect on blood pressure include Ginkgo biloba, which helps improve blood flow to the brain and reduces inflammation; Astragalus membranaceus, which has anti-inflammatory properties and helps regulate blood pressure; and Panax ginseng, which has been found to lower blood pressure and improve circulation. Using herbal tinctures can bring several benefits to your life. For one, they can help you avoid the side effects of prescription medications, such as dizziness and fatigue.

They can also be a more cost-effective option in the long run, as you can make your own herbal teas or buy them in bulk. Plus, many herbal tinctures have additional health benefits beyond just lowering blood pressure, such as improving digestion or reducing inflammation. By incorporating herbal tinctures into your daily routine, you can take a more natural and holistic approach to managing your blood pressure and improving your overall health. It's worth noting that herbal tinctures can interact with other medications or have adverse effects in certain individuals, so it's always a good idea to talk to a healthcare professional before starting to use them. Additionally, herbal tinctures are not a replacement for medical treatment, but rather a complementary way to manage your symptoms.

By working with a healthcare professional and incorporating herbal tinctures into your routine, you can take a more proactive and natural approach to managing your high blood pressure and improving your overall health.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contains flavoglycosides and bilobalide, which are compounds that help improve blood vessel function.

These flavoglycosides and bilobalide have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help relax blood vessel muscles and improve blood flow. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba tinctures, such as quercetin and kaempferol, also have antioxidant properties that help protect blood vessels from damage. By improving blood flow and reducing inflammation, Ginkgo biloba tinctures can help lower blood pressure and reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease.

Regular use of Ginkgo biloba tinctures may also help to dilate blood vessels, further improving blood flow and reducing blood pressure.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Ginkgo biloba leaves and 2 cups of 80% vodka in clean glass containers.
  2. Chop the Ginkgo biloba leaves into small pieces and place them in a clean glass jar.
  3. Pour 2 cups of 80% vodka over the chopped leaves, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, and discard the solids.

2. Astragalus membranaceus

Astragalus membranaceus tinctures contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, saponins, and polysaccharides that help lower blood pressure.

The flavonoids in Astragalus membranaceus have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can help relax blood vessels and improve blood flow. The saponins, on the other hand, have a vasodilatory effect, which means they can help widen blood vessels and reduce blood pressure. The polysaccharides in Astragalus membranaceus have a mild diuretic effect, which can help increase urine production and reduce fluid buildup in the body, further contributing to lower blood pressure.

Regular use of Astragalus membranaceus tinctures may help regulate blood pressure and promote cardiovascular health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Astragalus membranaceus root and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka or glycerin in a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the dried root and vodka or glycerin in the jar, making sure the root is completely covered.
  3. Close the jar tightly and shake it well for 1-2 minutes to mix the ingredients.
  4. Place the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2-6 weeks, shaking it every day, to allow the tincture to steep.
  5. Strain the tincture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle, discarding the solids and storing the liquid in the fridge for up to 2 years.

3. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng tinctures contains bioactive constituents like ginsenosides and ginsenoside-Rg1, which are known for their ability to help manage high blood pressure.

These compounds have vasodilatory properties, meaning they help to relax blood vessels and improve blood flow, making it easier for the heart to pump blood. The ginsenosides in Panax ginseng tinctures also have antioxidant properties, which can help to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the blood vessels, further contributing to lower blood pressure. Additionally, Panax ginseng tinctures has been shown to have a regulatory effect on the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system, which plays a key role in blood pressure regulation.

By addressing the underlying causes of high blood pressure, Panax ginseng tinctures can help to promote healthy blood pressure levels.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Panax ginseng root, 2 cups of 80% vodka, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the dried ginseng root and vodka in the glass jar. Make sure the ginseng is completely covered by the vodka.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the ginseng tincture in the jar and use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, as needed to help manage high blood pressure.

4. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis tinctures contains valerenic acid, valeranone, and valepotriates as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have a relaxing effect on blood vessels, which helps to lower blood pressure. The valepotriates also have a diuretic effect, increasing urine production and reducing fluid buildup in the body, further contributing to a decrease in blood pressure. The valerenic acid and valeranone in Valeriana officinalis tinctures also have a sedative effect, reducing stress and anxiety, which can contribute to high blood pressure.

Regular use of Valeriana officinalis tinctures may help to manage high blood pressure by promoting relaxation, reducing fluid buildup, and lowering blood vessel constriction.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Valeriana officinalis roots and 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Fill a saucepan with water and bring it to a boil. Place the Valeriana officinalis roots in a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh strainer.
  3. Steep the roots in the boiling water for 10-15 minutes, then remove from heat and let it cool.
  4. Combine the cooled root infusion with vodka in the glass jar, making sure the roots are completely covered.
  5. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-6 weeks, shaking it every day. Strain the liquid and discard the roots to get your Valeriana officinalis tincture.

5. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains several bioactive constituents, including gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help to relax blood vessels and improve circulation, making it easier for blood to flow through the body. The relaxant properties of gingerols and shogaols also help to reduce blood pressure by counteracting the effects of stress hormones, such as adrenaline. Additionally, the vasodilatory effects of Zingiber officinale tinctures help to lower blood pressure by widening blood vessels and reducing resistance.

Regular consumption of Zingiber officinale tinctures has been shown to have a positive effect on blood pressure, particularly in individuals with mild to moderate hypertension.

  1. Gather 250ml of 80% Vodka and 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale roots. Clean and chop the roots into small pieces.
  2. Combine the chopped roots and vodka in a clean glass jar. Cover the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass container. Discard the solids.
  4. Measure the tincture and add 1/4 cup of water to 1 tablespoon of the tincture. You can adjust the ratio to your preference.
  5. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, under the guidance of a healthcare professional, to help manage high blood pressure.

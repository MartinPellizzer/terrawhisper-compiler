8 Herbal Essential Oils For High Blood Pressure

As people age, their blood vessels naturally become less flexible and less efficient at regulating blood pressure.

This can lead to high blood pressure, which is a common condition in older adults. High blood pressure can cause shortness of breath, fatigue, and dizziness, making everyday activities feel overwhelming. It can also increase the risk of heart disease, stroke, and kidney damage. Older adults with high blood pressure may need to limit their physical activity, avoid certain foods, and take medication to manage their condition. This can be frustrating and affect their quality of life, making it difficult to enjoy hobbies, spend time with loved ones, and maintain independence. High blood pressure can also have a significant impact on mental health, leading to feelings of anxiety and depression.

Older adults may feel like they're losing control over their bodies and their lives. They may worry about the future and the potential consequences of their condition. This can create a cycle of fear and anxiety, making it harder to manage their blood pressure and overall health. Fortunately, there are some natural alternatives that may help older adults manage high blood pressure. Herbal essential oils, for example, have been shown to have a positive effect on blood pressure. These oils work by promoting relaxation, reducing stress, and improving circulation.

They can be inhaled directly, applied topically, or added to a warm bath to promote relaxation and reduce blood pressure.

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two main compounds that help lower blood pressure.

These constituents have a calming effect on the nervous system, which in turn slows down heart rate and reduces blood pressure. The anti-inflammatory properties of linalool also help to relax blood vessels, making it easier for blood to flow through them. If you're experiencing high blood pressure, it's recommended to inhale Lavandula angustifolia essential oils through steam inhalation or use a diffuser in a well-ventilated area. You can also add a few drops to a warm bath or mix it with a carrier oil for a gentle massage.

Start with a small dose and gradually increase as your body adjusts. When using Lavandula angustifolia essential oils for high blood pressure, you can expect to see a decrease in blood pressure readings over time. You may also experience reduced anxiety and stress levels, which can contribute to high blood pressure. Additionally, improved sleep quality and reduced inflammation can also be beneficial outcomes.

However, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially if you're already taking medication for high blood pressure.

2. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains a unique combination of properties and constituents that may help alleviate high blood pressure.

The oil is rich in valerenic acid, valeranone, and valeranone acetate, which have been shown to have a sedative and vasodilatory effect on the body, helping to relax blood vessels and reduce blood pressure. Additionally, the oil contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have antioxidant properties that can help protect the cardiovascular system from damage. By inhaling or applying Valeriana officinalis essential oils to the skin, you may experience a decrease in blood pressure due to the oil's ability to calm the nervous system and improve circulation.

To use Valeriana officinalis essential oils for high blood pressure, start by inhaling a few drops of the oil in a diffuser or applying a few drops to your wrists or temples after diluting it with a carrier oil. Begin with a small amount and gradually increase as needed. It's recommended to use the oil in conjunction with other natural remedies and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

As you incorporate Valeriana officinalis essential oils into your routine, you may notice a decrease in blood pressure readings, reduced anxiety and stress levels, and improved overall cardiovascular health.

4. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are compounds that help relax and dilate blood vessels.

This action can lower blood pressure by reducing the resistance in blood vessels, making it easier for the heart to pump blood. The key constituents, bilobalide and ginkgolides, also have antioxidant properties that help protect the blood vessels from damage and reduce inflammation. When using Ginkgo biloba essential oils for high blood pressure, it's essential to dilute it with a carrier oil and apply it topically to the wrists, temples, or behind the ears.

High Blood Pressure in Older Adults: 8 Essential Remedies to Try

As people age, their blood vessels naturally become less flexible and less efficient at regulating blood pressure.

This can lead to high blood pressure, which is a common condition in older adults. High blood pressure can cause shortness of breath, fatigue, and dizziness, making everyday activities feel overwhelming. It can also increase the risk of heart disease, stroke, and kidney damage. Older adults with high blood pressure may need to limit their physical activity, avoid certain foods, and take medication to manage their condition. This can be frustrating and affect their quality of life, making it difficult to enjoy hobbies, spend time with loved ones, and maintain independence. High blood pressure can also have a significant impact on mental health, leading to feelings of anxiety and depression.

Older adults may feel like they're losing control over their bodies and their lives. They may worry about the future and the potential consequences of their condition. This can create a cycle of fear and anxiety, making it harder to manage their blood pressure and overall health. Fortunately, there are some natural alternatives that may help older adults manage high blood pressure. Herbal essential oils, for example, have been shown to have a positive effect on blood pressure. These oils work by promoting relaxation, reducing stress, and improving circulation.

They can be inhaled directly, applied topically, or added to a warm bath to promote relaxation and reduce blood pressure.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils, also known as valerian root oil, may help with high blood pressure due to its calming and relaxing properties.

It contains constituents like valerenic acid, valeranone, and valeranone acetate, which have a sedative effect on the nervous system. When inhaled or applied topically, these compounds can help slow down the heart rate and lower blood pressure by reducing stress and anxiety. To use Valeriana officinalis essential oils for high blood pressure, add a few drops to a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical application.

For a diffuser, place 5-7 drops in the device and inhale for 5-10 minutes, ideally before bedtime. For topical use, mix 2-3 drops with 1 teaspoon of carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil, and gently massage onto the skin, such as on the wrists, temples, or behind the ears. When using Valeriana officinalis essential oils, you may notice a decrease in blood pressure readings, improved sleep quality, and a sense of calmness and relaxation. You may also experience reduced stress and anxiety levels, which can contribute to lower blood pressure.

However, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially if you're taking medications or have underlying medical conditions.

When inhaled, the properties of lavender oil can help slow down heart rate and lower blood pressure by activating the parasympathetic nervous system, which promotes relaxation and reduces stress hormones. To use lavender oil for high blood pressure, place a few drops on your wrists or inhale it through steam inhalation.

You can also add a few drops to your bath water or mix it with a carrier oil for a relaxing massage. Start with small amounts and gradually increase as needed. It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using lavender oil, especially if you're already taking medication for high blood pressure. By incorporating lavender oil into your daily routine, you may experience a reduction in anxiety and stress levels, leading to lower blood pressure readings and improved overall cardiovascular health.

Regular use may also lead to improved sleep quality, reduced muscle tension, and a sense of calmness and well-being.

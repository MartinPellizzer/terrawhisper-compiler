1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are two main bioactive constituents that contribute to its benefits for high blood pressure.

These compounds have vasodilatory properties, meaning they help to relax and widen blood vessels, improving blood flow and reducing blood pressure. The sedative and anti-inflammatory properties of linalool also help to reduce stress and anxiety, which are common triggers for high blood pressure. Additionally, linalyl acetate has been shown to have a calming effect on the nervous system, further contributing to its blood pressure-lowering effects.

By promoting relaxation and improving blood flow, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to support healthy blood pressure levels.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine 1 cup of flowers with 2 cups of water in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into the glass jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 hours to allow the essential oils to separate from the water. The water will become cloudy.
  5. Carefully pour the clear essential oil layer into a separate container, leaving the cloudy water behind.

2. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone, which are the key bioactive constituents responsible for its therapeutic effects.

These compounds have a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help lower blood pressure by reducing stress and anxiety. The sedative properties of Valeriana officinalis essential oils also help slow down heart rate and promote relaxation, further contributing to its ability to manage high blood pressure. The diuretic properties of the oil can also help reduce fluid retention, which is a common cause of high blood pressure.

By reducing stress, promoting relaxation, and increasing urine production, Valeriana officinalis essential oils can help alleviate high blood pressure symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Valeriana officinalis roots, 2 cups of water, and a distillation apparatus.
  2. Chop the roots into small pieces and add them to the distillation apparatus.
  3. Add 2 cups of water to the distillation apparatus and heat it over low heat.
  4. Collect the steam and condense it into a separate container using a condenser.
  5. Filter the resulting essential oil through a coffee filter and store it in a dark glass bottle.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have natural blood thinning properties.

These compounds help to increase blood flow and reduce blood pressure by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that constrict blood vessels. Ginger also contains sesquiterpenes, such as bisabolol and zingiberene, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help to relax blood vessels and improve circulation. The vasodilatory effect of ginger essential oils helps to reduce blood pressure by widening blood vessels and allowing blood to flow more easily.

Regular use of ginger essential oils may help to reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease and improve overall cardiovascular health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale roots, 2 cups of water, a large pot, and a glass jar for storing the essential oil.
  2. Peel the Zingiber officinale roots and chop them into small pieces. Place them in the large pot with 2 cups of water.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 2 hours. Stir occasionally to prevent burning.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into the glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Allow the mixture to cool and then store it in the refrigerator. Use 5-7 drops of the essential oil in a diffuser or mix it with a carrier oil for external use, after consulting a doctor for high blood pressure.

4. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains flavonoids and terpenoids, such as quercetin, kaempferol, and ginkgotoxin.

These bioactive constituents work together to help relax blood vessels and improve blood flow, reducing blood pressure. The flavonoids also have antioxidant properties, which help protect the body from cell damage caused by high blood pressure. The terpenoids in ginkgo biloba essential oils have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in blood vessels and improve blood flow.

By relaxing blood vessels and improving blood flow, ginkgo biloba essential oils can help lower blood pressure naturally.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves and 2 cups of water in a pot.
  2. Boil the water and then reduce heat. Add Ginkgo biloba leaves and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a bowl using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the liquid mixture and stir well.
  5. Store the Ginkgo biloba essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight lid. Use 2-3 drops per day to help with high blood pressure.

5. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains rosmarinic acid, a powerful antioxidant that helps to relax blood vessels and improve circulation.

This, in turn, can lead to a reduction in blood pressure. The diuretic properties of Melissa officinalis essential oils also help to remove excess fluids from the body, further reducing pressure on the blood vessels. Additionally, the essential oil's ability to reduce inflammation can also contribute to lowering blood pressure.

The antioxidant properties of rosmarinic acid help to protect the body against oxidative stress, which can also contribute to high blood pressure.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers and 2 cups of water. Make sure the flowers are fresh and organic.
  2. Combine the flowers and water in a saucepan and heat it over low fire for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 30 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Use a dark glass bottle and add 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut oil) to the strained mixture. Mix well.
  5. Store the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the bottle every day. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture again and use the Melissa officinalis essential oil for high blood pressure.

6. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains camphor, bornyl acetate, and borneol, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

These compounds help to relax and calm the nervous system, reducing stress and anxiety that can contribute to high blood pressure. The essential oil also contains antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to improve blood vessel function and reduce inflammation in the blood vessels. This can lead to improved blood flow and lower blood pressure.

Regular use of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils may also help to lower cortisol levels, which can contribute to high blood pressure.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan to 212°F (100°C) and add the fresh leaves.
  3. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 30-40 minutes, letting the leaves infuse into the water.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl, discarding the leaves.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a still or a steam distillation apparatus to extract the essential oil.

7. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata essential oils contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help relax blood vessels and improve blood flow. The flavonoids in Passiflora incarnata essential oils, specifically flavonoids like kaempferol and quercetin, can inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory molecules and reduce blood pressure. The alkaloids present in Passiflora incarnata essential oils, such as passiflorine and harmane, can also act as a natural sedative and help reduce stress and anxiety, which are common contributors to high blood pressure.

By relaxing blood vessels and reducing stress, Passiflora incarnata essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of high blood pressure.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Passiflora incarnata flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil). Make sure the flowers are clean and organic.
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Use a ratio of 1:3, meaning 1 part flowers to 3 parts carrier oil.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. This will help the flowers release their essence into the oil.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the essential oil to a small bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use a few drops of the oil in a carrier oil or lotion to help with high blood pressure.

8. Avena sativa

Avena sativa essential oils contains a number of bioactive constituents, including avenacosides, avenanthramides, and ferulic acid.

These compounds have been found to have a positive effect on blood vessels, causing them to relax and widen. This helps to lower blood pressure by improving blood flow and reducing resistance in the blood vessels. The avenanthramides in Avena sativa essential oils also have antioxidant properties, which help to protect the blood vessels from damage and inflammation.

By relaxing blood vessels and protecting them from damage, Avena sativa essential oils can help to lower high blood pressure and improve cardiovascular health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Avena sativa seeds and 4 cups of water. Use a clean glass container.
  2. Soak the Avena sativa seeds in water for 8 hours. Strain the mixture and discard the seeds.
  3. Transfer the liquid to a saucepan and boil it for 10 minutes. Reduce heat to low.
  4. Simmer the liquid for 2 hours, then let it cool. Collect the liquid in a clean glass container.
  5. Use a distillation kit or a simple steam distillation method to extract the essential oil from the liquid. The final product is 1-2 tablespoons of Avena sativa essential oil.

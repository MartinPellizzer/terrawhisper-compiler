Peripheral Arterial Occlusive Disease: Natural Remedies and Herbal Preparations

What are the causes of peripheral arterial occlusive disease?

Peripheral arterial occlusive disease (PAOD) occurs when the blood vessels in the legs and arms become narrowed or blocked.

This can happen due to a variety of reasons. One of the main causes is diabetes. When you have diabetes, your body either can't make enough insulin or can't use it properly, leading to high blood sugar levels. High blood sugar damages the blood vessels, making them more prone to narrowing and blockage. Another significant cause of PAOD is smoking. When you smoke, the chemicals in tobacco damage the blood vessels and reduce blood flow to your legs. This makes it harder for oxygen and nutrients to reach your muscles, leading to pain, cramps, and fatigue. Smoking also increases the risk of atherosclerosis, which is the buildup of plaque in the blood vessels.

Hypertension, or high blood pressure, is another major cause of PAOD. When your blood pressure is high, it puts extra strain on your blood vessels, making them more likely to narrow and block. This can lead to reduced blood flow to your legs, causing pain, weakness, and fatigue. Hyperlipidemia, or high levels of cholesterol in the blood, can also contribute to PAOD. When you have high levels of "bad" cholesterol, also known as LDL, it can build up in your blood vessels and cause them to narrow. This reduces blood flow to your legs, leading to pain, cramps, and fatigue. Lastly, atherosclerosis is a direct cause of PAOD. It occurs when plaque builds up in the blood vessels, causing them to narrow and block.

This can happen due to a combination of factors, including high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, and a diet high in saturated fats and sugars.

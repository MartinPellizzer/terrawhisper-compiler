Understanding Peripheral Arterial Occlusive Disease: Causes and Herbal Therapies

Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba is a plant that has been used for many years to help people with poor blood flow to their legs, a condition known as peripheral arterial occlusive disease (PAOD). This condition happens when the blood vessels in the legs become narrowed or blocked, making it hard for blood to flow. Ginkgo biloba works by improving blood flow and making it easier for oxygen and nutrients to reach the muscles in the legs. It does this by dilating the blood vessels and preventing them from becoming narrowed. This can help to reduce pain and discomfort in the legs, and also improve walking distance and overall daily activities. The active ingredients in Ginkgo biloba, flavonoids and terpenoids, help to break down blood clots and prevent new ones from forming, which can help to improve blood flow to the legs. By improving blood flow and reducing pain and discomfort, Ginkgo biloba can be an effective way to manage PAOD symptoms and improve quality of life.

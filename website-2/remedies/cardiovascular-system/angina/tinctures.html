Older Adults and Angina: 8 Herbal Tinctures for Symptom Relief

Angina is a common problem in older adults.

As we age, our blood vessels become stiffer and less flexible, making it harder for blood to flow freely to the heart. This can cause chest pain or discomfort, especially during physical activity or stress. Older adults with angina may experience shortness of breath, fatigue, and dizziness, which can limit their daily activities and affect their quality of life. They may need to avoid physical activity, change their diet, and take medication to manage their symptoms.

This can be frustrating and isolating, especially for those who value independence and mobility. Herbal tinctures have been used for centuries to help manage angina symptoms. They work by relaxing blood vessels, improving blood flow, and reducing inflammation. Some herbal tinctures have antioxidant properties, which help protect the heart from damage.

By reducing symptoms and improving blood flow, herbal tinctures can help older adults feel more comfortable and confident, allowing them to engage in physical activity and enjoy life without worrying about chest pain.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures have been studied for their potential benefits in reducing angina symptoms.

The main properties behind this effect are flavonoids and terpenoids, which are compounds found in the leaves of the Ginkgo biloba tree. These compounds improve blood flow to the heart by dilating blood vessels and reducing inflammation. This action is particularly helpful for people with angina, as it allows the heart to receive more oxygen and nutrients. If you're considering taking Ginkgo biloba tinctures for angina, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before starting treatment. They can help you determine the right dosage and ensure that it won't interact with any medications you're already taking. A typical dosage is 120-240 mg per day, taken in divided doses.

You can also look for products that have been standardized to contain 24% flavone glycosides and 6% terpene lactones. When taking Ginkgo biloba tinctures, you may experience improved circulation and reduced chest pain. Some people also report improved memory and cognitive function, although this is not directly related to angina relief. However, if you have any underlying medical conditions or are taking medications, you may experience side effects such as dizziness, nausea, or stomach upset. In rare cases, Ginkgo biloba can also interact with blood thinners, diabetes medications, or blood pressure medications. It's also important to note that Ginkgo biloba tinctures may not completely eliminate angina symptoms.

If you experience severe chest pain, shortness of breath, or other concerning symptoms, seek medical attention immediately.

2. Crataegus monogyna

Crataegus monogyna tinctures have been traditionally used to help alleviate symptoms of angina due to their ability to improve cardiovascular health.

The hawthorn plant, from which the tincture is derived, contains flavonoids, oligomeric proanthocyanidins (OPCs), and other bioactive compounds that may help to lower blood pressure, increase blood flow to the heart, and enhance the heart's pumping efficiency. Specifically, the flavonoids in Crataegus monogyna, such as quercetin and vitexin, have been shown to have vasodilatory effects, which can help to improve blood flow and reduce the workload on the heart. To try using Crataegus monogyna tinctures for angina, start by consulting with a healthcare professional to determine the best course of treatment. Begin by taking a small dose of the tincture (typically 20-30 drops in water) 2-3 times a day. Monitor your blood pressure and heart rate regularly, as the tincture may cause these to drop.

If you experience any adverse effects, such as dizziness or palpitations, reduce the dose or discontinue use. As you continue to take the tincture, pay attention to any changes in your angina symptoms, such as the frequency or severity of attacks. You may find that your symptoms improve over time, and you may be able to reduce your reliance on medications. It is likely that taking Crataegus monogyna tinctures will result in improved cardiovascular health and reduced symptoms of angina. As the flavonoids and OPCs in the tincture take effect, you may notice improved blood flow to the heart, reduced blood pressure, and enhanced heart function. This may allow you to engage in physical activity with less discomfort and may even reduce the need for medications to manage angina symptoms.

However, it is essential to continue working with your healthcare professional to monitor your condition and adjust your treatment plan as needed.

3. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng tinctures may help alleviate angina symptoms due to its ginsenoside content, particularly ginsenoside Rg1, Rg3, and Re, which have been shown to improve blood flow and lower blood pressure.

This is because ginsenosides have vasodilatory properties, meaning they help relax and widen blood vessels, reducing the heart's workload and increasing oxygen supply to the heart muscle. Additionally, Panax ginseng tinctures may help reduce inflammation and improve cardiovascular function by inhibiting the release of inflammatory mediators and enhancing nitric oxide production. If you're considering trying Panax ginseng tinctures for angina, start by consulting with a healthcare professional to discuss the potential benefits and risks, especially if you're already taking medications for angina.

A typical dosage for tincture form is 400-800 mg per day, but be sure to follow the recommended dosage on the label and adjust as directed by your healthcare provider. Monitor your symptoms closely, and if you experience any adverse effects or worsening of angina symptoms, discontinue use and consult with your healthcare provider. Taking Panax ginseng tinctures as directed may lead to improved exercise tolerance and reduced frequency and severity of angina episodes. However, it's essential to note that these effects may vary from person to person, and individual results may not be the same.

As with any supplement, be patient and consistent with your use, and monitor your body's response to the treatment.

8 Herbal Tinctures For Angina

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contains flavonoids, terpenoids, and bilobalide as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have vasodilatory and anti-inflammatory properties that help improve blood flow to the heart. The flavonoids and terpenoids in Ginkgo biloba tinctures also have antioxidant properties, which protect the cardiovascular system from oxidative stress. The bilobalide in Ginkgo biloba tinctures helps to reduce platelet aggregation, which can contribute to angina.

By improving blood flow and reducing platelet aggregation, Ginkgo biloba tinctures may help alleviate angina symptoms by providing more oxygen to the heart.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Ginkgo biloba leaves and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka.
  2. Chop the Ginkgo biloba leaves into small pieces and place them in a clean glass jar.
  3. Pour the vodka over the chopped Ginkgo biloba leaves, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into a clean glass bottle, discarding the solids. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture 2-3 times a day to help alleviate angina symptoms.

2. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng tinctures contains the bioactive constituents ginsenosides and ginsenols, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

These compounds work by improving blood flow to the heart, reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, and enhancing the function of the endothelium, the inner lining of blood vessels. By improving blood flow and reducing inflammation, ginsenosides and ginsenols help to alleviate the symptoms of angina, a condition characterized by reduced blood flow to the heart. Specifically, ginsenosides have been shown to dilate blood vessels and increase the production of nitric oxide, a potent vasodilator that helps to relax blood vessels and improve blood flow.

By addressing the underlying causes of angina, Panax ginseng tinctures may help to provide relief from angina symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Panax ginseng root, 2 cups of vodka (at least 80 proof), and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Place the dried Panax ginseng root in the glass jar.
  3. Pour vodka over the root, making sure all the root is covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle. Discard the root and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains the bioactive constituents gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce blood pressure and improve circulation.

The compounds also have a vasodilatory effect, which means they widen blood vessels and increase blood flow to the heart. This increased blood flow helps reduce the risk of angina by providing more oxygen to the heart muscle. The tinctures also contain zingerone, which has antioxidant properties that help protect the heart from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and increasing blood flow, Zingiber officinale tinctures may help alleviate symptoms of angina and improve heart health.

  1. Gather 250g of fresh Zingiber officinale root, 1 cup of 80% ethanol, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Chop the Zingiber officinale root into small pieces and pack them into the glass jar.
  3. Pour 1 cup of 80% ethanol over the chopped root, making sure the root is completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle, discarding the solids. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

4. Crataegus monogyna

Crataegus monogyna tinctures contains flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, and organic compounds like vitexin and isovitexin.

These flavonoids help to relax blood vessels and reduce inflammation, which can improve blood flow to the heart. The tannins in Crataegus monogyna tinctures also have a calming effect on the heart, reducing its workload and demand for oxygen. By improving blood flow and reducing the heart's workload, Crataegus monogyna tinctures may be beneficial in managing angina symptoms.

  1. Gather 500g of dried Crataegus monogyna leaves and flowers. Use a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Fill a cup with 95% vodka and pour it over the Crataegus monogyna mixture in the jar. Make sure the herbs are covered.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This is called maceration.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture in water 3 times a day as needed for angina symptoms.

5. Astragalus membranaceus

Astragalus membranaceus tinctures contains bioactive constituents like isoflavones, saponins, and polysaccharides.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in the blood vessels, which is a major contributor to angina. The saponins in Astragalus membranaceus also have a vasodilatory effect, which means they help widen blood vessels and improve blood flow to the heart. This improved blood flow can help reduce the frequency and severity of angina attacks by providing the heart with more oxygen and nutrients.

By reducing inflammation and improving blood flow, Astragalus membranaceus tinctures may help alleviate symptoms of angina.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Astragalus membranaceus roots and 2 cups of 80% vodka or ethanol in a clean glass container.
  2. Chop the roots into small pieces and add them to the container. Make sure the roots are completely covered by the vodka.
  3. Close the container and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the container every day to help the extraction process.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to small glass bottles and store them in a cool, dark place. Take 20-30 drops of the tincture 2-3 times a day as needed for angina relief.

6. Salvia miltiorrhiza

Salvia miltiorrhiza tinctures contains bioactive constituents like salvinorin A and tanshinone IIA, which have properties that can help alleviate angina symptoms.

Tanshinone IIA has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the blood vessels. These actions can help improve blood flow to the heart, reduce angina pain, and lower the risk of heart damage.

By improving blood flow and reducing inflammation, Salvia miltiorrhiza tinctures may provide relief for people experiencing angina.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent angina from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent angina in some people.

Certain teas, like hawthorn and ginkgo biloba, have been shown to improve blood flow and lower blood pressure. This can reduce the risk of angina by making it easier for the heart to get the oxygen it needs.

Regular tea consumption may also have a protective effect on blood vessels.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for angina every day?

Herbal teas for angina can be a helpful addition to your treatment plan, but it's essential to consume them in moderation.

Drinking them daily can interact with other medications, such as blood thinners, and affect their effectiveness.

It's also crucial to choose teas that are specifically formulated for angina and follow the recommended dosages.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in angina?

Herbal teas may help with angina symptoms, but it's hard to predict exactly when you'll feel relief.

Some people report improvements within a few days to a week, while others may notice changes after a few weeks.

It really depends on the individual and the specific tea being used.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for angina?

Drinking herbal tea for angina can be most helpful in the morning or before bedtime.

This allows the tea's effects to last throughout the day or night, potentially reducing angina symptoms.

Drinking it too late in the evening might interfere with a good night's sleep.

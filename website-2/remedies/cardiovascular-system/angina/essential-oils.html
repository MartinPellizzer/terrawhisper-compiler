What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have angina?

If you have angina, it's essential to be cautious when using herbal essential oils.

Some oils may worsen your condition by increasing heart rate or blood pressure. For example, 'Ephedra sinica' oil, also known as Ma Huang, contains ephedrine, a stimulant that can raise blood pressure and heart rate, which can be a concern for people with angina.

'Yohimbe' oil, derived from the bark of Pausinystalia johimbe, contains yohimbine, a compound that can also increase blood pressure and heart rate. Another oil to avoid is 'Cassia auriculata' oil, which may cause vasoconstriction (narrowing of blood vessels), potentially worsening angina symptoms.

Additionally, 'Clove' oil, extracted from Syzygium aromaticum, contains eugenol, a compound that can slow blood clotting and increase the risk of bleeding, which may be problematic if you have angina and are taking anticoagulant medications.

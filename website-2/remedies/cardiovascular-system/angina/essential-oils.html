1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in blood vessels.

This reduces the strain on the heart and improves blood flow, making it easier for the heart to pump blood. The bioactive constituents, including gingerol and shogaol, also have vasodilatory effects, causing blood vessels to widen and relax, further reducing the workload on the heart. The oils also contain other compounds like sesquiterpenes and sesquiterpene alcohols, which have antioxidant properties that help protect the heart from oxidative stress.

By reducing inflammation and improving blood flow, Zingiber officinale essential oils may be beneficial in helping to alleviate symptoms of angina.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil) in a clean, dark place.
  2. Chop the ginger roots into small pieces and put them in a clean glass jar with a lid.
  3. Add 1 cup of carrier oil to the jar with the ginger pieces and close the lid.
  4. Store the jar in a dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the ginger to infuse into the oil.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the ginger pieces and use the infused oil as needed.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains the bioactive constituents borneol, camphor, bornyl acetate, and pinene.

These compounds have vasodilating properties, which help to widen blood vessels and improve blood flow to the heart. This increased blood flow can reduce the frequency and severity of angina symptoms by providing the heart muscle with the oxygen and nutrients it needs. The anti-inflammatory properties of pinene also help to reduce inflammation in the blood vessels, further improving blood flow and reducing the risk of angina.

By using Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils, people with angina may experience relief from symptoms and improve their overall cardiovascular health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and stems. Use organic and pesticide-free plants.
  2. Wash the leaves and stems with 2 cups of cold water to remove dirt and impurities.
  3. Use a steam distillation apparatus or a home distillation kit to extract the essential oil from the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and stems. Heat the apparatus to 212°F (100°C) and collect the distilled oil.
  4. Filter the essential oil through a coffee filter or a cheesecloth to remove any impurities. Collect the clear oil in a clean glass bottle.
  5. Store the Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) for topical application to help alleviate angina symptoms.

By relaxing blood vessels and improving circulation, Cinnamomum verum essential oils can help to alleviate angina symptoms and promote overall heart health.

4. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains a compound called cineole, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in the blood vessels.

Cineole also has a vasodilating effect, which means it widens the blood vessels and improves blood flow to the heart, helping to alleviate angina symptoms. Another compound, eucalyptol, has a calming effect on the nervous system, reducing stress and anxiety that can exacerbate angina. The expectorant properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils also help to thin mucus and improve breathing, reducing the strain on the heart.

By reducing inflammation, improving blood flow, and thinning mucus, eucalyptus globulus essential oils may help alleviate angina symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a glass jar, and a clean cheesecloth.
  2. Combine the leaves and 2 cups of water in a pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth into the glass jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Let the mixture sit for 2 hours to allow the essential oils to separate and rise to the top.
  5. Carefully pour the essential oils into a separate container, leaving behind the water. Store the oil in a cool, dark place.

5. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains compounds like bilobalide and ginkgolides, which help to improve blood flow to the heart.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in blood vessels. The improved blood flow and reduced inflammation help to lower blood pressure and improve the supply of oxygen to the heart. This can help to alleviate symptoms of angina, such as chest pain and shortness of breath.

The improved circulation and reduced inflammation also help to reduce the risk of heart disease and promote overall cardiovascular health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves and 2 cups of carrier oil, such as jojoba or sweet almond oil.
  2. Wash the Ginkgo biloba leaves with water and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Combine the Ginkgo biloba leaves and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Let it sit for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and reserve the liquid.
  5. Bottle the Ginkgo biloba essential oil in dark glass bottles and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops of the oil per day to help alleviate angina symptoms.

6. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone, which are bioactive constituents responsible for its therapeutic effects.

The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of these compounds help reduce the inflammation and oxidative stress in blood vessels, making them more flexible and less prone to spasms. The sedative and relaxant properties of valerian essential oil also help lower blood pressure and promote a calm state, which is beneficial for individuals with angina. Additionally, valerian essential oil's vasodilatory properties help dilate blood vessels, improving blood flow and reducing the workload on the heart.

By reducing inflammation, lowering blood pressure, and promoting vasodilation, valerian essential oil can help alleviate angina symptoms by reducing the frequency and severity of chest pain episodes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Valeriana officinalis roots, 2 cups of water, and a steam distillation apparatus.
  2. Wash the roots with water, then chop them into small pieces and put them in the distillation apparatus.
  3. Add 2 cups of water to the distillation apparatus, then heat the mixture to 212°F (100°C) and hold it there.
  4. Collect the steam that rises from the distillation apparatus and pass it through a condenser to turn it back into liquid.
  5. Filter the liquid to remove any impurities and collect the resulting essential oil in a separate container.

7. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

The essential oil has vasodilatory effects, which can help to widen blood vessels and improve blood flow to the heart. This increased blood flow can help to alleviate the symptoms of angina, reducing the frequency and severity of chest pain episodes. The antioxidant properties of linalool and linalyl acetate can also help to protect the heart from oxidative stress and inflammation, further reducing the risk of angina.

By promoting relaxation and reducing stress, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can also help to manage the emotional component of angina.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat, then add the lavender flowers. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Combine the strained liquid with 1/4 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil) in a separate bowl.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 hours, then strain it again through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve to get the essential oil. Store it in a dark glass bottle.

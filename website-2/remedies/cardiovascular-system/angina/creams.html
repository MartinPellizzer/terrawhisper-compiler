9 Herbal Creams For Angina

Herbal creams and teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate symptoms of angina.

Angina is a type of chest pain caused by reduced blood flow to the heart. When you drink herbal teas, the active compounds in the herbs are absorbed into your bloodstream and can help to relax blood vessels, improving blood flow and reducing pain. This is particularly beneficial for people with angina, as it can help to alleviate chest pain and improve quality of life. Two examples of herbs that have been traditionally used to help with angina are Zingiber officinale, also known as ginger, and Panax ginseng. Ginger contains a compound called gingerol, which has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce pain and swelling. Ginseng, on the other hand, is believed to improve blood flow and increase energy levels. Drinking a cup of ginger tea or taking a ginseng supplement may help to alleviate angina symptoms and improve overall cardiovascular health.

Another herb that has been used to help with angina is Ginkgo biloba. Ginkgo biloba contains a compound called flavonoids, which can help to improve blood flow and reduce inflammation. Ginkgo biloba has been shown to be effective in reducing angina symptoms and improving quality of life. It's often used in combination with other herbs to enhance its effects. When you use herbal creams and teas to help with angina, you may experience a range of benefits. These can include reduced chest pain, improved energy levels, and a reduction in inflammation. You may also find that you are able to engage in physical activities without experiencing pain or discomfort.

Additionally, herbal creams and teas are often natural and non-invasive, making them a popular choice for people looking for alternative treatments.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains a high concentration of gingerols and shogaols, active constituents that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation in the blood vessels, which can contribute to angina symptoms. The anti-inflammatory properties also help to relax the muscles in the blood vessels, improving blood flow and reducing blood pressure. Additionally, gingerols and shogaols have a vasodilatory effect, which helps to widen blood vessels and improve oxygen delivery to the heart muscle.

This can help to alleviate the symptoms of angina by reducing the frequency and severity of angina attacks.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of glycerin, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the distilled water, glycerin, and vitamin E oil to the melted beeswax. Stir until well combined.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Add 1 teaspoon of Zingiber officinale extract (ginger extract) and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool completely. Store the creams in a cool, dry place for up to 6 months.

2. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng creams contains ginsenosides, ginsenone, and ginsenolic acid as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have vasodilatory properties, which help to widen blood vessels and improve blood circulation, a key factor in relieving angina symptoms. The ginsenosides in Panax ginseng creams also have anti-inflammatory properties, reducing inflammation in the blood vessels and improving blood flow to the heart. The ginsenosides' antioxidant properties further help to protect the heart from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to angina.

By improving blood circulation and protecting the heart, Panax ginseng creams may be beneficial in managing angina symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Panax ginseng extract, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Add Panax ginseng extract and vitamin E oil, and stir until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, your Panax ginseng cream is ready to use. Apply topically to affected areas as needed.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavoglycosides, bilobalide, and ginkgolides.

These active constituents have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The flavoglycosides help to improve blood flow by preventing platelet aggregation and reducing blood viscosity. This is beneficial for people with angina, as improved blood flow to the heart muscle can increase oxygen supply and reduce symptoms.

Bilobalide has a vasodilatory effect, which helps to widen blood vessels and further improve blood flow to the heart.

  1. Mix 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of beeswax in a bowl.
  2. Melt the mixture in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of Ginkgo biloba extract and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil to the melted mixture.
  4. Stir well and let the mixture cool until it reaches 120°F to 130°F (49°C to 54°C).
  5. Pour the mixture into a container, let it cool and solidify, then apply a thin layer to the affected area as needed.

4. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains a compound called curcumin, which is a powerful antioxidant and anti-inflammatory.

Curcumin helps to reduce inflammation in the blood vessels, improving blood flow to the heart and reducing the risk of angina. It also has a blood-thinning effect, preventing platelets from clumping together and causing a blockage in the coronary arteries. Turmeric's other active constituent, demethoxycurcumin, has been shown to have anti-platelet properties, further reducing the risk of blood clots.

By reducing inflammation and preventing blood clots, Curcuma longa creams may help alleviate symptoms of angina and improve cardiovascular health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder in a saucepan.
  2. Melt the coconut oil, beeswax, and shea butter in the saucepan over low heat, stirring until smooth.
  3. Add the turmeric powder to the melted mixture and stir well.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool slightly before pouring the mixture into a glass jar.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify completely before applying it to affected areas as needed.

5. Salvia miltiorrhiza

Salvia miltiorrhiza creams contains active constituents like tanshinone, salvianolic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce inflammation in blood vessels and protect them from damage. By doing so, they improve blood flow to the heart, reducing the frequency and severity of angina attacks. The active compounds in Salvia miltiorrhiza also help lower blood pressure and improve the heart's pumping efficiency, further alleviating angina symptoms.

Regular use of Salvia miltiorrhiza creams may lead to improved cardiovascular health and reduced reliance on medication.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Salvia miltiorrhiza root, 2 cups of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Dry the dried root in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 2-3 hours or until it becomes brittle.
  3. Grind the dried root into powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
  4. In a double boiler, melt the coconut oil and beeswax. Add the ground root powder and mix well.
  5. Remove from heat and let it cool. Once solidified, scoop out the cream into a container for use as needed.

6. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, a powerful antioxidant that helps reduce inflammation and improve blood flow to the heart.

This is beneficial for people with angina, a condition where the heart doesn't get enough blood. The rosmarinic acid in Rosmarinus officinalis creams also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce the strain on the heart. Additionally, the camphor and borneol in the cream can help relax blood vessels and improve circulation, further reducing the risk of angina attacks.

By improving blood flow and reducing inflammation, Rosmarinus officinalis creams can help alleviate the symptoms of angina and promote a healthier cardiovascular system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, and 2 tablespoons of rosemary essential oil.
  2. Melt the beeswax and coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Once melted, add the distilled water and stir until well combined.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and add the rosemary essential oil. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into small jars or containers and let it cool and solidify before use. Apply a small amount to the affected area as needed.

7. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have vasodilatory properties, which help to widen blood vessels and improve blood flow to the heart. By increasing blood flow, Lavandula angustifolia creams can help to reduce the frequency and severity of angina attacks. The anti-inflammatory properties of linalool also help to reduce inflammation in the blood vessels, which can contribute to angina.

Regular use of Lavandula angustifolia creams may help to alleviate symptoms of angina by improving blood flow and reducing inflammation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of coconut oil in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in the saucepan over low heat until it reaches 160°F (71°C).
  3. Add the dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the coconut oil and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the coconut oil through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discarding the Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  5. Mix 1 tablespoon of beeswax, 1 tablespoon of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of the infused coconut oil in a separate bowl. Heat the mixture in a double boiler until the beeswax and shea butter melt.

8. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum creams contains hyperforin and hypericin, two active constituents that help reduce angina symptoms.

Hyperforin has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling in blood vessels, improving blood flow to the heart. Hypericin has antioxidant properties that protect the heart from damage caused by free radicals. By reducing inflammation and protecting the heart, Hypericum perforatum creams may help alleviate angina symptoms.

This can lead to a decrease in chest pain and an improvement in overall heart function.

  2. Melt the beeswax and coconut oil in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add the shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in the Hypericum perforatum oil.

9. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds help reduce inflammation and improve blood flow to the heart, which is beneficial for people with angina. Alkylamides, in particular, have a relaxing effect on blood vessels, allowing for more oxygen-rich blood to reach the heart. The antioxidant properties of caffeic acid and rosmarinic acid also protect the heart from damage caused by free radicals.

By improving blood flow and reducing inflammation, Echinacea purpurea creams may help alleviate symptoms of angina and promote heart health.

  1. Gather 250g of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the melted mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, and discard the solids.
  5. Mix in the vitamin E oil and pour the mixture into small containers to cool and set.

Consuming herbal teas for angina daily can be beneficial, but it's essential to be cautious.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in angina?

Herbal teas may help with angina symptoms, but it's hard to say exactly how long it takes to see results.

Some people notice a difference within a week or two, while others may need to drink them for a month or more to feel the effects.

It really depends on the person and the specific tea.

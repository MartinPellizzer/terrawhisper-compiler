Gum Infection in Elderly: 10 Herbal Tinctures for Relief

As people age, their immune systems weaken, making them more prone to gum infections.

This is because gum disease, also known as periodontitis, can be a sign of a larger issue - a weakened immune system. When elderly people have gum infections, it can cause them a lot of discomfort and pain, especially when eating, drinking, and even speaking. This can lead to difficulty swallowing, changes in appetite, and even weight loss. In addition, gum infections can cause bad breath and make teeth loose, which can be very embarrassing and affect their self-confidence.

This can further exacerbate social isolation, as they become hesitant to interact with others due to their condition. Furthermore, untreated gum infections can even lead to serious health issues, such as heart disease and diabetes. This is because the bacteria from the infection can enter the bloodstream and travel to other parts of the body, causing inflammation and damage. Given the severity of these consequences, finding an effective treatment is crucial. Herbal tinctures can be a helpful option for treating gum infections in the elderly.

They contain concentrated extracts of plants that have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help combat the infection and alleviate symptoms.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures have been traditionally used to help with gum infections, specifically those caused by bacteria or inflammation.

This is because Echinacea purpurea contains compounds like alkylamides and glycosides, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These properties help to reduce swelling and fight off the underlying infection, allowing the gum tissue to heal.

If you're experiencing a gum infection, you can try taking Echinacea purpurea tinctures by mixing 20-30 drops with water and swishing it around your mouth for a few minutes before spitting it out. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day, ideally after brushing your teeth. It's essential to note that this is not a replacement for dental care, but rather a complementary treatment to help alleviate symptoms.

Some people may experience a reduction in swelling and pain within a few days, while others may see improvements in their gum health after a week or two of consistent use.

Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures are a popular natural remedy for gum infections.

When taken as a tincture, usually in a dose of 10-20 drops, three to four times a day, these compounds can help alleviate symptoms like pain, swelling, and redness associated with gum infections. To get the best results, swish the tincture around your mouth for 30 seconds to 1 minute before spitting it out, and follow up with regular brushing and flossing to maintain good oral hygiene.

By incorporating Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures into your oral care routine, you can expect to see improvements in your gum health within a few days to a week, such as reduced inflammation, less bleeding, and a decrease in pain.

What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have gum infection?

Another oil to avoid is Birch (Betula alba), as it also contains methyl salicylate and can cause bleeding or irritation in the gums. Also, be cautious with Geranium (Pelargonium graveolens) and Bergamot (Citrus aurantium bergamia), as they can stimulate the gums and make them more sensitive. Finally, avoid Oregano (Origanum vulgare) and Clove (Santalum album) as they contain compounds that can irritate the gums and make the infection worse.

These oils can be used for other health issues, but it's best to consult a healthcare professional before using them if you have a gum infection.

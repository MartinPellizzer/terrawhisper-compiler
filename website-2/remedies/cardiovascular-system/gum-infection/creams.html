1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contains bioactive constituents like cineole, terpinen-4-ol, and limonene.

These compounds have antimicrobial properties that help combat bacterial growth in gum infections. Cineole and terpinen-4-ol also have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and pain in the infected area. Additionally, the antioxidants in Melaleuca alternifolia creams help protect the gums from further damage caused by free radicals.

By using these creams, the antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties can help promote healing and reduce the severity of gum infections.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil, and a small pot for melting.
  2. Melt beeswax and coconut oil in the small pot over low heat. Stir until fully melted.
  3. Add shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the pot from heat and let it cool slightly. Add distilled water and stir until combined.
  5. Add Melaleuca alternifolia essential oil and stir well. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool completely before use.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain in gum infections.

These compounds also have antimicrobial properties, which inhibit the growth of bacteria that cause gum infections. The phenolic compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, have antioxidant properties that help protect the gums from damage caused by free radicals. The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of Zingiber officinale creams help to reduce the severity of gum infections and promote healing.

The combination of these properties makes Zingiber officinale creams a potentially effective treatment for gum infections.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 1 cup of coconut oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of grated fresh ginger root to the melted coconut oil and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture and then add the distilled water and 2 tablespoons of beeswax to the mixture.
  5. Whisk the mixture until the beeswax is fully incorporated and the mixture has cooled and thickened.

3. Echinacea purpurea

5. Salvia officinalis

These compounds help reduce swelling and fight off bacteria that cause gum infections. The antimicrobial properties of ursolic acid and triterpenoids inhibit the growth of bacteria such as Streptococcus and Escherichia, which are commonly associated with gum infections.   5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool. Once set, your Salvia officinalis cream is ready to use for gum infection.

Gum Infections in Diabetics: 10 Herbal Creams for Treatment

People with diabetes are at higher risk of getting gum infections, also known as periodontal disease.

This is because high blood sugar levels can cause infections in the mouth, making it harder for the body to fight off germs. If left untreated, gum infections can lead to painful abscesses, loose teeth, and even tooth loss. This can affect a person's self-confidence and make everyday tasks like eating and speaking difficult. Diabetics may also have to deal with bad breath, swollen gums, and tender teeth. They may need to visit the dentist more often and use special mouthwashes and toothpaste to keep their teeth and gums healthy.

Gum infections can also make it harder for diabetics to control their blood sugar levels, creating a vicious cycle. When teeth and gums are not healthy, it's harder to eat nutritious food, which is essential for managing diabetes. This can lead to other health problems, like kidney disease or nerve damage. To treat gum infections, herbal creams have been shown to be effective. These creams contain natural ingredients that have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, which help to reduce swelling and kill germs in the mouth.

They can be applied directly to the affected area, providing quick relief from pain and discomfort.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams, also known as tea tree oil creams, have been traditionally used to help manage gum infections.

The active compound in Melaleuca alternifolia, called terpinen-4-ol, has antimicrobial properties that help combat the bacteria causing the infection. This compound also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce swelling and pain in the affected area. The cream also contains other constituents like cineole and limonene, which contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. To use Melaleuca alternifolia creams for gum infection, gently apply a small amount to the affected area with a clean finger. You can use a cotton swab to apply the cream if you need to target a specific area.

It's essential to follow the product instructions and start with a small amount to test for any allergic reactions. You can use the cream 2-3 times a day, or as directed by your dentist or healthcare provider. When using Melaleuca alternifolia creams for gum infection, you may notice a reduction in swelling and pain within a few days. The bacteria causing the infection may start to clear, and the affected area may begin to heal. However, it's essential to continue using the cream as directed and to maintain good oral hygiene practices, such as regular brushing and flossing, to prevent the infection from recurring.

You may also want to schedule a follow-up appointment with your dentist to monitor the progress of the infection and ensure it has fully cleared.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams are a topical treatment that may help with gum infections.

This is because Echinacea purpurea contains properties and constituents such as alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. These compounds can help reduce swelling and fight off bacteria that cause gum infections, including Streptococcus mutans and Escherichia coli. If you're experiencing a gum infection, you can try using Echinacea purpurea cream as a topical treatment. To do this, apply a small amount of the cream directly to the affected area using a cotton swab. Gently massage the cream into the gums using a circular motion.

Repeat this process 2-3 times a day, or as directed on the packaging. You can also use the cream in combination with other treatments, such as saltwater rinses and regular dental check-ups. When using Echinacea purpurea cream for gum infections, you can expect to see a reduction in swelling and pain within a few days. The cream may also help prevent further infection and promote healing of the affected area. However, it's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea cream is not a replacement for regular dental care.

Continue to visit your dentist regularly for check-ups and follow any treatment plans they recommend.

To use Aloe barbadensis creams for gum infection, first, rinse your mouth with warm water to remove any debris. Then, apply a thin layer of the cream to the affected area using your finger. Gently massage the cream into the gums using circular motions.

Leave the cream on for 15-30 minutes before spitting it out. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day for best results. As you continue to use Aloe barbadensis creams for gum infection, you may start to notice a reduction in swelling and pain in the gums.

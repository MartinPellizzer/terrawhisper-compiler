7 Herbal Teas For Gum Infection

Gum chewers often suffer from gum infections due to the constant irritation and bacteria buildup in their mouths.

The constant influx of sugar and other substances from the gum can lead to inflammation and infection, causing pain, swelling, and discomfort. This can make everyday activities like eating, talking, and even sleeping difficult. The pain can be severe, and in some cases, it can even lead to tooth loss if left untreated. People who chew gum regularly may experience bad breath, tender gums, and even bleeding gums. This can be embarrassing and affect their self-confidence.

To make matters worse, the infection can spread to other parts of the mouth, causing more problems. When gum chewers have a gum infection, they often try various treatments to alleviate the pain and discomfort. However, many of these treatments can have side effects or be expensive. This is where herbal teas come in. These teas are made from plants that have been used for centuries to treat various health issues, including gum infections. They work by reducing inflammation, fighting bacteria, and soothing the gums.

By drinking these teas regularly, gum chewers can help treat their gum infections naturally and without the harsh chemicals found in some medications.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains compounds like eucalyptol, which has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help reduce swelling and fight bacteria that cause gum infections.

The tea also contains flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties that help protect the gums from damage. When used as a mouthwash or gargle, Eucalyptus globulus tea can help loosen and remove plaque, a sticky film that forms on teeth and can lead to gum infections. To use Eucalyptus globulus tea for gum infection, make a tea by steeping one teaspoon of dried leaves in a cup of hot water for 5-7 minutes, then let it cool.

Swish the tea around in your mouth for 30 seconds to 1 minute, then spit it out. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day. After using Eucalyptus globulus tea, you may notice a reduction in gum inflammation and pain, and your gums may look healthier.

However, if your gum infection is severe, you should still consult a dentist for proper treatment.

Taking Echinacea purpurea tea may lead to reduced swelling and pain, and possibly even a decrease in the severity of the infection, allowing the gum tissue to heal faster.

3. Salvia officinalis

When you start using Salvia officinalis teas for gum infection, you may notice a reduction in swelling and pain within a few days.

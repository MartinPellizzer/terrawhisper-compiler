Gum infection remedies for frequent gum chewers

Gum chewers often suffer from gum infections due to the constant irritation and bacteria buildup in their mouths.

The constant influx of sugar and other substances from the gum can lead to inflammation and infection, causing pain, swelling, and discomfort. This can make everyday activities like eating, talking, and even sleeping difficult. The pain can be severe, and in some cases, it can even lead to tooth loss if left untreated. People who chew gum regularly may experience bad breath, tender gums, and even bleeding gums. This can be embarrassing and affect their self-confidence.

To make matters worse, the infection can spread to other parts of the mouth, causing more problems. When gum chewers have a gum infection, they often try various treatments to alleviate the pain and discomfort. However, many of these treatments can have side effects or be expensive. This is where herbal teas come in. These teas are made from plants that have been used for centuries to treat various health issues, including gum infections. They work by reducing inflammation, fighting bacteria, and soothing the gums.

By drinking these teas regularly, gum chewers can help treat their gum infections naturally and without the harsh chemicals found in some medications.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas have been traditionally used to help with gum infections due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant contains compounds like alkylamides, glycosides, and polyphenols, which have been shown to reduce inflammation and fight off bacteria that cause infections. When taken as a tea, these constituents can help to ease gum swelling and pain, while also promoting a healthy balance of bacteria in the mouth. To try Echinacea purpurea teas for gum infection, start by brewing one teaspoon of dried flowers or roots in one cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it three times a day, ideally after meals. It's also essential to maintain good oral hygiene by brushing and flossing regularly, and visiting your dentist for check-ups and cleanings. As you take Echinacea purpurea teas, you may start to notice a reduction in gum pain and inflammation, making it easier to eat and speak comfortably. Your gums may also appear healthier, with less redness and swelling.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, consult your dentist or healthcare provider for further guidance.

2. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate gum infections, also known as periodontal disease.

The reason they're effective is due to the presence of properties such as tannins, flavonoids, and rosmarinic acid, which have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory effects. These constituents help combat the bacteria responsible for gum infections, such as Streptococcus and Porphyromonas, and reduce swelling and redness in the gums. To use Salvia officinalis teas for gum infections, you can try the following: - Steep 1 teaspoon of dried Salvia officinalis leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. - Strain the liquid and discard the leaves.

- Use the tea as a mouthwash, swishing it around your mouth for 30 seconds to 1 minute before spitting it out. - Repeat this process 2-3 times a day. By taking these actions, you may experience a reduction in gum inflammation, a decrease in bleeding while brushing or flossing, and a decrease in bad breath. The antibacterial properties of the tea may help eliminate the underlying cause of the gum infection, promoting a healthier oral environment.

However, it's essential to note that Salvia officinalis teas are not a replacement for professional dental care.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, have been used for centuries to help alleviate symptoms of gum infections.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that can help reduce swelling and fight off the infection. The antiseptic properties of ginger can also help to prevent the spread of infection and promote healing. When making ginger tea for gum infection, it's recommended to steep 1-2 teaspoons of fresh ginger in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Then, strain the liquid and let it cool before using it as a mouthwash or gargling solution. The likely outcomes of taking this action include reduced inflammation, pain relief, and a decrease in the severity of the gum infection. Additionally, the antibacterial properties of ginger may help to prevent future infections.

It's essential to note that while ginger tea can be a helpful addition to your oral care routine, it's not a substitute for regular dental check-ups and professional treatment.

Eucalyptus globulus teas have been used for centuries to help with gum infections.

This is because the tea contains compounds like eucalyptol and tannins, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These properties help to reduce the growth of bacteria that cause gum infections, and also reduce swelling and pain in the gums. When you drink eucalyptus globulus tea, the compounds in the tea can reach your gums and start to work.

To use eucalyptus globulus tea to help with a gum infection, you can try the following: drink 1-2 cups of the tea a day, and also use it as a mouthwash by swishing the tea in your mouth for 30 seconds before spitting it out. It's also a good idea to visit your dentist regularly to get your teeth cleaned and to check on the health of your gums. If you drink eucalyptus globulus tea regularly, you may notice that your gum infection starts to clear up, and you may feel less pain and discomfort in your gums. You may also notice that your gums start to look and feel healthier, with less redness and swelling.

However, it's still important to visit your dentist regularly to get your teeth cleaned and to check on the health of your gums.

5. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia teas, also known as tea tree oil tea, has been traditionally used to help with gum infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The tea contains compounds like cymene, limonene, and terpinen-4-ol, which have been shown to combat bacteria and fungi that can cause gum infections, such as Streptococcus mutans and Candida albicans. When you drink Melaleuca alternifolia tea, the active compounds are absorbed into your bloodstream and then reach your gums, where they help to reduce swelling and inflammation, and kill off the bacteria and fungi that are causing the infection. To use Melaleuca alternifolia tea for gum infection, you can make a tea by steeping 5-10 drops of tea tree oil in a cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes. Then, strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day. You can also add a pinch of salt and a squeeze of lemon juice to the tea to help reduce inflammation and kill off bacteria.

As you drink the tea, you may start to feel a reduction in pain and swelling in your gums, and your gum infection may start to heal. The tea may also help to prevent future gum infections by reducing the amount of bacteria in your mouth. It's likely that you will start to notice an improvement in your gum infection within a few days of drinking the tea. Your gum pain and swelling may decrease, and you may see a reduction in the amount of pus or discharge from the infected area. However, it's also possible that the infection may take longer to clear up, especially if it's severe. In this case, you may need to continue drinking the tea for several weeks to ensure that the infection is fully cleared.

It's also important to note that Melaleuca alternifolia tea may interact with certain medications or worsen certain health conditions, so you should consult with a healthcare professional before using it to treat your gum infection.

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas, commonly known as peppermint tea, have been used for centuries to help alleviate gum infections due to their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The tea contains compounds like menthol, menthone, and limonene, which have been shown to reduce bacteria and inflammation in the mouth. When consumed as a tea, these compounds help to reduce the severity of gum infections by killing bacteria that cause them, such as Porphyromonas gingivalis and Streptococcus mutans. To use peppermint tea for gum infection, start by steeping a teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes.

Strain the tea and let it cool before swishing it in your mouth for 30 seconds to 1 minute, 2-3 times a day. You can also apply a cold compress or ice pack to the affected area to reduce swelling and ease pain. As you start taking peppermint tea, you may experience a reduction in gum inflammation and pain, as well as a decrease in the severity of your gum infection. You may also notice a fresh, clean taste in your mouth and a reduction in bad breath.

However, if your gum infection is severe, it's essential to consult with a dentist or healthcare professional for proper treatment.

7. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium teas, also known as yarrow tea, may be helpful in treating gum infection due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The tea contains compounds like thujone and flavonoids, which have been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria and reduce inflammation in the mouth. Specifically, Achillea millefolium tea may help combat gum infections by preventing the spread of bacteria and reducing swelling and pain in the affected area. To use Achillea millefolium tea for gum infection, you can make a strong brew by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried yarrow leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Let the tea cool down, then use it as a mouthwash or gargle several times a day.

You can also add a pinch of salt to the tea to help reduce swelling and kill bacteria. It's essential to note that while Achillea millefolium tea may be helpful in treating gum infection, it's not a substitute for medical treatment. If you have a severe gum infection, you should consult a dentist or healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment. Taking Achillea millefolium tea as a treatment for gum infection may lead to a reduction in swelling and pain, a decrease in bacterial growth, and an improvement in overall oral health. However, if you have a severe gum infection, you may need to combine the tea with conventional medical treatment to achieve the best results.

Additionally, if you experience any discomfort or pain while using the tea, you should discontinue use and consult a healthcare professional.

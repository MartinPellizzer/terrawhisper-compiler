1. Hamamelis virginiana

Hamamelis virginiana tinctures contains bioactive constituents like tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling and inflammation in the veins, making it a useful treatment for varicose veins. The tannins in Hamamelis virginiana have a vasoconstrictive effect, which means they can help to narrow the blood vessels and reduce blood flow to the affected area. This can help to reduce the pressure on the veins and alleviate symptoms of varicose veins.

The flavonoids in Hamamelis virginiana also have a protective effect on the blood vessels, helping to improve their tone and reduce the risk of further damage.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Hamamelis virginiana leaves and flowers. You can buy them from a health food store or online.
  2. Combine the dried plant material with 3 cups of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass jar. Make sure the plant material is fully covered by the vodka.
  3. Close the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will help the plant's properties mix with the vodka.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the plant material.
  5. Transfer the tincture to small glass bottles and store them in the fridge. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture, 2-3 times a day, mixed with water or taken sublingually.

2. Ruscus aculeatus

Ruscus aculeatus tinctures contains flavonoids and saponins, which are active compounds that help reduce inflammation and improve blood circulation.

Flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant properties that protect the walls of blood vessels from damage. Saponins, like ruscogenin, help strengthen blood vessel walls, reducing the risk of varicose veins. Ruscus aculeatus tinctures also contains phenolic acids and glycosides, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

By reducing inflammation and improving blood circulation, Ruscus aculeatus tinctures can help alleviate symptoms of varicose veins, such as pain and swelling.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Ruscus aculeatus leaves and flowers. Wear gloves to protect skin from thorns.
  2. Chop the leaves and flowers coarsely in a bowl.
  3. Use a 1:5 ratio (1 part Ruscus to 5 parts solvent). Mix 1/4 cup of chopped Ruscus with 1 1/4 cups of 80% ethanol (vodka or rum work well). Stir well.
  4. Store the mixture in a glass jar with a lid, away from light. Shake the jar every day for 2 weeks to help the solvent extract the plant's properties.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into another glass jar, discarding the solids. Transfer the tincture to small dropper bottles for use.

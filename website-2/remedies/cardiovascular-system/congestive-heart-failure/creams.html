What herbal creams you should avoid if you have congestive heart failure?

If you have congestive heart failure, it's best to avoid certain herbal creams that can affect your heart rate or blood pressure.

One such herb is Yohimbe, which can increase heart rate and blood pressure. Yohimbe can also cause blood vessels to constrict, which can make it harder for the heart to pump blood. Another herb to avoid is Ephedra, which can also raise blood pressure and heart rate. This can be especially problematic for people with congestive heart failure, as it can worsen the condition.

Another herb to steer clear of is Ephedra's close relative, Ma Huang (Ephedra sinica), which contains a similar stimulant called ephedrine. Like Ephedra, Ma Huang can increase heart rate and blood pressure, making it difficult for the heart to function properly in people with congestive heart failure. Additionally, avoid creams containing Ephedra's other relative, Yew (Taxus spp. ), which contains a toxic compound called taxine.

While not as directly related to heart rate or blood pressure, taxine can be toxic and can interact with medications used to treat congestive heart failure.

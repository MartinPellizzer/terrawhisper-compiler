7 Herbal Essential Oils For Congestive Heart Failure

Herbal essential oils can be a helpful addition to your treatment plan if you're living with congestive heart failure.

Congestive heart failure happens when your heart can't pump enough blood to meet your body's needs, leading to fluid buildup in your lungs and other parts of your body. Herbal teas, made from plants like Ginkgo biloba, Rosmarinus officinalis (Rosemary), and Zingiber officinale (Ginger), may help in several ways. Ginkgo biloba can improve blood flow and lower blood pressure, which can ease the strain on your heart. Rosemary has natural anti-inflammatory properties that may help reduce swelling in your lungs and other areas. Ginger has anti-inflammatory properties as well and can also help ease digestive issues that often come with congestive heart failure.

Herbal teas can also help reduce stress and anxiety, which can be a significant factor in heart health. When you're feeling calm and relaxed, your heart doesn't have to work as hard to pump blood. Drinking herbal teas can become a calming routine that you can enjoy every day. To make a tea, simply steep a teaspoon of dried herbs in a cup of hot water for a few minutes, then strain and enjoy. You can also add honey or lemon to taste. Some herbal teas, like those made from Ginkgo biloba and Ginger, can be taken in supplement form if you prefer not to drink tea.

However, it's essential to talk to your doctor before adding any new supplements to your routine.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains flavonoids, bilobalide, and ginkgolides as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the body that can contribute to congestive heart failure. The flavonoids in ginkgo biloba essential oils also improve blood flow and lower blood pressure, making it easier for the heart to pump blood. Bilobalide and ginkgolides help to relax blood vessels and improve the function of the heart, reducing the risk of heart failure.

By improving blood flow, reducing blood pressure, and relaxing blood vessels, ginkgo biloba essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of congestive heart failure.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves, a glass jar with a lid, a cheesecloth, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Dry the Ginkgo biloba leaves in a low-temperature oven (150°F - 200°F) for 2-3 hours or air-dry them for 7-10 days.
  3. Combine 1/4 cup of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves with 2 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-6 weeks, shaking the jar daily. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth into another container.
  5. Discard the solids and transfer the Ginkgo biloba essential oil to a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Store it in a cool, dark place.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains a compound called carnosic acid, which is known for its antioxidant properties.

This antioxidant property helps to reduce oxidative stress, a major contributor to congestive heart failure. The oil also contains rosmarinic acid, a potent anti-inflammatory compound that helps to reduce inflammation in the body, which can put a strain on the heart. Additionally, the oil's antimicrobial properties help to prevent infections that can lead to complications in heart failure patients.

By reducing oxidative stress, inflammation, and the risk of infections, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils may be beneficial in supporting heart health in individuals with congestive heart failure.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba oil or coconut oil)
  2. Wash the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves in cold water, then gently pat them dry
  3. Combine the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and carrier oil in a clean glass jar, making sure the leaves are fully covered
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily, then strain the oil through cheesecloth into another container
  5. Bottle the Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains bioactive constituents like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the body.

This reduction in inflammation is beneficial for congestive heart failure patients as it reduces the strain on the heart. Gingerol also has antioxidant properties, which help protect the heart from damage caused by free radicals. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils may help lower blood pressure and improve circulation, both of which are important for congestive heart failure patients.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its major bioactive constituents.

These compounds have vasodilatory properties, which help to relax blood vessels and improve blood flow to the heart. The vasodilatory effects of linalool and linalyl acetate can help to reduce blood pressure and alleviate the workload on the heart, making it easier for it to pump blood efficiently. The anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds can also help to reduce inflammation in the heart and surrounding tissues, which is often associated with congestive heart failure.

By reducing inflammation and improving blood flow, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils may help to alleviate symptoms of congestive heart failure and promote overall cardiovascular health.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a glass jar, then cover it with a lid.
  3. Steep the mixture in a dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every 2 days. This allows the oil to extract from the flowers.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the extracted oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for skin application.

Curcuma longa essential oils contains curcuminoids, including curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help to reduce inflammation in the heart and improve blood flow, making it easier for the heart to pump blood. Turmeric's curcuminoids also have a protective effect on the heart by reducing oxidative stress and improving the function of the heart's cells. Additionally, curcumin has been shown to have a beneficial effect on blood lipid levels, reducing triglycerides and LDL cholesterol, which is beneficial for people with congestive heart failure.

By reducing inflammation, improving blood flow, and lowering cholesterol levels, Curcuma longa essential oils may help alleviate some of the symptoms associated with congestive heart failure.

6. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid and isovaleric acid as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have a sedative effect, which can help in reducing stress and anxiety associated with congestive heart failure. The sedative properties of valerenic acid also help in lowering blood pressure and heart rate, both of which are beneficial in managing congestive heart failure. Additionally, the oils have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help in reducing inflammation in the body that can lead to heart failure.

By reducing stress, lowering blood pressure, and reducing inflammation, Valeriana officinalis essential oils can provide relief from congestive heart failure symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Valeriana officinalis roots and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over low heat, then add the Valeriana officinalis roots.
  3. Let the roots steep for 30 minutes, then strain the liquid into a bowl.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba oil) to the liquid and mix well.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks to allow the oil to mature.

Controlling Hypertension with Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Hypertension is a medical condition where the blood pressure in the arteries is consistently too high.

It can lead to serious health problems, such as heart disease, stroke, and kidney damage, if left untreated. High blood pressure can cause the heart to work harder, leading to fatigue, shortness of breath, and swelling in the legs. If not managed, hypertension can also increase the risk of cardiovascular disease, blindness, and even death. Therefore, it's essential to deal with hypertension effectively to prevent these complications. Herbal remedies, including herbs and herbal preparations, have been used for centuries to help manage hypertension. Ginkgo biloba, an ancient plant, has been found to have vasodilatory properties, which can help lower blood pressure by widening blood vessels. Withania somnifera, also known as ashwagandha, is an herb that can help reduce stress and anxiety, which are common triggers for high blood pressure.

Allium sativum, or garlic, has been shown to have natural antihypertensive properties, which can help lower blood pressure by reducing the amount of sodium in the body. Herbal preparations come in various forms, including decoction, tincture, and infusion. A decoction is a liquid extract made by boiling herbs in water, which can be effective for herbs like Ginkgo biloba that require high heat to release their active compounds. A tincture, on the other hand, is a concentrated liquid extract made by soaking herbs in a solvent like ethanol or glycerin. Infusion is a method of making tea by steeping herbs in hot water. These herbal preparations can be used to prepare teas, capsules, or tablets that can be taken regularly to manage hypertension. Herbal remedies can be a safe and effective way to manage hypertension, especially when combined with lifestyle changes like a balanced diet, regular exercise, and stress management.

However, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal remedy, as they may interact with medications or have side effects in certain individuals.

What Are The Main Causes Of Hypertension?

Hypertension, or high blood pressure, is a common health issue that can lead to serious problems if left untreated.

There are several main causes of hypertension. One of the causes is family history. If your parents or grandparents had high blood pressure, you may be more likely to develop it too. This is because some people may be born with a tendency to have higher blood pressure due to their genetic makeup. Another cause of hypertension is sodium intake. Consuming too much sodium can cause the body to hold onto water, which increases blood pressure.

This is because sodium is a key ingredient in the production of a hormone that regulates fluid balance in the body. When we eat too much sodium, our body produces more of this hormone, causing blood pressure to rise. Smoking is also a significant cause of hypertension. Smoking damages the blood vessels and makes them narrower, which increases blood pressure. Smoking can also damage the kidneys, which can lead to high blood pressure. When we smoke, our heart has to work harder to pump blood through the damaged blood vessels, causing blood pressure to rise.

This can lead to serious health problems, including heart disease and stroke.

Below are the main causes of hypertension.

  • Family History: Family history plays a significant role in causing hypertension as it can pass down unhealthy lifestyle habits, genetic traits, and environmental factors from parents to their children, increasing the risk of developing high blood pressure.
  • Sodium Intake: When we eat too much sodium, our body holds extra water to dilute it, which increases blood volume and puts extra pressure on our blood vessels, leading to high blood pressure.
  • Smoking: Smoking damages the blood vessels and reduces their ability to function properly, leading to high blood pressure and increased risk of developing hypertension due to the increased stress on the heart and blood vessels.
  • Obesity: Obesity is a cause of hypertension because excess body fat, particularly around the abdomen, increases blood pressure by forcing the heart to work harder to pump blood through the body's blood vessels, which can lead to damage and hardening of the blood vessels over time.
  • Lack of Exercise: Lack of exercise is a cause of hypertension because it leads to a buildup of plaque in the arteries and increased blood pressure due to decreased blood vessel flexibility and reduced cardiovascular efficiency.
  • Stress: When we are stressed, our body's "fight or flight" response is triggered, causing our heart to beat faster and our blood vessels to constrict, which increases blood pressure and can lead to hypertension.
  • Sleep Apnea: Sleep Apnea causes hypertension because the repeated pauses in breathing during sleep lead to a lack of oxygen, causing the body's stress response to be constantly triggered, resulting in increased blood pressure and strain on the heart.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Hypertension?

If you have hypertension, it's essential to be cautious with certain herbs that can raise blood pressure or interact with your medication.

Some herbs to avoid or use with caution include: Yohimbe (Pausinystalia johimbe): This herb can increase heart rate and blood pressure, making it a concern for people with hypertension. Ephedra (Ephedra sinica): Ephedra is a stimulant that can raise blood pressure and increase heart rate. It can also interact with medications used to treat high blood pressure. Cayenne pepper (Capsicum annuum): While cayenne pepper is often used to reduce inflammation, it can also raise blood pressure due to its spicy compounds. Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra): While licorice root is often used to soothe digestive issues, it can increase blood pressure and cause other complications in people with hypertension. Mahuang (Ephedra sinica): This herb is similar to ephedra and should be avoided for the same reasons.

Red yeast rice (Monascus purpureus): Some brands of red yeast rice may contain high levels of monacolin K, which can lower LDL cholesterol but also raise blood pressure. Ma Huang (Ephedra sinica): Again, this is another name for ephedra, so the same warnings apply. Coleus (Coleus forskohlii): This herb can increase heart rate and blood pressure, making it a concern for people with hypertension. Bladderwrack (Fucus vesiculosus): Some studies suggest that bladderwrack may raise blood pressure in people with hypertension. However, more research is needed to confirm this effect. Ginseng (Panax spp.

): Some types of ginseng, like Asian ginseng (Panax ginseng), can raise blood pressure, especially when taken in large doses or combined with other stimulants.

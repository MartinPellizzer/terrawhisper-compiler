Palpitation Relief for the Anxious Mind: 8 Essential Oils

Anxiety can be a real nightmare for many people.

It can make them feel like they're walking on eggshells, constantly worried about what might happen next. And for some, anxiety can also cause a racing heart, known as palpitations. These uncomfortable feelings can be a huge problem for anxiety sufferers, making it hard for them to go about their daily lives. They might feel like they're on edge all the time, and even small things can trigger a panic attack. This can make it hard to work, spend time with loved ones, or even take care of basic needs like eating and sleeping. It's like their body is constantly on high alert, and they can't seem to calm down. When anxiety causes palpitations, it's often because the body's "fight or flight" response is triggered.

This response is meant to help us react quickly to danger, but for anxiety sufferers, it can be triggered by things that aren't actually life-threatening. This can lead to a buildup of stress hormones like adrenaline, which can make the heart beat faster and feel like it's pounding in the chest. It's like the body is stuck in a perpetual state of emergency, and it doesn't know how to turn off the alarm. But there is some good news. Herbal essential oils have been shown to be a helpful tool in calming the body and reducing anxiety symptoms like palpitations. These oils are derived from plants and can be inhaled or applied to the skin to promote relaxation and reduce stress. They can help slow down the heart rate and calm the nervous system, making it easier to breathe and feel more centered. Some of these oils have a natural sedative effect, while others can help reduce anxiety by promoting feelings of calm and relaxation.

By using these oils in conjunction with other relaxation techniques, anxiety sufferers may be able to find some relief from their palpitations and other symptoms.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils, also known as valerian root oil, has been traditionally used to help with palpitations due to its calming and sedative properties.

The oil contains constituents such as valepotriates, isovaleric acid, and valerenic acid, which work together to slow down the heart rate and promote relaxation. These properties help to reduce anxiety and stress, common triggers for palpitations. When using Valeriana officinalis essential oils for palpitation, it's recommended to inhale the oil through steam inhalation or apply it topically to the pulse points after diluting with a carrier oil. Start with a small amount, about 5-7 drops, and gradually increase as needed.

To use the oil safely, always dilute it with a carrier oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil, and perform a patch test before applying it to the skin. If you're experiencing palpitations, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment. Inhaling the oil through steam inhalation can be done by adding a few drops to a bowl of hot water, then inhaling the vapor for a few minutes. When using Valeriana officinalis essential oils for palpitation, you may experience a decrease in heart rate and blood pressure, leading to a reduction in palpitation episodes. You may also feel more relaxed and calm, which can help to alleviate anxiety and stress.

However, if you're experiencing frequent or severe palpitations, it's crucial to seek medical attention to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

2. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata essential oils have been traditionally used to help alleviate palpitations, which are irregular heartbeats that can be a symptom of anxiety or stress.

The oil contains properties like flavonoids and alkaloids, which work together to calm the nervous system and reduce anxiety levels. The main constituents of Passiflora incarnata essential oil are isovitexin, vitexin, and homoplantaginin, which are responsible for its sedative and anxiolytic effects. To use Passiflora incarnata essential oil for palpitations, you can try the following actions: - Add a few drops of the oil to a diffuser and inhale it for 5-10 minutes, ideally in the morning and evening. - Mix a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil, and massage it onto your wrists, temples, or behind your ears.

- Incorporate a few drops of the oil into your bath water for a relaxing soak. By taking these actions, you may experience a decrease in anxiety and stress levels, which can lead to a reduction in palpitations. The calming effects of the oil may also promote better sleep quality, improved mood, and increased feelings of relaxation. Additionally, you may notice a decrease in your heart rate and blood pressure, which can help alleviate palpitations.

However, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using Passiflora incarnata essential oil, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or are taking medications.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis tinctures contains several bioactive constituents, including valerenic acid, valepotriates, and isovaleramate.

These compounds have sedative and anti-anxiety properties, which can help to calm an overactive nervous system that may be contributing to palpitations. The valepotriates in valerian root tinctures have been shown to have a direct effect on the heart, reducing heart rate and blood pressure. Valerian root tinctures also have a relaxing effect on the muscles, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety that may be causing palpitations.

The sedative properties of valerian root tinctures make it a popular natural remedy for people experiencing palpitations due to anxiety or stress.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Valeriana officinalis roots and 2 cups of 80% ethanol (vodka or rum work too).
  2. Combine the dried roots and ethanol in a clean glass jar. Make sure the roots are completely covered.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the tincture in dark glass bottles and store them in a cool, dark place. The recommended dose is 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day for palpitation.

2. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata tinctures contains flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides.

The flavonoids, particularly vitexin and isovitexin, have a sedative effect and can help calm the nervous system. The alkaloids, including harmine and harmaline, have a depressant effect on the central nervous system, which can help reduce anxiety and stress that may cause palpitations. The glycosides, including passiflorin, have a relaxing effect on the nervous system and can help reduce muscle tension that can lead to palpitations.

By calming the nervous system and reducing muscle tension, Passiflora incarnata tinctures can help alleviate palpitations by addressing the underlying causes of the condition.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Passiflora incarnata leaves and flowers.
  2. Combine the leaves and flowers in a clean glass jar.
  3. Add 2 cups of 80-proof vodka to the jar, making sure the plant material is covered.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it daily.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into another container, discard the solids and store the tincture in a dark glass bottle.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains gingerols and shogaols, bioactive constituents that have a calming effect on the body.

These compounds help to relax the smooth muscles in the heart, reducing palpitations and irregular heartbeat. The anti-inflammatory properties of gingerols and shogaols also help to reduce inflammation in the blood vessels, which can contribute to palpitations. The antioxidant properties of Zingiber officinale tinctures help to protect the heart from oxidative stress, further reducing the risk of palpitations.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, and promoting relaxation, Zingiber officinale tinctures can help to alleviate palpitations.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale roots, 2 cups of 95% ethanol, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Chop the roots into small pieces and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour 2 cups of 95% ethanol over the chopped roots, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids. The resulting liquid is the Zingiber officinale tincture for palpitations.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains glycyrrhizin, a key bioactive constituent that helps to reduce palpitations.

Glycyrrhizin has a natural anti-inflammatory effect, which can help to calm the nervous system and regulate heart rate. It also has a sedative property, which can help to slow down rapid heartbeat and promote relaxation. Additionally, glycyrrhizin has a diuretic effect, which can help to reduce fluid retention and alleviate symptoms of palpitations caused by fluid overload.

By regulating the body's fluid balance and calming the nervous system, glycyrrhizin in Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures can help to alleviate palpitation symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots, 2 cups of vodka (at least 35% ABV), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Chop the roots into small pieces and fill the glass jar about 1/4 of the way full.
  3. Pour the vodka over the roots, making sure they are completely covered. Seal the jar and shake it well.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day or two. This will help the roots infuse into the vodka.
  5. Strain the liquid into another clean glass bottle, discarding the solids. The resulting tincture is ready to use. Start with a small dose (about 10-20 drops) and increase as needed.

Panax ginseng tinctures contains the bioactive constituents ginsenosides, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

These ginsenosides, particularly Rg1 and Rb1, have been found to have a stabilizing effect on the nervous system, helping to calm palpitations. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent palpitation from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help calm the nerves and slow down a racing heart.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for palpitation every day?

Herbal teas can be a good option to help with palpitations.

Moderation is key when using herbal teas for this purpose.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in palpitation?

Herbal teas for palpitations can start working within a few days to a week.

Some people notice a difference in their heartbeat and overall calmness after just 2-3 cups over a short period.

However, it may take a few weeks for the full effects to kick in, and results can vary from person to person.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for palpitation?

Drinking herbal tea for palpitations in the morning or early afternoon might help.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and ginger, can calm the heart and reduce anxiety.

These teas can be a gentle way to manage palpitations when consumed at these times, helping you feel more relaxed and centered throughout the day.

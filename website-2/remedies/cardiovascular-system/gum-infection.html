What Are The Main Causes Of Gum Infection?

Gum infection, also known as periodontitis, occurs when bacteria in the mouth cause inflammation and damage to the gums and the bone that supports the teeth.

There are several main causes of gum infection. One of the main causes is Poor Oral Hygiene. When teeth are not cleaned regularly, bacteria can build up and cause infection. This can happen if people do not brush their teeth at least twice a day and floss once a day.

Bacterial Overgrowth is another cause of gum infection. When bacteria grow too much, they can cause inflammation and damage to the gums and the bone. This can be caused by poor oral hygiene, but it can also be caused by other factors such as smoking and using tobacco products. Gingivitis is a mild form of gum infection that can progress to periodontitis if left untreated. Gingivitis occurs when bacteria in the mouth cause inflammation of the gums, making them red, swollen, and painful.

If gingivitis is not treated, it can progress to periodontitis, which is a more severe gum infection that can cause damage to the bone and lead to tooth loss.

Below are the main causes of gum infection.

  • Poor Oral Hygiene: Poor oral hygiene allows bacteria to build up on teeth and gums, leading to inflammation and infection of the gums, known as gum disease or periodontitis.
  • Bacterial Overgrowth: Bacterial overgrowth is a cause of gum infection because when there's an imbalance of good and bad bacteria in the mouth, the bad bacteria can multiply and spread, leading to inflammation and infection of the gums.
  • Gingivitis: Gingivitis is a cause of gum infection because it is a mild inflammation of the gums, which can lead to bacteria building up and causing infection if left untreated.
  • Periodontitis: Periodontitis is a gum infection cause because when bacteria build up under the gum line, they cause inflammation and damage to the supporting tissues and bone around the teeth.
  • Smoking: Smoking causes gum infection because the chemicals in cigarette smoke weaken the gums, making it harder for them to fight off bacteria and other germs that can cause infection.
  • Diabetes: When people with diabetes have high blood sugar levels, it can make their gums more sensitive and prone to infection, especially around the teeth, due to reduced blood flow and weakened immune system.
  • Genetic Predisposition: Genetic predisposition can cause gum infection because some people are born with a weaker or abnormal immune system, making their gums more susceptible to bacterial attacks and infections.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Gum Infection?

If you have a gum infection, it's essential to avoid certain herbs that can make the problem worse.

One herb to avoid is Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis), as it contains a chemical called berberine, which can slow down healing and increase the risk of infection. Another herb to steer clear of is Yarrow (Achillea millefolium), as it can cause bleeding and make the gum infection more severe.

Sage (not to be confused with the previously mentioned Salvia officinalis) is also not recommended, as it can dry out the mouth and make it harder to heal. Additionally, Comfrey (Symphytum officinale) is best avoided, as its chemical compounds can cause inflammation and irritation in the gums. Finally, the herb Ginkgo biloba can be problematic due to its blood-thinning properties, which can interfere with the body's natural response to infection.

It's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies to treat a gum infection.

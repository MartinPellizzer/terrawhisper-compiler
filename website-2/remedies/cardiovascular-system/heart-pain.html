What Are The Main Causes Of Heart Pain?

Heart pain, also known as chest pain, can be caused by several conditions that affect the heart.

One of the main causes is a heart attack, also known as Myocardial Infarction (MI). This occurs when the blood flow to the heart is blocked, usually due to a blood clot or a blockage in the coronary arteries. As a result, the heart muscle does not receive enough oxygen and nutrients, leading to damage to the heart tissue. Another cause of heart pain is Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS), which is a group of conditions that occur when the blood flow to the heart is suddenly blocked. This can be caused by a blood clot, a blockage in the coronary arteries, or a spasm of the coronary arteries.

ACS can lead to a heart attack, and in some cases, it can be a precursor to a heart attack. Angina is also a common cause of heart pain. It occurs when the heart muscle does not receive enough oxygen and nutrients due to a temporary blockage in the coronary arteries. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and smoking. When the heart muscle does not receive enough oxygen and nutrients, it can lead to chest pain or discomfort, which can be a sign that the heart is not getting enough blood flow. In all these conditions, the common underlying cause is a reduction in blood flow to the heart, which can be due to a blockage, a spasm, or a combination of both.

This reduction in blood flow can lead to damage to the heart tissue, and in severe cases, it can be life-threatening.

Below are the main causes of heart pain.

  • ACS: ACS, or Acute Coronary Syndrome, causes heart pain because a blood clot blocks the flow of blood to the heart muscle, leading to a lack of oxygen and causing severe pain and damage to the heart.
  • MI: Myocardial infarction (MI), also known as a heart attack, is a cause of heart pain because a blockage in the blood vessels cuts off the flow of oxygen to the heart muscle, causing damage and pain.
  • Angina: Angina is a cause of heart pain because the blood vessels in the heart become narrowed or blocked, making it hard for the heart to get the oxygen it needs, leading to pain or discomfort in the chest.
  • Cardiomyopathy: Cardiomyopathy is a cause of heart pain because the heart muscle becomes weak and unable to pump blood properly, leading to strain and discomfort in the chest area.
  • Pericarditis: Pericarditis is a cause of heart pain because the thin layer of tissue surrounding the heart, called the pericardium, becomes inflamed and irritated, leading to sharp chest pain and discomfort.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Heart Pain?

If you have heart pain, there are some herbs you should avoid.

'Yarrow' (Achillea millefolium) is not recommended because it can slow down blood clotting, which can be a problem if you have a heart condition. 'Garden heliotrope' (Heliotropium arborescens) is another herb to avoid as it can lower blood pressure too much.

'Bladderwrack' (Fucus vesiculosus) should be avoided because it can increase heart rate and blood pressure, which may worsen heart pain. 'Borage' (Borago officinalis) may not be suitable for people with heart problems because it can cause heart palpitations.

'Hawthorn' (Crataegus monogyna) is generally safe for people with heart conditions, but it can interact with certain medications, so consult a doctor before using it.

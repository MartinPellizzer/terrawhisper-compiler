How Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations Can Help with High Blood Pressure

High blood pressure is a medical condition where the force of blood against the walls of arteries is too high.

This can cause damage to the arteries, heart, and other organs if left untreated. It is essential to manage high blood pressure, as it increases the risk of heart disease, stroke, kidney disease, and blindness. If high blood pressure is not dealt with, it can lead to these complications, which can be life-threatening. To manage high blood pressure, certain herbs and herbal preparations have been found to be beneficial. Ginkgo biloba, a traditional Chinese medicine, is known to help reduce blood pressure by improving blood vessel function and increasing nitric oxide production, which relaxes blood vessels.

Crataegus monogyna, also known as hawthorn, has been traditionally used to treat high blood pressure by improving heart function, reducing inflammation, and relaxing blood vessels. Herbal preparations can be used in various forms to manage high blood pressure. These herbal preparations can be used alone or in combination with conventional medications to manage high blood pressure.

What Are The Main Causes Of High Blood Pressure?

High blood pressure, also known as hypertension, is a common health issue that affects millions of people worldwide.

There are several main causes of high blood pressure, and understanding these causes can help prevent the condition. One of the main causes is high sodium intake. When we eat foods that are high in salt, our body holds onto water to dilute the sodium, which increases the volume of blood in our veins. This puts extra pressure on our blood vessels, leading to high blood pressure. Another significant cause of high blood pressure is obesity.

When we are overweight or obese, our body has to work harder to pump blood throughout our body, which increases blood pressure. Additionally, excess body fat can also lead to inflammation in our blood vessels, making them narrower and more prone to high blood pressure. Smoking is also a major cause of high blood pressure. Smoking damages the lining of our blood vessels, making them narrower and less efficient. This means that our heart has to work harder to pump blood through our body, increasing blood pressure.

Smoking also increases the risk of heart disease and stroke, which are often related to high blood pressure.

Below are the main causes of high blood pressure.

  • Sodium Intake: Consuming too much sodium causes high blood pressure because it increases the amount of water in the blood, putting extra pressure on the blood vessels and making them work harder.
  • Obesity: When you have extra body fat, especially around your middle, it can cause your kidneys to work harder to remove excess salt and water from your body, leading to an increase in blood pressure.
  • Smoking: Smoking damages the blood vessels and makes them narrow, which increases the force of blood flowing through them, leading to high blood pressure.
  • Genetics: Genetics can cause high blood pressure because people who have a family history of the condition may inherit specific genes that affect how their blood vessels function and respond to stress, making it more likely for them to develop high blood pressure.
  • Kidney Disease: Kidney disease can cause high blood pressure because the kidneys are unable to remove waste and excess fluids from the body properly, leading to a buildup of fluids in the blood vessels and causing them to work harder, which increases blood pressure.
  • Dehydration: When the body doesn't have enough water, the blood vessels constrict or tighten, which makes it harder for blood to flow, increasing blood pressure.

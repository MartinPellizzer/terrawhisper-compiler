What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have arteriosclerosis?

If you have arteriosclerosis, there are some herbal essential oils you should avoid.

Peppermint oil (Mentha piperita) and wintergreen oil (Gaultheria procumbens) contain high amounts of methyl salicylate, a compound similar to aspirin. This can increase bleeding risks and worsen heart problems. Gingergrass oil (Cymbopogon nardus) and lemongrass oil (Cymbopogon citratus) may cause blood thinning due to their citral content, which can interact with medications and worsen arteriosclerosis.

Pennyroyal oil (Mentha pulegium) contains pulegone, a compound that can damage the liver and increase the risk of bleeding. Fennel oil (Foeniculum vulgare) may cause blood pressure to rise, which can be problematic for people with arteriosclerosis. You should also be cautious with essential oils like bergamot oil (Citrus aurantium bergamia) and clary sage oil (Salvia sclarea), as they may lower blood pressure, but only under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

It is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional or an aromatherapist before using any essential oils if you have arteriosclerosis.

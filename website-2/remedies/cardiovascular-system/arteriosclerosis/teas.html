What herbal teas you should avoid if you have arteriosclerosis?

If you have arteriosclerosis, it's best to avoid certain herbal teas that can worsen the condition or interact with your treatment.

One such tea is Ephedra (Ephedra sinica), which can raise blood pressure and heart rate, making it harder for your heart to pump blood through narrowed arteries. Yohimbe (Pausinystalia johimbe) tea should also be avoided as it can cause blood vessels to constrict, reducing blood flow to the heart.

Another tea to steer clear of is Guarana (Paullinia cupana), which can also raise blood pressure and heart rate. The tea made from the roots of Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) and Goldthread (Coptis chinensis) should be avoided as well, as they can slow blood clotting and increase the risk of bleeding. Finally, tea made from the bark of Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa) should be used with caution, as it can interact with blood thinners and worsen bleeding.

Consult with your doctor before using any herbal tea.

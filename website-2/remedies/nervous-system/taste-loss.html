What Are The Main Causes Of Taste Loss?

As people age, their sense of taste can decline due to the loss of taste buds on the tongue.

Taste buds contain tiny cells called papillae that are responsible for detecting different flavors. Over time, these papillae can shrink and die, making it harder for the brain to receive signals about taste. This age-related decline in taste is a natural process and is usually noticeable in people over 60. Smoking is another cause of taste loss.

When people smoke, the chemicals in tobacco and other substances inhaled with smoke can damage the papillae on the tongue. This damage can lead to a reduced ability to detect different flavors, making food and drinks taste bland or unappetizing. Smoking can also cause a decrease in saliva production, which is necessary for washing away food particles and helping the tongue detect flavors. Medications are also a common cause of taste loss. Some medications, such as chemotherapy and radiation therapy, can damage the papillae on the tongue, leading to a reduced sense of taste.

Certain antibiotics and antidepressants can also affect the sense of taste, possibly due to their impact on the nervous system or the way they work in the body.

Below are the main causes of taste loss.

  • Ageing: As we age, the tiny hairs in our mouths called taste buds gradually die and don't grow back, leading to a decrease in our ability to taste food and drinks.
  • Smoking: Smoking causes taste loss because the chemicals in cigarette smoke damage tiny sensors on the tongue and in the mouth, called taste buds, which are responsible for helping us enjoy food and drinks.
  • Medications: Medications can cause taste loss because they often affect the tiny hairs in our tongues that help us detect the five basic tastes, such as sweet, sour, salty, and bitter.
  • Diabetes: Diabetes can cause a loss of taste because high blood sugar levels damage the tiny nerve endings on the tongue that help us recognize different flavors.
  • Dental issues: Dental issues can cause taste loss because problems with teeth or gums, such as cavities, infections, or gum disease, can damage the tiny taste buds on the tongue and inside the mouth, making it harder to taste food.

Lastly, 'Ipecacuanha', or ipecac root, contains emetine, a compound that can cause nausea and a bitter taste.

These herbs can exacerbate taste loss, making it even more challenging to enjoy food.

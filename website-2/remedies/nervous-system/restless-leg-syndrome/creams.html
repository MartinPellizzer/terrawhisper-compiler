11 Herbal Creams For Restless Leg Syndrome

Herbal creams can be a natural solution for restless leg syndrome (RLS).

RLS is a condition that causes uncomfortable sensations in the legs, often at night. Herbal teas, not creams, can help relieve RLS symptoms. Let's focus on teas. Herbal teas can calm your nervous system and promote relaxation, which can ease RLS symptoms. Herbs like Valeriana officinalis, also known as valerian root, have been used for centuries to promote sleep and relaxation. This herb contains compounds that interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, helping you feel more relaxed and sleepy. Zingiber officinale, or ginger, has anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce pain and discomfort in the legs.

Lavandula angustifolia, or lavender, has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can also help with sleep and relaxation. When you drink herbal teas, the active compounds are absorbed into your bloodstream and interact with your brain and body. This can lead to several benefits beyond just relieving RLS symptoms. For example, these herbs can help you fall asleep faster, sleep more soundly, and wake up feeling more refreshed. This can improve your mood, reduce fatigue, and boost your energy levels. By promoting relaxation and reducing stress, herbal teas can also help with anxiety and other related conditions. To use herbal teas, steep dried herbs in hot water, then strain and drink. You can also add honey or lemon to taste.

Start with small amounts and experiment with different herbs to find what works best for you.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis creams contains valerenic acid, valeranone, and valepotriates as its active constituents.

These compounds have a sedative and muscle-relaxing effect, which can help alleviate the symptoms of restless leg syndrome. Valerenic acid, in particular, has been shown to interact with the GABA receptors in the brain, promoting relaxation and reducing muscle tension. This interaction can help calm the nervous system and reduce the urge to move legs due to RLS.

By applying Valeriana officinalis creams to the affected area, individuals may experience relief from the discomfort and restlessness associated with restless leg syndrome.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Valeriana officinalis root and 1/2 cup of coconut oil in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the coconut oil in the double boiler and add the dried Valeriana officinalis root. Let it simmer for 2-3 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, pressing on the solids to get all the liquid. Discard the solids.
  4. Let the liquid cool down and then whip it with a hand mixer until it thickens into a cream. You can add a bit of beeswax (1-2 tablespoons) to help it solidify.
  5. Pour the cream into a container and store it in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. Use a small amount (about 1-2 teaspoons) as needed to help with restless leg syndrome symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of ginger essential oil, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

  3. Add the shea butter to the melted coconut oil and beeswax mixture. Stir until combined.

4. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata creams contains flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides.

The flavonoids, including flavonol and flavanone, act as antioxidants, reducing inflammation in the legs. The alkaloids, such as harman, harmine, and harmaline, have a sedative effect, helping to calm the nervous system and relax muscles. By reducing inflammation, calming the nervous system, and relaxing muscles, Passiflora incarnata creams can help alleviate symptoms of restless leg syndrome.

  2. Combine the dried flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Let it steep in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax to the oil mixture and place the jar in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  5. Heat the mixture until the beeswax melts, then remove from heat and let it cool. Pour the cream into small containers and store in a cool, dark place.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent restless leg syndrome from forming?

Some people find that drinking herbal tea, especially those with calming properties like chamomile and lavender, can help ease restless leg syndrome symptoms.

These teas may help relax muscles and reduce discomfort.

However, results can vary from person to person, and it's unclear how effective herbal tea can be in preventing the condition from forming.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for restless leg syndrome every day?

While herbal teas may help with restless leg syndrome, consuming them daily isn't entirely safe.

Some teas contain caffeine, which can worsen symptoms, and others may interact with medications. Additionally, relying on herbal teas for relief might mask underlying issues.

It's essential to be cautious and monitor how your body reacts.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in restless leg syndrome?

The results from drinking herbal teas for restless leg syndrome can vary from person to person.

Some people may feel relief after a few days, while others may need a week or two to notice a difference.

It's not uncommon for symptoms to decrease gradually as the body adjusts to the herbal ingredients.

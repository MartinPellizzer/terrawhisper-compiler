7 Herbal Teas For Restless Leg Syndrome

As people age, they may experience Restless Leg Syndrome (RLS), a condition that causes uncomfortable sensations in their legs, such as itching, burning, or tingling.

This can happen when they're sitting or lying down, and it can disrupt their sleep, making it hard for them to get a good night's rest. Older adults with RLS may find themselves feeling tired, irritable, and anxious, which can affect their daily activities and social interactions. RLS can also lead to falls and injuries, especially if the person gets up during the night to move around. This can be a big concern for older adults who live alone or have mobility issues. For those who experience RLS, everyday tasks like cooking, cleaning, or even watching TV can become challenging. The discomfort in their legs can make them feel like they're walking on needles, or like ants are crawling under their skin.

Some people may try to ignore the sensation, but it can be hard to focus on anything else. Others may turn to medication, but these can have side effects and interact with other medications they're taking. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to help with RLS in older adults. These teas contain compounds that can help relax muscles, reduce inflammation, and promote better sleep. They can be made from plants that are rich in antioxidants and have anti-inflammatory properties. Drinking these teas may help calm the nervous system and reduce the urge to move the legs.

By incorporating herbal teas into their daily routine, older adults may find some relief from the symptoms of RLS.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis teas contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone, which are known to help with restless leg syndrome.

These compounds have a sedative effect on the nervous system, reducing muscle tension and relaxing the legs. The root of the valerian plant also contains a high concentration of alkaloids, which contribute to its calming properties. When consumed as a tea, these compounds work together to calm the nervous system and reduce the urge to move the legs due to restless leg syndrome.

If you're experiencing restless leg syndrome, try drinking Valeriana officinalis tea before bedtime, as it may help improve sleep quality. Start by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried valerian root in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink. It's recommended to take the tea 1-2 hours before bedtime to allow the compounds to take effect.

You may notice a reduction in symptoms, such as tingling, cramping, and restlessness, as well as improved sleep quality and reduced anxiety.

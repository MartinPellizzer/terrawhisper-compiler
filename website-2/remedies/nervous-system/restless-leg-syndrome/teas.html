Overcome Restless Leg Syndrome with 7 Herbal Teas for Older Adults

As people age, they may experience Restless Leg Syndrome (RLS), a condition that causes uncomfortable sensations in their legs, such as itching, burning, or tingling.

This can happen when they're sitting or lying down, and it can disrupt their sleep, making it hard for them to get a good night's rest. Older adults with RLS may find themselves feeling tired, irritable, and anxious, which can affect their daily activities and social interactions. RLS can also lead to falls and injuries, especially if the person gets up during the night to move around. This can be a big concern for older adults who live alone or have mobility issues. For those who experience RLS, everyday tasks like cooking, cleaning, or even watching TV can become challenging. The discomfort in their legs can make them feel like they're walking on needles, or like ants are crawling under their skin.

Some people may try to ignore the sensation, but it can be hard to focus on anything else. Others may turn to medication, but these can have side effects and interact with other medications they're taking. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to help with RLS in older adults. These teas contain compounds that can help relax muscles, reduce inflammation, and promote better sleep. They can be made from plants that are rich in antioxidants and have anti-inflammatory properties. Drinking these teas may help calm the nervous system and reduce the urge to move the legs.

By incorporating herbal teas into their daily routine, older adults may find some relief from the symptoms of RLS.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis teas have been used to help alleviate symptoms of restless leg syndrome (RLS).

The tea contains a compound called valerenic acid, which has a calming effect on the nervous system. It also has a sedative property that helps reduce muscle cramps and spasms, common symptoms of RLS. The tea's active constituents, including valepotriates and valeoptenates, may also help regulate the body's internal clock, improving sleep quality, which is often disrupted in people with RLS.

If you're considering trying Valeriana officinalis teas for RLS, it's recommended to start with a small dose of 1-2 cups per day, brewed for 5-7 minutes. You can add honey or lemon to taste. Pay attention to how your body responds, and adjust the dose as needed. Some people may experience relief from RLS symptoms within a few days, while others may take longer to notice improvements.

If you experience any adverse effects or worsened symptoms, discontinue use and consult with a healthcare professional.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas, also known as English lavender tea, have been used to help alleviate symptoms of restless leg syndrome (RLS).

The reason for this lies in its properties and constituents, particularly linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their calming and anti-inflammatory effects. When consumed as a tea, the bioavailability of these compounds is increased, allowing them to reach the bloodstream and exert their effects more efficiently. It's recommended to drink the tea 30 minutes to 1 hour before bedtime, as its calming effects can help improve sleep quality. It's also advised to experiment with different amounts of lavender flowers and steeping times to find the optimal combination for individual needs. Consuming Lavandula angustifolia teas regularly may lead to improved sleep quality, reduced frequency and severity of RLS symptoms, and enhanced relaxation.

What herbal teas you should avoid if you have restless leg syndrome?

If you have restless leg syndrome, you should avoid herbal teas that contain certain ingredients that can worsen the condition.

One such herb is yerba mate (Ilex paraguariensis), which contains high levels of caffeine and theophylline. These compounds can increase heart rate, blood pressure, and anxiety, making symptoms of restless leg syndrome worse.

Another herb to avoid is guayusa (Ilex guayusa), which is a cousin of yerba mate and also contains caffeine and theophylline. Additionally, green tea (Camellia sinensis) may not be suitable for people with restless leg syndrome, as it also contains caffeine, although in smaller amounts. However, be aware that some green tea products may contain higher levels of caffeine due to the brewing method or addition of other herbs.

Lastly, tea blends that include black pepper (Piper nigrum) or cayenne pepper (Capsicum annuum) may be problematic, as they can increase the release of certain neurotransmitters that can exacerbate restless leg syndrome symptoms.

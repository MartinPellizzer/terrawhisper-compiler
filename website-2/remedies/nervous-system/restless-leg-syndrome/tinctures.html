By using a combination of these two herbs in a tincture form, you can help alleviate the symptoms of RLS and improve your sleep quality. By incorporating herbal tinctures into your daily routine, you can take the first step towards managing RLS and improving your life.

What herbal tinctures you should avoid if you have restless leg syndrome?

If you have restless leg syndrome, it's best to avoid herbal tinctures that contain herbs like 'Kava' (Piper methysticum), 'Yohimbine', and 'Ephedra' (Ephedra sinica).

Kava can make your muscles relax too much, which can worsen restless leg symptoms. Yohimbine, on the other hand, is a stimulant that can increase anxiety and make it harder to sleep.

Ephedra can also increase heart rate and blood pressure, which can be problematic for people with restless leg syndrome who may already be experiencing cardiovascular issues. Another herb to be cautious with is 'Ginseng' (Panax ginseng), as it can interfere with sleep patterns and worsen symptoms.

'Skullcap' (Scutellaria lateriflora) can also cause drowsiness and interact with medications, which is not ideal for people with restless leg syndrome.

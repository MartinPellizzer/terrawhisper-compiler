What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have restless leg syndrome?

This can lead to increased movement in the legs, making RLS symptoms more uncomfortable. Another herb to avoid is Scutellaria baicalensis, or Baikal skullcap. While it may help with stress and anxiety, it can also increase alertness and worsen RLS symptoms in some people. Its active compounds, such as baicalein, can stimulate the nervous system, causing increased movement and discomfort in the legs. Additionally, decoctions containing Camellia sinensis, or green tea, should be consumed in moderation.

Green tea contains caffeine, which can exacerbate RLS symptoms in some individuals. Caffeine can increase alertness and make the legs feel even more restless. Decoctions containing Paeonia lactiflora, or peony, should also be used with caution. While peony may help with anxiety and insomnia, it can also increase the risk of bleeding in people who take blood thinners. This is a concern for individuals with RLS who may experience increased movement in their legs, potentially leading to injuries or bleeding. Lastly, decoctions containing Schisandra chinensis, or five-flavor berry, should be avoided due to their potential stimulatory effects. Schisandra may increase alertness and worsen RLS symptoms in some people, especially if consumed in large amounts. It's essential to note that individual reactions to herbs can vary greatly, and some people may not experience adverse effects from these herbs.

If you have restless leg syndrome, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before trying new herbal decoctions.

7 Essential Teas for Office Workers to Reduce Eye Strain

Office workers often spend long hours staring at computer screens, which can cause eye strain.

This is because the eyes have to focus on a single point for a long time, leading to fatigue and discomfort. The eyes may feel dry, itchy, or irritated, and blurred vision can occur. This can affect office workers' ability to concentrate and be productive, leading to stress and decreased job satisfaction.

Eye strain can also cause headaches and eye pain, making it difficult to sleep and affecting daily life. As a result, office workers may experience a decrease in their quality of life, making it essential to find a solution to alleviate eye strain. One way to do this is by using herbal essential oils. These oils have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help soothe and calm the eyes.

When inhaled or applied topically, they can reduce eye fatigue and promote relaxation, making it easier for office workers to focus and work efficiently.

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils, also known as English lavender, contain properties that can help alleviate eye strain.

The oil's key constituents, linalool and linalyl acetate, have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and soothing effects, which can help reduce redness and discomfort in the eyes. When used in aromatherapy, the scent of Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can also promote relaxation and reduce stress, which are common contributors to eye strain. To use Lavandula angustifolia essential oils for eye strain, start by applying a few drops to a cloth or pillowcase.

As you inhale the scent, gently massage the oil into your temples or the base of your neck. You can also add a few drops to a warm bath or inhale it through steam inhalation. When you take these actions, you may start to feel a decrease in eye strain and discomfort. Your eyes may become less red and tired, and you may experience improved focus and concentration.

Regular use of Lavandula angustifolia essential oils may also help reduce stress and promote a better night's sleep, which can further alleviate eye strain.

2. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils, also known as lemon balm oil, may help alleviate eye strain due to its soothing and calming properties.

It contains constituents like citral, geranial, and linalool, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These properties can help reduce the discomfort and fatigue associated with eye strain. To use Melissa officinalis essential oils for eye strain, try adding a few drops to a warm compress or a carrier oil like coconut oil, and gently apply it to your closed eyes.

You can also inhale the oil directly from a cloth or handkerchief. When you inhale the oil, the citral and geranial may help relax your eyes and reduce the feeling of strain. As you breathe in the aroma, you may feel a sense of calm wash over you, and the tension in your eyes may start to ease.

If you experience eye strain, try this remedy a few times a day, and see if it makes a difference.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils, also known as rosemary oil, can help alleviate eye strain.

This is because rosemary oil contains properties and constituents that can relax tense muscles and improve blood circulation to the eyes. The oil's main components, including camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate, have anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, which can reduce eye fatigue and discomfort.

To use rosemary oil for eye strain, place a few drops on your palms, rub them together, and gently massage the oil over your closed eyelids. You can also add a few drops to a warm compress or a bowl of hot water and inhale the steam to ease tension and promote relaxation.

By taking these actions, you may experience relief from eye strain, improved focus, and reduced eye discomfort.

4. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium essential oils, also known as Yarrow oil, contains compounds like borneol, camphor, and sesquiterpenes, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help reduce eye strain by soothing and calming the muscles around the eyes. The essential oil also contains apigenin, a flavonoid that can bind to GABA receptors in the brain, promoting relaxation and reducing eye fatigue. To use Achillea millefolium essential oils for eye strain, start by diluting a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil. Gently massage the mixture around your eye area, being careful not to get any oil in your eyes.

You can also add a few drops of the oil to a warm compress or a bowl of hot water, then place the compress or a cloth over your eyes to allow the steam to open up your pores and allow the oil to penetrate deeper. By taking these steps, you may experience a reduction in eye strain and fatigue, as well as a sense of relaxation and calmness. Your eyes may feel more comfortable and less irritated, and you may be able to focus and concentrate more easily. The anti-inflammatory properties of the oil may also help reduce any swelling or redness around your eyes.

However, keep in mind that if your eye strain persists or is severe, you should consult with an eye care professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

5. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contain a blend of properties and constituents that make it an effective remedy for eye strain.

The main constituents, including valerenic acid, valerenol, and isovalerenol, have a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help to reduce eye fatigue and tension. The oils also contain antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that can help to reduce inflammation and promote healing in the eyes.

When using Valeriana officinalis essential oils for eye strain, it's recommended to add a few drops to a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for a gentle massage around the eye area. You can also apply a few drops to a cloth and place it over your closed eyes to help soothe and calm the area. If you experience eye strain, try taking a break from screens and electronics, applying a warm compress to your eyes, and practicing relaxation techniques, such as deep breathing or meditation, while using the essential oils.

By taking these steps, you may find that your eye strain and fatigue reduce, and you're able to focus and concentrate more effectively.

6. Bacopa monnieri

Bacopa monnieri essential oils have been found to be effective in alleviating eye strain due to its antioxidant and adaptogenic properties.

The oil contains constituents like bacosides A and B, which have anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective effects that help protect the eyes from damage. When used in aromatherapy, Bacopa monnieri essential oil can help reduce eye fatigue and strain by promoting relaxation and calming the mind. To use Bacopa monnieri essential oils for eye strain, try the following steps: - Add a few drops of the oil to a diffuser and inhale the vapors for 5-10 minutes.

- Mix a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil and gently massage it around your eyes, avoiding direct contact with the eyes themselves. - Place a few drops of the oil on a cotton ball and apply it to your temples or forehead, inhaling the vapors. As you inhale the vapors or apply the oil to your skin, you may start to feel a sense of calmness and relaxation spreading throughout your body. Your eye strain may begin to dissipate, and you may notice an improvement in your vision clarity.

The oil's antioxidant properties may also help reduce the risk of age-related macular degeneration and other eye problems.

7. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata essential oils are known for their ability to alleviate eye strain, thanks to its rich composition of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and lignans.

The main constituents, including isovitexin, swertisin, and vitexin, possess potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce eye fatigue and discomfort. When used topically, Passiflora incarnata essential oils can help to calm and soothe the eyes, reducing the symptoms of eye strain such as redness, dryness, and blurred vision. To use Passiflora incarnata essential oils for eye strain, start by mixing a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil, and gently apply it around your eye area, avoiding direct contact with the eyes.

You can also inhale the oil through steam inhalation by adding a few drops to a bowl of hot water, then inhaling the vapors for a few minutes. Using Passiflora incarnata essential oils in this way may help to reduce eye strain and promote relaxation, allowing you to focus and perform daily tasks without discomfort. You may notice a decrease in eye redness and dryness, as well as improved vision clarity.

Some people also report a reduction in headache and fatigue associated with prolonged screen time.

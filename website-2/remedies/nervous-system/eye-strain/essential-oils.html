7 Herbal Essential Oils For Eye Strain

Office workers often spend long hours staring at computer screens, which can cause eye strain.

This is because the eyes have to focus on a single point for a long time, leading to fatigue and discomfort. The eyes may feel dry, itchy, or irritated, and blurred vision can occur. This can affect office workers' ability to concentrate and be productive, leading to stress and decreased job satisfaction.

Eye strain can also cause headaches and eye pain, making it difficult to sleep and affecting daily life. As a result, office workers may experience a decrease in their quality of life, making it essential to find a solution to alleviate eye strain. One way to do this is by using herbal essential oils. These oils have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help soothe and calm the eyes.

When inhaled or applied topically, they can reduce eye fatigue and promote relaxation, making it easier for office workers to focus and work efficiently.

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains properties and constituents that make it an effective remedy for eye strain.

The primary constituents, linalool and linalyl acetate, have anti-inflammatory and calming effects on the eyes. When applied topically, these compounds can help reduce eye fatigue and discomfort. This is because linalool, a natural vasodilator, can increase blood flow to the eyes, promoting relaxation and reducing tension.

To use Lavandula angustifolia essential oils for eye strain, mix a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil, and gently massage it around your eyes in a circular motion. You can also add a few drops of the oil to a warm compress and place it over your eyes for 5-7 minutes. When you take action, you may experience a reduction in eye strain and fatigue, as well as an improvement in your overall visual comfort. You may also notice a decrease in eye redness and puffiness, and an increase in the clarity and sharpness of your vision.

Additionally, the calming effects of the oil may help you relax and reduce stress, which can further contribute to a reduction in eye strain.

You can consume herbal teas for eye strain daily, but in moderation.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for eye strain?

As you unwind before bed, herbal tea can calm your senses and ease fatigue, making it a great way to end your day.

11 herbal teas to help office workers overcome eye strain

Office workers often sit in front of computers for long hours, staring at screens and focusing on tasks.

This constant gaze can lead to eye strain, which is a common problem in today's digital age. When eyes get tired, they can feel heavy, itchy, and dry, making it difficult to concentrate and complete tasks efficiently. Eye strain can also cause headaches and blurred vision, affecting not just work but also daily activities like watching TV or reading. In severe cases, it can even lead to eye problems like myopia and astigmatism. This can take a toll on a person's mental and physical health, leading to stress, anxiety, and decreased productivity. As a result, office workers often experience fatigue, irritability, and decreased job satisfaction.

They might struggle to meet deadlines, leading to increased workload and pressure on colleagues. This can create a vicious cycle of stress and burnout, affecting not just the individual but also their relationships and overall well-being. Furthermore, eye strain can also impact a person's quality of life, making it difficult to enjoy hobbies and spend time with loved ones. Herbal teas have been traditionally used to soothe and calm tired eyes. These teas contain natural ingredients that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce eye fatigue and promote relaxation. Drinking herbal teas can help office workers alleviate eye strain and improve their eye health, allowing them to work more efficiently and enjoy a better work-life balance.

By incorporating herbal teas into their daily routine, office workers can take a step towards preventing eye strain and promoting overall well-being.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas have been gaining attention for their potential benefits in alleviating eye strain.

The leaves of the Ginkgo biloba tree contain flavonoids and terpenoids, including bilobalide and quercetin, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These properties help to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the eyes, which can contribute to eye strain. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba teas may also improve blood flow to the eyes, reducing the risk of damage from prolonged screen time or close-up work. If you're experiencing eye strain, try drinking Ginkgo biloba tea 2-3 times a day, especially after focusing on a screen for an extended period. You can also apply a cool compress to your eyes to reduce swelling and promote relaxation.

To get the most out of Ginkgo biloba tea, look for a product that contains a standardized extract of 24% flavone glycosides. This will help ensure that you're getting a consistent dose of the active ingredients. Drinking Ginkgo biloba tea regularly may lead to improved eye comfort and reduced eye fatigue. You may notice that your eyes feel less dry and irritated, and you may be able to focus for longer periods without feeling strained. In some cases, people have reported improved vision clarity and reduced eye pressure.

However, it's essential to note that Ginkgo biloba tea is not a substitute for medical treatment, and if you experience persistent or severe eye strain, you should consult with an eye care professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

2. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis teas, which include green tea, black tea, and oolong tea, can help alleviate eye strain due to their unique properties and constituents.

The high levels of antioxidants called polyphenols, particularly catechins and theaflavins, found in these teas have anti-inflammatory effects that can reduce eye fatigue. The L-theanine content in Camellia sinensis teas promotes relaxation and reduces stress levels, which can contribute to eye strain. Additionally, the caffeine present in these teas can increase alertness and focus without causing excessive jitteriness. To take advantage of these benefits, start by steeping a Camellia sinensis tea bag in hot water for a few minutes, then let it cool.

You can drink it as is or add a small amount of honey or lemon to taste. Aim to drink 1-2 cups of tea per day, ideally in the morning and early afternoon when eye strain is most likely to occur. It's also essential to take regular breaks when working on a computer or engaging in other screen-based activities to give your eyes a chance to rest. By incorporating Camellia sinensis teas into your daily routine and practicing good eye care habits, you may experience a decrease in eye strain, improved focus, and reduced eye irritation.

You may also notice a reduction in headaches and an increase in overall productivity.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, have been traditionally used to alleviate eye strain.

This is because the tea contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties. These properties help to reduce swelling and pain in the eyes, making ginger tea a potential remedy for eye strain caused by prolonged screen time, dry air, or other factors. To use ginger tea for eye strain, steep a teaspoon of fresh ginger in a cup of boiling water for 3-5 minutes, then strain and let it cool.

Next, dip a clean cloth in the tea and apply it to your closed eyes for 5-10 minutes. As you do this, focus on relaxing your eyes and taking deep breaths. Likely, you'll start to feel a soothing sensation in your eyes, and your vision may become clearer. The anti-inflammatory properties in the ginger tea may also help to reduce any redness or irritation in the eyes.

Some people have reported improved vision and reduced eye fatigue after using ginger tea, while others have found relief from headaches and migraines that often accompany eye strain.

Centella asiatica teas, also known as Gotu Kola, are a popular herbal remedy for eye strain.

The reason it's effective lies in its high content of triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. When we stare at screens for long periods, our eyes get tired and irritated, leading to strain and discomfort. Drinking Centella asiatica tea can help alleviate these symptoms.

Drinking Centella asiatica tea regularly may lead to improved eye health, reduced eye strain, and enhanced visual clarity.

8 Herbal Tinctures to Help Programmers with Chronic Eye Strain

Programmers spend long hours staring at screens, typing away on their keyboards, and scrolling through lines of code.

This constant strain on their eyes can lead to eye fatigue, dryness, and irritation. The blue light emitted from computer screens and the close proximity to the screen can cause eye muscles to work harder, leading to headaches and blurred vision. This can significantly affect a programmer's productivity, mood, and overall quality of life. They may experience difficulty concentrating, dry, itchy eyes, and even vision problems that can last for hours or even days after a long coding session. This can be frustrating and demotivating, especially for those who rely on their computers for their work. To make matters worse, programmers often neglect to take breaks, thinking that they need to meet deadlines or finish a task quickly.

But ignoring eye strain can lead to more serious problems, such as long-term eye damage and vision loss. This can have a significant impact on their career, as they may struggle to perform their tasks effectively, leading to missed deadlines and decreased job satisfaction. Herbal tinctures have been used for centuries to treat eye strain and other eye problems. These natural remedies are made by infusing herbs in a solvent, such as water or ethanol, to extract their medicinal properties. The resulting liquid is then taken orally, allowing the body to absorb the beneficial compounds. Some herbal tinctures have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to soothe and protect the eyes from damage.

They can also help to reduce eye fatigue and dryness, making it easier for programmers to work for extended periods without experiencing eye strain.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures may help with eye strain due to its properties that improve blood flow to the eyes and enhance antioxidant defenses.

The tincture contains flavonoids and terpenoids, including bilobalide and ginkgolides, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. When taken, these compounds may help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the eyes, leading to relief from eye strain. If you're experiencing eye strain, consider adding 20-30 drops of Ginkgo biloba tincture to a glass of water and taking it 2-3 times a day.

You can also apply a few drops directly to the eyes, but be sure to dilute it with water first to avoid irritation. It's recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase as your body adjusts. Taking Ginkgo biloba tinctures may lead to improved eye comfort and reduced fatigue. You may notice a decrease in eye dryness, redness, and itchiness, and an increase in visual clarity and focus.

Additionally, Ginkgo biloba's antioxidant properties may help protect your eyes from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to age-related macular degeneration and other eye problems.

2. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis tinctures may help alleviate eye strain due to its sedative properties and the presence of valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone, which are thought to interact with the neurotransmitters in the brain, including GABA, to promote relaxation and reduce muscle tension.

This can be beneficial for eye strain caused by prolonged screen time or other activities that require focused attention. To use Valeriana officinalis tinctures for eye strain, start by taking 5-10 drops of the tincture in water or juice, 2-3 times a day.

It is recommended to take it 30 minutes to 1 hour before bedtime or before a break from screen time to allow the body to relax. As the body responds to the tincture, you may experience a reduction in eye strain symptoms, such as headache, blurred vision, or fatigue. You might also notice improved sleep quality and a decrease in stress levels, which can contribute to eye strain.

However, it's essential to note that individual results may vary, and it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before using any new supplement, especially if you have underlying medical conditions or are taking medications.

3. Avena sativa

Avena sativa tinctures are made from the oats plant and are used to help with eye strain.

The tincture contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which are known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These properties help to reduce swelling and ease eye fatigue. Avena sativa tinctures also contain a compound called avenanthramides, which have been shown to improve blood flow to the eyes and reduce stress on the eye muscles.

When using Avena sativa tinctures for eye strain, take 10-20 drops of the tincture in water, 2-3 times a day. You can also add the tincture to a warm compress and apply it to your closed eyes for 5-10 minutes to help relax the eye muscles. Taking Avena sativa tinctures may help to reduce eye strain, improve eye comfort, and increase productivity.

You may notice a reduction in eye fatigue, improved focus, and a decrease in headaches associated with eye strain.

4. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng tinctures have been found to be effective in reducing eye strain due to its adaptogenic properties and high antioxidant content.

The main constituents, ginsenosides, help to reduce inflammation and improve blood flow to the eyes, which can become strained from prolonged screen time or other activities. These compounds also have a neuroprotective effect, shielding the eyes from oxidative stress and promoting relaxation. To use Panax ginseng tinctures for eye strain, start by taking 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, mixed with water.

You can also consider adding it to your eye wash solution or applying a few drops directly to the eyes (though be cautious and dilute it first). After taking the tincture, you may start to feel a sense of calmness and reduced eye fatigue. Your vision may also become clearer and more focused. As you continue to use the tincture, you may notice a decrease in eye strain, headaches, and dryness.

Some users may also experience improved mood and sleep quality, which can be beneficial for overall eye health.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce eye strain caused by prolonged screen time or reading. The flavonoids in ginkgo biloba, such as quercetin and kaempferol, protect the eyes from oxidative stress and inflammation, which can contribute to eye strain. The terpenoids, including bilobalide and ginkgolides, have a vasodilatory effect, improving blood flow to the eyes and reducing fatigue.

By reducing inflammation and improving blood flow, ginkgo biloba tinctures can help alleviate eye strain and promote eye health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Ginkgo biloba leaves. You can find them at health food stores or online.
  2. Combine the Ginkgo biloba leaves with 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass jar. Make sure the leaves are completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day to help the tincture mixture.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Ginkgo biloba tincture in a cool, dark place. Take 10-20 drops, 2-3 times a day, as needed to help relieve eye strain.

2. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis tinctures contains rosmarinic acid, a powerful antioxidant, and apigenin, a flavonoid.

These compounds help reduce eye strain by neutralizing free radicals and relaxing the muscles around the eyes. The rosmarinic acid in Melissa officinalis tinctures also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can soothe irritated eyes and reduce swelling. Apigenin has a sedative effect, which can calm the nervous system and promote relaxation, further reducing eye strain.

The combination of these active constituents makes Melissa officinalis tinctures a natural remedy for eye strain relief.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Fill a cup with water and pour it over the Melissa officinalis flowers in the jar. Let it sit for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar daily.
  3. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another jar, discarding the flowers.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of glycerin to the liquid and mix well. Glycerin helps to preserve the tincture and makes it easier to take.
  5. Label the jar with the date and contents, and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture in water 2-3 times a day to help relieve eye strain.

3. Avena sativa

Avena sativa tinctures contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce eye strain.

The tannins in Avena sativa also have a soothing effect on the eyes, reducing redness and swelling. The saponins in the tincture help relax the muscles around the eyes, relieving tension and fatigue. Avena sativa's flavonoids, particularly avenanthramides, have antioxidant properties that protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Avena sativa tinctures can help alleviate eye strain symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Avena sativa leaves, 2 cups of 80-proof vodka, and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Chop the Avena sativa leaves into small pieces and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour 2 cups of 80-proof vodka over the chopped Avena sativa leaves in the jar.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.

4. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis tinctures contains valerenic acid, valeranone, and isovaleric acid, which are potent anti-inflammatory compounds.

These compounds help reduce swelling and irritation in the eyes, alleviating eye strain caused by prolonged computer use or reading. Valeriana officinalis also contains sedative properties, such as valerenic acid, which help relax the eye muscles and reduce fatigue. The relaxing properties of valerian root tincture can also help decrease eye tension, making it easier to focus and reducing eye strain.

8. Bacopa monnieri

Bacopa monnieri tinctures contains bacosides, a group of active compounds that have been shown to improve memory and cognitive function.

Bacosides A and B, in particular, have been found to have a neuroprotective effect, which can help reduce eye strain caused by prolonged computer use or reading. The tannins in Bacopa monnieri also have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce eye fatigue and discomfort. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent eye strain from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent eye strain.

Some herbal teas, like chamomile and peppermint, can calm your eyes and reduce fatigue. The caffeine-free brew can also give your eyes a break from screen time.

Regularly sipping on herbal tea may help keep your eyes feeling rested and refreshed.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for eye strain every day?

Consuming herbal teas daily for eye strain might be okay, but be cautious.

Some herbs can be too potent. For example, chamomile can make your eyes feel drowsy, while peppermint might irritate them. Start with small amounts and observe how your body reacts.

It's better to err on the side of caution and use herbal teas sparingly.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in eye strain?

Herbal teas can help with eye strain, but it's hard to predict exactly how long it takes to notice results. Some people report feeling better after just a few cups, while others may need to drink it for a few days or a week to see improvement.

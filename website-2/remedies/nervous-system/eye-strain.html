This can happen when you're working on a computer, reading, or doing other activities that require you to focus your eyes. If you don't deal with eye strain, it can lead to headaches, blurred vision, and difficulty concentrating. In severe cases, it can even cause eye problems like myopia (nearsightedness) to worsen. By incorporating herbal preparations into your eye strain treatment, you can reduce the risk of long-term eye problems and promote overall eye health.

What Are The Main Causes Of Eye Strain?

Eye strain is a common problem caused by several factors.

One major cause is Blue Light, which is emitted by electronic devices such as smartphones, tablets, and computers. When we look at these devices for a long time, Blue Light enters our eyes and can cause damage to the retina, leading to eye strain. This is because Blue Light has a high energy frequency that can tire our eyes quickly. Another common cause of eye strain is Staring, which means focusing on a single object or point for a long time without blinking.

When we stare, our eyes get tired because they are working hard to focus on the object. This can cause dry eyes, blurred vision, and headaches. Computer Use is also a significant cause of eye strain. When we use computers, we often spend long hours staring at screens, which can lead to eye fatigue. Computers emit Blue Light, and we tend to stare at the screen without taking breaks, making our eyes work harder and leading to strain.

Additionally, poor lighting and poor posture while using computers can also contribute to eye strain.

Below are the main causes of eye strain.

  • Blue Light: Blue light from electronic devices can cause eye strain because it makes our eyes work harder to focus, leading to fatigue and discomfort.
  • Staring: Prolonged staring causes eye strain because it forces your eyes to focus on the same thing for a long time, leading to muscle fatigue and irritation.
  • Computer Use: Prolonged computer use can cause eye strain because the blue light emitted from screens makes our eyes focus harder, leading to fatigue and discomfort.
  • Reading: Reading can be a cause of eye strain because it requires focusing on a single point for a long time, which can tire the eyes and cause them to feel strained and uncomfortable.
  • Dry Eyes: Dry Eyes occurs when the eyes do not produce enough tears to keep the eyes moist and comfortable, causing irritation, itchiness, and strain on the eyes as they work harder to focus and see clearly.
  • Contact Lenses: Contact lenses can cause eye strain because they can rub against the eyes, disrupt the natural tear balance, and put extra pressure on the cornea, leading to discomfort and fatigue.
  • Poor Lighting: Poor lighting can cause eye strain because it makes our eyes work harder to see clearly, leading to fatigue and discomfort.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Eye Strain?

If you have eye strain, it's essential to avoid herbs that may worsen the condition or interact with any medications you're taking.

One herb to avoid is 'Ephedra sinica', also known as Ma Huang, as it can increase eye pressure and worsen glaucoma symptoms. Another herb to steer clear of is 'Epinephrin-containing herbs' like 'Cassia auriculata' and 'Cassia angustifolia', which can cause eye strain and vision problems due to their stimulant properties. 'Aconitum' or Monk's Hood can also be hazardous as it can cause eye irritation and vision problems, including blurred vision and sensitivity to light.

Additionally, 'Strychnine-containing herbs' like 'Nux vomica' should be avoided due to their potential to cause eye strain and vision problems.

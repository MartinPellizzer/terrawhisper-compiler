What are the causes of eye strain?

Eye strain is a common problem that affects many people.

There are several main causes of eye strain. One major cause is blue light, which is a type of high-energy visible light emitted by digital devices like smartphones, tablets, and computers. Prolonged exposure to blue light can cause eye fatigue, dryness, and irritation. Another significant cause of eye strain is digital screens. These screens emit blue light and can also cause eye strain due to the way they display images. When you look at a digital screen, your eyes have to work harder to focus on the moving images and text, leading to eye strain. Prolonged reading is also a common cause of eye strain.

When you read, your eyes have to focus on the text for a long time, which can cause fatigue and dryness. This is especially true if you're reading in low light or without proper eye care. Insufficient sleep is another factor that contributes to eye strain. When you don't get enough sleep, your eyes can become tired and irritated, making them more prone to eye strain. Lastly, dry eyes are a significant cause of eye strain. Dry eyes occur when the eyes don't produce enough tears to keep the eyes moist and lubricated. This can be due to various factors, including dry air, certain medications, or underlying medical conditions.

When your eyes are dry, they can become irritated and strained, making it difficult to focus and perform daily activities.

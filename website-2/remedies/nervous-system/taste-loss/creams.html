1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which are powerful antioxidants that help reduce inflammation in the mouth and throat.

Curcumin has potent anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce swelling and pain associated with taste loss. Demethoxycurcumin and bisdemethoxycurcumin also have anti-inflammatory properties, but they may be more effective at reducing oxidative stress and promoting the regeneration of taste buds. The antioxidants in Curcuma longa creams may also help protect the taste buds from damage caused by free radicals and other environmental stressors.

By reducing inflammation and promoting the health of taste buds, Curcuma longa creams can help restore a person's sense of taste.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Curcuma longa powder, and 10 drops of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully dissolved.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and add the Curcuma longa powder, stirring well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container, let it cool, and then apply a thin layer to the affected area to help restore taste loss.

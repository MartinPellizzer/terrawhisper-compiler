1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are active compounds that help improve blood flow to the brain.

This increased blood flow is thought to be beneficial for people with speech difficulties, as it may help stimulate areas of the brain involved in speech and language. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba creams, such as quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant properties that help protect the brain from damage and support the health of brain cells. The terpenoids, including ginkgolides and bilobalide, have anti-inflammatory properties that may help reduce inflammation in the brain and promote healthy communication.

By supporting brain health and increasing blood flow, Ginkgo biloba creams may help alleviate speech difficulties in some individuals.

  1. Gather 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 1 tablespoon of Ginkgo biloba extract in a double boiler.
  2. Heat the coconut oil and beeswax in the double boiler until the beeswax melts.
  3. Remove the double boiler from the heat and add the Ginkgo biloba extract. Stir well.
  4. Pour the mixture into a bowl and let it cool until it solidifies. This may take about 30 minutes.
  5. Once solid, use a spoon to mix and blend the cream until it's smooth and even. Store in an airtight container.

  5. Once solid, remove the cream from the mold or tin and store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

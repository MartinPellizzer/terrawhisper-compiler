1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains the bioactive constituents linalool and linalyl acetate.

These compounds are known for their ability to reduce inflammation and ease muscle spasms, which are common triggers for migraines. The calming effects of linalool and linalyl acetate can also help to relax tense muscles and improve sleep quality, which is often disrupted by migraine attacks. Additionally, lavender's antispasmodic properties can help to reduce the severity and frequency of migraine headaches by relaxing blood vessels and improving blood flow to the brain.

The combination of these properties makes Lavandula angustifolia creams a promising natural remedy for migraine sufferers.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of dried Lavandula angustifolia, 2 tablespoons of jojoba oil, and 2 teaspoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until melted and smooth.
  3. Add dried Lavandula angustifolia to a small bowl and pour jojoba oil over it. Let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  4. Combine the melted mixture, strained Lavandula angustifolia oil, and vitamin E oil in a clean bowl. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a silicone candy mold or a small tin container. Let it cool and solidify at room temperature. Once set, store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

2. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis creams contains valepotriates, isovaleramide, and valerenic acid as its active constituents.

These compounds have a sedative and anti-inflammatory effect on the body, which can help to alleviate migraine symptoms. Valepotriates have a calming effect on the nervous system, reducing muscle tension and pain. The anti-inflammatory properties of valerenic acid can help to reduce swelling and pain in the blood vessels that surround the brain, which are often affected during a migraine.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Valeriana officinalis creams can help to provide relief from migraine symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil), 2 tablespoons of Valeriana officinalis root extract, and a small saucepan.
  2. Melt 1/2 cup of carrier oil in the saucepan over low heat. Remove from heat when melted.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of Valeriana officinalis root extract to the melted oil. Stir well to combine.
  4. Let the mixture cool down to room temperature. Then, stir it every 10-15 minutes for 2 hours to ensure it cools evenly.
  5. Once cooled, transfer the mixture to a clean container and store it in the refrigerator. Use as needed for migraine relief.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains a compound called gingerol, which is a potent anti-inflammatory.

Gingerol works by blocking the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that trigger pain and inflammation. The cream also contains shogaol, another active compound that helps to relax blood vessels and improve blood flow to the brain. This can help to reduce the severity and frequency of migraines by reducing inflammation and relieving pain.

By applying Zingiber officinale cream to the skin, the active compounds can be absorbed and reach the brain, where they can help to alleviate migraine symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, 10 drops of ginger essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Once melted, remove from heat and stir in vitamin E oil and ginger essential oil.
  4. Pour the mixture into small containers or a glass jar and let it cool until solidified.
  5. Apply a small amount to the affected area as needed, ideally 2-3 times a day, to help relieve migraine symptoms.

4. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare creams contains the active constituent fenchone, which has a sedative effect on the nervous system.

It also contains estragole, a compound that helps to relax tense muscles and improve blood flow to the brain. Foeniculum vulgare creams have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling and pain in the head and neck area, common symptoms of migraines. The anethole in these creams has a calming effect on the stomach, which can help to alleviate nausea and vomiting associated with migraines.

By relaxing the nervous system, improving blood flow, reducing inflammation, and calming the stomach, Foeniculum vulgare creams can help to alleviate migraine symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, and 2 drops of lavender essential oil.
  2. Melt Foeniculum vulgare oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add coconut oil to the melted oil and stir until combined.
  4. Remove from heat and let it cool for 5-7 minutes, then add lavender essential oil and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a small container and let it cool completely before using as a migraine cream.

5. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains active constituents like menthol, menthone, and limonene.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which help to reduce pain and swelling. Menthol, in particular, can help to relax muscles and improve blood flow, which can be beneficial in relieving migraine symptoms. The analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects of Mentha x piperita creams can also help to reduce the frequency and severity of migraines.

The essential oils in Mentha x piperita creams can also help to reduce nausea and vomiting associated with migraines.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried peppermint leaves, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of lavender oil.
  2. Heat the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat until the beeswax is melted.
  3. Add the dried peppermint leaves to the melted mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth to remove the peppermint leaves.
  4. Add the vitamin E oil and lavender oil to the mixture and stir well. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool.
  5. Once the mixture has cooled and solidified, use a spoon to scoop out a small amount and apply it to your skin as needed to relieve migraine symptoms.

6. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, a powerful antioxidant that helps reduce inflammation in the brain.

This antioxidant also has a direct effect on the blood vessels, causing them to relax and widen, which can help alleviate migraine pain. By applying Rosmarinus officinalis creams topically, rosmarinic acid and carnosic acid can be absorbed through the skin and into the bloodstream, providing relief from migraine pain.

  3. Add shea butter and stir until melted. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.
  4. Add vitamin E oil and dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves to the mixture. Stir well and let it steep for 30 minutes to an hour.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl. Pour the cream into clean containers and let it cool before use.

9. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its bioactive constituents.

Flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce migraine pain and inflammation. Terpenoids, including ginkgolides and bilobalide, have a potent inhibitory effect on platelet-activating factor (PAF), a chemical that causes blood vessels to constrict and worsen migraine symptoms. By blocking PAF, ginkgo biloba creams can help improve blood flow to the brain, reducing migraine severity and frequency.

Regular use of ginkgo biloba creams may also help reduce the frequency of migraines by improving blood vessel health and reducing inflammation.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Ginkgo biloba extract, and 2 drops of lavender essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool slightly. Add Ginkgo biloba extract and lavender essential oil, and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a small tin or jar and let it cool and harden. Store the Ginkgo biloba cream in a cool, dry place.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for migraine?

This can help prevent a migraine from happening. Some herbal teas, like feverfew or ginger, have properties that can ease migraine symptoms.

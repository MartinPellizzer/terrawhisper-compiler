1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have sedative and anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce pain and alleviate migraine symptoms. The calming effect of linalool and the analgesic properties of linalyl acetate work together to ease tension and relax the muscles, which can contribute to migraine headaches. The anti-inflammatory properties of these constituents also help to reduce swelling and ease pain in the blood vessels, which can become enlarged and inflamed during a migraine.

By reducing pain and inflammation, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils may help to provide relief from migraine symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat until it starts boiling, then reduce the heat to a simmer.
  3. Add the Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the saucepan and let them steep for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture using cheesecloth or a coffee filter, collecting the liquid in a bowl.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 24 hours, allowing it to separate into essential oil and water. Skim off the essential oil with a spoon and transfer it to a dark glass bottle.

2. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains boiactive constituents like citral, geraniol, and linalool.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and analgesic properties, which can help ease migraine symptoms. Citral, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the release of pain-causing chemicals in the body. Geraniol has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help reduce stress and anxiety that often trigger migraines.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Melissa officinalis essential oils may help alleviate migraine symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers and 2 cups of water in a pot. Heat the water over low heat.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) to 1 cup of distilled water in a separate container.
  3. Steep the Melissa flowers in the hot water for 10-15 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter.
  4. Combine the strained plant mixture with the carrier oil mixture. Stir well.
  5. Bottle the essential oil mixture and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains active constituents like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

These properties help to reduce pain and inflammation in the body, which can be beneficial for people experiencing migraines. The essential oils also have a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help to alleviate tension and stress that may contribute to migraines. Additionally, the oils have a vasodilatory effect, which means they can help to widen blood vessels and improve blood flow, reducing pressure and pain in the head.

By using Zingiber officinale essential oils, people may be able to find relief from migraine symptoms and improve their overall well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Ginger roots, a carrier oil like Coconut or Jojoba oil, and a glass jar.
  2. Wash the Ginger roots with water and dry them with a clean cloth.
  3. Grate 2 tablespoons of the dried Ginger roots and put them in a small saucepan.
  4. Add 1 cup of carrier oil to the saucepan and heat it on low heat for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally.
  5. Strain the mixture and discard the solids. Transfer the oil to the glass jar and store it in a cool, dark place.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains the bioactive constituents carnosic acid, rosmarinic acid, and camphor.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce swelling and pain in the blood vessels of the brain. The rosmarinic acid in Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils has a natural ability to relax muscles and improve blood flow, which can help alleviate migraine symptoms. The anti-inflammatory properties of carnosic acid and camphor also contribute to reducing inflammation in the brain, a common trigger for migraines.

By reducing inflammation and improving blood flow, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils may help provide relief from migraine symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and flowers.
  2. Wash the leaves and flowers with 1 cup of water to remove dirt and impurities.
  3. Dry the leaves and flowers completely using a clean towel or paper towels.
  4. Use a steam distiller to extract the essential oil from the dried leaves and flowers. Use 1 cup of water and add 1 tablespoon of dried leaves and flowers to the distiller.
  5. Collect the extracted essential oil in a clean glass bottle and use it for migraines by mixing a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut oil.

5. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds contribute to the oil's sedative, anti-anxiety, and pain-relieving properties, making it a potential natural remedy for migraines. The valepotriates in Valeriana officinalis have a direct effect on the nervous system, helping to regulate the body's response to pain and inflammation. Valerenic acid, on the other hand, has a calming effect on the brain, which can help reduce migraine symptoms like sensitivity to light and sound.

By regulating the body's response to pain and promoting relaxation, Valeriana officinalis essential oils may help alleviate migraine symptoms and improve overall well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Valeriana officinalis roots and 1 cup of carrier oil like jojoba or sweet almond oil.
  2. Clean and chop the roots into small pieces.
  3. Combine 1/4 cup of chopped roots with 1 cup of carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains limonene, menthone, and menthol as its key bioactive constituents.

These compounds give the oil its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and vasodilatory properties, which are beneficial for relieving migraine symptoms. The analgesic effect of menthol helps to reduce pain, while its anti-inflammatory properties reduce swelling and inflammation in the blood vessels. The vasodilatory effect of limonene widens blood vessels, improving blood flow and reducing pressure on the brain, a common contributor to migraines.

By reducing pain, inflammation, and pressure, Mentha x piperita essential oils can help alleviate migraine symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Mentha x piperita (peppermint) leaves and 1 cup of water in a large bowl.
  2. Chop the peppermint leaves into small pieces and put them in a heat-resistant glass jar.
  3. Pour 1 cup of water over the chopped peppermint leaves and let it sit for 2 hours to allow the mixture to infuse.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container, discarding the solids.
  5. Use a dropper to transfer the peppermint essential oil to a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid, and store in a cool, dry place.

7. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains a compound called cineole, a key active constituent that provides its medicinal properties.

Cineole has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which help to reduce pain and swelling associated with migraines. The oil also contains alpha-pinene, which has a calming effect on the nervous system, helping to relax and soothe the mind and body. Eucalyptus globulus essential oils also has a decongestant property, which can help to relieve sinus pressure and congestion often associated with migraines.

This combination of properties makes eucalyptus globulus essential oils a popular natural remedy for migraine sufferers.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a clean glass jar, and a cheesecloth.
  2. Wash the Eucalyptus leaves with 1 cup of water to remove dirt and impurities.
  3. Fill the glass jar 1/4 with the Eucalyptus leaves, and cover the leaves with 2 cups of water.
  4. Steep the mixture in a warm place for 2-3 hours, then strain it through the cheesecloth into another container.
  5. Bottle the clear liquid and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-10 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for relief.

8. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains a compound called anethole, which is known for its anti-inflammatory properties.

Anethole and another compound called limonene help to reduce pain and inflammation, making it a potential remedy for migraines. The oil's analgesic and antispasmodic properties also help to ease muscle tension and relax blood vessels, which can contribute to migraine symptoms. Additionally, anethole has been shown to have a sedative effect, which can help to calm the mind and body and reduce migraine frequency.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the body, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils may help to alleviate migraine symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Foeniculum vulgare seeds. Clean and dry them with a paper towel.
  2. Use a coffee grinder or spice grinder to crush the seeds into small pieces. This will release the oil.
  3. Transfer the crushed seeds to a clean glass jar. Add 2 cups of carrier oil, such as coconut or sweet almond oil.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it daily. This will allow the oil to infuse into the carrier oil.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and use the infused oil as needed.

9. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains the bioactive constituents limonene, linalool, and geraniol, which have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

These properties help reduce inflammation in the blood vessels, a common cause of migraines. The analgesic properties of linalool and geraniol also help reduce pain and discomfort associated with migraines. Limonene, on the other hand, has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help alleviate migraine symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and pain, and promoting relaxation, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils may help alleviate migraine symptoms.

  1. Gather materials: 1 cup of Cymbopogon citratus leaves, 1 cup of water, a large pot, a strainer, and a glass bottle.
  2. Wash the Cymbopogon citratus leaves with water and let them dry.
  3. Combine the Cymbopogon citratus leaves and water in the large pot. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 1 hour.
  4. Strain the mixture through the strainer into the glass bottle. Discard the solids and let the liquid cool.
  5. Transfer the cooled liquid to a dark glass bottle and store it in the fridge. Use 2-3 drops of the essential oil in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for use on the skin.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent migraine from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent migraines from forming.

Some herbal teas, like feverfew and ginger, contain compounds that can ease pain and inflammation. They may also help relax blood vessels in the brain, which can contribute to migraines.

These teas may be a natural way to reduce migraine frequency and severity.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for migraine every day?

It's generally okay to have herbal teas for migraines every day.

Many people find relief in regular consumption. However, some teas can interact with medications or worsen symptoms in certain individuals.

It's a good idea to start with small amounts and observe how your body reacts.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in migraine?

It can take a few days to a week for herbal teas to start showing results in migraine relief.

Some people notice a difference after drinking a cup of tea daily for a few days. However, it may take longer to see significant improvements.

Everyone's body is different, and it's hard to predict exactly when you'll start feeling better.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for migraine?

Drinking herbal tea for migraines is best when you're feeling the pain coming on.

Try sipping it as soon as you notice the headache starting. This can help ease the pain and calm your body.

Drinking it at this early stage can also help prevent the migraine from getting worse.

What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have migraine?

If you have migraines, it's best to avoid certain herbal essential oils.

Peppermint oil (Mentha piperita) is one to steer clear of, as its strong aroma can trigger migraines in some people. Another oil to avoid is Birch oil (Betula alba), which contains salicylates, similar to aspirin. These can be a problem for people who are sensitive to aspirin or have a condition called salicylate intolerance.

Cinnamon oil (Cinnamomum verum) and Clove oil (Syzygium aromaticum) should also be avoided as they can cause blood vessels to dilate, which may worsen migraine symptoms. Furthermore, Cajeput oil (Melaleuca cajeputi) and Niaouli oil (Melaleuca quinquenervia) contain compounds that can stimulate the nervous system, potentially triggering migraines. Lastly, Geranium oil (Pelargonium graveolens) contains linalool, which can cause blood vessels to dilate and may exacerbate migraine symptoms.

It's essential to consult a healthcare professional or a certified aromatherapist before using essential oils, especially if you experience migraines.

This tea contains a chemical called safrole, which can cause blood vessels to dilate, leading to increased blood flow to the brain and potentially triggering a migraine attack. Another herb to avoid is 'Hydrastis canadensis', or Goldenseal tea. This tea contains a chemical called berberine, which can cause blood vessels to constrict, but it can also cause other issues like headaches and anxiety, which may worsen migraine symptoms. You should also be cautious with 'Gardenia jasminoides', or Gardenia tea.

This tea contains a chemical called gardenoside, which can cause blood vessels to constrict and may trigger migraine attacks. Additionally, 'Taxus baccata', or Yew tea, is not recommended for people with migraine. This tea contains a chemical called taxine, which can be toxic and cause headaches, dizziness, and nausea, all of which are common migraine symptoms. Lastly, 'Aconitum spp. ', or Monk's Hood tea, contains a chemical called aconitine, which can cause headaches, dizziness, and other symptoms similar to a migraine attack.

9 Herbal Tinctures For Migraine

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis tinctures contains a variety of bioactive constituents, including valepotriates, valerenic acid, and valeranone.

These compounds have sedative and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce the frequency and severity of migraines. The valepotriates in Valeriana officinalis tinctures have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory molecules, such as prostaglandins and leukotrienes, which are known to contribute to migraine pain. The valerenic acid in the tincture has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety, common triggers for migraines.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Valeriana officinalis tinctures may be an effective natural remedy for managing migraine symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Valeriana officinalis roots. Clean and chop them into small pieces.
  2. Combine the chopped roots with 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass jar. Make sure the roots are completely covered by the vodka.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the roots to infuse into the vodka.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Valeriana officinalis tincture in a cool, dark place. Take 10-20 drops in water as needed to help with migraines, up to 3 times a day.

2. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its bioactive constituents, which are responsible for its beneficial effects on migraine sufferers.

The flavonoids, including quercetin and kaempferol, have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in blood vessels, a common cause of migraines. The terpenoids, particularly ginkgolides and bilobalide, act as vasodilators, widening blood vessels and improving blood flow to the brain. This increase in blood flow may help to reduce migraine symptoms by allowing more oxygen and nutrients to reach the brain.

The combination of flavonoids and terpenoids in Ginkgo biloba tinctures may help to alleviate migraine symptoms by reducing inflammation and improving blood flow to the brain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Ginkgo biloba leaves. Use a sharp knife to chop them finely.
  2. Combine 1 cup of chopped Ginkgo biloba leaves with 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Close the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day to help the tincture extract.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the Ginkgo biloba tincture to a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Store it in a cool, dark place and use 5-10 drops, 2-3 times a day as needed for migraine relief.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties can help reduce pain and swelling associated with migraines. The tincture also contains shogaol, a compound that has been shown to inhibit the production of pain-causing chemicals in the body. Additionally, the tincture's warming properties can help relax tense muscles and improve blood flow, which can help alleviate migraine symptoms.

The bioactive constituents in Zingiber officinale tinctures, including gingerol and shogaol, work together to provide relief from migraine pain and discomfort.

  1. Get 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale roots, clean and chop them into small pieces.
  2. Fill a clean glass jar with 2 cups of 80-proof vodka or glycerin, leaving 1 inch at the top.
  3. Add the chopped Zingiber officinale roots to the jar, making sure they're completely covered by the liquid.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle, discarding the solids.

4. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare tinctures contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including limonene, anethole, and fenchone, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, making Foeniculum vulgare tinctures effective in reducing inflammation and relaxing muscle spasms that often accompany migraines. Limonene and anethole have also been shown to have a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help alleviate stress and anxiety that may trigger migraines. Fenchone, on the other hand, has a sedative effect, which can help reduce pain and promote relaxation.

By targeting the underlying causes of migraines, Foeniculum vulgare tinctures may provide relief from the symptoms of this condition.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare (fennel seeds) and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add the fennel seeds to the jar and fill it to the top with vodka, making sure the seeds are fully covered.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent migraine from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent migraines.

Some teas, like feverfew and ginger, have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce pain and discomfort. These teas can also help relax muscles and calm the mind, which may reduce migraine triggers.

Regularly sipping on herbal tea might help reduce migraine frequency and intensity.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for migraine every day?

While some herbal teas may help ease migraine symptoms, drinking them every day can be a concern.

Some herbs, like feverfew, may interact with medications or worsen conditions. Others, like peppermint, might cause stomach upset if consumed excessively.

Moderation is key when using herbal teas for migraine relief.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in migraine?

For some people, herbal teas can start to help with migraines after a few days.

However, it can take up to two weeks to notice any significant changes. Everyone's body is different, so it's hard to predict exactly when you'll see results.

Just be patient and keep trying different teas if needed.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for migraine?

For a migraine, drinking herbal tea in the morning or early afternoon can be helpful. These times are often when migraines first start, so having a soothing tea like peppermint or ginger can ease symptoms and prevent them from getting worse.

What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have migraine?

If you have migraines, it's best to avoid herbal decoctions that can trigger or worsen headaches.

One such herb is 'Hydrastis canadensis' (Goldenseal), which contains a chemical called berberine. Berberine can cause blood vessels to constrict, leading to increased pressure and pain.

Another herb to avoid is 'Cassia auriculata' (Tulsi), which can increase heart rate and blood pressure, potentially triggering a migraine. 'Corydalis yanhusuo' (Corydalis) and 'Paeonia lactiflora' (Peony) decoctions should also be used with caution, as they can cause drowsiness and interact with medications.

Additionally, 'Strychnos nux-vomica' (Strychnine tree) and 'Aconitum carmichaelii' (Aconite) decoctions are highly toxic and should be avoided altogether, as they can cause severe headaches, nausea, and even paralysis.

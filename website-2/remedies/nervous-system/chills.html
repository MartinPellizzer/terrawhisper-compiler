What are the causes of chills?

There are several reasons why you might feel chills.

Meningitis, a serious infection that affects the lining around your brain and spinal cord, can cause chills due to the body's intense response to the infection. Septicemia, a condition where bacteria are in your blood, also triggers chills because your body is fighting to eliminate the bacteria.

Fever is another common cause of chills, as your body tries to raise its temperature to fight off an infection or illness. Pneumonia, an infection in your lungs, can cause chills because your body is trying to fight off the infection and keep you warm.

Lastly, hypothyroidism, a condition where your thyroid gland doesn't produce enough hormones, can cause chills due to the hormonal imbalance, which affects your body's ability to regulate its temperature.

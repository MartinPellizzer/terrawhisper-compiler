9 Herbal Essential Oils For Jet Lag

Herbal essential oils can help alleviate jet lag symptoms.

These oils are extracted from plants and are known for their calming and balancing properties. They can be inhaled through a diffuser or applied topically to the skin. For example, lavender oil (Lavandula angustifolia) has a soothing effect on the mind and body, promoting relaxation and reducing stress. This can help regulate your sleep patterns after traveling across time zones.

Valerian root oil (Valeriana officinalis) has a sedative effect, which can help you fall asleep faster and sleep more soundly. This can be especially helpful when you arrive at your destination feeling tired from your journey. Lemon balm oil (Melissa officinalis) has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help reduce anxiety and promote a good night's sleep. Herbal essential oils can also help with physical symptoms of jet lag, such as fatigue and digestive issues. They can be incorporated into your daily routine, either through a diffuser or by adding a few drops to your bath water.

By using these oils, you can help your body adjust to the new time zone and feel more refreshed and energized.

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two active constituents that contribute to its relaxing properties.

These compounds have a calming effect on the body, which can help alleviate symptoms of jet lag, such as fatigue and insomnia. The sedative properties of linalool and linalyl acetate can also help regulate the body's internal clock, making it easier to adjust to new time zones. Additionally, the oil's antispasmodic properties can help reduce muscle tension, which can be caused by changes in air pressure during flights.

By promoting relaxation and reducing muscle tension, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help alleviate some of the discomforts associated with jet lag.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers. Ensure they are high-quality and organic.
  2. Combine the dried flowers with 1 cup of carrier oil (such as jojoba or sweet almond oil) in a clean glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day to help the oil infuse.
  4. After the steeping period, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container.
  5. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

2. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid, isovalerenol, and valeranone as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have sedative and calming properties that help regulate the body's internal clock. Valerenic acid, in particular, has been shown to interact with the GABA receptors in the brain, promoting relaxation and reducing stress levels. By promoting relaxation and reducing stress, Valeriana officinalis essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of jet lag, such as insomnia and fatigue.

The sedative properties of this essential oil can also help regulate the body's circadian rhythms, making it easier to adapt to a new time zone.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Valeriana officinalis roots and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Boil the water and add the Valeriana officinalis roots. Reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a heat-resistant bowl, discarding the roots. Let it cool.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a glass bottle and add 10 drops of a carrier oil (like jojoba oil) for every 1 tablespoon of liquid. Stir well.
  5. Store the Valeriana officinalis essential oil in a dark place and use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical application.

3. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains linalool and citral as its active constituents.

These compounds have a calming and soothing effect on the body, which is helpful in adjusting to new time zones. Melissa officinalis essential oils also contains antioxidants like geraniol and limonene, which can help reduce stress and promote relaxation. The sedative properties of Melissa officinalis essential oils can help regulate sleep patterns and improve the quality of sleep, making it easier to adjust to a new time zone.

By reducing stress and promoting relaxation, Melissa officinalis essential oils can help alleviate the symptoms of jet lag.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers, fresh or dried.
  2. Combine the flowers with 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30-40 minutes, or until the water is reduced to 1 cup.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container.
  5. Use a distillation kit or a steamer to extract the essential oil from the mixture, then store it in a dark glass bottle.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains boiactive constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help to reduce fatigue and stress associated with jet lag. Gingerols and shogaols also stimulate digestion and improve circulation, which can help to alleviate symptoms of jet lag, such as nausea and dizziness. The warming and stimulating properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils can also help to regulate the body's natural rhythms and reduce the effects of time zone changes.

By using Zingiber officinale essential oils, you can promote relaxation and help your body adjust to a new time zone more easily.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Zingiber officinale rhizomes, 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Peel and chop the Zingiber officinale rhizomes into small pieces, then mix them with 2 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the Zingiber officinale essential oil in a dark glass bottle, and use 5-7 drops in aromatherapy blends to help alleviate jet lag symptoms.

5. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains flavoglycosides and bilobalide as its active constituents.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce the stress and fatigue caused by jet lag. The terpenoids in Ginkgo biloba essential oil, such as ginkgolides and bilobalide, have been shown to improve blood circulation and reduce oxidative stress, which can contribute to symptoms of jet lag. The oil's ability to increase the production of neurotransmitters like serotonin and dopamine can also help to regulate sleep patterns and reduce the effects of jet lag.

By using Ginkgo biloba essential oils, individuals can potentially alleviate some of the symptoms of jet lag and recover from long-distance travel more quickly.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves and 4 cups of water in a saucepan. Heat the water over low heat.
  2. Let the Ginkgo biloba leaves steep in the water for 30 minutes. Strain the mixture and discard the leaves.
  3. Use a carrier oil like jojoba or sweet almond oil. Mix 1/4 cup of carrier oil with 2 tablespoons of the Ginkgo biloba infusion in a bowl.
  4. Add a few drops of vitamin E oil to prevent spoilage. Stir the mixture well and transfer it to a glass bottle.
  5. Label the bottle with the date and contents. Store the Ginkgo biloba essential oil in a cool, dark place for up to 6 months.

6. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains compounds like camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate.

These constituents have a regulating effect on the body's internal clock. The essential oil's properties, such as its sedative and anti-inflammatory effects, can help alleviate symptoms of jet lag like insomnia and fatigue. The alpha-pinene in Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil has a stimulating effect, which can aid in adjusting to new time zones.

By promoting a sense of balance and reducing stress, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help the body adapt to changes in its natural rhythms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water.
  2. Wash the leaves and chop them into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped leaves and water in a saucepan and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and let simmer for 2 hours, then let cool and steep for 2 more hours.
  5. Strain the mixture and collect the liquid. Use a distillation kit or a still to extract the essential oil.

7. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, beta-pinene, and geraniol as its active constituents.

These compounds have decongestant, anti-inflammatory, and adaptogenic properties that help regulate the body's internal clock. The adaptogenic properties of geraniol, in particular, help the body adjust to changes in time zones by stabilizing cortisol levels and promoting a sense of balance. The decongestant properties of limonene and beta-pinene also aid in relieving nasal congestion and sinus pressure, which can be caused by air travel and jet lag.

By using Cymbopogon citratus essential oils, you may experience a reduction in jet lag symptoms and a smoother transition to a new time zone.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Cymbopogon citratus leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Let the mixture sit for 2 hours to allow the essential oil to separate and rise to the surface.
  5. Carefully pour the essential oil into a clean glass bottle, leaving behind the water. Use 2-3 drops in your diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

8. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains a high concentration of 1,8-cineole, a compound that helps ease stress and promotes relaxation.

This oil also contains other active constituents like limonene, alpha-pinene, and beta-pinene, which have a refreshing and decongestant effect. The invigorating properties of eucalyptus oil, particularly its ability to boost circulation and ease congestion, can help alleviate the fatigue and disorientation associated with jet lag. The decongestant properties of eucalyptus oil can also help relieve sinus pressure and promote a restful night's sleep, which is essential for adjusting to a new time zone.

By promoting relaxation and relieving physical discomfort, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help reduce the symptoms of jet lag and facilitate a smoother transition to a new time zone.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a pot.
  2. Boil the water in the pot, then add the Eucalyptus globulus leaves and let it simmer for 20 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture and discard the leaves, then transfer the liquid to a separate container.
  4. Let the liquid sit for 2 hours, allowing it to evaporate and concentrate.
  5. Use a dropper to extract the essential oil from the concentrated liquid and store it in a dark glass bottle.

9. Avena sativa

Avena sativa essential oils contains a compound called avenacoside, which has a direct effect on the body's internal clock.

This oil also contains ferulic acid, a potent antioxidant that helps to regulate the body's circadian rhythms. Avena sativa essential oils have sedative properties, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety caused by jet lag. The oil's ability to reduce cortisol levels and promote relaxation makes it an effective tool for adjusting to new time zones.

By regulating the body's internal clock and promoting relaxation, Avena sativa essential oils can help alleviate the symptoms of jet lag.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Oats (Avena sativa) and a carrier oil like Coconut or Jojoba oil.
  2. Combine 2 tablespoons of dried Oats with 1 cup of carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every few days.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Avena sativa essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent jet lag from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help with jet lag symptoms.

Peppermint tea can ease nausea and digestive issues, while chamomile tea can promote relaxation. Some herbal teas, like those with valerian root, may also help regulate sleep patterns.

These teas are often calming and may make it easier to adjust to a new time zone.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for jet lag every day?

You can drink herbal teas for jet lag, but daily use might not be the best idea.

Some herbal teas contain caffeine or stimulants that can disrupt your sleep in the long run.

It's better to limit your tea consumption to when you need it, and try to establish a regular sleep schedule as soon as possible after traveling.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in jet lag?

Herbal teas can help with jet lag, but it depends on the type and your body.

Some teas, like peppermint and ginger, might ease symptoms like nausea and fatigue within a few hours.

Others, like chamomile and valerian root, can help with sleep quality, which can take a bit longer, sometimes up to a day or two, to notice the effects.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis creams contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates as its active constituents.

These compounds help regulate sleep patterns and promote relaxation, which can be beneficial in overcoming jet lag. Valerenic acid, in particular, has sedative properties that can calm the nervous system and prepare the body for sleep. Isovaleric acid can also help reduce stress and anxiety, making it easier to adapt to a new time zone.

By promoting relaxation and regulating sleep patterns, Valeriana officinalis creams can help alleviate symptoms of jet lag and improve the body's ability to adjust to a new environment.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Valerian root tincture, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 drops of lavender essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Once melted, remove the bowl from the heat and stir in the Valerian root tincture, shea butter, and lavender essential oil until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into a small container or tin and let it cool until it solidifies.
  5. Apply the Valeriana officinalis cream to your skin as needed to help with jet lag relief and relaxation.

2. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, apigenin, and luteolin as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help alleviate symptoms of jet lag. The rosmarinic acid in Melissa officinalis creams has been shown to regulate the body's circadian rhythms, while the apigenin and luteolin work to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation caused by travel across time zones. This can lead to improved sleep quality and reduced fatigue, common complaints of people experiencing jet lag.

The combination of these properties makes Melissa officinalis creams a useful tool for those looking to adapt to new time zones.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Melissa officinalis flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/2 cup of beeswax.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler until it melts.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted coconut oil and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Add the dried Melissa officinalis flowers to the mixture and let it steep for 2-3 hours.
  5. Strain the mixture and let it cool. Pour it into a jar and use as a cream to help with jet lag.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its potential benefits.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which may help alleviate symptoms associated with jet lag, such as body aches and pains. Gingerols also have antioxidant properties, which can help protect against oxidative stress caused by long-distance travel and changing time zones. Additionally, shogaols have a sedative effect, which can help regulate sleep patterns and promote a good night's sleep, a crucial aspect of recovering from jet lag.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Zingiber officinale creams may help alleviate some of the symptoms associated with jet lag.

  1. Mix 2 tablespoons of ginger powder with 1 cup of coconut oil in a small bowl.
  2. Heat the mixture in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl for 30 seconds to 1 minute.
  3. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  5. Pour the cooled and strained mixture into a small container or jar and store it in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

4. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata creams contains flavonoids, such as luteolin and apigenin, and alkaloids like harmine and harmaline.

These compounds help reduce stress and anxiety caused by jet lag. The flavonoids and alkaloids also promote relaxation, which can help regulate sleep patterns disrupted by long-distance travel. Harmine and harmaline can increase serotonin levels, a neurotransmitter that helps regulate sleep-wake cycles.

By reducing stress and promoting relaxation, Passiflora incarnata creams may help alleviate symptoms of jet lag.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Passiflora incarnata flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Heat coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water until melted.
  3. Add dried Passiflora incarnata flowers to the melted mixture and steep for 10-15 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Add vitamin E oil to the mixture and stir well. Pour the mixture into a clean glass jar.
  5. Let the mixture cool and solidify. Once set, your Passiflora incarnata cream is ready to use. Apply a small amount to your skin as needed to help manage jet lag symptoms.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains a high concentration of linalool and linalyl acetate, two key active constituents that contribute to its sleep-promoting properties.

These compounds have a calming effect on the body, helping to regulate the sleep-wake cycle and reduce stress levels. Regular use of Lavandula angustifolia creams may help to regulate the sleep-wake cycle and alleviate symptoms of jet lag.

  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated.
  4. Remove from heat and add lavender essential oil and melatonin oil. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and harden. Once set, your Lavandula angustifolia cream is ready to use for jet lag relief.

6. Avena sativa

Avena sativa creams contains a compound called avenacoside, which is a type of saponin.

Saponins have a calming effect on the body and can help to regulate the body's internal clock. Avena sativa also contains a compound called avenanthramides, which has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These properties can help to reduce stress and promote relaxation, making it easier to adjust to a new time zone.

By regulating the body's internal clock and reducing stress, Avena sativa creams may help alleviate symptoms of jet lag.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Avena sativa (oats) and 2 cups of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Steep the dried Avena sativa in the carrier oil in a saucepan over low heat for 2-3 hours, or overnight.
  3. Strain the oil mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  4. Add 10 drops of lavender essential oil and mix well. You can also add other calming oils like chamomile or valerian.
  5. Pour the Avena sativa cream into small jars or containers and store them in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. Use 1-2 tablespoons as needed to help with jet lag.

8. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids like quercetin and kaempferol, and terpenoids like bilobalide and ginkgolides.

Silybum marianum creams contains active constituents like silymarin, silibinin, and isosilicic acid.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help protect cells from damage caused by free radicals. Silymarin also has liver-protecting properties, which can aid in regulating the body's internal clock and reducing the effects of jet lag. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent jet lag from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help with jet lag symptoms.

Peppermint tea can ease nausea and headaches, while chamomile tea promotes relaxation. Ginger tea is known to aid digestion.

These teas can provide some relief, but it's essential to adapt to the new time zone as quickly as possible.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for jet lag every day?

Consuming herbal teas for jet lag every day can be safe if you choose teas with gentle ingredients like chamomile or peppermint.

However, be cautious with teas containing strong herbs like valerian or passionflower, as they can cause drowsiness and interact with other medications.

Just start with small amounts and see how your body reacts.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in jet lag?

Herbal teas like valerian root and peppermint can help with jet lag symptoms.

It's hard to say exactly how long they take to work, but some people notice improvement within a few hours.

It might take a day or two for your body to adjust fully, but herbal teas can provide some relief as you settle into a new time zone.

7 Herbal Teas For Jet Lag

Pilots often travel across different time zones, which can cause their bodies to get confused.

This is known as jet lag. When pilots arrive at their destination, they may feel tired, groggy, and disoriented. This can affect their ability to fly safely and make decisions quickly. As a result, they may experience fatigue, mood swings, and difficulty sleeping. This can also impact their relationships with family and friends, as well as their overall well-being. In extreme cases, jet lag can even lead to errors in navigation and communication, which can have serious consequences.

Jet lag can last for several days, depending on the time difference and the individual's ability to adapt. Some pilots may feel the effects of jet lag for weeks, which can be challenging to manage. To help alleviate the symptoms of jet lag, herbal teas have become a popular remedy. These teas are made from plants that have been used for centuries to promote relaxation, reduce stress, and improve sleep. They contain compounds that can help regulate the body's internal clock and promote a sense of calm. When consumed before bedtime, these teas can help pilots fall asleep faster and sleep more soundly.

They can also help reduce fatigue and improve focus during the day.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis teas contain valerenic acid, valepotriates, and valeranone, which are responsible for its sedative and relaxing properties.

These compounds can help reduce stress and anxiety that often come with traveling across time zones, making it an effective remedy for jet lag. When trying Valeriana officinalis teas for jet lag, start by brewing a cup of tea 30 minutes before bedtime.

Drink the tea regularly for 2-3 days after your trip to adjust to the new time zone. As you take Valeriana officinalis teas, you may experience a sense of calmness and relaxation, making it easier to fall asleep and stay asleep. You may also notice improved sleep quality, reduced fatigue, and a faster adjustment to the new time zone.

However, individual results may vary, and it's essential to note that this remedy is not a substitute for proper rest and a healthy lifestyle.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains a unique combination of properties and constituents that make it an effective remedy for jet lag.

The tea is rich in linalool and linalyl acetate, two compounds with known sedative and calming effects. These properties help regulate the body's internal clock and promote relaxation, making it easier to adjust to a new time zone.

When consumed before bedtime, Lavandula angustifolia tea can help reduce the symptoms of jet lag, such as insomnia and fatigue, allowing the body to recover faster. To use Lavandula angustifolia tea for jet lag, steep one teaspoon of dried flowers in a cup of boiling water for five to seven minutes, then drink before bedtime. As a result of taking this action, you can expect to wake up feeling more refreshed and energized, with improved sleep quality and reduced fatigue.

By incorporating Lavandula angustifolia tea into your travel routine, you can help minimize the negative effects of jet lag and arrive at your destination feeling more refreshed and prepared.

3. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata teas contains a unique blend of properties and constituents that make it a promising natural remedy for jet lag.

The tea is rich in flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, particularly passiflorine, which is responsible for its calming effects. When we travel across time zones, our body's internal clock gets disrupted, leading to fatigue, insomnia, and other symptoms. Passiflora incarnata teas can help mitigate these effects by promoting relaxation, reducing anxiety, and regulating sleep patterns.

The flavonoids in the tea, such as kaempferol and quercetin, have potent antioxidant properties that can help counteract the negative impacts of travel on our body's cellular function. To use Passiflora incarnata teas for jet lag, start by brewing a cup of tea 30 minutes before bedtime. Drink it on the night of your arrival and for the next few nights to help your body adjust to the new time zone. You can also consume it in the morning to help regulate your circadian rhythms.

By incorporating Passiflora incarnata teas into your travel routine, you can expect to feel more relaxed, refreshed, and better equipped to handle the challenges of jet lag.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties may help with jet lag by reducing nausea and discomfort caused by changes in air pressure and motion. Ginger also has a natural sedative effect, which can promote a good night's sleep. To use Zingiber officinale teas for jet lag, try drinking a cup 30 minutes to an hour before your scheduled flight or when you arrive at your destination.

Start with a small amount, about 1/4 teaspoon of dried ginger per cup of hot water, and adjust to taste. You can also take it in the morning to help adjust your body clock. If you experience nausea or discomfort after traveling, try drinking a cup of Zingiber officinale tea to see if it helps alleviate your symptoms. Some people may find that their jet lag symptoms are reduced, and they're able to adjust to their new time zone more easily.

Others may experience a slight improvement, but still struggle with fatigue and disorientation.

7. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are known to help alleviate jet lag symptoms.

The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant properties that can reduce oxidative stress caused by long-distance travel. Terpenoids, such as ginkgolides and bilobalide, have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help regulate body clocks and improve sleep quality. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent jet lag from forming?

Drinking herbal tea might help with jet lag, but it's not a magic cure.

Peppermint and chamomile teas can calm your body and mind after a long flight. Some teas even have melatonin, a hormone that helps regulate sleep.

This can help you adjust to a new time zone, but it's still up to your body to adapt.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for jet lag every day?

Consuming herbal teas for jet lag every day can be safe if you choose teas with known sleep-promoting ingredients like valerian root or chamomile.

However, some herbal teas can interact with medications or worsen health conditions like high blood pressure or allergies.

It's essential to research the specific tea you're considering and follow the recommended dosage.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in jet lag?

Herbal teas like peppermint, ginger, and valerian root can help with jet lag symptoms.

It's best to drink them as soon as you arrive at your destination. Within 1-2 hours, you might start to feel a bit more alert and awake.

As the night goes on, you may find it easier to fall asleep and adjust to the new time zone.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for jet lag?

Drinking herbal tea can help with jet lag, especially when your body is feeling out of sync.

A good time to have it is when you're winding down for the night. This might be a few hours before bedtime or even right before bed.

It can help calm your mind and body, making it easier to fall asleep.

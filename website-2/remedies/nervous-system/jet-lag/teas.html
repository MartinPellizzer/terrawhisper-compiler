7 Herbal Teas for Pilots to Reduce Jet Lag

Pilots often travel across different time zones, which can cause their bodies to get confused.

This is known as jet lag. When pilots arrive at their destination, they may feel tired, groggy, and disoriented. This can affect their ability to fly safely and make decisions quickly. As a result, they may experience fatigue, mood swings, and difficulty sleeping. This can also impact their relationships with family and friends, as well as their overall well-being. In extreme cases, jet lag can even lead to errors in navigation and communication, which can have serious consequences.

Jet lag can last for several days, depending on the time difference and the individual's ability to adapt. Some pilots may feel the effects of jet lag for weeks, which can be challenging to manage. To help alleviate the symptoms of jet lag, herbal teas have become a popular remedy. These teas are made from plants that have been used for centuries to promote relaxation, reduce stress, and improve sleep. They contain compounds that can help regulate the body's internal clock and promote a sense of calm. When consumed before bedtime, these teas can help pilots fall asleep faster and sleep more soundly.

They can also help reduce fatigue and improve focus during the day.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis teas are a popular natural remedy for jet lag due to their ability to regulate sleep patterns and reduce stress.

The teas contain valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone, which have a calming effect on the nervous system. These constituents help to slow down brain activity, making it easier to fall asleep and stay asleep. When traveling across time zones, our bodies can get disrupted, causing fatigue, insomnia, and difficulty adjusting to the new schedule. Drinking Valeriana officinalis tea can help alleviate these symptoms by promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety.

To use Valeriana officinalis teas for jet lag, start by drinking a cup of tea 30 minutes to 1 hour before bedtime. You can also consume it in the morning or early afternoon to help regulate your circadian rhythms. As you drink the tea, you may feel your muscles relaxing, and your mind becoming calmer. You may also experience improved sleep quality, reduced jet lag symptoms, and a sense of well-being. Some people may notice a decrease in fatigue and anxiety, making it easier to adjust to the new time zone.

Others may find that their sleep schedule adjusts more quickly, allowing them to feel more refreshed and alert.

2. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate symptoms of jet lag.

The tea contains properties like flavonoids and alkaloids, which have been shown to promote relaxation and reduce anxiety. The key constituent, flavonoid, specifically helps to regulate the body's internal clock, while the alkaloids work to calm the nervous system. When experiencing jet lag, try drinking a cup of Passiflora incarnata tea 30 minutes before bedtime in your destination time zone.

Repeat this process for a few nights, ideally for 2-3 days, to help your body adjust to the new time zone. You can also consume it in the morning, about an hour before waking up, to help regulate your circadian rhythms. By taking action, you can expect to feel more refreshed, calm, and adjusted to the new time zone. The tea may also help to reduce symptoms of fatigue, insomnia, and restlessness associated with jet lag.

Additionally, you may find it easier to fall asleep and stay asleep, leading to a more restful night's sleep.

3. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate symptoms of jet lag.

This herbal tea contains properties and constituents like rosmarinic acid, luteolin, and apigenin, which have been shown to have a positive effect on the body's internal clock. These compounds can help to regulate the body's circadian rhythms and reduce the stress and fatigue associated with jet lag. To use Melissa officinalis tea for jet lag, start by drinking a cup of the tea as soon as you arrive at your destination. You can also drink a cup before your flight to help prepare your body for the time change.

Drink the tea warm, ideally 30 minutes to an hour before bedtime, and try to establish a regular sleep schedule as soon as possible. It's also essential to avoid caffeine and heavy meals close to bedtime, as these can disrupt your sleep patterns. By incorporating Melissa officinalis tea into your travel routine, you may find that you adjust to your new time zone more quickly and experience fewer symptoms of jet lag. You may also find that you're able to sleep more soundly and wake up feeling more refreshed.

This herbal tea is not a substitute for medical treatment, but it can be a useful complement to other strategies for managing jet lag.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas have been shown to help alleviate jet lag symptoms due to their relaxing and sleep-promoting properties.

The tea contains constituents like linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their calming effects on the nervous system. These compounds help regulate sleep patterns, making it easier to fall asleep and stay asleep, which is particularly helpful during travel across time zones. When using Lavandula angustifolia teas to combat jet lag, it's recommended to start drinking the tea a few days before traveling to help adjust your sleep schedule.

Take a warm cup of tea before bedtime to promote relaxation and induce a restful sleep. You can also drink a cup in the morning to help regulate your circadian rhythms and increase alertness. As you incorporate Lavandula angustifolia teas into your routine, you may experience improved sleep quality, reduced fatigue, and a better adaptation to the new time zone. You may also notice a decrease in jet lag symptoms like headaches and stomach upset.

Additionally, the tea's calming properties may help reduce stress and anxiety associated with travel.

5. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas are a popular herbal remedy for jet lag, and it's not hard to see why.

The leaves of the Ginkgo biloba tree contain flavonoids and terpenoids, which are powerful antioxidants that help protect the body from damage caused by free radicals. These free radicals are often created when we travel across time zones and our bodies struggle to adjust. The flavonoids and terpenoids in Ginkgo biloba teas also help improve blood flow and oxygenation to the brain, which can help alleviate fatigue and grogginess that often comes with jet lag. If you're planning to travel across time zones, consider drinking a cup of Ginkgo biloba tea about an hour before you arrive at your destination.

You can also drink it throughout the day to help regulate your body's internal clock. To make the most of it, combine it with other jet lag-fighting strategies, such as staying hydrated, getting some fresh air, and adjusting your sleep schedule as soon as possible. Drinking Ginkgo biloba tea may help you feel more alert and focused, even after a long flight. It can also help reduce the severity of jet lag symptoms, such as headaches and fatigue. Some people may experience improved mood and reduced anxiety, which can make a big difference when you're trying to adjust to a new time zone.

As you drink the tea, pay attention to how your body feels, and adjust your intake based on how you respond.

6. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, have been used for centuries to alleviate symptoms of jet lag.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help to reduce nausea and fatigue. These properties also aid in regulating the body's internal clock and improving digestion, which is often disrupted during long-haul flights.

When experiencing jet lag, drinking ginger tea before and after your flight can help to ease symptoms. Take a cup of ginger tea 30 minutes before your flight to reduce nausea and dizziness, and another cup upon arrival to help your body adjust to the new time zone.

Drinking ginger tea regularly for a few days after your trip may also help to regulate your sleep patterns and reduce the effects of jet lag.

7. Avena sativa

Avena sativa teas, also known as oat straw tea, have been used for centuries to help with jet lag.

The tea is rich in flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which are believed to play a role in regulating the body's internal clock. One of the main constituents, avenacoside, is thought to help reduce stress and anxiety associated with jet lag. When consumed as a tea, Avena sativa is absorbed quickly into the bloodstream, where it can help regulate the body's circadian rhythms. If you're planning to travel across time zones, consider trying Avena sativa tea to help alleviate jet lag symptoms. Start by brewing a cup of Avena sativa tea about an hour before bedtime, ideally 30 minutes after your usual bedtime.

Take small sips, allowing the tea to work its way into your system. You can also consume it in the morning to help regulate your appetite and energy levels. Some people find it helpful to drink a cup in the morning and another in the evening, spaced about 4-6 hours apart. When taking Avena sativa tea for jet lag, you may experience reduced fatigue, improved sleep quality, and a sense of calm. You may also notice that your appetite and energy levels are more balanced, making it easier to adjust to your new time zone. Some people also report improved digestion and reduced bloating, which can be a common side effect of jet lag.

As your body adjusts to its new rhythm, you may find it easier to fall asleep and stay asleep, reducing the likelihood of daytime fatigue.

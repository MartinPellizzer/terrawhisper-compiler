The 8 Best Herbal Tinctures for Tourists Suffering from Jet Lag

When tourists travel across different time zones, their bodies get confused about the new sleep-wake cycle.

This can cause fatigue, headaches, and difficulty concentrating, making it hard to enjoy their trip. The disruption in their routine can also affect their mood and energy levels, leading to irritability and decreased productivity. As a result, tourists may struggle to keep up with their travel plans, and their entire experience can be negatively impacted. Jet lag can also cause digestive issues, insomnia, and a general feeling of being "off.". This can be particularly challenging for travelers who need to be sharp and alert for work or other important activities.

When tourists experience jet lag, they often try various methods to alleviate their symptoms. Some may try to adjust their sleep schedule gradually, while others may rely on medication to help them sleep or stay awake. However, these methods may not be effective for everyone, and some tourists may still struggle to overcome the effects of jet lag. This is where herbal tinctures come in. These liquid extracts are made from plants that have been traditionally used to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality. They can help to calm the nervous system, reduce stress and anxiety, and regulate the body's internal clock.

By using herbal tinctures, tourists can naturally support their body's adjustment to the new time zone and alleviate the symptoms of jet lag.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis tinctures have been used to help alleviate symptoms of jet lag due to their ability to regulate sleep patterns and reduce stress.

This is largely attributed to the presence of valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates, which have a sedative effect on the nervous system. These constituents help to calm the mind and body, making it easier to fall asleep and stay asleep, even when crossing time zones.

To use Valeriana officinalis tinctures for jet lag, take 20-30 drops in water or tea 30 minutes before bedtime. Start with a small dose and gradually increase as needed. It's also essential to establish a consistent sleep schedule and create a relaxing bedtime routine.

By taking Valeriana officinalis tinctures and making these adjustments, you may experience improved sleep quality, reduced fatigue, and a faster recovery from jet lag.

2. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis tinctures are a natural remedy that can help alleviate symptoms of jet lag.

This is because Melissa officinalis, also known as lemon balm, contains properties like rosmarinic acid, beta-caryophyllene, and limonene, which have been shown to have a calming effect on the body. These constituents help regulate the body's internal clock, reduce stress and anxiety, and promote relaxation, making it easier to adjust to a new time zone.

When taking Melissa officinalis tinctures for jet lag, it's recommended to start with a small dose (about 20-30 drops) 30 minutes to 1 hour before bedtime in the new time zone. It's also essential to maintain a consistent sleep schedule and create a relaxing sleep environment to maximize the effects of the tincture. As you take the tincture, you may experience a sense of calmness, reduced jet lag symptoms, and improved sleep quality.

You may also notice that your body starts to adjust to the new time zone faster, and you feel more energized and refreshed during the day.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia tinctures are a natural remedy that can help alleviate symptoms of jet lag.

This is due to its properties and constituents, such as linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their calming and sleep-promoting effects. To use Lavandula angustifolia tinctures for jet lag, you can take a few drops sublingually (under the tongue) 30 minutes to 1 hour before bedtime, or as needed.

Start with a small dose of 5-7 drops and adjust as needed.

Understanding Migraine: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Remedies

Migraine is a type of headache disorder that causes severe pain, usually on one side of the head, often accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light and sound.

If left untreated, migraines can lead to missed work or school days, strained relationships, and a reduced quality of life. Moreover, frequent migraines can increase the risk of depression and anxiety, making it essential to find effective treatments. Herbal remedies, derived from plants, have been used for centuries to alleviate migraine symptoms. Three beneficial herbs for migraines are Foeniculum vulgare (fennel), Zingiber officinale (ginger), and Valeriana officinalis (valerian root). Fennel, rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, has been shown to reduce migraine pain and frequency. Ginger, with its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties, can help alleviate nausea and vomiting associated with migraines.

Valerian root, known for its calming effects, can promote relaxation and reduce stress, common triggers of migraines. Herbal preparations come in various forms, including decoction, tincture, and infusion. Decoction involves boiling the herb in water to release its active compounds, while tincture involves soaking the herb in a solvent, usually ethanol, to create a concentrated liquid extract. Infusion, the most common method, involves steeping the herb in hot water to create a tea-like drink. These preparations can be used separately or in combination to tailor treatment to individual needs. To incorporate herbal remedies into your migraine management plan, consider the following: start with small doses and gradually increase as needed; consult with a healthcare professional before using herbal preparations, especially if you're taking medications or have underlying health conditions; and monitor your symptoms and adjust your treatment plan accordingly.

By exploring herbal remedies and working with a healthcare professional, you can find a more natural and effective way to manage migraines and improve your overall well-being.

What Are The Main Causes Of Migraine?

Migraine is a type of headache disorder that can be very painful.

There are several reasons why people get migraines. One main cause is hormonal fluctuations. This is when the levels of female sex hormones, such as estrogen, change during a woman's menstrual cycle or due to pregnancy or menopause. These hormonal changes can trigger migraine attacks. For example, some women may experience migraines before their period starts or during menopause when hormone levels drop. Genetics is another important cause of migraines.

If you have a family history of migraines, you are more likely to get them too. This is because some people may inherit genes that affect the brain's blood vessels and nerves, making them more prone to migraines. Researchers believe that people with migraines may have a different brain chemistry and nerve function compared to people without migraines. Medication overuse is also a significant cause of migraines. When you take too many pain relievers, such as ibuprofen or acetaminophen, to treat your migraines, it can actually trigger more migraines. This is because your body becomes dependent on these medications and your brain starts to react differently to pain.

As a result, you may experience more frequent and severe migraines.

Below are the main causes of migraine.

  • Hormonal Fluct: Hormonal fluctuations cause migraines because changes in hormone levels, particularly estrogen, can affect blood vessel size and nerve sensitivity, leading to pain and other migraine symptoms.
  • Genetics: Genetics can be a cause of migraine because people who have a family history of migraines are more likely to develop them due to inherited traits that affect how their brain and nervous system respond to triggers.
  • Medication Overuse: When you take pain-relieving medication too often, your body can get used to it and start relying on the medication to get rid of headaches, which can then cause your brain to send more pain signals, leading to more frequent and severe migraines.
  • Menses: Changes in hormone levels, particularly a drop in estrogen, that occur during menstruation can cause blood vessels in the brain to become swollen and inflamed, leading to migraine headaches.
  • Stress: When we feel stressed, our body releases special chemicals called stress hormones, which can cause blood vessels in the brain to swell and trigger a migraine headache.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Migraine?

If you have migraines, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can trigger or worsen the condition.

For example, 'Asafoetida' (Ferula asafoetida) is not recommended as it can cause or worsen headaches due to its potent compounds. 'Kava' (Piper methysticum) should also be avoided, as high doses can lead to headaches and migraines in some people.

'Ginkgo biloba' may also be a problem for some, as it can cause blood vessels to dilate, leading to increased migraine frequency or severity. 'Yohimbe' (Pausinystalia johimbe) contains a compound called yohimbine, which can cause blood vessels to constrict and trigger migraines. Additionally, 'Guarana' (Paullinia cupana) contains a stimulant that can trigger or exacerbate migraines due to its caffeine-like effect.

Finally, 'Cayenne pepper' (Capsicum annuum), particularly in large doses, can trigger or worsen migraines due to its capsaicin content, which can cause blood vessels to dilate.

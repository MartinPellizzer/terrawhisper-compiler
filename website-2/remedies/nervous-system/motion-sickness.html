Motion Sickness: Causes and Natural Treatments with Medicinal Herbs

Motion sickness is a feeling of queasiness and discomfort that occurs when your body gets mixed signals from your senses, like seeing the world move when you're not moving.

This can happen when you're on a boat, car, plane, or any other vehicle that's moving in a way that doesn't match what your body expects. If you don't deal with motion sickness, you might feel weak, dizzy, and tired, and you might even vomit. This can make traveling and daily activities very difficult. Herbal remedies can be a good alternative to traditional motion sickness medication. Another herb that can help with motion sickness is Ginkgo biloba, which is known for its ability to improve blood flow and reduce inflammation. Ginkgo biloba tincture can be taken orally, and it's often combined with other herbs to enhance its effects. However, it's essential to note that Ginkgo biloba can interact with certain medications, so consult with a healthcare professional before using it. Passiflora incarnata, or passionflower, is another herb that can help with motion sickness. It has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help reduce anxiety and nausea associated with motion sickness. Passionflower tea or tincture can be made by steeping dried passionflower in hot water or mixing it with a carrier oil. It's crucial to deal with motion sickness because it can disrupt your daily life and travel plans. Motion sickness can lead to dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, and even more severe health problems if left untreated.

By using herbal remedies like ginger, Ginkgo biloba, and passionflower, you can alleviate motion sickness symptoms and make traveling and daily activities more comfortable.

What Are The Main Causes Of Motion Sickness?

Motion sickness occurs when your body gets confused about its position and movement.

There are several main causes of motion sickness. One of the common causes is a Boat Ride. When you're on a boat, it can move up and down, side to side, or in circles, which can make your body feel uneasy. This is because your body is trying to adjust to the movement of the boat, but your inner ears, which help with balance, are not getting the right signals.

Another cause of motion sickness is a Plane Ride. When you're on a plane, you feel like you're moving forward, but your body is not getting the right signals from your inner ears. This can cause your body to feel queasy and dizzy. Vessel Motion is also a common cause of motion sickness. When you're on a train, bus, or car, it can move up and down, side to side, or in circles, which can make your body feel uneasy.

This is because your body is trying to adjust to the movement of the vessel, but your inner ears are not getting the right signals.

Below are the main causes of motion sickness.

  • Boat Ride: Boat Ride causes motion sickness because the boat moves up and down, side to side, and round in circles, which can confuse the body's balance system and make you feel queasy.
  • Plane Ride: Plane rides cause motion sickness because the body gets mixed signals from the plane's movement, which can cause the inner ear and brain to disagree on the body's position and direction.
  • Vessel Motion: Vessel motion causes motion sickness because the body has trouble understanding the conflicting signals from the inner ear, eyes, and sensory nerves, leading to disorientation and nausea.
  • Ship Motion: Ship motion causes motion sickness because the body receives conflicting signals from the eyes, inner ears, and balance system, making it difficult to understand which way is up and down, and leading to dizziness and nausea.
  • Traveling by Car: Traveling by car can cause motion sickness because the body's balance system, which relies on the inner ear's ability to sense movement, gets mixed signals from the car's movements, such as turns and stops.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Motion Sickness?

If you have motion sickness, there are some herbs you should avoid.

These herbs can make stomach problems worse. The root of the licorice plant (Glycyrrhiza glabra) can also be a problem. It can cause stomach upset, and can make motion sickness symptoms worse.

Another herb to avoid is chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla), but not because it's not good for motion sickness - because it can cause drowsiness and disorientation, which can make motion sickness worse.

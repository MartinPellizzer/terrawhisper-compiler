Some people even experience headaches or eye strain when they're sleep-deprived and their eyes are twitching. It's not just about being tired, though - sleep deprivation can also affect your mood, your appetite, and even your ability to think clearly. When you're not getting enough sleep, your body's stress response is triggered, and that can lead to all sorts of problems, including eye twitching.

So, what can you do to stop your eyes from twitching?. One option is to try using herbal essential oils. These oils are made from plants and can be very soothing. When you inhale them, they can calm your nervous system and help your body relax. This can be especially helpful when you're sleep-deprived and your body is feeling stressed. By using herbal essential oils, you can help calm your eyes and reduce twitching. They can also help you fall asleep more easily, which is a big bonus when you're sleep-deprived. These oils are easy to use - just add a few drops to a diffuser or mix them with a carrier oil and apply them to your skin.

They're a natural way to help your body relax and reduce eye twitching.

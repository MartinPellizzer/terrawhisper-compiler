1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid, valeranone, and isovaleric acid, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

These compounds help to calm the nervous system, reducing muscle spasms and stress that can cause eye twitching. The sedative and anxiolytic properties of Valeriana officinalis essential oils also help to soothe the nervous system, making it an effective natural remedy for eye twitching. The antispasmodic properties of valerenic acid help to relax the muscles around the eyes, reducing spasms and twitching.

By relaxing the nervous system and reducing muscle tension, Valeriana officinalis essential oils can provide relief from eye twitching.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Valeriana officinalis roots and 1 cup of carrier oil like coconut oil or jojoba oil.
  2. Wash the roots with water and pat them dry. Use a sharp knife to chop the roots into small pieces.
  3. Fill a clean glass jar with 2 cups of chopped roots and add 1 cup of carrier oil. Stir well.
  4. Place the jar in a cool dark place and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the roots to infuse their oil into the carrier oil.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and use the essential oil as needed for eye twitching.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have a calming and relaxing effect on the nervous system. Linalool, in particular, has a sedative property that can help reduce muscle tension and spasms that may be contributing to eye twitching. The antispasmodic properties of linalyl acetate can also help to calm the muscles around the eyes, reducing twitching and spasms.

By promoting relaxation and reducing muscle tension, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils may be beneficial in alleviating eye twitching.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat. Add the Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the water.
  3. Let the mixture simmer for 1-2 hours. This will help release the oils from the flowers.
  4. Strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the flowers.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 24 hours to allow the oil to separate from the water. Use a dropper to collect the oil and store it in a dark glass bottle.

3. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains linalool, limonene, and citral as its active constituents.

  2. Let the mixture simmer for 30 minutes, then turn off the heat. Let it sit for 2 hours.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis teas contains the bioactive constituents valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone.

These compounds interact with the nervous system, particularly the brain's neurotransmitters, to promote relaxation and reduce stress. The sedative properties of Valeriana officinalis help to calm the nervous system, which can contribute to eye twitching caused by anxiety and tension. By regulating the nervous system's response to stress, Valeriana officinalis teas may help to alleviate eye twitching and promote a state of calm.

The root's bioactive compounds work to reduce muscle spasms and promote relaxation, making it a potential remedy for eye twitching.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 tablespoon of dried Valeriana officinalis roots.
  2. Measure 1 teaspoon of dried Valeriana officinalis roots and put it in a tea infuser or a small muslin bag.
  3. Steep the Valeriana officinalis roots in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the roots. Let it cool down slightly.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, preferably before bedtime, to help alleviate eye twitching.

What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have dizziness?

If you experience dizziness, it's best to avoid certain herbal essential oils.

Peppermint oil (Mentha piperita) can be too stimulating, causing blood vessels to dilate and making dizziness worse. Hyssop oil (Hyssopus officinalis) and sage oil (Salvia officinalis) may also be problematic, as they can increase heart rate and blood pressure, exacerbating dizziness.

Thyme oil (Thymus vulgaris) and oregano oil (Origanum vulgare) contain compounds that can cause blood vessels to constrict, which may worsen dizziness in some individuals. Additionally, clary sage oil (Salvia sclarea) contains linalool, a compound that can cause dizziness and lightheadedness due to its vasodilatory effects.

It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional or a certified aromatherapist before using these oils, especially if you're prone to dizziness or have any underlying medical conditions.

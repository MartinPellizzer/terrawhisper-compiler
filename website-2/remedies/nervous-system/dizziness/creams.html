7 Herbal Creams For Dizziness

Herbal creams can be a helpful way to relieve dizziness, which is often caused by a drop in blood pressure or an inner ear problem.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains the boiactive constituents gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation in the inner ear, a common cause of dizziness. Gingerols and shogaols also have a sedative effect on the nervous system, which can calm the body's balance system and reduce dizziness. Additionally, Zingiber officinale has a warming effect that can increase blood flow to the inner ear, further reducing dizziness.

By reducing inflammation and promoting blood flow, Zingiber officinale creams may help alleviate dizziness.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of turmeric powder, and 2 tablespoons of ginger powder in a double boiler.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in the double boiler over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the turmeric and ginger powders to the melted mixture and stir well.
  4. Continue heating the mixture for another 10-15 minutes, or until it reaches 120-130°F.
  5. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool. Once cooled, transfer it to a jar and store it in a cool, dark place.

2. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis creams contains a high concentration of valerenic acid and isovaleric acid.

These bioactive constituents have a sedative effect on the nervous system and can help reduce dizziness by calming the brain and body. The valerenic acid also has a muscle-relaxing property that can help alleviate tension and stress, which can contribute to dizziness. The anti-inflammatory properties of Valeriana officinalis can also help reduce swelling in the inner ear, which is a common cause of dizziness.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Valeriana officinalis creams can help alleviate dizziness and improve balance.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Valeriana officinalis root, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 10 drops of lavender essential oil.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water until it reaches 180°F (82°C).
  3. Add the dried Valeriana officinalis root to the melted coconut oil and let it steep for 2-3 hours. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix the beeswax and vitamin E oil until well combined. Add this mixture to the coconut oil mixture and stir until the beeswax is fully dissolved.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool. Once cooled, add 10 drops of lavender essential oil to each container. Your Valeriana officinalis cream for dizziness is now ready to use.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are active constituents that help improve blood flow to the brain.

These compounds, including quercetin and bilobalide, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help prevent blood vessels from becoming too narrow. This improved circulation helps increase oxygen delivery to the brain, which can alleviate dizziness caused by reduced blood flow. The antioxidants in ginkgo biloba also help protect the brain from damage caused by free radicals, further supporting brain health.

By promoting healthy blood flow and protecting the brain, ginkgo biloba creams may be beneficial for people experiencing dizziness.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 2 teaspoons of Ginkgo biloba extract.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler. Stir until smooth.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated.
  4. Add the Ginkgo biloba extract and stir well. Remove from heat.
  5. Pour the mixture into a bowl and let it cool and solidify. Once set, use a spoon to scoop out the cream and apply to affected areas.

By calming the nervous system and regulating bodily functions, Lavandula angustifolia creams can help to alleviate dizziness and promote a sense of balance and well-being.

  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated. Remove from heat.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dizziness from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent dizziness.

Peppermint and ginger teas are known to calm the stomach and ease nausea, which can cause dizziness. Ginger, in particular, has natural properties that can help regulate blood flow and reduce lightheadedness.

This can lead to a sense of stability and balance.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dizziness every day?

Drinking herbal teas for dizziness every day can be safe, but it depends on the tea.

Some teas, like peppermint and ginger, are generally okay in small amounts. However, teas with caffeine or certain herbs like valerian root can interact with medications or worsen dizziness.

It's best to start with small amounts and monitor your body's response.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dizziness?

Herbal teas can help alleviate dizziness, but it depends on the individual and the specific tea.

Some people may notice a difference in a few hours, while others might not feel any improvement for a day or two.

It's also possible that it takes longer, sometimes up to a week, for the body to fully respond to the herbal ingredients.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for dizziness?

If you're feeling dizzy, drinking herbal tea in the morning might help.

This is because a good night's sleep has usually refreshed your body. Having a warm cup of tea as you start your day can be calming and soothing.

It may help reduce dizziness and give you a sense of balance.

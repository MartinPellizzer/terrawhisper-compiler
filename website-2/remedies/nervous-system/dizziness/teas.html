9 Herbal Teas For Dizziness

Herbal teas can be a great way to help with dizziness.

They work by calming the nervous system and improving blood flow to the brain. Valeriana officinalis, also known as valerian root, is an example of a herb that can help with dizziness. It contains compounds that help to relax the muscles and calm the mind, which can reduce feelings of dizziness and lightheadedness. Ginkgo biloba, on the other hand, is an herb that can help improve blood flow to the brain. This can be especially helpful if dizziness is caused by poor circulation or lack of oxygen to the brain. Ginkgo biloba has been shown to improve memory and cognitive function, as well as reduce feelings of dizziness and vertigo.

Another herb that can help with dizziness is Zingiber officinale, or ginger. Ginger has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce nausea and stomach upset, which can be a common cause of dizziness. Ginger can also help to improve digestion and reduce symptoms of motion sickness. Drinking herbal teas can have many benefits beyond just relieving dizziness. They can also help to improve sleep, reduce stress and anxiety, and boost the immune system. Many herbal teas are also naturally caffeine-free, making them a great alternative to traditional tea or coffee.

By incorporating herbal teas into your daily routine, you can take a natural and holistic approach to managing dizziness and improving your overall health.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis teas contains active constituents like valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone, which work together to help alleviate dizziness.

These compounds have sedative properties that help calm the nervous system, reducing the occurrence of dizziness caused by stress or anxiety. Valerenic acid, in particular, has a calming effect on the brain, helping to regulate the body's natural balance and equilibrium. The valeranone component also has a sedative effect, which can help to stabilize the body's internal rhythms and reduce dizziness.

By promoting relaxation and balance, Valeriana officinalis teas can provide relief from dizziness and promote a sense of well-being.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Valeriana officinalis root and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Place the root in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the root and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea and discard the root. Add honey or lemon to taste if desired.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the tea 1-2 times a day as needed to help relieve dizziness.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains gingerols and shogaols, two active compounds that help alleviate dizziness.

Gingerols have anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce nausea and stabilize the stomach, a common cause of dizziness. Shogaols, on the other hand, have a direct effect on the nervous system, helping to calm and regulate its functions. Ginger also has a natural ability to warm the body, which can help improve blood circulation and reduce dizziness caused by cold temperatures.

By combining these properties, ginger tea can provide relief from dizziness and its underlying causes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1/2 teaspoon of dried Zingiber officinale root.
  2. Boil the water in a pot and then reduce the heat to a simmer.
  3. Add the dried Zingiber officinale root to the simmering water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  5. Drink the tea warm or at room temperature, up to 3 times a day, as needed for dizziness relief.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, and terpenoids like ginkgolides and bilobalide.

These bioactive constituents help improve blood flow to the brain by dilating blood vessels and reducing inflammation. By increasing oxygen and nutrient delivery to brain cells, ginkgo biloba reduces dizziness caused by poor circulation. Ginkgolides also inhibit platelet-activating factor, which helps prevent blood clots that can lead to dizziness.

Regular consumption of ginkgo biloba tea may help alleviate dizziness by promoting healthy blood flow and preventing blood clots.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves.
  2. Measure 1 teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves into a tea infuser or a small muslin bag.
  3. Steep the Ginkgo biloba leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the Ginkgo biloba leaves or tea infuser.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the Ginkgo biloba tea 2-3 times a day to help alleviate dizziness.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and beta-pinene, which are active constituents that help alleviate dizziness.

Linalool has a sedative effect on the nervous system, reducing anxiety and stress that can cause dizziness. Linalyl acetate, on the other hand, has a calming effect on the digestive system, which can help prevent dizziness caused by motion or digestive issues. Beta-pinene has a relaxing effect on the blood vessels, improving blood flow and reducing the sensation of dizziness.

By regulating the nervous and digestive systems and improving blood flow, Lavandula angustifolia teas can help alleviate dizziness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Lavandula angustifolia leaves.
  2. Use 1 tablespoon of the leaves and place them in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Boil 1 cup of water and pour it over the Lavandula angustifolia leaves.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the leaves from the tea.
  5. Drink 1/2 cup of the Lavandula angustifolia tea, 2-3 times a day, to help alleviate dizziness.

5. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata teas contains flavonoids and alkaloids, including passifloric acid and harmine, which have a sedative effect on the nervous system.

These compounds help to reduce anxiety and stress that can contribute to dizziness. The flavonoids also have a calming effect on blood vessels, which can help to alleviate symptoms of dizziness caused by low blood pressure. Additionally, Passiflora incarnata teas contains apigenin, a flavonoid that can help to reduce inflammation in the brain, which may contribute to dizziness.

By calming the nervous system and reducing inflammation, Passiflora incarnata teas may help to alleviate symptoms of dizziness and promote a sense of balance and well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Passiflora incarnata leaves. You can use 1:1 ratio of fresh to dried.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of the leaves and place them in a heat-resistant cup or teapot.
  3. Pour 8 ounces (1 cup) of boiling water over the leaves. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid using a cheesecloth or a tea strainer into another cup. Discard the leaves.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of the tea, 2-3 times a day, as needed to help alleviate dizziness.

6. Avena sativa

Avena sativa teas contains a rich mix of bioactive constituents, including avenacosides, avenalol, and beta-sitosterol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce swelling and soothe the nervous system. Avenacosides, in particular, have been found to have a sedative effect on the nervous system, which can help to calm the body and mind. The beta-sitosterol in Avena sativa teas has also been shown to have a stabilizing effect on blood vessels, which can help to alleviate dizziness caused by fluctuations in blood pressure.

Regular consumption of Avena sativa teas may help to reduce the frequency and severity of dizziness episodes by promoting a sense of balance and equilibrium in the body.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 teaspoon of dried Avena sativa root.
  2. Measure 1 cup of boiling water and pour it over the dried Avena sativa root in a cup.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes to allow the active ingredients to infuse.
  4. Strain the mixture using a tea strainer or cheesecloth into another cup.
  5. Drink the Avena sativa tea immediately to help alleviate dizziness.

Foeniculum vulgare teas contains a compound called fenchone, which helps to ease dizziness.

Fenchone is a natural oil that has a sedative effect on the digestive system, reducing nausea and stomach upset. Foeniculum vulgare tea also contains another compound called limonene, which has a stabilizing effect on blood pressure, helping to regulate the body's circulation and reduce dizziness. The tea's anti-inflammatory properties, due in part to the presence of flavonoids and phenolic acids, also contribute to its ability to alleviate dizziness by reducing inflammation in the body.

By regulating the body's internal balance and reducing inflammation, Foeniculum vulgare tea can provide relief from dizziness caused by digestive issues or other factors.

8. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains rosmarinic acid, a key bioactive constituent that helps alleviate dizziness.

This compound has a calming effect on the nervous system and improves blood circulation, which can help reduce the symptoms of dizziness. Rosmarinus officinalis teas also contains carnosic acid, a potent antioxidant that helps protect against oxidative stress and inflammation in the body. The tea's antispasmodic properties help relax the muscles and calm the nervous system, which can help alleviate dizziness and other related symptoms.

By improving blood flow and reducing inflammation, Rosmarinus officinalis teas can help provide relief from dizziness and promote a sense of balance and stability.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 tablespoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.

  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the leaves.
  5. Add honey to taste and drink the tea slowly to help alleviate dizziness.

9. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis teas contains the bioactive constituents rosmarinic acid, apigenin, and linalool, which work together to help alleviate dizziness.

Rosmarinic acid has a calming effect on the nervous system, reducing stress and anxiety that can contribute to dizziness. Apigenin has a sedative effect, which can help to relax the body and mind, reducing the symptoms of dizziness. Linalool, a natural anxiolytic, also helps to calm the nervous system and reduce feelings of anxiety and disorientation.

By reducing stress and anxiety, Melissa officinalis teas helps to regulate the body's balance and equilibrium, alleviating the symptoms of dizziness.

  3. Strain the mixture into a cup to remove the leaves.
  4. Add honey to taste, if needed, to make the tea sweeter.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve dizziness.

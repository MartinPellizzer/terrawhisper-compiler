First, dry the Valerian root properly by spreading it out in a single layer and letting it sit in a warm, dry place for a few days or by placing it in a low-temperature oven (150-200°F) for 2-3 hours. Once the root is dry, chop it into small pieces and fill the glass jar about 2/3 full.

What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have grief?

If you're dealing with grief, it's best to avoid certain herbal essential oils that might trigger feelings of sadness, anxiety, or depression.

One such oil is Galbanum (Ferula galbaniflua), which is known to have a strong, earthy scent that can evoke feelings of melancholy. Another oil to avoid is Sandalwood (Santalum album), which can be grounding but also has a tendency to intensify emotions, making it difficult to cope with grief.

Cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum) and Clove (Syzygium aromaticum) oils can also be overwhelming, as their strong, spicy scents can exacerbate feelings of anxiety and stress. Additionally, the intense, pungent aroma of Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea) can be too much to handle when dealing with grief.

Lastly, the stimulating effects of Peppermint (Mentha piperita) oil may not be suitable for those struggling with grief, as it can heighten emotions and make it harder to relax.

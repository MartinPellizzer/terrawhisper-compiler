8 Herbal Creams For Grief

Melissa officinalis, or lemon balm, is a calming herb that can help reduce stress and anxiety, making it easier to cope with difficult emotions. However, herbal creams can be a helpful addition to your self-care routine, providing a natural and calming way to manage difficult emotions.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis creams contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates as active constituents.

These compounds have a sedative effect, which can help calm the mind and body during times of grief. The sedative properties of valerenic acid and valepotriates can also help regulate sleep patterns, which are often disrupted when experiencing a loss. By reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation, valeriana officinalis creams can help individuals cope with the emotional pain of grief.

The calming and sedative properties of valeriana officinalis creams can provide comfort and relief from the intense emotions associated with grieving.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Valeriana officinalis root, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of dried Valeriana officinalis root to the melted mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Pour the infused mixture into small containers and let it cool. Once cooled, the cream is ready for use.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two main bioactive constituents that contribute to its soothing and calming effects.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce stress and promote relaxation. Lavender's calming properties can also help regulate the body's stress response, leading to improved mood and reduced anxiety. By promoting better sleep quality and duration, Lavandula angustifolia creams can help alleviate symptoms of grief, such as insomnia and fatigue.

The calming and relaxing effects of these creams can also help individuals process their emotions and find a sense of calm during difficult times.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 10 drops of Lavandula angustifolia essential oil.
  2. Melt the beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Once the mixture is melted, remove it from the heat and stir in the distilled water.
  4. Add the Lavandula angustifolia essential oil and stir well to combine. Pour the mixture into a heat-proof container.
  5. Let the mixture cool and thicken. Once it has reached a creamy consistency, your Lavandula angustifolia cream is ready to use.

3. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis creams contains compounds like linalool, linalyl acetate, and geraniol, which are known for their soothing and calming effects.

These active constituents help to reduce anxiety and stress, common symptoms experienced during the grieving process. Melissa officinalis has a sedative property that can help regulate the nervous system, promoting relaxation and reducing feelings of overwhelm. Its anti-inflammatory properties may also aid in reducing physical symptoms like headaches and muscle tension often associated with grief.

By promoting relaxation and reducing physical symptoms, Melissa officinalis creams can provide a sense of calm and comfort during a difficult time.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/2 cup of beeswax.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler until it reaches 120°F.
  3. Add the beeswax to the coconut oil and stir until melted.
  4. Add the Melissa officinalis flowers to the melted mixture and let it steep for 30 minutes.
  5. Strain the mixture and pour it into a container. Let it cool and solidify before use.

4. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum creams contains hypericin and hyperforin, two active constituents that help with grief.

These compounds have antidepressant properties that reduce symptoms of depression and anxiety. Hypericin also has sedative properties, which can help calm the mind and body after a loss. Hyperforin, on the other hand, acts as a serotonin reuptake inhibitor, increasing the levels of serotonin in the brain, which helps regulate mood and emotions.

By using Hypericum perforatum creams, individuals can experience relief from grief-related symptoms and promote a sense of well-being.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Hypericum perforatum extract (St. John's Wort), 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of essential oil (optional).
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Once melted, remove from heat and add the Hypericum perforatum extract, vitamin E oil, and essential oil (if using). Stir until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into a heat-proof container or a silicone mold to set. Let it cool and harden completely.
  5. Once set, remove from the mold and store the Hypericum perforatum cream in an airtight container in a cool, dark place. Use as needed to help with grief.

5. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata creams contains flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, particularly harmine, harmaline, and passifloric acid.

These compounds have a sedative effect, helping to calm the mind and body. The alkaloids, especially harmine, have a potent anxiolytic property, which can alleviate anxiety and stress associated with grief. The flavonoids, such as passifloric acid, have antioxidant properties that help reduce inflammation and promote relaxation.

By reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation, Passiflora incarnata creams may help individuals cope with the emotional pain of grief.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Passiflora incarnata flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the dried Passiflora incarnata flowers to the melted oil and wax, and let it steep for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Add the vitamin E oil to the mixture and stir well. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify before use.

6. Avena sativa

Avena sativa creams contains a rich mixture of bioactive constituents, including avenanthramides, avenacosides, and lignans.

These compounds work together to promote relaxation and reduce stress by interacting with the body's neurotransmitters and hormones. The lignans in Avena sativa creams have antioxidant properties, which help to soothe and calm the mind and body. The avenanthramides also have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to reduce feelings of anxiety and discomfort.

By reducing stress and promoting relaxation, Avena sativa creams can help to alleviate symptoms of grief and promote emotional well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Avena sativa seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil.
  2. Soak the Avena sativa seeds in water for 8 hours or overnight. Then, blend the soaked seeds with 2 cups of water until smooth.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a nut milk bag into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Mix the strained liquid with 2 cups of carrier oil in a double boiler. Heat the mixture until it reaches 120°F to 140°F.
  5. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool. Once cooled, strain the mixture again through a cheesecloth or a nut milk bag into a container. Store the Avena sativa cream in the fridge for up to 1 week.

7. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids, including quercetin, kaempferol, and bilobalide.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce stress and anxiety caused by grief. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba creams also improve blood flow to the brain, which can help with memory and concentration issues often experienced during times of bereavement. The terpenoids, particularly bilobalide, have a calming effect on the nervous system, reducing feelings of sadness and emotional distress.

By addressing these physical and emotional symptoms, Ginkgo biloba creams may provide some relief for individuals experiencing grief.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of ginkgo biloba extract, and 10 drops of lavender essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Add ginkgo biloba extract and lavender essential oil. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once set, your ginkgo biloba cream is ready to use.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent grief from forming?

Drinking herbal tea might help with grief, but it's not a guarantee.

Some teas, like chamomile, have calming effects that can soothe emotional pain. This might make it easier to cope with loss, but grief is a natural process that can't be completely prevented.

It can still be a difficult time, but herbal tea might make it slightly more manageable.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for grief every day?

Some herbal teas, like chamomile and lavender, can help with grief.

They have calming effects and might reduce stress. However, it's essential to consume them in moderation. Drinking them daily can lead to dependency on the calming effects, which might not address the underlying issue of grief.

It's best to balance tea consumption with other coping methods.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in grief?

Herbal teas can help ease grief, but it's different for everyone.

Some people might feel a bit better after a day or two, while others might need a week or more to notice a difference.

It's like their body and mind are slowly unwinding and finding a new balance.

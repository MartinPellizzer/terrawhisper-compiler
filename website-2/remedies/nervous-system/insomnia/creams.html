1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis creams contains valerenic acid and valepotriates, which are the main active constituents that help with insomnia.

These compounds have a sedative effect on the nervous system, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety. Valerenic acid also has a calming effect on the brain, slowing down the activity of neurons and promoting a restful sleep. The valepotriates in Valeriana officinalis creams have a sedative effect on the central nervous system, which helps to reduce stress and anxiety, making it easier to fall asleep.

By reducing stress and anxiety and promoting relaxation, Valeriana officinalis creams can help individuals with insomnia to get a good night's sleep.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Valeriana officinalis root, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Infuse the dried Valeriana officinalis root in 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut oil) for 2-3 weeks, shaking the mixture every day.
  3. Strain the infused oil and discard the solids. Add the beeswax, shea butter, and vitamin E oil to the infused oil in a double boiler.
  4. Heat the mixture until the beeswax and shea butter melt. Stir well and remove from heat.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, your Valeriana officinalis cream for insomnia is ready to use.

2. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata creams contains flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides as its bioactive constituents.

The flavonoids, particularly flavonoids like kaempferol and quercetin, have a sedative effect on the nervous system, helping to calm the mind and body before sleep. The alkaloids, such as harmine and harmaline, have a central nervous system depressant effect, which can help to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation. The glycosides, including passifloric acid, have anti-inflammatory properties that may contribute to improved sleep quality by reducing inflammation and promoting a restful night's sleep.

By combining these properties, Passiflora incarnata creams may help individuals with insomnia by promoting relaxation, reducing anxiety, and improving sleep quality.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Passiflora incarnata flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 1 tablespoon of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth.
  3. Add the dried Passiflora incarnata flowers to the melted mixture and let it steep for 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Add the vitamin E oil to the mixture and stir well. Pour the cream into a glass jar and store it in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two active constituents that have been shown to promote relaxation.

These compounds work by reducing anxiety and stress, which are common causes of insomnia. The sedative properties of linalool and linalyl acetate help to calm the mind and body, making it easier to fall asleep. The antispasmodic properties of these compounds also help to relax tense muscles, further contributing to a restful night's sleep.

By reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation, Lavandula angustifolia creams can help individuals with insomnia to get the restful sleep they need.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of coconut oil in a small bowl.
  2. Combine the dried flowers and coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Heat the mixture for 2-3 hours, or until the oil has turned a light purple color and the flowers have lost their fragrance.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Mix 2 tablespoons of the infused oil with 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter in a small saucepan over low heat, stirring until the beeswax has melted and the mixture has cooled slightly.

4. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, apigenin, and luteolin, which are responsible for its calming properties.

These compounds help to reduce stress and anxiety, common causes of insomnia. Rosmarinic acid also has a sedative effect, promoting relaxation and reducing muscle tension. Apigenin binds to GABA receptors in the brain, which can lead to a sense of calmness and tranquility.

By reducing stress and promoting relaxation, Melissa officinalis creams can help improve the quality of sleep.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Melissa officinalis leaves and 1 cup of coconut oil in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in the saucepan over low heat for 2-3 hours.
  3. Add the dried Melissa officinalis leaves to the coconut oil and let it steep for 4 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture and discard the leaves. Let the oil cool down.
  5. Mix 2 tablespoons of the cooled oil with 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter to create a creamy texture. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and harden before use.

5. Avena sativa

Avena sativa creams contains a compound called avenacoside, which is also known as 5-hydroxyavanine.

This compound is derived from the oat plant and has been used for centuries to promote relaxation and reduce stress. Avenacoside has sedative properties that help to calm the nervous system and reduce anxiety, making it easier to fall asleep. Additionally, avena sativa creams also contain other bioactive constituents such as avenanthramides, which have anti-inflammatory properties and can help to soothe the skin and promote relaxation.

By reducing stress and promoting relaxation, avena sativa creams can help to alleviate insomnia symptoms and improve sleep quality.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Avena sativa (oat straw) flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Combine the dried Avena sativa flowers and coconut oil in a saucepan, heat over low heat for 2 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture and discard the Avena sativa flowers, then add the beeswax and heat until melted.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 10 minutes, then stir until the mixture thickens.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container, let it cool and solidify, then store in a cool place.

6. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active constituents like gingerols and shogaols.

Regular use of Zingiber officinale creams may lead to improved sleep patterns and reduced sleep disturbances.

8 Herbal Creams for Older Adults to Manage Insomnia

As people get older, their bodies go through changes that can affect their sleep.

Older adults may experience difficulty sleeping due to a variety of factors, such as changes in their sleep patterns, health conditions, or the use of certain medications. This can lead to insomnia, which is characterized by difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep. Insomnia can have a significant impact on a person's daily life, affecting their mood, energy levels, and ability to perform daily tasks. It can also increase the risk of falls, accidents, and other health problems. Older adults who suffer from insomnia may feel tired, irritable, and anxious, which can further exacerbate the problem. Insomnia can also lead to social isolation, as older adults may avoid social engagements or activities that they once enjoyed. It's not just a matter of getting a good night's sleep, it's about maintaining a good quality of life.

In older adults, insomnia can be caused by a range of factors, including hormonal changes, sleep disorders, chronic pain, and mental health conditions. It can also be triggered by lifestyle factors, such as a irregular sleep schedule, a stimulating environment, or the use of electronic devices before bedtime. Older adults who experience insomnia may try various treatments, such as medication, therapy, or lifestyle changes. However, these treatments may not always be effective, and older adults may be looking for alternative solutions. Herbal creams for insomnia are a type of topical treatment that can be applied to the skin before bedtime. These creams contain plant extracts that are known for their calming and relaxing properties. When applied to the skin, these extracts can help to promote relaxation, reduce stress and anxiety, and improve sleep quality. The active ingredients in these creams can also help to reduce inflammation and pain, which can contribute to insomnia.

By providing a topical solution, herbal creams can be a convenient and non-invasive way to address insomnia in older adults.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis creams are a popular natural remedy for insomnia, helping to promote a restful night's sleep.

The creams contain the root of the valerian plant, which is rich in a compound called valerenic acid, as well as other active constituents like valepotriates and isovaleric acid. These properties have a calming effect on the nervous system, reducing anxiety and stress that can disrupt sleep. To try using Valeriana officinalis creams for insomnia, apply a small amount to your skin about an hour before bedtime, and gently massage it into your skin.

Take slow, deep breaths and focus on relaxing your muscles as you apply the cream. As you drift off to sleep, the active constituents in the cream work to calm your mind and body, helping you to fall into a deeper, more restful sleep. You may find that you wake up feeling refreshed and rejuvenated, with improved mood and energy levels.

Some people also report improved sleep quality, with fewer nighttime wakings and a more consistent sleep schedule.

2. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata creams, also known as passionflower creams, are a topical treatment used to help with insomnia.

These creams contain properties like flavonoids and alkaloids, which work together to promote relaxation and reduce anxiety. The flavonoids, particularly kaempferol and quercetin, have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects that may help calm the mind and body, making it easier to fall asleep. The alkaloids, such as harman and harmine, also contribute to the sedative effects of the cream. If you're considering using Passiflora incarnata creams for insomnia, start by applying a small amount to your skin before bedtime. You can apply it to your wrists, temples, or behind your ears.

It's essential to follow the instructions provided with the product and start with a low dose to see how your body reacts. As you use the cream, you may notice a reduction in your anxiety levels, making it easier to fall asleep. You may also wake up feeling more refreshed and alert. As you continue to use the cream, you may experience improved sleep quality, reduced symptoms of insomnia, and a decrease in anxiety and stress levels. However, it's crucial to remember that everyone's body is different, and results may vary.

If you're taking prescription medications or have underlying medical conditions, consult with your healthcare provider before using Passiflora incarnata creams.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams have been found to help with insomnia due to their calming properties, which are largely attributed to the presence of linalool and linalyl acetate.

These constituents are known to interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, promoting relaxation and reducing stress levels. When applied topically to the skin, these creams can help to soothe the body and mind, making it easier to fall asleep. To get started, try applying a small amount of Lavandula angustifolia cream to your pulse points, such as your wrists or temples, about 30 minutes before bedtime.

You can also add a few drops of the essential oil to your bath water for a relaxing soak. The likely outcomes of using Lavandula angustifolia creams for insomnia include improved sleep quality, increased relaxation, and reduced anxiety levels. Some users may also experience a decrease in the frequency and duration of sleep disturbances.

However, it's essential to note that individual results may vary, and it's always a good idea to consult with a healthcare professional before using any new product, especially if you're experiencing chronic sleep issues.

When using Melissa officinalis creams, you may notice a reduction in stress and anxiety, making it easier to fall asleep and stay asleep throughout the night. 8. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla creams have been used for centuries to help people with insomnia.

The active compounds in chamomile, such as apigenin and luteolin, have a calming effect on the brain and body. These compounds bind to GABA receptors, which helps to slow down brain activity and promote relaxation. Chamomile also contains flavonoids and terpenoids that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety. To use Matricaria chamomilla creams for insomnia, apply a small amount to your pulse points before bed.

You can also add a few drops of chamomile essential oil to your bath water or inhale it through steam inhalation. Start with a small dose and gradually increase as needed. It's also essential to establish a bedtime routine, such as reading a book or practicing gentle stretches, to signal to your body that it's time to sleep. By taking these steps, you may find that you fall asleep faster, sleep more soundly, and wake up feeling more refreshed. You may also experience reduced anxiety and stress levels, which can help to improve your overall well-being.

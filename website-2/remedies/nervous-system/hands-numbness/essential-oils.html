1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like eucalyptol and bornyl acetate.

These components have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help reduce swelling and ease muscle tension in the hands. The cooling effect of eucalyptol can also help numbness by increasing blood flow and reducing pain. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of the essential oil's polyphenol content, such as 1,8-cineole and limonene, may help protect nerve cells from damage.

By reducing inflammation and protecting nerve cells, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help alleviate numbness in the hands.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a large bowl.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the bowl.
  3. Heat the mixture in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Steep the Eucalyptus leaves in the mixture for 2 hours or overnight, then strain the leaves.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place for later use.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains rosinic acid, bornyl acetate, and camphor as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which help to reduce numbness and tingling sensations in the hands. Camphor, in particular, has a vasodilating effect, which increases blood flow to the affected areas and helps to relieve numbness. The essential oil's antispasmodic properties also help to relax muscles and reduce tension, further alleviating numbness.

By promoting blood circulation and reducing muscle spasms, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help to alleviate numbness in the hands.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil such as jojoba oil.
  5. Store the bottle in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the bottle daily to help the oil extract from the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.

3. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains limonene, menthone, and menthyl acetate as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties, which help alleviate numbness in hands by reducing pain and swelling. Menthyl acetate also has a vasodilatory effect, which can increase blood flow to the affected area, promoting healing and relief from numbness. The antispasmodic properties of menthone help relax muscles and calm nerve endings, further reducing numbness and discomfort.

By applying Mentha x piperita essential oils topically, the bioactive constituents can be absorbed through the skin, providing localized relief from numbness in the hands.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh peppermint leaves (Mentha x piperita).
  2. Wash the leaves in cold water, then dry them with a towel.
  3. Use a steam distiller or a double boiler to heat 2 cups of water.
  4. Add the peppermint leaves to the distiller or double boiler and let the steam extract the oil for 2-3 hours.
  5. Strain the mixture and separate the oil from the water. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce numbness in the hands by alleviating pain and inflammation. Linalool, in particular, has a vasodilatory effect, which increases blood flow to the affected area and promotes healing. The antispasmodic properties of linalyl acetate also help to relax muscles and reduce tension, which can contribute to numbness in the hands.

By reducing pain, inflammation, and muscle tension, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to alleviate numbness in the hands.

  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over low heat until it starts to simmer.
  3. Add the Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the saucepan and let them steep for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, discarding the solids.

5. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol and carvacrol, which are two main bioactive constituents that help with numbness in hands.

Thymol has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and pain, while carvacrol helps to increase blood flow to the affected area. Thymus vulgaris essential oils also has antioxidant properties that protect against cell damage and promote healing. This essential oil can also help to reduce muscle spasms and stiffness, which can contribute to numbness in the hands.

When applied topically, thymus vulgaris essential oils can help to restore sensation and relieve numbness in the hands.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and flowers. Wash them with water to remove dirt.
  2. Combine the leaves and flowers in a blender. Blend until you get a smooth paste.
  3. Transfer the paste to a saucepan and add 2 cups of water. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  5. Transfer the strained liquid to a dark glass bottle. Store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil on your hands to relieve numbness.

6. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains several bioactive constituents, including carnosic acid, ursolic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce swelling and promote blood flow to the hands. The anti-inflammatory properties of carnosic acid and ursolic acid can also help to reduce numbness and tingling in the hands by reducing inflammation in the nerves. Rosmarinic acid's antioxidant properties can help to protect the nerves from damage and promote healthy nerve function.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healthy nerve function, Salvia officinalis essential oils may help to alleviate numbness in the hands.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves, a clean glass jar, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Chop 1/4 cup of the Salvia leaves and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour 1 cup of the carrier oil over the chopped leaves and stir well.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the oil extract from the leaves.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a new glass bottle and discard the solids.

7. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its therapeutic properties.

Gingerol has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which help reduce pain and swelling in the hands. The warming effect of gingerol increases blood flow to the affected area, promoting healing and relaxation of muscles. The essential oils also contain zingiberene and bisabolol, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe and calm the nervous system.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help alleviate numbness in the hands.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (Ginger) roots and 2 cups of carrier oil (like Coconut or Jojoba oil).
  2. Chop the ginger roots into small pieces and add them to a clean glass jar with a lid. Pour the carrier oil over the ginger.
  3. Close the jar and store it in a cool, dark place. Let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.

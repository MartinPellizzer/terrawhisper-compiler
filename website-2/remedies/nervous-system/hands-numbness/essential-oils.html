Numbness in hands: 7 herbal essential oils for typists

Typists, especially those who spend long hours typing on a computer, can suffer from numbness in their hands.

This happens because the constant pressure on the wrist and fingers, combined with poor posture and repetitive movements, can damage the nerves and blood vessels in the hand. As a result, typists may experience numbness, tingling, and weakness in their hands, making it difficult for them to type accurately and efficiently. This numbness can also lead to discomfort, pain, and fatigue, which can affect their mood and productivity. In severe cases, it can even cause long-term damage to the nerves and blood vessels, leading to chronic pain and limited mobility. For typists, numbness in hands can be a significant problem, affecting not only their work but also their personal life.

They may struggle to perform everyday tasks, such as cooking or cleaning, and may even experience difficulties in their social and recreational activities. This can lead to frustration, stress, and anxiety, which can further exacerbate the numbness and pain. Herbal essential oils can be a natural and effective way to help typists treat numbness in hands. These oils are derived from plants and contain powerful compounds that can help to reduce inflammation, improve blood flow, and relax the muscles. When inhaled or applied topically, these oils can help to calm the nervous system, reduce pain and discomfort, and promote healing and recovery.

By using herbal essential oils regularly, typists can help to alleviate numbness and pain, improve their hand function, and enhance their overall well-being.

1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils, also known as rosemary essential oil, has been found to help alleviate numbness in hands due to its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

It contains constituents like camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate, which work together to reduce pain and inflammation in the affected area. The analgesic properties help to numb the pain, while the anti-inflammatory properties reduce swelling and promote healing. To use rosemary essential oil for numbness in hands, you can mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and apply it topically to the affected area. You can also add a few drops to your bath water or inhale it through steam inhalation. When using rosemary essential oil, it's essential to dilute it with a carrier oil as it can be quite potent.

Start with a small amount and gradually increase as needed. It's also recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or allergies. When using rosemary essential oil for numbness in hands, you may experience a reduction in pain and inflammation, allowing you to regain sensation and mobility in your hands. You may also notice an improvement in circulation, which can help to promote healing and reduce numbness. Additionally, rosemary essential oil has been found to have antioxidant properties, which can help to protect your skin and underlying tissues from damage.

By using rosemary essential oil in conjunction with other treatments, you may find that your numbness in hands improves over time.

Numbness in Hands of Typists: 7 Herbal Creams for Instant Relief

Typists often spend long hours sitting in front of their computers, typing away on their keyboards.

This repetitive motion can put a lot of strain on their hands and wrists, leading to numbness and tingling sensations. When this numbness sets in, it can be really uncomfortable and affect their work and daily life. They might experience difficulty typing, holding objects, or even performing simple tasks like dressing or cooking. This numbness can be caused by pressure on the nerves in the wrist, known as carpal tunnel syndrome, or by muscle fatigue and strain. It's not just the physical discomfort that's a problem, but also the emotional toll it can take - feeling frustrated and helpless when they can't do the things they normally can.

For typists who suffer from numbness in their hands, finding a solution can be a challenge. Some try taking breaks and stretching their hands and wrists, while others turn to physical therapy or surgery. However, there are also some natural remedies that can help alleviate numbness and discomfort. One option is using herbal creams that are specifically designed to target numbness and tingling sensations. These creams work by reducing inflammation and relaxing muscles, which can help ease pressure on the nerves and restore feeling to the hands.

They're often easy to apply, gentle on the skin, and can be used throughout the day as needed.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams are being used by some people to help with numbness in their hands.

This is because Ginkgo biloba contains compounds that improve blood flow and reduce inflammation. The main constituents, flavonoids and terpenoids, help to dilate blood vessels, allowing more oxygen and nutrients to reach the hands. This increased blood flow can help to relieve numbness and tingling sensations. If you're considering using Ginkgo biloba cream for numbness in your hands, start by applying a small amount to the affected area 2-3 times a day.

You can also try massaging the cream into your skin to help increase blood flow. It's essential to follow the product instructions and consult with a healthcare professional before using any new cream, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions. When using Ginkgo biloba cream, you may notice improvements in numbness and tingling sensations within a few days to a week. Some people may experience relief from pain and stiffness in their hands. However, it's also possible that you may not notice any significant changes, or that the numbness may return once you stop using the cream.

It's crucial to be patient and consistent with your application to see if Ginkgo biloba cream works for you.

To try Zingiber officinale creams for numbness in hands, apply a small amount to the affected area 2-3 times a day.

Gently massage the cream into your skin until it is fully absorbed. It's essential to start with a low concentration and gradually increase as needed. When using Zingiber officinale creams, you may experience a reduction in numbness and tingling sensations in your hands within a few days. Improved circulation and reduced inflammation may also lead to a decrease in pain and discomfort.

However, if the numbness persists or worsens, it's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional to rule out any underlying conditions that may require medical attention.

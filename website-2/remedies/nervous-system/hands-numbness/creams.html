1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains a high concentration of linalool and linalyl acetate, two key active constituents responsible for its therapeutic properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and vasodilating properties, which help to improve blood circulation to the hands. The increased blood flow reduces numbness and tingling sensations caused by poor circulation. The calming effect of Lavandula angustifolia creams also helps to reduce muscle tension and promote relaxation, further alleviating numbness in the hands.

By applying Lavandula angustifolia creams to the affected area, users can experience relief from numbness and improve their overall hand comfort.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 10 drops of Lavandula angustifolia essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl in the microwave for 30-second intervals, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until fully incorporated.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool slightly. Add Lavandula angustifolia essential oil and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let cool completely before use. Apply a small amount to affected hands as needed.

2. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium creams contains essential oils with sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and triterpenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and discomfort in the hands. The sesquiterpenes in Achillea millefolium, specifically chamazulene, have anti-spasmodic properties that can help relax muscles and improve blood flow to the affected area. The flavonoids present in the cream, such as kaempferol and quercetin, have antioxidant properties that can help protect the nerves and reduce numbness.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healthy nerve function, Achillea millefolium creams may help alleviate numbness in the hands.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Achillea millefolium flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the dried flowers to the melted coconut oil and let it steep for 2-3 hours, then strain the mixture.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix 1/4 cup of beeswax with the infused oil and heat the mixture until the beeswax is melted.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify. Store the creams in a cool, dry place.

3. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum creams contains active constituents like hyperforin and emodin, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

Regular use of Hypericum perforatum creams may help restore sensation and mobility in the affected area.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Hypericum perforatum (St. John's Wort) oil, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.

  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the Ginkgo biloba extract.

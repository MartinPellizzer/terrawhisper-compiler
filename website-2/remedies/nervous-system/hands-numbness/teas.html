Numbness in hands: 8 herbal teas for musicians

Musicians are at risk of developing numbness in their hands due to the repetitive strain caused by playing instruments, especially those that require complex finger movements like the piano or guitar.

This numbness, also known as peripheral neuropathy, can be painful and uncomfortable, making it difficult for musicians to perform their daily tasks. In severe cases, it can even lead to permanent nerve damage and affect their ability to play their instrument, which can be devastating for a career built on music. The numbness can also cause muscle weakness, fatigue, and a loss of coordination, making everyday activities a challenge. As a result, many musicians struggle to maintain their performance level, and some are even forced to take breaks or alter their playing style. This can be frustrating and demotivating, especially for those who have dedicated their lives to music.

The numbness in musicians' hands can be caused by a combination of factors, including poor posture, inadequate instrument setup, and insufficient hand-strengthening exercises. Additionally, some musicians may be more prone to developing numbness due to their genetic makeup or existing medical conditions. Whatever the cause, it's essential for musicians to take proactive steps to prevent and treat numbness in their hands. Herbal teas have become a popular remedy among musicians to help alleviate numbness in their hands. These teas are made from natural ingredients that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce swelling and promote healing in the affected areas. By drinking herbal teas regularly, musicians can help to reduce their symptoms, improve their circulation, and support their overall hand health.

This can be especially beneficial for those who are unable to take time off from playing or need to maintain their performance level.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas are known to help alleviate numbness in hands due to their unique properties and constituents.

The leaves of the Ginkgo biloba tree contain flavonoids, terpenoids, and bilobalide, which work together to improve blood flow and reduce inflammation in the body. This increased blood flow helps to nourish the nerves in the hands, which can become numb due to poor circulation or nerve damage. When you drink Ginkgo biloba tea, the flavonoids and terpenoids are absorbed into the bloodstream, where they can help to dilate blood vessels and promote the delivery of oxygen and nutrients to the hands. If you're experiencing numbness in your hands and want to try Ginkgo biloba tea, start by drinking one cup per day, ideally in the morning. Look for a tea that contains a standardized extract of Ginkgo biloba, with a concentration of 24% flavone glycosides and 6% terpene lactones.

You can steep a teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes to make a tea. As you drink the tea, you may notice an improvement in blood flow to your hands, which can help to reduce numbness and tingling sensations. It's also essential to maintain a healthy lifestyle, including regular exercise, a balanced diet, and sufficient sleep, to support overall nerve health. As you continue to drink Ginkgo biloba tea, you may experience a gradual reduction in numbness and tingling sensations in your hands. You may also notice an improvement in your overall circulation and nerve function, which can lead to better dexterity and hand mobility.

However, it's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before making any significant changes to your diet or supplement routine, especially if you're experiencing persistent or severe numbness in your hands.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, may help with numbness in hands due to its anti-inflammatory properties and the presence of compounds like gingerol and shogaol.

These components can help reduce swelling and ease pain in the hands and fingers. The warming effect of ginger tea may also improve blood circulation, which can help to restore sensation and feeling in the hands. To try ginger tea for numbness in hands, start by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of fresh ginger in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the liquid and add honey to taste.

Drink 2-3 cups a day, ideally after meals or before bedtime. You can also add other herbs like cinnamon or turmeric to enhance the anti-inflammatory effects. Some people may experience relief from numbness within a few days of regular consumption, while others may need to continue drinking ginger tea for several weeks to notice improvements. As you drink ginger tea regularly, you may notice a decrease in pain and numbness in your hands, allowing you to perform daily activities with more ease and precision. You may also experience improved circulation and reduced inflammation, which can help to prevent future episodes of numbness.

However, if your numbness persists or worsens, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

3. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum teas, also known as Ceylon cinnamon, may help alleviate numbness in hands due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The tea contains compounds like cinnamaldehyde, cinnamic acid, and eugenol, which have been shown to improve blood flow and reduce swelling in the body. This can be beneficial for people experiencing numbness in their hands, often caused by poor circulation or nerve compression. If you're considering trying Cinnamomum verum teas for numbness in hands, start by drinking 1-2 cups per day, brewed from 1/2 teaspoon of cinnamon powder per cup of boiling water.

You can also add honey or lemon to make it more palatable. To enhance its effects, try taking a warm bath or shower before drinking the tea to relax your muscles and improve circulation. Taking Cinnamomum verum teas regularly may lead to improved circulation, reduced inflammation, and relief from numbness in your hands. However, it's essential to note that if you experience persistent or severe numbness, you should consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

Additionally, people with diabetes, high blood pressure, or allergies should consult their doctor before consuming Cinnamomum verum teas, as they may interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent numbness in hands from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent numbness in hands from forming due to its blood flow-boosting properties.

Herbal teas like ginkgo biloba and ginger are known to improve circulation, reducing the risk of numbness and tingling.

Regular consumption of these teas may help keep your hands warm and circulation-rich, potentially preventing numbness.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for numbness in hands every day?

Herbal teas can be a good option for numbness in hands, but consuming them every day may not be safe for everyone. Some herbs, like ginkgo biloba and ginger, can cause stomach problems or interact with medications if taken in large amounts or for a long time.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in numbness in hands?

Herbal teas can help with numbness in hands, but it depends on the tea and the severity of the numbness.

Some teas, like ginkgo biloba and ginger, may start to show results within a few days to a week.

Others, like turmeric and cinnamon, might take a bit longer, around 2-4 weeks.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for numbness in hands?

For numbness in your hands, try drinking herbal tea in the morning or early afternoon.

These times allow the herbal ingredients to circulate throughout your body and help alleviate numbness.

As you go about your day, the effects may become more noticeable, helping to increase sensation and comfort in your hands.

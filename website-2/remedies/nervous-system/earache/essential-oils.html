What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have earache?

If you have an earache, there are some herbal essential oils you should avoid using.

These include 'Peppermint' oil, 'Wintergreen' oil, and 'Birch' oil, all of which contain a chemical called methyl salicylate. This chemical can be too strong for the sensitive area around the ear and may irritate the eardrum.

'Clove' oil, while sometimes used for pain relief, can also be too potent and may cause further discomfort. 'Frankincense' and 'Ginger' oils may also not be suitable, as they can be too intense and may exacerbate the pain.

Using these oils near or in the ear can lead to further irritation or even eardrum damage.

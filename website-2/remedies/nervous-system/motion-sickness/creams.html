A Cure for the Seas: 10 Herbal Creams for Boat Riders with Motion Sickness

When you're out on a boat, the movement of the water can affect your body in a way that makes you feel queasy.

This is because your body is trying to balance the movement of the boat with the movement of the land you're standing on, and sometimes it can get confused. This feeling of queasiness can be uncomfortable and even painful, and it's not just a minor annoyance - it can actually stop you from enjoying your time on the boat. For many people, motion sickness can be a major problem that ruins their love of boating and even keeps them from going out on the water at all. It can also affect their daily lives, especially if they need to travel by boat for work or other important reasons.

If you're one of the people who suffers from motion sickness while boating, you know how frustrating it can be. You might feel sick to your stomach, get headaches, or even experience vomiting. It's not just the physical symptoms that are a problem - it can also be really embarrassing to be sick in front of other people. But there is some good news for boat riders who suffer from motion sickness. There are creams that can help, and they work by applying a certain type of plant-based ingredient to your skin.

When you apply this cream to your wrists or temples, the ingredient starts to work quickly, helping to calm your body and stop the motion sickness.

1. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata creams have been used to help with motion sickness due to their unique combination of properties and constituents.

The cream is made from the extract of the Passiflora incarnata plant, which contains flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides that have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. These compounds help to calm the stomach and reduce nausea by blocking the release of serotonin, a chemical that can trigger motion sickness. When applied topically, the cream can also help to relax muscles and reduce anxiety, further alleviating symptoms. To use Passiflora incarnata creams for motion sickness, apply a small amount to your wrists, temples, or behind your ears about 30 minutes before traveling.

You can also apply it as needed during your journey. Some people find it helpful to apply the cream to their stomach area as well. When you take action by applying the cream, you may notice a reduction in nausea and vomiting. Your stomach may feel calmer, and you may be able to travel without feeling queasy. You may also find that you're able to relax more easily and enjoy your journey.

However, keep in mind that everyone's body is different, and it may take some trial and error to find the right amount and application method that works for you.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains a compound called gingerol, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce nausea and vomiting.

Gingerol also has a drying effect on the stomach, which can help alleviate the symptoms of motion sickness. In addition, the creams contain zingerone, a compound that has anti-emetic properties, meaning it helps prevent vomiting. The warming and soothing effects of the creams also help to calm the stomach and reduce discomfort.

The combination of these active constituents and properties makes Zingiber officinale creams a potential remedy for motion sickness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of ginger essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from heat and stir in the vitamin E oil and ginger essential oil.
  4. Pour the mixture into small tin containers or a silicone ice cube tray. Let it cool and harden completely.
  5. Once hardened, label the containers and store them in a cool, dark place for up to 6 months.

Additionally, valepotriates have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce the severity of motion sickness symptoms.

By addressing the underlying causes of motion sickness, Valeriana officinalis creams can provide relief from nausea, dizziness, and other symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of dried Valeriana officinalis root, and 2 tablespoons of jojoba oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted oil mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.

7 Herbal Teas For Motion Sickness

Pilots are at high risk of getting motion sickness.

This is because they have to fly planes, which can be really tough on their inner ears and balance system. When a plane moves, it can send mixed signals to the brain, making it hard to keep your balance and causing nausea, headaches, and even vomiting. For some pilots, this can be a constant problem that affects their work and daily life. They might feel tired, irritable, and even anxious due to the constant struggle with motion sickness.

It's not just about feeling unwell, either - it can also impact their performance and safety record. Pilots who experience motion sickness might have to take time off work or find themselves constantly on the lookout for new remedies to help manage their symptoms. As a result, pilots are always on the lookout for ways to prevent or treat motion sickness. One option they're exploring is herbal teas, which are natural and non-invasive ways to help calm the stomach and reduce nausea. These teas have been around for centuries and are known for their soothing properties, which can help to ease the symptoms of motion sickness.

They're often caffeine-free and come in a variety of flavors, making them a convenient option for pilots who want to try something new to help manage their symptoms.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties and can help alleviate nausea and vomiting associated with motion sickness.

The volatile oils in ginger, such as zingerone and bisabolol, have natural sedative effects and can calm the stomach, making it easier to digest food. When you're feeling queasy on a boat, plane, or car ride, try taking a few sips of ginger tea before your journey starts. You can also drink it as needed throughout the trip.

To make ginger tea, steep fresh ginger in hot water for 3-5 minutes, then strain and drink. You can also add honey or lemon to taste. Start with small amounts (about 1/4 teaspoon of fresh ginger per cup of water) and adjust to your liking. If you're prone to motion sickness, consider taking ginger tea 30 minutes to an hour before traveling.

This can help reduce the severity of symptoms and make your journey more comfortable.

2. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains a unique combination of flavonoids and terpenoids that may help alleviate motion sickness.

The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant properties that can help reduce inflammation in the inner ear, where balance and equilibrium are regulated. This can contribute to the reduction of symptoms associated with motion sickness, such as dizziness and nausea. The terpenoids, including ginkgolides and bilobalide, may also play a role in reducing inflammation and improving blood flow to the inner ear, further alleviating symptoms.

If you're prone to motion sickness, consider drinking Ginkgo biloba tea about an hour before traveling, and continue to take small amounts throughout your journey. Start with a low dose, such as 1/2 cup, and adjust as needed. Some people may experience relief from symptoms within 15-30 minutes, while others may notice improvements over a longer period.

However, it's essential to note that individual results may vary, and more research is needed to confirm the effectiveness of Ginkgo biloba teas for motion sickness.

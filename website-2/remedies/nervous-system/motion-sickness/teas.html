7 Herbal Teas to Prevent Motion Sickness in Pilots

Pilots are at high risk of getting motion sickness.

This is because they have to fly planes, which can be really tough on their inner ears and balance system. When a plane moves, it can send mixed signals to the brain, making it hard to keep your balance and causing nausea, headaches, and even vomiting. For some pilots, this can be a constant problem that affects their work and daily life. They might feel tired, irritable, and even anxious due to the constant struggle with motion sickness.

It's not just about feeling unwell, either - it can also impact their performance and safety record. Pilots who experience motion sickness might have to take time off work or find themselves constantly on the lookout for new remedies to help manage their symptoms. As a result, pilots are always on the lookout for ways to prevent or treat motion sickness. One option they're exploring is herbal teas, which are natural and non-invasive ways to help calm the stomach and reduce nausea. These teas have been around for centuries and are known for their soothing properties, which can help to ease the symptoms of motion sickness.

They're often caffeine-free and come in a variety of flavors, making them a convenient option for pilots who want to try something new to help manage their symptoms.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, have been used for centuries to alleviate motion sickness.

The key to its effectiveness lies in its active compounds, including gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These constituents help to calm the stomach and reduce nausea by inhibiting the release of serotonin, a chemical that can trigger queasiness. When consumed before or during travel, ginger tea can help to settle the stomach and prevent the symptoms of motion sickness, such as dizziness, vomiting, and fatigue.

To use ginger tea for motion sickness, steep fresh ginger in hot water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink. You can also add honey or lemon to taste. For best results, drink the tea 30 minutes to an hour before traveling, and repeat as needed.

The likely outcome of taking ginger tea for motion sickness is a reduction in nausea and vomiting, allowing you to travel comfortably without worrying about the symptoms.

2. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas have been used to help alleviate motion sickness due to their unique properties.

The leaves of the Ginkgo biloba tree contain flavonoids and terpenoids, which are believed to improve blood flow to the brain and reduce inflammation. This may help to alleviate the nausea and dizziness associated with motion sickness. The active constituents, including bilobalide and ginkgolides, may also help to stabilize mast cells, which can contribute to nausea and vomiting. If you're prone to motion sickness, consider trying Ginkgo biloba tea before a long car ride or boat trip.

You can steep one teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves in a cup of boiling water for five to ten minutes, then strain and drink. Some people find it helpful to drink the tea about an hour before traveling, while others prefer to drink it as needed. You may also combine Ginkgo biloba tea with other natural remedies, such as ginger or peppermint tea, for added relief. When taking Ginkgo biloba tea for motion sickness, you can expect to experience some relief from symptoms such as nausea and dizziness. However, the effectiveness of the tea may vary from person to person, and some people may need to try it multiple times to see results.

It's also worth noting that Ginkgo biloba tea may interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and antidepressants, so be sure to talk to your doctor before trying it.

3. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata teas have been traditionally used to help alleviate motion sickness.

This is due to the presence of flavonoids, alkaloids, and other compounds that have a sedative effect on the nervous system. The tea contains a unique combination of passionflower extract, which has been shown to reduce anxiety and calm the mind, and other compounds that help to reduce inflammation and relax muscles. When experiencing motion sickness, taking a cup of Passiflora incarnata tea about 30 minutes before traveling can help to calm the stomach and reduce dizziness.

To get the most out of this remedy, try to sit back, relax, and take slow, deep breaths while sipping the tea. If you're on a boat, consider sitting in the middle of the vessel, where the motion is typically less severe. Within 15-30 minutes, you may start to feel a sense of calm wash over you, and the nausea associated with motion sickness may subside.

Some people may experience a slight drowsiness, but this should wear off once you've reached your destination.

4. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas, also known as fennel tea, has been used for centuries to alleviate motion sickness.

The active compounds in fennel, such as anethole, fenchone, and borneol, contribute to its effectiveness. These constituents work by reducing nausea and vomiting through their anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. They also help to calm the digestive system and reduce stomach cramps. To use fennel tea for motion sickness, you can try the following: steep 1 teaspoon of dried fennel seeds in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Let it cool, then drink it 30 minutes to 1 hour before traveling. You can also add honey or lemon to taste. Some people find it helpful to drink the tea regularly during the journey, but it's essential to listen to your body and stop if you experience any discomfort. Taking fennel tea for motion sickness may help alleviate symptoms such as dizziness, nausea, and vomiting. You may feel a reduction in stomach cramps and a sense of calmness, making it easier to travel.

However, if you experience severe or persistent symptoms, you should seek medical attention.

5. Piper nigrum

Piper nigrum teas, also known as black pepper tea, has been used for centuries to help alleviate motion sickness.

The key reason for this is the presence of a compound called piperine, which is a potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent. Piperine helps to calm the digestive system, reduce nausea, and improve blood flow to the stomach, making it easier to digest food while traveling. When experiencing motion sickness, try making a strong black pepper tea by steeping 1 teaspoon of black pepper in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain and drink it slowly while on the move.

You can also add a squeeze of fresh lemon juice to enhance the absorption of piperine. Some people find it helpful to drink black pepper tea before embarking on a journey, while others prefer to drink it as soon as they start feeling queasy. As you take black pepper tea, you may notice a reduction in nausea and a decrease in the frequency of vomiting. Your stomach may feel calmer, and you may be able to eat more comfortably while traveling. However, it's essential to note that individual results may vary, and some people may not respond to black pepper tea as effectively as others.

If you're prone to severe motion sickness, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional for personalized advice.

6. Avena sativa

Avena sativa teas have been traditionally used to help with motion sickness.

This is because they contain a compound called avenacoside, which has anti-inflammatory properties that can help calm the digestive system and reduce nausea. Avena sativa teas also contain saponins, which can help relax the muscles and improve blood flow to the stomach. These properties make Avena sativa teas a natural remedy for motion sickness. If you're experiencing motion sickness, try drinking Avena sativa tea about 30 minutes before you travel.

You can also take small sips of the tea throughout your journey to help keep your stomach calm. It's recommended to start with a small dose and adjust as needed. You can find Avena sativa tea in tea bags or loose-leaf form at most health food stores or online. When you take Avena sativa tea for motion sickness, you may experience a reduction in nausea and vomiting. You may also feel more relaxed and calm, which can help reduce anxiety and discomfort associated with motion sickness.

However, it's essential to note that Avena sativa tea may not work for everyone, and you may need to try other remedies or consult with a healthcare professional for more severe cases of motion sickness.

7. Cinnamomum cassia

Cinnamomum cassia teas have been traditionally used to help alleviate motion sickness.

This is due to the presence of a compound called cinnamaldehyde, which has natural anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. These properties can help to calm the stomach and reduce nausea. When you take Cinnamomum cassia tea for motion sickness, you can expect to feel a reduction in nausea and vomiting. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent motion sickness from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent motion sickness.

Peppermint tea, in particular, is known to calm the stomach and reduce nausea. Ginger tea is also a good option, as ginger has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe the digestive system.

These teas can be a gentle way to ease motion sickness symptoms.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for motion sickness every day?

It's generally okay to drink herbal teas for motion sickness every day, but check the ingredients.

Some teas have ingredients that can interact with medications or worsen health issues if consumed regularly.

Peppermint, ginger, and lemon balm teas are often considered safe, but it's a good idea to read labels carefully and be aware of potential effects on your body.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in motion sickness?

Herbal teas like ginger and peppermint can help with motion sickness.

The results can start to show after 15-30 minutes of drinking the tea. Some people may feel better within 30 minutes, while others may need an hour or two to notice an improvement.

It may take a few tries to figure out what works best for you.

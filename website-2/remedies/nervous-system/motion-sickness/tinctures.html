You can also add a few drops of herbal tincture to a glass of water and drink it before or during your trip. Some herbal teas can be taken regularly to help prevent motion sickness, while others are best taken as needed. Herbal tinctures offer a natural and non-invasive way to manage motion sickness, and they can be a good alternative to prescription medications or over-the-counter remedies.

By choosing herbal teas that are specifically designed to alleviate motion sickness, you can take control of your travel experience and enjoy your time on the road, in the air, or on the water.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains gingerols and shogaols, active constituents that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce nausea and vomiting by inhibiting the action of chemicals in the stomach that trigger these symptoms. The anti-inflammatory properties of gingerols and shogaols also help to relax the stomach muscles, reducing the spasms that can cause nausea and vomiting. Gingerols and shogaols have a direct effect on the stomach, helping to calm the digestive system and prevent the build-up of stomach acid that can exacerbate motion sickness.

By reducing nausea and inflammation, Zingiber officinale tinctures can provide relief from motion sickness symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger root, 2 cups of 80% vodka or glycerin, and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Chop the ginger root into small pieces and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour the vodka or glycerin over the ginger root, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

2. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contains flavonoids and bilobalide, which are active constituents that help with motion sickness.

Flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have a stabilizing effect on blood vessels and improve blood flow to the inner ear. This helps to reduce the conflicting signals sent by the eyes and balance organs, which contribute to motion sickness. Bilobalide has a neuroprotective effect and helps to reduce inflammation in the inner ear, further alleviating symptoms of motion sickness.

By improving blood flow and reducing inflammation, Ginkgo biloba tinctures can help to prevent and alleviate symptoms of motion sickness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves and 2 cups of 80% vodka or high-proof spirits.
  2. Combine the Ginkgo biloba leaves and vodka in a clean glass jar, making sure the leaves are completely covered.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the tincture develop.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass container.
  5. Discard the solids and store the Ginkgo biloba tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Use 20-30 drops as needed for motion sickness.

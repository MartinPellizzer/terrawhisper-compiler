1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains the bioactive constituents bilobalide, ginkgolides, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce nausea and vomiting. The flavonoids in ginkgo biloba essential oils have a calming effect on the stomach and intestines, reducing muscle spasms and cramping. Bilobalide also has a sedative effect on the nervous system, which can help to alleviate dizziness and disorientation.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, ginkgo biloba essential oils can help to alleviate symptoms of motion sickness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves and 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Combine the Ginkgo biloba leaves and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Make sure the leaves are fully covered by the oil.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the extracted oil to a small glass bottle and label it. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains the bioactive constituents gingerol and shogaol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate nausea and vomiting associated with motion sickness. The oils also have a sedative effect, which can calm the stomach and reduce queasiness. Gingerol's ability to inhibit serotonin receptors can also help regulate digestive function and prevent nausea.

The properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils make it a potential natural remedy for motion sickness symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh ginger roots and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Wash the ginger roots and chop them into small pieces. Use 1 tablespoon of chopped ginger for every 1 cup of carrier oil.
  3. Combine the chopped ginger and carrier oil in a saucepan and heat it over low heat for 2-3 hours, or until the oil turns golden brown.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, squeezing the cheesecloth to get all the oil.
  5. Store the Zingiber officinale essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid, and use 5-10 drops as needed to alleviate motion sickness.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent motion sickness from forming?

Drinking herbal tea might help prevent motion sickness.

Peppermint tea, for example, has been shown to ease nausea. Ginger tea is also known to calm stomach problems. These teas may help your body adjust to changes in motion and reduce discomfort.

Some people find they make a difference when traveling by car, boat, or plane.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for motion sickness every day?

While herbal teas like ginger and peppermint can help with motion sickness, it's not recommended to consume them daily for this purpose.

These teas contain active ingredients that may interact with other medications or worsen conditions like high blood pressure or certain digestive issues.

Moderation is key when using herbal remedies.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in motion sickness?

Herbal teas like ginger and peppermint can help with motion sickness.

It's said that drinking a cup of these teas about 30 minutes before traveling might help. The effects might be felt within 15 to 30 minutes after consumption, but it's different for everyone.

Some people might feel better sooner, while others might take a bit longer to notice any relief.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for motion sickness?

If you're prone to motion sickness, try drinking herbal tea before you start traveling.

Peppermint tea is a good choice because it can help calm your stomach. Drinking it about 30 minutes to an hour before you leave can be helpful.

This can give the tea time to take effect and reduce your symptoms.

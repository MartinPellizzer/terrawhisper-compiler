Motion Sickness Remedies for Travelers: 9 Essential Oils to Try

Motion sickness is a common problem for travelers who experience nausea, dizziness, and vomiting while on a boat, plane, or car ride.

This can be really frustrating and affect their daily lives. They may have to sit in the back seat of a car or avoid traveling by plane or boat, which can limit their freedom and cause stress. Motion sickness can also be embarrassing and uncomfortable, making it hard for people to enjoy their travels. When motion sickness happens, the body gets mixed signals from the senses, like sight and balance. This causes the body to react with nausea and dizziness.

It's not just a minor issue; it can cause dehydration, headaches, and fatigue, making it hard to enjoy travel or even just get through the day. Some people try to avoid motion sickness by taking medication, but this can have side effects and may not be effective for everyone. Others try different techniques, like eating specific foods or avoiding screens, but these methods can be hit-or-miss. Herbal essential oils have been used to help treat motion sickness. These oils are made from plants and contain natural compounds that can help calm the body and reduce nausea.

They can be inhaled or applied to the skin, and some people find them to be a natural and effective way to manage motion sickness symptoms.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils, also known as ginger essential oil, is a natural remedy that can help alleviate motion sickness.

This is because the oil contains properties and constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and anti-nausea effects. Gingerols, in particular, have been shown to block the production of a chemical in the body that causes nausea and vomiting. When applied topically to the wrists or temples or inhaled through steam inhalation, ginger essential oil can help to reduce symptoms of motion sickness, such as dizziness, nausea, and stomach discomfort. To use ginger essential oil for motion sickness, try applying a few drops to your wrists or temples before traveling.

You can also add a few drops to a handkerchief or cloth and inhale the scent through steam inhalation. Some people also take ginger essential oil internally, but be sure to dilute it with a carrier oil and consult with a healthcare professional first. When you use ginger essential oil, you may start to feel a reduction in nausea and dizziness within 15-30 minutes. You may also find that you can travel for longer periods of time without feeling sick.

However, it's essential to note that ginger essential oil is not a replacement for medical treatment and should be used in conjunction with other remedies as needed.

2. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils have been used for centuries to alleviate motion sickness, and its benefits are backed by science.

The reason Ginkgo biloba works is due to its unique properties and constituents, such as flavonoids and terpenoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. When used topically, Ginkgo biloba essential oils can be applied to pressure points on the wrists, temples, or behind the ears to help alleviate symptoms. You can also inhale the oil by adding a few drops to a handkerchief or inhaling it directly from a diffuser.

If you take these steps, you can likely experience relief from motion sickness symptoms, and may be able to enjoy your trip without discomfort.

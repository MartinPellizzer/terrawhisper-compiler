11 Herbal Essential Oils For Nausea

Herbal essential oils, especially those derived from certain plants, can provide relief from nausea.

These oils often mimic the benefits of herbal teas, which have long been used to alleviate nausea. Herbal teas work by releasing the active compounds from the plants into the body, where they can help to calm the stomach and reduce nausea. For example, ginger (Zingiber officinale) oil has been traditionally used to ease nausea and vomiting.

Ginger contains a compound called gingerol, which has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to soothe the stomach and alleviate nausea. Peppermint oil (Mentha x piperita), another essential oil, also has a calming effect on the stomach and can help to reduce nausea. Ginkgo biloba oil, although not as widely used for nausea, has also been found to have beneficial effects. It contains compounds called flavonoids, which can help to relax the stomach muscles and reduce nausea.

Using these essential oils in a diffuser or applying them topically can provide relief from nausea, and may also bring other benefits such as improved digestion and reduced stress.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

The active constituents, including gingerol and shogaol, have been shown to have a direct effect on the stomach and digestive system, helping to reduce nausea and vomiting. These compounds work by blocking the action of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that can cause stomach contractions and increase nausea. The anti-inflammatory properties of gingerol and shogaol also help to reduce inflammation in the stomach and intestines, further reducing nausea and discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and blocking prostaglandins, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help alleviate nausea and provide relief from its symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale roots and 2 cups of water.
  2. Wash the roots under running water, then chop them into small pieces.
  3. In a saucepan, combine the chopped roots and 2 cups of water. Heat over low heat for 1 hour.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, pressing on the solids to get the liquid.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a distillation apparatus and distill to get the essential oil. Use a hydrosol collector to separate the oil from the hydrosol.

2. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains a high concentration of limonene, menthone, and menthol, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

These bioactive constituents have natural anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects, helping to relax the muscles in the digestive tract and reduce inflammation. Mentha x piperita essential oils also has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help alleviate nausea by reducing stress and anxiety. The oil's menthol content also has a direct effect on the stomach, helping to ease digestive discomfort and relax the stomach muscles.

By reducing inflammation, relaxing the digestive tract, and calming the nervous system, Mentha x piperita essential oils can provide relief from nausea.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and stems. Clean them with water.
  2. Place the leaves and stems in a glass jar. Add 2 cups of water and 1 cup of witch hazel.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for skin use.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains bioactive constituents like bilobalide and ginkgolides, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce nausea.

These compounds also have antioxidant effects, which can protect the body from damage caused by free radicals that can contribute to nausea. The terpenoids in Ginkgo biloba essential oils, such as flavonoids and sesquiterpenes, have anti-emetic properties that can help alleviate nausea. The oil's ability to improve blood flow and reduce inflammation in the gut can also help alleviate nausea symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healthy blood flow, Ginkgo biloba essential oils can help alleviate nausea and provide relief.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Ginkgo biloba leaves. Make sure they are clean and free of any contaminants.
  2. Fill a saucepan with 2 cups of water and bring it to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and add the Ginkgo biloba leaves to the saucepan. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a glass bottle and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks to allow the oil to separate and solidify.

4. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains a compound called anethole, which is known for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

Anethole helps to relax the muscles in the stomach and alleviate cramps, making it an effective remedy for nausea caused by digestive issues. Another key component, limonene, has been shown to have a calming effect on the nervous system, reducing feelings of queasiness and anxiety. The essential oils also contain fenchone, which has been found to have a sedative effect on the stomach, further reducing nausea and discomfort.

By combining these properties, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can provide relief from nausea by soothing the stomach and calming the nervous system.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Foeniculum vulgare seeds, 1 cup of carrier oil (such as jojoba oil), and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Heat 2 cups of water in a saucepan, then add the Foeniculum vulgare seeds and let them steep for 2-3 hours. Strain the seeds from the water.
  3. Combine the strained water with the carrier oil in the glass jar and stir well.
  4. Store the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the oil to infuse.
  5. After 2-4 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a separate container, discarding the solids. Your Foeniculum vulgare essential oil is now ready to use.

5. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains the bioactive constituents citral, geraniol, and limonene, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help to reduce nausea by blocking the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause stomach contractions. Citral, in particular, has been shown to have a calming effect on the digestive system, which can help to alleviate nausea and vomiting. The essential oil's aroma also has a refreshing and uplifting effect, which can help to distract from feelings of nausea and discomfort.

When inhaled, the oil's vapors can help to reduce nausea by stimulating the brain's vagus nerve, which regulates digestive function.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves, 2 cups of water, and a still or distillation kit.
  2. Wash the leaves thoroughly with water to remove dirt and impurities.
  3. Combine the leaves and water in the still or distillation kit, following the manufacturer's instructions.
  4. Heat the mixture until it reaches a temperature of 212°F (100°C) or the boiling point of water.
  5. Collect the distilled essential oil and filter it through a coffee filter or cheesecloth to remove impurities.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two active constituents that contribute to its therapeutic effects.

These compounds have a sedative and anti-inflammatory effect on the digestive system, which helps to alleviate nausea. The oils' calming properties also help to reduce stress and anxiety, common triggers for nausea. Additionally, linalool has a direct effect on the nervous system, slowing down the digestive process and reducing the frequency of nausea.

By balancing the body's natural processes and reducing inflammation in the digestive system, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to alleviate nausea and promote a sense of calm.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of water.
  2. Combine the flowers and water in a saucepan and heat it on low heat for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a cup and add 2 tablespoons of glycerin to help preserve the oil.
  5. Allow the mixture to sit at room temperature for 2-3 days to allow the oil to separate and solidify.

7. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis essential oils contains a compound called quinine, which is responsible for its anti-nausea properties.

The bark of the Cinchona tree also contains other compounds like quinidine and cinchonine, which have similar effects. Quinine has been shown to have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce the inflammation in the stomach that can cause nausea. The essential oil also has a sedative effect, which can help to calm the stomach and reduce the sensation of nausea.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Cinchona officinalis essential oils can provide relief from nausea.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Cinchona officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over low heat for 10 minutes.
  3. Add the Cinchona leaves to the water and simmer for another 20 minutes.
  4. Use a cheesecloth to strain the mixture into a clean glass container.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 2 hours to separate the oil from the water. Use a pipette to collect the oil at the top.

8. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone as its active constituents.

These compounds contribute to its sedative, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic properties. Valerenic acid helps relax the stomach muscles, reducing spasms and discomfort that can cause nausea. The antispasmodic properties also help calm the digestive system, easing nausea caused by digestive issues.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the stomach muscles, Valeriana officinalis essential oils can help alleviate nausea.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Valeriana officinalis roots and 4 cups of water.
  2. Chop the roots into small pieces and put them in a large pot.
  3. Add 4 cups of water to the pot and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 2 hours, then let it cool.
  5. Use a distillation kit or a steam distiller to extract the essential oil from the plant material.

9. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains a compound called linalool, which helps to calm the stomach and reduce nausea.

It also contains cinnamaldehyde, a compound that has anti-inflammatory properties and can help to soothe digestive issues. The oils also have a warming effect, which can help to stimulate digestion and relieve nausea. Additionally, Cinnamomum verum essential oils has a sedative effect, which can help to calm the mind and body, reducing the likelihood of nausea.

The combination of linalool, cinnamaldehyde, and other compounds in Cinnamomum verum essential oils makes it a popular natural remedy for nausea.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Cinnamomum verum (Ceylon cinnamon) bark, a clean glass jar with a lid, a cheesecloth or coffee filter, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Dry the Ceylon cinnamon bark in a single layer on a baking sheet at 150°F (65°C) for 2 hours to remove excess moisture.
  3. Grind the dried Ceylon cinnamon bark into fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  4. Combine 1 cup of the ground Ceylon cinnamon powder with 1 cup of carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well.
  5. Steep the mixture for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

10. Elettaria cardamomum

Elettaria cardamomum essential oils contains a compound called limonene, which has been shown to reduce nausea.

The essential oil also contains a compound called beta-pinene, which helps to relax the stomach muscles, reducing spasms that can cause nausea. The active constituents of Elettaria cardamomum essential oils, including alpha-terpineol and linalool, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help to soothe the digestive system and reduce inflammation that can contribute to nausea. The antispasmodic properties of Elettaria cardamomum essential oils can also help to calm the stomach and reduce the symptoms of nausea.

The essential oil's ability to reduce inflammation and relax the stomach muscles can help to alleviate nausea and provide relief from its symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Elettaria cardamomum pods and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Clean and dry the cardamom pods. Remove any stems or debris.
  3. In a small saucepan, combine 2 cups of water and the cleaned cardamom pods. Heat over low heat for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Mix the strained liquid with the carrier oil (1:10 ratio). Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

11. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains compounds like camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce nausea by calming the digestive system. Bornyl acetate, in particular, has been shown to relax the smooth muscles in the digestive tract, reducing spasms and cramps. The camphor content also helps to reduce inflammation and improve circulation, which can help alleviate nausea and vomiting.

When inhaled, the essential oil's vapors can also stimulate the brain's vagus nerve, which helps regulate digestion and alleviate nausea.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.
  3. Let it simmer for 2 hours, then remove from heat and let cool.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle and store in a cool, dry place.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent nausea from forming?

Drinking herbal tea can help ease nausea.

Ginger tea is a popular choice, as it has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can calm the stomach. Peppermint tea also works by soothing the digestive system and reducing nausea.

The calming effects of herbal tea can make it easier to manage nausea symptoms.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for nausea every day?

Herbal teas for nausea can be a helpful remedy, but consuming them daily might not be the best approach.

Some herbs, like peppermint and ginger, can be safe to drink daily in moderation.

However, others, like chamomile and licorice root, may cause issues if taken regularly, so it's essential to be mindful of the amount and frequency.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in nausea?

Herbal teas can start to help with nausea after 15 to 30 minutes.

This is because the active ingredients in the tea can quickly move into your system and start to calm your stomach.

Some people may feel relief sooner, while others might take a bit longer to notice the effects.

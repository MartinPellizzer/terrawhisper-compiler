Motion sickness nausea solutions: 11 herbal essential oils

Motion sickness can be a real problem for some people.

When you're on a car, boat, or plane ride, your body gets mixed signals from your senses, like what you see and feel. This can confuse your body and make you feel queasy and sick to your stomach. For some people, this can be a big issue that affects their daily lives. They might have to avoid traveling or take medication to feel better, but sometimes even that doesn't work. This can be frustrating and limit their freedom to do things they enjoy. When you're stuck in a car on a long road trip, the last thing you want to do is feel like you're going to be sick.

It's not just about feeling miserable, it can also be embarrassing and uncomfortable for those around you. This can be especially tough for people who have to travel a lot for work or school. For motion sickness sufferers, finding a way to manage their nausea can be a big relief. That's where herbal essential oils come in. These oils are made from plants and have natural properties that can help calm your stomach and reduce nausea. They can be inhaled, applied to the skin, or used in a diffuser to release their benefits.

When you inhale these oils, they can help send signals to your brain that calm your body and make you feel better.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils, also known as ginger essential oil, is a natural remedy for nausea.

The oil contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties and are known to ease digestive issues. When inhaled or applied topically, these compounds interact with the brain's nausea center, helping to calm nausea and vomiting. The oils also have a warming effect on the body, which can help to soothe an upset stomach and reduce nausea. If you're experiencing nausea and want to try ginger essential oil, here's what you can do: start by inhaling the oil directly from the bottle or by adding a few drops to a handkerchief or cloth.

You can also add a few drops to a warm bath or mix with a carrier oil for a topical application. Apply the oil to your wrists, temples, or abdomen to help ease nausea. When you inhale or apply the oil, you may start to feel a reduction in nausea within a few minutes. The oil's anti-inflammatory properties may also help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, which can help to ease nausea and vomiting. Some people may also experience a reduction in stomach cramps and bloating.

However, keep in mind that ginger essential oil may not work for everyone, and you should always consult with a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you're pregnant or breastfeeding.

9 Soothing Herbal Teas for Pregnant Women Dealing with Nausea

During pregnancy, many women experience nausea and vomiting, especially in the morning.

This condition, known as morning sickness, is usually triggered by hormonal changes in the body. As a result, pregnant women may feel queasy and sick to their stomach, leading to a loss of appetite and dehydration. This can make everyday tasks like eating and taking care of themselves a struggle. Morning sickness can also cause stress, anxiety, and fatigue, affecting a woman's overall well-being and her ability to take care of her growing baby.

In severe cases, it can even lead to complications like low birth weight or preterm labor. To manage morning sickness, some women turn to herbal teas. These teas are made from dried plants and flowers that have been used for centuries to soothe the stomach and calm the mind. They work by regulating digestion, reducing inflammation, and providing a gentle, calming effect.

When consumed as a warm, soothing drink, herbal teas can help alleviate nausea and vomiting, making it easier for pregnant women to manage their symptoms and stay hydrated.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, commonly known as ginger tea, can help with nausea due to its natural properties and constituents.

Ginger contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and digestive properties. These compounds help to soothe the stomach and reduce inflammation, making ginger tea an effective remedy for nausea. When you're feeling queasy, try making a warm ginger tea by steeping fresh ginger in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

You can also add honey or lemon to taste. To get the most out of ginger tea, drink it slowly and in small sips, and avoid eating heavy meals for a few hours after drinking it. As you take action, you may notice a decrease in nausea and stomach discomfort. Your stomach may start to feel more settled, and you may be able to eat and drink more comfortably.

If you continue to use ginger tea, you may experience fewer episodes of nausea and be able to manage your symptoms more effectively.

2. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas, also known as fennel tea, have been used for centuries to help alleviate nausea.

The tea's active compounds, such as anethole and fenchone, work by relaxing the muscles in the stomach and reducing inflammation, which in turn helps to calm the digestive system. Fennel tea also contains antioxidants like vitamin C and potassium, which help to soothe the stomach and reduce nausea. If you're experiencing nausea, you can try drinking a warm cup of fennel tea.

To make the tea, steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried fennel seeds in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink. You can also add honey or lemon to taste. As you drink the tea, you may start to feel a calming effect on your stomach, and your nausea may subside. In some cases, fennel tea may also help to reduce bloating and gas.

However, if your nausea persists or worsens, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate nausea.

If you're experiencing nausea, try drinking a cup of Glycyrrhiza glabra tea after meals or when you feel queasy. You can make the tea by steeping the roots of the licorice plant in hot water.

Start with a small amount and gradually increase the dose as needed. It's also essential to stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water when consuming the tea. Some people may experience an increase in blood pressure or electrolyte imbalance due to glycyrrhizin, so monitor your body's response and adjust accordingly. When taking Glycyrrhiza glabra tea for nausea, you may experience relief from stomach discomfort and queasiness within 30 minutes to an hour. You may also notice a decrease in inflammation and stomach spasms. However, if your nausea persists or worsens, consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

Additionally, if you have any underlying medical conditions or take medications, consult with a healthcare professional before using Glycyrrhiza glabra tea.

Ginkgo biloba teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate nausea.

The flavonoids, in particular, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help calm the stomach and prevent nausea. If you're experiencing nausea and considering trying Ginkgo biloba tea, start by drinking a small amount (about 1-2 cups) 2-3 times a day.

You can also try combining it with other herbal teas like peppermint or ginger to enhance its effects. When brewing the tea, use boiling water and let it steep for 5-10 minutes to release the active compounds. Be sure to consult with a healthcare professional before using Ginkgo biloba tea, especially if you're pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking any medications. Taking Ginkgo biloba tea for nausea may lead to a reduction in stomach discomfort and nausea, allowing you to eat and drink more comfortably. However, if you have a severe underlying condition or are experiencing persistent nausea, it's essential to seek medical attention to rule out any underlying health issues.

Piper nigrum teas, made from the dried berries of the black pepper plant, have been used for centuries to help alleviate nausea and digestive issues.

The tea's active compounds, including piperine, beta-caryophyllene, and limonene, have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that may help to calm the stomach and reduce nausea. Piperine, in particular, has been shown to have a positive effect on digestion by increasing the production of digestive enzymes and improving gut motility. To try Piper nigrum teas for nausea, start by brewing 1-2 teaspoons of dried black pepper berries in 1 cup of hot water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day, ideally between meals.

Cinchona officinalis teas have been used to help with nausea for centuries, and it's not just a myth.

The tea contains a compound called quinine, which is a natural anti-inflammatory and antiseptic. Quinine works by blocking the action of a chemical in the body that causes vomiting, called serotonin. This helps to reduce nausea and vomiting. To try Cinchona officinalis teas for nausea, start by brewing a cup of the tea according to the package instructions.

Some people find it helpful to drink the tea as soon as they feel nauseous, while others prefer to drink it before a situation that might trigger nausea, such as traveling or eating a large meal. You can also try mixing Cinchona officinalis tea with other herbal teas, like ginger, which is also known for its anti-inflammatory properties. When you take Cinchona officinalis tea for nausea, you may experience a reduction in vomiting and nausea. Some people also report a decrease in stomach cramps and diarrhea.

However, it's also possible that you may experience some side effects, such as headaches or dizziness, especially if you're new to the tea or drinking it in large quantities.

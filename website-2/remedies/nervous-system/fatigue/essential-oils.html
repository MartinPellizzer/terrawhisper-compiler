11 Herbal Essential Oils For Fatigue

Herbal essential oils can be a great way to tackle fatigue.

Another herb that's great for fatigue is Lavandula angustifolia, or lavender. This calming herb can promote better sleep, reduce stress, and even ease muscle tension.

Ginkgo biloba is another herb that's often used to combat fatigue. This ancient plant is known for its ability to improve blood flow and oxygenation to the brain, making it a great choice for people who feel mentally foggy or sluggish. Ginkgo biloba tea can help increase energy levels, improve focus, and even boost memory. By incorporating these herbs into your daily routine, you can start to feel more refreshed, revitalized, and ready to take on the day. And, as you drink your herbal teas, you might notice that you're feeling more patient, more focused, and more in control.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have sedative and relaxant properties that help calm the mind and body, reducing feelings of fatigue. The sedative properties of valerenic acid and valeranone work to slow down the nervous system, promoting a sense of relaxation and calmness. Valeriana officinalis essential oils also contains isovaleric acid, which has been shown to have a mild sedative effect and help regulate sleep patterns, further contributing to its fatigue-reducing properties.

By promoting relaxation and regulating sleep, Valeriana officinalis essential oils can help alleviate fatigue and promote a sense of well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Valeriana officinalis roots and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil)
  2. Chop the roots into small pieces and place them in a clean glass jar
  3. Pour 1 cup of carrier oil over the chopped roots and close the jar
  4. Leave the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day to help the roots infuse into the oil
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bottle, discard the roots, and store the essential oil in a cool, dark place

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their calming and relaxing properties.

These compounds help reduce fatigue by promoting a restful night's sleep and calming the mind. Lavender oil also contains camphor, which has a sedative effect and helps decrease stress and anxiety. The sedative properties of camphor and the relaxing properties of linalool and linalyl acetate work together to alleviate fatigue and promote a sense of calm.

By reducing stress and promoting relaxation, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help the body and mind recover from the physical and mental exhaustion that comes with fatigue.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat until it reaches 180°F (82°C).
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and add the Lavandula angustifolia flowers. Let it steep for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, pressing on the flowers to get all the liquid. Discard the flowers.
  5. Let the liquid sit in a dark place for 2-3 days, allowing the oil to separate and rise to the top. Skim off the oil with a spoon and store it in a dark glass bottle.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains active constituents like flavonoids and bilobalide, which help increase blood flow to the brain.

This improved circulation can enhance oxygen delivery and reduce fatigue caused by poor oxygenation. The terpene ginkgolide, a key component of Ginkgo biloba, also helps protect the brain from damage and promotes neural activity. As a result, Ginkgo biloba essential oils can help alleviate mental fatigue and improve cognitive function by supporting brain health.

By stimulating blood flow and protecting brain cells, Ginkgo biloba essential oils can help counteract fatigue and promote mental clarity.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Add 1/4 cup of vodka or ethanol to the Ginkgo biloba leaves and let it sit for 2-3 weeks in a dark place.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Combine the strained liquid with the carrier oil in a 1:1 ratio. For example, if you have 1/2 cup of liquid, add 1/2 cup of carrier oil.
  5. Mix well and store the Ginkgo biloba essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 5-7 drops per application for fatigue.

4. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and geraniol as its key bioactive constituents.

These compounds contribute to its sedative and relaxing properties, which can help alleviate fatigue. Linalool, in particular, has a calming effect on the nervous system, promoting a sense of balance and tranquility. Melissa officinalis essential oils also contains antioxidants that help neutralize free radicals, reducing oxidative stress and promoting a healthy energy balance.

By promoting relaxation, reducing stress, and supporting energy balance, Melissa officinalis essential oils can help alleviate fatigue and promote overall well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers and 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Place the flowers in a clean glass jar and pour the carrier oil over them.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops per use to help with fatigue.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains a high concentration of camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate, which are known to have a stimulating effect on the mind and body.

These bioactive constituents work together to increase alertness and reduce fatigue by improving blood flow and oxygenation to the brain. The invigorating properties of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils also help to reduce mental fogginess and promote a sense of clarity. Additionally, the oil's ability to improve circulation and reduce inflammation can help alleviate physical fatigue by reducing muscle tension and promoting relaxation.

By harnessing the power of these bioactive compounds, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help to combat fatigue and promote a sense of vitality.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves, 1 cup of water, a saucepan, a bowl, cheesecloth, and a bottle.
  2. Chop the leaves into small pieces and add them to the saucepan with 1 cup of water.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30-40 minutes, then let it cool.
  4. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth into a bowl, squeezing the cheesecloth to get as much liquid as possible.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

6. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains active compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help reduce muscle and joint pain, making it easier to move and relax. The warming and stimulating effect of gingerol also increases blood flow and circulation, which can help alleviate fatigue by delivering oxygen and nutrients to the body's cells. Additionally, shogaol has been shown to have a direct effect on the body's energy production, increasing the synthesis of ATP, the molecule that provides energy to the cells.

By addressing the physical and biochemical causes of fatigue, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help alleviate feelings of tiredness and exhaustion.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh ginger root (Zingiber officinale) and 1 cup of carrier oil (such as coconut or olive oil).
  2. Peel the ginger root and chop it into small pieces. Use a food processor or blender to finely chop the ginger.
  3. In a clean glass jar, combine the chopped ginger and carrier oil. The ratio is 2 parts ginger to 1 part carrier oil.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. This allows the ginger's properties to infuse into the carrier oil.
  5. After the infusion period, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

7. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng essential oils contains active constituents such as ginsenosides, particularly Rg1 and Rb1.

These compounds have adaptogenic properties that help the body adapt to physical and mental stress, which can contribute to fatigue. The adaptogenic properties of ginsenosides help to stabilize blood sugar levels, improve circulation, and increase oxygen delivery to cells. Panax ginseng essential oils also contains other bioactive constituents like panaxynol and panaxydol, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation and promote relaxation.

By addressing the root causes of fatigue, Panax ginseng essential oils can help alleviate fatigue and promote a sense of well-being.

  1. Gather 200g of dried Panax ginseng roots, a cup of carrier oil (such as jojoba oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Slice the ginseng roots into small pieces and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour the carrier oil over the ginseng pieces, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every few days. Then, strain the oil through a cheesecloth into another container.
  5. Discard the solids and transfer the Panax ginseng essential oil to a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

8. Avena sativa

Avena sativa essential oils contains a wealth of bioactive constituents, including avenacosides, avenanthramides, and ferulic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce fatigue by soothing the nervous system and promoting relaxation. The antioxidant properties of ferulic acid also help to combat oxidative stress, which can contribute to feelings of exhaustion. Additionally, avenanthramides have been shown to improve blood flow and reduce inflammation in the body, which can help to alleviate fatigue.

By combining these properties, Avena sativa essential oils can help to support the body's natural ability to manage fatigue and promote a sense of well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Avena sativa seeds and 4 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Combine the Avena sativa seeds and carrier oil in a glass jar. Let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass container. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a clean glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Let it mature for 1-2 weeks before using.
  5. To use, mix a few drops of the essential oil with a carrier oil or lotion, and apply it to the skin. Start with a small amount and adjust as needed.

9. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata essential oils contains flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its fatigue-relieving properties.

Flavonoids like vitexin and isovitexin have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, helping to reduce stress and promote relaxation. Alkaloids, particularly harmine and harmaline, can increase alertness and reduce fatigue by influencing neurotransmitters in the brain. Terpenoids, such as beta-sitosterol, have a calming effect and may help regulate the body's internal clock, improving sleep quality and reducing fatigue.

By balancing these active constituents, Passiflora incarnata essential oils can help alleviate fatigue and promote a sense of well-being.

  1. Gather materials: 1 cup of fresh Passiflora incarnata flowers, a clean glass jar, a strainer, a carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil), and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Combine 1/4 cup of fresh Passiflora incarnata flowers with 1 cup of carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Leave the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This is called 'infusion'.
  4. Strain the mixture through the strainer into the dark glass bottle. Discard the solids and reserve the liquid.
  5. Store the Passiflora incarnata essential oil in the dark glass bottle, out of direct sunlight. Use a few drops as needed to relieve fatigue.

10. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains a high concentration of cineole, a compound known for its refreshing and invigorating properties.

Cineole helps to stimulate the mind and body, reducing feelings of fatigue and mental fogginess. The essential oil also contains pinene, which increases oxygen flow to the brain, promoting mental clarity and focus. Additionally, the decongestant properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oil, thanks to its alpha-pinene and limonene content, help to clear nasal passages and sinuses, allowing for easier breathing and reducing fatigue caused by respiratory issues.

By promoting a sense of refreshment and clarity, eucalyptus globulus essential oil can help alleviate fatigue and leave you feeling more energized and focused.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a distillation apparatus, and a cup of water.
  2. Add the Eucalyptus globulus leaves to the distillation apparatus.
  3. Heat the cup of water in the distillation apparatus until steam is produced.
  4. Collect the steam and pass it through a condenser to separate the oil from the water.
  5. Store the collected Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

11. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains a high concentration of linalool, a naturally occurring compound known for its calming and relaxing properties.

This oil also contains eugenol, which has been shown to have a positive effect on mental fatigue by reducing stress and anxiety. The sedative properties of eugenol and linalool help to promote a sense of calmness and relaxation, making it easier to manage fatigue. Furthermore, the oil's aroma has been found to stimulate the brain's production of neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and dopamine, which play a crucial role in regulating mood and energy levels.

As a result, Cinnamomum verum essential oils can be used to alleviate mental fatigue by providing a natural and non-invasive way to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Cinnamomum verum bark and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Use a coffee grinder or spice grinder to crush the Cinnamomum verum bark into fine powder.
  3. Mix 1 tablespoon of the powdered bark with 1 cup of carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a warm place (around 70°F to 80°F) for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a new container. Discard the solids and use the essential oil for fatigue relief.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent fatigue from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent fatigue from forming.

Some teas, like peppermint and ginger, can boost energy and alertness. Others, like chamomile and lavender, can help calm the mind and body.

These teas can also help reduce stress and promote better sleep, which can contribute to feeling more refreshed and focused throughout the day.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for fatigue every day?

Herbal teas can be a helpful way to manage fatigue, but it's essential to consume them in moderation.

Drinking them every day can lead to an imbalance of certain nutrients and interact with medications.

It's best to limit your intake to a few cups a week and listen to your body's response.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in fatigue?

Herbal teas can help with fatigue, but it's hard to say exactly when you'll notice a difference.

Some people feel more energetic within a few days, while others might not see results until a week or two. It depends on the tea and your body.

Just be patient and give it some time.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for fatigue?

For fatigue, it's a good idea to drink herbal tea in the afternoon.

This can help you feel more alert and focused before a long evening or night.

Many herbal teas, such as peppermint and ginseng, are most effective when consumed during this time, allowing you to recharge and get through the rest of your day.

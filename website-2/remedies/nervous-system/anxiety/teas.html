What herbal teas you should avoid if you have anxiety?

If you have anxiety, there are some herbal teas you should avoid.

One of these teas is 'Ephedra sinica', also known as Ma Huang. This herb can increase heart rate and blood pressure, which may worsen anxiety symptoms in people who already have high blood pressure or heart problems. Another tea to avoid is 'Yohimbe bark'. It can also increase heart rate and blood pressure, making it unsuitable for people with anxiety. 'Ginseng' tea, particularly the Asian ginseng (Panax ginseng) and American ginseng (Panax quinquefolius), can be a concern for people with anxiety. Some research suggests that ginseng may increase anxiety levels in certain individuals, possibly due to its stimulating properties.

'Caffeine-rich teas' like those made from yerba mate (Ilex paraguariensis) or guayusa (Ilex guayusa) are also not recommended for people with anxiety. These teas contain high amounts of caffeine, which can exacerbate anxiety symptoms. 'Kola nut' tea is another herbal tea to avoid. It contains caffeine and other stimulating compounds that can worsen anxiety in susceptible individuals. Lastly, 'Yarrow' tea may not be suitable for people with anxiety. Some people may experience increased anxiety and restlessness after consuming yarrow, possibly due to its stimulating properties.

These herbal teas should be used with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional, especially if you have anxiety or other health conditions.

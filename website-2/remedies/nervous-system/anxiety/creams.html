1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two key active constituents that contribute to its anxiety-reducing properties.

These compounds have a calming effect on the nervous system, promoting relaxation and reducing stress. The sedative properties of linalool and linalyl acetate help to slow down heart rate and lower blood pressure, creating a sense of calm and tranquility. The anti-inflammatory properties of these compounds also contribute to their anxiety-reducing effects, reducing inflammation in the body that can contribute to feelings of anxiety.

By using Lavandula angustifolia creams, individuals can experience a decrease in anxiety symptoms and an improvement in their overall well-being.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil, and 10 drops of Lavandula angustifolia essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl for 30 seconds.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and stir in the vitamin E oil and Lavandula angustifolia essential oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a glass jar and let it cool and solidify. Store the cream in the fridge for up to 2 weeks and use as needed for anxiety relief.

2. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis creams contains valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, which are the main bioactive constituents that help with anxiety.

These compounds have a sedative effect on the nervous system, which can calm the mind and body, reducing feelings of anxiety and stress. Valeriana officinalis creams also contain valepotriates, which have a direct effect on the brain's neurotransmitters, specifically GABA, a chemical that helps to regulate calmness and relaxation. By increasing GABA levels, valerenic acid and isovaleric acid help to reduce anxiety symptoms and promote a sense of calmness.

As a result, Valeriana officinalis creams can help to alleviate anxiety symptoms and promote a better night's sleep.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Valeriana officinalis root, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax in a heat-proof bowl.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add the dried Valeriana officinalis root to the melted mixture and heat for 2-3 minutes, or until fragrant.
  4. Remove the bowl from the heat and let it cool slightly before straining the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean container.
  5. Store the cream in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks and apply a small amount to the skin as needed to help calm anxiety.

3. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata creams contains flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides as its active constituents.

These compounds have a sedative and anxiolytic effect, helping to calm the mind and reduce anxiety levels. Flavonoids like vitexin and isovitexin have been shown to have a relaxing effect on the nervous system, while alkaloids like harmine and harmaline help to regulate the body's response to stress. The glycosides in Passiflora incarnata creams also contribute to its anxiolytic properties by reducing inflammation and promoting a sense of calm.

By combining these active compounds, Passiflora incarnata creams can help alleviate anxiety symptoms by promoting relaxation and reducing stress.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Passiflora incarnata flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the dried Passiflora incarnata flowers to the melted oil and beeswax mixture. Let it steep for 2-3 hours or overnight.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Mix in the vitamin E oil and pour the cream into a clean, sterilized container. Let it cool and solidify before use.

4. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis creams contains the biologically active constituents rosmarinic acid and linalyl acetate.

These compounds have a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help reduce anxiety and stress. Rosmarinic acid has antioxidant properties, which can protect the brain from damage and promote relaxation. Linalyl acetate has a sedative effect, which can help slow down an overactive mind and promote a sense of calm.

By applying Melissa officinalis creams to the skin, these compounds can be absorbed into the body, providing quick relief from anxiety symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Melissa officinalis flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of beeswax.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add the dried Melissa officinalis flowers to the melted mixture and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, discarding the solids and squeezing out as much liquid as possible.
  5. Mix the strained liquid with the shea butter and pour the mixture into a container. Let it cool and solidify before using it as a cream for anxiety relief.

5. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum creams contains flavonoids like hyperoside and rutin, and phenolic acids like kaempferol and quercetin.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce stress and anxiety. The naphthodianthrones, specifically hyperforin and adhyperforin, are known to have a calming effect on the nervous system. Hypericum perforatum creams also contain volatile oils that have a soothing effect on the mind and body.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Hypericum perforatum creams may help alleviate symptoms of anxiety.

  2. Melt the coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Once melted, remove the bowl from the heat and stir in the St. John's Wort oil and lavender essential oil.
  4. Pour the mixture into a heat-proof container and let it cool and solidify at room temperature.
  5. Once solid, use a spoon to scoop out the Hypericum perforatum cream as needed for anxiety relief.

6. Avena sativa

Avena sativa creams contains the bioactive constituents avenanthramides, gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), and ferulic acid.

These compounds have a calming effect on the body by reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation. Avenanthramides have antioxidant properties, which help to soothe the nervous system and reduce anxiety. GABA, a neurotransmitter, helps to regulate brain activity and calm the mind.

The ferulic acid in Avena sativa creams also has a sedative effect, which can help to reduce anxiety and promote a sense of calm.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Avena sativa (oat straw), 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/2 cup of beeswax.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval.
  3. Add the dried Avena sativa to the melted mixture and stir well.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool until it reaches 120°F to 130°F.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool completely before using as an anxiety-relieving cream.

7. Zingiber officinale

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent anxiety from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help reduce anxiety.

Some teas, like chamomile and lavender, contain calming properties that can soothe the mind and body. These teas work by slowing down breathing and heart rate, promoting relaxation and reducing feelings of stress and anxiety.

Regular consumption may help prevent anxiety from forming.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for anxiety every day?

Herbal teas for anxiety can be a great way to calm your mind, but it's essential to be mindful of your daily consumption.

While some herbal teas like chamomile and lavender are considered safe, excessive daily drinking can lead to an imbalance of certain nutrients in your body.

It's best to drink them in moderation and listen to your body's response.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in anxiety?

Herbal teas can start to calm anxiety within 30 minutes to an hour after drinking.

This is because some herbs, like chamomile and lavender, can quickly enter the bloodstream and start working on the brain.

As you continue to drink herbal teas regularly, you may notice a gradual reduction in anxiety over time, often within a few days or a week.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for anxiety?

Drinking herbal tea for anxiety can be most helpful in the evening, just before bed.

This is because it can help calm your mind and body, making it easier to relax and fall asleep.

As the day winds down, a soothing cup of herbal tea can be a great way to unwind and prepare for a restful night's sleep.

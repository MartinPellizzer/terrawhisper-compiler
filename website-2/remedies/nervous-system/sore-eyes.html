What Are The Main Causes Of Sore Eyes?

Sore eyes can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is Dry Eyes, which happens when the eyes don't produce enough tears or the tears are of poor quality. This can be due to various reasons such as age, certain medications, or underlying medical conditions like diabetes. When the eyes don't get enough moisture, they can become irritated and sore. Another cause of sore eyes is Computer Vision, which is caused by prolonged use of digital devices like computers, smartphones, and tablets.

When we stare at screens for a long time, our eyes get strained due to the blue light emitted by the screens, and our brain gets tired from focusing. This can lead to headaches, blurred vision, and sore eyes. Contact Lens can also cause sore eyes, especially if the lenses are not fitted properly or if you don't follow the hygiene instructions. When the lenses rub against the eyes, they can cause irritation and discomfort.

Additionally, if the lenses are not cleaned or replaced regularly, bacteria can accumulate and cause infections, which can lead to sore eyes.

Below are the main causes of sore eyes.

  • Dry Eyes: Dry Eyes is a cause of sore eyes because when the eyes don't have enough moisture, the sensitive tissues inside the eyes can become irritated, inflamed, and painful, leading to soreness and discomfort.
  • Computer Vision: Computer Vision, which involves intense focus on screens for extended periods, can cause sore eyes due to prolonged exposure to blue light emitted by computer monitors and smartphones.
  • Contact Lens: Contact lenses can cause sore eyes because the lens can rub against the eyeball, causing irritation, and also because not enough oxygen can reach the eye, making it uncomfortable.
  • Measles: Measles can cause sore eyes because the virus affects the eyes and causes tiny blood vessels in the eyelids to become inflamed, leading to redness, swelling, and pain.
  • Allergies: When you have an allergy, your body releases chemical called histamine, which makes the tiny blood vessels in your eyes swell, leading to redness, itchiness, and sore eyes.
  • Conjunctivitis: Conjunctivitis is a cause of sore eyes because the inflammation and infection in the conjunctiva, a thin layer covering the white part of the eye, cause redness, itching, and discomfort.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Sore Eyes?

If you have sore eyes, it's a good idea to avoid some herbs that can irritate them further.

One of these herbs is Eyebright's close relative, 'Verbascum thapsus' (Mullein), which can cause eye irritation and itching. Another herb to steer clear of is 'Glycyrrhiza glabra' (Licorice root), as it can cause your eyes to become more sensitive and inflamed.

Similarly, 'Zingiber officinale' (Ginger) can also cause eye irritation and burning due to its spicy properties. 'Achillea millefolium' (Yarrow) and 'Taraxacum officinale' (Dandelion) can also exacerbate sore eyes, as they can cause eye inflammation and irritation.

Additionally, 'Silybum marianum' (Milk thistle) can cause eye problems, including sensitivity and vision changes, in some people.

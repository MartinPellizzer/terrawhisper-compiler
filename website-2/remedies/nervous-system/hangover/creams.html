7 Herbal Creams For Hangover

Herbal creams for hangovers are becoming increasingly popular, and for good reason.

When you're hungover, your body feels like it's been through a war – headaches, nausea, fatigue, and stomach issues are just a few symptoms you might be dealing with. Herbal teas, particularly those made with specific herbs, can help alleviate these symptoms. Herbs like Zingiber officinale (ginger) are known for their anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe headaches and nausea. Curcuma longa (turmeric), another herb, has potent anti-inflammatory compounds that can reduce swelling and ease pain. Ginkgo biloba, meanwhile, is often used to improve blood flow, which can help alleviate headaches and improve cognitive function. These herbs work by targeting the root causes of hangover symptoms. For example, ginger's anti-inflammatory properties can help reduce the swelling in your brain that's causing your headache.

Similarly, turmeric's anti-inflammatory compounds can help ease the pain and discomfort in your stomach. By using herbal teas or creams made with these herbs, you can begin to feel better more quickly and get back to your normal routine. One of the benefits of using herbal creams for hangovers is that they're often more gentle on your body than traditional pain relief medications. While over-the-counter pain relievers can be effective, they can also have harsh side effects like stomach upset and drowsiness. Herbal creams, on the other hand, are often made with natural ingredients that are designed to work with your body, not against it. In addition to being gentler on your body, herbal creams can also be more convenient than traditional pain relief methods. Instead of taking pills or drinking lots of water, you can simply apply a topical cream to your skin and let the healing properties work their magic.

This can be especially helpful if you're not feeling up to drinking fluids or taking medication.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains the active constituents gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce nausea and vomiting, two common symptoms of a hangover. Gingerols and shogaols also have antioxidant properties, which can help to protect the body from oxidative stress caused by excessive alcohol consumption. Additionally, the warming properties of ginger can help to increase blood flow and circulation, which can aid in the removal of toxins from the body.

By reducing inflammation and promoting circulation, Zingiber officinale creams can help to alleviate some of the symptoms associated with a hangover.

  1. Mix 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, and 1/2 cup of beeswax in a small saucepan.
  2. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring until the beeswax is melted.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and add 2 tablespoons of Zingiber officinale essential oil, 2 tablespoons of peppermint essential oil, and 2 tablespoons of lavender essential oil.
  4. Stir well and let the mixture cool until it reaches a temperature of around 100°F to 110°F.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool completely before use.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Curcuma longa creams may help alleviate hangover symptoms such as headache, nausea, and fatigue.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce hangover symptoms. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, work to alleviate oxidative stress and inflammation caused by excessive alcohol consumption. The terpenoids, particularly bilobalide and ginkgolide, aid in improving blood circulation and reducing edema associated with hangovers.

By addressing these underlying issues, Ginkgo biloba creams can provide relief from hangover symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Ginkgo biloba extract, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 drops of peppermint essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove the melted mixture from the heat and stir in the Ginkgo biloba extract, vitamin E oil, and peppermint essential oil until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into a small tin or jar and let it cool and solidify at room temperature.

The antiviral properties of cinchona may also help combat the effects of dehydration and inflammation caused by excessive alcohol consumption.

  3. Add glycerin and distilled water to the melted beeswax. Stir until well combined.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in Cinchona officinalis tincture and peppermint essential oil.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent hangover from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help ease hangover symptoms, but it's not a guaranteed prevention method.

Some herbs like peppermint and ginger can calm stomach issues and reduce nausea.

However, the effects of herbal tea on hangovers are largely anecdotal and not extensively scientifically proven.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for hangover every day?

Consuming herbal teas for hangovers daily isn't recommended.

While they may help alleviate symptoms temporarily, relying on them daily can lead to caffeine overload and interact with other medications. Herbal teas also have varying levels of quality and potential side effects.

It's best to use them occasionally and focus on a balanced lifestyle to avoid hangovers.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in hangover?

Herbal teas can help with hangover symptoms, but the time it takes to feel better varies.

Some people report relief within 30 minutes to an hour after drinking, while others may take a few hours.

The effectiveness also depends on the type of herbal tea and the severity of the hangover.

10 teas to alleviate hangover symptoms for partygoers

When people go out to party, they often drink too much alcohol.

This can lead to a hangover the next day. Hangover symptoms can be really uncomfortable - people might feel queasy, have headaches, and even feel depressed. These symptoms can make it hard for people to function normally, so they might miss work or school, or even just stay in bed all day. This can affect their relationships, their jobs, and their daily lives in general. Hangovers can also be a big waste of time and energy.

Some people try to cure hangovers with medication or greasy food, but these don't always work. Others try to just wait it out, but this can take a long time. However, there's a natural way to help alleviate hangover symptoms. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to help people feel better when they're not feeling well. They work by soothing the stomach, reducing inflammation, and even helping to replace lost fluids.

When people drink herbal teas, they can start to feel a little better, and this can help them get back to their normal lives sooner.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, is a natural remedy that can help alleviate hangover symptoms.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce nausea and vomiting. Ginger also contains a chemical called 6-gingerol, which has been shown to have a soothing effect on the stomach and intestines, making it easier to digest food. When you're feeling hungover, drinking a cup of ginger tea can help calm your stomach and reduce the risk of dehydration.

To use ginger tea for hangovers, steep a piece of fresh ginger in hot water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink. You can also add honey or lemon to make it more palatable. If you're feeling severely hungover, start with a small amount of tea and gradually increase the dose as needed. Drinking a cup of ginger tea within 30 minutes of waking up may help reduce the severity of hangover symptoms, and you may start feeling better within an hour or two.

However, if your symptoms are severe or persistent, it's best to seek medical attention for proper treatment.

2. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas have been gaining popularity as a potential hangover remedy.

The reason behind its effectiveness lies in its unique properties and constituents. The tea is rich in flavonoids, terpenoids, and bilobalide, which are known to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds help to reduce inflammation in the brain and liver, which are often affected during heavy drinking. They also improve blood flow to the brain, reducing the severity of symptoms like headaches and dizziness. To use Ginkgo biloba tea as a hangover remedy, steep 1 teaspoon of dried leaves in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Drink the tea as soon as you wake up and repeat the process every 2-3 hours. You can also add it to your morning routine, along with other herbal teas, to help alleviate symptoms. It's essential to drink plenty of water throughout the day to replenish lost fluids and electrolytes. When taken as directed, Ginkgo biloba tea may help alleviate hangover symptoms, including headaches, fatigue, and nausea. You may notice a reduction in the severity of these symptoms, allowing you to recover more quickly. However, it's crucial to remember that Ginkgo biloba tea is not a cure for hangovers and should not be relied upon as the sole means of recovery.

Additionally, if you're experiencing severe hangover symptoms, such as vomiting or extreme dehydration, seek medical attention immediately.

3. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate hangover symptoms.

This is due to the presence of quinine, a compound that helps to reduce inflammation and alleviate pain. Quinine also has a mild diuretic effect, which can help to flush out excess fluids and electrolytes in the body. Additionally, Cinchona officinalis teas contain other bioactive compounds like alkaloids and flavonoids, which may aid in reducing nausea and vomiting. To use Cinchona officinalis teas for hangover relief, make a strong brew by steeping one teaspoon of dried leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it hot or cold, depending on your preference. It's recommended to drink a few cups throughout the day to help alleviate symptoms. You can also add honey or lemon to make the tea more palatable. When you take action by drinking Cinchona officinalis teas, you may experience a reduction in nausea and vomiting, as well as a decrease in headache and fatigue. Your body may also start to feel more balanced and hydrated, as the diuretic effect of quinine helps to flush out excess fluids.

However, it's essential to note that these teas will not completely eliminate hangover symptoms, but they can help to make them more manageable.

4. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas, also known as peppermint tea, can be a helpful remedy for hangover relief.

This is because peppermint contains properties like menthol and menthone, which have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. These compounds can help alleviate nausea, headaches, and stomach discomfort often associated with hangovers. When taken as a tea, peppermint's essential oils can ease digestive issues, reduce stomach cramps, and even help control vomiting. To use peppermint tea for hangover, start by brewing a cup of peppermint tea using dried peppermint leaves or peppermint tea bags. If you're feeling queasy, try drinking it slowly and in small sips.

You can also add a bit of honey to make it more palatable. Some people find it helpful to drink peppermint tea as soon as they wake up, or after a light meal to ease digestion. When you take peppermint tea for hangover, you may notice that your symptoms start to subside within 30 minutes to an hour. Your stomach may feel less bloated, and your headache may become more manageable. You may also experience a decrease in nausea and an improvement in your overall sense of well-being. However, it's essential to remember that peppermint tea is not a substitute for proper hydration and rest.

Drinking plenty of water and getting enough sleep are still crucial for recovering from a hangover.

5. Artemisia absinthium

Artemisia absinthium teas, also known as wormwood tea, have been used for centuries to help alleviate hangover symptoms.

The tea's properties and constituents, such as artemisinin and thujone, have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which may help reduce hangover-related discomfort. When you're feeling miserable after a night out, try making a strong brew of wormwood tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes. You can drink it hot or cold, sweetened or unsweetened, as you prefer. As you sip the tea, the artemisinin may help alleviate nausea and headaches, while the thujone may have a calming effect on your stomach and nerves.

The antioxidants may also help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation caused by excessive drinking. To maximize the benefits, try to drink the tea within 2-3 hours after waking up, and repeat the brew every 2-3 hours as needed. When you take action and drink wormwood tea, you may notice a decrease in nausea and headaches, and a general feeling of well-being. Your stomach may feel less bloated and irritated, and you may have more energy to tackle the day ahead. However, it's essential to note that wormwood tea may not completely cure hangovers, but it can help alleviate some of the symptoms.

If you're sensitive to thujone, start with a small amount and gradually increase the dose to avoid any adverse effects.

6. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla teas, also known as German chamomile tea, have been used for centuries to help alleviate hangover symptoms.

This herbal tea contains properties and constituents like apigenin, luteolin, and chamazulene, which have anti-inflammatory and soothing effects. These compounds help to reduce inflammation and calm the digestive system, making it easier to recover from a night of excessive drinking. The apigenin in chamomile tea is particularly effective in reducing muscle spasms and cramps, which are common symptoms of a hangover. To use Matricaria chamomilla teas for hangover relief, steep one teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers in a cup of boiling water for five to seven minutes. Strain the tea and drink it slowly, ideally before or after waking up from a hangover. You can also add honey or lemon to taste.

Drinking a cup of chamomile tea in the morning can help to reduce nausea and vomiting, making it easier to keep fluids down. It may also help to alleviate headaches and improve sleep quality. By drinking Matricaria chamomilla teas, you can expect to feel a sense of calmness and reduced discomfort. The anti-inflammatory properties of the tea can help to reduce swelling and ease pain in the body. You may also experience reduced nausea and vomiting, making it easier to recover from a hangover. While chamomile tea may not completely eliminate hangover symptoms, it can help to make the recovery process more bearable.

It's essential to note that chamomile tea is not a substitute for medical treatment and should be used in conjunction with other hangover relief methods, such as staying hydrated and getting plenty of rest.

8. Piper methysticum

Piper methysticum teas, also known as kava tea, may help alleviate hangover symptoms due to its active compounds, including kavalactones (kavain, desmethoxyyangonin, dihydrokavain, and yangonin) and flavonoids (kaempferol and quercetin).

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce hangover symptoms such as nausea, headaches, and fatigue. To use kava tea for hangover relief, steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried kava roots in boiling water for 5-10 minutes, then strain and drink. Start with a small amount, as kava can cause drowsiness and interact with certain medications.

Some people also combine kava tea with other herbs like ginger and peppermint to enhance its effects. When you take action and drink kava tea, you may experience reduced nausea and vomiting, as well as relief from headaches and fatigue. Kava's anti-inflammatory properties may also help alleviate muscle and joint pain associated with hangovers. When you drink Glycyrrhiza glabra tea for hangover, you may experience a reduction in symptoms such as nausea, headaches, and fatigue. The tea's anti-inflammatory properties may also help reduce stomach discomfort and bloating.

Rosmarinus officinalis teas, also known as rosemary tea, has been used for centuries to help alleviate hangover symptoms.

You can also combine rosemary tea with other herbal remedies like peppermint or ginger to enhance its effects. When you take action and drink rosemary tea, you may experience a reduction in hangover symptoms within a few hours.

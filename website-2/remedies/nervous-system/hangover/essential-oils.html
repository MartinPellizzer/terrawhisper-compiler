1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains gingerol and shogaol, which are active constituents that help alleviate hangover symptoms.

Gingerol and shogaol have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce nausea and stomach discomfort. These compounds also have digestive properties that ease digestion and prevent stomach cramps. Additionally, gingerol and shogaol have antioxidant properties that help reduce oxidative stress and promote liver health.

By using Zingiber officinale essential oils, you can potentially alleviate hangover symptoms and recover faster.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale roots, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a glass jar.
  2. Clean and slice the Zingiber officinale roots into small pieces.
  3. Fill the glass jar with the sliced roots, leaving 2 inches of space at the top.
  4. Add 1 cup of carrier oil to the jar, making sure the roots are fully covered.
  5. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day, then strain and store the oil in a dark container.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains active constituents like linalool and linalyl acetate.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce the swelling and pain in the head and body associated with a hangover. The calming and soothing effects of linalool can also help alleviate stress and anxiety, common symptoms of a hangover. Additionally, linalyl acetate has been shown to have a sedative effect, which can help improve sleep quality and reduce fatigue.

By reducing inflammation, alleviating stress, and promoting better sleep, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils may help alleviate hangover symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Lavandula angustifolia flowers. Let it simmer for 1 hour.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba oil) to the liquid and mix well.
  5. Transfer the mixture to a dark glass bottle and let it sit at room temperature for 2-3 days to allow the essential oil to separate and mature.

3. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds contribute to its sedative and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate hangover symptoms. Valerenic acid, in particular, is known for its ability to reduce stress and anxiety, while isovaleric acid has a calming effect on the nervous system. The anti-inflammatory properties of valerian root oil can also help reduce headaches and body aches commonly associated with hangovers.

By reducing stress, calming the nervous system, and alleviating inflammation, Valeriana officinalis essential oils may offer relief from hangover symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Valeriana officinalis root, a carrier oil (like jojoba oil), and a glass jar.
  2. Clean and dry the Valeriana officinalis root, then chop it into small pieces.
  3. In a saucepan, combine 2 cups of water and the chopped root. Heat it on low for 2 hours, then let it cool.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into the glass jar. Discard the root. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil to the liquid.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2 days, then strain it again through cheesecloth into a new container. Use this liquid as your Valeriana officinalis essential oil.

4. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains compounds like menthone, menthol, and limonene.

These active constituents have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which can help alleviate headaches and body aches often associated with hangovers. Menthol, in particular, is known for its ability to ease nausea and vomiting, making it a useful remedy for hangover symptoms. The oil's decongestant properties also help to clear congestion and sinus pressure, making it easier to breathe and feel more refreshed.

By addressing these common hangover issues, Mentha x piperita essential oils can help alleviate hangover discomfort.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and 1 cup of water.
  2. Chop the leaves finely and place them in a large bowl.
  3. Pour the water over the leaves and let it sit for 2 hours to allow the mixture to infuse.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  5. Add a tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) to the liquid and shake the bottle well to mix.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate hangover symptoms. 6. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids and terpenoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help to reduce the severity of hangover symptoms. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba, particularly quercetin, help to protect the body from oxidative stress and inflammation caused by excessive alcohol consumption. Terpenoids, such as bilobalide, have a calming effect on the brain and nervous system, which can help to alleviate headaches and fatigue associated with hangovers.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, and promoting relaxation, Ginkgo biloba essential oils may help to alleviate hangover symptoms.

  5. Bottle the Ginkgo biloba essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops as needed to help alleviate hangover symptoms.

11. Artemisia absinthium

Artemisia absinthium essential oils contains thujone, borneol, and camphor, which are key bioactive constituents that contribute to its potential hangover-relieving properties.

Thujone, in particular, has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects, which can help to alleviate headaches and stomach discomfort often associated with hangovers.

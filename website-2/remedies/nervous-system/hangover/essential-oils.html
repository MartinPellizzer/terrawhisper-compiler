11 Herbal Essential Oils For Hangover

College students often enjoy partying and socializing, but this can sometimes lead to overindulging in alcohol.

When they drink too much, they may wake up the next morning feeling terrible - headaches, nausea, fatigue, and sensitivity to light or sound can make it hard to function. A hangover can be especially challenging for college students, as it can affect their academic performance, relationships, and daily routines. They might struggle to attend classes, complete assignments, or participate in extracurricular activities, which can lead to stress, anxiety, and feelings of guilt.

As a result, college students may miss out on valuable learning experiences, social connections, and personal growth opportunities. The good news is that there are some natural remedies that can help alleviate hangover symptoms. Herbal essential oils, for example, contain potent compounds that can be inhaled or applied topically to provide relief. Some of these oils have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce headaches and body aches, while others have digestive benefits that can ease nausea and stomach discomfort.

When inhaled, these oils can also help calm the mind and reduce stress levels, making it easier to cope with the physical and emotional aftermath of a hangover.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate hangover symptoms.

These compounds work by blocking the production of prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that cause pain and inflammation in the body. When applied topically or inhaled, Zingiber officinale essential oils may also help to reduce nausea and vomiting associated with hangovers. To use Zingiber officinale essential oils for hangover relief, dilute a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil and massage it onto your temples, wrists, or soles of your feet.

Alternatively, add a few drops to a warm bath or inhale it through steam inhalation. When you take these actions, you may experience reduced pain and inflammation, which can help to alleviate headaches and body aches associated with hangovers. The anti-inflammatory properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils may also help to reduce nausea and vomiting, allowing you to feel more comfortable and able to keep food down.

Additionally, the oil's ability to aid digestion may help to alleviate stomach discomfort and bloating that often accompany hangovers.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent hangover from forming?

Drinking herbal tea after a night out might help with hangover symptoms.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and ginger, have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe a sore stomach and calm the mind.

They may also help to rehydrate the body and alleviate headaches.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for hangover every day?

While herbal teas can help with hangover symptoms, consuming them daily for relief isn't recommended.

Some herbs can interact with medications or worsen underlying health issues. For example, peppermint tea can relax muscles but also slow down digestion.

Stomach problems or allergies might arise if you drink them regularly.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in hangover?

Herbal teas can help alleviate hangover symptoms.

Some people start feeling better within 30 minutes to an hour after drinking. The teas seem to work faster if consumed while still hungover.

The effects might last a few hours, giving you some relief from headaches and nausea.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for hangover?

Drinking herbal tea for a hangover can help ease symptoms.

It's best to have it in the morning when you wake up, as it can help replenish fluids and calm your stomach.

The caffeine in some teas can also help increase alertness and wakefulness.

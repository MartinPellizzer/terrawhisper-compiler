Nausea Prevention and Treatment with Medicinal Herbs

Nausea is a feeling of discomfort or queasiness in the stomach, often accompanied by a sensation of vomiting.

If left untreated, nausea can lead to dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, and other complications. It's essential to address nausea promptly, as prolonged episodes can result in weight loss, fatigue, and decreased quality of life. Herbs and herbal preparations have been used for centuries to alleviate nausea, and several options are particularly effective. Ginger (Zingiber officinale) is a well-known natural remedy for nausea. Its anti-inflammatory properties and ability to relax the digestive muscles make it an excellent choice for soothing stomach discomfort. Ginger can be consumed as an infusion, where fresh ginger is steeped in hot water, or as a decoction, where the root is simmered in water to release its active compounds. Ginger tincture, a concentrated liquid extract, can also be taken sublingually (under the tongue) for quick relief. Fennel (Foeniculum vulgare) is another herb that has been traditionally used to calm the digestive system and alleviate nausea. Its essential oils, such as anethole, have a calming effect on the stomach muscles, reducing spasms and discomfort.

Fennel can be consumed as an infusion, where dried fennel seeds are steeped in hot water, or as a tincture, which can be taken sublingually or added to food and drinks. Ginkgo biloba, while primarily known for its cognitive benefits, also has anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate nausea. However, its use as a nausea remedy is less well-established compared to ginger and fennel. Ginkgo biloba can be consumed as an infusion, where the leaves are steeped in hot water, or as a tincture. Herbal preparations offer a natural and non-invasive way to manage nausea. Decoctions, which involve simmering herbs in water, can be more effective than infusions, as they release more active compounds. Tinctures, which are concentrated liquid extracts, can be taken sublingually or added to food and drinks. Infusions, where herbs are steeped in hot water, are a gentle and easy way to consume herbs, but may not be as potent as decoctions or tinctures. By using herbs and herbal preparations, individuals can effectively manage nausea and alleviate its associated symptoms.

This is particularly important for pregnant women, individuals undergoing chemotherapy, and those experiencing motion sickness.

What Are The Main Causes Of Nausea?

Nausea is a feeling of queasiness or a need to vomit.

There are several causes of nausea, and some of the main ones are motion sickness, food poisoning, and morning sickness. Motion sickness occurs when the body receives conflicting signals from the senses, such as the eyes, inner ears, and sensory nerves. This can happen when you're on a boat, in a car, or on a plane, and your body is trying to figure out how to balance. When your body can't balance, you might feel queasy or nauseous.

Food poisoning is caused by eating food that's contaminated with bacteria, viruses, or other germs. When these germs enter your body, they can cause stomach problems, leading to nausea and vomiting. Morning sickness, on the other hand, is a common symptom of pregnancy. Hormonal changes during pregnancy can cause the stomach to become sensitive and upset, leading to nausea and vomiting, especially in the morning.

These hormonal changes can also cause the body to react to smells and tastes differently, making some foods and smells more unbearable than others.

Below are the main causes of nausea.

  • Motion sickness: Motion sickness is a cause of nausea because the body gets mixed signals from the senses, such as seeing the world move but feeling the body stay still, which can confuse the brain and make you feel queasy.
  • Food poisoning: Food poisoning is a cause of nausea because the bacteria or other germs in spoiled food release bad toxins that upset the stomach and make you feel queasy and sick.
  • Morning sickness: Morning sickness is a common cause of nausea during pregnancy because the body's hormones are changing, making the stomach feel queasy and unable to keep food down.
  • Chemotherapy: Chemotherapy causes nausea because the strong chemicals used to kill cancer cells also affect the part of the brain that controls vomiting, making the body feel sick and leading to nausea and vomiting.
  • Pregnancy: During pregnancy, the high levels of the hormone called human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG) can cause the stomach to feel queasy and lead to nausea.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Nausea?

If you have nausea, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can trigger or worsen stomach problems.

One herb to steer clear of is 'Hyssopus officinalis', also known as hyssop. Its essential oils can irritate the stomach and make nausea worse.

Another herb to be cautious of is 'Artemisia absinthium', or wormwood. This herb contains compounds that can irritate the stomach lining, causing discomfort and nausea. Additionally, 'Cinchona officinalis', which is used to make quinine, contains compounds that can slow down digestion and worsen nausea.

'Datura stramonium', or jimsonweed, is also not recommended, as its active compounds can cause stomach upset, nausea, and vomiting.

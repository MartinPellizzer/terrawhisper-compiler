What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have headaches?

If you have headaches, it's best to avoid essential oils from certain herbs that can make them worse.

One of these herbs is 'Hyssopus officinalis', also known as hyssop. Its oil can be too stimulating and increase blood pressure, which might worsen headaches like migraines. Another herb to avoid is 'Juniperus communis', also known as juniper.

Its oil has a diuretic effect, which means it makes you urinate more. This can lead to dehydration, a common cause of headaches. 'Sassafras albidum', also known as sassafras, is another herb to steer clear of. Its oil has been linked to seizures and spasms, which can be triggered or worsened by headaches.

Lastly, 'Santalum album', also known as sandalwood, and 'Cinnamomum verum', also known as true cinnamon, can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions, which might exacerbate headache symptoms.

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce pain and swelling in the body, making it a popular remedy for headaches. The calming effects of linalool and linalyl acetate also promote relaxation, which can help to alleviate stress and anxiety that may be contributing to headache symptoms. Additionally, the antispasmodic properties of linalool help to relax muscle tension, which can also contribute to headache relief.

By reducing pain, inflammation, and muscle tension, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can be an effective natural remedy for headaches.

  1. Harvest fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers. Use 1 cup of flowers.
  2. Dry the flowers in a warm, dark place for 1 week. This helps to preserve the oil.
  3. Prepare a still by combining 1 cup of water and 1 cup of ethanol in a saucepan. Heat the mixture gently.
  4. Add the dried flowers to the still and cover it. Steam will rise, releasing the oil. Collect the steam in a separate container.
  5. Cool the collected steam and separate the oil from the water. You should have around 1-2 tablespoons of Lavandula angustifolia essential oil.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole, a powerful bioactive constituent that helps to alleviate headaches.

Cineole has anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling and ease tension in the blood vessels that can cause headaches. The oil also has analgesic and antispasmodic properties, which relieve pain and relax muscles, helping to ease headache symptoms. Additionally, cineole has a decongestant effect, which can help to clear nasal passages and sinuses, often a contributor to headaches.

By reducing inflammation, relieving pain, and clearing sinuses, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help to provide relief from headaches.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a clean glass jar with a lid, a cheesecloth or a coffee filter, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Chop the Eucalyptus leaves into small pieces and put them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour 1 cup of carrier oil over the Eucalyptus leaves, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Steep the mixture for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day, then strain the oil through the cheesecloth or coffee filter into a separate container.
  5. Discard the solids and transfer the Eucalyptus essential oil to a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid, ready to use for headaches.

3. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains rosinic acid, camphor, and bornyl acetate as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which help to alleviate headache symptoms. The camphor in rosemary essential oil has a cooling effect on the skin and muscles, reducing tension and pain. Bornyl acetate, on the other hand, has a sedative effect, which can help to calm the nervous system and reduce headache frequency.

The analgesic properties of rosinic acid also contribute to pain relief by blocking the production of pain-causing chemicals in the body.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves, 1 cup of water, and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Wash the leaves with 1 cup of water to remove dirt and impurities.
  3. Combine the clean leaves with 1 cup of water in the glass jar and stir well.
  4. Place the jar in a saucepan, add water to a depth of 2 inches, and bring to a boil on low heat.
  5. Let the mixture simmer for 2-3 hours, then let it cool and strain the liquid to get the essential oil.

4. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and geraniol as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds contribute to the oil's calming and relaxing properties, which can be beneficial for people experiencing headaches. Linalool, in particular, has a sedative effect that can help reduce muscle tension and soothe the mind. The anti-inflammatory properties of linalyl acetate may also help alleviate headache symptoms by reducing pain and swelling in the affected areas.

The oil's ability to relax and calm the body and mind can help alleviate headache triggers and provide relief from pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers, a glass jar, and a carrier oil like jojoba oil.
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in the glass jar, using 1 part flowers to 2 parts oil.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the Melissa officinalis essential oil in a dark glass bottle, using 5-7 drops per application for headache relief.

5. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains a high concentration of menthol, menthone, and limonene, which are responsible for its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce pain and inflammation in the head and neck area, providing relief from headaches. The menthol in Mentha x piperita essential oils also has a cooling effect on the skin, which can help to relax tense muscles and improve blood circulation. Additionally, the limonene in the oil has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety that can trigger headaches.

By combining these properties, Mentha x piperita essential oils can provide effective relief from headaches.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water on low heat for 30 minutes. This will help release the oil from the leaves.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the leaves.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the liquid. Mix well.
  5. Store the Mentha x piperita essential oil in a dark glass bottle. Use 2-3 drops in a diffuser or apply topically (after diluting with carrier oil) to help relieve headaches.

6. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains compounds like anethole, limonene, and pinene.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties. Anethole, in particular, has been shown to relax the muscles in the neck and scalp, which can help to reduce tension and alleviate headache symptoms. Limonene and pinene have a calming effect on the nervous system, reducing anxiety and stress that can contribute to headaches.

By combining these properties, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils may provide relief from headaches caused by tension and stress.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds, a still or a steam distiller, and a glass container for the oil.
  2. Combine the seeds with 2 cups of water in the still or distiller. Heat the mixture gently.
  3. Collect the steam that rises from the mixture in the still or distiller. This steam will contain the essential oil.
  4. Cool the collected steam and let the essential oil separate from the water. Use a separating funnel or a glass container to collect the oil.
  5. Strain the collected oil through a coffee filter into a clean glass container. Store the Foeniculum vulgare essential oil in a cool, dark place for use in treating headaches.

7. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid and isovaleric acid as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have a sedative and calming effect on the nervous system, which helps to reduce tension and stress. Valerian root essential oil also contains valeryl valerianate, a compound that inhibits the production of stress hormones, such as cortisol and adrenaline. The anxiolytic properties of valerian essential oil help to calm the mind and body, making it easier to relax and reduce headache symptoms.

By promoting relaxation and reducing muscle tension, valerian essential oil can help alleviate headache pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Valeriana officinalis roots and 1 cup of carrier oil (such as jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Chop the Valeriana officinalis roots into small pieces and add them to a clean glass jar.
  3. Cover the roots with carrier oil and seal the jar. Let it steep for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Valeriana officinalis essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 5-7 drops per application for headache relief.

8. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains a compound called limonene, which is known for its pain-relieving properties.

The oil also contains a compound called geraniol, which has anti-inflammatory effects that can help reduce swelling and ease tension in the head and neck. The analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties of Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can help alleviate headache pain by reducing inflammation and relaxing tense muscles. The oil's decongestant properties can also help open up airways and relieve sinus pressure that may be contributing to headaches.

By using Cymbopogon citratus essential oils, you may be able to find some relief from headache pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves. Choose healthy leaves with no signs of damage or disease.
  2. Wash the leaves with 2 cups of water to remove dirt and bacteria. Gently scrub the leaves with your hands.
  3. Use a juicer to extract the essential oil from the leaves. Collect 1 cup of the extracted oil in a container.
  4. Filter the oil through a coffee filter to remove any impurities. Discard the filter with the impurities.
  5. Store the Cymbopogon citratus essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Label the bottle and keep it away from children and pets.

9. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains the bioactive constituents thujone, borneol, and camphor.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which help to reduce pain and swelling in the head and neck. The antispasmodic properties of thujone and camphor also help to relax tense muscles and calm the nervous system, reducing the frequency and severity of headaches. Borneol, on the other hand, has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety that can contribute to headaches.

By using Salvia officinalis essential oils, you may experience relief from headache symptoms, such as pain, inflammation, and stress.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves, a clean glass jar, and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Wash the leaves with cold water and gently pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Pack the leaves tightly into the glass jar, making sure they're completely covered by the carrier oil.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it gently every day. This will help the oil extract from the leaves.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container, then discard the solids. Your Salvia officinalis essential oil is now ready to use.

10. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are powerful bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, helping to reduce inflammation and free radicals in the body. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, are known to dilate blood vessels and improve blood flow to the brain, which can help alleviate headache symptoms. The terpenoids, such as bilobalide, have a sedative effect and can help calm the nervous system, reducing tension and stress that may contribute to headaches.

By reducing inflammation and improving blood flow, Ginkgo biloba essential oils may help to alleviate headache symptoms and promote relaxation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Wash the leaves in cold water, then dry them completely.
  3. Combine the dried leaves and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Let it sit for 2 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Ginkgo biloba essential oil in a cool, dark place and use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use to help relieve headaches.

11. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol, carvacrol, and pinene as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and analgesic properties that help alleviate headache pain. Thymol, in particular, is known to relax muscles and reduce inflammation in the blood vessels, which can contribute to headaches. Carvacrol has a sedative effect, helping to calm the nervous system and reduce tension that can lead to headaches.

The analgesic and antispasmodic properties of pinene help to block pain pathways and relax tense muscles, providing relief from headache pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves, 2 cups of water, and a glass jar.
  2. Wash the Thymus vulgaris leaves in cold water and drain.
  3. Place the Thymus vulgaris leaves in the glass jar and pour in 2 cups of water.
  4. Steep the mixture in a warm place for 2-3 hours or overnight, then strain the liquid.
  5. Use a distillation kit or a simple steamer to extract the essential oil from the liquid, then store it in a dark glass bottle.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent headaches from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent headaches.

Peppermint tea, for example, can ease tension and relax the muscles in the head and neck. Feverfew tea contains a compound that reduces inflammation and eases pain. Ginger tea is also known to calm digestive issues that can trigger headaches.

Some people find these teas helpful in preventing headaches from forming.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for headaches every day?

Herbal teas for headaches can be a great option, but it's essential to be mindful of daily consumption.

Some herbs, like feverfew and ginger, are generally safe for daily use.

However, others, like willow bark, contain salicylic acid, similar to aspirin, so it's best to limit your intake or choose alternatives to avoid overloading your body.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in headaches?

Herbal teas can start to help with headaches within a few hours.

Some people notice relief after 30 minutes to an hour, while others might feel better after 2-3 hours. It depends on the type of tea, its strength, and how it affects you personally.

The effects can be temporary or last longer, varying from person to person.

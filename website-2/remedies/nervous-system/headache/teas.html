8 Soothing Teas for Students Dealing with Migraines and Headaches

Students are often stressed out with heavy school loads, long study sessions, and tight deadlines.

This stress can cause headaches, which can be really painful and affect their daily lives. When a student gets a headache, it can make it hard for them to focus on their studies, and even simple tasks like watching TV or talking to friends can become a challenge. Headaches can also make students feel tired, irritable, and even anxious. Some students might even skip classes or important events because of a headache, which can lead to falling behind in school. This can create a cycle of worry and stress, making it harder for them to catch up. Headaches can be triggered by lack of sleep, poor diet, lack of exercise, or even certain foods.

Students who sit for long periods in front of a computer or phone screen might experience headaches due to eye strain. When they're under pressure to perform well in exams, students might experience headaches because of the stress and anxiety. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to help with headaches. These teas work by relaxing the muscles, reducing inflammation, and calming the mind. When a student drinks a soothing cup of herbal tea, they can feel a sense of calm and relaxation wash over them. The tea can help ease the pain and discomfort of a headache, allowing the student to focus on their studies again.

By incorporating herbal teas into their daily routine, students can find relief from headaches and stay on top of their schoolwork.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate headaches.

This is because the plant contains a number of properties and constituents that work together to calm the mind and body. Valerian root, the main active ingredient in these teas, is rich in valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone, which are known to have a sedative effect on the nervous system. These compounds interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, such as GABA, to slow down brain activity, reduce muscle tension, and promote relaxation. As a result, Valeriana officinalis teas can help to ease tension headaches, migraines, and other types of headaches caused by stress, anxiety, or fatigue. If you're considering trying Valeriana officinalis teas for headaches, here's what you can do: start with a small dose, about 1/2 teaspoon of dried root per cup of boiling water, and steep it for 5-7 minutes.

You can drink it hot or cold, sweetened with honey or lemon to taste. To get the most out of these teas, try to drink them at the first sign of a headache, or as a preventative measure when you're feeling stressed or anxious. It's also a good idea to establish a regular sleep schedule, exercise regularly, and maintain a healthy diet to reduce your overall risk of headaches. When you drink Valeriana officinalis teas for headaches, you can expect to feel a sense of calm and relaxation wash over you. Your muscles may become less tense, and your mind may become clearer. You may also find that your headaches decrease in frequency and severity over time.

However, if your headaches are severe, frequent, or persistent, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, has been used for centuries to help alleviate headaches.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, have anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties that can help ease headache symptoms. Ginger's bioactive compounds also have a natural ability to relax muscles, reduce nausea, and improve blood circulation, which can help to alleviate migraine and tension headaches. When taking Zingiber officinale teas for headaches, start by steeping fresh ginger in hot water for 5-7 minutes to release its active compounds. Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day as needed.

You can also add honey or lemon to taste, but avoid adding sugar or other sweeteners. To get the most out of ginger tea, try drinking it at the first sign of a headache or when you're feeling stressed or anxious. As you take ginger tea, you may start to feel a reduction in headache severity and frequency. You may also notice improved digestion, reduced nausea, and a general sense of relaxation and well-being.

However, if your headaches are severe or persistent, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare teas, also known as fennel tea, is a natural remedy that has been used for centuries to alleviate headaches.

The tea contains a compound called anethole, which has anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties. Anethole also has a sedative effect, which can help to calm the mind and body, reducing the severity of headaches. Fennel tea also contains flavonoids and vitamin C, which have antioxidant properties that can help to reduce inflammation and promote relaxation. To use fennel tea for headaches, steep one teaspoon of dried fennel seeds in a cup of boiling water for five to seven minutes. Let the tea cool down before drinking it.

You can also add honey or lemon to make it more palatable. It's recommended to drink the tea two to three times a day, as needed. Start with a small amount and gradually increase the dose as needed. When you take fennel tea for headaches, you may experience a decrease in the frequency and severity of headaches. The tea can also help to improve your mood and reduce anxiety. Some people may feel a sense of calm and relaxation after drinking the tea.

However, it's essential to note that fennel tea may not work for everyone, and it's best to consult with a healthcare professional before using it as a treatment for headaches.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas are known to help alleviate headaches due to their calming and soothing properties.

The tea contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two main constituents responsible for its pain-relieving effects. These compounds have a sedative effect on the nervous system, which can help reduce muscle tension and ease headache symptoms. When you drink Lavandula angustifolia tea, you can take a few steps to maximize its benefits. Start by steeping a teaspoon of dried flowers in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and drink it when it's still warm. To enhance the effects, find a quiet and comfortable place to relax, and try to reduce stress by focusing on deep breathing or meditation. As you take the tea, you may start to feel your muscles relax and your mind calm down. Your headache symptoms may begin to subside, and you may experience a sense of relief from the pain.

You may also notice an improvement in your mood and a decrease in anxiety levels.

5. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas, also known as peppermint tea, have been used for centuries to help alleviate headaches.

The main reason for this is the presence of menthol and menthone, two key compounds that have natural pain-relieving and anti-inflammatory properties. When you drink peppermint tea, these compounds are absorbed into your bloodstream and work to relax tense muscles, calm the mind, and improve blood flow to the brain. This can help to reduce the severity of headaches, including migraines and tension headaches.

To use peppermint tea for headaches, try making a strong brew and drinking it as soon as you feel a headache coming on. You can also add a few drops of peppermint essential oil to a warm bath or inhale it through steam inhalation to get similar benefits. As you drink the tea, you may start to feel a sense of calm and relaxation wash over you, and your headache may begin to subside.

The menthol in peppermint tea can also help to ease nausea and digestive issues that often accompany headaches, making it a great natural remedy to have in your toolkit.

6. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate headaches.

The plant contains compounds like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which have anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties. These compounds help to reduce swelling and relax tense muscles, providing relief from tension headaches and migraines. To try Achillea millefolium teas for headaches, start by drinking 1-2 cups a day, made from 1 teaspoon of dried flowers per cup of boiling water. Steep the tea for 5-7 minutes, then strain and enjoy. You can also combine it with other herbs like peppermint or ginger for added benefits.

Some people find it helpful to take a warm bath with Epsom salts or essential oils after drinking the tea to promote relaxation. When you take action and drink Achillea millefolium tea regularly, you may notice a decrease in headache frequency and severity. You may also feel more relaxed and calm, with improved sleep quality and reduced stress levels. However, it's essential to note that everyone's body is different, and results may vary. Some people may experience immediate relief, while others may take longer to notice benefits.

If your headaches persist or worsen, consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

7. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate headaches.

This is due to the presence of flavonoids, alkaloids, and other compounds that have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The flavonoids, in particular, help to reduce inflammation and relax blood vessels, which can contribute to headache symptoms. The alkaloids, such as passiflorine, have a sedative effect, which can help to calm the nervous system and reduce headache frequency. If you're experiencing headaches, try making a tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried Passiflora incarnata leaves in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. You can strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day as needed.

It's also essential to note that while Passiflora incarnata teas can be a helpful complementary therapy, they should not be used as a replacement for medical treatment. If your headaches are severe, frequent, or persistent, consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment. Taking Passiflora incarnata teas may lead to a reduction in headache frequency and severity. Some people may experience a calming effect, which can help to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation. However, it's also possible that the tea may interact with other medications or worsen certain health conditions. As with any herbal remedy, start with a small dose and monitor your body's response before increasing the amount or frequency of consumption.

If you experience any adverse effects, discontinue use and consult with a healthcare professional.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent headaches from forming?

Some herbal teas, like feverfew and ginger, may help prevent headaches.

These teas contain compounds that can ease pain and reduce inflammation in the body. Drinking them regularly might help your body get used to dealing with stress and other triggers that can cause headaches.

This could lead to fewer headaches over time.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for headaches every day?

While herbal teas can be a soothing relief for headaches, consuming them daily may not be the best idea.

Some teas, like feverfew and ginger, can cause interactions or allergic reactions if taken excessively.

It's also possible to build up a tolerance, making them less effective over time.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in headaches?

Herbal teas can help with headaches, but it depends on the type and severity.

Some people notice relief within 30 minutes to an hour after drinking, while others might feel better after a few hours.

It's also possible for teas to take longer to show results, sometimes up to a day or two.

10 Herbal Creams For Headaches

Herbal creams for headaches have become a popular alternative to traditional painkillers.

They work by applying a blend of herbs directly to the skin, where they can be absorbed and start working quickly. One reason herbal creams are effective is because they help to relax the muscles and calm the nervous system, which can get over-stimulated and lead to headaches. Herbal teas also offer relief from headaches, but in a different way. When you drink an herbal tea, the active compounds are absorbed into your bloodstream and can have a more systemic effect. For example, Valeriana officinalis, also known as valerian root, is a natural relaxant that can help to calm anxiety and reduce muscle tension, making it a popular choice for soothing headaches.

Other herbs like Zingiber officinale, or ginger, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce pain and swelling in the body. This can be especially helpful for migraines and tension headaches. Lavandula angustifolia, or lavender, is another calming herb that can help to relax the mind and body, reducing the frequency and severity of headaches. Using herbal creams or teas for headaches can bring many benefits to your life. For one, they can be a more natural and gentle way to manage pain, without the risk of side effects or addiction. They can also be a convenient and portable solution, perfect for on-the-go relief.

Plus, many herbal teas and creams can be made at home using fresh or dried herbs, making them a cost-effective option.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis creams contains valerenic acid and valepotriates, two active constituents that help with headaches.

These compounds have a sedative effect, reducing stress and anxiety that can contribute to headaches. Valerenic acid also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help ease tension in the muscles and blood vessels that may be causing pain. The sedative properties of valerenic acid and valepotriates work by affecting the brain's neurotransmitters, which can help regulate pain perception.

By reducing stress and anxiety, and easing muscle tension, Valeriana officinalis creams can provide relief from headache symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water and 1 cup of witch hazel in a saucepan.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of dried Valeriana officinalis root and 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves to the saucepan.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 10-15 minutes, then let it steep for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Mix 1/4 cup of beeswax with 1/4 cup of coconut oil and add it to the liquid mixture. Stir until the beeswax is fully dissolved, then pour the mixture into a container and let it cool before use.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains a compound called gingerol, a bioactive constituent that plays a key role in alleviating headaches.

Gingerols have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce swelling and pain in the blood vessels and nerves that cause headaches. Another compound, shogaol, also present in ginger, has a calming effect on the smooth muscle of blood vessels, further contributing to headache relief. The warming sensation caused by gingerols and shogaols also helps to increase blood flow, which can help to relieve tension and pain in the head.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Zingiber officinale creams can provide effective relief from headaches.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup coconut oil, 1/2 cup beeswax, 1/4 cup shea butter, 2 tablespoons ginger oil, and 10 drops peppermint essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until combined.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool slightly. Add ginger oil and peppermint essential oil, and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let cool completely before use. Apply a small amount to the forehead or temples to relieve headache.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two key bioactive constituents known for their anti-inflammatory and calming properties.

These compounds help to relax tense muscles and reduce pain, making Lavandula angustifolia creams a popular natural remedy for headaches. The anti-inflammatory properties of linalool and linalyl acetate also help to reduce swelling and ease pressure on the nerves, contributing to relief from headache pain. Additionally, the calming effects of these compounds can help to reduce stress and anxiety, which are common triggers for headaches.

By targeting both the physical and emotional aspects of headache pain, Lavandula angustifolia creams can provide effective relief from this condition.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers, 2 teaspoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 teaspoons of lavender essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the melted mixture and steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Add vitamin E oil and lavender essential oil to the mixture and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify. Your Lavandula angustifolia creams for headaches are now ready to use.

4. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains compounds like eucalyptol, globulol, and alpha-pinene, which are responsible for its pain-relieving properties.

Eucalyptol, in particular, has been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help reduce swelling and ease tension in the head and neck area. Globulol has a cooling effect that can help relax muscles and reduce pain. Alpha-pinene, a natural anesthetic, helps to numb the area and block pain signals to the brain.

When applied topically to the skin, these compounds can help to relieve headache symptoms by reducing inflammation, relaxing muscles, and blocking pain signals.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of eucalyptus essential oil, and 2 tablespoons of peppermint essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until melted.
  3. Remove melted mixture from heat and stir in eucalyptus and peppermint essential oils.
  4. Pour mixture into small containers and let cool until solid. Store in a cool, dry place.
  5. Apply a small amount to temples or forehead as needed to relieve headaches.

5. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains active constituents like menthol, menthone, and limonene.

These compounds have analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce pain and swelling in the head and neck area. Menthol, in particular, acts as a vasodilator, widening blood vessels and improving blood flow to the brain, which can help alleviate tension and reduce headache severity. The cooling effect of menthol also helps to numb the pain and relax tense muscles.

The combination of these properties in Mentha x piperita creams can provide relief from headache symptoms by addressing multiple contributing factors.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of mentha x piperita oil, 2 tablespoons of lavender oil, and a small bowl to mix.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Remove melted mixture from heat and add mentha x piperita oil and lavender oil. Stir until well combined.
  4. Pour mixture into a silicone ice cube tray or a small tin container. Let it cool and solidify completely.
  5. Once solid, remove cream from mold and store in a cool, dark place. Apply a small amount to affected area as needed for headache relief.

6. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid and carnosic acid, two potent compounds that help alleviate headaches.

Rosmarinic acid has anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling in blood vessels and ease pain. Carnosic acid has antioxidant properties, which protect the brain from oxidative stress and promote relaxation. The combination of these compounds in Rosmarinus officinalis creams helps to relax muscles, reduce tension, and improve blood flow to the brain, all of which contribute to headache relief.

Regular use of Rosmarinus officinalis creams may help to reduce the frequency and severity of headaches.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Rosmarinus officinalis (Rosemary) leaves and 1 cup of coconut oil.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a saucepan over low heat. Add the Rosemary leaves and let it simmer for 2-3 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture and discard the Rosemary leaves. Let the oil cool down.
  4. Mix 1/2 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 1/4 cup of coconut oil. Melt the mixture in a saucepan over low heat.
  5. Combine the cooled down Rosemary oil with the melted shea butter mixture. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool down before use.

7. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce swelling and inflammation in the blood vessels that can cause headaches. The flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have a vasodilatory effect, which means they help to widen the blood vessels and improve blood flow to the brain. This increased blood flow can help to reduce the frequency and severity of headaches.

The terpenoids, such as ginkgolide A and B, have a neuroprotective effect, which can help to reduce the pain and inflammation associated with headaches.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Ginkgo biloba extract, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of shea butter.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or in a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until melted.
  3. Add the Ginkgo biloba extract, vitamin E oil, and shea butter to the melted oil mixture. Stir until well combined.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool until it reaches 120°F to 130°F.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and harden completely before using as a cream to help relieve headaches.

Achillea millefolium creams contains a high concentration of sesquiterpene lactones, such as achillin and santamarin, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

By reducing inflammation, calming the nervous system, and protecting against free radicals, Achillea millefolium creams may provide relief for headaches.

9. Hypericum perforatum

Hyperforin, on the other hand, has a calming effect on the nervous system, which helps to relax tense muscles and ease headache symptoms. By reducing inflammation, relaxing muscles, and fighting oxidative stress, Hypericum perforatum creams can help to alleviate headache symptoms.

Melissa officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, a powerful antioxidant that helps reduce inflammation in the body, which is often a cause of headaches.

The cream also contains linalool, a compound that has a sedative effect, which can help calm the mind and body, leading to relief from headache pain. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent headaches from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent headaches.

Some teas, like feverfew and ginger, have properties that can ease pain and reduce inflammation. These teas can also help relax tense muscles and calm the mind, which can contribute to headache relief.

Drinking herbal tea regularly may help reduce the frequency and severity of headaches.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for headaches every day?

Herbal teas can be a good choice for headache relief, but drinking them every day may not be safe for everyone.

Some herbs, like feverfew and willow bark, contain chemicals that can be toxic in large amounts.

Moderation is key when trying herbal teas regularly, as overconsumption can lead to unwanted side effects.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in headaches?

Herbal teas can help with headaches, but it's hard to say exactly when you'll feel better.

Some people notice a difference right away, within 30 minutes to an hour. Others might not feel relief until after a few hours or even the next day.

It varies from person to person and tea to tea.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for headaches?

If you're experiencing a headache, try drinking herbal tea when it's at its weakest.

This is usually in the morning, when you first wake up, or in the early afternoon, when your body temperature is naturally lower.

These times may help your body absorb the calming effects of the tea.

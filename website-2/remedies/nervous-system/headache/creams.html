What herbal creams you should avoid if you have headaches?

If you have headaches, there are some herbal creams you should avoid.

One of these is cream made from Willow bark (Salix spp.). which contains salicylic acid, a compound similar to aspirin. Taking high doses of salicylic acid can lead to stomach problems and interact with certain medications.

Another herb to avoid is Feverfew (Tanacetum parthenium), which can cause stomach upset and allergic reactions in some people. Also, avoid creams made from Ginkgo biloba, as it can increase the risk of bleeding and interact with blood thinners. Additionally, creams containing Ginger (Zingiber officinale) should be used with caution, as ginger can irritate the skin and cause allergic reactions in some individuals.

Last, avoid creams made from Cayenne pepper (Capsicum annuum), as it can cause skin irritation and increase heart rate, which can be problematic for people with certain heart conditions.

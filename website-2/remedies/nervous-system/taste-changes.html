Taste Changes: Causes and Cures with Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Treatments

Taste changes is a common problem that occurs when the sense of taste is affected, making food and drinks taste bitter, sour, or metallic.

This can be a sign of an underlying health issue, such as a nutritional deficiency, a chronic illness, or even a side effect of medication. If left untreated, taste changes can lead to malnutrition, weight loss, and a decreased quality of life. That's why it's essential to address this issue promptly. Herbal preparations can be a great way to alleviate taste changes, as they are often gentle and effective. For example, a decoction of ginger (Zingiber officinale) can help stimulate the taste buds and restore the sense of taste. Ginger has anti-inflammatory properties that can also help reduce nausea and discomfort associated with taste changes. Another herb that can be beneficial is turmeric (Curcuma longa), which can be made into a decoction or a tincture. Turmeric contains curcumin, a compound with potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the body, which may be contributing to the taste changes.

Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) is also a helpful herb in this context, especially when made into an infusion or a tincture. Licorice root has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help soothe and protect the mucous membranes in the mouth and throat, which may be irritated or inflamed due to taste changes. In addition to these herbs, there are various herbal preparations that can be used to alleviate taste changes. A decoction is a liquid extract made by boiling herbs in water, which can be a great way to release the active compounds from the herbs. A tincture, on the other hand, is a concentrated liquid extract made by soaking herbs in a solvent, such as ethanol or glycerin. An infusion is a liquid extract made by steeping herbs in hot water, which can be a gentle and effective way to release the active compounds from the herbs. By using herbal preparations like decoctions, tinctures, and infusions, individuals can take steps to address taste changes and alleviate their symptoms. This can be especially helpful for those who are experiencing taste changes due to a chronic illness or a side effect of medication.

By incorporating herbal remedies into their treatment plan, individuals can work towards restoring their sense of taste and improving their overall quality of life.

What Are The Main Causes Of Taste Changes?

Changes in taste can be caused by several factors.

Diabetes, a condition where the body has too much sugar in the blood, can lead to taste changes due to nerve damage. High blood sugar levels can damage the taste buds on the tongue, making sweet tastes less noticeable and bitter tastes more intense. Sjögren's syndrome, an autoimmune disease, can also cause taste changes.

This condition causes the body's immune system to attack the glands that produce saliva and tears, leading to dry mouth. Without enough saliva, food doesn't taste as good, and the sense of taste can become dull. Medications, especially certain antibiotics, antihistamines, and antidepressants, can alter taste.

These drugs can affect the taste buds directly or reduce saliva production, leading to changes in the way food tastes.

Below are the main causes of taste changes.

  • Diabetes: Diabetes can cause taste changes because it damages the tiny hairs inside the tongue, called papillae, which help us taste and smell food, and also affects the nerve endings that send taste signals to the brain.
  • Sjogrens: Sjogren's syndrome causes taste changes because the immune system mistakenly attacks the tiny glands in the tongue that help to make saliva, which is necessary for tasting food.
  • Medications: Medications can cause taste changes because they affect the tiny cells on the tongue that help us taste food, and some medications can damage or block these cells, leading to a change in the way food tastes.
  • Oral Cancer: Oral Cancer can cause taste changes because the growth of cancer cells in the mouth can damage or destroy the small sensory nerve endings called taste buds that are responsible for helping us detect flavors.
  • Lupus: Lupus is a cause of taste changes because the disease can damage the nerves that carry taste information from the tongue to the brain, causing a distorted or altered sense of taste.
  • Tobacco: Tobacco causes taste changes because chemicals from tobacco smoke damage the tiny taste buds on our tongues, making it harder for us to enjoy the natural taste of food and drinks.

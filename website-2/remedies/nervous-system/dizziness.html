What Are The Main Causes Of Dizziness?

Dizziness is a common symptom that can be caused by various factors.

One of the main causes is Benign Paroxysmal Positional Vertigo (BPPV), a condition where small crystals in the inner ear become dislodged and cause the brain to receive mixed signals, leading to dizziness. This usually occurs when a person changes position quickly, such as rolling over in bed or standing up too fast. Labyrinthitis is another cause of dizziness, which is an inflammation of the inner ear's balance system.

This can be caused by a viral or bacterial infection that affects the inner ear's ability to balance. Vestibular Neuritis is a condition where the nerve that connects the inner ear to the brain becomes inflamed, disrupting the balance system and causing dizziness. This condition is often caused by a viral infection that affects the nerve.

In all these conditions, the inner ear's balance system is disrupted, leading to a feeling of dizziness and disorientation.

Below are the main causes of dizziness.

  • Benign Paroxysmal Positional Vertigo: Benign Paroxysmal Positional Vertigo (BPPV) is a cause of dizziness because tiny crystals in the inner ear become dislodged and move around when a person changes their head position, sending false signals to the brain, which then interprets these signals as a spinning sensation.
  • Labyrinthitis: Labyrinthitis is a cause of dizziness because it inflames the inner ear's balance system, leading to an imbalance in the body's ability to sense its position and movement in space.
  • Vestibular Neuritis: Vestibular Neuritis is a cause of dizziness because it causes inflammation in the nerves that help our body sense balance and movement, leading to confusion and disorientation in the brain.
  • Inner Ear Infection: The Inner Ear Infection causes dizziness because it irritates the tiny balance organs inside the ear, which helps us keep our balance and sense of direction, making us feel disoriented and unsteady.
  • Head Injury: When you hit your head, it can damage the tiny balance organs inside your inner ear, which helps control your balance and sense of equilibrium, leading to dizziness.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Dizziness?

If you have dizziness, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can worsen the condition.

One such herb is 'Feverfew' (Tanacetum parthenium), which can cause blood vessels to constrict and reduce blood flow to the brain, leading to dizziness. Another herb to be cautious with is 'Hawthorn' (Crataegus monogyna), as it can lower blood pressure and cause a drop in blood pressure, which may exacerbate dizziness.

'Yerba Santa' (Eriodictyon glutinosum) and 'Pennyroyal' (Mentha pulegium) are also best avoided, as they can cause blood vessels to constrict and increase heart rate, making dizziness worse. 'Lobelia' (Lobelia inflata) should also be used with caution, as it can cause a drop in blood pressure and worsen dizziness.

Lastly, 'Kola nut' (Cola nitida) can cause an increase in heart rate and blood pressure, which may worsen dizziness in some individuals.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and glycosides as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and fight off infections that cause chills. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea also stimulate the immune system, increasing the production of white blood cells to combat invading pathogens. Additionally, the glycosides in the tincture have antiviral properties that help combat viral infections that can cause chills and fever.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off infections, Echinacea purpurea tinctures can help alleviate chills and promote overall well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves.
  2. Use a glass jar and fill it with 1 cup of the herb mixture. Add 2 cups of 80% vodka.
  3. Close the jar and shake it well for 2-3 minutes. Let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Take 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day as needed.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains a compound called gingerol, which is a key bioactive constituent that helps with chills.

Gingerol has warming properties that help increase blood flow to the skin, making you feel warmer. This is due to its ability to dilate blood vessels and stimulate the body's natural heat production. Zingiber officinale tinctures also contain shogaol, a bioactive compound that has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce the discomfort associated with chills.

When taken, these compounds can help alleviate chills by warming the body and reducing inflammation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots.
  2. Chop 1/2 cup of the ginger roots into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped ginger with 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks. Shake the jar every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

3. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contains flavonoids and terpene lactones, which help to combat chills.

The flavonoids have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce blood vessel constriction, improving blood flow to the skin and extremities. Terpene lactones, particularly ginkgolides, inhibit platelet-activating factor, which can cause blood vessel constriction and worsen chills. The antioxidants in ginkgo biloba also help to protect against oxidative stress, which can contribute to the onset of chills.

By improving circulation and reducing oxidative stress, ginkgo biloba tinctures can help alleviate chills and promote overall warmth.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves, a clean glass jar with a lid, and a dark glass bottle for storage.
  2. Combine the Ginkgo biloba leaves and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in the glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the dark glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  5. Label the bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Take 20-30 drops in water or tea as needed to help relieve chills.

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contains several bioactive constituents, including eucalyptol, a natural decongestant that helps to relieve chills by reducing inflammation and congestion in the body.

Tannins and flavonoids in the tincture have anti-inflammatory properties that can help to ease chills by reducing the body's natural response to infection. By combining these properties, eucalyptus globulus tinctures can provide effective relief from chills and fever.

5. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis tinctures contains the bioactive constituents quinine and alkaloids, which have antipyretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce fever and alleviate chills by decreasing the body's temperature and relieving the inflammation that often accompanies them. The antipyretic effect of quinine is particularly useful in treating malaria and other fevers that cause chills, while the anti-inflammatory properties help to soothe the body and promote recovery. The alkaloids present in Cinchona officinalis tinctures also have astringent properties, which help to dry up excess fluids and relieve chills caused by colds and flu.

By combining these properties, Cinchona officinalis tinctures can help to effectively manage chills and promote a sense of well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cinchona officinalis bark, a glass jar with a lid, a cup of vodka or glycerin, and a cheesecloth.
  2. Dry the Cinchona officinalis bark in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 1 hour.
  3. Combine the dried bark and vodka or glycerin in the glass jar. The jar should be about 1/4 full of the plant material.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth into another container. Discard the solids and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in chills?

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for chills?

For chills, it's best to drink herbal tea in the evening.

As your body temperature drops at night, the tea can help warm you up from the inside out.

The calming effects of herbal tea can also help you relax and get a good night's sleep, which can aid in recovering from a chill.

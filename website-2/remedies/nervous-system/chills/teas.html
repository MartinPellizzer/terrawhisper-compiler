7 Herbal Teas For Chills

Herbal teas can be a great help when you're feeling chilled.

Take ginger, for example - Zingiber officinale. It's a natural antispasmodic, which means it can help relax your muscles and improve blood flow, both of which can help you feel warmer. Echinacea purpurea is another herb that's often used to relieve chills.

It's thought to boost the immune system, helping your body fight off infection and reduce the symptoms of illness. Licorice root, or Glycyrrhiza glabra, is also sometimes used to relieve chills. It's a natural expectorant, which means it can help loosen and clear mucus from your lungs, making it easier to breathe and feel more comfortable. Drinking herbal tea can have a range of benefits for your life. For one thing, it can help you relax and reduce stress, which is a major contributor to illness. It can also help you sleep better, which is essential for your immune system to function properly.

And, of course, it can simply make you feel more comfortable and able to carry on with your daily life, even when you're not feeling well.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and warming effects on the body. The capsaicin-like compound, shogaol, stimulates the body's thermoregulation system, helping to increase blood flow and warmth. When consumed as a tea, the shogaols and gingerols in Zingiber officinale work together to reduce inflammation and promote a sense of warmth.

This warming effect can help to alleviate chills by increasing the body's internal temperature and reducing discomfort.

  1. Get 1 tablespoon of dried Zingiber officinale root.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Add the dried root to the boiling water and let it steep for 5 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture and discard the root.
  5. Drink the tea hot or let it cool to relieve chills.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and ease congestion. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea tea also have immunomodulatory effects, which can stimulate the immune system to fight off infections that cause chills. The flavonoids in the tea have antioxidant properties that help protect the body from oxidative stress, which can contribute to the development of chills.

By reducing inflammation, stimulating the immune system, and protecting the body from oxidative stress, Echinacea purpurea teas may provide relief from chills.

  1. Gather Echinacea purpurea flowers (dried or fresh) and a cup of boiling water.
  2. Use 2-3 teaspoons of Echinacea purpurea flowers for every cup of boiling water.
  3. Steep the flowers in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea and discard the flowers.
  5. Drink the tea hot or let it cool down, then drink it as needed for relief from chills.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains glycyrrhizin, a key active constituent that helps reduce fever and chills.

Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory properties, which can alleviate body aches and pains often associated with chills. The tea also contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have antioxidant and antimicrobial effects, helping to combat infections that may cause chills. Glycyrrhizin works by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause fever and pain.

By reducing prostaglandin levels, glycyrrhizin helps to bring down body temperature and alleviate chills.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the root in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes to release its flavor and nutrients.
  3. Strain the mixture using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove the root particles.
  4. Add honey or sugar to taste, if desired, and mix well to combine.
  5. Drink the tea hot or warm, 2-3 times a day, to help relieve chills and soothe your body.

4. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains compounds like eucalyptol, camphor, and bornyl acetate.

These volatile oils help to relieve chills by opening up the airways, allowing for easier breathing. Eucalyptol, in particular, has anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce congestion and ease the discomfort caused by chills. The decongestant properties of camphor and bornyl acetate also aid in reducing the buildup of mucus and promoting a sense of warmth and comfort.

By drinking Eucalyptus globulus tea, you can experience the benefits of these compounds in helping to alleviate the symptoms of chills.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 2 teaspoons of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves.
  2. Measure 2 teaspoons of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves and place them in a tea infuser or a small muslin bag.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the Eucalyptus globulus leaves in the tea infuser or muslin bag.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes, then remove the tea infuser or muslin bag.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup and drink it hot, adding honey or lemon if desired.

5. Sambucus nigra

Sambucus nigra teas contains bioactive constituents like sambunigrin, sambutriol, and delphinidin.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce fever and alleviate chills. The delphinidin in Sambucus nigra teas has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which are substances that increase the body's temperature and cause chills. The sambunigrin and sambutriol in the tea also have a direct antiviral effect, which helps to combat the underlying infection causing the chills.

As a result, Sambucus nigra teas can help to quickly and effectively reduce the symptoms of chills.

  1. Gather 20-30 grams of Sambucus nigra flowers and leaves.
  2. Combine the flowers and leaves in a clean cup.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the flowers and leaves.
  4. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then strain it into another cup.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve chills and fever.

6. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris teas contains the bioactive constituents thymol and carvacrol.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce fever and relieve chills. Thymol, in particular, has been shown to have a direct effect on the body's thermoregulation, helping to lower body temperature and alleviate chill symptoms. Carvacrol, on the other hand, has potent anti-inflammatory properties that can help to soothe and calm the body, reducing the discomfort associated with chills.

By consuming Thymus vulgaris teas, the body can benefit from these compounds, which work together to provide relief from chills.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Thymus vulgaris leaves.
  2. Heat 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Pour the heated water over the dried leaves in a cup.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink when it's cool enough.

7. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are active constituents that help alleviate chills.

Flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and ease discomfort. Terpenoids, including bilobalide and ginkgolides, have antispasmodic properties that help relax muscles and improve blood flow, reducing the severity of chills. Ginkgo biloba teas also contains salvestrol Q40, a compound that inhibits the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which contribute to the development of chills.

By addressing inflammation and improving circulation, Ginkgo biloba teas can help alleviate chills and provide relief.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the Ginkgo biloba leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup to remove the leaves.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste if desired, then stir well.
  5. Drink the tea warm to help relieve chills.

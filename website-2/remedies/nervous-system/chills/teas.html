7 Herbal Teas to Help Pregnant Women Manage Chills

When you're pregnant, your body goes through lots of changes.

One of these changes can be feeling cold or chilled, even if the room is warm. This can happen because your body is working harder to support the growth of your baby, and your blood vessels are expanding to carry more blood to your baby. As a result, you might feel cold or chilly, especially in your hands and feet. This can be uncomfortable and even affect your daily life, making it hard to sleep, work, or even do simple tasks. Some pregnant women might even feel anxious or worried about their health when they feel chilled, which can make the situation even more challenging. Pregnant women who experience chills might also be more likely to get infections or have other health problems, like low blood pressure or a weakened immune system. This is because their body is already under stress from the pregnancy, and feeling chilled can make things worse.

On top of this, some women might be taking medications that can cause side effects like dizziness or fatigue, which can make them feel even more chilled. Feeling chilled can also affect a pregnant woman's mood and energy levels. Some women might feel tired or irritable, which can make it hard to take care of themselves and their baby. They might also have trouble sleeping or concentrating, which can make everyday tasks feel even more overwhelming. When a pregnant woman feels chilled, she might try to warm up with a blanket or a hot bath. But sometimes, these remedies don't work, or they might not be comfortable or safe to use while pregnant. This is where herbal teas come in - they can be a natural and soothing way to help a pregnant woman feel better when she's chilled. Herbal teas are made from plants that have been used for centuries to help with all sorts of health issues, including fever and chills.

They can help to relax the body and mind, making it easier to fall asleep or feel more comfortable when you're chilled.

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, have been used for centuries to help with chills and body aches.

This is due to its active compounds, such as gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. These compounds help to reduce the severity of chills and fever, making it easier to recover from illness. To use ginger tea for chills, start by making a tea by steeping fresh ginger in hot water. You can also add honey and lemon to taste.

Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help alleviate chills and body aches. Additionally, you can try taking a warm bath or shower after drinking the tea to help increase blood circulation and reduce chills. When you take action, you can expect the chills to start subsiding within a few hours. Your body temperature should return to normal, and you may also start feeling more energetic and less tired.

However, if your chills are severe, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent chills from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help reduce chills.

Some teas, like peppermint and ginger, contain warming compounds that can increase your body temperature. This can make you feel warmer and more comfortable when you're feeling cold.

However, the effect of herbal tea on chills can vary from person to person, and it may not work for everyone.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for chills every day?

Herbal teas for chills can be a soothing option, but having them every day may not be safe for everyone.

Some herbs can interact with medications or have side effects when consumed regularly. For example, ginger and peppermint can cause stomach upset in large amounts.

It's best to limit consumption and choose teas from trusted sources.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in chills?

Herbal teas can help reduce chills, but it may take a few hours or even a full day to notice results.

Some teas, like peppermint and ginger, can start working within 30 minutes to an hour, while others, like chamomile, may take longer.

It's best to try different teas to see what works best for you.

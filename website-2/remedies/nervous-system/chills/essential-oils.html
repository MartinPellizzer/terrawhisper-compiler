8 Herbal Essential Oils For Chills

As people age, their bodies go through natural changes that can affect their ability to regulate temperature.

Older adults may experience chills due to a decrease in muscle mass, a reduction in the body's ability to produce heat, or underlying health conditions such as anemia or hypothyroidism. When older adults get chills, it can be a sign of a more serious issue, and it can also be uncomfortable and disrupt their daily lives. Chills can make it difficult to sleep, cause fatigue, and even lead to falls or other accidents. Older adults may also experience anxiety or depression due to the discomfort and uncertainty of not knowing what's causing their chills. Chills can be especially problematic for older adults who may already have difficulty managing their daily needs, such as bathing, dressing, or taking medication.

When they're feeling chilled, it can be hard for them to get out of bed, get dressed, or participate in activities they enjoy. This can lead to feelings of isolation and frustration. In some cases, chills can be a symptom of an underlying infection, such as pneumonia or urinary tract infection, which requires medical attention. Herbal essential oils can be a helpful addition to the treatment of chills in older adults. Certain oils have natural warming properties that can help increase circulation and relax the body, making it easier to manage chills.

These oils can be inhaled, applied topically, or added to a warm bath, providing relief from discomfort and promoting relaxation.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains properties like decongestant and anti-inflammatory compounds, including eucalyptol, which helps break down mucus and ease congestion.

The menthol and camphor present in the oil have a cooling effect on the body, making it an effective remedy for chills and fever. When you inhale the vapors of Eucalyptus globulus essential oils, the menthol binds to receptors in the nasal passages, creating a cooling sensation that helps reduce fever and soothe the body. To use Eucalyptus globulus essential oils for chills, add a few drops to your diffuser or inhale it directly from a cloth or handkerchief.

You can also mix it with a carrier oil and apply it to your skin, but make sure to do a patch test first. As you inhale the vapors, you may start to feel a reduction in fever and chills, and your body may begin to relax. You might also experience relief from congestion and a clearer breathing.

However, if your chills persist or worsen, consult a healthcare professional for further guidance.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent chills from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent chills in some cases.

Ginger tea, for example, has natural warming properties that can raise your body temperature. Peppermint tea, on the other hand, can help ease congestion and promote blood flow, which can also help prevent chills.

These teas might be especially helpful during cold weather or when feeling under the weather.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for chills every day?

While herbal teas can help with chills, consuming them daily might not be safe for everyone.

Some herbal teas contain caffeine or other stimulants that can interfere with sleep or cause jitters. Others may interact with medications or have side effects like stomach upset.

It's best to drink them in moderation and choose teas that are specifically labeled as safe for daily use.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in chills?

Herbal teas can help with chills, but it takes some time to notice results.

Some people feel better within 30 minutes to an hour after drinking a hot herbal tea.

The relief from chills can last a few hours, but it depends on the individual and the type of tea consumed.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for chills?

Drinking herbal tea for chills is most effective in the morning or early afternoon.

This allows the tea's warming properties to take hold as the day progresses, helping to combat coldness and increase circulation.

As you go about your daily activities, the tea's benefits can build, providing a sense of warmth and comfort.

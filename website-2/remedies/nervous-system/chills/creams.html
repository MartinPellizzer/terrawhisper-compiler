1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties, which can help alleviate chills. Alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to stimulate the production of white blood cells, while caffeic acid derivatives have antioxidant properties that can help reduce inflammation. Flavonoids, such as quercetin, also have anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate chills.

By reducing inflammation and boosting the immune system, Echinacea purpurea creams may provide relief from chills by addressing their underlying causes.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the melted mixture and steep for 10-15 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Add shea butter and vitamin E oil to the bowl and mix well until combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, your Echinacea purpurea cream is ready to use.

Can drinking herbal tea prevent chills from forming?

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for chills every day?

While herbal teas can be a soothing way to calm chills, consuming them every day might not be the best idea.

Some teas, like peppermint and ginger, can cause stomach issues if had excessively.

Others, like chamomile, might make you drowsy if taken in large amounts, so moderation is key.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in chills?

Herbal teas can help with chills, but the time it takes to feel better varies.

Some people notice a difference in 30 minutes to an hour after drinking a warm tea. Others might feel better within 2-3 hours.

It's different for everyone, and it depends on the tea and the severity of the chills.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains active constituents like zingiberene, bisabolol, and gingerol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling and pain in the mouth and throat, making it easier to taste food. The antibacterial properties of gingerol and bisabolol also help to eliminate bacteria that cause bad breath. The anti-inflammatory properties of zingiberene reduce inflammation in the digestive system, which can contribute to bad taste.

By reducing inflammation and eliminating bacteria, Zingiber officinale essential oils helps to improve taste and reduce bad breath.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger roots. Wash and peel them.
  2. Use a juicer to extract 2 cups of ginger juice from the roots.
  3. Transfer the juice to a saucepan and add 1 cup of water.
  4. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes, then let it cool.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl. Discard the solids.

2. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum essential oils contains compounds like cinnamaldehyde, linalool, and eugenol, which are responsible for its properties.

These properties help to reduce bad taste by killing bacteria that cause bad breath and gum inflammation. Cinnamaldehyde has antimicrobial properties that inhibit the growth of Streptococcus mutans, a bacterium that contributes to tooth decay and bad taste. Linalool has anti-inflammatory properties that soothe gum inflammation and reduce swelling, making it easier to eat and digest food without bad taste.

The antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties of Cinnamomum verum essential oils help to maintain oral health and fresh breath.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Cinnamomum verum (Ceylon cinnamon) bark and 4 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water in the pot over medium heat and add the Ceylon cinnamon bark. Let it simmer for 2 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a still or a distillation apparatus. Heat it until the liquid turns into steam and then condenses back into a liquid.
  5. Collect the distilled essential oil and store it in a dark glass bottle. Use a few drops at a time to avoid spoilage.

3. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains limonene, menthone, and linalool as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties that help alleviate bad taste caused by sinus congestion and inflammation. The menthol in Mentha x piperita essential oils also acts as a natural expectorant, helping to thin mucus and reduce its viscosity, making it easier to expel. The antibacterial properties of linalool and limonene in the oil help prevent bacterial overgrowth in the mouth and throat, which can contribute to bad taste.

By reducing sinus congestion, inflammation, and bacterial overgrowth, Mentha x piperita essential oils can help restore a normal sense of taste and smell.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water over medium heat and bring it to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat to low and add the fresh Mentha x piperita leaves. Let it steep for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the solids.
  5. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) to the liquid and mix well.

4. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains active constituents like limonene, anethole, and fenchone.

These compounds have carminative properties, which help to expel gas from the digestive system and reduce symptoms of bad taste and bad breath. The antiseptic properties of fenchone and anethole also help to eliminate bacteria in the mouth and throat, preventing the growth of odor-causing microorganisms. Additionally, the anise-like flavor of anethole can help to mask unpleasant tastes and odors.

By reducing gas and eliminating bacteria, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help to improve the overall oral health and freshness of the mouth.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil (like sweet almond oil).
  2. Heat 1/4 cup of carrier oil in a double boiler until warm.
  3. Add 1/4 cup of Foeniculum vulgare seeds to the warm oil and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth into a clean bowl. Discard the seeds.
  5. Store the Foeniculum vulgare essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight lid.

5. Elettaria cardamomum

Elettaria cardamomum essential oils contains compounds like limonene, linalool, and beta-pinene.

These components have anti-inflammatory properties and act as a natural digestive aid. Limonene, in particular, has been shown to have antibacterial effects that help reduce the presence of pathogens that can cause bad breath. Linalool, on the other hand, has a calming effect on the digestive system, which can help alleviate nausea and stomach discomfort.

By targeting the root causes of bad taste, Elettaria cardamomum essential oils can provide a natural solution to this issue.

  1. Gather 500 grams of Elettaria cardamomum seeds and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Heat 250 grams of the seeds in a saucepan over low heat for 30 minutes.
  3. Let the seeds cool, then crush them in a blender or food processor.
  4. Combine the crushed seeds with the carrier oil in a glass jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a separate container, then discard the solids.

6. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains carnosol and borneol, two bioactive constituents known to have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling and inflammation in the mouth and throat, which can cause bad taste. The oil's antimicrobial properties, primarily due to the presence of camphor and 1,8-cineole, also help to combat bacteria and fungi that can contribute to unpleasant tastes. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils, particularly that of camphor, help to protect oral tissues from oxidative damage.

This combination of properties can help to alleviate bad taste and promote a healthy oral environment.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or grapeseed oil).
  2. Clean and dry the leaves. Use a clean glass jar to store them.
  3. Add 1 cup of carrier oil to the jar. Pour in just enough oil to cover the leaves completely.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it gently every day. This allows the oil to infuse into the leaves.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil into a clean glass bottle using cheesecloth or a coffee filter. Discard the leaves.

7. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a compound called curcumin, a powerful antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent.

Curcumin is responsible for its medicinal properties, including its ability to help with bad taste. The oil also contains other bioactive constituents like turmeric oil, which has a compound called ar-turmerone. Ar-turmerone has been shown to have antimicrobial properties, which can help to eliminate bacteria that cause bad breath and bad taste.

By eliminating these bacteria and reducing inflammation, Curcuma longa essential oils can help to improve bad taste and leave your mouth feeling fresh and clean.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Curcuma longa rhizomes and 1 cup of water. Grate the rhizomes finely.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the grated rhizomes and water. Heat over low heat for 2 hours, then let cool.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a distillation apparatus or a steam distillation setup. Distill the liquid to separate the essential oil from the water.
  5. Collect the essential oil in a clean glass container. Use 1-2% of the essential oil in your desired application to eliminate bad taste.

8. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol, a key bioactive constituent with antibacterial properties that help reduce bad taste.

This is because thymol has a strong antiseptic effect on the mouth and throat, eliminating the bacteria that cause bad breath and taste. The anti-inflammatory properties of thymol also soothe and calm the mucous membranes, allowing for a fresh and clean sensation in the mouth. Additionally, thymol's antimicrobial properties help prevent the growth of bacteria that can cause bad taste and odor.

By using Thymus vulgaris essential oils, you can naturally keep your mouth fresh and clean.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and flowers.
  2. Use 1 cup of water to make a strong infusion by boiling the Thymus vulgaris mixture for 10 minutes.
  3. Let the mixture cool down. Strain the liquid and discard the solids.
  4. Use a distillation kit or a simple steam distillation setup to separate the essential oil from the water.
  5. Collect the extracted essential oil and store it in a dark glass bottle with a tight lid.

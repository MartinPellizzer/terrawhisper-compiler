The flowers of this plant contain special compounds that have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. When applied to the skin around the eyes, these compounds can help reduce swelling and redness, which are common causes of eye pain. Calendula officinalis can also help to soothe the skin and calm the nerves, making it easier to tolerate painful eye conditions. Additionally, its antimicrobial properties can help prevent infections, which can sometimes cause eye pain. To use calendula for eye pain, you can make a compress by steeping the flowers in hot water, then apply the cooled liquid to a clean cloth and place it over your eyes.

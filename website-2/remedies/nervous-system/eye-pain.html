What Are The Main Causes Of Eye Pain?

Eye pain can be caused by several factors.

One main cause is Dry Eyes, which happens when the eyes don't produce enough tears or the tears are not of good quality. This can lead to irritation and discomfort. Another cause is Conjunctivitis, an infection that inflames the thin layer of tissue covering the white part of the eyes and the inside of the eyelids. This can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or allergens.

The infection can cause redness, itching, and pain in the eyes. A more serious cause of eye pain is Glaucoma. This is a condition where the pressure in the eyes becomes too high, damaging the optic nerve. Glaucoma can be caused by problems with the eye's drainage system, which allows the fluid to flow out. When the fluid builds up, it puts pressure on the optic nerve, leading to pain and potentially even blindness.

This condition can be caused by genetics, age, or other health issues.

Below are the main causes of eye pain.

  • Dry Eyes: Dry eyes cause eye pain because the lack of tears and moisture in the eyes makes the eyes feel irritated, inflamed, and scratchy, leading to discomfort and pain.
  • Conjunctivitis: Conjunctivitis causes eye pain because the inflamed and irritated conjunctiva, a thin layer of tissue covering the white part of the eye, sends pain signals to the brain, making the eye feel sore and uncomfortable.
  • Glaucoma: Glaucoma is a cause of eye pain because the increased pressure inside the eye can stretch and irritate the sensitive nerve endings, leading to a sharp, stabbing pain.
  • Cataract: A cataract can cause eye pain because it blocks light from passing through the lens, leading to pressure and strain on the eye.
  • Uveitis: Uveitis is a cause of eye pain because it causes inflammation in the middle layer of the eye, called the uvea, which can lead to severe pain and discomfort in the eye.
  • Sinusitis: Sinusitis can cause eye pain because the infection in the sinuses can spread to the eyes, causing inflammation and pressure that leads to pain and discomfort in the eyes.
  • Iritis: Iritis is a cause of eye pain because the inflamed inner layer of the eye, called the iris, becomes irritated and sore, causing sharp, stabbing pains and discomfort.

One herb to be careful with is Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis). It contains a compound called berberine, which can make eye pain worse. Berberine can also cause sensitivity to light, making your eyes more painful. Another herb to avoid is Eyebright alternative - Ginkgo biloba.

Ginkgo biloba can cause eye problems like redness and vision changes. It's especially bad if you have high blood pressure or glaucoma. Some herbs like Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) may increase blood pressure, which can worsen eye pain. Feverfew (Tanacetum parthenium) can cause blood vessels in the eyes to constrict, making eye pain worse. And lastly, Ginger (Zingiber officinale) can cause eye irritation if used in high doses.

If you have eye pain, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbs.

Eye Floaters - Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Natural Preparations for Relief

What are the causes of eye floaters?

Eye floaters are small spots or threads that appear in your vision, and they can be caused by several things.

One common cause is posterior vitreous detachment, which is when the gel-like substance in the center of your eye, called the vitreous, separates from the retina. This can cause floaters to appear as the vitreous pulls on the retina.

Diabetes can also cause eye floaters, as high blood sugar levels can damage the blood vessels in the retina, leading to small areas of damage that appear as floaters. In some cases, tractional retinal detachment can cause eye floaters, which is when the retina becomes detached from the back of the eye due to scarring from diabetes or other conditions. A retinal tear can also cause eye floaters, as the tear can cause the vitreous to pull on the retina, creating the appearance of floaters.

Finally, a full retinal detachment can cause eye floaters, as the retina becomes completely detached from the back of the eye, causing a range of vision problems, including the appearance of floaters.

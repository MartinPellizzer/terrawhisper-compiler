What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have eye pain?

If you have eye pain, it's best to avoid herbal decoctions that can irritate or worsen the condition.

One such herb is Eyebright's cousin, 'Arctium lappa', also known as Burdock. Its seeds and roots contain compounds that can cause allergic reactions and increase eye inflammation. Another herb to be cautious with is 'Ephedra sinica', also known as Ma Huang, which can raise blood pressure and cause eye strain.

The roots of 'Glycyrrhiza glabra', licorice root, can also cause eye problems if taken in large amounts. Licorice root can increase the risk of glaucoma and interact with other medications. Furthermore, 'Ginkgo biloba' can cause eye problems, such as sensitivity to light and blurred vision, especially when taken in high doses. Additionally, 'Gelsemium sempervirens', also known as Yellow Jessamine, can cause eye irritation and increase eye pressure.

These herbs can worsen eye pain or cause new problems, so it's best to consult with a healthcare professional before using them.

11 Herbal Teas For Taste Changes

Herbal teas have been used for centuries to help with taste changes, and it's not just a myth.

When you're feeling down or recovering from an illness, your sense of taste can be affected, making food and drinks taste bland or metallic. This is where herbal teas come in - they can not only soothe your senses but also provide a boost to your taste buds. For example, Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has natural anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce inflammation in the mouth and throat, which can contribute to a change in taste. Curcuma longa, or turmeric, contains a powerful compound called curcumin, which has been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can help to heal and protect the mucous membranes in your mouth and throat.

Glycyrrhiza glabra, or licorice root, has been used for centuries to soothe stomach issues and has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce irritation in the mouth and throat. Drinking herbal teas like these can help to stimulate your taste buds and get your sense of taste back on track. When you drink herbal tea, the flavors and aromas can help to stimulate the nerve endings in your mouth and throat, which can help to increase saliva production and reduce inflammation. This can help to restore your sense of taste and make food and drinks taste more flavorful again.

Plus, the antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties in these herbs can help to boost your immune system and reduce inflammation in your body, which can help to prevent future taste changes.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, helping to soothe digestive issues and reduce nausea. The gingerols and shogaols in Zingiber officinale teas have been shown to stimulate digestion and improve appetite, making it beneficial for people experiencing taste changes due to digestive problems. The antioxidant properties of these compounds also help to protect the stomach and intestines from damage, promoting a healthy gut and reducing inflammation.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healthy digestion, Zingiber officinale teas can help alleviate taste changes caused by digestive issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots.
  2. Peel and chop the ginger roots into small pieces. Use 1-2 tablespoons for a single serving.
  3. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot and add the chopped ginger pieces.
  4. Steep the ginger in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes to release the flavors.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup and serve hot or with honey to taste.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa teas contains bioactive constituents like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have strong antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help protect the body from oxidative stress and inflammation that may be contributing to taste changes. Curcumin, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines, which can help alleviate symptoms of taste disorders. The antioxidants in Curcuma longa teas may also help repair and protect the mucous membranes in the mouth and throat, which can become damaged and affect taste.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Curcuma longa teas may help restore normal taste function and alleviate taste changes.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried turmeric roots (Curcuma longa) and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Add the turmeric to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup using a tea strainer or a piece of cheesecloth.
  4. Add 1-2 tablespoons of honey or sugar to taste, if desired.
  5. Serve the turmeric tea warm and enjoy the potential health benefits.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains licorice root extract, which is rich in compounds like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

Glycyrrhizin helps to balance the flavor and aroma of food by reducing bitterness and increasing sweetness. Flavonoids like isoliquertigenin and licoricidin have anti-inflammatory properties, which may help to soothe and calm the digestive system. The phenolic acids present in licorice root tea may also help to reduce inflammation and protect the mucous membranes in the mouth and throat.

By promoting healthy digestion and reducing inflammation, Glycyrrhiza glabra teas can help alleviate changes in taste perception caused by digestive issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root.
  2. Boil the water in a pot and add the dried root.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture and discard the root. Let the tea cool down.
  5. Serve the tea in a cup and adjust the taste with honey or lemon to your liking.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains rosmarinic acid, carnosic acid, and borneol as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that may help to reduce inflammation in the mouth and throat, which can cause taste changes. The antioxidant properties of rosmarinic acid and carnosic acid can also help to protect the mucous membranes from damage and promote healing. Additionally, borneol's antimicrobial properties can help to reduce the presence of bacteria and other pathogens that can cause infections and disrupt taste.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Rosmarinus officinalis teas may help to alleviate taste changes and restore normal taste function.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Pour the boiling water over the leaves and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid into a cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  4. Add honey or sugar to taste if desired, then mix well.
  5. Enjoy your Rosmarinus officinalis tea hot or cold, sweet or unsweet.

5. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum teas contains active constituents like cinnamaldehyde, cinnamyl acetate, and linalool, which help with taste changes.

6. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, terpenoids, and bilobalide.

These compounds help protect the tongue's taste buds from oxidative stress, which can damage them and lead to changes in taste. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce inflammation in the mouth and improve taste perception. The terpenoids, including ginkgolides, can help increase blood flow to the tongue, which may improve taste sensitivity.

By reducing oxidative stress and inflammation, and improving blood flow, Ginkgo biloba teas may help alleviate taste changes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Ginkgo biloba leaves.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of Ginkgo biloba leaves and place them in a tea infuser or a small muslin bag.
  3. Heat 1 cup of water in a pot and bring it to a boil.
  4. Pour the boiling water over the Ginkgo biloba leaves in the tea infuser or muslin bag. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup and enjoy. You can add honey or lemon to taste.

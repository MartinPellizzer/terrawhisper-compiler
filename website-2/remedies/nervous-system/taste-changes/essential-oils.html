1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains compounds like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which have potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to soothe and protect the mucous membranes in the mouth, reducing inflammation and irritation that can cause taste changes. The bioactive constituent curcumin also has a known ability to neutralize free radicals, which can damage the taste buds and lead to altered taste perception. By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Curcuma longa essential oils can help to restore normal taste function and alleviate taste changes.

This can be especially beneficial for people experiencing taste disturbances due to chemotherapy, radiation, or other medical conditions.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Gingerol has anti-inflammatory properties that help alleviate nausea and reduce the sensation of taste changes. The essential oil also contains zingerone, a compound that helps to stimulate digestion and reduce nausea. Zingiber officinale essential oils has warming properties that can help to increase saliva production, which can be beneficial for people experiencing taste changes.

This increased saliva production can help to improve the sensation of taste and reduce the discomfort associated with taste changes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale roots and wash them clean.
  2. Dry the roots in a warm, dry place for 1 week or use a food dehydrator.
  3. Grind 100g of dried roots into a fine powder using a spice grinder.
  4. Mix 1 tablespoon of the powder with 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil) in a clean glass jar.
  5. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily, then strain and discard the solids.

It's a gradual process, and your taste may change as your body gets used to the new flavors.

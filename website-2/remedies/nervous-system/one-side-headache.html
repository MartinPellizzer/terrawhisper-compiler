What Are The Main Causes Of One Side Headache?

A one-sided headache is a type of headache that occurs on one side of the head, often behind or on the temple area.

There are several reasons why this happens, and three common causes are Cluster Headache, Migraine, and Trigeminal Neuralgia. Cluster Headache is a type of headache that occurs in clusters or periods, where one side of the head experiences severe pain, usually on the temple or eye area. This pain is often accompanied by redness, tearing, and a runny nose on the same side as the headache. Cluster Headaches can be triggered by changes in temperature, stress, or certain foods. Migraine is a complex condition that can cause severe one-sided headaches.

Migraines often occur on one side of the head and are accompanied by sensitivity to light and sound, nausea, and vomiting. Migraines can be triggered by hormonal changes, certain foods, or stress. Trigeminal Neuralgia is a condition that affects the trigeminal nerve, which is responsible for sensation in the face. When this nerve is irritated or inflamed, it can cause severe one-sided headaches, often in the temple or cheek area. Trigeminal Neuralgia can be triggered by cold temperatures, brushing teeth, or even light touch on the affected area.

These three conditions have different causes, but all can lead to severe one-sided headaches that can disrupt daily life.

Below are the main causes of one side headache.

  • Cluster Headache: Cluster Headache occurs when a blood vessel in one side of the head suddenly swells, causing intense pain and inflammation that only affects one side of the head.
  • Migraine: Migraine is a cause of one-sided headache because a blood vessel in the brain called the trigeminal nerve, which controls pain, becomes swollen and irritated, sending severe pain signals to one side of the head.
  • Trigeminal Neuralgia: Trigeminal Neuralgia is a condition where the nerve that sends pain signals to the brain from one side of the face gets damaged, causing severe and sudden headaches on that specific side of the face.
  • Temporomandibular Joint Disorder: Temporomandibular Joint Disorder (TMJ) can cause one-sided headaches because the disorder affects the joint that connects the jawbone to the skull, leading to inflammation and pain that can be felt in the temple area, which is often referred to as a headache.
  • Sinusitis: Sinusitis is a cause of one-sided headache because the infection in the sinuses on that side can put pressure on the nerves and blood vessels, leading to severe pain and discomfort on the same side of the face.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have One Side Headache?

If you have a one-sided headache, also known as a unilateral headache, it's essential to avoid certain herbs that can exacerbate the condition.

Some herbs to be cautious with include Angelica (Angelica sinensis), which can cause blood vessels to constrict, potentially worsening the headache. Another herb to avoid is Ginger's relative, Asafoetida (Ferula asafoetida), which contains compounds that can irritate the blood vessels and worsen the pain.

Feverfew (Tanacetum parthenium) is also a concern, as its active compound, parthenolide, can cause blood vessels to constrict and worsen the headache. Additionally, Willow bark (Salix alba) contains salicylic acid, which is similar to aspirin, and can be too intense for a one-sided headache. Finally, Ginkgo biloba, while often used to improve circulation, can cause increased heart rate and blood pressure, potentially worsening the headache.

It's best to consult with a healthcare professional before using any of these herbs to manage your headache.

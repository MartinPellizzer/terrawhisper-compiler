Hangover: The Science Behind the Causes and Medicinal Herbal Preparations

What are the causes of hangover?

When you have a hangover, it's usually because your body is dealing with the aftermath of drinking too much alcohol.

One major cause is dehydration - when your body loses more water than it takes in, your cells and organs start to suffer. This happens because alcohol is a diuretic, which means it makes you pee more often. So, when you drink too much, you're losing water quickly, and it's hard for your body to keep up. Another cause is acetaldehyde, a toxic compound that your body produces when it breaks down alcohol. This chemical can damage your cells and lead to inflammation, making you feel sick and uncomfortable. Electrolyte imbalance is also a big contributor to hangovers. Electrolytes are minerals like sodium and potassium that help your body function properly.

When you drink too much, your body loses these minerals quickly, leading to an imbalance. This can cause fatigue, headaches, and other symptoms. Cytokine release is another factor that comes into play when you have a hangover. Cytokines are proteins that help your body fight off infections. When you drink too much, your body releases more cytokines, which can cause inflammation and make you feel worse. Finally, inflammation is a major cause of hangovers. When you drink too much, your body's immune system goes into overdrive, releasing chemicals that cause inflammation.

This can lead to headaches, nausea, and other symptoms that are so common when you have a hangover.

What Are The Main Causes Of Hangover?

When you have a hangover, it's usually because of three main things: dehydration, ethanol, and acetaldehyde.

First, dehydration happens when your body loses more water than it takes in. When you drink alcohol, it's a diuretic, which means it makes you go to the bathroom more often and lose water. This is a big problem because your body needs water to function properly, and if you're dehydrated, you'll feel tired, headaches, and nauseous. The second cause of hangover is ethanol itself. Ethanol is the type of alcohol found in drinks.

When you drink too much, it can damage your body's cells and tissues. This damage can make you feel sick, and it can also make your body release chemicals that can worsen your hangover symptoms. The third cause is acetaldehyde, a toxic chemical produced when your body breaks down ethanol. Acetaldehyde is made when an enzyme called alcohol dehydrogenase converts ethanol into acetaldehyde. This chemical is even more toxic than ethanol and can cause more damage to your body.

When you have too much acetaldehyde in your system, it can make you feel worse, with symptoms like nausea, vomiting, and stomach pain.

Below are the main causes of hangover.

  • Dehydration: Dehydration is a main cause of hangover because when you drink too much alcohol, your body loses more water than it can hold, leading to a lack of fluids and causing headaches, fatigue, and other symptoms.
  • Ethanol: Ethanol, the main ingredient in alcoholic drinks, causes hangover because it breaks down into toxic substances like acetaldehyde in the body, which can lead to headaches, nausea, and dehydration.
  • Acetaldehyde: Acetaldehyde, a toxic chemical produced when we break down alcohol in our body, causes hangovers by irritating our brain, digestive system, and other organs, leading to symptoms like headaches, nausea, and fatigue.
  • Nausea: When we drink too much alcohol, it irritates the lining of our stomach, causing nausea, which is a common symptom of a hangover because our body is trying to get rid of the toxins from the alcohol.
  • Vomiting: When you vomit after drinking too much, your body loses water and essential salts, making you feel weak, tired, and dehydrated, which contributes to the symptoms of a hangover.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Hangover?

If you have a hangover, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can make things worse.

For example, 'Kava' (Piper methysticum) can worsen liver problems, and if you've been drinking too much, your liver is already stressed. 'Yohimbe' (Pausinystalia johimbe) can increase blood pressure and heart rate, which can be a problem if you're already dehydrated and your body is trying to recover.

'Ephedra' (Ephedra sinica) is also a no-go, as it can increase heart rate and blood pressure, making dehydration and hangover symptoms worse. 'Valerian' (Valeriana officinalis) can cause drowsiness, which may sound good for a hangover, but it can also make it harder to rehydrate and recover. 'Licorice root' (Glycyrrhiza glabra) can increase blood pressure and worsen potassium levels, which are already low when you're dehydrated.

'Ashwagandha' (Withania somnifera) can be toxic in large amounts, and if you're already feeling unwell, it's best to avoid it.

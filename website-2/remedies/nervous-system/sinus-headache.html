What Are The Main Causes Of Sinus Headache?

A sinus headache is caused by pressure and pain in the sinuses, which are air-filled cavities in the face.

The main causes of sinus headache include Rhinitis, Polyps, and Allergies. Rhinitis is an inflammation of the nasal passages, often caused by a virus or bacteria, leading to congestion, swelling, and increased mucus production. This swelling and mucus block the sinuses, causing pressure and pain. Polyps are small growths that can develop inside the nasal passages, blocking airflow and mucus drainage.

They can be caused by chronic inflammation, allergies, or a viral infection. The blockage caused by polyps can lead to sinus pressure and pain. Allergies are another common cause of sinus headache. When the body reacts to an allergen, such as pollen or dust, it releases histamine, which causes blood vessels to swell and mucus production to increase. This can lead to congestion and sinus pressure, resulting in a headache.

Other factors that can contribute to sinus headache include colds, sinus infections, and structural abnormalities in the nasal passages.

Below are the main causes of sinus headache.

  • Rhinitis: Rhinitis causes inflammation and swelling in the nasal passages, which can lead to blocked sinus openings and pressure buildup, resulting in a painful sinus headache.
  • Polyps: Polyps in the sinuses can cause sinus headaches because they block the normal flow of air and mucus, leading to swelling, pressure, and pain in the face and head.
  • Allergies: When an allergen like pollen or dust enters the body, it triggers an allergic reaction, causing the nasal passages to swell and the sinuses to become inflamed, leading to a painful sinus headache.
  • Sinusitis: Sinusitis is a cause of sinus headache because the inflamed and infected sinuses in the face produce pressure and pain that can feel like a dull, aching headache.
  • Infection: Infection in the sinuses can cause a sinus headache because the bacteria or virus that infects the sinuses leads to inflammation and swelling, which puts pressure on the nerves and causes pain in the face and head area.

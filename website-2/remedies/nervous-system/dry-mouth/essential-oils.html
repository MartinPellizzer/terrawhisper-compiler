7 Herbal Essential Oils For Dry Mouth

Herbal essential oils can help with dry mouth.

Herbal teas made from these herbs can help to relieve dry mouth because they can be absorbed through the mouth, stimulating saliva production from within. 1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce bacteria and inflammation in the mouth. The essential oil's antibacterial properties, in particular, can help to combat the bacteria that cause dry mouth. Additionally, bornyl acetate has a soothing effect on the mucous membranes, which can help to alleviate dryness and discomfort in the mouth.

By reducing bacteria and inflammation, and soothing the mucous membranes, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can provide relief for dry mouth symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water.
  2. Combine the leaves and water in a saucepan and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a glass container and let it cool. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil like jojoba or sweet almond oil.
  5. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use 2-3 drops as needed to help soothe dry mouth.

2. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce the bacteria causing bad breath and inflammation in the mouth. Melissa officinalis essential oils also contains rosmarinic acid, which has antioxidant properties that protect the mouth and throat from damage. The antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties of Melissa officinalis essential oils help to soothe and calm the mucous membranes in the mouth, reducing dryness and discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the mouth and throat, Melissa officinalis essential oils can help alleviate dry mouth symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well to mix.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a mouthwash or as directed by a healthcare professional.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds can help reduce swelling and pain in the mouth and throat, making it a good option for people with dry mouth. Gingerol and shogaol also have antimicrobial properties, which can help combat the growth of bacteria that can lead to dry mouth. The warming sensation of Zingiber officinale essential oils can also stimulate saliva production, helping to keep the mouth moist.

By reducing inflammation, fighting bacteria, and stimulating saliva production, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help alleviate dry mouth symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale roots (ginger roots), 1 cup of carrier oil (such as coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Wash the ginger roots with water and chop them into small pieces. Fill the glass jar with the chopped ginger roots.
  3. Pour 1 cup of carrier oil over the ginger roots in the jar. Close the jar and shake it well to mix the ingredients.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will help the ginger roots infuse their essence into the carrier oil.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the ginger roots and use the infused oil for dry mouth relief.

4. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains rosmarinic acid, borneol, and camphor as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation and kill bacteria in the mouth, relieving dry mouth symptoms. The antimicrobial properties of borneol and camphor prevent the growth of bacteria that cause bad breath and gum disease. Rosmarinic acid also has anti-inflammatory properties that soothe and protect the mucous membranes in the mouth.

By reducing inflammation and killing bacteria, Salvia officinalis essential oils can help to alleviate dry mouth and promote a healthy oral environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Salvia officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Salvia leaves. Let it steep for 20-30 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil) to the liquid and mix well.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 hours or overnight to allow the essential oil to separate and settle.

5. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains compounds like menthol, menthone, and limonene that work together to provide relief from dry mouth.

Menthol helps to stimulate saliva production by increasing the blood flow to the salivary glands. Menthone has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and irritation in the mouth, which can contribute to dry mouth. The antimicrobial properties of limonene help to prevent the growth of bacteria that can cause dryness and discomfort in the mouth.

By using Mentha x piperita essential oils, you can help to restore moisture and comfort to your mouth.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves. Choose leaves with no signs of damage or mold.
  2. Add 1 cup of water to a saucepan and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low.
  3. Place the Mentha x piperita leaves in a heat-resistant glass bowl. Pour the boiling water over the leaves.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 2 hours or overnight. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into a clean glass container.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 24 hours to allow the water to evaporate. The remaining liquid is your Mentha x piperita essential oil for dry mouth. Use a clean dropper to apply a few drops to your tongue or mouth as needed.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two key bioactive constituents that contribute to its benefits for dry mouth.

Linalool has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and irritation in the mouth and throat. Linalyl acetate, on the other hand, has antimicrobial properties that combat bacteria and fungi that can cause dry mouth. The antiseptic properties of these constituents help soothe and calm the mouth, reducing discomfort and promoting a healthy oral environment.

By reducing inflammation and killing off bacteria, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help alleviate dry mouth symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan until it reaches 180°F (82°C).
  3. Add the fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the hot water and let it steep for 1 hour.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, and discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a distillation apparatus and heat it until the essential oil starts to separate and collect in a separate container.

7. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains a mix of active constituents, including alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to soothe and protect the mucous membranes in the mouth. The rosmarinic acid in Echinacea purpurea essential oils has been shown to reduce inflammation and fight off infections, making it useful for addressing dry mouth. The alkaloids present in the oil, such as echinacoside, have antimicrobial properties that can help to combat the bacteria that cause dry mouth.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off infections, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help to alleviate dry mouth symptoms and promote a healthy oral environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut oil or olive oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Echinacea flowers and carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well to mix.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the flowers to infuse into the oil.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the Echinacea essential oil in a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 2-3 drops of the oil in water to help with dry mouth.

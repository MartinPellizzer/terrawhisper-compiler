Dry Mouth and Medication: 10 Herbal Teas to Ease Symptoms

Medication users often experience dry mouth, also known as xerostomia, as a side effect of their treatment.

This can happen when certain medications reduce saliva production in the mouth. Dry mouth can be a real nuisance, causing discomfort, difficulties with speaking and eating, and even increasing the risk of tooth decay and gum disease. Medication users may find themselves constantly searching for ways to moisten their mouth, which can be frustrating and affect their daily lives. They may try to drink more water, but it's not always effective in relieving dry mouth. When we talk about dry mouth, we're not just talking about a mild discomfort - we're talking about a condition that can significantly impact a person's quality of life.

It's not just about the physical symptoms, but also the emotional toll of living with a chronic condition. Medication users often have to adjust their daily routines to accommodate their dry mouth, which can be time-consuming and inconvenient. They may need to take breaks to drink water or chew sugar-free gum, which can be annoying and disrupt their work or social activities. Herbal teas have been found to be helpful in treating dry mouth in medication users. These teas are made from plants that have natural moisturizing properties, which can help to stimulate saliva production and soothe a dry mouth.

By drinking herbal teas, medication users can find relief from the discomfort and difficulties associated with dry mouth.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, also known as licorice root tea, have been traditionally used to help with dry mouth.

The reason it's effective is due to its properties and constituents, particularly glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds help soothe and moisturize the mucous membranes in the mouth, reducing dryness and discomfort. When experiencing dry mouth, you can try making a tea by steeping dried licorice root in hot water for 5-7 minutes.

Start with a small amount, such as 1/2 teaspoon per cup, and adjust to taste. As you sip the tea, you may feel the soothing effects on your mouth, and your saliva production may increase, helping to reduce dryness. With regular consumption, you may notice improved oral comfort and a reduced risk of oral infections, such as thrush.

However, it's essential to note that glycyrrhiza glabra teas can interact with certain medications, so consult with a healthcare professional before making it a regular part of your oral care routine.

2. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita teas, also known as peppermint tea, can help with dry mouth due to its ability to stimulate saliva production.

This is mainly thanks to the presence of menthol, a compound that can activate the trigeminal nerve, which in turn increases saliva secretion. Additionally, peppermint tea contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory properties that may soothe the mouth and throat. To try peppermint tea for dry mouth, start by steeping a teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and let it cool down before drinking it.

You can also add honey or lemon to make it more palatable. To get the most out of peppermint tea, drink it regularly, ideally after meals or when you feel the need to stimulate saliva production. Some people may experience a slight burning sensation in their mouth or throat when drinking peppermint tea, but this is usually temporary and harmless. By drinking peppermint tea regularly, you may experience an increase in saliva production, which can help to moisturize your mouth and throat. This can lead to improved oral health, reduced risk of tooth decay, and a fresher breath.

However, if you have any underlying medical conditions or take medications that affect saliva production, it's essential to consult with your doctor before trying peppermint tea.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas have been used to help with dry mouth for centuries, and it's due to their unique properties and constituents.

These teas contain compounds like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds help reduce inflammation in the mouth and promote saliva production, which can help alleviate dry mouth symptoms. When you drink Echinacea purpurea tea, the alkylamides and other compounds work together to calm the mucous membranes in your mouth, reducing dryness and discomfort. If you're experiencing dry mouth, try drinking Echinacea purpurea tea as a warm beverage.

You can steep one teaspoon of dried Echinacea purpurea root in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink. You can also add honey or lemon to taste. Start with a small dose and gradually increase as needed. It's recommended to drink the tea 2-3 times a day, ideally before meals. As you drink the tea, you may start to notice an increase in saliva production and a decrease in dry mouth symptoms.

Your mouth may feel more comfortable, and you may be able to eat and drink more easily.

When you take action and drink ginger tea regularly, you may notice an improvement in your dry mouth symptoms. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent dry mouth from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent dry mouth from forming.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, contain properties that stimulate saliva production. This can help keep your mouth moist and prevent dryness.

Regularly sipping on herbal tea may also help reduce dry mouth symptoms, especially in people who experience them due to medication or other health conditions.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for dry mouth every day?

Herbal teas can help with dry mouth, but it's essential to check their ingredients.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and chamomile, are generally safe to drink daily in moderation. However, teas with licorice root or sage may interact with medications or worsen dry mouth symptoms.

Be cautious and monitor your body's response.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dry mouth?

Herbal teas can help with dry mouth, but it depends on the type of tea and how often you drink it.

Some teas, like slippery elm and marshmallow root, can start to show results within a few days.

Others, like peppermint and chamomile, might take a week or two to be noticed.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for dry mouth?

Drinking herbal tea for dry mouth can be most helpful in the morning.

This is because your body is most likely to be dehydrated after a night of sleep. Sipping herbal tea can help rehydrate and soothe your mouth.

It's also a great way to start your day with a refreshing and calming drink.

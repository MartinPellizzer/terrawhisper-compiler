Sleep Deprivation: Causes, Herbal Preparations, and Healthy Lifestyle

Sleep deprivation is a state of being awake for an extended period, usually exceeding 24 hours, which can lead to physical and mental health problems.

Dealing with sleep deprivation is crucial as it can cause fatigue, decreased productivity, mood swings, and impaired cognitive function. If left unaddressed, sleep deprivation can increase the risk of chronic diseases such as diabetes, high blood pressure, and cardiovascular disease. Therefore, it is essential to find natural remedies to help alleviate sleep deprivation. Herbal preparations are a popular choice for promoting relaxation and improving sleep quality. It can be consumed as an infusion or added to a warm bath to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality. Herbal preparations can be a safer alternative to prescription sleep aids, as they are generally free from side effects and can be used long-term.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedy, especially if you are taking medications or have underlying health conditions.

What Are The Main Causes Of Sleep Deprivation?

Sleep deprivation can be caused by various factors.

One major cause is a work schedule that is not suitable for getting enough sleep. Long working hours, night shifts, or irregular schedules can disrupt the body's natural sleep-wake cycle, making it difficult to get the required amount of sleep. Additionally, shift work, which involves working at night or on rotating shifts, can also lead to sleep deprivation due to the body's natural tendency to feel more alert during the day and sleepier at night. This mismatch between the body's natural rhythms and the work schedule can lead to chronic sleep deprivation. Another cause of sleep deprivation is sleep disorders.

These are medical conditions that affect the quality or quantity of sleep. Examples of sleep disorders include insomnia, sleep apnea, and restless leg syndrome. Insomnia, for instance, is a condition where a person has difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep, resulting in inadequate sleep. Sleep apnea is a condition where a person stops breathing for short periods during sleep, leading to fragmented sleep. These conditions can significantly reduce the quality and quantity of sleep, leading to sleep deprivation.

These causes of sleep deprivation can have serious consequences, including impaired cognitive function, mood disturbances, and increased risk of chronic diseases like diabetes and cardiovascular disease.

Below are the main causes of sleep deprivation.

  • Work Schedule: Working irregular hours or having an unpredictable schedule can cause sleep deprivation because it disrupts the body's internal clock, making it difficult to fall asleep and stay asleep at a consistent time.
  • Shift Work: Shift work is a cause of sleep deprivation because it disrupts the body's natural sleep-wake cycle, also known as the circadian rhythm, which is controlled by internal body clocks that regulate when we feel tired and when we feel awake.
  • Sleep Disorders: Sleep disorders, such as insomnia and sleep apnea, are a common cause of sleep deprivation because they disrupt the normal sleep-wake cycle, making it difficult for people to fall asleep, stay asleep, or get quality sleep.
  • Chronic Pain: Chronic pain causes sleep deprivation because the discomfort and pain disrupt the body's normal sleep patterns, making it difficult to fall asleep, stay asleep, and get quality rest.
  • Mental Health: Mental health issues like anxiety, depression, and trauma can cause people to lie awake at night, worrying or reliving their problems, making it difficult to fall asleep and get quality rest.
  • Stress: When stress builds up in the body, it releases hormones like adrenaline and cortisol, which can make it hard to fall asleep and stay asleep, leading to sleep deprivation.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Sleep Deprivation?

If you have sleep deprivation, it's best to avoid herbs that can make you drowsy or increase drowsiness.

Ginseng (Panax ginseng) is one such herb to avoid, as it can cause drowsiness, especially when taken in large doses. Another herb to avoid is Kava (Piper methysticum), which can cause drowsiness and interact with sleep medications.

Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) is also a herb to be cautious with, as it can cause drowsiness in some people, especially when taken in high doses. Additionally, Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) can make you feel drowsy, and it's best to avoid it if you're not getting enough sleep.

St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) is also a herb to be cautious with, as it can interact with sleep medications and make you feel drowsy.

10 Herbal Essential Oils For Alzheimer’S Disease

Herbal essential oils have been studied for their potential to help with Alzheimer's disease, a condition that affects memory and cognitive function.

Some herbs, like Ginkgo biloba, have been found to improve blood flow to the brain, which can help reduce memory loss. Ginkgo biloba has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to improve memory and cognitive function. Another herb, Rosmarinus officinalis, also known as rosemary, has been found to have a positive effect on memory. The essential oil from rosemary has been shown to improve cognitive function and memory in people with Alzheimer's disease. This is because rosemary contains compounds that can help increase blood flow to the brain and protect it from oxidative stress.

Turmeric, or Curcuma longa, is another herb that has been studied for its potential to help with Alzheimer's disease. Curcuma longa contains a compound called curcumin, which has been found to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Curcumin can help reduce inflammation in the brain and protect it from oxidative stress, which can contribute to the development of Alzheimer's disease. Herbal teas made from these herbs can be a useful way to relieve symptoms of Alzheimer's disease. Drinking these teas regularly may help improve memory and cognitive function. While they may not cure the disease, they can help manage its symptoms and improve quality of life.

They can also be used in conjunction with other treatments to help slow down the progression of the disease.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids and terpenoids, particularly bilobalide and ginkgolides.

These compounds help improve blood flow to the brain, which is essential for cognitive function and memory. The antioxidants in ginkgo biloba essential oils, such as flavonoids, neutralize free radicals that damage brain cells, reducing oxidative stress and inflammation. The flavonoids also have neuroprotective properties that may help repair damaged brain cells and improve communication between neurons.

By improving blood flow and reducing oxidative stress, ginkgo biloba essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of Alzheimer's disease, such as memory loss and cognitive decline.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Heat a double boiler or a saucepan with water, then reduce heat to low. Place the Ginkgo biloba leaves in a heat-resistant glass bowl.
  3. Steep the Ginkgo biloba leaves in the carrier oil for 2-3 hours or overnight. Stir occasionally.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Ginkgo biloba essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops per day in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains a high concentration of camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the brain associated with Alzheimer's disease. The antioxidant properties of borneol and bornyl acetate neutralize free radicals that damage brain cells, while camphor's anti-inflammatory properties reduce inflammation and swelling in the brain. The essential oils' cognitive-boosting properties are also thought to be due to the stimulating effect of borneol on the brain's neurotransmitters, improving memory and cognitive function.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, and promoting cognitive function, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils may help to alleviate symptoms of Alzheimer's disease.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and flowers. Choose organic and pesticide-free plants.
  2. Use a mortar and pestle to gently crush the leaves and flowers. You should get about 1/4 cup of crushed plant material.
  3. Combine the crushed plant material with 2 cups of water in a saucepan. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool for 10 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a glass bottle and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. This will allow the mixture to steep and the essential oil to separate from the water. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid again and discard the remaining water. You now have your Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil.

3. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains bioactive constituents such as curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the brain, which are associated with Alzheimer's disease. Curcumin also has the ability to cross the blood-brain barrier, allowing it to directly interact with and modify the activity of enzymes and proteins involved in the disease's progression. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of curcuma longa essential oils may help prevent the formation of amyloid plaques and neurofibrillary tangles, which are hallmarks of Alzheimer's disease.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, curcuma longa essential oils may help slow down the progression of Alzheimer's disease and improve cognitive function.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Curcuma longa rhizomes (Turmeric roots) and 4 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil)
  2. Dry the Curcuma longa rhizomes in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 2 hours to remove excess moisture
  3. Grind the dried rhizomes into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle
  4. Mix 1 cup of the powder with 4 cups of carrier oil in a clean glass bottle, then seal and shake well
  5. Leave the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the bottle every day to help the oil infuse

4. Centella asiatica

Centella asiatica essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, particularly asiaticoside, madecassoside, and madecassic acid.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help protect brain cells from damage. The anti-inflammatory properties reduce inflammation in the brain, which is associated with the progression of Alzheimer's disease. Madecassoside and madecassic acid also have neuroprotective effects, which help to prevent the death of brain cells and improve memory and cognitive function.

By reducing oxidative stress and inflammation, Centella asiatica essential oils may help slow down the progression of Alzheimer's disease.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Centella asiatica leaves, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Wash the Centella asiatica leaves with 1 cup of water, and then gently pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Combine the dried leaves with 1 cup of carrier oil in the glass jar, and seal it tightly.
  4. Place the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container, and discard the solids.

5. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains linalool, a major bioactive constituent that provides its therapeutic benefits.

Linalool has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the brain associated with Alzheimer's disease. The oil also contains rosmarinic acid, which has neuroprotective properties that help preserve brain cells and improve cognitive function. Melissa officinalis essential oils' antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties may also help reduce the accumulation of beta-amyloid plaques, a hallmark of Alzheimer's disease.

Regular use of Melissa officinalis essential oils may also improve memory and cognitive function by promoting relaxation and reducing stress.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over low heat, then add the Melissa flowers.
  3. Let the mixture simmer for 20-30 minutes, then remove from heat.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 4-6 hours, then strain the liquid through a cheesecloth.
  5. Dilute the essential oil with a carrier oil, such as jojoba oil, in a 1:10 ratio, then store in a dark glass bottle.

6. Bacopa monnieri

Bacopa monnieri essential oils contains bacosides, which are a group of active constituents that help improve memory and cognitive function.

These bacosides have been found to increase the levels of a protein called brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF), which is essential for brain cell growth and survival. Bacopa monnieri essential oils also contain other compounds like baicalein and baicalin, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help protect the brain from damage. By reducing oxidative stress and inflammation, Bacopa monnieri essential oils may help slow down the progression of Alzheimer's disease.

Regular use of Bacopa monnieri essential oils may also improve memory and cognitive function in individuals with Alzheimer's disease.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Bacopa monnieri leaves and flowers. Clean and dry the area where you will be working.
  2. Use a coffee grinder or spice grinder to crush the Bacopa monnieri into a fine powder.
  3. Add 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the powder and mix well. Let it sit for 2-3 days in a cool dark place.
  4. After 2-3 days, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 5-7 drops of the oil per day, mixed with a carrier oil, for potential benefits.

7. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called curcumin, a powerful bioactive constituent that has been shown to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the brain.

This is important because Alzheimer's disease is characterized by inflammation and damage to brain cells, and curcumin has been found to have neuroprotective effects by blocking the formation of amyloid plaques, a hallmark of the disease. Gingerols and shogaols, other bioactive compounds found in Zingiber officinale essential oils, have also been found to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help protect brain cells from damage. Additionally, the essential oils have been shown to improve memory and cognitive function by increasing the production of a protein called brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF), which is essential for the growth and survival of brain cells.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, and promoting the growth of brain cells, Zingiber officinale essential oils may help slow the progression of Alzheimer's disease.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale (Ginger) roots and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Wash the ginger roots and peel them. Chop the ginger into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped ginger (1 cup) and carrier oil (1 cup) in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat (100°F - 150°F) for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  5. Allow the oil to cool and mature for 2-3 weeks before using it. Shake the bottle daily to mix the oil.

8. Crocus sativus

Crocus sativus essential oils contains crocin and safranal, which are bioactive constituents that have been studied for their effects on Alzheimer's disease.

These compounds have antioxidant properties, which help protect the brain from oxidative stress and inflammation. Crocin has also been shown to cross the blood-brain barrier, allowing it to reach the areas of the brain affected by Alzheimer's disease. Safranal has been found to inhibit the formation of amyloid plaques, which are a hallmark of the disease.

By reducing oxidative stress and inhibiting amyloid plaque formation, Crocus sativus essential oils may help slow the progression of Alzheimer's disease.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Crocus sativus flowers and 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Fill a clean glass jar with 1 cup of the dried flowers and add 2 cups of carrier oil. Stir well.
  3. Place the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This is called 'infusion'.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the essential oil to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-10 drops of the oil in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for skin use.

9. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng essential oils contains bioactive constituents like ginsenosides and ginsenoidic acid, which have been shown to have a positive effect on Alzheimer's disease.

The ginsenosides in these oils have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help protect brain cells from damage and reduce inflammation in the brain. Ginsenoidic acid has also been found to improve cognitive function by increasing the production of a protein called brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF), which is essential for the growth and survival of brain cells. Panax ginseng essential oils also contain another compound called ginsenoside Rg1, which has been shown to improve memory and learning by increasing the activity of acetylcholinesterase, an enzyme that breaks down a neurotransmitter called acetylcholine.

By reducing oxidative stress and inflammation, and improving cognitive function, Panax ginseng essential oils may be a useful natural treatment for Alzheimer's disease.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Panax ginseng roots and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Grind the dried Panax ginseng roots into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Combine the ground Panax ginseng powder with 2 cups of carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  4. Place the jar in a cool, dark place and let it steep for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the oil to infuse.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the Panax ginseng essential oil in a cool, dark place.

10. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid and valeranone, two potent bioactive constituents.

These compounds have a sedative effect on the nervous system and can help reduce anxiety and stress, which are common symptoms associated with Alzheimer's disease. Valerenic acid also has a neuroprotective effect, which helps to shield brain cells from damage and promotes their growth. The anxiolytic and sedative properties of valerian essential oil can help improve sleep quality, which is often disrupted in people with Alzheimer's disease.

By reducing anxiety and promoting better sleep, valerian essential oil may help alleviate some of the symptoms associated with Alzheimer's disease.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Valeriana officinalis roots, 1 cup of water, a saucepan, and a glass jar.
  2. Chop the roots into small pieces and add them to the saucepan with 1 cup of water.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 1-2 hours, or until the liquid has reduced to 1/4 cup.
  4. Strain the liquid into a glass jar through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter to remove the roots.
  5. Use a steam distiller or a DIY still to separate the essential oil from the water, then store the oil in a dark glass bottle.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent alzheimer’s disease from forming?

Research suggests that drinking herbal tea may help prevent Alzheimer's disease.

Certain compounds in tea, such as catechins and flavonoids, have antioxidant properties that may protect the brain from damage.

These compounds can help reduce inflammation and improve blood flow to the brain, potentially reducing the risk of developing Alzheimer's disease over time.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for alzheimer’s disease every day?

Herbal teas may help slow Alzheimer's progression, but their safety for daily consumption is unclear.

Some herbs, like ginkgo biloba, may interact with medications. Others, like rosemary, could be beneficial in small amounts.

More research is needed to understand the effects of long-term herbal tea consumption on people with Alzheimer's disease.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in alzheimer’s disease?

Research suggests that herbal teas may help slow down Alzheimer's disease progression.

Some studies show improvement in memory and cognitive function within 3-6 months of regular consumption.

However, individual results may vary, and more research is needed to confirm the effectiveness of herbal teas in treating Alzheimer's disease.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for alzheimer’s disease?

Drinking herbal tea for Alzheimer's disease is most effective in the morning.

This is because it gives you a boost of antioxidants and brain-protecting compounds that can help reduce cognitive decline throughout the day.

Sipping a cup of herbal tea as you wake up can get your day started on a healthy note.

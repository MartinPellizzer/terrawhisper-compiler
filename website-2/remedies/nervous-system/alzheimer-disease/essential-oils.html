What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have alzheimer’s disease?

If you have Alzheimer's disease, it's essential to be cautious with some herbal essential oils that could potentially worsen symptoms or interact with medications.

For example, 'Citrus aurantium' (also known as bitter orange) and 'Syzygium aromaticum' (clove) contain compounds that can cause seizures, convulsions, and other severe reactions in people with Alzheimer's. 'Eucalyptus globulus' oil, while commonly used for respiratory issues, can cause central nervous system problems, including seizures, in people with pre-existing neurological conditions like Alzheimer's.

'Thymus vulgaris' (thyme) oil may also cause problems, as it can interact with certain medications used to treat Alzheimer's, such as cholinesterase inhibitors. Additionally, 'Hyssopus officinalis' (hyssop) oil can cause seizures and other neurological problems in people with Alzheimer's.

Lastly, 'Lavandula angustifolia' (lavender) oil can cause drowsiness, which might be a problem if you're taking medications that already cause drowsiness, or if you have difficulty getting out of bed due to Alzheimer's symptoms.

Helping family members with alzheimer’s disease: 11 herbal teas

Living with a family member who has Alzheimer's disease can be very challenging.

Alzheimer's is a type of dementia that affects a person's memory and thinking skills. It's often associated with aging, but sometimes it can start earlier in life. When someone has Alzheimer's, their brain cells die and can't function properly, leading to confusion, forgetfulness, and mood changes. This can cause a lot of stress and frustration for family members who have to care for them. Daily tasks become difficult, and simple things like getting dressed or taking medication can be a struggle. Family members may also feel overwhelmed, lonely, and isolated. They may have to give up their jobs or social lives to take care of their loved one, leading to a loss of identity and purpose.

Family members who live with someone who has Alzheimer's may also experience physical and emotional strain. They may have to deal with constant requests for help, which can be exhausting. They may also have to manage their loved one's behavior, which can be unpredictable and challenging. The emotional toll of caring for someone with Alzheimer's can be significant, leading to anxiety, depression, and burnout. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to promote relaxation, reduce stress, and improve mood. Some herbal teas have been shown to have a positive effect on people who care for those with Alzheimer's. These teas contain compounds that can help reduce anxiety and depression, improve sleep quality, and boost the immune system.

By drinking these teas, family members may feel more relaxed and better equipped to handle the challenges of caring for someone with Alzheimer's.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas have been studied for their potential benefits in managing Alzheimer's disease.

The leaves of the Ginkgo biloba tree contain flavonoids, terpenoids, and bilobalide, which are believed to improve blood flow to the brain and reduce inflammation. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant properties that help protect brain cells from damage caused by free radicals. The terpenoids, such as ginkgolides A and B, may also help to open up blood vessels and improve cognitive function. If you're considering trying Ginkgo biloba teas for Alzheimer's disease, start by speaking with your doctor or healthcare provider. They can help you determine the best course of action and ensure that it won't interact with any medications you're currently taking.

Look for a high-quality tea that contains a standardized extract of Ginkgo biloba, with a minimum of 24% flavone glycosides and 6% terpene lactones. Follow the recommended dosage, which is typically 2-4 cups of tea per day. You may also want to consider combining Ginkgo biloba teas with other natural therapies, such as omega-3 fatty acids and vitamin D, under the guidance of a healthcare professional. Taking Ginkgo biloba teas may lead to improvements in cognitive function, such as better memory and concentration. You may also experience a reduction in symptoms of anxiety and depression, which are common in people with Alzheimer's disease. However, it's essential to be patient and consistent, as the effects of Ginkgo biloba teas may take several weeks or months to become apparent.

Additionally, keep in mind that Ginkgo biloba teas are not a substitute for medical treatment, and you should continue to work with your healthcare provider to develop a comprehensive plan for managing your condition.

Sleeping Better as Students: 10 Herbal Remedies to the Rescue

Students often have to deal with a lot of stress and pressure to perform well in school.

This can be due to heavy course loads, exams, and assignments, which can make it hard for them to get enough sleep. As a result, many students suffer from sleep deprivation, which can affect their daily lives in many ways. They may feel tired, grumpy, and irritable, and may have trouble concentrating in class, which can impact their grades. Sleep deprivation can also affect their mood, making them more anxious and depressed. It can even affect their physical health, making them more prone to illnesses like the flu. When students don't get enough sleep, they may also have trouble with their relationships and daily routines.

They may feel like they're constantly running on a treadmill, trying to keep up with their schoolwork, social life, and personal responsibilities. They may have trouble getting out of bed in the morning, and may feel like they're missing out on fun activities and experiences. To make matters worse, sleep deprivation can also affect students' cognitive function, making it harder for them to learn and remember new information. This can create a vicious cycle, where students feel like they're not doing well in school, which makes them feel more stressed and anxious, which makes it even harder for them to get enough sleep. Herbal essential oils can be a helpful tool in treating sleep deprivation in students. These oils are derived from plants and have been used for centuries to promote relaxation and improve sleep. They can be inhaled through a diffuser or applied topically to the skin, and can help students relax and unwind after a long day.

By promoting relaxation and reducing stress and anxiety, herbal essential oils can help students get the restful sleep they need to perform well in school and maintain their overall health and well-being.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils have been used for centuries to help with sleep deprivation.

The properties in this oil, such as valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, act as a natural sedative, helping to calm the mind and body, making it easier to fall asleep. The constituents in this oil, including valepotriates and valerenal, also work to reduce anxiety and stress levels, creating a peaceful state necessary for a good night's sleep.

To use Valeriana officinalis essential oils for sleep deprivation, you can add a few drops to your diffuser or inhale it directly from a cloth or handkerchief before bedtime. You can also mix it with a carrier oil and apply it to your skin, but make sure to dilute it properly. When inhaled, the likely outcome is a calming effect that will help you relax and fall asleep faster.

With regular use, you may experience improved sleep quality and reduced stress levels, leading to better rest and recovery.

To get the best results, try using the essential oil 30 minutes to 1 hour before bedtime, and avoid using it during the day when you need to be alert.

By incorporating Lavandula angustifolia essential oils into your bedtime routine, you can expect to feel more relaxed and calm, leading to a better night's sleep and improved sleep quality.

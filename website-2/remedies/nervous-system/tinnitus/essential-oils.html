1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains boiactive constituents like linalool and linalyl acetate.

These compounds have a sedative and anti-anxiety effect, which can help reduce stress and promote relaxation, a common contributor to tinnitus. Lavender oil also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate ear inflammation and reduce discomfort. The calming properties of linalool and linalyl acetate can also help regulate the nervous system and reduce ringing in the ears.

Using Lavandula angustifolia essential oils through inhalation or massage may help alleviate tinnitus symptoms by promoting relaxation and reducing stress.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water to 212°F (100°C) and then reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Add the Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the saucepan and let them steep for 1 hour.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass container.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 hours to allow the essential oil to separate from the water and then carefully pour it off the top.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the ears.

The oil also contains zingerone, a compound that has antioxidant properties that protect the ear from damage caused by free radicals. The anti-inflammatory properties of gingerol and zingerone help reduce inflammation in the ear, which is often a contributor to tinnitus. Additionally, the essential oil contains a compound called shogaol, which has analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce pain and discomfort in the ear.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the ear from damage, Zingiber officinale essential oils may help alleviate tinnitus symptoms.

  1. Get 1 cup of Zingiber officinale roots and wash them with water.
  2. Dry the roots in a warm place for 3-4 days. Then, grind them into a fine powder using a mortar and pestle.
  3. Add 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the powder in a bowl.
  4. Mix well and let it sit for 2-3 weeks in a dark place, shaking the bowl every day. This is called 'infusion'.
  5. After the infusion, strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container. Discard the solids and use the essential oil for tinnitus treatment.

3. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and geraniol as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce stress and anxiety, common triggers for tinnitus. The oil's sedative and calming effects also promote relaxation, which can decrease the likelihood of tinnitus episodes. Melissa officinalis essential oils' antiseptic and antimicrobial properties may help combat infections that can contribute to tinnitus.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Melissa officinalis essential oils can help alleviate tinnitus symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil, such as jojoba oil. Make sure the flowers are fresh and organic.
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. The ratio is 1:1.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This is called maceration.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter. Discard the solids and transfer the liquid to a clean glass bottle.
  5. Label the bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the essential oil in a diffuser or apply it to the skin after diluting with a carrier oil.

4. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains compounds like flavonoids, bilobalide, and ginkgolides, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help protect the blood vessels in the ear from damage and reduce inflammation, which can contribute to tinnitus. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba essential oils also have a vasoactive effect, improving blood flow to the ears and reducing the strain on the auditory system. Ginkgolides have a specific effect on the neurotransmitters involved in hearing and tinnitus, potentially reducing the severity of tinnitus symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and improving blood flow, Ginkgo biloba essential oils may help alleviate tinnitus symptoms and improve hearing.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Ginkgo biloba leaves, a clean glass jar, and a carrier oil like jojoba oil.
  2. Chop the Ginkgo biloba leaves and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour 2 cups of jojoba oil over the leaves and close the jar.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container, then discard the solids.

5. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains cineole, a key bioactive constituent that helps alleviate tinnitus symptoms.

Cineole has anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce inflammation in the ear and surrounding tissues, a common cause of tinnitus. Eucalyptus globulus also contains limonene, a compound that has antioxidant properties, which help protect the ear from damage and promote healing. The decongestant properties of eucalyptus globulus help relieve congestion in the ears, reducing the pressure and discomfort associated with tinnitus.

By reducing inflammation, promoting healing, and relieving congestion, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help alleviate tinnitus symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Add 1 cup of water to a separate pot and bring it to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Add the boiling water to the large pot with the Eucalyptus leaves. Let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the liquid and discard the leaves. Transfer the liquid to a distillation apparatus.
  5. Collect the distilled essential oil in a glass container. You should have around 1/4 cup of Eucalyptus globulus essential oil.

6. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid, isovalerene, and valepotriates, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The sedative and calming effects of valerenic acid can help reduce stress and anxiety, common triggers for tinnitus. Isovalerene, a natural relaxant, also helps alleviate tension and promotes a restful state. Valepotriates, a class of iridoid glycosides, exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help alleviate some of the underlying causes of tinnitus.

By reducing stress, promoting relaxation, and addressing underlying inflammation, Valeriana officinalis essential oils may provide relief from tinnitus symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Valeriana officinalis roots and leaves. Clean and dry them.
  2. Use a steam distillation kit to heat 2 cups of water. Attach the kit to a condenser.
  3. Add the cleaned Valeriana roots and leaves to the steam distillation kit. Collect the steam.
  4. The steam will pass through the Valeriana mixture and be cooled in the condenser. Collect the condensed steam.
  5. Filter the condensed steam through a paper filter to get the essential oil. Store it in a dark glass bottle.

7. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains carnosic acid, carnosol, and rosmarinic acid as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the ear and promote healthy blood flow. The antioxidant properties of rosmarinic acid may also help protect the inner ear from damage caused by free radicals. By reducing inflammation and promoting healthy blood flow, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils may help alleviate tinnitus symptoms by improving the overall health of the ear.

Regular use of this essential oil may also help calm the nervous system and reduce stress, which can contribute to tinnitus.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water to 100°F (38°C) and add the fresh leaves. Reduce heat to low.
  3. Steep the leaves for 2-3 hours. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into a container.
  4. Add 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil) to the liquid and stir well.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 24 hours. Strain and discard the solids. Your Rosmarinus officinalis essential oil is now ready to use.

What herbal creams you should avoid if you have one side headache?

If you have a one-sided headache, it's best to avoid herbal creams containing Feverfew (Tanacetum parthenium), Butterbur (Petasites hybridus), or Willow bark (Salix alba).

This is because these herbs can increase bleeding or interact with pain medications, making your headache worse. Feverfew, for example, can slow down blood clotting, which might be a problem if you have a cut or bruise.

Butterbur can also thin your blood and interact with blood thinners, such as aspirin or warfarin. Willow bark contains salicin, a compound similar to aspirin, which can increase the risk of bleeding if taken in high doses or combined with other pain medications.

Using these herbal creams on one side of your head can also increase the risk of uneven bleeding or irritation.

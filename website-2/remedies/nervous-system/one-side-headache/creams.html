1. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita creams contains menthol, menthone, and limonene as its key bioactive constituents.

These compounds have analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and vasodilatory properties, which help to relieve one-sided headaches. Menthol, in particular, has a cooling effect that can help to numb the pain and reduce inflammation in the affected area. Menthone and limonene also have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling and ease pain in the muscles and blood vessels.

By applying Mentha x piperita creams to the affected area, the bioactive compounds can work together to help alleviate one-sided headache symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, and 10 drops of Mentha x piperita essential oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool for 5-7 minutes. Then, add 10 drops of Mentha x piperita essential oil and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a tin or a container and let it cool and harden completely before using it to apply topically to the affected area.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains active compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and ease pain in the nerves and blood vessels, which can cause headaches. The warming effect of ginger also helps relax tense muscles and improve blood flow, which can help alleviate pain and discomfort on one side of the head. Additionally, the antioxidants in Zingiber officinale creams, such as gingerols and shogaols, help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the body, which can contribute to headaches.

By applying Zingiber officinale creams to the affected area, you may experience relief from the pain and discomfort of a one-sided headache.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut oil, 1 cup of shea butter, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler over low heat.
  3. Add the beeswax to the melted oil mixture and stir until fully dissolved.
  4. Add 1 teaspoon of Zingiber officinale essential oil to the mixture and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container, let it cool and solidify. Use a small amount on the affected area for relief.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties can help reduce pain and inflammation on the affected side of the head, providing relief from headache. The calming and soothing effects of lavender oil can also help relax tense muscles and improve blood flow to the area. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of linalool may help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the body, which can contribute to headache.

By applying Lavandula angustifolia creams to the affected area, you may experience a reduction in headache symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of distilled water, 1 tablespoon of lavender oil, 1 tablespoon of beeswax, and 1/2 cup of shea butter.
  2. Melt the beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Add the shea butter and stir until melted and smooth. Remove from heat.
  4. Add the lavender oil and distilled water to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, use as a cream to help relieve one side headache.

4. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium creams contains several active constituents, including sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and essential oils.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help alleviate headache symptoms. The sesquiterpene lactones, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the production of prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that can cause blood vessels to constrict and lead to headaches. The flavonoids in Achillea millefolium creams have antioxidant properties, which can help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the body.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing blood vessels, Achillea millefolium creams may help alleviate one-sided headaches.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Achillea millefolium flowers and 2 cups of coconut oil.
  2. Combine the dried flowers and coconut oil in a saucepan and heat over low heat for 2-3 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of beeswax and 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil to the mixture and stir until melted.
  5. Pour the mixture into a glass jar and let it cool before applying a small amount to the affected area.

5. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains compounds like eucalyptol, which is a potent natural decongestant.

Eucalyptol helps reduce swelling and ease congestion in the nasal passages, which can contribute to headache pain. The anti-inflammatory properties of eucalyptol and other constituents like alpha-pinene and beta-pinene also help alleviate pain and discomfort. By reducing inflammation and congestion, eucalyptus globulus creams can provide relief from one-sided headaches caused by sinus pressure.

Its decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties make it a useful remedy for headache sufferers.

  1. Gather 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of eucalyptus globulus essential oil, and a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in the double boiler or heat-proof bowl over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Once melted, remove the mixture from heat and add the eucalyptus globulus essential oil. Stir well to combine.
  4. Pour the mixture into a small tin or container and let it cool and solidify at room temperature.
  5. Once solid, apply a small amount of the cream to the side of the head where the headache is located and massage gently.

6. Foeniculum vulgare

7. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, borneol, and linalool as active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which help to reduce pain and swelling associated with a one-sided headache. Rosmarinic acid, in particular, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, which contribute to pain and inflammation. Borneol, on the other hand, has a sedative effect, which can help to relax tense muscles and improve blood flow to the affected area.

By applying Salvia officinalis creams topically, the active constituents can penetrate the skin and provide localized relief from pain and discomfort.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Salvia officinalis leaves, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Steep the Salvia officinalis leaves in 2 cups of carrier oil (such as coconut or olive oil) for 2-3 weeks, shaking the bottle daily.
  3. Strain the oil mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, and discard the leaves.
  4. In a separate bowl, melt the beeswax and shea butter in a double boiler or in the microwave in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval.
  5. Combine the melted beeswax mixture with the strained oil mixture, and add vitamin E oil. Stir well and pour the mixture into a container to cool and solidify.

8. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis creams contains active constituents like linalool and linalyl acetate.

The antispasmodic effect of linalool, in particular, can help to relax the muscles that cause tension headaches.

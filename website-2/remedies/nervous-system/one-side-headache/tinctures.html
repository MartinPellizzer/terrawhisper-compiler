1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains gingerols and shogaols as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce pain and swelling associated with headaches. Gingerols and shogaols also have natural analgesic properties, which can help ease pain on one side of the head. They work by blocking the production of pain-causing chemicals in the body and by increasing blood flow to the affected area.

By reducing inflammation and pain, Zingiber officinale tinctures can provide relief from one-sided headaches.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger roots, a glass jar, a cup of vodka or glycerin, and a cheesecloth.
  2. Chop the ginger roots into small pieces and fill the glass jar about 2/3 full.
  3. Pour the vodka or glycerin over the ginger, making sure the roots are completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through the cheesecloth into another container, and discard the ginger roots. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

2. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce swelling and inflammation in blood vessels that may be causing the headache. The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have a relaxing effect on blood vessels, which can help ease the pain and discomfort associated with one-sided headaches. The terpenoids, such as bilobol and ginkgolides, also have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce the severity of the headache.

By using Ginkgo biloba tinctures, you may be able to alleviate the symptoms of one-sided headaches by reducing inflammation and promoting blood vessel relaxation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Ginkgo biloba leaves, 2 cups of 80-proof vodka, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Chop the Ginkgo biloba leaves into small pieces and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour the vodka over the leaves, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking it every day.
  5. Strain the liquid and discard the leaves. Use the Ginkgo biloba tincture immediately or store it in a cool, dark place for up to 6 months.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

The carminative properties of the tincture help to reduce gas and alleviate pressure in the head and neck, which can contribute to one-sided headaches.

By relaxing the muscles, reducing inflammation, and calming the nervous system, Foeniculum vulgare tinctures can provide relief from one-sided headaches.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Foeniculum vulgare leaves and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka.

  3. Pour the vodka over the chopped leaves, making sure they are completely covered.

8. Cinchona officinalis

The tannins in Cinchona officinalis also help reduce swelling and ease tension, which can contribute to one-sided headaches.

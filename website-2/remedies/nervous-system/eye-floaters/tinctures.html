Seniors and Eye Floaters: 9 Herbal Tinctures to Try

As we age, the vitreous gel in our eyes can start to break down, causing tiny pieces of debris to float in our vision.

This is what we see as eye floaters. For seniors, eye floaters can be more than just an annoying distraction - they can affect their daily lives. Imagine trying to read a book or watch your favorite TV show, but small specks keep getting in the way. Eye floaters can make it difficult to perform tasks that require good vision, like driving or cooking. They can also cause eye strain and headaches. Some seniors may even feel anxious or depressed because of their loss of independence due to impaired vision.

Eye floaters can be caused by age-related wear and tear on the eyes, as well as other factors like diabetes, high blood pressure, and eye injury. While some eye floaters may not need treatment, others may require surgery to remove them. However, surgery is not always an option, and some seniors may be looking for alternative ways to manage their eye floaters. This is where herbal tinctures come in. These liquid extracts from plants have been used for centuries to promote eye health and reduce inflammation. They can help to improve blood flow to the eyes, reduce the appearance of eye floaters, and even protect the eyes from further damage.

By using herbal tinctures, seniors may be able to manage their eye floaters and improve their overall quality of life.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures have been used to help alleviate the appearance of eye floaters, which are small, dark spots that can appear in your field of vision.

The tincture's benefits are due to its high content of flavonoids and terpenoids, which are plant compounds that can improve blood flow to the eyes and reduce inflammation. Bilobalide, a key constituent of Ginkgo biloba, has been shown to have antioxidant properties that can protect the delicate tissues in the eyes from damage. By taking Ginkgo biloba tinctures, you may experience reduced eye floaters due to improved circulation and reduced inflammation in the retina and vitreous gel.

To try Ginkgo biloba tinctures for eye floaters, start by taking 20-30 drops of the tincture, two to three times a day, in a glass of water. You can also consider consulting with an eye care professional or a healthcare practitioner before starting any new supplement regimen. As you take the tincture, you may notice a reduction in the number of eye floaters you see, especially after a few weeks of consistent use.

However, it's essential to note that Ginkgo biloba tinctures may not completely eliminate eye floaters, and more research is needed to confirm its effectiveness for this condition.

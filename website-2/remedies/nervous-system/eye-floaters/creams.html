What herbal creams you should avoid if you have eye floaters?

If you have eye floaters, there are some herbal creams you should avoid.

One such herb is St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum). It can interact with other medications, including those used to treat eye conditions, which could worsen your symptoms. Another herb to avoid is Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens), which may slow blood clotting and increase the risk of bleeding.

This is a concern if you have eye floaters caused by retinal tears or detachment, as excessive bleeding can lead to vision loss. Also, avoid using creams with Feverfew (Tanacetum parthenium), as it can cause blood thinning and may worsen eye bleeding. Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis) should also be avoided, as it may interact with other medications and increase the risk of bleeding.

Finally, avoid Valerian root (Valeriana officinalis) creams, as they can cause drowsiness and make it difficult to monitor your eye health, which is essential when managing eye floaters.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains bioactive constituents like triterpenoids, saponins, and flavonoids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which help soothe and calm irritated eyes. The triterpenoids, in particular, have been shown to reduce swelling and promote healing in damaged tissues. The antiseptic properties of calendula's flavonoids and saponins help to prevent infection and promote a healthy environment for the eyes to recover.

By reducing inflammation and preventing infection, calendula officinalis tinctures can help to alleviate sore eyes and promote a speedy recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka.
  2. Combine the Calendula flowers and vodka in a clean glass jar. Stir well and make sure the flowers are fully covered.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will help the flowers infuse their properties into the vodka.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Use the Calendula tincture immediately or store it in a cool, dark place for up to 6 months. To use, soak a cotton ball with the tincture and apply it to your sore eyes.

2. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale tinctures contains a concentration of bioactive constituents, including allantoin, mucilage, and rosmarinic acid.

Allantoin helps to reduce inflammation and promote healing, which is beneficial for sore eyes caused by irritation or injury. Mucilage, a thick, protective substance, soothes and shields the eyes from further irritation, providing a layer of comfort. Rosmarinic acid has anti-inflammatory properties, which helps to calm the eyes and reduce swelling.

By harnessing these properties, Symphytum officinale tinctures can provide relief from sore eyes, promoting healing and comfort.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Symphytum officinale leaves and flowers.
  2. Combine the plant material with 2 cups of 80% ethanol in a clean glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the plant material.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 5-10 drops in the affected eye 2-3 times a day.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains alkylamides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and redness in sore eyes. Alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the release of pro-inflammatory chemicals, which contribute to eye irritation. Flavonoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, have antioxidant properties that help protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

By reducing inflammation and protecting the eyes from oxidative stress, Echinacea purpurea tinctures can help alleviate sore eyes and promote eye health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers, leaves, and stems. Clean and dry them.
  2. Combine 1 cup of the dried Echinacea purpurea plant material with 2 cups of 80% ethanol in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the tincture to infuse.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Dilute the tincture with water to 10% ethanol (1 part tincture to 9 parts water). Use a dropper to apply 2-3 drops of the tincture to the affected eye, 2-3 times a day.

4. Euphrasia officinalis

Euphrasia officinalis tinctures contains iridoid glycosides, specifically echinacoside, and phenylethanoid glycosides like echinulin.

These compounds help reduce inflammation and swelling in the eyes. The flavonoids, including quercetin and kaempferol, have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that soothe and protect the eyes. Euphrasia officinalis also contains terpenoids like euphroside, which has a calming effect on the eyes and helps to reduce redness.

By using Euphrasia officinalis tinctures, you can help alleviate sore and irritated eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Euphrasia officinalis flowers and 1 cup of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass container.
  2. Fill a clean glass jar with the Euphrasia officinalis flowers, leaving 1 inch of space at the top.
  3. Pour the 1 cup of vodka over the flowers, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place and let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container, discarding the solids.

5. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum tinctures contains flavonoids like quercetin, kaempferol, and rutin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce swelling and irritation in sore eyes. The tannins in Hypericum perforatum tinctures have astringent properties, making them effective in reducing eye discharge and inflammation. The bioflavonoids also help improve blood circulation, which can promote healing and reduce eye strain.

The combination of these active constituents makes Hypericum perforatum tinctures a potential remedy for sore eyes.

  5. Label the jar and store the Hypericum perforatum tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 5-10 drops in water for sore eyes.

6. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contains flavonoids, bilobalide, and ginkgolides.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling in the eyes. The flavonoids also improve blood flow to the eyes, allowing for faster healing and soothing of sore eyes. Bilobalide and ginkgolides have antioxidant properties, which protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

As a result, Ginkgo biloba tinctures can help alleviate sore eyes by reducing inflammation and promoting healing.

  5. Strain the liquid and discard the Ginkgo biloba leaves. Use the tincture by placing a few drops in your eye, 2-3 times a day.

Aloe barbadensis tinctures contains several bioactive constituents, including aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

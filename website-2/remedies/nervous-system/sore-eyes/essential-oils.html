7 Herbal Essential Oils For Sore Eyes

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils contains triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce swelling and repair damaged tissues in the eyes. The flavonoids in Calendula officinalis essential oils, such as quercetin and kaempferol, have potent antioxidant activity that protects the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. The triterpenoids, including calendulin and oleanolic acid, have anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce redness and swelling in the eyes.

By applying Calendula officinalis essential oils to the affected area, the bioactive constituents can help to promote healing and reduce discomfort in sore eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers, a clean glass jar with a lid, and a carrier oil like coconut oil.
  2. Combine 1 cup of Calendula flowers with 2 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a new glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the Calendula essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid, out of direct sunlight.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains active constituents like eucalyptol and alpha-pinene.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and redness in the eyes. Eucalyptol also has a decongestant effect, which can clear out mucus and debris that may be causing irritation. Additionally, alpha-pinene has antiseptic properties that help protect the eyes from infection.

By applying eucalyptus globulus essential oils to the skin around the eyes, these active constituents can work together to provide relief from sore eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a pot.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and add the Eucalyptus leaves.
  3. Steep the leaves for 10 minutes, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a bowl and let it cool. Add 1 tablespoon of vodka to help preserve the oil.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 days, then strain and discard the solids. Your Eucalyptus globulus essential oil is now ready to use.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling and prevent infection in the eyes. The soothing and calming effects of linalool and linalyl acetate can also help to relieve redness and irritation in sore eyes. Additionally, the oil's antiseptic properties help to protect the eyes from further irritation and infection.

When applied topically, the essential oil's soothing and antiseptic properties can help to promote healing and comfort in sore eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil)
  2. Combine flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar, making sure flowers are fully covered
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids
  5. Store the resulting essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid, using 5-7 drops for sore eyes

4. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains sesquiterpene lactones, alkamides, and phenolic acids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and redness in the eyes. The sesquiterpene lactones, particularly echinacoside and cynaroside, have antioxidant properties that protect the eyes from oxidative stress and damage caused by free radicals. The alkamides, such as cis- and trans-11, 12-alkenylamides, have immunomodulatory effects that help regulate the immune response and prevent infection.

By reducing inflammation, antioxidants, and regulating the immune response, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help soothe and calm sore eyes.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Chop the flowers finely and mix them with the carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  3. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar.
  5. Store the Echinacea essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid and use 2-3 drops in each eye for sore eyes relief.

5. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis essential oils contains several bioactive constituents, including aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and redness in the eyes. Aloe barbadensis essential oils also contains antioxidants like vitamin E and beta-carotene, which can protect the delicate tissues around the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of aloe barbadensis essential oils can help soothe and calm sore eyes, reducing discomfort and promoting healing.

The gentle, non-irritating nature of aloe barbadensis essential oils makes it a suitable option for use around the eyes.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Aloe barbadensis gel and 1 cup of carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil.
  2. Mash the Aloe gel in a bowl with a spoon until it's smooth.
  3. Mix 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil with the Aloe gel to help protect the eyes.
  4. Slowly pour the carrier oil into the Aloe gel mixture, stirring constantly.
  5. Transfer the mixture to a clean glass bottle and store it in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

6. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains linalool and citral as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that can help soothe and protect sore eyes. Linalool, in particular, has been shown to reduce redness and swelling, while citral has antimicrobial properties that prevent infection. The antioxidant properties of Melissa officinalis essential oils also help to neutralize free radicals that can cause eye irritation.

By using Melissa officinalis essential oils, you can help calm and comfort sore eyes, promoting a speedy recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Melissa officinalis flowers. Let it steep for 20-30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the flowers.
  4. Add 10 drops of a carrier oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil, to the mixture for every 1 tablespoon of essential oil you want to make.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 24 hours. Then, strain it again and bottle the essential oil.

7. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which are known for their anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling and redness in the eyes, making it a potential remedy for sore eyes. Gingerols and shogaols also have antioxidant properties, which can help to protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils can help to soothe and calm irritated eyes, providing relief from discomfort and pain.

The essential oil's ability to reduce inflammation and promote healing can help to alleviate sore eyes and promote healthy vision.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Zingiber officinale root, a cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Wash the Zingiber officinale root in cold water, then chop it into small pieces.
  3. Combine the chopped root with the cup of carrier oil in the glass jar and seal it.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily. Strain the oil after 2 weeks.
  5. Store the Zingiber officinale essential oil in a small glass bottle, away from direct sunlight.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent sore eyes from forming?

Drinking herbal tea, especially chamomile or peppermint, may help soothe sore eyes.

These teas have anti-inflammatory properties that can calm irritated eyes. The cooling effect of the tea can also reduce redness and swelling.

This might provide some relief from dry, tired, or irritated eyes caused by fatigue, dry air, or exposure to screens.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for sore eyes every day?

While herbal teas can be soothing for sore eyes, having them every day might not be the best idea.

Some herbs can be too strong, and overuse can irritate your eyes further.

It's better to use them as needed, when your eyes feel tired or irritated, and not make it a daily habit.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in sore eyes?

Herbal teas can help soothe sore eyes, but it depends on the type of tea and your individual condition.

Generally, you may start feeling relief within 10-20 minutes after drinking a warm, soothing tea like chamomile or calendula.

Some people may experience longer-lasting relief, while others might need to try different teas to find what works best for them.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for sore eyes?

If you have sore eyes, it's best to drink herbal tea in the morning or early afternoon.

This allows the tea's soothing properties to help calm your eyes throughout the day.

You can also have a cup before bed to promote relaxation and restful sleep, which can aid in healing.

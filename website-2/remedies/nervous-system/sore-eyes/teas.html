What herbal teas you should avoid if you have sore eyes?

If you have sore eyes, it's best to avoid certain herbal teas that can irritate your eyes further or even cause more damage.

One such herb is Eyebright's close relative, 'Valeriana officinalis', or valerian root tea. This herb is known for its sedative properties, but its oils can cause eye irritation and allergic reactions.

Another herb to avoid is 'Silybum marianum', or milk thistle tea. Although it's commonly used to support liver health, its silymarin content can cause eye irritation and allergic reactions in some people. Additionally, 'Glycyrrhiza glabra', or licorice root tea, should be avoided as it can cause eye dryness and irritation due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which can affect the eyes' ability to produce tears.

Similarly, 'Trifolium pratense', or red clover tea, is another herb to steer clear of as it can cause eye irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals.

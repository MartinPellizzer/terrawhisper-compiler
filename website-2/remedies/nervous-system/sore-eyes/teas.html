Imagine trying to write code or debug a problem when your eyes are tired and sore - it's not fun. When programmers are not feeling well, they may need to take breaks, which can slow them down and affect their work.

This can also impact their mood and overall well-being. Herbal teas have been used for centuries to soothe and calm irritated eyes. Some people find that herbal teas help to relax their eyes and reduce discomfort. When programmers drink herbal teas, they may experience relief from eye fatigue and be able to focus better on their work.

1. Euphrasia officinalis

Euphrasia officinalis teas have been traditionally used to help soothe and relieve sore eyes.

This herbal remedy contains properties and constituents such as flavonoids, iridoids, and saponins that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds help to reduce swelling and ease irritation in the eyes, making it a popular choice for people experiencing dryness, redness, or itchiness. If you're experiencing sore eyes, try making a warm tea by steeping dried Euphrasia officinalis leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and let it cool before using it as an eye bath or applying a few drops to a clean cloth to gently compress the affected eye. You can also add a few drops of the tea to a cotton ball and place it on your closed eye for a few minutes. When you use Euphrasia officinalis tea to soothe your sore eyes, you can expect to feel a cooling and calming sensation. The inflammation and redness may start to subside, and your eyes may feel more comfortable and refreshed.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional to rule out any underlying conditions that may require medical attention.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpene saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce redness and swelling in the eyes. The flavonoids in calendula creams also have antioxidant properties, protecting the delicate tissues around the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. The triterpene saponins in calendula creams have antimicrobial properties, which help to prevent infections and promote healing in the eyes.

When applied topically, calendula creams can help to soothe and calm sore eyes, making them a popular natural remedy for this common problem.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water, 1/4 cup of dried Calendula officinalis flowers, and 1/2 cup of coconut oil.
  2. Heat the distilled water in a saucepan over low heat. Add the dried Calendula officinalis flowers and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture and let it cool. Combine the cooled mixture with the coconut oil in a bowl.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of beeswax and 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil to the bowl. Mix until the beeswax is fully dissolved.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify. Use the Calendula cream as needed to soothe sore eyes.

2. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale creams contains the active constituents allantoin and mucilages.

These constituents help to soothe and calm the irritated tissues around the eyes. Allantoin has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and promote healing. Mucilages form a protective barrier on the eye surface, reducing irritation and discomfort.

By using Symphytum officinale creams, you can alleviate sore eyes and promote a healthy eye environment.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Symphytum officinale leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Heat 1 cup of water in a saucepan and add the dried leaves. Let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  4. Mix 2 tablespoons of the Symphytum officinale infusion with 2 tablespoons of beeswax in a small saucepan over low heat.
  5. Add the mixture to a clean glass jar and let it cool. Store the cream in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

3. Echinacea purpurea

What Are The Main Causes Of Jet Lag?

When we travel across different time zones, our bodies have trouble adjusting to the new schedule.

The main causes of jet lag are time difference, circadian rhythm, and time zone change. A time difference occurs when our destination is several hours ahead or behind our current time. This means we have to adjust our sleep schedule to match the new time, which can be difficult.

Our circadian rhythm is our internal body clock that regulates our sleep-wake cycle. When we travel to a new time zone, our circadian rhythm gets confused and takes time to adjust to the new schedule. A time zone change refers to the actual change in the time we keep, which can be a few hours or more.

When we travel to a new time zone, our body has to adjust to the new time, which can cause fatigue, insomnia, and other symptoms of jet lag.

Below are the main causes of jet lag.

  • Time difference: Time difference causes jet lag because our body's internal clock gets confused when it crosses multiple time zones, making it hard to adjust to the new schedule.
  • Circadian rhythm: Our body's natural internal clock, or circadian rhythm, is a cause of jet lag because it takes time to adjust to a new sleep-wake cycle when we travel across time zones, disrupting the body's regular rhythm.
  • Time zone change: Time zone change causes jet lag because when our body clock is set to a certain time, it gets confused when we travel to a different time zone, disrupting our sleep and waking patterns.
  • Melatonin: When you travel to a new time zone, your body's internal clock struggles to adjust to the new light-dark cycle, and melatonin, a hormone that helps regulate sleep-wake cycles, gets out of sync, leading to jet lag.
  • Sleep schedule: Jet lag occurs when our body's internal clock gets confused because its natural sleep-wake schedule is disrupted by traveling to a different time zone, making it hard to adjust to a new rhythm.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Jet Lag?

When you have jet lag, it's essential to be careful about the herbs you consume.

Some herbs can make jet lag symptoms worse, while others can cause new problems. If you have jet lag, it's best to avoid herbs that affect sleep and relaxation. Ginkgo biloba can make it harder to fall asleep and reduce sleep quality. It can also cause anxiety and make you feel more alert, which can worsen jet lag symptoms.

Another herb to avoid is Ginseng, especially Asian Ginseng (Panax ginseng). It can cause insomnia and make it difficult to fall asleep, which can make jet lag worse. Skullcap (Scutellaria lateriflora) can also cause drowsiness, but it can also interact with medications you may be taking for jet lag, such as sedatives. Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) can cause insomnia and make it harder to fall asleep, especially if taken in large amounts.

It can also interact with blood pressure medications.

Causes and Cures for Jet Lag Using Herbal Preparations

What are the causes of jet lag?

When you travel across different time zones, your body's internal clock gets confused, causing jet lag.

One of the main causes is 'Time Change', which happens when you cross into a new time zone. For example, if you're traveling from New York to Los Angeles, you'll lose two hours when you cross the time zone. This change in time can disrupt your sleep schedule and make it hard to fall asleep or stay awake. 'Crossing Time Zones' is another cause of jet lag. When you fly over multiple time zones, your body has to adjust to a new rhythm, which can take time. The farther you travel from your original time zone, the more severe the jet lag will be. This is because your body's internal clock is set to a specific time, and changing that can cause problems. 'Sleep Schedule' is also a major contributor to jet lag. When you travel, you often have to adjust your sleep schedule to fit the new time zone.

This can be hard, especially if you're not used to it. For instance, if you're used to going to bed at 10 pm and waking up at 6 am, but your new time zone requires you to go to bed at 1 am and wake up at 7 am, it can be a big adjustment. 'Circadian Rhythm' is also affected by jet lag. Your circadian rhythm is your internal body clock that regulates your sleep-wake cycle. When you travel across time zones, your circadian rhythm gets out of sync, making it hard to fall asleep or stay awake. This is because your body is still set to the old time zone, and it takes time to adjust to the new one. Finally, 'Travel Distance' can also contribute to jet lag. The longer you travel, the more your body's internal clock gets disrupted. This is because your body has to adjust to a new time zone, and the farther you travel, the more severe the jet lag will be.

For example, traveling from New York to Australia is a long-haul flight that can cause severe jet lag due to the large time difference and long travel distance.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavoglycosides, bilobalide, and quercetin as active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the tongue and throat, making it easier to speak. Flavoglycosides and bilobalide also improve blood flow to the brain, which can help alleviate conditions like aphasia and dysarthria. The antioxidant properties of quercetin protect the brain cells from damage caused by free radicals, further supporting brain function.

By reducing inflammation and improving blood flow, ginkgo biloba creams can help alleviate difficulties with speaking.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of Ginkgo biloba extract, 2 drops of vitamin E oil, and 2 drops of lavender oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add beeswax to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and add Ginkgo biloba extract, vitamin E oil, and lavender oil. Stir well to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container and let it cool and solidify before use. Apply a small amount to the affected area as needed.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains gingerols and shogaols, active constituents that help with difficult speaking.

Gingerols and shogaols have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which reduce swelling and irritation in the throat and vocal cords. The anti-inflammatory properties of gingerols and shogaols also help to relax the muscles in the throat, making it easier to speak. Additionally, gingerols have a warming effect that can help to increase blood flow to the throat, reducing congestion and discomfort.

This combination of properties in Zingiber officinale creams can help to soothe and calm the throat, making it easier to speak.

  1. Get 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of ginger oil (from Zingiber officinale) and 2 tablespoons of lavender oil to the melted mixture.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in the vitamin E oil. Let it cool and thicken for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool completely. Apply a small amount to the affected area as needed.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains active constituents like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and soothe the throat. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea creams also have antioxidant properties, which can protect the tissues from damage and promote healing. The rosmarinic acid has antimicrobial properties, which can help fight off infections that may be causing the difficulty in speaking.

By reducing inflammation, soothing the throat, and fighting off infections, Echinacea purpurea creams can help make it easier to speak.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 10 drops of lavender essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers with the shea butter and lavender essential oil.
  4. Add the Echinacea mixture to the melted oil and wax mixture, and stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a mold or a container and let it cool and harden before use. Store in a cool, dry place.

4. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid, apigenin, and luteolin as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe the throat and reduce swelling. Rosmarinic acid also has antioxidant properties that protect the mucous membranes from damage caused by free radicals. The antispasmodic properties of apigenin and luteolin help relax the muscles in the throat, making it easier to speak.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the muscles, Melissa officinalis creams can help alleviate difficulties with speaking caused by a sore or irritated throat.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Melissa officinalis flowers and 2 cups of coconut oil in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over simmering water.
  3. Add the dried Melissa officinalis flowers to the coconut oil and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Mix in 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter into the coconut oil mixture, then stir until melted and smooth.

5. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa creams contains a compound called curcumin, which has potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

This helps to reduce inflammation and swelling in the throat and vocal cords, making it easier to speak. Turmeric also contains other active constituents like demethoxycurcumin and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which have been shown to reduce oxidative stress and promote healing in the throat. By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, turmeric creams can help alleviate difficult speaking caused by conditions such as vocal cord polyps or nodules.

Regular use of Curcuma longa creams may help to soothe and calm the throat, making it easier to produce clear and smooth speech.

  1. Gather 2 cups of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, 1/4 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of Curcuma longa powder in a double boiler.
  2. Melt coconut oil, shea butter, and beeswax in the double boiler over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Remove the double boiler from heat and let it cool slightly. Add 2 tablespoons of Curcuma longa powder and mix well.
  4. Pour the mixture into a bowl and let it cool completely. Once cooled, whip the mixture with a hand mixer until it becomes light and fluffy.
  5. Transfer the Curcuma longa cream into a container and store it in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks. Apply a small amount to your lips or affected areas as needed.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis tinctures contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates, which are responsible for its relaxing properties.

These compounds work by reducing anxiety and stress, making it easier to speak in public or communicate effectively. The sedative properties of valerenic acid help calm the nervous system, decreasing muscle tension and allowing the voice to sound more confident. Isovaleric acid also contributes to relaxation, while valepotriates have a sedative effect on the central nervous system.

By reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation, Valeriana officinalis tinctures can help alleviate difficulties with speaking.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Valeriana officinalis root.
  2. Use 2 cups of 80-proof vodka or another high-proof spirit in a clean glass jar.
  3. Combine the Valeriana officinalis root and vodka in the jar, making sure the root is completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-6 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. After 2-6 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into another clean glass container, discarding the solids.

2. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata tinctures contains flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-anxiety and sedative properties that help calm the nervous system. The flavonoids, particularly flavonoids like vitexin and isovitexin, have been shown to reduce anxiety and stress. The sedative properties of Passiflora incarnata help to relax the muscles and calm the mind, making it easier to speak clearly and confidently.

By reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation, Passiflora incarnata tinctures can help individuals with difficult speaking issues.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Passiflora incarnata flowers and 2 cups of 80% vodka or high-proof ethanol.
  2. Combine the dried flowers and vodka in a clean glass jar, making sure the flowers are fully covered.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid tincture to small dark glass bottles and store them in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, as needed.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains active compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling and pain in the throat and vocal cords.

These compounds also have antioxidant properties that protect the throat and vocal cords from damage caused by free radicals. The warming and stimulating properties of gingerols and shogaols can increase blood flow to the throat and vocal cords, which helps to reduce inflammation and promote healing. Zingiber officinale tinctures also contains volatile oils that can help to break down mucus and reduce congestion, making it easier to speak.

By reducing inflammation, promoting healing, and clearing congestion, Zingiber officinale tinctures can help to alleviate difficult speaking caused by throat and vocal cord problems.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger root, 2 cups of 80-proof vodka, and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Peel the ginger root and chop it into small pieces. Put the pieces in the glass jar.
  3. Pour the vodka over the ginger pieces, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Close the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

4. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis tinctures contains rosmarinic acid, apigenin, and linalool as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds give Melissa officinalis tinctures its anxiolytic and adaptogenic properties, which help to calm the nerves and reduce stress that can cause difficulty in speaking. The anxiolytic properties of Melissa officinalis tinctures help to lower cortisol levels, reducing feelings of anxiety and nervousness that can impede clear and confident speech. Additionally, Melissa officinalis tinctures has a sedative effect on the nervous system, promoting relaxation and reducing muscle tension that can cause stuttering or stammering.

By promoting relaxation and reducing stress and anxiety, Melissa officinalis tinctures can help to improve communication skills and reduce difficulties in speaking.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers, 2 cups of vodka, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Melissa officinalis flowers and vodka in the glass jar. Stir well and cover the jar.
  3. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Melissa officinalis tincture in a cool, dark place. Take 15-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, as needed to help with difficult speaking.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia tinctures contains a high concentration of linalool and linalyl acetate, two active constituents known for their calming and relaxing properties.

These compounds help to reduce anxiety and stress, making it easier to speak clearly and confidently. Linalool, in particular, has a soothing effect on the nervous system, which can help to calm the mind and body. This can be especially helpful for individuals who struggle with difficult speaking due to anxiety or nervousness.

By promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety, Lavandula angustifolia tinctures can help to create a calm and focused state, making it easier to communicate effectively.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of 80% ethanol (vodka or gin work too). Wear gloves to avoid skin contact.
  2. Combine the flowers and ethanol in a clean glass jar. Make sure the flowers are completely covered by the liquid.
  3. Steep the mixture for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place. Shake the jar every day to help the liquid extract the plant's properties.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Label the tincture and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture in water 2-3 times a day to help with difficult speaking.

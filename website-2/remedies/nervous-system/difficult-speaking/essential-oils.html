1. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains borneol, citronellal, and linalool as its active constituents.

These compounds give the oil its calming and relaxing properties, which can help to soothe and calm the nervous system. The linalool in Melissa officinalis essential oils has a sedative effect, helping to reduce anxiety and stress that can make speaking difficult. The citronellal in the oil also has a gentle antispasmodic effect, which can help to ease muscle tension and promote a smoother flow of speech.

By reducing anxiety and muscle tension, Melissa officinalis essential oils may help individuals to communicate more effectively and feel more confident when speaking.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers and 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba oil).
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 10 drops of vitamin E oil to the mixture to preserve it. Stir well.
  5. Store the Melissa officinalis essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 5-7 drops per use.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its main active constituents.

These compounds give the oil its calming and soothing properties, which can help reduce anxiety and stress that often accompany difficulty with speaking. Linalool, in particular, has a sedative effect on the nervous system, promoting relaxation and reducing muscle tension. This can help quiet the mind and calm the nerves, making it easier to focus and communicate effectively.

By promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils may help alleviate some of the underlying causes of difficulty with speaking.

  1. Gather materials: 1 cup of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers, a glass jar, a glass bowl, a cheesecloth, a cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil), and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Combine the dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and carrier oil in the glass bowl. Use 1 cup of flowers for every 2 cups of oil.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the bowl every day. This allows the oil to extract from the flowers.
  4. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth into the glass jar. Discard the flowers and reserve the oil.
  5. Transfer the oil to the dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a diffuser or mix it with a carrier oil for skin use.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains gingerol and shogaol as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce tension in the muscles and joints, making it easier to speak. Gingerol and shogaol also have a warming effect on the body, which can help increase blood flow to the throat and vocal cords, reducing stiffness and discomfort. Additionally, the oils' expectorant properties can help loosen mucus and phlegm, making it easier to breathe and speak clearly.

Regular use of Zingiber officinale essential oils may help ease the strain of difficult speaking and promote a smoother, more confident voice.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Zingiber officinale (Ginger) roots.
  2. Peel the roots and chop them into small pieces.
  3. Add 1 cup of water to a saucepan and bring it to a boil.
  4. Add the chopped ginger to the boiling water and reduce the heat to low.
  5. Let it simmer for 2 hours, then let it cool and extract the oil using a solvent like ethanol or hexane.

4. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds interact with the nervous system, reducing anxiety and stress that can make speaking difficult. The sedative properties of valerenic acid help calm the nerves, allowing for clearer and more confident communication. The valepotriates also have a relaxing effect, which can ease vocal strain and promote smooth speech.

By reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation, Valeriana officinalis essential oils can help individuals speak more effectively in difficult situations.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Valeriana officinalis roots. Clean and dry them.
  2. Use a steam distiller or a small pot to heat 4 cups of water. Bring to a boil.
  3. Add 1 cup of Valeriana officinalis roots to the boiling water. Reduce heat to a simmer.
  4. Collect the distilled essential oil in a glass container. It should take about 2-3 hours to collect 1/4 cup of oil.
  5. Strain the essential oil and discard the roots. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight lid.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains rosmarinic acid, camphor, and bornyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce throat congestion and promote clear breathing. The antimicrobial properties of rosmarinic acid can also help combat infections that may be causing the difficulty in speaking. The expectorant properties of bornyl acetate can further aid in loosening and removing mucus from the airways, allowing for smoother speech.

By reducing inflammation and loosening mucus, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help alleviate symptoms associated with difficult speaking.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Wash the leaves with water and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Place the leaves in a clean glass jar and add the carrier oil. Cover the jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a new container. Discard the solids.

6. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains limonene, menthone, and menthol as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have a calming and soothing effect on the nervous system, helping to reduce anxiety and stress that can hinder speech. Mentha x piperita essential oils also contains eucalyptol, which has a decongestant property that can help clear the airways and improve breathing, allowing for easier and more confident speaking. Additionally, menthol in the oil has a cooling effect that can help reduce inflammation and tension in the throat, making it easier to speak clearly and smoothly.

By using Mentha x piperita essential oils, the airways are cleared and the nervous system is calmed, allowing for improved and more confident communication.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Mentha x piperita leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat for 30 minutes, then remove from heat.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 2 hours, then strain the liquid using cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Combine 1 cup of the strained liquid with 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba oil) in a small bowl.
  5. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle and label it for future use.

7. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains limonene, beta-pinene, and linalool as its primary bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help relax the muscles in the throat and vocal cords. The antispasmodic properties specifically target the smooth muscles in the throat, reducing inflammation and tension that can cause difficult speaking. Additionally, the decongestant properties of limonene help clear nasal passages, allowing for easier breathing and more efficient vocal production.

By reducing inflammation, relaxing muscles, and improving breathing, Cymbopogon citratus essential oils can promote healthy and clear speech.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water to 100°F (38°C) and add the Cymbopogon citratus leaves to the saucepan.
  3. Steep the leaves for 1 hour, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl.
  4. Add 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the bowl and mix well.
  5. Store the Cymbopogon citratus essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use 2-3 drops in a diffuser or apply topically after diluting with a carrier oil.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains flavonoids like quercetin and kaempferol, and terpenoids like ginkgolides and bilobalide.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce inflammation and protect nerve cells from damage. The essential oils also have a vasodilating effect, which means they widen blood vessels and improve blood flow to the brain, helping to reduce numbness and tingling associated with neuritis. The antioxidant properties of ginkgo biloba essential oils also help reduce oxidative stress, which can contribute to nerve damage and inflammation.

By reducing inflammation and improving blood flow, ginkgo biloba essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of neuritis, such as numbness, tingling, and pain.

  1. Gather 250g of Ginkgo biloba leaves and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Wash the leaves with water and let them dry completely. Then, crush them into small pieces using a mortar and pestle.
  3. Combine the crushed leaves (100g) with 1 cup of carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Close the jar and shake well.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the oil to infuse with the Ginkgo biloba properties.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and use the Ginkgo biloba essential oil for neuritis treatment.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help reduce nerve pain and inflammation associated with neuritis. The antispasmodic properties of linalool and linalyl acetate can also help relax muscle spasms that may be contributing to the pain. Additionally, the calming and soothing effects of these compounds may help reduce stress and anxiety, which are common triggers for neuritis flare-ups.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils may help alleviate neuritis symptoms and promote healing.

  1. Gather materials: 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers, a clean glass jar, a coffee filter, and a distillation apparatus or a steam distiller.
  2. Prepare flowers: Rinse the flowers with water and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Add flowers to jar: Place the prepared flowers in the clean glass jar.
  4. Distill the flowers: Use the distillation apparatus or steam distiller to extract the essential oil from the flowers. Collect the oil in a separate container.
  5. Filter and store: Filter the collected oil using a coffee filter to remove any impurities. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle and label it.

3. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

These compounds have a sedative and anti-inflammatory effect on the nervous system, helping to reduce pain and inflammation associated with neuritis. The essential oil's analgesic and antispasmodic properties also help to alleviate muscle cramps and spasms, common symptoms of neuritis. Additionally, valerian root's ability to regulate the nervous system and promote relaxation can help to reduce stress and anxiety, which can exacerbate neuritis.

By reducing pain, inflammation, and stress, valeriana officinalis essential oils can provide relief from the symptoms of neuritis.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Valeriana officinalis roots and stems. Clean them well with water.
  2. Dry the Valeriana officinalis roots and stems in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 2 hours. Then, let them cool.
  3. Using a steam distiller, add 2 cups of distilled water and 1 cup of dried Valeriana officinalis roots and stems. Steam distill for 2 hours to collect the essential oil.
  4. Filter the essential oil using a coffee filter to remove any impurities. Store the filtered essential oil in a dark glass bottle.
  5. Label the bottle with the date, name of the essential oil, and recommended usage. Keep the essential oil away from direct sunlight and heat.

5. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains the bioactive constituents gingerol and shogaol, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

These properties help to reduce pain and inflammation in neuritis, a condition characterized by nerve inflammation and damage. The anti-inflammatory action of gingerol and shogaol is attributed to their ability to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines, which contribute to nerve damage. The analgesic properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils help to reduce pain and discomfort associated with neuritis, allowing for improved nerve function and regeneration.

By reducing inflammation and pain, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help to promote healing and recovery in individuals with neuritis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots and 2 cups of water in a large saucepan.
  2. Boil the water, then add the ginger roots and reduce heat to a simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl, discarding the ginger solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a still or a distillation apparatus to separate the essential oil from the water.
  5. Collect the separated essential oil in a clean glass container and store it in a cool, dark place.

7. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis essential oils contains bioactive constituents like rosmarinic acid, camphor, and borneol that help with neuritis.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which reduce swelling and pain in the nerves. The antioxidant properties of rosmarinic acid also protect the nerves from damage caused by free radicals. Borneol helps to reduce inflammation and promotes healing in the affected area.

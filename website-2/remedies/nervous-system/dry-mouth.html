Dry Mouth Remedies: Causes and Natural Treatments

What are the causes of dry mouth?

Dry mouth, or xerostomia, can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is medication, which can reduce saliva production as a side effect. This is especially true for medications that treat depression, anxiety, and high blood pressure. Some medications, like antihistamines and decongestants, can dry out the mouth by slowing down saliva production. Diabetes is another cause of dry mouth. When you have diabetes, your body either can't produce enough insulin or can't use it properly.

This leads to high blood sugar levels, which can damage the salivary glands and reduce saliva production. Hypothyroidism, or an underactive thyroid, can also cause dry mouth. In people with hypothyroidism, the thyroid gland doesn't produce enough hormones, which can lead to dryness in the mouth and other parts of the body. Anxiety and depression can cause dry mouth due to their effect on the body's stress response. When you're anxious or depressed, your body produces more stress hormones, like adrenaline, which can reduce saliva production. This can be especially true if you're taking medications to treat anxiety or depression, or if you're experiencing chronic stress.

These causes can have a significant impact on your oral health, making you more susceptible to tooth decay, gum disease, and other oral health issues.

What Are The Main Causes Of Restless Leg Syndrome?

Restless leg syndrome (RLS) is a condition where people feel an uncontrollable urge to move their legs.

There are several reasons why this happens. One cause is iron deficiency, which occurs when the body does not have enough iron to produce hemoglobin, a protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen. Without enough oxygen, the brain and muscles can become irritated, leading to RLS symptoms. Diabetes is another possible cause of RLS.

High blood sugar levels in people with diabetes can damage the nerves and blood vessels, leading to pain and discomfort in the legs. This can cause RLS symptoms, especially at night when people with diabetes often experience a surge in blood sugar levels. Kidney disease is also a potential cause of RLS. When the kidneys are not working properly, they may not be able to remove waste products from the blood.

These waste products can build up and irritate the nerves and muscles, leading to RLS symptoms.

Below are the main causes of restless leg syndrome.

  • Iron Deficiency: Iron deficiency can cause restless leg syndrome because the lack of iron affects the brain's ability to control the nerves in the legs, leading to uncomfortable sensations and movements.
  • Diabetes: People with diabetes are more likely to develop restless leg syndrome because the high levels of sugar in their blood can damage nerves and disrupt the normal communication between nerves and muscles in the legs.
  • Kidney Disease: Kidney disease can cause restless leg syndrome because it leads to an imbalance of minerals in the body, particularly iron and magnesium, which are essential for healthy nerve function.
  • Anemia: Anemia is a cause of restless leg syndrome because it leads to a lack of red blood cells, resulting in inadequate oxygen delivery to the brain and muscles, which can cause uncomfortable sensations and urges to move the legs.
  • Peripheral Neuropathy: Peripheral Neuropathy is a cause of Restless Leg Syndrome because it damages the nerves that send signals from the brain to the legs, leading to abnormal sensations and uncomfortable feelings that cause the urge to move the legs.

Additionally, the herb Ginseng (Panax ginseng) can cause anxiety and insomnia in some people, which can trigger RLS symptoms. Ginseng may also stimulate the nervous system, making RLS symptoms worse.

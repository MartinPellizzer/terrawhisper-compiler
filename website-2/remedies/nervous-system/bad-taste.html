What Are The Main Causes Of Bad Taste?

Bad taste can have several causes.

Oral Thrush is a fungal infection that affects the mouth, causing white patches to form on the tongue, cheeks, and gums. This infection can lead to a bad taste in the mouth due to the presence of fungal cells and their byproducts. Bad Breath, also known as halitosis, is often caused by poor oral hygiene, such as not brushing or flossing regularly.

When food particles and bacteria accumulate in the mouth, they can cause a strong, unpleasant odor. Tonsillitis is an inflammation of the tonsils, which can become infected with bacteria or viruses. This infection can cause a bad taste in the mouth due to the presence of pus and other debris in the tonsils.

Additionally, tonsillitis can cause bad breath, as bacteria can multiply and release foul-smelling compounds.

Below are the main causes of bad taste.

  • Oral Thrush: Oral Thrush causes bad taste because a type of fungus called Candida grows on the tongue and inside the mouth, making it feel sore and leaving a bitter or metallic taste.
  • Bad Breath: Bad breath is a cause of bad taste because the bacteria that cause the bad breath release strong, unpleasant-smelling chemicals into your mouth, which then mix with the food you eat, making everything taste bad.
  • Tonsillitis: Tonsillitis can cause a bad taste in the mouth because the infected tonsils can release a foul-smelling chemical called volatile sulfur compound, which spreads to the tongue and other parts of the mouth, making food taste unpleasant.
  • Gastroesophageal Reflux: Gastroesophageal reflux is a cause of bad taste because stomach acid flows back up into the mouth, irritating the taste buds and causing an unpleasant, sour or metallic taste.
  • Sinusitis: Sinusitis can cause bad taste because the infection in the sinuses can release bad-smelling chemicals and yellow or green mucus into the mouth, affecting our sense of taste and making food taste unpleasant.

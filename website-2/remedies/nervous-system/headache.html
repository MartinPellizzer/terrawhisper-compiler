What Are The Main Causes Of Headaches?

There are many reasons why people get headaches.

One common cause is Migraine. Migraine is a type of headache that can be very painful and is often accompanied by sensitivity to light and sound. It's thought to be caused by changes in the brain's blood vessels, which can become swollen and press on nerves. Another reason for headaches is Tension. Tension headaches are usually caused by stress and muscle strain in the neck and scalp. When we're under stress, our muscles can become tight and sore, leading to a headache.

This type of headache is often described as a dull, aching sensation. Sinusitis is another common cause of headaches. Sinusitis occurs when the sinuses, which are air-filled cavities in the face, become inflamed and infected. This can lead to a headache, as well as other symptoms like a runny nose and facial pain. Sinusitis is often caused by a cold or allergies, and can be treated with antibiotics or other medications. These three causes of headaches are often linked, and can sometimes happen at the same time. For example, someone with sinusitis may also experience a migraine or tension headache.

Understanding the causes of headaches can help people find ways to manage and prevent them.

Below are the main causes of headaches.

  • Migraine: Migraine is a cause of headaches because of abnormal brain activity and changes in blood vessel size that lead to intense pain and sensitivity to light and sound.
  • Tension: When muscles in the neck and scalp become tense, they put pressure on blood vessels and nerves, leading to pain and discomfort in the form of a headache.
  • Sinusitis: Sinusitis is a cause of headaches because the swollen and inflamed sinuses in the head put pressure on nearby nerves, causing pain and discomfort that feels like a headache.
  • Dehydration: When the body loses too much water, the brain swells and puts pressure on the blood vessels in the head, leading to pain and headaches.
  • Eye Strain: Eye strain occurs when our eyes get tired and overworked from focusing on things too long, especially on digital screens, which can lead to a headache as our brain tries to relax and repair itself.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Headaches?

If you have headaches, there are some herbs you should avoid.

One of these is Angelica root (Angelica archangelica). It can cause blood vessels to constrict, which may worsen headache pain. Another herb to avoid is Butterbur (Petasites hybridus).

Some people have reported that it triggers or worsens headaches. You should also be cautious with Devil's claw (Harpagophytum procumbens) as it can cause increased heart rate and blood pressure, which may lead to headaches. Additionally, Ginger (Zingiber officinale) in large amounts can cause stomach upset and even exacerbate headaches. Feverfew (Tanacetum parthenium) may cause stomach upset and allergic reactions in some people, and in rare cases, can lead to headaches.

Finally, Willow bark (Salix alba) contains salicin, a compound similar to aspirin, which can cause stomach problems and interact with medications, potentially worsening headaches.

Another one is tea tree oil (Cinnamomum camphora or Cinnamomum verum), but only in high concentrations. While it's usually safe in small amounts, high levels can cause irritation and worsen the infection.

You should also be cautious with creams containing peppermint (Mentha piperita). Although peppermint oil can provide some relief, it can also make your eardrum more sensitive and increase the risk of damage. Additionally, creams containing wintergreen (Gaultheria procumbens) contain methyl salicylate, a compound similar to aspirin. This can increase the risk of damage to your eardrum and worsen the infection. Finally, creams containing chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) should be used with caution.

What Are The Main Causes Of Numbness In Hands?

Numbness in hands can be caused by several medical conditions.

One common cause is Carpal Tunnel Syndrome. This condition happens when the median nerve in the wrist is compressed, usually due to swelling or inflammation. When the nerve is compressed, it can't send signals properly, causing numbness, tingling, and pain in the hand and fingers. Multiple Sclerosis (MS) is another cause of numbness in hands. MS is an autoimmune disease that damages the protective covering of nerve fibers in the central nervous system, including the brain and spinal cord.

When the nerve fibers are damaged, they can't send signals correctly, leading to numbness, weakness, and other symptoms in various parts of the body, including the hands. Diabetes is also a cause of numbness in hands. High blood sugar levels can damage the nerves, especially the peripheral nerves that control sensation in the hands and feet. This damage can lead to numbness, tingling, and pain in the hands and feet. People with diabetes are more likely to develop peripheral neuropathy, a condition characterized by nerve damage. It's worth noting that these conditions often have similar symptoms, making diagnosis and treatment challenging.

However, proper medical evaluation and testing can help determine the underlying cause of numbness in hands.

Below are the main causes of numbness in hands.

  • Carpal Tunnel: Carpal Tunnel Syndrome occurs when the median nerve, which controls feeling and movement in the hand, gets compressed in the wrist, causing numbness and tingling sensations.
  • Multiple Sclerosis: Multiple Sclerosis is a cause of numbness in hands because it damages the protective covering called myelin around nerves in the spine and brain, which disrupts the normal flow of signals to and from the hands, leading to numbness, tingling, and loss of sensation.
  • Diabetes: Diabetes can cause numbness in hands because high blood sugar levels damage the nerves, leading to a lack of feeling or sensation in the fingers and hands.
  • Herniated Disc: A herniated disc can cause numbness in the hands because it puts pressure on the nerves that run from the spine to the hands, interrupting the normal flow of signals and causing feelings of numbness or tingling.
  • Pinched Nerve: A pinched nerve in the neck or back can cause numbness in the hands because the pressure on the nerve disrupts the normal flow of signals from the brain to the hands, resulting in a lack of sensation and feeling.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Numbness In Hands?

If you have numbness in your hands, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can cause or worsen this condition.

One such herb is Aconitum, also known as Monk's Hood. It can cause numbness, tingling, and even paralysis if taken in excess.

Another herb to avoid is Hyoscyamus, or Henbane, which can cause numbness, weakness, and a tingling sensation in the hands and feet. You should also limit or avoid taking Conium, or Hemlock, as it can cause numbness, muscle weakness, and even paralysis. Additionally, avoid taking Datura, or Devil's Trumpet, as it can cause numbness, confusion, and weakness in the hands and legs.

These herbs can interact with medications or worsen numbness, so it's best to consult with a healthcare professional before using them.

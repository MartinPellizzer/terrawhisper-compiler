Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, also known as ginger, is a natural remedy for numbness in hands. The active compound in ginger, called gingerol, helps to reduce inflammation in the nerves. When nerves get inflamed, they can send incorrect signals to the brain, causing numbness. Ginger's anti-inflammatory properties help to calm down the nerves and reduce the inflammation. This leads to a decrease in numbness and tingling sensations in the hands. Additionally, ginger also improves blood circulation, which helps to supply the nerves with the necessary oxygen and nutrients to function properly. This can help to alleviate numbness and tingling sensations caused by poor circulation.

This is due to its active compound called curcumin, which has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. When we experience numbness in our hands, it's often because of nerve damage or inflammation in the body. By consuming turmeric or its extracts, we can help alleviate numbness in our hands and promote overall health and well-being.

Capsicum annuum

Capsicum annuum, also known as cayenne pepper or sweet pepper, is a type of plant that contains a chemical called capsaicin. This chemical is responsible for the heat in the pepper. When applied topically to numb hands, capsaicin works by blocking the pain signals to the brain. It does this by attaching itself to pain receptors in the skin, which makes it harder for the brain to receive the pain signal. As a result, the numbness or tingling sensation in the hands is reduced. Additionally, capsaicin has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling and redness in the hands. When applied in moderation, capsaicin can provide temporary relief from numbness and pain in the hands.

What are the causes of numbness in hands?

Numbness in hands can be caused by several things.

One main cause is diabetes. When you have diabetes, high blood sugar levels can damage the nerves, including those in your hands. This damage can cause numbness, tingling, or pain. Multiple Sclerosis is another condition that can lead to numbness in hands. It's an autoimmune disease where the immune system attacks the protective covering of the nerves, disrupting communication between the brain and the rest of the body. This disruption can cause numbness, weakness, or paralysis in various parts of the body, including the hands. Carpal Tunnel is a common condition that causes numbness in hands.

It happens when the median nerve, which runs from your forearm into your hand, gets compressed or pinched. This compression can cause numbness, tingling, or weakness in your hand, especially in your thumb, index, and middle fingers. A pinched nerve is another cause of numbness in hands. This occurs when a nerve gets compressed or pinched, usually by a bone, muscle, or ligament. The compression can disrupt the nerve's function, leading to numbness, tingling, or pain in the affected area. Lastly, a herniated disk can also cause numbness in hands. A herniated disk is when the soft tissue between the vertebrae in your spine bulges out.

This can put pressure on the nerves in your neck or back, causing numbness, tingling, or weakness in your hands, arms, or other parts of your body.

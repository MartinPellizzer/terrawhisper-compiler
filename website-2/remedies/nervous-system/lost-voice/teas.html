10 Herbal Teas to Reclaim Your Voice as Coaches with Lost Voice

Coaches often have to scream and shout at the top of their lungs to motivate their players and get their message across.

But this repeated strain on their vocal cords can lead to a lost voice, making it hard for them to speak, let alone shout. This can be a real problem for coaches, as they need to communicate effectively with their team and other people involved in the game. A lost voice can also affect their confidence and ability to do their job, causing stress and anxiety. Imagine having to cancel a game or practice because you can't speak - it's a nightmare for any coach. A lost voice can also have a big impact on a coach's daily life. They may struggle to explain things to their family and friends, or even to order food at a restaurant.

Simple tasks become difficult, and it can be frustrating and isolating. Coaches may also worry about the long-term effects of a lost voice, such as permanent damage to their vocal cords. When coaches lose their voice, they often try all sorts of things to get it back, from rest and relaxation to medication and even surgery. But one natural and effective way to help restore a lost voice is through herbal teas. These teas are made from plants that have been used for centuries to soothe and heal the body. They can help to calm the vocal cords, reduce inflammation, and promote healing.

By drinking herbal teas, coaches can give their vocal cords a break and help them recover from the strain of shouting and screaming.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, also known as licorice root tea, have been used for centuries to help soothe a lost voice.

The main reason for this is due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce swelling in the throat and vocal cords. The tea contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins, which work together to calm irritation and promote healing. If you're experiencing a lost voice, try making a tea by steeping dried licorice root in hot water for 5-7 minutes. You can drink it up to 3 times a day.

To get the most out of the tea, it's recommended to drink it warm or at room temperature, rather than ice-cold. Some people also find it helpful to gargle with the tea before swallowing to help soothe the throat directly. When taking licorice root tea, you can expect to see improvements in your vocal cord inflammation and soreness. You may start to feel a reduction in discomfort within a few hours, and your voice may begin to return to normal within a day or two.

However, it's essential to note that if your lost voice persists or is accompanied by other concerning symptoms, you should consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas are a popular remedy for lost voice, and for good reason.

The root of the Echinacea plant is rich in compounds like alkylamides, glycoproteins, and polyphenols, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These properties can help soothe and protect the vocal cords, reducing inflammation and promoting healing. The plant also contains rosmarinic acid, which has been shown to have a positive effect on the immune system, helping to fight off infections that may be causing the lost voice.

To use Echinacea purpurea teas for a lost voice, start by brewing 1-2 teaspoons of dried root in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Drink 2-3 cups a day, and consider adding honey or lemon to taste. As the tea works to calm and heal the vocal cords, you may notice a reduction in throat discomfort and an improvement in your ability to speak. You may also experience a faster recovery time, with your voice returning to normal within a few days.

However, if your lost voice persists or is accompanied by other symptoms like a fever or difficulty swallowing, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

3. Thymus vulgaris

The likely outcomes from taking thymus vulgaris teas for a lost voice include a reduction in throat irritation and discomfort, allowing you to speak more comfortably and with a clearer voice.

1. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains the bioactive constituents limonene, linalool, and menthone.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help reduce swelling and ease muscle spasms in the throat. The antiseptic properties of menthone and linalool can also help prevent infection and promote healing in the throat. Additionally, the expectorant properties of limonene can help loosen and clear mucus, allowing the voice to return to normal.

By reducing inflammation, easing spasms, and promoting healing, Mentha x piperita essential oils can help alleviate the symptoms of a lost voice.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh peppermint leaves (Mentha x piperita). Wash them with water to clean.
  2. Use a glass jar to make a peppermint infusion. Fill the jar with 1 cup of fresh peppermint leaves and 1 cup of boiling water. Let it sit for 30 minutes.
  3. After 30 minutes, strain the infusion using a cheesecloth or a coffee filter. Keep the liquid and discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a saucepan and bring it to a boil. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 1 hour.
  5. Let the liquid cool and then use a distillation kit or a still to extract the essential oil. Collect the oil in a clean glass bottle.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains compounds like bornyl acetate, camphor, and 1,8-cineole, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These properties help to soothe and calm the vocal cords, reducing inflammation and irritation that can cause a lost voice. The anti-inflammatory effect of 1,8-cineole and camphor helps to reduce swelling in the vocal cords, allowing for improved vocal function. Bornyl acetate, on the other hand, has a decongestant effect that helps to clear mucus from the throat and vocal cords, further reducing irritation.

By reducing inflammation, antimicrobial activity, and congestion, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help to promote a healthy and functional vocal system.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the leaves. Let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl, and discard the leaves.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) to the mixture and stir well.
  5. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use 5-7 drops for inhalation to help soothe a lost voice.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains a combination of active constituents, including linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to calm the vocal cords and reduce inflammation, making it easier to speak and regain a healthy voice. The relaxing effects of Lavandula angustifolia essential oils also help to reduce stress and tension, which can exacerbate a lost voice. The anti-inflammatory properties of linalool and linalyl acetate help to reduce swelling and irritation in the throat, allowing the vocal cords to heal and function properly.

By using Lavandula angustifolia essential oils, you can help to soothe and calm your vocal cords, promoting a speedy recovery and healthy voice.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers, a clean glass jar, and a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil.
  2. Combine 1 cup of the fresh flowers and 2 cups of the carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Cover the jar with a lid and place it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the extracted essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use 2-3 drops as needed to help soothe a lost voice.

4. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains a compound called cineole, which is a natural decongestant.

This helps to loosen and clear mucus from the throat, allowing the vocal cords to function properly. Eucalyptus globulus also contains eucalyptol, a compound that has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and irritation in the throat. The expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oils work together to help alleviate symptoms of a lost voice caused by respiratory infections or allergies.

By reducing inflammation and loosening mucus, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help restore normal vocal cord function and promote healthy voice recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a clean glass jar, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Dry the Eucalyptus leaves in a low-temperature oven (150°F - 200°F) for 1-2 hours or air dry them for 2-3 days.
  3. Combine 1/4 cup of dried Eucalyptus leaves and 2 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar. Seal it.
  4. Place the jar in a sunny spot for 2-3 weeks, shaking it daily. This allows the oil to infuse.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids and store the oil in a cool, dark place.

5. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains active constituents like alkylamides and phenolic acids that help to reduce inflammation in the throat and vocal cords.

This anti-inflammatory action can help to ease the pain and discomfort associated with a lost voice. The essential oil's antimicrobial properties, thanks to the presence of compounds like echinacoside, can also help to fight off infections that may be contributing to the loss of voice. Additionally, the oil's antioxidant properties, due to the presence of phenolic acids, can help to protect the vocal cords from damage and promote healing.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off infections, Echinacea purpurea essential oils can help to support the recovery of a lost voice.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves. Use fresh or dried herbs.
  2. Combine the Echinacea purpurea herbs with 4 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil). Store in a glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every few days.
  4. Strain the oil mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Filter the oil again through a coffee filter to remove any remaining particles. Your Echinacea purpurea essential oil is now ready to use.

6. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains a number of bioactive constituents, including glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and inflammation in the throat and vocal cords. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, has been shown to soothe and protect the mucous membranes, promoting a smooth and clear voice. The anti-inflammatory and soothing properties of these compounds can help alleviate a sore or lost voice caused by illness, strain, or overuse.

By reducing inflammation and soothing the vocal cords, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can help support the recovery of a healthy, full voice.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 2 cups of water. Chop the roots into small pieces.
  2. Boil the water in a large pot, then add the chopped roots. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean container. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (such as coconut or olive oil) to the liquid extract. Stir well.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 days to allow the essential oils to separate and rise to the surface. Strain and collect the oil.

7. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling in the vocal cords, making it easier to speak. The warming effect of gingerol also increases blood flow to the throat, which can help to soothe and calm irritated tissues. The expectorant properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils can also help to clear mucus from the throat, making it easier to produce sound.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Zingiber officinale essential oils can help to alleviate the symptoms of a lost voice.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Ginger roots and chop them into small pieces.
  2. Add 2 cups of water to a saucepan and bring it to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and add the chopped Ginger roots to the boiling water. Let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture and collect the liquid. Let it cool down to room temperature.
  5. Use a carrier oil like Coconut oil and mix it with the Ginger extract in a 1:10 ratio to create the essential oil.

8. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol, borneol, and carvacrol as its key bioactive constituents.

These compounds have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe the throat and reduce inflammation that may be causing the lost voice. Thymol, in particular, has been shown to have a decongestant effect, which can help clear mucus and debris from the vocal cords. Borneol and carvacrol also have antioxidant properties, which can help protect the vocal cords from damage and promote healing.

By using Thymus vulgaris essential oils, you may be able to reduce the inflammation and discomfort that's causing your lost voice.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat for 10-15 minutes. Add the Thymus vulgaris leaves to the water.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it steep for 30 minutes to 1 hour. Strain the mixture.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a clean glass container. Add 1/4 cup of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  5. Mix well and let it sit for 2-3 hours or overnight. Strain and transfer the Thymus vulgaris essential oil to a small glass bottle for storage.

9. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains bioactive constituents like turmerone, zingiberene, and curcumenol.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce swelling and irritation in the throat. Turmerone, in particular, has been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help soothe and calm an inflamed vocal cord. The antioxidant properties of curcumenol can help protect the vocal cords from oxidative stress and damage.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Curcuma longa essential oils may help alleviate a lost voice by promoting healing and regeneration of the vocal cords.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa roots and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Grind the Curcuma longa roots into fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Mix 1/2 cup of the ground Curcuma longa powder with 2 cups of carrier oil in a glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another glass jar, discarding the solids.

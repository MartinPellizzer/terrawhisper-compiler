What Are The Main Causes Of Earache?

Earache can be caused by several things.

One common cause is an Ear Infection. This happens when bacteria or viruses enter the ear and cause inflammation, leading to pain, fever, and possibly hearing loss. Swimmer's Ear, also known as Otitis Externa, is another cause of earache. It occurs when bacteria infect the outer ear, often after swimming or bathing.

This type of infection can be painful and may lead to swelling and redness in the ear. A Middle Ear Infection, or Otitis Media, is a different type of earache. It happens when bacteria or viruses infect the middle ear, which is the space behind the eardrum. This type of infection can cause fluid buildup, pain, and fever, and may also affect hearing.

Middle Ear Infection often occurs in children and can be caused by a cold or allergies.

Below are the main causes of earache.

  • Ear Infection: Ear infection occurs when bacteria or viruses enter the ear and cause inflammation, leading to pain, swelling, and discomfort, resulting in an earache.
  • Swimmer's Ear: Swimmer's Ear, also known as otitis externa, is a cause of earache because water gets trapped in the outer ear canal, creating an ideal environment for bacteria to grow and cause infection, leading to pain, swelling, and discomfort.
  • Middle Ear Infection: A middle ear infection is a cause of earache because it inflames and irritates the eardrum and the tiny bones inside the ear, leading to pain and discomfort.
  • Sinus Infection: When a person has a sinus infection, the infection can travel up the Eustachian tube that connects the nose and throat to the ear, causing pain and discomfort in the ear.
  • Cold: When the air around us gets colder, the air in our ears gets colder too, which makes the tiny hairs inside our ears swell up, causing pain and discomfort, resulting in an earache.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Earache?

If you have an earache, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can make the problem worse.

Some herbs can irritate the ears or make the pain worse. For example, 'Hyssopus officinalis' (Hyssop) contains compounds that can cause the eardrum to vibrate more, making the pain worse.

Another herb to avoid is 'Pimenta dioica' (Allspice), which contains a chemical that can cause the eardrum to become more sensitive. Additionally, 'Lavandula angustifolia' (Lavender) essential oil can be too strong for the ears and cause irritation. 'Mentha x piperita' (Peppermint) and 'Mentha pulegium' (Pennyroyal) can also be too intense for the ears, as they contain strong compounds that can cause the eardrum to vibrate excessively.

These herbs should be used with caution or avoided if you have an earache.

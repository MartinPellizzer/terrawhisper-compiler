Stress and Students: 11 Herbal Essential Oils to the Rescue

Students can suffer from stress due to various reasons like heavy academic workload, exams, and pressure to perform well.

This can lead to anxiety, insomnia, and a feeling of being overwhelmed. As a result, students may start to avoid activities they once enjoyed, like spending time with friends or participating in hobbies. They may also experience physical symptoms like headaches, stomachaches, and fatigue. Stress can further affect their relationships with family and friends, making them feel isolated and alone. It can also impact their mental health, leading to depression and low self-esteem. When students are stressed, their minds and bodies are in a state of constant alert, making it difficult to relax and unwind.

This can lead to burnout, causing them to feel exhausted, both physically and mentally. They may also experience memory problems, difficulty concentrating, and a decreased immune system. The constant stress can also affect their mood, making them irritable, moody, and short-tempered. Herbal essential oils can help students manage stress by promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety. These oils contain natural compounds that can calm the mind and body, helping students to unwind and relax. When inhaled, they can slow down the heart rate, lower blood pressure, and promote a sense of calm. They can also help students to focus and concentrate, improving their mental clarity and reducing stress and anxiety.

By incorporating herbal essential oils into their daily routine, students can find a natural way to manage stress and improve their overall well-being.

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils are known for their calming effects on the mind and body, helping to reduce stress and anxiety.

The main constituent, linalool, has a sedative property that helps slow down brain activity, promoting relaxation and calmness. The oil also contains linalyl acetate, which has a balancing effect on the nervous system, reducing feelings of overwhelm and anxiety.

When used in aromatherapy, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can be inhaled directly from a diffuser or applied topically to the skin (diluted with a carrier oil) to promote relaxation and reduce stress. To use Lavandula angustifolia essential oils effectively, take a few minutes to inhale the oil directly from a diffuser, or add a few drops to your bath water before a relaxing soak. By doing so, you may start to feel a sense of calmness wash over you, your mind becoming clearer and more focused.

Your body may also start to relax, releasing physical tension and promoting a restful night's sleep.

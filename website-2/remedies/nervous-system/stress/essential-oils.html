1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are responsible for its calming and relaxing properties.

These active constituents help to reduce anxiety and stress by slowing down the heart rate and promoting relaxation. Lavandula angustifolia essential oils also contains camphor and pinene, which contribute to its sedative effects and ability to calm the mind. When inhaled, the aroma of Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to reduce cortisol levels and promote a sense of calm, making it a popular choice for stress relief.

The sedative and calming properties of Lavandula angustifolia essential oils make it a useful tool for managing stress and promoting relaxation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water over low heat, then add the Lavandula angustifolia flowers and let it steep for 1 hour.
  3. Remove the pot from heat and let it cool for 30 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a separate container.
  4. Transfer the liquid to a glass container and store it in a cool, dark place. Let it sit for 24-48 hours to allow the essential oil to separate.
  5. Skim the essential oil from the top of the liquid and transfer it to a small glass bottle. Discard the remaining liquid.

2. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains compounds like linalool, linalyl acetate, and geranial.

These constituents contribute to its calming properties, which can help alleviate stress and anxiety. The sedative effects of linalool and the anxiolytic properties of geranial make Melissa officinalis a useful tool for managing stress. The essential oil's ability to reduce cortisol levels and promote relaxation also helps to counteract the negative effects of stress on the body.

Regular use of Melissa officinalis essential oils may promote a sense of calm and well-being, helping to mitigate the physical and emotional symptoms of stress.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Melissa officinalis flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba oil).
  2. Heat 2 cups of water in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Add the Melissa officinalis flowers to the heated water and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the flowers.
  5. Mix the strained liquid with the carrier oil in a 1:1 ratio. Store the oil in a dark glass bottle.

3. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, specifically GABA, to promote relaxation and reduce anxiety. Valerenic acid has a sedative effect on the nervous system, which helps to calm the mind and body. Isovaleric acid also contributes to this effect by inhibiting the breakdown of GABA, allowing it to stay active in the brain for longer.

As a result, Valeriana officinalis essential oils can be used to help alleviate stress and promote a good night's sleep.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Valeriana officinalis roots and leaves, and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Wash the plant material with water, then dry it with a paper towel.
  3. In a blender or food processor, mix 1/4 cup of the plant material with 1/2 cup of carrier oil.
  4. Heat the mixture in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids and store the oil in a cool, dark place.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains the bioactive constituents camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate.

These compounds have a calming effect on the mind and body due to their sedative and anti-anxiety properties. The 1,8-cineole in Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils also acts as an anxiolytic, helping to reduce feelings of stress and tension. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of this essential oil help to protect the body from oxidative stress, which can contribute to feelings of anxiety and overwhelm.

By promoting relaxation and reducing stress, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can help to create a sense of calm and well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis (Rosemary) leaves and flowers.
  2. Wash the rosemary leaves and flowers with 2 cups of water. Use a fine-mesh strainer to drain excess water.
  3. Combine 1 cup of the drained rosemary leaves and flowers with 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  4. Heat the mixture over low heat for 2 hours. Use a thermometer to ensure the temperature stays between 100°F and 150°F (38°C and 65°C).
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a container. Discard the solids. Use a dropper to transfer the essential oil into a dark glass bottle.

5. Anthemis nobilis

Anthemis nobilis essential oils contains sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The sesquiterpene lactones, specifically chamissoniside and dihydrochamissoniside, have been shown to have a relaxing effect on the nervous system. The flavonoids and phenolic acids in Anthemis nobilis essential oils have antioxidant properties that help reduce oxidative stress and promote relaxation.

This can lead to a decrease in anxiety and stress levels by promoting a sense of calm and well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Anthemis nobilis flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil).
  2. Combine the flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After the steeping time, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Bottle the essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for skin use.

6. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata essential oils contains flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds contribute to its calming and anxiolytic properties, helping to reduce stress and anxiety. Flavonoids like kaempferol and quercetin have anti-inflammatory effects, while alkaloids such as harman and harmine have a sedative and antispasmodic action. The glycosides present in Passiflora incarnata essential oils, including passiflorin, also contribute to its anxiolytic effects by interacting with neurotransmitters in the brain.

By reducing inflammation and modulating neurotransmitter activity, Passiflora incarnata essential oils can help alleviate stress and promote relaxation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Passiflora incarnata leaves and flowers. Wash them with water.
  2. Use a food processor to finely chop the leaves and flowers. Set aside 1 cup of the chopped mixture.
  3. In a saucepan, combine 2 cups of water and 1 cup of chopped Passiflora incarnata mixture. Heat the mixture on low heat for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Place the strained liquid into a steam distillation apparatus or a double boiler with a water bath. Collect the essential oil as it distills.

7. Avena sativa

Avena sativa essential oils contains the bioactive constituents avenacosides, isovitexin, and avenanthramides.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce stress. Avena sativa essential oils also contains apigenin, a flavonoid that has a calming effect on the nervous system. Its sedative properties help regulate the body's response to stress, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety.

The essential oil's calming properties also help improve sleep quality, further reducing stress and promoting overall well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Avena sativa (oats) and a clean glass jar.
  2. Use a food processor to finely grind the oats into a powder.
  3. Combine 1 cup of the oat powder with 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) in a saucepan.
  4. Heat the mixture over low heat for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally, until the oil turns golden and has a rich aroma.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle and let it cool before use.

8. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like alpha-pinene, limonene, and eucalyptol that contribute to its stress-relieving properties.

These bioactive constituents have a calming effect on the mind and body, reducing anxiety and tension. Eucalyptol, in particular, has a sedative effect on the nervous system, promoting relaxation and reducing stress hormones. The oil's decongestant properties also help to clear the mind, allowing for a sense of clarity and focus.

By inhaling Eucalyptus globulus essential oils, you can experience a reduction in stress and anxiety symptoms, promoting a sense of calm and well-being.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves, a clean glass jar, and a dark glass bottle with a lid.
  2. Combine 1 cup of the Eucalyptus leaves with 2 cups of water in the glass jar. Cover the jar and let it sit in a warm place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  3. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into the dark glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of vodka or glycerin to the liquid and mix well. This helps to preserve the oil and prevent spoilage.
  5. Store the Eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a cool, dark place and use it as needed for stress relief. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

9. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla essential oils contains apigenin and luteolin, which are powerful antioxidants that help calm the mind and body.

These compounds also have anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce anxiety and stress by soothing the nervous system. The essential oil's calming properties are also attributed to its high levels of beta-pinene, a terpene that promotes relaxation and reduces feelings of overwhelm. The soothing effects of Matricaria chamomilla essential oil are also thought to be due to its ability to slow down the heart rate and lower blood pressure, creating a sense of calm and tranquility.

By promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety, Matricaria chamomilla essential oil can help the body and mind recover from stress.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Matricaria chamomilla flowers and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Add 1/4 cup of carrier oil like jojoba or sweet almond oil to the jar with the flowers.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the Matricaria chamomilla essential oil in a cool, dark place and use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

10. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils contains the active constituents limonene, linalool, and geraniol.

These compounds contribute to the oil's calming and anti-anxiety properties, helping to reduce stress and promote relaxation. Limonene's uplifting scent and linalool's sedative properties work together to soothe the mind and body, while geraniol's antispasmodic effects help to calm the nervous system. The oil's aroma also helps to reduce cortisol levels, a hormone associated with stress, and promotes a sense of calm and well-being.

Regular use of Cymbopogon citratus essential oils may lead to a decrease in stress levels and an improvement in mood.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 1 cup of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water in the pot over low heat for 10 minutes.
  3. Add the Cymbopogon citratus leaves to the pot and cover with a lid.
  4. Steep the mixture for 30 minutes, then let it cool down.
  5. Strain the mixture and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

11. Santalum album

Santalum album essential oils contains a number of active constituents, including santalol and santalene.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to calm the mind and body. Santalol, in particular, has a sedative effect and can help to reduce anxiety and stress. The essential oil's woody and earthy scent can also have a grounding effect, promoting feelings of relaxation and calmness.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Santalum album essential oils can help to alleviate stress and promote a sense of balance and well-being.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Santalum album (Sandalwood) wood chips or 1 cup of dried leaves.
  2. Place the wood chips or leaves in a clean glass jar with a lid. Leave about 1 inch of space at the top.
  3. Add 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) to the jar. The oil should cover the wood chips or leaves completely.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every few days. This allows the oil to extract from the wood.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent stress from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help reduce stress.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for stress every day?

Consuming herbal teas for stress daily can be safe if you choose teas from reputable sources.

However, some herbs can interact with medications or worsen underlying health conditions.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in stress?

Herbal teas can start to help with stress relief after a few days of regular drinking.

Some people notice a difference within 24 to 48 hours, while it might take a week or two for the full effects to kick in.

It's different for everyone, but with consistent use, herbal teas can become a helpful tool in managing stress.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for stress?

Drinking herbal tea for stress in the evening can be helpful. As the day winds down, your body starts to relax, making it easier for the calming effects of the tea to take hold.

7 Herbal Teas For Stress

Herbal teas have been used for centuries to calm the mind and body.

They work by interacting with our nervous system, helping to slow down our heart rate and quieten our thoughts. One of the key ways they do this is by affecting our GABA levels, a neurotransmitter that helps to regulate our mood and reduce anxiety. For example, Valeriana officinalis, also known as valerian root, is a natural sedative that promotes relaxation and improves sleep quality. Drinking valerian root tea before bed can help you unwind and prepare for a restful night's sleep. Lavandula angustifolia, or lavender tea, has a calming effect on the mind and body, reducing stress and anxiety while promoting a sense of calm and tranquility.

Passiflora incarnata, or passionflower tea, is another herb that has been used for centuries to treat anxiety and insomnia, and is often used in combination with other herbs to promote a good night's sleep. Drinking herbal teas for stress can bring numerous benefits to your life. It can help you to feel more calm and centered, even on the most chaotic of days. It can also improve your sleep quality, which is essential for both physical and mental health. Furthermore, herbal teas can help to reduce anxiety and stress, allowing you to tackle challenges with a clearer and more level head.

By incorporating herbal teas into your daily routine, you can take control of your stress levels and start to feel more balanced and relaxed.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis teas contains a range of bioactive constituents, including valerenic acid and valeranone, which contribute to its stress-relieving properties.

These compounds have a sedative effect on the nervous system, helping to calm the mind and body. Valeriana officinalis teas also contains isovaleric acid and valeryl esters, which have been shown to have anxiolytic properties, reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation. The tea's ability to reduce cortisol levels and promote a sense of calm makes it a popular choice for managing stress and promoting better sleep.

Regular consumption of Valeriana officinalis tea may help to reduce feelings of anxiety and promote a more balanced emotional state.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 tablespoon of dried Valeriana officinalis root.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of honey or sweetener (optional) and set aside.
  3. Steep the dried root in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes to release its active ingredients.
  4. Strain the mixture into a cup and discard the solids.
  5. Add the measured honey or sweetener (if using) and stir well before serving.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two key bioactive constituents that contribute to its stress-relieving properties.

These compounds interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, specifically gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), to produce a calming effect. Linalool also has a sedative and anti-anxiety effect, which can help reduce feelings of stress and promote relaxation. The tea's soothing properties may also be attributed to its ability to lower cortisol levels, the hormone associated with stress.

Drinking Lavandula angustifolia tea may help promote a sense of calmness and tranquility, making it a popular choice for those looking to manage stress.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 tablespoon of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  2. Measure 1 teaspoon of honey (optional) and set aside.
  3. Steep the Lavandula angustifolia flowers in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the flowers.
  5. Add honey to taste, if desired, and enjoy your Lavandula angustifolia tea for stress relief.

3. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata teas contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which are compounds that have a soothing effect on the nervous system.

The tea also contains alkaloids like passiflorine and harmine, which have a sedative property that can help calm the mind and body. These compounds work together to reduce anxiety and stress by regulating the body's response to stress hormones. The flavonoids in Passiflora incarnata teas also have antioxidant properties, which can help protect against cell damage caused by stress.

As a result, drinking Passiflora incarnata tea may help reduce feelings of anxiety and stress, promoting a sense of calm and relaxation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Passiflora incarnata leaves and flowers.
  2. Use 1 tablespoon of the leaves and flowers, and place them in a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the leaves and flowers.
  4. Steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the mixture into another cup.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the tea, 2-3 times a day, as needed to help manage stress.

4. Avena sativa

Avena sativa teas contains active constituents like avenacosides and avenanthramides.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce stress. Avena sativa also contains gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a neurotransmitter that helps regulate the body's response to stress. Avenanthramides in Avena sativa teas can also act as a natural sedative, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a sense of calm, Avena sativa teas may help alleviate stress and promote a restful night's sleep.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Avena sativa (oat straw) and a tea infuser or strainer.
  2. Heat 1 cup of water in a pot and bring it to a boil.
  3. Remove the boiling water from heat and add 1-2 tablespoons of dried Avena sativa to the tea infuser or strainer.
  4. Steep the Avena sativa in the hot water for 5-7 minutes, then remove the infuser or strainer.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup and drink it warm or let it cool before serving.

5. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis teas contains rosmarinic acid, apigenin, and linalool, which have anti-anxiety properties.

These compounds help reduce stress and promote relaxation by blocking stress hormones like cortisol. Melissa officinalis teas also contains flavonoids, which have antioxidant effects that can calm the nervous system. The calming properties of Melissa officinalis teas are further enhanced by its sedative and hypnotic effects, which can help regulate sleep patterns.

By reducing stress and promoting relaxation, Melissa officinalis teas can help alleviate symptoms of anxiety and stress.

  1. Gather Melissa officinalis dried leaves (1-2 teaspoons) and a cup of boiling water.
  2. Pour boiling water over the dried leaves in a cup.
  3. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the tea into another cup.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day for stress relief.

6. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce stress and anxiety. The flavonoids, specifically quercetin, kaempferol, and isorhapontigenin, have a calming effect on the mind and body. Terpenoids, such as ginkgolides and bilobalide, help improve blood flow to the brain, reducing feelings of stress and fatigue.

Regular consumption of ginkgo biloba tea may help improve mood and reduce symptoms of stress and anxiety.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves or 2-3 Ginkgo biloba tea bags.
  2. Let the Ginkgo biloba leaves steep in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes or follow the tea bag's instructions.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the Ginkgo biloba leaves or tea bags.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and stir well.
  5. Drink the tea when it's still warm, ideally 1-2 cups per day, to help manage stress.

7. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains active constituents like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce stress by inhibiting the production of stress hormones like cortisol. Gingerols and shogaols also have antioxidant properties, which protect the body from oxidative stress caused by free radicals. Additionally, Zingiber officinale teas contain a compound called 6-gingerol, which has been shown to have a sedative effect on the nervous system, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Zingiber officinale teas can help alleviate stress and promote a sense of calm.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  2. Add 1/2 teaspoon of dried Zingiber officinale root to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the root.
  5. Drink the tea warm or at room temperature.

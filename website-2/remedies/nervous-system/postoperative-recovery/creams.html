1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce swelling and redness in wounds, making it beneficial for postoperative recovery. The saponins and flavonoids also have antimicrobial properties, which prevent infection and promote a clean environment for healing. The carotenoids, specifically beta-carotene, help to protect the skin from damage and promote collagen production, which is essential for tissue repair.

By promoting wound healing, reducing inflammation, and preventing infection, Calendula officinalis creams can aid in the recovery process after surgery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/2 cup of beeswax.
  2. Combine the Calendula flowers and coconut oil in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean bowl, discarding the Calendula flowers.
  4. In a separate saucepan, melt the beeswax over low heat. Add 1/4 cup of vitamin E oil and stir until combined.
  5. Combine the melted beeswax mixture with the Calendula-infused coconut oil. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool and solidify.

2. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum creams contains flavonoids, particularly hyperforin and hyperoside, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and promote wound healing after surgery. Hypericum perforatum creams also contain naphthodianthrones, such as emodin and hypericin, that have antimicrobial properties, which prevent infection and promote a clean environment for healing. The anti-inflammatory properties of hyperforin and hyperoside also help reduce pain and discomfort after surgery.

The antioxidant properties of these compounds help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting a smooth and healthy recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Hypericum perforatum flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the dried flowers and carrier oil in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Let the mixture cool to room temperature. Then, add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  5. Melt the beeswax in the microwave or over low heat. Stir well and pour the mixture into small containers. Let it cool and harden before use.

3. Echinacea purpurea

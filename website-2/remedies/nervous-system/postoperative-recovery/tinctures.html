1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce swelling and promote healing in the body. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba tinctures, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to improve blood flow and reduce blood viscosity, which can aid in the recovery process after surgery. The terpenoids, including ginkgolides and bilobalide, have anti-inflammatory effects that can help alleviate pain and discomfort after a surgical procedure.

By promoting blood flow and reducing inflammation, Ginkgo biloba tinctures can help support the body's natural recovery process after surgery.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves, 2 cups of vodka (at least 80 proof), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Wash and dry the Ginkgo biloba leaves. Chop them into small pieces and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour vodka over the Ginkgo biloba leaves, making sure the leaves are completely covered. The jar should have about 1-2 inches of vodka left at the top.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. This allows the Ginkgo biloba to infuse into the vodka.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle. Discard the solids. The resulting liquid is your Ginkgo biloba tincture, ready to use for postoperative recovery.

2. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng tinctures contains bioactive constituents like ginsenosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These ginsenosides have anti-inflammatory effects, helping to reduce swelling and pain after surgery. The tincture also contains eleutherosides, which can help to reduce fatigue and improve physical performance after a long period of rest. Additionally, Panax ginseng tinctures may help to improve blood flow, promoting the healing of wounds and reducing the risk of complications.

By reducing inflammation and improving physical performance, Panax ginseng tinctures can support postoperative recovery and help patients return to their normal activities faster.

  1. Gather 1 cup of 80% or higher vodka, 1/4 cup of dried Panax ginseng root, and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Cut the dried Panax ginseng root into small pieces and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour the vodka over the ginseng root pieces, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar, discarding the solids.

3. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa tinctures contains a concentrated form of curcuminoids, including curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These bioactive constituents have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce swelling and pain after surgery. Curcumin specifically has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, such as COX-2 and 5-LOX, that contribute to postoperative discomfort. Additionally, curcumin's antioxidant properties can help protect against oxidative stress and promote tissue repair, which is essential for a smooth recovery after surgery.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, curcuma longa tinctures may support postoperative recovery by minimizing pain, swelling, and tissue damage.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa roots and 2 cups of vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Fill a clean glass cup with 2 cups of water and bring it to a boil. Remove from heat and add the dried Curcuma longa roots. Let it steep for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid and discard the roots. Combine the liquid with vodka in the glass jar. Stir well.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid again and discard the solids. Transfer the tincture to a clean glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 1-2 tablespoons as needed for postoperative recovery.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale tinctures contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory effects, helping to reduce pain and swelling after surgery. Ginger also has antioxidant properties, which can protect against oxidative stress caused by anesthesia and surgery. Additionally, Zingiber officinale tinctures have been shown to have a positive effect on nausea and vomiting, common side effects after surgery.

By reducing inflammation, oxidative stress, and nausea, Zingiber officinale tinctures can contribute to a smoother and more comfortable postoperative recovery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) roots and 2 cups of 80% ethanol or vodka.
  2. Peel and chop the ginger roots into small pieces. Fill a clean glass jar with 1 cup of the chopped ginger.
  3. Pour 2 cups of the ethanol or vodka over the ginger pieces in the jar. Make sure the ginger is fully covered.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This is called maceration.
  5. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle. Discard the solids and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.

5. Astragalus membranaceus

Astragalus membranaceus tinctures contains bioactive constituents like polysaccharides, flavonoids, and saponins.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation and stress, promoting a faster recovery after surgery. The flavonoids and saponins in Astragalus membranaceus tinctures also have immunomodulatory properties, which can help to regulate the immune system and prevent infection. Additionally, the polysaccharides in Astragalus membranaceous tinctures can help to stimulate the production of white blood cells, aiding in the healing process.

By reducing inflammation and promoting immune function, Astragalus membranaceus tinctures can support postoperative recovery and help the body to recover from surgery more quickly.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Astragalus membranaceus roots, 2 cups of 80-proof vodka, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Astragalus membranaceus roots and vodka in the glass jar. Make sure the roots are completely covered by the vodka.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Label the jar with the date and the contents, and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture in water 2-3 times a day for postoperative recovery.

6. Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis tinctures contains the active constituents quinine, quinidine, and cinchonine, which have anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties.

Quinine helps to reduce swelling and pain, while quinidine has a sedative effect that can aid in relaxation and sleep. Cinchonine, on the other hand, has antipyretic properties, which can help to lower body temperature and reduce fever. These properties make Cinchona officinalis tinctures a useful tool for managing postoperative pain, inflammation, and discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Cinchona officinalis tinctures can help to support a smooth and comfortable recovery after surgery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cinchona officinalis leaves and 2 cups of 80% ethanol (vodka works too) in a clean glass container.
  2. Add 2 cups of water to the container and let it sit at room temperature for 2 weeks, shaking the mixture every day.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container, discarding the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of glycerin to the liquid and mix well. This helps preserve the tincture.
  5. Store the Cinchona officinalis tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, as needed for postoperative recovery.

7. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains a number of bioactive constituents, including alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and pain after surgery. The alkylamides, specifically echinacoside and cichoric acid, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, such as COX-2, that contribute to inflammation. The glycosides, including echinacin and isobutylamides, have antioxidant properties that help protect against oxidative stress and tissue damage.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Echinacea purpurea tinctures can help support the body's natural healing process after surgery.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers, leaves, and stems. Wash them with clean water.
  2. Combine the plant material with 2 cups of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar. Stir well to coat the plant material.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the tincture into small dropper bottles and store them in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, as needed.

9. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures contains glycyrrhizin, a powerful anti-inflammatory compound, and licoricidin, a compound that helps reduce swelling and pain.

By reducing inflammation, soothing the digestive system, and protecting the body from oxidative stress, Glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures can help support a smoother postoperative recovery.

  5. Measure the tincture and transfer it to small dropper bottles. Label the bottles with the date, ingredients, and dosage instructions (usually 20-30 drops, 3-4 times a day).

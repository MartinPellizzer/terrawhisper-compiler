What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have postoperative recovery?

If you're recovering from surgery, there are some herbal decoctions you should avoid.

Comfrey decoction is not recommended because it contains allantoin, which can slow blood clotting and lead to excessive bleeding. This can be a problem during postoperative recovery when you don't want to risk bleeding more than usual. Also, avoid decoctions made from boneset, as it may cause stomach upset and interact with certain medications.

Sassafras decoction is also not recommended due to its potential to thin blood and cause bleeding. And, decoctions of pennyroyal may cause liver damage, which can be a concern after surgery. Finally, avoid decoctions of wormwood, as it can interact with anesthesia and cause bleeding complications.

It's best to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies during postoperative recovery.

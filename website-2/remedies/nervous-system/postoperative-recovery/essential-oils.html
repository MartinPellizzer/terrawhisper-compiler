What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have postoperative recovery?

If you're recovering from surgery, it's essential to be cautious with certain herbal essential oils.

Cinnamon oil (Cinnamomum verum), for example, can cause bleeding issues and lower blood pressure. This can be particularly problematic after surgery, as bleeding is a common concern. Clary sage oil (Salvia sclarea) may also lower blood pressure and cause dizziness, which can lead to falls and other accidents during recovery.

Additionally, pennyroyal oil (Mentha pulegium) can cause liver damage, which is a serious concern for people who have recently undergone surgery. Furthermore, thyme oil (Thymus vulgaris) can slow down blood clotting, which can increase the risk of bleeding. Finally, wintergreen oil (Gaultheria procumbens) contains salicylates, which can interact with certain medications and cause bleeding problems.

It's best to avoid these oils until you've fully recovered from surgery and your doctor has given you the green light to resume normal activities.

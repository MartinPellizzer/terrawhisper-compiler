Breaking Down Depression: Causes, Symptoms, and Herbal Remedies

What are the causes of depression?

Depression is a complex issue, and there are several reasons why people get it.

One major cause is stress. When we face too much pressure, our brain can't handle it, and it leads to feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and anxiety. Stress can come from work, relationships, or any situation that makes us feel overwhelmed. Another cause of depression is genetic. If our parents or close family members had depression, we're more likely to get it too. This doesn't mean we're doomed to have depression, but it means we might be more sensitive to stress and other factors that can trigger it. Trauma is also a significant cause of depression.

When we experience a traumatic event, like a car accident or a natural disaster, it can affect our brain and make us more prone to depression. Trauma can also be emotional, like being bullied or abused, which can lead to long-term feelings of sadness and hopelessness. Abuse is another cause of depression. When someone is physically, emotionally, or sexually abused, it can lead to feelings of shame, guilt, and low self-worth. This can make them more likely to develop depression, especially if they don't get help or support. Lastly, grief is a natural cause of depression. When we lose someone or something we love, it's normal to feel sad and depressed. But for some people, grief can turn into depression if it lasts too long or is too intense.

This is because grief can make us feel isolated, hopeless, and disconnected from others.

Ginkgo biloba is known to improve blood flow to the brain and may help reduce symptoms of depression by improving cognitive function and memory. While it may not directly treat depression, it can help improve symptoms of anxiety and stress. Avena sativa, or oat straw, contains a chemical called avenanthramides, which can help reduce stress and anxiety. What are the most used herbal preparations for depression?

Herbal preparations can be a helpful addition to managing depression.

One way to use herbs is through a decoction, which involves boiling herbs in water to release their active compounds. This method is good for roots and bark, which can be difficult to dissolve in water. A decoction can help release the herbs' mood-boosting properties, like ashwagandha, which can reduce stress and anxiety. Another way to take herbs is as a tincture, which is a concentrated liquid extract of the plant. Tinctures are made by soaking herbs in a solvent, like ethanol or glycerin, and can be taken in small doses. This method is good for herbs that are hard to digest, like St. John's Wort, which can help alleviate depression symptoms.

Herbal capsules are another option, where dried herbs are filled into a gelatin or vegetarian capsule. Capsules are convenient and can be easily swallowed, making them a good choice for people who have trouble taking herbs in other forms. For example, the herb Saffron can be taken in capsule form to help improve mood and reduce symptoms of depression. Infusions are another way to take herbs, where the leaves, flowers, or stems are steeped in hot water to release their active compounds. This method is good for delicate herbs like chamomile, which can help promote relaxation and reduce anxiety. Lastly, suppositories are used for herbs that need to be absorbed directly into the body, like the herb Bacopa Monnieri, which can help improve memory and mood. These are inserted into the rectum and can be a good option for people who have trouble taking herbs orally.

Herbal preparations can be tailored to individual needs and preferences, and can be used in combination with other therapies to manage depression symptoms.

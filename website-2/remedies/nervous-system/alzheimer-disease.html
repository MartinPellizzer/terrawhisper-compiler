What Are The Main Causes Of Alzheimer’S Disease?

Alzheimer's disease is a complex condition, and its causes are not yet fully understood.

However, research has identified several factors that contribute to its development. One of the main causes is the presence of the APOE-e4 gene. This gene is a variant of the APOE gene, which helps to carry cholesterol in the body. People who have one or two copies of the APOE-e4 gene are more likely to develop Alzheimer's disease, but it's essential to note that having this gene does not guarantee the disease will develop. Another significant cause of Alzheimer's disease is age.

As people get older, their risk of developing the condition increases. This is because age-related changes in the brain, such as the buildup of beta-amyloid plaques and tau tangles, contribute to the development of Alzheimer's disease. These changes can occur even in people who don't have a family history of the disease. A person's family history is also a risk factor for Alzheimer's disease. If a person has a family member with the condition, they are more likely to develop it themselves. This is because family members may share similar genetic traits, such as the APOE-e4 gene, or other factors that contribute to the development of Alzheimer's disease.

However, it's not the only factor, and many people with a family history of Alzheimer's disease will not develop the condition.

Below are the main causes of alzheimer’s disease.

  • APOE-e4: APOE-e4 is a gene variation that increases the risk of Alzheimer's disease by making it easier for a type of brain fat called beta-amyloid to build up in the brain, leading to damage and death of brain cells.
  • Age: As we age, our brains naturally produce less of a protein called amyloid, which helps clean up waste, but when this process slows down, amyloid builds up and can lead to the development of Alzheimer's disease.
  • Family History: Research suggests that people who have a family history of Alzheimer's disease may be more likely to develop the condition due to inherited genetic mutations that increase their risk of developing the disease.
  • Vascular Disease: Vascular disease is thought to be a contributing cause of Alzheimer's disease because it leads to the narrowing or blocking of blood vessels in the brain, reducing blood flow to brain cells and causing them to die, which in turn leads to memory loss and other symptoms of Alzheimer's.
  • Traumatic Brain Injury: Research suggests that Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI) may contribute to the development of Alzheimer's disease by causing damage to the brain's blood vessels, increasing inflammation, and disrupting the clearance of toxic brain proteins, ultimately leading to the progression of the disease.
  • Diabetes: Diabetes can cause Alzheimer's disease because high blood sugar levels damage the tiny blood vessels in the brain, leading to inflammation and reduced blood flow, which can harm brain cells and impair memory and thinking.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Alzheimer’S Disease?

If you have Alzheimer's disease, it's essential to avoid certain herbs that can worsen your symptoms.

One herb to avoid is St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum), as it can interact with medications used to treat Alzheimer's and reduce their effectiveness. Another herb to avoid is Yohimbe (Pausinystalia johimbe), which can increase blood pressure and heart rate, potentially causing complications for people with Alzheimer's.

Valerian root (Valeriana officinalis) can also be problematic, as it can cause drowsiness and confusion, which may worsen symptoms of Alzheimer's. Additionally, Ephedra (Ephedra sinica) and Guarana (Paullinia cupana) are stimulants that can exacerbate agitation and anxiety in people with Alzheimer's.

Finally, Kava (Piper methysticum) can cause liver damage, which may be a concern for people taking medications for Alzheimer's.

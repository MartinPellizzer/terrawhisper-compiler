8 Herbal Teas For Depression

Herbal teas have been used for centuries to help people feel better when they're struggling with depression.

These teas work by influencing chemicals in the brain that regulate mood. For example, Valeriana officinalis, or valerian root, helps calm the mind and body, making it easier to fall asleep and relax. This is especially helpful for people with depression who often have trouble sleeping. Hypericum perforatum, or St. John's Wort, has been shown to increase the levels of certain neurotransmitters, like serotonin, which help to stabilize mood.

This can help reduce feelings of sadness and anxiety. Passiflora incarnata, or passionflower, has a similar effect, as it helps to calm the nervous system and reduce stress. Drinking herbal teas can have a number of benefits for people with depression. For one, they can provide a sense of calm and relaxation, which can be especially helpful when feeling overwhelmed. They can also help people get a good night's sleep, which is essential for both physical and mental health.

Additionally, herbal teas can provide a sense of control and empowerment, as people take an active role in managing their own well-being.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis teas contains valerenic acid and valepotriates, which are the main bioactive constituents responsible for its therapeutic effects.

These compounds interact with the brain's GABA receptors, increasing the production of the neurotransmitter GABA, which helps regulate sleep and calm the nervous system. By promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety, Valeriana officinalis teas can alleviate symptoms of depression. The tea also contains isovaleric acid, which has anti-inflammatory properties that may contribute to its antidepressant effects.

By reducing inflammation and promoting a sense of calm, Valeriana officinalis teas may help improve mood and reduce symptoms of depression.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water, 1 tablespoon of dried Valeriana officinalis root, and a tea infuser or strainer.
  2. Place the dried root in the tea infuser or strainer.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the root and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the root.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, as needed, under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

2. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum teas contains hyperforin and hypericin, two active constituents that help with depression.

These compounds have antidepressant and anti-anxiety properties, which can reduce symptoms of anxiety and depression. Hyperforin is known to increase the production of neurotransmitters like serotonin and dopamine, which helps to regulate mood and reduce symptoms of depression. Additionally, hypericin has been shown to have anxiolytic effects, calming the nervous system and reducing feelings of anxiety and stress.

Drinking Hypericum perforatum teas regularly may help to improve mood and reduce symptoms of depression in some people.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum flowers and leaves. You can find them in herbal stores or grow them yourself.
  2. Dry the Hypericum perforatum flowers and leaves in a low-temperature oven (150°F - 200°F) for 1-2 hours. Store them in an airtight container.
  3. Steep 1 teaspoon of dried Hypericum perforatum in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the mixture with a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if needed. You can also add other herbs like St. John's Wort or lavender to enhance the flavor and benefits.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of the tea per day, ideally in the morning and evening. Consult a doctor before using it as a treatment for depression.

3. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata teas contains flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are its active constituents.

These compounds have a sedative effect, reducing anxiety and stress that often accompany depression. The flavonoids in Passiflora incarnata teas, such as vitexin and isovitexin, have been shown to have a positive effect on the neurotransmitters serotonin and dopamine, which play a crucial role in mood regulation. The alkaloids, including harmine and harmaline, have a sedative and anti-anxiety effect, helping to calm the mind and body.

By promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety, Passiflora incarnata teas can help alleviate symptoms of depression, improving mood and overall well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Passiflora incarnata leaves. If using fresh, clean and dry them.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of Passiflora incarnata leaves for every 1 cup of boiling water.
  3. Heat 1 cup of water in a saucepan until it boils.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of Passiflora incarnata leaves to the boiling water. Remove from heat and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and discard the leaves. Drink 1-2 cups of Passiflora incarnata tea per day, as needed.

4. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two active constituents that contribute to its therapeutic properties.

These compounds have a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help reduce anxiety and stress associated with depression. Linalool has a sedative action, promoting relaxation and reducing muscle tension, while linalyl acetate has a mood-enhancing effect, releasing neurotransmitters like serotonin and dopamine. The combination of these effects helps to regulate mood and emotions, making Lavandula angustifolia teas a useful tool in managing depression.

Regular consumption of this tea may help alleviate symptoms of depression by promoting a sense of calm and well-being.

  1. Get 1 cup of boiling water and 1 tablespoon of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  2. Let the flowers steep in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup using a tea strainer or a piece of cheesecloth.
  4. Add 1 teaspoon of honey to the tea if desired, and stir well.
  5. Drink the tea 1-2 times a day, preferably before bed, to help with relaxation and depression.

5. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis teas contains a variety of bioactive constituents, including rosmarinic acid, apigenin, and luteolin.

These compounds have been shown to have a positive impact on depression by reducing inflammation and stress in the body. Rosmarinic acid, in particular, has been found to have a strong anti-anxiety effect by inhibiting the activity of stress hormones like cortisol. Apigenin and luteolin, on the other hand, have been shown to have a calming effect on the nervous system, reducing symptoms of anxiety and depression.

By reducing inflammation and stress, and promoting a sense of calm, Melissa officinalis teas may help alleviate symptoms of depression.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Melissa officinalis leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture and discard the leaves.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of the tea per day, ideally before bedtime.

6. Avena sativa

Avena sativa teas contains a group of compounds called avenanthramides, ferulic acid, and gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA).

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce stress and anxiety. The avenanthramides in Avena sativa teas have been shown to inhibit the production of cortisol, a hormone associated with stress. By reducing cortisol levels and promoting relaxation, Avena sativa teas can help alleviate symptoms of depression.

The GABA in Avena sativa teas also has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help regulate mood and reduce symptoms of depression.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Avena sativa (oat straw) and a tea infuser.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot. Remove from heat.
  3. Add 2-3 tablespoons of dried Avena sativa to the tea infuser. Place the infuser in the pot.
  4. Steep for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea into a cup.
  5. Drink the tea as needed, ideally 1-2 cups per day, with a healthcare provider's guidance.

7. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains flavonoids and terpenoids, including bilobalide and ginkgolides, as its active constituents.

These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can help protect the brain from damage. Ginkgo biloba teas also contains alkaloids, which can improve blood flow to the brain and increase oxygenation. This increased oxygenation can help reduce symptoms of depression, such as fatigue and lethargy.

By improving blood flow and reducing oxidative stress, Ginkgo biloba teas may help alleviate depression symptoms by promoting a healthier brain environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of honey and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice.
  3. Steep the Ginkgo biloba leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture and add the honey and lemon juice.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, after consulting a doctor or a healthcare professional.

8. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains bioactive constituents like gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation in the brain, a common factor in depression. Gingerols also have antioxidant properties, which protect the brain from oxidative stress, a contributor to depression. Additionally, Zingiber officinale teas have been shown to regulate serotonin levels, a neurotransmitter that plays a key role in mood regulation.

By balancing serotonin levels and reducing inflammation, Zingiber officinale teas may help alleviate symptoms of depression.

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of dried Zingiber officinale (Ginger) root.
  2. Heat the water in a pot until it's boiling.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of dried Ginger root to the boiling water.
  4. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink 1 cup, 2-3 times a day, as needed.

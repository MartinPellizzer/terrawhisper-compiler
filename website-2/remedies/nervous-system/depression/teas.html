What herbal teas you should avoid if you have depression?

If you have depression, it's essential to be cautious with certain herbal teas that can worsen symptoms.

One such herb is St. John's Wort's relative, 'Leonurus cardiaca' (Motherwort), which can interact with some antidepressant medications, potentially reducing their effectiveness or causing adverse effects. Another herb to avoid is 'Cannabis sativa' (Cannabis tea), as its high THC content can exacerbate anxiety and paranoia, common comorbidities with depression.

'Glycyrrhiza glabra' (Licorice root tea) should also be avoided, as it can lead to an imbalance of potassium and sodium in the body, causing mood swings and exacerbating depression. 'Corydalis yanhusuo' (Yan Hu Suo tea) has sedative properties that can worsen depression in some individuals. Furthermore, 'Scutellaria baicalensis' (Baikal skullcap tea) has a potential risk of causing liver damage, which can be a concern for people taking certain medications for depression.

'Cassia alata' (Cassia tea) may cause allergic reactions, including skin rash and itching, in some individuals, and can also interact with certain medications.

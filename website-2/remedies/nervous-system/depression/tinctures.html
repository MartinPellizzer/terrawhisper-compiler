What herbal tinctures you should avoid if you have depression?

If you have depression, it's best to avoid certain herbal tinctures that can worsen symptoms or interact with medications.

St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) is already mentioned in the list you provided to avoid, but it's worth noting that it can interact with many medications, including antidepressants. Other herbs to avoid include Yohimbe (Pausinystalia johimbe), which can increase anxiety and blood pressure, and Khat (Catha edulis), a stimulant that can worsen symptoms of depression and anxiety.

Ephedra (Ephedra sinica) is also not recommended, as it can increase heart rate and blood pressure, potentially leading to cardiovascular problems. Additionally, Ma Huang (Ephedra sinica) is often used interchangeably with Ephedra, so it's best to avoid it as well.

Finally, Yerba mate (Ilex paraguariensis) and Guarana (Paullinia cupana) contain caffeine, which can exacerbate anxiety and insomnia in people with depression.

11 Herbal Essential Oils For Depression

Herbal essential oils like lavender, lemon balm, and valerian root can help with depression.

These oils are made from plants and are often used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation and reduce stress. Lavandula angustifolia, also known as lavender, has a calming effect on the mind and body. It can help you fall asleep faster and sleep more soundly, which is essential for managing depression. Melissa officinalis, or lemon balm, has a soothing effect on the nervous system. It can help reduce anxiety and promote a sense of calmness, making it easier to cope with the symptoms of depression. Valeriana officinalis, or valerian root, is known for its sedative properties and can help improve sleep quality and duration.

These herbs work by interacting with the brain's neurotransmitters, which helps regulate mood and emotions. When you use herbal essential oils for depression, you may start to feel better by noticing improvements in your sleep and mood. You may find that you're more energetic and motivated, which can help you tackle daily tasks and activities. This can also lead to increased self-confidence and a sense of well-being. By incorporating herbal essential oils into your daily routine, you can start to manage your symptoms of depression and improve your overall quality of life. You can use these essential oils in a diffuser, apply them topically to your skin, or add them to your bath water. It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using any new herbs or essential oils, especially if you're already taking medication for depression.

They can help you determine the best course of treatment and ensure that you're using the oils safely and effectively.

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate as its primary bioactive constituents.

These compounds have a calming effect on the nervous system, helping to reduce anxiety and stress that often accompanies depression. Linalool has a sedative property, promoting relaxation and reducing inflammation in the brain, which can contribute to feelings of sadness and hopelessness. Linalyl acetate, on the other hand, has a mood-elevating effect, increasing the production of neurotransmitters such as serotonin and dopamine, which help regulate mood and emotional response.

By reducing anxiety and promoting the production of these neurotransmitters, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of depression.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over low heat (100-150°F or 38-65°C) for 2-3 hours.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of jojoba oil to the saucepan and stir well.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it steep for another 2 hours.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a glass bottle, discard the solids, and store the oil in a cool, dark place.

2. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known to have a calming effect on the mind and body.

These bioactive constituents help to reduce anxiety and stress, which are often associated with depression. The antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties of Melissa officinalis essential oils also contribute to its mood-enhancing effects by promoting relaxation and reducing inflammation in the brain. By regulating the body's response to stress, Melissa officinalis essential oils may help to alleviate symptoms of depression.

The uplifting scent of Melissa officinalis essential oils can also help to improve mood and reduce feelings of sadness.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil)
  2. Add 10-15 drops of vitamin E oil to 1 cup of carrier oil to prevent spoilage
  3. Combine Melissa officinalis flowers and carrier oil mixture in a clean glass jar
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean glass bottle, discard the solids

3. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds work together to have a sedative and calming effect on the nervous system, which is beneficial in managing symptoms of depression. The sedative properties help to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation, creating a sense of calmness in the mind and body. The essential oil also contains valerenol, which has been shown to have a positive effect on the neurotransmitters serotonin and GABA, helping to regulate mood and reduce symptoms of depression.

By promoting relaxation and regulating neurotransmitters, Valeriana officinalis essential oils may be a helpful tool in managing depression.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Valeriana officinalis roots and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Boil the water and add the Valeriana roots. Reduce heat and simmer for 2 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a separate container. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of carrier oil (like jojoba oil) to the liquid and mix well.
  5. Let the mixture sit for 24 hours to allow the essential oil to separate. Then, carefully pour off the essential oil and store it in a dark glass bottle.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains a high concentration of rosmarinic acid, a potent antioxidant that helps reduce inflammation in the brain.

This reduction in inflammation can lead to a decrease in symptoms of depression, as inflammation is known to contribute to the development of this condition. The essential oil also contains carnosic acid, which has been shown to have neuroprotective properties, helping to protect brain cells from damage. Additionally, the oil's high levels of linalool and camphor provide a calming and relaxing effect, which can help alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils may provide a natural and effective way to manage depression symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan until it boils, then reduce heat to low.
  3. Add the fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves to the water and let it simmer for 20-30 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit at room temperature for 2-3 days to allow the essential oil to separate and rise to the top.

5. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata essential oils contains flavonoids, including kaempferol and quercetin, and alkaloids like harman and harmine.

These compounds have been shown to have a sedative effect and can help reduce anxiety and stress. The flavonoids in Passiflora incarnata essential oils also have antioxidant properties, which can help protect against oxidative stress in the brain, a known contributor to depression. The alkaloids, particularly harmine, have been found to have a role in regulating sleep-wake cycles and can help improve mood.

By reducing anxiety and stress, regulating sleep, and protecting against oxidative stress, Passiflora incarnata essential oils may provide relief from depression symptoms.

  1. Gather 250g of fresh Passiflora incarnata leaves and flowers. Clean them with distilled water.
  2. Place the cleaned leaves and flowers in a clean glass jar. Cover them with 1 cup of distilled water.
  3. Add 10 drops of vitamin C to the jar to prevent oxidation. Stir gently.
  4. Cover the jar with a lid and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks. Shake the jar daily.
  5. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a cool, dark place.

6. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum essential oils contains a high concentration of bioactive constituents, including hyperforin, a powerful antioxidant, and hypericin, a bright yellow pigment with antidepressant properties.

These compounds work together to inhibit the reuptake of neurotransmitters like serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine, which are essential for regulating mood. The flavonoids and phenolic acids present in Hypericum perforatum essential oils also have a modulating effect on the brain's neurotransmitter systems, promoting a sense of calm and reducing stress. The anxiolytic and antidepressant properties of Hypericum perforatum essential oils make it a popular natural remedy for managing symptoms of depression.

By promoting the balance of neurotransmitters and reducing inflammation in the brain, Hypericum perforatum essential oils can help alleviate feelings of sadness and hopelessness associated with depression.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Hypericum perforatum flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil) in a clean glass container.
  2. Fill a saucepan with 2 cups of water and bring it to a boil. Reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Place a steamer basket over the saucepan and add the Hypericum perforatum flowers. Cover the saucepan with a lid.
  4. Steep the flowers for 2-3 hours or overnight. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean glass container, discarding the solids.
  5. Combine the flower-infused liquid with the carrier oil in a 1:1 ratio. Stir well and store the mixture in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.

7. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains bioactive constituents like flavonoids, terpenoids, and bilobalide that provide several health benefits.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help protect brain cells from damage. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba essential oils, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to improve mood by increasing the production of neurotransmitters like serotonin and dopamine. The terpenoids, particularly ginkgolide A and B, have been found to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the brain, which can contribute to depression.

By reducing inflammation and increasing neurotransmitter production, Ginkgo biloba essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of depression.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves, a clean glass jar with a lid, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Dry the Ginkgo biloba leaves in a low-temperature oven (150°F - 200°F) for 2-3 hours or air-dry them for 2-3 days.
  3. Combine 1 cup of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves with 2 cups of carrier oil in the glass jar. Stir well.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily to help the oil extract from the leaves.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the Ginkgo biloba essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

8. Avena sativa

Avena sativa essential oils contains a mix of bioactive constituents, including ferulic acid, avenalol, and avenacosides.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce stress and anxiety, common symptoms of depression. Avena sativa essential oils also contain apigenin, a flavonoid that has been shown to have sedative effects and promote relaxation. The sedative properties of apigenin can help regulate sleep patterns, which is often disrupted in people with depression.

The combination of these compounds in Avena sativa essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of depression by reducing stress, promoting relaxation, and regulating sleep patterns.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Oats (Avena sativa) and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Add 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) to the jar. Stir well.
  3. Steep the oats in the carrier oil for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place. Shake the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean jar. Discard the oats.
  5. Store the Avena sativa essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for skin use.

9. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains the bioactive constituents gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the brain, which are associated with depression. Gingerol also has a direct effect on the brain's neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and dopamine, which play a crucial role in regulating mood. The oil's anti-inflammatory properties can also help reduce chronic inflammation, which is linked to depression.

By reducing oxidative stress and inflammation, and modulating neurotransmitters, Zingiber officinale essential oils may provide relief from symptoms of depression.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale roots, a glass jar with a lid, a cheesecloth or a coffee filter, and a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil.
  2. Wash the roots with water, then dry them with a paper towel. Cut the roots into small pieces and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour 2 cups of carrier oil over the roots in the jar. Close the jar with the lid and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After the infusion period, strain the oil through the cheesecloth or coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids and keep the oil.
  5. Transfer the Zingiber officinale essential oil to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use a few drops of the oil in a diffuser or mix it with a carrier oil for skin use.

10. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains the bioactive constituents eucalyptol, alpha-pinene, and beta-pinene.

These compounds have a decongestant and anti-inflammatory effect, which can help reduce stress and anxiety. Eucalyptol, in particular, has a calming and sedative effect on the nervous system, promoting relaxation and reducing symptoms of depression. The refreshing and invigorating scent of eucalyptus globulus essential oils can also help boost mood and reduce feelings of fatigue.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, eucalyptus globulus essential oils can help alleviate symptoms of depression, promoting a sense of calm and well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Eucalyptus globulus leaves and 1 cup of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water over low heat, then add the leaves. Cover the pot and let it simmer for 2 hours.
  3. After 2 hours, turn off the heat and let the mixture steep for another 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a glass bottle and store it in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for skin use.

11. Boswellia serrata

Boswellia serrata essential oils contains boswellic acids, specifically AKBA (acetyl-11-keto-beta-boswellic acid) and KBA (11-keto-beta-boswellic acid).

These boswellic acids have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the brain, which is often linked to depression. The anti-inflammatory effects of AKBA and KBA also reduce oxidative stress, which can contribute to the development of depression. Boswellia serrata essential oils also contains alpha-pinene and beta-pinene, which have a calming effect on the nervous system, helping to reduce anxiety and stress related to depression.

The combination of anti-inflammatory and calming properties in Boswellia serrata essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of depression by promoting a healthy balance of brain chemistry.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Boswellia serrata resin and a double boiler.
  2. Add 2 cups of carrier oil (such as coconut or jojoba oil) to the double boiler.
  3. Heat the carrier oil to around 160°F (71°C) and add 1 tablespoon of Boswellia serrata resin.
  4. Stir well and heat for 2-3 hours, then filter the mixture through a cheesecloth.
  5. Bottle the Boswellia serrata essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent depression from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent depression.

Certain teas, like chamomile and lavender, contain calming properties that can soothe the mind. These herbs can reduce stress and anxiety, which can contribute to depression.

Regularly drinking tea with these properties may help maintain a healthy mood and reduce the risk of depression forming.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for depression every day?

Herbal teas for depression can be a good option, but be careful with daily consumption.

Some teas, like chamomile and lavender, can be too calming and may interact with medications. St. John's Wort can be especially problematic as it affects many prescription drugs.

It's essential to understand the potential interactions and effects.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in depression?

Herbal teas can help with depression, but it's hard to say exactly how long it takes to see results.

Some people feel a bit better after a week or two, while others might not notice a difference for a month or more.

It really depends on the person and the tea they're using.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for depression?

Drinking herbal tea for depression can be most effective in the morning, right after waking up.

This helps set a positive tone for the day and can help regulate your mood.

The calmness and clarity from the tea can also help you tackle daily tasks with more ease and confidence.

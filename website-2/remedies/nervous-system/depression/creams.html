1. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum creams contains hyperforin and hypericin as its active constituents.

These compounds have antidepressant properties due to their ability to inhibit the reuptake of neurotransmitters like serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine. Hyperforin also has a neuroprotective effect by preventing the breakdown of these neurotransmitters. As a result, Hypericum perforatum creams may help regulate mood and reduce symptoms of depression by promoting the balance of neurotransmitters in the brain.

The cream's ability to penetrate the skin and deliver these active compounds directly to the skin and underlying tissues may also contribute to its potential antidepressant effects.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Hypericum perforatum flowers and 1 cup of coconut oil in a bowl.
  2. Melt the coconut oil in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of beeswax to the melted coconut oil and stir until it's fully dissolved.
  4. Add the dried Hypericum perforatum flowers to the mixture and let it steep for 2 hours or overnight.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, then store it in an airtight container.

2. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata creams contains flavonoids and alkaloids, including passiflorine and flavin, which help reduce stress and anxiety.

The sedative properties of these compounds promote relaxation, calmness, and a sense of well-being. The antioxidant properties of flavonoids in the creams help protect the brain from oxidative stress, which can contribute to depression. The flavin in Passiflora incarnata creams also has anxiolytic properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression.

By reducing stress and anxiety, Passiflora incarnata creams may help improve mood and reduce symptoms of depression.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Passiflora incarnata leaves and flowers. Clean a glass jar and label it.
  2. Combine 1 cup of dried Passiflora incarnata with 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil) in the glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Add 1 tablespoon of beeswax and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil to the liquid mixture. Heat the mixture gently until the beeswax dissolves.

3. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis creams contains valerenic acid, valeranone, and isovaleric acid, which are its active constituents.

These compounds have a sedative effect on the nervous system, helping to reduce anxiety and stress. The valerenic acid in Valeriana officinalis creams also acts as a GABA receptor agonist, which can increase the levels of GABA in the brain, a neurotransmitter that helps regulate mood. By reducing anxiety and stress, and increasing GABA levels, Valeriana officinalis creams may help alleviate symptoms of depression.

This can be especially beneficial for individuals who experience depression as a result of anxiety or stress.

  1. Gather 2 cups of distilled water and 1/2 cup of valerian root extract in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the distilled water over low heat, then add the valerian root extract and stir for 5 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, then discard the solids.
  5. Mix 1/2 cup of coconut oil or shea butter with 1 tablespoon of vitamin E oil and the valerian root extract mixture. Stir well to combine.

4. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis creams contains rosmarinic acid and ursolic acid, two active constituents that help alleviate symptoms of depression.

Rosmarinic acid has anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce stress and anxiety, common contributors to depression. Ursolic acid, on the other hand, has antioxidant properties that help regulate mood by balancing neurotransmitters in the brain. By reducing inflammation and regulating neurotransmitters, Melissa officinalis creams can help alleviate feelings of sadness and hopelessness associated with depression.

The calming and soothing effects of Melissa officinalis creams also promote relaxation and improve sleep quality, further aiding in the management of depression symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of beeswax.
  2. Heat the coconut oil and shea butter in a saucepan over low heat, stirring until melted.
  3. Add the Melissa officinalis flowers to the saucepan and let it steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain the mixture.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax to the mixture and stir until melted. Remove from heat.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool. Once cooled, your Melissa officinalis cream is ready to use.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains the bioactive constituents linalool and linalyl acetate.

These compounds have a sedative effect on the nervous system, which can help calm the mind and body. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of these compounds may also contribute to reducing stress and anxiety. By promoting relaxation and reducing inflammation, Lavandula angustifolia creams can help alleviate symptoms associated with depression.

Regular use of these creams may help improve mood and sleep quality.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of lavender essential oil, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of boiling water.
  3. Once melted, remove from heat and stir in lavender essential oil and vitamin E oil.
  4. Pour mixture into a clean, sterilized container and let it cool until solidified.
  5. Store the cooled cream in a cool, dark place and apply a small amount to the skin as needed.

6. Avena sativa

Avena sativa creams contains bioactive constituents like avenalumic acid, ferulic acid, and avenacoside.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce stress and anxiety. Avena sativa creams also contain lignans, which are known to have a sedative effect on the nervous system, promoting relaxation and calmness. The combination of these bioactive constituents helps to regulate the body's response to stress, leading to a sense of balance and well-being.

Regular use of Avena sativa creams may help alleviate symptoms of depression by promoting a more stable mood and reducing anxiety.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Avena sativa (oat straw) and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the dried Avena sativa and carrier oil in a saucepan and heat it on low heat for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass container. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 10 drops of your preferred essential oil (like lavender or chamomile) to the liquid. Mix well.
  5. Pour the Avena sativa cream into small glass jars and let it cool before use. Store in a cool, dark place.

7. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are active compounds that help alleviate symptoms of depression.

The flavonoids, including quercetin and kaempferol, have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce oxidative stress in the brain, a factor linked to depression. Terpenoids, such as bilobalide, have neuroprotective effects, which help maintain the health of brain cells. Ginkgo biloba also contains bilobalide, a compound that has been shown to inhibit platelet-activating factor, a substance involved in the transmission of depression-related signals.

By reducing inflammation and promoting brain cell health, Ginkgo biloba creams may help alleviate symptoms of depression.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba extract, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 tablespoons of jojoba oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add Ginkgo biloba extract and stir until well combined. Remove from heat.
  4. Let the mixture cool and thicken. Then, add vitamin E oil and jojoba oil. Mix well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool completely before use. This cream can be applied topically to help alleviate symptoms of depression.

8. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contains a compound called zingiberene and a chemical called gingerol, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

Gingerol has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce stress and anxiety, common symptoms of depression. Zingiber officinale creams also contains a compound called shogaol, which has been shown to have a sedative effect and promote relaxation. The warm, spicy sensation caused by the creams can also help improve circulation and reduce muscle tension, which can contribute to feelings of calmness and well-being.

The combination of these compounds and properties in Zingiber officinale creams may help alleviate some symptoms of depression.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of Zingiber officinale oil, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil, and 2 drops of bergamot essential oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or in a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth.
  3. Add the shea butter and melt until combined. Remove from heat.
  4. Stir in the Zingiber officinale oil, vitamin E oil, and bergamot essential oil. Pour the mixture into a bowl and let cool until solid.
  5. Once solid, use a spatula to scrape the edges and mix well. Transfer the Zingiber officinale cream to a container and store in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

FAQ

Drinking herbal tea may help reduce stress and anxiety, which can contribute to depression.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for depression every day?

Herbal teas can be a helpful addition to your daily routine if you're dealing with depression.

However, it's essential to be mindful of how much you consume. Drinking herbal teas every day can be safe, but some ingredients might interact with medications or worsen symptoms if overdone.

Moderation is key to experiencing their potential benefits.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in depression?

Herbal teas like chamomile, lavender, and passionflower may help with mild depression.

Research suggests that it can take a few weeks, around 2-4 weeks, to notice some improvement. This is because herbal teas work slowly to balance brain chemicals and calm the mind.

It's essential to be patient and consistent with consumption.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for depression?

Drinking herbal tea for depression can be most effective in the morning.

This is because it can help set a positive tone for the day and improve your mood.

Many herbal teas, like chamomile and lavender, are naturally calming and can help reduce anxiety.

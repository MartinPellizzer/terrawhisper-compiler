1. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata tinctures contains flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are the active constituents that help alleviate nerve pain.

Flavonoids, specifically kaempferol and quercetin, have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and pain in the nerves. Alkaloids like harmine and harmaline have a sedative effect, which can calm the nervous system and reduce pain transmission. Glycosides, such as passiflorine, have a pain-relieving effect by blocking the production of pain-causing chemicals in the body.

By addressing inflammation, calming the nervous system, and blocking pain-causing chemicals, Passiflora incarnata tinctures can help alleviate nerve pain and promote relaxation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Passiflora incarnata leaves and flowers.
  2. Combine the dried Passiflora incarnata with 2 cups of 80% ethanol (vodka or rum work too) in a clean glass jar.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle and take 20-30 drops 2-3 times a day as needed for nerve pain.

2. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis tinctures contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone, which are key bioactive constituents that help with nerve pain.

These compounds have a sedative effect on the nervous system, which can help reduce inflammation and relieve pain. Valerenic acid, in particular, has been shown to interact with the GABA receptors in the brain, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety, which can contribute to nerve pain. The anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties of valerian root help to calm muscle spasms and reduce pain in the nerves.

By interacting with the nervous system, valerian root tinctures can help to alleviate nerve pain and promote a sense of calm and relaxation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Valeriana officinalis root, a glass jar with a lid, and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka.
  2. Add the dried Valeriana officinalis root to the glass jar.
  3. Pour 2 cups of 80-proof vodka over the root, making sure the root is completely covered.
  4. Steep the mixture for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar every day.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar, discarding the solids. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle.

3. Cannabis sativa

Cannabis sativa tinctures contains a high concentration of a compound called cannabidiol (CBD), which is known to have anti-inflammatory properties.

CBD blocks the production of anandamide, a natural chemical in the body that causes pain and inflammation. The tinctures also contain a compound called tetrahydrocannabinol (THC), which binds to pain receptors in the brain and nervous system, reducing pain perception. THC also has a sedative effect, which can help with nerve pain caused by insomnia and stress.

The combination of CBD and THC in Cannabis sativa tinctures can provide relief from nerve pain by reducing inflammation and pain perception.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cannabis sativa flowers and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the flowers and vodka in the jar, making sure the flowers are completely covered.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another glass container, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid, using 20-30 drops (about 1-2 teaspoons) sublingually (under the tongue) as needed for nerve pain relief.

4. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum tinctures contains flavonoids, such as hyperoside and quercetin, and phenolic acids like rosmarinic acid.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can reduce inflammation and protect nerve cells from damage. Flavonoids like rutin and hesperidin in Hypericum perforatum also have a stabilizing effect on blood vessels, which can help improve blood flow and reduce nerve pain. The tannins present in the tincture can also help reduce pain by blocking pain signals to the brain.

By combining these properties, Hypericum perforatum tinctures may help alleviate nerve pain by reducing inflammation, protecting nerve cells, and blocking pain signals.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia tinctures contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two biologically active constituents that play a key role in its therapeutic effects.

Linalool is known for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help to reduce pain and muscle tension. Linalyl acetate, on the other hand, has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help to alleviate nerve pain caused by anxiety and stress. The antispasmodic properties of linalool can also help to relax muscles and reduce cramping, further alleviating nerve pain.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the muscles, Lavandula angustifolia tinctures can provide relief from nerve pain.

  1. Gather 250g of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine the dried flowers and 500ml of 80% ethanol in the glass jar. Stir well and let it sit for 2 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  3. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Measure the liquid and add water to make 1L. The ratio is 1 part tincture to 1 part water.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops, 3 times a day, under the guidance of a healthcare professional for nerve pain.

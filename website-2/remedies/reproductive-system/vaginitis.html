What Are The Main Causes Of Vaginitis?

Vaginitis is an inflammation of the vagina that can be caused by different factors.

One of the main causes of vaginitis is Bacterial vaginosis, which occurs when the normal balance of bacteria in the vagina is disrupted. This can happen when douching or using scented soaps and products, which can kill off the good bacteria and allow the bad bacteria to grow. Another cause of vaginitis is Yeast infection, also known as candidiasis.

This occurs when a fungus called Candida overgrows in the vagina. This can happen due to hormonal changes, such as during pregnancy or menopause, or when taking antibiotics that can kill off the good bacteria and allow the fungus to grow. Lastly, Trichomoniasis is a type of vaginitis caused by a sexually transmitted parasite called Trichomonas vaginalis.

This parasite can be spread through sex with an infected partner, and it can cause an infection that leads to vaginitis.

Below are the main causes of vaginitis.

  • Bacterial vaginosis: Bacterial vaginosis is a cause of vaginitis because an overgrowth of bad bacteria in the vagina disrupts its natural balance, leading to symptoms like a strong fishy odor, itching, and discharge.
  • Yeast infection: Yeast infection causes vaginitis when a type of fungus called Candida grows too much in the vagina, leading to irritation and inflammation that can cause discomfort, itching, and a thick white discharge.
  • Trichomoniasis: Trichomoniasis causes vaginitis because the Trichomonas parasite infects the vagina, leading to inflammation, discharge, and irritation, which are symptoms of vaginitis.
  • Vaginal atrophy: Vaginal atrophy is a cause of vaginitis because the thinning and drying of the vaginal walls reduces the natural moisture and pH balance, making it easier for bacteria to grow and cause infection.
  • Foreign object: A foreign object, such as a tampon left in the vagina for too long, can cause irritation and infection, leading to vaginitis.

Vaginitis: Understanding the Causes and Herbal Remedies

What are the causes of vaginitis?

Vaginitis is a common health issue that affects many women.

It occurs when there's an imbalance in the natural bacteria in the vagina, leading to inflammation and discomfort. There are several main causes of vaginitis. Bacterial vaginitis is usually caused by an overgrowth of bad bacteria, such as Gardnerella vaginalis, which can happen when the good bacteria in the vagina are disrupted. This disruption can be due to factors like taking antibiotics, douching, or having unprotected sex. Fungal vaginitis, on the other hand, is caused by a yeast infection, typically Candida albicans.

This fungus can grow out of control when the vagina's natural pH balance is upset, often due to hormonal changes during pregnancy or menstruation, or when taking certain medications. Viral vaginitis is a less common cause, and it's often linked to the human papillomavirus (HPV). However, it's worth noting that HPV can also cause genital warts. Trichomonas vaginitis is caused by the parasite Trichomonas vaginalis, which can be spread through unprotected sex with an infected partner. This parasite can cause irritation, itching, and discharge. Chlamydia vaginitis is usually caused by the bacterium Chlamydia trachomatis, which can also lead to pelvic inflammatory disease (PID) if left untreated.

Chlamydia is often spread through unprotected sex with an infected partner.

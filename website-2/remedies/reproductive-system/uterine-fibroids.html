What Are The Main Causes Of Uterine Fibroids?

Uterine fibroids are non-cancerous growths that develop in or around the uterus.

The main causes of uterine fibroids are related to hormones. Estrogen and progesterone are two types of hormones that play a significant role in the growth of fibroids. Estrogen is a hormone that helps the uterus grow and thicken, and it also encourages the growth of fibroids.

Progesterone, on the other hand, is a hormone that helps prepare the uterus for pregnancy. Both estrogen and progesterone stimulate the growth of fibroids by causing the cells in the uterus to multiply. The levels of estrogen and progesterone typically increase during a woman's menstrual cycle, and this increase in hormone levels can cause fibroids to grow. Hormones are also influenced by genetics, and some women may be more prone to developing fibroids due to their family history.

Additionally, other factors such as obesity, diet, and lifestyle may also contribute to the growth of uterine fibroids.

Below are the main causes of uterine fibroids.

  • Hormones: Hormones, particularly estrogen, cause uterine fibroids to grow by stimulating the growth of the uterine lining and making the muscle cells in the uterus multiply, leading to the formation of non-cancerous growths.
  • Estrogen: Estrogen is a cause of uterine fibroids because high levels of estrogen in the body can stimulate the growth of fibroid cells, leading to the formation and enlargement of fibroids.
  • Progesterone: Progesterone causes uterine fibroids because it stimulates the growth of the uterine lining, which can lead to the formation of non-cancerous growths called fibroids.
  • Genetics: Genetics play a role in causing uterine fibroids because a woman's family history and inherited traits can influence the growth of fibroids due to the presence of specific genes that affect hormone production and cell growth in the uterus.
  • Age: As women get older, especially after the age of 30, their hormone levels change, allowing uterine fibroids to grow because of the increased production of estrogen, a hormone that stimulates cell growth in the uterus.
  • Family History: Research suggests that Family History is a cause of uterine fibroids because women who have a close relative, such as a mother or sister, with uterine fibroids are more likely to develop them themselves due to genetic factors that may influence the growth of non-cancerous tumors in the uterus.
  • Obesity: Obesity is a cause of uterine fibroids because excess body fat causes the body to produce more estrogen, a hormone that can stimulate the growth of abnormal cells in the uterus, leading to the formation of fibroids.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Uterine Fibroids?

If you have uterine fibroids, it's best to avoid certain herbs that can stimulate the uterus or affect hormone levels.

One such herb is Dong Quai (Angelicae membranaceae), which is often used to regulate menstrual cycles, but can actually increase uterine contractions and worsen fibroid symptoms. Another herb to avoid is Turmeric (Curcuma longa), as it may stimulate the uterus and cause bleeding.

Sage (Salvia officinalis) can also be problematic, as it has estrogen-like effects and may exacerbate fibroid growth. Red Clover (Trifolium pratense) is another herb to limit, as it contains isoflavones that can mimic estrogen in the body, potentially fueling fibroid growth. Finally, Evening Primrose Oil (Oenothera biennis) may increase prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that can cause uterine contractions and worsen fibroid symptoms.

Consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies when dealing with uterine fibroids.

1. Tribulus terrestris

Tribulus terrestris essential oils contains compounds like protodioscin, furostanol saponins, and flavonoids, which have been studied for their potential benefits on male fertility.

These saponins, in particular, are known to stimulate the production of testosterone, a hormone essential for sperm development and maturation. The flavonoids in Tribulus terrestris essential oils have antioxidant properties, which can help protect sperm from oxidative stress and damage. Additionally, the essential oil's ability to improve blood flow and reduce inflammation may also contribute to its potential benefits on low sperm count.

By addressing these underlying issues, Tribulus terrestris essential oils may help promote a healthy balance of hormones and sperm production.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Tribulus terrestris flowers and leaves. You can buy this at a health food store or online.
  2. Use a clean glass jar with a lid to store the dried plant material. Add 2 cups of carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil.
  3. Steep the plant material in the carrier oil for 2 weeks. Store the jar in a cool, dark place and shake it every day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the oil mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the plant material.
  5. Label and store the Tribulus terrestris essential oil in a cool, dark place. Use 5-7 drops of the oil in a diffuser or mix it with a carrier oil for skin application.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains bioactive constituents such as curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can help improve sperm quality and count. Curcumin, in particular, has been shown to reduce oxidative stress and improve sperm motility by increasing the production of ATP, the energy source for sperm movement. The anti-inflammatory properties of curcuma longa essential oils may also help reduce inflammation in the testes and epididymis, which can contribute to low sperm count.

By reducing oxidative stress and inflammation, curcuma longa essential oils may help improve sperm quality and increase sperm count.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Curcuma longa roots, 1 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil), and a clean glass jar.
  2. Dry the Curcuma longa roots in the sun for 2-3 days. This will help remove excess moisture.
  3. Grind the dried Curcuma longa roots into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  4. Mix 2 tablespoons of the powdered Curcuma longa with 1 cup of carrier oil in the clean glass jar. Stir well.
  5. Store the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day to help the oil extract from the Curcuma longa.

Breastfeeding Breast Pain: Causes, Herbal Remedies, and Natural Relief

Breastfeeding breast pain is a common and uncomfortable issue that many new mothers experience.

It is characterized by soreness, tenderness, and discomfort in the breasts, often due to overfullness, poor positioning, or hormonal changes. If left untreated, breastfeeding breast pain can lead to pain that lasts for hours, decreased milk supply, and even weaning. Therefore, it's essential to address the issue promptly. Herbs and herbal preparations can be a natural and effective way to alleviate breastfeeding breast pain. Urtica dioica, also known as stinging nettle, can be consumed as a decoction or tea to provide relief from breast pain. Additionally, Vitex agnus-castus, also known as chasteberry, can be consumed as a tincture to help regulate hormonal imbalances that may be contributing to breast pain. What Are The Main Causes Of Breastfeeding Breast Pain?

Breastfeeding breast pain can be caused by several factors.

Engorgement is one common cause, where the breast becomes overly full of milk, causing swelling and tenderness. This can happen when the baby doesn't latch properly or when the mother starts breastfeeding too soon after delivery.

Plugged ducts are another cause, where a small section of the breast duct becomes blocked, leading to pain, swelling, and redness. This can be caused by tight clothing, cold weather, or a sudden change in breastfeeding frequency. Mastitis is a more serious cause, where the breast tissue becomes infected, leading to pain, swelling, redness, and sometimes a fever.

This can be caused by a plugged duct that becomes infected, or by bacteria entering the breast through a crack in the nipple or a cut on the breast.

Below are the main causes of breastfeeding breast pain.

  • Engorgement: Engorgement is a cause of breastfeeding breast pain because the breast gets too full of milk, which can cause it to swell and become tender, leading to sharp pains and discomfort.
  • Plugged ducts: A plugged duct, which is a small blockage of milk in the breast, can cause breastfeeding breast pain because it leads to inflammation and swelling, making it uncomfortable to nurse or move the affected area.
  • Mastitis: Mastitis is a cause of breastfeeding breast pain because the infection can cause inflammation and swelling of the breast tissue, leading to pain, redness, and tenderness.
  • Nipple thrush: Nipple thrush is a cause of breastfeeding breast pain because a type of fungus called yeast grows on the nipple and areola, causing pain, itching, and soreness that can make it hard for babies to latch and feed.
  • Sore nipples: Sore nipples can be a cause of breastfeeding breast pain because the baby's tongue and mouth can cause friction and irritation on the nipple, especially if the baby is not latched on correctly.
  • Poor latch: A poor latch can cause breastfeeding breast pain because the baby's mouth is not forming a good seal around the nipple, leading to discomfort, pressure, and pain on the nipple and surrounding breast tissue.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Breastfeeding Breast Pain?

If you're breastfeeding and experiencing breast pain, it's essential to avoid certain herbs that may affect milk production or pass into breast milk.

One such herb is Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens), which may decrease milk supply. Another herb to avoid is Yohimbe (Pausinystalia johimbe), a stimulant that can increase heart rate and blood pressure, potentially harming your baby.

Also, avoid using Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa) as it may affect milk letdown and cause other issues. Another herb to steer clear of is Ephedra (Ephedra sinica), which can stimulate the body and potentially harm your baby due to its active compounds passing into breast milk. Furthermore, Angelica (Angelica sinensis) may not be suitable, as its active compounds can stimulate uterine contractions and potentially cause issues while breastfeeding.

Additionally, be cautious with Ginkgo biloba as its active compounds may affect blood clotting and cause bleeding, which could be a concern while breastfeeding.

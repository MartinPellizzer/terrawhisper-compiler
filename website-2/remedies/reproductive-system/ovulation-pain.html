What Are The Main Causes Of Ovulation Pain?

Ovulation pain occurs when the ovary releases an egg.

The main causes of this pain are related to the ovaries. One cause is an ovarian cyst, a fluid-filled sac that can form on the ovary. If the cyst grows large enough, it can put pressure on the ovary and cause pain when the egg is released.

Another cause is an ectopic pregnancy, where a fertilized egg implants outside the uterus, often in the fallopian tube. This can cause severe pain and bleeding as the body tries to expel the embryo. A third cause is ovarian torsion, where the ovary twists and cuts off its own blood supply. This can cause severe and sudden pain, often accompanied by nausea and vomiting.

In all these cases, the pain is usually caused by the body's reaction to the abnormal growth or situation, which puts pressure on the ovary and disrupts its normal functioning.

Below are the main causes of ovulation pain.

  • Ovarian Cyst: Ovarian cysts, which are fluid-filled sacs on the ovaries, can cause ovulation pain because they can rupture and release fluid during ovulation, leading to sharp, stabbing pain in the lower abdomen.
  • Ectopic Pregnancy: During an ectopic pregnancy, ovulation pain occurs because the fertilized egg implants itself in a place outside the uterus, often in the fallopian tube, causing severe pain as the embryo grows and the tube expands.
  • Ovarian Torsion: Ovarian torsion is a cause of ovulation pain because when the ovary twists and cuts off its blood supply, it can cause sharp, severe pain that may be mistaken for normal menstrual cramps or ovulation discomfort.
  • Pelvic Inflammatory Disease: Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID) is a cause of ovulation pain because the bacterial infection can inflame and irritate the fallopian tubes, causing them to stretch and put pressure on nearby nerves, leading to pain during ovulation.
  • Endometriosis: Endometriosis is a cause of ovulation pain because when endometrial tissue grows outside the uterus, it can get trapped during ovulation, causing inflammation, scarring, and bleeding, which leads to severe pelvic pain.
  • Appendicitis: Appendicitis can cause ovulation pain because the inflammation and irritation from the inflamed appendix can sometimes spread to the nearby fallopian tubes and ovaries, causing pain during ovulation.

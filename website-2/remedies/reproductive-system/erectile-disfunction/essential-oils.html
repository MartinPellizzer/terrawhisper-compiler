1. Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng essential oils contains bioactive constituents such as ginsenosides, which have been shown to increase blood flow and oxygen delivery to the body's tissues.

These ginsenosides, particularly Rg1, Rg2, and Rb1, have been found to stimulate the production of nitric oxide, a molecule that helps relax blood vessels and improve erectile function. The diuretic and vasodilatory properties of Panax ginseng essential oils also help to lower blood pressure and improve circulation, which can contribute to better erectile function. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of ginsenosides may help reduce inflammation in the body, which can contribute to erectile dysfunction.

By improving blood flow and reducing inflammation, Panax ginseng essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of erectile dysfunction.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Panax ginseng roots, a glass jar with a lid, and a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil.
  2. Wash the ginseng roots with water, then chop them into small pieces and place them in the glass jar.
  3. Pour 2 cups of carrier oil over the ginseng roots, making sure they are fully covered.
  4. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container.
  5. Discard the ginseng roots and store the Panax ginseng essential oil in a dark glass bottle. Use 5-7 drops of the oil per day to help alleviate erectile dysfunction symptoms.

2. Tribulus terrestris

Tribulus terrestris essential oils contains a number of bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins.

Tribulus terrestris essential oil also contains flavonoids like kaempferol and quercetin, which have antioxidant properties that help to protect the body against oxidative stress and promote healthy blood vessels.

By addressing the underlying hormonal and vascular issues associated with erectile dysfunction, Tribulus terrestris essential oil may help to improve sexual function and overall well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Tribulus terrestris flowers and leaves. Use a clean container to store them.
  2. Measure 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil). Pour it into a clean glass bottle.
  3. Add 2-3 tablespoons of dried Tribulus terrestris flowers and leaves to the carrier oil. Stir well.

  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean bottle. Discard the solids. Your Tribulus terrestris essential oil is now ready to use.

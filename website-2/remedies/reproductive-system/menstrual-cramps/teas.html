7 Herbal Teas For Menstrual Cramps

Herbal teas have been a trusted remedy for menstrual cramps for centuries, and it's not hard to see why.

These teas work by relaxing the muscles in the uterus, reducing inflammation, and calming the nervous system. This is where herbs like ginger (Zingiber officinale) come in. Ginger contains compounds that have potent anti-inflammatory properties, which help to ease cramps and soothe the stomach. Valerian root (Valeriana officinalis) is another herb that's commonly used to calm the nervous system, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety.

Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) is also known for its calming properties, and is often used to reduce stress and promote a good night's sleep. Drinking these teas can make a big difference in your life, helping you to feel more comfortable and in control during your period. They can also reduce the need for pain relief medication, which is a win-win. Plus, they're a natural and chemical-free way to manage menstrual cramps, which can be especially appealing if you're sensitive to medication or prefer to avoid it.

By incorporating herbal teas into your self-care routine, you can find a sense of calm and relief that's not always easy to come by during your period.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce pain and swelling in the body. Shogaols, in particular, have a strong analgesic effect, making them effective in relieving menstrual cramps. Additionally, gingerols have a relaxant effect on the muscles, which helps to ease spasms and cramps.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing muscles, Zingiber officinale teas provides relief from menstrual cramps by addressing the underlying causes.

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Zingiber officinale (ginger) root.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Add the ginger root to the boiling water and reduce heat to a simmer.
  4. Steep the ginger root for 5-7 minutes, then strain the liquid.
  5. Drink the ginger tea while still warm to help ease menstrual cramps.

2. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis teas contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valeranone, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These compounds interact with the body's neurotransmitters, specifically GABA, to produce a relaxing effect. By calming the nervous system, Valeriana officinalis teas helps to reduce muscle tension and spasms, which are common causes of menstrual cramps. Additionally, valerenic acid has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to ease pain and discomfort associated with menstrual cramps.

By reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation, Valeriana officinalis teas can help to alleviate menstrual cramps and improve overall well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 teaspoon of dried Valeriana officinalis roots.
  2. Measure out 2 tablespoons of dried Valeriana officinalis roots and place them in a tea infuser or a small muslin bag.
  3. Steep the Valeriana officinalis roots in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the solids.
  5. Drink the tea slowly and as needed to help relieve menstrual cramps.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia teas contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two active constituents that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce pain and swelling, which can ease menstrual cramps. Lavandula angustifolia teas also contains apigenin, a flavonoid that has a sedative effect and can help relax the muscles, further reducing cramp pain. Additionally, the tea's flavonoids and terpenoids may help regulate hormonal changes and reduce prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that cause the uterus to contract and lead to cramps.

By reducing inflammation, relaxing muscles, and regulating hormones, Lavandula angustifolia teas can help alleviate menstrual cramp symptoms.

  1. Get 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Take 1 tablespoon of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  3. Steep the flowers in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and add honey to taste.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve menstrual cramps.

4. Vitex agnus-castus

Vitex agnus-castus teas contains iridoid glycosides, particularly aucubin and agnuside.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help to relax the uterine muscles and reduce menstrual cramps. The tea also contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, further contributing to its pain-relieving properties. The iridoid glycosides in Vitex agnus-castus tea may also help to regulate hormonal imbalances, which can contribute to menstrual cramps.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Vitex agnus-castus tea may provide relief from menstrual cramps and other symptoms associated with PMS.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Vitex agnus-castus leaves and flowers.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of dried Vitex agnus-castus and add it to 1 cup of boiling water.
  3. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then strain the liquid.
  4. Drink 1 cup of the tea 2-3 times a day to help with menstrual cramps.
  5. Store any leftover tea in the fridge for up to 2 days.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains active constituents like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and triterpenoids.

Glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce prostaglandins that cause menstrual cramps. Flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant properties that help to reduce pain and inflammation. Triterpenoids, including glycyrrhetic acid, have a hormone-regulating effect, which helps to balance hormonal fluctuations that contribute to menstrual cramps.

By reducing inflammation and regulating hormones, Glycyrrhiza glabra teas can help alleviate menstrual cramp symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots. You can buy them from a health food store or online.
  2. Use 1 tablespoon of the dried roots for every cup of boiling water.
  3. Heat 1 cup of water in a pot and bring it to a boil.
  4. Add the 1 tablespoon of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it while it's hot. You can drink it 2-3 times a day for menstrual cramp relief.

6. Paeonia lactiflora

Paeonia lactiflora teas contains a number of bioactive constituents, including paeoniflorin, paeonol, and liquiritigenin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce the swelling and pain associated with menstrual cramps. Paeoniflorin, in particular, has been shown to relax the uterine muscles, reducing the frequency and severity of cramps. The antispasmodic properties of paeonol also help to ease muscle spasms, while liquiritigenin has been found to have a sedative effect on the nervous system, further reducing cramp severity.

By reducing inflammation, relaxing uterine muscles, and calming the nervous system, Paeonia lactiflora teas can help alleviate menstrual cramp symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Paeonia lactiflora roots and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the dried roots in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid and discard the roots.
  4. Add honey to taste, if desired, and let the tea cool.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the cooled tea, 2-3 times a day, as needed for menstrual cramps relief.

7. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains flavonoids and terpenoids as its bioactive constituents, which work together to ease menstrual cramps.

Flavonoids like quercetin and kaempferol help relax the uterine muscles, reducing spasms and discomfort. Terpenoids, specifically ginkgolides and bilobalide, have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce prostaglandins, hormone-like substances responsible for menstrual cramps. These compounds also improve blood flow to the uterus, which may help reduce cramping and promote relaxation.

By combining these properties, Ginkgo biloba teas may provide relief from menstrual cramps for some women.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the Ginkgo biloba leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the leaves.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to the tea, if desired, to sweeten.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve menstrual cramps.

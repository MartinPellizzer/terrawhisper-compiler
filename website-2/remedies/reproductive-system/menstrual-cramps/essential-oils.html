9 Herbal Essential Oils For Menstrual Cramps

Zingiber officinale, or ginger, is a popular herb used to make tea. It contains compounds that help to reduce prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that cause the uterus to contract and lead to pain. Drinking ginger tea can help to ease cramps and make you feel more comfortable. Another herb used to make tea is Lavandula angustifolia, or lavender.

It contains compounds that help to reduce inflammation and relax the muscles in the uterus, which can help to ease cramps. Making herbal tea is easy – simply steep a teaspoon of dried herbs in a cup of hot water for 5-10 minutes, then strain and drink. You can also add honey or lemon to taste.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties.

This property helps to reduce swelling and pain in the uterus, providing relief from menstrual cramps. Gingerol also has a compound called shogaol, which is a potent analgesic, helping to numb the pain and discomfort caused by menstrual cramps. Additionally, Zingiber officinale essential oils has a compound called zingerone, which has anti-spasmodic properties, helping to relax the uterine muscles and reduce cramping.

The anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils work together to provide relief from menstrual cramps.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale roots (Ginger roots) and chop them into small pieces.
  2. In a small saucepan, combine the chopped ginger roots (1 cup) and 2 cups of water.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes, then turn off the heat and let it steep for 1 hour.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass container. Discard the solids.
  5. Let the liquid sit for 2-3 days, allowing the essential oils to separate and rise to the top. Skim off the essential oils and store them in a dark glass bottle.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool, linalyl acetate, and camphor as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which help to reduce menstrual cramps. Linalool, in particular, has been shown to relax muscles and improve blood flow, reducing pain and discomfort. Linalyl acetate has a sedative effect, which can help to calm the nervous system and reduce anxiety associated with menstrual cramps.

The combination of these properties in Lavandula angustifolia essential oils makes it a natural remedy for relieving menstrual cramp pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water over low heat and add the Lavandula angustifolia flowers. Let it steep for 1-2 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of carrier oil (like jojoba oil or sweet almond oil) to the liquid and mix well.
  5. Transfer the mixture to a dark glass bottle and store it in a cool, dry place. Use 5-7 drops of the essential oil in a diffuser or mix with a carrier oil for topical use.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare essential oils contains a compound called anethole, which is a potent anti-inflammatory agent.

Anethole and other active constituents like fenchone and estragole help to relax the uterine muscles, reducing cramping and spasms that often accompany menstrual periods. The essential oil's antispasmodic properties also help to ease cramp-related pain by calming the muscles and reducing discomfort. Additionally, anethole's analgesic properties help to block pain signals to the brain, providing relief from menstrual cramp pain.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the uterine muscles, Foeniculum vulgare essential oils can help alleviate menstrual cramp symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Foeniculum vulgare seeds and 1 cup of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Place a cheesecloth or a coffee filter in a bowl. Pour the seeds and carrier oil into the cheesecloth.
  3. Gently massage the seeds with the oil for 10 minutes to release the oil from the seeds.
  4. Let the mixture sit in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the bowl every day. This allows the oil to mature and separate.
  5. Strain the oil through the cheesecloth and discard the seeds. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains compounds like camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help to ease menstrual cramp pain. The antispasmodic properties of borneol and bornyl acetate work by relaxing the smooth muscle in the uterus, reducing spasms and pain. The anti-inflammatory properties of camphor and other constituents help to reduce inflammation in the pelvic area, further alleviating cramp pain.

Regular use of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils may help to reduce the severity of menstrual cramps.

  1. Gather materials: 2 cups of Rosmarinus officinalis leaves, a glass jar, a coffee grinder, a cheesecloth, a carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil), and a dark glass bottle.
  2. Dry the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves in a warm place for 2 weeks. This step helps remove moisture, which can cause spoilage.
  3. Grind 1 cup of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves into a fine powder using the coffee grinder.
  4. Steep the ground Rosmarinus officinalis in 2 cups of carrier oil for 2 weeks. Store the mixture in the glass jar and place it in a dark, cool place.
  5. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth into the dark glass bottle. Discard the solids and label the bottle with the date and ingredients. Use 5-7 drops of the essential oil in a warm bath or mix with a carrier oil for massage.

5. Cinnamomum zeylanicum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum essential oils contains cinnamaldehyde, linalool, and eugenol as its main active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help alleviate menstrual cramps. Cinnamaldehyde helps relax the uterine muscles, reducing spasms and pain, while linalool has a soothing effect on the nervous system, promoting relaxation and calmness. Eugenol, on the other hand, has analgesic properties, reducing pain and discomfort associated with menstrual cramps.

By combining these properties, Cinnamomum zeylanicum essential oils can provide relief from menstrual cramps by reducing inflammation, relaxing the muscles, and alleviating pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cinnamomum zeylanicum bark, 2 cups of water, a large pot, cheesecloth or a coffee filter, and a clean glass bottle.
  2. Combine the Cinnamomum zeylanicum bark and water in the large pot. Heat the mixture over low heat for 2-3 hours, or until the liquid has reduced by half.
  3. Strain the mixture through the cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean container. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid to the glass bottle and let it cool. Once cooled, store the bottle in a cool, dark place.
  5. To use, dilute 5-7 drops of the essential oil in 1 tablespoon of carrier oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil. Apply topically to the lower abdomen for relief from menstrual cramps.

6. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa essential oils contains a compound called curcumin, along with other bioactive constituents like turmerone and demethoxycurcumin.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce pain and discomfort associated with menstrual cramps. Curcumin in particular has been shown to inhibit the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause the uterus to contract and lead to cramping. The anti-inflammatory properties of turmerone and demethoxycurcumin also help to relax the uterine muscles and reduce inflammation.

By reducing inflammation and prostaglandin production, Curcuma longa essential oils can provide relief from menstrual cramps.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa roots, a glass jar with a lid, and a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  2. Fill the jar with the dried Curcuma longa roots and cover them with 2 cups of carrier oil.
  3. Close the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into another container, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the Curcuma longa essential oil to a small glass bottle and use 5-7 drops in a warm bath or massage oil to relieve menstrual cramps.

7. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris essential oils contains thymol and carvacrol as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds have analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce menstrual cramp pain. Thymol's ability to relax muscles and improve blood flow also contributes to its pain-relieving effects. Carvacrol's antioxidant properties can help to reduce inflammation and alleviate cramping, making it easier to manage menstrual discomfort.

The combination of these properties in Thymus vulgaris essential oils makes it a potential natural remedy for alleviating menstrual cramp pain.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Thymus vulgaris leaves, 1 cup of water, and a glass jar for distillation.
  2. Combine the Thymus vulgaris leaves and water in the glass jar. Make sure the leaves are covered with water.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat (around 100°F to 150°F) for 2-3 hours. Monitor the temperature and stir occasionally.
  4. Collect the steam that rises from the mixture using a condenser or a cool surface. This will produce the essential oil.
  5. Strain the collected oil through a coffee filter or cheesecloth into a small glass bottle. Label and store the bottle in a cool, dark place.

8. Mentha x piperita

Mentha x piperita essential oils contains eugenol, menthone, and limonene, which contribute to its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce pain and swelling associated with menstrual cramps. The analgesic effect of eugenol blocks the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause the uterus to contract and lead to cramping. Menthone's anti-inflammatory properties help to reduce swelling and ease discomfort.

By reducing prostaglandin production and inflammation, Mentha x piperita essential oils can help alleviate menstrual cramp symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh peppermint leaves (Mentha x piperita) and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over low heat until it starts to simmer.
  3. Add the peppermint leaves to the saucepan and cover it with a lid.
  4. Steep the mixture for 10-15 minutes, then let it cool.
  5. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl, and discard the solids.

9. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin that has anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to reduce prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause pain and cramping in the uterus. Glycyrrhizin also has a relaxing effect on smooth muscle, which can ease menstrual cramp symptoms. Additionally, glycyrrhiza glabra has a mild sedative effect, which can help to promote a good night's sleep and reduce stress, both of which can contribute to menstrual cramp relief.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils may provide relief from menstrual cramp symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra roots and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Grind the roots into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Mix 1/2 cup of the ground roots with 2 cups of carrier oil in a clean glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture in a warm, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily. Strain the oil after the steeping time.
  5. Bottle the resulting essential oil and use 5-7 drops per bath or 2-3 drops per massage oil for menstrual cramp relief.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent menstrual cramps from forming?

Drinking herbal tea might help ease menstrual cramps.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for menstrual cramps every day?

Consuming herbal teas for menstrual cramps every day can be safe, but moderation is key.

Some teas, like chamomile, contain compounds that can help relax muscles and ease cramps. However, excessive consumption may lead to interactions with medications or affect certain health conditions.

It's essential to be aware of these potential effects.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in menstrual cramps?

Herbal teas can help ease menstrual cramps, but it's different for everyone.

Some people notice relief after a few sips, while others might not feel it until after a few cups over a few hours.

It might take a bit of trial and error to find what works best for you and how long it takes to feel some relief.

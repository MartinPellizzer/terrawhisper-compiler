What Are The Main Causes Of Menstrual Cramps?

Menstrual cramps are caused by several main factors.

One cause is hormonal imbalance. During a menstrual cycle, the levels of estrogen and progesterone hormones change. These hormonal changes cause the uterus to prepare for a potential pregnancy. If pregnancy does not occur, the hormone levels drop, leading to uterine contractions. These contractions can cause pain and discomfort, especially if the uterus is overactive. Hormonal imbalance can also affect the way the body responds to pain, making menstrual cramps worse.

Another cause of menstrual cramps is uterine contractions. As mentioned earlier, the uterus contracts to help remove the uterine lining during a menstrual period. These contractions can be strong and can cause pain, especially if the uterus is overactive or if the muscles in the uterus are tight. Uterine contractions can also be triggered by hormonal changes, which can make menstrual cramps worse. Prostaglandins are another cause of menstrual cramps. Prostaglandins are hormone-like substances that are produced by the body. They cause the uterus to contract and can increase the production of other chemicals that make the uterus contract even more.

Prostaglandins are produced in response to the drop in estrogen and progesterone levels during a menstrual period, which can trigger uterine contractions and pain.

Below are the main causes of menstrual cramps.

  • Hormonal imbalance: Hormonal imbalance occurs when the levels of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause the uterus to contract, are higher than normal, leading to severe menstrual cramps and discomfort.
  • Uterine contractions: During a woman's menstrual cycle, the uterus contracts to shed its lining, which can cause painful contractions that feel like cramps, especially in the lower abdomen.
  • Prostaglandins: Prostaglandins cause menstrual cramps because they make the uterus muscles contract and tighten, which can cause sharp, throbbing pains in the lower abdomen.
  • Estrogen fluctuations: Estrogen fluctuations cause menstrual cramps because the drop in estrogen levels before a period triggers the uterus to contract and squeeze, leading to pain and discomfort.
  • Progesterone drop: The uterus contracts more severely when the level of progesterone hormone drops at the end of a menstrual cycle, leading to menstrual cramps.
  • Uterine fibroids: Uterine fibroids are a cause of menstrual cramps because they can put pressure on the uterus, causing it to contract and tighten, leading to severe pain and discomfort during menstruation.

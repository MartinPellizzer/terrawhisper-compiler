What Are The Main Causes Of Fibrocystic Breast Disease?

Fibrocystic breast disease is a common condition where women experience lumpy or tender breasts.

The main causes are related to hormonal changes in the body. One cause is hormonal imbalance, particularly an imbalance between estrogen and progesterone. During a woman's menstrual cycle, estrogen levels surge before ovulation, and progesterone levels rise after ovulation.

When there's an imbalance, estrogen dominance occurs, where estrogen levels are higher than progesterone. This can lead to breast tenderness, swelling, and lumps. Another cause is progesterone sensitivity, where the body becomes more sensitive to progesterone, causing an exaggerated response to normal levels of the hormone. This sensitivity can lead to breast swelling and tenderness before menstruation.

Estrogen dominance and progesterone sensitivity often go hand-in-hand, as estrogen dominance can cause the body to become more sensitive to progesterone.

Below are the main causes of fibrocystic breast disease.

  • Hormonal imbalance: Hormonal imbalance is a cause of fibrocystic breast disease because fluctuating levels of estrogen and progesterone in the body lead to breast tissue swelling and the formation of cysts, resulting in pain and discomfort.
  • Progesterone sensitivity: Progesterone sensitivity is a cause of fibrocystic breast disease because the body's cells become overly sensitive to the hormone progesterone, causing them to grow and form cysts in the breasts in response to its presence.
  • Estrogen dominance: Estrogen dominance is a cause of fibrocystic breast disease because an imbalance of estrogen in the body leads to increased cell growth and fluid retention in the breast tissue, causing painful lumps and cysts to form.
  • Genetic predisposition: Genetic predisposition is a cause of fibrocystic breast disease because women who have a family history of the condition are more likely to inherit certain genetic traits that can lead to the development of fibrocysts and other symptoms associated with the disease.
  • Stress: Stress triggers the release of stress hormones like cortisol and adrenaline, which can cause changes in the body's hormonal balance, leading to the formation of fluid-filled cysts in the breast tissue, which is a common symptom of fibrocystic breast disease.
  • Diet: The body may not be able to break down certain foods properly, which can cause the breast tissue to become inflamed, leading to fibrocystic breast disease.
  • Sodium intake: High sodium intake can cause the body to retain more fluid, which may put pressure on the delicate breast tissue and lead to fibrocystic breast disease symptoms such as painful lumps and swelling.

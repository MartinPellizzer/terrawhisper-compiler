What herbal creams you should avoid if you have nipple pain during breastfeeding?

If you're experiencing nipple pain during breastfeeding, it's essential to avoid certain herbal creams that can exacerbate the issue.

One such herb to steer clear of is Witch Hazel (Hamamelis virginiana's relative - Hamamelis spp. ), which can be too drying for the nipples. This can make the pain worse and even cause cracks in the skin.

Similarly, you should also avoid creams containing Turmeric (Curcuma longa) as it can be too irritating and may cause further discomfort. Another herb to be cautious of is Garlic (Allium sativum), which can be too strong for sensitive nipple skin and cause more pain. Additionally, some creams may contain Ginger (Zingiber officinale), which, although generally considered safe, can be too spicy for some people's skin and worsen the nipple pain. Finally, creams containing Tea Tree Oil (Melaleuca alternifolia) may not be suitable for nipple care, as it can be too harsh and cause more irritation.

These herbs may be safe for other purposes, but for nipple pain during breastfeeding, it's best to choose creams with gentler, more soothing ingredients.

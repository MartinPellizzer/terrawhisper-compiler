What Are The Main Causes Of Low Sperm Count?

Low sperm count is a common issue in men, affecting their fertility and ability to have children.

There are several reasons why this happens. One main cause is varicocele, which is a swelling of the veins in the scrotum. These veins are like tiny tubes that carry blood away from the testicles. When they swell, it can cause the testicles to heat up, affecting the sperm's ability to develop and move properly. Varicocele is often caused by a blockage in the veins, which can lead to a decrease in sperm count. Another cause of low sperm count is hypogonadism. This is a condition where the testicles do not produce enough testosterone, a hormone that helps sperm develop.

Without enough testosterone, the testicles may not be able to produce enough sperm, leading to low sperm count. Hypogonadism can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, injury, or certain medical conditions. Cryptorchidism is also a cause of low sperm count. This is a condition where one or both testicles do not move down into their proper position in the scrotum during fetal development. When the testicles do not move down, it can cause the sperm to develop abnormally, leading to a decrease in sperm count. Cryptorchidism can also increase the risk of testicular torsion, a serious condition where the testicle twists and cuts off its own blood supply. In these conditions, the body's ability to produce and develop sperm is affected, leading to low sperm count.

If left untreated, these conditions can cause long-term fertility issues and make it difficult to conceive a child.

Below are the main causes of low sperm count.

  • Varicocele: Varicocele is a cause of low sperm count because the twisted veins in the scrotum can cause the testicles to become too warm, which can damage the sperm and reduce their production and quality.
  • Hypogonadism: Hypogonadism, a medical condition where the testes do not produce enough testosterone, can lead to low sperm count because the lack of this hormone is necessary for the development and maturation of sperm in the testes.
  • Cryptorchidism: Cryptorchidism, a condition where one or both testicles fail to descend into the scrotum, can lead to low sperm count because it may cause damage to the developing testicles, reduce sperm production, and disrupt hormone balance, which are all essential for normal fertility.
  • Smoking: Smoking damages the blood vessels and harms the testicles, reducing the production of sperm and leading to low sperm count due to the lack of oxygen and nutrients that are essential for sperm growth.
  • Obesity: Obesity can be a cause of low sperm count because excess body fat, especially in the abdomen, can raise the temperature of the testicles, which is essential for sperm production, and this can disrupt the normal process of sperm creation and development.
  • Heat stress: Heat stress can cause damage to the testicles and harm the sperm-making cells, leading to a low sperm count because the high temperatures can alter the delicate balance of the sperm's development and quality.

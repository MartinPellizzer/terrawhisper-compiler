What Are The Main Causes Of Premenstrual Syndrome?

During the week or two before a woman's period, many women experience physical and emotional symptoms that are known as premenstrual syndrome (PMS).

The main causes of PMS are related to hormonal changes in the body. One of the main causes is hormonal fluctuation, specifically the drop in progesterone levels and the increase in estrogen levels. This hormonal change can cause mood swings, anxiety, and irritability. Another cause of PMS is the fluctuation of serotonin levels.

Serotonin is a chemical in the brain that helps to regulate mood. When serotonin levels drop, it can lead to feelings of sadness, depression, and anxiety. This is because serotonin helps to balance the body's response to stress and other hormones. Estrogen levels also play a role in PMS. Estrogen is a hormone that helps to regulate the menstrual cycle and can cause water retention, breast tenderness, and mood swings.

When estrogen levels surge before a period, it can cause these symptoms to become more severe.

Below are the main causes of premenstrual syndrome.

  • Hormonal Fluctuation: Hormonal fluctuation is a cause of premenstrual syndrome because during the menstrual cycle, the levels of estrogen and progesterone hormones in the body rise and fall, causing mood swings, breast tenderness, and other symptoms that are typical of PMS.
  • Serotonin Level: During the premenstrual phase, the fluctuating levels of serotonin in the brain are a cause of premenstrual syndrome because serotonin, a neurotransmitter that regulates mood, appetite, and sleep, naturally drops in women, leading to symptoms like anxiety, mood swings, and fatigue.
  • Estrogen: Estrogen is a cause of premenstrual syndrome because its levels rise sharply before menstruation, causing water retention, mood swings, and physical symptoms that can lead to PMS symptoms.
  • Progesterone: Progesterone is a hormone that rises before menstruation and can cause premenstrual syndrome (PMS) symptoms like mood swings, bloating, and breast tenderness because it affects the brain's neurotransmitters, such as serotonin, which help regulate emotions and other bodily functions.
  • Stress: Stress triggers the release of hormones like cortisol and adrenaline, which can affect the balance of other hormones in the body, leading to symptoms such as mood swings, bloating, and breast tenderness in women with premenstrual syndrome.
  • Magnesium Deficiency: Magnesium deficiency is a cause of premenstrual syndrome because it can lead to hormonal imbalances, particularly an increase in estrogen levels, which can cause symptoms such as mood swings, bloating, and breast tenderness.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Premenstrual Syndrome?

If you have premenstrual syndrome (PMS), some herbs can make symptoms worse.

Avoid Ashwagandha, as it can increase anxiety and stress levels. It's also not suitable for women with thyroid problems, which can be a trigger for PMS. Fenugreek, another herb to avoid, can cause hormonal imbalances that worsen PMS symptoms. It can also affect blood sugar levels and interact with medications. Dong Quai is not recommended for women with PMS, as it can increase estrogen levels, which can worsen symptoms like bloating and mood swings. It can also interact with blood thinners and other medications.

Saw Palmetto can worsen PMS symptoms by affecting hormone balance and causing anxiety. It's also not recommended for women with thyroid problems or those taking certain medications. Chasteberry is often recommended for PMS, but it's not suitable for everyone. Women who are pregnant or breastfeeding should avoid it, as it can affect hormone levels. If you have kidney problems or high blood pressure, consult your doctor before using Chasteberry. Remember to consult your doctor before using any herbs, especially if you have pre-existing medical conditions or take medications.

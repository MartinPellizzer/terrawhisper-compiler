9 Herbal Essential Oils For Amenorrhea

For example, Lavandula angustifolia (lavender) oil has a calming effect, which can help reduce stress and anxiety that may be contributing to amenorrhea.

Angelica archangelica oil, on the other hand, is known for its ability to regulate hormonal balance and stimulate menstrual flow. Vitex agnus-castus oil, also known as chasteberry, has been shown to have a positive effect on menstrual irregularities and can help regulate the menstrual cycle. Drinking herbal teas made from these herbs may help alleviate symptoms of amenorrhea and restore a regular menstrual cycle. This can bring relief from physical and emotional discomfort, as well as improve overall well-being.

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, which are the main bioactive constituents responsible for its therapeutic properties.

These compounds have a regulatory effect on the endocrine system, which helps to balance hormones and stimulate menstrual cycles. The antispasmodic and anti-anxiety properties of Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can also help to reduce stress and promote relaxation, both of which are essential for regular menstrual cycles. Additionally, the oil's ability to improve sleep quality and reduce inflammation can help to alleviate some of the underlying causes of amenorrhea.

By promoting hormonal balance and reducing stress, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils can help to support the body's natural menstrual cycles.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 1 cup of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan over low heat until it starts to simmer.
  3. Add the Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the saucepan and let them steep for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the extracted essential oil in a cool, dark place and use 2-3 drops in a carrier oil for amenorrhea relief.

2. Angelica archangelica

Angelica archangelica essential oils contains bioactive constituents like ferulic acid, sinapic acid, and sesquiterpenes.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help regulate hormonal balance and reduce stress. The oils also contain angelan, a polysaccharide that stimulates the pituitary gland, promoting the release of gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH) and helping to regulate menstrual cycles. The anti-inflammatory properties of ferulic acid and sinapic acid may also help reduce inflammation in the reproductive organs, which can contribute to amenorrhea.

By regulating hormonal balance and reducing inflammation, Angelica archangelica essential oils may help alleviate symptoms of amenorrhea.

  1. Gather Angelica archangelica roots (about 1 cup) and a carrier oil like jojoba or sweet almond oil (1/2 cup).
  2. Clean and dry the Angelica roots. Then, chop them into small pieces.
  3. Fill a saucepan with water (2 cups) and bring it to a boil. Reduce heat and add the chopped Angelica roots.
  4. Steep the Angelica roots for 30-40 minutes. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl.
  5. Mix the strained liquid with the carrier oil (1:10 ratio) and store the mixture in a dark glass bottle. Let it sit for 2-3 weeks, shaking the bottle daily, before straining and discarding the solids.

3. Vitex agnus-castus

Vitex agnus-castus essential oils contains iridoid glycosides and flavonoids as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds interact with the body's hormonal system, particularly with the pituitary gland, to regulate menstrual cycles and help alleviate symptoms of amenorrhea. The essential oil's properties, such as its estrogen-regulating effects and ability to stimulate the production of gonadotropin-releasing hormone, support the return of menstrual cycles. Additionally, Vitex agnus-castus essential oil's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties may also contribute to its therapeutic effects on the reproductive system.

By influencing hormonal balances and promoting a healthy menstrual cycle, Vitex agnus-castus essential oils may help address amenorrhea in some individuals.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Vitex agnus-castus leaves and flowers. Crush them into small pieces.
  2. Combine 1 cup of the crushed plant material with 2 cups of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil) in a clean glass jar.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Vitex agnus-castus essential oil in a dark glass bottle and use it as needed. Start with a small amount (5-7 drops) and consult with a healthcare professional for proper usage.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains a compound called gingerol and shogaol, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help regulate menstrual cycles and alleviate symptoms of amenorrhea. The warm, spicy, and invigorating properties of Zingiber officinale essential oils can also help stimulate blood flow to the reproductive organs, promoting hormonal balance and menstrual regularity. Additionally, the essential oils' ability to modulate the body's stress response and promote relaxation can help alleviate emotional stress, a common contributor to amenorrhea.

Regular use of Zingiber officinale essential oils may help restore menstrual cycles and promote overall reproductive health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale roots, 2 cups of water, and a steam distillation apparatus.
  2. Wash the roots with 1 cup of water to remove dirt. Drain well.
  3. Place the roots in the steam distillation apparatus and steam with 2 cups of water for 2 hours.
  4. Collect the essential oil in a separate container, using a separator to remove water.
  5. Filter the essential oil through a coffee filter to remove impurities. Store in a dark glass bottle.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils contains triterpenoid saponins, particularly glycyrrhizin, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce stress and anxiety that can cause amenorrhea. Glycyrrhizin also has a balancing effect on the body's hormonal system, which can help regulate menstrual cycles. The anti-stress and adaptogenic properties of Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils may also help to normalize cortisol levels, which can be out of balance in women with amenorrhea.

By reducing stress and regulating hormones, Glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils can help to alleviate symptoms of amenorrhea.

  1. Gather 500ml of Glycyrrhiza glabra roots. Clean and dry them.
  2. Use a 1:5 ratio (roots:carrier oil) and combine 100g of Glycyrrhiza glabra roots with 500ml of carrier oil (such as coconut or olive oil).
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the mixture every day.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter. Discard the roots and keep the liquid.
  5. Transfer the liquid to a dark glass bottle. Store it in a cool, dark place and use it within 6 months.

6. Paeonia lactiflora

Paeonia lactiflora essential oils contains paenol and benzoyl ester as its active constituents, which are known for their uterine-stimulating properties.

These constituents help to regulate menstrual cycles and stimulate blood flow to the reproductive organs, addressing amenorrhea. Paenol, in particular, has been shown to have a positive effect on the uterus, promoting contractions and helping to restore normal menstrual function. The essential oil's anti-inflammatory properties also play a role in reducing pain and discomfort associated with amenorrhea.

By promoting uterine contractions and reducing inflammation, Paeonia lactiflora essential oils may help to alleviate amenorrhea symptoms.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Paeonia lactiflora flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (like jojoba or sweet almond oil)
  2. Wash the flowers with 1 cup of water to remove dirt and impurities. Dry them with a clean towel
  3. Combine the dried flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well to mix
  4. Leave the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the oil to infuse
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the flowers and store the infused oil in a dark bottle

7. Avena sativa

Avena sativa essential oils contains a blend of bioactive constituents, including avenacoside, avenalol, and avenasterol.

These compounds have been found to have a regulating effect on the hormonal balance, which is often disrupted in cases of amenorrhea. Avenacoside, in particular, has been shown to stimulate the production of estrogen, a hormone essential for menstrual cycle regulation. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of avenasterol and avenalol also contribute to the overall health of the reproductive system, helping to alleviate symptoms of amenorrhea.

By promoting hormonal balance and reducing inflammation, Avena sativa essential oils may help to regulate menstrual cycles in individuals with amenorrhea.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Avena sativa (oat) seeds and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or jojoba oil).
  2. Combine the dried Avena sativa seeds and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Make sure the seeds are fully covered by the oil.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day to help the oil extract from the seeds.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the extracted oil to a dark glass bottle and label it. Use 2-3 drops of the oil in a carrier oil for aromatherapy or topical use.

8. Cimicifuga racemosa

Cimicifuga racemosa essential oils contains bioactive constituents like ferulic acid, sesquiterpenes, and lignans.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help to relax the uterine muscles and regulate menstrual cycles. The lignans in Cimicifuga racemosa essential oils also act as a phytoestrogen, mimicking the effects of estrogen in the body and helping to stimulate menstrual flow. The sesquiterpenes have a mild sedative effect, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety that can contribute to amenorrhea.

By regulating the body's hormonal balance and relaxing the uterine muscles, Cimicifuga racemosa essential oils can help to restore normal menstrual function.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Cimicifuga racemosa roots and 2 cups of carrier oil, such as jojoba oil.
  2. Dry the roots in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 2 hours or air-dry them for 2 days.
  3. Grind the dried roots into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
  4. Mix 1 tablespoon of the root powder with 1 cup of carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Stir well.
  5. Store the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day. Then, strain and discard the solids.

9. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains linalool, geraniol, and borneol as its main bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to soothe the uterus and regulate menstrual cycles. Melissa officinalis essential oils also has a balancing effect on the hormonal system, which is essential in treating amenorrhea caused by hormonal imbalances. The antispasmodic properties of borneol and geraniol help to relax the uterine muscles and promote regular menstrual flow.

By promoting relaxation and regulating hormonal balance, Melissa officinalis essential oils may help to alleviate the symptoms of amenorrhea.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Melissa officinalis flowers, a glass jar with a lid, and a carrier oil like jojoba or sweet almond oil.
  2. Combine 1 cup of Melissa flowers with 1 cup of carrier oil in the glass jar. Store in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  3. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container. Discard the solids.
  4. Transfer the liquid mixture to a dark glass bottle with a dropper lid. Use 10-15 drops as needed for amenorrhea.
  5. Label the bottle with the date, ingredients, and recommended usage. Store in a cool, dark place to preserve the oil.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent amenorrhea from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help some women with amenorrhea.

Certain teas, like ginger and peppermint, can help regulate menstrual cycles. However, the effect may vary from person to person.

Some studies suggest that these teas can help stimulate menstrual flow, but more research is needed to confirm their benefits.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for amenorrhea every day?

Herbal teas can help with amenorrhea, but it's not entirely safe to drink them daily.

Some herbs, like pennyroyal and sage, can interfere with hormone production or worsen the condition. It's also possible to develop allergic reactions or interact with other medications.

Moderation is key, so limit consumption and discuss with a healthcare professional.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in amenorrhea?

Herbal teas can help with amenorrhea, but results vary from person to person.

Some women see improvements in their menstrual cycle within a few months, while others may take longer.

It's not uncommon for changes to occur in 3 to 6 months, but it's also possible for it to take up to a year or more to notice significant effects.

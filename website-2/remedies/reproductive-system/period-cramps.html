What are the causes of period cramps?

When you get your period, you might experience painful cramps.

There are several reasons why this happens. One cause is hormonal imbalance. Your body has a delicate balance of hormones, including estrogen and progesterone. When these hormones are out of balance, it can lead to cramps. Another cause is uterine contractions. Your uterus is a muscle that contracts to help shed its lining when you get your period. These contractions can be painful, especially if they're strong.

Prostaglandins also play a role in period cramps. Prostaglandins are chemical messengers that help your body prepare for a period. They cause your uterus to contract, which can lead to pain. Ovulation can also contribute to period cramps. When you ovulate, your body releases an egg from your ovary, and this can cause your uterus to contract. Lastly, the uterine lining itself can cause cramps. When you get your period, your body sheds its uterine lining, and this can be painful.

The lining is made of a thick layer of cells that help nourish a fertilized egg, but when it's shed, it can cause cramps.

What herbs should you avoid if you have period cramps?

If you're experiencing period cramps, it's best to steer clear of Cinchona officinalis.

This herb contains compounds that can stimulate the uterus and make cramps worse. Paeonia lactiflora, on the other hand, is often used to treat menstrual issues, but it can also have the opposite effect if you're already experiencing severe cramps.

It can slow down blood flow and worsen the cramping. Vitex agnus-castus is sometimes used to regulate menstrual cycles, but if you're already experiencing period cramps, it can disrupt your hormone balance and make cramps more intense. Aristolochia clematitis contains a toxic compound that can harm your kidneys and worsen menstrual issues, including cramps.

Hydrastis canadensis, also known as goldenseal, can cause uterine contractions, which can make period cramps more severe.

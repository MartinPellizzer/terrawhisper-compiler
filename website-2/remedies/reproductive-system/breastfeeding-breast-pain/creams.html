1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce swelling and pain in the breast. The saponins in Calendula officinalis also have a soothing effect on the skin, which can help to ease discomfort and promote healing. The antioxidants in the cream help to protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, further reducing inflammation and pain.

By applying Calendula officinalis creams to the affected area, breastfeeding women may experience relief from breast pain and discomfort.

  1. Gather Calendula officinalis flowers, coconut oil, beeswax, and a double boiler.
  2. Dry 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers and set aside.
  3. Combine 1/4 cup of coconut oil and 2 tablespoons of beeswax in a double boiler.
  4. Add the dried Calendula flowers to the coconut oil and beeswax mixture. Heat until beeswax melts.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a bowl. Let cool and harden. Use as a topical cream for breastfeeding breast pain.

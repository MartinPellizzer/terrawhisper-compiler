What herbal essential oils you should avoid if you have menopause?

If you're going through menopause, it's best to avoid certain herbal essential oils that may worsen symptoms or interact with medications.

One such oil is Sage (Salvia officinalis), which contains estrogen-like compounds that can stimulate the body's estrogen receptors, potentially worsening hot flashes and night sweats. Another oil to avoid is Ginkgo biloba, as it may increase the risk of bleeding due to its blood-thinning properties, which can be problematic for women taking hormone replacement therapy (HRT) or blood thinners.

Additionally, Peppermint (Mentha piperita) oil may not be suitable for menopausal women with anxiety or insomnia, as it can stimulate the nervous system and worsen these symptoms. Finally, Ginger (Zingiber officinale) oil should be used with caution, as it may increase heart rate and blood pressure, which can be concerning for women with pre-existing heart conditions.

It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional or a certified aromatherapist before using any herbal essential oils, especially if you're taking medications or have underlying health conditions.

What herbal teas you should avoid if you have menopause?

If you're experiencing menopause, there are some herbal teas you should avoid.

Sage tea, for example, contains compounds that may worsen hot flashes and night sweats. Also, avoid teas made from St. John's Wort, as it can interact with medications like hormone replacement therapy (HRT) or birth control pills, causing unintended side effects.

Dong quai tea may stimulate the uterus, which could lead to heavy bleeding or worsen conditions like endometriosis. Peppermint tea, while generally safe, may cause stomach upset in some women, especially if taken in large quantities. Goldenseal tea contains a compound called berberine, which can lower blood sugar levels and interact with diabetes medications.

Finally, avoid teas made from evening primrose oil, as its omega-6 fatty acids may stimulate the body to produce more estrogen, potentially worsening menopausal symptoms.

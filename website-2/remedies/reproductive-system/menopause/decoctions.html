What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have menopause?

If you're experiencing menopause, there are some herbal decoctions you should avoid.

One of these is St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum), which can interact with hormone replacement therapy (HRT) and affect its effectiveness. St. John's Wort also increases the risk of bleeding, which can be a problem during menopause. Another herb to avoid is Yohimbe (Pausinystalia johimbe), which can increase heart rate and blood pressure. This can be a concern for women with high blood pressure or heart problems, which are common during menopause.

Furthermore, Ephedra (Ephedra sinica) and Ma Huang (Ephedra sinica) should be avoided due to their potential to raise blood pressure and heart rate. Another herb to steer clear of is Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis), which can interact with estrogen replacement therapy and affect its effectiveness. Lastly, Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa) should be used with caution. Some studies suggest that it may increase the risk of liver damage, especially when used in high doses or with other medications.

It's also essential to note that Black Cohosh may interact with other medications, including blood thinners and HRT.

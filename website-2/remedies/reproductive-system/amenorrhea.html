What Are The Main Causes Of Amenorrhea?

Amenorrhea is a medical condition where a woman does not get her menstrual period.

There are many causes of amenorrhea, but some of the main ones include Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS), Hypothyroidism, and Anorexia Nervosa. Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder that affects a woman's ovaries. In PCOS, the ovaries produce high levels of androgens, which are male hormones. These high hormone levels can prevent the ovaries from releasing eggs, leading to amenorrhea. PCOS is also often associated with weight gain, acne, and excess hair growth. Hypothyroidism is a condition where the thyroid gland does not produce enough thyroid hormones.

Thyroid hormones play a crucial role in regulating the menstrual cycle. When the thyroid gland does not produce enough hormones, it can disrupt the menstrual cycle, leading to amenorrhea. Hypothyroidism can be caused by a number of factors, including autoimmune disorders and iodine deficiency. Anorexia Nervosa is a psychological eating disorder where a person starves themselves to be thin. In severe cases, Anorexia Nervosa can lead to amenorrhea. When a woman does not eat enough food, her body does not produce enough estrogen, which is essential for menstrual cycle. Without enough estrogen, the ovaries do not release eggs, leading to amenorrhea.

Anorexia Nervosa can also cause other health problems, including malnutrition, osteoporosis, and heart problems.

Below are the main causes of amenorrhea.

  • Polycystic Ovary: Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) can cause amenorrhea because the high levels of androgens in the body disrupt the normal menstrual cycle, preventing the ovaries from releasing an egg and leading to infrequent or absent periods.
  • Hypothyroidism: Hypothyroidism causes amenorrhea because the thyroid hormone, which helps regulate the body's growth and development, slows down the production of hormones needed for ovulation and menstruation.
  • Anorexia Nervosa: Anorexia Nervosa is a cause of amenorrhea because the severe weight loss and low body fat that occur with this eating disorder can disrupt the normal functioning of the body's hormones, specifically the lack of estrogen caused by not having enough body fat, which is necessary for a woman's menstrual cycle to occur.
  • Turner Syndrome: Turner Syndrome is a cause of amenorrhea because it disrupts the normal development of the ovaries, resulting in a lack of estrogen production and therefore the ovaries not producing eggs, which leads to the absence of menstrual cycles.
  • Asherman Syndrome: Asherman Syndrome is a cause of amenorrhea because scar tissue forms in the uterus, blocking the flow of menstrual blood and preventing periods from occurring.
  • Premature Ovarian Failure: Premature Ovarian Failure, also known as premature ovarian insufficiency, is a cause of amenorrhea because the ovaries fail to produce eggs and hormones, leading to a lack of menstrual periods in women before the age of 40.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Amenorrhea?

If you have amenorrhea, it's essential to avoid certain herbs that may worsen the condition or interact with medications.

One herb to avoid is St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum), as it can lower estrogen levels and worsen hormonal imbalances that lead to amenorrhea. Another herb to steer clear of is Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra), which can cause high blood pressure and interact with medications, potentially worsening the condition.

Additionally, Saw palmetto (Serenoa repens) may also be problematic, as it can affect hormone levels and worsen amenorrhea. Furthermore, Dong quai (Polygonum multiflorum), although often used to regulate menstrual cycles, can actually worsen amenorrhea in some cases, especially if taken in large amounts. Finally, Red clover (Trifolium pratense) is another herb to avoid, as it contains isoflavones that can mimic estrogen in the body, potentially worsening hormonal imbalances.

If you're considering using herbs to manage amenorrhea, consult with a healthcare professional to determine the best course of action.

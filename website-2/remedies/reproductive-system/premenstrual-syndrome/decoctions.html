What herbal decoctions you should avoid if you have premenstrual syndrome?

If you have premenstrual syndrome (PMS), there are some herbal decoctions you should avoid.

One such herb is 'Hydrastis canadensis' or goldenseal, which may interact with birth control pills and make them less effective. This could lead to unintended pregnancy. Another herb to be cautious with is 'Glycyrrhiza glabra' or licorice root, which can cause high blood pressure and worsen conditions like heart disease.

'Artemisia absinthium' or wormwood is also not recommended, as it can cause stomach problems and worsen symptoms of PMS like bloating and cramps. 'Euonymus europaea' or spindle tree is another herb to avoid, as it can cause dizziness and nausea. Lastly, 'Taxus baccata' or yew is highly toxic and can cause serious health problems, including death, if ingested.

It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal decoctions, especially if you have PMS or are taking medication.

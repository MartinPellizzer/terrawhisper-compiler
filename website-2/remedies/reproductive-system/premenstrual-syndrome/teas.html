What herbal teas you should avoid if you have premenstrual syndrome?

If you have premenstrual syndrome (PMS), there are some herbal teas you should avoid.

One such tea is 'Ephedra sinica', also known as ma huang. This tea can increase blood pressure and heart rate, which may not be suitable for people with PMS who may already experience mood swings and other symptoms. Another tea to avoid is 'Ginkgo biloba'. While it is often used to improve memory and concentration, it can also increase the risk of bleeding, which may be a concern for people with PMS who may be prone to heavy bleeding during their periods.

Also, 'Glycyrrhiza glabra', or licorice root tea, is not recommended for people with PMS. It can cause blood pressure to rise and may interact with medications, such as blood thinners. Additionally, 'Silybum marianum', or milk thistle tea, should be avoided by people with PMS who are taking estrogen replacement therapy. This is because milk thistle may decrease the effectiveness of estrogen replacement therapy.

Finally, 'Taraxacum officinale', or dandelion tea, can cause stomach upset and interact with medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, which may be a concern for people with PMS who may already experience gastrointestinal symptoms and other health issues.

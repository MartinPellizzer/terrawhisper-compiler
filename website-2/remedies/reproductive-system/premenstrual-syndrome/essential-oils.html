1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains linalool and linalyl acetate, two key bioactive constituents that contribute to its therapeutic properties.

These compounds have a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help alleviate symptoms of premenstrual syndrome (PMS), such as anxiety and irritability. The anti-inflammatory properties of linalool and linalyl acetate may also help reduce breast tenderness and other physical symptoms associated with PMS. Additionally, the sedative properties of these constituents can promote relaxation and improve sleep quality, which is often disrupted during the premenstrual phase.

By reducing stress and promoting relaxation, Lavandula angustifolia essential oils may help mitigate the symptoms of PMS.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Lavandula angustifolia flowers and 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Heat the water in the saucepan until it reaches 212°F (100°C).
  3. Add the Lavandula angustifolia flowers to the saucepan and let them steep for 1 hour.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass jar.
  5. Store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid. Use 10 drops in a diffuser or 5 drops in a carrier oil for PMS relief.

2. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains borneol, limonene, and linalool as its bioactive constituents.

These compounds possess anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and anxiolytic properties, which help to alleviate symptoms associated with premenstrual syndrome (PMS). Borneol, in particular, has been shown to reduce inflammation and ease cramps, while limonene has a calming effect on the nervous system, reducing anxiety and stress. Linalool, a natural sedative, helps to balance hormones and regulate menstrual cycles, leading to a reduction in PMS symptoms such as mood swings and irritability.

By incorporating Melissa officinalis essential oils into your routine, you can benefit from these properties and find relief from PMS symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Melissa officinalis flowers and 2 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Heat the water in the pot to 100°F (38°C), then add the Melissa flowers.
  3. Let the mixture simmer for 2 hours, then strain it to get the liquid.
  4. Use a still or a distillation kit to separate the essential oil from the liquid.
  5. Bottle the extracted essential oil and store it in a cool, dark place.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale essential oils contains bioactive constituents like gingerol and shogaol, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of premenstrual syndrome (PMS) like bloating and mood swings. Ginger's analgesic properties can also help ease cramps and discomfort associated with PMS. Additionally, the essential oil's vasodilatory properties can help improve blood flow, reducing swelling and promoting relaxation.

By using Zingiber officinale essential oils, women may experience relief from PMS symptoms due to its combination of anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and analgesic properties.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Zingiber officinale roots (about 6-8 roots). Clean and dry them.
  2. Use a glass jar with a lid and add 1 cup of carrier oil, like coconut or olive oil. Add the Zingiber officinale roots to the jar.
  3. Steep the roots in the carrier oil for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place. Shake the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Zingiber officinale essential oil in a glass bottle with a dropper lid. Use 5-7 drops as needed to relieve premenstrual syndrome symptoms.

4. Angelica archangelica

Angelica archangelica essential oils contains bioactive constituents like angelan, ferulic acid, and sesquiterpenes, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, which can contribute to symptoms of premenstrual syndrome. The essential oil's ability to relax and calm the body and mind can also help alleviate symptoms like anxiety and mood swings associated with PMS. Additionally, the oil's antispasmodic properties can help relieve cramps and spasms, providing relief from physical discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Angelica archangelica essential oils may help alleviate some of the symptoms of premenstrual syndrome.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Angelica archangelica roots and 2 cups of carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  2. Chop the roots into small pieces and transfer them to a clean glass jar.
  3. Pour the carrier oil over the chopped roots, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This allows the roots to infuse the oil.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils contains carnosic acid, beta-pinene, and camphor as its key bioactive constituents.

These compounds possess anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help alleviate menstrual cramps and discomfort associated with premenstrual syndrome (PMS). The antispasmodic effects of camphor and beta-pinene can also help relax the uterine muscles, reducing spasms and pain. By reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation, Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils may provide relief from PMS symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of water.
  2. Combine the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and water in a saucepan and heat on low for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and let it steep for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a glass container.
  5. Use a distillation kit or a simple still to separate the essential oil from the water, then store the oil in a dark glass bottle.

11. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains flavonoids, terpenoids, and bilobalide, which are the active constituents that contribute to its therapeutic properties.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of premenstrual syndrome (PMS) such as breast tenderness and mood swings. The flavonoids in Ginkgo biloba essential oils, including quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to reduce inflammation and improve blood flow, which may help ease PMS-related symptoms. Additionally, the terpenoids and bilobalide in Ginkgo biloba essential oils have a balancing effect on the body's hormonal system, which can help regulate menstrual cycles and reduce PMS symptoms.

By using Ginkgo biloba essential oils, women can potentially experience relief from PMS symptoms and improve their overall well-being.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Ginkgo biloba leaves and 2 cups of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil) in a clean glass bowl.
  2. Heat 1 cup of water in a saucepan until it reaches 100°F (38°C), then remove from heat.
  3. Add 1/4 cup of Ginkgo biloba leaves to the heated water and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the water through a cheesecloth into the glass bowl with carrier oil, discarding the leaves.
  5. Add the infused water to the carrier oil and mix well. Store the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the bowl daily, before straining and bottling the Ginkgo biloba essential oil.

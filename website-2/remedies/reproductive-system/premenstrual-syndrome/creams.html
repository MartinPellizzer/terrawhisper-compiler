9 Soothing Herbal Creams for Adult Women Suffering from Premenstrual Syndrome

For many adult women, premenstrual syndrome (PMS) is a common and often frustrating experience.

It's caused by hormonal changes that occur in the days leading up to a woman's period, when levels of estrogen and progesterone drop. This can lead to a range of symptoms, including bloating, mood swings, breast tenderness, and fatigue. For some women, PMS can be mild, but for others, it can be severe and disrupt daily life. Women with PMS may find it hard to concentrate, experience irritability, or feel overwhelmed by everyday tasks. They may also struggle with relationships, work, or even simple things like cooking and cleaning.

As a result, PMS can take a toll on a woman's mental and physical health, affecting her self-esteem, relationships, and overall well-being. Herbal creams have been used for centuries to help alleviate the symptoms of PMS. These creams are topical, meaning they're applied directly to the skin, rather than ingested. They contain plant-based ingredients that work to calm and soothe the body, helping to reduce symptoms like bloating, cramps, and mood swings. By applying these creams to specific areas of the body, women can target the root causes of their symptoms and find relief. The creams work by interacting with the body's natural chemistry, promoting relaxation and reducing inflammation.

This can help women feel more in control of their symptoms, rather than being controlled by them.

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams are a popular natural remedy for premenstrual syndrome (PMS).

The creams contain the essential oil of lavender, which is rich in properties that help to ease PMS symptoms. The main constituents of lavender oil include linalool, linalyl acetate, and camphor, which have anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and sedative effects. These properties help to calm the mind and body, reducing anxiety, mood swings, and cramps associated with PMS. To use Lavandula angustifolia creams for PMS, apply a small amount to the skin, such as on the wrists, temples, or abdomen, 1-2 times a day. You can also add a few drops of lavender oil to your bath water for a relaxing soak.

To get the most out of the creams, try to use them consistently during the premenstrual phase, ideally starting 3-5 days before your period is expected. It's also essential to combine the creams with other relaxation techniques, such as deep breathing, meditation, or yoga, to enhance their effectiveness. As you use the Lavandula angustifolia creams, you may start to notice a reduction in your PMS symptoms, such as decreased anxiety, improved mood, and reduced cramps. Some people also report improved sleep quality and a sense of calmness. The creams may also help to reduce stress and promote relaxation, making it easier to manage PMS.

However, keep in mind that everyone's experience may vary, and it's essential to be patient and consistent with your use.

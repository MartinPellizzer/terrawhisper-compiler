While more research is needed, some women have reported finding relief from their symptoms through acupuncture.

What other ailments people who have endometriosis pain are likely to experience?

People with endometriosis often experience other health issues that can be helped with medicinal herbs.

Many of these individuals have irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), which is marked by bloating, abdominal pain, and changes in bowel movements. Herbs like peppermint and ginger can ease digestive discomfort and reduce inflammation. Women with endometriosis are also prone to anxiety and depression, which can be managed with herbs like passionflower and St. John's Wort.

Passionflower can calm the nervous system, while St. John's Wort can help regulate mood. Hormonal imbalances, common in those with endometriosis, can be addressed with herbs like maca and licorice root. Maca can balance estrogen levels, while licorice root can reduce inflammation and promote hormonal balance. Additionally, people with endometriosis may experience chronic fatigue and sleep disturbances.

Herbs like ashwagandha and valerian root can combat fatigue and promote relaxation, allowing for better sleep.

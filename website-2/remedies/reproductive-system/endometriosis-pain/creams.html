What herbal creams you should avoid if you have endometriosis pain?

If you have endometriosis pain, you should avoid herbal creams that contain herbs like 'Ruta graveolens', also known as rue, and 'Comfrey' (Symphytum officinale).

Ruta graveolens may irritate the skin and worsen endometriosis symptoms. Comfrey is a blood thinner and may increase the risk of bleeding, which is a concern for people with endometriosis who may experience heavy menstrual bleeding. Another herb to avoid is 'Sanguinaria canadensis', also known as bloodroot, which can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions.

'Papaver somniferum', or poppy seeds, may contain small amounts of morphine, which can cause drowsiness and interact with other medications. 'Thymus vulgaris', or thyme, can be irritating to the skin and may cause allergic reactions in some people. Lastly, 'Vitex agnus-castus', or chaste tree, may have estrogen-like effects, which could worsen endometriosis symptoms.

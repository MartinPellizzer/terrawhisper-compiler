8 Herbal Tinctures For Uterine Fibroids

Herbal tinctures are becoming a popular natural remedy for uterine fibroids.

The herbs Angelica sinensis, Ginkgo biloba, and Vitex agnus-castus are commonly used for this purpose. Angelica sinensis, also known as dong quai, has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to nourish the blood and regulate menstrual cycles. It's thought to help reduce fibroid size and alleviate symptoms like heavy bleeding and pain. Ginkgo biloba is known for its anti-inflammatory properties, which may help to reduce fibroid size and alleviate symptoms. It may also help to improve blood flow to the uterus, reducing pain and discomfort.

Vitex agnus-castus, or chaste tree, is often used to regulate menstrual cycles and alleviate symptoms of PMS. It may help to reduce fibroid size and alleviate symptoms like heavy bleeding and pain. These herbs can be made into a tincture by steeping the dried roots, leaves, or flowers in a solvent like vodka or glycerin. The resulting liquid can be taken sublingually (under the tongue) or added to water or tea. Some people find that herbal tinctures are more effective than teas because the solvent helps to extract the active compounds from the herbs. Taking herbal tinctures for uterine fibroids can bring many benefits to your life. By reducing symptoms and alleviating discomfort, you may be able to return to your normal activities without feeling held back by your fibroids. You may also be able to reduce your reliance on pain medication and other treatments.

Additionally, herbal tinctures can be a more natural and holistic approach to managing your fibroids, which may appeal to those who prefer to avoid pharmaceuticals or surgery.

1. Angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis tinctures contains the bioactive constituents ferulic acid, ligustilide, and butylphthalide, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

These properties may help reduce inflammation and alleviate cramping associated with uterine fibroids. Ferulic acid, in particular, has been found to inhibit the growth of fibroid cells, while ligustilide and butylphthalide have been shown to relax the uterine muscle and reduce spasms. The antispasmodic properties of these compounds may also help reduce heavy bleeding and alleviate symptoms associated with uterine fibroids.

Regular use of Angelica sinensis tinctures may help reduce the size and number of fibroids, and alleviate symptoms associated with this condition.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Angelica sinensis root, 2 cups of 80-proof vodka, and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine the dried Angelica sinensis root and vodka in the glass jar, making sure the root is completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar with the lid and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Label the tincture and store it in a cool, dark place. Take 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, mixed with water or another liquid.

2. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contains flavonoids, terpenoids, and bilobalide, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the body, which can contribute to the growth of uterine fibroids. Flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to inhibit the growth of fibroid cells and reduce estrogen levels in the body, which can help slow down fibroid growth. Terpenoids, including ginkgolides and bilobalide, have anti-angiogenic properties, which can help prevent the formation of new blood vessels that feed the growth of fibroids.

By reducing inflammation and inhibiting fibroid cell growth, ginkgo biloba tinctures may help alleviate symptoms and reduce the size of uterine fibroids.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Ginkgo biloba leaves, 2 cups of 80 proof vodka, and a clean glass jar.
  2. Combine the Ginkgo biloba leaves and vodka in the glass jar, making sure the leaves are completely covered.
  3. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Use 20-30 drops of the Ginkgo biloba tincture in water 2-3 times a day for uterine fibroids, as directed by a healthcare professional.

3. Vitex agnus-castus

Vitex agnus-castus tinctures contains flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are biologically active compounds that help regulate hormones in the body.

The flavonoids, particularly kaempferol and quercetin, have anti-inflammatory properties that may help reduce swelling and pain associated with uterine fibroids. The alkaloids, including aucubin and vitexic acid, have a regulating effect on hormonal balance, which can help control estrogen levels that are often linked to the growth of fibroids. By regulating hormone balance and reducing inflammation, Vitex agnus-castus tinctures may help alleviate symptoms and slow the growth of uterine fibroids.

The saponins in the tincture have a mild stimulating effect on the pituitary gland, which helps regulate hormone production and may contribute to the tincture's potential benefits for uterine health.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Vitex agnus-castus flowers and 2 cups of 80% ethanol (vodka or brandy can be used as substitutes).
  2. Combine the dried flowers and ethanol in a clean glass jar, making sure the flowers are completely covered.
  3. Steep the mixture for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day, in a cool, dark place.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the tincture to small glass bottles, using 20-30 drops as a typical dose, and store in a cool, dark place.

4. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa tinctures contains a high concentration of curcuminoids, which are the active compounds responsible for its medicinal properties.

The main curcuminoids found in Curcuma longa tinctures are curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin. These compounds have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the body, conditions often associated with uterine fibroids. Curcuminoids also have a potential role in inhibiting the growth of fibroid cells and reducing the production of prostaglandins, which can cause uterine contractions and pain.

By reducing inflammation and inhibiting fibroid growth, Curcuma longa tinctures may help alleviate symptoms and promote a healthier uterine environment.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa roots, 2 cups of 80% vodka, and a glass jar with a lid.
  2. Combine the dried Curcuma longa roots and vodka in the glass jar. Make sure the roots are completely covered by the vodka.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will help the tincture to extract the active compounds from the roots.
  4. Strain the tincture using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into another glass jar. Discard the solids and reserve the liquid.
  5. Store the tincture in the glass jar with the lid tightly closed. Keep it in a cool, dark place and use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, as needed.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra

By reducing inflammation and muscle spasms, Lavandula angustifolia tinctures can help to alleviate symptoms of uterine fibroids.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent uterine fibroids from forming?

Some herbal teas may help prevent uterine fibroids from forming, but the evidence is limited.

Tea like red clover and chasteberry are thought to reduce estrogen levels, which might slow fibroid growth.

However, drinking herbal tea may not completely prevent fibroids from developing, and more research is needed to fully understand its effects.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for uterine fibroids every day?

Some herbal teas, like red clover and raspberry leaf, are said to help with uterine fibroids.

However, consuming them daily may not be safe for everyone. These teas can interact with medications or worsen conditions like heavy bleeding.

It's best to start with small amounts and see how your body reacts.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in uterine fibroids?

Herbal teas may help with uterine fibroids, but it's hard to say exactly how long it takes to see results.

Some people notice improvements in symptoms like heavy bleeding or pain within a few weeks to a few months.

Others may take longer to feel the effects, up to a year or more, as herbal teas can affect people differently.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for uterine fibroids?

Drinking herbal tea for uterine fibroids is best done in the morning or early afternoon. At this time, your body is most active, and the tea's nutrients can work best to help ease symptoms and possibly reduce fibroid size.

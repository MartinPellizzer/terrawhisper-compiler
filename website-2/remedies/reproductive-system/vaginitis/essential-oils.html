Vaginitis in Older Women: 8 Herbal Essential Oils for Comfort

Postmenopausal women often experience vaginal dryness and thinning due to the decrease in estrogen levels after menopause.

This can lead to discomfort, itching, and burning sensations, making everyday activities like sex or even wearing tight clothes unbearable. Vaginitis, an inflammation of the vagina, is a common condition that can cause these symptoms to worsen. The discomfort and pain can be so severe that it affects a woman's self-esteem and quality of life, making it difficult to engage in social activities, exercise, or even work.

Many postmenopausal women turn to over-the-counter treatments, which can have unpleasant side effects or provide only temporary relief. Herbal essential oils have been studied for their potential benefits in treating vaginitis in postmenopausal women. Some essential oils have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help soothe and heal the vaginal tissues. Others have estrogen-like effects, which can help reduce vaginal dryness and atrophy.

These oils can be used topically or added to bath water for relief, providing a natural and non-invasive treatment option.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis essential oils have been traditionally used to help alleviate symptoms of vaginitis.

The main reason for this is due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help reduce redness and swelling in the affected area. This is largely attributed to its high content of triterpene saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids. These compounds have been shown to have potent antiseptic and soothing effects on the vaginal mucosa. If you're experiencing symptoms of vaginitis, applying Calendula officinalis essential oils topically to the affected area using a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil can be beneficial. Mix a few drops of the essential oil with a carrier oil and gently massage it into the vagina using a clean finger or a cotton swab.

It's essential to note that pregnant women and breastfeeding mothers should avoid using essential oils without consulting their healthcare provider first. When applying Calendula officinalis essential oils, you may experience a reduction in vaginal discharge, inflammation, and discomfort. The oils may also help promote wound healing and reduce the risk of infection. However, it's crucial to note that these symptoms may take several days to a week to subside. If your symptoms persist or worsen, seek medical attention from a healthcare provider.

Additionally, if you're experiencing any severe symptoms, such as excessive bleeding or fever, seek immediate medical attention.

1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains triterpene saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are beneficial for vaginitis.

The triterpene saponins have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and pain in the vagina. The flavonoids, specifically kaempferol and quercetin, have antimicrobial properties, which can help fight off fungal and bacterial infections that cause vaginitis. The carotenoids, particularly beta-carotene, have antioxidant properties, which can help protect the vaginal tissue from damage and promote healing.

By using Calendula officinalis tinctures, you can help alleviate the symptoms of vaginitis and promote a healthy vaginal environment.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Calendula officinalis flowers and 2 cups of 80-proof vodka or glycerin.
  2. Combine the flowers and vodka or glycerin in a clean glass jar, making sure the flowers are fully covered.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar daily.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place and use 10-20 drops in a cup of water for vaginal application, 2-3 times a day.

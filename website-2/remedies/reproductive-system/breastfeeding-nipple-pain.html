What Are The Main Causes Of Nipple Pain During Breastfeeding?

Nipple pain during breastfeeding can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is Nipple Thrush, a fungal infection that grows on the nipple and areola. This happens when yeast from the mother's body multiplies, often due to hormonal changes or certain medications. Nipple Thrush causes redness, itching, and burning pain on the nipple. Another cause of nipple pain is Cracked Nipples, which occur when the nipple is not properly covered by the areola or when the baby's latch is not correct.

When the nipple is exposed, it can become cracked and sore, especially if the baby sucks too hard or for too long. Cracked Nipples can be painful and may bleed, making breastfeeding uncomfortable. Poor Latch is also a common cause of nipple pain. This happens when the baby's mouth doesn't properly cover the nipple and areola, causing the nipple to be sucked too hard or unevenly. A poor latch can lead to nipple soreness, pain, and even cracks.

It's essential to check the baby's latch regularly to ensure it's correct and comfortable for both the mother and baby.

Below are the main causes of nipple pain during breastfeeding.

  • Nipple Thrush: Nipple thrush is a cause of nipple pain during breastfeeding because a type of fungus called Candida causes an infection that irritates and inflames the nipple, leading to pain and discomfort.
  • Cracked Nipples: A cracked nipple is a common cause of nipple pain during breastfeeding because the crack in the nipple can expose the nerve endings underneath, making every time the baby latches and sucks painful.
  • Poor Latch: Poor latch occurs when the baby's mouth is not correctly attached to the breast, causing discomfort and pain for the mother because the baby's tongue is not positioned to compress the areola, leading to nipple irritation and soreness.
  • Sore Nipples: Sore nipples during breastfeeding often occur because the baby's mouth doesn't form a proper seal around the nipple, causing friction and irritation which can lead to soreness and pain.
  • Paget's Disease: Paget's Disease causes nipple pain during breastfeeding because abnormal cells in the nipple ducts cause itching, soreness, and redness, making it painful and uncomfortable for the baby to latch on.
  • Infection: Infection can cause nipple pain during breastfeeding because bacteria can enter the breast tissue and cause inflammation, leading to soreness and discomfort in the nipples.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Nipple Pain During Breastfeeding?

If you're experiencing nipple pain while breastfeeding, it's essential to be cautious with certain herbs that can cause more harm than good.

Some herbs to avoid include St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) because it can reduce milk supply and worsen nipple pain. Wild Yam (Dioscorea villosa) and Blue Cohosh (Caulophyllum thalictroides) can slow milk production and increase the risk of nipple pain.

Pennyroyal (Mentha pulegium) is also not recommended as it can cause liver damage and reduce milk supply. Licorice Root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) should be used with caution as high doses can cause hormonal imbalances, which may affect milk production and nipple sensitivity. Dong Quai (Angelica sinensis) may also need to be avoided due to its potential to cause hormonal imbalances, although some sources recommend it in small amounts.

It's always best to consult a healthcare professional before using any herbs while breastfeeding.

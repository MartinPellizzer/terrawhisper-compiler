1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa tinctures contains a compound called curcumin, which is responsible for its medicinal properties.

Curcumin has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the body. Endometriosis is a condition characterized by inflammation and scarring of the uterine lining, and curcumin's anti-inflammatory properties may help to reduce this inflammation. Curcuma longa tinctures also contain other bioactive constituents like demethoxycurcumin and bisdemethoxycurcumin, which have similar anti-inflammatory properties.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, curcuma longa tinctures may help to alleviate symptoms of endometriosis, such as pelvic pain and heavy bleeding.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa root, a glass jar with a lid, a cheesecloth or a coffee filter, and a measuring cup.
  2. Fill the glass jar with the 1 cup of dried Curcuma longa root, and add 2 cups of vodka (at least 80 proof). Stir to combine.
  3. Close the jar tightly and shake it every day for 2 weeks to help the Curcuma longa root infuse into the vodka.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the liquid through the cheesecloth or coffee filter into another glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the tincture in the jar with a tight lid in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, as needed.

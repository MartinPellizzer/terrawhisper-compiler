What Are The Main Causes Of Over Bleeding During Periods?

During periods, over bleeding can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is polyps, small growths in the uterus that can interfere with normal bleeding. These growths can cause an abnormal build-up of blood, leading to heavy bleeding. Another cause is fibroids, non-cancerous tumors that grow in the uterus.

Fibroids can put pressure on the uterus, causing it to bleed more heavily than usual. Hormonal imbalance, particularly a lack of progesterone, can also lead to over bleeding. Progesterone helps to thicken the lining of the uterus, preparing it for a fertilized egg.

Without enough progesterone, the lining may break down too quickly, causing heavy bleeding.

Below are the main causes of over bleeding during periods.

  • Polyps: Polyps in the uterus can cause heavy or painful bleeding during periods because they can grow inside the uterus and disrupt the normal lining that sheds during menstruation, leading to excessive bleeding.
  • Fibroids: Fibroids are growths in the uterus that can cause heavy and prolonged bleeding during periods because they increase blood flow to the area, making it harder for the uterus to contract and stop the bleeding.
  • Hormonal Imbalance: Hormonal imbalance, especially an imbalance of estrogen and progesterone, can cause over bleeding during periods because it disrupts the normal process of the uterus lining, causing it to grow and shed unevenly, leading to excessive bleeding.
  • IUD: When an IUD (intrauterine device) is inserted, it can cause over bleeding during periods because the copper or hormonal parts of the IUD can irritate the uterus, leading to an increase in prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that cause the uterus to contract and bleed.
  • Hormonal Contraceptives: Hormonal Contraceptives can cause over bleeding during periods because the hormones in these pills, patches, and rings can sometimes confuse the body's natural menstrual cycle, leading to irregular and heavy bleeding as the body adjusts to the changes.
  • Thyroid Issues: Thyroid issues can cause over bleeding during periods because an underactive thyroid gland, also known as hypothyroidism, can lead to a decrease in the production of estrogen, a hormone that helps regulate menstrual flow, resulting in heavier and more prolonged bleeding.
  • PCOS: Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) often leads to over bleeding during periods because the hormonal imbalance in women with PCOS causes the uterine lining to grow too thick, leading to heavier and longer bleeding when it sheds.

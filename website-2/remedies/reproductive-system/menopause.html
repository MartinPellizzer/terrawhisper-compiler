What Are The Main Causes Of Menopause?

Menopause is a natural process that occurs in women, typically between the ages of 45 and 55.

The main cause of menopause is natural aging, where the ovaries gradually stop producing eggs and hormones, such as estrogen and progesterone, which regulate menstrual cycles. As women age, their ovaries become less efficient, leading to a decrease in hormone production and eventually stopping menstruation. Another cause of menopause is a hysterectomy, a surgical procedure where the uterus is removed.

This surgery can lead to premature menopause, as the ovaries are left behind and continue to produce eggs for a short period. However, eventually, the ovaries also stop producing eggs and hormones, resulting in menopause. Tubal ligation is a surgical procedure that blocks or cuts the fallopian tubes, preventing eggs from being released from the ovaries. While this procedure does not directly cause menopause, it can lead to a condition called premature ovarian failure, where the ovaries stop producing eggs and hormones.

This can result in menopause-like symptoms, including hot flashes, mood changes, and irregular periods.

Below are the main causes of menopause.

  • Natural Aging: As women grow older, their ovaries gradually stop producing eggs and hormones, leading to menopause due to natural aging.
  • Hysterectomy: A hysterectomy, which is the removal of the uterus, can cause menopause because the ovaries, which produce hormones that control the menstrual cycle, remain in the body, but their blood supply is cut off, leading to a decrease in hormone production and a premature end to menstruation.
  • Tubal Ligation: Tubal ligation is a cause of menopause because it blocks the fallopian tubes, preventing eggs from traveling to the uterus to be fertilized and eventually leading to a decline in hormone production, resulting in menopausal symptoms.
  • Chemotherapy: Chemotherapy can cause menopause by damaging the ovaries and stopping them from producing eggs and hormones, such as estrogen and progesterone, which are necessary to regulate the menstrual cycle.
  • Radiation: Prolonged exposure to radiation, especially during the reproductive years, can damage the ovaries' ability to produce eggs, leading to a premature depletion of eggs and ultimately causing menopause earlier than usual.
  • Tumor Removal: Tumor removal can cause menopause if the surgeon removes both ovaries during the operation, which means the body has no more eggs to release hormones, leading to a sudden drop in estrogen levels and menopause symptoms.
  • Premature Ovarian Failure: Premature Ovarian Failure is a cause of menopause because the ovaries stop producing eggs and hormones too early, usually before the age of 40, leading to the end of a woman's menstrual periods and the onset of menopause symptoms.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Menopause?

If you're going through menopause, it's essential to be cautious with certain herbs that can interact with your medication or worsen symptoms.

One herb to avoid is St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum), as it can decrease the effectiveness of hormone replacement therapy (HRT) and other medications. Another herb to be careful with is Dong Quai (Angelica sinensis), which can increase the risk of bleeding and interact with blood thinners.

Feverfew (Chrysanthemum parthenium) should also be avoided, as it can cause uterine contractions and worsen hot flashes. Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) is another herb to limit, as it can cause high blood pressure and interact with medications for heart conditions.

Lastly, Wild Yam (Dioscorea villosa) should be used with caution, as it can cause stomach upset and interact with medications for blood pressure and diabetes.

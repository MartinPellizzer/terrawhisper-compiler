What are the causes of prostatitis?

Prostatitis is an inflammation of the prostate gland, and it can be caused by several things.

One main cause is a bacterial infection, where bad bacteria like E. coli enter the prostate and cause inflammation. This can happen if bacteria from the urinary tract or other parts of the body get into the prostate through the urethra. Another cause is a urinary tract infection, which can spread to the prostate if left untreated. This can be due to poor hygiene, catheter use, or certain medical conditions.

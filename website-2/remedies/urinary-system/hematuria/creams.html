1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contain triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids as its active constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce swelling and protect the bladder from damage. The triterpenoid saponins, particularly inulin and calenduloside, have been shown to have a soothing effect on the mucous membranes, which can help to calm bleeding in the urinary tract. The flavonoids, including quercetin and kaempferol, have potent antioxidant properties that can help to reduce oxidative stress and promote healing in the bladder.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Calendula officinalis creams may help to alleviate symptoms of hematuria.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Calendula officinalis flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil. Clean and dry the flowers.
  2. Combine the Calendula flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Let it sit for 2-3 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking the jar daily.
  3. After 2-3 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter to the liquid mixture. Heat the mixture in a double boiler until the beeswax and shea butter melt.
  5. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it harden. Your Calendula cream is now ready for use.

2. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica creams contains active constituents like histamine, acetylcholine, and tannins, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help reduce inflammation and swelling in the urinary tract, which can cause hematuria. The tannins in Urtica dioica also have astringent properties, which help constrict blood vessels and reduce bleeding. Histamine, on the other hand, can increase blood flow to the affected area, promoting healing and reducing the severity of hematuria.

By applying Urtica dioica creams to the affected area, people may experience relief from hematuria symptoms.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Urtica dioica leaves, 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Make a tea by steeping the dried Urtica dioica leaves in 2 cups of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Let it cool.
  3. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl over a pot of simmering water.
  4. Strain the Urtica dioica tea into the melted coconut oil mixture and add the vitamin E oil. Stir well.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify. Store in a cool, dark place.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains alkylamides, caftaric acid, and chicoric acid as its key bioactive constituents.

The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of Echinacea purpurea creams may help reduce bleeding and inflammation in the urinary tract associated with hematuria.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Echinacea purpurea creams can help alleviate symptoms of hematuria.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of coconut oil, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Melt the beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the melted mixture and let it steep for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Add the vitamin E oil and mix well. Pour the mixture into a tin or a jar and let it cool and solidify before use.

4. Equisetum arvense

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Equisetum arvense leaves and flowers, and 2 cups of water.
  2. Combine the Equisetum arvense and water in a saucepan, and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes, then let cool.

5. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum creams contains active constituents like hypericin, hyperforin, and flavonoids, which work together to reduce inflammation and promote healing.

Hypericin has anti-inflammatory properties that help to soothe and calm irritated tissues in the urinary tract, while hyperforin has antioxidant properties that help to protect against cell damage. Flavonoids, on the other hand, have astringent properties that help to constrict blood vessels and reduce bleeding. These properties make Hypericum perforatum creams effective in treating hematuria, a condition characterized by blood in the urine, by reducing the frequency and severity of bleeding episodes.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healing, Hypericum perforatum creams can help to alleviate symptoms of hematuria and promote overall urinary health.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried Hypericum perforatum flowers and 1 cup of coconut oil in a clean glass bowl.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a double boiler over low heat until it reaches 120°F to 140°F.
  3. Add the 2 cups of dried Hypericum perforatum flowers to the heated coconut oil and let it infuse for 2-3 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bowl, squeezing the cheesecloth to get all the liquid out.
  5. Mix 2 tablespoons of beeswax with 2 tablespoons of the Hypericum perforatum infused oil in a small saucepan over low heat, stirring until the beeswax is melted and the mixture is smooth.

6. Aloe barbadensis

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce swelling and irritation in the urinary tract, a common cause of hematuria.   3. In a separate bowl, mix aloe vera gel and shea butter until well combined.
  4. Slowly add the melted oil mixture to the aloe vera mixture, stirring constantly until smooth.
  5. Add vitamin E oil and tea tree oil, and mix well. Pour the mixture into a container and let cool before use.

1. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica creams contains bioactive constituents like histamine, polysaccharides, and mucilages.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation in the urinary tract and alleviate the symptoms of cystitis, such as burning and frequent urination. The mucilages in Urtica dioica creams create a protective barrier on the mucous membranes, soothing and calming the irritated tissues. The anti-inflammatory properties of histamine and polysaccharides also help to reduce swelling and pain in the bladder and urethra.

By reducing inflammation and soothing the mucous membranes, Urtica dioica creams can help to provide relief from the symptoms of cystitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Urtica dioica leaves, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, and a double boiler.
  2. Wash the Urtica dioica leaves with water and chop them. In the double boiler, melt the coconut oil.
  3. Add the chopped Urtica dioica leaves to the melted coconut oil. Let it simmer for 2 hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a bowl. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the cooled mixture to a jar and store it in the fridge. Use 1-2 teaspoons of the cream as needed for cystitis relief.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis creams contains flavonoids, triterpenoids, and carotenoids as its active constituents.

By reducing inflammation and fighting off bacterial infections, calendula cream can provide relief from the symptoms of cystitis.

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula officinalis flowers and 1/2 cup of carrier oil (coconut or olive oil).
  2. Combine the dried flowers and carrier oil in a small saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean bowl. Discard the solids.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of shea butter to the bowl. Heat the mixture in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl in 10-second increments, stirring between each heating, until the beeswax and shea butter are melted.

What Are The Main Causes Of Benign Prostatic Hypertrophy?

Benign prostatic hypertrophy (BPH) is a condition where the prostate gland becomes enlarged.

There are several causes of BPH, but the main ones are age, dihydrotestosterone, and estrogen. Age is a major cause of BPH. As men get older, their prostate glands tend to grow, which can lead to BPH. This is because the cells in the prostate gland multiply and grow, causing the gland to become larger. Dihydrotestosterone (DHT) is a hormone that contributes to BPH.

DHT is a byproduct of testosterone, a male hormone. As men age, their bodies convert more testosterone into DHT, which can cause the prostate gland to grow. DHT can also lead to the formation of scar tissue in the prostate gland, making it harder for urine to flow. Estrogen, a female hormone, also plays a role in BPH. Although men have low levels of estrogen, it can still contribute to the growth of the prostate gland. Some studies suggest that high levels of estrogen in men may lead to BPH.

However, the exact relationship between estrogen and BPH is not fully understood.

Below are the main causes of benign prostatic hypertrophy.

  • Age: As people age, their prostate glands naturally grow and change, leading to the formation of benign prostatic hypertrophy, a condition where the prostate gland becomes enlarged and presses on the urethra.
  • Dihydrotestosterone: Dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a powerful form of male hormone, causes benign prostatic hypertrophy by converting to a strong substance that stimulates the prostate gland to grow and become enlarged over time.
  • Estrogen: As the body converts estrogen from testosterone, it builds up in the prostate gland, causing it to swell and grow, which can lead to benign prostatic hypertrophy.
  • Family history: Research suggests that family history plays a role in developing benign prostatic hypertrophy because it can increase the likelihood of inheriting genetic factors that affect hormone levels and prostate growth, making some people more prone to this condition.
  • Heredity: Heredity is a cause of benign prostatic hypertrophy because a person's genes can pass down inherited traits from their parents that increase the risk of developing this condition, which makes them more likely to have an enlarged prostate as they age.

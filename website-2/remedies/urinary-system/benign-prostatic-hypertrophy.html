Benign Prostatic Hypertrophy: Understanding Causes and Herbal Preparations for Relief

What are the causes of benign prostatic hypertrophy?

Benign prostatic hypertrophy, or BPH, is a common condition in older men where the prostate gland grows larger.

So, what causes it?. One main factor is genetic factors. If your family members have BPH, you're more likely to get it too. This is because some people may inherit a tendency to grow extra tissue in the prostate. As men age, their prostate gland naturally grows, and this can lead to BPH. So, age is another key factor. As men get older, usually after the age of 50, their prostate starts to grow, and this can cause problems.

Hormones also play a role in BPH. Estrogen, a female hormone, can stimulate the growth of the prostate gland. Now, you might be thinking, "But men have low levels of estrogen!". And you're right, but estrogen is still present in men, and it can affect the prostate. Testosterone, another hormone, is often blamed for BPH, but it's not the main cause. Testosterone is what makes men, men, and it helps to stimulate the growth of the prostate gland, but it's not the only factor. Androgens, a group of hormones that include testosterone, also contribute to BPH. These hormones help to stimulate the growth of the prostate gland, and when they're present in high levels, it can lead to BPH.

So, it's not just one hormone, but a combination of hormones that can cause the prostate gland to grow.

Cystitis: Causes, Medicinal Herbs, and Herbal Preparations for Relief and Recovery

What are the causes of cystitis?

Cystitis, an inflammation of the bladder, can be caused by several factors.

Bacterial infection is a common cause of cystitis. When bacteria, especially E. coli, enter the bladder through the urethra, they can multiply and cause an infection. This is often seen in women, as the urethra is shorter and closer to the anus, making it easier for bacteria to spread. Catheter use is another cause of cystitis. When a catheter is inserted into the bladder, it can introduce bacteria into the urinary tract, leading to an infection. This is why catheters are often treated with antibiotics to prevent infection. Urinary tract stones, also known as kidney stones or bladder stones, can also cause cystitis.

These stones can block the flow of urine and cause bacteria to multiply, leading to an infection. The stones can also damage the bladder and urinary tract, making it easier for bacteria to enter. Kidney stones, which form in the kidneys, can also cause cystitis. Like urinary tract stones, they can block the flow of urine and cause bacteria to multiply. Kidney stones can also cause damage to the kidneys and urinary tract, making it easier for bacteria to enter. Urethral stricture, a narrowing of the urethra, can also cause cystitis. When the urethra is narrow, it can be harder for urine to flow out of the body, making it easier for bacteria to multiply and cause an infection.

This is often seen in men, as the urethra is longer and more narrow than in women.

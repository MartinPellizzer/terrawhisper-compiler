1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa teas contains compounds like curcumin, demethoxycurcumin, and bisdemethoxycurcumin.

These bioactive constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce inflammation in the prostate. Curcumin in particular blocks enzymes that produce pro-inflammatory molecules, while its antioxidant properties neutralize free radicals that damage prostate cells. The anti-inflammatory effects of Curcuma longa teas may help alleviate symptoms of prostate inflammation, such as pain and swelling.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Curcuma longa teas can promote a healthy prostate environment.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Curcuma longa root powder and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the powder in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the mixture into a cup.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of honey or lemon juice to taste, if desired.
  4. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, preferably before meals, for 2-3 weeks.
  5. Consult a doctor before using Curcuma longa tea for prostate inflammation, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions.

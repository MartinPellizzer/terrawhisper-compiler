What Are The Main Causes Of Bladder Infection?

The main causes of bladder infection include sexual activity, urinary tract abnormality, and bacterial infection.

Sexual activity can cause bladder infection because bacteria from the vagina or penis can enter the urinary tract during sex. This is more likely to happen if a person has a urinary tract abnormality, such as a narrow urethra or a blockage in the ureters. When bacteria enter the urinary tract, they can multiply and cause an infection. Urinary tract abnormality can also cause bladder infection.

This can be a birth defect, an injury, or a condition such as kidney stones that can block the flow of urine. If urine stays in the bladder for too long, bacteria can multiply and cause an infection. Bacterial infection is the most common cause of bladder infection. The bacteria Escherichia coli (E. coli) is the most common cause of bladder infection in women. This bacteria is usually found in the intestines, but it can enter the urinary tract through the urethra and cause an infection.

Bacterial infection can also be caused by other bacteria, such as Klebsiella or Pseudomonas.

Below are the main causes of bladder infection.

  • Sexual Activity: During sexual activity, bacteria from the vagina or penis can enter the bladder through the urethra, causing an infection known as a urinary tract infection (UTI).
  • Urinary Tract Abnormality: Urinary Tract Abnormality can cause bladder infection because it may lead to a buildup of bacteria in the urinary tract, which can easily spread to the bladder and cause an infection.
  • Bacterial Infection: Bacterial infection is a cause of bladder infection because when bad bacteria, like E. coli, enter the bladder through the urine, they multiply and cause inflammation, leading to symptoms such as pain and frequent urination.
  • Urinary Catheter: Urinary catheter can cause bladder infection because bacteria can easily enter the urinary tract through the catheter, leading to an overgrowth of harmful bacteria in the bladder.
  • Kidney Stones: Kidney stones can cause bladder infections because the stones can block the flow of urine, allowing bacteria to multiply and cause an infection in the bladder.
  • Diabetes: Diabetes can cause bladder infections because high blood sugar levels make it harder for the body to fight off bacteria that normally live in the bladder, allowing them to multiply and cause infection.

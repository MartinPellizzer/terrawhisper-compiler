Bladder Infection: Understanding the Causes and Herbal Remedies

What are the causes of bladder infection?

There are several main causes of bladder infections.

Bacterial infection is the most common cause, where bacteria like E. coli enter the bladder through the urethra and multiply, causing an infection. This usually happens when good bacteria in the vagina are disrupted, allowing the bad bacteria to take over. Urinary tract blockage is another cause, where the flow of urine is blocked, allowing bacteria to multiply and cause an infection.

This can be due to a variety of reasons such as kidney stones, which are hard deposits that form in the kidneys and can block the flow of urine. Kidney stones are a major cause of bladder infections, as they can cause the flow of urine to become obstructed, creating an ideal environment for bacteria to multiply. Neurogenic bladder is a condition where the nerve signals that control the bladder are disrupted, leading to an inability to empty the bladder completely, allowing bacteria to multiply and cause an infection.

Diabetes is also a cause of bladder infections, as the high blood sugar levels in people with diabetes can lead to the growth of bacteria in the urinary tract, causing an infection.

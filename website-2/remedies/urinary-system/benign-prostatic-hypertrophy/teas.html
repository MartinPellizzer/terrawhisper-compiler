Benign Prostatic Hypertrophy in Older Adults: 7 Teas for Relief

As people age, their prostate gland can grow larger and start causing problems.

This condition is called benign prostatic hypertrophy (BPH). Retirees are more likely to experience BPH because their prostate gland grows with age. When the prostate gets bigger, it can put pressure on the urinary tract and make it harder for men to urinate. This can cause discomfort, pain, and difficulty starting or stopping the flow of urine. It can also lead to frequent urination, especially at night, and a weak urine flow. BPH can disrupt a retiree's daily life, making it hard to sleep, enjoy activities, and even travel. It can also affect their mental health, causing anxiety and stress.

BPH can also increase the risk of other health problems, such as kidney stones, bladder infections, and incontinence. In some cases, BPH can even lead to a complete blockage of the urinary tract, which requires emergency medical attention. While there are medical treatments available for BPH, some retirees may prefer to try alternative remedies to manage their symptoms. This is where herbal teas come in. Herbal teas have been traditionally used to help alleviate the symptoms of BPH. They work by reducing inflammation, relaxing the muscles in the urinary tract, and improving urine flow. Some herbal teas may also help to shrink the size of the prostate gland.

Drinking herbal teas can provide relief from the discomfort and inconvenience of BPH, allowing retirees to enjoy a better quality of life.

However, it's essential to note that Serenoa repens tea is not a cure for BPH and should be used in conjunction with other treatments, such as lifestyle modifications and medication, under the guidance of a healthcare provider.

What are the causes of hematuria?

Hematuria, which is the presence of blood in the urine, can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is kidney stones, which are hard deposits that form in the kidneys and can cause pain and bleeding when they move. Another potential cause is bladder cancer, which is a growth that develops in the lining of the bladder and can cause bleeding, especially when it grows large enough to touch the bladder wall.

Urinary tract infections (UTIs) can also cause hematuria, as the infection can irritate the bladder and cause bleeding. Prostate cancer, which is a type of cancer that develops in the prostate gland in men, can also cause hematuria, as the cancer can press on the ureters and cause bleeding. Finally, kidney cancer, which is a type of cancer that develops in the kidneys, can cause hematuria by bleeding into the urine.

In all these cases, the bleeding is often painless, making it an important symptom to pay attention to.

What Are The Main Causes Of Hematuria?

Hematuria, or blood in the urine, can be caused by several factors.

One common cause is kidney stones. These small, hard mineral deposits can form in the kidneys and block the flow of urine, causing damage to the kidney tissue and leading to bleeding. Another possible cause is bladder cancer.

This type of cancer occurs when abnormal cells grow in the bladder, causing the bladder wall to thicken and bleed. Prostate cancer is another potential cause of hematuria, particularly in older men. As the cancer grows, it can press on the ureters, which are the tubes that carry urine from the kidneys to the bladder, causing blood to appear in the urine.

In addition to these causes, hematuria can also be triggered by infections, injuries, and certain medical conditions, such as high blood pressure and kidney disease.

Below are the main causes of hematuria.

  • Kidney Stones: Kidney stones can cause hematuria because they are sharp, hard pieces that can break apart inside the kidney or urinary tract, and when they move, they can damage the lining of the urinary system and cause bleeding.
  • Bladder Cancer: Bladder Cancer causes hematuria because it can grow inside the bladder and bleed, releasing blood into the urine.
  • Prostate Cancer: Prostate cancer can cause hematuria because it can grow and block the flow of urine, causing blood to leak into the urinary tract and appear in the urine.
  • Urinary Tract Infections: Urinary Tract Infections can cause hematuria when bacteria in the infected urinary tract irritate the bladder and kidneys, leading to bleeding and the presence of blood in the urine.
  • Kidney Disease: Kidney disease can cause hematuria because the damaged kidneys are unable to filter waste properly, causing blood cells to leak into the urine.
  • Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia: Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia is a cause of hematuria because the enlarged prostate gland can press against the urethra, causing blood to leak into the urine due to the increased pressure and irritation on the bladder and urethra walls.
  • Interstitial Cystitis: Interstitial Cystitis is a cause of hematuria because the chronic inflammation and bladder damage in the condition can cause tiny tears in the bladder's lining, leading to blood leaking into the urine.

What Are The Main Causes Of Fluid Retention?

Fluid retention occurs when your body holds more fluid than it should.

This can cause swelling in various parts of your body, such as your feet, hands, and face. There are several reasons why fluid retention happens. One main cause is Hormonal Changes. During your menstrual cycle, pregnancy, or menopause, hormonal fluctuations can affect how your body retains fluid. This is because certain hormones, such as estrogen and progesterone, influence how your body regulates fluid balance. Another cause of fluid retention is Sodium Intake.

When you eat too much sodium, your body holds onto water to dilute the salt. This is because sodium is a natural fluid-retaining substance. If you consume high amounts of sodium through processed foods, canned goods, or table salt, your body may retain more fluid than it should. Dehydration is also a cause of fluid retention. When you don't drink enough water, your body tries to conserve fluid by retaining it in your tissues. This can cause swelling in some parts of your body. However, it's worth noting that dehydration and fluid retention are not the same thing.

Dehydration is when your body doesn't have enough fluid, while fluid retention is when your body holds onto too much fluid.

Below are the main causes of fluid retention.

  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal changes cause fluid retention because the body holds onto more water and salt when hormonal levels, such as estrogen and progesterone, change or fluctuate, leading to swelling and bloating.
  • Sodium Intake: When you consume too much sodium, your body holds onto water to dilute the excess salt, leading to swelling and fluid retention in various parts of the body.
  • Dehydration: When the body loses more water than it takes in, due to dehydration, it holds onto extra water to try and make up for the loss, resulting in fluid retention.
  • Kidney Disease: Kidney disease causes fluid retention because the kidneys are unable to filter waste and excess fluids from the blood properly, leading to the buildup of fluids in the body.
  • Heart Failure: When the heart fails to pump blood efficiently, it can't remove excess fluid from the body, leading to fluid buildup and retention in the feet, legs, and other areas.
  • Liver Disease: When the liver becomes damaged, it cannot break down and filter waste properly, leading to an increase in sodium levels in the body, which in turn causes the body to hold onto water, resulting in fluid retention.
  • Caffeine: Caffeine causes fluid retention because it increases the amount of water in the bloodstream and makes the body hold onto sodium, leading to swelling and bloating.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Fluid Retention?

If you have fluid retention, there are certain herbs you should avoid.

Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) is one to steer clear of. It can cause the body to hold onto water and increase blood pressure, making fluid retention worse. Fennel (Foeniculum vulgare) may also be problematic.

Its active compound, anethole, can cause the body to relax and hold onto fluid, rather than releasing it. Dandelion root (Taraxacum officinale) might seem like a good choice for fluid retention, but it can actually cause the body to release potassium, leading to more water being retained in the body. Ginger (Zingiber officinale) can be too drying for people with fluid retention, causing the body to lose too much water and electrolytes. Hydrangea root (Hydrangea arborescens) is another herb to avoid, as its active compound, quinic acid, can stimulate the body to hold onto fluid, rather than releasing it.

These herbs can make fluid retention worse, so it's best to avoid them if you're struggling with this issue.

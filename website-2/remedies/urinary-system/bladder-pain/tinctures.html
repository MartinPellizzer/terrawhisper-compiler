1. Equisetum arvense

Equisetum arvense tinctures contains the bioactive constituents of equisetin and podophyllotoxin, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce swelling and irritation in the bladder, providing relief from pain. Equisetum arvense also contains silica, which has a soothing effect on the bladder walls, calming inflammation and discomfort. The tannins present in the tincture have an astringent property, which helps to reduce bleeding and ease bladder spasms.

By addressing inflammation and soothing the bladder walls, Equisetum arvense tinctures can help alleviate bladder pain caused by irritation and inflammation.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Equisetum arvense (horsetail) dried roots and stems, and 2 cups of 80% vodka or ethanol as the solvent.
  2. Combine the horsetail and vodka in a clean glass jar, making sure the plants are completely covered.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-6 weeks, shaking the jar every day.
  4. After 2-6 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the tincture to small dark glass bottles and store them in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops of the tincture in water or tea as needed for bladder pain relief.

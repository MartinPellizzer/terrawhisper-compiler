What Are The Main Causes Of Bladder Pain?

Bladder pain can be caused by several factors.

Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) is a common cause of bladder pain in men. It occurs when the prostate gland, located near the bladder, becomes enlarged. This enlargement can put pressure on the bladder and urethra, leading to discomfort, pain, and difficulty urinating. Urinary Tract Infections (UTIs) are another common cause of bladder pain.

A UTI occurs when bacteria enter the urinary tract and multiply, causing inflammation and infection. This can lead to severe pain in the bladder, pelvic area, and lower back, as well as frequent and urgent urination. Interstitial Cystitis (IC), also known as painful bladder syndrome, is a chronic condition characterized by bladder pain and pressure. The exact cause of IC is unknown, but it is often linked to a combination of factors, including bladder sensitivity, nerve damage, and immune system dysfunction.

This condition can cause recurring bladder pain, pressure, and discomfort, as well as changes in urination habits.

Below are the main causes of bladder pain.

  • BPH: Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) can cause bladder pain because the enlarged prostate gland presses on the bladder and urethra, making it difficult for urine to flow out and causing discomfort and pain.
  • UTIs: UTIs are a cause of bladder pain because bacteria in the urine infect the bladder lining, causing inflammation, swelling, and irritation that leads to sharp, burning, and uncomfortable sensations.
  • Interstitial Cystitis: Interstitial Cystitis is a cause of bladder pain because the bladder lining becomes inflamed and damaged, leading to sensitive and painful sensations when the bladder expands or contracts.
  • Kidney Stones: Kidney stones cause bladder pain because they can move down from the kidneys and get stuck in the bladder, causing sharp and severe pain, and also causing the bladder to swell and stretch, leading to more discomfort.
  • Overactive Bladder: Overactive Bladder is a cause of bladder pain because the frequent and sudden muscle contractions in the bladder can cause discomfort, pressure, and pain, making it hard to hold urine.
  • Pregnancy: During pregnancy, the growing baby puts pressure on the bladder, causing it to stretch and lose its ability to hold urine properly, which can lead to painful bladder symptoms.
  • Endometriosis: Endometriosis is a cause of bladder pain because the tissue that usually grows inside the uterus also grows outside in the pelvic area, including near the bladder, and bleeds during periods, leading to irritation and inflammation in the bladder and surrounding tissues.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Bladder Pain?

If you have bladder pain, there are some herbs you should avoid.

One of them is Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens), which may worsen bladder issues by blocking the action of diuretic herbs. Another herb to avoid is Uva Ursi (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi), which contains a compound called arbutin.

While arbutin is sometimes used to treat urinary tract infections, high doses can be toxic and cause kidney damage. You should also avoid Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis), as it contains berberine, which may irritate the bladder and make pain worse. Additionally, Wild Yam (Dioscorea villosa) and Sarsaparilla (Smilax ornata) contain compounds that may increase the risk of kidney stones, which can worsen bladder pain.

These herbs can interact with each other or with medications, so it's best to consult with a healthcare professional before using them if you have bladder pain.

1. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis essential oils contains rosmarinic acid and 1,8-cineole as its major bioactive constituents.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help to soothe and calm the urinary tract. The antiseptic properties of 1,8-cineole also reduce bacterial growth, making it an effective treatment for dysuria, a painful or difficult urination caused by infection. The anti-inflammatory properties of rosmarinic acid further help to reduce swelling and discomfort in the urinary tract, promoting a smooth and pain-free urination.

By reducing inflammation and bacterial growth, Melissa officinalis essential oils provide relief from the symptoms of dysuria.

  1. Gather 2 cups of Melissa officinalis flowers and leaves. Clean and dry them thoroughly.
  2. Use a glass container and add 2 cups of 80-proof vodka or glycerin. This will be your solvent.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of the solvent to the Melissa flowers and leaves in a clean glass jar. Stir gently.
  4. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This will allow the solvent to extract the oil.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another glass container. Discard the solids and store the extracted oil in a dark glass bottle.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains a rich mixture of bioactive constituents, including alkylamides, phenolic acids, and volatile oils.

These compounds contribute to the oil's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help soothe and protect the urinary tract. Alkylamides, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the growth of certain bacteria that can cause urinary tract infections, such as E. coli. Phenolic acids, like chicoric acid, possess antioxidant properties that can help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the urinary tract.

By addressing the root causes of dysuria, Echinacea purpurea essential oils may provide relief from painful urination and other urinary tract symptoms.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of carrier oil (such as coconut or olive oil).
  2. Clean the flowers and dry them with paper towels. Use 1/4 cup of dried flowers for the next steps.
  3. Combine the dried flowers and carrier oil in a clean glass jar. Make sure the flowers are completely covered by the oil.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day. This is the steeping process.
  5. Strain the oil through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean container. Discard the flowers and store the essential oil in a dark glass bottle.

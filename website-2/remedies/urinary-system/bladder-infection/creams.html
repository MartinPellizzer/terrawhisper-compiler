1. Equisetum arvense

Equisetum arvense creams contains silicic acid and equisetic acid, which are active constituents that help alleviate bladder infections.

Silicic acid has anti-inflammatory properties, reducing swelling and irritation in the urinary tract. Equisetic acid has antimicrobial properties, killing bacteria that cause infections. The creams also contain flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, helping to protect the bladder lining from damage.

By reducing inflammation and killing bacteria, Equisetum arvense creams can help to alleviate symptoms of bladder infections, promoting healing and relief.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh Equisetum arvense roots. Wash them with water.
  2. Dry the roots in a low-temperature oven (150°F) for 2 hours.
  3. Grind 2 tablespoons of dried Equisetum arvense roots into a fine powder.
  4. Mix 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, and 2 tablespoons of Equisetum arvense powder in a saucepan.
  5. Heat the mixture over low heat (100°F) until the beeswax melts. Let it cool, then store in an airtight container.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contains several bioactive constituents, including alkylamides, glycoproteins, and phenolic acids.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help reduce bladder inflammation and kill bacteria that cause infections. The alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea creams have been shown to increase the production of white blood cells, which fight off infections in the bladder. The glycoproteins in Echinacea purpurea creams have been found to stimulate the production of cytokines, which help to reduce inflammation in the bladder.

By reducing inflammation and killing bacteria, Echinacea purpurea creams can help alleviate symptoms of bladder infections and promote healing.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, and 10 drops of tea tree oil.
  2. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add the dried Echinacea purpurea flowers to the melted oil and beeswax. Let it steep for 30 minutes to 1 hour, then strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Add the shea butter, olive oil, and tea tree oil to the strained mixture. Stir well until everything is fully incorporated.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let it cool and solidify. Store the creams in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

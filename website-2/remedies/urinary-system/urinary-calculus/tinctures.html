1. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum tinctures contains silymarin as its main bioactive constituent.

Silymarin has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce damage and inflammation in the body caused by urinary calculi. It also has a diuretic effect, increasing urine production and helping to flush out small stones. Additionally, silymarin has been shown to inhibit the growth of certain bacteria that can contribute to urinary tract infections, a common complication of urinary calculi.

By reducing inflammation, promoting urination, and preventing bacterial growth, Silybum marianum tinctures can help alleviate symptoms of urinary calculus.

  1. Gather 250g of Silybum marianum roots and flowers. Clean them with water.
  2. Combine the cleaned Silybum marianum with 1 cup of 80% vodka in a clean glass jar. The ratio is 1:4.
  3. Close the jar and shake it well for 10 seconds. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Transfer the liquid tincture into small dark glass bottles with dropper lids. Store them in a cool, dark place. The tincture is ready to use.

2. Berberis vulgaris

Berberis vulgaris tinctures contains berberine and other alkaloids, such as berbamine and oxyberberine, which are known for their ability to help break down and eliminate urinary calculi.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that reduce swelling and infection in the urinary tract, making it easier for the body to pass kidney stones. Berberine also has a diuretic effect, increasing urine production and helping to flush out the system of stone-forming substances. Furthermore, berberine's antioxidant properties help protect the kidneys from damage caused by oxidative stress, which can contribute to the formation of kidney stones.

By reducing inflammation, infection, and oxidative stress, Berberis vulgaris tinctures can help create an environment in the urinary tract that is more conducive to the passage of urinary calculi.

  1. Gather 250g of dried Berberis vulgaris roots and stems. Use a clean knife to chop them into small pieces.
  2. Add the chopped Berberis vulgaris to a clean glass jar with a lid. Use 1 cup of vodka as a solvent and pour it over the Berberis vulgaris.
  3. Close the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks. Shake the jar daily to help the tincture extract.
  4. After 2-3 weeks, strain the tincture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar. Discard the solids.
  5. Store the Berberis vulgaris tincture in a cool, dark place. Use 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, mixed with water or another liquid.

3. Juniperus communis

Juniperus communis tinctures contains essential oils like beta-pinene, alpha-pinene, and limonene, which have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that help reduce swelling and prevent infection in the urinary tract.

The diuretic properties of Juniperus communis tinctures help to increase urine production, which can help to flush out small stones and prevent their formation.

The combined effects of these properties make Juniperus communis tinctures a valuable remedy for people suffering from urinary calculus.

  1. Gather 250 grams of Juniperus communis berries and 750 ml of vodka.
  2. Combine the berries and vodka in a clean glass jar, making sure the berries are completely covered.
  3. Steep the mixture in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking the jar every day.

  5. Store the tincture in a cool, dark place. Take 10-20 drops, 3 times a day, as needed for urinary calculus.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra creams contains glycyrrhizin, a triterpenoid saponin that has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Glycyrrhizin also has a diuretic effect, increasing urine production and helping to flush out the kidneys and urinary tract. The licorice extract in these creams also contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects that can help protect the kidneys from damage. Additionally, glycyrrhizin has been found to inhibit the growth of certain types of bacteria that can contribute to urinary tract infections and the formation of urinary calculi.

By reducing inflammation and promoting urine production, glycyrrhiza glabra creams may help to prevent the formation of urinary calculi and alleviate symptoms associated with kidney stones.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of distilled water, 2 tablespoons of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root powder, 1 tablespoon of beeswax, 1 tablespoon of coconut oil, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin E oil.
  2. Heat the distilled water in a saucepan over low heat until it's warm.
  3. Add the dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root powder to the warm water and stir well. Let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture into a separate container and discard the solids. Add the beeswax, coconut oil, and vitamin E oil to the mixture. Stir until the beeswax is fully dissolved.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let it cool until it solidifies. Store the Glycyrrhiza glabra cream in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

2. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica creams contains the bioactive constituents histamine, acetylcholine, and polysaccharides.

These compounds have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and discomfort caused by urinary calculus, or kidney stones. The polysaccharides in Urtica dioica creams have been shown to inhibit the growth of certain bacteria that can contribute to the formation of kidney stones. By reducing inflammation and promoting urine production, Urtica dioica creams may help alleviate symptoms and promote recovery in individuals with urinary calculus.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Urtica dioica leaves, 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1/4 cup of beeswax, 2 tablespoons of shea butter, and 2 tablespoons of vitamin E oil.
  2. Dry the Urtica dioica leaves in a low-temperature oven (150°F - 200°F) for 1-2 hours. Then, grind the leaves into a fine powder.
  3. Melt the coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water. Stir until the beeswax is fully melted.
  4. Add the ground Urtica dioica powder, shea butter, and vitamin E oil to the melted mixture. Stir well to combine.
  5. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool until it reaches a consistency similar to thin honey. Pour the mixture into a container and let it cool completely before use.

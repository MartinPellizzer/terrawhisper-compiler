What Are The Main Causes Of Dysuria?

Dysuria, or painful urination, has several possible causes.

One main cause is a Urinary Tract Infection (UTI). A UTI occurs when bacteria enter the urinary system and multiply, causing inflammation and pain. This usually happens when bacteria from the skin or rectum enter the urethra, especially during sexual activity or when using a catheter. Another cause of dysuria is kidney stones. These are hard, pebble-like substances that form in the kidneys when minerals in the urine become concentrated.

Kidney stones can cause severe pain, especially during urination, as the stone moves through the urinary tract. The pain can be sharp and stabbing, and may be accompanied by a burning sensation. Overactive bladder is also a cause of dysuria. This condition is characterized by a sudden, urgent need to urinate, which can lead to frequent urination and pain during urination. Overactive bladder occurs when the muscles in the bladder contract too frequently, causing the bladder to empty too quickly.

This can lead to irritation and inflammation of the bladder and urethra, causing pain and discomfort during urination.

Below are the main causes of dysuria.

  • UTI: UTI, or urinary tract infection, is a cause of dysuria because the bacteria that infect the urinary tract irritate the bladder and urethra, causing a burning sensation when a person urinates.
  • Kidney stones: Dysuria occurs when a kidney stone moves through the urinary tract and presses against the bladder or urethra, causing a sharp pain or burning sensation while urinating.
  • Overactive bladder: Overactive bladder can cause dysuria because the frequent and sudden need to urinate can lead to incomplete emptying of the bladder, which can cause urine to leak back into the urethra, irritating it and causing pain or burning during urination.
  • Interstitial cystitis: Interstitial cystitis causes dysuria because the inflamed and irritated bladder lining makes it painful for the person to pass urine, causing a burning sensation when urinating.
  • Bacterial vaginosis: Bacterial vaginosis is a cause of dysuria because the abnormal bacteria in the vagina can cause inflammation and irritation, leading to painful urination and a burning sensation when passing urine.
  • Yeast infection: A yeast infection can cause dysuria because the fungus, typically Candida, irritates and inflames the urinary tract, especially the urethra, leading to a burning sensation and painful urination.

What Herbs To Avoid If You Have Dysuria?

If you have dysuria (painful urination), some herbs can make it worse.

Avoid 'Goldenseal' (Hydrastis canadensis) as it can irritate the urinary tract and increase discomfort. 'Juniper berries' (Juniperus communis) can also be problematic as they can cause kidney damage and worsen urinary issues.

'Yohimbe' (Pausinystalia johimbe) is another herb to steer clear of, as it can increase blood flow and worsen urinary problems. 'Ginseng' (Panax ginseng) can interact with medications for dysuria and make it worse. 'Saw palmetto' (Serenoa repens) may worsen urinary symptoms by blocking the hormone that helps with urination.

These herbs should be used with caution or avoided altogether if you have dysuria, as they may exacerbate the condition.

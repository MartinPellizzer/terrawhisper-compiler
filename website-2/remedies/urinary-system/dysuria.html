What are the causes of dysuria?

Dysuria, or painful urination, can be caused by several issues.

One of the main causes is Urinary Tract Infection (UTI). This is often due to E. coli, a type of bacteria that can enter the body through the urethra. The bacteria can multiply and cause an infection, leading to pain, burning sensations, and discomfort while urinating. Kidney stones, hard deposits that form in the kidneys, can also cause dysuria.

As the stones move, they can rub against the bladder and urethra, causing pain and discomfort. Bacterial vaginosis, an imbalance of bacteria in the vagina, can also lead to dysuria. This is because the bacteria can cause irritation and inflammation in the vagina and urethra, leading to pain and discomfort during urination. Lastly, vaginal yeast infection, a fungal infection that occurs when yeast overgrows in the vagina, can also cause dysuria.

The yeast can cause itching, redness, and irritation in the vagina and urethra, leading to painful urination.

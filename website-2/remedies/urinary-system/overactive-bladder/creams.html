The good news is that certain herbs have been shown to help calm the muscles in your bladder and make it easier to control your urination. Hypericum perforatum, or St. John's Wort, is another herb that has been traditionally used to help with bladder issues.

It has a natural calming effect that can help reduce the urge to urinate. Valeriana officinalis, or valerian root, is also a natural relaxant that can help calm the muscles in your bladder and reduce the frequency of urination. When you use herbal teas or creams for overactive bladder, you may notice a few benefits. You may find that you need to get up to use the bathroom less often, which can be a big relief if you're tired of getting up in the middle of the night. You may also find that you're able to hold your urine for longer periods of time, which can be helpful if you're doing activities like hiking or swimming.

Additionally, herbal creams and teas can be a natural and non-invasive way to manage your symptoms, which can be a big advantage if you're not a fan of medication.

1. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica creams contains compounds like histamine, tyramine, and caffeic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These properties help to calm the bladder muscles and reduce inflammation in the urethra and bladder. The caffeic acid in Urtica dioica creams has been shown to have a relaxing effect on the smooth muscle in the bladder, which can help to reduce symptoms of overactive bladder. The plant's anti-inflammatory properties can also help to reduce irritation and discomfort in the bladder and urethra, making it easier to manage overactive bladder symptoms.

By reducing inflammation and relaxing the bladder muscles, Urtica dioica creams may help to improve bladder control and reduce the frequency of urination.

  1. Gather 1 cup of Urtica dioica leaves, 1 cup of coconut oil, and 1/4 cup of beeswax.
  2. Dry the Urtica dioica leaves in a low oven (150°F) for 1 hour.
  3. Combine 1/2 cup of dried Urtica dioica leaves with 1 cup of coconut oil in a saucepan.
  4. Heat the mixture over low heat (150°F) for 2 hours. Strain the mixture and discard the leaves.
  5. Mix the strained coconut oil mixture with 1/4 cup of melted beeswax and pour into a jar. Let cool and solidify.

2. Hypericum perforatum

The flavonoids in the cream also have a soothing effect on the bladder lining, reducing irritation and discomfort.

By reducing inflammation and irritation, Hypericum perforatum creams may help to alleviate symptoms of overactive bladder.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of coconut oil, 1/2 cup of beeswax, 1/4 cup of shea butter, 2 tablespoons of Hypericum perforatum tincture, and 10 drops of tea tree oil.
  2. Melt coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler or a heat-proof bowl set over a pot of simmering water.
  3. Add shea butter to the melted mixture and stir until it's fully incorporated.
  4. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Stir in the Hypericum perforatum tincture and tea tree oil.
  5. Pour the mixture into a tin or jar and let it cool and solidify. Once solid, your Hypericum perforatum cream is ready to use.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for overactive bladder?

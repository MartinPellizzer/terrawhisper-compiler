9 Herbal Creams for Diabetics with Overactive Bladder

People with diabetes often struggle with overactive bladder, a condition where the bladder muscle contracts too often, causing sudden urges to urinate.

This can be due to nerve damage, known as neuropathy, which is a common complication of diabetes. When nerves are damaged, they can't send the right signals to the bladder, leading to frequent urination and incontinence. This can be very frustrating and stressful for people with diabetes, affecting their daily lives in many ways. They may have to limit their activities, avoid social events, and even experience anxiety and depression.

Simple tasks like traveling, exercising, or even cooking can become difficult and time-consuming. For those with diabetes, managing overactive bladder can be a challenge, especially when traditional treatments like medication or surgery may not be effective or suitable for everyone. This is where herbal creams come in as a potential solution. These creams are topical products that can be applied directly to the skin, where they can be absorbed and work locally to calm the bladder muscles and improve bladder control.

They often contain plant extracts that have been traditionally used to soothe and relax the muscles, reducing the urge to urinate and improving overall bladder function.

1. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica creams, also known as stinging nettle creams, have been used to help alleviate symptoms of overactive bladder.

The creams contain properties and constituents like urtica dioica extract, which has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. This means it can help reduce inflammation in the bladder and prevent spasms that can lead to urgency and frequency. The creams also contain compounds like flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have antioxidant and soothing effects, further reducing inflammation and discomfort. If you're considering using Urtica dioica creams for overactive bladder, start by consulting with a healthcare professional to ensure it's safe for you to use. They can recommend a suitable product and dosage.

When applying the cream, gently massage it into your skin, focusing on the lower abdomen area. You may need to apply it 2-3 times a day, as directed by the product instructions. When using Urtica dioica creams for overactive bladder, you may notice a reduction in symptoms like urgency, frequency, and incontinence. You might also experience less discomfort or pain during urination. Some users have reported improved bladder control and a decrease in nighttime awakenings due to urinary issues.

However, it's essential to note that results may vary, and individual experiences may differ.

Gently massage the cream into the skin until it's fully absorbed.

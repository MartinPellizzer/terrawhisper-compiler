What Are The Main Causes Of Overactive Bladder?

Overactive bladder is a condition where the bladder muscle contracts too often, leading to frequent urination.

There are several causes of this condition. Neurogenic bladder is one of them. It occurs when the nerves that control the bladder are damaged, either due to a birth defect or an injury. This damage disrupts the normal communication between the bladder and the brain, leading to an overactive bladder. Another cause is spinal cord injury.

When the spinal cord is injured, it can damage the nerves that control the bladder, leading to an overactive bladder. This is because the spinal cord acts as a messenger between the brain and the bladder, and damage to it can disrupt this communication. Diabetes is also a cause of overactive bladder. High blood sugar levels can damage the nerves that control the bladder, leading to an overactive bladder. This is because diabetes can damage the nerves over time, disrupting the normal function of the bladder. In addition to these causes, other factors such as aging, obesity, and certain medications can also contribute to overactive bladder.

However, the exact cause of overactive bladder can vary from person to person, and it's often a complex interplay of multiple factors.

Below are the main causes of overactive bladder.

  • Neurogenic bladder: Neurogenic bladder is a cause of overactive bladder because the nerves that control the bladder are damaged, leading to uncontrolled muscle contractions and frequent, sudden urges to urinate.
  • Spinal cord injury: Spinal cord injury can cause overactive bladder because the damaged nerves in the spinal cord can no longer send proper signals to the bladder, leading to sudden and frequent muscle contractions that cause the bladder to empty too quickly.
  • Diabetes: Diabetes can cause overactive bladder because high blood sugar levels damage nerves that control the bladder, leading to sudden and frequent urges to urinate.
  • Prostate enlargement: Prostate enlargement can cause overactive bladder because as the prostate gland grows, it can put pressure on the bladder and the urethra, leading to a weakened bladder that can contract too quickly, causing sudden and strong urges to urinate.
  • Caffeine: Caffeine is a cause of overactive bladder because it makes the bladder muscles contract more strongly and more often, making it harder to hold urine and leading to sudden urges to urinate.
  • Cystitis: Cystitis, an infection of the bladder, can cause overactive bladder by inflaming the bladder's lining and irritating its nerves, leading to sudden, frequent, and urgent needs to urinate.
  • Hypertension: High blood pressure, or hypertension, can cause overactive bladder because the pressure from the increased blood flow can weaken the muscles in the bladder, making it harder to control when it's full.

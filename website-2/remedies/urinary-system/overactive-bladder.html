What are the causes of overactive bladder?

Overactive bladder is a condition where the bladder muscle contracts too often, leading to sudden and uncontrollable urges to urinate.

There are several main causes of this condition. One cause is Neurogenic Bladder, a condition where the nerves that control the bladder are damaged, often due to a spinal cord injury or a neurological disorder. This damage disrupts the normal communication between the brain and the bladder, leading to an overactive bladder. Another cause is age. As we get older, our bladder muscles become weaker, and the nerves that control the bladder can become damaged. This can lead to an overactive bladder, especially in older adults. Diabetes is also a cause of overactive bladder.

High blood sugar levels can damage the nerves that control the bladder, leading to an overactive bladder. This is because diabetes can damage the nerves over time, disrupting the normal communication between the brain and the bladder. Spinal Cord Injury is another cause of overactive bladder. When the spinal cord is damaged, it can disrupt the normal communication between the brain and the bladder, leading to an overactive bladder. This is because the spinal cord plays a crucial role in controlling the bladder muscles. Finally, Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) is a cause of overactive bladder in men. BPH is a condition where the prostate gland becomes enlarged, putting pressure on the bladder and the urethra.

This can lead to an overactive bladder, as the bladder muscle contracts too often to try and empty the bladder.

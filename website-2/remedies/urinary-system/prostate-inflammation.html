What Are The Main Causes Of Prostate Inflammation?

Prostate inflammation can be caused by several factors.

Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) is a non-cancerous growth of the prostate gland. As the gland grows, it can press on the urethra, causing urine to flow slowly and leading to inflammation. This is due to the increased pressure on the gland and surrounding tissues. Prostatitis is an infection of the prostate gland, usually caused by bacteria.

When bacteria enter the prostate through the urethra, they can multiply and cause inflammation. This can be triggered by factors such as urinary tract infections (UTIs), kidney stones, or a weakened immune system. Prostatitis can also be caused by chronic inflammation, which can be a result of a lingering bacterial or viral infection. Ejaculation-induced inflammation, known as post-ejaculatory pain or epididymitis, can occur when bacteria from the urethra enter the epididymis, a tube-like structure behind the testicles that stores sperm. When sperm is released during ejaculation, bacteria can also be released, causing inflammation in the epididymis.

This type of inflammation is more common in men who have a history of UTIs or who are uncircumcised, as the foreskin can trap bacteria and create an ideal environment for infection.

Below are the main causes of prostate inflammation.

  • BPH: Benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) causes prostate inflammation because the enlarged prostate gland presses against the urethra, causing urine to back up and become trapped, leading to bacterial growth and infection in the prostate and urinary tract.
  • Prostatitis: Prostatitis is a cause of prostate inflammation because bacteria, viruses, or fungi can infect the prostate gland, leading to swelling, redness, and pain in the area.
  • Ej: Ejaculation can cause prostate inflammation because it leads to the backward flow of urine into the prostate gland, which can irritate and inflame the gland.
  • Bacterial Infection: Bacterial infection, such as prostatitis, occurs when bacteria like E. coli enter the prostate gland through the urethra, causing inflammation and swelling of the prostate.
  • Urinary Tract Infection: A Urinary Tract Infection (UTI) can cause prostate inflammation because the infection can spread from the bladder up to the prostate gland, leading to swelling and irritation in the prostate area.
  • Cancer: Cancer, specifically prostate cancer, causes prostate inflammation because the abnormal cells can block the normal flow of fluids through the prostate gland, leading to swelling and irritation of the surrounding tissues.
  • Fungal Infection: Fungal infections, such as candida, can cause prostate inflammation by entering the body through the bloodstream and colonizing in the prostate gland, leading to swelling, redness, and discomfort.

Ginkgo biloba can cause bleeding issues, including heavy menstrual bleeding and bleeding gums, which could be problematic for people with prostate inflammation. Additionally, licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) should be avoided due to its potential to raise blood pressure and worsen urinary problems.

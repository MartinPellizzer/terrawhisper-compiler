What are the causes of prostate inflammation?

Prostate inflammation can be caused by several factors.

Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) is a non-cancerous enlargement of the prostate gland, which can cause swelling and inflammation. This is often due to hormonal changes, particularly an increase in dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a male hormone that can stimulate prostate growth. Prostatitis is another major cause of prostate inflammation. This is a condition where the prostate gland becomes inflamed, often due to a bacterial infection. Bacteria like E. coli and Staph can enter the prostate through the urethra and cause an infection.

E. coli is a common type of bacteria that can cause urinary tract infections, and when it reaches the prostate, it can lead to inflammation. TB, or tuberculosis, is another rare cause of prostate inflammation. TB is a bacterial infection that can affect any part of the body, including the prostate. If TB bacteria enter the prostate, they can cause inflammation and damage to the gland. In addition to these causes, other factors like age, lifestyle, and genetics can also contribute to prostate inflammation. As men age, their prostate gland can become more susceptible to inflammation and enlargement.

A poor diet, lack of exercise, and smoking can also increase the risk of prostate inflammation.

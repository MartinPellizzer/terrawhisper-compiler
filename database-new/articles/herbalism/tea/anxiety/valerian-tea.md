 Valerian root has been used for centuries to treat anxiety and other conditions, and it is believed that this plant has calming properties due to its presence of compounds such as gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), which helps regulate nerve activity in the brain. Additionally, research indicates that valerian may decrease the levels of certain neurotransmitters associated with stress, such as cortisol and adrenaline, while increasing the production of others that promote relaxation, like gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA). These mechanisms, along with the herb's sedative effects, contribute to its ability to alleviate symptoms of anxiety and other sleep disorders.

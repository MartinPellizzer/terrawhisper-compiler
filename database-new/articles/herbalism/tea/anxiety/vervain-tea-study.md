
            This study examined the effects of Verbena officinalis extract (Vo.Cr) on various neuropharmacological models in mice. It was found that Vo.Cr at doses ranging from 100-500 mg/Kg significantly reduced seizure activity, including duration and mortality, in PTZ-induced seizures test. Additionally, Vo.Cr was shown to have anxiolytic effects as it increased time spent in open arms and decreased time spent in closed arms in the elevated plus maze test. It also improved the sleeping properties of animals, causing a decrease in onset time and increase in duration of sleep in thiopental-induced sleeping test. These results suggest that Verbena officinalis possesses anticonvulsant, anxiolytic, and sedative activities, providing scientific evidence for its use in treating neurological disorders such as epilepsy, anxiety, and insomnia.


            This study aimed to determine whether passionflower extract was effective in treating generalized anxiety disorder (GAD) and compared its efficacy to that of oxazepam, a commonly used medication for GAD. 
            
            The study found both Passiflora extract and oxazepam were effective in treating GAD, but there was no significant difference between the two at the end of the trial. Oxazepam showed a rapid onset of action, while Passiflora extract had a slower response. 
            
            Additionally, more problems related to impairment of job performance were reported by patients taking oxazepam compared to those using passionflower extract. 
            
            Overall, the study suggests that passionflower extract is an effective and safe treatment for GAD and has an advantage over oxazepam in terms of job performance impairment. Further research on larger sample sizes is needed to confirm these findings.


Hydro-alcohol extract of wild lettuce leaves showed anxiolytic activities as evaluated by EPM, OFT and marble burying test in mice models. The study found that hydro-alcohol extract of wild lettuce leaves possessed potent anxiolytic activity comparable to diazepam. The dried extract of the plant leaf in doses of 100, 200 and 400 mg/kg body weight was administered orally to mice for duration of 15 or 30 days and evaluated exploratory behaviour, locomotor and anxiolytic activities. The study concluded that hydro-alcohol extract of wild lettuce leaves exhibited potent anxiolytic activity and can be used as a natural alternative for the treatment of anxiety disorders.


            This review aims to assess the effectiveness of lavender administration on anxiety and anxiety-related conditions. The authors searched 5 electronic databases and identified 65 RCTs and 25 NRSs that met their criteria for inclusion. Overall, the qualitative synthesis found that 54 out of 65 RCTs reported at least a significant result in favor of lavender use for anxiety. The quantitative synthesis showed that lavender inhalation can significantly reduce anxiety levels, state anxiety, and trait anxiety as measured by validated scales. Lavender inhalation did not show a significant effect on systolic blood pressure as a physiological parameter of anxiety. Oral administration of lavender essential oil was found to be effective in the treatment of anxiety, while there is only an indication of an effect of reasonable size for inhalation due to the heterogeneity of available studies. Lavender essential oil administered through massage appears effective, but more high-quality RCTs with homogeneous study designs are needed to confirm these findings. Since treatments with lavender essential oil generally seem safe and simple, they are a therapeutic option that may be considered in some clinical contexts.

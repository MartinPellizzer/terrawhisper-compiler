
Our findings suggested that valerian could be a useful treatment option for improving sleep quality, reducing state anxiety, and decreasing depressive symptoms in hemodialysis (HD) patients.  The study was conducted using randomized design. There were 39 participants who were randomly allocated to the intervention group or control group. In the first phase of the study, 19 participants received valerian while the other 20 participants received a placebo. The second phase of the study had similar numbers of participants with the same allocation. 

The results showed that sleep quality improved in both groups, but the improvement was significantly higher in the intervention group compared to the control group. Similarly, the state anxiety scores and depressive symptoms were reduced in both groups, but the reduction was significantly lower in the intervention group compared to the control group in the second phase of the study. The findings suggest that valerian could be a useful treatment option for improving sleep quality, reducing state anxiety, and decreasing depressive symptoms in hemodialysis (HD) patients. Therefore, further research is needed to confirm these results and to determine the optimal dosage and duration of valerian treatment for improving these outcomes in HD patients.

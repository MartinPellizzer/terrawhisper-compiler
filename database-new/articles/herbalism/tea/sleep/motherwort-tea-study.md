
           In this study, motherwort (Leonurus cardiacae herba) is a perennial plant that is native to central Europe and Scandinavia but has also been introduced to North America. The study examines the compounds found in motherwort, including monoterpenes, diterpenes, triterpenes, nitrogen- containing compounds, phenylpropanoids, flavonoids, phenolic acids, volatile oils, sterols, and tannins. Historically, motherwort has been used internally for nervous heart conditions and digestive disorders, as well as externally in wounds and skin inflammations. The study also examines the pharmacological effects of motherwort, including its antibacterial, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic activity, as well as its effects on the heart and circulatory system. In clinical trials, sedative and hypotensive activity has been demonstrated.

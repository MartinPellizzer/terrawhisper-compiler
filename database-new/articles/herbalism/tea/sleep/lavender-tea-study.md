 According to this study, poor quality sleep affects a large portion of the UK population and many people turn to self-care remedies such as lavender oil aroma inhalation. While lavender oil is believed to have sedative or sleep enhancing properties and to cause few side effects, there is limited scientific evidence on its effectiveness in improving sleep architecture. The study identifies eight eligible studies that investigated the use of lavender oil aroma inhalation as a single intervention, including four randomised controlled trials, one counterbalanced trial, and three non-randomised controlled trials. However, most of these studies had small samples and methodological limitations, so further research is needed to investigate the true effect of lavender oil aroma inhalation on sleep. The study suggests that early results are promising but should be viewed with caution, and the use of wake outcome measures should also be considered due to the clinical significance of waking function as influenced by sleep.

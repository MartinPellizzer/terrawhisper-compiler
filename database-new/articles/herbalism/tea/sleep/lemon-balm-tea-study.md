A study examined whether valerian/lemon balm could improve sleep patterns in women aged 50-60 who were experiencing menopause and sleep disruptions, specifically hot flashes. The study used a random sampling method with two groups of 50 participants each; an intervention group that received valerian/lemon balm and a placebo group that received no treatment. The Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index was administered pre- and post-intervention to measure the effectiveness of the treatment. Results showed that there was a significant difference between the two groups, with the experimental group experiencing reduced levels of sleep disorders compared to the placebo group. Therefore, valerian/lemon balm may be an effective treatment option for reducing symptoms of sleep disorder during menopause.

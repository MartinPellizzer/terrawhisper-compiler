
            The present study investigates the effects of romaine lettuce leaves extract and skullcap root extract on sleep behaviors in animal models. HPLC analysis revealed that the main phenolic compounds present in these extracts include lactucopicrin, chlorogenic acid, caffeic acid, chicoric acid, baicalin, baicalein, wogonin, and delta wave. The mixture of the two extracts significantly increased total sleep time by 50.9% compared to the control in pentobarbital-induced sleep model. Electroencephalography (EEG) analysis showed that the mixture enhanced non-rapid eye movement (NREM) with an increase in delta wave of around 40%. The RE/SE mixture also decreased caffeine-induced wake time and increased NREM and delta wave in caffeine-induced wake model. Additionally, the RE/SE mixture bound to a gamma-aminobutyric acid type A (GABAA)-benzodiazepine (BZD) receptor stronger than the single extracts.

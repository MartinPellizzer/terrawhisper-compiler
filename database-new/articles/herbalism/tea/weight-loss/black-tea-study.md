
            The study aims to determine whether green tea has a function in body weight regulation through meta-analysis. The studies identified were about weight loss (WL) and weight maintenance (WM) after green tea supplementation. Effect sizes were computed and aggregated based on a random-effects model, and several moderators were examined. Catechins significantly decreased body weight and maintained it after a period of WL. However, the influence of high habitual caffeine intake and ethnicity as moderators failed to reach significance. The study suggests that catechins or an EGCG-caffeine mixture have a small positive effect on WL and WM, but the results indicate that habitual caffeine intake and ethnicity may be moderators of this effect.

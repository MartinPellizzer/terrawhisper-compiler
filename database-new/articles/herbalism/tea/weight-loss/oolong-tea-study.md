
            The study examined the anti-obesity effects of oolong tea on diet-induced overweight or obese subjects. The study involved 102 participants who were assigned to consume 8 grams of oolong tea per day for six weeks. Body weight, height, and waist measurements were taken at the beginning and end of the study, as well as ultrasonic echo measurements of subcutaneous fat thickness on the abdomen three centimeters to the right of the navel. Additionally, the effects of oolong tea ingestion on plasma triglyceride (TG) and total cholesterol (TC) levels were determined. Inhibition of pancreatic lipase by oolong tea extract and catechins in vitro was also assessed. The results showed that 70% of severely obese participants lost more than 1 kg in body weight, including 22% who lost more than 3 kg. Obesity and overweight participants also experienced significant weight loss, with subcutaneous fat content decreasing in 12% of subjects.


A new study examines the effects of peppermint juice on human biochemical and anthropometric profiles and blood pressure, based on the administration of peppermint juice twice daily for 30 days. The results indicate that 41.5% of the subjects showed a reduction in glycemia, 66.9% in total cholesterol levels, 58.5% in triacylglycerides, and that 52.3% presented an increase in HDL-c (high-density lipoprotein cholesterol). Furthermore, 70% of the subjects showed a decrease in GOT (glutamic-oxaloacetic transaminase) levels, 74.5% in GPT (glutamic-pyruvic transaminase) levels and that 52.5% showed a decrease in blood pressure. Lastly, 48.7% of the subjects presented a decrease in BMI.

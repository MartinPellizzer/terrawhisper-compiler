 Constipation is a condition in which bowel movements occur less frequently than normal, or when stools are hard, dry, and difficult to pass. It is estimated that 20 million people worldwide suffer from constipation every year. This problem can negatively affect the quality of life of those affected, leading to discomfort, pain, and reduced productivity. The main causes of constipation include age-related changes in bowel habits, a diet low in fiber, lack of physical activity, stress, and certain medical conditions such as irritable bowel syndrome. Constipation can also lead to other health problems such as hemorrhoids, anal fissures, and colon cancer.

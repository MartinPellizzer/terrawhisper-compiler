
            Licorice (Glycyrrhiza uralensis) has been used as a natural remedy for constipation due to its laxative properties. However, few studies have examined the mechanism of action and therapeutic effects of this herbal mixture in loperamide-induced constipated rats. In this study, we evaluated the effects of LGC on excretion parameters, histological structure, mucin secretion, and related protein levels in a rat model of constipation. We found that LGC significantly increased stool and urine excretion, restored intestinal transit ratio to normal levels, increased mucosal tissue, and mucin production. The laxative effect of LGC was closely related to the expression of muscarinic acetylcholine receptors (mAChR) and downstream signals. Our results suggest that LGC may be a potential natural remedy for constipation, which is an important public health issue worldwide.

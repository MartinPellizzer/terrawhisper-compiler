
Slippery elm is an herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to treat a variety of gastrointestinal problems, including acid reflux. The primary active ingredient in slippery elm bark is mucilage, which is a sticky substance that coats and protects the lining of the digestive tract. When consumed as a tea, slippery elm mucilage can form a barrier between the stomach acid and the esophagus, reducing the risk of acid reflux symptoms such as heartburn, regurgitation, and coughing. Additionally, slippery elm bark contains antioxidants that may help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, further alleviating acid reflux symptoms. Overall, slippery elm tea is a natural and effective remedy for acid reflux, and can be easily incorporated into a daily routine.

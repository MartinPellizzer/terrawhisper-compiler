The leaves of Chamomilla recutita are relatively simple in terms of complexity. They are bipinnate and finely dissected, forming a feathery appearance. The leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and they exhibit a horizontal to slightly drooping orientation.

In terms of size, the leaves typically measure between 1.5 to 5 centimeters (0.6 to 2 inches) in length and 0.2 to 0.8 centimeters (0.08 to 0.3 inches) in width. They are thin and have a bright green color. The leaf texture is smooth and glabrous, which means they lack hair or trichomes on their surface.

The leaves have an acute apex, and their base is cuneate, meaning it tapers to a narrow, wedge-like shape. The margins of the leaves are finely serrated, giving them an intricate appearance. The venation pattern is pinnate, with a prominent midrib running along the center of the leaf.

Notably, Chamomilla recutita leaves are sessile, meaning they are directly attached to the stem without the presence of petioles. Stipules, small leaf-like structures at the base of the leaf stalk, are absent in this species.

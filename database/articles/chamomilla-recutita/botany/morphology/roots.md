The root structure of Chamomilla recutita is primarily taproot, which means it has a central, thick root from which smaller lateral roots branch out. These lateral roots, further characterized by dichotomous branching, contribute to the overall stability and nutrient absorption of the plant.

The root tips of Chamomilla recutita are sharp and pointed, facilitating penetration into the soil and aiding in the plant's anchorage. Root density in this herb is notable, with an average of around 350 roots per square meter, ensuring efficient resource uptake from the surrounding soil.

When it comes to root depth, Chamomilla recutita typically extends to a range of 10 to 20 centimeters (4 to 8 inches) below the surface, which is well-suited for its growth and nutrient acquisition. The roots of this herb have a diameter ranging from 0.2 to 0.5 centimeters (0.08 to 0.2 inches), and their length can reach up to 30 centimeters (12 inches).

The root color of Chamomilla recutita roots is generally whitish to pale brown, and their surface texture is smooth, often covered with fine root hairs. However, this plant does not form root nodules.

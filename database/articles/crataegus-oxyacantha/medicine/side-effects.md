Hawthorn can have some side effects if used improperly, such as when a person consumes excess amount or uses it for treating incompatible conditions. Some of the major negative impacts are discussed below.

Hawthorn might reduce blood pressure and hence lead to dizziness, weakness or even fainting spells. It can also interfere with the medication used for controlling blood pressure, causing adverse reactions if used simultaneously. In addition, it has the potential to interact with medications for heart conditions, such as those used for treating arrhythmia (irregular heartbeats), angina or irregularities in the heart's electrical activity. Therefore, caution must be taken while using Hawthorn with other medicines for managing these cardiac issues.

Hawthorn has anticoagulant properties that can interfere with blood clotting and increase the risk of bleeding. This might lead to excessive bruising or a greater tendency to bleed from wounds, which could potentially become dangerous in case of injuries. It is advisable to avoid using Hawthorn if you have any conditions involving a higher risk of bleeding, such as those associated with bleeding disorders or certain surgeries. Consult a health care provider for guidance on how this might affect your situation.

Also, excessive consumption of Hawthorn may cause gastrointestinal side effects, including nausea, vomiting and stomach upset. These symptoms can be aggravated by other factors such as existing digestive issues or the use of certain medications that interact with Hawthorn. People experiencing any form of diarrhea, constipation, inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) or irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), may particularly need to exercise caution when using this herb.

Hawthorn has the potential to affect hormone levels in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as breast tenderness and irregular menstrual cycles. This can be an issue for women who are pregnant or breastfeeding, as well as those with a history of hormonal imbalances, particularly if they are using medications that interact with the herb. It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using Hawthorn in these cases.

Lastly, Hawthorn can interact with certain chemicals commonly present in alcoholic beverages (in particular quercetin and tannins) leading to stomach discomfort or even more severe reactions if consumed together. For optimal safety, it is advisable to refrain from mixing Hawthorn with alcoholic drinks.

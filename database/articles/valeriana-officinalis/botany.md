Valerian (Valeriana officinalis) belongs to Botany's domain Eukaryota and kingdom Plantae within Life forms, further categorized into the phylum Magnoliophyta which includes flowering plants like ours in question here; it then goes through class Mamonopsida under subclass Rosidae. It is a part of order Rubiales with family Valerianaceae as its member genus. Finally coming to species level, this specific one being "officinalis". Common names for the plant include garden valeriant or common vervain among others depending on locality and usage patterns worldwide throughout history up till present time frame making it a well-known botanical name amongst different groups of people across various cultures.

This is in reference to Valerian's several subspecies; notably "ciliata" that can be distinguished through distinct serrated leaf margins, while 'valeriifolia', another variant features more broad and oblong leaves relative as per their respective morphological traits setting them apart visibly for easy identification purposes among expert botanists.

Geographically distributed throughout Eurasia and parts of North America this herbaceous plant thrives mainly in woodlands, grassland ecosystems and mountain slopes with adequate light conditions that enable it to grow up successfully. Furthermore adapted environmental factors like humus-rich soils have proven crucial for their natural habitats as well due largely attributed towards them having such properties encompassed by rich nutrients necessary for optimal growth rates observed in these locations thus far recorded across time spans showing this species resilience and adaptability despite varying conditions encountered over years.


Galangal's ability to enhance skin health refers to its capacity to promote the skin's well-being, primarily by its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These properties are attributed to the constituents present in galangal, notably its rich content of polyphenolic compounds such as flavonoids and tannins. Galangal contains approximately 4-6% of essential oils, mainly composed of cineol, methyl cinnamate, and ethyl cinnamate. These constituents possess potent antioxidant effects that combat free radicals, preventing oxidative stress and cellular damage. Additionally, galangal's anti-inflammatory properties, driven by compounds like galangin and quercetin, aid in soothing and calming skin irritation or redness.

Enhancing skin health with galangal proves beneficial for various dermatological conditions. Its anti-inflammatory properties make it effective against skin irritations, eczema, and acne. The antioxidant-rich nature of galangal helps in fighting premature aging signs, such as wrinkles, fine lines, and loss of skin elasticity. Furthermore, its ability to neutralize free radicals supports the skin's natural defense mechanisms, reducing the risk of skin cancer caused by UV radiation damage. The anti-microbial properties of galangal's constituents also contribute to its use in treating skin infections and promoting overall skin health.

Various parts of the galangal plant are utilized to harness its skin-enhancing benefits. The rhizome, or root, is the primary part used, containing the highest concentration of beneficial compounds. Extracts from galangal rhizomes are employed in skincare products like creams, serums, and masks to leverage their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties for improving skin health. Additionally, galangal extracts are sometimes included in dietary supplements or consumed orally to complement topical applications, offering holistic benefits for skin wellness.

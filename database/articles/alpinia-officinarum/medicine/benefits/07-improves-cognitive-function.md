Galangal's ability to aid in improving cognitive function refers to its capacity to enhance various mental processes, including memory, attention, reasoning, and overall brain performance. This plant contains specific constituents that contribute significantly to this health benefit. The primary bioactive compounds responsible for enhancing cognitive function in galangal are flavonoids and phenolic compounds. Research has identified flavonoids such as galangin, kaempferide, and quercetin, along with phenolic compounds like 1'-acetoxychavicol acetate, as key contributors to its cognitive-boosting properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and neuroprotective effects, shielding the brain from oxidative stress and inflammation, thereby supporting cognitive health.

The improved cognitive function attributed to galangal can aid in managing and potentially reducing the risk of neurodegenerative conditions like Alzheimer's disease and dementia. Studies have suggested that the bioactive compounds present in galangal may help in preventing age-related cognitive decline by protecting neurons, enhancing neural pathways, and reducing the accumulation of beta-amyloid plaques associated with Alzheimer's disease. Moreover, the cognitive-enhancing properties of galangal may also benefit individuals experiencing cognitive impairments due to stress, aging, or neurological disorders, potentially improving their mental clarity and cognitive abilities.

Various parts of the galangal plant are utilized to harness its cognitive-boosting benefits. The rhizome, or underground stem, is the most commonly used part for its medicinal properties. It is rich in the aforementioned flavonoids and phenolic compounds, which are extracted for their cognitive-enhancing effects. Galangal rhizomes are processed to produce extracts, powders, or supplements that are incorporated into traditional medicine or health supplements aimed at improving cognitive function and supporting brain health. Additionally, galangal rhizomes are used in culinary practices, where their inclusion in dishes is believed to provide not only flavor but also potential cognitive health benefits.

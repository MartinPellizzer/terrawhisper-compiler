Galangal is utilized in modern medicine for its various health benefits, primarily due to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. It's employed to aid digestion, alleviate nausea, and relieve gastrointestinal discomfort. Additionally, studies suggest its potential in managing diabetes, reducing oxidative stress, and combating certain cancers due to its bioactive compounds.

Galangal contains key bioactive compounds like galangin, galangol, and kaempferol, renowned for their anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticancer properties. Additionally, its essential oils, comprising cineol, pinene, and terpineol, offer antimicrobial effects. Phenolic compounds, tannins, and flavonoids contribute to its diverse medicinal benefits, aiding in scavenging free radicals and reducing inflammation.

Galangal offers versatile medicinal preparations: Galangal tea, made from fresh or dried root, aids digestion and alleviates cold symptoms. Galangal extract, oil, or capsules are used for their concentrated benefits. Tinctures and poultices provide topical relief, while syrups and ointments are applied for various skin conditions. These preparations offer diverse methods of consumption and application, catering to different health needs.

Despite its benefits, precautions are necessary when using galangal. High doses may cause stomach upset or allergic reactions. Pregnant and breastfeeding individuals should avoid excessive intake due to insufficient safety data. Patients on blood-thinning medications should consult healthcare providers due to potential interactions. Overall, moderate consumption in culinary amounts is generally safe, but therapeutic usage requires supervision and consideration of individual health conditions.

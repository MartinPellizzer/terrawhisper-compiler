Nettle (Urtica dioica) belongs to a plant kingdom within the Plantae domain and represents stinging hairs that are characteristic of this species found all across most continents, with varying climates from tropical rainforests down to Mediterranean temperatures – including Northern temperate forests; hence its ability for adaptation. There have also been various common names given in different parts of these regions like "Sting Nettle," or simply referred as just 'Nettles' where it is more frequently encountered due primarily by farmers and gardeners on whose gardens are occasionally invaded with this plant species during late Spring seasons often viewed either beneficial (providing habitat for insects) but also a nuisance.

In regards to its morphology, the Nettle exhibits erect annual or perennial herbaceous shrub-like stems that can reach up to 1 meter tall at maximum heights with large oval leaves which are serrated on edges for defensive purposes against grazing animals and insects; additionally sport an alternate growth habit – one of its characteristic features.

There exists two distinct variants, known as "Urtica dioica subsp. galeopsifolia" (Greek Nettle) with larger leaves more commonly seen in Mediterranean regions but slightly lesser to UTica's typical leaf size and shape; the second form or variant is referred 'White-Flowered', due its specific unique white flowers which contrast against other variations colored generally greenish yellow.

The geographic distribution includes a wide spread through all continents, excluding Antarctica primarily where it thrives mainly in damp soil conditions such as bogs and marshes along riverside or coastal areas; also adapting well to disturbed sites like roadsides among different parts of the world from arid deserts up into tropical rainforests.

Nettles show rapid development that may be attributed partly due its ability for vegetative reproduction (asexual) where roots spread along a considerable distance while seeds can also travel far distances through wind, making this plant an adaptable species despite habitat variability on the planet Earth effectively sustaining itself across diverse landscapes – even under various stress conditions.

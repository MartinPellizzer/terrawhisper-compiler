Goji Berry thrives best under partial shade or full sun exposure with temperatures ranging from -5°C to up to30°C (-9 Fahrenheit to around86 °F). It requires well-drained soil that is slightly acidic (pH 4–7), which allows it ample aeration and nutrient absorption. Include good air circulation for better growth results in your planting location as the shrub can grow upwards of about four meters tall, with its leaves turning yellow when deprived from sufficient light or water supply due to poor conditions like dense shade.

To successfully cultivate Goji Berry bushes effectively it is suggested you transplant seedlings between February and August, making sure they are at least 1 meter apart in a sunny position where soil quality meets the ideal criteria discussed earlier for their healthy growth development over time - taking into account that proper pruning might be needed from around five years post planting to boost flower & fruit production.

Provide regular water supply during summer months but do not oversaturate as this can lead to root rot and fungal issues affecting the entire bush, instead let soil dry out slightly between irrigation periods ensuring roots grow more vigorously in search for adequate moisture levels - be sure never too apply any chemical fertilizers that might disturb essential balance within its natural ecosystem.

Harvest Goji Berries when they reach their full maturity after three or four months of blooming, which usually occurs around late summer to early autumn (depending on your specific region's climate). To ensure maximum yield and fruit quality always use scissors for pruning off clusters instead tearing them by hand that could potentially damage surrounding berries.

Unfortunately this plant is susceptible towards certain pests such as mealy bugs or mites so inspect plants regularly; employ integrated approaches using companion plantings - those known to deter insects, applying biological control measures where appropriate while avoiding chemical treatments except only when absolutely necessary according the local regulations in your area. It’s also important not neglect monitoring and identifying symptoms for any diseases like powdery mildew or root rot that might compromise its health negatively affecting future production potential significantly too if left unattended over time due to lack proper management strategy implemented promptly upon detection signs of distress within the plant system itself

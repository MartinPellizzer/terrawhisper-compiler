It's effective on various types of burns including sunburns, minor scalds, and chemical burns.

For similar reasons, it reduces the swelling and inflammation that may result from allergies, insect bites, cuts or bruises.

            Promoting wound healing is another key function of Aloe Vera. The gel has antibacterial properties that help prevent infection while also speeding up the growth of new tissue cells at the wound site. This allows wounds to heal more quickly and reduces the risk of scarring.

It helps relieve conditions such as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) by soothing the gut lining and improving bowel movements. Moreover, it can help with constipation due to its laxative effect and the high water content in the gel.

It contains powerful antioxidants that boost immunity, protecting against diseases by enhancing the body's ability to fight off harmful pathogens. Furthermore, this resilient plant can be used to treat heartburn by neutralizing stomach acid and reducing inflammation in the esophagus.

            Aloe Vera is also helpful for oral health by containing components that inhibit the growth of bacteria responsible for tooth decay and gum disease. It can even detoxify the body as it contains a high concentration of minerals, which help purge toxins from cells and tissues.

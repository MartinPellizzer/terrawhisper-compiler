
Supporting digestive health refers to the ability of Aloe vera to promote and maintain a healthy gastrointestinal system. Aloe vera contains various constituents that contribute to this particular benefit, primarily polysaccharides such as acemannan. Acemannan is a complex carbohydrate found in Aloe vera gel and is known for its ability to support digestive health by aiding in the regulation of bowel movements, reducing inflammation in the GI tract, and promoting the growth of beneficial gut bacteria. Aloe vera also contains enzymes like amylase and lipase that assist in breaking down sugars and fats, aiding digestion. Studies have shown that Aloe vera gel contains approximately 55-78% water, 8-14% mucopolysaccharides (such as acemannan), and various other compounds like amino acids, vitamins (including vitamin C, E, and B12), minerals (such as calcium, magnesium, and zinc), and enzymes, all contributing to its digestive health-promoting properties.

The digestive health benefits of Aloe vera can aid in alleviating conditions such as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), constipation, and acid reflux. The presence of polysaccharides and anti-inflammatory compounds in Aloe vera gel can soothe and calm the gastrointestinal tract, reducing discomfort associated with digestive issues. Moreover, Aloe vera's ability to support a healthy balance of gut bacteria contributes to overall digestive wellness, potentially reducing symptoms of bloating, gas, and indigestion.

Primarily, the gel obtained from the inner leaf of the Aloe vera plant is used for its digestive health benefits. The gel contains the highest concentration of beneficial compounds, including polysaccharides like acemannan and various enzymes. This gel can be extracted directly from the leaves and consumed as a juice or added to beverages. Additionally, Aloe vera supplements in the form of capsules or tablets, containing concentrated forms of the gel, are also utilized to support digestive health. However, it's important to note that while the gel provides numerous benefits, the latex or sap found near the skin of the Aloe vera leaf contains compounds like aloin, which can have laxative effects and may not be suitable for everyone, especially in high doses.

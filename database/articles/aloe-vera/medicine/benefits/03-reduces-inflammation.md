Reducing inflammation refers to the body's natural response to combat harmful stimuli, such as pathogens or injury, to begin the healing process. Aloe vera's anti-inflammatory properties primarily stem from its rich composition of bioactive compounds, notably polysaccharides, phenolic compounds like flavonoids and tannins, and sterols such as campesterol and β-sitosterol. Aloe vera contains approximately 75 potentially active constituents, among which the polysaccharides (acemannan being the primary one) stand out due to their anti-inflammatory effects. Acemannan promotes immune system modulation and has shown significant anti-inflammatory activity by inhibiting the production of various inflammatory mediators.

The reduction of inflammation facilitated by Aloe vera contributes positively to several health conditions, particularly skin ailments like sunburn, psoriasis, and acne. The plant's application helps soothe irritated skin, lessens redness, and aids in the healing of burns and wounds due to its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. Additionally, the ingestion of Aloe vera in various forms such as juice or supplements may assist in alleviating internal inflammations associated with conditions like irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) and arthritis.

For its anti-inflammatory benefits, various parts of the Aloe vera plant are utilized. The gel obtained from the inner leaf contains the highest concentration of bioactive compounds, making it the primary source for topical applications, such as in creams, gels, or lotions for skin conditions. Additionally, Aloe vera latex, found just beneath the plant's skin, contains anthraquinones that possess laxative properties. However, it's important to note that excessive intake of the latex may lead to adverse effects, thus the gel is predominantly preferred for its anti-inflammatory effects both topically and when used internally in controlled doses.

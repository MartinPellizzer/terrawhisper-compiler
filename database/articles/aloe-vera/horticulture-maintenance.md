Pruning and maintenance are essential to ensure a healthy Aloe vera plant. Remove dead or yellowing leaves at the base using sterilized pruning shears. Regularly inspect the plant for pests, particularly aphids and mealybugs, and treat promptly with insecticidal soap. Rotate potted Aloe vera plants to promote even sun exposure and prevent one-sided growth. Fertilize sparingly during the dormant season, reducing the frequency to every 8 to 12 weeks. To propagate, separate offsets or "pups" from the main plant and replant in fresh soil. With proper care, Aloe vera not only adds aesthetic appeal to your garden but also serves as a valuable natural remedy.

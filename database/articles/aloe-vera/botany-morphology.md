In terms of morphology, Aloe vera is characterized by a succulent stem with a rosette arrangement of lanceolate, succulent leaves. The stem, cylindrical and waxy, can reach heights of 60-90 cm, while the leaves, with parallel venation and a smooth, fleshy texture, exhibit an upright orientation. The plant's structure is adapted to arid conditions, featuring water storage tissues in its succulent leaves and a shallow, fibrous root system with dichotomous branching.

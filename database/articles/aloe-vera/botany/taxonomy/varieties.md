Aloe vera comes in several varieties, each with unique characteristics. The most common type is Aloe barbadensis miller, recognized by its thick green leaves with serrated edges. This variety is widely used in skincare products and has soothing and healing properties.

The Aloe arborescens, or tree aloe, stands out as a large, multi-branched shrub or small tree with long, narrow, blue-green leaves. Aloe saponaria, also known as the soap aloe, has soft, toothless leaves filled with gel, making it suitable for cleansing and skincare.

Aloe plicatilis, the fan aloe, features a unique fan-shaped leaf arrangement. Aloe maculata, or the zebra aloe, has green leaves with white spots or stripes, adding a decorative touch to gardens and landscapes.

Aloe brevifolia is a miniature Aloe vera with stubby, compact leaves. Aloe ciliaris, or the climbing aloe, boasts slender, arching leaves and coral-colored tubular flowers.

Aloe juvenna, the tiger tooth aloe, has upright leaves with spiky white edges, making it suitable for indoor succulent collections. Aloe variegata, or the partridge breast aloe, is slow-growing and features variegated leaves, making it a good fit for rock gardens.

Lastly, the spiral aloe, Aloe polyphylla, showcases a captivating spiral leaf arrangement. Native to South Africa, it's a rare and unique succulent that demands specific care.


In traditional folk medicine, Dong Quai (Angelica sinensis) holds a revered status across various cultures. Known as "female ginseng," it's a cornerstone in Chinese traditional medicine, primarily used for women's health. It's believed to regulate menstruation, alleviate menopausal symptoms, and promote blood circulation. Additionally, in Korean and Japanese traditional medicine, Dong Quai is utilized similarly for gynecological issues and as a general health tonic. Its usage extends to herbal remedies addressing anemia, arthritis, and improving overall vitality, with the dried root commonly employed in teas, tinctures, and powders.

Ancient cultures utilized Dong Quai not just for medicinal purposes but also in divination practices. In Chinese history, the root was used in rituals, burned for its aromatic properties, and its unique shape was believed to hold mystical powers. This plant's association with spirituality and the metaphysical led to its use in predicting future events, especially in Taoist rituals and spiritual practices.

Legends and myths surround Dong Quai in various cultures. Chinese folklore depicts Dong Quai as a symbol of female energy, often associated with the female reproductive system and fertility. Legends speak of its origins tied to angelic intervention, attributing its healing properties to celestial beings. Some tales also describe Dong Quai's discovery as a result of a dream guiding an individual to the plant, emphasizing its mystical origins and healing powers. Across cultures, Dong Quai is shrouded in myths that highlight its significance in promoting well-being and vitality.

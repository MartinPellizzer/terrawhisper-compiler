
Dong Quai (Angelica sinensis) can be cultivated in gardens or landscapes with specific considerations. It is a biennial or perennial plant that thrives in well-drained, moist soil with partial shade. When planting, ensure the seeds are sown at a depth of about 0.5 to 1 centimeter in the soil. The spacing between seeds should be approximately 30 to 45 centimeters apart to allow ample room for growth. Regular watering is essential, especially during dry periods, to maintain soil moisture levels.

Ideal growing conditions for Dong Quai include a cool climate with temperatures ranging between 15 to 20 degrees Celsius. It flourishes in partial shade or dappled sunlight, requiring a location with good air circulation to prevent fungal issues. The soil should be rich in organic matter and have a pH level of around 6.5 to 7.5. Incorporating compost or well-rotted manure into the soil before planting aids in fertility and drainage. Mulching the soil around the plants helps retain moisture and suppresses weed growth, contributing to healthier growth of Dong Quai.

Pruning and maintenance are crucial for sustaining the health of Dong Quai plants. Deadheading spent flowers can encourage continuous growth and prevent self-seeding, focusing the plant's energy on root and foliage development. Regularly remove any diseased or damaged leaves to prevent the spread of infections. In autumn, when the plant starts to die back, cutting the stems close to the ground helps prepare for the next growing season. Additionally, applying a layer of organic mulch in late autumn safeguards the plant's roots from extreme temperatures during winter, ensuring a stronger resurgence in the following spring.

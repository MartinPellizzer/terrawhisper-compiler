Dong Quai's ability to support liver health refers to its capacity to enhance liver function and promote its overall well-being. This is primarily attributed to its constituents, such as ferulic acid and ligustilide. Ferulic acid, a phenolic compound found abundantly in Dong Quai, exhibits antioxidant properties, protecting liver cells from oxidative damage. Studies indicate that ferulic acid content in Dong Quai ranges from approximately 0.5% to 2% in its dried root. Ligustilide, another essential component, demonstrates hepatoprotective effects by aiding in the regulation of liver enzymes and reducing inflammation. Dong Quai contains ligustilide levels averaging around 0.2% to 1% in its root.

This particular benefit of supporting liver health contributes significantly to conditions such as liver inflammation, hepatic oxidative stress, and liver function impairment. Dong Quai's compounds, particularly ferulic acid and ligustilide, exert hepatoprotective effects, aiding in the prevention and mitigation of liver diseases, including fatty liver disease and liver damage caused by toxins or medications.

Various parts of Dong Quai are utilized to support liver health, with the root being the primary component used for this purpose. The root contains the highest concentrations of ferulic acid and ligustilide, the key constituents responsible for its hepatoprotective effects. Typically, dried Dong Quai root is utilized in various preparations such as teas, tinctures, extracts, and capsules to harness these liver-supporting properties. The root is processed and incorporated into these forms to ensure the retention of its beneficial compounds for promoting liver health.

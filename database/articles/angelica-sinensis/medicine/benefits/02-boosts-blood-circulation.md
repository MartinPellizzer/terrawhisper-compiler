Dong Quai's ability to enhance blood circulation refers to its capacity to improve the movement of blood throughout the body, ensuring a more efficient transport of oxygen, nutrients, and immune cells to various tissues and organs. This circulatory boost is attributed to certain constituents within Dong Quai, notably ferulic acid and ligustilide. Ferulic acid, present in Dong Quai in quantities ranging from 0.5% to 1.7%, possesses antioxidant properties that aid in widening blood vessels, enhancing blood flow, and preventing blood clot formation. Meanwhile, ligustilide, found in Dong Quai at levels of approximately 1.5% to 2.5%, contributes to vasodilation, relaxing blood vessels, and promoting smoother blood circulation.

The improved blood circulation facilitated by Dong Quai offers benefits in various health conditions. It aids in addressing peripheral vascular diseases like Raynaud's phenomenon, enhancing blood flow to extremities and reducing symptoms of cold hands and feet. Furthermore, it supports cardiovascular health by potentially reducing the risk of blood clots and improving overall blood vessel function. Improved circulation can also contribute to better oxygenation of tissues, potentially aiding in wound healing and reducing muscle fatigue.

The root of Dong Quai is primarily used to harness its blood circulation-boosting properties. Extracts derived from the root, particularly those rich in ferulic acid and ligustilide, are utilized in various forms such as capsules, tinctures, and syrups to ensure a more systemic delivery of the beneficial constituents. These preparations allow for convenient consumption while targeting blood circulation issues and related health concerns.

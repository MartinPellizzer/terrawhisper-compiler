Horse chestnut (Aesculus hippocastanum) boasts diverse medicinal benefits supported by scientific evidence. Its positive effects on circulatory health are evident, enhancing blood flow by up to 20%, reducing the risk of venous insufficiency, and preventing circulatory disorders. The herb's anti-inflammatory properties significantly reduce swelling, with clinical trials showing a 30% decrease, and improve edema management. Horse chestnut strengthens capillaries, decreasing fragility by approximately 25%, and addresses varicose veins, resulting in a 40% reduction in pain and a 30% decrease in swelling.

In the realm of joint health, horse chestnut's anti-inflammatory properties contribute to a 30% reduction in joint pain, and it proves effective in managing conditions like arthritis. Its topical applications reduce itching associated with skin conditions by approximately 40%.

Key constituents like aescin, flavonoids, tannins, saponins, coumarins, phytosterols, proanthocyanidins, fatty acids, minerals, and vitamins contribute to these health benefits. Preparations include drying and grinding, tinctures, decoctions, capsules, topical applications, teas, and various infusions. However, precautions are essential, including consultation with healthcare professionals, precise dosage adherence, avoidance of raw consumption, awareness of allergies, caution for pregnant or breastfeeding women, monitoring for bleeding disorders and liver function, consideration for diabetes patients, awareness of potential interactions with medications, and avoiding prolonged use without medical supervision. These precautions ensure the safe integration of horse chestnut into healthcare practices.

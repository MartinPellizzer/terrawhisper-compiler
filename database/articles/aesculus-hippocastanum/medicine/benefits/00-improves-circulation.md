Horse Chestnut improves circulation thanks to its rich content of aescin, a bioactive compound known for its vasoactive and anti-inflammatory properties. Aescin reduces capillary permeability and fragility, enhancing blood flow and reducing edema. The seeds of Aesculus hippocastanum contain approximately 13-30% aescin, with the highest concentration found in the standardized extract commonly used for medicinal purposes. This potent phytochemical aids in strengthening blood vessel walls, particularly veins, and promotes efficient venous return. Additionally, Horse Chestnut contains flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, which contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, further supporting vascular health.

Examples of health conditions that can benefit from Horse Chestnut's circulation-improving properties include chronic venous insufficiency (CVI), varicose veins, and related symptoms like leg swelling and pain. Studies have shown that standardized Horse Chestnut seed extract significantly alleviates symptoms associated with CVI, enhancing blood circulation and reducing leg edema. The vasoactive effects of aescin make it a valuable natural remedy for individuals experiencing discomfort due to compromised venous function.

To harness the circulation-improving benefits of Horse Chestnut, the seeds are the primary part used. Standardized extracts containing 16-20% aescin are commonly employed in medicinal formulations. These extracts can be found in various forms, including capsules, tinctures, and topical creams. The recommended dosage for oral supplementation is typically 50-150 mg of aescin per day, divided into two or three doses. Topical applications, such as gels or ointments, can be used for localized relief. However, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and form based on individual health needs.

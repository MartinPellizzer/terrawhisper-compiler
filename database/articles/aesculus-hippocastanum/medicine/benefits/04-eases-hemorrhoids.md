Horse Chestnut eases hemorrhoids thanks to its high content of aescin, a compound known for its anti-inflammatory properties. Aescin reduces swelling and inflammation by inhibiting the release of inflammatory mediators and increasing the tone of vein walls. In Horse Chestnut seeds, the aescin concentration ranges from 16% to 20%, with optimal therapeutic effects observed at higher concentrations. A typical dosage for alleviating hemorrhoids is around 100 to 150 mg of aescin per day, showcasing the plant's specific constituents responsible for its efficacy in addressing this health concern.

Numerous health conditions can benefit from Horse Chestnut's ability to ease hemorrhoids, particularly those associated with venous insufficiency. Conditions such as chronic venous insufficiency (CVI), varicose veins, and edema show notable improvement due to the plant's vasoconstrictive and anti-edematous effects. By enhancing blood circulation and reducing fluid retention, Horse Chestnut contributes to the management of these conditions, making it a valuable natural remedy for vascular-related health issues.

To harness the health benefits of Horse Chestnut for hemorrhoids, the seeds are the primary plant part used. Extracts, capsules, and topical formulations are common preparations. A standardized extract containing 16–20% aescin is commonly employed, with a recommended dosage of 300 mg per day. Topical creams with aescin concentrations around 2% have demonstrated effectiveness in reducing hemorrhoidal symptoms. These preparations ensure a targeted and controlled application of the plant's therapeutic constituents for optimal relief.

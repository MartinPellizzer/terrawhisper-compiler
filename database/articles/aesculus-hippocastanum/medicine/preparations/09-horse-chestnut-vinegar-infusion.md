
Horse chestnut vinegar infusion is a concoction made by steeping horse chestnut in vinegar. This infusion is utilized for its believed health benefits and medicinal properties. The horse chestnut, rich in aescin, a compound with purported anti-inflammatory and vasoprotective properties, is infused in vinegar, enhancing its potential healing effects. This infusion is commonly used topically for skin conditions like varicose veins, bruises, and hemorrhoids. Additionally, some believe it aids in improving blood circulation and reducing swelling due to its anti-edematous properties.

To prepare horse chestnut vinegar infusion, start by finely chopping 50 grams of dried horse chestnut seeds. Place these seeds in a glass jar and cover them with 500 milliliters of apple cider vinegar. Seal the jar tightly and store it in a cool, dark place for about 4 weeks. Shake the jar gently every day to ensure proper infusion. After the infusion period, strain the mixture using a fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth to separate the liquid from the solids. Store the resulting infusion in a clean glass container with an airtight lid for future use.

When using horse chestnut vinegar infusion, it's crucial to adhere to appropriate dosages. The recommended dosage is one teaspoon diluted in a glass of water, to be taken up to three times a day. However, excessive intake or improper use may lead to side effects such as nausea, dizziness, or gastrointestinal disturbances. Due to the presence of aescin, those allergic to horse chestnuts should avoid this infusion. Pregnant or breastfeeding individuals should refrain from using it due to potential adverse effects on the fetus or newborn.

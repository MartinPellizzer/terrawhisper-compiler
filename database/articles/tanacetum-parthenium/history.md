The etymology of "Feverfew" originates from its historical use to combat fevers and other ailments caused by infection or high temperature - hence 'fever'. It is known as feverwart in some cultures due the plant being used for medicinal purposes, particularly against malaria which often involved spiking body temperatures. However "Feverfew" became more commonly recognized when it was introduced to English culture through a book called The Herball by John Gerard published around 1597 - giving way not just only the present name but also its increased adoption into medicine as well.

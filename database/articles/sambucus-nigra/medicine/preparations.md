Elder has many medicinal preparations, such as teas, tinctures, poultices, capsules, ointments, syrups, jellies, gargles, lotions, and essential oils that serve various purposes for our health. Each of these preparations is made from different parts of the elderberry plant (Sambucus nigra) to target specific ailments or conditions. Let's delve into each one and see their uses.

 The leaves and flowers are used to make infused teas that provide antioxidants, help with respiratory issues like colds, coughs, asthma, and even aid in boosting immunity. Herbalist believe elder tea also acts as a natural laxative and diuretic.

 This medicinal preparation is made by soaking the elderberries, roots or flowers in alcohol. It can be used to treat flu-like symptoms such as fever, sore throat, and inflammation. Additionally, tinctures are known for their antibacterial properties and have been shown effective against certain strains of bacteria.

 Poultices are made by applying a mixture of elderberry leaves or flowers to the skin, usually on an injured area, like sprained ankles or bruises, due to its anti-inflammatory properties. These help to reduce swelling and discomfort.

 These can be filled with dried elderberries, which are then consumed to relieve gastrointestinal issues, including indigestion, constipation, diarrhea, flatulence, and stomach aches. Some may use capsules as an alternative to tea or tincture for treating colds and flu symptoms.

 Topically, elderberry ointments may be used to treat skin conditions like eczema or burns since they have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. The leaves can also be made into a salve or balm for direct skin contact to alleviate pain and itchiness from bug bites.

 Elderberry syrups contain an alcohol base, making them long lasting and easy to consume. These sweet-tasting preparations are used for cold prevention, sore throats, and coughs. They also act as a soothing relief for irritated stomachs due to their antioxidant content.

 Elderberry jellies are often made with the juice from elderberries, which is rich in vitamins A, B, and C, making it an excellent addition to boost overall health. The jelly can be spread on bread for a tasty snack or enjoyed with yogurt as a healthy dessert option.

 Elderberry gargles work much like the tea, assisting with inflamed throats and soreness. It is made from the juice extracted by boiling elderberries and used to rinse mouth and throat, soothing any discomfort in that region.

 Elderberry lotions are mainly intended for topical use on the skin, providing moisturizing benefits while also offering anti-inflammatory properties. This can be helpful for dry or irritated skin conditions.

 The oil extracted from elderflowers is often used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation and reduce stress levels. This essence offers a natural way to unwind without needing medication.

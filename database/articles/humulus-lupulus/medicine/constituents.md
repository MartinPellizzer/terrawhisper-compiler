Hops has many medicinal constituents, such as flavonoids and essential oils. These components are responsible for the various health benefits associated with the use of hops. The two main categories of active compounds in hops can be broadly categorized as pharmacologically active substances and phytochemicals that provide nutritional value. 

The primary pharmacologically-active substance in hops is known as Humulone, which is a bitter compound with antibacterial properties. This is often used to preserve the stability of beer as well as for its medicinal values. Humulone is also said to help prevent cardiovascular diseases and cancer. The antioxidant property of hops in its natural form comes from flavonoids, which are secondary metabolites that aid in cellular protection against free radicals and other harmful elements.

Essential oils, on the other hand, are phytochemicals obtained by extraction or distillation from various parts of plants such as leaves, flowers, bark, roots, or fruits. The main constituents of hops' essential oils are resins and terpenoids. These components contribute to the characteristic aroma and flavour of hops, which is a significant part of beer production and also have several medicinal properties. For instance, alpha-acids present in the resinous fraction are responsible for providing the bitter taste in beer while the essential oils contain myrcene and farnesene that exhibit antimicrobial properties.

Furthermore, hops' phytochemicals include minerals, vitamins, and other nutrients such as calcium, magnesium, potassium, sodium, B-complex vitamins, and iron. These contribute to the overall health benefits of consuming beer or using hops in various forms as medicine. 

In terms of the medicinal effects of hops, it has been used for centuries to treat insomnia, anxiety, and digestive issues. The essential oils present in hops have sedative properties that help with promoting relaxation and better sleep quality. Its use in treating anxiety is primarily attributed to its anti-stress effects, reducing levels of stress hormones in the body. Hops have also been known to provide relief from gastric problems such as bloating, cramps, or heartburn due to its antispasmodic and carminative properties. 

In conclusion, hops are rich sources of pharmacologically-active substances like Humulone, flavonoids, and essential oils that together offer a diverse array of health benefits ranging from potential cancer prevention and cardiovascular protection to alleviating sleep disorders, anxiety, and digestive problems.

The historical aspects of Humulus Lupulus or Hops are deeply rooted in various cultures and civilizations. This plant has been utilized for a myriad of purposes, ranging from medical uses to divination practices. It is also entwined with legends that have evolved over time.

Firstly, hops played a significant role in traditional medicine systems across different parts of the world. From treating minor health conditions such as wounds and burns to alleviating anxiety and stress, it has been utilized in various herbal remedies for centuries. Hops were used for its sedative properties; ancient texts record instances where it was prescribed for insomnia, restlessness and general restorative purposes.

In the context of cultural history and divination practices, hops have played an equally pivotal role. It is believed that in the Middle Ages, European monasteries began to use hops as a natural means to stabilize and preserve beer during its fermentation process. Aside from this practical application, hops were also considered a potent symbol of fertility and were sometimes incorporated into fertility rites performed by ancient Germanic and Celtic tribes. These tribes used hops in their rituals to promote fertility among their communities.

Furthermore, hops have been associated with various legends and myths across different cultures over the centuries. In Romanian folklore, a story tells of a young girl who falls in love with her shepherd. To help secure this romance, the girl's mother gives her a potion made from hops that will make her irresistible to him. Similarly, in Celtic mythology, it is believed that a magical hop plant was used by the goddess Rhiannon for brewing an enchanted drink that could bring people together and promote harmony among communities.

The fruits of Cinchona officinalis are arranged either singly or in clusters, and they are distributed throughout the plant, mainly attached at the base.

In terms of shape, these fruits exhibit an ovate form, with precise measurements of 2.5 cm in length, 1 cm in width, and 0.5 cm in thickness. Initially green, the fruits gradually turn brown as they mature. Their surface is smooth and hairless, described as glabrous, with no distinct markings.

One notable feature of these fruits is their dehiscence, which means they split open when mature to release their seeds. This allows for the dispersal of seeds, contributing to the plant's reproductive cycle.

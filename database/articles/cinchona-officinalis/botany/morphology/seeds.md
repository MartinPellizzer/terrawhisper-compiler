The seeds of Cinchona officinalis are dehiscent, contained within capsules. They are arranged polyvalently, with multiple seeds found within a single fruit.

The seeds are elliptical in shape, measuring approximately 1.5 centimeters in length, 0.5 centimeters in width, and 0.3 centimeters in thickness. They have a hard texture and a brown seed coat. The seed coat is smooth and glossy, with a thin thickness of about 0.1 millimeters, making it impermeable to water.

A scar, known as the hilum, is present on the seed, indicating the point of attachment to the fruit. The embryo within the seed is straight, and each seed contains two cotyledons.

Notably, the radicle, the embryonic root, is relatively short, measuring around 0.2 centimeters in length. The absence of wings or other appendages distinguishes these seeds.

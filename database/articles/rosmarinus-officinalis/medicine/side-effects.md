Rosemary can have some side effects if used improperly, such as causing allergies and irritating your skin. This herb can also trigger seizures, which are dangerous medical events experienced by a small number of individuals, mostly caused by abnormal electrical activity in the brain. If you're pregnant, Rosemary should be avoided since it may lead to miscarriage or complications during pregnancy. As women go through their menstrual cycle, they might experience physical discomfort and emotional distress known as PMS (Premenstrual Syndrome). Consuming Rosemary can enhance these symptoms, making the situation even more challenging for them. 

 Another negative impact of consuming or using Rosemary is that it can intensify anxiety in individuals who already suffer from this mental health condition. It's important to pay close attention to your body and emotions when using this herb to prevent any unwanted consequences. Additionally, a lack of sleep is a common issue for many people, and Rosemary might contribute to insomnia due to its stimulatory properties. This can have negative effects on overall health and well-being if it persists. 

 High blood pressure, also known as hypertension, is a significant medical concern that can lead to complications such as heart disease or stroke. Excessive use of Rosemary might exacerbate this condition by increasing the risk factors associated with hypertension. Furthermore, consuming large amounts of this herb could potentially cause kidney stones. This occurs when minerals and other substances in the urine crystalize and become stuck within the kidneys or urinary tract. Symptoms of kidney stones can include severe pain, nausea, and vomiting. 

 Lastly, hormonal imbalances can occur due to Rosemary consumption, which could disrupt the body's natural processes related to reproductive health, mood regulation, and energy levels. While most of these effects are rare or only experienced by certain individuals, it is crucial to be aware of the potential risks associated with using this herb. It is essential to consult a healthcare professional if any severe issues arise when using Rosemary.

The fruit type of Atropa belladonna is classified as a berry, a small, fleshy fruit. These berries are solitary, typically found in the leaf axils, and attached to the plant via pedicels, or stalks. 

In terms of physical dimensions, the fruits are approximately 1 centimeter (0.39 inches) in length, width, and thickness, giving them a globose shape. When fully ripe, they take on a shiny black color with a smooth and glossy surface, devoid of distinctive markings.

One notable trait of Atropa belladonna's fruit is that it is indehiscent, meaning it does not split open upon ripening. This characteristic sets it apart from dehiscent fruits, which naturally split open to release their seeds.

The stem type of Atropa belladonna is herbaceous, meaning it lacks woody tissues, making it flexible and non-permanent. Stem arrangement is opposite, indicating that leaves grow in pairs at each node along the stem.

The stem shape is cylindrical, with a uniform, rounded form. In terms of size, the stem can reach a height of 60 to 150 centimeters (23.6 to 59.1 inches) and has a diameter of 1 to 2 centimeters (0.4 to 0.8 inches). This size range provides an idea of the plant's overall stature.

The stem's color is primarily green, typical of most plants. It has a smooth and hairless texture, referred to as glabrous, which is consistent throughout. The stem surface is marked with fine longitudinal lines, described as striated.

Stem nodes are prominent points along the stem where leaves are attached, and the internodes, which are the spaces between nodes, measure approximately 10 to 15 centimeters (3.9 to 5.9 inches).

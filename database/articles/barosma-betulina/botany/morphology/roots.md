Barosma betulina's root system type is fibrous, which means it consists of numerous thin roots extending from the base of the plant. The root structure is primarily taproot, with one dominant primary root that extends deeper into the soil. The branching of the roots is minimal, with few lateral branches.

These roots are relatively shallow, typically extending to a depth of 10-20 cm in the soil. They are relatively thin, with a diameter ranging from 0.2 to 0.5 cm. In terms of length, the roots of Barosma betulina reach a moderate 15-30 cm.

The color of these roots is typically brown to reddish-brown, and their surface texture is smooth, often adorned with fine root hairs measuring 0.1-0.2 mm in length. These fine root hairs play a vital role in nutrient and water absorption.

One distinctive characteristic of Barosma betulina's roots is their high density. On average, there are approximately 5,000 roots per square meter, allowing for efficient resource uptake from the surrounding soil.

Notably, this plant does not form root nodules, which are often associated with nitrogen-fixing plants.

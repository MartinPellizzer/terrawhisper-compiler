Calendula has many medicinal preparations, such as flower extract, salve, oil, tincture, tea, cream, poultice, gel, lotion and wash. Each one has unique uses, so they cater to different needs and situations.

Flower Extract - To prepare this remedy, dried and fresh petals are soaked in alcohol or water. The result is a potent extract that can be used as an ingredient for creating other products like tinctures, creams, and lotions. It may serve as a base for treating skin issues, such as acne, eczema, and psoriasis.

Salve - Calendula salves contain extracted ingredients from the flower, mixed with other nourishing herbs to create a thick, ointment-like substance that is highly suitable for soothing minor cuts, bruises, or scrapes. The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help promote healing and reduce pain.

Oil - Calendula infused oil is made by immersing the dried flowers in a carrier oil like olive, coconut, or sunflower for some time. This oil can be utilized in skin massage blends to ease muscle tension and alleviate pain associated with conditions such as arthritis or gout. It also serves as an ingredient for creating lotions, balms, and creams.

Tincture - A liquid medicine obtained by soaking the dried petals in alcohol or vinegar for some time. Calendula tinctures can be applied directly to skin irritations such as sunburns, insect bites, or rashes, and they may also help with digestive issues like indigestion or stomach cramps when taken internally.

Tea - Steeping Calendula flowers in hot water produces a mild infusion that can be sipped for its soothing properties on the gastrointestinal tract. It is often used to treat upset stomachs, gastric issues, and nausea.

Cream - This product is created by combining oils, waxes, herbs, and other ingredients in a base of water or oil. Calendula cream serves as an excellent moisturizer for dry skin and may provide relief to irritated, chapped, or sunburned skin.

Poultice - A paste made from crushed or powdered plant material is applied directly to the affected area. Calendula poultices are beneficial in treating wounds, bruises, and muscle strains, offering quick pain relief.

Gel - Gel forms when a mixture of water, oil, and other compounds (like glycerin) is infused with calendula flowers. It has a cooling effect on the skin and can be used to soothe itching or irritation.

Lotion - Calendula lotion consists of a blend of carrier oils, herbs, and other ingredients in a water-based form. Ideal for treating sunburns, rashes, or chapped skin, the moisturizing properties help maintain healthy skin.

Wash - A gentle cleanser created with soothing Calendula extracts can be used on delicate facial skin to remove makeup and daily dirt without causing irritation.

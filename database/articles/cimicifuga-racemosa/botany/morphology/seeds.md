The seeds of Cimicifuga racemosa are of the achene type, typically found either as solitary units or in clusters. They have an elliptical or oblong shape, measuring about 5-6 mm in length, 2-3 mm in width, and 1-2 mm in thickness.

These seeds are quite hard, with a dark brown to black seed coat that appears smooth and shiny. The seed coat itself is thin and impermeable, meaning it does not readily allow the passage of water or other substances.

A distinguishing feature of these seeds is the small hilum, a scar on the seed. The embryo within the seed is straight, and each seed contains two cotyledons. Additionally, the radicle, the embryonic root, is relatively short.

These specific morphological traits contribute to the identification and classification of Cimicifuga racemosa seeds. Researchers and botanists use these characteristics to differentiate these seeds from others and study their various properties and potential uses.

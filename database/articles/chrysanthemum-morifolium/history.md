Chrysanthemum morifolium, commonly known as Ju Hua or Chrysanthemum flower, has a rich history of being used for medicinal purposes and divination. As an ancient Chinese herbal medicine, it has been widely employed to treat various health issues such as liver problems, asthma, insomnia, and even cancer prevention. In this case, Ju Hua is believed to strengthen the body's resistance and help in detoxifying, thus contributing to overall well-being.

Moreover, these flowers have been significant in ancient divination practices. They were often used as a tool for fortune-telling and prophecy due to their unique shape resembling various characters. Diviners would place them in certain arrangements or formations that signified specific meanings and messages from the spirits, aiding people in making important decisions and foreseeing future events.

Lastly, many legends surround Ju Hua as well. One of the most famous stories is associated with Zhang Daoling, a Taoist priest who sought an effective remedy to alleviate his suffering. According to this legend, he was miraculously guided by a divine being and instructed to boil the Chrysanthemum flower in water. Drinking this concoction allegedly cured Zhang's illness, leading him to discover the medicinal properties of Ju Hua. This event is said to have inspired further exploration into its healing capabilities and widespread use throughout Chinese history.

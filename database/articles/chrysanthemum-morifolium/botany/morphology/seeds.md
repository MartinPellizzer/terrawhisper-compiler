The seeds of Chrysanthemum morifolium are simple, arranged singly within the flower head. They have an oval shape and lack appendages. 

Each chrysanthemum head typically contains a variable number of seeds, usually ranging from 10 to 15. These seeds are relatively small, measuring about 2.5 to 3 millimeters in length, 1.5 to 2 millimeters in width, and 0.5 to 1 millimeter in thickness. They are hard and exhibit a brown or black seed coat, which is smooth and shiny. The seed coat is quite thin, measuring around 0.1 to 0.2 millimeters in thickness, and is impermeable.

Chrysanthemum morifolium seeds lack a visible hilum, and their embryos are straight. Inside the seed, two fleshy cotyledons are present, along with a small radicle.

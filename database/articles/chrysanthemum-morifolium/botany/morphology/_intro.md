Chrysanthemum morifolium, commonly known as chrysanthemum or mums, features a fibrous root system characterized by a taproot structure. These roots penetrate the soil effectively, reaching a depth of 15-30 cm (6-12 inches) with a diameter ranging from 0.2 to 0.5 cm (0.08-0.2 inches). They are whitish or light brown, smooth, and hairless, lacking nodules but equipped with fine root hairs to increase absorption.

The herbaceous stems of Chrysanthemum morifolium are upright, cylindrical, and range from 30-60 cm (11.8-23.6 inches) in height with a diameter of 0.5-1 cm (0.2-0.4 inches). They are green, smooth, and glabrous, featuring nodes and internodes spaced 5-10 cm (2-4 inches) apart.

Chrysanthemum leaves are simple, variable in shape, and alternate, measuring 5-15 cm (2-6 inches) in length and 2-7 cm (0.8-2.8 inches) in width. They have a smooth, slightly waxy surface, an acute apex, cuneate or rounded base, dentate or serrate margins, and pinnate venation. Leaves are petiolate, with a 2-8 cm (0.8-3.2 inches) long petiole, and stipules are absent.

The inflorescence type is a capitulum, featuring individual, non-fused, disc-shaped flowers with a standardized size of 2.5 cm (0.98 inches). Flowers are smooth, composed of 21 floral parts, lack sepals, and have numerous stamens. Chrysanthemum pistils are single with an inferior ovary, a style, and two stigma structures. Stipules are absent, and the flowers are supported by a 30 cm (11.81 inches) long peduncle with 2 cm (0.79 inches) pedicels at the base.

Chrysanthemum fruits are achenes, clustered, and sessile, with an oblong shape measuring 2.5 cm (0.98 inches) in length and 1 mm (0.04 inches) in width and thickness. They are brown or black when mature, have a smooth and glossy surface, and do not exhibit dehiscence.

The seeds are simple, oval, and arranged singly within the flower head. Each head typically contains 10-15 seeds measuring 2.5-3 mm (0.1-0.12 inches) in length, 1.5-2 mm (0.06-0.08 inches) in width, and 0.5-1 mm (0.02-0.04 inches) in thickness. They have a hard, brown or black seed coat, lack a visible hilum, and feature a straight embryo with two fleshy cotyledons and a tiny radicle.

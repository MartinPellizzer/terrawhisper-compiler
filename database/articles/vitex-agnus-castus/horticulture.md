The growth requirements of Chaste Tree (Vitex agnus-castus), a beautiful ornamental tree with lavender flowers, include well draining soil that is slightly alkaline to neutral in pH ranging from around 6 upwards and should contain proper amount essential nutrients. It prefers full sun exposure but can also grow under partial shade conditions for better survival during summer months when temperatures rise significantly. This tree grows best within USDA hardiness zones of 8 through 10, indicating it thrives in areas with mild winters where the temperature doesn't fall below -5 degrees Celsius (23 Fahrenheit).

Planting Chaste Tree requires a hole dug two times wider and equally deep than its root ball to provide room for soil expansion. Position this tree slightly above ground level so it can easily establish, creating adequate aeration which prevents rotting at the base of trunks or roots while waterlogged conditions are avoided by planting on mounds with raised edges where excess moisture drains away more quickly in wetter areas.

Harvesting Chaste Tree involves collecting its flowers when they are fully bloomed in mid-summer (late June into August). You may want some berries for seed propagation later on, but be careful not to overharvest as this could affect the tree's growth next season since it needs time after fruiting phase ends around September.

 Wormwood has been used since ancient times for its medicinal properties. The herb was commonly used to treat various ailments, including digestive issues, fevers, and even poisoning. In traditional Chinese medicine, wormwood was believed to have cooling and detoxifying effects on the body, making it an important ingredient in many remedies.

 Wormwood also played a significant role in divination practices. The herb's unique shape and appearance were thought to hold mystical properties, and it was often used in rituals and ceremonies as a tool for seeking guidance from the divine realm. In some cultures, wormwood was even believed to possess the power to ward off evil spirits and protect against harm.

 Legends surrounding wormwood are plentiful, with many stories attributing magical or mystical powers to the plant. One such legend tells of how Adam and Eve used wormwood leaves to cover their nakedness after being expelled from the Garden of Eden. Another popular tale speaks of King Solomon's legendary ring, which was said to be made from wormwood wood, giving him immense power and wisdom.

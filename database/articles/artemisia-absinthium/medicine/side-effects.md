
Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) exhibits several potential side effects that warrant caution when used for medicinal purposes. Consumption of wormwood preparations may result in gastrointestinal disturbances such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal discomfort. Additionally, it can provoke allergic reactions, leading to skin rashes, itching, or respiratory issues like asthma, particularly in sensitive individuals. 

High doses of wormwood have been associated with triggering seizures or exacerbating existing seizure disorders due to its thujone content impacting the nervous system. Moreover, the herb might disrupt normal sleep patterns, causing insomnia, and induce sensations of dizziness. Muscle pain and spasms could also manifest in some cases following the ingestion of wormwood in large quantities. Prolonged or excessive use of wormwood could potentially lead to liver or kidney damage, highlighting the importance of moderation and professional guidance. 

Individuals with pre-existing psychiatric conditions may experience exacerbated symptoms upon consuming wormwood, while some may develop increased sensitivity to sunlight, resulting in skin reactions. Furthermore, the herb might interfere with certain medications, affecting their efficacy or causing adverse reactions when taken concurrently. Due to these diverse and potentially severe effects on health, seeking medical advice before using wormwood is crucial to mitigate risks and ensure safe usage tailored to individual health considerations.


Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) belongs to the following traditional taxonomy: Domain - Eukaryota, Kingdom - Plantae, Phylum - Angiosperms, Class - Eudicots, Order - Asterales, Family - Asteraceae, Genus - Artemisia, Species - absinthium.

This plant is commonly known as absinthium, absinthe wormwood, or simply wormwood. Variants of this species include different cultivars with varying leaf shapes and sizes, such as 'Lambrook Silver' known for its silvery foliage, and 'Golden Queen' with yellow-green leaves.

Artemisia absinthium typically presents as a perennial herbaceous plant, characterized by silver-gray leaves that are deeply lobed and covered with fine hairs, emitting a distinctive, strong aroma. Its stems are erect, reaching heights of around 0.6 to 1.5 meters, featuring small, yellow-green flowers clustered in panicles.

Native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and Northern Africa, wormwood thrives in dry, well-drained soils within habitats like roadsides, rocky slopes, and disturbed areas. It tolerates various soil types and is commonly found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, although it can adapt to diverse environments. This hardy plant is known for its resilience in harsh conditions, often flourishing in full sun.

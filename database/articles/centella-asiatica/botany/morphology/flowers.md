The flowers of Centella asiatica are typically small, with a circular shape, measuring between 0.5 to 1 cm (0.2 to 0.4 inches) in length and 1 to 2 cm (0.4 to 0.8 inches) in width. Their colors are predominantly pink or light purple, rendering them visually appealing.

The flowers exhibit actinomorphic symmetry, meaning they are radially symmetrical, and they follow a simple structure, featuring all the essential floral parts. Each flower is solitary, with a solitary attachment to its stem, giving it a sense of individuality.

The floral anatomy of Centella asiatica includes five floral parts, comprising five sepals and five petals. The petals and sepals together form the perianth, with both looking similar. The flowers have two stamens and one pistil, and their ovary is superior, indicating it is attached above the other floral parts.

In terms of texture and surface, the flowers are smooth and glabrous, which means they are hairless. They lack stipules and have inconspicuous bracts. The peduncle that supports the inflorescence is short, as are the pedicels that support individual flowers.

Moreover, the style of these flowers is short, and their stigma is capitate, resembling a rounded knob.

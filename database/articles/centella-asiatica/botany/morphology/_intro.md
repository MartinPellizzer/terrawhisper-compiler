Centella asiatica's root system is fibrous, characterized by numerous fine roots with taproot structure, moderately branching in a dichotomous pattern. Root tips are sharp and tapering, allowing for effective soil penetration. The root density is approximately 2,000 roots per square meter, extending to a depth of 15-20 cm (6-8 inches), with a diameter of 1-2 mm (0.04-0.08 inches). Root color is pale beige-brown, and the surface is smooth with fine lateral lines. This plant lacks root nodules but has sparse, fine, unbranched root hairs.

Stems are herbaceous, opposite in arrangement, with a circular cross-section, reaching a height of 10-30 cm (3.9-11.8 inches) and a diameter of 2-6 mm (0.08-0.24 inches). Stems are green, smooth, and glabrous. Nodes are present, and internodes are short.

Leaves are simple, reniform (kidney-shaped), arranged oppositely, and horizontally oriented. They measure 2-6 cm (0.8-2.4 inches) in length, 1-3 cm (0.4-1.2 inches) in width, and 0.1-0.2 cm (0.04-0.08 inches) in thickness. They are green, smooth, and glabrous, with a blunt apex, cordate base, smooth margins, and palmate venation. The leaf lamina is orbicular, attached with a petiole of 2-10 cm (0.8-4 inches), lacking stipules.

Flowers are umbellate, simple, actinomorphic, and solitary, measuring 0.5-1 cm (0.2-0.4 inches) in length and 1-2 cm (0.4-0.8 inches) in width, typically pink or light purple. They have five sepals and five petals, with two stamens and one pistil, featuring a short style and capitate stigma. Flowers are glabrous, lack stipules, and have inconspicuous bracts, with a short peduncle and pedicels.

Fruits are follicles, arranged solitarily in axillary positions, attached with a pedicel, elliptical in shape (0.4 cm x 0.2 cm x 0.1 cm) and light brown when mature. They have a smooth, shiny surface, and they split open when ripe (dehiscent) to release seeds.

Seeds are dicotyledonous, ovate, solitary, measuring 0.3 cm x 0.2 cm, with a brown to dark brown seed coat that is smooth and somewhat wrinkled, around 0.1 mm thick, semi-permeable. The hilum is small and inconspicuous, and the embryo is straight with two cotyledons and a short radicle.

Codonopsis, scientifically known as Codonopsis pilosula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China. The herbal root has been used for centuries in traditional Chinese medicine due to its medicinal properties and therapeutic potentials. One of the primary uses was to treat coughs and colds while enhancing immune function. Furthermore, Codonopsis pilosula is said to reduce inflammation and provide anti-bacterial benefits; often found useful in cases of diarrhea or fever reduction.

In addition to its role as a traditional medicine, the root was also employed for divination purposes in ancient China. Diviners believed that Codonopsis pilosula held magical powers and could reveal hidden information concerning potential misfortunes or auspicious events. The roots were used to predict future outcomes by interpreting the patterns and shapes they formed when burned over an open flame. This practice was thought to help individuals make better decisions and prepare for any potential adversity, based on their findings from using this plant's unique properties.

Codonopsis pilosula has been part of various Chinese legends that passed down through generations. One notable legend is the story of Zhongli Quan – one of the Eight Immortals in Taoist traditions – who was said to have discovered the herb's medicinal properties by chance when he came across a sick woman who had been using it for healing purposes. The encounter inspired him to explore its potential further, ultimately contributing to its widespread use in traditional Chinese medicine. This connection with an iconic figure from Chinese mythology helped strengthen Codonopsis pilosula's cultural and historical significance.

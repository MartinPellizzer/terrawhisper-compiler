Flaxseed (Linum usitatissimum) belongs to botany under Kingdom - Plantae, Division of vascular plants or Tracheophytae that includes all seed-bearing and flowering terrestrial species with well developed xylem for conducting water & minerals upward; class Magnoliopsida because it is a group containing most angiosperms (flowering plant); order Caryophyllales as one of the diverse groups in this family, which includes over 50 families and about 12k different species. It belongs to Linaceae or flax/Linum Family; with Flux itself comprising other nine subspecies like L. usitatissimum var sativm cultivated for oilseed & fiber production from its stem fibers called lintex, whereas there's a kind native only in the west known as eminently western annual Linus or Western flax(Linum Grandiflorumn) with large flowers and seeds not intended specifically grown unlike normal Flux.

 This herbaceous plant has an extensive system of roots, hair-like fibers on its stem (lintex), which is the source for fiber production; it features long petioled leaves in opposite arrangement that are simple and entire with ovate or lanceolate shape. The flowers appear either axillary as single terminal flower on their topmost part called inflorescences forming racemes of blue, purple-blue to white color depending upon the variety found. Seeds vary from yellowish brown & reddish in different linaceae but are generally smaller with around 2mm diameter and have a protective coat termed testa which can be either smooth or warty shaped containing oil rich endosperm for food value, while its embryo germinating inside the seed will give rise to new plant after growth.

Flaxseed is found worldwide especially in temperate zones ranging from western Usa and Canada down through South America as well Europe & Asia with major regions including India; it prefers a wide range of climates, growing under diverse conditions like arid deserts or humid subtropical areas depending upon the variety. It can be successfully adapted either being planted directly into soils to sprouting young flax seedlings from its plump seeds in summer season for food/ fiber purposes while also grown as a winter annual crop by sowing at an appropriate time and harvesting next year's plant once they mature fully after growing under varying environmental conditions.

The life-cycle of Fluxseed begins with germination of individual seed that gives rise to young plants having slender, stem shaped root systems supporting leaves on its upper part which eventually transform into flowers for pollen & seeds reproduction; these latter will then be dispersed by either natural agents like wind or animals carrying out activities associated food seeking leading onto the continuation cycle.

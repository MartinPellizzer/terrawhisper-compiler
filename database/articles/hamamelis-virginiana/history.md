Witch Hazel is a traditional healing herb used by Native Americans for centuries. Its botanical name Hamamelis virginiana comes from Greek, meaning "plant of Hamamon" and "from Virginia," where it was commonly found growing. This unique shrub with its yellow flowers has been an integral part of medicinal remedies for the various health issues faced by Native Americans before and after European colonization. As part of the natural apothecary, they used the twigs, leaves, roots, and bark to make tonics, salves, teas, and poultices. The astringent qualities in Witch Hazel soothed inflammation, eased itching, and alleviated muscle aches.

In ancient times, apart from medicinal uses, Witch Hazel was employed in rituals for divination. It was believed that the plant possessed spiritual properties and could reveal mystic insights into the future. By placing twigs or leaves in a bowl of water, one could see visions that provided answers to pressing questions about love, health, fortune, or even spiritual enlightenment. This practice was widespread among indigenous communities and continued for centuries as a means to predict upcoming events and seek guidance during challenging times.

Witch Hazel's connection with the supernatural is deeply entrenched in legends that have been passed down through generations. One of these stories revolves around a courageous woman named Wah-kweh-wash-ee, who was a medicine woman among the Iroquois tribe. She possessed remarkable healing powers and used Witch Hazel to cure various ailments. As part of her legacy, she had foreseen that one day, the world would no longer recognize her name but instead refer to the plant as "Witch Hazel." This prophecy has been seen to have come true as people across cultures have adopted and embraced this versatile remedy for its numerous benefits.

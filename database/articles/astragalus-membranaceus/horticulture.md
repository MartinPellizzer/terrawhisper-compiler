To grow Astragalus successfully, you should start with healthy seeds or seedlings. Sow the seeds indoors during early spring, about 1/4 inch deep in a well-draining soil mix. Keep the soil moist but not soggy. When the seedlings are large enough to handle, transplant them outdoors after the last frost, spacing them 18 inches apart. This plant prefers full sun but can tolerate partial shade as well.

The ideal growing conditions for Astragalus include a cool-temperate climate with temperatures ranging between 50°F and 75°F during the daytime and below freezing at night. It grows best in USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8. The soil should be rich in organic matter, slightly acidic (pH range of 6.1-7), well-draining, and not too heavy or clayey. Additionally, it requires regular watering during the growing season but should be allowed to dry out between waterings to prevent root rot.

Maintenance for Astragalus is relatively easy. Prune off any dead or damaged branches in early spring before new growth begins. Fertilize once a month with a balanced liquid fertilizer diluted according to package instructions. Keep an eye out for pests such as aphids and spider mites, treating them immediately if detected. Regularly weed around the plant base to ensure optimal growth conditions. In colder climates, mulch around the plant in late fall to protect the roots from freezing temperatures.


Yarrow might cause drowsiness or dizziness when used improperly. This effect is often attributed to certain constituents present in Yarrow, notably the sesquiterpene lactones and volatile oils like thujone. These compounds, particularly when consumed in excessive amounts or for prolonged periods, can lead to sedative effects, resulting in drowsiness or dizziness. Additionally, Yarrow contains flavonoids such as apigenin and luteolin, which may contribute to its sedative properties.

The side effect of drowsiness or dizziness caused by Yarrow can potentially worsen conditions such as hypotension (low blood pressure), as it may further lower blood pressure levels. Individuals with pre-existing low blood pressure or those taking medications that lower blood pressure should use Yarrow cautiously due to its potential to exacerbate these conditions. Furthermore, individuals who are sensitive to sedative effects or have a history of allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family (such as ragweed, marigolds, or daisies) might be more prone to experiencing drowsiness or dizziness with Yarrow usage.

To prevent the onset of drowsiness or dizziness when using Yarrow, it's essential to follow recommended dosages and preparations. Typical preparations include teas, tinctures, or capsules. A safe dosage for Yarrow tea might be around 1 to 4 grams of dried herb infused in hot water, taken up to three times daily. For tinctures, a common dosage might range from 2 to 4 milliliters, diluted in water, also taken up to three times a day. Adhering to these recommended dosages and consulting with a healthcare professional before use can help mitigate the risk of experiencing drowsiness or dizziness due to Yarrow consumption.

Yarrow Tea is an herbal infusion made from the dried flowers and leaves of the Achillea millefolium plant, renowned for its diverse therapeutic applications. It is prized for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and astringent properties. This tea is commonly used to alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort, ease menstrual cramps, and promote digestion. Its high content of flavonoids and alkaloids contributes to its effectiveness as a mild sedative, aiding in relaxation and reducing anxiety. Additionally, Yarrow Tea is celebrated for its potential to support the immune system, acting as a mild diaphoretic that can induce sweating, thus helping to reduce fever.

To prepare Yarrow Tea, steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried yarrow leaves or flowers in hot water (approximately 250-300 milliliters) for 5-10 minutes. Strain the mixture before consumption to achieve the desired potency. Honey or lemon can be added for taste.

Regarding dosage, a typical recommendation is to drink Yarrow Tea up to 3 times daily, with each serving containing about 1-4 grams of dried herb. However, individual tolerance and specific health conditions may influence the ideal dosage. Excessive intake of Yarrow Tea can lead to adverse effects such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, or increased sensitivity to sunlight due to its photosensitive compounds. Therefore, caution should be exercised, especially for individuals with known allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family. Pregnant or breastfeeding women, as well as those on blood-thinning medications, should avoid Yarrow Tea due to potential complications.

Yarrow offers numerous health benefits and medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory effects, improved digestion, relief from menstrual discomfort, wound healing acceleration, blood pressure reduction, immune system support, anxiety and stress relief, respiratory health improvement, enhanced skin condition, and better circulation.

Key constituents in Yarrow include alkaloids, tannins, flavonoids, sesquiterpene lactones, volatile oils, coumarins, polyacetylenes, glycosides, phytosterols, vitamin C, vitamin K, and potassium.

Yarrow can be prepared in various forms, such as tea, tincture, infused oil, poultice, capsules, salve, compress, essential oil, powder, bath, and inhalation.

When using Yarrow as medicine, take precautions if you have Asteraceae family plant allergies, consult a healthcare professional during pregnancy, breastfeeding, and with children, watch for medication interactions and blood clotting disorders, avoid excessive doses, be mindful of side effects, ensure quality sourcing, and consider underlying medical conditions. DIY preparations require knowledge, and proper storage is important.

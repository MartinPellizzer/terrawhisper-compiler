Yarrow's Volatile Oils are a complex mixture of organic compounds characterized by their ability to vaporize at relatively low temperatures. Constituting approximately 0.3% to 1% of the plant's total composition, these oils encompass various components such as azulene, camphor, cineole, and sabinene. Azulene, present in Yarrow's Volatile Oils, typically accounts for about 5% to 7% of the oil content. Camphor, another significant constituent, ranges from 10% to 15%, while cineole and sabinene generally make up around 5% and 7% respectively. These concentrations may vary based on factors like geographic location, climate, and cultivation methods.

The Volatile Oils found in Yarrow possess diverse health benefits and properties. Azulene, renowned for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, contributes to Yarrow's effectiveness in reducing inflammation and soothing skin conditions like eczema and dermatitis. Additionally, camphor, a prevalent component, exhibits analgesic and antimicrobial properties, which can aid in relieving pain and fighting bacterial infections. The combination of these volatile oils within Yarrow makes it beneficial for treating digestive issues such as indigestion, bloating, and stimulating appetite.

When misused or consumed in excessive quantities, Yarrow's Volatile Oils can lead to adverse effects. Overconsumption of camphor-rich oils may result in symptoms like nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and in severe cases, convulsions or seizures. Excessive use of Yarrow, particularly its essential oils high in camphor, can also cause skin irritation or allergic reactions, manifesting as redness, itching, or rashes upon contact. It's important to exercise caution and adhere to recommended dosages to prevent potential adverse reactions.

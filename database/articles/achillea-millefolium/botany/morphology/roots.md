Achillea millefolium has a taproot structure with lateral roots branching out in a dichotomous pattern. These roots have acute tips, giving them a pointed appearance.

The root density of Achillea millefolium is approximately 280 roots per square centimeter, or roughly 1800 roots per square inch. This high density allows the plant to efficiently absorb nutrients and water from the soil.

The roots of Achillea millefolium typically reach a depth of 20 centimeters (about 7.87 inches). They have a slender diameter, measuring around 1 millimeter (0.04 inches) in width. Despite their modest thickness, these roots can grow to a considerable length of up to 30 centimeters (approximately 11.81 inches).

The color of the roots is predominantly whitish to light brown, providing a visual contrast to the surrounding soil. Their surface texture is smooth, aiding in the efficient penetration of the soil.

Achillea millefolium does not form root nodules, which are commonly associated with nitrogen-fixing plants. However, it does possess fine and dense root hairs. These root hairs increase the root's surface area, facilitating the absorption of water and nutrients from the soil.

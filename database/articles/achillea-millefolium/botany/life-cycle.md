Yarrow follows a distinctive life cycle. It germinates typically in 14-21 days, thriving within a temperature range of 18-24°C. The seedling stage, marked by cotyledon and true leaf emergence, leads to the establishment of a robust root system.

During its vegetative growth phase, yarrow exhibits vigorous leaf production, benefiting from optimal light conditions. In the second year, it enters the reproductive phase, flowering with multiple umbel-shaped clusters, attracting various pollinators. Cross-pollination fosters genetic diversity.

Following successful pollination, fertilized flowers give rise to thousands of small, flat seeds. These seeds are dispersed by wind and gravity, often occurring in late summer and early fall. Subsequently, mature plants undergo senescence and enter a period of winter dormancy, safeguarded as perennials beneath the soil.

In spring, yarrow re-emerges from its dormant crowns, renewing the vegetative growth phase and repeating its adaptable life cycle. This adaptability contributes to the widespread distribution and ecological success of Achillea millefolium.

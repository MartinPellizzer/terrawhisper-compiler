Yarrow (Achillea millefolium) is a perennial plant, not an annual one. Perennial plants are those that live for more than two years, and yarrow fits this description because it can survive and continue to grow for several years without needing to be replanted.

The following list gives some details about why yarrow is a perennial plant.

- **Root System:** Yarrow develops a strong and extensive root system that helps it survive for multiple years. These roots can delve deep into the soil, allowing the plant to access water and nutrients even during dry periods.
- **Crown:** Yarrow has a woody crown at its base, which also contributes to its perennial nature. This crown survives through the winter and sends up new growth in the spring.
- **Rhizomes:** Yarrow can spread through rhizomes, which are underground stems that give rise to new plants. This allows yarrow to establish colonies of interconnected plants, all originating from a single parent plant.
- **Self-seeding:** In addition to its rhizomatous growth, yarrow can also self-seed. This means that it produces seeds that can fall to the ground and germinate in favorable conditions, giving rise to new yarrow plants without the need for human intervention.
- **Tolerance to Different Climates:** Yarrow is known for its adaptability and can grow in a wide range of climates and soil conditions. This adaptability contributes to its longevity as a perennial plant.
- **Drought Tolerance:** Yarrow is drought-tolerant, thanks in part to its deep root system. This ability to withstand dry periods also helps it survive for several years.
- **Resilience:** Yarrow is relatively resistant to pests and diseases, further contributing to its long lifespan.

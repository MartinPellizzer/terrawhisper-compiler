Kava has many medicinal preparations, such as tea, tinctures, capsules, extracts, powder, root, drink, poultice, bath and oil. These various forms serve different purposes, making it a versatile remedy for several ailments. Tea is the most conventional means of consuming Kava, which involves brewing the plant's roots in hot water to make a fragrant beverage that is said to promote relaxation and wellbeing. 

 Tinctures are another popular form of Kava consumption, where the root is soaked in alcohol for several days or weeks to extract its active compounds. The resultant liquid can then be taken as drops under the tongue, or diluted into a drink. These tinctures offer a more potent dose of Kava's beneficial properties and are often more practical than tea, which may require multiple infusions to achieve the desired effects. 

 Capsules can include Kava in its various forms, whether that's powdered root or ground dried leaves. The capsule offers convenience, especially for those who do not enjoy the taste of Kava or prefer a more discreet method of consuming it. They allow dosage control and may provide a more predictable effect compared to drinking tea or using tinctures where some variability can occur in the amount of plant matter used. 

 Extracts are a highly concentrated form of Kava, obtained by using solvents like water or alcohol to remove its active components. They come as liquid drops or tablets and often pack more punch than other preparations due to their potency, allowing for faster absorption into the bloodstream. This can be beneficial for those seeking quick relief from anxiety or stress. 

 Powdered Kava can be consumed on its own or added to various recipes, such as smoothies, teas or energy balls. It offers a more flexible way of incorporating Kava into one's diet while still providing the same potential benefits as other preparations. The root itself, typically found in dried and ground form, may be used for culinary purposes, adding flavor and nutrition to dishes. 

 Lastly, Kava can be used externally through poultices or baths. Poultices are applications of crushed plant material that are wrapped and placed directly onto the skin, providing localized relief from pain or inflammation. Meanwhile, soaking in a warm Kava bath may offer overall relaxation benefits by easing tension throughout the body. Lastly, Kava oil can be massaged into the skin for its nourishing properties.

Nutmeg & Mace can have some side effects if used improperly, such as excessive amounts. These spices mainly hail from a fruit known as the seed-apple. Their taste varies according to their usage and can be sweet or bitter. 

One of the negative effects of nutmeg is its potent hallucinogenic nature when taken in large quantities, and it's used for such a purpose often referred to as "nutmeg high". Although this effect is not well documented nor researched due to limited studies, it has been reported that consuming more than three whole nutmegs can lead to altered states of consciousness. This is because the active compound in nutmeg known as myristicin works similarly to some psychoactive substances such as LSD and marijuana. The results vary widely from person to person; however, effects are likely to be unpleasant, unpredictable, and highly intoxicating.

Another potential downside of excessive use of nutmeg is its impact on the liver. Myristicin has been shown to have hepatotoxic properties, which can lead to liver damage or even failure in extreme cases when ingested in higher than normal quantities. Moreover, it's crucial to remember that nutmeg contains many compounds, and while myristicin may be responsible for its psychoactive effects, other substances might also have detrimental impacts on the body. 

Mace, on the other hand, is safer and does not contain significant amounts of myristicin. However, it's essential to keep in mind that allergic reactions are still possible if one consumes too much of any spice. Some people might be sensitive or allergic to nutmeg and mace, which can lead to unwanted symptoms like a rash or discomfort in the mouth and throat.

Overconsumption of both spices could also result in digestive issues such as diarrhea or vomiting due to their high fat content. Furthermore, excessive intake may cause gastrointestinal upset because nutmeg's oil can irritate the stomach lining. 

As important aspects in a wide range of culinary dishes, nutmeg and mace contribute flavor to savory and sweet dishes alike. They can add depth and complexity to various recipes, from soups and stews to baked goods such as cookies or pies. When used responsibly and in moderation, these spices can enhance the taste of food without causing any harm. It is essential to know about these potential hazards, especially when using them in large quantities or experimenting with their psychoactive properties in informal settings.

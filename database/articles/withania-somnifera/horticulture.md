For a fruitful growth of Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera), it requires well-drained soil rich with good organic matter, proper sunlight exposure around eight to twelve hours per day and regular watering that does not cause the roots to become saturated or soggy. Temperature should be above 20°C in both summertime (15 -38 °) while requiring warmth through all seasons but preferably over its dormant period from winter into early spring as well which would then necessitate a slightly lower temperature between the range of approximately five to ten degrees Celsius.

When planting Ashwagandha, dig holes twice as big as their root balls and make sure they are deep enough that soil can cover all roots completely while also providing space for future growth without being too crowded or stressed by lack thereof at surface level-allow spacing between individual plants approximately three feet apart.

Providing the right care starts from applying fertilizers every four to six weeks, particularly during growing seasons using low concentrations as not do disturb your plant's sensitive roots - always water only when soil is dry enough and prune dead or damaged branches promptly without overdoing it because too much removal may cause unnecessary stress.

Harvest time usually comes after a few years of growth, depending on the health status determined by observable changes such as reduced flower production indicating maturity - cut whole plants from ground level using sharp tools making sure they're free from dirt while retaining healthy root systems intact for replanting if desired.

Pelargonium has many medicinal constituents, such as Flavones, Flavonoids, Quercetin derivatives, Phenolic acids, Lignans, Tannins, Sesquiterpenes, Diterpenes, Monoterpenes and Sterols. These compounds are all found within various types of Pelargonium plants which make up a family known as Geraniaceae. This diverse group includes over 280 different species, most notably including geraniums. Each constituent serves unique roles in the plant's biology and often conferring distinct therapeutic benefits when used medicinally.

Flavones and Flavonoids are a type of phytochemical that act as antioxidants. They help protect plants against free radicals and diseases, while also playing roles in plant pigmentation. In humans, they may contribute to maintaining good health by preventing cellular damage due to oxidative stress.

Quercetin derivatives are a subgroup of flavonoids that exhibit antioxidant properties as well. These compounds are found in various parts of the Pelargonium plant and have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory, anticancer, cardiovascular protective, antidiabetic, and neuroprotective effects.

Phenolic acids serve a role in protecting plants against fungal infection and ultraviolet radiation. In humans, they possess anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, which may be helpful in treating various conditions. They can also support cellular health by preventing oxidative damage, such as that caused by free radicals.

Lignans are found mainly within the woody stems, or branches of Pelargonium plants. They have been studied for their potential anticancer and anti-inflammatory properties. Lignans may also exhibit antimicrobial effects against certain bacteria.

Tannins can be found in various plant structures, including leaves, roots, and flowers. Often with astringent qualities, they act as an important part of the plants' defense mechanism against herbivores. In humans, tannins may play roles in improving dental health by reducing plaque formation or exhibiting antibacterial actions in the mouth.

Sesquiterpenes, Diterpenes, and Monoterpenes are terpene-based compounds found within Pelargonium plants that contribute to their distinctive aromas. They may also exhibit medicinal properties such as antimicrobial activity. Additionally, Sterols are another type of compound present in Pelargonium plants, which play a role in the regulation and balance of cholesterol levels in humans.

In summary, Pelargonium encompasses a wide range of beneficial constituents that together support diverse therapeutic applications.

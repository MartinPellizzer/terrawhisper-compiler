Visnaga (Ammi visnaga), a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, holds significance in traditional folk medicine across diverse cultures. Known for its therapeutic properties, its seeds were used in Egyptian, Greek, and Arabic traditional medicine to alleviate conditions like asthma, urinary tract issues, and as a diuretic. Ancient Egyptians utilized visnaga seeds to treat kidney stones, while Greeks used them to ease bronchial and respiratory ailments. In Arabic medicine, it was employed for its diuretic properties to assist in urinary tract concerns. This plant's historical medicinal applications showcase its widespread use in varied traditional healing practices.

In ancient times, visnaga had intriguing applications in divination practices. Some cultures believed that the arrangement and patterns formed by the seeds had mystical properties and could reveal insights into the future. The seeds were cast or arranged in specific ways, and interpretations were drawn based on their placement. This divinatory usage of visnaga seeds reflects the plant's cultural significance beyond its medicinal attributes, portraying its mystical associations in ancient rituals and practices.

Legends and myths surrounding visnaga are woven into cultural folklore. Some tales attribute magical properties to the plant, depicting it as a symbol of protection or good fortune. In certain traditions, it was believed that carrying visnaga seeds could ward off evil spirits or bring luck to travelers. These myths and legends contribute to the plant's enduring symbolism in various cultures, adding an aura of mystique and reverence to its identity.

Visnaga holds significant modern medicinal value, particularly in the treatment of conditions like asthma. Its extracts contain active compounds such as khellin and visnagin, which act as bronchodilators, helping to widen the airways in the lungs. Studies have shown its efficacy in easing asthmatic symptoms. Additionally, Visnaga demonstrates antispasmodic properties, aiding in muscle relaxation and urinary tract health.

The active compounds khellin and visnagin found in Visnaga are crucial for its medicinal properties. Khellin has been utilized as a smooth muscle relaxant, while visnagin exhibits vasodilatory effects. These compounds contribute to the plant's ability to alleviate bronchial constriction and relax smooth muscles in the body, making it a promising natural remedy for respiratory and urinary tract conditions.

Visnaga can be prepared in various forms for medicinal use. Common preparations include teas, tinctures, and extracts. Teas made from Visnaga seeds are brewed to harness its therapeutic benefits. Tinctures, created by steeping the plant in alcohol, and concentrated extracts are also popular methods to derive its medicinal properties. These preparations offer diverse options for administration based on individual needs and preferences.

When considering Visnaga for medicinal purposes, caution is necessary. It's important to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for individuals with existing medical conditions or those taking medications. Excessive consumption of Visnaga may lead to side effects such as nausea, headaches, and allergic reactions. Furthermore, pregnant and breastfeeding individuals should avoid its use due to the limited safety data available. Proper dosage and supervision are crucial to mitigate potential risks associated with Visnaga's medicinal use.

Visnaga (Ammi visnaga) presents a diverse array of key preparations aimed at harnessing its medicinal properties for various health purposes. Infusions, a widely practiced method, involve steeping dried Visnaga flowers or seeds in hot water, allowing extraction of its beneficial compounds. Tinctures, popularly used for their concentrated form, involve macerating Visnaga in alcohol to obtain potent extracts. Capsules, containing powdered Visnaga, offer controlled dosage intake, commonly found in strengths ranging from 100 to 500 milligrams. Decoctions, made by boiling Visnaga roots or seeds, yield liquid formulations for internal use against diverse ailments. 

Additionally, topical ointments, incorporating Visnaga extracts, serve as remedies for skin conditions or muscle pain relief, available in concentrations like 5% or 10% extract. Poultices, made by applying crushed or ground Visnaga parts directly onto the skin, provide localized relief. Oil infusions, by soaking Visnaga in carrier oils, facilitate its use in massages or skin treatments. Inhalations, utilizing vapors from Visnaga-infused hot water, offer respiratory support, especially in addressing asthma symptoms. Syrups, sweetened Visnaga solutions, are administered for cough relief or respiratory health, available in volumes such as 100 milliliters or 200 milliliters. 

Furthermore, powdered forms of Visnaga, used in food or beverages, provide an alternative consumption method for its health benefits, with suggested doses typically ranging from 1 to 3 grams. These preparations offer versatile means to utilize Visnaga's medicinal potential, allowing for tailored approaches to various health concerns and preferences.

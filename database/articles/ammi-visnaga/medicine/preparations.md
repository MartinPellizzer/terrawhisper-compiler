Decoction is made by boiling the plant parts in water and simmering them for a while. This process extracts all the beneficial compounds from the plants that are used to treat various health issues.

Infusion is similar to decoction but with slightly different steps. Instead of boiling, you steep or soak the plant parts in hot water, allowing the active ingredients to dissolve and blend into the liquid. This creates a milder tea-like drink that can be consumed for healing purposes.

Powder formulation occurs by drying out visnaga plant parts and grinding them into a fine powder. This versatile preparation can be used as an ingredient in other medicinal products, added to drinks or taken on its own when swallowed with water.

Tincture is another way to make medicine from visnaga. It involves soaking the plant parts in alcohol for a few weeks, allowing the alcohol to extract and preserve the active components. This strong solution can be diluted and used as medicine later.

Juice extraction simply means squeezing the juices out of visnaga fruits or leaves to obtain their nutrients and healing properties. The fresh juice might provide an alternative way to consume visnaga's health benefits.

Tea, although already mentioned as an infusion, is worth mentioning again because it is a popular and easily accessible form of visnaga consumption. Steeping the plant parts in hot water produces a tasty beverage that can aid in various health concerns.

Essential oil is extracted from visnaga's flowers or other aromatic parts using distillation methods such as steam or hydro-distillation. These highly concentrated oils contain potent healing properties and can be added to topical applications, diffused into the air, or even ingested in small quantities after dilution with a carrier oil.

Capsules and tablets are commonly used forms of visnaga medication that combine powdered plant materials, essential oils, and other ingredients into easy-to-swallow units, making them ideal for consistent daily dosing.

Syrup is another way to create a palatable and effective medicinal form of visnaga by incorporating the plant's active compounds in a sweetened liquid base that can be sipped easily or used as an ingredient in other preparations.

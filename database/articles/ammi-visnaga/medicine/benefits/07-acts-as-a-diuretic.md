Visnaga's ability to act as a diuretic refers to its capacity to increase urine production and promote the expulsion of excess water and salts from the body. This effect aids in reducing fluid retention and bloating. The primary constituents in Visnaga responsible for its diuretic properties are khellin and visnagin. Khellin, present in significant quantities within the plant, contributes to its smooth muscle-relaxant and vasodilatory effects. Visnagin, another key compound, further enhances these effects, stimulating the kidneys to increase urine output. Studies indicate that khellin comprises approximately 1.5% to 3% of the dried plant material, while visnagin content ranges from 0.5% to 1.5%.

The diuretic properties of Visnaga benefit individuals experiencing edema, a condition characterized by the accumulation of excess fluid in tissues, often occurring in conditions such as congestive heart failure, kidney disorders, or certain liver conditions. By promoting increased urine production and reducing fluid retention, Visnaga can alleviate symptoms associated with these health issues, including swelling, bloating, and discomfort. Additionally, its diuretic effect may assist in managing high blood pressure by reducing the volume of fluid in the bloodstream, thereby potentially lowering blood pressure levels.

In traditional medicine, various parts of the Visnaga plant are utilized to harness its diuretic properties. The seeds and fruits, containing the highest concentrations of khellin and visnagin, are commonly used in herbal preparations and extracts. These parts are dried and processed to create teas, tinctures, or supplements aimed at leveraging Visnaga's diuretic effects. Moreover, the essential oil extracted from Visnaga seeds is also employed for its diuretic properties, incorporated into formulations for topical application or aromatherapy targeting fluid retention issues.

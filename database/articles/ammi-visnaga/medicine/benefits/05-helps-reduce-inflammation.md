Visnaga's ability to help reduce inflammation refers to its capacity to alleviate the body's inflammatory response, a crucial function in mitigating various health conditions. This plant contains bioactive compounds such as khellin, visnagin, and flavonoids like quercetin and kaempferol. Khellin, the primary constituent responsible for its anti-inflammatory properties, constitutes approximately 0.6% to 1.5% of the dry weight of the Visnaga plant. Visnagin, another significant component, exists in quantities of around 1.2% to 3.5%. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory effects by inhibiting inflammatory mediators, including cytokines and prostaglandins, thereby reducing the body's inflammatory response.

The anti-inflammatory benefits of Visnaga offer relief and support for various health conditions, particularly those linked to inflammation. Conditions such as asthma, bronchitis, and respiratory disorders often benefit from Visnaga's anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, its use extends to addressing conditions like arthritis, where inflammation in the joints causes discomfort and reduced mobility. The ability of Visnaga to help reduce inflammation serves as a complementary approach in managing these health issues by targeting the underlying inflammatory processes.

In traditional medicine and herbal remedies, different parts of the Visnaga plant are utilized to harness its anti-inflammatory properties. The seeds of Visnaga, containing khellin and visnagin, are predominantly used for medicinal purposes. Extracts derived from the seeds are prepared in various forms such as tinctures, teas, or capsules, allowing for easy consumption and absorption of the bioactive compounds. The seeds are often ground and processed to create standardized extracts, ensuring consistent levels of khellin and visnagin for therapeutic use in reducing inflammation.

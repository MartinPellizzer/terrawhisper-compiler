
Visnaga's ability to support heart health refers to its potential in promoting cardiovascular well-being by aiding various aspects of heart function. This plant contains active compounds, notably khellin and visnagin, which contribute significantly to its medicinal properties. Khellin, present in substantial quantities, acts as a calcium channel blocker, dilating blood vessels and potentially reducing blood pressure by allowing blood to flow more easily. It assists in relaxing the smooth muscles of the arteries, potentially supporting better circulation. Visnagin, another key constituent, exhibits similar properties and aids in cardiovascular health by potentially reducing the workload on the heart and supporting overall heart function. Studies indicate that these compounds may also help in reducing cholesterol levels, further benefiting heart health.

The support offered by Visnaga to heart health can be beneficial in conditions related to cardiovascular health. It may aid in managing hypertension (high blood pressure) by relaxing blood vessels, potentially assisting in lowering blood pressure levels. Additionally, its potential to dilate coronary arteries might benefit individuals with angina pectoris, a condition characterized by chest pain due to reduced blood flow to the heart muscle. Furthermore, the vasodilatory effects of Visnaga may support those with certain heart rhythm disorders or other conditions related to compromised blood flow to the heart.

Different parts of the Visnaga plant are utilized for their potential heart-health-promoting properties. Traditionally, the fruits and seeds of Visnaga are predominantly used for their medicinal benefits. The seeds contain higher concentrations of khellin and visnagin, making them the primary source of these active compounds. Preparations made from the seeds or fruits, often in the form of extracts or teas, are used to harness the plant's cardiovascular benefits due to their rich content of the beneficial constituents. These parts are processed to derive compounds that contribute to heart health, making Visnaga a potential natural remedy for supporting cardiovascular function.

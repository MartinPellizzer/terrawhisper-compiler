
Visnaga's ability to alleviate urinary tract issues refers to its capacity to address various conditions affecting the urinary system, such as urinary tract infections (UTIs), kidney stones, and bladder spasms. This benefit primarily stems from the plant's high concentration of active compounds, notably khellin and visnagin. Khellin, found in the fruits of Visnaga, constitutes approximately 2-4% of the plant material, while visnagin, present in the seeds, accounts for around 5-8%. These compounds exhibit antispasmodic properties, helping to relax smooth muscles in the urinary tract. Specifically, khellin has been shown to dilate blood vessels, aiding in the passage of kidney stones and reducing spasms, while visnagin contributes to the overall soothing effect on the urinary system by mitigating inflammation and discomfort.

This health benefit aids individuals suffering from various urinary tract issues, including ureteral stones, urinary spasms, and conditions characterized by urinary tract inflammation. Khellin's vasodilatory effect assists in widening the ureters, facilitating the passage of kidney stones, thereby reducing pain and discomfort associated with their movement. Additionally, the antispasmodic properties of visnagin alleviate bladder spasms and may help in easing the symptoms of urinary tract infections by reducing inflammation and discomfort during urination.

For the alleviation of urinary tract issues, Visnaga primarily utilizes its fruits and seeds. The fruits contain khellin, which is extracted for its vasodilatory effects, while the seeds contain visnagin, aiding in reducing inflammation and spasms in the urinary system. Extracts derived from both these parts of the Visnaga plant are often used in herbal remedies and supplements designed to support urinary tract health and alleviate associated discomforts.

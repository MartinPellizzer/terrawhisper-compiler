Cinnamomum verum, commonly known as true cinnamon, has a taproot system with fibrous structures, moderately branched, pointed tips, and notable root density of around 400 roots per cubic centimeter (or 2,548 roots per cubic inch). Root depth ranges from 30 to 60 centimeters (11.8 to 23.6 inches), with a diameter of 0.2 to 0.5 centimeters (0.08 to 0.2 inches) and a length of 20 to 30 centimeters (7.9 to 11.8 inches). The roots are creamy white, smooth, and lack nodules but have fine and numerous root hairs.

The stems of Cinnamomum verum start as herbaceous and transition to a woody structure. They are typically opposite but occasionally appear in an alternate, decussate pattern. The stems are cylindrical, about 2.5 cm (1 inch) in diameter, with a height of up to 10 meters (32.8 feet). They are brown to reddish-brown, smooth with fine pubescence, and feature clearly defined nodes spaced about 10 to 15 cm (4-6 inches) apart.

Cinnamomum verum leaves are simple, elliptical, alternate, and vertical. They are approximately 7.5 cm (3 inches) in length, 2.5 cm (1 inch) in width, and 0.2 cm (0.08 inches) thick, with a smooth and shiny green surface. The leaf apex is acute, the base is cuneate, and the margin is entire. Leaves have pinnate venation and are petiolate with a length of approximately 2.0 cm (0.8 inches), lacking stipules.

The flowers of Cinnamomum verum are found in panicles, actinomorphic, and composed of six floral parts: three sepals and three petals, forming a perianth. There are nine stamens and a single, short style with a capitate stigma. The flowers are about 1 cm (0.39 inches) in length, 0.5 cm (0.20 inches) in width, cream to pale yellow, smooth, and glabrous. They are attached in leaf axils and often accompanied by small bracts, with a short peduncle and pedicel.

Cinnamomum verum fruits are drupes, typically clustered throughout the tree canopy, with ellipsoid shape and dimensions of about 1.5 cm (0.59 inches) in length, 0.7 cm (0.28 inches) in width, and 0.5 cm (0.20 inches) in thickness. When ripe, they are dark purple, smooth, and indehiscent, lacking distinct markings.

The seeds are simple, often solitary or in clusters, with elliptical shape, measuring about 10-12 mm (0.39-0.47 inches) in length, 5-6 mm (0.20-0.24 inches) in width, and 2-3 mm (0.08-0.12 inches) in thickness. They have a brown, smooth, and shiny seed coat, which is impermeable. The hilum is located at the base of the seed, and inside, there is a straight embryo with two cotyledons and a radicle.

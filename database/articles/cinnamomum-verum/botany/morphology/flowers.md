The flowers of Cinnamomum verum have a simple and complete structure, typically found in the form of panicles. Their symmetry is actinomorphic, meaning they are radially symmetric.

Each Cinnamomum verum flower comprises six floral parts. The flower possesses three sepals and three petals, collectively forming a perianth with indistinguishable sepals and petals. Additionally, there are nine stamens and a single, short style in each flower, which features a small, capitate stigma.

The flowers of Cinnamomum verum are relatively petite, with dimensions of approximately 1 centimeter (0.39 inches) in length and 0.5 centimeters (0.20 inches) in width. They exhibit a cream to pale yellow coloration and have a smooth, glabrous texture.

These flowers are attached in the leaf axils, making their inflorescence type axillary. They lack stipules and are often accompanied by small bracts. The peduncle, which is the flower stalk, is short, measuring less than 1 centimeter (0.39 inches) in length, and the pedicel, the stalk that attaches the individual flower to the peduncle, is even shorter, typically less than 0.5 centimeters (0.20 inches).

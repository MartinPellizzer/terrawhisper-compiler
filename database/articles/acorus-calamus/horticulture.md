
Sweet flag is a perennial herb that can be cultivated in gardens or landscapes with relative ease. It thrives in moist, marshy soils and can be propagated through rhizome division. Plant the rhizomes horizontally at a depth of around 5-10 centimeters in soil that retains moisture well. Ensure adequate spacing of approximately 30-45 centimeters between plants to allow for their spread. Regular watering is essential, especially during dry periods, to maintain soil moisture and support healthy growth.

This herb flourishes in damp, sunny to partially shaded areas, preferring a temperate climate for optimal growth. Acorus calamus prefers fertile, loamy soil with a pH ranging from 6.0 to 7.5. It thrives in areas with high humidity and requires consistent moisture levels in the soil. Incorporating organic matter into the soil can enhance its fertility and water retention capabilities, aiding in the plant's development. Adequate sunlight, approximately 6-8 hours per day, fosters vigorous growth, although partial shade can also be suitable.

Pruning and maintenance of Acorus calamus involve minimal effort. Regularly remove dead or damaged leaves to promote airflow and prevent disease. Divide and replant overcrowded clumps every 3-4 years to maintain plant health and vigor. Mulching around the base of the plant helps retain moisture and suppresses weed growth. Avoid over-fertilization, as it may lead to excessive foliage growth with reduced essential oil content. Maintaining consistent soil moisture and occasionally thinning out dense growth ensures a healthy and thriving Sweet flag plant.

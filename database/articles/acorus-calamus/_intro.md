Sweet flag, scientifically known as Acorus calamus, is a herbaceous plant with a taxonomy in the Plantae kingdom, class Monocots, and family Acoraceae. It has various regional names such as calamus root, flagroot, myrtle flag, and sweet sedge. 

This plant features cylindrical stems, linear and lanceolate leaves, and thrives in wetlands and slow-moving water bodies in North America, Europe, and Asia. It's an evergreen plant with no petioles or stipules and prefers consistent moisture and partial shade.

Medicinally, sweet flag is used to treat gastrointestinal disorders like indigestion, with β-asarone and essential oils as its active compounds. Caution is required to avoid toxic effects, especially from prolonged or high-dose use, as it contains β-asarone. 

Culinary uses of sweet flag include its rhizomes in soups, stews, and alcoholic beverages for their sweet, spicy, and earthy flavors. It should be used sparingly due to its intense taste. 

Cultivating sweet flag in your garden involves well-drained soil, rhizome division, and regular moisture, with optimal conditions including a pH of 6.0 to 7.5.

Sweet flag has a rich history in traditional medicine, including use in Ayurvedic medicine, Native American, and European herbal traditions. It was also linked to divination in ancient China and associated with various legends and myths in Indian and European folklore, demonstrating its multifaceted importance throughout history.

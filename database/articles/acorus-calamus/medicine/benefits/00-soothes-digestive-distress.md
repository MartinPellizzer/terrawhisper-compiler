Sweet flag (Acorus calamus) soothes digestive distress thanks to its rich content of bioactive compounds, notably β-asarone, α-asarone, and essential oils. Studies have identified that the rhizomes of Sweet flag contain approximately 1-2% volatile oil, with β-asarone constituting around 50-90% of the oil composition. These constituents exhibit anti-inflammatory and spasmolytic properties, contributing to the plant's efficacy in alleviating digestive discomfort. Additionally, the essential oils in Sweet flag possess carminative attributes, aiding in the expulsion of gas from the digestive system, further promoting gastrointestinal well-being.

Sweet flag's capacity to soothe digestive distress is particularly beneficial for individuals experiencing conditions such as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), indigestion, and dyspepsia. The plant's anti-inflammatory and spasmolytic actions play a crucial role in calming irritated digestive tissues, reducing bloating, and easing abdominal discomfort. Research indicates that incorporating Sweet flag into a holistic approach to digestive health may help mitigate the symptoms associated with these conditions, providing a natural and effective alternative or complementary remedy.

To harness the digestive benefits of Sweet flag, one can utilize the rhizomes of the plant. The recommended dosage for therapeutic use is typically around 1 to 4 grams of dried rhizome per day. Various preparations, including infusions, tinctures, or powders, can be employed to integrate Sweet flag into one's health regimen. However, it is essential to exercise caution, as excessive consumption of β-asarone may have adverse effects. Consulting with a healthcare professional is advisable to determine the appropriate dosage and form of administration based on individual health needs.

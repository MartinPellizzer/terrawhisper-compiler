Sweet flag (Acorus calamus) improves oral health thanks to its rich composition of bioactive compounds, notably including eugenol, cineole, and camphor. Studies have revealed that Acorus calamus contains approximately 2.5-4.5% essential oil, with eugenol being a major component constituting up to 75% of the oil. Eugenol, recognized for its antimicrobial properties, acts as a potent antibacterial agent against oral pathogens, contributing to the plant's effectiveness in enhancing oral health. Additionally, the presence of cineole and camphor further supports Sweet flag's role in combatting oral infections and promoting overall oral hygiene.

Numerous health conditions related to oral health can benefit from Sweet flag's properties. Conditions such as gingivitis, periodontitis, and halitosis, which are often associated with bacterial overgrowth in the oral cavity, can be ameliorated by the antimicrobial effects of the plant's constituents. Studies indicate that regular use of Acorus calamus extracts or preparations can significantly reduce the microbial load in the oral environment, leading to a decrease in the incidence of these oral health issues.

To harness the oral health benefits of Sweet flag, various plant parts can be utilized. The rhizomes of Acorus calamus are particularly rich in the essential oil containing eugenol, making them a key source for oral health preparations. Extracts can be obtained by distillation processes, yielding essential oils with concentrations ranging from 1.5% to 3.5%, depending on the extraction method. These extracts can be incorporated into mouthwashes, toothpaste formulations, or topical applications to capitalize on Sweet flag's natural oral health-promoting properties.

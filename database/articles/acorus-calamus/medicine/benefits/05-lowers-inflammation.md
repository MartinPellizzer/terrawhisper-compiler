Sweet flag (Acorus calamus) lowers inflammation thanks to its rich composition of bioactive compounds, particularly β-asarone, which exhibits potent anti-inflammatory properties. Studies have revealed that the rhizomes of Acorus calamus contain approximately 1-2% β-asarone by weight, contributing to its therapeutic effects. Additionally, the plant contains essential oils, such as α-asarone, cis-isoasarone, and methyl isoeugenol, further enhancing its anti-inflammatory potential. The synergistic action of these constituents inhibits pro-inflammatory cytokines and mediators, reducing overall inflammation in the body.

Numerous health conditions can benefit from Sweet flag's anti-inflammatory properties. Conditions such as rheumatoid arthritis, inflammatory bowel diseases, and asthma can see improvements due to the plant's ability to modulate inflammatory responses. The precise regulation of inflammatory pathways by Sweet flag contributes to alleviating symptoms associated with these conditions, providing relief to individuals suffering from chronic inflammation.

To harness the anti-inflammatory benefits of Sweet flag, the rhizomes are the primary plant parts used. These can be incorporated into various preparations, including teas, tinctures, and topical applications. A common method involves preparing a decoction by boiling 5 grams of dried rhizomes in 250 ml of water for 15 minutes. This infusion can be consumed as a tea or applied externally, depending on the targeted health concern. Care should be taken to adhere to recommended dosages, as excessive consumption may lead to adverse effects. Sweet flag's ability to lower inflammation underscores its potential as a natural remedy for a range of inflammatory conditions.


Sweet flag tincture is an alcoholic extract made from the rhizomes of the Acorus calamus plant. It involves soaking the dried and chopped rhizomes in alcohol (such as ethanol) to extract its active compounds, like β-asarone and essential oils. This preparation is known for its various medicinal uses, primarily as a digestive aid, anti-inflammatory agent, and for its potential in improving memory and cognitive functions. The tincture is believed to possess antispasmodic, carminative, and sedative properties, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

To prepare sweet flag tincture, finely chop 50 grams of dried Acorus calamus rhizomes and place them in a glass jar. Pour 500 milliliters of 70% ethanol or vodka over the rhizomes, ensuring they're fully submerged. Seal the jar and store it in a dark, cool place for about 4 to 6 weeks, shaking it occasionally. After this period, strain the mixture through a fine cloth or filter paper, and the resulting liquid is the sweet flag tincture.

Dosage for sweet flag tincture can vary based on individual needs and the intended use. Typically, a dosage of 1 to 3 milliliters (equivalent to about 20-60 drops) diluted in water or juice is recommended, taken up to three times daily. However, caution should be exercised due to potential side effects such as nausea, vomiting, and headaches, especially when taken in high doses or for prolonged periods. It's crucial to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding individuals and those with underlying health conditions or taking medications, to avoid adverse reactions or interactions.

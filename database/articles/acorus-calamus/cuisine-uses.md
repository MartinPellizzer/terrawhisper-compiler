Sweet flag (Acorus calamus) is a plant with a long history of culinary use in various parts of the world. While all parts of the plant are edible, it's primarily the rhizomes that are used for culinary purposes. The rhizomes are starchy and have been traditionally used in a variety of dishes, including soups, stews, and pickles. They are also sometimes used as a flavoring agent in alcoholic beverages like bitters and liqueurs.

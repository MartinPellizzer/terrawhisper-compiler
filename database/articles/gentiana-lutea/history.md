Gentian (Gentiana lutea), also known as Yellow Gentian, has a long history of use rooted mainly in traditional medicine and divination practices. It is native to Europe but has also been introduced in North America and Asia. The plant's root and flower have been used for centuries to treat various health conditions and to seek guidance during times of uncertainty.

In the realm of traditional medicine, Gentian was commonly employed as a remedy for digestive issues, including stomachaches and indigestion, since it contains bitter principles which stimulate digestion. Additionally, its antimicrobial properties were exploited to treat infections such as respiratory conditions or intestinal disorders. It also found use as an expectorant in cases of bronchitis and other ailments that require mucus thinning and removal from the respiratory system.

Beyond its medicinal applications, Gentian was used in divination practices as a tool for seeking guidance and insight into one's life situation, offering answers to questions about love, career, or personal growth. The plant held considerable cultural significance, particularly in Europe, where it was believed that its root possessed magical properties. This belief stemmed from ancient mythology where the Goddess of Wisdom, Athena, is said to have bestowed Gentian with healing powers after receiving a cup of water from Zeus.

The legends surrounding Gentian are rooted in the mystical and symbolic aspects of the plant. One such legend asserts that Gentian's yellow color represents the sun's golden rays, which endows those who consume it with strength and vitality. Another story tells of a king who fell in love with a maiden named Belides, who was actually the Goddess Athena in disguise. The king pursued her relentlessly until she finally agreed to meet him one last time at the spot where Gentian grew naturally, hoping to make him realize that he could not possess something so powerful and divine. When he attempted to dig up the plant, it remained unharmed, revealing its supernatural origin and ultimately driving the king to change his ways.

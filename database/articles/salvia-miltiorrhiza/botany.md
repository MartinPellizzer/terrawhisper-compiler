Dan Shen (Salvia miltiorrhiza), which has its scientific classification based on domain of eukaryota - kingdom plantae, belongs to the botanical family Lamiaceae and genus Salivia where it is one specific member or species called 'miltiorrhiz'. This medicinal herb has various common names across regions like Chinese Red Sage.

Dan's sibling variants are mainly categorized into two prominent divisions- Type A, which features purple blooming flowers; and type B with white blossoms that have a pinkish tinge to them. While both types exhibit same attributes structurally speaking - their leaves carry fringe like edges or wavy lines around the margin hence making this characteristic feature distinctive of Salvia species members such as these variants are known for exhibiting some morphological differences in terms leaf texture, height and flowering time with Type A being taller than B variant which has larger foliage but it generally smaller.

Salvia miltiorrhiza originated from China where its natural habitat is found mainly comprising the moist mountainous woodland area within diverse sub-regions such as southwestern part of Jiangxi province, southern parts in Hunan and other spots dispersed across Hubei Province too; it can also be seen growing in northeast areas like Liaoning.

The typical Dan Shen lifecycle includes seed germination (plant reproduction through seeds), growth from a young plant to an adult one which takes 3 or so months, flowering time between spring and autumn depending on climate conditions followed by the production of fruits containing numerous tiny black/purplish-black acutely angled nutlets that are dispersed once mature. The germinated seed is vital in terms of its propagation; however other than seeds Salvia miltiorrhiza can also be reproduced vegetatively via cutting which provides ease with cultivating this herb due to short growth cycle and ability adapts well under different environments.

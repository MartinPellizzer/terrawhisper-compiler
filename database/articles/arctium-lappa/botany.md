
Burdock (Arctium lappa) belongs to the following traditional taxonomy: Domain - Eukaryota, Kingdom - Plantae, Phylum - Angiosperms, Class - Eudicots, Order - Asterales, Family - Asteraceae, Genus - Arctium, Species - Arctium lappa.

The common names and variants of Burdock include Greater burdock, Edible burdock, Lappa, Wild rhubarb, and Niu Bang Zi.

Burdock is characterized by its biennial herbaceous nature, typically growing up to 1.5 to 2 meters in height. It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green on the upper side and downy underneath. The plant boasts robust taproots, thick stems, and prickly burrs that house the seeds.

This plant is widely distributed throughout Europe, Asia, and North America. It thrives in various habitats, particularly favoring disturbed areas, roadsides, meadows, and wastelands. Burdock tends to grow in well-drained, nitrogen-rich soils and can tolerate different climates, from temperate to subtropical regions.

Tea: To make a burdock tea, boil the dried roots or leaves in water for several minutes. Strain and let it cool down. Burdock tea can help detoxify the liver, aid digestion, and improve blood sugar levels.

Tincture: A tincture is an alcohol-based extract of the herb. To make burdock tincture, soak dried roots or leaves in a jar filled with high-proof vodka for around 2-3 weeks. Strain the liquid and store in dark glass bottles as they usually have a longer shelf life compared to teas.

Infusion: Similar to tea, an infusion of burdock involves steeping dried roots or leaves in hot water, but without boiling it to ensure gentler extraction of nutrients. Steep for 10-20 minutes and strain before drinking. Burdock infusions can help improve skin conditions like eczema due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Decoction: A decoction is boiling the roots or leaves in water for a longer period (15-30 minutes) to break down their cell walls and extract more nutrients and flavonoids than when using an infusion method. This process also makes it easier to absorb the herb's minerals into our bodies.

Poultice: A poultice is a soft, moist mixture of burdock leaves that can be applied directly to the skin for soothing relief from inflammation or muscle pain. To make a burdock poultice, grind the leaves in water and apply the paste to the affected area for 10-15 minutes.

Ointment: Burdock ointments are made by mixing an oil or cream with its herbal extracts, creating a soothing balm that can be used topically on skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Combine your choice of oil with burdock tincture to make an effective ointment.

Extract: Herb extractions are concentrated forms of herbal remedies that contain the active components in higher amounts than teas or infusions, while being easier to consume. Burdock extracts can be obtained through using solvents like glycerin or ethanol to pull out the beneficial compounds from the plant material.

Juice: Extracting burdock juice is a method similar to making tea but with fresh leaves, roots, or stems. Grind the chosen part of the plant and then press it to release its juices for a refreshing drink that can be combined with other fruits or vegetables for added health benefits.

Roots: Burdock roots can also be consumed as a vegetable, cooked in various ways like roasting or frying them. They are high in fiber and have antioxidant properties, making them a nutritious addition to any meal.

Capsules: These medicinal preparations can be encased within capsule shells for convenient intake as part of supplement regimens containing extracts or herb powder.

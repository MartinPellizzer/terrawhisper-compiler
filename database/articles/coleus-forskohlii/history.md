Coleus (Coleus forskohlii), a tropical plant native to India and Sri Lanka, has been used extensively throughout history for its medicinal properties, role in various traditional rituals, and even in popular superstitions. The roots of its usage can be traced back to ancient times when it was employed as an essential ingredient in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for treating various ailments like asthma, congestion, heart problems, gastrointestinal issues, and other respiratory concerns.

Coleus also played a significant role in ancient societies as part of their spiritual practices. In particular, its flowers have been used in divination rituals to foretell the future and gain insight into complex questions such as life decisions, relationship matters, or even predicting one's fortune. The plant's unique properties were believed to possess mystical powers that could reveal hidden truths and guide people towards making well-informed choices.

Additionally, this vibrant and versatile plant has been a subject of numerous legends and folklore across different cultures. In India, for instance, the leaf of Coleus is associated with the story of a king who sought to create a new form of entertainment for his people. The king had an artist paint a portrait that seemed to come alive when a Coleus leaf was placed over it. This magical painting became known as "Banyan Tree" and inspired the tradition of Rangoli art in which colorful patterns are created on floors during festivals.

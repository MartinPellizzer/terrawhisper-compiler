 Sibeer is a Russian word for people who live in or around modern-day Russia (this might also have been the name given to early settlers), and gin means silver, referring to both the color associated with its bark and some parts of this plant. However it isn't considered botanically related at all as compared to true Ginseng which belongs from genus Panax that is known for medicinal properties such in traditional Chinese medicine systems used worldwide across centuries by people who live near these areas due primarily because they have proven themselves useful time again under varied circumstances ranging between healing illnesses like malaria or cancer treatment up until boosting immune system functioning.

In some regions of Siberia, this plant is known as "King’s Ginseng" which holds great symbolism too – kings would gift the herb to their most loyal and esteemed followers signifying deep gratitude towards these valuable relationships built through shared experiences over time together on a journey full with upheavals yet still maintaining endurance till very last stage.

The mythological beliefs surrounding Siberian Ginseng have always been intriguing especially as it is said that consummating relations between humans and supernatural creatures (like fairies) often occurred when someone ate these special herbs which would give them power boost along with increased sexual desires, eventually enabling both parties to share secret intimate moments together.

In the literary world too there are references made towards this remarkable plant – Russian writer Anton Chekhov referred Siberian Ginseng for treating various ailments such as fever and diarrhea in his novella 'Ward No 6', thereby indicating its traditional medicinal importance among locals despite their lack of modern understanding about science behind it or methods used by contemporary healthcare providers.

Throughout history, people have come to recognize this plant's incredible healing properties - both internally as well externally through use in folk medicine remedies that include treatments for wounds and sore throat conditions while also being utilized during times when natural disasters struck which impact affected populations severely affecting their immune systems making them more susceptible towards common diseases prevalent around these regions.

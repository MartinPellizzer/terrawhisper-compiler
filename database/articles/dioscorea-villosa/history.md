From early times, Wild Yam (Dioscorea villosa), native to North America, has been utilized for its medicinal properties; as seen in traditional medicine where it is believed to possess many beneficial elements. It was often used as a diuretic, expectorant and laxative that facilitates the removal of unwanted waste material from one's body. The root's anti-inflammatory properties were also known to alleviate pain associated with rheumatism and arthritis while boosting strength and stamina. In some instances, it was even believed to treat certain types of cancer.

Additionally, the tuberous root was utilized in divination practices by Native Americans as a tool for predicting future events or revealing hidden knowledge. They would carve symbols into the yam's skin which they then roasted and burned over hot coals until charred, the pattern of residue that remained after cooling offered guidance for critical decision making, resolving disputes, or even forecasting the success or failure of upcoming ventures.

Wild Yam's rich history is also steeped in legends, many of which highlight its spiritual and symbolic importance. One such story originates from Native American mythology where it is said that the plant was created by a revered character, sometimes called The Creator or Great Spirit. The yam was given as a gift to humans to help them with their earthly concerns, providing healing, wisdom and nurture through its many uses.

Another legend tells of two hunter brothers who once discovered the plant while on a journey. They were amazed by its numerous medicinal benefits but struggled to remember all they had learned about it upon returning home. Filled with regret, they set out again in search of the magical yam and upon locating their cherished discovery, were granted the ability to recall its powers and share them with others.

The rich heritage and diverse uses of Wild Yam (Dioscorea villosa) have been intertwined with human experiences, not only for its practical benefits but also as a source of wisdom, guidance, and intricate cultural beliefs.

 Dating back to ancient times, lemon has played a vital role in the field of herbal medicine. It was valued for its antiseptic properties and was often used to treat wounds and infections by applying lemon juice or its extracts. Additionally, lemons were used to create medicinal tonics and beverages that aimed at promoting overall health and well-being. The fruit's vitamin C content made it useful for preventing scurvy during sailing journeys.

 Lemons have been employed as part of divination rituals throughout history, particularly in the form of reading their seeds. This practice involved carefully extracting lemon seeds after consumption, interpreting their shapes and patterns to foretell the future. The belief was that each seed's unique shape held a hidden message, revealing potential events or circumstances yet to come.

 Lemons have been the subject of various mythical tales over the years, primarily rooted in the Mediterranean region. One such legend originates from ancient Greece and involves the goddess Aphrodite. According to this story, a group of nymphs were turned into lemons after offending Aphrodite by rejecting her advances. As a result, they became symbols of beauty and desire, which explains their association with love and romance.

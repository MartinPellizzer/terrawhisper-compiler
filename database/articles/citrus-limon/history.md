The etymology of "Lemon" originates from a Latin word 'limon'. The fruit was named after its place or region known as Limonia which is believed to be located somewhere in South Asia possibly present-day Iran, India and Pakistan where the early form existed since ancient times but it's origins have been traced back far before this period. From those primitive stages when first cultivated around 2053 BCE; Lemons eventually spread across regions through various trading routes including Europe by Romans as a part of their dietary habits, Asia with Islamic merchants and Africa via traders crossing the Mediterranean Sea during medieval times where it became an important crop for several civilizations like Egyptian dynasties.

Lemons or rather Citrus limon has had significant cultural impacts across history ranging from religious beliefs in Eastern religions such as Hinduism to culinary practices, even its color and scent were believed by some cultures symbolized various elements including wealth due the rarity of it during certain periods when they weren't readily available for consumption. Lemonade made with lemons has been a popular beverage since ancient times which played crucial role in keeping people hydrated especially those who traveled long distances or worked under harsh conditions such as slaves and soldiers but over time this tangy drink became more than just refreshment; it transformed into representing freedom, prosperity, optimism.

A significant myth associated with Lemons stems back thousands of years ago when Egypt's Pharaoh Akhenaten was said to have found a cure for various illness using 'Lemon Water'. The belief that lemon could be used as medicine has persisted since then influencing healing methods over different eras right from ancient Greece through Renaissance Italy until now where scientists explore more about its benefits ranging antibacterial properties and weight loss capabilities along with other health-related aspects.

As Lemons started being recognized worldwide during the 19th century, it played prominent role as motifs on paintings or symbolic elements in literary masterpieces by various writers across different languages such William Shakespeare's 'The Tempest', Dante Alighieri’s ‘Divine Comedy’, and even more recent ones like Agatha Christie‘s famous mystery novels where its tart taste signified secrets about murderous characters.

Lastly, folkloristic uses of Lemons were also diverse – ranging from superstitions to home remedies for various ailments including toothache pain or curing indigestion - which shows how deeply connected humanity was and still is with these humble fruits that began its journey on earth thousands years ago.

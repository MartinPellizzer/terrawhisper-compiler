The leaves of Citrus limon are simple, elliptical in shape, and typically measure between 5 to 7 centimeters (2 to 2.75 inches) in length and 2.5 to 4 centimeters (1 to 1.5 inches) in width. They have a thickness of 0.1 to 0.2 centimeters (0.04 to 0.08 inches).

In terms of arrangement, lemon tree leaves are alternately positioned and spirally arranged along the branches. Their orientation is predominantly horizontal, creating a dense canopy.

The color of lemon tree leaves is glossy dark green, giving them a distinctive appearance. The upper surface is shiny and smooth, while the underside is dull. These leaves are known for their leathery texture.

The leaf apex is acute or pointed, and the base is cuneate, forming a wedge shape. Their margins are entire, meaning they are smooth and lack any serrations or lobes.

A prominent midrib runs down the center of each leaf, which is supported by a pinnate venation system, with smaller veins branching out from the main vein.

The leaf lamina is simple and undivided, and the leaves attach to the branches through petioles, which are 1 to 2 centimeters (0.4 to 0.8 inches) long. Stipules, small leaf-like structures at the base of the petioles, are notably absent in lemon tree leaves.

The inflorescence type of Capsicum annuum is an "umbel," where multiple flowers radiate from a common point, similar to the spokes of an umbrella. Each node typically bears a single flower, indicating a simple inflorescence arrangement.

The flowers of Capsicum annuum display an actinomorphic symmetry, meaning they are radially symmetrical and can be divided into equal halves in any direction. This symmetry is an important aspect of its floral structure.

In terms of flower structure, Capsicum annuum features complete flowers, where all floral parts are present, including sepals, petals, stamens, pistils, and other essential components.

Flowers are axillary, attaching themselves in the leaf axils, giving rise to a well-distributed floral pattern on the plant.

The shape of the Capsicum annuum flower is generally flat and circular, referred to as "rotate." Each flower measures approximately 2.5 cm (1 inch) in both length and width.

The color of these flowers varies, with common shades being white, green, or yellow. They possess a smooth texture and a glabrous (hairless) surface.

The flowers exhibit a pentamerous arrangement, meaning there are five of each floral part: sepals, petals, stamens, and a single pistil. The combination of sepals and petals is referred to as the perianth.

Stamens, the male reproductive parts, number five, while there is one pistil, the female reproductive organ, per flower. The ovary is superior in position.

The style is singular, and the stigma, the receptive part of the pistil, can be lobed or capitate, having a head-shaped appearance.

Notably, Capsicum annuum flowers lack stipules, small leaf-like structures often found at the base of the petioles.

At the base of the flower, you can find bracts, modified leaves that protect the developing flower bud. These flowers are attached to the plant via a variable-length peduncle, typically measuring between 1 to 3 cm (0.4-1.2 inches), and the individual flowers are connected to the peduncle by short pedicels, often less than 1 cm (0.4 inch) in length.

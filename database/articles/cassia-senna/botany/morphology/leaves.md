The leaves of Cassia senna have a compound, pinnate structure, meaning that they consist of multiple leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern along a central stalk.

In terms of leaf shape, Cassia senna leaves are elliptical, exhibiting a horizontal orientation. They are typically 15-30 cm (6-12 inches) in length and 2-5 cm (0.8-2 inches) in width. The leaves are relatively thin, measuring about 0.2-0.5 mm (0.008-0.02 inches) in thickness. Their color is predominantly green, with a smooth and glabrous texture on the leaf surface.

The leaves of Cassia senna have an acute apex and a cuneate base, which means the tips are pointed, and the bases are wedge-shaped. The leaf margins are entire, without serrations or lobes. Venation in these leaves is pinnate, with prominent and raised midribs on the lower surface of the leaflets.

One notable feature is the presence of stipules, which are lanceolate and about 1 cm (0.4 inches) in length. Stipules are small, leaf-like structures found at the base of the petiole.

Speaking of petioles, the leaves of Cassia senna are petiolate, meaning they have stalks that attach them to the stem. The petioles are typically 2-6 cm (0.8-2.4 inches) long and are glabrous.

These seeds of Cassia senna are typically found within elongated pods, with each pod containing approximately 5 to 10 seeds. The seeds themselves have an elliptical or oblong shape, measuring about 4 to 6 millimeters in length, 2 to 3 millimeters in width, and 1 to 2 millimeters in thickness.

The outermost layer of these seeds, known as the seed coat, is brown in color, smooth to the touch, and possesses a shiny texture. The seed coat is relatively thin, measuring around 0.1 millimeters in thickness, and is impermeable. The hilum, a small scar on the seed's surface, marks the point of attachment to the pod.

The seeds of Cassia senna are quite hard and firm, with a significant level of hardness. Inside the seed, an embryo is present, complete with cotyledons. Typically, there are two cotyledons, which are fleshy structures within the seed that serve as the initial food source for the developing plant.

Moreover, the seed includes a radicle, which is part of the embryo and will develop into the plant's primary root upon germination.

The fruit type of Cassia senna is a legume, often referred to as a pod. These fruits are typically arranged either singly or in clusters. They are distributed along terminal racemes, giving the plant a unique appearance.

The attachment of the fruits is stalked, with a length of 1-2 cm (0.39-0.79 inches). The fruits themselves exhibit specific dimensions. They are linear, oblong, flattened, and slightly curved, with lengths ranging from 7 to 20 cm (2.76-7.87 inches), widths of 0.5-1 cm (0.2-0.39 inches), and thicknesses of 0.3-0.4 cm (0.12-0.16 inches).

In terms of color, Cassia senna fruits are typically light to dark brown, making them easily distinguishable in the plant. They have a smooth and leathery texture, with a glabrous (hairless) surface. Some may exhibit transverse lines or mottling, adding to their distinct appearance.

One significant characteristic of these fruits is their mode of dehiscence. They are dehiscent, which means they split longitudinally into two valves to release their seeds. This dehiscence mechanism is an essential feature for seed dispersal and reproduction.

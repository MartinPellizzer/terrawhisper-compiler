Cassia senna possesses a taproot system, with a single, dominant primary root. This primary root structure provides stability to the plant and allows it to penetrate deeper into the soil.

The roots of Cassia senna are predominantly fibrous, indicating a network of fine roots that branch out from the primary taproot. This fibrous root structure enhances the plant's ability to absorb water and nutrients from a wider soil area.

Root branching in Cassia senna is moderate, ensuring a balance between stability and resource acquisition. The acute tips of the roots facilitate easy penetration into the soil, further aiding in nutrient uptake.

Root density in Cassia senna is noteworthy, with approximately 350 roots per cubic centimeter (2,240 roots per cubic inch). This high root density is a significant advantage, as it allows for efficient soil exploration and resource extraction.

The root system of Cassia senna reaches a depth of about 30 centimeters (11.8 inches), enabling the plant to access moisture and nutrients from deeper soil layers.

Roots of this plant have a diameter of approximately 0.5 centimeters (0.2 inches), which provides an appropriate balance between stability and nutrient uptake capacity. The root length extends to around 60 centimeters (23.6 inches), ensuring effective soil exploration.

The color of Cassia senna roots is light brown, and their surface texture is smooth, which aids in reducing soil adhesion and facilitating easier penetration.

Notably, Cassia senna lacks root nodules, which are typically associated with nitrogen-fixing plants. However, sparse fine root hairs are present, enhancing the plant's ability to absorb water and nutrients from the soil.

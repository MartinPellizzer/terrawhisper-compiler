These fruits of Azadirachta indica are classified as drupes, a type of fruit with a hard, stony seed enclosed by a fleshy outer layer. They can be found either singly or in clusters throughout the tree, usually attached to the tree via a pedicel measuring 0.5 to 2 centimeters (0.2 to 0.8 inches) in length.

The neem fruit exhibits an ellipsoid shape, with measurements of approximately 1.5 to 3 centimeters (0.6 to 1.2 inches) in length, 1 to 1.5 centimeters (0.4 to 0.6 inches) in width, and 0.6 to 1 centimeter (0.2 to 0.4 inches) in thickness. The color of the fruit varies during its maturation process. When young, neem fruits are green, but they turn yellow when they ripen.

In terms of texture, these fruits are characterized by a smooth and fleshy exterior, giving them a glossy appearance. One distinctive feature of neem fruits is the presence of longitudinal ridges or markings on their surface, which adds to their unique visual identity.

Furthermore, neem fruits are dehiscent, meaning they split open when they reach full ripeness. This characteristic facilitates the dispersal of seeds and is a significant feature for the tree's reproductive cycle.

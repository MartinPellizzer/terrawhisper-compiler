Aloe vera fruits are classified as capsules. These capsules are arranged in clusters, hanging vertically near the base of the inflorescence. They are sessile, directly attached to the stem.

The shape of Aloe vera fruits is oblong and cylindrical, with a length of approximately 2-3 centimeters (0.8-1.2 inches) and a width of about 1 centimeter (0.4 inches). The fruits have a thickness of 4-5 millimeters (0.16-0.2 inches).

Initially green, Aloe vera fruits turn brown as they mature. Their surface is smooth, and the texture is dry and papery, a characteristic important for seed protection.

Observing closely, you might notice faint longitudinal lines or markings on the fruits. This is a common feature, adding a subtle detail to the appearance of the capsules.

A significant trait of Aloe vera fruits is their dehiscence, a process where the fruits split open when they are mature. This allows the release of seeds and facilitates the plant's reproductive cycle.

The fruit type of Achillea millefolium is an achene, a small, dry, one-seeded fruit. It typically measures between 1/16 to 1/8 inch in diameter. These achenes are densely arranged in a specific structure called a capitulum, forming a cluster of numerous fruits. They are evenly distributed across the flower head.

These achenes are attached to the receptacle, which is the base of the florets, ensuring their firm connection to the plant. In terms of shape, yarrow achenes are oblong or cylindrical in appearance. They have a length of 1.5 to 3.2 mm (1/16 to 1/8 inch), a width of 0.6 to 1 mm (1/32 to 1/16 inch), and a thickness of approximately 0.3 mm (1/64 inch).

The color of these achenes tends to be brown to gray. They have a smooth and glossy texture, with a glabrous surface, meaning they lack hairs or trichomes. Notably, there are no distinctive markings on these achenes.

One unique characteristic of Achillea millefolium's fruits is the absence of dehiscence. This means that the achenes do not split open to release their seeds. Instead, they remain intact within the fruit.

Bupleurum chinense is characterized by its fibrous root system, primarily consisting of a taproot that reaches 5-10 cm in length and displays moderate branching. These roots have a slender diameter and grow 10-20 cm deep in the soil, with a light brown color and a generally smooth surface. They lack nodules and have sparse, short, non-glandular root hairs.

The herbaceous stems of Bupleurum chinense are typically arranged alternately, with heights ranging from 30 to 100 cm and diameters of 0.2 to 0.5 cm. Stem color varies from green to brown or reddish-brown, and texture can be smooth, rough, or pubescent. Distinct nodes serve as leaf attachment points, while internodes vary in length, typically measuring 5 to 15 cm.

The simple leaves are linear-oblong, measuring 2-5 cm in length, 0.2-0.5 cm in width, and 0.1-0.2 cm in thickness. They are green, smooth, and hairless, with an acute apex and cuneate base. The leaves lack teeth or lobes, have parallel venation, are sessile (directly attached to the stem), and do not have petioles or stipules.

The flowers are arranged in umbels, typically 5-10 cm across, with actinomorphic symmetry. The herbaceous, glabrous flowers are yellow, consisting of five sepals and five petals. Five stamens surround the pistil, which has an ovary, short style, and two-lobed stigma. The flowers are supported by pedicels and have bracts.

Bupleurum chinense produces schizocarp fruits arranged in umbels with 1-3 fruits per cluster, attached to pedicels. The elliptical fruits measure 4.5-6.5 mm in length and change from green to dark brown as they mature. They have a smooth, shiny, glabrous surface and two mericarps that separate into two one-seeded units.

The seeds are dicotyledonous, oval in shape, and lack appendages. They are small, firm, brown, and have a smooth, shiny seed coat with low permeability. The seed hilum is distinct, and the seeds contain two cotyledons and a straight embryo with a present radicle.

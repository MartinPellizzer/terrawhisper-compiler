Bupleurum chinense produces schizocarp fruits that are arranged in umbels, typically clustered with 1 to 3 fruits per cluster. These fruits are attached to the plant through pedicels measuring 2 to 4 millimeters in length. 

In terms of their physical appearance, the fruits are elliptical in shape, with a length ranging from 4.5 to 6.5 millimeters (approximately 0.18 to 0.26 inches), a width of 2.5 to 3.5 millimeters (around 0.10 to 0.14 inches), and a thickness of 1.5 to 2.5 millimeters (roughly 0.06 to 0.10 inches). 

The color of these fruits changes as they mature. While immature, they are green, but as they ripen, they turn dark brown. Their texture is described as smooth and shiny, and the fruit surface is glabrous, meaning it lacks any hairs or trichomes. 

Notably, these fruits do not have distinct markings, and they exhibit a unique dehiscence mechanism. Each fruit consists of two mericarps, which eventually separate into two one-seeded units.
